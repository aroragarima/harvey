Bar Council of Andhra Pradesh v Kurapati Satyanarayana

Supreme Court of India

15 November 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1387; (2003) 1 SCC 102; (2003) SCC (Cr) 155; AIR 2003 SC 175; AIR 2002 SCW 4609; 2003 (1) ALD(SC) 71; 2002 (5) AWC 3572; JT 2002 (9) SC 360; 2002(8) SCALE 401; 2002 (7) SLT 47; [2002] Supp4 S.C.R. 204; 2002 (8) Supreme 198; 2003 (1) UC 149; 2003 (1) UPLBEC 121

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges

Summary: Advocates and Judges - Advocates Act, 1961 - Professional misconduct - Appeal against the order of the Disciplinary Committee of the Bar Council of India, which set aside the order passed by the State Council removing the name of 'the Delinquent', from the roll of the State Bar Council - Order of setting aside, whether justified? - Held, No, the finding of the Disciplinary Committee of the Bar Council of India that there was no intention on the part of the Delinquent advocate to misappropriate the money of his client or to de-fraud him is not only unfounded and perverse but also lacks in serious thought which was required to be given by the Disciplinary Committee to the Bar Council of India in the discharge of quasi-judicial function while probing into the grave charge of professional misconduct by an advocate in the discharge of his duties as a counsel - Any laxity while judging the misconduct which is not bonafide and dishonest would undermine the confidence of the litigant public resulting in the collapse of legal system - Having regard to the serious nature of misconduct the punishment of removal of his name from the roll of Bar Council would be the only appropriate punishment - Appeal allowed.
