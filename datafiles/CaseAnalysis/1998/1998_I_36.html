Coir Board, Ernakulam, Cochin and Another v Indira Devi P. S. and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 March 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1627; (1998) 3 SCC 259; (1998) SCC (L&S) 806; AIR 1998 SC 2801; 1998 (1) CLR 866; JT 1998 (2) SC 332; 1998 (1) LLJ 937; 1998 (2) LLN 400; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 87

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Telephone, Charitable, Research, Coir Industry Act, 1953

Summary: Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2(j) - Industry - All embracing scope of definition laid down in Bangalore Water Supply and Sewerage Board AIR 1978 SC 548 - (A) Whether sweeping test propounded in aforesaid decision that every organisation which does useful service and employs people can be labeled as industry is contemplated by Act? - (B) Whether a larger Bench should be constituted to reconsider decision in Bangalore Water Supply and Sewerage Board? - (A) Such a sweeping test is not contemplated by Act - (B) Matter directed to be placed before Hon'ble Chief Justice of India to consider whether a larger Bench should be constituted - Appeal allowed.
