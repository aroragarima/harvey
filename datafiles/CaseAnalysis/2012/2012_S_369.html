Nafis Ahmad and another v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

26 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 202; (2012) 11 SCC 800; AIR 2012 SC 3901; 2012 (94) ALR 705; 2012 (6) AWC 5948; 2012 (117) RD 21; 2012(6) SCALE 643

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: U.P. Kshetra Samiti and Zila Parishad Adhiniyam, 1961

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Administrative - Uttar Pradesh Kshetra Samiti and Zila Parishad Adhiniyam, 1961 - Issuance of license - Failure to provide hearing - Effect - Respondents were issued license to hold private cattle market in their land on Tuesdays - Appellants were also granted license for holding private cattle market on their lands on Mondays - Appellants made representation to Zila Panchayat seeking permission to hold cattle market on Tuesdays - Zila Panchayats granted permission - Respondents filed writ petition - HC quashed license issued to appellants and directed Zila Panchayat to hear parties and decide the matter afresh - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC placed an erroneous interpretation on language of bye-law 34 framed under the Act - Held, HC had not placed any construction on language of bye-law 34 - Appellants' contention that HC had placed an erroneous interpretation on language of bye-law 34 was wholly misplaced and unfounded - HC had noted arguments of parties but had not given any interpretation on language of bye-law 34 - HC was not in error in adopting course by sending matter back to Zila Panchayat, as impugned order/licence to appellants was granted on without hearing respondents - Appeal dismissed.
