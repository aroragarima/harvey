Textile Labour Association & Anr v The Official Liquidator & Anr

Supreme Court of India

12 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, AR. Lakshmanan, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 277; (2004) 9 SCC 741; AIR 2004 SC 2336; 2004 (3) BC 424; 5 CLC 741; [2004] 120 Comp Cas 505; 2004 (2) CompLJ 409; 2004 (101) FLR 627; 2004 (4) G.L.R. 2993; 2004 (4) JhrCR(SC) 186; JT 2004 (Supp1) SC 1; 2004 (2) LLJ 760; 2004 (2) LLN 825; 2004(4) SCALE 414

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Corporate - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 142 - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 529, 529A, 530 - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1986, s. 20 - Purpose of Section 529 A is to ensure that workmen should not be deprived of their legitimate claims in event of liquidation of company and assets of company would remain charged for payment of workers' dues and such charge will be pari passu with charge of secured creditors - There is no other statutory provision overriding claim of secured creditors except s.529 A - This Section overrides preferential claims u/s. 530 also - U/s 529 A dues of workers and debts due to secured creditors are to be treated pari passu and have to be treated as prior to all other dues.
