Indian Aluminum Company Etc v Kerala State Electricity Board

Supreme Court of India

23 July 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, A. Alagiriswami, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 399; (1975) 2 SCC 414; AIR 1975 SC 1967; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 70

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, ss. 49, 59 and 79(j) - Agreement binding State Electricity Board to supply electricity at certain rates - Found that contractual rates are less than cost of generation, distribution and supply of electricity - Resulting in loss to State Electricity Board in its operations - Whether board can confer upon itself power to revise rates of electricity? - Held, neither s. 49 nor s. 59 of Act of 1948, confers any authority on Board to override a contractual stipulation as to rates and unilaterally enhance rates in derogation of such contractual stipulation even if it finds that rates stipulated in contract are not sufficient to meet cost of production and supply of electricity and it is incurring operational loss - Whether s. 79(j) of Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948 confers authority on board on to unilaterally exonerate itself from stipulation contained in contracts and enhance rates? - If power to enhance rates unilaterally in derogation of contractual stipulation does not reside in any provision of Act, it cannot be created by regulations made under Supply Act, either this power can be found in some provision of Act or it is not there at all - Regulations in nature of subordinate legislation cannot confer authority on Board to interfere with contractual rights and obligations, unless specified power to make such regulations is vested in Board by some provision in Statute, expressly or by necessary implication - Further held, it does not, therefore make any difference whether regulations u/s. 79 (j) of Act of 1948 were made or not at date when a surcharge was levied - Even if they were made, they could not have conferred authority, on Board to unilaterally exonerate itself from stipulation contained in contracts and enhance rates - Appeals dismissed.
