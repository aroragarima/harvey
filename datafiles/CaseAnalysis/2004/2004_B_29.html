Commissioner of Customs, Mumbai v B.V. Jewels

Supreme Court of India

14 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 848; (2004) 12 SCC 720; 2004 (172) E.L.T. 3; JT 2004 (7) SC 460; 2004 (64) RLT 323; 2004(7) SCALE 690; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 413

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Trade

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, ss. 111(d), 111(j), 111(1), 111(o), 111(m), 112(a), 112(b), 113(d), 113(i), 114(i) and 114 A - Customs Rules, 1966 - Operations in Warehouses Regulations 1966 - Whether CEGAT had erred setting aside order of Commr. and holding that accusations against respondent were not established? - Expected recovery was worked out on basis of figures supplied by concerned respondents and average has been worked out - There was practically no manufacturing activity carried out by M/s B V Star and, therefore question of any staff being there does not arise - Diversion of capital goods imported by M/s B V Star to M/s B V Jewels is clearly impermissible - Missing installed motors, brushes, hand pieces these were found to be in original pickings and were cleared by bills of entry Nos 3126 and 7382 D/- 8-7-1993 and 19-6-1993 respectively - On a bare reading of details of goods in respect of which demand was confirmed/ goes to show that it has nothing to do with promotion of exports of gem and jewellery - Held, it would be appropriate to direct CEGAT to work out details so far as alleged shortage of 73, 730 carats of diamonds valued at Rs. 26,29,54,490/- are concerned - Appeal disposed of.
