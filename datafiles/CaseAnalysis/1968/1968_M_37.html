Motibhai Fulabhai Patel and Company v Messrs R. Prasad and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 October 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 357; AIR 1970 SC 829; 1978 (2) E.L.T. 370; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 580

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Rules, r. 40 - Unlawful mixing of duty-paid tobacco with non-duty paid tobacco - Confiscation - Whether confiscation ordered by Collector, Central Excise is illegal and under any circumstance he could not have confiscated entire quantity of tobacco used in mixture? - Appellants were guilty of an unlawful act in mixing duty paid tobacco with non-duty paid tobacco - R. 40 permits Central Excise authorities to confiscate only those goods on which duty has not been paid - It does not permit them either specifically or by necessary implication to confiscate other goods - Therefore it was not permissible for Collector to confiscate entire tobacco mixture - At same time no person can be permitted to benefit by his wrongful act - No rule of law should be so interpreted as to permit or encourage its circumvention - If by wrongful act of a party he renders it impossible for authorities to confiscate u/r. 40 non-duty paid goods it is open to those authorities to confiscate from out of goods seized, goods of value reasonably representing value of non-duty paid goods mixed in goods seized - Held, Collector, Central Excise could have confiscated out of tobacco seized, so much of it as can be held to reasonably represent value of tobacco on which duty had not been paid - Appeal partly allowed.
