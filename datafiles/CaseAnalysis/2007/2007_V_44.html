Mohannakumaran Nair v Vijayakumaran Nair

Supreme Court of India

11 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1318; (2007) 14 SCC 426; AIR 2008 SC 213; 2007 (69) ALR 905; 2008 (1) AWC 335; 2008 (1) BC 203; 2008 (1) CTC 74; 2007 (4) KLT 509; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 1334; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 21; 2007(12) SCALE 130; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 24

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 15, 16, 17, 18, 20 and 115 - Suit for recovery of money - Discretionary jurisdiction - Determination of - Held, no part of cause of action arose within jurisdiction of court of Subordinate Judge, Attingal where suit was filed by respondent - Transaction in question was carried out at Saudi Arabia - Court exercises discretionary jurisdiction u/s. 115 - Discretion must be exercised in accordance with law and not de hors same - Question in regard to jurisdiction is required to be determined with reference to date on which suit is filed and entertained and not with reference to a future date - Plaintiff is dominus litus, but he can file a suit only at one or other places specified in CPC, 1908 and not at any place where he desires - Application of doctrine of dominus litus has no application where s. 20 is to be invoked - At all material times, parties were at Saudi Arabia - Respondent has not questioned finding of High Court that no part of cause of action arose in Kerala - Cannot raise plea at this stage - Material date for purpose of invoking s. 20 is one of institution of suit and not subsequent change of residence - Change of residence subsequent to decision of court would not confer territorial jurisdiction in court which it did not have - Appeal allowed.
