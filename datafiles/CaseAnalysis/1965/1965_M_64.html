Mohammad Safi v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

25 March 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, A.K. Sarkar, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 384; AIR 1966 SC 69; 1966 CRLJ 75; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 467

Case Digest

Subject: Socio-Economic

Summary: Socio-Economic - Code of Criminal Procedure,1898, s. 403 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 409 - Appellant convicted u/s. 409 - Urged before High Court that upon same facts and with respect to same offence appellant was tried earlier by another Judge and was acquitted - He could, therefore, not have been tried over again in respect of that offence and consequently his conviction and sentence are illegal - Acquittal was ordered by Judge who did not have jurisdiction - Whether trial and conviction of appellant for offence u/s. 409 were barred by provisions of s.403? - Defence of auterfois acquit, has no application where accused person was not liable lawfully to be convicted at first trial because court lacked jurisdiction - For proceedings to amount to a trial they must be held before a court which is in fact competent to hold them and which is not of opinion that it has no jurisdiction to hold them - Held, fact that all witnesses for prosecution as well as for defence had been examined and fact that appellant was also examined u/s. 342 cannot in law be deemed to be a trial at all - Present trial and eventual conviction of appellant were valid in law - Appeal dismissed.
