State of Maharashtra v Sayed Mohammed Masood and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1104; (2009) 8 SCC 787; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 945; 2010 CRLJ 461; 2009 (4) JCC 3025; JT 2009 (10) SC 442; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 1057; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 177; 2009(10) SCALE 625; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 275

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Post-Dated Cheque, Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934 (As Amended up to 2000), Stop Payment

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.482 - Quashing of FIR - By High Court - On application moved by respondent no. 1 - Respondent no. 1, Chairman-cum-Managing Director of a company which floated a scheme - Agreement entered into by and between the complainant and the said Company under the scheme - Respondent no. 2/complainant lodged FIR alleging cheating on part of company - Application for quashing of FIR moved by respondent no. 1 allowed by HC - After perusing clauses in agreement entered into between respondent 2 and company, HC held that there was no intention to defraud respondent 2 and no offence u/s.406, 420 and 120B, IPC was made out - Quashing of FIR, whether proper? - Held, no - FIR perused, memo of appeal filed by State noted - Certain materials had surfaced during investigation pointing towards certain illegalities committed by company - Court observed that though there cannot be any doubt that a simple breach of contract or a case involving pure civil dispute would not attract the penal provisions contained in IPC either u/s.406 or s.420, but certain evidences that surfaced during investigation needed to be taken to a logical end - Appropriate directions issued - Impugned judgment unsustainable, set aside - Appeal allowed.
