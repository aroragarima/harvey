Steel Authority of India Limited v Madhusudan Das and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2098; (2008) 15 SCC 560; (2009) 2 SCC (L&S) 378; AIR 2009 SC 1153; 2009(2) ALL MR 977; 2009 (122) FLR 677; 2009 (1) JhrCR(SC) 171; 2009 (3) LLJ 54; 2008(15) SCALE 39; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 824

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Re Appointment, Industrial Disputes, Compassionate Ground

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 12, 14, 16, 309 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 108 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 12(3) - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 - Appointment - Compassionate Grounds - Sustainability - 'B' (deceased) was employee of Ores Mines, belonging to appellant and was on C-3 Shift duty and was asked to continue in morning duty - While working, he suddenly collapsed and was declared dead at spot - Respondent No. 1-son made representation for his appointment on compassionate ground and same was rejected - Respondent no. 1 filed writ petition - Single Judge dismissed writ petition on ground that it involved disputed questions of fact - Appeal preferred by respondent no. 1, DB held that respondent be appointed on compassionate grounds - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Whether 'B' died in an accident arising out of and in course of employment - Held, averments made in writ petition, therefore, did not suggest that any accident had taken place resulting in death of 'B' - It was, thus, not held that death occurred due to accident - It was not even case of respondent - What would constitute 'an accident arising out of and in course of employment' had not been defined and evidently, said phraseology had been borrowed from provisions of the 1923 Act - (B) Whether judgment of DB was sustainable - Held, DB in Court opinion, was not correct in its view - SC in large number of decisions had held that appointment on compassionate ground could not be claimed as a matter of right - It should be provided for in rules - Criteria laid down therefor, viz., that death of sole bread earner of family, should be established - It was meant to provide for a minimum relief when such contentions were raised, constitutional philosophy of equality behind making such a scheme be taken into consideration arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution mandate that all eligible candidates should be considered for appointment in the posts which have fallen vacant - Appointment on compassionate ground offered to dependent of deceased employee was an exception to the said rule - It might be that such a provision was made as measure of social benefit but it did not lay down a legal principle that the court should pass an order to that effect despite fact that conditions precedent therefor had not been satisfied - Impugned judgment could not be sustained which was set aside - Appeal allowed.
