Kasturi v Iyyamperumal and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, N. Santosh Hegde, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1041; (2005) 6 SCC 733; AIR 2005 SC 2813; 2005 (3) ALD(SC) 83; 2005 (60) ALR 487; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 182; JT 2005 (4) SC 565; 2005 (3) MahLJ 1; 2005 (3) MLJ(SC) 124; 2005 (3) MPLJ 261; 2005 (141) PLR 326; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 690; 2005(4) SCALE 363; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 864

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Jurisdiction Of Court

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 1, r. 10 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 19 - Whether in a suit for specific performance of contract for sale of a property instituted by a purchaser against vendor, a stranger or a third party to contract, claiming to have an independent title and possession over contracted property, is entitled to be added as a party/defendant in said suit? - Necessary parties are those persons in whose absence no decree can be passed by Court or that there must be a right to some relief against some party in respect of controversy involved in proceedings and proper parties are those whose presence before Court would be necessary in order to enable Court effectually and completely to adjudicate upon and settle all questions involved in suit although no relief in suit was claimed against such person - Held, stranger to contract, making claim independent and adverse to title of vendor are neither necessary nor proper parties, and therefore, not entitled to join as party defendants in suit for specific performance of contract for sale - Appeal allowed.
