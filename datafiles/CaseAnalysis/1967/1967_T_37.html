State of Madras v T. Narayanaswami Naidu and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 271; AIR 1968 SC 194; 1968 (1) MLJ(SC) 157; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 622; 1968 (21) STC 1

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Taxable Event, Declared Goods, Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, Last Purchase

Summary: Sales Tax - Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939 - A dealer in cotton and cotton seeds - Levy of tax fixed at point of last purchase - Denial of exemption in respect of goods lying in stock - Whether purchased goods lying in stock at end of assessment year can be taxed being 'last purchase'? - When he files a return and declares stock in hand, stock in hand cannot be said to have been acquired by last purchase because he may still during next assessment year, sell it or he may consume it himself or goods may be destroyed - Held, a dealer is not liable to pay a tax on purchases until purchases acquire quality of being last purchases inside State - Appeal dismissed.
