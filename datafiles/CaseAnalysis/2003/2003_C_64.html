State of U.P. and another v Chandrapal Singh and another

Supreme Court of India

12 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 284; (2003) SCC (L&S) 556; (2003) 4 SCC 670; AIR 2003 SC 4119; 2003 (5) AWC 4373; 2003 (2) CLR 297; 2003 (5) ESC 114; 2003 (97) FLR 602; JT 2003 (3) SC 533; 2003 (2) LLJ 744; 2003(3) SCALE 390; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 1062; 2003 (2) SLJ 217; 2003 (3) SLR 297; 2003 (4) SLT 257; 2003 (3) Supreme 346

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - order of dismissal - Constitution of India - Art. 311(1) - contention that the authority being lower in rank than the appointing authority could not have initiated the disciplinary proceedings - Held, though Article 311(1) provides that no person who is a member of civil services of the Union or State shall be dismissed or removed by an authority subordinate to that by which he was appointed, however, it does not in terms require that the authority, which is empowered under that provision to dismiss or remove an official, should itself initiate or conduct enquiry proceeding.
