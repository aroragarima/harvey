Rajendrakumar Natvarlal Shah v State of Gujarat and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 May 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 71; (1988) 3 SCC 153; (1988) SCC (Cr) 575; AIR 1988 SC 1255; [1991] 70 Comp Cas 549; 1988 (2) Crimes 729; 1988 CRLJ 1775; 1988 CrLR(SC) 476; JT 1988 (2) SC 409; 1988(1) SCALE 915; [1988] Supp1 S.C.R. 287

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Criminal

Summary: Human Rights - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Gujarat Prevention of Anti-social Activities Act, 1985, s. 3(2) - (A) Whether delay in making order could make subjective satisfaction resulting in detaining order improper? - Held, no - Even though there was no explanation for delay - It could not give rise to a legitimate inference that subjective satisfaction arrived at by District Magistrate was not genuine or that grounds were stale or illusory or that there was no rational connection between grounds and impugned order of detention - (B) Whether there was a non application of mind by detaining officer? - Further held, there was non application of mind - Could not be said that there was lack of awareness on part of District Magistrate in passing order of detention - Two criminal cases pending against detenu and also a recital of fact that he was continuing his business surreptitiously and he could not be caught easily - There was compelling necessity to detain him - Order of detention not vitiated on ground of non-application of mind - (C) Whether detaining authorities' order is proper with respect to reasons and language? - Further held, detention could not be said to be vitiated merely because some words were added by way of superscription by detaining authority which were wholly unnecessary - Better to avoid such unnecessary verbiage which are of little or no consequence - Appeal dismissed.
