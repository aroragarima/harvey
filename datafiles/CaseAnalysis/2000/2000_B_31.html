Bahadur Naik v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3104; (2000) 9 SCC 153; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1186; AIR 2000 SC 1582; 2000 CRLJ 2466; JT 2000 (6) SC 226; 2000 (3) RCR(Criminal) 217; 2000(4) SCALE 697

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34 - Murder - Conviction - Appellant was convicted for the offence u/s. 302/34 IPC by Trial Court and sentenced to undergo life imprisonment - Conviction and sentence was confirmed by the HC in appeal - Hence instant appeal -

Held, appellant has not been able to shake the credibility of the eye-witnesses. No material contradiction in the case of the prosecution has been shown to us. Non-examination of the IO as a witness is of no consequence. It has not been shown what prejudice has been caused to the appellant by such non-examination. From the evidence on record it stands proved that there was sufficient light at the place of occurrence to identify the appellant. In instant case, the deceased was given 5/6 dagger blows. In view of the evidence on record the contention for converting the sentence as aforesaid cannot be accepted. Appeal dismissed.
