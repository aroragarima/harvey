Gopaldas Udhavdas Ahuja And Anr v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 471; (2004) 7 SCC 33; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1830; AIR 2004 SC 3830; 2004 (190) CTR 1; 2004 (176) E.L.T. 3; [2004] 268 ITR 273; JT 2004 (5) SC 300; 2004(6) SCALE 141; [2004] Supp2 S.C.R. 942; [2004] 140 TAXMAN 115

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Telephone, Gold (Control) Act, 1968, Defence of India Rules, 1962, Defence of India Act, 1962, Opium Act, 1878, Voluntary Disclosure Of Income And Wealth Act, 1976, Wealth Act, 1976, Defence of India (Amendment) Rules, 1963

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Gold (Control) Act, 1968 - Income-Tax Act, 1961 - Customs Act, 1962 - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973, s. 19 - Wealth Act, 1976 - Defence of India Rules, 1962, r. 126H(1)(B) - Defence of India (Amendment) Rules, 1963 - Confiscation of primary gold and penalty - Conscious possession of gold - Income-tax department conducted search and recovered primary gold and other gold items - Validity of seizure of primary gold on 30/8/1974, by Gold Control Officer - Whether from circumstances one can say that appellants were in conscious possession of primary gold - Whether appellants herein entitled to claim immunity from confiscation of gold and imposition of penalty under Voluntary Disclosure of Income and Wealth Ordinance, 1975 - Held, Gold Control Officer acted within his authority when he took over gold from Income-tax department on 30/8/1974 - In absence of any evidence of recovery of primary gold from western bedroom and telephone room and in absence of any material to show that appellants had knowledge of gold hidden in ornamental top of cupboard in eastern bedroom, contravention of provisions of 1968 Act not established against appellants and consequently orders of confiscation and penalty not sustainable.
