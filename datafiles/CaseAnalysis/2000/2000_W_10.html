Union of India and Another v Wing Commander T. Parthasarathy

Supreme Court of India

10 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2786; (2001) 1 SCC 158; (2001) SCC (L&S) 180; AIR 2001 SC 158; AIR 2000 SCW 4031; 2001 (1) ALD(SC) 40; 2001 (1) AWC 289; 2001 (1) CLR 519; 2000 (87) FLR 892; JT 2000 (Supp2) SC 490; 2001 LabIC 53; 2001 (1) LLN 16; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 9; 2000(7) SCALE 442; 2001 (2) SLJ 195; 2000 (5) SLR 760; 2000 (8) SLT 217; [2000] Supp4 S.C.R. 531; 2000 (7) Supreme 459; 2001 (1) UC 164; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 529

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Defense & Security Forces - Pre-Mature Retirement - Cancellation - Entitlement - Respondent was constrained to seek for pre-mature retirement due to unavoidable problems in family - Therefore, respondent submitted application dt. 21-7-1985 praying for same w.e.f. 31-8-1986 with 6 months leave preparatory to retirement and furnished certificate stating that he was aware that any request made by him later for cancellation of his application for pre-mature retirement would not be accepted - Respondent submitted application seeking to withdraw application for pre-mature retirement from service which was not accepted on ground that Headquarters did not accede to requests for such cancellation after initial approval of same by RRM and having regard to certificate given by respondent himself which was turned down - Aggrieved respondent filed writ petition before HC to quash order of pre-mature retirement with effect from 31-8-86 and for consequential direction to continue respondent in service with all consequential and attendant benefits which was quashed - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before DB which upheld Single Judge's order - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by lower Courts was sustainable - Held, reliance placed on so-called policy decision which obligated respondent to furnish certificate to extent that he was fully aware of fact that he could not later seek for cancellation of application once made for pre-mature retirement - It would be destructive of right of respondent, in law, to withdraw his request for pre-mature retirement before it ever became operative and effective and effected termination of his status and relation with Dept. - When legal position was clear it would be futile for appellants to base their rights on some policy decision of Dept. or mere certificate of respondent being aware of particular position which had no sanctity or basis in law to destroy such rights which otherwise inhered in respondent and available in law - No such deprivation of substantive right of person could be denied except on basis of any statutory provision or rule or regulation - There being none brought to notice in said case, claim of appellant could not be countenanced in Court's hands - Even that apart, reasoning of HC that case of respondent would not be covered by type or nature of mischief sought to be curbed by so-called policy decision also could not be said to suffer any conformity in law, to warrant Court's interference - Hence, time limit stipulated by Single Judge to settle claims and consequential benefits due to respondent should commence and be computed for compliance - Appeal dismissed.
