Purewal Associates Limited and another v Collector of Central Excise

Supreme Court of India

01 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 159; (1996) 10 SCC 752; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 452; 1996 (67) ECR 225; 1996 (87) E.L.T. 321; JT 1996 (8) SC 725; 1996(7) SCALE 378; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 117; 1996 (8) Supreme 134

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 35L(B), Item 52, 68 - Whether goods in question in two appeals fell within ambit of nuts, bolts and screws under tariff item 52 or as residual under tariff item 68? - Words in entries have to be understood by meanings attached in trade or in common parlance and not by a hyper technical approach - Mere existence of threads would not render an article as a bolt, nut or screw if it is recognisable as component part of an instrument, apparatus, appliance or machine - Held, items in question in one of appeals did not perform ordinary function of fastening and were indeed recognizable as components of a watch - Appeal allowed.
