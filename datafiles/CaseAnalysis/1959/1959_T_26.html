Commissioner of Income Tax, Mysore, Travancore-Cochin and Coorg, Bangalore v Indo Mercantile Bank, Limited

Supreme Court of India

23 February 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 197; AIR 1959 SC 713; [1959] 36 ITR 1; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 256

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Travancore Income Tax Act, 1121

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s 3 - Travancore Income Tax Act, 1121, s 4 - Profits or Gains - Exemption - Assessment - Income tax return - Deduction - Appellants in both appeals is Commissioner of Income-tax and respondents are Bank and company - Assessee company filed its income tax return for assessment year - Income-tax Officer determined its assessable income to be Rs.90,947 representing only profit and refused deduction for losses incurred outside the State - Appeal to Commissioner unsuccessful - Appellate Tribunal found it was entitled to deduct the losses incurred outside the State from the profits accruing and arising in that State - HC slightly modified the decision - Hence instant appeal by Commissioner - Whether s.10 of Act distinguish between business in British India and business in an Indian State - Held, no - S.10 of Indian Act does not distinguish between business in British India and business in an Indian State or so divide business - Therefore to say that business in s.10 means business in British India or business profits or gains of which are taxable in British India is to ignore definitions and ss.3, 4 and 6 - As profits or gains of business in an Indian State were exempted from payment of tax in British India business in s. 10 of Act must mean business in British India - Appeal dismissed.
