State of Bombay v Narul Latif Khan

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Raghubar Dayal, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 104; AIR 1966 SC 269; 1966 (2) LLJ 595; 1966 MahLJ 46; 1965 MPLJ 941; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 135

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Public Servants Inquiries Act, 1850 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(2) - Civil Services Regulations, art. 353 - Whether reasonable opportunity was given to respondent, to defend himself before passing final order of compulsorily retiring him u/art. 353 ? - If charge-sheeted officer starts cross- Examining departmental witnesses in an irrelevant manner, such cross-examination can be checked and controlled - It would be impossible to accept argument that if charge-sheeted officer wants to lead oral evidence, enquiry officer can say that having regard to charges framed against officer he would not hold any oral enquiry - Witnesses whom respondent wanted to examine would undoubtedly have given relevant evidence - Held, respondent wanted to examine his doctors, and though he intimated that he desired to examine his doctors, but he was not given an opportunity to do so - That, introduces a fatal infirmity in whole enquiry which means that respondent has not been given a reasonable opportunity to defend himself within meaning of art. 311(2) - Final order was defective inasmuch as it did not appear that appellant had taken into account representation made by respondent - Appeal dismissed.
