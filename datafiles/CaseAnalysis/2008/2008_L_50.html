Gautam Sarup v Leela Jetly and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2615; (2008) 7 SCC 85; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 363; 2008 (3) AWC 2844; 2008 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2141; 2008 (4) MLJ(SC) 468; 2008 (4) MPLJ 113; 2008 (1) RCR(Rent) 487; 2008(4) SCALE 459; [2008] 4 S.C.R. 523; 2008 (1) UC 613

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Family & Personal - Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6 r. 17 - Amendment of written statement - Sustainability - Testator executed a Will wherein he bequeathed all his properties in favour of appellant and 7th respondent/granddaughter - Appellant filed a suit for declaration of his title to the bequeathed property and for a decree of permanent injunction - Respondent Nos. 1 to 5 filed written statement affirmed the execution of the Will in favour of appellant - 6th respondent filed a separate written statement through another advocate, however, subsequently, 6th respondent filed a separate written statement denying and disputing the claim of appellants in toto and sought the permission to retract the earlier written statement on the ground that counsel forged her signature on earlier written statement - Trial Court permitted 6th respondent to retract earlier written statement - Appellant challenged Trial Court order before HC - HC set aside Trial Court order and directed an enquiry - Trial Court, in enquiry, found that concerned counsel had filed the earlier written statement only after the instruction from 6th respondent - 6th respondent, subsequently, sought amendment of her earlier written statement - Trial Court allowed amendment - Appellant challenged Trial Court's order permitting the amendment in a revision petition - Appellant's revision was dismissed by HC - Held, 6th respondent accepted the case of the appellant in its entirety and it went to the extent of accepting the plea of the appellant that his suit, claiming half share in the property left by the testator, may be decreed - Each and every contention of the appellant was accepted by 6th respondent in her earlier written statement - Trial Court was bound to determine the issue in regard to the validity of the Will in question, but such an issue has not been and cannot be raised at the instance of 6th respondent - Therefore, impugned HC judgment cannot be sustained in the facts and circumstances of the case - Appeal allowed.
