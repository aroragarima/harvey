Dr. T. S. Subramanian (Deceased) By Lrs v Andhra Bank Limited

Supreme Court of India

29 March 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, L.M. Sharma, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 540; (1989) Supp2 SCC 252; AIR 1989 SC 1420; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 58; 1989(1) SCALE 747; 1989 (1) UJ 638

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Tamil Nadu Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1960, ss. 10(3)(a)(iii) and 10(3)(c) - Suit for eviction - On the ground of splitting up of tenancy - Legality of - Whether court was right in considering eviction as amounting to partial eviction or that petition amounts to splitting up of tenancy? - Held, where bona fide need for a substantial portion of leased premises is established, and such need respecting a small portion is not shown, decree for possession of entire premises may have to passed, if provisions of statutes do not contemplate or enable a splitting up - A landlord who is occupying only a part of a building, whether residential or non-residential, may, notwithstanding anything contained in cl. (a) of s. 10, apply to Controller for an order directing any tenant occupying whole or any portion of remaining part of building to put landlord in possessing thereof, if he requires additional accommodation for residential purposes or for purposes of a business which he is carrying on, as case may be - It, therefore, follows that once a landlord is able to satisfy Controller that he is bona fide in need of additional accommodation for residential or non-residential purposes and that advantage derived by him by an order of eviction will outweigh hardship caused to tenant, then he is entitled to an order of eviction irrespective of any other consideration - This concludes matter in favour of landlord on point - It has to be held that second ground on which High Court declined relief cannot be supported - Appeal disposed of.
