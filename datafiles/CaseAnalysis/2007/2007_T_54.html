Gomti v Thakurdas and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 387; (2007) 11 SCC 160; 2007 (3) ALJ 614; 2007 (3) ALT 40; 2007 CRLJ 2431; 2007 (2) RCR(Criminal) 714; 2007(5) SCALE 655; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 90; 2007 (4) UC 241

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Human Rights

Keywords: Co- Accused, Arms Act, 1954

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 302, 201 , 120(B), 323; Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes Prevention of Atrocities Act; Arms Act, 1954, ss. 3(2), (5), 25 - Appeal by the complainant alleging that her husband has been killed by the respondents 1 to 5 - Respondents not entitled to bail - Trial for alleged commission of offences punishable under ss. 147, 148, 149, 302, 201 , 120(B) and 323 of the IPC and under ss.3(2) and (5) of the SCST Act and ss. 3(2) and (5) of the Arms Act - Accused found guilty - imprisonment for life - Convicted in terms of s.302 read with s. 149 IPC, and other sentences in respect of ss.148, 201 and ss.3(2) and 5 of the SCST Act - Acquitted of the charges relatable to s.25 of the Arms Act and s.120 B IPC - Other co- accused persons already been released on bail - Correctness of the orders has been questioned-No reason indicated except stating that the accused appellants were on bail during trial - The reasons indicated have to be germane to justify grant of bail - Factors which have weighted with the High Court are irrelevant and show non-application of mind - Grant of bail being discretionary, the High Court was justified in taking note of the relevant factors and granting bail - Reason for directing suspension of sentence and grant of bail is the absence of allegation of misuse of liberty during the earlier period when the accused- respondents were on bail - The effect of bail granted during trial loses significance when on completion of trial, the accused persons have been found guilty - Order directing suspension of sentence and grant of bail is clearly unsustainable and is set aside.
