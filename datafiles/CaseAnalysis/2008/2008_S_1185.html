Sharon Michael and Others v State of Tamil Nadu and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1996; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 103; (2009) 3 SCC 375; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 688; 2009 (1) JCC 640; JT 2008 (13) SC 551; 2009 (3) MLJ(Crl) 102; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 759; 2009(1) SCALE 627; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 1412

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Notice To Respondent, Malicious Prosecution

Summary: IPC, 1860, ss. 120-B, 409 and 420 - Applications for quashing the summons issued dismissed - Appeal against - Held, there is nothing to show that the appellants herein who hold different positions in the appellant-company made any representation in their personal capacities and, thus, they cannot be made vicariously liable only because they are employees of the company - Liability of the company is, therefore, a civil liability - Respondent No.2 was the supplier of garments which were found out to be defective in nature, dispute between the parties is civil in nature - Summons issued to the appellants are quashed - Appeal allowed.
