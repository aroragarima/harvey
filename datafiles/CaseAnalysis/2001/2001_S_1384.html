Hirachand Srinivas Managaonkar v Sunanda

Supreme Court of India

20 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20230; (2001) 4 SCC 125; AIR 2001 SC 1285; 2001 (2) ALD(SC) 117; 2001 (43) ALR 314; 2001 (2) AWC 1259; 2001 (3) Bom.C.R. 43; JT 2001 (3) SC 620; 2001 (2) RCR(Civil) 522; 2001(2) SCALE 514; 2001 (2) SCJ 412; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 491; 2001 (2) SLT 783; 2001 (2) Supreme 435; 2001 (1) UC 564

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, ss. 10, 13(1 - A)(i) and 23(1)(a) - Petition by wife - judicial separation - Adultery - Court ordered maintenance for wife and daughter - Order not complied by husband - Husband's petition for divorce - Whether husband who filed a petition seeking dissolution of marriage by a decree of divorce can be declined relief on ground that he has failed to pay maintenance to his wife and daughter despite an order of court? - Held, subsection (1-A) added to s. 13 by act 1964 states that a petition for divorce could be filed by either party to marriage irrespective of whether party presenting petition for divorce is a decree-holder or a judgment-debtor under decree for judicial separation or decree for restitution of conjugal rights - Object of subsection (1-A) was merely to enlarge right to apply for divorce and not to make it compulsive that a petition for divorce presented under subsection (1-A) must be allowed on a mere proof that there was no cohabitation or restitution for requisite period - Very language of s. 23 shows that it governs every proceeding under Act and a duty is cast on court to decree relief sought only if conditions mentioned in sub-section are satisfied, and not otherwise - Therefore, contention raised by learned counsel for appellant that provisions of s. 23(1) are not relevant in deciding a petition filed under subsection (1-A) of s. 13 of Act, cannot be accepted - After decree for judicial separation was passed on petition filed by wife it was duty of both spouses to do their part for cohabitation - Husband was expected to act as a dutiful husband towards wife and wife was to act as a devoted wife towards husband - If this concept of both spouses making sincere contribution for purpose of successful cohabitation after a judicial separation is ordered then it can reasonably be said that in facts and circumstances of case husband in refusing to pay maintenance to wife failed to act as a husband - Thereby he committed a wrong within meaning of s. 23 of Act - Therefore, High Court was justified in declining to allow prayer of husband for dissolution of marriage by divorce u/s. 13(1 - A) of Act - Appeal dismissed.
