Vikram Dhillon v State Of Haryana & Orsz

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Y.K. Sabharwal, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 22; (2007) 9 SCC 408; AIR 2007 SC 1067; 2007 (1) ESC 71; JT 2007 (2) SC 250; 2007(2) SCALE 67; 2007 (7) SCJ 254; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 297; 2007 (2) SLR 188; 2007 (2) UPLBEC 1121

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Education; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Education - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Admission - Denial - Compensation - Entitlement - Petitioner secured rank No. 418 in the Common Entrance Examination for medical courses and sought BDS course in 2nd respondent college in Open Category on third counselling - Petitioner was informed that no seat was available in 2nd respondent college in the Open Category , however, he came to know that the 6th respondent who secured rank No. 442 had been granted admission to the BDS course in 2nd respondent college - Petitioner filed representation to respondent authorities - Since there was no reply from the respondents, petitioner filed the instant writ petition - Whether the petitioner was entitled to compensation in the instant case - Held, no vacant seat was available in the 2nd respondent college till 30-9-2004, however, three vacancies arose thereafter due to default in paying the tuition fee of some candidates - 9th respondent Director of Medical Education filled up the said vacancies from the eligible candidates who were present on that day at the venue and hence, 6th respondent who was at rank No. 442, got the admission - On 30-9-2004, petitioner was not present and thereby admission was granted to 6th respondent - Further, petitioner made the first complaint as late as on 19-10-2004 by stating that he had come to know about the illegality of admission in favour of 6th respondent only on 17-10- 2004 - SC, in exercise of extraordinary powers under art. 32 of the Constitution, would not be appropriate to award compensation to the petitioner either from the authorities or from the 9th respondent Director in his personal capacity - Petition dismissed.
