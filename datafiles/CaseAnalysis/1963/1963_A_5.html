Chandi Kumar Das Karmarkar and And Others v Abanidhar Roy

Supreme Court of India

09 October 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 411; AIR 1965 SC 585; 1965 CRLJ 496

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 447, 379 and 504 - Appellants convicted u/s. 379 by High Court after reversing their acquittal by Addl. Sessions Judge - Charge against them was that they committed theft of fish by fishing in a tank which was in possession of respondent - Challenged - Offence of theft consists in dishonest taking of any moveable property out of possession of another without his consent - There can thus be theft if fish from a tank which belongs to another and is in his possession, if offender catches them without consent of owner and without any bona fide claim of right - Held, all embellishments in case about unlawful assembly, riot, force and threats have not been believed and catching of fish in this big tank with nets was done quite openly - There was an absence of animus furandi and circumstances bring this case within rule that where taking of moveable property is in assertion of a bond fide claim of right, act, though it may amount to a civil injury, does not fall within offence of theft - Acquittal of appellants ought not to have been set aside - Appeal allowed.
