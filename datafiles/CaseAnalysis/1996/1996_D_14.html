State of Karnataka and Others v D.C. Nanjudaiah and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2351; (1996) 10 SCC 619; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 679; 1996 (4) CCC 172; 1996 (4) CLT 123; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 471; 1996(7) SCALE 117; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 222; 1996 (8) Supreme 189

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1) and 6 - Declaration u/s. 6 was published, within three years, but conduct of enquiry was found fault with and it requires to be quashed - If it is quashed, necessarily an enquiry u/s. 5-A has to be conducted - Limitation, therefore, of conducting enquiry and publication of declaration within three years would start running from date of receipt of order of High Court and not from date on which original publication u/s. 4(1) came to be made - Held, appeals are to be allowed and declaration has to be quashed - Accordingly, declaration is quashed - Appellant is permitted to conduct an enquiry within a period of four months from date of receipt of this order and have declaration published within one month thereafter - Appeal allowed.
