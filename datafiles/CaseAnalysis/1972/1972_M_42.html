State of Maharashtra v Manglya Dhavu Kongil

Supreme Court of India

07 January 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

D.G. Palekar, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 710; (1972) 3 SCC 46; (1972) SCC (Cr) 237; AIR 1972 SC 1797; 1972 CRLJ 570; 1972 UJ 508

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 45 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302,376 and 511 - Code of Criminal Procedure,1898, s. 174 -Murder with attempt to commit rape - Death / life sentence - Appreciation of evidence - Post-mortem report concluded that death was caused by 'asphyxia due to throttling' - (A)Whether trial judge was justified in convicting accused? - Held, judgment of Trial Court was justified but death sentence to be commuted to life imprisonment as gap was too long - (B) Whether High Court was justified in instant case in drawing its own medical conclusions as to evidence? - Further held, where post-mortem report made on prescribed printed form definitely expresses opinion that death was caused by 'asphyxia due to throttling' and opinion is supported by evidence of expert, HC in appeal is not justified in circumstances of case in drawing on its own medical knowledge that asphyxia is also possible in case of poisoning and that possibility had not been completely eliminated by medical evidence including post-mortem report - There was absolutely no basis for it in evidence on record - Appeal allowed.
