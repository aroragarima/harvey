A. Raghu S/o Rajaiah and another v Government of Andhra Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

26 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 221; 2015 (4) ALD(SC) 7; 2015 (4) ALT(SC) 18; 2015(4) SCALE 220; 2016 (3) SLR 242

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Inter Se Seniority, Andhra Pradesh Police (Civil Police) Subordinate Service Rules, 1999

Summary: (A) Service - Andhra Pradesh Police (Civil Police) Subordinate Service Rules, 1999, r. 15 - Seniority List - Entitlement - Recruitment Board issued notification for filling up existing posts of Sub-Inspector of Police by way of direct recruitment - Lists of provisionally selected candidates were prepared on the basis of their inter se merit in the selection process - Tribunal directed the authorities to recalculate the vacancies under the PE, PM and SP quotas - All the 58 selected candidates except one Munuswamy, successfully completed their training - Competent authority issued a seniority list of Sub-Inspectors of Police - Impugned seniority list did not include the name of Munuswamy, who did not complete the training, few candidates who were selected under the PE, PM and SP quotas and candidates who had failed to join the police training college - Whether Munuswamy would be entitled to be included in the impugned seniority list.

Held, Munuswamy, must figure in the seniority list along with those deputed for training on 15.7.1991, for the simple reason, that his name existed in the list of names deputed for training. There can, therefore be no doubt whatsoever, that insofar as Munuswamy is concerned, even though he completed his training in the course which commenced from 14.6.1992, his position in the inter se seniority list was bound to be reflected alongwith those with whom he was deputed for training, according to the aggregate of marks obtained by him, on the completion of his training at the police training college, in terms of r.15 of the Rules. Appeals dismissed.

(B) Service - Andhra Pradesh Police (Civil Police) Subordinate Service Rules, 1999, r. 15 - Seniority List - Entitlement - Whether candidates selected under the PE, PM and SP quotas would be entitled to be included in the impugned seniority list.

Held, if quotas been correctly determined by the State Government, these candidates would have been originally appointed along with others. In the above eventuality, they would have been deputed for training in the very first instance on 15.7.1991. For no fault of theirs, despite their selection in the same recruitment process, which was conducted in furtherance of the notification dated 22.1.1991, they were deputed for training on 14.6.1992. The delay in deputing the candidates belonging to PE, PM and SP quotas, squarely falls on the appointing authority, and not on the candidates who were subsequently deputed for training from the PE, PM and SP quotas.Thus, candidates deputed for training against the PE, PM and SP quotas on 14.6.1992, being not in any manner responsible for their not having been deputed along with the originally selected candidates on 15.7.1991, are liable to a fixation in the seniority list. Appeals dismissed.

(C) Service - Andhra Pradesh Police (Civil Police) Subordinate Service Rules, 1999, r. 15 - Seniority List - Entitlement - Whether candidates who had failed to join the police training college would be entitled to be included in the impugned seniority list.

Held, all the provisionally selected candidates did not join the police training college(s) for the said training, and in such case, it was open to the appointing authority to depute further candidates for training, out of those whose names fell immediately below the names of candidates deputed for training - Competent authority, however, delayed in deputing the names of these candidates. It eventually deputed these candidates for training on 14.6.1992. it is not possible for us to accept, that the candidates, who were deputed for training on 15.7.1991, and those deputed for training on 14.6.1992 to fulfill the deficiency, can be described as two different batches. Appeals dismissed.
