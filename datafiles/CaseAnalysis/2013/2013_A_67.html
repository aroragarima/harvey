Gothamchand Jain v Arumugam @ Tamilarasan

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Radhakrishnan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 618; (2013) 10 SCC 472; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 463; 2013 (101) ALR 241; 2013 (7) MLJ 199; 2014 (1) RCR(Civil) 295; 2013(11) SCALE 580; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 181

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Portuguese Civil Code, Pondicherry (Extension of Laws) Act, 1968, Pondicherry (Administration) Act, 1962, Pondicherry (Laws) Regulation, 1963, Constitution (Fourteenth Amendment) Act, 1962, Pondicherry (Alteration of name) Act, 2006, Indian Limitation Act, 1956

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963 - Pondicherry (Extension of Laws) Act, 1968 - Applicability of the provisions of the Indian Limitation Act - Appellant preferred a suit which was resisted on the ground of limitation, which was ultimately decreed in favour of the respondent - Trial Court held that art. 2262 of French Code Civil shows that the limitation for original cause of action was 30 years and it was a well settled law that the said provision was applicable to the Union Territory-Pondicherry - Accordingly, suit claim was not time barred - On appeal, Additional District Judge confirmed the order of the Trial Court - HC allowed the appeal filed against said order and held that it was art. 54 of the 1963 Act that would apply in the matter of filing of the suit in Pondicherry and not art. 2262 of the French Code Civil - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the French Law of Limitation which had been in force till 1-1-1964, was in any manner repealed or modified by the 1963 Act - Held, 1968 Act, as amended, had adopted several such legislations in the State of Pondicherry, but the Act which governs limitation was the general law of the land that was the 1963 Act - Consequently, it was not art. 2262 of the French Code Civil that applies to the suit in question, but s. 54 of the 1963 Act - Under such circumstances, as rightly held by the HC, the suit filed beyond the period of limitation prescribed u/art. 54 of the 1963 Act was clearly barred - Appeal dismissed.
