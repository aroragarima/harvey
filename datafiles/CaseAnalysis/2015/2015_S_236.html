State of Haryana v Asha Devi and another

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 341; (2015) 8 SCC 39; AIR 2015 SC 3189; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 3266; 2015 CRLJ 3406; 2015 (3) JCC (Narcotics) 123; 2015 (3) MLJ(Crl) 119; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 1018; 2015(6) SCALE 258; 2016(6) SCALE 258; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 348

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Prosecution Evidence

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, s. 20 - Possession of Illicit drugs - Acquittal - Justification - On day of incident, police inspector received information that respondent deal in buying and selling of drugs in heavy quantity - Raid was conducted and drug in heavy quantity was sized by police from respondent's house - Samples along with residue and specimen seal impressions were taken into possession by police under recovery memo which was prepared by I.O. and witnessed by ASI and ASI and attested by DSP and thumb mark of respondent - Further, case property along with samples and witnesses were produced before Station House Officer, who after verifying facts affixed his seal thereon and were deposited in Police Malkhana - Case was registered against accused respondent wife u/s. 20(61) of Act and she was arrested - Due procedure was followed by Magistrate - However, Trial Court did not find favour with prosecution version as according to it, on receiving secret information, Sub Inspector did not join any independent witness during investigation of case despite fact that they were available at spot - Further, Trial Court held that respondent accused could not have fled in presence of five police officers; and link evidence of possession of seal "RP" transferring from ASI to I.O. is not proved, hence, respondents were acquitted - HC confirmed order passed by Trial Court and refused to grant leave to State to appeal against acquittal of respondents - Hence, present appeal.

Held, both DSP and I.O. have deposed that public persons were available when contraband was seized; however, none of public person acceded to their request of joining investigation as independent witness. Courts below have found it unbelievable but no reason for same is rendered. However, SC opined that consistent statement of both DSP as well as I.O. rather enhances veracity of circumstances as put forth by them. Further, with respect respondent who escaped, SC held that Courts below have proceeded on assumption and conjecture. There is nothing in evidence which could show that respondent could not have run away. There are positive statements by several prosecution witnesses that he ran away on seeing police party and these statements have withstood test of cross examination as well. Further, no other evidence was led to disprove fact of running away of accused respondent. Further, with respect to discrepancy in sealing of samples, SC held that there is clear evidence that initially samples were taken and sealed with 'RP' and 'MS' on 3-2-2006 at place of seizure and thereafter, on same day, SHO also sealed said samples with 'SS'. Further, samples were produced before Judicial Magistrate, where seal of one sample was broken and resealed with 'RP'. Thereafter, sample was deposited in Judicial Malkhana from where it was sent to FSL. FSL report notes that seal was intact and sample was un-tampered. All persons who possessed contraband sample have been brought on record to support that no tampering was done with samples. Defence failed to bring out anything in cross-examination of witnesses with respect to tampering of samples. Hence, SC held that samples were properly dealt with throughout and same was found to be Ganja. Further, with respect to seal that was handed over to ASI, defence failed to cross-examine I.O. as to how did he got possession of seal back from ASI Hence, SC failed to believe that prosecution was duty bound to explain movement of seal from one person to another in given circumstances. Since, movement of sample has been proved and found to be regular, prosecution has sufficiently proved its case to establish guilt of accused. SC held accused respondent guilty u/s. 20 of Act for possession of drugs and convicted them. Impugned judgment passed by lower court is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Investigation and procedure to carry samples cannot be challenged when same is in accordance with law.
