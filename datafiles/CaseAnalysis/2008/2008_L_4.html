Fcs Software Solutions Limited v La Medical Devices Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1398; (2008) 10 SCC 440; AIR 2008 SC 3137; [2008] 144 Comp Cas 391; JT 2008 (7) SC 499; 2008 (6) MLJ(SC) 655; 2008(10) SCALE 7; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 479

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Corporate - Indian Companies Act, 1956 - Auction - Procedural legality of - Whether the Company Judge is right in ordering fresh auction? - Held, though bid of appellant was highest and was accepted by official liquidator - Certain facts which were necessary to be brought to notice of intending purchasers were not set out in proclamation of sale nor were disclosed at time of sale notice - Those related to valuation of movable and immovable properties, fixation of reserve price, non-inventory of plant and machinery - Company Judge considered objections raised by other bidders and having been prima facie satisfied, ordered fresh auction - There was no illegality in approach - Fresh bids were received - Highest offer was of respondent No. 3 - Company Judge extended an opportunity to appellant to raise its bid, an offer which appellant declined on ground that his offer was highest and all proceedings taken by official liquidator and Company Judge thereafter were totally illegal and unlawful - Approach of Company Judge should be to get highest price so as to satisfy maximum claims against Company in liquidation - Procedure followed by Company Judge cannot be said to be illegal - Appeal partly allowed.
