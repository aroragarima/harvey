Shamim Akhtar v Iqbal Ahmad and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 October 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2647; (2000) 8 SCC 123; AIR 2001 SC 1; 2000 (42) ALR 131; 2001 (1) AWC 54; JT 2000 (Supp1) SC 606; 2000 (2) RCR(Rent) 509; 2000(7) SCALE 197; 2000 (7) SLT 549; 2000 (7) Supreme 153; 2001 (1) UC 107

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950, Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, Enemy Property Act, 1968

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution - Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972 - Enemy Property Act, 1968 - Evacuee Property Act, 1950 - Provincial Small Cause Courts Act, 1887 - Proceeding for eviction of tenant under Act, 1972 - Appellant claiming to be landlady of house filed a petition u/s. 20 of 1972 Act, for eviction of respondent 1-tenant from house, on ground that he had denied title of landlady and had defaulted in payment of rent - Held, in view of finding recorded by Trial Court, that appellant was owner of suit house and she had inducted respondent 1 as a tenant in house - Respondent tenant having denied title of landlady was liable to be evicted u/s. 20(2)(f) of Act - Appeal allowed.
