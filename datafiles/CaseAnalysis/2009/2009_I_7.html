Geo-Group Communications Inc v IOL Broadband Limited

Supreme Court of India

17 November 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2112; (2010) 1 SCC 562; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 271; 2010(3) ALL MR 958; 2010 (2) AWC 1591; 2010 (1) BC 13; 2010 (3) Bom.C.R. 708; JT 2009 (14) SC 543; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 906; 2009(14) SCALE 75

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 11(6) - Appointment of arbitrator - Applicant entered into a Share Subscription and Shareholders Agreement (SHA) with 'E' - Agreement provided that in lieu of supply of CISCO equipments, 'E' would issue equity shares to the applicant - As per the arrangements made between the parties under the agreement, 'E' issued 6920 equity shares to the applicant; but 'E' issued a Xerox copy of Share Certificate - In the year 2007, 'E' entered into a Scheme of Amalgamation (Scheme) with the respondent-company - Scheme of Amalgamation was approved by HC - Liabilities, duties, obligations and guarantees of the 'E' Company under SHA stood transferred to the respondent-company - However, shares to which the applicant was entitled to, were never issued or allotted to it by the respondent-company - Applicant made various requests and representations to the respondent-company but to no avail - Therefore, applicant invoked arbitration clause No. 11.7, contained in the SHA and called upon the respondent-company to resolve the disputes by concurring on the appointment of Sole Arbitrator - As respondent-company disputed the claim of the applicant and did not concur in the appointment of the Sole Arbitrator, the applicant filed the instant application - (A) It was contended that Arbitration Agreement was not duly stamped and, therefore, the application should be dismissed - Held, in the present case, Arbitration Agreement is in the form of an arbitration clause, i.e., Clause No.11.7 incorporated in the SHA - Law does not provide that arbitration clause incorporated in a contract should be stamped - Therefore, the plea that Arbitration Agreement is not stamped and therefore the applicant is not entitled to relief, cannot be accepted - (B) Whether a valid arbitration agreement exists between the parties? - Held, respondent-company is successor-in-interest of 'E' - After amalgamation of 'E', with the respondent, all the liabilities and obligations of 'E', including those mentioned in SHA stood transferred, in law, to the respondent-company - Therefore, Clause No. 11.7 of SHA is applicable to the respondent-company in the same manner as it was applicable to 'E' - On the facts of the case, it is held that there exists a valid arbitration agreement between the parties - Grievance of the applicant relates to non-payment of consideration for supply of equipment to 'E' under SHA - Further dispute raised by the applicant relates to non-issuance of shares by the respondent in terms of Amalgamation Scheme entered into between 'E' and the respondent-company - Thus the disputes are live and surviving - (C) Whether conditions for the exercise of power u/s. 11(6) fulfilled? - Held, procedure for appointment of arbitrator was agreed upon between the parties in Clause 11.7 of SHA - Respondent failed to act as required under that procedure in spite of service of notice - Agreement on the appointment procedure does not provide other means for securing the appointment of a Sole Arbitrator - Therefore, the conditions precedent set out in s. 11(6) of the Act are completely satisfied and the applicant is entitled to approach the Court for securing appointment of Sole Arbitrator for resolution of the disputes, which have arisen between the parties - Arbitrator appointed - Application allowed.
