Singer India Limited v Chander Mohan Chadha & Ors

Supreme Court of India

13 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 673; (2004) 7 SCC 1; AIR 2004 SC 4368; [2004] 122 Comp Cas 468; 2004 (4) CompLJ 413; 2004 (113) DLT 80; JT 2004 (8) SC 396; 2005 (1) MLJ(SC) 184; 2004 (138) PLR 618; 2004 (2) RCR(Rent) 271; 2004(6) SCALE 617; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 535

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Corporate - Delhi Rent Control Act, s. 14(b) - Eviction petition, on ground of sub-tenancy without written consent - Tenant initially being an American Company - Subsequently pursuant to scheme of amalgamation tenant was an Indian Company - (A) Effect of parting of possession of the tenanted premises as a result of sanction of scheme of amalgamation of companies u/s. 394 of the Companies Act by the High Court - Precedents referred to - Principle of law is, even if there is an order of a Court sanctioning the scheme of amalgamation u/s. 391 and 394 of the Companies Act whereunder the leases, rights of tenancy or occupancy of the transferor company get vested in and become the property of the transferee company, it would make no difference in so far as the applicability of s. 14(1)(b) is concerned, as the Act does not make any exception in favour of a lessee who may have adopted such a course of action in order to secure compliance of law - (B) Whether it is open to the company itself to efface itself or lift its own corporate veil so as to examine as to who are the directors and shareholders and who are in reality controlling the affairs of the Company? - No - (A) Held, it is not possible to hold that it is the American Company which is still in existence and is in possession of the premises in question - On the contrary, the inescapable conclusion is that it is the Indian Company which is in occupation and is carrying on business in the premises in question rendering the appellant liable for eviction - Appeals dismissed.
