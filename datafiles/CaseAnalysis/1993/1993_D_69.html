State of Maharashtra v Dr. Budhikota Subbarao

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1787; (1993) 3 SCC 339; (1993) SCC (Cr) 901; 1993 (3) Bom.C.R. 27; 1993 (2) CCR 123; JT 1993 (3) SC 379; 1993(2) SCALE 36; [1993] 2 S.C.R. 311; 1993 (1) UJ 753

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Atomic Energy Act, 1962 - Official Secrets Act, 1993 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Ex-Naval captain - Sanction - Accused found guilty of violating provisions of the 1993 Act and the 1962 Act - Complaint was filed against him before Metropolitan Magistrate - Effort was made by accused to assail framing of charge - Trial Judge turned down the plea and fixed date for framing charge - Revision, against order, was dismissed by HC - Challenged by way of special leave petition - It was permitted to be withdrawn - In revision filed by State, HC decided questions in favour of accused - Hence, instant appeal - Whether absence of sanction by appropriate authority u/s. 197 CrPC for prosecuting a retired public servant, vitiated the proceedings - Held, lower courts did not commit any error of law in concluding that entire proceedings were vitiated as no cognizance of offences could have been taken against accused without complying with provisions of s. 197 of CrPC - Since appeal failed for non-compliance with s. 197 of CrPC, order discharging accused had to be upheld - Since respondent was discharged for failure of mandatory requirement yet State went on filing revision before HC and appeal before SC and keeping in mind mental sufferings and financial loss caused to respondent, he was entitled to costs - Appeal dismissed.
