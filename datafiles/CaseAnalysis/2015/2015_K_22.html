K.R.C.D. (India) Private Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, Mumbai

Supreme Court of India

23 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 291; (2015) 6 SCC 397; 2015 (319) E.L.T. 364; JT 2015 (6) SC 48; 2015 (4) MLJ 610; 2015(5) SCALE 463; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 211

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Assessable Value, Central Excise Valuation (Determination of Price of Excisable Goods) Rules, 2000

Summary: Excise - Job Work - Imposition of royalty charges - Justification - Appellant started manufacturing duplicate CDs from master tape/CD issued to them by distributor who had copyright in contents of CD - Further, appellant assessee is only given master CD from which it duplicates such master tape/CD on blank CDs that are owned by it and then sold to distributor copyright holder, having paid lump sum royalty to producer of music which is on CD - Excise Authority demanded duty inter alia on royalty charges incurred by distributor/copyright holder - Lower authority issued show cause demanding differential duty which consisted of royalty payable to distributor/copyright holder - Order was confirmed and penalty was imposed on appellant assessee - Further, appellant authority held that royalty charges incurred by distributor/copyright holder is liable to be included in assessable value of CDs and hence to be paid by appellant - Appellant contended that job work done by appellant did not include any element of royalty - Hence, present appeal.

Held, master tape contains within it music/picture in digital form and there is no doubt whatsoever that music/picture supplied on master tape ought to be valued and has been valued as additional consideration that flowed from buyer to assessee, and its value has been accepted at rupee one per CD. Further, entirety of duplicate CDs is sold only to distributor who is copyright holder. Hence, copyright value in duplicate CD is not used in connection with sale of such goods inasmuch as no part of copyright which may have been passed on by distributor to assessee is used by assessee in selling duplicate CDs to distributor who is himself owner of copyright. Hence, on assumption that music/picture embedded in master tape is inextricably bound with copyright thereof, copyright is not used by appellant while selling duplicate CDs to distributor. Further, distributor having paid lump sum royalty to producer of music, then sells, after job work done by appellant, duplicate CDs in market with cost of royalty loaded thereon. Further, SC held that on assumption that music/picture component is art work in master CD, that alone is to be taken into account as it is necessary for production of duplicate CDs. Royalty payable for such music/picture cannot extend to art work that is necessary for production of duplicate CDs, as no part of it is in fact taken into account by either distributor who is copyright holder or appellant in job work done by appellant. Hence, no part of royalty can be loaded on to duplicate CDs produced by appellant. Impugned order passed by lower Court is set aside and refund, if any, to be made of additional duty collected pursuant to impugned judgment may be claimed by appellant in accordance with law. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Assessee who is conducting job work on behalf of distributor would not be liable to pay royalty charges which is been paid by distributor copyright holder.
