Supreme Court of India

28 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 211; (2003) 4 SCC 481; AIR 2003 SC 1987; 2003 (5) AWC 3596; 2003 (103) DLT 673; JT 2003 (5) SC 160; 2003 (1) KHCACJ 653; 2003 (2) RCR(Civil) 212; 2003(2) SCALE 509; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 424; 2003 (2) SLT 646; 2003 (Supp) RD 529; 2003 (2) Supreme 893; 2003 (2) UPLBEC 1718

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - valuation of lands - held, while determining the market value of the land acquired it has to be correctly determined and paid so that there is neither unjust enrichment on the part of the acquirer nor undue deprivation on the part of the owner. The element of speculation is reduced to minimum if the underlying principles of fixation of market value with reference to comparable sales are made: (i) when sale is within a reasonable time of the date of notification under Section 4(1); (ii) it should be a bona fide transaction; (iii) it should be of the land acquired or of the land adjacent to the land acquired; and (iv) it should possess similar advantages.
