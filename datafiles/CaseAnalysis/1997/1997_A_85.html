State Of Haryana & Ors. v Amar Nath Bansal

Supreme Court of India

15 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2264; (1997) 10 SCC 700; AIR 1997 SC 718; JT 1997 (1) SC 734; 1997 LabIC 550; 1997(1) SCALE 343; 1997 (1) SCJ 85; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 262; 1997 (2) SLR 55; 1997 (2) Supreme 254

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Punjab Reorganisation Act, 1966, Pepsu Service Regulations

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 363 - Pepsu Service Rules, 1952 - Jind State Civil Service Regulations, 1945 - Superannuation - Continuity of service up to 62 years - Entitlement of - (A) Whether covenant by rulers of Kathiawar States was an act of State and Municipal Courts were not competent to entertain any dispute arising out of covenant? - Held, when Wadhawan State merged with Saurashtra State and again when it acceded to Dominion of India all existing laws continued until repealed and appellant's rights under Dhara No. 29 of s. 2004 were still good and could have been enforced in Municipal Courts until either repealed or repudiated as an act of State - Municipal Courts would be entitled to examine contract and apply Dhara No. 29 and enforce whatever rights appellant had under that Dhara and his contract of service - (B) Whether there is a bar u/art. 363 of Constitution of India, 1950, in instant case? - Held, there was no dispute arising out of covenant and what appellant was doing was merely to enforce his rights under existing laws which continued in force until they were repealed by appropriate legislation - Further held, there is no bar u/art. 363 of Constitution - (C) Superannuation - Whether Jind State Service Regulations of 1945 which prescribed 62 years as age of superannuation was a law in force in PEPSU on date of commencement of Constitution? - Held, ordinances were issued whereby laws of covenanting States were repealed and laws of Patiala State were made applicable in entire territory of PEPSU - Jind State Service Regulations of 1945 which prescribed 62 years as age of superannuation was a not law in force in PEPSU on date of commencement of Constitution - (D) Whether respondent was entitled to continue in service till he attained age of 62 years on basis of provisions contained in Jind State Civil Service Regulations, 1945? - Held, once it is held that Pepsu Service Regulations and Punjab Service Rules were applicable to all PEPSU Govt. employees - The respondent, who was one such employee, has to be governed by Pepsu Service Regulations and Punjab Service Rules - Respondent cannot claim that he continued to be governed by provisions of Jind State Civil Service Regulations, 1945 - Further held, since age of superannuation prescribed u/art. 3.26 of Punjab Service Rules was 58, respondent was rightly retired on his attaining said age - Appeal allowed.
