Ashok Kumar v Ved Prakash And Others

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2043; (2010) 2 SCC 264; AIR 2010 SC 330; 2010 (78) ALR 739; JT 2009 (15) SC 371; 2010 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 241; 2010 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 299; 2010 (5) MLJ(SC) 937; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 559; 2010 (1) RCR(Rent) 63; 2009(14) SCALE 701; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 695

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: East Punjab Rent Restriction (Amendment) Act, 1956

Summary: Rent Control - East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1949, s. 13 - Haryana Urban (Control of Rent and Eviction) Act, 1973 - Eviction - Original landlord filed eviction petition against appellant for eviction from non-residential tenanted premises on ground of bona fide requirement - Rent Controller allowed petition filed by original landlord - On appeal, HC upheld order of rent controller - Hence, present appeal - Whether landlord would be entitled to evict his tenant from a non-residential premises on ground of bona fide requirement under Haryana Urban (Control of Rent and Eviction) Act, 1973 when s. 13 of Act provides for eviction of tenant only in case of residential building if landlord requires it for his own occupation, and is not occupying another residential building in urban area concerned and has not vacated such building without sufficient cause after commencement of East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1949 in said urban area? - Held, yes - Landlord shall not be entitled to evict a tenant from a non- residential premises for bona fide requirement, when such ground for eviction has been made available only in case of residential premises as amendment of s. 13 of East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1949 restrict landlord from seeking eviction to a tenant from non-residential premises held as unconstitutional - Appeal dismissed.
