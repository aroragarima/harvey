Ajit Kumar Barat v Secretary, Indian Tea Association and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 396; (2001) 5 SCC 42; AIR 2001 SC 2056; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 241; 2001 LabIC 1751; 2001 (2) LLJ 249; 2001 (3) LLN 52; 2001(3) SCALE 616; 2001 (2) SLR 628; 2001 (4) SLT 323; 2001 (4) Supreme 1; 2001 (2) UC 222

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21 and 32 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 12(5) - Is Petitioner a workman, challenged? - Writ petition filed by petitioner against judgment in Indian Tea Association v. Ajit Kumar Barat & Ors. [2000 Indlaw SC 136] on the ground that it violates fundamental right u/art. 21 as court ignores binding precedents of larger benches and to issue writ of certiorari - Held, certiorari shall not lie to quash a judicial order of this court and benches of this Court is not subordinate to larger benches and certiorari is not admissible for quashing of orders made on the judicial side of this Court - Further held, fundamental right is not violated - Writ petition dismissed without any costs.
