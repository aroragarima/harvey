Commissioner of Income Tax, Amritsar v Shiv Prakash Janak Raj and Company Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

30 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2519; (1996) 11 SCC 530; 1996 (136) CTR 421; [1996] 222 ITR 583; JT 1996 (8) SC 603; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 81; [1996] 88 TAXMAN 536; 1997 (2) TLR 21

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1961 - S. 256(1) - Income Tax - (A) Whether, on the facts and in the circumstances of the case, the Tribunal was right in holding that the interest for the Assessment Year 1971-72, had already accrued to the assessee on 31-10-1970, under the mercantile system of accountancy? - (B) Whether, on the facts and in the circumstances of the case, the Tribunal was right in holding that the subsequent relinquishment of interest by a resolution dated 24-11-1970, did not affect the tax liability of the assessee on accrual basis? - (C) Whether they represent an item of expenditure permissible under the provisions of s. 10(2)(xv) of the Indian Income Tax Act, in computing the assessee's income of that 'previous year' from its managing agency business? - Held, the concept of real income cannot be employed so as to defeat the provisions of the Act and the Rules - Where the provisions of the Act and the Rules apply, it is only those provisions which must be applied and followed - There is no room - Nor would be permissible for the court to import the concept of real income so as to whittle down, qualify or defeat the provisions of the Act and the Rules therefore the appeals relating to Assessment Years 1969-70, 1970-71 and 1971-72 (in the case of Shiv Prakash Janak Raj & Co.) are allowed and the appeal relating to Assessment Year 1968-69 (in the case of Shiv Prakash Janak Raj & Co.) is dismissed as not pressed - For the same reasons, the other appeals are allowed.
