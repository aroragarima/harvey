Manoj @ Panu v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

09 December 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 809; (2014) 2 SCC 153; AIR 2014 SC 644; 2014 (1) JCC 737; 2014 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1947; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 525; 2013(15) SCALE 149

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Attempt to murder

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25, 27, 39 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 173, 31 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 307 - Attempt to murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Accused/appellant committed offence punishable u/ss. 307 of IPC and u/ss. 25, 27, 39 of the Act - Trial Court passed conviction order - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before HC - HC dismissed the appeal and confirmed conviction order passed by Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified - Held, sentences were ordered to run consecutively, and same was upheld by HC in exercise of its appellate jurisdiction - Sentences imposed upon appellant for different offences to run consecutively under IPC and the Act, were erroneous in law, as same were contrary to law laid down by SC - Regard to age of appellant at time of committing offences, SC felt it would not be just and proper to allow sentences to run consecutively - As offences committed by appellant was committed under single transaction, it was well settled position of law that sentences must run concurrently and not consecutively - Appellant was entitled to relief as prayed for and sentences were modified to run concurrently and not consecutively, SC held that sentence must be reduced to ten years in total with regard to aforesaid settled position of law, as also keeping in view tender age of appellant on date of offence - Impugned order passed by HC was modified, by reducing sentence to ten years in total - Appeal partly allowed.
