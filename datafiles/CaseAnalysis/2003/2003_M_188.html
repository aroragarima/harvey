National Highway Authority of India v Messrs Ganga Enterprises and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 690; (2003) 7 SCC 410; AIR 2003 SC 3823; 2003 (5) AWC 4478; 2003 (3) BC 621; [2003] 117 Comp Cas 154; 2003 (106) DLT 634; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 85; 2004 (1) MLJ(SC) 1; 2003 (3) RAJ 200; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 575; 2003(7) SCALE 171; 2003 (1) SCW 4381; 2003 (5) SLT 448; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 114; 2003 (6) Supreme 527

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Contract Of Guarantee

Summary: [A.] Contract and Commercial - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 5 - Bid security given as earnest to ensure non-withdrawal of bidder during period of bid validity and/or that after acceptance, performance security is finished and agreement signed - Respondent withdrew before stipulated time period and so his money was forfeited - Whether forfeiture of security deposit is without authority of law and without any binding contract between parties and also contrary to Section 5 of Contract Act - Held, a person may have a right to withdraw his offer but if he has made his offer on a condition that some earnest money will be forfeited for not entering into contract or if some act is not performed, then even though he may have a right to withdraw his offer, he has no right to claim that earnest/ security be returned to him - Forfeiture of such earnest/security, in no way, affects any statutory right under Indian Contract Act Such earnest /security is given and taken to ensure that a contract comes into existence - It would be an anomalous situation that a person who, by his own conduct, precludes coming into existence of contract is then given advantage or benefit of his own wrong by not allowing forfeiture - It must be remembered that, particularly in Govt. contracts, such a term is always included in order to ensure that only a genuine party makes a bid - If such a term was not there even a person who does not have capacity or a person who has no intention of entering into contract will make a bid whole purpose of such a cl i e to see that only genuine bids are received would be lost if forfeiture was not permitted.
