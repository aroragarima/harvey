Orissa Public Service Commission and another v Rupashree Chowdhary and another

Supreme Court of India

02 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 539; (2011) 8 SCC 108; JT 2011 (8) SC 303; 2011 (7) MLJ(SC) 605; 2011 (2) OLR 585; 2011(8) SCALE 151; [2011] 9 S.C.R. 748; 2012 (1) SLR 478

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Service

Keywords: Orissa Superior Judicial Service and Orissa Judicial Service Rules, 2007

Summary: Service - Advocates & Judges - Orissa Superior Judicial Service and Orissa Judicial Service Rules, 2007, r. 24 - Appointment to the post of Civil Judge - Plea for rounding off marks - Denied - Challenged - Petitioner (Orissa Public Service Commission) published an advertisement inviting applications from suitable candidates for the Orissa Judicial Service Examination, 2009 - Respondent applied for the said post and appeared in the Preliminary Written Examination and was declared successful - However, respondent failed to qualify in Main Written Examination - Respondent applied for mark sheet from which she came to know that she had secured 44.93% of marks in aggregate and more than 33% of marks on each subject - Respondent filed writ petition submitting that she should have been called for the interview as the fraction of marks, i.e., 44.93%, secured by her should have been rounded off to 45% and in that way she would have fulfilled the criteria as per the r. 24 of Rules - HC allowed said writ petition filed by the respondent and ordered for rounding off of the aggregate marks of the respondent from 44.93% to 45% along with two other candidates but not parties before the Court and held her eligible to appear in the interview - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, a bare reading of the aforesaid rules would make it crystal clear that in order to qualify in the written examination a candidate has to obtain a minimum of 33% marks in each of the papers and not less than 45% of marks in the aggregate in all the written papers in the Main examination - When emphasis was given in the Rules itself to the minimum marks to be obtained making it clear that at least the said minimum marks have to be obtained by the concerned candidate there cannot be a question of relaxation or rounding off - Moreover, there was no power provided in the statute/Rules permitting any such rounding off or giving grace marks so as to bring up a candidate to the minimum requirement - Hence, no such rounding off or relaxation was permissible - In the instant case, HC had committed an error apparent on the face of the records by allowing two more persons, who secured marks between 44.5% and 45%, to be called for interview who were not even parties before it and who had not even shown interest subsequently to be appointed subsequent to the declaration of the results of the examination but despite the said fact the High Court directed them also to be called for the interview only on the ground that they have secured more than 44.5% of marks but less than 45% marks in the main written examination in aggregate - Impugned order of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
