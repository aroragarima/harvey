P. Malaichami v M. Andi Ambalam and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 April 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Alagiriswami, D.G. Palekar

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 104; (1973) 2 SCC 170; AIR 1973 SC 2077; 1974 (1) MLJ(SC) 113; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 1016

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 97 and 100(1)(d)(iii) - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - (A) Election Petition - (1) Petitioner praying for setting aside returned candidate's election and also for declaring himself as elected - Returned candidate's failure to file recrimination petition u/s 97 - Effect - (2) Improper rejection or reception of vote - Construction - (B) Evidence Act - Estoppel - Election petition - Successful candidate precluded from giving evidence against petitioner's claim for declaration that he himself was elected - Concession made by petitioner as to votes validly given in favour of successful candidate whether can be used as evidence in his favour - Held, he will not be allowed to lead any evidence raising any plea against validity of claim of election petitioner - Phrase 'improper rejection or reception of vote' in s. 100(1)(d)(iii) includes not merely cases where a voter appears before presiding officer at the time of polling and his vote is received where it should not have been received and his vote rejected where it should not have been rejected, but also mistakes or wrong judgments made by returning officer while counting and exercising his powers u/cls. (a) to (h) of r. 56 of conduct of election rules - Concessions cannot be used as evidence in favour of successful candidate - Appeal dismissed.
