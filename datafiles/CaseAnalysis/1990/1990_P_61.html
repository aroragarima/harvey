S.C. Agrawal, B.C. Ray, K.N. Saikia, M.H. Kania, S. Mukharji

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Punjab Reorganisation Act, 1966, Defence of India Act, 1962, Punjab Re-organisation Act, 1966, Institutes of Technology Act, 1961, Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging Houses Rates (Control) Act, 1947, Government Premises (Eviction) Act, 1950, Delhi Rent Control (Amendment) Act, 1988, Bombay Municipal Act, 1888, Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Act, 1958, Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Amendment Act, 1980, Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Act 1970 (40 of 1971), Transport Act, 1947, Commonwealth Bank Act, 1945, Delhi Tenants (Temporary Protection) Act, 1956

Summary: Rent Control - Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorized Occupants) Act ,1971,ss. 1, 2(e) and 2(a) - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958 , s. 1 - Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act ,1970, s. 3 - Constitution, arts. 14, 19(1)(g), 21, 39, 41, 246(1) and 246(4), sch.7, list 1, entries 32, 95, 97 and list 3 entries 6, 7, 13 - (A) Nationalized bank is a corporation established by a Central Act and it is owned and controlled by the Central Govt. - Whether the premises belonging to nationalized banks are public premises - (B) Whether the scope and ambit of definition of public premises u/s 2(a) covers both residential and commercial premises - (C) Occupation by tenant holding over is unauthorized occupation s. 2(g) - Fact that estate officer is not required by Act to be an officer well versed in law - Whether it is a ground to exclude case of tenant holding over from purview of Act - (D) Interpretation of Statutes - Inconsistent Laws namely - For both 1971 Act and 1958 Act , the source of legislative power falls in concurrent list of the constitution - Whether the plea that 1971 Acts falls in Union list and so overrides Rents Act of 1958 is tenable - (E ) Provisions of ss.1 and 15 of 1971 Act - Override provisions of 1958 Act - Person in unauthorized occupation of public premises - Cannot invoke protection of Rent Control Act. - Conflicting laws - Both laws special - Later laws to abrogate earlier contrary laws How to resolve the conflict - Held, the premises belonging to nationalized banks are public premises u/s. 2(e)(2)(ii) of the 1971 Act - No warrant to confine premises it to residential premises only - No valid ground - Law enacted under Central list to override law under Concurrent list, plea not tenable - Conflict to be resolved by reference to purpose and policy - Order accordingly.
