Pirthi v Mohan Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

02 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 615; (2011) 9 SCC 107; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 349; 2011(9) SCALE 669; [2011] 11 S.C.R. 122

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Punjab Pre-Emption Act, 1913, Punjab Pre-emption (Haryana Amendment) Act, 1995

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Civil Procedure - Punjab Pre-emption (Haryana Amendment) Act, 1995, s. 15- Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O.20 r.14 - Sale of Land - Notification - Pre-emption Right - Entitlement - Appellant/plaintiff claimed to be co-sharers with respondent no.3 who sold away land to respondent nos. 1,2, and 4 - Appellant filed suit for possession by way of pre-emption right - Respondents/defendant no. 1 and 4 filed application for dismissing suit on ground that after passing of the 1955 Act, right of pre-emption was not available to them - Trial Court accepting application filed by respondents/defendants dismissed suit - Aggrieved, appellant/plaintiff filed appeal before Second Appellate Court - Appellate Court dismissed appeal by holding that appellant/plaintiff had lost character of co-owner during pendency of suit - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that suit in question was instituted prior to amendment of the 1913 Act, - Hence amendment was not applicable and Courts below were erroneous in accepting objection of respondents/defendants - Whether appellant was entitled to pre-emption right as suit was filed prior to amendment of the 1913 Act - Held, in instant case on date of institution of suit appellant/plaintiff had right to claim to claim 'right of pre-emption' - However, during pendency of suit, amended Act came into force deleting right of pre-emption and in absence of such right on date of passing of decree by Court of first instance - Both Courts below had correctly appreciated effect of amendment and HC also rightly dismissed appeal stating that appellant/plaintiff had lost character of co-owner during pendency of suit by virtue of amended the 1955 Act - Thus in Shyam Sunder 2001 Indlaw SC 21401 (supra) relied on - Appeal dismissed.
