Uday Shankar Upadhyay And Others v Naveen Maheshwari

Supreme Court of India

18 November 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2060; (2010) 1 SCC 503; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 453B; 2010 (79) ALR 512; 2010 (2) AWC 1598; 2009 (5) CTC 782; 2010 (1) JhrCR(SC) 213; 2010 (1) MLJ(SC) 416; 2010 (2) MPLJ 243; 2010 (1) RCR(Rent) 554; 2009(14) SCALE 103; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 1002

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Suit for eviction - Landlord-appellant filed a suit for eviction of the tenant from the impugned shop on the ground that he needs the shop for setting up the business of his two sons - Trial court decreed the suit on ground that need of the landlord was bona fide - On appeal, appellate court held that the hall above the suit shop is a suitable alternative accommodation and it can be used for non-residential purpose - HC's upheld the order of appellate court - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC's order could be upheld? - Held, once it is not disputed that the landlord is in bona fide need of the premises, it is not for the courts to say that he should shift to the first floor or any higher floor that is for the landlord himself to decide - Hence, the view of the courts below that the sons of plaintiff No. 1 should do business on the first floor in the hall which is being used for residential purpose was, wholly arbitrary, and hence cannot be sustained - HC's order set aside - Appeal allowed.
