State of Uttar Pradesh v Ashok Dixit and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Phukan, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 150; (2000) 3 SCC 70; (2000) SCC (Cr) 579; AIR 2000 SC 1066; AIR 2000 SCW 548; 2000 ALJ 700; 2000 (1) CCR 222; 2000 (1) Crimes 257; 2000 CRLJ 1436; JT 2000 (2) SC 107; 2000 (1) RCR(Criminal) 841; 2000(1) SCALE 562; 2000 (2) SCJ 139; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 855; 2000 (2) SLT 179; 2000 (1) Supreme 616

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Indian Penal Code 1860, s. 300 - Murder - Discrepancies and omissions observed during appreciation of evidence - Omissions in statement of one of eye - witness, uncle of deceased as regards to identification of accused 'A' - Identification of accused 'C' by him was also not reliable - Uncorroborated evidence of other child witness also not reliable as to identification of accused 'A' - No explanation was given for non - examination of injured witness - Medical evidence not supporting and bullets recovered from body of deceased found to be not fired from pistols recovered - Held, accused entitled to acquittal.
