Property Owners' Association and Others v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, K. Venkataswami, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2585; (1996) 4 SCC 49; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 584; 1997 (1) Bom.C.R. 392; 1996 (2) CCC 261; JT 1996 (6) SC 23; 1996(4) SCALE 225; 1996 (2) SCJ 558; [1996] Supp2 S.C.R. 230; 1996 (4) Supreme 223

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Civil Procedure - Constitution - Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 31-C - Maharashtra Housing and Area Development Act, 1976, Chapter VIII-A - Whether the matter should be decided by a larger bench or not? - It would be more appropriate for a Bench of not less than five Judges to consider and decide these questions for an authoritative pronouncement on the same - Even if it is assumed that art. 145(3) of the Constitution, 1950 is not attracted, it does appear to that in order to settle the controversy on this point which is of some significance and to avoid the question being reagitated before another Bench of less than five Judges, the more appropriate course is to refer these matters for being heard and decided by a Bench of not less than five Judges - Held, directed that the papers be laid before the learned Chief Justice of India for constituting a larger Bench of not less than five Judges for hearing and deciding these matters.
