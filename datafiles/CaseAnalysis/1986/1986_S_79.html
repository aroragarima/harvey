Amarjit Singh v Smt. Khatoon Quamarain

Supreme Court of India

18 November 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 223; (1986) 4 SCC 736; AIR 1987 SC 741; JT 1986 SC 912; 1987 (1) RCJ 398; 1987 (1) RCR(Rent) 192; 1987 (1) RentLR 123; 1986(2) SCALE 827; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 275

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958 - Eviction of tenant sought on the ground of bona fide requirement - (A) Determination of constitutional validity of condition that landlord should not have in his or her possession any other reasonably suitable accommodation u/s.14(1)(c) of the Act - (B) Whether High Court was right in holding that landlady become disentitled to the right of eviction on dot availing the choice to go into the premises in question - Held, does not violate arts. 14, 21 of Constitution - He is disentitled to evict tenant on ground of bona fide need - High Court was wrong - Appeal allowed.
