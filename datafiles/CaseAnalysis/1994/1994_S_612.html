Goodricke Group Limited Etc v State of West Bengal and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 November 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, K.S. Paripoornan, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 2182; (1995) Supp1 SCC 707; AIR 1995 SCW 123; 1995 (123) CTR 516; 1995 (50) ECC 138; JT 1994 (7) SC 577; 1994(4) SCALE 1138; 1995 (98) STC 32; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 120

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Education Cess

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution - West Bengal Taxation Laws (Second Amendment) Act, 1989 - West Bengal Rural Employment and Production Act, 1976, ss. 4(1) and 4(2) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 301 - Tea Act, 1953 - Whether levy created by ss. 2 and 3 of Amendment Act is not a tax on lands and buildings within meaning of Entry 49 of List II of Seventh Schedule to Constitution, 1950 and is, therefore, beyond competence of State Legislature? - Held, levy of cess is an annual levy - Levy is upon 'a tea estate' at rate of twelve paise for each kilogram of green tea leaves produced in such estate - Power of legislature to make law with reference to Entry 49 of List-II is plenary like any other entry in List II - It is open to legislature to adopt such formula as it thinks appropriate for levying tax and so long as character of tax remains one contemplated by entry, it does not matter how tax is calculated, measured or assessed - If a tax on land can be assessed with reference to or on basis of previous year's income, no reason why it cannot be levied on current year's income - Once a tea estate is validly classified as a unit of taxation, levy of tax on basis of produce of such tea estate unit cannot be faulted - If tax is calculated or assessed on basis of such yield or income, it cannot be said that for that reason alone there is no reasonable connection between land and tax - Every tax enactment contains a provision for exemption - Said provision can make no difference to character of tax - A person other than person in possession could not have been made so liable - Making person in actual possession of land or building for payment of tax is a well-known feature in enactments relatable to Entry 49 of List-II and cannot be faulted - Impugned cesses are clearly relatable to Entry 49 of List - If Act is good, it is good both prospectively and retrospectively - Petitions dismissed.
