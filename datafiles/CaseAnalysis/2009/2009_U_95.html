Union of India and Others v Bishamber Das Dogra

Supreme Court of India

26 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 680; (2009) 13 SCC 102; AIR 2010 SC 3769; 2009 (4) AWC 3726; 2009 (2) CLR 723; 2009 (3) ESC 499; 2009 (3) ESC 499A; 2009 (122) FLR 578; JT 2009 (9) SC 175; 2009(8) SCALE 667; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 828; 2009 (5) SLR 128

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Central Industrial Security Force Rules, r. 34 - Absenteeism - Removal - Respondent a security guard in CISF - Absence from duty without leave on three occasions, three different punishments awarded, two incidents of desertion, after first such desertion show cause notice u/r. 34 was issued which could not be served as he again deserted, this time for 50 days; afterwards he joined the duty thus the SCN was served; explanation given, found unsatisfactory; regular departmental enquiry initiated; during pendency of enquiry respondent again deserted; Disciplinary Authority accepted the enquiry report; punishment of removal imposed, for which DA also took into consideration the past conduct of the respondent - Appeal filed, dismissed; observation made that respondent had not completed six years in service but had deserted the LINE five times, thus no lenient view was permissible - Writ petition filed, allowed - Writ appeal preferred, dismissed - High Court allowed writ petition and quashed order of punishment on ground that copy of enquiry report was not furnished and the respondent/employee was not given the opportunity to file the objections to the enquiry report, further ground was that past conduct could not have been taken into consideration while imposing the punishment - (A) Whether the delinquent employee is not supposed to establish de-facto prejudice in case the enquiry report is not supplied to him before awarding punishment? - Held, Yes prejudice has to be established - Principles of natural justice(PNJ) cannot be put into a strait jacket formula and its observance would depend upon the fact situation of each case - Precedents referred and relied, deliberations entered into - Legal position settled, that an order is required to be examined on the touchstone of 'doctrine of prejudice' - (B) Whether the order of punishment would be vitiated if the Disciplinary Authority takes into consideration the past conduct of the delinquent employee for the purpose of punishment? - Precedents referred and relied, deliberations entered into - Held, evidently it is desirable that delinquent employee may be informed by the DA that his past conduct would be taken into consideration while imposing punishment, but in case of misconduct of grave nature or indiscipline, even in absence of statutory rules, the authority may take into consideration the indisputable past conduct/service record for adding the weight to the decision of imposing the punishment if the facts of the case so require - Proposition settled, that habitual absenteeism means gross violation of discipline - Admitted facts further noted by the Court - No explanation whatsoever from the employee for repeated misconduct/absenteeism; no attempt had ever been made at any stage by respondent/employee to explain as to what prejudice has been caused to him by non-furnishing of the enquiry report; he never submitted that such a course has resulted in failure of justice - Impugned judgements of HC, set aside - Appeal succeeds - Appeal allowed.
