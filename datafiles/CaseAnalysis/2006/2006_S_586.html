State of Tamil Nadu and Another v P. Krishnamurthy and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 615; (2006) 4 SCC 517; AIR 2006 SC 1622; JT 2006 (4) SC 167; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 313; 2006(3) SCALE 460; 2006 (4) SCJ 375; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 396

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Mines & Minerals

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957, ss. 15 and 4A(2) - Tamil Nadu Minor Mineral Concession Rules, 1959, r. 38A - Mining - Quarrying of sands - Lease - Whether High Court division bench decision in upholding r. 38-A of the Tamil Nadu Minor Mineral Concession Rules is valid? - Held, directions issued while upholding r. 38-A that part of r. 38A which vests the exclusive right to quarry sand, in the State Govt., provision in r. 38A for refund of proportionate lease amount for the unexpired period of lease and unadjusted seigniorage fee, shall remain undisturbed and State Govt. is at liberty to prematurely terminate the leases for any of the causes mentioned in s. 4A(2), by giving a notice and hearing under s. 4A(3), if they want to terminate any lease within the said period of six months - Appeal allowed and parties to bear their own costs.
