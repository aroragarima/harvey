Rishabh Agro Industries Limited v P. N. B. Capital Services Limited

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 444; (2000) 5 SCC 515; AIR 2000 SC 1583; 2000 (4) ALD(SC) 85; 2000 (1) CLC 1156; [2000] 101 Comp Cas 284; 2000 (3) CompLJ 1; JT 2000 (6) SC 447; 2000(4) SCALE 543; [2000] Supp1 S.C.R. 38

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Practice & Procedure; Trade

Keywords: Articles Of Association, Status Quo, Legal Proceedings, Financial Reconstruction

Summary: Corporate - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1986, ss. 15 and 22 - Companies Act, 1956, s. 434 - Limitation Act, 1908 - Industrial company - Winding up order - (A) Determination of extent of power u/s. 22 of Act to stay of legal proceedings - (B) Whether reference to Board filed after winding up order and securing time from Company Court for making payment of dues is malafide and ultravires? - Held, not limited up to stage of passing of winding up order, winding up proceedings after order for winding up has been passed can also be stayed - Neither mala fide nor beyond powers of Board of Directors of Company - Appeal allowed.
