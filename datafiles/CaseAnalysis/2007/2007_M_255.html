Commissioner of Central Excise, Mumbai v Messrs Damnet Chemicals Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Sudershan Reddy, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 884; (2007) 7 SCC 490; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 857; 2007 (216) E.L.T. 3; JT 2007 (11) SC 30; 2007(11) SCALE 20; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 813

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Misstatement, Suppression, Registered Office, Trade Discount, Time-Barred

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 4(a), 4(3)(b), 11(A)1 and 35 L(b) - Notification No. 120/84-CE - (A) whether product 'CRC 2-26' is a blended lubricating oil and thus is entitled to exemption under Notification No. 120/84? - Held, negligible percentage of rust preventives does not make product in question to be a rust preventive one - Plea of department that product is not a lubricating oil is untenable - (B) whether respondent is entitled to benefit of Notification No. 175/86 in respect of product 'CRC Acryform'? - Further held, Tribunal came to correct conclusion that respondent continues to be a small-scale industry and entitled to benefit of said Notification - (C) whether invoking of s. 11(A)1 by department required and whether demand raised in show cause notice time-barred? - Further held, it is settled law that in order to invoke proviso to s. 11 A(1) a mere misstatement could not be enough - Tribunal did not commit any error in holding that extended period of limitation was not available to Deptt for initiating recovery proceedings u/s. 11 A(1) - (D) whether respondent and BBL are related persons within meaning of ss. 4(a) and 4(3)(b)? - Further held, there is no evidence on record in support of allegation that transactions between respondent-company and BBL were not on a principle to principal basis - It cannot be said that respondent-assessee and BBL were related persons - Appeal dismissed.
