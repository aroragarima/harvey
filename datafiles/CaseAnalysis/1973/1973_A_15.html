Amar Singh v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 440; (1973) SCC (Cr) 789; (1974) 3 SCC 81; AIR 1973 SC 2221; 1973 CRLJ 1409; 1973 CRLR 483; 1973 UJ 680

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Army service

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 149 - (A) Appreciation of evidence in a murder case - Credibility of interested witness - For non-examination of disinterested neighbours residing near place of occurrence - When there is no evidence to show that any person other than eye-witnesses saw occurrence - (B) IPC, 1860 - Charge u/s 302 r/ w s. 149 - Conviction u/s 302 r/w s. 34 of Act - Legality - Held, there non-examination is no ground for disbelieving eye-witnesses even though they are related to or interested in complainant - Conviction of an accused u/s 302 r/w s. 34 though charged u/s 302 r/w s.149 is not illegal when facts proved and evidence adduced would have been same if accused had been charged u/s 302 r/w s. 34 - Appeal dismissed.
