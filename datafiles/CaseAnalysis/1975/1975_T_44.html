Munnu Raja & Anr. v The Stae Of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

20 November 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 280; (1976) 3 SCC 104; (1976) SCC (Cr) 376; AIR 1976 SC 2199; 1976 CRLJ 1718; 1976 CrLR(SC) 54; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 764; 1976 UJ 154

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Practice and Procedure - Indian Evidence Act, 1872,s.32 - Statement made by the victim recorded as F.I.R. could be treated as dying declaration u/s..32 of Indian Evidence Act,1872 - Necessity for corroboration for its admissibility - (A)Whether dying declaration cannot be acted upon unless it is corroborated? - It is well-settled that though a dying declaration must be approached with caution for the reason that the maker of the statement cannot be subjected to cross-examination, there is neither a rule of law nor a rule of prudence which has hardened into a rule of law that a dying declaration cannot be acted upon unless it is corroborated - Held , Court must not look out for corroboration it comes to the conclusion that the dying declaration suffered from any infirmity by reason of which it was necessary to look out for corroboration - (B)Whether the full incident is to be narrated by the witness for the dying declaration to be acceptable? - Held, in order that the Court may be in a position to assess the evidentiary value of a dying declaration, what is necessary is that the whole of the statement made by the deceased must be laid before the Court without tampering with its terms or its tenor - Law does not require that the maker of the dying declaration must cover the whole incident or narrate the case history- (C) Whether the investigating officer is justified in recording the dying declaration ? - Since, victim in precarious condition while making statement -Investigating Officer himself recording statement without requistioning services of Magistrate - Held, Practice was bad and should not be encouraged- Appeal dismissed.
