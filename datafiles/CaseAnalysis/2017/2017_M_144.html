Mohammed Ansari v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 62; (2017) 3 SCC 740; 2017 (2) MLJ 688; 2017(2) SCALE 154

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Punishment, Jurisdiction, Relief, Promotion, Permanent, Appeal, Armed Forces, Dismissal, Army, Regular, Policy, Army Act, 1950, Army Rules, 1954, Posting, Service Matters, Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, Court-Martial, Central Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1965, Air Force Act, 1950, Navy Act, 1957, Misconduct, Armed Forces Tribunal Act, 2007, Writ Petition, Constitution of India, 1950, Training

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Armed Forces Tribunal Act, 2007 - Jurisdiction - Challenged - Appellant was initially appointed as Assistant Executive Engineer by competent authority of GOI - Appellant was promoted to post of Superintending Engineer in GREF - Appellant after completion of more than requisite years of service was not granted non-functional financial upgradation for officers of Organized Group and that compelled him to make representation to concerned authorities - Representations were turned down on ground that Appellant did not fulfill stipulated command posting of two years - Appellant preferred Application Tribunal and tribunal decided issue in favour of Appellant - Respondents preferred petition seeking quashment of order passed by Tribunal - HC held that remedy of Appellant/Respondent lies in making appropriate application in HC or in instituting appropriate suit for remedy of his grievances - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, HC was justified in holding that Tribunal does not have jurisdiction to deal with controversy. Held, it is not in dispute that Appellant is member of GREF in Border Roads Engineering Services. Army personnel are posted in GREF according to deliberate and carefully planned manning policy evolved with view to ensuring special character of GREF as force intended to support Army in its operational requirements. Posting of Army personnel in GREF units is in fact regarded as normal regimental posting and does not entitle Army personnel so posted to any deputation or other allowance and it is equated with similar posting in Army for purpose of promotion, career planning. It is clear that directly recruited personnel may be required to serve anywhere in India and outside India and when directed, they would have to proceed on field service and if required, they would also be liable to serve in any Defence Service or post connected with defence of India. It is noticeable that language of provisions is absolutely plain and Act does not apply to any member of armed forces and HC is justified in holding that Central Administrative Tribunal does not have jurisdiction to deal with controversy. Appeal dismissed. Practice & Procedure - Armed Forces Tribunal Act, 2007 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 Army Rules, 1954 - Original jurisdiction - Whether, after coming into force of 2007 Act, it will be Armed Forces Tribunal which shall deal with controversy or HC would still have original jurisdiction u/art. 226 of Constitution. Held, court clearly held that merely because respondent is subjected to 1950 Act would not by itself be sufficient to conclude that tribunal had jurisdiction to deal with any case brought before it by such person. In view of statutory framework, it is demonstrable that 1950 Act and Rules have been applied to civilian personnel of GREF only for purpose of discipline. Reasons are obvious. GREF is force raised and maintained under authority of Central Govt., its units are set up on lines of Indian Army, it works with and under close coordination with regular army in border areas, facilitates Indian Army to carry out its operational role. It has been felt appropriate that 1950 Act should be made applicable to force raised and maintained by Central Govt. as considered necessary in interest of discipline. Issue can be perceived from different perspective. GREF personnel are subjected by legislative scheme to dual disciplinary control, and such arrangement is permissible. In absence of lack of inherent jurisdiction to deal with issue, said judgment is nullity. It has no existence in law. It is well settled in law that judgment passed is nullity if it is passed by court having no inherent jurisdiction. Decree to be called nullity is to be understood in sense that it is ultra vires powers of court passing decree and not merely voidable decree. Appellant is granted liberty to approach HC for redressal of his grievances. Appeal dismissed.
