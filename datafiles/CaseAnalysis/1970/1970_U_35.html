Supreme Court of India

10 February 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vishishtha Bhargava, G.K. Mitter, C.A. Vaidyialingam, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, A.N. Ray, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, I.D. Dua, S.M. Sikri, J.M. Shelat

1970 Indlaw SC 575; (1970) 1 SCC 248; AIR 1970 SC 564; [1970] 40 Comp Cas 325; 1970 (1) CompLJ 244; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 530

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Safe Custody, Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940, Commercial Banks, National Co-Operative Development Corporation Act, 1962, Trading Corporations, Safe Deposit, Presidency Banks, Nationalisation, Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertaking) Act, 1969, Constitution (Fourth Amendment) Act, 1955, Prohibition Act, National bank, Agricultural Refinance Corporation Act, 1963, State Agricultural Credit Corporations Act, 1968

Summary: Banking - Nationalization of Banks - Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertaking) Act, 1969, ss. 4, 5 and 15(2) - Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertaking) Ordinance, 1969 - Banking Regulation Act, 1949 - ss. 5, 6(1), 22 and 27 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19, 19(1), 31 and 31(2) - Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertaking) Ordinance, 1969 provided for acquisition of undertakings of banking companies and could no longer conduct business as a bank - Petitioner held shares and was director was one of these banks - Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertaking) Act, 1969 was enacted - Validity of the Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertaking) Act, 1969 was challenged on grounds of violation of fundamental rights to trade and rights guaranteed under arts. 14, 19 and 31 of Constitution - Whether the nationalization of banks under the Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertaking) Act, 1969 was sustainable? - Held, no - Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertaking) Ordinance, 1969 and Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertaking) Act were violative of guarantee of freedom of trade, commerce and intercourse u/art. 301 of Constitution - State action which deprives a person of his right to property, or personal freedom, operates to extinguish the right or the remedy is to reduce the guarantee to an empty platitude is liable to be declared unconstitutional - No public purpose present to sustain compulsory acquisition under the legislation - Violative of art. 31(2) of Constitution - Legislation has the effect of depriving a business organization of assets, undertaking, managerial and other staff, premises, name, as well as right to carry on even non-banking business - Fundamental rights granted u/art. 19(1)(f) and art. 19(1)(g) of the Constitution infringed - Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1969 declared invalid - Action taken or deemed to be taken in exercise of the powers under the Act is declared unauthorized - Petitions allowed.
