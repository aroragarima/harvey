Smt. Rukmani Devi and Others v Narendra Lal Gupta

Supreme Court of India

13 September 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, D.P. Madon

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 374; (1985) 1 SCC 144; AIR 1984 SC 1866; 1985 (1) CCC 121; 1984(2) SCALE 589; 1985 UJ 196

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land and Property - Succession Act, 1925, ss. 270 and 273 - Will - (A) Resondent (son of the testator) on the strength of the will proceeded to execute the consent decree - Appellants challenged the validity of the will and contended that unless the will is probated, the respondent (son) was not entitled to execute the decree - Party receiving citation but not entering caveat to contest - Held, precluded from contesting validity of will in other proceedings - (B) Jurisdiction of probate Court - Determination of - Held, as the was staying with her son at the place for over 20 years before her death, the Court had jurisdiction to entertain the application as provided by Section 270 of the Act - Appeal dismissed.
