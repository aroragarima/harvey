Â D. S. Garewal v State of Punjab and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Sudhi Ranjan Das, S.K. Das, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 161; AIR 1959 SC 512; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 792; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 792

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: All-India Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1955,

Summary: Service - Parliament & Legislature - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 226, 312 and 392 - All India Services Act, 1951, ss. 3 and 4 - All India Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1955, rr. 4 and 5 - (A) Whether power to frame rules for regulation of recruitment and conditions of service can be delegated to executive authority? - Held, such delegation is possible - Parliament must maintain strict vigil and control over its delegate - (B)Whether President can make adaptations by way of omission u/art. 392? - Further held, yes - Power u/art. 392 is very wide and it is for President to decide whether adaptation should be by way of modification, addition or omission -(C) Whether state Govt. has authority to order an enquiry against a civil servant belonging to All India Services? - Further held, state Govt. has authority to do so - Question of punishment arises only after enquiry is over and relevant Govt. will consider that question based on report of enquiry officer - Appeal dismissed.
