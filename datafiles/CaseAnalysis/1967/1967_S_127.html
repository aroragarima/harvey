Â Prem Nath and Others v State of Rajasthan and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Vishishtha Bhargava, G.K. Mitter, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 109; AIR 1967 SC 1599; 1967 CRLJ 1569; 1967 (2) ITJ 502; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 186; 1967 SLR 872

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 309,124(2) 217(2) 222, 235, 236, 236(a), and 238 - Constitution (Twentieth Amendment) Act, 1966, art.233 A - Rajasthan Civil Courts Ordinance, 1950, s.6,13,19,16, 17 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss.6, 7, 9, 9, 9(3), 36, 37, 38 and 39- Rajasthan Higher Judicial service Rules, rr.1(2), 6, 6(3), 7, 10, 13, 13(2), 17, 22, 23, 24, ch 1 - Bengal, Agra and Assam Civil Courts Act, 1887, s.3 - In pursuance of Rajasthan Higher Judicial Service Rules, High Court of Rajasthan published a notice inviting applications for direct recruitment to posts of Civil and Addl. Sessions Judge- After scrutiny thereof and interviews granted to applicants, Selection Committee made appointments - Committee selected eligible candidates and prepared a list- HC submitted two lists prepared by Committee to Governor for appointments - No provision in said rules for consultation with HC before appointment - (A) Whether Rajasthan Higher Judicial Service Rules, 1955 are ultra vires art. 233? - Only function entrusted under Rules of 1955 to HC is that of transmitting to Governor two lists prepared by Committee - Held, rules of 1955 do not provide for consultation of HC and, therefore, contravene art. 233 which envisages consultation with HC and not with any other body such as Selection Committee which cannot substitute HC even though members thereof happen to be three Judges of HC - (B) Whether Selection Committee appointed under Rajasthan Higher Judicial Service rules and appointments made on basis of such selection is valid ? - Further held, rules being clearly inconsistent with mandate provided for in art. 233 Selection made by Selection Committee appointed under rules and appointments made on basis of such selection would be invalid - (C) Whether determination of number of appointments by Governor was contrary to r.10 or that such determination rendered subsequent proceedings of Selection Committee bad in law? - There nothing in r.10 or r.24 to preclude Governor from including them in number of appointments to be determined by him - Even if persons are appointed to officiate to such posts since their appointment would not be substantive appointment, they would not acquire a lien thereon - Therefore those posts remain unfilled until substantive appointments in respect of them are made - They can be included in number of appointments determined by Governor under r. 10 - Further held, determination of number of appointments by Governor and subsequent proceedings of Selection Committee is not bad in law - Appeal dismissed.
