Md. Mannan @ Abdul Mannan v State Of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

20 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 275; (2011) 5 SCC 317; 2011 CRLJ 3380; JT 2011 (5) SC 167; 2011 (4) MLJ(Crl) 716; 2011 (2) RCR(Criminal) 874; 2011(4) SCALE 809; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 518

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 302, 366 and 376 - Kidnapping - Rape - Murder - Conviction - Death sentence - Challenged - Appellant was convicted for offence punishable u/ss. 366, 376, 302 and 201 of the IPC and was awarded death sentence - Prosecution case was based on circumstantial evidence - Conviction and death sentence was upheld by HC - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Conviction - Whether circumstances on record lead to the conclusion that appellant was guilty? - Held, in a case based on circumstantial evidence the circumstances from which an inference of guilt was sought to be drawn were to be cogently and firmly established - Circumstances so proved must unerringly point towards the guilt of the accused - It should form a chain so complete that there was no escape from the conclusion that the crime was committed by the accused and none else - In the instant case, the circumstances so proved unerringly point towards the guilt of the appellant and the chain was so complete that there was no escape from the conclusion that the crime was committed by the appellant and none else - Conviction upheld - (B) Death sentence - Whether instant case was one of the rarest of the rare cases? - It was contended by appellant that the case did not fall within such category and as such the extreme penalty of death was not called for - Held, when the crime was committed in an extremely brutal manner so as to arouse intense and extreme indignation of the community and when collective conscience of the community was petrified, one had to lean towards the death sentence - Balance-sheet was to be prepared while considering the imposition of penalty of death of aggravating and mitigating circumstances and just balance was to be struck - In the instant case, appellant was a matured man, aged about 43 years and the victim girl was aged about 7 years - He held a position of trust and misused the same in calculated and preplanned manner - Instant case fall in the category of the rarest of the rare cases and the Trial Court had correctly inflicted the death sentence which had rightly been confirmed by the High Court - Appeal dismissed.
