Sanaullah Khan v State Of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

15 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 94; (2013) 3 SCC 52; 2013 CRLJ 1527; 2013 (2) JCC 1029; JT 2013 (3) SC 54; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 135; 2013(2) SCALE 505; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 1079

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 364 r/w s. 34, 300, 302, 120B and 201 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 354(3) - Triple murder - Conviction - Death penalty - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) and other co-accused allegedly murdered 3 persons and committed offences u/s. 364 r/w s. 34, 302, 120B and 201 of IPC - Trial Court considered the case as one of those rarest of rare cases and appellant was awarded with capital punishment of death - However, acquitted other co-accused - Thereafter, Trial Court referred the sentence of death to HC - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before HC, which was dismissed and HC confirmed death sentence of appellant - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeal - Appellant contended that there was not motive to kill deceased persons and that there were no eye witnesses and that there was no evidence to establish the circumstances based on which conviction was awarded - Whether impugned death penalty imposed by Trial Court and confirmed by HC was justified - Held, circumstances were established by prosecution was that deceased 'A' (who was working with appellant) came to tea stall and told deceased 'R' that he was being called by appellant and 'R' went with 'A' and within an hour thereafter 'A' again came to tea stall and told deceased 'S' (brother of 'R') that he was being called by appellant and 'S' went along with 'A' - Dead bodies of 3 deceased persons were recovered from a room in occupation of appellant - Pursuant to information divulged by appellant the incriminating materials were recovered by IO - Those chain of circumstances establish beyond reasonable doubt that it was appellant who had eliminated 3 deceased persons - Considering chain of circumstances which were fully established by prosecution, 5 golden principles laid down in Sharad Birdhichand Sarda v. State of Maharashtra, 1984 Indlaw SC 432, applied in instant case and only hypothesis was concluded from chain of circumstances was that it was appellant who committed murder of 3 deceased persons - However, motive for appellant to commit murder of 3 persons was not established and reasons given by Trial Court and HC for death sentence that murders were committed for petty monetary gain was not substantiated by evidence - There was no eyewitness to the manner in which appellant committed murder of 3 persons and culpability of appellant was established only by a chain of circumstances established by prosecution - Reasons for awarding extreme penalty of death was based on an apprehension and might not be enough to impose extreme penalty of death - There was sufficient evidence to establish culpability of appellant for offences of 3 murder - Considering facts of case, appellant was liable u/s. 302 of IPC for imprisonment for life for each of 3 offences of murder u/s. 300 of IPC and imprisonments for life should not run concurrently but consecutively - Impugned death sentence was convert to sentence for RI for life for each of 3 offences of murder - Sharad Birdhichand Sarda v. State of Maharashtra, 1984 Indlaw SC 432, referred to - Appeals allowed.
