State of Arunachal Pradesh v Nezone Law House, Assam

Supreme Court of India

01 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 658; (2008) 5 SCC 609; AIR 2008 SC 2045; 2008 (5) ALD(SC) 65; 2008(4) ALL MR 921; 2008 (2) AWC 1851; JT 2008 (4) SC 470; 2008 (5) MLJ(SC) 610; 2008(6) SCALE 28; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 948; 2008 (2) UC 739

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Rule Of Equity, Arunachal Pradesh

Summary: Petition by Respondent claiming that State Government had promised to purchase 500 sets of 'North Eastern Region Local Acts and Rules' from it but contrary to its promise it had refused to place any order - Held, in order to invoke the doctrine of promissory estoppel clear, sound and positive foundation must be laid in the petition itself by the party invoking the doctrine and bald expressions without any supporting material to the effect that the doctrine is attracted because the party invoking the doctrine has altered its position relying on the assurance of the Government would not be sufficient to press into aid the doctrine - Factual scenario goes to show the principles of promissory estoppel were clearly inapplicable to the facts of the case - Appeal allowed.
