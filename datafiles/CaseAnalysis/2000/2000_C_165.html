M. S. Jayaraj v Commissioner of Excise, Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 701; (2000) 7 SCC 552; AIR 2000 SC 3266; JT 2000 (Supp1) SC 487; 2000 (3) KLT 820; 2000(6) SCALE 674; 2000 (7) SLT 330; [2000] Supp3 S.C.R. 616; 2000 (7) Supreme 105

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Excise - Land & Property - Kerala Abkari Act - Kerala Abkari Shops (Disposal in Auction) Rules, 1974, r. 6(2) - (A) Whether Excise Commr. has power u/r. 6(2) of Auction Rules to transfer an abkari or foreign liquor shop outside limits notified in Gazette u/r. 4? - Held, Excise Commr. has no authority to permit shifting of a foreign liquor shop from one range to a totally different range - Order passed by Excise Commr. in favour of appellant is without authority of law and it has been rightly struck down - (B) Whether third respondent had no locus standi to maintain a petition u/art. 226 of Constitution against Excise Commr.'s order? - Further held, in light of expanded concept of locus standi and also in view of finding of High Court Division Bench that order of Excise Commr. was passed in violation of law, motion cannot be nipped out solely on ground of locus standi - Appeal dismissed.
