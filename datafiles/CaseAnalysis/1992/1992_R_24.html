Herdeo Kaur And Ors. v Rajasthan State Transport Corporation And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

13 March 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 920; (1992) SCC (Cr) 394; (1992) 2 SCC 567; 1992 (1) ACC 603; 1992 (1) ACJ 300; AIR 1992 SC 1261; AIR 1992 SCW 1213; 1992 (19) ALR 454; JT 1992 (2) SC 409; 1992(1) SCALE 662; [1992] 2 S.C.R. 272; 1992 (1) UJ 666

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Span Of Life

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 110 B - (A) Motor accident - Death - Compensation - Deceased, army officer - Tribunal concluded that being army officer deceased must be spending half of salary on mess expenses and drinks - Whether finding as to spending half of salary proper? - (B) Death - Life expectancy - (C) Compensation - Determination - Whether deduction on account of lump sum payment justified? - Held, (A) Not proper - Personal needs including drinks are supplied at subsidised rates - Finding set aside - (B) Span of life should be taken to be seventy years especially in respect of army officers - (C) Not justified in view of delay in final disposal of litigation and monstrous inflation - Appeal allowed.
