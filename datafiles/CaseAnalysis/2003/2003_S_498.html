Praveen Kumar v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

15 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 865; (2003) 12 SCC 199; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 431; 2003(8) SCALE 642

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Fingerprints

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, s. 313 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 27, 276 - IPC, 1860, ss. 302, 392, 397 - Death sentence - Trial Court awarded death sentence, which was confirmed by HC - Appeal - Prosecution relied on circumstantial evidence in absence of direct evidence - Appellant in possession of jewellery belonging to victims which were found missing immediately after murder and appellant had hidden part of said jewellery in his father's property and pledged a part of jewellery belonging to victims to a finance firm - Held, prosecution has established beyond all reasonable doubt that though evidence upon relied by it is circumstantial evidence, it has established every link in chain of circumstances which is consistent only with guilt of accused - Further held, in such circumstances only sentence that would befit facts of this case is that of extreme penalty of death which was rightly imposed by trial court after due consideration and affirmed by HC also after independent consideration of entire facts of case.
