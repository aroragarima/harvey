Surendranagar District Panchayat v Dahyabhai Amarsinh

Supreme Court of India

25 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

P.P. Naolekar, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 913; (2005) 8 SCC 750; AIR 2006 SC 110; 2006 (2) CalLT(SC) 99; 2006 (2) G.L.R. 1014; JT 2005 (9) SC 54; 2005(8) SCALE 631; 2005 (6) SLR 798; [2005] Supp4 S.C.R. 511

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - Service - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2 (oo), 25B, 25F - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946 - Termination from service - Labour Court directed reinstatement of workman with back wages of 20% from date of reference for non-compliance of ss.25 F, 25 G, 25 H - Appeal against - Held, burden to prove continuous service of 240 days in preceding 12 months lies on workman - In the absence of regular employment of the workman, the appellant was not expected to maintain seniority list of the employees engaged on daily wages and in the absence of any proof by the respondent regarding existence of the seniority list and his so called seniority no relief could be given to him for non-compliance of provisions of the Act - In order to entitle court to draw inference unfavourable to party, court must be satisfied that evidence is in existence and could have been proved - Appeal allowed.
