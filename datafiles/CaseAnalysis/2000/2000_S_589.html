Navinchandra N. Majithia v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 264; (2000) 7 SCC 640; (2001) SCC (Cr) 215; AIR 2000 SC 2966; 2000 (4) AWC 3040; 2000 (5) Bom.C.R. 906; 2000 (3) CCR 164; 2000 (4) CompLJ 193; 2000 (3) Crimes 222; JT 2000 (10) SC 61; 2000 (4) RCR(Criminal) 30; 2000(6) SCALE 262; 2000 (6) SLT 528; [2000] Supp3 S.C.R. 82; 2000 (6) Supreme 114

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, Oil and Natural Gas Commission

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226(2) - Whether writ can run beyond territorial jurisdiction of High Court? - Whether expression 'Cause of action' wholly or in part arises, carries same meaning as given in s. 20, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Whether petition before HC of one State with prayer inter alia to quash FIR filed in another State, is sustainable? - Held, writ can run beyond territorial jurisdiction of HC - Expression carries same meaning as given in s. 20, CPC - Petition before HC of one State to quash FIR filed in another State, is sustainable.
