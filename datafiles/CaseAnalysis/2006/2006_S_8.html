S. Rajeswari v S. N. Kulasekaran and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 304; (2006) 4 SCC 412; 2006 (63) ALR 786; 2006 (2) AWC 2008; 2006 (3) CTC 171; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 279; JT 2006 (4) SC 345; 2006 (2) MLJ(SC) 405; 2006 (4) RCR(Civil) 180; 2006 (101) RD 66; 2006(4) SCALE 9; 2006 (4) SCJ 584; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 610; 2006 (2) UC 1196

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, ss. 115 and 151, O. 21, rr. 97, 98, 100, 101 and 103 - Appellant obstructed execution of decree obtained by respondent No. 1 - Respondent No. 1 filed application before executing court u/s. 151 of CPC, 1908 for certain reliefs - Executing court rejected application - High Court u/s. 115 of CPC, 1908 allowed revision - Whether application filed u/s. 151, CPC, 1908 is maintainable? - Held, since appeal is provided u/O. 21, r. 103 of CPC, 1908 which treated order passed by executing court as a decree subject to same conditions as to appeal against such decree, a revision petition u/s. 115, CPC, 1908 is not maintainable - Appeal allowed.
