Gurmail Singh and Others v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

Baharul Islam, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 45; (1982) 3 SCC 185; (1982) SCC (Cr) 680; AIR 1982 SC 1466; 1982 BBCJ 174; 1982 CRLJ 1946; 1983 CRLR 436; 1982(1) SCALE 708

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 34, 300, 302, 323, 324 and 325 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 27 - Deceased T attempted to intervene in order to stop causing of further harm - Blow with a barchha given by accused G which landed on deceased T found to be fatal - Accused no. 3 caused fracture of phalanx of right index finger by blunt side of gandhali in which three accused including accused no. 3 also suffered injuries - Question of nature of offence and sentence in respect of accused G and question of sentence in respect of remaining three accused - Held, could not be said that accused G intended to cause that particular bodily injury which was found to have been caused - Accused G committed an offence under s. 304, Part II, IPC, 1860 and not under s. 302 - Sentence of 5 years rigorous imprisonment adequate sentence.
