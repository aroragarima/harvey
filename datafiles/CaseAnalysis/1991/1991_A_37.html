State of Bihar and Others v Akhouri Sachindra Nath and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

B.C. Ray, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 264; (1991) Supp1 SCC 334; (1991) SCC (L&S) 1070; AIR 1991 SC 1244; AIR 1991 SCW 1196; 1991 (16) ATC 936; 1991 (62) FLR 874; JT 1991 (2) SC 279; 1991 LabIC 1261; 1991 (2) LLJ 312; 1991 (2) LLN 400; 1991(1) SCALE 748; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 410; 1991 (2) SLR 608

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Challenged

Summary: Service - Seniority - Challenged - Respondents were appointed in Engineering Service Class II as Assistant Engineers - It was alleged that revised seniority list was prepared wherein respondents were shown as juniors - Respondents filed petition before HC challenging the seniority conferred on the respondent Nos. 6 to 23 - HC quashed the orders of the Govt. - Hence instant appeals by State - Whether the order recorded by HC was just and proper.

Held, promotee respondent Nos. 6 to 23 were not born in the cadre of Assistant Engineer in the Engineering Service, Class II at the time when the respondents were directly recruited to the post of Assistant Engineer and as such they cannot be given seniority in the service of Assistant Engineers over the respondent. It is well settled that no person can be promoted with retrospective effect from a date when he was not born in the cadre so as to adversely affect others. It is well settled by several decisions of SC that amongst members of the same grade seniority is reckoned from the date of their initial entry into the service. In other words, seniority inter-se amongst the Assistant Engineers in Engineering Service, Class II will be considered from the date of the length of service rendered as Assistant Engineers. This being the position in law the respondent Nos. 6 to 23 cannot be made senior to the respondents by impugned Govt. orders as they entered into the said Service by promotion after the respondents were directly recruited in the quota of direct recruits. Thus, judgment of HC quashing the impugned Government orders made is unexceptionable. SC confirms the judgment and order rendered by HC. Appeals dismissed.
