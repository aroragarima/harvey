Morarji Goculdas B and W Company Limited v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, S.C. Agrawal, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 115; (1995) Supp3 SCC 588; 1996 (64) ECR 348; 1996 (83) E.L.T. 259

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act,1944, s.11A - Central Excise Rules,1944 - Payment of enormous duty - Appellants/assessees under Act were covered by decision of this Court in J.K. Cotton Spg. and Wvg Mills Ltd case - By said decision this Court upheld as legal and valid the amendments made to rr.9 and 49 of rules - It was argued by appellants that if amendments are given retrospective effect from date Rules were framed, i.e., from 28-2-1944, appellants would be required to pay an enormous amount of duty - Hence instant appeals.

Held, Court appreciated contention that if duty has to be paid with retrospective effect from 1944 it would undoubtedly cause great hardship to assessees but concluded that in view of s.11A of Act such an apprehension was misplaced. Pointing to s.11A(1) of Act, it held that it engrafts a rule of limitation of six months and since proviso to s.11A of Act is not applicable demand though it may include even a demand for more than six months must be made within a period of six months from date of amendment. Having considered facts, in view of Court, it would be appropriate to direct that in cases where notices u/s. 11A of Act have been served and claims are raised within a period of six months from relevant date, Revenue would be entitled to realise dues. In case of dispute as to whether notice u/s.11A had been served or not, Asstt. Collector will decide issue. In cases where notices have not been served as yet Revenue would be entitled to do so within time limit prescribed by s.11A of Act. In either of said eventualities, orders will not be passed by authorities without giving an opportunity to assessee to make representations against proposed orders. If notices have already been served for said purpose, assessees would have to reply or to make a representation in a short period of time. Appeals disposed of.
