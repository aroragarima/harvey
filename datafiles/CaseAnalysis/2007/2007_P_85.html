Hariom Agrawal v Prakash Chand Malviya

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

P.P. Naolekar, B.N. Agrawal, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 989; (2007) 8 SCC 514; AIR 2008 SC 166; 2007 (6) ALD(SC) 105; 2007(6) ALL MR 930; 2007 (69) ALR 488; 2007 (4) AWC 4121; 2008 (3) CTC 457; JT 2007 (12) SC 49; 2007 (4) RCR(Civil) 548; 2007 (103) RD 635; 2007(11) SCALE 705; 2007 (7) SCJ 744; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 772

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Indian Stamp (Madhya Pradesh Amendment) Act, 1990, Madhya Pradesh Accommodation Control Act, Madhya Pradesh Stamp Rules, 1942

Summary: Indian Stamp Act, 1899; Madhya Pradesh Accommodation Control Act, s. 12(1) - Whether the court can impound the photocopy of the instrument (document) of improper description exercising its power under the provisions of the Indian Stamp Act, 1899? - Held, an instrument which is not duly stamped can be impounded and when the required fee and penalty has been paid for such instrument it can be taken in evidence under s. 35 of the Stamp Act - Appeal dismissed.
