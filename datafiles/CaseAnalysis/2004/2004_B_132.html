Jimmy Jahangir Madan v Bolly Cariyappa Hindley (Dead) By Lrs.

Supreme Court of India

04 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 904; (2004) 12 SCC 509; AIR 2005 SC 48; 2005 (1) BC 16; [2005] 126 Comp Cas 385; 2005 (2) CompLJ 68; 2005 CRLJ 112; JT 2004 (9) SC 558; 2004 (3) KLT 850; 2005 (141) PLR 209; 2004 (4) RCR(Criminal) 1001; 2004(9) SCALE 297; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 955; 2005 (1) UC 489

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 2(q), 302 - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 - Complaint - General Power of attorney - Maintainability - Complainant filed a complaint against appellant u/s. 138 of the 1881 Act - During Trial complainant died leaving behind her children who were residing aboard - General Power of attorney was executed by children in favour of Mr. X and Mrs. Y who filed applications u/s. 302 CrPC to continue prosecution before Trial Court which were allowed - Aggrieved by same revision petition's were filled before HC which were dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether application u/s. 302 CrPC was maintainable - Held, neither heirs of complainant filed petition u/s. 302 CrPC to continue prosecution nor any permission was sought by them from competent Court that they should be allowed to continue prosecution through their power of attorney holders, rather the prayer was made by the power of attorney holders, which is not permissible under law - Further, Trial Court was not justified in allowing petitions u/s. 302 CrPC and HC had committed an error in confirming said order which was set aside and petitions u/s. 302 CrPC were fit to be dismissed giving liberty to heirs either to make application themselves before Court concerned to continue prosecution or apply to Court to grant permission to them to authorize power of attorney holders to continue prosecution on their behalf - Appeals allowed.
