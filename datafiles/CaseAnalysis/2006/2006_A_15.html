A.P. Foods v S. Samuel and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 287; (2006) 5 SCC 469; AIR 2006 SC 3622; 2006 (7) AWC 7192; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 246; JT 2006 (6) SC 97; 2006(6) SCALE 533; 2006 (5) SLR 5; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 203; 2006 (3) UPLBEC 2252

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 9-A, 10 - Payment of Bonus Act, 1965 - Public Societies Registration (Telengana Area) Act, 1350 Fasli - Ex-gratia - Non-Payment - Alternative Remedy - Sustainability - Appellant-Nutrition Council was registered under the Andhra Pradesh (Telengana Area) Public Societies Registration Act, 2001 - In January, 1986, Executive Committee of Nutrition Council decided to sanction ex-gratia of one month's salary each year in lieu of bonus to employees - Subsequently, State directed its various Organisations not to pay ex-gratia until further orders - Consequently, appellant issued directions by its Circular dt. 24-11-1993 that payment of ex-gratia to employees should stand withdrawn until further orders - Writ Petition was filed by employees making grievance that stoppage of ex- gratia/bonus was unauthorized and contrary to law - HC allowed writ petition - Appellant filed appeal before DB, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether employees were entitled to bonus was industrial dispute and writ petition should not be entertained - Held, writ petition u/art. 226 of Constitution should not be entertained when statutory remedy was available under the 1947 Act, unless exceptional circumstances were made out - Both Single Judge and DB had failed to consider basic issues - In normal course Court would have left it to respondent to avail appropriate remedy under the 1947 Act - Bare reading of s. 22 of the 1947 Act made position clear that if dispute was arisen between employer and employees with respect to bonus payable under the 1947 Act or with respect to application of the 1947 Act in public sector then such dispute should be deemed to be industrial dispute within meaning of the 1947 Act - Hence, if alternative remedy was available under the 1947 Act, HC should not have entertained writ petition, and should have directed writ-petitioners to avail statutory remedy - Appeal allowed.
