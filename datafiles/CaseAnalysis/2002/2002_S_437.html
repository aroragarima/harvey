K. M. Ravi and others v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2367; (2009) 16 SCC 337

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: (A) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 143, 144, 148, 149, 325, 302 - Various charges - Acquittal - Challenge - Conviction - Validity - Charges on appellants/accused related offences u/ss. 143, 144, 148, 325, 302 r/w s. 149 of IPC - Trial Judge after noticing certain discrepancies, omissions and exaggerations in evidence found accused not guilty and so them acquitted - On appeal before, HC Judges of Division Bench proceeded to appreciate evidence independently and on basis of evidence of PWs 1, 2, 4, 10 convicted all accused for various charges, framed against them - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether conclusion of HC that exercise undertaken by trial court in that regard was erroneous was proper - Held, there was no justification to make once again elaborate reference to all those materials in detail, as reference to various materials had been made at considerable length, both by trial Judge as well as HC, in their own respective manner to justify conclusions arrived at by them - However, so far as evidence of eyewitnesses PWs 1, 2, 4, 10 were concerned it was found that Judges of HC that trial court committed grave error in making outright rejection of entire evidence and that reasons assigned for such total rejection were against all well-settled norms and principles of appreciation of evidence - Thus, conclusion of HC that exercise undertaken by trial court in that regard was erroneous and its conclusions were vitiated on account of perversity of approach was well merited entitling, HC to proceed with independent appraisal and assessment of evidence - Appeal disposed of.

(B) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 149, 324 - Offences - Requirement of witnesses - Conviction - Validity - Whether evidence of PWs 1, 2, 4, 10 sufficiently made out offence, with which all accused had been charged as was required in law - Held, Judges of HC though rightly interfered with acquittal of Accused no. 2, same could not be said of reversal of finding of acquittal in respect of other accused including Accused no. 4 whose conviction also could only be sustained in respect of offence u/s. 324 of IPC for causing injury to PW 1 - However, attempt to connect all accused with murder of deceased invoking s. 149 IPC on basis of parrot-like repetition of alleged exhortation to finish deceased on his arrival at place of worship seemed to be far-fetched - Further, there seemed to be legally acceptable material to prove, all accused acted as members of unlawful assembly, and except that they were found to be closely related nothing concrete to join them to connect with murder of deceased had come on record to attract application of s. 149 of IPC - Therefore, so far as appellants 1, 3 to 8 were concerned, judgment of HC was set aside and their acquittal was restored and regarding accused no. 2 he was convicted u/s. 324 of IPC - Appeals disposed of.
