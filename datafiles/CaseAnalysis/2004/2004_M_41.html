M.P. Vidyut Karamchari Sangh v M.P. Electricity Board

Supreme Court of India

18 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 198; (2004) SCC (L&S) 754; (2004) 9 SCC 755; AIR 2004 SC 2974; 2004 (55) ALR 18; 2004 (5) AWC 4600; 2004 (101) FLR 670; 2004 (3) JhrCR(SC) 41; JT 2004 (3) SC 423; 2004 (2) LLJ 470; 2004 (2) LLN 814; 2004(3) SCALE 383; 2004 (3) SLR 43; 2004 (2) UPLBEC 1313

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Fundamental and Supplementary Rules, Revised Leave Rules, Madhya Pradesh Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1961, Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946, Industrial Relations Act, Madhya Pradesh Civil Services (Temporary Service) Rules Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, Fundamental (Amendment) Rules, 1998

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 245, 246, 248, 254, 254(2), 309 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 19, 19(2) - Industrial Relations Act, ss. 31, 31(1), 31(2), 33 - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, ss. 15, 79(c) - Madhya Pradesh Industrial Relations Act, 1960, ss. 33, 99 - Madhya Pradesh Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1961, ss. 2, 2(2) - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946 - Madhya Pradesh Civil Services (Temporary Service) Rules Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, r. 14A - Fundamental and Supplementary Rules - Revised Leave Rules - Fundamental (Amendment) Rules, 1998 - Entire field relating to 'Electricity' is covered under Entry 38 of List III - State's legislative competence to exercise its legislative power under Entries 22, 23 and 24 not taken away - s.79(c), Electricity (Supply) Act provides for incidental power upon Board - Would not prevail over specific legislative competence granted to State to regulate conditions of service between an industrial undertaking and its employees nor thereby State Govt. would be denuded of its legislative power relating to regulation of industrial relations - Regulation made u/s. 79(c) of Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, as regard age of superannuation of an employee of an employee - Would prevail over agreement - HC committed no error in dismissing appeal.
