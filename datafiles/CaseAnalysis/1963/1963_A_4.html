South Indian Bank Limited v A.R. Chacko

Supreme Court of India

02 December 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 107; AIR 1964 SC 1522; 1964 (8) FLR 128; 1964 (1) LLJ 19; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 625

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Period Of Operation, Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950, Industrial Disputes (Banking Companies) Decision Act, 1955, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(5)(iv), 7, 19(3), 19(6), 33 C(2) and sch. II - Industrial Disputes (Banking Companies) Decision Act, 1955, s.4 - (A) Whether Industrial Tribunal has jurisdiction to deal with an application made u/s. 33 C (2)?- Held, s. 33 C(2) assign determination of amount of benefit to which workman is entitled to receive from employer and which is capable of being computed in terms of money to such Labour Court as may be specified in this behalf by appropriate Govt. - Thus, Labour court and not Industrial Tribunal, has jurisdiction to deal with an application made to it under S. 33 C(2) of Act - (B) What is difference between an award being in operation and an award being binding on parties according to s. 19(6) of Act?- Further held, after period of operation of an award has expired, award does not cease to be effective- For, it continues to be binding thereafter on parties until notice has been given by one of parties of intention to terminate it and two months have elapsed from date of such notice -(C) Whether an award which ceased to be in operation or in force and has ceased to be binding on parties under provisions of s. 19(6) would still be operative? - Further held, yes - It would be but as a contract between parties that has been made by industrial adjudication in place of old contract till this new contract is displaced by a fresh contract - (D) Whether a workman appointed as accountant doing supervisory work would be workmen u/s. 2(5)(iv) of I D Act?- Further held, a consideration of evidence on record as regards duties actually performed by a particular workman may lead to conclusion that he was merely a senior clerk, doing mainly clerical duties, and going by designation accountant and was in reality a workman as defined in Industrial Disputes Act and doing an element of supervisory work - So he does not cease to be a workman on his being appointed as an accountant - Appeal dismissed.
