Commissioner Of Income Tax v Mussadilal Ram Bharose

Supreme Court of India

28 January 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28123; (1987) 2 SCC 39; (1987) SCC (Tax) 173; AIR 1987 SC 814; 1987 (1) BankCLR 73; 1987 (60) CTR 34; [1987] 165 ITR 14; JT 1987 (1) SC 307; 1987(1) SCALE 196; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 67; 1987 TAXLR 469; [1987] 30 TAXMAN 546; 1987 (1) UJ 521

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss.256(1)(2), 271(1)(c) and 274(2) - A/y 1965-66 - Assessee was a licence vendor of country liquor - For the a/y 1965-66, the Income Tax Officer (ITO) rejected its account books on the ground that sales and expenses were not verified and the margin of profit shown was low - ITO adopted the net profit rate at 8% thereby computing the profit at Rs. 60,800/- and the total income was computed at Rs.60,936/- after addition of Rs. 136/- for interest receipts - On appeal, the Appellate Asst. Commissioner confirmed the order of the ITO - As the total income returned was less than 80% of the correct income computed, the case fell within the ambit of the Explanation to s. 271(1) of the Act - Appellate Asst. Commissioner issued show cause notice u/s. 274 r/w. 271 to the assessee - On appeal, Tribunal cancelled the penalty order and determined the income of the assessee at Rs. 50,750/- holding that the assessee had maintained certain types of books of account and had honestly believed that the same were sufficient for the true ascertainment of his profits - Revenue filed application before HC seeking a reference on the question of cancelling the penalty imposed u/s. 271(1)(c) of the Act - Said application of the revenue was dismissed by the HC on the ground that the assessee acted honestly - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, presumption raised u/s. 271(1)(c) of the Act are rebuttable presumptions - The initial burden of discharging the onus of rebuttal is on the assessee - Once the initial burden is discharged, the assessee would be out of the mischief unless further evidence was adduced - If the returned income is less that 80% of the assessed income, the presumption is raised against the assessee that the assessee is guilty of wilful neglect or of fraud or gross or wilful neglect as a result of which he has concealed the income but this presumption can be rebutted - In the present case, the Tribunal has borne in mind the relevant principles of law and has also judged the facts on record - Moreover, it is not a case that there was no evidence or there was such evidence on which no reasonable man could have accepted the explanation of the assessee - Hence, tribunal rightly rejected the claim for reference u/s. 256(1) of the Act and the HC correctly did not entertain the application for reference u/s. 256(2) of the Act - Revenue's appeal dismissed.
