State of Haryana v Chandra Mani and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3177; (1996) 3 SCC 132; 1996 (3) AD(SC) 1; AIR 1996 SC 1623; AIR 1996 SCW 1672; 1996 (3) CCC 25; 1996 (2) CLR 504; 1996 (3) CLT 62; 2002 (143) E.L.T. 249; JT 1996 (3) SC 371; 1996 (2) MLJ(SC) 137B; 1996 (1) RentLR 735; 1996(2) SCALE 820; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 1060; 1996 (5) Supreme 75; 1996 (2) UJ 105

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Limitation Act of 1963, s. 5 - State apply for condonation of delay - Delay of 109 days - Whether sufficient cause? - Considering Govt. as an impersonal machinery - Decisions taken at slow pace - Held, case is fit for condonation of delay - Govt. should constitute legal cells to examine cases - Appropriate officers should be made responsible for filing appeal and lapses in them.
