State of Madhya Pradesh and others v Premlal Shrivas

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 594; (2011) 9 SCC 664; 2012 (1) AWC 300; 2011 (131) FLR 361; 2011 (3) JabLJ 241; JT 2011 (10) SC 519; 2011(10) SCALE 600; [2011] 11 S.C.R. 444; 2011 (6) SLR 680

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Madhya Pradesh Financial Code, r. 84 - Correction of service records - Permissibility - Respondent Head Constable filed representation to appellant authority to correct his father's name and date of birth in the service record - Appellant rejected representation on the ground that that the service record of the respondent was prepared on the instructions of his maternal grandfather, accompanying the respondent at the time of enrolment, the same carried his finger and thumb impressions and was duly attested by the then Superintendent of Police - Respondent filed application before the State Administrative Tribunal - Tribunal dismissed application - Respondent filed writ petition - HC, by impugned order, directed the appellants to correct the service records of the respondent - Appellant contended that HC ought not to have directed a change in date of birth of the respondent, on his request, made after a lapse of over two decades of his joining the service - Appellant further contended that as per r. 84 of the Code, the date of birth recorded in the service record was conclusive and only a bonafide clerical mistake in the said record could be corrected - Held, no evidence had been placed on record by the respondent to show that the wrong date of birth recorded was due to the negligence of some other person - Respondent had failed to show that the date of birth was recorded incorrectly, due to want of care on the part of some other person, despite the fact that a correct date of birth had been shown on the documents presented or signed by him - In such circumstances, the HC ought not to have directed the appellants to correct the date of birth of the respondent u/r. 84 of the Code - Hence, impugned HC order holding that the respondent was entitled to get his date of birth corrected in the service record, could not be sustained and hence set aside - Appeal allowed.
