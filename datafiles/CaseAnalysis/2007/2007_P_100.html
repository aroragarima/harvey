Binapani Paul v Pratima Ghosh And Others

Supreme Court of India

27 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 461; (2007) 6 SCC 100; AIR 2008 SC 543; 2007 (5) ALD(SC) 113; 2007 (6) AWC 5472; 2007 (3) CHN 78; JT 2007 (7) SC 458; 2007 (4) MLJ(SC) 1076; 2007 (147) PLR 357; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 801; 2007 (492) SCJ 492; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 946

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Hindu Women's Right To Property Act, 1937, Dayabhaga

Summary: Appeal against the judgement of High court setting aside the decree of trial court wherein it was held that the suit property was purchased for the benefit of wife and the case was not of benami transaction - Contention of the appellant that the transaction was not a benami transaction- Held, Primary motive of the transaction was security for the wife and seven minor daughters as they were not protected by the law as then prevailing, the legal position obtaining at the relevant time may be considered to be a relevant factor for proving peculiar circumstances existing, Dayabhaga does not prohibit gift of immovable property in favour of his wife by her husband. It merely says that Dayabhaga did not recognize it to be her stridhan, it was only for the purpose of inheritance and succession, the same has nothing to do with the Benami Transaction of the Property and to determine the nature of transaction - Appeal allowed.
