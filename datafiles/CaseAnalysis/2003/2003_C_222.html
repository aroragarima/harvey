M/s. Hindustan Metal Pressing Works v Commissioner of Central Excise, Pune

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 208; (2003) 3 SCC 559; AIR 2003 SC 1775; 2003 (86) ECC 1; 2003 (108) ECR 25; 2003 (153) E.L.T. 11; 2003 (2) JhrCR(SC) 50; 2003 (3) JhrCR(SC) 91; JT 2003 (2) SC 272; 2003 (1) KHCACJ 589; 2003 (2) RCR(Civil) 210; 2003 (55) RLT 259; 2003(2) SCALE 474; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 404; 2003 (2) SLT 469; 2003 (2) Supreme 289

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Trade

Summary: Central Excise Act, 1944 - duty paid in excess of effective rate of duty and the excess duty paid refunded while assessing the RT-12 Returns - whether the principles of 'unjust enrichment' as incorporated in amended provisions would be applicable to the facts of the present case? - Held, there is basic error in approach by the Authorities below as the assessee has not filed any application under Section 11B of the Act for refund of the excise duty paid by him. - No question of application of principles of unjust enrichment as incorporated in Section 11B.
