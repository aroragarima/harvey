Indian Overseas Bank Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 220; (1970) 2 SCC 4; AIR 1970 SC 1530; [1970] 77 ITR 512; 1971 (1) MLJ 57; 1971 (1) MLJ(SC) 57; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 348

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Banking & Finance; Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Banking Companies Act, 1949, s. 17 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 10(2) and 66 - Development Rebate - Claim of - Whether creation of a reserve in compliance with s. 17 of 1949 Act is sufficient compliance with requirements of s. 10(2)(vi)(b), proviso (b), of Indian Income Tax Act, 1922? - Held, appellant claimed allowance by way of development rebate under proviso (b) of s. 10(2)(vi)(b) in computation of its business income - Appellant contended that as transfer to reserve is sufficient to meet requirements of s. 17 of Banking Regulation Act, 1949 as well as of proviso (b) to s. 10(2)(vi)(b) of Income Tax Act, 1922 - Appellant is not entitled to allowance by way of development rebate claimed - Rebate under proviso (b) of s. 10(2)(vi)(b) is a concession granted but that concession is made subject to fulfillment of certain requirements - Grant of allowance is made subject to conditions prescribed in proviso (b) to Explanation (2) to s. 10(2)(vi)(b) - Entries in account books required by proviso are not an idle formality - Appeal dismissed.
