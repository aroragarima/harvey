Moti Lal Chunnilal (Tak) v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1670; (1998) 9 SCC 401; 1999 (153) CTR 256; [1998] 234 ITR 472; [1999] 102 TAXMAN 571

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 185 - Registration of Firm - Public Policy - Excise Duty - Permission for License - License holder did not take permission of licensing officer before entering into any partnership agreement and claimed benefit of registration from respondent even without paying excise duty - Revenue refused registration whose action was affirmed by Tribunal and HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether firm entitled to registration even though constituted in violation of excise law - Held, no - License-holder to take prior permission of licensing officer before entering into any partnership agreement was made a condition of excise licence in order to ensure collection of dues of Excise Department - License holder cannot claim benefit of registration of a firm formed in violation of an express condition of license and It will clearly be against public policy to grant - Benefit of registration to a firm under the Act cannot be granted as the firm was set up in violation of an express condition of excise license specially when condition s inserted to ensure full payment of excise duty - Appeal allowed.
