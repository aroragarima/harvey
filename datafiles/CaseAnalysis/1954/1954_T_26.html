Â Dhirubha Devisingh Gohil v State of Bombay[With Connected Appeals]

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Jagannadhadas, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Vivian Bose, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 64; AIR 1955 SC 47; 1955 (57) BomLR 718; 1955 SCJ 1; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 691

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Land Revenue Code - Bombay Taluqdari Tenure Abolition Act, 1949 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1950 - Government of India Act, 1935, s. 299 - Whether Bombay Taluqdari Tenure Abolition Act, 1949 is valid in law or in that it violates provisions of s. 299 of Government of India Act? - Held, this is clearly a case where concerned right which was secured u/s. 299 of Government of India Act in the form of a fetter on competency of the Legislature and which in substance was a fundamental right, was lifted into formal category of a fundamental right along with other fundamental rights recognised in present Constitution - There is therefore nothing inappropriate in referring to this right which was pre-existing, along with other fundamental rights for the first time secured by this Constitution, when grouping them together, as fundamental rights 'conferred' by Constitution - It will be illogical to construe art. 31-B as affording protection only so far as these rights are taken away by an Act in violation of provisions of new Constitution but not when they are taken away by an Act in violation of s. 299 of Government of India Act which has been repealed - Appeals dismissed.
