Arnit Das v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 443; (2000) 5 SCC 488; (2000) SCC (Cr) 962; AIR 2000 SC 2264; AIR 2000 SCW 2037; 2000 (41) AllCriC 191; 2000 (3) AWC 2420; 2000 (2) CCR 140; 2000 (2) Crimes 334; 2000 CRLJ 2971; 2000 (2) JCC 545; JT 2000 (6) SC 320; 2000 (3) RCR(Criminal) 17; 2000(4) SCALE 515; 2000 (4) SLT 465; [2000] Supp1 S.C.R. 69; 2000 (4) Supreme 186

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Human Rights; Women & Children

Summary: (1) Juvenile Justice Act, 1986-Sections 2 (d) and 2 (h)-Juvenile-Determination of age-Date by reference to which age is to be determined-Scheme of Act contemplates its applicability coming into play only when person may appear or be brought before competent authority-Competent authority in relation to delinquent juvenile-Juvenile Court and where no such Court constituted, it includes any Court empowered under Section 7 (2)-Where juvenile charged with offence appears or is produced before Juvenile Court-Juvenile Court has to hold enquiry in accordance with provisions of Section 39-Thus, enquiry as to age of juvenile has to be made only when he is brought or appears before competent authority- Competent authority to hold enquiry as to that of that person for determining same by reference to date of appearance of that person before it or by reference to date when person brought before it-It is irrelevant what was age of person on date of commission of offence-Hence crucial date for determining question whether a person is juvenile?-Is date when he is brought before competent authority/Juvenile Court.

(2) Interpretation of Statutes-Aid-Preamble and Statement of Objects and Reasons-Can be taken into account for resolving ambiguity.

(3) Constitution of India-Article 141-Binding effect of law declared by Supreme Court-Decision not expressed, not accompanied by reasons and not proceeding on conscious consideration of issue-Cannot be law declared having binding effect.
