K.R. Anitha and Others v Regional Director, E.S.I. Corporation and Another

Supreme Court of India

17 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 754; (2003) 10 SCC 303; AIR 2003 SC 3393; 2003 (3) CLR 612; 2003 (99) FLR 341; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 359; 2003 (4) KHCACJ 365; 2003 (3) KLT 813; 2003 LabIC 3497; 2003 (3) LLJ 855; 2003 (4) LLN 420; 2003(7) SCALE 604; 2003 (1) SCW 4811; 2003 (6) SLR 449; 2003 (5) SLT 635; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 881; 2003 (6) Supreme 775; 2003 (3) UPLBEC 2682

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Insurance; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Employee's State Insurance Act, 1948 - Applicability of ESI Scheme to employees working in toddy shops - The contention of the appellant was that the primary responsibility to pay contribution was that of the Excise Department being the principal employer, if the Act is applicable to toddy shops - Held, the High Court was right in holding that toddy shop is a premises where the business of buying and selling is going on. Therefore, a toddy shop would come under the entry 'shop' in the schedule - Further held, the High Court was right in its conclusion that the said proviso did not cover the toddy shops of the appellants and the neither the State Government nor the Excise Department came in the picture of management of the business of the appellants. It is clear from the facts that the State had no participation in terms of finance and there was no Government participation in carrying on the business of liquor by the appellants either functionally or administratively.
