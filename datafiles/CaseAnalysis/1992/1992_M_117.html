Shyam Kishore and Others v Municipal Corporation of Delhi and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 September 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, Veeraswami Ramaswami, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 416; (1993) 1 SCC 22; AIR 1992 SC 2279; JT 1992 (5) SC 335; 1992(2) SCALE 403; [1992] Supp1 S.C.R. 349

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution - Delhi Municipal Corporation Act, 1957, ss. 126(1), 156, 170(b) and 457 - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226, 227 - Property tax - (A) Municipalities - Increase in rateable value of property - Proceedings for - Period of completion - (B) Levy of Property Tax - Appeal u/s. 170(b) - Scope and nature - (C) Constitution - Alternative remedy - A more satisfactory solution is available on terms of statute itself - Resort to Arts. 226 and 227 by way of writ petition - Whether proper? - Held - (A) Proceedings in pursuance of notice must not be completed within year in which notice is given to become effective from year in which notice is given - (B) Intra vires - Can be admitted or entertained - Cannot be heard or disposed of without pre-deposit of disputed tax - Appellate authority can adjourn hearing on appeal for giving time to deposit tax - Cannot stay recovery of tax till disposal of appeal - (C) Not proper - Order accordingly.
