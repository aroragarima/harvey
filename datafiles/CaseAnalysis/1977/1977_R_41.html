Â Rev. Stainislaus v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg, R.S. Sarkaria, P.N. Shingal, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 284; (1977) SCC (Cr) 147; (1977) 1 SCC 677; AIR 1977 SC 908; 1977 CRLJ 551; 1977 MLJ 320; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 611; 1977 UJ 156

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Constitution, art. 25(1), sch. 7, list 2, entry 1, list 1, entry 7 - M.P. Dharma Swatantraya Adhiniyam, 1968, ss. 3, 4 and 5(2) - Orissa Freedom of Religion Act, 1968, s. 1 - (A) Freedom of religion - Meaning of right to propagate one's religion - (B) meaning of public order - Legislation prohibiting conversion by force or inducement to one's own religion - Competency - Held, there is no fundamental right to convert any person to one's own religion - State Legislature is competent to pass - Appeal No.C.As. Nos. 1489 &1511 of 1974 and Cr. A. No. 255 of 1974 Dismissed; Appeal No.C.As. Nos. 344 - 346 of 1976 Allowed.
