State Of Madya Pradesh And Ors. v Sri S.S. Kourav And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

19 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1464; (1995) 3 SCC 270; (1995) SCC (L&S) 666; AIR 1995 SC 1056; 1995 (29) ATC 553; 1995 (2) CLR 237; 1995 (70) FLR 1060; JT 1995 (2) SC 498; 1995 LabIC 1574; 1995 (2) LLJ 849; 1995 (2) LLN 1118; 1995(1) SCALE 511; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 482; 1995 (2) SLJ 109; 1995 (2) SLR 16; 1995 (1) UJ 617

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Advocates & Judges - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 - Transfer of officer during imposition of President's rule - Necessity of approval of Governor - (A) Whether courts and tribunals can decide on transfers made on administrative grounds and go into expediency of posting officer at particular place - (B) Whether order signed by delegate of Governor is legal and valid - Held, (A) Not for appellate forum to decide on transfers matters and cannot go into expediency of posting - (B) Order is legal and valid - Appeal allowed.
