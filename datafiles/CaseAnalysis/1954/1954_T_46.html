Â Messrs. Dwarka Prasad Laxmi Narain v State of Uttar Pradesh and Two Others

Supreme Court of India

11 January 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Vivian Bose, Ghulam Hasan, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 60; AIR 1954 SC 224; 1954 ALJ 203; 1954 SCJ 238; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 803

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Abuse Of Power

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19(1)(g) and 19(6) - Uttar Pradesh Coal Control Order (1953), cls. 3(2)(b), 4(3), 7 and 8 - Petitioners are a firm of traders- Retail sellers of coal at a coal depot - Cancellation of license in pursuance of Coal Control Order - Power of granting or withholding licences of licensing authority in question -(A)Whether U P Coal Control Order, 1953, cl. 3(2)(b) is reasonable? - As u/cl. 3(2)(b) of Control Order an unrestricted power has been given to State Controller to make exemptions and even if he acts arbitrarily or from improper motives, there is no check over it and no way of obtaining redress - Held, cl. 3(2)(b) of Control Order is, prima facie unreasonable - However, this portion of Control Order, even though bad, is severable from rest - (B) Whether cl. 4(3) of Control Order is constitutionally valid? - Further held, provision of cl. 4(3) is void it imposes unreasonable restriction upon freedom of trade and business guaranteed u/art. 19(1)(g) of Constitution and not coming within protection afforded by art. 19(6) of Constitution - Petition allowed.
