S.Vasudeva Etc. Etc. v State Of Karnataka And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1017; (1993) 3 SCC 467; AIR 1994 SC 923; JT 1993 (2) SC 465; 1993 (2) MLJ(SC) 140; 1993(2) SCALE 244; [1993] 2 S.C.R. 715

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Land & Property; Tort

Keywords: Debt Laws, Appointed Day, Real Estate, Land Acquisition And Requisition, Land Ceiling

Summary: Land & Property - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976, ss. 20(1)(a) and 20(1)(b) - Vacant land in excess of ceiling limit - (A) Whether exemption from provisions of chap. III, can be granted for purpose of - (1) Transferring land for whatever reasons including undue hardship to landholder - (2) Paramount consideration u/cl. (a) of s. 20(1) is public interest - (3) Transfer of land either u/cls. (a) or (b) - (B) Object of Act - Held, cannot be granted - Conditions may be imposed, but it is not obligatory, nor necessary to record reasons for granting exemption - Purpose cannot be transfer of land either u/cls. (a) or (b) - Object is to acquire all vacant land in excess of ceiling limit and harmonious reading of all provisions consistent with that intention is necessary - Appeal allowed.
