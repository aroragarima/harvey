S. Surjit Singh Kalra and Others v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 February 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, L.M. Sharma, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 1057; (1991) 2 SCC 87; JT 1991 (1) SC 417; 1991 (1) RCJ 357; 1991 (1) RCR(Rent) 347; 1991 (1) RentLR 206; 1991(1) SCALE 179; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 364; 1991 (1) UJ 722

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Indirect Tax; Rent Control; Service

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Police Act, 1978 - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958 - s. 14 - Whether the landlord is rightful to evict the tenant u/s. 14-B of the Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958 and whether the tenant has the corresponding right to resist the eviction proceedings? - Held, The landlord cannot be denied possession of his own premises under s. 14-B when he is residing in a rented premises - The persons who have already retired may within one year from the date of their release or retirement from such Armed Forces or, within a period of one year from the date of introduction of s. 14-B, whichever is later apply to the Controller for recovering the immediate possession of their premises - Appeals disposed of.
