Hanumappa Channappa Hullur (Dead) by Lrs. v Shivamaruthappa Parappa Kalli and others

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

C. Nagappan, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 994; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 203; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 798

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Agreement Of Sale, Specific Relief Act, 1963, Joint Family

Summary: Land & Property - Specific Relief Act, 1963, ss. 12, 12(3) - Suit Land - Order Challenged - Appellant/plaintiff entered into agreement of sale of land with respondent/defendant no.1 and filed suit for specific performance of contract - Further, respondents/defendant nos. 2-3 filed suit against appellant for declaration that agreement of sale executed by their father, defendant no.1 was not binding and claimed 1/3rd share in suit land - Trial Court decreed suit in favour of plaintiff and dismiss suit that of defendant nos. 2-3 - First Appellate Court dismissed appeals of defendant nos. 2-3 and HC allowed appeal of defendant nos. 2-3, thereby decreed suit as prayed - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, it is well established principle of equity that where, in the course of concluding a contract, a person has represented that he can grant a certain property, or is entitled to a certain interest in that property, and it later appears that there is a deficiency in his title or interest, the other party can obtain an order compelling him to grant what he has got, and in an appropriate case, to submit to a reduction of the consideration for the grant. Further, even where any property is held jointly, and once any party to the contract has agreed to sell such joint property by agreement, then, even if the other co-sharer has not joined, at least to the extent of his share, he is bound to execute the sale deed. However, in the absence of the other co-sharer, there could not be any decree of any specified part of the property to be partitioned and possession given. The decree could only be to the extent of transferring the share of the appellants in such property to other such contracting party. In instant case, there is no impediment for enforcement of the sale agreement against vendor-father-defendant no.1 to the extent of his 1/3rd share in the suit property. Hence, impugned judgment of the HC dismissing the suit seeking for specific performance is set aside and suit is partly decreed to extent of 13rd share of defendant no.1. Appeals disposed of.
