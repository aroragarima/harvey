Commissioner Of Income Tax, Kanpur v The Elgin Mills Ltd., Kanpur

Supreme Court of India

31 July 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 819; (1986) 3 SCC 655; (1986) SCC (Tax) 749; AIR 1986 SC 1938; 1986 (58) CTR 188; [1986] 161 ITR 733; JT 1986 SC 12; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 408; 1986 (82) TAXATION 88

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Depreciation Reserve, Super Profit Tax Act, 1963

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Super Profit Tax Act, 1963, s. 2(9) - Companies (Profits) Surtax Act, 1964- Assessment - Depreciation - Maintainability - Assessment under Provisions of Companies Profits (Surtax) Act, 1964 dispute arose between assessee and revenue with regard to computation of 'Standard deductions' - Company claimed that amounts should be treated as reserves for computation of its capital - Tribunal held that accounts in question represented reserves under latter Act also - Commissioner made reference to HC - HC found that there were no additions to those funds during relevant previous year inasmuch as amount standing in those accounts were being brought forward from year to year - In those circumstances Tribunal was right in deciding admissibility of three accounts in question on principle application to Super Profits Tax Act, 1963- Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, HC was not right in holding that forfeited dividend reserve was reserve - HC should have followed in this respect its previous decision in respect of forfeited dividend reserve in Commissioner of Income-Tax v. British India Corporation, 1972 Indlaw ALL 78- Further, five items as mentioned i.e. investment reserve, rehabilitation reserve, capital reserve, depreciation reserve and forfeited dividends Court was of opinion that first four items constituted reserves and were entitled to be treated as such under Act and forfeited dividends did not represent reserve - HC in its order had excluded 'Forfeited dividend account' from reserve and was right - Appeals disposed of.
