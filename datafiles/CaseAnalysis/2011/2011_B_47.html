State Bank of India and others v Bidyut Kumar Mitra and others

Supreme Court of India

11 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, B. Sudarshan Reddy

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 41; (2011) 2 SCC 316; 2011 (130) FLR 328; JT 2011 (1) SC 146; 2011 (1) LLJ 735; 2011 (4) MLJ(SC) 562; 2011(1) SCALE 390; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 298; 2011 (1) SLR 635

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: State Bank of India (Supervising Staff) Service Rules

Summary: Service - State Bank of India (Supervising Staff) Service Rules, rr. 32(3), 32(4) - Dereliction of duty - Dismissal - Legality - Respondent/Branch Manager of appellant/Bank allegedly sanctioned mid-term loans to a large number of transport owners improperly - Appellant was charge-sheeted for negligence and contravention of rr. 32(3), 32(4) of the Rules - Enquiry Officer, during the inquiry, instructed the respondent to file list of witnesses and documents - Respondent informed the Enquiry Officer that since most of the witnesses were officers of the appellant/Bank and documents were in the possession of the management of the Bank, he was unable to produce the same in enquiry - Respondent requested the Enquiry Officer to summon the documents and witnesses for the purpose of enquiry - Enquiry Officer rejected respondent's request and held that respondent was negligent in disbursing the mid-term loans and thereby caused financial loss to the Bank - Having accepted the enquiry report, Appointing Authority dismissed respondent from service - Respondent's appeal and review thereafter were dismissed - Respondent filed writ petition before the HC - Single Judge dismissed writ petition, however, DB, in appeal, set aside the Single Judge order and quashed the enquiry report and all consequential orders on the ground of violation of principles of natural justice due to non-supply of documents which were in the custody of appellant/Bank - Hence the present appeal by appellant/Bank - Whether there was any violation of principles of natural justice - Held, DB erroneously proceeded to presume that there has been either any breach of the statutory rules or violation of rules of natural justice - DB also failed to take into consideration that the issue with regard to the non-supply of the documents was not even canvassed before the Single Judge at the time of arguments - Further, DB totally ignored the fact that the respondent did not care to raise the issue of non-supply of the documents during the entire course of the enquiry proceedings - Respondent failed to submit his list of documents and witnesses which he wanted to produce for the purpose of his defence within the date stipulated by the Inquiring Authority - Review Committee's order indicated that even though the grievance was made belatedly, the same was duly considered by the highest authority of the Bank - Respondent failed to point out as to what prejudice had been caused to him during the course of the enquiry - In such circumstances, DB was wholly unjustified in setting aside the entire disciplinary proceedings and the findings recorded by the Single Judge - Since the respondent failed to prove any prejudice caused which has resulted in miscarriage of justice, DB order is set aside - Appeal allowed.
