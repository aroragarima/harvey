Commissioner of Income Tax, U.P., Lucknow v Kanpur Coal Syndicate

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 77; AIR 1965 SC 325; [1964] 53 ITR 225; 1964 (2) SCJ 487; [1964] 8 S.C.R. 85

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income tax & Direct taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 3, 31 and 33 - Assessee consisted of several associations - Imposing tax' as per s. 3 of Income Tax Act - (A) Whether Appellate Tribunal has jurisdiction to cancel assessment made on association of persons? - Held, Appellate Tribunal has jurisdiction to give directions to appropriate authority to cancel assessment made on association of persons and to give appropriate directions to authority concerned to make a fresh assessment on members of that association individually - (B) Whether ss. 31 and 33 of Income Tax Act permits restriction of powers of Appellate Asst. Commr. or of Appellate Tribunal? - Held, the comprehensive phraseology used both in ss. 31 and 33 of Act does not countenance attempt of Revenue to restrict powers of Appellate Asst. Commr. or of Appellate Tribunal - Both of them have power to direct appropriate authority to assess members individually instead of association of persons as a unit - (C) Whether s. 3 of Income Tax Act directs appropriate authority to asses total income of an association? -Held, Income Tax Act impliedly gives an option to an appropriate authority to assess total income of either association of persons or members of such association individually - Appeal dismissed.
