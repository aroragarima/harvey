U.P. Co-Operative Federation Limited v Singh Consultants And Engineers (P) Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28483; (1988) 1 SCC 174; AIR 1988 SC 174; [1989] 65 Comp Cas 283; JT 1987 (4) SC 406; 1987(2) SCALE 1149; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 1124

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Banking & Finance - Civil Procedure - Contract & Commercial - Whether invocation of performance guarantees was illegal and if performance guarantees constituted independent and separate contracts between guarantor bank and beneficiary and created independent rights, liabilities and obligations under guarantee? - An irrevocable commitment either in form of confirmed bank guarantee or irrevocable letter of credit cannot be interfered with except in case of fraud or in case of question of apprehension of irretrievable injustice, otherwise very purpose of bank guarantees would be negatived and fabric of trading operation will get jeopardised - Appeal allowed.
