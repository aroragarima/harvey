Corn Products Refining Company v Shangrila Food Products Limited

Supreme Court of India

08 October 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 211; AIR 1960 SC 142; 1960 (62) BomLR 162; 1960 MLJ 418; 22 PTC 13; 1960 SCJ 509; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 968

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Keywords: Different Goods

Summary: Intellectual Property - Trade Marks Act, 1940, ss. 8(a) and 10(1) - Registration of mark - Respondent, manufacturer of biscuits, made the application for registration of the mark 'Gulvita' in respect of the goods specified in class 30 - Appellant opposed the respondent's application contending that it should be refused u/ss. 8(a) and 10(1) of Act on the ground that it had registered the mark 'Gulcovita' in respect of the goods specified in class 30 - The Deputy Registrar held that the the words 'Glucovita' and 'Gluvita' were not visually or phonetically similar and that there was no reasonable likelihood of any deception being caused by or any confusion arising from, the use of the respondent's proposed mark - HC held that there was no evidence that the appellant's trade mark had acquired a reputation among the public cannot be sustained - HC also held that impugned goods were not of the same description - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, trade connection between product of the appellant and product of the respondent established - Commodities concerned were so connected as to make confusion or deception likely in view of the similarity of the two trademarks - Hence, impugned order of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
