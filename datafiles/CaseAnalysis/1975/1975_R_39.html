Ramchandra Keshav Adke and Others v Govind Joti Chavare and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 March 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, Y.V. Chandrachud, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 414; (1975) 1 SCC 559; AIR 1975 SC 915; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 839

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948, s. 5(3)(b) - Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Rules, 1956, r. 2A - Bombay Tenancy Act, 1939 - Tenancy - (A) Requirements as to surrender by tenant - Mandatory - Effect of non-compliance with - Verification - Non-recording of satisfaction by authority - (B) Interpretation of Statutes - Intentions attributed to legislature - Imperative or directory - Effect of non-compliance - Held, surrender vitiated - No universal rule can be laid down as to whether mandatory enactments shall be considered directory only or obligatory and whole aim and object of the legislature would be plainly defeated if the command to do the thing in a particular manner did not imply a prohibition to do it in any other - Appeals dismissed.
