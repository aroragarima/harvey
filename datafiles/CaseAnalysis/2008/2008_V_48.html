S. Anand v Vasumathi Chandrasekar

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 241; (2008) 4 SCC 67; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 178; AIR 2008 SC 1296; 2008 CRLJ 1943; 2008 (3) G.L.R. 2652; 2008 (2) JCC (NI) 127; JT 2008 (2) SC 345; 2008 (2) RCR(Criminal) 76; 2008 (3) RLW 2711; 2008(2) SCALE 451; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 870

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Negotiable Instruments Act, s. 138; Code of Criminal Procedure, s. 256 - Complaint dismissed for default - High Court allowed the appeal - Appeal against - Whether the provision could have been resorted to in the facts of the case as the witnesses on behalf of complainant have already been examined? - Held, when the prosecution has closed its case and the accused has been examined under s. 311 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, the court was required to pass a judgment on merit of the matter - Appeal dismissed.
