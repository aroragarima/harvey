Spring Meadows Hospital and Another v Harjol Ahluwalia Through K. S. Ahluwalia and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 March 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1577; (1998) 4 SCC 39; AIR 1998 SC 1801; 1998 (33) ALR 48; 1998 (2) AWC 1259; 1998 CLT 684; [1998] 92 Comp Cas 797; 1998 CPJ(Sc) 1; JT 1998 (2) SC 620; 1998 (2) KLT 20; 1998 (3) MLJ(SC) 63; 1998(2) SCALE 456; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 428; 1998 (3) SLT 684; 1998 (3) Supreme 183

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Consumer Protection Act, 1986, ss. 2(1)(d)(ii), 14 - Medical Council Act, 1956, s. 20 A - Child admitted and treated in hospital by doctor - Child as well as his parents considered as 'Consumer' - (A) Awarding compensation to both of them for injury each one of them has sustained, if proper - (B) Functions of medical ethics regarding superiority of doctors - Held, proper - It is function of medical ethics to ensure that superiority of doctor is not abused in any manner - Appeal dismissed.
