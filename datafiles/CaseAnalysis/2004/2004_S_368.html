Rajureshwar Associates v State Of Maharashtra And Ors

Supreme Court of India

05 July 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 472; (2004) 6 SCC 362; AIR 2004 SC 3770; 2004 (3) AWC 2281; 2004 (3) BC 437; 2005 (1) Bom.C.R. 303; 2004(6) SCALE 118; 2004(6) SCALE 133; [2004] Supp2 S.C.R. 915

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Family & Personal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Indian Partnership Act, 1932 - Maharashtra Co-operative Societies Act - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 166(2), 166(3), 167(c), 226 - Ribbon Development Rules - Maharashtra Land Revenue (Disposal of Government Lands) Rules, 1971, rr. 15, 15(2), 16, 2(k-i) - Maharashtra Government Rules of Business, rr. 11, 11(2), 12, 8, 9 - Sale of property by official liquidator - Decision by Govt. to sell property - Execution of sale-deed - Total amount due towards consideration paid by appellant to Liquidator - State Govt. taken a decision to cancel agreement with appellant due to inadequate price - Whether State Govt. was estopped from going back from its promise of selling property to appellant, specially when it had already accepted total amount of consideration of Rs. 781 .33 lacs - Held, final decision taken by Govt. to cancel agreement of sale dated 27/11/2001 cannot be invalidated in fact of finding that there was no proper approval/sanction of Govt. for sale of subject land - Directed Govt. would be liable to refund amount to appellant along with simple interest @ 11% per annum.
