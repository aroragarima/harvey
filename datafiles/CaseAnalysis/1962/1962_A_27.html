Â Anakapalla Co-operative Agricultural and Industrial Society v Workmen

Supreme Court of India

23 October 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 89; AIR 1963 SC 1489; 1963 (6) FLR 1; 1962 (2) LLJ 621; 1964 (2) SCJ 601; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 730

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(oo), 10(1)(d), 25 F and 25 FFF - Re-employment after retrenchment - Entitled to - Whether retrenched employees due to sale of company are entitled to be reemployed by new owner? - Held, u/s. 25 H, a discharged workman may not be entitled to claim re-employment immediately after retrenchment or even soon thereafter - It is only if employer who discharged him as surplus requires additional workmen that his opportunity may occur - If transferor is by statue required to pay retrenchment compensation to his workmen? - It would be anomalous to suggest that workmen who received compensation are entitled to claim immediate re-employment in concern at hands of transferee - Contention that in cases of this kind workmen must get retrenchment compensation and re-employment almost simultaneously is inconsistent with very basis of concept of retrenchment compensation - Appeal allowed.
