Sumedha Nagpal v State of Delhi and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3986; (2000) 9 SCC 745; 2000 (40) ALR 570; JT 2000 (7) SC 450

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Family & Personal; Women & Children

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Minority and Guardianship Act, 1956, ss. 6(a) and 25 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Writ of Habeas Corpus - Custody of minor child aged two years sought by mother - (A) Whether writ petition is maintainable in view of alternative remedy u/s. 25 r/w s. 6 of Guardians and Wards Act? - Held, both parties do recognise that question of custody of child will have to be ultimately decided in proceedings arising u/s. 25 of Guardians and Wards Act r/w s. 6 of Act and while deciding such a question, welfare of minor child is of primary consideration - Allegations and counter-allegations have been made in this case by petitioner and respondent 2 against each other - Since these are disputed facts, unless pleadings raised by parties are examined with reference to evidence by an appropriate forum, a proper decision in matter cannot be taken and such a course is impossible in a summary proceeding such as writ petition u/art. 32 of Constitution - (B)Whether interim custody of child be given to mother as per s. 6 of Guardians and Wards Act? - Further held, in deciding such a question, what court have to bear in mind is welfare of minor child and not decide such a question merely based upon rights of parties under law - Petition dismissed.
