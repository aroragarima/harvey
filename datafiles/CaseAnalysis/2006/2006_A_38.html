Anil @ Raju Namdev Patil v Administration Of Daman & Diu, Daman & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

24 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 851; (2006) 13 SCC 36; (2008) 1 SCC (Cr) 72; 2007 (2) ALT 106; 2007 (1) JCC 307; JT 2006 (10) SC 586; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 911; 2006(12) SCALE 516; 2007 (3) SCJ 777; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 466

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 302, 304B, 306, 364-A, 498A - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 164, 215, 221, 24, 251, 313, 364, 464, 465 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Appellant was convicted and imposed with sentence of death - Appeal against - Whether appellant could have been convicted u/s. 364-A? - Held, it was obligatory on part of Sessions Judge, Daman to frame a charge which would answer description of offence envisaged u/s. 364 A - It may be true that kidnapping was done with a view to get ransom but same should have been put to appellant while framing a charge - Prejudice to appellant is apparent as ingredients of a higher offence had not been put to him while framing any charge - Appellant could not have been convicted u/s. 364- Found him guilty of commission of an offence u/s. 364 and sentenced to undergo rigorous imprisonment for life - Appeal dismissed.
