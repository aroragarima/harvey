State of Rajasthan v Shri Narayan

Supreme Court of India

30 July 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, B.P. Jeevan Reddy, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 528; (1992) 3 SCC 615; (1992) SCC (Cr) 781; AIR 1992 SC 2004; AIR 1992 SCW 2365; 1992 (2) CCR 224; 1992 (2) Crimes 1154; 1992 CRLJ 3655; JT 1992 (4) SC 282; 1992(2) SCALE 82

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1974, s. 386 - Victim, a married rural woman was raped by accused - There was delay in filing complaint as accused was in her relation - Held, witnesses were natural witnesses being neighbors and their testimony was also reliable plus testimony of prosecutrix was corroborated by medical evidence - Acquittal of accused set aside - Appeal allowed.
