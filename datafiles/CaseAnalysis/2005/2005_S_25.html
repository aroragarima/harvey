Dhodha House v S.K. Maingi

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 807; (2006) 9 SCC 41; AIR 2006 SC 730; 2006 (1) ALD(SC) 138; 2006 (1) AWC 864; 2006 (1) BLJR 29; JT 2006 (1) SC 123; 2006 (1) MLJ(SC) 36; 2006 (32) PTC 1; 2006 (1) RCR(Civil) 174; 2006 (1) RCR(Criminal) 385; 2005(10) SCALE 267; 2006 (1) SCJ 218; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 751

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Intellectual Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Intellectual Property - Copyright Act, 1957, ss. 62, 62(2) - Trade and Merchandise Marks Act, 1958, ss. 105, 134(2), 2(j), 5(3) - Companies Act, 1956 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 2, r. 3, o. 7, r. 10, ss. 16, 17, 18, 19, 20 - Jurisdiction of a civil court to determine a lis as regard infringement of the provision of the Copyright Act, 1957 - Infringement of trade mark - Territorial jurisdition of court - Plaintiff must actually voluntarily reside to carry on business or personally work for gain at place of filing suit - Merely because its goods are sold at said place would not mean that it carries business at that place - Civil Court at that place has no territorial jurisdiction to try suit filed by plaintiff in regard to infringement of its trade mark - Appeals disposed of.
