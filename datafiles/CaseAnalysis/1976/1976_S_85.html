Annapurna Carbon Industries Co v State Of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

09 March 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, A.N. Ray, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 615; (1976) SCC (Tax) 184; (1976) 2 SCC 273; AIR 1976 SC 1418; 1976 (5) CTR 161; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 561; 1976 (37) STC 378; 1976 TAXLR 1727; 1976 UJ 314

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Sales Tax - Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1957 - Tax on sales of Cinema Arc Carbons - Instant appeals challenging decision of HC that arc carbons are covered under entry no. 4 of schedule no. 1 of the Act - Whether sales of Arc carbons were rightly subjected to sales tax for A/Y 1965-66 and A/Y 1966-77.

Held, HC had correctly held that the main use of the arc carbons was duly proved to be that of production of powerful light used in projectors in cinemas. The fact that they could also be used for search lights, signaling, stage lighting, or where powerful lighting for photography or other purposes is required, could not detract from the classification to which the carbon arcs belong. That is determined by their ordinary or commonly known purpose or user. Further, it is evident from the fact that they are known as 'cinema carbons' in the market and said finding was enough, to justify view taken by HC that the goods under consideration are covered by relevant entry no. 4. Appeals disposed of.
