Jagdish v State Of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1158; (2009) 9 SCC 495; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 373; JT 2009 (12) SC 300; 2009 (4) MLJ(Crl) 1287; 2009(12) SCALE 580; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 727

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Bride Burning, Murder Reference, Mitigating Circumstance, Extra Judicial Confession

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.302 - Murder - Circumstantial evidence - Conviction - Appellant allegedly hacked his wife and children to death - Appellant convicted and sentenced to death by Trial Court - Conviction and death reference maintained by HC - Legality - Contention by appellant that he was of unsound mind at the time of incident - Held, plea of unsound mind not raised at lower courts - Case against appellant proved by PW1, PW3 and PW11 who had seen appellant sitting beside the dead bodies with a knife - Blood stained knife and clothes recovered by PW15 Investigating Officer at the instance of appellant - PW8 and PW9 post mortem doctors concluded that knife recovered could have been used to commit murders - PW5 doctor opined that 6 superficial incised injuries on person of appellant were self suffered - Evidence of PW5 corroborated by PW3 to whom appellant made a statement that after killing his family he had attempted to commit suicide - Therefore, all the incriminating circumstances constituted a chain even stronger than an eye witness account, hence conviction by lower courts fully justified.P /&gt;Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.302 - Murder - Conviction - Death sentence - Reference - Time lapse - Appellant convicted and sentenced to death by Trial Court for the murder of his wife and children - Conviction and death sentence confirmed by HC - Appellant contended that lapse of about three years between the sentence of death awarded by the Sessions Judge and the hearing of appeal by SC and such delay in execution of death sentence a dehumanizing and an unreasonable procedure and death sentence ought to be converted to one for life - Held, no delay in completion of trial and disposal of appeals - Appeals dismissed.
