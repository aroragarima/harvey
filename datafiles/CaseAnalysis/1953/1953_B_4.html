Bejoy Gopal Mukherji v Pratul Chandra Ghose

Supreme Court of India

28 January 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 36; AIR 1953 SC 153; 1953 SCJ 195; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 930

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Plaintiff sent a draft deed of sale for approval of Administrator-General of Bengal - Nothing having come out of negotiations between plaintiff and Administrator-General of Bengal latter invited offers for sale of lands - Defendant No. 1 made highest offer and this was accepted by Administrator-General in preference to offer made by plaintiff - Administrator-General accordingly executed a conveyance in favour of defendant Pratul Chandra Ghose who thereupon became tenant of premises - Defendant Pratul Chandra Ghose filed rent suits against plaintiff in respect of underlease held by latter under Administrator-General of Bengal and obtained rent decrees - Plaintiff, however, on strength of his new title derived from superior landlords under Mourashi Patta served notice on defendant Pratul Chandra Ghose requiring him to vacate premises - Defendant Pratul Chandra Ghose, not having vacated premises, plaintiff filed suit out of which present appeal has arisen - Issues arise - Whether tenancy of defendant was permanent, heritable and transferable and was not liable to be determined by notice? - Held, there have been many transfers and devolutions and landlords have accepted rent from transferees or successors - In spite of increase in land value and letting value, landlords, through whom plaintiff derives his title, did not at any time make may attempt to eject tenant or to get any further enhancement of rent - All these circumstances put together are explicable only on hypothesis of permanency of tenure and they irresistibly lead to conclusion that tenancy in question was heritable and a permanent one - Appeal dismissed.
