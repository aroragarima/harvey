Commissioner of Income Tax, Bangalore v K. Y. Pilliah and Sons

Supreme Court of India

13 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vishishtha Bhargava, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 209; [1967] 63 ITR 411

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Whether assessment on estimate of income and additions made was justified? - Tribunal confirmed estimate of income of assessee - Books of accounts of assessee was not accepted and gross profit disclosed by assessee was considered unreliable - Assessee offered no explanation for wide differences in gross profit disclosed and that earned by other merchants in locality carrying out similar business activity - Held, assessee contention were regarded as untrue in respect of cash credit entry and added to income as undisclosed income - Assessee admitted that transactions of certain amount 'kept out of accounts' - Judgement of High Court set aside, assessment was justified - Appeal allowed.
