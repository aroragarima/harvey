Smt. Saiyada Mossarrat v Hindustan Steel Limited, Bhilai Steel Plant, Bhilai (M. P.) and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.P. Thakkar, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 267; (1989) 1 SCC 272; AIR 1989 SC 406; 1988 (2) CCC 1080; JT 1988 (4) SC 243; 1988 (2) RCR(Rent) 521; 1988(2) SCALE 1208; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 690; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 690; 1989 (1) UJ 20

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Corporate

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Land and Property - Constitution , sch. 7, list 1, entries 32, 95, 97 - Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Act, 1973, Pre. , s. 2(e)(1)(i) - Parliament enacted 1971 Act - In the light of the Pre. , s. 2(e)(1)(i) , legislative competence to enact legislation is to be found in entry 32 of list 1 of sch. 7 of Constitution - Subject not covered in any state legislature as states cannot legislate for properties situated in more than one State or Govt. Companies situated in different States - Validity of the Act challenge on ground that Act extends to properties of Govt. Companies - Held, source of authority to encat such law can be traced to entry 97 r/w entry 95 of the Union List - Petition fails.
