Progressive Financers v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3271; (1997) 3 SCC 79; AIR 1997 SC 2160; 1997 (139) CTR 339; [1997] 224 ITR 595; JT 1997 (2) SC 729; 1997(2) SCALE 257; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 280; 1997 (2) Supreme 489; [1997] 91 TAXMAN 106; 1997 (2) TLR 620

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 184 - Construction of Partnership deed - Whether assessing officer can reject an application for registration of a firm solely on ground that in partnership deed shares of partners are not expressly specified? - Held, partnership profits is to be shared by minor - Losses to be distributed among major partners only - This was reasonable manner in which instrument of partnership was required to be construed - Because partnership deed had clearly mentioned amount of capital each member had to contribute including minor - Thus, assessing officer cannot reject an application for registration of a firm which complied requirement of s. 184 of 1861 Act - Appeal allowed.
