V. Sujatha and Others v State of Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1041; (1994) Supp3 SCC 436; (1994) SCC (Cr) 1786; 1994(4) SCALE 179; [1994] Supp3 S.C.R. 646

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: (I) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 279 and 337 - Criminal appeal arising out of SLP No. 180 of 1989 - Whether appellant guilty? - Held, it is not safe to rely on the evidence of PW 3 or on his report Ex. P-1 - HC did not appreciate the defence led by the respondent through the employees of the Road Transport Corporation - HC was in error in upsetting the well considered judgment passed by Chief Judicial Magistrate on merits - We set aside order of HC, restoring order of Chief Judicial Magistrate - Appellant acquitted of the charges - Appeal allowed. - (II) Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 420, 467, 468 and 471 - Crim.A. No. 625 of 1988 - Chief Judicial Magistrate acquitted the accused - HC convicted appellant u/ss. 420 and 471, IPC, Rs 10,000/- fine and one year RI - (A) Adverse remarks made by Padmanabhan, J. against Smt. V. Sujatha, Chief Judicial Magistrate - Appeal for expunction - One of the main principles is that a judge should take special care in making disparaging remarks against a judge of a subordinate court or against a person or authority whose conduct comes in for consideration before him in cases to be decided by him - Making uncalled for remarks against the said persons or authorities would be violation of judicial discipline - Held, all the harsh and adverse remarks made against her by HC in previous case would stand deleted and reference to her in derogatory terms shall be taken to have been pulled out - Application for expunction of remarks would stand allowed - (B) How could the HC take the appellant to have been charged u/ss. 471 and 420, IPC and not spell out a case of prejudice to him leading to miscarriage of justice? - Held, HC wrong in upsetting the correct judgment and order passed by learned Chief Judicial Magistrate - We restore it the order - Order of HC set aside, restoring the acquittal of the appellant - Appeal allowed - Held - All six appeals are allowed - Appellant in SLP (Crl.) No. 180 of 1989 and in Crim.A. No. 625 of 1988 stand acquitted - All the adverse remarks against Mrs. V. Sujatha by HC in its two upset orders would stand pulled out and expunged - Crim.A. No. 652 of 1989 and 653-55 of 1989 thus stand allowed.
