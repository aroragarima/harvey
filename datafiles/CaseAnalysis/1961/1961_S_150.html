Â The Durgah Committee, Ajmer Andanother v Syed Hussain Ali And Others

Supreme Court of India

17 March 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 126; AIR 1961 SC 1402; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 383

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Family & Personal

Keywords: Religious Denomination, Religious Endowments, Durgah Khawaja Saheb Act, 1955

Summary: Family & Personal - Trust & Associations - Durgah Khawaja Saheb Act, 1955, ss. 2(d)(v), 45, II(f), (h), 13, 14, 16 and 18 - Constitution of India, 1950, art.14, 19(1)(f) and (g), 25, 26, 31(1) and (2),32 - (A) Whether provisions take away and/or abridge their fundamental rights as a class and also fundamental rights of muslims belonging to Soofi Chishtia Order guaranteed by art. 14, 19 (1) (f) and (g), 25, 26, 31(1) and (2) as well as 32 of Constitution? - Held, claim for protection under art 26 have to be carefully scrutinised; protection must be confined to such religious practices as are an essential and an integral part of it and no other - It is obvious that art. 26(c) and (d) do not create rights in any denomination or its section which it never had; they merely safeguard and guarantee continuance of rights which such denomination or its section had - So if denomination never had right to manage properties endowed in favour of a denominational institution as for instance by reason of terms on which endowment was created it cannot be heard to say that it has acquired said rights as a result of arts. 26(c) and (d) - In instant case, since Chishti Soofies never had any rights of management over Durgah Endowment for centuries since it was created, attack on ss. 4 and 5 of Act must fail - (B) Whether ss. 2(d)(v) and 14 violates respondent's right to property under art. 19(1)(f)? - Further held,specific articles given for specific purposes as well as offerings made for general purposes of Durgah always belonged to Durgah and which are included within definition of Durgah Endowment by s. 2(d)(v) - Offerings or Nazars which are paid to Durgah and as such received on behalf of Durgah constitute Durgah Endowment and s. 14 authorises Nazim or his agent to receive such offerings and prohibit any other person from receiving them - In other words, effect of two provisions is that when offerings are made earmarked generally for Durgah they belong to Durgah and such offerings can be received only by Nazim or his agent and by nobody else - It is clear that these offerings never belonged to respondents and they can therefore have no grievance against either s. 2(d)(v) or s. 14 - Khadims' right to receive offerings which has been judicially recognised is in no manner affected or prejudiced by impugned provisions u/s. 2(d)(v) relevant portion of definition of Durgah Endowment is same as in earlier Act - Under earlier Act only sub offerings as were intended explicitly for use of Durgah were included in Durgah Endowment, while u/s. 2(d)(v) all Mazars and offerings which are received on behalf of Durgah are so included - Omission of word "explicitly" is merely intended to make it clear that if from nature of offering or circumstances surrounding making of offering or from other relevant facts it appears that offering was made for purpose of Durgah and was accepted on behalf of Durgah as such it would be an item of Durgah Endowment though offering may not have been explicitly made for Durgah as such - (C) Whether 11(f) and (h) violate fundamental right of respondents under art. 25(1)? - Further held, impugned provisions merely provide for regulation of discharge of duties by Khadims and nothing more, and so plea that freedom to practice religion guaranteed by art. 25(1) has been violated does not appear to be well founded powers conferred on Committee by s. 11(f) and (h) must be read in light of of s. 15 which makes it obligatory for Committee in exercise of its powers and discharge of its duties to follow rules of Muslim law applicable to Hanafi muslims in India, and so all ceremonies in Durgah have necessarily to be conducted and regulated in accordance with tenets of Chishti saint - U/s. 16, arbitration is provided for disputes between Committee and Khadims which is a very sensible provision - S. 13 lays down procedure for determining disputes as to succession to office of Sajjada- Nashin if a vacancy occurs suddenly as it always will in case of death for instance some interim arrangement must obviously be made; and s. 13(1) empowers Committee to make an appropriate interim arrangement in that behalf and to proceed to take necessary steps for appointment of a permanent successor as prescribed by other provisions of s.13 - Therefore it cannot be said that s. 13(1) offends against art. 25(1) of Constitution - Appeal allowed.
