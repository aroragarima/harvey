Dalmia Cement Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, M. Srinivasan

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1202; (1999) 4 SCC 124; AIR 1999 SC 2154; 1999 (153) CTR 401; [1999] 237 ITR 617; JT 1999 (3) SC 130; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 735; [1999] 104 TAXMAN 97; 1999 (2) TLR 595

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 9 and 60 - Income - Diversion by overriding title - Assessee agreeing to sell two cement factories - Agreement stipulating date for completion of transfer - On completion of transfer profits/losses accruing from factories stipulated to go to account of purchaser from earlier date - Held, sale completed by date profits were ascertained and assessed - It cannot be said that there was no diversion of income from earlier date since diversion was contingent on subsequent event, namely completion of transfer - Reasoning given by High Court is not sustainable on consideration of issue on a wider perspective.
