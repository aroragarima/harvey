Nirma Limited v Lurgi Lentjes Energietechink Gmbh and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1586; (2002) 5 SCC 520; AIR 2002 SC 3695; 2002 (48) ALR 808; 2002 (3) ARBLR 30; 2002 (5) AWC 4179A; 2002 (4) CompLJ 184; 2002 (2) CRC 524; JT 2002 (6) SC 117; 2002 (2) RAJ 627; 2002 (3) RCR(Civil) 724A; 2002(5) SCALE 10A; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 911; 2002 (4) SLT 735; 2002 (5) Supreme 302; 2002 (2) UC 609

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Civil Procedure - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 115 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 37(2) - Revision - Limitation - Exclusion of time spent in Supreme Court - Efficacious alternate remedy is available to the petitioner by way of filing a revision in the High Court u/s. 115 of CPC, 1908 - Merely because a second appeal against an appellate order is barred by the provisions of sub-section (3) of s. 37, the remedy of revision does not cease to be available to the petitioner - Relied on Shyam Sunder Aggarwal case [1996 Indlaw SC 1174] - SLP dismissed
