Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay v Imperial Surgical Company Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, Veeraswami Ramaswami

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 769; (1993) Supp4 SCC 2; 1992 (104) CTR 379; [1991] 192 ITR 646; [1992] 63 TAXMAN 508

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961 s. 40(a)(v) - Company paid bonus, commission and house rent allowance to employees, in addition to salary - (A) Whether these amounted to perquisites within meaning of s. 40(a)(v)? - Appellant applied to Tribunal for referring a question of law to High Court but Tribunal dismissed application - (B) Whether there is a question of law arising out of Tribunal's order which should have been referred to HC? - Held, no useful purpose will be served by directing a reference in this case - Appeal dismissed.
