Mahanadi Coalfields Limited and others v Dhansar Engineering Company Private Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 688; (2016) 10 SCC 571; AIR 2016 SC 4509; JT 2016 (9) SC 385; 2016 (4) RCR(Civil) 603; 2016(9) SCALE 339; [2016] 7 S.C.R. 127

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Extension Of Time, Contract, Communication, Variation, Capacity, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Contract & Commercial - Allocation of extra work - Breach of contract - In response to notice inviting tenders issued by appellants for the work of extraction and transfer of Coal/CMS by deploying 'Surface Miners' at various Open Cast Projects (Work), respondents submitted their bid and were declared lowest bidders - Later, LOI and Work Order was issued in favour of respondents and parties executed formal agreement on 26-5-2003 - While so, after completion of 70% of awarded quantity, respondents requested appellants to allow them to close contract by invoking power to reduce quantity by 30% of awarded quantity - However, said request of respondents was not acceded by appellants - Later, respondents requested for extension of time frame for completion of remaining contract and appellants extended contract period till 15-7-2004 - However, appellants also issued approval order to increase of 30% extra quantity but respondents reiterated that contract be treated as close as they were on the verge of completing quantity specified in contract by 15-6-2004 - Respondents vide letter dt.6-7-2004 sought closure of contract due to financial hardship but appellants called upon respondents to continue with remaining work as respondents completed only 105% of contact work out of 130% of work - Later, appellants issued letter for recovery of penalty for non-execution of balance contract work by respondents and including financial loss incurred by appellants due to allocation of work to third party at higher rate - Aggrieved, respondents field writ petition before HC and DB of HC observed inter alia, that it was not permissible for appellants to allot extra work to respondents at fag end of contract period and appellants had not offered any explanation as to in what circumstances decision to impose penalty was taken by Board of Directors - Further, it was also observed by DB of HC that new contract was already awarded to another and there was no loss of production caused to appellants and allowed writ petition - Hence, instant appeal by appellants - Whether impugned order of HC is sustainable.

Held, at the instance of respondents, contract was extended till 15-7-2004 and extra 30% work was allotted to respondents on 11-6-2004 before expiry of extended period. Hence, respondents are obliged to fulfill all contractual stipulations under the original agreement including to complete the assigned quantity of work - original or extra quantity - before 15-7-2004. However, as respondents failed to comply with contractual obligation of executing original quantity of work or extra work, respondents are liable to compensate appellants in terms of other express clauses of the contract to the extent of unfinished work and in particular the financial loss suffered by appellants for getting the same work executed through a third agency at higher rate. Further, stipulation of 45 clear days notice would come into play only if the respondents were also called upon to increase the machine capacity by upto 30% extra 'daily' quantity. However, appellants merely allotted extra 30% quantity without requiring the respondents to increase the daily quantity. Hence, said stipulation of providing for 45 clear days of notice was not an impediment for appellants to allot extra quantity of work upto 30% while the contract period was subsisting. Hence, appeal is partly allowed and judgment of DB is set aside Appeal partly allowed.

Ratio - U/cl.5 of the agreement dt.26-5-2003, appellants could reduce or increase the tendered quantity by +/- 30%, while the contract is subsisting.
