(1) Hardeep Singh; (2) Manjit Pal Singh v State Of Punjab And Anothers

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1726; (2009) 16 SCC 785; AIR 2009 SC 483; JT 2008 (12) SC 7; 2008 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3321; 2008 (6) Law Herald (P&H) 4239; 2009 (2) MLJ(Crl) 653; 2008 (4) RCR(Criminal) 947; 2008(14) SCALE 142; [2008] 15 S.C.R. 735

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cross-Case, Power Of The Court

Summary: IPC, 1860, ss. 307, 326, 336, 447, 427 r/w 148 and 149; Arms Act, 25, 27, 54 and 59 - Application to include the name of Kashmir Singh as an accused and to issue summons to him rejected - Appeal against - Referred two questions (1) When the power under s. 319(1) of the Code of addition of accused can be exercised by a Court? Whether application under s. 319 is not maintainable unless the cross-examination of the witness is complete? (2) What is the test and what are the guidelines of exercising power under s. 319(1) of the Code? Whether such power can be exercised only if the Court is satisfied that the accused summoned in all likelihood would be convicted? for the consideration of a Bench of three Hon'ble Judges - Order accordingly.
