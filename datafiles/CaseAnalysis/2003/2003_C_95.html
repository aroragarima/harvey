State of Maharashtra v Christian Community Welfare Council of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 851; (2003) 8 SCC 546; AIR 2004 SC 7; 2003 (4) CCR 276; 2003 (4) Crimes 367; 2004 CRLJ 14; 2004 (1) G.L.R. 841; 2003 (3) JCC 1879; JT 2003 (8) SC 84; 2003 (3) KLT 960; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 718; 2003(8) SCALE 621; 2003 (1) SCW 5524; 2003 (6) SLT 615; 2003 (7) Supreme 487

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Human Rights; Women & Children

Keywords: Custodial Death

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Human Rights - IPC, 1860, ss. 34, 302, 342, 354, 355 - Custodial death - Husband died while in custody and when wife came to enquire was locked up and molested - HC issued directions to in regard to laying down of guidelines to prevent and check custodial violence and procedures to be followed by police while arresting any person as also procedures to be followed by police after arresting such person, procedures to be followed in arresting a female person, manner in which such female person is to be detained etc as well as directed State Govt. to pay compensation to wife - Held, arresting authority while arresting a female person, should be made to keep a lady constable present, but in circumstances where arresting officers is reasonably satisfied that such presence of a lady constable is not available or possible and/or delay in arresting caused by securing presence of a lady constable would impede course of investigation, such arresting officer for reasons to be recorded either before arrest or immediately after arrest be permitted to arrest a female person for lawful reasons at any time of day or night depending on circumstances of case even without presence of a lady constable.
