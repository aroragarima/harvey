Opera House Exports Limited and others v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

14 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 533; (2014) 12 SCC 610; AIR 2014 SC 3383; 2014 CRLJ 4154; 2015 (315) E.L.T. 3; 2014 (4) JCC 2524; 2014 (3) RCR(Criminal) 871; 2014(8) SCALE 547; [2014] 9 S.C.R. 233

Case Digest

Subject: Fema; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Delay In Filing Appeal, Realisation of amount, Contravention of provisions of FERA

Summary: FEMA - Practice & Procedure - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973, ss. 18(2), 18(3), 40, 51, 52(2) - Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999 - Realisation of amount - Contravention of provisions of FERA - Delay in filing appeal - Condonation of delay - Legality - Appellant (Company) allegedly did not realize substantial amount of its export bills and that the bills pending realization were for the period 1991 to 1994 and was found that there was a prima facie case of violation of ss. 18(2) and 18 (3) of FERA by the Company, the respondent initiated inquiries against Company with regard to export bills pending realization - However, statement of General Manager (Finance) was recorded u/s. 40 of FERA and she furnished a copy of a letter from the Bank, confirming realization of an amount of Rs.9,72,203/- pertaining to the exports made during the period 1992-1995 - It was alleged that number of opportunities were provided to Company and its representatives to appear personally and explain the reasons for outstanding export bills, steps taken for realization of the pending proceeds etc - But Company did not avail those opportunities and failed to furnish any information - Thus, it was found that Company had contravened the provisions of s. 18(2) r/w notification nos. F.l/67/EC/73-1 and 3, both dt.1-1-1974 and s. 18(3) of FERA - Directors of the Company were found to have contravened the aforesaid provisions of FERA and directors were charged for contraventions of FERA - Thereafter, Enforcement Directorate passed adjudication order and holding charges of contravention of provisions of s. 18(2) r/w notification nos. F.l/67/EC/73-1 and 3, both dt.1-1-1974 and s. 18(3) of FERA and Adjudicating Authority imposed penalties on a number of Directors of Company - Aggrieved company filed an appeal before Tribunal for Foreign Exchange along with an application for condonation of delay of 40 days in filing appeal, which was dismissed - Aggrieved company filed appeal before HC, which were dismissed by impugned common judgment on the ground that appeals were filed beyond period of 90 days from the date of service of adjudication order - Whether for the purpose of counting delay in appeals against orders of Adjudicating Officer to the Appellate Board, the provisions of FERA or FEMA should apply -

Held, from s. 52 of FERA it was clear that appeal against order u/s. 51 of FERA could be filed within 45 days from the date on which the order was served on the person - Appellate Board was also empowered under proviso to s. 52(2) of FERA to entertain any appeal even after expiry of that period of 45 days but not after 90 days, from the date aforesaid subject to satisfaction that the appellant was prevented by sufficient cause from filing the appeal in time - Impugned order was passed by Adjudicating Authority on 11-8-2007 - It was by appellant and was not disputed by respondents that said adjudication order was served on the appellant on 25-10-2007 - Delay in filing appeal was 89 days - Even u/s. 52 of FERA the Appellate Board was empowered to condone the delay, as the appeal was filed before 90 days and not later than 90 days - Impugned judgment passed by HC and Tribunal was set aside - Case was remitted back to Tribunal to decide the same on merit - Thirumalai Chemicals Limited v. Union of India and others, 2011 Indlaw SC 294, relied on - Appeals allowed.
