Commissioner of Customs v Candid Enterprises

Supreme Court of India

13 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.P. Bharucha, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 311; (2002) 9 SCC 764; 2001 (130) E.L.T. 404; 2001 (44) RLT 702; 2001 (4) SLT 511; 2001 (5) Supreme 307

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Customs - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 17 - Duty free clearance against value based advanced licences pertaining to export of leather goods - Whether appeal barred by Act? - Held, Tribunal would appear to have lost sight of cardinal principle which is enshrined in s. 17 of Act that fraud nullifies everything - If Tribunal was satisfied, as it ought to have been upon these facts, that there might be some fraud, there was every reason for it to condone delay and to hear appeal - Appeal allowed.
