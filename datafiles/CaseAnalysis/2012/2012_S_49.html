Ahmedsaheb (D) by Lrs. and another v Sayed Ismail and another

Supreme Court of India

19 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 214; (2012) 8 SCC 516; AIR 2012 SC 3320; 2012 (6) ALD(SC) 115; 2012 (94) ALR 229; 2012 (5) AWC 5056; 2013 (2) Bom.C.R. 448; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 500; 2012 (6) MahLJ 503; 2012 (4) MPLJ 571; 2012 (4) RCR(Civil) 468; 2012 (117) RD 382; 2012(6) SCALE 505; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 984

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: Hyderabad Houses (Rent, Eviction and Leases) Control Act, 1954

Summary: Civil Procedure - Rent Control - Recovery of arrears of rent - Unregistered rent deed - Appellants filed suit against the respondent for recovery of arrears of rent for the period covering October 1971 to November 1980 - Said suits were decreed by the trial Court and the same was also confirmed by the Lower Appellate Court - However, HC set aside the judgment and decree of the Courts below on the sole ground that the rent deed as unregistered cannot be legally accepted in evidence for the purpose of recovery of rent and consequently the decree granted in favour of the appellants based on such inadmissible document cannot be sustained - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld - Held, though there can be no 2 opinion that the rent deed relied upon by the appellants being an unregistered document cannot form the basis to support the claim of the appellants for recovery of rent due - However, in the instant case there were other uncontroverted evidence available on record to support the claim of the appellants that would be sufficient to uphold the decree for recovery of rent from the respondent - Moreover, it was an admitted case of the respondent that the rent was due from him from October, 1971 till the suit was filed - SC were unable to appreciate as to how the appellants could have been non-suited solely on the ground that rent deed was not admissible in evidence - The dismissal of the suit on the simple ground that rent deed was not a registered document cannot be accepted - Appeals allowed
