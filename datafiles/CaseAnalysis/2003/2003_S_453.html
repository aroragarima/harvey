Madan Lal and Another v State of Himachal Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

19 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 631; (2003) 7 SCC 465; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1664; AIR 2003 SC 3642; 2003 (3) CCR 230; 2003 (2) CGLJ 395; 2003 (4) Crimes 60; 2003 (6) CRJ 667; 2003 CRLJ 3868; 2003 (89) ECC 241; 2003 (3) JCC 1330; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 100; 2003(6) SCALE 483; 2003 (2) ShimLC 429; 2003 (5) SLT 94; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 716; 2003 (6) Supreme 382

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 20, 20(b), 35, 41, 42, 43, 50, 54 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, ss. 100, 313 - Possession of contraband article - Non-compliance of ss.42, 50 - Conscious possession - Held, contention regarding non-compliance of provisions of ss.42, 50 is clearly without substance language of s 50 is implicitly clear that search has to be in relation to a person as contrasted to search of premises, vehicles or articles - Further held, once possession is established, person who claims that it was not a conscious possession has to establish it, because how he came to be in possession is within his special knowledge - Section 35 of Act gives a statutory recognition of this position because of presumption available in law - Similar is position in terms of s 54, where also presumption is available to be drawn from possession of illicit articles presumption available by application of logic flowing from ss 35 and 54 of Act clearly applies to facts of present case.
