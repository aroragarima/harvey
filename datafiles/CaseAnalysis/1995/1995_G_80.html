Manish Gupta and Others v Gurudas Roy

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, Faizan Uddin

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1726; (1995) 3 SCC 559; (1995) SCC (L&S) 753; AIR 1995 SC 1359; 1995 (29) ATC 646; 1995 LabIC 1611; 1995(1) SCALE 664; 1995 (2) SLR 58; 1995 (1) UJ 500

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution - West Bengal Services Revision of Pay and Allowances Rules, 1981, r. Part I55(4) Proviso - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, s. 2 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - (A) Service Rules - Pay re fixation - Pay drawn by Lower Division Assistant before his promotion to post of Upper Division Assistant - Was less than that drawn by employee junior to him - Claim fixation of his basic pay on same level as basic pay drawn by employee junior to him - (B) Contempt Act - Alleged violation of directions of High Court regarding fixation of basic pay of employee - Conduct not showing willful or deliberate disobedience - Holding appellate authority guilty of contempt of Court - Legality - Held, (A) Respondent cannot claim and proviso to r. 55(4) would be applicable - (B) Illegal - Appeal allowed.
