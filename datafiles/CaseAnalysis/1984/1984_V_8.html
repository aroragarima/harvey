Commissioner of Sales Tax, U. P., Lucknow v Vijai Int. Udyog

Supreme Court of India

15 October 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 89; (1984) 4 SCC 543; AIR 1985 SC 109; [1985] 152 ITR 111; 1985 (59) STC 49

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948, s. 11 - Doctrine of merger - Statutory right of appeal - Legality - Respondent was assessed to sales tax under the Act - Books of account were rejected and the disclosed taxable turnover of Rs. 51, 451 was enhanced on estimate to Rs. 1, 00, 000 by the assessment order - Assessee's appeal to the Assistant Commissioner succeeded in part and by order reduced the taxable turnover to Rs. 65, 000 while sustaining rejection of the books of account - Assessee and the Commissioner appealed to the Tribunal - Assessee's appeal was dismissed and the decision of the Assistant Commissioner was confirmed - Appeal by the Commissioner was partly allowed - Against the decision of the Tribunal in the connected appeal by the Commissioner by filing a revision u/s. 11 of the Act before the HC - Doctrine of merger applied and the judgment of the Assistant Commissioner (Judicial) stood merged in the judgment of the Tribunal. Hence instant appeal.

Held, SC does not accept the view of the HC that the doctrine of merger applied. Both the assessee and the Commissioner had a statutory right of appeal to the Tribunal against the decision of the Assistant Commissioner and in exercise of that right two separate appeals had been filed. On account of the mistake of the Tribunal in not clubbing the two appeals the statutory right of appeal of one party could not be negatived. It is a well-settled proposition of law that no party should suffer on account of the mistake of the Court on the Tribunal. That apart in a situation like this, the doctrine of merger has no application and the HC was in error in throwing out the Commissioner's appeal by applying the doctrine of merger. Both the appeals should be reheard by the Tribunal. Order of HC set aside. Appeal allowed.
