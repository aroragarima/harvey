Satya Narayan Athya v High Court Of M.P. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1965; (1996) SCC (L&S) 338; (1996) 1 SCC 560; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 387; AIR 1996 SC 750; 1996 (32) ATC 486; 1996 LabIC 757; 1995(7) SCALE 138; 1996 (1) SLR 52; [1995] Supp5 S.C.R. 679

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - M.P. Government Service (Temporary and Quasi-Permanent Service) Rules, 1960, r. 52(a) - M.P. Judicial Service (Classification Recruitment and Condition of Services)) Rules, 1955, r. 24(1) - Constitution, art. 309 - (A) Appointment on probation - No order of extension - Employee continued in service - Whether could be deemed to be confirmed automatically - (B) Appointment on probation - Continuation in service - During relevant period his performance not found to be satisfactory - Whether subsequent good or bad performance relevant - Discharge from duties during period of probation without charge sheet and enquiry - Whether illegal - Held, (A) Could not be deemed to be confirmed automatically - Until specific order is passed, he is deemed to have continued on probation - (B) Is meaningless - Not illegal - Appeal dismissed.
