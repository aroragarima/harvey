Â Sunil Batra and another v Delhi Administration and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, Y.V. Chandrachud, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.N. Shingal, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 289; (1978) 4 SCC 494; (1979) SCC (Cr) 155; AIR 1978 SC 1675; 1978 CRLJ 1741; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 392

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Legal Advisor

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.366(2) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 21 and 19 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, 73 - Maintenance of Internal Security Act, 1971, s.3 - Prisons Act, 1894 - ss. 30, 30(2), and 56 - Punjab Jail Rules - Solitary confinement - Death Penalty - Punishment of Torture - Fundamental Right - Petitioner No. 1 and 2 were arrested with an order of detention u/s. 3 of Act, 1971 - Both petitioners filed petitions/complaint regarding crime of punishment of torture - While undergoing imprisonment they were subjected to solitary confinement, contrary to provision of IPC, Cr PC, Prison Act, and Arts. 14, 19 and 12 of Constitution, which was arbitrary - Petitioners considered same as unreasonable, arbitrary and unconstitutional as they were not heard, by invoking arts. 14,19, 21,32 of Constitution - Hence instant appeal - Whether power conferred on respondent/Superintendent u/s. 56 of the Act was unguided and unanalyzed - Held, court held that even though s. 30 of Act was intra virus - Petitioner No. 1 and 2 could not be granted relief u/s. 56 of Act - Power u/s. 56 of Act was exercised only for reasons and considerations of safe custody of prisoner, as they were aimed primarily at preventing petitioners escape - Determination of necessity to put petitioners in bar fetters had to be made, after prison superintendent's application of mind to special characteristics of each individual prisoner - Hence, Classification of security purpose according to sentence, was certainly valid and s. 30(2) of Act did not violate art. 14 of Constitution - Imposing regulations for safety of and prison security was not violation of art. 19 of Constitution - s. 30(2) of Act was not arbitrary or there was any deprivation of personal liberty without authority of law -Order and direction of Court, had to strike just balance between dehumanizing prison atmosphere and preservation of internal order and discipline, maintenance of institutional security against escape, and rehabilitation of prisoners - Petition disposed of.
