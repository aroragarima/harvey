Suganthi Suresh Kumar v Jagdeeshan

Supreme Court of India

15 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 36; (2002) SCC (Cr) 344; (2002) 2 SCC 420; AIR 2002 SC 681; 2002 (1) AWC 610; 2002 (1) BC 564; 2002 (1) CalLT(SC) 59; 2002 (1) CCR 88B; [2002] 110 Comp Cas 133; 2002 (1) Crimes 265; 2002 (3) CRJ 422; 2002 CRLJ 1003; 2002 (1) JCC 315; JT 2002 (1) SC 220; 2002 (1) KarLJ 444; 2002 (1) KLT 581; 2002 (1) RCR(Criminal) 502; 2002(1) SCALE 269B; 2002 (1) SCJ 326; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 269; 2002 (1) SLT 347; 2002 (1) Supreme 227; 2002 (1) UC 413

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Corporate; Criminal

Keywords: Dishonour of cheques

Summary: (1) Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881-Section 138-Dishonour of cheque-Conviction and sentence-Quantum of sentence-Dishonour of two cheques involving Rs. 4,50,000-Amount not at all paid-Magistrate sentencing accused only to undergo imprisonment till rising of Court and pay fine of Rs. 5,000-Sentence should be of such nature as to give proper effect to legislation-Matter remitted to trial Magistrate to pass sentence which is condign.

(2) Constitution of India-Article 141-Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973-Precedent-Law laid down by Supreme Court-Is binding on all Courts in India-Impermissible for High Court to overrule decision of Supreme Court on ground that said Court laid down legal position without considering any other point-Single Judge of Kerala High Court committing impropriety by expressing that legal direction of Supreme Court in Hari Singh v. Sukhbir Singh, 1988 (4) SCC 551 should not be followed
