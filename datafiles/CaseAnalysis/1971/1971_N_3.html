Commissioner of Gift Tax, Madras v N. S. Getty Chettiar

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 245; (1971) 2 SCC 741; AIR 1971 SC 2410; 1972 (1) CTR 349; [1971] 82 ITR 599; 1972 (1) MLJ(SC) 78; [1972] 1 S.C.R. 736

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Gift Tax Act, 1958, s. 2(xxiv) - Family property - Gift - Tax - Legality - Assessee/respondent was karta of his undivided Hindu family consisting of himself, his son 'G' and six sons of 'G' - There was a partition of immovable properties of family through registered deed executed - After partition assessee took properties worth only Rs. 1,78,343/-, remaining properties were allotted to his son and grandsons - Gift Tax Officer/appellant overruling objection of assessee, came to conclusion that the assessee by allotting greater share to other members of coparcenary than to which they were entitled, must be held to have made 'gift' of portion of his share of property to other members and hence was liable to be taxed under the Act - Aggrieved assessee appealed to Appellate Assistant Commissioner - Appellate Assistant Commissioner held that assessee was not liable to pay any gift tax in respect of properties that fell to shares of his son and grandsons - Appellant/Department appealed against said order before Tribunal and same was dismissed - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before HC - HC held that partition in family of assessee did not come within mischief either of s. 2(xii) or s. 2(xxiv), there was no deemed 'gift' as contemplated by s. 4 of the Act - Hence instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was just and proper - Held, this leaves us with cl. (d) of s. 2 (xxiv) which speaks of transaction entered into by any person with intent thereby to diminish directly or indirectly value of his own property and to increase value of property of another person - A member of HUF who, as mentioned earlier, had no definite share in family property before division, could not be said to diminish directly or indirectly value of his property or to increase value of property of any other coparcener by agreeing to take share lesser than what he would have got if he had gone to court to enforce his claim - Till partition, his share in family property was indeterminate - He becomes entitled to share in family property only after partition - Therefore was no question of his either diminishing directly or indirectly value of his own property or of increasing value of property of anyone else - 'Transaction' referred to in cl. (d) of s. 2 (xxiv) takes its colour from main clause viz., it must be transfer of property in some way - This conclusion gets support from s. 2 cl. (xxiv), (a) to (c), each of which deals with one or other mode of transfer - If parliament intended to bring within scope of that provision partitions of type with which SC were concerned - In interpreting tax laws, courts merely look at works of section - If a case clearly comes within section, the subject was taxed and not otherwise - View taken by HC, Tribunal and Appellate Commissioner that assessee made no 'gift' under partition deed in question - Appeal dismissed.
