A. M. Moosa v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1053; (2007) 7 SCC 647; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 865; [2007] 294 ITR 1; JT 2007 (11) SC 102; 2007 (4) KLT 909; 2007(11) SCALE 31; 2007 (7) SCJ 169; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 831; [2007] 163 TAXMAN 741

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Income Tax, Act, 1961, s.80HCC(1)(3) - Assessment of Income - Exemption - Entitlement - Appellant/assessee claimed deduction u/s. 80 HHC of the Act, which was disallowed by assessing officer - Appellant filed appeal before Appellate Authority, which was dismissed - Appellant filed appeal before Tribunal, which affirmed view of assessing officer and Appellate authority - Reference was made to HC - HC answered reference in favor of respondent/department - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that 'profits' from exports of goods manufactured by him and 'profits' from export of trading goods were to be considered separately - Further persons who earn valuable foreign exchange could not be deprived of benefits of his export by adopting construction which would defeat very purpose for which provision had been created - Whether judgment of HC was sustainable - Held, meaning of word 'profit' would depend on context in which it was used - In s. 80HCC(1) indicate positive 'profit' and s.80HCC(3) provides how profits were to be worked out in computing total income considering both profits and losses - Thus, term 'profit' in ss. 80 HCC(1)(3) of the Act means positive profit worked out considering losses, if any - Appeal dismissed.
