Jyoti Kaul and Others v State of Madhya Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3638; (2002) 6 SCC 306; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1318; 2006 (1) ACC 570; AIR 2000 SC 3582; 2000 (6) ALD(SC) 76B; 2000 (40) ALR 442; JT 2000 (7) SC 367; 2000(3) M.P.H.T. 222; 2000 (3) MPLJ 520

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Computation Of Compensation, Appeal partly allowed.

Summary: Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 110-B and 168 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Whether High Court right in reducing multiplier from 15 to 10 in computing compensation payable to claimants on ground of death of claimants father at age of 75? - Principle of multiplier would depend on facts and circumstances of each case - Age of deceased, age of Dependants, not only existing salary when he did if any additional sum payable to deceased depending upon nature of job in which he was working, his chances of promotion, life expectancy etc. - Held, looking to facts of this case Tribunal has given good reasons for applying multiplier of 15 - This was in addition of taking into consideration that predecessor of deceased all lived for more than 80 years - HC reduced multiplier from 15 to 10 without taking into consideration circumstances prevailed - Findings of HC to extent application of multiplier set aside and other findings including reduction of interest upheld - Appeal partly allowed.
