Union of India and Others v Parul Debnath and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 702; (2009) 14 SCC 173; AIR 2009 SC 3085; 2009 (3) ESC 429; JT 2009 (9) SC 134; 2009 (4) LLN 118; 2009(7) SCALE 255; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 969; 2009 (7) SLR 191

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Eligibility Conditions, Manipur Home Guards Act, 1966, Andaman & Nicobar Islands Home Guard Regulation, 1964, Andaman & Nicobar Home Guard Rules, 1965

Summary: Service - Andaman & Nicobar Islands Home Guard Regulation, 1964, regs. 4 and 16 - Andaman & Nicobar Home Guard Rules, 1965 - State of West Bengal & Ors. vs. Pantha Chatterjee & Ors. [2003 (6) SCC 469 - Respondents were appointed u/reg. 4 - Claim for equal treatment and equal pay for equal work made by filing OA, on ground that they were being performing works of a permanent nature, so they be treated equally with regular Home Guards - OAs were disposed of and attained finality after confirmation by HC - Wherein HC in its Division Bench issued directions in tune with directions of the Tribunal for framing a Scheme and to take into consideration principles laid down in Pantha Chatterjee's case - In pursuance of directions Scheme was framed, wherein absorption of respondents was to be made in a phased manner - Challenged - Challenge accepted - Whether scheme framed by appellants was in tune with the direction of Tribunal and HC? - Held, No - Contentions heard, scheme in question noted - It was the intention, both of the Tribunal and the HC, as well as the present Court, that the respondent Home Guards were to be absorbed in the regular establishment of the Andaman & Nicobar Islands and no new appointment was required to be made; further intention was that that absorption was to be at one go and not in phases - Intention was that benefits be given to respondents uniformly and without making any discrimination - Impugned judgment upheld - Direction issued to implement directions given by Division Bench of HC - Appeal dismissed.
