Chhavi Mehrotra v Director General, Health Services

Supreme Court of India

26 July 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1240; (1995) Supp3 SCC 434

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.32,226 - Directions of Court - Issue of notification - Maintainability of independent writ- Petitioner moved writ petition before this Court for directions for consideration of her admission to MBBS course against 15% all-India quota of 1992 - Certain comprehensive directions were issued by this Court in matters for admission of students on which a notification dt.28-5-1993 was issued by Director General of Health Services - By instant application, Director General of Health Services alleged that despite whole matter being seized of by Court, petitioner moved an independent writ petition before HC and HC entertained said Petition and issued certain directions - Applicant alleged that said directions would not be consistent with consequences of implementation of this Court's order - Hence instant application.

Held, it is a clear case where HC ought not to have exercised jurisdiction u/art.226 of Constitution where the matter was clearly seized of by this Court in a petition u/art.32 of Constituting. Petitioner was eo nomine a party to proceedings before this Court. It is an unhappy situation that HC permitted himself to issue certain directions which, if implemented, would detract from plenitude of orders of this Court. HC's perception of justice of matter might have been different and abstinence that observance of judicial propriety, counsels might be unsatisfactory; but judicial discipline would require that in a hierarchical system it is imperative that such conflicting exercise of jurisdiction should strictly be avoided. Director General of Health Services has filed this IA for stay of orders of HC. Court stay orders dt.4-7-1993 as well as order dt.19-7-1993 made by HC and direct HC not to proceed with that writ petition. Though, by this legal manoeuvre petitioner has, prima facie, disentitled herself to any discretionary relief, Court however, do not intend to deny her, such benefit as she may obtain pursuant to selection made in terms of orders of this Court. Petitioner has been allotted to a college in Rajasthan. That benefit may not be denied to her. Order of HC directing that a seat be kept vacant in King George's Medical College, Lucknow, is stayed and Director General of Health Services is at liberty to fill it up otherwise. List this matter for final hearing. Order accordingly.

Ratio - When there is a petition u/art.32 of Constitution is pending, HC cannot entertain an independent writ petition u/s.226 of Constitution for same issue that was pending before SC.
