State of Uttar Pradesh v Jageshwar and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, V.D. Tulzapurkar

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 400; (1983) 2 SCC 305; (1983) SCC (Cr) 427; AIR 1983 SC 349; 1983 ALJ 281; 1983 (1) Crimes 978; 1983 CRLJ 686; 1983 CRLR 196; 1983(1) SCALE 113

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 27 - Murder - Acquittal of - Validity - Discovery u/s. 27 - Reliability of - Held, the discoveries u/s. 27 of Evidence Act, 1872 are not of guns and daggers used in a crime - Guns and daggers have an ancient origin and one does not have to hunt for an accused to discover them discovery, mostly and really is as regards authorship of concealment-Conduct and concealment are incriminating circumstances and their discovery becomes relevant and admissible u/s. 27 of Evidence Act, 1872 - HC rejected evidence led by prosecution by applying an easy and uniform yardstick that investigations was faulty - Confirm the order of acquittal by HC - Appeal dismissed.
