Superintendent, Central Excise, Bangalore v Bahubali

Supreme Court of India

05 October 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, P.S. Kailasam, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 469; (1979) 2 SCC 279; (1979) SCC (Cr) 447; AIR 1979 SC 1271; 1979 CRLJ 862; 1978 CrLR(SC) 535; 1979 (4) E.L.T. 1; 1979 MLJ 241; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 1104; 1979 UJ 77

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Criminal - Customs - Probation of Offenders Act, 1958, ss. 3 and 4 - Defence of India (Amendment) Rules, 1963, rr. 126-P(1)(i), 126-P(2)(ii) and 126-H(2)(d)(ii) - Customs Act, 1962, s. 135 - Defence of India Act, 1962, s. 43 - Respondent was proceeded u/s. 135(ii) of Customs Act, 1962, and rr. 126-P(2)(ii) and 126-P(1)(i) - Acquitted of charge u/s. 135 but convicted u/r. 126-I(1) and (10) r/w r. 126-P(2)(ii) - On appeal, High Court held that case within mischief of r. 126-P(2)(ii) but directed that respondent be released on probation of good conduct under Probation of Offenders Act, 1958 - Appeal - Whether in view of provisions of s. 43 of Defence of India Act, 1962, respondent was entitled to be released on probation of good conduct under Probation of Offenders Act, 1958? - Where there is a statute which bars exercise of judicial discretion in matter of award of sentence Probation of Offenders Act, 1958, will have no application or relevance - Held, as r. 126-P(2)(ii) manifestly bars exercise of judicial direction in awarding punishment or in releasing an offender on probation in lieu of sentencing him by laying down a minimum sentence of imprisonment, it has to prevail over provisions of Probation of Offenders Act, 1958, in view of s. 43 of Defence of India Act, 1962, which is later than Probation of Offenders Act, 1958, and has an overriding effect - Further held, benefit of ss. 3, 4 and 6 of Probation of Offenders Act, 1958, cannot be given where a person is found guilty of any of offences specified in r. 126-P(2)(ii) relating to gold control which prescribes a minimum sentence in view of emphatic provisions of s. 43 of Defence of India Act, 1962 - Appeal allowed.
