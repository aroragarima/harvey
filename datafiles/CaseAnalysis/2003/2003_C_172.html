Samtel India Ltd. v Commissioner Of Central Excise, Jaipur

Supreme Court of India

12 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1101; (2003) 11 SCC 324; 2003 (155) E.L.T. 14; 2003 (57) RLT 12

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 57F(17) - Refund of duty - Issuance of amended Rule - Entitlement - Appellants were manufacturers of black and white picture tubes which they export under bond - For manufacturing of black and white picture tube, they use inputs on which duty was paid by them - Under Modvat scheme, which was then in existence, they were entitled to credit for duty paid against duty payable on final product which was used for consumption or on final product which was exported after payment of duty or where such adjustment was not possible to refund of duty - Appellants paid duty on imports and had credit available to them - As export was under bond, under Rules prevailing upto February, 1997, appellants would be entitled to refund of duty because no adjustment was possible - However, in 1997, Rule was amended - Appellants filed claims for refund and those claims for refund were rejected on ground that under amended r. 57F(17) of the Rules credit had lapsed and that same could not be allowed - Said view prevailed with CEGAT which had dismissed their appeals - Hence, instant Appeals - Whether CEGAT was justified in rejecting refund of duty on ground that under amended r. 57F(17) of the Rules credit had lapsed - Held, assessee became entitled to take credit on input having been received in factory on basis of existing Scheme - Right to credit became absolute when input was used in manufacture of final product - Incident following thereto could take place in accordance with Scheme under which duty was paid on manufactured product - As if such situation was sought to be altered necessarily it followed that right which accrued to party got affected - Scheme sought to be introduced could not be made applicable to goods which had already come into existence in respect of which earlier Scheme was applicable and under which assessee had availed of credit facility for payment of taxes - Right which accrued to assessee on date when they paid taxes would continue until facility available thereto got worked out or until those goods existed - It was held that amended sub-rule could not be applied to goods manufactured prior to 1-3-1997 - Court held that this proviso did not take away vested right and merely laid down period of limitation - Therefore judgments of Court below were set aside and appellants case for refund could be processed in accordance with law - Appeals disposed of.
