S. M. Jakati and Another v S. M. Borkar and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 75; AIR 1959 SC 282; 1959 (2) MLJ(SC) 21; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 1384

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Land Revenue Code, s. 155 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Defendant No. 1 was Managing Director of a Bank which went into liquidation - Against a payment order, a bungalow belonging to defendant No. 1 was attached and sold on 02-02-1943 to respondent No. 1 - On 10-02-1944, respondent No. 1 sold property to defendants 8 to 10 who are respondents 2 to 4 - On 15-01-1943, Krishnaji a son of defendant No. 1 brought a suit for partition of joint family property and possession of his separate share - (A) Whether debt was avyavaharika? - Held, managing director of a Bank of position of defendant No. 1 who should have been more vigilant in investing monies of Bank cannot be said to have incurred liability for a cause 'repugnant to good morals' - Debt was therefore binding on sons - (B) Whether even if debt was not avyavaharika institution of suit for partition operated as severance of status between members of family and consequently in auction sale share of sons did not pass to auction-purchaser? - Further held, a son is liable, even after partition for pre-partition debts of his father which are not immoral or illegal and for payment of which no arrangement was made at date of partition - (C) What could legally be sold u/s. 155 of Bombay Land Revenue Code was right, title and interest of defaulter i.e., of father alone which could not include share of other members of joint family? - Further held, CPC at time of enactment of Bombay Land Revenue Code required that property sold in execution should be described as 'right, title and interest of judgment debtor' and same words have been used in s. 155 of Bombay Land Revenue Code - It is a question of fact in each case as to what was sold in execution of decree - That whole of property was put up for sale, was sold and was purchased as such is shown by documents referred to in this case - Appeal dismissed.
