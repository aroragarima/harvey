Surja Ram v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2481; (1996) 6 SCC 271; (1996) SCC (Cr) 1314; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 97; AIR 1997 SC 18; 1996 (4) Crimes 45; 1997 CRLJ 51; JT 1996 (8) SC 461; 1996(7) SCALE 192; 1996 (3) SCJ 275; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 783; 1996 (7) Supreme 248

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Keywords: Mitigating Factor, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Death sentence for rarest of rare case - Determination - Accused committed multiple murder of his real brother, brother's two minor sons and aunt while asleep - And also attempted to murder brother's wife and daughter without any provocation - Murders committed in cool and calculated manner - Held, it is rarest of rare case - Death sentence justified - Appeal and jail petition dismissed.
