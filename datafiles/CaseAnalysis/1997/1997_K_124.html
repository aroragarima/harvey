Krishnan and Another v Krishnaveni and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3048; (1997) 4 SCC 241; (1997) SCC (Cr) 544; AIR 1997 SC 987; 1997 (1) CCR 146; 1997 (1) Crimes 97; 1997 CRLJ 1519; JT 1997 (1) SC 657; 1997 (1) MahLJ 509; 1997(1) SCALE 458; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 511; 1997 (1) Supreme 628

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Continuous

Summary: Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 397, 401, 482 and 483 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 11 - Whether High Court is prohibited to entertain revisional power in every situation as warranted by s. 397 - Held, prohibition u/s. 397(3) is not applicable when State seeks revision u/s. 401 - Therefore it is duty of HC in exercise of its inherent power to avail revisional power, when there occurs a gross miscarriage of justice or abuse of process of court - However, same power should be used by HC quite sparingly.
