Ariane Orgachem Private Limited v Wyeth Employees Union and others

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 301; (2015) 7 SCC 561; AIR 2016 SC 1761; 2015 (145) FLR 985; JT 2015 (4) SC 381; 2015 (2) LLJ 513; 2016 (1) MahLJ 656; 2015(5) SCALE 679

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Failure Report

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act,1947, ss.10(1)(d),10(1),(2),(3),39(b) - Ref-erence of dispute - Denial of - Entitle to reinstatement - Respondent no.3 issued letters to its workmen working, informing them about sale and transfer of ownership and management of said factory and about their transfer for purpose of retiral/terminal benefits - Respondent no.3 wrote letter to Assistant Commissioner of Labour(ALC), seeking his intervention in respect of their demand with Company - Conciliation Officer sent failure report to Assistant Commissioner of Labour(ACL), office of Deputy Labour Commissioner(DLC) which took cognizance held that there was no industrial dispute in existence between parties - Re-spondent no.1 filed petition before HC who quashed order of Deputy Commissioner and di-rected him to refer industrial dispute of concerned workmen as per their demand - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether HC is justified in quashing order of refusal to make order of refer-ence regarding industrial dispute raised by respondent no.1.

Held, DLC is not entrusted with the power u/s.10(1)(d) of the Act as the delegatee of the State Govt. as per the new Notification, issued by the Industries, in exercise of its power conferred u/s.39(b) of the Act which is produced on record. As per the said notification, the State Govt. has conferred its power upon the Labour Commissioner and the ALC to exercise its power u/s.10(1),(2),(3) and other provisions of the Act. There is no other notification produced by the employer either before the HC to show that the DLC has got power as the delegatee of the State Govt. to make an order of reference under the provisions of s. 10(1)(d) of the Act. Order of the DLC, refusing to make order of reference regarding industrial dispute of concerned workmen is vitiated in law. Present dispute is with regard to the so-called 'Voluntary Retirement' of concerned workmen took place, wherein subject matter is the such 'Voluntary Retirement' was obtained by undue influence, coercion, fraud, etc. and whether the workmen are entitled to reinstatement with full back wages and continuity of service. Subject matter of the industrial dispute raised by the respondent no.1 under the provisions of the Act is totally different as they arise out of different cause of action. Therefore, judgment and order passed by the HC is perfectly legal and valid and the same does not call for interference by Court. Appeals dismissed.
