Commissioner of Customs (General), New Delhi and another v Gujarat Perstorp Electronics Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

05 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Mrs. Justice Ruma Pal, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1725; (2005) 7 SCC 118; JT 2005 (7) SC 490; 2005(6) SCALE 326; [2005] Supp2 S.C.R. 389

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: CEGAT, Collector Of Customs, Specified Goods, Export Promotional Capital Goods (EPCG) Scheme, Interpretation Rules, 1988

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 28(1), 111(m) and 112 - Customs Tariff Act, 1975, Schedule I, chapter headings 49.01, 49.06 and 49.11 - Assessee set up new plant and imported drawings, designs and plans under technical 'Know How' and 'Basic Engineering Agreement' from foreign company - Assessee claimed exemption from customs duty placing imported goods as 'printed books' under chapter heading 49.06 of Tariff - Revenue denied said claim of assessee and placed impugned goods under chapter heading 49.11 of Tariff on the ground that imported goods pertained to processes and were in the shape of manuals, brochures or leaflets, distinguishable from printed books - Revenue confirmed the duty demanded and also imposed penalty of Rs. 1 Crore on assessee - Tribunal allowed appeal filed against said order relying on its larger bench judgment in Parasrampuria Synthetics Limited vs. Commissioner of Customs New Delhi 2000 INDLAW CEGAT 433 - Revenue filed appeal against said order contending that the judgment upon which Tribunal had based its order had been overruled in another matter - Whether equipments, drawings, designs and plans acquired from foreign company relating to 'know-how' were 'printed books' covered under chapter heading 49.06 of the Tariff or covered under chapter heading 49.11 of the Tariff? - Held, it cannot be disputed that the basis heading was 49.01 of Tariff which covers 'Printed books, brochures, leaflets and similar printed matter' - On the other hand chapter heading 49.11 of Tariff covers 'other printed matter, including printed pictures and photographs' - Thus, specific or basic heading was 49.01 of Tariff and residual entry was 49.11 of Tariff - Priority, therefore, has to be given to the main entry and not the residual entry - As Tribunal had disposed of the appeal merely on the basis of larger bench decision in Parasrampuria Synthetics Ltd. and had not considered rival contentions on merits nor recorded findings thereon, hence, it would be appropriate to remit the matters to Tribunal to decide them on all points in accordance with law - Order passed by the tribunal set aside and matter remanded to tribunal for reconsideration - Appeals allowed.
