Ram Kumar Pande v The State Of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, A. Alagiriswami

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 129; (1975) SCC (Cr) 225; (1975) 3 SCC 815; AIR 1975 SC 1026; 1975 CRLJ 870; 1975 CrLR(SC) 215; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 519; 1975 UJ 423

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 134(1)(c) - Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970, s. 2(a) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 154 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 11 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34 and 324 - Conviction under s. 324, IPC and acquitted under ss. 302 and 34, IPC - Appeal against acquittal - High Court converted into conviction u/ss. 302 and 34, IPC, 1860 - Life imprisonment imposed - Appeal to Supreme Court - (A) Whether certificate of HC is required to appeal where an acquittal has been converted to a sentence of life imprisonment? - Held, no certificate of HC is required for an appeal where an acquittal has been converted into a conviction u/ss. 302 and 34, IPC, 1860 and a sentence of life imprisonment imposed upon an accused person - (B) Whether HC was justified in convicting accused? - Held, on appreciation of evidence that HC was not justified in convicting appellant u/s. 302 and 34, IPC, 1860 - HC's reasons to dislodge inference drawn by Trial Judge were insufficient - Appeal allowed.
