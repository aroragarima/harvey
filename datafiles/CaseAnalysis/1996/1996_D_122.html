State of Orissa v Divisional Manager, Lic and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 March 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2721; (1996) 8 SCC 655; 1996 (3) AD(SC) 735; AIR 1996 SC 2519; 1996 (28) ALR 98; 1996 (2) CCC 106; [1996] 87 Comp Cas 881; 1996 (2) CPJ(Sc) 31; 1996 (4) CTJ 382; JT 1996 (4) SC 288; 1996(3) SCALE 609; [1996] 3 S.C.R. 527; 1996 (3) Supreme 602

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Keywords: Contract Of Personal Service

Summary: Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Respondent filed a claim before State Commission for damages - State Commission awarded damages against the respondent No. 2 (LIC) - On appeal, National Commission awarded damages against the State in a sum of Rs. 1,00,000/- and directed to pay compensation within a period of 3 months - Hence, instant appeal by special leave.

Held, it was not in dispute that the respondent was a Government servant and, therefore, he is bound by the service conditions and the State was rendering services free of charge to the contesting respondent. Under those circumstances, the Government servant has been excluded from the purview of the Act to claim any damages against the State under the Act. Therefore, if any claim arises for the contesting respondent, it would be open to him to claim, in any other forum, but not under the Act. If the claim is barred by limitation, time taken during the entire proceedings shall stand excluded. Appeal allowed
