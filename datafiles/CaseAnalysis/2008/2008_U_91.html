Union Public Service Commission v Dr. Jamuna Kurup And Others

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, R.V. Raveendran, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2614; (2008) 11 SCC 10; AIR 2008 SC 2463; 2008 (6) AWC 6618; 2008 (5) ESC 789; 2008(6) SCALE 84; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 992

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Delhi Municipal Corporation Act, 1957 - MCD-respondent no. 38 had sent a requisition to UPSC-appellant for recruiting 45 Ayurvedic Vaids - UPSC issued an advertisement inviting applications for contract appointment on a fixed salary of Rs. 10,000/- for a period of six months or till such time such posts are filled on regular basis through UPSC - Respondents applied for such contractual employment and were appointed in April, 2001 - As the UPSC selections was delayed, the contract appointment of respondents were extended thereafter from time to time for further periods of six months each - Respondents filed writ petition seeking a direction to the Corporation to regularize their services in the vacant posts of Medical Officer (Ayurved) w.e.f. the respective dates of their initial appointment - Single Judge disposed of the writ petitions by order dt. 26-4-2004 directing that those writ petitioners who had crossed 35 years would be entitled to the benefit of age relaxation corresponding to the number of years they had worked as contractual Medical Officers (Ayurved) under the Corporation and should be treated as eligible with reference to age requirement - Single Judge also directed that the services of the writ petitioners should not be replaced by anyone save and except those appointed on regular basis after undergoing the selection process - UPSC filed appeal against said order submitting that age is relaxable for employees of MCD in the advertisement dt. 13.3.2004, is intended to refer only to regular and permanent employees of MCD and being short term contract employees, the respondents cannot claim to be 'employees of MCD' - Whether UPSC's submissions could be accepted and order of Single Judge could be set aside? - Held, Recruitment to posts in MCD is governed by the Delhi Municipal Corporation Act, 1957 and the term 'employee' is not defined in said Act nor is it defined in the advertisement of UPSC - Term 'employees of MCD' is no way restricted, makes it clear that the intention was to include all employees including contractual employees - Respondents who were appointed on contract basis initially for a period of 6 months, extended thereafter from time to time for further periods of six months each, were therefore, employees of MCD, and consequently, entitled to the benefit of age relaxation - If the intention of MCD and UPSC was to extent the age relaxation only to permanent employees, the advertisement would have stated that age relaxation would be extended only to permanent or regular employees of MCD or that the age relaxation would be extended to employees of MCD other than contract or temporary employees - UPSC directed to declare the withheld results of respondents who had participated in the examination in pursuance of the interim orders of SC and grant the benefit of age relaxation as per the direction of HC - Appeals dismissed.
