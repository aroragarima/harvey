M/s. Ahuja Industries Ltd., v State of Karnataka and others

Supreme Court of India

03 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 317; (2003) 5 SCC 365; AIR 2003 SC 3519; JT 2003 (3) SC 566; 2003(3) SCALE 623; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 351; 2003 (3) SLT 763; 2003 (3) Supreme 232

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Act, 1966 - Ss. 1(3), 3 (1), 2(8), 28(2) and 29(6) - challenge to the acquisition proceedings by the appellant - held, there can be no legal objection in all the three process provided under Section 1(3), 3(1) and 28 (1) of the Act being accomplished together by publication, on which only the exercise of power could be said to have been made. Further, failure on the part of the revenue authority to make entry in the register of mutation in favour of the subsequent owner would not render the acquisition proceedings bad in law on account of non-issuance of notice inviting objections to the acquisition proceedings or service thereof.
