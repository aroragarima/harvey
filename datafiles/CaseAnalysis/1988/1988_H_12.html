Karam Chand v Haryana State Electricity Board And Ors

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

B.C. Ray, M.P. Thakkar

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 269; (1989) Supp1 SCC 342; (1989) SCC (L&S) 409; AIR 1989 SC 261; 1989 (10) ATC 518; 1989 (1) CLR 102; 1988 (57) FLR 833; JT 1988 (4) SC 302; 1989 LabIC 1263; 1989 (1) LLJ 144; 1989 (1) LLN 282; 1988(2) SCALE 1203; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 702; 1988 (5) SLR 660; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 702; 1989 (1) UJ 220

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Electricity & Energy; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16, 309, and 311 - Punjab P. W. D. (Electricity Branch) Provincial Service Class III (Subordinate Posts) Rules (1952), r. 9, Exception - Appellant belonging to Schedule Caste - Appointment on promotion - Reservation of Scheduled Castes - Promotion to post of Superintendent - Question of seniority - Held, appointment on promotion is a regular one - Appointment letter does not articulate, promotion of appellant to post of Deputy Superintendent purely temporary - Promotion made on basis of quota of vacancies for being filled up by promotion of members belonging to Scheduled Castes - Exception to r. 9(ii) of said Rules cannot be applied to determine seniority of appellant in post of Deputy Superintendent and his seniority cannot be based in accordance with inter se seniority of respondents Nos. 2 and 3 in cadre of Assistants from which appellant and respondents Nos. 2 and 3 were promoted to post of Deputy Superintendent - R. 9 (i) of said Rules is applicable in this case and seniority of appellant is to be reckoned from date of his appointment in post of Deputy Superintendent - Appeal allowed.
