Smt. Kesar Devi v Union Of India & Ors

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 584; (2003) 7 SCC 427; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1652; AIR 2003 SC 4195; 2003 (2) AllCriC 616; 2004 (56) ALR 651; 2004 (2) ARC 349; 2003 (4) AWC 2856; 2004 (2) CLR 888; 2003 CRLJ 5750; 2003 (89) ECC 7; JT 2003 (6) SC 330; 2003 (3) RCR(Criminal) 880; 2003(6) SCALE 45; 2003 (4) SLT 934; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 1; 2003 (5) Supreme 425

Case Digest

Subject: Fema

Keywords: Sea Customs Act 1878

Summary: SAFEMA, 1976 - ss. 6(1), 6(2), 7, 7(1), 18 - COFEPOSA Act, 1974 - s. 3(1) - Customs Act, 1962 - Sea Customs Act, 1878 - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1947 or 1973 - Challenge to attachment of property under SAFEMA on the ground that the property belonged to the wife of the detenu bought out of her own income - No proof that the wife had any known sources of income - Held, under the Scheme of the Act, there is no requirement on the part of the competent authority to mention or establish any nexus or link between the money of the convict or detenu and the property sought to be forfeited. In fact, if such a condition is imposed, the very purpose of enacting SAFEMA would be frustrated, as in many cases, it would be almost impossible to show that the property was purchased or acquired from the money provided by the convict or detenu.
