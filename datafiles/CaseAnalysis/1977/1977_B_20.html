Bashir and others v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

03 October 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.S. Kailasam, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 304; (1977) 4 SCC 410; (1977) SCC (Cr) 608; AIR 1978 SC 55; 1978 (1) ALT(SC) 90; 1978 CRLJ 173; 1977 CRLR 449; [1978] 1 S.C.R. 585; 1978 (2) ShimLC 177; 1977 UJ 663

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 167(2), 437, 439 - Cancellation of bail - Complainant filed application for cancellation of bail granted to appellants u/s. 167(2) and Sessions Court allowed the same and on appeal, HC upheld cancellation of bail - Hence, instant appeal on ground that when bail was granted u/s. 167(2) of CrPC, it could be cancelled only under provisions of s. 437(5) and that bail could not be cancelled on mere ground that subsequently police had filed challan - Whether cancellation of bail is justified.

Held, as the provisions of ss. 437(1), 437(2) and 437(5) of CrPC are applicable to a person who has been released u/s. 167(2), the mere fact that subsequent to their release, a challan has been filed is not sufficient to commit appellants to custody. Further, fact that before an order was passed u/s. 167(2) the bail petitions of the appellants were dismissed on merits is not relevant for the purpose of taking action u/s. 437(5). Hence, cancellation of bail is not sound. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - The Court before directing arrest of the accused and committing them to custody should consider it necessary to do so u/s. 437(5) of CrPC and the same could be done by concluding that after the challan had been filed there are sufficient grounds that the accused had committed a non bailable offence and that it is necessary that he should be arrested and committed to custody.
