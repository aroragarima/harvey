Arunachalam v P. S. R. Sadhanantham

Supreme Court of India

06 March 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 584; (1979) 2 SCC 297; (1979) SCC (Cr) 454; 1979 (16) ACC 164; AIR 1979 SC 1284; 1979 CRLJ 875; 1979 CrLR(SC) 544; 1979 (2) MLJ(SC) 24; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 482

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 302 and 323 - Respondent and four others convicted u/ss. 148 and 302 - On appeal, High Court acquitted all accused of all charges - Present appeal - Where acquittal is based on irrelevant ground, or where HC allows itself to be deflected by red herrings drawn across track, or where evidence accepted by Trial Court is rejected by HC after a perfunctory consideration, or where baneful approach of HC has resulted in vital and crucial evidence being ignored, or for any such adequate reason, Supreme Court may feel obliged to step in to secure interests of justice, to appease judicial conscience, as it were - Held, two dying declarations may be relied upon without any reservation and evidence of PWs 1, 2 and 5 may also be safely accepted - Reasons given by HC for acquitting first accused wholly unsatisfactory - Judgment of HC set aside - Judgment of conviction and sentence passed against respondent on charge u/s. 302 restored - Appeal allowed.
