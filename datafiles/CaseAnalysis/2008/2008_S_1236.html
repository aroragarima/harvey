Kesarwani Zarda Bhandar v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 860; (2008) 8 SCC 305; 2008 (6) ADJ 229; AIR 2008 SC 2733; 2008 (4) AWC 3497; 2008 (227) E.L.T. 337; 2008 (1) FAC 328; JT 2008 (11) SC 50; 2008 (105) RD 104; 2008(8) SCALE 674; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 801; 2009 (13) S.T.R. 95; [2009] 19 VST 545

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Agricultural Produce Markets, Tobacco, Animal Husbandry, Warehousing, Warehousing Corporations Act, 1962, Madras General Sales Tax (Turnover and Assessment) Rules, 1939

Summary: Levy of market fee on Zafrani Zarda in terms of the provisions of the U.P. Krishi Utpadan Mandi Adhiniyam, 1964 - Having regard to the definition of 'agricultural produce' as contained in s. 2(a) as also s. 2(d) therein, market fee would be leviable only in the event Zafrani Zarda is held to be a processed form of tobacco and not the manufactured form - Zafrani Zarda being a 'manufactured tobacco' would not answer the description of processed tobacco - It is used by a class of consumers - It is used for a specific purpose - Tobacco as a processed form is used for many purposes, by many persons and in many ways - High Court had not considered this aspect of the matter - Appeals allowed.
