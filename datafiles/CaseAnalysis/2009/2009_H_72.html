Hdfc Bank Limited. v J.J. Mannan @ J.M. John Paul And Another

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2041; (2010) 1 SCC 679; AIR 2010 SC 618; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 654; 2010 CRLJ 2293; 2010 (1) JCC 489; JT 2009 (15) SC 484; 2010 (2) MLJ(Crl) 220; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 569; 2009(14) SCALE 724; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 590

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 438 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 419, 420, 468 and 473 - Anticipatory bail - FIR was lodged against respondent no. 1 on the basis of the complaint filed u/ss. 419, 420, 468, 473 r/w s. 120B, IPC - Respondent No.1 filed application for grant of anticipatory bail - HC held that since the investigating agency had already seized all relevant documents and recorded statements of all important witnesses, the custodial interrogation of the respondent No.1 was not required and granted anticipatory bail - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC erred in allowing the respondent No.1 application u/s. 438, CrPC? - Held, once the investigation makes out a case against accused and he was included as an accused in the charge-sheet, the accused has to surrender to the custody of the Court and pray for regular bail - On the strength of an order granting anticipatory bail, an accused against whom charge was framed, cannot avoid appearing before the Trial Court - Order of HC modified to the extent that the respondent No.1 shall surrender before the Trial Court and pray for regular bail - Appeal allowed to the above extent - Order accordingly.
