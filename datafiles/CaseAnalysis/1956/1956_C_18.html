Mela Ram and Sons v Commissioner of Income Tax, Punjab

Supreme Court of India

21 February 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Sudhi Ranjan Das

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 48; AIR 1956 SC 367; [1956] 29 ITR 607; 1956 (1) MLJ 175; 1956 (1) MLJ(SC) 175; 1956 SCJ 374; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 166

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 30(2), 31 - Limitation Act, 1963 - Delay Condone - Appeal - Barred by Limitation - Income-tax Officer assessed income of Appellant and notice of demand was served on account of income-tax and super-tax for year 1945-46 - Appellant preferred appeal against assessment - It was then out of time by 19 days but appeal was registered and notice for hearing u/s. 31 was issued - For next year Income-tax Officer assessed income of firm and notice of demand - Appellant preferred appeal before Assistant Commissioner (Appeals) and it was then 7 days out of time appeal was registered, and notice for hearing u/s. 31 was issued - It was heard along with other appeal - Department took objection that appeals were presented out of time, and were therefore liable to be dismissed - Appellant prayed for condonation of delay on ground that partition of country conditions were very unsettled, and that in view of exceptional circumstances, it had sufficient cause for not presenting appeals in time - Appellate Assistant Commissioner passed orders in both appeals, holding that there was not sufficient ground for condoning delay, and rejecting them in limine - These orders were purported to be passed u/s. 31 r/w s. 30(2) of 1922, Act - Aggrieved by said order appellant preferred appeals u/s. 33 of Act to Tribunal and same dismissed on ground that substance passed u/s. 30 (2) and not u/s. 31 and that no appeal lay against them u/s. 33 - Tribunal referred u/s. 66(1) of Income Tax Act following question for decision of HC - Whether in circumstances of case appeals lay to Tribunal against orders of Appellate Assistant Commissioner dismissing appeals against assessments for the years 19451946 and 1946-1947 in limine - HC followed decision of that court in Dewan Chand v. Commissioner of Incometax1950 Indlaw PNH 3 that orders of Appellate Assistant Commissioner were under s. 30(2) and not appealable u/s. 33 - Certificate to appeal to SC against this order having been refused by HC - Hence instant appeal - Whether order dismissing appeal presented u/s. 30 as out of time was one under s. 30(2) or u/s. 31 of the Act - Held, if an appeal was admitted without fact of delay in presentation having been noticed, clearly it must be open to Department to raise the objection at the time of hearing of appeal - That would also appear to be practice obtaining before Income-tax Tribunal, as appears from decisions cited - Similar considerations would apply to other objections of a preliminary character, such as one based on section 30 (3) - Therefore contentions relating to preliminary issues were open to consideration at time of hearing of appeal, and that jurisdiction of Appellate Assistant Commissioner was not limited to bearing of appeal on merits of assessment only - In this view, orders of Appellate Assistant Commissioner holding that there were no sufficient reasons for excusing delay and rejecting appeals as time-barred would be orders passed under s. 31 and would be open to appeal, and it would make no difference in position whether order of dismissal was made before or after appeal is admitted - Reference answered in favour of assessee.
