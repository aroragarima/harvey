Madhu Mehta v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

09 August 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 219; (1989) 4 SCC 62; (1989) SCC (Cr) 705; AIR 1989 SC 2299; 1989 (3) Crimes 104; 1989 CRLJ 2321; 1989 CrLR(SC) 728; JT 1989 (3) SC 465; 1989(2) SCALE 300; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 774

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Tort

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Constitution of India, arts. 21 and 32 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1974, s. 354(3) - Delayed execution of death sentence because of inordinate delay in disposing of mercy petition by President of India - Extent of jurisdiction of Supreme Court was in question - Accused made to suffer mental torture - Held, death sentence commuted to life imprisonment.
