Tribhuban Parkash Nayyar v The Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, J.M. Shelat, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 203; (1969) 3 SCC 99; AIR 1970 SC 540; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 732; 1970 UJ 35

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Human Rights

Keywords: Displaced Persons (Claims) Act, 1950, Displaced Persons (Claims) Supplementary Act, 1954, Displaced Persons (Verification of Claims) Supplementary Rules 1954

Summary: Displaced Persons (Claims) Act, 1950, s. 6 - Displaced Persons (Verification of Claims) Supplementary Rules, 1954, r. 18 and r. 8 cl.(iv) - Displaced Persons (Claims) Supplementary Act, 1954, ss. 2(f), 5(1)(a) and 5(1)(b) - (A) Power of Chief Settlement Commissioner u/s. 5(1)(b) - Scope - (B) Power in revision - Scope of - (C) Interpretation of cl. (iv) r. 18 - Rule of ejusdem generis - Applicability - (D) Chief Settlement Commissioner reduce the amount ordered by Claims Commissioner, in review - Concluding on pure conjectures and surmises and not legal evidence - Single Judge set aside the review order in writ petition - Ground, error apparent on face of record - This order also set aside in Letters Patent Appeals - Legality of - Held, it includes power to review final order under the 1950 Act - Includes valuation of claim - Rule of ejusdem generis does not apply - Held, that the decision of Letters Patent Bench was illegal - Appeal Allowed.
