State of Uttarakhand and another v Rajiv Berry and others

Supreme Court of India

10 August 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 612; 2016 (9) ADJ 264; AIR 2016 SC 3685; 2016 (119) ALR 49; 2016 (5) AWC 4823; 2016 (3) CLT(SC) 347; JT 2016 (8) SC 48; 2016 (4) RCR(Civil) 129; 2016 (133) RD 695; 2016(7) SCALE 655; [2016] 5 S.C.R. 140; 2016 (3) UPLBEC 2304

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Urgency, Constitution of India, 1950, Land Acquisition Act, 1894, Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 17, 17(1), 17(2), 17(3A), 17(4), 4, 4(1), 5A, 6 - Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013 - Notification and urgency - Land acquisition - Challenged - Notification dt. 4-5-2004 was issued u/s. 4(1) of the 1894 Act, land mentioned in schedule was notified for acquisition for the purpose of expansion of secretariat on both sides (North/South) of existing secretariat - Further, urgency clause u/s. 17(1) of the 1894 Act was invoked and enquiry u/s. 5A of the 1894 Act was dispensed - Appellants filed writ petition before HC challenging impugned acquisition which was disposed of by HC directing land owners to file objections before Land Acquisition Officer (LAO) shall dispose of the matter one way or the other after giving full opportunity of hearing - Appellants/land owners filed objections before LAO which was heard and awarded compensation - Simultaneously, State filed writ before HC challenging impugned order of setting aside of notification which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether there was any perversity or illegality in impugned judgment of HC.

Held, statute under which the acquisition was made is an expropriatory legislation and must be strictly construed. Though State had departed from its initial stand of invoking s. 17(4) of the 1894 Act and had given to the land-owners the opportunity contemplated by s. 5A of the 1894 Act, it had taken possession of the land prior to the passing of award by invoking the provisions of s. 17(1) of the 1894 Act. While there can be no manner of doubt that in instant case compensation u/s. 17(3A) of the 1894 Act was paid on 18-4-2006 after the date of taking over of possession on 17-3-2006, time and again, it has been held by SC that the said fact by itself would not invalidate the acquisition. Reference to Tika Ram and others v. State of Uttar Pradesh and others, 2009 Indlaw SC 1166. Further, taking over of possession without notice is concerned, SC do not find the said ground to be substantiated by the records. From the Possession Certificates, which are also on record, SC do not find contention of land-owners that taking over of such possession was symbolic to be substantiated in any manner. Therefore, SC cannot agree with the conclusion of HC that impugned acquisition suffers from any fundamental flaw or illegality which would require the same to be struck down as has been done by HC and impugned order was set aside. SC expressed no opinion on entitlement of any of the parties to apportionment of compensation no order will be called for in this regard except that compensation for the acquisition will now be determined in accordance with the provisions of the 2013 Act. Appeals disposed of.
