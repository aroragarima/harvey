Â Chandra Bhavan Boarding and Lodging, Bangalore v State of Mysore and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, S.M. Sikri, G.K. Mitter, A.N. Ray, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 32; (1969) 3 SCC 84; AIR 1970 SC 2042; 1969 (19) FLR 325; 1970 LabIC 1632; 1970 (2) LLJ 403; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 600

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Fair Wage

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitutiton - Minimum Wages Act, 1948, ss. 2, 3 and 5 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 43 - Whether notification issued by Govt. of Mysore, fixing minimum wages of different classes of employees in residential hotels and eating houses in State of Mysore is valid? - Failure to constitute a committee u/s. 5(1)(a) is not vitiated Constitution - Held, minimum wages fixed appear to be reasonable and if Govt. has power to reduce those rates, it necessarily follows that it has power to enhance them also - Appeal dismissed.
