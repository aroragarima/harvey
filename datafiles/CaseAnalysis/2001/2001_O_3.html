Punjab National Bank v O. C. Krishnan and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19845; (2001) 6 SCC 569; AIR 2001 SC 3208; 2002 (1) ALD(SC) 43; 2001 (4) AWC 2589; 2001 (2) BC 642; [2001] 107 Comp Cas 20; 2001 (4) CompLJ 179; 2001 (3) CTC 555; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 157; JT 2001 (6) SC 408; 2002 (130) PLR 250; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 668; 2001 (92) RD 782; 2001(5) SCALE 196; 2001 (5) SLT 778; 2001 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 162; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 466; 2001 (6) Supreme 81; 2001 (3) UPLBEC 2267

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Corporate

Summary: Banking & Finance - Recovery of Debts due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993, s. 20 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 227 - Alternative remedy - Recovery of Money from Principal Debtor and Guarantors - Suit transferred to Debts Recovery Tribunal - Decree passed by Debts Recovery Tribunal, Calcutta - Certificate issued and recovery proceedings started - Guarantor challenged through Writ - Whether writ remedy can be claimed against alternative remedy? - Held, order passed by Tribunal directing sale of mortgaged property was appealable u/s. 20 of Recovery of Debts due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993 - High Court ought not to have exercised its jurisdiction under art. 227 in view of provision for alternative remedy - Hierarchy of appeal should be followed - Appeal allowed.
