Union Of India And Another v H. R. Bangar (Irs)

Supreme Court of India

17 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2518

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Service - Misappropriation - Illegal gratification - Initiation of departmental proceeding - Enquiry proceedings were initiated against respondent for alleged misconduct committed by him - Tribunal closed down enquiry proceedings initiated against respondent on ground of inordinate delay in completing proceeding and directed appellants to complete enquiry proceedings within period of three months - On writ petition filed by appellants, Division Bench of HC affirmed order passed by Tribunal - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, that HC as well as Tribunal was not justified in closing down enquiry proceedings initiated against respondent for simple reason that proceedings were initiated only in 2001 and HC as well as Tribunal failed to appreciate that charges against respondent were very serious, i.e. misappropriation and accepting illegal gratification while in office. Nature of charges framed against respondent was very serious. Impugned orders are set aside. Authorities concerned shall complete departmental proceeding initiated against respondent within six months from date of communication of order after giving opportunity of hearing to parties. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Mere elapsing of long period from date of commencement of departmental enquiry was not a sufficient ground to close down proceeding, more so when department alone was not responsible for delay.
