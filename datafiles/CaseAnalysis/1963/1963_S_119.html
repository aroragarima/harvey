Chittaranjan Das v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

22 April 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 333; AIR 1963 SC 1696; 1963 CRLJ 534; 1964 (2) SCJ 311; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 237

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Misdirection

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 222(1) - Necessity to mention time and place of offence by prosecution - Appellant raped minor girl belonging to a refugee family and appellant ravished her a number of times, promising a job - Prosecution failed to mention date, time and place of offence -Whether charges framed against appellant/ prosecution version is not sustainable on ground that prosecution failed to mention date, time and place of offence? - Held, no - Particulars required by s. 222(1), may be provided wherever possible, but failure to mention such particulars may not invalidate charge - Conviction is not unsustainable merely because charges framed against accused are defective - Court should enquire whether defective charge has led to prejudice of accused - Victim has proved guilt of appellant beyond reasonable doubt - Appeal dismissed.
