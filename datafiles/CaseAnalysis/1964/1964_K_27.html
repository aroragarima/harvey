Karamshi Jethabhai Somayya v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

03 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, K.C. Das Gupta, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 116; AIR 1964 SC 1714; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 984

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Defence & Security Forces; Water

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 13, r. 1 - Government of India Act, 1935, s. 175(3) - Bombay Irrigation Act, 1879, s. 30 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 299 - (A) Whether it is duty of Govt. to place all relevant matters before court? - Held, while it is duty of a private party to a litigation to place all relevant matters before court, a higher responsibility rests upon Govt. not to withhold such documents from court - (B) Whether agreement between Superintending Engineer and private party in contravention of s. 173(3) is valid? - Further held, agreement was made in executive authority of Province - As it did not comply with s. 175(3) it was void - Agreement which excluded land of latter from factory area and giving supply of water for irrigating land permanently fell outside Bombay Irrigation Act - Such an order or agreement was entirely outside scope of Bombay Irrigation Act or Rules made there under - Appeal dismissed.
