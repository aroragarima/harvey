Rakhi Mishra v State of Bihar and others

Supreme Court of India

24 August 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

L. Nageswara Rao, S.A. Bobde

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 645; JT 2017 (8) SC 154

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cognizance, Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Criminal proceedings - Quashing - Legality - Appellant filed instant appeal challenging impugned order of HC whereby HC allowed application of respondents' u/s. 482 CrPC seeking quashing of criminal proceedings against them.

Held, HC erred in allowing the application filed by respondents and quashing criminal proceedings against them. A perusal of FIR would show that appellant alleged cruelty against respondents. It was settled law that the power u/s. 482 CrPC was exercised by HC only in exceptional circumstances only when a prima facie case is not made out against accused. A perusal of the complaint would disclose a prima facie case against respondents. The order passed by Trial Court by which cognizance was taken ought not to have been interfered with by HC. Impugned order of HC was set aside. Appeal allowed.
