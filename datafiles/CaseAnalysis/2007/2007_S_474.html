Balbir Singh v State of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

21 June 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1230; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 71; (2007) 6 SCC 226; AIR 2007 SC 2397; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 2103; 2007 CRLJ 3507; 2007 (4) JhrCR(SC) 51; JT 2007 (9) SC 569; 2007 (3) RCR(Criminal) 680; 2007(8) SCALE 642; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 1102

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: TADA Act

Summary: Criminal - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25 and 26 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 3, 4, 5, 6 and 20-A(2) - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 300, 309, 311, 258 and 482 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Appellant and others allegedly committed offence punishable under ss. 3, 4, 5 and 6, TADA Act, 1987 and ss. 25 and 26, Arms Act, 1959 - Alleged commission of offence on 05-12-1992 - Amendment to TADA Act, by which s. 20-A(2) introduced prior to filing of charge-sheet - By order D/- 19-04-1997 Designated Court held that in absence of sanction of Commissioner of Police as required under s. 20-A (2), TADA Act, acquittal of accused - Pursuant to order by concerned Court goods seized were retained - On 19-04-1997 sanction was accorded and order in that regard was passed and charge sheet was filed and summons were issued by impugned order - Appeal - Whether proceedings were non est as it amounted to infraction of s. 300, CrPC? - Designated court held that order on 19-04-1997 not an 'acquittal' on merits of case and could only operate as an order of discharge - In absence of sanction Court had no jurisdiction to proceed in matter and take cognizance of offence - But order passed in that regard cannot lead to acquittal of accused - Conditions for invoking bar under s. 300, CrPC are: (i) Court had jurisdiction to take cognizance and try accused and (ii) Court has recorded an order of conviction or acquittal and such conviction/ acquittal remains in force - Held, impugned order passed by Designated Court does not suffer from any infirmity to warrant interference - Appeal dismissed.
