Mahindra and Mahindra Limited v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 January 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, A.P. Sen, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 155; (1979) 2 SCC 529; AIR 1979 SC 798; [1979] 49 Comp Cas 419; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 1038; 1979 TAXLR 2064

Case Digest

Subject: MRTP

Keywords: Indian Evidence Act, 1882, Cease And Desist, Monopolies And Restrictive Trade Practices Rules, 1970

Summary: MRTP Act, 1969, ss. 2(o), 3(2), 33, 37, 38 and 55 - CPC, 1908, ss. 9 and 100 - General Clauses Act,1897 8(1) - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 92, 99 and 114 - Interpretation - A public limited company manufacturing motor vehicles and having a network of dealers - The cluases under agreement appointing them challenged as restrictive practice - And thereby questions comeup regarding - (A) Effect of amendment of s.100, CPC, on right of appeal in the light of s. 8 (1) of General Clause Act - (B) Scope and magnitude of power conferred on the commission u/s. 13(2) of MRTP Act, in the light of Regn. 85 - (C) Whether acquiescence can be inferred ,when application u/s.13 (2) is made instead of appeal u/s. 55 - (D) Estoppel, when arises as per s.115 of Evidence Act - (E) Necessary contents of application u/s. 10(a)(iii) of MRTP Act - (F) What amounts to 'Restrictive trade practice' u/s 2(o) and 33 of the MRTP Act in the light of ss.91and 92 of the Evidence Act, 1872 - (G) Two conditions precedent stated u/ss.37 and 38 for cease and desist order - Whether the order is valid as non -speaking order - Held, ammendment did not affect or restrict the grounds of appeal - Regulation does not limit the power u/s.13 (2) - Acquiescence, held could not inferred - Estoppel can arise only if a party to a proceeding has altered his position on the faith of a representation or promise made by another - Conents specified - ' A real probability that the presence of the clause itself would be likely to restrict competition ' detemine it - Invalid - Appeal allowed.
