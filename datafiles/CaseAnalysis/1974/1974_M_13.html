Â Maganlal Chhagganlal (P) Ltd. v Municipal Corporation Of Greater Bombay & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 April 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Alagiriswami, A.N. Ray, D.G. Palekar, Hans Raj Khanna, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, P.N. Bhagwati, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 270; (1974) 2 SCC 402; AIR 1974 SC 2009; [1975] 1 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Preventive Detention Act, 1950, Rajasthan Public Demands Recovery Act, 1952, Bombay Municipal Act, 1888, Taxation on Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947, Taxation of Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947, Travancore Taxation On Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1124, Income Tax (Amendment) Act, 1954

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution - Bombay Municipal Corporation Act, 1888 - Bombay Government Premises (Eviction) Act, 1955 -ss.8A, 105 A, 105B - Bombay Municipal Act, 1888 - Eviction - Unauthorized occupation - Discrimination - Legal training - Chapter V-A was introduced in 1888 Act by M Act 14 of 1961 contains ss.105A and 105B - According to provisions of ss.105A and 105B, Commissioner in relation to premises of B Electric Supply and Transport Undertaking were granted certain powers of eviction for unauthorized occupation - Persons failing to comply with order of eviction could be evicted by force - Person ordered to vacate on grounds of being in arrears of rent or acting in contravention of terms could be allowed to continue if he satisfies Commissioner - Petitioners contended that provisions of ss.105A and 105B was in vice of discrimination - Petitioners initiated suits and filed petitions was dismissed - Hence instant appeals - Whether provisions of ss.105A and 105B of Chapter V-A was in vice of discrimination - Held, there was no formula by which it could be said that one procedure was substantially more drastic and onerous than other - Need for speedy and expeditious recovery of public premises for utilization for important public uses, where dilatoriness of procedure may defeat object of recovery - Determination of liability to eviction would, be made by officer with proper and adequate legal training - It was open to aggrieved party to bring it before HC for examination u/arts. 226, 227 of constitution - Hence, could not accept that ss.105A and 105B in Chapter V-A were disparate as to attract vice of discrimination - Appeals disposed of.
