State of Bihar and Others v Sharda Prasad Rai and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Phukan, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3758; (2002) 9 SCC 677; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 236; JT 2001 (2) SC 625

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1950, ss. 4(h) and 4(g) - Whether proceedings initiated u/s. 4(h) of Act but subsequently dropped would bar initiation of proceedings u/s. 4(g) of said Act? - Cl.(h) empowers Collector to make an enquiry in respect of any transfer including settlement or lease of any land comprised in such estate or tenure or transfer of any kind of interest in any building used primarily as office or cutchery for collection of rent of such estate or tenure or part thereof and if on making enquiries Collector is satisfied that such transfer was made at any time after 01-01-1946 with object of defeating any provisions of Act or causing loss to State or obtaining higher compensation thereunder, he is required to give reasonable notice and opportunity of being heard to parties concerned and is enabled to annul such transfer, dispossess person claiming under such transfer and take possession of such property on such terms as may appear to him equitable, import of cl.(g) is entirely different which says that where by reason of vesting of estate or tenure or any part thereof in State under Act, Collector is of opinion that State is entitled to direct possession of any property, he is enjoined to serve a written order in prescribed manner on person in possession of such property requiring him to deliver possession thereof to State or to show cause, if any, against order within period specified therein and when such person fails to deliver possession or show cause or when a cause has been shown, Collector after giving such person, a reasonable opportunity of being heard rejects cause shown, for reasons to be recorded in writing, he can take or authorise taking of such steps including use of force as may be necessary for securing compliance with order or preventing breach of peace - Held, two clauses contemplate two different situations and operate in different fields and action u/cl. (h) does not debar State from taking action u/cl. (g) - Appeal allowed.
