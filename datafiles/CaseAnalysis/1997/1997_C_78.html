National Organic Chemical Industries Limited v Collector Of Central Excise, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

06 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 905; (1997) 2 SCC 587; AIR 1997 SC 690; 1997 (89) E.L.T. 643; JT 1997 (1) SC 637; 1997(1) SCALE 123; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 1; 1997 (106) STC 467

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Limitation Act, 1908 ; Central Excises and Salt Act 1944, Sch.I, Item 11A - Products manufactured were ethylene, butylene and propylene - Crude petroleum was refined to produce raw naphtha which on refining further gave rise to products manufactured - Held, since these products were obtained by refining of Crude petroleum and not naphtha, they are excisable under Item 11-AA.
