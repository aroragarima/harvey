Â E. S. P. Rajaram And Others v Union of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

01 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, G.B. Pattanaik, S. Rajendra Babu, Doraiswamy Raju, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21397

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Indian Railway Establishment Manual, 1968

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 142 - Scale of pay admissible for Traffic Apprentices in Railways appointed prior to the cut-off date - Claim of Traffic Apprentices appointed prior to the cut-off date that they should be given higher scale of pay, benefit of which was available to Traffic Apprentices recruited after the cut-off date raised before different benches of the CAT - Divergence of opinion between the different benches - Conflicting views taken by different benches of the CAT came up for consideration by SC in the case of Union of India and others vs. M. Bhaskar and others 1996 INDLAW SC 442, wherein SC upheld the validity of the memorandum and issued further directions considering the hardship which may be caused to the appellants concerned directed Union of India and its officers not to recover the amount already paid - In pursuance of the directions issued by SC in said judgment, the departmental authorities gave appropriate placement in the scale of pay to the appellants who were recruited as Traffic Apprentices prior to the cut-off date - Appellants filed applications - CAT dismissed the case with the observation that it would be appropriate for the applicants to approach the SC for any clarification/review of the judgment in the case M. Bhaskar - Present appeal - Held, decision in M. Bhaskar's case was taken on a detailed analysis of the relevant provisions of the Indian Railway Establishment Code and the Indian Railway Establishment Manual, and in the light of certain general principles of law relating to recruitment cogent reasons was given in support of the findings and conclusions arrived at in the judgment - In the facts and circumstances of the case decision was correct and warrants no interference - Appeal dismissed.Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 142 - Power of SC to issue the directions - Held, art. 142 of the Constitution vests power in the SC to pass such decree or make such order as is necessary for doing complete justice in any case or mater pending before it - The provision contains no limitation regarding the causes or the circumstances in which the power can be exercised nor does it lay down any condition to be satisfied before such power is exercised - The exercise of the power is left completely to the discretion of the highest court of the country and its order or decree is made binding on all the Courts or Tribunals throughout the territory of India - However, this power is not to be exercised to override any express provision - Appeal dismissed.
