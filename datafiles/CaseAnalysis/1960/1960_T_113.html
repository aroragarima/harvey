Â State of Rajasthan and Others v Thakur Pratap Singh

Supreme Court of India

18 August 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 220; AIR 1960 SC 1208; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 28; 1961 (1) SCJ 143; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 222

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Police Act

Summary: Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 15, 226 and 132(1) - Police Act, 1861, s. 15 - Exemption - Notification - High Court hold that para 4 of notification which exempted 'Harijan and Muslim inhabitants of villages' from levy, was violative of guarantee in art.15(1) against discrimination on ground of caste or religion etc. and struck down it as unconstitutional - Whether notification of exemption of 'Harijan' and 'Muslims' of villages, in which an additional police force was stationed, from bearing cost of that force, issued by state u/s 15 of Police Act is constitutionally valid? - It is not case of State, even at stage of petition before HC that there were no persons belonging to other communities who were peace-loving and law-abiding, though it might very well be, that according to State, a great majority of these other communities were inclined other way - If so, it follows that notification has discriminated against law-abiding members of other communities and in favour of Muslim and Harijan communities, on basis only of 'caste' or 'religion' - If there were other grounds they ought to have been stated in notification - Held, it is plain that notification is directly contrary to terms of art. 15(1) and that para 4 of notification has incurred condemnation as violating a specific constitutional prohibition - Thus, Court opined, Ld Judges of HC were clearly right in striking down para 4 of notification - Appeals dismissed.
