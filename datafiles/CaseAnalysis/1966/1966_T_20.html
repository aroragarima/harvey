Â Triloki Nath Tiku and Another v State of Jammu and Kashmir and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 140; AIR 1967 SC 1283; 1967 (14) FLR 282; 1967 (2) LLJ 271; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 265; 1967 SLR 192

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Petition u/art. 32 - To quash orders of promotion of respondents 3 to 83 and to direct State of J and K, 1st respondent, and Director of Education, J and K State, Srinagar, 2nd respondent, to promote petitioners to cadre of gazetted teachers with retrospective effect - Whether Mohammedans of entire State of J and K and Hindus of Jammu Province are backward and thereby they are not adequately represented in services of State? - Held, it is directed that High Court of J and K either directly or through a District Court to gather necessary material, such as, total population of entire State, break-up figures of two provinces, strength of different communities and extent of their social and economic backwardness and criteria applied by State in that regard - Order accordingly.
