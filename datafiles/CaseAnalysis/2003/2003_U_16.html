Teri Oat Estates (Private) Limited v U.T., Chandigarh and others

Supreme Court of India

19 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1504; (2004) 2 SCC 130; 2004 (136) PLR 899; 2004 (1) RCR(Civil) 540; 2003(10) SCALE 1016; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 1235

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Bombay Rents, Hotel & Lodging House Rates (Control) Act, 1947

Summary: Land & Property - Capital of Punjab (Development and Regulation) Act, 1952 - Chandigarh Lease Hold of Sites and Buildings Rules, 1973 - Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorized Occupants) Act, 1971 - Power of resumption of Estate Officer - Appellant purchased the disputed site on lease-hold basis in an open auction and constructed a building thereon - Although appellant paid the substantial amount of lease hold, he received a notice from the Estate Officer to the effect that he did not deposit the first instalment - Estate Officer cancelled the lease by way of resumption of land and building - Appellant filed appeal before the Chief Administrator - Chief Administrator set aside the impugned order of Estate Officer and restored the site to appellant with the condition of payment of remaining amount with forfeiture charge of 2% - Appellant filed revision before Advisor to the Administrator - Advisor directed Estate Officer to prepare the account statement indicating the balance amount to be paid by the appellant - Estate Officer forwarded the account statement wherein he charged the penal interest of 15% and 24% on two items - Estate Officer, in the meantime, directed the appellant and all other persons in occupation to vacate the suit premises - Appellant challenged Estate Officer's order before HC - HC dismissed appellant's writ petition - Held, appellant paid the entire amount which was due to the respondent, however, respondents were entitled to pay interest on the unpaid amount at the rate of 7% p.a. which in the event of non-payment was to be paid at a penal rate of 12% and subsequently enhanced to 15% and then to 24% as well the amount of penalty to be levied thereupon - Entire amount was recoverable through the process of law and in such circumstances, if the default is not absolute wilful or a dishonest one but occasioned due to situation which may be beyond one's control, the statutory right of the respondent in resuming the land may not be appropriate, if the entire dues stand discharged - However, appellants failed to make the payments of instalments within the stipulated period and they did not make any payment before the revisional authority despite the order passed by the appellate authority - Therefore, direction issued to the appellant to pay the remaining due amount of interest without any levy of penalty - Appeals disposed of.
