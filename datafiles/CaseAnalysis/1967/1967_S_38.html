Bishwanath And Anr. v Shri Thakur Radhaballabhji & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

06 February 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 468; AIR 1967 SC 1044; 1967 (2) ALT(SC) 301; 1967 (3) DLT 524; 1967 (69) PLR 761; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 618

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 92 - Suit filed for property on behalf of an idol of a deity - Whether the suit is maintainable by the idol represented by a worshipper as well as a person who had been assisting in the management of the temple? - Suit by the idol to enforce its private right - The suit also is for a declaration of the plaintiffs title and for possession thereof and is, therefore, not a suit for one of the reliefs mentioned in s. 92 of CPC, 1908 - The said section is not it bar to its maintainability - Held, the suit filed by the idol represented by a worshipper, in the circumstances of the case is maintainable - Appeal dismissed.
