Jiwan Mal Kochar v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 August 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Varadarajan, Amarendra Nath Sen, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 350; (1983) 4 SCC 148; (1983) SCC (L&S) 499; AIR 1983 SC 1102; 1983 (47) FLR 398; 1983 LabIC 1290; 1983 (2) LLN 683; 1983(2) SCALE 182; 1983 (2) SLR 456; 1983 UJ 852

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Land & Property; Service

Summary: Service - Board Of Revenue Act, 1913 - All India Services - Order of compulsory retirement - Validity - Held, so far as Enquiry Officer is concerned, appellant has admitted before us that he had no bias against him and that he was given all opportunities to defend himself - He has also admitted that there is nothing in finding of Enquiry Officer to show that he was influenced by any judgment against Manager of Central Stores of Roadways at relevant time - There is no non-compliance with any statutory rule or requirement or any principle of natural justice - Conclusion of Enquiry Officer regarding appellant's guilt is based on circumstantial evidence which has been accepted by Enquiry Officer and found to be acceptable even by learned Judges of High Court in light of three sets of documents and other circumstances - Appeal dismissed.
