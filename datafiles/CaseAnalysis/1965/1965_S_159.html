Â Babu and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

19 January 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 88; AIR 1965 SC 1467; 1965 CRLJ 539; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 771

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 429 -Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 133 and 134(1)(c) - What are circumstances in which High Court can grant certificate u/art. 134 of constitution? - Held, art. 134(1)(c) confers a discretionary power to HC but discretion must always be exercised on some judicial principles - Before granting certificate HC must be satisfied that case involves some substantial question of law or principle - In a criminal appeal HC can consider case on law and fact and if HC entertains doubt about guilt of accused or sufficiency of evidence it can always give benefit to accused there and then - Not necessary that HC should first convict him and then grant him a certificate so that Supreme Court, if it thought fit, may reverse decision - Certificate should not be granted to afford another hearing on facts unless there is error of a fundamental character - Decision given by third Judge was a proper decision and, therefore, certificate granted u/art. 134 of Constitution could not be based upon omission of third judge to discuss authenticity of First Information Report - Appeal allowed.
