Pandurang Sitaram Bhagwat v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1071; (2005) 9 SCC 44; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1198; AIR 2005 SC 643; 2005 CRLJ 880; JT 2005 (1) SC 91; 2005 (1) RCR(Criminal) 858; 2004(10) SCALE 508; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 1046

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 323, 34, 354, 504, 506 - Conviction - Appeal against conviction - Whether conviction sustainable? - Held, prosecution case should have been tested from angle that Trial Judge had acquitted all three accused persons who are said to have shared a common intention with Appellant not only in relation to offences under ss 323, 504 and 506 but also in relation to offence committed by appellant u/s.354 - Appellant is entitled to be given benefit of doubt.
