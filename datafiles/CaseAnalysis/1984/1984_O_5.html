Union of India and Others v Oswal Woollen Mills Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 March 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 329; (1984) SCC (Cr) 348; (1984) 2 SCC 646; AIR 1984 SC 1264; [1985] 58 Comp Cas 139; 1984 (18) E.L.T. 284; [1985] 154 ITR 135; 1984(1) SCALE 568; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 342; 1984 UJ 599

Case Digest

Subject: Trade

Summary: Trade - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947, s. 3 - Imports Control Order, 1955, cl. 8B - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, s. 2 - Writ petition is to prevent or to quash an apprehended or purported action u/cl.8-B of the Import Control Order - Interim relief - Ex-parte stay of statutory order - By injunction against issuing order of abeyance u/cl. 8B of 1955 Order - Non-disposal of ex-petitioner's application for licences etc. - Whether amounts to contempt of Court on the part of Import and Export Control Authorities - Held, ex-parte interim order deprecated - The authorities have not committed any contempt of Court - Appeal allowed.
