Priyanka Overseas Private Limited and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 November 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

N.M. Kasliwal, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 568; (1991) Supp1 SCC 102; AIR 1991 SC 583; 1992 (1) CalLT(SC) 1; 1990 (3) CompLJ 362; 1990 (31) ECR 569; 1991 (51) E.L.T. 185; JT 1990 (4) SC 490; 1990(2) SCALE 1028; [1990] Supp3 S.C.R. 138

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Warehoused Goods, Entry Inwards, Tariff Valuation, Private Warehouse

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 195, 248 and 261 - An appeal file u/s. 248 of 1961 Act - For the first time, the objection raised that no appeal u/s.248 was maintainable against order u/s. 195(2) - Whether on the facts and circumstances of the case in an appeal file u/s.248 of the Income Tax Act, 1961, the AAC had jurisdiction to deal with the quantum of the sum chargeable under the provision of the said Act from which the assessee was liable to deduct tax u/s.195 thereof ? - AAC held, is competent to fix the quantum or to revise the proportion of the amount - Such objection cannot be raised - Moreover s. 248 considered to be wide enough to cover any order u/ss. 195 and 261 - Appeal dismissed.
