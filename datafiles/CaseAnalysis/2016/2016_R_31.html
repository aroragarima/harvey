Satyendra Kumar and others v Raj Nath Dubey and others

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 340; AIR 2016 SC 2231; 2016 (4) ALD(SC) 52; 2016 (118) ALR 423; 2016 (4) ALT 54; JT 2016 (5) SC 523; 2016 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2302; 2016 (4) MLJ 650; 2016 (2) OLR 890; 2016 (131) RD 750; 2016(5) SCALE 34; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 391; 2016 (4) WLN(SC) 230

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Res Judicata, Jurisdiction, Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, Indian Evidence Act, 1872, Consolidation, Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950, Uttar Pradesh Consolidation of Holdings Act, 1953, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Finding on issues of law - Application of principle of Res-Judicata - Justification - Property in question was recorded in name of respondent in basic consolidation record - Petitioner filed objection for recording his name over 1/2 share of disputed land on ground of inheritance - However, Consolidation Officer rejected objection of petitioner on ground that earlier petition filed in respect of land of another village, between same parties was decided against petitioners by holding that three sons of deceased were illegitimate sons, hence, earlier judgment would operate as res judicata against petitioners - However, HC held that finding with respect to legitimacy of children are finding relating to facts and would operate as res judicata, however finding that illegitimate children are not entitled to inherit deceased being findings on issues of law, which would not operate as res judicata - Whether impugned order passed by HC is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, HC rightly held that previous proceedings would operate as res judicata only in respect of issues of facts and not on issues of pure questions of law when subsequent suit or proceeding is based upon different cause of action and in respect of different property though between same parties. Further, SC held that when cause of action and subject matter i.e., property in issue in subsequent suit are entirely different, res judicata is not attracted and competent Court is therefore not debarred from trying subsequent suit which may arise between same parties in respect of other properties and upon different cause of action. Hence, since Court is not debarred, all issues including those of facts remain open for adjudication by competent Court. Hence, SC held that impugned order passed by HC is justified and in accordance with law. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - Where decision is on pure question of law then Court cannot be precluded from deciding such question of law differently and bar cannot be invoked either on principle of equity or estoppel.
