Ashwani Kumar Saxena v State Of M.P.

Supreme Court of India

13 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 308; (2012) 9 SCC 750; AIR 2013 SC 553; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 3731; 2012 CRLJ 4731; 2013 (1) JabLJ 69; JT 2012 (10) SC 529; 2013(1) M.P.H.T. 109; 2013 (1) OLR 214; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 391; 2012(9) SCALE 90; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 540

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Keywords: Birth and Death Registration Act 1969

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Women & Children - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302/34 - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000, u/ss. 6, 7, 7A - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Rules, 2007, r. 12 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 7 - Murder - Conviction - Claim of juvenility - Denied - Challenged - Appellant (accused) allegedly committed offences u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC and was an under-trial - Appellant claimed to be juvenile on the date of incident and filed an application u/ss. 6 and 7 of 2000 Act before Trial Court - Trial Court dismissed relying on report (PW5) of Doctor (PW3) - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before Lower Appellate Court, which was dismissed, against which appellant filed appeal before HC - HC dismissed the same - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that as per school records, on the date of incident, appellant was juvenile - Whether appellant was a juvenile and impugned order was justified - Held, the Act required the Court or Board (Juvenile Justice Board) only to make an 'inquiry' and in what manner that inquiry had to be conducted was provided under the Rules - Court or Board could accept as evidence something more than an affidavit i.e, documents, certificates etc., as evidence need not be oral evidence - Only in absence of any matriculation or equivalent certificates, Court need obtain date of birth certificate from school first attended other than a play school - Only in absence of matriculation or equivalent certificate or date of birth certificate from School first attended, Court need to obtain birth certificate given by a corporation or a municipal authority or a panchayat (not an affidavit but certificates or documents) - Question of obtaining medical opinion from a duly constituted Medical Board arises only if said documents were unavailable - Act was not expected to conduct a roving enquiry and to go behind those certificates to examine the correctness of those documents, kept during normal course of business - Only in cases where those documents or certificates were found to be fabricated or manipulated, Court of Board or the Committee need to go for medical report for age determination - In instant case, Appellate Court, summoned original register of School where appellant was first admitted and same was produced by Principal of School and noticed the fact that appellant was born on 24-10-1990 - Entry made therein was not controverted or countered by State or private party, which was evident from proceedings recorded and which indicated that respondents had conceded that there was nothing to refute or rebut the factum of date of birth entered in School Admission Register - Thus, r. 12(3)(a)(i)(ii) of the Rules was satisfied - Under r. 12 of the Rules it was evident that if assessment of age could not be done, the benefit would go to child or juvenile considering age on lower side within the margin of 1 year - Admission register in school in which candidate first attended was a relevant piece of evidence of date of birth - Reasoning that parents could have entered a wrong date of birth in admission register hence not a correct date of birth was equal to thinking that parents would do so in anticipation that child would commit a crime in future and, in that situation, they could successfully raise a claim of juvenility - Appellant successfully established his juvenility on date of occurrence of crime - Impugned sentence awarded by Court below was set aside - HC was directed to place record before Board to award appropriate sentence in accordance with provisions of the Act - Appeal allowed.
