M. G. Badappanavar and Another v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, M.B. Shah, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2711; (2001) SCC (L&S) 489; (2001) 2 SCC 666; AIR 2001 SC 260; 2001 (88) FLR 808; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 408; 2001 (34) LabIC 293; 2000(8) SCALE 124; 2001 (2) SLJ 150; 2000 (5) SLR 801; 2000 (8) SLT 637; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 302; 2001 (1) Supreme 306; 2001 (1) UC 485; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 298

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Karnataka Govt. Servants (Seniority) Rules, 1957, rr. 2(c), 4 and 4A - Whether seniority on basis of roster promotions is valid? - Held, roster promotions were, it was held, meant only for limited purpose of due representation of backward classes at various levels of service - If rules are to be interpreted in a manner conferring seniority to roster - point promotees, who have not gone through normal channel where basic seniority or selection process is involved, then rules, it was held will be ultra vires arts. 14 and 16 of constitution - Appeals disposed of.
