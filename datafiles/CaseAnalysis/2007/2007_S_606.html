Amitsingh Bhikamsing Thakur v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 250; (2007) 2 SCC 310; (2007) 1 SCC (Cr) 582; AIR 2007 SC 676; 2007 (3) CalLT(SC) 23; 2007 CRLJ 1168; JT 2007 (1) SC 390; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 618; 2007(1) SCALE 62; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 191

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Arms Act, 1950

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 396, 506, 341, 379 r/w 120 B; Arms Act, s. 27 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, Relationship would not result in the mechanical rejection of the testimony of the witnesses - Settled norms of appreciation of evidence required that the evidence of such witnesses is to be assessed with caution. In the instant case the trial court has analysed the evidence with care and caution and the High Court has also done so - Appeal dismissed.
