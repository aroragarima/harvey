M. C. Mehta v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

25 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2237; (2009) 17 SCC 456; 2009(5) SCALE 463; 2009(10) SCALE 3

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Property of Municipal Corporation - Occupation - Monitoring Committee Report - Property in question which belongs to Municipal Govt. had been occupied by somebody else - Monitoring Committee was appointed to examine said area and submit report to MDC/Municipal Corporation of Delhi on said occupation of somebody - Applicant had filed applications seeking said report of Monitoring Committee - Hence instant applications.

Held, it is stated that response by Chandiwala Estate has been filed few days back, Monitoring Committee shall examine reply and indicate its views within period of four weeks. It appears from response of MCD that property in question does not belong to âKâ Plantation and belongs to somebody else. Applicant wants to take instructions and file further affidavit, if any. Let a copy of Report of Monitoring Committee be supplied to applicant, copy shall be supplied in respect of only Para 2 of Report relating to applicant. If applicant wants to file any affidavit in respect of report of Monitoring Committee, let same be done within three weeks. Let DDA file its reply within four weeks. Two weeks' further time is granted for filing response to reply, if any. Let affidavit by NDMC is filed within two weeks and its response within two weeks. Let copy of report submitted by Chief Vigilance Officer of MCD can be supplied to applicant. Affidavit in respect of same, if any, can be filed within three weeks. After copy is supplied, report can be kept in sealed cover. Let copy of report of Monitoring Committee can be supplied to applicant so far as it relates to applicant. An affidavit in respect of report, if any, by applicant shall be filed within three weeks. Let affidavit be filed by applicant indicating person(s) from whom institution claims to have purchased property and nature of title of vendor(s) affidavit shall be filed within two weeks. Order passed in interlocutory applications shall operate only in respect of Shop no. 4 to which present applicant relates. Permission granted shall be subject to challenge to changed parameters as spelt out in Notification. Order accordingly.
