C. Buchivenkata Rao v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 March 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 167; (1972) 1 SCC 734; AIR 1972 SC 1324; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 665

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Indian Companies Act, 1913 - Mineral Concession Rules, 1949, rr. 26, 27, 32 and 57 - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 61 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133(1)(a) - Application for mining lease - Grant of - Whether an application for mining lease should be rejected for being defective? - Held, r. 32, sub-r. (2), introduced in 1955 before grant of application of Venkatagiri, shows that individual qualifications of applicants including their special knowledge, their capacity to engage technically efficient staff, their financial soundness and stability, had to be taken into account in determining question of priority - R. 26, imposing certain restrictions, prohibits grant of lease to any person who does not hold a certificate of approval from State Govt. or who has not produced an Income tax clearance certificate - It does not prohibit any grant on ground that application for it is defective - Appeal dismissed.
