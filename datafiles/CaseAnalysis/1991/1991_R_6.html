Government of T.N. and Another v R. Thillaivillalan

Supreme Court of India

06 March 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 1086; AIR 1991 SC 1231; 1991 (2) MLJ 40; 1991 (2) MLJ(SC) 40

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - Constitution - Advocates & Judges - Miscellaneous - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - 'X' is an advocate, was appearing for Corporation of City of Madras in several of its cases - Dispute regarding his remuneration for his professional services aroused - Petition filed was dismissed by Single Judge - On appeal, Division Bench concluded that, in all events, even if all deductions claimed by Corporation were accepted a certain sum would yet remain due and payable to respondent - Whether permissibility of relief in exercise of jurisdiction u/art. 226 on facts taken into account by High Court, award of interest was wholly unjustified? - Held, on a consideration of matter, Courts finds that quantification of sum payable at Rs. 84, 212 21 does not call for interference - This has been arrived at after allowing all deductions claimed by appellants - Deductions include a sum of Rs. 48, 937 96 which was stated to have been paid by appellants to respondent towards expenses and which, according to appellants, has not been accounted for - Directions issued to Corporation to pay a sum of Rs. One lakh to respondent which shall include Rs. 84, 212 21 determined by HC as well as liability for any interest thereon - Liability for interest is limited to difference between Rs. One lakh and Rs. 84, 212 21 - Appeal disposed of.
