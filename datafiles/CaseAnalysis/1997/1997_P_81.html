Prahlad Saran Gupta v Bar Council of India and another

Supreme Court of India

26 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2456; (1997) 3 SCC 585; AIR 1997 SC 1338; 1997 (2) ARC 130; 1997 (3) AWC 1648; 1997 (2) CLT 127; JT 1997 (3) SC 186; 1997(2) SCALE 348; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 499; 1997 (2) Supreme 617; 1997 (1) UJ 514

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Advocates Act, 1961, s. 35 - Professional misconduct - Advocate, Standing Counsel of Railway alleged to have drafted notice against Railway - Draft notice not sent to handwriting expert for his opinion - (A) Whether conclusion about draft being in hand of delinquent advocate reached by Disciplinary Committee on basis of its own comparison of hand writing proper? - Held, conclusion not proper especially so, since charge of professional misconduct is quasi-criminal and requires proof beyond reasonable doubt - (B) Advocate of decree holder alleged to have addressed letter to another advocate to help judgment debtor in obtaining stay of execution of decree - Whether rejection of explanation by delinquent advocate on basis of photostat copy of affidavit filed by other person proper? - Further held, not proper and finding as to professional misconduct liable to be set aside - (C) Amount deposited with advocate for decree holder in connection with settlement in execution proceedings under negotiation between decree holder and judgment debtor - Settlement not fructifying - Property of judgment debtor auctioned - Advocate as yet retaining money - Whether advocate guilty of professional misconduct? - Further held, advocate is guilty - Penalty of reprimand imposed on advocate - Appeal partly allowed.
