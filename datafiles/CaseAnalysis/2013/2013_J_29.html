Jitendra Singh @ Babboo Singh and another v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

10 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 428; (2013) 11 SCC 193; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 2984; JT 2013 (11) SC 152; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 819; 2013(9) SCALE 18

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Keywords: Woman, Date Of Occurrence, Uttar Pradesh Children Act, 1951, Bihar Children Act, 1970

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Women & Children - Indian Penal code, 1860, ss. 147, 302, 304-B and 498-A - Juvenile Justice Act, 1986 - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000, s. 2(k) - Dowry death - Cruelty - Conviction - Challenged - FIR was filed alleging that deceased was set on fire by the appellants and two other persons on a demand for dowry, which she was unable to meet - Charge sheet alleged offences committed u/ss. 147, 302, 304-B and 498-A of the IPC - Appellants were convicted u/ss. 304-B (dowry death) and u/s. 498-A of the IPC (husband or relative of husband of a woman subjecting her to cruelty) - Appeal was filed before HC, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Whether the appellant was a juvenile or a child as defined by s. 2(k) of the Act, 2000 on the date of occurrence of the offence he was charged with - Held, there was documentary evidence to show from the school admission register that the date of birth of the appellant was 31-8-1974 - Medical examination of the also showed his age to be about 17 years - Therefore, appellant was about 17 years of age when the incident had occurred and that he had set up a claim of being a juvenile or child soon after his arrest and before the charge sheet was filed - Appellant was a juvenile or a child within the meaning of that expression as defined in s. 2(k) of the Act, 2000 - (B) Whether the conviction of the appellant can be sustained on merits and, if so, the sentence to be awarded to the appellant - Held, both the Trial Court and the High Court have found that Deceased had died of burn injuries as per the medical evidence - Demands were being made by the appellants for dowry soon before her death -Since the demands were not complied with, Deceased was frequently beaten and harassed - Also, deceased before her demise, had written a letter to her father about the beating and harassment given to her due to the inability to meet the dowry demands - Letter was proved by the prosecution and was relied on by the Trial Court as well as the HC in accepting the version of the prosecution - Therefore, the ingredients of s. 304-B of the IPC were made out - There was no apparent reason to disturb the concurrent findings of fact arrived at by the Trial Court and the HC and so the conviction of the appellant must be upheld - Further, offence was committed by the appellant when the Act, 1986 was in force - Therefore, only the 'punishments' not greater than those postulated by the Act, 1986 ought to be awarded to him - Matter to be remanded to jurisdictional Juvenile Justice Board constituted for determining the appropriate quantum of fine that should be levied on the appellant and the compensation that should be awarded to the family of Deceased - (C) Whether any appropriate measures could be taken to prevent the recurrence of a situation, such as the present, where an accused was subjected to a trial by a regular Court having criminal jurisdiction but he or she was later found to be a juvenile - Held, appropriate directions issued to all Magistrates which, would prevent such a situation from arising again - Appeal partly allowed.
