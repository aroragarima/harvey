Percept D'Mark (India) Private Limited v Zaheer Khan and Another

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 592; (2006) 4 SCC 227; 2006 (3) SCJ 338

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Continuing, Restraint Of Trade, Injunction To Perform

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Companies Act, 1956 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 9 - Specific Relief Act, 1963 - Whether the right of first refusal has been in restraint of trade? - The appellant can be adequately compensated in terms of money if injunction is refused - Cl. 31(b) contains a restrictive covenant in restraint of trade as it clearly restricts respondent No. 1 from his future liberty to deal with the persons he choses for his endorsements, promotions, advertising or other affiliation and such a type of restriction extending beyond the tenure of the contract is clearly hit by s. 27 of the Contract Act and is void - Held, in our view, no case is made out by the appellant for compelling respondent No. l to appoint the appellant as his agent in perpetuity - the Court directs (i) that all observations and findings of the High Court were for the limited purpose of deciding an interlocutory application, and hence will not bind parties at trial (ii) that all contentions raised by all parties are expressly kept open (iii) that the interim protection in paragraph 17 of the High Court's order will continue till the conclusion of the contract D/- 20-11-2003 (iv) that this Court is not expressing any opinion on merits of the rival claims and that the observation made in this judgment is only for the purpose of (vi) that the appellant is at liberty to proceed against the respondent for breach of the contractual terms before the appropriate forum in accordance with law and (vii) that liberty is reserved to the appellant to invoke Cl. 32(g) of the agreement - Appeal dismissed.
