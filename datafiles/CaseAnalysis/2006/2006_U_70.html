Union of India and Another v S.C. Parashar

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 261; (2006) 3 SCC 167; (2006) SCC (L&S) 496; AIR 2006 SC 3566; 2006 (5) AWC 5129; 2006 (2) ESC 131; 2006 (4) JhrCR(SC) 18; JT 2006 (3) SC 162; 2006(2) SCALE 527; 2006 (3) SCJ 361; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 529; 2006 (3) SLR 232; 2006 (2) Supreme 284; 2006 (2) UPLBEC 1429

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Central Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1964, rr. 11, 3(1)(ii) - Respondent failed to maintain absolute devotion to duty and acted in a manner unbecoming of a Govt. servant - Violations of rr.3(1)(ii), (iii) - Penalty of reduction to minimum of time-scale of pay for a period of 3 years with cumulative effect, including loss of seniority - Whether in terms of rules penalty imposed on respondent was permissible in law? - Held, respondent was entitled to be considered for promotion after a period of three years - Directed, punishment shall be reduction of pay to minimum of time scale of pay for a period of three years with cumulative effect - Appeal allowed.
