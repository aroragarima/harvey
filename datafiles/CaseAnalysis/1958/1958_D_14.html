Dr. Sailendra Nath Sinha and Another v Jasoda Dulal Adhikari and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 84; AIR 1959 SC 51; [1958] 28 Comp Cas 609; 1959 CRLJ 242; 1959 (1) MLJ 127; 1959 (1) MLJ(SC) 127; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 1263

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Companies Act, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Fisheries Act

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Companies Act, 1956 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 406, 467 and 477A - Interpretation of s. 237 of Indian Companies Act, 1956 - Complaint filed against appellant u/ss. 120B, 406, 467 and 477A of IPC, 1860 - Whether prosecution against appellant by liquidator is barred u/s. 237(1) of Indian Companies Act, 1956? - Liquidator has to function under directions of court which is in charge of liquidation proceedings - Liquidator has power to institute prosecutions in name and on behalf of company under liquidation with sanction of court - Companies Act, 1956 nowhere provides that without directions of Judge no criminal prosecution can be instituted - Neither s. 179 nor s. 237 indicates that if liquidator takes action without direction of court this action would be illegal or invalid or it would invalidate prosecution - Held, language of s. 237(1) as well as on precedent complaint made by the liquidator against appellants suffers from no such infirmity as to make proceedings null and void - Appeal dismissed.
