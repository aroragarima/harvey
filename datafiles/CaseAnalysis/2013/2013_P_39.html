G.M. Siddeshwar v Prasanna Kumar

Supreme Court of India

08 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, R.M. Lodha, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 132; (2013) 4 SCC 776; AIR 2013 SC 1549; 2013 (4) ALD(SC) 131; 2013 (1) CLT(SC) 296; 2013 (2) CTC 444; JT 2013 (4) SC 47; 2013 (3) KarLJ 665; 2013 (2) KLT 20; 2013 (3) MLJ 333; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 340; 2013(3) SCALE 287; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 1107

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Allegations Of Corrupt Practice, Purity, Code of Civil Procedure (Amendment) Bill, 1997

Summary: Election - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, 15(4)and 16 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, s. 83(1) and 90(3) - Election petition - Maintainability - Principal question of law raised was whether to maintain election petition, it was imperative for election petitioner to file affidavit in terms of O. VI r. 15(4) of CPC in support of the averments made in the election petition in addition to an affidavit (in case where resort to corrupt practices were alleged against the returned candidate) as required by proviso to s. 83(1) of Act - Held, in that case, Constitution Bench introduced two clear principles - Firstly, that if there was total and complete non compliance with the provisions of s. 81(3) of Act, election petition might not be election petition presented in accordance with the provisions of that part within s. 80 of Act and secondly, that if there was substantial compliance with the requirement of s. 81(3) of Act, election petition could not be dismissed by the Tribunal u/s. 90(3) of Act - Ch. Subba Rao v. Member, Election Tribunal, Hyderabad, 1964 Indlaw SC 429, relied on - In another case SC reiterated the doctrine of substantial compliance also introduced the doctrine of curability on the principles contained in the CPC - It was held that the defect in the affidavit in that case was curable and was not of such fatal nature as to attract dismissal of the election petition at the threshold - T.M. Jacob v. C. Poulose & Ors., 1999 Indlaw SC 1082, relied on - Applying these principles to the facts of instant case, it seemed quite clear that the affidavit filed by respondent in compliance with the requirements of the proviso to s. 83(1) of Act was not integral part of the election petition, and no such case was set up - It also seemed quite clear that the affidavit was in substantial compliance with the requirements of the law - Therefore, HC was quite right in coming to the conclusion that the affidavit not being in the prescribed format and with defective verification were curable defects and that opportunity ought to be granted to respondent to cure the defects - No submissions were made with regard to the striking out, in accordance with O. VI r. 16 of CPC, of specifically objectionable paragraphs in the election petition - Appeal dismissed.
