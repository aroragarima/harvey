(1) Basayya Prabhayya Hallur; (2) Virabasayya v State Of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1507; (2009) 17 SCC 55; 2009(14) SCALE 171

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 304 Part I - Unlawful assembly with common intent of committing murder - Trial Court convicted accused nos. 1 and 4 u/s. 304 Part I of IPC, however, acquitted all other accused - Appeal before HC - HC convicted accused nos. 1, 2, 3, 6 and 7, however, acquitted 4th accused - Whether the appellants-accused nos. 2, 4, 6, and 7 should be sent back to jail to serve out the remaining sentence after a lapse of several years - Held, ends of justice would be met if, while maintaining the conviction of accused nos.2, 4, 6 and 7, their sentence reduced to the period already undergone by them, provided they pay a fine of Rs.25, 000/- each -1st accuser's name stated by all injured eye witnesses - Specific role attributed by 1st accused in the commission of murder of deceased, therefore, no interference in the impugned judgement in the case of 1st accused - HC reversed the Trial Court order of acquittal of 3rd accused in a perfunctory manner - Impugned judgment of the HC was clearly contrary to the well settled position of law, hence set aside and 3rd accused acquitted - Appeal disposed of.
