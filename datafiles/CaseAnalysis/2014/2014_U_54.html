Magnum Promoters Private Limited v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

27 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 875; (2015) 3 SCC 327; AIR 2015 SC 444; 2015 (2) ALD(SC) 52; 2015(1) ALL MR 965; 2015 (113) ALR 4; 2015 (1) AWC 846; 2015 (1) CLT(SC) 46; JT 2014 (14) SC 77; 2015 (5) MahLJ 46; 2015 (3) MPLJ 540; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 765; 2016 (130) RD 93

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6, 9 and 10 - Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013, s. 24 - Acquisition proceedings - Legality - Appellant was the lawful owner of the land situated in the National Capital Territory of Delhi (NCT, Delhi) - Notification was issued on 1-6-1995 u/s. 4 of the 1894 Act (repealed Act) for the acquisition of the different parcels of land including appellant's land - On 24-6-1997, a declaration u/s. 6(1) of the 1894 Act was issued by the NCT, Delhi in respect of the land sought to be acquired including the land owned by the appellant - On 9-6-1999, notices u/ss. 9 and 10 of the 1894 Act with regard to the land in question were issued by the Land Acquisition Collector to the appellant - On 15-6-1999, the appellant filed a writ petition questioning the legality of the aforesaid acquisition notifications published u/ss. 4 and 6 of the 1894 Act - HC held that the acquisition of land by the NCT, Delhi was legal and valid and did not suffer from any legal infirmities - Hence, instant appeal - Whether acquisition notification elapsed as actual physical possession of land has not been taken by the Land Acquisition Collector even after 5 years after the award was passed as on the date of 2013 Act.

Held, the possession of the building structure situated on the appellant's land was not taken by him on 27-12-2013. Further, the memo of taking over possession of the acquired land must be in the presence of independent witnesses, undisputedly the same has not been done by respondent Nos. 2, 3 and 4 in the instant case. Therefore, the plea of the respondents that as per memo of possession dt. 31-12-2013 the possession of the said land of the appellant was taken and handed over to the DDA-respondent no.4 on the same day is not accepted as it is not done in fact and could not have been done in view of the interim order and also the same has not been done in the presence of independent witnesses as required in law. The acquisition proceedings in respect of the appellant's land have lapsed. The appeal is also allowed quashing the acquisition proceeding notifications in respect of the appellant's land. Appeal allowed.
