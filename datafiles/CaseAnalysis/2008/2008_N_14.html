Ramavilasom Grandhasala, Represented By Secy. And Another v N. S. S. Karayogam And Another

Supreme Court of India

27 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2564; 2009 (74) ALR 773

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Finding of fact, Gift deed

Summary: Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Gift deed - Finding of fact - Validity - Respondent filed suit alleging himself to be the owner of property in dispute - Original owner of property executed gift deed in favour of 'Yuva samithi' with condition that if Yuva samithi becomes defunct title and possession of property would revert to owner - Trial Court and First Appellate Court held that Yuva samithi was not defunct and was still working in different name - On appeal before HC, HC interferred with finding of fact of First appellate Court and held that the Yuva Samithi and Ramavilasom Grandhasala are different entities - Hence instant Appeal.

Held, The First Appellate Court based its finding that the Yuva Samithi is still functioning in a different name on the basis of Exs. B-1 to B-7 and it was incumbent upon the HC, if it wanted to interfere with this finding it has to examine Exs. B-1 to B-7 but it does not seem to have done so. The HC should specifically deal with Exs. B-1 to B-7 and other relevant material in support of the said finding of the First Appellate Court. The impugned judgment of the HC is set aside and the case is remanded to the HC to give a fresh decision in accordance with law. Appeal allowed.

Ratio: HC in second appeal can interfere with the judgment of the First Appellate Court only if there is an error of law and not an error of fact.

Legislation Cited

Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 s. 96
