State Of Himachal Pradesh v Mango Ram

Supreme Court of India

24 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 422; (2000) 7 SCC 224; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1331; AIR 2000 SC 2798; 2000 (3) CCR 138; 2000 (3) Crimes 179; 2000 CRLJ 4027; 2000 (2) JCC 712; JT 2000 (9) SC 408; 2000 (3) RCR(Criminal) 752; 2000(6) SCALE 89; 2000 (3) SCJ 283; 2000 (6) SLT 329; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 626; 2000 (5) Supreme 653

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal- Companies Act, 1956 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - IPC, 1860, s. 376 - Rape - Consent - Related teenagers - Prosecutrix was below 16 and resisted - Medical evidence - But no marks of violence - Submission of body under fear of terror construed as consent - Seven years after - Acquittal - Members of two families must have buried their hatchet - Issues - Acquittal challenged - Being serious offence consideration of trial court - Sentence - Held, acquittal on ground that there was consent, liable to be set aside - Evidence should be appreciated on broader probabilities and not to be carried away by insignificant contradictions - Sentence already undergone held sufficient - Appeal disposed of.
