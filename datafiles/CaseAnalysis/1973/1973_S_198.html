Bajwa and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

06 March 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, A. Alagiriswami, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 502; (1973) SCC (Cr) 596; (1973) 1 SCC 714; AIR 1973 SC 1204; 1973 CRLJ 769; 1973 CrLR(SC) 350; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 571

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Ballistics

Summary: Criminal - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 107, 161 and 423 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 149, 201 and 302 - Murder with rioting - Medical evidence did not support eye-witnesses - Trial Court acquitted all accused but on appeal convicted by High COurt - Held, HC rightly appreciated evidence - Trial Court gave undue importance to medical evidence - Prosecution witnesses claiming to have seen occurrence have named all appellants and approver has even named those acquitted by HC - But it would be safe only to convict those who are stated to have taken active part and about whose identity there can be no reasonable doubt - Some out of those to whom benefit of doubt is given were clearly amongst party of assailants and actively took part in occurrence though their identity cannot be safely fixed without risk of implicating possible innocent - Appeal partly allowed.
