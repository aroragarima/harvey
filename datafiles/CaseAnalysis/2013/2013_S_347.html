Ranjeet Goswami v State of Jharkhand and another

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 615; (2014) 1 SCC 588; 2013 CRLJ 4895; 2013 (4) JCC 2408; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 369; 2013(11) SCALE 577; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 497; 2014 (2) WLN(SC) 19

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Women & Children

Keywords: Date Of Occurrence

Summary: Criminal - Women & Children - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 302 and 376 - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000, s. 2(l) - Plea of juvenile - Denied - Challenged - Appellant was charge-sheeted for the offences punishable u/ss. 376, 302 and 201 of IPC, along with 3 others - Appellant, after submission of the charge-sheet, surrendered before the court and filed an application before the Chief Judicial Magistrate (CJM), stating that on the date of occurrence he was a juvenile as per the records kept in the Primary School - CJM forwarded the said application to the Juvenile Justice Board (JJ Board) to conduct an appropriate enquiry and to submit a report - JJ Board, accepted the report of the Medical Board and rejected the application holding that the appellant was not a juvenile on the date of occurrence - Sessions Judge allowed the appeal filed against said order on the ground that the JJ Board had not assigned any cogent reasons for discarding the School Admission Register and then to accept the medical report - HC allowed the criminal revision filed against said order and restored the order passed by the JJ Board - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld - Held, no cogent reasons have been stated by the HC to discard the school leaving certificate which was issued on 10-4-2004 by the then Principal of the school - Said certificate reveals the date of birth of the appellant was 10-5-1991 - There was no reason to discard said document - If that be so, as per the SC judgment in Ashwani Kumar Saxena v. State Of M.P 2012 Indlaw SC 308, there was no question of subjecting the appellant to a medical examination by a medical board - Going by the school leaving certificate since the appellant was a juvenile on the date of occurrence, he could be tried only by the JJ Board - Consequently, the order passed by the HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
