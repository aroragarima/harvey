Rajendra Prasad Mathur v Karnataka University and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 261; (1986) Supp SCC 740; AIR 1986 SC 1448; 1986(1) SCALE 981; [1986] 2 S.C.R. 912; 1986 (2) UJ 639

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Karnataka Universities Act, 1976

Summary: Education - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 136 - Karnataka State Universities Act, 1976, s. 12(5) - (A) Admission to B. E. Degree Course of Karnataka University for 1981-82 and subsequent academic years - Eligibility - Examinations passed by students from Rajasthan - If recognised, equivalent to qualifying examination passing of which constitutes basis of admission - (B) Appeal by special leave - Exercise of powers of Supreme Court - University determining equivalence of examinations of other Universities etc. with its own examination - (C) Ineligible students from Rajasthan admitted to B. E. Course of Karnataka University colleges - For capitation fee - Students pursuing course for about four years under Court orders - Permissibility - Held, not recorgnised, students are ineligible for admission - Supreme Court should not disturb decision taken by University - Colleges giving admission being responsible for wrongful admission, students are allowed to continue their studies - Appeal dismissed.
