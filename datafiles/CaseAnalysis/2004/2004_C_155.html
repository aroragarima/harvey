GTC Industries Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, Baroda

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 131; (2004) 3 SCC 704; AIR 2004 SC 4894; 2004 (92) ECC 504; 2004 (113) ECR 12; 2004 (164) E.L.T. 230; 2004 (2) G.L.R. 1808; 2004(5) SCALE 839B

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax; Trade

Keywords: Additional Excise Duty, Paid, Proviso To Section, Payment Of Duty, Schedule, Cost Of Packing, Levy Of Duty, Goods, Duty Paid, Sale Price, Price List, Normal Price, Amount Of Duty, Non-duty Paid, Additional Duties, Additional Duties Of Excise, Amount Of Duty Payable, Wholesale Price, Duties Of Excise, Already Paid, Additional Duty, Payable, Leviable, Cost Of Production, Payment Of Excise Duty, Profit Element, Place Of Removal, Ordinarily Sold, Removal Of Goods, Time And Place Of Removal, Wholesale Trade, Trade Discount, Selling Price, That Is To Say, Submission, Specific Rate Of Duty, Valuation Of Excisable Goods, Packed Condition, From So Much Of The Duty Of Excise Leviable Thereon, Determination Of Assessable Value, Excisable, For The Time Being In Force, Manufactured Tobacco, Duty Element, Appellate Tribunal, Central Government, Indian Tariff Act, 1934, Equivalent, Effective Duty, Effective Duty Of Excise, Payable On Such Goods

Summary: Valuation - Cigarettes manufactured from duty paid tobacco - Effective rate of duty - Notification No. 30/79-C.E. exempts cigarettes from duty of excise leviable thereon as is in excess of duty specified in Column 2 of the Table - As per proviso to Notification, in respect of cigarettes manufactured out of duty paid tobacco duty of excise as specified in table would stand reduced by Rs. 5.50 per thousand cigarettes - Whatever rate is mentioned in the table gets reduced by a sum of Rs. 5.50 per thousand cigarettes - Thus rate of duty would not be 400% plus Rs. Ill- but would be 400% plus Rs. 15.50/- for working out assessable value.
