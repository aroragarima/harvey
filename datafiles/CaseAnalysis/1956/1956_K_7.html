Â Keshav Nilkanth Joglekar v Commissioner of Police, Greater Bombay

Supreme Court of India

17 September 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, S.K. Das, P. Govinda Menon

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 103; AIR 1957 SC 28; 1957 CRLJ 10; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 653

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Preventive Detention Act, 1950

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950 - Preventive Detention Act, 1950, ss. 3 and 7 - Habeas Corpus - Petitioners detained by order of commissioner of police - Meaning of 'forthwith' in s. 3(3) of Act - Supreme Court relied over various Privy Council descisions - Held, 'forthwith' means to proceed with reasonable speed and expedition without unnecessary delay - If delay explained properly, no ground for release - Petition dismissed.
