IFCI Limited v Punjab Wireless Systems Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

17 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1528; (2008) 17 SCC 630

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Company shares, Interest amount

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1956, s. 457 - Companies (Court) Rules, 1959, r. 9 -Company shares - Interest amount - Entitled to - Respondent no.1 company owned 51 per cent shares in other company - Appellant advanced sum of Rs. 5 crores to other company by way of loan and was entitled to receive said amount of Rs 5 crores with interest thereon - Other company sought to be taken over by respondent no.3 company - Respondent no.2 M/s Motorola Inc. was owning 49 per cent of shares in other company - Respondent no.3 wanted to purchase entire shareholding of other company - There was agreement between appellant, other company and respondent no.2 - As per agreement, respondent no.3 agreed to pay Rs 2.5 crores plus accrued interest to appellant in discharge of liability under loan agreement between appellant and other company - Similarly, respondent no.2 also agreed to pay another Rs. 2.5 crores with accrued interest to appellant - Respondent no.3 offered sum of Rs. 78,26,011 to respondent no.1 towards acquisition of 51 per cent shareholding of latter - In meantime, went into liquidation and winding-up proceedings were initiated before Company Judge HC - Official Liquidator was appointed and assets of respondent no.1 were taken over by Official Liquidator - Respondent no.2 and respondent no.3 and appellant approached HC for direction to Official Liquidator of respondent no.1 to transfer shareholding of respondent no.1 favour of respondent no.3 for consideration of Rs. 78,26,011 - HC directed that respondent no.2 and respondent no.3 to pay to official liquidator the interest part that was payable by them to appellant - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that interest which was due to appellant should not be made payable to Official Liquidator on behalf of respondent no.1 as they have no right to claim that - Held, HC erred in directing payment of accrued interest to respondent no.1 as they were entitled to get only value of shares held by company - Official Liquidator submitted that as respondent no.1 wound up, huge amounts should be paid to secured and unsecured creditors and workers who were retrenched and that it was under such circumstances that HC passed the impugned order - It was submitted that in view of s. 457 of the Act r/w r. 9 of the Rules , HC was empowered to pass such orders - There was no force in contention of Official Liquidator, as respondent no.1 was entitled only to value of shares held by them in other company - Interest on loan advanced by appellant should not be claimed by respondent no.1 - Value of 51 per cent shares of respondent no.1 in other company was put at Rs. 85 lakhs instead of Rs. 78,00,000, which amount alone was now agreed to be paid - If that be so, having regard to huge liability of respondent no.1 SC directed that out of Rs. 90 lakhs deposited towards interest payable to appellant, amount of Rs. 7,00,000 be paid to Official Liquidator besides Rs 78,00,000 already ordered by impugned order - In all, Official Liquidator would receive Rs. 85,00,000 as value of 51% shares other company, held by respondent no.1 - Balance amount should be paid to appellant - HC order stood modified and Official Liquidator should pay balance amount of Rs. 83 lakhs to appellant - Appeal disposed of.
