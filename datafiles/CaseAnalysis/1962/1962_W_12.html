Workmen of The Bangalore Woollen, Cotton and Silk Mills Company, Limited v Its Management

Supreme Court of India

25 January 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 20; AIR 1962 SC 1363; 1962 (4) FLR 298; 1962 (1) LLJ 213

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 43 - Retrenchment - Dismissal - Gratuity claimed - Dispute arose between appellants-workmen and respondent-company - Respondent had agreed to pay gratuity and thus paid it some appellants whereas few appellants were discharged on account of old age and inefficiency but without any official enquiry as to their health and few were dismissed for misconduct - Appellants who were dismissed contended that they were entitled to gratuity - Tribunal held that appellants were not entitled to gratuity claimed by them hence, the present appeal - Whether, the appellants were entitled to gratuity u/s. 43 of 1947 Act - Held, one of the appellant was discharged for misconduct, that was a case of termination of service by way of disciplinary action and therefore not retrenchment within the definition and hence, were not entitled to the benefit of the award of gratuity - Another of these twenty-three appellants were discharged before 1-1-1953 and the award was only applied to workmen whose services had been terminated as from that date and therefore were not entitled to the benefit of the award of gratuity - It was the contract of service which was terminated and that contract which required certain physical fitness in the workmen therefore, appellants discharged on the ground of ill-health because they were unfit to discharge the service which they had undertaken to render and therefore it had really come to an end itself - Appellants' claim would be better under the Ordinance than under the 1947 Act, and because it was only under the Ordinance on which only the appellants could had based themselves, their claim could not be supported by the 1947 Act - Appeal dismissed.
