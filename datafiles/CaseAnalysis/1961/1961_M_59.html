Â State of Jammu Kashmir v Mir Gulam Rasul

Supreme Court of India

23 February 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 111; AIR 1961 SC 1301; 1961 (2) SCJ 39; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 969

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32(2A), 226, 311(2) - Public Servants (Inquiries) Act, 1977, s. 2 - Suspended - Demoted - Validity - Respondent/petitioner was Civil Engineer under appellant (Govt. of State of Jammu and Kashmir) - On 8-9-1954, respondent was placed under suspension by order of appellant and later on, passed another order on 12-2-1955, demoting respondent to post of Divisional Engineer - Aggrieved respondent filed petition before HC for writ which was issued as prayed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of HC was sustainable - Held, only fundamental right, however, on violation of which respondent counsel could rely in support of order of HC was that conferred by art. 14 of Constitution - - It seems that even if Rules were law and respondent had not been given benefit of them, all that could be said to have happened was that appellant had acted in breach of law, but that did not amount to violation of right to equal protection of laws - Otherwise, every breach of law by Govt. would amount to denial of equal protection of laws - SC was not aware of any authority in support of that proposition and none had been cited to SC, nor SC able to found any support for it in principle - However, it was not respondent's case that other servants of appellant had been given benefit of those Rules and such benefit had been designedly denied only to him - It seems to SC, that appeal might be allowed on simple ground that respondent's petition does not show violation of any fundamental right and HC, had no power to act u/art. 32 (2A) of Constitution - Hence, SC, thought it right to point out that arts. 226 and 311(2) of Constitution had not been applied to State of Jammu and Kashmir at any material time and no question of respondent's application being maintainable in view of those articles - Appeal allowed.
