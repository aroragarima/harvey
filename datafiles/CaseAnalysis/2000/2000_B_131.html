Patel Roadways Limited v Birla Yamaha Limited

Supreme Court of India

28 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2703; (2000) 4 SCC 91; AIR 2000 SC 1461; 2000 (3) ALD(SC) 21; 2000 (3) Bom.C.R. 312; 2000 (3) CompLJ 377; 2000 (1) CPJ 42; 2000 (1) CPJ(Sc) 42; 2000 (2) CPR 35; 2000 (3) CTC 59; 2000 (125) PLR 816; 2000(2) SCALE 559; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 665

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Consumer Protection; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Consumer Protection Act, 1986, ss. 9, 17, 18 and 21 - Consumer Forum's jurisdiction was in question - Complaint against carrier of goods - Attributing loss or damage to goods entrusted for transportation - Liability of a common carrier under Carriers Act is that of an insurer - This position is clear by provision in s. 9, in which it is specifically laid down that in case of claim of damage or loss to or deterioration of goods entrusted to a carrier, it is not necessary for plaintiff to establish negligence - Held suit could be entertained by Consumer Forums.
