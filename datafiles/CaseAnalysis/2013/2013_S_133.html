Lal Bahadur and others v State (NCT of Delhi)

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 210; (2013) 4 SCC 557; 2013 CRLJ 2205; 2013 (199) DLT 456; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 620; 2013(5) SCALE 467; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 744

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 149, 299, 302, 390, 395, 396, 436, 449 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 379 - Rioting - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused allegedly attacked deceased with sabbals, ballams, sariyas and lathis and burnt them alive - Accused were charged for offences punishable u/ss. 147, 149, 299, 302, 390, 395, 396, 436, 449 IPC - Trial Court acquitted accused and held that prosecution had failed to prove charges against appellants beyond all reasonable doubt - On appeal, HC reversed order of Trial Court and convicted appellants u/ss. 147, 149, 299, 302, 390, 395, 396, 436, 449 IPC and sentenced them to undergo RI - Hence, instant appeal - Whether conviction and sentence by HC was sustainable - Held, HC re-appreciated evidence of witnesses in detail and meticulously examined facts and circumstances of case in its right perspective and recorded a finding that prosecution had proved case against appellants - HC was entitled to re-appreciate entire evidence in order to find out whether findings recorded by Trial Court were perverse or unreasonable - Evidence of witnesses could not be brushed aside merely because of some minor contradictions, particularly for reason that evidence and testimonies of witnesses were trustworthy - Further, witnesses had consistently deposed with regard to offence committed by appellants and their evidence remain unshaken during their cross- examination - Mere marginal variation and contradiction in statements of witnesses could not be a ground to discard testimony of eye-witness who was none else but widow of one deceased - However, relationship could not be a factor to affect credibility of a witness - SC re-appraised entire evidence of prosecution witnesses including eye-witnesses and found that their testimonies had remained unshaken except some minor discrepancies which have to be ignored - Thus, HC correctly appreciated evidence and reversed findings of Trial Court - Appeal dismissed.
