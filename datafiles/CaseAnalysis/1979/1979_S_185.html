Mohammad Yousuf Rather v State of Jammu and Kashmir and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 August 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Shingal, R.S. Sarkaria, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 200; (1979) 4 SCC 370; (1979) SCC (Cr) 999; AIR 1979 SC 1925; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 258; 1979 UJ 708

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Freedom Of Movement

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(d), 19(5), 21, 22(5), 32 - Preventive Detention Act, 1950, Ss. 3, 3(1(a)(b) - Jammu and Kashmir Public Safety Act, 1978, Ss. 8(a)(i), 8(2) - Order of Detention - Fundamental Right - Maintenance of Public Order - Prejudice - Petitioner instigated "Educational" unemployed youth and a number of houses were set on fire, property looted and heavy damages caused to people - In this connection of case, FIR was registered against petitioner and others - Order of detention was passed by lower Court, as petitioner's activities were highly prejudicial to maintenance of public order - Petitioner was not allowed to exercise his fundamental right of making a representation u/art. 22 (5) of Constitution and order of detention, not communicated to him - Hence instant petition - Whether act of petitioner fall within mischief u/s. 8(3)(b) of the Act and was prejudicial to maintenance of public order u/s. 8(1)(a)(i) of the Act - Held, petitioner had instigated educated unemployed youth to go on hunger strike for purpose of pressing their demand for employment, would not amount to acting in any manner prejudicial to maintenance of public order as grounds of detention were vague - This Court had disapproved vagueness in grounds of detention, as it impinged fundamental right of detenue u/art. 22(5) of Constitution to make a representation against order of detention - So detention of petitioner was illegal and entitled to an order of release for that reason alone - Law providing for preventive detention and action taken under such a law, to pass should have to satisfy requirements of Arts. 19 and 22 of Constitution - Grounds of detention was therefore irrelevant and could not justify order of detention u/s. 8 of the Act - Petition allowed.
