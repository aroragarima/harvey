R. R. S. Chouhan and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 2218; (1995) Supp3 SCC 109; (1995) SCC (L&S) 1098; 1995 (30) ATC 539; 1995 (1) CLR 1007; 1995 (71) FLR 66; JT 1995 (3) SC 400; 1995(2) SCALE 436; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 1158; 1995 (2) SLJ 142; 1995 (2) SLR 441; 1995 (2) UPLBEC 796

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Service

Summary: Service - Administrative - All India Services Act, 1951 - Indian Forest Service (Initial Recruitment) Regulations, 1966 - Indian Forest Service (Cadre) Rules, 1966, rr. 8, 9, 10 - Cadre post - Appointment by promotion - Claim for seniority - Eligibility - Appellants were selected and appointed to post of Assistant Conservator of Forests in Forest Service of State of Madhya Pradesh in 1966 and 1967 after training and their posts were confirmed on 1-10-1968 - In 1966, service was constituted under the 1951 Act and came to be governed by various rules and regns provisions of which were substantially same as those contained in rules and regns governing IAS and IPS - State Govt by Order dt. 23-1-1974 abolished 27 posts in senior pay scale of Rs. 700-1250 in cadre of said service and in their place equal number of temporary posts of Officer on Special Duty (OSD) in pay scale of Rs. 680 - 1000-EB-1150 were created in State Forest Service - Appellants were promoted to posts of OSD in pay scale of Rs 680-1150 in 1977 - While they were thus officiating, appellants names were included in select list for year 1978 but could not be appointed as they were down in merit list - Again, select list for 1985 contained names of all appellants and by order dt. 24-9-1985, appellants were appointed to service and were allotted Madhya Pradesh Cadre - They were assigned 1981 as year of allotment for purpose of seniority in Service - Appellants filed application before Tribunal for redressal of their grievance that they should have been assigned 1971 as year of allotment as they were continuously holding senior post of Deputy conservator of Forests, cadre post in service uninterruptedly from 1977 to till date of their appointment to service - Tribunal dismissed application by Order dt. 9-8-1988 - Hence, instant appeal - Whether for purpose of determination of seniority of appellants, their officiation on post of OSD should be taken into account - Held, it was found that appointment of appellants was not on post of Deputy Conservator of Forests but on post of OSD, post carrying lower pay scale and falling in State Forest Service - Further, even if it be assumed that appellants were continuously officiating in senior post in Service during period 1977-85 they should not avail benefit of said officiation for purpose of seniority because after select list of 1978 which included their names next select list for year 1979 did not contain their names and so also their names were not included in select lists for years 1981 and 1984 - Names of some of appellants were included in select lists for years 1980, 1982 and 1983 and names of all appellants were included in select list for year 1985 - Explanation 1 to r. 3 (2Xc) would envisage that officer who was appointed to Service by promotion could take benefit of period of continuous officiation in senior post for purpose of seniority if, on date of his appointment to Service,

(a) he had been continuously officiating in senior post, and

(b) his name was in select list - Both these requirements must co-exist not only at stage of commencement of period but also during entire period for which benefit was claimed -

If either of these conditions ceased to exist at any stage before appointment to Service, there would be break in continuity of officiation and benefit of officiation would not be available for purpose of seniority - Fact that appellants were officiating in senior post during period when their names were not in select list, by itself, would not enable them to obtain benefit of such officiation for purpose of seniority - Appellants were, therefore, not entitled to count period of continuous officiation in post of OSD during period 1977-85 for purpose of determination of their seniority and Tribunal rightly denied benefit of such officiation to appellants - Appeal dismissed.
