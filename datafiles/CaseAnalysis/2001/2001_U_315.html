Common Cause, A Registered Society v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

22 November 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, S.S.M. Quadri, N. Santosh Hegde, S.N. Variava, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 207; (2002) 1 SCC 88; AIR 2002 SC 199; 2002 (92) FLR 287; JT 2001 (9) SC 593; 2001(8) SCALE 259; 2002 (1) SLR 174; 2001 (8) SLT 153; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 323; 2001 (8) Supreme 400

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Parliament & Legislature; Service

Keywords: Salaries and Allowances of Members of Parliament Act 1954, Salaries, Allowances and Pension of Members of Parliament Act, Salaries and Allowances of Members of Parliament (Amendment) Act, 1976

Summary: The competence to enact Section 8A of Salaries, Allowances and Pension of Members of Parliament Act is conferred upon Parliament by the residuary Entry 97 of List I, and there is no provision in Article 106 or elsewhere that bars the payment of pension to Members of Parliament.
