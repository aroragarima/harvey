Commissioner of Central Excise and Customs v Messrs I.T.C. Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 872; (2007) 1 SCC 62; JT 2006 (9) SC 469; 2006(11) SCALE 81; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 167

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Central Excise Act, 1944 s.11-Whether writ petition questioning that the completion of an assessment proceeding is a sine qua non for issuance of notice u/s.11-A of the Central Excise Act, 1944 is maintainable-After final assessment, a copy of the order on the return filed by the assessee has to be sent to him. Duty has to be paid by the assessee on the basis of the final assessment within ten days' time from the receipt of the return. No question of giving any notice u/s.11-A arises in such a case - It is only when even after final assessment and payment of duties, it is found that there has been a short-levy or non-levy of duty, the Excise Officer is empowered to take proceedings u/s.11-A within the period of limitation after issuing a show-cause notice. In such a case, limitation period will run from the date of the final assessment. In this case, the provisional assessment earlier made by the proper officer has been quashed and pursuant to the direction of the High Court, the proper officer has made the final assessment-Held, no question of failure of issuance of show-cause notice u/S.11-A arises in this case. Court does not find any infirmity in the order of the Tribunal-Held, appeal dismissed
