Â Shree Meenakshi Mills Limited, Madurai and Others v Sri A. V. Visvanatha Sastri and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 October 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Ghulam Hasan, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 74; AIR 1955 SC 13; [1954] 26 ITR 713; 1955 (1) MLJ(SC) 21; 1955 SCJ 62; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 787

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 34(1-A) - Taxation Of Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947, ss. 3, 5(1) - Taxation on Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947 - Rational Classification - Discrimination - Infringement -Jurisdiction - U/s. 5(1) of 1947 Act an official of respondent/Commission summoned petitioners and called upon to produce a number of statements and books of account and reported to Central Govt. that assesses and members of family had evaded income-tax and u/s. 5(4) of 1947 Act - Appellants filed appeal u/art. 32 of Constitution for restraining respondent/Commission from taking any action against him under the Act 1947 - Hence instant appeal - Whether s. 5(1) of Act 1947 infringed art. 14 of Constitution, as it was not based on rational classification and Whether classification was void, unenforceable, and discriminatory - Held, no justification for discriminating petitioners under 1947 Act - Thus s. 5(4) of Act, 1947 was hit by art.14 of Constitution and was void and unenforceable - Provisions of s. 5(1) of 1947Act were impugned as they contravened guarantee of equal protection u/art. 14 of Constitution and respondent had no jurisdiction to deal with cases of petitioners by applying discriminatory and drastic procedure and could not proceed under those provisions against petitioners - Court therefore directed that an appropriate writ be issued against respondent/Commission prohibiting it from proceeding further with petitioners under act 1947 - Order accordingly.
