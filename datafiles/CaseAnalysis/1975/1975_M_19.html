Madurai District Central Co-operative Bank Limited v Third Income Tax Officer, Madurai

Supreme Court of India

28 July 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, A.C. Gupta, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 330; (1975) SCC (Tax) 374; (1975) 2 SCC 454; AIR 1975 SC 2016; 1975 CTR 220; [1975] 101 ITR 24; 1976 (1) MLJ(SC) 1; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 136

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Association Of Persons, Legislative Powers, Excess Profits Tax, Capital Gains, Co-Operative Bank, Co-Operative Society, Business Income or Loss

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 81, 2(1)(a) and 2(8), - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 133(1)(a), 133(1)(c) and 271 - Finance Act, 1963, s. 2(8) - Appellant is a co-operative society engaged in business of banking - Challenge to assessment order, levying additional surcharge on its residual income - Held, additional surcharge indubitably partakes nature and essential characteristics of income tax - It is a tax on residual income - 'Residual income' would include non-business income which under Income tax Act is chargeable to income - Thus, additional surcharge, though levied by Act of 1963, independently of Act of 1961, is but a mode of levying tax on a portion of assessee's income computed in accordance with definition in s. 2(8) of Act of 1963 - Notice of demand u/s. 156 of Act of 1961 can lawfully call for payment of amount due from an assessee by way of additional surcharge - Judgment of High Court confirmed - Order accordingly.
