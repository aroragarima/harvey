Bench

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Rash and negligent driving - Enhancement of compensation - Entitlement of - Tractor was driven in rash and negligent manner by respondent no.1 which hit appellant's tempo due to which appellant sustained grievous injuries - Appellant filed claim petition before Tribunal who determined permanent disability at 30% and awarded compensation to appellant - Appellant filed appeal before HC for enhancement of compensation - After re-determination of compensation, HC held that appellant was entitled to enhancement - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether appellant is entitled for further enhancement of compensation.

Held, wage rate as per the minimum wage notification is only a yardstick and not an absolute factor to be taken to determine the compensation under the future loss of income. It would be just and reasonable to consider the appellant's daily wage at Rs.150/- per day as he was a driver of the motor vehicle. It can be inferred that Tribunal wrongly determined the loss of income during the course of his treatment and was liable to be enhanced. With respect to the permanent disablement, Tribunal and HC erroneously had taken the extent of permanent disability at 30% and 55% respectively for the calculation of amount towards the loss of future earning capacity. No doubt, doctor has assessed permanent disability of appellant at 55%. Therefore, cost incurred during medical treatment liable to be enhanced. Appellant is entitled for enhancement of award for other heads with interest. Hence, total compensation payable to the appellant by the respondent Insurance Company should be enhanced as awarded against different heads with interest. Appeal allowed.
