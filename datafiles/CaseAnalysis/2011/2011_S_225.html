Ashok @ Dangra Jaiswal v State Of M.P.

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 221; (2011) 5 SCC 123; AIR 2011 SC 1335; 2011 (2) JabLJ 1; 2011 (2) JCC (Narcotics) 111; JT 2011 (4) SC 34; 2011 (4) MLJ(Crl) 188; 2011 (2) OLR 161; 2011 (2) RCR(Criminal) 611; 2011 (3) RLW 1883; 2011(4) SCALE 273; [2011] 4 S.C.R. 253

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Narcotics Drugs & Psychotropic Substance Act, 1985, ss. 8 and 21(b) - Possession of contraband - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant was convicted u/s. 8 r/w s. 21(b) of the NDPS Act - Appeal filed thereagainst was dismissed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Held, alleged narcotic powder seized from the possession of the appellant was never produced before the Trial Court as a material exhibit and there was no explanation for its non-production - There was, thus, no evidence to connect the forensic report with the substance that was seized from the possession of the appellant - Appellant was entitled to the benefit of doubt - Appellant acquitted of the charges - Judgments and orders passed by the Trial Court and HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
