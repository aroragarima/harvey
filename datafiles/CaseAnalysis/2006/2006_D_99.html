Duncans Industries Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

22 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 586; (2006) 7 SCC 642; 2006 (201) E.L.T. 517; 2006(8) SCALE 463; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 860

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Finance (No.2) Act, 1998

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, ss. 4(4)(d)(i) and 11 A - Finance (No 2) Act, 1998 - Customs Act, 1962 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 9B, 9(2) and 52A - Whether tribunal had erred in setting aside duty liability adjudicated in earlier proceedings while upholding levy of penalty? - After grant of certificate under Kar Vivad Samadan Scheme, 1998 as having settled dispute and payment of amount determined no further proceedings could be initiated or proceeded with for period in question - There is not even an allegation much less finding by department that there has been any clandestine removal of goods without assessment - Held, no merits in appeal of revenue - Tribunal erred in upholding levy of penalty - Assessee's appeal allowed.
