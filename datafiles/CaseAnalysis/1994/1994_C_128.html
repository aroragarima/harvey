A.P. State Electricity Borad v Commissioner of Central Excise

Supreme Court of India

01 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1884; (1994) 2 SCC 428; 1994 (53) ECR 349; 1994 (70) E.L.T. 3; JT 1994 (1) SC 545; 1994 (1) RLT 44; 1994(1) SCALE 281; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 499; 1994 (95) STC 595

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 3 - Whether the prestressed cement concrete poles manufactured by the appellant, Andhra Pradesh State Electricity Board, are 'goods' within the meaning of s. 3? - Fact that in Kerala these poles are manufactured by independent contractors who sell them to Kerala State Electricity Board itself shows that such poles do have a market and hence, they are 'goods' within the meaning of s. 3 - Marketability of articles does not depend upon the number of purchasers nor is the market confined to the territorial limits of country - Held, conclusion arrived at by the Tribunal unobjectionable - Order accordingly.
