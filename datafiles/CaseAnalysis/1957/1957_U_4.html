Â Union of India v Chaman Lal Loona

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, P. Govinda Menon, Syed Jaffer Imam, Sudhi Ranjan Das

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 85; AIR 1957 SC 652; 1957 SCJ 719; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 1039

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Independence Order, 1947, art. 8 - Defence Order, 1947 -Arbitration Act, 1940, art. 8 - Indian Independence Act, 1947, art. 8 and 20 - Whether High Court is right in its view as to true scope and effect of relevant provisions of Independence Order, 1947, and Defence Order, 1947? - Three reasons are: (i) that on a true construction of art. 8 of Independence Order, 1947, contract under consideration in this case was as from appointed day (i.e.15-08-1947), a contract exclusively for purposes of Dominion of Pakistan and all rights and liabilities accrued shall be rights and liabilities of Dominion of Pakistan; (ii) that Defence Order, 1947, which set up a Joint Defence Council and provides for exercise of certain powers of control by said Council under art. 8 of that Order did not in any way affect rights and liabilities arising out of contract, which were governed by Independence Order, 1947; and (iii) claim in present case did not relate to military stores, Defence Order, 1947, had no application at all to facts of this case - Held, set aside judgment and decree of HC, and restore those of learned Subordinate Judge - Appeal allowed.
