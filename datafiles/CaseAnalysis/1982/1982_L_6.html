Rani Choudhury v Lt. Col. Suraj Jit Choudhury

Supreme Court of India

24 August 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 194; (1982) 2 SCC 596; AIR 1982 SC 1397; 1982 (2) DMC 330; 1982(1) SCALE 657; [1983] 1 S.C.R. 372; 1982 UJ 594

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 9 r. 13 - Dismissal of appeal - Applicability of Explanation in O. 9 r. 13 - Whether the dismissal of an appeal against an ex parte decree on the ground that the appeal is barred by limitation attracts the provisions contained in the Explanation in O. 9 r.13 of the CPC Code of Civil Procedure and creates a bar to the maintainability of an application under O. 9 r.13 of the CPC for setting aside the ex parte decree.

Held, if any appeal against an ex-parte decree has been disposed of on any ground other than the ground that the appellant has withdrawn the appeal, no application for setting aside the ex-parte decree under O. 9 r.13 of the CPC will be entertained. Further, where there has been an appeal against an ex- parte decree and the appeal has not been withdrawn by the appellant and has been disposed of on any ground, the application under O. 9 r.13 of the CPC will not lie and cannot be entertained. Further, the words used in Explanation make it abundantly clear that disposal of the appeal as contemplated in the Explanation is not intended to mean or imply disposal on merits resulting in the merger of the decree of the Trial Court with the decree, if any, of the Appellate Court on the disposal of the appeal. As the dismissal of the appeal on the ground of limitation results in the disposal of the appeal on any ground other than the ground of the withdrawal of the appeal by the appellant, the explanation is attracted, and the application for setting aside the ex-parte decree becomes in-competent after the disposal of the appeal and cannot be entertained. Hence, the application for setting aside the ex-parte decree does not lie and cannot be entertained, in view of the provisions contained in the Explanation. Appeal allowed.
