Dinesh Kumar Gupta v United India Insurance Co. Ltd. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Gyan Sudha Misra, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 840; (2010) 12 SCC 770; 2011 (2) ALD(SC) 16; 2011 (1) AWC 747; 2010 (4) JhrCR(SC) 163; JT 2010 (12) SC 81; 2011 (4) MLJ(SC) 683; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 696; 2011 (1) RLW 117; 2010(10) SCALE 647; 2010 (4) WLN(SC) 38

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution

Keywords: Rules of the High Court of Judicature for Rajasthan, 1952

Summary: Constitution - Advocates & Judges - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss. 2 (c) (iii) and 18 - Contempt petition - Petitioner (Insurance Company) challenged the award passed by the MACT, in favour of the claimant and others before HC - Single Judge on 22.03.2001 issued notice to the claimants and other respondents and also passed an order of stay in favour of the petitioner - Single Judge also directed that a copy of the said order be sent to the Registrar (Vigilance) of the HC to look into the matter from the administrative side implying enquiry against the Judge, MACT who had passed the award in favour of the claimants - In order to comply the order of the HC, the Registrar General (Vigilance) requested for a copy of the Award of the MACT and also asked whether writ petition filed against said order had been disposed of or not - Registrar General (Vigilance) was informed that writ petition against said order was pending - No action was taken after that - Subsequently, on 31.05.2003, the then Presiding Officer of MACT, who had passed the award of compensation in favour of the claimant retired from service and matter remained sub-judice - Appellant joined the post of the Deputy Registrar (Judicial) in the HC on 05.01.2005 and on 09.05.2005 he directed the Deputy Registrar (Judicial) that the copy of the required judgment of the HC passed in the writ petition filed against order of MACT be sent to the office of the Registrar General (Vigilance) - In the meantime, the claimant had also filed an application for vacating the order of stay passed by the HC - Single Judge on this occasion suddenly inferred that although an order had been passed staying implementation of the award passed in favour of the claimant, yet the copy of the order and stay had not been sent for compliance - Hence, it was inferred by the Single Judge that it appeared to be an attempt on the part of the Officer concerned to ensure that the Registrar (Vigilance) should not proceed with the enquiry against the MACT Judge and this not only amounted to contempt of the order of the Court, but was an attempt to shield the then MACT Judge who had passed the award and later retired from service - Single Judge issued contempt notice against the appellant who challenged said order submitting that he had joined the post of Deputy Registrar (Judicial) in the HC on 5.1.2005 could have possibly no role for shielding or protecting the officer who had retired on 31.05.2003 - (A) Whether the Single Judge of the HC was justified in initiating suo moto contempt proceeding against the appellant (Judicial Officer)? - Held, no -The Single Judge inferred and assumed erroneously that the appellant had the intention to obstruct the administration of justice by being instrumental in ensuring that the interim order passed in 2001 may not be implemented oblivious of the fact that the appellant was posted in the registry of the HC only 4 years later in 2005 and hence non-implementation of the interim order of stay cannot be attributed to the appellant - Thus, initiation of the contempt proceeding against the petitioner by the Single Judge is based on a wholly wrong premise based on unsustainable and unfounded facts which cannot be treated sufficient material so as to initiate contempt proceeding - (B) Whether a proceeding for contempt initiated against the appellant can be held to be sustainable merely on speculation, assumption and inference drawn from facts and circumstances of the instant case? - Held, no - Contempt of a civil nature can be held to have been made out only if there has been a wilful disobedience of the order and even though there may be disobedience, yet if the same does not reflect that it has been a conscious and wilful disobedience, a case for contempt cannot be held to have been made out - Impugned order of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
