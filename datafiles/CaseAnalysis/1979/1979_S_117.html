V. C. Shukla v State Through C. B. I

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.N. Shingal, D.A. Desai, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 331; (1980) Supp SCC 92; (1980) SCC (Cr) 695; AIR 1980 SC 962; 1980 CRLJ 690; 1980 CRLR 122; 1980 MLJ 664; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 380

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Civil Case, Warrant Case, Indian Bar Councils Act, 1926, Prohibition Act, Board Of Revenue Act, 1913, The Special Courts Act, 1979

Summary: Special Courts Act, 1979, s. 11 - CrPC, 1973, s. 397(2) - (A) Expression 'interlocutory order' in s. 11(1) of 1979 Act - Interpretation of Statutes - (B) Validity of appeal against Order of framing of charge is interlocutory - Held, has been used in natural sense, and not in wider sense as u/s. 397 (2) of CrPC, 1973 - Appeal against same is incompetent - Appeal Dismissed.
