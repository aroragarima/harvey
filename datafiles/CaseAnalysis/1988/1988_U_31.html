Chuharmal v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 294; (1988) 3 SCC 257; (1988) SCC (Cr) 664; AIR 1988 SC 1474; 1988 CrLR(SC) 341; 1988 (18) ECC 11; 1988 (17) ECR 209; 1988 (35) E.L.T. 612; JT 1988 (2) SC 433; 1988(1) SCALE 1105; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 797; 1988 (2) UJ 137

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Sea Customs Act 1962

Summary: Customs - Sea Customs Act 1962 - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 111 110, 124(a) 112, 123 - (A) Seizure of goods - Notice u/s.124(a) - Extension of period of notice - Whether opportunity of hearing (which is a must) is given to person concerned - (B) Arbitrariness in determination of its quantum of penalty - Plea that as there was no evidence to determine value of watches seized, penalty levied was arbitrary - And that quantification of the penalty was very high - Value in show cause notice not controverted - Burden u/s.123 not discharged - Sustainability of plea considering delay in paying penalty - Held on facts, notice was given in instant case - Held, is not sustainable - Consideration of benefit derived by person concerned by not paying penalty for years, not always a safe guide to decide arbitrariness - Petition rejected.
