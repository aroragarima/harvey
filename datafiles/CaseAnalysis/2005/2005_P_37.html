State of Madhya Pradesh through C.B.I. etc. v Paltan Mallah and others

Supreme Court of India

20 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 27; (2005) 3 SCC 169; (2005) SCC (Cr) 674; AIR 2005 SC 733; 2005 (3) CalLT(SC) 34; 2005 CRLJ 918; JT 2005 (1) SC 531; 2005 (1) RCR(Criminal) 949; 2005(1) SCALE 446; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 710

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ballistics

Summary: [A.] Arms Act, 1959, ss.25 , 27 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302, 34 , 120B - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.313 , 294 , s.302 , s.307 , s.323 , s.341 , s.353 , s.370 , s.380 , s.394 , s.397 , s.457 , s.120B , s.506-B -Shankar Guha Yogi case -Murder of trade unionist - Acquittal - A1 to A8 were sentenced to life imprisonment and A9 to capital punishment by Second Addl Sessions Judge - HC acquitted all accused - Appeal against acquittal - Extra-judicial confession made by the accused Paltan Mallah to PW-124 is unimpeachable - Recovery of the country-made pistol at his instance - Held, though there is no direct and convincing evidence against other accused, case against A9 is proved beyond reasonable doubt - He was acquitted by HC on fanciful reasons - Evidence of extra-judicial confessions was rejected without any valid reasons - Report of ballistic expert also was not appreciated in correct perspective by HC - DB seriously erred in acquitting respondent-accused A9 - Evidence of extra-judicial confessions was rejected without any valid reasons - Acquital of respondent accused set aside - Sentenced to imprisonment for life - All other appeals w.r.t. other accused dismissed. [B.] Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss.30 , 32 - Held, though there is no direct and convincing evidence against other accused, the case against Paltan Mallah is proved beyond reasonable doubt. He was acquitted by the High Court on fanciful reasons. The evidence of extra-judicial confessions was rejected without any valid reasons. The report of the ballistic expert also was not appreciated in the correct perspective by the High Court.
