Malojirao Narasingarao Shitole v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

07 March 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 552; (1969) 2 SCC 723; AIR 1969 SC 953; 1969 MPLJ 479; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 901

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Jagirdar, Possession Of Land, Board Of Revenue Act, 1913

Summary: Civil Procedure - Board Of Revenue Act, 1913 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Madhya Bharat Abolition of Jagirs Act, 1951 - Ryotwari Act, 1948 - Appellant was a Jagirdar, whose Jagir was resumed by the State on 4-12-1952, under the 1951 Act, and he received a memorandum from the Jagir Commissioner, on 23-2-1958, regarding the compensation payable to him, u/s. 13, of the 1951 Act - Appeal was filed by the appellant, under Art. 227 of the Constitution, against the HC, refusing to quash an order made by the Board of Revenue of the State throwing out the appeal of the appellant on the ground that it was barred by time - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the Board of Revenue, rightly threw out the appeal on the ground of it being barred by time - Held, that the Board of Revenue had not acted judicially in throwing out the appeal, and the HC was therefore not right in upholding the order of the Board of Revenue which ignored the provisions of ss. 34 and 149 (2) of the 1948 Act and the relevant provisions of the 1908 Act - If ss. 34 and 149(2), of the 1948 Act, were applicable to the facts of the case before the Board of Revenue, the time spent in obtaining a copy of the judgment forming the basis or giving the reasons of the decision should have been excluded in computing the period of 90 days under s. 29 of the 1951 Act - Appellant was not guilty of any laches in his appeal to the Board of Revenue - Appeal allowed.
