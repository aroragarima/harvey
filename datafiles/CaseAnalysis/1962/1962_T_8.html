Â Calcutta Gas Company (Proprietary) Limited v State of West Bengal and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 318; AIR 1962 SC 1044; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Parliament & Legislature; Petroleum & Gas

Summary: Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 32, 246, 249, 250 and 252 - Industries (Development And Regulation) Act, 1951, s. 2 - Oriental Gas Company Act, 1960, s. 20 - Constitutional validity of 1960 Act - (A) Whether legislature of state has exclusive power to make law in respect of gas industry? - Held, no power by virtue of entry 24 list II - (B) Constitutional validity of 1960 Act - Whether impugned Act was within legislative competence of State Legislature and was, therefore, validly made? - Court expressed view that Legislature of a State has exclusive power to make law in respect of gas industry by virtue of entry 25 of List II, and that entry 24 does not comprehend gas industry - S. 20, therefore, will not avail appellant to question validity of State action - Further held, therefore, court agree with High Court that impugned Act was within legislative competence of State Legislature and was validly made - Appeal dismissed.
