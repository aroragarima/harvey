Vijay Pal Singh and others v State of Uttarakhand

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 861; AIR 2015 SC 684; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 2841; 2015 CRLJ 1338; JT 2014 (14) SC 217; 2015 (1) MLJ(Crl) 116; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 393; 2014(14) SCALE 142

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code,1860, ss. 34, 201, 302, 304B and 498A - Dowry death - Conviction - Challenged - Appellants faced trial for offences punishable u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of the IPC, s. 304B r/w s. 34 of IPC, s. 498A of IPC and s. 201 of IPC - Sessions court acquitted all of them; but in appeal by the State, the HC convicted appellants u/s. 304B r/w s. 34 of IPC, s. 498A of IPC and s. 201 of IPC - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, once the ingredients of s. 304B of IPC are established, the presumption is that the death has been caused by the husband or his relatives, who caused the cruelty or harassment. That presumption can safely be drawn in the instant case, as all the ingredients u/s. 304B of IPC have been proved beyond doubt in the instant case particularly since there is no direct evidence on the part of the appellants to rebut the same. Further, since the independent witnesses PWs5 and 6 have recognized only the father-in-law and husband of the deceased (appellants Nos. 1 and 2), it will not be safe to conclude the offence u/ss. 304B, 498A or 201 of IPC as proved against appellants Nos. 3 and 4. Therefore, the conviction and sentence as against appellants Nos. 3 and 4 are set aside. The conviction and sentence as against appellants Nos. 1 and 2, are upheld. Appeal partly allowed.

Ratio - Once the ingredients of s. 304B of IPC are established, the presumption is that the death has been caused by the husband or his relatives, who caused the cruelty or harassment.
