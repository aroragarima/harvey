Shiv Shankar and Others v Board of Directors, U.P.S.R.T.C. and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 November 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 757; (1995) Supp2 SCC 726; (1995) SCC (L&S) 1018; 1995 (30) ATC 317; 1996 (1) LLJ 12

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Petition filed for a direction to State Road Transport Corporation to absorb appellants as conductors as Corporation was acting arbitrarily - High Court by interim order directed respondents to absorb appellants - Appellants were absorbed - However, petition dismissed by HC as infructuous - Appellants filed present appeal - Held, direction by HC to absorb respondents within a period of three months amounted to disposal of petition and yet HC had issued notice only - Issue of notice at this state unless there were other necessary parties to be heard was not of any purpose - Interim order is granted by court to protect right or interest of a party approaching court till claim is adjudicated finally - But order of HC directing respondents to absorb appellants could not be termed as interim order - Such order could be granted only by way of final adjudication as a result of decision on merits - However, to do justice set aside order passed and remit matter to HC - Appeal disposed of.
