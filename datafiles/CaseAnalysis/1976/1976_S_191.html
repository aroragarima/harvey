Krishnanand Agnihotri v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

17 December 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, A.C. Gupta, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 418; (1977) SCC (Cr) 190; (1977) 1 SCC 816; AIR 1977 SC 796; 1977 BBCJ 112; 1977 CRLJ 566; 1977 CRLR 57

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Joint Account

Summary: Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5(3) - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 101, 102, 103 and 104 - (A) Presumption u/s. 5(3) - Conditions for - Disproportion between pecuniary resources and known sources of income of accused - (B) Benami transaction - Nature of onus and proof - Lies on the person who asserts it - To be discharged by adducing legal evidence of a definite character - Held suspicion however strong cannot take the place of proof - Possession of pecuniary resources or property disproportionate to accused's known sources of income must be shown and he should be unable to satisfactorily account for such pecuniary resources or property - Appeal Allowed.
