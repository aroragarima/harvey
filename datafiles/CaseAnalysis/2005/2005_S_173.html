Commissioner of Income Tax, Rajkot v Shatrusailya Digvijaysingh Jadeja

Supreme Court of India

01 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 528; (2005) 7 SCC 294; 2005 (197) CTR 590; [2005] 277 ITR 435; JT 2005 (8) SC 50; 2005(7) SCALE 78; [2005] Supp2 S.C.R. 1119; [2005] 147 TAXMAN 566

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-Tax Act, 1961, ss. 143(3), 260A - Wealth Tax Act, 1957, ss. 246(A), 264 - Finance (No.2) Act, 1998, ss. 86, 98 - Kar Vivad Samadhan Scheme, 1998 - Whether department was right in rejecting Kar Vivad Samadhan Scheme declarations filed by respondent-assessee on ground that assessments had become final in year 1992 -93 and respondent filed revisions under Income Tax Act and Wealth Tax Act only to obtain benefit of Scheme, which came into force w.e.f. 1/9/1998? - Litigation was not pending on date of enactment of Scheme - Meaning of 'pending' - Orders of designated authority rejecting declarations filed by assessee are quashed - Appeal dismissed.
