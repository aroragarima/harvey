Commissioner of Income Tax, Assam, Tripura and Manipur v Jwalaprasad Agarwala

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 287; [1967] 66 ITR 154

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Gift To Minor

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Assessment - Evidence - Partnership - Gifts - Respondent divided balance of his capital account as partner in four equal parts and made gift to each of his four minor sons - Income-tax Officer included minors share sum in income of assessee for assessment - Hence, assessee appealed to Appellate Assistant Commissioner and it was rejected - Appeal made to Income-tax Appellate Tribunal was rejected - HC on reference, answered in favour of assessee - Hence instant appeal - Whether there was evidence to justify minor had admitted as partner - Held, there could had no reason why minor should be admitted to benefits of partnership in respect of department - no evidence of fact that minor's share of profits in firm arose out of assets transferred by father - There was no evidence on record to justify that minor had admitted as partner in firm - Appeal dismissed.
