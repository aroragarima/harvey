Smt. Ramkanya Bai And Another. v Jagdish And Others

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 412; (2011) 7 SCC 452; 2011 (4) AWC 3901; 2011 (4) CGLJ 58; JT 2011 (7) SC 515; 2011 (7) MLJ(SC) 797; 2011 (4) MPLJ 298; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 956; 2012 (2) RLW 1248; 2011(7) SCALE 50; [2011] 7 S.C.R. 817

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Easements

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Indian Easements Act, 1882, s. 18 - Madhya Pradesh Land Revenue Code, 1959, ss. 131, 257, 44 - Right to way - Easements - Jurisdiction - - Respondent no. 1 claims to be owner of a land made an application to Tehsildar, u/s. 131 of the Code claiming right of way over appellants land, to reach his lands - Tehsildar made on order holding that respondent no. 1, was entitled to pass through Govt. Road, and thereafter pass through land belonging to appellants, for reaching his land and appellants should not obstruct such passage - Appeal by appellants was dismissed and subsequent revision filed by appellants was dismissed - Appellants filed Suit for following reliefs declaration that respondent no. 1 did not have any right of way over their lands bearing to reach his lands and that they were entitled to enjoy their lands without any interference from respondent no. 1 and injunction restraining respondent no. 1 from creating/ constructing any new passage, over their lands - Suit was dismissed by Trial Court, on ground that having regard to s. 131 r/w s. 257 of the Code, Revenue Court (Tehsildar) alone had jurisdiction to grant relief on the basis of custom and convenience of parties, and it did not have jurisdiction - First and Second appeals filed by appellants was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Civil Court had no jurisdiction to entertain suit by owner of land for declaration that defendant did not had easementary right, customary or otherwise, over his property and order of Tehsildar u/s. 131 of the Code recognizing such right, was illegal and erroneous - Held, decision of Tehsildar after summary enquiry with reference to 'previous custom' and with due regard to conveniences of all parties, u/ s. 131(1) of the Code, was open to challenge in civil suit and subject to decision of Civil Court - Jurisdiction of Civil Court to try any suit relating to easements was not affected by ss. 131, 242 or 257 of the Code - Thus, was declared that Civil Court had jurisdiction to try suit filed by appellants - Appeal allowed.
