The State of Madhya Pradesh and Others v Gopal D. Tirthani and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 559; (2003) 7 SCC 83; AIR 2003 SC 2952; 2003 (4) ESC 569; JT 2003 (6) SC 204; 2003(5) SCALE 492; 2004 (1) SLJ 348; 2003 (5) SLR 119; 2003 (4) SLT 704; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 797; 2003 (5) Supreme 473; 2003 (3) UPLBEC 2115

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Health & Drug; Service

Keywords: Eligibility - Age Limit

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Education - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14 - Madhya Pradesh Chikitsa Shiksha Sanstha Niyantran Adhiniyam, 1973, s.10 - Madhya Pradesh Medical and Dental Post Graduation Admission (In-service) Rules, 2002, rr. 2, 6, 8 - MCI Regulations, regns. 9(2)(i), 9(2)(iii), 9(2)(iv) - Quota of seats for in-service candidates in medical education - Whether it is permissible to hold and conduct separate entrance examinations for in-service and open category candidates, or should there be only one common entrance test? - Whether it is permissible to compartmentalise evaluation of performance of in-service candidates? - Whether weightage can be given for length of service in rural/tribal areas, and if so, in what manner? - Whether women candidates who have served for three consecutive years in rural areas constitute a class by themselves so as to deserve special treatment - Held, there can be only one common entrance test for determining eligibility for post graduation requirement of minimum qualifying marks cannot be lowered or relaxed contrary to Medical Counsel of India Regulations framed in this behalf - In State of Madhya Pradesh allocation of 20% seats in post graduation in Universities of Madhya Pradesh for in-service candidates is not a reservation - It is separate and exclusive channel of entry or source of admission, validity whereof cannot be determined on constitutional principles applicable to communal reservations - Such two channels of entry or two sources of admission is a valid provision - Assigning of weightage for service rendered in rural/tribal area does not at all affect in any manner candidates in open category weightage would have effect of altering order of merit only as amongst candidates entering through exclusive channel of admissions meant for in-service candidates - Purpose of providing relaxation in eligibility criterion in favour of female doctors in not unreasonable and has nexus with object sought to be achieved rule does not suffer from any constitutional anathema.
