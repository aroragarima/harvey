Â Deputy Commissioner and Collector, Kamrup and Others v Durga Nath Sarma

Supreme Court of India

15 September 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 211; AIR 1968 SC 394; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 561

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Water

Summary: Land & Property - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Land Acquisition (Madras Amendment) Act, 1961 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Assam Acquisition of Land for Flood Control and Prevention of Erosion Act, 1955 - Assam Acquisition of Land for Flood Control and Prevention of Erosion (Validation) Act, 1959 (Assam Act No. 21 of 1960) - Compulsory - Land Acquisition - Justifiability - Assam Govt. took possession of lands of respondent and several other persons for construction of Pagdalia embankment u/s. 3 of Ordinance No. 2 of 1955 Act - Collector served notice upon respondent informing him of acquisition order and asking him to submit his claim for compensation - Respondent filed petition before Assam HC for an order declaring Act No. 6 of 1955 and Act No. 21 of 1960 to be invalid and directing State Govt. to forbear from giving effect to notices issued there under - HC allowed petition and issued writ of Mandamus directing State Govt. not to give effect to notices issued under Act No. 21 of 1960 - Hence instant appeal - Whether Assam Act No. 6 of 1955 was violative of art. 14 and 31(2) of Constitution and if Assam Act No. 21 of 1960 was valid - Held, classification of land required for works and other measures in connection with flood control and prevention of erosion and land required for other public purposes had no reasonable relation to object sought to be achieved viz, acquisition of land by State - In either case, owner loses his land and in his place, State becomes owner - There was unjust discrimination between owners of land similarly situated by mere accident of some land being required for purposes mentioned in Assam Act No. 6 of 1955 and some land being required for other purposes - We hold that Assam Act No. 6 of 1955 was violative of art. 14 - Record showed that even after passing of Act No. 6 of 1955 State of Assam had acquired other lands for erecting embankments in connection with flood control and had paid full compensation to owners of those lands under 1894 Act - It was sufficient to say that Assam Act No. 6 of 1955 was violative of art. 14 and 31(2) of Constitution and must be struck down - Assam Act No. 21 provides that any land taken over for purposes of construction of embankments or carrying out works or other development measures in connection with flood control or prevention of erosion before it came into force should be deemed to have been validly acquired under provisions of No. 6 of 1955 Act, unless acquisition was validly made under any other law for time being in force - By force of Assam Act No. 21 of 1960 land so taken over was deemed to be acquired under Assam Act No. 6 of 1955 - As Assam Act No. 6 of 1955 was invalid, deemed acquisition under Assam Act No. 21 of 1960 was equally invalid - State legislature had no power to enact that an acquisition made under constitutionally invalid Act was valid - It was to be seen that core of Assam Act No. 21 of 1960 was deeming provision of s. 2 under which certain lands were deemed to be acquired under earlier Act - As this deeming provision was invalid, all other ancillary provisions fall to ground along with it - Later Act was entirely dependent upon continuing existence and validity of earlier Act - As earlier Act was unconstitutional and had no legal existence, provisions of Act No. 21 of 196O were incapable of enforcement and were invalid - HC rightly held that notices of acquisition issued under Assam Act No. 21 of 1960 were invalid - Appeal dismissed.
