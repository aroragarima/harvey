Himanshu Chandravadan Desai and Others v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

16 November 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 732; (2005) 13 SCC 234; (2006) 2 SCC (Cr) 143; AIR 2006 SC 179; 2006 (1) BC 380; 2006 CRLJ 126; 2006 CRLJ 136; JT 2005 (9) SC 635; 2005 (4) RLW 2932; 2005(9) SCALE 367

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - CrPC, 1973, s. 439 - IPC, 1860, ss. 114, 120-B, 406, 409, 420, 465, 466, 467, 468, 470, 471 - Appeal against rejection of bail - First appellant and other Directors of bank siphoned off funds of bank by bogus loans and fictitious letters of credit - Held, crime in which petitioners are involved is very serious involving a conspiracy to cheat and defraud public institutions in a systematic manner and punishment is likely to be severe in event of conviction - HC has recorded a finding that material shows that petitioners are prima facie involved in offence - Having regard to huge amounts involved in systematic fraud, there is a danger of appellants absconding, if released on bail, or attempting to tamper with evidence by pressurizing witnesses - Appeal dismissed.
