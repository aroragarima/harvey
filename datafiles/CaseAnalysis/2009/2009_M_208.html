Mani @ Udattu Man and Others v State, Inspector of Police

Supreme Court of India

25 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 214; (2009) 12 SCC 288; 2009 CRLJ 2268; JT 2009 (4) SC 169; 2009 (3) MLJ(Crl) 959; 2010 (4) RCR(Criminal) 56; 2009(3) SCALE 431; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 338; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 1210

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Duty Of Court

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, s. 302 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, it has to be appraised in each case as to what extent the evidence is worthy of acceptance, and merely because in some respects the Court considers the same to be insufficient for placing reliance on the testimony of a witness, it does not necessarily follow as a matter of law that it must be disregarded in all respect as well - When the evidence of the interested witnesses in corroborated by the medical evidence, then the entire evidence of the interested witnesses ought not be rejected on that ground - Appeals dismissed.
