Â Messrs Rajputana Textiles (Agencies) Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay City

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah, S.K. Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 265; AIR 1962 SC 1267; [1961] 42 ITR 743; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 917

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct taxes - Taxation on Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947 -Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940 - Transferred shares - Excess profit - Promoters of assessee company entered agreement with Sassoons - Sassoons agreed to transfer their Managing Agency in a Mill Co. for Rs. 12-1/2 lacs with their holding of 19, 76, 000 shares - Result of sale of 13, 74, 000 shares, assessee company received Rs.16, 52, 600, excess over purchase price - Remaining shares retained by assessee company and submitted that profits of entire holding of shares was not worked out and not transferred to profit and loss account - Assessee company was taxed by Income-tax Officer but sum of Rs. 16, 52, 600, excess of sale price over purchase price was held not to be profit and not taxable - Report of Investigation Commission directed that appropriate assessment be made under Excess Profits Tax Act for corresponding chargeable accounting period - Assessee company referred to HC - HC answered question formulated in affirmative and against assessee company - Assessee company's Notice of Motion was dismissed by HC - Assessee company appealed by special leave in advisory jurisdiction - Constitutional question to discrimination u/art. 14 of Constitution sought to be raised - Whether nature of transaction of 13 lacs odd shares was in nature of trade and Rs. 16, 52, 600 excess of sale price over purchase price of share was taxable profits or not liable to tax - Held, question framed must answered in affirmative - Notice of Motion to raise other questions in HC was rightly dismissed - Apart from Notice of Motion was barred by time and there was no application for condonation of delay, questions which were sought to be raised were rightly detained either to be covered by question answered or they did not arise at all - Constitutional question u/art. 14 of Constitution cannot be raised because Court exercising its advisory jurisdiction and its power was confined to questions arise in appeal - Appeal dismissed.
