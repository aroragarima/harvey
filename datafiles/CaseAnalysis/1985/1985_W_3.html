Workmen Employed In Associated Rubber Industry Limited, Bhavnagar v Associated Rubber Industry Limited, Bhavnagar and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 107; AIR 1986 SC 12; [1986] 59 Comp Cas 134; 1986 (1) LLJ 142

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Extra bonus - Entitled to - Whether respondents created a subsidiary to avoid payment of extra bonus? - Respondents purchased shares of INARCO Ltd., dividend recieved from which was to be distributed as bonus - Respondents transferred said shares to its subsidiary Aril Holdings Ltd. leading to reduction in amount of bonus - There cannot be direct evidence that second company was formed as a device to reduce gross profits of principal company for whatever purpose - A possibility is to reduce amount to be paid by way of bonus - It is so obvious device that nor further evidence, direct or circumstantial, is necessary but Aril Holdings Ltd. was wound up and amalgamated with Associated Rubber Industry Ltd. and that this circumstance showed it was not a device of avoidance - But learned counsel for company was unable to explain why Aril Holdings Ltd. was created and why latter it was wound up - Appeal allowed.
