Harbans Lal v Jagmohan Saran

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 281; (1985) 4 SCC 333; AIR 1986 SC 302; 1986 ALJ 84; 1985 (11) ALR 769; 1986 (1) CCC 231; 1985 (2) RCJ 555; 1986 (1) RCR(Rent) 82; 1985(2) SCALE 891; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 634; 1986 (1) SLR 36; [1985] supp3 S.C.R. 634; 1986 (1) UJ 28

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Tenancy - Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, s. 12 - Deemed vacancy - Eviction of tenant on ground of subletting - Whether shop can be deemed to be vacant within meaning of s. 12 (l)(b) of Act? - U/s.12(1)(b) a tenant of a building is deemed to have ceased to occupy building if he has allowed it to be occupied by any person who is not a member of his family - Occupation of a person envisaged here cannot possibly include occupation by any person as agent of tenant - If contrary construction is accepted, and it is held that a person who is a mere agent or servant of original occupant falls within contemplation of s. 12(1)(b), it would be impossible for original occupant to engage any person to assist him in discharge of his responsibilities in place where he does so - It cannot be conceived that U.P. Legislature intended a person, occupying a building as a ten ant, to live or operate in such a building with members of his family and no one else - In present case, 'M' sat in shop conducting vegetable business on behalf of appellant - When he did so, it must be considered as an occupation by appellant - Held, when a person sits in premises and carries on a business on behalf of and for original occupant, it cannot be said that original occupant has thereby allowed accommodation to be occupied within meaning of s. 12 (1)(b) - HC is wrong in holding that case attracts provisions of s. 12(1)(b) of Act - Appeal allowed.
