Â Punjab Rao v D. P. Meshram and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 334; AIR 1965 SC 1179; 1965 (67) BomLR 812; 1965 MahLJ 162; 1965 MPLJ 257; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 849

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Socio-Economic - Elections - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 25(2)(b), Explanation II, art. 332 - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 100(1)(a) - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - (A)Who can be regarded as a Schedule Caste under (Scheduled Castes) Order, 1950, Cl. 3 of Indian Constitution?- Held, under (Scheduled Castes) Order,1950, Cl. 3 of Indian constitution - A person to be regarded, as Schedule Caste must profess either Sikhism or Hinduism as defined u/art. 25(2) (b) of Constitution - Whenever a person makes a declaration that he has ceased his old religion and accepted another religion, he shall be regarded to have been professing other religion irrespective of efficaciousness of conversion - (B) Whether signatures of converted persons is mandatory u/s. 35 of evidence act to prove his conversion? -Further held, u/s. 35, Evidence Act - Absence of signature of converted persons from Schedule Castes (Hinduism) to Buddhism does not deny his conversion - Appeal allowed.
