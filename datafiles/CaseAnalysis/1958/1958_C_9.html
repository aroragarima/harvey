Commissioner Of Income-Tax, Delhi v S.Teja Singh

Supreme Court of India

05 November 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 54; AIR 1959 SC 352; [1959] 35 ITR 408; 1959 (1) MLJ 154; 1959 (1) MLJ(SC) 154; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 394; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 394

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 18, 18A(3), 18A(9), 28 and 66(1) - (A) Whether a penalty u/s 28 can be levied by Income Tax authorities on failure to submit estimate of tax u/s. 18 A(3) - Held, objective of s. 18 A(9)(b) is to assimilate position of a person who has failed to send estimate u/s. 18A(3) to that of a person who has failed to furnish return under s. 22 - This object is sought to be achieved by enacting legal fiction which is contained in s. 18 A(9)(b) - A penalty u/s. 28 r/w s. 18 A (9) (b) may be imposed where there has been a failure to comply with s. 18A(3) - (B) How to interpret a legal fiction? - Further held, a legal fiction can be construed by assuming all those facts on which alone fiction can operate - A construction defeating object of legislature should be avoided - Appeal allowed.
