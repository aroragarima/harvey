Union Of India & Ors. v Lieut (Mrs.) E.Iacats

Supreme Court of India

06 August 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1500; (1997) 7 SCC 334; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1613; 1997 (77) FLR 277; JT 1997 (7) SC 279; 1997 (2) LLJ 830; 1997(5) SCALE 395; 1998 (1) SLJ 82; 1997 (7) Supreme 172; 1997 (2) UJ 413

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Disability Pension, Eligibility - Cut Off Date

Summary: Service - Army Instruction No. 14 issued on 12-03-1977 - Parity in service - Entitled to - (A) Whether the post of a Nursing Sister (Lieutenant) in the Military Nursing Service for local service entitled to claim parity in respect of superannuation age with other Military nursing service? - The terms and conditions attaching to the other two military nursing services are different from the terms and conditions attaching to Military Nursing Service (Local) - One major difference lies in the fact that they are not liable to transfer and that married women or widows with children can avail of this service without any problem - Held, all appointees in the service in question hold the rank of Lieutenant with no eligibility for further promotion - Therefore, they must retire at the same age as a Lieutenant, which is at 55 - (B) Whether the post of a Nursing Sister (Lieutenant) entitled the benefit of pension? - No - Pensioners under the old rules and pensioners under the new rules are not similarly situated - Each set of retiring employees will be governed by their own rules in force when they retire - Held, terms and conditions of service were known to the respondent at the time when she joined the service - At the time of joining service, respondent has signed an agreement to abide by the Rules and Regulations governing Military Nursing Service (Local) from time to time - Therefore, respondent not entitled pensionary benefit as per the agreement - Appeal allowed.
