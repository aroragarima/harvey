Comptroller and Auditor General v Kamlesh Vadilal Mehta

Supreme Court of India

21 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, Ashok Bhan, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 33; (2003) 2 SCC 349; AIR 2003 SC 1096; 2003 (2) AWC 1291; [2003] 113 Comp Cas 556; 2003 (2) G.L.R. 921; JT 2003 (1) SC 330; 2003(1) SCALE 351; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 471; 2003 (1) SLT 494; 2003 (1) Supreme 494

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Corporate

Keywords: Accounts And Audit

Summary: Constitution of India - Art. 14 - respondent's application for enrolment on the panel for audit of government companies rejected on account of the fact that his firm was not a partnership firm, but a proprietary concern - whether the sub-classification of partnership firms stands the test of reasonableness on the touchstone of Article 14? - Held, a Chartered Accountant cannot be discriminated against merely because he elected to invest his professional expertise in a proprietary concern, rather than to express it in the form of a partnership firm - Personal experience and integrity are the essential attributes of every successful professional and they are the virtues that win the day for the client, whether it is a corporation seeking an audit of its accounts or an individual seeking a remedy in court. - Therefore, the classification between proprietary and partnership firms is arbitrary and unfair, and accordingly falls on the anvil of Article 14 of the Constitution
