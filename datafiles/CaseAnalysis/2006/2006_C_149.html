Indian Refrigeration Industries v Commissioner of Central Excise, Delhi

Supreme Court of India

06 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1451; (2009) 16 SCC 405; 2010 (255) E.L.T. 491

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Practice & Procedure - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 10(1) - A/y 1978-79 - Limitation - Exemption - Notification No. 71/78 dt. 1-3-1978 - Appellant manufactures refrigerating and air conditioning machinery, appliances and parts thereof falling under Tariff item 29 A of the then Central Excise Tariff and filed classification list for the same - In classification list, it claimed exemption under Notification No. 71/78 dt. 1-3-1978 - Asst. Collector approved the classification list but denied the benefit of exemption under notification no. 71/78 - Appellant filed appeal against said order which was allowed and the matter was remanded back to the Asst. Collector by the Collector (Appeals) for de novo consideration - Asst. Collector again confirmed the demand - Appeal against said order was rejected by Collector - Tribunal also dismissed appeal filed against said order - Revenue issued show cause notice dt. 4-03-1980 demanding duty of Rs. 5,91,400/- - Appellant contended that said demand was barred by time as show cause notice issued much beyond the period of six months as provided under clause (1) of r. 10 of the Rules - Whether demand barred under limitation? - Held, show cause notice was issued on 4-03-1980 i.e. much beyond the period of six months as provided under clause (1) of r. 10 of the Rules - Extended period of limitation had not been invoked - Demand raised against the appellant is hereby dropped - Appeals allowed.
