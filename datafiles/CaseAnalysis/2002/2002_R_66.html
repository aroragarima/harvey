Jaipur Zila Sahakari Bhoomi Vikas Bank Limited v Ram Gopal Sharma and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, N. Santosh Hegde, S.N. Variava, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 734; (2002) 2 SCC 244; (2002) SCC (L&S) 279; AIR 2002 SC 643; 2002 (2) ALD(SC) 35; 2002 (18) CLR 789; 2002 (92) FLR 667; JT 2002 (1) SC 182; 2002 (35) LabIC 513; 2002 (1) LLJ 834; 2002 (1) LLN 639; 2002(1) SCALE 190; 2002 (1) SCJ 338; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 284; 2002 (2) SLJ 410; 2002 (1) SLR 775; 2002 (1) SLT 298; 2002 (1) Supreme 181; 2002 (1) UPLBEC 519

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial dispute Act, 1947, s. 33 (2)(b) - (A) Dismissal Proceedings pending before the Tribunal - Application u/s. 33(2)(b) filed by the appellant before the Tribunal for approval of dismissal - Respondent contended that salary for one month was not paid at the time of dismissal - Tribunal held, appellant failed to comply s. 33(2)(b) - High Court dismissed the writ of appellant - No merit - (B) Non-granting of approval u/s. 33(2)(b) - Operation of the order of dismissal - Relied on Tata Iron and Steel Co. case (1965 Indlaw SC 117) - Held, employer should pay or offer one month salary before the dismissal and at the same time apply for approval of the action before the authority - Both the actions forming the same transaction - If approval is granted, it takes effect from the date of the order passed by the employer for which approval was sought - If approval is not granted, the order of dismissal or discharge passed by the employer is wholly invalid or inoperative - Then, employee can continue the employment irrespective of dismissal or discharge - If the approval has not been accorded, the dismissal or discharge is incomplete and inchoate - (C) Distinction between s. 33 and amended s. 33(1) in 1956 - Held, object of unamended s. 33 is to maintain the peaceful atmosphere in industrial dispute - Unamended s. 33 is too stringent to make any alteration in conditions of service or passing of order of dismissal/discharge - Amended s. 33, on the other hand, permits the employer to make changes in conditions of service or to dismiss or discharge an employee in connection with the pending dispute - At the same time safe guarding the workmen who cannot be discharged or dismissed during the pendency of a dispute in unconnected matter - (D) Discharge or dismissal during the pendancy of industrial dispute - Held, proviso to s. 33 says no work man shall be discharged or dismissed unless he has been paid wages for one month and an application has been made by the employer to the authority where the proceedings being held - (E) Dismissal or discharge without making application u/s. 33(2)(b) - Effect of - Held, not making application u/s. 33 (2) (b) for approval or withdrawing an application once made before any order is made thereon, is a clear case of contravention of proviso to s. 33 (2)(b) - Employer cannot relieve his statutory obligation - (F) S. 31 - Nature of - Contention of s. 31 is a remedy of contravention of s. 33 is not correct - Availability of penal provision or remedy u/s. 33 to challenge the approval granted, cannot render the order of dismissal or discharge passed by the employer be inoperative or invalid unless such granted approval set aside - Appeal dismissed.
