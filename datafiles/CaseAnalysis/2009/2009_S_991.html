Gurmej Singh v State of Punjab and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1880; (2009) 12 SCC 440; AIR 2009 SC 2699; JT 2009 (13) SC 684; 2009 (2) MLJ(Crl) 1132; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 104; 2009(7) SCALE 506; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 395, 450, 342 - Principles of natural justice - Appellant was named in the FIR of a dacoity case but not identified by the complainant - Trial Court convicted 1st accused in the case - 1st accused filed appeal before HC - HC directed respondent to implicate and prosecute the appellant while disposing the appeal of 1st accused - Appellant contended that HC had given observations and directions without issuance of notice to him - Held, principles of natural justice violated - Therefore, observations and directions given by the HC qua the present appellant cannot be maintained, hence quashed - Appeal allowed.
