Mahboob Sahab v Syed Ismail and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1624; (1995) 3 SCC 693; AIR 1995 SC 1205; 1995 (2) CCC 268; 1995 (1) HLR 440; JT 1995 (3) SC 168; 1996 (6) KarLJ 194; 1995(2) SCALE 395; 1995 (2) SCJ 137; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 975

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Minority, Adoption & Guardianship, Debt Laws

Summary: Civil - Practice & Procedure - Res judicata - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - S. 11 - Whether the High Court was right in reversing the appellate decree on the doctrine of res judicata - Held, no - The oral gift or sale of land was not the subject-matter of the previous suit between parties - HC wrongly held that claim was barred principle of res judicata - For application of Res-judicata four conditions must be satisfied, namely (1) There must be a conflict of interest between the defendants concerned; (2) It must be necessary to decide the conflict in order to give the relief's which the plaintiff claims (3) The question between the defendants must have been finally decided; and (4) The co-defendants were necessary or proper parties in the former suit - Appeal allowed.
