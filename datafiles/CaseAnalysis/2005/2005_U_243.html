Amarendra Komalam and Another v Usha Sinha and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 260; (2005) 11 SCC 251; AIR 2005 SC 2758; 2005 (3) ALD(SC) 63; 2005 (60) ALR 760; 2005 (2) AWC 1504; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 163; JT 2005 (4) SC 98; 2005 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 550; 2005 (3) MLJ(SC) 101; 2005 (3) RCR(Civil) 276; 2005(3) SCALE 765; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 326

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Agreement For Lease

Summary: [A.] Renewal of lease deed - Allegation that there was interpolation in cl relating to renewal of lease deed in agreement between parties - Whether an issue, already settled in a suit between same parties in respect of certain subject matter, can be allowed to be raised again between very same parties in regard to same subject matter, but in a different suit? - Held, as in eviction suit, plaintiff is claiming eviction by termination of lease and denying renewal cl whereas in specific performance suit, appellants are claiming renewal of lease on basis of said renewal cl - In both suits, main issue is substantially and materially one and same and both cases are being tried simultaneously - Respondent is precluded from raising genuineness/interpolation or forgery of agreement dated 02/09/1978 - Appeal allowed. [B.] Practice & Procedure - Bihar Civil Court Staff (Class-II and Class-IV) Rules, 1992, r.7 - Notices should be placed on the notice board of the respective Civil Court premises and in the local daily newspaper of the District - Directive was binding on the District Judge - Instructions of the High Court were disregarded with impunity and a procedure evolved for appointment which cannot be said to be in any way fair or above board - Appeals dismissed.
