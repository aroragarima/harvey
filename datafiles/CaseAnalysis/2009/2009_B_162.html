Bishnupriya Palo v Bidyadhar Palo @ Pal

Supreme Court of India

11 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2250

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal

Summary: Criminal - Family & Personal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 125, 482 - Maintenance - Sustainability - Appellant filed petition for maintenance u/s. 125 of CrPC - Trial Court allowed the petition and awarded maintenance of Rs.500/- per month - Respondent-husband filed a revision challenging the award of maintenance which was rejected - Respondent-husband challenged the order of the Sessions Court in a petition u/s. 482 CrPC - HC allowed the petition - Hence instant appeal.

Trial Court had recorded the clear finding after appreciating the evidence that the husband was not interested to take back his wife, and that only to avoid maintenance, he had initiated a proceeding for restitution of conjugal rights and secured an ex parte decree. The said finding has been affirmed by the Appellate Court. In the circumstances, HC, in exercise of jurisdiction u/s. 482 CrPC ought not to have interfered with the said finding. Impugned order set aside the order of the HC and restore the judgment of the Trial Court affirmed by the Revision Court. Appeal allowed.
