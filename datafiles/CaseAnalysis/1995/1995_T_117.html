Anirudhsinhji Karansinhjijadeja And Anr v The State Of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

11 August 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, A.M. Ahmadi, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1354; (1995) 5 SCC 302; (1995) SCC (Cr) 902; AIR 1995 SC 2390; 1995 (3) AWC 1793; 1995 (4) CCR 15; 1995 (3) Crimes 518; 1995 CRLJ 4154; JT 1995 (9) SC 603; 1995(4) SCALE 715; [1995] Supp2 S.C.R. 637; 1995 (2) UJ 610

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, s. 207 - Approval by DSP for recording of information about offence under TADA, 1987 - DSP seeking permission of Addl. Chief Secy., instead of giving approval i.e. on basis of external dictation - And State Govt. also santioned without discussing it with investigating officer - Legality - Held, there is lack of due application of mind - Hence, proceedings quashed - Appeal allowed.
