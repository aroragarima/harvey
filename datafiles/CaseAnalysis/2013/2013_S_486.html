Arun Bhandari v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 15; (2013) 2 SCC 801; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 582; 2013 CRLJ 1020; JT 2013 (1) SC 467; 2013 (1) MLJ(Crl) 475; 2013 (1) OLR 877; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 261; 2013 (2) RLW 950; 2013(1) SCALE 229; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 961

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 406,420 - Agreement to sell - Execution of Sale Deed - Cheating - Cognizance - Civil in Nature - Protest Petition - Quashing - Sustainability - Appellant-Complainant a Non-Resident Indian [NRI] was looking for a property and came in contact with respondent No. 2 and her husband who claimed to be owner of property and offered to sell and executed Agreement and jointly received part payment - It was further agreed that respondent No. 2 and 3 would obtain permission from authority to transfer of property and execute deed of transfer - Appellant enquired about progress of delivery of possession by received conflicting and contradictory replies and was compelled to rush to find our real facts - On enquiry came to know that respondent No. 2 had transferred property to third party after obtaining rights from original allottee - Appellant lodged FIR and IO filed final report stating that case was civil in nature and no criminal offence had been made out - Appellant filed protest petition and concerned area office who handed over investigation to another SSI who recorded statements of Sub-registrar and CEO of authority which showed that accused persons were never owners of property - CJM after verifying file and on basis of investigation and statements recorded took cognizance u/ss. 420,406 of IPC and issued summons to respondents - Respondents preferred revision before Sessions Judge contending that FIR had been lodged with an ulterior motive to pressurize respondent to return earnest money - Revision was dismissed, aggrieved respondent approached HC, and filed writ petition - HC, partly allowed and quashed order taking cognizance and summoning of respondent No.2 -wife - Aggrieved instant appeal - Appellant contended that HC could not have scrutinized material brought on record as if it was sitting in appeal against judgment of conviction and also committed error in ignoring certain material facts which made order sensitively susceptible - Respondent contended that mere presence of respondent No. 2 at time of signing of agreement to sell does not amount to an offence u/s. 420 of IPC as she did not sign document nor did she endorse same as a witness - Whether HC, was justified in quashing order of summoning respondents passed by CJM - Held, SC, had no hesitation in coming to hold that neither FIR nor protest petition was mala fide, frivolous or vexatious, it was also not a case where there was no substance in complaint, manner in which investigation was conducted by officer who eventually filed final report and transfer of investigation earlier to another officer who had almost completed investigation and entire case diary which had been adverted to in detail in protest petition prima facie made out a case against husband and wife regarding collusion and intention to cheat from very beginning, inducing him to hand over a huge sum of money to both of them - Their conduct of not stating so many aspects, namely, Power of Attorney executed by original owner, Will and also sale effected by wife could not be brushed aside at this stage - Therefore, SC, held that HC, while exercising extraordinary jurisdiction, had not proceeded on sound principles of law for quashment of order taking cognizance - Appeal allowed.
