Associate Builders v Delhi Development Authority

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 801; (2015) 3 SCC 49; AIR 2015 SC 620; 2014 (4) CLT(SC) 339; 2015 (1) CTC 191; 2014 (215) DLT 204; JT 2014 (13) SC 204; 2015 (4) MahLJ 576; 2015 (3) MPLJ 368

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Foreign Awards (Recognition And Enforcement) Act, 1961, Prolongation of contract

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 18, 34, 34(2)(a), 37 - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Prolongation of contract - Arbitral award - Damages - Legality - Appellant (Builders) was awarded a certain construction work contract by respondent (Development Authority) and contract was to be completed in 9 months - As many as 15 claims were made by appellant and the HC appointed sole arbitrator to arbitrate the dispute - Arbitrator allowed the claims 9, 10, 11 and 15 and out of the total claim of Rs.37.28 lakhs, awarded Rs.23.39 lakhs and the objections of Authority were dismissed by Single Judge - The DB in an appeal u/s. 37 of 1996 Act has stepped in to set aside the judgment of Single Judge and negative those claims - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that DB acted as if it was a First Appeal from the award and DB had taken into account facts which were neither pleaded nor proved before the Arbitrator in order to negative certain claims and that entire delay of 25 months in execution of project was due to Authority alone.

Held, the DB while considering claims 9, 10, 11 and 15 found fault with the application of Hudson's formula which was set out by Arbitrator in order to arrive at the claim made under these heads. The DB held that it was not possible for an Arbitrator to mechanically apply a certain formula however well understood in the trade. This itself is going outside the jurisdiction to set aside an award u/s. 34 of 1996 Act. The DB has exceeded its jurisdiction in interfering with a possible view of the Arbitrator on facts. Completion certificate given by Authority to appellant, shows not only that the DB was wholly incorrect in its conclusion that the contractor has tried to pull the wool over the eyes over the Authority but it should also have realized that the Authority itself has stated that the work has been carried out generally to its satisfaction barring some extremely minor defects which are capable of rectification. It is clear, therefore, that the DB obviously exceeded its jurisdiction in interfering with a pure finding of fact forgetting that the Arbitrator is the sole Judge of the quantity and quality of evidence before him and unnecessarily bringing in facts which were neither pleaded nor proved and ignoring the vital completion certificate granted by the Authority itself. The DB also went wrong in stating that as the work completed was only to the extent of Rs.62,84,845/-, Hudson's formula should have been applied taking this figure into account and not the entire contract value into account. To apply Hudson's formula one has to take into account the contract value that is awarded and not the work completed. On this score again, the DB is to be faulted. DB has interfered wrongly with the arbitral award on several counts. It had no business to enter into a pure question of fact to set aside the Arbitrator for having applied a formula of 20 months instead of 25 months. Though this would inure in favour of the appellant, it is clear that the appellant did not file any cross objection on this score. Also, it is extremely curious that the DB found that an adjustment would have to be made with claims awarded under claims 2, 3 and 4 which are entirely separate and independent claims and have nothing to do with claims 12 and 13. The formula applied by DB was that it would itself do 'rough and ready justice'. Court is at a complete loss to understand how this can be done by any Court under the jurisdiction exercised u/s. 34 of 1996 Act. The whole approach of DB to setting aside arbitral awards is incorrect. The DB has lost sight of the fact that it is not a First Appellate Court and cannot interfere with errors of fact. Single Judge is clearly right. None of 15 claims are in fact overlapping. They are all contained under separate heads. Impugned judgment of DB is set aside. Appeal allowed.
