Dr. Ramesh Yeshwant Prabhoo v Shri Prabhakar Kashinath Kunte And Others

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, N.P. Singh, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1863; (1996) 1 SCC 130; AIR 1996 SC 1113; 1996 (1) CLT 258; JT 1995 (8) SC 609; 1995(7) SCALE 1; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 371

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Electorate

Summary: Representation of the People Act, 1951, s.123(3) - Corrupt practice - Election speech - Asking vote on Hindutva - Held, not necessarily be prejudicial to public order - Mention of religion in election speech is not prohibited, if secular stance - Use of expressions 'Hindutva' or Hinduism - Not by itself amounts to corrupt practice - It means way of life of people in sub-continent.
