State of Punjab v Harnam Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2276; (1997) 3 SCC 32; (1997) SCC (L&S) 623; AIR 1997 SC 1103; 1997 (1) CLR 606; 1997 (75) FLR 735; JT 1997 (2) SC 190; 1997 LabIC 1450; 1997(1) SCALE 619; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 377; 1998 (1) SLJ 224; 1997 (2) SLR 399

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16 - Schools taken over by Govt. from District Boards and Zilla Parishads - As per deed Govt. had a discretion to treat existing employees from those schools as fresh entrants into Govt. service vis-a-vis existing Govt. employees - (A) Whether parties are governed by conditions specified in deed of take-over? - (B) Whether previous service rendered by erstwhile teachers in District Boards and Zilla Parishads would not be counted for purposes of seniority and other benefits? - Held, (A) parties are governed by conditions of deed - (B) previous service rendered by teachers would not be counted for purposes of seniority and other benefits - Appeal allowed.
