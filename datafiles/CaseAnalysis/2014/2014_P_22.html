Parminder alias Ladka Pola v State of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

16 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 24; (2014) 2 SCC 592; AIR 2014 SC 1035; 2014 (2) JCC 965; JT 2014 (2) SC 305; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 682; 2014(1) SCALE 368; [2014] 1 S.C.R. 451

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 342, 354, 375, 376, 376(1), 376(2), 376(2)(g), 506, 511 - Rape - Conviction - Sustainability - Accused/appellant committed offence punishable u/ss. 342, 354, 375, 376, 376(1), 376(2), 376(2)(g), 506, 511 of IPC - Trial Court passed conviction order - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before HC and the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was sustainable - Held, it was clear that what was adequate and special would depend upon several factors and on facts of each case and no straitjacket formula was laid down by Court - Legislature, however, required the Court to record adequate and special reasons in any given case where punishment less than minimum sentence of seven years was to be imposed - Conduct of appellant at time of commission of offence of rape, age of victim/prosecutrix and consequences of rape on prosecutrix were some of relevant factors which Court should consider while considering question of reducing sentence to less than minimum sentence - Court found that prosecutrix was student of eighth class and was about 14 years on and she was of tender age - She had gone to house of appellant looking for her friend who was sister of appellant - When she asked appellant as to where sister of accused was, he told her that she was in the room and when she went inside room, he followed her into room and raped her - Considering age of prosecutrix, conduct of appellant and consequences of rape on prosecutrix, there were adequate and special reasons to reduce sentence to less than minimum sentence u/s. 376(1) of IPC - Appeal dismissed.
