Kum. Madhuri Patil & Anr. v Add1. Commnr., Tribal Development, Thane & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1164; (1997) 5 SCC 437; AIR 1997 SC 2581; AIR 1997 SCW 2532; 1998 (1) Bom.C.R. 479; 1997 (2) CLT 522; JT 1997 (5) SC 643; 1997(4) SCALE 258; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 1056; 1997 (4) SLR 585; 1997 (5) Supreme 201

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Research

Summary: Administrative - Grant of caste certificates - Recall of application - Application had been filed to recall judgment giving various directions and to substitute in their place directions sought for in prayer clause of application - Applicant prayed to recall order with regard to grant of caste certificates - Hence instant application.

Held, it is not advisable to recall the order for reason that Court had deliberated upon issue and entrusted power to issue certificates of social status to higher officer, namely, sub-Divisional officer, who is expected to objectively go into the question and grant or refuse to grant social status certificates. Prayer regarding grant of caste certificates is rejected. Instead of one committee of three officers, there will be three scheduled Tribe Caste scrutiny committees comprising of five members with quorum of three members, six caste scrutiny committees for SCs, Denotify Tribes, Nomadic Tribes, other Backward Classes and the special Backward category in existence would continue to scrutinise the certificates issued by the respective officers and take decision in that behalf. In case any certificate has been wrongfully refused by certificate issuing authority, committees also would go into question and decide in that behalf, finds that the refusal was wrongful, they are at liberty to direct authority to grant the certificate. Along with vigilance cell, Research Officer/Tribal Development or social Welfare officer would be associated in finding social status of eligibility of officers. Order accordingly.
