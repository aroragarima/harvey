U.P. State Road Transport Corporation v Krishna Bala and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 325; (2006) 6 SCC 249; 2006 (3) ACC 361; 2006 (5) ADJ 709; AIR 2006 SC 2688; 2006 (64) ALR 771; 2006 (4) AWC 3170; 2006 (4) JhrCR(SC) 42; JT 2006 (6) SC 327; 2006 (4) MLJ 1499; 2006 (3) RCR(Civil) 488; 2006(7) SCALE 92; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 506

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Computation Of Compensation, Assessment Of Damage

Summary: [A.] Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Compensation granted - Deceased was around 36 years and was earning Rs. 2300/- pm - Tribunal adopted multiplier of 22 on ground that deceased had 22 years of service left - Interest at rate of 12% from date of application was granted - Appeal against - Held, choice of multiplier is determined by age of deceased (or that of claimants whichever is higher) and by calculation as to what capital sum, if invested at a rate of interest appropriate to a stable economy, would yield multiplicand by way of annual interest - Considering age of deceased multiplier would be 13 - Rate of interest would be 9% from date of filing of claim petition - Appeal partly allowed.
