Mineral and Metal Trading Corporation v R. C. Mishra and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, N. Venkatachala, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1348; (1993) Supp3 SCC 29; AIR 1994 SC 1523; 1993 (115) CTR 547; 1997 (68) ECR 540; 1993 (65) E.L.T. 474; [1993] 201 ITR 851; JT 1993 (4) SC 222; 1993(2) SCALE 643; [1993] 3 S.C.R. 12; [1993] 69 TAXMAN 527; 1994 (2) TLR 326

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Customs; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Tax Credit Certificate (Exports) Scheme, 1965 - Income-Tax Act, 1961, s. 280(Z)(C) - Availability of tax credit certificate to the exporter - Contention of Indian supplier is that M.M.T.C. cannot be held as exporter for grant of Tax Credit Certificate to the exporter as it was only an agent, who received commission Held that the plea raised by supplier cannot be because on application of test of predominance to total relevant factors such as the substitution and superseding of initial agreement by two contracts on principal to principal basis - Letter of Credit signed by M.M.T.C and were in the name of M.M.T.C. - M.M.T.C. and not supplier was exporter and hence M.M.T.C., would be entitled to get Tax Credit Certificate- Appeal allowed.
