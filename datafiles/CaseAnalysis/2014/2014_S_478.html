Amar Singh Yadav v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 400; (2014) 13 SCC 443; AIR 2014 SC 2486; JT 2014 (7) SC 552; 2014 (3) MLJ(Crl) 614; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 604; 2014 (4) RLW 3640; 2014(8) SCALE 113; [2014] 6 S.C.R. 1044

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 307 and 436 IPC - Murder - Conviction - Award of death sentence - Sustainability - Appellant allegedly committed offence of murder and mischief by fire or explosive substance - Appellant was convicted by Trial Court u/ss. 302, 307 and 436 IPC - Hence instant appeal - Whether award of death sentence in the case was warranted -

Held, though SC was convinced that the prosecution had proved the guilt of the accused beyond all reasonable doubt, the accused committed the crime in a most cruel and inhuman manner - Helpless wife and young children, who fell victims to the avaricious conduct and lust of the appellant still the case did not fall within the four corners of the principle of 'the rarest of the rare case', though no leniency could be shown to the appellant - There was no reason to believe that the accused could not be reformed or rehabilitated and that he was likely to continue criminal acts of violence as would constitute a continuing threat to the society - In one case before SC, even while setting aside the sentence of death penalty and awarding life imprisonment in order to serve the ends of justice, the Court ordered that the appellant should not be released from the prison till the end of his life - Swamy Shraddananda vs. State of Karnataka, 2008 Indlaw SC 1128, relied on - Likewise in another case SC while setting aside the death sentence, directed that the appellant therein should serve a minimum period of 20 years including the remissions and would not be released on completion of 14 years of imprisonment - Ramraj v. State of Chhattisgarh, 2009 Indlaw SC 2087, relied on - SC in another case taking into note the decisions and facts and circumstances of the case, SC while holding that the imposition of death sentence to the accused was not warranted and while awarding life imprisonment, the Court held that the accused should serve a minimum of 30 years in jail without remissions before consideration of his case for premature release - (1) Sandeep (2) Shashi Bhushan v State Of U.P. 2012 Indlaw SC 505, relied on - In the instant case taking into the facts and circumstances of the case in hand, SC held that the imposition of death sentence to the accused was not warranted - Accordingly SC commuted the sentence to life imprisonment - Further, SC held that the accused should serve a minimum of 30 years in jail without remissions before consideration of his case for premature release - Appeals disposed of.
