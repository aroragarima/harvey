Nanak Chand v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

25 January 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Syed Jaffer Imam, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 66; AIR 1955 SC 274; 1955 CRLJ 721; 1955 (1) MLJ(SC) 141; 1955 SCJ 241; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 1201

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Curable, Distinct Offence

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 149 and 302 - Issue : whether the appellant could legally be convicted for murder and sentenced under s. 302, IPC, 1860 when he was not charged with that offence - A charge for a substantive offence under s. 302, or s. 325 of the IPC, 1860 etc. is for a distinct and separate offence from that under s. 302, read with s. 149 or s. 325, r/w. s. 149, etc - Difficult to hold in the circumstances of the present case that the appellant was not prejudiced by the non-framing of a charge under s. 302, IPC, 1860 - Having regard to the view expressed on the question of law, it is unnecessary to refer to the arguments on the facts - Held : appeal allowed and the conviction and the sentence of the appellant set aside and the case remanded to the Court of Sessions for retrial after framing a charge under s. 302 of the IPC, 1860.
