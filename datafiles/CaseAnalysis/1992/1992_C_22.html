S. A. Khan v Ch. Bhajanlal and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1330; (1993) 3 SCC 151; AIR 1993 SC 1348; 1993 CRLJ 1042; JT 1992 (Supp) SC 449; 1993 (1) SLR 392; [1992] Supp3 S.C.R. 735

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Intention And Attempt

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 - Contempt petition filed by DIG of police requesting court to initiate suo motu proceeding for criminal contempt allegedly committed by respondent/ contemner (Chief Minister) - Alleged that contemptuous statement made by Chief Minister as it appears in press note - Held, in present case, no evidence has been let in proof of statement of facts contained in newspaper report - Absence of any denial by respondent will not absolve applicant from discharging his obligation of proving statement of facts as appeared in press report - In fact, respondent in his counter-affidavit has taken a stand that statements attributed to him based on newspaper report are mere hearsay and cannot in law be relied upon for purpose of initiating such proceedings - Therefore, in absence of required legal proof, court will not be justified in issuing a suo motu notice for contempt of court - A perusal of news item does not spell out any reference to case of corruption or its proceeding pending before Supreme Court - Contempt petition no. 7 of 1989 dismissed - Order accordingly.
