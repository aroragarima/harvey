Y. Venkaiah v State Of A.P.

Supreme Court of India

03 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, S.B. Sinha, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 272; (2009) 12 SCC 126; AIR 2009 SC 2311; 2009 CRLJ 2834; 2009 (3) JCC 2091; JT 2009 (4) SC 432; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 462; 2009(4) SCALE 154; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 915

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Single Act

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 120(b), 420, 468, 477-A r/w 34; Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, charge of common intent among the accused persons has been clearly made out from the evidence which has been discussed in detail by both the Trial Court and the High Court - In spite of acquittal of one co-accused it is open to the Court to convict the other accused on the basis of joint liability under s. 34, if there is evidence against them of committing the offence in furtherance of the common intention - No perversity in the finding of the High Court nor any wrong legal conclusion has been reached on proved facts - Appeals dismissed.
