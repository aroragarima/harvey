Â Gram Panchayat of v Malwinder Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 July 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, V.D. Tulzapurkar, A. Varadarajan, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 403; (1985) 3 SCC 661; AIR 1985 SC 1394; 1985 (2) CCC 589; 1985(2) SCALE 35; [1985] Supp2 S.C.R. 28; 1986 (1) UJ 293

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Land and Property - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 254, 31, 31A, sch.7 list 2 entry 18 and list 3 entry 41 - Administration of Evacuee Property Act of 1950 - Punjab Village Common Lands (Regulation) Act of 1953 - Controversy in the writ petitions is between the right of the Gram Panchayats to the Shamlat-deh lands situated in those villages which fall within their jurisdiction and, on the other hand, the right of the Rehabilitation Deptt. of the Central Govt. to allot lands of that description, to the extent of the evacuee interest therein, to migrants from Pakistan - (A) Repugnancy - Parliament has passed a law on a matter which falls under Entry No. 41 of the Concurrent List, while the State Legislature has passed a law which falls under Entry No. 18 of the State List - Central Act of 1950 vesting evacuee interest in Shamilat-deh lands in custodian while subsequent State Act of 1953 vesting such lands in Panchayat of village - Question of law as to the alleged repugnancy the Acts - Held, pith and substance of Punjab Act being 'land' falling under entry 18 of State list it would prevail in Punjab over Central Act - (B) Issue regarding extention of efficacy of President's assent u/art.254(2) of the Constitution where assent was obtained for specific purpose - Its efficacy cannot be extended beyond that purpose; assent accorded by the President for the express purpose of art.31-A is not capable of automatic transformation into assent for the purpose of art. 254(2) of the Constitution - Appeal allowed.

Note: (per CHINNAPPA REDDY, J) (A) Constitution of India, 1950 - Whether the legislature of the State could make a law relating to agrarian reform in respect of property which included property which by a process of law had become vested in the Central Govt. or the Custodian - Held, State Legislature competent to pass such law
