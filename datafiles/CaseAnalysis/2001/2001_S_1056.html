State of Punjab v Dayanand Medical College and Hospital and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19939; (2001) 8 SCC 664; AIR 2001 SC 3006; JT 2001 (8) SC 529; 2001(7) SCALE 162; 2001 (5) SCJ 218; 2001 (5) SLR 694; 2001 (7) SLT 271; [2001] Supp4 S.C.R. 72; 2001 (7) Supreme 693; 2001 (3) UPLBEC 2440

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Health & Drug

Keywords: Prescription, Postgraduate Medical Education Regulations, 2000

Summary: Education - Constitution - Postgraduate medical courses - State reservation - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 15(4) - Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, s. 20 - Postgraduate Medical Education Regulations, 2000, regn. 9 - (A) Whether prescription made by appellants reducing minimum marks for admission is in contravention with regulations framed by Medical Council of India? - Held, prescription made by appellants reducing minimum marks to 40% in entrance examination for considering eligibility of candidates for admission to postgraduate medical courses and in respect of basic subjects fixing no minimum standard is plainly in contravention of regulations framed by Medical Council of India - (B) Whether Medical Council of India should also make reservation in respect of socially and educationally backward classes in terms of art. 15(4) of Constitution? - Further held, though in a sense Medical Council of India could also be a 'State' for certain purposes, such a body would not be suited to make necessary reservation in respect of socially and educationally backward classes in terms of art. 15(4) of Constitution - Appeal allowed.
