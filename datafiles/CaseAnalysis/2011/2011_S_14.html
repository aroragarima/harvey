Sadhwi Pragyna Singh Thakur v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

23 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 673; (2011) 10 SCC 445; JT 2011 (12) SC 56; 2011 (4) KLT 321; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 276; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 302; 2011 (4) RLW 3361; 2011(10) SCALE 771; [2011] 14 S.C.R. 617

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Armed Forces (Assam and Manipur) Special Powers Act, 1958, Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act, 1957, Assam Maintenance of Public Order Act, 1953

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 22(1), 22(2) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 50-A, 160, 160(1), 167(2), 401, 439 - Maharashtra Control of Organised Crime Act, 1999, ss. 21, 21(4), 21(2)(b) - Explosive Substances Act, 1908, ss. 3, 4, 5 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 153, 302, 307, 324, 427 - Illegal detention - Arrest - Charge sheet - Validity- - A bomb blast took place by making use of a two wheeler - Inspector investigating case learnt that same two wheeler was sold to appellant - Appellant was taken to office of ATS - Appellant pleaded that she was formally arrested but reasons of her arrest were not communicated to her and no legal assistance was made available to her - Appellant also questioned treatment meted out to her by police - Appellant claimed application for bail on plea that charge sheet was required to be filed within 90 days from date of arrest and as no charge sheet was filed within 90 days, appellant was entitled to bail u/s. 21(2)(b) of the 1999 Act ands. 167(2) CrPC - Bail Application was rejected - Appellant filed Criminal Application in HC - Application filed was rejected - Hence, instant appeal - Whether grounds for bail claimed by appellant were satisfactory and Whether there was violation of either art. 22(2) of Constitution or s. 167 CrPC - Held, Court found no substance in contentions of appellant - Court pointing out that grounds seeking bail either before Trial Court or before HC was not sought for on ground of violation of art. 22(2) of Constitution but it was confined only to plea that charge sheet was not filed within 90 days and this issue could not go into in S.L.P - Plea that art. 22(2) of Constitution was violated was based on averment by appellant that she was arrested on dt. 10-10-2008 which was factually wrong - Appellant was arrested only on 23-10-2008 - Prayer in said application had not asked for being set at liberty and only asked for an enquiry - Thus, question of violation of art. 22(2) did not arise - - Appeal dismissed.
