Union of India and Others v J. G. Glass Industries Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Srinivasan, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1185; (1998) 2 SCC 32; AIR 1998 SC 839; 1998 (78) ECR 761; 1998 (97) E.L.T. 5; JT 1997 (9) SC 750; 1997 (23) RLT 768; 1997(7) SCALE 548; 1998 (1) SLT 371; 1999 (114) STC 387; [1997] Supp6 S.C.R. 345; 1998 (1) Supreme 60

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Excise - Constitution - Central Excise and Salt Act, 1944, s. 2(f) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 265 - 'Manufacture' - (A) Whether process of printing on bottles is a 'manufacturing' process - Levy of duty on plain bottles at gate of main factory - And once again on printed bottles in premises where printing is carried on - (B) Whether amounts to double taxation under same tariff item - Held, (A) Printing on glass bottles does not bring into existence a new commercial commodity which is distinct and separate in its character, use and name - Does not amount to manufacture - (B) It amounts to double taxation under same tariff item which is not permissible - Appeal allowed.
