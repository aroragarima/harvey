Ashok Kumar Das and Others v University of Burdwan and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 180; (2010) 3 SCC 616; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 750; 2010 (2) ESC 201; 2010 (125) FLR 400; JT 2010 (3) SC 49; 2010 (4) MLJ(SC) 137; 2010(3) SCALE 110; [2010] 3 S.C.R. 429; 2011 (7) SLR 541

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Burdwan University Act, 1981

Summary: Service - Burdwan University Act, 1981, s. 21(xiii) - Resolution - Approval of State Government - Promotions to different grades of non-teaching staff of the Burdwan University were being done on the basis of seniority - On 26.06.1995, the Executive Council of the University resolved that criteria of 'Seniority-cum- Efficiency' would be followed for promotion to different grades of non-teaching staff of the University - Aggrieved by the Resolution of the Executive Council of the University, appellants filed the Writ Petition - Single Judge of the HC set aside the Resolution and directed the University to re-frame its guidelines for promotion - On appeal, DB of HC directed the University to send the proposal in the Resolution to the State Government for its approval - Pursuant thereto, State Government by its order dated 10.10.2002 approved the Resolution - Appellants submitted that the Resolution of the Executive Council of the University adopted on 26.06.1995 and approved by the State Government on 10.10.2002 cannot apply to promotions to vacancies which have occurred prior to 10.10.2002 - Held, approval to an act or decision can also be subsequent to the act or decision - Since the words used in s. 21(xiii) of the Act are "with the approval of the State Government", the Executive Council of the University could determine the terms and conditions of service of the non-teaching staff and obtain the approval of the State Government subsequently and in case the State Government did not grant approval subsequently, any action taken on the basis of the decision of the Executive Council of the University would be invalid and not otherwise - Promotions to different grades of non-teaching staff made by the University on the basis of the principles laid down in the Resolution of the Executive Council of the University adopted on 26.06.1995 are valid as the Resolution was approved by the State Government on 10.10.2002 - Appeal dismissed.
