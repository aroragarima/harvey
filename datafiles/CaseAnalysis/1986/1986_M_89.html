Pathumma and Another v Muhammad

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 51; (1986) SCC (Cr) 212; (1986) 2 SCC 585; AIR 1986 SC 1436; 1986 (12) ALR 452; 1986 (12) AWC 732; 1986 BBCJ 61; 1986 (2) Crimes 675; 1986 CRLJ 1070; 1986 CrLR(SC) 223; 1986 (2) DMC 208; 1986 (2) HLR 160; 1986(1) SCALE 603; [1986] 2 S.C.R. 731; 1986 (1) UJ 652

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Practice & Procedure -Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 125 and 401 - Revisional jurisdiction of High Court in exercise of claim for maintenance for applicant and her child u/s. 125, CrPC, 1973 - Magistrate holding applicant not to be married wife and child to be illegitimate and on those findings, granting maintenance to child while denying same to applicant - HC reappreciating evidence and holding child not to be illegitimate child and denying maintenance to child -Whether High Court was justified in substituting its own view for that of Magistrate on question of fact? -Applicant was married wife or whether child was an illegitimate child were pre-eminently questions of fact and HC in its revisional jurisdiction was not justified in substituting its own view for that of Magistrate on question of fact - Order accordingly.
