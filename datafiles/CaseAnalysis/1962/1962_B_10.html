Â Commissioner of Income Tax v Bai Shirinbai K. Kooka

Supreme Court of India

23 February 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 336; AIR 1963 SC 477; [1962] 46 ITR 86; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 391

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10(1) - (A) Whether profit is to be ascertained on difference between price realised and price at which these shares were brought or on a difference between price realised and market price ruling on 1-04-1945 when these shares were converted into stock in trade? - Held, assessable profits were difference between sale price and market value of shares prevailing on 1-04-1945 - Commercial profits out of transaction of sale of an article must be difference between what article cost business and what it fetched on sale - So far as business or trading activity was concerned, market value of shares as on 01-04-1945 was what it cost business - Appeal dismissed.
