Om Prakash and Others v State of UP and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Fundamental Duties

Summary: Constitution of India-Article 19 (1) (g)/19 (6)-U. P. Municipalities Act, 1916-Sections 241, 298 and List I, heading F-Sale of eggs within municipal limits of Rishikesh-Total prohibition imposed thereon by amending bye-laws though word 'eggs' not covered by heading F of List I of Act-Whether justified?-Held, âyesâ-Mere omission to mention 'eggs' in Section 241 or Section 298 (2) or List I, heading F-Not to make amended bye-laws invalid-Resolution to amend bye-law adopted in deference to religious and cultural demands of large number of residents and pilgrims visiting regularly-High Court right in holding that prohibition on sale of eggs not unreasonable restriction-Impugned bye-law cannot be held to be violative of Article 19 (1) (g).
