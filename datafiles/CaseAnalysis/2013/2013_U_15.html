Ayurved Shastra Seva Mandal & Anr. v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

06 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Vikramajit Sen, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 127; (2013) 16 SCC 696; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 232; 2013 (98) ALR 715; 2013 (3) Bom.C.R. 525; JT 2013 (4) SC 64; 2013(3) SCALE 213; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 1098; 2013 (3) SLR 445

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Indian Medicine Central Council (Permission to Existing Medical Colleges) Regulations, 2006, Establishment of New Medical College, Opening of New or Higher Course of Study or Training and Increase of Admission Capacity by a Medical College Regulations, 2003

Summary: Education - Admissions - Necessary criteria - Denial - Validity - Petitioners filed instant SLP's challenging order of HC with regard to admissions to various institutions teaching Indian form of medicines such as Ayurvedic, Unani, Siddha, etc. for academic year 2011-12 - Petitioners contended that since deficiencies had been removed, there could be no reason for permission for academic year 2011-12 to be withheld, since a large number of applications had been received from students intending to obtain admission for said year - Held, those who chose to file their applications did so at their own risk and it could not at present be contended that since they had been allowed to file their applications pursuant to orders passed by HC, they had acquired a right to be admitted in different institutions to which they had applied - Further, privilege granted to candidates could not be transformed into a right to be admitted in course for which they had applied - However, it was practically impossible for a student to pick up threads of teaching for entire 1st year when half course had been completed - Thus, it was not for us to judge as to whether a particular institution fulfilled necessary criteria for being eligible to conduct classes in concerned discipline or not - That was for experts to judge and according to experts, institutions were not geared to conduct classes in respect of year 2011-12 - It was also impractical to consider proposal of colleges of providing extra classes to new entrants to bring them to level of those who have completed major part of course for 1st year - Therefore, no reason to interfere with orders of HC and same were upheld - Petitions dismissed.
