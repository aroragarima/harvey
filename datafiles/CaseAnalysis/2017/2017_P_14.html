Brakewel Automotive Components (India) Private Limited v P. R. Selvam Alagappan

Supreme Court of India

21 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Amitava Roy, Arun Mishra

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 198; (2017) 5 SCC 371; AIR 2017 SC 1577; 2017 (4) ALD(SC) 29; 2017 (122) ALR 525; 2017 (3) ALT(SC) 21; 2017 (3) Bom.C.R. 439; 2017 (2) CLT(SC) 281; JT 2017 (5) SC 534; 2017 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1326; 2017 (2) RCR(Civil) 1047; 2017(3) SCALE 627

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Companies Act, 1956, Executing Court, Documentary, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 47 - Suit for recovery - Determination - Validity - Appellant filed suit against respondent for recovery of Rs. 20,94,953/- arising from business transactions - Trial Court decreed suit for Rs. 18,95,077/- - Respondent filed an application u/s. 47 CPC seeking to supplement the same by way of oral and documentary testimony - Appellant objected in its rejoinder as impermissible and prayed for obliteration of such evidence - Executing Court dismissed objection of appellant holding that it was necessary to examine as to how respondent was related to proprietorship firm, a question to be decided in the proceedings u/s. 47 CPC - On appeal, HC affirmed the determination made by Executing Court - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, it was no longer res integra that an Executing Court could neither travel behind the decree nor sit in appeal over the same or pass any order jeopardizing the rights of parties. It was only in the limited cases where decree was by a Court lacking inherent jurisdiction or was a nullity that the same was rendered non-est and was inexecutable. A decree of Court of law being sacrosanct in nature, the execution ought not to be thwarted on mere asking and on untenable and purported grounds having no bearing on the validity or executability. No case was made out to entertain the remonstrances against the decree or application u/s. 47 CPC. Both Executing Court and HC have not only erred in construing the scope and ambit of scrutiny u/s. 47 CPC, but have also overlooked the fact that the decree does not suffer either from any jurisdictional error or was otherwise invalid in law. The objections to the execution petition as well as to the application u/s. 47 CPC filed by respondent do not either disclose any substantial defence to the decree or testify the same to be suffering from any jurisdictional infirmity or invalidity which were rejected. Impugned orders of Courts below were set aside. Executing Court would proceed with the execution proceedings and take it to the logical end with utmost expedition. Appeals allowed.
