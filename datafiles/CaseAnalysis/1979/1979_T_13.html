Bai Vajia (Dead) By L. Rs. v Thakorbhai Chelabhai And Others

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

A.D. Koshal, P.S. Kailasam, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 270; (1979) 3 SCC 300; AIR 1979 SC 993; 1979 (20) GLR 641; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 291

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, ss. 14(1), 14(2) - Possession of Land - Arrears of Maintenance - Pre-existing Right - Limited Owner - According to terms of decree in suit, appellant/deceased would be entitled to take possession of land in default made by âDâ in payment of maintenance to her - Appellant took execution and obtained possession of land and enjoyed income, without being competent to sell, mortgage, bequeath, gift or otherwise transfer same - Arrears of maintenance was deposited by âDâ and filed an application that land in possession of appellant, in execution of decree be restored to them was dismissed - âDâ filed Suit for declaration that dismissal of his application was null and void and for recovery of possession of land, which was dismissed by Trial Court and in first appeal - Meanwhile, appellant made a sale in favor of Respondent No. 11, which was challenged u/s. 14(1) of Act before HC, which was dismissed - It would amount to mere recognition of a pre-existing right or a conferment of new title u/s. 14(2) of Act - Hence instant appeal - Whether Hindu widow had a right to property in lieu of her maintenance - Held, Court viewed that where appellant/widow was in possession of any specific property for the purpose of her maintenance, a purchaser buying with notice of her claim was not entitled to possession of that property without securing proper maintenance for her - Grant of maintenance, would not be without any pre-existing right in appellant/widow and she was entitled to same - As soon as deceased widow took possession of land, no rights of any kind whatsoever in relation thereto remained with appellant and thus they ceased to be owners for span of widow's life and whatever rights existed in relation to land during the life-time of widow/deceased appellant, were exercisable by her alone and by nobody else - After land was made over to her she became its owner for life, although with a limited right and therefore only as a limited owner u/s. 14(1) of the Act - Judgment and decree of HC were set aside - Appeal allowed.
