Municipal Corporation of Hyderabad v P.N. Murthy and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 January 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

M.P. Thakkar, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28142; (1987) 1 SCC 568; AIR 1987 SC 802; [1987] 167 ITR 204; JT 1987 (1) SC 285; 1987 (2) RCJ 382; 1987 (1) RCR(Rent) 302; 1987 (2) RentLR 692; 1987(2) SCALE 393; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 107; 1987 (1) UJ 325

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Legality, Levy of property tax

Summary: Municipalities and Local Governments - Land & Property - Hyderabad Municipal Corporation Act, 1955, ss. 202(1), 202(1)(c) - Hire purchase agreement - Levy of property tax - Legality - Respondents (petitioners) were allotted with houses by appellant (Municipal Corporation) under an agreement which provided that all municipal taxes and other charges would be borne by allottees - Thereafter, petitioners were served with demand notice to pay house taxes - Petitioners submitted their objections that they were not liable to taxes as they vest in Corporation - However petitioners filed petitions before HC and contended that u/s. 202(1) of the Act prohibit levy of general tax in respect of houses under the hire purchase agreement - Single Judge dismissed petition, on appeal before DB - DB held that title vest unto the Corporation at relevant time and set aside judgment of Single Judge - Hence, instant appeal - Whether respondents were liable to pay tax as claimed by Corporation - Held, Single Judge was right in taking view that buildings and lands vesting unto the Corporation not only in title but also in possession (as polarized from those vesting in title only but not in possession) were exempted from the obligation imposed by legislature to levy the property taxes - Buildings and lands which were merely owned by Corporation but were in actual possession or under the actual use and occupation of someone else, that was to say persons or bodies other than Corporation itself were not exempted - In order to attract s. 202(1)(c) a property should satisfy a dual test - Property should not only be owned by Corporation, it should also be in occupation of Corporation itself - There could be no principle in exempting the tenants inducted by Corporation in its property from payment of taxes if the terms of lease so provided - Just as tenants who occupy the properties belonging to private citizens have to pay property taxes if the terms of agreement so provide, the tenants inducted by Corporation in buildings owned by itself have to pay the property taxes if agreement so provided - There could be no rational basis for exempting the tenants or persons inducted by Corporation in its own buildings from payment of such taxes - Impugned judgment passed by DB was set aside and order passed by Single Judge was restored - Petition dismissed.
