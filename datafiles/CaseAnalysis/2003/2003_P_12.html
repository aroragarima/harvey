T. Lakshmipathi and others v P. Nithyananda Reddy and others

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 308; (2003) 5 SCC 150; AIR 2003 SC 2427; 2003 (3) ALD(SC) 42; 2003 (5) AWC 4428; 2003 (2) CGLJ 243; 2003 (1) RCJ 128; 2003 (3) RCR(Civil) 305; 2003 (2) RCR(Rent) 117; 2003(3) SCALE 523; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 173; 2003 (4) SLT 50; 2003 (3) Supreme 75

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - A P Buildings (Lease, Rent & Eviction) Control Act, 1960 - ss. 10(2)(i) and 10(3)(b)(iii) - Decree for eviction under - Challenged - Whether the appellants are absolved of their obligation of delivering possession over the suit premises to the landlord-respondent No 1 because the tenancy rights in the suit premises held by respondents No 2 and 3, and transferred by them to the appellants, have merged in the ownership entailing determination of tenancy - Doctrine of merger - Held, the lease cannot be said to have been determined by merger so long as the interests of the lessee, the lesser estate and of the owner, the larger estate do not come to coalesce in full - Tenancy cannot be said to have been determined by attracting applicability of the doctrine of frustration consequent upon demolishing of the tenancy premises - In the event of the tenancy having been created in respect of building standing on the land, it is the building and the land which are both components of subject matter of demise and the destruction of the building alone does not determine the tenancy when the land which was site of the building continues to exist; more so when the building has been destroyed or demolished neither by the landlord nor by an act of nature but solely by the act of the tenant or the person claiming under him - Appeal dismissed.
