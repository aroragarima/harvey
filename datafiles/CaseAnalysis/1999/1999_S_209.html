Satvinder Kaur v State (Govt. of Nct of Delhi) and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 527; (1999) 8 SCC 728; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1503; AIR 1999 SC 3596; 1999 (37) ALR 691; 1999 CRLJ 4566; 1999 (82) DLT 26; JT 1999 (8) SC 25; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 348

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments; Tort

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1974, s. 482, 156(2), 177, 178 and 154 - Inherent powers. - FIR lodged by appellant because alleged dowry items were entrusted to respondent at Patiala and that alleged cause of action for offence punishable u/s. 498 - A, IPC, 1860 arose at Patiala - Held, cannot be quashed on grounds that police station did not have territorial jurisdiction to investigate offence - Police officer cannot refuse to record FIR and/or investigate it for want of territorial jurisdiction.
