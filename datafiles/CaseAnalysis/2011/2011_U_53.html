Videocon Industries Limited v Union Of India And Another

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 337; (2011) 6 SCC 161; AIR 2011 SC 2040; 2011 (88) ALR 237; 2011 (4) AWC 3993; 2011 (2) CLT(SC) 404; 2011 (4) CompLJ 256; JT 2011 (6) SC 261; 2011 (6) MLJ(SC) 972; 2011 (5) RCR(Civil) 458; 2011(5) SCALE 678; [2011] 8 S.C.R. 569

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: International Commercial Arbitrations, Solicitor

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Constitution - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 9 - Jurisdiction - Respondent no. 1 (Government of India), executed a Production Sharing Contract (PSC) with a consortium of 4 companies (Appellants) for exploring, mining and producing hydrocarbons in India owned by respondent No.1 - Said PSC provided for arbitration in Kuala Lampur and the arbitration clause was to be governed by the laws of England - Dispute arose between the parties with respect to correctness of certain cost recoveries and profit - Matter was referred to the arbitral tribunal which fixed hearing at Kuala Lumpur, but due to outbreak of epidemic in Kuala Lampur, the arbitral tribunal shifted the venue of its sittings to Amsterdam and then subsequently to London - Arbitral Tribunal passed partial award - Respondent No.1 challenged said award by filing a petition in the HC of Kuala Lumpur - Appellants questioned the maintainability of the case before the HC of Kuala Lumpur contending that in view of the PSC only the English Courts have the jurisdiction to entertain any challenge to the award - Subsequently, respondent no. 1 approached Delhi HC seeking a declaration u/s. 9 of the Act to the effect that the Kuala Lampur was the contractual and juridical seat of arbitration - Said question referred to the SC - (A) Whether Kuala Lumpur was the designated seat or juridical seat of arbitration? - Held, as per the terms of agreement, the seat of arbitration was Kuala Lumpur - If the parties wanted to amend the agreement, they could have done so only by written instrument which was required to be signed by all of them - Admittedly, neither there was any agreement between the parties to the PSC to shift the juridical seat of arbitration from Kuala Lumpur to London nor any written instrument was signed by them for amending the agreement - Therefore, the mere fact that the parties to the particular arbitration had agreed for shifting of the seat of arbitration to London cannot be interpreted as anything except physical change of the venue of arbitration from Kuala Lumpur to London - Hence, Kuala Lumpur was the designated seat or juridical seat of arbitration - (B) Whether the Delhi HC could entertain the petition filed by the respondents u/s. 9 of the Act? - Held, SC in Bhatia International vs. Bulk Trading S.A 2002 INDLAW SC 265, held that Part I of the Act would apply to all arbitration including international arbitrations unless it was expressly or impliedly excluded by the parties - In the instant case, the parties had agreed that the impugned arbitration agreement shall be governed by laws of England - This necessarily implies that the parties had agreed to exclude the provisions of Part I of the Act - Hence, Delhi HC did not have the jurisdiction to entertain the petition filed by the respondents u/s. 9 of the Act - Appeal allowed.
