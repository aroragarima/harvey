G. M., Bharat Coking Coal Limited, West Bengal v Shib Kumar Dushad and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2801; (2000) 8 SCC 696; AIR 2001 SC 72; 2001 (1) CLR 36; JT 2000 (Supp2) SC 325; 2001 LabIC 28; 2001 (1) LLJ 532; 2000(7) SCALE 270; 2001 (2) SLJ 156; 2000 (5) SLR 604; 2000 (7) SLT 735; [2000] Supp4 S.C.R. 336; 2000 (7) Supreme 380; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 655

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: All India Services (Death-cum-Retirement Benefits) Rules, 1958,

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Mines Act, 1952 - Coal Mines Nationalisation Act, 1973 - All India Services (Delhi-cum-Retirement Benefits) Rule, 1958, r. 16 A - Whether High Court was right in interfering with date of birth recorded in service records maintained by employer, in proceeding u/art. 226 of Constitution? - Held, ordinarily HCs should not, in exercise of their discretionary writ jurisdiction, entertain a writ application/petition filed by an employee of Govt. or its instrumentality, towards fag end of his service, seeking correction of his date of birth entered in his 'Service and Leave Record' or Service Register with avowed object of continuing in service beyond normal period of his retirement - Prudence on part of every HC should, however, prevent it from granting interim relief in a petition for correction of date of birth filed u/art. 226 of Constitution by an employee in relation to his employment, because of well-settled legal position governing such correction of date of birth - In such circumstances, no scope for HC to interfere with date of birth as determined by employer HC erred in interfering with date of birth of respondent - After 20 years of service respondent-employee raised claim that his date of birth was 1946 and not 1932 matter has been determined by authority concerned by following procedure prescribed under Rules - Medical board determined age of employee to be 52 years in 1988 and same was accepted by him - Appeal allowed.
