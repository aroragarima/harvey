Municipal Board, Mainpuri v Kanhaiya Lal

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 214; AIR 1960 SC 184; 1960 CRLJ 281; 1960 SCJ 1028; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 941

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - U. P. Municipalities Act, 1916, s. 128 - Interpretation of s. 128 - Conviction - Acquittal - Payment of toll - Liability - State Govt. issued a notification defining the municipal limits - Municipality fixed a toll-barrier on this road between the railway goods-shed and the inhabited area of the town - Respondent was asked to pay toll-tax at the toll-barrier, but he did not pay it - Respondent was prosecuted u/s. 299(1) of the Act, r/w r. 1 of the Rules for assessment and collection of toll-tax - Respondent denied his liability to pay the tax - Magistrate convicted him under the said section - On appeal Appellate Court confirmed the same - On revision HC set aside the conviction and acquitted respondent - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in acquitting respondent - Held, the word 'entering' in s.128(vii) of the Act clearly indicated that the conveyance to the liable to the toll must enter the Municipality from places outside it - By no stretch of language it was possible to hold that a vehicle which was already in the limits of the Municipality, when it plies for hire, enters the municipal limits - Words 'bring within the limits of Municipality' in r. 1 emphasize the idea that a laden vehicle could not be brought within the Municipality until the toll due has been paid - One could not bring within the Municipality a vehicle which was already in the Municipality - When a laden vehicle passes from one part of the Municipality to another part, it has to pay toll if there is a barrier between the two parts - Find it well high impossible to hold that a vehicle was brought within the limits of the Municipality when it was brought from one part of the Municipality to another part - SC agreed with the construction put upon the section by the HC - Appeal dismissed.
