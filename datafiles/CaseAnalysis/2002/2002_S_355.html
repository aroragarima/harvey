Raghunath v State of Haryana and another

Supreme Court of India

13 November 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1397; (2003) SCC (Cr) 326; (2003) 1 SCC 398; AIR 2003 SC 165; 2003 (2) CalLT(SC) 41; 2002 (4) CCR 259; 2003 (1) Crimes 260; 2003 (2) CRJ 128; 2003 CRLJ 401; JT 2002 (9) SC 556; 2002(8) SCALE 445; 2002 (6) SLT 499; [2002] Supp4 S.C.R. 130; 2003 (1) Supreme 49

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Examination Of Accused

Summary: IPC - Ss. 148, 302, 325, 323, 452 and 436 r/w 149 - conviction and sentence under - appeal - held, prosecution has failed to establish its case beyond reasonable doubts. It is now well settled principle of law that if two views are possible, one in favour of the accused and the other adversely against it, the view favoring the accused must be accepted.
