Ugam Chand Bhandari v Commissioner of Central Excise, Madras

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 367; (2004) 5 SCC 757; AIR 2004 SC 3326; 2004 (167) E.L.T. 491; JT 2004 (Supp2) SC 197; 2004 (62) RLT 240; 2004(5) SCALE 365; [2004] Supp2 S.C.R. 58

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: [A.] Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 11A - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 173Q, 56 - Whether water-proofed fabrics are classifiable under Heading 52 07 or under Heading 59 06 of Central Excise Tariff Schedule; whether extended period of limitation under proviso to Section 11 A of Central Excise Act, 1944 is invokable; whether penalty under rule 173 Q of Central Excise Rules, 1944 is imposable - Fabric manufactured by appellants is impregnated and, therefore, has to be considered as fabric impregnated with materials other than those mentioned under Tariff 59.02 and 59.05 - Impregnated fabrics with a coating and which is visible to naked eye on material on record being one of finding of fact, cannot interfere with it - Extended period of limitation as provided u/s. 11 A of Act not correct - Appeals partly allowed.
