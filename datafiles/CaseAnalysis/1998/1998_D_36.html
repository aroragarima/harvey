H. M. M. Limited v Director General, Monopolies and Restrictive Trade Practices Commission

Supreme Court of India

11 August 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1280; (1998) 6 SCC 485; AIR 1998 SC 2691; [1998] 94 Comp Cas 132; 1998 (6) CTJ 249A; JT 1998 (5) SC 433

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Indirect Tax

Summary: Monopolies and Restrictive Trade Practices Act 1969, ss. 36 A(3)(a) and (b) - Question was whether unfair trade practice was observed and whether scheme framed was conduct of lottery or not - Argued for unfair trade practice that gifts were not free as scheme of free gifts introduced by manufacturer of consumer product after raising price of product - Held, scheme was not a lottery and due to price increase, terming scheme as unfair trade practice would be unfair - Moreover expenditure made on prizes was significantly lesser compared to that made on advertisement.
