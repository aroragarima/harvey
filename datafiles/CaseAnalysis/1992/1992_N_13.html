State of Rajasthan and Others v Nandlal and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S. Ranganathan, Veeraswami Ramaswami

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 630; (1993) Supp1 SCC 681; AIR 1992 SCW 3150; 1992 (3) CCC 763; JT 1992 (5) SC 655; 1992(2) SCALE 755; [1992] Supp1 S.C.R. 785; 1993 (1) UJ 36

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Excise

Keywords: Demand or recover deficit

Summary: Excise - Administrative - Excise duty - Demand or recover deficit - Validity -Respondents/licensees filed writ petitions before HC questioning demand of deficit guaranteed/stipulated amount - Single Judge allowed petitions holding that State WAS not entitled to demand or recover deficit amount from a licensee, whether under Guarantee System or under Exclusive Privilege System, for reason that such recovery would amount to recovery of Excise Duty on undrawn liquor - Hence, instant appeals - Whether short-lifting of liquor on part of petitioners during year 1967-68 was on account of default of their part or on account of inability of State to supply requisite quantities of liquor - Held, State was only source of supply for such licensees - Unless State supplies them liquor they could not carry on their business - Thus, it was essentially a commercial contract, no doubt governed by statutory provisions - The obligation to supply constitutes underpinning of contract - However, that did not mean that State was bound to supply as much as demanded or that its failure to supply on a given day or in a given week could be termed as failure to supply - Thus, all licensees should be treated in a fair and equal manner in matter of supplies, particularly during lean years - Due regard should be had to the rules, conditions of license and agreement and other provisions applicable in that behalf, in determining whether there was a failure on part of the State to supply - Again, extent of relief in case of failure on part of State to supply depends on length of period of non-supply, loss caused to licensee on that account, all of which had to be judged under relevant provisions of the Act, rules, conditions of license and agreement and other orders, if any, applicable - Appeals disposed of.
