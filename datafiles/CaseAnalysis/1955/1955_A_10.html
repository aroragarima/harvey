Amrik Singh v State of Pepsu

Supreme Court of India

28 February 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 9; AIR 1955 SC 309; 1955 CRLJ 865; 1955 (1) MLJ(SC) 198; 1955 SCJ 334; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 1302

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, misa

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898 - s. 197(1) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 161, 409, 465 and 477-A - Government of India Act, 1935, s. 270 - Whether sanction u/s. 197 (1) of CrPC, 1898 was necessary for prosecuting appellant u/s. 409 IPC? - When charge is one of misappropriation by a public servant, whether sanction is required u/s. 197(1) will depend upon facts of each case - If acts complained of are so integrally connected with duties attaching to office as to be inseparable from them, then sanction u/s. 197 (1) would be necessary - If there was no necessary connection between performance of those duties and occasion or opportunity for acts, then no sanction would be required - Held, acts with which appellant is charged falls within scope of his duties, and can be justified by him as done by virtue of his office - Therefore, sanction was required u/s. 197(1) before appellant could be prosecuted u/s. 409, and absence of such sanction is fatal to maintainability of prosecution - Conviction should, therefore, be quashed - Appeal allowed.
