Lakshmi Narain Agarwal v State Transport Authority, U.P. and Another

Supreme Court of India

26 September 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 217; AIR 1968 SC 410; 1968 ALJ 332; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 635

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Regional Transport Authority, Enhancement Compensation, Existing Operator

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 47(3) and 64-A - Whether decision of Regional Transport Authority on ground that a mere decision of Regional Transport Authority limiting number of stage carriages u/s. 47(3) of Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 could not form subject matter of a revision application is justified? - Held, s. 47(3) does not expressly say whether any representations can be made by persons already providing transport facilities or by associations representing persons interested in provision of transport facilities or by any local authority or police authority within whose jurisdiction route or area lies - Where a limit has been fixed u/s. 47(3) by Regional Transport Authority, and said authority proceeds to consider applications for permits u/s. 48 r/w s. 57, Regional Transport Authority must confine number of permits issued by it to those limits and on an appeal or revision by an aggrieved person, Appellate Authority or Revisional Authority must equally be confined to issue of permits within limits fixed u/s. 47(3) - S. 64-A is very wide in terms, only condition necessary for filing a revision is that it should be against an order made by Regional Transport Authority and against which no appeal lies - Appeal dismissed.
