Raghunath and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 January 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 505; (1973) SCC (Cr) 448; (1973) 1 SCC 564; AIR 1973 SC 1100; 1973 CRLJ 858

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 195(1)(c) - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 18 - (A) Nature of prohibition against complaint by private aggrieved parties - (B) Sworn testimony of accused's counsel - Binding effect on accused - Held, applies only if offence mentioned in section has been committed by a party to proceeding in his character as such party, that is after having become a party to proceeding - Admission by accused's counsel that thumb impression taken for comparison with that on forged deed was of plaintiff, witness cannot be rejected by court by applying rule that accused is not bound by his counsel's admission - Appeal dismissed.
