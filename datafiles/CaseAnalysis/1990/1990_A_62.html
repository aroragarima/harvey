P. Orr and Sons Private Limited v Associated Publishers (Madras) Limited

Supreme Court of India

09 November 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

T.K. Thommen, K.N. Saikia, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 432; (1991) 1 SCC 301; JT 1990 (4) SC 374; 1990 (2) MLJ(SC) 12; 1991 (2) RCJ 97; 1990 (2) RCR(Rent) 648; 1991 (1) RentLR 64; 1990(2) SCALE 960; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 615; [1990] Supp2 S.C.R. 615

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Demolition And Reconstruction

Summary: Rent control - Tamil Nadu Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1960, ss. 10 and 14(1)(b) - Whether respondent-landlord petition for eviction of appellant-tenant on ground specified u/s. 14(1)(b) of Act, is justified? - Held, test u/s. 14(1)(b) satisfied only if building is bona fide required by landlord for 'immediate', i.e., direct, sole and timely purpose of demolishing it with a view to erecting new building on site of existing building - Act does not accept requirement by landlord as a bona fide requirement within meaning of provision unless condition of building, in context of relevant circumstances, requires demolition - These are matter which are to be proved by evidence - Personal requirement of landlord or any member of his family for residence or business is not germane to s. 14, and to import that concept for construction of that Section, as High Court appears to have done, is to project s. 10 into s. 14, and that is an exercise which has no warrant in law - Crucial condition for demolition was absent - Petition dismissed - Appeal allowed.
