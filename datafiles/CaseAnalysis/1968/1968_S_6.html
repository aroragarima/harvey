Â Premier Automobiles Limited v S.N. Shrivastava and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 193; AIR 1970 SC 1386; [1970] 76 ITR 1; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 353

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act,ss. 161, 209, 212 and 210 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - (A) Whether u/ss. 209 and 210 of Indian Income-tax Act, no order for payment of advance-tax can be made against an agent of a non-resident? - (B)Whether a provision which authorises collection of advance-tax from an agent of a non-resident infringes equality clause of Constitution and is on that account void? - Since a non-resident is in respect of income which forms part of his total income liable to be assessed to tax, he may also be called upon to pay advance-tax in respect of income accruing to or received by him which forms part of his total income chargeable to tax u/ss. 209 and 210, empower Income Tax officer to compute advance tax in accordance with previous assessment - In case of no prior assesment he is required by s. 212(3) to make an estimate of his total income - It is expressly enacted by s. 161 that as regards income in respect of which a person is a representative assessee, he shall be subject to same duties, responsibilities and liabilities as if income were income received by or accruing to or in favour of him beneficially - It is clearly implicit therein that a representative assessee is not exempted from liability to pay advance tax - All of these rules apply to all of assessees and thus, there is no discrimination - Held, u/s. 161 an agent of a non-resident cannot escape liability to pay advance- Also Act does not accord discriminatory treatment between different assessees - Appeal dismissed.
