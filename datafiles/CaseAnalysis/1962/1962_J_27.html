Jumma Masjid, Mercara v Kodimaniandra Deviah

Supreme Court of India

11 January 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 422; AIR 1962 SC 847; 1963 (1) ALT(SC) 119; 1962 (2) MLJ 90; 1962 (2) MLJ(SC) 90; 1962 (2) SCJ 303; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 554

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 43 and 6(a) - (A) Whether a transferee is entitled to benefit of s. 43 when a transfer takes place on basis of a representation of transferee whereby he expresses his interest upon property? - Held, when transfer of a property takes place and transferor represents his interest in property, a transferee under s. 43 of can claim benefit as he had taken transfer on faith of representation and consideration - (B) Whether a person u/s. 43 of Transfer of Property Act making a representation can be heard if he alleges against a person acting upon that representation? - Further held, u/s. 43 of Transfer of Property Act rule estoppel operates whereby a person making a representation cannot be heard if he alleges contrary against a person acting on that representation - Appeal dismissed.
