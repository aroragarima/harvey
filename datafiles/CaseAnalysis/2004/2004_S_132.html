P. S. Pareed Kaka and Others v Shafee Ahmed Saheb

Supreme Court of India

23 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1463; (2004) 5 SCC 241; AIR 2004 SC 2049; 2004 (3) ALD(SC) 12; 2004 (56) ALR 122; JT 2004 (Supp2) SC 543; 2004 (2) KLT 130; 2004 (1) RCR(Rent) 503; 2004(3) SCALE 535

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 115 - Karnataka Rent Control Act, ss. 3, 14, 15, 16, 19, 21 and 50 - (A) Whether High Court has no jurisdiction to re-appreciate and evaluate evidence on record which has resulted in arriving at conclusion? - Held, finding of Trial Court cannot be sustained and HC on reappreciation of evidence, rightly so, held that landlord has established that his need for all four petition schedule premises is bona fide and reasonable - (B) Whether HC can interfere only if there is any mis-carriage of justice due to mistake of law and that finding of lower Court as to bona fide requirements? - Held, revisional powers of HC under Karnataka Rent Control Act, are wider than powers conferred upon it u/s. 115 of CPC, 1908 and HC is not precluded to appreciate evidence for arriving at conclusion regarding bona fide requirement, hence, HC has powers to entertain a revision and reappreciate evidence and dispose of same - Appeals dismissed.
