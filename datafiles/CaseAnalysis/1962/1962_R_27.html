Â Ram Lal Kapur and Sons Private Limited v Ram Nath and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 173; AIR 1963 SC 1060; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 242

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Rent Control - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 136 - Delhi and Ajmer-Merwara Rent Control Act, 1947, s. 7 A - (A) Whether Supreme Court should condone delay in making an application for special leave ex parte ? - Held, except in very rare cases, if not invariably, it should be proper that Supreme Court should adopt as a settled rule that delay in making an application for special leave should not be condoned ex parte - Before granting leave in such cases, notice should be served on respondent and later afford an opportunity to resist grant of leave - Such a course besides being just, would be preferable to having delay in making, same was improperly condoned, years after grant of leave when Court naturally feels embarrassed to decide applications for revoking leave at time of hearing appeal on ground that by injustice which would be caused to appellant if leave were then revoked, when he would be deprived of opportunity of perusing other remedies if leave had been refused earlier - Rules of Court should be amended suitably to achieve this purpose - Appeal allowed.
