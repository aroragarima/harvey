The Regional Director,E.S.I. Corporation And Anr. v Francis De Costa And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2548; (1996) 6 SCC 1; (1996) SCC (L&S) 1361; AIR 1997 SC 432; AIR 1999 SCW 2457; 1996 (2) CLR 812; 1996 (4) CLT 59; 1996 (74) FLR 2326; JT 1996 (8) SC 118; 1996 (2) KLT 799; 1996 LabIC 2720; 1997 (1) LLJ 34; 1996 (2) LLN 895; 1996 (2) MahLJ 911; 1997 (2) MLJ(SC) 70; 1996(6) SCALE 473; 1996 (3) SCJ 264; 1997 (1) SLJ 1; 1996 (6) SLR 553; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 797; 1996 (6) Supreme 678

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 - Employees State Insurance Act, 1948 - Place of employment - Accident - Claim - Respondent met an accident while he was on the way to place of employment - Respondent's claim for disablement was allowed by Employee's State Insurance Court - Appeal filed to HC was dismissed hence, instant Special Leave was filed - Whether, decision of HC was liable to set aside - Held, it could not be said that the injury suffered by the respondent one kilometer away from the factory while he was on his way to the factory was caused by an accident arising out of and in the course of his employment - Not every accident which had occurred to a man at the time of his employment, that was directly or indirectly engaged on what he was employed to do, gave a claim to compensation unless it arose out of the employment - Respondent was unable to prove that the accident had any causal connection with the work he was doing at the factory - Respondent had not suffered accident in the course of employment hence, the decision of HC was liable to be set aside - Appeal allowed.
