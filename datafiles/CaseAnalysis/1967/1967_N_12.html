National Conduits Private Limited v S. S. Arora

Supreme Court of India

01 September 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 193; AIR 1968 SC 279; [1967] 37 Comp Cas 786; 1967 (2) CompLJ 232; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 430

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 433 and 439 - Companies (Court) Rules, 1959, rr. 96 and 24 - Whether a court is bound under Companies (Court) Rules, to advertise petition once it is admitted? - Held, no, to advertise petition as soon as it is admitted, is contrary to plain terms of r. 96 - If it is accepted, that would make Court an instrument, in possible cases, of harassment and even of blackmail, for once a petition is advertised, business of Company is bound to suffer serious loss and injury - Appeal allowed.
