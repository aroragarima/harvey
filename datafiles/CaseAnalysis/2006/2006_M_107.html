Managing Director, North East K.R.T.C v Devidas Manikrao Sadananda

Supreme Court of India

15 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 821; (2006) 8 SCC 52; (2006) 3 SCC (Cr) 433; (2006) SCC (L&S) 1788; AIR 2006 SC 3273; 2006 (4) AWC 4005; 2006(9) SCALE 352; 2006 (7) SCJ 422; 2007 (2) SLR 441; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 284

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Corporate - Criminal - Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 10(4-A) - Whether the Labour Court had erred in the facts and circumstances of this case in not invoking the doctrine of "res ipsa loquitur" in a vehicular accident case? - The burden of proof was on the driver/respondent to show that the offending vehicle was not driven by him rashly and negligently - In cases of accidents it is not necessary as a relevant factor to examine before the enquiry officer passengers of the bus and that the Labour Court had failed to apply the correct standard of proof in relation to the domestic enquiry, which is "preponderance of probability" and thus a case for judicial review stood clearly made out by the Corporation/appellant - Held, the matter is remitted to the Labour Court to decide whether on the facts and circumstances of this case the maxim "res ipsa loquitur" applies or not - Civil appeal is allowed.
