Revenue Divisional Officer Fort, Kochi and others v Jalaja Dileep and another

Supreme Court of India

10 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 163; 2015 (1) KLT 984; 2015 (3) MLJ 225; 2015(3) SCALE 323; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 380

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Kerala Conservation of Paddy Land and Wetland Act, 2008, Kerala Land Utilization Order, 1967, Kerala Land Tax Act 1961

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Kerala Conservation of Paddy Land and Wetland Act, 2008 - Kerala Land Tax Act 1961, s. 18 - Kerala Land Utilization Order, 1967 - Conversion of Land - Change in Register - Justification - Respondent no.1 is joint owner of land in question and had approached Agricultural Officer who gave certificate to effect that land in question was recorded as converted land in Paddy Wetland in survey records, however, possession certificate was issued wherein it was reported that land in question is dry land - Further, for sale of land in question, purchasers of land insisted for production of order showing nature of land as dry land, as condition precedent for registering sale deed, but revenue authorities refused to correct same - HC vide order allowed petition directing that property of respondent no.1 will have to be classified as 'reclaimed purayidam' (dry land) in register and further issued direction to Tahsildar and Village Officer to effect appropriate correction as per terms of direction - Further, order was upheld by DB - Hence, present appeal.

Held, statutory enquiry to ascertain whether land is 'Paddy Land' or 'Wetland' and conversion of land for residential purpose or for any public purpose is governed by 1967 Order or 2008 Act for conversion of land from Wetland to Dry Land. SC held that concerned authorities constituted under 1967 Order or 2008 Act are competent authority. Nature of land cannot be changed or converted by directing changes in Register which is maintained only for purpose of land tax. SC held that rectification envisaged by s. 18 of 1961 Act can only be in respect of arithmetical or clerical error, that too in order of determining tax due. S. 18 of 1961 Act cannot be made use or same cannot be taken as means to effect conversion of nature of land bye-passing competent authority and procedure stipulated under 1967 Order and 2008 Act, hence, impugned judgment is liable set aside. Respondents are directed to approach competent authorities constituted under 1967 Order and 2008 Act. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Conversion of land cannot take place by changing entries in Register but can be made by competent authorities provide under statute.
