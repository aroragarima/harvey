Gajanan Jaikishan Joshi v Prabhakar Mohanlal Kalwar

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

M.H. Kania, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 341; (1990) 1 SCC 166; 1990 (1) CCC 262; 1990 (1) GLH 17; JT 1989 (4) SC 524; 1990 MahLJ 212; 1990 (1) RCR(Rent) 229; 1989(2) SCALE 1323; [1989] Supp2 S.C.R. 474

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure 1908, Special Relief Act, 1963

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Contract & Commercial - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 16(c) - Whether amendment of plaint is permissible as was sought by appellant? - Held, no fresh cause of action was sought to be introduced by amendment applied for - All that appellant sought to do was to complete cause of action for specific performance for which relief he had already prayed - Only one averment required in law to be made in a plaint in a suit for specific performance was not made, probably on account of some oversight or mistake of lawyer who drafted plaint and that error was sought to be rectified by amendment applied for - There was no fresh cause of action sought to be introduced by amendment and hence, no question of causing any injustice to respondent on that account arose - Amendment applied for by appellant is allowed - Appeal allowed.
