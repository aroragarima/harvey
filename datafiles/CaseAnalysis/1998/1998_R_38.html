Shreenath and Another v Rajesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 April 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Misra, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1632; (1998) 4 SCC 543; AIR 1998 SC 1827; AIR 1998 SCW 1619; 1998 (33) ALR 273B; 1998 (2) AWC 1403; 1998 (2) CLT 46; JT 1998 (3) SC 244; 1998 (1) RCR(Rent) 454; 1998(2) SCALE 725; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 709; 1998 (4) SLT 392; 1998 (Supp) RD 508; 1998 (4) Supreme 155

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21, r. 97 - Limitation Act, 1908 - Execution of decree for immovable property - (A) Procedural law - Interpretation of statutes - Where more than one interpretation is possible? - (B) Third party in possession claiming independent right as tenant if required to wait until he is dispossessed - Held, one which curtails procedure without eluding justice to be adopted - Need not wait, he can object and get his claim adjudicated when sought to be dispossessed by decree holder - Appeal allowed.
