Pramod v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

29 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 908; AIR 2016 SC 204; 2016(1) ALL MR 957; 2016 (2) AWC 1463; 2016 (148) FLR 555; 2016 (1) MLJ 403; 2016(1) SCALE 42

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: All India Council For Technical Education Act, 1987, Maharashtra Employees of Private Schools (Conditions of Service) Rules, 1981, Maharashtra Employees of Private Schools (Conditions of Services) Regulation Act, 1977, All India Council for Technical Education (Pay-scales, Service Conditions, and Qualifications of the Teachers and other Academic Staffs in Technical Institutions Diploma) Regulations, 2010, Principal, Head of Department, Lecturer and Workshop Superintendent in Government Polytechnic and Equivalent Institutes (Recruitment) Rules, 2012

Summary: Service - Maharashtra Employees of Private Schools (Conditions of Services) Regulation Act, 1977, ss. 2(24), 9 - Maharashtra Employees of Private Schools (Conditions of Service) Rules, 1981, rr. 3, 3(3) - Senior most member for appointment - Failure to promote to post - Justification - Appellant was appointed to post of Assistant Lecturer in Civil Engineering Department - Further, appellant was promoted during his service term - further, post of Principal feel vacant for which appellant claimed that he should be given charge of that post on basis of his seniority, however, another person was appointed as officiating Principal - Further, petition filed by appellant was dismissed on ground that appellant had alternative remedy of appeal u/s. 9 of Act before Tribunal - Tribunal dismissed appeal of appellant as pre-mature - Further, society issued advertisement inviting applications for direct appointment to post of Principal - Further, HC held in favor of appellant that he was entitled to all benefits as officiating Principal - However, respondent no.2 rejected appellant's prayer by holding that post of Principal had to be filled up by nomination, i.e., direct recruitment - Further, DB held in favor of appellant and provisions of Act and Rules are applicable to employees working in polytechnic colleges which being institutions imparting technical education stand covered by term 'School' as defined u/s. 2(24) of Act - Whether as senior most lecturer in private Polytechnic Institution, appellant is entitled to be considered for appointment to post of Principal because it is required to be made only by promotion by virtue of r. 3 of Rules framed under Act.

Held, view expressed by HC that Rules will not apply to private aided polytechnic such as respondent's polytechnic is confirmed by SC. Hence, there is no other Statutory Act or Rule to take away force of r. 3(3) of Rules which requires management of polytechnic, which is covered by definition of 'School' under Act, to fill up post of Head of Institution, i.e., Principal by appointing senior most member of teaching staff in accordance with guidelines laid down in Schedule 'F' from amongst teachers employed in school. However, SC held that HC has erred in law in holding that mode of appointment by promotion u/r. 3(3) of Rules cannot be applied to polytechnic although it is school because there is no separate qualification prescribed for polytechnic in r. 3(1) of Rules. Further it is nobody's case that appellant does not fulfill educational qualification for post of Principal of polytechnic even as prescribed by authority Hence, mode of appointment prescribed under statutory r. 3(3) of Rules could not have been ignored and since appellant was admittedly senior most member of teaching staff in polytechnic at relevant time, and he was declared entitled to all benefits of In-charge Principal of polytechnic since 9-7-2007, appellant could not have been denied appointment by promotion to vacant post of Principal. Hence, SC held that respondent Director erred in rejecting appellant's claim and in not issuing directions for his appointment by promotion. Further, with respect to age of superannuation, SC held that if correct view had been taken by respondent Director then by end of 2012, appellant would have been occupying post of Principal in respondent polytechnic and then he would not have superannuated before 65 years or in any case before 62 years of age. In interest of justice, SC deemed it proper to direct management for immediate reinstatement of appellant. Further, appellant shall also be entitled to all consequential benefits on basis of such promotion. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Promotion of candidate cannot be withheld when appointment is in accordance with law and conforms with rules framed.
