C. R. Bansi v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, Vishishtha Bhargava, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 198; (1970) 3 SCC 537; (1971) SCC (Cr) 143; (1973) SCC (Cr) 496; AIR 1971 SC 786; 1971 CRLJ 662; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 236

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Indirect Tax; Service; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 5(1), 5(2), 5(3) and 6 - Civil services(Classification, Control, Appeal) Rules - Appellant, an ITO, allegedly accepted illegal gratification and found in possession of disproportionate assets and convicted by special judge - Whether prior sanction needed to prosecute appellant? - Held, policy underlying s. 6, and similar sections, is that there should not be unnecessary harassment of public servants - But if a person ceases to be a public servant question of harassment does not arise - Fact that an appeal is pending does not make him a public servant - Appellant ceased to be a public servant when order of dismissal was passed - Further held that, appellant had pecuniary resources and property disproportionate to his known sources of income, and that both High Court and learned special judge rightly held that presumption arose u/s. 5(3) - Appeal allowed.
