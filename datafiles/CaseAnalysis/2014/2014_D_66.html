FOR EDUCATIONAL USE ONLY

Hitendra Singh S/o Bhupendra Singh and others v Dr. P. D. Krishi Vidyapeeth by Reg. and others

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

T. S. Thakur, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 220; (2014) 8 SCC 369; AIR 2014 SC 1635; 2014(4) ALL MR 476; 2015 (1) MahLJ 248; 2014(4) SCALE 673; [2014] 4 S.C.R. 717; 2014 (6) SLR 718

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Termination, Powers of Chancellor, Inquiry proceedings, In accordance with principles of natural justice

Summary: (A)Service - Appointment - Termination - Powers of Chancellor - Respondents invited applications for appointment against 24 vacancies in the cadre of Senior Research Assistants and 37 vacancies in the cadre of Junior Research Assistants - Several complaints appeared to have been made against selection process and resultant appointments made by University - Directions issued by Chancellor, disciplinary action appeared to have been initiated against those comprising Selection Committee in which officials were accused of having made illegal selection of 131 candidates including petitioners thereby not only causing financial loss to University but also bringing disrepute to it - Chancellor held that entire process of selection and appointment had lost its sanctity on account of irregularities in the same could not be approved or rectified - Chancellor accordingly cancelled appointments of 83 candidates of SRAs and JRAs who had been selected and taken into service of University on basis of a process which Chancellor found was vitiated and void ab initio - Services of appointees concerned were terminated - Aggrieved petition were filed by petitioner before HC - Petitions were dismissed by HC - Hence instant appeals - Whether Chancellor was competent to appoint Single Member Committee headed by Justice H.W. Dhabe to examine illegalities, irregularities, fairness and impropriety of selection process and consequent appointments to cadre of SRAs and JRAs -

Held, Chancellor was vested with power to cause inspection to be made by such person or persons as he might direct of any University, its building, farms, laboratories, libraries etc or of hostels administered and recognised by University or of teaching or other workshops conducted on behalf of University or any conduct of examinations or other functions of University - Inspection so directed was, however, distinct from inquiry which Chancellor might direct regarding any matter connected with administration or finance of University - Therefore, no hesitation was held that inquiry directed by Chancellor into illegalities and irregularities of selection process culminating in appointment of Senior and Junior Research Assistants was legally permissible - Chancellor exercises ample powers in regard to affairs of University and in particular in regard to affairs of administration of University - Justice Dhabe Committee was constituted by Chancellor for holding detailed inquiry into allegations - Appeals dismissed.

(B) Service - Appointment - Termination - Inquiry proceedings - In accordance with principles of natural justice - Whether inquiry proceedings entrusted to Justice Dhabe Committee conducted in accordance with principles of natural justice -

Held, inquiry proceedings before Justice Dhabe continued for nearly 3 years during which period petitioners made no grievance either before Committee or before any other forum regarding non-compliance with principles of natural justice - There was nothing on record to suggest that any point relevant to controversy was not considered by Justice Dhabe Committee or that there was any impediment in their offering effective defence before Committee - There was neither allegation nor any material to suggest that there was any reluctance or refusal on part of Committee to entertain any material which petitioner intended to place in their defence or to summon any record from any other quarter relevant to questions being examined by Committee - Appeals dismissed.

(C) Service - Appointment - Termination - Legality - Whether findings recorded by Justice Dhabe Committee in any manner illegal or perverse to warrant interference with same by Court -

Held, upon receipt of report from Justice Dhabe Committee matter was directed to be placed before Executive Council of University, that direction was meant to give Executive Council opportunity to examine findings of fact and conclusions drawn from same critically and to determine whether same were justified - Executive Council, it was common ground, had without any reservation approved findings recorded by Dhabe Committee, no matter with recommendation to Chancellor to take lenient view in matter, had regard to fact that petitioners had already served University for nearly 6 years - Recommendation of Executive Council did not, however, find anything amiss with conclusions drawn by Dhabe Committee as to the irregularities in the selection process culminating in illegal appointments of selected candidates - Vice Chancellor no doubt made attempt at segregating what according to him was valid part of selection from that which was not, but Chancellor did not approve of that exercise - Chancellor took view that entire selection stood vitiated by widespread irregularities, leaving hardly any room for distinction between the so called valid and invalid parts of selection process - Fact remained that there was no reason to interfere with findings recorded by Justice Dhabe Committee and sanctity of entire selection process was vitiated by irregularities and acts of nepotism - Appeals dismissed.

(D) Service - Appointment - Termination - Justifiability - Whether procedure adopted by University and Vice Chancellor fair and reasonable and in consonance with principles of natural justice -

Held, HC rightly rejected and correctly concluded that petitioners had failed to establish that Vice Chancellor had either violated principles of natural justice or that any prejudice was caused by procedure adopted by him in offering them hearing - As matter of fact Vice- Chancellor had in his anxiety to help petitioners tried to sit in judgment over findings and conclusions of inquiry Committee and taken stance that was overtly sympathetic towards petitioners - Uncharitable expression used by petitioners as to nature of process undertaken by the Vice Chancellor was not, therefore, justified - Vice Chancellor had acted fairly and fully complied with principle of natural justice - There was no gainsaid that requirements of audi alteram partem were not capable of strait jacket application - Application depended so much upon nature of Tribunal that was deciding the matter, nature of inquiry that was being made and consequences flowed from determination - Notice to petitioners who were likely to be affected and hearing afforded to them apart from written responses filed in reply to notices was substantial compliance with principles of natural justice - No further hearing was required to be repeated by Chancellor who had before whom recommendations of Executive Committee and Vice Chancellor who took final view of matter in regard to totality of circumstances - Appeal dismissed.

(E) Service - Appointment - Termination - Justifiability - Whether Chancellor and HC was justified in declining prayer of petitioners for continuance in service on account of time lag between date of their appointments and date on which their services were terminated -

Held, length of service put in by candidates who were selected on basis of such faulty selection process might be one of considerations that entered the mind of Court but there were other weighty considerations that should not be given go bye or conveniently forgotten lest those who did not adopt such malpractices or those who expect the system to protect their interest and their rights are eternally disappointed and left to believe that wrong once done would never be corrected just because legal process by which it was to be corrected was long and winding process that often took years to reach fruition - Contention of petitioners in support of their continuance in service was that they have become overage for any government employment at this stage - If ousted from service petitioners would have no place to go nor even opportunity to compete for vacancies against which they were appointed, that was aspect which could be and ought to be considered especially when there was no allegation leave alone evidence about any bribery having taken place in issue of appointment orders by officials concerned - Even so, continuance of petitioners in service would not, be justified in regard to background in which selection and appointments were made and eventually set aside by University - All that long years of service rendered with University might secure for petitioners, direction to effect that in any future selection against vacancies caused by their ouster and other vacancies that might be available for next selection petitioners should also be considered in relaxation of upper age limit prescribed for them - Such of petitioners who could try their luck in next selection and who succeed in same would also have benefit of continuity of service - Appeals dismissed.
