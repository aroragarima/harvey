Action Committee South Eastern Railway Pensioners and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 September 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

N.M. Kasliwal, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 235; (1991) Supp2 SCC 544; (1992) SCC (L&S) 222; 1992 (19) ATC 258; 1991 (62) FLR 219; JT 1991 (5) SC 8; 1991 (1) LLN 311; 1990(2) SCALE 456; 1990 (3) SLJ 147; 1991 (1) SLR 771; 1991 (1) UJ 114; 1990 (3) UPLBEC 1509

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service

- Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Manual of Railway Pension Rules 1969 - Why railway servants who retired before March 31, 1985 were not given pension benefits as given to those who retired on or after March 31, 1985? - Held, petitioners admittedly exercised their option for pension and other retirement benefits computed after taking into account dearness allowance as dearness pay - It is also admitted position that to mitigate loss graded relief has been granted to pensioners having opted in favour of new scheme - Petitioners unable to show any material difference in actual pension drawn by petitioners with those who retired after March 31, 1985 and only submitted that if formula adopted in case of employees having retired after March 31, 1985 vide circular dated May 17, 1985 is applied in case of petitioners then it would make substantial difference in calculation of amount of gratuity and commuted value of pension - No such claim can be allowed on any principle of equality enshrined u/art. 14 of Constitution in as much as petitioners form a different class from those who were continuing in service on or after March 31, 1985 - Petition dismissed.
