Panchanand Mandal @ Pachan Mandal and another v State of Jharkhand

Supreme Court of India

04 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 656; (2013) 9 SCC 800; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 240; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 4084; 2013 (4) JCC 2515; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 591; 2013(12) SCALE 356; [2013] 11 S.C.R. 328

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B, 34 - Dowry death - Conviction - Sustainability - Deceased was married to appellants-accused - Accused (husband and in-laws) inflicted mental and physical cruelty and harassed deceased with beatings for dowry - Accused killed deceased by burning her - Accused were charged u/ss. 306 (B), 34 IPC - Trial Court convicted and sentenced all 4 accused u/ss. 306(B), 34 IPC - Accused-husband was acquitted of charges on ground that he left village prior to occurrence which means that he was not present at the scene of occurrence - Appeal before HC - Appeal was dismissed and affirmed the order of conviction and sentence passed by Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether conviction and sentence order recorded by Court was sustainable - Held, ominous allegations have been made against in-laws of deceased - No specific incident had been stated by PW13- mother of deceased or PW14-brother of deceased in their statements - Nothing was on record to suggest that deceased was subjected to cruelty and harassment soon before her death and in connection with demand of dowry - There was no evidence to prove that there was any cruelty or harassment for or in connection with demand of dowry soon before death of deceased - However, deceased had not made any statement in her dying declaration indicating demand of dowry - Defence had successfully created valid doubt as to authenticity of dying declaration as police officer who recorded same was not examined - Such deficiency in evidence proved fatal for prosecution case as evidence of cruelty and harassment in general was not sufficient to attract s. 304B IPC - Hence, prosecution miserably failed to prove case beyond reasonable doubt - Conviction and sentence awarded should not be maintained - Accordingly, impugned judgment passed was set aside - Appeal is allowed.
