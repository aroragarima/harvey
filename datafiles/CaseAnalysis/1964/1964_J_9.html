Juggankhan v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

10 August 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 87; AIR 1965 SC 831; 1965 CRLJ 763; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 14

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, ss. 302 and 304A - An appeal against judgment High Court confirming the conviction and sentence of the appellant u/s. 302, IPC, 1860 - Appellant is a registered Homoeopathic medical practitioner u/ Madhya Pradesh Homoeopathic and Bio-chemic Practitioners Act - he started residing and practising at Akodiya Mandi - Issued a pamphlet advertising he treated Naru (guinea worm) - Lured by this, Smt. Deobi, aged about 20 years, suffering from guinea worm for six weeks went to the clinic of the appellant - She was examined by appellant and administered 24 drops of mother tincture stramonium and a leaf of dhatura - After taking medicine she started feeling restless and ill various antidote were given - She vomited twice - At about 5 p.m. same day she died - Whether he be convicted u/s. 302 or s. 304A, I.P.C, 1860? - Held, the courts below were right in concluding that death resulted from poisoning - Dr. Patodia, medical examiner could not say what poison caused her death - But he could say that death was due to something that was an irritant, and it could be due to dhatura or belladonna or any other poison -Deceased, was a healthy woman, and had not taken any other medicine before arriving at the clinic - She was at the clinic from 9 a.m. till she died - The only medicine she took, apart from antidotes, was what was administered, i.e., 24 drops of stramonium and a dhatura leaf - She started feeling restless and ill soon after taking these things - On these facts the conclusion of the courts below that death was the result of dhatura poison cannot be said to be erroneous - Care should be taken before imputing criminal negligence to a professional man acting in the course of his, profession, but even taking this care we have no doubt that the appellant was guilty of a rash and negligent act - He is guilty u/s. 304A, IPC, 1860 - Appellant's conviction u/s. 302, IPC, 1860 is set aside and he is convicted u/s. 304A and sentenced to 2 years' rigorous imprisonment - Conviction altered.
