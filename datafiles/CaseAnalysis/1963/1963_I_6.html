Commissioner of Income Tax, Punjab v Indian Woollen Textile Mills

Supreme Court of India

18 November 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 203; AIR 1964 SC 735; [1964] 51 ITR 291; 1964 (1) SCJ 253; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 427

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 , ss. 15C, 66(1), 66(2) - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Computation of capital - Employment - Income - Assessment - Assessee had at different places, branches one of which was an industrial undertaking conducted in name of E called for sake of brevity 'E' - E had advanced Rs. 3,21,460 to another concern, B Castle Mills/Castle - In assessment year 1951-52, assessee claimed u/s.15C of 1922Act exemption from tax in respect of 6% of capital employed as a newly established undertaking and sought to include in computation of capital - Income-tax Officer and Commissioner rejected claim - Tribunal modified assessment and directed inclusion of amount advanced to 'Castle' in computation of capital invested for purpose of s. 15C - An application submitted u/s. 66(1) of 1922 Act to Tribunal to refer a question which it was contended by Commissioner arose out of order of Tribunal was rejected and petition of Commissioner u/s. 66(2) for an order directing Tribunal to state case and refer it to HC was dismissed - With special leave Commissioner appealed to Court - Whether 'Castle' was a branch of assessee - Held, Non-inclusion of income of 'Castle' in assessment of assessee might had been a relevant circumstance, but its effect had to be considered in light of other circumstances on which Commissioner had relied - Tribunal suffered from a double infirmity, it assumed only fact on which its conclusion was founded and ignored other relevant matters on which Commissioner relied in support of his conclusion - Tribunal had misdirected itself in law in arriving at its finding, and in refusing to require Tribunal to state case and to refer it, HC was in error - Order accordingly.
