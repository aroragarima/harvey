Union of India and others v Angad Singh Titaria

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

N.V. Ramana, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 136; AIR 2015 SC 1898; 2015 (145) FLR 413; JT 2015 (5) SC 478; 2015(2) SCALE 640; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 524; 2015 (5) SLR 403

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Disability Pension, Casualty Pensionary Awards Entitlement Rules, 1982, Armed Forces Medical Services Regulations, 1983, Indian Air Force Pension Regulations, 1961, Invalidation from service

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Casualty Pensionary Awards Entitlement Rules, 1982, rr. 4, 5(a), 5(b) - Disability pension - Invalidation from service - In pursuance of recommendations of Medical Board that disabilities of respondent were constitutional in nature and not attributable to nor aggravated by service in Air Force, disability pension claim preferred by respondent was rejected by competent Pension Sanctioning Authority i.e. Air Force Record Office by its order dt.16-4-2009 - Aggrieved, respondent appealed before Appellate Committee but the same was rejected and second appeal was also rejected - Respondent filed application before Tribunal which was allowed by impugned judgment directing appellants to assess and release disability element of disability pension in favour of petitioner for 60% disability from the date of his discharge with interest on arrears - Hence, instant appeal by UOI - Whether disabilities caused to respondent during course of his employment are attributable to his service entitling him to benefit of disability pension.

Held, respondent was rendered ineligible for further promotion and thereby invalidated on the ground of his being in medical category A4 G4(permanent). Further, in the absence of any specific note on record as to the respondent suffering from any disease prior to his joining the service, he is presumed to have been in sound physical and medical condition while entering service as per r. 5(a) of the Rules. Hence, as per r. 5(b) of the Rules, respondent was denied promotion on medical grounds, deterioration in his health is presumed to have caused due to service. Hence, Tribunal had not committed any error in awarding disability pension to respondent. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - As per r. 4 of the Rules, invalidating from service is a necessary condition for grant of disability pension. Further, an individual who, at the time of his release under Release Regulations, is in lower medical category than that in which he was recruited will be treated as 'invalidated from service'.
