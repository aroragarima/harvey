Philips India Ltd. v Collector of Central Excise, Pune

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2403; (1997) 6 SCC 31; 1998 (74) ECR 722; 1997 (91) E.L.T. 540; JT 1997 (10) SC 707; 1997 (19) RLT 471

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Trade Discount

Summary: Excise - Central Excise and Salt Act, 1944, s. 4 - Whether tribunal erred in upholding finding of Collector that appellant was not eligible 'for deduction on account of that portion of normal trade discount as described by them, to extent of 2% of discount attributable to after-sales service and advertisement incurred by dealers on behalf of assessee'? - Advertisement which dealer was required to make at its own cost benefited in equal degree appellant and dealer and that for this reason cost of such advertisement was borne half and half by appellant and dealer - Making a deduction out of trade discount on this account was, therefore, uncalled for - Provision as to after-sales service benefited not only appellant, it was a provision of mutual benefit to appellant and dealer - Judgment and order under appeal set aside - Appeal allowed.
