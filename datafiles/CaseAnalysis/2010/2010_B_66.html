Kedar Shashikant Deshpande Etc v Bhor Municipal Council And Others

Supreme Court of India

10 December 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1067; (2011) 2 SCC 654; 2011 (1) Bom.C.R. 531; JT 2010 (13) SC 485; 2010(13) SCALE 289

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Maharashtra Local Authority Members Disqualification Rules, 1987, Bihar Legislative Council Members (Disqualification on ground of Defection) Rules, 1994, Maharashtra Zilla Parishads and Panchayat Samitis Act 1951

Summary: Election - Municipalities & Local Governments - Maharashtra Local Authority Members Disqualification Act, 1986, s. 3 - Maharashtra Local Authority Members Disqualification Rules, 1987, rr. 3, 4, 5 and 6 - Disqualification - General elections for the Bhor Municipal Council were held - Out of the elected councillors, 8 were NCP, 8 of Congress and 1 independent candidate - Subsequently 6 of the NCP candidates left NCP and formed Bhor Shahar Vikas Swabhimani Sanghathana (Sanghathana) - 6 councillors of the Sanghathana and 8 councillors of Congress (I) submitted requisition to the Collector for 'No Confidence Motion' against President - Meeting was held in which 'No Confidence Motion' with 14:3 votes was passed for removal of President and respondent No. 4 - Respondent Nos. 4 and 5 filed Disqualification Petition against 6 councillors who had formed the Sanghathana, for a declaration that they had defected from NCP and had incurred disqualification u/s. 3(1)(a) of Act - Respondent Nos. 4 and 5 submitted that the petitioners had incurred disqualification u/s. 3(1)(a) of the Act as they had voluntarily given up membership of NCP - Additional Collector disqualified the appellants as councillors of the Sanghathana - Writ petitions filed thereagainst were dismissed - Hence, present appeals - (A) Non-compliance of rr. 6(4) and 6(3) of the Rules - Effect thereof - Held, these provisions are directory in nature and defect in verification of the petition is curable - Irregularity regarding verification of the petition and annexures thereto would never affect the merits of the case - Hence, non-compliance with R. 6(4) and 6(3) of the Rules at the initial stage did not vitiate the disqualification petition nor affected the jurisdiction of the Additional Collector to decide the same - (B) Whether appellants have incurred disqualification within the meaning of provisions of the Act r/w Rules? - Held, s. 3(1)(a) of the Act, without any qualification or rider, provides that a councillor or a member belonging to any political party or aghadi or front shall be disqualified, if he has voluntarily given up his membership of such political party or aghadi or front - The provisions are absolute in terms and are mandatory - In the present case, appellants themselves admitted that they had left NCP party - Therefore, legal effect of proved and admitted facts was that the appellants had incurred disqualification in terms of s. 3(1)(a) of the Act and, therefore, they were not entitled to any of the reliefs - (C) Whether appellants incurred disqualification u/s. 3(1)(b) of the Act as they had failed to obey the whip issued to them by NCP and had voted contrary to the direction issued by NCP? - Held, councillor or a member belonging to any political party or aghadi or front shall be disqualified for being a councillor or a member if he votes or abstains from voting in any contrary to any direction issued by the political party or aghadi or front to which he belongs - In the present case, despite the whip, the appellants had signed the requisition requesting the Collector to call a meeting for consideration of no confidence motion against the President and also voted in favour of the said motion - Therefore, in the facts and in the circumstances of the case, appellants had also incurred disqualification in terms of s. 3(1)(b) of the Act - (D) It was submitted that there was non-application of mind on the part of the Additional Collector in passing the impugned order of disqualification - Held, merely quoting wrong provisions of the statute while exercising power would not invalidate the order passed by the authority if it is shown that such order could be passed under other provisions of the statute - Reference made by the Collector to s. 3(1)(c) instead of s. 3(1)(a) of the Act will have to be regarded as mistake on his part because of difference in Vernacular and English version of the Act - Appellants had incurred disqualification u/s. 3(1)(a) of the Act as pleaded by the respondent Nos.4 and 5 and not u/s. 3(1)(c) of the Act as mentioned by the Collector - Appeals dismissed.
