Bahrein Petroleum Company Limited v P. J. Pappu and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, J.R. Madholkar, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 407; AIR 1966 SC 634; 1966 (13) FLR 136; 1966 KerLJ 41; 1965 KLT 1193; 1966 (2) LLJ 144; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 461

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 20, 21 and 99 - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 2, 34 and 39 - Plaintiff instituted a suit for recovery of gratuity and arrears from defendant - Defendants applied to Cochin court for stay of suit, which was refused, and dismissed in appeal in District Court - Defendant disputed jurisdiction of court while filing written statement - Whether defendants are precluded by s. 21 of Code from raising objection after having neither alleged nor proved that there has been a failure of justice in consequence of order of High Court? - Held, when a case had been tried by a court on merits and judgment rendered, it should not be liable to be reversed purely on technical grounds, unless it had resulted in failure of justice, and policy of legislature has been to treat objections to jurisdiction both territorial and pecuniary as technical and not open to consideration by an appellate court, unless there has been a prejudice on merits - Appeal allowed.
