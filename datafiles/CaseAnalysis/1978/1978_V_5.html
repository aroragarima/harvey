Ramesh Chander Kaushal v Veena Kaushal and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 April 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 310; (1978) 4 SCC 70; (1978) SCC (Cr) 508; AIR 1978 SC 1807; 1979 CRLJ 3; 1978 CrLR(SC) 348; 1979 (15) DLT 155; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 782

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Criminal - Constitution - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss.125, 125(1), 488 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 15(3), 39, 137 - Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956 - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, 24 - Divorce - Maintenance - Illegitimate - Social Justice - Respondent/husband sought divorce and petitioner/wife claimed maintenance - District Court awarded maintenance and HC fixed rate per mensem for spouse as provisional figure - Lower court ordered ex-parte, with monthly maintenance of Rs. 1000/- for the mother and two children - Respondent submitted that s. 125 of Code clearly fixed ceiling of monthly allowance "for maintenance of wife or such child, father or mother, at such monthly rate not exceeding five hundred rupees in whole" - Hence instant petition - Whether maintenance could be awarded together to âwholeâ family members/children of Respondent - Court could not, agree obvious jurisdictional inequity by reading limitation, that all members of family who claimed maintenance could be awarded a maximum of Rs. 500/- and could not grant more than Rs. 500/- for each one of claimants which accords with social justice and semantics - Once divorce was decreed respondent/wife cease to had any right to claim maintenance u/s. 127 of Code was awarded and Court regret this error and pass order u/art. 137 of Constitution with consent of both sides - Petition dismissed.
