Atlas Cycle Industries Limited v State of Haryana and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 August 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, S.M. Sikri, D.G. Palekar

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 262; (1971) 2 SCC 564; AIR 1972 SC 121; [1972] 85 ITR 121; [1972] 1 S.C.R. 127

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Levy Of Octroi

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Indirect Tax - Punjab Municipal Act, 1911, ss. 5(4), 62(10) - Notification - Levy of octroi - Sustainability - Appellants carry on business of manufacturing bicycles and bicycle components and parts - Appellants filed instant appeals against common judgment passed by HC dismissing the applications of appellants for a writ of mandamus restraining respondent (Municipality) from levying against and collecting from appellants any octroi in respect of raw materials, components and parts imported by appellants into their factories - Respondent contended that notification issued u/s. 62(10) of the Act notifying imposition of octroi within octroi limits of Municipality became applicable to area included and that u/s. 5(4) of the Act imposition of octroi which was in force within municipality applied to area included within the limits of municipality by notifications - Whether notification imposing octroi within limits of Municipality became applicable by reason of provisions contained in s. 5(4) of the Act - Held, s. 5(4) of the Act speaks of rules, bye-laws, orders, directions and powers and did not significantly mention 'notifications' - Word 'notification' could not be said to be synonymous with rules, bye-laws, orders, directions and powers - Act speaks of notifications for imposition of tax and uses the word 'notification' separately from other words 'rules, bye-laws, orders, directions and powers' - In case of exclusions of areas, the Act speaks of notification ceasing to apply to excluded areas whereas in case of inclusion of areas the Act significantly omits any notification being applicable to such area - In instant appeals, HC came to conclusion that by reason of provisions contained in s. 5(4) of the Act taxes would automatically become 'leviable' to new areas added to municipal limits - HC erred in holding that taxes became automatically leviable in new areas, which conclusion was not supported either by decision of SC or by provisions of the statute - Taxing provision always receive a strict interpretation for the obvious reason that there should be clear and express language imposing a tax and date from which such tax should come into effect - Notifications, under the Act were the only authority and mandate for imposition and charge of tax - Notifications were not made applicable to included areas u/s. 5(4) of the Act - There could not be any taxation by implication - Municipality was not competent to levy and collect octroi from appellants by reason of provisions contained in s. 5(4) of the Act - Impugned judgment of HC was set aside - Appeals allowed.
