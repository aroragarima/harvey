Sabitri Dei And Others v Sarat Chandra Rout And Others

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3456; (1996) 3 SCC 301; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 73; 1996 (1) CCC 218; 1996 (1) CLT 428; JT 1996 (2) SC 1; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 1168; 1996 (2) Supreme 157; 1996 (1) UJ 586

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Orissa Estates Abolition (Second Amendment), Act, 1957, ss. 3(1), 6, 8A, 39 and 47 - (A) Whether High Court was right in directing execution of decree in question? - Held, estate in question having been vested by virtue of notification u/s. 3(1) of Act and no claim having been made by intermediary for getting deemed settlement of fixation of rent, by operation of s. 8 A(3), right of Intermediary stand extinguished and therefore under such circumstances Civil Court had no jurisdiction of pass decree in year 1965 - Decree in question is nullity and executing court can refuse to execute decree - HC was in obvious error in directing execution of decree - (B) Whether a decree holder having lost his right of proprietorship in property, can be refused to execute decree by Executing Court? - Held, Executing Court rightly refused to execute decree and HC was in error in setting aside said order - Proprietary rights of plaintiff intermediary became vested in the State of Orissa - Plaintiff could not execute decree for recovery of possession - Appeal allowed.
