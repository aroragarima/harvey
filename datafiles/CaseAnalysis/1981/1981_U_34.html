S.S. Moghe and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Balakrishna Eradi, Y.V. Chandrachud, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 250; (1981) 3 SCC 271; (1981) SCC (L&S) 490; AIR 1981 SC 1495; 1981 (43) FLR 297; 1981 LabIC 861; 1981 (2) LLJ 198; 1981 (2) LLN 455; 1981(1) SCALE 891; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 875; 1981 (2) SLJ 6; 1981 (2) SLR 291

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Service - Aviation Research Centre (Technical) Services Rules, 1976, rr. 6 to 8 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 309 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Whether rr. 6 to 8 of 'Aviation Research Centre (Technical) Service Rules, 1976' issued by President of India under proviso to art. 309 of Constitution, 1950 are unconstitutional? - Held, r. 6 (6) is not a provision applicable only to the erstwhile deputationists - On the other hand, the sub-rule itself makes it very clear that its provisions apply equally to all the persons who are eligible for absorption in the service u/sub-r. (1) irrespective of whether they are deputationists or direct recruits - Sub-r. (6) comes into operation when a person in the eligible category holding a post in a higher grade on the appointed day, who has been found suitable for permanent appointment in such higher grade cannot, however, be absorbed in the said grade on account of non-availability of a vacancy therein - What the sub-rule lays down is that in such eventuality the Screening Committee may recommend such a person for permanent appointment in a lower grade and thereby retain his services in the new Department - Rules are valid - Petition dismissed.
