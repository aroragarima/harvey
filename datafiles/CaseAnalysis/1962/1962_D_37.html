Vijay Pratap Singh v Dukh Haran Nath Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 January 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 125; AIR 1962 SC 941; 1962 ALJ 634; 1962 ALJ 6311; 1962 (2) ILR(All) 831; 1964 (1) KerLR 226; 1964 (1) MLJ(SC) 79; 1964 (1) SCJ 266; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 675

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Maharaja Sir Man Singh holder of Ajodhya Raj was a Taluqdar, who died in 1870 and Raj devolved upon his daughter's son Maharaja Pratap Narain Singh, who died leaving him surviving two widows Suraj Kumari and Jagdamba Devi and no lineal descendant - A will alleged to be executed by Maharaja Pratap Narain Singh was set up but it was void and ineffective - On death of Maharaja Pratap Narain Singh Raj devolved upon Maharani Suraj Kumari senior widow and on her death in 1927 upon Maharani Jugdamba Devi, and on death of latter on D/- 18-06-1928 upon Ganga Dutt Misir, grand father of plaintiff Ganga Dutt Misir died in 1942 and estate devolved upon his son Ramjiwan and his grandson, plaintiff as co-parceners in a Hindu joint family - Defendant No. 1 Dukh Haran Singh Claimed to be adopted as a son by Jagdamba Devi - Initially Ram Jiwan Misir supported will and plea of adoption set up by first defendant, but by an application D/- 21-04-1951, prayed that he be transposed as a petitioner - Holding that it did not disclose a cause of action Subordinate Judge rejected petition of plaintiff - High Court rejected petition of plaintiff holding that on death of Ganga Dutt in 1942 estate would devolve upon Ram Jiwan Misir alone according to rule of impartibility which governed devolution of estate - It also observed that there was nothing in petition to show that Ganga Dutt succeeded to estate 'on basis of his being nearest male reversioner under Ordinary Hindu Law ' - Whether HC erred in its judgment? - Held, it is unable to agree with view of HC that petition filed by plaintiff did not disclose a cause of action, or that O. 1, r. 10 of CPC cannot properly be resorted to for transposing a party in a petition for leave to sue in forma pauperis - Plaintiff had by his plaint set up an alternative case - In first instance he pleaded that will alleged to be executed by Maharaja Pratap Narain was 'void and ineffective' and estate devolved upon Ram Jiwan and plaintiff as members of a coparcenary alternatively, he pleaded that even if will was valid, by terms thereof and by other acts and declaration of Maharaja Pratap Narain Singh, estate was taken out 'of purview of Act I of 1869' and on death of Maharani Jagdamba Devi property devolved upon Ganga Dutt, nearest reversioner under Hindu law and on his death it devolved upon plaintiff and upon his father Ram Jiwan Misir - Subordinate Judge rejected application only on ground that it did not show a cause of action, and HC confirmed order also on that ground - But in ascertaining whether petition shows a cause of action court does not enter upon a trial of issues affecting merits of claim made by petitioner - It cannot take into consideration defences which defendant may raise upon merits nor is court competent to make an elaborate enquiry into doubtful or complicated questions of law or fact - HC, was in error in observing that there was nothing in plaint to show that Ganga Dutt succeeded to estate because he was nearest male reversioner under ordinary Hindu law - HC was also in error in holding that by an application to sue in forma pauperis, applicant prays for relief personal to himself - An application to sue in forma pauperis, is but a method prescribed by Code for institution of a suit by a pauper without payment of fee prescribed by Court Fees Act - Appeals allowed.
