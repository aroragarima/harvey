State of Jammu and Kashmir v MOHD. YAQOOB KHAN and OTHERS

Supreme Court of India

26 August 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, N. Venkatachala, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1260; (1992) 4 SCC 167; JT 1992 (5) SC 278; 1992(2) SCALE 424; [1992] Supp1 S.C.R. 43; 1992 (2) UJ 720; 1992 (2) UPLBEC 1166

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 47 - Contempt Of Courts Act, 1971 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Stay matter was fixed to be heard and there was no time indicated for compliance of direction - Petitioner, without waiting for next date of case, filed application on which contempt order was passed - Whether stay matter in writ petition was not finally disposed of can there be further proceeding and order in contempt case? - Scope of a contempt proceeding is very different from that of pending main case yet to be heard and disposed of (in future) - Respondents in a pending case are at a disadvantage if they are called upon to meet merits of claim in a contempt proceeding at risk of being punished - Therefore, not right to suggest that it should be assumed that initial order of stay got confirmed by subsequent orders passed in contempt matter - High Court should have first taken up stay matter without any threat to respondents in writ case of being punished for contempt - Order in nature of mandatory direction could not have been justified unless Court was in a position to consider objections and record a finding, prima facie in nature, in favour of writ petitioner - Besides challenging claim on merits, respondent was entitled to raise a plea of non-maintainability of a writ application filed for purpose of executing a decree - Held, direction of HC although passed as an interim order in a pending interlocutory matter was, in substance, a final order allowing writ petition in part without hearing other side - Orders passed in contempt proceeding were not justified, being premature, and must, therefore, be entirely ignored - Appeal allowed.
