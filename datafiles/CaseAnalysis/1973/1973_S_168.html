Ranbir and Others v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 114; (1973) 2 SCC 444; (1973) SCC (Cr) 858; AIR 1973 SC 1409; 1973 CRLJ 1120; 1973 CrLR(SC) 488; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 102

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 161 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148 and 325/149 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Challenge of conviction u/ss 148, 325/149, IPC, 1860 by 5 appellants - Appreciation of Evidence - Delay in examination of witnesses by police u/s 161 CrPC, 1898 - (A) Whether evidence can be termed trustworthy? - Held, maxim 'falsus in uno falsus in omnibus' not a sound rule to be applied in India - Duty of court to scrutinise rest of evidence with care and caution if a witness is found to have given unreliable evidence - Duty of Court to scrutinise rest of evidence with care and caution if remaining evidence is trustworthy and substratum of prosecution case remains intact - (B) Whether delay vitiates evidence? - Further held, evidence of a witness does not become untrustworthy merely because he was examined after delay by Investigating Officer -(C) Reappraisal of evidence by Supreme Court - Further held, normally Supreme Court would not appraise evidence unless some serious defect resulting in failure of justice or grave injustice is shown - Appeal dismissed.
