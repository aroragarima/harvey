Central Bank of India v Virudhunagar Steel Rolling Mills Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

29 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 910; AIR 2016 SC 191; 2016 (2) ALD(SC) 95; 2016 (115) ALR 509; 2016 (1) ALT(SC) 56; 2016 (1) BC 508; 2016 (1) DRTC 461; JT 2015 (12) SC 598; 2016 (1) MLJ 342; 2016 (2) RCR(Civil) 178; 2016(1) SCALE 29; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 64

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Guarantor

Summary: Banking & Finance - Execution of guarantee Deed - Liability of party - Determination - Respondents except respondent no.1 which was company received various credit facilities from Appellant Bank, of total amount of 12 lacs against security of moveable and raw materials - Further, these facilities were subsequently secured in favour of appellant Bank by means of continuing guarantee by Directors of Respondent Company - Further, appellant filed suit for recovery of amount - Further, Trial Court passed order against respondent Company, but dismissed it as against respondent Nos. 2 to 4 - Further, HC held that there was no cogent evidence to establish that claims raised in suit pertained to advance or credits made subsequent to 30-8-1974, date on which respondent nos. 2 to 4 had executed documents relied upon by Appellant Bank - Hence, present appeal.

Held, letter of Guarantee signed on 30-8-1974 by respondent Nos. 2 to 4 makes no mention of any old transactions, although it specifically records that liability of guarantors cannot exceed 12 lacs. SC held that Letters of Guarantee could easily have recorded liabilities outstanding against Respondent Company on 30-8-1974 with affirmation from respondent Nos. 2 to 4 that they were guaranteeing these outstandings. Further, HC noted that statement of PW1, Accountant of Appellant Bank, who has deposed to effect that Deed of Guarantee made no mention of any prior transactions. Further, DW1 stated that that Guarantee arrangements made no mention that they were effective in respect of prior debts. Hence, SC held that Guarantee Deeds executed by respondent nos. 2 to 4 on 30-8-1974 rendered them personally liable for any transactions or advances made by appellant Bank to respondent Company after 30-8-1974. SC held that Bank account lay dormant after this date, all dealings having been transacted much prior thereto. Hence, it is not open to Appellant Bank to pursue respondent nos. 2 to 4 for recovery of debts incurred by Respondent Company in favour of Appellant Bank. Order passed by lower court is justified and in accordance with law. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Party cannot be held liable for prior debts when guarantee arrangements made no mention that they were effective in respect of prior debts.
