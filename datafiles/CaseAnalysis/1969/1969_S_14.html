Samant N. Balkrishna and Another v George Fernandez and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 558; (1969) 3 SCC 238; AIR 1969 SC 1201; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 603

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Elections - Corrupt practice - Representation of Peoples Act, 1951, ss. 62, 123(4), 123(3 ), 100, 83, 83(5), 85, 100(1) and 101 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 326 and 327 - Whether respondents had resorted to a corrupt practice u/s. 123(4) of Representation of Peoples Act, 1950, during elections as alleged by appellants? - If conditions required by s. 100(1)(d) r/w s. 123(4) are satisfied, a corrupt practice avoiding election will be established - First condition is that candidate's belief in falsity of statements must be established - Second condition is that result of election in so far as returned candidate is concerned must be shown to be materially affected - Held, petitioner has failed to establish in manner laid down by Supreme Court, that result of election was materially affected in so far respondent was concerned - If petitioner had pleaded corrupt practices against appellant personally result might have been different - No evidence was led and they were not even referred to at hearing - Election petition was ill-considered and left out most vital charges - Appeals dismissed.
