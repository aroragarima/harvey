Director General, Indian Council of Medical Research and Others v Dr. Anil Kumar Ghosh and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 August 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Srinivasan, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 178; (1998) 7 SCC 97; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1795; AIR 1998 SC 2592; 1998 (2) CLR 76; JT 1998 (5) SC 317; 1999 (1) LLJ 1036; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 1034; 1999 (1) SLJ 288

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 and 311 - (A) Misconduct - Employee drawing house rent allowance (HRA) on basis of false certificates - Whether amounts to misconduct - (B)Departmental enquiry - Natural justice - Compliance - Delinquent charged of obtaining HRA on false certificates of municipal authorities - Whether enquiry was in breach of natural justice - (C) HRA - Entitlement of employee living in his own house - Only if rental value as assessed for municipal purposes is more than 10% of employee's salary - Rental value for HRA cannot be different from annual value entered in municipal record - Whether employee can claim HRA on basis of certificate of municipal officials showing a different rental value - (D) Termination of service - Proportionality - Employee obtaining HRA on basis of false certificates - Removing him from service - Whether disproportionate? - Fact that falsity of HRA claim was detected after ten years - Whether a mitigating circumstance - Held, (A) Amounts to misconduct - (B) Plea unjustified since fact of issuance of certificate was not disputed - Enquiry was not in breach of natural justice - (C) Employee cannot claim HRA on basis of a different rental value - (D) Removing him from service - Not disproportionate - Not a mitigating circumstance - Appeal dismissed.
