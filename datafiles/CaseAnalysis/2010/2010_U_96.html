Â Bhim Singh v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, K.G. Balakrishnan, R.V. Raveendran, D.K. Jain, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 342; (2010) 5 SCC 538; 2010 (4) AWC 3584; JT 2010 (5) SC 166; 2010 (4) MLJ(SC) 1125; 2010(5) SCALE 37; [2010] 6 S.C.R. 218

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Parliament & Legislature; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Finance Commission, Money Bills, House Of The People, Members of Parliament Local Area Development Scheme

Summary: (A) Constitution - Parliament & Legislature - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 113, 114 - Members of Parliament Local Area Development Scheme (MPLAD Scheme) - Funds earmarked from Consolidated Fund of Union - Constitutionality - Whether the funds ear-marked and being spent from the Consolidated Fund of Union for implementation of the MPLAD Scheme is in accordance with the constitutional provisions - Held, sanctifying expenditure from and out of the Consolidated Fund of India is the Appropriation Act, as prescribed in art. 114(3) which mandates that no money should be withdrawn from the Consolidated Fund of India except under appropriation made by law based in accordance with the provisions of art. 114(3) - After the estimates of expenditure laid before House of People in the form of 'demands of grants' has been passed, a Bill is to be introduced to provide for the appropriation out of the Consolidated Fund of India of all monies required to meet the grants made by the House of People - In other words, withdrawal of moneys for the scheme is done only by means of an appropriation made by law in accordance with the provisions of art. 114 - Further, upon demand of grant having been made u/art. 113, Appropriation Bills were introduced and enacted in each year to appropriate moneys for the purposes of the MPLAD Scheme - Appropriation of public revenue for the purposes of the MPLAD Scheme has been sanctioned by the Parliament by Appropriation Acts and therefore funds ear-marked and being spent from the Consolidated Fund of Union for implementation of the MPLAD Scheme is in accordance with the constitutional provisions - Therefore, impugned MPLAD Scheme is valid and intra vires of the Constitution - Petitions dismissed.

(B) Constitution - Parliament & Legislature - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 275, 282 - Members of Parliament Local Area Development Scheme (MPLAD Scheme) - Whether art. 275 is the only source for a regular and permanent scheme and whether art. 282 is intended to apply only in regard to special, temporary or ad-hoc schemes - Held, several welfare schemes were sponsored and are being formulated by the Union of India for implementing Directive Principles of the State Policy - No restriction can be placed on the scope and width of the art. 282 by reference to other Articles or provisions in the Constitution as the said Article is not subject to any other Article in the Constitution -Further, art. 282 empowers Union and the States to exercise their spending power to matters not limited to the legislative powers conferred upon them and in the matter of expenditure for a public purpose subject to fulfilment of such other provisions as may be applicable to the Constitution and hence Union and States powers are not restricted or circumscribed - Ever since the inception of the Constitution several welfare schemes advancing the public purpose/public interest by grants disbursed by the Union have been implemented and MPLAD is one amongst the several schemes which have been designed and implemented u/art. 282 - Hence, art. 282 can be the source of power for emergent transfer of funds, like the MPLAD Scheme and even otherwise, the MPLAD Scheme is voted upon and sanctioned by the Parliament every year as a Scheme for community development - Both arts. 275 and 282 are sources of spending funds/monies under the Constitution and art. 282 is normally meant for special, temporary or ad hoc schemes subject to fulfilment of condition 'public purpose' - Petitions dismissed.

(C) Parliament & Legislature - Members of Parliament Local Area Development Scheme (MPLAD Scheme) - Misuse of powers by Members of Parliament (MPs) - Whether MPs are using their powers in allocating the work and using the funds under the guise of the MPLAD Scheme is arbitrary and mala fide manner - Held, District Authority verifies the eligibility and technical feasibility of each recommended work - Decision making power in regard to technical, financial, administrative sanctions accorded under the scheme, vests in the district level functionaries - Sanctioning of eligible works and their execution is done by the District Authorities and State Governments monitor the MPLAD works implementation - Nodal District Authority has to coordinate with other districts falling in the same constituency (in case of Lok Sabha constituencies) and with all the districts in which the MP has recommended work (in case of Rajya Sabha MPs) - Scheme only supplements the efforts of the State and other local Authorities and does not seek to interfere in the functional as well as financial domain of the local planning authorities of the State - Respondents have highlighted that the collective responsibility ensures in implementing the Scheme and over the years, various checks are also put in place, including the measures to make the scheme more transparent in all respects - Therefore, Government of India is not delegating its power to the MPs to spend the money contrary to the mandate of the constitutional provisions - Even though MPs have been given a seemingly executive function, their role is limited to 'recommending' works and actual implementation is done by the local authorities and therefore, there is no removal of checks and balances since these are duly provided and have to be strictly adhered to by the guidelines of the MPLAD Scheme and the Parliament - Petitions dismissed.

(D) Parliament & Legislature - Members of Parliament Local Area Development Scheme (MPLAD Scheme) - Doctrine of Separation of powers - Violation of - Whether MPLAD Scheme violates the principle of Separation of Powers under the Constitution - Held, no rigid separation of powers existed under the Constitution and each one of the arms of the government at times perform other functions as well - An MP is ultimately responsible to Parliament for his action as an MP even under the Scheme - All MPs be it a Member of Lok Sabha or Rajya Sabha or a nominated Member of Parliament are only seeking to advance public interest and public purpose and it is quite logical for the MP to carry out developmental activities to the constituencies they represent - No reason to believe that the MPLAD Scheme would not be effectively controlled and implemented by the District Authority in the case of Panchayats and Commissioners/Chief Executive Officers, in the case of Municipalities and Corporations with adequate safeguards under the Guidelines for the implementation of the MPLAD Scheme - District Authority and Chief Executive Officer have been entrusted with the absolute authority to discharge upon the feasibility of works recommended, assess the funds required for execution of the work, implementation of works by engaging an implementing agency, supervision of work and ensure financial transparency by providing audit certificates and utilization certificate and as such it is clear that the District Authority and Municipal Authority play a pivotal role in implementation and execution of MPLAD Scheme - Therefore, there is no violation of principles of separation of powers between legislature and executive - Petitions dismissed.

(E) Constitution - Parliament & Legislature - Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. - Members of Parliament Local Area Development Scheme (MPLAD Scheme) - Whether the MPLAD scheme is inconsistent with Part IX and Part IX-A of the Constitution by encroaching upon the powers and functions of elected bodies - Held, even though the District Authority is given the power to identify the agency through which a particular work recommended by the MP should be executed, the Panchayati Raj Institutions (PRIs) will be the preferred Implementing Agency in the rural areas, through the Chief Executive of the respective PRI, and the Implementing Agencies in the urban areas would be urban local bodies, through the Commissioners/Chief Executive Officers of Municipal Corporations, Municipalities - PRIs, Municipal as well as local bodies have also not been denuded of their role or jurisdiction by the Scheme as due place has been accorded to them by the guidelines, in the implementation of the Scheme and hence the MPLAD Scheme is consistent with Part IX and IX-A of the Constitution - Petitions dismissed.

(F) Parliament & Legislature - Members of Parliament Local Area Development Scheme (MPLAD Scheme) - Implementation of - Effect on election - Whether the MPLAD Scheme gives an unfair advantage to the MPs in contesting elections by violating the provisions of the Constitution - Held, MPLADS makes funds available to sitting MPs for developmental work - If the MP utilizes the funds properly, it would result in his better performance and if that leads to people voting for the incumbent candidate, it certainly does not violate any principle of free and fair elections - Petitions dismissed.

(G) Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Members of Parliament Local Area Development Scheme (MPLAD Scheme) - Judicial review - Scope - Whether the MPLAD Scheme, even if it is otherwise constitutional is liable to be quashed for want of adequate safeguards, checks and balances - Held, court can strike down a law or scheme only on the basis of its vires or unconstitutionality but not on the basis of its viability - When a regime of accountability is available within the Scheme, it is not proper for the Court to strike it down, unless it violates any constitutional principle - In MPLAD Scheme, an accountability regime has been provided and efforts must be made to make the regime more robust, but in its current form, cannot be struck down as unconstitutional - Petitions dismissed.
