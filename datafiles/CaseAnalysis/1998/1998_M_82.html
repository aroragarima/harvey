Bharat Petroleum Corporation Limited v Maharashtra General Kamgar Union and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1829; (1999) SCC (L&S) 361; (1999) 1 SCC 626; AIR 1999 SC 401; 1999 (3) Bom.C.R. 54; JT 1998 (8) SC 487; 1999 (1) LLJ 35; 1999 (1) LLJ 352; 1999 (1) LLN 654; 1998(6) SCALE 537; 1998 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 118; [1998] Supp3 S.C.R. 517

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Certification, Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946

Summary: Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946, s. 5 - Right of representation of a workman - Standing Orders restricting it by fellow workman of parent establishment of workman - Validity - Held, reasonable, fair and in consonance with model standing order - Authority are empowered to examine fairness and reasonableness of standing orders.
