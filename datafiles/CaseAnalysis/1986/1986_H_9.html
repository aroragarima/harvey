Harbhajan Singh Dhalla v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

05 November 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 450; (1986) 4 SCC 678; AIR 1987 SC 9; 1987 (1) CCC 243; 1987 (1) CompLJ 76; JT 1986 SC 765; 1986(2) SCALE 728; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 114; 1987 (1) UJ 43

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908, ss. 86, 87, 87B, 86(1), 86(2), 86(6) - Sanction for suit against Foreign State (Algerian Embassy) - No provision of any appeal from the order of the Central Govt. in either granting or refusing to grant sanction u/s.86 of the Code - Determination of nature of power of Central Govt. to grant sanction - Held, although administrative, observance of principles of natural justice is implicit so CG must pass reasoned order and should not adjudicate on merits (Doctrine of Sovereign immunity) -

Directions issued.
