State of Uttar Pradesh v Ram Sajivan and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1835; (2010) 1 SCC 529; 2010 (1) ADJ 199; AIR 2010 SC 1738; JT 2009 (14) SC 626; 2010 (2) MLJ(Crl) 132; 2010 (3) RCR(Criminal) 452; 2009(14) SCALE 376; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 154; 2009 (3) UC 1806

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Power Of The Court, Police Protection, Charge- Sheet, Graphic Description

Summary: (A) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 149, 302 and 364 - Murder - Dacoity - Genocide and massacre - Atrocities - Appeal against acquittal - Trial Court convicted 18 persons u/ss. 302/149, IPC and s. 364, IPC - Allegedly the accused persons had butchered seven people - In appeal, HC acquitted all the accused persons - Whether HC was justified in acquitting accused persons? - Held, no - HC without analyzing the evidence and assigning any cogent reason set aside the well reasoned judgment of the Additional Sessions Judge and acquitted all the respondents - Findings of the HC are not based on proper analysis of the entire evidence on record - On proper scrutiny of the entire evidence and documents on record, no other view is possible except the guilt of the accused - Order accordingly.P /&gt;(B) Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 378 and 386 - Interfering in order of acquittal - Judicial parameters - Powers of the appellate court in appeal against acquittal - Held, appellate court may review the evidence in appeals against acquittal u/ss. 378 and 386, CrPC - Its power of reviewing evidence is wide and the appellate court can re-appreciate the entire evidence on record - There must also be substantial and compelling reasons for reversing an order of acquittal - Order Accordingly.
