Nathulal v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

22 March 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, R.S. Bachawat, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 487; AIR 1966 SC 43; 1966 CRLJ 71

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Criminal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Essential Commodities Act, 1955, ss. 3 and 7 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 40 - Appellant in possession of maunds and wheat for sale without license - Appellant acquitted by Trial Court but convicted by High Court - Whether mens rea a necessary ingredient for conviction of an offence u/s. 7 of Act? - Mens rea is an essential ingredient of a criminal offence - Mens rea by necessary implication may be excluded from a statute only where it is absolutely clear that implementation of object of statute would otherwise be defeated - Nature of mens rea that would be implied in a statute creating an offence depends on object of Act and provisions thereof - Having regard to object of Act, namely, to control in general public interest, among others, trade in certain commodities, it cannot be said that object of Act would be defeated if mens rea is read as an ingredient of offence - A person commits an offence u/s. 7 of Act if he intentionally contravenes any order made u/s. 3 of Act - Held, no doubt that appellant contravened s. 3 of Act but even Govt. authorities acted negligently and assured that license will be issued to appellant very soon and it was only on this belief that appellant kept possession of wheat and maunds - Conviction set aside - Appeal allowed.
