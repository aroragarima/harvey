Â Smt. Triveniben and Others v State of Gujarat and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 February 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

G.L. Oza, K. Jagannatha Shetty, K.N. Singh, L.M. Sharma, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 43; (1989) SCC (Cr) 248; (1989) 1 SCC 678; AIR 1989 SC 1335; 1989 (2) Crimes 391; 1990 CRLJ 1810; 1989 CrLR(SC) 267; JT 1989 (1) SC 314; 1989(1) SCALE 301; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 509

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Bride Burning

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950,arts. 21 and 72 - Commutation of death sentence - Long delay caused in execution of death sentence - Whether prolonged delay in execution of death sentence, entitled accused to seek death penalty commuted to imprisonment for life? - Held, no - Time taken by way of trial and appeal is for benefit of accused to ensure a fair trial - Time is spent in public interest for proper administration of justice - If court awards death sentence, despite of delay in trial, there cannot be a second look at sentence - If delay in disposal is not a mitigating circumstance, it would be inappropriate to consider delay to impeach execution - Delay in passing sentence does not render sentence unconstitutional, any subsequent delay cannot render it unconstitutional - Appeal dismissed.
