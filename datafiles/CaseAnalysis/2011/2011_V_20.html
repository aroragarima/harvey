Union Of India & Ors v Vikrambhai Maganbhai Chaudhari

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 389; (2011) 7 SCC 321; 2012 (6) AWC 6084; JT 2011 (7) SC 138; 2011 (4) MPLJ 248; 2011(6) SCALE 608; [2011] 6 S.C.R. 1096; 2011 (4) SLR 704

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Central Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1965, rr. 14, 16 and 29 - Suspension - Disciplinary Authority - Revision - Respondent, Postal Assistant refused to obey orders of his supervisor to accept M.O. forms along with the amounts for booking of money orders - Departmental action was initiated against respondent and he was suspended by an order of Superintendent of Post Office - Subsequently, the suspension order of the respondent was revoked and disciplinary action was initiated against the respondent u/r. 16 of Rules who awarded punishment of 'Censure' to the respondent - Thereafter, the Chief Post Master General vide notification dt. 29.05.2001 took the case of the respondent for review u/r. 29 of the Rules and directed the Superintendent of Post Office to initiate disciplinary proceedings against the respondent u/r. 14 of the Rules - Respondent filed application before tribunal challenging said review - Tribunal allowed said application and quashed notification dt. 29.05.2001 on the ground that in the said notification the time limit has not been provided for appellate authority to seek review the order of the disciplinary authority - HC dismissed application filed against said order - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether Tribunal rightly quashed notification dt. 29.05.2001? - Held, as per r. 29 (1)(v) of Rules where the appellate authority seeks to review the order of the disciplinary authority, the period fixed for the purpose was 6 months of the date of the order proposed to be revised - Impugned notification dt. 29.05.2001 has not specified any time limit within which power u/r. 29(1)(vi) of Rules was exercisable by the authority - Hence, impugned notification was not in terms with r. 29 and the Tribunal was fully justified in quashing the same - The HC had also rightly confirmed the said conclusion by dismissing the application filed by the appellants - Impugned order upheld - Appeals dismissed.
