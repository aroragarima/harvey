Bharat Chaudhary and Another v State of Bihar and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 821; (2003) 8 SCC 77; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1953; AIR 2003 SC 4662; 2003 (4) CCR 176; 2003 (4) Crimes 264; 2003 CRLJ 5038; 2003 (2) DMC 715; 2003 (3) JCC 1535; 2003 (4) JhrCR(SC) 237; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 542; 2003 (3) KLT 956; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 716; 2003(8) SCALE 340; 2004 (1) ShimLC 155; 2003 (6) SLT 135; 2003 (7) Supreme 224

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 406, 498A, 504 - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, ss. 3, 4 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 438, 439 - Anticipatory bail rejected by HC - Held, there is no restriction in regard to exercise of power of granting anticipatory bail in a suitable case either by Court of Sessions, HC or this Court even when cognizance is taken or charge sheet is filed object of s.438 is to prevent undue harassment of accused persons by per-trial arrest and detention fact, that a Court has either taken cognizance of complaint or investigating agency has filed a chargesheet, would not by itself prevent concerned courts from granting anticipatory bail in appropriate cases gravity of offence is an important factor to be taken into consideration while granting such anticipatory bail so also need for custodial interrogation, but these are only factors that must be borne in mind by concerned courts while entertaining a petition for grant of anticipatory bail and fact of taking cognizance or filing of charge sheet cannot be themselves be construed as a prohibition against grant of anticipatory bail - Further held, courts i e Court of Sessions, HC or this Court has necessary power vested in them to grant anticipatory bail in non-bailable offences u/s. 438, CrPC, 1973, even when cognizance is taken or charge sheet is filed provided facts of case require court to do so.
