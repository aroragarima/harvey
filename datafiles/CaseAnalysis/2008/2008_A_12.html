B. P. Moideen Sevamandir & Anr. v A. M. Kutty Hassan

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1987; (2009) 2 SCC 198; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1123; 2009 (2) AWC 1420; 2008 (6) CTC 328; 2009 (5) G.L.R. 4314; 2010 (1) JhrCR(SC) 92; JT 2009 (2) SC 320; 2009 (1) KLT 235; 2009 (2) MLJ(SC) 1063; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 722; 2008(16) SCALE 403; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 905

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Suit for declaration and mandatory injunction - Held, there can be no order by the Lok Adalat containing directions to the parties by way of final decision, with a further direction to the parties to settle the case in terms of such directions - In fact, there cannot be an 'award' when there is no settlement - Nor can there be any 'directions' by the Lok Adalat determining the rights/obligations/title of parties, when there is no settlement - The settlement should precede the award and not vice versa - If there was a final settlement before the Lok Adalat, there would have been an award and there was no need for the matter to come before the court for further hearing - Appeals allowed.
