Â Builders Supply Corporation v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 November 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 287; AIR 1965 SC 1061; 1965 (1) ITJ 441; [1965] 56 ITR 91; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 289

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Civil Procedure - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s.115 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 46(2), 6,7,8,9,10 - Public Demands Recovery Act, 1913, r. 22 - Recovery of tax dues - Unsecured debts - Doctrine of priority - Applicability of provision - Respondent no. 2 secured building contract from Govt. for construction of mint and made deposit of certain sum as security for due execution of contract - Respondent no. 2 obtained supply of building materials from appellant - Appellant was unable to secure payment for goods - Appellant sued respondent no. 2 for recovery of dues - Appellant's suit was decreed by Additional Subordinate Judge - Subordinate Judge in execution proceeding upheld respondent no.1 plea that tax amount due to it from respondent no. 2 had priority over decrial amount due to appellant from same debtor and that amount of certain sum in its custody under attachment should be paid to respondent no.1 - Appellant filed revision application before HC u/s. 115 of CPC who discharged rule - Hence, instant Application - Whether respondent no. 1 was entitled to claim that recovery of amount of tax due to it from citizen could take precedence and priority over unsecured debts due from citizen to his other private creditors - Held, basic justification for claim for priority made by respondent no.1 on well-recognized principle that State was entitled to raise money by taxation, it would not be able to function as sovereign Govt. - It was essential that as Sovereign, State should be able to discharge its primary governmental functions and to discharge such functions efficiently, it should be in possession of necessary funds, and that consideration emphasized necessity and wisdom of conceding to State right to claim priority in respect of its tax dues - However, same principle might not equally be applicable in respect of debts due to State however, they were contracted by citizens in relation to commercial activities which should be undertaken by State for achieving socio-economic good - Therefore, claim for priority made by respondent no. 1 had to be sustained, because it was based on common law doctrine - Further, for provision for recovery of arrears of tax, it could not be said that s. 46 of the 1922 Act dealt with or provided principle of priority of tax dues at all and it was impossible that s. 46 of the 1922 Act in terms displaces application of said doctrine - However, 1913 Act provided procedure to recover public debts, was not directly concerned with right to recover arrears, or with priority, of tax dues - Therefore, r.22 of 1913 Act which corresponded to O.21 r. 52 of CPC could no doubt be invoked to recover arrears of tax - Recovery of public debts and tax arrears could be treated as public debts by virtue of s. 46(2) of the 1922 Act - Thus, doctrine of priority of arrears of tax over private debts could be displaced by any of provisions of 1913 Act - Hence, HC had right held that respondent no. 1 was entitled to claim arrears of tax due from respondent no. 2 over decrial debt from same debtor - Appeal dismissed.
