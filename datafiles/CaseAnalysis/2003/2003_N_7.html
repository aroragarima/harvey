N. Khaderwali Saheb (Dead) by LRs and another v N. Gudu Sahib (Dead) and others

Supreme Court of India

05 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Kumar, M.B. Shah, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 461; (2003) 3 SCC 229; AIR 2003 SC 1524; 2003 (50) ARBLR 647; 2003 (2) AWC 1018; 2003 (2) CHN 147; [2003] 261 ITR 1; JT 2003 (1) SC 640; 2003 (1) RCR(Civil) 771; 2003 (1) RD 257; 2003(1) SCALE 642; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 868; 2003 (2) SLT 14; 2003 (1) Supreme 921; 175 TAXATION 431; [2003] 129 TAXMAN 597; 2003 (2) UC 778

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Distribution Of Assets

Summary: Registration Act, 1908 - s.17 - Two issues: (1) Whether registration under s.17, Registration Act, 1908 is required for an award by which residue assets of a partnership firm are distributed amongst the partners on dissolution of the partnership firm; (2) Whether such an award amounts to creation of or transfer of any fresh rights in movable or immovable properties so as to bring it within the ambit of s.17 of the Registration Act? - Held: (1) the award did not require registration since the document did not transfer or assign interest in any asset; (2) On dissolution of a partnership firm, the allotment of assets to individual partner is not a case of transfer of any assets of the firm...There is no transfer or assignment of ownership in any of the assets.
