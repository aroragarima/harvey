Het Ram Beniwal and another v Raghuveer Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

21 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

L. Nageswara Rao, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 810; (2017) 4 SCC 340; AIR 2016 SC 4940; 2017 CRLJ 175; JT 2016 (10) SC 383; 2016 (4) RCR(Civil) 884; 2016 (4) RCR(Criminal) 728; 2016(10) SCALE 313

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Contempt Of Court, Anticipatory Bail, Administration Of Justice, Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, Secretary, Comments, Apology, Judiciary, Bias, Constitution of India, 1950, Criminal, Corruption, Association

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss. 12, 12(1), 2(c), 2(c)(i), 5 - Contempt of court - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants addressed a huge gathering of their party workers in front of Collectorate, while addressing they made scandalous statements against HC which were published in local newspaper on 24-02-2001 - Respondent no.1 filed contempt petition before HC alleging that appellants were guilty of a systematic campaign to destroy public confidence in judiciary - HC held appellants guilty of committing criminal contempt and sentenced them to simple imprisonment of 2 months and fine of Rs. 2000/- each - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, it was the right and interest of public in the due administration of justice that have to be protected in Asharam M. Jain v. A. T. Gupta, 1983 Indlaw SC 129. The statements made by appellants were not only derogatory but also have the propensity to lower authority of Court. Accusing judges of corruption results in denigration of institution which has an effect of lowering the confidence of public in the system of administration of justice. A perusal of allegations made by appellants cannot be termed as fair criticism on the merits of case. Appellants indulged in an assault on the integrity of judges of HC by making baseless and unsubstantiated allegations. They were not entitled to seek shelter u/s. 5 of the Act. If there was calculated effort to undermine judiciary, Courts would exercise their jurisdiction to punish the offender for committing contempt. SC approves the findings recorded by HC that appellants have transgressed all decency by making serious allegations of corruption and bias against HC. The caustic comments made by appellants cannot, by any stretch of imagination, be termed as fair criticism. The statements made by appellants accusing judiciary of corruption lower the authority of Court. The stand taken by appellants in contempt petition and affidavit filed in SC does not inspire any confidence that apology was made bona fide. SC was in agreement with the judgment of HC that appellants were guilty of committing contempt of Court. SC modifies the sentence to only payment of fine of Rs. 2,000/- each. Appeals disposed of.
