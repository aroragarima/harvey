Satya Jain (D) through Lrs. and Others v Anis Ahmed Rushdie (D) through Lrs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 630; (2013) 8 SCC 131; 2013 (1) ALD(SC) 148; 2013 (96) ALR 291; 2013 (1) AWC 625; 2012 (195) DLT 373; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 319

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: (A) Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Breach of contract - Proof - Tenancy was created in favour of plaintiff no.1 and agreement to sell was executed - Plaintiff no.1 had received notice terminating tenancy qua half portion of suit property - Plaintiff nos.1,3 filed suit for specific performance of contract in respect of agreement executed between original plaintiff no.1 and original defendant in respect of property - Plaintiff nos.2 ,3/appellants were sons of original plaintiff no.1 - Suit was decreed in favour of plaintiff - Appeal was filed against impugned order which was allowed - Hence, instant Appeals - Whether appellants were in breach of terms and conditions of agreement - Held, total sale price was agreed at Rs. 3,75,000/- out of which sum of Rs.50,000/- was acknowledged to have been paid by purchaser(plaintiff no.1) to vendor (defendant) by means of account payee cheque - U/cl. 7 of agreement, purchaser was obliged to pay to Income Tax authorities such amount as could be desired by vendor in order to enable vendor to get required Wealth Tax clearance certificate - Cl. 7 or any other clause of agreement had not cast upon plaintiff no.1 duty to tender any further payment to defendant or to credit bank account of defendant with any further advance amount after payment of initial amount of Rs.50,000/- - Further, plaintiff no.1 had repeatedly asserted that he was always ready and willing to pay any amount within balance consideration payable to Income Tax department so that necessary tax clearance certificate could be issued in favour of defendant - Hence, it could be inferred that Plaintiff no.1 was ready and willing to perform his part of contract while it was defendant who had defaulted in execution of sale document - Appeals allowed.

(B) Civil Procedure - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s.20 - Relief of specific performance - Entitlement to - Whether plaintiff was entitled to decree for specific performance of agreement - Held, long efflux of time over 40 years that had occurred and galloping value of real estate in meantime were twin inhibiting facts - However, that had to be balanced with fact that plaintiffs were in no way responsible for delay that had occurred and their keen participation in proceedings till date showed live interest on part of plaintiffs to have agreement enforced in law - Discretion to direct specific performance of agreement and that too after lapse of long period of time had to be exercised on sound, reasonable, rational and acceptable principles - Parameters for exercise of discretion vested by s.20 of the Act could not be entrapped within any precise expression of language and contours thereof would depend on facts and circumstances of each case - Hence, efflux of time and escalation of price of property, by itself, could not be valid ground to deny relief of specific performance - Appeals allowed.
