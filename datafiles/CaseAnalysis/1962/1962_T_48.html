Â Bokaro and Ramgur Limited v State of Bihar and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 March 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 409; AIR 1963 SC 516; 1962 (2) KerLR 19; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 831; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 831

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Constitution - Land & Property - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(f), 31(1) and 226 - Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1950, s. 4(h) - (A) Whether any person whose title is not absolute can file a suit u/art. 226 - Held, he cannot - Before a party can complain of an infringement of his fundamental right to hold property he must establish that he has title to that property and if his title itself is in dispute and is subject of adjudication in proceedings legally constituted, for example an enquiry under s. 4(h) of Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1950, he cannot put forward any claim based on his title until as a result of that enquiry he is able to establish his title - It is only thereafter that question whether his rights in or to that property have been improperly or illegally infringed could arise - Application dismissed.
