Boddella Babul Reddy v Public Prosecutor, High Court of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

06 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 208; (2010) 3 SCC 648; AIR 2010 SC 3231; 2010 (1) JCC 710; JT 2010 (1) SC 24; 2010(1) SCALE 60; [2010] 1 S.C.R. 149

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860,ss. 147, 148, 324, 326, 307 and 302 - Explosive Substances Act, 1908, ss. 3 and 5 - Indian Arms Act, 1959, s. 25(1)(b) and 27 - 16 accused persons were tried for the offences punishable u/ss. 147, 148, 324, 326, 307 and 302, IPC r/w s. 149, IPC; ss. 3 and 5 of the Explosive Substances Act and ss. 25(1)(b) and 27 of the Arms Act - Trial Court observed that there was deliberate delay in giving the First Information Report (FIR) and FIR was given after due deliberations with the political leaders - Trial Court also held that the offence was not established as also the medical evidence was not consistent with the oral evidence on record - Trial Court also pointed out that the prosecution had failed to explain the injuries on the persons of the accused and as such the oral evidence, more particularly of PW-1 to PW-5, who were the Congress Party workers and who were inimical against the accused, could not be believed - Trial Court acquitted all the accused persons - On appeal, HC confirmed the judgment regarding all accused persons except appellant who was held guilty of hurling the bomb on deceased and was convicted for offence punishable u/s. 302, IPC - Hence, present appeal - Held, HC has not exercised the caution that was expected to while dealing with the judgment of acquittal by the Trial Court - HC has also left out of consideration the important findings regarding the FIR and the other important circumstance that before the FIR was given, the lawyers/legal advisors had already reached the place alongwith their leader - HC has also not considered the contradictions between the evidence of witnesses - Thus, HC was not justified in interfering with the well considered judgment of the Trial Court - Judgment of the HC set aside - Judgments of Trial Court restored - Appeal allowed.
