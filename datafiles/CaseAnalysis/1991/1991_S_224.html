Hanumantappa Krishnappa Mantur and Others v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Fathima Beevi, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 66; (1992) Supp2 SCC 213; (1992) SCC (Cr) 667; AIR 1992 SC 599; 1992 CRLJ 405

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304, Part I and 300 - Murder - Right to private defence was in question - Incident occured in deceased's field when he objected on removal of crops by accused persons - Medical evidence proving that death was caused by back portion of axe - Held, accused armed with axe convicted under s. 304 Part I for exceeding right to private defence whereas other accused were acquitted.
