Â V. P. Gindroniya v State Of Madhya Pradesh & Ors

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, A.N. Ray, I.D. Dua, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 352; (1970) 1 SCC 362; AIR 1970 SC 1494; 1970 (21) FLR 251; 1970 LabIC 1332; 1970 (2) LLJ 143; 1971 MLJ 188; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 448; 1970 SLR 329; 1970 UJ 318

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - M P Govt. Servants (Temporary and Quasi-Permanent Service) Rules, 1960, r. 12 - Suspension - Appellant was a appointed temporarily - (A) Whether order of State Govt. suspending appellant during pendency of departmental enquiry amounted to a suspension of contract of service and hence he could not have unilaterally terminated his services? - It is now well settled that power to suspend, in sense of a right to forbid a employee to work, is not an implied term in an ordinary contract between master and servant, and that such a power can only be creature either of a statute governing contract, or of an express term in contract itself - Ordinarily, therefore, absence of such a power either as an express term in contract or in rules framed under some statute would mean that an employer would have no power to suspend an employee of his and even if he does so in sense that he forbids employee to work, he will have to pay employee's wages during period of suspension - Held, 'Rules' do not provide for suspension during pendency of an enquiry - Therefore impugned order of suspension cannot be considered as an order suspending contract of service - From that conclusion it follows that when Appellant issued notice terminating his services on 06-06-1964, contract of service was in force and it was open to him to put an end to same - (B) What is legality of notice to Govt. by appellant terminating his services? - Held, notice satisfies requirements of main part of r. 12(a) - Appeal allowed.
