Bharat Sanchar Nigam Limited v Telecom Regulatory Authority of India and others

Supreme Court of India

06 December 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Balbir Singh Chauhan, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 815; (2014) 3 SCC 222; 2014 (1) CompLJ 229; 2014 (2) RCR(Civil) 584; 2013(14) SCALE 643; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 999

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Telecom

Keywords: Revenue Sharing, Port Charges, Claim of amount

Summary: Administrative - Telecom - Indian Telegraph Act, 1885 - Telecom Regulatory Authority of India Act, 1997, ss. 11, 11(1), 11(1)(a), 11(1)(b), 11(1)(vii), 11(1)(viii), 12, 12(4), 13, 14, 14(b), 18, 241, 29, 298, 298(2), 3, 33, 34, 35, 36, 36(1), 36(2), 37, 5 - Port Charges - Claim of amount - Entitled to - Port charges came to be prescribed in Schedule 3 of Regulations 1999 which came into force, by virtue of Cl. 8, regulations were given overriding effect qua the interconnection agreements - Instant appeals was filed by appellant/Bharat Sanchar Nigam Limited (BSNL) against order passed by Tribunal whereby appeal was preferred by appellant against Telecommunication Interconnection (Port Charges) was allowed and respondent/authority was directed to give fresh look at regulations and appellant was directed not to claim any amount from any operator during interregnum, from date of coming into force of regulations and date of order - Whether in exercise of power vested in it u/s. 14(b) of the Act, Tribunal has jurisdiction to entertain challenge to regulations framed by Authority u/s. 36 of the Act - Held, s. 14(b) had nothing to do with adjudication of disputes - Amendment made in 2000 was intended to vest original jurisdiction of Authority in Tribunal and same was achieved by s. 14(a) of the Act - Appellate jurisdiction exercisable by HC was also vested in Tribunal by virtue of s. 14(b) but this does not include decision made by Authority - Since HC while hearing appeal did not have power of judicial review of subordinate legislation, Tribunal could not exercise that power u/s. 14(b) - In exercise of power vested in it u/s. 14(b) of the Act, Tribunal does not have jurisdiction to entertain the challenge to regulations framed by Authority u/s. 36 of the Act - Contrary view taken by Tribunal and HC does not represent correct law - SC cleared that aggrieved person should be free to challenge validity of regulations framed u/s. 36 of the Act by filing appropriate petition before HC - Cases might be listed before an appropriate Bench for deciding questions framed - Order accordingly
