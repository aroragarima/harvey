Ramesh Vajabhai Rabari v Pratiksha Real Estate Private Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

03 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Gyan Sudha Misra, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 510; (2014) 12 SCC 190; AIR 2014 SC 2962; 2015 (2) ALD(SC) 177; 2015(2) ALL MR 973; 2014 (4) AWC 3662

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Development agreement - Execution - termination - Instant petitions assail the judgment and order passed by the DB which had reversed the judgment and order of the Single Judge who was pleased to grant an order of injunction in favour of petitioner - Whether HC was justified in reversing the order of injunction granted by Single Judge in favour of plaintiff/petitioner to his prejudice -

Held, petitioner although failed to prove any of the three prime factors to claim injunction succeeded in obtaining an order of temporary injunction which the DB has reversed and rightly so as petitioner obtained a development agreement from some of the successors of ED'M which however was also terminated apart from the fact that the original owner ED'M had already executed an agreement of sale in favour of VBP to whom the possession was also delivered and the respondent No. 1 had obtained an agreement in its favour from VBP - However, the decree of specific performance as already stated was yet to be passed in favour of petitioner whereas respondent No. 1 was claiming through VBP in whose favour the erstwhile owner ED'M had executed the agreement of sale in pursuance of which possession was also delivered 30 years ago - Petitioner having entered into an agreement of sale 30 years later from some of the co-sharers which agreement too was terminated on 2-4-2007 obviously could not be granted an order of injunction restraining the respondent No. 1 from claiming the property through VBP which prima facie not only has got interest in the property but also actual physical possession of the same - On the contrary, petitioner as of now has neither any semblance of title nor has possession on the property nor prima facie case nor balance of convenience in his favour as he was yet to take possession and develop the property - Single Judge was not justified in granting an order of injunction in favour of petitioner and the same was rightly reversed by DB of HC by assigning valid reasons - SC thus find no merit in these special leave petitions to interfere with the impugned judgment and order - Petitions dismissed.

.
