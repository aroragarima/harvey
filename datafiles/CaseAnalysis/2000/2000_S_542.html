Roy V. D v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

10 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2788; (2000) 8 SCC 590; (2001) SCC (Cr) 42; AIR 2001 SC 137; 2000 (4) CCR 236; 2000 (4) Crimes 196; 2001 CRLJ 165; 2001 (1) JCC 33; JT 2000 (Supp2) SC 481; 2001 (1) KLT 86; 2000 (4) RCR(Criminal) 762; 2000(7) SCALE 446; 2000 (8) SLT 231; [2000] Supp4 S.C.R. 539; 2000 (7) Supreme 430; 2001 (1) UC 206

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Health & Drug

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 482 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substance Act, 1985, ss. 20(b)(i), 41 and 42 - Search and seizure - Whether an arrest or search u/s. 41 and 42 of NDPS Act made by an officer not empowered or authorised to do so, be valid? - Held, for exercising powers enumerated u/sub-s. (1) of s. 42 at any time whether by day or by night a warrant of arrest or search issued by a Metropolitan Magistrate or a Magistrate of First Class or any Magistrate of Second Class who has been specially empowered by State Govt. in that behalf or an authorisation u/sub-s. (2) of s. 41 by an empowered officer is necessary - If arrest or search is made by any officer not empowered or authorised, it would per se be illegal and would affect prosecution case and consequently vitiate trial - Power u/s. 482, CrPC has to be exercised by HC, inter alia, to prevent abuse of process of any court or otherwise to secure ends of justice - Where criminal proceedings initiated based on illicit material collected on search and arrest which are per se illegal and vitiate not only a conviction and sentence based on such material but also trial itself, proceedings cannot be allowed to go on as it cannot but amount to abuse of process of court - Exercise of power u/s. 482, CrPC to quash proceedings in a case like one on hand, would indeed secure ends of justice - Order of HC set aside - Proceedings in Sessions Case No. 78 of 1993 quashed - Appeal allowed.
