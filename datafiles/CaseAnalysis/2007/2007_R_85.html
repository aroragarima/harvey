Ram Kumar Barnwal v Ram Lakhan (Dead)

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 513; (2007) 5 SCC 660; 2007 (4) ALJ 297; 2007 (68) ALR 136; 2007 (3) AWC 2866; 2007 (3) KLT 945; 2007 (6) MLJ(SC) 186; 2007 (148) PLR 224; 2007 (3) RCR(Civil) 279; 2007(7) SCALE 271; 2007 (5) SCJ 321; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 576

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: Regulation Of Letting, Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972

Summary: Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972 - Release petition - High Court came to the conclusion that even if it is found that the decisions of the Courts below are erroneous in law, the matter needs to be remanded to the prescribed authority - Appeal against - High Court was not justified in disposing of the writ petition in a summary fashion - Remanded the matter to the High Court for fresh consideration - Appeal partly allowed.
