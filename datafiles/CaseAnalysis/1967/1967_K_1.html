Sales Tax Officer v K. I. Abraham

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 266; AIR 1967 SC 1823; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 518; 1967 (20) STC 367

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Central Sales Tax (Kerala) Rules 1957

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, ss. 8 and 13 - Central Sales Tax (Kerala) Rules 1957, r. 6 - Whether High Court had erred in quashing orders of assessment of sales-tax and directing fresh assessment? - Legislature was conscious of fact that expression 'in manner' would denote only mode in which an act was to be done, and if any time-limit was to be prescribed for doing of the, act, specific words such as 'the time within which' were also necessary to be put in statute - Held, HC had not erred - Third proviso to r. 6(1) is ultra vires of s. 8(4) r/w s. 13(3) and (4) of Act - Assessee was not bound to furnish declarations in Form 'C' before 16-02-1961 in present case - Appeal dismissed.
