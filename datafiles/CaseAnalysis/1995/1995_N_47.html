Prem Nath Kapur and Another v National Fertilizers Corporation of India Ltd. and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.L. Hansaria, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1987; (1996) 2 SCC 71; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 195; 1996 (1) CLT 220; JT 1995 (9) SC 23; 1995(7) SCALE 109; [1995] Supp5 S.C.R. 790; 1996 (1) UJ 180

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Amendment Act, 1984, ss. 23(1), 23(1-A), 23(2), 28, 54 - Solatium - Compensation - Appropriation - Notification was published for acquiring large track of land and award of the Collector was confirmed - District Judge and the HC enhanced compensation - Appellants filed revision petition and claimed compensation, enhanced solatium and interest and additional amount u/ss. 23(1), 23(1-A), 23(2), 28 of the Amended Act 68 of 1984 which was granted by HC - Executing Court granted relief but HC in revision set-aside the order - Appellants aggrieved - Hence, instant appeals by special leave - Whether, the claimant appellant was entitled for claims under the provisions of the Amended Act, 1984 - Held, that when the deposit was made towards the specified amounts, the claimant/owner was not entitled to deduct from the amount of compensation towards costs, interest, additional amount u/s. 23 (1-A) with interest and then to claim the total balance amount with further interest - Revisional order for payment of additional benefits and enhanced solatium and interest under the Amendment Act 68 of 1984 was clearly in excess of the power or jurisdiction of the HC - Compensation u/s. 23(1) excluded the liability to pay interest on solatium and no solatium was payable on additional amount payable u/ s. 23(1-A) - The power to award additional amount u/s. 23 (1-A) and solatium u/s. 23 (2) were in addition to the market value determined u/s. 23 (1) and the interest u/s. 28 was on excess amount - Power and jurisdiction to award amounts under the provisions of the Amended Act, 1984 could arise only when the Court or HC u/s. 54 enhanced compensation - Appeals dismissed.
