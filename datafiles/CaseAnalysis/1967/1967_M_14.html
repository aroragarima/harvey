State of Andhra Pradesh v Madiga Boosena and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 437; AIR 1967 SC 1550; 1967 CRLJ 1398; 1968 (1) MLJ(SC) 57; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 871

Case Digest

Subject: Socio-Economic

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh (Andhra Area) Prohibition Act, 1937

Summary: Criminal - Trade - Andhra Pradesh(Andhra Area) Prohibition Act, 1937, ss. 3(8), 3(9) and 4(1)(a) - Whether s. 4(1) of the A.P Prohibition Act is contravened for carrying substance which had an odor similar to that of arrack as perceived by the police officer and can the accused be convicted merely on grounds of the smell without the substance being sent for any chemical examination? - Held, an offence u/s. 4(1)(a) of the Act requires the prosecution to establish two things: (1) that the article seized from the accused is liquor under s. 3(9) of the Act and that the accused "transported the same" - In the instant case except for a general statement, contained in the evidence of the witnesses regarding the smell, there is no other satisfactory evidence to establish that the article is one coming within the definition of the expression liquor - Merely trusting the smelling sense of the Prohibition Officers - Basing a conviction on an opinion expressed by those officers - Cannot justify the conviction of the respondents unless there is better proof, by a technical person, who has considered the matter from a scientific point of view - This is not only desirable but even necessary to establish that the article seized is one coming within the definition of 'liquor'- No further proceeding could take place in this case since the contravention was not proved - (B) On whom lies the burden of proof - Held, burden of proof fell upon the State - Appeal dismissed.
