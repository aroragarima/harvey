Premji Nathu v State Of Gujarat And Another

Supreme Court of India

09 April 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 117; (2012) 5 SCC 250; AIR 2012 SC 1624; 2012(3) ALL MR 434; 2012 (3) AWC 3017; 2012 (2) CLT(SC) 79; JT 2012 (4) SC 76; 2012 (5) MahLJ 514; 2012 (4) MLJ(SC) 335; 2012 (4) MPLJ 37; 2012 (3) RCR(Civil) 31; 2012 (117) RD 169; 2012 (2) RLW 1642; 2012(4) SCALE 235; [2012] 3 S.C.R. 1042

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Administrative - Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 6(1), 12(2), 18(2)(b), 54 - Land Acquisition - Compensation - Time barred - Sustainability - Appellant's land was acquired by Govt. for implementation of road scheme - Notification u/s. 4(1) was issued and declaration u/s. 6(1) was published - Special Land Acquisition Officer determined and fixed amount of compensation for irrigated and non-irrigated land - Collector issued notice u/s. 12(2) to appellant after award was passed - Appellant submitted application to Collector for making reference to Court for award of higher compensation - Reference Court enhanced compensation awarded to other land owners but declined relief to appellants on ground that application filed were beyond time specified in s. 18(2)(b) of Act - Aggrieved challenge was preferred before HC u/s. 54 of Act, which was dismissed by HC on ground that application were barred by time - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether application for higher compensation filed by appellant was time barred - Held, copy of award was not sent to appellant along with notice and without that he could not have effectively made an application for seeking reference - State Govt. did not file any evidence before Reference Court to show that copy of award was sent to appellant along with notice and Reference Court which mechanically concluded by stating that application filed was beyond time specified in s. 18(2)(b) - HC also committed serious error by approving view taken by Reference Court, albeit without considering fact that notice issued by Collector u/s. 12(2) was not accompanied by a copy of award which was essential for effective exercise of right vested in appellant to seek reference u/s. 18(1) - Hence, direction issued to respondent authorities to pay enhanced compensation, solatium etc. even to those who did not file appeals before HC and/or had not approached SC - Appeal allowed.
