Dr Rashlal Yadav v State Of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

23 June 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1577; (1994) 5 SCC 267; (1994) SCC (L&S) 1063; AIR 1994 SCW 3329; 1994 (27) ATC 855; 1995 (1) CLR 23; 1994 (69) FLR 406; JT 1994 (4) SC 228; 1994(3) SCALE 18; 1994 (3) SLJ 170; 1994 (4) SLR 449; [1994] Supp1 S.C.R. 231

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Corporate - Indirect Tax - Labour & Industrial Law - Service - Trade - Industries (Development And Regulation) Act, 1951, Section 10(7) - Bihar Non-Government Secondary Schools (Taking-over of Management and Control) Act, 1981 - Whether the removal /dismissal of the appellant from the office of the Chairman/Member of the Board is justified? It is upto the subjective satisfaction of the State Govt which subjective satisfaction must be reached on relevant material on record and not its whim and sweet will - The High Court examined the material on which the State Govt's decision for removal was founded, and rightly came to the conclusion that there was justification for the exercise of power - HC also examined the allegation of mala fides and its decisions are based on the assessment of facts and ordinarily this Court is loathe to re-evaluate the same unless it is shown it resulted in miscarriage of justice - Held, no reason to interfere with the HC's assessment - No infirmity in the HC's evaluation - No merit in the appeal, dismissed.
