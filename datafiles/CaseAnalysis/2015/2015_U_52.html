Dhole Govind Sahebrao and another v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

26 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 220; (2015) 6 SCC 727; 2015(3) ALL MR 920; JT 2015 (3) SC 423; 2015(4) SCALE 259; 2015 (3) SLR 764

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Right To Promotion, Direct Recruits, Chances Of Promotion, Equation Of Posts, Inter Se Seniority, States Reorganisation Act, 1956, Fourth Central Pay Commission, the Central Board of Excise & Customs, Department of Revenue, Ministry of Finance, framed the Electronic Data Processing Posts (Group 'C' Technical Posts) Recruitment Rules, 1992, Central Excise and Customs Department Senior Tax Assistant (Group 'C' Post) Recruitment Rules, 2003, Central Excise and Land Customs Department Group 'C' Posts Recruitment Rules, 1979

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 309 - Seniority - Department created cadre of Data Entry Operators out of the original ministerial cadre for discharging electronic data processing responsibilities - Consequent upon achievement of the above objective, the separate cadre so created, was sought to be re-amalgamated with the existing ministerial cadre - As result of bifurcation and re-amalgamation, some of employees in cadre of Data Entry Operators were given superiority on the subject of inter se seniority - Whether superiority assigned to Data Entry Operators on basis of seniority was justified in law.

Held, cadre of Data Entry Operators, was created out of the original ministerial cadre. It is, therefore apparent, that the members of the two cadres were originally discharging similar duties. Deficiencies in the two cadres sought to be merged, were sought to be overcome, by subjecting the members of the two cadres to different examinations, whereby, the two cadres were trained for discharging their duties efficiently, on merger, whilst holding the posts of Tax Assistants/Senior Tax Assistants. It is, therefore, not possible for us to accept, that there was any serious difference between the two merged cadres, either on the issue of nature of duties or in the qualifications prescribed for the posts. On account of the aforesaid, by and large similarity, merger of the cadres, and the determination of the inter se seniority on merger, were justifiably determined, on the basis of the different pay-scales of the cadres merged, under the TA Rules, 2003 and the STA Rules, 2003. By the mandate of the above Rules, all posts in equivalent pay-scales were placed at the same level. Appeals allowed.
