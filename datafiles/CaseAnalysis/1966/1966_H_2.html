H. B. Yeshwant Rao v Commissioner of

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 102; AIR 1967 SC 135; [1966] 61 ITR 444; [1966] Supp S.C.R. 419

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Wealth Tax (Amendment) Act, 1964 - Association of persons - Maintenance - Bonus shares - Charitable or religious entities - Appellant referred as assesses held certain shares in a company - He created two trusts, one may be called the Charitable Trust and the other the Sandur Rulers Family (Second) Trust may hereinafter be referred to as the Second Trust - Assesses transferred some shares to the Second Trust under conditions contained in the Trust Deed - Wealth Tax Officer and the Appellate Assistant Commissioner, in computing the net wealth of the assesses on March 31, 1958, and March 31, 1959, the valuation dates respectively for the A/Ys 1958-59 and 1959-60, included the value of these shares held by the Trustees under the Second Trust - Sums of Rs. 4,30,684 and Rs. 4,13,353 being the value of the shares were transferred by the assesses to the Sandur Ruler's Family (Second) Trust - On appeal, the Appellate Tribunal reversed the decisions of the authorities below and came to the conclusion that the value of the shares could not be taken into consideration in computing the net wealth of the assesses - Tribunal, however, at the instance of the Department referred the question of law already set out above for the opinion of the HC - HC as mentioned earlier, answered the question against the assesses - Whether the sums of Rs. 4,30,684 and Rs. 4,13,353 being the value of the shares transferred by the assesses to the Sandur Ruler's Family (Second) Trust could be included in the net wealth of the assessee for the A/Ys 1958-59 and 1959-60 under the provisions of S. 4(1)(a)(iii) of the 1964 Act - Held, the assesses out of natural love and affection for his minor children created the Trust in question, and that the minor children were the beneficiaries under the Trust - Under the clause as originally enacted, assets transferred for the immediate benefit of the individual, his wife or minor children alone may be included in the net wealth of the individual, and the liability of the settler must be determined under the provision as it stood enacted in 1957 - By the Family Trust the primary intention of the settlor as disclosed in the preamble was to make provision for his children, and for that purpose property was set apart by the Schedules read with cls. 1, 2 & 3 - By cl. 4 it was contemplated that other property will also be settled for the benefit of the children of the settlor - Settler intended that the income arising from the Trust property was to be utilized after the children attained the age of majority for their maintenance, education, health, marriage and advancement and not during their minority - cls. 9 & 26 together it appeared that the settlor intended that the trustees had power, notwithstanding other provisions in the deed of Trust, that the income of the property settled may be applied during the currency of the settlement for the benefit of the beneficiaries named therein, and in the event of death of any of the beneficiaries, for the benefit of his or her heirs - Therefore a vested interest immediately arising on the execution of the instrument, and the children of the settlor were the real beneficiaries - Hence, HC had held that the case fell clearly within 4(1)(a)(iii) of the Wealth Tax Act and during the periods specified in cls. 1, 2 & 3 the property mentioned in Schedules A, B & C was liable to be included in the computation of wealth tax of the appellant - Appeals disposed of.
