Messrs Larsen and Toubro Limited v State of Gujarat and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 March 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 646; (1998) 4 SCC 387; AIR 1998 SC 1608; 1998 (2) CLT 6; [1998] 92 Comp Cas 373; JT 1998 (2) SC 536; 1998(2) SCALE 373; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 339; 1998 (3) SLT 582; 1998 (3) Supreme 275

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Withdrawal From Acquisition

Summary: Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss.4, 6, 9, 39, 48, and 55 Land Acquisition (Companies) Rules, 1963, rr.3, and 4- Whether the Order for setting aside acquisition of the company is valid , when the allegations of non -compliance of the rr. 3 and 4 is not maintained in the petition - Whether it is necessary that the Collector should personally examine all details of the report , related with acquisition of the company -Whether after the Panchanama and revenue records, showing taking of possession of land are drawn, the affidavits filed by panchas, their signatures obtained on blank papers and that they had not gone to the site, are valid - Whether a particular mode has to be followed for withdrawal from acquisition - Whether a prior notice of withdrawal from acquisition to affected party , is necessary -Held, the Order setting aside acquisition for non-compliance of Rr. 3, 4 is not sustainable- Not necessary that all details should be personally examined by the collector - The affidavits are meaningless, cannot be relied upon to hold that possession was not taken-The withdrawl has to be by issuance of notification in Official Gazette- Not necessary when the affected party is the landowner, but it is necessary when company for whose benefit land is acquired is the affected party.
