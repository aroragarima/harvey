State Of A.P. v Punatiramulu

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1641; (1994) Supp1 SCC 590; (1994) SCC (Cr) 734; AIR 1993 SC 2644; 1993 CRLJ 3684; JT 1993 (Supp) SC 531

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Criminal - CrPC, 1973, ss. 154 and 162 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - (A) Complaint about cognizable offence - Refusal by police to record on ground that concerned police station has no territorial jurisdiction over place of crime - (1) Whether amounts to dereliction of duty - What is proper course - (B) FIR or statement recorded during investigation - Investigation Officer deliberately not recording FIR after receipt of information of cognizable offence - Registering the complaint as FIR after reaching the spot and after due deliberations, consultations and discussion - If complaint could be treated as FIR - (C) Testimony of interested witness - Held, (A) (1) Amounts to dereliction of duty - (2) Proper course is to record information and forward to police station having jurisdiction - (B) Could not be treated as FIR - It would be a statement made during investigation and hit by s. 162 of CrPC - (C) Testimony of interested witnesses could not be relied upon in absence of strong corroborative evidence of clinching nature - Appeal dismissed.
