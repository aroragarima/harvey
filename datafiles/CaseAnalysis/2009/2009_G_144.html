Vijaya Bank v Gurnam Singh

Supreme Court of India

02 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1895; (2010) 13 SCC 775; 2010 (78) ALR 716; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 87; 2009(14) SCALE 467

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Consumer Protection

Keywords: Overdraft Facility

Summary: Consumer Protection - Banking & Finance - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 21 - Deficiency of service - Complainant, having savings bank account with the appellant bank, lost his cheque book containing one unused leaf and the requisition slip - Subsequently it was found that the requisition slip had been used to get a new cheque book issued from the bank and the left over cheque in the lost cheque book had been misused to draw an amount of Rs.2,500/ from complainant's account - Having failed to get the amount so withdrawn from his account reimbursed by the bank, the complainant filed a complaint with the District Consumer Disputes Redressal Forum alleging deficiency in service by the Bank - It was held that the that there was gross deficiency in the service by the bank which resulted in loss to the complainant - Bank was directed to credit the amount - State Commission affirmed the findings recorded by the District Forum - National Commission dismissed the revision petition - Hence, the instant appeal.

Held, expert's opinion adduced in evidence by the bank in support of its stand that there was no forgery in the signatures on the cheques in question was no evidence in the eye of law. The report was not proved by summoning the expert. There is negligence/deficiency in the service of the bank. Appeal dismissed.
