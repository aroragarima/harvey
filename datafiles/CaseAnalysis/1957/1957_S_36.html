Â Ramji Lal Modi v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

05 April 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Syed Jaffer Imam, S.K. Das, P. Govinda Menon, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 116; AIR 1957 SC 620; 1957 CRLJ 1006; 1957 SCJ 522; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 860

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(a) and 19(2) - Indian Penal Code,1860,s. 295 A -Constitutional validity of s. 295 A,IPC,1860 - Petitioner is editor, printer and publisher of a monthly magazine - Agitation by muslims on comments on religious sentiments - Whether s. 295 A of IPC is protected by art. 19(2) of Constitution? - Calculated tendency of this aggravated form of insult is clearly to disrupt public order and section, which penalises such activities, is well within protection of cls.(2) of art. 19 as being a law imposing reasonable restrictions on exercise of right to freedom of speech and expression guaranteed by art. 19(1)(a) - Held, Section 295 A of IPC falls within protection of art. 19(2) of Constitution as being a law imposing reasonable restrictions on exercise of right to freedom of speech and expression guaranteed by art. 19(1)(a) - Appeal dismissed.
