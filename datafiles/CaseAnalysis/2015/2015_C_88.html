Commissioner of Central Excise, Chennai v Nebulae Health Care Limited

Supreme Court of India

27 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 862; 2015 (325) E.L.T. 431; 2015(12) SCALE 94; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 395

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Brand Names, Duty Of Excise, Rate Of Duty, Excisable Goods, Central Excise Rules, 1944, Modvat Credit, Exemption Notification, CENVAT Credit Rules, 2002

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - CENVAT credit on branded goods - Benefit under Notification not applicable - Justification - Assessees were manufactures of goods - Goods manufactured by assessee on its own account borne its own brand name and goods manufactured by third parties bear brand names belonging to those parties - During period in question, i.e., 1999 to 2003 - 2004, assessee had availed benefit of SSI exemption notifications that were in force - Availing benefit of Notifications in respect of goods manufactured by assessee on its own account had cleared said goods without payment of any excise duty - However, during this very period, in respect of goods bearing brand name of third parties manufactured by assessee, it paid excise duty thereupon - Further, it also availed CENVAT credit in respect of inputs used for manufacture of these branded goods - Authority issued notices on ground that since assessee had availed CENVAT credit in respect of inputs used for manufacture of branded goods, it had lost right to claim benefit of SSI exemptions under Notifications, hence, assesee had claimed exemption from payment of duties improperly - Commissioner vide order confirmed demand of duty - Order passed by Commissioner was confirmed by appellant authority - However, Tribunal vide impugned order reversed order passed by lower court and appeal filed by assessee was allowed - Whether availing benefit of MODVAT/CENVAT credit in respect of branded goods of third parties manufactured by assessees on job work basis, disentitles them from availing benefit of notifications.

Held, notification clearly states that for purposes of availing benefit of Notification by SSI Unit, clearances for home consumption only are to be taken into consideration, except in those cases where it is clearly provided otherwise. For this purpose, clearances bearing brand name or trade name of third parties are concerned, they are kept outside scheme inasmuch as they are not to be included for purposes of determining aggregate value of clearances for home consumption; and such products bearing brand names or trade names of third parties, even if manufactured by SSI Unit, are not eligible for any exemption and excise duty thereupon has to be paid. Hence, so far as manufacture of branded goods of third party on job work basis by SSI Unit is concerned, they are to be dealt with differently in sense that they do not come within ambit of exemption on which normally excise duty, as per provisions of Act, is payable. Further, once excise duty is paid by manufacturer on such branded goods manufactured, brand name whereof belongs to another person, on job work basis, SSI Unit would be entitled to CENVAT/MODVAT credit on inputs which were used for manufacture of such goods as on those inputs also excise duty was paid. Hence, branded goods manufactured by SSI Units meant for third parties are regulated by normal provisions of excise law and will have no bearing or relevance insofar as availing benefit of those exemption notifications in respect of its own products manufactured by SSI Units is concerned. Hence, impugned order passed by Tribunal is justified and in accordance with law. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - Assessee is not eligible to avail benefit of exemption of payment of duty when same is not provided by exemption notification.
