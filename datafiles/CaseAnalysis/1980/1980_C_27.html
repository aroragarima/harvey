Empire Jute Co. Ltd. v Commissioner Of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, V.D. Tulzapurkar, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 301; (1980) 4 SCC 25; (1980) SCC (Tax) 335; AIR 1980 SC 1946; 1980 (17) CTR 113; [1980] 124 ITR 1; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 1370; 1980 TAXLR 1092; [1980] 3 TAXMAN 69

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10(2)(xv) - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 37 - Deduction u/s. 10(2)(xv) - Right from 1939, the demand of jute in the world market was rather lean and with a view to adjusting the production of the mills to the demand in the world market, a working time agreement was entered into between the members of the Association restricting the number of working hours per week, for which the mills shall be entitled to work their looms - There was also provision of transfer of working hours according to which company which is not utilizing permitted working hour can transfer same to the other companies - Assessee bought said working hours from another company and claimed deduction in respect of same u/s. 10(2)(xv) of 1922 Act - Said claim was disallowed by the Income Tax Officer - On appeal, Assistant Commissioner accepted the claim and allowed the deduction on the ground that the assessee did not acquire any capital asset when it purchased the loom hours and the amount spent by it was incurred for running the business or working it with a view to producing day-to-day profits and it was part of operating cost or revenue cost of production - Tribunal upheld the order of the Asst. Commissioner - HC overturned the decision of the Tribunal and held that the amount paid by the assessee for purchase of the loom hours was in the nature of capital expenditure and was, therefore, not deductible u/s. 10(2) (xv) of the 1922 Act - Hence, present appeal - Whether amount paid by the assessee for purchase of the loom hours was in the nature of capital expenditure? - Held, no - The payment made by the assesses for the purchase of loom hours was expenditure laid out as part of the process of profit earning - It was an outlay of a business in order to carry it on and to earn profit out of the expense as an expense of carrying it on - It was part of the cost of operating the profit earning apparatus and was clearly in the nature of revenue expenditure and was allowable as a deduction u/s. 10 (2) (xv) of 1922 Act - Impugned order set aside - Appeal allowed.
