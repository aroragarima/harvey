Matru alias Girish Chandra v State of Utttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

03 March 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 275; (1971) 2 SCC 75; (1971) SCC (Cr) 391; AIR 1971 SC 1050; 1971 CRLJ 913; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 914

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Approver, Admissibility, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 302 and 382 - Murder - Appellant convicted for offence u/ss. 302 and 34 IPC, 1860 and u/s. 382 IPC, 1860 for former offence - Whether conviction if appellant is sustainable? - Identification tests do not constitute substantive evidence - Identification parade was delayed and delay not satisfactory explained - Mere absconding by itself does not necessarily lead to firm conclusion of guilty mind - Act of absconding is no doubt relevant piece of evidence to be considered along with other evidence but its value would always depend on circumstances of each case - Determining link in completing chain of circumstantial evidence which must admit of no other reasonable hypothesis than that of guilt of accused - No recovery of weapon of offence - No strains of blood in appellants clothes - Held, cumulative effect of circumstantial evidence falls far short of test required for sustaining conviction - Appellant conviction is set-aside - Appeal allowed.
