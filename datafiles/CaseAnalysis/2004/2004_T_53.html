K.C. Builders & Anr. v The Assistant Commissioner Of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 74; (2004) 2 SCC 731; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1092; 2004 (1) CCR 207; 2004 (2) Crimes 48; 2004 (186) CTR 721; [2004] 265 ITR 562; JT 2004 (2) SC 100; 2004 (1) KLT 596; 2004(2) SCALE 137; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 1134; 2004 (1) SLT 896B; 2004 (1) Supreme 1064; [2004] 135 TAXMAN 461

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Penalty levied cancelled by ITAT - High Court dismissing criminal revision filed by assessee to quash criminal proceedings before Magistrate - held, unjustified - finding of the appellate Tribunal - conclusive - prosecution cannot be sustained - penalty after having been cancelled by the complainant - following ITAT order - no offence survives under the Income Tax Act - quashing of prosecution is automatic.
