Vithalbhai Private Limited v Union Bank of India

Supreme Court of India

11 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 325; (2005) 4 SCC 315; AIR 2005 SC 1891; 2005 (3) ALD(SC) 6; 2005 (59) ALR 276; 2005 (2) AWC 1023; JT 2005 (3) SC 278; 2005 (2) KLT 491; 2005 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 125; 2005 (2) MLJ(SC) 185; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 124; 2005 (1) RCR(Rent) 357; 2005(3) SCALE 132; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 680; 2005 (Supp) RD 235

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: [A.] Rent Control - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, s. 111 - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 3 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7 r. 11, ss. 80, 9 - Specific Relief Act, 1877, s. 42 - Kerala Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1965, s. 11(3) - Lease of immovable property - Whether suit was premature on date of its institution and hence whether it is maintainable? - Held, act of plaintiff filing suit before 25/6/1984 cannot be said to be malicious or intended to overreach Court - Defendant's reply prompted plaintiff in filing suit inasmuch as plaintiff reasonably thought that a cloud was already cast on his entitlement to recover property and he should promptly approach Court - Single Judge rightly overruled defendant's objection and directed suit to be decreed - Appeal allowed.
