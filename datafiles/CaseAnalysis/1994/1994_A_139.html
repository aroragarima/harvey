Oswal Agro Mills Limited v Asstt. C.C.E.

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1452; (1994) 2 SCC 546; 1994 (51) ECR 330; 1994 (70) E.L.T. 48; JT 1994 (1) SC 610; 1994(1) SCALE 552; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 650

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise And Salt Act, 1944, s. 11-B - Whether it can be said that furnishing of a bank guarantee for all or part of disputed excise duty pursuant to an order of court is equivalent to payment of amount of excise duty? - Bank guarantee is security for revenue, that in event revenue succeeds its dues will be recoverable, being backed by guarantee of a bank - Amount of disputed tax or duty that this is secured by a bank guarantee cannot be held to be paid to revenue - There is no question of its refund and s. 11-B is not attracted - Held, bank guarantees given by appellants were not properly subject-matter of writ petition before High Court and HC was in error in directing appellants to renew same - First and second respondents directed to forthwith repay to State Bank of Patiala, Overseas Branch, Millar Gang, Ludhiana, amount of Rs. 1, 18, 00, 000 collected upon bank guarantees within two weeks - Petition dismissed.
