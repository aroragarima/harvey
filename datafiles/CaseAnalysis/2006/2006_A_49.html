State of Gujarat and Another v Akshay Arutlal Thakkar

Supreme Court of India

17 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1383; (2006) 2 SCC 309; AIR 2006 SC 943; 2006 (1) ESC 64; 2006 (2) G.L.R. 1562; JT 2006 (1) SC 417; 2006(1) SCALE 299; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 439; 2006 (2) SLR 1; 2006 (1) Supreme 325

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Bombay Home Guards Act, 1947, Bombay Home Guards (Extension and Amendment) Act, 1958, Bombay Home Guards Rules, 1953

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Bombay Home Guards Act, 1947, ss. 2,6B (1A) - Bombay Home Guards Rules, 1953 - Violation of guidelines - Orders of disengagement - Justifiability - Respondents were working as Home Guards and Home Guards Commandants were without legal sanction and orders of engagement of respondents were passed in purported in exercise of powers conferred u/s. 2 of the 1947 Act - Subsequently respondents activities were in violation of guidelines contained in Govt. Resolution No. HGD/1078/5355/F dt. 3-9-1997, of Home Department, orders of disengagement were passed - Respondent in civil appeal no. 2456 of 1998 filed writ application challenging order of disengagement, which was dismissed by Single Judge - Respondent in connected appeal had filed writ application before DB, which was allowed - Held, Act of the Rules did not empower Govt. to direct termination of services of any member of Home Guard or all members of Home Guards as was sought to be done by decision of Govt. on dt.2-12-1995, relied on in Anirudhsinhji Karansinhji Jadeja and Anr. v. State of Gujarat 1995 Indlaw SC 1354 - Hence, instant appeals - Whether order of DB was justifiable - Held, as per s. 2(2) of the 1947 Act makes it clear that State Govt. was appointing authority so far as Commandants and Commandant General were concerned was State Govt. and Commandants have power to appoint Home Guards - Hence, s. 6B(1-A) empowers Commandant to discharge any member of Home Guards at any time if opinion of Commandant services of such member were no longer required and provision did necessarily refer to discharge of member from Home Guards - Respondents in instant case were not sought to be discharged as members of Home Guards. Under Notification in question, discharge was from duties as District Commandant, Home Guards - Therefore, State Govt. was superior authority in matter of appointment and judgment of DB was set aside - Appeals allowed.
