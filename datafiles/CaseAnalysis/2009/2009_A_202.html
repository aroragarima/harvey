Ashok K. Jha And Others v Garden Silk Mills And Another

Supreme Court of India

28 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1172; (2009) 10 SCC 584; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 233; 2009(12) SCALE 160; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 886; 2010 (4) SLR 127

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cooperative, Hours Of Work

Summary: (A) Practice & Procedure - Bombay High Court Letters of Patent, cl.15 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.226, 227 - Special Civil Application - Order by Single Judge - Letters Patent Appeal before DB - Maintainability - If petitioner has invoked jurisdiction of HC for issuance of certain writ u/art.226, although art.227 also mentioned and principally judgment appealed against falls u/art.226, the appeal would be maintainable - Held, arts. 226 and 227 mentioned in cause title of writ petition filed by respondents - Writ petition not confined to supervisory jurisdiction but writ jurisdiction of HC also - Therefore, judgment of Single Judge traceable to art. 226, hence Letters Patent Appeal before DB maintainable.P /&gt;(B) Labour & Industrial Law - Bombay Industrial Relations Act, 1946 - Inter Departmental Transfer - Requirement of prior notice - Transfer of appellant employees from Crimping Department to Twisting Department - Failure to give notice of change u/s. 41(1) - Application filed before Labor Court by appellant - Dismissal of application - Appeal before Industrial Court - Appeal allowed by Industrial Court - Writ Petition before HC by respondent employer - Writ Petition dismissed by HC, holding that transfer amounts to illegal change, hence notice u/s. 41(1) to be given - Letters Patent Appeal filed by respondent before DB - Orders of Single judge and Industrial court set aside and restored Labor Court's order by DB - Legality - Held, no evidence led by appellants with regard to reduction of workers in Crimping Department and increment in Twisting Department - Appellants failed to led evidence that there is a basic difference in nature of machines in Crimping Department and Twisting Department and that workers are not trained to work at Twisting Machines - Therefore, notice u/s. 41(1) not required to be given in the present inter departmental transfer, hence order of DB upheld - Appeal dismissed.
