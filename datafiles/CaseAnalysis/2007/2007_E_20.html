Karan Singh v Executive Engineer Haryana State Marketing Board

Supreme Court of India

28 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 962; (2007) 14 SCC 291; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 989; 2007 (4) AWC 4111; JT 2007 (12) SC 141; 2007 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2883; 2008 (1) LLJ 289; 2007(11) SCALE 577; 2007 (7) SCJ 381; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 425; 2007 (6) SLR 674; 2008 (1) UPLBEC 741

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Daily Rate, Reemployment

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 10(1) - Termination of appellant - Writ petition filed by the appellant was dismissed on the ground that the demand notice had been raised after six years - Appeal against - Whether the reference of the Petitioner/workman could be rejected on the sole ground of delay when Government itself made reference for adjudication of the issue/ dispute? - Held, Tribunal had refused to grant any relief on the ground of delay - Tribunal has no authority to invalidate the reference, particularly when it has found that the order of termination violates s. 25F - Because of long passage of time, it would be inappropriate, particularly when appellant has not even offered any semblance of explanation for the delay - Directed that respondent-Board shall pay a sum of Rs.60, 000/- within a period of six weeks in full and final settlement of appellant's entitlements - Appeal partly allowed.
