Chaturbhuj Panda and Others v Collector, Raigarh

Supreme Court of India

23 July 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 433; AIR 1969 SC 255; 1969 ALJ 159; 1969 JabLJ 495; 1969 KerLJ 212; 1969 (1) SCJ 344; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 412

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3 and 167 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 3(a), 23(1) and 23(2) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 99 and 35 - (A) Determination of Valuation of trees for purpose of acquisition of land - Held, 15 percent allowance to be provided on value of land for purpose of determining market value of land - Ascertainment of value of trees is for determining market value of land under acquisition proceedings - (B) Whether after hearing arguments, court can have recourse to documents which are not part of records? - Held, no - It would be treated as fresh evidence and parties should be enabled to rebut them - (C) Appreciation of oral evidence in civil cases - In assessing value of evidence Judges are to call in aid their experience of life and test evidence on basis of probabilities - Evidence of only one party even when no evidence of rebuttal is led by opposite party need not necessarily be accepted - Oral evidence to be tested on basis of probabilities - Evidence need not necessarily be accepted when there has been no rebuttal by other party - (D)Enhancement of Compensation on basis of inadmissible evidence - Appellant cannot question it as otherwise compensation would have remained what had been granted by Land Acquisition officer - (E)Whether enhanced costs needs to be awarded ? - Held, no, because it is a highly exaggerated claim by claimant - Appeal partly allowed.
