Achutrao Haribhau Khodwa and Others v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 244; (1996) 2 SCC 634; 1996 ACJ 505; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 261; AIR 1996 SC 2377; 1997 (1) Bom.C.R. 571; 1996 (1) CCC 358; 1996 (1) CLT 532; 1996 (4) CTJ 950; JT 1996 (2) SC 624; 1996 (2) MLJ(SC) 105; 1996(2) SCALE 328; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 881; 1996 (2) SLR 29; 1996 (2) Supreme 751

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Consumer Protection; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Tort - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Death due to negligence in hospital - Vicarious liability of Govt. as to damages for tort committed by staff of Govt. medical hospital - Mop left in body of patient during operation formation pus which lead to death - Negligence cannot be contributed to particular staff - Held, doctrine of res ipsa loquitur clearly applies and State is liable to pay damages - Further held that absence of conclusive proof as to which of doctors or members of staff acted negligently is immaterial - Appeal allowed.
