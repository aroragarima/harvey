Rajasthan State Electricity Board Now, Ajmer Vidyut Vitran Nigam and others v Navin Kumar Saini and another

Supreme Court of India

29 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 919; (2010) 15 SCC 17; 2011 (1) AWC 867; 2010 (5) ESC 625; 2010 (127) FLR 819; JT 2010 (11) SC 427; 2010 (4) LLJ 853; 2010(11) SCALE 549; [2010] 12 S.C.R. 1173

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Regularisation - Respondents along with other persons were appointed as Helpers in work-charge establishment on 28.11.1979 for a period of three months - However, the respondents were allowed to continue in service after the expiry of the aforesaid period of three months - Later, respondents and other candidates participated in a limited competitive examination held by the Rajasthan State Electricity Board (Board) for appointment to the post of Junior Clerk and were appointed in the regular pay scale of the Junior Clerks on 26.06.1984 - Thereafter, Board regularised the persons, who joined as work charge employees like the respondents, w.e.f 1.04.1982 and were given regular pay scale of the post of Lower Division Clerk - Accordingly, respondents claimed the scale of pay of Junior Clerk on the plea that though they were engaged as helpers but they performed the work of Junior Clerk - Also regular pay scale of post of Junior Clerk from 1.04.1982 to 26.06.1984 was sought on the ground that persons junior to them were given the said scale of pay w.e.f 1.04.1982 - Claim was rejected by Board - Industrial Tribunal held that Board was correct in not regularising the services of the respondents on the post of Junior Clerk but directed that respondents shall be entitled to the wages of the Junior Clerk - Writ petition was filed thereagainst wherein Single Judge directed the Board to notionally grant the benefit of the pay of the post of Lower Division Clerk w.e.f 1.12.1979 with a rider that the respondents shall not be entitled to receive actual arrears of the said period - Present appeals - (A) Whether respondents were entitled for the scale of pay of the Junior Clerk from the date of their engagement as helpers on the principle of equal pay for equal work? - Held, plea of equal pay for equal work was founded on art. 14 of the Constitution, hence it was incumbent upon respondents to establish that they were performing the work of the Junior Clerk under orders of a competitive authority - Nothing on record by the respondents to show that they were asked to perform the function of a Junior Clerk by any competent authority - Further, respondents were admittedly engaged as helpers in work charge establishment and in that view of the matter for applying the principle of equal pay for equal work, mere the volume of work, shall not be relevant, there being qualitative difference as regards the liability and responsibility - Respondents not entitled for the scale of pay of the Junior Clerk even on notional basis from the date of their engagement as helpers - (B) Whether respondents entitled for scale of Junior Clerk from 1.04.1982? - Held persons junior to the respondents were given the regular pay scale of the post of Junior Clerk w.e.f 1.04.1982 whereas the respondents were appointed as Junior Clerks by order dtd. 26.06.1984 - Respondents cannot be allowed to suffer only because they qualified in written examination and appointed as Junior Clerks by order dtd. 26.06.1984 - Appellants directed to consider the cases of respondents for regularization as Junior Clerks w.e.f 1.04.1982 - Appeals partly allowed.
