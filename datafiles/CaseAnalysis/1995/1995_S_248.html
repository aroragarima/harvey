Ghaziabad Development Authority And Ors . v Sri Vikram Chaudhary And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

14 July 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1693; (1995) 5 SCC 210; (1995) SCC (L&S) 1226; AIR 1995 SC 2325; 1995 ALJ 1786; 1995 (31) ATC 129; 1995 (3) AWC 1596; 1995 (2) CLR 595; 1995 (71) FLR 462; JT 1995 (5) SC 636; 1995 LabIC 2474; 1995 (2) LLJ 703; 1995 (2) LLN 633; 1995(4) SCALE 545; 1995 (3) SCJ 390; 1995 (3) SLJ 239; 1995 (4) SLR 582; [1995] Supp2 S.C.R. 171; 1995 (3) UPLBEC 1415

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 25 F - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 and 14 - Writ jurisdiction - Natural justice - Contingent employees engaged on daily wages by Development Authority - For its projects - Claim - To regularise and pay on par with regular employees - (A) Whether High Court direction - To follow principles in ss. 25 F and 25 G of Industrial Disputes Act - Laid down that Development Authority is industry - (B) Whether negating relief, is consistent with principles of Natural Justice - Held HC did not intend to lay down that Development Authority is industry - Direction of HC, consistent with principles of natural Justice and equity, Justice and good conscience.
