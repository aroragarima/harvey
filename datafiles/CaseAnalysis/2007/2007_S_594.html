Alpesh Navinchandra Shah v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 239; (2007) 1 SCC (Cr) 653; (2007) 2 SCC 777; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 570; 2007 (210) E.L.T. 13; 2007 (1) JCC 843; JT 2007 (3) SC 630; 2007 (2) RCR(Criminal) 180; 2007(3) SCALE 598; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 223; 2008 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 168

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974 - Petition for issuance of a Writ of Habeas Corpus or any other appropriate writ quashing and setting aside the order of detention - Held, preventive detention prohibits smugglers from indulging in further smuggling activities - Investigation reveals consistent involvement of petitioner - detenue and his brother in smuggling activities, therefore, the Detaining Authority on the basis of evidence placed before him felt it necessary to issue the detention orders - Petitioner's brother has already been released on the ground there was no sufficient cause for the detention of the detenu - Petitioner who is also similarly placed and who has also paid the customs duty etc. pursuant to the order of the Settlement Commission and got the unconditional immunity by the Settlement Commission is entitled to the same treatment - Detention order quashed - Order accordingly.
