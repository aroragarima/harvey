Mohd. Mehtab Khan & Ors v Khushnuma Ibrahim & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

24 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 44; (2013) 9 SCC 221; AIR 2013 SC 1099; 2013 (3) ALD(SC) 64; 2013 (97) ALR 520; 2013 (2) AWC 2094; 2013 (1) CLT(SC) 166; 2013 (1) CTC 743; JT 2013 (2) SC 522; 2014 (2) MahLJ 150; 2014 (2) MPLJ 19; 2013 (171) PLR 1; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 295; 2013 (119) RD 354; 2013(1) SCALE 731; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 359; 2014 (1) WLN(SC) 100

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 6 - Self Acquired - Gift Deed - Illegal dis-possession - Repossession - Sustainability - Plaintiffs-respondent filed suit u/s. 6 of Act, along with interim application to possession contending that that suit properties were self-acquired properties of her husband and that he had gifted suit flat in her favour and a general power of attorney was executed for suit office in her favor and that when she was away for funeral of her husband defendants-appellants illegal took possession of properties - Defendants pleaded that they were not in possession of suit flat and instead were staying in house of plaintiff No. 1 and that defendants were in possession of flat and also that plaintiff was no in possession of office but was functioning from other office - Trial Judge held that there were inconsistencies and improbabilities in case of plaintiffs which needed to be established in trial of suit, accordingly, interim relief of direction to be put back in possession, as claimed by plaintiffs, was declined - Aggrieved plaintiffs appeal before appellate court which held that various household and electronic goods found in suit flat during inspection carried out by Receiver were proved to have been purchased by plaintiffs and also the application form submitted for admission was signed by deceased giving address of suit office and flat - Appellate Court found it fit for reversal of refusal of interim relief to plaintiffs and granted interim relief in appeal - Hence instant appeal - Whether appellate court was justified in reversing dismissal orders of trial court and granting interim relief to plaintiffs - Held, appellate court could not have interfered with exercise of discretion by Trial Judge unless such exercise was found to be palpably incorrect or untenable, reasons that weighed with Trial Judge, as already noticed, did not indicate that view taken was not a possible view, appellate Court, therefore, should not have substituted its views in matter merely on ground that in its opinion facts of case called for a different conclusion, such an exercise was not correct parameter for exercise of jurisdiction while hearing an appeal against a discretionary order, it should not be understood that appellate court was wrong in its conclusions what was sought to be emphasized was that as long as view of Trial Court was a possible view appellate court should not have interfered with same following virtually settled principles of law - Hence, appellate Bench of HC was not correct in interfering with order passed by Trial Judge - Appeal disposed of.
