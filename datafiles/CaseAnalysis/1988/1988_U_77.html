Nyadar Singh and another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 569; (1988) 4 SCC 170; AIR 1988 SC 1979; 1988 (8) ATC 226; 1988 (57) FLR 512; JT 1988 (3) SC 448; 1989 LabIC 14; 1988 (2) LLJ 506; 1989 (1) LLN 10; 1988(2) SCALE 409; 1989 (1) SLJ 1; 1988 (4) SLR 271; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 546; 1988 (2) UJ 644

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 309 and 311 - Limitation Act, 1908 - Central Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1965, r. 11(vi) - Interpretation - Statutory language - Reduction of employee initially recruited to higher time scale, grade or service or post to post in lower time scale, grade, service or post never held by employee before - Difference between reversion and reduction in rank - Scope of penalty of reversion-permissibility, limitations - Held, all reversions are not reductions and person working in higher post purely on officiating basis for valid reasons, can be reverted to his substantive post - Not permissible, rule not imposing such limitations - They have to be imported in view of relevant interpretative factors - Appeal disposed of.
