State of Madhya Pradesh v S. B. Johari and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 584; (2000) 2 SCC 57; (2000) SCC (Cr) 311; AIR 2000 SC 665; AIR 2000 SCW 189; 2000 (1) CCR 93; 2000 (1) Crimes 165; 2000 CRLJ 944; 2000 (1) JCC 348; JT 2000 (1) SC 169; 2000(3) M.P.H.T. 164; 2000 (1) RCR(Criminal) 523; 2000(1) SCALE 138; 2000 (3) SCJ 324; 2000 (1) SLT 377; 2000 (1) Supreme 142

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 5 and 13 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120 B - Charges against respondents for offences punishable u/ss. 5(1)(d) and 5(2) of Act r/w s. 120-B IPC - Criminal conspiracy to defraud Govt. - High Court quashed charges against respondents in its revisional powers - Whether HC was correct in quashing charges against respondents? - It is settled law that at the stage of framing the charge, Court has to prima facie consider whether there is sufficient ground for proceeding against accused - Court is not required to appreciate evidence and arrive at conclusion that materials produced are sufficient or not for convicting accused - Charge can be quashed if evidence which prosecutor proposes to adduce to prove guilt of accused, even if fully accepted before it is challenged by cross-examination or rebutted by defence evidence, if any, cannot show that accused committed particular offence - In such case, there would be no sufficient ground for proceeding with trial - Held, entire approach of HC is erroneous and illegal - While quashing any proceedings, HC has to see whether prima facie case against accused is made out or not - HC should not appreciate evidence on record to conclude whether case is made out or not - Case of 'Niranhan Singh Karam Singh Punjabi' followed - HC order set-aside - Appeal allowed.
