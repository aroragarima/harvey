Dalmia Cement (Bharat) Limited and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2857; (1996) 10 SCC 104; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 221; AIR 1996 SCW 3652; JT 1996 (4) SC 555; 1996(4) SCALE 14; [1996] Supp1 S.C.R. 825; 1996 (5) Supreme 164

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Reserve Bank of India Act, Constitutionality, Special Bearer Bonds (Immunities and Exemptions) Act, 1981, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Jute Packaging Material (Compulsory Use in Packing Commodities) Act, 1987, Kerala Marine Fishing Regulation Act, 1980, Jute Packaging Materials (Compulsory Use in Packing Commodities) Rules, 1987

Summary: Constitution - Reserve Bank of India Act - Essential Commodities Act, 1955 - Jute Packaging Material (Compulsory Use in Packing Commodities) Act, 1987 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1) (g) and 301 - Constitution - (A) Whether the prohibition of 100% use of gunny bags by sugar industry and 70% by the cement industry is reasonable? - (B) Whether the Act serves the public purpose and the restrictions are reasonable? - Held - The Standing Advisory Committee, therefore, had properly advised and the Govt. obviously has taken decision to continue the policy of compulsory packing of commodities or class of commodities with jute bags, regulated u/s. 3 of the Act - The provisions of ss. 3, 4 and 5 are not violative of arts. 14 or 19(1)(g) of the Constitution, 1950 - The Act and orders impose reasonable restriction saved by art. 19(6) of the Constitution, 1950 - The commerce clause in art. 301, therefore, stands no impediment for free flow of trade and commerce in the commodities or class of commodities covered by the provisions of the Act - The Act is not violative of art. 301 of the Constitution, 1950 - Transfer cases and writ petitions are accordingly dismissed and the applications disposed of.
