P. Satyanarayana Murthy v District Inspector of Police and another

Supreme Court of India

14 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Amitava Roy, H.L. Dattu, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 821; (2015) 10 SCC 152; AIR 2015 SC 3549; 2015 CRLJ 4670; 2015 (1) JCC 2674; JT 2015 (8) SC 255; 2015 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3460; 2016 (1) RLW 595; 2016 (1) WLN(SC) 215

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Valuable Thing, gratification

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1)(d), 13(1)(d)(i), 13(1)(d)(ii), 13(2), 20, 7 - Bribery - Confirmation of Conviction - Challenged - Appellant allegedly demanded illegal gratification from complainant for effecting renewal of recognition of tying institute - Trial Court convicted appellants u/ss. 7 and 13(1)(d)(i) &(ii) r/w s, 13(2) of the Act and sentenced to under RI for one year on each count with fine of Rs. 1,000 in default to suffer SI for three months for each offence - HC in appeal confirmed conviction and sentence of accused u/ss. 13(1)(d)(i) &(ii) of the Act and acquitted accused of charge u/s. 7 of the Act - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the proof of demand of illegal gratification is the gravamen of the offence u/ss. 7 and 13(1) (d)(i)&(ii) of the Act and in absence thereof, unmistakably the charge therefore would fail. Mere acceptance of any amount allegedly by way of illegal gratification or recovery thereof, dehors the proof of demand, ipso facto, would thus not be sufficient to bring home the charge under these two sections of the Act. Further, suspicion, however grave, cannot take the place of proof and the prosecution cannot afford to rest its case in the realm of 'may be' true but has to upgrade it in the domain of 'must be' true in order to steer clear of any possible surmise or conjecture. The Court must ensure that miscarriage of justice is avoided and if in the facts and circumstances, two views are plausible, then the benefit of doubt must be given to the accused. In instant case, prosecution has failed to prove unequivocally, the demand of illegal gratification by appellant. Hence, impugned judgment and order of HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.
