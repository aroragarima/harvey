Commissioner of Income Tax, Mumbai v General Insurance Corporation

Supreme Court of India

25 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 985; (2006) 8 SCC 117; 2006 (205) CTR 280; [2006] 286 ITR 232; 2006(9) SCALE 514; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 561; [2006] 156 TAXMAN 96

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Companies Act, 1956, s. 81 - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 260 - Whether expenditure incurred in connection with issuance of bonus shares is a capital expenditure or revenue expenditure? - Held, issuance of bonus shares does not result in any inflow of fresh funds or increase in capital employed, capital employed remains same - Issuance of bonus shares by capitalization of reserves is merely a reallocation of company's fund - If that be so, then it cannot be held that company has acquired a benefit or advantage of enduring nature - Total funds available with company will remain same and issue of bonus shares will not result in any change in capital structure of company - Issue of bonus shares does not result in expansion of capital base of company expenditure on issuance of bonus shares is revenue expenditure - Appeal dismissed.
