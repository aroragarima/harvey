Rajasthan State Road Transport Corporation v Alexix Sonier and another

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 923; 2015 ACJ 2682; 2015 (113) ALR 762; JT 2015 (9) SC 273; 2015 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4012; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 731; 2016 (2) RLW 1425; 2015(10) SCALE 377; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 564; 2016 (3) WLN(SC) 41

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Sustainability, Abkari policy, FL-3 policy

Summary: Excise - Abkari Act, 1077 - Punjab Excise Act, 1914 - Abkari policy - FL-3 policy - Sustainability - Petitioners, Two star, Three star, four star and heritage hotels, challenged the Abkari policy for the year 2014-15 and amendments to the Foreign Liquor Rules - Single Judge dismissed the petitions and carved out an exception vis-a-vis the eligibility of Four Star and Heritage category hotels to the grant of FL-3 licence, finding their exclusion to be arbitrary and violative of Article 14 of the Constitution - On appeal by the state HC reversed the judgment of the single judge consequently only Five Star hotels are permitted to serve alcohol in their bars - Hence instant appeals.

Held, five Star hotels have opened out some of their premises for consumption of liquor not only at depressed rates but also in surroundings which are not commensurate to their Five Star ratings. This may be a good and sufficient reason to denude these Hotels of their Five Star gradation. Five Star hotels violating the ambiance which they portray by enabling drinking in specially created bars at lower prices, the unregulated permission to consume beer and wine throughout the State by freely granting FL-11 licenses also is extremely difficult to appreciate. If the sale of beer and wine as a consequence of grant of FL-11 licenses discloses an increase or if there is a trend towards serving beer of a higher alcohol content, the State will have to review its stand, failing which it would inexorably invite further litigation. This curial warning also applies to any laxity in policing or ensuring that no person below the permissible age is allowed to consume alcohol in public. Additionally, it must be noted that thousands of workers at bars that lost their FL-3 licenses have been rendered unemployed as a result of the impugned policy, leading to over a dozen suicides. In this analysis it is unable to find reason or justification in accepting these Appeals. The impugned Judgment is founded on the strength of previous decisions of this Court. Appeals dismissed.
