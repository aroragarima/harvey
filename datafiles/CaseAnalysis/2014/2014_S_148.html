FOR EDUCATIONAL USE ONLY

Hakkim and others v State Represented by Deputy Superintendent of Police

Supreme Court of India

06 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 549; (2014) 13 SCC 427; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 3728; 2015 CRLJ 275; JT 2014 (9) SC 243; 2014(9) SCALE 226; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 978

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 452, 324, 307 and 302 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants allegedly committed offence of murder - Appellants were convicted by trial Court u/ss. 147, 148, 452, 324, 307 and 302 IPC - Hence instant appeal - Whether conviction of appellants u/ss. 147, 148, 452, 324, 307 and 302 IPC was sustainable -

Held, it was a case where the accused were apprehended on the spot and the recovery of the weapons was also carried out at the time when they were apprehended - The said factor could not be disputed in as much as apart from the eye witness account of injured witnesses PWs-1 to 4, the accused themselves were examined by the doctor PW-32 on 27-10-1996 at 4.35 p.m. - The injuries on their bodies were noted under Exhibits P-39 to 42 and according to PW32, at that point of time the accused themselves stated that they were thrashed by the public which was in tune with the case of the prosecution - It was also not in dispute that recovered knives were placed before the trial Court and marked as M.Os.2 to 5 - That apart, PW10, the post mortem doctor in her certificate Exhibit P-15 confirmed the multiple knife injuries found on the body of the deceased - While one of the injuries was on the neck which was attributed to A1, there were other injuries on the vital parts of the body as well as other parts numbering 13 and all of them were incised wounds ranging from 2cm x 1cm to 15cm x 7cm - Therefore, it was futile on the part of the appellant to contend that only one knife was used to cause so many injuries on the body of the deceased - That apart, according to PW-8, the doctor who examined PWs1 to 4 and issued Exhibits P-6 to P-9 certifying the injuries - Exhibits P-6 to P-9 revealed that PW1 suffered one grievous injury, PW2 suffered one cut injury on the back apart from one abrasion, PW3 suffered two simple injuries and PW4 suffered six cut injuries of which 1 to 3 were simple and 4 to 6 were grievous - One of the injuries suffered by PW4 resulted in severance of her right hand ring finger - When such extensive injuries were sustained by the injured eye witnesses, it was too late in the day for the appellant to contend and for the Court to accept that only one knife was used and the placement of the other three knives could not have been relied upon - Trial Court as well as the HC rightly rejected the submissions as such overwhelming evidence was available on record to support the case of the prosecution as regards the use of multiple weapons in the crime committed by the appellants - Once the said contention of the appellants was rejected, the other contentions, namely, that there was no scope to invoke ss. 109 and 149, IPC would also fall to the ground - If that was the outcome of the above discussion, there was no scope to find fault with the ultimate conclusion of the trial court having convicted the appellants for the offences found proved against them for which the sentence came to be imposed - SC, therefore, did not find any substance in the submission made on behalf of the appellants to hold that only s. 304 Part II, IPC could be applied and a lesser punishment should be imposed - Having regard to the extensive use of the weapons by the accused in the process of killing of the deceased and the inflicting of the injuries on PWs1 to 4, we do not find any scope to show any concession in the matter of punishment and consequently the said submission stands rejected - SC did not find any scope to refer to any of the decisions relied upon for reduction of sentence - Appeals dismissed.
