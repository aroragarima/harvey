Gopal Sardar v Karuna Sardar

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 192; (2004) 4 SCC 252; AIR 2004 SC 3068; 2004 (2) CHN 164; JT 2004 (Supp2) SC 554; 2004(3) SCALE 36; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 826; 2004 (2) SLT 428

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: West Bengal Land Reforms (Amendment) Act, 1972

Summary: [A.] Practice & Procedure - Land & Property - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 115, 115-A - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 227 - West Bengal Land Reforms (Amendment) Act, 1972 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, ss. 417, 417(4) - West Bengal Land Reforms Act, s.8 - Section 5 of Limitation Act is not attracted to proceedings initiated u/s. 8 of West Bengal Act - Right conferred u/s. 8 is a statutory right - West Bengal Act is a self- Contained Code inasmuch as Act provides to enforce rights of pre-emption, forum is provided, procedure is prescribed, remedies including appeals and revisions are provided, penalties are indicated for non-compliance of orders and powers are given for restoration of land - Further period of limitation is also specifically prescribed to make an application u/s. 8 of Act and for preferring appeals or revisions under provisions of Act - If in same Act, consciously and expressly, Legislature has made provision for application of Section 5 of Limitation Act or its principles expressly and specifically to other proceedings such as appeal or revision etc and such a provision is not made for initiation of proceedings u/s. 8 of Act - Necessarily follows that Legislature did not intend to give benefit of Section 5 to Limitation Act.
