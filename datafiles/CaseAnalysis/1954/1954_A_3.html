Supreme Court of India

11 February 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Vivian Bose, Ghulam Hasan

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 61; AIR 1954 SC 245; 1954 ALJ 280; 1954 (2) LLJ 678; 1954 SCJ 300; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 786

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Bihar and Orissa Services Code

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950 - Bihar and Orissa Services Code, r. 95 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 60 - Government of India Act, 1935, s. 240(3) - (A) Whether the High Court correctly held that r. 95 of Bihar and Orissa Services Code had no application to the case? - S. 240(3) states that no person shall be dismissed from service by an authority subordinate to that by which he was appointed - The Chief Secy. to the Govt. of Bihar, realising the untenability of the Govt.'s position, wrote to the Inspector-General of Police that the order of dismissal should be treated as null and void and that the respondent should be reinstated - Thus, the HC was correct in its decision - (B) Whether a suit for arrears of salary by a civil servant is competent in a civil court? - It is difficult to see on what grounds the claim that the Crown cannot be sued for arrears of salary directly by the civil servant, though his creditor can take it, can be based or substained - What could be claimed in England by a petition of right can be claimed in this country by ordinary process - Held, appeal dismissed.
