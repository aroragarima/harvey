Smt. Nagindra Bala Mitra and Another v Sunil Chandra Roy and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 421; AIR 1960 SC 706; 1960 CRLJ 1020; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Medical Witness, Misdirection, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 162, 297 and 441 A - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 323, 325 and 477 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 134, 134(1)(c) and 136 - (A) Whether charge by jury at third trial is so defective that it has led to a manifestly erroneous verdict, resulting in a failure of justice? - Held, charge to jury cannot be said to be a proper charge on any principle or precedent, and that verdict cannot be accepted - There no gross misdirection by Judge - Jury was, therefore, properly placed in possession of views of both doctors, and it was for them to decide which view should be accepted - (B) Whether verdict is vitiated by reason of any serious misdirection by Judge or of any misunderstanding on part of jury of law laid down by him, which in fact has occasioned a failure of justice? - Held, judge must decide in each case whether recitals intended to be used for contradiction satisfied requirements of law - Therefore, the Court did not think that failure to direct jury that it was open to them to return a verdict of guilty on a minor offence occasioned any failure of justice - Appeal dismissed.
