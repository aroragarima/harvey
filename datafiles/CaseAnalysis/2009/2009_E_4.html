State of Andhra Pradesh v E. Satyanarayana

Supreme Court of India

30 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1916; (2009) 14 SCC 400; 2009(6) SCALE 638; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 206

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Extra Judicial Confession, Tainted Evidence

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 24 - Murder - Extra judicial confession - Trial Court convicted respondent u/s. 302, IPC for alleged commission of murder of his wife and minor son - In appeal, HC acquitted respondent - Present appeal - Prosecution case rested on the alleged extra judicial confession made by respondent before the Village Administrative Officer - Held, before relying on the alleged confession, the court has to be satisfied that it is voluntary and is not the result of threat or promise envisaged u/s. 24 of the Evidence Act or was brought about in suspicious circumstances to circumvent ss. 25 and 26 of the Evidence Act - In present case, HC analysed the evidence and came to the conclusion that the extra judicial confession has not been established - Judgment of HC does not suffer from any infirmity to warrant interference - Appeal dismissed.
