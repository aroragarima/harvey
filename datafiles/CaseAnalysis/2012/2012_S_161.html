Bhimanna v State Of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

04 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 282; (2012) 9 SCC 650; AIR 2012 SC 3026; 2012 (4) JCC 2667; JT 2012 (8) SC 298; 2013 (3) KarLJ 323; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 533; 2012 (4) RLW 3514; 2012(8) SCALE 457; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 909

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 302, 304 Part-I, 447 and 504 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Appellants (A1, A2 and A3) allegedly assaulted deceased - Deceased succumbed to the injuries sustained in the assault - Trial court convicted A2 for the offences punishable u/ss. 447, 504, 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC - However, A1 and A3 were only convicted u/ss. 447, 504 r/w s. 34 of IPC - HC dismissed appeal of A2 and allowed the appeal of the State convicting A1 and A3 also u/s. 302 of IPC - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether impugned order of the HC could be upheld - Held, there was ample evidence on record particularly the deposition of PW1 (Wife of deceased) to show that when deceased fell down after receiving the said injuries, the appellants stopped the assault - A2 threw down the wood and all the appellants left the place of occurrence saying that the deceased had fallen - This clearly establishes that the appellants did not intend to kill the deceased and it all happened in the spur of the moment upon a heated exchange of words between the parties, after criminal trespass by the appellants on to the land of the deceased - Therefore, it does not seem to be a pre-determined or pre-meditated case - Ends of justice would, therefore, be met, if all the appellants were convicted u/s. 304 Part-I r/w s. 34 IPC and sentences were awarded accordingly - As a result, all the appellants were convicted u/ss. 447, 504 and 304 Part-I r/w s. 34 IPC - Appeals disposed of.
