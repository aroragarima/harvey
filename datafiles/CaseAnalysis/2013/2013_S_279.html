Gulam Sarbar v State of Bihar (Now Jharkhand)

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 669; (2014) 3 SCC 401; 2014 CRLJ 34; 2013(12) SCALE 504; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B and 302 - Criminal conspiracy - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Trial Court convicted appellants u/ss. 302 r/w s. 120-B of IPC alongwith other accused - Appeal filed thereagainst was dismissed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether conviction of appellants was correct - Held, appellants did not furnish any satisfactory explanation of the circumstances under which they were present at the place of occurrence - Also, manner in which they fled away after the commission of the crime clearly indicated their involvement in the offence to conduct a conspiracy - Further, HC reappreciated the evidence and upheld the findings of facts recorded by the Trial Court observing that the ocular evidence was in consonance and in conformity with the medical evidence and it was a clear cut case of conspiracy - HC rightly observed that normally the perpetrator of crime in a case of conspiracy did not take part in the execution rather such conspirator hired some criminal directly or indirectly to execute the evil design planned by him - Prosecution successfully established the involvement of the appellants in the crime and the manner in which the crime had been committed established the conspiracy - Hence, not interference required in the impugned judgment - Appeals dismissed.
