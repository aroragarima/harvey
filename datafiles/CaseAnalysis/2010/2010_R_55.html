Neeti Malviya v Rakesh Malviya

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 429; (2010) 6 SCC 413; 2010 (81) ALR 237; 2010 (4) AWC 3679; 2011 (3) Bom.C.R. 196; 2010 (4) CTC 733; 2010 (3) G.L.R. 2558; JT 2010 (6) SC 189; 2010 (8) MLJ(SC) 579; 2010 (3) RCR(Civil) 249; 2010(6) SCALE 49; [2010] 6 S.C.R. 1103

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Family & Personal

Summary: Constitution - Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 13-B(2) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 142 - Waiving of statutory period of six months in divorce petition by mutual consent - Jurisdiction of u/art. 142 - Whether the period of second motion in terms of sub-section (2) of s. 13-B of the Act can be waived or reduced by SC? - Held, SC in Anil Kumar Jain Vs. Maya Jain, 2009 INDLAW SC 1494 held that neither the civil courts nor even the HCs can pass orders before the period prescribed in s. 13-B(2) of the Act has expired - SC opined that it is only SC, in exercise of its extraordinary powers u/art. 142 of the Constitution, that can grant relief to the parties without even waiting for statutory period of six months stipulated in s. 13-B of the Act - However, recently in Manish Goel v. Rohini Goel, 2010 INDLAW SC 301 and Smt. Poonam Vs. Sumit Tanwar, 2010 INDLAW SC 198 observed that u/art. 142 of the Constitution, SC cannot altogether ignore the substantive provisions of the statute and pass orders concerning an issue which can be settled only through a mechanism prescribed in a statute - In the light of certain observations in the said decisions, it would be appropriate to refer the matter to a Bench of three Judges in order to have a clear ruling on the issue for future guidance - Question that whether the period prescribed in sub-section (2) of s. 13-B of the Act can be waived or reduced by SC in exercise of its jurisdiction u/art. 142 of the Constitution referred Bench of three Judges for the consideration - Order accordingly.
