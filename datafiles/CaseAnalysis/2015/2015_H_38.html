Citibank N. A. v Hiten P. Dalal and others

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 615; (2016) 1 SCC 411; AIR 2015 SC 3523; 2015 (6) ALD(SC) 58; 2015(5) ALL MR 932; 2015 (6) ALT(SC) 1; 2015 (3) CLT(SC) 402; [2015] 192 Comp Cas 343; 2015 (4) CompLJ 494; 2016 (4) MahLJ 686; 2016 (3) MPLJ 295; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 269; 2015(9) SCALE 134; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 583

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Restitution, Bond, Privy Council, Half Yearly Interest

Summary: Banking & Finance - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, 144 - Restitution - In pursuance of Court orders, Citibank was required to restore back the monetary benefits it received under the decree against Canfina - However, as decree gave an option to Canfina, it opted to deliver to Citibank IRFC Bonds of face value of Rs.50 Crore on 13-8-1996 and, dispute arose between parties as to how market value of bonds be calculated for purpose of effective and satisfactory restitution - Later, after SC judgment on 12-7-2004 Canfina by letter to Citibank demanded Rs.135,18,28,053 by way of restitution - However, Citibank made its own calculations and tendered aggregate amount of Rs.107,75,40,141 to Canfina - Canfina declined to accept said offer and so, Citibank approached Special Court for depositing said sum - Special Court vide its impugned order disposed of applications and allowed additional sum of Rs.30,13,55,175 - Hence, instant appeals by Citibank assailing common judgment dismissing Citibank's miscellaneous application and allowing that preferred by Canfina - Whether impugned order is sustainable.

Held, in the course of finding out the value of the bonds which are no longer available for restitution, Special Court committed clear error of law in ignoring relevant fact that the bonds in question were a tradable community on the stock market and its value could be easily ascertained either on the date when the bonds were handed over to the Citibank or at the time when the Citibank sold the bonds to third parties. Further, sale of the bonds by Citibank to third parties at a verifiable rate not being under dispute, it is evidently unjust to saddle Citibank with liability to repay the possible gains made by the third party or subsequent purchasers of the bonds. Hence, amount determined by Special Court for restitution and payment by Citibank is unjust and is a result of error in not keeping under view the relevant facts as well as in applying the settled legal propositions for the purpose of compensating Canfina by way of restitution and so, the same is set aside. Further, as per chart submitted on behalf ofCitibank, Citibank is entitled to refund by Canfina as on 25-4-2005 to amount of Rs.22,14,36,756. Appeals allowed to such extent. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - For ascertaining the value of the property which is no longer available for restitution on account of sale etc., the Court should adopt a realistic and verifiable approach instead of resorting to hypothetical and presumptive value. Further, in the context of restitution the Court should keep under consideration not only the loss suffered by the party entitled to restitution but also the gain, if any, made by other party who is obliged to make restitution.
