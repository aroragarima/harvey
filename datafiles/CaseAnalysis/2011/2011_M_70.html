State Of Haryana & Ot Hers v Mahabir Vegetable Oils Pvt. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 111; (2011) 3 SCC 778; JT 2011 (2) SC 429; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 933; 2011(2) SCALE 577; [2011] 4 S.C.R. 944; [2011] 38 VST 514

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Withdrawal, Quantum, Haryana General Sales Tax Rules, 1975

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Haryana General Sales Tax Act, 1973, s. 64-A - Haryana Sales Tax Rules, 1975, r. 28A - Exemption - The State of Haryana announced an industrial policy for the period 1.4.1988 to 31.3.1997 wherein inter alia incentive by way of sales tax exemption was to be given for the industries set up in backward areas in the State - Schedule III appended to the Rules provides for a negative list of the industries and/or class of industries which were not to be included therein - At the initial stage the Solvent extraction plant was admittedly not included in the negative list - On 16.12.1996 substituting Schedule III appended to the Rules issued whereby and where under the solvent extraction plant was included therein - On 3.1.1996 the respondent purchased land to set up a solvent extraction plant and applied for no-objection certificate from the Haryana State Pollution Control Board - No-objection certificate wherefor was granted on 22.11.1996 and thereafter respondent made huge investment in the said plant - The respondent had applied for grant of exemption from payment of sales tax as on 16.12.1996 which was rejected on the ground that the solvent extraction plants were included in the negative list w.e.f. 16.12.1996 - The writ petition filed by the respondent before the HC was dismissed - The respondent approached SC who in Mahabir Vegetable Oils Private Limited and another vs. State of Haryana and Others 2006 INDLAW SC 273, held that it has neither been denied nor disputed that during the relevant period, namely, August 1996 to 16.12.1996 not only have they invested huge amounts but also the authorities of the State sanctioned benefits, granted permissions - Hence respondent was entitled to for grant of exemption from payment of sales tax as on 16.12.1996 - However, SC did not interfere with the issue of the quantum of exemption which can be granted to the respondent and the matter was remanded to the Director Industries for fresh adjudication - Subsequently, the Lower Level Screening Committee made a recommendation for grant of eligibility certificate to the extent of Rs.94,48,911/- for a period of nine years i.e. from 29.03.1997 to 28.03.2006 - The said amount was calculated with reference to the investment made by the petitioner up to 16.12.1996 i.e. date of amendment, putting the unit in the negative list - The respondent challenged the said order before the HC who held that once the respondent has been treated to be eligible for exemption, there was no valid reason to further classify the benefit of investment up to the date of amendment, putting the unit in the negative list - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether the respondent is entitled to the benefit of Sales Tax exemption on the entire investment made by them in setting up the industrial unit i.e. Solvent Extraction Plant, or on the investments made up till 16.12.1996 i.e. date of amendment, putting the unit in the negative list? - Held, an exemption is nothing but a freedom from an obligation which an assessee is otherwise liable to discharge - The beneficiary of a concession has no legally enforceable right against the government to grant a concession except to enjoy the benefits of the concession during the period of its grant - In the instant case, it cannot be said that the respondent had altered its position relying on the promise in as much as even before steps were taken by the respondent for laying the Solvent Extraction Plant, the Petitioner had made its intention clear that it was likely to amend the law/rules - Amendments in the terms of the said rules were notified on 16.12.1996 substituting Schedule III appended to the Rules whereby and where under the solvent extraction plant was included therein - Hence, respondent not entitled to the benefit of Sales Tax exemption on the entire investment made by them in setting up the industrial unit - Impugned judgment of HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
