NTPC Limited v State of Bihar and others

Supreme Court of India

14 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1889; (2004) 12 SCC 96

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Acquisition of land - Enhancement of compensation - Entitled to - Large tracts of lands were acquired for the purposes of constructing a thermal power station - Not being satisfied with the amounts awarded by the Land Acquisition Officer, 1710 references were filed - Out of this 704 references were decided - 1006 references were still pending - From the 704 references, which have already been decided, in 212 cases the compensation awarded by the Land Acquisition Officer was confirmed - However, in 492 cases the Reference Court had enhanced the compensation - Out of 492 cases in which compensation was enhanced respondent/State had filed appeals in 302 cases and no appeals were filed in 190 cases - It should be mentioned that the appellants, who were the body on whose behalf land was acquired, had made applications for being impleaded in two references - However, their applications for impleadment was dismissed on 4-9-1992 and 6-1-1993 on the ground that they were neither necessary nor proper party - Appellants then filed a writ petition in HC claiming that they were necessary and proper parties in all references and that they should be impleaded in all references which were pending - They further claimed that awards of the Reference Court by which compensation was enhanced should be quashed and that those cases be referred back to the Reference Court with a direction that the appellants be impleaded as a party - Single Judge of HC, by his judgment held that in all the pending references i.e. the 1006 cases the appellants were to be impleaded as a party if not already impleaded - It was further held that in 302 appeals, which were pending before the HC, the appellants could make an application for being impleaded - Single Judge of HC opined that if such applications were made, they were bound to be allowed unless and until it was shown that the appellants had notice of the reference proceedings and were not prejudiced - Single Judge of HC further gave liberty to the appellants to file appeals in the remaining 190 cases in which the Reference Court had enhanced the compensation - Delay in filing appeals was condoned - Appeal against the order was dismissed by the DB by the impugned order - Whether the appellants had a right to be impleaded in all references - Held, appellants were entitled to be impleaded in the reference proceedings, the HC was in error in not setting aside the awards and referring the cases back to the Reference Court with a direction that they be impleaded - Fact that amounts were enhanced, showed that prejudice was caused to the appellants - SC, therefore, set aside the impugned judgment except to the extent that it directed that in all pending references the appellants would be impleaded as parties - Awards made by the Reference Court in the 492 cases, where the amounts were enhanced, were set aside - These cases were remitted back to the Reference Court - Appellants should be deemed to have been brought on record in all these cases as well as all other pending cases - Reference Court should try to dispose of the references as expeditiously as possible - Statement of witnesses already recorded on behalf of the claimants should not be recorded afresh - However, the appellants should have a right to cross-examine the witnesses, if they could so desire - For that purpose those witnesses should be recalled - If, however, those witnesses could not be recalled for a valid reason e.g. because they had died or could not be found then their statements should not be excluded from consideration and should be read in evidence - All claimants should also be entitled to adduce further evidence, if they so desired - Appellants would be entitled to adduce evidence if they so desire - Appeal disposed of.
