State of Gujarat and Another v Suryakant Chunilal Shah

Supreme Court of India

03 December 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 898; (1999) SCC (L&S) 313; (1999) 1 SCC 529; 1999 (1) CLR 229; 1999 (1) GLH 193; JT 1998 (8) SC 326; 1999 (1) LLJ 265; 1999 (1) LLN 52; 1999 (2) SLJ 28; [1998] Supp3 S.C.R. 293

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, Art. 136 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 - Promotion - Complaint - Inquiry - Suspension - Reinstatement - Respondent/employee was promoted in office of Food Controller and certain complaints were received against him regarding permit for cement, which was issued illegally by him - Therefore, respondent was placed under suspension on completion of inquiry by appellants and FIR was filed against him - Respondent made applications to appellants for revocation of suspension order - By another order, respondent was compulsorily retired from service in public interest, which was passed u/r. 161 of 1959 Rule - Order was challenged by respondent in petition before HC, which was initially dismissed but was allowed in appeal - Appellants were aggrieved and approach this Court u/art. 136 of Constitution - Hence instant appeal - Whether appellant/employee had outlived his utility and was to be compulsorily retired in public interest for maintaining an efficient administration - Whether integrity of respondent was doubtful - Held, no adverse remarks in character roll entries against appellant integrity was not doubted at any time, character roll entries subsequent to respondent's promotion were not available, these things concluded that respondent was not a man of doubtful integrity nor he was a fit person to be retired compulsorily from service - Involvement of appellant in a criminal case did not mean that he was guilty - It would be highly improper to deprive a person of his livelihood merely on basis of his involvement - Punitive Order, was passed for collateral purpose of his immediate removal, rather than in public interest - Court opined that, HC was justified in setting aside order passed by Single Judge and directing reinstatement of respondent - Appeal dismissed.
