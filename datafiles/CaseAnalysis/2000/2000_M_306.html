R. Kanthimathi and Another v Mrs. Beatrice Xavier

Supreme Court of India

08 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3783; (2000) 9 SCC 339; AIR 2003 SC 4149; 2001 (129) PLR 587

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Keywords: Wilful Default

Summary: Land & Property - Rent Control - Tamil Nadu Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1960, ss. 10(2)(i) and 10(2)(iii) - Whether on execution of agreement of sale by landlord with tenant and landlord having received substantial portion of sale consideration, relationship of landlord-tenant inter se between them ceases and fresh rights and obligations flow under this agreement? - Held, once there is agreement of sale between a landlord and a tenant, old relationship as such comes to an end - It goes on to record that even after cancellation of such agreement of sale status of tenant is not restored as such - In other words, on date of execution of aforesaid agreement of sale their status as that of landlord and tenant changed into a new status as that of a purchaser and a seller - Every conduct of landlady right from date of entering into agreement of sale, accepting money towards sale consideration, delivering possession in lieu of such agreement all clearly indicates and has to be construed in law that she repudiated her old relationship of landlord and tenant - Thus after this parties enter into a new cloak of seller and purchaser and their relationship is to be governed under said terms of agreement - Every right and obligation thereafter would flow from it - Even if parties under agreement of sale do not perform their obligations, remedy may be availed in law as permissible under in law - Appeal allowed.
