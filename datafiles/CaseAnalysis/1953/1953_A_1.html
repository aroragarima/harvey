Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal v A. W. Figgies and Company, and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 28; AIR 1953 SC 455; [1953] 24 ITR 405; 1953 SCJ 635; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 171

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Family & Personal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Indian Income Tax (Amendment) Act, 1939, Indian Income-tax Act, 1918

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 25, 3, 26, 48 and 55 - Partnership Act, 1932, ss. 4 and 40 - Taxation of partnership firm - (A) Whether succession results in change in constitution of firm? - Mere change in constitution of partnership does not necessarily bring into existence a new assessable unit or a distinct assessable entity and in such a case there is no devolution of business as a whole - Held, s. 25 of Act of 1922 does not regard a mere change in personnel of partners as amounting to succession and disregards such a change - (B) Whether a partnership firm can be charged as distinct assessable entity? - Further held, a firm can be charged as a distinct assessable entity as distinct from its partners who can also be assessed individually - Appeal dismissed.
