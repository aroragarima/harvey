Union of India etc. Represented Through Superintendent of Police v T. Nathamuni

Supreme Court of India

01 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 831; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 338; JT 2014 (14) SC 491; 2015 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 244; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 207; 2014(13) SCALE 541; [2015] 229 TAXMAN 129

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Illegality, Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, 1946

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 17 - Quashing of proceedings - Sanction not by proper authority - Respondent filed petition u/s. 482 of CrPC before HC to quash the entire proceedings on the ground that as per the provisions of s. 17 of Act, no officer below the rank of Inspector of Police was authorized by the Govt. to investigate the case without permission of the Court. - HC held that the Special Court without assigning any reason in the order permitted the Sub-Inspector of Police to investigate the matter and the same was not in accordance with law - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the order passed by the Magistrate permitting the Sub- Inspector, CBI, Chennai to investigate the matter could be sustained in law.

Held, on the basis of the permission accorded by the Magistrate, the Sub-Inspector, CBI proceeded with the investigation and finally submitted charge-sheet. It was only after that, said order of Magistrate was questioned by the respondent by filing a criminal petition in the HC. The Single Judge, appreciating the submission made by the respondent, held that since the special court without assigning any reason permitted Sub-Inspector of Police to investigate the matter, the order was not in accordance with law and disposed of the petition giving liberty to the prosecution to file a fresh petition before the court seeking permission to get the matter investigated by a competent officer. HC erred in overlooking the gist of order of Special Judge permitting the Sub-Inspector to investigate. Further, having regard to the fact that no case of prejudice or miscarriage of justice by reason of investigation by the Sub-Inspector of Police was made out, the order of the HC cannot be sustained in law. Impugned order passed by the HC was set aside. Appeal allowed.
