New India Assurance Co. Ltd v Vedwati & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

20 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 158; (2007) 9 SCC 486; 2007 (1) ACC 924; AIR 2007 SC 1334; 2007 (3) ALJ 193; 2007 (67) ALR 133; [2007] 136 Comp Cas 262; 2007 (2) CTC 664; 2007 (2) JCC 1078; JT 2007 (4) SC 28; 2007 (2) KLT 219; 2007 (3) MLJ(SC) 117; 2007 (146) PLR 72; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 115; 2007(3) SCALE 397; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 918

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Contractual Liability, Legislative Intent, Public Service Vehicle, Third Party Risks, Carried For Hire Or Reward, Individual Passenger, Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 1994, Workmen's Compensation Act. 1993

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 166 - High Court held that the respondent Nos.1 to 6 were entitled to compensation and that the same was to be paid by the insurer - Appeal against - Held, provisions of the Act do not enjoin any statutory liability on the owner of a vehicle to get his vehicle insured for any passenger travelling in a goods carriage and the insurer would have no liability therefore - Appeal allowed.
