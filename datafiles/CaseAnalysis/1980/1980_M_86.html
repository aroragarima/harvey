Chet Ram Vashist v Municipal Corporation of Delhi and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 November 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 609; (1980) 4 SCC 647; AIR 1981 SC 653; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 1073

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Delhi Municipal Corporation Act, 1957,ss. 312, 313(3) - Approval - Compliance - Validity - Appellant's father decided on developing land as residential colony and submitted lay-out plan for sanction u/s. 313 of Act - Plan was sanctioned and appellants father died - Appellant wanted to include provision for construction of cinema - Plots Nos. 33, 34 and 35 approved as separate units were selected as unit for cinema - Application indicating proposed changes, was filed by appellant - Town Planner of Corporation informed him that his application did not fall within purview of s. 313 of Act and that Master Plan did not envisage cinema within residential area, and request was not considered - Appellant filed writ petition in HC alleging that application had not been considered by Standing Committee, and as period prescribed by statute for doing so had expired revised lay-out plan must be treated as having been sanctioned - HC while disposed of writ petition - Corporation preferred Letters Patent Appeal, and DB allowed appeal, set aside judgment and order of Single Judge and dismissed writ petition - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, appellant was right in making application u/s. 313 of Act with regard to amalgamation of three plots for proposed construction of cinema building - Standing Committee had to determine whether lay-out plan proposed could be sanctioned - Sanction could be refused by reason of s. 313(4)on any of ground specified that would be matter for Standing Committee to consider - Appellate Bench of HC had held that appellant was not entitled to invoke s. 313(3) for grant of sanction to revised lay-out plan - HC was of view that s.313 of the Act was attracted only when owner of land had not yet utilised or otherwise dealt with land and application for sanction envisaged u/s. 313 of Act was first application made for purpose - HC had referred to circumstances that owner had already commenced to act on sanction granted to original lay-out plan - Limited view taken by HC was not justified - S. 312 of Act implies that land must be utilised in accordance with lay-out plan - If land had been utilised to any degree by appellant before 20-4-1967, utilisation shouldconform to original sanctioned lay-out plan - No utilisation by appellant in manner subsequently proposed was permissible unless and until sanction was accorded to revised lay-out plan - If such sanction was refused, it was original sanction which would continue to operate, and lay out plan to which such sanction was granted was one that matters - Hence, Court directed respondent no. 1/Municipal Corporation to refer application dt. 20-4-1967 along with lay-out plan accompanying it to its Standing Committee and Standing Committee would dispose of application expeditiously in accordance with law - Appeal disposed of.
