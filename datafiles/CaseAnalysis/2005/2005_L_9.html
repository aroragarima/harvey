Ishwar Dutt v Land Acquisition Collector and another

Supreme Court of India

02 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 445; (2005) 7 SCC 190; AIR 2005 SC 3165; 2005 (61) ALR 160; 2005 (4) AWC 3202; 2005 (4) JhrCR(SC) 142; JT 2005 (6) SC 540; 2005(6) SCALE 11; 2006 (Supp) RD 323; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 903

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 18, 23(1-A), 4, 4(1), 54 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 107, 11 - Principle of Rejudicata - Direction of HC was acted upon - Appeal confined to quantum of compensation - Held, principle of res judicata, would apply not only in different proceedings arising out of same cause of action but would also apply in different stages of same proceedings - Land Acquisition Officer took into consideration direction and awarded 12% additional compensation at market value - Subsequent judgment of HC not sustainable - Appeal allowed.
