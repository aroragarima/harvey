M. J. Jacob v A. Narayanan & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

06 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 239; (2009) 14 SCC 318; 2009 (3) ALD(SC) 1; JT 2009 (3) SC 337; 2009 (1) KLT 926; 2009 (5) MLJ(SC) 600; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 277; 2009(6) SCALE 536; [2009] 4 S.C.R. 305

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Freedom Of Election, Statement Of Fact, Leaflet, Electorate, Allegations Of Corrupt Practice, Corrupt Practice, Circulation, Political Character Of a Candidate, Private Character

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951 - High Court declared the election to be void on the ground that the appellant was guilty of corrupt practice under s. 123 (4) - Appeals against - Held, any false accusation relating to the personal character or conduct of any candidate calculated to prejudice the prospect of his election would amount to a corrupt practice - What is crucial is that the false statement should relate to the personal character or conduct of a defeated candidate - Where the false statement was about some one other than the candidate, Court has refused to consider the publication to be a corrupt practice under s. 123(4) - Many allegations and counter-allegations are made, and sometimes these are incorrect, but that does not mean that an election should be set aside straightaway on that account, unless all the ingredients of s. 123(4) of the Act are clearly made out - Appeals allowed.
