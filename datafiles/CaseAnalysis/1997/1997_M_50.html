Cantonment Board, Mhow, Saugor and Another v M.P State Road Transport Corpn, Rewa Transport Services, Rewa

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 963; (1997) 9 SCC 450; 1997 (1) ACC 586; AIR 1997 SC 2013; JT 1997 (4) SC 561; 1997(3) SCALE 484; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 813; 1997 (4) Supreme 36

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution - Cantonment Act, 1924, s. 60(1) - Madhya Pradesh Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1947, s. 6(1) - Madhya Pradesh municipalities Act, 1961, s. 127 - Motor vehicles - Municipalities - Imposition of entry tax - Validity - Entry tax on Three Cantonment Boards by separate notifications imposed entry tax on motor vehicles in exercise of power u/s. 60 of the 1924 Act - Challenging said notifications, petitions were filed before HC which held that Cantonment Boards had no power to levy entry tax on motor vehicles so long as prohibitions contained in s. 6 of 1947 Act continued and quashed notifications issued by Cantonment Boards - Hence, instant appeals - Whether Cantonment Boards could impose entry tax on motor vehicles - Held, tax leviable on motor vehicles when used or kept for use u/s. 3(2) of the 1947 Act would be different from tax leviable on Motor Vehicles entering limits of municipalities u/s. 127(1) (iii) of the 1961 Act and there was no repugnancy between the two and both provisions should therefore operate in their own field - Since u/s. 127(1)(iii) of the 1961 Act, municipalities could levy tax on motor vehicles entering limits of municipalities, the same could be levied by Cantonment Board in exercise of its power u/s. 60 of 1924 Act with previous sanction of Central Govt - Hence, notifications issued by three Cantonment Boards were valid notifications issued u/s. 60 of the 1924 Act and imposition of tax on motor Vehicles entering into limits of Cantonment Boards should not be said to be invalid or inoperative - HC committed error in striking down those notifications on ground of repugnancy - Impugned judgment of HC set aside - Appeals allowed.
