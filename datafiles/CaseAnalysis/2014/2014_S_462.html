Rishipal Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh and another

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

N.V. Ramana, Ranjana Prakash Desai

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 415; (2014) 7 SCC 215; AIR 2014 SC 2567; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2899; 2014 (3) RCR(Criminal) 637; 2014(8) SCALE 298; [2014] 6 S.C.R. 1012

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Quashing Of Criminal Proceedings

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 379, 411, 417, 418, 420, 457, 458 and 477, 34 - Cheating - Quashing of criminal proceedings - Entitled to - Appellant accused who was branch manager of bank allegedly mischievously obtained respondent no.2 complainant signatures on blank cheques and committed theft of their hand bag in which signed cheque book was kept - Complainant filed private complaint before Magistrate against appellant and three other accused u/ss. 34, 379, 411, 417, 418, 420, 467, 458 and 477 IPC - Magistrate issued summons against the accused - Non-bailable warrant was issued against appellant - Appellant was before HC to quash proceedings against him - HC refused to quash the criminal proceedings against the appellant - Hence instant appeal - Whether criminal proceedings against appellant was liable to be quashed - Appellant contended that appellant has nothing to do with the alleged offence and his name was unnecessarily dragged into the criminal complaint -

Held, complainant instructed appellant not to pass cheques which were already signed - It appeared from letter that only request was made to Bank that said four cheques should not be honoured - Complaint and letter addressed by complainant to appellant, entire grievance of complaint appeared to be that basing on written information which had been given to appellant, when stolen cheque was presented, he should have given complaint to police - As appellant had not chosen to give complaint to police, according to complainant other accused hatched conspiracy with appellant and accordingly cheated him - It was no doubt true that Courts had to be very careful while exercising power u/s. 482 CrPC - At same time Court should not allow litigant to file vexatious complaints to otherwise settle their scores by setting criminal law into motion, which was pure abuse of process of law and it had to be interdicted at threshold - Clear reading of complaint did not make out any offence against appellant , much less offences alleged u/ss. 34, 379, 411, 417, 418, 420, 467, 458 and 477 IPC - SC was in view that even assuming that appellant violated instructions in complaint in letter and spirit - It all amounted to negligence in discharging official work at maximum it should be said that it was dereliction of duty - Thus, SC concluded that continuation of criminal proceedings against appellant for commission of alleged offence was pure abuse of process of law and complaint case deserved to be quashed in interest of justice and impugned judgment of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed .
