Amalgamated Commercial Traders Private Limited v A C K Krishnaswami and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 January 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 378; [1965] 35 Comp Cas 456

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 397, 398, 434 and 439 - Debt - Bonafide or not - Whether debt was bona fide disputed by Co. on substantial grounds? - Appellant received legal advice and acted on it - Appellant Co. did not dispute debt in order to hide its inability to pay debts - Held, Co. had been advised that shareholders' resolution D/- 30-12-1959, to effect that a dividend of Rupees 100. 00 per share (taxable) on equity shares be paid to shareholders, payment to be effected when commissions due from principals are realised, is not declaration of dividend and/or does not constitute a proper and valid declaration of dividend and that liability to pay any dividend arises thereunder - Appeals allowed.
