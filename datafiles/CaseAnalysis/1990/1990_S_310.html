Kulwant Singh Gill v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

13 September 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, K.N. Saikia, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 435; (1991) Supp1 SCC 504; (1991) SCC (L&S) 998; 1991 (16) ATC 940; 1990 (61) FLR 635; JT 1990 (4) SC 70; 1990 (2) LLN 1019; 1990(2) SCALE 597; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 426; 1990 (3) SLJ 135; 1990 (6) SLR 73; [1990] supp1 S.C.R. 426

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Punjab Civil Services Rules 1920, rr. 5(iv), 5(v) - Whether stoppage of two increments with cumulative effect is a major penalty? - RR. 8 and 9 envisage conducting an enquiry into misconduct after giving an opportunity to delinquent employee in manner prescribed therein and on establishing charge to pass an appropriate order imposing a major penalty prescribed in either cls. (v) to (ix) or minor penalty u/cls. (i) to (iv) of r. 5 of Rules - If it is a minor penalty, indisputably need to conduct regular enquiry has been dispensed with - Held, withholding of increments of pay simpliciter undoubtedly is a minor penalty within meaning of r. 5(iv) - R.5(iv) does not empower disciplinary authority to impose penalty of withholding increments of pay with cumulative effect except after holding inquiry and following prescribed procedure - Then order would be without jurisdiction or authority of law, and it would be per se void - Held, impugned order would come within meaning of r. 5(v) of Rules; it is a major penalty and imposition of impugned penalty without enquiry is per se illegal - Appeal allowed.
