Kiran Tandon v Allahabad Development Authority and Another

Supreme Court of India

23 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, AR. Lakshmanan, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1464; (2004) 10 SCC 745; AIR 2004 SC 2006; 2004 ALJ 1438; 2004 (55) ALR 450; 2004 (3) JhrCR(SC) 133; JT 2004 (3) SC 579; 2004 (3) RCR(Civil) 3; 2004(3) SCALE 691; 2004 (2) UPLBEC 1760

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 417, 18 and 30 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 11, O. 1 r. 10 - (A) Whether High Court in transposing State of Uttar Pradesh as appellant in both appeals? - Held, in order to effectuate complete adjudication of question involved in appeal it was in interest of justice to transpose State of U.P. - (B) Whether claimant was only a lessee of acquired land, and entitled to entire amount of compensation? - Further held, leasehold rights being limited in nature and entirely different from ownership right, a lessee is not entitled to entire amount of compensation for acquired land, therefore claimant should get 20 per cent of compensation amount and balance 80 per cent is payable to State Govt. - Appeals dismissed
