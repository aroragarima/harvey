Sambhu Das @ Bijoy Das and another v State of Assam

Supreme Court of India

15 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 751; (2010) 10 SCC 374; AIR 2010 SC 3300; 2010 (4) JhrCR(SC) 182; JT 2010 (9) SC 470; 2011 (2) MLJ(Crl) 190; 2010 (4) RCR(Criminal) 799; 2010(9) SCALE 558; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 493

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 302 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 154 and 173 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Appellants were convicted u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC - On appeal, HC affirmed the order passed by the Sessions Judge - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether SC in exercise of the powers u/art. 136 of the Constitution, can upset the concurrent findings of fact recorded by the Trial Court and the Appellate Court? - Held, art. 136 of the Constitution, does not confer a right of appeal on a party - It only confers a discretionary power on the SC to be exercised sparingly to interfere in suitable cases where grave miscarriage of justice has resulted from illegality or misapprehension or mistake in reading evidence or from ignoring, excluding or illegally admitting material evidence - SC, in exercise of its powers u/art. 136 of the Constitution, will not re-open the findings of the HC when there are concurrent findings of facts and there is no question of law involved and the conclusion is not perverse - (B) It was contended that since the FIR was lodged after the inquest was held, the FIR was not reliable - Held, inquest report and post mortem report cannot be termed to be substantive evidence and any discrepancy occurring therein can neither be termed to be fatal nor even a suspicious circumstance which would warrant a benefit to the accused and the resultant dismissal of the prosecution case - Contents of the inquest report can be looked into to test the veracity of the witnesses - Further, FIR u/s. 154 of CrPC is not a substantive piece of evidence and can only be used to contradict or corroborate the maker thereof - No merit in submission made by appellants - (C) Evidence of PW1 (wife of the deceased) - Held, evidence of PW1, which was corroborated by the postmortem report and the evidence of other witnesses was trustworthy and reliable - Trial Court and HC accepted her evidence while holding that the appellants in furtherance of the common intention, assaulted deceased - Judgment of Trial Court and HC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
