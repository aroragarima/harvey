S. Govinda Menon v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 136; AIR 1967 SC 1274; 1967 (2) LLJ 249; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 566

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, All India Service (Discipline & Appeals) rules 1955

Summary: Constitution - Service - Practice & Procedure - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - All India Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules (1955), r. 4(1) - Tamil Nadu Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Act, 1951, ss. 80, 81, 29(4) and 99 - All India Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1955, rr. 2, 5, 7 - Instances where in Writ of Prohibition can be issued? - Held, jurisdiction for grant of a Writ of Prohibition is primarily supervisory and object of that writ is to restrain Courts or inferior tribunals from exercising a jurisdiction which they do not possess at all or else to prevent them from exceeding limits of their jurisdiction - But also in a case of departure from Rules of natural justice - Does not lie to correct course, practice or procedure of an inferior tribunal or a wrong decision on merits of proceedings - Writ cannot be issued to a Court or an inferior tribunal for an error of law unless error makes it go outside its jurisdiction - (B) Scope of words -'Acts and omissions' liable to be penalized in All India Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1955, r.4(1) - Further held, should reflect on reputation or integrity or good faith or devotion of duty of Civil Servant - Master and Servant relationship - Need not subsist - Even if Commr. is not subject to administrative control of the Govt., still proceedings for such acts can be instituted against him, if there is prima facie material to show recklessness or misconduct on his part - It cannot be said it relates only to an act or omission of an officer when serving under Govt. which means subject to administrative control of Govt. - Nor that disciplinary proceedings could be taken only on basis of relationship of master and servant - Not necessary that a member of Service should have committed alleged act or omission in course of discharge of his duties as a servant of Govt. in order that it may form subject-matter of disciplinary proceedings - If it reflects on reputation of officer for his integrity or good faith or devotion to duty - There is no reason why disciplinary proceedings should not be taken against him for that act or omission even though act or omission relates to an activity in regard to which there is no actual master and servant relationship - Test is whether act or omission has some reasonable connection with nature and condition of his service or whether act or omission has cast any reflection upon reputation of member of Service for integrity or devotion to duty as a public servant - By sanctioning lease of immovable property over 5 years without any auction therefore can be proceeded against departmentally under r. 4 (1) of All India Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1955 - If there is prima facie material for showing recklessness or misconduct on part of Commissioner in discharge of his official duty - (C) Ambit of word charges in All India Services (Discipline and Appeal Rules )1955 - Further held, word 'charges' in r. 7 (1) has to be given a wider meaning as denoting accusations or imputations against member of service - Appeal dismissed.
