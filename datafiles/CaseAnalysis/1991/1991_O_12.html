Smt. Sureshta Devi v Om Prakash

Supreme Court of India

07 February 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 675; (1991) 2 SCC 25; (1991) SCC (Cr) 292; AIR 1992 SC 1904; 1991 (17) ALR 263; 1991 (1) AWC 406; 1991 (1) Bom.C.R. 508; 1991 (1) CCC 422; 1991 (1) DMC 313; 1991 (1) GLH 440; 1991 (1) HLR 467; JT 1993 (3) SC 322; 1991 (1) KarLJ 553; 1991 MahLJ 324; 1991 MPLJ 382; 1991 (1) PLR 411; 1991(1) SCALE 156; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 274; 1991 (2) ShimLC 20; 1991 (2) ShimLC 118; 1991 (1) UJ 715

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Marriage & Divorce

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 13B - Special Marriage Act, 1954, s. 28 - (A) Divorce - Mutual consent - Party to petition - If can unilaterally withdraw consent - (B) Expression 'living separately' occurring in s. 13B - Connotion - (C) Expression 'have not been able to live together' - Inference - Held, (A) Can unilaterally withdraw consent, It is not irrevocable, mutual consent must continue till divorce decree is passed - (B) Connotes not living like husband and wife, it is immaterial whether spouses live under same roof or in different houses - (C) Indicates concept of broken down marriage and no possibility of reconcilliation - Appeal allowed.
