Ram Parshotam Mittal and Another v Hillcrest Realty Sdn. Bhd. and Others Etc

Supreme Court of India

20 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 884; (2009) 8 SCC 709; AIR 2009 SC 2859; [2009] 152 Comp Cas 477; 2009 (4) CompLJ 225; JT 2009 (9) SC 635; 2009(9) SCALE 689; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 1121; 2010 (1) ShimLC 244

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Statement In Lieu Of Prospectus

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 87(2)(b), exp. and 90(2) - Interpretation and application thereof, and the other connected provisions of the Act - M/s. Hotel Queen Road Pvt. Ltd was a special purpose vehicle for the purpose of taking over the assets of Hotel Ashok Yatri Niwas, a unit of ITDC, and for its management; all under disinvestment process of Govt. of India - 99.97% of the total voting equity share capital sold by GOI to one successful bidder - Hillcrest Realty purchased certain preference shares of the company(M/s. Hotel Queen) on two occasions - No dividend declared by the company for 2 years - Notices issued by Hillcrest Realty for conduct of Extraordinary General Meeting; denied by the company - Litigation ensued - Certain resolutions were passed by the company on 30-09-2002; suit by Hillcrest Realty was filed on basis of these resolutions - Suits filed by parties were dependent upon the question, whether by the resolutions adopted on 30-09-2002, the company had lost its private character and had been converted into a public company? - Held, Yes, it had converted into a public company - Resolutions were threesome and noted by Court - Materials on record perused - Vide the resolutions, a final decision was taken by the company to convert itself into a public company with immediate effect - Fact of filing of Form 23 alongwith the resolutions, coupled with the fact that a statement in lieu of Prospectus, which is required to be filed by a private company when it coverts itself into a public company, noted by Court, for the purpose of arriving at the prima facie conclusion as to such alteration of status - Definition of 'private company' perused - Court noted that as soon as the number of members exceeds 50, it loses its character as a private company; in instant case shares were allotted to 134 persons on 30-09-2002 - P /&gt;(A) Contention was raised that till such time as the records of ROC were not altered to show that the company had become a public company, it could not be treated as such - Whether acceptable? - Held, No - It is not the records of the ROC which determines the status of a company but whether it falls within the definition of a 'private company' or 'public company' as defined in ss. 3(1)(iii) and 3(1)(iv) of Act -P /&gt;(B) Effect of resolutions D/-30-09-2002 - The moment they were passed, provisions of the Companies Act became applicable and by operation of law, Hotel Queen simultaneously ceased to be a private limited company and under the conditions prescribed in the Act, Hillcrest Reality acquired voting rights in the meetings of the company by operation of s. 87(2)(b) and s. 44 of the Act - Observations made - Directions issued - Petitions dismissed.
