Dharmarth Trust J and K Jammu and others v Dinesh Chander Nanda

Supreme Court of India

08 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 768; (2010) 10 SCC 331; 2010 (6) AWC 5459; JT 2010 (9) SC 506; 2011 (3) MLJ(SC) 395; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 306; 2010(9) SCALE 447; [2010] 10 S.C.R. 1101

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Jammu & Kashmir Limitation Act, 1995

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Trusts & Associations - Jammu & Kashmir Limitation Act, 1995, arts. 56 and 119 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6 r. 17 - Period of limitation - Appellants-trust terminated service of respondent, who was engaged by appellants-trust for the purpose of providing professional consultancy services as an architect - After four years, a suit was filed by the respondent claiming compensation towards professional charges including interest thereon for various projects - During the pendency of the suit, respondent filed an application for amendment in the plaint, which was allowed - Appellants-trust filed civil revision thereagainst before HC wherein HC remanded matter back to Trial Court to decide issues regarding maintainability/limitation - Thereafter, Trial Court held that the case was governed by art. 119 and not by art. 56 of the Act and allowed the amendment to the plaint - Revision filed thereagainst was dismissed - Hence, present appeal - Whether present case was governed by art. 119 or by art. 56 of the Act? - Respondent contended that case was covered by the residuary art. 119 which provides for a period of 6 years on the ground that the nature of services provided were professional services and the remuneration for the services being termed as 'fee' and the same would not fall within the expression 'price' for 'work done' - Held, term 'price', as appearing in art. 56, is to be understood in common parlance/ordinary sense - 'Price' does not cover the services provided by the professionals such as Architect, Lawyer, Doctor etc., as professionals charge a 'fee' - Also, the term 'work done' in art. 56 will not be applicable to professionals such as Architect, Lawyer, Doctor etc. as these professionals render services to their clients - Further, remuneration of a professional is in the form of a 'fee' and therefore, it cannot be said that the professional earns a 'price' - Hence, term "price of work done" cannot be made applicable to professions where the professionals merely provide services for a "fee" - Claim of the respondent that the profession of an Architect is one such service, hence Art. 56 is not applicable to the present case accepted - No error in the conclusion arrived at by the Trial Court and affirmed by HC - Appeal dismissed.
