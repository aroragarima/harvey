K. J. Nathan v S. V. Maruty Reddy and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 423; AIR 1965 SC 430; 1964 (2) MLJ(SC) 162; 1964 (2) SCJ 671; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 727

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 58(f) - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - Plaintiff, alleging that first defendant had created a mortgage by deposit of title deeds in his favour in respect of his half share in properties - Whether finding of both lower courts that no mortgage by deposit of title deeds was effected for entire plaint claim was vitiated by fact that they had ignored a registered agreement entered into between plaintiff and 1st defendant on 05-07-1947, wherein said fact was clearly and unambiguously recorded - Held, there is no presumption of law that mere deposit of title-deeds constitutes a mortgage, for no such presumption has been laid down either in Evidence Act, 1872 or in Transfer of property Act - But a court may presume u/s. 114 of Evidence Act, 1872 that under certain circumstances a loan and a deposit of title-deeds constitute a mortgage - On 10-05-1947, 1st defendant deposited title-deeds with plaintiff physically as security for amounts advanced by plaintiff to 1st defendant up to that date - Even if evidence of witnesses as regards handing over of documents physically by 1st defendant to plaintiff was an embellishment of what took place on that date and that there was only constructive delivery, such delivery satisfied condition laid down by s. 58(f) of Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Appeal allowed.
