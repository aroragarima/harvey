Â Amireddi Rajagopala Rao and Others v Amireddi Sitharamamma and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 February 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, K. Subba Rao, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 476; AIR 1965 SC 1970; 1966 (2) MLJ(SC) 17; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 122

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Concubine, Avaruddha Stree

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956, ss. 4, 21 and 22 - (A) Whether illegitimate son of a Sudra was entitled to maintenance out of his father's estate? - Held, as per Mitakshara School of Hindu law - Prior to Act 78 of 1956 - Illegitimate son of a Sudra was entitled to maintenance out of his father's estate, though at time of his conception his mother was a married woman, her husband was alive and her connection with putative father was adulterous - (B) Whether a married woman who left her husband and lived with her paramour could claim maintenance from estate of her paramour? - Further held, a married woman who left her husband - Lived with her paramour as his permanently kept concubine - Status of an avaruddha stree by remaining faithful to her paramour could claim maintenance from estate of paramour as long as she preserved her sexual fidelity to him - (C) Whether right of illegitimate son and concubine is affected by difference in caste? - Further held, claim for maintenance cannot be defeated on ground that concubine was a Brahmin and her paramour was a Sudra - (D) Whether rights vested before commencement of Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956 are destroyed by enforcement of same? - Further held, ss. 21 and 22 r/w s. 4 of Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956 do not destroy right of maintenance out of estate of a deceased Hindu which was vested - Under Hindu law in force at time of his death before commencement of Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act 1956 - Appeal dismissed.
