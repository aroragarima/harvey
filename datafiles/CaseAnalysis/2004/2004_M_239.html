J.P. Srivastava and Sons Private Limited and v Messrs Gwalior Sugar Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 910; (2005) 1 SCC 172; AIR 2005 SC 83; 2005 (1) BC 142; 2005 (2) CalLT(SC) 31; 5 CLC 1537; [2004] 122 Comp Cas 696; JT 2004 (9) SC 507; 2004(9) SCALE 60; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 648

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1956, ss.397, 398 and 399(1) - Proceedings initiated by a group of minority shareholders complaining of mismanagement and oppression - Whether appellant held requisite one-tenth of issued share capital of company u/s. 399(1) when they filed petition under ss. 397 and 398 - Held, if Court is satisfied that petitioners represent a body of shareholders holding requisite percentage, it can assume that involvement of company in litigation is not lightly done and that it should pass orders to bring to an end matters complained of and not reject it on a technical requirement - Matter remanded back to Single Judge - Appeal allowed.
