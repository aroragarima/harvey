Jain Exports Private Limited and Anr v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 194; (1996) 5 SCC 619; 1996 (6) AD(SC) 175; AIR 1996 SC 2739; 1996 (66) ECR 421; 1996 (86) E.L.T. 478; JT 1996 (11) SC 97; 1996(5) SCALE 839; [1996] Supp4 S.C.R. 673; 1996 (6) Supreme 204

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Exemption Notifications

Summary: Customs Act, s. 25(2) - Whether appellants are entitled to exemption granted to State Trading Corporation u/s. 25(2) of Act? - Court may strike down an exemption but cannot at any time extend benefits of an exemption to a person, even if discrimination is found - Where exemption is shown to be based on existing grounds, court will not test sufficiency of those grounds if law vests discretion with executive - Mere compliance with an option in an interim order or a voluntary undertaking to court does not create an equitable claim - Held, even if discrimination was found, court could not extend exemption to appellants - Exemption granted to State Trading Corporation was valid since grounds for it existed - No equitable claim was created in favour of appellants - Appeal dismissed.
