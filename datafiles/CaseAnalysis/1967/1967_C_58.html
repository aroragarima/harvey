Commissioner of Income Tax, Gujarat v Jayantilal Amratlal and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 322; AIR 1968 SC 189; [1968] 67 ITR 1; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 946

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 16(1)(c) - Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934 - Immovable property - Debenture holder - Charitable or Religious entities - Respondent individual, hereinafter referred to as the settlor, executed a trust deed whereby he settled 80 ordinary shares of his company on trust created and known in the respondent settlor's name to carry out the objects set out in the Trust Deed registered with the Charity Commissioner under the Bombay Public Trust Act, 1950 - The Income tax department had accepted this trust as a valid charitable trust and gave the necessary relief to the trustees in respect of the income of the Trust till A/Y 1957-58 - The Income-tax Officer, while dealing with the assessment of respondent settlor's trust for the year 1958-59, wrote lengthy order holding that on the facts the case were covered by the first proviso to s. 16(1)(c) of the 1922 Act - ITO had accordingly held that the income of the Trust would not be computed in the hands of the trustees but would be computed in the hands of the settlor under s. 16(1)(c) of the 1922 Act for the A/Y 1959-60 - Four appeals were taken to the Appellate Assistant Commissioner who, by his two orders dismissed the appeals - Later, appeal was filed before Income-tax Appellate Tribunal - Tribunal reversed the orders that had been passed by Appellate Assistant Commissioner and held that the income from the Trust was not hit by the first proviso to s. 16(1)(c) of the 1922 Act - ITO appealed before the HC against the order of the Tribunal but the HC passed the order in favor of respondent settler - Hence the instant appeal before the SC by the Commissioner of Income-tax that is the appellant against the order passed by the HC - Whether the acts of respondent settler were subject to the first proviso to s.16(1)(c) of the 1922 Act and thus liable to be taxed - Held, as per the various clauses of the Trust Deed there was no doubt that the respondent settlor had retained power to see that his wishes were carried out while he was alive - But the respondent settler could only direct the carrying out of his wishes within the terms of the Trust Deed - Respondent settler could direct u/cl. 4 the application of income to a particular charitable purpose and u/cl. 6 of the Trust deed he could nominate the charitable object and the fund or investment which was to be utilized for that object - Respondent settlor could not legally direct a loan to be made to himself and nor could he subscribe to proposition that a loan to a company in which respondent settlor was interested would give power to settlor over assets within meaning of first proviso under s. 16(1)(c)of the 1922 Act - Cl. 21 only gave wide powers which settlor had reserved to himself - Hence, the acts of respondent settlor were not subject to the first proviso to s.16(1)(c) of the 1922 Act - Appeals dismissed.
