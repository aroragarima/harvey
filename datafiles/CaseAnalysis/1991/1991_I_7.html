Dr. Mahabal Ram v Indian Council of Agricultural Research and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 1073; (1994) 2 SCC 401; (1994) SCC (L&S) 642; AIR 1997 SCW 1345; 1994 (27) ATC 97; 1993 (66) FLR 949; 1991 (2) LLJ 112

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, s. 5(6) - Allocation of work - Transfer of cases - Jurisdiction - Appellant was transferred from Indian Council of Agricultural Research to Central Soil Salinity Research Institute by allocating work as single member - Further, there was letter from Director of Central Soil Salinity Research Institute that there was no scope for appellant to be fitted into that Institute to which appellant was transferred by impugned order - Case was transferred from two-Judge Bench to larger Bench - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether single member of Central Administrative Tribunal set up under the Act had jurisdiction to dispose of matters coming before Tribunal under the Act - Held, proviso of s. 5(6) of the Act indicated Parliament's concern to safeguard interest of claimants by casting obligation on Chairman and Members who hear cases to refer to regular bench of two members such cases which in their opinion required to be heard by bench of two Members - To make a distinction between Judicial Member and Administrative Member functioning u/s. 5 (6) of the Act could not be appropriate - Observation made in two-Judge Bench case that no provision was cited to them that Single Member could hear cases laid before Tribunal led to conclusion that judicial business of Administrative Tribunal was intended to be carried by bench of two Members - Subsequent to decision of Tribunal, there was letter from Director of Central Soil Salinity Research Institute that there was no scope for appellant to be fitted into that Institute to which appellant had been transferred by impugned order - It was further pointed out by appellant that he had undertaken certain research which was half way through prior to transfer and seek upon which he had carried his research would not have germinating capacity beyond end of May - That was collapsed and respondent could not be found fault with for not being present in Court - Appeal disposed of.
