Hari Shankers v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 306; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 2450; 2015 (3) JCC 1769; JT 2015 (4) SC 304; 2015 (2) MLJ(Crl) 625; 2015(5) SCALE 623; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 228

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 32, 34 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.161 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Four accused persons including appellant confronted 'X'/deceased, PW2 and one 'R' and threatened to kill 'X' - Appellant was carrying licensed pistol while co-accused were carrying country-made pistols - Appellant fired first shot from his pistol which hit 'X' on his right hand's wrist and 'X' tried to run away but co-accused/'R' caught hold of him by his waist - When 'R' released 'X', co-accused/ 'V' shot at deceased and he fell down and thereafter, other co-accused 'R' and 'M' also fired at 'X' - Trial Court found prosecution case proved against all accused and convicted them u/s. 302 with aid of s. 34 of IPC - On appeal, HC acquitted co-accused giving them benefit of doubt but maintained conviction of appellant u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC.

Held, there is concurrent finding of conviction of appellant by Trial Court and HC on the basis of statement of eye witness, PW2 and its corroboration by medical evidence. Since the acquittal of all co-accused has become final as State preferred no appeal, conviction of appellant u/s. 34 of IPC becomes unsustainable. There is sufficient ocular evidence to the fact that appellant had fired first shot which landed on wrist of 'X' and same is further corroborated by medical evidence. It cannot be said that evidence of PW2 is not acceptable as s. 161 of CrPC statement was recorded very late and is not worthy enough. PW2 has given a reason that he was out of station for days after the incident. There has neither been any effective cross examination of PW2 by defence on such point. Though weapon attributed to appellant has not been recovered, this should not be fatal to the case of prosecution. Role attributed to the appellant becomes proved. Appellant made a gunshot which caused an injury on the right hand wrist of 'X' and no doubt that such injury could not have caused death of 'X'. HC was misplaced in maintaining conviction of appellant u/s. 302 of IPC. Conviction of appellant is altered to one u/s. 326 of IPC. Appeal partly allowed.
