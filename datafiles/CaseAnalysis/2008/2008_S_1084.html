Sattatiya @ Satish Rajanna Kartalla v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

16 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 42; (2008) 3 SCC 210; (2008) 1 SCC (Cr) 733; AIR 2008 SC 1184; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 537; 2008 CRLJ 1816; 2008 (1) JCC 597; JT 2008 (1) SC 434; 2008 (3) RLW 2135; 2008(1) SCALE 399

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Blood Stains, Acquaintance

Summary: Indian Penal Code, s. 302 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Whether in the present case the prosecution succeeded in establishing the chain of circumstances leading to an inescapable conclusion that the appellant had committed the crime? - Held, circumstances from which an inference as to the guilt is drawn have to be proved beyond reasonable doubt and have to be shown to be closely connected with the principal fact sought to be inferred from those circumstances - Statement of PW11 shows that the same is full of contradictions - Prosecution failed to establish the chain of circumstances which could link the appellant with the crime - Appeal allowed.
