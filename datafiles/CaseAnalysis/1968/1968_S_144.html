Â Romesh Chandra Mehta v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

18 October 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 2; AIR 1970 SC 940; 1970 (72) BomLR 787; 1970 CRLJ 863; 1999 (110) E.L.T. 324; 1971 (2) SCJ 123; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 461

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Excise - Customs Act, 1962 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 25 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120B - Sea Customs Act, 1878, ss. 167(81), 171A - Import and Export Control Act, 1947, s. 5 - (A)Whether statements tendered in evidence by Customs Officer must be deemed by virtue of s. 160 of Customs Act of 1962 to be recorded under provisions of that Act and their admissibility may be adjudged in light of that Act alone? - Held, even after repeal of Sea Customs Act, admissibility of statement made in a complaint made before a Magistrate for contravention of provision of that Act must be adjudged in light of taint, if any, attaching thereto when statement was made - (B)Whether an Officer of Customs is a 'police officer' within meaning of s. 25 of Indian Evidence Act, and a confessional statement made before him is admissible in evidence at trial of appellant and his co-accused? - Held, a Customs Officer is not a member of police force - He is not entrusted with duty to maintain law and order - He is entrusted with powers which specifically relate to collection of customs duties and prevention of smuggling - (C)Whether statements made before Customs Officer were otherwise inadmissible, because appellant and others being persons accused of an offence were compelled by provision of s. 171 A of Sea Customs Act, to be witnesses against themselves within meaning of art. 20(3) of Constitution? - Held, to bring statement in question within prohibition of art. 20(3), person accused must have stood in character of an accused person at time he made statement which is not case u/s. 171 A of Sea Customs Act - Appeals dismissed.
