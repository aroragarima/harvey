Â State of Bombay v R. S. Nanji

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Syed Jaffer Imam, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, B. Jagannadhadas, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 8; AIR 1956 SC 294; 1956 (58) BomLR 978; 1956 SCJ 288; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 18

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Bombay Land Requisition Act, 1948

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Service - Bombay Land Requisition Act, 1948, s. 5 - Road Transport Corporations Act, 1950, s. 19 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 31 - Appeal against act of Govt. - In exercise of powers conferred by s. 5(1) of Bombay Land Requisition Act, 1948, Govt. of Bombay requisitioned part of a building for a public purpose, namely, for housing an Officer of State Road Transport Corporation which is a public utility service - (A) Whether living accommodation for employees of Road Transport Corporation is a public purpose? - Courts have jurisdiction and it is their duty to determine matter whenever a question is raised whether a requisition order is or is not for a 'public purpose' - Held, activities of Corporation are so interlinked with its successful functioning and efficient working of Road Transport that issuing requisitions for employee accommodation would be regarded as a public purpose - Appeal allowed.
