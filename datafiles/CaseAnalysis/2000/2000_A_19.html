Government of A.P. and Others v A. P. Jaiswal and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, G.B. Pattanaik, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3531; (2001) SCC (L&S) 316; (2001) 1 SCC 748; AIR 2001 SC 499; 2001 LabIC 479; 2000(8) SCALE 181; 2000 (6) SLR 734; 2000 (8) SLT 707; 2000 (8) Supreme 575

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Service

Keywords: Appeals allowed.

Summary: Service - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, s. 14 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16, 141 - States Reorganisation Act, 1956 - Formation of new state merging 2 States - Disputes regarding seniority - Challenged - Appellant/ State was formed on dt.1-11-1956 merging states of Andhra and Hyderabad - Disputes arose regarding seniority between civil servants from two states - Instant appeals pertained to retrospective regularization of services of Andhra engineers and equation of posts in public works department -Tribunal on remand entertained all connected matters and passed Award by three member bench in 1982 which became final as was not challenged - In 1984 different view was taken by Tribunal in same matter which was challenged and SC by its Order ad upheld decision of 1982 as became final and binding on parties and directed State Govt. to implement 1982 Order - Accordingly State Govt. had prepared fresh provisional common gradation list which was challenged in Tribunal - Tribunal by impugned Award reconsidered and set aside its 1982 Order holding as bad in law and directed appellant to promote respondents with consequential benefits with 10% interest - Hence instant appeals - Whether Order of Tribunal was justified - Held, subsequent Bench of Tribunal could not have reopened main judgment -Courts had evolved rule of precedents and principle of stare decisis etc to achieve consistency in judicial pronouncements based on public policy - SC in S.I. Rooplal and Anr. vs. Lt. Governor through Chief Secretary, Delhi & Ors, 1999 Indlaw SC 895 held that precedent law should be followed by all concerned and subordinate Courts were bound by enunciation of law made by superior courts and Coordinate Bench of Court could not pronounce judgment contrary to declaration of law made by another Bench and it could only refer it to larger Bench if it disagrees with earlier pronouncement -SC in number of cases held that Courts could not issue mandamus to promote - SC in State of Mysore & Anr. vs. P.N.Nanjundiah & Anr, 1969 Indlaw SC 550 held that HC ought not to issue mandamus directing State Govt. to promote aggrieved officers with retrospective effect but should direct State Govt. to perform its duty and to consider seniority in promotion enabling consequential benefits which was not followed by Tribunal - Impugned Order of Tribunal in reopening the question of retrospective regularization and granting relief without quashing graduation list was erroneous and its directions were without jurisdiction and hence set aside - Appeals allowed.
