Â N. Raghavendra Rao v Deputy Commissioner, South Kanara, Mangalore

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 402; AIR 1965 SC 136; 1965 (1) SCJ 327; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 549

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - States Reorganisation Act, 1956, s. 115(7) - Mysore General Service (Revenue Subordinate Branch) Rules, 1959- Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - Mysore General Services (Revenue Subordinate Branch) Recruitment Rules (1959) - Madras Public Service Commission- Petitioner, a lower division clerk - Qualifying exam for promotion - (A)Whether in instant case there is previous approval as per proviso to s. 115(7) of Act of 1959? - As per memorandum, Central Govt., after examining various aspects, came to conclusion that it would not be appropriate to provide for any protection in matter of travelling allowance, discipline, control, classification, appeal, conduct, probation and departmental promotion - Held, memorandum amounted to previous approval within proviso to s. 115(7) - (B)Whether s. 115(7) of State Re-organisation Act, 1956 governs petitioner insofar as conditions of service have been varied to his disadvantage ? - Further held, rules were validly made, hence, s. 115(7) of State Re-organisation Act, 1956 governs petitioner pertaining to conditions of service - Petition dismissed.
