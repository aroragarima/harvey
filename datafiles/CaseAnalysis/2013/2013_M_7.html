The Assistant Commissioner Of Income Tax, Chennai v M/S A.R. Enterprises

Supreme Court of India

14 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, H.L. Dattu, J. S. KHEHAR

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 22; (2013) 3 SCC 196; [2013] 350 ITR 489; JT 2013 (1) SC 442; 2013(1) SCALE 326; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 295; [2013] 212 TAXMAN 531

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Advance Payment

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss.132,139,143,158B,190,2(45),207,210(1), 260A,5 - A/y 1995-96 - Non-disclosure of Income - Advance Tax - Proceedings - Sustainability - Respondent-assessee firm was doing business since 1992, on scrutiny on 26-02-1996, Assessing officer found that assessee had taxable income for a/y 1995-96, but no return of income had been filed till date of search - Assessing Officer satisfied that assessee had not disclosed their income pertaining to a/y 1995-96, initiated action u/s. 158BD of Act requiring assessee to file their return of income - Assessee objected to action initiated on ground that for a/y 1995-96, advance tax had been paid, therefore income for that period could not be deemed to be undisclosed - Rejecting plea of assessee, AO compute total undisclosed income for block period 1993-94 to 1995-96, treating income returned by assessee for period 1995-96 as NIL, as stipulated s. 158BB (1)(c) of Act - Assessee preferred appeal before Tribunal, which allowed appeal holding that assessee had paid advance tax and declared assessment made u/s. 158BD as null and void - Revenue preferred appeal before HC, u/s. 260A of Act - Revenue pleaded that since return for a/y 1995-96 had not been filed by due date, by filing return after search, assessee could not escape consequences as stipulated in Chap. XIVB of Act - HC, dismissed appeal of revenue holding that income disclosed by assessee on payment of advance tax would be an income disclosed to Revenue and could not be treated as an income undisclosed for relevant a/y - Hence instant appeals - Whether payment of Advance Tax by an assessee would by itself tantamount to disclosure of income for relevant a/y and whether such income can be treated as undisclosed income for purpose of application of Chap. XIVB of Act - Held, after search was conducted on 23-2-2006, it was found that for a/y 1995-96, respondent-assessee had not filed its return of income by due date, it was only when block assessment proceedings were initiated by AO, that assessee filed its return for a/y on 11-7-1996 u/s. 158BC of Act, showing its total income as Rs.7,02,768/- - Assessee claimed, that since Advance Tax had been paid in three installments, it could not had been said that income had not been disclosed or that there was no intention to disclose income - As held earlier payment of Advance Tax, which was based upon estimated income, could not tantamount to disclosure of total income, which must be declared in return, fact that assessee had not filed its return of income by due date, AO was correct in assuming that assessee would not have disclosed its total income, hence, decision of HC could not be sustained - Appeals allowed.
