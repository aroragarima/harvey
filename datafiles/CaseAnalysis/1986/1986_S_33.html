Sardar Sucha Singh v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

M.P. Thakkar

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 707; (1987) Supp SCC 127

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 299 - Government of India Act, 1935, s.175(3) - Withdrawal of tender - Asking for return of deposit - Sustainability - Plaintiff had submitted a tender which he withdrew subsequent to its acceptance by the defendant - Suit was for the return of Rs. 16, 000 which he had deposited along with the tender which was accepted by the Chief Engineer expressly on behalf of the President of India - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that no legal contract which satisfied the requirements of art. 299(1) of the Constitution of India had come into existence - Appellant was therefore entitled to the refund of Rs. 16, 000 deposited by him along with the tender - Held, there was no formal document executed on behalf of the Union of India - Tender notice was issued by the Govt. - There was correspondence and ultimately there was an acceptance notice which was signed by the Chief Director of Purchases on behalf of the Governor General of India - However, a formal agreement had not been drawn up - In the background of these facts SC had taken the view that s. 175(3) of Act did not require the execution of a formal document - Two essential requirements enjoined by the said provision were that the acceptance must be in writing and that it should be in the name of the Governor General - Law stated in the context of the corresponding provision of s. 175(3) Act which was in pari materia holds good in the context of art. 299(1) of Constitution as well - So far as the second point was concerned, the argument of appellant was that the acceptance of the tender by the Chief Engineer was a conditional one - HC had read this letter of acceptance as one of unconditional acceptance of the tender - It was a letter requiring the appellant to deposit a further sum of Rs. 700 after the tender was accepted and the contract came into existence - It was not obligatory on the appellant to comply with the request if it was not a part of the condition of the tender - Appeal dismissed.
