Â Rev. Sidhajbhai Sabhai and Others V v State of Bombay and Another

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 259; AIR 1963 SC 540; 1962 KerLJ 135; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 837

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Religious Denomination

Summary: Constitution - Education - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(f), 19(1)(g), 26 and 30 - (A) Whether interference with right of management of educational institution by Govt. infringe art. 19(1)(f) ? - Held, interference by State Govt. by an executive order with right of bare management of an educational institution does not amount to infringement of right to property under art. 19(1)(f) - Word 'property' in art. 19(1)(f) must doubtless be extended to all those recognised types of interest which have insignia or characteristics of proprietary rights, but it cannot be said that Society maintaining educational institution has any such rights vested in it - Nor is right of society guaranteed under art. 19(1)(f) infringed by executive action - (B) Whether an educational institution be regarded as institution for charitable purpose u/art. 26(1)? - Further held, in a larger sense, an educational institution may be regarded as charitable - (C) Whether restriction on educational institution be imposed u/art. 30(1)? - Further held, unlike art. 19, fundamental freedom to establish and administer educational institutions by minorities guaranteed u/art. 30(1) is absolute in terms - It is not made subject to any reasonable restrictions of nature fundamental freedoms enunciated in art. 19 may be subjected to - All minorities, linguistic or religious have by art. 30(1) an absolute right to establish and administer educational institutions of their choice - Any law or executive direction which seeks to infringe substance of that right under art. 30(1) would to that extent be void - (D) Whether state can discriminate in giving grants on basis that particular education institution is a minority institution? - Further held, art. 31(2) is only a phase of non-discrimination clause of Constitution and does not derogate from provisions made in art. 31(1) - Clause is moulded in terms negative - State is thereby enjoined not to discriminate in granting aid to educational institutions on ground that management of institution is in hands of a minority, religions or linguistic, but form is not susceptible of inference that State is competent otherwise to discriminate so as to impose restrictions upon substance of right to establish and administer educational institutions by minorities, religious or linguistic - (E) Whether imposing regulation for receiving grants by minority institution is valid? - Further held, right established by art. 30(1) is intended to be a real right for protection of minorities in matter of setting up of educational institutions of their own choice - Right is intended to be effective and is not to be whittled down by so called regulative measures conceived in interest not of minority educational institution but of public or nation as whole - If every order which while maintaining formal character of a minority institution destroys power of administration is held justifiable because it in public or national interest, though not in its interest as an educational institution, right guaranteed by art. 30(1) will be but a 'teasing illusion', a promise of unreality - Regulations which may lawfully be imposed either by legislative or executive action as a condition of receiving grant or of recognition must be directed to making institution while retaining its character as a minority institution effective as educational institution - Such regulation must satisfy a dual test - Test of reasonableness, and test that it is regulative of educational character of institution and is conducive to making institution an effective vehicle of education for minority community or other persons who resort to it - Appeal allowed.
