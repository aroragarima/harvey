M. Nizamudeen v Chemplast Sanmar Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 158; (2010) 4 SCC 240; AIR 2010 SC 1765; JT 2010 (2) SC 623; 2010 (4) MLJ(SC) 333; 2010(2) SCALE 792; 2010 (3) S.T.R. 315; 2011 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 127

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Water

Keywords: Hazardous Substances, Manufacture, Storage and Import of Hazardous Chemical Rules, 1989

Summary: Water - Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Coastal Zone Management Plan of Tamil Nadu, 1996 - Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974 - Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981 - Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 - Hazardous Chemicals (Manufacture, Storage and Import) Rules, 1989 - Respondent proposed to set up a project for manufacturing Poly-Vinyl Chloride (PVC) - Environmental Impact Assessment Report (EIA) as well as Risk Analysis Report (RA) for the proposed PVC project was obtained - One of the raw-materials for manufacturing PVC was Vinyl Chloride Monomer (VCM) which was to be imported - Respondent in their proposal also proposed to install a Marine Terminal Facility (MTF) near the seashore for receiving and transferring VCM from the ships to the PVC plant through underground pipeline - Ministry of Environment and Forests, Government of India (MOEF) after examining proposal submitted by the Respondent granted environmental clearance under the provisions of Coastal Regulation Zone Notification, 1991 (1991 Notification) - TNPCB in light of the environmental clearance granted by the MOEF accorded its consent for the PVC plant as well as MTF and pipeline project of the Respondent - Executive Engineer granted permission for carrying seawater and raw-materials through pipelines - However, Executive Engineer, cancelled the aforesaid permission observing that VCM may cause pollution and health hazard to the public - Writ petition filed thereagainst was allowed - Appellant filed PIL before the HC praying therein that the order passed by the Executive Engineer be quashed - HC dismissed the same - Hence, present appeal - A writ petition u/art. 32 of the Constitution also filed praying therein that the permission granted by the MOEF be quashed - (A) Whether Uppanar river and its banks at the point where pipelines pass, fall in the Coastal Regulation Zone (CRZ) III area? - Held, Uppanar river and its banks at the relevant place where the pipelines laid by the Respondent pass do not fall under CRZ III area as per 1996 Plan and no environmental clearance was needed for such pipelines - Stand of the MOEF that they have granted permission to the onshore pipelines insofar as these pass through the CRZ abutting the sea, i.e. 500 meters from the HTL and no clearance was granted as it was not required for laying of pipelines under the Uppanar river was correct - (B) Whether paragraph 2(ii) of 1991 Notification restricts transfer of VCM (hazardous substance) beyond port area to the PVC plant through pipelines? - Held, this paragraph prohibits manufacture or handling or storage or disposal of hazardous substances, as specified in the Notifications issued by MOEF, except transfer of hazardous substances from ships to ports, terminals and refineries and vice-versa, in the port areas - VCM is hazardous substance - By applying purposive construction, the expression, 'in the port areas' should be read as, 'in or through the port areas' - Exception in paragraph 2 (ii) then would achieve its objective and read, 'except transfer of hazardous substances from ships to ports, ships to terminals and ships to refineries and vice versa, in or through the port areas' - This construction will be harmonious with paragraph 3(2)(ii) which permits the activity of laying pipelines in the CRZ area - No infirmity in the permission granted by the MOEF on 19th December, 2005 - No illegality in the permission granted by the Executive Engineer on February 27, 2008 either - Petitions dismissed.
