Mansukhlal and Brothers v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay City Ii

Supreme Court of India

03 September 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 266; AIR 1969 SC 835; [1969] 73 ITR 546; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 970

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Avoidance Of Tax

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Concealment of amount - Assessment - Levy of penalty - Assessee firm dealing in hessian, twines, gunny bags, etc, on wholesale basis - For the A/Y 1948-49 the assessee had returned income amounting to Rs. 45, 904 - Income Tax Officer added two items of Rs. 24, 000 and Rs. 90, 000 as profits and income from undisclosed sources - Penalty of Rs. 62, 000 was imposed within the maximum limits provided by s. 28(1)(c) of Act - Appellate Assistant Commissioner found that only item of Rs. 24, 000 be treated as concealed income and other amount of Rs. 90, 000 could not be treated as such for purpose of imposing a penalty and imposed a penalty of Rs.20, 000 only on the assessee - Appellate Tribunal agreed with Appellate Assistant Commissioner that there was no concealment in the matter in respect of Rs. 90, 000 but affirmed finding of concealment of amount of Rs. 24,000 - Whether computing for purposes of levy of penalty u/s. 28(1)(c) the amount of income-tax and super-tax would have been avoided - Held, HC was right in holding that word avoided does not mean evaded and that it has been used in sense of escapement - Income as shown in return been accepted as correct and 1 1/2 times of said amount would be maximum limit within which penalty can be imposed - S.22 of Act, contains provisions relating to return of income and the words income as returned would clearly mean income as disclosed or shown in the return filed u/s.22 - Penalties which have been provided by s.28(1) were meant for acts of omission or commission which were set out therein and once an assessee was proved to have been guilty of them the penal provisions were attracted and with reference to cl. (c) of s.28(1) of Act irrespective of the amount concealed- Appeal dismissed.
