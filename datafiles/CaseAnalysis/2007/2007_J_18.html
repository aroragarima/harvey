Union of India and Others v Jai Prakash Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1237; (2007) 10 SCC 712; AIR 2007 SC 1363; 2007 (3) ALJ 252; 2007(3) ALL MR 393; 2007 (67) ALR 504; 2007 (2) AWC 1604; 2007(4) SCALE 299; 2007 (5) SCJ 196; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 757

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Respondent 1 in petition alleged that he was not granted permission to operate Gramin PCO - Prayer of petitioner could not be accepted in view of the guidelines - High Court by a cryptic non-reasoned order held that the conditions in the guidelines 'appear to be arbitrary' and hence violative of art. 14 - Whether judgment passed by HC is sustainable? - On plainest consideration of justice, HC to have set forth its reasons, howsoever brief, in its order indicative of an application of its mind, all the more when its order is amenable to further avenue of challenge - Absence of reasons has rendered HC's judgment not sustainable - Reasons are live links between the mind of the decision taker to the controversy in question and the decision or conclusion arrived at - Reasons substitute subjectivity by objectivity - Appeal allowed.
