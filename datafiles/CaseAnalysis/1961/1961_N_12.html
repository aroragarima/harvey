State of Punjab v Nathu Ram

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 89; AIR 1962 SC 89; 1962 (1) ALT(SC) 306; 1961 (2) KerLR 548; 1961 (2) MLJ 182; 1961 (2) MLJ(SC) 182; 1961 (2) SCJ 637; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 636

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Defence & Security Forces; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Defence of India Act, 1939 - Punjab Govt. acquired on lease certain parcels of land belonging to Labhu Rain and Nathu Ram, for different military purposes - Labhu Ram and Nathu Ram, brothers, refused to accept compensation offered to them by collector and applied to Punjab Govt., through collector - State Govt. referred matter to an arbitrator as required u/r. 10, who, after enquiry, passed an award ordering payment of an amount higher than what was offered by collector - State Govt. appealed against award to High of Punjab - During pendency of appeal, Labhu Ram, one of respondents, died - HC, holding that appeal abated against Labhu Ram and that its effect was that appeal against Nathu Ram also abated, dismissed appeal - What is effect of abatement of appeal against Labhu Ram on State appeal against Nathu Ram? - Held, in view of O. XXII, r.4, CPC, 1908 appeal abated against Labhu Ram, deceased, when no application for bringing on record his LRs. had been made within time limited by law - Code does not provide for abatement of appeal against other respondents - If court can deal with matter in controversy so far as regards rights and interests of appellant and respondents other than deceased respondent, it has to proceed with appeal and decide it - It is therefore, necessary to determine, on facts of this case, whether state appeal could proceed against Nathu Ram - Award of arbitrator in each of these cases was a joint one, in favour of both respondents Labhu Ram and Nathu Ram - Result of abatement of appeal against Labhu Ram is therefore, that his legal representatives are entitled to get compensation on basis of this award, even if they are to be paid separately on calculating their rightful share in land acquired, for which this compensation is decreed - Such calculation is foreign to appeal between State of Punjab and Nathu Ram - Decree in appeal will have to determine not what Nathu Ram's share in this compensation is but what is correct amount of compensation with respect to land acquired for which this compensation has been awarded by arbitrator - Subject matter for which compensation is to be calculated is one and same - Appeal before HC was an appeal against a decree jointly in favour of Labhu Ram and Nathu Ram - Appeal against Nathu Ram alone cannot be held to be properly constituted when appeal against Labhu Ram had abated - In absence of one joint-decree holder, appeal is not properly framed - It follows that State appeal against Nathu Ram alone cannot proceed - Appeal dismissed.
