K. T. Veerappa and Others v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Lokeshwar Singh Panta, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 126; (2006) 9 SCC 406; (2006) SCC (L&S) 1823; JT 2006 (6) SC 408; 2006(4) SCALE 293; 2006 (5) SLR 698

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Karnataka High Court Act, 1961, Karnataka Civil Services (Revised Pay) Rules, 1970

Summary: Karnataka Civil Services (Revised Pay) Rules, 1970 - Appellants are non-teaching staff in the University of Mysore - Revision of pay scales to the University employees - Order of Single Judge directing University of Mysore to extend the revised pay scales of 1977 and the subsequent revision to the petitioners- appellants and pay the difference of monetary benefits to them within two months - Division Bench ordered that it was not permissible on the part of Single Judge to issue a direction of the nature impugned - Appeals against - Held benefits having been given to 23 employees of the University in compliance with the decision dated 21.6.1989 recorded by Single Judge - Without resorting to any of the methods the other employees identically placed, including the appellants, would have been given the same benefits - Defence of State Government that as appellants were not petitioners in writ petition filed by 23 employees to whom benefit of revised pay scales was granted by Court, appellants are estopped from raising their claim of revised pay scales in the year 1992-94, is wholly unjustified, patently irrational, arbitrary and discriminatory - Appeals allowed.
