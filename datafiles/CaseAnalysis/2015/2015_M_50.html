Pukhrem Sharatchandra Singh v Mairembam Prithviraj @ Prithibiraj Singh

Supreme Court of India

01 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 659; AIR 2015 SC 3783; 2015(6) ALL MR 424; 2015 (113) ALR 746; 2016 (2) ALT(SC) 59; 2015 (6) AWC 5638; 2015 (6) Bom.C.R. 282; 2015 (4) CLT(SC) 107; JT 2015 (9) SC 113; 2015 (7) MLJ 378; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 626; 2015(10) SCALE 285B; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 591; 2016 (3) WLN(SC) 62

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Constituency, Candidate, Representation Of The People Act, 1951, Purity

Summary: Election - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 173 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 36(2), 86(7) - Challenge to election - Adjournment of case - Determination - Appellant and respondent contested for election in which respondent became successful and was declared as member of legislative assembly - Appellant objected to nomination of respondent as per s. 36(2) of Act on ground that he had failed to file proper affidavit as prescribed u/art. 173 of Constitution and further affidavit was forged as respondent has falsely stated about his education qualification - Further, Returning Officer declined to reject nomination - Further, HC passed order in favor of respondent granting adjournment of case - Appellant contended that every election petition shall be tried as expeditiously as possible and respondent has been successful in getting matter adjourned on numerous occasions by filing variety of applications - Hence, present appeal.

Held, election petition has to be decided in quite promptitude as there is obligation cast upon Court to dispose of same within period of six months and not to allow parties to take resort to unnecessary adjournments or file vexatious applications. SC held that elected candidate has been taking time at his own pleasure and leisure and filing applications as he desired giving vent to his whim and fancy and Court has granted adjournment in extremely liberal manner. Further, SC held that respondent has filed series of applications and petition pending before HC has to be decided with extreme alertness and in quite promptitude. As court has not framed issues, it shall proceed to frame issues. Thereafter, evidence shall commence and court shall, regard being had to statutory command and norms in democratic polity, dispose of election petition. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - Election petition has to be disposed of within specified period of time as fundamental purpose for expeditious disposal of election petition is to sustain purity of parliamentary democracy.
