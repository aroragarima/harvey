State of Uttar Pradesh v Rajiv Gupta

Supreme Court of India

14 July 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 357; (1994) 5 SCC 686; 1994 (2) CCC 629; 1994 (2) RentLR 213; 1994(3) SCALE 552; [1994] Supp1 S.C.R. 686; 1994 (2) UJ 348

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 6, 11, 11-A and 30 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Land Acquisition - Before award could be made respondents filed writ petition seeking direction to respondents to take possession of lands after paying them due compensation - High Court allowed writ petition and directed State to take possession of lands and pay compensation immediately, as per award - Whether order of HC is valid in law? - Held, since prior approval was not given before expiry of award date, there is no award made by Land Acquisition Officer u/s. 11 of Act - S. 11-A is mandatory and on expiry of two year s from date of publication of declaration entire proceedings under Act stood lapsed - Therefore, HC is not right and direction to take further steps in that behalf are clearly illegal - Appeal allowed.
