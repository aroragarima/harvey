K. Ramadas Shenoy v Chief Officer, Town Municipal Council, Udipi and

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, A.N. Ray, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 648; (1975) SCC (Cr) 746; (1976) 1 SCC 24; AIR 1976 SC 994; 1975 (1) ALR 546; 1976 CRLJ 722; 1975 UJ 705

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss. 12(1), expln and 2(a) - Contempt of Court - (A) Supreme Court Judgment declaring resolution of Town Municipal Council allowing conversion of certain lecture hall into a cinema hall as bad - Ground, contravention of Town Planning Scheme - Meeting of Municipal Council - Resolution recommending to Government to revoke scheme - Nor do Chief Officer and President give any direction to the licencee to stop exhibition of the films in the hall as they had no power to do so - If guilty - (B) Apology - Exhibitor of film tendering apology in case he was found guilty of contempt - (C) Petition for revoking licence to exhibit films on basis of SC judgment - DM refusing to act on photostat copy of judgment unless a certified copy was produced - Granted renewal of license for few days under bona fide belief that it was not prohibited - No wilful disobedience - Held, Chief Officer and President were not guilty of any contempts but the licencee was guilty of contempt in exhibiting the films in the hall which could not be used as cinema hall according to the judgment of the SC - (B) Exhibition of film contrary to SC judgment found to be in bona fide belief - Held, such conditional apology accepted - (C) Held no contempt - Order accordingly .
