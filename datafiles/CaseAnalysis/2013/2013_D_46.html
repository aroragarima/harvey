Master Mallikarjun v Divisional Manager, National Insurance Company Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

26 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 540; (2014) 14 SCC 396; 2013 (3) ACC 924; 2013 ACJ 2445; AIR 2014 SC 736; 2013 (101) ALR 271; 2013 (6) AWC 5740; 2013 (6) Bom.C.R. 69; 2014 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 200; 2013 (172) PLR 745; 2013 (4) RCR(Civil) 295; 2013 (4) RLW 3224; 2013(10) SCALE 668; [2013] 8 S.C.R. 268; 2013 (4) WLN(SC) 57

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: (1) Motor Vehicles Act, 1988-Sections 166 and 168-Compensation-For disability-Enhancement-Case of child victim-Child aged 12 years suffered disability in motor accident-Tribunal and High Court not properly appreciated medical evidence available in case-Age of child and deformities on his body resulting in disability not duly taken note of-Appellant had longer period of hospitalisation for about two months causing also inconveniences and loss of earning to parents-Claimant entitled to total compensation of R 3,75,000 alongwith interest @ 6% p.a. instead of R 1,09,500 as awarded by High Court-Impugned order of High Court-Modified.(2) Motor Vehicles Act, 1988-Section 168-Case of child victims-What is just and fair compensation to be awarded to child, who suffered disability in motor accident?-Difficult to have accurate assessment of compensation in case of children suffering disability on account of motor vehicle accident-However, main elements of damage in case of child victims are pain, shock, frustration, deprivation of ordinary pleasures and enjoyment associated with healthy and mobile limbs-Compensation awarded should enable child to acquire-Something or to develop lifestyle which will offset to some extent inconvenience or discomfort arising out of disability-Appropriate compensation for disability should take care of all non-pecuniary damages.
