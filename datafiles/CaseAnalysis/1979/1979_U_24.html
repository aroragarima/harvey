Jaswant Singh and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 August 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 193; (1979) 4 SCC 440; (1980) SCC (L&S) 36; AIR 1980 SC 115; 1979 LabIC 1362; 1979 (2) LLJ 371; 1980 (1) LLN 44; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 420; 1979 SLJ 575; 1979 (2) SLR 375

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Central Civil Services (Temporary Service) Rules, 1965 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 and 32 - Punjab Reorganization Act, 1966, ss. 78, 79 and 80 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 12, 18(3)(d) and 25 J(1) - Whether petitioners are employees of Central Govt; if so, whether their conditions of service are governed by rules which apply to temporary employees of Central Govt; and lastly, whether orders of retrenchment proposed or passed against them are violative of art. 14 and 16 of Constitution? - Held, Beas Project was to be undertaken by Central Govt. on behalf of successor States and State of Rajasthan but direct and immediate responsibility to construct and complete works of Beas Project was imposed by statute on Central Govt. and not on successor States and State of Rajasthan - Conditions of service will be primarily governed by terms of their appointment but, if they are entitled to benefit of any of rules of Central Civil Services (Temporary Service) Rules 1965, they may make representations in that behalf to appropriate authorities - Since very basis of their claim is fallacious, as they have no right to be transferred to employment of BM Board, their claim to equal treatment with deputationists has to fall with it - Proposed retrenchment of the petitioners does not offend against the guarantee of equality contained in arts. 14 and 16 of the Constitution, since the petitioners and the deputationists belong to two different and distinct classes - Appeal dismissed.
