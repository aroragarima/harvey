Uttamrao Shivdas Jankar v Ranjitsinh Vijaysinh Mohite-Patil

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 706; (2009) 13 SCC 131; AIR 2009 SC 2975; JT 2009 (9) SC 111; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 452; 2009(9) SCALE 18; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 538

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Corrupt Practices, Election Petition, Election Agents, Electoral Roll, Grounds For Declaring Election To Be Void, Material Particulars, Onus Of Proof, Representation Of People Act, 1951, Disqualifications For Membership, Any Other Person, Conduct of Election Rules, 1961, Allegations Of Corrupt Practice, Union Territories, Improper Rejection, Non-Joinder Of Necessary Parties, Public Notice Of Election

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 36(1)(2)(5), 100, 100(1)(a)(c), 2(i) and 116A - Conduct of Election Rules, 1961 - 'Handbook for Returning Officers' by Election Commission of India, Chapter VI, paras. 1, 2, 6 and 7 - (A) What is the extent of jurisdiction of a returning officer(RO) to determine a question as to whether a nomination paper filed by an applicant to enable him to contest an election in terms of the provision of the Act, 1951 on the premise that the names of the proposers were forged? - Election to Maharashtra Legislative Council Biennial Elections, 2003 - Submission of nomination papers and scrutiny thereof - Scrutiny to be completed before 11.00 am of next day of filing nomination papers - 10 proposers proposed for appellant's name - Signatures of two alleged to be forged, in which regard such proposers also filed affidavits affirming that they had not signed the nomination papers of appellant - Affidavits filed by appellant also - On request of appellant records of Municipal Council of which such proposers were members, called for, for purpose of signature verification - Scrutiny was adjourned for one day; RO compared the signatures in presence of such proposers; thereafter finding was given that signatures are not genuine, thus nomination of appellant was rejected - Election petition preferred before HC, interalia questioning mode and manner in which decision making process had been resorted to by the RO - HC framed issues - One concession was made by Counsel of appellant as to non-raising of contention with regard to genuineness of signatures, on which basis HC had proceeded - Dismissal of petition - Present appeal - Object and scheme of Act examined, provisions of Act, guidelines by ECI, materials on record, perused and contentions heard - Important deliberations entered into upon various essential aspects - Questions which arose for consideration, (i) Whether the Returning Officer having shifted the onus of proof upon the appellant committed an error in its decision making process? - Held, yes - (A)(i) RO an important statutory authority who has quasi judicial powers; by its order the fulcrum of the democratic process, viz. election can be set at naught; thus the effect of improper rejection of nomination paper can be comprehended; once a finding is made as to such impropriety, election has to be set aside; impropriety would take within its umbrage not only the decision making process but also merit of the decision; as per handbook opportunity is to be given to candidate to rebut the objections raised against nomination by placing sufficient materials on record and a presumption has to be raised as to validity of nomination papers; no such exercise found to be undertaken by RO, on examining process it undertook; when there exists a presumption in favour of a party, it is for the other party to adduce evidence; and in instant case signatures were not found to be prima facie discrepant by RO; appellant affirmed vide affidavits his case and further through affidavits of other prospers; thus RO had proceeded on the basis that it was for the appellant to produce any evidence; RO was under an obligation to draw a presumption which he did not - (ii) Whether the High Court was correct in confining itself to the 'decision making process' on the part of the Returning Officer while determining the genuineness of signatures of the two proposers? - Held, No - HC acts as a Court of original jurisdiction in an election petition, and the election petition is a civil trial and the jurisdiction in such a trial, stricto sensu cannot be said to be appellate in nature - HC acted illegally in treating its power only as an appellate authority for it only proceeded to try and determine as to whether or not the decision making process is legal; HC should have dealt with the case in a adjudicatory manner basing its decision on merits, instead it acted otherwise and dismissed the petition without allowing the parties to produce evidence in support of their contention - Court debated upon scope of Judicial Review as evolved till date from point of view of s. 100, which provides that election be declared void if nomination papers has been improperly rejected; impropriety can be in the decision making process by RO and also in the incorrectness of the decision taken; Court observed that though there is a distinction between a decision making process and the merit of the decision; in the former, Court would apply the standard of judicial review; and in the later, it may enter into the merit of the matter; but concept of JR has now evolved to the extent that its scope viz., illegality, irrationality and procedural impropriety has now extended, to include 'an error of fact touching the merit of the decision vis-a-vis the decision making process' within its ambit - (iii) Whether the purported concession was wrong and in any event, by reason thereof, the appellant was precluded from adducing any evidence in regard to the genuineness of the signatures of the such proposers, to which he was otherwise entitled to? - Held, yes - A statutory right of a party to file an election petition cannot and should not be denied only on the basis of a wrong concession made by a Counsel - Impugned judgment set aside - Matter remitted to HC for consideration afresh - Directions issued - Appeal allowed.
