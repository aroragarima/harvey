Abdul Sathar Haji Moosa Sait Dharmastapanam v Commissioner of Agricultural Income Tax, Kerala

Supreme Court of India

07 March 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 235; (1974) 3 SCC 257; AIR 1974 SC 1795; 1973 (2) CTR 423; [1973] 91 ITR 5; 1974 TAXLR 480

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Agricultural Income-Tax

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution of India, 1950 - Kerala Agricultural Income Tax Act, 1950, s. 4(b) - Income of property bequeathed under will divided into four parts, 1/4th for public charitable purpose, 2/4ths for giving assistance to poor relations of testator and remaining 1/4th for augmenting corpus - HC agreed with assessee that first 1/4th should be considered as charitable trust but rejected contention of assessee in other respects - Hence instant appeal - Whether the part of bequest was considered as a public charitable trust - Held, that the HC was right as the considered part of the bequest could not be considered as a public charitable trust - Will provided that if purpose of will becomes impossible of performance then 3/4ths of income should be expended as provided in paragraph 8 and remaining 1/4th utilised for purpose of augmentation of corpus - 3/4ths of income of B schedule properties was primarily earmarked for benefit of near relations of testator - Appeal dismissed.
