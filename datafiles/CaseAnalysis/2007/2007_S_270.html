Bishnu Prasad Sinha & Anr v State Of Assam

Supreme Court of India

16 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 31; (2007) 11 SCC 467; AIR 2007 SC 848; 2007 (2) ALT 187; 2007 CRLJ 1145; JT 2007 (2) SC 428; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 724; 2007(2) SCALE 42; 2007 (4) SCJ 293; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 916

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bride Burning, Mitigating Circumstance, Extra Judicial Confession

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376(2)(g), 302, 201 r/w 34 - Appellants were convicted and sentenced to death by Sessions Judge and confirmed by High Court - Appeal against - Held, entire prosecution case is based on circumstantial evidences - A confession which is not retracted even at a later stage of the trial and even accepted by the accused in his examination under s. 313 of Cr. P.C, can be fully relied upon - Apart from conduct, confessional statement, which is admissible in evidence under s. 164 Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, is clear pointer to the guilt - Appellants had rightly been found guilty of committing the offence - Not a case where extreme death penalty should be imposed - Punishment of rigorous imprisonment for life imposed - Appeal dismissed.
