Â Maharana Shri v State of Gujrat

Supreme Court of India

22 December 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, A.K. Sarkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 329; AIR 1962 SC 821; 1962 CRLJ 103; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 411

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Bombay Land Revenue Code, 1879, Bombay Tenancy Act, Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands (Amendment) Act, Gujarat Taluqdars Act, 1888, Bombay Taluqdari Tenure Abolition Act, 1949, Bombay Land Tenure Abolition Laws (Amendment) Act, 1958

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Labour Welfare Fund Act, 1953 - Bombay Land Revenue Code, 1879 - Bombay Land Tenure Abolition Laws (Amendment) Act, 1958 - Bombay Taluqdari Tenure Abolition Act, 1949 - Bombay Tenancy Act - Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands (Amendment) Act - Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948 - Gujarat Taluqdars Act, 1888 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Whether ss. 3 and 4 r/w s. 6 of Bombay Land Tenure Abolition Laws (Amendment) Act, 1958 are unconstitutional? - Held, u/s. 3, if any one of three circumstances mentioned therein exists, then by a fiction of law a person who fulfils that circumstance must be deemed to be a permanent tenant - For application of s. 4, a tenant must be in possession of tenure-land on date of commencement of Taluqdari Abolition Act, 1949 (15-08-1950) and further more must have been in possession of same tenure-land or of any other tenure-land for continuous periods aggregating more than twelve years immediately before said date - A person who fulfills aforesaid two conditions shall be deemed to be a permanent tenant of land unless it is proved by tenure-holder that he would not have been a permanent tenants of basis of possession referred to in cl (b) - In other words, s. 4 of Impugned Act, 1958, completely changes definition of a permanent tenant, creates a new class of permanent tenants who were not permanent tenants on D/-01-04-1957 - There is a good deal to be said in favour of this view, then s. 4 of Impugned Act, 1958 in spite of giving tenure -holder an opportunity of proving that tenant was not in possession for an aggregate continuous period of twelve years u/s. 4 r/w s. 6, undoubtedly changes very definition of permanent tenant and by that change wipes out a large part of purchase price which petitioners were entitled to get on 01-04-1957 from some of their tenants - On this view of s. 4, impugned legislation would be unconstitutional inasmuch as it would bring within category of permanent tenants persons who were non-permanent tenants under previous law and there by deprive tenure-holders of part of purchase money which they were to get from them - This combined effect of ss. 3, 4 and 6 of Impugned Act, 1958 does deprive tenure-holder of any real opportunity of contesting claims of his tenants and makes them permanent tenants once they are recorded in record of rights, thereby depriving tenure-holder of purchase price which he was entitled to get from them u/s. 32H of Tenancy Act, 1948 - Petitions allowed.
