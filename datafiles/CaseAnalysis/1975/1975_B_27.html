Jose Da Costa and Another v Bascora Sadasiva Sinai Narcornim and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 August 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 368; (1976) 2 SCC 917; AIR 1975 SC 1843

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Legal Proceedings

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 132, 133, 134, 136(1)(c)(3), 239 and 240 - Goa, Daman and Diu (Extension of CPC and Arbitration Act), 1965, s. 4(1)(c) - Judgment of Judicial Commr. - Plea of prescription rejected on ground that Reclamacao not filed before Judicial Commr. - Appeal to Supreme Court - Applicability of proviso to s. 4(1)(c), Goa, Daman and Diu (Extension of CPC and Arbitration Act), 1965 - (A) Whether appellants in appeal filed before Supreme Court from a decision on Reclamco can be debarred from canvassing plea of prescription? - Procedural provisions of Portuguese Code relating to Reclamacao, and appeal from a decision on Reclamacao from High Court in Goa, Daman and Diu stood repealed and superseded by extended Indian laws when judgment now under appeal was rendered - Held, in an appeal filed before Supreme Court appellants, who failed to file any reclamacao in Judicial Commr.'s Court regarding a plea of prescription raised by them in lower Courts, cannot be debarred from canvassing plea of prescription - (B) Whether in right of appeal institution of a suit carries with it that all successive appeals available under law then in force would be preserved to parties to suit? - While provisions of statute dealing merely with matters of procedure may properly, unless that construction be textually inadmissible, have retrospective effect attributed to them, provisions which touch a right in existence at passing of statute are not to be applied retrospectively, in absence of express enactment or necessary intendment - Held, right of appeal being a substantive right, institution of a suit carries with it implication that all successive appeals available under law then in force would be preserved to parties to suit throughout rest of career of suit - Order accordingly.
