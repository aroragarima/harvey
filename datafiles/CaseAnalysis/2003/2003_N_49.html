M/s. Tamil Nadu State Transport Corporation v Natarajan and others

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 442; (2003) 6 SCC 137; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1295; 2003 (2) ACC 1; AIR 2003 SC 2232; 2003 (4) ALD(SC) 59; 2003 (51) ALR 574; 2003 (3) AWC 2501; 2004 (1) Bom.C.R. 367; [2003] 114 Comp Cas 685; 2003 (3) JhrCR(SC) 124; JT 2003 (4) SC 446; 2003 (3) KHCACJ 80; 2003(4) SCALE 527; 2003 (1) SCW 2680; 2003 (3) SLT 509; 2003 (3) Supreme 698

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Joint Liability

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - accident - liability for compensation - contention that there was no justification in law for the Division Bench to suo motu implead the Corporation as a party in the Letters Patent Appeal and to fasten liability to the extent of 50% of the total sum awarded on the Corporation - held, since the Corporation was not at fault and the accident was caused because of the contributory negligence of the drivers of both the buses, the Corporation could not be held liable under the provision of Motor Vehicle Act. - The Division Bench of the High Court committed a serious error in apportioning and fastening 50% liability of compensation on the appellant/Corporation.
