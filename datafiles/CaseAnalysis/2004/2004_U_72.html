Pritam Singh v Union of India and Ors

Supreme Court of India

22 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1482; (2005) SCC (L&S) 728; (2005) 9 SCC 748; AIR 2004 SC 4750; 2004 (6) ALD(SC) 66; 2004 (4) AWC 3224; 2004 (3) CLR 763; 2004 (103) FLR 310; JT 2004 (7) SC 576; 2005 (1) LLJ 6; 2004 (4) LLN 262; 2004(8) SCALE 116; 2004 (6) SLR 84; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 608

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Services Conduct Rules, 1966, rr. 31(i) and 31(ii) - Order of compulsory retirement - Validity of - Whether the act of appellant to give 'absentee' statement to a delinquent employee and his use of such statement in disciplinary proceedings against him, amounts to act of misconduct and leakage of official information? - Held, appellant being superintendent Grade II and in charge of information acted bonafide in good faith while supplying information - Supply of information neither misconduct nor serious mistake - No material to show compulsory retirement was in public interest - Relied on State of Gujarat and Anr v. Suryakant Chunilal Shah (1998 Indlaw SC 898) - In case of compulsory retirement public interest would be the primary consideration - Appeal allowed.
