M/S Telestar Travels Pvt. Ltd. & Ors. v Special Director Of Enforcement

Supreme Court of India

13 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 90; (2013) 9 SCC 549; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 1041; 2013 (3) ALD(SC) 72; 2013 (3) AWC 2366; 2013 (1) CLT(SC) 379; [2013] 178 Comp Cas 321; 2013 (4) CompLJ 228; 2013 (289) E.L.T. 3; JT 2013 (3) SC 157; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 3; 2013(2) SCALE 519; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 1005

Case Digest

Subject: Fema; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947, Adjudication Rules

Summary: FEMA - Indirect Tax - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973, ss. 8, 14 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Cheap seaman tickets - Quantum of penalty - Validity - Appellant carries on a travel agency and specialises in booking of tickets for crew members working on ships - Appellant approached another travel agency in UK for getting cheap seaman tickets out of money so received and transfer funds to 'Y' towards price of tickets apart from realising 3% of ticket price towards commission payable to appellant - Show cause notice was issued by Directorate/respondents to appellants - Appellants filed their replies in which they denied allegations that 'X' was a paper company same was being controlled from India by appellants - Adjudicating Authority passed an order holding appellants guilty of violation of provisions of ss. 8 and 14 of the 1973 Act - Aggrieved by order appellants appealed to Appellate Tribunal - Tribunal, allowed appeals in part and to limited extent of reducing penalty imposed by Adjudicating Authority by 50% and affirmed findings recorded by Adjudicating Authority - On further appeals before HC were dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether there was any perversity or illegality in judgments of Courts below - Held, Adjudicating Authority had mainly relied on statements of appellants and documents seized in course of search of their premises - However, disclosure of documents to appellants and opportunity given to them to rebut and explain same was a substantial compliance with principles of natural justice - That being so, there was and could be no prejudice to appellants nor was any demonstrated by appellants before Courts below - Thus, no reason to interfere much less a compelling one to interfere with quantum of penalty imposed on appellants by Tribunal. The Adjudicating Authority had, as noticed earlier, imposed a higher penalty - Appeals dismissed.
