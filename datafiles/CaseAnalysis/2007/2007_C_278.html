Continental Foundation Joint Venture Sholding, Nathpa H.P. v Commissioner of Central Excise, Chandigarh

Supreme Court of India

29 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 986; (2007) 10 SCC 337; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 929; 2007 (216) E.L.T. 177; JT 2007 (11) SC 286; 2007(10) SCALE 459; 2007 (7) SCJ 1; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 554

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Trade

Keywords: Misstatement, Appeals allowed., Adjudicating Authorities

Summary: Excise - Section 11A, Central Excise Act, 1944 - Limitation - Extended period - Demand - Commissioner issued a show cause notice alleging that the construction companies were manufacturing Ready Mix Concrete (RMC) on which no central excise duty is being paid - Since RMC falls under Chapter Heading No.3824.20 of the Schedule to the Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985, duty is payable - Held, when the Revenue invokes the extended period of limitation under s. 11-A, the burden is cast upon it to prove suppression of fact - An incorrect statement cannot be equated with a willful misstatement - Held, factual scenario show that there was scope for entertaining doubt, and taking a particular stand which rules out application of s. 11A - There cannot be suppression or misstatement of fact, which is not wilful and yet constitute a permissible ground for the purpose of the proviso to s. 11A - Misstatement of fact must be willful - Adjudicating authorities were not justified in raising the demand.
