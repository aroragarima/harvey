Vimaleshwar Nagappa Shet v Noor Ahmed Sheriff & Ors

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 354; (2011) 12 SCC 658; AIR 2011 SC 2057; 2011 (4) ALD(SC) 189; 2011 (86) ALR 906; 2011 (4) AWC 3990; 2011 (2) CLT(SC) 417; JT 2011 (5) SC 476; 2011 (7) MLJ(SC) 402; 2011 (113) RD 528; 2011(5) SCALE 596; [2011] 6 S.C.R. 392

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 20 - Suit for specific performance - Execution of sale deed - 'X', Owner of the impugned property died leaving behind defendant nos. 1, 2 and 4 (sons), defendant nos. 5 to 7 (daughters) and defendant no. 3 (minor son) - After the demise of 'X', each of the surviving sons succeeded to an extent of 2/11th share and each of the daughters succeeded to 1/11th share in the impugned property - As the division in the scheduled property was impractical, defendant nos. 1, 2 and 4 to 7 desired to sell the impugned property and to distribute sale proceeds between them - Agreement of sale executed between plaintiff and defendant nos. 1, 2 and 4 to 7 and plaintiff paid a sum of Rs. 1,53,000/- in all to defendants - However, as the defendants did not execute the sale deed, the plaintiff filed a suit for specific performance - Trial Court decreed the suit in favour of the plaintiff and directed the defendants to execute the sale deed in terms of the agreement - On appeal, HC held that as defendant no. 3 was not a party to the agreement and he proposes to purchase the 9/11th share of the impugned property by paying value to the plaintiff, defendant no. 3 be directed to pay Rs. 11,42,590/-, the market value of 9/11th share of the impugned property within 3 months - Hence, the instant appeals - Whether order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, agreement of sale does not refer to defendant no. 3 at all or his share in the property - However, in the plaint, the plaintiff clearly admitted the share of defendant no. 3 who was a minor and the fact that no guardian was appointed for the minor - Without defendant no. 3 joining the other co-sharers, no agreement of sale could be entered with the plaintiff for the entire property including the minor's share - Consequently, the agreement of sale covering the entire property was void and ineffective - However, as defendant no. 3 was agreed to purchase the 9/11th share of impugned property and HC granted 3 months time to him to pay the said amount, directions of the HC to be applied and implemented - Impugned order of the HC upheld - Appeals dismissed.
