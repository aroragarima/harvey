State of Madhya Pradesh v Lattora

Supreme Court of India

13 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21460; (2003) 11 SCC 761

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 302, 364 - Murder - Conviction - Presumption - Accused/ respondent was alleged to have abducted one âLâ/deceased from his house - PW5 father of âLâ registered FIR and accused was arrested - Accused gave statement upon which body of âLâ was discovered - Appellant stood charged u/ss. 302, 364, 201 of IPC - Trial Court found guilty on accused and convicted him - First Appellate Court on appeal acquitted accused u/s. 302 of IPC and confirmed conviction u/s. 364 of IPC - Hence instant appeal.

Held, it is found that when it is proved that deceased was abducted and was found murdered, abductors should satisfy Court as to occurrence of events after abduction. If there is no explanation it is open to court to draw presumption that abductor is murderer also. In interest of justice, HC should consider appeal filed by accused all over again. It is to enable accused to canvass regarding conviction u/s. 364 of IPC also. If conviction is to be maintained HC has to consider how far presumption that abductor is murderer will apply to situation of this case. For enabling HC to reconsider appeal afresh judgment is set aside. Appeal filed before HC shall stand remitted to HC. Order accordingly.

Ratio - When deceased is proved to have been abducted by accused and is found murdered soon thereafter it is for abductors to satisfy Court as to how else abducted victim was dealt with by them.
