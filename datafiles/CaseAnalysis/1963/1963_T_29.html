Â Rai Ramkrishna and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 198; AIR 1963 SC 1667; 1963 (42) ILR(Pat) 796; [1963] 50 ITR 171; 1964 (2) SCJ 749; [1964] 1 S.C.R. 897

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution

Keywords: Competence Of The Legislature, Legislative Power, Reasonableness, Finance Act, 1950, Uttar Pradesh Trade Tax Act, 1948, Amending Act, Sales Tax Laws Validation Act, 1956, Bihar Taxation On Passengers And Goods (Carried By Public Service Motor Vehicles)

Summary: Constitution - Indirect Tax - Constitution of India, 1953 art. 19(5), 19(6), 19(1)(f), 19(1)(g) and 304(b) - Bihar Taxation On Passengers and Goods (Carried By Public Service Motor Vehicles) Act, 1961 - Issue arise - Whether retroactive operation of Act valid? - It is competent to appropriate legislature to pass validating law so as to make provisions of an earlier law effective from date when it was passed - Test of length of time covered by retrospective operation cannot by itself, necessarily be a decisive test to judge unreasonableness - Held, that if in its essential features a taxing statute is within legislative competence of legislature which passed it by reference to relevant entry in List, its character is not necessarily changed merely by its retrospective operation so as to make said retrospective operation outside legislative competence of said legislature - Having regard to all relevant facts of this case, restrictions imposed by said retrospective operation must be held to be reasonable and in public interest under art. 19(5) and (6) and also reasonable u/art. 304(b) - Challenge to validity of retrospective operation of Act cannot be sustained - Appeal dismissed.
