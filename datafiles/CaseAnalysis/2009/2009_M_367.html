Mustaq Ahmed Mohammed Isak and Others v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1987; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 449; (2009) 7 SCC 480; AIR 2009 SC 2772; 2009 CRLJ 3952; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 122; 2009(8) SCALE 642; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 465

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: TADA Act

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Maharashtra Control of Organized Crime Act, 1999, s. 21(2)(b) (MCOC) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 167(2) - Failure to complete the investigation within specified period - Appellants were booked under the provisions of MCOC - Prosecution filed application for the second extension of remand period for completing the investigation and to file charge-sheet - Trial Court allowed the application - Appellants filed bail application, challenging the order of remand - Trial Court dismissed the bail application - Appellant challenged the Trial Court order before HC - HC upheld the order of Trial Court - Whether the Trial Court has the power to grant second extension of remand - Trial Court can exercise power under the second proviso to s. 167(2) of CrPC to grant remand from time to time, however, the total period for filing charge sheet/challan cannot exceed 180 days - Held, respondent filed charge sheet on the 180th day as per the second proviso to s. 167(2) of CrPC - Appellant filed application challenging the extension only on the date on which the charge-sheet was submitted and no application for rejection on ground of default was filed prior to that - Even if the appeal before the HC was considered as the appellant's bail application as contended by the appellants the same was not tenable on default ground - Prosecution filed the charge sheet within the period of second extension itself, therefore, impugned HC order does not suffer any infirmity warrant interference - Appeal dismissed.
