Maheshwar Peri and others v High Court of Judicature At Allahabad, Through Registrar General

Supreme Court of India

30 June 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 473; AIR 2016 SC 3267; 2016 (119) ALR 890; 2016 CRLJ 3681; 2016 (5) CTC 215; 2016 (4) JCC 2307; JT 2016 (7) SC 482; 2016 (4) MLJ(Crl) 262; 2016 (3) RCR(Criminal) 910; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 213

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Limitation, Subordinate, Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, Constitution of India, 1950, Criminal

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 129, 215 - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss. 15, 20 - Suo moto contempt proceedings - Period of limitation -Appellants published an article dealt with Provident Fund Scam wherein names of Judges who were involved in the case was published - Aggrieved, respondents/advocates filed petition before HC for contempt proceedings - DB allowed petition holding that remarks were derogatory and lower the authority of higher Judiciary and took suo motu action - Hence, instant appeal - What is the period of limitation for suo motu initiation of contempt proceedings.

Held, criminal contempt of court subordinate to High Court can be initiated either suo motu or on a motion made by the Advocate General. The suo motu action is set in motion on a Reference made to it by the subordinate court. In view of the process involved in making the Reference by the subordinate court, in Pallav Sheth case 2001 Indlaw SC 19968, it has been held that the Reference is the starting point of the process of initiation of the Action for contempt. The application is the motion provided u/s. 15 of the Act. Any other application made by a person without the consent of the Advocate General, is not an application in the eyes of law. In the instant case, HC was clear in its mind that action u/s. 15 of the Act is initiated suo motu. It can only be suo motu because application filed by advocates was without the consent in writing of Advocate General. The only application other than by Advocate General, contemplated u/s.15 of the Act is the motion made by any person with the consent in writing of Advocate General. Being a jurisdiction which, when exercised, is fraught with serious consequences, Parliament has thought it justifiably fit to provide for such safeguards. Thus, impugned article, having been published on 10-11-2008 and HC having initiated suo motu action only on 28-04-2015, the same is hit by limitation of one year prescribed under the Act. Impugned order of HC was set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - No court shall initiate any proceedings of contempt, either on its own motion or otherwise, after expiry of a period of one year from the date on which contempt is alleged to have been committed.
