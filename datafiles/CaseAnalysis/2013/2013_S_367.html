Madhu @ Madhuranatha and another v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

28 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 763; (2014) 12 SCC 419; AIR 2014 SC 394; 2014 (1) JCC 809; 2014 (2) KarLJ 158; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 203; 2013(14) SCALE 502; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 947

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 364, 302, 201 - Kidnap and Murder - Circumstantial evidence - Conviction and death sentence - Sustainability - Appellants allegedly kidnapped and murdered the deceased - Trial Court convicted appellants u/ss. 364, 302, 201 IPC and sentenced them to death - HC confirmed conviction and death sentence - Whether conviction and death sentence recorded against appellants were sustainable - Held, courts below took into consideration evidence and appreciated the same meticulously - Courts below reached to a conclusion that appellants had committed crime - A number of witnesses deposed of seeing deceased in the company of appellants before incident - In cases where accused was last seen with deceased victim (last seen-together theory) just before incident, it became duty of accused to explain circumstances under which victim's death occurred - Courts below had not disbelieved either of witnesses - However, facts of the case did not warrant death penalty - Facts and circumstances did not meet the requirement of rarest of rare cases - Hence, appellants were awarded the sentence of 30 years without remission - Appeals disposed of.
