Commissioner of Customs (Preventive) v Aafloat Textiles (I) Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

16 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 798; (2009) 11 SCC 18; 2009 (235) E.L.T. 587; 2009(4) SCALE 94; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 490

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Fraudulent, Unfair Advantage, Innocent Misrepresentation

Summary: Customs - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 28, 28AB, 111(d), 111(o) and 114A - Demand - Interest - Penalty - Exemption under Notification No. 117/94-Cus., dt. 27-04-1997 denied to importer on ground that Special Import License (SIL) purchased by him from brokers for clearance of gold and silver was forged, therefore, not valid for consignment - Demand with interest as well as penalty raised against petitioner - Commissioner upheld said demand - On appeal, Tribunal cancelled said demand and held that appeal could be disposed of only on ground of limitation without going into merits of matter - Hence, present appeal - Whether demand with interest could be upheld? - Held, SILs were not genuine documents and were forged that was sufficient to extend period of limitation - Demand sustainable - Revenue's appeal allowed.
