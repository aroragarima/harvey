Manzoor Ahmed Margray v Gulam Hassan Aram & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

05 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 528; (1999) 7 SCC 703; AIR 2000 SC 191; JT 1999 (8) SC 34; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 380

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Orchard

Summary: Land & Property - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Specific Relief Act, 1963 - Plaintiff had entered into an agreement for sale of suit land in favour of plaintiff - Agreement further stated that defendant I had an authority from his younger brother and minor son, Defendant 2 and Defendant 3 respectively to sell land - As defendant refused to execute sale deed, plaintiff filed a suit for specific performance of contract - Defendant 1 submitted that he had offered to execute sale deed in favour of plaintiff qua his share - Defendant 2 feigned ignorance regarding agreement for sale - Defendant 3 contended that defendant 1 had no authority to permanently transfer his share in land - (A) Whether decree for specific performance is not required to be granted in view of s. 23 of Jammu and Kashmir Specific Relief Act as agreement itself provides a default clause to effect that in case of non - fulfillment of agreement, defaulting party shall pay to other, an amount of Rs. 10,000 as damages? - Held, aforesaid clause is strictly a penalty clause for securing performance of contract - This would not mean that contract is not to be performed - It would only mean that if there is a breach of some terms and conditions of contract, defaulting party has to pay penalty specified therein - (B) Whether decree should not be granted as plaintiff is not ready and willing to perform his part of contract and that there was delay in filing suit? - Further held, on basis of facts of case, it cannot be said that High Court erred in giving finding in favour of plaintiff that he was ready and willing to perform his part of contract - Suit is filed within period of limitation and there is no delay on part of plaintiff which would disentitle him to have equitable relief - (C) Whether contract is indivisible and there is no question of granting specific performance of 2nd and 3rd defendants' share? - Further held, s. 15 of Jammu and Kashmir Act makes it abundantly clear that where a party to a contract is unable to perform whole of his part of it, court may at suit of other party, direct party in default to perform specifically so much of his part of contract as he can perform - (D) Whether defendant 1 had no right to transfer share of his minor son in suit land? - Further held, land was purchased by father from his own funds and father was in fact owner of said property - Appeals disposed of.
