Vishwanath S/o Sitaram Agrawal v Sau. Sarla Vishwanath Agrawal

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 168; (2012) 7 SCC 288; AIR 2012 SC 2586; 2012 (6) ALD(SC) 86; 2012(4) ALL MR 506; 2012 (94) ALR 265; 2012 (5) ALT 1; 2012 (5) AWC 5026; 2012 (4) Bom.C.R. 501; 2012 (3) CLT(SC) 398; 2012 (4) CTC 839; 2012 (192) DLT 672; 2012 (6) MahLJ 1; 2012 (4) MPLJ 265; 2012 (2) OLR 456; 2012 (3) RCR(Civil) 702; 2012(6) SCALE 190; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 607; 2012 (3) WLN(SC) 1

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Hindu Marriage Laws (Amendment) Act, 1976, Hindu Married Women's Right to Separate Residence and Maintenance Act (19 of 1946

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 13(1)(ia) - Divorce - Cruelty - Denied - Challenged - Appellant (Husband) filed a petition for divorce u/s. 13(1)(ia) of Act on the ground of cruelty - Appellant submitted that the respondent (Wife) was left at the house of her parents and the appellant stayed in his house with the 2 sons - Appellant also submitted that a notice issued by her advocate was published in the daily newspaper stating that the appellant was a womanizer and addicted to liquor - Appellant further submitted that the respondent created a violent atmosphere in the house as well in the office by damaging the property and causing mental torture to the appellant - The asseverations made in the petition were controverted by the respondent stating that because of the involvement of the appellant with the 'X' (Another girl), he had concocted the story of cruelty and filed the petition for divorce - Trial Court dismissed said petition and also dismissed the application of the respondent for grant of permanent alimony - Trial court held that the impugned notice published in newspaper was to protect the interest of the sons in the property and the public notice was not unfounded or baseless - The first appellate court dismissed the appeal filed against said order - On appeal, HC held that there were concurrent findings of fact and no substantial question of law was involved - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether the appellant had made out a case for mental cruelty to entitle him to get a decree for divorce - Held, the conduct and circumstances make it graphically clear that the respondent had really humiliated him and caused mental cruelty - Her conduct clearly exposits that it has resulted in causing agony and anguish in the mind of the appellant - Respondent had made wild allegations about character of appellant in newspaper - Respondent had made an effort to prosecute him in criminal litigations which she had failed to prove - The feeling of deep anguish, disappointment, agony and frustration of the appellant was obvious - It was abundantly clear that with this mental pain, agony and suffering, the appellant cannot be asked to put up with the conduct of the respondent and to continue to live with her - Therefore, appellant was entitled to a decree for divorce - However, keeping in view the totality of the circumstances and the social strata from which the parties come from and regard being had to the business prospects of the appellant, permanent alimony of Rs.50 lacs to be fixed - Appeal allowed.
