Municipal Corporation, Faridabad v Siri Niwas

Supreme Court of India

06 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 719; (2004) 8 SCC 195; (2004) SCC (L&S) 1062; AIR 2004 SC 4681; 2004 (4) AWC 2847; 2004 (20) CLR 543; JT 2004 (7) SC 248; 2004 (3) LLJ 760; 2004(7) SCALE 437; 2004 (5) SLR 816; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 145; 2004 (3) UPLBEC 2627

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 25F - Retrenchment - Order of retrenchment by Industrial Tribunal - Adverse inference drawn by HC for not producing muster rolls by the appellants and consequent setting aside of award of Tribunal, whether proper? - Held, No - Provisions of the Indian Evidence Act per se are not applicable in an industrial adjudication - It is imperative for the Industrial Tribunal to see that the principles of natural justice are complied with - Order retrenching a workman would not be effective unless the conditions precedent therefore as stipulated in s 25F are satisfied - HC committed a manifest error in setting aside the award of the Tribunal only on the basis of adverse inference drawn against the Appellant for not producing the muster rolls - Impugned judgment set aside - Appeal allowed.
