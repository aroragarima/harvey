Government of Andhra Pradesh and Others v V. Appala Swamy

Supreme Court of India

25 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1567; (2007) 14 SCC 49; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 587; 2007 (6) AWC 6552; 2007 (112) FLR 1028; 2008 (3) LLN 103; 2007(3) SCALE 1; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 19

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Civil Service (CCA) Rules, 1963

Summary: Service - Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Andhra Pradesh Civil Service (CCA) Rules, 1963, r. 9 - Misconduct - Departmental proceedings - Justifiability - Respondent was working as principal in various colleges - Departmental proceedings were initiated against respondent for misconduct and also recovery proceeding were initiated - Respondent retired before action could be taken on report submitted by Inquiry Officer - Application was filed by respondent before Tribunal which was disposed of - Aggrieved by same respondent filed writ petition which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in passing orders - Held, HC in its impugned judgment did not address itself said question it was noticed from very beginning proceeded on premise that pension was payable to respondent on his retirement - Further, where by reason of delay, employer condoned lapse on part of employee and where delay caused prejudice to employee then proceedings could be directed to be quashed - Thus, departmental proceedings were initiated after 6 Years and continued for a period of 7 years so appellant was prejudiced - Appeal allowed.
