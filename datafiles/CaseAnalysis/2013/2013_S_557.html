C.N. Paramsivan & Anr. v Sunrise Plaza Tr. Partner & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

09 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 39; (2013) 9 SCC 460; AIR 2013 SC 2941; 2013 (3) ALD(SC) 165; 2013(4) ALL MR 455; 2013 (97) ALR 218; 2013 (2) AWC 1825; 2013 (1) BC 757; [2013] 177 Comp Cas 484; 2013 (3) CompLJ 15; 2013 (2) CTC 356; 2013 (1) DRTC 620; JT 2013 (2) SC 324; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 488; 2013 (119) RD 97; 2013(1) SCALE 619; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Recovery Officer, Osmania University Act, 1959

Summary: Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 86,87 - Income Tax Rules, 1962, rr. 57,58 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21, r. 84,85,86 - Equitable Mortgage - Default - Auction - Delivery of Possession - Illegal auction - Sustainability - Loan was advanced on basis of an equitable mortgage of properties owned by partners of firm by deposit of title deeds - Borrower-respondent had defaulted in repayment of loan amount, respondent-bank filed application before Debt Recovery Tribunal, respondents failed to appear and contest claim made against them culminated in passing of an ex-parte decree in favour of bank - Application for setting aside of decree was made by borrower-respondent which was dismissed by Tribunal for default and application for recall of order too was dismissed by Tribunal - Proceedings for execution of recovery certificate was issued in favor of bank - Respondents filed application before Tribunal for setting aside auction sale which was conditionally allowed subject of depositing amount, but application to set aside was dismissed as not maintainable - Borrower filed petition for setting aside exparte orders before HC, which was allowed and was affirmed by SC in SLP - Bank and appellants-auction purchaser filed review petition before HC, against setting aside exparte orders which were dismissed - Appellants-auction purchasers filed application before Tribunal seeking delivery of possession, which was allowed - Respondents challenged order of Tribunal before Appellate Tribunal which was allowed and order passed by Tribunal were set aside - Appellants challenged before DB, which set aside order and remitted matter back to Appellate Tribunal to decide issue whether or not right of a bona fide purchaser get curtailed if ex-parte decree on basis whereof auction sale was conducted was eventually set aside - Tribunal set aside order holding that appellants-auction purchasers were not bona fide purchasers as they were aware of pending legal proceedings - Aggrieved appellant field writ petition before HC, which was dismissed by examining validity of auction itself and came to conclusion that auction conducted by Recovery Officer was illegal and void because of non-compliance with provisions of Act - Hence instant appeal - Whether non-compliance of provisions of Act would render auction non-est - Held, it was clear from a plain reading that provision was mandatory in character, use of word 'shall' was both textually and contextually indicative of making of deposit of amount being a mandatory requirement - Provisions of rr. 57, 58 of Rules, had their equivalent in O. XXI rr. 84, 85, 86 of CPC, which were parimateria in language, sweep and effect and had been held to be mandatory - SC, held that rr. 57, 58 of Rules, were anything but mandatory in nature, so that a breach of requirements under those Rules will render auction non-est in eyes of law - Appeal dismissed.
