Haresh Mohandas Rajput v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

20 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 595; (2011) 12 SCC 56; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 3593; JT 2011 (10) SC 493; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 117; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 257; 2011(10) SCALE 629; [2011] 14 S.C.R. 921

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 376 - Rape and Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant accused allegedly raped and murdered the deceased minor - Trial Court convicted u/ss. 302, 376 IPC and sentenced him to life imprisonment - Respondent State filed appeal before the HC for enhancement of punishment - HC confirmed the conviction recorded by the Trial Court, however, altered the sentence of life imprisonment to death sentence - Whether the prosecution case met the requirement of proof on circumstantial evidence and the facts of the case warranted the imposition of death sentence - Held, instant case was based on circumstantial evidence as there was no eye-witness of the incident and the HC had awarded the death sentence to the appellant - Appellant, in his examination u/s. 313 of CrPC, admitted that he was sitting outside his house when the police arrived - Police had searched appellant's house and the dead body of the deceased lying below the cot in his house was recovered - Appellant was present at the place of occurrence when the children including the deceased were playing in front of his house - It had been proved that the deceased disappeared while playing in front of the house of the appellant - Further, none of the prosecution witnesses had any animosity with the appellant and thus, there could be no reason to enrope him falsely - Information was given to the police in close vicinity at the time of commission of the crime, though exact time of death was not known - Appellant had admitted the recovery of deceased's body from his house while answering the question in his examination u/s. 313 CrPC and that circumstance to the extent that the dead body was recovered from the house of the appellant stood fully proved - Evidence led by the prosecution clearly established the circumstances leading to the crime - Out of the circumstances put forth by the prosecution, only a very few which were immaterial and were not vital to determine the case, stood fully proved against the appellant - In such a fact-situation, there was no cogent reason to interfere with the well-reasoned judgments of the courts below so far as the conviction of the appellant is concerned, and therefore, appellant's conviction u/ss. 302 and 376 IPC were confirmed - However, the instant case did not fall within the 'rarest of rare cases,' thus the HC was not justified in enhancing the punishment - Therefore, in the facts and circumstances of the case, punishment of death sentence awarded by the HC was set aside and the sentence of life imprisonment awarded by the Trial Court was restored - Appeal disposed of.
