Sajjan Sharma v State Of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 15; (2011) 2 SCC 206; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 618; 2011 CRLJ 1169; 2011 (1) JCC 278; 2011 (1) JhrCR(SC) 224; JT 2011 (1) SC 173; 2011 (2) MLJ(Crl) 493; 2011(1) SCALE 137; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 629

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Chargesheet was submitted against accused persons of whom five were named in the Fard-e-beyan/FIR and two namely appellant and 'M' were not named in the Fard-e-beyan/FIR - Prosecution case that led to the conviction and sentence of the appellant was based on the Fard-e-beyan of PW4 recorded by the officer-in-charge of Police Station - However, appellant was not named there as one of the accused - Trial Court held that the prosecution was able to fully establish the guilt of the accused and convicted all the accused u/s. 302 of IPC - Appeals filed thereagainst was dismissed by HC - Present appeal - Whether conviction of appellant can be legally sustained u/s. 302 of the IPC? - Held, Fard-e-beyan was recorded soon after the occurrence when there was hardly any time for deliberation and for false implication of anyone who was actually not among the accused - However, appellant was not as one of the accused in the Fard-e-beyan of PW4 despite of fact that appellant lived in the same village and appellant's father and brother were duly named in the Fard-e-beyan/FIR - Further, name of the appellant was stated in the deposition of PW6, who did not assign them any particular weapon and did not assign any role to the appellant - In these circumstances, evidence of PW6 insofar as he names the appellant also as one of the members of the unlawful assembly cannot be accepted - Hence, it would be wholly safe to maintain the conviction of the appellant u/s. 302 of the IPC and applying the rule of caution, he must be given the benefit of doubt - Appeal allowed.
