Sri Kumaresh v The Divl. Manager National Insurance Co. Ltd. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 365; (2011) 12 SCC 488; 2011 (4) ACC 815; 2011 ACJ 1975; 2011 (87) ALR 294; 2011 (4) AWC 3601; JT 2011 (6) SC 35; 2011 (164) PLR 110; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 913; 2012 (1) RLW 505; 2011(5) SCALE 461

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 166 - Workmen Compensation Act, 1923 - Accidental claim - Enhancement of compensation - Appellant, aged 20 years working as a building centering worker, filed a claim petition u/s. 166 of the Act claiming Rs.15 lacs as compensation - Motor Accident Claims Tribunal (MACT) held that in motor accidents cases, strict proof of rash and negligence need not be established as was required in criminal cases, and accordingly concluded that it was clearly established that the appellant sustained injuries as a result of the accident - MACT assessed disability of the whole body at 20%, and took monthly salary to be Rs.3,500 - Total compensation payable to the appellant amounted to Rs.2,81,200 - On appeal, HC modified the compensation awarded by the MACT and total compensation was enhanced to Rs.5,48,000 with 6% interest p.a. - Instant appeal - Held, appellant was aged just 20 years and one of his legs was amputated below the knee - It would not only severely affect his future prospects of earning, but he would also have to be permanently disabled for life and suffer the necessary discomforts accompanying living without a leg - Hence, compensation amount awarded enhanced to Rs. 10,00,000 at 9% p.a to be paid to the appellant jointly and severally by the respondents - Appeal partly allowed.
