Sunil Batra (Ii) v Delhi Administration

Supreme Court of India

20 December 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Pathak, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 329; (1980) 3 SCC 488; (1980) SCC (Cr) 777; AIR 1980 SC 1579; 1980 CRLJ 1099; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 557

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Punjab Prison Manual - Brutal assault - Prisoner - Petition by a prisoner complaining of a brutal assault by a Head Warden on another prisoner - Case is revelatory of several sins in this Central Penitentiary - When offences are alleged to have taken place within prison, there should be no tinge or trace of departmental collusion or league between police and prison staff - Held, prisoner, has been tortured illegally and Superintendent cannot absolve himself from responsibility even though he may not be directly a party - Lack of vigilance is limited guilt - State shall take action against investigating police for apparently collusive dilatoriness and deviousness - No solitary or punitive cell, no hard labour or dietary change as painful additive, no other punishment or denial of privileges and amenities, no transfer to other prisons with penal consequences, shall be imposed without judicial appraisal of Sessions Judge and where such intimation on account of emergency, is difficult, such information shall be given within two days of action - Clear directives to State - Directions issued to District Magistrates and Sessions Judges, Superintendent - Petition allowed.
