Collector, Central Excise, Bombay v Messrs S.D. Fine Chemicals Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 406; (1995) Supp2 SCC 336; 1995 (77) E.L.T. 49; JT 1995 (3) SC 353; 1995(2) SCALE 550; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 84; 1995 (99) STC 313

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944 - Respondent engaged in the manufacturing of laboratory chemicals and fine chemicals - Claim that the process of purification and distillation undertaken by them does not amount to process of manufacture and accordingly, claimed exemption from duty in respect of such goods u/notification No. 77 of 1983 D/- 01-03-1981 - Whether distillation and recrystallization carried out by the respondent amounts to 'manufacture'? - Held, it is clear from the perusal of the opinion of the third member of the tribunal that he has not dealt with the case in a full and proper manner and has disposed of the issue in a cryptic manner - It has, therefore, become necessary to remit the matter for the fresh opinion of the third member of the tribunal.
