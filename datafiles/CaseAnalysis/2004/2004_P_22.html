P.M. Bhargava & Ors. v University Grants Commission & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 396; (2004) 6 SCC 661; AIR 2004 SC 3478; 2004 (4) ALD(SC) 99; 2004 (5) AWC 3869; 2004 (3) ESC 337; JT 2004 (Supp1) SC 548; 2004(5) SCALE 551; 2004 (3) SLR 722; [2004] Supp2 S.C.R. 186; 2004 (2) UPLBEC 1663

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Education - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(C), 226, 30(1), 51A - University Grants Commission Act, 1956, s. 5 - Challenge introduction of 'Jyotir Vigyan', (science of astrology) as a course of study by University Grants Commission - No breach of any statutory provision, rule or regulation alleged - Challenge made to inclusion of Jyotir Vigyan as a course of study on ground that same will violate or impinge upon concept of secularism enshrined in Constitution has therefore no merit - Courts are not expert in academic matters and it is not for them to decide as what course should be taught in university and what should be their curriculum - It will not be proper to interfere with decision of HC specially when no violation of any statutory provisions is demonstrated.
