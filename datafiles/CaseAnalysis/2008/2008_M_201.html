Dadu Dayalu Mahasabha, Jaipur (Trust) v Mahant Ram Niwas and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 844; (2008) 11 SCC 753; AIR 2008 SC 2187; 2009(1) ALL MR 965; 2009 (74) ALR 517; 2008 (3) AWC 2656; JT 2008 (6) SC 440; 2008 (151) PLR 261; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 936; 2009 (106) RD 493; 2008 (3) RLW 2224; 2008(7) SCALE 460; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 237

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Matter Directly And Substantially In Issue, Rajasthan Public Trust Act 1959

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure - Applicability of the principles of Res Judicata and O. 2 r. 2 having regard to an observation made by this Court - Held, judgment of a court, it is trite, should not be interpreted as a statute - Meaning of the words used in a judgment must be found out on the backdrop of the fact of each case - Court while passing a judgment cannot take away the right of the successful party indirectly which it cannot do directly - An observation made by a superior court is not binding - What would be binding is the ratio of the decision - Issue indisputably was the claim of entitlement to Gaddi by the first respondent and a plea contra thereto raised by the appellants - Once the issue of entitlement stood determined, the same would operate as res judicata - Appeal allowed.
