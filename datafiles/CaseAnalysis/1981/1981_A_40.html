Idandas v Anant Ramchandra Phadke (Dead) By LRs

Supreme Court of India

20 November 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 600; (1982) 1 SCC 27; AIR 1982 SC 127; 1982 (2) Bom.C.R. 270; 1982 MahLJ 328; [1982] 1 S.C.R. 1197

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 106 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - A lease was given to defendant in respect of an open piece of land - Defendant was not using land for any other purpose except running a flour mill - Whether lease in question granted by landlord to appellant-tenant was a lease for manufacturing purposes, which could be terminated only by giving six months' notice u/s. 106 of Transfer of Property Act, 1882? - Held, on point of fact, in instant case lease was from year to year and, therefore, a month's notice was not legal if lease was for a manufacturing purpose - Tests laid down by this Court for determining a manufacturing purpose are that commodity should be so transformed so as to lose its original character - In instant case, wheat, was transformed, by manufacturing process which involved both labour and machinery, into flour - Commodity before manufacture was wheat - End product was flour which was fit for human consumption and used by all persons and its complexion has been completely changed - Name of commodity after product came into existence is Atta and not Gehun (wheat) - Present lease was one for manufacturing purposes - Appeal dismissed.
