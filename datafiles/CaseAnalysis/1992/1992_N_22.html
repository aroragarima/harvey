Madan Gopal Kakkad v Naval Dubey and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 446; (1992) 3 SCC 204; (1992) SCC (Cr) 598; AIR 1992 SCW 1480; 1992 (2) CCR 88; 1992 (2) Crimes 168; 1992 CRLR 514; 1992 CrLR(SC) 514; JT 1992 (3) SC 270; 1992(1) SCALE 957; [1992] 2 S.C.R. 921

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Tort

Keywords: Respectable, Parents, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 417 and 423 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 378, 417, 418 and 423 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 354, 375, 376 and 511 - Constitution of India, 1950 - (A) What is scope of power of High Court to review evidence while examining an order of acquittal sitting in its appellate jurisdiction? - S. 378 of new code (corresponding to s. 417 of old Code) gives HC full power to review at large evidence upon which order of acquittal was founded and to reach its own conclusions upon that evidence - HC is clothed with plenary powers to go through entire evidence and to come to its own conclusions as warranted by facts of case concerned - Ss. 417, 418 and 423 of Code give to HC full power to review at large evidence upon which order of acquittal was founded - Circumspect and cautious approach of HC may be in dealing with those appeals by exercising its plenary and unlimited statutory powers - (B) Whether proved facts establish offence of rape or only attempt to commit rape? - Held, offence is nothing short of rape punishable u/s. 376 IPC, 1860 - Merely because inexperienced medical officer has opined that it was an attempt to commit rape, probably on ground that there was no sign of complete penetration - A medical witness called in as an expert to assist Court is not a witness of fact and evidence given by medical officer is really of an advisory character - It is possible to commit legally offence of rape without - (C) Whether complete penetration is necessary to constitute an offence of rape? - There must be penetration in technical sense, slightest penetration would be sufficient and a complete act of sexual intercourse is not at all necessary - Held, Appeal allowed.
