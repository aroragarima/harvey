R. Sarala v T. S. Velu and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 268; (2000) 4 SCC 459; (2000) SCC (Cr) 823; AIR 2000 SC 1731; 2000 CRLJ 2453; 2001 (2) JCC 767; JT 2000 (4) SC 449; 2000 (2) RCR(Criminal) 637; 2000(3) SCALE 285; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 1; 2000 (4) SLT 5; 2000 (3) Supreme 398

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Prevention of Corruption Act, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 173 and 24 - Police report - Filing of police report to court - Public Prosecutor has no role to play - Investigation and prosecution are two different facets in administration of criminal justice and are not responsible to each other - Therefore, investigation officer cannot be compelled to file a charge - sheet in compliance with prosecutor - Police officer in charge of police station is required to forward a police report to Magistrate subject only to supervision of superior officer and here also he is not legally bound to take opinion of prosecuting authority - Held, investigating officer cannot be directed to be influenced by opinion of Public Prosecutor.
