Dr. Mukhtiar Chand and Others v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 October 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1128; (1998) 7 SCC 579; AIR 1999 SC 468; 1998 (4) CLT 203; JT 1998 (7) SC 78; 1998(5) SCALE 501; 1999 (1) SCJ 185; 1998 (8) SLT 204; [1998] Supp2 S.C.R. 143; 1998 (7) Supreme 575

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Health & Drug

Keywords: Medical Council Act, 1933, Siddha, Schedules, Unani System, Punjab Ayurvedic and Unani Practitioners Act, 1963, Prescription, Maharashtra Medical Practitioners Act, 1961, Punjab Medical Registration Act, 1916, Bombay Medical Act, 1912, Rajasthan Indian Medicine Act, 1953, Bihar And Orissa Medical Act, 1916, Bombay Medical Practitioners Act, 1938, Pepsu Ayurvedic And Unani Practitioners Act, 2008 Bk (12 Of 2008 Bk), Rajasthan Medical Act, 1952, Drugs[and Cosmetics] Act, 1940, Pharmacy Act,1948

Summary: Health & Drug - Constitution - Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940, ss. 2(d), 3(a), (b) and 33 - Pharmacy Act, 1948, s. 2(f) - Indian Medical Degrees Act, 1916 - Indian Medicine Central Council Act, 1970 - Maharashtra Medical Council Act, 1965 - Bombay Medical Act, 1912, s. 7(3) - Maharashtra Medical Practitioners Act, 1961 - Medical Council Act, 1933 - Medical Degrees Act, 1916 - Medicine Central Council Act, 1970, s. 28 - Pepsu Ayurvedic and Unani Practitioners Act, 2008 Bk (12 Of 2008 Bk) - Punjab Ayurvedic and Unani Practitioners Act, 1963 - Punjab Medical Registration Act, 1916 - Rajasthan Indian Medicine Act, 1953 - Rajasthan Medical Act, 1952 - Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, ss. 2(k),(j), 15, 21, 25 and 27 - Drugs and Cosmetics Rules, 1945, r. 2(ee)(iii) - (A)(1) Whether r. 2(ee)(iii) of the Drugs Rule is bad for want of legislative competence? - It was necessary rule-making authority to define the expression "registered medical practitioner" for the purposes of the Act and the Rules - r. 2(ee) does no more than define that expression, which is within the scope of s. 33(1) as well as s. 33(2)(e) - Held, the rule-making authority was lacking legislative competence to make r. 2(ee) - (A)(2) Are the impugned notifications issued by the State Governments under cl. (iii) of the said Rule, declaring the categories of persons who were practising modern system of medicine invalid in law? - Notifications issued by the State Govts. are well within the confines of cl. (iii) of r. 2(ee) - Held, the said circular and the notification issued by the said State Govts. declaring the categories of vaids/hakims who were practising modern system of medicine and were registered in the State Medical Registers, are valid in law - (C) Whether person enrolled on the State Register of Indian Medicine or the Central Register of Indian Medicine can practise modern scientific medicine in any of its branches? - Held, a harmonious reading of s. 15 of the 1956 Act and s. 17 of the 1970 Act leads to the conclusion that there is no scope for a person enrolled on the State Register of Indian Medicine or the Central Register of Indian Medicine to practise modern scientific medicine in any of its branches unless that person is also enrolled on a State Medical Register within the meaning of the 1956 Act - Writ disposed of .
