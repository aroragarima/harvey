Delhi Development Authority v Ravindra Mohan Aggarwal and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1016; (1999) 3 SCC 172; 1999 (2) ACJ 1109; AIR 1999 SC 1256; 1999 (2) AWC 1537; JT 1999 (2) SC 368; 1999(2) SCALE 139; 1999 (3) SLT 32; 1999 (3) Supreme 18

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land and Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 43 - Constitution, art.226 - Estoppel - Auction of property by Delhi Development Authority - Bid accepted with deposit of 25%of bid amount - Respondent filing writ petition seeking a direction to the DDA to conclude the auction-sale proceedings and hand over possession - Setting aside of auction by High Court due to its finding that plot being situated in the green belt, could not have been put top auction at all - HC directing DDA to allot an alternate plot - Held, DDA cannot be compelled to finalise sale and deliver possession to bidder - Doctrine not applicable merely because of the fact that dC isability attached with plot has ceased to exist on date of petition - HC order set aside - Appeal allowed.
