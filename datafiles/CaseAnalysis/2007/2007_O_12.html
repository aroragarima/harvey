Oriental Insurance Co. Ltd v Syed Ibrahim And Ors

Supreme Court of India

17 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 909; (2007) 11 SCC 512; 2008 (1) ACC 406; AIR 2008 SC 103; 2008 (1) ALD(SC) 12; 2007 (69) ALR 315; 2007 (4) AWC 3556; 2007 (4) JhrCR(SC) 205; JT 2007 (11) SC 113; 2008 (1) KarLJ 63; 2007 (4) KLT 319; 2007 (6) MLJ(SC) 1477; 2007 (4) RCR(Civil) 351; 2007(11) SCALE 184; 2007 (7) SCJ 29; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 1052

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Pecuniary Benefit, Renewal Of Licence, Measure Of Damages

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988-Sections 149, 166 and 168-Compensation for death of child aged 7 years-Liability of insurer-Driver licensed to drive L.M.V. and not lorry (H.G.V.)-Insured was father of driver-In view of National Insurance Co. Ltd. v. Swaran Singh, (2004) 3 SCC 297, insurer not liable to indemnify award-However, compensation amount deposited pursuant to order of Supreme Court-Hence, quantum maintained -Claimants permitted to withdraw amount so deposited alongwith accrued interest.
