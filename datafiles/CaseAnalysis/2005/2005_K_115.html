Chief Engineer (Construction) v Keshava Rao (D) by Lrs.

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 244; (2005) 11 SCC 229; (2005) SCC (L&S) 872; 2005 (2) CLR 112; 2005 (2) ESC 243; 2005 (105) FLR 378; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 96; JT 2005 (3) SC 451; 2005 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 118; 2005 (2) LLJ 479; 2005(3) SCALE 269; 2005 (3) SLR 434; 2005 (2) Supreme 604; 2005 (2) UPLBEC 1675

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - - Termination - Perverse order - Whether HC right in setting aside order of Tribunal which confirmed termination? - HC noticed the finding of fact recorded by the Tribunal that the respondent had voluntarily abandoned his service but found the finding to be perverse - Held, Labour Court recorded two crucial findings of fact on basis of evidence on record - Finding of Tribunal did not suffer from vice of perversity or unreasonableness - HC was in error in interfering with findings of fact recorded by Tribunal - Appeal allowed.
