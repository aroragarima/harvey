P.D. Agrawal v State Bank of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 623; (2006) 8 SCC 776; AIR 2006 SC 2064; 2006 (5) AWC 5005; JT 2006 (5) SC 235; 2006(5) SCALE 54; 2006 (4) SCJ 736; 2006 (4) SLR 47; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 454

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Land & Property; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Master And Servant Relationship

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 353, 448 and 506 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 14(1)(b) - State Bank of India (Supervising Staff) Service Rules, rr. 32(1), 32(4), (5) and 49 - Whether the disciplinary authority terminating appellant from his service without issuing show cause notice is violation of principle of natural justice? - Relied on State of M.P. v. Bani Singh and another 1990 Indlaw SC 57, State of Punjab and others v. Chaman Lai Goyal 1995 Indlaw SC 162, and Canara Bank and others v. Debasis Das and others 2003 Indlaw SC 269 - Held, neither breach of principle of natural justice nor denial of reasonable tarnished and neither findings of single nor division judge bench had been proved - Further held, not a fit case for this court to exercise its discretion jurisdiction u/art. 136 - Appeal dismissed without any costs
