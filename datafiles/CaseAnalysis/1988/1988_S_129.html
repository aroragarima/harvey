Awadhesh And Anr. v State Of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 44; (1988) SCC (Cr) 361; (1988) 2 SCC 557; AIR 1988 SC 1158; 1988 CRLJ 1154; 1988 CrLR(SC) 269; JT 1988 (2) SC 72; 1988(1) SCALE 698; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 513

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Ballistics

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 34 - Appeal against acquittal - Witnesses to recovery did not support prosecution - No effort was made by prosecution for securing evidence of ballistic expert - No independent witness examined by prosecution - Medical and ocular evidence inconsistent - The testimony of eye witnesses not consistent - Medical expert's opinion corroborated by other circumstances - Acquitted by Trial Court - Convicted by HC - Empty cartridges not recovered, instead Investigation Officer planted cartridges to support prosecution case - Prosecution case is doubtful - Acquittal not to be disturbed unless findings of Trial Court are perverse - No finding of HC to effect that view of Trial Court was not possible - Conviction of accused by taking different view and explaining infirmities in prosecution witnesses not proper.
