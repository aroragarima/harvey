Nihal Singh and another v State of Punjab and others

Supreme Court of India

07 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 495; (2013) 14 SCC 65; AIR 2013 SC 3547; 2014 (1) AWC 130; 2013 (139) FLR 309; JT 2013 (11) SC 289; 2013(10) SCALE 162; [2013] 11 S.C.R. 1; 2013 (5) SLR 436

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Ex-servicemen

Summary: (1) Employment-Absorption-Regularisation-Police service-Appellants ex-servicemen recruited as Special Police Officers-Appellants appointed by State in exercise of statutory power-Appellants amenable to disciplinary control of State as in case of any other regular police officers-Only distinction is that they are to be paid daily wages-No justification for State to take defence that after permitting utilisation of services of large number of people like appellants for decades to say that there are no sanctioned posts to absorb appellants-Sanctioned posts do not fall from heaven-State to create sanctioned posts by conscious choice on basis of some rational assessment of need-In absence of sanctioned posts, State cannot be compelled to absorb persons like appellants into services of State-Not tenable ground-Held-Appellants entitled to be absorbed in services of State-Direction to State to regularise services of appellants by creating necessary posts-Upon such regularisation, appellants entitled to all benefits of services attached to post which are similar in nature already in cadre of police services of State-Impugned judgments-Set aside.

(2) Employment-Regularisation -Umadevi case, (2006) 4 SCC 1 : 2006 (2) SCCD 109 : 2006 (5) AWC 5325 (SC)-Constitution Bench decision of Supreme Court in Umadevi's case-Cannot become licence for exploitation by State and its instrumentalities.
