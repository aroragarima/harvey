Vijay Syal and Another v State of Punjab and others

Supreme Court of India

22 May 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 484; (2003) 9 SCC 401; (2003) SCC (L&S) 1112; AIR 2003 SC 4023; 2003 (5) AWC 3620; 2003 (3) ESC 360; 2003 (98) FLR 246; JT 2003 (5) SC 241; 2003 (3) KHCACJ 59; 2003 (3) LLN 821; 2003(5) SCALE 27; 2003 (1) SCW 2954; 2003 (3) SLJ 13; 2003 (4) SLR 452; 2003 (4) SLT 296; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 242; 2003 (4) Supreme 299; 2003 (4) UPLBEC 3186

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Employment-Selection for 12 posts of Assistant District Transport Officer-First list of 78 candidates declared successful for interview-Then 40 more candidates declared successful in second list-Six persons selected in general category, namely, 4 from first list and 2 from second list-Writ petition of appellants in general category challenging selection list and for considering their claims on merit from amongst candidates of first list-Petition dismissed-Appellants making misrepresentation in S.L.Ps. on allocation of marks for written test and interview-Disentitling themselves for any relief-Even on merit, they have no case-Appointees continuing in service for 5 years-Appellants knowing criteria fixed for selection and allocation of marks participated in interview-On being unsuccessful, not open to them to turn around and attack criteria-No interference called for-However, as appellants tendered apology and expressed regrets for misrepresentation in S.L.Ps. supported by affidavit-No further action to be taken except administering warning - Appeals dismissed with costs.
