Satish Mehra v State Of N.C.T. Of Delhi & Anr

Supreme Court of India

22 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 475; (2012) 13 SCC 614; AIR 2013 SC 506; 2013 CRLJ 411; JT 2012 (11) SC 336; 2012 (4) MLJ(Crl) 811; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 883; 2012(11) SCALE 193; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal -Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B,420,464,467,468,471 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Joint Account - Fraud - Transfer of FDR - Quashing of Charges - Maintainability - Appellant-complainant was married to accused and had series of litigations - Accused-wife and accused-father-in-law of appellant forged signatures of appellant on joint FDR's and got same renewed in sole name of accused-wife - FIR was registered on complaint of appellant, cancellation report was filed against which objection were filed by appellant and trial court ordered for investigation and summoned Branch Managers - Accused moved application against order of summons and for quashing proceeding as a whole but same was disposed of - Trial Court framed charges against accused-appellants-Bank managers - Aggrieved accused filed petition for quashing of charges framed against them - HC, declined any relief to appellants set aside charges framed against accused-father-in-law and also against accused-wife - Hence instant appeal - (A) Whether HC, was justified in dismissing quash petition filed by accused-Bank managers - Held, all that appears against aforesaid 2 accused was that one was Chief Manager of Bank whereas other accused was at relevant time working as Senior Manager, what role, if any, either of accused had in renewing two fixed deposits in sole name of accused-wife was not disclosed either in FIR filed or materials collected during course of investigation or in charge sheet filed before court - There could be no manner of doubt that some particular individual connected with Bank must have authorized aforesaid acts, however, identity of person does not appear from materials on record - A criminal trial could not be allowed to assume character of fishing and roving enquiry, it would not be permissible in law to permit a prosecution to linger, limp and continue on basis of a mere hope and expectation that in trial some material may be found to implicate accused, such a course of action was not contemplated in system of criminal jurisprudence that had been evolved by courts over the years, therefore, criminal proceeding in present form and on allegations leveled was clearly not maintainable against either of accused-Bank managers - (B) Whether HC, was justified in quashing charges against accused-father-in-law and other accused - Held, from materials on record it appeared that in so far as FD No. 22/91 was concerned an endorsement on reverse of FD was made by accused-father-in-law that said F.D. may be renewed in name of accused-wife, however, renewal of FD was made by Bank on basis of a letter dt. 9-10-1992 written by accused-wife to Bank - If above fact had been revealed in course of investigation of FIR no liability in respect of FD bearing No.22/91 could be fastened on accused-father-in-law, neither was there any allegation against accused-father-in-law with regard to receipt of money against aforesaid FD by accused-wife, there was no allegation that renewal of FD was made on basis of any endorsement or request made by accused-father-in-law, hence it could not be held that HC had committed any error in quashing charges u/ss. 120B, 420 of IPC against accused-father-in-law - Appeals disposed of.
