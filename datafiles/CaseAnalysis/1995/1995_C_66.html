M/S. Chandrapur Magnetwires (P) Limited Nagpur v Collector O

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1906; (1996) 2 SCC 159; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 329; 1996 (53) ECC 139; 1996 (62) ECR 361; 1996 (81) E.L.T. 3; JT 1995 (9) SC 568; 1996 (12) RLT 1; 1995(7) SCALE 220; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 593

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 57F(2) - Notification No.69/86-CE D/- 10-02-1986 - Notification No. 106/88 D/- 1-03-1988 - Whether appellants entitled to the benefit of MODVAT scheme and was entitled to get credit for the duty of excise paid on the inputs which were utilised for manufacture of final product? - As per department circular where manufacturer produces dutiable final products and also final products which are exempt from duty and it is not reasonably possible to segregate inputs utilised in manufacture of the dutiable final products from the final products which are exempt from duty - In such a case, the manufacturer may take credit of duty paid on all the inputs used in the manufacture of final products on which duty will have to be paid - This can be done only if the credit of duty paid on the inputs used in the exempted products is debited in the credit account before the removal of the exempted final products - Held, in view of the clarification by the department, there is no reason why the assessee cannot make a debit entry in the credit account before removal of the exempted final product - Claim for exemption of duty on disputed goods cannot be denied on plea that the assessee has taken credit of the duty paid on the inputs used in manufacture of these goods - Appeal allowed.
