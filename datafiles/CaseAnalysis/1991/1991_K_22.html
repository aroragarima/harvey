Commissioner of Income Tax v Kalyan Das Rastogi

Supreme Court of India

01 February 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 443; (1993) Supp1 SCC 663; 1992 (104) CTR 368; [1992] 193 ITR 713; [1992] 63 TAXMAN 382

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act,1961 - Mens rea - Assessment - Penalty - Jurisdiction - Income Tax Appellate Tribunal by its order that assessee could not have been under bona fide belief that its income was below taxable limit and under no obligation to file return of its income voluntarily - Tribunal ordered that penalty proceedings being criminal or quasi-criminal establishment of mens rea was essential ingredient and such statutory obligation on Revenue to prove that assessee acted deliberately in defiance of law or was guilty of conduct, contumacious or dishonest or acted in conscious disregard of its obligation - Whether Income Tax Appellate Tribunal was justified in cancelling penalty orders and deleting penalty of levied u/s. 271 (1) (a) of Act , for assessment years - Held, nothing in s.271 (1) (a) of Act, requires that mens rea must be proved before penalty be levied and penalty imposed for tax delinquency was civil obligation, remedial and coercive in its nature and far different from penalty for crime or fine or forfeiture provided as punishment for violation of criminal or penal laws - As per referred case, element of mens rea not required to be proved in proceedings taken by Income Tax Officer u/s. 271 (1) (a) of Act ,against assessee for assessment years - HC had wrong in taking view that no question of law arose out of order of Tribunal - Order of HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
