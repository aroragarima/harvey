Â Col. A. S. Iyer and Others v V. Balasubramanyam and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 October 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, Y.V. Chandrachud, N.L. Untwalia, P.N. Shingal, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 82; (1980) SCC (L&S) 145; (1980) 1 SCC 634; AIR 1980 SC 452; 1980 LabIC 231; 1980 (1) LLJ 441; 1980 (2) LLN 1; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 1036; 1980 (1) SLR 89

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Survey Of India (Class I Recruitment) Rules, 1960, Survey of India Rules 1950

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14, 16, 133 and 141 - Survey of India (Recruitment from Crops of Engineer Officers) Rules, 1950, r. 5 - Validity of r. 5 of Survey of India Rules - Costs when not allowed - Held, not violative of arts. 14 and 16 - Intricate constitutional questions when decided by Supreme Court to declare law u/art. 141 should be an exception to conventional rule of costs following event unless other circumstances warrant - Appeal allowed.
