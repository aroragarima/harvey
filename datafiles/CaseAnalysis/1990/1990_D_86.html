Neelima Misra v Harinder Kaur Paintal and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 March 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, Veeraswami Ramaswami

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 760; (1990) SCC (L&S) 395; (1990) 2 SCC 746; AIR 1990 SC 1402; 1990 (16) ALR 417; 1990 (13) ATC 732; 1990 (1) AWC 641; JT 1990 (2) SC 103; 1990 LabIC 1229; 1990(1) SCALE 596; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 84; 1990 (2) SLR 435; 1990 (2) UJ 90

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Service

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure 1908, Administrative Decision, Bombay University Act, 1974, Kerala University Act, 1974, Aligarh Muslim University Act, 1920, Uttar Pradesh State Universities Act 1973, Calcutta University Act 1979, Jawaharlal Nehru University Act, 1966

Summary: Constitution - Service - Education - Aligarh Muslim University Act, 1920, s. 27(5) - Bombay University Act, 1974, s. 57(2)(e) - Calcutta University Act, 1979, s. 32(2) - Constitution, 1950, art. 14 - Delhi University Act, 1922 - Jawaharlal Nehru University Act, 1966, s. 27(4) - Kerala University Act, 1974 - Madhya Pradesh Vishwavidyalaya Adhiniyam, 1973, s. 49(2) - Uttar Pradesh State Universities Act, 1973, ss. 31(4)(a), 31(8)(a) and 68 - Appointment as reader - Not possess a Ph.D. degree - Appointment quashed by High Court - Whether Chancellor has only to consider the recommendation of the Selection Committee in the light of disagreement if any, expressed by the Executive Council and direct appointment of a candidate in the select list or to act judicially - Held, power of the Chancellor u/s. 31(8)(a) is purely of administrative character and is not in the nature of judicial or quasi-judicial power and no judicial or quasi-judicial duty is imposed on the Chancellor and any reference to judicial duty seems to be irrelevant in the exercise of his function - Further held, his decision nonetheless is a decision on the recommendation of the Selection Committee, such a power cannot be considered as a quasi-judicial power, therefore judgment of HC is set aside - Appeal allowed.
