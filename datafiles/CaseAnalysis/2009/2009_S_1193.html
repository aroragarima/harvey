Steel Authority of India Limited v Gupta Brother Steel Tubes Limited

Supreme Court of India

09 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1126; (2009) 10 SCC 63; 2009 (4) AWC 4100; 2009 (4) CompLJ 12; JT 2009 (12) SC 135; 2009 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2547; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 101; 2009(12) SCALE 393; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 253

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Reasoned Award, Time Barred Claim, Award Compensation

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Indian Arbitration Act, 1940 - Liquidated damages - Admissibility - Arbitrator allowed liquidated damages in favor of respondent in the award - Appellant raised objections with regard to validity of award before Sub Court - Sub Court overruled appellant's objection and made award absolute - Appellant's appeal before District Court dismissed - Appellant's Revision Petition before HC also dismissed - Legality - Appellant contended that arbitrator could not have allowed damages beyond Liquidated Damages cl.7.2 - Held, claim of damages by respondent was essentially distinct from breaches contemplated under cl.7.2 - Breach due to refusal of supply and delayed supply founded on adverse grounds - View of arbitrator was flowed from reasonable construction of cl.7.2 - Arbitrator had given elaborate reasons that Liquidated Damages cl.7.2 was not attracted - Award was not amenable to correction by court - However, respondent not entitled to claim damages u/s.74 of Indian Contract Act - Contention that lack of jurisdiction of arbitrator was not raised before HC, hence the same could not be raised before SC - Therefore, award of arbitrator was valid and no interference called for - Appeal dismissed.
