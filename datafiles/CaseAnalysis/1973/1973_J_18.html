Jaydayal Poddar (Deceased) Through His L.Rs Andanother v Mst. Bibi Hazra And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

19 October 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 135; (1974) 1 SCC 3; AIR 1974 SC 171; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 90; 1973 UJ 911

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Land & Property - Sale - Illegal possession - Declaration - Defendant no.1 in order to clear mortgage dues agreed to sell his house to plaintiff no.1 - Defendant no.1 got said property registered and plaintiff no.2 got mutated in Municipal records - Defendant nos. 1 to 3 continued in illegal possession of said property and thereafter filed suit before trial court for declaration, possession and title which was decreed in favour of defendant no.1 - Defendant no.2 preferred appeal before HC which reversed finding of HC - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, that circumstances and probabilities of the case, we have no hesitation in holding that the plaintiffs/appellants had failed to prove that one 'X' in whose name the sale deed (Ex.D/1) stood, was not the real purchaser but only a benamidar of her husband. Thus, decision of the High Court is affirmed. Appeal dismissed.
