ICICI Bank Ltd v Sidco Leathers Ltd. and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 310; (2006) 10 SCC 452; AIR 2006 SC 2088; 2006 (3) AWC 2114; 2007 (2) BC 51; 2006 (2) CTC 847; JT 2006 (6) SC 274; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 276; 2006(5) SCALE 27; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 528

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Companies Act, 1956-Sections 529 and 529A - Provincial Insolvency Act, 1920-Section 47-Transfer of Property Act, 1882-Section 48-Loanee company in liquidation-Rights of loaners being first and second charge holders-Single Judge of High Court held that as first charge holders I.F.C.I. and I.D.B.I. joined winding up proceedings and submitted proof of their debts before official liquidator, they shall be taken to have given up their securities-And thus cannot claim any priority over assets of P.N.B. on fixed assets-Relying on Allahabad Bank v. Canara Bank, AIR 2000 SC 1535, single Judge, Division Bench dismissed special appeal of appellant-Whether High Court justified?-Held, ânoâ-Scope of provisions of Section 529A of Companies Act, Section 48 of T.P. Act vis-a-vis aforesaid Section 529A and Section 47 of Insolvency Act analysed-High Court overlooked provisions of relevant Acts including Provincial Insolvency Act-Impugned judgment set aside.
