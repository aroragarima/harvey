Balwantbhai B. Patel v State of Gujarat and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1953; (2009) 10 SCC 684; AIR 2010 SC 1158; 2009 (5) G.L.R. 3807; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 352; 2009(12) SCALE 549; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 306

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 114, 302 r/w. 34 - Murder - Conviction - Trial Court convicted appellant on basis of evidence of three primary witnesses - Judgment of Trial Court was confirmed in appeal by HC - Hence, present appeal - Whether conviction of appellant u/s. 302 r/w. 34 of IPC correct? - Held, no evidence to show that PW had received any injury as his injury statement is not on record - Therefore, finding of HC about appellant presence appears to be on shaky foundations - Allegations of catching hold of an attack victim or of an exhortation are invariably made when number of injuries on injured party do not co-relate to number of accused or in alternative in an attempt to rope in as many persons as possible from other side - Moreover, appellant has already undergone more than six years of sentence - HC's order not sustainable - Appellant acquitted - Appeal allowed.
