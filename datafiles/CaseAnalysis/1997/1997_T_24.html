Mohammad Ali Khan and Others v Commissioner of Wealth Tax

Supreme Court of India

04 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2519; (1997) 3 SCC 511; AIR 1997 SC 1165; 1997 (139) CTR 335; [1997] 224 ITR 672; JT 1997 (3) SC 250; 1997(2) SCALE 442; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 658; 1997 (2) Supreme 722; [1997] 92 TAXMAN 52; 1997 (2) TLR 371

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Wealth Tax Act, 1957, s. 5(1)(iii) - Merged States (Taxation Concessions) Order, 1949 - Net wealth - Whether palace declared to be official residence of ruler under 1949 Order, if so, whether assessee entitled exemption? - When palace was consisted of number of buildings which were rented - Held, buildings let out cannot be considered as in occupation of assessee within meaning of s. 5(1)(iii) of Act - And assessee would be entitled to exemption in respect of self occupied portion of building - Appeal dismissed.
