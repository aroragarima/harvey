Pawan Pratap Singh and others v Reevan Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

10 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 93; (2011) 3 SCC 267; 2011 (8) ADJ 103; 2011 (3) AWC 3011; 2011 (1) ESC 175; JT 2011 (2) SC 248; 2011(2) SCALE 327; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 831; 2011 (2) SLR 21

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Determination Of Seniority, U.P. Civil Service (Judicial Branch) Rules, 1951, Date Of Substantive Appointment, Uttar Pradesh Subordinate Services Selection (Commission) Act, 1988, Uttar Pradesh Jail Executive Subordinate (Non-Gazetted) Service Rules, 1980, Bihar Judicial Service (Recruitment) Rules, 1955, U.P. Agriculture Group 'B' Service Rules, 1995, Andhra Pradesh Police Service Rules, 1966, Orissa Service of Engineers Rules, 1941

Summary: Service - Uttar Pradesh Jail Executive Subordinate (Non- Gazetted) Service Rules, 1980 - Uttar Pradesh Government Servants Seniority Rules, 1991, rr. 2, 3 - Direct recruits - Inter se Seniority - Determination - State Public Service Commission (SPSC) issued notification for recruitment to the posts of Deputy Jailor - Subordinate Services Selection Commission (SSSC) was constituted by the State in the meantime - SSSC, subsequently, notified the vacancies for Deputy Jailor - SSSC conducted the selection test and issued appointment letters in the year 1991- SPSC also conducted the selection and issued appointment letters to the selected candidates in the year 1994 - Tentative seniority list was published wherein candidates appointed in 1991 (appellants) were shown senior to the candidates appointed in 1994 (respondents) - Tentative seniority list was challenged by the respondents before the HC - HC directed the authorities to place the respondents above the appellants in the seniority list - Hence the instant appeals - (A) Whether the seniority between 1991 appointees and 1994 appointees were determined by the 1980 Rules - Held, recruitment to the posts of Deputy Jailor in the State was governed by the 1980 Rules which had been framed by the Governor in exercise of the powers conferred by the proviso to art. 309 of the Constitution - However, by subsequent Rules, namely, 1991 Rules which too were made by the Governor under the proviso to art. 309 of the Constitution, comprehensive provisions had been made for the determination of seniority of all government servants in the State - R. 2 of the 1991 Rules stating that said rules should apply to all government servants in respect of whose recruitment and conditions of service, rules might be or had been made by the Governor under the proviso to art. 309 of the Constitution and r. 3 gave to the 1991 Rules overriding effect notwithstanding anything to the contrary contained in earlier service rules - Hence, inter se seniority amongst 1991 and 1994 appointees by direct recruitment was required to be determined under the 1991 Rules and not that of the 1980 Rules - (B) Whether the appellants (1991 appointees) were entitled to be placed senior over respondents (1994 appointees) - Held, HC fell into grave error in not appreciating that r. 5 of the 1991 Rules operates where service rules provide for appointments by direct recruitment only - For determination of inter se seniority between the two rival groups (1991 and 1994 appointees by direct recruitment) what is relevant was the date of the order of their substantive appointment and since the substantive appointment of 1991 appointees was much prior in point of time, they must rank senior to the 1994 appointees - R. 8 of the 1991 Rules contemplated that 1991 appointees who were selected and appointed in accordance with the service rules could not be made junior to 1994 appointees even if it was assumed that the selection and appointment of 1994 appointees was for earlier vacancies - 1991 appointees having been appointed substantively much prior in point of time, they were entitled to rank senior to 1994 appointees - R. 5 of the 1991 Rules had no application for determination of inter se seniority of the Deputy Jailors appointed by direct recruitment in 1991 and 1994 - Consideration of the matter by the HC was apparently flawed and could not be sustained - 1994 appointees, therefore, could not legitimately claim their seniority over 1991 appointees - Impugned HC judgment was set aside - Appeals allowed.
