Delhi Electric Supply Undertaking v Basanti Devi and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1386; (1999) 8 SCC 229; AIR 2000 SC 43; 1999 (37) ALR 554; 1999 (4) AWC 3458; 1999 (2) CCC 133; [1999] 98 Comp Cas 695; 1999 (4) CompLJ 415; 1999 (3) CPJ 15; JT 1999 (7) SC 486; 1999 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 104; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 219; 1999 (2) UJ 1536

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Insurance

Summary: Insurance - Consumer Protection - - Life Insurance Corporation Act, 1956 - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 s. 18 - Indian Contract Act, 1872 s. 182 - Employer collecting premium - Implied agent of LIC - Sustainability - Life Insurance Corporation(LIC)/insurer floated 'Salary Savings Scheme' under which deceased/insured, employee of Delhi Electric Supply undertaking (DESU) took insurance policy - Insurance policy commenced on 28-1-1992 and premium for subsequent months was deducted by DESU from salary of deceased but was not remitted to LIC - Deceased died on 17-8-1992, respondent/widow of deceased requested for payment of amount due under policy - LIC said that since default was committed in payment of premium, policy taken out by deceased lapsed - Respondent filed complaint before State Commission against LIC and DESU - State Commission by its judgment dt. 10-11-1993, directed DESU to pay sum of Rs. 50, 000/- with interest at rate of 15% per annum and LIC was absolved of any liability - On appeal National Consumer Commission, affirmed order of State Commission by impugned judgment dt. 13-1-1995 - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned judgment dt. 13-1-1995 was justified - Held, DESU was certainly not insurance agent within meaning of Insurance Act and the regulations but DESU was certainly agent as defined in s. 182 of the 1872 Act - Mode of collection of premium was indicated in scheme itself and employer was assigned role of collecting premium and remitting same to LIC and as far as employee as such was concerned, employer would be agent of LIC - As far as deceased was concerned, there was no obligation cast on him to pay premium direct to LIC, under agreement between LIC and DESU, premium was payable to DESU who was to deduct it every month from salary of deceased and to transmit same to LIC - DESU had, therefore, implied authority to collect premium from deceased on behalf of LIC and there was, thus, valid payment of premium by deceased - Further, formation of contract of insurance was between LIC and employee of DESU, scheme was introduced by LIC purely on business considerations and not for any particular benefit of insurance conferred on employee working in Organisation - Therefore, SC directed that LIC should pay to respondent insurance amount of Rs. 50, 000/- with interest at rate of 15% per annum from 17-12-1992, till payment, thus substituted LIC of India for DESU, as ordered by State Commission and upheld by National Commission - Appeal disposed of.
