Afaq Husain v Upsrtc and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 758; (2008) 5 SCC 715; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 324; 2008 (3) CLR 101; 2008 (3) ESC 365; 2008 (119) FLR 91; JT 2008 (6) SC 367; 2009 (1) LLJ 22; 2008 (3) LLN 111; 2008(7) SCALE 249; [2008] 6 S.C.R. 999; 2009 (2) SLR 321

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Re Employment

Summary: U.P. Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Appellant was appointed as a Conductor on an ad hoc basis as a daily wager - Termination with one month's notice pay - Held, only when the services of an employee continues for a period of more than one year, apart from the notice pay, he becomes entitled to be paid compensation equivalent to 15 days' wage for every completed year of service or any part thereof in excess of six months - Requirements of s. 6N had not been complied with - Appellant has been awarded a just compensation, particularly, in view of the fact that he, without any right, worked in the Corporation for a period of 18 years - Appeal dismissed.
