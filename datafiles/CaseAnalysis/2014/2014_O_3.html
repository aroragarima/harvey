Oil and Natural Gas Corporation Limited v Official Liquidator of Ambica Mills Company Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 259; (2015) 5 SCC 300; AIR 2014 SC 3011; 2014(4) ALL MR 440; [2014] 184 Comp Cas 405; 2014 (3) CompLJ 69; JT 2014 (5) SC 309; 2014(5) SCALE 190

Case Digest

Subject: Petroleum & Gas; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Levied price, Supply of gas, Secured creditor

Summary: Petroleum & Gas - Contract & Commercial - Practice & Procedure - Oil and Natural Gas Commission (Transfer of Undertaking and Repeal) Act, 1993 - Levied price - Supply of gas - Secured creditor - Legality - Appellant/Oil and Natural Gas Corporation Ltd. was statutory corporation - Industries subscribing to gas supplied by appellant formed an association in 1978 called 'The Association of Natural Gas Consuming Industries of Gujarat' (Association) - Respondent/Ambica Mills Co. Ltd. was one among members of said Association - Appellant and members of Association entered into an agreement for supply of natural gas - Later in new contract levied price for supply of gas was Rs.504/- per unit - Association/respondent filed application before HC and the same was allowed - Appellant file application before Single Judge in which directions were sought that outstanding amounts of appellant be paid by respondent, an injunction was sought restraining respondent from creating any charge alienation and discharging of immoveable assets of company in liquidation and the same was dismissed - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before DB and the same was dismissed by DB - Hence instant appeal - Whether Judgments rendered by Single Judge and DB call for any interference -

Held, respondent had not identified any particular immovable assets which would be made available in discharging liabilities in favor of appellant - SC had no hesitation in rejecting submission appellant that interim order read with undertaking expressed an intention to create an enforceable charge of any particular asset of company in liquidation - Appellant submitted that respondents had specifically agreed to make assets available for discharging liability of appellant, this, according appellant was tantamount to creating an enforceable charge - SC was unable to accept aforesaid submission - In face of directions given by SC in the case of Oil and Natural Gas wherein SC had directed that appellant was at liberty to take immediate steps to recover charges due from respondents in light of judgment - SC did not direct that in view of undertaking the respondents had created enforceable charge in favor of appellant - Furthermore, it was matter of record that even appellant did not consider itself to be a secured creditor - At time when respondent came under jurisdiction of Official Liquidator, none of the two options adverted to earlier was exercised by appellant - Plea of being secured creditor was clearly an afterthought - Judgments rendered by Single Judge and DB do not call for any interference - Appeals dismissed.
