Suraj Parkash Gupta and Others Etc v State of Jammu and Kashmir and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2858; (2000) 7 SCC 561; AIR 2000 SC 2386; JT 2000 (5) SC 413; 2000 LabIC 2588; 2000(4) SCALE 268; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 807; 2000 (4) SLR 486; 2000 (5) SLT 360; 2000 (3) Supreme 637

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - J and K Engineering (Gazetted) Service Recruitment Rules, 1978, r. 5 - J and K Public Service Commission (Limitation of Functions) Regulations, 1957, Regn.4(d)(ii) - J and K Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1956, r. 23 - Constitution of J and K, art. 133 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 320 - Service Rules - Validity of the order passed by the State Govt. regularizing, at one stroke, several ad hoc promotions is in issue - Ad hoc promotion without consultation with Public Service Commission - Against quota of promotes and also 10% out of 20% quota of direct recruits - Resulting in delay in direct recruitment that too only against 10% of quota - Validity of retrospective regularization on the ground of delay, without recommendation of Commission - Held, invalid and that the ad hoc/stop gap service of the promotes cannot be treated as non-est merely because P.S.C. was not consulted in respect of continuance of the ad hoc/stop gap service beyond six month - There is no 'rota' rule applicable and the 'quota' rule has not broken down - Appeal disposed of.
