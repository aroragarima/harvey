Commissioner of Income Tax v Bhurangya Coal Company

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, A.K. Sarkar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 116; AIR 1959 SC 254; 1959 CRLJ 565; [1958] 34 ITR 802

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Land & Property - Contract & Commercial - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Trade - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 12 B and 66 - Sale of Goods Act, 1930, s. 2(7) - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 38 and 8 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 3(34) - (A) What is relevant date for determining transfer of title of an immovable property? - Held, relevant date is date of registration of deed of conveyance and not date of agreement to sell - In case of movable property title is passed with delivery of goods - (B)Whether fixtures attached to land are to be regarded as movable property? - Further held, fixtures will be held to be movable only if they are intended to be severed and sold separately - Appeal dismissed.
