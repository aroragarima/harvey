All India Federation of Tax Practitioners and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 834; (2007) 7 SCC 527; AIR 2007 SC 2990; 2007 (5) CompLJ 488; [2007] 293 ITR 406; JT 2007 (10) SC 305; 2007 (6) MLJ(SC) 1062; 2007(10) SCALE 178; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 147; [2007] 9 VST 126

Case Digest

Subject: Service Tax

Keywords: Finance Act, 1998, Medicinal Act, 1955, Andhra Pradesh Tax on Luxuries Act, 1987, Uttar Pradesh Tax on Luxuries Act, 1995, West Bengal Luxury Tax Act, 1994, Haryana Passengers and Goods Taxation Act, 1952, Constitution (Eighty-Eighth Amendment) Act,2003

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 246(1), 256 and 276, entry 60 list II sch. 7 and entry 97 list I sch. 7 - Income Tax Act - Finance Act, 1994 - Legislative competence of Parliament to levy service tax under Finance Act, 1994 and 1998 - Whether High Court in upholding Parliament to levy service tax on practising chartered accountants and architects having regard to entry 60 list II sch. 7 and art. 276 is justified? - Relied on Southern Pharmaceuticals and Chemicals, Trichur and Ors. v. State of Kerala and Ors. 1981 Indlaw SC 396 and Western India Theatres Ltd. v. Cantonment Board 1959 Indlaw SC 130 - Held, entry 60 list II, mentions 'Taxes on professions, trades, callings and employments', entry 60 is a taxing entry and is not a general entry so tax on professions to be read as a levy on professions, trades and art. 276 imposes professional tax for benefit of State, municipality and district board etc. - Further held, Parliament competent to levy service tax by way of Finance Act - Appeal dismissed without any costs.
