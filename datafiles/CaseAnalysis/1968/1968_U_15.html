South Bihar Sugar Mills Limited, Etc v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 249; AIR 1968 SC 922; 1989 (25) ECR 447; 1978 (2) E.L.T. 336; 1968 (2) SCJ 433; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 21

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Kiln Gas

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise and Salt Act, 1944, schedule 1, item 14A - Appellant manufactured sugar by carbonation process and paid excise duty on the same - Respondent contended that the process employed by the appellant involves compressing Co2 with the pressure achieved pushing it through sugarcane juice - Therefore, appellant produced Co2 through the lime kiln which was taken first to the Co2 pump and there compressed and then pushed into the tank - Respondents regarded appellant as manufacturers of compressed Co2 and levied excise duty on them under Item 14-H in Sch. 1 to the Act - Appellant filed writ petition submitting that the lime kiln was maintained to generate a mixture of gases and not Co2 and at no stage in the process of generating this mixture and passing it through the sugarcane juice was Co2 which formed one of the contents of the mixture - Said writ petition was dismissed by HC - Hence, present appeal - Whether the mixture of gases generated by the appellant was kiln gas or compressed Co2 covered by Item 14-H of 1944 Act? - Held, the gas generated by the appellant was kiln gas and not Co2 as known to the trade - Impugned kiln gas is neither Co2 nor compressed Co2 and therefore, cannot attract item 14-H in the first Schedule - The mere fact that at one stage or the other kiln gas is pressed at 40 to 45 lbs. per sq. inch by a pump or otherwise cannot mean that it is compressed Co2 - At the same time the duty being on manufacture and not on sale the mere fact that kiln gas generated by these concerns is not actually sold would not make any difference if what they generate and use in their manufacturing processes is carbon dioxide - Appeals allowed.
