Bhagwan Prasad Srivastava v N. P. Misra

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 56; (1970) 2 SCC 56; (1970) SCC (Cr) 292; AIR 1970 SC 1661; 1970 CRLJ 1401; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 317

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 197 - Defamatory language - Complaint - Cognizance - No previous sanction - Challenged - Respondent/complainant was working as civil assistant civil surgeon in hospital and appellant joined hospital as civil surgeon - Respondent filed complaint against appellant alleging insult and humiliation in public and use of defamatory language - Trial court, appellate court and revisional court upheld preliminary objection raised by appellant on the absence of previous sanction which was required u/s.197 of CrPC - Respondent filed revision before HC which disagreed with view of courts below and held s.197 CrPC to be inapplicable to instant case and directed Trial court to make further enquiry - Hence instant appeal by appellant - Whether complainant's case is covered u/s. 197 of CrPC and previous sanction of the superior authority is necessary before the trial court can take cognizance of the complaint.

Held, alleged offence consists of the use of defamatory and abusive words and of getting the complainant forcibly turned out of the operation theatre - Therefore instant court followed SC case of Prabhakar V. Sinari v. Shanker Anant Vertekar, 1968 Indlaw SC 179, it would be open to the appellant to place material on the record during, the course of the trial for showing what his duty as Civil Surgeon was and also that the impugned acts were inter-related with his official duty so as to attract the protection afforded u/s. 197, Cr. P.C. Hence, no material is found on the existing record suggesting that the impugned acts were done by the appellant in the discharge of his official duty or that they are directly connected with it. Appeal dismissed.
