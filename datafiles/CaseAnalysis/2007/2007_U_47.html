Ashok Kumar Sonkar v Union Of India & Others

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 150; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 19; (2007) 4 SCC 54; 2007 (3) AWC 2846; 2007 (1) ESC 169; JT 2007 (6) SC 127; 2007 (3) LLN 6; 2007(3) SCALE 517; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 95; 2007 (3) SLR 501; 2007 (2) UPLBEC 57; 2007 (3) UPLBEC 2835

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Banaras Hindu University Act, 1915

Summary: Banaras Hindu University Act, 1915 - Service - Filling up a vacant post of lecturer in Tridosa Vigyan in the Department of Basic Principles - President of India in his capacity as the Visitor of the University annul the appointment of appellant - Writ petition also dismissed - Appeal against - Held, in absence of any cut-off date specified in the advertisement or in the rules, the last date for filing of an application shall be considered as such - Appellant did not hold the requisite qualification as on the said cut-off date and he was, therefore, not eligible therefore - But if an appointment is illegal, it is non est in the eye of law, which renders the appointment to be a nullity - In making appointment of the appellant, the provisions of art. 14 and 16 of the Constitution and statutory rules were not complied with - Appeal dismissed.
