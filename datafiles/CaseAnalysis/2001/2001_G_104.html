Sharma Transport Represented By D. P. Sharma v Government of A. P. and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 December 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, B.N. Kirpal, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19917; (2002) 2 SCC 188; 2002 (1) ACC 85; AIR 2002 SC 322; 2002 (1) ALD(SC) 66; JT 2001 (Supp2) SC 1; 2001(8) SCALE 417; 2002 (1) SCJ 67; 2001 (8) SLT 234; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 390; 2001 (8) Supreme 496

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 301, 303 and 304 - Prohibition Act - Property Tax - Andhra Pradesh Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1963, s. 9(1)(b) - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Australian Constitution (ENG) - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Karnataka Motor Vehicle Rules - Motor Vehicles (All India Permit for Tourist and Transport Operators) Rules, 1993 - The Central Govt. after discussions with state governments and with their consent formulated policies in matter of concessions to be extended to tourist vehicles - A notification D/- 01-07-1995 was issued pursuant to a directive of Central Govt. - By notification issued by State Govt. in GOMs No. 83, Transport, Roads and Buildings (Tr II) Department D/- 05-06-2000 u/s. 9(1)(b) of Andhra Pradesh Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1963, order D/- 01-07-1995 was cancelled - (A) Whether plea of promissory estoppel is maintainable? - Held, it has to be noted that even though a concession is extended for a fixed period, same can be withdrawn in public interest - A notification granting exemption of tax can be withdrawn at any point of time - There cannot be estoppel against any statute - (B) Whether action is clearly violative of guarantees and protections provided by art. 301 of Constitution? - Further held, there were no pleadings in this regard in writ petitions, excepting some general statements about violation of art. 301 - It has been fairly conceded that President's assent as required has been obtained - Thus case is not relatable to art. 301, but art. 304 - Art. 304(b) provides that notwithstanding anything in art. 301 or 303, legislature of a state may by law impose such reasonable restrictions on freedom of trade, commerce or intercourse with or within that State as may be required in public interest - While power of Parliament u/art. 302 is subject to prohibition of preferences and discriminations decreed by art. 303(1) unless Parliament makes declaration contained in art. 303(2), state's power contained in art. 304(b) is made expressly free from prohibition contained in art. 303(1), because opening words of art. 304 contain a non obstante clause both to arts. 301 and 303 - Appeals dismissed.
