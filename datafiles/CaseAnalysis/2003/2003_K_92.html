Ranbir Singh and others v Kartar Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

25 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 249; (2003) 3 SCC 518; AIR 2003 SC 1858; 2003 (2) JhrCR(SC) 205; 2003 (2) KHCACJ 38; 2003 (2) RCR(Civil) 226; 2003(2) SCALE 424; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 291; 2003 (2) SLT 788; 2003 (2) Supreme 326

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Female Heirs, Ancestral Immovable Property

Summary: Punjab Limitation (Custom) Act, 1920 - Limitation Act, 1963 - whether the suit filed by the plaintiffs is barred by time. - The answer to this question depends on whether the suit filed by a daughter (plaintiff) in the year 1969 challenging the gift made by her mother (after her death in 1968) in favour of the defendants in the year 1936 as void ab initio is governed by the provisions of the Punjab Limitation (Custom) Act or Limitation Act. - Held, the Act does not govern suits brought by female heirs or other cognate relations to challenge alienations made by a widow who is in possession of the property of her husband, which had descended to him from his ancestors. - Therefore, the suit was not barred by time as the Act did not apply. It was the Limitation Act, 1963 that is applicable as held by the trial court as well as the first appellate court.
