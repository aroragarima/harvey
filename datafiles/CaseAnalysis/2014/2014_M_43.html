Annapurna v Mallikarjun and another

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 243; (2014) 6 SCC 397; 2014 (3) ALT(SC) 33; 2014 (4) Bom.C.R. 158; 2014 (2) CGLJ 630; 2014 (3) CLT(SC) 58; 2014 (6) MahLJ 551; 2015(1) M.P.H.T. 285; 2014 (4) MPLJ 498; 2014 (2) OLR 112; 2015 (177) PLR 33; 2014 (2) RCR(Civil) 663; 2014 (124) RD 624; 2014(5) SCALE 59; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 299; 2014 (2) WLN(SC) 57

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, O. 21 rr. 89, 92 - Auction - Sale certificate - Limitation - Maintainability - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 127 - Executing Court put a house to auction and after rejecting the objections raised by judgment-debtor/respondent No. 1 in an application under O. 21 r. 89 CPC confirmed the Court Sale by issuing Certificate of Sale in favour of the auction purchaser/appellant - Appeal filed by respondent No. 1 against the said order was dismissed - Respondent No. 1 then preferred Writ Petition, HC allowed the writ petition by quashing the impugned order of the Executing Court and remitting the matter back to the Executing Court for fresh disposal of respondent No. 1 application under O. 21 r. 89 CPC - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC could have ignored the settled law that u/art. 127 of the Act an application to set aside a sale under O. 21 r. 89, CPC had to be filed within 60 days from the date of sale and same was the period for making the required deposit -

Held, rr. 89 and 92 of O. 21 CPC and art. 127 of the Act leave no manner of doubt that although O. 21 r. 89 CPC does not prescribe any period either for making the application or the required deposit, art. 127 of the Act now prescribed 60 days as the period within which such an application should be made - In absence of any separate period prescribed for making the deposit - In instant case it should be held that the HC committed grave error of law in not noticing the relevant provisions of CPC and the Act and in allowing the Writ Petition for re-consideration of the petition under O. 21 r. 89 CPC - In absence of required deposit made by the judgment-debtor within the time mandated by law, such an exercise would be only an exercise in futility because the Executing Court does not have any option but to reject the petition - In such a situation, the judgment under appeal was set aside - Appeal allowed.
