N. Satyanathan v K. Subramanyan and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 March 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Vivian Bose, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 14; AIR 1955 SC 459; 1955 (2) MLJ(SC) 31; 1955 SCJ 455; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 83

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Madras Motor Vehicles Rules

Summary: Election - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 48(d) - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 103 and 136 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 7(d)and 100(1)(c) - Madras Motor Vehicles Rules, r. 160B - Whether appellant is disqualified u/s. 7(d) of Act, 1951? - Held, yes - Present case is a straightforward illustration for the kind of contract contemplated by s. 7(d) of Act - At all material times appellant had been directly concerned, for his own benefit, in contract of carrying mail bags and postal articles entered into by him with Govt. in Postal Department - There was a valid contract between Govt. and appellant - Conflict between public duty and private interests - Appeal dismissed.
