Â Anandji Haridas and Company Private Limited v S. P. Kushare, S. T. O. Nagpur and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 September 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 220; AIR 1968 SC 565; 1968 (1) ITJ 586; 1968 (1) SCJ 820; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 661; 1968 (21) STC 326

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Keywords: Failure to file returns

Summary: Constitution - Sales Tax - Central Provinces and Berar Sales Tax Act, 1947, ss. 2, 6, 8, 10, 11, 11(4)(a), 11A, 11A(3), 11A(1), 22A and 22B - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 226 - Escapement of assessment - (A) Whether s. 11(4)(a) of Central Provinces and Berar Sales Tax Act, 1947 is ultra vires art. 14 of Constitution, 1950? - Main contention advanced on behalf of appellants is that sub-s. (3) of s. 11A has brought about a discrimination between those dealers proceeded against u/s. 11(4)(a) and those dealt with u/s. 11A - Held, classification made between registered and unregistered dealers is not a reasonable classification - S. 11(4)(a) is liable to he struck down as being discriminatory in view of s. 11A(3) - S. 11(4)(a) is separable from rest of sub-section - Its separation from that sub-section does not affect implementation of other provisions of Act - S. 11(4)(a) is void as it is violative of art. 14 - (B) Applicability of s. 11A - Held, before a person can be dealt with u/s. 11A, it must be shown that in consequence of any information which has come into his possession, Commr. is satisfied that any turnover of that dealer during any period has been under-assessed or has escaped assessment or assessed at a lower rate or any deduction has been wrongly made there from - (C) Whether it can be said that appellants' turnovers for period 01-05-1952 to 30-10-1955 had escaped assessment? - Held, those turnovers had not been assessed - It was duty of appellants not only to submit their quarterly returns but send along with those returns treasury challans in proof of payment of tax admittedly due from them - As they have failed to do so within prescribed period, it follows that turnovers in question had escaped assessment - (D) Whether in instant case assessing authority can be said to have been satisfied about escapement of assessment as a consequence of any information which had come into his possession? - Held, assessing authorities knew that appellants had neither submitted their returns nor treasury challans in proof of payment of tax due from them - Knowledge of fact that appellants had not submitted their quarterly returns as well as treasury challans constituted information to assessing authority from which it could be satisfied and in fact it was satisfied that turnovers with which we are concerned in this case bad escaped assessment - Appellants' case falls both u/s. 11(4)(a) and s. 11A(1) - Therefore, it was open to assessing authority to proceed against them under any one of those two sections - (E) Whether notices issued by assessing authority in 1955 were valid notices? - Held, period 01-11-1952 to 31-01-1953 forms part of quarter commencing from 01-11-1952 - No notice was given in respect of that quarter - A quarter forms a unit by itself - Every escapement of assessment coming within scope of s. 11(4)(a) is also an escapement of assessment u/s. 11 A(1), a notice issued u/s. 11 (4)(a) would be a valid notice in respect of a proceeding u/s. 11A(1) - Assessing authority has no competence to assess turnovers of appellants in respect of quarters commencing from 01-05-1952 and ending with 31-01-1953 as same is barred by time u/s. 11A - Therefore, it follows that proceeding in respect of that quarter is barred by limitation - (F) Whether notices issued in 1955 in respect of turnovers relating to other quarters were in accordance with law? - Held, it is not a notice in respect of any particular quarter, it is a notice in respect of period 01-01-1953 to 31-12-1953 - From notice in question it cannot be made out whether assessing authorities wanted to deal with appellants u/s. 10(1) or u/s. 11(4) - Direction issued to respondents to refrain from assessing appellants in respect of those turn overs - Order accordingly.
