Sayed Muhammed Mashur Kunhi Koya Thangal v Badagara Jumayath Palli Dharas Committee and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1601; (2004) 7 SCC 708; AIR 2004 SC 4365; 2004 (57) ALR 320; 2004 (56) ALR 737; 2004 (4) AWC 2893; 2004 (2) CGLJ 416; JT 2004 (6) SC 556; 2005 (1) LCD 55; 2005 (1) MLJ(SC) 194; 2004 (3) RCR(Civil) 785; 2004 (2) RD 730; 2004(7) SCALE 53

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Muslim Wakfs Act, 1954

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Muslim Wakfs Act, 1954, s. 3(f) - Whether High Court was justified in allowing second appeal u/s. 100, CPC, 1908 without giving any opportunity to parties to put forth their case on substantial questions of law framed by it? - Serious infirmity being contrary to requirement of s. 100, CPC, 1908 - Held, HC in second appeal, was not right in upsetting findings of fact recorded by first Appellate Court, that too without putting parties on notice on substantial question of law - Appeal partly allowed.
