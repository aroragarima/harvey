State Of Punjab v Central Bureau Of Investigation & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

02 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 619; (2011) 9 SCC 182; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 3279; 2011 CRLJ 4928; 2012 (2) MLJ(Crl) 420; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 152; [2011] 11 S.C.R. 281

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 161, 164, 173 173(1), 173(2), 173(8), 482 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136, 226 - Immoral Traffic (Prevention) Act, 1956, ss. 4, 5 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 120B, 195, 201, 202, 211, 218, 219, 221, 224, 323, 342, 344, 366, 366A, 376, 384, 406, 420, 465, 468, 471, 506 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7, 13(2) - Order for Reinvestigation - Sustainability - Challan had been filed on 1-6-2007 in respect of FIR no. 82 dt. 18-04-2007 and Trial Court had also framed charges on 8-11-2007 - HC had passed Order dt. 11-12-2007 directing CBI to carry out reinvestigation into alleged case - Petitioner/State contended that HC had powers u/s. 173(8) CrPC to direct further investigation by police, but HC had no power to direct reinvestigation into alleged case by police - Hence, instant petition - Whether Order of HC directing CBI for reinvestigation in to alleged case to secure ends of justice was sustainable - Held, even where charge sheet or challan had been filed by police u/s. 173(2) CrPC, police could undertake further investigation but not fresh investigation or re-investigation in respect of offence u/s. 173(8) CrPC - However, s. 482 CrPC had provided that nothing in CrPC to limit or affect inherent powers of HC to make such orders as was necessary to give effect to any order under CrPC or to prevent abuse of process of any Court or otherwise to secure ends of justice - Hence, provisions of CrPC. did not limit or affect inherent powers of HC to make such orders as might be necessary to give effect to any order under Court or to prevent abuse of any process of Court or otherwise to secure ends of justice - HC was right in justification that justice would not be done to alleged case if investigation stayed in hands of local police and hence investigation of case should be handed over to CBI - Hence, even though investigation was complete in one case and charge sheet had been filed by police, it was necessary in ends of justice that CBI should carry out investigation into case - Appeal dismissed.
