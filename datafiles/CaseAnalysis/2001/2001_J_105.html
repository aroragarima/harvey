Fuerst Day Lawson Limited v Jindal Exports Limited

Supreme Court of India

04 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 307; (2001) 6 SCC 356; AIR 2001 SC 2293; 2001 (4) ALD(SC) 4; 2001 (2) ARBLR 1; 2001 (3) CompLJ 9; 2001 (91) DLT 373; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 263; 2001 LabIC 2073; 2001 (2) RAJ 1; 2001(3) SCALE 708; 2001 (4) SCJ 206; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 479; 2001 (4) SLT 67; 2001 (4) Supreme 141

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Arbitration (Protocol And Convention) Act, 1937

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Arbitration, Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 48 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 367 - General Clauses Act, 1897 - Foreign Awards (Recognition and Enforcement) Act, 1961, ss. 7 and 8 - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Arbitration (Protocol and Convention) Act, 1937 - International General Produce Association ('IGPA') - An ordinance issued by President or Governor - Stare decisis - Incuria - (A) Whether provisions of Ordinance as well as Act are same and have same force? - Held, all provisions of Ordinance as well as Act are same, therefore, use of word 'the Ordinance' shall also mean Act and vice versa - Said judgment of High Court is affirmed by this Court in Thyssen judgment - Thyssen judgment has not failed to notice either a statutory provision in substance and effect or a binding precedent running counter to reasoning and result reached - (B) When did Act legally come into force? - Further held, Act came into force on 22-08-1996, for all practical and legal purposes it shall be deemed to have been effective from 25-01-1996 particularly when provisions of Ordinance and Act are similar and there is nothing in Act to contrary so as to make Ordinance ineffective - Impugned judgment and order are set aside - Appeal allowed.
