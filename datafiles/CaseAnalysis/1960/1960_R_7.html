Â R. S. A. C. Kasi Iyer v Commissioner of Income Tax, Mysore, TravancoreCochin

Supreme Court of India

01 September 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 131; AIR 1961 SC 210; [1960] 40 ITR 494; 1961 (1) SCJ 330; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 466

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Travancore Income Tax Act, Opium and Revenue Laws (Extension of Application) Act, 1950, Taxation of Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947, Travancore Taxation On Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1124

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922,ss.25, 42 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Opium and Revenue Laws (Extension of Application) Act, 1950 - Taxation of Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947 - Travancore Income Tax Act, s.25 - Travancore Taxation On Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1124 - Appellants were a firm of merchants carrying on business in yarn - Appellants submitted return under Act showing a net return of Rs.4,78,5945-0 as assessable income - Assessed to income-tax and super tax by Income Tax Officer on that return - In 1124 Legislature was enacted Act conferring authority upon Govt. to constitute a commission to investigate in accordance with the provisions of Act u/s.5 - Commission detained by its report that appellants had in the accounting year 1118 made a secret profit of Rs.1,31,750 which was not included in the earlier assessment - Commission then proceeded to compute the tax payable by the appellants and found that the amount of tax payable by the appellants on their true income was Rs. 1,35,736-8-0 and that they were liable to pay that amount subject to credit for the tax, already paid - Income Tax Officer commenced reassessment proceedings against the appellants and directed the appellants to pay income-tax and super tax on the concealed income - Held, no force in contentions - Expression " the report " in s.8(2) refers to the report made u/s.8(1) by members of Commission appointed by Govt. under Act and on a consideration of report, Govt. of India has, since the enactment of the 1950 Act, power to direct that proceedings for assessment or re- assessment be taken under Act - On plain language used by Legislature in s.3(c) of 1950 Act, the contention raised on behalf of the appellants was unsustainable - S.8(2) removed the bar of limitation which arose by s. 25 of Act - It was competent to Income Tax Officer to reopen assessment proceedings notwithstanding any lapse of time - Previous order of assessment not operate as a bar to such reassessment - Appeal dismissed.
