Anurag Patel v Uttar Pradesh Public Service Commission and others

Supreme Court of India

29 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 953; (2005) SCC (L&S) 563; (2005) 9 SCC 742; AIR 2005 SC 1262; 2005 (1) AWC 511; 2005 (1) LLN 91; 2004(8) SCALE 857; 2004 (8) SLR 642; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 888

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Service - Combined State Services/ Upper Subordinate Services (Preliminary) Examination for selection to various posts - By Uttar Pradesh Public Service Commission - Re-allocation of posts - Appointment on basis of merit list - Petitioners appointed as Sales Tax Officer-II, whereas persons who secured ranks lower to them, got appointment as Deputy Collectors and Division Bench of HC only directed reallocation of the posts according to the merit prepared in the select list - Whether there is any irregularity in the order? - Held, that it is a clear injustice to persons who are more meritorious and directed that a list of all selected backward class candidates shall be prepared separately including those candidates selected in general category and their appointments to posts shall be made strictly in accordance with merit as per select list and preference of a person higher in select list will be seen first and appointment given accordingly - No interference in HC's order - Appeals Dismissed.
