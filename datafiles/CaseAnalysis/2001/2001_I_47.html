Vikramaditya Pandey v Industrial Tribunal, Lucknow and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20343; (2001) 2 SCC 423; (2001) SCC (L&S) 438; AIR 2001 SC 672; 2001 (1) CLR 975; 2001 (88) FLR 741; JT 2001 (1) SC 608; 2001 (34) LabIC 646; 2001 (1) LLJ 701; 2001 (1) LLN 852; 2001(1) SCALE 211; 2001 (1) SLT 577; 2001 (1) Supreme 319; 2001 (1) UC 356

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Dookan Aur Vanijya Adhishthan Adhiniyam, 1962, Workmen&apos;s Compensation Act, 1923, Cooperative Societies Employees&apos; Service Regulations, 1975

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(s), 6-N - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Cooperative Societies Employees &' Service Regulations, 1975, regn. 103 - Termination of services - Entitlement for reinstatement - Back wages - Appellant was employed as clerk on ad hoc basis by respondent no. 2 Bank - Appellant was serving continuously with small motivated breaks to ensure that appellant did not have continuous service of more than 89 days - His services were terminated by oral order - Appellant raised industrial dispute - Respondent no. 1 Industrial Tribunal held that termination of services of appellant was clearly retrenchment as defined in s.2(S) of 1947 Act, and was contrary to s.6(N) of 1947 Act - Tribunal refused to grant relief of reinstatement on ground that regular appointment to post held by appellant could be made by U.P. Cooperative Institutional Service Board as per Regulations, and appellant could not be reinstated in service as regular employee - However, Tribunal granted benefits of retrenchment with 12% interest for relevant period - Appellant filed petition before HC who concurred with finding recorded by Tribunal - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether appellant was entitled to reinstatement in service with back wages, when termination of his services held to be illegal - Held, inconsistent provisions contained in Regulations should be inoperative, not provisions of other statutes mentioned in regn. 103 of the Regulations - Tribunal correctly understood Regulations but wrongly refused relief on ground that no reinstatement could be ordered on regular employment in view of provisions contained in Regulation - HC read Regulations and misunderstood that there was any inconsistency between Regulations and 1947 Act, and other labour laws - Misreading and wrong approach of HC resulted in wrong conclusion - Thus, both Tribunal as well as HC were not right and justified on facts and in refusing relief of reinstatement of appellant in service with back wages - However, it just and appropriate in interest of justice to grant back wages only to extent of 50% - Thus, award of Tribunal and order of HC in regard to denial of relief of reinstatement of appellant with back wages was set aside and directed his reinstatement in service, with 50% back wages - Appeal allowed.
