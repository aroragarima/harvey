Commissioner of Income Tax, Lucknow v Madho Pd. Jatia

Supreme Court of India

17 August 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, R.S. Sarkaria, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 167; (1976) 4 SCC 92; (1976) SCC (Tax) 443; AIR 1977 SC 420; 1976 CTR 438; [1976] 105 ITR 179; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 202; 1977 TAXLR 202; 1976 UJ 810

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s.66(1) - Exemption - Deduction - Assessments - Irrecoverable - Assessee was owner of building and income from this building was assessed from year to year u/s.9 of 1922 Act as income from property - Dispute arises between assessee and her tenant representing and rent due became irrecoverable - At time of assessment assessee was able to secure deduction as unrealised rent in previous years - Assessee made similar claims for deduction at time of assessment not made before Income tax Officer but was made in appeal before Appellant Assistant Commissioner was dismissed - Appeal made to Tribunal directed Income-tax Officer to compute total rent and directed that extent irrecoverable rent had not been exempted in previous assessment - Appeal made by appellant to HC favoured respondent - Hence, instant appeal - Whether assessee was entitled for deduction under irrecoverable rent of property - Held, taxing authorities had computed income of that property to its bona fide annual value - Item 38 exempts from payment of tax such part of income in tax payable under head property as was equal to amount of rent payable for year but not paid by tenant of assessee and proved to be irrecoverable - Assessee gets relief in respect of amount of irrecoverable rent - Deduction claimed by assessee at assessment cannot exceed amount of rent payable for year - Amount of irrecoverable rent exceeds amount of rent payable for year, right of assessee to claim benefit of exemption does not, exhausted by his claimed exemption in one year - Hence, by item 38 assessee entitled to claim deduction under head property - Appeal dismissed.
