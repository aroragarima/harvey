State of Madhya Pradesh v Pappu @ Ajay

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2283; (2008) 16 SCC 758; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 461; 2008 (3) JCC 2066; JT 2008 (8) SC 647; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 948; 2008(11) SCALE 1; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 793

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376(1) r/w. s. 511, 324 and 452 - Rape - Conviction - Trial court convicted appellant u/s. 376 (1) r/w. s. 511, 324 and 452 and sentenced him to undergo rigorous imprisonment for four years with a fine of Rs.2,000/- - On appeal, HC reduced the sentenced to the period already undergone - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC was justified in reducing the sentence? - Held, no - No reason indicated by HC while directing reduction of sentence - It is duty of every court to award proper sentence having regard to the nature of the offence and the manner in which it was executed or committed etc - For deciding just and appropriate sentence to be awarded for an offence, the aggravating and mitigating factors and circumstances in which a crime has been committed are to be delicately balanced on the basis of really relevant circumstances in a dispassionate manner by the Court - HC's order not sustainable, hence set aside - Trial Court's order restored - Appeal allowed
