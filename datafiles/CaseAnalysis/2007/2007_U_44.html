Vijay Singh v Union Of India & Ors

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 152; (2007) 9 SCC 63; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 664; AIR 2007 SC 1384; 2007 (5) AWC 5305; 2007 (2) ESC 325; 2007 (113) FLR 249; 2007(3) SCALE 581; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 11; 2007 (3) SLR 871

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Deputy Commissioner Of Police

Summary: Delhi Police (Punishment and Appeal) Rules, 1980, r. 15(2) - Appellant was head constable in Delhi Police - Charge was framed after a preliminary enquiry - Order of dismissal - Plea that no prior approval was obtained from the Additional Commissioner of Police - Held, Additional Commissioner of Police being higher in hierarchy next to DGP, the requirement of his approval is mandatory, so that the delinquent officer is not prejudiced or harassed unnecessarily in a departmental enquiry - It is a case of violation of mandatory provisions of law - Order of dismissal set aside - Reinstatement without back wages ordered - Appeal allowed.
