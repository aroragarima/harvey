S. Renuka and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, G.B. Pattanaik, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1258; (2002) 5 SCC 195; (2002) SCC (L&S) 689; AIR 2002 SC 1523; 2002 (93) FLR 1141; 2002 (2) JhrCR(SC) 42; JT 2002 (3) SC 246; 2002 LabIC 1451; 2002 (3) LLN 589; 2002(3) SCALE 85; 2002 (2) SCJ 590; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 697; 2002 (3) SLR 407; 2002 (2) SLT 662; 2002 (2) Supreme 618; 2002 (2) UPLBEC 1551

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Marriage & Divorce

Summary: Family & Personal - Constitution of India, 1950 - Family Courts Act, 1984 - Special Rules for Andhra Pradesh State Higher Judicial Service, r. 2 - Andhra Pradesh State and Subordinate Service Rules, r. 22 - Writ petition - Establishment of Family Courts and Mahila Courts in Andhra Pradesh - High Court desired these courts be manned by women - Unavailability of enough women Judges - HC sent names of nine candidates to State Govt. for appointment after written examination and interview - Govt. brought to notice of HC Rule 22 of A. P. State and Subordinate Service Rules - HC informed that appointments of nine provisionally selected candidates shall be provisionally as Family Court Judges under Family Courts Act to man Family Courts and Mahila Courts only - Whether provisionally selected candidates recruited as District Judges in Andhra Pradesh State Higher Judicial Service can be kept out of service by provisional appointment to some other post and recruiting them to posts of District Judges on availability of vacancies reserved for women? - Held, it must be noted that vacancies were for Judges of Family Courts and Mahila Courts - These courts could be manned by District and Sessions Judges, Grade II - State Govt. had thus created 10 posts of District and Sessions Judges, Grade II - Advertisement was also for appointment to posts of District and Sessions Judges, Grade II - Appointments were to be not to any ex-cadre posts but to posts in cadre of District and Sessions Judges, Grade II - Rules prescribed that in cadre of District and Sessions Judges there had to be reservations for Scheduled Tribes, Scheduled Castes, Backward Classes (Group A, B, C or D) and women - Rules did not allow 100% reservation for women - By reserving all 10 posts for women HC had inadvertently created a 100% reservation for women - Further, posts advertised were 5 open competition, 2 Scheduled Castes, 1 Scheduled Tribe, 1 Backward Class Group A and 1 Backward Class Group B - Yet panel sent to Govt. consisted of 7 open competition candidates, 1 Scheduled Caste candidate and 1 Backward Class Group D candidate - Such a selection was entirely against Rules and against Reservation policy - Rules also required that if no SC or ST candidate was available then vacancy had to be carried forward - Similarly vacancy of Backward Class Groups A, B, C and/or D could not be converted into another category - Because of these difficulties persons empanelled could not be appointed in cadre of District and Sessions Judges, Grade II - HC initially considered that petitioners could be appointed in ex-cadre posts as Family Court and/or Mahila Court Judges and then absorb them in cadre of District and Sessions Judges, Grade II as and when vacancy for women arose - HC correctly realised that this could not be done - It was also noticed that candidates provisionally selected, that is, petitioners had got less marks than those normally prescribed for such selection - Further, as posts were lying vacant for such a long period of time initially, it was suggested that if petitioners filed an undertaking before this Court, that they are willing to be appointed in ex-cadre posts of Judges of Family Court and/or Mahila Court and that they will not claim any right to be subsequently absorbed in cadre of District and Sessions Judges, Grade II then Court could consider directing State Govt. to appoint these nine petitioners - Eight of these petitioners have filed undertakings before this Court - However, on a proper consideration of matter, this Court cannot direct State Govt. to appoint these petitioners - If such a direction were to be given, this Court would be creating ex-cadre posts and making appointments contrary to Rules - Thus it is not possible for this Court to appoint petitioners, on ex-cadre posts, as Judges of Family Courts and Mahila Courts - Writ petition dismissed.
