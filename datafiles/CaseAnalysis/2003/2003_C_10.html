Guruvayur Devaswom Managing Committee and Another v C.K. Rajan and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 633; (2003) 7 SCC 546; AIR 2004 SC 561; JT 2003 (7) SC 312; 2003 (4) KHCACJ 67; 2003 (3) KLT 618; 2003(6) SCALE 401; 2003 (1) SCW 6039; 2003 (7) SLT 421; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 619; 2003 (6) Supreme 107

Case Digest

Subject: Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Guruvayoor Devaswom Act, 1978, Guruvayoor Devaswom Rules, 1980, Kerala Public Men's Corruption (Investigations and Inquiries) Act, 1987

Summary: [A.] Trusts & Associations - Guruvayoor Devaswom Act, 1978, ss. 6, 17, 18, 23, 24, 33, 36, 38 - Guruvayoor Devaswom Rules, 1980, r.10 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 21 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 12, 14, 142, 21, 226, 23, 25, 26, 32 - Commissions Of Inquiry Act, 1952, ss. 3, 3(4), 4, 5, 5A, 5B, 8B, 8C - Kerala Public Men's Corruption (Investigations and Inquiries) Act, 1987 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 92 - Scope and ambit of a Public Interest Litigation in matter of management of a temple governed by provisions of a statutory enactment - Held, Court should be circumspect in entertaining public interest litigation - There may be dispute amongst devotees as to what practices should be followed by temple authorities or rites and rituals to be performed in people or omission thereof - Any dissuasion in favour of one sector of people may hurt sentiments of other Courts normally, thus, at first instance would not enter into such disputed arena, particularly, when by reason therefore fundamental right of a group of devotees under art.25 and 26 may be infringed - Further held, principles of natural justice and fair play ought to be followed even in pro bono public proceedings - Courts undoubtedly would be parens patriae in relation to idols, but when statute governs field and State takes over management, ordinarily Courts would not step in It is trite that only because floodgates of cases will be opened, by itself may not be no ground to close doors of courts of justice doors of courts must be kept open, but Court cannot shut its eyes to ground realities while entertaining a public interest litigation.
