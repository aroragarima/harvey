Dattatreya Shanker Mote and Others v Anand Chintaman Datar and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 October 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, A. Alagiriswami

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 350; (1974) 2 SCC 799; [1975] 2 S.C.R. 224

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 21, r. 66, O. 34, r. 14 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 52 and 100 - Appellants had filed Civil Suit for recovery from respondents - A compromise decree was passed under which a charge was created for decretal amount on three pieces of property - This decree was registered but due to inadvertence charge on Kakakuva Mansion was not shown in Index of registration - Thereafter respondents mortgaged said property to plaintiff-respondent No. 14 - Respondents created a further charge in favour of said plaintiff - In meantime appellants had recovered some amounts by execution of their decree by sale of other 2 properties - In spite of these sales a large balance was still due, and in order to recover appellants filed a suit Civil Judge against Mansion over which there was a charge created in favour of appellants by decree - Notices were issued under O. 21, r. 66 of CPC, 1908 to respondents - Executing Court held that presence of plaintiff - Respondent No. 14 was not necessary for purposes of effecting sale of appellants and accordingly, it vacated notices - Against said order respondent No. 14 filed First Appeal in HC - During pending of this suit, suit property was put up for sale and it was purchased by appellants with leave of Court - In view of this development respondent No. 14 impleaded appellants as purchasers of equity of redemption - Trial Judge held that appellants had a prior charge over property and were bound by mortgage in favour of plaintiff-respondents No.s 14 and 15 - It further held that rights of a simple mortgagee are not 'property in hands of' mortgagee who could not be protected by proviso to s. 100 of Act - HC by its judgment modified decree of trial Judge holding that as mortgage in favour of respondent was protected under proviso to s. 100 of Act it is free from charge in favour of appellants - Whether the order of HC is valid in law? - Held, on evidence, came to conclusion that respondent No. 14 had no notice of prior charge inasmuch as search of indices did not disclose that there was charge on suit property - SC accepts finding of both courts that respondent No. 14 had no notice of prior charge created by decree - A reference to proviso to s. 100 of Act would show that in order to make it applicable it has to be shown that (a) property against which charge is to be enforced must have been transferred for consideration; (b) transferee has no notice of charge; (c) property which is subject-matter of charge is in hands of person to whom such property has been transferred - Doctrine of notice, even apart from statutory provisions, is firmly embedded in jurisprudence of this country as part of equitable principles which courts administer in conformity with maxim 'justice, equity and good conscience' - Thus, a subsequent mortgagee with notice of a prior charge takes mortgage subject to charge - But as in this case finding is that respondent did not have notice of appellants charge, appeals will have to be dismissed - Appeal dismissed.
