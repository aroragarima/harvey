State of Himachal Pradesh v Nikku Ram and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1201; (1995) 6 SCC 219; (1995) SCC (Cr) 1090; AIR 1996 SC 67; 1995 (4) CCR 70; 1995 CRLJ 4184; 1995 (60) DLT 568; 1996 (1) DMC 131; 1995 (2) HLR 598; 1995(5) SCALE 94; [1995] Supp3 S.C.R. 177

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Dowry Prohibition Act 1961, s. 2 - Indian Penal Code 1860, ss. 304 B, 306, 324 and 498 A - Evidence Act 1872, s. 113 A - 'Dowry' demand made after solemnization of marriage would also constitute Dowry - Proof for Dowry-death, and abetment of suicide was in question - Accused persons alleged to have demanded Dowry and ill-treated deceased leading to suicide - Held, s. 304-B was not attracted as injuries found on person of deceased was not sufficient to have caused her death - No evidence showing deceased was harassed within meaning of Expl. (b) of s. 498-A and presumption under s. 113-A of Evidence Act cannot be raised making no Offence under s. 306 - Offence under s. 324 was made out but on considering 80 years of age, fine of Rs. 3,000/- was imposed.
