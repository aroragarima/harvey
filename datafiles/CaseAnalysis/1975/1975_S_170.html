Govind v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 March 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, P.K. Goswami, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 629; (1975) 2 SCC 148; (1975) SCC (Cr) 468; AIR 1975 SC 1378; 1975 (1) ALR 252; 1975 CRLJ 1111; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 946

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Public Nuisance

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19, 22 and 21 - Police Act ss. 23 and 46 - Madhya Pradesh Police Regulations, regn. 855 and 856 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 325, 324, 341, 147 and 34 - Petitioner, a habitual criminal and was under continuous surveillance by police - (A) Constitutional validity of regns. 855 and 856 - Provision in regn. 856 for domiciliary visits and other actions by the police is intended to prevent commission of offences - Object of domiciliary visits is to see that the person subjected to surveillance is in his home and has not gone out of it for commission of any offence - Regns. 855 and 856 have the force of law - (B) Whether provisions of regn. 856 offend any of the fundamental rights of the petitioner? - Held, right to privacy is part of art. 21 but there cannot be absolute freedom, and freedom can be curtailed by procedure established by law - Regn. 855 empowers surveillance only of persons against whom reasonable materials exist to induce the opinion that they show 'a determination, to lead a life of crime - Regns. 855 and 856 valid and constitutional - Petition dismissed.
