Swamy Devi Dayal Hospital and Dental College v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

27 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 543; (2014) 13 SCC 506; AIR 2014 SC 284; JT 2013 (11) SC 600; 2013(10) SCALE 608; 2013 (5) SLR 547; 2013 (4) UPLBEC 2714

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Education

Keywords: Dental Council of India (Establishment of New Dental Colleges, Opening of New or Higher Course of Study or Training and Increase of Admission Capacity in Dental Colleges) Regulations, 2006, Dental Council of India Act, 1948

Summary: Education - Administrative - Dental Council of India, 1948, s. 10A - MDS Specialty courses - Permission - Denial - Legality - Respondent No.1 Central Govt. did not grant renewal of permission to petitioner Dental College for continuing 2 MDS specialty courses, on the basis of inspection report furnished by respondent No. 2 Dental Council of India (DCI) - Petitioner challenged respondent No.1 decision before HC - HC dismissed writ petition - Hence, instant petition - Petitioner contended that it was not given opportunity of personal hearing u/s. 10A of the Act - Held, cases of renewal should not be excluded from provisions of s. 10A of the Act - When petitioner applied for renewal of permission, application was processed in accordance with procedure laid down in s. 10A of the Act - Procedure prescribed in s. 10A contained requirement of following principle of natural justice at two stages; when DCI found deficiencies while examining school; and when Central Govt. before it passed away adverse orders - Law, thus specifically required that at the stage of a decision by Central Govt., again an opportunity of being heard was to be provided - S. 10A, thus, acknowledged the need of and conferred a very valuable right in favour of petitioner - If opportunity of being heard was given, applicant would get a chance to point out mistakes if any, factual or otherwise, in the report of DCI and Central Govt. would have version of applicant also before it at the time of taking final decision on report - Since no such opportunity of being heard requirement of proviso to s. 10A(4) of the Act was not afforded to petitioner, impugned decision of Central Govt. was set aside - Matter was remitted back to Central Govt. for taking fresh decision - Petition disposed of.
