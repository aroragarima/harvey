Â Har Shankar and Others v Dy. Excise and Taxation Commissioner and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, A.N. Ray, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, A. Alagiriswami, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 424; (1975) 1 SCC 737; AIR 1975 SC 1121; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 254; 1975 TAXLR 1569

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Service; Socio-Economic

Summary: Constitution - Constitution, arts. 19(1)(g), 19(6), 226 ,265 - Punjab Excise Act, 1914, s. 59(d) - Maintainability - (A) Whether writ petition can be used to dispose of contractual obligation? - Held, a writ petition is not an appropriate remedy for impeaching validity of contractual obligations -(B) Whether fundamental right to trade in intoxicants is violated by the regulations? - Held, no - There is no fundamental right to do trade or business in intoxicants - The State, under its regulatory powers, has the right to prohibit absolutely every form of activity in relation to intoxicants - Its manufacture, storage, export, import, sale and possession - (C ) Fixed fees levied on vendors of foreign liquor - Whether valid?- Held , Valid - As the amount payable by the licensees on the basis of the bids offered by them in auctions and on the basis of 'Fixed and Assessed Fees is neither a fee in the technical sense nor a tax but is in the nature of the price of a privilege, there is no question of the Financial Commissioner lacking the power to organise auctions so as to authorise the recovery of any amount which is not a fee properly so-called - Appeal dismissed.
