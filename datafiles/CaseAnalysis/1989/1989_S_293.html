Union Of India And Ors. v Suksha International And Nutan Gems And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

02 January 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 14; (1989) Supp1 SCC 422; AIR 1989 SC 690; 1989 (1) CompLJ 225; 1989 CRLR 340; 1989 CrLR(SC) 340; 1989 (20) ECC 20; 1989 (25) ECR 159; 1989 (39) E.L.T. 503; JT 1989 (1) SC 10; 1989(1) SCALE 4; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 1; 1989 (1) UJ 310

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Trade

Keywords: Shipment

Summary: Trade - Constitution - Import Export Policy 1982-83, paras 185(4) and 185(7) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136 and 226 - Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947, s. 3 - (A) Export Houses - Having Imprest Licences - Importing uncut and unpolished diamonds - Exporting after cutting and polishing - Time consuming process - Applicability of cl. (4) of para 185 of Policy - (B) Delay - Plea of unexplained delay in filing writ petition - High Court should specifically deal with it - Appeal by special leave - Held, conditions u/cl. (4) would not be attracted - Supreme Court remitted matter to High Court for fresh consideration whether delay was satisfactorily explained, assuming that plea though raised was not dealt with - Order accordingly.
