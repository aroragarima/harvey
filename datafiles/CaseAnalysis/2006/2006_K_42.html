Union of India and Another v Kankuben (Dead) By Lrs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 89; (2006) 9 SCC 292; AIR 2006 SC 1784; 2006 (2) AWC 1704; 2006 (4) G.L.R. 3435; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 149; 2006(3) SCALE 296; 2006 (3) SCJ 781; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 48; 2006 (7) SLR 746

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Railway Servants (Hours of Employment) Rules, 1961

Summary: [A.] Service - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10, 33-C(2) - Indian Railways Act, 1890, ss. 71-A, 71-H - Railway Servants (Hours of Employment) Rules, 1961 - Maintainability of recovery application u/s. 33 - Labour Court allowed claims in respect of certain claims of overtime allowance which according to them was payable in view of what is called as 'on and off duty' for taking out and bringing in locomotives from shed as was required to be done for purpose of operating them at and from different stations - HC confirmed award - Appeals against - Held, HC ought not to have presumed that award of Labour Court for grant of back wages is implied in relief of reinstatement or that award of reinstatement itself conferred right for claim of back wages - Appeals allowed.
