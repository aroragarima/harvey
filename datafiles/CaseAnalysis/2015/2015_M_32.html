Lal Shah Baba Dargah Trust and others v Magnum Developers and another

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 889; AIR 2016 SC 381; 2016 (2) ALD(SC) 55; 2016(1) ALL MR 934; 2016 (115) ALR 523; 2016 (2) AWC 1167; 2016 (1) Bom.C.R. 775; 2016 (1) CTC 540; JT 2015 (12) SC 245; 2016 (1) MLJ 330; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 558; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 966

Case Digest

Subject: Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Wakf Act, 1995, Repugnancy, Wakf (Amendment) Act, 2013, Wakf (Amendment) Bill, 2010

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Wakf Act, 1955, ss. 6, 7, 83(4), 83(9), 85 - Wakf Amendment Act, 2013 - Suit Property - Oust of Jurisdiction Challenged - Appellant no.1/plaintiff /trust filed suit claiming suit property as wakf property held by respondents/defendant nos. 1-7 - Tribunal allowed application of plaintiff and granted ad interim injunction restraining defendants from developing portion of suit plot - HC allowed revision of respondent and set aside order of Tribunal for want of jurisdiction on basis of amended Act - However, HC does not warrant with interim order and conferred jurisdiction to civil court to decide all those suits - Both the parties aggrieved by order of HC filed instant appeals.

Held, the implied repeal of an earlier law can be inferred only where there is enactment of a later law which had the power to override the earlier law and is totally inconsistent with the earlier law and the two laws cannot stand together. If the later law is not capable of taking the place of the earlier law, and for some reason cannot be implemented, the earlier law would continue to operate. To such a case, the rule of implied repeal may result in a vacuum which the law making authority may not have intended. However, there is presumption against implied repeal and reason against presumption that the legislature while enacting a law has complete knowledge of the existing laws on the subject matter and, therefore, when it is not providing a repealing provision, it gives out an intention not to repeal the existing legislation. Further, it is common practice that the old institution/ member continues to exercise duty till the time any new institution/member takes charge of that duty. In instant case, the intention of the Parliament while substituting s. 83(4) of earlier Act is not that one member tribunal vanishes or ceases to exist till a three member tribunal is constituted. Intention to bring new sub- section (4) in s. 83 is nothing but improvement in the constitution of the Tribunal and both the earlier and the substituted sub-sections are not inconsistent with each other. HC has committed serious error of law. Hence, impugned order passed by HC set aside, thereby allow appeal filed by trust. However, appeal that of defendants is dismissed holding that interim order passed by Tribunal shall continue. Appeals disposed of.
