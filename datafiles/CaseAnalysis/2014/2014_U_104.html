Usha Bharti v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

28 March 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 198; (2014) 7 SCC 663; 2014 (4) ADJ 587; AIR 2014 SC 1686; 2014(5) ALL MR 453; 2014 (3) AWC 2926; JT 2014 (4) SC 298; 2014 (123) RD 693; 2014(4) SCALE 275; [2014] 4 S.C.R. 1076; 2014 (3) UPLBEC 2325

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Council Of States, Dismissed, Counting Of Votes, Vote, Panchayats, Constitution (Seventy-Third Amendment) Act, 1992, Australian Constitution, U.P. Kshettra Panchayat & Zila Panchayat Act, 1961, U.P. Panchayat Law (Amendment) Act, 1994

Summary: Constitution of India-Article 243C-U. P. Kshettra Panchayat and Zila Panchayat Act, 1961 (1961 Act)-Sections 28 and 28A-Removal-Adhyaksha of Zila Panchayat-Adhyaksha belonging to reserved category can be removed on basis of vote of no confidence-Person who is elected by members of Zila Panchayat can only remain in power so long as majority support is with such person-No merit in contention that person once elected to position of Adhyaksha would be permitted to continue in office till expiry of five years terms, even though he/she no longer enjoys confidence of electorate-No merit in contention that State Legislature will have no power to make provision for no-confidence motion against Adhyaksha of Zila Panchayat-No merit in contention that petitioner being Scheduled Caste lady cannot be removed through vote of no-confidence-Provision contained in Section 28 of 1961 Act cannot be said to be repugnant to 73rd constitutional amendment-Provision contained in Section 28A of 1961 Act in no manner dilutes or nullifies protection given to candidates belonging to Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Backward Classes in 73rd constitutional amendment of the Constitution.
