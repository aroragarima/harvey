Patel Rajnikant Dhulabhai and Another v Patel Chandrakant Dhulabhai and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1168; (2008) 14 SCC 561; AIR 2008 SC 3016; 2008(5) ALL MR 409; 2008 (5) AWC 5116; 2008 CRLJ 3891; 2009 (1) G.L.R. 454; JT 2008 (8) SC 364; 2008 (7) MLJ(SC) 376; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 819; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 808; 2008(10) SCALE 349; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 1169

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Court Of Record, Ex-Parte, Breach Of Injunction

Summary: Contempt petition - Plea that in spite of the orders passed by this Court, the contemners sold the property, received consideration and executed sale-deeds - Held, punishing a person for contempt of Court is indeed a drastic step and normally such action should not be taken. At the same time, however, it is not only the power but the duty of the Court to uphold and maintain the dignity of Courts and majesty of law which may call for such extreme step - Respondents/contemners guilty under s. 12 of the Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, read with s. 94(c) and r. 2-A of O. 39 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Order accordingly.
