Â Joseph Kuruvilla Vellukunnel v Reserve Bank of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 March 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur, J.C. Shah, J.R. Madholkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 395; AIR 1962 SC 1371; [1962] 32 Comp Cas 514; 1963 (1) CompLJ 56; 1962 KerLJ 545; 1962 KLT 69; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 632; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 632

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Companies Act, 1913, Preventive Detention Act, 1950, Winding Up Of a Banking Company, Religious Endowments Act, 1863, Influx from Pakistan (Control) Act, 1949, Ajmer Government Wards Regulation, 1888, Charitable Endowments Act, Companies (Amendment) Act, Orissa Hindu Religious Endowments Act, Criminal Law Amendment Act, 1958, Indian Criminal Law Amendment Act, 1908, Punjab Special Powers (Press) Act, 1956

Summary: Banking & Finance - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(f), 19(1)(g) and 301 - Banking Companies Act, 1949, ss. 38(1) and 38(3)(b)(iii) - Banking Companies - Special law dealing with winding up - Appointing RBI as sole Judge - (A) Constitutionality / Validity - (B) Interpretation of Statutes - Aid of American concepts, laws of precedents - Held, no violation of art. 14, 19(1)(f) and (g) and 301 and ss. 38(1) and 38(3)(b)(iii) of 1949 Act were neither discriminatory nor unreasonable - Aid of American concepts, laws precedents in interpretation of our laws is not always without its dangers and have therefore to be relied upon with some caution if not without hesitation - Appeal allowed.
