Northern Coalfield Limited v Heavy Engineering Corporation Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

13 July 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 535; (2016) 8 SCC 685; AIR 2016 SC 3715; 2016 (5) ALT(SC) 49; 2016 (5) AWC 4626; 2016 (3) CLT(SC) 128; JT 2016 (7) SC 437; 2016 (4) RCR(Civil) 160; 2016(6) SCALE 820; [2016] 5 S.C.R. 69

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Arbitral Award, Contract, Arbitration Act, 1940, Arbitration And Conciliation Act, 1996, Settlement, Arbitration, Conciliation, Absence, Solicitor, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Summary: (A) Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7 r. 11(d) - Dispute - Arbitral award - Challenged - Appellant/corporation issued a tender for construction of a Coal Handling Plant - The construction work was meant to be carried out under two contracts which were awarded to respondent - Disputes have arisen between parties in relation to the two contracts - Award was passed under which respondent no.1 was held entitled to a sum of Rs.16,87,61,981.11/-, while appellant was awarded Rs.56,05,000/- - Both parties filed appeals before Appellate Authority which held that a sum of Rs.15,84,50,000/- apart from Rs.3.73 crores due as interest was recoverable from appellant - Respondent no.2 filed suit before HC against two corporations seeking to restrain respondent no.1 from settling disputes with appellant - Appellate Authority remanded matter back to Arbitrator for reconsideration - Aggrieved, appellant filed suit before HC claiming declaration that respondent no.1 committed a breach of Cl. 3 of Contracts by sub-letting contract to respondent no. 2 - Appellant further prayed for a declaration that respondent no.1 was not entitled to claim any relief under those contracts nor was respondent no. 2 - Single Judge by an order restrained implementation/execution of awards passed by Appellate Authority and held that there was no privity of contract between appellant and respondent no. 2 and that suit between two public sector undertakings could not be filed without clearance from Committee on Disputes - On appeal, DB dismissed appeal and affirmed the order of Single Judge - Hence, instant appeal - Whether requirement of clearance of High Power Committee (COD) could be insisted upon even at this stage.

Held, filing of suit in itself was not barred, what was restrained was further progress in suit till such time permission from COD was obtained. In as much as HC considered absence of permission from COD to be a mandatory legal requirement for institution of suit it committed a mistake. No such legal requirement could be read into the judgment of SC. The question whether the requirement of clearance of COD could be insisted upon even at this stage is in negative. COD stands abrogated/dissolved and orders directing constitution of such a Committee reversed. Since there is no COD at present there is no question of either obtaining or insisting upon any clearance from the same. Impugned orders passed by HC rejecting plaint on ground that same was not preceded or accompanied by permission from COD is unsustainable and was liable to be set aside. Appeal allowed.

(B) Arbitration & ADR - Civil Procedure - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7 r. 11(d) - Dispute - Arbitration - Legality - Whether SC ought to remand the matter back to Civil Court for adjudication and if that were not a desirable course of action whether adjudication of matters in dispute by way of arbitration would be a better option.

Held, a suitable order of reference to a sole arbitrator for adjudication of all outstanding disputes between two corporations especially because alternative to such arbitration is a long drawn expensive and cumbersome trial of suit filed by appellant before a Civil Court and the difficulties that beset execution of an award made under a non-statutory administrative mechanism. Both these courses are unattractive with no prospects of an early fruition even after the parties have fought each other for nearly twenty years.

The dispute regarding liability of appellant under the contract continues to loom large so long as it is not resolved finally and effectually in accordance with law. No such effective adjudication recognized by law has so far taken place. The right of appellant to demand such adjudication cannot be denied simply because it happens to be a Govt. owned company for even when appellant is a govt. company, it has its legal character as an entity separate from Govt. Just because it had resorted to permanent procedure or taken part in proceedings there can be no estoppel against its seeking redress in accordance with law. Impugned judgment of HC was set aside. Former Chief Justice of SC was appointed as Sole Arbitrator to adjudicate upon all claims. Appeal allowed.
