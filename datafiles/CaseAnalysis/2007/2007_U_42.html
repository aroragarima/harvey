Supreme Court Bar Association v Union Of India & Ors

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 374; (2007) 4 SCC 353; AIR 2007 SC 1670; 2007 (6) AWC 6116; 2007 (2) ESC 349; 2007 (3) G.L.R. 2757; JT 2007 (5) SC 438; 2007 (3) MLJ(SC) 826; 2007 (147) PLR 83; 2007(5) SCALE 630; 2007 (6) SCJ 270; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 96; 2007 (3) SLR 351

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Solicitor General

Summary: Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987, s. 6(2) - Writ petition - Appointment of a retired Judge as Chairman of State Legal Service Authority - Contention that it has the effect of stalling the effectiveness in functioning of the State Legal Service Authorities - Scope for favouritism - Also, provision appears to suggest that a retired Judge could be considered only as an alternative - Most of the functions are intrinsically interwoven with the duties and the powers of the High Court under art. 235 - Objectives underlying ss. 7 and 8 may be better achieved with a sitting Judge as Executive Chairman - Held, there is ample scope for favouritism in appointment of a retired Judge - Advantage of having a sitting Judge as the Chairman far outweigh the disadvantages - A sitting Judge should be appointed as the Chairman and only when unusual difficulties exist, a retired Judge may be appointed.
