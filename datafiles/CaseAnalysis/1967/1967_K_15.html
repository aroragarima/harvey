Hiralal Vallabhram v Kastorbhai Lalbhai and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Vishishtha Bhargava, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 121; AIR 1967 SC 1853; 1968 (9) G.L.R. 41; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 343

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 s. 115 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 111(h)- Bombay Rents Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, ss. 12, 13, 14,15(2),and 28 - Appellant, a sub tenant, and 3 others tenants in chief - Respondents had given notice to tenants in chief terminating tenancy - Appellate court ordered ejectment of appellant - No such order against tenants in chief - Whether Appellate Court had jurisdiction to eject merely appellant without determination of tenancy of tenants in chief? - A decree against a trespasser could only be passed by a regular civil court in a suit brought under CPC - Held, Appellate Court had no jurisdiction to pass a decree for ejectment against appellant u/s. 28 of Act of 1947- (B) Whether a sub-tenant of a contractual tenant can be deemed to be a tenant of landlord? - Where interest of a tenant comes to an end completely, pre-existing sub-tenant may, if conditions u/s. 14 are satisfied, be deemed to be a tenant of landlord - Further held, s. 14 of Act of 1947 would come into play in favour of sub-tenant only after tenancy of contractual tenants has been determined by notice and contractual tenant has been ordered to be ejected u/s. 28 on any of grounds u/s. 12 or 13 - Till that event happens or till he gives up tenancy himself interest of a tenant who may be a contractual tenant, for purposes of s. 14, cannot be said to have determined - Appeal allowed.
