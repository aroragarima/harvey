U.P. Cooperative Federation Limited. v M/S. Three Circles

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2050; (2009) 10 SCC 374; 2009 (4) AWC 4150; JT 2009 (12) SC 123; 2009 (4) RCR(Civil) 546; 2009(12) SCALE 443; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 310

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 20 - Arbitral award - Challenged - Appellant executed contract with respondent - Agreement included a arbitration clause - Dispute arose between parties - Respondent approached HC u/s. 20 of Act for appointment of an arbitrator - Arbitrator was appointed - Arbitrator by a reasoned award directed appellant to pay a sum to respondent along with interest at rate of 15% till date of final payment - Appellant filed an application for setting aside said award, which was dismissed - Appeal filed before DB of HC was partly allowed - Hence, present special leave petition - (A) Whether there was error apparent on face of award on issue of 'balance steel recovery'? - Held, arbitrator considered entire materials on record and rival submissions of parties and then came to conclusion of fact, which was accepted by courts below, it is not open to SC to interfere with such conclusions until and unless it is manifest that such conclusions are perverse or arbitrary - No ground for setting aside award - (B) Payment of interest - DB of HC after analyzing decisions of SC, Executive Engineer, (Irrigation) Balimela & Ors. vs. Abhaduta Jena & Ors. 1987 INDLAW SC 28416, Secretary, Irrigation Department, Government of Orissa & Ors. vs. G. C. Roy 1991 INDLAW SC 421 came to conclusion of law which was that Arbitrator has power to award pendente lite interest and where contract was silent as to awarding of interest, Arbitrator has power to award interest for pre-reference period if there is a substantive law which empowers him to do so or if there is a usage of trade for payment which has force of law - Held, HC was perfectly justified in holding that Arbitrator has power to award interest for pre-reference period - (C) Awarding of 'interest on interest' - Held, HC on this question rightly relied on a decision of SC in case of Oil and Natural Gas Commission vs. M/s. M .C. Clelland Engineers S.A. 1999 INDLAW SC 1111 - Interest, which is damages for wrongful retention of money that rightfully belonged to one, thus can be paid on costs as well - However, considering fact that there was a long lapse of time and for ends of justice, judgment of DB modified only to extent that rate of interest should be reduced from 15% to 7% - Appeal dismissed.
