Messrs Maharana Mills Private Limited v Income Tax Officer, Porbandar

Supreme Court of India

14 April 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 65; AIR 1959 SC 881; [1959] 36 ITR 350; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 547

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 10(2)(vi) and 35 - Rectification of assessment - Written down value - Res judicata - Estoppel - Appellant continued to be assessed to income- tax in assessment years 1952-53 and 1953-54 - Application dt.8-8-1955 made by appellant for rectification u/s. 35 of the Act and in application pointed out several mistakes in calculations in regard to depreciation amount but Income-tax Officer corrected Written Down Value of different properties of appellant - Aggrieved by Order appellant appealed to Tribunal which was ordered against him and on appeal order was confirmed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Income-tax Officer entitled to go behind original cost accepted by his predecessor ever since assessment year 1939-40 - Held, neither principle of res judicata nor estoppel nor terms of s. 10(2)(vi) of the Act prevented Income-tax Officer from determining actual cost of machinery - Depreciation to be calculated for every year and was open to Income-tax Officer not merely to perform a mathematical operation on basis of Written Down Value of previous year but one of determining Written Down Value himself - Appeal dismissed.
