Narinder Mohan Arya v United India Insurance Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 120; (2006) 4 SCC 713; AIR 2006 SC 1748; 2006 (5) AWC 5314; JT 2006 (4) SC 404; 2006(4) SCALE 181; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 932; 2006 (3) SLR 92; 2006 (2) UPLBEC 1526; 2006 (2) UPLBEC 2029

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: General Insurance (Conduct, Discipline & Appeal) Rules , 1975

Summary: [A.] Service - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - General Insurance (Conduct, Discipline & Appeal) Rules , 1975, r. 37 - Issuance of four insurance cover notes - Allegation of antedating one insurance cover note for Rs. 1 lakh - Removal from service - Appeal against - Held, Judgment and order of Single Judge suffers from several infirmities - He had observed that 'the disadvantages of an employer as such acts are committed in secrecy and in conspiracy with person affected by accident' - No such finding or charge arrived at in the disciplinary proceedings - Single Judge did not at all deliberate on contentions raised by appellant - Discussion on materials available on record for purpose of applying legal principles was imperative - Appellant reinstated in service with 50% of back wages - Appeal allowed.
