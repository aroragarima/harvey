Sahibdar Khan and another v Sadloo Khan (Dead) by LRs and others

Supreme Court of India

22 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Misra, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 4097; (2001) 10 SCC 464; AIR 2003 SC 2073; AIR 2002 SCW 63; 2000 (41) ALR 799; 2000 (4) AWC 3391; JT 2000 (Supp1) SC 569

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: North Western Provinces Tenancy Act, 1901, U.P. Zamindary Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950

Summary: Land & Property - U.P. Zamindary Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950 - Validity of gift deed - 'S' mother of plaintiffs was recorded as occupancy tenant in the Fasli Year 1320 over the property left by her husband - 'S' executed a gift deed in favour of the plaintiffs to the extent of her half share and retained the other half for herself - After her death plaintiffs claimed half share out of share retained by her - Subsequently, defendant no. 1, one of the son of 'S' executed a gift deed in favour of his daughters - Plaintiffs challenged said gift deed submitting that defendant No. 1 had no interest left in the land - Defendant No. 1 contended that on the death of his father 'Y', the tenancy devolved by inheritance upon defendant No. 1 and plaintiff equally - Hence deed of gift executed by 'S' was void and would have no effect in law - Trial Court decreed the suit by holding that 'Y' surrendered his occupancy right in favour of his wife and she was recorded as occupancy tenant in her own right - Appellate Court also dismissed the appeal filed by the defendant No. 1 - On appeal, HC set aside the findings of both the courts below and held that there was no evidence of any surrender or abondonment by 'Y' of his occupancy tenant right in his life time - Hence, the present appeal - (A) Whether 'S' mother of plaintiffs matured herself as absolute owner over the property left by her husband by virtue of her being recorded as occupancy tenant in the Fasli Year 1320? - Held, on the facts of the present case, entry of 'S' as occupancy tenant, could be treated to have acquired validity as it was not challenged by the parties - Accordingly, she became the owner with transferable right after coming into force of the Act, when she acquired Bhumidar right - (B) Whether defendant No. 1 had any right to gift through registered gift deed to his daughters, part of the same property which, 'S' gifted through gift deed in favour of plaintiff? - Held, gift deed executed by 'S' was valid and 'S' was competent to execute the same - Consequently the registered gift deed executed by defendant No. 1 in favour his four daughters to the extent it encroached the right of plaintiffs flowing from the gift deed gift deed executed by 'S' could not be sustained - Therefore, later gift deed by defendant No. 1 was not valid to the extent it affected the right of the plaintiffs gained through the gift deed executed by 'S' - However, to the extent of right of defendant No. 1 received through inheritance out of half share of his mother the later gift deed was valid - Appeal allowed.
