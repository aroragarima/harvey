In Re: Illegal Detention of Machal Lalung

Supreme Court of India

24 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, R.V. Raveendran, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1691; (2007) 15 SCC 18; 2008(12) SCALE 125

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Asylum, Lunacy Act, 1912

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 330(1) - Prisoners Act, 1900, ss. 39, 38, 30 - Mental Health Act, 1987, s. 40 - Undertrial prisoner - Medical check up - Implementation of provisions - News item was published stating that resident of Assam continued to languish as undertrial prisoner in psychiatric hospital for period of thirty eight years, even after he was declared fit by hospital - Resident of Assam Machal Lalung was directed to be released from psychiatric hospital - HC's Registries were required to collect and furnish particulars of undertrial prisoners lodged in mental asylums - Reports received from HC showed that there were several other cases where mentally ill persons who had allegedly committed various offences were kept as undertrial prisoners in various psychiatric hospitals/nursing homes in various States - Hence, instant Petitions - Whether undertrial prisoners was subjected to periodic medical check-up and there was implementation of ss. 38, 39 of 1900 Act - Held, undertrial prisoners was confined to psychiatric hospital for more than period of maximum sentence - It was clear that trial Courts had not considered whether undertrial prisoners could be released pending investigation or trial u/s. 330(1) of CrPC - It was also possible that no friend or relative of prisoner came forward with sufficient security and seek release under said provision - There were several cases where undertrial prisoners had been charged for offence u/s. 302 of IPC and there was no periodic medical check-up, nor periodic consideration by court - S. 40 of 1987 Act empowered medical officer in charge of psychiatric hospital/nursing home to discharge any mentally ill prisoner, on recommendation of two medical practitioners one of whom shall preferably be psychiatrist in manner provided in s. 30 of 1900 Act or in any other relevant law - From reports filed by Registries of various HC it was evident that monthly, quarterly and half-yearly inspections/examinations were not regularly being carried out as required u/ss. 38, 39 of 1900 Act and Magistrates/courts authorizing prisoners to be detained were not receiving quarterly/half-yearly reports u/ss. 39(1), 39(3), 39(4) of 1900 Act - It was unfortunate that provisions of ss. 38 , 39 of 1900 Act were not being implemented - Order accordingly.
