Motor Owners Insurance Company Limited v Jadavji Keshavji Modi and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 360; (1981) 4 SCC 660; (1982) SCC (Cr) 28; 1981 ACJ 507; AIR 1981 SC 2059; 1981 (7) ALR 620; [1982] 52 Comp Cas 454; 1982 CRLR 86; 1981 (22) GLR 1208; 1981(3) SCALE 1529B; [1982] 1 S.C.R. 860; 1982 UJ 127

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Travelling

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Expressions with more than one meaning - Expression 'any one accident' in s. 95(2) - It admits of two meanings - Held, in the context of purpose of the Act, it signifies as many accidents as number of persons injured in an accident - Limit of compensation of Rs.20,000 extends to each claimant - Appeals Dismissed.
