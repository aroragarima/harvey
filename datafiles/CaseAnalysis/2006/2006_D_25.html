Avtar Singh Hit v Delhi Sikh Gurdwara Management Committee and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, K.G. Balakrishnan, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 694; (2006) 8 SCC 487; JT 2006 (9) SC 111; 2006(10) SCALE 271; 2006 (8) SCJ 162; [2006] Supp7 S.C.R. 316

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Delhi Sikh Gurdwaras Act, 1971, Tamil Nadu Panchayats Act, 1958, Indian Advocates Act

Summary: [A.] Practice & Procedure - Election - Delhi Sikh Gurdwaras Act, 1971, ss. 16, 16(5), 2(c), 3, 31, 33, 39(2), 4, 5 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 136, 226, 329(b) - Delhi Municipal Corporation Act, 1957, ss. 15, 16, 17, 18, 19, 20, 22, 23, 24, 25, 27, 28, 29, 30 - Election of members of Executive Board of Delhi Sikh Gurdwara Management Committee - Validity of - Held, dispute raised was purely factual in nature as to whether some confusion had been created regarding date fixed for holding of meeting of Committee for electing office bearers of Executive Board - WPs ought not to have been entertained for resolving dispute relating to election and on this count alone WPs were liable to be dismissed - Appeal dismissed.
