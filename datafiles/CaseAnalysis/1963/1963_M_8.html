M/S. Kesoram Cotton Mills Ltd v Gangadhar And Others

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 49; AIR 1964 SC 708; 1963 (7) FLR 213; 1963 (2) LLJ 371; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 809

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 33, 33(2)(b) and 33(1)(b) - Whether case for reinstatement or compensation arose? - Evidence on other charges was considered and found unworthy of acceptance - Whether tribunal should have allowed full wages to workmen? - Workmen entitled to full wages till tribunals award permitting dismissal becomes enforceable - Termination - Held, only because of defect in enquiry, dismissal was held unjustified - Tribunal permit termination of service of four workmen under one of standing orders and finally ordered payment of wages for a period of one month along with compensation - Appeal by management dismissed - Appeal by workmen partly allowed.
