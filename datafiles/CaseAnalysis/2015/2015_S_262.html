Sher Singh @ Partapa v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

09 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 15; (2015) 3 SCC 724; AIR 2015 SC 980; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 2817; 2015 (1) CalLT(SC) 70; 2015 CRLJ 1118; JT 2015 (1) SC 441; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 790; 2015(1) SCALE 250; [2015] 1 S.C.R. 29; 2015 (1) WLN(SC) 131

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code,1860, ss.304B,498A - Dowry death - Conviction - One person informed Complainant that deceased had committed suicide by consuming some poisonous substance at her matrimonial house - Complainant, along with brothers of deceased and other members of village, rushed to matrimonial house of deceased and after confirming her death, lodged FIR - Four accused persons were tried by Sessions Judge u/ss.304B,498A of IPC - Sessions Judge had convicted all accused persons u/ss.304B,498A of IPC - Two separate appeals were filed by accused persons before HC in which accused nos.2,3 were acquitted however, appeal filed by accused no.1-appellant was dismissed - Hence, instant Appeal.

Held, in normal course dowry demands are articulated when marriage is agreed upon and is certainly reiterated at time when it is performed and such demands continue into couple of years of matrimony. In normal course, if woman is being tortured and harassed, she would not remain reticent of this state of affairs and would certainly repeatedly inform her family. This is specially so before she takes extreme step of taking her own life. Added to this are inconsistencies and contradictions between statements of PWs 4,7 with regard to panchayat and presence of and knowledge of maternal uncle of deceased. Therefore, prosecution has not shown/presented and or proved even by preponderance of probabilities that deceased had been treated with cruelty emanating from or founded on dowry demands. It is in realm of possibility that ingestion of aluminium phosphate may have been accidental. In his examination, accused has proffered details of his defence. This is not case where he has merely denied all questions put by Court to him. Because of insufficiency or satisfactory nature of facts or circumstances shown by prosecution, burden of proving his innocence has not shifted to appellant. Thus, judgment of convicting and punishing appellant is set aside. Appeal allowed.
