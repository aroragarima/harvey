Rustees Of The Port Of Madras v Engineering Constructions Corporation Limited

Supreme Court of India

14 August 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1140; (1995) 5 SCC 531; AIR 1995 SC 2423; AIR 1995 SCW 3584; 1995 (2) ARBLR 331; 1995 (3) AWC 1740; 1995 (3) CCC 352; 1996 (2) CLT 202; JT 1995 (6) SC 48; 1995 (2) MLJ(SC) 110; 1995(4) SCALE 742; 1995 (3) SCJ 571; [1995] Supp2 S.C.R. 672

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 30 - Setting aside of award - Court cannot re-appraise evidence/material before umpire and come to different finding of fact - Legality of order setting aside award - When neither award not vitiated by error of law apparent on face of it nor umpire misconducted - Held, must appear from award itself or from any document or note incorporated in it or appended to it and it is not permissible to travel beyond - Illegal - Appeal allowed.
