Â State of Maharashtra v Ministerial Service Association

Supreme Court of India

08 October 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 248; AIR 1966 SC 625; 1966 (2) LLJ 132; 1966 MahLJ 366; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 134

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: PARLIAMENT & LEGISLATURE, Allocated Government Servants (Association, etc) Rules of Bombay 1957

Summary: (A) Service - States Reorganization Act, 1956, u/ss. 115, 116 - Allocated Government Servants (Absorption, Seniority, Pay & Allowances) Rules 1957, rr. 10, 12 - Equivalent post - Discrimination - Challenged - Appellants/respondents/petitioners/First Grade clerks filed petition before HC for writ of mandamus against Govt. for equation of their posts with Aval Karkuns under appellant/respondent/State of Bombay and also asked Govt. Resolution No. SR/INT/ 1057/VI dt. 21-10-1957, together with Item No. 8 and Note 5 of Statement accompanying that resolution which says posts of respondents in Deputy Commissioner's offices were ordered to continue on existing scale of pay of Rs. 80-5-130 - Alternatively, respondents asked that Govt. Resolution No. SR/ INT/2159/21365-F dt. 12-10-1960, should be quashed inasmuch as it fixed new scales of pay (Rs. 100-8-140) for posts held by respondents from 1-5-1960 and for mandamus commanding Govt. to fixed scale from 1-11-1956, on which date States reorganization under above Act took effect - HC, rejected contention of respondents as to equivalence but accepted their contention that new scales of pay might to commence on 1-11-1956 and not 1-5-1960 as ordered by Govt. and appropriate writs to effectuate latter part of order of HC issued - Hence, instant appeals - Whether respondents raised question of discrimination was sustainable - Held, respondents contend that Govt. was bound to found equivalent post for them and they submit that nearest equivalent post was that Aval Karkuns and also contend that by not assigning them to equivalent post they had discriminated against and that r. 12, which enables that post need not be equated to equivalent post was discriminatory - In SC judgment neither submission was correct - There was no question of discrimination because it was always possible that special post might not fit into kinds of posts there were in principal successor State - Such post would be required to be treated by itself and regard being had to scales of pay obtaining generally in principal successor State, old scales of pay would either be retained or modified for such post - In case of respondents no equivalent post was found as duties of Aval Karkuns in appellant was entirely different from those which respondents performed - Therefore, it was not possible to made post of Aval Karkuns as equivalent post to that of respondents - SC, do not think that State Govt. was wrong in declining to equate posts nor do SC think that there was discrimination in doings or r. 12 under which it was done was discriminatory - Rule which provides for special treatment of odd case was not necessarily discriminatory - Discrimination could be proved only if equivalence was not carried out although equivalent post was available - R. 12 was made in view of multifariousness of posts existing in different components from which principal successor State was formed because it was obvious that some existing posts could not simply be equated with posts in principal successor State - They had to be treated on independent footing and that was what had done - There was also nothing in ss. 115 and 116 of the 1956, Act which compels equivalence in every case - Appeals dismissed.

(B) Service - States Reorganization Act, 1956, u/ss. 115, 116 - Allocated Government Servants (Absorption, Seniority, Pay & Allowances) Rules 1957, rr. 10, 12 - Revised scales of pay - Legality - Whether revised scales of pay should begin from 1-5-1960 or 1-11-1956 - Held, instant appeal might, in SC opinion, also failed - Govt. seems to have acted u/rr. 10, 12, which SC quoted earlier, and had fixed special pay for respondents - Scale of pay which they enjoyed immediately before 1-11-1956 was Rs. 80-5130 - By order made on 12-10-1960 (Resolution No. SR/INT/2159/ 21365-F) pay-scale of post was raised to Rs. 100-8-140 but order was to take effect from 1-5-1960 - Power which was conferred on Govt. to prescribe new pay-scale might be exercised from 1-11-1956 - Those powers intended to enable Govt. to made change in scale of pay but not to change date 1-11-1956, was always fixed date line - Non-obstante clause, with which r. 12 opens, could not be construed to that effect - It was obviously intended to enable Govt. to consider special cases which does not fall within r. 10, but which nevertheless might be provided for on and from 1-11-1956 - In that view of matter order of HC cancelled date 1-5-1960, as starting point of new scales of pay and fixed 1-11-1956, as date of start might be upheld - Appeals dismissed.
