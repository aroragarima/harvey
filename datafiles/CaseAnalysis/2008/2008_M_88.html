M/S Anis Ahmad And Sons v Commissioner of Income Tax (Appeals), Kanpur and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Lokeshwar Singh Panta, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 82; (2008) 2 SCC 362; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1350; [2008] 297 ITR 441; JT 2008 (1) SC 621; 2008(1) SCALE 638; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 1021; [2008] 167 TAXMAN 84

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Appeal allowed, Opportunity Of Being Heard, Income Tax Act, 1961, Original Order, Penalty Notice

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1961 - Order treating appellant-assessee as 'a Trader' and not as 'a Commission Agent' and assessed its total income Rs.4, 06, 810/- for payment of income tax - Held, record reveals that for the year 1983-84, the Assessing Authority had accepted the claim of the appellant- assessee dealing in the business of hides and skins as 'a Commission Agent' - Appellant 'assessee filed a chart of payments made to the purchasers by the traders through the appellant 'assessee acting as a Commission Agent - Five traders, who appeared before Assessing Authority, have supported the claim of appellant-assessee to be 'a Commission Agent' and not 'a Trader' and the Assessing Authority has accepted their evidence - Order holding appellant 'assessee as 'Trader' and not 'Commission Agent' is quashed - Appeal allowed.
