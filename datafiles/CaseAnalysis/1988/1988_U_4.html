U. P. Hotels and Others v U. P. State Electricity Board

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 261; (1989) 1 SCC 359; AIR 1989 SC 268; 1989 ALJ 33; 1989 (1) ARBLR 244; 1988 (3) CompLJ 334; JT 1988 (4) SC 478; 1988(2) SCALE 1235; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 670; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 670

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Electricity & Energy - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 33 - Indian Electricity Act, 1910 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, ss. 49, 59 and 60 - Whether respondents were justified in raising rates of electricity unilaterally and in contravention of terms of supply agreement? - Held, there was only an agreement by which Electricity Board accepted agreement which was held by Umpire to have become operative - Once that agreement was binding on board, its terms could not be varied from uniform rate under sub-section (1) and (2) of s. 49 - Umpire was right - Umpire committed no error in arriving at such conclusion - Appeal allowed.
