Chuhar Singh v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

21 July 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Bhagwati, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 545; (1976) SCC (Cr) 215; (1976) 1 SCC 879; AIR 1977 SC 386; 1975 AllCriC 282; 1975 BBCJ 730; 1977 CRLJ 243; 1975 CRLR 463; 1975 UJ 576

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution, art. 136 - Appeal by special leave from conviction u/s. 304 Part I, IPC, 1860 -Interference with findings of fact recorded by Lower Courts - Permissibility - Conviction based on evidence of only eye-witness which on scrutiny by the Supreme Court was found to suffer from serious infirmities - Held, conclusion of the lower courts that he was an eye-witness to the occurrence could not be accepted as correct and therefore, the conviction could not be sustained - Appeal Allowed.
