Feroz Din And Others v The State Of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

25 November 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 119; AIR 1960 SC 363; 1960 CRLJ 527; 1960 (1) LLJ 244; 1960 SCJ 248; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 319

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 24(3) ,27 - Public utility service - Discharge of employees - Strikes - Lock-out - Illegal - Factory was declared by Govt. to be a public utility service - Respondent's strikes were illegal and appellants had instigated them - Appellant challenged that strikes were not illegal - Appellants rely on s.24(3) of 1947 Act under which a strike declared in consequence of an illegal lock-out shall not be deemed to be illegal -Three hundred workers of Hot Mill were discharged and had not been locked-out - It was clear that if there was such lock-out, it was illegal - Appellants were convicted for an offence u/s. 27 of 1947 Act for having instigated and incited others to take art in an illegal strike - Hence, instant appeal - Whether discharge of employees by an employer amounts to lock-out - Held, lock-out included discharge there would be conflict between ss. 22,23 - ss. 22 and 23 prohibit a lock-out of workers during the pendency of conciliation proceedings - Refusal to continue to employ include discharge - Rules of interpretation do not prevent us from giving to words used in definition meaning refusal by employer to allow any number of persons employed by him to attend to their duties without effecting termination of service - Appeal dismissed.
