Dhagamwar Narsingh v S. S. Grewal And Another

Supreme Court of India

09 October 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 325; AIR 1962 SC 422; 1966 (13) FLR 261; 1962 (1) LLJ 627; 1962 (2) SCJ 187; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 32; [1962] Supp1 S.C.R. 32

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Mines & Minerals

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Mines Rules 1955, rr. 72(1), 72(4), 74(2) - Mines Act 1952 - Termination - Challenged - Appellant was appointed as Chief Labour officer on 25-9-1947 by respondent no. 2 and was terminated from services by notice - On such discharge appellant claiming to be a Welfare Officer of mine within r.74(2) of 1955 and filed an appeal before respondent no.1 Chief Inspector of Mines in India, questioning validity of such discharge by respondent no.2 - Respondent no. 1 held that appellant was not a Welfare Officer within that rule and refused to entertain his appeal - Aggrieved appellant filed writ petition before HC which was dismissed - Hence instant appeal- Whether appellant was a Welfare Officer within the rules of 1955 - Held, as Rules of 1955 were framed under The Act of 1952, and came into force on 2-7-1956 and appellant was appointed prior to passing of Rules 1955 - We propose to deal with instant appeal on basis of contentions raised by respondent no.1 that a Welfare Officer u/r. 74(2) is one who is appointed after Rules of 1955 came into force with requisite qualification to discharge duties of welfare officer and pointed out that proviso u/r. 74(2) clearly contemplated that continuance of service of such a person as a Welfare officer with relaxation where such was necessary and was granted and stated that r. 72(4) was inapplicable to appellant as he had been appointed long ago and proviso to r. 72(2) indicated that its application was not intended and even appellant also accepted that the Welfare officer contemplated is one appointed in respect of one mine, now appellant was on his own case, Welfare Officer of several mines of respondent no.2 and not of one of such mines only - Therefore, we think that he was not a Welfare officer within r. 72(1) and hence not within the proviso to r. 74(2) - Hence that appellant might be considered as having been severally and independently appointed as Welfare officer of each of the Company's several collieries in his charge, we think that would be an impossible view to take - Appeal dismissed.
