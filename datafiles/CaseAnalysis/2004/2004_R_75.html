Kalyan Chandra Sarkar v Rajesh Ranjan Alias Pappu Yadav & Anr

Supreme Court of India

12 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1041; (2004) 7 SCC 528; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1977; AIR 2004 SC 1866; 2004 (2) CGLJ 299; 2004 CRLJ 1796; JT 2004 (3) SC 442; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 254; 2004(3) SCALE 257

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Mla, Fire-Arms, Successive Applications

Summary: Criminal Procedure Code - Bail - Grant of bail in non-bailable offences - Several bail applications - Finally allowed bail - Appeal against granting bail - Whether High Court was justified in not taking into consideration facts while deciding to grant bail in a case where this Court has earlier come to conclusion that grant of bail on the ground of period of incarceration by itself was not proper? - Held, no fresh ground recorded by High Court while granting bail - Approach of High Court is violative of principle of binding nature of judgments of superior court rendered in a lis between same parties, and in effect tends to ignore and thereby render ineffective principles enunciated therein which have a binding character - Bail-bonds cancelled.
