Balbir Kaur v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 867; (2009) 15 SCC 795; AIR 2009 SC 3036; 2009 CRLJ 4139; 2009 (3) JCC (Narcotics) 143; 2009 (3) JhrCR(SC) 146; JT 2009 (9) SC 628; 2009 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1857; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 580; 2009(9) SCALE 424; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 298

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, s. 52 - Possession of poppy husk - Conviction - Legality - Trial Court convicted appellant and First Appellate Court upheld the same - PW6, IO, categorically stated the fact of seizure which was unshaken - Recovery of poppy husk from the bags under the custody of appellant, hence, no violation of s. 52 - Appellant admitted the possession of article, hence, it was a conscious possession - No case of bias made out against the IO - Non-disclosure of the purpose of search and grounds of arrest, is merely technical nature, not affect the material case of prosecution - Delay of sending samples for chemical analysis of having no consequence, especially, when recovery of possession stands proved - Sole independent witness was won over by appellant who appeared as defence witness - Failure to quote any other independent witness cannot vitiate search and recovery - No merit - Appeal dismissed.
