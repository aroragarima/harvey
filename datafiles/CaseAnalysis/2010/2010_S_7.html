State of Haryana and others v S. L. Arora and Company

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 416; (2010) 3 SCC 690; AIR 2010 SC 1511; 2010 (3) AWC 2615; 2010 (3) CGLJ 348; 2010 (1) CLT(SC) 370; JT 2010 (2) SC 534; 2010 (3) MLJ(SC) 760; 2010(3) M.P.H.T. 113; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 223; 2010(2) SCALE 541; [2010] 2 S.C.R. 297

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 34 and 31(7) - Interest awarded for the pre-award period and post-award period - Appellants awarded a construction contract to the respondent - Delay in completion of work led to claims by the contractor and counter-claims by the appellants - Disputes were referred to Arbitrator who made an award - Application filed by the appellants to set aside the said the award was rejected - Thereafter, respondent levied execution against the appellants to recover the amount - Respondent made an application for modification of the amount claimed, contending that inadvertently, a lesser amount had been claimed in the execution petition - Executing Court accepted the revised calculation made by the respondent - Revision petition filed by the appellants against the said order was dismissed by HC - Hence, present appeal - Whether s. 31(7) of the Act authorizes and enables arbitral tribunals to award interest on interest from the date of award? - Held, interest unless otherwise specified, refers to simple interest, that is interest paid on only the principal and not on any accrued interest - S. 31(7) of the Act merely authorizes the arbitral tribunal to award interest in accordance with the contract and in the absence of any prohibition in the contract and in the absence of specific provision relating to interest in the contract, to award simple interest at such rates as it deems fit from the date on which the cause of action arose till the date of payment - It also provides that if the award is silent about interest from the date of award till date of payment, the person in whose favour the award is made will be entitled to interest at 18% per annum on the principal amount awarded, from the date of award till date of payment - In the present case, operative portion of the award states that future interest is awarded on the "sums due", it refers to the "total amount of the award", that is total of the amounts awarded on substantive claims excluding the claim relating interest - Therefore, what was awarded by the Arbitrator was future interest at the rate of 18% per annum on the amounts awarded on various claims and not upon the interest awarded thereon upto to date of the award - Hence, respondent was entitled only to simple interest on the principal amount - Appeal allowed.
