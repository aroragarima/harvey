English Electric Company of India Limited and Another v The Deputy Commercial Tax Officer and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 September 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 602; (1976) 4 SCC 460; AIR 1977 SC 19; 1976 (5) CTR 454; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 631; 1976 (38) STC 475; 1976 TAXLR 2157; 1976 UJ 850

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Contract of sale - Inter-State sale - Challenged - Appellant (company) had its registered office at Calcutta and branches were at Bombay, Delhi, Madras and Lucknow and main factory was at Madras - Bombay branch received Bombay buyer's order and send the same to Madras branch factory and when Bombay buyer asked for quotation of prices Bombay branch wrote to Madras branch and gave all specifications and stated that 'goods were for Bombay buyer - Madras branch in reply referred to order of Bombay buyer and gave particulars mentioning that price was F.O.R. Madras - Bombay branch thereafter wrote to Bombay buyer reproducing all particulars, conditions of sale and mode of dispatch as stated by Madras branch and further stated that goods -would be manufactured at Madras branch factory - Bombay branch wrote to Bombay buyer that some of goods against order were ready for dispatch and asked Bombay buyer to give details of dispatch and insurance instructions per return of post - Bombay branch thereafter wrote to Madras branch factory and stated that 'please dispatch equipment covered by our above' indent by goods train to Bhandup Railway Station freight to pay - Railway Receipt and other documents to be send to us for disposal - Madras branch factory dispatched goods from Madras to Bombay by goods train and gave intimation to Bombay 'branch and goods were delivered to Bombay buyer at Bhandup through clearing agents and claimed goods sold was intra-state - Hence, instant appeals and petitions - Whether contract for sale of goods was an inter-State sale or an intra-State sale - Held, When branch of company forward buyer's ,order to principal factory of company and instructs them to dispatch goods direct to buyer and goods are send to buyer under those instructions it would not be sale between factory and its branch - If there was conceivable link between movement of goods and buyer's contract, and if in course of inter-State movement goods moved only to reach buyer in satisfaction of his contract of purchase and such nexus was otherwise inexplicable, then sale or purchase of specific or ascertained goods ought to be deemed to had been taken place in course of inter-State trade or commerce as such sale or purchase occasioned movement of goods from one State to another - Presence of an intermediary such as seller's own representative or branch office, who initiated contract may not make matter different - Such an interception by known person on behalf of seller in delivery State and such person's activities prior to or after implementation of contract may not alter position - Therefore, steps taken from beginning to end by Bombay branch in co-ordination with Madras factory show that Bombay branch was merely acting as intermediary between Madras factory and buyer and that it was Madras factory which pursuant to covenant in contract of sale caused movement of goods from Madras to Bombay - Hence, inter-State movement of goods was result of contract of sale and fact that contract emanated from correspondence which passed between Bombay branch and company could not made any difference - Thus, for foregoing reasons appeals were dismissed and writ petitions were also dismissed - Appeals dismissed.
