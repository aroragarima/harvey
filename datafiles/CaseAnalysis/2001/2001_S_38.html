Maruti Wire Industries Private Limited v S. T. O., Ist Circle, Mattancherry and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, S.P. Bharucha, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20218; (2001) 3 SCC 735; AIR 2001 SC 1413; JT 2001 (4) SC 212; 2001 (2) KLT 100; 2001(3) SCALE 11; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 829; 2001 (4) SLT 18; 2001 (122) STC 410; 2001 (3) Supreme 59

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1963, s. 23(3) - Whether appellant liable to pay any penal interest on assessed tax u/s. 23(3) of Act from date when return was due though neither a return was furnished nor any tax paid on self-assessment basis? - Provision by which authority is empowered to levy and collect interest, even if construed as forming part of machinery provisions, is a substantive law, not adjectival law, and interest cannot be recovered by way of damages for wrongful detention of amount - 'Tax payable' or 'tax due' is that amount which becomes due ex hypothesis on turnover and taxable turnover shown in or based on return or as to which an order of assessment has been made - Held, a failure to file return of taxable turnover may render assessee liable for any other consequences or penal action as provided by law but cannot attract liability for payment of penal interest u/s. 23(3) of Act - Appeal allowed.
