Union of India v Mohindra Supply Company

Supreme Court of India

05 September 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.C. Das Gupta, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 339; AIR 1962 SC 256; 1962 ALJ 1; 1962 (2) AnWR 63; 1962 (2) MLJ 63; 1962 (2) MLJ(SC) 63; 1962 (2) SCJ 179; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 497

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Government of India Act, misa, Succession Act 1925

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 39 - Bombay Civil Courts Act, 1869 - Government of India Act, 1915 - Indian Succession Act, 1925 - Succession Act, 1925 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 133 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 104 (1) - Whether appeal u/cl. 10 of letters patent of High Court against order in arbitration proceedings is maintainable? - In so far as letters patent deal with appeals against orders passed in arbitration proceedings, they must be read subject to provisions of s. 39(1) and (2) of Arbitration Act - Held, right of appeal can be exercised against orders in arbitration proceedings only u/s. 39 and no appeal except an appeal to this court will lie from an appellate order - Appeal allowed.
