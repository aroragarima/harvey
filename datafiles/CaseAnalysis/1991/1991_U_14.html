Union Of India And Another Etc. Etc. v Zora Singh Etc. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

22 November 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

M.H. Kania, Kuldip Singh, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 383; (1992) 1 SCC 673; 1992 (1) CCC 177; JT 1991 (4) SC 538; 1992 (2) MLJ(SC) 42; 1991(2) SCALE 1128; [1991] Supp2 S.C.R. 478; 1992 (1) UJ 229

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition (Amendment) Act, 1984 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 3(d), 4, 6, 11, 11-A, 18(1), 23(1-A), 23(2) and 30(1)(a) - Notifications for acquisition published on 1979 and 1981, respectively - Special Land Collector made and declared award of compensation in respect of acquisition on March, 1981 - Aggrieved by award, landowners filed reference applications, decided by District Judge in 1985 and 1986 - Benefits u/s. 23(1-A) of Land Acquisition Act, 1894 granted to land owners - State appealed to High Court - HC confirmed grant of benefits u/s. 23(1-A) - Letters patent appeals filed by State not allowed - Whether benefit of s. 23(1-A) of Act was available in cases where Collector made his award after April 30, 1982 being date of introduction of Land Acquisition (Amendment) Bill, 1982 in House of People not before? - Held, yes - Provisions of s. 23(1-A) makes it clear that sub-s. (1-A) deals with substantive rights - Similarly, s. 23(2) also confers a substantive right - Under rules of interpretation, provisions of the Act, being substantive in nature, can have only prospective but language in which provisions are couched, read in context, shows that intention of legislature was to give retrospective effect to them - Language of s. 23(1-A) shows that a duty is cast on court to award an amount calculated as stated therein in addition to market value of land acquired for period commencing from date of publication of s. 4 Notification to date of award of Collector or date of taking possession, whichever is earlier - On a correct interpretation of provisions of s. 23(1-A) r/w. s.30 (1)(a) of Amendment Act of 1984, an additional amount calculated in manner indicated in s. 23(1-A) is also payable in those cases where Collector had not made his award on or before April, 1982, even in cases where court might have made its award before September, 1984 - All that is material is the award by Collector or by Court should have been made between April, 1982, and September, 1984 - Relied on K. Kamalajammanniavaru v. Special land Acquisition Officer (1985 Indlaw SC 182) - The Reference Court has made its award after September 24, 1984 the benefit of the provisions of s. 23(1-A) was clearly available to the claimant as held in the impugned judgment -

Special Leave Petition (Civil) No. 14297 of 1990 dismissed.
