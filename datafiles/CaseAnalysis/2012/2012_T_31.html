Suresh Sakharam Nangare v The State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

21 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 304; (2012) 9 SCC 249; 2012 (4) JCC 2745; JT 2012 (9) SC 116; 2012 (4) MLJ(Crl) 398; 2012(9) SCALE 245; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 1186

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Approver

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302/34, 201 - Murder - Common intention - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused No. 3) allegedly committed murder of deceased - Trial Court convicted appellant and other accused u/ss. 302/34 and 201 of IPC and sentenced them to undergo rigorous imprisonment for life - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before HC - DB by impugned judgment dismissed appeal and confirmed the conviction and sentence passed by Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that even the sole testimony of PW7 (accused No. 2), the approver, it could not be said that appellant shared common intention and that post mortem report (Exh.21) indicated that victim did not die due to assault but the cause of death was due to 100% burns - Whether impugned order of DB was justified - Held, evidence of PW1 (complainant) was related to cause of death by suicide was negative by evidence of Doctor (PW10) and deposition of PW1 shows that he has not implicated appellant in the crime - In entire evidence of PW3 (wife of accused No. 1), she has not implicated appellant - Even if it was accept that evidence of PW7, who turned approver, the role allotted to appellant was that of only holding the legs of deceased as directed by accused No. 1 - Merely because the approver (PW-7) has stated that based on direction of accused No. 1, appellant caught hold of the legs of deceased, in absence of any motive or intention, mere act of holding legs of deceased that too at the end of the event when accused No. 1 throttled deceased's neck by sitting on his abdomen, appellant could not be mulcted with the offence of murder with the aid of s. 34 of IPC, particularly, when medical evidence for cause of death was due to 100% burns - There was no material from the side of prosecution to show that appellant had any common intention to eliminate deceased, who was physically disabled - Only adverse thing against appellant was that he used to associate with accused No. 1 for smoking 'Ganja' - In the absence of common intention, convicting appellant with the aid of s. 34 of IPC could not be sustained - It was clear from evidence of Doctor that cause of death of deceased was due to 100% burn injuries and not because of assault - From categorical evidence of Doctor, it was clear that appellant was nothing to do with cause of death of deceased - It was accused No. 1, who took deceased to other room where he burnt to death, which important aspect was not considered by Trial Court and HC - Prosecution failed to establish the guilt of appellant - Impugned order of Trial Court and DB was set aside - Appeal allowed.
