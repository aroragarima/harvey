Attorney General of India v Lachma Devi and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Madon, G.L. Oza, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 23; (1989) Supp1 SCC 264; (1989) SCC (Cr) 413; AIR 1986 SC 467; 1986 CRLJ 364; 1989 CRLR 763; 1985(2) SCALE 1266

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Practice & Prodecure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 354(5) - Execution of death sentence by public hanging is in question - Whether hanging of accused is violative art. 21 of Constitution of India, 1950? - Held, execution of death sentence by public hanging would be a barbaric practice clearly violative of art.21 of Constitution - Jail Manual of no State in country makes provision for execution of death sentence by public hanging which is a revolting spectacle harking back to earlier centuries - Petition disposed off.
