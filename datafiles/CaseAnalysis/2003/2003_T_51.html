Chief Conservator of Forests, Govt. of A.P. v The Collector of others

Supreme Court of India

18 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 251; (2003) 3 SCC 472; AIR 2003 SC 1805; 2003 (4) ALD(SC) 27; 2003 (51) ALR 124; 2003 (2) JhrCR(SC) 175; JT 2003 (5) SC 210; 2003 (2) MLJ(SC) 57; 2003(2) SCALE 429; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 180; 2003 (2) SLT 461; 2003 (2) Supreme 349; 2003 (2) UC 901

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh (Abolition of Jagirs) Regulations, 1358, Andhra Pradesh (Telengana Area) Forest Act, 1355, Andhra Pradesh (Telengana Area) Land Revenue Act, 1317

Summary: Civil Procedure - Constitution, art. 300, 131 - C.P.C., s. 79, r. 9 of O. I, r. 10 of O. I - Whether the Chief Conservator of Forest as the petitioner/appellant in the writ petition/appeal is a mere misdescription for the State of Andhra Pradesh or whether it is a case of non-joinder of the State of A P , a necessary party? - Held, it was not only inappropriate but also illegal for the Chief Conservator of Forest, though he might have done so in all good faith, to have questioned the order of the Commissioner of Survey, Settlement and Land Record before the HC of Andhra Pradesh - Chief Conservator of Forests as the petitioner can neither be treated as the State of A P nor can it be a case of misdescription of the State of A P - Further held, filing of a writ petition by one department against the other by invoking the extraordinary jurisdiction of the HC is not only against the propriety and polity as it smacks of indiscipline but is also contrary to the basic concept of law which requires that for suing or being sued, there must be either a natural or a juristic person - States/Union of India must evolve a mechanism to set at rest all inter-departmental controversies at the level of theGovt.and such matters should not be carried to a court of law for resolution of the controversy - Appeals Dismissed.
