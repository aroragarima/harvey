State Of Bank Bikaner & Jaipur v National Iron & Steel Rolling Corporation And Others

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, S.C. Agrawal, Faizan Uddin

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1983; (1995) 2 SCC 19; AIR 1995 SCW 214; [1995] 82 Comp Cas 551; [1995] 212 ITR 428; JT 1995 (2) SC 14; 1994(5) SCALE 249; 1995 (96) STC 612; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 566; 1995 (1) UJ 235

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1954 - Appellant-bank filed suit for recovery of sums along with interest and for realisation of mortgage property - Property has been sold by auction - It was contended by Commercial Taxes Officer that sales tax dues of first respondent were liable to be paid first out of sale proceeds - Whether provisions of s. 11 AAAA of Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1954, as amended, entitle Commercial Taxes Officer to a first charge as against an existing mortgage? - Held, where a mortgage is created in respect of any property, undoubtedly, an interest in property is carved out in favour of mortgagee, but title to property remains with mortgagor - When a statutory first charge is created on property of dealer, property subjected to first charge is entire property of dealer - Interest of mortgagee is not excluded from first charge - First charge, therefore, which is created u/s. 11 AAAA will operate on property as a whole and not only on equity of redemption - Appeal dismissed.
