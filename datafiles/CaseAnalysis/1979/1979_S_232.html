Panalal Damodar Rathi v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

24 January 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

P.S. Kailasam, P.N. Shingal, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 505; (1979) 4 SCC 526; (1980) SCC (Cr) 121; AIR 1979 SC 1191; 1979 CRLJ 936; 1979 CRLR 574; 1979 UJ 627

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Criminal - Bombay Prohibition Act - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 5(1)(d), 161 and 165A - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5(2) - Charge against appellant was that while working as a Police Prosecutor, he demanded illegal gratification from complainant as a motive for not bringing to notice of court previous conviction of complainant - Appellant was convicted - Appeal - Whether evidence of complainant should be corroborated in material particulars? - After introduction of s. 165-A IPC, 1860 making person who offers bribe guilty of abetment of bribery, complainant cannot be placed on any better footing than that of an accomplice and corroboration in material particulars connecting accused with crime has to be insisted upon - Case of prosecution depends upon acceptance of evidence relating to conversation between complainant and appellant during which appellant demanded money have to see whether this part of evidence of complainant has been corroborated - Omission by PW 3 to refer to any mention of money by appellant would show that there is no corroboration of testimony of complainant regarding demand for money by appellant - Held, in circumstances, unsafe to base a conviction on sole testimony of panch witness - Evidence of complainant is not corroborated on these material particulars - Further held, constrained to give benefit of doubt - Appeal allowed.
