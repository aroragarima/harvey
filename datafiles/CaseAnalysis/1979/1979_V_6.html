State of Tamil Nadu v V. Krishnaswami Naidu and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

P.S. Kailasam, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 557; (1979) 4 SCC 5; (1979) SCC (Cr) 887; AIR 1979 SC 1255; 1979 CRLJ 1069; 1980 (1) MLJ 16; 1980 (1) MLJ(SC) 16; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 928

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Court Martial

Summary: Criminal Law (Amendment) Act, 1952, ss. 6, 7 and 8 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 155, 157, 167, 173, 193, 262, 265 and 350 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 161, 162, 163, 164, 165 and 165A - Prevention of Corruption Act, s. 5 - General clauses Act, s. 32 - Whether Special Judge under Criminal Law (Amendment) Act, 1952 can exercise power conferred on a Magistrate u/s. 167 of CrPC to authorise detention of accused in custody of police? - Provisions of s. 8 of Criminal Law (Amendment) Act empowers special judge to take cognizance even without committal - Held, Special judge, a magistrate and empowered to try a case u/s. 167 CrPC - Appeal allowed.
