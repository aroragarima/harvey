Â State of Mysore v K. Manche Gowda

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 319; AIR 1964 SC 506; [1964] 4 S.C.R. 540

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(2) - Government of India Act, 1935, s. 240(3) - Whether Govt. is entitled to take into consideration previous record of a Govt. servant in awarding punishment to him and it is not incumbent on it to bring to notice of Govt. servant said fact in second notice? - Doctrine of 'presumptive knowledge' or that of 'purposeless enquiry', as their acceptance will be subversive of principle of 'reasonable opportunity' - It is incumbent upon authority to give Govt. servant at second stage reasonable opportunity to show cause against proposed punishment and if proposed punishment is also based on his previous punishments or his previous bad record, this should be included in second notice so that he may be able to give an explanation - Govt. servant shall be given a reasonable opportunity to know that fact and meet same - Held, show cause notice did not give only reason which influenced Govt. to dismiss respondent from service, notice given clearly contravened provisions of art. 311(2) of Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
