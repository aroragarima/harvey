Dabur India Limited v K.R. Industries

Supreme Court of India

16 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 980; (2008) 10 SCC 595; AIR 2008 SC 3123; 2008 (4) AWC 3515; 2008 (5) MLJ(SC) 1394; 2008 (37) PTC 332; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 523; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 316; 2008(8) SCALE 385; [2008] 9 S.C.R. 652

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Intellectual Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Registered User, Infringement Of Copyright, Confusion And Deception, Trade Marks Act, 1958, Supplemental Proceeding

Summary: Copyright Act, 1957; Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7 r. 11 - Suit alleging infringement of copyright - Territorial jurisdiction - High Court dismissed the suit holding that it had no jurisdiction - Appeal against - Held, a composite suit would not entitle a court to entertain a suit in respect whereof it has no jurisdiction, territorial or otherwise - A composite suit within the provisions of the 1957 Act would mean the suit which is founded on infringement of a copy right and wherein the incidental power of the Court is required to be invoked - Appeal dismissed.
