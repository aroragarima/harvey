Â State of Maharashtra v Prabhakar Pandurang Sangzgiri and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 155; AIR 1966 SC 424; 1966 CRLJ 311; 1966 MahLJ 141; 1966 MPLJ 165; 1966 (1) SCJ 679; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 702

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Defence & Security Forces; Land & Property; Service

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Constitution - Defence & Security Forces - Defence of India Rules, 1962, r. 30(1)(b)- Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 19, 21, 226, 229 and 359 - Bombay Conditions of Detention Order, 1951 - A scientific book written by detenu while in jail- Applied to Govt. of Maharashtra seeking permission to send manuscript out of jail for publication- Rejected- Whether state Govt. acted contrary to law in refusing to send manuscript out of jail for eventual publication? -No restrictions except as provided under sub-rule (4) of r. 30 of Rules of 1962 can be imposed - There is no condition in Bombay Conditions of Detention Order, 1951, prohibiting a detenu from writing a book or sending it for publication- If appropriate authority seeks to impose on a detenu a restriction not so prescribed, authority will be interfering with personal liberty of detenu in derogation of law where under he is detained - Held, state Govt. acted contrary to law in refusing to send manuscript out of jail for eventual publication - State of Maharashtra infringed personal liberty of first respondent in derogation of law whereunder he is detained- Appeal dismissed.
