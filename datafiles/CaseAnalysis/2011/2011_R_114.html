Dayanandi v Rukma D. Suvarna And Others

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 828; (2012) 1 SCC 510; 2012 (3) ALD(SC) 19; 2012 (91) ALR 469; 2012 (1) AWC 766; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 377; 2012 (1) CTC 206; 2012 (3) MahLJ 182; 2012 (2) MPLJ 280; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 856; 2012 (115) RD 769

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Family & Personal - Indian Succession Act, 1925, s. 71 - Will -Partition - Correctness/altercations - Validity - Respondent No.1 filed suit for partition and separate possession of her share in plaint Schedule 'B' property - Trial Court dismissed suit holding that respondent No.1 was not entitled to any share in suit property - On appeal, Single Judge, allowed appeal and reversed judgment and decree passed Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether hand written endorsement made at end of typed Will was made at instance of testator before he affixed his left thumb mark and whether HC committed an error by reversing finding recorded by Trial Court - Held, if corrections/alterations had been made before testator had appended his left thumb mark, there was no reason why line showing deletion of name of respondent No.1 and corrections in figures were not reflected in typed Will - Further, why line was inserted in little space left between concluding portion of Will and space where left thumb mark was put by testator - Therefore, view taken by HC that corrections/alterations made in Will could not be said to have been duly attested by testator as per requirement of s. 71 of the Act - Hence, respondent No.1 was entitled to share in suit property specified in Schedule 'B' appended to plaint - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Land & Property - Family & Personal - Second will - Execution - Redundant - Validity -Whether 2nd Will was duly executed by father of respondent No. 1 therefore, 1st Will would be deemed to have become redundant - Held, if father of respondent No. 1 had consciously decided to disinherit respondent No.1 in 1st Will by appending his left thumb mark after corrections/alterations were made and name of respondent No.1 was deleted, there was no reason for him to execute 2nd Will - In her evidence, appellant and respondent No.2 could not offer any tangible explanation as to why it became necessary for her father to execute 2nd Will after he had already disinherited respondent No.1 - Thus, execution of 2nd Will was not a voluntary act of testator - Appeal dismissed.
