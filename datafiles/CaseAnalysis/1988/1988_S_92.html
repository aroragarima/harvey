Kehar Singh & Ors. v State (Delhi Admn.)

Supreme Court of India

03 August 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

G.L. Oza, B.C. Ray, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 958; (1988) 3 SCC 609; AIR 1988 SC 1883; 1988 (36) DLT 441; JT 1988 (3) SC 191; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 24

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Murder Reference, Power Of The Court, Religious Feelings, Public Trial, Open Trial, Sacred, Commissions of Inquiry (Amendment) Act, 1986, Tribunals of Inquiry (Evidence) Act, 1921, Special Commission Act, 1888

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 120-B, 34, 307 - Indian Arms Act , s.27 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused shot at deceased who was PM of Country - Accused were charged for offences punishable u/s. 302 r/w ss. 120-B, 34, 307 IPC and u/s. 27 of the Act - HC convicted and sentenced appellants to death - Hence, instant appeal - Whether orders of conviction and death sentence passed by HC was sustainable - Held, testimony coupled with other clinching circumstances available on record, was not even iota of doubt about crime committed by accused no.3 - Normal sentence for murder imprisonment for life and not sentence of death - Court required to give special reasons for awarding death sentence - Special reasons meant specific facts and circumstances obtained in case justifying extreme penalty - In view with Bachan Singh v. State of Punjab, 1980 Indlaw SC 586 indicated certain guidelines to be applied to facts of each individual case where question of imposing death sentence arose - It was observed that in cases where there was no proof of extreme culpability extreme penalty need not be given - It might be given only in rarest of rare Cases, where there was no extenuating circumstance - HC examined these principles and given reasons why death sentence should be awarded in instant case - Therefore, punishment measured was deserved - There should not be two opinions on instant issue - Blue Star Operation was not directed to cause damage to Akal Takhat nor it was intended to hurt religious feelings of Sikhs - Decision was taken by responsible and responsive Govt. in national interest - PM was made target for consequences of decision - Security guards who were duty bound to protect PM at cost of their lives, themselves became assassins - It was most foul and senseless assassination preparations for and execution of egregious crime do deserve dread sentence of law - Appeals disposed of.
