Â Bagalkot City Municipality v Bagalkot Cement Company

Supreme Court of India

23 October 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 281; AIR 1963 SC 771; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 710

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Indirect Tax - Bombay District Municipal Act, 1901 - Bombay General Act, 1904 - Appeal against a judgment of High Court which held that respondent was not liable to pay any octroi duty to appellant municipality in respect of dutiable goods brought to its factory as on a proper interpretation of appellant's by-law fixing octroi limits, respondent's factory was outside those limits - Interpretation of that by-law - By-law would bring within octroi- Limits of appellant municipality respondent's factory and some other premises though by-law had not been published to respondent or owners of premises - Held, if a by-law is invalid because it had not been published to persons likely to be affected by it, it would not become valid when municipal district of municipality concerned was extended on notice to everyone entitled to object to extension - Further held, by-law cannot be made u/s. 48 so as to affect people by its own terms or force unless to them it had been previously published - Present by-law cannot be read as including within octroi limits municipal district as extended from time to time; to do that would be to give it a meaning against its context and this, General clausess Act does not warrant - Appeal dismissed.
