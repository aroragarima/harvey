Pushpadevi M. Jatia v M. L. Wadhavan, Additional Secretary, Government of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28088; (1987) 3 SCC 367; (1987) SCC (Cr) 526; AIR 1987 SC 1748; 1988 (1) Bom.C.R. 480; 1987 (89) BomLR 461; [1988] 64 Comp Cas 228; 1987 (2) Crimes 705; 1987 CRLJ 1888; 1987 CRLR 447; 1987 (12) ECC 356; 1987 (30) E.L.T. 13; JT 1987 (2) SC 296; 1987(1) SCALE 896; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 46

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Fema

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1963

Summary: Criminal -Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 22 and 22(5) - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1975, ss. 3, 3(1), 5A, 8(b) and 11 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 340 and 389 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 193 and 224 - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973, ss. 3, 4(3) and 40(1) - Petition for issuance of a writ of habeas corpus for release of her husband - Accused detained by an order of Additional Secretary to Govt. of India, Ministry of Finance, u/s. 3(1) of COFEPOSA, 1975, on being satisfied that it was necessary to detain him - (A) Whether official acts of a person whose validity of appointment to same is doubtful is recognised? -Held, where an office exists under law, it matters not how appointment of incumbent is made - It is enough that he is clothed with insignia of office, and exercises its powers and functions - The official acts of such persons are recognised as valid under de facto doctrine, born of necessity and public policy to prevent needless confusion and endless mischief - (B)Whether detention in instant case could be rendered invalid on grounds of violation of art. 22(5) of Constitution? - Further held, there was no failure on part of Govt. to discharge its obligation u/art. 22(5) as relevant records of Enforcement Directorate clearly showed that there was sufficient material for formation of subjective satisfaction of detaining authority u/s. 3(1) of Act of 1975 -They also show that detenu was afforded a reasonable opportunity for making an effective representation against his detention - Therefore, order of detention passed against detenu was valid - (C)Whether COFEPOA, 1975 period of parole can be treated as a period in detention? - Held, the Act of 1975 specifically lays down that a person against whom an order of detention has been passed shall not be released on bail or bail bond or otherwise (vide s. 12(6) of Act) and that any revocation or modification of order of detention can be made only by Govt. in exercise of its powers u/s. 11 of COFEPOSA, 1975 - Petition Dismissed.
