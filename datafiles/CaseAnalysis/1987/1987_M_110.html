U.P. Pollution Control Board v Modi Distillery And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

06 August 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28091; (1987) SCC (Cr) 632; (1987) 3 SCC 684; AIR 1988 SC 1128; [1988] 63 Comp Cas 77; 1987 (2) CompLJ 298; 1987 (3) Crimes 223; 1988 CRLJ 1112; 1987 CrLR(SC) 532; JT 1987 (3) SC 221; 1987(2) SCALE 208; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 798; 1988 (1) ShimLC 161; 1987 (2) UJ 595

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974, ss. 25, 26, 44 and 47 - Messers Modi Industries Limited is is a large business organisation - Prior to commencement of Act of 1974 it had established an industrial unit engaged in business of manufacture and sale of industrial alcohol - During process of manufacture of such industrial alcohol, said industrial unit discharged its highly noxious and polluted trade effluents into nearby river (A) Whether Chairman, Vice-Chairman, Managing Director and members of Board of Directors of Respondent Company could be prosecuted and could be deemed to be guilty of offence with which they are charged? - Non obstante clause in s. 47(2) of Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974 expressly provides that notwithstanding anything contained in s. 47(1), where an offence under Act has been committed by a company and it is proved that offence has been committed with consent or connivance of, or, is attributable to any neglect on part of, any director, manager, secretary or other officer of company, such director, manager, secretary or other officer shall also be deemed to be guilty of that offence, and shall be liable to be proceeded against and punished accordingly - Held,the Chairman, Vice-Chairman, Managing Director and members of Board of Directors of Messrs Modi Industries Limited, Company who owned industrial unit Messrs Modi Distillery could be prosecuted as having been in charge of and responsible to company for business of industrial unit Messrs Modi Distillery owned by it and could be deemed to be guilty of offence with which they are charged - (B) Whether industrial unit should be allowed to avoid facing trial on a technical flaw which is not incurable for their alleged deliberate and wilful breach of provisions contained in ss. 25(1) and 26 made punishable u/s. 44 r/w s. 47 of Act of 1974 ? - Held, where industrial unit itself wilfully failed to furnish requisite information to Board regarding particulars and names of Managing Director, Directors and other person responsible for conduct of Company resulting in mentioning incorrect name of Company in complaint (Modi Distillery instead of Modi Industries Ltd., in instant case) it was not open to them to take advantage of lapse of their own industrial unit (Modi Distillery) and claim that prosecution be quashed against them - The technical flaw of describing name of Company wrongly could be rectified by amending complaint accordingly - Appeal allowed.
