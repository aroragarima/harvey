Md. Abdul Kadir and Another v Director General of Police, Assam and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1928; (2009) 2 SCC (L&S) 227; (2009) 6 SCC 611; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1054; 2009(8) SCALE 615; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 611; 2009 (7) SLR 212

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Ad Hoc Appointee, Ex-servicemen, PIF Additional Scheme, 1987

Summary: Service - Regularization - Appellants, Sub Inspectors, were appointed on ad hoc basis under the Additional Scheme, 1987, formulated under the Prevention of Infiltration of Foreigners Scheme (PIF Scheme) - Appellants sought regularization and parity in service benefits - Appellant challenged the procedure laid down by the Circular dated 17-3-1995 for the appointment/continuation of ad hoc staff - Single Judge allowed the writ petition, however, DB quashed the same in respondents appeal - (A) Whether the persons engaged under the Additional Scheme are entitled to be regularized in service - Held, Scheme had been in operation for some decades or that the employee concerned has continued on ad hoc basis for one or two decades would not be entitled to seek permanency or regularization - Even if any posts are sanctioned with reference to the Scheme, such sanction is for ad hoc or temporary posts co-terminus with the scheme and not for permanent posts - (B) Whether the procedure introduced by Circular dated 17.3.1995 is valid - Held, process of termination and re-appointment every year should be avoided and the appellants should be continued as long as the Scheme continues, but purely on ad hoc and temporary basis, co-terminus with the scheme - Circular dated 17.3.1995 directing artificial breaks by annual terminations followed by fresh appointment, being contrary to the PIF Additional Scheme and contrary to the principles of service jurisprudence, hence quashed - Appeal partly allowed.
