Hira Lal Hari Lal Bhagwati v C.B.I., New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 403; (2003) 5 SCC 257; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1121; AIR 2003 SC 2545; 2003 (2) CCR 160; 2003 (3) Crimes 100; 2003 (5) CRJ 689; 2003 CRLJ 3041; 2003 (182) CTR 1; 2003 (104) DLT 699; 2003 (108) ECR 457; 2003 (155) E.L.T. 433; [2003] 262 ITR 466; 2003 (2) JCC 928; JT 2003 (4) SC 381; 2003 (3) RCR(Criminal) 273; 2003 (56) RLT 723; 2003(4) SCALE 471; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 1118; 2003 (3) SLT 511; 2003 (4) Supreme 274; [2003] 129 TAXMAN 989

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, Interest Tax Act, 1974, Expenditure Tax Act, 1987, Kar Vivad Samadhan Scheme, Finance Act, 1998

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Customs - Customs Act, 1962, ss.111, 112(a), 112A, 28, 87 - Kar Vivad Samadhan Scheme, s. 88 - Finance Act, 1998 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 420 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, 482 - Customs Tariff Act, 1975 - Kar Vivad Samadhan Scheme, 1998 absolves appellants from criminal liability under IPC - Held, true fact and import of Kar Vivad Samadhan Scheme is that once said Scheme is availed of and all formalities complied with including payment of duty, immunity granted under provisions of Customs Act also extends to such offences that may prime facie be made out on identical allegations i e of evasion of Customs Duty and violation of any Notification issued under said Act - There is no prima facie case made out in respect of alleged offence u/s. 120 B r/w s.420 of IPC and, therefore, charge sheet and process issued thereunder has to be quashed - Further, for establishing offence of cheating, complainant is required to show that accused had fraudulent or dishonest intention at time of making promise or representation - As there was absence of dishonest and fraudulent intention, question of committing offence u/s. 420 of IPC does not arise - Appeals allowed.
