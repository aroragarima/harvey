Mahesh Kumar Joshi v Madan Singh Negi

Supreme Court of India

15 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 25; AIR 2015 SC 974; 2015 (109) ALR 642; 2015 (5) ALT(SC) 4; 2015 (2) AWC 2059; 2015 (1) CLT(SC) 183; JT 2015 (1) SC 317; 2015 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 992; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 673; 2015 (127) RD 533; 2015(1) SCALE 418; 2015 (1) WLN(SC) 38

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 37 r. 4 - Summary suit - Ex parte order - Respondent filed a summary suit for recovery of amount specified in two cheques issued by appellant - Trial Court passed ex parte decree - Appellant filed application under O. 37 r. 4 CPC for setting aside decree - Trial Court dismissed application - HC affirmed Trial Court order - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, a debatable issue does arise for consideration and it will be fair and just to give the appellant an opportunity to contest the suit subject to the appellant depositing the entire amount claimed in the suit but without interest or costs. Further, in the present case, it will be in the interests of justice that the ex-parte decree is set aside but the interest of the respondent is safeguarded by the deposit of the amount in question by the appellant as a condition precedent for setting aside the decree. Appeal allowed.
