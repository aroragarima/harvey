G. Ekambarappa & Ors. v Excess Profits Tax Officer, Bellary

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 438; AIR 1967 SC 1541; 1967 (2) ITJ 509; [1967] 65 ITR 656; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 864

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940 - General Clauses Act, 1897 - Notice - Maintainability - Appellants carried on a partnership business - On the date of commencement of business appellants were partners each of them representing their joint families - Firm was dissolved - One of the appellants partners died - For the chargeable accounting period Excess Profits Tax Officer had taken steps to assess the 'escaped' profits of the firm - He issued the necessary notices to the partners of dissolved firm - Whether the appellants continued to be liable to be proceeded against under s. 15 of the 1940 Act on the profits which had escaped taxation - Held, all the provisions of the Act stood repealed so far as the district of Bellary was concerned - The Adaptation of the Laws Order, 1956 only modified the provisions of s. 1 (2) of the 1940 Act and did not repeal the Act as such - S. 6 of the 1897 Act applies even in the case of a partial repeal or repeal of part of an Act - Notice issued u/s. 15 of the 1940 Act was legally valid and the appellants representing the original partners of the firm continued to be liable to be proceeded against under that section for the profits which had escaped taxation - Appeal dismissed.
