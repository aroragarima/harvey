Premji Ratansey v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

22 July 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 902; (1994) 5 SCC 547; AIR 1995 SCW 2425; 1995 (2) Bom.C.R. 374; 1994 (2) CCC 673; JT 1994 (6) SC 585; 1994(3) SCALE 562; [1994] Supp2 S.C.R. 117; 1994 (2) UJ 385

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Land and Property - Civil Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4 (1) and 17 - Specific Relief Act, 1963 - Issuance of injunction - Whether an injunction can be issued against true owner? - Held, title of land of railways was undisputed - Possession was delivered to railways - Injunction is absolutely a discretionary and equitable relief - It may be given to protect possession of owner or person in lawful possession - Interest of right not shown to be in existence, cannot be protected by injunction - Thus, injunction would not be issued against true owner - Further Court held, even assuming that petitioner had any possession, their possession is wholly unlawful possession of a trespasser and an injunction cannot be issued in favour of a trespasser or a person who gained unlawful possession, as against owner - Appeal dismissed.
