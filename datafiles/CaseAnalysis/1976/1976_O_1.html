Â Commissioner of Wealth Tax, Andhra Pradesh v Officer-In-Charge (Court of Wards), Paigah

Supreme Court of India

06 August 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, A.N. Ray, R.S. Sarkaria, P.N. Shingal, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 503; (1976) SCC (Tax) 411; (1976) 3 SCC 864; AIR 1977 SC 113; 1976 CTR 404; [1976] 105 ITR 133; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 146; 1976 TAXLR 952

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Agriculture & Agri. Products - Wealth-tax Act, 1957 - Andhra Pradesh land Revenue Act - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Agricultural land - Scope - Wealth tax - Exemption - Certain lands were situated in state of Andhra Pradesh - Respondents contended that lands were agricultural lands and were excluded from definition of assets, thus, entitled to be exempted from wealth tax - WTO said lands were not agricultural lands - AAC confirmed order of WTO - Tribunal confirmed order of AAC - HC accepted appeal of respondents and held that lands were agricultural lands within meaning of s. (2) of the 1957 Act - Whether alleged lands were agricultural lands within meaning of s. 2(e)(i) of the 1957 Act - Held, minimal test of agricultural land were to be applied in such cases - It was not correct to give wide meaning to terms used in statute simply because statute did not define them - Statute was to be viewed as whole and interpretation had to be done in consonance with purposes of statute - Object of the 1957 Act was to tax surplus wealth - It was imperative to give reasonable limits to scope of agricultural lands - Exemption had to be given more restricted meaning than wide ambit given by HC - There had to be connection between agricultural rural purpose and user and not mere possibility of user to use land for agricultural purpose in future - Entries in revenue records were good prima facie evidence - HC decision was set aside - Tribunal was directed to determine question afresh from a correct angle - Appeals allowed.
