Powanammal v State of Tamil Nadu and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 January 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, D.P. Wadhwa, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 915; (1999) SCC (Cr) 231; (1999) 2 SCC 413; AIR 1999 SC 618; 1999 (1) CCR 14; 1999 CRLJ 831; 1999 (1) JCC 74; JT 1999 (1) SC 31; 1999 (1) RCR(Criminal) 694; 1999(1) SCALE 49; 1999 (1) SCJ 624; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 104; 1999 (1) SLT 191

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Tamil Nadu Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Boot-leggers, Drug-offenders, Forest-offenders, Goondas, Immoral Traffic Offenders and Slum-grabbers Act, 1982, s. 3 - Preventive detention - Order of remand in English relied upon by detaining authority to reach subjective satisfaction - Tamil version of said order was not supplied to detenue who could not understand English - Causes prejudice to her as she is being detained - Held, her continued detention was illegal.
