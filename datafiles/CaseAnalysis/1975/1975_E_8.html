M. K. Paplah and Sons v Excise Commissioner and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, P.K. Goswami, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 669; (1975) SCC (Tax) 128; (1975) 1 SCC 492; AIR 1975 SC 1007; 1975 (4) CTR 71; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 607; 1975 (35) STC 537; 1975 TAXLR 1523

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Education Cess

Summary: Karnataka Excise Act, 1966, ss. 19, 16(e) and 23 - Karnataka Sales tax Act, 1957 - Constitution, sch.7, list 2, entries 54 and 245 - (A) Excise Duty - (1) Warehouse established or licensed u/s.16 (e) - It is a 'warehouse' within meaning of s. 23 - Determination of liability of exice duty as regard to sale from Govt. Depot - Liability as to Duty - (2) Constitutionality of provisions of ss. 22 and 71of the - Constitutionality - (C) Provision u/s.19 - Constitutionality - Sale from Government Depot is liable to Excise Duty - Provision not invalid on ground of excessive delegation of legislative function - Provision not ultra vires - Appeal Dismissed.
