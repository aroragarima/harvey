P. P. K. Gopalan Nambiar v P. P. K. Balakrishnan Nambiar and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1680; (1995) Supp2 SCC 664; AIR 1995 SC 1852; 1995 (1) HLR 450; JT 1995 (5) SC 163; 1995 (2) MLJ(SC) 87; 1995(2) SCALE 560; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 585; 1995 (2) UJ 61

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 22, r. 10 - Succession Act, 1925, s. 74 - (A) Death of some parties to partition suit - Parties had sold their shares to respondents who are on record - Legal representatives, need to bring them on record -(B) Validity of will - Whole estate given to son but daughter excluded and there was also discrepency in evidence of attestator - Will executed and registered 8 years prior to death of testator - Held, no need to bring their legal representatives on record - Discrepancy would not vitiate validity of a registered will which was duly endorsed by registrar and exclusion not itself sufficient to generate suspicion - Appeal allowed.
