Chhel Singh v M.G.B. Gramin Bank Pali and others

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 443; (2014) 13 SCC 166; AIR 2015 SC 598; 2014(5) ALL MR 423; 2014 (4) AWC 4046; 2014 (142) FLR 624; JT 2014 (10) SC 376; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2938; 2014(8) SCALE 485; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 217; 2015 (1) SLR 20; 2014 (3) WLN(SC) 85

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Absence From Duty

Summary: Service - Marwar Gramin Bank (Staff) Service Regulations, 1980 - Reinstatement - Denial of - Appellant, working with the respondent-Bank as Clerk-cum-Cashier, remained absent from duty without obtaining prior permission of the competent authority - Show cause notice was issued - After due process, appellant was removed from service - Appeal filed thereagainst before Appellate Authority was dismissed - Petition was filed before HC - Single Judge allowed the writ petition, quashed the order of removal and directed the respondent to reinstate the appellant in service - On appeal, DB of the HC though accepted that the Inquiry stood vitiated but set aside the order of reinstatement and remitted the matter for fresh enquiry - Hence, instant appeal - Whether DB of the HC rightly set aside the order of reinstatement -

Held, DB of the HC was wrong in setting aside the order of reinstatement - Order of termination being quashed by the HC, in absence of any observation and grounds to refuse the reinstatement, the appellant automatically stood reinstated - Without reinstatement in service, the question of further inquiry did not arise - There was no occasion for the DB of the HC to direct further inquiry, without reinstatement of appellant - Further, Inquiry Officer had also not held that appellant's absence from duty was willful and deliberate - It was neither case of the Disciplinary Authority nor the Inquiry Officer that the medical reports submitted by the appellant were forged or fabricated or obtained for any consideration though he was not ill during the said period - In absence of such evidence and finding, it was not open to the Inquiry Officer or the Disciplinary Authority to disbelieve the medical certificates issued by the doctors without any valid reason and on the ground of 24 days delay - Impugned judgment and order passed by the DB of the HC set aside - Order passed by the Single Judge upheld - Appeal allowed.
