TATA SKY Limited v State of Madhya Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 242; (2013) 4 SCC 656; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 178; JT 2013 (6) SC 562; 2014(3) M.P.H.T. 1; 2013 (3) MPLJ 609; 2013(5) SCALE 744; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 849; 2013 (30) S.T.R. 337; [2013] 60 VST 1

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Media & Entertainment

Keywords: Entertainment Tax, Madhya Pradesh Entertainments Duty and Advertisements Tax Act, 1936, Uttar Pradesh Entertainments And Betting Tax Act, 1979, Madhya Pradesh Entertainment Duty and Advertisement Tax Rules, 1942, Madhya Pradesh Entertainment Duty and Advertisements Tax (Amendment) Act, 2009

Summary: Indirect Tax - Media & Entertainment - Madhya Pradesh Entertainments Duty and Advertisements Tax Act, 1936, ss. 2(a), 2(b), 2(d), 3, 3(1), 3B, 4 and 5(E) - Finance Act, 1994 - Demand of entertainment tax - Appellants filed writ petition challenging the demand of entertainment tax by the State Govt.- Appellants submitted that direct-to-home (DTH) broadcasting was one of the notified services under the 1994 Act and was chargeable to service tax by the Central Govt. - HC dismissed said writ petitions uphold the demand by the State Govt. - Hence, instant appeal - Whether on the basis of the provisions of 1936 Act, it was permissible or possible for the State of Madhya Pradesh to levy on what in the lexicon of broadcasting was called DTH -

Held, u/s. 3 r/w ss. 2(d) and 2(a) of 1936 Act, the charge or levy of tax was attracted only if an entertainment takes place in a specified place or locations and persons were admitted to the place on payment of a charge to the proprietor providing the entertainment - In the present case, as DTH operation was not a place-related entertainment, it was not covered by the charging s. 3 r/w ss. 2(a) and 2(b) of the 1936 Act - Consequently, the question of going to s. 2(d)(iv) of 1936 Act does not arise - Moreover, even if s. 2(d)(iv) of 1936 Act was to be read as an extension of s. 3 of 1936 Act and, thus, as a part of the charge, it does not make any difference at all because s. 2(d)(iv) of 1936 Act refers to 'entertainment' which takes us back to s. 2(b) of 1936 Act and finally to s. 2(a) of 1936 Act - Moreover, if no duty could be levied on DTH operation under the 1936 Act prior to the issuance of the notification dt. 5-5-2008 , how duty can be levied under the 1936 Act after the issuance of the notification - Appeals allowed.
