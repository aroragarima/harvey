Baldev Singh Mann v Gurcharan Singh (Mla) and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, N.P. Singh, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3303; (1996) 2 SCC 743; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 1; AIR 1996 SC 1109; JT 1996 (1) SC 692; 1996(1) SCALE 683; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 99; 1996 (1) Supreme 772; 1996 (1) UJ 638

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: High Court Rules

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951 - Election Rule(1961) - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 123 - Corrupt practice - (A) Allegation quasi criminal in nature - Whether strict proof is necessary for act of booth capturing? - Held, strict proof is necessary (B) Booth capturing - What elements constitute booth capturing? - Threatening polling agent of candidate and asking him not to go inside polling station and not to raise objections regarding identity of persons - Would it be booth capturing - Further held, it would be an offence but not booth capturing - (C) Booth capturing - Proof - Allegation that there were incidents of large number of booth capturing by returned candidate, his agents and supporters - Petitioner did not examine his polling agent who was alleged to have first hand information - Neither any oral nor a written complaint was made to Returning Officer, Presiding Officers or Police personnel who were on election duty - Evidence of Returning Officer, Presiding Officers of booths and Police Superintendent, on other hand showed that elections were peaceful - Whether allegation of booth capturing proved? - Further held, allegation of booth capturing was not proved (D) Representation of People Conduct of Election Rules (1961), r. 93 - Election petition - Allegations of booth capturing - Petitioner applying for inspection of marked copies of electoral rolls as well as packets of counter foils of used ballot papers, to prove booth capturing and casting of bogus votes - Whether allegations of booth capturing proved? - Whether application of petitioner liable to be rejected as it was made when election petitioner had already concluded his evidence and as it was not in compliance with High Court rules? - Further held, not even prima facie proved - Liable to be rejected - Appeal dismissed.
