Surinder Singh v Hardial Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 October 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, P.N. Bhagwati, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 305; (1985) 1 SCC 91; AIR 1985 SC 89; 1985 (1) CCC 267; 1984(2) SCALE 659; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 1059

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Furnishing Of Security

Summary: Election - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 116 C, 123, 100, 127, 123(2) and 100(1)(b) - Corrupt practice - (A) Election petition - Appeal to Supreme Court - Nature and extent of jurisdiction - When SC can interfere with the finding of fact - (B) Disturbing election meeting does not amount to corrupt practice u/s. 123(2) - It is an electoral offence u/s. 127 - And not covered by grounds u/s. 100(1)(b) - Validity of election on ground that election meeting was disturbed - (C) Allegations of corrupt practice have to be strictly pleaded - Candidate instructing some persons to disturb opposite party meeting - Those persons threatening voters - Effect - (D) Alleged corrupt practice by supporter and criminal offences - Candidate protecting him and his family, even quarrel with local police - If presumption can be drawn as to candidate's consent to his supporter's earlier Acts of corrupt practice - (E) Contesting candidate exercising political influence legitimately to remove voter's grievance with Electricity Brand - If corrupt practice - (F) Proof of corrupt practice - (1) Suffuciency - (2) Oral evidence of witness belonging to rival political party - Credibility - Held, possible when lower Court's approach is wrong - Mere preponderance of probabilities not sufficeint - Election cannot declared void on that ground - In absence of specific plea in petition, consent of candidate for threatening cannot be presumed - No corrupt practice - Proof must be beyond reasonable doubt as in criminal cases - It cannot form sole basis of proof as it comes from tainted source - Finding of High Court that he was guilty of corrupt practice u/ss. 123(1) and (2) stands vacated and appeals allowed.
