Goa Plast Private Limited v Chico Ursula D'Souza

Supreme Court of India

20 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1038; (2004) 2 SCC 235; (2004) SCC (Cr) 499; AIR 2004 SC 408; 2004 (1) BC 246; 2003 (4) CCR 403; [2003] 117 Comp Cas 781; 2004 (1) Crimes 81; 2004 CRLJ 664; 2004 DRTC 264; 2003 (3) JCC 305; JT 2003 (9) SC 451; 2004 (3) KLT 93; 2004 (2) MahLJ 348; 2004 (2) MPLJ 27; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 179; 2003(9) SCALE 791; 2004 (1) ShimLC 247; 2003 (7) SLT 247; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 835; 2003 (8) Supreme 490

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Stop Payment

Summary: Negotiable Instruments Act - ss. 138, 142 - Cheque dishonoured as the respondent had issued instructions to stop payment - Both the Trial Court and the High Court held that the appellant had failed to prove the liability on the part of the respondent to pay the sum - Held, ss 138 and 139 of the Act were enacted in view of the fact that cheques were issued for payment of admitted liability but the drawer used to dishonour the said liability by issuing instructions to the Bank for stop payment. To avoid the aforesaid and to create an element of credibility and dependability, the aforesaid Sections were enacted which provide a criminal remedy of penalty if the ingredients of the Sections are satisfied - Further held, the respondent has committed an offence punishable under the provisions of s. 138 of the Act and is liable to be punished.
