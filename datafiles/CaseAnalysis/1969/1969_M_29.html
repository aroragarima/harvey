State of Rajasthan v Man Industrial Corporation Limited

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 411; (1969) 1 SCC 567; AIR 1969 SC 1245; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 505; 1969 (24) STC 349

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Whether High Court erred in holding that contract between respondent and Executive Engineer was a 'building contract' and amount received by respondent was not taxable? - Test in each case is whether object of party sought to be taxed is that chattel as chattel passes to other party and services rendered in connection with installation are under a separate contract or are incidental to execution of contract of sale - In present case, specifications of windows were set out in contract - Primary undertaking of respondent was not merely to supply windows but to 'fix' windows - Service is not rendered under a separate contract nor is service shown to be rendered customarily or normally as incidental to sale by person who supplies window leaves - It was only upon 'fixing' of window- Leaves and when window-leaves had become a part of building construction that property in goods' passed under terms of contract - Held, HC had not erred - Appeal dismissed.
