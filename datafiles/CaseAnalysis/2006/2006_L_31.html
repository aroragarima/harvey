Lingegowd Detective and Security Chamber Private Limited v Mysore Kirloskar Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 195; (2006) 5 SCC 180; AIR 2006 SC 1967; 2006 (7) AWC 6784; JT 2006 (5) SC 452; 2006 (3) RLW 2202; 2006(5) SCALE 227; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 844

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Forced Labour

Summary: [A.] Service - Minimum Wages Act, 1948, ss. 2(b), 2(e), 2(g), 2(i), 27, 3, 5, 5(2) - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 21, 23, 258 - Applicability of Act to undertaking of Appellant-Lingegowd - Plea that appellant is an establishment of providing security personnel to various organization was not a scheduled employment as detailed in Schedule to Act and as no specific Notification was issued in that behalf - Single Judge come to conclusion that Lingegowd had no liability and directed principal employer Mysore Kirloskar Limited to pay a sum of Rs. 1, 00, 000/- As ex-gratia to workmen - DB has held that where a person provides labour or services to another for remuneration, which is less than minimum wages, labour or services provided by him fell within scope and ambit of words forced labour - Whether workmen of Lingegowd were entitled to grant of minimum wages? - Held, detective services do not form part of scheduled employment as detailed in Schedule - Mysore Kirloskar having not challenged learned Single Judge's order, is required to make payment - Appeals partly allowed.
