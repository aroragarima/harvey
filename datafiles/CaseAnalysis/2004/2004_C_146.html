Commissioner Of Police And Ors. v Smt. C. Anita

Supreme Court of India

23 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 714; (2004) 7 SCC 467; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1944; AIR 2004 SC 4423; 2004 CRLJ 4853; JT 2004 (7) SC 355; 2004 (4) RCR(Criminal) 247; 2004(7) SCALE 120; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 701

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Human Rights; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Drug offenders

Summary: Criminal - Preventive Detention - Andhra Pradesh Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Bootleggers, Dacoits, Drug-offenders, Goondas, Immoral Traffic Offenders and Land-Grabbers Act, 1986, s. 3(2) - Order of detention, whether proper? - Detenue has previous record - Court cannot substitute its own opinions for that of the detaining authority when the grounds of detention are precise, pertinent, proximate and relevant - No vagueness or staleness - Incidents clearly substantiate the subjective satisfaction arrived at by the detaining authority as to how the acts of the detenu were prejudicial to the maintenance of public order - These aspects have not been considered by the HC - Order of detention has a specific purpose to serve and thus is proper and valid - Appeal allowed.
