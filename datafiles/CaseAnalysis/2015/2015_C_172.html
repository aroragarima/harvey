Maruti Suzuki India Limited v Commnr. of Central Excise, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

12 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 225; 2015 (318) E.L.T. 353; JT 2015 (6) SC 203; 2015(4) SCALE 85; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 180

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Duty Of Excise, Central Excises And Salt Act, 1944, Tissue Paper

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 57A, 57F(ii) - Valuation of Processed Goods - Evasion of Tax - Respondent-department served notice on appellantcompany on ground that appellant had cleared inputs/ spares after processing, but duty was only paid equivalent to the MODVAT credit taken on these inputs before processing - Respondent alleged that appellant had escaped duty on account of the value addition of EDC on various spare parts relatable to motor vehicles - Competent authority declared that duty that was evaded by the appellants was Rs.1,68,07,499/- instead of Rs.2,00,20,310/- as stated in the show cause notice - Whether duty would be payable upon valuation of the processed goods.

Held, inputs that were not ultimately used in the final product but were removed from the factory for home consumption remain the same despite ED coating and consequent value addition. Law laid down in S.R. Tissues Pvt. Ltd.'s case states that on account of mere value addition, it would be hazardous to say that manufacture has taken place, when in fact, it has not. It is clear, therefore, that the inputs procured by the appellants in the present case, continue to be the same inputs even after ED coating and that r. 57F(ii) proviso would therefore apply when such inputs are removed from the factory for home consumption, the duty of excise payable being the amount of credit that has been availed in respect of such inputs u/r. 57A. Penalty imposed upon appellant was set aside. Appeals disposed of.
