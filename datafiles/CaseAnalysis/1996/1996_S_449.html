State Anti-Corruption Bureau, Hyderabad and another v P. Suryaprakasam

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 4139; (2008) 14 SCC 13

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 239, 240 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5(2) - Possession of disproportionate assets - discharge - Opportunity to prove case - Case was registered against respondent u/s. 5(2) of the Act, for possessing assets disproportionate to his known sources of income - Principal Special Judge rejected application seeking discharge u/s. 239 of CrPC - Respondent filed revision before HC in which HC disposed of revision petition by directing State Govt. - Thereafter respondent filed another application before trial Judge seeking discharge on ground that Govt. did not consider his explanation in terms of direction of HC but it was rejected by trial Judge - Respondent filed another revision petition before HC which was allowed and proceeding against respondent was quashed - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether opportunity of giving explanation and hearing was liable to be granted to accused and non-grant of same would vitiate order of sanction in case u/s 5(2) of the Act - Held, instead of adverting to and confining its attention to documents referred to in ss. 239, 240 of CrPC, HC should dealt with rival contentions of parties and on threadbare discussion thereof passed order - Course so adopted could not be supported because finding regarding commission of offence could not be recorded on basis of affidavit evidence and at stage of framing of charge Court could not usurp functions of trial court to delve into and decide upon merits of case - Therefore, order of HC was patently wrong and thus set aside - Trial Judge would proceed with case in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
