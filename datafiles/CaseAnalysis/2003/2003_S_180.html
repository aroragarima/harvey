Dalip Singh and Others v Sikh Gurudwara Prabhandak Committee and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 849; (2003) 10 SCC 352; AIR 2004 SC 137; JT 2005 (6) SC 182; 2004 (136) PLR 450; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 788; 2003(8) SCALE 637; 2003 (1) SCW 5736; 2003 (7) SLT 6; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 775; 2003 (7) Supreme 355

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Sikh Gurdwaras Act, 1925, Punjab Occupancy Tenants (Vesting of Proprietory Rights) Act, 1952

Summary: Punjab Occupancy Tenants (Vesting of Proprietory Rights) Act, 1952 - Sikh Gurdwara Act, 1925 - s. 10(3) - Appellants bought property from respondents 2 and 3 - Original owner occupancy tenant - Respondent 1 claimed to be the owner of the land - Held, it is for the appellants who had filed a suit for the declaration of the title to prove that the original owner had acquired right, title or interest over the suit properties either under the general law of succession or tenancy law - Further held, it is proved by satisfactory evidence that the property belonged to the Sikh Gurdwara and the appellants failed to prove that their predecessors-in-interest, namely, respondent nos. 2 & 3 acquired any title from Bhola Singh. Consequently, the sale deed in favour of the appellants did not confer any title on them in respect of the suit properties.
