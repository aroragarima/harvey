K. Ravindranathan Nair v Commissioner of Income Tax, Ernakulam

Supreme Court of India

30 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, Doraiswamy Raju, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 811; (2001) 1 SCC 135; 2000 (164) CTR 498; 2001 (127) E.L.T. 11; [2001] 247 ITR 178; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 555; 2000(8) SCALE 20; 2000 (8) SLT 611; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 244; 2000 (8) Supreme 133; [2001] 114 TAXMAN 53

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 37 - Whether expenditure involved was allowable as deduction u/s. 37? - Expenditure was incurred in respect of industrial problem in some units - Tribunal found that all units run by assessee constituted a single business - Held, High Court ignored principle that Tribunal is final fact finding authority - Expenditure allowable - Appeal dismissed.
