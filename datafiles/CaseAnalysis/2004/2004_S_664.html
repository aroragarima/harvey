M/s. Girnar Traders v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 863; (2004) 8 SCC 505; 2005 (3) Bom.C.R. 343; JT 2005 (11) SC 544; 2005 (Supp) RD 38; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 490

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land and Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning Act, 1966, chapt. VII - Whether all provisions of LA Act, as amended by Central Act 68 of 1984 can be read into provisions under chapt. VII of Maharashtra Act, for an acquisition there under? - Held, no good reason as to why provisions introduced in LA Act vide amendment should not be read into an acquisition under chapt. VII of Mahrashtra Act, to extent not precluded by Maharashtra Act - s. 11A being one such section, it may have to be applied to acquisition under chapt. VII of Mahrashtra Act - Decision in 'State of Maharashtra vs. Sant Joginder Singh Kishan Singh' requires reconsideration by a larger Bench - Order accordingly.
