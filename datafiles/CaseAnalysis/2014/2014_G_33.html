Gold Quest International Private Limited v State of Tamil Nadu and others

Supreme Court of India

08 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 596; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 4504; 2015 CRLJ 589; JT 2014 (10) SC 106; 2014 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4039; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 206; 2014(10) SCALE 343; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 677; 2014 (4) WLN(SC) 190

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Quashing of FIR, Entitled to, Cheating

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 420 - Prize Chits and Money Circulation (Banning) Act, 1978, ss. 4, 5, 6 - Code of Criminal Procedure, s. 482 - Constitution of India, 1950, art, 226 - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - Cheating - Quashing of FIR - Entitled to - Case was registered u/s.420 IPC r/w ss. 4, 5, 6 of the Act against appellant accused - Appellant filed writ petition before HC for FIR registered against him to be quashed - Single Judge quashed FIR and petition was disposed of - Respondents filed appeal and challenged said order passed by Single Judge before DB - DB allowed appeal filed by respondents - Hence instant appeal - Whether DB erred in law in setting aside order of Single Judge quashing FIR on basis of compromise and settlement between complainant and appellant -

Held, disputes which are substantially matrimonial in nature, or the civil property disputes with criminal facets, if the parties have entered into settlement, and it has become clear that there are no chances of conviction, there is no illegality in quashing the proceedings u/s. 482 CrPC. r/w art. 226 of the Constitution. However, the same would not apply where the nature of offence is very serious like rape, murder, robbery, dacoity, cases under the 1988 Act , cases under 1985 Act and other similar kind of offences in which punishment of life imprisonment or death can be awarded. After considering the facts and circumstances of instant case, SC viewed that Single Judge did not commit any error of law in quashing FIR after not only complainant and appellant settled their money dispute but also other alleged sufferers entered into an agreement with appellant, and they too settled their claims. Thus, impugned order passed by DB was liable to be set aside. Appeal allowed.
