Vinita S. Rao v Essen Corporate Services Private Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

17 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 627; (2015) 1 SCC 527; AIR 2015 SC 882; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 4170; 2015 (2) BC 58; 2015 CRLJ 147; 2015 (1) DRTC 18; JT 2014 (12) SC 366; 2015 (4) KarLJ 307; 2014 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 3821; 2015 (3) MahLJ 573; 2015 (2) MPLJ 605; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 253; 2014(10) SCALE 632; 2015 (4) WLN(SC) 241

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Complaint by power of attorney holder

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138, 142(a) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 200 - Dishonour of cheque - Complaint by power of attorney holder - Legality - Respondents (accused) were financial consultants and sub-brokers who are engaged in business of trading and appellant transferred his shares to Demat account of respondents - Respondents confirmed that a sum of Rs.1,54,000/- was due to appellant on account of dividends and profits from share transactions - However, appellant (power of attorney) filed a complaint for offence punishable u/s. 138 of the Act against respondents - Trial Court convicted respondents u/s. 138 of the Act and sentenced to pay a fine of Rs.30,12,000/- - Aggrieved respondents filed an appeal before Lower Appellate Court, which was dismissed - Aggrieved respondents filed revision petitions before HC and Single Judge by impugned common judgment overturned the concurrent judgments of Courts below - Hence, instant appeals - Appellant contended that when statement of appellant was recorded on oath, power of attorney was produced.

Held, it is clear that the complaint u/s. 138 of the Act can be filed through the power of attorney holder. There can be no dispute about the fact that in instant case, the power of attorney holder being the husband of the appellant has witnessed all transactions and he possesses due knowledge about them. He is associated with all transactions at all crucial stages. The appellant has placed this fact in the forefront in her complaint. Appellant's evidence substantiates her case that her husband had knowledge about the entire transaction. Hence, the submission that the complaint could not have been filed through power of attorney holder must fail. The power of attorney holder's sworn statement was recorded and summons was issued. This exercise cannot be faulted and is in complete accord with s. 200 of CrPC. At that stage, the power of attorney holder had stepped in the shoes of the appellant. Otherwise, there was no point in the appellant giving power of attorney to her husband. SC in A.C. Narayanan v. State of Maharashtra, 2013 Indlaw SC 599, nowhere states that if the complaint is filed by the complainant through power of attorney holder, the complainant must file affidavit in support of the complaint prior to issuance of summons. Matter is remanded to HC with a request that HC should hear both sides and decide whether the cheques in question were issued as a security or for the purpose of repayment of legally recoverable debt. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - When the complainant herself has stepped in the witness box, Court do not see the need for the power of attorney holder to examine himself as a witness. Law cannot be reduced to such absurdity. The purport of the Act will be frustrated if such approach is adopted by the courts.
