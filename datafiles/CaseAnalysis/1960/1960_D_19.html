Â Pandit M. S. M. Sharma v Dr. Shree Krishna Sinha and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 August 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 130; AIR 1960 SC 1186; 1961 (1) KerLR 8; 1961 (2) SCJ 73; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 96

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(a), 21, 32, 194(3) and 212 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 11 - (A) Whether mere non-compliance with rules of procedure cannot be a ground for issuing a writ u/art. 32? - Held, courts have always recognised basic difference between complete want of jurisdiction and improper or irregular exercise of jurisdiction - Mere non-compliance with rules of procedure cannot be a ground for issuing a writ u/art. 32 - (B)Whether delay in proceeding against petitioner in contempt is justified? - Further held, this is a matter within jurisdiction of legislature which must decide whether or not it was recent enough to be taken serious notice of, or whether any punishment in event of petitioner being found guilty is called for - Petition dismissed.
