Cox and Kings Limited and Another v Chander Malhotra Smt

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, K. Ramaswamy, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2017; (1997) 2 SCC 687; JT 1996 (11) SC 493; 1997 (1) RCJ 253; 1997 (1) RCR(Rent) 493; 1997(1) SCALE 229; [1996] Supp10 S.C.R. 1; 1997 (2) Supreme 234

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Banking & Finance - Corporate - Exchange Control & FDI - Foreign Exchange Management Act, 2000 - Companies Act, 1956 - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, s. 14(1)(b) - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973, ss. 28, 29 and 47 - United Kingdom Companies Act - RBI - Rent Control - Whether or not the involuntary transfer of the leasehold interest from Foreign Co. to the Indian Co. is sub-letting within the meaning of s. 14(1)(b) of the Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958? - Sub-section (b) of s. 14 envisages that the tenant shall not, after 09-06-1952, sub-let, assign, or otherwise part with the possession of the whole or any part of the premises, without obtaining the written consent of the landlord - By operation of FERA, 1973, the foreign Co. had wound up its business and it assigned, under the agreement, the leasehold interest in the demised premises to the Indian Co. which is carrying on the same business in the tenanted premises without obtaining the written consent of the landlord - Held, there was a clear assignment between the foreign Co. and the Indian Co. of the demised premises without any written consent of the respondent-landlord, thus it is a case of "sub-letting" within the meaning of s. 14(1)(b) of the Act - Appeal dismissed.
