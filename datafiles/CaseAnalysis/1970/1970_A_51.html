Awadhi Yadav and Another v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

17 August 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 474; (1971) 3 SCC 116; AIR 1971 SC 69; 1971 CRLJ 23; 1970 (2) SCJ 1; 1970 UJ 776

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 34 - Enmity between accused and deceased - Murder - Whether conviction u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 can be based on circumstantial evidence? - No eye-witnesses - Bitter enmity between accused and deceased established - Death as a result of injuries inflicted by sharp cutting weapons like Bhallas and Parsas - Accused and others seen carrying dead body - Circumstances in question satisfactorily established - Held, proved circumstances bring home offence to accused beyond reasonable doubt - Appeal dismissed.
