Mohd. Rashid Ahmad and another v State Of U.P. & Anr

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, R.S. Sarkaria, V.D. Tulzapurkar

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 209; (1979) SCC (L&S) 82; (1979) 1 SCC 596; AIR 1979 SC 592; 1979 ALJ 340; 1979 (1) LLJ 146; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 826; 1979 (1) SLR 327

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Service - U.P. Mahapalika Adhiniyam, 1959, ss. 107(1), 108, 112A and 577(ee) - U.P. Palika (Centralised) Services Rules, 1966, r.6(2) (iii) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Termination - Prayer for re instatement - Sustainability - Respondent had by its circular entrusted the task of determining the suitability or otherwise of officers and servants holding Centralised Services posts drawing less than Rs. 500/-, to Divisional Committees, and reserved such function in respect of officers and servants drawing Rs 500/- or more, to the State Selection Committee, and by its subsequent circular dt. 23-2-1967 maintained the classification of such officers and servants for purposes of judging their suitability for absorption in the Centralised Palika Services, the final orders of absorption in each case under s.112A of Adhiniyam were passed by respondent - Eventually, respondent in exercise of its powers u/cl.(iii) of r.6(2) of Rules determined the services of the appellant No.1 and that of Ashfaq Hussain/Appellant No.2 on the ground that they were not found fit for absorption under s.112A of the Adhiniyam - Two appellants and several other employees of the erstwhile Municipal Boards and District Boards challenged the validity of the various orders passed by respondent terminating their services before HC on several grounds - Amongst others, they challenged the validity of r.6(2) (iii) of Rules, framed u/s. 112A of the Adhiniyam, on the ground that it was ultra vires respondent as it brought about extinction of the relationship of employer and employees between them and the erstwhile Municipal Boards - It was also urged that the impugned orders were violative of arts. 14 and 16 of the Constitution because the classification made by respondent by its circular entrusting the task of determination of the suitability or otherwise of officers and servants holding Centralised Services posts drawing less than Rs. 500/- to Divisional Committees, which were enjoined to give them the right of a personal hearing to have their say in the matter of final absorption, and entrusting of such function in respect of officers and servants drawing Rs. 500/- and above to the State Selection Committee without a corresponding right of hearing, was without any rational basis - It was also urged that the impugned orders were vitiated being in breach of the rules of natural justice - HC, however, rejected all these contentions - Hence instant appeal - Whether re instatement of appellants was to be considered - Held, appellant No.2 was called for an interview by the Divisional Committee - Respondent in its return has placed material showing that he had a uniformly bad record and there were adverse entries in his character rolls for several years - It was not disputed that appellant No.2 had been called for an interview by the Divisional Committee - SC wase not impressed by the submission that the adverse remarks were not put to him when he appeared before the Divisional Committee - It was clear from the two circulars of respondent that in all cases in which the services of an officer or servant were to be determined on the ground of his unsuitability, they were to be given an opportunity of personal interview by the Committee - Whole purpose of the personal interview was that when it was proposed to declare such an official unsuitable for absorption, the Committee had to afford an opportunity to appear before it and clear up his position - It was reasonable to presume that when the appellant No.2, was called for that purpose, the adverse remarks in his character rolls should have been put to him - On an overall view of the record of service of appellant No.2, the Divisional Committee was not wrong in recommending to the Govt. to terminate his services, and the Govt. was within its rights in passing the impugned order of termination in regard to him - In the case of the appellant No.1 it however appeared from the return filed by respondent that no such opportunity was afforded to him before respondent passed the impugned order terminating his services - It was evident, no doubt, from the return filed by respondent that the service record of the appellant was before the Govt., on the basis whereof it was decided that he was unsuitable for being finally absorbed and also that the Secretary for Local Self-Govt. in his note recommended that he was not suitable for final absorption in the Centralised Services, but it was clear that the Minister for Local Self-Govt. before passing the impugned order of termination did not give to the appellant an opportunity of a hearing - Order of termination of his services passed by respondent, therefore, suffered from a serious legal infirmity - It was said, however, on behalf of respondent that u/s.107(1) of the Adhiniyam no appointment to a post carrying an initial salary of not less than Rs. 500/- per mensem, could be made except after consultation with the Public Service Commission, and that the Commission did not find the appellant fit for appointment as Executive Engineer, Municipal Corporation - It was also pointed out that under s. 108 of Adhiniyam the appellant could not hold the post beyond the period of one year - It was, therefore, urged that respondent was justified in terminating the services of the appellant as he could not be finally absorbed in the post of an Executive Engineer in the Centralised Services - It was said that the post had to be advertised for filling up the vacancy as required under s. 107 of the Adhiniyam - Under s. 112A (1) of the Adhiniyam, respondent having by Rules, constituted the Centralised Palika Services, the appellant No.1, who was performing the duties and functions of the post of Executive Engineer u/s. 577(ee) of Adhiniyam, was entitled to be considered, if found suitable, for absorption under s.112A(2) of Adhiniyam - Admittedly, the appellant was not heard in the matter of his final absorption - It was also not in dispute that the procedure laid down in the URules was not followed - If the appellant was at all found fit for absorption, it was for the respondent next to decide the suitable post on which he could be absorbed - Method of recruitment provided by r. 20 of Rules had to be followed - Evidently, it was not done - Appeals disposed of.
