State of Maharashtra v Wasudeo Ramchandra Kaidalwar

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, Baharul Islam, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 405; (1981) 3 SCC 199; (1981) SCC (Cr) 690; AIR 1981 SC 1186; 1981 CRLJ 884; 1981 CRLR 390; 1981 CrLR(SC) 390; 1981(1) SCALE 819; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 675; 1981 SLR 68

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Anti- Corruption Laws (Amendment) Act, 1964

Summary: Anti-Corruption Laws (Amendment) Act, 1964 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 101 - 104 and 114 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5(2) r/ w s. 5(1)(e) - Public servant - Assets disproportionate to income - No satisfactorily reason for - Extent of proof required to convict u/s. 5 (1) r/ w s. 5(1)(e) - (A) On Prosecution u/s. 5(1)) - (B) On accused u/s. 5(2) - Held, prosecution need not disprove all possible sources of his income - Accused need not prove his innocence beyond all reasonable doubt and preponderance of probability as to possession set out by accused is sufficient - Appeal Dismissed.
