Jai Bhagwan v Commissioner of Police and others

Supreme Court of India

05 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 408; (2013) 11 SCC 187; AIR 2013 SC 2908; 2013 (6) AWC 6051; 2013 (138) FLR 776; JT 2013 (9) SC 176; 2013(8) SCALE 392; 2013 (4) SLR 522; 2013 (3) WLN(SC) 101

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Misconduct - Dismissal from service - Legality - Appellant was posted as Asst. Wireless operator and a cabin was provided to him for that purpose - When Inspector went for checking cabin used by appellant he found same locked from inside - Inspector knocked at door but got no response from within cabin - Appellant was charged with misconduct - Appellant found guilty and was dismissed from service by an order passed by Disciplinary Authority on 29-3-2002 - Aggrieved, appellant preferred appeal before prescribed appellate authority, which was dismissed - Appellant then approached CAT for redress but remained unsuccessful - Appellant filed writ petition before HC, which was also dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the charges against appellant were satisfactorily proved - Held, punishment of dismissal from service for kind of misconduct proved against appellant appeared to be grossly disproportionate - There was no allegation that appellant had manhandled police Inspector who had gone to check cabin - Delay of 10 minutes in opening cabin door, which according to appellant was open but had got stuck because of humidity leading to expansion of the wooden frame, was not a matter that ought to have led to appellant's dismissal after he had served police force for over 10 years - Even assuming that version given by appellant was not acceptable same did not constitute a misconduct of a kind that would justify appellant's dismissal from service leading to forfeiture of his past service - Appellant was not in uniform might also be breach of discipline calling for administrative action against him but not so severe as to throw him out of police force - Dismissal from service of appellant was a harsh punishment order for dismissal could be substituted by an order of reduction to rank of a constable with direction that while appellant should have benefit of continuity of service he should not be entitled to any arrears of pay or other financial benefits for period between date of dismissal and date of his reinstatement against lower post of constable - Appeal allowed.
