KSL and Industries Limited v Arihant Threads Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

27 October 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sharad Arvind Bobde, H.L. Dattu, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 729; (2015) 1 SCC 166; AIR 2015 SC 498; 2015 (2) ALD(SC) 15; 2015 (108) ALR 250; 2014 (4) BC 596; [2015] 190 Comp Cas 328; 2015 (1) CompLJ 241; 2015 (1) DRTC 330; JT 2014 (12) SC 244; 2014 (4) KLT 471; 2015 (3) MahLJ 413; 2015 (1) OLR 134; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 396; 2015 (126) RD 718; 2014(12) SCALE 313

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Recovery Officer, Industrial Reconstruction Bank Of India Act, 1984, Idbi, Banking Companies (Legal Practitioners Accounts) Act, 1949, Permissibility, Sick Industrial Companies Bill

Summary: Banking & Finance - Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993, ss. 34, 34(2) - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985, s. 22 - Sick industrial company - Recovery proceedings - Permissibility - Appellant company bought respondent No.1 company's property in auction - DRT set aside auction on an application of respondent No.1 - Respondent and appellant filed appeals before Appellate Tribunal - Appellate Tribunal set aside DRT order and confirmed auction sale - Respondent challenged Appellate Tribunal order before HC - HC set aside Appellate Tribunal order - Respondent invoked 1985 Act by filing reference before Board of Industrial Finance and Reconstruction (BIFR) - BIFR dismissed reference, however, Appellate Authority (AAIFR) declared respondent No. 1 as 'Sick Company' - Hence, instant appeal - In view of a difference of opinion on interpretation of s. 34 of 1993 Act, instant matter was referred for decision.

Held, though the 1993 Act is the later enactment, s. 34(2) of 1993 Act specifically provides that the provisions of the Act or the rules thereunder shall be in addition to, and not in derogation of, the other laws mentioned therein including 1985 Act. The purpose of the two Acts is entirely different and where actions under the two laws may seem to be in conflict, Parliament has wisely preserved the proceedings under the 1985 Act, by specifically providing for s. 34(2) of 1993 Act, which lays down that the later Act 1993 shall be in addition to and not in derogation of the 1985 Act. Undoubtedly, in the narrower sense an application for recovery of debt can be giving a restricted meaning i.e. a proceeding which commences on filing and terminates at the judgment. However, there is no need to give such a restricted meaning, since the true purpose of an application for recovery is to proceed to the logical end of execution and recovery itself, that is by way of execution and distraint. S. 22 of 1985 Act clearly covers and interdicts such an application for recovery made under the provisions of the 1993 Act. Court found nothing contrary in the intention of the 1985 Act to exclude a recovery application from the purview of s. 22 of 1985 Act, indeed there could be no reason for such exclusion since the purpose of the provision is to protect the properties of a sick company, so that they may be dealt with in the best possible way for the purpose of its revival by the BIFR. Impugned judgment and order of HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Provisions of 1985 Act, in particular s. 22 of 1985 Act, shall prevail over the provision for the recovery of debts in the 1993 Act.
