Electronics Corporation of India v G. Muralidhar

Supreme Court of India

17 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 343; (2001) 10 SCC 43; 2001 (2) AWC 1293; 2001 (2) CLR 29; 2001 (89) FLR 327; JT 2001 (3) SC 549; 2001 (1) LLJ 1343; 2001 (4) SLT 286

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Termination - Order of termination had been passed not by disciplinary authority but by Appellate Authority - High Court came to the conclusion that by taking recourse to such procedure, a right of appeal was denied to delinquent and therefore, order of termination is vitiated - Reinstatement - Hence, present appeal - Held, a valuable right of appeal having been denied to delinquent respondent - Trial of respondent vitiated as right of appeal denied - Reinstatement upheld without back wages - Relied on Surjit Ghosh v. Chairman and Managing Director, United Commercial Bank and Ors 1995 Indlaw SC 445 - Appeal disposed of.
