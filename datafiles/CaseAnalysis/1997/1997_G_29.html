Geep Industrial Syndicate Limited Etc. v Collector of Central Excise, Allahabad Etc.

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2507; (1997) 2 SCC 602; AIR 1997 SC 970; 1997 (69) ECR 232; 1997 (90) E.L.T. 271; JT 1997 (2) SC 221; 1997 (18) RLT 671; 1997(1) SCALE 681; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 907; 1997 (2) Supreme 629

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 4(4)(d)(i) - Valuation of goods for levy of excise duty - Cost of packing - (A) What amounts to packing provided at time of delivery of goods at gate? - (B) 7 ply corrugated cartons - Used for packing medium size cartons - For protecting them from damage during course of transport, i.e, transport after delivery - Not necessary for putting articles in condition in which they are generally sold in wholesale market at factory gate - If value of said packing can be included in value of goods packed - Held, packing is necessary for purpose of excisable articles in condition in which they are generally sold in wholesale market at factory gate - Cannot be included - Appeal No. 14407 of 1996 dismissed; Appeal No. 4608-4612 of 1996 and 4960 of 1996 allowed.
