S. W. Palanitkar and Others v State of Bihar and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20406; (2002) SCC (Cr) 129; (2002) 1 SCC 241; AIR 2001 SC 2960; AIR 2001 SCW 4435; 2002 (46) ARBLR 353; 2001 (4) CCR 204; 2002 (1) Crimes 146; 2002 (2) CRJ 464; 2001 CRLJ 4765; 2001 (2) JCC 361; JT 2001 (9) SC 151; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 572; 2001(7) SCALE 430; 2001 (7) SLT 439; [2001] Supp4 S.C.R. 397; 2001 (8) Supreme 216; 2002 (1) UC 67B

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 200, 202, 203, 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss 120-B, 405, 406, 415, 420 - Breach of trust - Conviction - Sustainability - Under an agreement between appellant No. 1-company and respondent No. 2, he was appointed as consignment stockist of company subject to certain terms and conditions - Respondent No. 2- complainant served notice on Manager Marketing and Regional Manager of company requesting them to make payment of Rs. 15.00 lacs to it within 15 days or in alternative refer disputes and differences to arbitration as per cl. 29 of agreement - Thereafter company offered to supply Ammonium Sulphate to respondent and respondent rejected offer finding terms unreasonable - Respondent filed complaint on alleging offences u/ss. 406 and 420 r/w s. 120B IPC inter alia stating that accused persons in collusion and connivance of each other with wrongful objects and motive to wrongfully squeeze money/ gratification from complainant and in their own benefit used complainant wrongly and have cheated complainant by practising fraud and have acted fraudulently against complainant and by doing such acts they have committed criminal breach of trust and put complainant to wrongful loss and have gained wrongfully - Accused persons have also cheated complainant by using office and godown premises of complainant on basis of false assurances given to complainant and without making any farthing for such costly and valuable premises and thus committed criminal breach of trust, fraud and cheating which caused loss of rupees fifteen lacs - Trial Court issued summons against appellants - Appellants approached HC for quashing order of Trial Court - HC by impugned order dismissed petition - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Trial Court was justified in issuing summons to accused - Held, there was nothing either in complaint or in sworn statements of complainant and three witnesses that any property was entrusted to any of appellants at all or appellants had domain over any of properties of respondent No. 2 which they dishonestly converted to their own use so as to satisfy ingredients of s. 405 IPC punishable u/s. 406 IPC - Further agreement also did not require entrustment of any property to appellants - Taking complaint and statements of witnesses as they are, it cannot be said even prima facie, that appellants committed any offence punishable u/s. 406 IPC, since ingredients of that offence were not satisfied - Hence Trial court committed serious error in issuing process against appellants for said offence - Exercise of inherent power was available to HC to give effect to any order under CrPC, or to prevent about of process of any court or otherwise to secure the ends of justice - When a person approaches HC u/s. 482 CrPC to quash very issue of process, HC had to exercise powers with circumspection as stated above to really serve purpose and object for which they are conferred - Thus, issue of process against appellant Nos. 1-6 and 8 was set aside and the process issued against appellant no. 7 namely for offences u/ss. 406 and 120-B was also set aside - However, issue of process against him u/s. 420 IPC was maintained - Appeal partly allowed.
