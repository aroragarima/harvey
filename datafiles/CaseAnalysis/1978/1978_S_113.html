Porritts and Spencer (Asia) Limited v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, V.D. Tulzapurkar, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 287; (1979) SCC (Tax) 38; (1979) 1 SCC 82; AIR 1979 SC 300; 1989 (25) ECR 443; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1607; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 545; 1978 (42) STC 433; 1979 TAXLR 1692; 1978 UJ 697

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Punjab General Sales Tax Act, 1948, sch. B, item 30 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Customs Tariff Act, 1975, chapter 59 - Textiles - Cotton/Woolen dryer felts - Whether 'dryer felts' manufactured by the assessee fall within the category of 'all varieties of cotton, woollen or silken textiles,' specified in Item 30 of Schedule 'B' of the 1948 Act and therefore exempted from sales tax both under the 1948 Act and the Central Sales Tax Act, 1956? - Held, item 30 covers all varieties of cotton, woollen or silken textiles including rayon artificial silk or nylon whether manufactured by handloom or powerloom or otherwise but not including pure silk fabrics, carpets, druggets, woollen durees and cotton floor durees - However, the word 'textiles' is not defined in the 1948 Act - But in chapter 59 of Customs Tariff Act, 1975 it is mentioned that there is a reference to 'textile fabrics and textile articles, of a kind commonly used in machinery or plant' and clause (4) of that Chapter provides that this expression shall be taken to apply inter alia to woven textile felts of a kind commonly used in paper making or other machinery - This reference in a statute which is intended to apply to imports made by the trading community clearly shows that 'dryer felts' which are woven textile felts - Therefore, dryer felts are 'textiles' within the meaning of that expression in Item 30 of Schedule 'B' of 1948 Act - Appeal allowed.
