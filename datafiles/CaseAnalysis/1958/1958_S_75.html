Â Pranab Kumar Mitra v State of West Bengal and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 October 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 28; AIR 1959 SC 144; 1959 CRLJ 256; 1959 (1) MLJ(SC) 166; 1959 SCJ 489; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 63; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 63

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 250, 439, 431 and 435 - (A) Whether a pending application in revision made under s. 439 of CrPC, 1898, finally abates on death of petitioner in High Court? - Held, revisional power of High Court is discretionary in nature - It has power to determine case even after death of convicted person, if a sentence of fine was also imposed on him, because that sentence affects property of deceased in hands of his LR - (B) Whether there is any limit on discretionary power of HC to examine correctness, legality or propriety of any finding, sentence or order of fine? - Held, HC has power to examine whole question of correctness, propriety or legality of sentence of fine, which necessarily involves examining order of conviction itself - Appeal allowed.
