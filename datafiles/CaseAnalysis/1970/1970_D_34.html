Vallabh Das v Dr. Madan Lal & Ors

Supreme Court of India

02 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 336; (1970) SCC (Cr) 564; (1970) 1 SCC 761; AIR 1970 SC 989; 1970 CRLJ 895; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 211

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 23, r. 1 - Whether present suit has same cause of action as earlier suit? - Held, the expression 'subject-matter' is not defined in the CPC, 1908 - It does not mean property - That expression has a reference to a right in the property which the plaintiff seeks to enforce - That expression includes the cause of action and the relief claimed - Unless the cause of action and the relief claimed in the second suit are the same as in the first suit, it cannot be said that the subject-matter of the second suit is the same as that in the previous suit - Where the cause of action and the relief claimed in the second suit are not the same as the cause of action and the relief claimed in the first suit, the second suit cannot be considered to have been brought in respect of the same subject matter as the first suit - Appeal dismissed.
