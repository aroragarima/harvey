Harjit Singh and others v State of Punjab and others

Supreme Court of India

16 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1764; (2002) 6 SCC 739; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1518; AIR 2002 SC 3040; 2002 (3) CCR 187; 2002 (2) CGLJ 424; 2002 (3) Crimes 5; 2002 (6) CRJ 343; 2002 (3) JCC 1601; JT 2002 (6) SC 123; 2002 (3) RCR(Criminal) 843; 2002(5) SCALE 644; 2002 (4) SLT 777; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 581; 2002 (5) Supreme 378

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Kirpan

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302,307,323,324,326 r/w ss. 148, 149 - Appreciation Of Evidence - Unlawful Assembly - Private Defence - Sustainability - Trial Court convicted all six accused for offences u/ss. 302,307,323,324,326 r/w ss. 148, 149 IPC and sentenced them all to life imprisonment and separate sentences with fines for other offences for which they were charged - HC in appeal set aside conviction of accused IS, AS and SS - HC also acquitted other three co-accused for charge u/ss. 148, 149, IPC - Three accused had been convicted u/ss. 302, 307,326,324 and 323 r/w s. 34 IPC and sentences imposed on them by Trial court had been maintained - All six accused persons had been alleged to be members of unlawful assembly and with common object alleged to have committed murder of deceased - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was sustainable - Trial court attached importance to fact that on disclosure statement of accused pistol alleged to had been used by IS was recovered - Statement of PW-9 was referred to and was unable to explain reason for not procuring attendance and signature of independent witnesses on disclosure statement and memorandum of recovery - It was noted that these memoranda had been signed only by two police officers -It was unbelievable that all accused persons who had alleged to use their fire arms/weapons kept all arms concealed in open field in gunny bag under heap of straw - In absence of independent witnesses and alleged place of concealment being accessible to public, evidence of disclosure statement and recovery of arms and weapons had not inspired confidence - It was not piece of evidence which could be relied by Trial court to convict accused by treating it as eye-witness account -In appeal against acquittal appreciation of evidence done by HC unless it appeared to be unreasonable, should not be interfered with - Court looked into whole evidence on record and opined that none of three accused persons against whom no specific overt acts had been proved could be held guilty either u/s. 149 or 34 IPC - Hence, acquittal was sustained - Acquittal of three co-accused persons, remaining three accused persons could not be convicted u/ss. 148 and 149 IPC and their acquittal for those charges was also sustained - Appeal disposed of.
