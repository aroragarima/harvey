Commissioner Of Income-Tax, Lucknow v Bazpur Co-Operative Sugar Factory Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 369; (1989) Supp2 SCC 240; AIR 1989 SC 1866; 1989 (2) BankCLR 515; 1989 (77) CTR 136; [1989] 177 ITR 469; JT 1989 (2) SC 562; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 840; [1989] 44 TAXMAN 281

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Excess Profits Tax, Commercial Expediency, Capital Redemption Reserve, Co-Operative Society

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 36(1)(ii) - Borrowed Capital - Co-operative Society, running sugar mill - Allotting partly paid shares to farmer members - Establishing Loss Equalisation and Capital Redemption Reserve Fund - Members required to deposit in it at certain rate against supply of cane - To be used firstly for converting partly paid-up shares into fully paid up, then defraying loan and balance was to be refunded to members - Whether interest paid on balance is admissible deduction - Held, Deposits by members are not loans advanced to society - Interest paid on balance ia not admissible deduction - Appeal allowed.
