State Of Rajasthan & Ors v M/S Khandaka Jain Jewellers

Supreme Court of India

16 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1143; (2007) 14 SCC 339; AIR 2008 SC 509; 2007 (7) AWC 7084; 2008 (1) CTC 60; JT 2007 (13) SC 82; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 1139; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 91; 2007(13) SCALE 190; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 105; 2008 (1) WLN(SC) 26

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Stamps Act, 1952, Rajasthan (Amendment) Stamp Duty Act, Merchant Shipping Act, 1894, Indian Registration of Ships Act, 1841

Summary: Suit for specific performance of the contract - Suit decreed - whether the valuation should be assessed on the market rate prevailing at the time of registration of the sale deed or when the parties entered into agreement to sell? - Appeal against - Held, a taxing statute has to be construed as it is all these contingencies that the matter was under litigation and the value of the property by that time shot up cannot be taken into account for interpreting the provisions of a taxing statute - Simply because the matter have been in the litigation for a long time that cannot be a consideration to accept the market value of the instrument when the agreement to sale was entered - At the time when registration is made, the valuation is to be seen on that basis - Appeal allowed.
