District Collector and Another v P. Chinna Reddy and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 October 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, P.B. Sawant, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 273; (1990) Supp SCC 7

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Socio-Economic - With a view to provide cultivable lands to sixteen tribals within East Godawari District of Andhra Pradesh, Govt. gave few acres of land to each of persons and terms of lease were provided in D-Form patta - After lessees came into possession of their respective leasehold lands, they found that there were lots of overgrowth, some of which they cut down and wanted timber transit permits to be given to them for removing of timber of felled trees - When timber transit permits were not granted they applied to High Court for a direction for their grant - Appeal and special leave petitions arose out of such proceedings taken before High Court - It was case of appellants who were respectively District Collector and District Forest Officer of East Godawari that lands which were intended to be given by way of lease were different from what respondents have been claiming and property which they claimed to be in their possession as lessees are really forest lands with thick overgrowth - Held, Courts are satisfied that State Govt. intended to benefit tribals by providing cultivable lands and lands which have been given or, at any rate in which lessees claim to be in possession, are not immediately suitable for cultivation - Thus, with a view to doing complete justice to parties and freeing tribals from litigation court finds proper to dispose of these cases with certain directions - Appeal disposed of.
