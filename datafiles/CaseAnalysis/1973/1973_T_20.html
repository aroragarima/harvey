Â Sambhu Nath Sarkar v State of West Bengal and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Ray, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, Hans Raj Khanna, Y.V. Chandrachud, K. Jagannatha Shetty, A.K. Mukherjea

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 105; (1973) SCC (Cr) 618; (1973) 1 SCC 856; AIR 1973 SC 1425; 1973 CRLJ 1155; 1973 CrLR(SC) 424; 1973 MLJ 512; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Preventive Detention Act, 1950, Defence Of India Act, 1971

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Maintenance of Internal Security Act 1971, s. 17 A - Constitution of India 1950, art. 12, 14, 22, 22(7)(a) and 137 - Review - Ground - Order of detention challenged - S. 17 A of act is in violation with art. 22(7)(a) - Parliament may depart from rule laid down in art. 22(4) only on two conditiions - Circumstances and cases in which a person is detained for a period longer than three months without obtaining opinion of an advisory board - Held, that matter involving right of personal liberty is good ground for reviewing of its earlier decision - Order accordingly.
