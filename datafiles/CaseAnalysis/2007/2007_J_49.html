Oriental Insurance Company Limited v Jhuma Saha and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 639; (2007) 9 SCC 263; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 443; 2007 (1) ACC 420; AIR 2007 SC 1054; 2007 (66) ALR 795; 2007 (3) ALT 57; 2007 (1) CCC 263; [2007] 135 Comp Cas 675; 2007 (1) JCC 609; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 219; JT 2007 (2) SC 646; 2007 (2) MLJ(SC) 660; 2007 (146) PLR 59; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 761; 2007(2) SCALE 255; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 979

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Contractual Liability, Public Service Vehicle, Damage To Property, Additional Premium

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 147 - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 - Whether in view of s. 147 of Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, jurisdiction of Tribunal was confined to a third party claim and, thus, impugned judgment cannot be sustained? - Held, liability of insurer-company is to extent of indemnification of insured against respondent or an injured person, a third person or in respect of damage to property - Thus, if insured cannot be fastened with any liability under provisions of Motor Vehicles Act, question of insurer being liable to indemnify insured, therefore, does not arise - An owner of a vehicle can only claim provided a personal accident insurance has been taken out - In this case there is no such insurance - Additional premium was not paid in respect of entire risk of death or bodily injury of owner of vehicle, if that be so, s. 147(b) of Motor Vehicles Act which in no uncertain terms covers a risk of a third party only would be attracted in present case - Appeal allowed.
