C. Antony v K.G. Raghavan Nair

Supreme Court of India

01 November 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1369; (2003) 1 SCC 1; (2003) SCC (Cr) 161; AIR 2003 SC 182; 2006 (4) BC 295; [2002] 112 Comp Cas 611; 2003 (1) Crimes 76; 2003 (2) CRJ 625; 2003 CRLJ 411; 2003 (1) JCC 41; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 750; 2002(8) SCALE 266; 2002 (6) SLT 272; 2002 (7) Supreme 598

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal

Summary: Negotiable Instruments Act - S. 138 - conviction and sentence under - Review - power of appellate court to review - Held, when, the learned Magistrate had given cogent reasons for not accepting the evidence led on behalf of the respondent and on that basis came to the conclusion that the complainant/respondent had not established his case, then the High Court could not have substituted its finding merely because another contrary opinion was possible based on material on record. - Further, there was an obligation on the part of the High Court to come to a definite conclusion that the findings of the trial court are either perverse or the same are contrary to material on record.
