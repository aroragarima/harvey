Raghubir Singh and Others Etc v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

19 September 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 617; (1986) 4 SCC 481; (1986) SCC (Cr) 511; AIR 1987 SC 149; 1986 (12) AWC 1134; 1987 CRLJ 157; 1986 CRLR 491; JT 1986 SC 481; 1986(2) SCALE 452; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 802

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Foreign Jurisdictions

Summary: Criminal - Practice and Procedure - Constitution, arts. 21, 32 and 136 - CrPC, 1973 - Criminal Law Amendment Act, 1952, s.6 - (A) Delay in police investigation - Whether inference of infringement of right to speedy trial can be drawn - (B) Validity of creation of Special Judge's Court at the place of imprisoned of the accused in the interests of security - (C) Whether charges of conspiracy and sedition are sustainable against a person who is not the author of the letters containing seditious material, which were recovered in search - (D) Order of release on bail under proviso to s.167(2) not defeated by filing of charge-sheet or by remand to custody u/s.309(2) of the Code - Held, cannot be inferred merely from delay in police investigation , it depends on facts and circumstances of each case - No evil design can be attributed to Govt. as step tended to convenience of accused and was in the interests of security - Charges are still justified - It can be cancelled only u/s.437(5) or s.439(2) - Petition dismissed.
