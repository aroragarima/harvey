Potti Lakshmi Perumallu v Potti Krishnavenamma

Supreme Court of India

13 August 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, K. Subba Rao, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 386; AIR 1965 SC 825; 1965 (2) MLJ(SC) 105; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 26

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Severance

Summary: Land & Property - Family & Personal - Hindu Women's Rights to Property Act, 1937, s. 3(2) - Out of three brothers in a family, Sitaramaiah died in 1938, leaving behind him his widow - Krishnamurti died without leaving any issue or a widow - Plaintiff-respondent instituted suit for partition against defendant-appellant, surviving brother - (A) Whether a will executed by Sitaramaiah itself resulted in severance in status of two brothers constituting joint family? - Held, a perusal of will does not however unmistakably show that intention of Sitaramaiah was to separate himself from joint family - Nowhere in will has he stated that he wanted an end to coparcenary - (B) What is quantum of interest to which a Hindu Widow is entitled u/s 3(2) of Act? - Further held, widow does not, by virtue of interest given to her by new law become a coparcener she being entitled to claim partition of joint family property is in same position in which her deceased husband would have been in matter of exercise of that right - When Act says that she will have same right as her husband had it clearly means that she would be entitled to be allotted same share as her husband would have been entitled to had he lived on date on which she claimed partition - Interest devolving upon widow need not necessarily be either by survivorship or by inheritance but could also be in a third way, i.e. by statute and where interest is taken by her under a statute no further difficulty arises - High Court was right in allotting to respondent half share in family property as its partition - Appeal dismissed.
