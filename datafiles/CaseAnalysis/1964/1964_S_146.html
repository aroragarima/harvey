Messrs Jaipuria Brothers Company v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 204; AIR 1965 SC 1213; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 780; 1965 (16) STC 494

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act,1948,ss. 2(c), 9, 10, 11 and 21 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Whether High Court had erred in holding that limitation u/s. 21 of U P Sales Tax Act would not apply when fresh assessment is being done on directions of court? - Words used by legislature in amended s.21 are precise and admit of only one interpretation that proceedings taken for assessment or re-assessment in consequence of, or to give effect to an order of appellate or revising authority or an order passed by HC u/s. 11 may be taken notwithstanding expiry of period prescribed by sub-section (2) of s. 21 - Held, in initiating proceeding for assessment, pursuant to direction of revising authority, Sales Tax Officer was, by virtue of s. 21 as amended, subject to no restrictions as to period within which order of assessment could be made - Appeal dismissed.
