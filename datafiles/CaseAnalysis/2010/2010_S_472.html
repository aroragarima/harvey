Girjesh Shrivastava and others v State of Madhya Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

22 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 884; (2010) 10 SCC 707; 2011 (2) AWC 1482; 2010 (5) ESC 679; 2011 (128) FLR 19; 2011 (1) JabLJ 314; JT 2010 (11) SC 598; 2011 (4) MLJ(SC) 309; 2010(11) SCALE 256; [2010] 12 S.C.R. 839; 2010 (6) SLR 311

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Service

Keywords: Ex-servicemen, Madhya Pradesh Panchayati Raj Avam Gram Swaraj Adhiniyam, 1993, Madhya Pradesh Panchayati Raj Act, 1993, Madhya Pradesh Panchayat Contractual Teachers (Conditions of Appointment and Service) Rules, 2001

Summary: Service - Municipalities & Local Governments - Madhya Pradesh Panchayati Raj Avam Gram Swaraj Adhiniyam, 1993 - Madhya Pradesh Panchayat Contractual Teachers (Conditions of Appointment and Service) Rules, 2001, r. 5(4)(b) - Selection - Validity - Zila Panchayat Office issued an advertisement for the appointment of contractual teachers - Appellants applied for the advertised posts and were selected and appointed as contractual teachers - Appointments, however, were challenged in two Public Interest Litigations (PILs) before the HC - Petitioners in PIL contended that near relatives of the members of the Selection Committee had appeared before the Committee as candidates for selection and got selected, therefore, selection was invalid - Petitioner, further, contended that no proper provision for reservation for ex-servicemen was made in advertisement which was in contravention of r. 5(4)(b) of the Rules - HC allowed PILs and invalidated the selection, however, PILs allowed only on the ground that selection was in violation of r. 5 (4) (b) of the 2001 Rules which mandated a reservation of 10 per cent for ex-servicemen - Appellant/appointed contractual teachers, subsequently, filed review petitions before the HC, however, HC dismissed the review petitions - Hence the present appeal by appellant/contractual teachers - Whether HC was right in quashing the appointment - Held, in one district, 5 ex-servicemen were appointed out of a total of 9 applicants, in another district, none was so appointed - If there was an issue with respect to the reservation policy of the ex-servicemen it ought to have been brought up as a service dispute and not in a PIL - HC should have displayed a little more restraint and balance before quashing a selection process in which the persons selected had already put in 3 years of service - Further, para 10 of the application form issued for the candidates stated that if the applicant person was either a handicap or an ex-serviceman then he was required to mention so in the application form and that a certificate to the same effect from a competent authority should be enclosed - Therefore, it would be incorrect to say that the advertisement was so made so as to prevent ex-servicemen from applying - Further, HC should have been mindful of the fact that an order for cancellation of appointment would render most of the appellants unemployed and would severely affect the economic security of many families - Alleged participation of near relatives in the selection process was not such a factor as to vitiate the entire selection process - Even if there were some illegal beneficiaries from the selection process, they should have been weeded out instead of striking down the entire selection process - Therefore, equities were not properly balanced in the exercise of discretion by the HC, hence impugned judgments of the HC are quashed and selection proceedings are upheld - Appeal allowed.
