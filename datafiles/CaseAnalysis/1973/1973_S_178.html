Guli Chand and Others v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

30 November 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 371; (1974) SCC (Cr) 222; (1974) 3 SCC 698; AIR 1974 SC 276; 1974 CRLJ 331; 1974 CRLR 53; 1974 UJ 121

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution, art.136 - Evidence Act, 1872 - (A) Appreciation of evidence - (1) Credibility of chance witness - (2) Relationship - Fact that a witness is a relation of a victim is not sufficient to discard his testimony - (C) Appeal by special leave u/art. 136 of Constitution - Concurrent findings of fact - Interference with - Held, testimony of a chance witness, although not necessarily false, is proverbially unsafe - Mere fact that a witness is a relation of a victim is not sufficient to discard his testimony - Where after considering the totality of evidence it cannot be said that the concurrent findings of fact given by the lower Courts as regards the proof of guilt of accused beyond reasonable doubt are erroneous, the Supreme Court will not interfere - Petition Allowed.
