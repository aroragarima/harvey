K.P.O. Moideenkutty Hajee v Pappu Manjooran and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3310; (1996) 8 SCC 586; 1996 (3) AD(SC) 6; AIR 1996 SC 3356; 1997 (1) BC 357; 1996 (3) CCC 33; 1996 (2) CLT 86; 1997 CPJ 143; JT 1996 (3) SC 329; 1996(2) SCALE 784; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 227; 1996 (5) Supreme 113

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Summary: Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 118(a) and 4 - Promissory note - Presumption of consideration - Defendant entered into sale agreement for land - Defendant executed promissory note for possession on some part of property - Plaintiff pleaded different consideration than that from the one found in negotiable instrument - Initial presumption not available to plaintiff - Documents indicating receipt of consideration under promissory note for transfer of some land produced - Held, promissory note supported by valid consideration.
