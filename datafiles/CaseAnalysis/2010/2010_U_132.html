Uttar Pradesh Land Development Corporation And Another v Mohd. Khursheed Anwar And Another

Supreme Court of India

05 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 450; (2010) 7 SCC 739; AIR 2010 SC 2287; 2010 (6) AWC 5912; 2010 (3) ESC 410; 2010 (126) FLR 832; JT 2010 (6) SC 431; 2010 (4) LLJ 289; 2010(6) SCALE 194; 2010 (4) SLR 351; 2010 (3) UPLBEC 1823

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Pay - Parity in - Respondents, graduates in engineering applied for being employed under the Appellant-Corporation as Assistant Engineers and were appointed on contract basis for a period of three months on a consolidated salary of Rs.2000 per month with a stipulation that their claim for regular appointment will not be entertained - Tenure of engagement of the respondents was extended from time to time - After completing three years' service, respondents jointly filed writ petition for issue of a mandamus to the appellants to pay them salary in the regular pay scale prescribed for the post of Assistant Engineer and also regularize their services on that post - HC negatived the respondents' claim for regularization of service but accepted their plea for issue of a mandamus to the appellants to pay them salary in the pay scale prescribed for the post of Assistant Engineer - Present appeal - Whether direction given by HC to the appellants to pay salary to the respondents in the regular pay scale prescribed for the post of Assistant Engineer legally correct? - Held, HC granted relief to the respondents by presuming that two posts of Assistant Engineer were utilized for appointing them - This assumption is ex facie fallacious because the documents produced show that the respondents were engaged for a fixed period on a consolidated salary - Nothing from which it can be inferred that the respondents were appointed against the sanctioned posts of Assistant Engineer (Civil) - Direction given by the HC for payment of salary to the respondents in the regular pay scale prescribed for the post of Assistant Engineer cannot be sustained - However, appellants were not justified in continuing the respondents on a consolidated salary despite the fact that at the time of their selection, two sanctioned posts of Assistant Engineer and one post of Junior Engineer were lying vacant and proposal for appointing the respondents without any nomenclature was made with the sole object of taking work of the particular post from them without paying salary in the regular pay-scale of any post - Very fact that the respondents were engaged on a consolidated salary of Rs.2,000 per month and the prescribed pay-scale of the post of Assistant Engineer in other branches was Rs.2200-4000/- and that of the Junior Engineer was Rs.1,600 - 2,660 gives a clear indication that they were engaged to do the work of Assistant Engineer - Respondents had been arbitrarily deprived of their legitimate right to get minimum of the pay-scale prescribed for the post of Assistant Engineer - Impugned order set aside - However, the appellants directed to pay to the respondents minimum of the pay-scale prescribed for the post of Assistant Engineer - Appeal partly allowed.
