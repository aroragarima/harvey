Gmr Energy Limited v Commissioner of Customs, Bangalore

Supreme Court of India

27 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 892; (2016) 2 SCC 427; 2015 (325) E.L.T. 445; JT 2016 (1) SC 283; 2015(12) SCALE 109; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 106

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Imported Goods, Part, Importer, Customs Valuation (Determination Of Price Of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988, Statements, India

Summary: (A) Customs - Indirect Tax - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 138B, 17(3), 46(1), 46(4) - Customs Valuation (Determination of Price of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988, rr. 5, 10 - Evasion of Customs Duty - Confiscation of Imported Goods Challenged - Appellant imported parts of naphtha based power plant, which was replaced after fired hours of use under Long Term Assured Parts Supply Agreement (LTAPSA) - However, respondent/ department issued notice demanding custom duty and confiscation of goods - Commissioner of Customs, confirmed demand of 1/3rd of invoice value of imported parts to be added to arrive correct assessable value - Tribunal in appeals upheld findings of Commissioner - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, r.10(1) of the Rules, consists of two sub-clauses and u/cl. (a) of the said rule, the assessee/importer has to submit a declaration disclosing full and accurate details relating to the value of the imported goods. Further, s. 46(4) of the Act, states that 'importer while presenting a bill of entry shall make and subscribe to a declaration as to the truth of the contents of such bill of entry and shall, in support of such declaration, produce to the proper officer the invoice, if any, relating to the imported goods'. Further, a conjoint reading of s. 17(3) of the Act, and r. 10(1)(b) of the Rules, would make it clear that the proper officer may require the importer to produce any contract with reference to the imported goods consequent upon which the importer shall produce such contract. In instant case, the proper officer has not called upon the assessee to produce any contract in relation to the imported goods. This being the case, it is clear that there is no infraction of r. 10 of the Rules. Hence, judgment of Tribunal is set aside thereby appeal of assessee is allowed. Appeals disposed of.

(B) Customs - Indirect Tax - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 138B, 17(3), 46(1), 46(4) - Customs Valuation (Determination of Price of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988, rr. 4, 9 - Notification of Scheme - Grant of Exemption Challenged - Notification was issued by respondent/department providing exemption to goods in respect of scheme for renovation or modernization of power plant - However, respondent/Commissioner denied benefit of exemption and Tribunal granted benefit of exemption in respect of goods imported under two bills of entry - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, once these authorities are satisfied that the impugned goods are required for renovation, the customs department does not need to go deep into the matter and by hairsplitting and semantic niceties deny the benefit of the exemption notification. Hence, the finding of the Commissioner has been correctly set aside by the Tribunal, thereby appeal of revenue is dismissed. Appeals disposed of.
