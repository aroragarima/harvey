Ajay Kumar Agrawal and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 November 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 574; (1991) 1 SCC 636; AIR 1991 SC 498; 1991 ALR 452; JT 1991 (1) SC 168; 1990(2) SCALE 1041; 1991 (1) SLR 776; [1990] Supp3 S.C.R. 184; 1991 (1) UJ 54; 1991 (1) UPLBEC 430

Case Digest

Subject: Health & Drug; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Meritorious

Summary: Education - Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 226 - Admission in Post graduate medical course - State Govt. fixing cut -off date for completion of internship beyond date for selection examination - Candidates not qualified for acquiring full fledged MBBS degree admitted to selection examination and also selected - Non compliance of court's order - Held, Supreme Court indicated basis on which admission in respect of seats representing state quota should be made - Warning to State of Uttar Pradesh and Principals of Medical Colleges that any further infraction of these directions shall be severely dealt with - Appeal allowed.
