Ashok Leyland Limited v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1020; (1997) 9 SCC 10; JT 1997 (2) SC 666; 1997(2) SCALE 242; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 224; 1997 (105) STC 152; 1997 (3) Supreme 495

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Foreigners; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1955, ss. 6-A and 8(2-A) - Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Act - Indian Constitution, 1950, art. 269 - Sales and purchase - (A) Whether, the contract of sale was in respect of specific or ascertained goods? (B) Whether, it was in respect of unascertained or future goods and if it is the latter, when did the appropriation of the goods to the contract of sale take place? - Held - Questions of fact which do not arise for consideration in these appeals - If the assessing authorities decide against the appellant, it shall be open to them to file appeal(s) before the tribunal directly - If and when the tribunal decides against the appellant, it shall be open to the appellant to approach this court for appropriate directions - Tamil Nadu Sale Tax Appellate Tribunal comes to the conclusion that the transactions in question are inter-state sales upon which Central Sales Tax is leviable in the State of Tamil Nadu, the State of Tamil Nadu shall not enforce their demand for a period of eight weeks from the date of the decision of the tribunal - Further, till the issue is decided by the Sale Tax Appellate Tribunal, no Central Sales Tax shall be demanded from the appellant, provided it is established by the appellant that in respect of the transaction, the appellant has paid tax in another State treating it as an intra-state sale in that other state - Appeals dismissed.
