Babu Ram Prakash Chandra Maheshwari v Antarim Zila Parishad Muzaffar Nagar

Supreme Court of India

02 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 93; AIR 1969 SC 556; 1969 ALJ 596; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 518

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - U.P. District Boards Act, 1922 , ss. 108, 114 and 124 - Statutory appeal - Availability of alternative remedy - Held, fact that aggrieved party has another and adequate remedy may be taken into consideration by superior court in arriving at a conclusion as to whether it should, in exercise of its discretion, issue a writ of certiorari to quash proceedings and decisions of inferior courts subordinate to it - Ordinarily superior court will decline to interfere until aggrieved party has exhausted his other statutory remedies, if any - Discretion of High Court has not been exercised in accordance with law and judgments of Division Bench and of Single Judge summarily dismissing Writ Petition are defective in law - Appeal allowed.
