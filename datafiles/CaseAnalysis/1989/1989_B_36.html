Kali Prasad Agarwalla And Others v Bharat Coking Coal Limited And Others

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 562; (1989) Supp1 SCC 628; AIR 1989 SC 1530; 1989 BBCJ 112; JT 1989 (3) SC 170; 1989(1) SCALE 852; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 283; 1989 (2) UJ 218

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Land & Property

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Civil Procedure - Constitution - Coal Mines (Nationalisation) Act, 1973, ss. 2(h)(iv) and 2(h)(vi) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6, r. 2 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133 -(A) Term 'mine' - Scope of - Land adjacent to a mine, used for purposes thereof - For carrying on mining operations in respect of part of seam lying immediately below surface - Also for purposes of adjacent mine - (B) Appeal before Supreme Court - Pleading - Evidence - Consideration of - Parties going to trial with full knowledge of what they had to prove and adducing evidence in support of respective claims - Evidence considered by courts below - Plea that in absence of proper pleadings evidence should not be looked into - Held, it is 'mine' within ss. 2 (h)(iv) and 2(h)(vi) of Coal Mines (Nationalisation) Act - Not maintainable - Appeal disallowed.
