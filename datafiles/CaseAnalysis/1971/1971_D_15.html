Darshan Singh Ram Kishan v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

02 September 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, I.D. Dua, Subimal Chandra Roy

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 135; (1971) SCC (Cr) 628; (1971) 2 SCC 654; AIR 1971 SC 2372; 1971 CRLJ 1697; [1972] 1 S.C.R. 571; 1972 UJ 54

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal- Indian Penal Code,1860,s. 120B - Criminal Procedure Code,1898,s. 196 A(2) - Offence u/s 120 B, IPC, 1860 - Cognizance - Consent - Whether committal order vitiated for want of consent? - Where a charge-sheet filed by police disclosed offence of abetment forging and impersonation and a magistrate after perusing only records submitted by police u/s 173 CrPC,1898 in which none of witnesses alleged either directly or indirectly that accused persons had entered into criminal conspiracy, considered that material before him, framed a charge of criminal conspiracy for forging passport and committed accused for Offence u/s 110 B, IPC,1860 it was quite clear that he took cognizance of offence alleged in chargesheet and not of Offence u/s 120 B- Held, committal order was not vitiated for want of Consent - Appeal dismissed.
