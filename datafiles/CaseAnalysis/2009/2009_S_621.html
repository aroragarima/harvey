Subramaniam v State Of Tamil Nadu & Anr

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 774; (2009) 14 SCC 415; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1493; 2009 (3) CGLJ 290; 2009 CRLJ 3002; JT 2009 (7) SC 403; 2009 (4) MLJ(Crl) 60; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 481; 2009(7) SCALE 317; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 725

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Medical Witness

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 498A - Dowry Prohibition Act, s. 4 - Acquittal by Trial Court - Acquittal confirmed u/ss. 498A IPC and 4 of DP Act, but acquittal u/s. 302 reversed and conviction thereunder awarded by High Court - Judgement of HC whether proper? - Held, No - Deceased and appellant, wife and husband - Death after about three years of marriage - Both were together at the time of death - Plea of alibi was raised by appellant - Contentions heard and impugned judgement and evidences perused - (A) Cause of death as coming out of PM report noted which was 'the death is due to Asphyxia. May be due to smothering.' - PW10/Dr proved PM report; contradiction found between her statement on PM report and admissions made in cross examination - PW10/Dr admitted that if death was caused by asphyxia among other things the eyes would be open and tongue would get protruded, but nothing of that sort found on the deceased - Absence of symptoms of asphyxia admitted by PW10/Dr. - Court posits, whether in view of absence of some symptoms as accepted by the autopsy surgeon, death could be caused by asphyxia? - Passages from Modi's Medical Jurisprudence and Toxicology, 23rd Edition, noted - Court gave finding that as large number of symptoms were absent which ordinarily point out to the cause of death of asphyxia by smothering, a different conclusion has to be drawn - Most of the symptoms noticed by Modi should have remained present but were not - Further, frothy fluid discharge was found oozing from the mouth and nostrils of the deceased, but nothing was found on the pillow which was allegedly used to smother - (B) HC had given the finding that as both were living together and were last seen together, husband has to give explanation as to cause of death of his wife - Finding whether proper? - Held, No - Entirety of the situation should be taken into consideration; though it is a strong circumstance but that by alone in absence of any evidence of violence on the deceased cannot be held to be conclusive - No mark of violence found in the instant case; appellant was found innocent by both the Courts below u/ss. 498A IPC and 4 of DP Act; prosecution story suggesting strong motive on part of appellant to commit the murder, thus ruled out - Plea of alibi raised by appellant was found to be weak but Court said that it could be additional circumstance but before such additional circumstance is taken into consideration, the prosecution must prove all other circumstances to prove his guilt - Circumstances brought on record by the prosecution found to be lacking in arriving at a definite conclusion that appellant and appellant alone had committed the offence - Impugned judgment unsustainable - Appeal allowed.
