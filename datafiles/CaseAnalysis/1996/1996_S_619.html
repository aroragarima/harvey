Sasa Musa Sugar Works and Others v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 July 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2951; (1996) 9 SCC 681; AIR 1997 SC 188; JT 1996 (6) SC 329; 1996(5) SCALE 155; [1996] Supp3 S.C.R. 149; 1996 (5) Supreme 509

Case Digest

Subject: Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Madras Commercial Crops Act, 1953, Bihar Agriculture Produce Markets Act, 1960, Bihar Agricultural Produce Markets (Amendment) Act, 1992, Bihar Agricultural Produce Markets (Amendment) Act, 1993, Bihar Agricultural Produce Market Rules, 1975

Summary: (A) Agriculture & Agri. Products - Bihar Agricultural Produce Markets (Amendment) Act, 1992, s. 33M - Bihar Agriculture Produce Markets Act, 1960, ss. 15, 27, 3, 3(1), 39, 4, 4(1), 4A, 5 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(g), 19(6), 213 - Customs Act, 1962 - Essential Commodities Act, 1955, s. 2(a) - Madras Commercial Crops Act, 1953 - Several appeals and special leave petition involved common question of law and they arose out of the common judgment passed by the Division Bench of the Patna HC - By the impugned judgment, the Division Bench of the Patna HC allowed in part the W.P.s filed by several sugar mills of Bihar challenging the validity of ss. 4A and 4B inserted by the 1993 Act, s. 33M as inserted by the 1992 Act, notification dt. 31-8-1992 issued u/s. 4 of the 1960 Act(Markets Act), and also challenging the validity of imposition of market fee under the Markets Act in view of exemption of all the sugar mills in Bihar from the provision of s. 15 of the Markets Act - Whether or not the Notification dt. 21-5-1997 cancelling the earlier Notification dt. 2-5-1977 automatically restored the state of affairs which had existed prior to 2-5-1977, and if it did so, then did it mean that sugar was automatically included in the Schedule under the Markets Act and, became subjected to levy of market fee as agricultural produce in 'the specified area' under the Act - Held, it was noted in view of s. 4A which had been inserted in the Market Act by specifically indicating in s. 2 of Amending Act that the said s. shall always be deemed to have been inserted ,deletion of an item and subsequent inclusion of the same u/s. 39 was to be made in accordance with s. 39 read with s. 4A - Sub-s. (2) of s 4A made it imperative that deletion could be made after hearing objection - Declaration of annulment of notification dt. 2-5-1977 flows from s. 4A (2) - Both the parts of s. 4B therefore, if deletion is non-est, annulment of notification dt. 2-5-1977 is a matter of course - HC also held that even if it was assumed that the effect of notification dt. 21-5-1977 was to add sugar in the Schedule of the Markets Act, such inclusion did not authorise imposition of market fee u/s. 27 of the said Act because it was necessary to comply with the requirements u/s. 3 and of the Markets Act before including any item in the Schedule of the Markets Act - Appeals disposed of.

(B) Agriculture & Agri. Products - Bihar Agricultural Produce Markets (Amendment) Act, 1992, s. 33M - Bihar Agriculture Produce Markets Act, 1960, ss. 15, 27, 3, 3(1), 39, 4, 4(1), 4A, 5 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(g), 19(6), 213 - Customs Act, 1962 - Essential Commodities Act, 1955, s. 2(a) - Madras Commercial Crops Act, 1953 - Whether even if it was assumed that Notification dt. 21-5-1977 validly cancelled the earlier Notification dt. 2-5-1977 which resulted in the inclusion of sugar in the Schedule, was it necessary that procedure etc. contemplated by ss. 3 and 4 of the Act had to be applied afresh before levying market fee - Held, even if notification dt. 21-5-1977 purporting to rescuing the notification dt. 2-5-1977, by which sugar was deleted form the Schedule, was held invalid for the reason indicated by the HC, such deletion stood invalidated u/s. 4A (2)- Hence, declaration of annulment of notification dt. 2-5-1977 flowed from s. 4A (2) - The result was that sugar must be deemed to be always in the Schedule in respect of which controls had been operative - Both the parts of s. 4B therefore, if deletion was non-est, annulment of notification dt. 2-5-1977 was a matter of course - Similarly, levy and realisation of market fee on the items which were included in the Schedule, but exclusion of which was of no consequence, could not be held invalid - Appeals disposed of.
