M. Chockalingam and Another v Commissioner of Income Tax Madras and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 October 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 481; AIR 1963 SC 1456; [1963] 48 ITR 34; 1963 (1) MLJ(SC) 65; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 599

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Advance Payment

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 35 - Rectification of Assessment - Enhancement of assessment - Notice of Hearing - Opportunity of being heard - Assessment was passed by respondent against appellant - Rectification of assessment done without giving any notice to appellant which resulted in enhancement of assessment - HC affirmed action of respondent - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Income-tax Officer to give notice and a hearing to an assessee when effect of rectification would be enhancement of assessment - Held, authorities acting under the Indian Income-tax Act have to act judicially and one of the requirements of judicial action is to give a fair hearing to a person before deciding against him - S. 35 of the Act itself makes it incumbent upon Income-tax Officer to give notice and a hearing to an assessee when effect of rectification would be enhancement of assessment - Appeals allowed.
