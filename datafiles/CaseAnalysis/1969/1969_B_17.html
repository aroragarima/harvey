State of Maharashtra v Baishankar Avalram Joshi and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 399; (1969) 1 SCC 804; AIR 1969 SC 1302; 1970 (20) FLR 289; 1969 (10) G.L.R. 851; 1969 LabIC 1534; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 917; 1969 SLR 268

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Bombay Reorganisation Act, 1960

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(2) - Bombay Reorganisation Act, 1960, ss. 60 and 61 - (A) Whether non-supply of copy of report of enquiry held against plaintiff contravened art. 311(2)? - Held, failure on part of competent authority to provide plaintiff with a copy of report of Enquiry Officer amounted to denial of reasonable opportunity contemplated by art. 311(2) of Constitution - (B) Liability in respect of arrears of pay on State of Maharashtra - Held, that some elements of relationship between a public servant and Govt. are based on contract within meaning of s. 60 of Bombay Re-organisation Act, 1960 - In particular, liability to pay salary, when it has been fixed, arises out of a contract to pay salary - Decree for arrears of salary more properly falls u/s. 60 - Appeal dismissed.
