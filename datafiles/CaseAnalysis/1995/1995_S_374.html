Abdul Aziz v State Of West Bengal And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

21 July 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 468; (1995) 6 SCC 47; (1995) SCC (Cr) 1022; AIR 1996 SC 3305; 1995 (4) CCR 114; 1995 (3) Crimes 738; 1996 CRLJ 2881; JT 1997 (10) SC 583; 1995(5) SCALE 169; [1995] Supp2 S.C.R. 299

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: TADA Act

Summary: Criminal - Terrorists and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 1(4) and 14 - (A) Constitutional validity - Continuation of trial of offenders even after lapse of Act - Offender who committed offences when Act was in force are offenders under Act and those who committed offences after expiry of Act are not offenders under Act - Can these two classes of persons be treated at par - Whether s. 1(4) is ultra vires - (B) Trial under Act - Allegation that Judge was entertaining bias mind against accused - Ground - Article written by concerned Judge in a magazine in an objective way analysing various provisions and purpose of Act - No biased view is discernible from such article - Police had provided police car for ensuring security of Judge and his family when hearing sensitive cases under TADA Act - Whether illegal and a ground to hold that judge would be biased and inclined to oblige police - Held, (A) Cannot be treated at par - Cannot be held ultra vires - (B) Cannot per se be illegal and not a ground to hold that judge would be biased - Appeal dismissed.
