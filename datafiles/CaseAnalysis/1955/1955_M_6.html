Machander, Son of Pandurang v State of Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

27 September 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 24; AIR 1955 SC 792; 1955 CRLJ 1644; 1956 (1) MLJ(SC) 25; 1956 SCJ 34; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 524

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, s. 342 - Conviction for murder challenged - Appellant has been on his trial in one way or another for over four and a half years - Held, while it is incumbent to see that guilty do not escape it is even more necessary to see that persons accused of crime are not indefinitely harassed - They must be given a fair and impartial trial - Error in present case is not a mere technicality - Appellant appeared to have been ready to disclose all, make a clean breast of everything, and yet police waited eight days before getting a confession judicially recorded - Not prepared to order a retrial in this case because of facts that appear here - Conviction and sentence are set aside - Appeal allowed.
