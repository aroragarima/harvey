Palraj v Divisional Controller, NEKRTC

Supreme Court of India

07 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 739; (2010) 10 SCC 347; 2011 (2) ACC 169; 2010 ACJ 2859; 2010 (6) AWC 6388; 2010 (127) FLR 655; JT 2010 (10) SC 94; 2010 (4) LLJ 609; 2011 (4) MLJ(SC) 719; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 384; 2010(9) SCALE 169; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 89; 2010 (6) SLR 99

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923, schd. IV and s, 4 - Compensation for permanent and functional disability - Appellant-Driver in Karnataka State Road Transport Corporation, met with an accident in which he sustained grievous injuries - The Medical Officer who examined the appellant came to the conclusion that he had suffered 65% of total body disability and 20% of functional disability - Taking the above percentage of disability, both permanent and functional, the Commissioner made an Award granting compensation amounting to Rs.1,75,970/-, together with interest @12% per annum, to the appellant - On appeal, HC held that Commissioner, Workmen's Compensation, had erroneously taken 85% to be the extent of disability suffered by the appellant and that the same ought to have been 20% instead and reduced the amount of compensation to Rs.41,404.80/- - Appellant filed appeal contending that the doctors had certified that the appellant was 100% disabled as far as his functioning as a Driver was concerned and that his total disability had been found to be 65% while his functional disability was assessed at 20% - Appellant further contended that taking the two together the Commissioner had rightly found the appellant to have suffered 85% disability that entitled him to a sum of Rs.1,75,970/- - Whether contentions of the appellant could be accepted and order of the HC could be set aside? - Held, although the appellant has lost the use of his legs for the purpose of driving a vehicle, which could be said to be total disablement so far as driving of a vehicle is concerned, he is in a position to earn a living other than by functioning as a driver, which, in fact, he is currently doing, having been posted as a Peon by the respondent - Loss of earning capacity has to be computed keeping in mind the alternate employment given to the appellant on the same salary as he was enjoying while performing the duty of a bus driver - The same cannot be ignored in computing the amount of compensation which the appellant was entitled to - Hence, impugned order of HC to be maintained but percentage of functional disablement to be modified from 20% to at least 35%, having regard to the appellant's mobility on account of the medical treatment received after the accident and also because of the appellant's loss of future earnings and also promotion - Appeal disposed of.
