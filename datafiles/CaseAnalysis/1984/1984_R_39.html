Ravi Dutt Sharma v Ratan Lal Bhargava

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 109; (1984) 2 SCC 75; AIR 1984 SC 967; 1984 (1) RCJ 325; 1984 (1) RCR(Rent) 357; 1984 (1) RentLR 331; 1984(1) SCALE 285; [1984] 2 S.C.R. 614; 1985 UJ 408

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, chap. IIIA, ss. 25A, 25B, 25C, 14(1)(e) and 14A - Slum Areas (Improvement and Clearance) Act, 1956, s. 19(1)(a) - Tenancy - (A) Whether suits for eviction by the landlords were not competent in view of want of permission u/s.19(1)(a) from the competent Authority under the Slum Act - Held, is not necessary - (B) Constitutionality of new procedure incorporated in chap. III-A of Delhi Rent Control Act - Held, is in public interest and is not violative of art. 14 - Appeal dismissed.
