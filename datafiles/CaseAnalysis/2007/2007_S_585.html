Shanti Lal v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 988; (2007) 11 SCC 243; (2008) 1 SCC (Cr) 1; 2007 (3) ALT 235; 2008 CRLJ 386; 2007 (4) JCC (Narcotics) 145; JT 2007 (12) SC 1; 2007 (1) KLT 503; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 563; 2007(11) SCALE 686; 2007 (7) SCJ 585; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 727

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Municipal Magistrate, Recovery Of Fine, Bihar Wakfs Act, 1947, Towns Nuisances Act (Madras), 1889, Calcutta Municipal Act, 1923

Summary: Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 8 r/w 18 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Whether a Court of law can order a convict to remain in jail in default of payment of fine? - Held, there is no express power in a Court to order imprisonment in default of payment of fine - It has been held since more than a century that such an order can be passed by a competent Court of law having power to impose fine as one of the punishments - Though an amount of payment of fine of rupees one lakh which is minimum as specified in s. 18 of the Act cannot be reduced in view of the legislative mandate, ends of justice would be met if we retain that part of the direction, but order that in default of payment of fine of rupees one lakh, the appellant shall undergo rigorous imprisonment for six months instead of three years as ordered by the trial court and confirmed by the High Court - Appeal partly allowed.
