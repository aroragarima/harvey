Swan Gold Mining Limited v Hindustan Copper Limited

Supreme Court of India

22 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 644; (2015) 5 SCC 739; 2014(6) ALL MR 971; 2014 (6) AWC 5915; 2014 (4) CalLT(SC) 77; 2014 (4) CLT(SC) 287; 2015 (1) CompLJ 447; JT 2014 (12) SC 169; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 565; 2014(11) SCALE 33; [2014] 10 S.C.R. 559

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 34 - Arbitral award - Challenged - Respondent and appellants executed contract for re-commissioning and operation of Mine and Concentrator Plant - Dispute arose between parties - Arbitrator held that clause related to payment of taxes was deleted by appellant's representative on 19-1-2007 and since work order was acknowledged, it was binding on appellant - Petition was filed before HC u/s. 34 of the Act - Single Judge upheld award of arbitrator and dismissed appellants petition - Appellant filed appeal before DB - Appeal was dismissed by DB - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order recorded by DB was sustainable .

Held, under work order cl. 4.9.1 quoted specifically mentions therein that the rate quoted by the appellant was inclusive of sales tax, service tax and the other taxes. Representative of the appellant signed the Work Order and acknowledged and admitted the terms and conditions for the said work. It is evident that the appellant has accepted the liability of payment of excise duty, sales tax, service tax and other taxes and hence it cannot be held that the cl. 4.9.1 of the Work Order is inconsistent with the terms and conditions of contract documents. Arbitrator has gone in detail of the dispute raised by appellant and rightly came to conclusion that the responsibility on the appellant is to abide by the terms and conditions of the Work Order. HC rightly came to the conclusion that there is no patent illegality in the Award passed by the Arbitrator which needs interference u/s. 34 of the Act. Hence, there was no merit in instant appeal. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Arbitrator appointed by the parties is the final judge of the facts. The finding of facts recorded by him cannot be interfered with on the ground that the terms of the contract were not correctly interpreted by him.
