Supreme Court of India

28 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, Arijit Pasayat, Ashok Bhan, B.N. Kirpal, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1851; (2002) 8 SCC 237; AIR 2003 SC 87; JT 2002 (8) SC 389; 2002(7) SCALE 614; 2002 (6) SLT 599; [2002] Supp3 S.C.R. 366; 2002 (7) Supreme 437

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Council Of States, House Of The People, Purity, Kerala Education Bill, 1957, Representation of People (Amendment) Act, 1951

Summary: Election - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 143 and 174(1) - Constitution (First Amendment) Act, 1951 - Government of India Act, 1935 - Representation of People Act, 1951 - (i) Whether art. 174 is subject to the decision of the Election Commission of India u/art. 324 as to the schedule of elections of the Assembly? - (ii) Can the Election Commission of India frame a schedule for the elections to an Assembly on the premise that any infraction of the mandate of art. 174 would be remedied by a resort to art. 356 by the President? - (iii) Is the Election Commission of India under a duty to carry out the mandate of art. 174 of the Constitution, 1950 by drawing upon all the requisite resources of the Union and the State to ensure free and fair elections ?
