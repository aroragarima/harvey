Himadri Coke and Petro Limited v Soneko Developers Private Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1059; (2005) 12 SCC 364; 2006 (1) DRTC 619

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Non-deposit of sale amount, Fresh sale

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Auction purchase - Non-deposit of sale amount - Fresh sale - Legality - Respondent no. 1 was auction purchaser of properties belonged to judgment-debtor (respondent no. 3) but, failed to deposit balance amount and Tribunal cancelled the sale to respondent no. 1 - Aggrieved respondent no. 1 filed an appeal before Presiding Officer and in the meantime appellant (company) submitted an offer for purchase - However, HC by impugned order allowed the application of respondent no. 1 and confirmed sale in favour of respondent no. 1 - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified - Held, respondent no. 1 was bound by the terms and conditions of sale as was the Authority concerned - It was not up to them to extend the dates for submission of balance price when there was no clause in terms and conditions of sale allowing the Authority to extend the time beyond the period specified in advertisement for making the initial deposit or balance price - Appellant could not have been permitted to enter into the arena by making an offer which was not in response to advertisement - Entire sale should be set aside - Sale should be advertised in 2 newspapers and parties were entitled to participate and bid for property - Appeal disposed of.
