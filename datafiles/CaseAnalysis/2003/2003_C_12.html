CDC Financial Services (Mauritius) Limited v BPL Communications Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1762; (2003) 12 SCC 140

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: International arbitration

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss.5, 11 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Securities Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1956 - International Arbitration - Interim relief - Entitled to - Respondents no. 7 to 11 were shareholders of respondent no. 1 - Respondents no. 7 to 11 and appellant entered into an agreement - Appellant became entitled to enforce pledge agreement against shareholders - Shareholders were called upon to deposit their shares - Shareholders did not comply with demand on ground that preconditions for enforcement of pledge agreement was fulfilled - Appellants approached SC for appointment of an arbitrator and the same was allowed - Appellant filed statement of claim before arbitrator - Arbitrator passed order - Respondent no. 1 filed writ petition before Single Judge for investigating the transactions - Single Judge did not grant any interim relief - Respondent no. 1 preferred appeal before DB - DB passed order of status quo - Said order was communicated to arbitrator - Arbitrator replied to remove him stating that it was open for him to decide on that issue u/s. 13(2) of 1996, Act - Accordingly respondent no. 1 filed interlocutory applications before DB - Interim order granted was also directed to continue until further orders - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified - Held, HC should have regard to s. 5 of 1996, Act before granting reliefs it did - U/s. 5 of 1996 Act Courts were restrained from interfering with arbitration except in manner provided in 1996 Act - Orders passed by HC would amount to violation of this mandate was not seriously disputed by respondents - Orders passed by HC was set aside without expressing merits of contentions of parties in any manner - Respondents were restrained from moving any applications in pending petition which would have effect of interfering with continuance and conclusion of arbitration proceedings - Appeals allowed.
