Commissioner Of Customs, Calcutta v South India Television Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

09 July 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 848; (2007) 6 SCC 373; 2007 (3) CalLT(SC) 62; 2007 (214) E.L.T. 3; JT 2007 (9) SC 294; 2007(9) SCALE 142; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 95

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax; Trade

Summary: Customs - Rule 8, Customs Valuation Rules, 1988 - Section 2(41), 14(1), 14(1A), Customs Act, 1962 - Valuation - Assessable value - Differential duty - Imported Ceramic Capacitors and Diodes - Allegation of under-invoicing value of goods to evade huge amount of the Government's revenue - No evidence from the side of the Department showing contemporaneous imports at higher price - Importer contended that the charge of undervaluation can not be based on xerox copies of the declarations which were not even certified by the competent authority - Held, charge of under-invoicing has to be supported by evidence of prices of contemporaneous imports of like goods and it is for the Department to prove that the apparent is not the real - Casting suspicion on invoice produced by the importer is not sufficient to reject it as evidence of value of imported goods - When there is no evidence of contemporaneous imports at a higher price, the invoice price is liable to be accepted - Impugned order upheld.
