State of Punjab and Another v Shri Sukh Raj Bahadur

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 392; AIR 1968 SC 1089; 1968 CRLJ 687; 1968 LabIC 1286; 1970 (1) LLJ 373; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 234; 1968 SLR 701

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Punjab Civil Service (Executive Branch) Rules, 1930, rr. 21 and 22 - Punjab Civil Service Rules - Punjab Civil Services (Punishment and Appeal) Rules, 1952, r. 9 - Validity of order removing him from Punjab Civil Service - Held, respondent did not cease to be a probationer at time when impugned order was passed on him - Although period of probation was fixed at 18 months and although respondent had passed all departmental examinations, he could not merely, as a consequence thereof, be considered to be holding a post substantively - Apart from question as to right of respondent under Punjab Civil Service Rules, respondent could not complain merely because he had been reverted to post formerly held by him - He would have cause to complain if he could show that order of reversion was by way of punishment - Appeal allowed.
