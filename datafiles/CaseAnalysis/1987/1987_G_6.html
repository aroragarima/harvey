Ganesh Dass Sreeram, Etc. A v Income Tax Officer, ’A’ Ward, Shillong And Others Etc.

Supreme Court of India

30 October 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, R.S. Pathak, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28790; (1987) Supp SCC 442; (1988) SCC (Tax) 62; AIR 1988 SC 427; 1987 (66) CTR 135; [1988] 169 ITR 221; JT 1987 (4) SC 208; 1987(2) SCALE 894; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 689; [1987] 35 TAXMAN 36

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Unregistered Firm

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 139(1) Proviso, cl.(iii)(a), 139(2) Proviso and 139(4) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Income Tax - Charging of interest for late filing of returns - (A) Whether application for extension of time and granting of extension is a pre-condition - (B) Validity, when entire amount of tax assessed paid in shape of advance tax - (C) Constitutionality of provision for treating registered firms as unregistered firms for purpose of payment of interest - Held, not a condition precedent - Charging of interest is not justified - No discrimination - Order accordingly.
