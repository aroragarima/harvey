Noor Sk. Bhikan v State Of Maharashtra & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 443; (2011) 7 SCC 589; 2012 (1) Bom.C.R. 768; 2011 (3) CLT(SC) 237; JT 2011 (11) SC 432; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 768; 2011(7) SCALE 374; [2011] 7 S.C.R. 1070

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Re-Settlement Act, 1965

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Land acquisition - Error in allotment of alternative site - Appellant's house property and agricultural land was acquired by respondent State for development purposes - Appellant was issued a certificate whereby he had been allotted alternative land (suit land) - 5th respondent filed an application before the Collector pointing out that the suit land was her land and the respondent authority committed an error in marking the boundaries and possession of alternative land to appellant - Collector verified the 5th respondent's representation and found that while handing over possession of alternative land the Circle Inspector had committed an error in marking the boundaries and possession of alternative site handed over to the appellant - Collector, being satisfied about the mistake committed by the Circle Inspector, ordered that the area allotted to the appellant needed a change - Tehsildar, in furtherance thereto, issued an order to the Circle Inspector to take corrective steps - Appellant approached the HC challenging the notices and he averred that he remained in possession of the allotted land and he had made a substantial investment thereon - HC dismissed writ petition, however, directed the respondent authorities to allot the appellant another alternative site - Whether the impugned HC order was proper - Held, HC did not accept the contentions raised on behalf of the appellant in regard to the allotment of another alternative site, however, HC granted relief to the appellant in relation to an alternative site - Right of the appellant was to seek agricultural land under the provisions of the Re-Settlement Act, 1965 and in so far as that right was protected, the appellant could not ask for a particular land - Some distance between the offered land and the land which was in dispute had rightly not been considered to be a sufficient ground for requiring the Court to grant the relief prayed for in its terms - Grant of relief in relation to the alternate land should not be faulted with inasmuch as if there was a mistake committed by the Revenue Authorities which was subsequently corrected, no advantage could be claimed by the appellant in that regard, particularly when the mistake was in relation to a root controversy - Land which was not subject matter of the acquisition could not be treated as the land having been offered to the appellant validly and in accordance with law - HC passed multi-fold directions in relation to granting of alternate land and conducting of an enquiry by the competent authority as well - Thus, the directions sufficiently took care of the interest of the appellant - Impugned HC judgment was well-reasoned and even granted the appropriate relief to the appellant - HC, not only gives appropriate relief to the appellant but also taking care of the correction of errors and enquiry into the relevant issues by the concerned authorities - As far as the claim of compensation placed by the appellant is concerned, again it had been left for the Government to decide as per its policy - No scope for interference on HC order - Appeal dismissed.
