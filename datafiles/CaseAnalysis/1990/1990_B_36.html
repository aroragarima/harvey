Bharat Cooking Coal Limited v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 August 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 377; (1990) 4 SCC 557; JT 1990 (3) SC 533; 1990(2) SCALE 256; [1990] 3 S.C.R. 744

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Coking Coal, Extraction, Coal Bearing Areas (Acquisition And Development) Amendment Act, 1957

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Land & Property - Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957, ss.5, 18 - Coal Bearing Areas (Acquisition and Development) Act, 1957, s 7 - Coal Mines - Slurry ponds - Briquettes - Commercial value - Appellant extracted coal and washed it in washery near coal mine - Slurry deposited in slurry ponds constructed nearby - State Govt. granted lease to Respondents for collection of slurry - Appellants alleged slurry deposited belonged to Appellants - Appellant filed writ petition against grant of lease which was dismissed - Hence instant Appeal - Whether Appellants were entitled to relief against grant of lease to Respondents by State Govt - Held, plot where slurry ponds were situated belonged to Appellants as it was acquired by Central Govt - State Govt had no authority to grant lease of slurry ponds to Respondents - Slurry escaped from Appellants washeries were minerals and were within exclusive jurisdiction of Central Govt - Impugned settlements made in favour of Respondents by State Govt was held illegal - Respondent/lessees had no right or title to collect slurry deposits -Appeals disposed of.
