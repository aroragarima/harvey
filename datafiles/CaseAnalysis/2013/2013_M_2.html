Anil Kumar and others v M. K. Aiyappa and another

Supreme Court of India

01 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Radhakrishnan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 648; (2013) 10 SCC 705; 2014 CRLJ 1; 2013 (6) KarLJ 1; 2013 (4) KLT 125; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 586; 2013 (4) RLW 3604; 2013(12) SCALE 283; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 869

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 5(4) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 190, 200 and 156(3) - Prevention of Corruption - Power of Magistrate to order investigation - Appellants filed a private complaint u/s. 200 of CrPC before the Additional City Civil and Special Judge for prevention of corruption - Said complaint of the appellants was that the respondent No. 1 with mala fide intention passed an order in connivance with other officers and restored valuable land in favour of a private person - Special Judge referred the complaint to Deputy Superintendent of Police-3 Karnataka Lokayukta, Bangalore Urban u/s. 156(3) of CrPC for investigation and to report - On appeal, HC, quashed the order passed by the Special Judge, as well as the complaint filed against the appellants - Hence, instant appeals - (A) Whether the Special Judge/Magistrate was justified in referring a private complaint made u/s. 200 of CrPC for investigation by the Deputy Superintendent of Police-Karnataka Lokayukta, in exercise of powers conferred u/s. 156(3) of CrPC without the production of a valid sanction order u/s. 19 of Act - Held, a Special Judge was deemed to be a Magistrate u/s. 5(4) of the Act and, therefore, clothed with all the magisterial powers provided under the CrPC - When a private complaint was filed before the Magistrate, he had 2 options - Magistrate may take cognizance of the offence u/s. 190 of CrPC or proceed further in enquiry or trial - A Magistrate, who was otherwise competent to take cognizance, without taking cognizance u/s. 190 of CrPC, may direct an investigation u/s. 156(3) of CrPC - Magistrate, who was empowered u/s. 190 of CrPC to take cognizance, alone had the power to refer a private complaint for police investigation u/s. 156(3) of CrPC - (B) Whether, in the instant case, the requirement of sanction was a pre-condition for ordering investigation u/s. 156(3) of CrPC, even at a pre-cognizance stage - Held, s. 19(3) had an object to achieve, which applies in circumstances where a Special Judge had already rendered a finding, sentence or order - In such an event, it should not be reversed or altered by a court in appeal, confirmation or revision on the ground of absence of sanction - That does not mean that the requirement to obtain sanction was not a mandatory requirement - Once it was noticed that there was no previous sanction, the Magistrate could not order investigation against a public servant while invoking powers u/s. 156(3) of CrPC - No error in the order passed by the HC - Appeals dismissed.
