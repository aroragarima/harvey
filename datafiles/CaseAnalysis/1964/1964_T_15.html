Â Hukumchand Mills Limited v State of Madhya Bharat and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 107; AIR 1964 SC 1329; 1964 (1) ITJ 562; [1964] 52 ITR 583; 1964 (1) SCJ 561; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 857

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution of India, 1950 - Finance Act, 1950 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Madhya Bharat Taxes on Income (Validation) Act, 1954 - Indore Industrial Tax Rules 1927 - Assessments - Notification - Validity of amendments - Discriminatory in nature - Appellant was a cotton mill and in 1927 a tax was imposed on cotton mills - Procedure under Tax Rules provided for board of assessing officers - Authorities under Tax Rules made assessments under Indian IncomeTax Act - Validity of certain provisions of 1947 Rules and assessments made for years 1940 to 1948 - Appellant challenged validity of assessments made against under Tax Rules - Hence, instant appeal - Whether validity of the amendments made in Tax Rules was justified - Held, amendments which were made in Tax Rules could be justified on basis of Act - Mere mistake in opening part of notification in reciting wrong source of power does not affect validity of amendments made in Tax Rules could be upheld u/s.5 of Act as regulation - Validating Act had done and failed to see grounds - By mistake however that provision was overlooked and old assessments were made by old officers under old law - Assessments were carried on by old officers under old law and Validating Act specifically validates such assessments - Assessments have not been validated by Validating Act - Validating Act could be said to be discriminatory in nature - Validating Act was passed only when certain things have been done which require validation - Appeal dismissed.
