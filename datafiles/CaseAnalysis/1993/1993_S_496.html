Lakhjit Singh and Another v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1041; (1994) Supp1 SCC 173; (1994) SCC (Cr) 235; AIR 1993 SCW 2938

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Crminal Procedure Code, s. 313 - Evidence Act, s. 113-A - Indian Penal Code, ss. 302 and 306 - Deceased Kailo was the wife of A-1- three accused were demanding dowry and they used to ill-treat the deceased - Trial Court taking the circumstances into consideration and relying on the post-mortem evidence as well the chemical examiner's report, held that it was a case of poisoning and the circumstances were enough to convict all the three accused and accordingly convicted them - Two appellants, viz., Lakhjit Singh (original accused 1) and Sukhpal Kaur (original accused 2) along with one Vir Singh (original accused 3) were tried for offence punishable u/s. 302 IPC, 1860 and convicted by the trial court and sentenced to undergo imprisonment for life, also to pay a fine of Rs. 2000/- and in default to undergo six months rigorous imprisonment - All of them preferred an appeal, but the High Court acquitted Vir Singh (A-3) and confirmed the convictions of the two appellants - Special leave granted - Held, conviction of appellants u/s. 302 and sentence of imprisonment for life are set aside - They are convicted u/s. 306 IPC, 1860 and each sentenced to 5 years rigorous imprisonment and sentence of fine of Rs 2000/- with default clause are confirmed - If the appellants have already served out the sentence of 5 years, they need not be sent to jail again - Appeal disposed of.
