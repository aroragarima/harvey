Kerala State Cooperative, Marketing Federation v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

13 May 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.C. Agrawal, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 736; (1998) 5 SCC 48; AIR 1998 SC 2303; 1998 (147) CTR 29; [1998] 231 ITR 814; JT 1998 (4) SC 145; 1998(3) SCALE 578; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 443; 1998 (5) SLT 94; 1998 (5) Supreme 85; [1998] 98 TAXMAN 313

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Corporate - Income Tax Act, 1961, s.80P - Exemption from tax - Held, only condition required for qualifying the assessee's income for exemption was that the assessee's business must be that of marketing, the marketing must be of agricultural produce and the agricultural produce must be of the members of the assessee society - Has to be seen if an item falls under one of the exemption heads - Appeal dismissed.
