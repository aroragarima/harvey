Ram Kanai Jamini Ranjan Pal Private Limited v Member, Board of Revenue, West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 65; (1976) 3 SCC 369; AIR 1976 SC 1545; 1976 (38) STC 1; [1976] Supp S.C.R. 110; 1976 TAXLR 1773

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 34 - Kerala General Sales Tax Rules, 1950, r. 33 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 33B and 115 - Madras General Sales Tax Rules, 1939, r. 14-a - Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941, ss. 11(1), 14(1), 20(1), 20(3) and 21(3) - Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, ss. 5(2)(b), 12(2) and 21(2)(b) - Whether revisional power of Addl. Commr. u/s. 20(3) of Act was limited one and he is not competent to act as an original assessing authority and reassess gross turnover by taking into consideration addl. material comprising fresh sources of revenue which was not available to assessing officer? - Held, combined reading of provisions of s. 20(3) of Act and r. 80A of Rules would show that Commr., Commercial Taxes, is empowered to make or cause to be made such enquiry as he may think fit for proper exercise of revisional jurisdiction conferred on him u/s. 20(3) of Act - (B) Whether Addl. Commr., could not admit or rely on report of Commercial Tax Officer, Central Section, based on enquiry u/s. 14(1) of Act which was initiated long before filing of revision application before Commr., Commercial Taxes? - Held, power u/r. 14-A of Madras General Sales Tax Rules, 1939, to make further enquiry in cases where after being satisfied about illegality or impropriety of order or irregularity in proceeding, revising authority thinks it just for rectifying defect to do so does not amount to enlarging jurisdiction conferred by s. 12(2) - Appeal dismissed.
