Jindal Vijayanagar Steel (JSW Steel Limited ) v Jindal Praxair Oxygen Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

29 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 451; (2006) 11 SCC 521; 2006 (6) Bom.C.R. 309; JT 2006 (8) SC 230; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 295; 2006(8) SCALE 668; [2006] Supp5 S.C.R. 579

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 9 - Original Civil Jurisdiction of the Bombay High Court - Plea that High Court of Bombay has wrongly assumed jurisdiction to entertain petitions under s. 9 despite holding that the entire cause of action has arisen outside its territorial jurisdiction - Held, provisions of ss. 16, 17 and 20 of the Code of CPC are inapplicable to Chartered High Courts exercising Original Civil Jurisdiction under the Letters Patent - Under Clause 12 of the Letters Patent, the Bombay High Court would have jurisdiction to entertain and try an Arbitration Petition even if no cause of action has arisen within its jurisdiction, provided the Respondent has an office at Mumbai - Bombay High Court has jurisdiction to entertain s. 9 application of the respondents - Appeal dismissed.
