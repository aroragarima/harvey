Lok Prahari, throught its General Secretary, S. N. Shukla v State of U. P. and others

Supreme Court of India

21 November 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

D.Y. Chandrachud, T.S. Thakur, A.M. Khanwilkar

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 840; (2017) 1 SCC 244; AIR 2016 SC 5593; 2017(1) ALL MR 901; 2016 (6) AWC 6403; JT 2016 (11) SC 177; 2016 (8) MLJ 659; 2016(12) SCALE 195; [2016] 8 S.C.R. 363

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Legislative Assembly, Constitution of India, 1950, Uttar Pradesh District Planning Committee Act, 1999

Summary: Parliament & Legislature - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136, 226, 243ZD - Uttar Pradesh District Planning Committee Act, 1999 - Scheme implementation - Directions issued - Validity - Appellant filed writ petition before HC challenging the legality of Vidhayak Nidhi Scheme in State which provides for annual budgetary grants to Members of Legislative Assembly and Legislative Council for facilitating development work in their constituencies - DB dismissed writ petition holding that judgment of Constitution Bench in Bhim Singh v. Union of India and others, 2010 Indlaw SC 342, was dispositive of the controversy - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, there could be no objection to State implementing a scheme of nature that was upheld by Constitution Bench in Bhim Singh, 2010 Indlaw SC 342, the safeguards which form a part of MPLAD Scheme should be duly considered so as to ensure that the role which was ascribed to district planning authorities and institutions of local self-governance was not denuded. SC was in agreement with the view of HC that Vidhayak Nidhi Scheme does not per se violate art. 243ZD of Constitution or the Act. Elected representatives have a vital role in democracy. They have an intrinsic connection with their constituencies and have a legitimate role to discharge in meeting the development needs of their constituencies. Art. 243ZD of Constitution does not exclude their role. On contrary, they perform a supplemental role by enhancing and supporting the work of institutions of local self-governance. However, it was in SC view necessary that the guidelines which were formulated by State Govt. were revisited and directions set out above were complied with so as to ensure that guidelines were in conformity with the spirit and underlying purpose of Parts IX and IXA of Constitution in terms as held by Constitution Bench of SC in Bhim Singh, 2010 Indlaw SC 342. The revised guidelines should apply to all projects to be undertaken hereafter under Vidhayak Nidhi Scheme. Such exercise should be completed by State Govt. not later than a period of 2 months. Appeal disposed of.
