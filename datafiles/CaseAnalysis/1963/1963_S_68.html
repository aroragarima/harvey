State of Bihar v Kameshwar Prasad Verma

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, K.C. Das Gupta, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 132; AIR 1965 SC 575; 1965 CRLJ 494; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 183

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Jail Manual

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 134(1)(c) and 226 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 491 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 323, 324, 417 and 511 - Appeal against order of High Court in the matter of detention of one Bipat Gope - Bipat Gope, a resident of Patna, convicted u/ss. 323 and 324 r/w s. 511 IPC, 1860 and sentenced six months' rigorous imprisonment by HC - On appeal against acquittal u/s. 417 CrPC, 1898 not taken into custody, kept under armed guard in Patna Medical College Hospital on the ground of being seriously ill - On application by respondent he was released by District Magistrate under the rules of Jail Manual when his unexpired period of imprisonment was four months and three days - Sureties for Bipat Gope were called upon to produce him but they had failed to do so - Notices issued - By the same order nonbailable warrant for arrest was ordered- Bipat Gope moved a petition u/art. 226 against the order of the District Magistrate he prayed for an opportunity to present his case to HC and to avoid his maltreatment at the hands of the police of which he was apprehensive - No order on the record showing what the District Magistrate did but from the respondent's petition in the High Court it appears that the application before District Magistrate was taken up by the Senior Deputy Collector Patna, who ordered Bipat Gope to be taken into custody and sent him to jail-earlier petition filed in HC was withdrawn - HC heard the petition filed by the respondent, after some amendments, petition was allowed and Bipat Gope was ordered to be released from custody - HC held that the order of release by DM above referred to was an order for, his unconditional release and therefore he could not be rearrested -against that order that the State has come to this court by special leave u/art. 134(1)(c) - Held, it has not been shown in this case that there was any lawful authority under which Bipat Gope was rearrested and in the absence of such lawful authority his detention cannot be supported and is illegal remedy u/art. 226 rightly applicable to the facts of this case - Appeal dismissed.
