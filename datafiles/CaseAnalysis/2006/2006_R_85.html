Ramdas and Others v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1302; (2007) 2 SCC 170; AIR 2007 SC 155; 2007 (3) CalLT(SC) 1; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 967; 2006(11) SCALE 340

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Human Rights

Keywords: Family Life

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 376 - Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989, s. 3 - Appellants challenged their conviction u/ss. 376/34 - Whether delay in lodging report adversely affects case of prosecution? - Mere delay in lodging of report may not by itself be fatal to case of prosecution, but delay has to be considered in background of facts and circumstances in each case and is a matter of appreciation of evidence by court of fact - Time of occurrence, distance to police station, mode of conveyance available, are all factors which have a bearing on question of delay in lodging of report - Held, considered in light of an earlier report made by PW2 in relation to a non cognizable offence, second report lodged by her after a few days raises suspicion as to its truthfulness - Prosecution has not proved its case beyond reasonable doubt - Strong suspicion that case put forward by prosecution may not be true - In any event appellants are entitled to benefit of doubt - Conviction and sentence of appellants set aside - Appeal allowed.
