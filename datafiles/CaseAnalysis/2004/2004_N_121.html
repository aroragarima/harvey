Messrs Bharat Coking Coal Limited v New Govindpur Coal Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

22 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1558; (2004) 4 SCC 529; AIR 2004 SC 2967; 2004 (3) JhrCR(SC) 33; JT 2004 (5) SC 571; 2004(3) SCALE 519

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Mines & Minerals

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Coking Coal Mines (Nationalisation) Act, 1972, ss. 22, 23 and 25 - Coking Coal Nationalisation Laws (Amendment) Act, 1986 - Whether appellant could claim recovery of excess payment made to respondent in exercise of s. 25 of Coking Coal Mines (Nationalisation) Act, 1972? - Held, respondent during period of management i.e. from 17-10-1971 to 17-10-1971, earned large profits and received huge accounts from sale proceeds and from other sectors but could not and did not maintain proper accounts of receipts and payments - Appellant was authorised to claim u/s. 25 only on basis of these accounts - Auditor appointed by appellant company itself could not be satisfied regarding correctness of account and auditor statement of account failed in support of claim of Central Govt. - It is clear that accounts statement in relation to period of management by appellant is not correct and procedure prescribed u/s. 22 of Act, 1972 had not been followed at all - Appeal dismissed.
