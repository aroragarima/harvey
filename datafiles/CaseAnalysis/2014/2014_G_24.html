Richhpal Singh Meena v Ghasi @ Ghisa and others

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, Ranjana Prakash Desai

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 532; (2014) 8 SCC 918; AIR 2014 SC 3595; 2014 CRLJ 4339; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 61; 2014(8) SCALE 193; [2014] 9 S.C.R. 857

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Penal Code (Amendment) Act, 1870

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300, 302, 325, 447, 34, 72 - Culpable homicide - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Charge sheet was filed against respondents/convicts for an offence punishable u/ss. 302, 34 and 447 IPC - Trial Court convicted respondents for an offence punishable u/s. 302 IPC as well as for an offence punishable u/s. 447 IPC - They were acquitted of the charge framed u/ss. 302, 34 IPC - On appeal HC concluded that respondents could be convicted only u/ss. 325, 34 IPC and not u/s. 302, 34 IPC - HC also held that they could not be convicted u/s. 447 r/w s. 302 IPC - Hence instant appeal - Despite a death having resulted in an incident involving the respondents, their conviction for murder by the High Court has been altered to a conviction for voluntarily causing grievous hurt, ignoring or overlooking a homicide -

Held, in SC's opinion, a five-step inquiry was necessary (i) was there a homicide? (ii) If yes, was it a culpable homicide or a 'not-culpable homicide'? (iii) If it was a culpable homicide, was the offence one of culpable homicide amounting to murder (s. 300 IPC) or was it a culpable homicide not amounting to murder (s. 304 IPC)? (iv) If it was a 'not-culpable homicide' then a case u/s. 304-A IPC was made out (v) If it was not possible to identify the person who has committed the homicide, the provisions of s. 72 IPC might invoke - It was quite possible in some cases, such as that conclusive or specific evidence was lacking to actually pin down the person who has committed homicide (culpable or not culpable) - In such cases, the accused would have to be given the benefit of s. 72 IPC - Such cases arose if the investigation was defective or if the evidence was insufficient - But where it was possible to ascertain who was responsible for the homicide, the five-step inquiry can easily be carried out - There was a homicide, namely the death of S - Assailants gave two lathi blows to S which resulted in the fracture of his ribs and piercing of his lungs - The injuries were not accidental or unintentional - Assailants had a common intention of grievously injuring S and it was not as if they intended to cause some injury to him other that the ones inflicted - Opinion of Dr. confirmed that the injuries caused to S were sufficient to cause death in the normal course - Consequently, the homicide was a culpable homicide - It was clear that respondents were guilty of the murder of S, the offence falling u/s. 300 (thirdly) of IPC and punishable u/s. 302 IPC - SC set aside the decision of HC and restore the decision of the Trial Court and convict respondents the offence of murdering S - State would take necessary steps to apprehend the convicts so that they undergo life imprisonment as required by law - Appeal allowed.
