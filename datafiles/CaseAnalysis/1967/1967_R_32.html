Â Ramekbal Tiwary v Madan Mohan Tiwary and Another

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 148; AIR 1967 SC 1156; 1967 CRLJ 1076; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 368

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 207 A(6), 209, 253 and 437 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 149,307,326 and 338 - Unlawful assembly - Charge u/s. 307(A), IPC,1898 - Appeal against acquittal of accused by Magistrate - (A)Under what circumstances is the Magistrate competent to try a case triable exclusively by a Court of Session or High Court ? - Held, if at that stage of examining witnesses and accused, he is of opinion that no ground for committing accused for trial exists Magistrate can, after recording his reasons, discharge accused - If it appears to Magistrate that such person should be tried by himself or some other Magistrate he must proceed accordingly - This contingency will arise if Magistrate forms an opinion that no case exclusively triable by Court of Session is disclosed but a less serious offence which it is within competence of Magistrate to try is disclosed - (B) Whether Sessions Judge had jurisdiction to set aside order of Magistrate and to order accused to be committed for trial on major offence? - Magistrate framing charge of minor offence without giving express order discharging accused of charge under major offence but in his order giving reasons for holding that no case made out under major offence to justify order of commitment - Held, order of Magistrate was tantamount to implied order of discharge - Sessions Judge had jurisdiction to set aside order of Magistrate and to order accused to be committed for trial on major offence - (C)Whether order of Addl. Sessions Judge is ultra vires since he had no jurisdiction to set aside judgment of Magistrate acquitting appellant of charges u/s. 326 and 338, IPC,1860? - Addl. Sessions Judge has no authority to set aside acquittal of appellant under provisions of s.437, CrPC - But order of Addl. Sessions Judge has been affirmed by HC in its order under appeal and u/s. 439, CrPC - HC has jurisdiction to interfere with an order of acquittal in revision and to direct that accused may be retried on graver offence - Held, HC must be deemed to have itself set aside order of acquittal under this section - (D) Whether principle of 'Res judicata pro veritate accipitur' is applicable in the instant case ? - Appellant having been acquitted at first trial on charge of having ammunition in his possession, prosecution was bound to accept correctness of that verdict and was precluded from taking any step to challenge it at second trial - Appellant was no less entitled to rely on his acquittal in so far as it might be relevant in his defence - Further held, principle does not apply to present case because order of acquittal of appellant by Magistrate must be deemed to have been validly set aside by HC - Appeal allowed.
