Baul and Another v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 165; AIR 1968 SC 728; 1968 CRLJ 872; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 450

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860,s. 302 r/w s. 34 - Plea of alibi - Legality of - Held, High Court did not consider evidence of alibi at all - Offence charged against Sadhai was commission of murder in furtherance of common intention of two persons, himself and Ramdeo - The HC mentioned alibi but did not consider it in its judgment - Evidence of alibi was not satisfactory and did not displace evidence of eyewitnesses - If there was common intention established in case prosecution would not have been required to prove which of injuries was caused by which assailant, but when common intention is not proved prosecution must establish exact nature of injury caused by each accused and more so in this case when one of accused has got benefit of doubt and has been acquitted - Sadhai alone did not cause all injuries on head of deceased and Baul's instigation was likely to result in kind of injury which was caused to deceased - Appeal partly allowed.
