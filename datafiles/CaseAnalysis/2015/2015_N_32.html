National Institute of Technology and another v Pannalal Choudhury and another

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 442; AIR 2015 SC 2846; JT 2015 (6) SC 247; 2015 (5) MLJ 625; 2015(7) SCALE 314; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 78; 2015 (5) SLR 719

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Assam Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1964

Summary: Service - Competent authority - Dismissal from service - Respondent was issued with show cause notices for irregularities and misconduct committed during his tenure as Registrar/Deputy Registrar(Accounts) in appellant-NIT and later, Board Of Governors (BOG) by order dt.17-2-1995 had suspended respondent pending departmental inquiry - Later, Inquiry Committee placed its report before BOG and BOG accepted all findings of the Committee and show cause notice was sent to respondent proposing punishment of dismissal of respondent from services and later, Principal and Secretary dismissed respondent from services as no reply was received from respondent - Aggrieved, respondent filed petition before HC challenging dismissal from service - HC allowed writ petition and set aside dismissal order on ground that same was not passed by competent authority i.e., BOG - Aggrieved, appellant-NIT filed appeal before DB but the same was dismissed upholding view taken by Single judge of HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether removal of respondent from service was by competent authority.

Held, resolutions passed by BOG in connection with the matter reveal that BOG monitored, dealt with and eventually decided the case of the respondent in their various meetings and also authorized the Principal and Secretary to deal with the same in consultation with the Chairman of Board of Governors and to do the needful by passing appropriate orders. Further, in the last meeting held on 22-8-1996, BOG approved the Resolution passed in the earlier 68th meeting held on 11-3-1996, which had dealt with the case of respondent. Furthermore, expressions 'authorization', 'to take necessary action as the Chairman advises' and 'to do the needful accordingly' in resolution dt.11-3-1996 were wide enough to clothe the Principal and Secretary with a power to pass the dismissal order, if occasion so arises. Hence, in view of contents of resolutions, dismissal order dt.16-8-1996 was passed by BOG and Principal and Secretary only signed the order for and on behalf of BOG on the strength of authorization made in his favor by BOG vide resolution dt.11-3-1996. Hence, dismissal order dt.16-8-1996 was passed by competent authority as prescribed in Rules and the same was upheld and impugned order is set aside. Appeal allowed.
