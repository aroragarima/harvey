Â S. G. Jaisinghani v Union Of India And Ors.(With Connected Writ Petition)

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, S.M. Sikri, C.A. Vaidyialingam, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 280; AIR 1967 SC 1427; 1967 (1) ITJ 903; [1967] 65 ITR 34; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 703; 1967 SLR 482

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16(1) - Constitutional validity of what has been described as the "seniority rule" in regard to the Income-tax Service, Class I, Grade II, along with the improper implementation of the "quota" recruitment to that service - (A) Whether 'seniority rule' in regard to Income-tax Service, Class I, Grade II, along with improper implementation of 'quota' recruitment to that service infringes guarantee of arts. 14 and 16(1) of Constitution? - Held words 'in respect of any employment' used in art. 16(2) must include all matters relating to employment as specified in art. 16(1) - That promotion to selection posts is included both u/arts. 16(1) and (2), r. 1(f)(iv) of the Rules of Promotions is based on a reasonable classification and does not offend guarantee u/arts. 14 or 16(1) of Constitution - It cannot be said that rule of seniority proceeds on an unreasonable basis, reason for classification is objective of filling higher echelons of Income-tax Service by experienced officers possessing not only a high degree of ability but also first rate experience - Having regard to particular circumstances of this case Seniority rule is not unreasonable when r/w quota rule having fixed quota in exercise of their power u/r. 4 of the Promotion Rules between two sources of recruitment - Appellant is entitled to a writ in nature of mandamus commanding respondents Nos 1 to 3 to adjust seniority of appellant and other officers similarly placed like him and to prepare a fresh seniority list in accordance with law after adjusting recruitment for period 1951 to 1956 - Appeal partly allowed.
