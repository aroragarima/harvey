T. Aruntperunjothi v State

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 121; (2006) 9 SCC 467; AIR 2006 SC 2475; 2006 CRLJ 3290; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 364; JT 2006 (4) SC 300; 2006 (2) RCR(Criminal) 551B; 2006 (4) RLW 2809; 2006(4) SCALE 103; 2006 (6) SCJ 352; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 799; 2006 (3) UC 1470

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - CrPC, 1973, s. 176 - IPC, 1860, s. 304B - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, s. 2 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113-B - Appeal against conviction - Held, it was necessary for prosecution to establish that deceased must have been subjected to cruelty or harassment by her husband or relative of her husband soon before her death - No cogent evidence had been adduced by prosecution to establish that appellant had demanded any dowry - Demand of dowry or any harassment being cause for death of deceased, cannot be said to have been established beyond all reasonable doubt - Appeal allowed.
