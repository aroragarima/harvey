Â Atlas Cycle Industries, Limited v Its Workmen

Supreme Court of India

08 February 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 65; AIR 1962 SC 1100; 1962 (4) FLR 317; 1962 (1) LLJ 250; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 89; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 89

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes (Amendment and Miscellaneous Provisions) Act (Punjab Amendment) - Industrial Disputes (Amendment and Miscellaneous Provisions) Act, 1956, ss. 7, 30 and 30(2) - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 7 and 10(1)(c) - Whether tribunal validly constituted to adjudicate Industrial dispute? - Held, object of s. 30(2) is to provide of hearing of disputes which were pending before old tribunal, and its operation is entirely retrospective - s. 30 expressly provides for life of tribunal being extended for period specified therein, and that necessarily implies a power to continue tribunal - Appeal dismissed.
