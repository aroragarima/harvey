Tribeni Devi And Ors. v Collector Of Ranchi

Supreme Court of India

25 January 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, K.S. Hegde, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 93; (1972) 1 SCC 480; AIR 1972 SC 1417; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 208; 1972 UJ 665

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 ss.4, 6, 17(1), 17(4), 5(A), 18, 23(2) - Acquisition - Award - Enhancement - Denied - Challenged - Respondent had issued notifications u/ss. 4 and 6 of the Act for acquisition of lands of appellants and possession u/17(1) of the Act was taken after declaration u/s.17(4) of the Act that provisions u/s. 5(A) of the Act should not apply - Respondent had awarded Rs. 1,20, 419-6-11 compensation and on instance of appellants, respondent filed reference u/s. 18 of the Act for enhancement and solatium before Judicial Commissioner which was denied u/s.23(2) of the Act - Aggrieved appellants filed appeal before HC which revised amount of compensation and reduced amount of compensation to Rs. 90,000 granted in award - Hence, instant appeals - Whether order of HC is contrary to well established principles applicable for determining value of lands acquired under the Act.

Held, that Rs. 1,35,000/- per acre would be a reasonable rate at which compensation could be awarded to the claimants and HC was not justified in giving 10% towards potential value because that element is inherent in the fixation of the market value of the land and could not be assessed separately - Therefore, HC was not justified in disallowing 5% awarded by the Judicial Commissioner, as compensation for severance merely because there was an entrance to the land - Further, when a portion is left out there would be a diminution in the value of the land that is left out for which some compensation has to be allowed and 5% allowed by the Judicial Commissioner, is reasonable - Thus, the claimants would be entitled to a decree in respect of the lands acquired at the rate of Rs. 1,35,000 per acre, 5% severence and 15% solatium on the market value computed and interest at 6% from date of taking possession - Hence, appeals of claimants are allowed and claimants will be entitled to proportionate costs on the difference between the amounts decreed and those that are now awarded in each of the two appeals filed by them. Appeals allowed.
