Mahindra and Mahindra Limited v Avinash Dhaniramji Kamble

Supreme Court of India

03 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Tarun Chatterjee, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1697; (2010) 2 SCC 233; 2010(1) ALL MR 966; 2010 (1) Bom.C.R. 79; 2010 (124) FLR 69; JT 2009 (14) SC 597; 2010 (1) LLJ 310; 2009(14) SCALE 299; 2010 (7) SLR 128; 2010 (4) SLR 527

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions and Prevention of Unfair Labour Practices Act, 1971 - Scheme of Model Standing Orders - Temporary Workman - Rotational Employment - Termination - Justifiability - Respondents/employees filed complaint with Labour Court, seeking declaration of unfair practices on part of appellant/employer - Labour Court rejected complaint and no relief was granted as respondents had not completed 240 days of continuous service as per Model Standing Orders - Respondents filed petition before HC - HC held period during which employer engaged other batch or other persons in place of respondents/employees had to treated as period of 'involuntary unemployment' - Appellants filed appeal before DB - DB held that temporary workmen appointed for fixed term were not re-employed or continuous service, but were given break - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of DB was justifiable - Held, in instant case Industrial Court recorded a categorical finding of fact in respect of respondents/employees was correct - However DB treated gaps between diverse spells of employment as part of continuous service on ground that these were due to involuntary unemployment - Thus, order of DB suffers from legal flaw for more than 1 reason - First this was not even case set up by respondents in complaint and secondly termination and fresh employment in respect of temporaries and none of respondents challenged their termination being illegal as and when their services brought to end on expiry of period under contract of services - Hence, order of DB was set aside - Appeals disposed of.
