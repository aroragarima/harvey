Monika Ranka and Others v Medical Council of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 September 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, P. Sathasivam, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1522; (2010) 10 SCC 233

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - Eligibility of students admitted to MBBS course - Entrance examination - Irregular admission - Whether admission of students having less than 50% marks illegal and irregular? - Regulation prescribed that candidates should have secured more than 50% marks in entrance examination - Held, nothing on record to show that appellants were informed of marks secured by them in entrance examination - As appellants have already completed one year of their course, equities are in favour of appellants - Appellants may be allowed to continue their MBBS course as a special case and their results of 1st year MBBS course may also be declared so that they may continue with their studies - As admission is found to be irregular, equal number of students shall be reduced from management quota for year 2009-10 - Appeals disposed of.
