Anathula Sudhakar v P. Buchi Reddy (Dead) By Lrs And Others

Supreme Court of India

25 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1765; (2008) 4 SCC 594; AIR 2008 SC 2033; 2008(5) ALL MR 451; 2008 (2) AWC 1768; 2008 (6) CTC 237; 2009 (1) MLJ(SC) 1001; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 879; 2008(4) SCALE 718; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 331

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Declaratory Suit, Appurtenant Land, Demand Notice, Denial Of Title, Principle Of Res Judicata, Suit For Declaration With Consequential Relief

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s.100 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s.41 - Possession - Injunction - Justifiability - Plaintiff No.1 and 2 claimed to be respective owners in possession of said two sites having purchased them under two registered sale deeds from PW4 and were mutated in their names in municipal records - Plaintiffs alleged that when they were digging trenches in order to commence construction, defendant interfered with said work - Plaintiffs filed suit before Trial Court, for permanent injunction to restrain defendant from interfering with their possession - Trial Court gave judgment in favour of plaintiff No.1 and 2 - Aggrieved defendants field appeal before Appellate Court which reversed order of Trial Court and gave judgment in favour of defendants - Aggrieved plaintiffs filed second appeal before HC which restored judgment and decree of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC exceed its jurisdiction and judgment delivered by it was justifiable - Held, HC exceeded its jurisdiction u/s. 100 CPC firstly in re-examining questions of fact, secondly by going into questions which were not pleaded and which were not subject matter of any issue, thirdly by formulating questions of law which did not arise in second appeal, and lastly, by interfering with well reasoned judgment of first appellate court which held that plaintiffs ought to had filed suit for declaration - Therefore, in absence of prayer of declaration of title and issue regarding title, let alone pleadings required for declaration of title, parties could not be said to had opportunity to had full-fledged adjudication regarding title - Hence, set aside HC's judgment and directed appellants/defendants to file suit for declaration and consequential reliefs in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
