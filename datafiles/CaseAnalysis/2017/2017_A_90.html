State of U. P. and another v Anand Kumar Yadav and others

Supreme Court of India

25 July 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 511; 2017 (4) AWC 4037; JT 2017 (7) SC 321

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Service

Keywords: Ultra Vires, Backward Classes, Jurisdiction, Experience, Violation Of, Age, Selection, Vacancies, Leave, Pay, Qualification, Teacher, Pay Scale, Rule, Date, Posts, Qualifications, Recruitment, Selection Committee, Educational Qualifications, Service Rules, National Council for Teacher Education Act, 1993, Breach Of, Regular, Absorption, Interest, Policy, Eligibility, Amendment, Scheme, Relaxation, Reservation, Uttar Pradesh Basic Education Act, 1972, National Council for Teacher Education (Determination of Minimum Qualifications for Recruitment of Teachers in Schools) Regulations, 2001, Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009, Uttar Pradesh Basic Education Teachers Service Rules, 1981, Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Rules, 2010, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Constitution of India, 1950, Post, Right to, Training, Appointment, Uttar Pradesh Basic Education (Teachers) (Nineteenth Amendment) Rules, 2014, U.P. RTE Rules, 2011

Summary: Education - Service - Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009, ss. 23(1), 23(2) - Uttar Pradesh Basic Education (Teachers) (Nineteenth Amendment) Rules, 2014 - Regularization of Shiksha Mitras - Batch of writ petitions were filed before HC by persons who claimed to be eligible for appointment but that their chances were affected by filling up of vacancies of teachers by regularizing the Shiksha Mitras against said vacancies and as such prayed for quashing of notification dt.30-5-2014 and also for quashing GOs dt.7-2-2013 and 16-6-2014; and for restraining authorities from taking any action on basis of impugned notifications and GOs - Upon hearing parties, HC allowed petitions and quashed notification dt. 30-5-2014 whereby r.16-A was introduced as arbitrary and ultra vires; further, few provisions in 2014 Rules were also set aside as unconstitutional and ultra vires and also quashed and set aside all consequential executive orders of State Govt. providing for absorption of Shiksha Mitras into regular service of State as Assistant Teachers - Hence, instant appeals by the State and also by Shiksha Mitras - Whether Shiksha Mitras have, under the law, right to be appointed or absorbed as teachers de hors the prescribed qualifications.

Held, in view of clear mandate of law statutorily requiring minimum qualification for appointment of teachers to be appointed after the date of notification dt.23-8-2010, no appointment was permissible without such qualifications. However, appointments in instant case are after said date. Further, relaxation provision could be invoked for a limited period or in respect of persons already appointed in terms of applicable rules relating to qualifications but Shiksha Mitras in instant case do not fall in the category of pre 23-8-2010 notification whose appointment could be regularized. Further, appointment of Shiksha Mitras was not only contractual, but it was also not as per qualification prescribed for a teacher and neither on designation of teacher nor in pay scale of teachers. Thus, they could not be regularized as teachers. Further, since Shiksha Mitras were never appointed as teachers as per applicable qualifications and are not covered by relaxation order u/s. 23(2) of the Act, they could not be appointed as teachers in breach of s. 23(1) of the Act, and the State is not competent to relax the qualification. Hence, there are no errors in view taken by HC. However, in view of peculiar fact situation, opportunity is given to Shiksha Mitras to be considered for recruitment if they have acquired or they acquire the requisite qualification in terms of advertisement for recruitment for next two consecutive recruitments and they could be given suitable age relaxation and some weightage for their experience. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Consideration for career of 1.78 lakh Shiksha Mitras, over and above their legal right, could not be at the cost of fundamental right of children to free quality education by duly qualified teachers in terms of legislative mandate. Further, it is permissible to give weightage to experience or some age relaxation but mandatory qualifications could not be dispensed with.
