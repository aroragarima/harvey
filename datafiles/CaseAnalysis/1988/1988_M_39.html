Markandey Singh, I.P.S., And Ors. v M.L. Bhanot, I.P.S., And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, R.S. Pathak, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 434; (1988) 3 SCC 539; (1988) SCC (L&S) 827; 1988 (7) ATC 953; JT 1988 (2) SC 509; 1988 (2) LLN 941; 1988(1) SCALE 855; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 847; 1989 (1) SLJ 193; 1988 (2) SLR 694; 1988 (2) UJ 195

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Year Of Allotment, All India Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1969, Indian Police Service (Fixation of Cadre Strength) Regulations, 1955, All India Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules 1969, Indian Police Service (Regulation of seniority) Rules 1954

Summary: Service - Constitution - All India Services Act, 1951 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Examination - Representation - Limitation - Allotment - S/Appellant joined in State. of U as Deputy Superintendent of Police by passing competitive examination - Appellant had officiated as Superintendent of Police but was reverted back and was confirmed in the Union Territory Cadre of I.P.S. - Appellant made representation against order of allotment to claim benefit of officiation was rejected by Govt. - Appellant made second representation - B/Respondent had appeared in I.P.S. Examination was successful and recruited in Service and was promoted as Superintendent of Police - Govt. by its order gave benefit of officiation to S - B contended that acceptance of second representation of S was in violation of principles of natural justice - B filed W.P in HC was allowed - Appellant and Govt. filed appealed were disposed of by Division Bench of HC - Hence instant appeal - Whether acceptance of second representation of S was in violation of principles of natural justice - Held, it could not be accepted that there was no period of limitation for grant of approval of second representation - There was no scope of acceptance of second representation and it should had done upon notice to B - B would be adversely affected and his seniority would be affected and change was sought from Govt. reversing previous decision and in meantime B had acquired year of allotment - Hence, HC was right in dismissed petition of appellant - Govt. was directed that salary scale of appellant should be refixed - Appeals disposed of.
