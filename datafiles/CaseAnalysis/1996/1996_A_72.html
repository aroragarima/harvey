Chandigarh Administration, Unionterritory, Chandigarh And Ors. v Ajay Manchanda Etc.

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 689; (1996) 3 SCC 753; (1996) SCC (L&S) 870; 1996 (3) AD(SC) 628; AIR 1996 SC 3152; 1996 (73) FLR 1192; JT 1996 (4) SC 113; 1996 LabIC 1267; 1996 (2) LLJ 675; 1996 (2) LLN 243; 1996(3) SCALE 419; [1996] 3 S.C.R. 918; 1996 (2) SLR 673; 1996 (5) Supreme 29; 1996 (2) UJ 381; 1996 (4) UPLBEC 2878

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Tort

Summary: Constitution of India, art.311(2)(b) - Dismissal - Dispensation of Enquiry - Police officer - Complaint of extortion - Enquiry - Report, complainant and other witnesses were so terrorised by delinquent that they showed inability to pursue matter in Court of law - Finding by disciplinary authority that it was not reasonably practicable to hold inquiry against delinquent - Held, proper in view of situation obtaining in Punjab at relevant time - Order dismissing police officer, not illegal.
