Fibre Boards (Private) Limited Bangalore v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bangalore

Supreme Court of India

11 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 552; (2015) 10 SCC 333; [2015] 376 ITR 596; JT 2015 (7) SC 512; 2015 (6) MLJ 591; 2015(8) SCALE 633B; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 906

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Plant And Machinery, Finance Act, 1990, Finance Act, 1987, New Machinery, Defence Of India Rules, Finance Bill, 1987, Finance Bill, 1990

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 24 - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 54A, 54G - Sale of fixed assets - Capital gains - Determination - Assessee private limited company having industrial unit in notified urban area with view to shift it to non-urban area sold its fixed assets and earned capital gain - Further, appellant paid by way of advances various amounts to different persons for purchase of land, plant and machinery, construction of factory building etc. and claimed exemption u/s. 54G of 1961 Act on entire capital gain earned from sale proceeds of its erstwhile industrial undertaking in view of advances so made being more than capital gain made by it - However, AO refused to grant exemption to appellant u/s. 54G of Act on ground that due to non-declaration of area to be non urban area by Central Govt. and its failure to deposit capital gain in Capital Gains Deposit Account, assessee's claim is not applicable - Order passed by AO is affirmed by Commissioner - However, Tribunal reversed order passed by lower authority and passed order in favor of assessee - Further, HC reversed order passed by Tribunal - Whether impugned order passed by HC against appellant is in accordance with law.

Held, on omission of s. 280ZA and its re-enactment with modification in s. 54G of 1961 Act, s. 24 of 1897 Act would apply, and notification of 1967, declaring Thane to be urban area, would be continued under and for purposes of s. 54A of 1961 Act. Reading of s. 54G of 1961 Act makes it clear that assessee is given window of three years after date on which transfer has taken place to 'purchase' new machinery or plant or 'acquire' building or land. SC held that HC has completely missed window of three years given to assessee to purchase or acquire machinery and building or land. SC held that for A/Y. in question all that is required for assessee to avail of exemption is to 'utilize' amount of capital gains for purchase and acquisition of new machinery or plant and building or land. SC held that entire amount claimed in A/Y. in question has been so 'utilized' for purchase and/or acquisition of new machinery or plant and land or building. Further, SC held that advances paid for purpose of purchase and/or acquisition of assets would certainly amount to utilization by assessee of capital gains made by him for purpose of purchasing and/or acquiring assets. Hence, impugned judgment passed by HC is set aside. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Assessee is liable to claim exemption for capital gains earned even if amount is utilised for purchase of new fixed assets.
