Â Ratilal Bhanji Mithani v Asstt. Collector Of Customs, Bombay & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, Vishishtha Bhargava, G.K. Mitter, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 228; AIR 1967 SC 1639; 1967 CRLJ 1576; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 926

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Trade

Summary: Criminal - Govternment of India Act, 1935 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 225, 136 and 21 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.120 B - Sea Customs Act, 1878,s 167 - Imports and Exports Control Act, 1947,s 5 - CrPC, ss. 61 and 497 - Appellant accused of bailable offence and was granted bail - Bail of appellant cancelled by High Court in its inherent powers - Challenged - If at any subsequent stage of proceedings, it is found that any person accused of a bailable offence is intimidating, bringing or tampering with prosecution witnesses or is attempting to abscond, HC has power to cause him to be arrested and to commit him to custody for such period as it thinks fit - Held, HC cancelled previous bail orders, as it found that appellant was intimidating and tampering with certain German citizens whom prosecution intended to examine as witnesses - HC order not violative of of art.21 and was in accordance with Law - Order modified - Appeal dismissed.
