Ghulam Nabi Zaki v State of Jammu and Kashmir

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 215; (1969) 3 SCC 851; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 35

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Preventive Detention Act, 1950, Jammu and Kashmir Preventive Detention Act 1964

Summary: Criminal - Jammu And Kashmir Preventive Detention Act 1964, ss. 3, 8 and 13 - Petitioner challenging an order of detention - Whether second detention order could not be validly made except on some fresh material? - Held, second order of detention could not be passed without there being additional or fresh material in hands of detaining authority as contemplated by s. 14(2) - Further, established principle that once an order of revocation is made, another order detaining same person can only be passed if some additional or fresh material is in possession of State Govt. on which action can be based - Revocation or expiry of previous order cannot lead ipso facto to a revival of detention by passing of a fresh order, because a person who is entitled to his liberty can only be put in a second jeopardy when there are additional or fresh facts against him - Release of detenu ordered.
