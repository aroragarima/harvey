Haryana State Cooperative Labour and others v Unique Cooperative Labour and others

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, D.Y. Chandrachud

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 537; 2017 (4) MLJ 221

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Limitation Act, 1963, Limitation, Arbitration And Conciliation Act, 1996, Review, Arbitration, Revisional, Applicant, Access, Societies, Haryana Co-Operative Societies Act, 1984, Constitution of India, 1950, Land & Property

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s.34 - Condonation of delay - Imposition of exemplary costs for abuse of judicial process - In 1981, Haryana Federation was assigned work of construction of low income group houses which later entered into separate construction contracts for execution of the work with Construction Society - While so, Construction Society raised dispute and matter was referred to arbitration, whereupon Arbitrator passed Award dt.20-2-2008 - Haryana Federation assailed said Award before Registrar, Cooperative Societies through appeal but the same was dismissed as not maintainable since provisions of the Act were available to Haryana Federation and accordingly granted liberty to approach Civil Court in consonance with law - However, Haryana Federation took recourse to authorities to impugn arbitral award including HC but having failed in getting remedy, finally, filed objections u/s.34 of the Act before Additional District Judge with delay of 5 years and 8 months but the same was dismissed since delay beyond 3 months and 30 days could not be condoned - Aggrieved, Haryana Federation preferred appeal before HC but the same was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether delay could be condoned.

Held, having attained a clear and unambiguous determination vide order dt.17-5-2014 passed by Additional District Judge, there remained absolutely no justification for Federation to continue to avail the judicial redress, firstly, before HC and then before SC. Hence, considering facts and circumstances, appeal is dismissed with exemplary costs, on account of abuse of judicial process by Haryana Federation. Appeal dismissed with costs quantified at Rs. 5,00,000. Further, other petitions being filed with delay of 1297 and 1043 days, and since there are no justifiable grounds for condonation, delay is not condoned. Further, even on merits, petitions could not be entertained and same are dismissed with costs of Rs.5,00,000. Petitions disposed of. Order accordingly.
