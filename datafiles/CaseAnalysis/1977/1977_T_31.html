Thiru John and Another v Returning Officer and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, V.R. Krishna Iyer, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 127; (1977) 3 SCC 540; AIR 1977 SC 1724; [1977] 3 S.C.R. 538

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Conduct of Elections Rules

Summary: Election - Disqualification - Indian Evidence Act, 1872,ss. 17 - 21 - Election of petitioner was declared void by reason of him being less then 30 years of age - Appeal - Whether on date of scrutiny of nominations, petitioner was below age of 30 years? - Held, Yes - Overwhelming documentary evidence of a cogent and convincing character produced by election petitioner - Documentary evidence includes several admissions and declarations made by petitioner himself about his age, between March 1964 and July 1973 - These documents relate to university examinations and certificates - An admission, if clearly and unequivocally made, is best evidence against party making it - Petitioner failed to prove these documents are incorrect - Appeal dismissed.
