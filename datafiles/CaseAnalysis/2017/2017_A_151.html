Asha Ranjan and another v State of Bihar and others

Supreme Court of India

15 February 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 108; (2017) 4 SCC 397; AIR 2017 SC 1079; 2017 CRLJ 2272; JT 2017 (2) SC 565; 2017 (2) RCR(Criminal) 146; 2017(2) SCALE 709

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Fundamental Right, Life Imprisonment, Fair Trial, Undertrial, Right To Life, Jurisprudence, FIR, CBI, Jurisdiction, Specific Relief Act, 1963, Review, Proof, Treatment, Restriction, Criminal Trial, Prayer, Jail, Denial, Criminal Justice, Speedy Trial, Cognizable, Fear, Freedom, Justification, Dealing, Prisoner, Arms Act, 1959, Morality, Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, 1946, Religion, Convert, Legitimacy, Prisoners Act, 1900, Transfer of Prisoners Act, 1950, Criminal Law (Amendment) Act, 1972, Prisons Act, 1894, Explosives Act, 1884, Madhya Pradesh Dharma Swatantrya Adhiniyam, 1968, Orissa Freedom of Religion Act, 1967, Registered, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Husband, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Plea, Corruption, Association

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Criminal - Transfer of Prisoners Act, 1950, s. 3 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 142 - Transfer of prisoner from jail located in one State to a jail located in another State - Fair Trial - Petitioner's husband was shot dead and FIR was registered for offences punishable u/ss.302/120(B) and 34 of IPC and petitioner informed police about involvement of respondent no.3 in list of accused and later, investigation in the matter was transferred to CBI - While so, three sons of petitioner were kidnapped by henchmen of respondent no.3 and of them two sons were drenched in acid while the third escaped - However, during litigation, third son, a material eye witness in murder of other two sons of petitioner, was also murdered and FIR was registered against respondent no.3, his son and other unknown persons for murder of third son of petitioner - Later, HC granted bail to respondent no.3 in FIR No.131/04 and also granted bail in murder case of third son of petitioner - However, bail orders were set aside by SC - Under such circumstances, petitioner filed instant petitions seeking transfer of respondent no.3 from Siwan jail to a jail outside Bihar and conducting of trial in pending cases by video conferencing - Whether SC in exercise of power u/art.32 and 142 of the Constitution could direct transfer of an accused from one State to another and direct conducting of pending trials by way of video conferencing.

Held, respondent no.3 was involved in numerous cases and was booked in at least 75 cases, out of which he was convicted in 10 cases, facing inter alia life imprisonment in two cases and was also declared a history sheeter Type 'A'. Respondent no.3 is an influential person of the locality, for he has been a representative to Assembly on two occasions and elected as MP four times. Hence, it is not a normal and usual case. The right u/art.21 of the Constitution is not absolute and it could be curtailed in accordance with law. The curtailment of the right is permissible by following due procedure that could withstand the test of reasonableness. The contention that if respondent no.3 is not shifted out of Siwan Jail, the pending trials would result in complete farce, for no witness would be in a position to depose against him and they, in total haplessness, are bound to succumb to the feeling of accentuated fear that is created by his unseen tentacles, is not an artifice and could not be ignored. Under such circumstances, Court has to balance the rights between accused and the victims and thereafter weigh on scale of fair trial whether shifting is necessary or not. Further, if respondent no.3 is not shifted from Siwan Jail and the trial is held at Siwan, justice, which is necessitous to be done in accordance with law, will suffer an unprecedented set back and petitioners would remain in a constant state of fear, which clearly implies unbalancing of rights. Further, the discord that arises when there is intra-conflict in the same fundamental right especially, in the context of fair trial, has to be resolved regard being had to the obtaining fact situation. Hence, right to right to fair trial is not singularly absolute, as is perceived, from the perspective of accused. It takes in its ambit and sweep the right of the victim and the society at large. Further, when there is an intra-conflict in respect of the same fundamental right from the true perceptions, it is the obligation of the Constitutional courts to weigh the balance in certain circumstance, the interest of the society as a whole, when it would promote and instill Rule of Law. Hence, directions are issued to transfer respondent no.3 from Siwan Jail to Tihar Jail, Delhi and hand over prisoner to competent officer of Tihar Jail after giving prior intimation for his transfer in Delhi. Further, authorities escorting respondent no.3 from Siwan Jail to Tihar Jail would strictly follow the rules applicable to transit prisoners and no special privilege is to be extended. The trial in respect of pending trials is to be conducted by video conferencing by concerned trial court. The competent authority in Tihar Jail and the competent authority of the State is to make all essential arrangements so that the accused and the witnesses would be available for the purpose of trial through video conferencing. Petitions disposed of.

Ratio - S.3 of 1950 Act does not create an impediment on the part of Court to pass an order of transfer of an accused or a convict from one jail in a State to another prison in another State because it creates a bar on the exercise of power on the executive only. Further, in exercise of power u/art.142 of the Constitution, Court could not curtail the fundamental rights of the citizens conferred under the Constitution and pass orders in violation of substantive provisions which are based on fundamental policy principles, yet, in certain cases, issue appropriate directions so that criminal trial is conducted in accordance with law.
