Mohammad Swalleh and Others v Third Additional District Judge, Meerut and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 November 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28224; (1988) 1 SCC 40; AIR 1988 SC 94; 1987 ALJ 1467; 1988 (14) ALR 19; 1988 (1) AWC 312; JT 1987 (4) SC 291; 1988 (1) RCJ 138; 1987 (2) RCR(Rent) 690; 1987(2) SCALE 971; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 840; 1988 (1) UJ 121

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, Temporary Control of Rent and Eviction Act 1947

Summary: Rent Control - UP (Temporary) Control of Rent and Eviction Act, 1947, s. 3 - UP Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, s. 43 (2) (rr) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136 and 226 - (A) Suit filed on basis of permission to evict tenant, obtained u/s 3 of 1947 Act - Application for withdrawal so as to file application u/s 43 (2) (rr) of Act of 1972 - Suit dismissed as infructuous - Maintainability of subsequent application u/s 43 (2) (rr) - (B) Invalid order of prescribed authority - High Court declined to interfere with order of district judge, setting aside order in appeal, though appeal did not lie - Held, maintainable - Further held, justice had been done and improper order had been set aside and hence no objection could be taken - Appeal dismissed.
