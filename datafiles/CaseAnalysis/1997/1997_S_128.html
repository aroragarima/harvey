Syndicate Bank and Others v Shankar Paul and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 August 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 423; (1997) 6 SCC 584; AIR 1997 SC 3091; JT 1997 (7) SC 155; 1997 LabIC 2913; 1997 (2) LLJ 814; 1997(5) SCALE 313; 1998 (1) SLJ 96; 1997 (4) SLR 801; 1997 (7) Supreme 60

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, sch. 2, item 6 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 47, r. 1 - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1970, s. 19 - Claim of temporary employees of bank for absorption was rejected - On ground that panel expired after one year - With direction to consider them for future vacancies if within age limit - Subsequent scheme - Eligibility criteria as to age limit stipulated in scheme more favourable than under Court direction - (A) If temporary employees can claim permanent absorption in service - (B) High Court reviewing its earlier direction and directing consideration of petitioners first - Direction contrary to scheme evolved by Bank - Held, cannot claim - Direction liable to be set aside - Appeal allowed.
