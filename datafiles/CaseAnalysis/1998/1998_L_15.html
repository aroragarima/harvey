Mohan Singh v Late Amer Singh Thr. The Lrs

Supreme Court of India

01 September 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Srinivasan, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 616; (1998) 6 SCC 686; AIR 1999 SC 482; JT 1998 (6) SC 98; 1998 (2) RCJ 381; 1998 (2) RCR(Rent) 292; 1998(5) SCALE 115; 1998 (7) SLT 305; [1998] Supp1 S.C.R. 252; 1998 (7) Supreme 147

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: Ex-Parte

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 21 and 38 - Appellant a tenant under grant-father of present respondents with respect to premises - A joint application was filed before ARC u/s. 21 of Act for permission to let premises for a limited period of two years - Same was granted and another joint application was filed for permission to create a limited tenancy for two years and statements of landlord and tenant were recorded by ARC - Permission was granted for a period of 3 years - On expiry of that period tenant did not vacate premises and landlord filed an application for execution - Notice was issued to tenant, tenant appeared before court and filed his objections - An application was also moved for cancellation of warrant of possession - By order ARC recorded that there was no justification for issuance of ex-parte stay to stop execution of warrant of possession and ordered notice of application to counsel for landlord - ARC passed an order that objections filed by tenant could not be dismissed without recording evidence and granted permission to tenant to lead evidence in support of objection - Tenant filed objection - Consequently ARC held that permission granted u/s.21 of Act was without jurisdiction and could not be enforced - Accordingly, objections of tenant were upheld and landlord's application for execution was dismissed - On appeal Tribunal allowed appeal and set aside order of ARC - Whether the order of Tribunal is valid? - Held, contention that there was a subsisting tenancy on 29-6-81 and 30-6-81 is factually fallacious - Tenant has in more than one place in objection filed by him before ARC and in his deposition D/- 10-10-86 stated that earlier limited tenancy commenced on 29-6-79 - What is relied on at present is that first order granting permission for limited tenancy was passed on 3-7-79 - Records and proceedings relating thereto are not available - In absence of such records and on face of express admission made by tenant that tenancy commenced on 29-6-79 , therefore, permission granted on 3-7-79 was post facto and tenancy expired on 28-6-81 - Hence, when application was filed on 29-6-81 for permission u/s. 21, there was no subsisting prior tenancy - Tenant has failed to prove his alleged absence from court on 30-6-81, it follows that both landlord and tenant were present before ARC and made statements as recorded on 30-6-81 - Factually there is no concealment or suppression of facts and much less fraudulently by landlord before ARC - There was nothing wrong in ARC accepting statements of landlord and tenant made before him - There is not merit in second contention that order dated 18-10-85 had become final and therefore it was not open to landlord to argue before appellate Tribunal that tenant was not entitled to raise objection to validity of permission after expiry of tenancy - Order D/- 18-10-85 was of interlocutory nature and on a prima facie view it permitted parties to adduce evidence after holding that objections of tenant required consideration - That would not prevent landlord from contending before appellate Tribunal that tenant was not entitled to raise objections to validity of permission after expiry of tenancy - Appeal dismissed.
