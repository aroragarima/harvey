Madhu Kishwar and Others v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, Kuldip Singh, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 729; (1996) 5 SCC 125; AIR 1996 SC 1864; JT 1996 (4) SC 379; 1996(3) SCALE 640; [1996] Supp1 S.C.R. 442; 1996 (4) Supreme 192

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Family & Personal; Tort; Women & Children

Keywords: Female Heirs, Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Family and Personal - Constitution - Indian Succession Act, 1925, s. 29 - Hindu Succesion Act, 1956, s. 2 - Chhota Nagpur Tenancy Act , 1908, ss. 7and 8 - Constitution, arts.14, 15 and 21 - (A) Tribals - Intestate succession - Whether Hindu Succession Act, or Succession Act or Shariat Law is applicable to custom governed tribals -

(B) Intestate succession - Customs of tribal inhabitants - Whether to be declared as offending arts. 14, 15 and 21 - Held, (C) Tribals - Intestate succession - Whether ss. 7 and 8 can be read down so as to include female descendants alongside the male descendants - Held, (A) Neither is applicable, as custom varies from people to people and region to region - (B) Not desirable to be declared as offending arts. 14, 15 and 21 - Each case must be examined when full facts are placed before Court - (C) Cannot be read down so as to include female descendants - Minority View - Dissented - Petition dismissed.
