Deepali Gundu Surwase v Kranti Junior Adhyapak Mahavidyalaya (D.Ed.) and others

Supreme Court of India

12 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 579; (2013) 10 SCC 324; 2013(6) ALL MR 903; 2013 (139) FLR 541; JT 2013 (12) SC 322; 2014 (2) MahLJ 480; 2013(11) SCALE 268; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 1; 2013 (6) SLR 642

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Service

Keywords: Payment Of Subsistence Allowance, Termination Of Services, Indian Railway Construction Co. Ltd. (Conduct, Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1981

Summary: Service - Education - Maharashtra Employees of Private Schools (Conditions of Service) Rules, 1981 - Maharashtra Employees of Private Schools (Conditions of Service) Regulation Act, 1977, s. 16 - Payment of back wages - Period of suspension - Appellant was appointed as a teacher in Nandanvan Vidya Mandir (Primary School) - Headmistress of the school, addressed a letter to all the employees including the appellant requiring them to contribute a sum of Rs.1500/ p.m. towards the tax liability - Appellant refused to comply with same - Management placed her under suspension - Education Officer did not approve the appellant's suspension - However, the letter of suspension was not revoked - Appellant was not even paid subsistence allowance - Petition filed by the appellant questioning her suspension was disposed of by the DB and it was declared that the appellant would be deemed to have rejoined her duties from 14-3-2007 and entitled to consequential benefits - Meanwhile, the management issued notice for holding an inquiry against the appellant - Inquiry Committee conducted ex parte proceedings and the management terminated the appellant's service - On appeal, School Tribunal, (Tribunal) quashed the termination of the appellant's service and also directed management to pay full back wages to the appellant - Writ petition was filed thereagainst wherein Single Judge expressed his agreement with the Tribunal that the decision of the management to suspend the appellant and to terminate her service were vitiated due to violation of the statutory provisions and the principles of natural justice - However, Single Judge set aside the direction given by the School Tribunal for payment of back wages - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the appellant was entitled to wages for the period during which she was forcibly kept out of service by the management of the school - Held, in case, the management suspended an employee without obtaining prior approval of the competent authority, then it had to pay the subsistence allowance till the completion of inquiry - Also, in cases of wrongful termination of service, reinstatement with continuity of service and back wages was the normal rule - In the instant case, management's decision to terminate the appellant's service was preceded by her suspension albeit without any reason and even though the DB declared that she would be deemed to have rejoined her duty on 14-3-2007 and entitled to consequential benefits, the management neither allowed her to join the duty nor paid wages - Further, Single Judge agreed with the Tribunal that the action taken by the management to terminate the appellant's service was per se illegal but set aside the award of back wages by making a cryptic observation that she had not proved the factum of non-employment during the intervening period - Single Judge erred by interfering with the order passed by the Tribunal for payment of back wages, ignoring that the charges levelled against the appellant were frivolous and the inquiry was held in gross violation of the rules of natural justice - Impugned order was set aside and the order passed by the Tribunal was restored - Appeal allowed.
