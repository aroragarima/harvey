Commissioner Of Income-Tax, Kerala v M/S. Manick Sons

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover, V. Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 241; (1969) 1 SCC 671; AIR 1969 SC 1122; [1969] 74 ITR 1; 1969 KLT 215; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 708

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes -Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Reopening of assessment - Jurisdiction - For a/y 1952-1953 respondents assessed to tax in status of registered firm and their income was computed at Rs.15,331 inclusive of Rs.15,000 being undisclosed income - For a/y 1953-54 respondents returned Rs. 40,887 as their income from business - Income Tax Officer as result brought to tax total income of Rs.1,31,179 being Rs. 56,487 as income from business and Rs. 74,692 as income from other sources and assessed respondents as unregistered firm - Appellate Assistant Commissioner in appeal reduced income of respondents from business to Rs.38,420 and income from other source to Rs. 46,620 - Tribunal aggregated income for assessment years 1952-1953 and 1953-1954 for two years which he rounded off at Rs. 1,00,000 and apportioned in equal shares in two years - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Tribunal had Jurisdiction to direct Income Tax Officer to reopen assessment for year 1952-1953 - Held, in assessment year 1952-1953 respondents were assessed in status of registered firm and income of firm to be distributed amongst partners and shares of partners could be assessed to tax in their hands - In year 1953-1954 respondents were unregistered firm and total income of unregistered firm was liable to be taxed - Direction of Tribunal was that total income of respondents be reduced to Rs. 50,000 for year 1953-54, business income being Rs. 28,820 and balance being income from other sources - Tribunal had no jurisdiction to proceed to combine income for two years 1952-1953 and 1953-1954 and to divide it for purpose of assessment between two years equally - Appeal allowed.
