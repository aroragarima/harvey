R. K. Trivedi and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1188; (1998) 9 SCC 58; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1112; JT 1998 (9) SC 216

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Seniority - Whether, decision of Central Administrative Tribunal at least insofar restoring seniority of respondents 6 to 11 with effect from date they were appointed on ad hoc capacity, can be sustained? - Rules were amended vide notification D/- 31-8-1982 - Amendment did not change mode of selection which remained on basis of merit attained in written test - It was clearly stated that appointments would be on ad hoc basis and incumbents would have no right to claim seniority or even regularisation - When statutory rules provide a particular mode of selection, Tribunal could not in law have directed essential mode to be given a go-by as far as respondents 6 to 11 were concerned - Held, respondents 6 to 11 cannot claim seniority over appellants entitled to seniority on basis of merit position obtained by them in competitive examination - Appeals allowed.
