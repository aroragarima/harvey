Ram Das v Salim Ahmed and Another

Supreme Court of India

26 February 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1503; (1998) 9 SCC 719; 1999 (35) ALR 289; JT 1998 (8) SC 534; 1999 (2) SLT 475; 2000 (Supp) RD 619

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Whether defendant's claim of easement right and also claim of title by way of adverse possession could be accepted when defendant's title to suit property cannot establish plaintiff's title? - Held, High Court has held that copy of will was produced and since will was an old one and nobody had objected against validity of any subsequent challenge to will at a later date was barred by limitation - Plaintiff was not entitled to get declaration of title if such title could not be established by plaintiff by leading convincing evidence - Findings of lower appellate court was based on evidence in detail and by cogent reasons and should not have reversed by HC - Appeal allowed.
