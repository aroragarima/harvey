Kartar Singh v Harjinder Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 February 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 720; (1990) 3 SCC 517; AIR 1990 SC 854; 1990 (16) ALR 284; 1990 (1) CCC 540; JT 1990 (1) SC 253; 1990(1) SCALE 276; 1990 (1) UJ 341

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Special Relief Act, 1963

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Special Relief Act, 1963, s. 12 - Whether the present case is a case which is covered by s. 12 of Act? - Held, it is clear from s. 12 that it relates to specific performance of a part of a contract - Present case is not a case of performance of a part of contract but of whole of contract so far as contracting party, namely, respondent is concerned - Under agreement, respondent had contracted to sell whole of his property - Two contracts, viz for sale of his share and of his sister's share were separate and were severable from each other although they were incorporated in one agreement - In fact, there was no contract between respondent and respondent's sister and only valid contract was with respondent in respect of his share in property - This is not a case which is covered by s. 12 of Act - Appeal allowed.
