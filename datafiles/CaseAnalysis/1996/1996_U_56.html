M.C. Mehta v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

30 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, Faizan Uddin

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1082; (1997) 2 SCC 353; AIR 1997 SC 734; 1997 ALJ 254; 1997 (1) CLT 1; 1996 CPJ 1; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 167; 1997 LabIC 667; 1997(1) SCALE 61; [1996] Supp10 S.C.R. 973; 1997 (1) Supreme 418

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Emission, Closure, Oil and Natural Gas Commission, Safety, Taj Mahal, Industrial Development, Cng, Polluting, Mathura refinery

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Constitution of India, 1950 - Air (Prevention And Control of Pollution) Act, 1981 - Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 - Oil and Natural Gas Commission Act, 1959 - Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974 - Industrial Dispute Act, ss. 25-B and F-(b) - Labour & Industrial Law - Whether the workmen employed in above mentioned 292 industries, shall be entitled for any benefits and rights? - Held - The above-mentioned 292 industries shall as per the schedule indicated hereunder change-over to the natural gas as an industrial-fuel - The industries which are not in a position to obtain gas connections for any reason - Shall stop functioning with the aid of coke/coal in the Taj Trapezium and may relocate themselves as per the directions - All emporia and shops functioning Within the Taj premises have been directed to be closed - Directions have been issued to the Govt. of India to decide the issue, pertaining to declaration of Agra as heritage city within two months - We are mentioning these issues dealt with by this Court because it may be necessary to monitor some of these matters to take them to a logical extent to be listed on 04-04-1997 to look into these matters - Issue relating to 292 industries disposed of.
