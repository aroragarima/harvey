K. K. Handique v Member, Board of Agricultural Income Tax, Assam

Supreme Court of India

16 November 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 337; AIR 1966 SC 1191; 1966 (1) ITJ 396; [1966] 60 ITR 216

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Assam Agricultural Income Tax Act, 1939, ss. 12, 13 - Trust deed - Disbursements - Assessment - Agricultural income - Two appeals raised question of construction of relevant provisions of Act - X executed in respect of his properties a deed of trust and appointed four trustees, with an option to co-opt another person as a trustee - X appointed his son/appellant as a managing trustee - Trustees had to administer estate and realized income and made disbursements in manner prescribed in trust deed - For assessment years 1954-55 and 1955-56, managing trustee was assessed to agricultural income-tax on total income from trust properties u/s. 12 of Act - Assessee contended that assessment should have been made u/s. 13 of Act and not u/s. 12 - Income-tax Officer and on revision, Commissioner of Taxes rejected X's contention - Hence instant appeal - Whether trustees could be described as common managers for assessment years 1954-55 and 1955-56 made u/ss.12(1), 13 of Act - Held, On interpretation of document, it was manifest that it could not fall u/s. 13, for trustees could not be described as common managers appointed under any law for time being in force or under any agreement - They were obviously not receivers, administrators or like on behalf of persons jointly interested in such land or in agricultural income derived there from - No beneficiaries shall have right to transfer by way of sale whole or any part of his undivided share of trust property to any stranger when another beneficiary agrees to purchase same for a reasonable price which shall be fixed by trustees if vendor and vendee cannot agree as to what should be reasonable price and in no case shall a beneficiary had a right to transfer his undivided share in trust property by way of gift, mortgage or lease except to another beneficiary - Appeal disposed of.
