Collector Of Central Excise, New Delhi v Ballarpur Industries Ltd

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, N.D. Ojha, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 768; (1989) 4 SCC 566; AIR 1990 SC 196; 1990 (27) ECR 279; 1989 (43) E.L.T. 804; [1990] 186 ITR 244; JT 1989 (4) SC 396; 1990 (77) STC 282; [1989] supp1 S.C.R. 323

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Sales Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 8(1) - Question relating to what constitutes raw material - Input that gets burnt-up or consumed in process of manufacture constitutes raw material in case emergence of desired end product depends on its presence - Presence in end product is not essential for an input to qualify as raw material - Appeal dismissed.
