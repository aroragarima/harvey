Commissioner of Sales Tax, M.P., Indore and others v Radhakrishan and others

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

P.S. Kailasam, Jaswant Singh, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 221; (1979) SCC (Tax) 108; (1979) 2 SCC 249; AIR 1979 SC 1588; 1978 CRLR 620; [1979] 118 ITR 534; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 33; 1979 (43) STC 4; 1979 TAXLR 1843

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Constitution - Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Constitution, art. 14 - M.P. General Sales Tax Act, 1959 , ss. 2(d) , 18 46 - Assessment of tax - (A) Whether the firm is a separate entity under s. 2(d)(1) - Held, yes - There is no specific provision - There will be no liability on the for tax against the firm - (B) Whether provisions of the Act prescribing different procedures for collection of tax is constitutionally valid - Held, yes, it is constitutionally valid , the commissioner in graver cases can tale drastic steps to prosecute the defaulting assesses in the criminal court as well - Appeal dismissed.
