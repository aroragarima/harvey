State Of Kerala And Others v Kandath Distilleries

Supreme Court of India

22 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 108; (2013) 6 SCC 573; AIR 2013 SC 1812; 2013 (98) ALR 214; JT 2013 (3) SC 275; 2013 (1) KLT 797; 2013 (3) MLJ 119; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 494; 2013(2) SCALE 789; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 1053

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Excise

Keywords: Foreign Liquor (Compounding, Blending and Bottling) Rules, 1975

Summary: Administrative - Excise - Foreign Liquor (Compounding, Blending and Bottling) Rules, 1975, r. 4 - Kerala Abkari Act, ss. 14,29 - Licence - Rejected - Recommendations - Eligibility - Respondent had submitted an application before Commissioner of Excise for a licence to establish a compounding, blending and bottling unit along with several others, all of them were directed to first obtain approval of Central Govt. for setting up of new blending and bottling units and then approach State Govt. - State in year 1998 received several applications for grant of licences for setting up of large number of distilleries out of which State Govt. entertained 4 applications favourably and accorded approval u/s. 14 of Act, respondents application was not considered - Respondent filed revision before Minister for Excise to consider his application and for grant of licence - State Govt. constituted Scrutiny/ Selection Committee to shortlist application received for setting up IMFL units and considering recommendations of Committee took a policy decision not to grant more licences for setting up distillery units, which was communicated to respondent - Respondent preferred OP before HC to quash GO, Single Judge quashed letter and directed State Govt. to consider application submitted by respondent - Excise Commissioner heard respondent and after obtaining views of State Govt. rejected application of respondent - Respondent filed Contempt case before HC for not following directions given by HC, which felt that Govt. had committed contempt court by ignoring directions and passed order placing matter before DB, Govt. withdrew offered apology and wanted to pass fresh orders in conformity with judgment - Govt. passed orders rejected application - Aggrieved respondent filed writ petition - Single Judge took view that no reason other than Constitution of firm and date of its effect, was noticed for refusing licence and that there was no other ground found by Govt. to refuse licence and quashed Govt. order and directed State Govt. to grant licence - Aggrieved State preferred appeal before DB, which held that State Govt. had ingenuously made a classification to weed out respondent and noticed that HC had directed to consider its application submitted as early as in 1987 and that State Govt. had no case that respondent applicant was not suitable, nor such contention had ever been taken in previous litigations, DB held that there was no illegality in directions given by single Judge giving a positive direction to grant licence, which was necessary to uphold majesty of rule of law and dismissed appeal - Hence instant appeal - SC, held that it was not prepared to say that application of respondent was rejected solely on ground that application could not be treated as an application put forward by a firm based on a partnership deed, which came into existence on 10-4-1991, as per Cl. 3 of Partnership Deed but on various other grounds as well - State Govt. had considered respondent's application with regard to conditions that existed in year 1998 - Govt. letter dt. 28-6-1994, would indicate that, apart from respondent, few other applications were also pending prior to year 1994 - State Govt. during year 1998, from 3-2-1998 to 21-11-1998, had received 52 applications for establishing compounding, blending and bottling units in IMFLs in various parts of State - Excise Commissioner had reported that there was an unprecedented flow of applications, that was situation prevailing in year 1998, a factor which was taken note of in not entertaining respondent's application, whether it was submitted on 12-1-1987 or on 22-11-1998, Court could not, in any way, activate an out-modeled, outdated, forgotten liquor policy of 1998, in year 2013, by a Writ of Mandamus - Hence single Judge as well as DB had overlooked those vital factors while issuing a Writ of Mandamus directing State Govt. /Commissioner to grant distillery licence to respondent for setting up of a new distillery, thinking that impugned order was nothing but old wine in new bottle - Hence, single Judge as well as DB were not justified in issuing a Writ of Mandamus directing issuance of a distillery licence to respondent - Appeal allowed.
