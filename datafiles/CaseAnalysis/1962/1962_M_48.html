Â Mohammadbhai Khudabux Chhipaand Another v The State Of Gujarat And Another(And Connected Petitions)

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 369; AIR 1962 SC 1517; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 875; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 875

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Trade; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Summary: (A) Agriculture & Agri. Products - Municipalities & Local Governments - Bombay Agricultural Produce Markets Act, 1939, ss. 5A, 11, 29(B)(3) - Bombay and Saurashtra Agricultural Produce Markets (Gujarat amendment and validating Provisions) Ordinance, No. 1 of 1961, rr. 53, 65, 66 and 67 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(g) - Agricultural produce - Markets - Regulations - Constitutionality - In petition with respect to market in Ahmedabad challenging constitutionality of main provisions of the 1939 Act, SC held that provisions rr. 53, 65, 66 and 67 of the 1961 Ordinance ultra vires ss. 5A and 11 of the 1939 Act and prohibited respondents in that petition from enforcing provisions of the Act, Rules and Bye laws against petitioners in that petition till market was established for that area u/s. 5A and levying any fee u/s. 11 till maximum was prescribed under the Rules - Later, State Govt amended r. 53 by notification dt. 23-6-1961 - State Govt on 26-6-1961 promulgated Ordinance by which certain amendments were made in certain sections of the Act and new s. 29B was inserted in the 1939 Act validating certain acts done prior to Ordinance - Hence, instant petitions challenging main provisions of the Act, rules and bye laws framed thereunder - Was notification dt. 23-6-1961 fixing maximum fee to be charged was discriminatory and hit by art. 14 of Constitution - Held, notification by itself did not impose any fee on any commodity - What it did was to carry out terms of s. 11 which required maxima to be prescribed subject to which market committee could levy fees on agricultural produce - Imposition of fees still remained to be made by market committee under power conferred on it by s. 11 of the 1939 Act subject to maxima prescribed in notification therefore notification by itself should not be, said to be discriminatory - Petitions dismissed.

(B) Agriculture & Agri. Products - Trade - Bombay Agricultural Produce Markets Act, 1939 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19(1)(g) - Agricultural produce - Markets - Regulations - Constitutionality - Whether main provisions of the Act after its amendment by ordinance liable to be struck down as unreasonable restriction on fundamental right to carry on trade and business u/art. 19(1)(g) - Held, under bye laws as they stood at relevant time, two classes of traders, A class traders and B class traders were mentioned - This classification was reasonable - A class traders were wholesale traders who were permitted both to buy and sell in market yard and were thus charged higher licence fee whereas B class traders were ordinary retailers who in order to carry on their retail trade were permitted to buy in market yard but they were not permitted to sell there - They were small traders, and were therefore charged lower licence fees - It appeared that in order to avoid monopoly of A class traders, who were a few in number, B class traders were permitted only to buy in market yard on payment of small licence fee in order that the producer who brought his produce in market yard might get fair price - Therefore, there was no discrimination in creating two classes of traders, for there was fair basis of classification of traders nor should this restriction be deemed to be unreasonable restriction on the right to carry on trade and business - Petitions dismissed.

(C) Agriculture & Agri. Products - Bombay Agricultural Produce Markets Act, 1939, ss. 11, 29B(3) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 31(1), 265 - Licence fees - Refund - Prevention - Validity - Whether s. 29 B of the 1939 Act was bad in view of art. 31 (1) of the Constitution insofar as it prevented refund of licence fee collected before Ordinance came into force - Held, it was not disputed that legislature would have power to legislate retrospectively with respect to taxation - Art. 265 of Constitution provided that 'no tax shall be levied or collected except by authority of law' - S. 29 B (3) of the 1939 Act was the law which retrospectively authorised levy of licence fees collected in this case - Retrospective power of legislature to make law being there even in case of taxation SC failed to see how provisions of s. 29 B(3) of the1939 Act which validated levy and collection of licence fees could be held to be invalid u/art. 31(1) of Constitution - Same would apply to fees collected u/s. 11 of the 1939 Act and validated by s. 29 B (3) of the 1939 Act- Petitions dismissed.
