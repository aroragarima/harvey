Kunstocom Electronics (I) Private Limited v Gilt Pack Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

24 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 47; (2002) SCC (Cr) 336; (2002) 2 SCC 383; AIR 2002 SC 739; 2002 (1) CCR 99; 2002 (1) Crimes 275; 2002 (2) CRJ 212; 2002 CRLJ 1012; 2002 (1) JCC 318; JT 2002 (1) SC 268; 2002 (1) RCR(Criminal) 587; 2002(1) SCALE 323; 2002 (1) SCJ 449; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 435; 2002 (1) SLT 391; 2002 (1) Supreme 252; 2002 (1) UC 252

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cheating, Cognizable offence, Challenged, Summoning order

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 156(3), 245, 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 420 - Cheating - Summoning order - Challenged - Respondent filed private complaint in Trial Court alleging that appellant committed an offence of cheating - Application filed by appellant challenging summoning order was disposed of by observing that it was not fit case to entertain petition - Petition filed by appellant before HC u/s. 482 of CrPC was disposed with observation that appellant shall have right to raise all grounds at time of framing charge - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, on an earlier occasion when the appellant moved the HC, the HC directed the Trial Court to consider the objections raised by the appellant and to pass a reasoned order. Thereafter, the Trial Court passed an order overruling the objections and decided to proceed with the case. When the appellant approached the HC again against the speaking order passed by the Trial Court the HC once again declined to decide the petition on merits, but left it to be raised before the Trial Court at the time of framing the charge. It would be more appropriate and proper to raise objections of this nature at the time of the charge-framing, but this is not a case of this nature, especially looking at the observations made in the earlier order of the HC. The impugned order of the HC set aside and matter remitted back to the HC for fresh disposal of the petition for a decision on merits expeditiously. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Determination of question as regards propriety of order of Magistrate taking cognizance and issuing process need not necessarily wait till stage of framing charge.
