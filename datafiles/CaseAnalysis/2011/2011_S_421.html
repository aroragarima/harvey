State of Rajasthan and others v Sanyam Lodha

Supreme Court of India

25 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 677; (2011) 13 SCC 262; 2011 (6) AWC 5702; 2011 (8) MLJ(SC) 1107; 2011(9) SCALE 379

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Rajasthan Chief Minister's Famine and Relief Fund Rules, 1979, Rajasthan Chief Minister's Relief Fund Rules, 1999

Summary: Constitution - Administrative - Socio-Economic - Rajasthan Chief Minister's Relief Fund Rules, 1999, rr. 4, 5, 7, 8, 10, 11 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 162 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 357, 357A - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376(2)(5), 376(2)(f) - Minor Rape Victims - Ex-gratia - Non-uniformity - Validity - Challans/Chargesheets were filed in 392 cases relating to rape of minor girls - Out of 392 cases, 13 minor girls were granted relief ranging from Rs.10,000/- to 50,000/-, one victim was given Rs.3,95,000/-, another victim was given Rs.5,00,000/- and 377 minor girls did not get any relief or assistance from Chief Minister's Relief Fund ('Relief Fund') - Respondent approached HC against arbitrary and discriminatory disbursement of relief under Relief fund for minor rape victims - DB allowed respondent's petition by holding that all minor victims of rape required to be treated equally for purpose of grant of relief by interpreting r. 5 of the 1999 Rules - Appellants contended that r. 5 of the 1999 Rules were not under challenge in writ petition and DB was not called upon to consider validity of said Rule - Hence, instant appeal - (A)Whether DB could have substituted r. 5 of the 1999 Rules - Held, the 1999 Rules was not delegated legislation - The 1999 Rules were norms/guidelines issued in exercise of executive power of State u/art. 162 of Constitution - The 1999 Rules were not under challenge in writ petition filed before DB - State was not called upon to satisfy DB about validity of the 1999 Rules - Hence, DB should not to have modified or read down r. 5 of the 1999 Rules - (B) Whether Court was justified in holding that all victims should be treated equally while granting relief under Relief Fund - Held, assumption that no distinction was made in regard to either punishment under IPC where victim was under 12 years of age, and therefore, all such victims should get equal amount as monetary relief, was erroneous - S. 357 CrPC did not provide that compensation awarded should be uniform fixed amount - Inference that monetary relief awarded under Relief Fund should be identical for all victims of rape under age of 12 years, was illogical and should not be accepted - The 1999 Rules did not create any right in any victim to demand or claim monetary relief under Relief Fund nor do Rules provide any scheme for grant of compensation to victims of rape or other unfortunate circumstances - Authority vested with discretion might take note of any of several relevant factors, including age of victim, shocking or gruesome nature of incident or accident or calamity, serious nature of injury or resultant trauma, need for immediate relief, precarious financial condition of family, expenditure for any treatment and rehabilitation, for purpose of extension of monetary relief - Hence, authority at discretion, might or might not grant any relief at all under the 1999 Rules, depending upon facts and circumstance of - (C)Whether rule could be interfered merely on ground it vests unguided discretion - Held, all functionaries of State were expected to act in accordance with law, eschewing unreasonableness, arbitrariness or discrimination - Inflow into Relief Fund and disbursals there from were fully accounted - Rule of law prevails there was nothing like unfettered discretion or unaccountable action - When discretion was vested in high public functionary, power would be exercised by applying reasonable standards to achieve purpose for which discretion was vested - Relief Fund Rules did not confer absolute unguided discretion on Chief Minister - Discretion u/r. 5 of the 1999 Rules was intended to be exercised in rare and extraordinary circumstances - When there were no guidelines or when it was difficult to limit discretion in high functionary by guidelines, authority should be careful in exercising discretionary power - Hence, failure to give uniform ex-gratia relief was neither arbitrary nor unconstitutional - Appeal disposed of.
