M. Narayanan Nambiar v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

05 December 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 240; AIR 1963 SC 1116; 1963 CRLJ 186; 1963 KerLJ 668; 1963 KLT 1015; 1963 (2) LLJ 660; 1963 MLJ 581; 1963 (2) SCJ 582; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 724; [1963] Supp2 S.C.R. 724

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5(1)(D) - (A) Whether u/s. 5(1) of impugned act an employee can be punished in a case of wrongful loss caused to Govt. by abuse of his power? - Held, yes, he must - An Act is a socially useful measure conceived in public interest, it should be liberally construed so as to bring about desired object, i.e. to prevent corruption among public servants and to prevent harassment of honest among them - Case of a public servant causing wrongful loss to Govt. by benefiting a third party squarely falls within it - (B) Whether court acting upon document filed by prosecution without giving opportunity to accused to file objections violates principle of natural justice? - Further held, it does violate - Principles of natural justice, require that no court shall give a finding whether on fact or law and particularly on facts without giving an opportunity to all contesting parties - Remitted for submission for fresh finding.
