L.B. Sugar Factory and Oil Mills Private Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, U.P.

Supreme Court of India

26 August 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 599; (1981) 1 SCC 44; AIR 1981 SC 395; 1980 (19) CTR 185; [1980] 125 ITR 293; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 523; [1980] 4 TAXMAN 5

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10(2)(xv) - Revenue expenditure - Deduction - Appellant-assessee was carrying on business of manufacture and sale of crystal sugar in a factory - Collector requested appellant-assessee to make some contribution towards contribution of dam - Appellant-assessee contributed a sum towards contribution of dam and also contributed some amount to State - Appellant-assessee claimed to deduct those two amounts u/s. 10(2)(xv) of the Act - Income-tax Officer disallowed the claim by the appellant-assessee - Appeal to tribunal was failed - HC answered the reference in favor of revenue and against appellant-assessee - Hence instant appeal by the appellant-assessee - Whether the appellant-assessee should be allowed the claim for deduction u/s. 10(2)(xv) of the Act - Held, as far as expenditure of amount contributed to State was concerned, it was in nature of revenue expenditure laid out wholly and exclusively for the purpose of appellant-assessee's business and allowable as a deduction u/s. 10(2)(xv) of the Act - However, a sum contributed towards contribution of dam was purely as an act of good citizenship, and it could not be said to had been laid out wholly and exclusively for the purpose of the business of the appellant-assessee and was therefore rightly disallowed as deductible expenditure u/s. 10(2)(xv) Act - Appeal partly allowed.
