Ranjeet Mal v General Manager, Northern Railway, Baroda House, New Delhi and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 December 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 342; (1977) SCC (L&S) 164; (1977) 1 SCC 484; AIR 1977 SC 1701; 1977 LabIC 1546; 1978 (1) LLN 10; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 409; 1977 SLJ 142

Case Digest

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Termination - Challenged - Appellant, an employee of the Northern Railway, was removed from service - Appellant filed appeal against said order of removal which was rejected by the General Manager - Appellant felt aggrieved and filed the application u/art. 226 of Constitution - Trial court rejected the application on the ground that the Union of India was not impleaded - Appellant contended that the General Manager was the authority to hear these matters regarding the removal, and, therefore, that was the appropriate party - HC dismissed appeal filed by the appellant - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the Union of India represents the Railway administration. The Union carries administration through different servants. These servants all represent the Union in regard to activities whether in the matter of appointment or in the matter of removal. It cannot be denied that any order which will be passed on an application u/art. 226 of Constitution which will have the effect of setting aside the removal will fasten liability on the Union of India, and not on any servant of the Union. Therefore, from all points of view, the Union of India was rightly held by the HC to be a necessary party. The petition was rightly rejected by the HC. Appeal dismissed.
