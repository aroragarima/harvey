State of Maharashtra v Dr. R. B. Chowdhary and 2 Others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 257; AIR 1968 SC 110; 1968 CRLJ 95; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 708

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Media & Entertainment; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Media & Entertainment - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 500 - Press and Registration of Books Act, 1867, s. 7 - Defamatory article - Publication - Conviction - Sustainability -Appellants filed a complaint against 4 persons who were members of Editorial Board of a Weekly u/s. 500 IPC - Trial Court framed charges against respondent nos. 2, 3 and 4 and held that prosecution would have to lead satisfactory and cogent evidence to prove and establish that respondent nos. 2, 3 and 4 were Editors, Printers and Publishers - Respondent nos. 2, 3 and 4 filed an application for revision before HC - Single Judge held that there was no good round for framing a charge against respondents and they ought to be discharged - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Members of Editorial Board other than Editor could be prosecuted for defamatory article - Held, where there was mentioned an editor as a person who was responsible for selection of material s. 7 of the Act raises presumption in respect of such a person - However, name of that person had to be printed on copy of newspaper and in instant case name of one of accused was printed as Editor of Maharashtra in copy of Maharashtra which contained defamatory article - Declaration in Form I shows name of one of accused not only as printer and publisher but also as editor - Hence, presumption would attach to one of accused as having selected material for publication in newspaper - However, not only presumption could not be, drawn against others who had not declared themselves as editors of newspaper but it was fair to leave them out because they had no concern with publishing of article in question - Therefore, order of discharge made by Single Judge was proper and no reason to interfere - Appeal dismissed.
