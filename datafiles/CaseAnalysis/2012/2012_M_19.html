M/S Micro Hotel P. Ltd v M/S Hotel Torrento Limited & Ors

Supreme Court of India

06 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 278; (2012) 10 SCC 290; AIR 2012 SC 3291; 2013(1) ALL MR 929; 2012 (94) ALR 918; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 389; 2013 (1) DRTC 5; JT 2012 (8) SC 608; 2013 (1) OLR 238; 2013 (3) RCR(Civil) 733; 2012 (117) RD 804; 2012(8) SCALE 486; [2012] 8 S.C.R. 233

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - State Financial Corporation Act, 1951, s. 29 - Respondent Nos. 2 and 5 disbursed a term loan of Rs. 51,27,200/- and loan in lieu of subsidy of Rs. 23.30 lakhs to respondent No. 1 for establishing a hotel project - There was default in repayment of the loan amount, which led respondent No. 2 issuing a demand notice to respondent no. 1 followed by a recall notice - Respondent No. 1 filed a writ application before HC to quash the recall notice and for rehabilitation - HC directed respondent Nos. 2 and 5 to consider the request of respondent No. 1 for rehabilitation package - Respondent No. 2 introduced One time settlement (OTS) scheme - Respondent No. 1 applied for the same and the benefit of same was extended to respondent No. 1, subject to certain terms and conditions - Respondent No. 1 did not complied with the terms and conditions of the OTS scheme, consequently, respondent Nos. 2 and 5 informed respondent No. 1 that they had withdrawn OTS offer - Another OTS scheme was launched by respondent No. 2 and, again, an offer was made to respondent No. 1 to avail of the benefit of that scheme - Respondent No. 1 did not complied with that request as well, consequently, respondent No. 2, withdrew the offer and advised respondent No. 1 to pay the entire dues as per the agreement, failing which recovery proceedings would be initiated for realization of the dues as per s. 29 of the Act - Respondent No. 1 filed a writ petition before HC to quash the demand notice and for a direction to consider its claim under the OTS scheme - HC directed that the respondent No. 1 to deposit Rs. 50,00,000/- to respondent nos. 2 and 5 and also filed application for settlement of the dues before both the respondents - Respondent no. 1 did not complied the said order - Respondent No. 2 then issued a registered notice to respondent No. 1 asking to liquidate the entire outstanding amount, failing which respondent No. 1 was informed that respondent Nos. 2 and 5 would be initiating action u/s. 29 of Act - Respondent No. 2 issued a seizure order of the property and possession of the unit was taken over - Respondent No. 2 got prepared a valuation report and after due process sold the property to the appellant - Respondent No. 1 filed writ petition before HC to quash the cancellation of the OTS scheme, sale letter and also for other consequential reliefs, which was granted by the DB - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether impugned order of the DB could be upheld - Held, procedure laid down u/s. 29 of the Act has been followed by the respondent Nos. 2 and 5 - No illegality in the procedure adopted by the respondent Nos. 2 and 5, since respondent No. 1 had failed to complied with the directions issued by the HC, which gave liberty to the respondent Nos. 2 and 5 to proceed in accordance with s. 29 of Act - DB had overlooked those vital facts and had wrongly reopened a lis and issued wrong and illegal directions - Impugned order of the DB was set aside - Appeal allowed.
