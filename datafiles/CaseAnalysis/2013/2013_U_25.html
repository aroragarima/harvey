Arun Kumar Agrawal v Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 308; (2013) 7 SCC 1; AIR 2013 SC 3127; JT 2013 (7) SC 218; 2013(7) SCALE 333; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 508

Case Digest

Subject: Petroleum & Gas; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Petroleum & Gas - Acquisition - Challenge - Sustainability - Petitioner, through this Public Interest Litigation, had challenged the approval granted by the Govt. of India for the acquisition of majority stake in Cairn India Limited (CIL) for US $8.48 billion and also for a direction to the Oil and Natural Gas Corporation of India (ONGC) to exercise its right of pre-emption over sale of shares of CIL on the same terms without causing any loss or profit to the Cairn Energy, and also for a direction to (Central bureau of Investigation)CBI to investigate the reasons for ONGC, Govt. of India Undertaking, in not exercising their legal rights under the Right of First Refusal (RoFR) and giving clearance to CIL - Vedanta Deal on the basis of the existing right to share the royalty and cess on pro-rata basis and also for the consequential reliefs - Whether SC sitting in its jurisdiction was justified in interfering with complex economic decision taken by State or its instrumentalities in the absence of violation of any statutory provision or proof of mala fide or on extraneous and irrelevant considerations - Held, ONGC and the Govt. of India had taken prudent commercial and economic decision in public interest - SC was not prepared to say that the decision was mala fide or actuated by any extraneous or irrelevant considerations or improper motive - Petitioner had placed considerable reliance on the Comptroller and Auditor General (CAG) Report - Some of the comments in the CAG Report were highlighted by petitioner to contend that the declaration of fresh discoveries during the appraisal/development phases within delineated discovery/development areas amounted to irregular extension of exploration activities, which was not in consonance with the terms of (Production Sharing Contract) PSC - CAG's report was always subject to parliamentary debates and it was possible that PAC (Public Accounts Committee) could accept the ministry's objection to the CAG report or reject the report of the CAG - CAG was an independent Constitutional functionary, however, it was for the Parliament to decide whether after receiving the report i.e. PAC to make its comments on the CAG's report - SC mighty, however, point out that since the report was from a Constitutional functionary, it commanded respect and could not be brushed aside as such, but it was equally important to examine the comments what respective ministries had to offer on the CAG's report - Ministry could always point out, if there was any mistake in the CAG's report or the CAG had inappropriately appreciated the various issues - For instance, SC could not as such accept the CAG report in the instance case - Art. 2.6 of PSC permitted extension of the exploration period for three years from the end of the seven year period prescribed in art. 2.2 of PSC - Period extended in pursuance to art. 2.6 of PSC expired on 14-5-2005 - CAG had assumed that any exploration carried out beyond the period was beyond the provision of PSC - Art 2.6 of PSC specifically contemplated extension of the exploration phase pursuant to the terms of the PSC - Last part of art. 2.6 to art. 2.9 of PSC, however, permitted further extension of the exploration period for a period of 30 months, therefore, it was factually and legally incorrect to suggest that any exploration carried out beyond 14-5-2005 was beyond the provision of PSC - CAG views on that aspect could not be accepted - In such circumstances, SC found no merits in the writ petition which was filed without appreciating or understanding the scope of the decision or the making process concerning economic and commercial matters which gave liberty to States and its instrumentalities to take appropriate decision after weighing advantages and disadvantages of the same - Petition dismissed.
