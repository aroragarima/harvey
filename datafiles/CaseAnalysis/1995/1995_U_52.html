Hari Pada Khan v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

04 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 706; (1996) SCC (L&S) 333; (1996) 1 SCC 536; AIR 1996 SC 1065; 1996 (32) ATC 481; JT 1995 (9) SC 343; 1996 LabIC 934; 1996 (1) LLJ 1044; 1996 (1) LLN 462; 1995(7) SCALE 315; 1996 (1) SCJ 21; 1996 (1) SLR 641; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 157

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.14, 21 and 16 - Dismissal from service - Employee of Oil Corporation involved in theft of oil - Rule providing for termination of services of such employee without holding enquiry subject to result of criminal trial - Whether violative of art.14 or 21of the Constitutiton? - Rule being made to meet specified contingency - Whether principle of natural justice would apply - Held, it is not violative of art.14 or 21 - They would have no application - Appeal dismissed.
