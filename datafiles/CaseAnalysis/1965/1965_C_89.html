Kesoram Industries and Cotton Mills Limited v Commissioner of Wealth Tax (Central), Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 344; AIR 1966 SC 1370; 1966 (1) CompLJ 154; 1966 (1) ITJ 277; [1966] 59 ITR 767; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 688

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: (Majority Order)

(Subba Rao and Sikri JJ)

Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Wealth Tax Act, 1957, ss. 2, 3, 7, 7 (1), 7 (2) and 27 - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 2 and 55 - Companies Act, 1956, s. 21 - Computation of net-wealth - Appellant, a company, subscribed capital at the end of the relevant accounting year ending 31.03.1957 was Rs. 2,29,99,125/- - The original cost of the said assets was Rs. 2,30,32,833/- which were shown in the balance sheets issued by the appellant from time to time at the added value less depreciation calculated on the original cost - In the profit and loss amount for the said year a sum of Rs. 15,29,855/- was shown as the amount of dividend - In computing the net wealth, the Wealth Tax Officer (WTO) accepted the said valuation of the fixed assets u/s. 7(2) of the 1957 Act, however, rejected the appellant's plea that each item should be valued at the market rate u/s. 7(1) of the 1957 Act - WTO also disallowed the claim of the appellant in respect of the proposed dividend and estimated tax liability on the ground that the said items were not debts within the meaning of s. 2(m) of the 1957 Act - On appeal, Tribunal and HC confirmed the order passed by the WTO - Hence, the instant appeal - (A) Whether HC was right in agreeing with the Tribunal that the appellant's revaluation of the assets should be accepted for the purposes of the Wealth Tax Act? - Held, under s. 7 of the 1957 Act, WTO determine the net value of the assets of the business as a whole, having regard to the balance sheet of the business as on the valuation date and when the appellant himself had shown the net value of the assets at a figure, the WTO rightly accepted it - It was open to the appellant to convince the authorities that the figure was inflated for acceptable, reasons but no such attempt was made - Hence, WTO was justified in taking the value of the assets of the assessee as shown in its Balance Sheet on the relevant valuation date - (B) Whether the amount set apart as dividend by the Directors was a debt owed by the appellant on the valuation date? - Held, the Directors cannot distribute dividends but they can only recommend to the General Body of the Company the quantum of dividend to be distributed - Under s. 217 of the Indian Companies Act, there shall be attached to every balance-sheet laid before a company in general meeting a report by its board of directors with respect to, inter alia, the amount, if any, which it recommends to be paid by way of dividend - Till the company in its general body meeting accepts the recommendation and declares the dividend, the report of the directors in that regard was only a recommendation which may be withdrawn or modified - As on the valuation date nothing further happened than a mere recommendation by the directors as to the amount that might be distributed as dividend, it was not possible to hold that there was any debt owed by the appellant to the shareholders on the valuation date - (C) Whether in computing the net wealth of the appellant, the amount of the provision for payment of income-tax and super-tax in respect of the year of account was a debt owed within the meaning of s. 2(m) of the 1957 Act, and as such deductible in computing the net wealth of the appellant? - Held, under s. 3 of the 1957 Act, the net wealth of the appellant was assessable as on the valuation date, at the rate or rates specified in the Schedule of the Act - A debt owed within the meaning of s. 2(m) of the 1957 Act can be defined as a liability to pay in praesenti or in futuro an ascertainable sum of money - Under ss. 3 and 67B of the 1957 Act, the appellant was liable to pay income tax and super tax on its income ascertained during the accounting year ending with 31st March, at the rates prescribed under the Finance Bill or the previous Finance Act, whichever was less - The conclusion will then flow that the tax liability at the latest will arise on the last day of the accounting year - Hence, the liability to pay the tax was a debt within the meaning of s. 2(m) of the Act and it arose on the valuation date during the accounting year and therefore, was deductible in computing the net wealth of the appellant - Appeal disposed of.

(Minority Order)

(J. C. Shah, J)

Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Wealth Tax Act, 1957, ss. 2, 3, 7, 7 (1), 7 (2) and 27 - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 2 and 55 - Companies Act, 1956, s. 21 - Computation of net-wealth - Whether in computing the net wealth of the appellant, the amount of the provision for payment of income-tax and super-tax in respect of the year of account was a debt owed within the meaning of s. 2(m) of the 1957 Act, and as such deductible in computing the net wealth of the appellant? - Held, under s. 3 of the 1957 Act, liability to be taxed becomes effective not later than the last day of the year of account - But the liability to may tax arises, not from the estimate made, but only when the Finance Act becomes operative on the first day of April of the assessment year - Therefore, the existence on the Statute Book of s. 67B of the Act does not convert what was an inchoate liability on the valuation date into a completed or, effective liability to pay tax - Hence, in the instant case, the liability to pay tax arose on the first day of April, 1957 but that under the Wealth Tax Act, was not the valuation date - The liability to pay wealth tax becomes effective on the valuation date though the tax was levied for the assessment year, and on the valuation date there was normally no completed or effective charge for income-tax payable for the assessment year, because, the liability to tax did not give rise to any obligation to pay a sum of money either determined or determinable in the light of factors existing on that date - The power conferred upon the WTO by s. 7(2) of 1957 Act was to arrive at a valuation of the assets and not to arrive at the net wealth of the assessee - Hence, the liability to pay the tax was not a debt arising on the valuation date and therefore was not deductible in computing the net wealth of the assessee u/s. 2(m) of 1957 Act - Appeal disposed of.
