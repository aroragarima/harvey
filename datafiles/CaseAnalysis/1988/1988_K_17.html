Haridas Amarchand Shah Of Bombay v K.L. Verma And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

B.C. Ray, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 59; (1989) SCC (Cr) 111; (1989) 1 SCC 250; AIR 1989 SC 497; 1989 (1) Crimes 647; 1989 CRLJ 983; 1989 CRLR 136; 1989 CrLR(SC) 136; 1989 (19) ECC 196; 1989 (39) E.L.T. 329; JT 1988 (4) SC 632; 1988(2) SCALE 1507; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 1031; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 1031; 1989 (1) UJ 390

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Criminal - National Security Act, 1980, s. 3 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - (A) Detaining authority - Subjective satisfaction - Documents revealing facts leading to arrest of detenu and conditional grant of bail placed before detaining authority - Change in condition of bail - Application - Magistrate ordered - Not placed before detaining authority - Variation order having no relation to activities carried by detenu - Effect on detention order - (B) Preventive detention - Validity - Ground, non-supply of copies of documents to detenu - Which were mentioned in Panchanama but were not considered by detaining authority for arriving at subjective satisfaction - Held, (A) Detention order not vitiated thereby - (C) Need not be supplied, in absence of application for them - Appeal dismissed.
