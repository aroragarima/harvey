Dadu and Tulsidas v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, K.T. Thomas, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 797; (2000) 8 SCC 437; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1528; AIR 2000 SC 3203; 2000 CRLJ 4619; 2001 (135) E.L.T. 3; 2001 (1) G.L.R. 647; JT 2000 (Supp1) SC 449; 2000 (4) RCR(Criminal) 275; 2000(6) SCALE 746; [2000] Supp3 S.C.R. 703

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Excise; Health & Drug; Indirect Tax

Summary: Health & Drug - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 32 A, 21 and 27 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 21, 19, 72 and 161 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 389, 432 and 433 - Maharashtra Jail Manual - Opium Act - Dangerous Drugs Act - International Convention of Psychotropic Substances, 1971 - Petitioners arrested and convicted under NDPS Act - Vires of s. 32A NDPS Act challenged - (A) Whether parole can be claimed under NDPS Act? - 'Parole' means release of a prisoner temporarily for a special purpose before expiry of a sentence, on promise of good behaviour and return to jail - Grant of parole is essentially an Executive function to be exercised within limits prescribed in that behalf - Court cannot substitute period of detention either by abridging or enlarging it - Held, parole does not amount to suspension, remission or commutation of sentences which can be withheld under garb of s. 32 A NDPS Act - Convict is entitled to parole, subject to conditions governing grant of it under statute - (B)Whether s. 32 A is constitutionally valid? - Section deals with three different matters, suspension, remission and commutation of sentences - S. 32 A takes away powers both of Appellate Court and State Executive in matter of suspending, remitting and commuting sentence of a person convicted under Act - Restriction imposed upon Executive are for a reasonable purpose and object sought to be achieved by NDPS Act - Such exclusion cannot be held unconstitutional, on account of its not being absolute in view of constitutional powers conferred upon Executive - Distinction of convicts under NDPS Act so far as it relaters to exercise of Executive Powers u/ss. 432 and 433 CrPC, cannot be termed to either arbitrary or discriminatory being violative of art. 14, Constitution - Procedure prescribed for holding trial cannot be termed to be arbitrary, whimsical or fanciful - There was no International Agreement to put a blanket ban on power of court to suspend sentence awarded to a criminal under NDPS Act notwithstanding constitutional principles and basic concepts of its legal system - Judicial review is basic feature of Constitution - Thus, s. 32 A in so far as it ousts jurisdiction of court to suspend sentence awarded to a convict under Act is unconstitutional - Further held, despite s. 32 A being unconstitutional to extent it affects functioning of criminal courts in country, whole of section not declared unconstitutional - Appeal disposed of.
