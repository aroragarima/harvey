South Bengal State Transport Corporation v Swapan Kumar Mitra and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 578; (2006) SCC (L&S) 553; (2006) 2 SCC 584; AIR 2006 SC 3533; 2006 (5) AWC 4785; JT 2006 (2) SC 307; 2006(2) SCALE 141; 2006 (2) SCJ 390; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 30; 2006 (2) SLR 568

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Service - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 127, 279, 301-A and 338 - Whether order of removal of respondent No. 1 from service justified? - Since the degree of proof in a criminal proceeding is much higher than in a departmental proceeding, acquittal in the criminal case cannot be a bar for the disciplinary authorities either for initiating a departmental proceeding against the employee or from proceeding with the same and imposing penalty of removal from service against the employee - Held, after considering these comments, the disciplinary authority is directed to reach a fresh and final conclusion, on the question whether an order of removal from service of respondent No. 1 can be passed - It would be open to respondent No. 1 or his authorised representative to cross-examine the witnesses, and also to raise the question of admissibility of the xerox copy of the report of the District Magistrate before the disciplinary authority - Judgment of the Division Bench of High Court is set aside and the order of the Learned Single Judge is restored subject to modifications - It is also directed that the respondent No. 1 during the pendency of the departmental proceeding shall be paid subsistence allowance in accordance with the rules of the Corporation - Appeal allowed.
