State of Bihar and Others v Bihar Pensioners Samaj

Supreme Court of India

27 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 178; (2006) 5 SCC 65; AIR 2006 SC 2100; 2006 (7) AWC 6627; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 305; JT 2006 (5) SC 131; 2006(4) SCALE 600; 2007 (1) SLR 502

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Service

Keywords: Competence Of The Legislature

Summary: [A.] Service - Bihar State Government Employees Revision of Pension, Family Pension and Death-cum-Retirement Gratuity (Validation and Enforcement) Act, 2001, ss. 4, 5 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 226 - Reasonable basis of fixation of cut-off date - Financial burden on State - Validation Act making revised pensionary benefit available only from a later date and denying arrears - Date so fixed because of financial burden on the State - Act cannot be struck down on ground of violation of art. 14 - Legislature can alter law retrospectively by removing very premise on which earlier judgment declared a certain action as invalid - Appeal allowed.
