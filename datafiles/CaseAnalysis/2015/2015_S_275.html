State of Jammu and Kashmir v R. K. Zalpuri and others

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 925; AIR 2016 SC 3006; JT 2015 (9) SC 214; 2015(10) SCALE 575B; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 285; 2015 (6) SLR 714

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Justification, Order of dismissal, Misappropriation of money

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136, 226 - Misappropriation of Money - Order of Dismissal - Justification - Respondent no. 1 was terminated from services on ground of misappropriation of money by appellant - Trial Court dismissed order of dismissal of respondent no. 1 on ground of denial of reasonable opportunity to delinquent official - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, on perusal of factual exposition, it is vivid that respondent no. 1 was dismissed from service dt. 6.9.1999, and he preferred petition on dt. 18.2.2006, after lapse of almost five and a half years. Writ Court while deciding writ petition is required to remain alive to nature of claim and unexplained delay on part of writ petitioner. Stale claims are not to be adjudicated unless non-interference would cause grave injustice. Instant case did not justify adjudication. Appeals dismissed.
