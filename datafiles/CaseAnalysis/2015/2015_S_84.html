Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) and another v Sahara India Real Estate Corporation Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

19 June 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, T.S. Thakur, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 433; 2015 (112) ALR 21; 2015 (4) AWC 4248; 2015 (3) CLT(SC) 67; 2015 (3) CompLJ 401; 2015 CRLJ 3418; JT 2015 (8) SC 330; 2015(7) SCALE 173; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 1025

Case Digest

Subject: Securities Exchange Board of India

Keywords: Default, Refund of amount

Summary: Securities and Exchange Board of India - Refund of Amount - Default - Respondent company had collected whopping amount of more than Rs. 22,000 crores from the public in an illegal and unauthorised manner - HC had directed respondent, in particular, applicants to refund the amount collected from the investors with the petitioner-SEBI - Respondent failed to deposit interest amount, resultantly, applicants were taken into judicial custody - Applicants filed applications praying for grant of bail which was allowed with a condition that applicants would deposit at least Rs. 10,000 crores, that too with relaxed provision of deposit of 50% thereof by means of bank guarantee only - Applicants failed to deposit amount for purpose of conditional bail - Applicants filed applications praying to permit respondent to utilize the balance, if any, of the proceeds obtained from the sale/encumbrance of assets, and also, allow respondent to meet their respective statutory and other liabilities - Hence, instant applications.

Held, it is projected that respondent has finally found the buyers for certain assets and/or financers who are ready to provide the requisite finance against some of the respondent properties and that would bridge the gap insofar as conditions of interim bail are concerned. Since most of the properties owned by respondent remain frozen by the order of the Court, the applicants require time to enable them to deposit the balance outstanding. In case the bank guarantee is made encashable on default, the trigger point for encashment would be the default by the applicants in depositing the balance amount in terms of the directions proposed. It is in that spirit that the bank guarantee format furnished by the applicants is granted and also sufficient time would be granted to deposit the balance amount that remains to be deposited. Applications disposed of.
