Kishore Lal v Chairman, Employees State Insurance Corporation

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

P.P. Naolekar, B.N. Agrawal, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 478; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 1; (2007) 4 SCC 579; AIR 2007 SC 1819; 2007 (4) ALD(SC) 36; 2007 (3) AWC 3082; 2007 (6) Bom.C.R. 345; 2007 (1) CCC 180; 2007 (2) CompLJ 385; 2007 (2) CPJ(Sc) 25; 2008 (1) CPJ(Sc) 13; 2007 (3) CTC 561; 2007 (114) FLR 219; 2007 (3) JhrCR(SC) 189; JT 2007 (6) SC 541; 2007 (2) KLT 979; 2007 (3) LLJ 69; 2007 (3) LLJ 181; 2007 (4) MLJ(SC) 540; 2007 (4) RCR(Civil) 179; 2007(6) SCALE 660; 2007 (4) SCJ 465; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 139; 2007 (3) UC 1521

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Employees State Insurance (Central) Rules, 1950

Summary: Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948; Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Appellant seeking compensation towards mental agony, harassment, physical torture, pains, sufferings and monetary loss for the negligence of the authorities; (ii) direction for removal of, and improvement in, the deficiencies; and (iii) direction for payment of interest on the amount of reimbursement bills - State Commission and National Commission held that free medical services were not covered by the CP Act - Appeal against - Whether the service rendered by an ESI hospital is gratuitous or not, and consequently whether it falls within the ambit of 'service' as defined in the Consumer Protection Act, 1986?; whether ss. 74 r/w 75 of the Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948 ousts the jurisdiction of the consumer forum as regards the issues involved for consideration? - Held, service rendered by the medical practitioners of hospitals/nursing homes run by the ESI Corporation cannot be regarded as a service rendered free of charge - Service provided by the ESI hospital/dispensary falls within the ambit of 'service' as defined in s. 2(1)(o) - Jurisdiction of the consumer forum is not ousted by virtue of sub-section (1) or (2) or (3) of s. 75 of the Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948 - Matter is remitted back to the District Forum - Appeal allowed.
