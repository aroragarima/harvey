Transmission Corporation of A. P. Limited and Another v Commissioner of Income Tax, A. P

Supreme Court of India

17 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 636; (1999) 7 SCC 266; AIR 1999 SC 3036; 1999 (155) CTR 489; [1999] 239 ITR 587; [1999] Supp1 S.C.R. 504; [1999] 105 TAXMAN 742; 1999 (2) TLR 843

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Payments made to non-residents - Tax deducted at source - Determination of - Held, assessee is liable to deduct income tax u/s. 195 of IT Act in respect of payments made to non-residents - Such tax incidence is liable to be determined on gross sum of money paid to non residents - S. 195(1) casts an obligation to deduct appropriate tax by payee at rates on force - (B) Sum paid to non-resident - Not wholly represent income - Applicability of s. 195 - Held, ITO correct in determination of tax u/s. 195 in respect of such payments - (C) Trading receipts - Tax deductible at source on gross amount or portion of chargeable income - Determination of - Held, obligation to deduct tax u/s. 195 limited only to appropriate proportion of income chargeable under Act forming part of gross sums of money - Appeal dismissed.
