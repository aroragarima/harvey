State of Rajasthan v Teja Ram and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1014; (1999) SCC (Cr) 436; (1999) 3 SCC 507; AIR 1999 SC 1776; AIR 1999 SCW 1514; 1999 (38) AllCriC 627; 1999 (36) ALR 207; 1999 (2) CalLT(SC) 106; 1999 (2) CCR 33; 1999 (2) Crimes 45; 1999 CRLJ 2588; JT 1999 (2) SC 279; 1999 (2) RCR(Criminal) 285; 1999(2) SCALE 169; 1999 (2) SCJ 11; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 29; 1999 (3) SLT 88; 1999 (3) Supreme 391

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 227 and 153 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 149 - Murder - Dying declaration - Accused persons convicted by Trial Court but acquitted by High Court on appeal - Whether HC was correct in not placing any reliance on dying declarations of deceased and acquitting accused persons? - Held, there was prior enmity between parties and that was enough motive to commit offence - Witnesses consistent in their version throughout - Acquittal order set aside - Appeal allowed
