Ram Chander v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

25 February 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, Baharul Islam

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 35; (1981) 3 SCC 191; (1981) SCC (Cr) 683; AIR 1981 SC 1036; 1981 BBCJ 65; 1981 CRLJ 609; 1981 CRLR 246; 1981(1) SCALE 428; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 12

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Role of judge - Witness - Coercion - Appellant and co-accused were tried by ASJ for murder of a person - ASJ convicted them u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC and sentenced them to life imprisonment - HC acquitted co-accused but confirmed conviction of appellant - Appellant contended that evidence of P.Ws. 8 and 9 should not have been accepted by Sessions Court and that he did not have fair trial as Sessions Judge assumed role of prosecutor - Whether s. 11 of the 1872 Act should be invoked with regard to evidence put forward by P.Ws. 8 and 9 - Held, judge should have participated in trial by evincing active interest by putting questions to witnesses in order to ascertain truth - Judge should not have assumed role of prosecutor in putting questions - S. 11 of the 1872 Act was not invoked - Decision in Jones v National Coal Board, 1957 (2) Q.B. 55, followed - Appeal allowed.
