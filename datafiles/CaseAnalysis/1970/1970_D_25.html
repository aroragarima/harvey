Dindyal & Anr v Rajaram

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 333; (1970) 1 SCC 786; AIR 1970 SC 1019; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 298

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 14 (2) - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Limitation Act, 1963 - Possession taken over by widow as trespasser - Status of full owner - Entitlement of - Held, widow inherited the property after the demise of her husband - Gifted to her daughter - Gift was a valid gift - After the death of her daughter, the right of widow to take back the property as a trespasser - Widow held to have had no interest in those properties thereafter - Therefore, when widow took possession of those properties in 1951, she did so as a trespasser - Appeal dismissed.
