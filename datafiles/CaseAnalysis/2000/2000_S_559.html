Bhola Ram Kushwaha v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

16 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2773; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1; (2001) 1 SCC 35; AIR 2001 SC 229; 2000 (4) CCR 229; 2000 (4) Crimes 232; 2001 CRLJ 116; JT 2000 (Supp2) SC 632; 2000 (4) RCR(Criminal) 745; 2000(7) SCALE 439; 2000 (8) SLT 200; 2000 (7) Supreme 500; 2001 (1) UC 148

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, s. 21 - Appreciation of evidence - Possession of Narcotic drugs - Whether conviction of appellant u/s. 21 of NDPS Act justified? - Held, prosecution failed to prove its case against appellant beyond all reasonable doubts - Appellant cannot be convicted on basis of evidence led, which in this case, is found to be contradictory and not reliable - Appellant acquitted of charges framed against him u/s. 21 of NDPS Act - Appeal allowed.
