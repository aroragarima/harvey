Commissioner of Income Tax-II v Krishi Utpadan Mandi Samiti

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 426; (2012) 12 SCC 267; [2012] 348 ITR 566; JT 2012 (9) SC 604; [2012] 8 S.C.R. 934; [2012] 211 TAXMAN 32; 2012 (3) UPLBEC 2441

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Uttar Pradesh Krishi Utpadan Mandi Adhiniyam, 1964, ss. 16, 19(2), 19(3), 19-B, 26-PP(2), 26-PPP(2), 10(20), (29) - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 12AA, 11(1)(a), 11(1)(d), 12(1) - Transfer of funds - Applicability of Act - Challenged - Respondent/assessee was a Market Committee carries out its activities in accordance with s. 16 of 1964 Adhiniyam under which it was required to provide facilities for sale and purchase of specified agricultural produce in the Market Area - Source of income of the assessee was in the form of receipt collected as market fee from buyers and their agents, development cess on sale and purchase of agricultural products and licence fees from traders - Under 1964 Adhiniyam, broadly, there were two distinct entities or bodies one was Mandi Samiti i.e., assesse and the other was Mandi Parishad - S. 19(2), of the Act inter alia, stated that all expenditure incurred by the Committee in carrying out the purposes of 1964 Adhiniyam should be defrayed out of Market Committee Fund and surplus, if any, shall be invested in such manner as may be prescribed - Expenses to be incurred and debited were indicated in s. 19(3) and s. 19-B of 1964 Adhiniyam deals with establishment of Market Development Fund - Said payment from the Market Committee [Mandi Samiti] should be credited to the State Marketing Development Fund u/s. 26-PP - The State Marketing Development Fund should be utilized by the Mandi Parishad [Board] for purposes indicated u/s. 26-PP(2) and s. 26-PPP dealt with establishment of Central Mandi Fund to which amounts should be credited s. 26-PPP(2), inter alia, states that the Central Mandi Fund shall be utilized by Mandi Parishad [Board] for rendering assistance to financially weak and under developed Market Committees; that the Funds would be used for construction, maintenance and repairs of link roads, market yards and other development works in the Market Area - After the amendment of ss. 10(20) and (29) by Finance Act No.2 of 2002 the word 'Local Authority' had lost its restricted meaning and, therefore, assessee had to satisfy the conditions of s. 12AA r/w s. 11(1)(a) of 1961 Act, like any other body or person - Hence instant appeals - Appellant contended that in view of the said Amendment vide Finance Act No. 2 of 2002, the assessee had to show that, during the relevant A/y, income had been derived from property held under Trust and that the said income stood applied to charitable purposes - Whether amounts transferred by the assessee to Mandi Parishad would constitute application of income for charitable purposes within the meaning of s. 11(1)(a) of the 1961Act - Held, AO had not understood the scheme of the 1964 Act - On reading the 1964 Adhiniyam (Act) it was clear that the word 'contribution' in the Adhiniyam was in the context of what the members contribute to the Fund(s) held statutorily by the Mandi Samiti which merely transfered the amount(s) to the Fund(s) of Mandi Parishad - Therefore, the question of voluntary contribution u/s. 11(1)(d) or u/s. 12(1) did not arose - Question of 'control' might be relevant in the context of s. 11(1)(d) or u/s. 12(1) - In instant case, question framed dealt with application of income u/s. 11(1)(a) - Hence, the AO had erred in invoking s. 12(1) and s. 11(1) dealt with four items of 'income' from property held for charitable purposes - S. 11(1)(d), inter alia, refered to income in the form of voluntary contributions made with a specific direction that it should form part of the corpus of the Trust or Institution whereas s. 12(1) referred to non-corpus voluntary contribution - Neither s. 11(1)(d) nor s. 12(1) of 1961 Act was attracted - Narrow controversy was, whether, in the facts and circumstances of the case, the amounts statutorily transferred to Mandi Parishad would constitute application of income for charitable purposes u/s. 11(1)(a) of 1961 Act - Transfer of the amounts by Mandi Samiti constituted application of income u/s. 11(1)(a) of 1961 Act - Appeals dismissed.
