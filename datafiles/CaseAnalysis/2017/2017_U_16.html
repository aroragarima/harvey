Umesh v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

03 February 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, A.M. Khanwilkar

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 87; (2017) 3 SCC 112; JT 2017 (2) SC 330; 2017(3) SCALE 251

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 239 - Criminal proceedings - Quashed - Challenged - Appellant/accused filed instant appeals challenging impugned order of HC whereby HC quashed criminal proceedings pending before Magistrate.

Held, SC found it difficult to appreciate the contentions taken by appellant on their face value also it was for Magistrate concerned to consider those contentions in an appropriate application filed u/s. 239 CrPC. Appellant should surrender before Magistrate where criminal cases were pending, within 4 weeks. Appellant would be free to file applications u/s. 239 CrPC. SC direct Magistrate to consider the applications, if any filed, having regard to the contentions taken by appellant and dispose of the same, in accordance with law. Appeals disposed of.
