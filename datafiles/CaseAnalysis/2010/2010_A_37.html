Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Loss Of Future Earning

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 163A - Persons with Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection of Rights and Full Participation) Act, 1995 - General principles relating to compensation in injury cases - Assessment of future loss of earnings due to permanent disability - Held, general principles relating to compensation in injury cases are (i) All injuries (or permanent disabilities arising from injuries), do not result in loss of earning capacity; (ii) The percentage of permanent disability with reference to the whole body of a person, cannot be assumed to be the percentage of loss of earning capacity; (iii) The doctor who treated an injured-claimant or who examined him subsequently to assess the extent of his permanent disability can give evidence only in regard the extent of permanent disability - The loss of earning capacity is something that will have to be assessed by the Tribunal with reference to the evidence in entirety (iv) The same permanent disability may result in different percentages of loss of earning capacity in different persons, depending upon the nature of profession, occupation or job, age, education and other factors - Further, after the insertion of s. 163A in the Act (with effect from 14.11.1994), if a claim for compensation is made under that section by an injured alleging disability, and if the quantum of loss of future earning claimed, falls under the second schedule to the Act, the Tribunal may have to apply the following principles laid down in Note (5) of the Second Schedule to the Act to determine compensation - Appeal partly allowed.
