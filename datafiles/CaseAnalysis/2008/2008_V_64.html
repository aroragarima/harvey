National Insurance Co. Ltd v Vidhyadhar Mahariwala And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

17 September 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1499; (2008) 12 SCC 701; 2008 (4) ACC 714; AIR 2009 SC 208; 2009(1) ALL MR 465; 2008 (4) AWC 3820; 2008 (1) CCC 373; 2008 (6) CTC 254; 2008 (8) MLJ(SC) 206; 2008 (152) PLR 746; 2008 (4) RCR(Civil) 485; 2008(12) SCALE 577; [2008] 13 S.C.R. 604

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Breach Of Condition, Definition Clause, Cause Of Accident, Intervening Cause

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 173 - A sum of Rs.4, 03, 650/- was awarded to the claimant-respondent No.1 - Appeal against - Dispute related to the rejection of appellant's claim for exoneration on the ground of violation of policy condition that the driving license of the driver of the offending vehicle was not in force on the date of accident - Held, insurance company would have no liability in the case of this nature - Appeal allowed.
