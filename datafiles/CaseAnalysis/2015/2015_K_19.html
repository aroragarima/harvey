Kalyani Mathivanan v K. V. Jeyaraj and others

Supreme Court of India

11 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 182; (2015) 6 SCC 363; AIR 2015 SC 1875; 2015 (2) CTC 192; JT 2015 (3) SC 473; 2015 (2) MLJ 723; 2015 (3) RLW 2525; 2015(3) SCALE 369; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 467; 2015 (4) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Educational Institutions, Revision Of Pay, University Grants Commission Act, 1956, Open University Act, Madurai Kamaraj University Act, 1965, UGC Minimum Qualifications for Appointment of Teachers and other Academic Staff in Universities and Colleges and Measures for the Maintenance of Standards in Higher Education Regulations, 2010, University Grants Commission(Minimum qualifications required for the appointment and career advancement of teachers in Universities and Institutions affiliated to it) Regulations, 2000

Summary: (A) Education - UGC Minimum Qualifications for Appointment of Teachers and other Academic Staff in Universities and Colleges and Measures for the Maintenance of Standards in Higher Education Regulations, 2010 - State University - Appointment of Vice-Chancellor - Applicability of Central Act - Respondents challenged appointment of appellant as Vice Chancellor of University before HC - HC, by impugned judgment, quashed appointment on the ground that appellant did not satisfy eligibility criteria stipulated by Regulations - Whether the Regulations is mandatory in nature.

Held, Regulations though a subordinate legislation has binding effect on the Universities to which it applies; and consequence of failure of the University to comply with the recommendations of the Commission, the UGC may withhold the grants to the university made out of the Fund of the Commission. Further, the Regulations are mandatory to teachers and other academic staff in all the Central Universities and Colleges thereunder and the Institutions deemed to be Universities whose maintenance expenditure is met by the UGC. However, the Regulations is directory for the Universities, Colleges and other higher educational institutions under the purview of the State Legislation as the matter has been left to the State Govt. to adopt and implement the Scheme contained in Appendix-I to the Annexure of the Regulations. Thus, the Regulations is partly mandatory and is partly directory. Appeals allowed.

(B) Education - UGC Minimum Qualifications for Appointment of Teachers and other Academic Staff in Universities and Colleges and Measures for the Maintenance of Standards in Higher Education Regulations, 2010 - University Grants Commission Act, 1956, s. 14 - Madurai Kamaraj University Act, 1965 - Appointment of Vice-Chancellor - Applicability of Central Act - Extent of - Whether the provisions of the Regulations would prevail over the 1965 Act in the event of conflict.

Held, Vice-Chancellor under the 1965 Act is a post of an Officer. The 1956 Act is silent about this aspect. The erstwhile Regulations, 2000 are also silent in regard to post of Vice-Chancellor. Provisions regarding Vice-Chancellor have been made for the first time under the Regulations. However, the Regulations is not applicable to the Universities, Colleges and other higher educational institutions coming under the purview of the State Legislature unless State Govt. wish to adopt and implement the Scheme contained in Appendix-I to the Annexure of the Regulations subject to the terms and conditions therein. Further, it is also not the case of the respondents that the Scheme has been adopted and implemented by the State Govt. It is also apparent from the facts that 1965 Act has not been amended in terms of the Regulations nor was any action taken by the UGC u/s. 14 of 1956 Act as a consequence of failure of University to comply with the recommendations of the Commission u/s. 14 of the 1956 Act. However, State Legislation is in conflict with Central Legislation including sub-ordinate legislation made by the Central Legislation under Entry 25 of the Concurrent List shall be repugnant to the Central Legislation and would be inoperative. Thus, Regulations having not adopted by the State, the question of conflict between State Legislation and Statutes framed under Central Legislation does not arise. Once it is adopted by the State Govt, the State Legislation to be amended appropriately. In such case also there shall be no conflict between the State Legislation and the Central Legislation. Appeals allowed.
