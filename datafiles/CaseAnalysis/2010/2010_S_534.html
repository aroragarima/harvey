RPG Life Sciences Limited and another v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 375; (2010) 6 SCC 540; AIR 2010 SC 2765; 2010 (80) ALR 936; 2010 (3) RCR(Civil) 210; 2010 (3) RCR(Criminal) 366; 2010(5) SCALE 582

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug

Keywords: Drugs (Prices Control) Order, 1979

Summary: Criminal - Health & Drug - Drugs (Prices Control) Order, 1979 - Essential Commodities Act, 1955, ss. 7(1)(a)(ii) r/w. s. 3(2)(c) - Drug Inspector found 68 bottles of Haloperidol 5 Mg. tablet containing about 100 Tablets each from the premises of A-3 without the label which indicates the maximum retail price - With regard to the same, a letter was given by A-4, the Partner of A-3 Firm, admitting the fact that maximum retail price column has not been furnished on the label of the bottle and the details regarding the purchase of the bottles will be furnished - Show cause notice issued and accused were charged for violation of para 20 of DPCO, 1979 punishable u/s. 7(1)(a)(ii) of 1955 Act r/w. s. 3(2)(c) of the said Act - Trial court convicted accused u/s. 7(1)(a)(ii) of 1955 Act r/w. s. 3(2)(c) of the said Act - On appeal, HC has confirmed the order of conviction and sentence of the accused passed by the Trial Court - Accused contended that it is not the case of the prosecution that the tablet was either misbranded or does not conform with the quality prescribed, but the only infringement is that the maximum retail price has not been printed on the bottle - Accused further contended that as Drug had been marketed and sold in the generic name Haloperidol, exclusively for hospital supplies, bottle need not contain the maximum retail price - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of HC could be upheld? - Held, yes - According to para 20 of DPCO, 1979 either the manufacturer or the distributor of a specific medicine has to print that the medicine is for sale on the bottle and also the maximum retail price has to be mentioned - Hence in this case the accused come under punishable offence as per para 20 of DPCO 1979 - As the incident is of 31-5-1985 and it may not be desirable to send the accused to jail after a lapse of about 25 years, ends of justice would be met if while maintaining the conviction of the appellants, instead of sending them to serve out three months of imprisonment, the sentence of fine is substantially increased - With this modification of the Trial Court order as affirmed by the HC, appeals disposed of.
