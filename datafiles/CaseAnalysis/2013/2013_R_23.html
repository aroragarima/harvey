Tej Prakash Pathak & Others v Rajasthan High Court & Others

Supreme Court of India

20 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, R.M. Lodha, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 163; (2013) 4 SCC 540; JT 2013 (8) SC 498; 2013 (2) KLT 83; 2013(5) SCALE 1; 2013 (4) SLR 478

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Selection - Rejection - Legality - Respondent/HC issued notification for filling up 13 posts of Translators - Petitioners appeared for the examination - Respondent No. 1 declared only 3 candidates were successful - Aggrieved, petitioners/unsuccessful candidates filed writ petition - Petitioners contended that selection process on ground that decision of Chief Justice to select only those candidates who secured a minimum of 75% marks should amount to 'changing the rules of the game after the game was played' - HC dismissed the petition preferred by petitioners - Aggrieved, petitioners filed instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, it was a salutary principle not to permit State or its instrumentalities to tinker with 'rules of the game' insofar as prescription of eligibility criteria was concerned as was done in case of C. Channabasavaiah v. State of Mysore, 1964 Indlaw SC 190, in order to avoid manipulation of recruitment process and its results - Whether such principle should be applied in context of 'rules of the game' stipulating procedure for selection more particularly when change sought was to impose a more rigorous scrutiny for selection requires an authoritative pronouncement of a Larger Bench - Hence, matter placed before Larger Bench for appropriate orders - Order accordingly.
