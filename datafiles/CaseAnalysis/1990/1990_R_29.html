New India Assurance Company Limited v Ram Dayal and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 797; (1990) SCC (Cr) 432; (1990) 2 SCC 680; 1990 (2) ACC 344; 1990 (2) ACJ 545; 1990 (16) ALR 554; 1990 (1) AWC 689; 1990 (2) CCC 339; [1990] 68 Comp Cas 580; 1990 CrLR(SC) 559; JT 1990 (2) SC 164; 1990 (1) KLT 817; 1990 (2) PLR 144; 1990(1) SCALE 783; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 570; 1990 (1) UJ 712

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Civil Procedure; Corporate; Criminal

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh General Clauses Act, 1904

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Whether insurer is liable to pay compensation to owner when policy had been taken after accident? - Held, yes - Held, a policy taken on a particular date, its effectiveness is from commencement of date and, therefore, High Court was right in holding that insurer was liable in terms of Act to meet liability of owner under award - Relied on Jaddoo Singh v. Malti Devi (AIR 1983 All 87) - Appeal dismissed.
