Rattan Anmol Singh and Another v Atma Ram and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 May 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 44; AIR 1954 SC 510; 1954 (2) MLJ(SC) 435; 1954 SCJ 731; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 481

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Representation of the People (Conduct of Elections and Election Petitions) Rules, 1951

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 33 and 36(2)(d) - Representation of People (Conduct of Elections and Election Petitions) Rules, 1951, r. 2(2) - Both proposer and seconder illiterate - Thumb impression not followed by attestation - (A) Whether Returning Officer was right in rejecting petitioner's nomination papers ? - Held, yes - Returning officer correctly rejected nomination papers - (B) Whether attestation is a substantial or a mere technical requirement? - Further held, it is a substantial requirement - In absence of attestation, form has to be rejected - Absence of attestation cannot be dismissed as technical and unsubstantial - (C) Whether attestation can be compelled by persons concerned at scrutiny stage ? - Further held, no - Attestation and satisfaction must exist at presentation stage - Total omission of such feature cannot be subsequently validated - Appeal allowed.
