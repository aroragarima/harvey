National Textile Corpn. (Up) Ltd. v Dr. Raja Ram Jaipuria & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, J. S. KHEHAR

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 367; (2013) 7 SCC 406; AIR 2013 SC 2783; JT 2013 (9) SC 249; 2013(8) SCALE 120; [2013] 7 S.C.R. 28

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Land & Property

Keywords: Swadeshi Cotton Mills Company Limited (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1986, Swadeshi Cotton Mills Company (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Ordinance, 1986

Summary: Land &Property - Swadeshi Cotton Mills Company (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Ordinance, 1986 - Industries (Development and Regulation) Act, 1951, s. 18AA - Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorized Occupants) Act, 1971, ss. 5, 7, 9 - Swadeshi Cotton Mills Company Limited (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1986, ss. 2(c), 3, 4, 8, 27 - Acquired property - Handing over of possession - Legality - Swadeshi Cotton Mills Company Limited/SCMCL was engaged in business of activity of operating and managing textile mills - SCMCL acquired property and constructed 'Swadeshi House' consisting of three buildings, viz., Bungalow No. 1, 2 and 3 - Central Govt. vide notification took over management of SCMCL including National Textile Corporation Limited/appellant - Possession and custody of various properties were taken over belonging to SCMCL but Bungalow No. 2 continued to be in physical possession of respondent - Aggrieved by order of takeover, SCMCL filed Writ Petition before HC - HC upheld notification but certain assets were excluded - Aggrieved by aforesaid judgment with regard to validity and legality of order of takeover, Appellant and Union of India preferred Appeal before SC - SC held that said takeover was invalid on ground that no opportunity of hearing was given to SCMCL before takeover - SCMCL also preferred Writ Petition before HC which was transferred to SC - SC held that ownership and control of SCMCL vests with NTC, it was also held that Bungalow No. 1 and Administrative Block also vested in Central Govt. and disposed of the matter - SCMCL failed to handover possession of Bungalow No. 2 of Swadeshi House, NTC filed petition and same was dismissed - Appellant was successor-in-interest to NTC preferred Criminal Complaint against respondent before Trial Court and same was dismissed - Aggrieved by said order NTC filed Criminal Revision before Trial Court and same was dismissed - NTC preferred Writ Petition before HC - Meantime, NTC filed Contempt Petition before SC alleging violation of judgment in Doypack 1988 Indlaw SC 695 but same was dismissed - HC dismissed above said writ petition - Appellant moved an application for eviction of respondent from Bungalow - During pendency of proceedings before Estate Officer, Respondent no. 2 removed certain valuables from Bungalow no. 2 - NTC filed an application for restraining respondents for same before Estate Officer which was allowed - Aggrieved respondent no. 16 related entity of SCMCL, preferred a Writ Petition before HC - HC restrained respondents from removing any article kept in Bungalow No. 2 - Estate Officer passed order and rejected all preliminary objections filed by SCMCL - Respondents preferred an Appeal before Lower Appellate Court - Lower Appellate Court Vide order allowed said appeal - Aggrieved, appellant preferred Writ Petition before HC - HC dismissed the same - Aggrieved by order of HC, appellant preferred instant appeal - Whether impugned orders passed by Trial Court as well as HC was justified - Held, it was alleged by appellant in said contempt petition that since contemnors therein have sold Bungalow No. 2 to one Builders Ltd., they had violated judgment in Doypack 1988 Indlaw SC 695 and, therefore, they were liable to be punished for contempt - Director of said Builders Ltd. was also impleaded as Contemnor No. 3 in said contempt petition - By order SC dismissed said contempt petition - After several rounds of litigation appellant filed Writ Petition before Single Judge and same was dismissed holding that u/s. 27 of Act 30 of 1986 complaint could only have been filed by appellant if property had vested in them - In addition appellant initiated further proceedings for their eviction u/ss. 5 and 7 of 1971 Act - Similarly, after rounds of litigation, claim of appellant got rejected and finally appellant herein filed Writ Petition before HC and same was dismissed - Taking note of all the said applications/petitions it was abundantly clear that appellant herein have time and again filed various proceedings on premise that Bungalow no. 2 formed part of Swadeshi House but failed in all attempts - It was not in dispute that all proceedings went against appellant - Various orders and decisions by different courts negatived claim of appellant and same issue was now again sought to be raised by appellant in present proceedings - SC was satisfied that in view of categorical decision of SC in Doypack 1988 Indlaw SC 695 rejection of subsequent application filed by appellant for clarification/modification, direction to approach Trial Court, initiation of proceedings under 1971 Act which ended in dismissal, dismissal of complaint u/s. 27 of 1986 Act, were passed by various courts which undoubtedly go against claim and stand of appellant - It was also brought to SC notice by newly impleaded parties that they had purchased said property in bona fide manner with clean title of property vested in SCMCL, therefore, they were entitled for same - It was clear that SC had not expressed any thing about said issue - SC was in entire agreement with orders passed by Trial Court as well as HC - Appeals dismissed.
