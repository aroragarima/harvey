State of Maharashtra v Sant Dnyaneshwar Shikshan Shastra Mahavidyalaya and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Y.K. Sabharwal, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 110; (2006) 9 SCC 1; 2006 (4) Bom.C.R. 773; 2006 (3) ESC 340; JT 2006 (4) SC 201; 2006(2) SCALE 675; 2006 (5) SCJ 225; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 638; 2006 (4) Supreme 548

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Madras University Act, 1923, Tamil Nadu Private Colleges (Regulation) Act, 1976, National Council for Teacher Education (Norms & Conditions for recognition of Bachelor of Elementary Education) Regulations, 1995, National Council for Teacher Education (Form of application for recognition, the time limit of submission of application, determination of norms and standards for recognition of teacher education programmes and permission to start new course or training) Regulations, 2002

Summary: [A.] Education - Administrative - Bombay Public Trusts Act, 1950 - Societies Registration Act, 1860 - National Council for Teacher Education Act, 1993, s. 14(6) - Maharashtra Universities Act, 1994 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 226, 245, 246 - Constitution (Eighty-Sixth Amendment) Act, 2002 - Constitution (Forty-Second Amendment) Act, 1976 - All India Council For Technical Education Act, 1987 - National Council for Teacher Education (Norms & Conditions for recognition of Bachelor of Elementary Education) Regulations, 1995, regn. 5 - National Council for Teacher Education (Form of application for recognition, the time limit of submission of application, determination of norms and standards for recognition of teacher education programmes and permission to start new course or training) Regulations, 2002, regn. 6 - NCTE approved and granted recognition for B Ed college to be opened by petitioner - Application to Govt. of Maharashtra for grant of permission to start college - Policy decision not to grant 'No Objection Certificate' to any institution for starting new B Ed college, by State of Maharashtra - Held, State Govt. has no power to reject prayer of an institution or to overrule decision of NCTE - Action of State Govt, therefore, was contrary to law and has rightly been set aside by HC - Appeals dismissed.
