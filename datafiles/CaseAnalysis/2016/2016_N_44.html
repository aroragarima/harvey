Nathiya v State, represented by Inspector of Police, Vellore

Supreme Court of India

08 November 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Amitava Roy, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 796; (2016) 10 SCC 298; 2016 (4) MLJ(Crl) 618; 2016(10) SCALE 567

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: FIR, Indian Evidence Act, 1872, Place Of Occurrence, Chain, Dead Body, Medical Evidence, Informant, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Suspicion

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34 - Circumstantial Evidence - On complaint made by complainant that appellants caused death of deceased, FIR was registered and later, charge sheet was filed against appellants - Appellants were convicted by Trial Court and their conviction and sentence was upheld by HC by impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeal - Whether conviction of appellants u/s. 302 read with s. 34 of IPC could be upheld.

Held, chain of circumstantial evidence relied upon by prosecution to prove the charge is visibly incomplete and incoherent to permit conviction of the appellants on the basis thereof without any trace of doubt. Though the materials on record raised needle of suspicion towards appellants, still, prosecution failed to elevate its case from realm of 'may be true' to the plane of 'must be true' as is indispensably required in law for conviction on a criminal charge. Further, the materials on record admit of substantial doubt vis-Ã -vis the complicity of the appellants in the crime. Hence, considering the evidence adduced, conviction of appellants could not be sustained and so, appellants are entitled to benefit of doubt. Appeal allowed.
