Shri Kalanka Devi Sansthan v Maharashtra Revenue Tribunal, Nagpur and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 503; (1969) 2 SCC 616; AIR 1970 SC 439; 1970 MahLJ 1; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 936

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands (Vidarbha Region) Act, 1958, s. 2(12) - Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948, Pre. - Constitution, arts. 14, 19(1)(f) and 31A - (A) Hindu idol is juristic person - Expln. I to s. 2(12) , requires cultivation personally - (B) Hindu Law - Religious endowments - Distinction between Manager or shebait of idol and trustee -(C) Constitutional validity of both the Acts challenge on the of violation of art. 14 and 19 (1)(f) - Held, it cannot cultivate personally within expln. I to s. 2(12) - The properties of the trust in law vest in the trustee whereas in the case of an idol or a Sansthan they do not vest in the manager or the Shebait and it is the deity or the Sansthan which owns and holds the properties while only the possession and the management which vest in the manager - Both Acts are protected by art. 31A and their validity cannot be challenged on ground of violation of arts. 14 and 19(1)(f) - Appeal Dismissed.
