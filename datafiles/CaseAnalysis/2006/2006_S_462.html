Orient Paper and Industries Limited v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1329; (2006) 12 SCC 468; 2007 (5) AWC 5255; JT 2006 (10) SC 208; 2006(12) SCALE 98; 2007 (7) SCJ 438; 2006 (148) STC 649; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 790

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Summary: Agriculture & Agri.Products - Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Madhya Pradesh Upaj Mandi Adhiniyam, 1972, - Public Limited Company - Notified Agricultural Produce - Market Fee - Justifiable - Respondent issued general circular fixing liability of persons who were liable to pay market fee - Appellant filed writ petition challenging circular before HC, which upheld order of respondent - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that notified agricultural produce brought inside market area was not used for purpose of processing and end-user was manufacturer - Further, HC had clearly erred in holding that Committee was authorized to levy market fee - Whether order of HC was justifiable - Held, when a language was plain and unambiguous and admits of only one meaning no question of construction of a statute arises, for the Act speaks for itself - In Nathi Devi v Radha Devi Gupta 2004 Indlaw SC 1066 relied on if words used are capable of one construction, only then, it would not be open to Courts to adopt any other hypothetical construction on ground that such construction was more consistent with alleged object and policy of the Act - Spirit of law might well be elusive and unsafe guide and supposed spirit could certainly be not given effect to in opposition to plain language of the sections of the Act - Appeal allowed.
