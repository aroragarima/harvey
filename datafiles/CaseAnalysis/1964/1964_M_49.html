Â Raza Buland Sugar Company Limited v Municipal Board, Rampur

Supreme Court of India

30 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 377; AIR 1965 SC 895; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 970

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Summary: Water - U. P. Municipalities Act, 1916, ss. 94(3) and 131(3) - Whether there was no publication as provided by s. 131(3) r/w s. 94(3) of Act, and are provision of s. 131(3) mandatory and not complied with, all subsequent action taken for imposition of tax become bad? - No serious general inconvenience or injustice to anyone if this part of provision is held to be mandatory on other hand it will be unjust to tax-payers if this part of provision is held to be directory - Held, first part of s. 131(3) is mandatory - Provisions of s. 94(3) in this case, even though there is a technical defect inasmuch as local paper in which publication has been made is published in Urdu and not in Hindi - Substantial compliance with s. 94(3) and as decided provision to be directory it must be held that s. 131(3) has been complied with - Further, as all condition followed tax do not become void - (B)Whether tax could not be levied on most of premises belonging to appellant as there was no standpipe or other waterwork whereas water was made available to public by respondent within 600 feet of all of buildings of appellant? - Question is one of fact and High Court has pointed out that there was dispute on this question of fact and there was no sufficient material before it to enable it to come to a definite finding whether all buildings of appellant were beyond radius of 600 feet from nearest standpipe - In this state of evidence question must be left open and appellant can pursue such remedies as he may be advised to take - Appeal dismissed.
