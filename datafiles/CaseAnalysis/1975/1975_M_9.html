State of Madhya Pradesh v M. V. Narasimhan

Supreme Court of India

15 July 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 619; (1975) 2 SCC 377; (1975) SCC (Cr) 589; AIR 1975 SC 1835; 1975 (1) ALR 490; 1975 CRLJ 1639; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 6; 1976 (1) SLR 64

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Prevention Of Corruption Act 1947, s. 2 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 21 - Subsequent Act incorporating provisions of previous Act - Effect on subsequent Act of amendment on previous act - General rule and exceptions - What happens if subsequent Act incorporates provisions of previous Act? - Held, cannot be said that Act and IPC were statutes in pari materia and form part of one system - Act is a completely self-contained statute with its own provisions and has created a specific offence of criminal misconduct which is quite different from offence of bribery as defined in IPC - Both these statutes have different objects and create offences with separate ingredients.
