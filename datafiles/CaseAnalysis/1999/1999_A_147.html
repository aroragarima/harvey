E. I. D. Parry (I) Limited and Others v Assistant Commissioner of Commercial Taxes and Another

Supreme Court of India

17 December 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 902; (2000) 2 SCC 321; AIR 2000 SC 551; JT 1999 (10) SC 145; 2000 (49) KarLJ 12; 1999(7) SCALE 567; 1999 (10) SLT 459; 2000 (117) STC 457; [1999] Supp5 S.C.R. 466

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Constitution of India, 1950 - Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Act, 1959,ss.2 (r) - The Madras Sugar Factories Control Act, 1949 and Rules ,ss. 10, 10(2), 11,11 A,12 ,rr. 11(6A), 11(7) - Sugar Control Order, 1966, Cls. 3, 5(a) - A/ys. 1990-96 - Appellants were sugar manufacturers and in sales tax returns filed did not include amounts paid as planting subsidy and transport subsidy in its taxable turnover - Assistant Commissioner assessed tax after including above subsidy amounts - Aggrieved, appellants filed appeal to Deputy Commissioner but was dismissed - Appellants filed appeal to appellate authority and was pending - For years 1991-96 also appellant had followed same pattern and same view was taken by respondents and against them appeals were filed and pending - To avoid recurring of same situation every year appellants filed writ petitions in HC and were dismissed by Full Bench - Hence, instant appeals - Whether HC was justified in holding that planting and transport subsidy should be included in taxable turnover - Held, sales tax provides for levy of tax on sale or purchase of goods and tax would be determined based on taxable turnover - SC in Hindustan Sugar Mills v. State of Rajasthan, 1978 Indlaw SC 292 held that sale price would include all amounts payable by purchaser for sale of goods and excise duty - SC in M/s George Oakes (Pvt.) Ltd, v. The State of Madras & Ors., 1961 Indlaw SC 38 and Hindustan Sugar Mills v. State of Rajasthan, 1978 Indlaw SC 292 held that sales tax paid by dealer forms part of sale price - SC in Sarju Pd. Pritam Lai v. Judge, Revisions, Sales Tax, UP,1962 Indlaw ALL 105 held that weighing charges also forms part of the sale price - SC in S.C. Johar & Sons (P) Ltd v. Sales Tax Officer, Ernakulam, 1969 Indlaw SC 55 held that transport charges also forms part of sale price - So also there were number of decisions of SC holding that khodki charges paid by sugar mills to sugarcane suppliers and also freight and handling charges als forms part of sale price - Two agreements regarding planting subsidy and sale of sugarcane though appear to be independent but constitute one single transaction - Planting subsidy was given by appellants to cane growers not as social welfare measure but to purchase sugarcane from them - It was rightly pointed out by HC in State of Tamil Nadu v. National Co-operative Sugar Mills Ltd., 1991 Indlaw MAD 183 that giving planting subsidy earlier and buying sugarcane later were closely linked for supply of sugarcane and therefore sums paid by appellant as planting subsidy were rightly treated as part of sale price and should be included in taxable turnover for assessing tax liability - Transport subsidy forms part of sale consideration and hence should be included in taxable turnover - Penalty imposed by respondent was not justified as correct position of law was doubtful till judgment of HC dt.15-7-1991 in Perambalur Sugar Mills Ltd v. State of Tamil Nadu, 1990 Indlaw MAD 136 and Tribunal also in subsequent Orders till 19-3-1993 held that transport subsidy was not includible in taxable turnover - Non inclusion of planting and transport subsidies by appellants was not intentional but on bonafide belief that they could not be included in taxable turnover - Judgment of HC holding that planting and transport subsidy should be included in taxable turnover was upheld and imposition of penalty was set aside - Appeals partly allowed.
