All India Sc and St Employee's Association and Another v A. Arthur Jeen and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 April 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20097; (2001) 6 SCC 380; AIR 2001 SC 1851; 2001 (2) ESC 415; 2001 (89) FLR 1051; JT 2001 (5) SC 42; 2001 LabIC 1726; 2001(3) SCALE 378; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 1183; 2001 (3) SLR 631; 2001 (4) SLT 434; 2001 (3) Supreme 427

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Indirect Tax; Service

Keywords: Eligibility - Qualifications

Summary: Service - Indian Railway Establishment Manual, para 179 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Whether the decision of the tribunal could be challenged? - The selected candidates who were seriously affected had every right to challenge the decision of the Tribunal on all the grounds available to them - ICF Administration by its decision to cancel the Employment Notification and the panel of selected candidates unilaterally could not defeat or destroy the interest of the successful candidates - It is also submitted before us that ICF Administration pursuant to the judgment of the High Court passed in the writ petitions has given appointment to about 100 candidates from out of the panel of the selected candidates - This being the position, we are of the view that the writ petitions had not become infructuous - Held, Petition dismissed.
