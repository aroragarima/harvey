Mohar Rai and Bharath Rai v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

22 March 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 381; AIR 1968 SC 1281; 1968 ALJ 1094; 1969 (6) AllCriC 45; 1968 CRLJ 1479; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 525

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3, 115 and 157 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 109, 307 and 324 - Appeal against conviction - Prosecution case is that because of previous enmity Mohar Rai('X') shot and injured P.W. I , at the instigation of Appellant(Bharat Rai),on the evening - Legality of convictions of appellants - Appellant Mohar Rai u/s. 324 of IPC and appellant(Bharath Rai) u/ss. 324/109 of IPC is challenged - Trial Court convicted former u/s. 307 of IPC and latter u/ss. 307/109 of IPC - HC in appeal, altered their convictions - Held, Subordinate Courts relied on Bharath Rai's statement that it was Naulakh Rai who fired a pistol to contradict the statement of Mohar Rai in his complaint that a pistol was fired by Dudhnath - No portion of Ex. 4 could have been used for that purpose either u/s. 157 or s. 145 of Evidence Act - Further held, as Bharath Rai was not examined as a witness in the present case his previous statement could not have been used either to contradict his evidence or corroborate it even if it is to be held that it is a statement coming under s. 154 of CrPC - Again, circumstances noticed not merely affect the value and weight to be attached to prosecution evidence, but they persuade to doubt the prosecution version - Acquittal of appellants - Appeals allowed.
