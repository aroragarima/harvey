Registrar of Trade Marks v Ashok Chandra Rakhit Limited

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 88; AIR 1955 SC 558; 1955 (2) MLJ(SC) 76; 22 PTC 1; 1955 SCJ 562; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 252

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Summary: Intellectual Property - Trade Marks Act, 1940, ss. 13, 2, 46(4) and 76 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133 - English Act, 1905, ss. 4, 9 and 15 - Registrar, registration of trade mark, shall give no right to the exclusive use of the word 'Shree'- High Court allowed appeal and set aside order of Registrar rectifying register - Validity of HC judgment challenged - Court held, attention of HC was not drawn to an important consideration, namely, strong possibility of respondent company claiming a statutory right to word 'Shree' by virtue of registration of its trade mark and subject others to infringement actions only on strength of the registration and without proof of facts therefore, HC have not properly exercised its discretion - Appeal allowed.
