Â Kalyani Stores v State of Orissa and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 September 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 262; AIR 1966 SC 1686; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 865

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Levy Of Duty, Constitution (Fifth Amendment) Act,1955

Summary: Constitution - Excise - Constitution (Fifth Amendment) Act, 1955 - Consitution of India, 1950, arts. 301, 302, 304, 305 and 372 - Bihar And Oriss Excise Act, 1915, s. 27 - Notification D/-31-03-1961 - Foreign Liquor - 'Counter vailing duty' - (A) Whether impugned notification enhancing levy must be regarded as invalid? - Impugned notification infringes guarantee of freedom u/art. 301, and may be saved only if it falls within exceptions contained in arts. 302, 303 and 304 - Arts. 302 and 303 are apparently not attracted and have not been relied upon, and notification does not comply with requirements of Constitution contained in art.304 - Held, impugned notification enhancing levy by must, therefore, be regarded as invalid as offending art.304 and is therefore unenforceable - (B) Expression 'Countervailing duty' - Expression 'countervailing duties' has not been defined in Constitution or Bihar and Oriss Act, 1915 - Therefore, court has to depend upon its etymological sense and context in which it has been used in Entry 51 - Further held, in its etymological sense, it means to counter-balance; to avail against with equal force or virtue; to compensate for something or serve as an equivalent of or substitute for - Appeal partly allowed.
