Radhu v State Of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

14 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, B. Sudarshan Reddy

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 898; (2007) 12 SCC 57; 2007 (9) ADJ 358; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 847; 2007 CRLJ 4704; 2007 (4) JCC 2603; JT 2007 (11) SC 91; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 158; 2007(11) SCALE 96

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Wrongful Confinement

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 376, 323, 342 r/w 34 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, absence of injuries on the private parts of the victim will not by itself falsify the case of rape, nor construed as evidence of consent - Similarly, the opinion of a doctor that there was no evidence of any sexual intercourse or rape, may not be sufficient to disbelieve the accusation of rape by the victim - No evidence is placed about results of the examination of the vaginal swabs and petticoat - Medical evidence does not corroborate the case of sexual intercourse or rape - Evidence of prosecutrix when read as a whole, is full of discrepancies and does not inspire confidence - Appeal allowed.
