The Public Passenger Service Limited v M. A. Khader And Two Others

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, K. Subba Rao, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 179; AIR 1966 SC 489; [1966] 36 Comp Cas 1; 1966 (1) CompLJ 1; 1966 (1) MLJ(SC) 23; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 683

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1913 - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 155, 237 and 402 - Arts. 29 and 30 of Articles of Association of appellant Co. - Whether forfeiture of shares is invalid due to non mention of payment of interest on calls on shares? - Held, a proper notice u/art. 29 is a condition precedent to forfeiture, u/art. 30 - Here, notice u/art. 29 is defective, and condition precedent is not complied with - Forfeiture was invalid - Having found that notice was defective and forfeiture was invalid, court could not arbitrarily refuse relief to respondents - Unwarranted proceedings u/ss. 402 and 237, and other vexatious proceedings started by respondents have no relation to invalidity of forfeiture and relief of rectification and are not valid grounds for refusing relief - Appeals dismissed.
