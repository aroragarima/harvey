Supreme Court of India

04 May 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, R.S. Bachawat, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 85; AIR 1967 SC 295; [1966] 36 Comp Cas 639; 1966 (2) CompLJ 151; 1966 (2) SCJ 623; [1966] Supp S.C.R. 311

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Companies Act, 1956, s. 237(b) - Constitution, arts. 14, 19(1)(g) - Limitation Act, 1908 - Company - Provision of s. 237(b) of 1956 Act under judicial scrutiny - (A) Existence of any intent to defraud, fraud, misfeasance or other misconduct is to be checked considering - Meaining of ' If in the opinion of Central Government' , exercise of discretion, materiality of circumstances like delay, bungling and faulty planning of project etc. on the facts of the case - (B) Whether s. 237(b), violative of arts. 14 or 19(1)(g) or is the company a citizen u/art. 5 and can claim benefit of art. 19(1)(g) - (C) Under the procedural requirements for deciding disputed question u/art. 226 of Constitution, what happen when avertment in affidavit is not based on personal knowledge and on whom burden of proof lies in an allegation as to bad faith, or indirect motive or purpose - Circumstances suggesting inference of what has been set out in sub-cls. (i), (ii) or (iii) must be made out - Intention to deprive by deceit - Held, these circumstances could not by themselves have suggested intent to defraud or fraudulent management - And s. 237 (b) is not violative of arts. 14 or 19 (1) (g) - Exercise of discretion by HC would not be lightly interfered with while hearing appeal by special leave - Source of information must be disclosed and affidavits should be modelled on lines of O. 19, r. 3 of CPC - Allegation cannot be held established except on clear proof thereof - Order Accordingly.
