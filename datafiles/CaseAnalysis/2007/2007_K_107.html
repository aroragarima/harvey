Makhan Singh (D) By Lrs v Kulwant Singh

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 288; (2007) 10 SCC 602; AIR 2007 SC 1808; 2007 (3) AWC 2662; 2007 (6) Bom.C.R. 397; 2007 (4) G.L.R. 3260; 2007 (3) JhrCR(SC) 107; JT 2007 (5) SC 288; 2007 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1703; 2007 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1761; 2007 (3) MLJ(SC) 834; 2007 (147) PLR 65; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 826; 2007(5) SCALE 226

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Co-Parceners, Self- Acquired Property

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, s. 100 - Suit for specific performance - High Court reversed the findings of trial court and first appellate court - Appeal against - Held, High Court has not made a simpliciter re-appraisal of the evidence to arrive at conclusions different from those of the courts below, but has corrected an error as to the onus of proof on the existence or otherwise of a Joint Hindu Family property - Appeals disposed of.
