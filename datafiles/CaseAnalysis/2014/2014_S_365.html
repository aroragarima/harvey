Homi Rajvansh v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

27 March 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, N.V. Ramana, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 190; (2014) 12 SCC 556; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 1876; 2014 (2) JCC 1231; 2014 (3) RCR(Criminal) 368; 2014(4) SCALE 190; [2014] 4 S.C.R. 475

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 409, 411, 420, 467, 468, 471 - Cheating - Criminal proceedings - Quashing of - A case was registered against accused persons/appellant and respondent no. 3 u/ss. 409, 411,420, 467, 468, 471 of IPC - Respondent no. 3 filed petition before HC for quashing of criminal proceedings and same was allowed - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified -

Held, HC committed an error in quashing complaint against respondent no. 3 without hearing appellant who was co-accused in case as their alleged roles were interconnected - HC committed an error in coming to finding against appellant without appellant being party in writ petition filed by respondent no. 3 - Perusal of impugned order clearly showed that HC simply agreed with submissions of respondent no. 3 against appellant herein without giving him an opportunity of being heard - HC in impugned order, over exercised its jurisdiction which was complete violation of principles of natural justice since appellant, who was a co-accused, was not heard on allegations levelled against him respondent no. 3 - Court observed that extraordinary power should be exercised sparingly and with great care and caution - HC would be justified in exercising said power when it was imperative to exercise same in order to prevent injustice - Appellant was not impleaded as one of parties before HC, specific finding against his alleged role, based on submissions of respondent no. 3 herein without giving an opportunity of being heard, could not be sustained - Impugned judgment was set aside and matter was remitted to HC for fresh disposal - Appeal allowed.
