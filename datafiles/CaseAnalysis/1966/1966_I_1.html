Commissioner of Wealth Tax West Bengal v Imperial Tobacco Company of India Limited

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 86; AIR 1967 SC 230; [1966] 61 ITR 461; [1966] Supp S.C.R. 174

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Wealth Tax Act, 1957, ss.17(b),16(3) ,25(2), 27(1), 27(3) - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 34(1)(b) - Assessment - Deduction - Claim - Entitlement - Respondent had submitted wealth tax for A/Ys 1957- 58 and 1958- 59 and claimed deduction for said A/Ys - Wealth tax Officer had accepted claim for deduction - Later on Commissioner of Wealth tax u/s. 25(2) of the Act disallowed claim for A/Y 1957-58 - On 2-6-1960, Wealth tax Officer issued notices u/s. 17(b) of the Act for reassessment of net wealth for A/Ys. 1957-58 and 1958-59, for which orders were passed u/s. 16(3), r/w s. 17(b) of the Act and disallowed claim which was earlier allowed - Aggrieved respondent appealed before Assistant Commissioner which upheld decision of Wealth tax Officer with respect to reassessment - Respondent appealed before Tribunal contending notices issued u/s. 17(b) of the Act was invalid which was accepted - Appellant/Commissioner appealed before Tribunal for references u/s. 27(1) of the Act which was rejected - Aggrieved appellant appealed before HC u/s. 27(3) of the Act, for direction to Tribunal to state a case, which was rejected - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of HC was sustainable - Held, language u/s. 17(b) of the Act was in pari materia with language u/s. 34(1)(b) of the Act and decisions u/s. 34(1)(b) of the 1922 Act, would be relevant in construing scope and effect u/s. 17(b) of the Act - There does appear to be divergence of opinion among HC with respect to meaning of word "information" u/s. 34(1)(b) of the 1922 Act, and in view of that divergence it was held that a question of law would arise as to interpretation of word "information" in u/s. 17(b) of the Act and should have been referred by Tribunal - Therefore appeals were allowed and order of HC was set aside and directed the Tribunal to state a case referring question of law according to appellant and Tribunal was free to decide whether to refer matter to HC u/s. 27(1) of the Act or to this Court u/s. 27 (3A) of the Act - Appeals allowed.
