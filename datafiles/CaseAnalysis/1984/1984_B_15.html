Bhag Mal v Ch. Parbhu Ram and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 October 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Varadarajan, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 304; (1985) 1 SCC 61; AIR 1985 SC 150; 1985 (1) CCC 345; 1984(2) SCALE 702; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 1099; 1985 UJ 537

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 27 (1), 116, 101, 97(1), 100(1)(D)and 100(3) - Conduct Of Election Rules, 1961, rr. 64, 63(2), 63(6)and 56 A(2)(G) - What is effect of omission to make recrimination petition by returned candidate ? - In absence of recrimination petition Election Tribunal has no jurisdiction to go into question whether any wrong votes were counted in favour of election petitioner - Held, Election Tribunal (HC) was justified in directing recount of rejected ballot papers of respondent 1 and declining to take into account 8 ballot papers relating to appellant found by District Judge (Vigilance), Punjab to have been improperly rejected in absence of a recrimination application u/s. 97 (1) of Act - Further held, election of appellant had been materially affected by improper rejection of 14 ballot papers relating to respondent 1 - Respondent 1 is entitled to be declared to have been duly elected - Appeal allowed.
