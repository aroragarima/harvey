Collector of Central Excise, Ahmedabad v Itec Private Limited, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1816; (2002) 7 SCC 473; AIR 2002 SC 3322; [2002] 112 Comp Cas 470; 2002 (83) ECC 743; 2002 (105) ECR 6; 2002 (145) E.L.T. 280; 2003 (1) G.L.R. 533; JT 2002 (7) SC 305; 2002 (4) RCR(Civil) 211; 2002 (52) RLT 501; 2002(6) SCALE 553; 2002 (4) SCJ 381; 2002 (5) SLT 405; [2002] Supp2 S.C.R. 492; 2002 (6) Supreme 483

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Excise; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 4(4)(c) and 11-A - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 9(2) - Notification No. 71/78 D/- 1-03-1978 - (A) Whether CEGAT erred in holding that International Co. was so associated with respondent that they had interest in each other and were related persons? - It was found that respondent and M/s International were having common Directors and that they were relatives of one another, a further finding was also noted that both companies were family concerns and were beneficiaries of their ventures and that benefits of both concerns are shared by members of one and same family - Held, respondent and International Co. are not related persons - (B) Whether, on facts and in circumstances, it is open to Revenue to invoke provisions of s. 11-A? - It is clear that respondent had disclosed correct facts including price at which goods were sold to related person and difference in price - Further held, larger period of limitation provided in s. 11-A is not available to Revenue - Appeal allowed in regard to 'related person' - In other respect it is dismissed.
