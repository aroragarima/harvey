Nature Lovers Movement v State Of Kerala And Others

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, B.N. Agrawal, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1136; (2009) 5 SCC 373; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1573; 2009(4) SCALE 132; [2009] 4 S.C.R. 687

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Keywords: Forest Conservation, Reserve Forests, Sustainable Development, Afforestation, Kerala Government Land Assignment Act, 1960, Kerala Forest Act, 1961, Kerala Survey and Boundaries Act, 1961, Travancore Forest Regulations, 1068, Gujarat Minor Mineral Rules, 1966

Summary: Constitution - Environment, Wildlife and Animals - Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980, s. 2 - Kerala Government Land Assignment Act, 1960, s. 7 - Kerala Land Assignment (Regularization of Occupation of Forest Lands Prior to 1.1.1977) Special Rules, 1993 - Retrospective or prospective operation - Determination of - Before the enactment of the Forest Act, the Government of Kerala had taken a policy decision to regularize the illegal/unauthorised occupation and encroachments of forest land made prior to 1.1.1977 and also to remove all unauthorised occupation/encroachments made on and after 1.1.1977 - After taking note of the recommendations made in the Forest Ministers Conference and committee appointed by it, Central Government issued guidelines for grant of approval to the decision taken by the State Governments before the enforcement of the Forest Act, i.e., 25.10.1980 to regularize encroachments made on forest land and/or use thereof for non-forest purpose - Writ petition filed thereagainst was dismissed - HC held that Forest Act is prospective in operation and the provisions contained therein are not applicable to the cases in which the forest land has already been used for non-forest purpose prior to 25.10.1980 - Whether s. 2 of the Forest Act is prospective in operation? - Held, approval granted by Central Government necessarily implies that where the Government of Kerala had not taken any policy decision to regularize pre-25.10.1980 occupation/encroachment of forest land no order for regularization of such occupation/encroachment can be passed without obtaining prior approval of the Central Government in terms of s. 2 of the Forest Act - Policy decision taken by the Government of Kerala to assign 28, 588.159 hectares of forest land to unauthorized occupants/ encroachers after seeking approval from the Central Government does not suffer from any legal infirmity and the HC rightly declined to interfere with the said decision - Appeal disposed of.
