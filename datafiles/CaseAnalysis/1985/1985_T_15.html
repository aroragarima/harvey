Dr. K. George Thomas v The C.I.T. Kerala, Ernakulam

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, V.D. Tulzapurkar

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 334; (1985) Supp SCC 580; AIR 1986 SC 98; 1985 (49) CTR 204; 1986 (52) CTR 382; [1985] 156 ITR 412; 1985(2) SCALE 1135; [1985] Supp2 S.C.R. 936; [1985] 23 TAXMAN 46; 1986 (2) TLR 96; 1985 UJ 1057

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Capital Gains, Business Income or Loss

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax - Receipts arising out of vocation - (A) Taxability - (B) Receipt whether of revenue character - Burden of proof - Finding of facts by tribunal - Scope of interference by High Court - (A) Held, taxable - (B) Burden is on revenue but availability of exemption is for assessee to establish - HC cannot afresh go into facts overruling facts found by tribunal unless there is a question to that effect challenging facts found by tribunal - Appeal dismissed.
