State Of U.P. v Babu Ram

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 509; (2000) 4 SCC 515; (2000) SCC (Cr) 845; AIR 2000 SC 1735; 2000 ALJ 1439; 2000 (2) CCR 75; 2000 (2) Crimes 260; 2000 CRLJ 2457; 2000 (2) JCC 447; JT 2000 (4) SC 456; 2000 (2) RCR(Criminal) 618; 2000(3) SCALE 215; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 1201; 2000 (3) SLT 614; 2000 (3) Supreme 722

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3, 8 and 27 - IPC, 1860, s. 300 - Triple murder of parents and brother by accused who himself stating about motive i.e. deceased declined for partition of land - Disinterment of dead bodies from spot pointed out by him - (A) Motive - Whether a prosecution must fail because it failed to prove the motive or even whether inability to prove motive would weaken the prosecution to any perceptible limit (B) Validity of rejection of sturdy circumstance on flimsy grounds - (C) Misleading by accused, incredible explaination if can be concluded as not given - (D) Circumstancial evidences, belated interrogation, if can be rejected - (E) Deposition by witnesses, judicial scrutiny - Held, motive factor cannot be said to have weakened case of prosecution - Not proper - Cannot be so concluded - Cannot be rejected - Different yardstick cannot be prescribed for different categories of witnesses - Conviction Restored and respondent is sentenced to imprisonment for life u/s. 302 IPC - Appeal allowed.
