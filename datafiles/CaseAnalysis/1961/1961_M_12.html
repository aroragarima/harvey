Haji Sk. Subhan v Madhorao

Supreme Court of India

16 October 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 400; AIR 1962 SC 1230; 1962 JabLJ 498; 1962 MPLJ 265; 1962 (2) SCJ 575; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 123; [1962] Supp1 S.C.R. 123

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 47 - Whether respondent is not entitled to execute decree for possession as he had lost proprietory right which entitled him to get possession? - Respondent not in possession of land in suit on date of vesting - Fields in suit could not be his home-farm and therefore, he has no right to retain possession over them - Land cannot come within definition of 'home farm' which had not been actually recorded as sir and khudkasht in name of proprietor or which had not been acquired by proprietor by surrender from tenants after years 1948-49 till date of vesting - Held, respondent was not recorded and could not have been recorded to have khudkhast in land in suit in papers of 1948-49 and therefore, could not have claimed this land as his 'home-farm' - His proprietary lost, got vested in state - Had no subsisting right to recover possession - Though khudkasht of respondent was wrongly recorded as occupancy land of appellant - But an erroneous recording of a khudkasht land as an occupancy land would not in law alter real character of that land - Appeal allowed.
