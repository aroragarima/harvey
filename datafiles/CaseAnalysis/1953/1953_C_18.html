Â Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v K. R. M. T. T. Thiagaraja Chetty and Company

Supreme Court of India

14 October 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Ghulam Hasan, M. Patanjali Sastri, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Vivian Bose, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 59; AIR 1953 SC 527; [1953] 24 ITR 525; 1953 (2) MLJ(SC) 823; 1953 SCJ 734; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 258

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss.4(1)(b)(i),10,12,13,66 - Indian Independence Act,1947 - Agreement - Remuneration - Interest - Assessment - Assessee was registered firm and managing agent of Company owning spinning mill - Firm also conducted insurance business and business of ginning cotton in ginning factory at another place - Under terms of agreement managing agents were entitled to a remuneration of Rs. 1, 000 per mensem and commission of I per cent. on all purchases, I per cent. on all sales and 10 per cent - Commission on net profits of mills before allowing for depreciation - Whether profit of business could be computed during the accounting year - Held, income where there was condition that commission will not be payable until expiry of definite period or making up of account, it might be said with some justification - Income had not accrued, but there was no such condition - Profit of business could be computed only after the 31st March, and the commission could not be subjected to tax when it was no more than a mere right to receive - If profits of company are said to have accrued on 31st of March, upon a parity of reasoning, it must be conceded that commission also accrued on the same date - Accrual of commission as to do with accrual of profits - It was faintly suggested that managing agency was not business but this was immaterial for income-tax purposes - Appeal allowed.
