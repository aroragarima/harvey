Sushil Kumar Jain v Manoj Kumar and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 586; (2009) 14 SCC 38; AIR 2009 SC 2544; 2009 (76) ALR 138; 2009 (3) AWC 2658; 2010 (4) Bom.C.R. 437; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 899; 2009 (2) RCR(Rent) 179; 2009 (4) RLW 3562; 2009(7) SCALE 103; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 944

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: (1) Code of Civil Procedure, 1908-Order VI, Rule 17-Amendment of pleadings-Amendment in written statement sought-Not even issues yet framed-Documents not yet filed-Evidence not yet adduced-Proviso to Rule 17, Order VI not applicable-As trial has not yet commenced-Impugned orders set aside-Amendment allowed.

(2) Code of Civil Procedure, 1908-Order VI, Rule 17-Amendment of pleadings-Amendments of plaint and written statement-Not necessarily governed by same principle-Adding new ground of defence-Or substituting or altering defence-Does not raise same problem-As adding, altering, substituting new cause of action.
