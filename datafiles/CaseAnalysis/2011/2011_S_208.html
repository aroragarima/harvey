K. Balakrishnan Nambiar and others v State Of Karnataka & Ors

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 327; (2011) 5 SCC 353; AIR 2011 SC 1628; JT 2011 (6) SC 18; 2011 (5) KarLJ 218; 2011(5) SCALE 407; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 1143

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Land & Property

Keywords: Tamil Nadu Forest Act, 1882

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Land & Property - Forest Conservation Act, 1980 - Forest conservation - Non-renewal of lease - Lease was made in the favour of original lessee for a period of 50 years for the purpose of raising areca nut plantation - Thereafter, on the death of the original lessee, his legal representatives, alienated the lease hold rights in favour of the appellant - On expiry of lease period, appellant submitted an application for renewal of the lease - However, as no action was taken, apprehending eviction, appellant filed a writ petition - HC directed the respondents to consider the application for the renewal of the lease in accordance with law - Appellant thereafter made another representation to respondent No. 2 seeking renewal of the lease - State Government cancelled the lease deed and directed the appellant to hand over the possession of the lease hold land back to the forest department - Writ petition filed thereagainst was dismissed - On appeal, DB held that the issue was concluded by SC in the case of T.N. Godavarman Thirumulkpad Vs. Union of India, 1996 INDLAW SC 2014 wherein it had been held that no forest area shall be used for nonforestal activities - Instant appeal - Held, Government had given cogent and valid reasons for non-renewal of the lease - Intention of the Government was to develop naturally grown forests over the lands and this could only be done if the possession was taken by the Government - Order upheld - Appeal dismissed.
