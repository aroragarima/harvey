Chembra Orchard Produce Limited and Others v Regional Director of Company Affairs and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1951; (2009) 2 SCC 547; AIR 2009 SC 1278; [2009] 147 Comp Cas 677; 2009 (1) CompLJ 1; JT 2009 (1) SC 412; 2009(1) SCALE 1; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 45

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Objects, Debenture-Holder, Publication Of Notice, Dissenting

Summary: Companies Act, 1956 - Whether an application filed by the Company under s. 391 (1) seeking directions to convene a meeting of creditors and members to consider a scheme of amalgamation is required to be heard and decided ex-parte as per r. 67 of the Companies (Court) Rules, 1959? - Held, there is a clear dichotomy between the threshold stage of issuance of directions to convene a meeting and the subsequent stage of a notice of meeting which is contemplated by r. 73 and for that precise reason r. 67 states that the summons shall be moved ex-parte - If at the threshold stage of directions to convene a meeting hearing is required to be given to the members as held in the impugned judgment the scheme of the Companies (Court) Rules 1959 will become unworkable - Appeals allowed.
