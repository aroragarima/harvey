D. P. Mishra v Kamal Narain Sharma and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 386; (1970) 3 SCC 558; AIR 1971 SC 856; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 257

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Representation of the People Act, 1951, s. 99(1) - Constitution, art. 136 - Press and Registration of Books Act, 1867, ss. 7 and 19D(b) - (A) Election Petition - Corrupt practice - Non-party -Opportunity to show cause - (B) Appeal by special leave u/art. 136 of Constitution - Supreme Court in appeal will not normally re-appraise evidence for acquittal of corrupt practice - (C) Presumption u/s. 7 of 1867 Act - (D) The annual report published by the Press Registrar containing the name of a person as editor of a newspaper - Whether displace the effect of the return published u/s. l9D(b) of 1867 Act containing not the name of such person but some other person - Held, non-party must be given opportunity to show cause why he should not be named - SC will not normally re-appraise unless High Court has misconceived the evidence or the conclusion is basically faulty or perverse or where the procedure adopted entails miscarriage of justice or there are similar reasons - Force of presumption u/s.7 in a charge u/s. 123 (4) of 1951 Act would differ with circumstances - Being only for the information of the Govt. and not made under any statutory provision cannot displace the effect of the return - Appeal Dismissed.
