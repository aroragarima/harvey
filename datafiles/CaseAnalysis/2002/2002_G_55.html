P. Tulsi Das and others v Government of A.P. and others

Supreme Court of India

24 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1366; (2003) SCC (L&S) 51; (2003) 1 SCC 364; AIR 2003 SC 43; AIR 2002 SCW 4433; JT 2002 (8) SC 560; 2002 LabIC 3567; 2003 (1) LLN 2; 2002(8) SCALE 250; 2003 (1) SLJ 164; 2002 (8) SLR 1; 2002 (6) SLT 248; [2002] Supp3 S.C.R. 306; 2002 (7) Supreme 524

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Service

Summary: Constitution - Education - Andhra Pradesh Educational Service Untrained Teachers (Regulation of Services and Fixation of Pay) Act, 1991, ss.2, 3 - Challenge to the constitutional validity of ss.2 and 3 of the Act - Due to dearth of trained graduates for appointment as Teachers theGovt.passed orders to appoint untrained graduates in the minimum pay of Rs 130 - On the Tribunal's direction that even the untrained graduates in Humanities should be allowed the minimum of Rs 130, theGovt.created supernumerary posts of B Ed Assistants with retrospective effect - As a result, even SGBT Teachers who were appointed in the scale of pay of Rs 80-150 became entitled to the scale of pay attached to the post of School Assistants - Held, such rights, benefits and perquisites acquired by the Teachers concerned cannot be said to be rights acquired otherwise than in accordance with law or brushed aside and trampled at the sweet will and pleasure of the Govt, with impunity - Provisions of s.s 2 and 3(a) insofar as they purport to take away the rights from 10-2-1967 and obligates those who had them to repay or restore it back to the State is hereby struck down as arbitrary, unreasonable and expropriatory and as such is violative of art.14 and 16 of the constitution - Appeal disposed of.
