Maru Ram and others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 November 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, P.N. Bhagwati, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 577; (1981) 1 SCC 107; (1981) SCC (Cr) 112; AIR 1980 SC 2147; 1980 CRLJ 1440; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 1196

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Victimology

Summary: KRISHNA IYER, J.

(A) Constitution - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 72,161 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 433A - Remission - Constitutionality of provision - Exercise of powers - Petitioners stated that Parliament had broken law of Constitution by enacting s. 433-A of CrPC - All petitioners were either sentenced by Court to imprisonment for life in cases where conviction was for offences carrying death penalty as graver alternative or were persons whom Court had actually sentenced to death which was since commuted by appropriate Govt. u/s. 433(a) of CrPC to life imprisonment - Before enactment of s. 433-A of CrPC that 'lifers' were treated, in matter of remissions and release from jail, like others sentenced to life terms for lesser offences which did not carry death penalty as either/or possibility - Petitioners challenged vires of s. 433A of CrPC which compels 'caging' of two classes of prisoners, at least for fourteen eternal internal years, regardless of benign remissions and compassionate concessions sanctioned by prison law and human justice - Hence, instant Petitions - Whether s. 433A of CrPC could be invalidated as indirectly violative of arts.72, 161 of Constitution - Held, it was apparent that two powers, one constitutional and other statutory, were co-extensive - It could not agree that power which was creature of Code could be equated with high prerogative vested by Constitution in highest functionaries of Union and States - S. 433A of CrPC was actually social piece of legislation which by one stroke seeks to prevent dangerous criminals from repeating offences and on other protects society from harm and distress caused to innocent persons - Thus, in cases where s. 433A of CrPC applied, no question of reduction of sentence arose at all unless President of India or Governors chose to exercise their wide powers u/arts.72 or 161 of the Constitution which had to be exercised according to sound legal principles and any reduction or modification in deterrent punishment would far from reforming criminal be counterproductive - Parliament by enacting s. 433A of CrPC had rejected reformative character of punishment - President of India u/art.72 and State Govt. u/art.161 of the Constitution had absolute and unfettered powers to grant pardon, reprieves, remissions and that power could neither be altered, modified or interfered with by any statutory provision - It could not be doubted as proposition of law that where power was vested in very high authority, it should be presumed that said authority would act properly and carefully after objective consideration of all aspects - Therefore, there were no real inconsistency between s. 433A of CrPC and arts.72, 161 of the Constitution and all grounds on which constitutional validity of s. 433A of CrPC was challenged should fail - Hence, s. 433A of CrPC could not be invalidated as indirectly violative of arts.72, 161 of Constitution - Petitions dismissed.

KOSHAL, J

(B) Constitution - Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 433A - Constitutionality of provisions - Sustainability - Whether s. 433A of CrPC could be invalidated as indirectly violative of provision of the Constitution - Held, contrary proposition would mean stepping of judiciary into field of legislature which was not permissible - It was outside scope of inquiry undertaken by Court into vires of provisions contained in s. 433A of CrPC to find out extent to which object of reformation was sought to be achieved - Person who had committed murder in heat of passion could not repeat his act at all later in life and reformation process in his case need not be time-consuming -Reformative aspect of punishment if, were to be given priority and predominance in every case murderer could deserve, in given set of circumstances, no more than six months' period of incarceration while thief could have to be trained into better ways of life from social point of view over long period and death penalty - Hence, s. 433A of CrPC could not be attacked on ground that they were hit by provision the Constitution inasmuch as they were arbitrary or irrational as they ignore reformative aspect of punishment - Petitions dismissed.
