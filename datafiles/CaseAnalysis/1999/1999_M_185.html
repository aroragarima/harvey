Marshall Sons and Company (I) Limited v Sahi Oretrans Private Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 947; (1999) 2 SCC 325; AIR 1999 SC 882; 1999 (35) ALR 458; 1999 (3) Bom.C.R. 80; JT 1999 (1) SC 326; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 311

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Unpaid rents

Summary: Rent Control - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Subletting - Unpaid rents - Eviction - Legality - Appellant filed eviction suit against respondent before Trial Court - Appellant obtained decree for eviction - On appeal before Appellant Court decree was confirmed and decree of dismissal against defendant no. 2 to 4 was also confirmed - Aggrieved appellant filed petition before HC - HC confirmed decree against original defendant no. 1 and order passed by Trial Court dismissing suit against them was also reversed by passing a decree against original defendant's no. 2, 3 and 4 - Hence instant appeal - Held, without expressing any opinion on merits of contentions of parties in pending suit, SC thought it was appropriate to dispose of this matter - Suit in question be disposed of as expeditiously as possible, preferably within one year - Respondents were directed to pay mesne profits/compensation at rate of Rs.10/- per sq. ft. from 1984 till today and at rate of Rs. 20/- from today till disposal of suit - While making this payment, payments already made should be adjusted - So far as arrears were concerned, it be paid in 12 equal monthly installments - Appeal disposed of.
