Lal Bhagwant Singh v Rai Sahib Lala Sri Kishen Das

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 35; AIR 1953 SC 136; 1953 ALJ 249; 1953 (2) ILR(All) 367; 1953 SCJ 188; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 559

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Decreeholder, Debt Laws

Summary: Civil Procedure - Uttar Pradesh Encumbered Estates Act, 1934 s.4 - Mortgage - Compromise deed - Decree - Execution - 'X' obtained decree of compromise for Rs. 3,88,300-26 with pendent lite and future interest based on two mortgages executed in his favour by 'Y' - Compromise deed contained that 'Y' would sell to 'X' some villages out of mortgaged properties selected and sufficient to satisfy decree and reserved right to get back sold villages after five years and before expiry of fifteen years on payment of stipulated price - 'X' made selection of eight villages and deeds of sale and relinquishment was prepared and executed - 'Y' filed application u/s.4 of 1934 Act for liquidation of his debts before Civil Judge, which altered decreetal amount and directed to pay in installments - 'X' filed Revision against said order before Chief court which was allowed and called 'Y' to execute sale deed in his favour - On his failure to comply with this order, court executed deed of sale in his favour and delivered possession of property - Hence instant appeal by appellant/legal heir of 'Y' - Whether appellant is discharged of his debt by sale of villages by 'X'/decreeholder.

Held, that sale being inevitable under the amended decree the 'judgment-debtor/'Y' was clearly not entitled to restitution. It was held in, Gansu Ram v. Parvati Kuer 1941 AIR(Pat) 130, followed, that where a judgment-debtor could not have paid even the reduced decretal amount and the sum realized at the sale was less than the decretal amount the situation could not have been altered in any way had the decree been modified before, instead of after the sale, and the judgment -debtor could not invoke the provisions of section 144, except by showing that the sale was in substance and truth a consequence of the error in the original decree. Thus, observations made in instant case have apposite application to the facts and circumstances and there is no merit in either of these appeals. Appeals dismissed.
