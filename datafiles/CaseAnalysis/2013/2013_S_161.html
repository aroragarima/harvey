Nirmala J. Jhala v State Of Gujarat & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

18 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 159; (2013) 4 SCC 301; AIR 2013 SC 1513; 2013(2) ALL MR 960; 2013 (2) CLT(SC) 51; 2013 (138) FLR 227; 2013 (3) G.L.R. 2308; JT 2013 (6) SC 407; 2013 (5) KarLJ 447; 2013 (3) MLJ 621; 2013(4) SCALE 579; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 128; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 195; 2013 (4) SLR 127; 2013 (4) WLN(SC) 133

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Gujarat Civil Services (Discipline & Appeal) Rules, 1971

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Inquiry against service - Punishment of compulsory retirement - Sustainability - Appeal was preferred against the impugned judgment and order passed in application by way of which the challenge to punishment order of compulsory retirement of the appellant was turned down - Whether in the instant case the material collected in preliminary enquiry could be used against the appellant - Held, in the preliminary enquiry, dept. placed reliance upon the statements made by the accused/complainant - in his statement has given the same version as he has deposed in regular enquiry. Shri Gajjar did not utter a single word about the meeting with the appellant on 17.8.1993, as he had stated that he had asked the accused/complainant to pay Rs. 20,000/- as was agreed with by Shri P.K. Pancholi, advocate. Of course, Shri C.B. Gajjar , complainant, has definitely reiterated the stand he had taken in his complaint. The chargesheet served upon the appellant contained 12 charges. Only first charge related to the incident dated 17.8.1993 was in respect of the case of the complainant. The other charges related to various other civil and criminal cases. The same were for not deciding the application for interim reliefs etc - Chargesheet was accompanied by the statement of imputation, list of witnesses and the list of documents. However, it did not say that so far as Charge No. 1 was concerned, the preliminary enquiry report or the evidence collected therein, would be used/relied upon against the appellant - There is nothing on record to show that either the preliminary enquiry report or the statements recorded therein, particularly, by the complainant/accused or Shri C.B. Gajjar, advocate, had been exhibited in regular inquiry. In absence of information in the chargesheet that such report/statements would be relied upon against the appellant, it was not permissible for the Enquiry Officer or the High Court to rely upon the same. Natural justice is an inbuilt and inseparable ingredient of fairness and reasonableness. Strict adherence to the principle is required, whenever civil consequences follow up, as a result of the order passed. Natural justice is a universal justice - S.L. Kapoor v. Jagmohan, 1980 Indlaw SC 287, relied on - Appeal allowed.
