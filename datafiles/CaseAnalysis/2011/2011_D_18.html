State of West Bengal and others v Debasish Mukherjee and others

Supreme Court of India

14 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 655; (2011) 14 SCC 187; 2011 (5) ESC 714; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 1077; 2011 (5) SLR 782

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Allahabad High Court Officers and Staff (Conditions of Service and Conduct) Rules, 1976, Rajasthan High Court (Conditions of Service of Staff) Rules, 1953, West Bengal Services Revision of Pay and Allowances Rules, 1970, Calcutta High Court Service Rules, 1960, WB (ROPA) Rules, 1981, W.B. ROPA Rules, 1990

Summary: Service - Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India,1950,arts. 14, 229(2) - West Bengal Services Revision of Pay and Allowances Rules, 1970,rr. 42 (1)(ii), 49,55(4) - High salary for junior - Seniors claimed pay at par with junior - Validity - 'X' was appointed as section writer/typist in Calcutta HC - Firstly was promoted as typist in grade 1 and secondly awarded higher scale under benefit scheme and pay scale increased - Employees senior to 'X' submitted representation to Chief Justice (CJ)requesting to enhance their salaries at par with 'X' u/r.55(4) of the 1970 Rules - Matter referred to special committee of three Judges and said committee recommended that representation be rejected as 'X' was given undue benefits and under settled law one could not claim same benefits on basis of such illegality - Hence, was rejected by CJ - Later Dy. Secretary of Judicial Department by memo dt.5-12-2000 stated benefits granted to 'X' and others were in order - Based on that memo 50 employees senior to'X' made another representation dt.10.1.2001 to CJ for pay hike - Referred to three judge Special Committee and said committee recommended pay hike on principle of pay protection - As recommended by three judge committee 50 senior employees were given pay hike and bills sent to Pay & Accounts Office and were returned for want of governors approval - HC submitted that CJ was empowered and needs no approval of governor but was not accepted by Pay & Accounts Office stating that they had no authority to pay instant bill without directions from State Govt. and requested HC to resubmit bills after obtaining clarification from state government and consent of Governor - State Govt. informed HC that 'X' promotion was erroneous and he was not entitled to Grade I promotion as not confirmed in that post - Accordingly Registrar of HC corrected service book of 'X' and confirmed same to Accountant General -State Govt. requested HC to review pay of 50 employees in issue as admittedly pay fixed to 'X' was erroneous - Meanwhile Respondents and State filed separate writs in HC and Single judge by common order dt. 17-11-2003 held that r.55(4) of the 1970 Rules was not applicable as two conditions of Rule were absent and respondents could not claim illegal benefits given to and dismissed writ petitions of respondents and allowed writs of State - Aggrieved, respondents filed appeals to DB and were allowed by DB holding that once rules framed by CJ were approved by Governor CJ would stand on equal footing with Governor and would not require any further approval - Aggrieved, State Govt.filed instant appeals - Whether respondents were entitled to pay hike at par with u/r.55 (4) of the 1970 Rules - Held, r.55(4) of the 1970 Rules would not attract as was rightly affirmed by Single judge and DB- Facts clearly show that 'X' was given higher pay for wholly erroneous reasons - When two benefits erroneously given to 'X' were deleted there would be no ground for seniors to claim any benefit on basis of pay parity - Seniors were neither entitled to claim benefit u/r. 55(4) of the 1970 Rules nor under general principles of service jurisprudence - Mistake committed by Administration would not furnish valid or legitimate ground for Courts or Tribunals to direct Administration to go on repeating that mistake Chandigarh Administration vs. Naurang Singh,1997 Indlaw SC 2697 followed - In state of Andhra Pradesh vs. G. Sreenivasa Rao,1989 Indlaw SC 511, SC held that whenever junior was given higher pay his seniors could not claim same on 'equal pay for equal work' - None of seniors were entitled to any relief of pay hike - Order of DB was set aside and Order of Single Judge restored - Appeals allowed.
