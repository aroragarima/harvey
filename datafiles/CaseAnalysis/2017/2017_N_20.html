Consortium of Titagarh Firema Adler S. P. A. - Titagarh Wagons Limited, through Authorized Signatory, West Bengal v Nagpur Metro Rail Corporation Limited, through its General Manager (Procurement), Nagpur and another

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 410; (2017) 7 SCC 486; 2017(3) ALL MR 931; 2017 (3) BC 69; JT 2017 (5) SC 490; 2017 (4) MLJ 623; 2017(6) SCALE 177

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Auction, Contract, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Tender invitation - Eligibility criteria - Challenged - Respondent no. 1 invited tender for Metro Rail Project - Appellant and respondent no. 2 submitted their bids - Tender Committee of respondent no. 1 accepted offer of respondent no. 2 - Aggrieved, appellant filed writ petition before HC contending that respondent no. 2 does not meet eligibility criteria on its own relying on the experience of its subsidiary - DB dismissed petition holding that decision of technical evaluation committee would fall within the ambit of 'rationality' - Aggrieved, appellant filed review petition before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Appellant contended that respondent no. 2 does not fulfill the eligibility criteria however, respondent no. 1 deliberately closed its eyes to the fact that was projected and conferred the status of single entity on respondent no. 2.

Held, with regard to the satisfaction of respondent no. 1 it was highlighted that respondent had thoroughly examined the bid documents and satisfied itself about the capability, experience and expertise of respondent no. 2 and there was a thorough analysis of technical qualification of respondent no. 2 by independent General Consultant and reports of Appraisal and Tender Committee of respondent no. 1 and no-objection was received from bank which was funding the entire project. There was material on record that respondent no. 2, a Government company, was the owner of subsidiaries companies and subsidiaries companies have experience. Respondent no. 1 has applied its commercial wisdom in understanding and interpretation which was given the concurrence by concerned Committee and financing bank. The concept of "Govt. owned entity" cannot be conferred a narrow construction. It would include its subsidiaries subject to the satisfaction of owner. There need not be a formation of a joint venture or a consortium. In the obtaining fact situation, the interpretation placed by respondent no. 1 in the absence of any kind of perversity, bias or mala fide should not be interfered with in exercise of power of judicial review. Decision taken by respondent no. 1, as is perceptible was no way against public interest. Therefore, SC concur with the view expressed by HC. Appeals dismissed.
