Bhola Bhagat and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

24 October 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3086; (1997) 8 SCC 720; (1998) SCC (Cr) 125; AIR 1998 SC 236; AIR 1997 SCW 4205; 1997 (35) AllCriC 835; 1997 (4) CCR 72; 1997 (4) Crimes 123; 1998 CRLJ 390; JT 1997 (8) SC 537; 1998 (1) RCR(Criminal) 21; 1997(6) SCALE 558; [1997] Supp4 S.C.R. 711; 1997 (9) Supreme 61

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Bihar Children Act, 1982, s.32, Juvenile Justice Act, 1986, s.32 - Presumption and determination of age - Bieng a socially oriented legislation, it is the obligation of the Court where such a plea is raised to examine that plea with care and to return a positive finding regarding that plea - Held, the court is under an obligation to examine the plea of age and give findings in this regard after an enquiry and cannot in any way disregard any beneficial provisions of Acts on technical grounds.
