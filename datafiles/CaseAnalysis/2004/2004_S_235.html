Special Deputy Collector and others v Chakali Linga and others

Supreme Court of India

02 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 2038; (2005) 13 SCC 404

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss.18, 23(2) - Acquisition of Land - Enhancement of compensation - Respondents were owners of land and said land was acquired under Act for which compensation for said land and trees was granted by appellant/Deputy Collector - Unsatisfied respondent approached Reference Court for enhancement but enhanced compensation was granted only to acquired lands - Respondents filed review petition for compensation of trees which was allowed - Appellant filed revision petitions Revisional Court which were dismissed - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, respondents filed interlocutory applications before Reference Court claiming benefit u/s. 23(2) of Act, as amended by Act of 1984. It was case of respondents that judgment and decree passed by Reference Court was reviewed by its order, by granting enhanced compensation in respect of trees. Therefore, according to respondents, decree finally passed by Reference Court was one passed for vacating decree earlier passed. Relying upon doctrine of merger, it was claimed that since decree was passed by Reference Court on 31-12-1983, respondents were entitled to solatium at rate of 30% on market value instead of 15%, as earlier awarded. Contention was upheld by Reference Court and affirmed by HC decree that is subsequently passed on review, that it modifies, reverses or confirms decree originally passed, is new decree superseding original one having regard to these Authorities, Court finds no infirmity in order passed by HC. Appeals dismissed.
