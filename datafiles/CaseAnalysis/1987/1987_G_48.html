V. Veerarajan and Others v Government of Tamil Nadu and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 January 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28514; (1987) SCC (L&S) 64; (1987) 1 SCC 479; AIR 1987 SC 695; 1987 (1) CCC 424; 1987 (54) FLR 180; JT 1987 (1) SC 141B; 1987 LabIC 494; 1987 (1) LLJ 209; 1987 (1) LLN 288; 1987(1) SCALE 42; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 997; 1987 (2) SLJ 32; 1987 (1) SLR 467; 1987 (1) UJ 417

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10, 11 A and 12(5) - Whether respondents were justified in not referring dispute to Industrial Tribunal? - Held, this is a fit case where a reference should be made - In order of this Court in present case on July 9, 1985 it has been clearly stated that a direction to make a reference would have been given but for submission advanced by Dr Chitale that matter should go back and State Govt. should be given an opportunity of giving other valid reasons, if any, in support of its order - Order accordingly.
