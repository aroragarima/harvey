Balakrishnan v Malaiyandi Konar

Supreme Court of India

17 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 51; (2006) 3 SCC 49; AIR 2006 SC 1458; 2006 (63) ALR 489; 2006 (2) AWC 1255; 2006 (2) Bom.C.R. 520; 2006 (3) CTC 180; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 223; JT 2006 (2) SC 442; 2006 (2) MLJ(SC) 289; 2006 (2) RCR(Civil) 168; 2006 (100) RD 805; 2006(2) SCALE 403; 2006 (2) SCJ 671; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 363

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - CPC,1908, o. 21, rr. 64, 92, 94, 95, s. 47 - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 134 - Tamil Nadu Debt Relief Act, 1980 - Challenge to judgment of Madras HC holding that auction sale held in an execution proceeding and confirmation thereof was illegal - Appellant who is decree holder purchased property in Court auction sale - Held, though question of applicability of o. 21, r. 64 should not have been considered by HC in view of dismissal of earlier Civil Revision Petition, even otherwise no relief could have been granted to appellant in view of art.134 of Limitation Act - Order passed by HC remitting matter is maintained - Question that has to be considered will not be validity of sale, but maintainability of application for delivery of property - Appeal dismissed.
