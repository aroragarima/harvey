Khandelwal Metal and Engineering Works and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 June 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, R.S. Pathak, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 406; (1985) SCC (Tax) 466; (1985) 3 SCC 620; AIR 1985 SC 1211; 1985 CRLR 462; 1985 CrLR(SC) 462; 1985 (28) DLT 375; 1985 ECR 2571; 1985 (20) E.L.T. 222; 1985(1) SCALE 1073; [1985] Supp1 S.C.R. 750; 1985 TAXLR 2556

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Classification Of Goods, Taxable Event, Legislative Competence

Summary: Customs - Constitution - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 12(1)and 25 - Customs Tariff Act, 1975, ss. 2(d) and 3(1) - Rules for interpretation of First Schedule (Import Tariff) to Tariff Act, 1975, rr. 1, 2, 3 and 4 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Notification No. 97, D/- 25-06-1977 and No. 156, D/-16-07-1977 - (A) Whether 'additional duty' of customs which is in nature of countervailing duty, cannot be levied on brass scrap because, such scrap which consists of damaged brass articles is not manufactured in India? - Held, if articles are brass scrap, limited inquiry which has to be made is whether brass scrap can come into being during process of manufacture, if answer is in affirmative, imported brass scrap will be chargeable to additional duty in accordance with section 3 (1) of Tariff Act - Appellant would be entitled to exemption from customs duty to extent of 20% only, under Notification No. 156, D/-16-07-1977 - (B) Whether brass scrap is a 'master alloy' and, therefore, falls under notification D/-25-06-1977 or should it be comprehended within expression 'copper waste and scrap' and therefore falls under notification D/-16-07-1977? - Further held, brass or brass scrap is not used as a raw material in manufacture of other alloys and therefore, it is not a master alloy - (C) Whether item 'copper waste and scrap' under Heading No. 74. 01/02 includes brass scrap? - Since copper gives its 'essential character' to brass, brass scrap has to be classified as 'copper waste and scrap' within meaning of Heading No. 74. 01/02 - Cl. (a) of Note 3 to Section XV of First Schedule (Import Tariff) provides that an alloy of base metals is to be classified as an alloy of metal which predominates by weight over each of other metals - Since brass is an alloy of copper and zinc in which copper predominates by weight, brass has to be classified as an alloy of copper - (D) Whether imposition of Excise duty on 'waste and scrap', which is referred to in cl. (1 b) of Entry 26 A of First Schedule to Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944 is either ultra vires s. 3 of that Act or beyond legislative competence of Parliament? - Further held, Entry 26 A (1 b) of First Schedule of Act of 1974 cannot be held to be beyond legislative competence of Parliament because, pre-condition of excisability of articles mentioned therein, namely, waste and scrap, is in manufacturability of those articles - Parliament has legislative competence to make 'waste and scrap' excisable under Entry 84 of List I of Seventh Schedule to Constitution - Appeals dismissed.
