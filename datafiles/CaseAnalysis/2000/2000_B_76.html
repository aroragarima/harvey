Nath Bros. Exim International Limited v Best Roadways Limited

Supreme Court of India

27 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2701; (2000) 4 SCC 553; 2000 (1) ACC 434; AIR 2000 SCW 2116; 2000 (3) ALD(SC) 1; 2000 (4) Bom.C.R. 296; 2000 (1) CPJ(Sc) 25A; JT 2000 (3) SC 433; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 182; 2000 (125) PLR 461; 2000 (2) RCR(Civil) 620; 2000(2) SCALE 537; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 538

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Corporate; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Consumer Protection - Carriers Act, 1865, ss. 3, 4, 5, 6, 8 and 9 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss. 151 and 152 - English Carriers Act of 1830 (ENG) - Whether respondent has at its own volition dropped goods diverted destination of delivery of goods? - It has been successfully proved by carrier that consignment of petitioner was diverted from Mulund Check post to Bhiwandi on specific instructions of consignee and further that loss was caused by fire which was beyond their control - It has been mentioned by them that they took due care, within their capacity and now they have lodged a claim on owner of adjoining godown from where fire started - Appeal allowed.
