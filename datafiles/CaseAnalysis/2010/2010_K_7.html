K. Manorama v Union Of India Rep. By Genl. Manager Southern Railway & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

29 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 809; (2010) 10 SCC 323; 2011 (1) MLJ(SC) 357; 2010(10) SCALE 304; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 841; 2011 (2) SLR 324

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Service

Summary: Service - Advocates & Judges - Promotion - Appellant (Scheduled Caste Candidate) challenged promotion of respondent no. 3 (Scheduled Caste Candidate) as an Assistant Law Officers submitting that he was promoted to the post of Assistant Law Officer on his merit and not because he was a Scheduled Caste Candidate, hence, instead of respondent no. 4 belonging to the open category, she should have been promoted to the post of Assistant Law Officer on the basis of her status as a Scheduled Caste candidate - Tribunal on the basis of SC decision in R.K. Sabharwal and Ors. vs. State of Punjab and Ors. 1995 INDLAW SC 1756, held that the selection of respondent no. 3 was in an unreserved vacancy on his own merit - Tribunal further directed respondents to empanel the appellant in the reserved category provided that she was qualified according to the marks and seniority in the selection made, and if there was no SC candidate above her either on marks or in seniority - HC allowed the writ petition filed against said order - Hence, present appeal - Whether appellant's submissions could be accepted that respondent no.3 was promoted on his merit and not because he was a Scheduled Caste Candidate and order of the HC could be set aside? - Held, principle that when a member belonging to a Scheduled Caste gets selected in the open competition field on the basis of his own merit, he will not be counted against the quota reserved for Scheduled Castes, but will be treated as open candidate, will apply only in regard to recruitment by open competition and not to the promotions effected on the basis of seniority-cum- suitability - In present case, tribunal did not realize that the respondent no. 3 had in fact got marks lesser than the respondent no. 4, and his selection was basically because he was a Scheduled Caste candidate - Hence, there is no error in the judgment and order rendered by the HC - Submissions of the appellant rejected - Appeal dismissed.
