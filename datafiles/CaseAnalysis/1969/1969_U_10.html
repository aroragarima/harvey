V. Venugopala Ravi Varma Rajah v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

26 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover, V. Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 248; (1969) 1 SCC 681; AIR 1969 SC 1094; [1969] 74 ITR 49; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 827; 1969 (74) STC 49

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Sales Tax

Summary: Land & Property - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940 - Expenditure Tax Act, 1957 - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Whether law which enables Expenditure-tax Officer to assess tax on expenditure of all members of Hindu undivided family governed by Marumakkattayam law, discriminates, on ground of religion, between Hindu undivided family and a Mappilla undivided family governed by Marumakkattayam law resident in North Malabar? - Held, equal protection clause of Constitution does not enjoin equal protection of laws as abstract propositions - A taxing statute is not, therefore, exposed to attack on ground of discrimination merely because different rates of taxation are prescribed for different categories of persons, transactions, occupations or objects - It is for Legislature to determine objects on which tax shall be levied, and rates thereof - Mappilla families governed by Marumakkattayam law reside in a small part of country and form numerically a small community - Parliament has again been accustomed in enacting tax laws to make a distinction between a Hindu Undivided Family consisting of Hindus and undivided families of Mappillas - By taxing Acts Parliament could have treated Mappilla tarwads as units of taxation - But mere fact that law could, have been extended to another class of persons who have certain characteristics similar to a section of Hindus but have not been so included is not a ground for striking down law - Appeals dismissed.
