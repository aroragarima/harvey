Supreme Court of India

16 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: In order to protect the right to speedy trial of an accused the Court cannot devise and enact bars of limitation since it is an activity beyond the power which the Constitution confers on judiciary and such bars of limitation run counter to the doctrine of precedents and their binding efficacy as laid down by Constitution Bench in A.R. Antulay's case. Further, the Criminal Courts should exercise their available powers, such as those under Sections 309, 311 and 258 of Cr.PC to effectuate the right to speedy trial, while the High Courts can invoke their jurisdiction under Section 482 of Cr.P.C. and Articles 226 and 227 of the Constitution.
