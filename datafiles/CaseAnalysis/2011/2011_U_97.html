A. Sridhar v United India Insurance Company Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

13 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 581; (2011) 14 SCC 719; 2011 (3) ACC 930; 2011 ACJ 2473; 2012 (5) ALD(SC) 154; 2011 (89) ALR 246; 2011 (6) AWC 5944; 2012 (1) OLR 45; 2011 (164) PLR 804; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 843; 2011(10) SCALE 428; [2011] 11 S.C.R. 386

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Insurance - Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 140, 166 - Motor accident claim - Reduction of compensation by HC - Justifiability - Appellant met with an accident, while he was riding motor cycle along with a pillion rider, due to oil spill on the road and suffered grievous injuries - Appellant filed claim petition against insurer of the vehicle - Tribunal awarded compensation of Rs. 1,60,000/- - Insurance Company filed appeal before the HC - HC held that the Tribunal was not justified in allowing the claim petition moved u/s. 166 of the Act and ought to have determined the compensation payable u/s. 140 of the Act - HC modified the award and reduced the compensation payable to Rs. 25,000/- Hence the instant appeal by appellant claimant - Held, Tribunal held that the appellant, while driving the motor vehicle on the fateful day, met with an accident not because of the fault of the owner of the vehicle or because of the fault of the other vehicle, but because of the oil spill on the road - Therefore, the negligence could be attributable only on the person who was driving the vehicle and hence, was not entitled to compensation under the Insurance Policy - HC was justified in invoking the beneficial legislation and in directing the Insurance Company to pay limited amount by way of compensation to the injured person of an accident arising out of the use of a motor cycle on the basis of 'no fault liability,' since the accident had arisen out of use of motor vehicle and had resulted in grievous injuries to the claimant - No legal infirmity in the order passed by the HC - Appeal dismissed.
