Biru Mahato v District Magistrate, Dhanbad

Supreme Court of India

15 October 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 137; (1982) 3 SCC 322; (1983) SCC (Cr) 31; AIR 1982 SC 1539; 1983 (1) Crimes 57; 1982 CRLJ 2354; 1982 CrLR(SC) 495; 1982(2) SCALE 899; [1983] 1 S.C.R. 584

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Criminal - Defense & Security Forces - Constitution of India, 1950 - National Security Act, 1980, s. (2) - Preventive detention - Validity of - Held, date on which detention order came to be made he was already prevented from pursuing any activity which may prove prejudicial to maintenance of public order, hence no order of detention could be made against him - The detaining authority was not even aware that detenu was already in jail and order suffers from vice of non-application of mind - Unless activity is interdicted by a preventive detention order activity which is being indulged into is likely to be repeated, this is postulate of s. 3(2) - Antecedent history and past conduct on which order of detention would be based would, in such a case, be proximate in point of time and would have a rational connection with conclusion drawn by authority that detention of person after his release is necessary - Subjective satisfaction of detaining authority must comprehend very fact that person sought to be detained is already in jail or under detention and yet a preventive detention order is a, compelling necessity - Not a word is said that detaining authority was aware of fact that detenu was already in jail and yet it became a compelling necessity to pass detention order - Quashed and set aside the order of detention - Appeal allowed.
