M/S Hans Steel Rolling Mill v Commnr. Of Central Excise, Chandigarh

Supreme Court of India

10 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 155; (2011) 3 SCC 748; 2011 (265) E.L.T. 321; JT 2011 (3) SC 448; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 675; 2011(3) SCALE 421; [2011] 3 S.C.R. 841

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Hot Re-Rolling Steel Mills Annual Capacity Determination Rules, 1997

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Hot-Re-rolling Steel Mills Annual Capacity Determination Rules, 1997, r. 5 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 96ZP - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 3A and 11A - Compounded levy scheme - Provisions of s. 11A - Applicability - Appellants were engaged in the manufacture of iron and steel products - During the period 01.09.1997 to 31.3.2000, the goods manufactured by the appellants were chargeable to Excise Duty as per s. 3A of the Act - Subsequently, Hot-Re-rolling Steel Mills Annual Capacity Determination Rules, 1997 were introduced - Department issued show cause notice u/s. 11A of the Act to the appellants for recovery of duty contending that the demand of the duty has to be based on the capacity determination of Hot-re-rolling steel mills - Commissioner determined the annual capacity of the period 1.09.97 to 31.3.2000 to be 1890MT - On appeal, Tribunal held that in case of the manufacturer operating under Compound Levy Scheme, recovery mechanism provided in terms of s. 11A of the Act was not to be followed - Appellants filed appeal against said order contending that the provisions of s. 11A of the Act were mandatory for recovery of any duty short levied and short paid - Whether the provisions of s. 11A of the Act are applicable to the recovery of amounts due under the compound levy scheme? - Held, the compounded levy scheme for collection of duty based on annual capacity of production u/s. 3 of 1944 Act and Hot Re-rolling Steel Mills Annual Capacity Determination Rules, 1997 was a separate scheme from the normal scheme for collection of central excise duty on goods manufactured in the country - Rule 96P of the 1944 Rules stipulate the method of payment and contains detailed provision regarding time and manner of payment - Thus, this was a comprehensive scheme in itself and general provisions in the Act and Rules were excluded - Hence, Tribunal rightly held that time limit prescribed for one scheme could be completely unwarranted for another scheme and time limit prescribed u/s. 11A of the Act was no exception - Impugned order of the HC set aside - Appeals dismissed.
