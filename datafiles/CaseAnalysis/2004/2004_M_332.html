Mukund Balkrishna Kulkarni v Kulkarni Powder Metallurgical Industries and another

Supreme Court of India

13 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 2080; (2004) 13 SCC 750

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Suit for dissolution of partnership firm, Admission of oral agreement of partnership

Summary: Trust & Associations - Civil Procedure - Indian Partnership Act, 1932, ss. 69, 69(3) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Suit for dissolution of partnership firm - Admission of oral agreement of partnership - Legality - Appellant (plaintiff) had filed a suit against respondents for dissolution of partnership firm and accounts - Appellant claimed that he had in fact invested monies pursuant to agreement and had taken part in the business of firm - Trial Court decreed suit declaring that Respondent no. 1 was a partnership business in which both appellant and Respondent no. 2 had equal shares and that firm should be dissolved and on appeal, Lower Appellate Court dismissed the same - Aggrieved respondents filed an appeal before HC, which was allowed holding that there was no partnership in fact and that document did not evidence the existence of a partnership as had been found by Courts below - Whether impugned judgment passed by HC was justified - Held, decision of HC was outside the scope of its jurisdiction u/s. 100 of CPC - The inclusion of words 'perverse' and 'interpretation' in the framed questions did not give a licence to HC to in fact sit in appeal and reappraise the entire evidence as if it were sitting as a Court of first appeal - Finding that there was a partnership firm, had been arrived at as a conclusion of fact not only on the basis of material which had been produced before Lower Courts but also on the basis of deposition of parties - Right of partner to ask the dissolution of a firm was a right the enforcement of which was otherwise forbidden u/s. 69(1) of the Act - It was because of exception u/s. 69(3) of the Act that a person suing as a partner could enforce a right under the contract for dissolution of firm and accounts - Claim for a half share in firm's assets would be a necessary corollary to a prayer for dissolution - Without the prayer for specified shares in the firm's assets and business, the relief that might be granted in a suit for dissolution would be ineffective - Impugned judgment of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
