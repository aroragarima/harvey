Satish Shetty v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

03 June 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 397; AIR 2016 SC 2689; 2016 CRLJ 3147; 2016 (3) JCC 1880; JT 2016 (6) SC 10; 2016 (4) KarLJ 145; 2016 (3) MLJ(Crl) 611; 2016 (3) RCR(Criminal) 373; 2016(5) SCALE 770; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 145

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Presumption, Criminal Case, Suicide, Cruelty, Harassment, Marriage, Indian Evidence Act, 1872, Abetment, Reliance, Two Views, Dowry, Matrimonial, Resorting, Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Husband, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113A - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 107, 304, 304B, 306, 498A - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, ss. 2, 3, 4, 6 - Abetment of Suicide - Presumption - Sustainability - Deceased/wife committed suicide leaving behind a son and was also mothering a life of twenty weeks in her womb - FIR was registered against appellant/husband and his parents for offences u/ss. 498-A, 304-B IPC and ss. 3 and 4 of the 1961 Act - Trial Court acquitted appellant and his parents of all charges - On appeal, HC allowed appeal and reversed judgment of acquittal in part - HC convicted appellant with RI of three years and fine - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in resorting to exercise such a discretion as was available to it u/s. 113A of the 1872 Act.

Held, once prosecution succeeds in establishing component of cruelty leading to conviction u/s. 498A, in a rare case, Court could refuse to invoke the presumption of abetment, if other requirements of s. 113A of the 1872 Act was satisfied. This proposition is amply supported in case of (1) K. Prema S. Rao and another; (2) State of Andhra Pradesh vs (1) Yadla Srinivasa Rao and others; (2) Yadla Ranga Rao and another 2002 Indlaw SC 1363. HC has given good reasons on basis of facts brought on record through evidence for exercising discretion of invoking presumption u/s. 113A of the 1872 Act. It was found deceased committed suicide because she was not permitted to go to her mother's place does not inspire confidence and has rightly been rejected by HC. Only for such a trivial matter, a hale and hearty young woman having a ten months old son and a pregnancy of twenty weeks was not at all expected to take her life. Appellant not only gave absolutely no explanation for injuries on deceased, rather he chose to conceal them by keeping mum. Appellant failed to rebut the presumptions raised against him u/s. 113A of the 1872 Act. Order accordingly.

Ratio - In a rare case, Court can refuse to invoke the presumption of abetment, if other requirements of s. 113A of the 1872 Act stand satisfied.
