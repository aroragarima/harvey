Sanjay and another v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

06 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 7; (2016) 3 SCC 62; AIR 2016 SC 282; 2016 CRLJ 1117; 2016 (1) JCC 523; JT 2016 (1) SC 78; 2016 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1161; 2016 (1) RCR(Criminal) 1020; 2016 (2) RLW 1020; 2016(1) SCALE 137

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Bullet

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300, 302, 304, 307, 34, 452 - Murder - Conviction - Justification - It was alleged that appellant i.e. brother of deceased had dispute with sale of land and on day of incident, appellant along with other accused fired at deceased due to which he died - Charge-sheet was filed against appellants u/ss. 307, 452, 302 of IPC -Trial Court convicted appellant accused persons u/ss. 302 of IPC r/w s. 34 of IPC and s. 307 of IPC r/w s. 34 and 452 of IPC - HC upheld conviction order passed by Trial Court - Appellant contended that deceased was discharged from hospital and his condition was stable and two months thereafter deceased died due to septicaemia, hence, conviction of the appellants u/s. 302 IPC was not sustainable - Whether conviction of appellants u/s. 302 of IPC is sustainable.

Held, death of deceased occurred sixty two days after occurrence due to septicaemia and it was indirectly due to injuries sustained by deceased. SC held that proximate cause of death on 13-10-1998 was septicaemia which of course was due to injuries caused in incident on 11-8-1998. Further, SC held that prosecution failed to elicit from doctor PW 9 that head injury sustained by deceased was sufficient in ordinary course of nature to cause death. Hence, having regard to fact that deceased survived for sixty two days and that his condition was stable when he was discharged from hospital, inference cannot be drawn that intended injury caused was sufficient in ordinary course of nature to cause death so as to attract s. 300(3) of IPC. SC held that bullet injury was on head, vital organ and appellant intended of causing such bodily injury, hence, conviction of appellant is altered from s. 302 IPC to s. 304 Part I of IPC. Further, SC held that conduct of appellants and manner in which crime has been committed is sufficient to attract s. 34 of IPC as both appellants acted in furtherance of common intention. Hence, conviction of appellants is modified to conviction u/s. 304 Part I of IPC. Further, conviction of appellants for other offences and respective sentence of imprisonment imposed on appellants and fine is affirmed. Appeals partly allowed.

Ratio - It is justified to alter conviction of accused when prosecution has failed to prove guilt of accused beyond reasonable doubt.
