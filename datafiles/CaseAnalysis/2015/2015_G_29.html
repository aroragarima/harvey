Commissioner of Central Excise, Aurangabad v Goodyear South Asia Tyres P. L. and others

Supreme Court of India

22 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 542; 2015 (322) E.L.T. 389; JT 2015 (7) SC 1; 2015(8) SCALE 359

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 4, 4(4)(c) - Alleged Related Party - Mutuality of Interest - Determination - Respondent assessee manufacture of goods was originally one company, however, later it entered into joint venture to form third company - Further, both companies were holding 50% share each in assessee company and were exclusive buyers of goods manufactured by assessee - Further, assessee company received loan from its buyers i.e. joint venture company - Commissioner issued show cause notice alleging that joint venture companies i.e. buyers are related persons of assessee within meaning of s. 4(4)(c) of and selling price of buyers shall be assessable value of goods produced by assessee u/s. 4 of Act - Commissioner passed order against assessee and held that assessee is related party of buyer - Further, appeal filed by assessee is allowed by Tribunal - Whether assessee and buyer i.e. joint venture companies are related party within meaning of s. 4(4)(c) of Act.

Held, assessee did not have any interest in business of buyers. Hence, requirement of 'mutuality of interest' which is pre-requisite u/s. 4(4)(c) of Act does not get satisfied. Further, providing of loan by itself may not be reason to hold them as related persons within meaning of s. 4(4)(c) of Act. In absence of any mutuality of interest existing between them, giving of this interest free loan could have been basis to include notional interest while arriving at cost of product sold by assessee to two buyers. Hence, assessee cannot be regarded as related party to buyer under Act. Further, with respect to assessee being subsidiary of one of joint venture company and existence of related party between companies, SC held that after taking over of assessee company, more than 70 per cent of sales by assessee company are to third parties and goods sold to outsiders are at lesser rates than sold to holding company. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - In absence of any documentary proof and mutuality of interest it cannot be held that two companies are related party under Act for payment of duty.
