Collector of Central Excise, Bombay v Kohinoor Mills, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 995; (1995) Supp2 SCC 411; AIR 1995 SC 1417; 1995 (77) E.L.T. 42; JT 1997 (10) SC 427; 1995 (7) RLT 703; 1995(2) SCALE 585; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 159

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 37 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 9(1) - Levy of excise duty - Yarn was manufactured in weaving department of a composite textile mill and was captively consumed in spinning department for manufacturing fabric therefrom - Whether any excise duty was payable on manufactured yarn prior to 15-7-1977, when an earlier notification D/- 18-6-1977 exempting such yarn from payment of excise duty was in operation, only because ultimate fabrics got manufactured from such yarn on and from 15-7-1977 when earlier notification stood rescinded? - Charge under Central Excise Act, 1944, is fastened on a manufactured item once manufacture takes place - But liability to pay duty thereon will have to be decided in light of appropriate tariff applicable at time when such manufactured commodity is cleared by manufacturer as per provisions of Excise Rules, 1944 - Held, a conjoint reading of r. 9(1) and Explanation makes it clear that if a manufactured item covered by charge of excise duty by charging provisions of Central Excise Act, 1944, is captively consumed, it would amount to removal of such manufactured item - Consequently once yarn is manufactured in weaving department of composite textile mills and is taken to spinning department for being captively utilised by way of consumption in spinning department, and gets consumed, it is deemed to have been removed within meaning of r. 9(1) - Once that happens, liability of such manufactured yarn to pay excise duty at then prevalent rate of duty is crystallized - Thereafter, question as to when subsequently fabric is manufactured or emerges or is removed from factory gate becomes irrelevant for purpose of deciding question of liability of excise duty on such captively consumed yarn - Further held, that yarn manufactured by appellant composite mills from 18-6-1977 up to 14-7-1977 and removed for captive consumption and used as such during that time in spinning department for manufactured of fabric will not be liable to bear any excise duty as per later notification dated 15-7-1977 - Order accordingly.
