U.P. Avas Evam Vikas Parishad v Sheo Narain Kushwaha & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 312; (2011) 6 SCC 456; 2011 (4) ALD(SC) 161; 2011 (87) ALR 745; 2011 (5) AWC 4488; 2012 (1) CLT(SC) 169; 2011 (4) CTC 213; JT 2011 (5) SC 364; 2011 (5) MLJ(SC) 732; 2011 (5) RCR(Civil) 384; 2011 (114) RD 183; 2011(5) SCALE 260; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 869; 2011 (3) UPLBEC 2059

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 54 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 41 r. 11 - Appellant, for whose benefit certain were acquired, filed an appeal before HC challenging the judgment of the Reference Court which increased the compensation for the acquired land of respondents - Appeal was dismissed summarily by the impugned non-speaking order upholding the award - HC stated that it was doing so, in exercise of the power under O. 41 r. 11 of CPC - Hence, instant appeal filed u/s. 54 of the Act - Held, O. 41 r. 11 of the CPC, while relieving the HC from the obligation to write a 'judgment', did not dispense with the obligation to assign reasons in brief, when summarily dismissing the appeal - Further, order of HC dismissing the first appeal should be sufficiently reasoned to disclose the application of mind to the grounds of appeal and make out that the HC was resorting to dismissal in limine as it found the appeal either to be vexatious or wholly without merit - In the instant case, appellant raised sufficient grounds which required to be dealt with and decided by the HC on merits - Matter remanded to the HC for disposal of the appeal on merits - Appeal allowed.
