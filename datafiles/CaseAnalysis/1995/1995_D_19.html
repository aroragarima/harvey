State of Gujarat and Others v Daya Shamji Bhai and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 August 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1183; (1995) 5 SCC 746; AIR 1996 SC 133; 1995 (26) ALR 566; 1995 (3) CCC 404; JT 1995 (6) SC 475; 1995 (2) RentLR 607; 1995(5) SCALE 248; [1995] Supp3 S.C.R. 89

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 11(1) (2), (4) and 18 - Reference - Whether claimants of land are entitled to seek reference u/s. 18 and civil court can determine higher compensation? - Held, by operation of s. 11(4), need for registration of agreement is obviated - As seen in contract, respondents have foregone their right of seeking reference in lieu of 25 per cent more than compensation determined by Collector u/s. 11(2) of Act - In fact, 25 per cent in addition to market value determined by Collector in his award u/s. 11(1) had been paid as consideration to forego reference - Even otherwise, once an agreement was entered by parties, question of objection to receive compensation under protest does not arise - Claimant have no right to seek a reference to Civil Court u/s. 18 of Act - Appeal allowed.
