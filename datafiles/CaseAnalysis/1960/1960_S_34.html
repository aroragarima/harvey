Godavari Sugar Mills Limited v Shri D. K. Worlikar

Supreme Court of India

14 March 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 145; AIR 1960 SC 842; 1960 (1) FLR 38; 1960 (2) LLJ 247; 1960 SCJ 1243; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 305

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Bombay Industrial Relation Act, 1946, s. 2(4) - Industrial Disputes Act,1947 - Disciplinary proceedings - Dismissed for misconduct - Challenged - Respondent who was working in head office of appellant's company was dismissed after his guilt was proved in enquiry proceedings - Respondent challenged dismissal order in Labour Court based on notification issued by State Govt. and was opposed by appellant - Labour Court dismissed claim of respondent holding that notification was not applicable to appellants head office - Aggrieved respondent filed appeal and was dismissed by industrial Court - Aggrieved, respondent filed appeal in Labour appellate Tribunal which allowed appeal of respondent holding that notification was applicable to appellant and remanded matter back to labour court for fresh disposal - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeal - Whether notification applied to appellants head office - Held, notification issued by state Govt. u/s. 2(4) of the 1946 Act would not apply to sugar industry but applies to manufacture of sugar and its byproducts - Appellate had manufacturing units in villages miles away from its head office - Notification was not applicable to appellants head office - Appellate Tribunal had erred in holding that notification applied to sugar industry - Respondent could not claim benefit under notification as he works in head office of sugar industry to which this notification was not applicable - Appellate Tribunals order was set aside - Appeal allowed.
