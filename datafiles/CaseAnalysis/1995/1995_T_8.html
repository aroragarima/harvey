Messrs Hindustan Petroleum Corporation Limited v Collector of Central Excise

Supreme Court of India

27 April 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 434; (1995) Supp3 SCC 45; 1995 (58) ECR 599; 1995 (77) E.L.T. 256; JT 1995 (4) SC 88; 1995 (8) RLT 877; 1995(3) SCALE 1B; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 839

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 35(A) - Central Excise Rules, rr. 12 and 13 - Whether appellant who exported concerned excisable goods has to pay on these goods excise duty as per r. 13 or r. 12? - Held, rr. 12 and 13 deal with excisable goods which are exported from country of their manufacture to outside countries - If excisable goods are exported after payment of duty they may claim refund as per notification laid down by r. 12 - If these excisable goods are found in bonded warehouse covered by bond to pay excisable duty payable thereon, in case they are exported as laid down by r. 13 they may claim exemption from payment of duty in same manner as laid down by r. 12 - Two rules are complementary to each other and cover same topic of payment of appropriate excise duty on excisable goods exported outside India - Liability to pay excise duty u/r. 13 is linked with r. 12 - It is not as if u/r. 13 excisable goods which are subjected to export directly from warehouse of licensed factory do not incur any excise duty - R. 13 calls upon exporter to enter into a bond for payment of requisite full duty in case situation arises for same and that bond is not to be discharged and obligation under bond has to continue for benefit of revenue till proof of export is made available to satisfaction of Collector - Appellants' contention that rr. 13 and 12 are independent cannot be accepted - Appeals dismissed.
