P. Radhakrishna Murthy v M/S. N.B.C.C. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

05 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 123; (2013) 3 SCC 747; AIR 2013 SC 1904; 2013 (2) AWC 1677; JT 2013 (3) SC 600; 2013 (4) KarLJ 161; 2013 (4) MahLJ 11; 2013 (3) MPLJ 81; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 355; 2013(3) SCALE 234

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Karnataka High Court Rules, Award of damages and interest

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 15, 16, 17, 30, 33 - Arbitration award - Award of damages and interest - Legality - Appellant (contractor/Corporation) filed instant appeals questioning the common judgment and order passed by HC in first appeal alongwith cross Objection wherein the appeal of appellant was partly allowed and award passed by Arbitrator was modified in regard to claims 1(a), (b), (c) and 2(a), (c), (e), (f) and (g) - HC set aside the award of Rs.8,16,412/- in regard to claim nos. 1(a),(b), (c) and 2(a), (c), (e), (f) and (g) - Also, the rejection of claims under 1(d) and 1(e) was upheld and award of Rs.13,050/- and 10,204/- against claims 2(c) and (d) were also upheld - Cross objection filed by contractor regarding grant of certain items of claims made by him was dismissed and consequently it was held that contractor would be entitled to Rs.9,01,871.53 with interest at the rate of 12% p.a from 28-12-1987 till the date of deposit of payment by Corporation - Amount paid by Corporation in pursuance of impugned interim order passed by HC would be adjusted to the amounts payable and if excess was received by contractor, then he should repay the same within 3 months to Corporation - That Order of HC was under challenge in instant appeals - Aggrieved by award of Arbitrator, appellant had filed petition u/ss. 15, 16, 17, 30 and 33 of the Act before Trial Court against Corporation to modify the award, which was subsequently converted into suit - Trial Court by impugned judgment affirmed the award of Arbitrator and modified claim no. 3 holding that contractor was entitled for 18,45,568/- applying 58.5% over and above the rates quoted by the contractor - Aggrieved Corporation challenged the award before HC by filing an appeal, which was allowed and cross objection filed by contractor was dismissed by common judgment - Hence, instant appeals - Whether impugned common judgment passed by HC was justified -

Held, HC had rightly held that Arbitrator was not a conciliator and his duty was to decide the disputes submitted to him according to the legal rights of the parties and not according to what he might consider to be fair and reasonable - HC had further rightly made observation in impugned judgment that an Arbitrator could not ignore law or misapply it, nor could he act arbitrarily, irrationally, capriciously or independent of the contract while passing the award - Courts of law have a duty and obligation to maintain purity of standards and preserve full faith and credit as well as to inspire confidence in the minds of litigants while adjudicating the claims of the parties by resorting to alternate dispute redressal method of arbitration under the provisions Act - Finding of fact recorded by HC on the relevant aspect holding that the Arbitrator did not record reasons regarding the delay in handing over the location of the sites in favour of contractor and rightly held that mere delay in handing over sites would not entitle the contractor to claim damages unless it was established that the same was the consequence of breach committed by the other party -Reasoning of HC on the aspect of claims was perfectly legal and valid as it had assigned valid and cogent reasons in support of conclusions arrived at and rightly disallowed the claims - HC found fault with the award of Arbitrator in increasing the claim under items 3(a) to (d) to Rs.18,45,568.90 and reduced the enhanced amount to Rs.10,04,117.42 plus Rs.1,14,500.11 which amounts to Rs.1,14,500.11/- under claims 3(a) and (d) and rejected the claims under 3(b) and (c) - Challenge on the award of damages of Rs.2,40,000/- against the contractor in favour of Corporation which was accepted by Trial Court was rightly rejected by HC - HC examined the rate of interest at 16.5% on the amount awarded in favour of Contractor by Trial Court and considered the contention urged on behalf of Corporation that the rate of interest awarded is excessive and also the contention that there was no contract of payment of interest on the same and alternatively contended that interest rate should not normally exceed 6% p.a - Reasons recorded by HC on contentious issues while examining the claims allowed by Arbitrator in the award with reasons which was affirmed by Trial Court, wherein certain claims were rightly disallowed, certain other claims accepted and yet some other claims modified by HC by adding certain amounts, were based on sound legal principles and after coming to the conclusion that the findings of Arbitrator and Court were erroneous and contrary to law - Thus, HC held that impugned award passed by Arbitrator which was made the rule of the Court by the civil Court was erroneous in law and it amounts to misconduct - Accordingly, HC rightly set aside certain claims of contractor and affirmed the award and granted extra amount on certain claims - It had also interfered with damages awarded in favour of Corporation and partly allowed the appeal of Corporation by allowing certain claims, setting aside certain other claims and also reducing the rate of interest at 12% from 16.5% - Thus, Court did not find any good reason whatsoever to interfere with the same in exercise of SC's jurisdiction in instant appeals - Appeals dismissed.
