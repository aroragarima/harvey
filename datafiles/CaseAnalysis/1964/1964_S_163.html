Dhirendra Nath Gorai and Others v Sudhir Chandra Ghosh and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, K.C. Das Gupta, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 418; AIR 1964 SC 1300; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 1001

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Distribution Of Assets

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, O. 21 r. 66, O. 21 r. 90 - Bengal Money-Lenders Act, 1940, s. 35 - Mortgage estate - Sale of mortgage property - Mandatory provision - Respondent nos. 2 and 3, representing first mortgagee's estate, filed title suit - Preliminary decree by consent was made in suit where under judgment debtor was directed to pay sum to appellant in 7 equal annual installments, however, he failed to pay installments - Respondent no. 1 received notice under O. 21 r. 66 of CPC, as against he application in executing Court for setting aside sale of mortgage property under O. 21 r. 90 of CPC, inter alia, on ground that s. 35 of the Act was not complied with - On appeal Division Bench of HC set aside sale and directed appellant to refund the money with interest - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether s. 35 of the Act was mandatory or directory sale held in violation of said provision was only illegal but not nullity and, therefore, it could be set aside only in manner and for reasons prescribed in O. 21 r. 90 of CPC - Held, it was true that many provisions of the Act were conceived in interests of public, however same could not be said of s. 35 of the Act, which was really intended to protect interests of judgment-debtor and to see that larger extent of his property than was necessary to discharge debt was not sold - Provision of s. 35 of the Act intended for his benefit could not be construed in such way as to work to his detriment - However, it was said that proviso to s. 35 of the Act indicates contrary intention - In one contingency said proviso Works for benefit of judgment-debtor, for he would be relieved of part of his indebtedness - Therefore, anyhow it did not show that main provision was not intended for benefit of judgment-debtor - Hence, on true construction of s. 35 of the Act, it was intended only for benefit of judgment-debtor and, therefore, he could waive right conferred on him u/s. 35 of the Act - Appeals allowed.
