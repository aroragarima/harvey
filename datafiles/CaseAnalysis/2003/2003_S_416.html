Jindal Stripe Limited and Others v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 809; (2003) 8 SCC 60; JT 2003 (8) SC 62; 2004 (136) PLR 601; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 610; 2003(8) SCALE 206; 2003 (6) SLT 457; 2004 (134) STC 303; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 154; 2003 (7) Supreme 198

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Tax Levied, Rajasthan Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1951, Haryana Municipal Corporation Act, 1994, Uttar Pradesh Municipalities Act, Haryana Panchayat Raj Act, 1994, Haryana Local Area Development Tax Act, 2000, Assam Taxation (on Goods carried by Roads or Inland Waterways) Act, 1954

Summary: Haryana Local Area Development Tax Act, 2000 - Constitution of India, 1950 - arts. 145(3), 301, 304 - Constitutional validity - Violative of art. 301 of the Constitution - The Act seeks to levy sales tax on inter-state sales, which is outside the competence of the State Legislature - Held, since the concept of compensatory tax has been judicially evolved as an exception to the provisions of art. 301 and as the parameters of this judicial concept are blurred particularly by reason of the decisions in various cases, the interpretation of art. 301 vis-Ã -vis compensatory tax should be authoritatively laid down with certitude by the Constitution Bench under art. 145(3) - Matters placed before the Chief Justice for appropriate directions.
