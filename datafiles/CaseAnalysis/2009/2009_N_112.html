State of Himachal Pradesh v Nazar Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 647; (2009) 12 SCC 89; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1805; 2009 CRLJ 3984; JT 2009 (13) SC 415; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 134; 2009(7) SCALE 666; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 1117

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304 Part II r/w 34 and 323 r/w 34 - Culpable homicide not amounting to murder - Simple hurt - Common intention - Conviction by Trial Court - Reversed by High Court - Whether proper? - Held, Yes - Deceased went to his field on hearing the barking of his dog late in the evening - Brother(PW1) of the deceased heard sounds of deceased's screaming, whereupon he ran towards the field and found that one accused Sarwan armed with 'gandasi' and others armed with lathis had been assaulting the deceased; as per prosecution case said Sarwan had inflicted two injuries with the gandasi on his head whereas the others were said to have inflicted lathi blows on him; PW2 afterwards reached the spot and alongwith PW 1 tried to save the deceased but were attacked by the assailant; deceased was brought to hospital in an injured condition wherein his medical history was recorded and he put his left thumb impression, but he had not named any person responsible for injuries - Nature of injuries sustained looked into, medical evidence noted - Actual arrest was made on 01-04-1993 but formal arrest was shown to be 02-04-1993 - Accused Sarwan and one other Jagtar were dead during pendency of appeal; Court proceeded on the basis of assumption that accused Sarwan could have been found guilty u/s. 304 Part II - Motive as per prosecution was a dispute arose with regard to laying down of a pipeline three months prior to the date of occurrence, and subsequent quarrels - Non naming of accused by deceased and variance in actual date of arrest and formal date of arrest, noted - As per prosecution case handle of gandasi was broken; why such handle was not seized, not put forth - Further discrepancies found as to registration of FIR and time of death - In statement made by deceased before Medical Officer, assault by gandasi was not mentioned; Court posits that if Sarwan had any intention to cause the death, he could have used his gandasi from the sharp end - Finding, no intention on part of any one of the accused to cause death - Impugned judgment upheld - Appeal dismissed.
