Harbhajan Singh v Press Council of India and others

Supreme Court of India

11 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 176; (2002) 3 SCC 722; AIR 2002 SC 1351; 2003 (1) ALD(SC) 12; 2002 (96) DLT 691; 2002 (5) ESC 276; JT 2002 (3) SC 21; 2002(2) SCALE 605; 2002 (2) SCJ 390; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 369; 2002 (2) SLT 504; 2002 (2) Supreme 368

Case Digest

Subject: Media & Entertainment

Keywords: University Act, 1922

Summary: On a plain reading of Section 6(7) of the Press Council Act, 1978 a person is not disqualified or made ineligible from holding the office of a member of the Council for more than two terms in his life. Further, as such an interpretation does not lead to any hardship, inconvenience, injustice, absurdity or anomaly, therefore, the rule of ordinary and natural meaning being followed cannot be departed from
