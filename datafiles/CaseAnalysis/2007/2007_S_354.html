Seema Dhamdhere, Secretary, M.P.S.C. v State Of Maharashtra And Others

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1535; (2008) 2 SCC 290; (2008) 1 SCC (L&S) 932; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1887; 2008(5) ALL MR 436; 2008 (70) ALR 499; 2008 (1) AWC 845; JT 2007 (13) SC 658; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 489; 2007(14) SCALE 500; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 804

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Malpractice - Examination - Justifiability - 2 practicing advocates filed writ petition before HC alleged that based on some news paper report that there was large scale of mal practice in examination conducted by Maharashtra Public Service Commission (commission) - Respondent/ investigating Officer conducted investigation into allegations - Direct General indicated that investigation was completed -Petitioner contended that investigation was not yet complete - Respondent took more time to file affidavit - HC opined that filing of delayed affidavit was not considered but matter before Special Court was pending issued necessary directions after conclusion of investigation if any, further materials collected against any accused persons could be brought on record and observed there was different perception of investigation between petitioner and respondent - Thus, accordingly disposed of - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Order passed by HC was Justifiable - Held, it was true that all allegations were found to be substantiated it would affect image of Commission - But that could not be ground to stall investigation which had to be done in transparent manner and credibility of any institution depends upon transparent action of its functionaries - Further it was pointed out by Commission that it would be in the interest of all concerned if examinations which were said to had been not held for nearly five years were held early - Thus, process of selection of Chairman and members would be initiated forthwith if not already done - It had been stated that new Secretary of Commission had taken - Court did not find any scope for interference with orders passed by HC - Appeal disposed of.
