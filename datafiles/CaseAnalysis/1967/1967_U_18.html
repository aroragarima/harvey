Â Roshan Lal Tandon v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

14 August 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 245; AIR 1967 SC 1889; 1968 (1) LLJ 576; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 185; 1967 SLR 832

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16 - Whether directives contained in notification of Railway Board No E(NG)65 PMI-26 D/- 27-10-1965, Annexure 'D' to Writ Petition, in so far as it grants protection to existing Apprentice Train Examiners and lays down procedure to fill upgraded vacancies, is discriminatory? - Held, as a consequence of impugned notification a discriminatory treatment is made in favour of existing Apprentice Train Examiners who have already been absorbed in Grade 'D' by 31-03-1966, because notification provides that this group of Apprentice Train Examiners should first be accommodated en bloc in grade 'C' upto 80 per cent of vacancies reserved for them without undergoing any selection - As regards 20 per cent of vacancies made available for category of Train Examiners to which petitioner belongs basis of recruitment was selection on merit and previous test of seniority-cum-suitability was abandoned - Relied on Mervyn v Collector (1966 Indlaw SC 6 ) - Where there is only one source of recruitment, normal rule will apply, namely, that a person promoted to a higher grade gets his seniority in that grade according to date of promotion subject always to his being found fit and being confirmed in higher grade after period of probation is over - In such a case, it is continuous appointment in higher grade which determines seniority for source of recruitment is one - No question in such a case of reflecting in higher grade seniority of grade from which promotion is made to higher grade - In so far therefore as respondent is doing what it calls restoration of seniority of direct recruits in Appraisers' grade when they are promoted to Principal Appraisers' grade, it is clearly denying equality of opportunity to Appraisers which is only source of recruitment to Principal Appraisers' grade - Present method by which respondent puts a direct recruit from grade of Appraiser, though he is promoted later, above a promotee who is promoted to grade of Principal Appraiser on an earlier date clearly denies equality of opportunity where grade of Principal Appraiser has only one source of recruitment, namely from grade of Appraisers - It is 'true that origin of Govt. service is contractual - There is an offer and acceptance in every case - Once appointed to his post or office Govt. servant acquires a status and his rights and obligations are no longer determined by consent of both parties, but by statute or statutory rules which may be framed and altered unilaterally by Govt. - Arts. 14 and 16 are found violated - Petition allowed.
