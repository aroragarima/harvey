Bose Abraham v State of Kerala and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20312; (2001) 3 SCC 157; AIR 2001 SC 835; AIR 2001 SCW 466; JT 2001 (2) SC 314; 2001 (1) KLT 730; 2001(1) SCALE 573; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 808; 2001 (1) SLT 802; 2001 (121) STC 614; 2001 (1) Supreme 465

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Indirect Tax

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 2(28) and 3 - Entry Tax Act, 1990 - Whether 'motor vehicle' as defined in s. 2(28) of Motor Vehicles Act would include excavators and roadrollers so as to attract levy under Kerala Tax on Entry of Motor Vehicles into Local Areas Act? - Held, under Entry 57 of List II of Seventh Sch. to Constitution, what is required to be considered is a tax on vehicle which is suitable for use on roads - But incidence of taxation in context of Entry 52 of List II of Seventh Sch. to Constitution is entry of goods into a local area for sale, use or consumption therein - In order to attract tax under provision of s. 3 of Act, a motor vehicle must have entered into a local area for use or sale therein, and secondly, which is liable for registration under Motor Vehicles Act - Court hold that excavators and roadrollers are motor vehicles for purpose of Motor Vehicles Act and they are registered under that Act - Appeals dismissed.
