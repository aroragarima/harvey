Chapsibhai Dhanjibhai Danad v Purushottam

Supreme Court of India

05 April 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 663; (1971) 2 SCC 205; AIR 1971 SC 1878; 1971 RCJ 441; 1971 RCR(Rent) 428; [1971] S.C.R. 335; [1971] Supp S.C.R. 335

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 105 - Lease - 30 years at fixed rent - Construction on rest of land by lessor - Close vicinity to western boundary of leased land - Whether lease is a permanent lease? - Whether a lease was permanent or for lifetime only of lessee, even where it was for building structures and was transferable, depended upon terms of lease and Court must, therefore, look at substance of it to ascertain whether parties intended it to be a permanent lease - But fact that lease provided that lessee could continue in possession of property so long as he paid stipulated rent did no mean that lease was for perpetuity - It would usually be regarded as a lease for an indefinite period and as such for lessee's lifetime - A lease may provide a fixed period and then include a provision that in event of lessee dying before expiry of such period, his heirs would be entitled to have benefit of lease for remainder of period - In such a case, although lease may provide for heirs to succeed to interests in leased land, it would only mean that such heirs succeed to rights up to expiry of lease period - If lease, on other hand, were for and indefinite period, and contains a provision for rights thereunder being heritable, then, such a lease, though ordinarily for lifetime of lessee, would be construed as permanent - Held, lease was not intended to be permanent - Appeal dismissed.
