K.Ashok Reddy v K.Ashok Reddy

Supreme Court of India

07 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, Kuldip Singh, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 352; (1994) 2 SCC 303; AIR 1994 SC 1685; AIR 1994 SCW 1431; JT 1994 (1) SC 401; 1994 (2) MLJ(SC) 22; 1994(1) SCALE 377; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 662; 1994 (1) SLR 809; 1994 (2) UJ 61; 1994 (1) UPLBEC 424

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 222 - Transfer of Judges - Whether Judges of High Courts are not liable to be transferred from one to another HC? - Exercise of power of transfer u/art. 222 is to subserve a public purpose and to promote 'public interest' for better administration of justice, which is inherent guideline implicit in art. 222 - Guideline of 'public interest', i.e., 'for promoting better administration of justice throughout country' is sufficient for proper exercise of power and to exclude any arbitrariness - Application of broad guidelines to individual cases according to norms evolved in practice has to be left to discretion of Chief Justice of India to be exercised in consultation with his colleagues whose opinion is required to be taken for this purpose - Judicial review of transfer of members of transferable services is exceptional and is made only in extraordinary cases on limited grounds - Transfer of a HC Judge is justifiable only on grounds indicated in Judges' Case-III and only at instance of transferred Judge himself and not anyone else - Decision made by collective exercise of several Judges at apex level or objective criterion on which recommendation of Chief Justice of India is based, is an in-built check against arbitrariness - Appeals dismissed.
