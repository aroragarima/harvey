New India Assurance Company Limited v Yadu Sambhaji More and others

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 20; (2011) 2 SCC 416; 2011 (1) ACC 1; AIR 2011 SC 666; 2011 (4) ALD(SC) 36; 2011 (85) ALR 233; 2011 (2) AWC 1089; 2011 (2) Bom.C.R. 1; JT 2011 (1) SC 73; 2011 (5) MLJ(SC) 678; 2011 (2) MPLJ 468; 2011 (162) PLR 468; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 613; 2011(1) SCALE 130; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 160

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Insurance - Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 92A - Fire explosion in petrol tanker - Motor accidental claim - Sustainability - Petrol tanker in question collided with another vehicle and as a result petrol started leaking from the tanker - Large number of people gathered at the site and started to collect petrol from the leaked tanker - Tanker caught fire and exploded wherein 46 persons lost their lives - Legal heirs of deceased (claimants) filed claim petitions before the Tribunal u/s. 92A of the Act - Appellant (insurer of the petrol tanker) questioned the jurisdiction of the Tribunal to entertain such petitions on the ground that the fire and the explosion causing the death of those who had assembled at the accident site could not be said to be an accident arising out of the use of a motor vehicle - Tribunal accepted appellant's contention and dismissed claimant's petitions - Tribunal order was challenged before the HC - HC reversed the Tribunal order - Hence the present appeal by insurer/appellant - Whether the death of the victims resulted from an accident arising out of the use of the petrol tanker was concluded by the decision of SC in Shivaji Dayanu Patil and anr. v. Vatschala Uttam, 1991 INDLAW SC 696 and any finding recorded by the Tribunal contrary to the decision of SC was completely illegal and untenable - Held, SC, in Shivaji Dayanu Patil case, held that explosion and fire resulting in the injuries which led to the death of victim was due to an accident arising out of the use of the motor vehicle viz. the petrol tanker - Tribunal may arrive at a finding at variance with the finding recorded by a superior court on the same issue on an application u/s. 92A of the Act, however, such finding by the tribunal must be based on some material facts coming to light from the evidences led before it that were not available before the superior court while dealing with the proceeding u/s. 92A of the Act - No new material was produced by the appellant which was not already before the SC in Shivaji Dayanu Patil case - Further, no new points were raised before the Tribunal that can be said to have not been raised before SC in Shivaji Dayanu Patil - Therefore, in the facts and circumstances of the present case, the decision rendered in Shivaji Dayanu Patil was completely binding on the Tribunal and it was not open to the Tribunal to come to any finding inconsistent with the aforesaid decision of SC - No merit in the appeal - Appeal dismissed.
