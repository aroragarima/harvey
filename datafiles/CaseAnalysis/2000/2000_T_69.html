Skypak Couriers Ltd. Etc. Etc. v Tata Chemicals Ltd. Etc. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, G.B. Pattanaik, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 57; (2000) 5 SCC 294; AIR 2000 SC 2008; 2000 (40) ALR 255; 2000 (2) ARBLR 660; 2000 (3) AWC 2326; 2000 (4) Bom.C.R. 220; 2000 (3) CompLJ 353; 2000 (2) CPJ(Sc) 6; 2000 (2) CPJ(Sc) 6B; JT 2000 (6) SC 560; 2000 (3) MLJ(SC) 74; 2000(4) SCALE 580; [2000] Supp1 S.C.R. 324; 2001 (1) UC 328

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Corporate

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of

Summary: Consumer Protection Act, 1986, ss. 3, 13 and 22 - (A) Whether consumer commission can refer dispute to consensual arbitration and pass orders on it? - Held, no - Commission cannot refer dispute to consensual adjudication by a third person dehors Arbitration Act and pass orders based on such decision - Commissions under Consumer Protection Act do not have jurisdiction to refer dispute pending before it, for a consensual adjudication by third person and then make said decision of so - called consensual arbitrator, an order of commission itself - Commission not applying its own mind or adjudication on disputes and is merely putting its assent on decisions given by third parties - By doing this it is abdicating its own functions and duties - (B) Whether during existence of an arbitration clause, a complaint can be preferred to Consumer Protection Act? - Existence of an arbitration clause will not be a bar to entertainment of complaint by redressal agency, constituted under Consumer Protection Act, since remedy provided under Act is in addition to provisions of any other law for time being in force - Matter remitted back to commission - Appeals disposed of.
