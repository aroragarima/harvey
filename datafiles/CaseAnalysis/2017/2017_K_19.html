State represented by the Principal Secretary to Government, Home (Police XIX) Department and others v K. S. Palanichamy and others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

N.V. Ramana, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 400; AIR 2017 SC 2590; 2017 (2) MLJ(Crl) 741; 2017(6) SCALE 298

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: BANKING & FINANCE, Tamil Nadu Protection of Interests of Depositors (in Financial Establishments) Act, 1997, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Attachment, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 406, 420 - Tamil Nadu Protection of Interests of Depositors (in Financial Establishments) Act, 1997, ss. 3, 3(i), 3(ii), 4, 4(1), 4(2), 4(3), 4(4), 5 - Complaint filed - Fresh proceedings initiated - Validity - Complainant deposited huge sums of money with the Financial Establishment, administered by respondents - Respondent no. 1 returned back only a part of deposited amount to complainant and absconded - FIR was lodged against respondents u/ss. 406, 420 IPC r/w s. 5 of the Act - G.O. dt. 24-12-2012, was issued attaching immovable properties of respondents u/s. 3 of the Act - Respondents filed criminal original petition before HC challenging the same - HC allowed petition by setting aside G.O. and directed competent authority to initiate fresh proceedings in terms of ss. 3 and 4 of the Act - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, in order to curb the malady of financial swindlers and to deal with such problems effectively, State enacted that statute to protect the interest of innocent investors. Therefore, as per the procedure envisaged u/ss. 3 and 4 of the Act unequivocally the power to set the process into motion vests with State Govt. by passing an ad-interim attachment order and thereafter act mandates appointment of competent authority u/s. 4(1) of the Act, to take further recourse as per the procedures laid down u/s. 4 of the Act. SC was not able to appreciate the reasoning recorded by HC. The language employed u/ss. 3 and 4 of the Act was plain, unambiguous and it does not call for any interpretation as sort to be placed by counsel appearing for respondents. While enacting the provisions the legislature has consciously given the discretion for passing an ad-interim order and was evident from the language used in section by inserting "Government may". In instant case, even before the other provisions were complied with HC erroneously interfered with the ad-interim order which defeats the very purpose of the provision which was incorporated to safeguard the interest of investors. Impugned order passed by State Govt. u/s. 3 of the Act attaching immovable properties standing in the name of respondents, basing on complaint of investors and directing competent authority to take u/s. 4(3)(4) of the Act was valid and in consonance with the provisions of the Act. Impugned order of HC was set aside. Appeal allowed.
