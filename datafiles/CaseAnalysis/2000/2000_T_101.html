D. P. Chadha v Triyugi Narain Mishra and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2743; (2001) 2 SCC 221; AIR 2001 SC 457; 2001 (42) ALR 435; 2001 (1) AWC 407; 2001 (1) CGLJ 546; 2001 (1) CompLJ 283; 2001 (89) DLT 49; 2001 (3) G.L.R. 2687; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 505; 2001 (2) MLJ(SC) 9; 2000(8) SCALE 76; 2001 (1) SLT 13; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 345; 2000 (8) Supreme 251; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 241

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Advocates, Personal Presence, CPC (Amendment) Act, 1976

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Advocates Act, 1961, s. 37 - Advocate, appellant, held guilty of professional misconduct by Rajasthan State Bar Council and punished with suspension from practice for a period of five years - Enhancement of punishment by Bar Council Of India - Appeal - Charge of misconduct is a serious matter for a practising advocate - Very wide jurisdiction has been conferred on Bar Council of India by sub-section (2) of s. 37 - An order enhancing punishment, being an order prejudicially affecting advocate, proviso mandates exercise of such power to be performed only after giving advocate reasonable opportunity of being heard - Advocate should be given a reasonable opportunity of showing cause against such proposed enhancement and then he should be heard - Held, non - Compliance of s. 37 - Appellant is about 60 years of age - Misconduct alleged relates to year 1993 - In facts and circumstances of case finding that appellant is guilty of professional misconduct is upheld but sentence awarded by Rajasthan State Bar Council suspending appellant from practice for a period of five years is upheld and restored - Order of Bar Council of India, only to extent of enhancing punishment, is set aside - Appeal partly allowed.
