The Consumer Action Group & Anr. v State Of Tamil Nadu & Ors.!The Consumer Action Groupvs.State Of Tamil Nadu & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

18 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 417; (2000) 7 SCC 425; AIR 2000 SC 3060; JT 2000 (9) SC 272; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 13; 2000(6) SCALE 1; 2000 (6) SLT 425; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 523; 2000 (5) Supreme 602

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Tamil Nadu Private Educational Institutions (Regulation) Act, 1966, Tamil Nadu Mineral Concession Rules, 1959

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.14, 21, 32 - Delhi Laws Act,1912 - Essential Supplies (Temporary Powers) Act, 1946 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Tamil Nadu Private Educational Institutions (Regulation) Act, 1966, s. 22 - Tamil Nadu Town and Country Planning Act, 1971, ss.113,122, 123, 124, 124(3) -Tamil Nadu Mineral Concession Rules, 1959, r. 39 - Constitutional validity - Ultra vires - Delegation by Legislature - Exemptions - Petitioner challenges constitutional validity of s.113 of1971 Act as it being ultra vires of arts.14 and 21 of Constitution and also validity of orders passed under it, granting exemptions by respondent no.1 - Petitioner had brought to notice impunity with which executive power of State S.113 of 1971 Act as ultra vires under Act - To wipe out any development without any check which amounts to delegation by Legislature of its essential legislative power - Whether power exercised by Govt. in passing impugned sixty two G.Os u/s.113 of 1971 Act could be arbitrary or illegal - Held, that State Govt. granting exemptions to various persons u/s.113 of Act could not be sustained - In absence of s.113-A of 1961 Act consequence of demolition would have been only option - S.113 of 1971 Act was valid - It does not suffer from vice of excessive delegation of any essential legislative function - Preamble, Objects and Reasons and various provisions of Act give clear-cut policy and guidelines to Govt. for exercising its power - S.113-A measure brought in through 1998 Act was valid piece of legislation and not ultra vires - Petition partly allowed.
