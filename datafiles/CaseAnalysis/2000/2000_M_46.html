M. K. Shanmugam and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, N. Santosh Hegde, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 291; (2000) 4 SCC 476; AIR 2000 SC 2704; JT 2000 (5) SC 601; 2000 LabIC 2874; 2000 (2) LLN 953; 2000(4) SCALE 590; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 554; 2000 (3) SLJ 306; 2000 (4) SLR 418

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Fixation of seniority - Respondents were directly recruited through the Union Public Service Commission as Assistant Executive Engineers (Electrical) Class I - Both the appellants and the other respondents were subsequently promoted on ad hoc basis and thereafter they were regularised as Executive Engineers (Electrical) - Respondents were aggrieved by the revision of final seniority list of Executive Engineers - Application was filed before Tribunal - Tribunal was of the view that ad hoc service to count for seniority must be rendered continuously till the date of regularisation for 15 years or more and, therefore, it held that the appellants could not take advantage of the ad hoc promotions made purely as a stop gap arrangement and it was only in special circumstances such ad hoc service could be counted for purpose of seniority as noticed in some of the decisions of this Court - It was declared that the appellants were not entitled to count their ad hoc service in the post of Executive Engineers (Electrical) for seniority, confirmation, promotion, etc. - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, conclusions reached by the Tribunal is correct and call for no interference. Appeals dismissed.
