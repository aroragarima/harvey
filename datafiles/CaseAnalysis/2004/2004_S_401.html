Ram Swaroop And Others v State Of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

15 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 188; (2004) 13 SCC 134; (2005) SCC (Cr) 61; AIR 2004 SC 2943; JT 2004 (3) SC 350; 2004(3) SCALE 303

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 302, 304, 307, 323, 34, 341 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, ss. 161 - Appeal against acquittal - Held that this was not a case in which HC ought to have interfered with order of acquittal passed by trial court - Well settled that if two views are reasonably possible on basis of evidence on record, view which favours accused must be preferred - HC ought not to interfere with an order of acquittal if view taken by trial court is also a reasonable view of evidence on record and findings recorded by trial court are not manifestly erroneous, contrary to evidence on record or perverse - Appeal allowed - Acquittals confirmed.
