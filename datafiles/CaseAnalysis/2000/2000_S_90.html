State of Maharashtra and Others v Santosh Shankar Acharya

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 572; (2000) 7 SCC 463; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1400; AIR 2000 SC 2504; 2000 AllCriR 704; 2000 (5) Bom.C.R. 751; 2000 (3) CCR 106; 2000 (3) Crimes 148; 2000 CRLJ 3939; 2000 (2) JCC 576; JT 2000 (8) SC 374; 2000 (3) RCR(Criminal) 578; 2000(5) SCALE 387; 2000 (5) SLT 762; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 67; 2000 (5) Supreme 418

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bombay General Act, 1904

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Maharashtra Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Slumlords, Boot-leggers, Drugs Offenders and Dangerous Persons Act, 1981,ss. 3(2), 8(1) - General Clauses Act, 1897 - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - - Bombay General Act, 1904, ss.14, 21 - Detention order - Non information of rights - Sustainability - Detention Orders were issued by concerned officer empowered by State Govt. u/s. 3 (2) of the 1981 Act and grounds of detention were communicated but was not informed of his right of representation and was challenged - Appellant referred question to HC as to whether non information of rights would constitute infraction of valuable right under constitution - DB referred to Full Bench and Full Bench relying on Constitutional Bench of SC in Kamlesh Kumar Ishwardas Patel vs. Union of India,1995 Indlaw SC 1014 and after analyzing different provisions of the 1981 Act answered in favour of respondents and against appellant/State Govt. - Hence instant appeals - Whether finding of Full Bench of HC was justified - Held, s. 8 of the 1981 Act specifically provides that detenu must be communicated grounds of detention Order as soon as possible but not later than 5 days from date of detention - It was mandatory obligation on both authorities passing detention orders either u/ss. 3 (1) or 3 (2) of the 1981 Act - Legislature engrafted every part of statute for specific purpose and intention of legislature was that every part of statute should be given effect - Detenu had right to representation to detaining authority till order of detention was not approved by State Govt. and non-communication of his rights to detenu would constitute infraction of valuable constitutional right guaranteed to detenu u/art. 22(5) of Constitution failing which detention order would be held invalid - Upheld decision of Full Bench - Appeals dismissed.
