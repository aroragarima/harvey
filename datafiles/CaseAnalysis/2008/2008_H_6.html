Emp. State Insurance Corporation v H M T Ltd & Anr

Supreme Court of India

11 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 154; (2008) 3 SCC 35; AIR 2008 SC 1322; 2008(2) ALL MR 336; 2008 (1) CLR 764; 2008 (116) FLR 543; JT 2008 (1) SC 645; 2009 (6) KarLJ 488; 2008 (1) LLJ 814; 2008 (3) MLJ(SC) 83; 2008(1) SCALE 341; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 646; 2008 (2) UPLBEC 1694

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948, Employees State Insurance (General) Regulations, 1950

Summary: Employees State Insurance Act; Employees State Insurance (General) Regulations, 1950 - Interpretation and application of s. 85-B and reg. 31C - Held, when a discretionary jurisdiction has been conferred on a statutory authority to levy penal damages by reason of an enabling provision, the same cannot be construed as imperative - An endeavour should be made to construe such penal provisions as discretionary, under the statute is held to be mandatory in character - Statute itself does not say that a penalty has to be levied only in the manner prescribed - Legislation does not provide that adjudication for the purpose of levy of penalty proceeding would be a mere formality or imposition of penalty as also computation of the quantum thereof became a foregone conclusion - Matter remitted to High Court.
