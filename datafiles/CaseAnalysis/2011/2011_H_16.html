Harjit Singh v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 210; (2011) 4 SCC 441; 2011 CRLJ 2332; 2011 (2) JCC (Narcotics) 118; JT 2011 (4) SC 100; 2011 (2) RCR(Criminal) 560; 2011(4) SCALE 116; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 691

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Opium Act, 1878

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 2, 8 and 18 - Possession of contraband - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant was convicted for the offence punishable u/s. 18 of the NDPS Act - On appeal, HC affirmed the judgment and order of conviction passed by Special Judge - Hence, instant appeal - Whether in view of the fact that the entire substance recovered from the appellant was opium and not any kind of mixture, the question of determining the quantity or percentage of morphine in the substance could not arise? - Held, opium derivatives have to be dealt with under Entry No.93, so in case of pure opium falling under clause (a) of s. 2(xv), determination of the quantity of morphine was not required - In the instant case, the material recovered from the appellant was opium - It was of a commercial quantity and could not have been for personal consumption of the appellant - The instant case squarely falls under clause (a) of s. 2(xv) of the NDPS Act and Clause (b) thereof was not attracted for the reason that the substance recovered was opium in the form of the coagulated juice of the opium poppy - It was not a mixture of opium with any other neutral substance - There was no preparation to produce any new substance from the said coagulated juice - Thus as the case falls under clause (a) of s. 2(xv), no further consideration was required - Thus the appellant being in possession of the contraband substance had violated the provisions of s. 8 of the NDPS Act and was rightly convicted u/s. 18(b) of the NDPS Act - Appeal dismissed.
