Acharya Jagdishwaranand Avadhuta, Etc v Commissioner of Police, Calcutta and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 October 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, Amarendra Nath Sen, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 431; (1983) 4 SCC 522; (1984) SCC (Cr) 1; AIR 1984 SC 51; 1984 (1) Crimes 318; 1983 CRLJ 1872; 1984 CrLR(SC) 37; 1983(2) SCALE 565; [1984] 1 S.C.R. 447

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 144 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 25 and 26 - Performance of Tandava dance - Order u/s. 144 - Validity of - Whether it is a religious rite or practice essential to tenets of religious faith of Ananda Margis? - Held, even a cursory study of growth and development of Hindu religion through ages shows that whenever a saint or a religious reformer attempted task of reforming Hindu religion and fighting irrational or corrupt practices which had crept into it, a sect was born which was governed by its own tenets, but which basically subscribed to fundamental notions of Hindu religion and Hindu philosophy - It is competent to a legislature to pass a law permitting an appropriate authority to take anticipatory action or place anticipatory restrictions upon particular kinds of acts in an emergency for purpose of maintaining public order - Impugned order u/s. 144 of Code did not ban processions or gatherings at public places even by Ananda Margis, but prohibition was with reference to carrying of daggers, trishuls and skulls - Even performance of tandava dance in public places, which is not an essential part of religious rites to be observed by Ananda Margis, has not been prohibited - Petition dismissed.
