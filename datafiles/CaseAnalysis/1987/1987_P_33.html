Reserve Bank Of India v Peerless General Finance And Investment Co. Ltd.Ors. And Vice

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28121; (1987) 1 SCC 424; AIR 1987 SC 1023; 1987 (2) BankCLR 267; 1987 (1) CCC 721; [1987] 61 Comp Cas 663; 1987 (1) CompLJ 162; JT 1987 (1) SC 246; 1987(1) SCALE 100; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 1; 1987 (1) UJ 586

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Reserve Bank of India Act, Commercial Banks

Summary: (A) Constitution - Interpretation of statute - Held, interpretation must depend on the text and the context - That interpretation is best which makes the textual interpretation match the contextual - A statute was best interpreted when it was known why it was enacted - With this knowledge, the statute must be read, first as a whole and then section by section, clause by clause, phrase by phrase and word by word - No part of a statute and no word of a statute could be construed in isolation - Appeals dismissed.

(B) Banking & Finance - Contract & Commercial - Prize Chits and Money Circulation Schemes (Banning) Act, 1978 - (A) Interpretation of the definition of 'Prize Chit' in s. 2(e) of the Prize Chits and Money Circulation Schemes (Banning) Act, 1978 - Held, it could not be said that by using the word 'includes' in the definition in s. 2(e) of the Act, the Parliament intended to so expand the meaning of prize chit as to take in every scheme involving subscribing and refunding of money - Word 'includes', the context showed, was intended not to expand the meaning of 'prize chit' but to cover all transactions or arrangements of the nature of prize chits but under different names - (B) Whether Clauses (i) and (ii) of s. 2(e) were disjunctive? - Held, how Clauses (i) and (ii) of s. 2(e) have to be read depends on the context - Context required the definition to be read as if both clauses have to be satisfied - Two requirements mentioned in the two Clauses (i) and (ii) of the definition were not to be read disjunctively; they were two distinct attributes of 'Prize Chits', each of which had to be satisfied - Appeals dismissed.

(C) Banking & Finance - Contract & Commercial - Prize Chits and Money Circulation Schemes (Banning) Act, 1978 - Whether the Endowment Scheme piloted by the Company falls within the definition of prize chit of the Prize Chits and Money Circulation Schemes (Banning) Act? - Under this scheme, a subscriber was required to pay a fixed annual subscription for a fixed number of years varying between the minimum of 10 years and the maximum of 30 years - On the expiry of the period, the subscriber will be paid by the company a sum of money called the Endowment Sum which was the face value of the Certificate - Held, s. 2(e) did not contemplate a scheme without a prize and, therefore, the Endowment Certificate Scheme of the Peerless Company was outside the Prize Chits and Money Circulation Schemes (Banning) Act - Appeals dismissed.
