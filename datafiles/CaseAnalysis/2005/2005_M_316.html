Shri Banarsi Dass v Mrs. Teeku Dutta and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 324; (2005) 4 SCC 449; 2005 (3) ALD(SC) 78; 2005 (5) Bom.C.R. 778; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 189; JT 2005 (4) SC 627; 2005 (2) KLT 729; 2005 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 813; 2005 (2) MLJ(SC) 181; 2005 (141) PLR 229; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 666; 2005(4) SCALE 433; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 923

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Family & Personal - Indian Succession Act, 1925, s. 372 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 115, 151 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 112, 4 - DNA Test - Grant of succession certificate - Whether a direction for Deoxyribonucleic Acid Test (commonly known as DNA test) can be given in a proceeding for issuance of succession certificate under Indian Succession Act, 1925? - Held, trial court erroneously held that documents produced by respondents were not sufficient or relevant for purpose of adjudication and DNA test was conclusive - It is for parties to place evidence in support of their respective claims and establish their stands - DNA test is not to be directed as a matter of routine and only in deserving cases - Appeal dismissed.
