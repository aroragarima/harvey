Â Minerva Mills Limited and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Bhagwati, A.C. Gupta, N.L. Untwalia, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 460; (1980) 2 SCC 591; AIR 1980 SC 1789; [1981] S.C.R. 206; 1980 UJ 727

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution (Forty-Second Amendment) Act, 1976 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Amendment - S. 4 of the Constitution (Forty-Second Amendment) Act, 1976, which came into force with effect from 3-1-1977 amended art. 31-C of the Constitution by substituting the words 'all or any of the principles laid down in Part IV' for the words 'the principles specified in cl. (b) or cl. (c) of art. 39' - S. 4 was beyond the amending power of the Parliament and was void since it damaged the basic or essential features of the Constitution and destroys its basic structure by a total exclusion of challenge to any law on the ground that it was inconsistent with, or takes away or abridges any of the rights conferred by art. 14 or art. 19 of the Constitution, if the law was for giving effect to the policy of the State towards securing all or any of the principles laid down in Part IV of the Constitution - Hence, the present appeal - Whether s. 4 of the Constitution amending art. 31-C of the Constitution was constitutionally valid - Held, SC could not be persuaded to pass an order pronouncing upon the question without a reasoned judgment, since the question was one of grave and momentous consequence involving, as it did, the validity of a constitutional amendment - Order accordingly.
