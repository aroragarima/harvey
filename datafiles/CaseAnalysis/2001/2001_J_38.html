Dr. Vijay Laxmi Sadho v Jagdish

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20414; (2001) 2 SCC 247; AIR 2001 SC 600; AIR 2001 SCW 223; JT 2001 (1) SC 382; 2001 (2) MLJ(SC) 120; 2001(1) SCALE 63B; 2001 (2) SCJ 449; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 95; 2001 (1) SLT 339; 2001 (1) Supreme 41; 2001 (1) UC 379B

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Allegations Of Corrupt Practice, High Court Rules, Madhya Pradesh High Court Rules

Summary: Election - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 329(b), 348(1) and 348(2) - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 81, 83(1) and 86(1) - Madhya Pradesh High Court Rules, rr.2(b), 9 and 94-A - Elections to Madhya Pradesh Legislative Assembly - Appellant was declared elected - Election petition - Election petition drawn up in Hindi language - Maintainability of election petition - (A) Affidavit filed in support of allegations of corrupt practice was not drawn up in manner prescribed by s. 83(1) - Whether can be ground for dismissal of election petition? - Election petition is liable to be dismissed in limine u/s. 86(1) of Act only if election petition does not comply with either provisions of s. 81 or s. 82 or s. 117 of Act - Held, election petition is not liable to be dismissed in limine u/s. 86 of Act, for alleged non-compliance with provisions of s. 83(1) of Act or of its proviso - Defect in verification of an affidavit is curable and does not merit dismissal of an election petition in limine u/s. 86(1) of Act - (B) Whether election petition can be dismissed on ground that election petition and affidavit filed in support thereof, had been drawn up in Hindi language and not in English language? - Rules framed by HC relating to trial of election petitions are only procedural in nature and do not constitute 'substantive law' - Those rules have to be read along with other statutory provisions to appreciate consequences of non-compliance with High Court Rules - By a notification D/- 18-09-1971 issued by Governor of Madhya Pradesh, in exercise of powers conferred by cl. (2) of art. 348 of Constitution, authorised use of Hindi language in all proceedings of HC other than for drawing up decrees, orders and judgments of HC, subject to certain conditions - R. 2(b) cannot be so construed as to render constitutional provisions contained in art. 348(2) as 'meaningless' - R. 2(b) has to be read along with notification issued by Governor and when so construed, it follows that an election petition may be filed in Hindi language and it cannot be dismissed at threshold u/s. 86 of Act for alleged non-compliance with r. 2(b) - Appeal dismissed.
