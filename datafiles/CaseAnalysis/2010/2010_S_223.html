Dahyabhai Ranchhoddas Dhobi & Anr. v State Of Gujarat & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

23 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 535; (2010) 7 SCC 705; AIR 2010 SC 3006; 2010 (82) ALR 241; 2011 (1) AWC 178; JT 2010 (7) SC 444; 2010 (3) KLT 403; 2010 (111) RD 196; 2010(7) SCALE 232; [2010] 8 S.C.R. 575; 2010 (3) UPLBEC 2300

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Land Acquisition (Amendment and Validation) Ordinance, 1967, Gujarat Town Planning & Urban Development Act, 1976

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 6(2), 11A - Land acquisition - Award u/s. 11A - Limitation - Appellants' land were acquired by the respondent/State for construction of a school - Land Acquisition Officer passed the award u/s. 11A of the Act - Appellant contended that award u/s. 11A had been published after the stipulated period of two years from the date of s. 6(2) declaration - Held, period of two years from the date of publication of the declaration prescribed u/s. 11A for passing the award, must be calculated from the last of the series of the publications referred to u/s. 6(2) - Notification u/s. 6 was last published on 19-4-1990, by affixing a copy of the Notification on the notice board of the office of City Mamlatdar and also by affixing the same in the locality of acquisition - Hence the period of two years stipulated in s. 11A would begin to run from 19-4-1990 and, therefore, the publication of the award u/s. 11 of the Act on 18-4-1992 was within the stipulated time limit of two years - Appeals dismissed.

(B) Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Gujarat Town Planning and Urban Development Act, 1976, ss. 17, 20 - Change of classification - Land acquisition - Validity - Respondent/Corporation, by a notification, has changed the classification of the land in question and designated as 'residential use' in an earlier occasion - Appellant/landowners contended that since the land was designated as 'residential purpose', respondents were not permitted to set up a school in that land - Held, land in question was reserved for school purpose in the development plan sanctioned by the State Government u/s. 20 of the Act - Further, inasmuch as the land in question was kept under reservation for school in the sanctioned development plan for the State Government u/s. 17 of the Act, it was the duty of the respondent/Corporation to acquire the land for implementing the provisions of the same - Appeals dismissed.

(C) Land & Property - Land acquisition - Need and necessity - Determination of - Appellant/landowners contended that in view of existence of many schools in and around the vicinity, there was no need to establish a school in the land of the appellants - Held, there was no municipal school near the site in question and that the schools of the respondent/Corporation were very far from the land under acquisition and due to increase in population in the city, they were justified in establishing a school for providing primary education to the children in the said area - In respect of 'need' and 'necessity', it is for the Government and their authorities to take a decision considering various aspects and if such a decision is taken based on materials it is not for the Court to doubt their claim - Appeals dismissed.
