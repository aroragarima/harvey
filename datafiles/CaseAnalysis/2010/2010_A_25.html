Abu Salem Abdul Qayoom Ansari v State of Maharashtra and another

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 727; (2011) 11 SCC 214; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 251; JT 2010 (10) SC 202; 2010 (4) MLJ(Crl) 39; 2010 (4) RCR(Criminal) 710; 2010(9) SCALE 460

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Extradition (Amendment) Act, 1993, Theft Act, 1968, United Kingdom Extradition Act, 1870, International Extradition and World Public Order, 1974, Fire Arms Act, 1968

Summary: (A) Criminal - Constitution - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 3(3), 3(2)(i), (ii), 3(3), 3(4), 5 and 6 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 302, 307, 326, 324, 427, 435, 436, 201 and 212 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Explosives Act, 1884, ss. 9-B(1), (a), (b), (c) - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25(1A), (1B) - Extradition Act, 1962, ss. 2 and 21(b) - Explosive Substances Act, 1908, ss. 3, 4(a), (b), 5 and 6 - Prevention of Damage to Public Property Act, 1984, s. 4 - Bombay Bomb Blast case - Jurisdiction to try in respect of offences which do not form part of extradition judgment - A single charge-sheet was filed in the Designated Court against 189 accused persons, of which, 44 accused persons were shown as absconding - As appellant was absconding, Designated Court, Mumbai issued Non-bailable Warrant against appellant and Interpol Secretariat General, Lyons, France also issued a Red Corner Notice for his arrest - Appellant was detained by Portuguese Police in view of the Red Corner Notice - Government of India submitted request for extradition of the appellant in 9 criminal cases relying on the International Convention for the Suppression of Terrorist Bombings and on an assurance of reciprocity as applicable in international law - Thereafter, the Government of India issued Gazette Notification, directing that the provisions of the Extradition Act, other than Chapter-III, shall apply to the Portuguese Republic with effect from 13.12.2002 - Request for the extradition of the appellant was considered and examined by the Government of Portugal and by the Court of Appeals, Lisbon, Supreme Court of Justice, Portugal and Constitutional Court of Portugal - Authorities/Courts in Portugal granted extradition of the appellant in 8 criminal cases - Extradition order did not include the same offences for which the charges were framed by the Designated Court, Mumbai - Upon extradition, custody of the appellant was handed over by the Government of Portugal to Indian Authorities - After completion of further investigation against the appellant-Abu Salem, a Supplementary Report u/s. 173(8) of CrPC was filed in the Designated Court, Mumbai - Thereafter, the prosecution filed application seeking separation of the trial of the accused from the main trial in the Bombay Bomb Blast case - Designated Court allowed the application of the Prosecution for separation of trial - It was also held by the Designated Court that although the overt acts with which the appellant was charged may not be cognate with the ingredients of offence with which he was charged, however, they are lesser offences for the purposes of s. 21 of the Extradition Act - Present appeals filed challenging the said order and a writ petition u/art. 32 of the Constitution also filed - Whether criminal courts in the country have jurisdiction to try in respect of offences which do not form part of the extradition judgment, by virtue of which appellant was brought to this country and he can be tried only for the offences mentioned in the extradition decree? - Appellant contended that he can be tried only for the offences covered u/art. 2(1) of the said Convention - Held, a complete reading of art. 2 of the said Convention makes it clear that it deals not only with those accused who commit the substantive offences as defined in art. 2(1) but also includes all the conspirators and those who have constructive liability for commission of the substantive offences - In the present case, appellant was extradited not only under the said Convention but also in the light of the principle of reciprocity made applicable through the application of the Extradition Act to the Republic of Portugal - In addition to extradition judgment, appellant can also be tried for lesser offence/offences in view of s. 21 of the Extradition Act disclosed by the facts proved for the purposes of securing his surrender - Also, in view of the fact that the provisions of the Extradition Act, 1962 have been made applicable to Portugal, provisions contained in s. 21 of the Act would come into operation while conducting the trial of appellant - Hence, Designated Judge correctly concluded that the appellant can be tried for 'lesser offences', even if, the same are not covered by the Extradition Decree since the same is permitted u/s. 21(b) of the Extradition Act - Appeals dismissed.

(B) Criminal - Constitution - Extradition Act, 1962, ss. 2 and 21(b) - "Lesser offence" - Meaning of - Held, it means an offence which is made out from the proved facts and provides lesser punishment, as compared to the offences for which the fugitive has been extradited - Offence has to be an extradition offence, as defined u/s. 2(c)(ii) of the Act i.e. an offence punishable with imprisonment for a term which shall not be less than one year under the laws of India or of a foreign State - Lesser offence cannot be equated with the term "minor offence" as mentioned in s. 222 of the CrPC - Thus, the punishment provided for the offence is relevant and not the ingredients for the purposes of interpretation of the term "lesser offence" - Appeals dismissed.
