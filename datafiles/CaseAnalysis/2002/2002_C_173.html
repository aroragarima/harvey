Commissioner of Income Tax v Silver and Arts Palace

Supreme Court of India

18 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1935; (2003) 11 SCC 344; 2003 (180) CTR 309; [2003] 259 ITR 684; 174 TAXATION 742; [2003] 129 TAXMAN 56

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Assessment year - Counter sales - Deduction - Introduction of Explanation - Foreign exchange - Appeal directed against judgment of HC who in turn dismiss the appeal u/s.260A of Act carried by Revenue against order of Income-tax Tribunal dismissing its appeal - Assessee has a business of sale of semi-precious and precious stones, handicrafts, jewellery, etc - In return filed for assessment year 1996-97, it had declared a total income of Rs.1,51,000 and claimed deduction of Rs.80, 12, 786 u/s.80HHC of Act - Assessing Officer found that sales effected by assessee included counter sales of Rs.9,76,40,672 and, therefore, deduction claimed by assessee was disallowed - On appeal Commissioner of Income-tax failed, but its appeal to Tribunal succeeded - HC considered the effect of introduction of Explanation (aa) to s.80HHC(4A) of Act - Whether there was any disputes between parties within the meaning of Explanation (aa) to s.80HHC (4A) of Act - Held, no dispute between parties that transactions of counter sales effected by respondent involved customs clearance within the meaning of Explanation (aa) to s.80HHC (4A) of Act - Sales were in convertible foreign exchange - Revenue accepted and consistently followed position of law settled in case of assessee itself - No merit in appeal - Appeal dismissed.
