Ganeshlal v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 86; (1992) 3 SCC 106; (1993) SCC (Cr) 435; AIR 1992 SCW 1175; 1992 (3) Bom.C.R. 671; 1992 (2) CCR 48; 1992 (2) Crimes 161; 1992 CRLJ 1545; 1992 CrLR(SC) 433; 1993 (1) DMC 326; 1992 (3) HLR 596; JT 1992 (2) SC 592; 1992(1) SCALE 811; [1992] 2 S.C.R. 502

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Custodial Death

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 313 - Appellant, husband of deceased and his family members were charged for murder of deceased - Motive of offence is not very clear but apparently deceased was ill-treated due to poor financial status of her parents - Whether death was suicidal or homicidal and appellants are liable to be convicted? - Held, yes - Death of deceased was homicidal in nature - Testimony of sister of deceased that deceased was being beaten when she had gone to her house - Further conduct of appellant was very much suspicious as he did not attempt to save her when she was in flames - Appellant failed to give plausible explanation as to death of deceased except denying prosecution case - Testimony of witnesses and facts are consistent with hypothesis that appellant is a prime accused - Failure to discover motive of offence does not signify non-existence of crime - Conviction upheld - Appeal dismissed.
