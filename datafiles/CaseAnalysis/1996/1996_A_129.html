Andhra Bank Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2576; 1997 (140) CTR 334; [1997] 225 ITR 447; [1997] 92 TAXMAN 534

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 147 - Whether on facts and in circumstances of case, assessments for years 1960-61, 1961-62 and 1962-63 have been validly reopened u/s. 147(b) of Income Tax Act, 1961? - Held, ITO allowed change in method of accounting for assessment years concerned knowingly - It was not a case of an inadvertent mistake which was discovered later on after completion of assessment or oversight - Once it is found that change in method of accounting was knowingly allowed by ITO after taking into account all relevant facts it is not permissible for ITO, or his successor, to reopen assessment at a later point of time u/s. 147(b) - Question referred answered in negative, in favour of assessee and against revenue - Appeals allowed.
