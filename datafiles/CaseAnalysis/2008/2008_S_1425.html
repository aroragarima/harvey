United India Insurance Company Limited v Suresh

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2061; (2008) 12 SCC 657; 2008 (2) ACC 453; AIR 2008 SC 2871; 2008 (6) ALD(SC) 87; 2008 (3) AWC 2182; 2008 (2) CPJ(Sc) 25; JT 2008 (6) SC 244; 2008 (4) KLT 552; 2009 (3) MLJ(SC) 317; 2008 (152) PLR 387; 2008 (4) RCR(Civil) 907; 2008(6) SCALE 589; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 1178

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Gratuitous Passenger, Owner Of Goods

Summary: Constitution of India-Article 142-Motor Vehicles Act, 1988-Sections 147 (1), 166 and 168-Compensation - Liability-Applicability of Section 147 (1)-Claimant hired auto rickshaw being goods vehicle-Accident occurred while he was sitting by side of driver-No other person whether as passenger or owner of vehicle-Supposed to share seat of driver-Violation of condition of contract of insurance proved-Tribunal and High Court should have held that owner of vehicle guilty of breach of conditions of policy-But in view of facts and circumstances of case-And with a view to doing complete justice between parties-Appellant insurer to pay amount of compensation to claimant-And realise same from owner of vehicle.
