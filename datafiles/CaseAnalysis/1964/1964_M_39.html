First Additional Income Tax Officer, Kozhikode v Mrs. Suseela Sadanandan and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 283; [1965] 57 ITR 168

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Constitution - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 24-B(1), 24-B(2) and 34 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 19(1)(f) - (A) Whether High Court should have dismissed writ petition in exercise of its discretion as question raised by 1st respondent involved complicated questions of fact and law which could not be satisfactorily disposed of in a summary proceeding u/art. 226? - Held, existence of an alternative remedy is no ground for refusing to issue an appropriate writ u/art. 226 of Constitution if fundamental rights are affected - HC, therefore, rightly disposed of petition on merits - (B) Whether reassessment orders made pursuant to notices issued u/s. 34 of Act were void on ground that they did not comply with provisions of ss. 24B(1) and (2) of Act? - Held, in context of this question neither affidavits filed by parties before HC nor material placed before it disclose crucial facts necessary for giving a definite answer to said question - Except affidavit filed by ITO, which is vague to extreme, and that filed by respondent, which is equally unhelpful, no material has been placed - Order set aside - Matter remanded to HC for fresh disposal - Appeal disposed of.
