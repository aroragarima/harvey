F. Hoffmann-La Roche and Company Limited v Geoffrey Manners and Company Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

08 September 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 101; (1969) 2 SCC 716; AIR 1970 SC 2062; 1971 (73) BomLR 119; 22 PTC 88; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 213

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Intellectual Property - Trade And Merchandise Marks Act, 1958, ss. 2(1)(d), 9(1), 11, 12, 12(1), 12(3), 56(1), 56(2) and 136(3) - Drug Rules, 1945, r. 61(2) - (A) Whether two trademarks in question are likely to cause confusion or deception? - True test is whether totality of proposed trade mark is such that it is likely to cause deception or confusion or mistake in minds of persons accustomed to existing trade mark - It is also important that marks must be compared as wholes - Held, there is no real tangible danger of confusion if respondent's trade mark was allowed to continue as there is no reasonable probability of confusion between words either from visual or phonetic point of view - (B) Whether respondent trademark 'DROPOVIT' was an invented word or a descriptive Word? - To be an invented word within meaning of Act a word must not only be newly coined but must be such as not to convey any obvious meaning to ordinary person - Held, word 'DROPOVIT' is coined out of words commonly used by and known to ordinary persons knowing English, but resulting combination produces a new word, a newly coined word - Word 'DROPOVIT' is an invented word - Appeal dismissed.
