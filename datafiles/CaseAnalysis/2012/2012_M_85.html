Devinder Singh Narula v Meenakshi Nangia

Supreme Court of India

22 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 237; (2012) 8 SCC 580; AIR 2012 SC 2890; 2013 (1) ALD(SC) 145; 2012(5) ALL MR 895; 2012 (94) ALR 688; 2012 (5) ALT 33; 2012 (5) AWC 5209; 2012 (6) Bom.C.R. 113; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 398; 2012 (5) CTC 251; 2013 (1) G.L.R. 836; 2012 (6) MahLJ 769; 2012 (4) MPLJ 553; 2012 (2) OLR 906; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 372

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, ss. 12 and 13-B - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 142 - Divorce by mutual consent - Cooling period of 6 months - Appellant filed appeal against order of the Trial court whereby Trial court while entertaining a joint petition filed by the parties u/s. 13-B of the Act posted the matter on 15-10-2012 for the purpose of second motion - Appellant submitted that since more than 18 months had elapsed since the original petition u/s. 13 of the Act, have been filed, the said period could be counted towards the cooling period of 6 months stipulated u/s. 13-B of the Act - Further submitted that except for the formality of not having made an application u/s. 13-B of the Act, the other criteria had been duly fulfilled and having regard to the language of s. 13-B of Act, a decree of dissolution of the marriage by way of mutual divorce should not be denied to the parties, since 4 months out of waiting period of 6 months had already been completed - Appellant also submitted that SC could invoke its powers u/art. 142 of the Constitution in the best interest of the parties - Whether decree of divorce could be granted to the parties after waiving cooling period of 6 months stipulated u/s. 13-B of the Act - Held, although the marriage between the parties was solemnized on 26-3-2011, within 3 months of the marriage the parties filed a petition u/s. 12 of the Act, for a decree of nullity of the marriage - Thereafter, they have not been able to live together and lived separately for more than 1 year - In effect, there appears to be no marital ties between the parties at all - It was only the provisions of s. 13-B(2) of the Act which was keeping the formal ties of marriage between the parties subsisting in name only - It was only on account of the statutory cooling period of 6 months that the parties have to wait for a decree of dissolution of marriage to be passed - The marriage was subsisting by a tenuous thread on account of the statutory cooling off period, out of which 4 months have already expired - SC invoked and exercise the powers vested u/art. 142 of the Constitution and grant a decree of mutual divorce to the parties - Appeal allowed.
