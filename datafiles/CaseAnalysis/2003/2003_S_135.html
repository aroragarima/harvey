State of Karnataka v Shariff

Supreme Court of India

27 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 51; (2003) 2 SCC 473; (2003) SCC (Cr) 561; AIR 2003 SC 1074; 2003 (1) CCR 194; 2003 (2) Crimes 144; 2003 (4) CRJ 654; 2003 CRLJ 1254; 2003 (1) DMC 450; 2003 (1) JCC 445; JT 2003 (2) SC 15; 2003 (1) KHCACJ 547; 2003 (1) RCR(Criminal) 667; 2003(1) SCALE 469; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 572; 2003 (1) SLT 522; 2003 (2) Supreme 16; 2003 (1) UC 679

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: IPC - S. 302 - conviction under - on appeal the High Court set aside the judgment discarding the testimony of the witnesses and also several dying declarations of the deceased - Held, the testimony of PW3 is fairly reliable on the factum of the incident and the same cannot be discarded only on account of a stray sentence in his cross-examination - dying declaration - Further held, in view of the clear statement of the Doctor that the victim was in a position to make a statement, the High Court erred in discarding the dying declaration merely on the basis of the deceased's injury report and post-mortem examination report. - The view taken by the High Court is wholly perverse and also contrary to settled principles of law and therefore cannot be sustained.
