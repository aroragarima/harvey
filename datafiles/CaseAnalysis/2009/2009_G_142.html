Gurmukh Singh v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

25 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1056; (2009) 15 SCC 635; 2010 CRLJ 450; 2009 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2893; 2009 (4) MLJ(Crl) 993; 2009(11) SCALE 688; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 548

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.302 - Murder - Conviction - Appellant made a lathi blow to deceased and thereby deceased died, alleged - Appellant was convicted u/s.302 by Trial Court - Conviction upheld by HC - Legality - Contention of appellant that appellant should not have been convicted u/s. 302 but u/s. 304 Part II - Held, no pre-arranged plan or common intention - Occurrence had taken place at the spur of moment - A single lathi blow on the head of deceased was inflicted by appellant - Other acquitted co-accused had not indulged any overt act - No intention or pre-meditation in the mind of appellant to cause death of deceased - Therefore, conviction u/s.302 converted to one u/s. 304 Part II - Appeal partly allowed.
