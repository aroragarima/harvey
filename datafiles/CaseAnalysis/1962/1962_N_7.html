Â Nawab Zain Yar Jung and Others v Director of Endowments and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 202; AIR 1963 SC 985; 1964 (1) MLJ(SC) 84; 1964 (1) SCJ 285; [1963] 1 S.C.R. 469

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Wakf Act, 1954, ss. 3(1) and 28 - Hyderabad Endowment Regulation, 1348-F (1939) - Trust created by Nizam of Hyderabad - Whether trust a wakf for purpose of appklication of Act? - Purpose and object of wakf - Meaning of trust under Mohammadan law - Held, Trust held not a wakf and did not fall within provisions of Act - Under Wakf Act, purpose for which a wakf can be created must be one which is recognised by Muslim law as pious, religious, or charitable, and objects of public utility which may constitute beneficiaries under wakf must be objects for benefit of Muslim community - Wakfs with which Act deals are trusts which are treated as wakfs under definition of s. 3(1) and as such, a trust which does not satisfy tests prescribed by said definition would be outside Act - Differences between Muslim law and religious endowment - Trusts recognised by Hindu Law on one hand and public charitable trusts as contemplated by English Law - Mohammadan law owes its origin to a rule laid down by Prophet of Islam and means the tying up of property in ownership of God Almighty and devotion of profits for benefit of human beings upon creation, right of wakf is extinguished and ownership is transferred to Almighty - Manager of wakf is mutawalli, governor, superintendent, or curator - He has no right in property belonging to wakf - Property is not vested in him and he is not a trustee in legal sense - Different from trust as is known to English law - On construction that trust created by Nizam of Hyderabad by a trust deed executed by him on 14-6-1954, must be held to have created a trust for public charitable purposes, some of which were outside limits of wakf - Trust created as not a wakf but a secular comprehensive public charitable trust - Act could not apply to trust and its registration under s. 28 was invalid and inoperative - Appeal dismissed.
