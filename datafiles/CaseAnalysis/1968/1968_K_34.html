Shakuntala Devi Jain v Kuntal Kumari and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 September 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, S.M. Sikri, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 480; AIR 1969 SC 575; 1969 CurLJ 69; 1974 (2) ILR(Del) 45; 1969 (1) SCJ 912; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 1006

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 2(2), 47, 96 and 144, O. 41, r. 1 - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Respondent filed an application for execution of a final decree in a partition suit - Appellant filed objections s. 47 of CPC, 1908 but Subordinate Judge dismissed objections and appellant filed an appeal against this order in High Court - Along with appeal she filed a plain copy of order and an application praying that appeal be entertained without a certified copy of order - Appellant stated that she wanted urgent interim relief and would be seriously prejudiced if she waited for a certified copy - A Bench of HC admitted appeal, granted an interim stay - Respondents raised an objection that appeal was incompetent as a certified copy of order under appeal had not been filed - Appellant filed an application for condonation of delay in filing copy u/s. 5 of Act - HC held, that as appeal was not accompanied by a certified copy of order, appeal was incompetent, and that there was no sufficient ground for condoning delay in filing copy and dismissed appeal and application - (A) Whether appeal from order disposing objections u/s. 47 incompetent in view of fact that appeal was not accompanied by certified copy of order appealed from? - (B) Whether delay in filing appeal should be condoned u/s. 5 of Act? - Held, s. 2(2) of CPC, 1908 defines 'decree' as 'the formal expression of an adjudication which so far as regards court expressing it, conclusively determines rights of parties with regard to all or any of matters in controversy in suit and may be either preliminary or final - It shall be deemed to include rejection of a plaint and determination of any question with s. 47 or 144 - It is because determination of any question within s. 47 is a decree that appellant could file an appeal from order u/s. 96 of Code - O. 41, r. 1 of Code provides that every appeal shall be preferred in form of a memorandum signed by appellant or his pleader and memorandum shall be accompanied by a copy of decree appealed from and (unless appellate Court dispenses therewith) of judgment on which it is founded' - Under O. 41, r. 1 Appellate Court can dispense with filing of copy of judgment but it has no power to dispense with filing of copy decree thus, memorandum of appeal should have been accompanied by a certified copy of order and in absence of requisite copy appeal was defective and incompetent - Next question is whether delay in filing certified copy or, to put it differently, delay in re-filing appeal with certified copy should be condoned - Appellant cannot be held responsible for laches of copying department - Appellant could have filed another copy had it been supplied to her by copying department - It is acceptable that the statement that she was under bona fide impression that certified copy was not ready, and, that is why it was not supplied to her by copying department - In result, application filed by appellant u/s. 5 of Act is allowed - Appeal allowed.
