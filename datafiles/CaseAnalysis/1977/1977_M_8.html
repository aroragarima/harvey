Madan Lal Lohia v Assistant Controller & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 April 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 117; (1977) 3 SCC 189; AIR 1977 SC 1871; 1977 (6) CTR 232; [1977] 108 ITR 627; [1977] 3 S.C.R. 489

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Excise - Indian Income-Tax Act, 1922, s. 46(5A) - Estate Duty Act, 1953, s. 73(5) - Assessment - Tax deductions - Sustainability - Appeal by special leave raised a short question of law as to the scope of granishee proceeding u/s. 73(5) of the 1953 Act r/w. s. 46(5A) of 1922 Act - (A) Whether the notice dated 9th January, 1962 issued by the Assistant Controller to the appellant was valid notice under which the appellant was bound to pay the amount of rent in respect of the leased premises to the Assistant Controller - Held, notice started with the recital of the fact that sum of Rs. 1,40,090.20 was due from respondent Nos. 3 and 4 on account of estate duty 'as accountable persons to the estate of deceased' - That recital stated very clearly that the amount of Rs. 1,40,090.20 was due on account of estate duty payable on the death of the deceased - Then it went on to require the appellant to pay to the Assistant Controller the amount which was due or may become due from him to the estate of the deceased or which was held or may subsequently be held by him for or on account of the estate of the deceased upto the amount of Rs. 1,40,090.20 - Amount which the appellant was called upon to pay to the Assistant Controller was the amount due or to become due to the estate of the deceased - Purpose for which such amount was required to be paid was to meet 'the amount due by the accountable person in respect of the arrears of estate duty' and the appellant was intimated that any payment made by him in compliance with the notice would in law be 'deemed to have been made under the authority of the accountable person' and the receipt of the Assistant Controller would constitute good and sufficient discharge of the liability of the appellant 'to the accountable person' - Argument of the appellant was that the words 'accountable person' in the context meant respondent Nos. 3 and 4 and since the amount of rent was payable by him to the lessors and not to respondent Nos. 3 and 4, the notice was inoperative and did not obligate him to pay the amount of rent to the Assistant Controller - That argument might, at first blush, appeared little attractive, but close look at the scheme and relevant provisions of 1953 Act would be sufficient to repel it - (B) Even if the notice dt. 9-1-1962 was valid notice and it obligated the appellant to Day the amount of rent to the Assistant Controller, whether any penalty could be levied on the appellant for contravention of the terms of the notice - Held, penalty u/s. 46(1) could obviously be imposed on an assessee only when the assessee was in default in making payment of income tax and u/s. 45 of 1922 Act, the assessee would be deemed to be in default when he fails to pay the amount of income tax specified as payable in a notice of demand served, inter alia, u/s. 29 of 1922 Act within the time mentioned in the notice of demand or if no time was so mentioned, then on or before the first day of the second month following the date of service of the notice of demand - Thus, two conditions should be fulfilled before penalty could be imposed u/s. 46(1) of 1922 Act, one was that the person on whom penalty was sought to be imposed should be an assessee and the other was that the assessee shouldbe in default within the meaning of s. 45 of 1922 Act - Where garnishee was required by notice issued u/s. 46(5A) to pay to the Income-tax Officer so much of the money due or which might become due from the garnishee to the assessee or held or which might subsequently be held by the garnishee for or on account of the assessee, as was sufficient to pay the amount due by the tax payer in respect of the arrears of income tax, he did not become an assessee as defined in s. 2(2) of 1922 Act - S.2(2) of 1922 Act defined an assessee to mean a person by whom income tax or any other sum of money was payable under the Act and the amount which the garnishee was required to pay to Income Tax Officer in virtue of a notice u/s. 46(5A) of 1922 Act was not 'income tax or any other sum of money-payable under this Act' - Garnishee merely paid the amount which was due from him to the assessee and such payment was in discharge of the debt owned by him to the assesse - It was not a payment the liability for which is created under any provision of the Act - Garnishee was thus not an assessee within the meaning of the definition of that term in s. 2(2) of 1922 Act nor was there any provision in the Act which by a legal fiction made him an assesse - 1922 Act also did not contain any provision that the garnishee who failed to comply with the notice issued u/s. 46(5A) should be deemed to be an assessee in default - It was interesting to compare the provisions of 1922 Act with the corresponding provisions of 1961 Act - Cl. (x)(3) of s. 226 of 1961 Act provided in clear and explicit terms that if the garnishee, to whom a notice under sub-s. (3) was sent, failed to make payment in pursuance thereof to the Income Tax Officer, he should be deemed to be an assessee in default in respect of the amount specified in the notice - But no such provision was to be found in 1922 Act - It was, therefore, obvious that no penalty could be imposed on a garnishee u/s. 46(1) of 1922 Act, even if he failed to comply with the notice issued to him u/s. 46(5A) of 1922 Act - Scheme of collection and recovery of estate duty under the 1953 Act is substantially the same as that under the income-tax law - S.73(1) and s.73(2) of the 1953 Act corresponded to s.45(1)of 1922 Act - These two sub-sections provided that where any estate duty, penalty or interest was due in consequence of any order passed under 1953 Act, the Controller should serve upon the person accountable or other person liable to pay such estate duty, penalty on interest, a notice of demand in the prescribed form specifying the sum and the time within which it should be payable and any amount specified as payable in the notice of demand should be paid within the time, at the place and to the person mentioned in the notice, or if no time was so mentioned, then on or before the first day of the second month following the date of service of the notice and any person accountable failing so to pay should be deemed to be in default - 1953 Act also, therefore, contemplated issue of a notice of demand to the accountable person after an order of assessment is made under the Act and it was only when the accountable person failed to pay in accordance with the requisition contained in the notice of demand that he was to be deemed to be in default - S.73(5) of 1953 Act then incorporated the provisions of s. 46(1), (1A) (2), (3), (4), (5), (5A), (6) and (7) of 1922 Act and made them applicable for the purpose of collection and recovery of estate duty - It should follow a fortiori that penalty could be imposed on a garnishee u/s. 73(5) of 1953 Act r/w. s.46(1) of 1922 Act only if the garnishee could be said to be an accountable person in default - But, for like reason as those discussed while dealing with the provisions of 1922 Act, the garnishee could not be regarded as an accountable person, since section 2(12A) of 1922 Act defined 'accountable person to mean the person accountable for estate duty within the meaning of the Act and the garnishee did not come within the category of persons specified in ss. 53 and 54 of 1953 Act as persons accountable for estate duty - There was also no provision in 1953 Act deeming a defaulting garnishee as an accountable person in default by legal fiction - It was therefore, difficult to see how an order imposing penalty could be passed against the appellant even if the notice dt. 9-1-1962 was a valid notice under which the appellant was bound to pay the amount of rent in respect of the leased premises to the Assistant Controller and be failed to do so - Order dt. 25-3-1964 imposing penalty of Rs. 3000/- on the appellant should therefore be held to be outside the Dower of the Assistant Controller under 1953 Act and it should be quashed and set aside - Appeal allowed.
