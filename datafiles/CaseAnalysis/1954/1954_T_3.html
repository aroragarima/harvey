Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay South, Bombay v Messrs. Ogale Glass Works Limited, Ogale Wadi

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 102; AIR 1954 SC 429; [1954] 24 Comp Cas 520; [1954] 25 ITR 529; 1954 (2) MLJ(SC) 67; 1954 SCJ 522; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 185

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Income Tax & Direct taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922 - Contract Act, 1872, ss. 50,Illustration(d) and 182 - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 82 - Indian Post Office Act, 1898, s. 18 - Cheque sent by post on request of creditor - Whether receipt in India comes with in meaning of s. 4(1)(a) of 1922 Act - In light of conditional discharge u/s. 82 of 1881 Act and new plea as to ss. 66 and 66 A of 1922 Act - Held, money received by assessee in British India - Posting in Delhi, in law, amounted to payment in Delhi - Appeal allowed.
