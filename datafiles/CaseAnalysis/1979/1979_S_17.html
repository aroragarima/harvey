Biswabani Private Limited v Santosh Kumar Dutta and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 64; (1980) 1 SCC 185; AIR 1980 SC 226; 1980 (1) RCJ 156; 1980 (1) RCR(Rent) 263; 1979 (2) RentLR 470; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 650

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Doctrine Of Part Performance, Bengal Premises Rent Control (Temporary Provision) Act 1950

Summary: Rent Control - Bengal Premises Rent Control (Temporary Provision) Act, 1950 - Registration Act, 1908, s. 17 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 53A and 107 - Status of the tenant after the expiry of the lease - Whether on expiry of agreed period of lease there was a covenant for not getting any renewal of lease, tenant would be a trespasser? - Held, on expiry of contractual tenancy tenant continues as a statutory tenant except where he surrenders possession or is evicted under enabling provisions of relevant Rent Restriction Act - Therefore, appellant was a tenant and continued to be a tenant and was entitled to protect its possession by appropriate proceeding unless evicted in due course of law - Appeal allowed.
