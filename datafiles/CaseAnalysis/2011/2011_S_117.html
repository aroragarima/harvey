Executive Engineer and another v Sri Seetaram Rice Mill

Supreme Court of India

20 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, S.H. Kapadia, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 822; (2012) 2 SCC 108; 2012 (1) AWC 861; 2012 (1) ILR(Cut) 554; JT 2011 (12) SC 386; 2012 (3) MahLJ 536; 2012 (2) MPLJ 628; 2012 (3) RCR(Civil) 633; 2011(12) SCALE 243; [2011] 15 S.C.R. 211

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Electricity Reform Act, 1998, Orissa Electricity Regulatory Commission Distribution (Conditions of Supply) Code, 2004, Tamil Nadu Rent Act, Orissa Electricity Reforms Act, 1995, Orissa Electricity Regulatory Commission Distribution (Condition of Supply) Regulations, 2004, (General Conditions of Supply) Regulations, 1995

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Over drawl use of electricity - Unauthorized use of electricity - Challenged - Respondent partnership firm was small scale industrial unit engaged in production of rice - For carrying business, it had obtained electric supply under agreement was executed with appellant for supply of power - Appellant executive engineer inspected business premises of respondents unit and dump was conducted - Appellant issued dump report, it was stated that there was unauthorized use of electricity and Maximum Demand (MD) was consumed up to 142 KVA on part of respondent - Appellant passed provisional assessment order dt. 25-7-2009, assessment was made u/s. 126(1) of the 2003 Act for unauthorized use of electricity - Respondent challenged the provisional assessment order and intimation of unauthorized use before HC - HC allowed case of respondent and held that words 'unauthorized use of electricity' and as provided in s. 126 of the 2003 Act were exhaustive - Over drawl of MD should not fall under scope of 'unauthorized use of electricity' as defined under the 2003 Act, - However, appellants had no jurisdiction to issue intimation and pass assessment order in terms of s. 126 of the 2003 Act - Aggrieved, appellant preferred instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, In instant case, no statutory appeal lay against a provisional order of assessment and respondents were required to file objections as contemplated u/s. 126 (3) of the 2003 Act - It was only when final order of assessment was passed that respondents should prefer a statutory appeal which admittedly was not done in instant case - HC did not fall in error of jurisdiction in entertaining writ petition but certainly failed to finally exercise jurisdiction within prescribed limitations of law for exercise of such jurisdiction - High Court should answered the question of law relating to lack of jurisdiction and exercise of jurisdiction in futility without travelling into and determining validity of demand which squarely fell within domain of specialized authority - HC should remanded case to assessing officer with a direction to respondent to file its objections including non-applicability of the tariff before assessing authority and for determination in accordance with law - However, the language of s. 127 of the Act, only a final order of assessment passed u/s. 126(3) was an order appealable u/s. 127 and notice-cum-provisional assessment made u/s. 126(2) was not appealable - HC did not committed any error of jurisdiction in entertaining writ petition against order raising jurisdictional challenge to notice/provisional assessment order dt. 25-7-2009 - However, HC transgressed its jurisdictional limitations while travelling into exclusive domain of Assessing Officer relating to passing of an order of assessment - Judgment of HC was set aside and matter was remanded to Assessing Officer to pass a final order of assessment - Appeal allowed.
