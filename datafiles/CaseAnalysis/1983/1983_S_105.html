Naib Singh v State Of Punjab And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 436; (1983) 2 SCC 454; (1983) SCC (Cr) 536; AIR 1983 SC 855; 1983 CRLJ 1345; 1983 CrLR(SC) 348; 1983(1) SCALE 425; [1983] 2 S.C.R. 770

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Human Rights

Summary: Criminal - Practice and Procedure - Prisoners Act, 1900 s.32 - IPC, 1860, ss.53 A and 55 - CrPC, s.433 - (A) Whether a sentence of transportation either for a term or for life could be and a sentence of life imprisonment can be made executable in local jails? - Held, yes - A sentence of transportation either for a term or for life could be and a sentence of life imprisonment can be made executable in local jails by constituting such jails as 'places' within meaning of s.32 under orders of State Govts - (B) Continued detention - Convicted for death punishment but later commuted to life imprisonment - Contended that he had served for more than prescribed sentence - Whether entitled to be released? - Further held, sentence of imprisonment for life means rigorous imprisonment for life - In absence of order under s.55 IPC or s.433 (b) of CrPC, convict cannot be released forthwith even after expiry 14 years - Petition dismissed.
