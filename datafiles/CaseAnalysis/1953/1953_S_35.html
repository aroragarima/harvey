Habeeb Mohammad v State of Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

05 October 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 118; AIR 1954 SC 51; 1954 CRLJ 338; [1954] S.C.R. 475

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Wrongful Confinement

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Hyderabad Indian Penal Code, ss. 124, 248, 272 and 82 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 256 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 136 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Whether procedure for trial laid down in Regulation X of 1359-F became void after 26th January 1950 by reason of its conflict with equal protection clause embodied in art. 14 of Constitution? - Had there been a grievous disregard of proper forms of legal process and violation of principles of criminal jurisprudence in such a fashion as amounted to a denial of justice and whether injustice of a serious and substantial character occurred - A conviction arrived at without affording opportunity to defence to lead whatever relevant evidence it wanted to produce causes serious prejudice and cannot be sustained - If there are mere mistakes on part of court of a technical character which does not occasioned any failure of justice or if question was purely one of this court taking a different view of evidence given in case, there can be no interference by court under provisions of art. 136 of Constitution - Held, no fair and proper trial therefore conviction set aside - Appeal allowed.
