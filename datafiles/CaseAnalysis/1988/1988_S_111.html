Vikram Deo Singh Tomar v State Of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

02 August 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, L.M. Sharma, N.D. Ojha

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 775; (1988) Supp SCC 734; (1989) SCC (Cr) 66; AIR 1988 SC 1782; 1988 BBCJ 109; 1989 CRLR 750; JT 1988 (3) SC 186; 1988(2) SCALE 325; [1988] Supp1 S.C.R. 755; 1988 (2) UJ 404

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Women and Children - Constitution, arts. 21 and 39 - Right to life - Care homes maintained by the State - Must provide at least the minimum conditions ensuring human dignity of the inmates - It petition arises upon a letter received from the Yuva Adhivakta Kalyan Samiti, alleging that the female inmates of the 'Care Home'are compelled to live in inhuman conditions in an old dilapidated building, that they are ill-treated, provided food which is both insufficient and of poor quality - Supreme Court directed State of Bihar to take immediate steps for welfare of inmates of care home, Patna - Petition disposed of.
