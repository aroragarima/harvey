Silvey and others v Arun Varghese and another

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2674; (2008) 11 SCC 45; AIR 2008 SC 1568; 2008 (2) AWC 1544; 2008 (2) CHN 92; 2008 (3) MLJ 951; 2008 (3) MLJ(SC) 951; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 591; 2008(3) SCALE 205; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 442

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Kerala Private Forest (Vesting and Assignment) Act

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 16C - Specific performance - Agreement to sell immovable properties - Plaintiffs filed a suit for specific performance against defendants submitting that they entered into an agreement for sale of impugned property by defendants and defendants received an advance of Rs. 50,000/- for the same - Defendants were to furnish the plaintiffs with all documents in their possession - Plaintiffs submitted that the defendants were never ready with the requisite documents for executing the sale deed in favour of the plaintiffs and repeated demands by the plaintiffs for execution of the document did not meet with the proper response - Defendants filed a written statement contending that the plaintiffs were in default as plaintiffs never demanded performance of the agreement, therefore, they were under the impression that the plaintiffs have abandoned the agreement - Trial Court dismissed the suit for specific performance - However, the trial court granted a decree for recovery of the advance of Rs. 50,000/- paid by the plaintiffs to the defendants at the time of entering into the agreement along with interest at the rate of 6% per annum thereon from the date of suit till date of realization - On appeal HC reversed the judgment and decree of the trial court and held that the suit was to be decreed in favour of the plaintiffs for specific performance - Hence, the present appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, the conduct of the defendants cannot be ignored while weighing the question of exercise of discretion for decreeing or denying a decree for specific performance - HC has, after analyzing the factual position, come to the conclusion that the defendants were really not ready to perform their obligation in terms of the contract and had taken a false plea in the written statement - Impugned order of the HC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
