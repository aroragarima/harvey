Â Amalgamated Coalfields Limited and Another v Janapada Sabha, Chhindwara

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 490; AIR 1964 SC 1013; 1964 JabLJ 404; 1965 MahLJ 6; 1964 MPLJ 934; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 172

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ground Of Res Judicata

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governmets - Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Central Province Local Self Govt. Act, 1920 - Res-judicata - Whether writ petition challenging validity of levy of coal tax was barred by res-judicata? - Constructive res-judicata was creature of statute and its application could not be extended to those questioning tax liability for different years - General principle of res judicata applies to writ petitions filed u/art. 32 or 226 - Application of doctrine of res judicata to petitions filed u/art. 32 does not in any way impair or affect content of fundamental rights guaranteed to citizens of India - Held, appropriate direction or order is issued restraining respondent from recovering tax at a rate higher than 3 pies per ton and also restraining respondent from recovering any additional tax in respect of years for which tax has already been assessed against appellants - Appeals allowed.
