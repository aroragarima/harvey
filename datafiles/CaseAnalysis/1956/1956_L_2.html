Lakshmi Devi Sugar Mills Limited v Pt. Ram Sarup

Supreme Court of India

24 October 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, S.K. Das, P. Govinda Menon

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 100; AIR 1957 SC 82; 1957 (1) LLJ 17; 1957 SCJ 46; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 916

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 33(a), 33(b) and 26 - Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950, s. 22 - (A) Whether permission of Conciliation Officer, Board, or tribunal is required before declaring a lockout? - Held, lockout does not come within scope of s. 33(a) or s. 33(b) - Prior permission not required - (B) Whether tribunal under s. 22 of Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950 can adjudicate upon any industrial dispute between employer and workman? - Held, only jurisdiction which tribunal has is to decide whether ban which is imposed on employer on matter of altering conditions of employment to prejudice workman or his discharge or punishment during pendency of proceedings should be lifted - Appeal allowed.
