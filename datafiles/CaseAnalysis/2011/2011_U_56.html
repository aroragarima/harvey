Union Of India And Ors. v Nitdip Textile Processors Private Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

03 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 829; (2012) 1 SCC 226; 2011 (273) E.L.T. 321; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 26; [2011] 203 TAXMAN 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Finance Act, 1998, Constitutionality, Finance Act, 1958, Finance (No. 2) Bill, 1998, Travancore Cochin Agricultural Income Tax Act

Summary: Indirect Tax - Excise - Constitution - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 11A, 11AB, 11AC, 14 - Finance Act, 1998, ss. 87(m)(ii), 95 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Assessment - Cut off date - Tax deduction - Validity - Appellants/Revenue Officers found that respondents had cleared Man Made Fabric admeasuring 38,726 l.m. of Rs. 5,38,449/- without payment of excise duty of '84,290/- Show Cause Notice was issued to respondents demanding a duty of '84,290/- u/ss. 11A, 11AC, 11AB of the 1944 Act - Meantime, Scheme was introduced by Finance Minister through 1998 Budget, wherein cut-off date prescribed by Scheme u/s. 87 (m) (ii) (a) and (b) of the 1998 Act for availing benefits under Scheme excluded respondents from its ambit - Aggrieved, respondents filed application before HC, seeking a writ to strike down words 'on or before 31-3-1998' occurring in s. 87 (m) (ii) of the 1998 Act - HC struck down expression 'on or before 31-3-1998' in s. 87 (m) (ii) (b) of the 1998 Act as being unconstitutional - HC further directed competent authority to entertain and decide declarations made by assessees in terms of Scheme - Hence, instant appeals - Held, classification made by legislature appears to be reasonable for reason that legislature had grouped two categories of assessees namely, assessees whose dues were quantified but not paid and assessees who were issued with Demand and Show Cause Notice on or before a particular date, month and year - Legislature had not extended this benefit to those persons who did not fall under this category or group - Therefore, Scheme did not violate equal protection clause where there was an essential difference and a real basis for classification which was made - However, mere fact that line dividing classes was placed at one point rather than another would not impair validity of classification - Thus, taxation laws were no exception to application of principle of equality enshrined in art. 14 of Constitution - Hence, impugned order and judgment of HC was set aside - Appeals allowed.
