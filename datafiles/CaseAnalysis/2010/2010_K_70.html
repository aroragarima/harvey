Bhagmal & Ors. v Kunwar Lal & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

27 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 557; (2010) 12 SCC 159; AIR 2010 SC 2991; 2010 (5) AWC 5405; 2010 (5) CTC 827; 2010 (4) JhrCR(SC) 63; JT 2010 (7) SC 597; 2011 (1) MLJ(SC) 369; 2011 (1) OLR 187; 2010 (160) PLR 267; 2010 (111) RD 666B; 2011 (1) RLW 459; 2010(7) SCALE 490; [2010] 8 S.C.R. 1104

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 9 r. 13 r/w. s. 151 - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Respondents filed a suit against the petitioner for declaration of title, possession and permanent injunction against the petitioner in respect of the house in dispute - The Court proceeded ex-parte and the decree came to be passed - When the execution proceeding started petitioner moved an application u/o. 9 r. 13 r/w. s. 151 of CPC for setting aside the ex-parte decree submitting that there was a compromise effected on 10.12.1983, which was an out-of-Court settlement, wherein it was agreed between the parties that the respondent No. 1 would withdraw the suit on account of the understanding having been arrived at between the parties - Petitioner further submitted that since the application had been moved within 30 days from the knowledge, a separate application for condonation of delay was not required - Said application was allowed by appellate court - On appeal, HC set aside the order of the appellate Court - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, HC was not justified in taking a hypertechnical view - Petitioner had clearly pleaded that they did not earlier come to the Court on account of the fact that they did not know about the order passed by the Court proceeding ex-parte and also the ex-parte decree which was passed - It was further clearly pleaded that they came to know about the decree when they were served with the execution notice - This was nothing, but a justification made by the petitioner for making the o. 9 r. 13 application at the time when it was actually made - This was also a valid explanation of the delay - The question of filing o. 9 r. 13 application was, rightly considered by the appellate Court on merits and the appellate Court was absolutely right in coming to the conclusion that petitioner was fully justified in filing the application u/o. 9 r. 13 at the time when they actually filed it and the delay in filing the application was also fully explained on account of the fact that they never knew about the decree and the orders starting the ex-parte proceedings against them - Petitioner was a party which bona fide believed the assurance given in the compromise panchnama that the respondent No. 1 would get his suit withdrawn or dismissed - Under such circumstances, the non-attendance of the petitioner, which was proved in the further proceedings, was quite justifiable - Impugned order of HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
