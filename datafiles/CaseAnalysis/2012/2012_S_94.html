Y. Nagaraj v Smt. Jalajakshi And Others

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 4; (2012) 2 SCC 161; 2012 (3) ALD(SC) 164; 2012 (91) ALR 233; 2012 (2) ALT 25; 2012 (2) AWC 1459; 2012 (1) CLT(SC) 29; JT 2012 (1) SC 146; 2012 (2) KarLJ 185; 2012 (3) MahLJ 497; 2012 (115) RD 631; 2012(1) SCALE 34

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Partition - Joint Family Property - Self Acquired - Settlement deed - Sustainability - Respondent No 1 filed suit for partition against appellant and other respondents - Trail Court partly decreed suit, stating that respondent No 1 miserably failed to prove her case with regard to some properties in suit schedule - Appellant pleaded that suit properties were not joint family properties - Respondent No 2 and 3 had admitted claim of respondent No 1 - During pendency of suit respondent No 2 had filed suit for declaration of title with regard to property claiming same through registered settlement deed - Trial Court dismissed suit filed by respondent No 2 -Appellant and respondent No 2 filed appeal in HC, HC dismissed appeal of appellant - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that HC committed grave error by granting substantive relied to respondent No 1 despite the fact that she did had not filed appeal or cross-objection and that decree in favor of respondent No 2 on basis of Settlement Deed ignoring that she had failed to prove in suit filed by her - Respondents contended that C rightly invoked principle underlying O. 41 r. 33 CPC for purpose of doing full justice - Whether HC was justified in considering Settlement Deed valid - Held, that HC did not even refer to detailed reasons recorded by Trial court for holding that respondent No.2 has failed to prove that suit property was self-acquired property of the executant because Sale Deed was not produced by her as she had claimed that property was purchased by her father - Whether HC was justified in sustaining order of Trial Court - Held, that it is not in dispute that respondent No.1 had not challenged findings recorded by trial Court on various issues she also did not file cross-objections in appeal preferred by appellant, it is possible to take view that even in absence of an appeal having been preferred by respondent No.1, HC could have exercised power under Order 41 Rule 33 CPC, hence order of HC cannot sustain - Appeals allowed.
