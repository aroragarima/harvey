Ajay Bansal v Anup Mehta & Ors

Supreme Court of India

16 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 29; (2007) 2 SCC 275; AIR 2007 SC 909; 2007 (67) ALR 158; 2007 (2) AWC 1672; 2007 (3) CalLT(SC) 18; 2007 (3) CTC 604; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 275; JT 2007 (2) SC 239; 2007 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 476; 2007 (3) MahLJ 72; 2007 (5) MLJ(SC) 782; 2007 (2) MPLJ 302; 2007 (2) MPLJ 1302; 2007 (145) PLR 649; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 769; 2007(2) SCALE 99; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 953; 2007 (2) UC 724

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Chartered High Courts

Summary: Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Suit for recovery of a sum of Rs. 2, 93, 987/- with interest on account of dishonoured cheques - Refused to grant leave to defend - Decreed - Application under art. 227 allowed - Appeal against - Held, an application under art. 227 of the Constitution Of India, 1950 would not be maintainable where an appeal lies - An appeal lay from the decree under s. 96 - When an appeal could be filed, ordinarily, an application under art. 227 would not be entertained - Directed the writ petition to be converted into a first appeal - Appeal allowed.
