Saradamani Kandappan and another v S. Rajalakshmi and another

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 400; (2011) 12 SCC 18; 2011 (5) ALD(SC) 100; 2011 (3) CLT(SC) 57; 2011 (4) CTC 640; JT 2011 (8) SC 129; 2011 (6) MLJ(SC) 149; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 130; 2013 (120) RD 564; 2011(6) SCALE 768; [2011] 8 S.C.R. 874

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss. 51 to 55 - Specific Relief Act, 1963 - Immovable property - Agreement of sale - Mode of payment on balance amount - Failure on payments - Cancel agreement - Succeed - Respondent nos. 1 to 4 entered into agreement of sale dt. 17-1-1981 with plaintiff/appellant for sale of properties, at price of Rs. 3,75,000/- and on date of agreement Rs. 1,00,000/- was paid as advance to respondents - Balance consideration to be paid in 3 installments that was Rs.1,00,000 on or before 28.2.1981, Rs.1,00,000 on or before 6.4.1981, and Rs.75,000 on or before 30-5-1981 and Cl. 5 of agreement makes it clear that if any of abovementioned dates of payment was subsequently declared as holiday, then next immediate working day should be the date of payment - This shows clear intention that payment should be made on stipulated dates and even a day's delay was not acceptable unless due date was declared to be holiday and Cl. 6 of agreement specifically stipulates that payments on due dates was essence of the contract and in case of failure on the part of purchaser vendors should cancel agreement - In pursuance of agreement appellant paid further advances of Rs. 1,00,000/- on dt. 28-2-1981 and of Rs. 25,000/- on dt. 2-4-1981 and balance of Rs. 75,000/- in regard to installment payable on dt. 6-4-1981 was not paid by appellant - Respondents issued notice to appellant, cancelling agreement dt. 17-1-1981 on ground of default in paying balance of sale consideration - Appellant filed suits against respondent nos. 1 to 4 for specific performance of agreement of sale dt. 17-1-1981 and direction to respondent nos. 1 to 3 to execute sale deed after receiving balance and for return of Rs. 1,25,000/- paid as commission along with interest before Single Judge - Single Judge by his common judgment dt. 29-11-1991, dismissed suits of appellant by considering oral and documentary evidence - Aggrieved by judgment, appellant filed appeals before DB - DB dismissed appeals by common judgment, however DB directed respondents to repay Rs. 3,50,000/- with interest at 9% per annum for period during which appellant was not acting as caretaker till complete payment was made - Hence, instant appeals were filed by respondents - Plaintiff contended that agreement of sale laid down the order in which reciprocal promises were to be performed, that it first required respondents 1 to 3 as vendors, to furnish original title deeds and nil encumbrance certificate to satisfy plaintiff about their title, that plaintiff had to pay balance of sale price only after respondent discharged their said obligation and plaintiff was entitled to withhold balance sale price till respondents discharged their liabilities - Whether judgment of HC was succeed - Held, affirmed decision of Single Judge and DB that plaintiff was not entitled to relief of specific performance, it could not be said that plaintiff had filed false, frivolous and mischievous suits and in view of terms of agreement and in terms of its offer, plaintiff was entitled to recover amounts paid by her and sum of Rs.2,25,000/- was paid under agreement of sale to defendants 1 to 3 - Hence, DB was justified in granting decree in favour of plaintiff for Rs.3,50,000/- with interest -Respondents 1 to 3 should refund sum of Rs. 3,50,000/- to plaintiff as directed by HC, with interest at 12% per annum - Hence, time stipulated for payment of balance price by plaintiff was essence of contract and when same was not paid, defendants 1 to 3 were justified in cancelling sale agreement, but there was no provision in agreement for forfeiture of amounts already paid, even in event of breach by purchaser - On other hand it provides that if vendors did not satisfy purchaser in regard to their title, the amounts received would be refunded - Appeals partly allowed.
