Balvant N. Viswamitra And Ors v Yadav Sadashiv Mule (D) through Lrs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, R.C. Lahoti, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 610; (2004) 8 SCC 706; AIR 2004 SC 4377; 2004 (57) ALR 108; 2004 (3) AWC 2689; 2005 (2) Bom.C.R. 259; JT 2004 (6) SC 403; 2004 (4) RCR(Civil) 230; 2004 (2) RD 571; 2004(6) SCALE 636; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 519; 2004 (2) UC 1263

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Void decree vis-a-vis irregular or erroneous decrees - Erroneous or illegal decision, which is not void, whether can be objected in execution or collateral proceedings? - Held, No - Suit for possession of property rented out - Respondents not made party defendants in suit - HC while exercising supervisory jurisdiction under art 227 of Constitution, held decree sought to be executed as void ab initio - Whether decree passed by trial court can be said to be 'null' and 'void' - Held, all irregular or wrong decrees or orders are not necessarily null and void - An erroneous or illegal decision, which is not void, cannot be objected in execution or collateral proceedings - Respondents could not be said to be 'necessary party' to suit - Non-joinder of respondents, hence, would not make a decree passed by Court of Small Causes nullity or in-executable - HC was wrong in interfering with decree passed by Small Court - Appeal allowed.
