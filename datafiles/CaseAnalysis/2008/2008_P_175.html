A.K. Jain v Prem Kapoor

Supreme Court of India

28 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1174; (2008) 8 SCC 593; AIR 2008 SC 3194; 2008 (72) ALR 784; JT 2008 (8) SC 615; 2008 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2162; 2008 (152) PLR 210; 2008(10) SCALE 507; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 414

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Keywords: Punjab Ayurvedic and Unani Practitioners Act, 1963, Haryana Urban (Control of Rent & Eviction) Act, 1973, Punjab Medical Registration Act, 1916, Punjab Homoeopathic Practitioners Act, 1965

Summary: Haryana Urban (Control of Rent & Eviction) Act, 1973, s. 13 - Tenancy - Eviction sought on ground of personal necessity - Appeal by special leave - (A) Against decision of High Court allowing revision and setting aside eviction - On the ground that the appellant landlord had failed to make the necessary averments in the Eviction Petition as required by s. 13(3)(a)(i) of the Act - Contended that plea of personal necessity was false and unfounded as landlord lives in his official residence and his daughter stayed some other city in connection with her studies - Landlord retired within few days of dismissal of proceeding pending (for 6 years ) before Rent Controller - (B) Whether prejudice was caused to the respondent because the Appellant did not make any formal amendments in the pleadings or because the retirement order filed before the Authority was not accompanied with a formal petition under O. 41 r. 27 - (C) Applicability of s. 13(3)(a)(ii) of the Act - Growing needs of landlord's son - Grounds of eviction , if - On a perusal Supreme Court satisfied that the pleadings fully comply with the requirements of s.13(3)(a)(i) (b) and (c) of the Act - Held, no prejudice caused and contention is calculated only to frustrate the proceeding - Held, s.13(3) (a) (ii) will have application only in case the eviction is sought for the son's requirement independently and separately from the landlord; considering view in view in Joginder Pal Vs. Nawal Kishore Behal, 2002 INDLAW SC 337 - Appeal allowed
