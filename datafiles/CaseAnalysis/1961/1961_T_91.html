Â Bhagwati Saran and Another v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

20 January 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 52; AIR 1961 SC 928; 1961 CRLJ 24; 1961 (2) SCJ 217; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 563

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 364 - Essential Supplies (Temporary Powers) Act, 1946, ss. 7 and 8 - Iron and Steel Control of Production and Distribution Order, 1941 - Prosecution for contravention of notification fixing maximum prices at which certain categories of iron and steel could be sold - Appellants prosecuted for selling iron-bark more than control rate - Whether notification of Controller for contravention of which appellants were being prosecuted was ultra vires or not? - Base-price for commodity having been fixed, there are incidentals which by their very nature were incapable of definite quantification, since they were dependent on each individual case - Notification fixing prices together with conditions appended thereto are valid and enforceable - Report states that accused had effected sales above maximum prices specified in notification, requirements of s. 11 are satisfied - S. 11 does not require report to be or to contain either charge-sheet or evidence in support of charge, its function being merely to afford basis for enabling magistrate to take cognizance of case - Held, report on which prosecution was launched satisfied requirements of s. 11 of Act - Appeal dismissed.
