Vishwanath Prasad Jaiswal v Satya Narain Sharma

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

J. S. KHEHAR, Shiva Kirti Singh, Arun Mishra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 842; 2015 (110) ALR 195; 2015 (1) AWC 1; JT 2014 (14) SC 355; 2015 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 74; 2015 (1) RCR(Rent) 17; 2014(13) SCALE 740; 2015 (1) WLN(SC) 113

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Eviction, Structural Alteration, Unauthorised construction

Summary: Rent Control - Uttar Pradesh Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, s. 20(2)(c) - Unauthorised construction - Structural alteration - Eviction - Justifiability - Appellant (landlord) leased his property to respondent (tenant) - Appellant sought eviction of respondent u/s. 20(2)(c) of the Act, for alleged unauthorised construction/structural alteration in the leased property (shop) - HC by impugned order rejected the same - Hence, instant appeal - Whether shifting of the shutter affixed on the shop by respondent, had diminished the value of premises, and/or had diminished the utility of the building, and/or had disfigured it.

Held, there is no material on the record of this case, to establish any of the ingredients u/s. 20(2)(c) of the Act. Undoubtedly, the shop premises leased out by the appellant to the respondent originally measured 22ft.x11Â½ ft. Even if the dimensions of the shop have been increased to 30 ft.x11Â½ ft., it is not possible for Court to record a conclusion, that the value of the shop has been decreased, nor the utility of the shop has been reduced. On the contrary, by increasing the dimensions of the shop, it may legitimately be concluded, that its value and utility had been enhanced. The only remaining question is, whether by removing the shutter from its existing location, and by installing it at the opposite end of the verandah, the respondent has disfigured the premises. There is no material on the record of instant case, to arrive at such a finding. It is not possible for Court to accept, that the appellant, has been able to establish, the ingredients of the ground of eviction, envisaged in s. 20(2)(c) of the Act. The action of the respondent, in shifting the shutter, had resulted in the issuance of a show cause notice to the rival parties, by the Development Authority. Appellant was exonerated from the imposition of any penalty. The appellate authority, however, determined the issue against the respondent and a writ petition assailing the order passed in appellate proceedings, which is pending. Said proceeding does not create any right of eviction, rather the appellant having been exonerated, cannot claim any prejudice on the basis of the proceedings initiated by the Development Authority. Appeal dismissed.
