Dr. Rajendra Prakash Sharma v Gyan Chandra & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

27 March 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 134; (1980) 4 SCC 364; AIR 1980 SC 1206; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 207

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950, ss. 7, 28, 46, 2(c), 20 - Administration of Evacuee Property (Amendment) Act, 1954, s. 27 - suit for declaration - Evacuee property - Compensation - Validity - Defendant/respondents filed suit for ejectment and arrears of rent against plaintiff/appellant's father which was decreed - Plaintiff filed suit for declaration of his title to property in question and for an injunction restraining defendants from dispossessing him in execution of said decree - Trial Court dismissed suit holding that plaintiff did not acquire any title by virtue of sale held by Authorities under the Act - On appeal, Appellate Court dismissed appeal and affirmed findings of Trial Court - On further appeal, HC dismissed appeal and upheld concurrent findings of Courts below - Hence, instant appeal - Whether there was any perversity or illegality in judgment and decree of Courts below - Held, s. 46 of the 1950 Act would not bar jurisdiction of Civil Court where custodian had never declared property as evacuee property after taking proceedings u/s. 7 of the 1950 Act - Custodian had no jurisdiction under the Act to declare suit house as evacuee property - Thus, jurisdiction of Courts below to go into such question was thus not barred by anything in ss. 28 and 46 of the 1950 Act - If property was never evacuee property u/s. 2(c) of the 1950 Act, it did not legally form part of compensation pool and could not be disposed of u/s. 20 of the Act - S. 27 of the 1954 Act was not attracted because plaintiff was not questioning finality of order of sale alleged to have been made by managing officer - It was defendants who were resisting plaintiff's claim on ground that sale was a nullity - Hence, it could not be made under the 1954 Act that it was never declared evacuee property u/s. 7 of the 1950 Act and thus never formed part of compensation pool- Thus, language of s. 27 of the 1954 Act was not as wide as that of s. 46 of the 1950 Act - Appeal dismissed.
