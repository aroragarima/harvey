V. Narayanaswamy v C. P. Thirunavukkarasu

Supreme Court of India

19 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, A.S. Anand, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 591; (2000) 2 SCC 294; AIR 2000 SC 694; 2000 (1) CLT 273; JT 2000 (1) SC 194; 2000(1) SCALE 153; 2000 (2) SCJ 503; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 292; 2000 (1) SLT 475; 2000 (1) Supreme 290

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Election - Election Laws (Amendment) Act, 1975 - Representation of the People Act, 1951,ss. 39, 78, 81, 82, 83(1), 83, 83(1)(b), 83(1)(a), 86(5), 94-A, 100(1)(b), 100(1)(d), 100(2), 100(1), 101, 117, 123(1)(B)(b), 123(2) - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Elections for filling up of vacant Rajya Sabha seat were held and appellant and respondent were contesting candidates - Out of 27 votes polled appellant got 12 and respondent got 15 votes and as such respondent was declared elected - Aggrieved appellant filed election petition in HC challenging victory of respondent on grounds of corrupt practices - Respondent denied allegations by filing counter affidavit - Respondent also filed application underO. 6 r. 16 and O. 7 r. 11 CPC to dismiss election petition as devoid of material facts and not properly verified - HC framed issues and dismissed election petition at trial stage itself holding that there was no triable issue and pleadings were not properly verified - Hence instant appeal - Whether judgment of HC was justified - Held, Both verification and affidavit did not meet requirement of law - As per s. 83 of the 1951 Act it was mandatory that every election petition must contain conscise statement of material facts with full particulars of corrupt practice committed with date and place - Election petition on charges of corrupt practice was required by law to be supported by affidavit and petitioner was obliged to disclose his source of information in respect of commission of corrupt practice - Non-compliance of s. 83 leads to dismissal of petition if matter falls within scope of O.6 r.16 and O.7 r.11 CPC - Courts could dismiss petition if on averments found no triable issue - Election petition must contain full particulars of corrupt practices alleged - Where several paragraphs of election petition alleging corrupt practices remain unaffirmed under verification clause unsworn allegation could have no legal existence and Court could not take cognizance - Charge of corrupt practice being quasi-criminal in nature court should insist for strict compliance with provisions of law - Court could dismiss election petition in absence of material facts, material particulars, correct verification and affidavit - Constitutional Bench of SC dismissed T.M. Jacob vs. C. Poulose and others , 1999 Indlaw SC 1082 and Dr. Shipra vs. Shanti, 1996 Indlaw SC 630 for non-compliance of s. 81(3) Of the 1951 Act - SC in R.P. Moidutty vs. P.T. Kunju Mohammad & Anr, 1999 Indlaw SC 749 held that heavy onus lies on petitioner in election petition on grounds of corrupt practice - Constitutional Bench of SC in Bhikaji Keshao Joshi & Anr. vs. Brijlal Nandlal Biyani & Ors.,1955 Indlaw SC 56 held that primary responsibility for furnishing full particulars of alleged corrupt practices and to file petition in full compliance with provisions of law was on petitioner - Petitioner failed to furnish material particulars as to names of voters who were taken to hotel and to other places and who took them there or who paid their bills and who brought them back etc were lacking - Election petition not only lacked material facts but also lacked material particulars with defective verification and affidavit filed was also not in prescribed form - Petition lacked ingredients of corrupt practices as defined in ss. 123(1)(B) and 123(2) of the 1951 Act - Election petition did not disclose any cause of action or triable issue - HC was right in dismissing election petition - Appeal dismissed.
