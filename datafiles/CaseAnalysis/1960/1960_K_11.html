Â State of Orissa and Another v Karamshi Waghji Chauda

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 113; 1961 (12) STC 236

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Keywords: Orissa Sales Tax Rules, 1947

Summary: Sales Tax - Oriss Sales Tax Act, 1947 - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Orissa Sales Tax Rules, 1947 - Whether High Court erred in holding that notification D/-01-03-1949, by which relevant provisions of Oriss Sales Tax Act were extended to Kalahandi was invalid in law? - As to fees paid, HC followed its earlier decision in Chakobhai Ghelabhai 1955 Indlaw ORI 7 - But in State of Oriss v Chakobhai Ghelabhai and Co 1960 Indlaw SC 238, Supreme Court held that fees realised u/r. 59 of Oriss Sales Tax Rules, 1947, are valid and decision of HC to contrary is wrong - For post-Constitution quarters assessee was not liable to be taxed, because admittedly goods were delivered outside Orissa for consumption in delivery state - Held, assessment orders of quarters prior to and ending on 31-12-1949, are valid, but assessment orders for subsequent quarters are invalid and must be quashed - Order of HC for refund of fees paid by respondent is set aside - Appeal partly allowed.
