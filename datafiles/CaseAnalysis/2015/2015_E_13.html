Commissioner of Customs, Ahmedabad v Essar Steel Limited

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 261; (2015) 8 SCC 175; 2015 (319) E.L.T. 202; 2016 (1) G.L.R. 111; JT 2015 (4) SC 91; 2015(5) SCALE 1B; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 1062

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Importation, Customs Valuation (Determination Of Price Of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988

Summary: Indirect Tax - Customs - Customs Valuation (Determination of Price of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988, r. 9 - Technical Services - Assessment of Duty - Agreement was entered into between the respondent and company to associate Company as a technical consultant to render technical services in relation to implementation of a project to set up a plant in India for production of Hot Rolled Steel Coils - Competent authority charged addition in the value for assessment to customs duty of charges paid by the respondent to Company for supply of technical services - Respondent stated that none of the provisions of r. 9 of the 1988 Rules would apply as no payment was made for technical services as a condition of sale of imported goods - Aggrieved respondent filed appeal before appellate authority which was allowed - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, r. 9 of the 1988 Rules makes it clear that only those costs and services that are actually paid or payable for imported goods pre- import are to be added for the purpose of determining the value of the imported goods. Technical services agreement read as a whole is really only to successfully set up, commission and operate the plant after it has been imported into India. It is clear, therefore, that cl. 9(1)(e) of the 1988 Rules would not be attracted on the facts of this case and consequently the consideration for the technical services to be provided by Company cannot be added to the value of the equipment imported to set up the plant in India. If any amount is to be paid after the importation of the goods is complete, inter alia, by way of transfer of licence or technical know-how for the purpose of setting up of a plant from the machinery imported or running thereof, the same would not be computed for the said purpose. Any amount paid for post-importation service or activity, would not, therefore, come within the purview of determination of assessable value of the imported goods so as to enable the authorities to levy customs duty or otherwise. Appeal dismissed.
