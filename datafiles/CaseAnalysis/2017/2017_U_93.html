B. K. Pavitra and others v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

09 February 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 91; AIR 2017 SC 820; JT 2017 (2) SC 277; 2017 (2) KarLJ 369; 2017(2) SCALE 296

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Backward Classes, Equity, Accelerated Promotion, Limitation, Vacancies, Promotion, Representation, Seniority, Employment, Rule, Merit, Posts, Recruitment, Validity Of, Cadre, Inter Se Seniority, Policy, Government Servants, Amendment, Determination Of Seniority, Public Employment, Constitution (Eighty-Fifth Amendment) Act, 2001, Roster Points, Reservation, Constitution (Seventy-Seventh Amendment) Act, 1995, Constitution (Eighty-Second Amendment) Act,2000, Constitution (Eighty-first Amendment) Act, 2000, Uttar Pradesh Government Servants Seniority Rules, 1991, Writ Petition, Constitution of India, 1950, Karnataka Government Servants (Seniority) Rules, 1957, Karnataka Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and other Backward Classes (Reservation of seats in Educational Institutions and of appointments or posts in the services under the State) Act, 1994, Karnataka Determination of Seniority of the Government Servants Promoted on the basis of Reservation (to the posts in the civil services of the State) Act, 2002

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 16(4A), 309 - Karnataka Determination of Seniority of the Government Servants Promoted on the basis of Reservation (to the posts in the civil services of the State) Act, 2002, ss. 3, 4 - Promotion - Consequential seniority - Entitled to - Appellants filed writ petition before HC seeking that grant of consequential seniority to candidates promoted by way of reservation affected efficiency of administration and was violative of arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution and State Govt. had not provided any material or data to show inadequacy of reservation to the members of SC/ST nor has given any thought to the issue of overall administrative efficiency - HC dismissed writ petition holding that reservation in promotion was provided only upto the cadre of Assistant Executive Engineers and there was no pleading that overall efficiency of service would be hampered by promoting persons belonging to SCs and STs - Hence, instant appeals - Whether the Act was in consistent with arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution.

Held, exercise for determining 'inadequacy of representation', 'backwardness' and 'overall efficiency', was a must for exercise of power u/art. 16(4A) of Constitution. Mere fact that there was no proportionate representation in promotional posts for population of SCs and STs was not by itself enough to grant consequential seniority to promotees who were otherwise junior and thereby denying seniority to those who were given promotion later on account of reservation policy. It was for State to place material on record that there was compelling necessity for exercise of such power and decision of State was based on material including the study that overall efficiency was not compromised. In instant case, no such exercise was undertaken. HC erroneously observed that it was for petitioners to plead and prove that the overall efficiency was adversely affected by giving consequential seniority to junior persons who got promotion on account of reservation. Plea that persons promoted at the same time were allowed to retain their seniority in the lower cadre was untenable and ignores the fact that a senior person might be promoted later and not at same time on account of roster point reservation. Depriving him of his seniority affects his further chances of promotion. Further plea that seniority was not a fundamental right was equally without any merit in the present context. In absence of exercise u/art. 16(4A) of Constitution, it was the 'catch up' rule which fully applies. SC declare the provisions of the Act to the extent of doing away with the 'catch up' rule and providing for consequential seniority u/ss. 3 and 4 of the Act to persons belonging to SCs and STs on promotion against roster points to be ultra vires u/arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution. The judgment would not affect those who have already retired and would not affect financial benefits already taken. Consequential promotions granted to serving employees, based on consequential seniority benefit, would be treated as ad hoc and liable to be reviewed. Seniority list might be revised within 3 months. Impugned judgment and order of HC was set aside. Appeals allowed.
