Subramani and Others v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

28 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1922; (2002) 7 SCC 210; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1659; AIR 2002 SC 2980; 2002 (3) CCR 250; 2002 (4) Crimes 54; 2002 (6) CRJ 616; 2002 CRLJ 4102; 2002 (3) JCC 1728; JT 2002 (6) SC 262; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 213; 2002(6) SCALE 106; 2002 (5) SLT 52; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 718; 2002 (6) Supreme 1; 2002 (2) UC 683B

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 302/34, 324, 326 149, 304 34 - Self-defence - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant No.1 was father of appellant Nos.2, appellant No.3 and appellant No.4 - They have impugned judgment and order of HC whereby while setting aside their conviction u/ss. 302 and 302/34 IPC HC found them guilty of having exceeded their right of self-defence and found them guilty of offence punishable u/s. 304 Part I r/w s. 34 IPC - HC also found appellants guilty variously of offences u/ss. 324 and 326 IPC - HC acquitted them of charge u/s. 447 IPC on finding that land in question was in their cultivating possession as tenants and they could not be held guilty of offence of criminal trespass - Accused Nos.5 and 6 were put up for trial before Trial Court and charged variously of offences u/ss. 302, 302/34, 324, 326 IPC r/w s. 149 as also u/ss 147, 148 and 447 IPC - Trial Court acquitted accused Nos. 5 and 6 finding no evidence against them, but found appellants guilty and sentenced them to various terms of imprisonment - Appellants No.1 and 2 were convicted by Trial Court for offence u/s. 302 IPC and appellants No.3 and 4 for offence u/ss. 302/34 IPC and sentenced to suffer imprisonment for life - All appellants were also found guilty of offence u/s. 447 IPC for which they were sentenced to three months rigorous imprisonment - Appellant nos. 1 and 3 were found guilty of offences u/ss. 324 and 326 IPC and sentenced to rigorous imprisonment for 2 years and 5 years respectively - Appellants No.2 and 4 were found guilty of offence u/s. 324 IPC and sentenced to two years rigorous imprisonment - Appeal by special leave was directed against judgment and order of HC -Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was sustainable - Held, appellants were in possession of plot in question for over 50 years - On previous evening prosecution party had attempted to dispossess appellants but on protest of appellants they gave up - Again attempt was made the following morning when appellants appeared on scene and protested, which ultimately resulted in assault on them - Court held that it could not be contended that members of prosecution party were in possession of land in question or that appellants had no right to evict trespassers and to assert their right to possess land - Court further held that their conviction with the aid of Section 34 IPC is not warranted in law - In absence of any finding by HC as to which of appellants had exceeded his right of private defence benefit was given to all - Once it was held that appellants did not exceed their right of private defence, it must logically follow that they could not be convicted of lesser offences u/ss. 324 and 326 IPC, because in same transaction and in exercise of their right of private defence they had caused injuries to some of prosecution witnesses - Appeal allowed.
