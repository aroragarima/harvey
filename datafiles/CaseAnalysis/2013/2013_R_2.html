Bhanwar Kanwar v R. K. Gupta and another

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 198; (2013) 4 SCC 252; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 187; 2013 (4) ALD(SC) 100; 2013(3) ALL MR 439; 2013 (99) ALR 72; 2013 (3) AWC 2190; 2013 (4) Bom.C.R. 240; 2013 (2) CompLJ 481; 2013 (2) CPJ(Sc) 5; JT 2013 (8) SC 296; 2013 (2) OLR 256; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 657; 2013 (2) RLW 1520; 2013(5) SCALE 373; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 151

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Keywords: U.P. Indian Medicine Act, 1939, U.P. Indian Medical Council Act, 1939

Summary: (A) Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 14(1)(f) - U.P. Indian Medical Council Act, 1939 - U.P. Indian Medicine Act, 1939 - Medical Negligence - Compensation - Entitlement of - Complainant/Appellant's son suffered from febrile convulsions during fever at age of six months - Appellant came across an advertisement published in newspaper offering treatment of patients having fits with Ayurvedi medicine by respondent No. 1 - In spite of using medicines given by respondent No. 1 for so long, condition of appellant's son got worsened - Appellant consulted another doctor who told her that there was no hope of child becoming normal and he will not grow as normal child - On enquiry as to nature of medicines prescribed by respondent No. 1, it was revealed that small white tablets were Selgin which was not meant for children - Respondent No.1 was passing off Allopathic medicines as Ayurvedic medicines - Appellant filed a complaint before national Commission - National Commission by impugned judgment quantified compensation payable by respondents as Rs. 5,00,000/- and directed respondent No. 1 to pay consolidated sum of Rs. 2,50,000/- to appellant and to deposit the remaining amount of Rs.2,50,000/- in account of Consumer Legal Aid of National Commission - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeal - Whether respondent No. 1 was entitled to practice and prescribe modern Allopathic medicines - Held, respondent No. 1 had nowhere pleaded that he was registered with Medical Council or enrolled in State Medical Register - He had not cited even registration number and no specific plea had been taken that he had already been registered with U.P. State Medical Council - Even registration number had not been mentioned - Merely on basis of a vague plea, National Commission held that respondent No.1 was entitled to practice and prescribe modern Allopathic medicine - National Commission had already held that respondent No. 1 was guilty of unfair trade practice and adopted unfair method and deceptive practice by making false statement orally as well as in writing - Appeal allowed.

(B) Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 14(1)(f) - U.P. Indian Medical Council Act, 1939 - U.P. Indian Medicine Act, 1939 - Medical Negligence - Compensation - Entitlement of - What was amount of compensation to which appellant was entitled - Held, appellant and her son suffered physical and mental injury due to misleading advertisement, unfair trade practice and negligence of respondents - Appellant and her son thus were entitled for an enhanced compensation for injury suffered by them - Further, SC found no reason given by National Commission for deducting 50% of compensation amount and to deposit same with Consumer Legal Aid Account of Commission - Impugned order passed by Commission was set aside and enhanced amount of compensation at Rs. 15 lakhs for payment in favor of appellant with direction to respondents to pay amount to appellant - Appeal allowed.
