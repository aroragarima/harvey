Smt. Lata Devi (Mall) v Haru Rajwar

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Saikia, N.D. Ojha

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 198; (1989) 4 SCC 773; AIR 1990 SC 19; JT 1989 (3) SC 470; 1989(2) SCALE 355; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 921

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Change Of Symbol

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 30(d) - Conduct of Election Rules, 1961, r. 10(5) - Change in symbol - (A) Candidate received notice of Returning Officer to change his election symbol on 14-02-1985 - Symbol changed on next day - Poll scheduled to be held on 05-03-1985 - Legality - (B) Election challenged - Ground, change of symbol - Assertions that it affect results - Held, s. 30(d) was complied with - Election petitioner must prove that result of election, in so far as it concerns returned candidate, was materially affected - Mere assertions by him not enough - Appeal allowed.
