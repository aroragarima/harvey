Hari Singh v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

13 April 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 713; (1993) 3 SCC 114; (1993) SCC (Cr) 631; AIR 1993 SCW 2357; 1993 (22) ALR 11; 1993 (2) CCR 189; 1993 (2) Crimes 269; 1993 CRLR 420; 1993 CrLR(SC) 420; 1993 (66) E.L.T. 23; JT 1993 (3) SC 73; 1993(2) SCALE 490; [1993] 3 S.C.R. 61

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Accused were convicted u/ss. 302, r/w 149, 148 and 323, r/w 149 of IPC - Hence, present appeal - Whether the rejection of the Special Leave Petition could seal the fate of the appeals - Held, the mere rejection of the Special Leave Petition, could not seal the fate of the appeals of the appellants - Had the intention of the accused been to cause the murder of the deceased, they would not have given blows by the back side of the Pharsa on the head of the deceased - If it was held that as the Special Leave Petition filed on behalf of appellants had been rejected, Court could not alter the conviction or sentence passed against the three appellants, although the Court was satisfied on the materials on record, then there was no purpose, while rejecting the Special Leave Petition to grant leave to appeal - Prosecution had not been able to prove and establish that appellants had the common object or shared the common intention to cause the murder of the victim, setting aside the conviction - Appeal allowed
