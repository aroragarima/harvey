Deepa Bhargava and Another v Mahesh Bhargava and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2092; (2009) 2 SCC 294; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 788; 2009 (2) ALD(SC) 61; 2009(1) ALL MR 938; 2009 (75) ALR 317; 2009 (2) AWC 1409; 2009 (2) CTC 364; JT 2009 (1) SC 151; 2009 (3) MLJ(SC) 1363; 2009 (153) PLR 521; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 507; 2008(16) SCALE 321; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 636

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Civil Procedure - Contract & Commercial - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 47, O.23 r.3 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 59, 60, 61, 74 - Us urious Loans Act, 1918 - Payment of Interest - Executive Court - Jurisdiction - Appellants filed suit for declaration and permanent injunction in respect of their share in suit property which they were said to have inherited - Compromise petition in terms of O. 23 r. 3 CPC was filed which was accepted by Court by order - Payment had not been made in terms of consent decree, appellants filed application for execution - Respondents deposited sum of Rs. 18 lacs and also objection were also filed by them u/s. 47 CPC before Executing Court and objection petition was rejected - Civil Revision application was filed contending that respondents were not liable to pay interest at rate of 18% per annum - HC rejected objection that consent decree was beyond subject matter of suit - Contention raised that interest at rate of 18 per cent per annum being excessive was hit by provisions of the 1918 Act was rejected - However, HC opined that question as to whether stipulation of payment of interest at rate of 18 per cent per annum on judgment debtors come within purview of s. 74 of the 1872 Act or not, should be considered afresh by Executing Court - Executing Court, by order directed that amount of interest payable should be calculated at rate of 14 per cent per annum - Respondent filed Writ petition against by Executing court- HC by its impugned judgment reduced rate of interest payable to decree holder to 9% per annum - Hence, instant appeals - Whether executing court had any jurisdiction to travel beyond decree of Trial court - Held, executing Court had no such jurisdiction - HC while exercising revisional jurisdiction also had no jurisdiction to invoke provisions of s. 74 of the 1872, Act, which for all intent and purport amounts to modification of valid decree passed by competent court of law - Decision of HC, therefore, was wholly without jurisdiction - Furthermore, HC did not hold that s. 74 of the 1872 Act would have application - It only remitted matter to executing court - SC did not find that any legal principle had been adverted to by executing Court in reducing rate of interest to 14 per cent and HC in reducing same further to 9 per cent - Further, there were large number of decisions where interest has been directed to be paid even at the rate of 18 per cent or 21 per cent per annum - Hence, impugned judgment could not be sustained - Appeals allowed.
