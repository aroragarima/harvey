Hiten P. Dalal v Bratindranath Banerjee

Supreme Court of India

11 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, B.N. Kirpal, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19949; (2001) 6 SCC 16; (2001) SCC (Cr) 960; AIR 2001 SC 3897; 2001 (2) AllCriR 1492; 2001 (3) AWC 2135; 2001 (2) BC 773; 2001 (3) CCR 43; [2001] 106 Comp Cas 574; 2001 (3) CompLJ 313; 2001 (3) Crimes 220; 2002 (1) CRJ 400; 2001 CRLJ 4647; 2001 (3) CTC 243; 2001 (2) JCC 51; JT 2001 (5) SC 386; 2001 (3) RCR(Criminal) 460; 2001(4) SCALE 275; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 900; 2001 (5) SLT 179; 2001 (5) Supreme 49; 2001 (2) UC 344

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal; Securities Exchange Board of India

Keywords: Dishonour of cheques

Summary: Criminal - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138, 139 and 118 - Special Court (Trial of offences relating to Transactions in Securities) Act, 1992 - (A) Presumptions u/ss. 138 and 139 of N.I. Act, 1881 - Held, both ss. 138 and 139 require that Court shall presume liability of drawer of cheques for amounts for which cheques are drawn - Obligatory on Court to raise this presumption in every case where factual basis for raising of presumption had been established - Such a presumption is a presumption of law, as distinguished from a presumption of fact which describes provisions by which Court may presume a certain state of affairs - Presumptions are rules of evidence and do not conflict with presumption of innocence, because by latter, all that is meant is that prosecution is obliged to prove case against accused beyond reasonable doubt - Obligation on prosecution may be discharged with help of presumptions of law or fact unless accused adduces evidence showing reasonable possibility of non-existence of presumed fact - (B) Jurisdiction of Special Courts - Both in civil as well as criminal proceedings - Whether permissible? - Held, statements and objects and preamble of Act show that purpose of Special Courts Act was to deal with word transaction and not offence therefore, not merely confined to criminal cases - Appeal dismissed.
