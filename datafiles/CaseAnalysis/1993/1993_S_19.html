S. V. Chandra Pandian and Others v S. V. Sivalinga Nadar and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 January 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K. Ramaswamy, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1332; (1993) 1 SCC 589; AIR 1993 SCW 570; 1993 (1) ARBLR 386; 1993 (1) CCC 222; [1995] 212 ITR 592; JT 1993 (3) SC 278; 1993 (1) MLJ(SC) 41; 1993(1) SCALE 141; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 58; [1995] 80 TAXMAN 151

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 30 - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 10(2)(v) and 26-A - Indian Registration Act, 1866, s. 17 - Partnership Act, 1932, ss. 2, 4, 14z,15, 18, 19, 22, 29, 30, 48, 31, 38, 36, 37, 41, 42, 43 and 44 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Application u/s. 30 of Arbitration Act to set aside award - Allotment of partnership properties under award required registration u/s. 17(1) of Registration Act, 1866 - Division Bench of High Court in appeal came to conclusion that award required registration u/s. 17(1) of Registration Act, 1866 - Whether award required registration u/s. 17(1) of Registration Act, 1866? - Held, no - Award read as a whole makes it absolutely clear that arbitrators had confined themselves to properties belonging to two firms and had scrupulously avoid other properties in regard to which they did not reach conclusion that they belonged to firm - Award seeks to distribute residue after settlement of accounts on dissolution - While distributing residue arbitrators allocated properties to partners and showed them in scheduled them in schedules appended to award - On a true reading of award as a whole, there is no doubt that it essentially deals with distribution of surplus properties belonging to dissolved firms award, therefore, did not require registration u/s. 17(1) of Registration Act, 1866 - Appeal allowed.
