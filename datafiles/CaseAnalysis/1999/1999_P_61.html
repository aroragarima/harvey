Prataprai N. Kothari v John Braganza

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Srinivasan, G.T. Nanavati, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1065; (1999) 4 SCC 403; AIR 1999 SC 1666; 1999 (3) Bom.C.R. 580; JT 1999 (4) SC 443; 2000 (Supp) RD 310B

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss.100, O. 41, r. 27 - Title and possession - Judgment of High Court fell within scope of cl. 15 of Letters Patent and whether the appeal was maintainable? - Held, question as to maintainability of letters patent appeal was not raised either before Division Bench of HC or in special leave petition filed in Supreme Court - HC overlooked that when there was no plea or issue on question of title, no evidence was admissible regarding same - Acted beyond jurisdiction in permitting additional evidence to be filed - HC did not take note of principle of possessory title - Appeal dismissed.
