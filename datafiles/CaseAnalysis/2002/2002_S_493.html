Yunis @ Kariya v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

10 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Kumar, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1476; (2003) SCC (Cr) 341; (2003) 1 SCC 425; AIR 2003 SC 539; 2003 (1) CCR 13; 2003 (2) Crimes 281; 2003 (3) CRJ 446; 2003 CRLJ 817; 2003 (1) RCR(Criminal) 378; 2002(9) SCALE 245; 2002 (7) SLT 303; 2003 (2) Supreme 899

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: IPC - Ss. 302, 147, 148 and 149 - convictions and sentence under - appeal - held, the oral testimony of the eye-witnesses as well as medical and other evidence on record clearly establish the commission of crime, the manner in which it was committed and the place where it was committed. - Further, failure to prove motive for crime is of no consequence, when the role of the accused persons in the crime stands clearly established. It is settled law that establishment of motive is not a sine qua non for proving the prosecution case. - Both the courts below have found the prosecution case to be fully established and proved beyond any doubt whatsoever and we see no reason to take a different view.
