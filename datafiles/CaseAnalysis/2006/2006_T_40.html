Tarkeshwar Sahu v State of Bihar (Now Jharkhand)

Supreme Court of India

29 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 563; (2006) 8 SCC 560; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 342; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 603; 2006(10) SCALE 45; [2006] Supp7 S.C.R. 10

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Women & Children - Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 306, 325, 354, 366, 375, 376, 407, 509, 511 - CrPC, 1973, s. 222 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113-A - Sexual Offences Act, 1956 - Appeal against conviction - Sentence of seven years rigorous imprisonment - Held, there has not been any attempt of penetration to slightest degree - In absence of any attempt to penetrate, conviction under ss.376, 511 is wholly illegal and unsustainable - Crime committed by accused was at initial stage of preparation - Offence committed squarely covers ingredients of ss 366 and 354 - Sentenced to undergo imprisonment for five years u/s. 366 and two years rigorous imprisonment u/s. 354 - Appeal partly allowed.
