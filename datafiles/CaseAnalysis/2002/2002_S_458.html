George v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

03 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, M.B. Shah, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1957; (2002) 4 SCC 475; (2002) SCC (Cr) 824; AIR 2002 SC 1647; 2002 (2) CCR 55; 2002 (2) Crimes 129; 2002 (4) CRJ 195B; 2002 CRLJ 2031; 2002 (2) JCC 770; JT 2002 (3) SC 582; 2002 (2) RCR(Criminal) 538; 2002(3) SCALE 287; 2002 (2) SCJ 702; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 895; 2002 (3) SLT 1; 2002 (3) Supreme 138; 2002 (2) UC 117

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 392 - Drowning of deceased - Conviction - Circumstantial evidence - PW-3/brother of deceased came to know that his brother was missing and started search for him - PW-3 saw photograph of one dead body was published in newspaper and identified body as of his brother/deceased where two gold rings and watch which deceased was putting on were missing - Further, in investigation was taken up and post-mortem examination was conducted which indicated drowning, as cause of death - Additional Sessions Judge convicted appellants u/s. 392 of IPC - HC upheld conviction passed u/s. 392 of IPC by II Additional Sessions Judge - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether appellant was responsible for drowning of deceased - Held, no internal or external injury was found on person of deceased - Presumption of robbery had been drawn as against appellant in view of fact that he was found in possession of looted property next day which could be said to be soon after incident of robbery previous day - It could though be well before body was recovered as decomposition had set in but fact that body did not bear any mark of external or internal injury, death by mere drowning did not provide any link with robbery and death of deceased - There could be possibility of different incident having taken place resulting in drowning of deceased - It was not circumstance which could lead to irresistible inference that appellant and none else was responsible for drowning of deceased - Drowning did not appear to be direct or indirect result of incident of robbery in which deceased was deprived of his valuables - It could not be said that it was circumstance which was wholly incompatible with innocence of appellant so far charge of murder was concerned - It would not be possible to draw any inference that murder was committed by appellant - Hence, conviction and sentence of appellant for imprisonment for life was set aside as awarded by trial Court and upheld by HC - Appeal partly allowed.
