Raja and others v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

04 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Amitava Roy, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 791; (2016) 10 SCC 506; AIR 2016 SC 4930; 2017 CRLJ 186; 2016 (4) JCC 2546; JT 2016 (10) SC 18; 2017 (1) KarLJ 159; 2017 (1) OLR 77; 2016 (4) RCR(Criminal) 581; 2016 (4) RLW 2986; 2016(9) SCALE 627B; [2016] 9 S.C.R. 615

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Presumption, Rape, FIR, Glass, Indian Evidence Act, 1872, Spot, Version, Medical Evidence, Intercourse, Oath, Identification, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Husband, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 366, 376(g),392 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Gang rape - False implication - On complaint made by prosecutrix, case was registered u/ss. 366, 376(g),392 read with s. 34 of IPC and charge sheet was filed against appellants - However, Trial Court acquitted appellants of charges levelled but HC by impugned judgment reversed order of acquittal and convicted appellants u/ss. 376(g) and 392 read with s. 34 of IPC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned order of HC convicting appellants could be upheld.

Held, PW8, who medically examined prosecutrix, opined in clear terms that she was accustomed to sexual intercourse and that no sign of forcible intercourse was discernible. The medical opinion that she was accustomed to sexual inter course when admittedly she was living separately from her husband for 1 and Â½ years before the incident is of much significance as the medical evidence in attendant facts and circumstances belies the allegation of gang rape. Further, as per testimony of PW2, prosecutrix used to take financial help from accused persons and used to indulge in dubious late night activities for which her husband deserted her. The defence plea of false implication as the accused persons had declined to oblige the prosecutrix qua her demand for financial help could not be lightly discarded in the overall factual scenario. Further, the seized articles per se in absence of any evidence of corroboration of charge would not, irrefutably prove the involvement of the appellants in the offence alleged. Under the circumstances, prosecution case does not generate the unqualified and unreserved satisfaction indispensably required to enter a finding of guilt against the appellants. Hence, the view taken by Trial Court is the possible one and on contrary, HC overlooked many irreconcilable inconsistencies, anomalies and omissions rendering prosecution case unworthy of credit in its findings. Hence, prosecution failed to prove the charge against appellants to the hilt as obligated in law and thus, they are entitled to benefit of doubt and so, impugned judgment and order is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Even though the testimony of a victim of rape or non-consensual physical assault ought to be accepted as true and unblemished, it would still be subject to juridical scrutiny lest a casual, routine and automatic acceptance thereof results in unwarranted conviction of the person charged.
