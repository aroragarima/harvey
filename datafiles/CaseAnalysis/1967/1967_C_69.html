Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v Mahalakshmi Textile Mills

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 231; AIR 1968 SC 101; [1967] 66 ITR 710; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 957

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 10(2)(vi-b) and (v) - A/y 1956-57 - Deduction u/s. 10(2)(v) - Assessee carries on the business of manufacture and sale of cotton yarn, spent Rs. 93,215/- for introduction of 'Casablanca conversion system' in its spinning plant and claimed deduction u/s. 10(2)( vi-b) in respect of same - Income-tax Officer disallowed the claim of the assessee for Rs. 93,215/- on the ground that it was not admissible as 'development rebate' since the introduction of Casablanca conversion system did not involve installation of 'new machinery' - Appellate Assistant Commissioner agreed with the Income-tax Officer - On appeal, Tribunal held that the expenditure incurred for introducing the Casablanca conversion system, though not admissible as-development rebate, was admissible as an allowance u/s. 10 (2) (v) of IT Act - HC upheld the order of the Tribunal - Revenue filed appeal against said order contending that tribunal had no jurisdiction to decide whether the sum of Rs. 93,215/- constituted an allowable item of expenditure u/s. 10(2)(v) of IT Act or not as assessee claimed deduction u/s. 10 (2)(vi-b) of IT Act - (A) Whether Tribunal had jurisdiction to decide whether the sum of Rs. 93,215/- constituted an allowable item of expenditure u/s. 10(2)(v) of the Act ? - Held, there is nothing in the IT Act which restricts the Tribunal to the determination of questions raised before the departmental authorities - All questions whether of law or of fact which relate to the assessment of the assessee may be raised before the Tribunal - If for reasons recorded by the departmental authorities in rejecting a contention raised by the assessee, grant of relief to him on another ground is justified, it would be open to the departmental authorities and the Tribunal, and they would be under a duty to grant that relief - The right of the assessee to relief is not restricted to the plea raised by him - (B) Whether the sum of Rs. 93, 215/- or any portion thereof is allowable as an expenditure incurred for current repairs u/s. 10(2) (v) of the Act ? - Held, Tribunal held that the expenditure incurred fell within the terms of s. 10(2) (v), though not under s. 10(2) (vi-b) of IT Act as as' a result of 'the stress and strain of production over a long period' there was need for change in the plant and that the assessee had replaced old parts by introducing the Casablanca conversion system - Order of tribunal as confirmed by the HC upheld -Appeal dismissed.
