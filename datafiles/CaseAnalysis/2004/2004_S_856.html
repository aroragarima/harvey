Dr. Prodip Kumar Biswas v Subrata Das & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

02 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 411; (2004) 4 SCC 573; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1341; 2004 (55) ALR 491; 2004 (2) Crimes 135; 2004 (2) JCC 684; 2004 (3) JhrCR(SC) 28; JT 2004 (4) SC 363; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 669; 2004(4) SCALE 157; 2004 (3) SLT 128; 2004 (3) Supreme 53; 2004 (3) UPLBEC 2414

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Practice & Procedure - Contempt Of Courts Act, 1971, ss. 14, 15, 17, 19, 23 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 215 - Contempt of Courts, Calcutta High Court Rules, 1975, rr. 15, 19, 20, 29 - Appellant giving out in advertisements and prospectus that his institution has been approved by HC - Single Judge restrained appellant from using name of court - Purpose of contempt jurisdiction is to uphold majesty and dignity of courts of law - Neither any notice of contempt was issued to appellant nor any hearing took place - Infirmities noticed - Appellant has undertaken not to mention name of HC in any advertisement or publicity in connection with his institution in future - Set aside impugned judgment and order and and dispose of contempt case by accepting undertaking of appellant.
