Collector of Customs, Bombay v S. H. Kelker and Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 40; (2000) 10 SCC 478; 1998 (102) E.L.T. 241

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs Tariff Act, 1975 - Whether 'Abbalide' imported by respondents is classifiable as 'an organic chemical' under Chapter 29 or a mixture of odoriferous substances falling under Heading 33.02 of Chapter 33? - Cl. 1(e) of Notes in Chapter 29 postulates that if a product mentioned in sub - cls. (a), (b) or (c) of cl. 1 is dissolved in solvent and solution constitutes a normal and necessary method of putting up these products adopted solely for reasons of safety or for transport, then product would fall within Chapter 29 only if solvent does not render product particularly suitable for specific use rather than for general use - In terms of certificate D/ - 19-09-1986 issued by manufacturer, compound imported by Respondents cannot be used in condition it is manufactured and for making it suitable for use and for retaining its suitability for use it has to be dissolved in a solvent - Need of solvent is not only for purpose of storage and transport of chemical, but also for retaining suitability of product after it Is manufactured - Its dissolution in solvent is necessary in order to make product suitable for use - Since product is used only for perfumery and not for any other purpose, it has to be held that product is intended for specific use only - In view of cl. 1(e) of Notes in Chapter 29, it may be held that product imported by Respondents cannot be regarded as falling under Chapter 29 of Tariff and would fall under Heading 33.02 in Chapter 33 of Tariff - Appeals allowed.
