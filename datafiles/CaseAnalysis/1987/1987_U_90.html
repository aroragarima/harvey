Messrs J. K. Cotton Spinning and Weaving Mills Limited and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 October 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, R.S. Pathak, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28457; (1987) Supp SCC 350; (1988) SCC (Tax) 26; AIR 1988 SC 191; 1987 (13) ECR 1280; 1987 (32) E.L.T. 234; JT 1987 (4) SC 421; 1987(2) SCALE 903; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 700; 1988 (68) STC 421

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Finance Act, 1982

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 9 and 49 - Finance Act, 1982, s. 51 - Yarn produced at intermediate stage - Levy of duty - Appellants have a composite mill wherein it manufactures fabrics of different types - In order to manufacture the said fabrics, yarn was obtained at an intermediate stage - The yarn so obtained was further processed in an integrated process in the said composite mill of appellants for weaving the same into fabric - Department issued a Circular dt. 24.09.1980 directed that the use of the goods in the manufacture of another commodity would attract duty - Subsequently, the Central Government by a Notification No. 20/82-CE. Dt. 20.02.1982 amended rr. 9 and 49 of the Rules - As the implementation of the said Circular worked to the prejudice of the appellants, they filed a writ petition challenging the validity of the said circular - HC held that the yarn which was produced at an intermediate stage in the mill of the appellants and subjected to the integrated process of weaving the same into fabrics, will be liable to payment of excise duty in view of the amended provisions of rr. 9 and 49 of the 1944 Rules with s. 51 of 1982 Act, however, the sized yarn actually put into the integrated process would not attract excise duty as retrospective effect allowed by s. 51 of 1982 Act would be subject to the provisions of ss. 11A and 11B of 1944 Act - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether yarn which was produced at an intermediate stage in mill of appellants and subjected to integrated process of weaving same into fabrics, will be liable to payment of excise duty? - Held, s. 51 of 1982 Act did not contain any non-obstante clause, hence, it was difficult to hold that s. 51 of 1982 Act overrides the provision of s. 11A of the 1944 Act - In the instant case, appellants were liable to pay excise duty on the yarn obtained at an intermediate stage and thereafter, further processed in an integrated process for weaving the same into fabrics as after the yarn was produced, it was sized and thereafter, subjected to a process of weaving the same into fabrics - Commodity which is obtained at an intermediate stage of an integrated process of manufacture of another commodity, is liable to payment of excise duty - Yarn that was produced by appellants was also liable to payment of excise duty - Appeal dismissed.
