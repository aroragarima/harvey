Damodar Lal v Sohan Devi and others

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 6; (2016) 3 SCC 78; AIR 2016 SC 262; 2016 (3) ALD(SC) 5; 2016 (114) ALR 694; 2016 (1) AWC 965; 2016 (1) CLT(SC) 32; 2016 (1) CTC 475; JT 2016 (1) SC 44; 2016 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1151; 2016 (5) MahLJ 508; 2016 (1) MLJ 346; 2016 (4) MPLJ 57; 2016 (1) OLR 618; 2016 (1) RCR(Rent) 98; 2016 (2) RLW 922; 2016(1) SCALE 120; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 607; 2016 (1) WLN(SC) 98

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Material Alteration, Findings Of Fact, Structural Alteration

Summary: Rent Control - Structural changes - Eviction of tenant - Justification - Suit was filed by appellant-landlord for eviction of respondent-tenant on ground that respondent-tenant has made structural changes in rented property in question - Trial Court and First Appellant Court passed order against respondent-tenant and ordered eviction - However, HC vide impugned order reversed order passed by lower Courts and held that finding arrived by lower court on issue of material alteration was perverse and not based upon sound and trustworthy evidence - Hence, suit for eviction was dismissed - Whether impugned order passed by HC in favor of respondent-tenant was justified and in accordance with law.

Held, there is clear and cogent evidence on side of appellant that there has been structural alteration in premises rented out to respondents without his consent. SC held that material alteration of property is not fact confined to exclusive/and personal knowledge of owner. It is matter of evidence, be it from owner himself or any other witness speaking on behalf of appellant who is conversant with facts and situation. PW-1 is vendor of appellant and also his power of attorney who has stated that there was structural alteration in violation of rent agreement. PW-2 has also supported case of appellant. Further, witnesses on behalf of respondent, partially admitted that respondent had effected some structural changes. Hence, SC held that burden has been successfully discharged by appellant by examining PWs 1 and 2. Hence, HC in second appeal was not justified in upsetting finding which is pure question of fact. Impugned order passed by HC is set aside and order passed by Trial Court and confirmed by appellant Court is confirmed. Further, time is granted to respondent to vacant rented property in question. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Eviction order passed against tenant is justified on grounds of material alteration to suit property when same is proved on evidence on record.
