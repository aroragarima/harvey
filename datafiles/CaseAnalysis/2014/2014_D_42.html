Shivshankar Gurgar v Dilip

Supreme Court of India

03 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, Ranjana Prakash Desai

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 14; (2014) 2 SCC 465; AIR 2014 SC 1182; 2014(3) ALL MR 983; 2014 (103) ALR 232; 2014 (2) JabLJ 1; JT 2014 (1) SC 611; 2014(3) M.P.H.T. 107; 2014 (2) MPLJ 663; 2014 (1) RCR(Rent) 85; 2014(1) SCALE 52; [2014] 1 S.C.R. 18

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Madhya Pradesh Accommodation Control Act, 1961, ss.12(1) and 13 - Recovery of arrears of rent - Eviction - Entitlement of - Appellant filed civil suit u/s. 12(1)(a) of Act for eviction of the respondent and recovery of arrears of rent - Compromise memo was signed by both the parties under which the respondent acknowledged his liability to pay arrears of rent to the appellant - In view of the said compromise, the matter was referred to the lok adalat and the civil suit was decreed in terms of the compromise - Thereafter, appellant filed an application for the execution of the compromise decree alleging that the respondent failed to fulfil his obligations arising out of the compromise decree and, therefore, the appellant was entitled to recover possession of the premises - Executing court recorded a finding that the respondent had paid the entire amount due under the compromise decree in the executing court although such a payment was made beyond the period of six months stipulated in the compromise decree - Further, the executing court examined the submission made by the respondent that in view of s. 13(1)(a) of Act the compromise decree insofar as it provided for eviction of the respondent in the event of his failure to make the deposit of arrears within the stipulated time was void - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that tenant fell in arrears of rent - Such a ground was one of the grounds in s. 12(1)(a) of Act which enabled the landlord to evict the tenant if he could successfully establish that the tenant did infact fall in arrears of rent and had neither tendered nor paid the amount within the period specified u/s. 12(1)(a) of Act despite a demand - Held, reading of s.13 of Act indicated that the payment or the deposit of rent into the court by the judgment debtor (tenant) was contemplated only during the pendency of the suit for eviction or an appeal (by the tenant) against a decree or order of eviction - S.13 of Act had no application to the execution proceedings of a decree for eviction - Language of s.13(1) of Act was very clear and explicit in that regard - SC failed to understand as to how the Court could read into s.13 of Act, a possibility of enabling the judgment debtor (tenant) to protect his possession by making the payment during the execution proceedings in spite of the fact that he had already been adjudged to be in default of payment of the rent to the landlord - Such an interpretation of s.13 of Act would be wholly destructive of s.12(1)(a) of Act - Therefore, not only the language of s.13(1) of Act, but also an irreconcilable inconsistency that would arise between ss.12(1)(a) and 13(1) of Act if the interpretation placed by the executing court was accepted - It was sufficient to hold that the executing court's interpretation of s.13(1) of Act was unsustainable - In the case on hand the tenant was clearly in arrears of the rent which fact was acknowledged by the compromise memo signed by the tenant which was incorporated in the decree - Looked at any angle, SC was not able to agree with the judgment under appeal, nor able to sustain the executing court's order dismissing the landlord's execution petition - Appeal allowed.
