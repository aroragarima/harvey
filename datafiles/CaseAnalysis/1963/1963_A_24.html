Associated Cement Company Limited v Workmen and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 297; 1963 (7) FLR 269; 1963 (2) LLJ 396; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 652

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Industrial dispute - Dismissal of workmen - Unemployment - Principles of natural justice - Industrial dispute between appellant and respondents, their workmen - Dispute was in regard to dismissal of five workmen employed by appellant - Respondents contended that dismissal of workmen was unjustified and dismissed workmen should be reinstated and wages for period of enforced unemployment should be paid - Industrial Tribunal urged by respondents that none of the three enquiries was conducted according to principles of natural justice and dismissals of 5 workmen were based on the findings recorded enquiries could not be legal or valid - Tribunal had no alternative to hold that dismiss of workman was unjustified and that inevitably led to order of reinstatement and payment of wages during period of employee's enforced unemployment - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Tribunal was justified in holding that enquiry was conducted in accordance with principles of natural justice - Held, misconduct alleged against workman /'V' under Standing Order was proved - Principle on which rule was based of natural justice and enquiry officers acted unfairly against workma /V' - Tribunal was right in holding that report made by enquiry officers against workman/'v' cannot be accepted as a report made after holding a proper enquiry in accordance with principles of natural justice - Tribunal had upheld respondents that all three enquiries were not conducted according to principles of natural justice - Dismissals of five workmen were unjustified and award had been made directing appellant to reinstate five workmen with continuity of service coupled with direction that workmen should be paid their full wages from date of their dismissal to date of their restatement as compensation for wrongful dismissal - Appeal allowed.
