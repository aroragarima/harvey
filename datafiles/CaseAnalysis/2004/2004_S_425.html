Girija Shankar v State Of U.P.

Supreme Court of India

04 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 78; (2004) 3 SCC 793; (2004) SCC (Cr) 863; AIR 2004 SC 1808; 2004 ALJ 683; 2004 (1) Crimes 337; 2004 (2) CRJ 740; 2004 CRLJ 388; 2004 (1) JCC 392; JT 2004 (2) SC 140; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 839; 2004(2) SCALE 205; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 73; 2004 (2) SLT 231; 2004 (1) Supreme 876; 2004 (1) UC 444

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Specific Plea

Summary: IPC, 1860, s.307 - not essential that bodily injury capable of causing death should have been inflicted - Section makes a distinction between an act of the accused and its result, if any - not necessary that the injury actually caused to the victim of the assault should be sufficient under ordinary circumstances to cause the death of the person assaulted - court to see whether act done with the intention or knowledge and under circumstances mentioned in the Section - attempt in order to be criminal need not be the penultimate act - intent coupled with some overt act sufficient - appellant rightly convicted.
