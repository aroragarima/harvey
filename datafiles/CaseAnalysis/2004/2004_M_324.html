Union of India and Others v Mukesh Hans

Supreme Court of India

17 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, A.K. Mathur, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1413; (2004) 8 SCC 14; AIR 2004 SC 4307; 2004 (4) AWC 3318; JT 2004 (7) SC 526; 2004 (3) KLT 462; 2004 (4) RCR(Civil) 315; 2004(8) SCALE 1; 2004 (3) UPLBEC 2908

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 5-A and 17(4) - Whether inquiry contemplated u/s. 5-A rightly dispensed in acquisition proceedings u/s. 17(4)? - Mere existence of an urgency u/s. 17(1) or unforeseen emergency u/s. 17(2) would not by itself be sufficient for dispensing with s. 5-A inquiry - Limited right given to an owner/person interested u/s. 5-A to object to acquisition proceedings is not an empty formality and is a substantive right which can be taken away for good and valid reason and within limitations prescribed u/s. 17(4) - It requires an opinion to be formed by Govt. concerned that along with existence of such urgency or unforeseen emergency there is also a need for dispensing with s. 5-A inquiry which indicates that legislature intended appropriate Govt. to apply its mind before dispensing with s. 5-A inquiry - Held, in view of facts that earlier attempt to acquire 40 bighas of land for very same purpose was allowed to be lapsed and that in file there is no reference to need for invoking s. 17(4), decision of Lt. Governor to dispense with s. 5-A inquiry suffered from vice of non-application of mind - Appeals dismissed.
