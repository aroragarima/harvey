Revenue Divisional Officer, Kurnool Dist. v M. Ramakrishna Reddy(D) By Lrs.

Supreme Court of India

08 December 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1098; (2011) 11 SCC 648; 2010(13) SCALE 427; 2011 (1) WLN(SC) 4

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Orchard

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1) and 23(1A)- Quantum of compensation - Respondents were the owners of a sweet lime orchard - Said lands, alongwith surrounding lands were acquired - Land Acquisition Officer, while making the award, did not value the land as a sweet lime orchard and valued it as bare land - Reference Court adopted the yield capitalisation method, to arrive at the compensation and awarded additional market value at 12% p.a on such market value from the date of notification u/s. 4(1) of the Act till date of award or possession whichever was earlier, 30% solatium on the market value and interest at 9% p.a from date of possession for a period of one year and thereafter at 15% p.a till date of payment on the aggregate of compensation, additional market value and solatium - It adopted the multiplier of 13 - Appeal filed challenging the quantum, as being excessive, was dismissed by the HC - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether Reference Court was justified in applying multiplier of 13? - Held, general trend is to adopt a multiplier of 8 to 10 in regard to plantations, fruit groves and orchards and a multiplier ranging from 10 to 12 to agricultural crop land - However, there are no special circumstances to apply the higher multiplier of 12 or 13 or the lower multiplier of 8 - In the present case, having regard to the evidence in regard to the nature, standard and position of the orchard, standard multiplier of 10 should be applied - Therefore, compensation for the land acquired determined accordingly - (B) Whether Reference Court was justified in awarding additional amount u/s. 23(1A) at 12% p.a from the date of preliminary notification? - Held, award of additional amount u/s. 23(1A) of the Act would arise only where the possession is taken after the notification u/s. 4(1) of the Act - Where possession is taken prior to the date of notification u/s. 4(1) of the Act, no additional amount is awardable u/s. 23(1A) of the Act - Hence, in the present case, award of such amount cannot be sustained - (C) Whether Reference Court was justified in awarding interest u/s. 28 of the Act from the date of possession and not from the date of notification u/s. 4(1) of the Act? - Held, even if the land owner may not be entitled to interest from the date of possession but only from the date of preliminary notification, he will be entitled to compensation for wrongful use and occupation from the date of actual dispossession till the date of notification u/s. 4(1) of the Act - In the present case, though respondents were not entitled to interest u/s. 28 of the Act, from a date prior to the date of preliminary notification, they were entitle to damages for wrongful use and damages of the lands from the date of possession till date of notification u/s. 4(1) of the Act - Appeal partly allowed.
