Bhandara District Central Co-Operative Bank Limited and Others v State of Maharashtra and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 September 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, N. Venkatachala, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 657; (1993) Supp3 SCC 259; AIR 1993 SC 59; 1993 (1) BankCLR 3; 1993 (3) Bom.C.R. 156; JT 1993 (Supp) SC 428; 1992(2) SCALE 617; [1992] Supp1 S.C.R. 501; 1993 (1) UJ 14

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Constitution - Trusts & Associations - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.14, 19(1)(c) and 19(1)(g) - Maharashtra Co-operative Societies Act, 1960, ss. 73-A and 145 - Validity of s. 73-A of Act under challenged - (A) Whether impugned provision of Act imposes restrictions on members to conduct affairs of society in accordance with their choice? - Held, Act does not place any restriction on formation of any association or union for carrying on any trade or business, nor does it require such unions or societies to be registered under Act; petitioner-societies were free to proceed as they wished without being subjected to any condition placed by Act, but in that case they would not be entitled to benefits of Act - (B) Whether s. 145 bans use of word 'co-operative' - Held, restriction is clearly reasonable and in interest of general public and is, therefore, saved by cl. (6) of art. 19 - Purpose of s. 145 is to ensure that general public has adequate notice that a society they may have to deal with, is unregistered and, therefore, not amenable to provisions of Act, before taking a decision about their relationship with same - Persons desirous of running a society have been placed under an obligation to publicly declare that their society is not registered under Act - (C) Whether provisions separating Chairman and President from other members of managing committee for application of impugned restrictions are discriminatory - Held, basis of distinction made between 'designated officers' as defined in Act and other members have clear nexus with object which amendment seeks to achieve; there is clear basis for selecting category of persons to be referred to by expression 'designated officer' for purpose of ss. 73-A, 73-C, 73-D and 73-E, and policy in this regard is perfectly reasonable, having regard to object of amendment - (D) Whether substitution of consecutive period of 10 years by a period of 10 years in aggregate and its application to every case irrespective of period for which a person has already remained in office in past is discriminatory? - Held, concept of consecutive period of 10 years in existing s. 73-A(5), provisions of Act were not being implemented in letter and spirit of law', therefore, Committee, thought that this disqualification should be applicable in cases of period of more than 10 years instead of existing consecutive period of 10 years - Special leave petitions dismissed.
