Raj Kishore (Dead) By Lrs. v Prem Singh And Others

Supreme Court of India

10 December 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1069; (2011) 1 SCC 657; 2011 (5) ALD(SC) 136; 2011 (85) ALR 518; 2011 (2) AWC 2141; 2011 (1) JabLJ 244; JT 2010 (13) SC 535; 2011 (2) MLJ(SC) 490; 2011 (2) MPLJ 291; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 383; 2011 (113) RD 61; 2010(13) SCALE 281

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Conditional Sale, Mortgage By Conditional Sale, English Mortgage

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 58(c) and 58(e) - Specific Relief Act 1963, s. 16(c) - Re-conveyance - Suit was filed by plaintiffs for a decree for declaration to the effect that the sale-deed executed by him in favour of defendant no.1 was void and ineffective and that he continued to be in possession of the land as owner thereof - It was stated that the transfer was only by way of security for the repayment of Rs.6,000/- which according to the plaintiff was taken only as a loan - Trial Court held that the sale-deed in question was not executed as security for any loan as alleged by the plaintiff nor was the execution of agreement proved - On appeal, First Appellate Court held that there was no stipulation in the sale-deed that the land transferred thereby shall be retransferred to the Vendor by executing a sale-deed in his favour and that while the execution of the agreement was proved there was no mention in the same about the sale-deed in question being by way of security for repayment of the loan - On second appeal, HC held whenever a sale-deed was accompanied by a document for re-conveyance of the property sold, the transaction between the parties would amount to a mortgage, subject to the condition that the mortgagee must get the property re-conveyed within the period stipulated for that purpose - HC allowed the appeal partly and restrained defendant no.1 from interfering with the possession of the plaintiff till such time he obtained a decree for partition from the revenue court concerned - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether statement of law made by the HC to the effect whenever conveyance of any property accompanied by a document for re-conveyance of the same to the seller the transaction would amount to a mortgage was correct? - Held, for a transaction to constitute mortgage by conditional sale it is necessary that the condition is embodied in the document that purports to effect the sale - That requirement is stipulated by the proviso to s. 58(c) of Transfer of Property Act which admits of no exceptions - In the present case, sale-deed executed by the plaintiff does not embody any such condition - HC overlooked the proviso according to which the condition regarding payment of the mortgage money as a condition for transfer of the property to the seller must be embodied in the sale-deed itself - Hence, statement of law made by the High Court to the effect that every sale accompanied by an agreement for re-conveyance of the property will constitute a mortgage by conditional sale was not, therefore, correct - Finding of HC as to the legal effect of the transaction of sale followed by an agreement for re-transfer of the property was not, therefore, legally sound - (B) Whether transaction in the present case constitutes an English mortgage? - Held, transaction to constitute an English mortgage the following essential conditions must be satisfied: (1) mortgagor must bind himself to re-pay the mortgage money on a certain date; (2) property mortgaged should be transferred absolutely to the Mortgagee; (3) such absolute transfer should be made subject to proviso that the Mortgagee shall re-convey the property to the Mortgagor upon payment by him of the mortgage money on the date the Mortgagor binds himself to pay the same - In the present case, requirements mentioned above were not satisfied - (C) Whether plaintiff could rely upon the agreement for re-conveyance and pray for a decree for specific performance thereof? - Held, in a suit for specific performance it is absolutely necessary for the plaintiff to assert that he/she was always ready and willing to perform the essential terms of the contract sought to be enforced against the defendant - S. 16(c) of the Specific Relief Act makes that requirement mandatory - In the present case, there was no averment as to the readiness and willingness of the plaintiff to perform his part of the contract - In the absence of such an averment, amendment of the plaint to incorporate a prayer for specific performance of the agreement for re-conveyance would not have advanced the case of the plaintiff or the appellants who have succeeded him - (D) Whether Courts below correctly held that the plaintiff had failed to prove that he had tendered the amount of Rs.6,000/- to the purchaser within the date stipulated in the agreement for re-conveyance? - Held, this being a finding of fact any claim for re-conveyance made in default of the said stipulation must fail for the right of re-conveyance cannot in the case of default be saved from forfeiture - In the present case there was no allegation of fraud, accident or surprise to call for intervention of equity so as to save the plaintiffs right of re-conveyance of the property against forfeiture - Appeal dismissed.
