Katta Sujatha Smt v Fertilizers and Chemicals Travancore Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

23 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1772; (2002) 7 SCC 655; (2003) SCC (Cr) 151; 2003 (50) ALR 232; 2003 (1) AWC 612; 2005 (3) BC 575; 2003 (1) Bom.C.R. 517; [2003] 114 Comp Cas 27; 2003 (1) CTC 127; 2003 (1) JIC 611; JT 2002 (10) SC 134; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 502; 2002(6) SCALE 169; 2004 (2) SLT 873

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal; Health & Drug

Summary: Banking & Finance - Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940, s. 18-A - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138 and 141 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Cheque issued by firm through one partner - Dishonour of cheque - Whether other partners will also be liable for prosecution u/s. 138 of Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, when cheque is issued by one partner? - Relied on State of Karnataka v. Pratap Chand, (1981 Indlaw SC 279) - K. P. G. Nair v. Jindal Menthol India Ltd, (2000 Indlaw SC 2686) - Partner is liable to be convicted for an offence committed by firm if he was in charge of and was responsible to firm for conduct of business of firm or if it is proved that offence was committed with consent or connivance of, or was attributable to any neglect on part of partner concerned - Criminal proceedings to be quashed - Appeal allowed.
