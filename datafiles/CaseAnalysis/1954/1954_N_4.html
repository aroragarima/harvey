Narain Swadeshi Weaving Mills v Commissioner of Excess Profits Tax

Supreme Court of India

25 October 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 77; AIR 1955 SC 176; [1954] 26 ITR 765; 1955 SCJ 30; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 952

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940, ss. 10A, 2(5), 21, 4, 5 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 66(1) - Assessment - Amalgamated of income - Excess profits tax - Accounting periods - Appellant was a firm constituted upon terms and conditions in a deed of partnership - Funds available to company were not sufficient to enable it to take over all assets of assessee firm - Company purchased only buildings and leasehold rights at an annual rent of Rs. 40,000 - Purpose of formation of company and two firms was avoidance of liability to excess profits tax - Excess Profits Tax Officer issued notices u/s.10A of 1940 Act to company and three firms - Three firms were really one and therefore, amalgamated income of all three and proceeded to assess assessee firm to excess profits tax on that basis for four several chargeable accounting periods - Appeal by special leave arises out of a consolidated reference made u/s.66(1) of 1922 Act r/w s.21 of 1940 Act by Income Tax Appellate Tribunal - Whether amalgamating income of firms was correct and valid in law - Held, that company dealt with part of its property which bad become redundant and was sublet purely to produce incomes transaction quite apart from ordinary business activities of company - Therefore, letting out of plant, machinery could not be detained u/s.2(5) of 1940 Act - S.1O-A with to amalgamating income of firms with income of appellant firm was correct and valid in law - Appeal allowed.
