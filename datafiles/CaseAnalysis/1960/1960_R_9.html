Â STATE of BIHAR v RANI SONABATI KUMARI

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 408; AIR 1961 SC 221; 1961 BLJR 285; 1960 (2) KerLR 681; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 728

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O 39, rr. 2(3), 4 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 133(1)(c) - Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1950, s. 3(1) - Respondent instituted suit against State of Bihar inter alia for a declaration that Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1950 was ultra vires of Bihar Legislature, and for a permanent alongwith temporary injunction for restraining defendant, its officers, servants, employees and agents from issuing any notification under provisions of Act with regard to plaintiff's estate - Whether a State is liable to be proceeded against under O. 39, r. 2(3) of CPC, when it wilfully disobeys an order of temporary injunction passed of nominee against it? - Held, State is bound by CPC, scheme of Code being that subject to any special provision made in that regard, as respects Govts., it occupies same position as any other party to a proceeding before Court - Appeal dismissed.
