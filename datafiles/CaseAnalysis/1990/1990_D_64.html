U. P. Junior Doctors' Action Committee v Dr. B. Sheetal Nandwani and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 August 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 419; (1990) 4 SCC 633; AIR 1991 SC 909; JT 1990 (3) SC 690; 1990(2) SCALE 462; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 130; [1990] supp1 S.C.R. 130; 1990 (2) UJ 671; 1990 (2) UPLBEC 1321

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure 1908

Summary: Miscellaneous - Admission to Post Graduate Medical Course in State of UP for seven medical colleges - Condition for selection, entrance examination - By showing of a fake judgement of Supreme Court directions extracted from High Court - By reason of such fake judgement it was represented that condition of entrance examination was done away with and criteria was the result in the MBBS - Admission taken on basis of order of HC - Seriousness of matter noted by the Court - Direction issued for investigation by Central Bureau of Investigation - Admissions quashed - Further directions issued.
