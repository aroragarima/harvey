Hazari and Others v Neki and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 January 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 402; AIR 1968 SC 1205; 1968 CurLJ 703; 1968 (70) PLR 823; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 833

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Punjab Pre-emption Act, 1913, ss. 14, 15(1)(a) and 31 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 22, rr. 1, 2, 10 and 14 - Three suits in court of Subordinate Judge for possession of lands covered by three sales on ground he had a superior right of pre-emption u/s. 15(1)(a) of Punjab Pre-emption Act, 1913 decreed in favour of plaintiffs - Appellants' appeal dismissed by Senior Subordinate Judge - Further four appeals and LPA dismissed by High Court - (A) Whether right of pre-emption claimed by deceased plaintiff was a personal right which died with him upon his death and his LRs. were not entitled to be granted a decree for pre-emption? - Held, right to sue has not been extinguished, plaintiff had right of pre-emption under Amended Act at time he, instituted suit and his right was not extinguished on his death but passed to his LR - (B) Whether respondent was entitled to a decree in view of s. 31 of Punjab Pre-emption Act, 1913 as amended by Punjab Act 10 of 1960 which came into force on 4-02- 1960? - Held, plain language of s. 31, substantive law enacted by legislature in amended s. 15 of Pre-emption Act should be applied and decree for pre-emption in favour of first respondent should be set aside - Appeals dismissed.
