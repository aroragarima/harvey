Baiju Alias Bharosa v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

19 January 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Shingal, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 122; (1978) SCC (Cr) 142; (1978) 1 SCC 588; AIR 1978 SC 522; 1978 (26) BLJR 54; 1978 CRLJ 646; 1978 CRLR 66; 1978 CrLR(SC) 66; 1978 MLJ 300; [1978] 2 S.C.R. 594; 1978 UJ 93

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code,1860, ss. 300 -Indian Evidence Act, 1872, 114(a) -Murder, robbery - Appellant unable to explain possession of stolen property - Circumstantial evidence pointing towards accused - S. 114(a) of Evidence Act,1872 attracted-Whether accused is liable to be convicted u/s 114(a) of Evidence Act,1872 ? - Held, there was ample justification for reaching inevitable conclusion that it was appellant and no one else who had committed four murders and robbery- In face of overwhelming evidence on which reliance has been placed by High Court, it is futile to argue that murders could not have been committed by a single person - Appeal Dismissed.
