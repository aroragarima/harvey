Rajnit Prasad v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3599; (2000) 9 SCC 313; (2001) SCC (L&S) 73; AIR 2000 SC 3469; 2000 (2) AWC 1133; JT 2000 (2) SC 31; 2000 (2) LLN 86; 2000 (1) SLR 663

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Armed Forces

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950 - All India Services Act, 1951 - All India Service, (Conduct) Rule, (1968), r. 3(1) - Determination of locus standi - Held, disciplinary action against an employee is taken by Govt. for various reasons principally for 'misconduct' on part of employee - This action is taken after a 'domestic' enquiry in which employee is provided an opportunity of hearing as required by constitutional mandate - It is essentially a matter between employer and employee, and a stranger, much less a practicing advocate, cannot be said to have any interest in those proceedings - Public interest of general importance is not involved in disciplinary proceedings - In fact, if such petitions are entertained at instance of persons who are not connected with those proceedings, it would amount to an abuse of process of Court - Petition dismissed.
