Ramesh Yadav v District Magistrate, Etah and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 September 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 63; (1985) 4 SCC 232; (1985) SCC (Cr) 514; AIR 1986 SC 315; 1985 (2) Crimes 728; 1986 CRLJ 312; 1985 CRLR 451; 1985(2) SCALE 486; 1985 UJ 1045

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - National Security Act, 1980, s. 3 - Petitioner was detained u/s 3(2) of National Security Act - Petitioner had already been in Mainpuri Jail as an under trial prisoner in connection with certain pending criminal cases - Grounds of detention were served on petitioner along with order of detention- Certain papers asked for with a view to making an effective representation were rejected - Whether an order of detention under National Security Act should be passed on ground that as an undertrial prisoner was likely to get bail? - It is clear that order of detention was passed as detaining authority was apprehensive that in case detenu was released on bail he would again carry on his criminal activities in area - If apprehension of detaining authority was true, bail application had to be opposed and in case bail was granted, challenge against that order in higher forum had to be raised - Held,merely on ground that an accused in detention as an undertrial prisoner was likely to get bail an order of detention under National Security Act should not ordinarily be passed -Petition allowed.
