Durgi Devi & Ors v State Of U.P

Supreme Court of India

05 April 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, N.L. Untwalia, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 176; (1978) 3 SCC 101; AIR 1978 SC 1124; 1978 ALJ 523; 1978 (4) ALR 343; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 595; 1978 UJ 430

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Rules 1952, Uttar Pradesh Tenancy Act, 1939, Uttar Pradesh Zamindar Abolition and Land reforms Act 1950 (Uttar Pradesh 1 of 1951)

Summary: Land & Property - U.P. Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950, ss. 34(1), (i), (ii), (iv) and (v), 39(1)(e)(i), 39(1) (g), 44 (c) - Land abolition - Compensation - Entitlement of - Appellants were the heirs and legal representatives of X who was an intermediary (landlord) in the State possessed 2 villages - On issuance of notification u/s. 4 of the Act, all rights and interests of the intermediary, including the forests in villages vested in the State - Draft Compensation Roll purporting to be u/s. 40 of the Act was prepared in respect of the intermediary's interests - Intermediary was entitled to a further sum of Rs. 13,471,11.6 over and above the one shown as due to him in the draft Compensation Roll - Aggrieved on appeal before HC allowed the appeal - Hence instant appeals by intermediary and State - (A) Whether the HC was right in taking a period of 20 years only u/s. 39(1)(e)(i) of the Act for the purpose of calculating the average annual income of the forest's from Village 'S' - Held, for fixing the number of years at 35 u/s. 39(1)(e)(i) of the Act, Compensation Officer had not given any reason whatever related to the criteria in r. 34(1) of the Act - HC took due note of the factors of r. 34(1), (i), (ii), (iv) and (v) in determining the period at 20 years for calculating the average annual income - In determining the period at 20 years, HC rightly gave due weight to the fact that its exploitation had been scientific and prudent, and it had started yielding income from sale of trees only during 10 or 11 years preceding the date of vesting and even on the date of vesting its condition was good - Since the first fellings were within 20 years of the vesting, it was reasonable to determine the period at 20, the same being the minimum fixed in the statute - Thus, the determination of such period as 20 years by the HC, in the facts of the case, comforts best with the guidelines indicated in r. 34(1) - (B) Whether the Compensation Officer was required to appraise the annual yield of forests on the date of vesting and take such yield, also, into consideration while assessing the average annual income from the forests u/s. 39 (1) (e) - Held, HC in instant case, while determining the yield u/s. 39 (1) (e) (ii) has relied upon the evidence of a retired Forest Officer, who took all the relevant factors into consideration - HC also accepted the evidence of Forest Contractor, which was to the effect that for the year ending March 1953, the sale of one coupe of this forest by the Forest Department of the State, fetched Rs. 53,000/- - No fault therefore, could be found with the HC's finding that on the date of vesting, the annual yield of the forest, appraised u/s. 39 (1) (e) (ii) of the Act was not less than Rs. 47,128/- - Figure worked out by the HC u/s. 39 (1) (e) (i) of the Act by dividing the total income of sales during the preceding 10 or 11 years, i.e. Rs. 6,13,334-8-3 by 20, was Rs. 30,666-11-3 - Total of these figures thus worked out u/s. 39 (1) (e) (i) and (ii) of the Act, comes to Rs. 77,794/- - If the figure was divided by 2, the average annual income u/s. 39 (1) (e) of the Act would come to Rs. 38,897/- - HC rounded off and raised this figure to Rs. 40,000/- - SC did not disturb that approximation because the sale of a coupe of the forest for the year immediately following the vesting, showed that the appraised yield of the forest on the date of vesting could be around Rs. 50,000/- - (C) Whether u/s. 44 (c) of the Act, 15% was deductible from the average annual income from a forest worked out in accordance with s. 39(1) (e) of the Act - Held, HC was, right in holding that 15% towards the cost of management could not be deducted by the Compensation Officer in respect of the income from forest calculated u/s. 39(1) (e) of the Act - Nor could the words 'gross assets' occurring at the close of s. 44 (c), of the Act be divorced from the context of rents and construed in the spacious sense in which they have been used in s. 39 of the Act - Their meaning and scope in the context was confined to the income from rents - If these words were torn out of the context and interpreted in comprehensive sense in which they were used in s. 39 of the Act, it would lead to wholly unreasonable oppressive and absurd results - It was demonstrated by HC, with reference to the income from mines worked directly by an intermediary, calculated u/s. 39(1) (g) of the Act - S. 44 (f) of the Act provided that 95 per centum of the gross income determined of s. 39 (g) of the Act would be deductible for the purpose of computing the net assets of the intermediary from this source - Appeals dismissed.
