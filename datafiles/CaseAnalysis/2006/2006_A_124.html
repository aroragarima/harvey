T. N. Godavarman Thirumulpad v Ashok Khot and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 209; (2006) 5 SCC 1; AIR 2006 SC 2007; 2006 (6) Bom.C.R. 877; 2006 CRLJ 2773; 2006 (4) JhrCR(SC) 57; JT 2006 (5) SC 492; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 170; 2006 (3) RCR(Civil) 34; 2006 (3) RCR(Criminal) 110; 2006(5) SCALE 361; 2006 (5) SCJ 662; 2006 (5) SCJ 677; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 215

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Practice & Procedure - Contempt petition - Bombay Forest Rules, 1942, r. 23(i)(ii), r. 88 - Bombay Transit of Forest Produce (Vidarbha region Saurashtra and Kutch Area) Rules, 1960 - By order dated 4/3/1997 this court directed closure of all un- Licensed saw mills, veneer and plywood industries and no State Govt. would permit opening of any saw mill, veneer and plywood industry without prior permission of Central Empowered Committee - State of Maharashtra granted permission to six units to operate in State on basis of decisions taken by contemnors 1 and 2 - Held, applications of those eligible for grant of licenses were required to be sent to CEC, who was then required to submit a report to this Court - This Court would have decided on question of entitlement for license - Procedure mandated by this Court was not followed - Custodial sentence of one month simple imprisonment ordered.
