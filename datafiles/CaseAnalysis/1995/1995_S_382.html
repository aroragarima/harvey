K.C.P. Limited v State Trading Corporation of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 August 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 488; (1995) Supp3 SCC 466; JT 1995 (6) SC 83; 1995(4) SCALE 697

Case Digest

Subject: Accounts & Audits; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Accounts & Audits - Contract & Commercial - Cement Control Order,1956 - Essential commodities - Cement factory - Rebates - Reversal of entries - Appellant had a cement factory - Govt. of the composite State of Madras was considering establishment of a cement factory by a private enterprise - Appellant offered to start such a factory and applied for a licence to start the factory - State Trading Corporation stated that the rebate of Rs. 7/- per ton, the difference between the ex-works price would be allowed to the appellant which was fixed by the Govt. of India, and the concessional rate would be allowed, and the appellant would be paid by the State Trading Corporation the ex-works price less rebate and the rebate would be further passed on to the Control Board, the purchaser - Appellant was appointed by the State Trading Corporation as their selling agent for the purpose of supplying cement to the Control Board - Appellant, as producer of cement, was entitled to receive the price of cement so supplied from the State Trading Corporation, as it recovered the price from the purchaser as agent of the State Trading Corporation - In the bills drawn by the appellant the sale price was shown as fixed by the Cement Control Order and a rebate of Rs. 7/- was shown as being deducted from the ex-works price - Appellant collected from the Control Board/the State of Andhra Pradesh the price of Rs. 47.50 per ton - Appellant from time to time submitted its accounts to the State Trading Corporation in respect of the cement so supplied to the Control Board retaining with itself the price at the rate of Rs. 47.50 per ton - Notified price of cement was increased from Rs. 54.50 to Rs. 60.50 - Appellant, however, continued to give a rebate of Rs. 7/- to the Board while supplying cement - Bills which were drawn by the appellant, the price was shown as the controlled price less rebate of Rs. 7 - Appellant did not show any rebate in the bills supplied to the Board after the coming of the New Cement Control Order into operation and claimed the price as fixed under the Cement Control Order - It was alleged by the State of Andhra Pradesh that appellant claimed the full amount of the controlled price from the State Trading Corporation and retained it with itself by debiting the full price to the State Trading Corporation in the statements of accounts - It was contended that the price which the appellant had agreed to charge to the Control Board for supply of cement for the said project was a fixed price of Rs. 47.50 per ton, and that the appellant had recovered excess amounts from the State of Andhra Pradesh by charging controlled price less rebate of Rs. 7 - Whether the appellant was entitled to claim the amount of rebate from the State Trading Corporation - Held, appellant was not entitled to claim the amount of rebate from the State Trading Corporation after the statements of accounts submitted by it to the State Trading Corporation - Claim of the State Trading Corporation for recovery of the excess amount for which credit was thus taken by the appellant was upheld - As selling agent, appellant was receiving the full price minus Rs. 7/- per ton as rebate - It was only that price which should had been paid to the appellant as producer - Reversal of entries in the accounts made by the appellant as selling agent of the State Trading Corporation was unwarranted and clearly illegal - Appeals dismissed.
