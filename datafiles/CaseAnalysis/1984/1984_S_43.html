A. C. Jose v Sivan Pillai & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

05 March 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 151; (1984) 2 SCC 656; AIR 1984 SC 921; 1984 KLT 510; 1984(1) SCALE 454; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 74; 1984 UJ 558

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 59 and 100(1)(d) - Conduct of Election Rules, 1961 Part IV - Constitution, art. 324 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - (A) Determination of extent of power of election commission to conduct of elections in regard to Commissioner's order of casting of votes by mechanical process - (B) Applicability of doctrine of estoppels to appellant challenging process as unauthorised by law Meeting before introduction of the voting machines - Legality - (A) Commission cannot override provisions of Act or Rules - Order is without jurisdiction - (B) Held, he cannot be estopped from challenging the same, irrespective of fact that he had agreed or participated in the meeting - Appeal allowed.
