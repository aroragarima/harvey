J.K. Bardolia Mills v M.L. Khunger,Dy.Collector

Supreme Court of India

18 July 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 869; (1994) 5 SCC 332; (1994) SCC (Cr) 1497; 1994 (31) ACC 663; 1994 (3) CCR 682; 1994 (72) E.L.T. 813; JT 1994 (4) SC 515; 1995 (1) SCJ 48; [1994] Supp1 S.C.R. 756

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, ss. 123 and 110(2) - Whether provisions of s. 123 would be attracted in a case where retention of goods has become illegal u/s. 110(2) of Act because no notice as required under said provision was served within statutory period? - Seizure of goods under Act - Requirement of notice u/s. 124 cl. (a) with respect to seized goods, within six months of seizure - Not complied with - (1) Whether such non-compliance makes such seizure of goods illegal as one of conditions for applicability of s. 123 is not satisfied? - Held, No - When goods are seized u/s. 110(1) that amounts to seizure of goods under Act and one of conditions for invoking provisions of s. 123 of Act are satisfied by mere factum of seizure - Thus effect of non-compliance of provisions of s. 110(2) would only be that seized goods are returned to person from whose possession they were seized and it would not render initial seizure of goods illegal - Further held, in case of no notice provision of s. 123 would not be attracted as seizure of goods u/s. 110(1) by itself is sufficient to comply with requisite condition u/s. 123 of Act - Appeal dismissed with cost.
