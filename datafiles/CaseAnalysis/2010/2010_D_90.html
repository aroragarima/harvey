Pinninti Kistamma and another v Duvvada Parsuram Chowdary and another

Supreme Court of India

08 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 32; (2010) 2 SCC 452; 2010 (5) ALD(SC) 109; JT 2010 (6) SC 297; 2010(1) SCALE 393; [2010] 1 S.C.R. 297

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Estate Land Act, 1908, Estates Abolition Act, Madras Estates Abolition and Conversion into Raiyotwari Act of 1948, Andhra Pradesh Record of Rights in Land Act, 1971

Summary: Land & Property - Suit for permanent injunction - Respondent/tenants claimed tenancy right over the land in question - Respondents alleged that the revenue records were manipulated by the appellant/Landlords - Tehsildar conducted an enquiry wherein it was found that respondents were occupants and cultivators in the suit property - Appellants challenged the order of Tehsildar before HC - HC quashed the Tehsildar's order, however, granted liberty to the Tehsildar to conduct fresh enquiry - Tehsildar, after the fresh enquiry, found that the respondents and their ancestors had been cultivating the land in dispute since the time of immemorial - Collector and Commissioner of Land Revenue also confirmed the Tehsildar's order - Respondents, in the meantime, filed a suit for restraining the appellants from the peaceful possession of the suit property - Trial Court decreed the suit in favour of respondents - Appellants challenged the Trial Court order before the First Appellate Court - First Appellate Court allowed the appeals - Respondents filed Second Appeal before the HC - HC allowed the Second Appeal - Whether the Civil Court was justified in setting aside the orders of three statutory authorities, namely, the Tehsildar, the District Collector and the Commissioner of Land Revenue without there being any challenge to those orders - Held, order of the Tehsildar, having achieved the status of finality, cannot be upset by the Civil Court - HC granted a decree for permanent injunction in favour of the respondents mainly on the basis that the respondents were in possession and cultivation of the disputed lands and after considering the fact the appellants had failed to prove their possession and cultivation in respect of the lands in question by producing reliable and material evidence before the court - No interference on the impugned order - Appeals dismissed.
