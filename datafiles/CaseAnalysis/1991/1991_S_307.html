Ram Sewak Prasad v State of Uttar Pradesh Others

Supreme Court of India

11 July 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 868; (1991) Supp2 SCC 114; (1991) SCC (L&S) 1140; AIR 1991 SC 1818; 1991 (17) ATC 346; 1991 (2) AWC 1114; 1991 (2) CLR 481; 1991 (63) FLR 365; JT 1991 (3) SC 84; 1991 LabIC 1675; 1991 (2) LLN 410; 1991(2) SCALE 45; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 884; 1992 (3) SLJ 201; 1991 (4) SLR 562; 1991 (2) UJ 364

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution, 1950, art. 32 - Uttar Pradesh Subordinate Excise service Rules, 1967, rr. 3(ix), 5 and 23 - Uttar Pradesh Subordinate Excise service Rules, 1983, rr. 3(g), 3(j), 5 and 21(1) - Ad hoc Appointment - Promotion - Name not shown in seniority list - Whether petitioner's promotion to post of inspector was in violation of the 1967 Rules? - Court held, no, petitioner shall be deemed to be appointed by way of promotion as substantive Excise Inspector under 1983 Rules with effect from February 24, 1972 and petitioner shall be entitled to the benefit of his entire period of service as Excise Inspector from February 24, 1972 towards fixation of his seniority in the cadre of Excise Inspector - Further directed , petitioner shall be considered for promotion to the post of Excise Superintendent from a date earlier than the date when respondents were promoted to the said post and petitioner shall also be entitled to be considered to the post of Assistant Excise Commissioner in accordance with the Rules from a date earlier than the date when any of his juniors were promoted to the said post - Petition allowed.
