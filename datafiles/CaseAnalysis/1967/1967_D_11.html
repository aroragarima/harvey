Harjinder Singh Alias Jinda v Delhi Administration

Supreme Court of India

14 November 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 188; AIR 1968 SC 867; AIR 1991 SCW 654; 1968 CRLJ 1023; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 246

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.300, 302, 304 and 326 - Appellant and brother of deceased were fighting when deceased tried to intervene and stop them from fighting - Appellant took out a knife and stabbed deceased in thigh due to which he died - Whether appellant is guilty of murder of deceased? - Held, Yes - Appellant stabbed deceased fearing that he may be overpowered by deceased and his brother - Stab was in thigh of deceased as deceased was in crouching position when he was stabbed - Appellant had no intention to cause death of deceased but he had knowledge that use of knife would cause serious injury or even death of deceased - Conviction altered from one u/s. 302 to s. 304 Part I - Appeal allowed.
