Commissioner of Central Excise, New Delhi v G.T.C. Industries Limited

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1851; (2008) 17 SCC 731; 2008 (228) E.L.T. 505; 2009 (16) S.T.R. 516

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Sale Price, Levied, Flow back

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 35C - Flow back - Excise duty - Levied - Searches were conducted on premises of respondent and as a result show cause notices were issued to respondent - On appeal before Collector the duty demand and penalty was confirmed - Aggrieved by order of Collector Respondents filed appeals before Tribunal and the same was allowed - Hence instant appeals.

Held, Tribunal did not advert to question as to whether there were allegations in show cause notice regarding flow back and its effect. Tribunal has not even taken note of finding recorded by Collector of Central Excise regarding flow back. Tribunal has not recorded any finding regarding the flow back to GTC. Since there is an allegation of flow back in the show cause notice and a finding recorded by Collector of flow back, Tribunal should have adverted to this fact and recorded its finding. SC is not satisfied in the way in which order has been recorded by Tribunal. SC is also not satisfied with finding recorded by Tribunal on the question of limitation. According to us, there should have been a detailed finding by the Tribunal on question of limitation. Impugned order is set aside and matter is remand to Tribunal to decide same afresh in accordance with law keeping in view observations made in this order. Nothing stated in this order be taken as an expression of opinion on merits of the dispute. All contentions are left open. Tribunal should record a separate finding on question of liability in case of GTC Industries Limited vis-a-vis job workers. Appeals disposed of.
