Vinoy Kumar v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20170; (2001) 4 SCC 734; (2001) SCC (Cr) 806; AIR 2001 SC 1739; 2001 (3) ALD(SC) 46; 2001 ALJ 932; 2001 (43) ALR 574; 2001 (2) AWC 1462; 2001 (2) CCR 141; JT 2001 (4) SC 506; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 434; 2001(3) SCALE 376; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 1196; 2001 (3) SLT 467; 2001 (3) Supreme 343; 2001 (2) UPLBEC 1332

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution

Summary: Constitution of India, arts. 226 and 32 - Advocates Act, 1961, s. 30 - PIL - Locus standi - Whether High Court in dismissing writ petition on the ground that advocate has no locus standi to challenge the legality of order passed by District and Session Judge by way of writ petition is valid? - s. 30 of Advocates Act entitles advocate to practice the profession and not to substitute himself for his client - Further held, petition not filed in public interest and does not disclose circumstances which prevented affected person from approaching Court - Petition dismissed.
