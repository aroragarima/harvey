State of Maharashtra v Gajanan and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1188; (2003) 12 SCC 432; AIR 2004 SC 1188; 2004 (1) CCR 6; 2004 (1) Crimes 164; 2004 (2) CRJ 578; 2004 CRLJ 919; 2004 (3) JhrCR(SC) 227; JT 2003 (10) SC 164; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 317; 2003(10) SCALE 1009; 2003 (7) SLT 750; 2003 (8) Supreme 816

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Summary: Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - s. 7 - High Court stayed the sentence and conviction imposed by the trial court - Held, the High Court has in the impugned order nowhere pointed out what is the exceptional fact which in its opinion required it to stay the conviction - Further held, the High Court also failed to note the direction of the Court that it has a duty to look at all aspects including ramification of keeping such conviction in abeyance.
