Pattakkal Kunhikoya (Dead) By Lrs v Thoopiyakkal Koya and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 898; (2000) 2 SCC 185; JT 1999 (10) SC 178; 1999(7) SCALE 594; 2000 (1) SCJ 468; 2000 (1) SLT 33; [1999] Supp5 S.C.R. 371; 2000 (1) Supreme 72

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Land & Property - Laccadive, Minicoy and Amindivi Islands (Civil Courts) Regulation, 1965 - Dispute relates to certain properties which belonged to a tarwad called Kandangalam which became extinct on death of its last member in year 1958 - (A) Whether Pattakkal is a branch of Pandambeli tarwad? - Held, Pattakkal people were never treated as having had anything to do with Pandambeli and properties of that tarwad - If Pattakkal was a collateral branch of Pandambeli they should have been first to be consulted before converting tarwad properties into separate properties - As a matter of fact Pandambeli people had not recognised Pattakkal as a branch of that tarwad and that Pattakkal people never claimed to be so and much less as having an interest in properties - (B) Whether plaintiffs can claim properties of Kandangalam branch which stands extinct in 1958? - Entitlement to property ought to have its origin through some common ancestors - Further held, claim by plaintiffs, however, has not been traced to a common ancestor and there is no evidence on record on that score - (C) When a divided branch becomes extinct who could be heirs to inherit its property obtained from common and parent tarwad - Further held, parties are governed by customary law - What has to be done is only to apply customary law which, if disputed will have to be proved in same manner in which a custom is to be established - A custom disentitling a surviving branch from succeeding to an extinct branch has not been pleaded or established - Appeal dismissed.
