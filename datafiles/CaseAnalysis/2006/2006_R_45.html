Rajbir Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 83; (2006) 4 SCC 51; 2006 (9) ADJ 640; AIR 2006 SC 1963; 2006 CRLJ 2458; 2006 (1) JCC 516; JT 2006 (3) SC 372; 2006 (2) RCR(Criminal) 410; 2006(3) SCALE 125; 2006 (4) SCJ 767; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 1134

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Human Rights; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989, s. 3(2)(v) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 301, 302, 34 - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss.161, 227, 397, 401 - Charges framed against respondent set aside by HC - Appeal against - Held, reason given by HC for setting aside charges against respondent no 2 is that firing was not aimed at deceased but she accidently received injuries as she was passing through that way and was hit - S 301 shows that if killing took place in course of doing an act which a person intends or knows to be likely to cause death, it ought to be treated as if real intention of killer had been actually carried out - Reasons given by HC for quashing charges are, therefore, wholly erroneous in law and cannot be sustained - Appeal allowed.
