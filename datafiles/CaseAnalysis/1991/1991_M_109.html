Joginder Singh and Others v Mst Chanan Dei W/O Anant Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 July 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, T.K. Thommen

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 726; (1991) Supp2 SCC 175; JT 1991 (3) SC 327; 1991(2) SCALE 224; 1991 (2) UJ 285

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Jammu and Kashmir Agrarian Reforms Act, 1976, Jammu and Kashmir Agrarian Reforms Act 1972, Jammu and Kashmir Big Landed Estate Abolition Act 2007 (1950 AD), Jammu and Kashmir Restitution of Mortgaged Properties Act 2006 (1950 AD), Jammu and Kashmir Tenancy Act 19M (1923AI)

Summary: Land & Property - Big Landed Estates Abolition Act, 2007 (1950 A.D.) - Jammu and Kashmir Restitution of Mortgaged Properties Act, 2006 (1950 AD) - Jammu and Kashmir Tenancy Act, 1980, s. 3(1)(a) - Occupancy tenant of grade 'A' under Tenancy Act - Mortgagee thereof - Judgment of Jammu and Kashmir Special Tribunal wherein it held that mortgagee was not entitled to ownership rights under Abolition Act, 2007 (1950 A.D.) as he was not a tiller of soil being not in cultivatory possession in Kharif 2007, material date under Act, thus as consequence mortgagor was entitled to restoration of possession in pursuance of decree passed for restitution of possession under Restitution Act, 2006 (1950 AD) - Judgment of Tribunal confirmed by High Court - Whether judgment of Tribunal as confirmed by HC, proper? - 'Land of a proprietor' - Specified in s. 7 of Act, inter alia, lands of any class held or acquired in ownership by proprietor and land mortgaged with or without possession or leased out by proprietor - Undisputed is position that ownership of proprietor in land extinguished u/s. 4 r/w s.7 of Abolition Act, 2007 (1950 A.D.) since land was leased out to predecessor in interest of mortgagor by land holder - S.6 of Restitution Act, 2006 (1950 AD) entitled a mortgagor to file petition for restitution, within four years from date of Act - Under Restitution Act, 2006 (1950 AD) order in question was passed in favour of mortgagor in 1956 directing restoration of possession - Restitution Act, 2006 (1950 AD) applied to all mortgages of immovable property value in cash or kind of which did not exceed Rs. 10,000 - Abolition Act, 2007 (1950 A.D.) did not make provisions of Restitution Act, 2006 (1950 AD) inapplicable - Intention of legislature in enacting Abolition Act, 2007 (1950 A.D.) could not have been to eliminate mortgagor and extinguish his rights in land in dispute even if mortgage was of value of less than Rs. 10,000 as that would have been contrary to spirit of Restitution Act, 2006 (1950 AD) which was meant to grant relief to poor and destitute debtor - But once a decree for restitution was passed and mortgage extinguished u/s. 9(1) of Act right of occupancy stood determined - Nothing survived to be apportioned - Thus impugned judgment was valid and within jurisdiction - Appeal dismissed.
