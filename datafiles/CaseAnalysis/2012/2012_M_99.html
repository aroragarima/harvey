Abdul Rehman & Anr. v Mohd. Ruldu & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 628; 2013 (1) ALD(SC) 1; 2012 (6) AWC 5448; 2012 (5) CTC 803; 2012 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3486; 2013 (1) RLW 29

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6 r. 17 r/w s. 151 - Forcible dispossession - Amendment of plaint - Cause of action - After death of real owner of land mutation of inheritance was sanctioned in favour of his son and on death of son his wife sold lands to respondent nos.1 to 3 and tried to forcibly dispossess appellants and respondent No.4 from lands under their possession - Appellants filed application under O. 6 r. 17 r/w s. 151 of CPC for amendment of plaint - Trial Court dismissed said application - Appellants filed Civil Revision before HC who dismissed said revision - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether appellants had made out case for amendment of plaint in terms of O. 6 r. 17 of CPC - Held, it was settled law that if necessary factual basis for amendment was already contained in plaint, relief sought on said basis would not change nature of suit - Therefore, contrary view expressed by trial Court and HC could not be sustained - However, date of sale deeds and date on which application was filed for amendment of plaint, satisfied that reliefs claimed were not barred in law and no prejudice should have been caused to respondent Nos. 1 to 3 if amendments were allowed and would in fact avoid multiciplity of litigation - Proposed amendment to include relief of declaration of title, in addition to permanent injunction, was to protect their interest and not to change basic nature of suit as alleged - Thus, all amendments which were necessary for purpose of determining real questions in controversy between parties should be allowed if it did not change basic nature of suit -Therefore, change in nature of relief claimed should not be considered as change in nature of suit and power of amendment should be exercised in larger interests of doing full and complete justice between parties - Hence, appellants have made out case for amendment and by allowing same, respondents were in no way prejudiced and they were also entitled to file additional written statement if they so desire - Appeal allowed.
