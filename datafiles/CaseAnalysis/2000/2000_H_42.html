Kasturchand and Another v Harbilash and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 465; (2000) 7 SCC 611; AIR 2000 SC 3037; JT 2000 (Supp1) SC 33; 2000(6) SCALE 404; 2000 (6) SLT 757; [2000] Supp3 S.C.R. 293; 2000 (6) Supreme 277

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Madhya Bharat Zamindari Abolition Act, ss. 2(a) and 4 - Appellants are proprietors of land in dispute within meaning of s. 2(a) of Abolition Act - Appellants filed suit out of which present appeal arises, for recovery of possession of suit land against original defendant (son of Gayadeen) on allegation that in Samvat year 2008 Gayadeen dispossessed them and therefore, they are entitled to recover possession after ejectment of respondents - They say that in Samvat year 2005 one Balku who was cultivating suit land, abandoned it and appellants cultivated suit land (khudkasht) during Samvat years 2006, 2007 and 2008 and same is so recorded in annual village papers (khasra) - Whether appellants acquired a right to continue in possession u/s. 4 (2)? - Held, a perusal of sub - section (2) makes it clear that it vests a right in proprietor to continue to remain in possession of his khudkasht land, so recorded in annual village papers before date of vesting - A proprietor claiming right to continue to remain in possession of khudkasht land, has to show that he was in possession of land as a khudkasht cultivator and that fact is recorded in khasra, annual village papers before date of vesting - There is no dispute in this case that for Samvat years 2006 and 2007 (Exhibits P - 2 and P - 3) names of appellants are recorded as khudkasht possessors - If that be so, appellants are entitled to continue in possession of suit land u/s. 4(2) of Abolition Act - Appeal allowed.
