Mohammad Baqar and Others v Naim-Un-Nisa Bibi and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 November 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 117; AIR 1956 SC 548

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Family & Personal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Indian Limitation Act,1908 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O.20, r.12, O.23, r.3 - Suit - Partition of shares - In estates of father - Family custom of excluding female heirs - (A) Whether family custom of excluding females from taking as heirs must be held to be established? - Held, burden of proving a custom in derogation of general law being heavily on party who sets it up, it was incumbent on appellants to prove by clear and cogent evidence that there was such a custom as was pleaded by them - (B) Whether claim of plaintiffs is barred by limitation? - Held, parties to action are co-sharers, and as under law, possession of one co-sharer is possession of all co-sharers, it cannot be adverse to them unless there is a denial of their right to their knowledge by person in possession, and exclusion and ouster following thereon for statutory period - Appeal dismissed.
