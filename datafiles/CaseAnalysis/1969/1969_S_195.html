Jankinath Sarangi v State of Orissa

Supreme Court of India

11 March 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 25; (1969) 3 SCC 392; 1969 (35) CutLT 769; 1970 (1) LLJ 356; 1969 UJ 340

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Whether principles of natural justice were violated in the conduct of the enquiry of the appellant and was denied the right of leading his evidence in defence and also that he was not given inspection of some material which was used against him but was collected behind his back? - The replies were not put in the hands of the appellant but he saw them at the time when he was making the representations and curiously enough he used those replies in his defence - They were not collected behind his back and could be used to his advantage and he had an opportunity of so using them in his defence - No prejudice was caused to the appellant by not examining the two retired Superintending Engineers whom he had cited - The pleas about rain and floods were utterly useless and the Chief Engineer's elucidated replies were not against the appellant - Held, a fetish of the principles of natural justice is not necessary to be made - No case is made out that the principles of natural justice are violated - The appeal fails and is dismissed.
