State Of Rajasthan And Anr. v Shamsher Singh

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 28; (1985) Supp SCC 416; (1985) SCC (Cr) 421; AIR 1985 SC 1082; 1985 (2) Crimes 242; 1985 CRLJ 1348; 1985 CrLR(SC) 270; 1985(2) SCALE 306; [1985] Supp1 S.C.R. 83

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Advocates and Judges - COFEPOSA Act, 1974,s.8 - Constitution of India, 1950, art.22(5) - National Security Act, 1980, ss. 10, 3, 11 and 12 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - Detention - An advocate, was ordered to be detained by the State Govt. u/s. 3(2) of the 1980 Act and he was actually taken into custody and detained on the following day - Reference within a week of detention without detenu making any representation till then - Representation forwarded within six days of receipt - (A) Whether a day's delay would vitiate detention - (B) Presumptions from Board Report of Board - Board of High Court Judges holding detention of paractising advoacte justified - (C) Detenu , if entitled to disclosure of confidential source of information - Held, board not required to adjourn consideration of matter and delay would not vitiate detention - Must be assumed to have done so on consideration of all materials - Is not entitled - Appeal allowed.
