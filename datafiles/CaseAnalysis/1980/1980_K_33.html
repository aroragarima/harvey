Mangat Rai & Anr. v Kidar Nath & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

21 August 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, Y.V. Chandrachud, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 293; (1980) 4 SCC 276; AIR 1980 SC 1709; 1981 (1) RCJ 326; 1980 (2) RCR(Rent) 673; 1980 (2) RentLR 678; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 476

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Punjab Relief of Indebtedness Act, 1934, Indebtedness

Summary: Rent Control - East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1949, s.13 - Punjab Relief of Indebtedness Act, 1934, s.31 - Eviction - Default rent - Deposit of arrears - Suit decreed - Respondent/Landlord filed suit for eviction against Appellant/Tenant for default in payment of rent u/s.13 of 1949 Act - Appellant/Tenant alleged deposit of rent in court u/s.31 of 1934 Act and also notice was invalid - Trial Court decreed suit for eviction in favour of Respondent/Landlord - Appellant/Tenant filed appeal and DJ allowed appeal on grounds of default notice - Respondent/Landlord filed Revision in HC which in turn allowed revision on ground that notice was not necessary - Aggrieved by order Appellant/Tenant preferred Appeal - Whether Appellant/Tenant was entitled to hold possession of property as against order of eviction by HC - Held, all Subordinate Judges of First Class were appointed as Rent Controllers by virtue of notification - Tenant who owed money to Landlord as arrears of rent was allowed to deposit in court u/s. 31 of 1934 Act - Appellant/Tenant had complied with s.13(2) of 1949 Act - Order of HC was set aside and suit filed by Respondent/Landlord was dismissed - Appeal allowed.
