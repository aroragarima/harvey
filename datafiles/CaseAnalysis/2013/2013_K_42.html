Kashmiri Lal v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 339; (2013) 6 SCC 595; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 426; 2013 CRLJ 3036; 2013(7) SCALE 411

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug

Keywords: Narcotic Drugs And Psychotropic Substances Amendment) Act, 2001

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 18 and 50 - Possession of opium - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant accused allegedly committed offences punishable u/s. 18 of the Act - Trial Court convicted appellant u/s. 18 of the Act and sentenced him to undergo rigorous imprisonment for period of 10 years - Appellant preferred appeal before HC - HC concurred with view expressed by Trial Court and dismissed the appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Petitioner contended that there had been non-compliance of s. 50 of the Act as he had not informed about his right to be searched in presence of a gazetted officer or a Magistrate and that vitiates conviction - Held, there was no dispute over fact that seizure had took place from tool box of scooter - There was ample evidence on record that scooter belongs to appellant - When a vehicle was searched and not person of an accused, needless to emphasise, s. 50 of the Act was not attracted - HC had opined that as opium was seized on 23-12-1992, amendment brought in statute book would have no applicability - It was also worth noting that appeal was preferred in year 1996 - Appeal was pending in 1996, ameliorative provision brought by way of amendment in year 2001 should not be applicable to appellant - It was crystal clear that scooter belonged to appellant and search and seizure was made in tool box of scooter - Trial Court and HC were justified in convicting the appellant - Appeal dismissed.
