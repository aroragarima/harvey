Mycon Construction Limited v State of Karnataka and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1690; (2003) 9 SCC 583; AIR 2002 SC 2089; JT 2002 (Supp1) SC 118; 2002(4) SCALE 468; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 854; 2002 (3) SLT 668; 2002 (127) STC 105; 2002 (4) Supreme 146

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Constitution (Forty-Sixth Amendment) Act, 1982 - Constitution of India, 1950 - General Sales Tax Act, 1963 - Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957 - Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1963, s. 17(6) - Whether s.17(6) of Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957 as amended by Act No. 5 of 1996 is unconstitutional? - Held, same argument was advanced before this court in State of Kerala v. Builders Association of India - Principles laid down by this court in aforesaid decision squarely apply to facts of this case having regard to similarity of provisions in two Acts - Complete agreement with HC and hold that s. 17(6) of Karnataka Sales Tax Act is constitutionally valid and challenge on ground of lack of legislative competence of State Legislature must be repelled - (B) Whether amendment brought in s. 17(6)(i) of Act by Act No. 7 of 1997 retrospectively is also unconstitutional? - Further held, if Legislature has legislative competence to enact a statute and statute so enacted does not breach any constitutional provision, same cannot be said to be unconstitutional merely because it is retrospective in operation - Appeals dismissed.
