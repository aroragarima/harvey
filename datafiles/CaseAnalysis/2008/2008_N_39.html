Maganlal Son Of Kishanlal Godha v Nanasaheb Son Of Udhaorao Gadewar

Supreme Court of India

16 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Lokeshwar Singh Panta, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2084; (2008) 13 SCC 758; AIR 2009 SC 278; 2008(6) ALL MR 981; 2008 (73) ALR 906; 2009 (1) Bom.C.R. 704; 2008 (155) DLT 49; JT 2008 (11) SC 405; 2009 (1) RCR(Rent) 16; 2008(13) SCALE 636

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Residential Purpose, Change Of User, Central Provinces and Berar Letting of Houses and Rent Control Order, 1949

Summary: Rent control - Central Provinces and Berar Letting of Houses and Rent Control Order, 1949, s. 13(3) (vi) - Eviction - Bonafide requirement - Sustainability - Appellant-landlord, filed application before Rent Controller seeking permission to terminate tenancy of respondent-tenant u/cl. 13 (3) (iv) and (vi) of Rent Control Order for his bonafide requirement - Rent Controller granted permission to appellant-landlord to terminate tenancy u/cl. 13 (3) (iv) and (vi) of Order - Being aggrieved by order of Rent Controller, respondent-tenant preferred appeal u/cl. 21 of Rent Control Order and Additional District Magistrate, (Appellate Authority) by order allowed said appeal and set aside order of Rent Controller - Against Appellate authority's order, appellant-landlord filed Writ Petition before HC - Single Judge, by order partly allowed writ petition upholding order of appellate authority to extent it quashed order of Rent Controller granting relief to appellant-landlord u/ cl. 13 (3) (iv) of Rent Control Order - Being Aggrieved, tenant filed a writ appeal before DB, which was allowed by DB whereby judgment and order passed by Single Judge was set aside and order of Appellate Authority was restored - Hence, instant appeal - Whether DB had erred in appreciating evidence considered by Single Judge in his order and whether order impugned was not in conformity with provisions provided in Rent Control Order - Held, Eviction of respondent-tenant recorded by Rent Controller had been confirmed by Single Judge on ground of bona fide requirement of appellant-landlord in terms of Clause 13 (3) (vi) of Rent Control Order - As there was no statutory requirement that landlord should issue notice of eviction to tenant before initiating proceedings under cl 13(3) of the Rent Control Order - DB was not right in drawing an inference against appellant-landlord for not stating ground of bona fide requirement of premises in notice issued to respondent-tenant before institution of eviction proceedings which commenced - In light of evidence on record, order of DB could not be sustained - Entire approach of DB in setting aside finding recorded by Rent Controller and affirmed by Single Judge was erroneous and DB mis-directed itself while considering question of bona fide requirement in terms of Cl 13 (3) (vi) of Rent Control Order, which postulates that there should be element of need as opposed to mere desire or wish of landlord - Respondent-tenant was directed to handover vacant possession of suit premises to appellant-landlord - Appeal allowed.
