Sardari and Others v Sushil Kumar and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 334; (2008) 17 SCC 208; 2008 (2) ACC 426; 2008 (2) AWC 1105; 2008 (1) CCC 175; 2008 (2) CPJ(Sc) 21; 2008 (3) JhrCR(SC) 3; JT 2008 (3) SC 458; 2008 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 932; 2008 (151) PLR 427; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 379; 2008(3) SCALE 570; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 929

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Insurance

Keywords: Breach Of Condition, Exercise Of Jurisdiction, Licensed Driver

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 110 A - Application for grant of compensation was dismissed on the ground that driver of the tractor did not hold a valid and effective license - High Court held that appellants were entitled to compensation of Rs. 63, 000/- from the respondent Nos. 1 and 2 - Appeal against - Held, owner would be liable for payment of compensation in a case where the driver was not having a licence at all - It was the obligation on the part of the owner to take adequate care to see that the driver had an appropriate licence to drive the vehicle - Insurance Company is not liable - Appeal dismissed.
