Sat Paul v Delhi Administration

Supreme Court of India

30 September 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 644; (1976) 1 SCC 727; AIR 1975 SC 294; 1976 CRLJ 295; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 11

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, s. 5 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 3, 162, 288 and 342 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 161 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 8, 137, 142, 145, 54, 155 and 157 - Bribery - Appellant convicted by Trial court and upheld by High Court - Whether conviction of appellant proper? - Held, the witnesses were not reliable and they had reasons to falsely implicate appellant - Inconsistency found in the evidence of prosecution witnesses - Not safe to lead conviction on such witnesses - Defence witnesses not impeached - Appeal allowed, conviction set aside.
