Asit Bhattacharjee v Messrs Hanuman Prasad Ojha and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 599; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 31; (2007) 5 SCC 786; AIR 2007 SC 1925; 2007 (4) ALJ 567; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 2052; 2007 (104) CutLT 488; JT 2008 (8) SC 138; 2007 (3) RCR(Criminal) 176; 2007(7) SCALE 241; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 939

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Criminal Misappropriation

Summary: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 156(3) - High Court directed that F.I.R. under ss. 120B, 420, 406, 465, 468, 471 I.P.S., P.S. Shakespeare Sarani, Kolkata be transmitted to the appropriate Police Station of Uttar Pradesh through the Secretary Home, State of West Bengal and the Secretary, Home, State of Uttar Pradesh - Appeal against - Held, Direction of High Court, is vague and indefinite - Investigating Officer attached to one Police Station may feel handicapped in carrying out the investigation within the entire State - In this case, it may be necessary for the Investigating Officer to make investigation even in other States including Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, West Bengal etc. for the aforementioned purpose - Directed that further investigation shall be carried out by C.B.C.I.D. of the State of Uttar Pradesh - Appeal disposed of.
