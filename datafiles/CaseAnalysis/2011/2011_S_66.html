Institute of Chartered Accountants of India v Shaunak H. Satya and others

Supreme Court of India

02 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 635; (2011) 8 SCC 781; 2012 (1) ALD(SC) 143; 2012 (1) AWC 100; 2011 (3) CLT(SC) 412; 2011 (5) CTC 780; JT 2011 (10) SC 128; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 488; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 336; 2011(9) SCALE 639; [2011] 14 S.C.R. 328; 2011 (3) UPLBEC 2133

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Education; Intellectual Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Chartered Accountants Act, 1948

Summary: Education - - Intellectual Property - Practice & Procedure - Chartered Accountants Act, 1948 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 12 - Copyright Act, 1957s. 51, s. 52(1) cl.a - Right to Information Act, 2005, ss. 8(1)(d), 9 - Chartered Accountants Regulations, 1988, regn. 39(2) - - Final examination - Verification of marks - Validity - Appellant, a corporate body conducted examination of candidates who enrolled as chartered accountants - Respondent no. 1 who was not successful in examination applied for verification of marks - Appellant carried out verification in accordance with provisions of the 1988 Regulations and found that there was no discrepancy in evaluation of answer scripts and informed respondent no. 1 -- Dissatisfied respondent filed an appeal before Appellate authority - Appellate authority dismissed appeal, and passed an order concurring with order of Chief Public Information (for short 'CIC')Officer - - Aggrieved respondentfiled writ petition before HC which was allowed and HC passed an order directing appellant to supply information in regard to queries 3, 5 and 13 -- Hence, instant appeal - ( A) Whether instructions and solutions to questions (if any) given by ICAI to examiners and moderators, were intellectual property of ICAI, disclosure of which would harm competitive position of third parties and therefore exempted u/s. 8(1)(d) of the 2005 Act and also whether providing access to information sought would involve an infringement of copyright and could be rejected u/s. 9 of the 2005 Act- Held, solutions to questions (or model answers) which were furnished to examiners and moderators in connection with evaluation of answer scripts, were literary works which were products of human intellect and therefore subject to a copyright - Information under the 2005 Act was exempted from disclosure at one point of time may cease to be exempted at a later point of time, depending upon nature of exemption - Information relating to intellectual property, question papers, solutions/model answers and instructions, in regard to examination conducted by appellant could not be disclosed before examination was held, as it would harm competitive position of innumerable third parties who were taking said examination - - An application for information would be rejected under s. 9 of the 2005 Act, only if information sought involves an infringement of copyright subsisting in a person other than State, ICAI being a statutory body wasState - Therefore, ICAI was not entitled to claim protection against disclosure u/s.9 of the 2005 Act - (B) Whether instructions and solutions to questions were information made available to examiners and moderators in their fiduciary capacity and therefore exempted u/s. 8(1)(e) of the 2005 Act - Held, exemption u/s. 8(1)(e) of the 2005 Act was available not only in regard to information that was held by a public authority (in this case examining body) in a fiduciary capacity, but also to any information that was given or made available by a public authority to anyone else for being held in a fiduciary relationship - CIC rightly held that information sought under queries (3) and (5) were exempted u/s. 8(1)(e) of the 2005 Act and that there was no larger public interest requiring denial of the statutory exemption regarding such information - HC fell into an error in holding that information sought under queries (3) and (5) was not exempted - (C) Whether HC was justified in directing appellant to furnish to respondent no. 1five items of information sought (in query No.13) relating to regn. 39(2) of the 1988 Regulations- Held, the 2005 Act provides access to all information that was available and existing - As Information sought under parts (i), (iii) and (v) of query (13) were not maintained and was not available in form of data with appellant in its records, ICAI was not bound to furnish same- Therefore,order of CIC was restored and setting aside order of HC- Appeal partly allowed.
