Jawahar Singh @ Bhagat Ji v State of Nct of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1918; (2009) 6 SCC 490; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 1122; AIR 2009 SC 2391; 2009 CRLJ 2860; 2009 (3) JCC (Narcotics) 97; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 127; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 978; 2009(6) SCALE 739; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 495; 2009 (2) UC 1139

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Material Change, New Offence, TADA Act, Temporary Act, Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances (Amendment) Act, 2001

Summary: Criminal - Health & Drug - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, s. 21 - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances (Amendment) Act, 2001, s. 8 - Retrospective effect of Amending Act - Appellant was convicted for the possession of contraband articles - Amending Act amended the provisions relating to the quantum of punishment - Appellant sought the benefit of amended provisions - Whether appellant is entitled to the benefit of amended provisions - Quantum of punishment to be inflicted on an accused upon recording a judgment of conviction would be as per the law, which was prevailing at the relevant time - Held, alleged offence took place on 26-9-1999 and appellant was convicted by a judgment dated 5-11-2000, however, the Amending Act came into force on 2-10-2001 - Amending Act cannot be said to have any retrospective effect, therefore, appellant is not entitled to the benefit of amended provisions - Appeal dismissed.
