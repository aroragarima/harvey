M.P. Electricity Board, Jabalpur v Vijaya Timber Company

Supreme Court of India

05 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1652; (1997) 1 SCC 68; 1996 (9) AD(SC) 387; AIR 1997 SC 2364; 1996 (4) CCC 256; 1997 (1) CLT 169; JT 1996 (10) SC 834; 1996(9) SCALE 2; 1996 (8) Supreme 542; 1997 (1) UJ 218

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Electricity & Energy; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Electricity & Energy - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Electricity Act, 1910, s. 52 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 9 - Suit filed for damages against Electricity Board for laying high power transmission lines through construction of plaintiff without his consent and permission - (A) Whether provisions of Electricity Act applicable? - (B) Whether civil suit barred? - (C) Whether plea that suit was barred by limitation can be allowed to be raised for first time before Supreme Court? - Held, (A) Provisions of Act would not apply - (B) Civil suit not barred - (C) Its being a mixed question of fact and law cannot be allowed to be raised - Appeal dismissed.
