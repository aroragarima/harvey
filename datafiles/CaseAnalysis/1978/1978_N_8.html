State of Orissa v Nakula Sahu and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 November 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 113; (1979) SCC (Cr) 283; (1979) 1 SCC 328; AIR 1979 SC 663; 1979 (47) CLT 624; 1979 CRLJ 594; 1979 CrLR(SC) 64; 1979 MLJ 456; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 442

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure,1898, ss. 439, 435 - Interference by High Court in exercise of revisional power - Jurisdiction of HC u/s. 439 to be exercised - When glaring defect in procedure - Manifest error of law - In spite of wide language of s. 435, HC is not expected to act under s. 435 or s. 439 as if it is hearing an appeal held, neither trial Court nor Sessions Judge committed any error of fact or law - HC upset their concurrent findings in exercise of revisional jurisdiction - Acquittal set aside - Appeal allowed.
