District Collector and Chairman, Vizianagaram Social Welfare Residential School Society, Vizianagaram and Another v M. Tripura Sundari Devi

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 798; (1990) SCC (L&S) 520; (1990) 3 SCC 655; 1990 (14) ATC 766; JT 1990 (2) SC 169; JT 1990 (2) SC 169B; 1990 (2) LLJ 153; 1990(1) SCALE 806; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 559; 1990 (2) SLJ 95; 1990 (4) SLR 237; 1990 (1) UJ 710; 1990 (3) UPLBEC 2032

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Appointment - Validity of - Whether appointment of respondent despite lack of proper qualifications is valid? - Held, respondent applied and was appointed despite lack of qualifications as advertised by mistake but was refused employment later - When an advertisement mentions a particular qualification and an appointment is made in disregard of same, it is not a matter only between appointing authority and appointee concerned aggrieved are all those who had similar or even better qualifications than appointee or appointees but who had not applied for post because they did not possess qualifications mentioned in advertisement - It amounts to a fraud on public - Appeal partly allowed.
