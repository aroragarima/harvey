Narsing Prasad v Anil Kumar Jain & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

27 March 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 119; (2012) 4 SCC 642; AIR 2012 SC 1569; 2012 (95) ALR 122; 2012 (4) AWC 3766; 2012 (133) FLR 400; JT 2012 (4) SC 37; 2012(4) SCALE 18; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 177; 2012 (3) SLR 848; 2012 (2) UPLBEC 879

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Avas Evam Vikas Prishad (Appointment and Conditions of Service of Chief Engineer) Regulations, 1990

Summary: Service - Uttar Pradesh Avas Evam Vikas Prishad (Appointment and Conditions of Service of Chief Engineer) Regulations, 1990, regns. 2, 7, 11 - - Appointment - Seniority - Eligibility - Post Chief Engineer fell vacant and Parishad, after deliberation, appointed appellant to officiate as Chief Engineer - Respondent invoked extraordinary jurisdiction of the HC challenging the said appointment on many a ground that he was senior in cadre of Superintending Engineer and, therefore, charge should have been given to him - HC held that at no point of time, criteria of merit was considered before passing order, in absence of merit selection, a senior most person was entitled to hold charge unless there was any legal impediment - HC quashed the order passed by the Parishad and directed that in case any officiating arrangement was to be made, respondent's case should be first considered and he should be given the charge unless there was any legal impediment till the regular selection was made - Aggrieved instant Appeal - Whether HC was justified in expressing view that respondent no. 1 was entitled to hold post of Chief Engineer till regular selection was made on ground that he was senior - Held, it was not clear that finding of selection committee was brought to notice of HC - First time, a document showing order of respondent No. 2 had been brought before SC - Respondent No. 2 was Chairman of Parishad, regn. 8 of Regulations laid down procedure for selection for promotion - Regn. 11 stipulated for preparation of list by selection committee which was required to be constituted by Board as per regn. 7 - It appeared that decision was taken by Chairman but not by Board - In interest of administration, someone had to remain in charge, Parishad should choose someone to hold officiating charge - It was made clear that decision in favour of any candidate to hold additional charge would not inure to his benefit and no claim should be put forth on said base at time of consideration for regular promotion - Appeal allowed.
