Municipal Corporation of Greater Mumbai and others v Kohinoor CTNL Infrastructure Company Private Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 852; (2014) 4 SCC 538; 2014(2) ALL MR 422; 2014 (1) Bom.C.R. 586; JT 2014 (2) SC 40; 2014 (5) MahLJ 100; 2013(15) SCALE 379

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Development Control Regulations for Greater Mumbai, 1991, Maharashtra Fire Prevention and Life Safety Measures Act, 2006, Building permission

Summary: Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 21 - Development Control Regulations for Greater Mumbai, 1991, regns. 23, 31(1), 38(34), 38(34)(iv), 43(1)(A), ss. 33(7), (8), (9) - Maharashtra Fire Prevention and Life Safety Measurers Act, 2006 - Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning Act, 1966, ss. 51 - Building permission - Height of the building - Impact of construction - Exemptions - Legality - Appellant (Corporation) issued stop work notice to the Respondents (construction company) and restricted respondent to a height of a ground floor and 4 upper floors in deviation of the permission granted earlier - Aggrieved respondent filed a petition before HC - DB by impugned order allowed petition and quashed the stop work notice issued by appellant and order passed by Addl. Municipal Commissioner - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by DB was justified - Held, second proviso to (Development Control Regulations) DCR 43(1)(A) was discriminatory as against the occupants of plots up to the size of 600 sq. mts. and thus violative of art. 14 of Constitution - Provision was likely to lead to a hazardous situation, affecting the life of occupants, and was violative of art. 21 of Constitution - Thus, the said provision to be bad in law - If the fire was to be extinguished at the earliest the fire-engine should be able to reach the spot of fire, without any delay - Maneuverability of the fire engine was of utmost importance - If there was no adequate space for fire engine to enter the property, situation would become worse - Even for redevelopment proposals of plots up to the size of 600 sq. mts. under DCR 33(7), an open space of width of 6 meters within the property which was accessible from the road on one side, would have to be maintained unless the building abuts roads of 6 meters or more on two sides, or another appropriate access of 6 meters to the building was available apart from the abutting road - That would be subject to the decision of Chief Fire Officer in writing - It was necessary to direct that fire department should insist from the developer/society of all the buildings, to certify at least once in 6 months that the access to building, the internal exits and internal firefighting arrangements were maintained as per the expectations under the DCR - The provision under DCR 38(34) could not be read in derogation to one under DCR 23 with respect to the recreational area, and also that second proviso to DCR 43(1)(A) on fire protection requirements was hazardous and discriminatory against the occupants of the schemes under DCR 33(7), it was noted that any such declaration/changes be implemented with prospective effect, namely, where the commencement certificate (CC) has yet not been granted - Exemptions from DCR 31(1) for schemes u/ss. 33(7) to (9) of DCR, though apparently meant for laudable purpose, were very often resulting into extreme crowding, and traffic congestion - It was necessary that while granting exemptions from DCR 31(1), there should be a scheme-wise approach, and there ought to be a proper supervision of the construction - Those development schemes and additional FSI (Floor space index) thereunder, should be examined locality-wise - The impact of such high-rise buildings on the adjoining locality as well as on the traffic, was required to be examined before granting such permission - There was a need to restrict the additional pressure on existing infrastructure so that it did not affect the quality of life - Thus, State Govt., planning Authority, and committee entrusted with drafting of new plan should consider contribution by the existing occupants themselves to a good extent towards the construction cost, or State should contribute through its agencies or from the amount of cess-collected - That would result into curtailing the number of additional entrants and would not add to the density of the population - That approach should particularly be examined where the plots were small or are in congested areas, and particularly where the proposal was under DCR 33(7) - Minimum recreational space as laid down under DCR 23, could not be reduced on the basis of DCR 38(34) - Recreational space, if any, provided on the podium as per DCR 38(34)(iv), should be in addition to that provided as per DCR 23 - Govt. was directed to re-constitution the technical committee for high-rise buildings - Appeal disposed of.
