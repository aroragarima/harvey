Kanhiyalal Omar v R.K. Trivedi and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 264; (1985) 4 SCC 628; AIR 1986 SC 111; 1985(2) SCALE 1370; [1985] supp3 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Delimitation Of Constituencies

Summary: Election -Constitution of India , 1950, art. 324 - Conduct of Election Rules, 196, rr. 5 and 10 - Election Symbols (Reservation and Allotment) Order, 1968 - Petitioner challenges constitutional validity of Election Symbols (Reservation and Allotment) Order, 1968 - (A)Whether Symbols Order which is legislative in character could not have been issued by Commission because commission is not entrusted by law, power to issue such an order regarding specification, reservation and allotment of symbols that may be chosen by candidates at elections in parliamentary and assembly constituencies? - Election commission is empowered to issue Symbols Order - Held, when Commission issued Symbols Order it cannot be said that it was not doing so on its own behalf but as delegate of some other authority - The power to issue Symbols Order is comprehended in power of superintendence, direction and control of elections vested in Commission - (B) Whether art. 324 of Constitution which vests power of superintendence, direction and control of all elections to Parliament and to Legislature of a State in Commission can be construed as conferring power on Commission to issue Symbols Order ? - Art. 324 of Constitution operates in areas left unoccupied by legislation and words 'superintendence', 'direction' and 'control' as well as 'conduct of all elections' are broadest terms which would include power to make all such provisions as Sybold order - Even if for any reason, any of provisions contained in Symbols Order are not traceable to Representation of the People Act or Conduct of Election Rules, power of Commission u/art. 324 (1) of Constitution which is plenary in character can encompass all such provisions- Further held, any part of Symbols Order which cannot be traced to rr. 5 and 10 of Conduct of Election Rules can easily be traced in this case to reservoir of power u/art. 324(1) which empowers Commission to issue all directions necessary for purpose of conducting smooth, free and fair elections - (C) Whether reference to is unauthorised and against political system adopted by our country? - Tenth Schedule to Constitution which is added by above Amending Act acknowledges existence of political parties and sets out circumstances when a member of Parliament or State Legislature would be deemed to have defected from his political party and would thereby be disqualified for being a member of House concerned - Further held, reference to recognition, registration etc., of political parties by Symbols Order is not unauthorized and against political system adopted by our country - Petition dismissed.
