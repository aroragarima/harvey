Sanjay Batham v Munnalal Parihar And Others

Supreme Court of India

01 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 763; 2011 (4) ACC 725; 2011 ACJ 2869; AIR 2012 SC 459; 2012 (2) ALD(SC) 153; 2012 (1) AWC 196; 2012 (2) MahLJ 489; 2012 (2) MPLJ 4; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 937

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act 1988, s. 166 - Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection of Rights and Full Participation) Act 1995 s. 2(i) - Enhancement of Compensation - Accident - Disability - Appellant was met with an accident and sustained grievous injuries - Appellant filed a petition u/s. 166 of the 1988 Act for award of compensation with interest before Tribunal - Tribunal held, accident was caused due to rash and negligent driving by respondent no. 1 and appellant had suffered 45% temporary disability and awarded compensation with interest at 9% per annum - On appeal, Single Judge assessed disability of appellant to be 50% and increased compensation but reduced rate of interest from 9 % to 7 % per annum - Hence, instant appeal - Whether compensation awarded by HC was just and reasonable or appellant was entitled to higher compensation - Held, any of the disabilities enumerated in s. 2(i) of the 1995 Act was the result of injuries sustained in a motor accident were permanent disabilities to claim compensation - HC committed an error by applying the multiplier of 16 ignoring that at the time of accident, appellant's age was only 20 years - Hence, multiplier of 18 deserves to be applied for the purpose of determining the compensation payable to appellant in lieu of the loss of earning - Award made by HC for pain, suffering and trauma and in lieu of loss of the prospects of marriage was wholly inadequate - Appellant, suffered paralysis on left part of the body and was neither be able to work as a labourer nor was be able to lead a normal life - Appellant marriage prospects was also bleak - Thus, it was apposite to award reasonable and just compensation to appellant for pain, suffering and trauma caused due to the accident and loss of amenities and enjoyment of life - Impugned judgement of HC was modified - Appeal allowed.
