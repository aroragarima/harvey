Anil Kak v Kumari Sharada Raje and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 761; (2008) 7 SCC 695; AIR 2008 SC 2195; 2008 (3) AWC 2388; JT 2008 (5) SC 589; 2008 (5) MLJ(SC) 682; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 918; 2009 (1) RLW 760; 2008(6) SCALE 597; [2008] 6 S.C.R. 1009

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Distribution Of Assets, Executors, Execution Of Document, Grand Children, Grand Daughter, Collateral, Per Capita

Summary: Applications for grant of probate/ letters of administration with the copy of the annexed Will in respect of the assets of Late Maharani Sharmishthabai Holkar, widow of Late Maharaja Tukoji Rao Holkar, former ruler of the erstwhile Holkar State dismissed - Appeal against - Held, division has not been made per stripe or per capita but by species - Each one of the jewelleries which was to be bequeathed to each of the beneficiary thereunder had specifically been specified - From the valuation report, it would appear that the respective distribution purported to have been made in terms of the appendices would not make them of equal value or nearabout which was the desire of the testatrix - High Court has assigned good and cogent reasons in support of its judgment for not accepting the evidence - Appendices which were required to be read as a part of the main Will so as to effectuate the intention of the testatrix have not been proved - No case has been made out for interference with the impugned judgment - Appeals dismissed.
