Kaushalya Devi and Others v Baijnath Sayal and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 184; AIR 1961 SC 790; 1962 (1) SCJ 684; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 769

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 32, r. 797 - Whether non-compliance with r. 7(1) of CPC, 1908 makes agreement void against minor? - Held, impugned agreement can be avoided by minor against parties who are major - It cannot be avoided by parties who are major against minor - Non-observance of r. 7(1) of CPC, 1908 makes agreement or decree only voidable at instance of minor and not void against him - (B) Can final decree be appealed against if preliminary decree had not been appealed against in first instance? - Further held, u/s. 97 of CPC, 1908 any party feeling aggrieved by a preliminary decree must appeal against that decree - If he fails to appeal against such a decree correctness of such a decree cannot be challenged by way of an appeal against final decree, which means that preliminary decree would be taken to have been correctly passed - Appeal dismissed.
