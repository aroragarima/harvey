Rani Gupta & Ors v M/S. United India Insurance Co. Ltd. & Ors

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 448; (2009) 13 SCC 498; 2009 (2) ACC 333; AIR 2009 SC 3226; 2009 (4) ALD(SC) 1; 2009 (75) ALR 651; 2009 (2) AWC 1894; 2009 (2) JhrCR(SC) 128; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 900; 2009 (4) RLW 2852; 2009(5) SCALE 439; [2009] 5 S.C.R. 721

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Future Pecuniary Benefit, Pecuniary Benefit, Higher Multiplier, Damage To Property, Gratuitous Passenger, No Fault Liability, Carried For Hire Or Reward, Death Or Injury, Life Expectancy, Liability To Third Party

Summary: Insurance - Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Applicability of multiplier method for the purpose of calculating the amount of compensation - Held, in a given case even in terms of the Second Schedule where the compensation is payable on the basis of a no fault liability, the amount of compensation may be higher than the one which has been specified in the Second Schedule in case of a fault liability - Deceased was a businessman - What was the actual loss of dependency to the family was his contribution to run the business - Assets of the business remained - Amount of compensation, therefore, was required to be determined keeping in view that factor in mind - Application of the multiplier of 10, therefore, cannot be said to be bad in law - Appeal dismissed.
