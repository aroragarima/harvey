Commissioner Of Customs & Central Excise v M.P. Steel Corporation

Supreme Court of India

12 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1119; (2004) 13 SCC 357; 2003 (154) E.L.T. 12; 2003 (56) RLT 605

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: CEGAT

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, ss. 128 and 129A - Declaration in Bill of Entry - Not accepted - Calculation of light displacement tonnage (LDT) - Internal correspondence between Asst. Collector and Collector on calculation of LDT - Order of Superintendent of Customs and Central Excise - Whether appeal was maintainable before CEGAT as Superintendent's order is nothing more than a communication of order passed by Collector (Appeals)? - Held, reasoning of CEGAT cannot be sustained - Decision taken by Collector was not in his capacity as Collector (Appeals) - Also order by which respondent is aggrieved is order passed by Superintendent - An appeal against that order has to be filed before Commr. (Appeals) u/s. 128 - By virtue of s. 129A, CEGAT has no jurisdiction to entertain such an appeal - Therefore impugned order is passed without any jurisdiction and cannot be sustained - Order is thus set aside - Appeal allowed.
