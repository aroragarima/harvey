Sahabuddin and another v State of Assam

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 544; (2012) 13 SCC 213; 2013 CRLJ 1252; JT 2012 (12) SC 436; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 817; 2012(12) SCALE 241; [2012] 13 S.C.R. 1067

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Poisoning

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304(B), 302, 34 - Dowry Harassment - Death - Interested Witnesses - Reliability - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant-accused-husband was married to deceased and deceased had complaint to her parents about harassment by appellant and his family members for demand of dowry - Deceased was found dead with swollen neck, black marks on abdomen and suspecting foul play mother of deceased lodged FIR and case under section 304(B) of IPC was registered - Court of Competent jurisdiction held that on basis of police report found that prima facie case u/ss. 302,34 of IPC was made out and put appellants to trial - Trial Court appreciating evidence on record convicted appellants - Aggrieved appellant appealed before HC, which was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that story of prosecution was improbable and that they had not established its case beyond reasonable doubt and witnesses were all interested witnesses - Whether Trial Court was justified in convicting appellant - Held, an interested witness was one who was desirous of falsely implicating accused with an intention of ensuring their conviction, merely being a relative would not make statement of such witness equivalent to that of an interested witness and statement of a related witness could safely be relied upon by Court, as long as it was trustworthy, truthful and duly corroborated by other prosecution evidence - Once, presence of PW2 and PW3 was shown to be natural, then to doubt their statement would not be a correct approach in law, it had unequivocally come on record through various witnesses including PW4 that there was a 'Satyanarayan Katha' which was attended by various villagers and that on their way back at midnight when PW2 and PW3 had seen occurrence in dark with help of torches that they were carrying - Fact that PW2 happened to be related to PW1 and to deceased, would not result in doubting statement of these witnesses which otherwise had credence, and were reliable and were duly corroborated by other evidence - It was only members of family who come forward to depose, once it was established that their depositions do not suffer from material contradictions, were trustworthy and in consonance with principles, Court would not be justified in overlooking such valuable piece of evidence - Hence, there was no doubt that statements of PWs were reliable and trustworthy, as they were fully corroborated by other prosecution, documentary and ocular evidence - Appeal dismissed.
