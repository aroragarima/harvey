Ghasi Ram v Dal Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 February 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 232; AIR 1968 SC 1191; 1968 (2) SCJ 558; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 102

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Election - Representation of Peoples Act, 1951, ss. 116-A and 123 - Whether certain corrupt practices committed by answering respondent? - Held, corrupt practice which is a charge quasi-criminal in nature, must be proved like any other fact and should be fully established and gift or promise must be proved to have a direct or indirect connection with votes - Gift must admit of no other reasonable excuse - Dividing line between an evil practice and a corrupt practice is a very thin one - It should be understood that energy to do public good should be used not on eve of elections but much earlier and that even slight evidence might change this evil practices into corrupt practice - There wasn't any corrupt practice - Appeal dismissed.
