Mahesh Dattatray Thirthkar v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

04 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 377; (2009) 11 SCC 141; AIR 2009 SC 2238; 2009 (3) AWC 3077; 2009 (6) Bom.C.R. 354; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 49; 2009 (108) RD 633; 2009(4) SCALE 193; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 1122

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Whether the High Court was justified in reversing the finding of fact arrived at by the Reference Court on reappreciation of evidence under art. 136 of the Constitution of India? - Held, High Court was not justified in interfering with the findings of the Reference Court based on mere suggestions made by the State Respondent and setting aside its order - Findings made by the High Court were arbitrary and improper inasmuch as the High Court had failed to consider the total lack of evidence adduced by the State Respondent - Inconsistencies pointed out by the High Court in the evidence adduced by the appellant are only minor inconsistencies and do not warrant non- reliance on the same - Appeal allowed.
