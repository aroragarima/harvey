Babu Lal v Habibnoor Khan (Dead) By Lrs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 303; (2000) 5 SCC 662; AIR 2000 SC 2684; JT 2000 (5) SC 365; 2000 (3) MLJ(SC) 94; 2000 (3) RCR(Civil) 290; 2001 (92) RD 229; 2000(4) SCALE 313; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 684; 2000 (4) SLT 310; 2000 (4) Supreme 659

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution

Summary: Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 115 - Partition Act, 1893, s. 4 - Whether application moved u/s. 4 of Partition Act, 1893 by respondent 1, who was decree-holder in partition suit, was maintainable in law? - Held, stranger-purchaser, had not filed any application for separating his share from dwelling house, either at stage of preliminary decree or final decree or even thereafter in execution proceedings, therefore, application u/s. 4 of Partition Act has to be treated as not maintainable - Appeal allowed.
