Gurdev Kaur and Others v Kaki and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 637; (2007) 1 SCC 546; AIR 2006 SC 1975; 2007 (2) ALD(SC) 20; 2006 (3) AWC 2373; 2007 (3) Bom.C.R. 869; 2007 (1) CTC 334; JT 2006 (5) SC 72; 2006 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1414; 2007 (2) MPLJ 1; 2006 (2) RCR(Civil) 561; 2007 (1) RLW 636; 2006(3) SCALE 436; 2006(4) SCALE 436; 2006 (5) SCJ 506; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 27; 2006 (2) UC 1315

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Whether, according to true delineated scope of s. 100 CPC, High Court was justified in interfering with concurrent findings of fact? - Impugned judgment shows that no procedure u/s. 100 CPC was followed - Where findings of fact by lower appellate Court are based on evidence, HC in second appeal cannot substitute its own findings on reappreciation of evidence merely on ground that another view was possible - In second appeal, court does not sit in appeal over right or wrong of testator's decision, it's role is limited to examining whether instrument propounded as last Will of deceased is or is not that by testator and whether it is product of free and sound disposing mind - Held, HC seriously erred in interfering with findings of facts arrived at by trial court and affirmed by first appellate court - Appeal allowed.
