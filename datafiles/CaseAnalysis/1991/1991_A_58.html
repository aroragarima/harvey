Hindustan Zinc Limited Etc. Etc. v Andhra Pradesh State Electricity Boards And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, M.N. Venkatachaliah, N.D. Ojha

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 181; (1991) 3 SCC 299; AIR 1991 SC 1473; AIR 1991 SCW 1329; JT 1991 (2) SC 403; 1991(1) SCALE 869; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 643

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Annual Financial Statement, Bonds, Local Authorities Loans Act, 1914

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Constitution - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, ss. 16(5), 49 and 59 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Revision of tariffs - (A) Effect on validity of failing to seek advice of consultative council before revision - (B) Generation of surplus by Board - Criticism - When adjusting tariffs is after amendment of s. 59 by 1978 Act and quantum not yet specified by Govt. - (C) Fuel cost adjustment charges - Classification of consumers and applying charges only to H T consumers - Validity - (D) Revision of tariffs - Judicial review - Imposition of fuel cost adjustment on H.T. consumers - Board claiming fuel cost adjustment formula to be passed on report of committee appointed for that purpose - Claim by Board that formula is on scientific basis linked to increase in fuel cost - (E) Board Memo, D/-18-11-75 - Fuel cost adjustment made applicable to H.T. consumers - Is it applicable to power intensive consumers also - Held, (A) Revised tariffs are not invalidated - (B) Not open to criticism, it has to be shown that Board thereby acted as private trader - (C) H.T. consumers form distinct class, impost not unreasonable nor discriminatory as it not a case of making good loss on supply to others at cheaper rates - (D) Revision of tariffs not within available scope of judicial review - (E) Applicable, as provisions in memo expressly provides for - Appeal dismissed.
