Jugal Kishore Baldeo Sarai v Commissioner of Income Tax, U.P., Lucknow

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 175; AIR 1967 SC 495; [1967] 63 ITR 238; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 416

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act,1922, s.10(2) (xv) - Partnership business -Remuneration for service - Permissible deduction - Expenditure - Assessee /Hindu undivided family carrying on joint family business deriving income from some property and from some partnership business in which A was partner representing interest of Hindu undivided family - Hindu undivided family as assessee claimed sum of Rs.12,000 every year should be deducted as expenditure u/s. 10 (2) (xv) of 1922 Act - Income Tax Officer rejected claim and order was upheld by Appellate Assistant Commissioner as well as by Tribunal - Reference was made to HC - HC concluded that under Hindu Law, karta bound by reason of his being karta to manage all business of family without being entitled to any remuneration for service of management - When under law karta represents family it would be anomalous to think of karta as being employee of himself or being entitled to remuneration for acting as such and receiving payment from his own self - A being karta of family not entitled to draw any remuneration for carrying on business of Hindu undivided family - Whether salary paid or credited to Karta of family for looking after family's business was permissible deduction u/s. 10(2)(xv) in computing income of family business - Held, if remuneration paid to karta of family under valid agreement bonafide and in interest of expedient for business of family and payment was genuine and not excessive such remuneration must be expenditure laid out wholly and exclusively for purpose of business of family and allowed as expenditure u/s. 10(2) (xv) of 1922 Act - Member of joint Hindu family conceivably do business in his individual capacity and render services to joint family trading firm in consideration of which firm might pay him such remuneration as outsider - No indication that payment to A was high or not commensurate with services rendered by him - Agreement does not envisage any payment to A for services rendered to partnership firms - Order passed by HC set aside - Appeals disposed of.
