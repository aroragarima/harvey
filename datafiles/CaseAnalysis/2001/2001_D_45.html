Rajinder Pershad (Dead) By LRs v Darshana Devi Smt

Supreme Court of India

10 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20039; (2001) 7 SCC 69; AIR 2001 SC 3207; 2002 AllCJ 34; 2001 (44) ALR 843; JT 2001 (6) SC 400; 2001 (2) RCR(Rent) 239; 2001(5) SCALE 203; 2001 (4) SCJ 323; 2001 (5) SLT 780; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 442; 2001 (6) Supreme 82; 2001 (2) UC 415

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Rent Control - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 146 - Tenant did not pay rent of suit premises in spite of service of notice - He denied service of demand notice - Rent Controller, Delhi, found that tenant refused to receive notice and there was default in payment of rent and, therefore, ordered eviction of tenant from suit premises - Appeal dismissed - Second appeal - Whether there has been no valid service of notice? - Notice was sent by registered post and same was taken by postman to address of tenant - Postman was examined - Not suggested to him that he was not on duty during period in question and endorsement 'refused' on envelope was incorrect - In absence of cross-examination of postman on this crucial aspect, his statement in chief examination rightly relied upon - If correctness of statement of a witness is disputed opportunity must be given to him to explain his statement by drawing his attention to that part of it which is objected to as untrue, otherwise his credit cannot be impeached - Appeal dismissed.
