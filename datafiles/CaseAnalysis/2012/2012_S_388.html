Raj Paul Singh and another v State through P. S. Musheerabad, Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

09 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 367; (2012) 10 SCC 144; 2012 (4) MLJ(Crl) 352; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 504; 2012(10) SCALE 137; [2012] 8 S.C.R. 1203

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 302 - No premeditation - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Appellants/accused allegedly caused bleeding injury to deceased with knife, as result he died - Trial Court found guilty of appellants and convicted them for offences punishable u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC - On appeal, DB sustained conviction passed by Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Appellants contended that nature of the offence committed by appellants was not murder as defined in s. 300 of IPC, but culpable homicide not amounting to murder u/s. 304 of IPC, for which a punishment less than life imprisonment, might be imposed on the appellants - Further, there was no premeditation on part of appellants and there was a sudden quarrel and a sudden fight and the appellant no. 1 stabbed the deceased in the heat of passion and therefore s. 300, exception 4 of IPC was attracted - Whether Courts below were rightly convicted appellant for offence u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Held, conviction of appellants for the offence u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC was based on the evidence of PW-1 and PW-2, the 2 eye witnesses - It was clear from their evidence that deceased was unarmed and there was absolutely no physical threat from deceased to appellants and appellant no. 1 after being provided with a knife by appellant no. 2 stabbed the deceased on the left side of the chest on the instigation of appellant no. 2 and because of these injuries deceased died - Hence, it was a case where appellants had taken undue advantage and had acted in a cruel or unusual manner - Therefore, instant case did not fall u/s. 300, exception 4 of IPC - Appeal dismissed.
