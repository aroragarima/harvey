M. Mohan and another v The State Represented By The Deputy Superintendent Of Police

Supreme Court of India

01 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 138; (2011) 3 SCC 626; AIR 2011 SC 1238; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 1659; 2011 CRLJ 1900; 2011 (2) JCC 1078; JT 2011 (3) SC 32; 2011 (4) MLJ(Crl) 273; 2011 (2) RCR(Criminal) 272; 2011(3) SCALE 78; [2011] 3 S.C.R. 437

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304-B, 306 and 498-A - Quashing of criminal prosecution - Complaint was filed by father of deceased alleging offence punishable u/ss. 304-B, 498-A and 306 of IPC against appellants - Magistrate summoned the appellants - Appellants, aggrieved by the prosecution, filed petition u/s. 482 of CrPC for quashing the proceedings - Single Judge, while quashing the charges u/ss. 498-A and 304-B of IPC against the appellants, partly allowed their petition and held that they had to face trial for the offence u/s. 306 of IPC - HC held that the A-1 and A-3 were torturing the deceased on some pretext or the other especially in connection with getting a vehicle from her father - Instant appeal - Whether HC erred in not quashing the charge u/s. 306 of IPC? - Held, there was no material on record to proceed against the appellants either for cruelty or for dowry harassment - In view of facts and circumstances of the case, appellants were not even remotely connected with the offence u/s. 306 of the IPC - HC was not justified in rejecting the petition filed by the appellants u/s. 482 of the CrPC for quashing the charges u/s. 306 of IPC against them - Charges u/s. 306 of IPC against the appellants were quashed - Appeal disposed of.
