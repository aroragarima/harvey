Council of The Institute of Chartered Accountants of India v B. Mukherjea

Supreme Court of India

10 September 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 54; AIR 1958 SC 72; 1958 (1) AnWR 81; [1958] 28 Comp Cas 1; 1958 (1) MLJ(SC) 81; 1958 SCJ 312; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 371

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Corporate

Summary: Accounts & Audit - Auditors Certificate Rules, 1932 - Chartered Accountants Act, 1949, ss. 2(2)(iv), 21(3)and 22 - Professional misconduct - Respondent, a registered chartered accountant, appointed as liquidator of three companies by High Court of Calcutta - Charged with 'gross professional misconduct' - Respondent held guilty of gross negligence in conduct of his professional duty in not handing over charge of assets and books of account of such companies to newly appointed liquidator, by Disciplinary Committee of Council of Institute of Chartered Accountants of India(ICAI) - Question as to nature, scope and extent of disciplinary jurisdiction which can be exercised under provisions of the Act, 1949 against respondent? - Conduct of respondent as liquidator whether conduct of chartered accountant under CA Act - Provisions of Act and Regulations thereunder, perused - Held, yes - Conduct of respondent while he discharges his duties as a liquidator is professional conduct of a chartered accountant - Expression 'to be in practice' has been deliberately and artificially extended by virtue of s. 2(2)(iv) - Broader interpretation has to be given to provisions of Act, which is a self contained code in respect of chartered accountants - Further held, respondent is guilty of professional misconduct under CA Act - Order passed as to that respondent's name be removed from Register for four years - Order accordingly.
