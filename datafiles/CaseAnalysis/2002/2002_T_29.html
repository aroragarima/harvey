Jhareswar Prasad Paul and another v Tarak Nath Ganguly and others

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 333; (2002) 5 SCC 352; (2002) SCC (L&S) 703; AIR 2002 SC 2215; 2002 (4) ALD(SC) 64; 2002 (2) CCR 249; 2002 (2) Crimes 430; 2003 (4) CRJ 150; 2002 CRLJ 2935; 2002 (2) JCC 1085; JT 2002 (Supp1) SC 290; 2002 (2) RCR(Criminal) 835; 2002(4) SCALE 546; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 913; 2002 (4) SLT 56; 2002 (4) Supreme 349

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Challenged, Non-compliance of order, Contempt jurisdiction

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 215 - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 - Non-compliance of order - Contempt jurisdiction - Challenged - DB of HC held that appellants/respondents had not complied with order of DB holding that respondents should be given benefits due in terms of GO effectively and in appropriate manner - Hence, instant appeals - Appellants contended that HC was in error inasmuch as judgment of DB did not contain any specific order or direction for treating ex-cadre posts of Clerk-cum-Cash Collector as posts in cadre of Lower Division Clerk/Assistant.

Held, the directions issued by the DB in the impugned judgment in effect granted substantive reliefs not covered by the judgment/order passed in the original proceeding. In the judgment no direction was issued by the HC that the respondents will be admitted to the cadre of Upper Division Clerks/Assistants in the Directorate. Such a dispute can only be determined on consideration of all relevant aspects of the matter and cannot be and should not be ordered in the summary proceeding for taking action for contempt of court. Further the question of entry of holders of ex-cadre posts, like the respondents, into an existing cadre is a matter of policy which the Government has to decide. It was not open to the court to pass any order granting substantive relief to the respondents on the plea that the question raised was also a part of their grievance in the writ petition. Judgment/order passed by the HC was without jurisdiction. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Court exercising contempt jurisdiction is not entitled to enter into questions which have not been dealt with and decided in judgment or order.
