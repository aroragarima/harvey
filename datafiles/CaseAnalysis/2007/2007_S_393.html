Abbas Ali v State Of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

15 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 125; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 80; (2007) 9 SCC 129; AIR 2007 SC 1239; 2007 (3) ALT 75; 2007 CRLJ 1667; JT 2007 (3) SC 373; 2007 (2) RCR(Criminal) 870; 2007(3) SCALE 198; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 714

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Pelting Stones

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Conviction and sentence of life imprisonment - Appeal against - Which was the appropriate provision to be applied? - Held, it will be sufficient to say that clause (4) of s. 300 would be applicable where the knowledge of the offender as to the probability of death of a person or persons in general as distinguished from a particular person or persons being caused from his imminently dangerous act, approximates to a practical certainty - Appropriate conviction is under s. 304 Part I - Custodial sentence of 10 years imposed - Appeal partly allowed.
