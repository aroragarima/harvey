Anil Kumar Srivastava v State Of U.P. & Another

Supreme Court of India

20 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 645; (2004) 8 SCC 671; AIR 2004 SC 4299; 2004 ALJ 3247; 2004 (4) AWC 3037; 2004 (4) CompLJ 576; JT 2004 (7) SC 35; 2005 (139) PLR 456; 2004(7) SCALE 66; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 675; 2005 (1) UPLBEC 209

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Constitution - Land and Property - Constitution, art. 14 - Scheme by New Okhla Industrial Development Authority (NOIDA) for construction of a commercial hub on a plot - Challenge to scheme as arbitrary and violative of norms contained in Board Resolution - Loss to State exchequer - Whether tender price of Rs 31, 850/- Per sq mtr is understated? - Held, when a Scheme is challenged it has to be looked as an entire package - Tender price, the cost of putting up amenities like ECS, the cost-benefit ratio, the future projections in terms of increase in revenue, employment etc to be looked into - None of these facts have been brought out - Petition dismissed.
