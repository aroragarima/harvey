Woolcombers of India Limited v Woolcombers Workers Union and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 August 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Dwivedi, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 183; (1973) SCC (L&S) 551; (1974) 3 SCC 318; AIR 1973 SC 2758; 1973 (27) FLR 380; 1973 LabIC 1613; 1974 (1) LLJ 138; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 504; 1973 SLJ 984

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 11,Chap. 3, item 1 - (A) Industrial Tribunal - Award - Need for reasons in support of conclusions - Absence of reasons Effect - (B) Wages - Minimum wage, fair wage and living wage - Meaning - Fixation of basic wage and dearness allowance -Absence of documentary evidence - Held, serious flaw in an award, however award cannot be set aside simply on that score, if there is evidence on record in support of tribunal's conclusions - Further held, absence of documentary evidence was a fatal defect, and to avoid further delay Supreme Court called for a fresh finding from tribunal in regard to basic wages and dearness allowance - Order accordingly.
