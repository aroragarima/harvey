Kumar Jagdish Chandra Sinha (By LRs) v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3500; (1996) 9 SCC 257; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 496; AIR 1996 SC 1895; 1996 (133) CTR 143; [1996] 220 ITR 67; JT 1996 (4) SC 709; [1996] Supp1 S.C.R. 657; 1996 (5) Supreme 194; [1996] 86 TAXMAN 122; 1996 (2) TLR 494

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 139(4), 139(5), 153(1)(b), 153(1)(c), 271(1)(c) - Assessment Order - Limitation - Penalty proceedings - Legality - Appellant/assessee did not file returns within prescribed period for two A/Ys, 1964-65 and 1965-66 but he filed returns u/s. 139(4) and revised returns u/s. 139(5) in respect of two assessment years - Income Tax Officer (ITO) did not complete assessment within four years in respect of both assessment years - ITO made assessment order in respect of A/Y 1964-65 & 1965-66 after expiry of four years and initiated penalty proceedings against appellant in respect of both assessment years - Appellant preferred appeal before Appellate Assistant Commissioner challenging Orders of assessment in respect of both assessments - Appellate Assistant Commissioner allowed appeals - Revenue challenged decision before Tribunal - Tribunal allowed appeals of revenue - At the request of appellant and revenue, Tribunal reffered question of law u/s. 256(1) to HC - HC held that Tribunal was right in holding that on facts and circumstances of the case, larger period of eight years provided by s. 153(1)(b) of the ? was also attracted in instant case and on this account also assessment orders must be held to have been made within period of limitation prescribed by law - Hence instant appeals - Appellant contended that revised returns filed by him were inadmissible in law and therefore could not serve to extend period for marking assessment as provided by s. 153(1)(c) of the Act - Held, s. 153(1)(c) employ both expressions return and revised return and refers to both sub-sections (4) and (5) of Section 139 - Reasonably read it would mean return filed u/s. 139(4) of the Act and revised return filed u/s. 139(5) of the Act - It would not be reasonable to construe said clause as indirectly conferring right which was not conferred directly by s. 139(5) - HC drew distinction between revised return and rectified return - Might be, there was distinction and SC would not be concerned with rectified return but what was avowedly revised return and which was in truth new return - It would be difficult to agree with rest of reasoning of HC on this aspect - Therefore, no revised return could be filed u/s. 139(5) in case where return was filed u/s. 139 (4) of the Act - Once this was so revised returns filed by appellant for both said assessment years were not valid in law and could not have been treated and acted upon as revised returns contemplated by s. 139(5) which meant s. 153(1)(c) was not attracted in instant case - Further, to attract larger period of eight years limitation, case should fall within s. 271(1)(c) of the Act - For a case to fall u/s. 271(1)(c) of the Act, ITO, within period of four years of A/Y, must either initiate proceedings u/s. 271(1)(c) of the Act or record his opinion that it was case falling u/s. 271(1)(c) and unless any such step was taken, it should not be said that it was case falling u/s. 271(1)(c) of the Act - Admittedly, ITO had not initiated proceedings u/s. 271(1)(c) of the Act within period of four years prescribed by s. 153(1)(a)(i) nor had he made any order or record or note in relevant file indicating that it was case falling u/s. 271(1)(c) of the Act - If he made any such order of note in file, he should have communicated it to appellant - In such situation it should be held that orders of assessment in respect of both assessment orders concerned herein were barred by time and must be held to be invalid in law - Appeals allowed.
