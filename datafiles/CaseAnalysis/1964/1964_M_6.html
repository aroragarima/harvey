State of Orissa v M.A. Tulloch and Company

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 325; AIR 1966 SC 365; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 816; 1964 (15) STC 641

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947, ss.5(2)(a)(ii)and 12(2); Orissa Sales Tax Rules, 1947, r. 27(2) - Respondent filed a return - Claimed deduction u/s. 5(2)(a)(ii) - Two assessment orders passed u/s. 12(2)- Respondent challenged assessment by tax authorities-revisions- Meanwhile amendment of Orissa Sales Tax Act - Effect was that revisions were treated as appeals to Sales Tax Tribunal, and it enabled Govt. to file cross-objections - Tribunal directed fresh assessment - Whether production of declaration u/r. 27 (2) of Orissa Sales Tax Rules is obligatory on part of a selling dealer when claming exemption? - If sales tax officer is satisfied from other facts on record, it is not necessary that selling dealer should produce a declaration in form required in r. 27 (2) -Held, production of declaration u/r. 27 (2) is not always obligatory on part of a selling dealer when claiming exemption It is open to him to claim exemption by adducing other evidence so as to bring transaction within scope of s. 5 (2) (a) (ii) of Act,1961- Appeal dismissed.
