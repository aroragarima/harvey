Kiran Gupta and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 480; (2000) 7 SCC 719; (2000) SCC (L&S) 1020; AIR 2000 SC 3299; 2000 (41) ALR 453; 2000 (4) AWC 3223; JT 2000 (Supp1) SC 105; 2000 LabIC 3331; 2000(6) SCALE 552; 2000 (8) SLR 753; 2000 (7) SLT 205; [2000] Supp3 S.C.R. 474; 2000 (6) Supreme 555; 2000 (3) UPLBEC 2765

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Ad Hoc Appointment

Summary: Service - Uttar Pradesh Intermediate Education Act, 1921, ss. 16 E and 16 F - Uttar Pradesh Secondary Education Services Commission and Selection Board Act, 1982, ss. 9 and 10 - Uttar Pradesh Secondary Education Services Commission (Amendment) Rules, 1995, r. 12 - Whether non regularisation of senior teachers as headmasters/ principals was valid? - Held, where only one senior teacher from an institution/school participated in interview and he secured higher marks, he was accordingly selected - But where a general candidate secured higher marks than senior teachers of institution/school he was not selected and a tick - mark was put against name of general candidate, which does not indicate that he was necessarily selected for same institution/school - It is proper to observe that regularisation of candidates u/s. 33 - C(1)(a)(ii) is made to depend on a mere chance of a substantive vacancy either being filled in or selection for that vacancy being completed - Appeal dismissed.
