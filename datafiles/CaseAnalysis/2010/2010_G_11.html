Alva Aluminium Ltd. Bangkok v Gabriel India Limited

Supreme Court of India

16 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 965; (2011) 1 SCC 167; 2011 (84) ALR 742; 2011 (1) AWC 65; JT 2010 (12) SC 210; 2011 (1) MLJ(SC) 811; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 849; 2010(12) SCALE 93; 2010 (4) WLN(SC) 74

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 2(f), 11 - International Commercial Arbitration - Appointment of arbitrator - Petitioner/Company entered into an agreement of sale with the respondent/Company - Respondent failed to open the requisite Letter of Credit despite repeated requests and reminders sent by the petitioner/Company - Petitioner send a legal notice to respondent claiming damages - Respondent replied that the contract referred to by the petitioner had not been signed by an authorized person of respondent, hence no valid contract and arbitration agreement existed between the parties - Whether the SC in a petition u/ss. 11(5) and 11(9) of the Act is required to determine the existence of an arbitration agreement between the parties - Held, once the existence of the arbitration agreement itself is questioned by any party to the proceeding initiated u/s. 11 of the Act, the same will have to be decided by the Chief Justice/designate as the case may be since the existence of an arbitration agreement is a jurisdictional fact which will have to be addressed while making an order on a petition u/s. 11 of the Act - Power available to the Arbitral Tribunal u/s. 16 of the Act does not imply that the issue can be or ought to be left to be determined by the Arbitral Tribunal even in cases where one of the parties has filed a petition u/s. 11 of the Act and the other party opposes the making of a reference on the ground that there exists no arbitration agreement between them - Therefore, the question whether or not an arbitration agreement exists between the parties will have to be answered for and it is only if the answer to that question is in the affirmative that the Chief Justice or his designate can pass an order of reference of the disputes for adjudication - (B) Whether any agreement has been executed between the parties in the present case to call for the appointment of an arbitrator for adjudication of the disputes and differences that have arisen between them - Held, existence of written contract between the parties and existence of an arbitration clause therein was not in dispute - Correspondence between the parties exchanged before signing of the document indicated that the person who signed the contract on behalf of respondent was the competent authority to sign the document - Correspondent exchanged and the documents executed suggested that the parties had finalized and signed the contract - A heavy duty lies upon the party who seeks to avoid a contract on the ground of misrepresentation, fraud or coercion to prove any such allegation, however, respondent did not place on record any charter of duties and powers of the signatory of the contract nor it chosen to place on record any material to suggest that any action was indeed taken against the signatory for the alleged transgression of the limits of his authority and if so the nature of the disciplinary action taken against him - Petitioner/Company had in any event no reason to believe or even suspect that signatory with whom it was dealing did not have the authority to sign the contract which was finalized between the two companies acting through their representatives - If such signatory was competent to negotiate the terms of the contract, the petitioner cannot be said to have induced or defrauded him into signing of the contract, which was forwarded to the respondent and which was returned duly signed by the signatory for and on behalf of respondent - Therefore, petitioner was justified in proceeding on the basis that the contract was duly negotiated and signed on behalf of the respondent/Company and a legally valid contract had indeed come into existence between the parties which contained an arbitration clause for adjudication of disputes that may arise between them - Arbitrator is appointed - Petition allowed.
