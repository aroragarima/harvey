Sharma Agro Industries and another v State of Haryana and others

Supreme Court of India

12 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 896; (2015) 3 SCC 341; 2015 (3) ALD(SC) 69; 2015 (1) AWC 199; 2015 (3) CalLT(SC) 1; JT 2014 (13) SC 490; 2015 (5) MahLJ 34; 2015 (3) MPLJ 523; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 266; 2014(12) SCALE 681

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 6, 11 - Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013, s. 24(2) - Land acquisition - Failure to take possession - Effect - Appellant's industrial land was proposed to be acquired by respondent State for public purpose - However, Land Acquisition Collector (LAC) recommended not to acquire land in question - State did not consider recommendation and issued declaration u/s. 6 of the 1894 Act - Later award was also passed u/s. 11 of the 1894 Act - Appellant challenged acquisition before HC - HC dismissed appellant's writ petition - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, award was made 5 years prior to the date of commencement of the 2013 Act but possession was not taken. Therefore, the acquisition proceedings of the land of the appellants have lapsed in view of s. 24(2) of the 2013 Act. Further, the report of LAC was neither accepted by the Govt. nor did the Govt. assign any reasons before issuing the declaration by holding that the land is required for public purpose, hence, the acquisition proceedings are vitiated in law. The report submitted by LAC in respect of the proposed acquisition of the land of the appellants, the State Govt. has to consider the report while issuing declaration of the acquisition of the land u/s. 6 of the 1894 Act. If the Govt. comes to the conclusion contrary to the report of the LAC then the Govt. shall assign valid and cogent reasons for not accepting the same. Therefore, the declaration by the State Govt. issued without accepting the recommendation of the LAC and without assigning reasons for not accepting the same is bad in law. Hence, impugned judgment is set aside. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Land acquisition proceedings initiated are deemed to have lapsed where an award has been made five years or more prior to the commencement of 2013 Act and possession of the land is not taken or compensation has not been paid to the land owners.
