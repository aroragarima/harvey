Brij Lal v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 173; (2002) SCC (Cr) 245; (2002) 1 SCC 700; AIR 2002 SC 291; 2001 (4) CCR 353; 2002 (1) Crimes 200; 2002 (2) CRJ 785; 2002 CRLJ 581; 2002 (1) JCC 8; JT 2001 (10) SC 510; 2001(8) SCALE 359; 2001 (8) SLT 277; 2001 (8) Supreme 531; 2002 (1) UC 242

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Murder, Conviction, Sustainability

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 307, 34 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - A case was registered against accused appellant u/ss. 302, 307 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Trial Court passed conviction order - On appeal before HC the same was dismissed and order passed by Trial Court was confirmed - Hence instant appeal.

Held, evidence of two eyewitnesses clearly showed that appellant first shot deceased and there was a second shot at PW4. It is true that PW3 deposed that appellant first shot his brother deceased on backside of the skull. But medical evidence shows that this shot hit on eye brow. Based on this, it was contended that it was not appellant but somebody else hiding on rear side must have caused this injury. SC do not find any force in this contention. Incident happened all of a sudden and when firing took place it would be difficult to state on which part of body bullet hit. In the instant case, evidence of PW4 shows that he himself sustained an injury at hands of appellant. It is clearly proved that it was appellant and none else who was responsible for the crime. Minor discrepancies in evidence only lend assurance to credibility of prosecution case. Appeal dismissed.
