Islamic Academy of Education and Another v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, K.G. Balakrishnan, Arijit Pasayat, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 611; (2003) 6 SCC 697; AIR 2003 SC 3724; AIR 2003 SCW 4240; 2003 (5) ALD(SC) 1; 2003 (4) AWC 3119; 2004 (1) Bom.C.R. 593; 2003 (4) ESC 509; JT 2003 (7) SC 1; 2003(6) SCALE 325; 2003 (5) SLR 1; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 474; 2003 (6) Supreme 303; 2003 (3) UPLBEC 2424

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Summary: [A.] Education - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 142, 30, 30(1) - Unaided professional educational institutions - Whether educational institutions are entitled to fix their own fee structure - Whether minority and non-minority educational institutions stand on same footing and have same rights - Whether private unaided professional colleges are entitled to fill in their seats, to extent of 100%, and if not to what extent - Whether private unaided, professional colleges are entitled to admit students by evolving their own method of admission - Held, there can be no fixing of a rigid fee structure by Govt. - Each institute must have freedom to fix its own fee structure taking into consideration need to generate funds to run institution and to provide facilities necessary for benefit of students - Non-minority educational institutions do not have protection of art.30 - Thus, in certain matters they cannot and do not stand on similar footing as minority educational institutions - Different percentage of quota for students to be admitted by management in each minority or non-minority unaided professional college/s shall be separately fixed on basis of their need by respective State Govt, and in case of any dispute as regards fixation of percentage of quota, it will be open to management to approach Committee - No institute, which has not been established and which has not followed its own admission procedure for lat, at least, 25 years, shall be permitted to apply for or be granted exemption from admitting students in manner set out hereinabove.
