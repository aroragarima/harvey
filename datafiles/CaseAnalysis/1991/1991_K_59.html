Vimala (K.) v Veeraswamy (K.)

Supreme Court of India

20 March 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Fathima Beevi, A.M. Ahmadi, Veeraswami Ramaswami

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 582; (1991) 2 SCC 375; (1991) SCC (Cr) 442; AIR 1991 SCW 754; 1991 (17) ALR 466; 1991 (1) Crimes 828; 1991 CRLR 434; 1991 CrLR(SC) 434; 1991 (2) DMC 52; 1991 (1) GLH 380; 1991 (2) HLR 186; JT 1991 (2) SC 182; 1991 (2) KarLJ 162; 1991(1) SCALE 495; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 904; 1991 (2) UJ 383

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Woman, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Family & Personal - Women & Children - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 5 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 125 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 101 - Whether appellant eligible to received maintenance? - Respondent claimed that appellant was not his legally wedded wife - Law which disentitles the second wife from receiving maintenance from her husband u/s. 125, CrPC, 1973 for the sole reason that the marriage ceremony though performed in the customary form lacks legal sanctity can be applied only when the husband satisfactorily proves the subsistence of a legal and valid marriage particularly when the provision in the code is a measure of social justice intended to protect women and children - Held, respondent failed to discharged the heavy burden by tendering strict proof of the fact in issue - No dispute that appellant was married to respondent - Magistrate rightly awarded maintenance to appellant as she had no means to maintain herself - High Court order set aside - Appeal allowed.
