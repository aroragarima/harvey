Ministry of Commerce v Haldor Topsoe A/S

Supreme Court of India

20 July 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 368; (2000) 6 SCC 626; AIR 2000 SC 2556; 2000 (1) CLC 1635; 2000 (120) E.L.T. 11; JT 2000 (8) SC 120; 2000 (39) RLT 1017; 2000(5) SCALE 239; 2000 (5) SLT 694; 2000 (5) Supreme 242

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Customs; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Corporate - Customs - Trade - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Customs Tariff Act, 1975, ss. 2 and 7A - Customs Tariff (Amendment) Act, 1995 - Ss. 9-C and 9-A(1) - Whether the tribunal rightly held that though the export of catalyst were below the normal value, no injury was caused to the domestic market, no dumping duty was to be levied reducing the dumping duty imposed by the appellant? - If material is available in regard to the comparable price in the ordinary course of trade in the exporting country or territory, then the normal value will have to be determined on that basis, if such material in regard to comparable price is not available, then the authority has been given a choice between the comparable representative export price and cost of production in the country of origin of the goods - "Normal value" will have to be determined with reference to comparable price - Held, by holding anti-dumping duty to be exporter-specific, the tribunal could not have restricted the scope of the investigation only to materials to be produced by a party against whom an investigation is being conducted - Held, the reasons advanced by the respondent for not furnishing the information are not worthy of acceptance - By withholding the information which the respondent was bound to have disclosed, the respondent has not cooperated with the investigation and has caused impediments in determination of the normal value - Held, authority was justified in proceeding to determine the normal value of the subject catalysts on the basis of "best judgment assessment" - Held, impugned orders of the Tribunal are set aside - Appeals are allowed
