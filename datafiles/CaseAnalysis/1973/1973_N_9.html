Charan Lal Sahu v Nandkishore Bhatt and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 August 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, S.N. Dwivedi

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 147; (1973) 2 SCC 530; AIR 1973 SC 2464; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 294; 1973 UJ 750

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951 ss. 86,117, 86(1) - Election - Failed to deposit Security amount - Elections were conducted and respondent nos. 1 to 5 were elected - Aggrieved appellant filed election petition but did not deposit security amount as required u/s. 117 of the Act - Appellant contended that deposit u/s. 117 of the Act was only directory and not mandatory, it was only to secure cost of trial, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, right to vote or stand as a candidate for election is not a civil right but is a creature of statute or special law and must be subject to the limitations imposed by it. Therefore, reference to trial in a larger sense and deals with the steps in a trial rather than in a narrower sense of a trial commencing after the notice of the petition is directed to be served on the respondent. Thus, marginal note u/s. 86 of the Act, namely, 'Trial of election petitions' does not indicate that u/s 86(1) of the Act, election petition cannot be dismissed for non-compliance with the provisions set out therein, unless notice is issued to the respondent. Where the language is clear and can admit of no other meaning such as is evidence u/s. 86(1) of the Act, the marginal cannot be read, to control that power. Thus, non-deposit of the security along with the election petition as required u/s 117 of the Act leaves no option to the Court but to reject it. Appeal dismissed.
