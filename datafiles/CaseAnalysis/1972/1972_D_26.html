Deputy Asstt. Iron & Steel Controller & Anr v L. Manickchand, Proprietor, Katrella Metalcorpn. Madras

Supreme Court of India

05 January 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, Hans Raj Khanna, J.M. Shelat, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 81; (1972) 3 SCC 324; AIR 1972 SC 935; 1973 (1) MLJ 28; 1973 (1) MLJ(SC) 28; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Trade

Summary: Trade - Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947, s. 3(1)(a) - Whether application for import license in question should be considered in accordance with policy in force when license is granted or when application is made? - Held, respondent's application for license was considered as per policy in force - In granting licenses for imports, authority concerned has to keep in view various factors which may have impact on imports of other items of relatively greater priority in larger interest of over-all economy of country which has to be supreme consideration - An applicant has no absolute vested right to an import license in terms of policy in force at time of his application because from very nature of things at time of granting license - Authority concerned may often be in a better position to have a clearer over-all picture of various factors having an important impact on final decision on allotment of import quota to various applicants - Respondent's application be considered in accordance with import policy for year 1970 -71 - Appeal allowed.
