Hitendra Vishnu Thakur v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

12 July 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, Faizan Uddin

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1706; (1994) 4 SCC 602; (1994) SCC (Cr) 1087; AIR 1994 SC 2623; AIR 1994 SCW 3699; 1995 (2) Bom.C.R. 491; 1994 (2) CCR 604; 1994 (2) Crimes 916; 1995 CRLJ 517; JT 1994 (4) SC 255; 1994(3) SCALE 105; [1994] Supp1 S.C.R. 360; 1994 (2) UJ 786

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Punishments, Inflammable Substances Act, 1952

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Terrorists And Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, ss. 18, 20 (4), 20 (b) and 20 (bb)- Criminal Procedure Code, 1908, s. 167(2) - Transfer of case by designated Court - Court of opinion after taking cognizance, that offence is not such as is triable by designated Court as offence does not fall within parameters of TADA - (A) Designated Court if obliged to transfer case to Court having jurisdiction under CrPC - Held, designated Court is obliged to transfer case - It has to release accused but, in absence of application from accused it is not required to act on its own motion - (B) Bail Grant of - Period for filing charge sheet expiring - Accused seeking to be released on bail and furnishing requisite bail - Whether designated Court has to release accused on bail? - (C) Application for bail on failure to complete investigation within prescribed period u/cl. (b) or extended period and submitting of report by Public Prosecutor for extension of period for completing investigation - Procedure - Further held, notice of application u/cl. (b) to Public Prosecutor and of report by Public Prosecutor to accused - Appeal partly allowed.
