Â Alok Kumar Roy v Dr. S. N. Sarma and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 October 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 493; AIR 1968 SC 453; 1968 (70) BomLR 198; 1968 MahLJ 500; 1968 MPLJ 552; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 813

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - Expulsion order - Unqualified apology - Dismissal of petition - Irregularity of order cured - Appellant was expelled from the medical college, on 26-10-1966, and tendered an unqualified apology on 27-10-1966 - Principal did not accept the apology and when on 4-11-1966, he went to deposit the fee, he was told that as he had been expelled and the order of expulsion stood, no examination fee could be accepted from him - W.P. filed by the appellant got dismissed - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the HC was right in dismissing the petition - Held, that the HC was not right in dismissing the petition as it did on 24-5-1967 - When the petition came to the HC, the irregularity in presentation should be held to have been cured - It was open to the HC to, consider whether the irregular order of stay should be regularised - Even if the irregular stay could not be regularised, there was no reason why the petition should have been dismissed merely on the ground that it was irregularly presented, when it finally did reach the HC - Appeal allowed.
