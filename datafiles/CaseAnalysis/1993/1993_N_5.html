Desh Bandhu Gupta v N.L.Anand And Rajinder Singh

Supreme Court of India

17 September 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1656; (1994) 1 SCC 131; AIR 1993 SCW 3458; 1994 (1) BC 45; 1993 (3) CCC 737; 1994 (1) GLH 215; JT 1993 (5) SC 313; 1994 (1) MLJ 78; 1994 (1) MLJ(SC) 78; 1993 (2) RCJ 628; 1993 (2) RentLR 577; 1993(3) SCALE 791; [1993] Supp2 S.C.R. 346

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 1(1), 7(4), 8, 10, 47, 54(1-A), 90, 92 and 64 - O. 21, r. 66 (2), O 21, r. 90, O. 1, r. 3 - Execution of decree - (A) Procedure for sale of immovable property - Sale by public auction - Estimate of value of property - Material particulars to be decided by court in each case - Held, non-application of mind whether sale of a part of property would satisfy decree debt is a material irregularity doing substantial injury to appellant attracting O. 21, r. 90 - Court should normally state valuation given by decree holder and judgment debtor - Service of notice on judgment debtor is mandatory - Sale set aside - (B) Jurisdiction of executing court - Further held, value of property sold at execution is not relevant to determine jurisdiction of execution court - Appeal allowed.
