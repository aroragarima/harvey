Bihar State Government Secondary School Teachers Association v Bihar Education Service Association And Others; (2) Anup Mukherjee And Others

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, B.N. Srikrishna, Lokeshwar Singh Panta, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 479; (2012) 13 SCC 33; AIR 2013 SC 487; 2013 (2) AWC 1230; JT 2012 (11) SC 539; 2012(11) SCALE 291; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 50; 2013 (1) SLR 399; 2012 (4) UPLBEC 3201

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Service

Summary: Education - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 141 - Recommendation by Committee - Promotions - Merger - Resolution/Notification - Quashing - Sustainability - Respondent No. 66 had set up 3 member committee keeping in view of strength and promotional means to tackle problem and promotional opportunities - 2 reasons for stagnation noted by committee were 1. relatively heavy recruitment of officers of same age group in certain years, and lack of adequate number of promotional posts at different levels of organizational hierarchy - Committed after scrutinizing and analysing situations recommended few suggestions for consideration of education department, which was accepted by State Govt. and notification was published in Gazette Extra Ordinary - Inspite of notification Govt. did not implement though several representations were made - Aggrieved petitioners approached HC and filed writ petition for direction to Govt. to implement recommendations as published in notification - Another writ petition was filed relying on notification praying for preparation of common seniority list for education service - HC, after hearing petitioner as Gov.t failed to file counter, passed orders directing Govt. to act upon resolution as far it relates to Education Service of ED - State aggrieved filed 2 LPA and contended that there was no proposal to merger sub-ordinate teachers into Education Service Class-II and further pointed out that 50% posts of Education Service Class-II were filled by promotion of subordinate teachers - State filed appeal before DB, which was dismissed against which State filed SLP's and SC, directed State to implement resolution - Fresh writ petition was filed for implementation of orders passed by SC - HC, directed State to implement notification in totality within a period of 6 weeks, State passed orders stating that teachers of subordinate education service were merged into Education service class-II in accordance with notification - Education Service filed writ petition challenging Govt. resolution for merger contending Subordinate education service to which secondary teachers belonged was quite different from education service class-II - Govt. contended that as per order of HC, and opinion of Advocate General Govt. had no option but to implement notification - HC, allowed writ petition and quashed resolution dt. 7-7-2006 - Aggrieved LPA was filed before DB, which took view that unless rules were framed there could not be merger since there was no parity in pay of subordinate teachers and Service Class-II employees and dismissed appeal - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether HC, was justified in quashing notification - Held, in first round of litigation when State Govt. filed SLP, this Court observed that final direction given to State was to implement resolution in manner it was meant to be implemented, and disposed of SLP, thus, it was clear at end of first round of litigation that petition filed by appellant had been allowed by SJ, and that order had been left undisturbed in appeals therefrom by DB, as well as by this Court - In the present appeals legality of Govt. Resolution dt. 7-7-2006 which State Govt. defended before SJ but gave up defence in appeal before DB contending that decision could not be treated as binding although it had been confirmed by DB - SC, held that it was not necessary for HC to reopen entire controversy and passed orders but as per hierarchy of Courts it requires HC also to accept decision of this Court, and its interpretation of orders issued by executive, any departure therefrom will lead only to indiscipline and anarchy, HC could not ignore art. 141 of Constitution which clearly states, that law declared by this Court was binding on all Courts, law of finality of decisions which was enshrined in principle of res-judicata or principles analogous thereto, does not permit any such re-examination, and HC, clearly failed to recognize same - Appeals disposed of.
