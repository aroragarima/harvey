Jivendra Nath Kaul Etc. v The Collector/District Magistrate And Anr. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

24 July 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, M.N. Venkatachaliah, P.B. Sawant, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1107; (1992) 3 SCC 576; AIR 1994 SC 85; JT 1992 (4) SC 409; 1992(2) SCALE 73; [1992] 3 S.C.R. 642; 1992 (2) UJ 279; 1992 (2) UPLBEC 1052

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Challenged, Removal from the post of president

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Practice & Procedure - Uttar Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1916, ss. 87-a(13), 87-A(12) - Removal from the post of president- Challenged - Appellant was elected president of Zilla Parishad - Appellant filed writ petitions before HC and challenged the proceedings of the meeting and also his removal from the office of the president - Appellant even challenged the nomination of other two persons on the ground that on date of their nomination both of them were in Govt. service and as such were disqualified to be members of Zilla Parishad, being holders of officer of profit - HC partly allowed the petitions and set aside the nominations of two persons holding it illegal - Hence instant appeal.

Held, HC interpreted 'for the time being' in s. 87-a (13) of the Act to mean the members of the board as they existed on the date of its constitution and not on the date when the motion of no confidence was considered. So far as s. 87-A(12) of the Act was concerned HC interpreted the expression 'total number of members of the Board' to mean to total number of member who were functioning as such at relevant time which means on the date of the meeting and did not include members or members who had been removed from office. The only meaning which can be given to the expression 'half of the total number of members of the Board' is the members as existed on the date of its constitution. The total number of members on the date of the composition of the municipal board, Mugal Sarai was 16 and as such not with standing the removal of member/members the motion of no confidence could only be passed if the motion was supported by more than 8 votes. HC's interpretation is not the fact of it contrary to the plain language of the sub-section. Similarly the HC fell into grave error by not appreciating the plain meaning of the words 'for the time being' in s. 87-A(13) of the Act. 'For the time being' means at the moment or existing position. These words indicate the actual membership in existence on the date of the motion of no confidence. HC on the basis of strained reasoning has given interpretation which does not flow from the simple language of s. 87-a(12)(13) of the Act. Appeal dismissed.
