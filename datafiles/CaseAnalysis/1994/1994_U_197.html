Rollatainers Limited v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

14 July 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 48; (1994) 3 SCC 292; (1994) Supp3 SCC 293; 1996 (62) ECR 274; 1994 (72) E.L.T. 793; JT 1994 (4) SC 458; 1994 (4) RLT 40; 1994(3) SCALE 311; 1995 (1) SCJ 10B; 1994 (95) STC 556; [1994] Supp1 S.C.R. 663

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 8 - Whether printed cartons manufactured by appellant Co. are 'Products of Printing Industry' and are exempt from payment of duty of excise under Exemption Notification No. 55/75? - Single judge allowed printed cartons to be products of printing industry - However, division bench of High Court reversed order - A carton remains a carton whether it is plain carton or printed carton - What is exempt under Notification is 'Product' of 'Printing Industry'? - 'Product' in this case is carton - Printing Industry by itself cannot bring carton into existence - Any amount of fancy printing on a cardboard would not make it a carton - In process of manufacturing printed cartons, card-board has to be cut, printed, creased and given shape of a carton by using paste or gum - Simply because there are expensive prints on carton, such a printed carton would not become product of Printing Industry - It shall remain product of Packaging Industry - Upheld judgment of division bench of HC - Appeal dismissed.
