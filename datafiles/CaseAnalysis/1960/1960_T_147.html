Messrs Burn and Company Limited and Others v Their Employees

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 158; AIR 1960 SC 896; 1960 (1) FLR 245; 1960 (2) LLJ 261; 1960 SCJ 1220; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 423

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Incentive Bonus

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Incentive bonus - Entitled to - Whether clerical and subordinate staff entitled incentive bonus? - Held, clerical and subordinate staff also take part in production and there is no reason for excluding them from scheme of incentive bonus - Fact that dearness allowance was paid to clerical staff at a higher scale is also not a reason for depriving them altogether of benefits of incentive bonus scheme - No reason why, where an incentive bonus is in force in a concern for majority of its workmen, tribunal should not be able to extend same to remainder of workmen - No interference of order of Tribunal - Appeals dismissed.
