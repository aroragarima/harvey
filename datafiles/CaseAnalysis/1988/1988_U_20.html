Mukul Dalal Etc. Etc. v Union Of India And Ors. Etc. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 433; (1988) 3 SCC 144; (1988) SCC (Cr) 566; 1988 (1) Bom.C.R. 410; 1988 CRLR 350; 1988 CrLR(SC) 350; JT 1988 (2) SC 280; 1988(1) SCALE 909; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 868

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: Criminal - Practice and Procedure - CrPC, 1973, ss. 24, 25 - Maharashtra Law Officers (Appointment, Conditions of Service and Remuneration), r.22 - Whether appointment by State of Special Public Prosecutors and Assistant Public Prosecutors u/ss 24 and 25 of CrPC, 1973 at cost of private complainants, is justifiable? - Held, in most of States, Remembrancer of legal Affairs looks after State litigations - He is a responsible officer and normally with judicial experience - When an application for services of Special Public Prosecutor or an Assistant Public Prosecutor is made in a given case, power would be vested in him to examine facts and take decision as to whether case merits appointment of a special Public Prosecutor or an Assistant Public Prosecutor - Without screening on basis of guidelines prescribed or to be prescribed, services of a Special Public Prosecutor should not be made available to a private complainant - Further held, to leave private complainant to pay to Special Public Prosecute would indeed not be appropriate - Rate of fees should be prescribed and private complainant should be called upon to deposit fees either with Remembrancer of Legal Affairs or a prescribed State agency from where fees would be drawn by Special Public Prosecutor - R. 22 of Maharashtra Rules is bad and State Govt. should properly modify same - Order accordingly.
