Rajesh Valel Puthuvalil and another v Inland Waterways Authority of India and another

Supreme Court of India

15 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

C. Nagappan, T. S. Thakur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 454; 2014 (106) ALR 239; 2014 (5) AWC 4911; JT 2014 (8) SC 245; 2014 (125) RD 436; 2014(8) SCALE 592; 2014 (3) WLN(SC) 144

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Land Acquisition, Award

Summary: Land & Property - Land acquisition - Award - Legality - Appellants land and structures was acquired by respondent no. 1 for purpose of widening narrow stretches of National Waterways, accordingly award was passed - Appellants filed reference before Reference Court and same was re-determined land value and value of building, by enhancing it - Respondent no. 1 filed appeal before HC - HC confirmed land value re-determined by Reference Court and reduced value of structures - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified -

Held, HC held that regard to cost of construction of building in year 1997 value of construction fixed by Reference Court was on higher side and re-fixed value of building at Rs. 3,50,000/- on guess estimate - SC found force in submission of appellant that guess estimate was not warranted when material evidence in shape of Ext.C-3 Valuation Report was available on record - There was no rebuttal evidence adduced by respondents insofar as valuation of building was concerned and HC committed error in resorting to guess estimate for reducing value of building and impugned judgment in regard was liable to be set aside - Impugned judgment of HC insofar as re-fixing value of structures concerned was set aside and its determination made by Reference Court was restored - Appeal allowed.
