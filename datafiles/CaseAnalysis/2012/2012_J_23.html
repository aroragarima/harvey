Gaytri Bajaj v Jiten Bhalla

Supreme Court of India

05 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 339; (2012) 12 SCC 471; AIR 2013 SC 102; 2013 (1) ALD(SC) 127; 2012(6) ALL MR 438; 2012 (95) ALR 597; 2012 (6) AWC 6396; JT 2012 (10) SC 139; 2012 (7) MLJ(SC) 887; 2013 (1) OLR 50; 2013 (170) PLR 295; 2012 (4) RCR(Civil) 603; 2012(10) SCALE 55; [2012] 8 S.C.R. 1142

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Summary: Family & Personal - Women & Children - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 13B - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 151 - Custody of minor - Reluctance of children - Visitation rights - Entitled to - Appellant (wife) and respondent (husband) were blessed with two daughters - Appellant and respondent due to irreconcilable differences decided to dissolve their marriage and entered into an agreement that respondent was to have sole custody of two minor daughters and appellant had agreed to forego her rights of visitation, in view of best interest and welfare of children - Appellant and respondent filed a petition u/s. 13B of the Act before Trial Court - Trial Court granted a decree of divorce u/s. 13B of the Act - Thereafter, appellant after 3 years, filed a suit seeking declaration of decree was null and void and alleged that that her consent was obtained by acts of fraud and deceit committed by respondent - Meanwhile, appellant filed an application u/s. 151 of CPC to recall said decree of divorce - Trial Court, recalled decree of divorce - Aggrieved respondent filed appeal before HC, which was allowed - Against order of HC appellant filed review petition before HC, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether appellant was entitled to custody of children and visitation rights, if any - Held, after interacting with both children SC by its order dt.16-12-2011 had recorded that children, who were aged about 17 and 11 years, were very clear and categorical that they wanted to continue to live with their father and they did not want to go with their mother - It was not the better right of either parent that would require adjudication while deciding their entitlement to custody - Desire of child coupled with availability of a conducive and appropriate environment for proper upbringing together with the ability and means of concerned parent to take care of child were some of relevant factors that have to be taken into account by Court while deciding issue of custody of a minor - It was the desire, interest and welfare of minor which was crucial and ultimate consideration that should guide the determination required to be made by Court - In the instant case, irrespective of question whether abandonment of visitation rights by appellant was occasioned by fraud or deceit practiced on her, as claimed, an attempt was made by SC, even by means of a personal interaction with children, to bring issue of custody and visitation rights to a satisfactory conclusion - From the record, children, one of whom was on the verge of attaining majority, did not want to go with her mother (appellant) - Both appeared to be happy in company of their father (respondent) who appeared to be in a position to look after them; provide them with adequate educational facilities, maintain them in a proper and congenial manner - Children having expressed their reluctance to go with mother, even for a short duration of time, any visitation right to mother would be adverse to interest of children - In view of reluctance of children to even meet their mother, arrangement of visitation could not made possible by order of Court - Children should continue to remain in custody of father (respondent) until age of majority - Appeals dismissed.
