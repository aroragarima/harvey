K. Venkatachala Bhat and Another v Krishna Nayak (D) by Lrs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 187; (2005) 4 SCC 117; 2005 (59) ALR 294; 2005 (2) AWC 1012; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 14; JT 2005 (3) SC 161; 2005 (141) PLR 668; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 356; 2005(3) SCALE 77; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 623; 2005 (Supp) RD 221B; 2005 (2) UC 1051

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Writ appeal - Delay of 1440 days - Division Bench of Karnataka High Court refused to condone the delay - Appeal against this order - Held, acting on the basis of memo or affidavit which was singed by respondents only dispute could not have been settled against appellants without their consent - When writ petition was disposed of in an indefensible manner by Single Judge, Division Bench should have condoned the delay even if it was substantial in view of reasons indicated by appellants - Matter remanded to Single Judge for disposal on merits of the case.
