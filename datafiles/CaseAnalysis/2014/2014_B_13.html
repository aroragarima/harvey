Leela Krishnarao Pansare and others v Babasaheb Bhanudas Ithape and others

Supreme Court of India

07 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Dismissal of suit for cancellation of agreement to sell - Legality - Appellants had filed a suit against the respondents for a declaration to the affect that the agreement to sell entered into between the appellants and the respondents should be cancelled and the appellants should be put in possession of the land in question, which had been agreed to be sold in pursuance of the agreement to sell dt. 17-8-1995 - Suit filed by the appellants had been dismissed on 6-9-2008 and being aggrieved by dismissal of the said suit, First Appeal was filed in the HC by appellants - Appeal was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC committed error in dismissing suit filed by appellant for cancellation of agreement to sell -

Held, upon perusal of the impugned judgment delivered by the HC, it was clear that the entire sale consideration had not been paid but at the same time it was also an admitted fact that the appellants did not get the entry with regard to the 'Deosthan Inam' deleted - There was no discussion about the efforts made by the appellants for getting the said entry deleted - HC did also not discuss the consequences of non deletion of the said entry and the efforts made by the appellants for not getting it deleted - Similarly, there was no definite finding as to how much consideration was paid and at what time or stage - Even the amount payable by the respondent towards purchase price had not been paid in full to the appellants and the said thing has not been properly discussed - SC found that the relevant evidence was neither discussed nor properly appreciated by the HC - It was very much necessary for the HC to decide whether the appellants and the respondents had performed their respective duties, which they had to perform in pursuance of the agreement with regard to sale of the land in question - Thus, without appropriate appreciation of the evidence, the HC should not have dismissed the appeal - Appeal allowed.
