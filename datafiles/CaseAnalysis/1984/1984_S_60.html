Lt. Col., S.J. Chaudhary v State (Delhi Administration)

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, E.S. Venkataramiah, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 97; (1984) SCC (Cr) 163; (1984) 1 SCC 722; AIR 1984 SC 618; 1984 CRLJ 340; 1984 CrLR(SC) 68; 1984(1) SCALE 92; [1984] 2 S.C.R. 438

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Advocates Act, 1961, s. 35 - CrPC, 1973, s. 231 - Supreme Court gave a direction that on the commencement of the trial, it should proceed from day-to- day - Alleging that his two Advocates are not prepared to appear in the case from day-to-day as the trial is likely to be prolonged, the petitioner has filed, the present application for modification of the earlier order of this court by the deletion of the direction that - Issues arise : (A) Till when the Sessions trial proceed - (B) Advocate accepting brief in criminal case - Must attend trial from day to day - In case he not do so - Held, must proceed de die in diem until concluded - If he fails to attend, he will be committing breach of his professional duty - Petition dismissed.
