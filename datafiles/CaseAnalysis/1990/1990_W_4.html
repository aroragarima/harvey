Welfare Association of Absorbed Central Government Employees In Public Enterprises v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 880; (1991) 2 SCC 265; (1991) SCC (L&S) 693; 1991 (16) ATC 546A; 1991 (1) LLN 958

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 32 - Pension - Class action on behalf of Welfare Association of Absorbed Central Govt. Employees in Public Enterprises asking for benefit of judgment of this Court in 'Common Cause' (1986 Indlaw SC 321) - Commuted pension - Petitioners are persons who have, at time of retirement from Govt. service and entering into public sector, taken advantage of commuting entire pension - Held, Petitioners belong to a class different from those whose case was before this Court at instance of Common Cause - No basis in allegation that by not extending benefit of decision of this Court referred to above, to category reprinted by petitioner there is any infringement of art. 14 of Constitution - Petition dismissed.
