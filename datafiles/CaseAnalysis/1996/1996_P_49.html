High Court of Judicature At Patna v Pandey Madan Mohan Prasad Sinha and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3028; (1997) 10 SCC 409; 1999 (2) LLN 669

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Grounds For Termination

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(2) - Civil Services (Classification, Control And Appeal) Rules, 1930, r. 55B - Whether non-communication of adverse remarks vitiates action that has been taken against Respondent 1, viz,termination of his services on ground that he was not fit for confirmation on post of Munsif? - Held, an order terminating services of a probationer can be questioned only if it is shown that it has been passed arbitrarily or has been passed by way of punishment without complying with requirements of art. 311(2) of Constitution - Principles of natural justice have no application in case of termination of services of a probationer during period of probation since he has no right to hold post - Decision to terminate services of respondent 1 was taken by HC after considering entries in service record of respondent 1 covering entire period of probation and not on basis of a particular act of misconduct - Appeal allowed.
