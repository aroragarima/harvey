Â Jagmohan Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 October 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

D.G. Palekar, S.M. Sikri, A.N. Ray, I.D. Dua, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 736; (1973) 1 SCC 20; (1973) SCC (Cr) 169; AIR 1973 SC 947; 1973 CRLJ 370; [1973] 2 S.C.R. 541

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 14, 19, 21 and 245 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Provision of s. 302 - Capital punishment of death sentence - Constitutionality - Ancillary issues - (A) Validity of death sentence in case of pre-meditated murder motivated by ill-feeling nurtured for years - (B) Whether provision suffers from vices of excess delegation? - (C) Whether provision is violative of art. 14? - (D) No procedure is laid down by law for determining whether sentence of death or something less is appropriate in case - (E) Reasonability and Public interest - Held, death sentence held proper - Provision does not suffer from vice of excessive delegation on ground that legislature has abdicated its essential function in not providing by legislative standards in what cases judge should pass death sentence - Provision is not violative of art. 14 on ground that unguided and uncontrolled discretion is given to judges to impose capital punishment or imprisonment for life - Death sentence is not unconstitutional for not laying down any procedure by law for determining appropriateness - Cannot be regarded per se as unreasonable or not in public interest - Appeal dismissed.
