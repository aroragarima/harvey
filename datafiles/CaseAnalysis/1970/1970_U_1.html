Ugamsingh & Mishrimal v Kesrimal & Ors

Supreme Court of India

26 November 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, J.M. Shelat, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 192; (1970) 3 SCC 831; AIR 1971 SC 2540; [1971] 2 S.C.R. 836

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure 1908

Summary: CPC, 1908, s. 9, O. 18, r. 18 - (A) Procedure and Procedure - Right to worship - Dispute as to - If cognizable by Civil Court - (B) Hindu Law - Religious endowment - Idol - Right to worship - Jains - Digambari and Swetambari Sects - Distinction between worship of the two Sects - (C) Provision of O.18, r.18 of CPC, 1908 - Inspection by Court - Inspection made only for purpose of understanding evidence - Validity of judgement - Dispute held, cognizable by Civil Court - While the Digambaries will not worship an idol which has chakshus (artificial eyes) or which has clothes or muket,Swetambaries would worship a Digambari idol without these; right to worship a Digarrbari idol by both the sects is possible - Judgment, not being based solely on result of personal inspection, is not vitiated - Appeal Dismissed.
