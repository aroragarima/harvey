Chhetriya Pardushan Mukti Sangharsh Samiti v State Of U.P And Ors

Supreme Court of India

13 August 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, K.N. Saikia

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 199; (1990) 4 SCC 449; AIR 1990 SC 2060; JT 1990 (3) SC 685; 1990(2) SCALE 332; [1990] 3 S.C.R. 739; 1990 (2) UJ 566

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Water

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act 1974, s. 21 - Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974, s. 2(e) - Petition was filed alleging environmental pollution because of smoke and effluents caused by running of mills and plants - Misuse of rights u/art. 32 - Legality of application - Use and misuse of right to legal remedies - Held, owners of these mills and plants were found to be complying with statutory provisions - It was found that allegations were result of long history of enemity and animosity - Application legally devoid of any merit or principles of public interest and public protection - Application creates bottlenecks in courts, abuse of process of Supreme Court - Duty of Supreme Court has a duty to enforce fundamental rights - Weapon u/art. 32 should not be misused or permitted to be misused - Appeal dismissed.
