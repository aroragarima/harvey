Barot Vijaykumar Balakrishna and another v Modh Vinaykumar Dasrathlal & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

05 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 403; (2011) 7 SCC 308; AIR 2011 SC 2829; 2011(4) ALL MR 849; 2012 (2) ESC 182; 2012 (1) G.L.R. 1; JT 2011 (7) SC 266; 2011 (8) MLJ(SC) 91; 2011(7) SCALE 12; [2011] 7 S.C.R. 154; 2011 (4) SLR 582

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Assistant Public Prosecutor, Gujarat General State Service Class II Recruitment (Examination) Rules, 2008

Summary: Service - Advocates & Judges - Appointments of Assistant Public Prosecutor (Class-II) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - Selection process - Challenged - On 17.10.2008, Commission issued an advertisement inviting applications for filling up 242 posts of Assistant Public Prosecutor (Class II) - In the said advertisement under the marginal heading, 'Particulars of Examination', it was stated that the examination would consist of two parts, i.e., written (objective test) and oral interview - The written test was held by the Commission on 1.1.2009 and its result was published on 20.03.2009 - Subsequently, Commission took a decision fixing 10 out of 30 as the minimum qualifying mark for the viva voce - The viva voce of all the short listed candidates was held and final result was declared - Respondents filed writ petition questioning the validity of the appointments of Assistant Public Prosecutor (Class-II) on the ground that the minimum qualifying mark, separately fixed for the viva voce, was introduced just two or three days before the commencement of the oral tests though it was not stipulated in the advertisement issued by the Commission for filling up the posts - Respondents also submitted that the introduction of the minimum qualifying mark for the viva voce, after the commencement of the selection process was, illegal and actuated by bias on the part of the Commission - Single Judge dismissed all the writ petitions filed by the respondents - On appeal, DB set aside the judgment of the Single Judge and held that the action of the Commission in introducing the minimum qualifying mark for the viva voce, in the middle of the selection process, was bad - DB accordingly, quashed the select list and the appointments made on its basis and directed that a fresh list be drawn up on the basis of the aggregate of marks obtained by the candidates in the written test and the viva voce regardless of the minimum qualifying mark prescribed by the Commission for the viva voce - Hence, the instant appeals - Whether the order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, though the rules framed u/art. 309 of the Constitution governing the selection process mandated that there would be minimum qualifying marks each for the written test and the oral interview, the cut off mark for viva voce was not specified in the advertisement - In view of the omission, there were only two courses open, one, to carry on with the selection process and to complete it without fixing any cut off mark for the viva voce and to prepare the select list on the basis of the aggregate of marks obtained by the candidates in the written test and the viva voce - That would have been clearly wrong and in violation of the statutory rule governing the selection - The other course was to fix the cut off mark for the viva voce and to notify the candidates called for interview about it - This was the course that the Commission followed - This was in compliance with the rules and it did not cause any prejudice to any candidate either - Hence, no illegality at all in the selection process - Impugned order of DB set aside - Appeals allowed.
