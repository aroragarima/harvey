T. Anjanappa and Others v Somalingappa and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 927; (2006) 7 SCC 570; 2006 (65) ALR 151; 2006 (4) AWC 3638; 2006 (5) CTC 378; JT 2006 (8) SC 382; 2006 (6) KarLJ 1; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 19; 2006 (2) RCR(Rent) 593; 2006(8) SCALE 624; 2006 (7) SCJ 563; [2006] Supp5 S.C.R. 200; 2006 (3) UC 1820

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 64 - Adverse possession - Whether appellate Court was justified in dismissing suit of appellants for injunction which was decreed by Principal Munsiff and appreciating oral and documentary evidence regarding plea adverse possession by defendants? - Adverse possession contemplates a hostile possession - Principle of law is firmly established that a person who bases his title on adverse possession must show by clear and unequivocal evidence that his possession was hostile to real owner and amounted to denial of his title to property claimed - It is a matter of fundamental principle of law that where possession can be referred to a lawful title, it will not be considered to be adverse - Held, defendant do not have to prove who is true owner and even if they believed that Govt. was true owner, requirements of proving adverse possession have not been established and no reasons has been indicated by HC to differ from factual findings recorded by appellate court so order passed by HC is not sustainable - Appeal allowed.
