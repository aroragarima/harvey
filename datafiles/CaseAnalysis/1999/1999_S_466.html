Molai and Others v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Kurdukar, K.T. Thomas, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 786; (1999) 9 SCC 581; (2000) SCC (Cr) 438; AIR 2000 SC 177; 1999 (4) CCR 225; 2000 CRLJ 392; 2000 (1) JCC 30; JT 1999 (8) SC 361; 1999 (4) RCR(Criminal) 844; 1999(6) SCALE 606; 1999 (3) SCJ 686; 1999 (9) SLT 56; [1999] Supp4 S.C.R. 104; 1999 (8) Supreme 669

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 201, 302 and 376 - Indian Evidence Act, 1873, s. 27 - (A) Whether Courts have committed error in convicting both accused for offences punishable u/ss. 302, 376 and 201 r/w s. 34 IPC in facts and circumstances of case? - Held, no - Evidence on record and medical evidence positively established that sexual assault was committed by accused and that deceased was subjected to rape - (B) Whether present case falls within category of rarest of rare cases and therefor capital punishment is justified? - A 16 years old girl, was preparing for other 10 th examination at her house and suddenly both accused took advantage of she being alone in house and committed a most shameful act of rape - They strangulated her and thereafter caused injuries with a sharp edged weapon - Criminality in their conduct by throwing dead body into septic tank totally disregarding respect for a human dead body - Capital punishment to both accused is only proper punishment - Present case squarely falls in category of one of rarest of rare cases - Appeal dismissed.
