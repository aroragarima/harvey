K. P. Poulose v State Of Kerala & Anr

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, A. Alagiriswami, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 356; (1975) 2 SCC 236; AIR 1975 SC 1259; [1975] Supp S.C.R. 214; 1975 UJ 414

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Arbitration Act, 1940 s. 30(a) - Contract - Jetting - Extra expenditure claimed - Appellant was contractor and successful tenderer for construction of three overhead reserviours - Appellant had assumed that site condition were same as notified in schedule inviting tenders and submitted his original design on that basis, but site condition was found to be different - On advice of Research Institute jetting had resorted to extra expenditure and appellant had claimed additional amount for work of jetting which was refused and said claim was referred to arbitration u/cl. 34 of tender notification - Arbitrator made award against appellant - Appellant had filed appeal before Trial court which was allowed - Respondent filed appeal before HC which set aside order of Trial court and upheld award of arbitrator - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, rejection of the claim by arbitrator on the ground that 'jetting, however, is not an authorised extra covered by the agreement' cannot be anything but rationally inconsistent. Therefore, the award suffers from a manifest error apparent u/s. 30(a) of the Act and award can be set aside when an arbitrator has misconducted himself or the proceedings. Misconduct u/s. 30(a) has not a connotation of moral lapse but it comprises legal misconduct which is complete if the arbitrator on the face of the award arrives at an inconsistent conclusion even on his own finding or arrives at a decision by ignoring very material documents which throw abundant light on the controversy to help a just and fair decision. Therefore, arbitrator has misconducted the proceedings and award and judgement of HC was set aside and that of Trial court is restored and directed that the arbitrator will complete the proceedings after considering all the relevant documents including Ext. P. 11 and Ext. P. 16 after giving opportunity to the parties. Appeal allowed.
