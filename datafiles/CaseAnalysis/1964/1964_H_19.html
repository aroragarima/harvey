Â Ram Sewak Yadav v Hussain Kamil Kidwai and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 95; AIR 1964 SC 1249; 1964 ALJ 569; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 238

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Elections - Conduct of Election Rules, 1961, rr. 53, 55, 57, 63, 64, 84(3), 93, 100(1)(d)(iii), 101 and 102 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 11, r. 15 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 80, 83 (1), 90 (1), 92, 94 and 128 (1) - In an election, losing contestant filed petition for declaring election void on ground of discrepancies - Whether Election Tribunal grant an order for inspection of ballot papers which had been lodged before it in sealed boxes by Returning Officer? - Held, an order for inspection of ballot papers cannot be granted to support vague pleas made in petition not supported by material facts or to fish out evidence to support such pleas, and without disclosing a prima facie case in support - Appeal allowed.
