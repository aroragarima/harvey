Collector of Central Excise, Hyderabad v Electrolytic Foils Limited

Supreme Court of India

27 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 192; 1997 (91) E.L.T. 543

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Maintainability of appeal - Want of prosecution - Whether appeal filed by Excise Department is maintainable against respondent company in spite of notices returned unserved.

Held, service on respondent has not been possible because respondent Unit has closed down and information supplied by Deputy Commissioner (Legal) is that even land on which respondent Unit stood has since been sold to âNâ which is constructing its own factory on land purchased by it. It is, obvious that respondent Unit has completely closed down and even parcel of land owned by it has been disposed of and, Court see no reason in permitting appellant Department to pursue this appeal. Court dispose of appeal for want of prosecution since respondent cannot be served. There will be no order as to costs. Appeal disposed of.
