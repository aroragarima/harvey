Mohd. Hussain @ Julfikar Ali v The State (Govt. Of Nct) Delhi

Supreme Court of India

31 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, A.R. Dave, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 332; (2012) 9 SCC 408; AIR 2012 SC 3860; 2012 CRLJ 4537; 2012 (192) DLT 393; JT 2012 (8) SC 318; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 927; 2013 (1) RLW 715; 2012(8) SCALE 308; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 480

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1860

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 307 and 120-B - Explosive Substances Act, 1908, ss. 3 and 4 - Fair Trial - Denied due process of law - De novo trial - Sustainability - An explosion took place inside the bus and the incident resulted in death of 4 persons and injury to 24 persons - Charge-sheet was filed u/ss. 302, 307 and 120-B IPC and ss. 3 and 4 of the 1908 Act against 4 accused persons one of them being present appellant - 3 accused other than appellant were discharged by Trial Court - Appellant was charged u/ss. 302 and 307 IPC and ss. 3 and 4 of the 1908 Act - On appeal before HC, reference was also made to the DB for confirmation of death sentence - DB of HC confirmed the death sentence and maintained other sentences - Hence instant appeal - DB heard the appeal and was unanimous that appellant was denied the assistance of a counsel in substantial and meaningful manner in the course of trial although necessity of counsel was vital and imperative and that resulted in denial of due process of law - Further held that appellant had been put to prejudice rendering the impugned judgments unsustainable in law - Appellant was awarded the sentence of death more than 7 years ago and at such distance of time it shall be travesty of justice to direct for the appellants de novo trial - Whether the matter requires to be remanded for a de novo trial in accordance with law or not - Held, it could not be ignored that the offences with which appellant had been charged were of very serious nature and if the prosecution succeeds and appellant was convicted u/s. 302 IPC on retrial, the sentence could be death or life imprisonment - S. 302 IPC authorises the Court to punish the offender of murder with death or life imprisonment - Gravity of the offences and the criminality with which appellant was charged were important factors that need to be kept in mind, though it was a fact that in the first instance accused had been denied due process - While having due consideration to the appellants right, the nature of the offence and its gravity, the impact of crime on the society, more particularly the crime that had shaken the public and resulted in death of four persons in a public transport bus could not be ignored and overlooked - It was desirable that punishment should follow offence as closely as possible - In an extremely serious criminal case of the exceptional nature like instant one, it would occasion in failure of justice if the prosecution was not taken to the logical conclusion - Retrial of appellant, in the facts and circumstances, was indispensable - It was imperative that justice was secured after providing appellant with the legal practitioner if he did not engage a lawyer of his choice - Matter was required to be remanded for a de novo trial - Trial judge should proceed with the trial of appellant from the stage of prosecution evidence and should further ensure that the trial was concluded as expeditiously as might be possible and in no case later than 3 months from the date of communication of this order - Reference answered.
