Anjani Singh and others v Salauddin and others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Entitlement of

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Motor accident claim - Enhancement of compensation - Entitlement of - Deceased was about 35 years met with an accident and succumbed to injuries - Appellant/claimants were widow, parents and children of deceased - Appellants filed claim petition before Tribunal - Tribunal awarded compensation - Appellant filed appeal before HC and the same was allowed and award was passed - Dissatisfied with order passed by HC appellant filed instant appeal seeking enhancement of compensation - Whether impugned award passed by HC was justified -

Held, monthly salary of deceased was taken as Rs. 4030/- by Tribunal - HC had taken 50% of monthly salary to arrive at multiplicand - Towards future prospects at rate of 50% with monthly income of Rs. 4030/- it would come to Rs. 2015/-, making total monthly income to Rs. 6045/-, one fourth Rs. 1511/- should be deducted towards personal expenses of deceased, multiplier would be 16 which would come to Rs. 8,70,528/- under head of loss of dependency - Rs. 25,000/- was awarded towards funeral expenses, Rs. 1,00,000/- towards loss of love and affection of children and parents, and further Rs. 1,00,000/- towards loss of consortium by widow of deceased - Rs. 25,000/- was awarded for cost of litigation - Therefore, amount would come to Rs. 11,20,528/- - In total appellants received Rs. 5,05,519/- - Appellants were entitled to remaining amount, Rs. 6,15,009/-, the amount should bear interest - Appeal allowed.
