Punjab and Sindh Bank v Vinkar Sahakari Bank Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19948; (2001) 7 SCC 721; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1395; AIR 2001 SC 3641; 2002 (1) AllCJ 185; 2002 (1) AWC 3; 2002 (3) Bom.C.R. 377; [2001] 107 Comp Cas 208; 2001 (4) CompLJ 188; 2001 (4) Crimes 289; 2002 (1) CRJ 210; 2002 CRLJ 93; 2001 (2) JCC 268; JT 2001 (8) SC 22; 2001 (3) KLT 533; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 245; 2001(6) SCALE 352; 2001 (3) SCJ 617; 2001 (6) SLT 602; [2001] Supp3 S.C.R. 104; 2001 (6) Supreme 875; 2001 (2) UC 598

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal

Summary: Banking & Finance - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 - Bills of Exchange Act, 1882 - Dishonour of pay order - Holder filed complaint u/s. 138 of Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 - Bombay High Court quashed complaint on premise that pay order is not a cheque - Whether pay order is a cheque within meaning of s. 138 of the Negotiable Instrument Act? - Held, a cheque is a bill of exchange drawn on a specified banker and not expressed to be payable otherwise than on demand - Bill of exchange is an instrument in writing containing an unconditional order, signed by maker, directing a certain person to pay a certain sum of money only to, or to order of, a certain person or to bearer of instrument - Pay order is a negotiable instrument governed by Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 - Hence, pay order is a cheque - Appeal allowed.
