Rajesh Bajaj v State Nct of Delhi and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 447; (1999) 3 SCC 259; (1999) SCC (Cr) 401; AIR 1999 SC 1216; AIR 1999 SCW 881; 1999 (39) AllCriC 560; 1999 (2) CCR 4; 1999 (1) Crimes 136; 1999 CRLJ 1833; 1999 (82) ECR 225; 1999 (1) JCC 202; JT 1999 (2) SC 112; 1999 (2) RCR(Criminal) 160; 1999(1) SCALE 697; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 1012; 1999 (2) SLT 523; 1999 (2) Supreme 442

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 415 - Commercial transaction - In a cheating complaint, complainant is not required to reproduce verbatim all ingredients of alleged offence - Averments, prima facie making out case for investigation - (A) Validity of quashing of FIR on ground that complaint did not disclose offence - (B) Facts narrated in complaint revealing commercial transaction - If valid ground for holding that offence of cheating would elude from such transaction - Held, not proper - No ground for holding that offence of cheating would elude from such transaction, crux is intention of accused person - Appeal allowed.
