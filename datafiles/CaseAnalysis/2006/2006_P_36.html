Paramjeet Singh Patheja v ICDS Limited

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 874; (2006) 13 SCC 322; AIR 2007 SC 168; JT 2006 (10) SC 41; 2006(11) SCALE 459; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 178

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Objects, Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1995, Arbitration Act, 1899, Bankruptcy Act, 1883, Presidency Town Insolvency Act, 1909, Bombay Insolvency Rules, 1910

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Corporate - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act,1995,ss. 15 , 22 - Presidency Town Insolvency Act,1909,s. 9(2) - Arbitration Act, 1899,ss. 12,15 - Code of Civil Procedure,1908,ss. 522, 325, 2(14), 2(2), 89 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act,1996,s. 36 - Recovery proceedings on guarantor - Arbitration Award - Insolvency notice - Challenged - Respondent /company was registered with Board of Industrial Financial Reconstruction (BIFR) - Respondent initiated arbitration proceedings to recover amounts from its debtors - 'X' company was liable to pay amounts to respondent and appellant stood as guarantor to that amount - Respondent sued appellant/guarantor for recovery of money - Arbitration proceedings were commenced and Award passed against appellant - On basis of arbitration Award insolvency notice u/s. 9(2) of the 1909 Act was issued to appellant - Aggrieved, appellant filed notice of motion in HC challenging insolvency notice - Single Judge having differed with earlier judgments of HC referred question of law to DB - DB answered reference and held that award was 'decree' for purpose of s. 9 of the 1909 Act and that insolvency notice could be issued based on arbitration Award - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of DB was justified - Held, Under Section 15 of 1899 Act, award itself was enforceable 'as if' it were a decree but it would not become decree - Arbitration Act was in force when the 1909 Act came into operation and therefore could be seen that law makers were conscious of what was 'decree' 'order' and 'award' - Several judgments held that s.9 (e) attachment in execution of award was not one in executive of decree and Award u/s.15 of the 1899 Act was decree for purpose of enforcing that award - In Manilal vs. The Bharat Spinning & Weaving ,1935 Indlaw MUM 105 and in Ramshai v. Joylall,1928 Indlaw CAL 240 followed - In Sidharth Srivastava v. K.K. Modi Investment & Financial Service P.Ltd, 2002 Indlaw MUM 723 it was held that Award had force of decree but did not fulfill essential conditions of decree as contemplated by s. 2(2) of the 1908 Act - Insolvency notice could not be issued u/s. 9(2) of the 1909 Act based on Arbitration Award - Execution proceedings could not be proceeded in view of statutory stay u/s. 22 of the 1995 Act and so no insolvency notice was liable to be issued on appellant - Arbitration award was neither decree nor Order for payment within meaning of s. 9(2) of the 1909 Act and could not satisfy any requirements of decree -Award under the 1996 Act, could not be construed to be 'decree' for purpose of s. 9(2) of the 1909 Act - Insolvency notice should be in strict compliance with requirements in s. 9(3) and rules made thereunder - Award which was incapable of execution could not form basis of insolvency notice - Insolvency notice issued u/s. 9(2) of the 1909 Act could not be sustained based on arbitral award - Order of DB was set aside - Appeal allowed.
