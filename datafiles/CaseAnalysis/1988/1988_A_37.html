Birad Mal Singhvi v Anand Purohit

Supreme Court of India

02 August 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 363; (1988) Supp SCC 604; AIR 1988 SC 1796; JT 1988 (3) SC 339; 1988(2) SCALE 328; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 1; 1988 (2) UJ 442

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Different Constituency

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951,ss.33(5), 36 - Challenge to election of appellant - (A) Whether rejection of nomination papers of contestants justified? - S.33(5) is mandatory in nature - S.33(5) requires a candidate who is an elector of a different constituency, to file a copy of the electoral roll of constituency or a relevant part of that roll or a certified copy of relevant entries along with his nomination paper - Non-compliance with s. 33(5) is fatal to nomination and no other mode is prescribed by Act for proving eligibility of candidate - Held, Yes - Candidates failed to fulfill requirements laid down in s.33(5) of Representation of People Act, which is mandatory in nature - Non-compliance creates defeat of substantial character - (B) Whether candidate is required to prove his date of birth in order to comply with nomination procedure? - Candidate has to prove his qualification with respect to age and non-compliance of same would lead to his candidature being rejected - Candidate has to prove his eligibility at time when he is required to do so and if he fails, his nomination paper is liable to be rejected - Order accordingly.
