Ramniklal Pitambardas Mehta v Indradaman Amartlal Sheth

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 462; AIR 1964 SC 1676; 1964 (5) G.L.R. 798; 1965 (2) SCJ 608; [1964] 8 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Bombay Rent, Hotel and lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, ss. 13(1)(g) and 13(1)(hh) - Appellant is a tenant of ground-floor of a house owned by respondent - Respondent sued for ejectment of appellant on ground that he required entire house including portion occupied by appellant, for his residential purpose - Whether respondent's case came within provisions of s. 13(1)(g) of Act or fell within provisions of s. 13(1)(hh)? - Held, respondent's case falls under cl. (g) when he bona fide requires premises for his own occupation - Mere fact that he intends to make alterations in house either on account of his sweet will or on account of absolute necessity in view of condition of house, does not affect question of his requiring house bona fide and reasonably for his occupation, when he has proved his need for occupying house - There is no such prohibition either in language of cl. (g) or in any other provision of Act to effect that landlord must occupy house for residence without making any alterations in it - There could not be any logical reason for such a prohibition - Appeal dismissed.
