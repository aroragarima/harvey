State of Tamil Nadu Represented by its Secretary Home, Prohobition & Excise Department and others v K. Balu and another

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

D.Y. Chandrachud, T.S. Thakur, L. Nageswara Rao

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 901; (2017) 2 SCC 281; 2017 (1) ACC 1; AIR 2017 SC 262; 2017 (1) AWC 335; 2017 (1) CLT(SC) 63; JT 2016 (12) SC 82; 2017 (1) KarLJ 177; 2017 (1) KLT 132; 2016 (8) MLJ 758; 2017 (1) OLR 583; 2017 (1) RCR(Civil) 1029; 2016(12) SCALE 968

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution

Keywords: Jurisdiction, Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, Constitution of India, 1950, Punjab Excise (Amendment) Act, 2016, Punjab Excise Act, 1941

Summary: Administrative - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136, 47A - Road Accidents - Prohibition Challenged - In view of alarming rise/figures of death or injury to drunken driving, Ministry of Road Transport and Highways (MoRTH) issued a circular to all state govt. thereby advised them to remove liquor shops situated along national highways and not to issue fresh licenses - HC of two states imposed prohibition of liquor on national highways and state highway - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, art. 47A of Directive principles of state policy deals with duty imposed on the State to raise the level of nutrition and the standard of living and to improve public health. The law on preventing drunken driving requires proper enforcement. The court must accept the policy of the union govt. for more than one reason. First and foremost, it is trite law that in matters of policy, in this case a policy on safety, the court will defer to and accept a considered view formed by an expert body. Secondly the view of the union govt. is based on statistics and data which make out a consistent pattern year after year. Third the existence of liquor vends on highways presents a potent source for easy availability of alcohol. The existence of liquor vends; advertisements and sign boards drawing attention to the availability of liquor coupled with the arduous drives particularly in heavy vehicles makes it abundantly necessary to enforce the policy of the Union government to safeguard human life. Once it is an accepted position that the presence of liquor vends along the highways poses a grave danger to road safety an exception cannot be carved out to permit the sale of liquor along a stretch of the highway which passes through the limits of a city, town or local authority. Such an exception would be wholly arbitrary and violative of art. 14 of Constitution. Hence, issue certain directions that all states and union territories shall forthwith cease and desist from granting licences for the sale of liquor along national and state highways;(ii) The prohibition contained in (i) above shall extend to and include stretches of such highways which fall within the limits of a municipal corporation, city, town or local authority; (iii) The existing licences which have already been renewed prior to the date of this order shall continue until the term of the licence expires but no later than 1 April 2017;iv) All signals and advertisements of the availability of liquor shall be prohibited and existing ones removed forthwith both on national and state highways. Order accordingly.
