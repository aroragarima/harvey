Chilukuri Venkateswarlu v Chilukuri Venkatanarayana

Supreme Court of India

08 December 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 121; AIR 1954 SC 176; 1954 (1) MLJ(SC) 152; [1954] S.C.R. 424

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Civil Procedure - Family & Personal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 12 - Suit filed by plaintiff represented by his maternal uncle as next friend, for recovery of possession on partition, of a half share in properties on allegation that they were joint family properties of himself and his father, defendant no. 1 - Defendant no. 1 denied that he was father of plaintiff - Whether plaintiff was legitimate son of defendant No. 1 by defendant No. 2 his second wife? - Trial Court decided this point in favour of plaintiff and against defendant No. 1 and in that view substantially allowed plaintiff's claim - On appeal, decision of Trial Court was reversed - Present appeal - On evidence, it is clear that defendant No. 1 did not succeed in proving that there was no opportunity for intercourse between him and defendant No. 2 at time when plaintiff was conceived - Held, High Court erred in holding that there was no opportunity for access between parties at material period, relying mainly upon what husband himself said and did much after estrangement of feelings took place between parties, no matter whatever that was due to - On evidence in record, findings of HC cannot possibly stand - Appeal allowed.
