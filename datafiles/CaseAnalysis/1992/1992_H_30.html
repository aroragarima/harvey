Pritam Pal v High Court Of Madhya Pradesh, Jabalpur Through Registrar

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 221; (1993) SCC (Cr) 356; (1993) Supp1 SCC 529; AIR 1992 SC 904; AIR 1992 SCW 681; 1992 CRLJ 1269; [1992] 1 S.C.R. 864; 1992 (2) SLR 16

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss. 2(b), 14, 17, 20 and 22 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 129 and 215 - (A) Power of Supreme Court and High Court to punish contempt - (B) Applicability of summary procedure in contempt proceeding - (C) Criminal contempt - Advocate made libelous allegations against sitting judges of HC - Interference with administration of justice - (D) Criminal contempt - Sentence - Advocate practising in HC - No apology, contrition or repentance shown either before HC or Supreme Court - Held, (A) Not restricted or trammelled by ordinary legislations - Power is to be used sparingly - Procedure should be fair - ( B) Summary procedure to be followed - (C) Amounts to interference with administration of justice - (D)Two months imprisonment awarded - Appeal dismissed.
