Supreme Court of India

10 March 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Ghulam Hasan

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 54; AIR 1954 SC 321; 1954 (56) BomLR 1176; 1954 CRLJ 886; 1954 (1) MLJ(SC) 618; 1954 SCJ 385; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 930

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 497 - Appellant prosecuted for adultery - Whether s. 497 contravenes arts. 14 and 15 of Constitution? - Held, art. 14 is general and must be r/w other provisions which set out ambit of fundamental rights - Sex is a sound classification and although there can be no discrimination in general on that ground, Constitution itself provides for special provisions in case of women and children - Two articles read together validate impugned clause in s. 497 - Appeal dismissed.
