Pulicherla Nagaraju @ Nagaraja Reddy v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

18 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 405; (2006) 11 SCC 444; AIR 2006 SC 3010; 2006 CRLJ 3899; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 95; 2007 (1) RLW 419; 2006(8) SCALE 133; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 633

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Self Defence

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 300, 302, 304, 307, 313, 34 - Trial Court acquitted all accused by extending them benefit of doubt - Appeal allowed by HC holding that rejection of evidence of PW-1 and PW-2 by trial court was unjustified and perverse - Conviction under ss.302, 324 and sentence of imprisonment for life - Appeal against - Held, evidence of a witness cannot be discarded merely on ground that he is either partisan or interested or closely related to deceased, if it is otherwise found to be trustworthy and credible - HC can review and consider entire evidence and come to its own conclusions by either accepting evidence rejected by trial court or rejecting evidence accepted by trial court - Inention to cause death or at all events intention of causing bodily injury which is sufficient in ordinary course of nature to cause death was made out - Crcumstances to bring case under Exception (4) to s. 300 do not exist - Appeal dismissed.
