Simranjit Singh Mann v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 75; (1992) 4 SCC 653; (1993) SCC (Cr) 22; AIR 1993 SC 280; AIR 1992 SCW 3133; 1993 CRLJ 37; JT 1992 (5) SC 441; [1992] Supp1 S.C.R. 592

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 303 and 304 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Locus Standi of a third party to challenge conviction and sentence of accused - Whether a person who is a total stranger to to prosecution culminating in conviction of accused has locus standi to challenge conviction? - Held, a third party can neither under provision of Constitution nor under CrPC, 1973 or any other statute is permitted to question correctness of conviction and sentence imposed by Court after a regular trial - Petition dismissed.
