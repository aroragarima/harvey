Shankar Narayan Bhadolkar v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 268; (2005) SCC (Cr) 22; (2005) 9 SCC 71; AIR 2004 SC 1966; 2004 CRLJ 1778; JT 2004 (3) SC 211; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 508; 2004(3) SCALE 78; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 868; 2004 (2) SLT 498; 2004 (3) Supreme 652

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ballistics

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, ss 302, 304 A, 304 Part II, 201 and 80 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s 27 - Arms Act, s.25 (1 A) - Where act of accused itself criminal in nature protection u/s. 80 not available - If pleads exception within meaning of s.80 there is a presumption against him and burden to rebut presumption lies on him - For protection u/s. 304 A there should be neither intention nor knowledge to cause death - When any of these two elements found to be present, s.304 A has no application - For s 300, there must be an intention to inflict that particular bodily injury - Offence committed covered by ss 304 Part II - S 201 has been established beyond even a shadow of doubt that dead bodies were discovered on basis of discovery statement in terms of s 27 of Indian Evidence Act - Appeal disposed of.
