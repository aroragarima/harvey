Rakesh Kumar Sharma and another v Government of (NCT of Delhi) and others

Supreme Court of India

29 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 636; (2013) 11 SCC 58; 2014 (1) AWC 1001; 2013(10) SCALE 42; [2013] 8 S.C.R. 486; 2014 (3) SLR 444

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Employment-Termination-Recruitment to post of Trained Graduate Teachers-Appointment, temporary/provisional, subject to verification of various aspects including that of educational qualification-Appellant did not possess requisite qualification on last date of submission of application though he applied representing that he possessed the same-Service of appellant terminated for not possessing requisite eligibility as on last date of submission of applications-Whether appellant could claim any relief, if for one reason or other his result had not been declared up to last date of submission of application form?-Held, "no"-Settled legal proposition that selection process commences on date when applications are invited-Any person eligible on last date of submission of application has right to be considered for appointment against vacancy provided he fulfill requisite qualification-Grant of any benefit to appellant would be violative of doctrine of equality-No obligation on Court to protect illegal appointment-Usurpation of post by ineligible candidate in any circumstance - Impermissible-Process of verification and notice of termination in instant case followed within very short proximity of appointment and was not delayed at all so as to even remotely give rise to expectancy of continuance-No special features warranting any interference with impugned order of High Court upholding termination order.
