Sheo Nath Singh v Appellate Assistant Commissioner Of Income Tax, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

12 August 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 266; (1972) 3 SCC 234; AIR 1971 SC 2451; 1973 (2) CTR 484; [1971] 82 ITR 147; [1972] 1 S.C.R. 175; 1971 UJ 802

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Taxation of Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947 - Constitution, art. 136 and 226 - (A) Petition u/art. 226 - Procedure - Preliminary issue - High Court after sustaining the preliminary objection of respondent proceed to decide the question on merits - (B) Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 34(1A) - Reasons for belief - Held, in petition u/art. 226 the correct course is not to proceed to decide the question on merits but to dismiss the Writ Petition, however in appeal u/art. 136 by the same petitioner when the HC has given decision on merits which would be binding on authorities concerned, SC cannot decline to go into question of merits - Held, to stem proceedings for reassessment there must be some material or fact in reasons on which any belief can be founded u/s. 34(1A) of 1922 Act - Appeal Allowed.
