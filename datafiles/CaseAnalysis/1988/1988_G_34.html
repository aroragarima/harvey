Tejinder Kaur v Gurmit Singh

Supreme Court of India

23 February 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 359; (1988) 2 SCC 90; (1988) SCC (Cr) 313; AIR 1988 SC 839; 1988 (1) AWC 523; 1988 (1) DMC 355; 1988 (1) HLR 584; JT 1988 (1) SC 395; 1988 (1) PLR 629; 1988(1) SCALE 398; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 1098; 1988 (1) UJ 491

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Special Marriage Act, 1954 - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 - Limitation Act, 1963 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Dissolution of marriage by decree for divorce - Appeal against - Dismissal by High Court - Whether right for Special Leave Petition of one spouse can be taken away by a decree of dissolution of marriage granted by HC? - Held, spouse won in HC and got a decree of dissolution of marriage - Marrying immediately after HC's judgment cannot take away right of application for special leave to appeal from other spouse.
