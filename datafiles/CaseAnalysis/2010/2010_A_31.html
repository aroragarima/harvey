Administrator, Union Territory of Dadra and Nagar Haveli v Gulabhia M. Lad

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 320; (2010) 5 SCC 775; 2010(3) ALL MR 968; 2010 (3) AWC 3242; 2010 (125) FLR 880; 2010 (3) JhrCR(SC) 24; 2010 (4) LLJ 12; 2010 (4) LLJ 17; 2010 (5) MLJ(SC) 514; 2010(4) SCALE 454; [2010] 5 S.C.R. 309; 2010 (4) SLR 747; 2011 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 145

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Dadra and Nagar Haveli Land Reforms Regulation, 1971

Summary: Service - Central Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1965, r. 14 - Central Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1964, r. 3 - Dadra and Nagar Haveli Land Reforms Regulation, 1971 - Discrimination in inflicting punishment - Respondent while functioning as Land Reforms Officer allegedly granted occupancy rights of the government land to five persons with ulterior motive by getting the survey conducted from 'X' Surveyor and without following the procedure prescribed under 1971 Regulations - Disciplinary enquiry were initiated against the respondent and two other delinquents and order of removal from service were passed against them - Respondent filed appeal against said order of punishment before Appellate Authority who dismissed said appeal but partly allowed the appeals of other two delinquents - Tribunal accepted the argument of the respondent that he has been discriminated in the matter of imposition of punishment and allowed the original application filed by the respondent - Petitioner-Department challenged said order of tribunal which was dismissed by HC on the ground that authorities did not challenge the orders passed by the Appellate Authority in respect of co-delinquents, hence, order of tribunal did not call for any interference - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of HC could be upheld? - Held, substance of the charge against the respondent was that as a Land Reforms Officer, he granted occupancy rights to the government land to five persons with ulterior motive by getting the survey conducted from co-delinquent 'X', Surveyor and without following the procedure prescribed under the Regulations - On the other hand, the main charge against 'X' was that he prepared a map by not following the procedure and without verifying the documentary evidence as was required under the Regulations and assigning new plot numbers without any authority in flagrant violation of law - As regards, 'Y', he was principally charged for having prepared the statement on oath of each of the applicants in his own handwriting in the absence of the applicants and thereby abusing his official position as Patel Talati - Thus, there was variation in allegations of misconduct and all the three delinquents could not have been put on par although joint enquiry was held and there was common evidence - Moreover, Tribunal failed to notice that respondent was holding an important position as Land Reforms Officer during the relevant period having been conferred with various powers and duties under the Regulations and co-delinquents were only his subordinates and they carried out his instructions - Therefore, respondent and the two co-delinquents cannot be said to have been similarly placed - Appeal allowed.
