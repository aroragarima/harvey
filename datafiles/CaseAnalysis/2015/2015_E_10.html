Escorts Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, Faridabad

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 310; (2015) 9 SCC 109; 2015 (319) E.L.T. 406; JT 2015 (7) SC 493; 2015 (4) MLJ 325; 2015(5) SCALE 653; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 241

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Duty Of Excise, Central Excises And Salt Act, 1944, Excisable Goods

Summary: Excise - Intermediate product - Payment of excise duty - Tractors that were manufactured had engines that were below 1800 CC and were covered by exemption notification - By show cause notice, Department for period relied upon evidence to show that Transmission Assemblies of tractors was commodity known to market as such and, therefore, came into category of excisable goods - Commissioner by order held that transmission assembly was not chassis and since notice had failed to declare transmission assembly chassis assembly, suppression of fact was clear - In appeal, CESTAT dismissed appeal - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether excise duty is payable on intermediate product which comes into existence during manufacture of tractors made by appellant.

Held, the Transmission Assemblies of tractors are commercially known products. The fact that not a single sale of such Assembly has been made by the appellants is irrelevant. The Transmission Assembly of the tractor on the facts is clearly an intermediate product which is a distinct product commercially known to the market as such. Added to this, the appellants have also clear that not a single Transmission Assembly has in fact been sold by them in the market. There was no suppression on the part of the appellants nor was there any willful at-tempt to evade duty. Appellant has been manufacturing tractors. There has never been any change in the manufacturing process. Show-cause notice itself clear that the issue of manu-facture and captive consumption of Transmission Assemblies for tractors is the same as that for IC engines. Further, Transmission Assembly is only recognized as an Assembly line in-termediate product and not as a product in itself which is separately identifiable as in the case of other Assemblies such as axel shaft, engine, gear parts, instrument panels, etc. In successive declarations made by the assessee had declared not merely tractor but the chassis therefor. The assessee bonafide believed that the declaration of the chassis would suffice as according to them Transmission Assemblies were not taxable goods. Therefore, show cause notice needs to be quashed on this ground alone. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Moment product is commercially known in sense of fulfilling practical test of being known to persons in the market who buy and sell, the test is satisfied.
