Â State of Jammu and Kashmir and Others v Caltex (India) Limited

Supreme Court of India

17 December 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, P. Satyanarayana Raju

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 83; AIR 1966 SC 1350; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 149; 1966 (17) STC 612

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution - Sales-Tax Laws Validation Act, 1956 - Jammu and Kashmir Motor Spirit (Taxation of Sales) Act, 2005 - Constitution (Sixth Amendment) Act, 1956 - (A) Whether sales tax could be imposed on respondent for period from October, 1955, to May, 1959, in view of prohibition contained in art. 286(2) of Constitution as it stood before its amendment? - Held, on a reading of facts it is manifest that there was movement of goods from State of Punjab to State of Jammu and Kashmir under contract of sale and there was completion of sale by passing of property and delivery of goods to purchaser - Thus, two conditions of inter-State trade, namely, a sale of goods and a transport of those goods from one State to another under contract of sale, is satisfied - (B) Whether sales tax could be validly levied on sales taking place between 01-01-1955 to 06-09-1955, in view of provisions of Sales Tax Laws Validation Act, 1956? - Further held, respondent will be liable to pay sales tax if it is shown that it has made retail sales of motor spirit within meaning of s. 3 of Act - On facts of this case, s. 3 of Act applies to transactions of sale of petrol made by respondent for period from 1955 to 06-09-1955, assessment of sales tax made by taxing authorities for this period is legally valid - (C) Whether High Court was in error in taking view that taxing authorities were not entitled to levy sales tax for period from 01-01-1955 to 06-09-1955? - Further held, it is true that there was one order of assessment for period from 1955 to May, 1959, but assessment can be easily split up and dissected and items of sale can be separated and taxed for different periods - In reaching conclusion that entire assessment was invalid HC has relied on decision of Judicial Committee in Bennett and White (Calgary) Ltd. v. Municipal District of Sugar City No. 5 - But principle has no application in present case because sales tax is imposed, in ultimate analysis, on receipts from individual sales or purchases of goods effected during entire period and it is possible to separate assessment of receipts derived from sales for period from 01-01-1955 to 06-09-1955, and to allow taxing authorities to enforce statute with respect to sales taking place in this period and also prevent them by grant of a writ from imposing tax with regard to sales for exempted period - Appeal partly allowed.
