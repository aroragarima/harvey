D. Velusamy v D. Patchaiammal

Supreme Court of India

21 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 876; (2010) 10 SCC 469; 2011 CRLJ 320; 2010 (6) CTC 216; 2010 (173) DLT 1; 2010 (4) JCC 2891; JT 2010 (11) SC 325; 2010 (4) KLT 384; 2010 (4) MLJ(Crl) 345; 2011(1) M.P.H.T. 228; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 827; 2010 (4) RCR(Criminal) 746; 2011 (1) RLW 198; 2010(11) SCALE 112; 2010 (3) UC 1825; 2010 (4) WLN(SC) 96

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 125 - Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005, s. 2(a) - Maintenance for live-in-relationships - Respondent filed petition u/s. 125 CrPC before Family Court alleging that she was married to the appellant on 14.9.1986, and that he left her after two or three years of living together with her in her father's house - Respondent submitted that as she did not have any kind of livelihood, appellant be directed to pay maintenance to her - Petitioner contended that he was already married to 'X' and respondent was not married to him though he lived with her for some time - Family Court Judge held that the appellant was married to the respondent and not to 'X' - HC upheld the order of the Family Court - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of the Family Court as affirmed by the HC could be upheld and appellant was liable to pay maintenance to the respondent? - Held, no - In order to get maintenance, a woman, even if not married, has to fulfil the four requirements i.e. (1) The couple must hold themselves out to society as being akin to spouses, (2) they must be of legal age to marry, (3) they must be otherwise qualified to enter into a legal marriage and (4) they must have voluntarily cohabited and held themselves out to the world as being akin to spouses for a significant period of time - Merely spending weekends together or a one night stand would not make it a 'domestic relationship' - In the present case, HC and the Family Court erred in law in holding that the appellant was not married to 'X' without even issuing notice to 'X' - Hence this finding has to be set aside and the matter remanded to the Family Court which may issue notice to 'X' and after hearing her give a fresh finding in accordance with law - The question whether the appellant was married to the respondent or not can, be decided only after the aforesaid finding - Appeal allowed.
