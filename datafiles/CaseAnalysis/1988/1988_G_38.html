Rachappa Guruadappa Bijapur v Gurusiddappa Nuraniappa And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

16 November 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 277; (1989) 3 SCC 245; AIR 1989 SC 635; 1989 (1) ARBLR 157; 1988 (2) CCC 904; 1989 (1) GLH 443; JT 1988 (4) SC 497; 1988(2) SCALE 1455; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 884; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 884; 1989 (1) UJ 144

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration Act, 1940 - Stay of legal proceedings - Requirement of filing application for stay before filing written statement or taking any other step - Seeking adjournment for filing written statement amounts to any other step - Stay not permissible thereafter - Where counsel appearing for party to suit had sought adjournment specifically for filing written statement and obtained time for more than one occasions for such purpose, subsequent application for stay of suit would not be maintainable - Held, party evinced an intention to have matter adjudicated by Court.
