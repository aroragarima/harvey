Devendra Swamy v Karnataka State Road Transport Corporation

Supreme Court of India

05 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20558; (2002) 9 SCC 644; (2002) SCC (L&S) 1093; AIR 2002 SC 2545; 2002 (92) FLR 400; JT 2001 (10) SC 433; 2002 LabIC 2475; 2002 (1) LLJ 454; 2001 (8) SLT 535

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 11 and 10(1)(c) - Whether order of the disciplinary authority awarding major punishment of dismissal of the appellant from service was rightly restored by the Divisional Bench? - Appellant had committed defalcations/misconduct on 41 occasions prior to the initiation of departmental enquiry and was found guilty and punished, which was a weighty factor - Labour Court did not suitably advert to the material brought to its notice - Appellant was found to have been involved in 36 instances of misconduct, this fact is not disputed by the appellant in rejoinder filed to the counter-affidavit of the respondent - Appellant has not learnt any lesson and is incorrigible - Held, gravity of charge of misconduct for which the disciplinary proceedings were initiated and which charge was found to be substantiated by the Labour Court, seen in the light of previous service record of the appellant fully justified the punishment awarded by Disciplinary Authority - No case is made out calling for interference with the order of the Division Bench, which is a very fair, reasonable and just order - The appeals are dismissed
