Â Shree Digvijay Cement Company Limited and Others v State of Rajasthan and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 December 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, S.P. Bharucha, V.N. Khare, D.P. Mohapatra, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 382; (2000) 1 SCC 688; AIR 2000 SC 680; JT 1999 (10) SC 201; 2000 (117) STC 395; [1999] Supp5 S.C.R. 428

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Keywords: Collected, Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, Central Sales Tax (Amendment) Act, 1957

Summary: Sales Tax - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 8(4)(5) - Notification D/- 12-03-1997 - Validity of - Writ petition against notification issued by State of Rajasthan u/s. 8(5) of Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 whereby it reduced rate of sales tax on inter-State sale of cement by any dealer from that State to 4 per cent and did away with requirement of furnishing of declaration in Form C or certificate in Form D contemplated by s. 8(4) of Act - Special Leave Petition granted - It was held that reduction in tax will reduce sale of cement and thereby affect free trade - Appeal in same court was made earlier but matter was referred to a Larger Bench - Held, as per s. 8 of Central Act chances of reducing sales tax below 4% shall be nil - This section also classifies goods but an amendment took place which enabled a State Govt. (in place of Central Govt. under amended provisions), if it so desired, to exempt any goods or class of goods from Central sales tax, or to prescribe a lower rate of tax - The Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 is enacted under authority of Union Parliament, but tax is collected through agency of States and is levied ultimately for benefit of States and is statutorily assigned to States - Variation in tax rate allowed as per s. 8(5) and no trade barrier created - Dispensing with requirement of furnishing declaration in Form C did not have effect of facilitating evasion of payment of tax and was violative of scheme of Constitutional provisions - Validity of notification upheld - Writ petition dismissed.
