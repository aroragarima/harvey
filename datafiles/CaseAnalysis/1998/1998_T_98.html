The Deputy Commissioner, Andaman District, Port Blair. v Consumer Co-Operative Stores Limited., Hari

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1913; (1999) 1 SCC 507; AIR 1999 SC 696; 1999 (80) ECR 276; 2000 (121) E.L.T. 10; JT 1998 (8) SC 467; 1998(6) SCALE 473; 1998 (10) SLT 150; [1998] Supp3 S.C.R. 454; 1998 (9) Supreme 335

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Excise Regulation

Summary: Andaman and Nicobar Islands Regulation, 1876, Regn.31A; Andaman Excise Rules 1934, r.2 - Levy of excise duty - Respondents having licence to vend Indian made foreign liquor on the basis of a public auction - Notification by Chief Commissioner of Andaman & Nicobar Islands prescribing the rate of excise duty payable by the licensees-respondent liquor - Levy of excise duty declared unconstitutional by Apex Court - Burden of payment, however, not passed on to purchaser till then - Held, State not entitled to resist claim of refund by application of doctrine of 'unjust enrichment' - Direction by High Court to refund illegal levy collected - Not improper.
