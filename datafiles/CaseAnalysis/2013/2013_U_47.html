Union of India and others v Bhanwar Lal Mundan

Supreme Court of India

27 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 581; (2013) 12 SCC 433; AIR 2014 SC 776; 2013(6) ALL MR 474; 2014 (1) AWC 501; 2013 (139) FLR 1003; JT 2013 (12) SC 69; 2013 (6) MLJ 736; 2013(10) SCALE 646; [2013] 8 S.C.R. 559; 2013 (6) SLR 220

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Employment-Pension-When date of superannuation approached and pension was going to be determined-It was noticed by accounts department that respondent had been given excess pay due to erroneous fixation of pay scale-Competent authority re-fixed respondent's pay prior to his retirement and directed recovery of excess sum-Central Administrative Tribunal quashed order of refixation and directed benefit of pension be extended to respondent on basis of pay he was actually drawing before retirement, failing which employer liable to pay interest @ 15% p.a.-Fixation of pay was erroneous-Therefore, authorities were within their domain to rectify same-Irresistible conclusion that Central Administrative Tribunal and High Court fallen into error by opining that respondent would be entitled to get pension on basis of pay drawn by him before his retirement-Impugned orders of Tribunal and High Court directing fixation of pension on basis of last pay drawn-Set aside-However, no recovery from excess amount paid to respondent.
