Chabungbam Ibohal Singh v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, Kuldip Singh, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1576; (1995) Supp2 SCC 83; (1995) SCC (L&S) 768; 1995 (29) ATC 663; JT 1995 (3) SC 163; 1995(1) SCALE 857; 1995 (2) SLR 374; 1995 (1) UJ 507; 1995 (2) UPLBEC 966

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16 - Whether appellant's fundamental right u/art. 16 has been infringed by not promoting him in Indian Administrative Service in time as required by provisions concerned governing promotion to that service? - Held, on a perusal of service record of appellant from 1970 till 1982 and proceedings of Selection Committee from 1974 till 1980, it is found that in 1974 non-recommendation was not only due to non-issuance of integrity certificate by officer concerned of State which, it may be stated, was because of some disciplinary proceeding pending against appellant in relation to his integrity, which proceeding came to be dropped afterwards, but also because Selection Committee took note of adverse remarks in ACRs of appellant and observed that his speed in working was slow and he was censured in 1969-70 - As appellant was being superseded by one of his juniors, if it was enough on part of Selection Committee to have merely stated 'unfit', and then to recommend name of one of his juniors - Perusal of ACRs of appellant shows that he cannot be rated as 'very good'; indeed, it would be difficult to regard him even as 'good' - So, no injustice has really been caused to appellant in recommendation of one of his juniors for promotion - No illegality had been committed by Selection Committee or injustice had been caused in not recommending name of appellant prior to 1981 for his promotion to Indian Administrative Service - Appeal dismissed.
