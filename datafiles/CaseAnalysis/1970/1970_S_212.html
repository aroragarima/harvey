Tapinder Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 323; (1970) 2 SCC 113; (1970) SCC (Cr) 328; AIR 1970 SC 1566; 1970 CRLJ 1415; 1971 (1) SCJ 751; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 599; 1970 UJ 656

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32 - Murder and Dying Declaration - Deceased implicated appellant as his assailant - No other witness present on the spot where deceased was shot - Whether conviction of the appellant solely on the dying declaration of the deceased is sustainable? - Held, yes - Dying declaration was recorded by Magistrate within four hour of incident and it cannot be said that within that short span of time deceased had been tutored to falsely name the appellant as his assailant - Principles laid down: (1) No absolute rule of law that a dying declaration cannot form the sole basis of conviction unless corroborated; (2) Each case must be determined on its own facts keeping in view the circumstances of dying declaration; (3) Dying declaration is not a weaker kind of evidence than other pieces of evidence and stands on the same footing as another piece of evidence; (4) Dying declaration recorded by a competent magistrate in the proper manner can be sole basis of conviction - No reason for interference with the conviction of the appellant - Appeal dismissed.
