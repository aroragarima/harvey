(1) Sirsilk Limited; (2) Century Spinning and v Textiles Committee & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

12 September 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 708; (1989) Supp1 SCC 168; (1989) SCC (Tax) 219; AIR 1989 SC 317; JT 1988 (4) SC 592; 1988(2) SCALE 975; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 880

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Textiles

Keywords: Textile Committee Rules 1965, Textiles Committee Rules, 1965

Summary: Textiles - Constitution, arts. 226 and 265 - Textiles Committee Act, 1963, s. 2(g) - Textiles Committee Rules, 1965, r. 21 - Averments - Company is a manufacturer of Rayon and Nylon yarn - Their products are species of man made fibers - (A) Whether the plea that Rayon Yarn and Nylon Yarn manufactured by them are 'filaments' and not 'fibre' or that they are not 'yarn' be accepted - (B) Whether Rayon and Nylon Yarn are yarn of artificial silk within meaning of s. 2(g)of the Textiles Committe1963 s. 2(g) (as it stood prior to 1973) - (C) Whether the levy of fee u/r. 21of 1965 Rules is unconstitutional u/art. 265 - Held, the plea cannot be countenanced -Interpretation of Statutes in furtherance of object of Act and considering statute in pari materia - Industries (Development and Regulation) Act, 1951 and the Textiles Committee Act are yarn of artificial silk -The levy of fees u/r. 21is unconstitutional for absence of sufficient quid pro quo - Appeal dismissed.
