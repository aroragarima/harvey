Smt. Mona Panwar v The Hon’Ble High Court Of Judicature At Allahabad Through Its Registrar And Others

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 74; (2011) 3 SCC 496; 2011 (2) ACR 1498; 2011 (2) ADJ 503; 2011 (1) AllCJ 719; 2011 (3) AllCriC 216; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 1015; 2011 CRLJ 1619; 2011 (2) JCC 969; 2011 (1) JhrCR(SC) 185; 2011 (2) JIC 200; JT 2011 (2) SC 75; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 856; 2011(2) SCALE 150; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 413; 2011 (2) SLR 102

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 156 - Expunging of disparaging remarks - Appeal filed by appellant, member of judicial service of the State of Uttar Pradesh, for expunging the remarks made by the Single Judge of the HC of Allahabad in criminal application while setting aside order passed by the appellant, registering the application filed by the respondent No. 3 u/s. 156(3) of the CrPC alleging that she was raped by her father-in-law and directing the Registry to present the file before the appellant for recording the statement of the complainant u/s. 200 of CrPC - Single Judge of the HC was of the view that the appellant had done the gravest injustice to the respondent No. 3 and the order indicated total non-application of mind by the appellant - Single Judge also expressed the view that the appellant had passed the order ignoring all judicial disciplines and had not at all applied her judicial mind and had only referred to some of the judgments of the Allahabad HC, which were contrary to the opinion expressed by the SC rendered in many decisions - Whether Single Judge of HC was justified in making these observations against the appellant? - Held, when the complaint was presented before the appellant, the appellant had mainly two options available to her; one was to pass an order as contemplated by s. 156(3) of the CrPC and second one was to direct examination of the complainant upon oath and the witnesses present, if any, as mentioned in s. 200 of the CrPC and proceed further with the matter as provided by s. 202 of the CrPC - Taking cognizance does not involve any formal action or indeed action of any kind but occurs as soon as a Magistrate applies his mind to the suspected commission of an offence - In the instant case, appellant had called for report from the concerned police station and considered the said report wherein it was mentioned that no case was registered on the basis of the application made by the respondent No. 3 - Under the circumstances the appellant had exercised judicial discretion available to a Magistrate and directed that the application, which was submitted by the respondent No. 3 u/s. 156(3) of the CrPC, registered as complaint and directed the Registry to present the said complaint before her for recording the statement of the respondent No.3 u/s. 200 of the CrPC - Judicial discretion exercised by the appellant was in consonance with the scheme postulated by the CrPC - There was no material on the record to indicate that the judicial discretion exercised by the appellant was either arbitrary or perverse - Appellant was the responsible judicial officer on the spot and after assessing the material placed before him he had exercised the judicial discretion - In such circumstances, HC had no occasion to interfere with the discretion exercised judiciously in terms of the provisions of CrPC - Disparaging remarks made by the Single Judge of the Allahabad HC, were not justified and therefore set aside - Appeal disposed of.
