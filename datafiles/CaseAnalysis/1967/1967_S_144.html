Madan Lal v Sunderlal and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 March 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 396; AIR 1967 SC 1233; 1967 ALJ 589; 1967 BLJR 669; 1967 (2) ILR(All) 423; 1968 (1) SCJ 572; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 147

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Award set aside of - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 17, 30 and 33 - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 158 - An agreement was entered into between appellant and respondents referring certain differences between them to arbitration of three persons - An award was made, signed by two out of three arbitrators - Award was filed in court and respondents prayed for a decree in accordance with award - Notice of filing of award was issued to appellant and appellant filed an objection in nature of a written statement - By this objection appellant attacked the validity of award on various grounds - When matter came to be heard in Trial Court, respondents contended that so-called objection was in nature of an application to set aside award and contained grounds coming u/s. 30 of Act - Therefore, as objection was filed more than 30 days after notice was served on appellant, it was barred by limitation under art. 158 of Act - Trial Court also held that objection could not be treated as an application u/s. 33 of Act in view of fact that it was beyond 30 days as required by art. 158 of Act - High Court came to same conclusion that award could not be set aside on grounds - Whether the order of HC justifiable in law? - Held, the grounds specified u/s. 30 are the only grounds on which an award can be set aside and it will be seen that if a party wants an award to be set aside on any of these grounds it has to made an application - Thus any party wishing to have an award set aside on ground that it was improperly procured or otherwise invalid has to make an application - It is clear therefore from scheme of act that if a party wants an award to be sat aside on any of grounds mentioned in s. 30 it must apply within 30 days of date of service of notice of filing of award as provided in art. 158 of Act and if no such application is made award cannot be set aside on any of grounds specified in s. 30 of Act - Appeal dismissed.
