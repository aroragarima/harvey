A. Rajendra Kumar v Registrar, Supreme Court of India and others

Supreme Court of India

12 July 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1901; (2005) 13 SCC 443

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Supreme Court Rules, 1966, O.21 r.15(1)(e) - Constitution of India, 1950, art.145 - Summery dismissal of appeals - Validity of provision - Petitioner filed instant petition challenging vires of O.21 r.15(1)(e) of Rules on ground that u/art.145 of Constitution, this Court can only frame rules empowering this Court to dismiss an appeal summarily in cases only when appeal was frivolous or vexatious or brought for purposes of delay - It was contended that O.21 r. 15 of Rules does not provide that summary disposal is to be only when appeal was frivolous, vexatious or brought for the purposes of causing delay - It was submitted that rule was ultra vires inasmuch as it vests in this Court a power to summarily dismiss even where an appeal was not frivolous, vexatious - Hence instant petition.

Held, this Court is final Court. Against orders and judgments of Court, there is no further appeal. Therefore, this Court has repeatedly held that it need not give any reasons while disposing of matter. Thus, when O.21 r.15 of Rules provides for summary disposal, it need not state that such summary disposal can only be where Court is satisfied that appeal is vexatious or frivolous or that it has been filed merely for purposes of causing delay. Merely because rule and/or order of Court does not use words âvexatiousâ, âfrivolousâ or âfor the purposes of causing delayâ does not mean that appeals can be summarily dismissed even though appeals are not vexatious or frivolous or filed for causing delay. Court considers each case on its merits. Without coming to conclusion that matter is vexatious or frivolous and/or was brought only for purposes of causing delay, no appeal is summarily rejected. It must not be forgotten that before disposing of appeal summarily, Judges have read appeal in detail. They have considered merits of appeal. Thereafter reasonable hearing is granted to counsel. It is only after being satisfied that there is no substance in appeal, same is dismissed summarily. Even thereafter, remedy of review is available. At time of review, this Court again considers matter. O.21 r. 15(1)(e) of Rules has been in existence for last over 40 years and it has stood test of time and cannot be set aside at this stage at behest of party like petitioner who is disgruntled litigant. Petition dismissed.

Ratio - When a provision had been used by Court for long years and it was found to be reasonable, it shall not be quashed for unmerited reasons.
