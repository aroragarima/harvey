Â Sita Ram and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

24 January 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, P.N. Shingal, P.S. Kailasam, D.A. Desai, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 509; (1979) SCC (Cr) 576; (1979) 2 SCC 656; AIR 1979 SC 745; 1979 CRLJ 659; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 1085

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 21, 132, 134(1)(2), 136 and 145 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 366, 374, 375, 379, 382 and 384 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 149 - SC Rules, O. 21, r. 15(1)(c) - Constitutional validity of cl. (c) of sub-r. (1) of r. 15 of O. 21 of Supreme Court Rules challenged - Held, the contention of the impugned rule is beyond the rule-making power of the SC under art. 145 of the Constitution cannot be accepted as art. 145(1)(b) specifically enables the Supreme Court to frame rules as to the procedure for hearing appeals - The contention, that the rule is opposed to the provisions of laws made by Parliament and is thus beyond the scope of rule-making powers under art. 145 cannot also be upheld - Validity of vires of O. 21, r.15(1)(c) of SC Rules and also s. 384 of CrPC, 1973 and is within rule-making power of SC - To be directed to notice to respondent.
