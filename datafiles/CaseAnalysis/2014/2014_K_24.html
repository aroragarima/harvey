Ashraf Kokkur v K. V. Abdul Khader Etc.

Supreme Court of India

29 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 558; (2015) 1 SCC 129; AIR 2015 SC 147; 2014(5) ALL MR 880; 2014 (6) AWC 5977; 2014 (4) CLT(SC) 81; JT 2014 (11) SC 248; 2014 (3) KLT 1091; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3225; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 236; 2014(9) SCALE 780; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 1123

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Material Facts, Disqualified, Office Of Profit Under

Summary: Election - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 191(1)(a) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7 r. 11(a) - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 83 - Office of profit - Disqualification - Respondent was Chairperson of State Wakf Board, contested State Legislative Assembly election - Appellant (petitioner) objected the nomination of respondent as respondent was holding office of profit under State Govt., which was rejected - Appellant filed petition before HC, which was dismissed holding that it did not disclose a complete cause of action - Whether averments in election petition disclose a cause of action as required under O. 7 r. 11(a) of CPC - Appellant contended that respondent was disqualified u/art. 191(1)(a) of Constitution -

Held, pleadings, if taken as a whole, would clearly show that they constitute the material facts so as to pose a triable issue as to whether the respondent no. 1 was disqualified to contest election to the Kerala State Legislative Assembly while holding an office of profit under the State government as Chairperson of the State Wakf Board. The question was not whether the Chairperson of the State Wakf Board was an office of profit or not. That was the issue to be tried. Question was whether the appellant (petitioner) has raised such a question in the election petition. The disqualification under the Constitution being, holding an office of profit under the State Govt. Petitioner has furnished all the material particulars in that regard. Therefore, the petition discloses a cause of action. After all, the inquiry under O. 7 r. 11(a) of CPC was only as to whether the facts as pleaded disclose a cause of action and not complete cause of action. The limited inquiry was only to see whether the petition should be thrown out at the threshold. In an election petition, the requirement u/s. 83 of 1951 Act is to provide a precise and concise statement of material facts. The expression 'material facts' plainly means facts pertaining to the subject matter and which are relied on by the election petitioner. Election petition disclosed a cause of action. Election petition is remitted to the HC for trial in accordance with law. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - In an election petition, requirement of material facts, u/s. 83 of 1951 Act, plainly means facts pertaining to the subject matter and which are relied on by the election petitioner. If the party does not prove those facts, he fails at the trial.
