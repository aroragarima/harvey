Girish Yadav and Others v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

29 March 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2789; (1996) 8 SCC 186; (1996) SCC (Cr) 552; 1996 (3) AD(SC) 327; AIR 1996 SC 3098; AIR 1996 SCW 1557; 1996 (2) CCR 21; 1996 (2) Crimes 1; 1996 CRLJ 2159; JT 1996 (3) SC 615; 1996(3) SCALE 168; [1996] 3 S.C.R. 1021; 1996 (3) Supreme 168

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 300 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 8 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 319 - (A) Murder - FIR promptly recorded and not ante-timed or doctored one - Report sent promptly to Magistrate - Contents of FIR corroborated by eye - witnesses - Eye - witnesses residing in same locality coming of their own on spot - Merely because they were known to complainant - Could it be said that they would depose falsely? - Medical evidence supporting prosecution version regarding inflicting of injuries by accused - Fact that contusions on arm or palm might not have been noticed by doctor - Whether sufficient to discard eye-witnesses account? - If conviction of accused to be interfered with - (B) Evidence Act, s. 8 - Hearsay evidence - Murder - Would recital in site map prepared by police remain hearsay - Could it be read as evidence in case? - (C) Framing of charge - Police not submitting charge sheet against certain persons - Subsequently on recording evidence of eye-witnesses it was found that there was enough involvement of said persons in commission of offence - Whether court in exercise of power u/s. 319 can proceed against them? - Held, (A) It could not be said only on that ground - Not sufficient - Not interfered with - (B) Would remain purely hearsay and could not be read as evidence - (C) Court can proceed against them - Appeal dismissed.
