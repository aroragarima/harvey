R.V.E. Venkatachala Grounder v Arulmigu Viswesaraswami and V.P. Temple and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 841; (2003) 8 SCC 752; AIR 2003 SC 4548; 2004 (1) ALD(SC) 18; 2004 (2) JhrCR(SC) 34; JT 2004 (6) SC 442; 2004 (136) PLR 612; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 704; 2003 (2) RCR(Rent) 579; 2003(8) SCALE 474; 2003 (1) SCW 5316; 2003 (6) SLT 307; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 450; 2003 (8) Supreme 193

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 13, r. 4, s. 100 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 116, 3, 32(2), 34 - Tamil Nadu Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Act, 1959, s. 63 - Appeal filed against judgment and decree by HC - Ownership of property - HC framed questions on assumption as if property belongs to temple whereas findings of courts below were to contrary - Held, no question of law, much less a substantial question of law arose in case worth being gone into by HC in exercise of its second appellate jurisdiction u/s. 100 - HC was bound by findings of fact arrived at by two Courts below and should not have entered into exercise of re-appreciating and evaluating evidence findings of facts arrived at by courts below did not suffer from any perversity - There was no non-reading or misreading of evidence - A high degree of preponderance of probability proving title to suit property was raised in favour of appellant and courts below rightly concluded burden of proof raised on plaintiff having been discharged while onus shifting on defendant remaining undischarged - Further held, judgment of HC cannot be sustained and has to be set aside.
