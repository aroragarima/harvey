State of Kerala and Others v Koliyat Estates

Supreme Court of India

13 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 695; (1999) 8 SCC 419; 1999 (37) ALR 445; JT 1999 (6) SC 531; 1999 (3) KLT 553; 1999(5) SCALE 461; 2000 (Supp) RD 443; [1999] Supp2 S.C.R. 307

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Prohibition Act, 1949 - Kerala Private Forest (Vesting and Assignment) Act, 1971 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 60 - Kerala Land Reforms Act, 1970, ss. 86(2) and 85(5) - Respondent, a firm possessed extensive acres of plantation in Kerala - In 1967 firm obtained a loan from Central Bank of India - As loan was sanctioned under a scheme propounded by Govt. called Agricultural Refinancing Scheme, a tripartite agreement was executed between bank and respondent and State Govt. of Kerala - While mortgage was subsisting Kerala Land Reforms Act came into force - Consequently right, title and interest of firm over an area of more than 1200 acres of land covered by mortgage became vested in Govt. - By virtue of Act, 1971 another area of 273.75 acres of land, included in mortgage deed, stood vested in Govt. - Respondent filed suit for redemption of mortgage - Trial Court held in favour of respondent - High Court confirmed order - (A) When a mortgaged property, is vested in Govt. by operation of law, would it amount to Govt. 'acquiring' said property as contemplated in s. 60, TP Act, 1882? - Vesting of land in Govt. was free from all encumbrances - If land is covered by a mortgage liability therein would not remain with that part of land which Govt. got through vesting process - (B) Whether mortgagor can claim of pro tanto reduction of mortgage liability can now be considered? - S. 60, TP Act,1882 deals with right of a mortgagor to redeem, on payment or tender of mortgage money - In order to invoke solitary exception to last paragraph of s. 60 TP Act there must be a conjunction of two postulates - One is that share of mortgagor in property should have been 'acquired' - Second is that person who so acquired should have been mortgagee - State has acquired possession of so much portion of mortgage property when it vested in State by statutory process envisaged in two enactments - Govt. became owner of part of mortgaged property not as a mortgagee, even apart from fact that such vesting was through statutory process - By becoming owner of a part of mortgaged property it is not necessary that mortgage money would have been discharged even proportionately - It depends upon how mortgagee got a share in mortgaged property - Held, vesting of portion of mortgaged property with Govt. and subsequent assignment of mortgage right in favour of Govt. are not sufficient to formulate exception provided in last paragraph of s. 60, TP Act, 1882 - Mortgagor not entitled to pro tanto reduction in mortgage money - Appeal disposed of.
