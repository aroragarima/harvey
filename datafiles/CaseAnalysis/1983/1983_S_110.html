Darshan Singh @ Bhasuri & Ors. v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 82; (1983) 2 SCC 411; (1983) SCC (Cr) 523; AIR 1983 SC 554; 1983 (1) Crimes 1059; 1983 CRLJ 985; 1983 CRLR 235; 1983 CrLR(SC) 235; 1983(1) SCALE 299; [1983] 2 S.C.R. 605

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Practice and Procedure - CrPC, 1974, s. 154 -

Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32 - Conviction - (A) Contended in defence that that their name didn't appear in F.I.R - Held, there is no rule of law which stipulates that an accused whose name is not mentioned in an F. I. R. is entitled to an acquittal - (B) Dying declaration was questioned - Medical evidence on record found that the vital organs of the deceased, like peritoneum, stomach and spleen were completely smashed and under above circumstances dying declaration can't be accepted - Appeal partly allowed.
