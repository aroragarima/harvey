M/S. Gas Authority Of India Limited And Another v M/S. Keti Construction (I) Limited And Others

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 493; (2007) 5 SCC 38; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 378; 2007 (3) AWC 2802; 2007 (4) CompLJ 1; JT 2007 (6) SC 581; 2007 (5) MahLJ 441; 2007 (5) MLJ(SC) 1251; 2007 (3) MPLJ 458; 2007 (3) RCR(Civil) 133B; 2007(7) SCALE 98; 2007 (5) SCJ 793; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 439

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Appointment Of Sole Arbitrator

Summary: Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 34 - High Court set aside the award given by the arbitrator - Appeal against - Held, where a party raises a plea that the arbitral tribunal has not been properly constituted or has no jurisdiction, it must do so at the threshold before the arbitral tribunal so that remedial measures may be immediately taken and time and expense involved in hearing of the matter before the arbitral tribunal which may ultimately be found to be either not properly constituted or lacking in jurisdiction, in proceedings for setting aside the award, may be avoided - If plea of jurisdiction is not taken before the arbitrator as provided in s. 16 of the Act, such a plea cannot be permitted to be raised in proceedings under s. 34 of the Act for setting aside the award, unless good reasons are shown - Appeal allowed.
