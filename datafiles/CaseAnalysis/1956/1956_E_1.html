Caltex (India) Limited v E. Fernandes and Another

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, S.K. Das, P. Govinda Menon

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 124; AIR 1957 SC 326; 1957 (1) LLJ 1

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 33 - Respondent smoking on duty at Airport Service Station - Dismissal for misconduct - Whether decision of Labour Appellate Tribunal was without jurisdiction as appeal before Labour Appellate Tribunal did not involve any substantial question of law? - Punishment is sole discretion of employer who is to judge what is punishment commensurate with offence proved against workmen - Industrial Tribunal has no jurisdiction while entertaining an application u/s. 33 to consider whether punishment is harsh or excessive - Only jurisdiction which Industrial Tribunal has u/s. 33 is to determine whether a prima facie case made out by any mala fides or unfair labour practice or victimization - Held, Industrial Tribunal had no power to substitute another punishment - Appeal allowed.
