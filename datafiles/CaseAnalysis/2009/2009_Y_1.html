State of Karnataka v Y. Moideen Kunhi (Dead) By Lrs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 576; (2009) 13 SCC 192; AIR 2009 SC 2577; 2010 (1) CLT(SC) 87; JT 2009 (13) SC 560; 2009(6) SCALE 677; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 392

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Delay In Filing Appeal, Directions issued, Application For Condonation Of Delay In Filing Appeal

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - 'Sufficient Cause' - Large pieces of agricultural lands purchased through partnership firm - Land reform proceeding - Determination of land held in excess of ceiling limit - Order D/- 27-09-1982 passed by Land Tribunal in respect of lands held by firm, giving finding as to extent of excess land - State and the partners of firm preferred writ petitions - Writ petition of State was dismissed on merits on 07-11-1990 - Present SLPs filed thereagainst - Delay is of about 6500 days against original order - Delay whether condonable or whether there is 'sufficient cause' to warrant such condonation? - (1) Whether such delay should result in the negation of the state's claim and at the cost of the interest of the members of the public whose cause has not been carefully espoused? - Submission made by appellant State that in such cases, delay must be visited with consequences but the interest of the inhabitants of the State must be protected - Decisions on subject referred and deliberated upon - Huge delay pitted against cause of substantial justice - Matters concerning the State Govt. and the Central Govt. are delayed either by the nature of bureaucratic process or by deliberate manipulation of the same by taking advantage of loopholes in the conduct of litigation - Court took judicial notice of many instances of late filing of appeal/petition where revenue involved runs to several crores of rupees - In instant case more than 4000 acres of land are involved - (1) Court has to protect public justice, the same cannot be rendered ineffective by skillful management of delay in the process of making challenge to the order which prima facie does not appear to be legally sustainable - Expression 'sufficient cause' must receive a liberal construction so as to advance substantial justice - Held, in view of important question of law involved, delay condoned but with condition of cost - Cost imposed - On payment of cost, SLPs shall be listed for admission - Directions issued.
