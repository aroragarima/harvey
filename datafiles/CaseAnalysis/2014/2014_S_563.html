State Bank of India and others v Surya Narain Tripathi

Supreme Court of India

11 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 118; 2014 (4) ADJ 223; 2014 (2) MLJ 494; 2014(3) SCALE 536; 2014 (6) SLR 210; 2014 (1) UPLBEC 634

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16 - Compassionate appointment - Denied - Challenged - X, father of respondent No. 1 was working in the State Bank of India from 27-12-1969 and he died while in service on 19-1-1998 after completing more than 28 years of service - Respondent No.1 applied for a job on compassionate basis and his application was turned down by the Bank which led to the writ petition - Said writ petition was allowed by the Single Judge - DB confirmed the judgment of a Single Judge - Hence, this appeal by special leave - Whether order of the Single Judge as affirmed by the DB could be upheld -

Held, in all the matters of compassionate appointment it must be noticed that it was basically a way out for the family which was financially in difficulties on account of the death of the bread earner - It was not an avenue for a regular employment as such - This was in fact an exception to the provisions u/art. 16 of the Constitution - That being so, if an employer points out that the financial arrangement made for the family subsequent to the death of the employee was adequate, the members of the family could not insist that one of them ought to be provided a comparable appointment - In the instant case, deceased left behind a large family - The fact however, remains that by now 15 years have gone since then - Besides the Bank had made appropriate financial provision at par with similar arrangement - Therefore it was not possible for that the Court could have directed the Bank to consider compassionate appointment - Judgment rendered by the Single Judge as well as by the DB were set aside - Appeal allowed.
