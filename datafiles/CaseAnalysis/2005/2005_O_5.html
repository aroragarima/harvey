Andhra Bank v Official Liquidator and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 215; (2005) 5 SCC 75; AIR 2005 SC 1814; 2005 (2) BC 425; 6 CLC 531; [2005] 124 Comp Cas 453; 2005 (4) CompLJ 33; 2005 (1) DRTC 705; JT 2005 (3) SC 506; 2005 (3) MLJ(SC) 19; 2005(3) SCALE 178; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 776

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Recovery Officer

Summary: Banking and Finance - Companies Act - Correctness of statement of law in para 76 of judgment of this Court in Allahabad Bank vs Canara Bank and Another [(2000) 4 SCC 406] - Andhra Bank directed to pay a sum of Rs. 38 lacs to official liquidator for purpose of disbursing forthwith salary to officers, staff and workers - Whether impugned judgment could have been passed by way of an ad hoc measure in view of fact that company was sold as a going concern and workers' dues were to be paid from sale proceeds of assets of company? - Argument that payment could be made to parties to winding up proceedings only upon considering claims of all creditors and in terms of certificate issued by Debts Recovery Tribunal under RDB Act not accepted - Held, order passed by Single Judge cannot be sustained on amongst others, ground of not assigning any reason in support thereof - Rights and obligations of the parties would only be crystallized after the lis is adjudicated upon - Appeal allowed.
