Ashvinbhai Jayantilal Modi v Ramkaran Ramchandra Sharma and another

Supreme Court of India

25 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 651; (2015) 2 SCC 180; 2014 (4) ACC 1; 2014 ACJ 2648; 2015 (1) ALD(SC) 131; 2014 (6) AWC 6134; 2015 (2) G.L.R. 1175; 2014 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4549; 2015 (4) MahLJ 36; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 543; 2014(11) SCALE 427; 2014 (4) WLN(SC) 176

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Claim - Enhancement - Entitlement of - Claimant/appellant filed a claim petition, claiming Rs.28,73,000/- as compensation - Tribunal awarded an amount of Rs.18,59,200/- with interest at the rate of 9% p.a. to the appellant - Appellant preferred appeal for enhancement of compensation, whereas respondent/Insurance Co. preferred appeal for the reduction of compensation awarded by Tribunal - HC dismissed the appeal filed by appellant and allowed the appeal filed by respondent - Hence instant appeals.

Held, there was no negligence on the part of the deceased. The courts below have failed to examine the facts of the case on hand with respect to the opinion of SC given in Juju Kuruvila & Ors. v. Kunjujamma Mohan & Ors., 2013 Indlaw SC 355. From the evidence produced, the two- wheeler of the deceased was dragged up to a stretch of about 20-25 feet on the road after the collision with the offending truck. SC is of the considered view, that to be able to create this kind of enormous effect on the two-wheeler of the deceased, the offending truck must have been travelling at a fairly high speed and that its driver did not have sufficient control over his vehicle. The driver of the offending truck should have been aware that he was driving the heavy motor vehicle and taken sufficient caution. SC do not see any direct evidence that shows negligence on the part of the deceased that led to the accident. Therefore, as per the principles laid down by this Court in the case referred to above in this aspect, the contributory negligence apportioned by the courts below on the part of the deceased is set aside. Total compensation payable to the appellant by the respondent will be Rs.27,25,000/- with interest at the rate of 9% p.a. Appeals allowed.
