Uttam Construction Company v Income Tax Officer, Central Circle, Amritsar

Supreme Court of India

05 March 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, V.D. Tulzapurkar, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 114; (1982) 2 SCC 543; [1982] 133 ITR 909

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Assessment - False return - Prosecution validity - Appellant/assessee was firm which filed its return on income for relevant assessment years - Revenue found that âSâ was one of partner of assessee firm who was not genuine while at filling its return - Revenue initiated prosecution against partner âSâ of assesseeâs firm for filling false returns which was confirmed by Tribunal - Hence instant appeal.

Held, after heard parties special leave granted. In view of finding recorded by Income tax Appellate Tribunal that it was clear on appraisal of entire material on record that âSâ was partner of assesseeâs firm and that firm was a genuine firm, Court do not see how assessee can be prosecuted for filing false returns. Court, accordingly, allow appeal and quash prosecution. Appeal allowed.
