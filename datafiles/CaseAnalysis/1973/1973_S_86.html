Balkrishna Chhaganlal Soni v State Of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

22 October 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 136; (1974) SCC (Cr) 45; (1974) 3 SCC 567; 1974 ACJ 13; AIR 1974 SC 120; 1974 CRLJ 280; 1973 CrLR(SC) 771; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1527; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 107

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Defence of India Rules, 1962, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Sea Customs Act 1962

Summary: Constitution, art. 134 - Defence of India Rules, 1962, rr. 126i and 126P(2)(ii) - CrPC, 1898, s. 32 - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 107 and 135(b) - (A) Interference with concurrent findings of fact - When - (B) Interpretation of Statutes - Meaning of 'Any person' u/s. 107 of Customs Act - (C) Offence of possession of gold without declaration under Rules - Conviction - Sentence of imprisonment - Propriety - Held, SC will exercise jurisdiction u/art. 134, only exceptionally when grave miscarriage of justice arising from gross perversity in appreciation of evidence has fouled such a finding - The expression 'any person' in s.107 includes a person who is subsequently arraigned as an accused in the trial in respect of smuggled gold, he can be examined u/s. 107 - Held, plea for elimination of sentence cannot be accepted in view of the new horizon in penal treatment to smugglers, hoarders, adulterators etc - Appeal Dismissed.
