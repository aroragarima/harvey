New India Assurance Company Limited v Gopali and others

Supreme Court of India

05 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 637; (2012) 12 SCC 198; 2012 (5) AWC 4807

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Constitution of India-Article 142-Motor Vehicles Act, 1988-Sections 166 and 168-Compensation for death-Enhancement - Multiplier-Deduction-While making deduction towards personal expenses, hard realities of life to be kept in mind-Application of rule of 1/3 deduction in all cases-Wholly unrealistic-Single Judge of High Court did not commit any error in not following rule of 1/3 deduction towards personal expenses of deceased-High Court justified in determining amount of compensation by granting 100% increase in income of deceased-Application of multiplier of 10 wholly unjustified-As per Sarla Verma, (2009) 6 SCC 121 : 2009 (2) ACCD 924 2009 (3) AWC 2138 (SC), multiplier should be 13-As deceased was aged 36 years at time of accident-Appeal dismissed-With a view to doing complete justice to claimants court suo motu redetermined amount of compensation to total amount of R 10,63,040 with 12% interest per annum.
