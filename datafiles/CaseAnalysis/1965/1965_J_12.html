Commissioner of Income Tax, Calcutta v Jaipuria China Clay Mines Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

01 November 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 231; AIR 1966 SC 1187; [1966] 59 ITR 555; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 449

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax (Amendment) Act, 1953 - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 6, 10 and 24 - Whether unabsorbed depreciation of past years should be added to depreciation of current year and aggregate of unabsorbed depreciation and current year's depreciation be deducted from total income of previous year relevant for assessment year? - Unabsorbed depreciation allowance is carried forward under proviso (b) to s. 10(2)(vi) of 1922 Act and method of carrying it forward is to add it to amount of allowance or depreciation in following year and deeming it to be part of that allowance - Effect of deeming it to be part of that allowance is that it falls in following year within cl. (vi) and has to be deducted as allowance - In case of depreciation and to extent that loss was caused by depreciation being not fully absorbed there would be no limit to carrying forward of that depreciation, and that depreciation can be set off at any time so long as business showed a profit in future - Appeal dismissed.
