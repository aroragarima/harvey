M. Janardhana Rao v Joint Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Ruma Pal, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 79; (2005) 2 SCC 324; AIR 2005 SC 1309; 2005 (4) CompLJ 23; [2005] 273 ITR 50; JT 2005 (2) SC 402; 2005 (1) MahLJ 1142; 2005 (2) MPLJ 401; 2005(1) SCALE 637; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 874; [2005] 142 TAXMAN 722

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: [A.] Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-Tax Act, 1961, s. 260A - Finance (No.2) Act, 1998 - Finance Act, 1999 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 100, 100(1), 100(3), 100(4), 100(5) - Manner of disposal of appeals by HC questioned - Whether HC right in disposing of appeals? - Held, it is clear that no substantial question of law was formulated at time of admission of appeal - HC has formulated questions subsequently after conclusion of arguments for purpose of adjudication - It is clearly against scheme of s.260 A - Matter remitted to HC to deal with matter afresh keeping in view prescriptions of s.260 A - Appeals disposed of.
