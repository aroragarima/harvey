Ajambi (Dead) By Lrs. v Roshanbi and others

Supreme Court of India

30 August 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 715; (2010) 11 SCC 168; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 46; 2011 (84) ALR 286; JT 2010 (9) SC 229; 2011 (3) KarLJ 219; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 805; 2011 (112) RD 259; 2010(8) SCALE 577; [2010] 10 S.C.R. 717

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 22 r. 5 - Plaintiffs filed suit seeking a decree for partition and separate possession in the suit property - Trial Court decreed the suit by awarding 1/8th share to the plaintiff Nos. 1 to 6 and 1/16th share to plaintiff Nos. 7 and 8 in the suit property and also 1/8th share in favour of the defendant - Appeal was filed before Civil Judge - During pendency of the appeal, the defendant produced some additional documents - First Appellate Court dismissed the appeal - On appeal, HC remanded the matter to the First Appellate Court for fresh disposal with a direction to receive documentary evidence produced before it by the defendant by way of additional evidence - During the pendency of the second appeal defendant died leaving behind his wife 'A' and she was brought on record as the legal heir - Additional Civil Judge allowed the appeal and held that the additional documents 'Will' were executed by the predecessor-in-interest during his lifetime and he disposed of the property as per his Will by dividing the property into two parts as claimed by the defendant - On appeal, HC set aside the judgment and decree of the first appellate court - HC held that a partition deed was to be compulsorily registered and since Will was not a registered document, it could not be relied upon - HC held that said document cannot be acted and relied upon for the purpose of establishing a prior partition of the suit property - Present appeal filed by appellant alongwith an application praying for bringing appellant on record as the legal representative of the deceased 'A' - Appellant claimed herself to be the beneficiary under the Will executed by 'A' during her lifetime through which 'A' allegedly bequeathed her property to the appellant - Held, issues, namely, the status of the appellant and whether she would claim to be a legal representative along with the question as to whether will propounded by the appellant was legal and valid and how far the same could be relied upon, are disputed questions of fact which are required to be determined by the court more appropriately by resorting to the provisions of O. 22 r. 5 of the CPC - The said two issues being questions of fact, the parties must be allowed to lay their evidence in support of their respective cases - In that view of the matter, it is necessary to issue a direction in the present case to the aforesaid extent in terms of the provisions of O. 22 r. 5 of the CPC - Trial Court directed to take evidence on the two issues - Order accordingly.
