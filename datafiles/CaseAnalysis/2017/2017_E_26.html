Eregowda @ Vasu v Divisional Manager, united India Insurance Company Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

18 June 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, L. Nageswara Rao, Navin Sinha

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SCO 409; 2017 (155) FLR 978

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Absence, age, doctor, driver, Editor, Industrial, Labour, profession, Workmen's Compensation

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law â Compensation â Enhancement of â Appellant was a driver and in an accident, he had suffered certain injuries resulting in the some disabilities - Doctor had assessed the physical disability suffered to be to the extent of 52% - Labour Commissioner awarded compensation by taking into account the loss of earning capacity to be 50% of the income â Appellant filed appeal for enhancing of compensation which was rejected by the HC â Hence, instant appeal.

Held, appellant would not be able to discharge the duties of driver any further. Though the physical disabilities in the present case has been assessed at 52%, the loss of earning capacity would be 100%. For the aforesaid reasons, SC modify the award of the Workmens Compensation Commissioner and award compensation on the basis of 100% loss of earning of the appellant i.e. Rs.4,500/- per month. The compensation will now be recomputed on the aforesaid basis by the Commissioner and the balance compensation along with interest at the rate of 6% per annum thereon be paid to the appellant by the Insurer forthwith. Appeal allowed.
