Union of India and others v Ajay Wahi

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 456; (2010) 11 SCC 213; AIR 2010 SC 2603; 2010(5) ALL MR 471; 2010 (6) AWC 5570; 2010 (126) FLR 904; JT 2010 (6) SC 411; 2010 (6) MLJ(SC) 421; 2010(6) SCALE 282; 2010 (4) SLR 368; 2010 (3) UPLBEC 1831

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Disability Pension, Pension Regulations

Summary: (A) Defence & Security Forces - Service - Pension Regulations, regn. 48 and 50 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Entitlement for disability pension - Respondent sought premature retirement, inter alia, stating that his 'falling health is affecting' his performance - Commanding Officer recommended for consideration of his case for 'invalidment/premature retirement after obtaining the opinion of a Senior Adviser' - However, by order dt. 26-07-1994, respondent's prayer for premature retirement was approved and he was allowed to leave the unit - Respondent made claim for grant of disability pension - He was informed that he is neither entitled for service pension nor disability pension vide letter dt. 30-03-1995 - Respondent filed writ petition contending that he ought to have been granted premature retirement on medical ground and sought voluntary retirement under pressure and, therefore, entitled to disability pension - Said writ petition was dismissed by Single Judge on the ground that since the petitioner has sought voluntary retirement, he is not entitled to any disability pension award - On appeal, the DB set aside the order of Single Judge and directed for payment of disability pension - Hence, present appeal - Whether respondent entitled for disability pension? - Held, an officer is entitled for disability pension only when he is invalided out of service on account of disability attributable to military service or aggravated thereby and shall not be entitled for disability pension in case of voluntary retirement, unless it is found and held that the officer deserved to be invalided out of service on account of disability attributable to military service but the same was not granted to him for unjustified reasons and forced to seek voluntary retirement - In the present case it has not been determined in accordance with Appendix II of the Regulations that respondent's voluntary retirement was accepted on the ground of disability attributable to or aggravated by military service and, therefore, he shall not be entitled for disability pension - Impugned judgement of DB set aside and order of Single Judge restored - Appeal allowed.

(B) Defence & Security Forces - Service - Pension Regulations, regn. 48 and 50 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Whether regn. 50 of the Regulations is discriminatory and thus violative of art. 14 of the Constitution of India? - Held, no - provision of the Statute can be declared ultra vires only when it patently violates some provision of the Constitution - Regulation under challenge, does not suffer from any such error - Appeal allowed.
