Mysore Minerals Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

01 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1183; (1999) 7 SCC 106; AIR 1999 SC 3185; AIR 1999 SCW 3146; 1999 (156) CTR 1; [1999] 239 ITR 775; JT 1999 (6) SC 444; [1999] Supp2 S.C.R. 182; [1999] 106 TAXMAN 166; 1999 (2) TLR 866

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 32 - Whether depreciation u/s. 32 is allowable on building when possession has been received against part payment but deed of conveyance not executed? - The provision should be so interpreted and words used therein should be assigned such meaning as would enable assessee securing benefit intended to be given by Legislature to assessee - Held, building was used for purposes of business of assessee - Depreciation legitimately belongs to one who has invested in capital asset and is utilizing capital asset - Assessee is entitled to depreciation on building - Answered in favour of assessee - Appeal allowed.
