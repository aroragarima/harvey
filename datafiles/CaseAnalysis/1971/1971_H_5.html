Hakam Singh v M/S. Gammon (India) Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

08 January 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 223; (1971) 1 SCC 286; AIR 1971 SC 740; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 314; 1971 UJ 167

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: CPC, 1908, s. 20, Expln. II - Contract Act, 1872, s. 28 - (A) CPC, 1908, s. 20 - Parties cannot by agreement confer jurisdiction on Court not possessed by it under the Code - But agreement that one of the Courts having such jurisdiction alone shall try dispute - Legality - (B) Scope of word 'Corporation' in Expl. II to s. 20 of 1908 Act - Held, is not contrary to public policy and does not contravene s. 28, Contract Act - Word includes not only statutory Corporation but also company registered under Companies Act - Appeal Dismissed.
