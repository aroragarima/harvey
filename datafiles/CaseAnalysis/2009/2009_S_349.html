Mohammad Yasin v State (Nct Of Delhi) & Others

Supreme Court of India

30 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 919; (2009) 14 SCC 644; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1956; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 3731; 2009 CRLJ 4405; 2009 (4) JCC 2483; 2010 (3) MLJ(Crl) 559; 2009(10) SCALE 302; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 1188

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Custodial Death, Eye- Witness

Summary: Criminal - Custodial death - Deceased died in police custody - Police apprehended deceased at 1.30 pm on 4.8.1999 and he was taken to ESI Hospital at 3.10 pm on same day where he was declared brought dead - Writ petition filed by appellant (brother of deceased) seeking directions to station house officer of concerned police station to register a case of murder against delinquent officers was disposed by HC - HC held that death of the deceased was caused due to cardiac arrhythmias - Hence, present appeal - Appellant relied on judgment in D.K. Basu v. State of West Bengal 1996 INDLAW SC 1546 and submitted that respondents violated directions given in said judgment - Deceased was a proclaimed offender and was wanted by police in nine cases - Admitted position that deceased had a past history of heart ailments - Board of three Doctors gave clear and categorical finding that cause of death was due to ventricular arrhythmias consequent to old compromised heart in a person having hypertrophied heart (460 gms) myocardial fibrosis and atherosclerosis of coronaries - Board of Doctors further examined injuries and came to conclusion that all injuries present over body were antemortem, recent in duration and simple in nature and were insufficient to cause death individually as well as collectively in ordinary course of nature - Held, police and/or investigating agencies cannot torture any criminal despite his bad criminal record - Further held, in view of report of Board of Doctors and Sub-Divisional Magistrate inquiry, it is difficult to reach at any other conclusion except that deceased died because of his serious heart disease - No violation of directions given in D.K. Basu's case - Appeal dismissed.
