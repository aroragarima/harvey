Bihar Finance Service H. C. Coop. Soc. Limited v Gautam Goswami and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2598; (2008) 5 SCC 339; AIR 2008 SC 1975; 2008 (2) AWC 2102; JT 2008 (4) SC 374; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 177; 2008(4) SCALE 306; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 1137

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Patna Development Authority Act

Summary: (A) Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 - Parameters of jurisdiction of SC under the Contempt of Courts Act - Held, while dealing with such an application, the court is concerned primarily with : (i) whether the order passed by it has attained finality or not; (ii) whether the same is complied with or not - While exercising the said jurisdiction SC does not intend to reopen the issues which could have been raised in the original proceeding nor shall it embark upon other questions including the plea of equities which could fall for consideration only in the original proceedings - SC is not concerned with to the fact whether the original order was right or wrong - SC must not take a different view or traverse beyond the same - It cannot ordinarily give an additional direction or delete a direction issued - Petition disposed of.

(B) Practice & Procedure - Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4 and 6 - Contempt proceeding - Acquisition of land - Land acquisition proceedings were initiated for Bihar State Finance Service House Construction Cooperative Society - Several writ applications filed before the HC questioning the said proceedings were allowed - However, on an appeal preferred thereagainst, SC remitted the matters to the HC - HC pursuant to the said direction passed an order directing release of 12.9603 acres of land - Claims in respect of the rest of the lands were rejected and the District Magistrate was directed to identify the lands and deliver possession thereof to the petitioner-society, if necessary, after the demolition of the constructions made thereon - Meanwhile, several transactions were made, some of which were totally illegal - Appeal was filed thereagainst - SC further released 17.68 acres of land in favour of various contenders directing that remaining available land, shall be allotted to the Bihar State Finance Service House Construction Cooperative Society for whose benefit the acquisition of land was made - Patna Regional Development Authority (PRDA) was called upon to proceed in accordance with law as regards constructions already made in violation of the extant statute - Present contempt petition - Alleged, the said order was not complied with - Held, lands have been acquired in terms of the proceedings - Validity of the said proceedings has been upheld by SC - Amount of compensation has been deposited. Awards have been made - The court can at this stage neither go behind the awards nor various orders passed by SC - Petition disposed of.
