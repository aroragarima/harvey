Union of India and Others v Bihari Lal Sidhana

Supreme Court of India

25 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3017; (1997) 4 SCC 385; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1076; AIR 1997 SC 3659; 1997 (66) DLT 789; JT 1997 (4) SC 541; 1997 LabIC 2077; 1997 (3) LLN 73; 1997(3) SCALE 459; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 364; 1997 (2) SLR 523; 1997 (3) Supreme 679; 1997 (2) UPLBEC 1175

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - Central Civil Services (Temporary Service) Rules, 1965, r. 5(1) - Termination of services - Petitioner a temporary Govt. servant - Terminated employee acquitted in Criminal Case from charge of misappropriation of funds - Termination order indicates his suspension - Held, acquittal would not automatically entitle him to reinstatement - Suspension does not constitute stigma - Termination without enquiry u/r. 5(1) valid - Order of High Court to compensate employee - Not proper.
