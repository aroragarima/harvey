Gauri Shanker v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 275; 2015 (145) FLR 671; JT 2015 (4) SC 82; 2015(5) SCALE 275; 2015 (3) SLR 730; 2015 (4) WLN(SC) 81

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Forest, Rajasthan Industrial Disputes Rules, 1958, Central Industrial Dispute Rules, 1957

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 25F(a), 25F(b), 25G, 25H - Rajasthan Industrial Disputes Rules, 1958, rr.77, 78 - Retrenchment - Back-wages - Appellant-workman raised industrial dispute challenging order of retrenchment passed by respondent-department on ground that same was in violation of ss. 25F(a), 25F(b), 25G, 25H of the Act and so, retrenchment was void ab initio and prayed for setting aside the same - On perusal of record, Labour Court passed award of reinstatement holding that respondent-Department failed to comply with mandatory requirements as provided u/ss. 25F(a), 25F(b), 25G, 25H of the Act and so, action of respondent-department was in contravention of statutory provisions of the Act and rr.77 and 78 of the Rules and so, termination order was illegal and void ab initio - Aggrieved, respondent-Department filed writ petition before HC which held that order of termination was void ab initio but as appellant-workman was working just on casual basis, awarded compensation of Rs.1,50,000 in lieu of reinstatement - Aggrieved, appellant-workman filed appeal before DB of HC but the same was dismissed and hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned judgment and order of Single Judge of HC as affirmed by DB of HC is sustainable.

Held, non-compliance of mandatory requirements as provided u/ss.25F(a), 25F(b), 25G, 25H of the Act read with rr.77 and 78 of the Rules has rendered order of termination passed against appellant-workman void ab initio in law and Labour Court in absence of any material evidence on record in justification of case of respondent-department has rightly recorded the finding of fact and held that order of termination passed against the appellant-workman is bad in law, the same being void ab initio in law. Further, Labour Court rightly followed normal rule of reinstatement of workman in his original post as it has found that the order of termination is void ab initio in law for non compliance with mandatory provisions of the Act. However, Labour Court erred in denying back wages without assigning any proper and valid reasons and also same was erroneously modified by Single Judge of HC exceeding his jurisdiction u/arts. 226 and 227 of Constitution and principles laid down by SC in Harjinder Singh v. Punjab State Warehousing Corporation, 2010 INDLAW SC 234 and erroneously awarded compensation of Rs.1,50,000 in lieu of reinstatement. Hence, impugned judgment and order of Single Judge of HC as affirmed by DB of HC is set aside and award of Labour Court is restored so far as order of reinstatement is concerned. Further, respondent-department is directed to reinstate appellant-workman with payment of 25% back wages till date of award passed by Labour Court and pay full salary from date of award by Labour Court till date of his reinstatement. Appeal allowed.
