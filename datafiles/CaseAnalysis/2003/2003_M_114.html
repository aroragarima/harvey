Renu Devi v Mahendra Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

04 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 183; (2003) 10 SCC 200; AIR 2003 SC 1608; 2003 (2) ALD(SC) 74; 2003 (50) ALR 718; 2003 (5) AWC 3599; 2003 (2) CHN 130; 2003 (2) JhrCR(SC) 195; JT 2003 (5) SC 164; 2003 (94) RD 495; 2003(2) SCALE 22; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 820; 2003 (1) SCW 993; 2003 (2) SLT 444; 2003 (1) Supreme 1003

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land and Property - Family and Personal - (A) Partition - Held, if the rights of the parties are finally determined and no further inquiry remains to be held for the purposes of completing the proceedings in partition then there is nothing in law which prevents the Court from passing a final decree in the very first instance - Often such are the cases which are based on compromise - The present one is such a case - However, still one of the parties sought for a final decree being drawn up - The Court and the parties acted under the misapprehension that the decree dated 13/2/1978 was a preliminary decree and therefore a final decree was needed to be drawn up - HC was not right in holding that unless and until the final decree was passed on 13/2/1978 till then a gift of the property covered by the two decrees could not have taken place in between on 13/2/1978 (B) Rule of feeding the estoppel - Applicabilit of doctrine - Held, if a man, who has no title whatever to the property, grants it by a conveyance which in form carries the legal estate, and he subsequently acquires an interest sufficient to satisfy the grant, the estate instantly passes - Doctrine may not apply if the deed of transfer itself was invalid or if the third party has acquired title bona fide, for consideration and without notice - In the present case the execution and registration of deed of gift dated 13/2/1978 is not disputed Nor is there any illegality or invalidity attaching with the deed - The rule of estoppel by deed would clearly apply - Appeals allowed.
