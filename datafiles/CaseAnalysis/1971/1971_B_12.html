Bakhshish Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 737; (1971) 3 SCC 182; (1971) SCC (Cr) 251; AIR 1971 SC 2016; 1971 CRLJ 1452; 1971 UJ 281

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: IPC, 1860, s. 300 - Evidence Act,1872, s. 27 - Murder - (A) Motive - Inference of , from fact that deceased after having returned home after long period had claimed his share in family property from accused father and brothers - (B) Recovery of dead body by police on information given by accused on being questioned - Conclusiveness - (C) Appreciation of evidence - Case resting on circumstantial evidence - Chain of evidence - Nature - Held, cannot be inferred from mere fact - Held, is not a conclusive circumstance but it merely raises strong suspicion against him - In a case resting on circumstantial evidence chain of evidence must be such as not to leave any reasonable ground for conclusion consistent with the innocence of the accused and it must be such as to show that within all human probability the act must have been done by the accused - Appeal Allowed.
