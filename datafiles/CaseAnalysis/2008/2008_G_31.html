Kurella Naga Druva Vudaya Bhaskara Rao v Galla Jani Kamma Alias Nacharamma

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1241; (2008) 15 SCC 150; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 690; 2008 (3) AWC 2957; JT 2008 (9) SC 295; 2008(11) SCALE 160; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 849

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh (Andhra Areas) Tenancy Act, 1956

Summary: Andhra Pradesh (Andhra Areas) Tenancy Act, 1956 - Suit seeking possession of suit property and mesne profits - Whether the plaintiff's suit for possession in the civil court was not maintainable and whether the remedy was only by way of an eviction petition under section 13 of the Act; Whether the suit was not maintainable for want of a prayer for declaration of title; Whether the concurrent findings of fact recorded by the trial court and High Court that plaintiff was the owner of the suit property and that defendant had not made out title by adverse possession call for interference - Held, when plaintiff's case is that the defendant is a trespasser and the case of defendant is that he was the owner and he was never a tenant of the suit land either under plaintiff or anyone else, the suit was not for eviction of an agricultural tenant, and therefore, s. 13 - Neither any perversity nor omission to consider evidence nor any error of law has been pointed out with reference to consideration and appreciation of evidence by the trial court and the High Court - Appeal dismissed.
