Rekha Jain v National Insurance Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 480; (2013) 8 SCC 389; 2013 (3) ACC 424; 2013 ACJ 2161; AIR 2013 SC 3429; 2013 (6) ALD(SC) 35; 2013 (100) ALR 236; 2013 (5) AWC 4353; 2013 (3) CLT(SC) 252; JT 2013 (11) SC 185; 2013 (6) MahLJ 929; 2013 (6) MLJ 477; 2013 (4) MPLJ 546; 2013 (3) RCR(Civil) 996; 2013 (4) RLW 2853; 2013(9) SCALE 752

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Future Loss Of Income, Permanent Partial Disablement

Summary: (A) Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Compensation - Enhancement - Sustainability - Appeals were directed against the judgment of HC allowing the appeal of the Insurance Company/respondent and dismissing the appeal of the appellant by which she had prayed to set aside the impugned judgment, award and order and had further prayed for enhancement of compensation by award of just and reasonable compensation allowing the appeals urging various facts and legal contentions - Whether the impugned judgment, order and award passed by HC was legal and valid and Whether the Tribunal was justified in awarding Rs.17 lakhs and odd towards pecuniary damages in favour of the appellant namely, medical charges, medicine expenses and other expenses on the basis of the documentary evidence on record -

Held, Tribunal, in the absence of rebuttal evidence and the nature of cross examination of the appellant/ PW3 made by respondent/Insurance Company and the evidence adduced by the appellant and the claim made by her under the pecuniary damages towards the medical expenses, tests, surgeries etc. and other incidental purposes, had accepted and has rightly awarded a sum of Rs. 17,51,726/- under the heading of medical expenses - Same was arbitrarily and unreasonably, without assigning any cogent and valid reason, interfered with by HC and it had erroneously modified the judgment by reducing the amount from Rs. 17,51,726/- to Rs. 7,77,000 - It was very lightly interfered with by HC without application of mind and consideration of legal evidence on record particularly in the absence of rebuttal evidence and further, respondent/ Insurance Company was unable to show that the documents referred to supra produced by the appellant in her evidence were fabricated documents, which were produced with deliberate intention to prefer a false claim in this regard as contended by respondent/ Insurance Company - SC had carefully examined the evidence on record and the findings of the Tribunal to examine as to whether the findings recorded by HC in the impugned judgment for reduction of pecuniary damages from Rs.17,51,726/- to Rs.7,77,000/- was correct - On careful perusal of the evidence and documents produced by the appellant SC had to hold that the finding and reason recorded by HC was wholly erroneous in law as the same was contrary to the facts pleaded and proved by producing evidence on record - Therefore, the same required to be interfered with by SC in the appeals - Appeals allowed.

(B) Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Compensation - Enhancement - Sustainability - Appeals were directed against the judgment of HC allowing the appeal of the Insurance Company/respondent and dismissing the appeal of the appellant by which she had prayed to set aside the impugned judgment, award and order and had further prayed for enhancement of compensation by award of just and reasonable compensation allowing the appeals urging various facts and legal contentions - Whether the appellant was entitled to enhanced compensation under the different heads of non pecuniary damages -

Held, appellant was substantially entitled to enhancement of compensation under various heads of non-pecuniary damages having regard to the concurrent findings recorded on this aspect of the matter by HC - Both the Tribunal and HC had accepted the nature of injuries sustained by appellant and the percentage of permanent partial disablement suffered by her due to the Motor Vehicle Accident as per the Disability Certificate issued by the Chief Medical Officer - That aspect of the matter was very relevant for the purpose of examining the claim of the appellant and also to find out as to whether the Tribunal and HC were justified in not awarding just and reasonable compensation in favour of the appellant under the various heads of non- pecuniary damages - SC was required to keep in mind justice, equity and good conscience which should be the primary, relevant and important aspects for awarding just and reasonable compensation to an unfortunate victim, the appellant who had sustained grievous injuries to her body and whose future prospects were completely doomed - Appeals allowed.

(C) Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Compensation - Enhancement - Sustainability - Appeals were directed against the judgment of HC allowing the appeal of the Insurance Company/respondent and dismissing the appeal of the appellant by which she had prayed to set aside the impugned judgment, award and order and had further prayed for enhancement of compensation by award of just and reasonable compensation allowing the appeals urging various facts and legal contentions - If so, to what compensation and for what award the appellant was entitled to -

Held, Tribunal awarded compensation of Rs.2,00,000/- for the loss of amenities, pleasure of life and her inability to attend social functions in future, which was inadequate, therefore, it should be enhanced to Rs.10,00,000 - Towards the pain and suffering, the Tribunal awarded Rs.1,00,000 - It should be proper to award another Rs.9,00,000 as appellant had undergone ordeal for the period of 4 years continuously taking treatment in Odisha and Kerala States and the damages for mental and physical shock, pain and suffering, disfigurement of the face and other bodily injuries she already suffered continuously or likely to suffer - Tribunal awarded Rs.17,15,726/- towards the medical expenses based on the legal evidence and, therefore SC affirmed the compensation awarded by the Tribunal - Thus, the total compensation amounting to Rs.79,65,726 which was rounded of to Rs. 79,66,000/-along with interest at the rate of 6% per annum was awarded from the date of application till the date of deposit of the amount - Appeals allowed.
