Employees State Insurance Corporation v All India I.T.D.C. Employees Union and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 95; (2006) 4 SCC 257; 2006 (2) AWC 1714; JT 2006 (4) SC 26; 2006(3) SCALE 492; 2006 (4) SCJ 202; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 361; 2006 (2) UPLBEC 1290

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Employees State Insurance (Central) Rules, 1950

Summary: [A.] Practice & Procedure - Service - Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948, s. 1(4), s. 3 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Employees State Insurance (Central) Rules, 1950 - Notification that employees who were drawing monthly salary of Rs. 6, 500/- Which limit was earlier Rs. 3, 000/- Were required to make contributions at enhanced rate of 1.75 % in place of earlier figure of 1.5% - Stay was granted by HC - Appeal against - Plea that merely because of order of stay was granted, there was no bar on Corporation recovering amounts and HC's order virtually means that Notification has to operate prospectively - Held, while disposing of WPs with a direction to 'consider', there is a need for HC to make direction clear and specific - Order should clearly indicate whether HC is recording any finding about entitlement of petitioner to relief or whether petition is being disposed of without examining claim on merits - Corporation shall now give opportunity to concerned parties i e respondents 1, 2 and 3 in each case to present their respective stand before Corporation so that after consideration necessary order can be passed in accordance with law - Appeals disposed of.
