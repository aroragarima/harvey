Organon (India) Limited v Collector Of Excise

Supreme Court of India

22 July 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, M.M. Punchhi, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1036; (1995) Supp1 SCC 53; AIR 1994 SC 2489; JT 1994 (4) SC 438; 1994 (2) MWN 39; 1994(3) SCALE 421; [1994] Supp2 S.C.R. 50

Case Digest

Subject: Health & Drug

Keywords: Poppy, Dangerous Drugs Act, 1930, Opium Act, 1878, Narcotics Commissioner, Cotton Textiles (Control of Movement) Order, 1948

Summary: Health & Drug - Constitution of India, 1950 - Govt. of India Act, 1935 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 372 - Madhya Pradesh Poppy Husks Rules, 1959, rr. 3 and 8 - Dangerous Drugs Act, 1930 - Essential Supplies (Temporary Powers) Act, 1946 - Cotton Textiles (Control of Movement) Order, 1948, s. 5 - Opium Act, 1878 - (A) Whether State of Madhya Pradesh and its authorities are entitled in law to levy and collect 'duty' as a condition for permitting export of poppy husks from Madhya Pradesh when said export is not an export from one State to another State within India but an export across customs frontier of India? - Held, earlier movements, that is, from one State to another within India not involving crossing of customs frontiers would be governed by Opium Act and if Opium Act provides for levy of export duty on such export, it has to be paid export permit granted by Collector is an independent permit and is limited and operative within four corners of Opium Act and Rules made thereunder - Further, Narcotics Commr. is concerned only with movement of goods from Bombay across customs frontiers, by sea - (B) Whether duty levied u/s. 5 of Opium Act is valid? - Held, having regard to context in which said expression occurs, it cannot be understood in sense it carries in Entry 51 of List II In context, expression 'duty' means an amount which shall be collected as a condition for granting permission to possess, transport, import/export or sell opium - Thus duty provided by r. 3-A r/w r. 8 of Madhya Pradesh Poppy Husks Rules is perfectly within four corners of s. 5 - (C) Whether s. 5 is void on ground of excessive delegation of legislative power? - Held, s. 5 is not a taxing provision but only a regulatory provision and hence valid - Appeal dismissed.
