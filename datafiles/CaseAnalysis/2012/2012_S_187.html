Maruti Nivrutti Navale v State Of Maharashtra & Anr

Supreme Court of India

07 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 285; (2012) 9 SCC 235; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 3747; 2013 (1) G.L.R. 816; 2012 (4) JCC 2703; JT 2012 (9) SC 285; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 235; 2012(8) SCALE 572; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 979

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420, 465, 471 r/w s. 34 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 438 - Anticipatory bail - Denied - Challenged - Respondent (complainant), was a Trust, leased out its property to appellant (accused), who was manager of a society, to run a school - Respondent filed a complaint against appellant for violation of lease terms and for alleged offences u/ss. 420, 465, 471 r/w s. 34 - Appellant filed an application for anticipatory bail before Trial Court, which was dismissed - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before HC, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant had made out a case of grant of anticipatory bail u/s. 438 of CrPC - Held, respondent in March, 2008, offered to lease out school building for a period of 87 years to appellant and also to sell other property of respondent to appellant - Based on negotiations, 2 separate Memorandum of Understandings (MoUs) dt.10-5-2008 were signed between parties - It was claimed by respondent that a lease deed for a period of 35 months w.e.f. 15-4-2008 was executed and registered between parties and it was agreed not to act upon the 2 MoUs and on expiry of lease period i.e. on 9-3-2011, appellant was to handover possession of said building and land to respondent - All the materials were scrutinized/analyzed by Trial Court and HC while considering application for anticipatory bail - It was true that parties have approached Civil Court for various reliefs - At the same time, as pointed out by respondents, considering seriousness related to corrections/additions/alterations made in various documents, information furnished to the Educational Authorities which, according to them, are incorrect, in order to bring out all material information and documents, custodial interrogation was required, more particularly, to ascertain in respect of documents which were alleged to have forged and fabricated - Documents and materials which were in possession of appellant and allegation against him that he made false representation before Public Authority on the basis of those documents for obtaining necessary permission, in order to secure possession of those documents, custodial interrogation was necessary, for which Trial Court and HC rejected anticipatory bail application of appellant - It was alleged after granting interim protection order dt.23-9-2011 by SC, appellant misused his liberty in creating hindrance to investigation and continues to scuttle it and intimidated, pressurizing respondent (complainant) and prosecution witness - Interim protection order was vacated - Impugned order of HC was upheld - Appeal dismissed.
