Â Ramanbhai Ashabhai Patel v Dabhi Ajitkumar Fulsinji and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 378; AIR 1965 SC 669; 1965 (6) G.L.R. 423; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 712

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Leaflet, Prohibition Act

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 123(3) - Whether appellant could be said to be guilty of a corrupt practice contemplated by s. 123(3) ? - A reference to prophets or religions or to deities venerated in a religion or to their qualities and deeds does not necessarily amount to an appeal to religious sentiment of electorate - Something more has to be shown for this purpose - In considering question as to whether a particular appeal made by a candidate falls within mischief of s. 123(3), courts should not be astute to read into words used in appeal anything more than can be attributed to them on its fair and reasonable construction - High Court was in error in reversing judgment of tribunal, and that voters religious sentiments may be aroused would be a too far fetched a conclusion to be justified - Appeal allowed.
