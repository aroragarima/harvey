Shin-Etsu Chemical Company Limited v M/s. Aksh Optifibre Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Y.K. Sabharwal, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 532; (2005) 7 SCC 234; 2005 (4) AWC 3851; [2005] 127 Comp Cas 97; 2005 (5) CompLJ 483; JT 2005 (7) SC 426; 2005 (4) MLJ(SC) 84; 2005 (3) RCR(Civil) 740; 2005(6) SCALE 561; [2005] Supp2 S.C.R. 699; 2005 (3) UC 1836

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Foreign Award, Geneva Convention

Summary: [A.] Dissenting opinion of B.N. SRIKRISHNA J.- Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 44, 45, 48(1)(a), 50, 50(1)(a), 52, 8 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 11 - Foreign Awards Act, 1961, s. 3 - English Arbitration Act 1996, ss. 32, 32(2) - Nature of adjudication - When words in an earlier statute have received an authoritative exposition by superior Court use of same words in a similar context in a later Act will give rise to a strong presumption that the Parliament intends that the same interpretation should also be followed for construction of these words in the later statute - Whether application u/s.45 to be decided prima facie or it would be decided after trial? - Difference of opinion - Majority view is that application u/s.45 would have to be determined by trial court after arriving at prima facie satisfaction that there exists an arbitral agreement, which is not null and void, inoperative or incapable of being performed - Matter remitted to trial court, but not for a full trial as directed by impugned judgment of HC.
