Mohd. Akhtar Hussain Alias Ibrahim Ahmed Bhatti v Assistant Collector of Customs (Prevention)

Supreme Court of India

31 August 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 589; (1988) 4 SCC 183; (1988) SCC (Cr) 921; AIR 1988 SC 2143; 1988 (2) AWC 1379; 1988 (3) Crimes 291; 1989 CRLJ 283; 1988 CRLR 769; 1988 (17) ECR 545; 1988 (37) E.L.T. 163; JT 1988 (3) SC 586; 1988(2) SCALE 552; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 747

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Summary: Criminal - Customs Act, 1962 - CrPC, 1974 - Sentencing - (A) Consecutive and concurrent sentences - If can be inposed - When transaction relating to offences not same or facts constituting two offences, quite different - (B) Maximum sentence - Enormity of the crime - Accused pleading guilty in both cases - When 18 other accused facing the trial in the same case - Maximum sentence awarded in first case and also in the second case, making it non consecutive - Legality - Held, consecutive sentences may be imposed - Not proper as in consecutive sentences, the court cannot afford to be blind to imprisonment which the accused is already undergoing - Appeal allowed.
