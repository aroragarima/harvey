Sree Swayam Prakash Ashramam And Anr. v G. Anandavally Amma And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 12; (2010) 2 SCC 689; AIR 2010 SC 622; 2010 (2) ALD(SC) 60; 2010 (78) ALR 720; 2010 (1 ) AWC 695; 2010 (1) CLT(SC) 1; 2010 (1) CTC 455; JT 2010 (1) SC 123; 2010 (2) MLJ(SC) 191; 2010 (1) OLR 848; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 916; 2010 (110) RD 248; 2010(1) SCALE 74; [2010] 1 S.C.R. 271

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Easements, Severance

Summary: Land & Property - Indian Easements Act, 1882, s. 13(b) - Easement rights - Easement by grant - Pathway - Plaint A and B schedule properties originally belonged to 'Y' - After her death, her disciples executed a settlement deed whereby Schedule 'A' property of plaint was allotted to plaintiff (deceased) - Schedule 'B' pathway of plaint was granted to plaintiff as easement right by said 'Y' - Pathway was situated within property under control and use of defendants - Plaintiff apprehended that defendant nos. 2 to 4 might forcibly close down pathway - Hence, he filed a suit for declaration of easement of necessity or of grant and permanent injunction restraining defendants from obstructing schedule 'B' pathway - Trial Court accepted version of plaintiff that there was no alternate pathway and, therefore, plaintiff was entitled to easement right in respect of pathway - In appeal, Appellate Court found that on evidence, it was proved that there was an alternate way and existence of an alternate pathway, howsoever inconvenient, would defeat claim of easement of necessity - Appellate Court held that claim of plaintiff regarding right of easement of necessity over plaint schedule 'B' pathway was not sustainable - It was further held that apprehension of plaintiff on attempted obstruction of schedule 'B' pathway was well-founded and, therefore, Trial Court was justified in granting relief of permanent injunction against defendants - Second appeal was filed before HC - HC held that though original grant was by 'Y' that grant could not perfect as an easement for reason that 'Y' herself was owner of both schedule 'A' and schedule 'B' properties and consequently there was no question of schedule 'B' property becoming servient tenement and schedule 'A' property becoming dominant tenement - However, it was the desire of 'Y' that was implemented by her disciples by virtue of settlement deed - Therefore, right of plaintiff to have schedule 'B' property as a pathway could not have been taken away by very same deed - Hence, present special leave petition - (A) Whether order passed by HC justified? - Held, HC was perfectly justified in holding that when it was the wish of 'Y' to grant easement right to plaintiff by way of an implied grant, right of plaintiff to have schedule 'B' property of plaint as a pathway could not have been taken away - HC was also fully justified in holding that there was implied grant of schedule 'B' property as pathway, which can be inferred from circumstances for reason that no other pathway was provided for access to schedule 'A' property of plaint and there was no objection to use of schedule 'B' property of plaint as pathway by plaintiff at least up to 1982, when cause of action for suit arose - (B) Defendants raised argument that since no case was made out by plaintiffs in their plaint about easementary right over schedule 'B' pathway by implied grant, no decree can be passed by courts below basing their conclusion on implied grant - Held, although there is no specific issue on question of implied grant, but as parties have understood their case and for purpose of proving and contesting implied grant had adduced evidence, Trial Court and HC had come to conclusion that plaintiff had acquired a right of easement in respect of schedule 'B' pathway by way of implied grant - Trial Court was justified in holding that such pleadings were not necessary when it did not make a difference to finding arrived at with respect to easement by way of grant - No substance in argument - Appeal dismissed.
