The Commissioner Of Income Tax v M/S. Catapharma (India) Pvt. Ltd

Supreme Court of India

23 July 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 746; (2007) 11 SCC 145; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 706; [2007] 292 ITR 641; JT 2007 (9) SC 359; 2007(9) SCALE 318; 2007 (8) SCJ 250; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 465; [2007] 162 TAXMAN 455

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax - Section 80 HHC (3)(b), Income Tax Act, 1961 - Excise duty and sales tax - Inclusion of - While making assessment under s. 143(3), assessing officer included excise duty and sales tax on the total turnover for computing the deduction under s. 80 HHC (3)(b) - Ratio in Commissioner of Income Tax, Coimbatore v. M/s. Lakshmi Machine Works JT 2007(6) SC 236: 'sales tax and excise duty also do not have any element of "turnover" which is the position even in the case of rent, commission, interest etc. It is important to bear in mind that excise duty and sales tax are indirect taxes. They are recovered by the assessee on behalf of the Government. Therefore, if they are made relatable to exports, the formula under Section 8OHHC would become unworkable' .
