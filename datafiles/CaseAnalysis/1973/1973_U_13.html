Masood Alam and another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 January 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, A. Alagiriswami, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 507; (1973) SCC (Cr) 435; (1973) 1 SCC 551; AIR 1973 SC 897; 1973 CRLJ 627; 1973 CRLR 173; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 268

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: communal harmony, communal violence, communalism

Summary: Criminal - Preventive Detention - Validity of - Habeas Corpus - Constitution of India, 1950,art. 32 - Maintenance of Internal Security Act, 1971, s. 3(1)(a)(i), - Code of Criminal Procedure, s. 144 - Petitioner was arrested pursuant to an order of detention made u/s. 3(1)(a)(i) and (ii) of the Act - (A) Whether detention of petitioner void as he had been served with order of detention when he was in jail? - No legal bar in serving an order of detention on a person who is in jail custody if he is likely to be released soon thereafter and there is relevant material on which detaining authority is satisfied that if free, person concerned is likely to indulge in activities prejudicial to security of state or maintenance of public order - Held, merely because person concerned has been served while in custody when it is expected that he would soon be released that service cannot invalidate order of detention - (B) Whether earlier order of detention was either revoked or had expired with result that unless present detention is passed on fresh facts arising after expiry or revocation of earlier order it must be held to be invalid? - Held, Act fixes maximum period of detention to be 12 months from date of detention with proviso that appropriate Govt. can revoke or modify detention order at any earlier time - Power of preventive detention being an extraordinary power intended to be exercised only in extraordinary emergent circumstances legislative scheme of ss. 13 and 14 of Act - In present case it is not urged and indeed it is not possible to urge that after actual expiry of original order of detention made by District Magistrate, which could only last for 12 days in absence of its approval by State Govt., any fresh facts could arise for sustaining fresh order of detention - (C) Whether grounds of detention were vague as alleged by petitioner? - Petitioner is an active member of Muslim Majlis and also a member of Youth Majlis - He was arrested while defying order promulgated u/s. 144, CrPC - Held, activities clearly bring petitioner's case within s. 3 of Act, being calculated to incite communal violence - Instances under both grounds are relevant and germane to object which is sought to be achieved by s. 3 of Act for purpose of detaining persons who are likely to act in a manner prejudicial to security of State or maintenance of public order - Petition dismissed.
