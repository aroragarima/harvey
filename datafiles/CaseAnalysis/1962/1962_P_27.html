Punjab National Bank, Limited v Kharbanda K. L

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 25; AIR 1963 SC 487; 1962 (4) FLR 257; 1962 (1) LLJ 234; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 977

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 33 C(2) - What is meaning of word 'benefit' used s. 33 C(2)? - Held, word is not confined merely to non-monetary benefit which could be converted in terms of money but is concerned with all kinds of benefits, whether monetary or non-monetary, to which a workman may be entitled - Under an award and sub-section comes into play when benefits have to be computed or calculated and there is a dispute as to calculation or computation - As Sastry award had conferred a benefit on workman - It was open to workman to apply to labour court for computation of that benefit in terms of money, and labour Court would have jurisdiction to entertain application and compute amount due on basis of benefit conferred by award - Appeal dismissed.
