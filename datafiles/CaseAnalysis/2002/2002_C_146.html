Messrs Escorts JCB Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, Delhi-II

Supreme Court of India

24 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1171; (2003) 1 SCC 281; AIR 2002 SC 3708; 2002 (84) ECC 225; 2002 (105) ECR 545; 2002 (146) E.L.T. 31; JT 2002 (8) SC 298; 2002 (53) RLT 1; 2002(7) SCALE 475; 2002 (6) SLT 147; [2002] Supp3 S.C.R. 261; 2002 (7) Supreme 368

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Place Of Removal, Indian Sale Of Goods Act, 1930

Summary: Central Excise Rules, 1944 - Rule 9(2) - Central Excise Act - S. 11 - ownership of the property insured - Ownership in the property may not have any relevance in so far insurance of goods sold during transit is concerned. - It is not necessary that insurance of the goods and the ownership of the property insured must always go together. - the Commissioner of Central Excise and the CEGAT erred in drawing an inference that the ownership in the property continued to be retained by the assessee till it was delivered to the buyer for the reason that the assessee had arranged for the transport and the transit insurance. Such a conclusion is not sustainable.
