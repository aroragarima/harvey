State of Punjab and Others v Sukhwinder Singh

Supreme Court of India

14 July 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, R.C. Lahoti, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 417; (2005) 5 SCC 569; (2005) SCC (L&S) 705; AIR 2005 SC 2960; 2005 (106) FLR 613; JT 2005 (6) SC 170; 2005 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 516; 2005 (3) LLN 744; 2005 (141) PLR 402; 2005(5) SCALE 451; 2005 (5) SLR 587; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 580

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Punjab Police Rules, rr. 12.21, 16.24 - Absence from duty - Discharge from service - Whether HC was right in holding that respondent's absence from duty was foundation of order, which necessitated an inquiry? - Held, respondent was on probation having been appointed about eight months back - Period of probation gives time and opportunity to employer to watch work ability, efficiency, sincerity and competence of servant and if he is found not suitable for post, master reserves a right to dispense with his service without anything more during or at end of prescribed period, which is styled as period of probation - Appeal allowed.
