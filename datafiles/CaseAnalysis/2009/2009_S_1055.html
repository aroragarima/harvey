Kanti Lal and Another v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Lokeshwar Singh Panta, B. Sudarshan Reddy

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 528; (2009) 12 SCC 498; AIR 2009 SC 2703; 2009 (3) JCC 1604; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 892; 2009(6) SCALE 116; [2009] 6 S.C.R. 525; 2009 (2) UC 740

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Bride Burning, Match-Box

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B and 498A - Dowry death - Dying declaration - Conviction by Trial Court - Confirmation by High Court for Accused 1 (husband) and accused 3 (husband's brother), rest of accused persons acquitted - Present appeal - Demand of dowry - Deceased dying due to 90% burn injuries - Trial Court and HC gave finding, on testimonies of PWs and materials on record, that prosecution has proved beyond reasonable doubt that deceased was being constantly harassed and tortured by A1 and A3 for demand of dowry, and a sum of Rs. 50,000 paid to them as loan amount was also adjusted by them as dowry money; findings perused, evidence analysed, upheld - Undisputed was the position that death had occurred within 7 years of marriage - Defence main reliance was on dying declaration which they were claiming to have been made by deceased, stating therein that death was accidental, which Trial Court and HC had rejected as unreliable - Court perused dying declaration(true translation of its content), facts and circumstances surrounding it, authority before whom it was made - Dying declaration(DD) was made by DW2, Naib Tehsildar, on a letter of request by one police constable - No certificate taken by DW2 from the doctor to prove that the deceased was in a fit state of mind to give statement, and also no endorsement found to that effect on alleged DD, no preliminary questions asked from the deceased before recording alleged DD; doctor stated that DD was recorded by Reader of DW and not by DW which belied statement given by DW2; DD was not sealed and was handed over unsealed to SHO; original copy of letter of request by constable not produced on record but instead a carbon copy was produced; presence of mother of deceased(PW6) and A3 with the doctor and DW, alleged by prosecution at time when DD was recorded, and categorical refusal by PW6/mother that she ever had put in the thumb impression or signature as is alleged by the prosecution: all these aspects made DD an unreliable document - Important tests for credibility of Dying Declaration, deliberated upon - Finding of Trial Court and HC on DD as to its unreliability, upheld - Impugned judgements well reasoned, upheld - Appeals dismissed.
