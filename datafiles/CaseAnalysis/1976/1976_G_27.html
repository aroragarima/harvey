Tata Engineering and Locomotive Company Limited v Gram Panchayat Pimpri Waghere

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 378; (1976) 4 SCC 177; (1976) SCC (Tax) 457; AIR 1976 SC 2463; 1976 CRLJ 1736; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 306

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Municipalities & Local Government - Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950 - Resolution - Inconsistent - Amendment - Repeal - Respondent was village Panchayat constituted under provisions of Act - Respondent in exercise of powers conferred for imposition of taxes by resolution and revised tax by another resolution - Respondent by notice made demand of taxes on factory building of appellant - Appellant contended that respondent had no power to impose taxes on factory buildings - Appellant contended that resolution was not valid as it was not passed in accordance with Act and Rules - Act was repealed - Hence instant appeal - Whether tax imposed on appellant was inconsistent with 1959 Act - Held, it was significant that tax was not on houses alone but on lands as well - In amendment of 1954 building was substituted for word house and it would not help appellant to suggest that factories would be included within buildings and not within houses - No distinction made between dwelling houses and buildings - Demands made by respondent under 1952 resolution were valid and legal as resolution of 1952 had never superseded - There was nothing to show that tax was inconsistent with 1959 Act - Appeal dismissed.
