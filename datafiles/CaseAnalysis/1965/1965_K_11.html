Kaluram Onkarmal And Another v Baidyanath Gorain

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 193; AIR 1965 SC 1909; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 34

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Land & Property - W B Premises Tenancy Act,1956, ss. 13, 17, 21 and 22 - (A) What are objects of s. 17 and how does it impact s. 21(1) of Act? - Held, s. 17(1) controls relationship between landlord, tenant once a suit or proceeding for ejectment was instituted, a period of one month from service of writ of summons on defendant had expired - U/s. 17(1) tenant must pay interest on defaulted amount calculated in manner prescribed by it - Although if he fails to comply u/s. 17(3) landlord's claim that defense of tenant against delivery of possession should be struck out - Deposit of rent made in court u/s. 17(1) is result of a statutory obligation imposed by said sub-section - As amount is same as that of contract but mode of payment is prescribed by statute - Deposit of rent made u/s 21 before Controller is based on contractual obligation of tenant to pay rent, and he makes deposit because landlord does not receive rent or there is a dispute as to who real landlord is - Tenant cannot plead under a deposit u/s 20(1) for an application made u/s. 17(3) - (B) To whom tenant would have to pay rent when a suit is brought for eviction? - Further held, when landlord refuses to accept rent or where there is a bona fide doubt as to who is entitled to receive rent that tenant is empowered u/s 21 to deposit rent with Controller- In other cases if tenant was paying rent to landlord and is faced with a suit for eviction s. 17(1) will unambiguously apply and amount of rent will have to be paid in court as required by it - Even where tenant might have been depositing rent u/s. 21 with Controller he must comply with s.17(1) before expiry of period prescribed by s. 17(1) - Its object is obviously that when a suit or proceeding for ejectment has commenced between landlord and tenant, and tenant has received notice of it, payment of rent should be made in court in order to avoid any dispute in that behalf - (C) Whether Rent Controller is court within meaning of s. 17(1)? - Further held, no, he cannot - U/ss. 21 and 22 a tenant may deposit rent with Controller as soon as a suit is brought against him by landlord - S. 17 being a special provision comes into operation and it must prevail in cases covered by it - Appeal dismissed.
