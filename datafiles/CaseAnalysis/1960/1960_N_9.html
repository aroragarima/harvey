Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay City v Narsee Nagsee and Company

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 360; AIR 1960 SC 1232; [1960] 40 ITR 307; 1961 (2) SCJ 403; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 988

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Business Profits-tax Act, 1947, ss. 11(1), 14 - Limitation - Respondent was a firm carrying on business in Bombay - Income-tax Officer (ITO) issued notice on respondent u/s. 11(1) of the 1947 Act in respect of A/Y and chargeable accounting period - Respondent filed a return under protest and stated that notice was served after four years and therefore notice was barred u/s. 14 of the 1947 Act - Assessment was completed by the ITO - Respondent appealed to the Appellate Assistant Commissioner on the ground that respondent was not liable to Business Profits Tax because it was beyond the period of four years limitation u/s. 14 of the 1947 Act - Plea was upheld by Appellate Assistant Commissioner - ITO then appealed to Appellate Tribunal and it confirmed the order of the Appellate Assistant Commissioner - Hence, the present appeal - Whether assessment was issued in time, and action could be taken within time specified - Held, if the operation of s. 11 of the 1947 Act was confined to those cases where there had been no prior assessment and the operation of s. 14 of that Act to those cases where after a prior assessment there was an escaped assessment, under-assessment or excessive relief - For the subsequent and reopened assessment there was a limit of four years, but for the assessment for the first time there was no limit - Appeal dismissed.
