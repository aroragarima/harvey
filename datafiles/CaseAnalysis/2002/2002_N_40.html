Janki Narayan Bhoir v Narayan Namdeo Kadam

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1504; (2003) 2 SCC 91; AIR 2003 SC 761; 2003 (2) ALD(SC) 45; 2003 (1) CGLJ 190; 2003 (2) CHN 104; 2003 (2) G.L.R. 1493; JT 2002 (10) SC 340; 2003 (1) RCR(Civil) 409; 2003 (94) RD 294; 2002(9) SCALE 628; 2003 (1) SCW 177; 2002 (7) SLT 361; [2002] Supp5 S.C.R. 175; 2003 (1) Supreme 297

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice and Procedure - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s.71 and 68 -Attestation of will by two witnesses - Requirement of - Whether fulfilled? - Held, where the attesting witness, who is called to prove the execution, is not in a position to prove the attestation of the will by the second witness, the evidence of the witness called falls short of the mandatory requirements of s.68 of the Evidence Act - Further, s.71 of the Evidence Act has no application when one attesting witness has failed to prove the execution of the will and other attesting witnesses were available who could prove the execution if they were called - When, it was not established that the two witnesses attested the Will, the HC committed a serious error in reversing the judgment and decree of the first appellate court on a finding of fact, which was based on proper and objective appreciation of evidence - Further, the HC was also wrong in treating the scribe of the Will as an attesting witness without any basis - Appeal allowed.
