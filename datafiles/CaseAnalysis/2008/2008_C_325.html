Commnr. Of Income Tax-I, Ahmedabad v Gold Coin Health Food Pvt. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

18 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1309; (2008) 9 SCC 622; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 780; [2008] 304 ITR 308; JT 2008 (9) SC 312; [2008] 12 S.C.R. 179; [2008] 172 TAXMAN 386

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Rule Against Retrospectivity

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1961 - Correctness of the judgment rendered by a Division Bench of Supreme Court in Virtual Soft Systems Ltd. V. Commissioner of Income Tax, Delhi (2007 (9) SCC 665) - Held, explanation 4(a) to s. 271(1) (c) intended to levy the penalty not only in a case where after addition of concealed income, a loss returned, after assessment becomes positive income but also in a case where addition of concealed income reduces the returned loss and finally the assessed income is also a loss or a minus figure - Even during the period between 1.4.1976 to 1.4.2003 the position was that the penalty was leviable even in a case where addition of concealed income reduces the returned loss - Explanation 4 to s. 271(1)(c) is clarificatory and not substantive - View expressed to the contrary in Virtual's case is not correct - Appeals disposed of.
