Malabar Fisheries Co, Calcutta v Commissioner Of Income Tax, Kerala

Supreme Court of India

19 September 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, P.N. Bhagwati, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 189; (1979) 4 SCC 766; (1980) SCC (Tax) 49; AIR 1980 SC 176; 1979 (12) CTR 415; [1979] 120 ITR 49; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 696; 1990 TAXLR 109; [1979] 2 TAXMAN 409

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Distribution Of Assets, Corporate Body

Summary: Income Tax & Direct taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 2(47), 34(3)(b) and 155(5) - Transfer of assets - Appellant, a dissolved firm as originally constituted on 1.04.1959 consisted of four partners and carried on six different business in six different names and styles - Appellant was dissolved on 31.03.1963 and under the deed of dissolution executed by and between the partners, the shares and assets of firm distributed among the partners - A dissolved firm had installed various items of machinery in respect of which it received development rebate in its respective tax assessment years - On dissolution of the firm, the Income-tax Officer took the view that s. 34 (3) (b) of the Act applied on the ground that there was a sale or transfer of the machinery by the firm within the period mentioned in that section and accordingly acting u/s. 155 (5) of the Act he withdrew the development rebate allowed to the firm for the said a/ys, the amending, orders being passed against the dissolved firm - Appellate Asst. Commissioner dismissed appeal filed against said order - Tribunal allowed the appeal filed against said order and held that there was no question of any sale or transfer within the meaning of s. 34(3)(b) in a transaction involving the adjustment of the rights of the partners of a dissolved firm - HC held that the dissolution of a firm amounted to extinguishment of the rights of the firm in the assets of the partnership and accordingly was a transfer within the meaning of s. 2(47) of the Act and that, therefore the provisions of s. 34(3)(b) applied to the case - Hence, present appeal - Whether there was a transfer of assets within the meaning to the words 'otherwise transferred' occurring in s. 34(3) (b) to the IT Act? - Held, before invocation of s. 34(3)(b) of IT Act three conditions are required to be satisfied (a) that the ship, machinery or plant must have been sold or otherwise transferred, (b) that such a sale or transfer must be by the assessee and (c) that the same must be before the expiry of 8 years from the end of the previous year in which it was acquired or installed - In the present case, the firm as such has no separate rights of its own in the partnership assets but it is the partners who own jointly in common the assets of the partnership and therefore, the consequence of the distribution, division or allotment of assets to the partners which flows upon dissolution after discharge of liabilities is nothing but a mutual adjustment of rights between the partners and, there is no question of any extinguishment of the firm's rights in the partnership assets amounting to a transfer of assets within the meaning of s. 2(47) of the IT Act - Hence, s. 34(3)(b) of the Act was not applicable in the present case - Appeal allowed.
