Rajinder Kaur v Punjab State And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

08 August 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 90; (1986) 4 SCC 141; (1986) SCC (L&S) 745; AIR 1986 SC 1790; 1986 (2) CCC 779; 1986 (53) FLR 501; JT 1986 SC 86; 1986 LabIC 1620; 1987 (1) LLJ 93; 1986 (2) LLN 750; 1986(2) SCALE 203; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 500; 1987 (1) SLJ 33; 1986 (3) SLR 78; 1986 (2) UJ 709

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Punjab Police Rules, 1934, r. 12.21 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 - Whether discharge of appellant from service by an order D/- 9-9-1980 u/r. 12.21 volume 7 of Punjab Police Rules, 1934 is correct? - Held, Govt. has, by contract or under rules, right to terminate employment without going through procedure prescribed for inflicting punishment of dismissal or removal or reduction in rank, Govt. may, nevertheless, choose to punish servant and if termination of service is sought to be founded on misconduct, negligence, inefficiency or other dis-qualification, then it is a punishment and requirements of art. 311 must be complied with - Form of order is not conclusive of its true nature and it might merely be a clock and camouflage for an order founded on misconduct - It may be that an order which is innocuous on face and does not contain any imputation of misconduct is a circumstance or a piece of evidence for finding whether it was made by way of punishment to administrative routine - But entirety of circumstance preceding or attendant on impugned order must be examined and overriding test will always be whether misconduct is a mere motive or is very foundation of order - Impugned order of discharge though couched in innocuous terms, in merely a camouflage for an order of dismissal from service on ground of misconduct - This order has been made without serving appellant any charge-sheet, without asking for any explanation from her and without giving any opportunity to show cause against purported order of dismissal from service and without giving any opportunity to cross-examine witnesses examined that is in other words order has been made in total contravention of provisions of art. 311(2) of Constitution, 1950 - Impugned order is liable to be quashed and set aside - Appeal allowed.
