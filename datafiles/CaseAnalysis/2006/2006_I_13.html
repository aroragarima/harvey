Shyamali Das v Illa Chowdhry & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

01 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 732; (2006) 12 SCC 300; AIR 2007 SC 215; 2007 (1) ALD(SC) 51; 2007 (66) ALR 163; 2007 (7) AWC 6612; JT 2007 (1) SC 56; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 64; 2006(11) SCALE 267; 2006 (Supp) RD 299; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 310

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 11-A, 18, 3(b), 30, 5, 5-A - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 1 r. 10(2), O. 7 r. 11 - Held, appellant cannot be said to have any right title and interest in property - A disputant is entitled to an interim order, provided he is a party thereto - If for one reason or other, he cannot be impleaded as a party to proceeding, Court would have no jurisdiction to pass any interim order in his favour - If impleadment application was not maintainable, it was, required to be dismissed in limine - It could not have been entertained only for pressing an interim order - Appeal dismissed.
