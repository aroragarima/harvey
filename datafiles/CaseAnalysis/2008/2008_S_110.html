Renu Kumari v Sanjay Kumar and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 315; (2008) 12 SCC 346; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1958; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 1429; 2008 (2) JCC 1032; JT 2008 (4) SC 456; 2008 (2) RCR(Criminal) 549; 2008(4) SCALE 294; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 1006

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Private Complainant

Summary: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482; Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 498 A; Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, ss. 3, 4 - Appeal against quashing of proceedings - Held, court must be careful to see that its decision, in exercise of this power, is based on sound principles - Inherent power should not be exercised to stifle a legitimate prosecution - Allegations of mala fides against the informant are of no consequence and cannot by themselves be the basis for quashing the proceedings - Impugned order is indefensible and is set aside - Appeal allowed.
