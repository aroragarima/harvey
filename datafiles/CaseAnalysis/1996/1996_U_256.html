Sheshank Sea Foods Private Limited, Karnataka v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 214; (1996) 11 SCC 755; 1997 (68) ECR 17; 1996 (88) E.L.T. 626; 1996 (17) RLT 617; 1996(8) SCALE 461; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 802; 1996 (8) Supreme 527

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Duty Exemption

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, ss. 25(1) and 111(o) - Import and Export Policy, 1988 - 91 - Exemption Notification No. 116/1988 d/- 30-04-1988 - Whether Custom Authorities have jurisdiction to investigate alleged violation of the terms of Exemption Notification? - Held, licensing authority's power to conduct such an investigation does not by itself preclude Customs authorities from doing so - A breach of terms of said Exemption Notification is also a breach of terms of licence, entitling licensing authorities to investigate - But breach is not only of terms of licence, it is also a breach of condition in Exemption Notification upon which appellants obtained exemption from payment of Customs duty and, therefore, terms of s. 111(o) enable Customs authorities to investigate - Appeals dismissed.
