Ramakant Mayekar v Smt. Celine D'SILVA

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, N.P. Singh, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1859; (1996) 1 SCC 399; AIR 1996 SC 826; JT 1995 (9) SC 73; 1995(7) SCALE 72; 1996 (2) SCJ 222; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 492

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Practice & Procedure - Representation of the People Act, 1951, s. 116A - Corrupt practices - Religious appeal - Implied consent - Appellant and others had contested the Assembly elections which were declared void on the ground of commission of corrupt practices - Leaders and veterans of the appellant's political party during the election campaign had resorted to making speeches which were provocative for creating enmity between two religions, Hindus and Muslims - Election petitions were filed against the appellant and other candidates including their election agents and party leaders for committing corrupt practices of appealing for votes on the grounds of the candidates religion, community and castes and thereby trying to create enmity between the two religions - HC passed order declaring the elections as void - Appellant and others aggrieved - Hence, instant appeals - Whether, the order of the HC declaring the elections as void was liable to be set-aside - Held, that the election petitions were not based on individual acts of individuals but based on a plank of Hindutva/Hinduism that had been adopted by the political parties and declared by their leaders at the joint public meetings - Provocative speeches were made before the candidature of the appellant, documentary evidence of photograph relied on was not conclusive proof of his presence at the joint public meetings and so his consent was an implied, could not be justified - Pleadings regarding commission of corrupt practices based on wall paintings posters banners and video cassettes, were improper, not specifically pleaded, inadmissible and no reliance could be placed on them - Assumption of HC, that no specific pleading or proof of consent of the appellant was necessary if the act was attributed by any leader or member of the same political party, was erroneous - Corrupt practices held to be proved against the appellant as well the order of dismissal of the election was liable to be set-aside - Appeals allowed.
