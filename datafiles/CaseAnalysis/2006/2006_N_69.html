Niranjan Umeshchandra Joshi v Mrudula Jyoti Rao & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 978; (2006) 13 SCC 433; AIR 2007 SC 614; JT 2007 (1) SC 466; 2007 (103) RD 38; 2006(14) SCALE 186; 2006 (7) SCJ 157; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 1214

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Will - Testamentary Application filed by Appellant - Trial Court held that circumstances surrounding execution of Will were so suspicious that it was impossible to believe that the Will had been executed at the place, time and in the manner suggested by Appellant' - Appeal against - Held, burden of proof that the Will has been validly executed and is a genuine document is on the propounder - Onus would be on the applicant to remove the suspicion by leading sufficient and cogent evidence if there exists any - In the case of proof of Will, a signature of a testator alone would not prove the execution thereof, if his mind may appear to be very feeble and debilitated - No case has been made out to interfere with the findings of both the learned Single Judge as also the Division Bench of the High Court - Appeal dismissed.
