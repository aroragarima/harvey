Director of Industries, U.P. and Others v Deep Chand Aggarwal

Supreme Court of India

06 February 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 155; (1980) 2 SCC 332; AIR 1980 SC 801; 1980 ALJ 354; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 1015; 1980 UJ 374

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Validity of UP Public Moneys (Recovery of Dues) Act, 1965 - Act providing for speedier remedy for recovery of Govt. dues - Held, that the Govt. is not acting like a normal banker, the loan it advances is for the development of agriculture, animal husbandry and other such purposes - Moneys advanced by the State Govt. have got to be recovered expeditiously so that fresh advances may be made from the State Govt. and therefore the Act is not violative of art. 14 of constitution - Appeal allowed.
