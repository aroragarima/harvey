S. K. Sarkar, Member, Board Of Revenue, U.P., Lucknow v Vinay Chandra Misra

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 235; (1981) SCC (Cr) 175; (1981) 1 SCC 436; AIR 1981 SC 723; 1981 CRLJ 283; 1981 CrLR(SC) 72; 1981 MLJ 281; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 331

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Court Of Record, Ex-Parte, Suo Motu

Summary: Advocates and Judges - Practice and Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 s.15 - Jurisdiction and powers of a HC to take action suo motu u/s.15 of 1971 act - Respondent sought HC to take suo motu action u/s.15(1) against appellant - Whether HC competent to take cognizance of contempt of Sub-ordinate court when it is not moved in either in two modes mentioned u/art.15(2)? - Held, s.15(2) does not restrict power of HC to take cognizance of and punish contempt of Sub-ordinate Court on its own - S. 15 does not specify basis or source of information on which HC can act on its own motion - Mode of taking suo motu cognizance of contempt of a subordinate court, should be resorted to sparingly - Frequent use of this suo motu power on information furnished by an incompetent petition, may render these procedural safeguards provided in sub-s. (2), otiose - Appeal dismissed.
