Commissioner Of Income-Tax, Madras v T.S.P.L.P. Chidamebaram Chettiar (Dead) Throughl.

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 527; (1971) 1 SCC 417; AIR 1971 SC 2074; [1971] 80 ITR 467; 1972 (1) MLJ(SC) 26; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 428

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 10 and 34(1)(a) - (A) When ITO can initiate proceedings u/s. 34 of the Act - (B) Assessment order u/s. 34 is set aside by Appellate Assistant Commissioner - Ground, assessee had not been given a proper opportunity to put forward his case - Whether ITO is required to issue fresh notice u/s. 34(1)(a) - (C) Amount received by creditor-assessee - Without direction from debtor about its appropriation - Not entered by assessee in his account books - Treatment of - Held, when at the time of issuing notice u/s. 34(1)(a) he has formed a belief on the basis of material before him that there was under-assessment and that the same has resulted from non-disclosure of material facts - ITO is not required to issue fresh notice u/s. 34(1)(a) - That amount can be treated as income by way of interest - Appeal No.C.A. 671 of 1967 Dismissed; Appeal No. C.A. 365 of 1967 Allowed.
