Hamant Yashwant Dhage v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

10 February 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 115; (2016) 6 SCC 273; AIR 2016 SC 814; 2016 ALL MR (Cri) 1360; 2016 CRLJ 1270; 2016 (1) JCC 629; 2016 (2) JCC 831; JT 2016 (2) SC 154; 2016 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 949; 2016 (2) RCR(Criminal) 350; 2016(2) SCALE 271; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 997

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Challenged, Denial, Cognizable, Registration of FIR

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 156(3), 156 - Registration of FIR - Denial - Challenged - Appellant complainant approached Judicial Magistrate for registration of FIR - Judicial Magistrate came to wrong conclusion that in the absence of any specific direction of the Court, the prayer of the complainant for registration of FIR had to be rejected - Complainant filed petition before HC which was disposed of by impugned order and declined to issue a direction for registering FIR by taking the view that it was open for the petitioner to seek clarification/modification of the order from the apex Court - Hence instant appeal.

Held, registration of FIR involves only the process of recording the substance of information relating to commission of any cognizable offence in a book kept by officer In-charge of the concerned police station. The law was further elucidated by pointing out that to enable the police to start investigation, it is open to the Magistrate to direct the police to register FIR and even where a Magistrate does not do so in explicit words but directs for investigation u/s. 156(3) CrPC, the police should register FIR because s. 156 falls within chapter XII CrPC which deals with powers of the police officers to investigate cognizable offences, police officer concerned would always be in better position to take further steps contemplated in Chapter XII once FIR is registered in respect of the concerned cognizable offence. Thus, in view, the same was the import of SC order passed on 12-4-2010. In the light of the said earlier order, the legal position noticed and the stand of the parties, SC have no difficulty in directing the concerned Magistrate and the police officer to rectify their mistake by ensuring registration of appropriate FIR. The delay in lodging of such FIR occurring after 12-4-2010 shall not have any effect on the investigation already carried out by the investigating officer(s). SC also directs the police to complete the investigation fairly and in accordance with law at an early date, preferably within six months. Appeal allowed .
