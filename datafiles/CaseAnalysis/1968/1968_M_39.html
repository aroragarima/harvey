Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay v Messrs Shree Goverdhan Limited Bombay

Supreme Court of India

09 January 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 252; AIR 1969 SC 292; [1968] 69 ITR 675; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 731

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Finance Bill, Finance Act

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 23-A and 23-A(1) - Assessee is a public limited company - Its share capital consists of 50,000 shares subscribed and paid up - Assessee entered into a partnership on 20-04-1950 with a firm called 'India Steel Syndicate' - There was a reconstitution of this firm on December 1, 1950 - Shares of profit of assessee from this firm as up to 30-11-1950 and upto 31-03-1951 were included in assessment of assessee for assessment year 1951-52 - Whether order passed against assessee u/s. 23-A of Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 was not justified and valid for assessment year 1951-52? - Held, in present case that Indian Steel Syndicate, closed accounts of partnership for first time for first set of partners on to 30-11-1950 and for other set of partners on 31-03-1951 and assessee as a partner was therefore, entitled to share of profits as on last day of accounting period of partnership i.e., 31-03-1951 - Income Tax Officer was right in holding that amount which was share of assessee's income from its partnership with Indian Steel Syndicate for period ending 31-03-195 should be included in assessable profits of company for assessment year 1951-52 and should be treated as part of distributable profits of company for purpose of s. 23-A(1) of Act - Held, order made by Income Tax Officer against assessee u/s. 23-A of Act for assessment year 1951-52 must be held to be justified and valid - Appeal allowed.
