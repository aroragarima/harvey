Panchdeo Singh v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19910; (2002) SCC (Cr) 211; (2002) 1 SCC 577; AIR 2002 SC 526; 2001 (4) CCR 362; 2002 (1) CHN 88; 2002 (1) Crimes 231; 2002 (2) CRJ 109; 2002 CRLJ 973; 2002 (1) JCC 3; JT 2001 (10) SC 322; 2002 (1) RCR(Criminal) 126; 2001(8) SCALE 393; 2002 (1) SCJ 117; 2001 (8) SLT 301; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 503; 2001 (8) Supreme 610; 2002 (1) UC 344

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1963

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1963, ss. 313 and 302 - Appellants hurled bomb at deceased - Conviction by Addl. sessions judge, and that of High Court on basis of dying declaration of deceased - Whether a declaration by itself would tantamount to substantial evidence against appellant? - Held, no doctor's certification as regards state of condition of declarant - Only judicial magistrate, stated that declarant was in a fit condition to make statement - Left thumb impression of deceased not on declaration - Declaration must be such so as to evoke confidence in factual context - Unable to record confidence on such a declaration so as to lend support and concurrence to judgment under appeal - Appeal allowed.
