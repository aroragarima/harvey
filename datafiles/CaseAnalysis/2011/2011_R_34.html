Rajendra Harakchand Bhandari & Ors v State Of Maharashtra & Anr

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 238; (2011) 13 SCC 311; AIR 2011 SC 1821; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 1641; 2011 CRLJ 2891; JT 2011 (4) SC 137; 2011 (2) RCR(Criminal) 614; 2011 (3) RLW 2246; 2011(4) SCALE 450

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 149, 307, 332 and 353 - Conviction - Challenged - Six persons including appellants were sent for trial for various offences punishable under IPC - Trial Court acquitted two of them but convicted the appellants for the offences punishable u/s. 307 r/w s. 149; s. 332 r/w s. 149; s. 353 r/w s. 149; s. 147 and 148 of IPC - On appeal, HC set aside the conviction of the appellants for the offences punishable u/ss. 147 and 148 of IPC and altered their conviction to s. 307 r/w s. 34, s. 332 r/w s. 34 and s. 353 r/w s. 34 of - Instant appeal - Held, Trial Court and HC on appreciation of the entire evidence on record have accepted the prosecution case that A- 1, A-2, A-5 and A-6 armed with sword and sticks attacked PW- 8 and as a result thereof he sustained injuries which were sufficient in the ordinary course of nature to cause his death - Conviction upheld - However, having regard to the fact that the appellants were agriculturists by occupation and have no previous criminal background; there was reconciliation amongst parties; ends of justice would be met if the substantive sentence awarded to the appellants was reduced to the period already undergone while maintaining the amount of fine - Appeal partly allowed.
