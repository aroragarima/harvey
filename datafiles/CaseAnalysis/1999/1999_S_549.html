National Engineering Industries Limited v State of Rajasthan and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 December 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 472; (2000) 1 SCC 371; AIR 2000 SC 469; 2000 (1) CLR 389; JT 1999 (9) SC 377; 2000 (33) LabIC 260; 2000 (1) LLJ 24; 2000 (1) SLJ 194; 1999 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 138; [1999] Supp5 S.C.R. 87; 2000 (1) UPLBEC 436

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Working Journalists, Age Of Superannuation, Majority Union, Failure Report, Holiday, Trade Union Act, 1926, Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Trade Union Act, 1926 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s.10(1)(d) r/w s.12(5) - Rajasthan Industrial Disputes Amendment Act, 1958 - Charter of demands - Tripartite settlement - Validity of reference - In 1983 three unions in appellant/company registered under 1926 Act as amended under 1958 Act made their charter of demands - Tripartite settlement arrived at between management, Labor Union and staff union - In respect of demand made by die Workers Union, failure report was submitted - Worker Union made representation to State Government - Request declined by State Govt. - State Govt. issued notification for reference of disputes related to demands raised by Workers Union - Appellant filed writ petition in HC challenged validity of reference - Lower Court challenge of appellant to validity of notification issued by State Govt. u/s.10(1)(d) r/w s.12(5) of 1947 Act - Hence instant appeal - Whether reference of disputes related to demands raised by Workers Union was valid - Held, recognized union with majority of members was expected to protect legitimate interest of labor and enter into a settlement in best interest of labor - Settlement arrived at in course of conciliation proceedings with recognized majority union binding on all workmen of establishment - Lower Court did not examine with reference to each of demands raised by Workers' Union as to why it was not covered under tripartite settlement and even earlier settlement of 1983 - Industrial Tribunal was creation statute got jurisdiction on basis of reference could not go into tile question on validity of reference - Wholesale reference of all disputes in charter of demands of Workers' Union for adjudication was also bad inasmuch as many of such disputes were already subject matter of tripartite settlement also showed non-application of mind by State Govt. in making reference - Reference in itself was bad as tripartite settlement bind members of Workers' Union as well - Appeal allowed.
