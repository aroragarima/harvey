Action Committee, Un-Aided Private Schools and v Director of Education, Delhi and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1107; (2009) 10 SCC 1; 2009 (162) DLT 50; 2009 (4) ESC 658; 2010 (1) MLJ(SC) 83; 2009(11) SCALE 77; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 631; 2009 (7) SLR 4

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Fixation

Summary: Education - Delhi School Education Act, 1973, ss.17(3) and 24(3) - Delhi School Education Rules, 1973, r.177 - Constitution of India, 1950, art.19(1)(g)/(6) - Writ petition filed against unaided recognized public schools in Delhi, Union of India and Govt. of NCT of Delhi to take necessary steps to regulate admissions in such schools; on allegation that private schools were indulging in large scale commercialization - Director of Education (DoE) issued order D/-10-09-1997, directing measures to regulate 'registration fee', 'admission fee', 'caution money/security' etc. - Writ petitions filed by managements of several unaided private schools questioning said directions - High Court upheld order of DoE, and gave decision relying on case of 'Unni Krishnan, J.P. v. State of AP' - HC gave direction for constitution of a Committee - Appeal preferred against decision of HC - While appeal pending, Committee submitted its report on basis of which report DoE issued notification D/-15-12-1999 (notification) - Appeal disposed of by Supreme Court on 27-04-2004 upholding directions issued in notification as well as issuing additional directions - Review petition filed - Contention raised that in view of decision in 'P.A. Inamdar v. State of Maharashtra, (2005) 6 SCC 537', directions issued by DoE cannot be sustained as schools and in particular minority schools have a greater autonomy in laying down their own fee structure - Held decision in Unni Krishnan which was basis of HC judgment overruled in 'T.M.A. Pai Foundation vs. State of Karnataka'; further T.M.A. Pai's, and 'Islamic Academy of Education' case was clarified in P.A. Inamdar vs. State of Maharashtra's case - Thus when judgment in instant case was pronounced, Supreme Court did not have benefit of decision in P.A. Inamdar case - On basis of judgment in P.A. Inamdar case, present review petition was filed - Held - court upheld cl. 8 of Notification D/- 15-12-1999 and found it to be in consonance with r.177 of Rules - Submission to add rider to Cl.8 to state 'except under the management of the same society or trust' after 'no amount whatsoever shall be transferred from the recognized unaided school fund of a school to the society or the trust or any other institution' upheld - Act and Rules cannot come in way of management to establish more schools; so long as there is a reasonable fee structure in existence and so long as there is transfer of funds from one institution to other under same management, there cannot be any objection from DoE - Additional directions to every school to prepare balance sheet and profit and loss account given vide impugned judgment upheld as being not beyond r.177 but just a part of gap-filling exercise and discipline to be followed by management - R.177 seeks transparency and accountability, and additional directions bring about that transparency - Clarification thus made in impugned judgement - Petitions dismissed.
