State of Orissa and Others v Harapriya Bisoi

Supreme Court of India

20 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 555; (2009) 12 SCC 378; AIR 2009 SC 2991; JT 2009 (7) SC 92; 2009(6) SCALE 415; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 34

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Equitable Doctrine, Tenure-Holder, Orissa Tenancy Act, 1913, Orissa Estate Abolition Act, 1951, Orissa Survey and Settlement Act, 1958, Orissa Communal Forest and Private Lands (Prohibitions of Alienation) Act, 1948

Summary: Land & Property - Orissa Estate Abolition Act, 1951 - Orissa Survey and Settlement Act, 1958 - Dispute relates to an alleged lease - Held, s. 3(1) prohibited landholders from selling, mortgaging, converting into ryoti land, leasing or otherwise assigning or alienating any communal or forest land in an estate without the previous sanction of the District Collector, on or after the date on which the ordinance which preceded the Act came into force - Matter needs to be re-considered by the High Court - Appeals partly allowed.
