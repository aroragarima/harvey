Â Messrs Anwarkhan Mahboob Company v State of Bombay (Now Maharashtra) and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, S.K. Das, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 239; AIR 1961 SC 213; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 709; 1960 (11) STC 698

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Summary: Constitution - Sales Tax - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(f), 32 and 286 - Bombay Sales Act, 1953, ss. 14(6) and 16(4) - Illegal imposition of purchase tax on tobacco - Petition u/art. 32 - Assessement order by Sales Tax Officer - Appeal preferred before Asst. Collector of Sales Tax - Appeal set aside - Mandamus Writ against Respondent-State of Bombay, STO, Asst. Collector of Sales Tax - Petitioner, a partnership firm carrying on business of manufacture of bidis and having its head office at Jabalpur within Madhya Pradesh - Violation of fundamental rights u/arts. 19(1)(f) and (g) by illegal imposition of a purchase tax on certain purchases of tobacco made by it in State of Bombay - (A) Whether imposition of purchase tax under Bombay Sales Tax illegal if transaction outside State of Bombay and violates art 286(1)(a) ? - Held, even if these transactions were in course of inter-state trade, bar of art. 286(2) stands removed by Sales Tax Laws Validation Act, for entire period upto 06-09-1955 - Levy of tax for period 07-09-1955 - 29-09-1955, would be illegal if these transactions are in course of inter-state trade - Petitioner failed to establish any, violation of its fundamental right - (B) Whether tobacco delivered in State of Bombay for consumption and fall within art. 286(1)(a) with violation of fundamental rights? - Held, on facts of this case when tobacco was delivered in the State of Bombay for purpose of changing it into a commercially different article, viz,bidi patti delivery was for purpose of consumption - Purchases in this case therefore within meaning of explnation to srt. 286(1)(a) and held to have taken place inside State of Bombay - Petition dismissed.
