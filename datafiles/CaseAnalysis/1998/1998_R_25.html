Muneshwar (Dead By Lrs. v Raja Mohammad Khan & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

20 August 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 831; (1998) 6 SCC 582; AIR 1998 SC 2995; 1998 (3) AWC 2206; JT 1998 (5) SC 583; 1998 (89) RD 615; 1998(4) SCALE 618; 1998 (7) SLT 28; [1998] Supp1 S.C.R. 53; 1998 (6) Supreme 502

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Limitation Act, 1963 - Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition And Land Reforms Act, 1950, ss. 5, 210, 176 and 338 - Uttar Pradesh Consolidation of Holdings Act, 1953, ss. 9, 9-A and 4 - Consolidation proceedings were taken up in village of appellant - Thereafter, notices were issued inviting objections to records so prepared - Land in question in records was shown in names of respondents to which appellant filed objections - Consolidation Officer upheld objections of appellant and names of respondents were removed from records and name of appellant was entered - Writ Petition before HC - Quashed orders of authorities and disputed land stood in names of respondents in revenue records - Present appeal - Held, HC while exercising jurisdiction u/art. 226 of Constitution was not justified in arriving at a contrary conclusion in face of clear evidence of possession and entries in records of right sufficiently discussed by three consolidation authorities - Appeal allowed.
