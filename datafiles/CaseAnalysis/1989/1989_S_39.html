Shafiq Ahmad v District Magistrate, Meerut & Ors

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 856; (1989) 4 SCC 556; (1989) SCC (Cr) 774; AIR 1990 SC 220; 1989 (2) AWC 1250; 1989 (3) Crimes 278; 1990 CRLJ 573; JT 1989 (3) SC 659; 1989(2) SCALE 565; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 56; [1989] supp1 S.C.R. 56

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - National Security Act, 1980, ss. 3(2) and 7 - (A) Whether detention under Act is for purpose of preventing persons from acting in any manner prejudicial to maintenance of public order and whether there was ordinary delay in performing such detention? - Manner of Preventive detention u/s. 3 of act be subject to closest scrutiny and examination by courts in interest of public order, for greater good of community - It becomes imperative to detain a person in order to prevent him and not merely to punish him from threatened or contemplated or anticipated course of action - S. 7 of Act gave power to authorities to take action in case persons were absconding and in case order of detention cannot be executed - It was stated that in this case no warrant u/s. 7 of Act has been issued in respect of his property or person - Hence, respondent was not justified in raising plea that petitioner was absconding - Held, there was undue delay, delay not commensurate with facts situation in this case conduct as aforesaid betrayed that there was no real and genuine apprehension that petitioner was likely to act in any manner prejudicial to public order - Order, therefore, quashed and set aside - Petition allowed.
