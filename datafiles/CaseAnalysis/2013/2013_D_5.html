State Of Madhya Pradesh v Dal Singh & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

21 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 337; (2013) 14 SCC 159; 2013 (5) ADJ 517; AIR 2013 SC 2059; 2013 CRLJ 2983; 2013 (3) JabLJ 175; 2013 (3) JCC 2010; JT 2013 (8) SC 625; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 1; 2013(7) SCALE 513; [2013] 8 S.C.R. 968

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. u/ss. 34, 302, and 498A IPC - Dowry death - Acquittal - Sustainability - Respondents accused allegedly harassed the deceased, poured kerosene and set ablaze, which resulted into death of deceased - Respondents accused allegedly committed offences punishable u/ss. 34, 302, and 498A IPC - Magistrate (PW12) recorded dying declaration of deceased - Trial Court convicted the respondents u/ss. 34, 302, 306 and 498A IPC and sentenced them to undergo imprisonment for life - Aggrieved, respondents preferred appeal before HC - HC allowed the appeal and acquitted the respondents - Aggrieved, appellant State preferred instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in acquitting the respondents - Held, evidence led by prosecution witnesses to extent that deceased had died within a short span of 10 months of her marriage, and that she had ill-treated by her parents-in-law as she was not given proper food etc - Deceased had harassed and tortured by her in-laws, as she was not good looking, could not cook well, and had unable to do household work properly - Deceased was considered to had a temperamental nature, and thus had also been slapped - However, that evidence had not challenged by defence - As defence did not put any question either to Magistrate, or to I.O., or to doctors who had examined her or conducted post-mortem, with respect to whether any part of thumb had skin on it or not, as in both dying declarations, ridges and curves had clearly found to exist - There was no reason to dis-believe the version of events provided by Magistrate and I.O., who had recorded dying declarations - No suggestion was made to either of them in that regard, nor was any explanation furnished with respect to why these 2 independent persons who had recorded dying declarations, should had deposed against respondents - In event that both of them had found deceased to be in a fit physical and mental condition to make a statement, there exists no reason to disbelieve same - Impugned judgment passed by HC was set aside and judgment and order of Trial Court was restored - Appeal allowed.
