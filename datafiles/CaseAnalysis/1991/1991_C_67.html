Garden Silk Weaving Factory v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

22 March 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 922; (1991) 2 SCC 684; AIR 1991 SC 1322; 1991 (2) CompLJ 621; 1991 (94) CTR 136; [1991] 189 ITR 512; JT 1991 (5) SC 160; 1991(1) SCALE 501; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 909; [1991] 56 TAXMAN 416

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Assessment - Unabsorbed depreciation - Unabsorbed business loss - Carry forward - Set off - Assessee, a registered firm, returned a certain amount as total income for the A/Y and a provisional assessment u/s. 141 of 1961 Act was made - Income Tax Officer (ITO) found that assessee had deducted unabsorbed depreciation, unabsorbed development rebate and unabsorbed business loss to arrive at the total income - ITO found that unabsorbed depreciation and unabsorbed business loss could not be carried forward and added back that the aggregate of the amount to the total income of assessee - Tribunal held assessee was not allowed to carry forward and set off the unabsorbed business loss carried from the previous year - But, unabsorbed depreciation could be carried forward and set off - On appeal, HC answered in favour of the Revenue and against the assessee - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether assessee was entitled to carry forward and set off unabsorbed depreciation and unabsorbed business loss against its total income for the A/Y - Held, assessee was entitled to a carry forward of unabsorbed depreciation computed for the A/Y and have it set off in its assessment - Unabsorbed business loss computed for the A/Y could not be carried forward by assessee to be set off in its assessment - Further, HC was right in holding that unabsorbed business loss of one year could not be carried forward and set off by firm in a subsequent year - But if there was any unabsorbed depreciation computed for the A/Y, it could be allowed to be brought forward and set off in assessment for the A/Y - Appeals were allowed to the extent indicated and assessments directed to be modified appropriately - Appeals allowed.
