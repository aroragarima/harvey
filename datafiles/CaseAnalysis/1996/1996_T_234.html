Balbir Chand v The Food Corporation Ofindia Ltd. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3685; (1997) 3 SCC 371; (1997) SCC (L&S) 808; AIR 1997 SC 2229; JT 1996 (11) SC 507; 1997 (2) LLJ 879; 1997 (1) SLJ 756; 1997 (1) SLR 756; [1996] Supp10 S.C.R. 156; 1997 (3) Supreme 26

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Petitioner was working as Manager in Food Corporation of India - Disciplinary action was initiated against appellant and some others for their dereliction of duty and misconduct in their failure to submit a verification report truthfully - After conduct of joint enquiry, petitioner removed from service - On appeal dismissed by High Court - (A) Whether, since Petitioner was required to be dismissed by disciplinary authority, namely, Zonal Manager, who alone is competent to remove him, order of dismissal passed by Managing Director is bad in law? - Held, in normal circumstances where Managing Director being appellate authority should not pass order of punishment so as to enable delinquent employee to avail of right of appeal - No prohibition in law that higher authority should not take decision or impose penalty as primary authority in matter of disciplinary action - No discrimination violating art. 14 or causing material prejudice - (B) Whether common proceeding is violative of petitioner's rights? - Held, common proceedings should be ordered only as a last resort and in case such proceedings are ordered, charge should be also common against all charged officials involved - When more than one delinquent officer are involved, then with a view to avoid multiplicity of proceedings, it is always necessary that common enquiry should be conducted against all delinquent officers - Disciplinary enquiry should not be equated as a prosecution for an offence in a criminal court where delinquents are arrayed as co-accused - The need to split cases is redundant, time consuming and dilatory and should not be encouraged - (C) Whether fact that some of delinquents were let off with a minor penalty while petitioner was imposed with a major penalty is violative of art. 14? - Held, merely because one of officers was wrongly given lesser punishment compared to others against whom there is a proved misconduct, it cannot be held that they too should also be given lesser punishment - Cannot be held as arbitrary or discriminatory leading and violative of art. 14 - Special Leave Petition dismissed.
