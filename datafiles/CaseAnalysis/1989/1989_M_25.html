State of Uttar Pradesh and Others v Maharaja Dharmander Prasad Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 395; (1989) 2 SCC 505; AIR 1989 SC 997; JT 1989 (1) SC 118; 1989 (2) RCJ 230; 1989 (1) RentLR 353; 1989(1) SCALE 106; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 176; 1989 (1) UJ 474

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 1 and 11 - Uttar Pradesh Urban Planning Development Act, 1973, ss. 14, 15 and 41 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Lease - Nazool land - Forfeiture and Cancellation - By State Govt. - (A) Validity as lessor can resume possession only in a manner known or recognised by law as per Transfer of Property Act - Whether this issue can be agitated u/art. 226 - (B) Permission for construction - Obtained by misrepresentation and fraud - Violative of conditions of grant - Can be revoked by Vice Chairman - Cancellation of permission - (C) Powers under 1973 Act - If at par with judicial powers - (D) Natural justice - Permission for construction - By Development Authority - Cancellation of - Large investments and factual matters of some complexity yet to be determined - Opportunity of being heard - (E) Judicial review u/art. 226 - Whether

can be converted into Appeal - Held, (A) Cannot be allowed to be agitated u/art. 226 - (B) Not vitiated by surrender of discretion - (C) Cannot be equated with judicial power - (D) Statutory authority should afford a personal hearing to such persons - (E) Cannot be as it is directed not against decision but is confined to examination of decision making process - Order accordingly.
