Sanjeev Kumar Jain v Raghubir Saran Charitable Trust & Ors

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 740; (2012) 1 SCC 455; 2012 (90) ALR 480; 2012 (2) ALT 7; 2012 (1) AWC 408; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 507; 2012 (1) CTC 874; 2011 (4) KLT 966; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 329; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 903; 2012 (115) RD 409; 2011(12) SCALE 278; [2011] 12 S.C.R. 744

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Model Case Flow Management Rules

Summary: Civil Procedure - - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 20A r. 2, ss. 35, 35(2), 35A - Delhi High Court Act, 1966 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996ss. 11, 31(8)(a)(b) - Suit for Eviction - Appeal - Award of Costs - Validity - Appellant was tenant under respondent in regard of first floor unit and also mezzanine floor unit - Respondents initiated proceedings for eviction of appellant in regard to mezzanine floor unit and obtained vacant possession thereof - Appellants filed suit for permanent injunction to restrain respondents from obstructing him from using said staircase to reach first floor unit - Interim relief was given to appellant and subsequently interim order was vacated - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal in HC - DB awarded sum of Rs. 45,28,000/- as costs in respect of appeal to be paid by appellant to respondents - Hence, instant appeal - Whether sum of Rs. 45,28,000/- could be awarded as costs in appeal against vacating temporary injunction in injunction suit - Held, DB indicated that losing party should pay litigation expenses relating to appeal which was reiteration of what was stated in s. 35 CPC - However there was no consent for sum of Rs.45,28,000/- was being determined or was being awarded as costs - S. 35 CPC provided that if there were any conditions or limitations prescribed in Code or in any rules, Court should not ignore them in awarding costs - Actual realistic cost should have correlation to costs which were realistic and practical - Mechanically ordering losing party to pay costs of Rs. 45,28,000/- in appeal against grant of temporary injunction in pending suit for permanent injunction was unwarranted and contrary to law - Maximum that should be awarded as compensatory costs in regard to false and vexatious claims was Rs. 3,000/- - Compensatory costs awardable u/s. 35A CPC was restitutive rather than punitive - Costs should be commensurate with time spent by Courts - - Reasonableness and certainty about total costs were key in arbitration proceedings also - Respondents had succeeded before HC and award of such costs was not at instance of respondents - Hence, appellant should pay costs of appeal before HC as per Rules plus Rs. 3000/- as exemplary costs to respondents - Appeal allowed.
