Central Provinces Transport Services Limited v Raghunath Gopal Patwardhan

Supreme Court of India

06 November 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, S.K. Das, P. Govinda Menon

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 22; AIR 1957 SC 104; 1957 (1) LLJ 27; 1957 SCJ 58; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 956; 1957 (8) STC 463

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Textiles, Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946, Central Provinces and Berar Industrial Dispute Settlement Act, 1947

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Indirect Tax - Practice & Procedure - Sales Tax - Berar Industrial Disputes Settlement Act, 1947, ss. 2 and 16 - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1946 - (A) Whether a dismissed employee will be denied relief only by reason that he was not employed at time of application? - Held, a dismissed employee is an 'employee' within s. 2(10) of Berar Industrial Disputes Settlement Act, 1947, and thereby, cannot be denied relief for not being employed at time of application - (B) Whether a dispute between an employer and a workman would amount to an industrial dispute? - Held, expression 'Industrial Dispute' is wider than what it bears in Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 so as to cover not only disputes of workman as a class but also individual disputes - Context 'industrial dispute' must be interpreted as including claim of an employee who has been dismissed - S. 16 of Act enables an employee to enforce individual rights - Appeal dismissed.
