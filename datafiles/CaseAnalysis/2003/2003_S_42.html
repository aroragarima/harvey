Federal Bank Limited v Sagar Thomas and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 806; (2003) 10 SCC 733; AIR 2003 SC 4325; 2003 (3) CLR 801; [2004] 120 Comp Cas 63; 2004 (1) ESC 38; 2004 (100) FLR 1231; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 470; 2003 (4) KHCACJ 216; 2003 (3) KLT 876; 2003 LabIC 3455; 2004 (1) LLJ 161; 2003 (4) LLN 469; 2003(8) SCALE 143; 2004 (1) SLJ 436; 2003 (6) SLR 396; 2003 (6) SLT 65; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 121; 2003 (7) Supreme 22; 2003 (3) UPLBEC 2728

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Bombay Trust Act, 1950, International Airports Authority Act, 1971, Uttar Pradesh Co-operative Societies Act, Uttar Pradesh Co-operative Land Development Bank Act, 1964, Banking Regulation Act, 1941, Uttar Pradesh Co-operative Land Development Banks Rules, 1971

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 12, 14, 19(2), 226, 311 - Banking Regulation Act, 1949, ss. 35, 35(4), 36, 36AB, 45, 46-A, 5(c)(a) - Respondent working as a bank manager was dismissed from service - Maintainability of WP under art.226 - Private bank - HC held that since Federal Bank Limited was performing public duty, it is covered under expression of 'other authority, within meaning of art 12 of Constitution, hence WP is maintainable before HC - Whether appellant bank is a private body or falls within definition of State or local or other authorities under control of Govt. - Held, a private company carrying on banking business as a scheduled bank, cannot be termed as an institution or company carrying on any statutory or public duty - A private body or a person may be amenable to writ jurisdiction only where it may become necessary to compel such body or association to enforce any statutory obligations or such obligations of public nature casting positive obligation upon it - Further held, merely regulatory provisions to ensure such activity carried on by private bodies work within a discipline, do not confer any such status upon company nor puts any such obligation upon it which may be enforced through issue of a writ under art.226 of Constitution.
