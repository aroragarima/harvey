M/s. Shyam Oil Cake Limited v Collector of Central Excise, Jaipur

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, AR. Lakshmanan, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1076; (2005) 1 SCC 264; AIR 2005 SC 1192; 2004 (174) E.L.T. 145; JT 2004 (10) SC 518; 2005 (2) MLJ(SC) 75; 2004 (65) RLT 789; 2004(9) SCALE 641; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 346

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Trade

Summary: [A.] Excise - Central Excises And Salt Act, 1944, s. 2(f) - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Duty - Whether processing of edible vegetable oil, purchased by Appellants, results in manufacture - Held, neither in section Note nor in Chapter Note nor in Tariff no any indication that process indicated is to amount manufacture - To start with product was edible vegetable oil, after refining, it remains edible vegetable oil - As actual manufacture has not taken place, deeming provision cannot be brought into play in absence of it being specifically stated that process amounts to manufacture - There is no manufacture and refined oil is not excisable.
