Regional Manager & Anr. v Pawan Kumar Dubey

Supreme Court of India

08 March 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, A.N. Ray, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 161; (1976) 3 SCC 334; (1976) SCC (L&S) 436; AIR 1976 SC 1766; 1976 (2) ALR 438; 1976 LabIC 1146; 1976 (2) LLJ 266; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 540; 1976 SLJ 387; 1976 (2) SLR 44; 1976 UJ 410

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution, arts. 16 and 311(2) - (A) Precedents - What constitute 'Ratio decidendi' - (B) Proof of violation of art. 16 - But discrimination must be proved - Order of reversion - Held, it is the rule deducible from the application of law to the facts and circumstances of a case which constitutes its ratio decidendi and not some conclusion based upon facts which may appear to be similar and one additional or different fact can make a world of difference between conclusions in two cases even when the same principles are applied in each case to similar facts - Order of reversion held was violative of art.311(2) - Appeal Dismissed.
