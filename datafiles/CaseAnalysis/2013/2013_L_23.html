Omprakash v Laxminarayan and others

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 662; (2014) 1 SCC 618; 2014 (1) ALD(SC) 83; 2013(6) ALL MR 941; 2013 (101) ALR 224; 2014 (1) AWC 325; 2013 (4) CLT(SC) 168; 2013 (6) CTC 227; 2014 (2) JabLJ 391; 2014 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 519; 2014(3) M.P.H.T. 319; 2014 (3) MPLJ 16; 2013 (4) RCR(Civil) 747; 2013 (121) RD 476; 2014 (123) RD 736; 2013(12) SCALE 441; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 923; 2014 (1) WLN(SC) 36

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Indian Stamp (Madhya Pradesh Second Amendment) Act, 1990

Summary: Stamp Act, 1899-Sections 2 (10) and 35-Suit for specific performance of contract, possession and permanent injunction in respect of un-irrigated land founded on agreement to sell-Agreement to sell in question contains recital that possession handed over to purchaser-Agreement to sell in question is on stamp paper of R 50 only-Agreement to sell deemed to be conveyance and stamp duty leviable on instrument whereby possession has been transferred-Thus, agreement to sell in question is conveyance within meaning of Section 2 (10) and is to be duly stamped-Section 35 makes instruments not duly stamped inadmissible in evidence-Agreement to sell in question insufficiently stamped-Same inadmissible in evidence-Court being authority to receive document in evidence to give effect thereto-Agreement to sell with possession of property is instrument which requires payment of stamp duty applicable to deed of conveyance within meaning of Section 2 (10)-Duty as required, not paid, hence, trial court rightly held same to be inadmissible in evidence-Impugned order of High Court not sustainable and set aside-Order of trial court-Restored.
