Papaiah v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2330; (1996) 10 SCC 533; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 590; AIR 1997 SC 2676; JT 1996 (9) SC 292; 1996(7) SCALE 100; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 206; 1996 (7) Supreme 617

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Constitution - Contract Act, 1872, s. 23 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 69(b) - Act in contravention of public policy - Effect - Alienation of land assigned to member of depressed class - Rule governing matter specifically prohibiting alienation by allottee - Assignment being in furtherance of constitutional obligation of Govt. u/art. 39(b), alienation in contravention thereof and also against public policy u/s. 23 of 1872 Act, purchaser does not get any right - Proceedings for title by adverse possession laid before 30 years - Plea of adverse possession against real owner i.e. State, whether acceptable? - Held, plea does not arise - Appeal dismissed.
