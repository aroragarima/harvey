Satish Ambanna Bansode v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

05 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 258; (2009) 11 SCC 217; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 1306; AIR 2009 SC 1626; JT 2009 (3) SC 329; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 294; 2009(3) SCALE 560; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 1166

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, s. 302 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, this is a case where the basis of conviction of the accused is the dying declaration - Once the court is satisfied that the declaration was true and voluntary, undoubtedly, it can base its conviction on the same without any further corroboration - It cannot be laid down as an absolute rule of law that the dying declaration cannot form the sole basis of conviction unless it is corroborated - Appeal dismissed.
