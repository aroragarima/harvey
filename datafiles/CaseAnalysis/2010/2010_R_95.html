Ranjeet Singh @ Dara v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

20 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, B. Sudershan Reddy

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 772; (2010) 11 SCC 629; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 3653; JT 2010 (11) SC 190; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 372; 2010(10) SCALE 15; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 986

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Trial Court convicted appellant for the offences punishable u/s. 302 of IPC and sentenced him to imprisonment for life and imposed a fine of Rs. 5000/- - On appeal, HC confirmed the conviction of the appellant u/s. 302 of IPC - Appellant filed appeal against said order submitting that it was a case of false implication and registration of the FIR was deliberately delayed in order to shield the real culprit - Whether trial court and HC rightly convicted appellant u/s. 302 of IPC? - Held, there is no eye-witness account of the murder - Both the courts below have examined the entire evidence and have reached the conclusion that the murder has been committed by none other than the appellant - Furthermore, the appellant has failed to give any explanation for his presence in the room of the deceased - There is no explanation about the presence of blood stained sword in appellant's hand - All the circumstances taken together clearly point towards the guilt of the appellant - Moreover, sufficient explanation has been given about the delay of registration of FIR by PW 13 - Conviction of appellant upheld - Appeal dismissed.
