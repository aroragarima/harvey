Official Liquidator v Dayanand And Others

Supreme Court of India

04 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, B.N. Agrawal, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1715; (2008) 10 SCC 1; 2008 (4) CompLJ 401; JT 2008 (11) SC 467; 2009 (3) LLJ 305; 2008(13) SCALE 558; [2008] 15 S.C.R. 331; 2008 (Supp) AD(SC) 1177

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Emoluments, Consolidated Fund Of India, Cooperative, Age Of Superannuation, Daily Rate, General Public, Pendency Of Proceedings, Parity, Housing, Meritorious, Central Civil Services (Conduct) Rules

Summary: Companies (Court) Rules, 1959 - Appeals against orders whereby directions have been issued to the appellants to absorb the persons employed by the Official Liquidators attached to those High Courts under r. 308 against the posts sanctioned by the Government of India, Department of Company Affairs - Held, directions given by the High Courts for creation of supernumerary posts to facilitate absorption of the company paid staff are legally unsustainable - Conditions of their appointment clearly envisaged cessation of employment at the end of fixed tenure or on completion of liquidation proceedings - No material has been placed to show that any promise was made or any assurance was held out to the respondents by any competent authority of the Government of India for their absorption in the regular cadres - There is nothing in the language of r. 308 of the 1959 Rules from which it can be inferred that those employed as additional staff in connection with the liquidation proceedings will, in future, be absorbed in the regular cadres - Any direction by the Court for absorption of all company paid staff would be detrimental to public interest - 1999 Scheme does not suffer from any legal or constitutional infirmity insofar as it provides for absorption of the company paid staff only to the extent of 50% vacancies in direct recruitment quota of Group C posts - Appeals allowed.
