Virender Kumar, General Manager, Northernrailways: New Delhi v Avinash Chandra Chadha And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 814; (1990) 3 SCC 472; (1991) SCC (L&S) 62; AIR 1991 SC 958; 1990 (14) ATC 732; 1990 (61) FLR 669; JT 1990 (3) SC 503; 1991 LabIC 435; 1990(1) SCALE 857; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 769; 1990 (2) UJ 100

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 311 - Promotion - Retrospective emoluments - No directions about promotion to Class II service and emoluments of higher posts with retrospective effect, categorically denied by High Court - However, Railways worked out promotions to Class II service on basis of new seniority list - Whether employees entitled to claim promotions to higher posts? - Held, neither equity nor justice was in favour of employees to award them emoluments of higher posts with retrospective effect - Employees not working in said posts - Principle of 'no work no pay' attracted - Employees not entitled to claim promotions - Appeal allowed.
