FOR EDUCATIONAL USE ONLY

Mohammed Faizal K. A. v D. Sali and others

Supreme Court of India

04 October 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 774

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Punishment, Vacancy, Competent Authority, Selection, Vacancies, Promotion, Representation, Seniority, Rule, Date, Merit, Appeal, Cadre, Reduction, Dpc, Eligibility, Notional Promotion, Entitlement, Police, Date Of Promotion, Kerala State and Subordinate Service Rules, Writ Petition, Employee, Post

Summary: (A) Service - Promotion - Non-inclusion - Challenged - Respondent no.1 filed writ petition challenging G.O. (Rt) No. 2527/04/ dt. 20-11-2004 seeking his non-inclusion in select list for promotion to the post of Dpty. Superintendent of Police for the years 2000 and 2001 by Dept. Promotion Committee (DPC) - Single Judge held that it was not open to Competent Authority to consider punishment imposed on respondent no.1 in the year 1997 to deny his inclusion in the select list for the year 2001 - On appeals, DB dismissed appeals and upheld judgment of Single Judge - Hence, instant appeals - Whether appellant had locus to challenge decisions of Single Judge.

Held, crux of direction issued by Court was to consider claim of respondent no.1 for promotion against vacancy of 2001 on the basis of his Confidential Records for the years 1998, 1999 and 2001 "in accordance with law". SC find no infirmity in the direction issued by HC. For, the nature of direction given by Single Judge provided full play to DPC to select or not to select respondent no.1 against the vacancies of 2001 after considering Confidential Records of respondent no.1 for three preceding years, i.e. 1998, 1999 and 2000 "in accordance with law". That was how DPC as well as Competent Authority of State understood the direction and after due deliberations issued an order 20-11-2004. Competent Authority while considering the claim referred to the fact that major punishment (i.e. increment bar for 2 years with cumulative effect vide PHQ Order No.G5/73080 dt. 5-11-1997) was still operating against respondent no.1. That punishment was to operate for two years with cumulative effect from 1997. Appeals allowed.

(B) Service - Promotion re-allocation - Permissibility - Whether consideration of punishment awarded in 1997 for promotion of respondent no.1 against the vacancy of 2001 was permissible.

Held, fact that Competent Authorities were ill-advised not to challenge such untenable direction of Single Judge would not preclude aggrieved person from challenging the same. Appellant was justified in challenging impugned decision of Single Judge because of consequential orders passed by Competent Authorities allocating notional date of promotion and assignment of seniority to respondent no. 1. Delay in filing appeals was duly explained by appellant. Further, authorities might have to re-visit the case of respondent no.1 to allot him date of promotion in terms of order dt. 20-11-2004 and restore his seniority position in the cadre of Dpty. Superintendent of Police and correspondingly re-allocate notional date of promotion for the next promotion to the post of Superintendent of Police, if respondent no.1 has completed qualifying service period for being considered for promotion thereto. SC has no hesitation in holding that merely because other officers similarly placed as appellant have not questioned impugned decision, would be no impediment in reviving and restoring G.O. dt. 20-11-2014. No prejudice would be caused to them in directing concerned authorities of State Govt. to complete necessary formalities in furtherance of such order within 3 months. Appeals allowed.
