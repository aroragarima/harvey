Sister Mina Lalita Baruwa v State of Orissa and others

Supreme Court of India

05 December 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 798; (2013) 16 SCC 173; AIR 2014 SC 782; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 368; 2014 CRLJ 671; JT 2014 (1) SC 190; 2014 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1951; 2014 (1) OLR 478; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 257; 2013(14) SCALE 600

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Miscarriage

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss.301 and 311 - Rape - Recall of witness - Denial - Legality - Appellant was allegedly raped by respondent No.9 - Test Identification Parade (TIP) was conducted by PW18/Sub-Divisional Judicial Magistrate and statement of appellant was recorded - However, it was pointed out by appellant, that in the proceedings recorded by PW18, there was no reference to any statement made by the appellant as regards the behaviour of respondent No.9 except mere identification of the suspects, namely, respondents No.3 and 9 and wrong identification of an under trial prisoner - According to the appellant, she approached the Special Public Prosecutor to set right the said deliberate misstatement of PW18 in the evidence and confront PW18 as to whatever stated by him was not reflected in the test identification parade report - According to the appellant, the Special Public Prosecutor having not bothered to take any steps, an application was moved by the appellant herself before Trial Court - In the proceedings of the Trial Court while making reference to the petition filed by the appellant for recalling PW18, Trial Court by stating that such a petition at the instance of the victim not having been filed by the Special Public Prosecutor, the same was rejected after hearing the appellant solely on the ground of maintainability - Aggrieved by the said order, the appellant moved the HC - HC while making reference to s.301 CrPC, took the view that the appellant as an informant had a very limited role to play so far as the trial was concerned, that she could not have filed the petition to recall certain witnesses and that such a step was beyond the authority granted to an informant or a private person u/s. 301 CrPC - Whether denial of Trail Court and HC to recall witness was sustainable - Held, grievances as projected by the appellant as a victim, who was a victim of an offence of such a grotesque nature, Trial Court as well as the HC instead of rejecting the application of the appellant by simply making a reference to s.301 CrPC in a blind folded manner, ought to have examined as to how the oral evidence of PW18 which did not tally with statement recorder by him, the author of whom was PW18 himself, to be appropriately set right by either calling upon the Special Public Prosecutor himself to take necessary steps or for that matter there was nothing lacking in the Court to have remedied the situation by recalling the said witness and by putting appropriate Court question - If any witness and in the case on hand a statutory witness happened to make a blatantly wrong statement not born out from the records of his own, SC failed to understand why at all Trial Court, as well as the HC, should have hesitated or adopted a casual approach instead of taking appropriate measures to keep the record straight and clear any ambiguity in so far as the evidence part was concerned and also ensure that no prejudice was caused to any one - Both Trial Court and HC should have made an attempt to reconcile ss.301 and 311 CrPC in such peculiar situations and ensured that the trial proceeded in the right direction - Criminal Court cannot remain a silent spectator. It has got a participatory role to play and having been invested with enormous powers under S. 311 of Cr.P.C., as well as S. 165 of the Evidence Act, a trial Court in a situation like the present one where it was brought to the notice of the Court that a flagrant contradiction in the evidence of PW-18 who was a statutory authority and in whose presence the test identification parade was held, who is also a Judicial Magistrate, ought to have risen to the occasion in public interest and remedied the situation by invoking S. 311 of Cr.P.C., by recalling the said witness with the further direction to the public prosecutor for putting across the appropriate question or court question to the said witness and thereby set right the glaring error accordingly - Zahira Habibulla H. Sheikh v. State of Gujarat, 2004 Indlaw SC 1682, relied on - Appeal allowed.
