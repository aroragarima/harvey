R. Saragapani (Dead) and another v Special Tahsildar, Karur-Dindigul Broadguage Line

Supreme Court of India

23 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 674; (2011) 14 SCC 177; 2012(1) ALL MR 485; 2011 (89) ALR 462; 2012 (1) AWC 1081; 2012 (1) CLT(SC) 122; JT 2011 (11) SC 559; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 656; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 811; 2012 (115) RD 15; 2011(11) SCALE 1

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 18, 54 - Land Acquisition -- Award - Justification - Govt. issued notification u/s. 4(1) of the Act, for acquisition of land owned by appellants - Land Acquisition Officer passed award and declared that appellants were entitled to compensation - Dissatisfied with award of Land Acquisition Officer appellants filed applications to enhance compensation - Thereupon, Collector made reference to Court for determination of compensation payable to appellants - Reference Court passed award according to market - Respondent challenged award of Reference Court by filing appeals u/s. 54 of the Act before HC - DB re-evaluated evidence produced by parties and held trees existing on acquired land should have been 2 - 4 years old only - Further, DB did not agree with method of valuation adopted by Reference Court for determining market value of acquired land and held that appellants were entitled to enhanced compensation - Hence, instant appeals - Whether HC was justified in substantially reducing compensation determined by Reference Court - Held, both Reference Court and HC committed an error by awarding interest w.e.f 20-.5-.1987 - Further Court found that while Reference Court had relied on reports of Agricultural Development Officer and Court Commissioner for purpose of recording finding that as on date of notification u/s. 4(1) of the Act, age of trees could be 8 to 9 years and in due course even flowering trees would become fruit bearing trees and yield income for next 60 to 70 years - HC totally ignored two reports and fixed market value of young trees by treating same as timber - Therefore, HC committed an error by upsetting view taken by Reference Court on issue of market value of trees - Hence, award passed by Reference Court was restored with modification that appellants should be entitled to interest on enhanced amount w.e.f dt. 11-3-1985, i.e. date on which possession of land was taken by Department - Appeals allowed.
