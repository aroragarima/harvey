Kartar Singh And Others v State Of Haryana Through Inspector .General Of Prison, Chand

Supreme Court of India

26 August 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, Baharul Islam, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 44; (1982) 3 SCC 1; (1982) SCC (Cr) 522; AIR 1982 SC 1439; 1983 (1) Crimes 43; 1982 CRLJ 1772; 1982 CrLR(SC) 414; 1982(1) SCALE 671; [1983] 1 S.C.R. 445; 1982 UJ 621

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1861, s. 301 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 428 - Life imprisonment - Premature release - Entitlement of - Petitioners on conviction u/s. 301 IPC were sentenced to imprisonment for life - Petitioners contended that they are entitled to be considered for premature release if to their total period of imprisonment inclusive of remissions is added the period of their under-trial detention as on such reckoning the total detention exceeds 14 years and their continued detention is illegal - But respondent State has issued an order to all Jail Superintendents in the State whereunder instructions have been issued that for the purpose of considering cases of pre-mature release while calculating 8-1/2 years substantive sentence and 14 years imprisonment including remissions the benefit of under-trial period is not to be given to life convicts who have been convicted before 18-12-1978 - Hence instant petition - Whether persons sentenced to imprisonment for life are entitled to set off their under-trial period of detention against their sentence u/s. 428 of the CrPC -

Held, it is obvious that the mischief sought to be remedied has no relevance where gravity of offence requires the imposition of imprisonment for life. It is clear that the benefit of the set off contemplated by s. 428 CrPC would not be available to life convicts. In SC's view, the decision of the Gujarat HC in the case of Kalidas Vanmalibhai is erroneous and the contrary view taken by Kerala HC in Kanthalot Karunan & others v. State of Kerala, by Bombay High Court in Rajahusein Gulamhusein Lakhani v. The State of Maharashtra 1976 Indlaw MUM 3344, Rafiq Abdul Rehman v. The State of Maharashtra 1977 Indlaw MUM 3542 and by Rajasthan HC in Bhimsen v. The State of Rajasthan 1976 Indlaw RAJ 7 is correct. Impugned order passed by the respondent-State, being in conformity with s. 428 CrPC, is perfectly legal and valid. Petition dismissed.
