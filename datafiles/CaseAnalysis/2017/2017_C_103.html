Formula One World Championship Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, International Taxation - 3, Delhi and another

Supreme Court of India

24 April 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Ashok Bhushan

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 325; [2017] 394 ITR 80; JT 2017 (6) SC 276; 2017 (4) MLJ 405; 2017(5) SCALE 228; [2017] 248 TAXMAN 192

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Maintenance, Agent, Goods Or Merchandise, Resident, Advance Ruling, Permanent Establishment, Tax At Source, Business Connection, Promoter, Royalty, Constitution of India, 1950, Income-tax Act, 1961

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 195, 195(2), 201, 5(2)(b), 9, 9(1)(i), 9(1)(vi), 92F - Appellants/Formula One World Championship Limited (FOWC), Jaypee Sports International Limited (Jaypee) filed applications before Authority for Advance Ruling (AAR) - AAR held that consideration paid or payable by Jaypee to FOWC amounted to 'Royalty' under Double Taxation Avoidance Agreement(DTAA) and it did not have any PE in India - Appellants filed writ petitions before HC challenging that payment would not constitute Royalty - HC reversed the findings of AAR holding that Jaypee was bound to make appropriate deductions from the amount payable to FOWC u/s. 195 of the Act - Hence, instant appeals - Whether FOWC carried on any business and commercial activity in India or not.

Held, appellants were trying to trivialize the issue by harping on the fact that duration of event was 3 days and control, if at all, would be for that period only. The question of PE has to be examined keeping in mind that the aforesaid race was to be conducted only for 3 days in a year and for the entire period of race control was with FOWC. HC has rightly concluded that having regard to the duration of the event, which was for limited days, and for the entire duration FOWC had full access through its personnel, number of days for which the access was there would not make any difference. Mere construction of track by Jaypee at its expense would be of no consequence. Its ownership or organising other events by Jaypee was immaterial. However, those became possible, in instant case, only with the Actual conduct of those races and active participation of FOWC in the said races, with access and control over circuit. Fixed place of business in the form of physical location, i.e. Buddh International Circuit, was at the disposal of FOWC through which it conducted business. Aesthetics of law and taxation jurisprudence leave no doubt that taxable event has taken place in India and non-resident FOWC was liable to pay tax in India on the income it has earned on that soil. Since, payments made by Jaypee to FOWC under Race Promotion Contract (RPC) were business income of FOWC through PE at Buddh International Circuit and chargeable to tax, Jaypee was bound to make appropriate deductions from the amounts paid u/s.195 of the Act. It would be for AO to adjudicate upon the aforesaid aspects while passing Assessment Order, namely, how much business income of FOWC was attributable to PE in India, which was chargeable to tax. At that stage, Jaypee could also press its argument that penalty etc. be not charged as the move on the part of Jaypee in not deducting tax at source was bona fide. Appeals dismissed.
