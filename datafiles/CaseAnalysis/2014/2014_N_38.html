National Institute of Technology v U. Dinakar and another

Supreme Court of India

30 June 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 410; (2014) 13 SCC 180; AIR 2015 SC 476; 2014 (5) AWC 5043; 2014 (143) FLR 782; JT 2014 (9) SC 380; 2014(8) SCALE 243; [2014] 6 S.C.R. 960

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Enquiry Committee, Karnataka Government Rules

Summary: Service - Letter of appointment - Clerical mistake - Correction of - Respondent No.1 was selected and appointed as Deputy Registrar of Karnataka Regional Engineering College (appellant-Institute) - Notification was issued giving an option to the existing incumbents either to continue in the Central pay scale or opt for State pay scale - Respondent No.1, who was working as Deputy Registrar in the appellant-Institute opted for the Central pay scale with respect to the post of Deputy Registrar - Subsequently, pursuant to a advertisement, respondent No.1 applied for the post of Registrar of the appellant-Institute and was selected - Appointment letter was issued prescribing the Central scale of pay i.e. Rs.3000-100-3500-125-4500 instead of the State pay scale of Rs.2375-75-2900-100-3700-125-4450 as provided in the advertisement notification - Enquiry Committee issued a show cause notice to respondent No.1 seeking explanation for the said anomaly - Thereafter, appellant-Institute, issued an order rectifying the pay scale of respondent No.1 - Aggrieved by the order, respondent No.1 filed an appeal - Appeal was rejected - Writ petition filed thereagainst was dismissed - On appeal, DB of HC held that respondent No. 1 was entitled to the Central pay scale and denial of such scale would be bad in law - Whether respondent No. 1 was entitled to the Central pay scale and denial of such scale would be bad in law -

Held, competent authority had inherent power to correct the mistake if any committed in the order of appointment after giving proper opportunity to the concerned employee/officer - In the instant case, authority had given notice to respondent no.1 and brought to his notice that there was a genuine mistake in his letter of appointment and he has been wrongly given a higher pay of scale of Rs.3000-4500 - Appellant had committed no error in correcting the letter of appointment by replacing the correct scale of pay to which respondent no.1 was entitled i.e. Rs.2375-75-2900-100-3700-125-4450 as provided in the advertisement/notification - Impugned judgment and order passed by the DB of HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
