Additional Commissioner (Legal) and Another v Messrs Jyoti Traders and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 November 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1772; (1999) 2 SCC 77; AIR 1999 SC 526; 1999 (152) CTR 177; JT 1998 (8) SC 60; 1998(6) SCALE 141; 1998 (9) SLT 388; 1999 (112) STC 277; [1998] Supp3 S.C.R. 67; 1998 (8) Supreme 517

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Sales Tax - Assessment - Uttar Pradesh Trade Tax Act, 1948, s. 21 - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax (Amendment and Validation) Act, 1991 - Amendment to s. 21 prescribed a period of 8 years - Commr. of Sales Tax issued notices to the respondents for re-assessment - High Court set aside the notice of Sales Tax authority for re-assessment - Whether assessment completed under the U.P. Sales Tax Law can be re-opened after prescribed period when that period was extended by subsequent amendment - Held, yes - Retrospective effect can be given - Immaterial if period for assessment or reassessment before the addition of the said proviso had expired - Completion of assessment or reassessment under s. 21 can be done before the expiration of 8 years of that particular assessment year - Appeals allowed.
