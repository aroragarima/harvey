Makhan Singh v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

27 July 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

N.D. Ojha, G.L. Oza, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 797; (1988) Supp SCC 526; (1988) SCC (Cr) 916; AIR 1988 SC 1705; 1988 (2) Crimes 942; 1988 CRLR 501; JT 1988 (3) SC 126; 1988(2) SCALE 87; [1988] Supp1 S.C.R. 613

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss.24, 8; Indian Penal code, 1860, s.300 - Extra judicial confession - Evidentiary value - Motive - Relevancy - Charge of murder - Son alleged to committing murder of father - To have lands of father transferred to him - Will by deceased indicating transfer of all kinds of lands of deceased to accused son - Held, Motive is dislodged - Confession is hardly of any value.
