Intellectuals Forum, Tirupathi v State of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 149; (2006) 3 SCC 549; AIR 2006 SC 1350; 2006 (2) CTC 71; JT 2006 (2) SC 568; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 201; 2006(2) SCALE 494; 2006 (2) SCJ 293; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 419

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Keywords: Fundamental Duties

Summary: Constitution - Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Constitution of India, 1950 - Protection of environment and fresh water resources - The instant case relates to the preservation of and restoration of status quo ante of two tanks, historical in nature being in existence since the time of King Srikrishnadevaraya in 1500 A.D. - Appellant in the instant case alleged that, the cry of socially spirited citizens calling for judicial remedy was not considered in the right perspective by the HC despite there being over-whelming evidence of the tanks being in existence and were being put to use not only for irrigation purpose but also as lakes which were furthering percolation to improve the ground water table, thus serving the needs of the people in and around these tanks - It was submitted that the HC had given precedence to the economic growth by completely ignoring the importance and primacy attached to the protection of environment and protection of valuable and most cherished fresh water resources - Whether, appellant should be granted with the relief - Held, on the facts and circumstances, instant court was of the view that, due to the persistent developmental activities over a long time, much of the natural resources of the lakes had been lost, and considered irreparable and though it was regrettable, was beyond the power of instant Court to rectify it - However, instant court, placing the reliance on the findings of the expert committee appointed by it which had considered the factual situation and the feasibility of revival of the two tanks, court passed the orders and directions with regard to two tanks which were the subject matter of the case - Some of the directions issued by the instant court were that, no further constructions to be made upon those, supply channels to be cleared and revitalized, a small check dam was ordered to be removed to ensure the free flow and supply to the tank, percolation tank to be constructed and artificial recharge to be done to ensure the revival of the tank, no borewell/tubewell for any purpose to be allowed in the area - Appeal disposed of.
