Pratap Press, Etc v Their Workmen

Supreme Court of India

23 February 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 55; AIR 1960 SC 1213; 1960 (1) LLJ 497

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Whether several activities going on in different units come under definition of 'Industry' or separate industrial units? - Functional integrality should be considered and question of unity of finance and employment - Functional integrality meaning thereby such functional interdependence that one unit cannot exist conveniently and reasonably without other - Held, functions of press and Vir Arjun paper cannot be considered to be so interdependent that one cannot exist without other - It cannot be therefore said that there is such functional interdependence between press unit and paper unit thus forming one industrial unit - Activities of press are independent of activities of paper unit and there is no record from which it can be ascertained how employer himself treated these two units - Appeal dismissed.
