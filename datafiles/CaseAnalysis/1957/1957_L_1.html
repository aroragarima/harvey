Â Messrs Bombay Salt and Chemical Industries v L. J. Johnson and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 October 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, S.K. Das, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 173; AIR 1958 SC 289

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Act, 1954, ss.19, 20 and 29 - Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Rules - Whether appellants are in lawful possession of property and whether u/s. 29 of Act, had statutory immunity from eviction for a period of 2 years? - Power to sell is given by s.20 and it permits a sale by auction but whether there is a transfer or not depends on conditions of auction; in this case certain conditions of auction sale had been imposed and set out in notice of sale - Held, it is clear from rules and conditions of sale that declaration that a person was highest bidder at auction does not amount to a complete sale and transfer of property to him and correct position is that on approval of bid by Settlement Commr., a binding contract for sale of property to auction-purchaser comes into existence, then provision as to sale certificate would indicate that only upon issue of it a transfer of property takes place; since appellants have not stated that sale certificate was issued, nor that balance of purchase money had been paid, there has yet been no transfer of salt pans to respondents No.'s 4 and 5 and appellants cannot therefore claim benefit of s.29 and in view of this, question whether appellants can be said to have been in lawful possession does not arise - Appeal dismissed.
