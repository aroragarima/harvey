Luhar Amrit Lal Nagji v Doshi Jayantilal Jethalal and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 464; AIR 1960 SC 964; 1962 (1) ALT(SC) 327; 1962 (1) MLJ 134; 1962 (1) MLJ(SC) 134; 1962 (1) SCJ 282; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 842

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Doctrine of inevitably - Whether if a Hindu son wants to challenge an alienation made by his father to pay his antecedent debt is it necessary for him to prove not only that said antecedent debt was immoral but also that alienee had notice of immoral character of said debt? - Held, in case of Brij Narain v Mangla Prasad ((1923) L R 51 I A 129)51 LR(IA) 129, Privy Council laid down propositions which show that a mortgage created by father for payment of his antecedent debt would bind his sons, so that, if sons want to challenge validity of mortgage they would have to show not only that antecedent debt was immoral but that alienee had notice of immoral character of said debt - Further, when Privy Council laid down two propositions in case of Suraj Bunsi Koer ((1879) L R 6 I A 88)6 LR(IA) 88 what was really intended was to protect bona fide alienees against frivolous or collusive claims made by debtor's sons challenging transactions - Since said propositions have been laid down with object of doing justice to claims of bona fide alienees, Court do not see any justification for disturbing this well-established position - Appeal dismissed.
