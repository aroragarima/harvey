Rabindra Nath Singh and another v Rajesh Ranjan @ Pappu Yadav and another

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 577; (2010) 6 SCC 417; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 3324; 2010 (3) JCC 1825; JT 2010 (5) SC 551; 2011 (1) MLJ(Crl) 533; 2010 (2) OLR 483; 2010 (2) RCR(Criminal) 859; 2010(4) SCALE 567; [2010] 5 S.C.R. 1156

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Bail - Grant of - Respondent/accused was granted bail by the HC in a murder case - Hence the present appeal by the appellant/State challenging the HC order granting bail - Held, SC rejected earlier two bail applications of the respondent and gave direction to the effect that no further bail application of respondent would be considered by any court - HC granted bail to respondent despite clear direction of SC that no further bail application of the respondent should be entertained - HC granted bail for no good reason except by saying that the appeal was not likely to be heard in six months - Therefore, impugned HC order amounted to contempt of SC, hence set aside - Appeals allowed.
