O. M. Cherian @ Thankachan v State of Kerala and others

Supreme Court of India

11 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 766; (2015) 2 SCC 501; AIR 2015 SC 303; 2015 CRLJ 593; JT 2014 (13) SC 34; 2014 (4) KLT 678; 2014 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4673; 2014 (4) MLJ(Crl) 622; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 922; 2015 (2) RLW 1644; 2014(12) SCALE 636; [2014] 11 S.C.R. 140

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Two Offences

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 498A, 306 - Cruelty - Abetment to suicide - Sentence to run concurrently - Appellant/accused No. 1 was charged u/ss. 498A and 306 IPC - Trial Court convicted appellant - On appeal HC confirmed the conviction and sentence - Hence instant appeal -

Held, SC answer the Reference by holding that s. 31 CrPC leaves full discretion with the Court to order sentences for two or more offences at one trial to run concurrently, having regard to the nature of offences and attendant aggravating or mitigating circumstances. SC do not find any reason to hold that normal rule is to order the sentence to be consecutive and exception is to make the sentences concurrent. Of course, if the Court does not order the sentence to be concurrent, one sentence may run after the other, in such order as the Court may direct. Trial Court directed the sentences imposed on appellant/accused u/ss. 498A and 306 IPC to run consecutively, which was affirmed by the HC. When the Trial Court declines to exercise its discretion u/s. 31 CrPC in issuing direction for concurrent running of sentences, normally the appellate court will not interfere, unless the refusal to exercise such discretion is shown to be arbitrary or unreasonable. When the Trial Court as well as the appellate court declined to exercise their discretion, normally we would have refrained from interfering with such direction of the courts for consecutive running of sentences. In SC's view, the sentences imposed on appellant could be ordered to be run concurrently. Appeal partly allowed.
