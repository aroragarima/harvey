Tukaram and Others v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

13 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 696; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 339; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 1772; 2008 (2) JCC 1038; JT 2008 (4) SC 467; 2008 (3) KarLJ 650; 2008(4) SCALE 543; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 37

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 304(II) - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, trial court and the High Court have analysed the evidence in great detail and have concluded that the appellant was the author of the crime - Stand that PWs. 1, 2 & 5 were related to the deceased is really of no consequence - Relationship is not a factor to affect credibility of a witness - It is more often than not that a relation would not conceal actual culprit and make allegations against an innocent person - Appeal dismissed.
