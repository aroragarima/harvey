Behram Khurshed Pesikaka v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 124; AIR 1955 SC 123; 1955 (57) BomLR 575; 1955 CRLJ 215; 1955 (1) MLJ 32; 1955 SCJ 73; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 613

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Competence Of The Legislature

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 338 - Bombay Prohibition Act, 1949, s. 66(b) - Rash and negligent Driving - Consumption of Medicinal Preparations - Permitted Usage - Constitutional Validity - Appellant/ Regional Transport Officer while proceeding in his car, knocked down three persons - Police arrested appellant and examined by doctor for alleged consumption of liquor - Presidency Magistrate, acquitted appellant for offences, u/s. 338 of the Code, for causing grievous hurt, by a rash and negligent act, and u/s. 66(b) of 1949 Act, - Evidence did not show that appellant had consumed alcohol without a permit, which were certain medicinal preparations, allowed to be used by law - On appeal of respondent before HC, which confirmed acquittal of charge u/s. 338 of the Code, but reversed order of acquittal u/s. 66(b) of 1949 Act - Hence instant appeal - Whether burden of proof lie on appellant, that "liquor" consumed by him was or was not medicinal or toilet preparations, though it contained alcohol and whether declaration of s. 13(b) of 1949 Act was unconstitutional - Held, Circumstance that appellant was smelling of alcohol and that he had consumed liquor in some form or other would not be an unequivocal circumstance pointing his guilt - Evidence of P.W. 1/doctor u/s. 106 of 1949 Act, only showed that appellant had consumed alcohol in some form or other, which was not inadmissible - Therefore, Court concluded that respondent/prosecution failed to prove that appellant had committed offence with which he was charged - Hence, Court allowed appeal, and quashed conviction and sentence passed on appellant by HC - But, Court opined that conviction of appellant u/s. 66(b) of 1949 Act, was confirmed, and reduced sentence of imprisonment to period already undergone - On a review petition presented by appellant for constitutional opinion, Judges granted review to enable them to obtain opinion of Constitution Bench of this Court on constitutional question formulated - As declaration of s. 13(b) of 1949 Act, prohibits consumption or use of liquid medicinal or toilet preparations containing alcohol, infringed citizens fundamental right u/art. 19(1) (f) of Constitution - Therefore, unconstitutional as declared by this Court - Conviction of appellant quashed - Appeal allowed.
