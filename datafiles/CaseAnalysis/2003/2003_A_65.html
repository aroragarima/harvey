National Insurance Co.Ltd v Ajit Kumar And Ors

Supreme Court of India

02 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 685; (2003) 9 SCC 668; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1914; 2003 (3) ACC 277; AIR 2003 SC 3093; 2003 (52) ALR 822; 2003 (5) AWC 4345; [2003] 117 Comp Cas 106; 2004 (1) JhrCR(SC) 63; JT 2003 (7) SC 520; 2003 (4) KHCACJ 389; 2003 (3) KLT 688; 2004 (136) PLR 153B; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 560; 2003(7) SCALE 136; 2003 (1) SCW 4120; 2003 (5) SLT 453; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 321; 2003 (6) Supreme 435; 2004 (1) UC 498B

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Consumer Protection; Insurance; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Contractual Liability

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - 149(2) - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - 96(2)(b)(ii) - Whether the insurer is liable to pay compensation under the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 for the death or bodily injury to a person traveling in goods vehicle as passenger - Held, the inevitable conclusion is that provisions of the Act do not enjoin any statutory liability on the owner of a vehicle to get his vehicle insured for any passenger traveling in a goods carriage and the insurer would have no liability therefor.
