State of Madhya Pradesh and others v Sanjay Kumar Pathak and others

Supreme Court of India

10 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1592; (2008) 1 SCC 456; JT 2007 (12) SC 219; 2007(12) SCALE 72; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 951

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Non-Gazetted Class III Education Service (Non-Collegiate Service) Recruitment and Promotion Rules, 1973

Summary: Service - Constitution - Madhya Pradesh Non-Gazetted Class III Education Service (Non-Collegiate Service) Recruitment and Promotion Rules, 1973 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Recruitment of teacher - Government of India sponsored project known as Operation Black Board for recruitment of additional teachers in primary/middle schools having only one teacher - With a view to expedite implementation of the project, r. 10(3) of the Recruitment Rules, 1973 was amended by Government by adding a proviso which prescribe the criteria and procedure for selection of candidates - When interviews were going on in district of Damoh, 'X' filed application challenging said amendment - Tribunal declared the said amendment to be illegal being violative of arts. 14 and 16 of the Constitution of India - On appeal, HC directed that State Government shall prepare a list of candidates who had approached the Tribunal in the original applications in the order of merit as per the select list and offer them appointments in respect of Assistant Teachers or equivalent posts within a reasonable period of time - HC also directed that Tribunal that candidates who had not been party to the original application could not be benefited by this order for the simple reason that he who is not vigilant loses his right - Whether order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, no - Tribunal as also the HC did not call for the documents pertaining to the selection process - No finding of fact has been arrived at that the respondents were bound to be selected and consequently appointed - As the selection process itself was not complete, there was nothing before the Tribunal as also the HC to indicate that they had acquired legal right of any kind whatsoever - Even where, it is trite, the names of the persons appeared in the selection list, the same by itself would not give rise to a legal right unless the action on the part of the State is found to be unfair, unreasonable or mala fide - State could not substitute itself for the Selection Committee - Impugned judgment set aside - Appeals allowed.
