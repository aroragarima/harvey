Laxmi Devi v State of Bihar and others

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 453; (2015) 10 SCC 241; AIR 2015 SC 2710; 2015 (5) ALD(SC) 135; 2015 (112) ALR 490; 2015 (4) AWC 3866; 2015 (3) CLT(SC) 39; 2015 (3) KLT 590; 2016 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1592; 2015 (5) MLJ 575; 2016 (2) RCR(Civil) 130; 2015 (129) RD 537; 2015(7) SCALE 555; [2015] 11 S.C.R. 179

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Standing Crops, Bihar Panchayat Raj Act, 1947, Statement of Objects and Reasons of Act, 1984

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 11A, 17 - Delay in passing of award - Instant appeals by appellants-landowners praying that land should revert to them u/s. 11A, since award u/s. 11 was not yet made despite passage of three decades from date of notification - Whether award is required to be passed when lands are expropriated u/s. 17 of the Act by State.

Held, appellants were denied just and fair compensation for their land in proceedings which commenced in 1987, despite the directions of HC passed in 1988 to pass an award within four months. Further, while allowing acquisition of land that the Govt. finds necessary to be set aside, it need not be held that the land revert to appellants, as the alternative of permitting the Govt. to keep possession provided it re-acquires the land with a new s. 4 notification exists. Furthermore, said option, does the least violence to the intent and content of the Act, as it upholds s. 11A even in cases of acquisition u/s. 17 while preserving the requirement of s. 17 that the unencumbered possession of the land remain vested in Govt. Furthermore, it also protects rights of the landowners, thus fulfilling intent of s. 11A, while allowing Govt. to acquire land in cases of emergencies without its title being challenged, which is the intention of s.17. Hence, acquisition dt.18-11-1987 is set aside for non-compliance with the provisions of s.11A of the Act. Further, as all subsequent notifications by respondent-State were lapsed, State is directed to issue a fresh s. 4 Notification and State is restrained from contending that the land is no longer required by it or that it should revert to appellants and appeal allowed to such extent. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Once land has vested in the State, the question of re-vesting its possession in the erstwhile landowners is no longer available as an option to the State.
