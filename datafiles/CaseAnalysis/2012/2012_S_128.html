Ramnaresh & Ors v State Of Chhattisgarh

Supreme Court of India

28 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 81; (2012) 4 SCC 257; AIR 2012 SC 1357; 2012 CRLJ 1898; JT 2012 (2) SC 588; 2012 (2) MLJ(Crl) 683; 2012(3) SCALE 234; [2012] 3 S.C.R. 630

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Approver

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376, 377, 302 - Rape - Murder - Conviction - Death sentence - Sustainability - Appellant accused allegedly raped and murdered deceased minor - Trial Court convicted appellant u/ss. 499, 376(2)(g), and 320 IPC on the basis of sole eye witness PW6 and sentenced him to death - Appellant appealed to HC which was dismissed by impugned order - Hence instant appeal - (A) Whether prosecution was able to establish involvement of appellants in alleged crime beyond reasonable doubt - Held, statement of PW6 does not suffer from any contradictions nor is at variance with case of prosecution, expert evidence clearly demonstrates, particularly in view of injuries caused to deceased during heinous crime, that it could not have been done by a single person and, therefore, involvement of two or more persons is most probable and in line with story of prosecution, cumulative effect of oral/documentary and expert evidence was that prosecution had been able to prove its case beyond any reasonable doubt - (B) Whether instant case was a case of rarest of rare cases where awarding death sentence was justified - Held, accused are guilty of offences for which they were charged, it is correct that possibility of their being reformed cannot be ruled out - Court had to consider various parameters and balance mitigating circumstances against need for imposition of capital punishment - Factors to be considered could be different than mitigating circumstances - It appears that age of accused, possibility of death of deceased occurring accidently and possibility of accused reforming themselves, they cannot be termed as 'social menace', it is unfortunate but a hard fact that all these accused have committed a heinous and inhumane crime for satisfaction of their lust, but it cannot be held with certainty that this case falls in 'rarest of rare' cases, accordingly commuted sentence of death to that for life imprisonment (21 years) - Appeals partially allowed.
