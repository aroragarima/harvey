 In Re : Special Reference No. 1 of 2004 (Under Article 143 (1) of the Constitution of India) “ In Re: The Punjab Termination of Agreement Act, 2004”

Supreme Court of India

10 November 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Anil R. Dave, Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Shiva Kirti Singh, Adarsh Kumar Goel, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 820; AIR 2016 SC 5145; 2016 (8) MLJ 271; 2016(10) SCALE 623

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Water

Keywords: Jurisdiction, Legislative Power, Punjab Reorganisation Act, 1966, Inter-State Water Disputes Act, 1956, Constitution of India, 1950, Supreme Court Rules, 2013, Punjab Satluj Yamuna Link Canal Land (Transfer of Proprietary Rights) Bill, 2016, Punjab Satluj Yamuna Link Canal Land (Transfer of Proprietary Rights) Act, 2016, Punjab Termination of Agreement Act, 2004

Summary: Water - Constitution of India, 1950, art.143(1) - Inter-State Water Disputes Act, 1956 - Punjab Reorganization Act, 1966 - Punjab Termination of Agreement Act, 2004 - Unilateral termination of Agreement - State of Punjab enacted 2004 Act with an intention to terminate the Agreement dt.31-12-1981 and all other Agreements relating to sharing of waters of rivers Ravi and Beas - Further, it was also intended to discharge the Govt. from the obligations arising under said Agreement and to nullify decrees of the Court pronounced in related matters - Hence, instant reference was preferred by President of India for advisory opinion u/art.143(1) of the Constitution in relation to enactment of 2004 Act by State of Punjab and whether it had validly terminated the Agreement dt.31-12-1981 and other agreements relating to Ravi-Beas waters so as to discharge it from the obligations which it had to discharge under certain valid orders passed by appropriate authorities.

Held, when after considering the relevant provisions, even with regard to s.78 of 1966 Act, Tribunal had taken a judicial decision, said decision is sought to be disturbed by virtue of enactment of 2004 Act. Furthermore, agreement dt.31-12-1981 is about sharing of waters of Ravi and Beas rivers but said agreement could not have been unilaterally terminated by one of the parties to the Agreement by exercising its legislative power and if any party or any State does so, such unilateral action of a particular State has to be declared contrary to Constitution as well as the provisions of 1956 Act. Hence, as one State, which is a party to the litigation or an Agreement, could not unilaterally terminate the Agreement or nullify the decree of the highest Court of the country, the State of Punjab could not discharge itself from its obligation which arises from the judgment and decree dt.15-1-2002 and the judgment and order dt.4-1-2004 of SC. Hence, 2004 Act could not be considered to be legal and valid and the State could not absolve itself from its duties or liabilities arising out the Agreement. Order accordingly.

Ratio - As per observations of SC, where a dispute between two States has already been adjudicated upon by SC, which it is empowered to deal with, any unilateral law enacted by one of the parties that results in overturning the final judgment is bad not because it is affected by the principles of res judicata but because it infringes the doctrine of separation of powers and rule of law, as by such law, the legislature has clearly usurped the judicial power.
