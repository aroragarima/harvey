State Of U. P. & Ors v Gobardhan Lal, D.K. Shukla & Ors

Supreme Court of India

23 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 230; (2004) 11 SCC 402; AIR 2004 SC 2165; 2004 ALJ 1433; 2004 (5) AWC 4228; 2004 (3) CLR 78; 2004 (3) ESC 402; 2004 (101) FLR 586; JT 2004 (5) SC 454; 2004 (3) LLJ 749; 2004 (4) LLN 24; 2004(3) SCALE 574; 2004 (3) SLR 239; 2004 (3) SLT 210; 2004 (3) Supreme 92; 2004 (3) UPLBEC 2225

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Disputed questions of facts - Conditions of service or rights, which are personal to parties concerned, are to be governed by rules as also inbuilt powers of supervision and control in hierarchy of administration of State or any Authority as well as basic concepts and well-recognised powers and jurisdiction inherent in various authorities in hierarchy - No scope for HC to generalise situations based on its own appreciation and understanding of prevailing circumstances as disclosed from some write ups in journals or newspaper reports - To seek relief even in relation to an order of transfer or appointment or promotion or any order passed in disciplinary proceedings on certain well-settled and recognized grounds or reasons, when properly approached and sought to be vindicated in manner known to and in accordance with law open to aggreived - An impression that Courts are attempting to take over reigns of executive administration should be avoided with utmost care and seriously and zealously courts endeavour to safeguard rights of parties - Appeals allowed.
