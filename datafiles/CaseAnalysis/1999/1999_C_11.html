Supreme Court of India

15 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, K. Venkataswami, S.B. Majmudar, Sujata V. Manohar, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1082; (1999) 4 SCC 274; AIR 1999 SC 1359; 1999 (2) AWC 1614; JT 1999 (3) SC 72; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 659

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (1) Representation of People Act, 1951-Section 81 (3)-Conduct of Election Rules, 1961-Rule 94A and Form 25-Election petition-Requirements-Supply of 'true copy' of Election petition to respondent-Defect in 'true copy'-Wide observations made in Dr. Shipra v. Shanti Lal Khoiwal, JT 1996 (4) SC 67-Made in context of facts of that case only-Judgment in said case-Confined to 'fact situation' obtaining in that case-Has no application to established facts of present case.

(2) Representation of People Act, 1951-Sections 81 (3) and 86 (1)-Conduct of Election Rules, 1961-Rule 94A and Form 25- Election petition-Service of 'true copy' on respondent-Word 'copy' in Section 81 (3)-Does not mean absolutely exact copy-But means that copy shall be so true that nobody can by any possibility misunderstand it-Object of serving 'true copy' of election petition and affidavit filed in support of allegations of corrupt practice on respondent-Is to enable respondent to understand charge against him so that he can effectively meet same in written statement and prepare his defence-Requirement is of substance and not of form-Hence, word 'copy' in Section 81 (3)-Means copy which is substantially so-And which does not contain any material or substantial variation of vital nature-Copy of affidavit served on appellant-Containing endorsement that affidavit duly signed, verified and affirmed by election petitioner before Notary-Below endorsement of attestation also mentioned âSd. Notaryâ-But omission to mention name and particulars of Notary and his stamp and Seal-Omission inconsequential-Not of vital nature -By no stretch of imagination can it be said that appellant could have been misled by absence of name and seal or stamp of Notary on copy of affidavit-Hence there was substantial compliance with requirements of Section 81 (3) read with proviso to Section 81 (1) (3) (c)- Principle of substantial compliance with Section 86 (1) read with Section 81 (3) applicable-No failure on part of election petitioners to comply with last part of Section 81 (3)-Section 86 (1) not attracted-Election petition not liable to be dismissed-Single Judge of High Court justified in his view-No merit in appeal.
