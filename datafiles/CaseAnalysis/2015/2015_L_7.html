Chauharya Tripathi and others v L.I.C. of India and others

Supreme Court of India

11 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 349; (2015) 7 SCC 263; AIR 2015 SC 2275; 2015 (3) AWC 2263; 2015 (146) FLR 4; 2016 (1) MahLJ 164; 2016 (1) MPLJ 65; 2015 (4) RLW 3472; 2015(3) SCALE 480; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 186

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2(s) - Review of decision - Judgment declared per incuriam - Determination - Management of respondent corporation imposed penalty of reduction of salary of employees, i.e. Development Offices by three steps in their time scale of pay after conducting enquiry in disciplinary proceeding in respect of certain charges levelled against them and finding them guilty of charges that was levelled against officers was that they had claimed inflated incentive bonus to which they were not entitled to - Respondent corporation contended before Tribunal that proceeding before it was not maintainable as Development Officers could not be put in compartment of workmen under Act - Tribunal negatived plea of maintainability and answered other issues in favour of Development Officers and resultantly, it directed restitution of pay-scale and payment of arrears that was due to them - Further, HC relying on earlier judgment held that development officers were not workmen, hence, Tribunal had no jurisdiction to entertain lis and consequently, it unsettled award passed by Tribunal - Further, review petition was filed At stating, inter alia, that order warranted review in view of subsequent pronouncement of HC in other case passed in 2008 which was rejected by HC - Hence, present appeal by special leave.

Held, SC held that decision in question passed in 2008 cannot be regarded as precedent for proposition that Development Officer in LIC is 'workman'. Further, said judgment has been rendered in ignorance of ratio laid down by Constitution Bench in other case, hence, SC is compelled to hold that pronouncement 2008 decision is per incuriam. Hence, SC held that development officers working in LIC are not 'workmen' u/s. 2(s) of Act and accordingly order passed by HC is confirmed. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - Once judgment has been declared per incuriam, it does not have precedential value.
