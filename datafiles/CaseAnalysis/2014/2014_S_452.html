Krishnan @ Ramasamy and others v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 941; (2014) 12 SCC 279; 2014 (3) CGLJ 280; 2014 (3) JCC 1785; 2015 (1) RLW 295; 2014(8) SCALE 135; [2014] 9 S.C.R. 340

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 302, 34, 364, 365 - Murder - Conviction Challenged - Appellants/accused nos. 1-3 along with other three accused were charged for commission of offence of murder of 'X' - Trial Court convicted accused nos. 1-3 for offences u/ss. 364, 302, 201 of IPC, based on last seen evidence - However, Trial Court acquitted accused nos. 4-5 of charges leveled against them and HC in appeal by appellants affirmed conviction and sentence - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, last seen evidence will not complete the chain of circumstances to record the finding that it is consistent only with the hypothesis of the guilt of the accused and, therefore, no conviction on that basis alone can be founded. However, last seen theory comes into play where time-gap between the point of time when the accused and the deceased were seen last alive and the deceased is found dead is so small that possibility of any person other than the accused being the author of the crime becomes impossible. In instant case, there is no other positive material on record to show that the deceased was last seen together with the accused and intervening period of seven days there was nobody in contact with the deceased. Hence, impugned judgments passed by HC as well as Trial Court are set aside and appellants are directed to be released forthwith. Appeal allowed.
