Kanta Kathuria v Manak Chand Surana

Supreme Court of India

16 October 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.M. Sikri, A.N. Ray, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 381; (1969) 3 SCC 268; AIR 1970 SC 694; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 835

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte

Summary: Constitution, arts. 191, 191(1)(a) - CPC, 1908, s.2(7), O. 27, rr. 4, 8B - Rajasthan Legislative Assembly Members (Prevention of Disqualification) Act, 1969, s. 2 - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 82 - (A) Meaning of word 'Office' u/art. 191(1)(a) of Constitution - Appointment of advocate as Special Govt. Pleader to conduct particular case - Question of incurring disqualification - (B) Disqualification - Provision of s. 2 of 1969 Act - Removal of disqualification retrospectively - Effect - Validity - (C) Election - Joinder of parties to petition - Allegation of corrupt practice against elected candidate nd a candidate from another constituency - Held, he does not hold 'Office' so no question of incurring disqualification - Act not invalid on that ground and it does not amend 1951 Act - Petition not bad for non-joinder of another candidate Words ' any other candidate' in s. 82 mean candidates from same election and not candidates at other elections -

Appeal allowed.
