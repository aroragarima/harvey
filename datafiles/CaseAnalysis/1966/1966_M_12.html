State of Madras v Madurai Mills Company, Limited

Supreme Court of India

04 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 148; AIR 1967 SC 681; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 732; 1967 (19) STC 144

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Keywords: Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939

Summary: Sales Tax - Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, s. 12 - Whether order of Board of Revenue was illegal because there was a contravention of rule of limitation laid down by s.12(4)(b) of Madras General Sales Tax Act in as much as order of Board of Revenue was made after a period of 4 years from date on which order of Deputy Commercial Tax Officer was communicated to assessee? - It cannot be said that there was a merger of order of assessment made by Deputy Commercial Tax Officer with order of Deputy Commr. of commercial taxes because question of exemption on value of yarn purchased from outside State of Madras was not subject-matter of revision before Deputy Commr. of commercial taxes - There were two seperate questions raised before Deputy Commr. and Board of Revenue - Doctrine of merger cannot be invoked in circumstances of present case - Appeal dismissed.
