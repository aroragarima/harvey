J. K. (Bombay) Limited v Bharu Matha Mishra and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19973; (2001) SCC (Cr) 393; (2001) 2 SCC 700; AIR 2001 SC 649; 2001 (1) CCR 168; 2001 (2) CLC 130; [2001] 104 Comp Cas 424; 2001 CRLJ 961; 2001 (88) FLR 962; 2001 (1) JCC 95; JT 2001 (2) SC 36; 2001 (1) LLJ 726; 2001 (1) RCR(Criminal) 561; 2001(1) SCALE 232; 2001 (2) SCJ 61; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 439; 2001 (1) SLT 631; 2001 (1) Supreme 147; 2001 (1) UC 323

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Trade

Summary: Corporate - Constitution - Companies Act, 1956, s. 630 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - Whether family members of an employee or an ex-employee of a Co. can be proceeded, convicted and sentenced for commission of an offence u/s. 630 of Companies Act, 1956? - Held, no - Penal law cannot be interpreted to cover such persons who are left out by legislature - In criminal cases liberal construction, with aid of assumption, presumption and implications cannot be resorted to for roping in such persons who are otherwise not intended to be prosecuted or dealt with by criminal court - Amount to violation of fundamental right of personal liberty as enshrined u/art. 21 - Appeal dismissed.
