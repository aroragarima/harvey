Maharashtra State Road Transport Corporation v Babu Goverdhan Regular Motor Service and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 September 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, I.D. Dua, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 491; (1969) 2 SCC 746; AIR 1970 SC 1926; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 319

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Administrative - Motor Vehicles Act 1939 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Road Transport Corporations Act, 1950, ss. 47, 47(1)(e) - Application - Permit - Maintainability - Appellants filed application before Authority for illegal grant of permits to respondent - Aggrieved by said order Respondent filed appeals before Appellate Committee which allowed - Appellants preferred appeal before HC which was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether order of authority was maintainable - Held, s. 47 of the 1950 Act makes it obligatory on a Regional Transport Authority, in considering an application for stage carriage permit, to have regard to the various matters mentioned therein - Further, answer given by appellant appears to be fairly satisfactory and in respect of items 14 and 15, HC view appears to be that over and above number of permits mentioned - Appellant should have given an exhaustive list of other permits held by it in State under item 14 or in any other State, under item 15 - Furthermore, Transports Authorities would be acting within their jurisdiction when they take into account matter governed by s. 47(1)(e) of the 1950 Act in calling upon a party to give more complete details and gave opportunity to other parties before it to state their objections - Thus, HC treating appellant's applications as invalid and excluding them from consideration of Transport Authority was not warranted by provisions of Act - Appeal allowed.
