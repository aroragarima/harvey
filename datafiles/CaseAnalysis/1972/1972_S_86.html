State of Rajasthan and Others v Shri Noor Mohammad

Supreme Court of India

09 August 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

D.G. Palekar, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 368; (1972) 2 SCC 454; AIR 1973 SC 2729; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 841; 1973 UJ 220

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act,1939, ss. 44(3)(b),43(1)(iv)and 44(3)- State Transport Authority - (A) Interpretation of - Contingencies in which State Transport Authority entitled to perform duties of Regional Transport Authority - (B) Interpretation of expression 'Save as otherwise provided by or under this Act' u/s 44(3)(b) - (C) Exercise of powers u/s 44(3) by State Transport Authority - Is subject to directions issued by State Govt. u/s 43(1) - Held, (A) State Transport Authority is entitled to perform duties and exercise functions of Regional Transport Authority (i) where there is no such authority, (ii) when State Transport Authority thinks it fit to perform duties of Regional Transport Authority in respect of any route common to two or more regions or (iii) where State Transport Authority is required by Regional Transport Authority to perform those duties in respect of any route common to two or more regions - (B) Expression in context would mean 'save as otherwise expressly barred by Act' and since there is no express provision in Act which bars performance by State Transport Authority of duties referred to in cl. (b) of s. 44(3), State Transport Authority would not be barred from performing duties u/cl. (b) - (C) State Transport Authority cannot act contrary to it - Appeal dismissed.
