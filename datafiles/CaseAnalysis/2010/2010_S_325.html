03 June 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Punjab Urban Estates (Development and Regulation) Act, 1964, Punjab Urban Estate (Sale of Sites) Rules, 1965

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Punjab Urban Estate (Sale of Sites) Rules, 1965 - Punjab Urban Estates (Development and Regulation) Act, 1964 - Appellant made an application on 23.02.1987 for allotment of a residential plot - Administration, vide letter dated 25.02.1987, issued the allotment letter in favour of the appellant - However, as the proper calculation was not made, appellant was directed to deposit provisional price - Subsequently, additional demand was made - Writ petition challenging said Demand Notice was dismissed - Hence, present appeal - (A) Appellant relied on D.S. Laungia & Anr. v. State of Punjab & Ors. 1992 INDLAW PNH 136 wherein after re-determining the additional price, no recovery has been made from Sh. D.S. Laungia till date - Appellant submitted that said Judgment has attained finality as Special Leave Petition (SLP) against the said Judgment was withdrawn - Whether such an order of withdrawal passed by SC amounts to confirmation/approval of the judgment and order of the HC and as to whether appellant could be treated differently? - Held, an order rejecting the SLP at the threshold without detailed reasons does not constitute any declaration of law or a binding precedent - It simply means that SC did not consider the case worth examining for the reason, which may be other than merit of the case - Nor such an order of SC operates as res judicata - Further, parity cannot be claimed with D.S. Laungia in view of the settled legal proposition that art. 14 of the Constitution of India does not envisages for negative equality - A wrong order/decision in favour of any particular party does not entitle any other party to claim the benefits on the basis of the wrong decision - Thus, even if some other similarly situated persons have been granted some benefit inadvertently or by mistake, such order does not confer any legal right on the petitioner to get the same relief - (B) Appellant submitted that HC committed an error in dismissing the said Writ Petition relying upon the Judgment of SC in Preeta Singh & Ors. vs. Haryana Urban Development Authority & Ors. 1996 INDLAW SC 2833 - Whether order passed by HC justified? - Held, in Preeta Singh it was held that allottee is liable to pay a sale price including the additional price and the cost incurred and also the cost of improvement of the sites - Present case is squarely covered by the said judgment - (C) Allotment - Legality - Held, allotment had been made to the appellant within 48 hours of submission of her application though in ordinary cases, it takes about a year - Appellant had further been favoured to pay the provisional price of Rs. 93,000/- in four installments in two years - Making the allotment in such a hasty manner itself is arbitrary and unreasonable and is hit by art. 14 of the Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
