Collector of Central Excise v Messrs Ponds India Limited

Supreme Court of India

19 October 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 272; (1989) 4 SCC 759; (1990) SCC (Tax) 144; AIR 1990 SC 374; 1989 (25) ECR 457; 1989 (44) E.L.T. 185; JT 1989 (4) SC 137; 1989(2) SCALE 849; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 479; [1989] SUPP1 S.C.R. 479; 1990 (1) UJ 241

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Packing material - Inclusion of value of, in value of goods - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 4(4)(d)(i) - Respondent manufacturer of talcum powder - Respondent claimed deduction of cost of packing for transportation in respect of small packings - Asst. Collector came to the conclusion that the amount as claimed by the respondent was not deductible in respect of post-manufacturing expenses - Relying on judgment of Godfrey Philips case (1985 Indlaw SC 275) Tribunal held that cost of outer or bigger carton in which small cartons containing powder tins are packed, as not includible in assessable value - Hence, present appeal - S. 4(4)(d)(i) of the Act lays down that where goods are delivered at factory gate in a packed condition, cost of packing should be included in the assessable value - Held, cost of that much of packings, be they primary or secondary, which are required to make articles marketable would be includible in value - Tribunal was in error in approaching problem as question is not whether these goods could be so sold, but question is whether these goods are so sold usually and as such used to become marketable in such manner - Matter remanded back.
