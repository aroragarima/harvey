Babu v State Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

11 August 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 630; (2010) 9 SCC 189; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 3342; 2011 (1) CGLJ 457; 2010 (4) JCC 2657; JT 2010 (8) SC 560; 2010 (4) MLJ(Crl) 365; 2011 (5) RCR(Criminal) 113; 2010(8) SCALE 185; [2010] 9 S.C.R. 1039; 2010 (3) UC 1464

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Cyanide

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant was charge sheeted for murdering his wife by poisoning her - Prosecution's case was that the appellant had persuaded deceased to take Sodium Cyanide under the guise of an ayurvedic medicine - Trial Court dis-believed the prosecution witnesses and acquitted the appellant - On appeal, HC held all the circumstances necessary to record a finding of guilt against the appellant stood proved by the prosecution - HC reversed judgment and order of acquittal - Hence, present appeal - Held, in the case of circumstantial evidence the burden on prosecution is always greater and prosecution has to prove its case beyond reasonable doubt - In the present case, circumstances pointed out by prosecution raised doubts about the prosecution's cases and there was material contradictions in the case of the prosecution - Also prosecution failed to establish a motive - Hence, HC erred in emphasising that onus to prove his innocence was on the appellant - Judgment and order of the HC set aside - Appellant acquitted - Appeal allowed.
