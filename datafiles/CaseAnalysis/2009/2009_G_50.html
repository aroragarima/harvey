G. Sekar v Geetha & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 485; (2009) 6 SCC 99; AIR 2009 SC 2649; 2009 (75) ALR 814; 2009 (3) AWC 2740; 2009 (6) Bom.C.R. 413; 2011 (4) CGLJ 275; 2009 (4) MLJ(SC) 1237; 2009 (4) MPLJ 112; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 844; 2009 (107) RD 569; 2009(5) SCALE 559; [2009] 5 S.C.R. 1005

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Female Heir, Coparcener, Death Of a Coparcener, Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952, General Rules Of Succession, Punjab Pre-Emption Act, 1913, Black Administration Act, 1927

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Effect of the amendment insofar as s. 23 has been omitted - Held, although omission of a provision operates as an amendment to the statute but then s. 6 of the General Clauses Act, could have been applied provided it takes away somebody's vested right - Restrictive right contained in s. 23 cannot be held to remain continuing despite the 2005 Act - Not a fit case where discretionary jurisdiction under art. 136 of the Constitution of India should be exercised as the fact remains that s. 23 as it stood was to be applicable on the date of the institution of the suit - Concurrent finding that the Will was not validly proved - Not find any reason to differ - Appeal dismissed.
