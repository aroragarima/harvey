Vinod Sharma and Others v Director of Education (Basic) U. P. and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 March 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Misra, K. Venkataswami, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1561; (1998) 3 SCC 404; (1998) SCC (L&S) 892; 1998 (2) AWC 1286; 1998 (2) CLT 24; 1998 (79) FLR 122; JT 1998 (2) SC 572; 1998 (2) LLJ 33; 1998(2) SCALE 407; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 382; 1998 (3) SLJ 198; 1998 (2) SLR 286; 1998 (3) SLT 603; 1998 (3) Supreme 127; 1998 (2) UPLBEC 1082

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Payment of Salary Act, 1978 - Uttar Pradesh Recognised Basic Schools, Recruitment and Conditions of Service of Teachers and Other Employees Rules, 1975, r. 10 - Uttar Pradesh Recognised Basic Schools (Junior High Schools) (Recruitment and Conditions of Service of Teachers) Rules, 1978 - High Court issued direction to respondents to pay salary of appellants under Payment of Salary Act from 29-08-1991 - Grievance is, claim is they should be paid arrears of their salary not from 29-08-1991 but from 01-07-1975 - Grievance that respondents concerned did not bring appellants under Payment of Salary Act though they are entitled since year 1975 - Held, r. 10 casts an obligation to pay salary to a teacher on recognised school and not on State Govt. - State Govt. is obliged only under Payment of Salary Act - Hence, even though a school may be recognised but that by itself does not create any right in appellants to receive their salary under it - However, when grievance of appellants was accepted in first writ petition to bring them in parity with Junior High School teachers, payment from 1991 cannot be construed to be correct on facts of this case - Considering direction issued by HC, in its first judgment, where clear direction is to pay these appellants under Payment of Salary Act as in same institution another set of teachers are being paid under it and institution being one unit, same cannot be denied to teachers in primary sections - Appeal allowed.
