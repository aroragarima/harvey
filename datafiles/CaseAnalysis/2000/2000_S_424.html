Abdul Rashid Ibrahim Mansuri v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

01 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, A.S. Anand, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 705; (2000) SCC (Cr) 496; (2000) 2 SCC 513; AIR 2000 SC 821; 2000 (5) Bom.C.R. 442; 2001 (1) CalLT(SC) 33; 2000 (1) CCR 155; 2000 (1) Crimes 187; 2000 CRLJ 1384; 2000 (2) G.L.R. 1129; 2000 (1) JCC 287; JT 2000 (1) SC 471; 2000 (1) RCR(Criminal) 611; 2000(1) SCALE 361; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 542; 2000 (1) SLT 580; 2000 (1) Supreme 363

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conviction, Sustainability, Possession of Contraband articles

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, s. 20b(ii) - Bombay Prohibition Act, 1949, s. 66(I)(b) - Possession of Contraband articles - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant accused allegedly was in possession of contraband articles(charas) - Accused was charged for offence punishable u/s. 20b(ii) of the 1985 Act and u/s. 66(I)(b) of the 1949 Act - Trial Court acquitted accused - Appeal by State before HC - HC convicted and sentenced accused - Hence instant appeal -

Held, in instant case non-recording of vital information collected by police at the first instance can be counted as circumstance in favour of appellant. Next is that even the information which PW2 recollected from memory is capable of helping the accused because it indicates that real culprits would have utilized services of auto-rickshaw driver to transport the gunny bags and it is not necessary that auto-rickshaw driver should have been told in advance that gunny bags contained such offensive substance. The possibility is just the other way around that the said culprits would not have disclosed that information to auto-rick-shaw driver unless it is shown that he had entered into a criminal conspiracy with the other main culprits to transport the contraband. Prosecution did not adduce any evidence to show any such connivance between appellant and real culprits. There is nothing even to suggest that those culprits and the appellant were close to each other, or even known to each other earlier. Yet another circumstance discernible from the evidence in instant case is that the police had actually arrayed two other persons as the real culprits and made all endeavour to arrest them, but they absconded themselves and escaped from the reach of the police. Thus, accused had discharged the burden of proof in such a manner as to rebut the presumption envisaged in s. 35 of the Act. He is therefore, not liable to be convicted for the offences pitted against him. Hence, conviction and sentence passed on the appellant by HC in the impugned judgment is set aside. Order of acquittal passed in his favour by Trial Court is restored. Appeal allowed.
