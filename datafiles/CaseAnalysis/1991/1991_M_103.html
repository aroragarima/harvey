Moti Lal Chhadami Lal Jain v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

08 April 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, N.M. Kasliwal, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 929; (1991) Supp1 SCC 229; 1991 (94) CTR 195; [1991] 190 ITR 1; JT 1991 (2) SC 256; 1991(1) SCALE 669; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 237; [1991] 56 TAXMAN 4

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Trusts & Associations - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 11 and 24 - Indian Trusts Act, 1882, s. 6 - (A) Whether income from properties directly paid to trust was not assessable in hands of assessee-family? - Assessee leased property to company - Assessee and company entered into an agreement by which Rs. 10,000 out of total rent of Rs. 21,000 was to be paid to College trust - College trust could sue and recover same amount directly from company - Held, this is a mode of recourse adopted to trust and does not alter income of assessee - This is a case of diversion of income - Assessee liable to tax on entire rental income - (B) Whether on a proper construction of trust deeds executed in 1947 and 1960, sum of Rs. 10,000 is income of assessee and not of college trust? - On construction of trust deeds, income is that of trust and not of assessee - Language of deeds and conduct of parties make it clear that assessee's ownership and right to enjoy properties gets restricted on execution of such a trust deed - Over-riding title created - Further held, income from properties of trust to be utilised for trust purposes only and no longer held by assessee as absolute owner - Trust deeds construed as valid trust - Effect - Income diverted and ceased to be income of assessee - Appeals partly allowed.
