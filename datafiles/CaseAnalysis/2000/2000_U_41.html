Uttam Singh Dugal & Co.Ltd. v Unied Bank Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

08 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 404; (2000) 7 SCC 120; AIR 2000 SC 2740; [2000] 102 Comp Cas 118; JT 2000 (9) SC 78; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 71; 2001 (128) PLR 694; 2000 (4) RCR(Civil) 89; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 187

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908, O. 12, r. 6 - Specific Relief Act, 1877 - Civil Procedure - Company - Suit for recovery against - Minutes , letter and pleadings considered as admission - Decree - Judgment on 'admissions' - Party making plain admission entitling the other party to succeed - (A) Applicability of r. 6 of O.12 Civil Code - (B) Decree challenged on the ground that resolution or minutes of Meeting or the letter could not be said to be statements not in course of pleadings or otherwise - Rule held, applies to such case , it also applies where there is clear admission of facts in face of which it is impossible for party making such admission to succeed - Could not be challenged as pleadings raised in the case in the shape of the applications under that Rule and the answering affidavit clearly reiterated the admission - Appeal dismissed.
