Vasanthi v Venugopal (Dead) Through Lrs

Supreme Court of India

21 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Amitava Roy, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 209; (2017) 4 SCC 723; AIR 2017 SC 1569; 2017 (4) ALD(SC) 20; 2017(3) ALL MR 461; 2017 (122) ALR 538; 2017 (3) Bom.C.R. 391; 2017 (2) CLT(SC) 177; 2017 (2) RCR(Civil) 538; 2017(3) SCALE 674

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Immovable Property, Agreement Of Sale, Part Performance, Contract, Suit For Specific Performance, Specific Relief Act, 1963, Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, Limitation, Letters Patent, Appellate Decree, Enforcing, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Transfer of Property, 1882, s. 53A - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 16 - Bonafide Purchase of Suit Property - Order Challenged - Appellant/ plaintiff purchased suit property from 'X' and respondent/defendant was tenant in suit property - Rent Control Court dismissed eviction petition of plaintiff on ground that possession by tenant was protected u/s. 53A of 1882 Act - Single Judge of HC dismissed appeal of plaintiff and DB of HC dismissed LPA of plaintiff for want of maintainability - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, the protection of a prospective purchaser/transferee of his possession of the property involved, is available subject to the following prerequisites:(a) there is a contract in writing by the transferor for transfer for consideration of any immovable property signed by him or on his behalf, from which the terms necessary to constitute the transfer can be ascertained with reasonable certainty (b) the transferee has, in part performance of the contract, taken possession of the property or any part thereof, or the transferee, being already in possession, continues in possession in part performance of the contract (c) the transferee has done some act in furtherance of the contract and has performed or is willing to perform his part of the contract. Further, s. 16 of 1963 Act, specific performance of a contract cannot be enforced in favour of a person who, inter alia, fails to aver and prove that he has performed or has always been ready and willing to perform the essential terms of the contract which are to be performed by him unless prevented or waived by the other party thereto. In instant case, the original defendant or the present respondents that his/their performance of the contract had been either prevented or waived by either the vendors of the appellant or their predecessor-in-interest at any point of time. In other words, though the LPA preferred by the appellant is not maintainable in law, the respondents are not entitled to the benefit of the protection of s. 53A of 1881 Act r/w s. 16 of 1963 Act. Hence, order passed by Single Judge of HC affirming order of Rent Control Court is set aside and suit is decreed as prayed for. However, appeal against order of DB of HC is dismissed. Appeals disposed of.
