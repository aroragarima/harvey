Bhaskar Laxman Jadhav and others v Karamveer Kakasaheb Wagh Education Society and others

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 528; (2013) 11 SCC 531; AIR 2013 SC 523; 2013 (96) ALR 281; JT 2012 (12) SC 372; 2012(12) SCALE 87; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 767

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Practice & Procedure - Land & Property - Bombay Public Trusts Act, 1950, s. 36 - Sale of Trust Land - Public Notice - Sanction - Remand - Sustainability - Trust wanted to sell trust land by calling for tenders from public at large, pursuant to resolution public notice was issued and petitioner was highest bidder who entered into agreement of sale for trust land - As required u/s. 36 of Act, trustees moved application for sanction before Charity Commissioner to sell trust land - Joint Charity Commissioner granted sanction to trustees on condition that sale transaction should be completed within 1 year - Petitioner mutually agreed to extend time for completing sale transaction and moved application before JCC to extend time - Due to difference between trustees and petitioners application was moved for revised permission - JCC, passed ordered rejecting both application, which attained finality as it was not challenged - Even after order, trustees and petitioners continued to discuss and eventually entered into third agreement - Trustees issued public notice for sale of trust land and respondent No. 1 gave highest bid, meanwhile trustees filed application for extension of time, JCC dismissed - Aggrieved petitioner appealed before HC, which ordered that second application for extension of time be remanded to JCC for fresh hearing on merits - Respondent No. 1 and other interested purchasers filed application for impleadment -JCC heard all applications and rejected them - Aggrieved respondent No. 1 preferred writ petition before HC, challenging order passed by JCC, along with alternative prayer that JCC, be directed to consider bid of respondent No. 1 for sale of trust land - HC, passed order remanding and directing CC to decide to whom land should be sold depending on highest bid - Hence instant petition - Whether HC, was justified in remanding and directing CC, to decide purchaser - Held, s. 36 of Act clearly provides that trustees may be allowed by CC to dispose of immoveable property of trust with regard being had to interest, benefit or protection of trust, it could not be doubted that interest of trust would be in getting maximum for its immoveable property - Following consistent view taken by this Court as well as language of s. 36 of Act, SC, had no hesitation in concluding that only course available to HC was to mould relief and direct CC to have a re-look at all bids received pursuant to public notice - It was quite clear that due to passage of time, mainly because of flip-flop of trustees and petitioners, value of Trust land had increased considerably, in these circumstances, it would be in best interest of trust if maximum price was available for Trust land from open market - While this may or may not have been a consideration before HC, it was certainly consideration before SC, for not interfering with order passed by HC, even though it may have, in a loose sense, over-stepped its jurisdiction, hence, SC. declined to grant special leave to appeal to petitioners for suppression of a material fact and directed CC to have a fresh look at sale of Trust land, in accordance with directions of HC - Petition disposed of.
