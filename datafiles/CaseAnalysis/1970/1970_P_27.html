Prem Nath and Others v Commissioner of Income Tax, Delhi and Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

12 August 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 255; (1970) 2 SCC 477; [1970] 78 ITR 319

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Assessment Year, Capital Gains, Compulsory Acquisition of Land and Property, Debenture Holder, Business Income or Loss

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Whether remuneration paid to Karta of assessee Hindu undivided family as being partner of the firm is assessible as income of the assessee Hindu undivided family? - Remuneration received by the coparcener in substance though not in form was but one of the modes of return made to the family because of the investment of the family funds in the business, is income of the Hindu undivided family - A compensation made for the services rendered by the individual coparcener, is income of individual coparcener - Held, income received was for sevices rendered - No real and sufficient connection was noticed between the investment of Hindu undivided family assets and remuneration paid - Appeals allowed.
