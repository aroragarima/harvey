Taparia Tools Limited v Joint Commissioner of Income Tax Special Range- I, Nasik

Supreme Court of India

23 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 404; (2015) 7 SCC 540; [2015] 372 ITR 605; 2015(4) SCALE 67; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 746; [2015] 231 TAXMAN 5

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Mercantile System

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 35D, 36(1)(iii), 43(ii) - Upfront Payment of debenture Interest - Failure to claim deduction in first year - Justification - Assessee issued debentures granting two options to debenture holders as regards payment of interest i.e. periodical payment of interest or upfront payment of interest - Two of debenture holders opted for upfront payment of interest which was paid by assesee to debenture holders in three A/Y's and deduction was claimed by assessee, however, same was disallowed by AO treating it as deferred revenue expenditure to be written off over period of five years - Further, it held that assessee himself given this treatment in books of accounts, viz, spreading it over period of five years in its final accounts - Further, appeal filed before Commissioner, Tribunal and HC is also dismissed confirming order passed by AO - Whether liability of assesee to pay interest upfront to debenture holder is allowable as deduction in first year itself or it is to be spread over period of five years, being life of debentures.

Held, AO clearly erred as he ignored by ignoring terms on which debentures were issued. Debentures were issued providing two methods of payment of interest, however, by allowing only 1/5th of upfront payment actually incurred, though entire amount of interest is actually incurred in very first year, AO, treated both methods of payment at par, which was clearly unsustainable. SC held that AO tampered with terms of issue, which was beyond his domain. Further, SC held that matching concept invoked by HC confirming view of AO is unsustainable . Further, in order to be entitled to have deduction of amount, only aspect which needed examination was as to whether provisions of s. 36(1)(iii) read with s. 43(ii) of Act were satisfied or not and once these are satisfied, there is no question of denying benefit of entire deduction in year in which such amount was actually paid or incurred. Further, there is no concept of deferred revenue expenditure in Act except under specified sections, i.e. where amortization is specifically provided, such as s. 35-D of Act. SC held that assessee did not want spread over of this expenditure over period of five years as in return filed by it, it had claimed entire interest paid upfront as deductible expenditure in same year. In such situation, when that course of action was permissible in law to assessee as it was in consonance with provisions of Act which permit assessee to claim expenditure in year in which it was incurred, merely because different treatment was given in books of accounts cannot be factor which would deprive assessee from claiming entire expenditure as deduction. SC held that entries in books of accounts are not determinative or conclusive and matter is to be examined on touchstone of provisions contained in Act. SC held that there is no estoppel against Statute and Act which enables and entitles assessee to claim entire expenditure in manner it is claimed. Order passed by lower court and authorities is set aside and assessee would be entitled to deduction of entire expenditure in year in which amount was actually paid. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Assessee who is paying upfront debenture interest to their holders are eligible to claim deduction of entire expenditure in year in which amount is paid.
