Union of India and another v National Federation of Blind and others

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjana Prakash Desai, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 899; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 306A

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution

Keywords: Backward Classes, Companies Act, 1956, Societies Registration Act, 1860, Persons With Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection Of Rights And Full Participation) Act, 1995, Cantonments Act, 1924, Persons with Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection of Rights and Full Participation) Rules, 1996, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: (A) Administrative - Constitution - Persons with Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection of Rights and Full Participation) Act, 1995, ss. 2(a), 2(i), 2(j), 2(k), 32, 33, 72, 73 - Reservation computation - Justifiability - Respondent no. 1/society filed writ petition before HC seeking implementation of s. 33 of the Act alleging that appellants/UOI had failed to provide reservation to the blind and low vision persons and they were virtually excluded from the process of recruitment to Govt. posts as stipulated under the Act - HC disposed of petition directing UOI to modify Office Memorandum (OM) dt. 29-12-2005 being inconsistent with the provisions of s. 33 of the Act and issued directions to be complied with by appellants - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the manner of computing 3% reservation for the persons with disabilities as per s. 33 of the Act was justifiable.

Held, in instant case meaning of s. 33 of the Act was that every appropriate Govt. has to appoint a minimum of 3% vacancies in an establishment out of which 1% each should be reserved for persons suffering from blindness and low vision, persons suffering from hearing impairment and persons suffering from locomotor or cerebral palsy. The Act was enacted for protection of the rights of disabled in various spheres like education, training, employment and to remove any discrimination against them in the sharing of development benefits vis-a-vis non-disabled persons. In the light of the legislative aim it as necessary to give purposive interpretation to s. 33 of the Act with a view to achieve legislative intendment of attaining equalization of opportunities for persons with disabilities. The fact that vacancy-based roster was to be maintained does not mean that 3% reservation has to be computed only on the basis of vacancy. As a consequence the percentage of reservation has to be worked out in relation to the number of posts which from the cadre-strength. The concept of 'vacancy' has no relevance in operating the percentage of reservation. Therefore, 3 % reservation for disabled has to be computed on the basis of total strength of the cadre i.e. both identified as well as unidentified posts. Appeal disposed of.

(B) Administrative - Constitution - Persons with Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection of Rights and Full Participation) Act, 1995, ss. 2(a), 2(i), 2(j), 2(k), 32, 33, 72, 73 - Vacancy based reservation - Validity - Whether post based reservation should be adhered to or vacancy based reservation.

Held, computation of reservation for persons with disabilities has to be computed in case of Group A, B, C and D posts in an identical manner viz., "computing 3% reservation on total number of vacancies in the cadre strength" which was the intention of legislature. Further, certain clauses in OM which were contrary to the above reasoning were struck down and direct appropriate Govt. to issue new OM(s) in consistent with the decision rendered by SC. However, reservation for persons with disabilities has nothing to do with the ceiling of 50%. Appellant was directed to issue an appropriate order modifying OM dt. 29-12-2005 and subsequent OMs consistent with SC's Order within 3 months. SC direct "appropriate Govt." to compute number of vacancies available in all the "establishments" and identify the posts for disabled persons within a period of 3 months. Appellant should issue instructions to all depts./public sector undertakings/Govt. companies declaring that non-observance of the scheme of reservation for persons with disabilities should be considered as an act of non-obedience and Nodal Officer in dept./public sector undertakings/Govt. companies, responsible for the proper strict implementation of reservation for person with disabilities, be departmentally proceeded against for the default. Thus, in unequivocal terms, reservation policy stipulated in the Act was vacancy based reservation. Appeal disposed of.
