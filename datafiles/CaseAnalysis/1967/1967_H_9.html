Calcutta Credit Corporation Limited, and Another v Happy Homes Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

23 October 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 492; AIR 1968 SC 471; 1968 (2) MLJ(SC) 42; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 20

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - West Bengal Rent Control (Temporary Provisions) Act, 1950,ss. 12(1)Â© - Statutory tenant - Interest in property - Subletting - Building was leased to a company - According to terms of lease sub-letting of building without previous consent of landlord was prohibited - After expiry of lease period company continued to hold over premises - Later notice was sent to landlord intimating intention to vacate premises - Through another notice landlord was informed that there was no intention to vacate premises and earlier notice was to be treated cancelled - Landlord said notice could not be withdrawn - Company said they were holding premises according to provisions of the 1950 Act and sublet part of premises to respondent - Tribunal passed decree in favour of landlord - HC passed decree in favour of landlord - Division Bench dismissed claims of landlords - Whether respondent was direct tenant of landlord and was entitled to benefits of s. 12(1)(c) of the 1950 Act - Held, statutory tenant merely had personal right to protect his possession and had no interest in premises occupied by him he could not convey interest which he did not possess - Statutory tenant by parting with possession forfeited protection of the 1950 Act and unless the statute expressly provided otherwise, person inducted by him could not claim protection of the Act - S. 12(1)(c) of the 1950 applied only to case were tenant had interest in estate which he could sublet - Appeal allowed.
