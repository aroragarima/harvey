V. K. Mishra and another v State of Uttarakhand and another

Supreme Court of India

28 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 512; (2015) 9 SCC 588; AIR 2015 SC 3043; 2016 (2) CalLT 49; 2015 CRLJ 4021; 2015 (1) JCC 2387; JT 2015 (6) SC 516; 2015 (3) MLJ(Crl) 727; 2016 (1) RLW 258; 2015(8) SCALE 270; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Life Imprisonment, Affair

Summary: Criminal - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, ss. 3, 4 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B, 438A - Alleged dowry demand - Suicide - Conviction - Deceased married appellant accused on 28-6-1997 - Deceased father PW1 stated that before marriage and after engagement, PW1 Had given amount and jwellery to deceased family on three occasions - Further, PW 1 alleged that deceased was tortured for dowry by appellant and his family - Further, on 13-8-1997, deceased was admitted in hospital as suspected case of poisoning and she died due to poisoning - Charges were framed against appellants accused by Trial court u/ss. 304B, 498A IPC and u/ss. 3 and 4 of Act - Conviction order passed by Trial Court is confirmed by HC - Whether conviction order passed by HC against appellant is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, PW1 father of deceased in his evidence clearly stated that one year before marriage he had sold his land and he has stated that he withdrew money from banks three-four months prior to marriage. Further, PW1 has clearly narrated about details of money paid to appellants on engagement ceremony and fixed deposit amount which was matured was also withdrawn and paid to appellants on 11-7-1997. Evidence of PW 1 regarding making payments to appellants is cogent and consistent and is amply strengthened by bank statements. Further, PW 2 brother of deceased that he met deceased at their parental home on 9-8-1997 and that deceased informed him about demand of money and cruelty meted out to her and that he proceeded to house of appellants to settle dispute amicably is quite natural and inspires confidence. PW2 remained consistent throughout his cross-examination and nothing substantial was elicited to discredit his version. Further, SC held that contents of suicide note does not affect consistent version of PW s 1, 2. Further, with respect to inland letter dt. 10-8-1997 purportedly written by person with whom deceased is said to have had love affair, SC held that it is difficult to believe that deceased had preserved same so that it may reach hands of her husband and her in-laws. Further, letter allegedly written by deceased to her brother-in-law, letter was also not recovered during investigation but produced by accused along with bail application. SC held that prosecution has established beyond reasonable doubts that 'soon before her death' deceased was subjected to cruelty and harassment by her husband and her in-laws in connection with demand of dowry. Hence, conviction order passed by lower court is justified and in accordance with law. However, SC held that appellant husband is in custody for more than five years which includes remission and occurrence was of year 1997, interest of justice would be met if life imprisonment awarded to him is reduced to imprisonment for period of ten years. Further, with respect to appellant nos. 1 and 2, each of them have undergone imprisonment of more than one year and are aged about seventy and sixty four years and are said to be suffering from various ailments, hence, life imprisonment imposed on appellants is also reduced to imprisonment of seven years each. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - In case of dowry death, prosecution has to prove beyond reasonable doubts that 'soon before her death' deceased was subjected to cruelty and harassment by her husband and her in-laws in connection with demand of dowry.
