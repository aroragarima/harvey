Smt. Violet Issac And Ors v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

08 February 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 727; (1991) SCC (L&S) 551; (1991) 1 SCC 725; 1991 (16) ATC 491; 1991 (2) AWC 961; 1991 (1) CLR 704; 1991 (44) DLT 513; 1991 (62) FLR 414; JT 1991 (1) SC 337; 1991 (2) LLJ 409; 1991 (2) LLN 76; 1991(1) SCALE 159; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 282; 1991 (1) SLR 735; 1991 (1) UJ 719; 1991 (1) UPLBEC 338

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Railway Family Pension Rules 1964

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Insurance - Railway Family Pension Rules 1964 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - Whether family pension payable under service rules could be bequeathed by means of a Will by deceased employee during his lifetime? - Relied on Jodh Singh v. Union of India 1980( Indlaw SC 193) - Held, what was not payable during lifetime of deceased over which he had no power of disposition could not form part of his estate entitling him to disposed of same by testamentary disposition - Further held, widow of deceased railway employee entitled to receive family pension in accordance with family pension rules and not to brother - Appeal allowed.
