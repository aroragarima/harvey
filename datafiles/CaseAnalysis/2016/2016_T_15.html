Gujarat Urja Vikas Nigam Limited v Tarini Infrastructure Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

05 July 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 486; (2016) 8 SCC 743; AIR 2016 SC 5580; 2017 (3) AWC 3098; 2016 (5) Bom.C.R. 72; 2016 (3) CLT(SC) 23; 2017 (2) G.L.R. 1468; JT 2016 (6) SC 380; 2016 (3) RCR(Civil) 758; [2016] 5 S.C.R. 990

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Tariff, Licensee, Energy, Electricity Act, 2003, Generating Company, Karnataka Electricity Regulatory Commission (Power Procurement from Renewable Sources by Distribution Licensee) Regulations, 2004, Dead, General Clauses Act, 1898, Electricity Regulatory Commissions Act, 1988, Gujarat Electricity Regulatory Commission (multi-year tariff) Regulations, 2016

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Electricity Act, 2003, ss.125, 61, 62, 63, 64, 86, 86(1)(a), 86(1)(b) - Electricity Regulatory Commissions Act, 1988 - General Clauses Act, 1898, ss. 14, 21 - Gujarat Electricity Regulatory Commission (multi-year tariff) Regulations, 2016, regns. 23, 31 - Karnataka Electricity Regulatory Commission (Power Procurement from Renewable Sources by Distribution Licensee) Regulations, 2004, reg. 5.1 - Agreement - Modification of tariff - Sustainability - Respondent no. 1/power producer entered into a PPA (Power Purchase Agreement) with appellant/distribution licensee for sale of electricity from generating stations to extent of contracted quantity till date of commercial operation - Subsequently, respondent set up a biomass based power generation plant and entered into a PPA with appellant - Respondent approached State Regulatory Commission seeking modification of tariff on account of air cooled condenser and sought increase in biomass fuel cost - State Regulatory Commission by its order allowed an increase in tariff on account of air cooled condenser and rejected request of price of biomass fuel cost - On appeal, Appellate Tribunal overruled view taken by State Regulatory Commission on a consideration of the provisions of the 2003 Act and terms and conditions of PPA(s) - Hence, instant appeals - Whether tariff fixed under PPA inviolable and beyond review by State Regulatory Commission which is statutory authority for fixation of tariff under the 2003 Act.

Held, tariff incorporated in PPA between generating company and distribution licensee is the tariff fixed by State Regulatory Commission in exercise of its statutory powers. It is not possible to hold that tariff agreed by and between parties is the result of an act of volition of parties which in no case be altered except by mutual consent. Rather, it is a determination made in exercise of statutory powers which got incorporated in a mutual agreement between two parties involved. Modification of tariff on account of air cooled condensers and denying the same on account of claimed inadequate pricing of biogas fuel is itself contradictory. Therefore, u/s. 86(1)(b) of the 2003 Act Court must lean in favour of flexibility and not read inviolability in terms of PPA insofar as tariff stipulated therein as approved by Commission. Further, Court having due regard to the provisions of ss. 14 and 21 of the 1898 Act. Thus, respondent would not be relieved of its contractual obligations under PPA. Impugned order of Appellate Tribunal upheld. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - If tariff order is subject to periodic review it is difficult to see incorporation of a particular tariff prevailing on date of commissioning of power project would bind power producer for entire duration of plant life.
