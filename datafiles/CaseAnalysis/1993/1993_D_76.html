Dr. Kashinath G. Jalmi and Another v Speaker And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, N.M. Kasliwal, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1048; (1993) 2 SCC 703; AIR 1993 SC 1873; JT 1993 (3) SC 594; 1993(2) SCALE 280; [1993] 2 S.C.R. 820; 1993 (2) UJ 113

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election

Keywords: Legislative Assembly (Disqualification On Grounds Of Defection)

Summary: Election - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 142 and 226, sch. 10, para 6 - (A) Laches - Discretion of High Court - Order of disqualification of some Ministers including Chief Minister on ground of defection passed by Speaker of Assembly - Review of order by Speaker setting aside earlier order of disqualification - Writ petition questioning Speaker's power of review - Alleged usurpation of power by said Ministers continuing - Whether writ petition can be dismissed on ground of laches ? - (B) Constitution, sch 10, para 6 - Order of disqualification made u/ Para 6 - If Speaker has power to review such order - (C) Powers of Supreme Court, under - Order of disqualification of Chief Minister - Order set aside by Speaker in purported exercise of power of review under Tenth Schedule - Writ petition dismissed, as not pressed - Law as regards validity of Tenth Schedule and power of review settled subsequently - Writ petition, whether liable to be revived ? - Fact that petitioner was Chief Minister - If relevant - Held, (A) Cannot be dismissed merely on ground of laches - (B) Speaker has no such power - Order is subject to correction only by judicial review - (C) Liable to be revived for hearing on merits along with interim order of stay - Irrelevant - Appeal allowed.
