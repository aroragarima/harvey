Jugendra Singh v State Of U. P.

Supreme Court of India

29 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 148; (2012) 6 SCC 297; AIR 2012 SC 2254; 2012 CRLJ 3005; 2012 (3) JCC 2218; JT 2012 (5) SC 374; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 698; 2012 (3) RCR(Criminal) 817; 2012(5) SCALE 691; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 193

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 376, 511 - Attempt to Rape - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Prosecution submitted the chargesheet u/ss. 302 and 376 r/w s. 511 of the IPC against appellant - Trial Court held that there were discrepancies and contradictions in the testimony of the witnesses and that prosecution had failed to prove its case beyond reasonable doubt and accordingly acquitted the appellant of the charge - On appeal, HC perused the evidence on record and opined that unnecessary emphasis had been laid on minor discrepancies by the Trial Court and the view expressed by it was perverse and remotely not a plausible one - HC over-turned the judgment of acquittal to that of conviction and sentenced the appellant for the offences u/ss. 302 and 376 r/w s. 511 of IPC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the judgment of the HC withstand the close scrutiny or conviction was recorded because a different view could be taken - Held, Trial Court had given unnecessary importance on minor discrepancies which did not go to the root of the matter and the HC correctly treated the analysis to be perverse - Trial Court proceeded on a wrong footing that the case of the prosecution was that the appellant committed rape on the deceased (child) whereas on a perusal of the FIR, it was clear that the allegation was that the appellant - Thus, view of Trial Court was unreasonable, perverse and on total erroneous appreciation of evidence contrary to the settled principles of law - In the instant case only a singular view was possible that the appellant had made an attempt to commit rape and he was witnessed while he was strangulating the deceased with a shirt - Judgment of HC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
