Vice-Chancellor, Lucknow University Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh v Akhilesh Kumar Khare and another

Supreme Court of India

08 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 612; (2016) 1 SCC 521; AIR 2015 SC 3473; 2015(6) ALL MR 453; 2015 (6) AWC 5562; 2015 (147) FLR 410; JT 2015 (8) SC 152; 2015 (4) LLJ 257; 2015(9) SCALE 625; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 852; 2015 (6) SLR 68; 2015 (4) UPLBEC 2881

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: University, Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, U.P. State Universities Act, 1973

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Casual Workers - Regularization - Justification - In year 1989, appellant Finance Officer of University of engaged respondents as Routine Grade Clerk by oral engagement as daily wagers. Further, office order was issued on 3-8-1990 by authority notifying that daily wagers would not be allowed to continue in any case after 31-12-1990 unless prior written approval was obtained from Vice-Chancellor - However, Finance Officer neither dispensed with respondents daily wagers nor did he obtain written approval from Vice-Chancellor and engagement of respondents came to end w.e.f. 1-1-1991 - Respondent raised industrial dispute against termination and demanded reinstatement in service and back wages - Labour Court held that termination of respondent was illegal and directed reinstatement of respondent no.1 with full back wages - Order passed by lower Court is affirmed by HC - Whether it is justified to reinstate respondent along with back wages.

Held, there is no appointment letter issued to respondents by Registrar on which they were engaged and respondents have based their claim on service certificate issued by Finance Officer. Further, respondents have admittedly not produced any document to show that they were appointed by university against sanctioned posts in accordance with statutory rules. If original appointment was not made following due process of selection as envisaged by relevant rules, respondents cannot seek regularisation. Hence, SC held that Labour Court and High Court, in our view, fell in error in directing regularisation of respondents. Further, with respect to letter of State Govt. regarding absorption of non-teaching posts in University, it is clearly mentioned that if there is any disruption in service of any employee, then services of prior period from said disruption may not be calculated. Perusal of minutes of Sub-Committee constituted by Executive Committee, it is clear that employees who were continuously working in university were only regularised. SC held that respondents have been out of employment from 1-1-1991 and at time of regularization, respondents were not in service, hence, they cannot seek parity with persons absorbed. However, SC held that keeping in view fact that respondents are facing hardship on account of pending litigation for more than two decades and fact that some of respondents are over aged and thus have lost opportunity to get job elsewhere, interest of justice would be met by directing appellant-university to pay compensation of rupees four lakhs to each of respondents. Impugned order passed by HC is modified. Appeals partly allowed.

Ratio - Casual workers do not have any vested right to be regularized against posts unless they are appointed against sanctioned post.
