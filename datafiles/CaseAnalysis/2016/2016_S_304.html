T. Kocha v State of Kerala and others

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 268; AIR 2016 SC 1801; 2016 (4) ALT 48; 2016 (3) AWC 2372; 2016 (149) FLR 603; JT 2016 (4) SC 106; 2016 (2) KLT 452; 2016(4) SCALE 103; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 368

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative

Keywords: SERVICE, Review, Leave, Appeal, Amendment, Entitlement, Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes Orders (Amendment) Act, 1976, Constitution (Scheduled Castes) Order, 1950, Constitution Scheduled Castes (Modification) Order, 1956, Writ Petition, Constitution of India, 1950, Appointment, Constitution (Scheduled Castes) Order (Amendment) Act, 2007

Summary: Administrative - Constitution (Scheduled Castes) Order (Amendment) Act, 2007 - Benefit derived under reserved category - Claimed to belong to Scheduled Community - Determination - Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes Orders (Amendment) Act, 1976 - Appellant claims to belong to a Thandan Community which is Scheduled Caste in State - Further, appellant applied for post of school under reserved category claiming herself to be Scheduled Caste where she was selected and was give appointment - However, respondent no.3 alleged that appellant does not belong to Thandan Community, hence, she was not person belonging to Scheduled Caste Community - Further, order was passed against appellant by respondent no.2 - HC vide impugned order dismissed petition filed by appellant - Hence, present appeal.

Held, appellant was treated as belonging to Thandan Community, hence, belonging to Scheduled caste community on basis of Amendment Act, 1976 and she was appointed as School Assistant on 3-2-1989 treating her as Scheduled Caste. Further, if even assumed that appellant belonged to Ezhuvas/Thiyyas, that is irrelevant as appellant is contained as these castes were treated as part of Thandan Community and were held entitled to be treated as Scheduled Caste. Further, SC held that position changed only w.e.f. 30-8-2007 with Amendment Act, 2007 when Thiyyas and Ezhuvas are not to be treated as part of Thandan, Scheduled Caste but those who have already conferred benefit would entitle to continue such benefit. SC held that since appellants have been continued in service because of interim order passed by SC, they are treated as validly appointed giving them benefit of members of Scheduled Caste category. Impugned order passed by lower Court is set aside and petition for quashing order of respondent nos. 2 and 3 are allowed. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Person belonging to Schedule Community is entitled to receive benefit when such benefits are granted before amendment of statute.
