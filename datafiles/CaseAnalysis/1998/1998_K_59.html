Gurdial Kaur and Others v Kartar Kaur and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1580; (1998) 4 SCC 384; AIR 1998 SC 2861; 1998 (33) ALR 179; 1998 (4) AWC 890; 1998 (2) CLT 35; JT 1998 (3) SC 37; 1998 (89) RD 390; 1998(2) SCALE 649; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 486; 1998 (4) SLT 263; 1998 (3) Supreme 504

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Declaratory Suit, Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Family & Personal - Succession Act, 1925, s. 61 - Succession - Onus of proof to dispel that execution of Will was under suspicious circumstances is on propounder - Appellate Court not accepting validity of execution on grounds that some of natural heirs were disinherited, legatees to will not saying anything about existence of will and doubt as to existent of will - Moreover specific finding that suspicion as to execution was not dispelled - Hence order rejecting valid execution of will upheld - Appeal dismissed.
