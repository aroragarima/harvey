Â Manharlal Bhogilal Shah v State Of Maharashtrra

Supreme Court of India

05 April 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, S.M. Sikri, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 187; (1971) 2 SCC 119; (1971) SCC (Cr) 487; AIR 1971 SC 1511; 1971 CRLJ 1157; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1450; [1971] S.C.R. 359; [1971] Supp S.C.R. 359

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Importation, Sea Customs Act 1878

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860 s. 120(B) - Sea Customs Act, 1878 ss. 187(A), 167(81) - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 14 - Import and Export (Control) Act, 1947 s.5 - Import - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant was carrying business of import of spectable frames, welding glasses etc. - Appellant with object of defrauding Govt. of customs duty payable on certain goods and with view to evade prohibition imposed on import of such goods was party to criminal conspiracy - Appellant was convicted by Trial court and sentenced u/s. 167(81) of 1878 Act and u/s. 120(B) of 1860 Act, r/w s.5 of 1947 Act - On appeal before HC which upheld order of conviction and appeal was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether u/s. 187(A) of 1878 Act is unconstitutional on ground that it is violative of art. 14 of Constitution.

Held, object and purpose of the Act has already been noticed as also its scheme and the relevant provisions and power conferred u/s. 187(A) of 1878 Act has to be exercised for effectuating the object and purpose of the Act. Therefore, any unguided discretion or power has been conferred of the nature which would come within the inhibition of art. 14 of Constitutionand principal contention of the learned counsel for the appellant based on art. 14 of Constitution must fail. Thus, HC has rightly upheld the conviction for the offences in question but taking into consideration every aspect of the matter instant court consider that the sentence of imprisonment already undergone by the appellant together with fine which has been imposed apart from the order relating to the confiscation of goods will serve the ends of justice. Hence, appeal is consequently allowed only to the extent that the sentence of imprisonment for each offence is reduced to one already undergone. Appeal partly allowed.
