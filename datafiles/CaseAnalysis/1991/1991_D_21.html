State Of West Bengal And Ors. Etc. v Debdas Kumar and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Fathima Beevi, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 518; (1991) Supp1 SCC 138; (1991) SCC (L&S) 841; 1991 (17) ATC 261; 1992 (2) CalLT(SC) 34; 1991 (2) CLR 10; 1991 (62) FLR 647; JT 1991 (2) SC 36; 1991 (2) LLN 464; 1991(1) SCALE 271; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 517; 1991 (2) SLR 368; 1991 (2) UJ 30

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - West Bengal Services (Revision of Pay and allowance) Rules, 1970 - Notification - Applicability - Respondents were diploma holder engineers employed in various departments of State Govt. in the post of Operator-cum-Mechanics/Electricians etc. in the scale of pay of Rs.230-425 - Respondents filed writ petitions before HC, claiming that by virtue of sub-para (ii) of Para IV of Notification No. 10303.F dt.19-11-1974, petitioners who were diploma holders in engineering were to be termed as Sub- Assistant Engineers and given benefit of that post and scale of pay of Rs.300-600 - Petitioners contended inter alia that benefit of aforesaid Notification was given to similarly situated persons in other departments of State Govt. and petitioners employed in Agriculture Department were subjected to discriminatory treatment - Single held that petitioners come within purview of Notification for purpose of the pay-scale and allowed petitions - On appeal, DB affirmed judgment while holding that sub-para (ii) of Para IV of Notification dt.19-11-1974 could not be construed to include Operator-cum-Mechanics/Electricians who were holders of diploma in engineering and drawing the scale of pay of Rs. 230-425 - Appeal was dismissed with observation that petitioners should have been admitted to the benefit of scale of pay of Rs.300-600 with higher initial start long before others holding the same position as petitioners have been granted benefits and that great injustice were done to petitioners by keeping them in panel since 1974 without taking any steps for their appointments to post of Sub-Assistant Engineers although others have been appointed to said post in implementation of impugned Notification - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that DB having come to specific conclusion that Notification in question was not applicable to petitioners/respondents, petitions ought to have been dismissed - Further contented that appointment of 17 other persons without considering the case of respondents even if irregular could not be the basis for making Notification applicable to respondents - Held, basis of respondents' claim was that they were diploma holder engineers who were to be designated as Sub-Assistant Engineers for the purpose of revised pay-scale by virtue of Notification dt.19-11-1974 - Appellants admitted that in 1970 pay-scale of Overseers and Sub-Overseers was revised and both Overseers and Sub-Overseers were brought within the scale of Rs.300- 600 - DB had recorded a finding to the effect that Overseers. Estimators and Sub-Overseers were already included in categories of Sub-Assistant Engineers under Sch. I Part-B of 1970 Rules even before same was amended by Notification - It appeared that DB assumed that post of Sub-Assistant Engineers were ultimately a promotional post for Operators-cum-Mechanics through intermediary promotions in intermediary grades - This was incorrect - Under the Rules, post of Sub-Assistant Engineers was not at all a promotional post for any categories of employees in the State, on contrary, it was a direct recruitment post - It was not contested that 17 other employees similarly placed as petitioners/respondents were given benefits of said amended Notifications and were conferred both status of Sub-Assistant Engineers and also the pay-scale thereof for the reason that they were also diploma holder engineers though they were not in pay-scale of Rs.300-600 - This was a concurrent finding that these respondents were discriminated and State Govt. had acted arbitrarily without any rational basis by conferring benefits of Notification to 17 other employees in other departments while denying said benefits to said respondents in Agriculture Dept. - Since injustice was done to respondents by keeping them in panel since 1974 without taking any steps for their appointments as Sub-Assistant Engineers along with others when such appointments were made and assurance made before Court - Fact that in implementation of judgment of Single Judge, Petitioners/respondents were already admitted to benefits of amended Rule, was an additional reason for SC not to interfere with impugned judgment - Appeals dismissed.
