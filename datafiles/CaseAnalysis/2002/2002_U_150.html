Union of India v Shree Gajanan Maharaj Sansthan

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 288; (2002) 5 SCC 44; (2002) SCC (L&S) 627; 2002 (4) ALD(SC) 43; 2002 (93) FLR 1200; JT 2002 (Supp1) SC 94; 2002 (2) LLJ 554; 2002 (3) LLN 855; 2002(4) SCALE 230; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 600; 2002 (3) SLT 572; 2002 (4) Supreme 370

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1982 - amendment of Section 2(c) - definition of "industry"- Held, when enforcement of a provision in a statute is left to the discretion of the Government without laying down any objective standards no writ of mandamus can be issued directing the Government to consider the question whether the provision should be brought into force and when it can do so.
