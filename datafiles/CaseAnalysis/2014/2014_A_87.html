Ashok Debbarma @ Achak Debbarma v State of Tripura

Supreme Court of India

04 March 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 142; (2014) 4 SCC 747; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 1521; 2014 CRLJ 1830; 2014 (2) JCC 1132; JT 2014 (3) SC 644; 2014 (2) MLJ(Crl) 324; 2014 (3) RCR(Criminal) 261; [2014] 4 S.C.R. 287

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Murder, death penalty, Mitigating Circumstance

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 149, 302, 326, 307, 436 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Arms Act, 1959, s. 27(3) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 161, 366(1) - Murder - Death penalty - Sustainability - Appellant (accused no. 11) along with other co-accused allegedly set on fire a number of houses at 'J' village and that people had been shot dead and injured grievously and thereby committed offence u/ss. 148, 149, 302, 326, 307, 436 r/w s. 34 of IPC and u/s. 27(3) of the Act r/w s. 34 of IPC - Appellant was charge sheet for offence u/ss. 326, 436 and 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC and u/s. 27(3) of the Act r/w s. 34 of IPC - Trial Court by impugned judgment convicted appellant to death and Trial Court u/s. 366(1) of CrPC referred the matter to HC for confirmation of death sentence awarded to appellant - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before HC - HC by impugned judgment set aside conviction of appellant u/s. 27(3) of the Act - However, death sentence u/s. 302 of IPC r/w s. 34 of IPC and sentence u/ss. 326, 436 of IPC r/w s. 34 of IPC was sustained - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned death conviction of appellant was sustainable -

Held, in view of oral evidence of PW10 and PW13, Trail Court and HC have rightly appreciated their evidence and the involvement of appellant in the incident, including the fact that he had fired at various people, which led to the killing of relatives of PW10 and PW13 - Since accused persons were known to the witnesses and they were identified by face, the fact that no Test Identification Parade was conducted at the time of investigation, was of no consequence - Whole object behind the Test Identification Parade was really to find whether or not the suspect was the real offender - If the evidence tendered by witness in the witness box was creditworthy and reliable, that evidence could not be rejected merely because a particular statement made by the witness before the Court did not find a place in the statement recorded u/s. 161 of CrPC - Police officer recorded statements of witnesses in an incident where 15 persons lost their lives, 23 houses were set ablaze and large number of persons were injured - PW10 lost his real brother and PW13 lost his daughter as well as his wife and in such a time of grief, they would not be in a normal state of mind to recollect who were all the miscreants and their names - Witnesses might be knowing the persons by face, not their names - Thus, mere fact that they had not named the accused persons in s. 161 of CrPC statement, at that time, that would not be a reason for discarding the oral evidence if their evidence was found to be reliable and creditworthy - Answers given by accused while examining him u/s. 313 of CrPC, fully corroborated the evidence of PW10 and PW13 and hence the offences levelled against the appellant stand proved and Trial Court and HC have rightly found him guilty for the offences u/ss. 326, 436 and 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Participation and involvement of appellant, in instant crime, was proved beyond reasonable doubt - At the time of commission of offence, he was 30 years of age, now 45 - Facts would clearly indicate that he as one of the members of group of extremist persons, waging war against the linguistic group of people in the respondent State - Persons like appellant armed with sophisticated weapons like AK 47, attacked unarmed and defenceless persons, which included women and children - Prosecution has stated that the minority community in the State was often faced with some extremists' attacks and no leniency be shown to such persons, at the peril of innocent people residing in the State - circumstances favoured appellnt in instant case, to hold it as not a rarest of rare case, which are that the appellant alone could not have executed such a crime, which resulted in the death of 15 persons and leaving so many injured and setting ablaze 23 houses, that was the entire elements of the crime could not have been committed by the appellant alone - Further, appellant was a tribal, stated to be a member of extremist group raging war against the minority settlers, apprehending perhaps they might snatch away their livelihood and encroach upon their properties, possibly such frustration and neglect might have led them to take arms, thinking they were being marginalized and ignored by the society -Viewed in that perspective, Court was of view that instant case was not a rarest of rare case for awarding death sentence - Impugned death sentence was altered to that of imprisonment for life, fixed the term of imprisonment as 20 years without remission - Appeals disposed of.
