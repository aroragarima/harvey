Ishwar Chandra and Others v Oriental Insurance Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 319; (2007) 10 SCC 650; 2007 (2) ACC 63; AIR 2007 SC 1445; 2007 (3) ALJ 374; 2007 (67) ALR 316; 2007 (3) ALT 35; 2007 (1) CCC 353; 2007 (2) JCC 1109; 2007 (3) JhrCR(SC) 18; 2007 (4) MLJ(SC) 557; 2007 (146) PLR 70; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 370; 2007(4) SCALE 292; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 720; 2007 (2) UC 1325

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Breach Of Condition, Definition Clause, Cause Of Accident, Intervening Cause

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 166 - Tribunal granted an amount of compensation payable to the respondent at Rs.1, 06, 000/- - Appeal against - Plea that Insurance Company was liable to reimburse the amount of compensation payable by the appellants to the claimant-respondents - Held, driver, did not have a valid licence on the date when the vehicle met with the accident - Appeal dismissed.
