Paul George v State Of N.C.T. Of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

14 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 447; (2008) 4 SCC 185; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 768; 2008 (2) JCC 858; JT 2008 (3) SC 545; 2008 (2) RCR(Criminal) 478; 2008(3) SCALE 614; [2008] 4 S.C.R. 1208

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 279, 304-A - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, any action against a wrongful act by a police officer done under the 'colour of duty' has to be initiated within three months from the date of the act complained off and if this time limit is exceeded, it would bar the suit or prosecution - Case of the appellant would not be covered by Section 140 and that the initiation of proceedings and the prosecution beyond three months from the date of accident was not beyond limitation - Directed that the appellant be released on probation under s. 4 of the Probation of Offenders Act, 1958 on conditions to be imposed by the Trial Court - Appeal disposed of.
