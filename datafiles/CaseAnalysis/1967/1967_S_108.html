Suleman Rehiman Mulani and Another v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

01 December 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, S.M. Sikri, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 147; 1968 ACJ 51; AIR 1968 SC 829; 1968 CRLJ 1013; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 515

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Criminal

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201 and 304A - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 3, 5, 89 and 112 - (A) Whether on facts found by courts below, appellants were properly held to be guilty of all or any of offences for which they have been convicted? - Held, no - To impose criminal liability u/s. 304A, it is necessary that death should have been direct result of a rash and negligent act of accused, and that act must be proximate and efficient cause without intervention of another's negligence - It must be cause causans, it is not enough that it may have been cause sine qua non - (B) Whether driving a vehicle on a public road by a person, who does not know driving and is consequently unable to control vehicle, is a rash and negligent act as contemplated by s. 304A IPC, 1860? - There must be proof that rash or negligent act of accused was proximate cause of death - There must be direct nexus between death of a person and rash or negligent act of accused - Appeal allowed.
