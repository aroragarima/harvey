Suresh Estates Private Limited and Others v Municipal Corp. of Greater Mumbai and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, Lokeshwar Singh Panta, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1493; (2007) 14 SCC 439; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 220; 2007(2) ALL MR 768; 2008 (3) Bom.C.R. 233; JT 2007 (13) SC 630; 2007(14) SCALE 572; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 882

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Land & Property; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Town Planning, Taj Mahal, Building Regulations, Development Control Rules, 1967, Draft Development Control Regulations, 1989

Summary: Land & Property - Contract & Commercial - Administrative - Constitution - Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning Act, 1966, ss. 45(5) & 46 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Environment (Protection)Act, 1986, ss. 3(1), 3(2), 5 & 24 - Additional FSI - Permission - Denial - Validity - Appellants/companies were running construction business, submitted plan to develop land by constructing luxury hotel in terms of the 1967 Rules, claiming additional FSI of 3.73 times in addition to allowed FSI as per r. 10(2) of the 1976 Rules to respondents - Appellants were deemed to had been granted permission u/s. 45(5) of the 1966 Act - Appellants did not get any further communication from respondents - Appellant filed writ petition before DB praying certain reliefs - DB refused to grant reliefs and directed respondent to consider application of appellants - Hence, instant appeal - Whether DB was justified in refusing to grant reliefs claimed by appellants - Held, question of FSI would be subject to discretion to be exercised by competent authority u/r. 10(2) of the 1967, Rules on analysis of objective facts placed before it - However, it was well-settled by catena on reported decisions that discretion vested in authority had to be exercised judiciously - Exercise of discretion should be in consonance with principles incorporated in art. 14 of constitution, so that it did not suffer from vice of arbitrariness - Therefore, assertion made by respondent that it was prerogative of State Govt. to grant additional FST could not be accepted - Further, proviso to s. 45(5) of the 1966 Act, makes it clear that deeming provision would apply only if permission applied for was strictly in conformity with relevant DC regulations - Competent authority had no occasion to consider whether plans submitted by appellants for development of their plot were in accordance with the 1967 Rules - Therefore, appellants were not entitled to a declaration that permission applied for was deemed to had been granted to them as Planning Authority had failed to communicate its decision whether to grant or refuse permission within 60 days from date of receipt of their application - Thus, when statute confers discretionary power to be exercised by authority, Court could not direct to exercise discretion in a particular manner - It could only direct to exercise discretion vested in it in accordance with law - Hence, respondent was directed to exercise discretion vested after taking into consideration relevant material including fact that other hotels were in past granted additional FSI - Appeal partly allowed.
