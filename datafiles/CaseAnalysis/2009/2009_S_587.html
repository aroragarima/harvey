State Of Punjab v Surjit Singh & Anr

Supreme Court of India

22 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1967; (2009) 13 SCC 472; AIR 2009 SC 2439; 2009 (3) JCC (Narcotics) 106; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 117; 2009(7) SCALE 439; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 306

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs & Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, s. 15 - Conviction u/s. 15 - Trial Court convicted respondent u/s. 15 of Act - On appeal, HC acquitted respondent on ground that only official witnesses were examined and no independent witness was examined - Whether acquittal of respondent correct? - Held, no material was brought on record by defence to discredit evidence of official witnesses - Evidence of official witness did not suffer from any infirmity - HC was in error in holding that prosecution version became vulnerable due to non-examination of any person who was not an official witness - Impugned order of HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
