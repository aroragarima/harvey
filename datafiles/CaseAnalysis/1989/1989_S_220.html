Sukhram S/O Ramratan v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 388; (1989) Supp1 SCC 214; (1989) SCC (Cr) 357; AIR 1989 SC 772; 1989 (1) AWC 513; 1989 (2) Crimes 320; 1989 CRLJ 838; 1989 CRLR 207; JT 1989 (1) SC 435; 1989(1) SCALE 66

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 300, 302 and 436 - (A) Setting fire to shed - Loss of two lives - Evidence - Prosecution witnesses's version conflicting with each other - Regarding their perception of flight of culprits who had set fire - Conviction - Sustainability - (B) Conviction u/s. 302 r/w ss. 34 and 436 r/w s. 34 of Code - Co-accused acquitted by High Court, by giving benefit of doubt - Other accused fate to be determined - Held, (A) Conviction of accused could not be sustained - (B) Other accused would also be entitled to acquittal - Appeal allowed.
