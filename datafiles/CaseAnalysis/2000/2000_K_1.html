State Bank of India v Chandra Govindji (Km.)

Supreme Court of India

08 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2793; (2000) 8 SCC 532; 2000 (41) ALR 778; JT 2000 (Supp2) SC 433; 2001 (1) MLJ(SC) 98; 2000 (4) RCR(Civil) 747; 2000 (2) RCR(Rent) 578; 2001 (92) RD 72; 2001 (1) UC 120

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972

Summary: Rent Control - Uttar Pradesh Building Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction Act, 1972, s. 21(8) - Enhancement of Rent - Eviction - Entitlement - Respondent was owner of suit schedule property and tenanted to appellant/Bank - Respondent sought rent to be enhanced from Rs. 300/- to Rs.1,350/- per month and with further renewal on increase of rent @ 25 percent on rent of Rs. 1,350/- - However, said proposal of respondent was not accepted by appellant/Bank - Aggrieved respondent filed suit for eviction and relied on valuation report which assessed market value of building at Rs. 16,50,000/- on basis of said report respondent claimed rent of Rs. 13,750/- per month from 01-01-1986 and filed application for enhancement of rent u/s. 21(8) Proviso I of the Act which was allowed by Rent Control Court fixed rent at Rs. 13,750/- per month - Aggrieved, appellant preferred application before District Judge which was dismissed and affirmed order of Rent Controller - Appellant filed appeal against said order before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by Courts below was justifiable - Held, ascertaining whether appellant had reasonable opportunity to put forward his case or not, one should not ordinarily go beyond date on which adjournment was sought for - However, earlier adjournments, if any, granted would certainly be for reasonable grounds and that aspect need not be once again examined if on date on which adjournment was sought for party concerned had reasonable ground - Thus, mere fact that in past adjournments had been sought for would not be of any materiality - If adjournment had been sought for on flimsy grounds same would had been rejected - Court opined that HC as well as District Judge and Rent Controller have all missed essence of matter - Hence, Court set aside order made by Rent Controller as affirmed by District Judge and HC and remit matter to Rent Controller for fresh consideration from stage when matter was set down on 24-11-1992 and after notice to parties proceed to dispose of matter - Appeal allowed.
