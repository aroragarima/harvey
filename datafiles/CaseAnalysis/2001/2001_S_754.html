Rakesh and Another v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

25 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20101; (2001) 6 SCC 248; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1090; AIR 2001 SC 2521; 2001 (3) CalLT(SC) 79; 2001 (3) CCR 109; 2002 (1) CGLJ 25; 2001 (3) Crimes 330; 2002 (1) CRJ 685; 2001 CRLJ 3511; 2001 (2) JCC 132; JT 2001 (5) SC 639; 2001 (3) KLT 70; 2001 (3) RCR(Criminal) 681; 2001(4) SCALE 522; 2001 (4) SCJ 384; 2001 (5) SLT 431; 2003 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 291; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 1; 2001 (5) Supreme 300; 2001 (2) UC 273

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 319 - Meaning and scope of - Word 'evidence' - For the purpose of s. 319 - (A)Whether cross examination of witness not necessary before his deposition can be relied upon to array another person as an accused u/s. 319? - (1)Whether uncrossed deposition constitutes evidence? - Held, s. 319 does not contemplate an additional stage of first summoning person and giving him an opportunity of cross-examining witness who has deposed against him and thereafter deciding whether such person is to be added as accused or not - Word evidence occurring in sub-s. 1 of s. 319 is used in a comprehensive and broad sense which would also include material collected by investigating officer and material or evidence which comes before court and from which court can prima facie conclude that person not arraigned before it is involved in commission of crime - It is therefore not necessary that for a person to be cross examined in order for his deposition to constitute evidence - Appeal dismissed.
