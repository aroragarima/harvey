Satish Jaggi v State of Chhattisgarh and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 137; (2007) 2 SCC (Cr) 33; (2007) 3 SCC 62; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 631; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 828; 2007 CRLJ 1786; 2007 (1) JCC 689; JT 2007 (3) SC 508; 2007 (2) RCR(Criminal) 116; 2007(3) SCALE 405; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 1070

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Mla, Communal Tension, Transfer Of Case

Summary: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 407 - Transfer petition for transferring of Sessions Trial no.329/2005 pending in the Court of Sessions Judge, Raipur, Chhattisgarh to some other Court dismissed - Appeal against - Held, a judicial officer in whatever capacity he may be functioning has to act with the belief that he is not to be guided by any factor other than to ensure that he shall render a free and fair decision which according to his conscience is the right one on the basis of materials placed before him - To ensure that justice is not only done, but also seen to be done - It will be appropriate if the High Court transfers the case to some other Sessions Court in Raipur itself - Appeal disposed of.
