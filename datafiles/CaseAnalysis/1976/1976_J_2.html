Coal Mines Provident Fund Commissioner Dhanbad & Other v J. Lala & Sons

Supreme Court of India

13 February 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 97; (1976) SCC (L&S) 161; (1976) 1 SCC 964; AIR 1976 SC 676; 1976 (32) FLR 243; 1976 LabIC 482; 1976 (2) LLJ 91; 1976 (1) LLN 259; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 365; 1976 UJ 263

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Mines & Minerals

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Mines & Minerals - Coal Mines Provident Fund and Bonus Schemes Act, 1948 ss. 10F, 78, 78(3) - Delay in contributions - Damages - Appellant had directed respondent/employer to pay provident fund contributions amounting to Rs. 5821.21 and damages for said delay amounting to Rs. 1455.50 u/s. 10F of the Act - Employer prayed that damages at 25 per cent would not be imposed for delay in payment which was not accepted - Aggrieved employer filed application before HC for quashing of demand notice which was allowed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant has to hear employer before making an order requiring to pay damages u/s. 10F of the Act.

Held, provisions contained u/s. 78 of the Act indicate that the appellant may determine the amount due from the employer, and, for this purpose he may conduct such enquiry as he may deem necessary. Therefore, an enquiry is contemplated u/s.78(3) speaks of reasonable opportunity being given to an employer to represent his case and the provisions u/s. 10F of the Act also indicate that determination of damages is not a mechanical process. Thus, when a body or authority has to determine a matter involving rights judicially the principle of natural justice is implied if the decision of that body or authority affects individual rights or interests and in such cases having regard to the particular situation it would be unfair for the body or authority not to have allowed a reasonable opportunity to be heard. Hence, HC was correct in holding that an opportunity should have been given to the employer to be heard before the damages were determined. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Computation of amount of damages should arise upon consideration of facts and circumstances and a mechanical computation of damages is not contemplated.
