Thiru Manickam and Company v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 78; (1977) 1 SCC 199; AIR 1977 SC 518; 1977 (6) CTR 87; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 950; 1977 (39) STC 12; 1977 TAXLR 1621; 1976 UJ 943

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 15(b) - Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Act, 1959, ss. 4 and 38 - Central Sales Tax (Amendment) Act, 1972 - Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Rules, 1959, r. 23 - Madras General Sales Tax Rules, 1959 - Whether appellant is entitled to refund of tax amounting to Rs. 16,769.96 in respect of yarn sold by it in course of inter-State trade? - Amended provision of s. 15(b) of Central Sales Tax Act makes it plain beyond any pale of controversy that tax levied under State Act in respect of declared goods has to be reimbursed to person making sale of those goods in course of inter-State trade or commerce - An amendment which is by way of clarification of an earlier ambiguous provision can be useful aid in construing earlier provision, even though such amendment is not given retrospective effect - Held, appellant entitled to be paid amount of sales tax levied under State Act in respect of goods sold by it in course of inter-State trade provided appellant has paid sales tax under Central Act in respect of those sales - Appeal allowed.
