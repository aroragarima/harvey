P. K. Kalburqi v State of Karnataka and others

Supreme Court of India

02 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1457; (2005) 12 SCC 489

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Mysore Land Acquisition Act, 1961

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Mysore Land Acquisition Act, 1961, s. 17(2) - Karnataka Urban Development Authorities Act, 1987, s. 19(7) - Respondent State acquired appellant's land under the provisions of 1961 Act, however, it failed to take actual possession thereof - Appellant sought release of his land from acquisition - Government (Minister) issued gazette notification u/s. 19(7) of 1987 Act releasing appellant's land from acquisition - Respondent State challenged decision of Government - Single Judge allowed writ petition holding that respondent had taken possession of appellant's land by way of symbolic possession - Appellant's appeal before DB was dismissed thereafter - Whether Government's action of denotifying appellant's land from acquisition was proper - Held, s. 17(2) of Act indicated that power conferred on Deputy Commissioner was enabling in nature, and if any notification was issued by him for taking possession of acquired land it should be evidence of fact that possession was taken, though not conclusive - Such a notification would be a piece of evidence which may establish that possession of lands was in fact taken, however, it was not as if in absence of such a notification Court should not consider other evidence on record which had a bearing on question - HC was right in coming to conclusion that possession of lands was taken by State and there was, therefore, no authority in Government to issue a notification denotifying lands u/s. 19(7) of 1987 Act - Appeal dismissed.
