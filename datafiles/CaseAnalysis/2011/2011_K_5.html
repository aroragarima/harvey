United India Insurance Co. Ltd v K. M. Poonam And Others

Supreme Court of India

18 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 751; 2011 ACJ 917; 2011 (5) ALD(SC) 60; 2011 (86) ALR 725; 2011 (3) AWC 2355; 2011 (3) CGLJ 260; 2011 (4) MLJ(SC) 710; 2011 (162) PLR 477; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 194; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 1026

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Insurance - Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 147(1)(b)(ii), 149 - Motor accident claim - Payment of compensation - Sustainability - Deceased met with an accident and succumbed to injuries - Respondent/Claimant filed petition claiming compensation - Tribunal held, owner of offending vehicle and insurer were liable to pay compensation and awarded Rs. Rs.2,47,000 as compensation - Aggrieved by judgment Insurance Company filed appeals before HC, which were dismissed holding that claimants would be entitled to sum of Rs. 2,75,800/- and rate of interest was reduced from 9% per annum to 7.5% per annum - Hence, instant appeals - Appellant contended that having regard to provisions of s. 149 of the Act, liability if any of Insurance Company for payment of compensation would have to be limited to number of passengers validly permitted to be carried in vehicle covered by insurance policy and did not extend to number of passengers carried in excess of permitted number - Whether persons travelling in vehicle in excess of permitted number was entitled for compensation by insurance company - Held, insurance policy of owner of vehicle covered 6 occupants of vehicle in question including driver, liability of insurer would be confined to 6 persons only, notwithstanding larger number of persons carried in vehicle - Such excess number of persons would have to be treated as third parties, but since no premium had been paid in policy for them, insurer would not be liable to make payment of compensation amount as far as they were concerned - However, liability of Insurance Company to make payment even in respect of persons not covered by insurance policy continues u/s. 149(1) of the Act, as it would be entitled to recover same if it could prove that one of conditions of policy had been breached by owner of vehicle - Hence, any of persons travelling in vehicle in excess of permitted number of 6 passengers, though entitled to be compensated by owner of vehicle, would still be entitled to received compensation amount from insurer, would then recovered it from insured owner of vehicle - In Baljit Kaur's vs 2004 Indlaw SC 57 relied on - Appeals disposed of.
