Employers, Management, M. Colliery Messrs Bccl v Bihar Colliery Kamgar Union

Supreme Court of India

22 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 116; (2005) 3 SCC 331; (2005) SCC (L&S) 412; AIR 2005 SC 2006; 2005 (5) AWC 3911; 2005 (104) FLR 1224; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 100; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 151; JT 2005 (2) SC 444; 2005 (2) LLN 47; 2005(2) SCALE 241; 2005 (2) SLR 688; 2005 (2) UPLBEC 1102

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 11A - Whether Tribunal was justified in interfering with quantum of punishment awarded by Management after coming to conclusion that finding of domestic inquiry was fair and legal?; whether the circumstances recorded by Tribunal would be a sufficient ground to reinstate workmen? - Held, substituting order of dismissal in an act of grave misconduct, withholding of one increment is wholly disproportionate to gravity of misconduct and is unsupportable - Fact that victim did not die is not a mitigating circumstance to reduce sentence of dismissal - Order of dismissal upheld - Appeal allowed.
