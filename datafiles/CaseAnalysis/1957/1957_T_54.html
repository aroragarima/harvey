Supreme Court of India

18 September 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Sudhi Ranjan Das, A.K. Sarkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 80; AIR 1957 SC 882; 1958 ALJ 126; 1958 (2) LLJ 259; 1958 (1) MLJ(SC) 67; 1958 (60) PLR 126; 1958 SCJ 142; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 499

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 311 (2) -

Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Reasonable Opportunity - Bribe - Maintainability - Respondent was Assistant Controller in Commerce Department of Union Govt. - B representative of Calcutta firm styled Messrs. Gattulal Chhaganlal Joshi, came to Delhi with view to get name of firm removed from black list in which it had been placed, and for that purpose, he was contacting officers in Department - Information was given to T assistant in Ministry of Commerce and Industry (Complaints Branch), that B was offering to give bribe for getting order in his favour and immediately reported matter to Special Police Establishment- B however, was willing to pay bribe only after order in his favour had been made and communicated, but he offered that he would get respondent to stand as surety for payment by him - respondent received notice from Secretary to Ministry of Commerce and Industry charging him with aiding and abetting B in offering illegal gratification to T and attempting to induce T to accept gratification offered by B -

On July 28, 1953, Mr. Byrne submitted his report, and there in, he found that charges against respondent had been clearly established - Respondent then filed application in HC for appropriate writ to quash order of dismissal for reason that there was no proper enquiry - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was maintainable - Held, version of respondent that he was not allowed to put any questions to witnesses was not accepted by Court - Evidence of JN showed that only question on which respondent wished this witness to testify was put to him by Br. - Evidence of B and FS was in support of respondent - Findings of Br were based entirely on appreciation of oral evidence taken in presence of respondent - Appeal allowed.
