Tara Chand v Sagarbai @ Chaiyalibai

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 505; (2007) 5 SCC 392; AIR 2007 SC 2059; 2007 (3) CHN 64; JT 2007 (6) SC 534; 2007 (4) MLJ(SC) 521; 2007 (4) MPLJ 18; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 870; 2007(6) SCALE 706; 2007 (7) SCJ 678; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 231

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: Surrender Of Lease

Summary: M.P. Accommodation Control Act, 1961; Transfer of Property Act - Suit for redemption of the mortgage was filed by the respondent - Trial Judge dismissed the suit holding that the respondent being a landlord can obtain possession of the premises in question, only in terms of the provisions of the 1961 Act - High Court revered the judgment - Appeal against - Held, right of a Usufructuary Mortgagor to redeem the mortgage and recover possession is well known, and with a view to enforce the same, a mortgagor may file a suit for redemption or may take recourse to the summary process of deposit and notice under s. 83 of the Transfer of Property Act - There is a clear intention on the part of the mortgagee only to retain his interest in that capacity and not as a tenant - The parties altered their position, A new relationship was created - It was acted upon and in that view of the matter, the High Court was correct in its view - Appeal dismissed.
