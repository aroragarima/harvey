Bhavarlal Labhchand Shah v Kanaiyalal Nathalal Intawala

Supreme Court of India

07 January 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 813; (1986) 1 SCC 571; AIR 1986 SC 600; 1986 (1) Bom.C.R. 446; [1986] 1 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property; Rent Control

Keywords: Testamentary Succession

Summary: Rent Control - Bombay Rent, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, s. 5(11)(c)(ii) - Whether a person occupying a non-residential premises as a tenant after contractual period is over can bequeath his right to occupy property as a tenant under a will in favour of a legatee who is not a member of his family carrying on business, trade or storage with him in said premises at time of his death under provisions of Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947? - Held, right to occupy premises after determination of lease cannot be bequeathed to any person under a will who does not satisfy qualification, referred to in s. 5(11)(c)(ii) of Act - When statute has imposed such a restriction, it is not possible to say that tenant can bequeath right to such tenancy in case of premises let for business, trade or storage in favour of a person not possessing qualification referred to in s. 5(11)(c)(ii) of Act - Even extended meaning given to expression 'tenant' by sub-sec (11) of s. 5 of Act does not authorise disposition of right to tenancy of premises governed by Act under a will - Ordinarily it is only an interest that can be inherited that can be bequeathed - Petition dismissed.
