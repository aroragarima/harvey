M. S. Kazi v Muslim Education Society and others

Supreme Court of India

22 August 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

D.Y. Chandrachud, T.S. Thakur, A.M. Khanwilkar

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 599; (2016) 9 SCC 263; AIR 2016 SC 3917; 2016 (119) ALR 894; 2017 (3) AWC 2409; 2016 (5) CTC 220; 2016 (151) FLR 819; 2017 (2) G.L.R. 930; JT 2016 (8) SC 252; 2016 (3) KLT 887; 2016 (6) MLJ 388; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 80; 2016 (3) WLN(SC) 190

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Teacher, Reduction In Rank, Appeal, EDUCATION, Dismissal, Reduction, Constitution of India, 1950, Gujarat Secondary Education Act 1972

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 227 - Gujarat Secondary Education Act, 1972 - Misconduct - Departmental enquiry - Dismissal - Appellant was employed as an Assistant Teacher in respondent/school - Chargesheet was issued to appellant alleging that he had proceeded on a pilgrimage without prior permission and was absent without sanctioned leave - Departmental enquiry was conducted and appellant was dismissed from service - Appellant filed application before Tribunal challenging impugned order of dismissal which was dismissed - Appellant filed special civil application u/arts. 226 and 227 of Constitution before HC challenging order of Tribunal - Single Judge dismissed writ petition holding that charge of misconduct stood established and there was no illegality in the view taken by Tribunal or in the decision of disciplinary authority - On appeal, DB dismissed appeal - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, lis is between management and a member of its teaching or non-teaching staff, as the case may be. It is for the person aggrieved to pursue his or her remedies before Tribunal. An order of Tribunal is capable of being tested in exercise of power of judicial review u/arts. 226 and 227 of Constitution. When the remedy is invoked, Tribunal is not required to step into arena of conflict for defending its order. Hence, Tribunal is not a necessary party to the proceedings in a special civil application. The lawfulness of punishment imposed upon appellant was a matter for employer to defend against a challenge of illegality in special civil application. Tribunal was not required to defend its order in writ proceedings before Single Judge. Even if HC was to require the production of record before Tribunal, there was no necessity of impleading Tribunal as a party to proceedings. Tribunal not being required in law to defend its own order, proceedings u/arts. 226 and 227 of Constitution were maintainable without Tribunal being impleaded. Therefore, HC was in error in dismissing appeal on ground that it was not maintainable. Thus, impugned judgment and order of DB was set aside and appeal was restored before HC for disposal on merits. Appeals allowed.
