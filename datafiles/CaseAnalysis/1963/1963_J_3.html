Provincial Government of Madras v J. S. Basappa

Supreme Court of India

20 November 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, A.K. Sarkar, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 207; AIR 1964 SC 1873; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 517; 1964 (15) STC 144

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Keywords: Madras General Sales Tax Act 1908 (IX of 1908)

Summary: Sales Tax - Civil Procedure - General Sales Tax Act, 1908 - Limitation Act, s. 18 - Assessment for 1944-45,1945-46 and 1946-47 - Civil suits filed by respondent allowed against assessments - (A)Whether Civil Court had no jurisdiction to try suits against sales tax assessment - Held, unless Civil Court' jurisdiction is completely taken away by statute either expressly or by necessary implication, jurisdiction of Civil Court would not be 'taken and at least where action of authorities is wholly outside law and is not a mere error in exercise of jurisdiction - (B) Whether suits are time barred as decided by Civil Court? - Held, period of limitation prescribed in s. 18 of Limitation Act apply only to suits for damages and compensation and does not apply to kind of suits which were filed by respondent - (C) Whether assessment as a whole must fail or only in respect of part which was outside the jurisdiction of sales tax authorities - Further held, High Court was right in declaring total assessment to be affected by-the portion which was illegal as void - Appeal dismissed.
