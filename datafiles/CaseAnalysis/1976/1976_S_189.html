I. N. Saksena v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

23 January 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 572; (1976) 4 SCC 750; (1977) SCC (L&S) 36; AIR 1976 SC 2250; 1976 LabIC 1446; 1976 (2) LLJ 154; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 237; 1976 SLJ 712; 1976 (1) SLR 624; 1976 UJ 223

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Competence

Summary: M.P. Shasakiya Sevak Anivarya Sevanivritti Ka Vidhimanyata Karan Adhiniyam, 1967, s. 5(b) and 5 (c) - Constitution, arts. 19, 31, 141, 142, 245, 246 and 144, sch. 7, list 2, entry 41 - Validating law - (A) Validity - Tests - (B) Distinction as to 'Legislative' and 'judicial' functions - If 1967 Act encroaches judicial power - (C) Scope of ss. 5(b) and 5(c) of the 1967 Act - Held, tests specified - Not an encroachment on judicial power - Held s.5, particularly cl.(b) and (c), effectively vacate the previous decree of the SC in favour of the Government servant and for removing doubts, these clauses declare that the SC's decree will not be enforceable by initiating proceedings in any Court thereon in figure - Appeal Dismissed.
