Sona Chandi Oal Committee & Ors. v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1060; (2005) 2 SCC 345; AIR 2005 SC 635; 2005 (3) Bom.C.R. 227; 2004(10) SCALE 454; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 971

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Calcutta Municipal Act, 1951, Uttar Pradesh Sheera Niyantran Adhiniyam, 1964, Bombay Money Lenders Act, 1946

Summary: [A.] Socio-Economic - Bombay Money Lenders Act, 1946, ss. 2(5-A), 3, 6(4), 8, 9A - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 265 - Validity of s. 9-A - Whether levy of inspection fee for renewal of money lender's licence ultra vires provisions of Constitution of India? - whether fee of nature impugned is, as a matter of fact, a tax in guise of fee and whether it is so excessive or unreasonable as to loose character of fee? - Held, since Act is a social legislation with intention to protect debtors from malpractices in business State is performing its duties - It cannot be held that fees are either arbitrary or excessive - Fees are ordinarily uniform but absence of uniformity is not sole criterion on which it can be said that levy is in nature of tax - S 9-A not ultra vires provisions of Constitution.
