Patel Engineering Limited v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 379; (2012) 11 SCC 257; AIR 2012 SC 2342; 2012 (5) ALD(SC) 131; 2012 (5) AWC 4481; 2012 (2) CLT(SC) 278; 2012 (3) CTC 757; JT 2012 (5) SC 387

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Practice & Procedure - Capital of Punjab (Development and Regulation) Act, 1952 - Invite for Tender - Highest Bidder - Inability to Confirm - Commercial Viable - Blacklisted - Barred from Future Bidding - Validity - Respondent No. 2 had invited offers for selecting a private entity for development and operation/maintenance of 6 lane road of National Highway-6 on basis of long term Concession Agreement - Petitioner applied and was declared highest bidder and was informed to confirm acceptance within 7 days - Petition expressed its inability to confirm its acceptance on ground that bid was not found not commercially viable - Respondent No. 2 issued show cause notice calling upon petitioner to explain why action debarring (blacklisting) petitioner for 5 years from participating or bidding in future projects should not be taken - Petitioners reply was not considered and order barring petitioner was issued - Petitioner made representation to Ministry of Road, Transport and Highways seeking intervention, but no result - Petitioner approached HC, but SJ and DB upheld decision of Respondent No. 2 - Aggrieved instant petition - Whether Respondent No. 2 was justified in issuing order of barring petitioner from bidding in future projects - Held, SC, did not find any illegality or irrationality in conclusion reached by respondent No. 2 that petitioner was not (commercially) reliable and trustworthy in light of its conduct in context of transaction in question and also because petitioner chose to go back on its offer of paying a premium of Rs. 190.53 crores per annum, after realising that next bidder quoted a much lower amount - reasoning adopted by respondent No. 2 either irrational or perverse, dereliction, such as one indulged in by petitioner, if not handled firmly, was likely to result in recurrence of such activity not only on part of petitioner, but others also, who deal with public bodies, such as respondent No. 2 giving scope for unwholesome practices - No doubt, fact that petitioner was blacklisted by respondent No. 2 was likely to have some adverse effect on its business prospects but as decided Power of judicial review will not be invoked to protect private interest at cost of public interest, or to decide contractual disputes - SC, do not see any reason to interfere with Judgment under Appeal - Petition dismissed.
