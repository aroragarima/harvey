Hindustan Lever and Another v State of Maharashtra and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1081; (2004) 9 SCC 438; AIR 2004 SC 326; [2003] 117 Comp Cas 758; JT 2003 (9) SC 67; 2003(9) SCALE 751; 2003 (1) SCW 6238; 2003 (7) SLT 720; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 685; 2003 (8) Supreme 937

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Companies Act, 1913, Andhra Pradesh Buildings (Lease, Rent & Eviction) Control Act, 1960, Stamp Act, 1891, Maharashtra Tax Laws (Levy, Amendment and Validation) Act, 1997, Bombay Stamp Act, 1958.

Summary: Practice and Procedure - Corporate - Companies Act, 1913, s. 394 - Companies Act, 1956 - Bombay Stamp Act, 1958 -

(A) Legislative competence of state legislature to impose stamp duty on the order of amalgamation passed by a court; - (B) whether an order sanctioning a scheme of amalgamation under s 394 r/w s 391, Companies Act, 1956, is liable to be stamped in accordance with the provisions of the Bombay Stamp Act in its application in the State of Maharashtra - (A) Held, the compromise arrived at between the parties is an instrument which transfers the properties and would fall within the definition of s 2(1), Bombay Stamp Act, which includes every document by which any right or liability is transferred - The State Legislature would have the jurisdiction to levy stamp duty under Entry 44, List III of the seventh Schedule of the constitution and prescribe rates of stamp duty under Entry 63, List II; - s 2(1)(g)(iv) of the Act does not in any way describe any alternate procedure as compared to the one appearing in s 394, Companies Act, 1956 - (B) The question of repugnancy of s 2(g)(iv) of the Act vis-a-vis s 394, Companies Act, 1956, is therefore irrelevant - Appeals Dismissed.
