Rajendra & Anr. v State Of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 430; (2009) 13 SCC 480; AIR 2009 SC 2558; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 2764; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 558; 2009(5) SCALE 423; [2009] 5 S.C.R. 589

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 r/w 34 - Culpable homicide amounting to murder - Common intention - FIR - Statement made therein - Deviation therefrom - Effect - Principle 'falsus in uno, falsus in omnibus' - Conviction by Trial Court - Imprisonment for life awarded - Conviction and sentence upheld by High Court - Conviction and sentence whether proper? - Deceased was a goldsmith - Had taken certain jewellery from one of the accused - Deceased had not returned such jewellery despite repeated requests - Incidence of assaults on deceased by accused - Deceased out of the village most of the times due to apprehension of such assaults - One day accused persons one of whom was 'pradhan' of village came to deceased's house looking for deceased - Deceased's wife wanted an assurance that her husband will not be harmed - Assurance was given by the pradhan - On such assurance deceased came back to the village - Accused persons again visited the deceased's house and demanded the jewellery - Deceased showed helplessness and urged that he will repay soon - Accused persons dragged him, one of them held deceased by his neck - They dragged him to the well nearby and thrown him in it - Death caused by asphyxia - Evidence of eye witnesses and expert witness, perused - Except of one witness who turned out to be hostile, all other witnesses found to be reliable and cogent - Corroboration also met with materials on record - Fear factor of pradhan noted by the Court due to which PW1/widow had deviated from her statement made in FIR - Court observed that it is now well settled that in India the principle 'falsus in uno, falsus in omnibus' has no application - Thus, only because PW1/widow had deviated from her statement made in the FIR in respect of pradhan, her evidence cannot be held to be totally unreliable - Impugned judgement upheld - Appeal dismissed.
