Mrs. Rubi (Chandra) Dutta v M/S. United India Insurance Co. Ltd

Supreme Court of India

18 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Deepak Verma, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 207; (2011) 11 SCC 269; 2011 ACJ 2310; 2011 (86) ALR 734; 2011 (3) AWC 2908; 2011 (2) CPJ(Sc) 19; JT 2011 (3) SC 586; 2011 (7) MLJ(SC) 818; 2011 (163) PLR 79; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 591; 2011(3) SCALE 654; [2011] 3 S.C.R. 977

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Insurance

Keywords: Duplicate Licence

Summary: Consumer Protection - Insurance - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, ss. 12, 15 and 21(b) - Vehicle insurance - Repudiation of claim - Challenged - Appellant, owner of the impugned vehicle took an insurance policy from respondent (Insurance Company) with respect to the impugned vehicle - Said impugned vehicle met with an accident and was massively damaged - Appellant lodged a claim for the same which was not settled by the respondent - Appellant filed a claim before District Forum who allowed said claim and directed the respondent to pay to the appellant a total sum of Rs. 4,00,000/- together with an interest at the rate of 9% - Finding of the District Forum were confirmed by the State Commission - However, National Commission set aside the orders of the District Forum and State Commission and held that the driver of the impugned vehicle at the relevant point of time was not holding a valid driving licence - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether at the relevant point of time, driver of the impugned vehicle was holding a valid driving licence or not? - Held, the copy of the duplicate licence issued by Licensing Authority categorically states that the said duplicate licence was issued to the driver of the impugned vehicle only after 'verification from the original' - There remains no doubt that the said duplicate licence was issued by the Licensing Authority in favour of driver of the impugned vehicle after checking the previous credentials of the driver - Moreover, even if the original application was not available but since the duplicate licence was issued by the same licensing Authority it cannot be challenged that the original licence was fake, forged, manufactured or engineered document - Therefore, at the relevant point of time driver of the impugned vehicle was holding a valid driving licence - Impugned order of the National Commission was set aside and respondent be directed to pay the amount of Rs. 2,72,517/- to the appellant with interest at the rate of 9% per annum - Appeal allowed.
