J. K. International v State (Govt. of Nct of Delhi) and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19897; (2001) 3 SCC 462; (2001) SCC (Cr) 547; AIR 2001 SC 1142; 2001 (1) CCR 267; 2001 (3) CompLJ 23; 2001 CRLJ 1264; 2001 (2) CTC 564; 2001 (90) DLT 157; JT 2001 (3) SC 130; 2001 (1) KLT 870; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 106; 2001(2) SCALE 216; 2001 (1) SCJ 481; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 90; 2001 (2) SLT 377; 2001 (3) Supreme 214; 2001 (1) UC 593

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1963

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 173 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120 B - Complaint of private person in a Criminal Court - Right to be heard - Held, Court is given power to permit private person to submit his written arguments in Court including Sessions Court - If private person submits any such written arguments Court has a duty to consider such arguments before taking a decision - Private person who is permitted to conduct prosecution in Magistrate's Court can engage a counsel to do needful in Court in his behalf - Further amplifies position that if a private person is aggrieved by offence committed against him or against anyone in whom he is interested he can approach Magistrate and seek permission to conduct prosecution by himself - If Court thinks that cause of justice would be served better by granting such permission Court would generally grant such permission - Magistrates' Courts, as right of such private individual to participate in conduct of prosecution in Sessions Court is very much restricted and is made subject to control of Public Prosecutor - If Magistrate finds that there is no sufficient ground for proceeding further informant would certainly be prejudiced because FIR was lodged by him - Though there is no obligation on Magistrate to issue notice to injured person or to a relative of deceased in order to provide him an opportunity to be heard at time of consideration of final report of police, informant who lodged FIR is entitled to a notice from Magistrate - In other instances, injured or any relative of accused can appear before Magistrate at time of consideration of police report if such person otherwise comes to know that Magistrate is going to consider report - If such person appears before Magistrate it is duty of Magistrate to hear him - Single Judge has done wrong to appellant when he closed door of HC before him by saying that HC is going to consider whether criminal proceedings initiated at his behest should be quashed completely and that complainant would not be heard at all even if he wants to be heard - Appeal allowed.
