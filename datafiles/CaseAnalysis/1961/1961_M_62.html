Mohamed Noorullah, Representing The Estate of Late Khan Sahib Mohd. Oomer Sahib v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

18 January 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 355; AIR 1961 SC 1043; [1961] 42 ITR 115; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 179; 1961 (1) SCJ 607; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 515

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Association of persons - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 66 - Whether income-tax assessment of business of 'Spade Clover Beedies' belonging to estate of deceased and carried on as an association of persons is valid? - None of parties wanted to break continuity of business after death of father - Business was put up for sale by auction between co-heirs - Profits of business were assessed to tax in hands of receivers as income of an association of persons - Appellant's contention is that share of profits of each of co-heirs should have been separately taxed - Held, after death of father, his estate was inherited and run by a combination of individuals who had pooled their resources for common purpose of earning income - Business was that of an association of persons, and appellant and other co-heirs were rightly assessed as an association of persons - Further held, income was income of a business which was carried on as a single business by consent of all parties - Mere fact that a suit was pending at time for administration of estate of deceased or for separation of shares of co-heirs does not affect incidence of taxation in this case because business was carried on as one business with unitary control and by consent of parties - High Court was right in holding that income was assessable as an income of an association of persons - Appeal dismissed.
