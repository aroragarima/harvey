Central Bureau of Investigation v A. Ravishankar Prasad and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 662; (2009) 6 SCC 351; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 1063; 2009 CRLJ 3437; 2009 (4) MLJ(Crl) 87; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 466; 2009(7) SCALE 747; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 1025

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Power Of The Court

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420 r/w 120B - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(2) r/w 13(1)(d) - Inherent power of High Court - Exercise thereof - Whether properly exercised or whether the complaint and charge sheet prima-facie had constituted any offence against the respondents so as to sustain criminal proceedings against them? - Bank fraud - As per appellant, respondents have committed serious offences, such as forgery, fabrication of documents and using the said documents as genuine; they entered into conspiracy with the Chairman-cum-Managing Director and other officials of the Indian Bank with the object of cheating the Indian Bank in the matter of recommending/sanctioning/disbursing huge credit facilities in different forms - Filing of charge sheets by appellant; and registering of four cases against among others respondents - Recovery proceedings were pending before DRT; all dues were paid by respondents and thus petitions for recovery were dismissed as settled out of Court - Application u/s. 482 moved before HC - Application allowed and proceedings against respondents were quashed by its order - Court recapitulated certain averments and materials incorporated in the charge-sheet filed against respondents - Court further noted that 92 witnesses have already been examined and trial was at the advanced stage - Court observed that the case under appellant seems to be one of the very few well investigated cases - Serious allegations found - Legal position on s. 482 deliberated upon from ratio of decided English cases and cases of the Court - Facts of instant case found to be very close to the facts in 'Smt. Rumi Dhar v. State of West Bengal & Another JT 2009 (5) SC 321''s case - Charge sheet held to be clearly revealing substantial material on record making a clear case u/s. 120-B r/w 420 IPC against respondents and their connivance with the bank officials - Held, complaint and charge-sheet prima facie constituting offence against respondents, and material on record taken at its face value clearly makes out a case u/s. 120-B r/w 420 IPC - HC found to have been applied wrong ratio of the case of 'Inder Mohan Goswami & Anr. V. State of Uttaranchal & Ors. 2007(12) SCALE 15' in favour of respondents, which led to grave miscarriage of justice - Impugned judgement set aside - Appropriate directions as to conclusion of trial issued - Appeals allowed.
