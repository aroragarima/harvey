Bharvagi Constructions and another v Kothakapu Muthyam Reddy and others

Supreme Court of India

07 September 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Abhay Manohar Sapre, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 666; 2017 (5) ALT(SC) 35; 2017 (5) AWC 4665; JT 2017 (8) SC 586

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Rejection, Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7 r. 11(d) - Award passed - Barred - Challenged - Respondent no. 5 filed suit against plaintiffs for specific performance of agreement of sale in respect of suit land - Lok Adalat passed an award and disposed of suit as having been compromised - Plaintiffs filed suit for declaration that award passed by Lok Adalat was obtained by defendants by playing fraud/misrepresentation on plaintiffs and award be declared null and void - Trial Court rejected plaint holding that remedy of plaintiffs was to challenge the award by filing writ petition before HC - On appeal, HC allowed appeal holding that since suit was founded on the allegations of misrepresentation and fraud, it was capable of being tried on its merits by Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, SC in no uncertain terms, has laid down that challenge to the award of Lok Adalat could be done only by filing a writ petition before HC and that too on very limited grounds. The only remedy available to aggrieved person(respondents/plaintiffs) was to file a writ petition before HC for challenging the award passed by the Lok Adalat. It was then for the writ Court to decide as to whether any ground was made out by writ petitioners for quashing the award and, if so, whether those grounds are sufficient for its quashing. HC was not right in by passing the law laid down by SC on ground that suit could be filed to challenge the award, if the challenge is founded on the allegations of fraud. In SC view, it was not correct approach of HC to deal with the issue in question to which SC do not concur. Thus, when SC has laid down a particular remedy to follow for challenging the award of Lok Adalat then in SC view, the same was required to be followed by the litigant in letter and spirit as provided therein for adjudication of his grievance in the first instance. SC cannot concur with the reasoning and conclusion arrived by HC and the same was set aside. Respondents would be at liberty to challenge the legality and correctness of the award passed by Lok Adalat by filing writ petition before HC. Appeal allowed.
