Â V. N. Sarin v Major Ajit Kumar Poplai

Supreme Court of India

09 August 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, S.M. Sikri, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 450; AIR 1966 SC 432; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 349

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 14(1)(e),14(6) - Partition of property - Eviction - Acquisition by transfer - Bonafide requirement - Premises in question were part of bungalow, which originally belonged to joint Hindu family consisting of respondent No. 2 and his two sons, respondent No. 1 - Three members of undivided Hindu family partitioned their coparcenaries property and as result of said partition, present premises fell to share of respondent No. 1 - Appellant was inducted into premises as tenant by respondent No. 2 before partition at monthly rental - After respondent No. 1 got property by partition, he applied to Rent Controller for eviction of appellant on ground that he required premises bona fide for his own residence and that of his wife and children who are dependent on him - As result of finding recorded against respondent No. 1 u/s. 14(1) (e) of the Act application for appellant's eviction failed - Respondent No. 1 preferred appeal to Rent Control Tribunal which was allowed and eviction of appellant was ordered - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether partition of coparcenaries property among coparceners could be said to be 'acquisition by transfer' within meaning of s. 14(6) of the Act - Held, person who acquired property by partition could fall within scope of provision of s. 14(6) of the Act even though property which he acquired by partition did in sense belong to him before such transfer - Where property belonged to undivided Hindu family and on partition it fell to share of one of coparceners of family, there was no doubt change of landlord of said premises, but said change was not of same character as change which was effected by transfer of premises to which s. 14(6) of the Act - However, when appellant was inducted into premises, it belonged to undivided Hindu family consisting of respondent No. 1, his father and his brother - After partition, instead of undivided Hindu family, respondent No. 1 alone became landlord of premises - Thus, it would be unreasonable to hold that allotment of one parcel of property belonging to undivided Hindu family to individual coparcener as result of partition was acquisition of said property by transfer by said coparcener within meaning of s. 14(6) of the Act- Hence, HC was right in coming to conclusion that s. 14 (6) of the Act did not create bar against institution of application by respondent No. 1 for evicting appellant interpretation of s. 14(6) of the Act - Appeal dismissed.
