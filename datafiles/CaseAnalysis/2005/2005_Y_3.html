M/s. Prem Chand Vijay Kumar v Yash Pal Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 359; (2005) 4 SCC 417; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1153; 2005 (3) BC 198; [2005] 125 Comp Cas 338; 2005 (3) G.L.R. 1884; JT 2005 (5) SC 318; 2005 (2) KLT 914; 2005 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 777; 2005 (4) MahLJ 100; 2005 (141) PLR 713; 2005(4) SCALE 541; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 1029

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: [A.] Banking & Finance - Criminal - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138, 138(b), 142, 142(c) (the Act) - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, s. 482 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 20 - Petition u/s. 482 of CrPC - Whether HC rightly held that proceedings initiated on basis of a complaint alleging infraction of s. 138 of Act was not maintainable for non-compliance with s. 142? - Held, if dishonour of a cheque has once snowballed into a cause of action it is not permissible for a payee to create another cause of action with same Cheque - Following Sadanandan Bhadran (1998 Indlaw SC 1179) - What was kept alive was a fresh right and not cause of action - Appeal dismissed.
