Vishal Agrawal and another v Chhattisgarh State Electricity Board and another

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 56; (2014) 3 SCC 696; AIR 2014 SC 1539; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 746; 2014 CRLJ 1317; 2014 (2) JCC 1056; JT 2014 (2) SC 367; 2014 (1) KLT 1013; 2014 (1) MLJ(Crl) 489; 2014 (1) RCR(Civil) 899; 2014(1) SCALE 660; [2014] 1 S.C.R. 857

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.173 - Electricity Act, 2003, ss. 135, 136, 137, 138 and 151 - Electricity Rules, 2005, r.12 - Offence with regard to Electricity - Cognizance under different laws - Maintainability - Whether the amendment in s.151 of Act which empowered the Court to take cognizance of an offence upon a report made by the police u/s. 173 of CrPC, would be applicable to the pending complaints filed before the amendment - Held, even when a Magistrate was to take cognizance when a complaint was filed before it, that would not mean that no other avenue was opened and the complaint/FIR could not be lodged with the police - It was stated at the cost of repetition that the offences under Act was also to be tried by applying the procedure contained in CrPC - Thus, it could not be said that a complete machinery was provided under 2003 Act as to how such offences were to be dealt with - In view thereof, SC was of the opinion that the respondent's Counsel was right in his submission that if the offence under CrPC was cognizable, provisions of Chapter XII containing s.154 CrPC and onward would become applicable and it would be the duty of the police to register the FIR and investigate into the same - Ss. 135 and 138 of Act only prescribed that certain acts relating to theft of electricity etc. would also be offences - It also enabled certain persons/parties, as mentioned in s.151 of Act, to become complainant in such cases and file complaint before a Court in writing - When such a complaint was filed, the Court would be competent to take cognizance straightway - However, that would not mean that other avenues for investigation into the offence which were available would be excluded - It was more so when no such special procedure for trying the offences under the Act was formulated and the cases under the Act was also to be governed by CrPC - In such backdrop, the notification dt. 8-6-2005 issued by the Central Govt. in exercise of powers u/s. 176 of 2003 Act also required a mention - Vide the notification, Rules were framed and r.12 of Rules, which was relevant, read as under (1) Police should take cognizance of the offence punishable under the Act on a complaint in writing made to the police by the Appropriate Govt. or the Appropriate Commission or any of their officer authorized by them in this regard or a Chief Electrical Inspector or an Electrical Inspector or an authorized officer of Licensee or a Generating Company, as the case might be - (2) Police should investigate the complaint in accordance with the general law applicable to the investigation of any complaint - For the purposes of investigation of the complaint, the police should have at the powers as available under CrPC - (3) Police should after investigation, forward the report along with the complaint filed u/r.12(1) of Rules to the Court for trial under the Act - (4) Notwithstanding anything contained in r.12(1), (2) and (3) of Rules, the complaint for taking cognizance of an offence punishable under the Act might also be filed by the Appropriate Govt. or the Appropriate Commission or any of their officer authorized by them or a Chief Electrical Inspector or an Electrical Inspector or an authorized officer of Licensee or a Generating Company, as the case might be directly in the appropriate Court - (5) Notwithstanding anything contained in CrPC, every special Court might take cognizance of an offence referred to in ss. 135 to 139 of the Act without the accused being committed to it for trial - Abhay Tyagi v. State NCT of Delhi & Anr. 2009 Indlaw DEL 576, relied on - Appeal dismissed.
