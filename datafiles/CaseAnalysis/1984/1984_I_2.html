Â Inderjit Barua and Others v Election Commission of India

Supreme Court of India

28 September 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, Amarendra Nath Sen, V. Balakrishna Eradi, Ranganath Misra, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 368; (1985) 1 SCC 21; AIR 1984 SC 1911; 1984(2) SCALE 441; 1985 UJ 180

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Election - Constitution of India, 1950 - Representation of the People Act, 1950 - Electoral Registration Rules, 1960 - Representation of the People Act, 1951 - Electoral rolls - Revision - Defective - - Invalid - Petitioners filed petitions contended that Election Commission/respondent had erroneously directed electoral authorities in charge of revision of electoral rolls and electoral rolls were invalid and elections held were void - HC dismissed petitions declared that it was not open to anyone to challenge election from any constituencies on ground that electoral rolls were defective and it could not be condemned as invalid - Hence instant petition - Whether respondent could be directed not to hold elections - Held, it was entirely for respondent to decide in exercise of its discretion to carry revision u/r.25 of 1960 Rules - Respondent could not be directed to carry out revision - Only direction could be given to respondent was to carry out revision of electoral rolls in accordance with procedure prescribed in 1950 Act - Hence, could not issue direction to respondent not to hold elections until revision of electoral rolls was completed - Petition dismissed.
