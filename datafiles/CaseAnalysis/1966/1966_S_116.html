Â Badku Joti Savant v State of Mysore

Supreme Court of India

01 March 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.M. Sikri, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 350; AIR 1966 SC 1746; 1966 CRLJ 1353; 1984 ECR 618; 1978 (2) E.L.T. 1323; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 698

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Sea Customs Act, 1878, s. 167(81) - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1947, s. 8(1) - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 25 - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, ss. 6, 8, 9, 13, 14, 18, 19, 21 and 37(2) - Bihar and Orissa Excise Act, 1915, s. 78(3) - (A) Whether view by High Court differing from view taken by Calcutta HC in Sitaram Agarwal's case ((1962) Cr L J 43) with respect to interpretation of s. 167(81) was correct? - Held, Hon'ble Supreme Court in Sachidananda Bannerjee, Asst. Collector of Customs v Sitaram Agarwal and Another has overruled Sitaram Agarwala case and held that s. 167(81) of Sea Customs Act can also take in persons who may not be concerned with actual import of prohibited goods - (B) Whether statement made by appellant to Deputy Superintendent of Customs and Excise was admissible in evidence in view of s. 25 of Indian Evidence Act? - Held, even though Central Excise Officer may have when making enquiries for purposes of Act powers which an officer-in-charge of a police station has when investigating a cognizable offence, he does not thereby become a police officer even if we give broader meaning to those words in s. 25 of Evidence Act - Further held, appellant's conviction is correct - Appeal dismissed.
