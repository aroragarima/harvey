Punjab University v Subash Chander and Another

Supreme Court of India

17 May 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Varadarajan, A.P. Sen, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 323; (1984) 3 SCC 603; AIR 1984 SC 1415; 1984(1) SCALE 925; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 822; 1984 UJ 772

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Punjab University Act

Summary: Education - Punjab University Act, 1961, s. 31(2)(n) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 245 - Grace marks - Retrospective reduction, if - In May 1970 an amendment was made by the University in the form of an exception to Rule 2.1 which corresponds to Rule 7.1 which was in force in 1965 - Full Bench of the High Court is of the view that there is retrospectivity in the change brought about by the addition of the exception to Rule 2.1 of the calender for the year 1970 - Percentage of grace marks reduced by amendment - Held, there is no question of the change in the rule made in the year 1970 having retrospective operation merely because it was applied in 1974 to respondent who had joined the M.B.B.S. course in 1965 when the rule regarding award of grace marks was different - And hence invalid - Appeal allowed.
