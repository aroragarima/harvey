Deepak Rai and another v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, M.Y. Eqbal, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 621; (2013) 10 SCC 421; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 791; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 3694; JT 2013 (12) SC 609; 2013 (4) MLJ(Crl) 171; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 924; 2013(11) SCALE 677

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Criminal Consipracy

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, ss. 120B, 148, 302 r/w s. 149, 307 r/w s. 149, 326, 429, 436, 452 - Criminal conspiracy - Murder - Death sentence - Sustainability - Appellants-A1 to A4 and others allegedly surrounded deceased house and set the house on fire with deceased wife and children who were trapped inside the house - Later deceased was set on fire - Accused were charged for offences punishable u/ss. 147, 148, 149, 452, 342, 324, 326, 427, 436, 307 and 302 IPC - Trial Court convicted and sentenced A1 to A3 to death and acquitted other co-accused - Appellants filed appeal before HC - Appeals were dismissed by HC - Hence instant appeals - Whether offence committed by accused fell into the category of rarest of the rare cases as to warrant death sentence - Held, ruthlessness of accused was reflected through brutal murders of young, innocent children and wife of deceased by burning them alive to avenge their cause being non-withdrawal of FIR filed by deceased for theft of his buffalo against A1 - Role played by A3 during commission of crime established was to hold barrels of kerosene along with one other - It was only A1 who had threatened deceased of burning his house in case FIR against his family and him were not withdrawn - Further, A1 during occurrence not only scripted and instructed rest of unlawful assembly but also lighted matchstick to burn e house as well deceased body - A2, pushed deceased to ground and later fired at him - Possibility of A3 being less culpable than other accused should not be answered in affirmative - Therefore, it was proper to sentence A3 to imprisonment till end of his life would appropriately serve as punishment proportional to degree of offence committed by him - A1 and A2, falls into such category of rarest of the rare cases where culpability had assumed proportion of extreme depravity and accused were perfect example of blood thirsty, scheming and hardened criminals who slayed 6 innocent lives to quench their thirst for revenge and such revenge evolving out of fellow citizens refusal to abstain from resorting to machinery of law to protect his rights - Entire incident was extremely revolting and shocked collective conscience of community - Acts of murder committed by accused were gruesome, merciless and brutal that aggravating circumstances far outweighed mitigating circumstances - A1 and A2 have committed cold blooded murder in pre-ordained fashion without any provocation whatsoever - Motive behind gruesome act was to avenge act of deceased in approaching machinery of law enforcement inspite of threats by accused - Wrath of A1 and A2 was reflected in their act of first gagging deceased thereafter attempting to burn him alive and later when he tried to escape firing at him thereby leaving no stone unturned in translating their threats into reality - Crime was enormous in proportion having wiped off whole family was committed brutally that it pricked and shocked not only judicial conscience but even collective conscience of society - It demanded just punishment from Court and Court was bound to respond within legal parameters - Gravity of offence committed in regard to A1 and A2 instant case falls into category of rarest of the rare cases and was not case where imprisonment for life was adequate sentence and thus, constrained to reach inescapable conclusion that death sentence imposed on A1 and A2 was confirmed and sentence awarded to A3 was commuted to life imprisonment till rest of his life - Appeals disposed of.
