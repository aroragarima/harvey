C. P. Kalra v Air India Through Its Managing Director, Bombay and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 April 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1260; (1994) Supp1 SCC 454; (1994) SCC (L&S) 476; 1994 (27) ATC 70; 1995 (1) LLJ 164; 1993 (7) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Meritorious

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Promotion Policy evolved by Air India - Appellant seeking promotion to the next higher post of Station Superintendent - However on account of the change in the promotion policy in June 1988 he has been kept out from promotion - (A) Whether 40 per cent marks reserved for the interview was excessive? - Promotion had to be on the criteria of merit-cum-seniority - Method of assessing the merit of the candidate cannot be said to be, in any manner, arbitrary or one which has no relevance to the object to be achieved - On the basis of the appraisal reports and the grading given to each candidate, marks are assigned out of 60 marks - Out of the remaining 40 marks, marks are assigned to each candidate on the basis of the performance at the interview - The minimum marks required for being empanelled is 70 percent - Amongst the candidates who have secured 70 per cent and above, a list has to be prepared in the order of their inter se seniority regardless of the marks secured and promotions given according to their placement in the panel - The appellant appeared at the interview and fell short of the minimum requirement of 70 per cent marks for being entered in the list of candidates suitable for promotion to the next higher post - Held a cut-off line has to be drawn for determining merit and in this case it is fixed at 70 per cent - Nothing arbitrary, unfair or irrational about the prescription of the minimum eligibility marks for empanelment - (B) Whether promotion policy was unconstitutional? - The This 40 per cent is divided under different heads or factors - No hard and fast rule can be laid down in these matters - Held that the weightage for the viva voce test which is 40 per cent, it is per se excessive and hence arbitrary, cannot be accepted - Held, selection process was not in any manner vitiated - Appeal dismissed.
