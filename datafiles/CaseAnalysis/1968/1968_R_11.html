Raj Kumar Mohan Singh, and Others v Raj Kumar Pasupatinath Saran Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 472; AIR 1969 SC 135; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Equitable Doctrine

Summary: Land & Property - Oudh Estates Act, 1869, ss. 11 and 22 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 43 - (A) Whether deed of trust Dt/- 29-08-1932, executed by Raja Bishwanath was valid and operative so as to create an interest in favour of Senior Rajkumar? - Under Will, devise of residue in favour of Raja Bishwanath could become effective only on his adoption by Rani Jagannath Kuar and between date of his death and adoption of a son, there was intestacy in respect of taluqdari estate which was not lawfully disposed of; further, as under Hindu Law, so under provisions of Oudh Estates Act, estate does not remain in abeyance and on death of testator therefore, widow took estate by virtue of s. 22(7) of Oudh Act and that estate must ensure for lifetime of widow; when widow takes estate of a taluqdar on intestacy, she does not inherit estate as an heir, but takes it by virtue of statutory right conferred upon her and holds estate as an owner which she is entitled to enjoy it during her lifetime and she cannot alienate or encumber estate or any part thereof beyond her lifetime, but so long as she is alive, no one has any vested interest in estate and person or heir who would take estate will be determined on termination of her natural span of life - Held, there is no provision in Act which forfeits such interest and son adopted by her in pursuance of authority of her husband does not divest her of estate and adopted son inherits estate on her death u/cl. (8) of s.22 and not before, thus, during lifetime of Rani Jagannath Kuar, Raja Bishwanath had no interest and his interest in taluqdari estate arose on death of Rani Jagannath Kuar and not before; Raja Bishwanath, though was not precluded by virtue of s. 13 from setting up Will, was still not entitled to a present vested interest during lifetime of Rani Jagannath Kuar and he was accordingly, during lifetime of Rani Jagannath Kuar, not competent to settle estate for benefit of his creditors - (B) Whether those claiming under Raja Bishwanath's Will were estopped by equitable doctrine embodied in s. 43 of Transfer of Property Act? - Doctrine incorporated in s. 43 may apply if transfer is for consideration and not otherwise - Held, by undertaking duties, trustees rendered no consideration and transfer cannot be said to be one for consideration, and High Court rightly held that trust deed was not executed for consideration and, therefore, principle of s. 43 had no application - Appeal allowed.
