State Bank Of India v Ram Lal Bhaskar And Another

Supreme Court of India

13 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.V. Raveendran, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 701; (2011) 10 SCC 249; 2012(1) ALL MR 450; 2012 (90) ALR 495; 2012 (5) AWC 4748; 2011 (131) FLR 1109; JT 2011 (12) SC 286; 2011(11) SCALE 589; [2011] 12 S.C.R. 1036; 2012 (5) SLR 260

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: State Bank of India Officers' Service Rules, 1992, UCO Bank Officers Employees Service Rules, 1979

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - State Bank of India Officers' Service Rules, 1992, r. 19(3) - Misconduct - Dismissal from service - Legality - 1st respondent branch manager of the appellant bank was served with a charge-sheet alleging various acts of misconduct - Enquiry officer submitted report holding that four out of the six charges were proved against 1st respondent - 1st respondent submitted his representation against the enquiry report - Appointing authority, however, on the basis of enquiry report, dismissed 1st respondent from service - 1st respondent filed departmental appeal but appellate authority dismissed appeal - 1st respondent's review was dismissed by reviewing committee thereafter - 1st respondent challenged the dismissal before the HC - HC allowed writ petition directing the appellant to release retirement benefits to 1st respondent since he had superannuated in the meantime - (A) Whether the disciplinary proceedings against the 1st respondent was sustainable for the reason that the delinquent had already superannuated when the charge-sheet was issued - Held, charge-sheet was issued when the 1st respondent was in service and there were clear provisions in r. 19(3) of the Rules that in case disciplinary proceedings under the relevant rules of service had been initiated against an officer before he ceased to be in the Bank's service by the operation of, or by virtue of, any of the rules or the provisions of the rules, the disciplinary proceedings may, at the discretion of the Managing Director, be continued and concluded by the authority by which the proceedings were initiated in the manner provided for in the rules as if the officer continued to be in service, so however, that he should be deemed to be in service only for the purpose of the continuance and conclusion of such proceedings - Thus, there was no merit in the contention of the 1st respondent that the enquiry and the order of dismissal were illegal and without jurisdiction - (B) Whether the findings of enquiry officer were based on evidence - Held, enquiry officer had found that charges no. 1, 2, 4 and 6 had been proved against the 1st respondent - While arriving at the said findings on the four charges proved against the 1st respondent, the enquiry officer had considered a number of documents marked as exhibits and had also considered the documents produced on behalf of the 1st respondent and marked as exhibits - Findings of the enquiry officer were based on evidence and the appointing authority had agreed with the findings of the enquiry officer - (C) Whether the HC was justified in interfering the findings of disciplinary authority - Held, in a proceeding u/art. 226 of the Constitution, the HC should not sit as an appellate authority over the findings of the disciplinary authority and so long as the findings of the disciplinary authority were supported by some evidence the HC should not re-appreciate the evidence and come to a different and independent finding on the evidence - However, by the impugned judgment, HC had re-appreciated the evidence and arrived at the conclusion that the findings recorded by the enquiry officer were not substantiated by any material on record and the allegations levelled against the 1st respondent did not constitute any misconduct and that the 1st respondent was not guilty of any misconduct - Impugned HC order was set aside - Appeal allowed.
