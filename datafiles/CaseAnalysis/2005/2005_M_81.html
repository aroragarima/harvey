Commissioner of Central Excise, Calcutta v M/s. Hindustan National Glass and Industries

Supreme Court of India

11 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Ruma Pal, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 240; (2005) 3 SCC 489; AIR 2005 SC 1906; 2005 (182) E.L.T. 12; JT 2005 (3) SC 361; 2005 (67) RLT 342; 2005(3) SCALE 148; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 744

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise and Salt Act, 1944 - Packing charges realized by assessee from its customers - Different situations for different types of packing - Whether it would form part of assessable value of final product or not to attract duty? - Some of show-cause notices were issued beyond period prescribed under s.11 A - Held, CEGAT with reference to factual background come to conclusion that packing was not necessary for concerned excisable articles in condition in which it is generally sold in wholesale market at factory gate - Findings are factual and cannot be termed to be perverse in any manner to warrant interference - Appeals Dismissed.
