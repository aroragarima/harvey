Mackinon Mackenzie and Company Limited v Mackinnon Employees Union

Supreme Court of India

25 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 130; (2015) 4 SCC 544; AIR 2015 SC 1373; 2015 (145) FLR 184; JT 2015 (5) SC 331; 2015 (2) LLJ 151; 2015(2) SCALE 707; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 45; 2015 (5) SLR 358

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Closure, Junior, Breach, Shipping, Clearing, Unfair Labour Practices, Retrenchment of workmen

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 25G, 25F(a), 25F(c), 25FFA - Industrial Disputes (Bombay) Rules, 1957, r. 81 - Retrenchment of workmen - Unfair labour practices - Appellant-Company served notice of retrenchment upon approximately 98 workmen on ground that appellant-Company was accumulating losses - Aggrieved, respondent-Union filed complaint before Industrial Court alleging unfair labour practices on part of appellant-Company and Industrial Court held that appellant-Company committed unfair labour practices inter alia by breach of s. 25G of 1947 Act r/w r. 81 of the Rules and set aside notice of retrenchment served upon workmen - Hence, appellant-Company filed a petition before HC to quash Award passed by Industrial Court - However, HC dismissed petition and affirmed findings of fact recorded by Industrial Court and on further appeal, DB reaffirmed the judgment of Single Judge - Hence, instant appeal - Whether findings recorded by Industrial Court on relevant issues in award in favour of workmen are erroneous warranting interference.

Held, workmen were served with retrenchment notices stating amount of retrenchment compensation and one month's salary in lieu of notices that would be due to concerned workman. However, no cogent evidence has been adduced by appellant-Company to prove said one month's salary of concerned workmen in lieu of retrenchment notice has been actually paid to them. Further, the concerned workmen were given notice of retrenchment with Statement of Reasons appended therewith on 27-7-1992 effective from 4-8-1992. Hence, one month notice was not given to concerned workmen before their retrenchment came into effect nor one month's salary in lieu of retrenchment notice was paid to concerned workmen. Hence, said action by appellant-company is clear breach of provision of condition precedent for retrenchment of workmen provided u/s. 25F(a) of the Act. Further, appellant-Company has not produced cogent evidence that notice to State Govt. was served prior to retrenchment of concerned workmen as mandated u/s. 25F(c) of the Act. Hence, appellant-Company has not complied with conditions precedent to retrenchment as per ss. 25F(a) and 25F(c) of the Act which are mandatory. Further, it has not been explicitly made clear by appellant-Company that Board of Directors of the Company have taken decision to close down Clearing and Forwarding Section, which is part of the undertaking of appellant-Company. However, by cumulative effect of pleadings, Statement of Reasons appended to the retrenchment notice, it was very clear that the retrenchment notice served upon the concerned workmen was an action of closure of Clearing and Forwarding section of appellant-Company. However, as per finding of fact recorded by Industrial Court, there was no mandatory notice served on State Govt. by appellant-Company u/s. 25FFA of the Act about intended closure of department/unit of Company. Further, there are no valid reasons assigned in explanatory note to justify action of appellant-Company in not following the principle of 'last come first go' as mandated u/s. 25G of the Act r/w r. 81 of the Rules to retrench the concerned workmen who are seniors to workmen who were retained in department. Hence, concurrent finding of fact recorded by HC with regard to non-compliance of s. 25G of the Act by appellant-Company is also statutory violation on part of appellant-Company in retrenching certain concerned senior workmen. Hence, impugned judgment and order of DB of HC is affirmed. Further, appellant-Company is directed to comply with terms and conditions of award passed by Industrial Court by computing back-wages on the basis of revision of pay scales of concerned workmen and other benefits. Appeal dismissed.
