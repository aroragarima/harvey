Union of India and others v Ex.Flt.Lt.G.S. Bajwa

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 437; (2003) 9 SCC 630; AIR 2004 SC 808; 2003 (104) DLT 618; 2003 (3) ESC 337; JT 2003 (4) SC 505; 2003 (2) LLN 948; 2003(4) SCALE 494; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 1092; 2003 (1) SCW 7009; 2003 (2) SLJ 288; 2003 (3) SLT 465; 2003 (4) Supreme 96; 2003 (2) UPLBEC 1479

Case Digest

Subject: Air Law; Constitution; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Court Martial, Attorney-General, Freedom Of Association

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(c), 19(4), 21, 22, 33 - Air Force Rules, 1969, rr. 100, 102, 110, 111, 125, 88, 89 - Air Force Act, 1950, ss.41(2), 65 - Air Force Rules, 1969, r.100 - Constitution of India, art.21 - Court Martial - Dismissal from service - Challenged - On appeal HC directed reinstatement - Appeal - (1 ) held, provisions of Act cannot be challenged on ground that they infringe fundamental right guaranteed to respondent u/art. 21 - Since Air Force Act is a law duly enacted by Parliament in exercise of its plenary legislative jurisdiction r/w art 33 of Constitution of India, same cannot be held to be invalid merely because it has effect of restricting or abrogating right guaranteed u/art 21 or under any of provisions of Chapter III of Constitution - (2 ) Further held, provisions of Act and Rules were scrupulously followed in conduct of Court Martial proceedings and respondent chose to defend himself without seeking help of defending officer or friend of accused - It, therefore, does not lie in his mouth to complain that he was prejudiced in his defence on account of State not providing him defence counsel at State expense - (3 ) Principles of Natural Justice - Held, r.100 itself incorporates principle of natural justice by giving to respondent an opportunity to go through proceedings and for this purpose to inspect same at all reasonable times - This meets requirement of natural justice and respondent cannot complain on ground that he was not given a copy of proceedings - Appeal allowed.
