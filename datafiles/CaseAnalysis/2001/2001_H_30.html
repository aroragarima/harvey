The Oriental Insurance Co. Ltd v Hansrajbhai V. Kodala & Ors

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19785; (2001) 5 SCC 175; (2001) SCC (Cr) 857; 2001 (1) ACC 618; 2001 ACJ 827; AIR 2001 SC 1832; 2001 (2) AWC 1447; [2001] 105 Comp Cas 743; 2001 (3) G.L.R. 2156; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 168; JT 2001 (4) SC 477; 2001 (2) KLT 235; 2001 (2) MLJ(SC) 180; 2001 (128) PLR 663; 2001 (2) RCR(Civil) 629; 2001(3) SCALE 223; 2001 (3) SCJ 191; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 999; 2001 (3) SLT 449; 2001 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 643; 2001 (3) Supreme 252

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Corporate; Insurance; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Social Security, Death Or Permanent Disablement, No Fault Liability, Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 1982

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 163 A - Fatal Accidents Act, 1855 - General Insurance Business (Nationalisation) Act, 1972 - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 - Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 1982 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Whether compensation payable u/s. 163 A of Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, as per structured formula basis is in addition or in alternative to determination of compensation on principle of fault liability, after following procedure prescribed under Act? - Held, from provisions of ss. 140-143, 161-168, it appears that no specific mention is made that remedy provided u/s. 163 A is in addition or in alternative to determination of compensation on basis of fault liability - Purpose of this section and Second Schedule is to avoid long-drawn litigation and delay in payment of compensation to victims or his heirs who are in dire need of relief - This benefit can be availed of by claimant only by restricting his claim on basis of income at a slab of Rs. 40,000 which is highest slab in Second Schedule which indicates that Legislature wanted to give benefit of no fault liability to a certain limit - This would clearly indicate that scheme is in alternative to determination of compensation on fault basis under Act - Object underlying said amendment is to pay compensation without there being any long-drawn litigation on a predetermined formula, which is known as structured formula basis which itself is based on relevant criteria for determining compensation and procedure of paying compensation after determining fault is done away with - Compensation amount is paid without pleading or proof of fault, on principle of social justice as a social security measure because of ever increasing motor vehicle accidents in a fast moving society - Further, law before insertion of s. 163 A was giving limited benefit to extent provided u/s. 140 for no fault liability and determination of compensation amount on fault liability was taking a long time - That mischief is sought to be remedied by introducing s. 163 A and disease of delay is sought to be cured to a large extent by affording benefit to victims on structured formula basis - Appeals allowed.
