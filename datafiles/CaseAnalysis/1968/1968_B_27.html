Bhanuprasad Hariprasad Dave Rajuji Gambhirji v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, G.K. Mitter, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 45; AIR 1968 SC 1323; 1968 CRLJ 1505; 1970 (1) LLJ 643; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 22

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 161 - Prevention of Corruption Act, ss. 5(1)(d) and 5(2) - Bombay Police Act, 1951, s. 161(1) - (A) Whether conviction of appellants solely on basis of testimony of witnesses who, are all interested witnesses and their evidence not having been corroborated by any independent evidence, is correct? - Held, yes - They are partisan witnesses as they were interested in success of trap laid by them - They were accomplices - Law does not require that their evidence should be corroborated before being accepted as sufficient to found a conviction - In case of partisan witnesses, corroboration that may be looked for is corroboration in a general way and not material corroboration as in case of evidence of accomplices (B) Whether offence can be said to have been made out u/s. 161 of IPC? - Held, yes - To establish offence u/s. 161 of IPC all that prosecution had to establish was that appellants were public servants and that they had obtained illegal gratification for showing or forbearing to show, in exercise of their official functions, favour or disfavour to someone - If he had used his official position to extract illegal gratification requirements of law are satisfied - (C) Whether prosecution case was with in colour of their duty? - Held, no - In present case appellants cannot be said to have received bribe under colour of their duty - There was no connection between duties to be performed by them and receipt of bribe in question - All that can be said in present case is that appellant 1, a police officer, taking advantage of his position as a police officer - Appeal dismissed.
