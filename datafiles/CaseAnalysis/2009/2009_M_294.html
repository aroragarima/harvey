State Of Punjab & Anr. v Mohammed Iqbal Bhatti

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 949; (2009) 17 SCC 92; 2009(5) ALL MR 984; 2010 CRLJ 1436; 2010 (4) CTC 458; JT 2009 (13) SC 180; 2009 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2459; 2010 (4) MLJ(Crl) 652; 2010 (1) MLJ(SC) 116; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 861; 2009(10) SCALE 451; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 790

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 197 - Whether the State has any power of review in the matter of grant of sanction in terms of s. 197 of CrPC, was the issue - Corruption charges against respondent - Sanction sought from competent authority, which was denied - With the change of Govt. and after more than nine months, matter again placed before competent authority, and than sanction was granted - Writ petition preferred thereagainst, which was allowed - HC, opined that the State has no power of review and in any event, the impugned order could not have been passed as the State while passing its earlier order D/-15-12-2003 has exhausted its jurisdiction - Subsequent order granting sanction, which was denied in the earlier order, whether proper in facts of the case? - Held, No - Contentions heard, precedents referred - HC noted that several queries were raised but remained unanswered; departmental proceeding initiated against the respondent was dropped; thus the non-grant of approval of sanction; no material was placed before competent authority as to giving ground for subsequent sanction; just a communication was received wherein reference was made to a certain letter of an earlier date; HC viewed it not to be a new material - Findings of HC, upheld - Nothing on record to show as to why reconsideration became necessary; on what premise such procedure was adopted was unknown; application of mind found to be absent to show the necessity for reconsideration or review of the earlier order - Being without merit - Appeal dismissed.
