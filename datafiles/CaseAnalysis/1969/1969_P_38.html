Town Municipal Council, Athani v Presiding Officer, Labour Court, Hubli and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 March 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 162; (1969) 1 SCC 873; AIR 1969 SC 1335; 1969 (18) FLR 373; 1969 LabIC 1538; 1969 (2) LLJ 651; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 51

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Claim for overtime work, Labour Court Jurisdiction

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 33C(2)

Minimum Wages Act, 1948, s. 20(1), 20(3) - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 137 - Claim for overtime work - Labour Court Jurisdiction - Legality - Respondent (workmen) of appellant (Council) filed applications u/s. 33C(2) of 1947 Act before Labour Court, Hubli, seeking computation of their claim for overtime work for period between 1-4-1955 to 31-12-1957 and for work done on weekly off-days for the period between 1-4-1955 and 31-12-1960 - Labour Court allowed all applications and directed appellant to make payment of amounts found - Aggrieved appellant filed petitions before HC - HC by impugned common judgment dismissed petitions - Hence, instant appeals - Appellant contended that jurisdiction of Labour Court to deal with claims of workmen u/s. 33C(2) of the Act, was barred by fact that same relief could have claimed by workmen u/s. 20(1) of 1948 Act - Whether claims made by respondents under 1947 Act were of such a nature that they could have brought before Authority u/s. 20(1) 1948 Act - Held, it could not be accepted that s. 20(1) of 1948 Act should be interpreted as intended to cover all claims in respect of minimum wages or overtime payment or payment for days of rest even though there might be no dispute as to the rates at which those payments were to be claimed - It was true that, u/s. 20(3) of 1948, power was given to Authority dealing with an application u/s. 20(1) of 1948 Act to direct payment of Actual amount found due; but that, it appeared, was only an incidental power granted to that Authority, so that directions made by Authority u/s. 20(1) of 1948 Act might be effectively carried out and there might not be unnecessary multiplicity of proceedings - Power to make orders for payment of actual amount due to an employee u/s. 20(3) of 1948 Act could not, therefore, be interpreted as indicated that jurisdiction to Authority u/s. 20(1) of 1948 Act was given for the purpose of enforcement of payment of amounts and not for the purpose of ensuring compliance by employer with various rates fixed under 1948 Act - Remedy u/s. 20(1) of 1948 Act could not have sought by respondents in any of applications - No question could, thus, arise of the jurisdiction of Labour Court to entertain applications u/s. 33C(2) of 1947 Act being barred because of provisions of 1948 Act - An Industrial Tribunal or a Labour Court dealing with applications or references under 1948 Act were not Courts and they were in no way governed either by CPC or CrPC - Thus, it could not be accepted art. 137 of 1963 Act would apply even to applications made to an Industrial Tribunal or a Labour Court - Thus, art. 137 of 1963 Act did not apply to applications u/s. 33C(2) of 1947 Act - Appeals dismissed.
