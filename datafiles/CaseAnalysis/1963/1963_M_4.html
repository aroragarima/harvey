Manipur Administration v M. Nila Chandra Singh

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 212; AIR 1964 SC 1533; 1964 CRLJ 465; 1964 (2) SCJ 444; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 574

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Socio-Economic; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Essential commodities

Summary: Trade - Health & Drug - Manipur Foodgrains Dealers Licencing Order, 1958, cl. 2(a) - Essential Commodities Act, 1955, s. 7, Cl. 3(1) and 3(2) - (A) What are essential requirements of being a dealer u/cl. 2 (a)? - Held, they must be carrying on a business of purchase, sale or storage and sale must be in quantity of 100 mds. or more at one time, of goods specified in schedule - Transaction must be a continued one - Single or casual transactions or retail dealings in lesser quantities not sufficient - (B) Whether there can be conviction u/s. 7 on basis of mere presumption u/cl. 3(2)?- Further held, statutory presumption raised by cl. 3(2) is a rebuttable presumption - Fact that storage was for purpose of business has to be proved by independent evidence - Element of business which is essential to attract provisions of cl. 3(1) is not covered by presumption raised under cl. 3(2)- Fact that cl. 3(2) directly refers to cl. 3(1) does not help to widen scope of presumption which is allowed to be raised by it - In construing cl. 3(2), it would not be open to Court to add any words to said provision, and in fact, words reasonably construed cannot justify raising of a presumption which would take in requirement as to business which is one ingredient in the definition of a dealer - Appeal dismissed.
