Qudrat Ullah v Municipal Board, Bareilly

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, D.G. Palekar, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 542; (1974) 1 SCC 202; AIR 1974 SC 396; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 530

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Easements Act, s. 52 - Uttar Pradesh (Temporary) Control of Rent and Eviction Act, 1947, ss. 2 and 106 - General Clauses Act, 1897 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 105 - Uttar Pradesh Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972 ss. 13 and 4 - Whether agreement in question creates lease or license? - Held, there is no simple litmus test to distinguish a lease as defined in s. 105, Transfer of Property Act from a license as defined in s. 52 Easements Act, but character of transaction turns on operative intent of parties - Parties to an agreement cannot, however, turn a lease into a license merely by stating that document is to be deemed a license or describing it as such - Relationship of parties is determined by law on a consideration of all relevant provisions of agreement; nor will employment of words appropriate to a lease prevent agreement from conferring a license only if from whole document it appears that it was intended merely to confer a license - Appeal No. 1727 of 1968 dismissed and Appeal No. 1728 of 1968 allowed.
