Anand Prakash v State Of U.P. And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 342; (1990) SCC (Cr) 96; (1990) 1 SCC 291; AIR 1990 SC 516; 1990 (1) Crimes 265; 1990 CrLR(SC) 94; JT 1989 (4) SC 557; 1989(2) SCALE 1326; [1989] Supp2 S.C.R. 479

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Criminal - National Security Act, 1980, s. 8 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 411 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - Preventive detention - (A) Theft of electric wires - Case u/s. 411 of Code - No past criminal history - Other accused's statement in recovery memo not-indicating detenu's involvement - If sufficient for satisfaction for detention - (B) Detenu in jail - Solitary instance of his alleged crime - No credible information to warrant inference that if released on bail he would continue illegal activities - If preventive detention, be allowed to supplant criminal prosecution - (C) Ground of detention - Proximity - Recovery of melted wire - Detenu arrested 2 months thereafter and detention order passed on next day of arrest - No satisfactory explanation for delay - Whether detention vitiated? - Held, (A) Not sufficient for satisfaction for detention - (B)Cannot be allowed - (C)Theft and recovery of electric wire could not be proximate cause, detention vitiated - Petition allowed.
