Baidyanath Mahapatra v State Of Orissa And Anr

Supreme Court of India

10 August 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 804; (1989) 4 SCC 664; (1990) SCC (L&S) 38; AIR 1989 SC 2218; 1989 (15) ALR 686; 1989 (11) ATC 886; 1989 (2) CLR 517; 1989 (25) ECR 24; 1989 (59) FLR 393; JT 1989 (3) SC 360; 1989 LabIC 2089; 1994 (3) LLJ 268; 1989 (2) LLN 722; 1989(2) SCALE 271; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 803; 1989 (4) SLR 220; 1989 (2) UJ 666

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Service

Summary: Service - Atomic Energy Act, 1962 - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, s. 29 - Orissa Service Code, r. 71(a) - Whether order of premature retirement based on recommendations of review committee which was on basis of adverse entries awarded to appellant in remote past especially when appellant had been promoted to post of Superintending Engineer in 1976 and he had further been permitted to cross efficiency bar in 1979 was valid or not? - Held, since communication of adverse entries to appellant was highly belated, representation against those adverse remarks should have been considered on merits and same could not be rejected on ground of delay as Govt. itself was guilty of inordinate delay - Appellant had right to make representation against adverse entries within six months period, therefore, adverse entries awarded in years 1981-82 and 1982-83 could not be taken into account as prescribed period had not yet expired - Chairman of tribunal also being head of review committee could not have presided over proceedings of tribunal, it being a violation of principles of natural justice - Order of premature retirement was, thus, vitiated and tribunal committed error in upholding same - Appeal allowed.
