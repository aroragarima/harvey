Aggarwal and Modi Enterprises Private Limited and Another v New Delhi Municipal Council

Supreme Court of India

31 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 870; (2007) 8 SCC 75; AIR 2007 SC 3131; 2007 (4) AWC 3992; JT 2007 (10) SC 545; 2007(10) SCALE 549; 2007 (7) SCJ 755; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 675

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Oil Mills, NDMC Act, 1994

Summary: NDMC Act, 1994, s. 141(2) - Appellants were held to be unauthorized occupants of the premises in dispute namely, that of Chanakya Cinema Complex situated in Diplomatic Enclave, New Delhi - Primary stand was that true scope and ambit of s. 141(2) has not been kept in view and the manner in which NDMC is interpreting it, goes against the intended legislative object - Held, any immovable property belonging to NDMC is to be sold, leased, licensed or transferred on consideration which is not to be less than the value at which such immovable property could be sold, leased, or transferred in fair competition - NDMC is obligated to adopt the procedure by which it can get maximum possible return/consideration for such immovable property - Public auction not only ensures fair price and maximum return it also militates against any allegation of favouritism on the part of the Government authorities while giving grant for disposing of public property - Appeal dismissed.
