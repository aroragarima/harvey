Messrs Gotan Lime Syndicate v Commissioner of Income Tax, Delhi and Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

15 November 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 214; AIR 1966 SC 1564; [1966] 59 ITR 718; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 596

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Capital Expenditure, Business Income or Loss

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Lease - Royalty payment - Computation of business profit - Revenue expenditure - Appellant/assessee carried on business of manufacturing lime from lime stone - By an indenture dt.4-3-1949, assessee was granted right to excavate lime stone in certain area subject to certain conditions - Lease extended from time to time by Govt. for short periods - Govt. adopted a new policy for leasing out lime stone quarries - On 4-10-1954 Govt. sanctioned leasing out of 15 sq.miles of lime deposits to assessee - Assessee never executed any lease but continued to work lime deposits and payments made were finalised by letter dt.30-11-1959 from Mining Engineer to assesee - For a/ys 1954-55, 1955-56 and 1956-57, assessee paid Rs.96,000 to Govt. and claimed it as a revenue deduction against its profits for those years - Income Tax Officer(IO) disallowed that expenditure as being of a capital nature - Appellate Assistant Commissioner uphold view of IO - Appellate Tribunal observed that payment should be treated as a revenue expenditure - HC found on a reference that payment was capital expenditure and could not allowed as a revenue deduction in computing business profit of assessee - Hence instant appeal - Whether Yearly payment of Rs.96,000 made by assessee was treated as revenue expenditure or not - Held, rent and royalty payments would deductible as revenue expenditure - Royalty payment was not a direct payment for securing an enduring advantage - Mining lease provided for a capital sum payment - No material had placed on record to show that any part of royalty must treated as premium and referable to acquisition of mining lease - Royalty payment including dead rent have relation only to lime deposits to be got - Yearly payment of Rs. 96,000 should treated as revenue expenditure - Answer to question referred to HC must be in favour of assessee - Appeals allowed.
