District Basic Eduction Officer v Dhananjai Kumar Shukla And Anr

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1609; (2008) 3 SCC 481; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1464; 2007 (5) ESC 760; 2008 (116) FLR 728; JT 2008 (1) SC 508; 2008 (2) MLJ 658; 2008 (2) MLJ(SC) 658; 2008(1) SCALE 396; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 972; 2008 (3) SLJ 32; 2008 (2) SLR 119

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Recognised Basic Schools (Junior High Schools) (Recruitment and Conditions of Services of Teachers) Rules, 1978

Summary: Service - Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 8 r. 5 - Uttar Pradesh Recognised Basic Schools (Junior High Schools) (Recruitment and Conditions of Services of Teachers) Rules, 1978, r. 6 - Challenge to appointment - Failure to file counter affidavit - Legality - 1st respondent/Headmaster in a recognized School applied to the vacancy of Headmaster in 2nd respondent/School wherein 1st respondent's father was the manager - 1st respondent, consequently, was appointed as Headmaster on ad hoc basis, however, appellant/Education officer did not sanction his salary - 1st respondent filed writ petition, however, appellant did not file the counter affidavit - Single Judge, by an order, directed the appellant to pay 1st respondent's due salary - Appellant challenged the Single Judge order by filing a special appeal before the DB - DB dismissed the special appeal holding that since appellant did not file any counter affidavit, the principles of O. 8 r. 5 of the CPC would apply and thus all the statements made in the writ petition would be deemed to have been admitted - Hence the present appeal by appellant/Education Officer - Whether the averments of a writ petition would be deemed to have been admitted in the absence of counter-affidavit of respondent - Held, provisions of CPC are applicable in terms of the High Court rules framed by the concerned HC despite s. 141 of the CPC - A Court of law can not only call upon the plaintiff to prove his case despite non-filing of the written statement but also there cannot be any doubt whatsoever, that no relief can be granted by the HC in exercise of its jurisdiction u/art. 226 of the Constitution of India which would be contrary to law - Appointment of 1st respondent being contrary to the mandatory provisions as contained in r. 6 of the Rules, the same was a nullity - An appointment which was per se illegal could not have been directed to be legalized only because the appellant did not file its counter affidavit - Rules of pleading contained in the CPC do not cover questions of law - If a fact stands admitted the same in terms of s. 56 of the Indian Evidence Act, 1872 need not be proved - 1st respondent's appointment was made by defeating the statutory provisions - Therefore, whether in law or in equity, it would be wholly improper to permit 1st respondent to continue to act as a Headmaster - Impugned HC order is set aside - Appeal allowed.
