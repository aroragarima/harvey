Hardeep Singh Sohal v State of Punjab Through C.B.I

Supreme Court of India

28 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 788; (2004) 11 SCC 612; AIR 2004 SC 4783; 2004 CRLJ 4627; JT 2004 (8) SC 87; 2004 (4) RCR(Criminal) 688; 2004(8) SCALE 257; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 783

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Fingerprints

Summary: Criminal - Defence and Security Forces - Practice and Procedure - Socio-Economic - TADA Act, s. 3(3) - IPC, s 120B r/w s 302; TADA Act, s 3(3) - Conviction - Whether confession can be taken as evidence against appellants? - Held, motive by itself is not sufficient to prove guilt of accused - Though voluminous evidence was adduced by prosecution, there is none which would come within parameters of admissible item of evidence - Appellants acquitted of all charges framed against them - Appeals allowed.
