Biswanath Bhattacharya v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

21 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 38; (2014) 4 SCC 392; AIR 2014 SC 1003; 2014 (1) CLT(SC) 152; 2014 CRLJ 1277; 2014 (301) E.L.T. 593; JT 2014 (2) SC 268; 2014 (2) MLJ 108; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 873; 2014(1) SCALE 514; [2014] 1 S.C.R. 885

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Conservation of foreign exchange, Forfeiture of properties

Summary: Customs - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974 - Smugglers and Foreign Exchange Manipulators (Forfeiture of Property) Act, 1976, s. 6(1), 7(1), 13(3) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 20, 20(1), 31B - Conservation of foreign exchange - Forfeiture of properties - Sustainability - Appellant was initially detained by order dated under provisions of the 1974 Act (COFEPOSA) on ground that he in collaboration with his brother, who was living in London was indulging in activities which was prejudicial to conservation of foreign exchange - Appellant challenged detention order - Later appellant was released on bail - While appellant was in custody respondent issued notice u/s. 6(1) of the 1976 Act to explain sources of his income which he acquired assets described in schedule to notice - Some correspondence ensued between respondent on one hand and appellants wife and appellant on other hand - Respondent passed order u/s. 7(1) of the 1976 Act forfeiting properties mentioned in the schedule - Appellant filed appeal before Tribunal - Appeal was partly allowed - Appellant filed petition and challenged the order of Tribunal before HC - Petition was partly allowed and held that forfeiture of the property was illegal - Aggrieved by the order of Single Judge, respondents file appeal before DB - Appeal was allowed - Hence instant appeal - Whether notice issued u/s. 6 of the Act was defective and therefore illegal as notice did not contain reasons which made Competent authority believe that notice scheduled properties were illegally acquired properties and that forfeiture, provided under the Act was violative of art. 20 of the Constitution - Held, there was no express statutory requirement to communicate reasons which led to issuance of notice u/s. 6 of the Act - Reasons, though was not initially supplied along with notice it was subsequently supplied thereby enabling appellant to effectively meet case of respondents - Appellant could have effectively convinced respondents by producing appropriate material that further steps in furtherance to notice u/s. 6 need not be taken - Apart from that, order of forfeiture was appealable order where correctness of decision u/s. 7 of the Act to forfeit properties could be examined - Forfeiture provided in s. 13(3) of the Act in case of offences which involved embezzlement of Government money or property was really speedier method of realizing Government money or property as compared to a suit which it was not disputed - Government could bring for realizing money or property and was not punishment or penalty within meaning of art. 20(1) of the Constitution - Such suit could ordinarily be brought without in any way affecting right to realize fine that might have been imposed by Criminal Court in connection with offence - SC opined that the Act was not violative of art. 20 of the Constitution - . Even otherwise as was rightly pointed out by Solicitor General, in view of its inclusion in IXth Schedule, the Act was immune from attack on ground that it violates any of rights guaranteed under Part III of the Constitution by virtue of declaration art. 31-B of the Constitution - Hence, there was no other ground on which correctness of order of forfeiture was assailed in petition - Appellant did seek re-appreciation of evidence without even appropriate pleading in petition - It was different matter that HC in exercise of its writ jurisdiction did not normally re-appreciate evidence - Therefore, there was no reason to remit the matter to HC - S. Narayanappa & Ors. v Commissioner Of Income-Tax, Bangalore 1966 Indlaw SC 186 followed on - Appeals dismissed.
