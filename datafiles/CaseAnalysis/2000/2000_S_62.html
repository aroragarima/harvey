Punjab and Sind Bank and Others v Sakattar Singh

Supreme Court of India

29 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, D.P. Mohapatra, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2631; (2001) SCC (L&S) 209; (2001) 1 SCC 214; 2001 (1) AWC 473; 2001 (88) FLR 383; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 450; 2001 LabIC 301; 2001 (1) LLJ 174; 2001 (1) LLN 758; 2000(7) SCALE 686; 2000 (8) SLT 553; 2000 (8) Supreme 47; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 304

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Bipartite Settlement, cl. XVI of IV - Industrial Dispute Act, 1947 - Service - Muster Roll - Termination - Challenged - Respondent had joined service of appellant-Bank as apprentice and was regularly appointed as Clerk-cum-Cashier w.e.f. 18-12-1978 - Respondent took many leaves on account of very serious eye ailment and he had such ailment even in year 1993-94 and after obtaining fitness certificate from doctor he submitted his joining report to appellant/Branch Manager on 4-4-1994 which was not accepted by him - Therefore, respondent submitted application to higher authorities of Bank - Meanwhile, however, by notice dated 12-3-1994 respondent was asked to explain his unauthorized absence after expiry of sanctioned leave - Respondent, however, claimed to have appeared before competent authority to permit him to join duty but no order was passed - On other hand, order dt. 18-4-1994 came to be passed informing respondent's termination from his services - Aggrieved, respondent filed writ petition against said order before HC which quashed orders made by appellants and made clear to appellants to take action in accordance of law and principles of natural justice - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justifiable - Held, in Syndicate Bank v. General Secretary, Syndicate Bank Staff Association & Anr., 2000 Indlaw SC 29, relied on appellant's stand was that material was also produced only after order of termination had been made and in seeking review of said order - Respondent claimed that he had sent several communications regarding his illness or to extend his leave or to rejoin duty but there did not appear to be any record with bank nor respondent was in position to produce any proof of his having sent such letters - Court did not find any material on record to show that respondent had reported for duty within period indicated in notice issued in terms of cl. XVI of IV Bipartite Settlement - In said circumstances Court found HC had proceeded on erroneous basis of non- compliance with principles of natural justice, whereas true content of principles of natural justice should had been borne in mind, particularly when there was agreement between parties as to manner in which, situation should be dealt with and consequence that would ensue thereof - Hence, set aside order of HC and dismissed writ petition filed by respondent - Appeal allowed.
