Jagmohan Bahl and another v State (NCT of Delhi) and another

Supreme Court of India

18 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 916; JT 2014 (14) SC 369; 2015 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 668; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 291

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Litigant

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 420 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 438 - Anticipatory bail - Allowed - Challenged - Respondent No. 2 filed an FIR against the appellants alleging that they had conspired against him and had subjected him to cheating, criminal breach of trust and have misappropriated the money - Appellants moved an application u/s. 438 of CrPC for grant of anticipatory bail which was dismissed by the Trial Court - Thereafter, the appellants after expiry of 3 weeks filed second application u/s. 438 of CrPC which was allowed by the Trial Court - On appeal, HC set aside the order of the Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the second application u/s. 438 of CrPC was allowed by another Additional Sessions Judge who had not dealt with the first application. The Second Judge could not have entertained the bail application especially when the earlier Judge was available. To elaborate, the Additional Sessions Judge who has dealt with the matter on the first occasion, had neither been transferred from the said court, nor had he become incapacitated to come to court nor was he absent for a considerable length of time. As it appears, the HC has taken exception to the fact that the application was moved when the 2nd Judge was allotted the roaster to deal with the application u/s. 438 of CrPC. The natural corollary would have been to set aside the order as it has been passed in an illegal manner. However, in the present case, the allegations, are quite different. The FIR was instituted u/s. 420/34 of IPC and relates to execution of an agreement. In such a situation, SC do not intend to set aside the order and direct the appellants to move a fresh application for bail u/s. 438 of CrPC. The bail order granted in appellant's favour shall remain in force and the appellants shall abide by the terms and conditions imposed by the Court and would not deviate from any of the conditions. Appeal disposed of.
