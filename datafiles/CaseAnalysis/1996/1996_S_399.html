State of Maharashtra Etc v Som Nath Thapa, Etc

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, A.M. Ahmadi, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 859; (1996) 4 SCC 659; (1996) SCC (Cr) 820; 1996 (3) AD(SC) 502; AIR 1996 SC 1744; AIR 1996 SCW 1977; 1996 (2) Crimes 64; 1996 CRLJ 2448; JT 1996 (4) SC 615; 1996(3) SCALE 449; [1996] Supp1 S.C.R. 189; 1996 (3) Supreme 539

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Terrorists, Terrorist Act, Terrorist

Summary: Criminal - Arms Act, 1959 - Explosive Substances Act, 1908 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Prevention of Damage to Public Property Act, 1984 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987 - Several bomb blasts occurred at different places in Mumbai on 12-3-1993 - Charges were framed against 127 persons, out of whom 4 had come in appeal, for not having been discharged - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the appellants should be discharged - Held, that the appellant nos. 1 and 2 were discharged whereas the appeal of the appellant nos. 3 and 4 was dismissed - In case of the appellant no. 1, the mere fact of the amount having been credited in the name of the appellant's firm could not suggest, with corroborated evidence, that the air journey of the 11 persons was financed by the appellant, setting aside the conviction in his case - In case of the appellant no. 2, the materials on record showed the after the appellant came to know about the parking of the vehicles, which were loaded with arms and ammunition, he immediately asked the co-accused to remove the jeep from his compound, which showed that he had not intentionally aided the co-accused and this was sufficiently corroborated by confession of the co-accused, allowing his appeal - In case of appellant no. 3, the alleged facts prima facie showed that the appellant had aided the terrorist act in question , even as per the definition of the word 'abet' given in s. 109 of the IPC, so his appeal got dismissed - In case of appellant no. 4, the allegation related to facilitating movement of arms, RDX etc., which act would amount to abetment, as it would be an assistance, defining the word 'abet', and the individual charge against him was for commission of offence u/s. 3(3) of 1987 Act, which, inter alia, made abetment punishable, dismissing his appeal - Appeals disposed of.
