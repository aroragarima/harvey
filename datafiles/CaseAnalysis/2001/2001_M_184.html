Union of India and Others v Manager, Messrs Jain and Associates

Supreme Court of India

06 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19763; (2001) 3 SCC 277; AIR 2001 SC 809; AIR 2001 SCW 607; 2001 AllCJ 540; 2001 (42) ALR 689; 2001 (1) ARBLR 494; 2001 (2) AWC 914; JT 2001 (2) SC 286; 2001 (1) RAJ 194; 2001 (2) RCR(Civil) 119; 2001(1) SCALE 635; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 889; 2001 (1) SLT 914; 2001 (1) Supreme 593

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Ex Parte Decree

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 15, 16, 17, 30, 33, 39 and 41 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 141, O. 20, r.4(2), O. 8, r. 5, O. 9, r. 13 - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Whether provisions of O. 9, r. 13 or principles thereof are applicable in a case where objections u/s. 33 are not filed and ex parte decree is passed on basis of award filed before Court by making Award rule of Court? - Held, s. 41 leaves no doubt that in a proceeding where an application is filed for passing decree on basis of Award submitted by Arbitrator, provisions of CPC, 1908 are applicable and there is no provision which excludes operation of Order 9 in case where judgment is pronounced u/s. 17 - As per s. 141, procedure prescribed is to be followed as far as it can be made applicable to all proceedings - Provisions of CPC including O. 9, r. 13 would be applicable - Delay in filing objection application u/s. 30/33 condoned - Appeal allowed.
