Â State of Uttar Pradesh v Kartar Singh

Supreme Court of India

06 February 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 154; AIR 1964 SC 1135; 1964 CRLJ 229; 1964 (2) ILR(All) 918; 1964 (2) SCJ 666; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 679

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Consumer Protection; Health & Drug; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Food Adulteration

Summary: Health & Drug - Prevention Of Food Adulteration Act, 1954, ss. 7 and 16(1)(a)(i) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 134(1) - Prevention of Food Adulteration Rules, 1955 - Compliance of stutary standards - (A) Whether the High was justifed in revising the conviction of the respondent and acquitting him? - if one could legitimately discard the standard prescribed in the rules, as the Learned Judge has done, we do not see any principle in holding, as he seems to indicate - Held, was not justified in allowing the revision of the respondent and acquitting him - Appeal allowed
