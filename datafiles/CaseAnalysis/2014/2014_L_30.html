Govind Rubber Limited v Louids Dreyfus Commodities Asia Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 922; 2015(6) ALL MR 485; 2015 (108) ALR 735; 2016 (2) CLT(SC) 111; JT 2014 (14) SC 340; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 572; 2014(14) SCALE 92

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; International

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - International - Arbitration & Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 46 - Execution of foreign award - Appellant carrying on business at Mumbai of import and export of commodities and the respondent company having its office at Singapore, entered into a sales contract - Dispute arose between the parties - Respondent referred the matter to Singapore Commodity Exchange for arbitration in accordance with the terms of sale contract - Appellant contended that the Singapore Commodity Exchange or its committee did not have any jurisdiction and the jurisdiction would be in Mumbai - However, appellant in response to the notice made a counter claim before the Arbitral Tribunal contending that the appellant had incurred huge loss in view of the failure on the part of the respondent to supply the goods in time - Arbitral Tribunal made award directing the appellant to pay to the respondent a sum of US 716283 for breach of contract and also to bear cost and expenses of said arbitration - Respondent filed arbitration petition for enforcement and execution of the said award as decree - Single Judge held that the said foreign award was enforceable under Part II and was binding for all purposes on the parties u/s. 46 of the Act - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the respondent issued a sale contract for supply of goods incorporating in the said sale contract various terms including hundred percent payment against letter of credit and also providing the governing terms as 'Singapore Commodity Exchange'. The intention of the parties, as appearing from the correspondence, it can safely be inferred that there had been meeting of mind between the parties and they were ad idem to the terms of sale contract which contained the forum of dispute resolution at Singapore Commodity Exchange. Also, after the dispute was referred to Singapore Commodity Exchange for arbitration, the appellant in response to the notice made a counter claim before the Arbitral Tribunal. By making a counter claim, the appellant indeed submitted to the jurisdiction of the arbitrator. No valid ground to oppose the enforcement of the foreign award. The HC in the impugned order has rightly held that the foreign award is enforceable under Part II and is binding for all purposes on the parties. Appeal dismissed.
