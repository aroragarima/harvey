Suresh H. Rajput and Others v Bhartiben Pravinbhai Soni and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 873; (1996) 7 SCC 199; (1996) SCC (Cr) 254; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 74; AIR 1996 SC 2883; 1996 CRLJ 1621; JT 1995 (9) SC 210; 1995(7) SCALE 217; 1996 (1) SCJ 229; [1995] Supp5 S.C.R. 747

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Food Adulteration

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Food adulteration - Appellant-Food Inspector inspected the premises of the respondent and purchased pasteurized toned milk in the presence of witnesses - Appellant divided samples in three bottles and gave one of them to the respondent - Appellant took with him two samples of which one was deposited with the court and the other was sent to the analyst - Report of the analyst indicated that the milk was adulterated - Local Health authority granted sanction u/s. 20(1) of the 1954 Act for laying the complaint against the respondent - A complaint was laid u/s. 16 r/w s. 7 of the 1954 Act against the respondent - Magistrate acquitted the respondent on the ground that the sanction given by the local health authority was not valid in law, though he found on merits in favour of the prosecution - Whether the sanction granted by the local health authority u/s. 20 of the 1954 Act was in accordance with law - Held, a reading of s. 20(1) of the 1954 Act clearly indicated that before laying the prosecution for an offence under the Act, not being an offence u/s. 14A of the 1954 Act, the condition precedent was that written consent of the Central Govt. or the State Govt. or the person authorised in that behalf by general or special order by the appropriate Govt. was mandatory - In the instant case, the local health authority had no power to delegate the power to launch prosecution to the Food Inspector, but in terms of s. 20(1) of the 1954 Act, the authority could give its consent in writing when it was satisfied that prima facie case existed in the facts of a particular case for laying the prosecution - The learned Magistrate was not right in law in holding that the sanction granted u/s. 20(1) of the 1954 Act was not valid in law - Appeal disposed of.
