Haryana Urban Development Authority v Om Pal

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 332; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 255; (2007) 5 SCC 742; AIR 2008 SC 475; 2007 (6) AWC 6085; 2007 (113) FLR 831; 2007 (4) JhrCR(SC) 144; JT 2007 (5) SC 560; 2007 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1242; 2007 (2) LLJ 1030; 2007 (2) LLN 995; 2007 (147) PLR 488; 2007(5) SCALE 507; 2007 (5) SCJ 117; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 1091; 2007 (3) SLR 513

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Back-Wages

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Whether Industrial Tribunal-cum-Labour Court was justified in directing reinstatement of the respondent with full back-wages and continuity of service? - Held, once two establishments are held to be separate and distinct having different cadre strength of the workmen, if any, the period during which the workman was working in one establishment would not enure to his benefit when he was recruited separately in another establishment, particularly when he was not transferred from one Sub-Division to the other - Direct the appellant to pay him a sum of Rs.25, 000/- Appeal partly allowed.
