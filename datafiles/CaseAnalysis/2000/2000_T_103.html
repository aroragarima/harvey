Trustees of H. E. H. The Nizam's Pilgrimage Money v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

20 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 734; (2000) 4 SCC 179; AIR 2000 SC 1802; 2000 (160) CTR 242; [2000] 243 ITR 676; JT 2000 (4) SC 622; 2000(3) SCALE 391; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 179; [2000] 111 TAXMAN 228

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Trust Property

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Trusts & Associations - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 92 - Indian Trusts Act, 1882, s. 34 - Exemption - Trust property - Legality - Instant appeals were filed by appellants against order passed by HC pertaining to the Assessment Years 1974-75 and 1975-76.

Held, in instant case, no general principle of law of trusts is embodied in s. 34 of the Act which is a special provision conferring jurisdiction on Courts to pass appropriate order in management of the Trust. SC cannot also accept contention of counsel that application u/s. 34 of the Act be treated as suit for reasons more than one. Suffice it to say that application purported to be u/s. 34 of the Act does not satisfy requirements of s. 92 of CPC. It cannot be disputed that when to give effect to a charitable and religious trust is impossible or impracticable initially or becomes so subsequently, court will save trust from failing by invoking cypres doctrine and utilize the Trust property for some other charitable and religious purpose as near as possible to object of Trust mentioned by settlor. Having regard to nature of present proceedings question of invoking doctrine of cypres does not arise, therefore, SC do not propose to deal with that aspect. Judgment of Trial Court does not have effect of altering object of Trust. Therefore, second contention of counsel for appellant also fails. For foregoing reasons SC hold that HC has rightly answered question in favor of the Revenue. Judgments and orders under appeal do not suffer from any illegality. Appeal dismissed.
