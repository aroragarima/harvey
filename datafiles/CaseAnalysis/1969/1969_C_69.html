Commissioner of Income Tax, Excess Profits Tax v V. Jagan Mohan Rao and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 July 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 507; (1969) 2 SCC 389; AIR 1970 SC 300; [1970] 75 ITR 373; 1970 (1) MLJ(SC) 49; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 726

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 34 and 10(2)(xv) - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940 - Interpretation -(A) Word 'information' in s.3 4(1)(b) would cover information as to relevant judicial decisions - Valid proceedings started u/s. 34(1)(b) - Income escaped assessment during relevant year - (B) Provision of s.10(2)(xv) - Capital expenditure or Revenue expenditure - Money paid in consideration or acquisition of a source of profit of income is capital expenditure - Once valid proceedings are started u/s. 34(1)(b) the whole assessment proceedings start afresh and ITO can levy tax on entire income escaped assessment during relevant year - Such amount held, could not be apportioned between capital and income - Appeal No. C.A. Nos. 893 to 898 of 1966 Dismissed; Appeal No.C.A. Nos. 1381 to 1386 of 1966 Allowed.
