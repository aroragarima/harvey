State of Uttar Pradesh and Another v Dinkar Sinha and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1222; (2007) 10 SCC 548; 2007 (7) AWC 7425; 2007(7) SCALE 8; 2007 (6) SCJ 1; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 305; 2007 (5) SLR 444

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Defence Service, U.P. Police Service Rules, 1942, U.P. Non Technical (Class-II) Services (Reservation of Vacancies for the Demobilized Officers) Rules, 1968, U.P. Non Technical (Class-II) Services (Reservation of Vacancies for the Demobilized Officers) Rules, 1973, U.P. Non Technical (Class - II/ Group "B" Services) Appointment of Demobilized Officers Rules, 1980, Uttar Pradesh Non-Technical (Class - II/ Group "B") Services (Appointment of Demobilized Officers) (Second Amendment) Rules, 2000

Summary: Service - Defence & Security Forces - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Government of India Act, 1935, s. 241 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 309,352, & 372 - Uttar Pradesh Non Technical (Class - II/ Group "B" Services) Appointment of Demobilized Officers Rules, 1973, r. 3 - Uttar Pradesh Non Technical (Class - II/ Group "B" Services) Appointment of Demobilized Officers Rules, 1980, rr. 4 & 5 - Seniority List - Benefit of 1980, Rules - Recruitment - Sustainability - Respondent was placed at sl.no 137 in seniority list, as belonging to 1984 batch as direct recruit - Respondent made application claiming benefit of 1980 Rules, which was rejected by appellant - Respondent filed writ petition before HC - HC allowed petition and held that respondent was entitled to seniority on basis of his commissioned service - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that 1980 Rules does not provide for reservation, save and except to limited extended, which could not be applied in case of respondent, who was recruited against vacancies which arose in 1984 only in year 1988 - Further 1980 Rules could not be said to be retrospective in nature nor same was in continuation of 1973 Rules - Thus, HC had committed a manifest error in passing judgment - Whether judgment of HC was sustainable - Held, 1973 Rules was a temporary statute - It died its natural death on expiry thereof - 1980 Rules does not contain any repeal and saving clause - Provisions of relevant provisions of General Clauses Act will, thus, had no application - Once a statute expires by efflux of time, question of giving effect to a right arising there under might not arise - In any event, no such right accrued to respondent - Provision for reservation made in general by State in exercise of its executive power could not had conferred a benefit in terms of provisions of a rule which seeks to apply to a particular category of employees in service - 1980 Rules provide for new set of rules, which had limited application - Not only nomenclature of 1980 Rules was different from that of 1973 Rules, purport and object was also different - 1973 Rules provided for reservation of vacancies for demobilized officers and 1980 Rules provided for appointment of demobilized officers to a limited category of employees - Thus, 1980 Rules would cover only those persons who were appointed against vacancies referred to in 1973 Rule and not those who joined much later - In instant case, respondent admittedly joined state services after emergency was over and in year 1988, when Rules ceased to had any force - Hence, judgment of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
