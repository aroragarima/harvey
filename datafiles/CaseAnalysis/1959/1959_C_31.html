Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal v Calcutta National Bank Limited

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 154; AIR 1959 SC 928; [1959] 37 ITR 171; 1960 SCJ 980; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 660

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940, Sch. I r. 4(4) - Rental income - Profit Calculation - Excess Profit Tax - Other Kind of Business - Assessee/respondent was a banking company and owns a six-storeyed building where its offices located on ground floor and a part of 6th floor while the rest of the building is let out to tenants - Annual rental income derived from portion let out was about Rs. 86,000/ - Appellant charged respondent to pay excess profits tax and on appeal affirmed by Tribunal further on appeal HC reversed order - Hence, instant appeal - Whether assessee liable to excess profits tax for annual rental income received - Held, yes - Mention of other kinds of businesses in r. 4 taken with an incomplete definition of term in Act shows that legislature was defining term business' as and when necessary as it laid down rules for calculation of profits of a business - Further, it was including different kinds of businesses within Act and indicating how in those cases profits to be calculated - Definition given in the Act cannot be said to control everything in Schedule in spite of definition of I profits and heading given to Schedule - Appeal allowed.
