H. C. Pradeep Kumar Rai and others v Dinesh Kumar Pandey and others

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 353; AIR 2015 SC 2342; 2015 (4) AWC 3780; JT 2015 (7) SC 475; 2015 (5) MLJ 98; 2015(6) SCALE 238; 2016(6) SCALE 238; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 825; 2015 (6) SLR 498; 2016 (1) UPLBEC 621

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Sealed Cover Procedure

Summary: Service - Promotion Process - Alleged Illegality - Determination - State Govt. took decision for recruitment of departmental candidates to posts of Sub-inspectors in State, both by direct recruitment and by promotion of Constables and head Constables - Three different orders were issued by Govt. for selection and promotion of candidates - After conducting test, candidates were selected for interview - Further, after declaration of interview results, unsuccessful candidate challenged interview process on several grounds - HC directed State to conduct fresh interview for vacancies of rank of Sub-Inspectors - Further, DB allowed appeal filed by State Govt., reversing judgment of lower Court and directed State to appoint candidates who were selected after interview already held, for rank of Sub-Inspectors - Appellants contended that procedure for selection of departmental candidates for promotion to rank of Sub-inspectors was changed and was amended by every Govt. Order - Whether selection process conducted by respondent was in accordance with law.

Held, regulation in question cannot be said to prevail over or co-exist with Govt. Order in respect of number of candidates to be called for interview as both these provisions provide different requirement for being called for interview. Further, as stated by DB, appellants had participated in process of interview and not challenged it till results were declared. There was gap of almost four months between interview and declaration of result. However, appellants did not challenge it at that time. Hence, only when appellants found themselves to be unsuccessful, they challenged interview and this cannot be allowed. SC held that candidates cannot approbate and reprobate at same time. Further, DB has correctly dealt with issue of sealed cover procedure as process of sealed cover procedure was devised to prevent any prejudice being caused to persons against whom disciplinary or criminal proceedings are pending. Further, it is nobody's case that such persons are prejudiced. Further, with respect to awarding consolidated marks by all panelists in interview, SC held that purpose of constituting multi member interview panel is to remove arbitrariness and ensure objectivity. It is required by each member of interview panel to apply his/her own mind in giving marks to candidates. best evidence of independent application of mind by each panelist is that they awarded separate marks. However, if only consolidated marks are awarded at interview, it becomes questionable, though not conclusive, whether each panelist applied his/her own mind independently. SC held that only because panelists on interview committee did not award separate marks, cannot be ground to quash entire process. Further, Govt. Order dt. 3-2-1999 provided that marks must be separately awarded by interview panelists which was continuation of Govt. Order dt. 23-1-1999, which was superseded expressly by Govt. order dt. 27-2-1999 and it did not provide any condition that marks were to be separately awarded by each interview panelist. Hence, it cannot be argued that Govt. did not follow rules framed by itself. SC held that in present case there is no miscarriage of justice to warrant interference. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Where Executive action of State is challenged, Court must tread with caution and not overstep its limits and interference by Court is warranted only when there are oblique motives or there is miscarriage of justice.
