Â State of Maharashtra and Another v Lok Shikshan Sansatha and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 July 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, S.M. Sikri, G.K. Mitter, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 214; (1971) 2 SCC 410; AIR 1973 SC 588; [1971] S.C.R. 879; [1971] Supp S.C.R. 879

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Maharashtra Secondary Education Board Regulation 1966

Summary: Education - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14,19, 226 & 358 - Grant-in-aid Code (Maharashtra) Rules, rr. 3(1) and (2) -(A) State policy in matter of giving permission to start educational institutions - Interference by High Court -(B) Constitutionality of rr. 3(1) and 3(2) of Rules - (C) Writ of Mandamus - Order by educational authorities refusing to grant permission to start educational institution - Struck down by HC - Without considering reasons - Held, HC should not interfere so long as fundamental rights and principles of natural justice are not violated - Rule has no constitutional force - Rule could not be struck down during emergency i.e. 28-10-1962 to 10-1968 as violative of arts. 14 and 19 - Cannot be struck down by HC without considering reasons given by such authorities - Appeal dismissed.
