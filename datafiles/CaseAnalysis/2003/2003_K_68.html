Municipal Corporation of Greater Mumbai and another v Kamla Mills Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

11 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 509; (2003) 6 SCC 315; AIR 2003 SC 2998; 2003 (3) AllCJ 2086; 2003 (52) ALR 438; 2004 (2) Bom.C.R. 172; JT 2003 (5) SC 462; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 265; 2003(5) SCALE 237; 2003 (4) SLT 417; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 500; 2003 (4) Supreme 530

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Rent Control

Summary: [A.] Rent Control - Bombay Municipal Act, 1888 - Bombay Rent Control Act, 1947 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14 - When a building constructed upon land previously assessed to Municipal tax is demolished for construction of new building, is it open to Municipal Corporation to assess rateable value of land till construction of building by taking market value of land - Held, in facts of present case standard rent would be limit of rateable value - Further held, we find that there was no material produced on record at any stage by respondent to show what standard rent was either in respect of vacant land or in respect of land on which building was constructed and demolished, or in respect of building after it was constructed attention of parties has not been focused on core issue, as a result of which, perhaps, there was failure to produce relevant material before assessor to show what was standard rent interests of justice would require that issue be reconsidered after giving an opportunity to respondent to discharge burden placed upon it under law.
