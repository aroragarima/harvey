Ramkrushna v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

27 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 425; (2007) 13 SCC 525; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 369; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 1759; 2007 CRLJ 3431; 2007 (2) JCC 1466; JT 2007 (6) SC 637; 2007 (3) RCR(Criminal) 33; 2007(6) SCALE 247; 2007 (7) SCJ 611; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 818

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Serologist Report

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - s. 302/304 - Appeal against the judgement of High Court dismissing the petition filed to set aside the conviction order passed by Additional Sessions Judge - Held, even if the prosecution has not been able to establish as to the exact role played by each of the accused, the fact that both the accused had common intention to commit the crime stood established, it is well-settled that the courts are entitled to rely upon a part of the testimony of a witness who has been permitted to be cross-examined by the prosecution - Sufficient evidence available to prove the guilt - Appeal dismissed.
