Govind Mehta v State Of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 203; (1971) 3 SCC 329; (1971) SCC (Cr) 608; AIR 1971 SC 1708; 1971 CRLJ 1266; [1971] S.C.R. 777; [1971] Supp S.C.R. 777; 1971 UJ 720

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: CrPC, 1898, ss.195 and 476, 195, 190 - (A) Duty of magistrate taking cognisance of case - (B) Effect of non-compliance of s. 195 - (C) Power to take cognisance u/s. 190 - Bar upon - Magistrate held, must before taking cognisance examine the facts of the complaint and determine whether his power is barred under any clause of s. 195(1), since the time at which legality of cognisance taken is to be adjudged is the time when cognisance is actually taken - Non-compliance, which imposes a limitation on power of magistrate to take cognisance of the offences mentioned in section, deprives him of jurisdiction to take cognisance of those offences - Held Court's jurisdiction u/s. to take cognisance of complaint filed by public prosecutor on sanction u/s.197 against a magistrate for offences u/ss. 167, 465, 466 and 471 IPC was not barred by s. 195 or s. 476 - Appeal Dismissed.
