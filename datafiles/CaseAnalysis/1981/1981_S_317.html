Sukhvinder Pal Bipan Kumar v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 December 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, A.D. Koshal, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 286; (1982) 1 SCC 31; AIR 1982 SC 65; 1981(3) SCALE 1795; [1982] 2 S.C.R. 31; 1982 UJ 113

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative

Keywords: Suspension Of Licence, Punjab Food grains Defers Licensing and Price Control Order 1978

Summary: Administrative - Punjab Food grains Dealers Licensing and Price Control (First Amendment) Order, 1980, cl. 11(1) - Suspension of licence - Legality - Petitioners were food grains dealers in State, had suspended by orders passed by District Food and supplies Controllers, fit a period not exceeding 90 days under second proviso to cl. 11(1) of the Order - Petitioners preferred writ petitions before HC, which was dismissed - Petitions filed instant petitions challenging the validity of second proviso to cl. 11(1) of the Order - Whether respondents were justified in suspending the licences of petitioners - Held, second proviso to cl. 11 (1) of the Order furnished sufficient guidelines for exercise of power of suspension of a licence during pendency of or in contemplation of proceedings for cancellation thereof, and it did not suffer from vice of arbitrariness and was, not violative - Allegations in writ petitions were not sufficient to constitute an averment of mala fides so as to vitiate impugned orders of suspension - Court should be justified in refusing to carry out investigation into allegations of mala fides, if necessary particulars of charge making out a prima facie case were not given in petition - However, burden of establishing mala fides lies very heavily on person who alleges it - Petitioners who sought to invalidate impugned orders of suspension should establish charge of bad faith or bias or misuse by Govt. of its powers - Impugned orders of suspension ex facie show breaches of conditions of licence by petitioners - Petitions dismissed.
