Dr (Mrs) Kirti Deshmankar v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 266; (1991) 1 SCC 104; (1991) SCC (L&S) 388; 1991 (15) ATC 693; JT 1991 (5) SC 291; 1990(2) SCALE 471; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 355; [1990] supp1 S.C.R. 355

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Education - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Post-Graduate Course in Obstetrics and Gynaecology - Admission to - Whether admission of respondent 5 a foreign national was valid in absence of clearance from Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, it being a condition precedent for admission? - The role of the Ministry of External Affairs is distinctly different from that of the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, and a certificate of no objection by one department cannot be a substitute for the clearance by the other - Scrutiny by the Ministry of External Affairs is made with a view to screen the person concerned to find out whether he is desirable person at all to enjoy the hospitality of the country in the background of various relevant factors in this regard - So far, the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare is concerned, it has to take into account the question whether a seat for the medical course either up to the degree standard or the post-graduate stage can be spared for a foreign national - The State has to spend a large sum of money in running institutions of higher technical education and the seats are limited - In such a situation a seat can be allotted to a foreign national only at the cost of a citizen of this country - The College and Hospital Council was, therefore, not right in deciding to admit respondent 5 on the strength of no objection certificate by the Ministry of External Affairs - Held, instruction is to ensure that no foreign national is allowed to occupy a seal ordinarily meant for citizen of country without permission of Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, Govt. of India, and once hurdle is cleared, purpose is fully satisfied - After production of necessary clearance, there does not remain any reason for rejecting claim of a more meritorious candidate - Respondent 5 having secured higher percentage of marks than appellant in MBBS examination was adjudged a better candidate on merits - Appeal dismissed.
