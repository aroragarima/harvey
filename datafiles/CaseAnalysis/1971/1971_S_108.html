Standard Refinery & Distillery Ltd v Commissioner Of Income-Tax, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

18 January 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 528; (1971) 1 SCC 315; AIR 1971 SC 2293; [1971] 79 ITR 589; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 378; 1971 UJ 187

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 24(2) - Whether the business of the company of dealing in shares and the business of manufacturing sugar and other commodities constitute the same business within the meaning of s. 24(2) of the Act, in force in the year of assessment.

Held, Tribunal found that there was complete unity of control and shares were one of a number of commodities in which the company dealt in the ordinary course of business and that there was no element of diversity or distinction or separateness about the transaction in shares, and accordingly upheld the claim. On a reference the HC held that the essential matter to be considered was the nature of the two lines of business and not merely their unity of control and that therefore the Tribunal erred in holding that the whole trading activity formed one business. Reversing the decision of the HC, SC ruled that the decisive test was unity of control and not the nature of the two lines of business. Appeal allowed.
