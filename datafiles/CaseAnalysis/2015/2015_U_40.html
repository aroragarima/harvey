Union of India and others v Major General Shri Kant Sharma and others

Supreme Court of India

11 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 183; (2015) 6 SCC 773; AIR 2015 SC 2465; 2015 (1) CLT(SC) 324; 2015(3) SCALE 546; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 676; 2015 (4) SLR 610

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Armed Forces, Air Force Act, 1950, Navy Act, 1957, Armed Forces Tribunal Act, 2007

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Armed Forces Tribunal Act, 2007, ss. 30, 31 - Constitution of India,1950, arts.226,136(2) - Right to appeal - Jurisdiction - Respondents moved before Tribunal for adjudication of disputes and complaints with respect to condition of service - Having not granted relief, army personnel assailed order passed by Tribunal before HC - Appellant challenged same before HC who granted relief - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether right of appeal u/s.30 of the Act against order of Tribunal u/s.31 of the Act or leave granted by HC to appeal before SC u/art.136(2) of the Constitution will bar jurisdiction of HC u/art.226 of the Constitution.

Held, s.30 of the Act provides for an appeal to this Court subject to leave granted u/s.31 of the Act. By art.136(2) of the Constitution appellate jurisdiction of this Court u/art.136 of the Constitution has been excluded in relation to any judgment, determination, sentence or order passed or made by Tribunal constituted by or under any law relating to armed forces. If any person aggrieved by order of Tribunal, moves before HC u/art.226 and HC entertains petition and passes judgment or order, then that person cannot challenge both orders in one joint appeal. Once, HC entertains petition u/art.226 of the Constitution against order of Tribunal and decides matter, person who approached HC will also be precluded from filing an appeal u/s.30 of the Act with leave to appeal u/s.31 of the Act against order of Tribunal as he cannot challenge order passed by HC u/art.226 of the Constitution. Thus, HC while entertaining petition u/art.226 of the Constitution bypassed machinery created u/ss.30,31 of Act. Therefore, judgments passed by HC are set aside. Appeals disposed of.
