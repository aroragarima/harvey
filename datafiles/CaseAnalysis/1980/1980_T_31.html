Â P. N. Eswara Iyer v The Registrar, Supreme Court Of India

Supreme Court of India

01 February 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, D.A. Desai, R.S. Pathak, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 100; (1980) 4 SCC 680; AIR 1980 SC 808; 1980 (2) SCJ 119; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 889

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Tort

Summary: Constitution - Constitution, arts.14, 19, 21, 137 and 145(e) - Supreme Court Rules, 1966, O. 40, rr. 2 and 3 - Constitutionality of changes introduced by Amendment of 1978 Challenged - And maintainability of challenge in bunch of cases challenges the vires of a recent amendment made by the Supreme Court u/art. 145 in the matter of review petitions whereby the judges will decide in circulation, without the aid of oral submissions, whether there is merit in the motion and, in their discretion, choose to hear further arguments in court - Held, not ultra vires the Constitution - Cannot be challenged - Petition dismissed.
