Bharat Karsondas Thakkar v Kiran Construction Company And Others

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 674; (2009) 13 SCC 46; 2009(8) SCALE 467; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 515

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of

Summary: Land & Property - Lease - Grant of lease of certain lands for 999 years in favour of one Sowar Ramji Vaity in 1949 by Collector of Thane - After death four legal representatives(Vaitys) left - Rights and interests in the land agreed to be transferred to one K.L. Danani (respondent 44) vide agreement D/-01-10-1973, who subsequently constituted partnership firm with three others; possession of the lands were handed over in part performance of agreement, and sale was still uncompleted at that point of time - Passing of Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976 - R44 filed statement u/s. 6 of Act - Further, Govt. of Maharashtra executed lease in favour of Vaitys for 60 years - On 18-06-1979 Vaitys transferred development rights in lands to one M/s. Modern Development Corporation (R24) who in turn on 24-08-1979 entered into agreement with one Kiran Construction (R1) wherein beneficial interest was transferred in such lands - Suit 252 of 1980 was than filed by appellant and one other partner against KLD and one other for declaration that they were retired from the firm; a notice of motion was filed in such suit wherein an undertaking by KLD not to part with the lands till disposal of suit was given; such notice of motion was disposed of by appointing Official receiver for the lands - Agreement with M/s. R24 terminated on 15-05-1981; suit no. 1578 filed by R1 thereagainst for specific performance of 1973 and 1979 agreements and further restraint order was obtained vide interim order, as to non-dealing with lands in any way till disposal of such suit - Death of one of the Vaitys in 1994; all parties to suit no. 252 entered into settlement with Vaitys, KLD and R47 and on the basis of such settlement suit no. 252 was decreed - In suit no. 1578 notices of motion were taken out (140/1999 and 2700/1999) by R48 and Vaitys respectively against restraint order - R1 took out Chamber Summons 1203/2000 seeking amendment of plaint for impleading two R13 and R19 as defendants and for incorporating challenge to decree passed in suit no. 252 - High Court dismissed such Chamber summons and allowed notices of motion by Vaitys and R48 - Three appeals preferred by R1, one against dismissal of his Chamber summons, other two against allowing of notices of motion by receiver and R48, which was allowed by Division Bench by its three different orders - Thus the present appeal - Whether judgement of HC in its Division Bench, proper? - Held, No - Order of HC wherein amendment in plaint was allowed was disposed of as dismissed but through inadvertence appeal against rest of two orders passed on notices of motion for vacating interim order were remained undisposed - Disposing them, Court observed that once amendment sought was rejected question of restraining Vaitys and partners of firm from dealing with the suit properties will not arise - There was no privity of contract between R1 Kiran Construction and the Vaitys; R1's claim thus is restricted to Modern Development Corporation (R24) alone and is also dependent upon the right of R24 to specific performance of its agreement with the Vaitys; claim attempted to be set up by R1 found to be highly tenuous and entirely dependent upon the claim of R24 - Interim order of injunction cannot be continued in such circumstances and thus has to be vacated - Appeals by official receiver and Vaitys allowed - Appeals disposed of.
