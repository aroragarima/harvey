State of Kerala and others v Kerala Rare Earth and Minerals Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 1062; (2016) 6 SCC 323; 2016(4) SCALE 67

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Grant Of Mining Lease, Production, Minerals, Mineral Concession Rules, 1960, Atomic Energy Act, 1962, Mining Leases, Mining Operations, Mineral Development, Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: T.S. Thakur

(A) Mines & Minerals - Administrative - Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957, ss. 10, 11(5), 17A, 17A(2), 18, 2, 30 - Mining leases - Grant - Validity - Appellants/State Govt. sanctioned grant of mining leases for a period of 20 years u/s. 11(5) of the Act - Respondents filed revision applications u/s. 30 of the Act before UOI - UOI allowed applications and remanded matters to appellants to reconsider the cases - Appellants by an order rejected revision applications on ground that it had the power and control over minerals lying in the land within its territory and was entitled to safeguard the same in larger public interest - Aggrieved, respondents filed writ petitions before HC challenging impugned order - Single Judge allowed petitions quashed impugned order and remitted back to appellants to consider the applications in accordance with law - On appeals, DB dismissed appeals affirming impugned order of Single Judge - Hence instant appeals - Whether State Govt. has the right to decline leases on the ground that minerals or the areas where the same were found have been reserved for exploitation by govt. companies or corporations.

Held, State Govt. was the owner of mineral deposits in the lands which vest in Govt. as was the position in instant case, Parliament has by reason of the declaration made in s. 2 of the Act acquired complete dominion over the legislative field covered by said legislation. The Act does not denude State of the ownership of minerals situate within its territories but there was no manner of doubt that it regulates to the extent set out in the provisions of the Act development of mines and minerals in Country. It follows that if State Govt. proposes to reserve any area for exploitation by State owned corporation or company, it should resort to making of such reservation in terms of s. 17A of the Act with the approval of Central Govt. and by a notification specifying boundaries of the area and mineral or minerals in respect of which such areas would be reserved. State Govt. have not so far issued any notification in terms of s. 17A of the Act Industrial Policy - 2007 of State Govt. does not have the effect of making a valid reservation within the comprehension of s. 17A of the Act. HC was justified in holding that there was no valid reservation as at present no matter govt. could make such a reservation if so advised in the manner prescribed by law. No reason for SC to interfere with the judgment and order passed by HC. Appeals disposed of.

R. Banumathi

(B) Mines & Minerals - Administrative - Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957, ss. 10, 11(5), 17A, 17A(2), 18, 2, 30 - Mineral Concession Rules, 1960, r. 59 - State policy - Prior approval - Applicability - Whether State Govt's policy of reservation for exploitation of beach sand minerals by its Public Sector Undertakings was untenable on the ground of non-compliance of the procedure stipulated u/s. 17A(2) of the Act.

Held, Industrial Policy of State could be said to be a prelude before State makes the proposal reserving the area for exploitation of the mineral by public sector undertakings. Respondent was not right in contending that under the guise of policy decision, State has bye-passed the procedure stipulated u/s. 17A(2) of the Act. Authority of State to make reservation of a particular mining area within its territory for its own use was the offspring of State's authority of ownership over the mines and minerals. S. 17A(2) of the Act reserves the power of State Govt. with the approval of Central Govt. to reserve any area not already held under prospecting licence or mining lease. S. 17A(2) of the Act uses the words "with the approval of Central Govt." and does not use the expression "prior approval". Therefore, what logically follows from s. 17A(2) of the Act was that State Govt. might seek approval of Central Govt. even after framing of the policy. Appeals disposed of.
