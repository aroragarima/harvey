Janata Dal and Others v H.S. Chowdhary and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 August 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 231; (1991) 3 SCC 756; (1991) SCC (Cr) 933; AIR 1991 SC 756; AIR 1993 SCW 248; 1992 (1) CCR 71; 1991 (3) Crimes 337; 1991 CRLJ 838; JT 1991 (3) SC 497; 1991(2) SCALE 400; [1991] 3 S.C.R. 752

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act 1947 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - FIR - PIL - Locus standi - CBI registered FIR dt.22-1-90 for offence punishable u/s. 120-B r/w ss. 161, 162, 163, 164 and 165A of IPC r/w ss. 5(2), 5(1)(d) and 5(2)/5(1)(c) of 1947 Act r/w ss. 409,420,468 and 471 of IPC against 14 accused - Rest of 11 accused were stated in general as Directors/employees/holders/beneficiaries of account code and public servants of Govt. of India - Core of allegations was that these accused entered into a criminal conspiracy, obtained illegal gratification in the form of money from BOFORS, a Swedish company through agent firms/companies/persons as motive or reward for such public servants who by corrupt or illegal means or by otherwise dishonestly using their official position as public servants caused pecuniary advantage to themselves, BOFORS, the agents and others in awarding contracts to BOFORS for supply of guns to Govt. of India and in transaction also committed offences of criminal breach of trust, cheating of Union of India, forgery and using of forged documents etc. - CBI filed application before Special Judge, stating inter alia that investigation of case had to be conducted not only in India, but also in Switzerland, Sweden and other countries, to collect documentary and oral evidence relating to all aspects of accounts in banks in Switzerland to which remittances were made by Bofors - In said application after referring to exchange of letters dt.20-2-89 between Govt. of India and Switzerland for mutual assistance agreeing that Authorities of both countries should provide to each other widest measure for assistance in investigation of criminal matters, it was stated that competent authority to ask for assistance in India and abroad was the Court/Tribunal/Judge or Magistrate exercising jurisdiction - Special Judge allowed said application of CBI - When matter stood thus, 'A' on strength of notification issued by Administrator of Union Territory of Delhi assumed charge as a Special Judge in place of 'J' - Before 'A', Special Judge, Advocate filed PIL - Special Judge dismissed petition holding that question as to whether any action u/s. 340 of CrPC was to be initiated or not - No opinion on the merits of main case was being expressed - On revision - HC passed an order directing all applications for intervention to be kept on record and observed that interveners would be heard only if Court feels the necessity of hearing further arguments after conclusions of arguments of Govt. of India and CBI - On appeal, SC held that interveners also have no right to seek for impleadment or intervention and that Judge having held so, took suomoto cognizance of matter for reasons assigned in his order and directed issue of show cause notice to CBI and State (Union of India) as to why proceedings initiated on strenth of FIR, pending before Special Judge be not quashed - Hence instant appeals - Held, initiation of instant proceedings by 'C' u/art. 51-A of Constitution could not come within true meaning and scope of PIL - Suo moto action of 'C' in taking cognizance in exercise of powers u/ss. 397 and 401 r/w s. 482 of CrPC based on convoluted and strained reasoning and directing office of HC to register a case under title Court on its motion - Hence, directions of 'C' calling upon CBI and State to show cause as to why proceedings initiated on strength of FIR be not quashed, could not be sustained - Thus, SC agree with' first part of the Order of 'C' holding that 'C' and other intervening parties had no locus standi - However, SC set aside second part of impugned order whereby he had taken suo moto cognizance and issued show cause notice to State and CBI and accordingly Show cause notice issued by him was quashed - All proceedings initiated in pursuance of FIR relating to Crime on the file of Special Judge, including issuance of letter request as they stand now, remain unaffected and they could be proceeded with in accordance with law - Appeals disposed of.
