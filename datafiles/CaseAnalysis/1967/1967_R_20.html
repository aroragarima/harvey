Raja Mohan Raja Bahadur v Commissioner of Income Tax, U.P

Supreme Court of India

06 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 126; AIR 1968 SC 114; [1967] 66 ITR 378; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 482

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Uttar Pradesh Encumbered Estates Act, 1934 - (A) Whether receipt of Encumbered Estate Bonds during previous year 1947-48 amounted to receipt of cash during that previous year and not during previous year 1948-49 when Bonds were in fact sold at less than their face value? - Held, Govt. of State undertook to pay amount of bonds in satisfaction of liability of debtor - Liability of original debtor was extinguished and a fresh obligation was undertaken by State Govt. in substitution of original liability - Govt. had right to recover amount due under bonds from landholder, but on that account Govt. did not become agent of landholder for payment of his debts - Income was received when Bonds were received - (B) Whether in circumstances of case, mere receipt of Encumbered Estate Bonds was tantamount to receipt of income assessable in year 1948-49? - Further held, income tax authorities have brought to tax difference between face value of bonds and principal which remained due in relevant previous year - But what was taxable was only that income which represented difference between amount due as principal and market value of bonds (payable in twenty instalments) at date of receipt Respondent agrees to make necessary adjustment - In view of respondent's undertaking it is not necessary to modify High Court order - Appeal dismissed.
