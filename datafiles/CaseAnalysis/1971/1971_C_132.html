Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal Ii v Durga Prasad More

Supreme Court of India

26 August 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 405; AIR 1971 SC 2439; 1973 CTR 500; [1971] 82 ITR 540

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1922, ss. 66(1) and 66 (2) - A/y 1958-59 and 1959-60 - Addition - Income from property - Assessee claimed before Income-Tax Officer (ITO) that the income of the impugned property should not be taxed in his hands as it was trust property in his hands - ITO rejected said claim and included that income in the total income of the assessee - The Appellate Assistant Commissioner and the Income-tax Appellate Tribunal affirmed the decision of the ITO - However, HC allowed the appeal filed against said order by the assessee - Hence, the present appeal - Whether tribunal was right in holding that the impugned property was not Trust property? - Held, the orders of the Income-tax Officer, Appellate Assistant Commissioner and the Tribunal proceeded on the basis that the assessee was attempting to conceal a portion of his income by putting forward the story that the income from the impugned property is the income of the trust created by his wife - The proof of that charge depends on the correctness of the findings of those authorities - Assessee failed to prove that charge - Moreover, assessee kept a separate account in respect of the income and expenditure relating to the impugned property is of little evidentiary value if one takes into consideration the past history of the case - Hence, HC was not justified in directing the Tribunal to state a case and answer given by the HC to the question referred to it is unsustainable - Appeal allowed.
