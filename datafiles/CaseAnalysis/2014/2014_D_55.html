Saraladevi and others v Divisional Manager, Royal Sundaram Alliance Insurance Company Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

20 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 559; 2014 (3) ACC 898; 2014 ACJ 2391; 2014 (6) ALD(SC) 152; 2014 (106) ALR 906; 2014 (5) AWC 5323; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3260; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 194; 2014(9) SCALE 431

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Motor Accident Claim

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 166 - Motor accident claim - Enhancement of compensation - Appellants (claimants) filed claim petition u/s. 166 of the Act before Tribunal, seeking compensation on account of death of deceased in a Motor accident - Tribunal awarded compensation of Rs.37,33,248/- - Aggrieved appellant (Insurance) before HC sought for the modification of compensation awarded in favour of appellants by Tribunal contending that the same was excessive - HC re-determined compensation at Rs.15,84,750/- - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellants were entitled for enhancement of compensation -

Held, the HC erred in not considering the principles laid down in Smt. Sarla Verma & Ors. V. Delhi Transport Corporation & Anr 2009 Indlaw SC 488, in so far as deduction of 1/4th of the monthly income of the deceased to arrive at the multiplicand and reducing the compensation by adopting the split up multiplier. Further, recording the finding of contributory negligence on the part of the deceased in the absence of evidence on record in that regard rendered the finding erroneous in law and error in law as the same was contrary to the decision of SC in Jiju Kuruvila and Ors. v. Kunjujamma Mohan & Ors., 2013 Indlaw SC 355. At the time of death, deceased was 58 years old and was earning a salary of Rs.50,809/- per month i.e. Rs.6,09,708/- annually. By applying the appropriate multiplier of 8 as laid down in Kerala Road Transport Corporation v. Susamma Thomas, 1993 Indlaw SC 1302, the loss of dependency comes to Rs.48,77,708/-. Further, deduction towards personal expenses of the deceased out of the annual income would be 1/4th as held in 2009 Indlaw SC 488. The HC committed an error in law in deducting 1/3rd amount towards personal expenses of the deceased. Thus, as per judgment in 2009 Indlaw SC 488 the deduction ought to be 1/4th only as correctly calculated by Tribunal. Thus, after deducting 1/4th i.e. Rs.12,19,416/- towards personal expenses; the loss of dependency would be Rs.36,58,248/. Further, sum granted by Tribunal as Rs.5,000/- for funeral expenses, under the head of loss of estate at Rs.10,000/-, loss of consortium at Rs.10,000/- and Rs.50,000/- for loss of love and affection of the deceased was affirmed. Award of the Tribunal was restored. Appeal allowed.
