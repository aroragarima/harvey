Kalika Prasad and Another v Chhatrapal Singh (Dead) By Lrs

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1544; (1997) 2 SCC 544; AIR 1997 SC 1699; 1997 (4) CLT 17; JT 1997 (1) SC 372; 1997(1) SCALE 207; [1996] Supp10 S.C.R. 371; 1997 (1) Supreme 384

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 64 - M P Land Revenue Code, 1959, ss. 189 and 190 - Suit filed for declaration of title and possession - Adverse possession claimed by respondents - Possession was permissible under a Power of Attorney which was subsequently cancelled - Thereafter, estate was abolished and appellant obtained Patta u/s. 189 of Act - No notice was given to respondent - No action was taken to eject him from land and he remained in uninterrupted possession for 12 years - Whether respondents perfected title by adverse possession? - Held, respondents perfected title - Appeal dismissed.
