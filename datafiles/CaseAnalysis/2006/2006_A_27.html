Abhujit Gupta v S.N. B. National Centre, Basic Sciences and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 151; (2006) 4 SCC 469; AIR 2006 SC 3471; 2006 (3) AWC 2272; 2006 (3) CalLT(SC) 1; JT 2006 (5) SC 12; 2006(4) SCALE 380; 2006 (5) SCJ 416; 2007 (1) SLR 45; 2006 (2) UPLBEC 1638

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Petitioner was being appointed on probation for a period of one year - Order of termination - Whether discontinuation of probationer-appellant was for unsatisfactory services or for a misconduct? - Held, removal of appellant from service was only because respondents, after giving a long rope to appellant, had come to conclusion that appellant's service was unsatisfactory and there was no hope of his improvement - HC has correctly found that letter dated 07-04-1998 was not punitive in nature and stated, albeit in prolix fashion, that service of appellant were unsatisfactory - Appeal dismissed.
