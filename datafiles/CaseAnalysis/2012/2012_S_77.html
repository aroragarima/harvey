Gurminder Singh Kang v Shiv Prasad Singh & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 522; (2013) 11 SCC 332; AIR 2013 SC 520; 2013 (2) ALD(SC) 4; 2013 (96) ALR 698; 2013 (1) AWC 27; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 594; JT 2012 (12) SC 151; 2013 (1) MLJ 132; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 915; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 64; 2012(12) SCALE 1; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 240; 2013 (3) UPLBEC 1802

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Dismissal from service - Time Bound Promotions - Conditional re-appointment - Contempt of Court - Validity - Respondent No. 1 was In-charge Block Supply Officer and was dismissed from service in year 1977 on charges of bribery, by Commissioner, subsequently, considering his representation, he was reappointed on 28-2-1980, while reappointing him, order mentioned that respondent would get basic starting pay of Rs.296/- and will not be entitled for any future promotions, order became final and respondent was reappointed as per order - Respondent filed writ petition praying for direction to accord time bound promotion as per State Govt. scheme, HC, disposed of petition, by directing Commissioner to dispose representation of respondent by reasoned order - Respondent was granted time bound promotion and appellant- Commissioner in his order dt. 25-7-2003, held that grant of time bound promotion was in contravention of conditions contained in reappointment order and cancelled promotions - Aggrieved respondent filed writ petition, HC, held that order passed by appellant was in violation of specific orders passed in previous writ petition and issued show cause notice to appellant for contempt of court - HC, not satisfied with reply held appellant guilty of contempt of court and imposed punishment of 2 months SI and fine - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that he did not understand the order in totality as such passed such orders and since he had tendered unconditional apology, he should be dealt with leniently - Whether HC, was justified in holding appellant guilty and sentencing him for contempt of court - Held, order of SJ discloses that instead of displaying such fair conduct before Court, appellant appeared to have attempted to justify his action by resorting to an escape route and stated to have offered his regret and unconditional apology as a last resort to pardon him from being punished for any contempt action - Orders and judgments of Court are meant to be obeyed and not to be disobeyed, with impunity, appellant was a senior level IAS Officer with not less than 30 years of experience in State Administration came forward with a lame and flippant statement that he did not understand implication of order of HC which led him to pass such orders in total derogation of directions contained in orders of HC - SC, held that orders impugned in this appeal in having concluded that appellant committed contempt of its order dt. 21-08-95 does not call for interference, however, take into account age of appellant as well as the remorse conduct displayed imposed stern warning and relaxed SI and retained fine - Appeal disposed of.
