Industrial Development Corporation of Orissa Limited v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 July 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 935

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Coal Mines (Nationalisation) Act, 1973 - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957, ss. 8 and 18 - Mineral Conservation and Development Rules, 1988 - Mineral Concession Rules, rr. 59 and 60 - (A) Whether the High Court of Orissa was justified in striking down the decisions of Central Govt. D/- 03-06-1993 and 03-06-1993 on the ground that the requirement of s. 8(3) of the Mines Act had not been met? - Ordinarily, a lease is not to be granted beyond the time and number of periods mentioned in cls. (1) and (2) of s. 8 - If, however, the Central Govt. is of the view that to allow a lessee's lease to be renewed further would be in the interest of mineral development, then, it is empowered to do so, provided there exist on record sound reasons for such an action and those reasons are recorded - Held, the Central Govt.'s orders do not profess to give any reasons for such a decision and for that reason falls foul of the requirement of s. 8 (3) - (B) Whether Rao Committee 's report and decision of this Court in Indian Metals case, relevant for consideration of renewal of leases u/s. 8(3)? - It is clear from Indian Metals case that in the opinion of this Court, the Rao Report had made such a comprehensive study of issue that it merited treatment as a decision of the Central Govt. - Further Held, the Central Govt., though not bound to follow the recommendations of the Rao Report, was at the very least under an obligation to record reasons why it sought to depart from the recommendations of the Report, especially in matters such as these where leases of considerable commercial value are granted for long periods of time - (C) Whether the prospective applicants has any locus standi in proceedings considering the issue of renewal of TISCO's lease? - Vide order D/- 03-06-1993, the Central Govt. had, in exercise of powers conferred on it by r. 59(2), relaxed the requirement of r. 59(1) to enable the other parties to be granted leases and since these issues involve considerably high stakes, both in terms of commercial value and the effect that such a decision will have on the concept of mineral development and consequent national interest, to that extent, those likely to be affected and indeed, those who can legitimately have a stake in the proper formulation of such a vital policy, can be heard - Further Held, no exception can be taken to the High Court treating them as proper parties and directing the Committee to hear them - (D) Whether the Committee was justified in interpreting the concept of 'mineral development' u/s. 8(3) as requiring the assessment of captive mining requirement of different industries and the application of principle of equitable distribution of mining leases? - Captive requirement of an industry is a factor that has to be kept in mind while granting leases but, it is to be done on a comparative scale and while the Central Govt. exercises its discretion in granting or renewing a lease, it is clear that the capacity of an industry to effectively exploit the ore will be a predominant considerations; based on the information supplied to it, the Committee made an assessment for a total period of 50 years of the captive and net requirements of ICCL, IMFA, FACOR and JSL and this being a finding of fact that has been recorded by the Committee, the argument of captive consumption does have a basis in the facts of the present case - Further Held, the Committee had correctly interpreted the relevant material available for appreciating the concept of 'mineral development' and adopting the stance that it encompassed the concept of captive mining as well as the principle of equitable distribution - (E) Whether the Central Govt. in its order D/- 03-06-1993, had correctly analysed the needs and requirements of TISCO in recommending that it's lease be renewed over land measuring 406 hectares? - Central Govt. is vested with discretion to determine the policy regarding the grant or renewal of leases; on the matters affecting policy and those that require technical expertise, this Court has followed the recommendations of the Committee which is more qualified to address these issues - Further Held, the Central Govt. was justified in issuing its order D/- 03-06-1993 - Appeals dismissed.
