Shiromani and Others v Hem Kumar and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 376; AIR 1968 SC 1299; 1968 ALJ 1025; 1982 (8) ALR 748; 1969 (1) ALT(SC) 41; 1968 MahLJ 791; 1968 MPLJ 792; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 639

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Dharam

Summary: Family & Personal - Registration Act, 1908, s. 17(1)(b) - Respondents Nos. 1 and 3, and appellant No. 1, sons of late Dharam Singh('X') - Appellant No. 2 is the second wife of 'X' and mother of appellant No. 1., the mother of respondents 1 and 3 died before 'X' married appellant No. 2 - Admittedly parties are governed by Benares, School of Hindu Law - Validity of partition deed (Ex. D-4 ) was challenged - Appellants prayed that there should be a fresh partition by metes and bounds of joint family properties and they should be given 1/4 th share each therein - High Court viewed that deed, Ex. D-4 was neither an award nor was it a document effecting partition of immovable properties of value of more than Rs. 100 - It was held that Ex. D-4 was admissible in evidence in order to show that there was separation of status between the coparceners - Second Appeal was also dismissed - (A) Whether deed, Ex. D-4 is admissible in evidence? - Held, in view of the recitals in Ex. D-4 there is allotment of specific properties to individual coparceners and the document therefore falls within the mischief of s. 17(1)(b) of Registration Act - It follows that Ex. D-4 is not admissible in evidence to prove the title of any of the coparceners to any particular property or to prove that any particular property has ceased to be joint property - However, document is admissible to prove an intention on the part of coparceners to become divided in status, i.e., to prove that parties ceased to be joint from the date of the instrument - (B) Whether Hemkumar was entitled to a greater share of joint family properties for the reason that he was the eldest brother on the principle of 'Jethansi'? - Held, custom of Jethansi alleged on behalf of respondents has not been established by proper evidence and finding of HC is vitiated because it is not supported by proper evidence - Accordingly argument of respondents that Hemkumar was entitled to a larger share of the joint family properties on the basis of the alleged custom of Jethansi in his caste is rejected - (C) Whether appellants are entitled to a preliminary decree for partition of joint family properties? - Held, upon examination of evidence, court expressed its view that intention of the parties was that document Ex. D-4 should be the sole evidence of partition and since Ex. D-4 is not admissible in evidence on account of non-registration to establish when the property was so partitioned, it is manifest that no oral evidence is admissible to prove any subsequent partition having regard to provisions of s. 91 of Evidence Act - Therefore, appellants are entitled to a preliminary decree for partition of joint family properties - Appeal allowed.
