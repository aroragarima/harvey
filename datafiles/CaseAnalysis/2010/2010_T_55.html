Challa Jaya Bhaskar and others v Thungathurthi Surender and others

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 899; (2010) 13 SCC 348; 2011 (2) ALD(SC) 94; 2010 (4) ESC 611; JT 2010 (11) SC 633; 2010(11) SCALE 151; 2011 (2) SLR 296

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: A.P. Medical and Health Services Rules

Summary: Service - Special Rules for Andhra Pradesh Medical and Health Services, 1988, r. 14 - Seniority - Determination of - Appellants, already had a Post Graduate qualification, were appointed as Assistant Professors, however, respondent/Civil Assistant Surgeons were appointed as Assistant Professors who acquired the Post Graduate qualification subsequently - Number of vacancies aroused for the post of Professors and respondents were promoted to the post of professors disregarding the provisions of 1988 Special Rules - Since the department was not implementing the Special Rules, various Original Applications were filed before the State Administrative Tribunal - Appellants contended that service of respondent/Civil Assistant Surgeons without Post Graduate qualification cannot be counted for the purpose of determination of seniority with respect to the promotion to the post of Professors - Tribunal disposed off the petitioner with directions to the department to strictly follow the 1982 Special Rules issued under G.O.M. No.154 dated 4-5-2002 - Respondents challenged the Tribunal order before the HC - HC set aside the Tribunal order and directed the department to count the previous service of those respondent/Civil Assistant Surgeons, who had acquired their Post-Graduate degree subsequent to their entry into service as Civil Assistant Surgeons - Hence the present appeal - Whether persons who had joined earlier as Civil Assistant Surgeons, with only MBBS qualifications, and could not be appointed as Assistant Professors since they did not have Post Graduate qualifications, and were subsequently appointed as Assistant Professors after attaining such qualifications, would be entitled to seniority over others who had already been appointed as Assistant Professors earlier - Held, although there was no formal separation between teaching and non-teaching staff, there was a distinction that those belonging to the non-teaching line could not be appointed in the teaching line till they had acquired the Post-Graduate degree - Said distinction always remained till the 1988 Rules when teaching and non-teaching posts were treated as different cadres and the seniority in the teaching cadre was calculated from the date of their appointment as Assistant Professors - Even if the feeder post for appointment of Assistant Professors was Civil Assistant Surgeons at the initial stage, the said situation stood altered with the amendment of the Rules where under Assistant Professors could be recruited only by way of direct recruitment - Therefore, respondents who had obtained lateral transfer from the non-teaching to the teaching line, would not be entitled to carry their period of service as non-teaching staff for the purpose of computing their seniority in the cadre of Assistant Professors, since the basic qualification for being appointed as Assistant Professors in the teaching line was a Post-Graduate degree, which the respondents acquired during the course of their service as Civil Assistant Surgeons and were thereafter transferred to the teaching line - Appeals allowed.
