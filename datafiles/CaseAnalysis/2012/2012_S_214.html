Jodhbir Singh v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

03 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 493; (2012) 13 SCC 591; AIR 2013 SC 1; 2013 (1) CGLJ 190; 2013 (1) JCC 514; JT 2012 (11) SC 475; 2013 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 542; 2013 (1) MLJ(Crl) 350; 2013 (1) OLR 777; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 272; 2013 (1) RLW 573; 2012(11) SCALE 595; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 230

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000, s. 7A - Juvenile Justice Rules, 2007, r. 12 - Juvenile - Declaration - Dismissal - Sustainability - Appellant was apprehended by the SP/Anti-Smuggling Squad while they were waiting for a party to deliver the consignment of 2 kg Heroin - Case was registered u/ss. 21, 25, 29, 61 and 85 of the 1985 Act - An application was filed by appellant for sending the case against him to the Juvenile Justice Board for trial and produced certificates to prove that he was a juvenile - Trial Court dismissed the application - Aggrieved on revision, HC affirmed the order of Trial Court and dismissed the revision petition - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that committed a grave error in not properly appreciating the scope of s. 7A of the 2000 Act and r. 12 of the 2007 Rules - Further contended that the Courts have committed a grave error in placing reliance on the certificate issued by the village Chowkidar as against the certificate issued by the State Council for Education Research and Training, and the certificate dated 19-10-2000 issued by the Government High School - Whether - Held, an affidavit was filed by Dy. Superintendent of Police, State Special Operation Cell, who examined the genuineness of the certificates referred to in order - SC noticed the genuineness of the certificate issued by said departments had not been questioned - SC in Ashwani Kumar Saxena v. State of M.P, 2012 Indlaw SC 308 held that 'Age determination inquiry' had to be conducted u/s. 7A of the 2000 Act r/w r. 12 of the 2007 Rules - Where genuineness of the school leaving certificate had not been questioned, the Trial Court and the HC were not justified in placing reliance on certain statements made by, mother of appellant in the cross-examination - Trial Court also committed an error in placing reliance on the certificate issued by the village Chowkidar who was examined as RW2 - When the law gave prime importance to the date of birth certificate issued by the school first attended, the genuineness of which was not disputed, there was no question of placing reliance on the certificate issued by the village Chowkidar - Impugned order passed by Courts below was set aside - Appeal allowed.
