The Land Acquisition Officer v Shivabai & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3090; (1997) 9 SCC 710; AIR 1997 SC 2642; 1998 (1) AWC 399; 1997 (2) CLT 439; JT 1997 (5) SC 123; 1997(3) SCALE 751; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 647; 1997 (4) Supreme 550; 1997 (2) UJ 35

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land and Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 18 - Acquisition of land -Maintainability of application for reference for enhancement of compensation - Parties present on date of award and also received award without any protest - Held, notice as to award not necessary if parties are present at time of award - Claimants who receive compensation under protest and who have made application u/s. 18(1) are only alone entitled to seek reference - Third parties impleaded to reference cannot claim higher compensation - Order of reference Court enhancing compensation of such claimants is not legal - Appeal allowed.
