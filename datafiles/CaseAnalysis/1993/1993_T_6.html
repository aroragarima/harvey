Salil Dutta v T. M. and M. C. Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1128; (1993) 2 SCC 185; AIR 1993 SCW 1178; 1993 (1) CCC 635; JT 1993 (4) SC 525; 1993 (1) RentLR 332; 1993(1) SCALE 451; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 794; 1993 (1) UJ 743

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 9, r. 13, O. 14, r. 5, O. 6, r. 6, s. 151 - Trial Court dismissed application to set aside ex parte decree - Appeal was preferred by defendant to High Court, heard by a Division Bench and judgment pronounced in open court dismissing appeal - An application was moved by defendant for alteration or modification and/or reconsideration of said judgment mainly on notice of DB decision of Supreme Court in Rafiq 1981 Indlaw SC 297 - DB reopened case - Whether HC was right in reopening case? - Held, order of HC set aside - It is difficult to believe that defendants implicitly believed their advocate's advice - Being educated businessmen they would have known that non-participation at final hearing of suit would necessarily result in an adverse decision - Story set up by defendant in his application under O. 9, r. 3 is an after-thought and ought not to have been accepted by DB in its order - Appeal allowed.
