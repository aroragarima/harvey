Waryam Singh v Baldev Singh

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1371; (2003) 1 SCC 59; 2003 (1) AllCJ 577; 2002 (49) ALR 707; JT 2002 (9) SC 189; 2002 (2) RCR(Rent) 594; 2002(8) SCALE 270; 2002 (6) SLT 295; [2002] Supp4 S.C.R. 9; 2002 (7) Supreme 593

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Summary: East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1949 - S. 13(2)(iii) - Eviction Petition - ground of material alteration - whether enclosing the verandah would amount to impairing materially the value or utility of the shop - An Order for eviction can be passed only if the landlord proves (a) that the tenant had carried out the construction, (b) that the same was without the consent of the landlord and (c) that the value or utility had been materially impaired. - Held, in the case of a shop, particularly in a business locality, the area of the shop gets increased by the verandah getting enclosed, which would increase the value and utility of the shop. Further, by putting up a rolling shutter in the front the flow of light and air is increased. - Therefore, in the absence of any proof of material impairment in value or utility, the High Court was right in concluding that no decree for eviction could be passed.
