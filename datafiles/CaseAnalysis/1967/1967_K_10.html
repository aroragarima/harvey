Â Union of India v Kamalabai Harjivandas Parekh and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 September 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 515; AIR 1968 SC 377; 1968 (70) BomLR 316; 1968 MahLJ 542; 1968 MPLJ 573; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 463

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, ss. 4(1), 6, 7 and 8 - Requisitioning and Acquisition of Immovable Property Act, 1952, ss. 7(1)and 8(3) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(f) and 31(2) - Madras Electricity Supply Undertakings (Acquisition), 1954, ss. 4, 5 and 8 - Notification was issued u/s. 7(1) of Requisitioning and Acquisition of Immovable Property Act - To effect that land was being acquired by Govt. of India - Petitioner claimed a higher rate of compensation - Chief Judge, Court of Small Causes, Bombay was appointed as arbitrator u/s. 8 of Act - Whether provisions of s. 8(3) of Act were unconstitutional? - Held, u/art. 31(2) of Constitution, a person whose land was acquired was entitled to compensation i.e.,a 'just equivalent' of land of which he was deprived - Cls. (a) of s. 8(3) lays down a principle aimed at giving owner of land something which approximates its just equivalent on date of acquisition - Cls. (b), however directs arbitrator to measure price arrived at in terms of cl.(a) with twice amount of money which requisitioned property would have fetched if it had been sold on date of requisition and to ignore excess of price computed in terms of cl.(a) over that in terms of cls. (b) - Acting u/cls. (b), arbitrator would be acting arbitrarily inasmuch as he would be proceeding on a formula for which there is no rational basis - Court accept proposition that impugned clause satisfies requirements of art. 31(2) of Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
