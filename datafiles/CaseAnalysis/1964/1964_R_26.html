Â Town Municipal Committee, Amravati v Ramchandra Vasudeo Chimote and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 304; AIR 1964 SC 1166; 1964 (66) BomLR 492; [1964] 53 ITR 44; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 947

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution - Central Provinces and Berar Municipalities Act, 1922,ss. 66, 67 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 277 - Tax levied - Validity of tax imposed - Legislative incompetency - Petitioner/Municipal Committee was established under 1922 Act, Chapter IX of the Act deals with imposition, assessment and collection of taxes which should be imposed by petition - There was notification, under which list of goods liable to terminal tax imported into or exported out of Municipal area, not merely by rail, but also by road were added three new items and these three specified items were subjected to tax at same rates as was imposed on other articles by notifications which were in force from before the Constitution - Validity of tax imposed by notification was challenged by respondent no. 1 who was carrying on business within municipality on ground of legislative incompetency which was not saved by Art. 277 of the Constitution - Therefore, petition filed by petitioner was allowed by HC - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether tax which was being levied by municipal authority before the Constitution and for whose continuance article provided - Held, before tax could be collected from any tax payer, its quantum should be ascertained and assessed, and for this to be lawfully done there should be legislative sanction-in other words imposition of charge, because it was charge under Statute that was quantified by authorities acting under taxing enactment - Though, petitioner did not pass necessary resolutions and impose tax there was no charge levied on commodity, so that it could not be said that tax 'was being lawfully levied' before commencement of the Constitution - Therefore, words 'was being lawfully levied' obviously mean 'was actually levied' and it would not be sufficient to satisfy those words that Municipal authority could lawfully levy tax, but was not availed itself of that power - Though, last portion of art. 277 of the Constitution uses words 'continue to be levied' and to be applied to same purposes - Thus, word 'levied' were understood in sense that, there could be no 'application' of proceeds of tax to same purposes as at commencement of the Constitution - Hence, for exconcessis at that date there were no proceeds to be applied, where relationship between levy and application of tax was referred to as aid to construction of expression 'continue to be levied' in art. 277 of the Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
