Â Shivacharana Singh T. G. and Others v State of Mysore

Supreme Court of India

13 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 9; AIR 1965 SC 280; 1967 (15) FLR 224; 1967 (2) LLJ 246

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Mysore Civil Services Rules, 1958 - Termination - Validity - Whether order passed for terminating petitioner from service is unjustified? - Allegations made by petitioner held vague and unsatisfactory - Validity of impugned note cannot be challenged - Whether or not petitioner's retirement was in public interest, is a matter for State Govt. to consider, and as to plea that order is arbitrary and illegal - Held, it is impossible to hold on material placed by petitioner that impugned order is malafide - Petition dismissed.
