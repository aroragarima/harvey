Swami Vivekanand College Of Education And Others v Union Of India & Ors

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 738; (2012) 1 SCC 642; 2012 (3) AWC 2773; 2012 (3) Bom.C.R. 659; [2011] 12 S.C.R. 941; 2011 (6) SLR 752; 2012 (1) UPLBEC 27

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: National Council for Teacher Education (Recognition Norms & Procedure) Regulations, 2005, NCTE Regulations, 2002, National Council for Teacher Education (Recognition Norm and Procedure) Regulations, 2009, National Council for Teacher Education (Recognition Norms & Procedure) (Amendment) Regulations, 2006

Summary: A. Constitution - Education - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(g) - National Council for Teacher Education Act, 1993, ss. 12, 12(k), 13 - National Council for Teacher Education (Recognition Norm and Procedure) Regulations, 2009 - National Council for Teacher Education (Recognition Norms & Procedure) (Amendment) Regulations, 2006 - National Council for Teacher Education (Recognition Norms & Procedure) Regulations, 2005, regs. 8(3), 8(4) - National Council for Teacher Education (Recognition Norms & Procedure) Regulations, 2007, regs. 8(3), 8(4), 8(5) - NCTE Regulations, 2009 - Accreditation Rules - Validity - Appellants-Institutions were permitted by National Council for Teacher Education (âCouncilâ) to take additional students for B.Ed. course - Council framed regns. 8(4) and 8(5) under Regulations, 2007 by notification dt. 10-12-2007 by which institutions were required to be accredited with National Assessment and Accreditation Council (âNAACâ) with Letter Grade B and those institutions which had been granted additional intake in teacher training courses after promulgation of Regulations, 2005 were required to get themselves accredited with NAAC with Letter Grade B before 1-4-2010 - Appellants challenged validity regns. 8(5) and 8(5) on grounds that right to establish and run their institutions enshrined u/art. 19(1)(g) of Constitution of India stood curtailed, unauthorized outsourcing of statutory functions, giving retrospective effect to Regulations and non-performance of statutory duties by Council - DB of HC dismissed petition holding that regn. 8(4) merely puts condition for making application that applicant should have itself accredited with NAAC with Letter Grade B and regn. 8(5) was prospective in nature - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Council had sub-delegated any of its functions and duties conferred by parent Act to NAAC u/regn. 8(4) of the 2007 Regulations and delegation was constitutional - Held, Council was empowered to cause inspection of any such institution in such manner as might be prescribed u/s. 13 of the 1993 Act - Council was authorized to recognise institutions offering course or training in teacher education u/s. 14 of the 1993 Act - Council was empowered u/s. 15 to grant permission in such form and in such manner as might be determined by regulations - S. 32 empowered Council to frame regulations and to lay down conditions for proper functioning of institution and conditions for granting recognition u/ss. 14(3)(a) and 15(3)(a)respectively of 1993 Act - Regn. 8(4) was in broad conformity with objects and policy of the 1993 Act and was not in conflict with any statutory or constitutional provisions, regulation made by Council should have to be held to be valid - Hence, Council was empowered to frame regulation laying down conditions for proper conduct of new course or training u/s. 15(3)(a) - Appeal dismissed.

B. Constitution - Education - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(g) - National Council for Teacher Education Act, 1993, ss. 12, 12(k), 13 - National Council for Teacher Education (Recognition Norm and Procedure) Regulations, 2009 - National Council for Teacher Education (Recognition Norms & Procedure) (Amendment) Regulations, 2006 - National Council for Teacher Education (Recognition Norms & Procedure) Regulations, 2005, regs. 8(3), 8(4) - National Council for Teacher Education (Recognition Norms & Procedure) Regulations, 2007, regs. 8(3), 8(4), 8(5) - NCTE Regulations, 2009 - Accreditation Rules - Validity - Whether regn. 8(5) of the 2007 Regulations was retrospective in nature affecting fundamental rights of appellants guaranteed u/art. 19(1)(g) of Constitution - Held, regns. 8(3) and 8(4) of the 2007 Regulations were already in vogue since 13-1-2006 when regn. dt. 27-12-2005 came into effect - Norms and standards were notified by the 2002 Regulations and retained in the 2005 Regulations- Regns. 8(3) and 8(4) of the 2007 Regulations remained in force for all teachers education courses even after amended regns. 2006 - When regns. 2007 were enacted, regns. 8(3) and 8(4) of the 2005 Regulations were retained - By regn. 8(5) it was clarified that if any institution had been granted additional intake in B.Ed. and B.P.Ed. teachers training courses after enactment of the 2005 Regulations, such institution was required to be accredited itself with NAAC with Letter Grade B - Regns. 8(3), 8(4) and 8(5) of the 2007 Regulations had nexus with maintenance of standards of teacher education and to make qualitative improvement in system of teacher education by phasing out sub-standard teaching, hence validity of regns. 8(4) and 8(5) should not be questioned - Hence, regn. 8(5) of the 2007 Regulations should not be held to be retrospective - Appeal dismissed.
