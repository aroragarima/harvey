Shabnam and another v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, Sharad Arvind Bobde, Arun Mishra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 501; (2015) 6 SCC 632; JT 2015 (5) SC 359; 2015(6) SCALE 433; 2016(6) SCALE 433; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 943

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Life Imprisonment, Jurisprudence, Death Sentence, Aggravating

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34 - Alleged Murder - Death Penalty awarded - Justification - On day of incident, upon hearing cries of appellant accused, PW1 neighbour entered house and found death bodies of seven members of family - It was alleged that appellant accused had illicit relationship which was not acceptable by her family and in order to acquire entire family property appellant accused committed murder of her family members - FIR was lodged u/s. 302 read with s. 34 of IPC - Trial Court convicted appellants accused for offence u/s. 302 read with s. 34 of IPC and sentenced them to death taking into consideration mitigating and aggravating circumstances - Death penalty imposed by Trial Court is affirmed by HC classifying case as 'rarest of rare' - Whether imposition of death penalty upon appellant accused is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, appellants-accused have put end to seven innocent lives while they lay asleep defenseless and unsuspecting, in safety of their own house, absolutely unaware of gory scheme of their daughter and her paramour. Further, mitigating circumstances regarding young age of appellants-accused at time of commission of crime do not bear any significance in terms of outweighing aggravating circumstances of their wanton act. SC held that appellant-accused persons' preparedness, active involvement, scheming execution and subsequent conduct reeks of calculated and motivated murders and act of slaughtering ten month old child by strangulation in no chance reflects immature action but evidence for lack of remorse, kindness and humanity. SC held that crime is committed in most cruel and inhuman manner which is extremely brutal, grotesque, diabolical and revolting. Hence, SC held that extreme culpability of both appellants-accused makes them most deserving for death penalty. Hence, order passed by Trial Court and HC confirming death penalty is affirmed. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - Death penalty is awarded taking into consideration aggravating and mitigating circumstances which include age of offender, manner in which crime was committed and various other factors.
