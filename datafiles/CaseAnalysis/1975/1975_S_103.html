A.B. Abdul Kadir & Ors v State Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

12 November 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, P.N. Bhagwati, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 298; (1976) 3 SCC 219; (1976) SCC (Tax) 270; AIR 1976 SC 182; 1976 (5) CTR 126; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 690; 1976 TAXLR 1293

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Sugarcane Cess (Validation) Act, 1961, Luxury Tax On Tobacco (Validation) Act, 1964

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Finance Act, 1950 - Luxury Tax On Tobacco Act, 1964 - Sugarcane Cess Act, 1961 - Dealers - License - Luxury Tax - Refund - Appellants were A class licensee dealers in tobacco and tobacco preparations - State enacted 1909 Act and A class licensees were to pay specified minimum fee and additional fee for additional quantity- Appellants filed W.P for refund of license fee as Tobacco Acts stood repealed by 1950 Act in extension of 1961 Act was dismissed - Aggrieved appellants appealed was allowed - Appellants filed W.P for balance amount of along with interest - During pendency 1964 Act was enacted - State. called appellants to pay refunded amount - Appellants filed petition on HC of Single bench was dismissed - Appellants appealed to Division Bench of HC was allowed and quashed demand notice issued for refund - Respondent appealed was remanded to HC - Remand affidavits were filed on behalf of appellants and respondent - Whether provisions of Luxury Tax on Tobacco enacted by State. was valid - Held, levy imposed had no nexus with manufacture of article, tax could not be regarded to be one in nature of excise duty - Legislatures had wide discretion in law of taxation as provisions of Act were protected by art. 304(b) of Constitution - It was within competence of State. to make law in respect of luxury tax and to recover that tax in shape of license fee for vend and stocking of tobacco - Validation of levy entailed converting character of collection from impermissible excise duty into permissible luxury tax would not render it unconstitutional - Legislature would be within its competence to enact law for recovery, though already refunded to party, partakes of nature of luxury tax in light of law - There would be no legal impediment to recover as luxury tax - Appeals disposed of.
