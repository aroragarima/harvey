Hiralal and Others v Badkulal and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 March 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 100; AIR 1953 SC 225; 1953 (1) MLJ(SC) 725; 1953 SCJ 315; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 758

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 64 - Acknowledgment of liability - Maintainability of - (A) Whether suit can be maintained merely on basis of an acknowledgment of liability? - Unconditional acknowledgment implies a promise to pay because that is natural inference if nothing is said to contrary - Held, an unqualified acknowledgment and statement of account under which entry had been made, were sufficient to furnish a cause of action to plaintiffs for maintaining present suit - (B) Whether reliance can be placed on testimony of munims ? - One of munim was dismissed by plaintiff - Another munim was sentenced to one year's imprisonment in a criminal case started by plaintiff - Further held, much reliance cannot be placed on statement of a dismissed and disgruntled employee - Appeal dismissed.
