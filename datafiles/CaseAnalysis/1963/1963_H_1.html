Â Union of India v H. C. Goel

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, J.R. Madholkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 293; AIR 1964 SC 364; 1964 ALJ 38; 1963 CurLJ 153; 1964 (9) FLR 161; 1964 (1) LLJ 38; 1964 (66) PLR 305; [1964] 4 S.C.R. 718

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Service - Government of India Act, 1935, s. 240 - Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, r. 5 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 311 - (A) Whether Govt. is competent to differ from findings of fact recorded by enquiry officer conducting a departmental enquiry against a delinquent Govt. servant? - Held, if enquiry officer makes findings against public servant, on respondent's contention Govt. can never re-examine matter, so that even if Govt. were erroneous, it must proceed on that basis - This proposition is entirely inconstant with Constitutional rights of appellant - (B) Whether High Court in dealing with a writ petition filed by a Govt. officer who has been dismissed from Govt. service is entitled to hold that conclusion reached by Govt. in regard to his misconduct is not supported by any evidence at all? - Further held, it is obvious that when learned Chief Justice refers to charges proved against Govt. servant, it is not intended to be suggested that findings made by enquiry officer in that behalf are final - Enquiry report along with evidence recorded constitute material on which Govt. has ultimately to act - That is only purpose of enquiry held by competent officer and report which he makes as a result of said enquiry - Appeal dismissed.
