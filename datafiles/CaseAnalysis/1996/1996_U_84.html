All India Indian Overseas Bank Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes Employees Association And Others v Union of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1835; (1996) 6 SCC 606; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 564; 1997 (29) ALR 140; JT 1996 (10) SC 287; 1996(7) SCALE 943; 1996 (6) SLR 6; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 295; 1996 (8) Supreme 298; 1997 (1) UJ 70

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Constitution (Sixty-Fifth Amendment) Act, 1990

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 338 - Constitution (Sixty-Fifth Amendment) Act, 1990 - National commission for Scheduled Castes/Scheduled Tribes directed executive director of Indian Overseas bank relating to reservations for Scheduled Castes/Scheduled Tribes - Whether National Commission has powers to issue directions in nature of interim injunction? - Held, powers of Civil Courts have been conferred on commission but only with limited powers - Powers of a Civil Court of granting injunctions, temporary or permanent, do not inhere in commission nor can such a power be inferred or derived from a reading of cl. (8) of art. 338 of Constitution - Commission has not been specifically granted any such power - High Court rightly set aside directive - Appeal dismissed.
