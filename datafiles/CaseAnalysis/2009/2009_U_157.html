T. N. Godavarman Thirumalpad v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, S.H. Kapadia, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2236; (2009) 17 SCC 776; 2009(12) SCALE 425

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Land & Property; Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Orissa Saw Mills and Saw Pits (Control) Act, 1991

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Orissa Saw Mills and Saw Pits (Control) Act, 1991 - Court passed order regarding closing of sawmills in State and large number of sawmills were closed - State was of view that for sawmills appropriate location could not be found as many of areas were covered by definition of forest - Applicant sought for to set up industrial estate(s) for establishment of sawmills for which they required amendment of the Act - Held, SC directed that urgent steps could be taken in that direction at earliest - Order accordingly.

(B) Mines & Minerals - Forest area - Determination of - Applicant was having mining activities and it was alleged that mining area was within reserved forest - Dispute arose regarding boundary of forest area - Survey of India had conducted survey and it was found that there was some discrepancy about area of Reserved Forest and there was also some dispute regarding boundary of forest area - Applicant sought for some measures should be taken in that regard - Held, applicant proposed to file representations to CEC and to amicus curiae - In case any representation was filed, CEC would file formal report on that application/representation - Order accordingly.

(C) Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Construction of road - Application was filed by Ministry of Home Affairs seeking permission for construction of road passing through Cold Desert Wildlife Sanctuary - CEC had examined and had filed its report - CEC had stated conditions in its report viz; that project authorities would strictly comply with conditions on which project was recommended by Chief Wildlife Warden and Standing Committee of NBWL and had also stated that 5% of project cost would be deposited in Compensatory Afforestation Fund - Held, conditions were acceptable to applicant - Subject to that payment and observations of conditions, project was cleared - Order accordingly.
