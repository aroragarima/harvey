Â Penu Balakrishna Iyer and Others v Sri Ariya M. Ramaswami Iyer and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 416; AIR 1965 SC 195; 1965 MLJ 10; 1965 (1) MLJ(SC) 10; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 49

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Respondent filed suit representing other residents of locality - Appellant accused of encroachments on public street - Subordinate judge granted order in favour of of appellants - Respondent preferred second appeal before High Court - HC reversed order - Appellant contended judgment not maintainable in view of method adopted by judge - Whether present appeal should be dismissed solely on ground that appellants did not apply for leave under relevant cl of Letters Patent of Madras HC? - Appeal cannot be rejected solely on ground that appellants did not prefer appeal before Division Bench of HC - Held, decree passed in second appeal by learned judge does not satisfy basic and legitimate requirements of judgment under code - Appeal allowed and second appeal remitted back to HC.
