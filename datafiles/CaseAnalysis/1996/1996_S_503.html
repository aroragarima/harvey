Haryana Unrecognised Schools Association v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 864; (1996) 4 SCC 225; (1996) SCC (L&S) 923; 1996 (3) AD(SC) 693; AIR 1996 SC 2108; 1996 (2) CCC 158; 1996 (1) CLR 1000; 1996 (2) CLT 257; 1996 (73) FLR 1086; JT 1996 (4) SC 363; 1996 LabIC 1809; 1996 (2) LLJ 639; 1996 (2) LLN 271; 1996(3) SCALE 685; 1996 (2) SLR 856; [1996] Supp1 S.C.R. 253; 1996 (3) Supreme 734; 1996 (2) UJ 209

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Minimum Wages Act 1948, ss.2(i), s.5(2), s.27, part 1, sch. Item 40 - Whether Teachers of educational institutions - Can be brought within purview of employee u/s 2(e) of the act - By adding the employment in educational institution in the Schedule by State Government - Validity of Government notification fixing minimum wages of such teachers - Held, teachers are not employed to do any skilled or unskilled or manual or clerical work and therefore could not be held to be an employee under s.2(i) - State Government is not entitled to fix the minimum wage of such teachers.
