Goodyear India Limited v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, S.P. Bharucha, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20081; (2001) 6 SCC 112; AIR 2001 SC 2603; JT 2001 (5) SC 545; 2001(4) SCALE 425; 2001 (5) SLT 339; 2001 (123) STC 577; 2001 (5) Supreme 226

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Haryana General Sales Tax (Amendment and Validation) Act, 1983

Summary: Sales Tax - Haryana General Sales Tax Act, 1973, ss. 9 and 15 - Haryana General Sales Tax (Amendment and Validation) Act, 1983 - Whether Sales Tax Authorities can raise demands on assessee on basis of reassessment orders when original assessment orders for same assessment years and for same turnover have been held to be valid? - As a consequence of revival and restoration of original orders of assessment, authorities cannot base their demand on orders of reassessment - Held, respondents cannot now rely upon orders of reassessment - Reassessment orders may have been valid when made but on restoration and revival of original orders of assessment, in circumstances, state can recover tax calculated on basis only of original orders of assessment and is precluded from raising any demand of tax based upon reassessment orders - Question of interest is a matter of accounting between parties which aspect will be gone into and decided by assessing authority in light of this decision - Appeal allowed.
