Abdul Nawaz v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

10 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 641; 2012 (2) JCC 1490

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Weapon, FIR, Vessel, Spot, Place Of Occurrence, Version, Head Constable, Obstruction, Scuffle, Registered, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 109, 120B, 201, 300, 302, 304, 332, 34, 341, 392, 409, 411 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant/accused got into a scuffle with deceased, allegedly inflicted injury on the head of deceased with dao who died instantly - Charge-sheet was filed against accused for offences punishable u/ss. 302/392/411/201/120B/341/109 IPC - Trial Court convicted appellant u/s. 302 IPC and sentenced him to undergo imprisonment for life - On appeal, HC dismissed appeal and upheld conviction and sentence of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, in instant case, injury on the head and push into the sea have to be construed as one single act which appellant ought to have known was likely to cause death of deceased. Prosecution evidence sufficiently suggests that a scuffle had indeed taken place on the dinghy where appellant and his companions were trying to recover the dinghy while deceased was preventing them from doing so. In the course of that sudden fight and in the heat of passion appellant assaulted deceased and pushed him into the sea eventually resulting in his death. The act of appellant was more appropriately punishable u/s. 304(I) IPC instead of s. 302 IPC invoked by Courts below. Impugned conviction of appellant for the offence of murder u/s. 302 IPC, we convict him for culpable homicide not amounting to murder punishable under Section 304(I) of the IPC and sentence him to undergo imprisonment for a period of eight years. Sentence of fine and imprisonment in default of payment of fine is, however, affirmed. Appeal partly allowed.
