Janardan Dattuappa Bondre and another v Govind Prasad Shivprasad Choudhary and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 394; (1979) 4 SCC 516; AIR 1979 SC 1617; 1979 MahLJ 526; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 897; 1979 UJ 532

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Notice Of Recrimination

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 97 and 116 A - General elections to Legislative Assembly of Maharashtra - Appellant was declared elected - Election of appellant was questioned - High Court did not find substance in most of grounds raised in election petition, but allowed fresh scrutiny and re-count of votes - HC declared election of appellant void and order declaring fifth respondent duly elected is justified - Challenged - Mistake committed by Returning Officer while packing ballot papers in two envelopes of appellant and respondent - Appellant was concerned with his claim to his 250 votes - Claim did not involve any reconsideration of validity of any votes, whether cast in his favour or any other candidate; what was called for was a mere mechanical process of counting - Held, when re-count was taken, HC had not yet concluded that election of appellant was invalid - It was in process of determining question, and question could properly be determined only after giving to appellant benefit of all votes cast for him - HC erred in declining to court appellant's 250 votes in his total on ground that no notice of recrimination had been given - HC should not have declined to include in appellant's total 250 votes cast in favour of appellant, if those votes are included in appellant's total, appellant secures highest number of votes and is entitled to be declared elected - Further held, order of HC declaring election of appellant void and declaring fifth respondent duly elected is set aside, election petition is dismissed - Order accordingly.
