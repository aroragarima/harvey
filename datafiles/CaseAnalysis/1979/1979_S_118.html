Raj Kapoor and Others v State and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 85; (1980) 1 SCC 43; (1980) SCC (Cr) 72; 1980 (17) ACC 110; AIR 1980 SC 258; 1980 CRLJ 202; 1980 CrLR(SC) 64; 1980 MLJ 439; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 1081

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Media & Entertainment; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Film Censor

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Cinematograph Act, 1952 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 397 and 482 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Whether High Court was right in refusing to exercise its inherent power u/s. 482 of 1973 Act because subject fell under its revisional power u/s. 397? - Held, revision was rejected because a copy of order was not filed though original itself was in file - S. 482 is pervasive it should not subvert legal interdicts written into same Code in s. 397(2) - If impugned order clearly brings about a situation which is an abuse of process of interference by HC is absolutely necessary - Present case undoubtedly falls for exercise of power of HC in accordance with s. 482 of 1973 Code - Matter remitted back to HC.
