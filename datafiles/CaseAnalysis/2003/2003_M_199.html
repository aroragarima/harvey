Hindustan Petroleum Corporation Limited v Messrs Pinkcity Midway Petroleums

Supreme Court of India

23 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 537; (2003) 6 SCC 503; AIR 2003 SC 2881; 2003 (5) ALD(SC) 26; 2003 (52) ALR 577; 2003 (51) ARBLR 666; 2003 (4) AWC 2674; 2003 (4) JhrCR(SC) 140; JT 2003 (6) SC 1; 2003 (2) RAJ 599; 2003 (3) RCR(Civil) 686; 2003(5) SCALE 402; 2003 (4) SLT 553

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Standards Of Weights And Measures (Enforcement) Act, 1985, Weights and Measures Act, 1976

Summary: [A.] Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration And Conciliation Act, 1996, ss.16, 5, 8 - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 8(1)b - Essential Commodities Act, 1955, s. 3 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s.115, o. 39, rr. 1, 2 - Standards Of Weights And Measures (Enforcement) Act, 1985 - Petroleum Act, 1934 - Weights and Measures Act, 1976 - Whether arbitration clause found in Dealership Agreement does not apply to a dispute of nature which was pending in suit before Civil Judge and What would be role of Civil Court when an argument is raised that such an arbitration clause does not apply to facts of case in hand - Maintainability of petition u/s. 115 - Whether courts below have erred in coming to conclusion that appellant did not have legal authority to investigate and proceed against respondent for its alleged misconduct under terms of Dealership Agreement - Held, it is clear that if, as contended by a party in an agreement between parties before Civil Court, there is a clause for arbitration, it is mandatory for Civil Court to refer dispute to an arbitrator - Courts below ought not to have proceeded to examine applicability of arbitration cl to facts of case in hand but ought to have left that issue to be determined by Arbitral Tribunal as contemplated in Dealership Agreement and required u/ss. 8 and 16 of Act - Trial court failed to exercise its jurisdiction vested in it u/s. 115, when it rejected application of appellant filed u/ss. 8 and 5 of Act - In such a situation, refusal to refer dispute to arbitration would amount to failure of justice as also causing irreparable injury to appellant - Power conferred under Agreement does not in any manner conflict with statutory power under 1985 Act, nor does prescribed procedure under 1985 Act in regard to search and seizure and prosecution apply to power of appellant to suspend supply of its petroleum products to an erring dealer power exercised by appellant in such a situation is a contractual power under agreement and not a statutory one under 1985 Act - Existence of dual procedure; one under criminal law and other under contractual law is a well-accepted legal phenomenon in Indian jurisprudence - Appeal allowed.
