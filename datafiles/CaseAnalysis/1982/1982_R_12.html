Municipal Corporation of Delhi v Ram Kishan Rohtagi and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 December 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 186; (1983) 1 SCC 1; (1983) SCC (Cr) 115; (1983) SCC (L&S) 833; 1983 (20) ACC 50; AIR 1983 SC 67; 1983 (203) AllCriC 50; 1983 CRLJ 159; 1983 CrLR(SC) 9; 1982(2) SCALE 1124; [1983] 1 S.C.R. 884; 1983 UJ 148

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Food Adulteration

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Health & Drug - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, ss. 7, 16 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Substandard toffees - vicarious liability - Quashing of proceedings - Challenged - Respondent nos. 1 to 5 were manager (accused no. 3) and directors (accused nos. 4 to 7) of a company, which manufactured 'Morton Toffees' - Appellant (Corporation) officials found said toffees did not conform prescribed standards - Appellant filed a complaint against respondents for alleged offence u/s. 7 and 16 of the Act before Magistrate - Aggrieved respondents filed quash petition u/s. 482 of CrPC before HC, which was allowed by impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeal - Whether on allegations made in complaint, a clear case was made out against all respondents and HC ought not to have quashed proceedings on the ground that complaint did not disclose any offence - Held, proceedings against an accused in initial stages could quashed only if on the face of complaint or papers accompanying the same, no offence was constituted - Taking allegations and complaint as they were, without adding or subtracting anything, if no offence was made out then HC would be justified in quashing proceedings in exercise of its powers u/s. 482 of CrPC - In instant case, manager (accused no. 3) would undoubtedly be vicariously liable for offence; vicarious liability being an incident of an offence under the Act - Directors (respondent nos. 2 to 4) were concerned, there was not even a whisper nor a shred of evidence nor anything to show, apart from presumption drawn by complainant, that there was any act committed by Directors from which a reasonable inference could be drawn that they could also be vicariously liable - Thus, no case against Directors was made out ex facie on allegations made in complaint and proceedings against them were rightly quashed by HC - Manager of company and who directly incharge of its affairs, could not fall in same category as Directors - However, mere fact that proceedings were quashed against respondent nos. 2 to 5 would not prevent Court from exercising its discretion if it was fully satisfied that a case for taking cognizance against them was made out on additional evidence led before it - Sharda Prasad Sinha v. State of Bihar, 1976 Indlaw SC 238, referred to - Impugned judgment quashing proceedings against Manager was set aside - Appeal partly allowed.
