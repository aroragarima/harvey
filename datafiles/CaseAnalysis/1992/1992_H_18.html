Union of India v Hari Krishan Khosla (Dead) By Lrs

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, N. Venkatachala, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 605; (1993) Supp2 SCC 149; AIR 1993 SCW 105; JT 1992 (5) SC 574; 1992(2) SCALE 621; [1992] Supp1 S.C.R. 620; 1993 (1) UJ 68; 1993 (1) UPLBEC 145

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Maharashtra State Agricultural Lands (Ceiling on Holdings) Act, 1961, Defence of India Act, 1962, Constitution (Thirty-Ninth Amendment) Act, 1975, Requisitioned Land (Continuance of Powers) Act, 1947, Requisition of Land (Continuance of Powers) Act, 1947, Uttar Pradesh Town Improvement (Appeals) Act, 1920

Summary: Land & Property - Defence of India Act, 1962 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Constitution (Thirty-Ninth Amendment) Act, 1975 - Defence of India Act, 1939 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Bombay Town Planning Act, 1954 - Nagpur Improvement Trust Act, 1936 - Requisition of Land (Continuance of Powers) Act, 1947 - Requisitioning and Acquisition of Immovable Property Act, 1952 - Uttar Pradesh Town Improvement (Appeals) Act, 1920 - Maharashtra State Agricultural Lands (Ceiling On Holdings) Act, 1961 - Whether s. 8(3)(a) of Requisitioning and Acquisition of Immovable Property Act, 1952 is violative of art. 14 of constitution? - Held, s. 8 (3) (a) lays down a principle aimed at giving owner of land something which approximates its just equivalent on date of acquisition - When properties are acquired under Act there is a displacement of private ownership by ownership of State - However, sine qua non for such an acquisition is public purpose - S. 8(3)(a) provides for payment of just equivalent - If this be so, there is no scope for awarding solatium and interest - Absence of such a provision under Act would not, in any way, makes it violative of art. 14 of Constitution - Act had come to be included in Ninth Schedule of Constitution - Effect of such inclusion will be protection u/art. 31-B of Constitution - Appeals dismissed.
