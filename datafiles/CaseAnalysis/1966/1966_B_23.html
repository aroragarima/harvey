Ramchandra Spg. and Wvg. Mills v Bijli Cotton Mills and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 286; AIR 1967 SC 1344; 1967 ALJ 449; 1967 BLJR 427; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 301

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 2(2), 47, 90, O.21, rr.66, 84 and 90 - Whether an order by an executing Court setting aside an auction sale as a nullity is an appealable order? - Held, auction sale was ordered by executing Court in pursuance of decree passed against appellant - Appellant challenged auction sale and contended that order of trial court being interlocutory order under O.21, r. 84 and no appeal lies - Combined effect of s.2(2) and 47 makes order passed in execution proceeding a decree so far it conclusively determines the question arising between parties - Appeal against impugned order can be preferred u/s.90 - Appeal dismissed.
