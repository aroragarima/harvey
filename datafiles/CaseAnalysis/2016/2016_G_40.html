Governing Body, L. P. Shahi College, Patna and others v Seema Mishra and others

Supreme Court of India

05 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 709; (2016) 9 SCC 449; AIR 2016 SC 4595; 2016 (151) FLR 815; 2016(9) SCALE 644

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Violation Of, Selection, Date, Posts, Appeal, Advertisement, Universities, Bihar State Universities Act, Writ Petition, Post, Appointment, Bihar College Service Commission Act, 1976

Summary: Service - Bihar College Service Commission Act, 1976, ss. 2(9), 2(10) - Bihar State Universities Act, s. 57A - Appointments in colleges - Recommendations of Commission - In 1994, Commission issued advertisement for teaching posts in different colleges of undivided Bihar including two posts in appellant-college and Commission recommended two names Dr.Siyaram Sharma and Smt. Seema Mishra who was respondent no.1, to appellant-college - However, appellant-college appointed Dr.Siyaram Sharma and respondent no.1 was denied appointment and aggrieved by denial of appointment, respondent no.1 filed writ petition before HC - Single Judge of HC held that Commission intended to recommend two names against two posts and directed that respondent no.1 be appointed to second post of Lecturers w.e.f. the date when Dr.Siyaram Sharma joined post with notional benefits - Aggrieved, appellant-college went on appeal before DB of HC wherein it was held that Commission violated provision of s.2(9) of 1976 Act and had failed in its duty in not recommending the names of persons for second post which was advertised and was available and affirmed other directions of Single Judge - Hence instant appeals, one by appellant-college and another appeal by appellant no.2, who was lecturer appointed in appellant-college on temporary basis - Whether impugned judgment of HC could be upheld.

Held, u/s. 2(10) of 1976 Act, the Governing Body of the college could select for appointment from the names recommended by Commission. Thus, the Governing Body has the right to select the candidates in the order of preference. But as two posts were available, the Commission ought to have recommended two plus two candidates. Hence, HC rightly observed that Commission had violated mandatory provision of s.2(9) of 1976 Act. However, as there was violation of statutory provisions by respondent no.2-Commission, the name of respondent no.1 is to be considered for second post available and she should not be made to suffer injustice for no fault of her own. Further, in second post available, appellant no.2 is working on temporary basis and in violation of s. 57A of Bihar Universities Act, appellant-college continued him in said post and as such continuance of appellant no.2 in post was improper, arbitrary and in disregard of statutory provisions. However, appellant no.2 retired in January 2016, and said post is presently vacant. Since there was violation of s.2(9) of 1976 Act and that of s.57A of Bihar University Act, HC rightly allowed writ petition filed by respondent no.1, inter alia, issuing impugned directions. Hence, impugned judgment of HC is upheld and appeals are dismissed. Appeals disposed of.
