K. P. Singh v State of (NCT of Delhi)

Supreme Court of India

28 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 775; 2017 (1) CalLT(SC) 51; 2016 CRLJ 358; 2015 (1) JCC 2651; JT 2015 (9) SC 20; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 483

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Minimum Sentence, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, Special Reasons, gratification

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1)(d), 13(2), 7, 7(2), 8 - Alleged Bribe - conviction - Reduction of Sentence - HC vide impugned order affirmed conviction against appellant for offence punishable u/s. 8 of Act and reduced sentence awarded from 2 years to 1 year retaining Rs.5000/- fine imposed with default sentence of 2 months after re-appreciation of evidence - SC vide order issued notice to respondent to re-consider quantum of sentence subject to condition that appellant surrender to Jail to undergo sentence and file proof thereof within week - Further, appellant surrendered to Jail - Appellant stated that lower court have erred in convicting appellant despite fact that main accused has been acquitted for offence u/ss. 7,8, 13(1)(d) read with s. 13(2) of Act - Whether it is justified to reduce sentence of appellant.

Held, appellant served sentence for more than 7.5 months as per certificate dt. 6-9-2015 issued by Deputy Superintendent of Jail and has paid fine amount awarded by Lower Court which fine amount as sentence is affirmed by HC. SC held that having regard to facts and circumstances of case, particularly in light of fact that main accused no. 2, against whom charges were levelled u/ss. 7, 8, 13 (1) (d) read with s. 13 (2) of Act, was acquitted for want of evidence on record, SC held that justice would meet if period of sentence already undergone by appellant be treated as sentence to be imposed for conviction on charge framed against him. Hence, to that extent impugned order of sentence imposed by HC is modified with regard to period of sentence already undergone by appellant is treated as sentence imposed upon him for charge proved against him. Hence, Superintendent of Jail is directed to release appellant from custody, if he is not required in any other criminal case. Further, order passed by first judge of bench is confirmed by second judge. Further, it is held by second judge of bench that fact act that trial and appeal proceedings have in case at hand continued for nearly 17 years causing immense trauma, mental incarnation and anguish to appellant and also given fact that bribe amount was just about Rs.700/- and that appellant has already undergone 71/2 months against statutory minimum of 6 months imprisonment, reduction of sentence is justified. Appeal allowed.
