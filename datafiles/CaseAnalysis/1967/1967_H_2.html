Â Kanti Lal Babulal v H. C. Patel

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 178; AIR 1968 SC 445; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 735; 1968 (21) STC 174

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Sales Tax

Keywords: Collected

Summary: Sales Tax - Government of India Act, 1935 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 19(1)(f) and 286 (1)(a) - Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1946, s. 12 A(4) - Whether s. 12 A(4) is ultra vires Constitution being violative of art. 19(1)(f)? - Constitutional validity of a provision has to be determined on construing it reasonably - Power conferred u/s. 12 A(4) is arbitrary, unguided, uncanalised and uncontrolled - Held, a provision like s. 12 A(4) can hardly be considered reasonable and hence is ultra vires Constitution - Appeal allowed.
