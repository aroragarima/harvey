State of Madhya Pradesh v Kedia Leather and Liquor Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 666; (2003) 7 SCC 389; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1642; AIR 2003 SC 3236; AIR 2003 SCW 4611; 2003 (3) CCR 235; 2003 (4) Crimes 23; 2004 (1) CRJ 516; 2003 CRLJ 4335; 2003 (3) JCC 1383; JT 2003 (7) SC 370; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 543; 2003(6) SCALE 736; 2003 (1) SCW 4611; 2003 (5) SLT 100; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 727; 2003 (6) Supreme 213; 2004 (1) UC 35

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Water

Summary: [A.] Practice & Procedure - Water (Prevention And Control Of Pollution) Act, 1974, ss. 30, 32, 33 - Air (Prevention And Control Of Pollution) Act, 1981, ss. 18, 20, 22A - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, ss. 133, 144, 397 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 21 - To determine whether a later statute repeals by implication an earlier, it is necessary to scrutinise terms and consider true meaning and effect of earlier Act - Until this is done, it is impossible to ascertain whether any inconsistency exists between two enactments area of operation in Code and pollution laws in question are different with wholly different aims and objects and though they alleviate nuisance, that is not of identical nature - They operate in their respective fields and there is no impediment for their existence side by side - Further held, provisions of s 133, CrPC, 1973, are in nature of preventive measures, provisions contained in two Acts are not only curative but also preventive and penal provisions appear to be mutually exclusive and question of one replacing other does not arise.
