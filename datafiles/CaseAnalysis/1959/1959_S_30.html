Shri Chandrika Prasad Tripathi v Shri Siv Prasad Chanpuria and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 April 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 244; AIR 1959 SC 827; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 527

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Representation of People Act, 1951,s. 117 - Respondent challenged election of appellant to legislative assembly - Whether appellant election to legislative assembly is void? - Appellant raised objection that respondent had not complied provisions of s. 117 of 1951 act - Petition dismissed by Election Tribunal - High Court held that provisions of s. 117 had substantially complied by respondent so set aside order of Tribunal - Held, s. 117 should not be strictly or technically construed and Tribunal should not dismiss election petitions u/s. 90(3) on technical grounds if shown that there has been substantial compliance with its requirements - S. 117 subsequently amended by act 58 of 1958 and reference to secretary deleted - Appeal dismissed.
