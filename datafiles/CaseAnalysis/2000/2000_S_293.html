Gulam Hussain & Anr. , Shabnam , Roshan v State Of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 262; (2000) 7 SCC 254; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1343; AIR 2000 SC 2480; 2000 (3) CCR 111; 2000 (3) Crimes 142; 2000 CRLJ 3949; 2000 (2) JCC 565; JT 2000 (8) SC 466; 2000 (3) RCR(Criminal) 714; 2000(5) SCALE 430; 2000 (5) SLT 769; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 141; 2000 (5) Supreme 381

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32 - Dying declaration - Validity - Admissibility - Statement recorded by Police Officer - And not investigating officer - Witnesses to statement - Real brothers of deceased - Not supporting prosecution version - Magistrate not recording statement himself but dictated to police officer - Held, cannot be challenged - As police Officer did not possess capacity of investigating officer when investigation not commences - Prosecution fails to fully establish recording of dying declaration.
