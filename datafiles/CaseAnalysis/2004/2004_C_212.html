Commissioner of Police and Others v Acharya Jagdishwarananda Avadhuta and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, S. Rajendra Babu, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1449; (2004) 12 SCC 770; AIR 2004 SC 2984; JT 2004 (3) SC 224; 2004 (3) MLJ(SC) 28; 2004(3) SCALE 146; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 1019

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950 - Breach of peace - Freedom of religion - Respondents took out a procession with human skulls, daggers, trident etc - Police Authorities declared the assembly unlawful - Permission was refused by the Calcutta Police Commissioner - W.P. was filed by the respondents u/art. 32 of the Constitution in the court challenging the said order of the Police Authorities refusing permission - Direction was issued to petitioners that they were at liberty to go before the HC - Writ application was heard and allowed by the HC - Appellants filed an appeal before the Division Bench and obtained stay of operation of the said order of the learned Single Judge - Division Bench of the HC dismissed the stay application and the appeal and affirmed the order of the learned Single Judge of the HC of Calcutta - Hence this appeal - Whether the Division Bench of the HC was right in affirming the order of Single Judge of HC - Held, HC wrongly proceeded on the assumption that the finding of the Court regarding the non essential nature of Tandava dance to the Ananda Margi faith was due to the non availability of any literature or prescriptions by the founder - HC was under the wrong impression that an essential part of religion could be altered at any subsequent point of time - It was for the Court to decide whether a part or practice is an essential part or practice of a given religion - Practice of Tandava dance in public was not an essential part of Ananda Margi faith - Appeal dismissed.
