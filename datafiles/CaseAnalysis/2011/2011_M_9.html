The Greater Hyderabad Municipal Corporation v M. Prabhakar Rao

Supreme Court of India

28 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 471; (2011) 8 SCC 155; 2011 (5) ALD(SC) 144; 2011(5) ALL MR 969; 2011 (131) FLR 314; JT 2011 (8) SC 174; 2011 (7) MLJ(SC) 1232; 2011(8) SCALE 90; [2011] 9 S.C.R. 594; 2012 (1) SLR 282

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Fundamental Rules

Summary: Service - Fundamental Rules, 54-B(3) - Illegal gratification - Suspension - Back wages for suspension period - Entitlement - Respondent Bill Collector was placed under suspension by appellant competent authority for receiving illegal gratification - However, respondent was reinstated into service pending the criminal prosecution against him - HC subsequently acquitted the respondent from prosecution - Respondent sought back-wages and other consequential benefits for the suspension period, but the same was rejected by appellant on the ground that the suspension was not wholly unjustified - Respondent challenged the appellant's order before the Tribunal - Tribunal set aside the appellant's order- Appellant challenged the Tribunal order before HC - HC dismissed appellant's writ petition - Whether the respondent was entitled to back wages and consequential benefits for the period of his suspension - Held, HC, in the criminal prosecution against respondent, found that PW1, who made the complaint that the respondent had demanded illegal gratification, turned hostile and did not support the prosecution version - HC also held that the recovery of the amount was proved by the prosecution, but the purpose for which the amount was paid could not be proved and therefore the Trial Court rightly came to the conclusion that there was a doubt whether the amount that was paid to the respondent was towards illegal gratification - On these materials, the competent authority had formed the opinion in its impugned order that the suspension of the respondent should not be regarded as wholly unjustified and had declined to grant any salary and allowance to the respondent during the period of suspension - Opinion of the competent authority was a possible view on the materials which the competent authority could form in the facts and circumstances of the case while passing an order in exercise of his powers under sub-rule (3) of F.R. 54-B, declining to allow the salary and allowances of the respondent for the period of suspension - However, Tribunal had found fault with the impugned order and the reasons given by the it in its order were that the prosecution had failed to prove the case beyond reasonable doubt about the demand and acceptance of the bribe and the criminal court had acquitted the respondent and it was open for the authorities to proceed against the respondent departmentally, but no such departmental proceedings were initiated to prove the misconduct of the respondent - Approach of the Tribunal was not correct since sub-rule (3) of F.R. 54-B did not state that in case of acquittal in a criminal proceedings the employee was entitled to his salary and allowances for the period of suspension - Sub-rule (3) of F.R. 54-B also did not state that in such case of acquittal the employee would be entitled to his salary and allowances for the period of suspension unless the charge of misconduct against him was proved in the disciplinary proceedings - Sub-rule (3) of F.R. 54-B vested power in the competent authority to order that the employee would be paid the full pay and allowances for the period of suspension if he was of the opinion that the suspension of the employee was wholly unjustified - Hence, even where the employee was acquitted of the charges in the criminal trial for lack of evidence or otherwise, it was for the competent authority to form its opinion whether the suspension of the employee was wholly unjustified and so long as such opinion of the competent authority was a possible view in the facts and circumstances of the case and on the materials before him, such opinion of the competent authority would not be interfered by the Tribunal or the Court - Impugned order of HC and Tribunal were set aside - Appeal allowed.
