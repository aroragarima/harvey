R. Rudraiah and Another v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1619; (1998) 3 SCC 23; AIR 1998 SC 1070; JT 1998 (1) SC 435; 1998(1) SCALE 375; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 553; 1998 (3) SLT 351; 1998 (3) Supreme 244

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Land & Property - Delhi Urban Art Commission Act, 1973 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Karnataka Village Offices Abolition Act, 1961, ss. 121-A ,48-A - Occupancy right - Condonation of delay - Time barred - Period of limitation - Unambiguous - Application for grant of occupancy right was filed on dt.7-3-1984 beyond period prescribed by s.48-A of 1961 Act - Revisions filed in HC u/s.121-A of 1961 Act were allowed setting aside orders passed by appellate authority and by Land Tribunal on 1987 registering occupancy rights in favour of first appellant - Aggrieved by orders of HC - HC detained that time stood extended only for 6 months from dt.1-1-1979 i.e. up to dt.30-6-1979 and date being not in dispute application filed by appellant no.1 before Land Tribunal was time barred - Appellant No.1 sought to have period of limitation extended beyond dt.30-6-1979 by linking up commencement of limitation u/s.48-A of 1961 Act with certain orders passed in proceedings against respondent Nos.3,4,5 - Hence, instant appeal - Whether language in s.48-A of 1961 Act fixing a period of limitation was clear and unambiguous - Held, it was correct - Legislature wanted to make a deliberate departure and introduced an amendment to take away power of condonation of delay, it was difficult to accept that s.48-A 1961, Act was capable of more than one interpretation - No scope for implying any 'ambiguity' at all - HC was right in dismissing application while exercising revisional powers - Period of limitation for filing application u/s.45 of 1961 Act, was unambiguous and operates by its own force - Appeal disposed of.
