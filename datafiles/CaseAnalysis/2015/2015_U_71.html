T. M. Sampath and others v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

20 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 42; (2015) 5 SCC 333; JT 2015 (1) SC 417; 2015(1) SCALE 527; [2015] 1 S.C.R. 748; 2015 (2) SLR 769

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Provident Fund Rules, Contributory Provident Fund Rules (India), 1962, National Water Development Agency Contributory Fund Rules, 1982

Summary: (A) Service - Contributory Provident Fund Rules,1982 - Constitution of India,1950, art.12 - Pension scheme - Applicability of - Governing body of National Water Development Agency(NWDA) rejected proposal for introduction of Pension-cum-GPF-DCRG Scheme in NWDA - Appellants filed appeal before Tribunal assailing decision of Governing body rejecting their request to switch-over to Pension Scheme and letter issued by Finance Ministry whereby request of appellants to switch-over to Pension Scheme pursuant to Central Govt. Office Memorandum(O.M.) had been turned down - HC held that as NWDA had framed its own Rules thus, Bye-law 28 had no role to play - Hence, instant Appeals.

Held, NWDA had framed its regulation, the Rules and they were duly approved by Governing body of NWDA. As NWDA is autonomous body under Ministry of Water Resources, it has framed it own bye-laws governing employees. It has been time and again reiterated that Court must adopt attitude of total non-interference or minimal interference in matter of interpretation of the Rules framed by autonomous institutions. Hence, as appellants are governed by the Rules, O.M. applicable to Central Govt. employees is not applicable to them. On issue of parity between employees of NWDA and Central Govt. employees, even if it is assumed that the Rules did not exist or were not applicable on date of O.M., relevant date of parity, principle of parity cannot be applicable to employees of NWDA. Therefore, NWDA cannot be treated as instrumentality of State u/art.12 of the Constitution merely on basis that its funds are granted by Central Govt. Thus, employees of NWDA cannot be said to be 'Central Govt. Employees' as stated in O.M. for its applicability. Appeals dismissed.

(B) Service - Contributory Provident Fund Rules,1982 - Pension scheme - Applicability of - Employees of Samiti(JNVS) demanded that they be brought under GPF-cum-Pension Scheme like their counterparts in other educational institutions - Central Govt. formulated New Pension Scheme for employees of JNVS in response to their repeated demands - Cutoff date for New Pension Scheme was 01-01-2004; therefore, it was not available to employees who had joined service prior to cut-off date - Appellant filed petition in HC against Govt. to implement Pension Scheme under the Rules for employees of JNVS - HC held that New Pension Scheme which was formulated by Govt. in 2004 and notified in 2008 was in response to demands of employees of JNVS and that cut-off date was domain of employer and dismissed petition - Hence, instant Appeals.

Held, employees were not promised any pension at time of their appointment and no deductions were made during their service towards any pension fund. Thus, it cannot be said that employees have been denied what was rightfully theirs. Further, appellants had raised issue of New Pension Scheme which was notified in 2008 and whose cut-off date was 01-01-2004 in petition and SLP, however, it wasn't pressed during arguments. In any case, they have claimed that New Pension Scheme is also discriminatory and that said cut-off date is arbitrary. Cut-off date is domain of employer and so introduction of new scheme of pension will be done considering all relevant factors including financial viability of same. No interference is warranted unless there is gross injustice is perpetrated. Therefore, appellants have failed to prove any arbitrariness and discrimination with respect to New Pension Scheme. Appeals dismissed.
