Thomas Antony v Varkey Varkey

Supreme Court of India

15 November 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 824; (2000) 1 SCC 35; AIR 2000 SC 1; JT 1999 (9) SC 105; 2000 (1) KLT 12; 1999 (90) RD 796; 1999(7) SCALE 117; 2000 (1) SCJ 280; 1999 (9) SLT 583; [1999] Supp4 S.C.R. 431; 1999 (9) Supreme 274

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Summary: Agriculture & Agri. Products - Municipalities & Local Governments - Administrative - Kerala Land Reforms Act, 1963, ss. 125(3) and 74 - Kerala Land Reforms (Amendment) Act, 1969 - Respondent got possession of the suit property on the basis of the decree - From 1973-75 the Respondent engaged the Appellant to look after the cultivation work - Appellant created obstruction in the work - Appellant came upon the property and took possession of the same without any manner of right, title or interest therein - Trial Court referred matter to Land tribunal - Trial Court contrary to Land Tribunal decreed the suit with costs and directed the appellant to surrender possession - High Court dismissed appeal - (A) Whether Court had no jurisdiction to record a finding contrary to that recorded by the Land Tribunal in the reference made under s. 125(3) KLR Act, 1969? - S. 125 makes provision regarding bar of jurisdiction of Civil Courts - S. 125(1) lays down that no Civil Court shall have jurisdiction to settle, decide or deal with any question or to determine any matter which under KLR Act, 1969 is required to be settled, by the Land Tribunal - The proviso to s. 125(1) makes an exception in case of proceedings pending in any Court at the commencement of the Kerala Land Reforms (Amendment) Act, 1969 - When a question regarding status of a person as a tenant or as a kudikidappukaran arises in any suit or proceeding before a Civil Court that Court shall refer the matter to the Land Tribunal - On receipt of the decision of the Tribunal the Trial Court shall decide the suit or proceeding accepting the decision of the Tribunal on the question referred to it - While the Trial Court is to accept the decision of the Tribunal and base its decision in the suit or proceeding on the same, no such constraint is placed on the Appellate Court while deciding the appeal arising from the suit or proceeding - The legislative scheme appears to be that at the trial stage adjudication on the question should be confined to one forum i.e., the Tribunal and the Civil Court should not go into the very same question again after the decision of the Tribunal is received by it - In the present case the contrary finding recorded by the Trial Judge on the question of the claim of tenancy by the defendant is without jurisdiction and therefore unsustainable - It cannot be disputed that the question could be examined by the Appellate Court - S. 74 which makes the provision regarding prohibition of future tenancies declares that after the commencement of this KLR Act, 1969 no tenancy shall be created in respect of any land and that any tenancy created in contravention of the provisions of sub-s. (1) shall be invalid - Held, on the facts and circumstances of the case and in view of the legal position the Courts below rightly decreed the suit - Appeal dismissed.
