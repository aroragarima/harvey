Gautam Chand Jain and Others v Sushila Kumari Jain and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 515; (1985) Supp SCC 14; AIR 1986 SC 513; 1985 ALJ 1008

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, Order Of Release

Summary: Land & Property - Rent Control - Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, s. 21 - Suit for eviction - On ground of bonafide requirement for starting a clinic - Whether court below correctly determined comparative hardship issue in release order based on facts of case? - Held, in course of hearing of appeals learned counsel for respondents suggested that an alternative accommodation may be found out for tenants - Keeping in view fact that landlady has several premises and appeared to a person of status and influence - Adjourned matter with a view to giving an opportunity to her to suggest an alternative accommodation in neighbourhood of premises in dispute - On adjourned date, her learned counsel informed that such accommodation has not been found - That is a feature which justifies stand of tenants that premises are rare in Saharanpur as in any urban area in this country - If comparative hardship is taken into account, in backdrop indicated above, there is no scope to dispute conclusion that tenants would be harder hit and, therefore, their eviction should not be permitted - Application dismissed.
