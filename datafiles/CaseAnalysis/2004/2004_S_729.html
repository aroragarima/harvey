Balwant Singh and Another v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

12 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1403; (2004) 7 SCC 724; (2004) SCC (Cr) 2057

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304-B and 498A - Whether appellant 1 is guilty of offence u/s. 304-B IPC, 1860 and whether conviction u/s. 498A is warranted? - Held, u/s. 304-B, death in all probability should have been aftermath to such cruelty or harassment - At least for a year and three months before victim death there is no evidence to even remotely suggest that victim was subjected to cruelty or harassment of nature specified in s. 304-B, IPC, 1860 - Proximity test is, therefore, not satisfied - No sufficient evidence on basis of which conviction u/s. 304-B, IPC, 1860 can be founded - Further held, evidence on record leaves no room for doubt that victim was harassed and treated with cruelty about a month after marriage and thereafter, she was again treated with cruelty 2 months after she had gone back to her matrimonial home on persuasion of Panchayat - Facts clearly make out a case u/s. 498-A - Order accordingly.
