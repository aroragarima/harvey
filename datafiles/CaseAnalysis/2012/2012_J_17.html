Jayesh Dhanesh Goragandhi v Municipal Corporation Of Greater Mumbai & Ors

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 511; (2012) 13 SCC 305; AIR 2013 SC 882; 2013(1) ALL MR 892; 2013 (1) Bom.C.R. 316; JT 2012 (12) SC 220; 2012(11) SCALE 645; [2012] 12 S.C.R. 727

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Power Of State Government, Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning (Amendment) Act, 1993, Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning (Second Amendment) Act, 1972, Bombay Town Planning Act, 1919

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - The Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning Act, 1966, Chapter. VII and ss. 67, 86 and 126 - Land Acquisition - Through certain provisions - Denied - Challenged - Question that had come up for consideration before HC was whether after framing Town Planning Scheme and the final scheme brought into force, after reserving plots for public purposes, providing compensation under Chapter V of Act could the land owner insisted that the land be acquired only by following the provisions of Chapter VII of Act, especially u/s. 126 of Act - Held, SC's judgement in one case clearly indicated that the scheme of town planning under Act was code by itself, which had provision for determination of compensation, right of appeal, dispute resolution mechanism etc. - On detailed survey of the provisions of the Act and the related judgment interpreting the provisions of Act, it might be noted that the provisions of scheme contained in Chapter V of the Act was self operative scheme by itself - Laxminarayan R. Bhattad and Others v. State of Maharashtra and Another, 2003 Indlaw SC 329, relied on - Town Planning Scheme, as per the Act, was meant for planned developments of certain local areas depending on various factors in order to make available utilities and facilities to the general public in the said area - For the purpose of said Town Planning Schemes, various facilities, utilities and services were required to be provided for which certain lands are required - These Town Planning Schemes were for immediate need of the community and not for acquisition on deferred basis and therefore these sections under Chapter V provided machinery to prepare and develop the area and implement such schemes - These schemes were not for future projections but for making available resources at the immediate time - In view of these circumstances, the lands required for implementation of various utilities and facilities, services of any public need and requirement would be for public purpose and therefore the same had to be made available the Govt. immediately so as to implement the scheme - Once the town planning scheme was finally sanctioned u/s. 86, compensation is finally determined by the Arbitrator, the property vests under S. 88 in the State Government, then there is no question of resorting to further acquisition u/s. 126(2) of the Act. The words "town planning scheme" used in S. 126(2) is in respect of the town planning scheme which is yet to be finalized and sanctioned under S. 86 by the State Government as a final scheme for inviting objections under S. 67 of the Act. Provisions of S. 126(2) providing for acquisition of land, therefore will apply only prior to the town planning scheme is finally sanctioned under the provision of S. 86 of the Act - HC, therefore, held that the provisions of s. 126 of Act could apply only when the scheme was not sanctioned and the amount of compensation was not determined by arbitrator - Therefore, in cases where town planning scheme was already sanctioned and the property vests in the State Govt. u/s. 88 (a) of the Act, question of resorting to s. 126(2) of the Act did not arise - HC also rejected the contention that under the scheme, if any property was acquired by the Planning Authority and if it was required for the beneficial use of the persons, it was only then that the Arbitrator could fix the compensation and pass the award - If the property was taken over by the Planning Authority for the construction of its office and all civic amenities could be provided by the Planning Authority and if the office of the authority was located in area where the scheme was framed then it would be beneficial to the public as well - Since, it was also for public purpose covered by the scheme, contention that the area earmarked for the Town Planning Authority could be acquired only by following s. 126 of the Act, had no basis - Appeal dismissed.
