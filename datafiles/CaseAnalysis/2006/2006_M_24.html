Kalyani Baskar v M. S. Sampoornam

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Lokeshwar Singh Panta, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1246; (2007) 2 SCC 258; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 820; 2007 (3) CalLT(SC) 12; [2007] 135 Comp Cas 197; 2007 (2) CTC 364; 2007 (1) JCC (NI) 1; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 74; JT 2007 (1) SC 77; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 311; 2006(13) SCALE 459

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Corporate; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Powers of Magistrate - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 243 - Respondent filed complaint against appellant u/s. 138 of Act, 1881- Appellants application seeking opinion of expert on signatures was dismissed by Trial Court and High Court - Whether Trial Court and HC wrongly disallowed application of appellants? - Held, yes - Appellant was entitled to rebut allegations of respondent - Magistrate should have send cheque for expert opinion - Appellant could not be convicted without an opportunity to present her evidence - Fair trial includes fair and proper opportunities allowed by law - Appeal allowed.
