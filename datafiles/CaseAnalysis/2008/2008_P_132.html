Union of India v Prabhakaran Vijaya Kumar and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 743; (2008) 9 SCC 527; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 813; 2009 (1) ACC 270; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 383; 2008(5) ALL MR 917; 2008 (5) AWC 5181; 2008 (3) G.L.R. 2400; JT 2008 (4) SC 598; 2008 (2) KLT 700; 2009 (1) MLJ(Crl) 772; 2008 (4) MLJ(SC) 323; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 577; 2009 (1) RLW 236; 2008(9) SCALE 182; [2008] 7 S.C.R. 673; 2009 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 334

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Railways Act - Railway Claims - Claim petition by the husband, mother and minor son of deceased who died on 23.5.1996 in a train accident disallowed by tribunal - Compensation of Rs. 2 lacs with interest @ 12% from the date of the petition till the date of payment was granted by High Court - Appeal against - Held, s. 124A lays down strict liability or no fault liability in case of railway accidents - If a case comes within the purview of s. 124A it is wholly irrelevant as to who was at fault - Plea that there was no fault on the part of the Railways, or that there was contributory negligence, is based on a total misconception and hence has to be rejected - Appeal dismissed.
