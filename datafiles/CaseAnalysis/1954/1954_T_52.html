Â Tahir Hussain v District Board, Muzaffarnagar

Supreme Court of India

25 January 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Ghulam Hasan, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 189; AIR 1954 SC 630

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19(1)(g) - United Provinces District Board Act, 1922, s. 174(2)(1) - Bye Laws thereunder - Bye law 2 - Whether bye law ultra vires power u/s. 174(2)(1) of Act and whether bye law violative of art. 19(1)(g)? - Petitioner owner of certain land on which he holds a market for sale of cattle and charges some commission on sales - Notice issued by Board not to hold market and to show cause why they should not be prosecuted - Provisions perused shows that power of Board to make bye-laws is to be exercised for purpose of promoting or maintaining health, safety and convenience of inhabitants of area within its jurisdiction and that this power includes power to regulate markets as mentioned in sub-s. 2(1), but bye law in question is not one passed for regulating market but for prohibiting petitioner from holding it - Held, bye law beyond jurisdiction and further it interferes with fundamental right of petitioner u/art. 19(1)(g) and prevents him from carrying on business of holding market - Petition allowed.
