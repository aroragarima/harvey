Adivekka And Others v Hanamavva Kom Venkatesh ’D’ By Lrs. And Another

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 511; (2007) 7 SCC 91; AIR 2007 SC 2025; 2007 (4) AWC 3316; 2007 (5) KarLJ 1; 2007 (6) MLJ(SC) 355; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 811; 2007(7) SCALE 37; 2007 (4) SCJ 867; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 264; 2007 (3) UC 1604

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Execution Of Document

Summary: Suit for declaration and permanent injunction - Appellants are wife and children of deceased - Allegedly executed the Will in favour of Respondent No. 1 - Requirements in regard to the nature of proof of a Will in view of existing suspicious circumstances - High Court opined that the execution of the Will has been proved - Held, where there are suspicious circumstances, the onus would be on the propounder to remove suspicion by leading appropriate evidence - High Court was not correct in reversing the judgment of Trial Judge - Appeals allowed.
