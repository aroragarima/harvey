Bai Hira Devi and Others v Official Assignee of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 23; AIR 1958 SC 448; 1958 (2) MLJ 108; 1958 (2) MLJ(SC) 108; 1958 SCJ 766; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 1384

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Evidence Act, Indian Evidence Act, Representative In Interest, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Presidency towns Insolvency Act, 1909, s. 55 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 91, 92 - Insolvency - Gift - Oral Evidence- Applicability -Respondent /official assignee filed case u/s. 55 of the 1909 Act against appellants before HC for declaration that deed of gift executed by insolvent in favour of appellants was void - It appears that some creditors of insolvent filed petition in HC with the result that estate of insolvent vested in the respondent under s. 17 of the 1909 Act - Respondent took out present notice of motion - Impugned deed of gift had been executed by insolvent in favour appellants - In reply to notice of motion appellants, No. I to 3 filed joint affidavit setting out facts and circumstances under which the said deed of gift had been executed by insolvent in their favour - Appellants sought to lead evidence in support of said plea, but respondent objected and urged that evidence which appellants wanted to lead was inadmissible u/s. 92 of the 1872 Act - Single Judge did not accept appellant's contention and granted declaration claimed by respondent u/s.55 of the 1909 Act - Aggrieved appellants filed appeal before DB which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellants were entitled to lead oral evidence with view to show real nature of impugned transaction - Held, Court opinioned true position was that, if terms of any transfer reduced to writing were in dispute between stranger to document and party to it or his representative in interest - Therefore, restriction imposed by s. 92 of the 1872 Act in regard to exclusion of evidence of oral agreement was inapplicable and both stranger to document and party to document or his representative in interest were at liberty to lead evidence of oral agreement notwithstanding fact that such evidence, if believed, might contradict, vary, add to or subtract from its terms - Thus rule of exclusion enunciated by s. 92 of the 1872 Act applies to both parties to document and was based on doctrine of mutuality - Therefore, s.92 of the 1872 Act was wholly inapplicable to instant proceedings and appellants were entitled to lead evidence in support of plea raised by them - Hence, set aside decree passed by HC and remanded back case to HC - Appeal allowed.
