Mylapore Club v State Of Tamil Nadu & Anr

Supreme Court of India

28 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, R.C. Lahoti, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1443; (2005) 12 SCC 752; AIR 2006 SC 523; JT 2005 (9) SC 443; 2006 (1) MLJ(SC) 27; 2005(9) SCALE 150; [2005] Supp4 S.C.R. 996

Case Digest

Subject: Parliament & Legislature; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: Tamil Nadu Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, Madras City Tenants' Protection (Amendment) Act, 1955, Madras City Tenants' Protection Act, 1921, Madras City Tenants' Protection (Amendment) Act, 1994

Summary: Rent Control - Practice & Procedure - Parliament & Legislature - Madras City Tenants' Protection Act, 1921 (Parent Act) - Madras City Tenants' Protection (Amendment) Act, 1994 (Amendment Act) - Validity of Amendment Act - Parent Act was amended by Amendment Act by inserting cl. (f) to s. 3 whereby it was declared that any proceeding instituted by a tenant in respect of any land owned by a religious institution or religious charity, which was being exempted from operation of Act pending before any court or other authority, would stand abated and all rights and privileges conferred by extension of 1921 Act would cease and would become unenforceable - S. 3 of Amendment Act, however, excluded concluded cases where orders for possession had been executed or decrees satisfied in full before date of amendment - Appellant Club challenged validity of Amendment Act before HC - Full Bench of HC upheld validity - Hence the instant appeal - (A) Whether HC was right in holding that withdrawal of benefit available u/s. 3 of Act to tenant was valid and constitutional - Held, once power to exempt an institution or entity from operation of Act was conceded to legislature, it should not be argued that benefit of one section in that enactment could not be withdrawn - Right u/s. 3 itself was extended only by extension of Act, therefore, it could be withdrawn by legislature - Reasoning in S.M. Transport (P) Ltd. v. Sankaraswamigal Mutt, 1962 INDLAW SC 489, would cover situation demonstrated in instant case - HC in judgment under appeal, therefore, rightly noticed that right of tenant in respect of building put up by him would be governed by terms of contract and by general law - (B) Whether provision in Amendment Act for abating all proceedings initiated u/s. 9 of Parent Act at instance of tenant, would amount to interference in decision-making process and hence invalid in law - Held, legislature could bring in a law that had retrospective operation and when it affected vested rights or accrued rights, that question would have to be considered in that context but right to take advantage of a statute was not an accrued right - Once applicability of Act itself was withdrawn, no relief could be granted to a person who could have been or who was earlier a beneficiary under that enactment, after such withdrawal - S. 3 of Amendment Act pari materia with s. 9 of Parent Act provided that even if some steps had been taken pursuant to claim by tenant u/s. 9 of Parent Act, proceeding should not be continued in view of exemption enacted in favour of institutions, however, legislature had taken care to save concluded transactions by providing that nothing contained in section should be deemed to invalidate any suit or proceeding in which a decree or order passed had been executed or satisfied in full before said date - It could not be said that s. 3 was a legislative intervention with a judicial decision since proviso had saved concluded transactions based on judicial adjudications - S. 3 made it explicit that amendment was intended to apply to pending proceedings - In the context of s. 6 of General Clauses Act, unless it was shown that any right had accrued to claimant, such a provision making it clear that Act could not be applied any more to pending proceedings was not in any way invalid or incompetent - Unless proceedings had concluded and rights of landlord had passed to tenant, no right accrued to tenant and he was only in process of acquiring a right and that process having been set in motion at his instance - When pending proceedings were affected by an amendment, it was open to legislature to provide that said process should not continue - As in case of concluded judicial proceedings and cases where orders for possession had been executed or decrees satisfied in full before date of amendment, they had been saved by proviso thereby ensuring that there was no interference by legislature with judicial proceedings which had reached a conclusion, even though that judicial proceeding related to a religious or charitable institution exempted by amendment from purview of Parent Act - No merit in challenge of s. 3 of Amendment Act - No interference on impugned HC order - Appeal dismissed.
