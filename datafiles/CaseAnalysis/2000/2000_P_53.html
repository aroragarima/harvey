Parents' Association and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, A.S. Anand, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 602; (2000) 2 SCC 657; AIR 2000 SC 845; 2000 (3) CalLT(SC) 30; JT 2000 (1) SC 410; 2000(1) SCALE 325; 2000 (2) SCJ 569; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 429; 2000 (1) SLR 735; 2000 (2) SLT 17; 2000 (2) Supreme 372; 2000 (2) UPLBEC 1525

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Tort

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 15, 16, 19(1)(g), 21 and 240 - Petitioners filed writ petition (under O. 1, r. 8 Civil Procedure Code), seeking to set aside the proceedings of the UOI, Ministry of Home Affairs as being violative of arts. 14, 15, 16, 19(1)(g) and 21 - In the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, there are several categories of persons residing - (i) Tribals, (ii) Those who settled there prior to 1942, (iii) Others who settled after 1942 under rehabilitation schemes, and (iv) Those who have gone to the islands for business or professional purposes and who have put in 10 years' education - Petitioners belong to the last of these categories - Quotas were fixed for the abovesaid categories of persons under various orders, from time to time, for the purpose of admission to engineering/medical etc. seats - These seats are reserved in various colleges in the Indian mainland from time to time - (A) (1) Whether reduction of quota for the 'locals with 10 years' education' from 35% to 20% is illegal or otherwise vitiated? - (2) Whether the provision for 50% quota for the pre-and post-1942 settlers suffers from any legal infirmity? - Held, contention is not legally tenable - Ratio given in Indra Sawhney v. UOI (1992 Supp (3) SCC 2171992 (S3) SCC 2171992 Indlaw SC 735 : 1992 SCC 2171992 SCC 217 : 1992 SCC (L&S) Supp 1 : (1992) 22 ATC 3851992 (22) ATC 3851991 Indlaw SC 735) relied upon - Merely because, the 'ten-year education category' like the petitioners are more in number, they cannot claim a larger percentage of reservation on that basis - Even if the petitioners' category of ten-year educated persons consists of 57% of the student population, it is not possible to give them a higher quota as compared to the pre-1942 and the post-1942 settlers who were identified as backward, both socially and economically - Further held that the Central Govt. in its orders has considered the facts revealed in the survey, it has considered the submissions of all the groups and the historical basis of the reservation of 50% in favour of the pre-1942 and the post-1942 settlers - It has kept in mind that the petitioners are affluent and are more advanced, educationally and socially - Therefore, impugned order does not suffer from any irrationality - Thus, it cannot be said that any relevant facts were not considered or any irrelevant facts were taken into consideration - For the aforesaid reasons, court holds on point (1) That the reduction of the quota for the petitioners from 35% to 20% was perfectly justified and on point (2) That the prescription of 50% for the pre-and post-1942 settlers was equally justified - Held, there was no violation of arts. 14 or 15(4) or any other provision of the Constitution - (B) Whether the impugned order has not made any provision for merit candidates? - Held, it is true that normally it is expected that reserved categories cannot exceed 50% of the quota as decided in various decisions of this court and the rest must go to merit candidates - But case relating to the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, the present classification and quota cannot be said to be offending the said principle - The impugned order refers to the statistics from 1992-93 and shows that though 20% quota was reserved for the tribals, the said quota was never fully utilised - Therefore, it was specifically provided that the unutilised quota of 20% for tribals would go to merit candidates - A provision was made in respect of the merit candidates amongst the pre-1942 and the post-1942 categories by providing a sub-classification in which 1/3rd of 50% would go to such merit candidates and the unutilised quota was to go to the general merit candidates - Even in respect of the 10% quota for Central Govt. employees and deputationists and the 20% quota for the ten-year education group, it was directed that the unutilised quota would go to the merit candidates - Having regard to the rather special facts obtaining in the islands, therefore, it cannot be said that adequate provision has not been made in favour of merit candidates - Petition dismissed.
