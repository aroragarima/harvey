Allahabad Bank etc. etc. v Bengal Paper Mills Company Limited and Others etc.

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, R.C. Lahoti, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 839; (2004) 8 SCC 236; 2005 (1) ALD(SC) 96; [2004] 122 Comp Cas 482; 2004 (4) CompLJ 245; JT 2004 (8) SC 340; 2004 (4) RCR(Civil) 502; 2004(8) SCALE 551; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 143; 2005 (1) UC 170

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Corporate - Indian Companies Act, 1956, s. 457(1)(c) - Companies (Court) Rules, 1959, r. 272, 273, 274 - Winding up proceedings - Winding up petition filed by creditors - Sale confirmed by Company Court but set aside by this court - Application for a clarification of Judgment - Prayer that applicant was also entitled to the amount of Rs 1,56,21,839 25 being the interest that has accrued on the purchase price of Rs 2 crores paid by it - Held, this Court while setting aside sale and issuing certain other directions, has very clearly directed that 'that same shall be resold after a fresh valuation report thereof has been obtained, the reserved bid fixed and due advertisement published' - It is for company court to take a decision on fresh sale to be conducted by it - There is no question of any clarification of directions of this Court - Applications dismissed.
