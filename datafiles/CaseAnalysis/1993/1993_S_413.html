Varkey Joseph v State of Kerala, Rep. By The Circle Inspector of Police

Supreme Court of India

27 April 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 86; (1993) Supp3 SCC 745; (1993) SCC (Cr) 1117; AIR 1993 SC 1892; 1993 (2) CCR 218; 1993 CRLJ 2010; 1993 CrLR(SC) 363; JT 1993 (3) SC 163; 1993 (2) KLT 617; 1993 (2) PLR 643; 1993(2) SCALE 709; [1993] 3 S.C.R. 390

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 141, 142 and 145 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 300 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 21 - Evidence Act, - Leading question - Prosecutor putting leading questions on material part of evidence which a witness intends to give against accused - (A) Whether offend right of accused to fair trial - (B) Is it curable irregularity - Held, (A) Offends the right of accused to fair trial enshrined u/art. 21 of Constitution - (B) It is not curable irregularity - Appeal allowed.
