Madamsetty Satyanarayana v G. Yellogi Rao and Two Others

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 336; AIR 1965 SC 1405; 1965 (2) MLJ(SC) 145; 1965 (2) SCJ 678; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 221

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Specific Relief Act, 1887

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Specific Relief Act, 1887, s. 22 - Appeal against High Court's order giving decree for specific performance of an agrrement to sell - Relief could be refused only if plaintiff abandons or waives his right under contract - U/s. 22 of Specific Relief Act, relief of specific performance is discretionary but not arbitrary - Discretion must be exercised in accordance with sound and reasonable judicial principles - Held, HC rightly held that it was a fit case where plaintiff should have been given a relief of specific performance - Appeal dismissed.
