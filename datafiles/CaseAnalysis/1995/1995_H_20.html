Prof. Ramchandra G. Kapse v Haribansh Ramakbal Singh

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, N.P. Singh, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1864; (1996) 1 SCC 206; AIR 1996 SC 817; JT 1995 (9) SC 89; 1995(7) SCALE 60; 1996 (2) SCJ 107; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 471; 1996 (1) UJ 471

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Representation of People Act, 1951, s.123 - Corrupt practice - Allegation against candidate of political party - Manifesto of - Not pleaded as basis of allegation nor specific use in campaign attributed to candidate in pleading or evidence - Held, merely because he was contesting as candidate of that party - Is not ground to guilt - More so when there was no legal evidence to prove allegation of corrupt practice.
