P.T. Thomas v Thomas Job

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 449; (2005) 6 SCC 478; AIR 2005 SC 3575; 2005 (61) ALR 150; 2005 (3) AWC 3048; 2006 (1) CGLJ 206; 2006 (1) G.L.R. 246; 2005 (4) JhrCR(SC) 90; JT 2005 (10) SC 304; 2005 (3) KLT 1042; 2005 (4) MLJ(SC) 72; 2005 (3) RCR(Civil) 621; 2005(6) SCALE 85; [2005] Supp2 S.C.R. 20

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: [A.] Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987, ss. 20(1), 21 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 115, 96(3) - Indian Post Office Act, 1898, s. 27 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - IPC, 1860, ss. 193, 219, 228 - CrPC, 1973, s. 195 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - Property dispute - Award of Lok Adalat - Award provided for sale to Appellant - Non performance - Execution petition dismissed by HC - Whether award of Lok Adalat can be equated with a decree? - Held, award of Lok Adalat is final and permanent which is equivalent to a decree executable, and same is an ending to litigation among parties - Directed respondent herein to execute sale deed - Appeal allowed.
