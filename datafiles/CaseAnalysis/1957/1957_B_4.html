Kamala Devi v Bachu Lal Gupta

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 118; AIR 1957 SC 434; 1957 (1) MLJ 66; 1957 (1) MLJ(SC) 66; 1957 SCJ 321; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 452

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Family & Personal - Women & Children - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 14 - Whether deed regarding gift of immovable property by widow that was executed in favour of marriage of her daughter could be binding on reversioners u/s. 14 of 1956 act? - In fulfillment of ante-nuptial promise while deciding terms of marriage of her daughter - Widow allotted her share by partition deed - Suit brought by step-sons of widow alleging that deed of gift was void and inoperative so not binding on reversioners - Held, deed of gift valid as per Hindu Law so would be binding on reversioners - Appeal allowed.
