Sidhbali Steels Limited and others v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

20 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, Balbir Singh Chauhan, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 89; (2011) 3 SCC 193; AIR 2011 SC 1175; 2011 (5) AWC 4986; 2011 (3) MLJ(SC) 721; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 663; 2011(1) SCALE 676; [2011] 3 S.C.R. 134

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Electricity Reforms Act, 1999, Uttar Pradesh Regulatory Reforms Act, 1999

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Constitution - Electricity Supply Act, 1948, s. 49 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(g), 21 and 32 - Electricity Tariff - Petitioners were industrial units carrying on business of manufacturing iron rods, ingots, strips in furnaces/re-rolling mills in hill areas - The industrial units of the petitioners were connected with power loads in the year 1996-97 by U.P. State Electricity Board - Petitioners submitted that from the year 1986, the State Government, in order to develop hill areas declared various exhaustive industrial policies granting various incentives including rebate of 33.33% on total amount of electricity bills to industrial units to be established in hill areas of U.P. - Petitioners further submitted that the Government of U.P., pursuant to the aforesaid policy issued an order dt. 16.10.1990 to implement all the instructions contained in the said industrial policy for the 5 years - Later, Government by notification dt. 28.6.1996 extended the Hill Development Rebate for a further period of next 5 years - Petitioners further submitted that the 33.33% rebate promised to industrial units established in the hill areas was accordingly granted to the petitioners who had established their industrial units in the year 1996-97 - Petitioners further submitted that vide Notification dt. 18.06.1998 and 25.01.1999, uniform tariffs were introduced by the Government, which had reduced the rebate from 33.33% to 17% - Petitioners also submitted that subsequently, Government had issued a new tariff by notification dt. 07.08.2000 and had increased exorbitantly the rate of charges and had completely withdrawn the Hill Development Rebate which had made it impossible for the petitioners to run their industrial units located in the hill areas - Petitioners further submitted that therefore, the notification dt. 07.08.2000 should be regarded as illegal, arbitrary, discriminatory and violative of provisions of arts. 14, 19(1)(g) and 21 of the Constitution as well as contrary to the principles of promissory estoppel - (A) Whether the Hill Development Rebate of 33.33% on total amount of electricity bills under industrial policy of the erstwhile State Government of U.P. granted to the writ petitioners will cease to have effect after 14.01.2000? - Held, the rebate which was granted to the petitioners, was, a freedom from an obligation which the appellants otherwise were liable to discharge - The rebate was a privilege granting an advantage which was not made available to others - The rebate granted u/s. 49 of the Electricity Supply Act of 1948 was, therefore, a concession granted by the State Government so that the beneficiaries of such concessions were not required to pay the electricity tariff, they were otherwise liable to pay under the said Act during the period of its grant - Said right to enjoy was a defeasible one in the sense that it was liable to be taken away or withdrawn in exercise of the very power under which the exemption was granted - Hence, petitioners cannot raise plea of estoppel against the notification dt. 7.8.2000 reducing Hill Development Rebate to 0% as there can be no estoppel against the statute - (B) Whether the term stipulated in the contract entered into between the petitioners and the U.P. State Electricity Board stipulating that the Government would give 33.33% rebate to the petitioners, was legally enforceable and whether in view of the said term the Government precluded from changing the tariff rates? - Held, in view of the terms and conditions stipulated in the agreement, petitioners were precluded from challenging revision of the tariff in exercise of statutory powers conferred on the Government in the larger public interest - There is no prohibition in the agreement by which the Government was bound to give 33.33% rebate to the petitioners in all the circumstances or was precluded from changing the tariff rates - (C) Whether the Court of law would be justified in interfering with the policy decision of the Government either to grant or not to grant rebate to certain industrial units? - Held, the policy of the State Government in not granting rebate to industrial units situated in a particular area was basically a fiscal decision and in absence of arbitrariness or unreasonableness in the said policy, it cannot be a subject-matter of judicial review of this Court while exercising powers u/art. 32 of the Constitution - Therefore, no case was made out by the petitioners to interfere with the said policy - Petition dismissed.
