Assitant Commissioner, Ernakulam v Hindustan Urban Infrastructure Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 20; (2015) 3 SCC 745; [2015] 189 Comp Cas 283; 2015 (2) CompLJ 259; JT 2015 (1) SC 456; 2015 (1) MLJ 626; 2015(1) SCALE 392; [2015] 1 S.C.R. 100; [2015] 79 VST 5

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941, Registered Dealer, Collected, Point Of Levy, Sale In The State, Auction purchase, U.P. Sales Tax Act, 1940, Liability to pay sales tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1963, ss. 2(viii), 2(viii)(f), 5A - Companies Act, 1956 - Kerala General Sales Tax Rules, 1963, r. 54 - Companies (Court) Rules, 1959, r. 233 - Auction purchase - Liability to pay sales tax - Legality - Respondent no. 2 was appointed as Official Liquidator by HC to take charge of assets and liabilities of Company in question in a winding proceedings - Official Liquidator issued a notice inviting tenders, in respect of sale of assets of Company and respondent no. 1 was declared auction purchaser, and sale was confirmed - Thereafter, auction purchaser requested the Official Liquidator to incorporate the relevant sales tax registration numbers in sale invoices, which was declined - Official liquidator filed application before HC and sought clarification on matter whether auction purchaser would be liable to pay tax on the purchase of goods, pursuant to the auction conducted and further to direct the auction purchaser to pay any tax as may be leviable by the Sales Tax dept. - Single Judge held that machinery purchased in auction sale conducted by Official Liquidator was not be liable to be taxed under Act - On review, DB by impugned judgment held that Official Liquidator was not a 'dealer' under the Act and that auction purchaser would be liable to pay purchase tax u/s. 5A of 1963 Act - Hence, instant appeals - Whether an 'Official Liquidator' was a 'dealer' within the meaning of s. 2(viii) of 1963 Act and therefore would be required to collect sales tax in respect of sales effected by him pursuant to winding up proceedings of a company in liquidation.

Held,. Since the Official Liquidator is akin to an agent employed for the purpose of winding up of a company, he steps into the shoes of the Directors of the said Company for the purposes of discharging the statutory functions of an Official Liquidator. Thus, during the said proceedings, the Directors cease to exercise any functions from the date on which the Official Liquidator is appointed and all powers and functions for carrying on the business of the company thereafter vest with the official liquidator. An Official Liquidator is an officer of the Court and that for the purpose of discharging statutory obligations imposed under the Act, the Official Liquidator merely steps into the shoes of the company in liquidation. By virtue of the notice issued by the Official Liquidator for inviting tenders, it is amply evident that the liquidator intended to conduct a transfer of the said goods in liquidation. Since the conduct of an auctioned sale involved transfer of goods, it falls within the wide ambit of s. 2(viii)(f) of 1963 Act. R. 54 of 1963 Rules contemplates a situation where a business owned by a dealer, is under the control of a receiver or manager or any other person, irrespective of his designation, who manages the business on behalf of the said dealer. In the said scenario, the said person, in-charge of the business on behalf of the dealer, would be exigible to sales tax in the same manner as it would have been leviable upon and recoverable from the dealer itself. Therefore, it can be concluded that the liability to pay sales tax, in the instant case, would be on the Official Liquidator in the same manner as the dealer, that is, the Company in liquidation. Therefore, the Official Liquidator would be required to pay the tax payable on the sale of the assets of the company in liquidation. The offer of the auction purchaser, as accepted by Official Liquidator and confirmed by HC, was inclusive of all taxes. It would have been the bounden duty of the Official Liquidator to have separated an amount for the payment of taxes under 1963 Act to avoid any liability. Impugned judgments and orders passed by HC are set aside. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - An Official Liquidator steps into the shoes of the Director of the company in liquidation and performs his statutory functions in accordance with the directives of the Court.
