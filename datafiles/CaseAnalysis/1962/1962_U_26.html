Supreme Court of India

21 December 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 316; AIR 1963 SC 1241; 1963 BLJR 675; [1964] 1 S.C.R. 371

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Indian Independence Act, 1947, Constitution (Fourth Amendment) Act, 1955, Coal Bearing Areas (Acquisition And Development) Amendment Act, 1957, Constitution (Fifth Amendment) Act,1955

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Coal Bearing Areas (Acquisition and Development) Act, 1957, ss. 4 and 7 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 246(2)and 31, Sch 7, List 3, Entry 42, 52, 54, 254(2), 298, 294 and 296 - Government of India Act, 1935, s. 127 - (A) Whether State of West Bengal is entitled to issue an injunction restraining Union from applying ss. 4 and 7 of Coal Bearing Areas (Acquisition and Development) Act, 1957 for coal-bearing lands of State - Held, Under Constitution - Powers are divided between state and center - State cannot be immune from exercise of legislative power of union parliament - Regarding state property acquisition - Doctrine of immunity of inapplicabilities - Not attracted - (B) Does power to legislate developments of mines include power to acquire? - Further held, Legislation for regulation and development of mines and minerals by Union - Includes power to acquire same - When done under art. 31 of Constitution - Suit dismissed with costs.
