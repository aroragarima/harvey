State of Haryana v Ranbir Alias Rana

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 119; (2006) 5 SCC 167; AIR 2006 SC 1796; 2006 CRLJ 2142; 2006 (3) CTC 48; 2006 (2) JCC (Narcotics) 73; JT 2006 (4) SC 350; 2006 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2399; 2006 (2) RCR(Criminal) 513; 2006(4) SCALE 113; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 864; 2006 (2) UC 1179

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Narcotic Drugs And Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 20, 42, 50 - Applicability of s. 50 - Trial Court convicted respondent - In appeal HC acquitted him - Appeal against acquittal - Held, s. 50 would be applicable only in a case of personal search of accused and not when it is made in respect of some baggage like a bag, article or container etc which accused at relevant time was carrying - Judgment of HC is set aside - Appeal allowed.
