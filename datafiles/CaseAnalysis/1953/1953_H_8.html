Sukhdev Singh v Hone'Ble C. J., S. Teja Singh and The Hon'Ble Judges of The Pepsu High Court At Patiala

Supreme Court of India

25 November 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 142; AIR 1954 SC 186; 1954 CRLJ 460; 1954 (1) MLJ(SC) 137; [1954] S.C.R. 454

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Contempt - Contempt of Courts Act, 1952 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 1(2) and 527 - Contempt proceedings before Pepsu High Court - Application for transferring such contempt proceedings from Pepsu High Court to another HC and in the alternative to change the Judge before whom the matter was pending disposal - Maintainability of application or whether Supreme Court has power to transfer contempt proceeding before a HC to another HC? - Held, no - Power of a HC to institute proceedings for contempt and punish where necessary is a special jurisdiction which is inherent in all Courts of Record and s. 1(2) of the Code expressly excludes special jurisdictions from its scope - Contempt is a special subject and the jurisdiction is conferred by a special set of laws peculiar to Courts of Record - Held further, Code does not apply in matters of contempt triable by the HC - The HC can deal with it summarily and adopt its own procedure - If the Code does not apply, then there is no other power which Supreme Court can exercise - The Constitution gives every HC the right and the power to punish a contempt of itself - If Supreme Court were to order a transfer to another court in this case, it would be depriving the Pepsu HC of the right which is so vested in it - Thus the Supreme Court has no more power to do that than has a Legislature - As for transfer from one Judge to another, there again there is no original jurisdiction which the Court can exercise - It is not a fundamental right and so art. 32 of Constitution, 1950, have no application and there is no other law to which recourse can be had - Petition being incompetent, dismissed.
