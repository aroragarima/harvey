Commissioner of Sales Tax, M.P v Purshottam Premji

Supreme Court of India

13 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 52; (1970) 2 SCC 287; 1970 (26) STC 38; 1970 UJ 562

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Madhya Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1958, s. 2(n) - Contracts with South Eastern Railway for breaking stones and supplying ballast - Quarry belonged to railway administration and respondent merely required to quarry stones from those quarries, break them into pieces of specified size and supply them to railway administration - Whether transactions referred to in those references are 'sales' within meaning of Act or works contracts? - Primary difference between a contract for work or service and a contract for sale of goods is that in former there is in person performing work or rendering service no property in thing produced as a whole notwithstanding that a part or even whole of materials used by him way have been his property - In case of a contract for sale, thing produced as a whole has individual existence as sole property of party who produced it, at some time before delivery, and property therein passes only under contract relating thereto to other party for price - Mere transfer of property in goods used in performance of a contract is not sufficient; to constitute a sale there must be an agreement express or implied relating to sale of goods and completion of agreement by passing of title in very goods contracted to be sold - Held, property in ballast at all relevant times was with S E Railway - Ballast was never property of assessee and therefore there was no question of assessee transferring any property in it to S E Railway - Appeal dismissed.
