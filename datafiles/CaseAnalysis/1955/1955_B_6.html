Â Bhikaji Keshao Joshi and Another v Brijlal Nandlal Biyani and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Jagannadhadas, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Syed Jaffer Imam, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 56; AIR 1955 SC 610; 1955 (2) MLJ(SC) 154; 1955 SCJ 658; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 428

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Election - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6, r. 15 and O. 19, r. 3 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 80, 82, 83(2) 85 and 90(4) - (A) Whether there was sufficient cause for presentation of petition one day out of time? - Held, yes - There was sufficient ground for condoning delay - Tribunal should not have interfered with decision of Election Commission to condone delay - (B) Whether petition was defective for non-joinder of certain parties as respondents? - Held, no - Even if any of the necessary parties other than returned candidate has not been impleaded, petition is not liable to be dismissed in limine on that sole ground but that it is a matter to be taken into consideration at appropriate stage with reference to final result of case - (C) Whether petition is defective for want of proper verification? - Held, though relevant and important, it would be wrong to dismiss application on this sole ground - It can be remedied by adding a supplementary verification giving reason for earlier omission - (D) Whether petition was defective for vagueness of particulars relating to corrupt practices set out in Schedule A thereto? - Held, particulars should be specific - Appeal allowed.
