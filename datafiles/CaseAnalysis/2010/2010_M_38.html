M/S. Rashtriya Chemicals & Fertilizers Ltd. v M/S Chowgule Brothers & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 518; (2010) 8 SCC 563; AIR 2010 SC 3543; 2010 (82) ALR 301; 2010 (5) AWC 5245; 2010 (5) Bom.C.R. 698; 2010 (127) FLR 549; JT 2010 (6) SC 624; 2010 (4) LLJ 6; 2010 (3) RCR(Civil) 769; 2010(6) SCALE 393; [2010] 7 S.C.R. 962

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Arbitration & Conciliation Act, 1996 - Appellant, a Government of India undertaking invited tenders for allotment of clearing, forwarding, handling and stevedoring jobs initially for a period of one year commencing from 15-01-1983 upto 14-01-1984 but extendable at the option of the appellant for a further period of one year on the same terms and conditions - Respondent submitted a tender which was accepted - Subsequently, appellant extended the contract for a further period of one year which was accepted by the respondent and was pointed out that statutory revisions in the wages of Mormugao Dock Labour Board (M.D.L.B.) that had come about during the period of one year need be considered while extending the contractual period - Respondent had incurred an additional amount of Rs.24.74 lakhs towards the increase in the wages payable to such workers for extended period of contract - A claim for reimbursement of the said amount was accordingly made by the respondent-company which was refuted by the appellant on the ground that the rates at which the contract was initially awarded had to remain firm throughout the period of one year from the date of award and were not subject to any escalation whatsoever - Dispute referred to Arbitration as per agreement - Arbitrator dismissed the claim of the respondents - On appeal, Single Judge set aside the award holding that the same was contrary to the contract executed between the parties - DB allowed the appeal filed by the appellant and set aside the order of the Single Judge and restored the award made by the arbitrator - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of DB could be upheld and respondents entitled for an additional amount towards the increase in the wages payable to dock workers? - Held, a careful reading of the agreement leaves no manner of doubt that the rate at which the contract was initially awarded was to remain firm throughout the period of one year from the date of the award of the contract - The only difference between the first and the second year rates thus is that the rates were firm even for the second year but the same had to be fixed taking into consideration the statutory increases in the wages of the dock labourers - Moreover, as to how the parties understood Clause 2.03 of the agreement is also an important factor - While accepting the extension of the contract, the respondent-contractor had simply referred to the statutory revision in the wages by M.D.L.B. during the 'last year' - Since the letter of acceptance is of 7-12-1983 the statutory revision which the contractor wanted to be taken into consideration were revisions before 1983 and not those made at any time after the extended period of contract - Hence, entitlement of the respondent to claim any amount on account of escalation consequent upon the increase in the wages of M.D.L.B. workers is not established - Appeal allowed.
