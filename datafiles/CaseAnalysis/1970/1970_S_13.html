Rani and Another v Santa Bala Debnath and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 October 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 280; (1970) 3 SCC 722; AIR 1971 SC 1028; [1971] 2 S.C.R. 603; 1971 UJ 51

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: CPC, 1908, O. 22, r. 2 and s. 96 - (A) Hindu law - Joint family property - Alienation - For legal necessity by limited owner - Suit to set it aside - (1) Recital in sale-deed that full consideration is paid - Onus, to prove that recital is false - (2) Proof - Recital in sale-deed of legal necessity - Evidentiary value - (3) Plea of fraudulent representation and exercise of undue influence over vendor - Denial by vendee - No issue raised and no evidence led in trial - Held, Appellate Court could not decree suit in appeal on ground of false representation and undue influence - (B) CPC, 1908, O. 22, r. 2 - Suit against several defendants - One of them against whom no relief is claimed dies - No reason given in plaint for impleading him as defendant - Failure to bring his LRs on record - (A) Onus lies on plaintiff to prove that recital is false and vendor did not in fact receive consideration - Held, sale could not be set aside as legal necessity was amply proved - Onus lies on plaintiff to prove that recital is false and vendor did not in fact receive consideration - (B) Appeal does not abate - Appeal Allowed.
