Joginder Singh and Others v Deputy Custodian General of Evacuee Property

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 141; AIR 1967 SC 145; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 738

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950

Summary: Constitution - Land & Property - Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950, s. 27 - Administration of Evacuee Property Rules, r. 14(6) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133(1)(c) - Whether Custodian General hearing an appeal from an order made by Custodian has power to cancel an allotment of rural evacuee property on a quasi-permanent basis except upon grounds set out in r. 14(6)? - Just as power can be conferred expressly by statute it can also be taken away or restricted and where it is taken away or restricted then, whether power was statutory in its origin or was inherent in court, it will be either wholly unexercisable or exercisable only subject to conditions laid down in statute, as case may be - Notification D/- 22-07-1952 substituted present sub-r. 6 of r. 14 for original sub-r. 6 - Amended sub-rule has placed a limitation on powers of Custodian to cancel allotment of rural evacuee property on a quasi-permanent basis - An allotment of such land can be cancelled only in circumstances specified in that sub-rule - Making of restitution is not within exceptions and, therefore, it will have to, be said that inherent power of Custodian to cancel an allotment for making restitution has been abrogated by amended sub-rule - Held, law does not confer any express power on Custodian to make restitution - Custodian General has no power to do something which Custodian or District Judge could not have done or was prohibited from doing - Appeal allowed.
