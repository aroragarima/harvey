Bishandayal and Sons v State of Orissa and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2736; (2001) 1 SCC 555; AIR 2001 SC 544; 2001 (43) ALR 413; JT 2001 (1) SC 58; 2001 (3) RAJ 375; 2001 (92) RD 503; 2000(8) SCALE 172; 2000 (8) SLT 816; 2000 (8) Supreme 375; 2001 (1) UC 211

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 80 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 299 - (A) Whether an agreement is unenforceable in law as provisions of art. 299 of Constitution have not been complied with? - Plea not been taken in written statement nor raised before Trial Court - Even though there may have been some agreement same was not in compliance with constitutional requirement u/art. 299 of Constitution - Held, appellants would not be entitled to specific performance of such an agreement - (B) Whether amended suit maintainable for want of notice u/s. 80 of CPC? - If a new cause of action is being introduced a fresh notice u/s. 80 CPC would be required to be given - Further held, in absence thereof, suit on this cause of action was not maintainable - Appeal dismissed.
