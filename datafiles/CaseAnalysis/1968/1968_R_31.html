Â State of Madhya Pradesh v Ranojirao Shinde and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 March 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, Mohammad Hidayatullah, R.S. Bachawat, A.N. Grover, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 143; AIR 1968 SC 1053; 1968 (2) SCJ 760; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 489

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950,arts.19(1)(f), 133(1)(c), 31(1), 31(2), 372, 19(5) - Pradesh Abolition of Cash Grants Act, 1963 - Whether Madhya Pradesh Abolition of Cash Grants Act of 1963 is ultra vires provisions of Constitution - Respondents in these appeals were entitled to receive cash grants from Govt. of Madhya Pradesh - Impugned Act abolished such grants but provided for payment of certain compensation to grantees - Choses in action and money could not be acquired u/art. 31(2) - If it is held that State by exercise of its power of eminent domain can acquire choses in action and money belonging 'to its citizens, by paying' a fraction of money taken as compensation, fundamentals - Right guaranteed u/art. 19(1)(f) would be deprived of all its contents and that Article will cease to have any meaningful purpose - It is true that abolition of cash grants would argument resources of State but that cannot be considered as a public purpose u/art. 31(2) - If it is otherwise it would be permissible for legislatures to enact laws acquiring public debts due from State, annuity deposits returnable by it and provident fund payable by it by providing for payment of some nominal compensation, to persons whose rights are acquired as acquisitions in question would augment resources of State - But nothing so can be said to be within contemplation of art. 31(2) - Art. 31(2) must be construed harmoniously with art. 19(1)(f) - If so construed, it is obviously that public purpose contemplated by that Article does not include enrichment of coffers of State - Further compensation referred to in Art. 31(2) is just equivalent of value of property taken - A law which authorises State to, deprive a person of his property must be a valid law. It must not violate art. 19(1)(f) which means that it must satisfy requirements of art. 19(5) - Whether apply u/art. 31(2) or art. 19(5), impugned Act cannot be upheld and it must be struck down - If art. 19(5) is interpreted to mean that State can take by authority of law anyone's property for purpose of increasing its assets or revenues, guarantee given by art. 19(1)(f) would become illusory, a proposition to which SC cannot subscribe - Appeal dismissed.
