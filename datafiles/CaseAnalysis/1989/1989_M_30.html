Trideshwar Dayal And Anr. v Maheshwar Dayal And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

19 December 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 350; (1990) 1 SCC 357; AIR 1990 SC 485; 1990 (1) AWC 367; 1990 (40) DLT 460; 1990 RD 143; 1989(2) SCALE 1436; [1989] Supp2 S.C.R. 529; 1990 (1) UJ 458; 1990 (1) UPLBEC 367

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Stamp Act, 1899, ss. 33, 47A and 56 - (A) Chief Controlling Revenue Authority - Revisional jurisdiction - Scope - Erroneous order or order beyond jurisdiction passed by Collector - (B) Impounding of insufficiently stamped arbitration award - Limitation - Application for making award rule of Court - Court directing collector to initiate proceedings - Opposite party filing application before Collector for impounding of award - Follow up steps taken - (C) Collector - Powers of - Valuation of property - Correctness - Held, (A) Chief Controlling Revenue Authority can correct it u/s. 56 - (B) Since direction was issued by Court and opposite party's application was by way of reminder as further steps for impounding were not promptly taken, no question of limitation from date of opposite party's application to collector could arise so follow up steps taken by collector were in pursuance of Civil Court's direction - (C) Collector is authorised to examine - Order accordingly.
