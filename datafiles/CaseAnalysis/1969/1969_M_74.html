Meghraj and Others v Mst. Bayabai and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 137; (1969) 2 SCC 274; AIR 1970 SC 161; 1969 MahLJ 776; 1969 MPLJ 793; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 523

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 21 r.1 - Madhya Pradesh Money Lenders Act, 1934, s. 9 - Whether appellants/mortgagers contended that on a proper account of monies paid by them in satisfaction of dues under mortgage decree, thus, said mortgage was satisfied and mortgagees were overpaid? - Held, there is no evidence on record that mortgagees/respondents were informed that amounts were deposited towards principal due, nor is there evidence that mortgagees accepted amounts towards the principal - Unless mortgagees were informed that mortgagors had deposited amount only towards principal and not towards interest, and mortgagees agreed to withdraw money from Court accepting conditional deposit, normal rule that amounts deposited in Court should first be applied towards satisfaction of interest and costs and thereafter towards principal would apply - Court is only concerned to pass a decree absolute for sale in a mortgage suit and not concerned to determine respective rights of mortgagees inter se - Mortgagees' interest is fully represented before Court - Appeals dismissed.
