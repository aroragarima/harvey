Kedarnath v Mohan Lal Kesarwari and others

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 14; (2002) 2 SCC 16; AIR 2002 SC 582; 2002 (46) ALR 377; 2002 (1) AWC 502; 2002 (2) G.L.R. 1707; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 494; JT 2002 (1) SC 83; 2002 (1) MLJ(SC) 189; 2002 (1) RCR(Rent) 137; 2002(1) SCALE 87; 2002 (1) SCJ 239; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 144; 2002 (1) SLT 144; 2002 (1) Supreme 72; 2002 (1) UC 330

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Provincial Small Causes Courts Act, 1887-Section 17, proviso-Code of Civil Procedure, 1908-Order IX, Rule 13-Ex parte decree for eviction and arrears of rent by S.C. Court-Application for setting aside-Condition for maintainability -Proviso to Section 17 P.S.C.C. Act mandatory-Application seeking to set aside ex parte decree-Must be accompanied by deposit in Court of decretal amount-Provision for deposit can be dispensed with by Court if previous application for permission to furnish security filed by applicant-Neither deposit made nor application for dispensing with deposit and permission for furnishing security made-Hence, application for setting aside ex parte decree incompetent-Trial court rightly rejecting application-District Court wrong in interfering in revision-High Court also wrong in not correcting error committed by District Court-Orders of District Court and High Court set aside-Order of trial court rejecting application for setting aside ex parte decree restored.
