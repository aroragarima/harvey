State of Andhra Pradesh and Ors. v Mcdowell and Company And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

21 March 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, A.M. Ahmadi, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 660; (1996) 3 SCC 709; 1996 (3) AD(SC) 428; AIR 1996 SC 1627; 1996 (2) CCC 385; 1996 (2) CLT 148; JT 1996 (3) SC 679; 1997 (1) MLJ(SC) 82; 1996(3) SCALE 146; [1996] 3 S.C.R. 721; 1996 (4) Supreme 604

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Constitutionality, Registration of Foreigners Act, 1939, Engaged In Production, Constitution (Third Amendment) Act, 1954, Andhra Pradesh Prohibition (Amendment) Act, 1995

Summary: Civil Procedure - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 47 - Andhra Pradesh Prohibition (Amendment) Act, 1995, ss. 33 and 35 - Liquor - Prohibition of - Art. 47 of the Constitution considers intoxicating drinks and drugs as injurious to health and impending the raising of level of nutrition and the standard of living of the people and improvement of the public health - Therefore, ordains the State to bring about prohibition of the consumption of intoxicating drinks which obviously include liquors except for medicinal purposes - State has power to completely prohibit the manufactures sales possession, distribution and consumption of potable liquor as a beverage, both because it is inherently a dangerous article of consumption and also because of the directive principle contained in art. 47 except when it is used and consumed for medicinal purposes - A restriction imposed upon a fundamental right can be struck down if it is disproportionate, excessive or unreasonable and quite another thing to say that the Court can strike down enactment if it thinks it unreasonable, unnecessary or unwarranted - Held, that the power to make a law with respect to manufacture and production and its prohibition (among other matters mentioned in Entry 8 in List-II) belongs exclusively to the State Legislatures - Item 26 in the First Schedule to the Industries (Development and Regulation) Act, 1951 must be read subject to Entry 8 - Said item does not and cannot deal with manufacture, production or with prohibition of manufacture and production of intoxicating liquors - All the petitioners before the Court engaged in the manufacture of intoxicating liquors - State Legislature is therefore, perfectly competent to make a law prohibiting their manufacture and production - Further held, the attack upon the constitutionality of the Andhra Pradesh (Amendment) Act 35 of 1995 both on the grounds of legislative incompetence and violation of fundamental rights fails - Amending Act, which has been given retrospective effect from the date of commencement of the Principal Act, i.e., Andhra Pradesh Prohibition Act, 1995, is constitutionally valid - Writ petitions challenging its validity are accordingly dismissed - Appeal dismissed.
