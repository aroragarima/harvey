Jaswant Singh v State

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 79; (1978) 4 SCC 85; (1978) SCC (Cr) 523; AIR 1979 SC 190; 1978 CRLJ 1869; 1978 CrLR(SC) 527; 1979 (15) DLT 172; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 777

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Circumstantial evidence - Reliability of - Whether prosecution has succeeded in bringing home offence u/s. 302 of IPC to appellant? - Held, prosecution case rests wholly on circumstantial evidence and dying declarations - The circumstantial evidence in order to sustain conviction must be complete and must be incapable of explanation on any other hypothesis than that of guilt of accused - The dying declaration which is not recorded by a Magistrate has to be scrutinised closely, but if court is satisfied on a close scrutiny of dying declaration that it is truthful, it is open to court to convict accused on its basis without any independent corroboration - Evidence inevitably points to conclusion that it was appellant alone who intentionally caused death of deceased and there being no reason to interfere with findings concurrently arrived at by court - Appeal dismissed.
