T. S. Baliah v T. S. Rengachari

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 153; AIR 1969 SC 701; [1969] 72 ITR 787; 1969 (2) MLJ(SC) 9; 1969 (1) SCJ 890; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 65

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 28, 52 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 177 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 6 - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Assessing years 1958-62 (A/ys) - Income tax filings - Penalty proceedings - Prosecution - Maintainability - Appellant/assessee was assessed to income-tax and penalty proceedings were instituted u/s. 28 of the 1922 Act and penalties were imposed - Respondent no. 1/authority filed 4 complaints and appellant was charged by Magistrate u/s. 52 of the 1922 Act and u/s. 177 IPC - Appellant filed 4 applications before Magistrate praying that legality of trial for both offences should be tried as preliminary issue, which was dismissed - Appellant filed Criminal revision petitions in HC against orders of Magistrate, which were dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether impugned order of HC and Magistrate was justified - Held, in instant case u/s. 6 of the 1897 Act legal proceeding in respect of offence committed under the 1922 Act might be instituted even after repeal of the 1922 Act by the 1961 Act and punishment might be imposed as if repealing Act had not been passed on behalf of appellant, Kalawati Devi Harlalka v. C.I.T. West Bengal 1967 Indlaw SC 223, relied on - Parliament had not made any detailed provision for institution of prosecutions in respect of proceedings which were pending at commencement of the 1961 Act - Therefore provisions of s. 6 of the 1897 Act were applicable in instant case and prosecution of appellant u/s. 52 of the 1922 Act was legally valid - Thus, when act or omission constituted offence under 2 enactments, offender might be prosecuted and punished under either or both enactments but should not be liable to be punished twice for same offence - Appeals dismissed.
