Indian Administrative Service (S. C. S.) Association, U. P. and Others v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 November 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, A.M. Ahmadi, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 716; (1993) SCC (L&S) 252; (1993) Supp1 SCC 730; 1993 (23) ATC 788; 1993 (1) CLR 907; JT 1992 (Supp) SC 83; 1992(3) SCALE 126; 1993 (1) SLR 69; [1992] Supp2 S.C.R. 389

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Affirmative Action, Right To Promotion

Summary: Constitution - Service - All India Services Act, 1951 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Seniority Rules - Right to promotion - Petitioners were an association and its members representing the officers of testate Civil Service of U.P. - Association represented to the Govt. of India requesting to remove wide disparity prevailing in different States and for promotional avenues from the State Civil Services to All India Administrative Service - Officers from Andhra Pradesh and Kerala, on completion of 8 to 9 years of service were deemed to be qualified for promotion to All India Administrative Service, while the officers from States like Uttar Pradesh and Bihar would be eligible only after 24 to 27 years of service - A committee of senior secretaries constituted by the Union Govt. recommended, after due consideration, to evolve equitable principles of comparable seniority from different States for promotion to I.A.S - Rules were repealed and replaced by I.A.S. under the head 'New Seniority Rules, 1987' - Respondent issued circulars to testate Govts. indicating amendments for fixation of seniority of officers promoted from State Civil Services to I.A.S. to give weightage over and above 4 years in the assignment of year of allotment as per the existing relevant rules, namely, four years for the first 12 years state service with additional weight age of one year for every two to three years completed service subject to a maximum of five years - After receiving suggestions or comments from State Govt.s, the Central India amended the 1987 Rules which amendment was published in the Gazette of India on 3-2-, 1989 titled 'First Amendment Rules' - Proviso thereto was made limiting its operation prospectively from 3-21989 - Petitioners filed WPs for a mandamus to extend the benefits flowing from the First Amendment Rules to its members promoted prior to January 1988 - Whether the proviso was violative of the arts. 14 and 16(1) of the Constitution - Held, that the offending proviso limited the operation of r. 3(3)(ii)(a)and (b) of the First Amendment Rules that such an officer shall not be assigned an year of allotment earlier than the year of allotment assigned to the officers senior to him in that select list or appointed on the basis of an earlier select list - Writ of mandamus commanding the respondents to give full benefit of weightage of r. 3(3) (ii) and (b) of the First Amendment Rules would amount to direct the executive to disobey the proviso which was intra vires of the Constitution - Although Govt. had power to amend the New Seniority Rules by First Amendment Rules prospectively giving weight age of total 9 years services to promotee officers of state Civil services in assigning a year of allotment, no direction or mandamus could be issued commanding the Central Govt. to disobey the proviso or to apply the rules retrospectively to all the officers even to word out monetary benefits - Petition dismissed.
