New India Assurance Company Limited v Genus Power Infrastructure Limited

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 826; (2015) 2 SCC 424; 2015 (2) ALD(SC) 167; 2015(1) ALL MR 988; 2015 (1) AWC 6; JT 2014 (14) SC 95; 2015 (6) MahLJ 545; 2015 (4) MPLJ 578; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 590; 2014(13) SCALE 530

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Insurance

Keywords: Voluntary signing of letter of subrogation, Bald pleas

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Insurance - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 11 - Voluntary signing of letter of subrogation - Bald pleas - Appointment of Arbitrator - Legality - Respondent (company) had a manufacturing unit for which it had purchased a Standard Fire and Special Perils Policy from appellant (Insurance company) - After an incident, respondent's unit was damaged and on assessment of damage on the basis of report of a surveyor, respondent signed a detailed letter of subrogation accepting full and final settlement of its claim under the policy - Thereafter, respondent issued notice to appellant and sought to appoint its nominee arbitrator - However, respondent filed a petition u/s. 11 of the Act before HC, alleging that it had accepted the payment because of extreme financial difficulty, duress and coercion - HC by impugned order appointed a sole arbitrator to adjudicate the disputes between parties - Hence, instant appeal - Whether discharge in instant case upon acceptance of compensation and signing of subrogation letter was not voluntary and whether respondent was subjected to compulsion or coercion and as such could validly invoke the jurisdiction u/s. 11 of the Act.

Held, a bald plea of fraud, coercion, duress or undue influence is not enough and the party who sets up a plea, must prime facie establish the same by placing material before the Chief Justice/his designate. The plea raised by respondent is bereft of any details and particulars, and cannot be anything but a bald assertion. Given the fact that there was no protest or demur raised around the time or soon after the letter of subrogation was signed, that the notice itself was nearly after three weeks and that the financial condition of the respondent was not so precarious that it was left with no alternative but to accept the terms as suggested, Court is of the firm view that the discharge in the instant case and signing of letter of subrogation were not because of exercise of any undue influence. Such discharge and signing of letter of subrogation was voluntary and free from any coercion or undue influence. Upon execution of the letter of subrogation, there was full and final settlement of the claim. No arbitrable dispute existed so as to exercise power u/s. 11 of the Act. The HC was not therefore justified in exercising power u/s. 11 of the Act. Impugned order of HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.
