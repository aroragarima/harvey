Dinesh and Another v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

10 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20979; AIR 2002 SC 2374; 2002 (1) CCR 76; 2002 CRLJ 2970; JT 2001 (10) SC 144; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 200; 2002 (1) SLT 232

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 302 - Son of deceased had an altercation with appellants - Later in day, appellants assaulted deceased and inflicted 4-5 injuries on her head with sharp edged weapons - Appellants convicted u/ss. 302/34 - Conviction challenged - Held, prosecution case was that all three accused assaulted deceased with sharp edged weapons, however, there were only two injuries caused by sharp edged weapon found on body of deceased - Seized weapons were not shown to doctor for soliciting his opinion as to whether injuries on deceased body could be caused by those weapons - No site plan was available on record - Reasonable doubt about appellants having participated in incident and in manner as alleged by prosecution, benefit whereof must go to them - Conviction set aside - Appeal allowed.
