Bhaskar Lal Sharma & Anr. v Monica

Supreme Court of India

27 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 927; (2009) 10 SCC 604; 2009 (161) DLT 739; 2009 (3) JCC 2453; JT 2009 (10) SC 109; 2010 (3) MLJ(Crl) 571; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 866; 2009(10) SCALE 744; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 408

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal

Keywords: Family Business, Interim Maintenance, Distinct Offence, Gifts, Non Bailable Warrants, Stridhana

Summary: Criminal - Family & Personal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 498A and 406 r/w 34, and 420 - Complaint petition was filed by respondent/wife against her husband and his family members - Summons were issued; non-bailable arrest warrants were also issued - S. 482 application for quashing summons and arrest warrants moved - Further complaint was made u/s. 420 IPC - HC dismissed the application for quashing the summoning order - Whether proper? - Held, No, except for appellant no. 2/mother-in-law for offence u/s. 406 - Provisions perused, and contention heard - Husband was married to the respondent for the second time; two issues were there from the first marriage; attempts of conciliation and mediation between respondent/wife and appellant/husband, failed - HC gave finding as to irretrievable breakdown of marriage - Complaint petitions further perused - (A) Whether case u/s. 498A made out in the complaint? - Held, No - Allegations relating to the place where marriage took place has nothing to do with an offence u/s. 498A; Court noted, allegations that appellant no. 2/mother-in-law kicked the respondent/wife and told her that her mother is a liar, may make out some other offence but not one punishable u/s. 498A; threatening respondent/wife by saying that her son may take divorce for the second time could not also bring out the offence under such section - (B) Whether complaint petition disclosed any offence u/s. 406 of the IPC? - Held, Yes for appellant no. 2/mother-in-law of respondent - Streedhan, what is?; deliberated upon - Provisions of s. 405 and 406 perused - Only allegation which brings the case within the purview of s. 406 is that appellant no. 2/mother-in-law had taken all the gifts/cash given by the invitees/guests - Prima facie case u/s. 406 found against appellant no. 2 - Summoning order passed against appellants except appellant no. 2, set aside - Appeal partly allowed.
