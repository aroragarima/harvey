State v A. Parthiban

Supreme Court of India

09 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 676; (2006) 11 SCC 473; AIR 2007 SC 51; 2006 CRLJ 4772; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 54; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 649; 2006(10) SCALE 62

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Illegal Gratification, Old Act, Defence of India Act, 1962, Coal Mines Provident Fund and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1948

Summary: Criminal - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1)(d), 13(2), 19, 7 - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 220, 360 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 8 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5(2) - Probation of Offenders Act, 1958, s. 18 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 71 - HC held that for a single act it would not be proper to convict accused under both sections - Respondent was directed to be released on probation - Appeal against - Whether alleged act is an offence and whether it is capable of being construed as offence under one or more provisions? - Held, act alleged against respondent, of demanding and receiving illegal gratification constitutes an offence both u/s. 7 and u/s. 13(1)(d) - For conviction u/s. 13(2) principles enunciated under Probation Act cannot be extended at all in view of mandate contained in s.18 - Appeal allowed.
