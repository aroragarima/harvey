M.P. Mathur and Others v Delhi Transport Corporation and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1251; (2006) 13 SCC 706; AIR 2007 SC 414; 2007 (5) AWC 4877; 2007 (136) DLT 297; 2006(12) SCALE 446; 2007 (3) SCJ 671; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 519

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Public Sector, Housing

Summary: Land & Property - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Specific Relief Act, 1963 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947 - Between 1962-63 and 1965-66, 5144 tenements were constructed by Municipal Corporation of Delhi in six colonies of Delhi administration - Appellants herein are industrial workers and they were allotted service quarters in Hari Nagar and G T Road colonies - In 1978 above scheme, sponsored by Govt. of India was amended allowing DTC to transfer allotted houses on ownership basis to occupants plaintiffs - 300 tenements belonging to DTC in two colonies of Hari Nagar and G T Road which remained untransferred - On 28-10-1978 a settlement was signed under Industrial Disputes Act between DTC and union of workers under which DTC was given six months' time to take decision on workers' demand for transferring of tenements to occupants - Before expiry of six months, DTC, by way of resolution D/- 18-04-1979, decided in principle to sell service quarters to occupants - By another resolution D/- 31-08-1979, DTC approved scheme to sell tenements to occupants subject to certain conditions being satisfied by each of occupants - However, on 3-12-1979 Chairman of DTC requested board to reconsider its decision - Ultimately, vide resolution D/- 02-03-1981 DTC board rescinded its decision to sell and stated that it will not implement policy decision of Govt. of India - (A) Whether resolution of DTC D/- 02-03-1981 was flawed and baseless? - Held, DTC, in principle, had agreed to transfer tenements on ownership basis to industrial workers - However, when DTC examined ground reality, it found acute shortage of resources coupled with increased costs of replacement running into Rs. 3 crores - Central Govt. also did not fund full cost of construction - In circumstances, ultimately DTC informed Govt. of India that under above circumstances it was not possible for it to implement scheme - Conduct of DTC cannot be faulted - There was no contract entered into by DTC with any individual workman - In circumstances, Resolution D/- 31-08-1979 bearing No. 139/79 was a tentative decision and not a final and binding decision as alleged - Therefore, it cannot be said that said Resolution created a legal right by itself - (B) Whether, on facts and circumstances of this cast, plaintiffs could compel transfer of tenements in their favor on basis of promissory estoppel? - Further held, court find that in present case doctrine of promissory estoppel had no application - On balancing of equities we are of view that DTC which is a public sector undertaking had to act in public interest in sense that had to keep transport service running for which they had to accommodate in-service industrial workers which they could not have done if it had to sell existing service quarters to retirees - Appeal dismissed.
