Pernod Ricard India Private Limited v Commissioner of Customs, ICD Tughlakabad

Supreme Court of India

26 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 922; (2010) 8 SCC 313; JT 2010 (7) SC 602; [2010] 8 S.C.R. 996

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Price List, Discount, Customs Valuation (Determination Of Price Of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988, Cab

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Customs Act, 1962, s. 130E - Customs Valuation (Determination of Prices of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988, r. 6 - Doctrine of merger - Undervaluation - Identical goods - Scope of adjustment - Appellant was a subsidiary of the 'X' (Foreign Company), manufacturing/ blending of non-molasses based spirits - Appellant imported Concentrate of Alcoholic Beverages (CAB) from 'Y', a subsidiary of 'X' - Revenue issued two show cause notices to the appellant proposing demand of differential duty of customs in respect of said imports - Commissioner adjudicated upon both the show cause notices and confirmed the demand - Tribunal rejected the plea of the appellant that supplier was a 'related person' and remanded the matter to the adjudicating authority for a fresh consideration on the question of applicability of r. 6 of 1998 Rules - HC dismissed appeal filed against said order of tribunal - SC also dismissed appeal against order of the HC - Subsequently, the tribunal in another round of appeal again reopened the issue of applicability of r. 6 of 1988 Rules - (A) Whether the Tribunal was justified in re-examining the question of applicability of r. 6 of 1988 Rules? - Once a statutory right of appeal is invoked, dismissal of appeal by the SC, whether by a speaking order or non speaking order, the doctrine of merger does apply - Held, no - Issue related to the applicability of r. 6 of 1988 Rules had attained finality on the summary dismissal of the appellant's appeal by SC - Hence, doctrine of Merger would be attracted and the appellant is estopped from raising the issue of applicability of r. 6 of 1988 Rules - Tribunal erred in re-opening and examining afresh the question as to applicability of r. 6 of 1988 Rules - (B) Whether the direction by the Tribunal regarding adjustment to the tune of 20% in the price difference between CAB of the appellant and the corresponding CAB of the competitor, on account of volume of imports, is justified? - Held, no - In the absence of some documentary evidence indicating that any rebate/discount was given to the appellant by the supplier, adjustments u/r. 5(1)(c) of 1988 Rules cannot be justified - In the present case, it is evident from the impugned order that though the Tribunal had felt that requisite evidence to establish the range of adjustment was lacking and for that purpose, according to it the matter was required to be remanded to the Commissioner but being influenced by the fact that there had already been three rounds of appeals to the Tribunal, it undertook the exercise itself - Hence, approach of the tribunal was not in order and therefore, in the absence of any demonstrated evidence, its direction for ad-hoc adjustment @ 20%, cannot be sustained - Appeal dismissed
