B. S. E. Brokers Forum and Others v Securities and Exchange Board of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19971; (2001) 3 SCC 482; AIR 2001 SC 1010; 2001 (1) BC 779; 2001 (2) Bom.C.R. 326; [2001] 104 Comp Cas 506; 2001 (1) CompLJ 193; JT 2001 (2) SC 242; 2001(1) SCALE 575; 2001 (2) SLT 45; 2001 (1) Supreme 479

Case Digest

Subject: Securities Exchange Board of India

Keywords: Excessive, Depositories Act, 1996, Securities and Exchange Board of India (Stockbrokers and Sub-brokers) Regulations, 1992

Summary: Securities Exchange Board of India - Companies Act, 1956 - Constitution, 1950, arts. 14, 19, 123, 226 and 265 - Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992, ss. 3, 4, 11, 12, 29 and 30 - Securities Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Evidence Act, 1872 - Securities and Exchange Board of India (Stockbrokers and Sub-brokers) Regulations, 1992, regns. 3 and 10 - Validity of regn. 10 of Securities and Exchange Board of India (Stockbrokers and Sub-brokers) Regulations, 1992, r/w. sch. III challenged - Court held, it would be futile to contend that the impugned fee merely because it is levied on the basis of the turnover of the brokers would either amount to a turnover tax or a tax on income, levy based on annual turnover of the brokers is valid - Petition dismissed.
