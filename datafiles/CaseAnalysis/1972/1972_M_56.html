Gauri Shanker v Messrs Hindustan Trust Private Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 April 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 563; (1973) 2 SCC 127; AIR 1972 SC 2091; 1974 (2) ILR(Del) 35; 1972 RCJ 850

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 15 and 39(2) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - (A) Tenancy - (1) Pure question of law not raised or abandoned in Lower Appellate Court - Permissibility to raise - (2) Raising a point in Memo of Appeal - Whether show that point was argued or passed before Appellate Court - Statement that no such point was argued - (B) Practice & Procedure - Plea of absence of valid notice terminating contractual tenancy - Plea taken in original written statement - Amendment to include plea after 8 years - Permissibility - Held, can be allowed to be raised but Court must exercise its discretion in permitting it and look at all facts especially conduct of party seeking to raise it - Is not sufficient to show that point was argued or passed before Appellate Court and statement in judgment that no such point was argued has prima facie to be accepted as correct - Further held, should not be allowed on account of gross delay and laches - Order accordingly.
