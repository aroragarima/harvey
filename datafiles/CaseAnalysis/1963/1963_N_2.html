N.A. Malbari and Bros v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

25 November 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 210; AIR 1964 SC 1807; [1964] 51 ITR 295; 1964 SCJ 229; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 560

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s 28 - Assessment - Penalty - Statement of account - Concealment - assessment of appellant/ a firm for year 1951-52 in respect of which accounting year was calendar year 1950 - Assessee carried on business at S it had a branch at B to which it exported cloth from I - Branch made purchases locally and sold them - During last world war business at B had been in abeyance but it was re- started after termination of hostilities - In its return for assessment year 1949-50 assessee did not include any profit of B branch but stated books of account of B branch were not available and therefore its profit might now be assessed on an estimate basis subject to action u/ss. 34, 35 on production of statement of account - Assessment was made on basis of profit at 5 % on export to B branch - For year 1950-51 there was no reference to B branch in return and a similar estimate was made - For year, 1951-52 also B business profits were not shown but Income-tax Officer issued a notice to assessee u/s. 22(4) of Act to produce profits and loss account and balance-sheet with relevant books - Whether levy of Rs. 68,501/- as penalty for concealment in original return for assessment year 1951-52 was legal - Held - Assessee excused itself by alleging that books were at B and profit and loss account and balance-sheet could not be drawn up unless its partner, H went personally and there was no certainty as to when he would go there and promising that in following year those accounts for calendar year 1950 would be produced - Income-tax Officer made an estimate of sales of B branch at Rs. 7,50,000 and of net profits at 5%, amounting to Rs. 37, 5001 - On same day issued a notice under s. 28(3) of Act requiring assessee to show cause why a penalty u/s.28(1)(c) for concealment of particulars of income of 1950 should not be levied - Assessee was heard on notice and Income-tax Officer imposed a penalty on it as its explanation was not acceptable - No application to present case - If default which entailed penalty was within knowledge of authority when it passed final order in prior proceeding no penalty could be later imposed - Appeal dismissed.
