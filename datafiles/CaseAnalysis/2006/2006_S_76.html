Santosh Kumar v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

25 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 435; (2006) 10 SCC 595; (2007) 1 SCC (Cr) 28; AIR 2006 SC 3098; 2006 CRLJ 4594; 2006 (3) JCC 1575; JT 2006 (8) SC 171; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 123; 2006(8) SCALE 560; [2006] Supp5 S.C.R. 381

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, s. 376(2)(g) - CrPC, 1973, s. 313 - Conviction and sentence of 10 years RI and a fine of Rs. 500/- Whether proved facts establish offence of rape? - Held, absence of injuries on private parts of a victim specially a married lady cannot, ipso facto, lead to an inference that no rape has been committed - Medical examination report of victim shows that she received injuries on front portion of body and also on her hands - Prosecution has established its case against appellant beyond any shadow of doubt - Appeal dismissed.
