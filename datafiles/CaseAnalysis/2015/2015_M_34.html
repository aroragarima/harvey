Adani Agri Fresh Limited v Mahaboob Sharif and others

Supreme Court of India

02 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 842; AIR 2016 SC 92; 2016 (2) ALD(SC) 81; 2016 (114) ALR 871; 2016 (1) AWC 509; 2016 (1) CLT(SC) 52; 2016 (1) DRTC 338; 2016 (2) RCR(Civil) 12; 2015(13) SCALE 488B

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Communication

Summary: Banking & Finance - Contract & Commercial - Grant of injunction - Unconditional bank guarantee - Appellant, supplier of fruit and vegetables, entered into a contractual agreement with a wholesale dealer X in fruits, whose proprietor was respondent No.1 - Appellant required respondent No.1 to furnish bank guarantees, whereby the appellant would be entitled to recover the proceeds of the products, transported by it to X - Bank guarantee was unconditional guarantee - Appellant issued an 'Outstanding Certificate' seeking payment, on account of despatch of fruit - Payment was not made by respondent No.1, therefore, the bank guarantee was sought to be invoked - Respondent No.1 filed suit, wherein interim order was passed by Trial Court, whereby banks were restrained from making payment of schedule amount - Order was affirmed - Hence, instant petition.

Held, the courts below were not justified in injuncting the invocation of the bank guarantees, executed by the Bank, at the instance of X. Bank was directed to honour the same forthwith. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - Bank is always obliged to honour its guarantee as long as it is an unconditional and irrevocable one.
