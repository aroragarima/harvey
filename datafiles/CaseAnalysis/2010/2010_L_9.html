Lalu Prasad Yadav and Another v State Of Bihar And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

01 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 239; (2010) 5 SCC 1; AIR 2010 SC 1561; 2010 CRLJ 2427; JT 2010 (3) SC 433; 2010 (2) MLJ(Crl) 621; 2010 (2) RCR(Criminal) 480; 2010(3) SCALE 443

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1882, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1861, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1872

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 377 - Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, 1946 - Investigation and trial by the Central agency - Right of appeal of State Government - Appellant/former State Chief Ministers allegedly acquired asset disproportionate to their known sources of income during their tenure - Delhi Special Police Establishment (CBI) investigated the case as per the HC direction and charge-sheeted appellants, however, the Court of Special Judge (CBI) acquitted appellants - Central Government after considering the findings of the Trial Court took a decision that no ground whatsoever was made for filing an appeal against the Trial Court judgment - State Government, however, filed leave to appeal against the Trial Court order of acquittal before the HC - Appellants raised the preliminary objections with regard to the maintainability of the appeal - Single Judge overruled the objections and held that appeal preferred by the State Government was maintainable - Whether the State Government has competence to file an appeal from the judgment passed by Special Judge, CBI acquitting the appellants when the case has been investigated by the Delhi Special Police Establishment (CBI) - Held, Central Government appoints its public prosecutors for conducting prosecution, appeal or other proceedings on its behalf and a State Government appoints its public prosecutors in conducting prosecution, appeal or other proceedings on its behalf and hence one has no control over the other - Further, investigation under 1946 Act is a central investigation and the officers concerned are under the superintendence of the officer appointed by the Central Government - Central Government only has the superintendence over the Delhi Special Police Establishment and it is the Central Government only which is concerned with the investigation of the case by Delhi Special Police Establishment and its ultimate result - Legislature has maintained a mutually exclusive division in the matter of appeal from an order of acquittal inasmuch as the competent authority to appeal from an order of acquittal in two types of cases referred to in s. 377(2) is the Central Government and the authority of the State Government in relation to such cases has been excluded - State Government is not competent to direct its public prosecutor to present appeal from the judgment passed by the Special Judge, CBI and therefore, impugned HC order is set aside - Appeal allowed.
