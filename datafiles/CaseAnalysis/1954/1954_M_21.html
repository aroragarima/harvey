Chatturbhuj Vithaldas Jasani v Moreshwar Parashram and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 February 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 23; AIR 1954 SC 236; 1954 SCJ 315; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 817

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Election; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Scheduled Caste

Summary: Election - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 299 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss. 230(3) and 236(3) - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 7(d) - (A) Whether candidate was subject to disqualifications u/s. 7(d) of Representation of People Act, 1951 - Held, yes - Contract in question is hit by s. 7(d) - Candidate was interested in a contract for supply of goods to Central Govt. - (B) Whether candidate belonging to Scheduled Caste had ceased to be a member of caste by virtue of conversion ? - Further Further held, No - Despite conversion, candidate continued to be associated with with his previous caste - Nomination wrongfully rejected - Convert may renounce old law by which he was bound, as he has renounced his old religion, or if he thinks fit, he may abide by old law, notwithstanding he has renounced religion - Conversion to sect imports intellectual acceptance of certain ideological tenets and does not alter convert's caste status - Appeal dismissed.
