Apollo Tyres Limited with Commissioner of Income v Commissioner of Income Tax, Kochiand Apllo Tyres

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 302; (2002) 9 SCC 1; AIR 2002 SC 2131; 2002 (174) CTR 521; [2002] 255 ITR 273; JT 2002 (4) SC 556; 2002 (2) KLT 283; 2002(4) SCALE 407; 2002 (3) SLT 536; 2002 (4) Supreme 126; 169 TAXATION 522; [2002] 122 TAXMAN 562

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax Act - Sections 115 -J, 32AB - Unit Trust of India Act - Section 32(3) - Held, (i) the assessing officer does not have the jurisdiction to go behind the net profit shown in the profit and loss account except to the extent provided in the Explanation to Section 115 -J; (ii) if, the dividend income earned by the assessee company from its investment in the UTI is shown under a different head of income, it would not deprive the company of its benefit under Section 32AB so long as it is held that the investment in the units of the UTI by the assessee company is in the course of its "eligible business"; and (iii) In the absence of any specific deeming in the UTI Act in regard to the units as shares, it would be erroneous to extend the provisions of Section 32(3) of the UTI Act to the units of UTI for the purpose of holding that the unit is a share.
