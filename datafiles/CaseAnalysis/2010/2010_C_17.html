Central Bureau of Investigation (C.B.I.) v Hopeson Ningshen and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, Deepak Verma, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 329; (2010) 5 SCC 115; AIR 2010 SC 1617; 2010 (3) JCC 2211; JT 2010 (4) SC 392; 2010 (2) RCR(Criminal) 833; 2010(4) SCALE 540; [2010] 5 S.C.R. 666

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure (Amendment) Bill, 2008

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 406 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 365, 368 - Transfer of cases - Transfer petition filed by Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) seeking transfer of cases from the Court of the Chief Judicial Magistrate, Manipur to a competent Criminal Court in Delhi - In these cases, the respondent has been accused of the kidnapping and murder of three government employees in the State of Manipur - Respondent is an activist of a militant organisation - CBI has urged that the trial in these cases be transferred to Delhi, in view of social unrest created in the wake of the killings and in view of the specific threat to the life of the respondent-accused which could frustrate the objective of conducting a fair trial - However, the near relatives of the deceased persons have objected to the transfer of the cases - Held, referred Zahira Habibulla H. Sheikh v. State of Gujarat 2004 INDLAW SC 408 wherein the Court observed that denial of a fair trial is as much injustice to the accused as is to the victim and the society - Fair trial would mean a trial before an impartial judge, a fair prosecutor and atmosphere of judicial calm and fair trial means a trial in which bias or prejudice for or against the accused, the witnesses, or the cause which is being tried is eliminated - In the present case, in order to ensure that a fair trial takes place in the cases in question, Court must account for the interests of the accused, the witnesses, the prosecutors, the near relatives of the victims as well as society at large - CBI in its capacity as the investigating agency has clearly conveyed the risks associated with conducting the trial in Manipur - Furthermore, conducting the trial in Manipur could also reasonably lead to more friction in the State of Manipur which in turn could affect the trial proceedings themselves - There is also a risk of intimidation of the witnesses as well as undue prejudice seeping into the minds of those who may be involved in the legal proceedings in different capacities - In this scenario, it would be expedient in the ends of justice to conduct the trial in Delhi - Impugned cases be transferred from Manipur to a designated CBI Court in New Delhi - Petitions disposed of.
