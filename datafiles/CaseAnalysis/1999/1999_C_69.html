Mukesh Kumarand Others v Col. Harbans Waraich and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 792; (1999) 9 SCC 380; AIR 2000 SC 172; 1999 (37) ALR 859; 2000 (2) CalLT(SC) 33; JT 1999 (8) SC 563; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 110; 2000 (124) PLR 179; 1999(6) SCALE 644; 1999 (9) SLT 173; 2000 (Supp) RD 451; [1999] Supp4 S.C.R. 141; 1999 (9) Supreme 338

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, s. 21 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 1, r. 10 - Respondent No. 4, as Manager or Karta of Hindu Undivided Family comprising defendants Nos. 1 to 5, entered into oral agreement to sell suit property to plaintiffs and received a sum as earnest money - Hindu Undivided Family was heavily in debt facing suits - Pleading and evidence showed that plaintiffs were always ready and willing to perform their part of contract, but respondents committed breach of contract - (A) Whether finding recorded by Courts below in regard to legal necessity is not correct? - Fact that joint family properties have been attached for payment of income tax extending to over Rs. 3 lakhs itself was sufficient to hold that sale of property was for purpose of benefit of joint family - Held, no infirmity in finding recorded by Courts below as to legal necessity - (B) Whether half of property sought to be purchased by third plaintiff transposed from array of defendants and that his transposition as plaintiff was far beyond period of limitation for enforcing agreement in relation to sale of his half share in property in question? - Further held, s. 21(2) of Limitation Act applies only to those cases where claim of person transposed as plaintiff can be sustained on plaint as originally filed or where person remaining as a plaintiff after said transposition can sustain his claim against transposed defendant on basis of plaint as originally filed - Where a suit as originally filed is properly framed with proper parties on record, mere change of a party from array of defendants to that of plaintiffs u/O. 1, r. 10 of CPC will not make him a new plaintiff and will not bring case within this Section and in such a case sub- s. (4) will not apply - Appeal dismissed.
