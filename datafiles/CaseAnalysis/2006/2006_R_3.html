State Bank of India v Messrs Ranjan Chemicals Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1010; (2007) 1 SCC 97; 2006 (6) ALD(SC) 138; 2006 (65) ALR 800; 2007 (1) AWC 340; 2007 (1) DRTC 164; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 52; 2007 (2) MPLJ 18; 2007 (145) PLR 24; 2007 (1) RLW 764; 2006(10) SCALE 150; 2006 (8) SCJ 154; [2006] Supp7 S.C.R. 145

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cash Credit, Multiplicity Of Proceedings, Cash Credit Facility

Summary: Banking & Finance - Recovery of Debts due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993, ss. 18, 19(1), 19(6), 19(7), 19(8), 19(9), 19(10) and 19(11) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 8 r. 6 A - Respondent-company filed suit for damages claiming that because of delay on part of Appellant-bank in fulfilling its obligations under cash credit facility, Respondent-company had incurred loss - Appellant-bank field application before Debt Recovery Tribunal for recovery of dues from Respondent-company - Bank also moved application in suit of Respondent for transfer of suit to Debt Recovery Tribunal for joint trial - Court opined that there was no bar under DRT Act for Civil Court to entertain such suit - High Court affirmed order of Subordinate Judge - Whether HC right to affirm order of Subordinate Court? - Held, Court has power in appropriate case to transfer suit for being tried with another if circumstances warranted and justified it - Both proceedings involved nature of loan transaction and cash credit facility extended, relationship that had spring out of transactions, right and obligations arising out of them, their breach if any, and who was responsible for breach and its extent - Duplication of evidence could be avoided if two actions were tried together - Hence, fit case where two actions should have been ordered to be tried together - Appeal allowed.
