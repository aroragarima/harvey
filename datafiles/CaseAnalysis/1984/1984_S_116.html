Bhim Singh v State of Jammu and Kashmir

Supreme Court of India

31 August 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 377; (1984) Supp SCC 504; (1985) SCC (Cr) 60; 1984 CRLR 439; 1984(2) SCALE 370

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution

Keywords: Detention Order

Summary: Administrative - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Detention order - Habeas corpus - Petitioner filed instant Habeas Corpus petition u/art. 32 of the Constitution against the order of detention passed against detenue, who was sitting member of Legislative Assembly.

Held, counsel for respondent (State) has placed on record a copy of the teleprinter message from the concerned authority that detenu, whose detention was challenged in instant petition has been released on 24-8-1984. It is difficult to believe that the State would make through its counsel a wholly false statement in respect of a detenu. Petitioner is at liberty to take other steps if the petitioner is not released as stated by State counsel. Petition disposed of.
