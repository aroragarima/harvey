Ravinder Singh v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

07 February 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 124; (1975) SCC (Cr) 202; (1975) 3 SCC 742; AIR 1975 SC 856; 1975 CRLJ 765; 1975 CrLR(SC) 207; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 453

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3, 113 and 115 - Constitution, art. 20 - CrPC, 1898, ss. 403 and 423 - (A) Evidence - (1) Credibility of approver's testimony - (2) When principle of 'Issue estoppel' to be invoked - (C) Appreciation of evidence in criminal case - (D) Appeal against acquittal - Interference by High Court with finding of trial Court - Held, corroboration with facts deposed to by the independent witnesses - If it appears by record of itself or as explained by proper evidence, that the point was determined in favour of a prisoner in a previous criminal trial which is brought in issue on a second criminal trial of the same prisoner - Once the evidence of a witness has been rejected by the court it should not be made a basis for judging the veracity of other evidence by the yardstick of that unreliable evidence - Where prima facie appreciation of the recorded evidence is opposed to even a reasonable appraisement of the same bearing in mind the relevant point or points sought to be established by the evidence, there will be no option to HC in the interest of justice but to step in and interfere with the acquittal - Appeal Dismissed.
