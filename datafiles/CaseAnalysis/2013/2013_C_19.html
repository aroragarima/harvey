Abu Salem Abdul Qayyum Ansari v Central Bureau of Investigation and another

Supreme Court of India

05 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 487; (2013) 12 SCC 1; JT 2013 (11) SC 14; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 42; 2013(10) SCALE 31; [2013] 7 S.C.R. 1061

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; International; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Prevention of Damage To Public Property Act, 1984

Summary: (A) International - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Extradition Act, 1962 - Extradited convict - Additional charges - Withdrawal of - Appellant accused was extradited from Republic of Portugal - However, Portugal Court terminated extradition - Respondent investigating agency leveled additional charges against appellant, which were affirmed by SC in Abu Salem Abdul Qayoom Ansari v. State of Maharashtra and Another, 2010 Indlaw SC 727 - Respondent filed instant applications seeking to modify said SC judgment for withdrawing such additional charges - Held, Constitution bestowed upon SC inherent power to reconsider, modify and revise its earlier decisions - Respondent sought modification for harmonizing divergent views with regard to violation of Principle of Speciality - Petition was moved only to avoid endless deferral of appellant's trial - No prejudice would be caused if the present application for modification was allowed - However, it should not be construed that SC was reviewing judgment of Portugal Court - Respondent was allowed to withdraw the charges - Appeals disposed of.

(B) International - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Extradition Act, 1962 - Extradition - Annulled by foreign court - Effect - Appellant accused was extradited from Republic of Portugal - However, Portugal Court terminated extradition - Appellant filed applications before Trial Court challenging extradition - Trial Court dismissed applications - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the order of Extradition stood annulled - Held, Portugal Court categorically held that Portuguese law did not provide for any specific consequence for violation of the Principle of Speciality - Findings of Portugal Court should not be construed as a direction to UOI to return appellant to Portugal - Govt. of Portugal did not seek return of appellant on the legal basis on findings of Portugal Court - Hence, impugned order of extradition stood valid and effective in the eyes of law - Appeals disposed of.
