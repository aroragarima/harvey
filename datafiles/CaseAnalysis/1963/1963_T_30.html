Bombay Union of Journalists and Others v State of Bombay and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 December 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 226; AIR 1964 SC 1617; 1964 (8) FLR 236; 1964 (1) LLJ 351; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 22

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Failure Report, Trade Union Act, 1926, Working Journalists (Conditions of Service) and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1955

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10(1), 12(4), 12(5), 25 F(c), 25 F (a), 25 F(b) and 38 - Industrial Disputes (Bombay) Rules, 1956, r. 80 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - (A) Whether Govt. is precluded from considering prima facie merits of dispute and refuse to refer dispute u/s. 10? - Held, no, it is not - On questions regarding reference to be made u/s. 12(5) Govt. has to act u/s. 10(1) of Act - S. 10(1) confers discretion on appropriate Govt. either to refer dispute or not - S. 12(5) imposes an obligation on it to record reasons for not making reference when dispute has gone through conciliation and a failure report has been made u/s. 12(4) - On disputed questions of law, appropriate Govt. should not purport to reach a final decision on said questions of law, because that would normally lie within jurisdiction of Industrial Tribunal - On disputed questions of fact, appropriate Govt. cannot purport to reach final conclusions, for that again would be province of Industrial Tribunal - A prima facie examination of merits cannot be said to be foreign to enquiry which appropriate Govt. is entitled to make in dealing with a dispute u/s. 10(1) but it can refuse on this basis only - (B) Whether r. 80 of Act treats notice u/s. 25F(c) as notice subsequent to retrenchment or as condition precedent to retrenchment? - Further held, provisions of s. 25F(c) being directory, would treat as notice subsequent to retrenchment and not as condition precedent to retrenchment - R. 80 shows that where employer has retrenched employee by offering to pay him requisite amount of remuneration in lieu of notice prescribed by s. 25F(a), employer is required to serve notice of said retrenchment within seven days of date of retrenchment, and that means that in such a case, notice has not to be served on Govt. before retrenchment is effected - (C) Whether court can enquire propriety or correctness of reasons for refusal of dispute in a petition for mandamus? - Further held, yes, Court can - Because Court is not sitting in appeal when entertaining such mandamus - Party concerned should be told clearly and precisely reasons why no reference is made, because object of s. 12 (5) appears to be to require appropriate Govt. to state its reasons for refusing to make a reference, so that reasons should stand public scrutiny but that does not mean that a party challenging validity of Govt's decision not to make a reference can require Court in writ proceedings to examine propriety or correctness of said reasons - But argument that of pleas raised by retrenched workman, two have been considered and not third, would not necessarily entitle party to claim a writ u/art. 226 of Constitution - (D) Whether non compliance with cl. (c) of s. 25 F, assuming it to be condition precedent, give rights to mandamus - Further held, no, it does not - Cl. (c) of s.25 F cannot receive same construction as cls. (a) and (b) of s. 25 F - Reading latter part of cls. (a) and (c) together, it seems to follow that in cases falling under latter part of cl. (a) notice prescribed by cl. (c) has to be given not before retrenchment, but after retrenchment; otherwise option given to employer to bring about immediate retrenchment of workman on paying him wages in lieu of notice would be rendered nugatory - Use of word 'until' no doubt are in favour of contention that cl. (c) is a condition precedent, but context seems to require a different treatment to provision contained in cl. (c) - Appeal allowed.
