Mrs. Satimbla Sharma And Ors. v St. Paul’S Senior Secondary School And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 500; (2011) 13 SCC 760; 2011(5) ALL MR 927; 2011 (6) AWC 5672; 2011 (5) ESC 734; 2011 (130) FLR 1065; JT 2011 (8) SC 611; 2011 (3) KLT 674; 2011 (3) LCD 1869; 2011 (8) MLJ(SC) 554; 2011(9) SCALE 92; [2011] 10 S.C.R. 203; 2011 (5) SLR 427; 2011 (4) UPLBEC 3381

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Service

Keywords: Executive Instructions, Grant-in-Aid Rules

Summary: Service - Constitution - Education - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 12, 14, 30, 36, 39 - Teachers in unaided private minority school - Parity in pay scale - Entitlement - Appellant teachers of the 1st respondent unaided private school were being paid less than the teachers of Government schools and aided schools in the State - Appellants filed writ petition seeking a direction to respondents to pay the salary and allowances at par with the teachers of Government schools and Government aided schools - Single Judge allowed writ petition, however, DB, in appeal set aside the Single Judge order - Whether the appellants were entitled to parity of pay at par with the teachers of Government schools and aided schools - Held, teachers of Government schools were paid out of the Government funds and the teachers of Government aided schools were paid mostly out of the Government funds, whereas the teachers of private unaided minority schools were paid out of the fees and other resources of the private schools - Further, unaided private minority schools over which the Government had no administrative control because of their autonomy u/art. 30(1) of the Constitution, were not State within the meaning of art. 12 of the Constitution - As the right to equality u/art. 14 of the Constitution was available against the State, it should not be claimed against unaided private minority schools - Further, unaided private schools were not State within the meaning of art. 36 r/w art. 12 of the Constitution and as the obligation to ensure equal pay for equal work in art. 39(d) was on the State, a private unaided minority school was not under any duty to ensure equal pay for equal work - Hence, HC rightly held in the impugned judgment that the teachers of private unaided minority schools had no right to claim salary equal to that of their counterparts working in Government schools and Government aided schools - Appeal disposed of.
