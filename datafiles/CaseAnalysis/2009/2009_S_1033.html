Hari Ram v State of Rajasthan and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 606; (2009) 13 SCC 211; 2009 (6) CTC 464; 2009 (3) JCC 2296; 2010 (3) MLJ(Crl) 632; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 878; 2009(6) SCALE 695; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 623; 2009 (2) UC 1029

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Women & Children

Keywords: Children's, Date Of Occurrence, School Register, Juvenile Offenders, Jharkhand Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Rules, 2003, West Bengal Children's Act, 1959

Summary: Women & Children - Criminal - Juvenile Justice Act, 1986 - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000, ss.2(k), 2(l), 7A, 15, 20, 49 and 64 (Provisions as stood amended vide amendment Act, 2006) - Juvenile Justice Rules, 2007, rr. 12 and 98 - Could a person who was not a juvenile within the meaning of the 1986 Act when the offence was committed, but had not completed 18 years, be governed by the provisions of the JJ Act, 2000, and be declared as a juvenile in relation to the offence alleged to have been committed by him? - Conjoint reading of ss. 2(k), 2(l), 7A, 20 and 49 r/w rr. 12 and 98 of Rules, 2007 - Held, Yes - Questions which are fundamental to the understanding and implementation of the objects for which Act, 2000 was enacted, deliberated upon - Scheme is rehabilitatory in nature and not adversarial; requirement of complete change of mind set for implementing the said law, stressed upon - Admitted fact that appellant was less than 18 years of age at the time of offence - Offences charged with, ss. 148, 302, 149, 325 r/w 149 and 323 r/w 149 of IPC - Two main questions on the subject on which controversy at hand depends, is at rest now; first question was settled in 'Pratap Singh vs. State of Jharkhand & Another [(2005) 3 SCC 551]' after which amendment was brought in 2006 in JJ Act 2000, second question was also settled therein but is now overruled vide such amendment; firstly, juvenility of a person in conflict with law has to be reckoned from the date of the incident and not from the date on which cognizance was taken by the Magistrate, secondly, provisions of JJ Act, 2000 were also made applicable to juveniles who had not completed eighteen years of age on the date of commission of offence - '..all persons who were below the age of 18 years on the date of commission of the offence even prior to 1st April, 2001(commencement date of JJ Act, 2000), would be treated as juveniles, even if the claim of juvenility was raised after they had attained the age of 18 years on or before the date of commencement of the Act and were undergoing sentence upon being convicted'; such position stands re-emphasised by virtue of amendments introduced in s. 20, by inserting proviso and explanation - Impugned judgement of HC, set aside - Matter remitted to Juvenile Justice Board - Appropriate directions issued - Appeal allowed.
