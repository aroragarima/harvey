State of West Bengal and another v T. K. Ghosh and others

Supreme Court of India

04 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1749; (2005) 10 SCC 339

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Public Works Department Code, 1970

Summary: Service - Similar emoluments for similar work - Entitlement - Relevant factors - Draftsmen consisting of different categories were members of Subordinate Engineering Services - Their payscale were revised - Thereafter, Pay Committee was constituted and draftsmen were placed in five categories - Draftsmen/respondents placed in Categories (ii) and (iii), namely, those who had Govt. school-pass certificate or SF certificate and those who had only experience to their credit, claimed to be placed in category of overseer and sub-overseer in scale of Rs 380-910 - Respondents filed petition in HC claiming same emoluments for similar work and also other allowances, like special allowance, cycle allowance and arduous duty allowance, etc. - Single Judge of HC allowed petition - Appeal preferred against impugned order before DB was dismissed - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether judgments and orders passed by HC that draftsmen were discharging similar duties and they were entitled to same emoluments were not sustainable - Held, one would be entitled to equal pay for equal work as per proposition of law but that alone could not be sole criterion where it was not established that all persons of Subordinate Engineering Services constitute one class of draftsmen performing identical nature of duties - However, even though there could be similar nature of work yet distinction was permissible based upon their educational qualifications - Further where more than one class of employees were designated as draftsmen, having qualifications ranging from overseers to nil technical qualification or only experience, duties of higher responsibilities requiring more efficient handling would obviously be assigned to persons having better and higher technical qualifications rather than to those who had no qualification or had mere experience or some other certificate plus experience - Hence, judgments and orders passed by HC were not sustainable - Appeal allowed.
