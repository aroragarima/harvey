Union of India v Pramod Gupta (D) by Lrs. and others

Supreme Court of India

07 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 829; (2005) 12 SCC 1; JT 2005 (8) SC 203; 2005 (4) RCR(Civil) 235; 2005(7) SCALE 187; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 48

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Valuation Of Land, West Bengal Land (Requisition and Acquisition) Act, 1948, Zamindar, Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands (Amendment) Act, 1956, Punjab Minor Mineral Rules, 1934

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 18, 25, 28, 54, 6 - CPC, 1908, o. 41, r. 27, o. 6, rr. 17, 18 - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 142, 19(1)(f) - Delhi Land Reforms Act, 1954, s. 7 - Punjab Land Revenue Act, 1887, ss. 31, 41, 42(2), 60-C - Mines Act, 1952, s. 2(j) - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 4 - Punjab Tenancy Act, 1887, s. 5 - Punjab Minor Mineral Rules, 1934, rr. 3, 5, 7 - Punjab Tenants (Security of Tenure) Act, 1950 - Compensation - Enhancement of compensation by reference court and also granted compensation @ Rs. 10,000/- Per bigha for minor mineral - HC dismissed appeals filed against enhancement - Appealed here - Principle of res judicata - Held, Reference Court and HC unfortunately did not consider question as to what amount was required to be expended for bringing said area back to normal so as to enable University authorities to raise construction thereon - Impugned judgments are set aside and matters are remitted to HC for fresh consideration - Appeals disposed of.
