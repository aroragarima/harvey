Usha Rectifier Corporation (India) Limited (Presently known as Usha (India) Limited) v Commissioner of Central Excise, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 31; (2011) 11 SCC 571; 2011 (86) ALR 44; 2011 (263) E.L.T. 655; JT 2011 (3) SC 183; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 843; 2011(1) SCALE 416; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 347

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 11A, 11AB and 11AC - Manufacture - Testing equipments - Appellant is a manufacturer of electronic transformers, semi-conductor devices and other electrical and electronics equipments - During the course of such manufacture the appellant also manufactured machinery in the nature of testing equipments to test the final products of the assessee company costing Rs. 31,27,405/- - A show cause notice was issued to the appellant directing them to show cause as to why central excise duty should not be levied on it along with interest and penalty - The appellant submitted that for research and development wing of the company and to carry out trials, experiments and for undertaking development job based on the latest technology available worldwide or through their own resources it had bought out items, parts, components, etc., and for that purpose such items were assembled in the factory - Appellant also submitted that after research is so done, and if it was not successful, the same was disassembled - The job that was carried out in that process was purely for research and developmental works and it was not manufacture of testing equipments - Addl. Collector confirmed the demand of duty amounting to Rs. 4,92,566.28/- and imposed a penalty of Rs. 50,000/- and held that in view of the documentary evidence and the balance sheet it had been proved beyond doubt that they had manufactured plant and machinery/testing equipments worth Rs. 31.26 lacs - Collector and Tribunal dismissed appeal filed against said order - Hence, the present appeal - Whether testing equipments are excisable goods and Addl. Collector rightly confirmed the demand of duty amounting to Rs. 4,92,566.28/- and imposed a penalty of Rs. 50,000/-? - Held, yes - The demand for payment of central excise duty in the present case appears to have been made on the basis of statement made by the appellant in his balance sheet to the effect that there is an addition to plant and machinery including testing equipments worth Rs. 31.26 lacs which have been made in the company by capitalisation of the expenditure on (i) raw material, (ii) stores and spares and (iii) salary/wages and other benefits - From the aforesaid own admission of the appellant and from the facts brought out from the records it is clearly proved and established that the appellant had manufactured machines in the nature of testing equipments worth Rs. 31.26 lacs to test the final products manufactured by them - Moreover, even if such equipments were used for captive consumption and within the factory premises, considering the fact that they are saleable and marketable, duty was payable on the said goods - Hence, and Addl. Collector rightly confirmed the demand of duty amounting to Rs. 4,92,566.28/- and imposed a penalty of Rs. 50,000/- on the assessee - Appeal dismissed.
