Kesri Commissariat & Others v Ministry Of Food And Civil Supplies, Govt. Of Maharashtra, Mumbai & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

03 April 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 109; (2012) 5 SCC 187; AIR 2012 SC 1271; 2012 (3) Bom.C.R. 19; JT 2012 (4) SC 1; 2012 (5) MahLJ 212; 2012(4) SCALE 124; [2012] 3 S.C.R. 1010

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Rent Control - Maharashtra Rent Control Act, 1999 - Bombay Public Trust Act, 1950 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 80 - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, ss. 15, 4(1) - Eviction - Recovery of Possession - Protected Tenant - Sustainability - Plaintiff-Trust owner of suit building let out premises to defendant No. 1 and defendant No. 1 without knowledge of plaintiff inducted defendant No. 2 as sub-tenant - Plaintiff filed suit for recovery of possession and mesne profit - Plaintiff claimed that defendants were not protected u/s. 391)(b) of 1999 Act and were liable for eviction - Defendant No. 1 contended that suit was misconceived and not maintainable as proper remedy on part of plaintiffs was to take recourse to s. 16 of 1999 Act - Defendant No. 2 contended that, no notice u/s 80 of CPC was issued and that they were protected tenant under 1999 Act and relief of eviction was untenable - Trial Court decreed suit against defendant No. 1 and dismissed suit against defendant No. 2 - Plaintiff appealed against decree and judgment - Appellate Court, however, decreed suit against both defendants - Defendant No. 2 preferred writ petition before HC - HC unsettled decrees of Court below holding that defendant No. 2 who was inducted by tenant before 01-02-1973 became tenant within meaning of s. 5(11) of 1947 Act, as such he became tenant and enjoyed protection of 1999 Act- Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in unsettling decree passed by Courts below - Held, it was clear that defendant No. 1 was not protected under 1999 Act - Once it was held that defendant No. 1, original tenant, was not protected, question would be whether subtenant could be protected under Act, it had been clearly laid down that s. 4(1) of 1947 Act applied to premises and not to parties or their relationship - S. 3 used term 'premises', commenced with non-obstante clause that Act did not apply to any premises belonging to Govt. or a local authority - S. 3(1)(b) made it clear that Act did not apply to any bank, public sector undertaking or certain other categories of tenants - Insurance Company was covered u/s. 3(1)(b) thus, as a logical corollary, Act did not apply to premises held by Insurance Company who was a tenant - HC had allowed protection to Govt. Department on foundation that it had become a tenant, however, such analysis was fundamentally erroneous - When Act did not cover tenant, namely, Insurance Company as basically exemption applied only to premises and not to any relationship, subtenant who became a deemed tenant could not enjoy a better protection or privilege by ostracizing concept of premises which was spine of provision - Impugned HC order was set aside and order of Appellate Court was restored - Appeal allowed.
