M. L. Sethi v R. P. Kapur and Another

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, V. Ramaswami, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 298; AIR 1967 SC 528; 1967 CRLJ 528; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 520

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - First Information Report - Cognizable - Appellant lodged a First Information Report against respondent and his mother-in-law - The charge was based on the allegation that respondent and his mother-in-law, by conspiring together, cheated appellant and his wife of certain amount by persuading appellant to take a sale-deed of some land which had already been acquired by Govt. - The offence was registered as a cognizable offence and investigation was started - Respondent denied the allegation and added that appellant was fully aware of the land acquisition proceedings and allegations made in the report by appellant were false to his knowledge - Complaint filed by respondent against appellant, as well as the proceedings instituted by the Police on the basis of the report were transferred under the orders of this Court to the Court of the Additional District Magistrate - Case ended in an order of discharge passed by HC, when the charge framed against respondent and his mother-in-law by the trying Magistrate was quashed - Additional District Magistrate dismissed two applications presented by appellant for dismissing a complaint pending before him for commission of offences u/ss. 211, 204, 385 of the IPC - Hence, the present appeal - Whether cognizance of the complaint, u/s. 211, IPC filed by respondent was rightly taken by the Courts - Held, there was no doubt that in the complaint before the Magistrate a charge u/s.211, against appellant was included - Person accused of a serious crime should not be given the advantage of putting his complainant in jeopardy by instituting a case against him for the offence u/s. 211, IPC - Therefore, cognizance of the offence u/s. 211, IPC had been rightly taken and the trial for that offence was not vitiated - Appeal dismissed.
