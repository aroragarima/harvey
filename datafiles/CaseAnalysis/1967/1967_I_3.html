I.N.Saksena v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

30 January 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 134; AIR 1967 SC 1264; 1967 (15) FLR 56; 1967 (2) LLJ 427; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 496; 1967 SLR 204

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 309 and 311(2) - Madhya Pradesh (Age of Compulsory Retirement) Rules, 1965, rr. 6 and 8 - Madhya Pradesh Judicial Service (Classification, recruitment, Condition of Services) Rules (1955), r. 7(2) - Govt. of M.P. issued a memorandum for compulsory retirement of Govt. employees - Validity of memoranum and rules framed thereafter - (A) Whether removal from Govt. services amounts to removal u/art. 311 of Constitution? - Held, where an order requiring a Govt. servant to retire compulsorily contains express words from which a stigma can be inferred, that order may amount to removal within meaning of u/art. 311 of Constitution - (B) Whether memorandum itself amounts to a rule under art.309 of Constitution? - It is said in memorandum itself that necessary amendments to State Civil Service Regulations will be issued in due course - Further held, memorandum is merely an executive direction and not a rule u/art. 309 of Constitution - Appeal allowed.
