Raj Kumar Dixit v Vijay Kumar Gauri Shanker

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 355; (2015) 9 SCC 345; 2015 (146) FLR 158; 2015(6) SCALE 265; 2016(6) SCALE 265; 2015 (4) SLR 660

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Retrenchment - Alleged Illegal - Determination - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 6N - appellant who had fallen sick approached respondent-firm for his outstanding salary, however, respondent-firm terminated him from his services - Further, appellant-workman respondent-establishment refused reinstatement of service which action amounts to retrenchment without following due procedure laid u/s. 6N of Act - Labour Court directed reinstatement of appellant-workman in his post along with 50% back wages, however, HC modified award by granting compensation of Rs. 2 lakhs to be paid to appellant-workman in place reinstatement - Whether order of HC modifying award of lower court is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, various records such as court orders or report given at police station clearly show that appellant-workman worked in capacity of Munim/Clerk/Manager in establishment of respondent firm. Even power of Attorney executed by respondent firm clearly states that appellant workman was authorised to carry out whatever action necessary in connection with release of tankers of respondent firm either from police custody or court. Hence, appellant has worked in capacity of not only mechanic in establishment of respondent firm but also as accounts clerk. Further, witnesses on behalf of respondent firm had further deposed that appellant-workman used to carry out repair work of tankers of respondent-establishment on regular basis and said work was done by appellant-workman only. Hence, HC has gravely erred in quashing award of reinstatement of appellant-workman with 50% back wages in establishment of respondent firm by awarding compensation of Rs.2 Lakhs in lieu of same which modification of award of lower Court is not only erroneous but also suffers from error in law. Further, SC held that HC has exceeded in its jurisdiction in setting aside award passed by lower Court as HC has not noticed fact that appropriate Govt. has referred dispute to lower Court for its adjudication on points of dispute referred to it. Further, since, there was non-compliance of mandatory requirements as provided under provisions of Act by respondent-firm at time of passing order of termination against appellant workman, hence, same has been held to be bad in law. HC has without assigning any cogent and valid reason which is not only erroneous in law but suffers from error in law as well, as same is contrary to catena of decisions of SC has modified award passed by lower court. Order passed by HC is set aside and order of lower court is restored. SC directed respondent firm to pay full back wages to workman from date of passing of award by lower Court till date of his reinstatement in service. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Retrenchment of employee from service without following due procedure of law is illegal and not maintainable.
