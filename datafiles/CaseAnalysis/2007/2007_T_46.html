Bank of India and Another v Tarun Kr. Biswan and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 July 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 781; (2007) 7 SCC 114; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 600; AIR 2007 SC 2669; 2007(6) ALL MR 418; 2007 (3) CalLT(SC) 79; 2007 (3) CLR 230; 2007 (3) ESC 491; JT 2007 (9) SC 543; 2007 (3) LLJ 359; 2007 (4) LLN 154; 2007(9) SCALE 443; 2007 (7) SCJ 6; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 625; 2007 (5) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Surplus Staff

Summary: Service - Absorption/regularization of Budlee Sepoys - Writ petitions by respondents praying for issuance of writ in the nature of mandamus directing the Bank to regularize service of the respondents as subordinate staff in the Bank - Held, onus is on the employee to establish that he had worked for more than 240 days - High Court is not right in holding without factual aspect having been established by the respondents that each of them had worked for more than 240 days - Effect of decision not to fill up the vacancy was also not considered - Appeals allowed.
