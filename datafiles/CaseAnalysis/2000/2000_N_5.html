Shashi Gaur v N. C. T. of Delhi and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 July 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3012; (2001) 10 SCC 445; 2000 (4) AWC 3240; 2000 (3) CLR 585; 2000 (87) FLR 561; JT 2000 (10) SC 481; 2000 (5) SLR 248

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Education - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Service - Termination - Delhi School Education Act, 1973, ss. 8(3) and 11 - Whether a teacher of Pvt. school whose services stood terminated not as measure of penalty but on account of the fact that he allegedly did not have the requisite qualification, could move the Delhi School Tribunal against the order of termination, constituted u/s. 11 of the Act, 1973 or not? - Held, Act provided for tribunal to confer remedy to teachers taken out of service by management of Pvt. institutions and u/s. 8(3) of Act, appeal is provided against an order not only of dismissal, removal of reduction in rank but also against termination - Appellant already approached tribunal - Appeal disposed of.
