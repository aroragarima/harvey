Collector Of Aurangabad & Anr. v Central Bank Of India & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 225; AIR 1967 SC 1831; 1968 (70) BomLR 146; 1968 MahLJ 239; 1968 MPLJ 149; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 855; 1968 (21) STC 10

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Sales Tax

Keywords: Security Bond, Hyderabad Land Revenue Act, 1317

Summary: Constitution - Land & Property - Indirect tax - Sales Tax - Hyderabad Land Revenue Act, 1317, ss.16, 104, 116, 117, 119 and 144 - Hyderabad General Sales Tax Act, s.13 - (A) Whether order of Collector distraining amount due to Govt. from second respondent on account of arrears of sales-tax is legally valid? - Held, procedure followed by Collector is justified u/s. 119 and there is nothing in section prohibiting Collector from making an order of distraint over movable property in custody and possession of a Court - (B) Whether arrears of sales-tax due to Govt. has priority over dues of respondent? - Further held, priority u/s. 104 applies only in respect of land revenue and not for other taxes wherein, only procedure for recovery u/s. 16 applies and not substantive law of priority u/s. 104 - (C) Whether appellants can claim priority to payment of sales-tax according to Common Law doctrine of 'Priority of Crown debts'? - Further held, since there is no proof that doctrine was given judicial recognition in territory of Hyderabad State prior to its incorporation into Indian Republic, English Common Law doctrine of priority of Crown debts can't be applied - Appeal dismissed.
