Sharma Transports and others v State of Maharashtra and another

Supreme Court of India

02 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 567; (2011) 8 SCC 647; 2011 (3) ACC 706; 2011 (88) ALR 478; 2011 (5) AWC 5123; 2011 (5) Bom.C.R. 5; 2011 (7) MLJ(SC) 805; 2012 (165) PLR 469; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 308; [2011] 9 S.C.R. 699

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Luggage, Tourist Vehicles, Compounding Fee

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 2, 2(13), 86, 88, 110, 200 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19(1) (g) - Central Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989, rr. 93, 128(9), 128(a) - Tourist vehicles - Luggage restrictions - Challenged - Appellants /transporters operate tourist busses between States of Karnataka and Maharashtra - Respondents by circular dt. 15-12-1995 issued instructions to all subordinate authorities not to permit luggage on roof of tourist vehicles as not permitted under law - Acting on this instruction, checking authorities started imposing and collecting fines from transporters carrying goods on roof of tourist vehicles - Aggrieved, appellants filed writ petition in HC - DB dismissed writ petition holding that by virtue of r. 128 (9) of the 1989 Rules luggage of passengers could be stored only in rear and side compartments of vehicle and not on roof of vehicle - Hence, instant appeal - Whether act of appellants carrying luggage on roof of tourist vehicles was permitted under law - Held, vehicles registered as tourist vehicles and endorsement recorded on Registration Certificate that tourist vehicles should comply with all requirements of r. 128 of the 1989 Rules - R.128(9) of the 1989 Rules provided that luggage holds should be provided at rear or at sides or both of tourist vehicle - When rules specifically made provision with regard to luggage holds luggage should be placed in specified area and not elsewhere - Since rule was clear and unambiguous no other construction need be resorted -Chapter V of 1988 Act relates to control of transport vehicles - S.84 deals with general conditions attached to all permits and deemed to be incorporated in every permit - S.200 of the 1988 Act conferred State Govt. could by notification in official gazette specify various compounding fees for breach of permit conditions - RR. 93 (3) and (3A) of the 1989 Rules would not apply to tourist vehicles as these rules specifically referred to vehicles that were carrying heavy loads and not carrying tourists - Restrictions imposed by rule were reasonable restrictions taken keeping in view safety of passengers in tourist vehicle - Rule could not be said either arbitrary or unreasonable or violative of art. 19(1)(g) of Constitution - Appeals dismissed.
