Â Narasimhachar M v State of Mysore

Supreme Court of India

12 October 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 52; AIR 1960 SC 247; 1960 (1) LLJ 798; 1960 SCJ 516; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 981

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution - Revised Pension Rules - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 216, 226, 294 to 297 and 302 - (A) Whether order directing appellant to retire on completion of fifty-five years of age is tenable? - Held, yes - It was open to Govt. to retire a Govt. servant if person was not efficient to be kept in further service - (B) Whether, reduction in pension of appellant was beyond reasonable grounds? - Further held, Govt. has right to recover from pension and compassionate allowances of a Govt. servant on account of losses caused due to negligence - If service of employee has not been satisfactory authority should make reduction in amount as it thinks proper -Reduction in pension is a matter of discretion with Govt. - Appeal dismissed.
