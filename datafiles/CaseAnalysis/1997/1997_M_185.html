Visakhapatnam Port Trust and Another v Messrs Ram Bahadur Thakur Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

10 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 959; (1997) 4 SCC 582; AIR 1997 SC 1057; 1997 (2) CLT 13; JT 1997 (2) SC 599; 1997(2) SCALE 111; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 1080; 1997 (5) Supreme 459

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Water

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Railway Act, 1890 - Major Port Trusts Act, 1963, ss. 52, 53 and 54 - Constitution of India, 1950 - (A) Whether, the impugned circulars and the impugned resolution of the board amount to remission of the then existing rates of handling charges for manganese ore covered by s. 53 of the Act of whether these rates require prior sanction of the Central Govt. u/s. 52 of the Act before they could become effective? - Held, the effect of the new scheme of handling charges introduced by the board by the impugned resolution cannot be said to be amounting to a mere remission from the erstwhile earlier existing scales of handling charges - High Court was justified in taking the view that the impugned Resolution seeking to bring into effect new rates of handling charges in the light of entirely new system of services then offered by the Board required prior sanction of the Central Govt. u/s. 52 of the Act and could not be treated to be representing a scheme of remission as envisaged by s. 53 of the Act - (B) Whether, the impugned rates of handling charges were unreasonable, excessive and based on no proper quid pro quo between the services rendered by the Board and the charges levied by the Board for such services? - Further held, a perusal of ss. 52 and 54 of the Act leaves no room for doubt that under the scheme of the Act itself the Central Govt. is the ultimate authority for deciding about the propriety and justness of the scales of rates of services to be rendered to the shippers by the Board of the concerned Ports governed by the Act - In the light of this statutory scheme, therefore, the reasonableness of the settled scales of charges for handling goods as tried to be recovered by the Board under the Act could be validly made the subject-matter of scrutiny of the Central Govt. by aggrieved parties by invoking the Central Govt's powers u/s. 54 of the Act - Appeals dismissed.
