Mangat Ram and Another v Sardar Meharban Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28269; (1987) 4 SCC 319; AIR 1987 SC 1656; JT 1987 (2) SC 571; 1988 (1) RCR(Rent) 585; 1987 (3) RentLR 250; 1987(1) SCALE 964; 1987 (1) UJ 694

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Land & Property

Summary: Rent Control - Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, s. 20(2) -- Evidence Act, 1872, s.116 - Tenancy - Whether the appellants were liable to be evicted from the demised premises u/s. 20(2) of the 1972 Act and also liable for arrears of rent - In absence of non fulfillment of condition precedent - Conditions that tenant was in arrears of rent for not less than four months and that he had failed to pay the sum to the landlord within one month from the date of service upon him of the notice of demand - No notice of demand in terms of s.20(2)(a) of the Act - Held on facts that none of the conditions was satisfied - Further held that s.116, Evidence Act was not attracted - Appeal allowed.
