Northern Railway Administration, Ministry of Railway, New Delhi v Patel Engineering Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

18 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 11(6) - Appointment of arbitrator - Scope of s. 11(b) - SC in Ace Pipeline Contracts (P) Ltd vs. Bharat Petroleum Corpn. Ltd, 2007 INDLAW SC 292 and in Union of India vs. Bharat Battery Mgf. Co. (P) Ltd. 2007 INDLAW SC 807 gave two different views - Hence, present appeal - Held, bare reading of the scheme of s. 11 shows that the emphasis is on the terms of the agreement being adhered to and/or given effect as closely as possible - Court must first ensure that the remedies provided for are exhausted - It is not mandatory to appoint the named arbitrator or arbitrators - Due regard is to be given to the qualifications required for arbitrator by the agreement and other considerations - HC did not set aside the said requirement, hence appointment set aside - Matter remanded back for fresh appointments - Appeal disposed of.
