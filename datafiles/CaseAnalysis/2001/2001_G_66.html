State of Himachal Pradesh v Gian Chand

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, A.S. Anand, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20676; (2001) 6 SCC 71; (2001) SCC (Cr) 980; AIR 2001 SC 2075; AIR 2001 SCW 1903; 2001 (2) CCR 174; 2002 (1) CRJ 792B; 2001 CRLJ 2548; 2001 (2) JCC 92; JT 2001 (5) SC 169; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 666; 2001(3) SCALE 565; 2001 (4) SCJ 512; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 247; 2001 (3) SLT 740; 2001 (3) Supreme 588; 2001 (2) UC 41B

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 84 and 376 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1963, ss. 313 and 329 - Accused convicted u/s. 376, IPC, 1860 for having committed forcible sexual inter - course with prosecutrix, a girl of age of 5 years and 6 months - On appeal, High Court set aside conviction and directed accused to be released - Appeal - (A) Delay in filing FIR - Held, delay in filing rape cases cannot be regarded as ritualistic formula for doubting prosecution case and discarding same solely on ground of delay - Further held, prosecution fails to satisfactorily explain delay and there is a possibility of embellishment in prosecution version on account of such delay, delay would be fatal to prosecution - If delay is explained to satisfaction of court, delay cannot by itself be a ground for disbelieving and discarding entire prosecution case - Delay in making FIR has been satisfactorily explained and, therefore, does not cause any dent in prosecution case - Relied on State of Punjab v Gurmit Singh 3841996 Indlaw SC 1087 - (B) Whether HC was right in rejecting witness due to tender age? - Further held, HC has failed to see is that these girls were of tender age and could hardly be expected to describe act of forcible sexual intercourse committed by accused - Accused guilty of an offence punishable u/s. 376, IPC, 1860 along with sentence passed is restored - Appeal allowed.
