Indian Aluminium Cables Limited v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

27 May 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, R.S. Pathak, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 215; (1985) 3 SCC 284; (1985) SCC (Tax) 383; AIR 1985 SC 1201; 1985 ECR 1465; 1985 (21) E.L.T. 3; 1985(1) SCALE 976; 1987 (64) STC 180; [1985] Supp1 S.C.R. 731; 1985 TAXLR 2551; 1985 UJ 1070

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Revision Application, Rod

Summary: Excise - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 3, sch. I, item 27(a)(ii) - Excise Duty - Whether 'Properzi Rods' manufactured and cleared by the appellant fall within entry No. 27(a)(ii) of the First sch. to 1944 Act and, if so, under which category of the articles mentioned therein - Held, they are wire rods and thus would fall under item 27(a)(ii); process of manufacture of a product and the use to which it is put, cannot necessarily be determinative of the classification of that product under a fiscal schedule like the Central Excise Tariff - (B) Interpretation of statutes - Meaning of words ' Tariff Schedule' - To be construed in the sense in which they are understood in the trade, by the dealer and the consumer - (C) Practice and Procedure - Acceptability of affidavits of persons dealing in wire rods - Statements cannot be accepted at their face value as their affidavits do not show any familiarity with dealings in Properzi Rods and the knowledge claimed by them does not stem from their personal experience but is in the nature of hearsay - Appeal dismissed.
