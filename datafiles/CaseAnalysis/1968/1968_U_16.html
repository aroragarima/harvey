Â Udai Ram Sharma and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 February 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat, J.M. Shelat, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 234; AIR 1968 SC 1138; 1969 (1) MLJ 51; 1969 (1) MLJ(SC) 51; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 41

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Land Acquisition (Amendment and validation) Act, 1967, Land Acquisition (Madras Amendment) Act, 1961, Agra Tenancy Act, 1926, Hindu Marriages (Validation of Proceedings) Act, 1960

Summary: Land & Property - Agra Tenancy Act, 1926 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Government Of India Act, 1935 - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 - Hindu Marriages Act, 1960 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Land Acquisition Act, 1967 - Notification - Acquisition - Compensation - Appreciation - Respondent/State issued notification u/s. 4(1) of 1967 Act that acquisition of land for construction of steel plant needed for public purpose -Petition filed against validity of notification u/s. 6 was accepted by HC - Appeal filed by State. was dismissed - Hence instant petitions - Whether notification issued u/s.4(1) of 1967 Act was justified - Held, that Act not provide for average price - Act saved Govt. to issue fresh s.4 notification and to pay compensation calculated on market value and deprived expropriated owner benefit of appreciated value - Object of s. 4 of 1967 Act was to save State. from compensate for appreciation - Under device of validating all that was done under exhausted s. 4 notification and in reality fixing anterior date - On basis of values frozen by notifications u/s.4, acquisitions had lost their efficacy and required test of land to be notified afresh and to pay compensation on date notifications and s.4 was invalid - Petitions disposed of.
