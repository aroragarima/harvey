Thakur Mohd. Ismail v Thakur Sabir Ali

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 537; AIR 1962 SC 1722; [1963] 1 S.C.R. 20

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Rule Against Perpetuity, Mussalman Wakf Validating Act, 1913

Summary: Family & Personal - Oudh Estates Act, 1869, ss. 11, 12 and 18 - Mussalman Wakf Validating Act, 1913 - Creation of wakfalal-aulad - Whether wakf deed was valid as property was subject to special provisions of Act and therefore said deed was not valid, particularly in view of provisions contained in ss. 11 and 12 of Oudh Estates Act, No. 1 of 1869? - S. 12 provides rule against perpetuity - S. 13 deals with procedure relating to transfers by gifts and provides that transfer by gift will be made by an instrument signed by donor and attested by two or more witnesses not less than three months before his death and presented for registration within one month from date of its execution and registered - S. 18, deals with gifts for religious and charitable uses - Wakf in present case, though in theory it vests property in God Almighty, is not for charitable or religious purposes - Wakf Validating Act of 1913 makes no differences to this position - That Act specifically provides by s. 3 that a Muslim can lawfully create wakf-aulad but does not mean that purpose of such a wakf is a religious or charitable purpose - This is made clear by proviso to s. 3, which provides that ultimate benefit in such a case must be for a religious or charitable purpose - Proviso would have been unnecessary if purpose of a wakf-aulad was recognised as religious or charitable by this law - Same will follow from provision in s. 4 - In such case s. 12 must invalidate this wakf - (B) Whether wakf violates s. 12 of Act? - Misconception that usufruct vests in unborn descendants arises from refusing to recognize that in Muslim law a wakf-aulad is wholly a gift to charity and everything vest in God and nothing in objects of charity - It seems to this Court that if present wakf is a valid vesting of property transferred is not postponed at all therefore s. 12 is not violated by a transfer by way of wakf - Appeal dismissed.
