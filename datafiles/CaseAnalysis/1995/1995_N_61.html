M/S. Complete Insulations (P) Ltd. v New India Assurance Company Ltd

Supreme Court of India

21 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, S.C. Agrawal, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 2146; (1996) 1 SCC 221; 1996 ACJ 65; AIR 1996 SC 586; 1995 (4) CCC 167; [1997] 88 Comp Cas 366; 1996 (4) CTJ 383; 1995(6) SCALE 629; 1996 (2) SCJ 144; [1995] Supp5 S.C.R. 500

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Civil Procedure; Corporate; Indirect Tax

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss.157 and 147 - Scope - Liability of insurance company - Transfer of vehicle - No agreement between insurer and transferee, the former undertaking to cover the risk or damage to vehicle - No policy regarding this transferred to transferee by insurer - Held, of s.157 confined only to third party risks - Transferee cannot be said to be third party qua the vehicle - Insurer not liable to make good the damage to the vehicle.
