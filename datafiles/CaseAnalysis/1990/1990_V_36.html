Vinod Krishna Kaul v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 September 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

N.M. Kasliwal, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 613; (1991) Supp1 SCC 19; (1991) SCC (L&S) 720; AIR 1990 SC 2167; 1991 (16) ATC 551; JT 1991 (5) SC 21; 1990 LabIC 1863; 1991 (1) LLN 7; 1990(2) SCALE 535; 1991 (2) SLJ 60; 1991 (1) SLR 766; 1990 (2) UJ 637; 1991 (1) UPLBEC 99

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14 and 16 - Joint Secretary in Central Govt. - Ex cadre Post - Appointment - Discrimination - Alleged by I P S officer - Appointment of officers juniors to him - He was not available at relevant time - One appointee belonging to Indian Postal service, no seniority qua - Another belonging to Indian Police Service - Constitutionality - Held, With respect to him, seniority as per position in parent cadre irrespective of worked as Joint Secretary - No violation of art. 14 and 16 - Appeal dismissed.
