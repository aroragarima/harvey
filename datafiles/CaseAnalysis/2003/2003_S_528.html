Ajay Mitra v State of M.P. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, S. Rajendra Babu, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 60; (2003) 3 SCC 11; (2003) SCC (Cr) 703; AIR 2003 SC 1069; 2003 (1) CCR 306; 2003 (1) CGLJ 351; 2003 (2) Crimes 196; 2003 (4) CRJ 384; 2003 CRLJ 1249; 2003 (2) JhrCR(SC) 42; JT 2003 (1) SC 418; 2003 (3) MPLJ 49; 2003 (1) RAJ 226; 2003 (1) RCR(Criminal) 674; 2003(1) SCALE 487; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 622; 2003 (1) SLT 672; 2003 (2) Supreme 602; 2003 (1) UC 652

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Criminal; Trade

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, ss.155(2), 156(1), 156(3), 173(2), 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, 120B, 34, 415, 420, 511 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - Trade Mark - Bottling agreements entered between Cadbury Schweppes Beverages India Pvt Ltd and Sanjiva Bottling Company for a period of five years - Non-renewal of agreements at end of initial term - Held, either party to agreement had a right to terminate agreement expiring at end of initial term by giving not less than 12 calendar months notice in writing - Cheating - Further held, a guilty intention is an essential ingredient of offence of cheating In other words 'mens rea' on part of accused must be established before he can be convicted of an offence of cheating - Since, appellants were not in picture at all at time when complainant alleges to have spent money in improving bottling plant, neither any guilty intention can be attributed to them nor there can possibly be any intention on their part to deceive complainant - Where complaint or FIR does not disclose commission of a cognizable offence, whether same can be quashed at initial stage? - Further held, First Information Report, which does not allege or disclose that essential requirements of penal provision are prima facie satisfied, cannot from foundation constitute starting point of a lawful investigation - Therefore, complaint filed by respondent and FIRs registered in pursuance thereof are liable to be quashed - Appeals allowed.
