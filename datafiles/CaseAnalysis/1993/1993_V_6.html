Commissioner of Income Tax v V. Venkatachalam

Supreme Court of India

13 April 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1075; (1993) Supp3 SCC 413; AIR 1994 SC 1267; 1993 (113) CTR 55; [1993] 201 ITR 737; JT 1993 (Supp) SC 563; [1993] 70 TAXMAN 231

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 14, 45 to 48, 80-T and 256(1) - Finance Act, 1987 - Whether assessee was entitled, for assessment year 1973 -74, relief u/s. 80-T on an amount calculated with reference to gross capital gains of Rs. 1,02,740? - Language of s. 80-T is plain and unambiguous; deductions provided for by said provision had to be made from out of capital gains - Held, in present case, capital gain was Rs. 1,02,740, it is on said sum that deductions provided for by s. 80-T had to be applied; in such a case, no question can arise of business loss being set off against this capital gain; words 'such income', meant and referred to capital gains and not total income of assessee - Appeal dismissed.
