N. Balaji v Virendra Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

P.P. Naolekar, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 858; (2004) 8 SCC 312; 2005 (3) Bom.C.R. 370; 2004 (114) DLT 304; 2005 (1) JhrCR(SC) 130; JT 2004 (8) SC 449; 2004(8) SCALE 528; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 96; 2005 (1) UPLBEC 268

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Election Dispute

Summary: Civil Procedure - Election - Practice and Procedure - Trusts and Associations - Multi State Cooperative Societies Act, 2002 - Election petition - Central Registrar accepted petition - HC set aside order of registrar on ground that it is barred by limitation - Whether petition is barred by limitation? - Held, petition has to be read in continuation of several representations and objection petitions filed earlier, from time to time and cannot be considered to be a separate and independent petition - Central Registrar shall now proceed with hearing of petition of appellant - Appeal allowed.
