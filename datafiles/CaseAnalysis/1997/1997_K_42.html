S.P. Subramanya Shetty & Ors. v K.S.R.T.C. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1115; (1997) 11 SCC 250; AIR 1997 SC 2076; 1997 (30) ALR 218; 1997 (2) CLT 370; JT 1997 (4) SC 594; 1999 (90) RD 368; 1997(3) SCALE 514; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 370; 1997 (4) Supreme 5

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s.9 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss.4 (1), 48 - Notification - Acquisition - Claim of injunction - Notification u/s.4(1) of Act was issued and petitioners' land was acquired - Petitioners had challenged notification for acquisition - Petitioner made representation to denotify the part of land on ground that he was willing to give 5 out of 11 cents, free of cost - Petitioner filed civil suit for injunction and for restraining others from interfering with his possession - District Court vacated interim injunction granted by Trial Court - HC dismissed writ petition - Special leave petition filed by petitioners was dismissed by Court - Hence instant petition.

Held, settled legal position that if notification had become final and proceedings had attained finality, then civil suit was not maintainable. Civil suit relating to acquisition proceedings is not maintainable and by implication, cognizance u/s.9 of CPC, is barred. Court cannot issue mandatory injunction against the state to denotify the acquisition u/s.48 of Act. Therefore, question of granting injunction against the authority, from proceedings in accordance with the law does not arise. HC was right in refusing to grant injunction. Court cannot compel Govt. to withdraw notification u/s.4 (1) of Act. It is for Govt. to consider same on merits and public interest. Notification was upheld by Court and has become final Govt. cannot retract from steps taken. Special leave petition is accordingly dismissed. Petition dismissed.
