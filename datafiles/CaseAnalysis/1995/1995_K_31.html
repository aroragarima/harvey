Kameshwar Prasad v State Of Bihar And Ors

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1363; (1995) 6 SCC 44; (1995) SCC (L&S) 1350; 1995 (31) ATC 468; JT 1995 (9) SC 612; 1995(5) SCALE 466; 1995 (6) SLR 5B; [1995] Supp3 S.C.R. 521; 1996 (1) UJ 28

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Service - Petitioner appointed as vaccinator in Smallpox Eradication Programme - Promoted as clerk - Letter of appointment indicated that promotion would be subject to confirmation by Director-in-Chief(DIC) - DIC cancelled appointment holding that any promotion made would be subject to policy of confirmation according to rules on basis of seniority-cum-fitness and also observing rule of reservation - Held, DIC is required to follow principles laid down in Resolution No. 2215 D/-11-2-1985, even if he be appointing authority - Appellant was really required to be absorbed in an equivalent post because he was found to be a surplus vaccinator - Since appellant has already worked from 08-05-1991 till 10-12-1992 as a clerk, he is entitled to salary attached to post of clerk for said period - Appeal allowed.
