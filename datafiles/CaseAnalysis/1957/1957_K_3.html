Pirgonda Hongonda Patil v Kalgonda Shidgonda Patil and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 February 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 98; AIR 1957 SC 363; 1957 (59) BomLR 401; 1957 SCJ 371; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 595

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6, r. 17 - Limitation Act, 1908, s. 3 - (A) Whether amendment to pleading ought to be allowed or not ?- Held, all amendments ought to be allowed - Which satisfy two conditions, (a) not working injustice to other side, and (b) of being necessary for purpose of determining real questions in controversy between parties - (B) Whether permission should be given to plaintiff to make an amendment after period of limitation for suit expired or not? - Held, no permission should be given to plaintiff to make an amendment thereafter - This was not a circumstance for not exercising power of amendment by High Court- (C)Whether power of amendment exercised by HC was undoubtedly one within discretion of HC or not? - Held, power exercised was undoubtedly within discretion of HC - Discretion was not exercised on a wrong principle - Amendment did not really introduce a new case - Appeal dismissed.
