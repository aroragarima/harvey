State Of Maharashtra And Another v Gorakhnath Sitaram Kamble And Others

Supreme Court of India

16 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1075; 2011 (5) AWC 4805; 2011 (128) FLR 370; JT 2010 (13) SC 723; 2011 (1) MLJ(SC) 1340; 2010(13) SCALE 355; 2011 (7) SLR 510

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Births, Deaths and Marriages Registration Act, 1886, Maharashtra Civil Services (General Conditions) Rules, 1981

Summary: Service - Maharashtra Civil Services (General Conditions) Rules, 1981, r. 38(2)(f) - Service Record - Alteration of date of birth - Admissibility - 1st respondent/Assistant Teacher sought to alter his date of birth in service record on the ground that the date of birth mentioned in the School Certificate, which was filed at the time of appointment as proof of date of birth, was not correct - 1st respondent's application was rejected by respondent/Authorities - 1st respondent filed writ petition - 1st respondent contended that he was entitled to change the date of birth in service records by virtue of r. 38(2)(f) of the Rules - HC allowed 1st respondent's writ petition - Hence the present appeal by State authorities - Whether the 1st respondent is entitled to alter the date of birth in the service records - Held, HC virtually re-wrote the Rules while interpreting r. 38(2)(f) of the Rules - Expression 'normally' was not used in r. 38(2)(f) of the Rules and interpretation of that expression had led to an erroneous finding in the impugned judgment - HC failed to give any sustainable or acceptable reasons in the impugned judgments as to why the date of birth was permitted to be changed at the end of the career of 1st respondent - No alteration of the entry should be allowed after five years of joining of the service as per Notification dated 24-12- 2008 - 1st respondent filed application of alternation at the end of his service career after a lapse of twenty eight years - Correction at the end would be at the cost of large number of employees, therefore, any correction at that stage must be discouraged by the Court - Approach of the HC in re-writing the rules cannot be approved or sustained and hence set aside - Appeal allowed.
