Dr. Janet Jeyapaul v SRM University and others

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 888; AIR 2016 SC 73; 2016(1) ALL MR 971; 2016 (2) AWC 1179; 2016 (1) CTC 240; JT 2015 (12) SC 259; 2016 (1) MLJ 231; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 1049; 2016 (1) SLR 497; 2015 (4) UPLBEC 3176

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: University Grants Commission Act, 1956, Bombay Public Trusts Act, 1950

Summary: Service - University Grants Commission Act, 1956, s. 3 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 12, 22 - Reinstatement in Service - Non-amenable Challenged - Appellant rendered service as Lecturer with respondent no.1/university, which was declared as deemed university by University Grants Commission - However, on basis of complaints from students, termination notice was served to appellant - Single Judge in writ petition directed university to reinstate appellant into service - DB of HC allowed writ appeal by university on ground that university was neither state nor authority within meaning of art. 12 of Constitution and as consequence writ was not maintainable - Further, granted liberty to appellant to approach Tribunal for ventilating her grievances - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, while deciding the question as to whether the writ lies u/art. 226 of Constitution, against any person, juristic body, organization, authority etc., the test is to examine in the first instance the object and purpose for which such body/authority/organization is formed so also the activity which it undertakes to fulfill the said object/purpose. Further, when a private body exercises its public functions even if it is not a State, the aggrieved person has a remedy not only under the ordinary law but also under the Constitution by way of petition u/art. 226 of Constitution. In instant case, respondent no.1, deemed university, discharging public function by way of imparting higher education to students at large and for the benefit of public. Thus, it is a authority as provided in art.12 of Constitution, then necessary consequences becomes amenable to writ jurisdiction of HC u/art. 226 of Constitution. Hence, impugned order passed by DB of HC is set aside and matter remanded back to DB to decide the respondent's appeal on merits on the question as to whether the Single Judge was justified in allowing the writ petition on merits. Appeal allowed.
