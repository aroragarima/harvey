Esthuri Aswathiah v Income Tax Officer, Mysore State

Supreme Court of India

05 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 189; AIR 1961 SC 1149; [1961] 41 ITR 539; 1961 (2) SCJ 208; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 911

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 22(2) - Notice - Return - A/y sessment year - A Hindu Undivided Family carrying on business in groundnuts and other commodities territory which formed part of the former State of Mysore - Mysore Income Tax Act was repealed and the Indian' Income Tax Act was brought into force in the Part-B State of Mysore - After the Indian Income Tax Act was applied to the State of Mysore notice u/s. . 22(2) of Act was served upon the appellants requiring them to submit their return of income for the a/y 1950-51 - Appellants submitted their return stating that for the year ending 30-6-1949 , corresponding to the a/y 1949-50, they were assessed under the Mysore Income Tax Act, that their income for the year ending 30-6-1950, was assessable under the Act in the a/y 1951-52 and that they had no assessable income for the a/y 1950-51- Income Tax Officer held that Rs. 1,37,000 out of the opening balance in the books of account represented income from an undisclosed source- Before the appeal was disposed of by the Appellate Assistant Commissioner appellants had submitted a fresh return for the a/y 1950-51 purporting to do so under s. 22(3) of the Act -Whether notice u/s. 34 of Actwas properly issued - Held, notice u/s. 34 of Act was also not issued after the expiry of the period prescribed in that behalf - The notice was issued by the Income Tax Officer because he had reason to believe that by reason of failure on the part of the appellants to disclose fully and truly all material facts necessary for the a/y 1950-51, income had escaped assessment - Such a notice fell manifestly within s. 34(1)(a) of Act and could be issued within 8 years, from the end of the year of assessment - The impugned notice u/34 of Act for reassessment of the income of the appellants for the a/y 1950-51 was properly issued - Appeal dismissed.
