Â State of Bihar v Messrs Karam Chand Thapar and Brothers Limited

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 262; AIR 1962 SC 110; 1962 (2) SCJ 17; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 827

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Arbitration Act, Stamp Act, 1899, Government of India Act 1935

Summary: Constitution - Civil Procedure - Contract & Commercial - Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1953 art. 299(1) - Government of India Act, 1935, ss. 175(3)and 175(8) - Stamp Act, 1899, s. 35 - (A) What are conditions to be satisfied when Govt. of a province enters into a contract? - Held, u/s. 175(3) of Govt. of India Act, 1935 - Contract expressed to be made by Governor - Executed by Governor - Execution by person as Governor might authorize - (B) What is mode in which authority may be conferred on a person to execute a contract? - Further held, u/s. 175(8) of Govt. of India Act, 1935 - Conferment by notification in official gazette - Nothing to preclude authorisation being conferred ad hoc on any person - Executive engineer authorised by Governor acting through his Secy. to execute agreement - (C) Can there be validation of copy of an instrument if it is unstamped? - Further held, u/s. 35 of Stamp Act, 1899 - Validation only of original, when it is unstamped or insufficiently stamped - Copy of an instrument cannot be validated - Appeal dismissed.
