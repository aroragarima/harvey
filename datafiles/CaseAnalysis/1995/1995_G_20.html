State Of Himachal Pradesh And Ors.Etc. v Ganesh Wood Products And Ors.Etc.

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1850; (1995) 6 SCC 363; AIR 1996 SC 149; AIR 1995 SCW 3847; JT 1995 (6) SC 485; 1995(5) SCALE 303; [1995] Supp3 S.C.R. 477

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Keywords: Poverty

Summary: Constitution - Industries (Development And Regulation) Act, 1951, s.11 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Constitution, arts.162 and 226 - PIL for restraining Govt. from granting permission for setting up industry - Adverse effect of proposed factories on environment and ecology - (A) Powers of State Govt. to setting up katha industry - (B) Authority proceeding on assumption that in absence of commitment on part of Government to supply raw material, khair wood to factories every proposal before it can be approved - Analysis of approach - Held, is entitled to lay down policies and empowered to refuse approval - Approach by authority is faulty and myopic and also contrary to public interest involved in preserving forest, health as there is no absolute or unrestricted right to establish forest based industries notwithstanding policy of liberalisation of Government of India - Appeal allowed.
