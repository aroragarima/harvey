Goan Real Estate And Construction Limited And Another v Union Of India Through Secretary, Ministry Of Environment And Others

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 247; (2010) 5 SCC 388; 2010 (4) Bom.C.R. 762; JT 2010 (3) SC 462; 2010(3) SCALE 512

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Environment (Protection) Rules, 1986 - Central Government issued Coastal Regulation Zone Notification declaring the area up to 100 meters from the High Tide Line as 'No Development Zone' - However, Central Government issued another notification amending the earlier notification and relaxing the 'No Development Zone' to 50 meters from 100 meters - Petitioner/landowner who had earlier obtained construction permissions in respect of a project beyond 100 meters, sought permission for construction of 18 blocks between 50 meters and 100 meters - Panchayat and Town and Country Planning Authority accorded permission to petitioner and thereby, petitioner resumed the construction - SC, in the meantime, declared the amendment Notification as illegal in another Public Interest Litigation - Additional Collector issued a stop work order on a complaint by 4th respondent/NGO - Petitioner made a representation to the Central Government to issue necessary clarifications to the authorities - Central Government clarified that any developmental activity which had been initiated, after the amendment Notification and before the said SC order, after obtaining all the requisite clearances from concerned agencies including the Town and Country Planning Authority should be construed as an on-going project and would be exempted - Additional Collector passed an order purporting to vacate the stop work order but, in fact, permitting the construction beyond 100 meters and not 50 meters - Petitioner challenged the Additional Collector order before the HC - HC disposed the petitioner's writ petition off on the basis of the Advocate-General's statement to the effect that the State would withdraw the stop work order - Panchayat renewed its permission for construction in favour of petitioner - An NGO challenged the Panchayat order before HC - HC ordered status quo to the petitioner - Whether the constructions made or on-going pursuant to the plans sanctioned on the basis of amendment Notification would be affected or not - Held, under the main Notification, it was stipulated that all development and activities within Coastal Regulation Zone (CRZ) would be valid and would not violate the provisions of the same Notification till the Management Plans are approved - Thus, the intention of legislature while issuing the main Notification was to protect the past actions/transactions which came into existence before the approval of the Notification - SC had quashed the amendment Notification because the amendment would permit new constructions to take place which was contrary to the provisions of the Environment Act, 1986 and not because of the reason that there was evidence before the Court that constructions had already made or on-going pursuant to the plans sanctioned on the basis of amendment Notification had, in fact, frustrated the object of the Act - SC judgment which had struck down the amendment Notification would operate prospectively and further clarified that any developmental activity which has been initiated after the amendment Notification and before the SC judgment after obtaining all requisite clearances from the concerned agencies including the Town and Country Planning should be construed as on-going projects and are not hit by the SC judgment - Operation of the main Notification or amended Notification were neither stayed by SC nor by the Government, therefore, a citizen was entitled to act as per the amendment Notification - Rights of the parties were crystallized by the amending notification till part of the same was declared to be illegal by SC - Therefore, notwithstanding the fact that part of the amending Notification was declared illegal by SC, all orders passed under the said Notification and actions taken pursuant to the said notification would not be affected in any manner whatsoever - Petition partly allowed.
