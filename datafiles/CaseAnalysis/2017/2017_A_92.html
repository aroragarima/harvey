Anant Singh @ Anant Kumar Singh v State of Bihar and others

Supreme Court of India

12 April 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Rohinton Fali Nariman, Mohan M. Shantanagoudar

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 304; AIR 2017 SC 3250; JT 2017 (8) SC 215; 2017(5) SCALE 460

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Revocation, General Clauses Act, 1897, Jail, Detaining Authority, Preventive Detention, Defence of India Rules, 1962, Preventive Detention Act, 1950, Bihar Control of Crimes Act, 1981, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Bihar Control of Crimes Act, 1981, ss. 12(1), 12(3), 17, 18, 2(d), 21(3), 23(2) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21, 22, 22(5) - Detention order - Challenged - Appellant/accused who was alleged to be involved in grave crimes was in jail since 24-6-2015 - HC passed preventive detention order dt. 21-9-2016 by which appellant was preventively detained under the Act for a period of 1 year - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that grounds on which the 21-9-2016 order passed were all prior to that date there was a direct infraction of s. 23(2) of the Act and would be sufficient to render said detention order illegal.

Held, the second order of detention dt. 21-9-2016 was passed only on grounds which arose prior to the order of revocation dt. 17-9-2016, it would fall foul of s. 23(2) of the Act. SC found that direct judgment which covers instant case was judgment of SC in Hadibandhu Das v. District Magistrate, Cuttack and Another, 1968 Indlaw SC 437, which was repeatedly been followed and was the law declared by SC on that subject. The expression "where fresh facts have arisen" was followed by "the date of revocation or expiry." The Act being a statute which provides for preventive detention, it has to be construed keeping the subject's liberty in mind i.e. it has to be construed keeping arts. 21 and 22 of Constitution in mind. Here no supposed object of the Act can be looked at to defeat the articles of Constitution particularly when literal language of s. 23(2) of the Act leads only to the conclusion that it was the date of revocation order and not the date of original order of detention that was referred to. Impugned judgment of HC was set aside. Appeal allowed.
