State of Haryana and Others v Manoj Kumar

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 190; (2010) 4 SCC 350; AIR 2010 SC 1779; 2010 (79) ALR 726; 2010 (2) AWC 1670; JT 2010 (2) SC 577; 2010 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1380; 2010 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1533; 2010 (3) MLJ(SC) 1412; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 296; 2010 (110) RD 100; 2010(2) SCALE 783; [2010] 3 S.C.R. 175

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Haryana Amendment to Stamp Act

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 227 - Indian Stamp (Haryana Amendment) Act, 1986, s. 47A - Evasion of stamp duty - Agreement to sell a commercial plot executed in favour of respondent - Entire sale consideration was paid and the actual possession was also given to the respondent but the sale deed was not executed - Respondent filed a suit without impleading appellants as parties to the suit for specific performance of agreement to sell executed by appellants - Said suit was decreed in favour of the respondent - Appellant filed suit alleging that in order to evade substantial stamp duty, respondent filed a suit for specific performance of agreement to sell and respondent has paid only Rs. 31,000/- towards stamp duty which was under-valued - District Collector, directed the respondent to make payment of difference of the amount of stamp duty amounting to Rs.4,82,050/- - On appeal, Commissioner confirmed the order of District Collector - HC set aside the concurrent findings of facts of the courts below and held that the authenticity of the decree passed by the court cannot be questioned - Therefore, the genuineness of the sale price has to be presumed - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC's order could be upheld? - Held, no - In order to ensure that there is no evasion of stamp duty, circle rates are fixed from time to time and the notification is issued to that effect - Issuance of said notification has become imperative to arrest the tendency of evading the payment of actual stamp duty - It is a matter of common knowledge that usually the circle rate or the collector rate is lower than the prevalent actual market rate but to ensure registration of sale deeds at least at the circle rates or the collector rates such notifications are issued from time to time by the appellants - Moreover, if as per the HC, genuineness of the sale price entered into by the buyer and the seller cannot be questioned, then in majority of the cases it is unlikely that the State would ever receive the stamp duty according to the circle rate or the collector rate- Approach of the HC is totally unrealistic - HC's order set aside - District Collector's order restored - Appeal allowed.
