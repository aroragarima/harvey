Jagdish Chander Gupta v Kajaria Traders (India) Limited

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.C. Das Gupta, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 341; AIR 1964 SC 1882; 1964 ALJ 971; 1964 (66) BomLR 709; 1965 MahLJ 45; 1965 MPLJ 34; [1964] 8 S.C.R. 50

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Arbitration Act, Presidency Small Causes Courts Act, 1882

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Trusts & Associations - Arbitration & ADR - Partnership Act, 1932, s. 69(3) - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 8 and 8(2) - (A) Application of rule of ejusdem generis - Held, interpretation by ejusdem generis or noscitur a sociis need not always be made when words showing particular classes are followed by general words - Before general words can be so interpreted there must be a genus constituted or a category disclosed with reference to which general words can and are intended to be restricted - Expression 'claim of set off' in s.69 (3) of Partnership Act does not disclose a category or genus - Words 'other proceeding' which follow must, therefore, receive their full meaning untrammeled by words a claim of set off - Latter words neither intend nor can be construed to cut down generality of words 'other proceeding' - Sub-section provides for application of provisions of sub-ss. (1) and (2) to claims of set off and also to other proceedings of any kind which can properly be said to be for enforcement of any right arising from contract except those expressly mentioned as exceptions in sub-s. (3) and sub-s. (4) - A partner of an unregistered partnership applies u/s. 8 (2) of Arbitration Act to enforce a right arising from a contract between partners, proceedings are barred by s. 69 (3) of Partnership Act - Appeal allowed.
