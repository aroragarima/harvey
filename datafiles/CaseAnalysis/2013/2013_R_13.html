Rafique @ Rauf and others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 398; (2013) 12 SCC 121; AIR 2013 SC 2272; JT 2013 (11) SC 415; 2014 (2) RCR(Criminal) 757; 2013(7) SCALE 708; [2013] 7 S.C.R. 293

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 149, 307, 452, 148, 147 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused allegedly murdered deceased and caused injuries to PW nos. 2 and 3 - Trial Court convicted appellants accused for offences punishable u/ss. 302 r/w s. 149, u/s. 307 r/w s. 149 and also for offences punishable u/ss. 452, 148 and 147 of IPC - On appeal, HC affirmed order of Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order was sustainable - Held, even in the evidence nothing was stated to have been brought out in order to reject said case pleaded by prosecution - There was also no dispute about fact that occurrence took place in premises of deceased, as well as complainant and other injured witnesses - There was also no serious argument raised as regards registration of FIR relating to occurrence - Both Courts below, therefore, held in one voice that there was no chance of any manipulation at instance of police - HC reached conclusion that deceased, was in a position to speak and that statement was given by him who expired on next day evening - It further stated that since it was last statement of deceased to IO it could very well be treated as a dying declaration - HC was conscious of fact that Trial Court did not place any reliance on said statement which in opinion of HC was erroneous - There was nothing to contradict from material available on record in form of evidence either documentary or oral in order to hold that deceased, could not have made statement before PW6 - As was noted by Courts below, there was no delay involved in reporting occurrence to police and registration of FIR - Therefore, SC convinced that such reliance placed upon by HC was perfectly justified - Appeal dismissed.
