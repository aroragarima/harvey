Â Mowu v Superintendent, Special Jail, Nowgong, Assam and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, A.N. Ray, Mohammad Hidayatullah, G.K. Mitter, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 393; (1971) 3 SCC 936; (1972) SCC (Cr) 184; 1971 UJ 111

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: List Of Witnesses, Preventive Detention Act, 1950

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure , 1898, ss. 204(a) and 344 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 22(2), 21 and 32 - Petition for Habeas Corpus - Challenge of detention - Remand orders made u/s. 344 - S. 204(1-A) requires that a Magistrate shall not issue a process until a list of prosecution witnesses has been filed before him - This provision is intended to be a safeguard for an accused person so that he knows beforehand what evidence is likely to be produced against him - Held, no breach of art. 21 or art. 22 of Constitution - Further held, when order-sheet speaks of Magistrate having perused police report for remand, reasons and circumstances set out in report would be reasons for passing orders of remand - Hence, it is difficult to say that s. 344, assuming it applies, was contravened - Direction issued - Appeal dismissed.
