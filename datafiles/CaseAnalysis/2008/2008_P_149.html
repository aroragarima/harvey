Union Of India And Others v Prakash Kumar Tandon

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2590; (2009) 1 SCC (L&S) 394; (2009) 2 SCC 541; AIR 2009 SC 1375; 2008 (6) CTC 257; 2009 (1) ESC 57; JT 2009 (1) SC 436; 2009 (3) LLJ 198; 2009 (2) MLJ(SC) 1132; 2009(1) SCALE 175; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 855; 2008 (7) SLR 655; 2009 (2) UPLBEC 1268

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Establishment Code of Indian Railways - Principle of natural justice - Penalty - Reduction of pay - Charge sheet was issued against respondent alleging that he had accepted substandard quality of wood - Respondent requested the Enquiry Officer to summon the Asst.Engineer as a witness which was not considered by Enquiry officer - Respondent was found guilty and as penalty, his pay was reduced - On appeal, Appellate Authority enhanced penalty - Tribunal allowed application filed by respondent contending that as Asst. Engineer was not examined as a witness, order imposing penalty was unsustainable - HC dismissed writ petition filed by appellant - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of imposing penalty not sustainable for non-compliance of the principles of natural justice? - Held, principles of natural justice demand that an application for summoning a witness by the delinquent officer should be considered by the enquiry officer - It was obligatory on the part of the enquiry officer to pass an order in the said application - An enquiry officer is a quasi judicial authority - He, therefore, must perform his functions fairly and reasonably which is even otherwise the requirement of the principles of natural justice - In present case as principles of natural justice was not followed, order of imposing penalty not sustainable - Appeal dismissed.
