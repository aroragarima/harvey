M/S. Kalinga Mining Corporation v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

07 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 75; (2013) 5 SCC 252; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 905; 2013(2) ALL MR 451; JT 2013 (2) SC 597; 2013(2) SCALE 286; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 814

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Conduct of Business Rules, Minor Concession Rules, 1961

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Administrative - Minerals, Mineral Concession Rules, 1960 - Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957 - Grant of Mining Lease - Legal Heirs - Recommendations - Abated - Sustainability - Appellant applied to Govt. for prospecting license for grant of mining lease, same was not considered and appellant filed revision before Central Govt. which was disposed of by intimating State Govt. to consider, similar applications for grant of mining lease were pending for disposal which were rejected - Aggrieved appellant filed writ petition seeking direction from HC, for grant of mining lease which was dismissed - State Govt. recommended grant of mining lease in favor of respondent No. 10 and sought approval from Central Govt. which was rejected - Appellant and respondent No. 10 filed revision before Central Govt. allowed revision with direction to pass fresh orders on merit - Respondent No. 10 filed writ petition against orders of Central Govt. but pending disposal he died and an application was filed by legal heirs for substituting deceased before HC, which directed state Govt. to inform stage and date of hearing of revision petition, State contended that on death of applicant writ petition will abate which was rejected by HC - Central Govt. conveyed to State Govt. its approval of grant of mining lease in favour of legal representatives of deceased - Appellant challenged order passed by Central Govt., even though order was not made available to parties, HC disposed petition by directing State Govt. to reconsider matter and pass a fresh and speaking order after hearing appellant, legal representatives of deceased Central Govt. approved recommendations of State Government for grant of lease in favour of legal representatives, thereafter, terms and conditions were offered by State Govt., which were accepted - Appellant challenged order before HC, it was held that on death of original applicant, her application for mining lease does not abate, Court also held that this being a pure question of law, issue had become final and shall not be reopened in hearing before Central Govt. - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether Central Govt. was justified in passing orders by granting contract to legal heirs of deceased - Held, it was by now well settled that judicial review of administrative action/quasi-judicial orders passed by Govt. was limited only to correcting errors of law or fundamental procedural requirements which may lead to manifest injustice, when conclusions of authority were based on evidence, same could not be re-appreciated by court in exercise of its powers of judicial review - Court does not exercise powers of an appellate court in exercise of its powers of judicial review, it was only in cases where either findings recorded by administrative/quasi-judicial authority were based on no evidence or are so perverse that no reasonable person would have reached such a conclusion on basis of material available that court would be justified to interfere in decision - Scope of judicial review was limited to decision making process and not to decision itself, even if same appeared to be erroneous - Conclusions reached by HC were in accordance with settled principles of law although a large number of cases had been cited for parties on either side, but it was not necessary to consider all of them individually as principles with regard to observance of natural justice were well entrenched in our jurisprudence - Undoubtedly, any decision, even if it was administrative in nature, which causes adverse civil consequences must be passed upon hearing concerned parties, Central Govt. had fully complied with principle in passing order - Appeals dismissed.
