Mahesh Anantrai Pattani And Another v Commissioner Of Income-Tax, Bombay North

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 325; AIR 1961 SC 946; [1961] 41 ITR 481; 1961 (2) SCJ 226; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 742

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 7(1) - Salary - Monthly pension - Contemporaneous document - Personal gift - Assessee was appointed as Chief Dewan of Bhavnagar State - Maharaja appointed assessee as Chairman of Bhavnagar Durbar but no salary was fixed - Another Hazur Order the Maharaja granted a monthly pension of Rs. 2,000 to the assessee - Assessee was gifted Rs. 5 lakhs for his loyal and meritorious services - Assessability of Rs. 5 lakhs was raised in course of assessment proceedings - Income tax officer detained Rs. 5 lakhs received was liable to income-tax - On appeal to Appellate Assistant Commissioner it was dismissed - Appeal was taken to the Income tax Appellate Tribunal but the Tribunal also dismissed the appeal - HC apprehended that it was not possible to regard receipt of sum of money by assessee - Whether amount received by assessee was consider as gift - Held, Rs. 5 lakhs was not paid to assessee in token of appreciation for services rendered as a Dewan but as a personal gift for personal qualities of assessee and as a token of personal esteem - Payment due to or received by an assessee from an employer or former employer or from a provident or other fund was to extent to which it does not consist of contributions by assessee or interest on such contributions a profit received in lieu of salary unless payment was made solely as compensation for loss of employment and not by way of remuneration for past services - Maharaja indeed made a gift but order quite clearly disclosed that it was by way of remuneration for past services - Appeal dismissed.
