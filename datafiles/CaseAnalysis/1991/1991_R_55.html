Union of India and Others v Ramlakshi Finance Corporation Limited

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, Veeraswami Ramaswami

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 840; (1992) Supp1 SCC 443; AIR 1992 SC 711; 1992 CRLR 41; 1992 (38) ECR 486; 1991 (55) E.L.T. 433; JT 1992 (1) SC 85; 1991(2) SCALE 635; 1991 (2) UJ 617

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Constitution - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, ss. 35 and 35 A - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Appeal - Powers of Asst. Collector, Central Excise - Matter disposed of by Asst. Collector by issuing two appellate orders of authorities under Act in regard to same issue - Whether High Court criticising such conduct of Asst. Collector causing harassment to assessee justified - Held, justified - Need to follow judicial discipline and to give effect to orders of higher appellate authorities stressed - Petition dismissed.
