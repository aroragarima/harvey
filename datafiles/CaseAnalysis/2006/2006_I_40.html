Francis Stanly @ Stalin v Intelligence Officer, Narcotic Control Bureau, Thiruvananthapuram

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 967; AIR 2007 SC 794; 2007 CRLJ 1157; 2007 (1) JCC (Narcotics) 1; 2007 (1) KLT 941; 2006(13) SCALE 386; 2006 (3) ShimLC 371; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 977

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985 - Held, there is no allegation that the appellant himself was found in possession of any narcotics - Allegation was only that he handed over some narcotics to accused No. 1 - Evidence of accomplice can never be relied upon, since such evidence is admissible under s. 133 of Evidence Act - On the evidence of this particular case it would not be safe to maintain the conviction of the appellant, and he must be given the benefit of reasonable doubt - Appeal allowed.
