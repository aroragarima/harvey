Hem Raj and Another v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

09 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 712; (2003) 12 SCC 241; AIR 2003 SC 4259; 2003 CRLJ 4987; JT 2003 (7) SC 614; 2003(7) SCALE 278; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 466

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, ss. 154, 379 - IPC, 1860, ss. 33, 34, 120 B, 302, 323, 324 - Supreme Court (Enlargement Of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970, s. 2(a) - HC reversed judgment of acquittal by trial court - Held, evidence on record to be very unsatisfactory and inconsistent - Further held, evidence adduced by prosecution is rather inconsistent and creates a serious doubt about truthfulness of prosecution case - Even if it may be possible to take a different view, it cannot be said that view taken by trial court is not a reasonable view of evidence on record - It is well settled that if on basis of same evidence two views are reasonably possible and trial court takes view in favour of accused, appellate court, in an appeal against acquittal, will not be justified in reversing order of acquittal, unless it comes to conclusion that view taken by trial court was wholly unreasonable or perverse and it was not possible to take view in favour of accused on basis of evidence on record.
