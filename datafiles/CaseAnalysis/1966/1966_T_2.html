Bihta Co-Operative Development Cane Marketing Union Limited v Bank of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 172; AIR 1967 SC 389; [1967] 37 Comp Cas 98; 1967 (1) CompLJ 112; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 848

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bihar and Orissa Co-operative Societies Act 1935

Summary: Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Bihar and Orissa Co-operative Societies Act, 1935, u/ss. 48(1), 48(9) - Society Fund - Negligence - Embezzlement - Jurisdiction - Maintainability - Appellants/plaintiffs, a registered society under the Act had filed suit in trial court against defendants alleging that sum of Rs.11,000/- had been withdrawn from society account through loose cheque form which had been converted into cheque, which was decreed against defendant nos. 1, 2, 4, 5, and 7 - Aggrieved defendant nos. 1 and 2 appealed before HC contending that it should not be held liable because of defendant nos. 6 and 7, who were agents and were negligent and dishonest in discharge of duties entrusted to them by appellants and jurisdiction of trial court was ousted by combined operation u/s. 48(9) r/w s. 57 of the Act which was allowed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether trial court was justified to entertain suit u/s. 48(1) r/w s. 57 of the Act - Held, one of the signatures was forged so that there never was any mandate by customer at all to banker and question of negligence of customer in between signature and presentation of cheque never arose - Not only was there negligence on part of banker in not ascertaining whether signatures on cheque were genuine, circumstances attending encashment of cheque show conclusively that banker was negligent and some of its officers fraudulent right from beginning - Cheque form did not come out of customer's cheque book, loose cheque form returned by, in ex-constituent had been used for purpose of making out a cheque purported to be drawn by customer - Entries in register for issue of such loose forms were so suspicious that it was difficult to believe that employees of bank concerned with encashment of cheque were acting bonafide - There was no negligence on part of customer according to whose resolution, cheque had to be signed jointly by two persons - Therefore, fraud could only be perpetrated because of complicity of employees of bank, no doubt, with help of one of officers of society - Hence, dishonesty of a particular officer of society was not proximate cause of loss to bank and appeal succeeds, judgment of HC was set aside and restored decision of trial court - Appeal allowed.
