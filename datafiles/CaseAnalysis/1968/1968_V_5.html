Â State of Gujarat v Vakhtsinghji Sursinghji Vaghela and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 April 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, Mohammad Hidayatullah, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 375; AIR 1968 SC 1481; 1969 (10) G.L.R. 48; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 692

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Land Revenue Code, 1879 - Bombay Taluqdari Tenure Abolition Act, 1949 -Constitution of India, 1950 - Gujarat Taluqdars Act, 1888 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Immovable property - Land acquisition - Claimants - Awards - Collector made his awards of compensation u/ss. 7 and 14 - Claimants filed appeals before the Revenue Tribunal and later petitions u/art. 227 of the Constitution to the HC - Appeals were filed by the State of Gujrat and some of the claimants - Principal matters in controversy in these appeals, relate to the award of compensation under the heads i) difference in Jama and full assessment, (ii) solatium of 15% on the market value, (iii) irrigational bunds tanks and wells and (iv) river and river beds - Estate of the Taluqdar and their rights were defined in detail - Whether art. 227 of the Constitution gives the HC the power of superintendence over all courts and tribunals throughout the territories in relation to which it exercises jurisdiction - Held, s. 12 of the 1949 Act makes the decision of the Tribunal final and conclusive and the HC had no jurisdiction to interfere with the decision - The supervisory jurisdiction extends to keeping the subordinate tribunals within the limits of their authority and to seeing that they obey the law - It was the duty of the Revenue Tribunal to award compensation to the Taluqdars in accordance with the provisions of ss. 7 and 14 of the 1949 Act - Tribunal had misconceived its duties u/ss. 7 and 14, the HC could not only set aside its decision, but also direct it to make further inquiries after taking evidence - HC in the exercise of its supervisory jurisdiction under art. 227 cannot only annul the decision of the Tribunal but, can also issue further direction in the matter - Appeals dismissed.
