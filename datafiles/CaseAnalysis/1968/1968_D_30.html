Â Bidya Deb Barma Etc v District Magistrate, Tripura, Agartala

Supreme Court of India

06 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vishishtha Bhargava, J.M. Shelat, G.K. Mitter, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 102; AIR 1969 SC 323; 1969 CRLJ 525; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 562

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Preventive Detention Act, 1950, Defence Of India Act, 1971

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Preventive Detention Act, 1950, s. 3 - (A) Whether detention became illegal because 4 days were allowed to pass from order of detention and 2 days from date of arrest? - Held, No delay has been sufficiently explained - Besides holidays, circumstances explain cause of delay - District Magistrate communicated order 'forthwith' as required by law - (B) Whether approval ought to have been communicated to detenu and without this communication detention could not be legal? - S. 3(3) of Preventive Detention Act does not specify that order of approval is anything more than an administrative approval by State Govt. - (C) Whether State Govt. was also guilty of undue and unreasonable delay in reporting to Central Govt.? - Time can only be calculated from moment matter reached State Govt. - State Govt. took a week to consider these cases and it is reasonable to think that there might be a few more cases at same time - Having reached decision on Feb 19, action of State Govt. in communicating matter to Central Govt. on Feb 22 cannot be said to be so delayed as to render detention illegal - Petitions dismissed.
