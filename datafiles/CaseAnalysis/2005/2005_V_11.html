U.O.I. Through Govt. of Pondicherry and another v V. Ramakrishnan and others

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 881; (2005) 8 SCC 394; (2005) SCC (L&S) 1150; AIR 2005 SC 4295; 2005 (6) AWC 5825; 2006 (2) DRTC 517; 2005 (4) ESC 605; JT 2005 (9) SC 422; 2005(8) SCALE 273; [2005] Supp4 S.C.R. 291

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Criteria For Promotion, Eligibility - Qualifications

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 162, 309 - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985 - Central Administrative Tribunal (Procedure) Rules, 1987, r. 12 - Promotion - Held, valid rules made under proviso appended to art.309 of Constitution operates so long rules are not repealed and replaced - Draft rules, therefore, could not form basis for grant of promotion, when Rules to contrary is holding field - A deputationist has no legal right to continue in post - A deputationist indisputably has no right to be absorbed in post to which he is deputed - Questions which were raised before Central Administrative Tribunal and HC are pure questions of law - No infirmity in judgment of HC.
