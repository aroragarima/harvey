Ashok Kumar and Others v Rishi Ram and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 July 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1307; (2002) 5 SCC 641; AIR 2002 SC 2520; 2002 (2) AllCJ 1493; 2002 (48) ALR 401; 2002 (2) ARC 160; 2002 (3) AWC 2428; 2002 (2) CRC 180; JT 2002 (5) SC 99; 2002 (2) RCR(Rent) 139; 2002(5) SCALE 10B; 2002 (3) SCJ 219; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 1158; 2002 (4) SLT 230; 2002 (4) Supreme 465

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Framing Of Issues

Summary: Rent Control - Civil Procedure - Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, ss. 20(4), 30 and 38 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 5, rr. 1 and 2, O. 15, rr. 5 and 15 - Whether tenant paid rent at first hearing of suit within meaning of s. 20(4) of U P Act? - Held, effective date of first hearing of suit on which court proposed to apply its mind, was 10-10-1980, amount of arrears of rent having been paid on that date, therefore compliance of s. 20(4) of U P Act so tenant is entitled to benefit of said provision - Appeal allowed.
