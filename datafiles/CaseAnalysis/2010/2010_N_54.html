Arvind Kumar Mishra v New India Assurance Company Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

29 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 898; (2010) 10 SCC 254; 2010 ACJ 2867; 2010 (6) AWC 6383; JT 2010 (10) SC 254; 2011 (2) MLJ(SC) 308; 2011 (1) MPLJ 566; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 917; 2010(10) SCALE 298; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 857

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 166 - Enhancement of compensation - Assessment of loss of earnings of victim - Appellant-Student of Engineering was seriously injured in an accident, claimed compensation of Rs. 22 lakhs - Owner of the vehicle as well as Insurance company contested the claim petition - Tribunal held that the owner of the vehicle and the insurer were liable to pay the compensation to the appellant as accident occurred due to rash and negligent driving of the truck - Tribunal allowed the total compensation of Rs. 2,50,000/- along with the interest @ 9 % per annum - Appellant dissatisfied with the assessment of the compensation by the Tribunal, approached HC for enhancement of compensation - HC increased compensation amount from Rs. 2,50,000/- to Rs. 3,50,00/- - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC rightly awarded compensation of Rs. 3,50,000/- to the appellant? - Held, no - It is fair and reasonable to assess appellant future earnings at Rs. 60,000/- per annum taking the salary and allowances payable to an Assistant Engineer in public employment - Future earnings may be discounted by 30% as appellant suffered 70% permanent disability, hence, multiplicand should be Rs. 42,000/- - As per SC decision in Sarla Verma and Ors. vs. Delhi Transport Corporation and Anr 2009 INDLAW SC 488, loss of future earnings by multiplying the multiplicand of Rs. 42,000/- by a multiplier of 18 comes to Rs. 7,56,000/- - The Tribunal awarded appellant Rs. 1,50,000/- towards treatment including the medical expenses, same is maintained as it is and, accordingly, the total amount of compensation to which the appellant is entitled is Rs. 9,06,000/- - Hence, compensation awarded by the HC in the sum of Rs. 3,50,000/- is enhanced to Rs. 9,06,000/- - Appeal partly allowed.
