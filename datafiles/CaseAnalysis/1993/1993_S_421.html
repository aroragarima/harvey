Valsala v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1091; (1993) Supp3 SCC 665; (1993) SCC (Cr) 1082; AIR 1994 SC 117; 1993 (2) Crimes 267; 1994 CRLJ 1; JT 1993 (4) SC 549; 1993 (2) KLT 550

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substance Act, 1985, s. 21 - Officer in charge of Police Station saw a lady, Smt Valsala sitting under a tree near Sabudra Hotel on the eastern side of the beach having a packet by her side - Police party became suspicious - They examined the packet and found it to be a powder - Suspecting it to be brown sugar, they seized the same - Sent to the Chemical Examiner, examined and found it to be brown sugar - Appellant convicted by Sessions Judge, u/s. 21 of NDPS Act and sentenced to 10 years' RI and to pay a fine of rupees one lakh, in default of payment RI for one year - Appeal preferred by her was dismissed by High Court - Present appeal - Held, no doubt the trafficking in narcotic drugs is a menace to the society but in the absence of satisfactory proof, the courts cannot convict - The judgment of Learned Sessions Judge as affirmed by HC is set aside and the convictions and sentences passed against the appellant are also set aside - If the appellant is in jail, she shall be set at liberty forthwith - Appeal allowed.
