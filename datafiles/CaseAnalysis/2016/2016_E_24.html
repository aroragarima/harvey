Gujarat Urja Vikas Nigam Limited v Essar Power Limited

Supreme Court of India

09 August 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 587; (2016) 9 SCC 103; 2016 (5) AWC 5001; JT 2016 (8) SC 86; 2016 (4) RCR(Civil) 75; 2016(7) SCALE 742; [2016] 5 S.C.R. 101

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Tariff, Energy, Electricity Act, 2003, Telecom Regulatory Authority of India Act, 1997, Constitution (Forty-Second Amendment) Act, 1976, Generating Station, Station, Sale Of Goods Act, National Tax Tribunal Act, 2005, Sale of Goods Act, 1920, Constitution of India, 1950, Power Purchase Agreement, Supreme Court (Number of Judges) Act, 1956

Summary: (A) Electricity & Energy - Electricity Act, 2003, ss. 113(3), 113(b)(i), 113(b)(ii), 113(b)(iii), 12, 125, 14, 2(28), 2(39), 5, 78, 84, 85, 86(i)(f) - Electricity - Wrongful allocation - Challenged - Appellant filed petition before Commission u/s. 86 (i)(f) of the Act for adjudication of dispute arising out of Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) and sought compensation for wrongful allocation of electricity by respondent to its sister concern in preference to appellant - Commission held that appellant was entitled to claim compensation for the energy diverted to sister concern from the capacity allocated to appellant under the PPA and directs respondents to declare availability for entire capacity and it shall not divert any power to its sister concern in a manner contrary to the proportionate principle - On appeal, Tribunal allowed appeal and set aside impugned order of Commission - Hence, instant appeal - Whether interpretation of PPA to determine if there is any obligation to declare availability of power in the ratio of 300 : 215 was valid.

Held, agreement contemplates the proportion of allocation of a capacity. Commission was right in observing that once entire capacity has been allocated in two parts in a particular proportion, contention of respondent that it could sell power to ESL beyond allocated capacity could not be accepted. Respondent was under obligation as per Schedule VI to declare weekly schedule of capacity available and dispatch instructions were to be issued on the basis of said declaration. Tribunal erred in holding that there was no obligation to declare available capacity on proportionate basis. The finding of Commission is the correct interpretation of agreement. Appeal allowed.

(B) Electricity & Energy - Electricity Act, 2003, ss. 113(3), 113(b)(i), 113(b)(ii), 113(b)(iii), 12, 125, 14, 2(28), 2(39), 5, 78, 84, 85, 86(i)(f) - Effect of letters - Acknowledged - Validity - Whether effect of letters acknowledged on the rights of the parties was valid.

Held, letters of respondent acknowledged its liability to allocate the generated power to appellant and to sister concern in ratio of 58:42. Tribunal held that said letters could not be relied upon in support of claim that appellant was entitled to be allocated generated power in proportion of 58:42. Therefore, such finding is erroneous and is without any basis and is liable to be set aside. The finding of Commission is based on record. Appeal allowed.

(C) Electricity & Energy - Electricity Act, 2003, ss. 113(3), 113(b)(i), 113(b)(ii), 113(b)(iii), 12, 125, 14, 2(28), 2(39), 5, 78, 84, 85, 86(i)(f) - Dispatch instructions -Determination - Whether interpretation of Schedule VI to determine obligation to issue dispatch instructions arose before declaration of availability.

Held, in interpreting Schedule VI, Commission held that respondent was liable to declare weekly capacity available and on that basis dispatch instructions were required to be issued. The contrary view taken by Tribunal was contrary to the agreement between parties as reflected in Schedule VI. Appeal allowed.

(D) Electricity & Energy - Electricity Act, 2003, ss. 113(3), 113(b)(i), 113(b)(ii), 113(b)(iii), 12, 125, 14, 2(28), 2(39), 5, 78, 84, 85, 86(i)(f) - Relief - Entitled for - Whether appellant was entitled to any relief.

Held, order of Tribunal was erroneous. Tribunal erroneously held that there was no pleading for making the claim. Thus, Tribunal has committed error of law as well as of record in recording its finding. Commission has left actual working out of the loss to be worked out separately and on that basis appellant has already filed its claim which was pending consideration before Commission. The said proceeding can be revived in the light of SC finding. Impugned order of Tribunal was set aside and restore that of Commission. Appeal allowed.
