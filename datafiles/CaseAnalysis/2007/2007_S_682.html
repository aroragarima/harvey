Sunder Lal v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P.K. Balasubramanyan, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1312; (2007) 10 SCC 371; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 601; 2007 (3) ALT 294; 2007 (2) JCC 1538; JT 2007 (6) SC 451; 2007(6) SCALE 649; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 76

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Credibility of Dying declaration - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32 - Deceased was assaulted by appellant - In his dying declaration, deceased named appellant as his assailant - Conviction in present case based on dying declaration - Whether conviction of appellant can be solely based upon dying declaration of deceased? - Held, yes - If Court is satisfied that declaration was true and voluntary, undoubtedly, it can base its conviction without any further corroboration - No absolute rule of law that dying declaration cannot form sole basis of conviction unless it is corroborated - Exclusion of dying declaration will result in miscarriage of justice because victim being only eye-witness - Court should ensure that statement of deceased was not as a result of tutoring, or a product of imagination and he was in a fit state of mind after a clear opportunity to observe and identify assailant - Conviction modified to be u/s. 304 part I of IPC to meet ends of justice - Order accordingly.
