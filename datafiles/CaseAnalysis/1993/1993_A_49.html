All India Imam Organization and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 May 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1153; (1993) 3 SCC 584; AIR 1993 SC 2086; JT 1993 (3) SC 536; JT 1993 (Supp) SC 536; 1993(2) SCALE 955; [1993] 3 S.C.R. 742; 1993 (4) SLR 117; 1993 (2) UJ 155

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Petition under art.32 of Constitution - Enforcement of fundamental right - Exploitation by Wakf Boards - Whether petitioners are employees of Board and should be paid basic wages to enable them to survive? - Held, financial difficulties of institution cannot be above fundamental right of a citizen - If Boards have been entrusted with responsibility of supervising and administering Wakf then it is their duty to harness resources to pay those persons who perform most important duty namely of leading community prayer in a mosque very purpose for which it is created - It is to be directed that the Mosques which are under control of Govt. shall not be governed by this order but if their Imams are not paid any remuneration and they have no independent income Govt. may fix their emoluments on basis as Central Wakf Board may do for other mosques in pursuance of this order - For other mosques, except those which are not registered with Board of their respective State or which are not manned by members of Islamic faith scheme shall provide for payment of remuneration to such Imams taking guidance from scale of pay prevalent in State of Punjab and Haryana - Order accordingly.
