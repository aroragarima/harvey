Beharilal Ramcharan v Income Tax Officer, Special Circle 'B' Ward, Kanpur and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 July 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 233; (1981) 3 SCC 473; AIR 1981 SC 1585; 1981 (24) CTR 116; [1981] 131 ITR 129; 1981(3) SCALE 1059; [1982] 1 S.C.R. 1; 1981 TAXLR 1441; [1981] 6 TAXMAN 69; 1981 UJ 935

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 226(3) - Interpretation of s. 226(3) - Whether u/s. 226 (3) the Income tax Officer hold an inquiry before he came to the conclusion that the statement contained in the affidavit filed on behalf of the petitioners was false in any material particular - After receipt of the affidavit, the Income Tax Officer straight away reached the conclusion that the statement on oath was false in material particulars - The Income Tax Officer did not offer any opportunity to the petitioners - Held, that it will be open to the Income Tax Officer to proceed to hold an inquiry in accordance with the principles of natural justice for the purpose of determining whether the statement on oath was false in material particulars and appeal partly allowed
