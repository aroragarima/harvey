State of Maharashtra v Association of Court Stenos., P.A., P.S. and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, R.P. Sethi, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 13; (2002) 2 SCC 141; (2002) SCC (L&S) 218; AIR 2002 SC 555; 2002 (18) CLR 781; 2002 (93) FLR 174; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 453; JT 2002 (1) SC 55; 2002 (35) LabIC 530; 2002 (1) LLN 675; 2002(1) SCALE 98; 2002 (1) SCJ 35; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 124; 2002 (2) SLJ 429; 2002 (1) SLR 751; 2002 (1) SLT 163; 2002 (1) Supreme 44; 2002 (1) UPLBEC 510

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Jurisdiction of HC - Parity in pay scale - Entitlement - Respondents, Court Stenographers, Personal Assistants and Personal Secretaries attached to the Judges of Bombay HC, filed petition alleging that prior to 30-9-1990, they were getting the same scale of pay, as was being paid to the Senior Personal Assistants, attached to the Chief Secretary and the Additional Chief Secretary in the State of Maharashtra, but that parity had not been maintained after 1-10-1990 - Prayer was, therefore made that the Pay-scale should be fixed in the same scale - HC by the impugned judgment, held that there was a parity of pay and there is no reason why the respondents would not be entitled to the same - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC in its discretionary jurisdiction u/art. 226 of the Constitution, can itself examine the nature of work discharged by its employees and issue a mandamus, directing a particular pay scale to be given to such employees.

Held, it would not be appropriate for the HC in exercise of its discretionary jurisdiction u/art. 226 to examine the nature of work discharged by the staff attached to the Judges of the Court and direct grant of any particular pay scale to such employees, as that would be a matter for the Chief Justice within his jurisdiction u/art. 229(2) of the Constitution. Appeal allowed.
