Indian Iron and Steel Company Limited v Prahlad Singh

Supreme Court of India

03 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2798; (2001) SCC (L&S) 239; (2001) 1 SCC 424; AIR 2001 SC 69; 2001 (1) AWC 167; 2000 (87) FLR 790; JT 2000 (Supp2) SC 354; 2001 LabIC 26; 2000 (2) LLJ 1653; 2001 (1) MLJ(SC) 95; 2000(7) SCALE 297; 2000 (5) SLR 627; 2000 (7) SLT 747; [2000] Supp4 S.C.R. 357; 2000 (7) Supreme 710

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Claim of respondent workman in raising dispute after a period of about 13 years from date of termination - Tribunal held that order of termination of services of respondent was justified and he was not entitled to any relief - High Court quashed award of Tribunal, directing appellant to reinstate respondent - Appellant contended that impugned orders could not be sustained at all - Whether HC was right in quashing award passed by Tribunal without even stating as to how findings of fact recorded by Tribunal were wrong? - Held, relief can be declined on ground of delay and laches, depends on facts and circumstances of each case - In this case claim was made almost after a period of 13 years without any reasonable or justifying ground and there was nothing on record to explain this delay - On merits also he had no case, even otherwise findings of facts recorded by Tribunal in light of Standing Orders cannot be said to be untenable or perverse - Appeal allowed.
