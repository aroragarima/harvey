Suraj Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

24 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1177; (2008) 16 SCC 686; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 631; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 2591; JT 2008 (8) SC 411; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 88; 2008(10) SCALE 536; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 286

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Demeanour Of Witness, Common Sense, Subordinate Court, Central Act, Earlier Decision, Old Code, Power Of The Court, Finding Of Facts, Medical Witness, Non Examination, Hearing Of Appeal, Eye- Witness, Gunshot, Possibilities

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 r/w 34 - Trial Court directed acquittal of the appellant primarily on the ground that there was discrepancy between the ocular evidence and the medical evidence, independent witnesses were not examined - High Court reversed the acquittal - Appeal against - Held, no restrictions have been imposed by the legislature on the powers of the appellate court in dealing with appeals against acquittal - When such an appeal is filed, the High Court has full power to reappreciate, review and reconsider the evidence at large, the material on which the order of acquittal is founded and to reach its own conclusions on such evidence - It would be erroneous to accord undue primacy to the hypothetical answers of medical witnesses to exclude the eyewitnesses' account which had to be tested independently and not treated as the "variable" keeping the medical evidence as the "constant" - Appeal dismissed.
