Jagatjit Industries Limited v Intellectual Property Appellate Board and others

Supreme Court of India

20 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 56; (2016) 4 SCC 381; AIR 2016 SC 478; 2016 (2) ALD(SC) 172; 2016 (2) AWC 2029; 2016 (1) CLT(SC) 110; JT 2016 (1) SC 316; 2016 (65) PTC 242; 2016(1) SCALE 450; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 808

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Keywords: Extension Of Time, Certificate Of Registration, Rectification, Trade and Merchandise Marks Act, 1958, Notice Of Opposition, Rectification Proceedings, Trade Marks Act, 1999

Summary: Intellectual Property - Trade Marks Act, 1999, ss. 23, 21, 57(4), 125 - Registration of trademark - Within statutory period of three months, Respondent no. 4 filed Form TM-44 seeking extension of one month's time for filing its notice of opposition against appellant's application for registration of identical trademark - While so, appellant was granted trademark registration certificate on 13-1-2004 but later, show cause notice was issued by Registrar to appellant, to effect that registration certificate had been issued wrongly and it was proposed to rectify register u/s. 57(4) of the Act - However, Registrar recalled show cause notice on ground that u/s. 125 of the Act, proceedings could legally continue only before Appellate Board and not before him - On appeal, Appellate Board reversed Registrar' order and held that registration of trademark on 13-1-2004, that is even before expiry of extended one month, would obviously be contrary to s.23 of the Act and so, invalid in law and also set aside orders of Registrar dt.14-11-2005 - Aggrieved, appellant filed writ petition before HC and Single Judge of HC set aside order of Appellate Board and sustained order passed by Registrar - However, on appeal, DB of HC set aside order of Single Judge, holding that s. 23(1) of the Act had been violated, and that s. 125 would not apply on the facts of case as it is the duty of Registrar to maintain the purity of register and held that grant of registration on 13-1-2004 was itself invalid being contrary to s. 23(1) of the Act and allowed appeal - Hence, instant appeal by appellant - Whether impugned judgment of HC could be upheld.

Held, respondent no.4 sought extension of one month's time for filing its notice of opposition within three month period granted to it u/s. 21(2) and did in prescribed statutory Form TM-44 stating that the reason for extension would be that they have to seek legal advice before filing notice of opposition. Further, as evidenced by letter dt.16-2-2004, time has been extended by Registrar. Hence, it is clear that any registration certificate granted prior to 30 days extended period form 6-1-2004 would be violative of s. 23(1) of the Act. Hence, appellate board and DB are clearly right in declaring that registration certificate, having been issued on 13-1-2004, would be violative of s. 23(1)(a) and the register would have to be rectified by deleting the said trademark there from. Hence, judgment of DB is upheld. Appeal dismissed.
