Nirmal Kanta (Dead), through Lrs. v Ashok Kumar and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2416; (2008) 7 SCC 722; AIR 2008 SC 1768; 2008 (72) ALR 816; 2008 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1743; 2008 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2097; 2008 (151) PLR 431; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 632; 2008 (1) RCR(Rent) 378; 2008(4) SCALE 752; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 588; 2008 (2) UC 963

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: East Punjab Rent Restriction Act, 1949

Summary: Rent Control - East Punjab Rent Restriction Act, 1949, ss. 13 and 15 - Subletting - Eviction - Tenant was running cloth business in the tenanted shop and allowed a tailor to sit in part of impugned shop with his sewing machine - Landlord filed petition for eviction from impugned shop on the ground of subletting - Rent Controller dismissed said petition - Appellate authority dismissed appeal filed against said order - Tenant filed revision petition before HC - HC allowed said revision on the finding that by allowing a tailor, even on payment, to sit in a part of the shop-room with his sewing machine, while retaining his possession and rights as a tenant over the premises leased to him, the tenant did not create a sub-lease and the tailor could at best be said to be a licensee - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC's order could be upheld? - Held, main ingredient of the creation of a sub-tenancy and/or grant of a sub-lease not having been established, it may at best be said that the respondent No.2 was a licensee under respondent No.1 which would not entitle the landlord to obtain a decree for eviction against the respondent No.1-tenant on the ground of sub-letting - Tailor only assisting tenant in his cloth business by helping customers to assess amount of cloth required for their particular purposes - Such activity did not give exclusive possession for that part of shop from where he was operating and where his sewing machine was affixed - HC's order upheld - Appeal dismissed
