Rajesh Kumar & Ors. v D.C.I.T. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

01 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 733; (2007) 2 SCC 181; AIR 2007 SC 181; [2006] 287 ITR 91; JT 2006 (10) SC 76; 2006(11) SCALE 409; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 284; [2006] 157 TAXMAN 168

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: [A.] Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 195 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 105 - Chartered Accountants Act, 1949 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14 - Appointment of an auditor for special audit of accounts in terms of s.142(2A) - Held, Bombay HC and Delhi HC, are not correct in stating that a direction issued u/s. 142(2 A) of Act to be administrative in nature - When books of accounts have been produced and examined, assessing officer would be proceeding to make ultimate order of assessment - An appeal against order of assessment would not serve any real purpose as appellate authority would not go into such a question since direction issued u/s. 142(2 A) of Act is not an appellate order - Appeal allowed.
