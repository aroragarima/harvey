State of Maharashtra v Shashikant S. Pujari and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1258; (2006) 13 SCC 175; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 499; AIR 2007 SC 373; 2007(1) ALL MR 981; 2006 (7) AWC 7400; 2006(12) SCALE 495; 2007 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 763; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 490

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Precedent

Summary: Education - Constitution - Permanent appointment of lecturer - Qualification - University Grants Commission Act, 1956, ss. 12, 12A, 13, 25 and 26 - (A) Whether respondent was to be given relaxation for minimum qualification for teachers? - Held, purport relaxation granted in terms of G R in regard to qualifications of teacher was in relation to those who were already in service in permanent position duly selected by University Selection Committee prior to revision of pay scales - It was, therefore, not applicable to case of respondent - (B) Whether respondent is to be benefited under Govt. resolution D/-25-10-1977? - Further held, on that date, respondent was not in service - If on that date he was not in service, question of his being duly selected by University Selection Committee on permanent basis would not arise - Appeal allowed.
