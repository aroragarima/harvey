Â Shri Radeshyam Khare and Another v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 September 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, K. Subba Rao, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 174; AIR 1959 SC 107; 1959 (1) MLJ 5; 1959 (1) MLJ(SC) 5; 1959 MPLJ 185; 1959 SCJ 6; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 1440

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Central Provinces and Berar Municipalities Act, 1922

Summary: (A) Municipalities & Local Governments - Central Provinces and Berar Municipalities Act, 1922 ss. 53-A, 57 - Municipal committee - Performance of duties - Incompetence - Abuse of power - State Govt. issued a notification u/s. 53-A of the 1922 Act appointing an executive officer to the municipal committee for a period of 18 months on a finding that the municipal committee had proved itself incompetent to perform its duties - Notification included findings of incompetency and abuse of power against the municipal committee - Whether the notification issued u/s. 53-A of the 1922 Act was, in substance and in reality, made u/s. 57 of the 1922 Act - Held, the State Govt. was well within its rights to take action, u/s. 53-A of the 1922 Act as it had in terms purported to do - To say that because some of the findings amounted to abuse of power the State Govt. should have acted u/s. 57 of the 1922 Act was to deprive it of its discretion which the Act undoubtedly conferred on it - Fact that the notification recorded, apart from the findings of incompetency, certain findings of abuse of power, did not lead to the conclusion that the State Govt. had taken action u/s. 57 of the 1922 Act and not u/s. 53-A of the 1922 Act although, in terms, it said it acted under the latter s. - Also, the State Govt. thought that the abuse of power was not of a very grave nature but evidenced only incompetency - A committee which abused its power could also have been reasonably regarded as incompetent to perform the duties imposed on it - That apart, even if the committee was guilty of incompetency as well as of some abuses, there was nothing to prevent the State Govt., as a matter of policy, to take action for incompetency u/s. 53-A of the 1922 Act - Mere inclusion of the findings of abuse of power in the catalogue of the municipal committee's misdeeds did not obliterate the findings on incompetency - Therefore, there was no warrant for contending that the impugned notification could be regarded as having been made u/s. 57 of the 1922 Act - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Municipalities & Local Governments - Central Provinces and Berar Municipalities Act, 1922 ss. 53-A, 57 - Municipal committee - Performance of duties - Quasi-judicial function - Administrative function - Whether in taking action u/s. 53-A the State Govt. was to be regarded as functioning as a quasi-judicial body or a mere administrative body - Held, action taken by the State Govt. u/s. 53-A of the 1922 Act was not a judicial or quasi-judicial act but was an administrative act - Simple fact that the incompetency of the municipal committee went to the root of the jurisdiction of the State Govt. to exercise its power u/s. 53-A of the 1922 Act did not require that that fact should be determined judicially - There was no need for any express provision that the State Govt. should act judicially - It was sufficient if the duty was implied from the provisions of the statute - Mere fact that a question of fact had to be determined as a preliminary condition before action could be taken under the statute by itself did not carry that implication - There should have been some indication in the statute as to the manner or mode in which the preliminary fact was to be determined - There was nothing in s. 53-A which in terms imposed any duty on the State Govt. to act judicially - No form of procedure was laid down or even referred to from which such a duty could be inferred - It was also material to note that whereas an order u/s. 57 of the 1922 Act was of a permanent character the one to be made u/s. 53-A of the 1922 Act was to be of a limited duration - Further, s. 53-A of the 1922 Act contemplated swift action and a judicial hearing could easily frustrate the very purpose contemplated by s. 53-A of the 1922 Act, for a judicial act would be subject to the powers of superintendence of the superior courts and the operation of the order u/s. 53-A of the 1922 Act could be postponed, as it was done in the instant case, by taking, the matter from court to court until it was set at rest by the instant court - If the determination of a jurisdictional fact was not by itself sufficient to indicate that, it had to be done judicially, there was nothing else in s. 53-A of the 1922 Act or in any other s. of the Act which would lead to the conclusion that the State Govt. should act judicially - Appeal dismissed.
