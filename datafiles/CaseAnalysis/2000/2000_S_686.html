Hukam Singh and Others v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

14 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 467; (2000) 7 SCC 490; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1416; AIR 2000 SCW 4416; 2000 (4) Crimes 23; 2001 CRLJ 511; 2001 (2) JCC 772; JT 2000 (10) SC 411; 2000 (4) RCR(Criminal) 167; 2000(6) SCALE 383; 2000 (6) SLT 723; [2000] Supp3 S.C.R. 284; 2000 (6) Supreme 245

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Prosecution Witness - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 226 and 231 - Two categories of witnesses to occurrence - Public Prosecutor did not examine some of witnesses - Whether public prosecutor is obligated to examine each and every witness during a trial? - Held, no - Public Prosecutor has a discretion to limit to one or two witnesses as he considers appropriate - In present case Public Prosecutor came to know that witnesses would not support prosecution version - If Prosecutor honestly and bona fide believes that some of witnesses have been won over, it would be unreasonable to insist that he must tender such witnesses before court - Prosecutor can state in court that fact and refrain from examining that witness as prosecution witness - On other hand, defence can cite and examine skipped witness as defence witness - Appeal dismissed.
