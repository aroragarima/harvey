Â Lakshmi Charan Sen And Ors Etc. v A.K.M. Hassan Uzzaman And Ors. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, D.A. Desai, A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah, Baharul Islam

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 232; (1985) 4 SCC 689; AIR 1985 SC 1233; 1985(2) SCALE 384; [1985] Supp1 S.C.R. 493; 1986 (1) UJ 104

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure; Tort

Summary: Election - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Representation of the People Act, 1950, ss. 21, 23, 146 - Registration of Electors Rules, 1960, r. 20, 23 - Election - Validity - (A) determmination of nature of right to be included in electoral roll or to challenge inclusion of any name in the roll - (B) Claims for inclusion of names and objections relating to inclusion of certain names, in electoral rolls, not disposed of - Election, if vitiated thereby - (C) What is the effec of violation of direction of Election Commission (not law) to Chief Electoral Officers - Held, the right is conferred upon an individual and not a political party as Election laws do not recognize political parties except in r. 11(c) of 1960 Rules - Not vitiated , it has to be held on basis of electoral roll in force on last date for making nominations - Election is not rendered invalid - Order accordingly.
