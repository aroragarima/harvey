Vaikuntam Chandrappa and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

14 August 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, A.K. Sarkar, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 284; AIR 1960 SC 1340; 1960 CRLJ 1681

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 r/w s. 34 - Four appellants and one other were convicted u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 and were sentenced to death - Present appeal - Evidence against appellants was both direct and circumstantial - Direct evidence consisted of statements of three eye witnesses - Prosecution Witness 1 prepared to identify nine persons irrespective of being certain that he had seen them at the time of incident, no reliance placed on such an identification even of a witness who is disinterested as far as A-3 and A-4 concerned - Once rejected cannot be used as corroboration of evidence of Prosecution Witness 3 - Circumstantial evidence by itself not such as to make certain presence of witness at the time of murder - Accused also entitled to benefit of doubt and must be acquitted, once evidence of Prosecution Witness 2 with respect to him is rejected - However, accused A-5 and A-7 because of High Court holding that there was sufficient corroboration of evidence of Prosecution Witness 3, their appeal fails - Appeal of A-4 allowed and Appeal of A-7 dismissed.
