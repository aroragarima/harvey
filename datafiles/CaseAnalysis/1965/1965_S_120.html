Â K. S. Venkataraman and Company Private Limited v State of Madras

Supreme Court of India

18 October 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 323; AIR 1966 SC 1089; 1966 (1) ITJ 415; [1966] 60 ITR 112; 1966 (1) MLJ(SC) 108; 1966 (1) SCJ 515; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 229; 1966 (17) STC 418

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, art. 96 - Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, s. 2(h)(i) - Refund of sales tax - Works Contracts - Limitation - During years 1948-49 to 1952-53 appellants were assessed to sales tax by State of Madras on basis that contracts executed by them were 'works contracts' - On 05-04-1954, High Court of judicature at Madras held in Gannon Dunkerley and Co. v State of Madras that relevant provision of Madras General Sales Tax Act empowering State of Madras to assess indivisible building contracts to sales tax was ultra vires the legislature of the Province of Madras - Subsequently appellants claimed refund of amounts collected from them - (A) Whether s. 2(h) and Explanation (1)(i) of s. 2(i) of Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, read in light of r. 4(3) of Turnover and Assessment Rules, were ultra vires legislature of Province of Madras? - Legislative power to levy tax on sales of goods was confined to transactions of sales and any attempt of legislature to tax under guise of or under pretence of such a power transactions which were wholly outside it, was ultra vires - Held, 'sale' in s. 2(h) after it was amended by Madras General Sales Tax (Amendment) Act 25 of 1947 was ultra vires the legislature of the Province of Madras - (B) Whether a suit for refund of tax was maintainable in view of general scheme of Act, and in particular of s. 18 A, which was added by Madras Act 6 of 1951? - Further held, Deputy Commercial Tax Officer had power to assess transaction of sale in works contracts - Assuming that he erred in interpretation of contract or relevant statutory provision, order was on that account not without jurisdiction - It could only bet set aside by appropriate proceedings under Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, and not otherwise - Suit was therefore barred by scheme of Act and s. 18 A which was later incorporated by Act 6 of 1951 - (C) Whether suit was barred by law of limitation? - Further held, appellant's suit was instituted within three years from date on which appellants claim that they came to know about decision of Madras HC in Gannon Dunkerley and Co. case and claim was unquestionably within limitation - Appeal dismissed.
