Vimal Chadh v Vikas Choudhary and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 904; (2008) 15 SCC 216; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 790; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 450; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 1743; 2008 CRLJ 3190; 2008 (2) JCC 1399; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 274; 2008(8) SCALE 608; [2008] 9 S.C.R. 911

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Women & Children

Keywords: Date Of Occurrence, School Register

Summary: Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000 in issue -Determination of age of a juvenile in delinquency within the meaning of JJ Act, 2000 in issue - Held, relevant date for determination is the age of the accused would be the date on which the occurrence took place - If an offence has been a continuing offence, then the age of the juvenile in delinquency should be determined with reference to the date on which the offence is said to have been committed by the accused - Giving calls for payment of ransom is an offence; in case of murder coupled with abduction in a given case may be considered to be a continuous offence - Appeal allowed.
