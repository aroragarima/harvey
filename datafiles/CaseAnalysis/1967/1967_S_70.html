Â State of Assam and Others v Labanya Probha Debi

Supreme Court of India

11 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah, R.S. Bachawat, J.M. Shelat, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 270; AIR 1967 SC 1575; 1968 (1) SCJ 718; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 611

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Rajasthan Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1951, Amending Act, Assam Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1936

Summary: Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 254(2) and 301, sch. 7, List 2, Entry 57 and List 3, Entry 35 - Assam Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1963 - Assam Motor Vehicles Taxation (Amendment) Acts, 1963 and 1966 - Validation of amending acts - (A)Whether said Amending Acts of 1963 and 1966 increasing rate of tax are ultra vires art. 301 of Constitution ? - Amending Acts are only regulatory measures imposing compensatory taxes for facing trade, commerce and intercourse - Held, Amending Acts, 1963 and 1966 are not hit by art. 301 of Constitution - (B) Whether any of provisions of Amending Acts is repugnant to any of provisions of existing law with respect to any of matters enumerated in concurrent List, hence, bringing a conflict between Entries 35 in List III and 57 in List II? - Amendment Acts do not lay down any principles on which taxes on motor vehicles are to be levied within meaning of Entry 35 of Concurrent List but are solely concerned with taxes on vehicles within meaning of Entry 57 of List I - Entries 35 in List III and 57 in List II deal with two different heads - Further held, Amending Acts do not come into conflict with existing law in respect of any principles of taxation but only deal with a subject - (C) Whether there is application of art. 254(2) of Constitution in instant case? - Held, Amending Acts deal with matter which is exclusively within legislative competence of State Legislature, hence there is no scope for application of art. 254(2) of Constitution - Appeal allowed.
