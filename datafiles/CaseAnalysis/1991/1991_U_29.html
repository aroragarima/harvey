Daya Singh v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

24 April 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 103; (1991) 3 SCC 61; (1991) SCC (Cr) 523; AIR 1991 SC 1548; 1992 (1) CalLT(SC) 38; 1991 (2) Crimes 180; 1991 CRLJ 1903; 1991 CrLR(SC) 476; JT 1991 (2) SC 349; 1991(1) SCALE 780; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 462; 1991 (2) UJ 131

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 257 - Cognizance taken by Supreme Court on letter from appellant, who was sentenced to death and was waiting for execution for a long time - Appellant prayed for quashing of death sentence on grounds of delay in execution - Held, petitioner in continuous detention since 1972 - Having regard to all circumstances of case, it is fit to substitute sentence of imprisonment for life in place of petitioner's death sentence - On conversion of death sentence to life imprisonment, petitioner would now be governed and dealt with as a life convict for all purposes - Petition allowed.
