Saleem Bhai and others v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1514; (2003) 1 SCC 557; AIR 2003 SC 759; 2003 (2) ALD(SC) 84; 2003 (3) AWC 2157; 2003 (5) Bom.C.R. 454; 2003 (1) DMC 166; JT 2002 (10) SC 558; 2003 (2) LCD 876; 2003 (1) RCR(Civil) 464; 2002(9) SCALE 616; 2003 (1) SCW 174; 2003 (1) SLT 5; [2002] Supp5 S.C.R. 491; 2003 (1) Supreme 433; 2003 (1) UC 270

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC - O. 7 r. 11 and s. 151 - Rejection of plaint - Application therefor - Whether should be decided on the allegations in the plaint and filing of the written statement by the contesting defendant is irrelevant and unnecessary - Held, for the purposes of deciding an application under cls (a) and (d) of r. 11 O. 7 C P C the averments in the plaint are germane; the pleas taken by the defendant in the written statement would be wholly irrelevant at that stage, therefore, a direction to file the written statement without deciding the application under O. 7 r. 11 cannot but be procedural irregularity touching the exercise of jurisdiction by the trial court - The order, therefore, suffers from non-exercising of the jurisdiction vested in the court as well as procedural irregularity - Cases remitted to the trial court for deciding the application on the basis of the averments in the plaint, after affording an opportunity of being heard to the parties in accordance with law - Appeals allowed.
