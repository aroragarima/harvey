State of Madhya Pradesh v Santosh Kumar

Supreme Court of India

14 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 324; (2006) 6 SCC 1; AIR 2006 SC 2648; 2006 CRLJ 3636; 2006 (3) JCC 1313; JT 2006 (6) SC 320; 2006 (3) RCR(Criminal) 554; 2006(7) SCALE 101; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 548

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 342, 372(2)(f), 375, 376(2)(f), 376-A, 376-B, 376-C, 376-D - Trial court found respondent guilty of offences and sentenced to undergo imprisonment for 10 years R I with a fine of Rs. 500/- With default stipulation for first offence and to undergo imprisonment of three months for second - HC reduced sentence for offence punishable u/s. 376(2)(f) to 5 years - Appeal against reduction - Held, in order to exercise discretion of reducing sentence statutory requirement is that Court has to record 'adequate and special reasons' in judgment - Reason has not only to be adequate but also special - Only reason indicated by HC is young age of accused and fact that he belongs to a Scheduled Tribe is neither adequate nor special - HC was clearly in error in reducing sentence without recording any adequate and special reason in imposing lesser sentence - Appeal allowed.
