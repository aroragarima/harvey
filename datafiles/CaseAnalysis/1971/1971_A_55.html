Â Jagannath Etc v Authorised Officer, Land Reforms and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, A.N. Ray, I.D. Dua, Subimal Chandra Roy, D.G. Palekar, S.M. Sikri, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 501; (1971) 2 SCC 893; AIR 1972 SC 425; [1972] 1 S.C.R. 1055

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution

Keywords: Competence Of The Legislature, Constitutionality, Constitution (Seventeenth Amendment) Act, 1964, Maharashtra State Agricultural Lands (Ceiling on Holdings) Act, 1961, Unconstitutionality, Constitution (Fourth Amendment) Act, 1955, Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands (Amendment) Act, 1956, Constitution (First Amendment) Act, 1951Constitution (Second Amendment) Act, 1952, Uttar Pradesh Land Acquisition (Rehabilitation Of Refugees) Act, 1948, Constitution (Fifth Amendment) Act,1955

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 13(2), 31-B and 39 - Madras Ceiling Act, 1961 - Whether Act has been validly struck down as void ab initio and whether art. 31-B could not validate it without a separate validating Act being passed by Legislature? - Held, no - The wide scope of art. 31-B which sought to cure the defect, if any, in the Acts specified in the Ninth Schedule on the ground that any such Act or any provision thereof was inconsistent with or took away or abridged any of the rights conferred by any provisions of Part III of the Constitution - The words of art. 31-B made it amply clear that this was sought to be done not only prospectively but retrospectively by the use of the words - In construing words in a constitutional enactment conferring legislative power, most liberal construction should be put upon words so that same may have effect, in their widest amplitude - It was held that having regard to above principle of construction impugned Act was covered by Entry 18 in List II of Seventh Sch and was a legislation with reference to land within competence of State Legislature - Whole object of Act is to abolish intermediaries and leave lands either with tiller or cultivator - State is paying compensation to persons dispossessed under principles prescribed by s. 12 - Amongst persons entitled to such compensation tenants are included, and when State proceeds to settle lands on tenants it expects them to pay a fair amount of price for land and put a ceiling on this price and it shall never exceed amount of compensation payable in respect of said land - Appeal dismissed.
