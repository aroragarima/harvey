Vadlakonda Lenin v State Of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

22 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 472; (2012) 12 SCC 260; JT 2012 (11) SC 412; 2012 (4) MLJ(Crl) 684; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 82; 2012(11) SCALE 237; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 1135

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 304B - Murder - Circumstantial evidence - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant/accused allegedly murdered deceased/wife due to nonfulfillment of demand of dowry - Trail Court convicted appellant for offence punishable u/s. 302 of IPC - On appeal, HC affirmed conviction passed by Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that prosecution had failed to prove the most vital circumstance of the case, namely, motive of the accused for committing the alleged crime and no charge against the accused was framed u/s. 498A of IPC in spite of specific allegations of demand of dowry and harassment etc. of the deceased by accused motive for commission of the alleged offence remain unsubstantiated - Held, principles of law governing proof of a criminal charge by circumstantial evidence would hardly require any reiteration save and except that the circumstances on which the prosecution relies should be proved beyond all reasonable doubt and such circumstances should be capable of giving rise to an inference which was inconsistent with any other hypothesis except the guilt of the accused - It was only in such an event that the conviction of the accused, on the basis of the circumstantial evidence brought by the prosecution, would be permissible in law - In instant case, from evidence of PWs-1 and 2, it was clear that accused was persistently demanded additional dowry from deceased and was ill-treated her - It was also clear that immediately before the incident there was a quarrel between accused and deceased - Further, explanation offered by accused for his absence for a period of nearly 15 days following the death of his wife was unnatural and opposed to all cannons of acceptable human conduct and behavior - Aforesaid circumstances which were proved and established by prosecution, squarely satisfied the test laid down by SC in Sharad Birdhichand Sarda v. State of Maharashtra, 1984 Indlaw SC 432 - Principles laid down in the aforesaid decision was consistently reiterated by SC and exhaustively considered in a very recent decision in Sathya Narayanan v. State Rep. by Inspector of Police 2012 Indlaw SC 391 - Prosecution had established beyond all reasonable doubt that it was the accused alone and nobody who had committed the offence - Hence, impugned order was affirmed - Appeal dismissed.
