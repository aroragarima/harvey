Assistant Commissioner of Income Tax v Thanthi Trust Etc

Supreme Court of India

31 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, N. Santosh Hegde, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 241; (2001) 2 SCC 707; AIR 2001 SC 1043; 2001 (165) CTR 681; [2001] 247 ITR 785; JT 2001 (2) SC 229; 2001(1) SCALE 522; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 727; 2001 (1) SLT 864; 2001 (1) Supreme 559; [2001] 115 TAXMAN 126

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Trusts & Associations - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 11, 148, 11(4) and 11(4A) - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 4(3)(i), 11(1)(a) and 13(1)(bb) - Issues arise - (A) Exemption - Provisions of s. 13(1)(bb) - Held, s. 13(1)(bb), will apply to a public charitable trust for relief of poor, education or medical relief that carries on a business, regardless of whether or not that business is held by trust in trust, that is, as a part of its corpus - Even a business that is held by such a trust as a part of its corpus is carried on by trust and, therefore, s. 13(1)(bb) will apply to such trust - Words used in s. 13(1)(bb) are wide enough to control not only profit from an activity carried on in course of actual carrying out of purpose of trust or institution but also income from corpus of trust property if corpus of trust includes a business - (B) Whether trust was entitled to exemption u/s. 11 in respect of newspaper business? - Held, newspaper business that is carried on by trust does not fall within s. 11(4A) - Trust is not only for public religious purposes so it does not fall within cl. (a) - It is a trust not an institution, so it does not fall within cl. (b) - It must, therefore, be held that for assessment years in question trust was not entitled to exemption contained in s. 11 in respect of income of its newspaper - (C) Whether substituted sub-s. (4A) of s. 11 was couched in wide language and a trust was entitled to benefit of s. 11 if it utilized income of its business for purposes of achieving its objects? - Held, substituted sub-s. (4A) gives a trust or institution a greater benefit than was given by s. 13(1)(bb) - If object of Parliament was to give trusts and institutions no more benefit than that given by s. 13(1)(bb), language of s. 13(1)(bb) would have been employed in substituted sub-section - As it stands, all that it requires for business income of a trust or institution to be exempt is that business should be incidental to attainment of objectives of trust or institution - A business whose income is utilized by trust or institution for purposes of achieving objectives of trust or institution is, surely, a business which is incidental to attainment of objectives of trust - In any event, if there be any ambiguity in language employed, provision must be construed in a manner that benefits assessee - Trust, therefore, is entitled to benefit of s. 11 for assessment year 1992-93 and thereafter - Order accordingly.
