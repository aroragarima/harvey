Risal Singh v State of Haryana and others

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 378; (2014) 13 SCC 244; AIR 2014 SC 2922; 2014(4) ALL MR 973; 2014 (4) AWC 4128; 2014 (3) RLW 2202; 2014(7) SCALE 473; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 544; 2014 (6) SLR 519; 2014 (3) WLN(SC) 44

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Dismissal from services

Summary: Service - Dismissal from services - Challenged - Appellant Assistant Sub-Inspector was serving in Department of Police - As alleged appellant was involved in corruption sting operation in television channel - Appellant was dismissed from services - Appellant filed petition before HC - HC upheld the order of authority - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order passed by HC was just and proper -

Held, conclusion was that order passed by Superintendent of Police dispensing with inquiry was totally unsustainable and was annulled - As foundation founders, order of HC gave stamp of approval to ultimate order without addressing lis from proper perspective was indefensible and resultantly order of dismissal passed by disciplinary authority had to pave path of extinction - Consequently appellant should be deemed to be in service till date of superannuation, as he had attained age of superannuation in meantime, he should be entitled to all consequential benefits - Arrears should be computed and paid to appellant within period of three months hence, respondents were not precluded from initiating any disciplinary proceedings, if advised in law - As lis had been pending before Court, period that had been spent in Court should be excluded for purpose of limitation for initiating disciplinary proceedings as per rules - However, Court might hasten to clarify that observations herein should not be construed as mandate to authorities to initiate proceeding against appellant - Further, to add that State Government should conduct itself as model employer and act with objectivity which was expected from it - Appeal allowed.
