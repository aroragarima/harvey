Mohan Mali and Another v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 326; (2010) 6 SCC 669; AIR 2010 SC 1790; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 2289; 2010 CRLJ 2849; 2011 (1) JabLJ 414; 2010 (4) MLJ(Crl) 657; 2010 (2) OLR 276; 2010 (159) PLR 304; 2010 (2) RCR(Criminal) 838; 2010(4) SCALE 493; [2010] 5 S.C.R. 373; 2010 (1) UC 661

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Women & Children

Keywords: Birth and Death Registration Act, 1969

Summary: Criminal - Women & Children - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 326 and 324 r/w. s. 34 - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000, s. 7A r/w. s. 64 - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Rules, 2007, r. 98 - Bail - Trial Court convicted appellants along with two other co-accused u/ss. 302/34, 326/34 and 324/34 of IPC - On appeal, HC confirmed trial court's order - Special leave petition filed against said order, when the matter was being heard for grant of leave, a plea of juvenility was made on behalf of Appellant No.2 who produced Birth Certificate issued by the Chief Registrar (Birth and Death), Municipal Corporation - Whether appellant no. 2 entitled to the benefit of s. 7A r/w. s. 64 of the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000? - Held, juvenile, appellant no. 2 had already been tried along with adults and had been convicted u/ss. 302/34, 326/34 and 324/34 of IPC and was sentenced to life imprisonment, out of which he has already undergone about 9 years of the sentence - Hence, r. 98 of the 2007 Rules, squarely applies to appellant No.2 - His case is to be considered not only for grant of bail, but also for release in terms of the said Rule, since he has completed more than the maximum period of sentence as provided u/s. 15 of the 2000 Act - Appeal allowed.
