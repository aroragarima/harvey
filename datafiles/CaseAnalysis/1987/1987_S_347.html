Dalbir Singh and Others v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

11 May 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

G.L. Oza, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28118; (1987) 3 SCC 360; (1987) SCC (Cr) 519; AIR 1987 SC 1328; 1987 CRLJ 1065; 1987 CRLR 309; JT 1987 (2) SC 580; 1987(1) SCALE 830; 1987 (2) UJ 116

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Indirect Tax; Service

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Unlawful assembly - Evidence - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 149 and 302 - Appellants killed two persons - Assault caused with weapons like kirpans and gandasi - Two policemen found deceased persons lying injured and their statements were recorded - All appellants were mentioned in statements - Whether conviction of appellants was justified? - Held, yes - Manner of incident establishes that appellants were members of an unlawful assembly and were waiting for deceased persons - Dying declaration cannot be discarded merely on ground of lack of precise description - Dying declaration was substantially corroborated by medical evidence and also corroborated by testimony of eye-witness - No hard and fast rule can be laid down as regards appreciation of evidence - It is after all a question of fact and each case has to be decided on the facts as they stand in that particular case - Appeal dismissed.
