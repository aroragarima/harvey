Commissioner of Income Tax, Madhyapradesh, Nagpur v Swadeshi Cotton and Flour Mills

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 66; AIR 1964 SC 1766; [1964] 53 ITR 134; 1964 (2) SCJ 483; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 810

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Profit Bonus

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 10(2)(x) and 10(5) - (A) Whether income tax can be charged on profit bonus? - Held, a profit bonus is strictly not wages, at least not for purpose of computing liability to income tax - It is not an expense, in ordinary sense of term, incurred for purpose of earning profits - It was only in 1949 that claim to profit bonus was settled by an award of Industrial Tribunal - Therefore, only year liability could be properly attributed to was 1949, and could be deducted in assessment year 1950-51 - As there could be no reopening of accounts so far as receipts were concerned, same would be position in respect of expenses - (B) What is meaning of word for year in question in s. 10(2)(x) of Income Tax Act? - Held, words for year in question in proviso to sub-s. 10(2)(x) do not mean for year in which allowance is claimed - Words 'for year in question' mean year in respect of which bonus is paid - Appeal dismissed.
