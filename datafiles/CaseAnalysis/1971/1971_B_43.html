Commissioner of Income Tax, Uttar Pradesh v Bharat Engineering and Construction Company

Supreme Court of India

08 September 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 385; (1972) 4 SCC 401; 1972 CTR 249; [1972] 83 ITR 187; 1972 TAXLR 447; 1972 UJ 137

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss.66(1), 66(2) - Cash credit entries - Finding of fact - Assessee-company was an engineering construction company - In their account books, there were several cash credit entries in the first year of its business - These cash credit entries totalled up to certain amount - Income-tax Officer (ITO) called upon assessee to explain those cash credit entries - Explanation given by assessee was found to be false by the ITO, the Appellate Assistant Commissioner and the Tribunal - Tribunal came to the conclusion that the cash credit entries brought to tax by the Income-tax Officer on the ground that they represented the income of the assessee-respondent from undisclosed sources was not correct - Aggrieved by the decision of the Tribunal, the Commissioner of Income-tax moved the Tribunal u/s. 66(1) of the 1922 Act, to refer certain questions to HC for its opinion - Tribunal rejected the application on the ground that its findings were findings of fact and that no question of law arose from them - Thereafter, assessee moved HC u/s. 66(2) of the 1922 Act - Application was rejected by HC - Hence, the present appeal - Whether order of Tribunal was right - Held, it was reasonable to assume that those cash credit entries were capital receipts though for one reason or other the assessee had not come out with the true story as regards the person from whom it got those amounts - It was true that in the absence of satisfactory explanation from assessee the ITO may assume that cash credit entries in its books represent income from undisclosed sources - HC after careful examination concluded that Tribunal's findings were findings of fact - Appeal dismissed.
