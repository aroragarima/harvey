Nagesh v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 171; (2012) 6 SCC 477; AIR 2012 SC 1965; 2012 CRLJ 2927; 2012 (3) JCC 1896; JT 2012 (5) SC 417; 2013 (1) KarLJ 1; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 525; 2012 (5) RCR(Criminal) 483; 2012(5) SCALE 584; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 872

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal code, 1860, ss. 201 and 302 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Father of the deceased, PW9, lodged a complaint with the police, on the basis of which the FIR was registered - Investigating Officer, upon completing the investigation, filed charge-sheet against five accused - Appellant was charged with an offence u/s. 302 of IPC while all others were stated to have committed an offence punishable u/ss. 201 and 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Trial Court acquitted all the accused for all offences except appellant who was convicted for the offence u/s. 302 of IPC - HC confirmed the judgment of the Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that this was a case of circumstantial evidence and the prosecution failed to establish the complete chain of events pointing towards the guilt of the appellant - Whether there were discrepancies in the case of the prosecution - Held, statement of the parents of the deceased, PW4 and PW9, the neighbours, PW1 and PW2 and the Investigating Officer, PW15 clearly established the case of the prosecution - Statement of these witnesses provide a complete chain as to how the deceased was brought to Belgaum and was last seen with appellant, whereafter she died and her body was cremated in the village despite protest by her parents - There was no major discrepancy or even an iota of real doubt in the case of the prosecution - Prosecution was able to establish the complete chain of events pointing undoubtedly towards the guilt of the appellant - Conviction upheld - Appeal dismissed.
