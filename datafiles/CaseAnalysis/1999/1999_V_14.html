K. S. Satyanarayana v V. R. Narayana Rao

Supreme Court of India

27 July 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 608; (1999) 6 SCC 104; AIR 1999 SC 2544; 1999 (5) ALD(SC) 7; 1999 (37) ALR 93; JT 1999 (5) SC 127; [1999] 3 S.C.R. 1213; 2000 (Supp) RD 504

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Defence & Security Forces; Tort; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Examination Of Parties, Quasi-Contract

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 10, r. 1 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 73 - Contract Act, 1872, ss. 70 and 72 - Undue enrichment - Agreement for sale of property through defendant No. 2 whom 1st defendant, owner had authorised to enter into such agreement on his behalf - Payment of money by plaintiff to both defendants, as sale consideration - Denial of his signature on such writing by defendant No. 1 - Denial of his signature by him also on written statement and Vakalatnama - Defendant No. 1 plea, there was no privity of contract between him and plaintiff - Held, doctrine of undue enrichment would squarely apply as payment was not gratuitous - It could be payment under mistake also if defendant No. 1's case was accepted - Plaintiff was entitled for restoration in any case - Dismissal of suit by Trial Court on protracted trial - Illegal.
