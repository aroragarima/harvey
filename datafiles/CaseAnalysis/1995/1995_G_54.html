The Hindustan Corporation Co. Limited v Governor Of Orissa And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

02 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, A.M. Ahmadi, Ms. Justice Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1522; (1995) 3 SCC 8; AIR 1995 SC 2189; 1995 (2) ARBLR 1; 1995 (1) CCC 593; JT 1995 (2) SC 561; 1995(2) SCALE 105; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 441

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Counter-Affidavit

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Escalation charges not paid - Challenged - Work was completed before that date - However, escalation charges were paid by the Executive Engineer of respondent in the running bills only till 31 March 1985 after which no payment in this respect was made - Matter referred to the Arbitration Tribunal - Arbitration Tribunal held that the dispute involved a claim for more than Rs. 1 crore directed the State Govt. to exercise power under proviso to s. 41A (1) of the Act - Special Tribunal with the consent of both the parties, the period for making the award was extended - Appeal against said order has been allowed by the HC and the award of the Special Tribunal has been set aside - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, Court while considering the question whether the award should be set aside, does not examine that question as an Appellate Court. While exercising the said power the Court cannot re-appreciate all the materials on the record for the purpose of recording a finding whether in the facts and circumstances of a particular case the award in question could have been made. Such award can be set aside on any of the grounds specified in s. 30 of the Act. No ground has been made out on behalf of respondent to set aside the award holding it to be invalid. Impugned order of the High Court is set aside. Appeal allowed.
