Shasidhar and others v Ashwini Uma Mathad and another

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 17; AIR 2015 SC 1139; 2015 (2) ALD(SC) 182; 2015 (109) ALR 720; 2015 (3) ALT(SC) 7; 2015 (2) AWC 1345; 2015 (1) CLT(SC) 105; JT 2015 (1) SC 119; 2015 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 213; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 616; 2015 (127) RD 576; 2015(1) SCALE 341; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 1; 2015 (3) WLN(SC) 122

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Succession Act,1925 - Shares in property - On death of deceased disputes arose between his legal representatives regarding their respective shares in properties which led to filing of civil suit by plaintiff-daughters of defendant no.1 from his first wife - Trial Court partly decreed plaintiffs' suit and accordingly passed preliminary decree in relation to suit properties - Defendants filed first appeal which was disposed of by DB of HC by modifying judgment and decree of Trial Court to detriment of defendants - Hence, instant Appeal.

Held, appellants did not make any application for settlement of dispute in relation to any of suit property in writing. There is nothing on record to show that appellants wanted to give up their claim or/and wished to settle matter in relation to some properties. HC ought to have gone into merits of claim of respective parties in its proper perspective and then recorded finding regarding extent of shares received by each coparcener/co-owner keeping in view nature of properties such as whether it was self acquired property or ancestral property and, if so, in whose hands, its source of acquisition by such person, manner of devolution on legal representatives of such person etc. These findings were required to be recorded after appreciating evidence keeping in view provisions of the Act and other related laws applicable to issues arising in case. Therefore, judgment of HC is set aside and case is remanded to HC for deciding first appeal and cross-objections afresh. Appeal allowed.
