Dr. K. A. Dhairyawan and Others v J. R. Thakur and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Syed Jaffer Imam, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 50; AIR 1958 SC 789; 1958 SCJ 1060; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 799

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bombay Rent, Hotel and lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Rent, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - On 23-05-1927, trustees of Mankeshwar Temple, executed a registered lease for 21 years in favour of respondents, whereby they demised a parcel of land - Under terms of lease lessee had to construct within six months from date of lease a double storeyed building, and cost of construction was to be not less than Rs. 10,000 - On 14-05-1948, shortly before lease was to expire, appellants who were then trustees of temple gave notice to respondents to deliver possession of demised premises and building on expiry of lease, that is to say, on 22-05-1948 - (A) Whether on a proper construction of lease, it can be held that not only land but also building to be constructed on it had been demised under it? - Held, on a reading of various clauses of lease, we are satisfied that not one of them, if properly construed, indicates that there was any contract between parties to effect that building to be erected on land would be in ownership of lessors and that same would be deemed to have been demised to lessees along with land - (B) Whether respondents could not be evicted from demised land of which they were tenants within meaning of Act? - Further held, provisions of Act do not provide for continuation of a lease beyond specified period stated therein - All that Act does is to give to person who continues to remain in possession of land, although period of lease had come to an end, status of a statutory tenant - Appellants merely seek a declaration of their right to collect rents and profits from building - There seems, therefore, to be no valid objection in law to granting relief sought by appellants - Appeal allowed.
