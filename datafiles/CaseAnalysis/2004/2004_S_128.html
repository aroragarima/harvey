Mukesh K. Tripathi v Senior Divisional Manager, Life Insurance

Supreme Court of India

06 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 718; (2004) 8 SCC 387; (2004) SCC (L&S) 1128; AIR 2004 SC 4179; 2004 ALJ 3425; 2004 (4) AWC 3088; 2004 (20) CLR 534; 2004 (103) FLR 350; JT 2004 (7) SC 232; 2004 (3) LLJ 740; 2004(7) SCALE 442; 2004 (5) SLR 808; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 127; 2004 (3) UPLBEC 2632

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Insurance; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Master And Servant Relationship

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2(s) - Apprentices Act, 1961 - Life Insurance Corporation of India (Apprentice Development Officers) Recruitment Scheme, 1980 - Workman and Apprentice - Applicant whether workman or apprentice? - Applicant must not only establish that he is not covered by the provisions of the Apprenticeship Act but must further establish that he is employed in the establishment for the purpose of doing any work contemplated in the definition - Even in a case where a period of apprenticeship is extended, a further written contract carrying out such intention need not be executed - In cases where a person is allowed to continue without extending the period of apprenticeship either expressly or by necessary implication and regular work is taken from him, he may become a workman - Onus of proof on person claiming that he is an apprentice as there are rights under the Apprentices Act also - Appeal dismissed.
