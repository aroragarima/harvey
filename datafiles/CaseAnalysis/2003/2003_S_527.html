Vidyadharan v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

14 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 998; (2004) 1 SCC 215; (2004) SCC (Cr) 260; AIR 2004 SC 536; 2003 (4) CCR 388; 2003 (4) Crimes 497; 2004 CRLJ 605; 2004 (1) JCC 133; JT 2003 (9) SC 89; 2003 (4) KHCACJ 677; 2004 (1) KLT 105; 2004 (2) MPLJ 251; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 28; 2003(9) SCALE 581; 2003 (1) SCW 6511; 2004 (1) ShimLC 160; 2003 (7) SLT 219; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 524; 2003 (8) Supreme 121

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Human Rights

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 354, 448 - Scheduled Castes And Scheduled Tribes (Prevention Of Atrocities) Act, 1989, ss. 14, 3(1)(xi) - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973 - Conviction and sentence imposed by Trial Court affirmed by HC - False implication - Legality of trial u/s. 3(1)(xi) by Sessions Judge - Held, when a plea is taken about false implication, courts have a duty to make deeper scrutiny of evidence and decide acceptability or otherwise of accusations In instant case, both Trial Court and HC have done that - There is no scope for taking a different view - Further held, inevitable conclusion is that learned Sessions Judge could not have convicted appellant for offence relatable to s.3(1)(xi) of Act in background of legal position - Further, for offence under ss 354 and 448, IPC, custodial sentence for period already undergone would meet ends of justice considering background facts and special features of case.
