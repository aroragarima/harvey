J. R. Parashar, Advocate and Others v Prasant Bhushan, Advocate and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20736; (2001) 6 SCC 735; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1242; AIR 2001 SC 3395; 2001 (4) AWC 2989; 2001 (3) CCR 221; 2001 (4) Crimes 217; 2002 (1) CRJ 265; 2001 CRLJ 4207; JT 2001 (7) SC 189; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 171; 2001(5) SCALE 524; 2001 (6) SLT 165; [2001] Supp2 S.C.R. 239; 2001 (6) Supreme 450; 2001 (2) UC 498

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Court, 1971, s. 5 - Whether holding of dharna against order of court would amount to contempt? - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 has empowered Courts to punish actions which fall within definition of civil and criminal contempt in that Act - 'Criminal Contempt' - Publication (whether by words, spoken or written, or by signs, or by visible representations, or otherwise) of any matter or doing of any other act whatsoever which -(i) scandalizes or tends to scandalize, or lowers or tends to lower authority of, any court, or (ii) prejudices, or interferes or tends to interfere with, due course of any judicial proceedings; or (iii) interferes or tends to interfere with, or obstructs or tends to obstruct, administration of justice in any other manner - Does not mean that a judgment not open to fair criticism - S. 5 of Act says that a person shall not be guilty of contempt of court for publishing any fair comment on merits of any case which has been heard and finally decided - In guise of criticizing a judgment, personal criticism of Judge is impermissible - Holding a dharna by itself may not amount to contempt - But if by holding a dharna access to courts is hindered and officers of court and members of public are not allowed free ingress and egress, or proceedings in court are otherwise disrupted, disturbed or hampered, dharna may amount to contempt because administration of justice would be obstructed - Held, no allegation in petition that participants in dharna had picketed gates of Supreme Court and prevented lawyers or litigants from entering and leaving court premises - Nor is it petitioners' case that 'dharna' disturbed or prevented courts from functioning - While holding that a dharna held to protest a decision of a court may not per se amount to contempt, must not be understood as approving holding of a 'dharna' before court - Further held, respondent No. 3 appears to have committed contempt and has imputed motives to specific courts for entertaining litigation or passing orders against her and has accused Courts of 'harassing' her as if judiciary were carrying out a personal vendetta against her - Proceedings initiated on basis of petition against all three respondents dismissed - Notice in prescribed form issued to respondent No. 3 - Order accordingly.
