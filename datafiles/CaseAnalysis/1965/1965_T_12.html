Commissioner of Income Tax, Assam v Panbari Tea Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 139; AIR 1965 SC 1871; [1965] 57 ITR 422; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 811

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 405 and 409 - (A) Whether partner's dominion over property is enough to prove criminal breach of trust u/s. 405 ?- Held, u/s. 405 in order to prove that person has committed criminal breach of trust one has to establish that person was either entrusted with or entrusted with dominion over property which he is said to have converted to his own use - Mere existence of that person's dominion over property is not enough - It has to be shown that his dominion was result of entrustment - Since this is a kind of dominion which every owner of property has over his property - So, it is necessary to establish that dominion over assets or a particular asset of partnership was by a special agreement between parties, entrusted to accused person - In contrary cases he cannot be said to be entrusted with dominion over partnership properties - Where under an agreement working partner is authorized to recover dues of partnership and to spend money for business of partnership, he cannot be said in have been guilty of criminal breach of trust even with respect to dues realized by him from certain person by not depositing them in bank as alleged by prosecution - (B) Whether a partner using partnership asset for his own use would be held guilty for dishonest misappropriation of property u/s. 403?- Further held, no - Since he is owner and an owner of property can use his property and intention do not matter, even if he is not exclusive owner thereof - A partner has undefined ownership along with other partners over all assets of partnership - If he uses them for his own purposes, he may be accountable civilly to other partners, but he does not commit any misappropriation - Appeal allowed.
