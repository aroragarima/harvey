Bharat Petroleum Corporation Limited v Rama Chandrashekhar Vaidya & Another

Supreme Court of India

02 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, Ranjana Prakash Desai

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 189; (2014) 1 SCC 657; 2013 (3) Bom.C.R. 512; 2013 (2) CTC 881; JT 2013 (8) SC 301; 2014 (4) MahLJ 69; 2013 (3) MLJ 617; 2014 (3) MPLJ 39; 2013 (3) RCR(Civil) 336; 2013 (2) RCR(Rent) 29; 2013(5) SCALE 229; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 674

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Burmah Shell (Acquisition of Undertakings in India) Act, 1976, s. 5(2) - Occupation of premises - Renewal of lease - Eviction - Legality - Predecessor of appellant came in occupation of a piece of land on basis of a registered deed of lease dt.22-9-1955 - Lease was for a period of 25 years beginning from 1-3-1955 - Lease was to come to end, appellant gave a notice to respondent/lessor invoking renewal clause in lease deed - Appellant alleged that lease after its renewal should be expiring on 28-2-2005 and appellant was desirous of continuing in occupation of premises for another period of 30 years - Respondent filed suit for eviction of appellant - Trial Court dismissed the suit preferred by respondent - Aggrieved, respondent filed an appeal before Appellate Court, which was allowed - Appellant filed a revision before HC, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, original 1955 lease (which, as a matter of fact, was only lease deed that came into existence between parties) was for a period of 25 years and was due to expire on 28-2-1980 - Appellant gave notice of renewal invoking renewal clause in lease deed - In renewal notice, there was no reference at all to any provision, much less s. 5(2) of the Act - After 28-2-1980, appellant admittedly continued in occupation of suit premises but it was undeniable that no fresh deed of lease was executed and registered renewing terms of previous lease - In case renewal was claimed in terms of stipulation in lease deed, in absence of a fresh deed of renewal, appellant's status became that of a month to month tenant and after 25 years, in that relationship it should be ludicrous for appellant to turn around and claim renewal of lease u/s. 5(2) of the Act - Though in renewal notice dt. 17-10-1979 there was no reference to s. 5(2) of the Act, renewal should be deemed to have took place under that provision because the Act had come into force on 24-1-1976 and by virtue of s. 5(2) of the Act, renewal clause of existing lease stood superseded - HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Appeal dismissed.
