Ravindra Ramchandra Waghmare v Indore Municipal Corporation and others

Supreme Court of India

29 November 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 869; (2017) 1 SCC 667; JT 2016 (11) SC 361; 2016(12) SCALE 423; [2016] 9 S.C.R. 373

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Municipal Corporation, Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, Government of India Act, 1935, Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976, Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporations Act, 1949, Punjab Town Improvement Act, 1922, Taxation on Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947, Madhya Pradesh Municipal Corporation Act, 1956, Madhya Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1961, Madhya Pradesh Nagar Tatha Gram Nivesh Act, 1973, M.P. Town Improvement Trust Act, 1960, Constitution of India, 1950, Land Acquisition Act, 1894, Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013, Land Acquisition (Madras Amendment) Act, Bhopal Vikas Adhiniyam, 1984

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013, ss. 11, 16, 8 - Madhya Pradesh Municipal Corporation Act, 1956, ss. 278, 279, 291, 292, 305, 306, 330, 387, 5(45), 5(49), 5(55), 66(1)(y), 78, 79 - Re-erection of buildings - Permissibility - State Govt. through Municipal Corporation decided to develop Bus Rapid Transit System Corridor (BRTS) - Appellants filed writ petitions before HC questioning the action initiated by Municipal Corporation on ground that such an action was impermissible under the provisions of ss. 305, 306 of the 1956 Act - Single Judge allowed petitions holding that the land be acquired under the provisions of the 2013 Act - On appeals, DB allowed appeals setting aside impugned order of Single Judge - Hence, instant appeals - Whether provisions contained in the 1956 Act were repugnant to the provisions contained in the 2013 Act.

Held, u/s. 295 of the 1956 Act Commissioner has the power to refuse erection or re-erection of buildings. Further, u/s. 299A of the 1956 Act State Govt. has the power to cancel or revise permission for construction of a building. S. 303 of the 1956 Act confers power on Commissioner to direct removal of person from a building in which works were unlawfully carried on or which were unlawfully occupied. Erection and use of temporary building was to be approved by Commissioner as provided u/s. 304 of the 1956 Act. U/s. 305 of the 1956 Act, Corporation has the power to regulate line of buildings. S. 306 of the 1956 Act deals with compensation to be paid. With respect to dangerous and insanitary buildings, Corporation has the power from ss. 309 to 316 of the 1956 Act. Thus, it was apparent that ample and widest power has been conferred under the 1956 Act on local authorities in such matters in public interest. Appeals dismissed.

(B) Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Madhya Pradesh Municipal Corporation Act, 1956, ss. 278, 279, 291, 292, 305, 306, 330, 387, 5(45), 5(49), 5(55), 66(1)(y), 78, 79 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 10A, 305, 306, 387 - Madhya Pradesh Nagar Tatha Gram Nivesh Act, 1973, ss. 10,11, 13(1), 13(3),18, 19, 19(5), 25, 40, 49, 49(ix), 50, 50(1), 50(2), 53, 56 - Developmental plan - Implementation thereof - Whether Provisions contained in the 1973 Act were also required to be taken note of along with the provisions contained in the Town Improvement Act which has a reference in s. 292 of the 1956 Act.

Held, it was apparent that the development plan once prepared was binding upon the development authorities in planning area as well as on Municipal Corporation and other local authorities as the case might be. They cannot modify and permit the user in contravention thereof. The restriction was imposed upon the owners on enjoyment of the property in violation of the development plan/regional plan, as the case might be. The town development scheme has to be executed by the authority within 3 years by acquiring the land in case it is necessary as per the provisions contained in s. 56 of the 1973 Act. It was apparent from the provisions contained in the 1973 Act the three different provisions for preparation of regional plan, development plan (master plan) and town development scheme. The regional plan was prepared by State Govt. Development plan was prepared as per the provisions contained in Chapter IV, ss. 13 to 19 of the 1973 Act and once development plan was finalized, it was binding on development authorities as well as Municipal Corporation and other local authorities functioning in planning area. Town development scheme could be framed by the development authorities and it might declare its intention to do so with the prior approval of State Govt. Appeals dismissed.

(C) Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Madhya Pradesh Municipal Corporation Act, 1956, ss. 278, 279, 291, 292, 305, 306, 330, 387, 5(45), 5(49), 5(55), 66(1)(y), 78, 79 - M.P. Town Improvement Trust Act, 1960, ss. 31, 51, 52 - Madhya Pradesh Nagar Tatha Gram Nivesh Act, 1973, ss. 10,11, 13(1), 13(3),18, 19, 19(5), 25, 40, 49, 49(ix), 50, 50(1), 50(2), 53, 56 - Town planning scheme - Sanction - Whether Municipal Corporation has any right for Town Planning Scheme of the 1956 Act.

Held, s. 291 of the 1956 Act enjoins upon Municipal Corporation to prepare town planning scheme and in case the scheme was sanctioned under the provisions of Town Improvement Act, it was provided by s. 292 of the 1956 Act that no town planning scheme should be made by Corporation. It was apparent that s. 292 of the 1956 Act refers to Town Improvement Act which was in vogue in different areas of erstwhile State which has ultimately consolidated different Acts into the 1960 Act. S. 31 of the 1960 Act dealt with types of improvement schemes such as general improvement schemes, re-building, re-housing, street, deferred street schemes and development scheme. When scheme was proposed, consideration of representation was also provided. State Govt. had the power to sanction, reject or return the improvement scheme as provided in s. 51 of the 1960 Act. In case State Govt. sanctioned the scheme it was required to be notified u/s. 52 of the 1960 Act. However, the 1960 Act has ceased to be operative in the areas, once the 1973 Act was made applicable by establishing the authorities under the provisions of ss. 38 to 40 of the 1973 Act. Therefore, the expression 'scheme' in s. 292 of the 1956 Act has to be taken to mean the regional plan, development plan and also to any scheme framed u/s. 49 of the 1973 Act. The provision was not confined to a scheme prepared u/ss. 49 and 50 of the 1973 Act of 1973 and there cannot be two schemes for the same area. Appeals dismissed.

(D) Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Madhya Pradesh Municipal Corporation Act, 1956, ss. 278, 279, 291, 292, 305, 306, 330, 387, 5(45), 5(49), 5(55), 66(1)(y), 78, 79 - Public street determination - Validity - Whether the provisions of s. 305 of the 1956 Act should be construed by SC so as to negate the power of removal with Municipal Corporation.

Held, s. 305 of the 1956 Act deals with the power of Corporation to regulate line of buildings. If any part of the building falls within the regular line of a public street either existing or as determined for the future or beyond the front of immediately adjoining building, Corporation might issue a notice either that part which was projecting or some portion of the part projecting, should be removed or that when the building was rebuilt, the portion projecting should be set back to and the portion of the land added to the street by such "setting back or removal", should henceforth be deemed to be part of public street and should vest in Corporation. SC found no ground to accept that the procedure prescribed u/s. 309 of the 1956 Act or the provisions thereof should guide the interpretation of s. 305 of the 1956 Act and for similar reason the provisions contained in ss. 310 and 313 of the 1956 Act relating to dangerous building and removal of building material from any places in certain cases which might be considered harming or breeding places for riot or other source of danger or nuisance to the occupier, then a notice should be required to be issued and on failure Commissioner was empowered to remove whereas the provisions of s. 305 of the 1956 Act cast a mandate upon Corporation to remove whatever was projecting beyond the regular line of public street. The intendment of aforesaid provisions was different render no help or guide so as to interpret the provisions of s. 305 of the 1956 Act. Thus, when all the provisions were considered, the interpretation of s. 305 of the 1956 Act was fortified that it primarily mandates Corporation to take action of removal on satisfaction of exigencies specified. Appeals dismissed.

(E) Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Madhya Pradesh Municipal Corporation Act, 1956, ss. 278, 279, 291, 292, 305, 306, 330, 387, 5(45), 5(49), 5(55), 66(1)(y), 78, 79 - Madhya Pradesh Nagar Tatha Gram Nivesh Act, 1973, ss. 10,11, 13(1), 13(3),18, 19, 19(5), 25, 40, 49, 49(ix), 50, 50(1), 50(2), 53, 56 - Vesting of property - Legal fiction - Applicability - Whether possession/deemed to be part of public street and vesting u/s. 305 of the 1956 Act.

Held, the language of s. 305 of the 1956 Act was plain, simple and clear and there was no defect in the phraseology used. The exigencies when the notice could be issued including the vesting part and deeming fiction were clear. SC do not find any deficiency in the phraseology used in s. 305 of the 1956 Act. There is no omission or lacunae, much less casus omissus as submitted, in the provisions contained in section 305 of the Act of 1956. Nonetheless the fact remains that Municipal Corporation has initiated action u/s. 305 of the 1956 Act pursuant to the permission given by HC by its interim order to enforce the provisions of Development Plan, 2005 notified in which the width of corridor was so provided. It was apparent that the power conferred u/s. 305 of the 1956 Act has to be exercised with respect to regular line of a public street, either existing or as determined for future, when hearing has already been afforded while laying down regular line u/ss. 18/19 of the 1973 Act and the power was conferred by notice to remove the building u/s. 305 of the 1956 Act which includes all the powers and steps which were necessary for removal of such building. The vesting takes place, as soon as the building was removed or notice was served for the building to be set back, land was deemed to be part of the street and should vest in Corporation. Thus, by deeming fiction vesting takes place, as such there was no necessity of specific provision for taking over of the possession that was implicit in deeming part and vesting of property by legal fiction. Appeals dismissed.

(F) Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Madhya Pradesh Municipal Corporation Act, 1956, ss. 278, 279, 291, 292, 305, 306, 330, 387, 5(45), 5(49), 5(55), 66(1)(y), 78, 79 - Madhya Pradesh Nagar Tatha Gram Nivesh Act, 1973, ss. 10,11, 13(1), 13(3),18, 19, 19(5), 25, 40, 49, 49(ix), 50, 50(1), 50(2), 53, 56 - Acquisition - Possession - Compensation determination - Whether recourse to s. 305 of the 1956 Act was made by Corporation was it necessary to make acquisition u/ss. 78 or 79 of the 1956 Act.

Held, provisions contained in section 387(5) of the 1956 Act which empower Corporation to take possession after determination of compensation by arbitration or by District Court, would be applicable only to the acquisition resorted to u/ss. 78 and 79 of the 1956 Act, particularly under the provisions of s. 79 of the 1956 Act and not to a case which was covered by the special provisions contained in s. 305 of the 1956 Act where the vesting was deemed to be by operation of law as soon as there was deemed vesting, the area should vest in Corporation and it should be deemed to be a part of public street. Thus, when recourse to s. 305 of the 1956 Act was made by Corporation, it was not necessary to make acquisition u/ss. 78 or 79 of the 1956 Act. There was no dispute with the aforesaid propositions but in instant cases the specific procedure prescribed for widening of public street to remove the projection in the regular line of public street was adopted which was a wholesome procedure. Therefore, there was no violation of law as the prescribed mode was being followed. Appeals dismissed.

(G) Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Madhya Pradesh Municipal Corporation Act, 1956, ss. 278, 279, 291, 292, 305, 306, 330, 387, 5(45), 5(49), 5(55), 66(1)(y), 78, 79 - Madhya Pradesh Nagar Tatha Gram Nivesh Act, 1973, ss. 10,11, 13(1), 13(3),18, 19, 19(5), 25, 40, 49, 49(ix), 50, 50(1), 50(2), 53, 56 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19, 19(1)(g), 21, 26(b), 30(1A), 300A, 31, 31(A), 31(B), 31(C) - Land acquisition - Arbitrariness - Violation - Whether exercising the power for acquiring land by following the procedure u/ss. 305 and 306 of the 1956 Act suffers from arbitrariness and violative of arts. 14 and 19 of Constitution.

Held, provisions contained in ss. 305, 306 and 387 of the 1956 Act cannot be said to be violative of the principles laid down by SC as the amount of compensation payable was specified and principles regarding the same have been fairly culled out. The provisions of the 1956 Act cannot be said to be violative of the principles or dictum laid down by SC rather qualify to them and cannot be said to be violative of arts. 14 and 19 of Constitution. The provisions of ss. 305 and 306 of the 1956 Act cannot be read in isolation. It has to be read with wholesome provision of s. 387 of the 1956 Act and what was contemplated u/s. 387 of the 1956 Act has to be taken to be the principle of reasonable compensation even in ss. 305 and 306 of the 1956 Act. Monetary value has to be worked out and it can be balanced with FAR in appropriate cases which is quite reasonable method of arriving at compensation. Appellants have questioned the very notice, initiation of action and when no time limit was fixed for payment of compensation, it goes without saying that it has to be awarded within a 'reasonable time'. Law envisages speedy action without unreasonable delay and that was what expected of concerned authorities, in respect of the obligation imposed on them to be discharged. Due to that, the provision cannot be struck down as arbitrary nor it could be said to be confiscatory in nature. Therefore, SC expect that the concerned Corporations would do well while offering the compensation to appellants as expeditiously as possible that was sufficient to take care of their unfounded fear. Appeals dismissed.
