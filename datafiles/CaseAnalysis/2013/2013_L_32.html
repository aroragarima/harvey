M/S Today Homes And Infrastructure v Ludhiana Improvement Trust And Anr

Supreme Court of India

10 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 305; (2014) 5 SCC 68; 2013 (6) ALD(SC) 31; 2013(4) ALL MR 442; 2013 (4) AWC 3872; 2013 (3) CompLJ 322; JT 2013 (8) SC 13; 2013 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1900; 2014 (5) MahLJ 483; 2014 (4) MPLJ 41; 2013 (3) RCR(Civil) 559; 2013(7) SCALE 327; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 589

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Validity of agreement

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 11(6) and 16 - Appointment of arbitrator - Validity of agreement - Legality - Respondent trust invited request of proposal document, for construction of City Center with intention of entering into a Joint-Venture with developers in private sector - After evaluation of bids, appellant was found to be highest bidder and a Letter of Intent was issued in its favour - Appellant deposited Rs. 3.72 crores with respondent as performance security - Tripartite Agreement dt.24-5-2005 was signed between appellant and respondent and the Bank - Thereafter, disputes arose between parties readings deposits made in Escrow Account, which referred to arbitration - Appellant filed application before HC u/s. 11(6) of the Act - HC appointed arbitrator to adjudicate upon disputes between parties - Respondent challenged the appointment of arbitrator before SC - SC set aside the order of HC and remitted matter for fresh to HC - However, matter was, taken up by designate Judge who came to a finding that agreement dt.24-5-2005 was not legal and valid and disputes between parties arose out of said agreement could not be referred to arbitrator - Application u/s. 11(6) of the 1996 Act was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - HC was only required to decide such preliminary issues such as jurisdiction to entertain application, existence of a valid arbitration agreement, whether a live claim existed or not, for purpose of appointment of an arbitrator - By impugned order, much more than what was contemplated u/s. 11(6) of the Act was sought to be decided, without any evidence adduced by parties - Legislature made it clear that while considering any objection with regard to existence or validity of arbitration agreement, arbitration clause, which formed part of contract, had to be treated as an agreement independent of other terms of contract - HC misunderstood scope of order dated 14-10-2008, passed in earlier proceedings and provisions of s. 16 of the Act in going into a detailed examination regarding merits of case and existence of an arbitration agreement and in holding that once main agreement between parties was declared void, entire contents, including any arbitration clause that might had incorporated in main agreement, were rendered invalid - HC was directed to re considered de novo in light of observations made hereinabove - Appeals disposed of.
