Supreme Court of India

07 May 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Raghubar Dayal, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 152; AIR 1964 SC 1545; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 573

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Practice & Procedure - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 81(1) (c), 82, 83(1) (c) and 90 - (A) Whether Election Tribunal can strike out name of party who is not a necessary party within meaning of s. 82 of Act? - Held, yes, it can and no question of dismissing petition u/sub-s. (3) of s. 90 arises - Although position will be different if a person who is required to be joined as a necessary party u/s. 82 is not impleaded as a party to petition - (B) Whether a defect in verification made in manner laid down in CPC, 1908 for verification of pleadings as required by cl. (c) of sub-s (1) of s. 83 is fatal to maintainability of petition?- Held, no, it does not - A defect in verification in matter of election petition is a matter which comes with in cl. (c) of sub-s. (1) of s. 83 - Such a defect does not attract sub-s. (3) of s. 90 inasmuch as that sub-section does not refer to non-compliance with provisions of s. 83 as a ground for dismissing an election petition - (C) What is meant by copy u/s. 81(3)? - Further held, meaning of word 'copy' does not mean an absolutely exact copy, but means that copy shall be so true that nobody can by any possibility misunderstand it - (D) Whether mistake of Oaths Commr. in verifying affidavit can be a sufficient ground for dismissal of petitioner's petition? - Further held, it cannot be a ground for dismissal of petition- Provisions of s. 83 are not necessarily to be complied with in order to make a petition valid and such affidavit can be allowed to be filed at a later stage also - Appeal dismissed.
