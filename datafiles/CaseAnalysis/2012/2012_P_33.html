Vasanti Bhat v Premlata A Agarwal & Anr. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

22 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 483; (2012) 13 SCC 700; 2013 (96) ALR 692; 2013 (1) Bom.C.R. 702; JT 2012 (11) SC 424; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 575; 2012(11) SCALE 261; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 1148

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Agreement of Sale - Possession Letter - Court Receiver - Injunction - Sustainability - Appellant entered into agreement for sale with respondent No. 2-Developer for purchase of Flat No. 703, and had paid substantial amount and obtained possession letter of respondent No. 2 - Respondent No. 1 had filed suit for specific performance against respondent No. 1 with regard to 4 flats 801,802 in A wing and 801,802 in B wing - Respondent No. 2 informed court that they had sold away all said flats and that only 2 flats were not sold - HC, appointed court receiver and directed respondent No. 2 not to execute, register or alienate 2 flats - HC, after coming to know through Respondent No. 1 that respondent No. 2 allowed appellant to furnish in suit flats, ordered, CR to seal suit flats - Appellant filed notice of motion for setting aside orders and SJ, was pleased to set aside orders and directed CR to return possession of flat - Aggrieved 4 appeals were filed before DB, allowed appeals of respondents and set aside order passed in notice of motion - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether DB, was justified in setting aside orders passed by SJ - Held, it was not in dispute that main suits were still pending and it was also brought to notice that because of enhancement of jurisdiction, in October, 2012, suits filed by plaintiff in original side of HC, they were being transferred to City Civil Court - Taking note of fact that main suits were pending and any decision in respect of issues raised by all parties would undoubtedly affect ultimate stand of parties and will have bearing on suits, hence, SC, decided not to analyse and arrive at a definite conclusion one way or other and disposed appeals with direction of HC - Appeals disposed of.
