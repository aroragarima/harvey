Cantonment Board, Jammu and others v Jagat Pal Singh Cheema

Supreme Court of India

09 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 228; (2012) 8 SCC 189; AIR 2012 SC 3139; 2012 (6) AWC 5969; 2012 (134) FLR 944; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 714; 2012(7) SCALE 271; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 1192; 2012 (5) SLR 481

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Service - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Cantonments Act, 1924, ss. 51, 51(1), 52, 52(1), 52(2) - Public servant - Departmental enquiry - Termination - Re-instatement - Sustainability - Respondent was employee of appellant cantonment board - On various charges a departmental enquiry was held against respondent, whereafter respondent was removed from service by an order dt. 6-9-1997 -Appeal filed against said order was dismissed - Respondent filed writ petition before HC challenging order of removal from service, on ground that report of enquiry held against respondent was not furnished to him at any stage - HC allowed writ petition on ground that order of officer commanding-in-chief, the command, affirmed order of appellant removing respondent from service was passed on basis of an invalid reference made to officer commanding in-chief, the command, under provisions of the Act - Appellant filed a LPA before HC - HC affirmed conclusions given under writ petition, though for reasons different - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by officer commanding-in-chief, the command, was justified - Held, to read provisions of s. 52(1), to cover situations where decision of Board might have reached officer commanding-in chief, the command, otherwise than by way of a valid reference, as in instant case, apart from suo moto exercise of power by said authority would effectuate legislative intent behind enactment of s. 52 - Above manner of reading power conferred by s. 52(1), would also not render provisions of s. 52(2), nugatory in as much as s. 52(2), dealt with situations where decisions of appellant had reached officer commanding-in-chief, the command, by way of a valid reference - Therefore, power to interfere with any decision of board vested in officer commanding-in-chief, the command, and provisions of s. 52, merely enumerated slightly different modes of exercise of power in different circumstances contemplated therein - Thus, conclusions reached by HC ought not to be sustained and hence set aside - Appeal allowed.
