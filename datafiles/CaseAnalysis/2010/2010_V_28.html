Sainath Mandir Trust v Vijaya And Others

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Gyan Sudha Misra, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1094; (2011) 1 SCC 623; 2011 (84) ALR 690; 2011 (2) AWC 2148; 2011 (4) Bom.C.R. 671; 2012 (2) CGLJ 422; 2011 (1) JhrCR(SC) 204; JT 2010 (13) SC 474; 2013 (1) OLR 866; 2011 (113) RD 337; 2010(13) SCALE 339

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Land & Property - Trusts & Associations - Bombay Public Trusts Act, 1950, ss. 50 and 51 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 123 - Recovery of possession of suit property - Respondent no. 8 dedicated the impugned property to the idol of Saibaba by way of a gift deed executed way back on 31.1.1974 and the possession was handed over to the appellant (Trust) - Subsequently, predecessor of the respondent nos. 1-7 purchased the impugned property from respondent no. 8 by a registered sale deed for a consideration of Rs. 17,000/- - While putting the fence around said property, predecessor of the respondent nos. 1-7 noticed a board on impugned property which was put up by the appellant stating that respondent no. 8 had given the impugned property to the appellant for construction of a residential accommodation for the devotees of Saibaba Mandir - Respondent nos. 1-7 filed suit for recovery of possession of said property and damages valued at Rs. 17,500/- - The trial Court dismissed the suit and denied the relief regarding the recovery of possession of the said property - However, the suit was decreed to the extent of damages of Rs.17,500/- to be paid to the respondent nos. 1-7 by the respondent No. 8 with future interest - On appeal, First Appellate Court reversed the judgment of the trial court - Single Judge confirmed the order of the First Appellate Court and held that s. 123 of the 1882 Act lays down the procedure in which the property can be transferred by way of a gift and it is necessary that the said document should have been registered and it should have been signed by the donor attested by two witnesses - As none of the requirements have been complied with and, therefore, the appeal against the judgment and order of the First Appellate Court was not fit to be entertained - Appellant filed appeal against said order contending that the gift deed which was executed in favour of the deity clearly reveals that the same is a 'Dedication' to an idol and not a 'living person' by the respondent no. 8 and thus the same can be said to be a valid transfer in terms of s. 123 of the 1882 Act - Whether order of the Single Judge could be upheld and transfer of impugned property by the respondent no. 8 to appellant is not valid? - Held, gift deed was an unregistered instrument and no title could pass on the basis of the same u/s. 123 of the 1882 Act - However, when the document is in the nature of a dedication of immovable property to God, the same does not require registration as it constitutes a religious trust and is exempt from registration - Moreover, in view of the possession of the impugned property dedicated in favour of the idol by virtue of a deed of gift, of which the appellant is a trustee, it was obligatory on the part of the purchasers of the property to seek permission from the Charity Commissioner u/s. 51 of the 1950 Act to recover the property by filing a suit or initiating a proceeding - Therefore, suit filed by the predecessor of respondent nos. 1-7 for possession was expressly barred in terms of the Bombay Public Trusts Act 1950 - Impugned orders of the HC set aside and order of the trial court restored with modification that as the appellant-trust is the rightful owner of the impugned property the amount be refunded to Respondent Nos.1-7 without interest by the appellant - Appeal allowed.
