Collector Singh v L.M.L. Limited, Kanpur

Supreme Court of India

11 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 764; (2015) 2 SCC 410; 2014 (6) AWC 6405; 2015 (144) FLR 447; JT 2014 (12) SC 485; 2015 (1) LLJ 86; 2015 (4) MahLJ 1; 2015 (1) OLR 225; 2014(12) SCALE 616; 2015 (2) SLR 285; 2015 (1) UPLBEC 10; 2014 (4) WLN(SC) 171

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Dismissal from services

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Dismissal from services - Justifiability - Appellant was working as semi-skilled workman with respondent-company - Appellant was guilty of misconduct and on basis of enquiry report, appellant was dismissed from services - Labour Court held that the termination of services of the appellant was justified - Petition before HC - HC dismissed appellants petition and upheld the award passed by Labour Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether punishment of dismissal from service of appellant was disproportionate.

Held, punishment of dismissal from service for misconduct proved against appellant is disproportionate to charges. Punishment was harsh and disproportionate to gravity of the charge imputed to delinquent and modified penalty to stoppage of two increments with cumulative effect. Having said that punishment of dismissal from service is harsh and disproportionate, SC in ordinary course would either order reinstatement modifying punishment or remit matter back to disciplinary authority for passing fresh order of punishment. Nearly two decades have passed since his termination and over these years appellant must have been gainfully employed elsewhere. Further, appellant was born in the year 1955 and has almost reached age of superannuation. In such circumstances, there cannot be any order of reinstatement and award of lump sum compensation would meet the ends of justice. Considering the length of service of appellant in establishment and his deprivation of the job over the years and his gainful employment over the years elsewhere, lump sum amount of compensation of Rs.5,00,000/- would meet the ends of justice in lieu of reinstatement, back wages, gratuity and in full quit of any other amount payable to the appellant. Hence, impugned order of HC passed in petition confirming award of Labour Court is set aside. Appeal allowed.
