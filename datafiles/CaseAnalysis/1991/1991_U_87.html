Gudalure M. J. Cherian and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 302; (1992) 1 SCC 397; 1992 (1) Crimes 2; 1992 CRLR 139; 1992 CrLR(SC) 139; JT 1992 (4) SC 535; 1991(2) SCALE 1305; [1991] Supp3 S.C.R. 251

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - This petition u/art. 32, in public interest, has been filed by three prominent Christians regarding alleged rape of two nuns at Gajraula in Uttar Pradesh by criminals who broke into residential quarters of a School and assaulted several other nuns before decamping with certain amount of money in cash - Whether investigation of case can be entrusted to Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) and case be transferred from file of Sessions Judge, Moradabad to Sessions Judge, Delhi? - Held, investigation have been completed by police and charge-sheet submitted to court, it is not for this Court, ordinarily, to reopen investigation specially by entrusting same to a specialised agency like CBI - Court is also conscious that of late demand for CBI investigation even in police cases is on increase - Nevertheless in a given situation, to do justice between parties and to install confidence in public mind, it may become necessary to ask CBI to investigate a crime - It only shows efficiency and independence of agency - Further Court held that affidavit of Senior Superintendent, Police shows that nuns who are victims of tragedy are not coming forward to identify culprits in an identification parade to be held by Magistrate - Petitioners on other hand, have alleged that four persons who have been set up as accused by police are not real culprits and police is asking sisters to accept four arrested persons as culprits - Further Further held, in face of these averments and keeping in view facts and circumstances of this case, Court is of view that ends of justice would be met if case is directed to CBI to hold further investigation in respect of offences committed - It is not to accept prayer of petitioners to transfer criminal case from file of IX Additional Sessions Judge, Moradabad - Petition disposed of.
