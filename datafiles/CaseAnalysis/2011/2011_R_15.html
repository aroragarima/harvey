Union of India, Through its Secretary Ministry of Defence v Rabinder Singh

Supreme Court of India

29 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 683; (2012) 12 SCC 787; AIR 2012 SC 247; 2012 (1) JCC 277; JT 2011 (11) SC 339; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 708; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 522; 2011(11) SCALE 231; [2011] 15 S.C.R. 793

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Practice & Procedure - Army Act, 1950, ss. 52, 191 - Army Rules, 1954, rr. 23, 177 - Modification of vehicle - Expenditure - Countersigned bills - Fictitious documents - Sustainability - Respondent was Commanding Officer of 6 Armoured Regiment which was new raising at relevant time in Army, unit was authorized for one signal special vehicle - Appellant case was that not single vehicle came to be modified, money was kept separately and expenditure was personally controlled by respondent, though counter-foils of cheques showed names of some vendors but amount was withdrawn by respondent himself - This led to conducting of Court of Inquiry to collect evidence and to make report u/r. 177 of the 1955 Rules and u/ss. 52(f), 191 of the 1950 Act, on conclusion of inquiry disciplinary action was directed against respondent - General Court Martialfound respondent guilty of charges and awarded punishment of 1 year RI and cashiering - Aggrieved, respondent filed petition before Single Judge, which was dismissed and appealed to DB, which was allowed and relied on S.Harnam Singh 1976 Indlaw SC 535 - DB held, allegation leveled against respondent was that he had countersigned the contingent bills for claiming cost of modifications of vehicles, but there was no charge of wrongful gain against him - Hence, instant appeal - Whether judgment of DB was sustainable - Held, appellants had charged respondents for action with 'intention to defraud' and therefore it was not necessary for appellants to refer s. 52(f) of the 1950 Act in charge and DB relied on in S. Harnam Singh v State (Delhi Admn.) 1976 Indlaw SC 535 to justify conclusions drawn by it was clearly erroneous - When any such charge was proved against a senior officer, reputation of Army also gets affected - Therefore, any officer indulging into such acts could no longer be retained in services of Army, and order passed by General Court Martial could not be faulted - Hence, Single Judge was right in passing order whereby he declined to interfere into decision rendered by General Court Martial and DB had clearly erred in exercising its appellate power when there was no occasion or reason to exercise same - Appeal allowed.
