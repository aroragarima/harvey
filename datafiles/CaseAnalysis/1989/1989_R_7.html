State of U. P. Through CBI SPE and another v R.K. Srivastava And Another.

Supreme Court of India

11 August 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, S. Ratnavel Pandian, T.K. Thommen

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 299; (1989) 4 SCC 59; (1989) SCC (Cr) 713; AIR 1989 SC 2222; 1990 ALJ 62; 1990 (1) BankCLR 263; 1990 (2) BC 148; 1989 (3) Crimes 109; 1989 CRLJ 2301; 1989 CrLR(SC) 747; JT 1989 (3) SC 347; 1989(2) SCALE 262; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 834

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Quashing Of Criminal Proceedings

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 420, 468 and 471 - Cheating - Quashing of criminal proceedings - Entitled to - Appellants filed instant appeals against the order recorded by HC - HC quashed the criminal proceedings against respondent High Court took the view that allegations made in the First Information Report (FIR) did not constitute any offence

Held, FIR was filed against respondent u/ss. 120-B, 420, 468 and 471 IPC and u/s. 5(2) r/w s. 5(1)(d) of the Act. HC has rightly held that as the criminal proceedings have been started against respondent on the basis of FIR which does not contain any definite accusation, it amounts to an abuse of process of the court and, as such, is liable to be quashed. HC quashed the proceedings only as against the respondent No. 1. Thus, when the allegations in the FIR are the same against all accused persons, the entire proceedings as against all accused persons should be quashed. Accordingly, judgment of HC is upheld, and entire criminal proceedings is quashed against all the accused. Appeals disposed of.
