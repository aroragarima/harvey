Gopal Krishnaji Ketkar v Mahomed Haji Latif and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 458; AIR 1968 SC 1413; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 862

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Practice & Procedure - Bombay Public Trusts Act, 1950, s. 18 - Disputed plot - Withholding of document - Adverse inference - Appellant made application to Deputy Charity Commissioner u/s. 18 of the Act for registration of 'Dargah' - Deputy Charity Commissioner made order declaring that Dargah was public trust - Appellant preferred appeal to Charity Commissioner against impugned order which was dismissed - District Judge held that appellant was hereditary trustee of Trust but did not give decision about ownership of disputed plot - As against appeals filed was allowed by HC who held that appellants were not owners of disputed plot - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether land in dispute was property of Dargah or it belonged to appellant - Held, entry from 'Sud' for year 1858 in connection with plot, however, there was no convincing reason was furnished on behalf of appellant to show why his name was not entered in 'Sud' - Further, appellant had furnished no documentary evidence to show how his family acquired title to land from earliest time, there was no sanad or grant produced by appellant to show that he had acquired title to disputed land - Appellant had not produced account of Dargah income - Thus, even if burden of proof did not lie on party, Court could draw adverse inference if he withheld important documents in his possession which could throw light on facts at issue - Therefore, it was not sound practice for those desiring to rely upon certain state of facts to withhold from Court best evidence which was in their possession which could throw light upon issues in controversy and to rely upon abstract doctrine of onus of proof - Hence, HC was right concluded that disputed plot belonged to Dargah and should be shown as property belonging to Public Trust - Appeal dismissed.
