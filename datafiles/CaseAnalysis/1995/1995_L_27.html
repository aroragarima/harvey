Parimal Chandra Raha & Ors. v Life Insurance Corporation Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

29 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 183; (1995) Supp2 SCC 611; (1995) SCC (L&S) 983; AIR 1995 SC 1666; 1995 (30) ATC 282; 1996 (1) CalLT(SC) 22; 1995 (2) CLR 194; JT 1995 (3) SC 288; 1995 LabIC 2064; 1995 (2) LLJ 339; 1995 (1) LLN 816; 1995(2) SCALE 518; 1995 (2) SCJ 342; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 34; 1995 (2) SLR 456; 1995 (2) UJ 505; 1995 (2) UPLBEC 846

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Factories Act, 1948 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Life Insurance Corporation Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133 - Appellants working in canteens at offices of Life Insurance Corporation - Appellants claimed minimum wages as paid by Life Insurance Corporation to its regular employees and also claimed status of regular employee - Canteen services was provided to employees of Life Insurance Corporation - Canteens were managed either through canteen Committees or Employees co-operative society or contractors - Held, where it is obligation on employer either statutory or otherwise to provide a canteen for his employees, it becomes a part of establishment and, therefore, workers employed in such canteen are employees of management - Committee, society or contractor managing canteen were only agency of Corporation - Therefore, workers employed in canteen were employees of Life Insurance Corporation - Appeal allowed.
