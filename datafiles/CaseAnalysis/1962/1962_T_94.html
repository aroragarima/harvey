Express Newspapers, Limited v Their Workers and Staff and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 August 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.K. Das, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 28; AIR 1963 SC 569; 1962 (5) FLR 205; 1962 (2) LLJ 127; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 540

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(k), 10 and 10(4) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - (A) Whether a closure comes within ambit of term industrial dispute? - Held, if action taken by employer is not a Lockout but is a closure, bona fide and genuine - It is not an industrial dispute at all - A Lockout is disguised as a closure, and a dispute is raised in respect of such an action, it would be an industrial dispute to be dealt with - (B) Whether employer can approach High Court prior to Industrial Tribunal on issue of jurisdiction of industrial tribunal? - Further held, employer is entitled to move HC even at initial stage of proceedings proposed to be taken before industrial Tribunal and seek to satisfy it that dispute is not an industrial dispute deny jurisdiction to Industrial Tribunal - Writ of certiorari can lie to Industrial Tribunal as it is in nature of quasi- Judicial proceedings - Appeal partly allowed.
