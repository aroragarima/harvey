Kattinokkula Murali Krishna v Veeramalla Koteswara Rao and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1575; (2010) 1 SCC 466; AIR 2010 SC 24; 2010 (1) ALD(SC) 40; 2010 (2) AWC 1754; JT 2009 (14) SC 458; 2009(14) SCALE 123; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 1063

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Election Agents, Ballot Paper, Contesting Candidate, Order For, Andhra Pradesh Panchayat Raj Act, 1994, Decision Of The High Court, Inspection Of Ballot Papers, Margin Of Votes, Andhra Pradesh Panchayat Raj (Conduct of Elections) Rules, 1994

Summary: Election - Andhra Pradesh Panchayat Raj Act, 1994, s. 233 - Corrupt practices - Recounting of votes - Appellant declared as the returned candidate in the election to the post of Sarpanch of Gram Panchayat - 1st respondent alleged corrupt practice in the election and sought re-counting of votes - Appellant was declared as elected after the re-counting as there was a majority of 2 votes in favour of appellant - 1st respondent filed an Election Petition before Election Tribunal - Election Tribunal ordered re-counting of the votes - Appellant filed Revision Petition before HC - HC dismissed the Revision - Legality - Held, Election Tribunal had itself observed that 1st respondent failed to state material facts regarding the alleged corrupt practice in the Election Petition - A narrow margin of 2 votes between appellant and 1st respondent does not per se give rise to a presumption that there had been an irregularity or illegality in counting of votes - No material on record available on the basis the Election Tribunal could have arrived at positive finding that a case to order re-count of ballot papers had been made out - Therefore, order of re-count passed by Election Tribunal was illegal and HC erred in upholding it - Appeal allowed.
