State Of Bihar And Ors. v Ramjee Prasad And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 April 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 869; (1990) 3 SCC 368; AIR 1990 SC 1300; 1991 (16) ATC 438; 1990 (1) CLR 781; 1990 (60) FLR 889; JT 1990 (2) SC 225; 1990 LabIC 1042; 1990 (2) LLN 1095; 1990(1) SCALE 742; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 468; 1990 (2) SLR 746; 1990 (2) UPLBEC 1075

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 136 and 226 - Appointment - Posts of teachers in Govt. Medical Colleges and Hospitals - Advertisement - Cutoff-date - Selections made on that basis - High Court holding that cut-date as specified was wrong considering dates fixed in past - (A) Validity of assumption - (B) Procedure - Production of additional facts before Supreme Court - Govt. contending that HC committed grave error in assuming to that effect - (C) Maintainability of appeal - Undertaking given by Govt. to HC that appointment would be made from panel as per directions of HC - Held, (A) Erroneous assumption of fact by HC - (B) Liable to be quashed - (C) SC permitted Govt. to place material on record to justify its stand - Appeal allowed.
