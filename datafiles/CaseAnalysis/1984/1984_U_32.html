Liberty Oil Mills and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 326; (1984) 3 SCC 465; AIR 1984 SC 1271; 1984 (2) CompLJ 124; 1984 CRLR 495; 1984 CrLR(SC) 495; 1984(1) SCALE 750; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 676

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Soap

Summary: Administrative - Imports (Control) Order, 1955, cl. 8 - Imports and Exports Control Act, 1950, ss. 3(3) and 4M - Licences kept in abeyance - Sustainability - It was also discovered that beef tallow had been allowed to be imported even by 'non-actual users' under letters of authority given by licensees who had obtained import licences against the entitlement based on the value of their exports - As result of these discoveries it was thought that drastic action was called for - So, notification was issued u/s. 3(3) of Act totally banning the import of beef, buffalo and pig tallow into India and five circulars, styled abeyance circulars' and marked 'secret' were issued by the Deputy Chief Controller of Imports and Exports, in respect of as many as 192 concerns (business houses), directing licensing authorities to keep in 'abeyance' for period of six months from the respective dates of the circulars any application received from any of them for the grant of import licence or Customs clearance Permits and allotment of imported goods through agencies like the State Trading Corporation of India Limited, Minerals and Metals Trading Corporation of India Ltd or any other similar agency - Investigation into certain allegation mentioned u/cl. 8 of Order were pending against the under mentioned concerns, all the licensing authorities were requested to keep in abeyance for six months from the date of issue of circular any application received from them for the grant of import licence of Customs Clearance Permit and allotment of imported goods through agencies like the State Trading Corporation Of India Ltd./Minerals and Metals Trading Corpn. of India Ltd. or any other similar agency - Appellant claimed that there was no justification what for making order u/cl. 8B of Order against them - Appellant accordingly sought the issue of Writ to quash the circular - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that order u/cl. 8B of Order could only be made if the Central Govt. or the Chief Controller of Imports and Exports was satisfied that the grant of licences and allotment of imported goods would not be in the public interest - Held, not only was the requisite satisfaction of the appropriate authority not recorded in the circular said to have been issued u/cl. 8B of Order but there was no material whatsoever upon which such satisfaction could have been arrived it - It was further contended that the circular order u/cl. 8B of Order as actually issued was not confined to the banned item of animal tallow or to items which could be said to have some connection with the banned item but extended to all items for which applications for the grant of licences or for allotment had been made appellant whether or not such items had the remotest connection with animal tallow - Appellant contended that the very general nature of the order disclosed total nonapplication of the mind since there was no nexus between the alleged misuse of licence for importing beef tallow or the misuse of the imported beef tallow and the abeyance of applications for grant of import licences and for allotment of other items - It was also argued that as many as 61 firms were clubbed together and dealt with by single circular and there was no indication whatsoever that the facts relating to each of the firms had been considered separately - Circular was omnibus one and revealed total non-application of the mind - It was suggested that the abeyance orders far from advancing the public interest, would, on the other hand, prejudicially affect the public interest by bringing to halt several industries and throwing hosts of workers out of employment - It would also affect exports from India and reduce foreign exchange earnings - It was submitted that there was no substance in allegation that appellant was not 'actual users' of beef tallow but they had none the less obtained letters of authorisation for import of beef tallow as if they were actual users and they had thus misused the import licences of other licencees - It was pointed out that the beef tallow imported by the under letters of authority had either been sold by them to actual users on the high seas or had been actually used by them to produce marketable beef tallow for use by soap manufacturers and fatty acid plants, by subjecting the imported beef tallow to 'air-treatment' - It was also argued by appellant that the circumstances that there was public agitation about the import of beef tallow was totally irrelevant circumstances for making order u/cl. 8B of Order - It was also contended by appellant that the satisfaction contemplated by cl. 8B of Order was not omnibus satisfaction but satisfaction which should disclose application of the mind to the facts of each individual case and each individual application for licence or allotment - One nonparty who appeared as intervener contended that secret orders affecting rights of parties could not lawfully be made since secrecy would militate against natural justice and against the right of appeal provided by s. 4M of Act - Intervener also submitted that in the absence of express recital of satisfaction which was the foundation for the exercise of the jurisdiction u/cl. 8B of Order should be held not to conform to cl. 8B of Order and therefore, vitiated - Intervener also contrasted cls. 8A and 8B of Order and argued that the public interest contemplated by cl. 8B of Order should be such as to exclude pre-decisional hearing - There was no such public interest involved in the case - There was not even recital to that effect - For that reason also the order was vitiated - Intervener further submitted that cl. 8B of Order could only be made after the investigation u/cl. 8 of Order had commenced, that was after show cause notice had been issued u/cl. 8 of Order - Held, party instead of representing his case to the appropriate authority chose the path of litigation obviously deterred by the clumsy attempt at secrecy made by the concerned authority and the failure to communicate the decision to the party - One of the submissions made to SC was that the abeyance order was never formally communicated to the petitioners and it was, therefore, to be treated as non est - In instant case, 'abeyance' order was undoubtedly communicated to the licensing authorities, the State Trading Corporation, the Minerals and Metals Trading Corporation and other similar agencies - Despite the attempt at secrecy made by the concerned authority and the failure to formally 'communicate the decision to the party, abeyance circular was very soon public knowledge - Affected party also learnt about it but probably deterred by the attempt at secrecy, chose the path of litigation, instead of representing his case to the appropriate authority - SC might have considered the question of what relief the petitioners were entitled to had the secrecy been maintained and knowledge of the order continued to be held back from the party - But the person affected did come to know of the order he filed copy of the circular along with the writ petition and in the final analysis, object of communication was only to impart knowledge - Since then, volume of water had flown under the bridge and SC proceeded on the basis that the affected party was aware of the decision - SC could not, of course, proceeded to consider the question whether there was sufficient relevant material in support of the allegations made against petitioners - In fact SC could not enter upon the merits of the controversy at all - SC could not consider the question whether the material available justified prima facie conclusion that the petitioners had made illegal imports of beef tallow - According to the contention of the petitioners, they were entitled to import beef tallow even after 5-6-1981 if they had valid licences and if they had entered into firm contracts and opened letters of credit before 5-6-1981 - According to the authorities too it was not permissible - Effect of the Import Policy was that the amendment which was made on 5-6-1981 took effect from 1-4-1981 and permitted import of beef tallow only where firm contracts had been entered into and letters of credit had been opened before 1-4-1981 but if the contracts had not been entered into and letters of credit had not been opened before 1-4-1981, imports had to be through the channel of the State Trading Corporation only - In the circumstances, SC thought that it would be proper if it directed the authority concerned to consider any representation that might be made by the party within 10 days from the date of its receipt - Appeal disposed of.
