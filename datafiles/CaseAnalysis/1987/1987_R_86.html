General Electric Company v Renusagar Power Company

Supreme Court of India

11 August 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28792; (1987) 4 SCC 137; 1987 (2) BankCLR 475; JT 1987 (3) SC 277; 1987(2) SCALE 257; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 858; 1987 (2) UJ 410

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; International

Keywords: Arbitration (Protocol And Convention) Act, 1937

Summary: Arbitration and ADR - Civil Procedure - Electricity and Energy - International - Practice and Procedure - Arbitration (Protocol And Convention) Act, 1937 - CPC, 1908 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Arbitration Act, 1940,s.34 - Foreign Awards (Recognition and Enforcement) Act, 1961,s.3 (the Act) - (A) Whether the Applications (8-C or 13-C) could be called to be a step in the suit, disentitling the Appellant from seeking a stay of the suit u/s 3 of the Act? - (B) Whether 13-C was a 'written statement', the filing of which precluded the appellant from seeking a stay? - Neither the title of the documents nor the prayer in the documents would justify calling them 'written statements' - Applications raised preliminary objections and were not answer to the Plaint - They cannot be treated as a step in the proceeding - An invitation to the court to decide the preliminary objections was a request to the court not to proceed with the trial of the suit on merits - Held, substance of the matter, ignoring technicalities, suggests that the appellant sought a stay of the suit before filing a written statement or taking any other step in the suit and he never abandoned his right to have the suit stayed - Appeals allowed.
