Security Printing and Minting Corporation of India Limited and Another v Messrs Gandhi Industrial Corporation

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1374; (2007) 13 SCC 236; 2008 (1) AWC 426; 2008 (3) Bom.C.R. 339; 2007 (217) E.L.T. 489; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 16; 2007(12) SCALE 488; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 86

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Modvat Scheme, Central Excise Rules, 1994

Summary: Arbitration - Section 11, Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Award - Modvat - Benefit - Arbitrator held that since modvat credit is only available to the manufacturer on account of book credit and the claimant was the manufacturer, the appellant cannot get that benefit - Award given by the sole arbitrator was affirmed by the Single Judge of the High Court - In appeal, it was further affirmed by the Division Bench - Claimant submitted that at the time when the tender was floated, there was no condition incorporated therein that the Modvat credit will be claimed by the appellant - Whether the appellant-security press was entitled to get the benefit of modvat credit - Held, when the terms and conditions have been reduced in the supply order, the condition of modvat credit was incorporated and it was accepted by the claimant - Claimant has to restore the modvat credit received by it to the appellant - View taken by the Arbitrator, Single Judge and the Division Bench of the High Court cannot be sustained; (2) Sub silentio - Application - There was no question of applying the principle of sub silentio when the terms and conditions were well known and clearly understood between the parties.
