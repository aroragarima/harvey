Â Kannan Devan Hills Produce Company Ltd. v The State Of Kerala And Another

Supreme Court of India

27 April 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, A.N. Ray, I.D. Dua, Hans Raj Khanna, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 374; (1972) 2 SCC 218; AIR 1972 SC 2301; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 356

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Socio-Economic; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Summary: Kannan Devan Hills (Resumption of Lands) Act, 1971, ss. 4 and 5 - Kannan Devan Hills (Resumption of Lands) Act and Tea Act, 1953 - Constitution, sch. 7, list 1, entry 52, list 2, entry 18, list 3, entry 42, arts. 31A and 254 - (A) Constitutional validity of ss. 4 and 5 of 1971 Act - (B) Repugnancy , if any - With provisions of Kannan Devan Hills (Resumption of Lands) Act and Tea Act, 1953 - (C) Constitutional validity of s. 9 of 1971 Act - Held, subject matter of provisions of ss. 4 and 5 is covered by entry 18 of list II and entry 42 of list III amd State Legislature cannot be denied the power to legislate on ground that legislation will affect subject matter of entry 52 of list I - Held, are not repugnant to the 1953 Act as does not prohibit voluntary sale or compulsory acquisition - Three purposes mentioned in section are covered by the expression 'agrarian reform' and hence the Act is protected from challenge by art. 31-A of the Constitution - Petition dismissed.
