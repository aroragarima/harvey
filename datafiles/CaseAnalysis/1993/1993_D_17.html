Home Secretary, U. T. of Chandigarh and Another v Darshjit Singh Grewal and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 July 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1234; (1993) 4 SCC 25; AIR 1993 SCW 2585; JT 1993 (4) SC 387; 1993(3) SCALE 218; 1993 (4) SLR 556; [1993] Supp1 S.C.R. 321; 1993 (2) UJ 443

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Education - Affiliated colleges - Joint Entrance Test - Qualifying marks - Migration - Punjab University Act, ss. 20(1) and (5) - (A) Whether Principal of college precluded from raising an objection at time of admission to which he had consented earlier either by doctrine of promissory estoppel or any other rule of equity? - Held, neither rule of promissory estoppel nor any other equitable rule, bars him from raising said objection at any time before actual admission of students - (B) Whether consent of principal in violation of rules is valid? - Further held, Principal could accord his consent to transfer only in terms of said Rules - Rules are emphatic and left no choice to him but to refuse his consent - Consent given by him was thus contrary to law and ultra vires his powers - Appeal allowed.
