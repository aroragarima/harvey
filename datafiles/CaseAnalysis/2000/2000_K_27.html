Nedungadi Bank Limited v K. P. Madhavankutty and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 123; (2000) SCC (L&S) 283; (2000) 2 SCC 455; AIR 2000 SC 839; 2000 (2) AWC 923; JT 2000 (1) SC 388; 2000 LabIC 703; 2000 (1) LLJ 561; 2000(1) SCALE 306; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 459; 2000 (3) SLJ 22; 2000 (1) SLR 636; 2000 (1) UPLBEC 853

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 10 - Industrial dispute - Reference - Tenability - Bank employee - Dismissal from service after enquiry - Appeal availed but unsuccessfully - Seven years thereafter - Employee complains of discrimination - Ground, two other dismissed employees were reinstated - Without any known circumstances - Held, not a industrial dispute - After such long time lapse - Reference is bad.
