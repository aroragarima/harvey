Md. Ankoos & Ors v The Public Prosecutor, High Court Of A.P

Supreme Court of India

06 November 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1493; (2010) 1 SCC 94; AIR 2010 SC 566; 2010 CRLJ 861; 2010 (1) JCC 539; 2009 (4) JhrCR(SC) 142; JT 2009 (14) SC 6; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 77; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 917; 2009(13) SCALE 584; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 616

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Power Of The Court, Distinct Offence

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 149 and 302 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 161(3) and 172 - Murder - Conviction - 77 persons tried for offence punishable u/ss. 148, 448, 307, 302, 120-B r/w s. 109, IPC - Trial Court acquitted them - On appeal, HC confirmed judgment of acquittal of 59 accused but convicted 19 persons for offence punishable u/s. 302 r/w s. 149, IPC - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether HC justified in using case diary in evidence? - Held, criminal court can use case diary in aid of any inquiry or trial but not as evidence - HC committed a serious error of law in discarding evidence of PW-20 on basis of case diary summoned in exercise of power conferred on Court u/s. 172 of CrPC - HC also erred in placing reliance upon evidence of PW-2 to PW-4 by verifying their statements recorded u/s. 161(3) of CrPC from case diary - (B) Whether HC justified in reversing judgment of acquittal and convicting appellants? - Held, appellants have been expressly charged for offence punishable u/s. 148 IPC and have been acquitted thereunder, they cannot be legally convicted for offence punishable u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 IPC - Further, view of Trial Court in passing judgment of acquittal is a possible view and cannot be said to be palpably wrong on facts or based on erroneous view of law and, therefore, High Court was not justified in interfering with judgment of acquittal - Appeal allowed.
