Ramraj @ Nanhoo @ Bihnu v State Of Chhattisgarh

Supreme Court of India

10 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2087; (2010) 1 SCC 573; AIR 2010 SC 420; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 646; 2010 CRLJ 2062; 2010 (1) JCC 522; JT 2009 (15) SC 170; 2010 (2) MLJ(Crl) 426; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 285; 2009(14) SCALE 533; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 367

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Life Convict, West Bengal Correctional Services Act, 1992

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 433A - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 and 201 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Petitioner assaulted victim with a stick causing severe internal and external injuries as a result of which victim died - Thereafter, petitioner buried the body of the deceased - On postmortem examination, it was found that cause of the death of victim was internal haemorrhage due to repture of the liver which is homicidal in nature - Trial court convicted petitioner u/s. 302 of IPC - On appeal, HC upheld the order of trial court and held that conduct of the petitioner in not reporting the matter to the petitioner and, on the other hand burying the body of the victim in an attempt to shield himself of the offence, does enure to the benefit of the petitioner - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC's order could be upheld? - Held, no reason to interfere with the judgment and order of the HC as far as conviction and sentence is concerned - As far as plea regarding premature release, it is not a fit case where the petitioner should be released on completion of 14 years' imprisonment - Petitioner is fortunate to have escaped the death penalty - Petition dismissed.
