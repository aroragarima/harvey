Â Shamrao Vishnu Parulekar v The District Magistrate, Thana(And Connected Petition)

Supreme Court of India

17 September 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, S.K. Das, P. Govinda Menon

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 30; AIR 1957 SC 23; 1957 ALJ 33; 1957 CRLJ 5; 1956 SCJ 710; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 644

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Preventive Detention Act, 1950

Summary: Criminal - Preventive Detention Act, 1950, ss. 3 and 7 - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 32 - Foreigners Act, 1946 - Habeas Corpus - Detention orders passed by magistrate - Petitioner contended that grounds furnished were vague and magistrate did not send all grounds along with report to Govt. for approval of detention - Held, grounds not vague and states clearly that disclosure of complete grounds is not in public interest - Failure on part of District Magistrate to send along with his report u/s. 3(3), very grounds which he subsequently communicated to detenu u/s. 7 is not a breach of requirements of that sub-section, and that it was sufficiently complied with when he reported materials on which he made order - Petition dismissed.
