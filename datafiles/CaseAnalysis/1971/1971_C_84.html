Commissioner of Income Tax, Assam, Tripura, Manipur and Nagaland v Rameshwari Lal Sanwarmal

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 430; (1972) 4 SCC 342; 1972 (1) CTR 300; [1971] 82 ITR 628; [1972] 1 S.C.R. 854

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 2(6A)(e), 23(4), 27, 34(3) - A/ys 1955-1956 and 1956-1957 - Fresh assessment - Ex-party assessment - Hindu undivided family - Assessee filed return for A/y 1955-1956, on behalf of his H.U.F. and Income-tax Officer passed an ex-parte assessment order on him as individual u/s. 23(4) of the Act - Aggrieved assessee filed an application u/s. 27 of the Act, to cancel ex-parte assessment and Income-tax Officer set aside order of assessment made and stated that fresh assessment would be made in due course on basis of return submitted for A/y 1955-1956 - Dept. contended that reassessment in that case was made u/s. 27 of the Act, and that contention was upheld by Appellate Assistant Commissioner as well as by Tribunal and Tribunal after stating case referred six questions to HC for opinion who had answered first two questions in favour of assessee and it did not go into other questions as it opined that in view of answers given to question no. 2, there was no need to answer remaining questions - Hence, instant appeals - Whether order passed by HC was sustainable - Held, only question that was in issue was that as shares in question stood in name of assessee could they in law be considered as shares of H.U.F - HC held for purpose of the Act, they might be considered as shares of assessee and therefore any loan granted by X to H.U.F. could not come within scope of s. 2 (6A)(e), of the Act - In arriving at that conclusion, HC differed from view taken by Mysore High Court in Kishanchand Lunidasing Bajaj v. Commissioner of Income- tax, Mysore 1963 Indlaw MYS 19, wherein that Court held that provisions of ss. 18(5), 23A and 16(2) and other provisions of the Act, relating to shares and dividends did not lead to conclusion that for purposes of assessment to income-tax dividend income derived by benami holder of shares should be treated as his own income and not that die real owner of shares which had yielded dividend income - That decision was affirmed by SC in Kishanchand Lunidasing Bajaj v. Commissioner of Income-tax, Bangalore 1966 Indlaw SC 206 - SC held that where shares acquired with funds of H.U.F. were held in name of karta, H.U.F. could be assessed to tax under the Act, on dividend from those shares - In view of that decision SC might hold that HC erred in its answer to question no. 2 - - Appeals disposed of.
