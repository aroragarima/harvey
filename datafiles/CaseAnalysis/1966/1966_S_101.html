Arnold Rodricks and Another v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 March 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 116; AIR 1966 SC 1788; 1967 MahLJ 1; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 885

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Land Acquisition (Bombay Amendment) Act, 1948

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 5-A, 6, 9 (3), 11 and 17 - Bombay Land Revenue Code, 1879,s.5 - Bombay Commissioners of Divisions Act, 1957, s. 3(4) - Essential Supplies (Temporary Powers) Act, 1946, s. 4 - Respondent issued notification for aquisition of land which was objected by appellant - Bombay Legislature amended definition of expression 'public purpose' in s. 3 of Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Act was amended, by virtue of notification issued u/s 3(4) of Bombay Commissioners of Divisions Act, 1957 - Whether s. 3(4) of Bombay Commissioners of Divisions Act, 1957 is bad, because legislature should have performed functions entrusted to State Govt.? - Held, validity of Act on ground that Legislature has not abdicated itself in favour of executive but it has laid down essential legislative policy and wisely left it to State Govt. to reorganise administration consequent on setting up of Commr. division - Appeal dismissed.
