State of Punjab v Brij Lal Palta

Supreme Court of India

26 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 490; AIR 1969 SC 355; 1969 CRLJ 645; 1969 (5) DLT 168; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 853

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 169, 173, 193 and 195 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 ss. 408, 420, 467, 471, 381, 385, 211, 193, 109, 182 and 34 - High Court quashed proceedings against respondents, hence this SLP by Govt. - Charge-sheet against respondent who himself lodged FIR against other persons - Whether prosecution of respondent possible until FIR lodged by respondent is not cancelled by Magistrate? - It is well settled by now that while investigating commission of a cognizable offence police officer is not debarred from investigating any non-cognizable offence which may arise out of same facts; He can include that non-cognizable offence in charge-sheet which he presents for a cognizable offence - Held, prosecution proper - There can be no objection therefore to continuance of proceedings relating to offences alleged against respondent other than those covered by ss. 182, 211 and 193, IPC - High Court order set aside - Appeal allowed.
