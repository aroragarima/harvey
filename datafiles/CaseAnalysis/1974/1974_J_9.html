Beliram Bhalaik v Jai Beharilal Khachi and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 December 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, A. Alagiriswami

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 317; (1975) 4 SCC 417; AIR 1975 SC 283; 1975 UJ 31

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Representation of the People (Conduct of Elections) Rules, 1961, rr. 63(2) and 93 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 100(1)(d)(iii), 101 and 102 - Elections - Recount of votes - Application for - (A) 'Grounds' - Application simply stating that the candidate is not satisfied with the counting - No mention of any irregularity or illegality - (B) Application for recounting - Not supplemented by an antecedent or contemporaneous oral statement of the author or any of his agents with regard to any irregularities in counting - (C) Recount of votes - Application for recounting presented about half an hour after the Returning officer had completed and signed the result sheet in form 20 - (D) Secrecy of ballot - Recount or production of ballot papers - When to be ordered - Held, application does not comply with mandatory requirement of r. 63 (2) - It is liable to be rejected summarily u/r. 63(3) - Application is liable to be rejected as belated - Held, only where all the material facts on which the allegations of irregularity or illegality in counting are founded, are pleaded adequately in petition, and the court is prima facie satisfied that such an order is imperatively necessary to decide dispute and justice - Appeal Dismissed.
