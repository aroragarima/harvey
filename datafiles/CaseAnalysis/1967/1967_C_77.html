Sundaram and Company Private Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 317; AIR 1968 SC 124; [1967] 66 ITR 604; 1968 (1) MLJ(SC) 20; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 798

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Assessment - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - S. 23A - Finance Act, 1956 - S. 2 - Appellate Tribunal Rules and Income Tax (Amendment) Act, 1853 - Rule 27 - Income Tax officer served notice on the assessee company under section 23A of the assessee company being issued notice under Section 23A Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 for distribution of income of company amongst shareholders - the order was complied with by the Assessee - Assessee company was not allowed benefit of rebate of Super Tax under section 34(1)(b) - Commissioner and Tribunal in appeal held the order of re-assessment was justified - whether the order of re-assessment was justified - Held, Yes - Super-tax and rebate are part of total Income and rebate is to be determined on amount of dividend - Re-assessment was justified as the income of assesse was assessed at a lower rate - Assesse was granted excessive relief - Matter remanded back to High Court to determine validity of proceeding - Disposed accordingly.
