Ayaaubkhan Noorkhan Pathan v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

08 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, J. S. KHEHAR

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 420; (2013) 4 SCC 465; AIR 2013 SC 58; 2013 (3) Bom.C.R. 113; 2013 (136) FLR 574; JT 2012 (11) SC 95; 2013 (4) MahLJ 561; 2012 (8) MLJ(SC) 679; 2012(11) SCALE 39; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 994; 2012 (6) SLR 587

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Maharashtra Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, De-notified Tribes, (Vimukta Jatis), Nomadic Tribes, Other Backward Classes and Special Backward Category Regulation of Issuance and Verification of Caste Certificate Act, 2000, Public Premises ( Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Act, 1971

Summary: Administrative - Service - Practice & Procedure - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - Scrutiny Committee - Caste Certificate - Validity - Maintainability - Appellant was appointed as Senior Clerk in Municipal Corporation, against vacancy reserved for persons under Scheduled Tribes category on basis of caste certificate issued by competent authority - Corporation scrutinised caste certificate through Caste Certificate Scrutiny Committee - Respondent No. 5, after 9 years filed complaint on validity of certificate alleging that appellant obtained certificate by way of misrepresentation - Scrutiny Committee rejected application holding that it does not have power to recall or review a caste validity certificate - Respondent No. 5 challenged order before HC, by filing Writ Petition, which was disposed of remitting matter to Scrutiny Committee directing to hear parties in accordance with law - Aggrieved instant appeal - Appellant contended that respondent no.5 does not belong to any reserved category, but belongs to General category, hence, he has no right or locus standi, to challenge appellant's certificate - Whether HC, was justified in remanding matter to Committee to hear parties in accordance with law - Held, Scrutiny Committee in ordinary circumstances examined matter and after investigation through its Vigilance Cell and considering all documentary evidence on record and after being satisfied, granted caste verification certificate in 2000, s. 114 Ill.(e) of 1972, Act provided for court to pronounce that decision taken by Scrutiny Committee had been done in regular course and caste certificate had been issued after due verification - Respondent no. 5 had no legal peg for a justifiable claim to hang upon, once respondent no. 5, had challenged caste certificate under garb of acting as a public spirited person espousing cause of legitimate persons who had been deprived of their right of being considered for appointment, respondent no. 5 must have acted seriously and brought material before Scrutiny Committee to show that earlier decision was improbable or factually incorrect - Scrutiny Committee had already conducted an inquiry in relation to this matter, and only grievance of appellant was that there had been non-compliance with principles of natural justice, and fact that applications filed by him, were not decided upon, SC, directed that before submission of any report by Scrutiny Committee, his application for calling witnesses for cross-examination must be disposed of, and appellant must be given a fair opportunity to cross-examine witnesses, who have been examined before Committee and Committee shall pass appropriate order in accordance with law - Appeal disposed of.
