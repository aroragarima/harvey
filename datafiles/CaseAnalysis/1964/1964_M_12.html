Mahant Narayana Dasjee Varu and Others v The Board of Trustees Tirumalai Tirupathi, Devasthanam and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 September 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, A.K. Sarkar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 455; AIR 1965 SC 1231

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: (A) Trust & Associations - Civil Procedure - Tirumalai Tirupathi Devasthanam Act, 1933, s. 45(2) - Petition for recovery of possession - Claim for Mesne profits - Allowed - Validity - Plaintiff filed petition u/s. 45(2) of the Act to District Court for direction that defendant should be directed to put them in possession of property to which District Judge referred plaintiff to regular suit - Plaintiff filed two suits to recover possession of property - Subordinate Judge decreed two suits and granted to plaintiff possession and directed enquiry into and decree for mesne profits payable by defendant - Defendant/appellant made appeal which was dismissed - Defendant/Appellant made further appeal to HC which substantially reduced amount of mesne profits and also reduced rate of interest allowed by Subordinate Judge - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether there were any circumstances to reduce rate of interest and give interest at rate less than six per cent - Held, as record showed Matathipathi was managing trust properties along with his own properties for about century and properties of both institutions were mixed up and jointly managed - However, it was not case of suit for mesne profits Against trespasser but one against previous trustee who was managing property in same way in which his predecessor in interest was managing for century - Thus, decree for mesne profits was also for large amount - Further, for interest of justice, defendant was directed to pay simple interest at rate of four per cent per annum on profits from date profits accrued each year till date of the decree and on aggregate amount at three per cent per annum from date of decree till date of payment - Appeal allowed.

(B) Trust & Associations - Dispute relating to position of trustee - Whether appellant was trustee who had been removed from office and therefore stood in position different from that of trespasser - title of plaintiff to property was disputed and suits had to be filed for recovery of their possession, and litigation conducted which took 12 years to finish - However, if during that period appellant appropriated to himself income from property, there was no justification for his not being made liable for interest at normal rate - Thus, in any event, if trial court in its discretion awarded interest at 6 per cent, and that was admittedly not per se unreasonable rate, there was no compelling equity in appellant to justify interference with that discretion - Therefore, HC was not justified in reducing rate of interest from 6 per cent to 4 per cent on aggregate sum thereafter and method suggested by Judges for calculation of interest on mesne profits each year was correct and interest at 6 per cent p.a. would have to be calculated on that basis before and after decree - Appeal allowed.
