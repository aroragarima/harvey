Haryana Seeds Developments Corporation Limited v Sadhu and another

Supreme Court of India

18 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 146; (2005) 3 SCC 198; AIR 2005 SC 2023; 2005 (2) AWC 1121; 2005 (3) CalLT(SC) 1; 2005 (11) CCC 335; 2005 (4) CompLJ 1; 2005 (2) CPJ 13; 2005 (2) CPJ(Sc) 13; 2006 (1) JhrCR(SC) 274; JT 2005 (2) SC 592; 2006 (2) MLJ(SC) 368; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 138; 2005(2) SCALE 270; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 181

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Summary: Consumer Protection Act, 1986, ss. 13(1)(c) and 27 - Complaint filed against appellant alleging that he had sown seeds purchased and germination of seeds was not up to standard but was 'very much poor' - Seeds were of sub-standard quality - Claim of complainant was accepted and appellant was held liable to pay compensation - Challenged - Held, from record it is abundantly clear that appellant had constituted an Expert Committee - Reading report in its entirety, it is clear that Expert Committee was satisfied that variation in condition of crop was not and could not be attributed to quality of seeds but to other factors - Hence, contention that word 'not' was inserted either to favour Corporation or to cause prejudice to farmers cannot be accepted - National Commission was not right in observing that at most, report could be said to be ambiguous - Appeal allowed.
