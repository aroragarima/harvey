Agra Electric Supply Company Limited v Alladin and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 August 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Vishishtha Bhargava, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 28; (1969) 2 SCC 598; AIR 1970 SC 512; 1969 (19) FLR 293; 1970 LabIC 411; 1969 (2) LLJ 540; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 808

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946, ss. 3 and 5 - Agra Electric Supply Co. Ltd., Standing Orders, O. 32, 2(c) and 14 - U.P. Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 -Constitution, art. 136 - CPC, 1908, s. 11 - Service - Standing Orders - (A) Object of Act is to have uniform standing orders - Standing Orders after certification - Binding upon, whom - (B) Appeal by special leave u/art. 136 - Standing Orders, O.2(c) and O.14 - Termination of service of probationer - It can be only for misconduct - Labour Court finding that particular termination was punitive and not simple termination of service - (C) Civil Procedure - Provision of s. 11of CPC, 1908 - Award of Labour Court holding that Standing Orders of a Company were not applicable to persons employed prior to certification of Standing Orders - Decision based on Supreme Court Judgment - Becoming final as special leave to appeal was refused - Subsequent decision of SC holding that Standing Orders were applicable after certification to employees who were employed prior to such certification - Decision of Labour Court , if act as res judicata -Held, Standing Orders after certification bind all employees presently employed as well those employed thereafter - Decision will not be interfered with - Decision of Labour Court does not act as res judicata - Appeal Partly Allowed.
