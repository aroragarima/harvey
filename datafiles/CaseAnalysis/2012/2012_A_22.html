State Of N.C.T. Of Delhi v Ajay Kumar Tyagi

Supreme Court of India

31 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, R.M. Lodha, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 276; (2012) 9 SCC 685; 2012 CRLJ 4489; 2012 (192) DLT 478; JT 2012 (8) SC 190; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 297; 2012(8) SCALE 424; [2012] 8 S.C.R. 208; 2012 (5) SLR 645

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 3 and 7 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Corruption - Quashing of criminal proceedings - Exonerated in disciplinary proceeding - Challenged - It was alleged that the appellant being a public servant in discharge of his official duties by corrupt and illegal means or otherwise, abusing his official position, demanded, accepted and obtained Rs. 1000/- as illegal gratification from 'X' in consideration for giving a report on the water connection - The Enquiry officer conducted the departmental inquiry and submitted its report that the charge against the appellant has not been proved due to lack of evidence on record - As no action was taken on the report of the Enquiry officer due to pendency of the criminal case pending against the appellant, appellant filed writ petition before HC praying for conclusion of the departmental proceedings - HC dismissed said writ petition on the ground that the action of the respondents in keeping the departmental proceedings in abeyance to be in any manner was not unjustified specially when the appellant inspite of the pendency of the criminal case against him has not been suspended from service and was continuing to perform his duties - Thereafter, appellant resorted to another remedy u/s. 482 of the CrPC and prayed for quashing of the FIR lodged against him u/s. 7/13 of the Act on the ground that since the appellant has been exonerated in the disciplinary proceeding by a detailed speaking order, the FIR deserves to be quashed on that ground alone - HC allowed the said petition and quashed the criminal proceedings - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld - Held, it was well settled that the standard of proof in department proceeding was lower than that of criminal prosecution - It was equally well settled that the departmental proceeding or for that matter criminal cases have to be decided only on the basis of evidence adduced therein - Truthfulness of the evidence in the criminal case can be judged only after the evidence was adduced therein and the criminal case cannot be rejected on the basis of the evidence in the departmental proceeding or the report of the Enquiry Officer based on those evidence - Therefore, the exoneration in the departmental proceeding ipso facto would not result into the quashing of the criminal prosecution - However, if the prosecution against an appellant was solely based on a finding in a proceeding and that finding was set aside by the superior authority in the hierarchy, the very foundation goes and the prosecution may be quashed - But that principle will not apply in the case of the departmental proceeding as the criminal trial and the departmental proceeding were held by 2 different entities - Further they were not in the same hierarchy - Hence, impugned order of the HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
