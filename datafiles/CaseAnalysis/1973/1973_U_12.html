Dilbagh Rai Jarry v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 November 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 40; (1974) SCC (L&S) 89; (1974) 3 SCC 554; AIR 1974 SC 130; 1973 (27) FLR 428; 1974 LabIC 149; 1974 (1) LLJ 164; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 178; 1974 SLJ 91; 1974 (1) SLR 1; 1974 UJ 1

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 227 and 102 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 509 - Payment of Wages Act, 1936, ss. 15(2), 7(2)(h), 15(1), 15(3), 3, 15, 7, 13 and 7(2)(b) - Railway Establishment Code, r. 2044 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. VI, rr. 17 and 18 - (A) Claim application u/s. 15(2) - Limitation of - Held, expression 'accrue/due' in art. 102 of Limitation Act, 1908, which does not govern applications u/s. 15(2) of Act - Necessity of examining comparative meaning and distinction between 'deduction from wages' or 'delay in payment of wages due' and two alternative starting points of limitation relatable to these expressions, did not arise in case - (B) Traveling allowance - Entitlement of - Held, running allowance was due to appellant as part of his wages for entire period of his inactive service - Traveling allowance or running allowance is eligible if officer has traveled or not - Appeal allowed.
