Munnawar and others v State of Uttar Pradesh etc.

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 337; (2010) 5 SCC 451; 2010 (6) ADJ 200; 2010 (3) JhrCR(SC) 81; JT 2010 (4) SC 505; 2010 (3) RCR(Criminal) 419; 2010(5) SCALE 30; [2010] 5 S.C.R. 1202; 2010 (2) UC 725

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Promptness

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 149, 302 and 307 - Murder - Conviction - Dying declaration - Challenged - Trial court convicted the appellant for an offence u/s. 307 of IPC but acquitted them of the offence u/s. 302 of IPC - Two appeals were filed - HC by the impugned judgment dismissed the appeal filed by the appellants but allowed the State appeal and convicted the appellants for the offence punishable u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 of IPC - Hence, present appeal - (A) Dying declaration - Two dying declarations one recorded by Police office and another by Magistrate - Held, relied on Balak Ram vs. State of U.P. 1974 INDLAW SC 121 wherein SC dealt with two dying declarations, one recorded by the investigating officer in the case diary which was held to be unreliable and the other by the Executive Magistrate which was held to be reliable notwithstanding the fact that the injured, when taken to the hospital, was in a very critical condition - In the present case, first statement had not been recorded in the manner provided by the Police Regulations with regard to the recording of dying declarations by Police Officers - However, there was no suspicion with regard to the second dying declaration recorded by the Executive Magistrate which gave full details as to the identity of the assailants, the weapons they were using, the site of the injury - Doctor also gave a certificate that deceased was fully conscious and lucid at the time of its recording - (B) Presence of eye-witnesses - Held, promptness of the FIR was a clear reflection of the fact that the two eye witnesses had been present at the time of incident - Presence of the two eye witnesses accepted - (C) Conviction - Held, as per medical evidence injuries were grievous and were fatal to life and all the injuries were sufficient to cause death as they were on sensitive parts of the body and that the injured was under severe shock - Conviction u/s. 302 of IPC upheld - Appeals dismissed.
