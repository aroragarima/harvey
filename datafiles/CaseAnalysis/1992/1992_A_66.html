Ashok Kumar Sharma and Another v Chander Shekher and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

T.K. Thommen, V. Ramaswami, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1278; (1993) Supp2 SCC 611; (1993) SCC (L&S) 857; 1993 (24) ATC 798; 1993 (2) CLR 688; JT 1992 (Supp) SC 684; 1993 (1) SLR 379; [1992] Supp3 S.C.R. 768

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Public Service Commission Business Rules, r. 37 - Whether or not candidates who were fully qualified to be appointed as Junior Engineers on dates of interview, but whose results had not been declared on dates of submission of their applications, were entitled to be considered for appointment to post of Junior Engineer ? - Held, if rule making authority goes beyond power conferred on it rule is rendered invalid - A rule framed under one Statute, therefore, cannot be invoked for carrying out objective of another enactment - R. 37 is in terms applicable only to Public Service Commission candidates and due notice of provisional entertainment of their application, subject to their passing examination before date of interview, is a requirement peculiar to r. 37 and is not applicable to present case - By allowing appellants to sit for interview and by their selection on basis of their comparative merits, recruiting authority was able to get best talents available - It was in public interest that better candidates who were fully qualified on dates of selection were not rejected, notwithstanding that results of examination in which they had appeared had been delayed for no fault of theirs - Appeal allowed.
