Pushpa @ Leela and others v Shakuntala and others

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 26; (2011) 2 SCC 240; 2011 (1) ACC 342; 2011 ACJ 705; AIR 2011 SC 682; 2011 (3) ALD(SC) 112; 2011 (85) ALR 206; 2011 (2) AWC 1094; 2011 (2) CPJ(Sc) 9; 2011 (2) CTC 693; JT 2011 (1) SC 117; 2011 (3) MLJ(SC) 219; 2011(2) M.P.H.T. 1; 2011 (162) PLR 472; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 616; 2011 (1) RLW 401; 2011(1) SCALE 303; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 334

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicle Act, 1988, ss. 2(30) and 157 - Liability to pay compensation amount - 'X', Deceased had hired the truck for carrying some materials and food articles for a wedding in the family - He got the materials to be transported loaded on the truck by a labourer, 'Y' whom he had engaged for that purpose and took him along with him on the truck for unloading the consignment at the destination - 'Z' driver of the truck rashly driving the truck, as a result, the truck met with an accident leading to the death of all the 3 persons - Impugned truck was earlier belonged to 'A' who sold the truck to 'B' - However, despite the sale of the vehicle by 'A' to 'B', the change of ownership of the vehicle was not entered in its certificate of registration - 'B' has taken a insurance policy for the truck from 'C' in the name of the 'A' - Tribunal held that the heirs and legal representatives of 'X' were entitled to a sum of Rs. 5,16,000/- as a compensation and heirs and legal representatives of the 'Y' were entitled to a sum of Rs.2,42,000/- as a compensation - Tribunal also held that no liability for payment of compensation to the claimants would attach to 'A' since he had ceased to be the owner of the vehicle after its sale to 'B' and 'B' was liable for payment of the compensation amount to the two claimants i.e. 'X' and 'Y' - HC dismissed the appeal filed said order of Tribunal - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the liability to pay the compensation amount as determined by the Tribunal was of the purchaser of the vehicle alone i.e. 'B' or whether the liability of the recorded owner of the vehicle i.e. 'A' was coextensive and from the recorded owner it would pass on to the insurer of the vehicle i.e. 'C'? - Held, it is undeniable that notwithstanding the sale of the vehicle neither the transferor 'A' nor the transferee 'B' took any step for the change of the name of the owner in the certificate of registration of the vehicle - In view of this omission 'A' must be deemed to continue as the owner of the vehicle for the purposes of the Act - Hence, 'A' as the owner of the truck was equally liable for payment of the compensation amount - Further, since an insurance policy in respect of the truck was taken out in his name he was indemnified and the claim will be shifted to the insurer, 'C' - Hence, 'C', the insurance company is directed to make full payment of the compensation amount as determined by the Tribunal to the appellants - Appeal allowed.
