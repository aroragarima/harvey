V. C. K. Bus Service Limited v Regional Transport Authority, Coimbatore

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 156; AIR 1957 SC 489; 1957 ALJ 746; 1957 (2) MLJ 43; 1957 SCJ 409; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 663

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Writ Proceedings

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Prohibition Act - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 58(1) - (A) When a permit is renewed, is it a continuation of original permit, or is it, in fact, a new one? - Held, s. 58(1) enacts that a permit shall be effective for period specified therein, but this is qualified by words 'without renewal' - Therefore, when there is a renewal, effective period is not original period specified, but period up to which renewal is granted - That indicates that life of a renewed permit is one and continuous - R. 184(1) provides that when a renewal is granted, it shall be endorsed on the permit itself - Thus, what is renewed is 'this permit' - A reading of relevant provisions of Act and of rules leads indubitably to a conclusion that a renewal is a continuation of permit previously granted - (B) Is grant of a renewed permit subject to implied condition that it is liable to be cancelled, if original permit is cancelled? - Further held, there is no legal obstacle in implying a condition that renewal should stand cancelled if right of appellant to original permit was negatived by High Court - As all papers relating to grant of original permit and subsequent proceedings were part of record before Regional Transport Authority when he renewed permit on 23-06-1955, it is impossible to resist the conclusion that he really intended to renew permit only subject to decision of HC - It was an implied condition of renewal that it was to be subjected to decision of HC in that appeal, and that in event which had happened, it had ceased to be effective - Appeals dismissed.
