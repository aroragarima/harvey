Chairman, Grid Corporation On Orissa Limited (Gridco) and Others v Sukamani Das Smt. and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 709; (1999) 7 SCC 298; AIR 1999 SC 3412; AIR 1999 SCW 3383; JT 1999 (7) SC 109; 1999(5) SCALE 539; [1999] Supp2 S.C.R. 458

Case Digest

Subject: Tort

Keywords: Act Of God

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Writ jurisdiction - Torts - Negligence - Claim of compensation for death due to electrocution by touching snapped gridwire - Petition filed by wife of deceased - Wife averred that there was negligence on part of appellant and its officers in maintaining transmission line - (A) whether that admittedly/prima facie amounted to negligence on part of appellants? - Factum of death denied by Grid Corporation - Also defence taken that line snapped because of an act of God and not because of any negligence - (B) whether compensation of Rs. 1 lakh awarded by High Court without deciding disputed questions of facts proper - Held, (A)HC committed an error in entertaining writ petitions - (B) Where disputed questions of facts are involved a petition u/art. 226 is not a proper remedy - All these cases were actions in tort and negligence was required to be established firstly by claimants - Appeals dismissed.
