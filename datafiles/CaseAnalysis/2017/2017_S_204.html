Srinivasaiah v H. R. Channabasappa (Since Dead) by his LRs and others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Abhay Manohar Sapre, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 323; AIR 2017 SC 2141; 2017 (4) ALD(SC) 111; 2017 (123) ALR 839; 2017 (4) Bom.C.R. 1; 2017 (2) CLT(SC) 449; 2017 (4) CTC 678; JT 2017 (4) SC 513; 2017 (4) MLJ 484; 2017 (3) RCR(Civil) 118; 2017(5) SCALE 306

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Conditional Sale, Contract, Limitation Act, 1963, Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, Limitation, Indian Contract Act, 1872, Redemption, Review, Executing Court, Mortgage By Conditional Sale, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 58(c) - Suit for injunction - Decreed - Validity - Respondent/plaintiff filed suit against appellant/defendant no.1 seeking for a declaration that sale made by defendant no.1 in favour of defendant no. 2 was not binding and for recovery of possession of suit land - Trial Court decreed suit holding that plaintiff was entitled to restoration of suit land from defendants - On appeal, Appellate Court allowed appeal and set aside judgment of Trial Court - On further appeal, HC allowed appeal and restored judgment of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeals - Whether sale was "mortgage by conditional sale" or a "sale out and out with a condition to repurchase.

Held, it satisfies the third condition of s. 58(c) of the Act "on condition that such payment being made, the buyer should transfer the property to seller". It also satisfies the tests laid down by SC in Pandit Chunchun Jha v. Sheikh Ebadat Ali and Another, 1954 Indlaw SC 145, first, the transaction was concluded in one document, second, the document styled as a "Deed of Conditional Sale" itself contains the condition of repurchase on offering the sale money without interest for the reason that defendant no.1 was allowed to use the land till the money is not paid back to him by seller/plaintiff and third, parties' intention as per terms was supported by the evidence which was accepted by Courts below. HC rightly took note of the law laid down in case of Chunchun Jha, 1954 Indlaw SC 145, and the requirements of s. 58(c) of the Act came to a right conclusion. Thus, reasoning and conclusion arrived by Trial Court and HC while holding that "mortgage deed by conditional sale" as defined u/s. 58(c) of the Act was just and proper and it deserves to be upheld by SC. Appeals disposed of.

(B) Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 61(a) - Right to redeem - Justifiability - Whether HC was justified in holding that suit was filed within limitation.

Held, instant case would be governed by art. 61(a) of the Act which provides a limitation of 30 years when the right to redeem or to recover possession accrues to mortgagor. Sale deed was of dt. 28-07-1969. In terms of the conditions, 5 years expired on 27-07-1974. Plaintiff filed suit on 19-09-1987 and was filed within 30 years. Plaintiff should deposit a sum of Rs.1500/- in executing Court for being paid to the defendant within 3 months as an outer limit. Defendant no.1 should deposit in executing Court a sum of Rs.30,000/- for being paid to appellant within 3 months. Defendant no.1 and appellant would jointly execute the sale deed in plaintiffs' favour and hand over the possession of suit land to plaintiffs simultaneously and then would withdraw the money deposited for them in Court. The executing Court would ensure completion of proceedings within the time fixed and would record due satisfaction of decree in accordance with law. Appeals disposed of.
