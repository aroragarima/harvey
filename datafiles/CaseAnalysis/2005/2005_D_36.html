FOR EDUCATIONAL USE ONLY

Debashis Roy and another v Calcutta Municipal Corporation and others

Supreme Court of India

25 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, H.K. Sema, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1614; (2005) 12 SCC 317

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Calcutta Municipal Corporation Act, 1980, s. 416 - Change of use of land - Validity - Building plans were initially sanctioned by the Corporation, a substantial area on the ground floor was shown as parking space - Most of the flat-owners have purchased and paid for individual parking space - Nevertheless, the disputed portion of the ground floor was shown in the plan as parking space - Building owner was alleged to have entered into an agreement for sale of little less than 2000 sq ft area of the ground floor in favour of respondent No. 6 - It was also not in dispute that till date there was no deed of sale executed by the owner in favour of respondent No. 6 - Respondent No. 6 moved an application to Corporation seeking change of use u/s. 416 of the Act - Demand for charges for converting the change of use was raised on 7-3-2001 and complied with on the same date by depositing Rs 92,100/- - Revised plan was ready for release on 13-3-2001/14-3-2001 and was actually released on 14-3-2001 - Flat-owners had filed a writ petition in HC seeking restraint on change of use of the open space, designated as parking space and sought for intervention of the HC - Single Judge of the HC disposed of the writ petition by dismissing the same on 3-12-2002 - Principal reason which prevailed with the Single Judge was the availability of alternate remedy to the writ petitioners of approaching the Municipal Corporation and/or filing a civil suit - Aggrieved writ petitioners filed an intra-court appeal before DB which dismissed the appeal summarily by an unreasoned order forming an opinion that no case was made out for interference with the order of the Single Judge - Hence instant appeal - Whether DB was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, writ petition filed in the HC was not confined to the raising of a dispute between private parties - There was essentially an element of public interest involved as serious questions alleging violations of building laws and town planning were raised - Whether the power under Section 416 was available to be exercised and if so, whether it was rightly exercised by the local authority, was another question - They called for a serious consideration on the part of the HC - If at all a case of illegal change of use and violation of building laws or town planning was made out, then it was a clear case of calling for an order of demolition of unauthorised construction - Petitioners was at pains in submitting that the change of use and the constructions were done during the pendency of the writ petition and also in defiance of the interim orders passed by the HC which contention was not received the consideration of the HC - Impugned orders passed by Single Judge as well as by DB were set aside - Case was remanded to the HC for hearing afresh and decision in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
