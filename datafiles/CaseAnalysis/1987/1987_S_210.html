Indian Metals and Ferro Alloys Limitedand Another v State Of Orissa And Ors. Etc. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Balakrishna Eradi, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28755; (1987) 3 SCC 189; AIR 1987 SC 1727; 1987 (2) BankCLR 648; 1987 (3) CompLJ 90; JT 1987 (2) SC 362; 1987(1) SCALE 1213; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 265; 1987 (2) UJ 297

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Trade

Keywords: Club, Electricity Act, 1910, Orissa State Electricity Board (General Conduction of Supply) Regulation 1981

Summary: Electricity and Energy - Orissa State Electricity Board (General Conditions of Supply) Regulations (1981), Regn. 28 - Electricity Act, 1910, s. 22B - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Power intensive industries - State Govt. regulate supply of energy - Benefit of clubbing of power allotted to different units - Then an order imposing restrictions on consumption of energy made with retrospective effect - (A) Is it with in the power of the govt. to decide on the allotment matter - (B) Whether benefit of clubbing can be taken away retrospectively - (C) Whether the benefit of clubbing can be denied merely on ground that it is an export oriented unit - Held, govt. has power to decide as a matter of policy - Benefit cannot be taken away retrospectively - Benefit of clubbing cannot be denied - Petition allowed.
