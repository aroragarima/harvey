Pondicherry Khadi and Village Industries Board v P. Kulothangan and Another

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 922; (2004) SCC (L&S) 32; (2004) 1 SCC 68; AIR 2003 SC 4701; 2003 (99) FLR 1175; 2004 (1) JhrCR(SC) 203; 2003 LabIC 3709; 2003 (3) LLJ 1153; 2004 (1) LLN 508; 2004 (1) MLJ(SC) 116; 2004 (137) PLR 130; 2003(9) SCALE 115; 2003 (1) SCW 5725; 2004 (1) SLR 267; 2003 (6) SLT 515; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 62; 2003 (7) Supreme 590

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Pondicherry

Summary: [A.] Service - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 11, 17 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 11 - Termination - Res judicata - Respondent not allowed by appellant to rejoin his duties after a period of absence - HC held that respondent was neither entitled to regularisation nor to reinstatement as appointment of respondent was a temporary one pending sanction of permanent posts and that appellant Board could make recruitments only in accordance with statutory rules - Held, industrial dispute pertained to same subject matter dealt with in earlier writ proceedings and was barred by principles of res judicata - Further held, it was not open to respondent to re-agitate issue before Labour Court and Labour Court was incompetent to entertain dispute raised by respondent and re-decide matter in face of earlier decision of HC in writ proceedings - HC erred in upholding award of Labour Court having regard to s 11, CPC.
