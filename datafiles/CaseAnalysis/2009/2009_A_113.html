Assistant Commissioner (CT) LTU and Another v Amara Raja Batteries Limited

Supreme Court of India

27 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1112; (2009) 8 SCC 209; JT 2009 (10) SC 322; 2009(10) SCALE 114; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 953; [2009] 24 VST 536

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative

Summary: Administrative - Expansion and Modernization of industries - G.O.Ms. No. 108 dt.20-5-1996 - Benefit of subsidy, tax holiday and deferment of tax - Entitlement - Eligible respondent industries sought deferment of sales tax - AO rejected respondents' request - Writ sought against - HC allowed writ petition of respondents - Whether respondent industries required to be complied with the conditions stipulated in earlier G.O.Ms for availing the benefit of present G.O.Ms? - Held, earlier industrial policies have nothing to do with present industrial policy - Each schemes specified in each policy operated in different fields - Introduction of new scheme means modifications of all earlier orders - Therefore, earlier G.O.Ms were not necessary for construction of present G.O.Ms. No.108 - Amount of sales tax paid in earlier years would have any relevance for enforcement of present scheme - Term 'turn over' in G.O. means quantity of goods, not its monetary value - Further, State Level Committee or other authority has been vested with liberty to clarify 'turnover' in present G.O. - State intended to depart from conditions laid down in earlier G.O.Ms - State cannot incorporate condition of local sale by reference or otherwise - State cannot be permitted to alter its stand after entrepreneurs has not allowed to collect tax from its consumers - Appeals allowed.
