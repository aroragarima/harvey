G. Kaushalya Devi Smt v Ghanshyamdas

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 561; (2000) 2 SCC 1; AIR 2000 SC 656; 2000 (2) ALD(SC) 1; JT 2000 (1) SC 127; 2000 (2) MLJ(SC) 104; 2000 (1) RCJ 211; 2000 (1) RCR(Rent) 155; 2000(1) SCALE 131; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 193; 2000 (1) SLT 264; 2000 (1) Supreme 98

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Andhra Pradesh Buildings (Lease, Rent and Eviction) Control Act, 1960, ss. 10(3)(a)(iii) and 22 - Mysore House Rent and Accommodation Control Act, 1951, s. 8 - Appellant is a tenant - His eviction from suit premises affirmed by High Court in a revision filed by him - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of eviction of appellant on ground of bona fide requirement of landlord was bad in law as it was contrary to provisions of s. 10(3)(a)(iii) of Act? - S. 10(3)(a)(iii) of Act provides for eviction of tenant when it is found that landlord requires building, whether residential or non-residential, for his own occupation - Expression 'to possession of which he is entitled' was construed by Constitution Bench of Supreme Court in M. Padmanabha Setty v. K. P. Papaiah Setty 1966 Indlaw SC 344 - Court held that a tenant who can be evicted under conditions prescribed in s. 8(2) of Act cannot be said to be entitled to possession of premises of which he is a tenant - No doubt he cannot be evicted till one or more of conditions prescribed by section are fulfilled, but it is difficult to equate his right to stay in premises till he is evicted to an entitlement of possession of premises - Held, no - S. 8(3)(a)(ii) of Mysore Act are quite in pari materia with provisions of s. 10(3)(a)(iii) of Act, therefore, though respondent is already having his business in a leased premise of which he is in possession, he can seek eviction of appellant, also other conditions of s. 10(3)(a)(iii) are satisfied in favour of landlord - Appeal dismissed.
