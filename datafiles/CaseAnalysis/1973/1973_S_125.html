Supreme Court of India

23 February 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Dwivedi, S.M. Sikri, A.N. Ray, D.G. Palekar, A.K. Mukherjea

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Dismissal from service, Non-payment of subsistence allowance, Ex-parte enquiry

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(2) - Dismissal from service - Non-payment of subsistence allowance - Ex-parte enquiry - Justifiability - Appellant (employee) was charged and enquiry proceeded ex-parte against him - Appellant was dismissed from service - Aggrieved appellant filed a petition before HC, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that on account of non- payment of subsistence allowance, he could not face the enquiry.

Held, there is nothing on the record to show that he has any other source of income except pay. As appellant did not receive subsistence allowance was made to him on 20-3-1965 after a part of the evidence had already been recorded on February 9, 10 and 11, 1965. The enquiry proceedings during those days are vitiated accordingly. The report of the enquiry officer based on that evidence is infected with the same defect. Accordingly, the order of the Govt. dismissing him from service cannot stand. It was passed in violation of the provisions of art. 311(2) of Constitution for the appellant did not receive a reasonable opportunity of defending himself in the enquiry proceedings. It will be open to Govt. to start a fresh enquiry in accordance with law against the appellant. Impugned orders are set aside. Appeal allowed.
