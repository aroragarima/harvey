Debashis Daw and others v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

05 August 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

B. SUDARSHAN REDDY, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 655; (2010) 9 SCC 111; AIR 2010 SC 3633; 2011 (1) CalLT 29; JT 2010 (8) SC 489; 2010(7) SCALE 769; [2010] 9 S.C.R. 654

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 324 r/w. 149 and 304 part I r/w. s. 149 - Unlawful assembly - Culpable homicide not amounting to murder - Conviction - Challenged - Appellants formed themselves into an unlawful assembly and voluntarily caused hurt to PW 2 and also assaulted deceased as a result of which the deceased succumbed to his injuries - Trial court found all the appellants guilty for the offences punishable u/ss. 148, 324/149 and 304 part I/149, IPC and accordingly sentenced them - HC confirmed the order of trial court - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of the HC and trial court could be upheld? - Held, yes - Appellants contended that PW-2 named only 4 persons in his statement made to the Doctor (PW 7) who treated him in the hospital - Mere fact that he did not mention the names of all the accused is no reason to disbelieve his evidence - His presence at the scene of offence along with the deceased cannot be doubted - Hence, Courts below rightly placed reliance on the evidence of PW 2 - Also no particular reason suggested as to why he should depose falsely against the appellants - Moreover, version of PW-5 has been fully corroborated by the evidence of PW 8 - Hence, conviction of the appellants upheld while quantum of the sentence reduce the sentence to that of the period already undergone - Appeals disposed of.
