Â Smt. Gian Kaur v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

21 March 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, G.N. Ray, N.P. Singh, Faizan Uddin, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 662; (1996) SCC (Cr) 374; (1996) 2 SCC 648; 1996 (3) AD(SC) 192; AIR 1996 SC 1257; 1996 (2) CCR 126; 1996 (1) Crimes 197; 1996 CRLJ 1660; JT 1996 (3) SC 339; 1996(2) SCALE 881; [1996] 3 S.C.R. 697; 1996 (3) Supreme 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression, Right To Die, Freedom Of Movement, Euthanasia, Attorney-General, Freedom Of Association

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21 and 14 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 107, 306 and 309 - Probation of Offenders Act, 1958 - Abetment of suicide - Appellant convicted by trial court and confirmed by High Court - Whether ss. 306 and 309, IPC, 1860 is unconstitutional? - Right to die' is not included in the right to life' u/art. 21 - Right to live with human dignity' cannot be construed to include within its ambit the right to terminate natural life, at least before commencement of the natural process of certain death - S. 306 prescribes punishment for abetment of suicide' while s. 309 punishes attempt to commit suicide' - Abetment of suicide or attempted suicide is a distinct offence which is found enacted even in the law of the countries where attempted suicide is not made punishable - S. 306, IPC, 1860 enacts a distinct offence which can survive independent of s. 309 in the IPC, 1860 - Held, neither of the two ss. 306 and 309 IPC, 1860 are constitutionality invalid - Question of law accordingly decided and appeals listed before divisional bench for decision on merits - Order accordingly.
