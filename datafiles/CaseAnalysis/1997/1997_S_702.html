Meharban and Others Etc v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Saghir Ahmad, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1199; (1997) 6 SCC 54; AIR 1997 SC 2664; 1997 ALJ 1517; 1997 (30) ALR 699; 1998 (1) AWC 229; 1997 (3) CLT 181; JT 1997 (5) SC 645; 1997(4) SCALE 363; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 1149; 1997 (6) Supreme 96

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land and Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 23 - Acquisition of land - Compensation - Sale deeds filed by parties - None connected with sale deeds was examined - (A) Whether sale deeds are inadmissible in evidence - Certified copies marked u/s. 51A of the Act are available - (B) Whether all sale deeds are excluded - (C) Whether compensation determined by reference Court and HC was proper and also properly determined - (D) Whether matter remitted to reference court - Held, (A) Inadmissible in evidence - (B) All sale deeds stand excluded - (C) Compensation was not proper - (D) Since the paucity of evidence, the SC instead of remitting matter and prolonging the agony of claimants determined compensation - Appeal allowed.
