M. M. Thomas v State of Kerala and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 548; (2000) 1 SCC 666; AIR 2000 SC 540; AIR 2000 SCW 73; 2000 (1) CLT 249; JT 2000 (1) SC 26; 2000 (1) KLT 799; 2000 (2) MLJ(SC) 46; 2000(1) SCALE 14; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 33; 2000 (1) SLT 180; 2000 (1) Supreme 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Court Of Record, Debt Laws

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Kerala Land Reforms Act, 1963, ss.8A, 8C - Kerala Private Forests (Vesting And Assignment) Act, 1971 - Limitation Act, 1963 - Possession - Transferred to Govt. - Claim for exemption - Appellant raised a claim in respect of 20 acres of land as not vested in Govt. - Ownership and possession of all private forests in State of Kerala stood transferred to and vested in Govt. free from all encumbrances with effect from appointed day - Tribunals were constituted for adjudicating disputes regarding applicability of exceptions - Tribunal detained that appellant was entitled to exemption in respect of 12 acres of land claimed u/s.3(2) of Act - Tribunal erred in declining relief to appellant at least to extent of property - Forest Tribunal dismissed petition - Appeal before HC of Kerala u/s. 8A of 1963 Act - Whether power to review a rendered under 1971 Act could be exercised in absence of any of conditions specified in s.8C of 1963 Act - Held, appellant did not make a claim for exemption under that sub-section at all - Claim for exemption u/s. 3(2) of Act in respect of an area was brought under cultivation by before appointed day - Appellant was entitled to exemption u/s.3(2) of Act - Forest Tribunal could exercise power of review in conformity with s.8C of Act - Therefore, it was only proper to think that plenary powers of HC would include power of review relating to errors apparent on face of record - Appeal dismissed.
