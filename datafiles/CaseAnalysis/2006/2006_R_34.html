Raj Kumar and Others v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1401; (2006) 1 SCC 737; AIR 2006 SC 938; 2006 (1) AllCJ 377; 2006 (5) AWC 4558; 2006 (2) ESC 75; 2006 (1) JhrCR(SC) 278; JT 2006 (1) SC 49; 2006(1) SCALE 67; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 169; 2006 (1) SLR 802; 2006 (1) Supreme 159; 2006 (2) UPLBEC 1481

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Defense & Security Forces - Border Security Force Act, 1968 - Border Security Force Rules, 1969, rr. 19, 19(1) - Central Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1972 - Before attainment of age of retirement - Resign - Pensionary benenefits - Succeed - Hence, instant petition - Petitioners were holding different posts under the Force, constituted under the BSF Act, 1968 - On dt. 27-12-1995 respondent no. 2 with approval of respondent no. 1 and in consultation with Department of Pension and Pensioners Welfare issued G.O. to mean that any member of the Force could resign with the permission of the competent authority, even before completing the qualifying service for pension and would be eligible for pension under Rule 19(1) of the BSF Rules - Further, on 17.10.1998 the Deputy Director (Personnel) issued a circular conveying that those personnel whose resignations had been accepted after the circular dated 27.12.1995 u/r. 19 of the 1969 Rules under mistaken impression of their entitlement to pensionary benefits but who had not yet been granted pension, should be called back to rejoin immediately - Whether petitioners were succeed their Petition - Held, relied on in Union of India and Others v Rakesh Kumar and Others 2001 Indlaw SC 20210 - Authorities seemed to have realized their mistake as evidenced by letter dt. 15-01-1998 followed by circular dt. 17-10-1998 and the BSF personnel who had resigned in hope of getting pensionary benefits, although not eligible for pension under the CCS Pension Rules 1972, had been given the opportunity of getting back into service by virtue of circular dt. 17-10-1998, despite the deadline for reporting being extended from dts. 30-04-1999 to 31-08-1999, about 697 personnel had failed to avail of opportunity of returning to service, there could not be any equity in favor of those that failed to avail of opportunity of rejoining service - Therefore, no doubt that position in law was that declared in Rakesh Kumar 2001 Indlaw SC 20210, that r. 19 of the 1969 Rules does not grant any right to pension in cases where pension was not payable under CCS Rules 1972 - Thus, there was no question of this Court directing payment of pension to persons who were otherwise ineligible under the 1972 Rules and contention raised in all these petitions on question of law should fail - Petition dismissed.
