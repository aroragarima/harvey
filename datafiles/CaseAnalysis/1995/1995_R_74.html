Rameshwar Dass and Others v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, A.M. Ahmadi, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 2252; (1995) 3 SCC 285; 1995 (2) LLJ 716; 1995 (1) LLN 1131; 1995(2) SCALE 79; 1995 (2) SLR 422

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2(oo) - Appellants employed as drivers in Haryana Roadways - While in service, their eyesight became defective and subnormal - Appellants were retired from service of Haryana Roadways on that ground - Whether Respondent can retire bus drivers on ground of their defective or subnormal eyesight developed during course of employment? - Service conditions of appellants facilitate safeguards because they have developed defective eyesight or subnormal eyesight because of occupational hazards - A scheme is framed for providing alternative jobs along with retirement benefits and for payment of additional compensation proportionate to length of service in case of non-availability of alternative jobs - Appellants suffered serious injuries during course of their employment which incapacitated them from performing their duties - Held, respondent should consider if appellants who have suffered injuries and have become medically unfit can be put to some alternative jobs by way of rehabilitation - Additional compensation should be paid only when it is not possible to provide alternative jobs - Appeal allowed.
