Â S. K. G. Sugar Limited v Sri Ali Hassan, Chairman, Industrialtribunal, Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 November 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 41; AIR 1959 SC 230; 1959 (1) LLJ 420; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 254; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 254

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Industrial Disputes (Central) Rules, 1947, misa, Industrial Disputes (Central) Rules

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Companies Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 133, 226, 227 - Industrial Disputes Act,1947, ss.10(1), 20(3) - Industrial Disputes (Central) Rules, 1947 - Possession - Discharge - Conditions of service - Jurisdiction - Appellant was in possession of Mills under terms of lease - No notice was given by respondent No. 1 to appellant - Appellant was concerned proceedings before respondent No.1 were ex parte - Two applications filed u/s. 33A of 1947 Act - Discharges and change in conditions of service had been effected by appellant during pendency of disputes before Industrial Tribunal without permission of Industrial Tribunal obtained u/s.33 of 1947 Act - Whether employer was entitled to discharge or alter conditions of service without permission in writing of authority u/ss.33, 33A of 1947 Act - Held, employer contemplated by ss.33, 33A of 1947 Act must be identical employer concerned in industrial dispute which subject matter of adjudication - Appellant bound either by reference or award made by Industrial Tribunal - Identity of employers at date of reference with employers at time when acts complained of in applications u/s. 33-A of Industrial Disputes Act were purported to be done by them not been established - Appellant was not by any count party to reference and not being such .party was not an "employer" within the meaning of ss. 33,33-A of 1947 Act - Proceedings in Miscellaneous Cases before respondent No.1 were without jurisdiction and liable to be quashed - Appellant entitled to its costs throughout against contesting respondents - Appeal allowed.
