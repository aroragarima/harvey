Assam Small Scale Industries Development Corporation Limited and Others v Messrs J.D. Pharmaceuticals and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 609; (2005) 13 SCC 19; AIR 2006 SC 131; 2006 (1) BC 71; JT 2005 (9) SC 111; 2005(8) SCALE 298; [2005] Supp4 S.C.R. 232

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Principal And Agent, Del Credere Agent

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - SSI - Assam Preferential Stores Purchase Act, 1989, ss. 2(d), 3, 7(1)(c) - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 12, 166 - Interest on Delayed Payment To Small Scale and Ancillary Industrial Undertaking Act, 1993 - CPC, 1908, s. 34 - Supply of medicine manufactured by Respondent - Payments not made for the supply of medicines - Held, principal purpose of Act was to give encouragement to growth of industries in State of Assam and patronizing products of small scale and cottage industries on preferential basis - Act contemplates acts which would be for betterment of SSI units and not acts which would be detrimental to their interest - If payment was to be made by Corporation to Respondent both under contract as also in terms of statutory provision, it cannot now turn round and contend that it was not part of its duty and leave matter at that - Corporation was not constituted merely to act as a conduit pipe and was bound to perform its statutory duties under the 1989 Act - Appeal partly allowed.
