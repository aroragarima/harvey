Prabhjot Singh Mand and Others v Bhagwant Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1993; (2009) 9 SCC 435; (2009) 9 SCC 445; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1767; JT 2009 (10) SC 190; 2009(10) SCALE 316; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 143; 2009 (7) SLR 667

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Punjab Civil Services (Executive Branch) Rules, 1976

Summary: Service - Punjab Civil Services (Executive Branch) Rules, 1976, rr. 18 and 21 - Seniority between direct recruits and promotees - Appellants were appointed in 1986 by direct recruitment w.e.f. 1985 - Seniority List in respect of officers appointed from 1976 to 1985 was finalized in 1993, direct recruits were not included in that list - Seniority List in respect of direct recruits of 1986 was finalized in 1994 in which direct recruits were placed below last officer in seniority list of 1993 - Appellant filed a writ petition challenging 1994 seniority list which was dismissed by HC - Appellant filed SLP against said order in which it was held that seniority of direct recruits and promotees should be fixed by applying roster as per r. 18 r/w r. 21 of Punjab Civil Services (Executive Branch) Rules, 1976 - Thereafter, final seniority list prepared in 2007 in which all direct recruits were placed before respondent - Respondents filed writ petitions challenging seniority list of 2007 - HC held that writ petitioners would not be reverted till further orders - As per UPSC recommendation, six other officers, including Respondent No. 1, who were promoted on basis of old seniority list of 1993 required to be reverted back - Respondent No. 1 filed application before Tribunal challenging recommendation of UPSC - Tribunal directed that promotions would be subject to final outcome of applications - On appeal, HC stayed reversion of promotes, though no such relief sought for by promotes - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC's order could be upheld? - Held, Order of stay of reversion vacated - Direct recruits were entitled to be promoted as of right - - Reversion of promoteees was necessary in public interest as well as to give effect to doctrine of comity and/or amity - 12 vacancies of IAS stated to be existing - Directions were issued to consider cases of respondents for promotion to cadre of I.A.S. against any of those vacancies if they are found ultimately eligible therefor - Order accordingly.
