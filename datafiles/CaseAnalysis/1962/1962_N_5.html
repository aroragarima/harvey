Â National Insurance Co. Ltd., Calcutta v Life Insurance Corporation Of India.

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, A.K. Sarkar, Raghubar Dayal, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 452; AIR 1963 SC 1171; [1963] 33 Comp Cas 513; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 971; [1963] Supp2 S.C.R. 971

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance

Keywords: Actuary, Liabilities, Controlled Business, Actuarial investigation, Average business in force

Summary: Insurance - Trade - Life Insurance Corporation Act, 1956, Sch.I, Part A, paras. 17 and 16(2) - Life Insurance Corporation Rules, 1956, r. 18 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 34 - Interest Act, 1839, s. 1- (A) Whether Para 1 in Part A of sch. I of Act is intended to prescribe an uncertain system of calculation only? - Held, no, but something definite - Its intention is to get a true average spread over a number of years so that compensation may not be related to any exceptional year or years, whether in favour of insurer or against him - Based upon what represents average business done by a Co. over a number of years - (B) What does word allocation means under Act? - Further held, allocation as made in abstracts - Abstracts are summary of investigations over a particular period and allocation of bonus and dividends must also be for same period - Abstracts contain no reference to any future period - Share comes out of profits which accrue to Company during period of investigation allocation must also be taken to be for period during which profits arise - Investigations entitle policy holders as well as share holders to share in profits whether by way of bonus or dividend - To connect profits with a future period is to make scheme unworkable because insurance business is based upon actuarial assessments of position of company - (C)Whether Corporation is entitled to a set-off in respect of amount due to it under Act? - Further held, according to Act, Corporation shall pay compensation due to an insurance Co. - Insurance Co. shall pay in lieu of assets appertaining to controlled business a definite sum - Read together entitled to set off - (D)Whether LIC Act and rules contain any provisions for grant of interest on delayed payment of compensation? - Further held, it does not contain - Principle - On entering possession purchaser becomes entitled to rents but if he has not paid price, interest in equity is deemed payable by him on purchase price which belongs to seller - Claim for interest proceeds on assumption that when owner of immovable property loses possession of it he is entitled to claim interest in place of right to retain possession - Appeal partly allowed.
