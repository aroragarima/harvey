Major Singh and another v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, Amitava Roy, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 384; (2015) 5 SCC 201; AIR 2015 SC 2081; 2015 CRLJ 2593; 2015 (2) JCC 1385; JT 2015 (5) SC 435; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2153; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 546; 2015(4) SCALE 599; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 777

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Dragging

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B, 498A - Alleged demand of dowry - Murder - Conviction - PW1's daughter was married to accused son of appellant no.1 - Complainant stated that victim daughter was harassed by her husband and in laws in connection with demand of dowry - Victim daughter complained many times to her father about ill-treatment - PW1 stated that on later date when he visited village, he witnessed accused assaulting his daughter while she was struggling to breathe and victim died due to it - FIR was lodged u/ss. 304B and 498A of IPC against accused persons - Trial Court vide judgment convicted accused husband, his father and mother u/ss. 304B of IPC, however, gave benefit of doubt to sister of accused and acquitted her and also acquitted all accused u/s. 498A IPC - Order passed by Trial Court was confirmed by HC - Whether conviction order passed by HC against appellant accused is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, victim daughter died to poisoning is not disputed. However, SC held that prosecution has not examined any independent witness or Panchayatdars to prove that there was demand of dowry and that deceased was subjected to ill-treatment. SC held that deceased was subjected to harassment or cruelty in connection with demand of dowry is not proved by prosecution. Further, both courts below have acquitted all accused for offence punishable u/s. 498A of IPC. Further, conduct of PWs 1 and 3 raises serious doubts about their presence in house of accused at time of occurrence and witnessing accused dragging deceased and PWs 1 and 3 have not raised any alarm nor tried to chase accused and that PW1 did not inform anyone in village of accused looks quite unnatural. Subsequent conduct of PWs 1 and 3 raises doubt about their presence at time of occurrence and prosecution version. Further, deceased died within 21/2 years of marriage otherwise under normal circumstances and in absence of proof that deceased was subjected to cruelty and harassment 'soon before her death', conviction of appellants cannot be sustained. SC held that there is no evidence as to demand of dowry or cruelty and that deceased was subjected to dowry harassment 'soon before her death'. Except demand of vehicle, there is nothing on record to substantiate allegation of dowry demand. Assuming that there was demand of dowry, it can only be attributed to husband who in all probability could have demanded same for his use. SC held that Trial Court and HC have not analyzed evidence in light of essential ingredients of s. 304B IPC and conviction of appellants u/s. 304B IPC is set aside. Conviction of appellants u/ss. 304B of IPC is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - In cases of dowry death, prosecution is obliged to show that 'soon before occurrence' deceased was subjected to cruelty or harassment.
