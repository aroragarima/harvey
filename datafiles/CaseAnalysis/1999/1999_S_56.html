Continental Chemicals Limited v Sales Tax Officer and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Srinivasan, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 369; (2000) 9 SCC 484; AIR 1999 SC 974; 1999 ALJ 682; 1999 (35) ALR 551; 1999 (2) AWC 1103; 1999 (2) JIC 81; JT 1999 (1) SC 339; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 340; 1999 (112) STC 660

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948 - Factories Act, 1948 - In support of dismissal, High Court has relied upon second Explanation to s. 4 - A of Act by which 'date of production' was defined - By utilising that definition and last part of reasoning found in order of second respondent, HC took view that appellant had started production as required by notification on 01-11-1985 with a capital investment below Rs. 3 lakhs and, therefore, appellant was entitled to exemption for a period of four years only from 01-11-1985 - Whether decision of HC is correct? - Held, HC has overlooked definition of 'industrial unit' found in notification of exemption D\ -26-12-1985 which was passed in supersession of earlier notification D\ -29-01-1985. There is no dispute that appellant got permanent registration with Directorate of Industries only on 11-09-1986 and became an industrial unit as defined by notification - That industrial unit had commenced production only on 03-03-1986 and first sale was effected by that unit on 05-03-1986 - Hence, relevant date for purpose of granting exemption is 05-03-1986 and not 01-11-1985 as decided by second respondent and HC - Appeal allowed.

Legislation Cited

Factories Act, 1948

Uttar Pradesh Trade Tax Act, 1948

Uttar Pradesh Trade Tax Act, 1948 s. 4-A
