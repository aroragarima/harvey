Punjab National Bank and others v K. K. Verma

Supreme Court of India

07 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 717; (2010) 13 SCC 494; AIR 2011 SC 120; 2010(6) ALL MR 420; 2011 (1) AWC 940; 2010 (4) ESC 600; 2010 (127) FLR 750; JT 2010 (9) SC 610; 2010 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3107; 2010 (4) LLJ 569; 2010 (8) MLJ(SC) 583; 2010(9) SCALE 107; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 311; 2010 (5) SLR 625

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Punjab National Bank Officers Employees (Discipline and Appeal) Regulations, 1977, PNB Officer Employees (Service) Regulations, 1979

Summary: Service - Punjab National Bank Officers Employees (Discipline and Appeal) Regulations, 1977, regn. 7(2) - Removal from service - Legality - Respondent/Manager in the appellant/Bank was served charge-sheet containing four charges - Enquiry Officer held that three charges were proved but fourth charge was not proved - Disciplinary Authority accepted Enquiry Officer's findings with regard to three charges, however, differed with regard to the findings of fourth charges - Disciplinary Authority imposed penalty of removal of respondent from service without supplying the copies of enquiry report - Respondent preferred a Departmental appeal and then a review petition both of which were rejected - Respondent challenged the appellant/Bank's removal order before the HC - Single Judge allowed respondent's writ petition and ordered for reinstatement on the ground of violation of principles of natural justice - Appellant filed appeal - DB dismissed appeal - Hence the present appeal - Whether the respondent was entitled either in law or as per the rules governing his service conditions to a copy of the enquiry report before issuance of the order of punishment - Held, regn. 7(2) of the Regulations requires the Disciplinary Authority to record its reasons for disagreement wherever it disagrees with the findings of the Enquiry Officer, therefore, there is a requirement to furnish a copy of the inquiry report and the order of the Disciplinary Authority recording its disagreement therewith to the employee prior to any decision on the penalty being arrived at and that would secure to the delinquent employee an opportunity to make his submissions on the adverse findings and to prove his innocence - Disciplinary Authority, in the instant case, reversed the findings of the Enquiry Officer and held that the charges were proved, however, Disciplinary Authority did not supply the copies of enquiry report - Disciplinary Authority differed with the findings of the Enquiry Officer before it arrived at the guilt of the respondent - Thus, there was no error on the part of the Single Judge in interfering with the order of removal of the respondent from the service - Regulations are, therefore, required to be followed in letter as well as in spirit - Impugned order of Single Judge and DB are confirmed - Appeal disposed of.
