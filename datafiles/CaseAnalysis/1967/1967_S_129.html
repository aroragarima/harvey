State of Uttar Pradesh and Another v Sri C.S. Sharma

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 389; AIR 1968 SC 158; 1968 LabIC 190; 1969 (1) LLJ 509; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 848; 1967 SLR 582

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, rr. 55(1)and (3) - Enquiry - Validity - Whether inquiry was made u/r. 55(1) or (3)? - S. 55(3) deals with unsuitability of an officer for service or with a charge for any specific fault which is of more serious nature, affecting character of individual concerned - Collocation of words 'any specific fault' or 'on account of unsuitability for service give clue of distinction between sub-rr. (3) and (1) - Held, it cannot charge a man with criminal conduct without affording him adequate opportunity to clear his character u/sub-r. (1) - Not complied elementary principles of natural justice - Enquiry, if Govt. so decides, must proceed before an officer who will examine witnesses in support of charge in manner laid down by this court and afford deliquent an opportunity of leading his defence, if any - Appeal dismissed.
