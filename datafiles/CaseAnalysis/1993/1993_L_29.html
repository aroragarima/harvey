Lohia Properties Private Limited, Tinsukhia, Dibrugarh, Assam v Atmaram Kumar

Supreme Court of India

17 August 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, M.N. Venkatachaliah

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 563; (1993) 4 SCC 6; 1994 (1) AWC 304; JT 1993 (5) SC 223

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Rent Control - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 8, rr. 3 and 5 - Assam Non-Agricultural Urban Areas Tenancy Act, 1955,s. 11 - Default in payment of rent and also subletting a part of structure standing thereon to pro forma defendants 2 - 5 - Landlord bona fide required land for their use and occupation - Defendant after notice of ejectment did not vacate and deliver vacant possession - Original defendant died pending suit - Trial Court decreed suit - Appeal before ADJ dismissed - Second appeal in High Court allowed - Whether there was a valid notice terminating tenancy and defendant had been properly served? - R. 5 provides that every allegation of fact in plaint, if not denied in written statement shall be taken to be admitted by defendant - Any allegation of fact must either be denied specifically or by a necessary implication or there should be at least a statement that fact is not admitted - If plea is not taken in that manner, then allegation shall be taken to be admitted - Held, yes - In written statement it was said that notice of ejectment is not according to law - Non-traverse would constitute an implied admission - It is a case to which O. 8, r. 5 was attracted - Appeal allowed.
