Bombay Housing Board (Now The Maharashtrahousing Board) v Karbhase Naik & Co., Sholapur

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, P.N. Bhagwati, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 421; (1975) 1 SCC 828; AIR 1975 SC 763; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 407; 1975 UJ 190

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure; Water

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Tender invited for construction of blocks - Tender submitted by respondent accepted - Amount paid to respondent on 30-03-1951 under final bill was accepted by it under protest - Disputes between parties in respect of claims made by respondent - Suit for recovery of balance of amount due on account of extra construction work carried out by respondent decreed - Whether, in view of cl. 14 of contract it was open to respondent to make claim on basis of rate quoted by it in notice? - Cl. 14 empowered Engineer-in-charge to make any alterations in, or additions to, original specifications and contractor shall be bound to carry out work in accordance with any instructions in this connection - Held, respondent was not bound to carry out additions, and alterations as there was no reply to notice stating rates it intended to charge - Until rates were settled by agreement respondent was under no obligation to carry out additional or altered work - Failure to cancel order for additional or altered work on receipt of notice specifying rate would not result in an agreement as to rate to be charged - Clause only gave Engineer-in-charge liberty to cancel order and get work done by another contractor - Fact that an express power was given to Engineer-in-charge by clause to cancel order if he did not agree to rate would not mean that failure to cancel order would result in an agreement as to rate or rates - In absence of some positive act on part of Engineer-in-charge agreeing to rate, there was no agreement as to rate and that respondent was not bound to carry out work - Further held, Board has statutory duty to frame schemes for construction of houses and execute them - Contract entered into Board for construction of buildings might be an act done in pursuance to provisions of Act - Act complained of by respondent was non-payment of money as damages or compensation resulting from an alleged breach of contract - Claims of respondent in items A-3 and A-4 disallowed - Decree of High Court in respect of claims in items C-1 and C-2 - Appeal partly allowed.
