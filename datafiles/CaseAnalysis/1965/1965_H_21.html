State of Madhya Bharat (Now the State of Madhya Pradesh) v Hiralalji

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 204; AIR 1966 SC 1546; 1966 MPLJ 1195; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 752; 1966 (17) STC 313

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Madhya Bharat Sales Tax Act, Samvat 2007, 1950, ss. 7(i) and 14(1)(c) - Essential Goods (Declaration and Regulation of Tax on Sales or Purchases) Act, 1952 - Whether said iron bars, flats and plates are not iron and steel within meaning of Item No. 39 of Notification? - A comparison of said two Notifications brings out distinction between raw materials of iron and steel and goods prepared from iron and steel; while former is exempted from tax, latter is taxed - Iron and steel used as raw-material for manufacturing other goods are exempted from taxation - Held, bars, flats and plates sold by assessee are iron and steel exempted under Notification - Appeal dismissed.
