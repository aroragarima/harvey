Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur v Raja Ram Verma and others

Supreme Court of India

24 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1106; 2011 (8) ADJ 10; 2011 (4) AWC 4105; 2011 (1) ESC 25; JT 2010 (13) SC 81; 2010(12) SCALE 435; 2011 (7) SLR 288

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Service

Keywords: Institutes of Technology Act, 1961

Summary: Service - Education - Institutes of Technology Act, 1961 - 1st respondent/Assistant Registrar in Indian Institute of Technology (appellant) was due to retire on 31-08-2000 on attaining the age of 60 years - 2nd respondent/Ministry of Human Resource Development, in the meantime, issued a communication to all Universities and Institutions that the age of superannuation of University and College teachers would be at 62 years with the liberty reserved to the Universities and Colleges to re-employ superannuated teachers within the existing guidelines framed by the University Grant Commission - 1st respondent, on the basis of the said communication, sought to continue in service till the attainment of 62 years of age - Board of Governors of appellant sought clarification from the 2nd respondent - 2nd respondent, by vide communication dated 24-4-2000, clarified that the increase of retirement age would be applicable only to those categories of employees of IITs, IIMs and IISc who were being treated at par with the teachers and whose age of superannuation was 60 years - Board of Governors of appellant, consequently, by vide memorandum dated 17-7-2000, held that those Assistant Registrars who were in service beyond 60 years only would be entitled to retire on 62 years - 1st respondent challenged the memorandum before the HC - HC allowed 1st respondent's writ petition and quashed appellant's memorandum dated 17-7-2000 - Hence the present appeal by IIT/appellant - (A) Whether the 1st respondent entitled to continue in service till the attainment of 62 years - Held, 1st respondent was not able to establish that he was a member of teaching staff or he was treated at par with the teachers - Hence, 1st respondent's claim to continue in service after 60 years of age cannot be sustained and the HC came to an erroneous finding in taking a contrary view -Whether a particular employee has to be treated at par with the teaching staff is by and large a decision of the management of the appellant/institute and it is difficult for the Court to interfere with the said decision unless it is ex facie perverse - Court should not normally venture and judgment in the said areas of the decision of management, should be best left with the authorities who are in-charge of running or managing such institutes - Further, there is a valid rationale in allowing teachers and persons holding posts which are at par with teachers to work beyond 60 years since it is very difficult to find a good faculty of academicians for doing the job of teachers - In any discipline and especially in a discipline in an institute like IIT, it is very difficult to replace an experienced teacher with years of learning, maturity and experience - Therefore, there is no error in the decision of the appellant whereby the benefit of service up to 62 years is confined to teachers and to those employees who are on a par with teachers - Thus, in passing the impugned order of retirement of the 1st respondent on his attaining the age of 60 years, the appellant has not committed any illegality - (B) Whether the autonomy of IITs and IIMs, which are Institutes of National importance, are being diluted by applying the r. 209(6)(iv)(a) of Grants-in-aid and loan rules - Held, in order to minimize the Government's interference in the functioning of the IITs, it has been decided that the employees of the IITs instead of addressing their grievance directly to the Ministry or directly to the Visitor should refer their grievances or representations within the institute and institute may evolve a procedure for redressing grievance and appeals of such employees so that the Ministry does not have to intervene - Autonomy of appellant/institute is not being diluted and an attempt is made by the Government of India to improve the academic ambience of IITs by recommending extension of age of retirement of the Director of the Institute and of members of the academic faculty - Attempt has also been made to attract the best talent by a progressive revision of the pay scale - In view of disclosure of materials by 2nd respondent/Ministry, it is satisfied that the autonomy of IITs is preserved and they are structurally built up as centres of academic excellence - Appeal allowed.
