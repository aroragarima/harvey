Â Devilal Modi, Proprietor, Messrs Daluram Pannalal Modi v Sales Tax Officer, Ratlam and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 161; AIR 1965 SC 1150; 1965 (1) ITJ 508; 1968 MahLJ 245; 1968 MPLJ 160; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 686; 1965 (16) STC 303

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Sales Tax

Keywords: Constructive

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Madhya Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1958, ss. 19 and 30 - Appellant challenged validity of same order of assessment twice by two consecutive writ petitions - Whether principle of constructive res judicata can be invoked against a writ petition? - General principle underlying doctrine of res judicata is ultimately based on considerations of public policy - Decisions pronounced by courts of competent jurisdiction should be final, unless they are modified or reversed by appellate authorities - If a new ground is urged, court may have to consider it on merits as principle of res judicata may not apply to such a case - Result of previous decision in earlier appeal clearly shows that appeallnt had failed to challenge validity of impuned order - Held, second writ petition filed by appellant in present case is barred by constructive res judicata - Appeal dismissed.
