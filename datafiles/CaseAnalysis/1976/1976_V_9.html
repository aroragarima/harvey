Smt. Nagawwa v Veeranna Shivalingappa Konjalgi and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 621; (1976) SCC (Cr) 507; (1976) 3 SCC 736; AIR 1976 SC 1947; 1976 CRLJ 1533; 1976 CrLR(SC) 246; [1976] Supp S.C.R. 123

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 202, 204 - Issuance of process - Jurisdiction of HC - Magistrate passed orders directing process to be issued against respondent nos. 1 and 2 u/s. 204(1)(b) of CrPC and so, respondent nos. 1 and 2 filed revision which was allowed by HC by impugned order - Hence, instant appeal by appellant - Whether HC was in error in interfering with order of Magistrate.

Held, since accused had no locus standi, Magistrate had absolutely no jurisdiction to go into any materials or evidence which could be produced by the accused who could be present only to watch the proceedings and not to participate in them. Hence, Magistrate was fully justified in completely excluding the documents from consideration and Magistrate exercised his discretion and has given cogent reasons for his conclusion. Whether the reasons were good or bad, sufficient or insufficient, is not a matter which could have been examined by HC in revision. Hence, order of HC suffers from serious legal infirmity and HC exceeded its jurisdiction in interfering in revision by quashing the order of Magistrate. Hence, order of HC is set aside and order of Magistrate issuing process against respondent nos. 1 and 2 is restored. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - In proceedings u/s. 202 of CrPC, accused has got absolutely no locus standi and is not entitled to be heard on the question whether the process should be issued against him or not. Further, at the stage of issuing process, the Magistrate is mainly concerned with the allegations made in the complaint or the evidence led in support of the same and he is only to be prima facie satisfied whether there are sufficient grounds for proceeding against the accused.
