Â State of Bombay and Another v United Motors (India) Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Patanjali Sastri, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Vivian Bose, Ghulam Hasan, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 97; AIR 1953 SC 252; 1953 (1) MLJ(SC) 743; 1953 SCJ 373; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 1069; 1953 (4) STC 133

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Trader, Tax On The Sale Or Purchase Of Goods, Practice, Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1952

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 13, 14 and 19 (1) (g) - Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1952, ss. 5, 10 and 18 - Whether High Court had erred in declaring Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1952 ultra vires state legislature? - There is no provision in Act excluding in express terms sales or purchases effected in Bombay but delivered for consumption outside Bombay, but neither is there any provision purporting to impose tax on such sales or purchases - Two charging sections of Act, ss. 5 and 10, purport, in express terms, to impose tax on all sales made 'within State of Bombay', and s. 18, which lays tax on purchases, is limited in its operation to purchases of goods delivered to a buyer in State of Bombay for consumption therein - Art. 286(1)(a) r/w explanation prohibits taxation of sales or purchases involving inter-State elements by all States except State in which goods are delivered for purpose of consumption therein in wider sense explained above - Later State is left free to tax such sales or purchases, which power it derives not by virtue of Explanation but u/art. 246(3) r/w Entry 54 of List II - Held, State of Bombay cannot be considered to have made a 'law imposing or authorising imposition of a tax' on sales or purchases excluded u/art. 286 - Impugned order set aside - Order accordingly.
