District Magistrate, Nowgong & Anr v Sarat Mudoi

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 138; (1984) SCC (Cr) 21; (1984) 1 SCC 25; AIR 1984 SC 43; 1983 (2) Crimes 758; 1983 CRLJ 1728; 1983 CrLR(SC) 487; 1983 (1) GLR 225; 1983(2) SCALE 302; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 957

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - National Security Act, 1980 ss. 3(2), 3(3) - Order of detention - Challenged - Respondent/detenu was detained by order of Trial Court - Respondent /detenu aggrieved by said order of detention filed appeal before HC which quashed order of detention passed by Trial court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether without particulars of supplies and services specified in order of detention was justifiable.

Held, if the citizen is detained without trial and on the basis of satisfaction of a notified authority the right to represent assumes importance and the detenu would be in a position to effectively represent only when specific particulars are provided to him and the grounds are intended to provide that material to him. Therefore, court is inclined to take the view that a full disclosure made in the grounds in no way prejudices the right guaranteed to the detenu to make an effective representation challenging his detention. Further, it is open to the detaining authority to take note of the past conduct of a detenu and apprehending repetition of such conduct in future an order of detention can be made with a view to preventing such action. Hence, if past conduct confined to any or all of the 16 of the items in the notification could be satisfied the detaining authority could also on the basis of reasonable apprehension of repetition of such conduct in future make an order of detention for its prevention. Appeal disposed of.
