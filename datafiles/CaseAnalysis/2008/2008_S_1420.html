State of Himachal Pradesh v Suresh Kumar @ Chhotu

Supreme Court of India

28 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2662; (2008) 10 SCC 104; AIR 2009 SC 1109; JT 2008 (10) SC 366; 2009 (1) MLJ(Crl) 247; 2008 (4) RCR(Criminal) 358; 2008(12) SCALE 415; [2008] 13 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 363, 366 and 376 - Kidnap and rape - Conviction - Challenged - Trial Judge acquitted the co-accused of the charges but convicted the accused and imposed sentenced him to undergo rigorous imprisonment for 7 years - On appeal, HC acquitted the accused and held that the accusations were not established and the prosecutrix, as evident from the documents was more than 16 years of age at the time of occurrences - Hence, the present appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, as per oral and documentary evidence, HC was justified in its conclusions about the prosecutrix having accompanied the accused on her own and being a consenting party to the sexual acts - HC also concluded that the date of birth of the prosecutrix as claimed by the prosecution has not been established by analysing the oral and documentary evidence - Impugned order of the HC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
