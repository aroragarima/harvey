Fazalbhoy Currimbhoy Etc v Official Trustee Of Maharashtra & Ors., Etc

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 205; (1979) 3 SCC 189; AIR 1979 SC 687; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 699

Case Digest

Subject: Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950, Evacuee Interest (Separation) Act, 1951, Evacuee Interest Separation Act, 1951, Displaced Persons Act 1960, Defence of India Rules 1962

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 254(2) - Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950, s. 2 - Sir Currimbhoy Ebrahim Baronetcy Act, 1913, ss. 8, 10 and 27 - Whether there is or not a resulting trust to grantor as to surplus, with respect to which there is no declaration of trust and for purpose of determining that question, is it necessary to look carefully to language of deed, and to circumstances of particular case? - Held, upon provisions of Baronetcy Act and of Will, a direction by first Baronet must be necessarily presumed that if trust created by Baronetcy Act fails or is revoked, trust properties and funds must go to last Baronet - However, ultimate determination must turn on validity of claim made by Custodian of Evacuee Property that by virtue of s. 7(4) of Repealing Act trust properties and funds to which Fourth Baronet would be entitled must vest in Custodian - Official Trustee shall transfer to and vest, in Custodian of Evacuee Property accumulated trust properties and funds settled and created under Sir Currimbhoy Ebrahim Baronetcy Act, 1913, subject to directions and orders by Bombay High Court for prior payment of liabilities, on account of Income Tax, Wealth Tax, House Tax and other taxes and other charges, expenses, fees and costs incurred by Official Trustee - Appeal partly allowed.
