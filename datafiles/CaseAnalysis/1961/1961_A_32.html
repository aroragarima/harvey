Â Ramavatar Budhaiprasad etc. v Assistant Sales Tax Officer, Akola

Supreme Court of India

14 March 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah, S.K. Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 351; AIR 1961 SC 1325; 1962 (1) MLJ 27; 1962 (1) MLJ(SC) 27; 1962 (1) SCJ 43; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 279; 1961 (12) STC 286

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Sales Tax

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, Central Provinces and Berar Sales Tax Act 1947

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - C.P. & Berar Sales Tax Act, 1947 - Betel leaves - Taxability -Petitioners, betel leaves dealers, filed instant writ petition challenging orders demanding sales tax on betel leaves - Petitioners contended that order demanding tax was without authority of law inasmuch as betel leaves were not taxable u/s. 6 r/w second Schedule of Act - Held, two items were mentioned in Schedule, namely, item 6, 'vegetables', and item 36, 'betel leaves', but subsequently item No. 36 was omitted by an amendment of Act - Use of two distinct and different items i.e., 'vegetables' and 'betel leaves' and subsequent removal of betel leaves from Schedule were indicative of Legislature's intention of not exempting betel leaves from taxation - Word 'vegetable' should be interpreted not in a technical sense but in its popular sense as understood in common language i.e., denoting a class of vegetables which were grown in a kitchen garden or on a farm and were used for table - Betel leaves, therefore, were not exempted hence taxable - Petitions dismissed.
