Shiy Kumar Sharma v Haryana State Electricity Board, Chandigarh and

Supreme Court of India

27 July 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 531; (1988) Supp SCC 669; AIR 1988 SC 1673; 1988 (8) ATC 792; 1988 (2) CLR 314; 1988 (57) FLR 373; JT 1988 (3) SC 731; 1989 LabIC 1321; 1988 (2) LLJ 360; 1988 (2) LLN 601; 1988(2) SCALE 79; 1989 (1) SLJ 14; 1988 (3) SLR 524; [1988] Supp1 S.C.R. 621; 1988 (2) UJ 314

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Electricity and Energy - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, s.79 - Constitution, art. 14 - Seniority - List of - Probationary period complete - After 3 years minor penalty of stoppage of one increment without any future effect - After one year he was confirmed - Whether the Board was justified in confirming the appointment to the post of Assistant Engineer, Class II and placing him below his juniors in the seniority list - Substantive posts falling vacant on same day - Held, action of the authorities is arbitrary and illegal - Plea, not sustainable - Appeal allowed.
