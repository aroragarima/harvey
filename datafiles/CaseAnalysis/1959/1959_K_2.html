Kadiri Kunhahammad v State of Madras

Supreme Court of India

27 January 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 273; AIR 1960 SC 661; 1960 CRLJ 1013

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 409 and 120 B - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 222(2) proviso - Criminal breach of trust by public servant, merchant, banker or agent - Form of charge - Appellant-accused was President of one Society - Dishonest misappropriation of property of Society - Conviction by Trial Court u/s. 409 - Confirmed by High Court - Present appeal - Evidence of approver - Findings of fact by Trial Court and upholding of same by HC with some minor variation - Case against appellant-accused was proved beyond reasonable doubt and it could not be said finding against appellant-accused was based merely on evidence of approver but on certain material admissions made by appellant in respective exhibits - Contention raised by appellant/accused as to that charge in question contravenes mandatory provisions of s. 222(2) of CrPC, 1898 - Provision perused - It provides for, that it is sufficient to specify gross sum in respect of which offence is alleged to have been committed and dates between which said offence had been committed - Proviso further says that it is unnecessary to specify particular items or exact dates provided that time included between first and last of such dates shall not exceed one year - HC found no prejudice caused as it had observed, that in fact appellant has been acquitted of main charge of conspiracy and other subsidiary charges framed at trial and it has also pointed out that charge of which appellant has ultimately been convicted is a straight and simple charge - Appellant knew fully well case which he had to meet under said charge, and in fact appellant led evidence in support of his defence to said charge - Held, impugned judgment upheld - Appeal dismissed.
