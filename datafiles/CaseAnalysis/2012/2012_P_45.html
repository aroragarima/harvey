Punjab Urban Planning & Dev. Authority & Ors. v Raghu Nath Gupta & Ors

Supreme Court of India

16 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 264; (2012) 8 SCC 197; AIR 2012 SC 3194; 2012(5) ALL MR 901; 2012 (5) AWC 5164; 2013 (170) PLR 379; 2012(7) SCALE 335; [2012] 8 S.C.R. 118

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Delayed payment of instalments - Claim of interest, penal interest and penalty - Challenged - Whether the respondents were legally obliged to pay the interest, penal interest and penalty on account of the delayed payment of instalments after having accepted the allotment of commercial plots by way of auction - Held, terms and conditions of the allotment letter clearly stated that in case the instalment was not paid by the 10th of the month following the month, in which it falls due, the Estate Officer shall proceed to take action for imposition of penalty charged @ 2% per month of the amount i.e. from the due date in addition to normal simple interest - In case of non-payment of the instalment along with interest due thereon for a continuous period of 3 months, the whole or any part of the money paid in respect of the site shall be forfeited and the Estate Officer shall cancel the allotment and resume the site - After having accepted the offer of the commercial plots in a public auction with a super imposed condition i.e. on 'as is where is' basis and after having accepted the terms and conditions of the allotment letter, including instalment facility for payment, respondents cannot say that they were not bound by the terms and conditions of the auction notice, as well as that of the allotment letter - Moreover, there was no inordinate delay on the part of PUDA (Petitioner) in providing those facilities - HC was not justified in holding that the respondents were not liable to pay the interest, penal interest and penalty for the period commencing from 1-6-2001 to 31-12-2002 for the belated payment of instalments - Impugned order of the HC was set aside - Appeals allowed.
