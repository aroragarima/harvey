Shankar Sitaram Sontakke and Another v Balkrishna Sitaram Sontakke and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Ghulam Hasan, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 67; AIR 1954 SC 352; 1955 (57) BomLR 1; [1955] S.C.R. 99

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Examination Of Accounts

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 11, o. 2 r.2 and o. 23 r.3 - (A) Whether consent decree is applicable in partition suit under CPC, 1908 ? - Held, plaintiff was bound by terms of compromise and consent decree following upon it - A consent decree is as binding upon parties thereto as a decree passed by invitum - Compromise having been found not to be vitiated by fraud, misrepresentation, misunderstanding or mistake, decree passed thereon had binding force of res judicata and plaintiff was barred from reagitating question of accounts in a fresh suit - Cause of action in two suits being same, suit is barred u/o. 2 r. 2(3) of CPC, 1908 - Appeal allowed.
