R.K. Mittal & Ors. v State Of U.P. & Ors

Supreme Court of India

05 December 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Ranjana Prakash Desai

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 816; (2012) 2 SCC 232; AIR 2012 SC 389; 2012 (3) AWC 3109; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 1142; [2011] 15 S.C.R. 877

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: rent restriction Act, Uttar Pradesh, Industrial Area Development Act, 1976, New Okhla Industrial Development Area (Preparation and Finalisation of Plan) Regulations, 1991, Rent Restriction Acts

Summary: Land & Property - U.P. Industrial Area Development Act, 1976, ss. 2, 2(b)(d), 3, 6, 6(2)(b), 6(2)(h), 7, 8, 8(2), 14, 15, 18 and 19 - New Okhla Industrial Development Area (Preparation and Finalization of Plan) Regulations, 1991, regn. 2, 2(1)(k), 2(g) (l), 3(1)(6), 4(1)(b), 5 to 11 - Allotment of land - Lease - Residential use - Lease deed was executed between Development Authority and original lessee - Lessee raised some construction which remained unfinished - Lessee thereupon transferred plot in question along with unfinished superstructure vide Transfer Deed to appellants - Original lease deed contained specific stipulations with regard to use of land - Development Authority, issued notices to stop commercial use in said premises as it was leased for residential purpose - Appellants filed objections contending that Development Authority, in furtherance to the proposal to permit running of consulting clinics, banks and guest houses in the residential areas, permitted such use on main roads, on payment of 30% of existing residential rate on per square meter area of plot per annum and invited suggestions from the general public - Development Authority rejected objections filed by appellants and ordered to vacate the premises - Appellants filed Writ Petition - HC dismissed said writ petition and also directed the Development Authority to take immediate and strong action against those who started using residential plots, wholly or partially, for other non-residential uses - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the residential premises could be, wholly or partly, used by the original allottee or even its transferee, for any purpose other than residential - Held, transfer deed which was executed in favour of appellants, with approval of the Development Authority, also contained similar clauses and also provided that the terms and conditions imposed by Development Authority from time to time should be binding on transferee -Transfer deed stipulated that transferee should put property to use exclusively for residential purpose and should not use it for any purpose other than residential - After raising the construction on the plot in question, admittedly, the appellants had put the property to a different use other than residential - Banking or nursing homes or any other commercial activity not permitted, in any sector, in the Development Area earmarked for 'residential use' - Lessees of the plots should ensure that the occupant banks, nursing homes, companies or persons carrying on any commercial activity in the residential sector should stop such activity and shift the same to appropriate sectors i.e. commercial, commercial pockets in industrial/institutional area and specified pockets for commercial use within the residential sector, strictly earmarked for that activity in the development Plan, Regulations and provisions of the Act - Appeals disposed of.
