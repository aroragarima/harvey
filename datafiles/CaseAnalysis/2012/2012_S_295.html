Avtar Singh v State of Haryana and another

Supreme Court of India

10 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 346; (2012) 9 SCC 432; AIR 2013 SC 286; JT 2012 (10) SC 127; 2012 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3407; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 747; 2012(10) SCALE 88; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 790

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 302, 326, 325, 324, 323 r/w s. 149 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants (accused) and other co-accused were allegedly committed offence u/s. 148, 302, 326, 325, 324, 323 r/w s. 149 of IPC - Trial Court convicted appellants u/s. 302, 148, 326, 325 of IPC and sentenced them to undergo imprisonment for life - Aggrieved appellants filed appeals before HC, which were partly allowed acquitting 3 co-accused and confirmed conviction of appellants - Hence, instant appeals - Appellants contended that there was no pre-meditation and there was no motive and if at all there was any motive, it might be against PW11, while deceased (father of PW10) was totally unconnected to dispute relating to land and any attack on said deceased was so sudden, there was no common object in alleged murder of deceased - Whether impugned conviction of appellants was justified - Held, instant case was related to disputed land situated in a village as between DW2 and PW11 - Evidence of PW10 (complainant) (son of deceased), PW11, 13 (injured) read along with version of DW2 as regards the manner of infliction of injuries amply established a considerable extent the fact about happening of occurrence on way to disputed land in question - Evidence of Doctor who attended on the injured witnesses PW.10, 11 and 13 and other injured persons disclosed that everyone of them suffered cut injuries with the aid of dangerous weapon - In the occurrence took place where deceased was murdered while other injured persons were inflicted with severe injuries and none of assailants suffered any injuries except DW2 whose grievance was quite independent of genesis of crime alleged against appellants - Trial Court and HC rightly pointed that if really case sought to be pleaded at instance of DW2 as against complainant party were true and he really suffered any injury at the hands of complainant party, it was not known why he did not pursue his complaint of such a serious nature by taking appropriate recourse to law - There was considerable doubt and suspicion as regards the version spoken to by DW2 and 3 witnesses in particular about the nature of injuries sustained and its truthfulness - Admittedly while occurrence had took place on 9-4-2003 and according to Doctor, DW-3 and DW-2 approached only at 4.10 p.m. on 10-4-2003 and were subject to medical examination - Extent of doubt about factum of medical examination held on person of DW2 by DW3 rightly persuaded the Courts below not to give credence to claim of DW2 as regards the injuries alleged to have sustained by him at the hand of complainant party -Thus, there was no doubt that case of prosecution to hold that whole case was not fabricated by tempering records - Whole evidence read with evidence of DW2, shows that prosecution story as placed before Trial Court which was appreciated while finding appellant guilty of offence alleged against them was fully justified - Thus, role played by appellant in causing serious injuries on deceased as and on other injured witnesses as found proved did not call for any interference - Trial Court held that it was accused party who had attacked complainant party and thereby complainant party could not be held to be aggressors was perfectly justified - Trial Court noted that when two groups happened to clash and from among two groups, members of group of complainant party were only the sufferers as several of them sustained injuries and everyone of them suffered cut injuries, which were demonstrated before Court by medical evidence in uncontroverted terms that they were caused by either 'gandasi or kirpan or sword' and injuries sustained by deceased which was caused for his death as opined by medical evidence while at same time none of persons in accused party sustained any injury - Ultimate conclusion of Court below in holding appellants were squarely responsible and they indulged in aggression could not be found fault with - Extensive injuries and death of deceased in the process of assault shows that plea of self-defence was wholly a make a belief version which had no legs to stand and was rightly rejected by Trial Court and HC - Every member of accused party should have fully aware that having regard to fact that dangerous weapons were in their possession and that there was every likelihood of offence of that magnitude would be ultimate outcome of grave offence, ultimately brought them within four corners of said Sections and there was no escape from it - Impugned conviction of appellants was justified - Appeal dismissed.
