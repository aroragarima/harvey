A. Lakshmanarao v Judicial Magistrate, 1ST Class, Parvatipuram and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, S.M. Sikri, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 190; (1970) 3 SCC 501; (1971) SCC (Cr) 107; AIR 1971 SC 186; 1971 CRLJ 253; 1971 (2) MLJ(SC) 92; [1971] 2 S.C.R. 822; 1971 TAXLR 556

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Adjourn, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Habeas Corpus - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 121A, 122, 302 and 395 - Appellant was arrested without warrant - Produced before Judicial Magistrate and informed that he was charged for offences u/ss. 120A, 122 r/w 302 and 395 of IPC, 1860 - His name was not mentioned in charge sheet - He was remanded to judicial custody and on expiry extension was sought - He could not be produced in court due to non-availability of any escort and order of remand was made in his absence - Whether remand order made in his absence is liable to be quashed? - Held, no - Personal presence of an accused before a Magistrate is not a necessary requirement for purpose of his remand u/s. 344 of CrPC, 1898 - Order was not illegal to warrant release of appellant by resort to habeas corpus - Appeal dismissed.
