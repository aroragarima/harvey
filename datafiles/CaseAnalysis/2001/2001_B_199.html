Commissioner of Income Tax v Bhupen Champaklal Dalal and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 258; (2001) 3 SCC 459; (2001) SCC (Cr) 544; AIR 2001 SC 1096; 2001 (1) CCR 292; 2001 (2) Crimes 56; 2001 (167) CTR 283; [2001] 248 ITR 830; 2001 (1) JCC 323; JT 2001 (3) SC 271; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 140; 2001(2) SCALE 266; 2001 (2) SCJ 335; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 178; 2001 (2) SLT 385; 2001 (2) Supreme 417; [2001] 116 TAXMAN 746

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 277, 276 C and 278B - Cases were lodged against respondents under Act in relation to assessments arising under Act, appeals had been preferred either before CIT(A) or Income Tax Appellate Tribunal - Respondents filed applications for stay of proceedings arising before Criminal Court - Interim order granted staying proceedings in criminal cases - Whether High Court was justified in granting an interim order in view of facts of case? - Prosecution in criminal law and proceedings arising under Act are undoubtedly independent proceedings and, therefore, there is no impediment in law for criminal proceedings to proceed even during pendency of proceedings under Act - However, a wholesome rule will have to be adopted in matters of this nature where courts have taken view that when conclusions arrived at by appellate authorities have a relevance and bearing upon conclusions to be reached in case necessarily one authority will have to await outcome of other authority - Held, there is no claim of quashing of proceedings - When ultimately result to come out of proceedings before appellate authorities have a definite bearing on cases alleged against respondents, HC is justified in granting interim order it did and such an interim order do not calls for interference - Petitions dismissed.
