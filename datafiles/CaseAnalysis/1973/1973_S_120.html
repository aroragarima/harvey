C. P. Damodaran Nayar and P. S. Menon v State of Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 December 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 219; (1974) SCC (L&S) 280; (1974) 4 SCC 325; AIR 1974 SC 1343; 1974 LabIC 994; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 867; 1974 SLJ 195; 1974 (1) SLR 488; 1974 UJ 107

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: States Reorganisation Act, 1956, s. 115 - (A) Reorganisation of judicial service in Kerala - Decision with regard to fixation of seniority by Central Govt.- Madras State Judicial Service Rules not applicable - (B) Integration of judicial service in Kerala - Meaning of expression K. L. M. principle - (C) Integration of Judicial Service - Govt.'s power to create separate cadre - Held, Madras State Judicial Service Rules were not applicable in view of the Central Govt. decision - It means that the relative seniority of the Travancore and Cochin personnel in any class or grade in the common seniority list will be determined with reference to the date of commencement of continuous service in the same or similar class or grade of posts subject - It is open to the State Government to constitute as many cadres as they choose according to administrative convenience and expediency - Appeal Partly Allowed.
