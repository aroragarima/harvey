Haradhan Das v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 554; (2013) 2 SCC 197; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 737; JT 2012 (12) SC 490; 2013 (1) MLJ(Crl) 214; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 755; 2012(12) SCALE 416; [2012] 13 S.C.R. 981

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 458, 459, 326, 302 and 120B, 148, 302/149, 326/149 and 460 - Dacoity - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - According to complainant 3- 4 persons armed with weapons criminally trespassed into deceased house and committed dacoity and also hurled bombs assaulted them and demanded documents relating to their land-property - Due to injuries inflicted by accused deceased succumbed to injuries - Accused were charged for offences punishable u/ss. 458, 459, 326, 302 ,120B , 148, 302/149, 326/149 and 460 IPC - Trial Court with ocular and documentary evidence noticed that there was long standing civil litigation between parties and found certain discrepancies in prosecution case and acquitted 3 accused persons and convicted and sentenced accused no.1 for offence punishable u/ss. 302/149 IPC which was upheld by HC - Hence instant appeal by appellant - Whether the orders of Courts were sustainable - Held, doctor stated that kind of wounds were sufficient to cause death and that injuries were caused by sharp weapon - Statement of witnesses showed that prosecution had been able to prove its case beyond reasonable doubt - Accused was identified by injured witnesses and by other persons who were present in house at the time of occurrence - Accused was identified by PW9, wife of deceased who was present in the room - There was no reason to disbelieve her statement - Injuries were caused with intention to kill deceased and they were caused on vital parts of body - Accused established ingredients of s. 302 IPC and made him liable to be punished for said offence - Therefore, conviction of accused u/s. 302 itself would be sustainable and accused would be liable to be punished accordingly - There was no reason to interfere with impugned judgments in instant appeal - Appeal dismissed
