Rambhai Nathabhai Gadhvi and Others v State of Gujarat and another

Supreme Court of India

06 August 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1491; (1997) 7 SCC 744; (1997) SCC (Cr) 1169; AIR 1997 SC 3475; 1997 (3) CCR 80; 1997 (3) Crimes 154; 1997 CRLJ 4086; JT 1997 (7) SC 290; 1997(5) SCALE 388; [1997] Supp3 S.C.R. 356; 1997 (7) Supreme 228

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - TADA Act, 1987, s. 12 and 20A(2) - (A) Validity of sanction for prosecution - Only two documents available before Director General of Police while according sanction, one being FIR and other being application by Superintendent of Police seeking sanction narrating some facts - No other document relating to investigation or copy thereof produced before Director General - Whether sanction was valid - (B) Power of Designated Courts with respect to other offences - Whether trial for offences under TADA could validly be held without valid sanction - Whether designated Court has independent power to try other offences - Held, (A) Sanction was not valid being passed without application of mind - (B) Could not validly be held for want of valid sanction - Has no independent power to try any other offence - No valid trial can be held by that Court into any offence under Arms Act - Appeal partly allowed.
