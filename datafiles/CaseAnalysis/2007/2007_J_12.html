Smt. Mayadevi v Jagdish Prasad

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 135; (2007) 2 SCC (Cr) 48; (2007) 3 SCC 136; AIR 2007 SC 1426; 2007 (3) ALD(SC) 87; 2007 (67) ALR 129; 2007 (5) ALT 21; 2007 (2) AWC 1833; 2007 (2) CalLT(SC) 24; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 191; JT 2007 (4) SC 65; 2007 (4) MLJ(SC) 357; 2007 (146) PLR 506; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 309; 2007(3) SCALE 392; 2007 (5) SCJ 162; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 1040; 2007 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 361

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 28 - Respondent filed an application for divorce on the ground of cruelty - Granted the decree of divorce - Appeal against - Held, instances of cruelty highlighted by the trial Court and also by the High Court clearly prove that the husband was subjected to mental and physical cruelty - It is not a fact as submitted by counsel for the appellant that the conviction in the criminal case was the foundation for the decree - On the contrary, the trial Court clearly mentioned that the aspect was not taken note of as the appeal was pending - Appeal dismissed.
