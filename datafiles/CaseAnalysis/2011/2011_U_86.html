Jeet Singh and another v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

15 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.R. Dave, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 593; (2011) 13 SCC 534; 2011 (89) ALR 703; 2012 (1) AWC 305; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 431; JT 2011 (11) SC 568; 2012 (115) RD 141; 2011(10) SCALE 516

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 6, 11A - Land acquisition - Operation of interim stay - Delay in passing award - Justifiability - Appellants filed a writ petition in the HC praying that the land acquisition proceedings in question be quashed as the award made in respect of the land in question was made in violation of the provisions of s. 11A of the Act - Appellant contended that according to the provisions of s. 11A of the Act, the award u/s. 11 should be made within two years from the date on which declaration u/s. 6 of the Act was made - HC dismissed the petition on the ground that there was a stay operating against the passing of award u/s. 11A of the Act - Whether there was any delay in passing the award u/s. 11 of the Act by respondent authority - Held, appellants and their father made all possible efforts to delay the proceedings - Acquisition proceedings were delayed because of the litigation initiated by appellants - Appellants wanted the acquisition proceedings to fail - Appellants' father challenged the acquisition proceedings and in the said proceedings, an interim direction to maintain status quo was granted - On the other hand, another petition was filed making a grievance that no compensation was paid to appellants and in that petition a direction was given to pay compensation to them - Thus, appellants had shown their intention to claim and get compensation and compensation should be given only if their land was acquired - Again appellants filed another petition submitting that the proceedings had lapsed - Considering the conduct and attitude of the appellants and their father and in such a set of facts and circumstances, they would not be entitled to any discretionary relief in a petition filed u/art. 226 of the Constitution of India - Acquisition proceedings should not be permitted to lapse, especially when the Land Acquisition Collector had acted promptly after getting a certified copy of the order whereby the stay granted was vacated - Hence, there was no fault with the Land Acquisition Collector for not making the award before getting a certified copy of the order vacating stay especially when he was not informed about the said fact earlier - HC was justified in dismissing appellant's petition - Appeals dismissed.
