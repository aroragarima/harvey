Director General of Income Tax (Investigation) Pune and others v Spacewood Furnishers Private Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 356; [2015] 374 ITR 595; JT 2015 (6) SC 258; 2015(6) SCALE 291; 2016(6) SCALE 291; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 807; [2015] 232 TAXMAN 131

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Search And Seizure, Finance Act, 2009

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Finance Act, 2009 - Income-tax Act, 1961, s. 132 - Income-tax Rules, 1962, r. 112(2) - Search warrant procedure - Interference by HC - Determination - Appellant No.2 was authorized to exercise power u/s. 132 of Act in respect of territorial areas falling within jurisdiction of city - Further, various mandatory procedure and approvals were laid down for authorities which were conducted by authorities and satisfaction note leading to issuing of warrant of authorization against respondent-assessee were placed before HC - However, HC vide impugned order held that there are four satisfaction notes of four different authorities and held that warrant of authorization was not in accordance with law - Further, it held that there is possibility that file containing satisfaction note was manipulated and thus is of doubtful credibility - Further, various other irregularities were found by HC, hence, HC thought it proper to cause inference with measures undertaken by Revenue against assessee - Hence, present appeal.

Held, necessity of recording of reasons, despite amendment of r. 112 (2) of Rules is to ensure accountability and responsibility in decision making process. However, it would not confer in assessee right of inspection of documents or to communication of reasons for belief at stage of issuing of authorization. It is only at stage of commencement of assessment proceedings after completion of search and seizure, if any, that requisite material may have to be disclosed to assessee. SC held that HC had committed serious error in reproducing in great details contents of satisfaction note containing reasons for satisfaction arrived at by authorities under Act. Further, with respect to reason assigned by HC , SC held that different steps in decision making process is lucidly laid down in instructions contained in search and seizure manual published by department. Steps delineated have been scrupulously followed. Besides Additional Director was not one of competent authorities u/s. 132 of Act on 8-6-2009 (date of his note) inasmuch as it is by Finance Act, 2009 effective from 19-8-2009 that Additional Director came to be included amongst authorized officials though with retrospective effect from 1-10-1998. Reading of relevant part of satisfaction note of Director goes to show that on basis of materials produced satisfaction was duly recorded by him that authorization for search should be issued. Further, file was put up before Director General (Investigation) for accord of administrative approval as required by notification dt. 7-3-2001. Further, order of Director General showed that all that he did was to record view that satisfaction of Director, Income Tax (Investigation) was reasonable and therefore administrative approval should be accorded. The view taken by HC, hence, it cannot be sustained. Further, suspicion ought not to be basis of any judicial order and this is where HC seems to have erred. Further, remaining findings of HC with regard to satisfaction recorded by authorities appear to be in nature of appellate exercise touching upon sufficiency and adequacy of reasons and authenticity and acceptability of information on which satisfaction had been reached by authorities and such exercise is alien to jurisdiction u/art. 226 of Constitution. Order passed by HC is set aside and proceedings against respondent-assessee will commence from stage at which same was interdicted by HC by its impugned order. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Procedure of search carried by authority cannot be questioned by Court when same is in accordance with law.
