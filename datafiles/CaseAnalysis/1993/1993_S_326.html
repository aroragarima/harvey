Gaunter Edwin Kircher v State of Goa, Secretariat Panji, Goa

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 62; (1993) 3 SCC 145; (1993) SCC (Cr) 803; AIR 1993 SC 1456; AIR 1993 SCW 1297; 1993 (2) ALT 103; 1993 (2) CCR 149; 1993 (1) Crimes 1183; 1993 CRLJ 1485; 1993 CrLR(SC) 669; JT 1993 (2) SC 285; 1993(2) SCALE 28; [1993] 2 S.C.R. 337; 1993 (1) UJ 706

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985 - Expert Evidence - Charas - Minimum quantity - Chemical testing - Self consumption - On trial the accused appellant had been convicted u/s. 20(b)(ii) of the Act and sentenced to undergo 10 years rigorous imprisonment and to pay a fine of Rs. 1 lakh - Appeal filed by the accused appellant was dismissed by the HC - Hence, the instant appeal - Accused appellant contended that the search was not as per procedure and the 2 cylindrical pieces recovered from him did not contain Charas, only 1 piece was sent for chemical testing and it could not be inferred that the other piece weighing 7 gms also contained Charas - Accused appellant argued that he was entitled to benefit u/s. 27 of the Act since the substance recovered from him was of minimum quantity - Whether the prosecution had established beyond all reasonable doubt that the accused appellant had in his possession two pieces of Charas weighing 7 gms and 5 gms and thus could not claim benefit u/s. 27 of the Act - Held, for the purposes of s. 27 of the Act 'small quantity' meant such quantity specified by the Central Govt. by the notification in the Official Gazette - 5 gms or less quantity of Charas would be small quantity - Also under circumstances by which it was seized from the accused appellant it showed that accused appellant had it in his possession for personal consumption and not for sale - Moreover it was not proved beyond reasonable doubt that the other piece of 7 gms had Charas - Accused appellant was a foreigner and as a tourist appeared to have carried the substance for his personal consumption - Accused appellant was liable to be punished u/s.27 of Act and not by enhanced punishment u/s. 20(b)(ii) of the Act - Conviction of the accused appellant u/s. 20(b)(ii) of the Act and sentence of 10 years rigorous imprisonment was set aside - He was convicted u/s.27 of the Act and was sentenced to undergo six months rigorous imprisonment and to pay a fine of Rs. 1 lakh - Appeal disposed of.
