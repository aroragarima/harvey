Ashoka Kumar Thakur v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 651; (2007) 4 SCC 361; 2007 (6) AWC 5585; 2007 (2) ESC 274; 2007 (3) JhrCR(SC) 97; JT 2007 (5) SC 276; 2007 (2) MLJ(SC) 1211; 2007(5) SCALE 179; 2007 (7) SLR 4; 2007 (3) UPLBEC 2208

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Affirmative Action, Central Educational Institutions (Reservation in Admission) Act, 2006, National Commission for Backward Classes Act, 1993

Summary: Central Educational Institutions (Reservation in Admission) Act, 2006 - Challenging policy of 27% reservation for the Other Backward Classes - Held, cases have to be examined in detail as to whether the stand of Union of India that creamy layer rule is applicable to only art. 16(4) and not art. 15(5) is based on any sound foundation - It would be desirable to keep in hold the operation of the Act so far as it relates to s. 6 thereof for the OBCs category only - Not staying operation of the Statute, particularly, Section 6 so far as the Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes candidates are concerned - Application disposed of.
