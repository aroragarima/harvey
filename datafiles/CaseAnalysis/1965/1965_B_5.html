Mahadeo v Babu Udai Pratap Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 November 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, P. Satyanarayana Raju

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 219; AIR 1966 SC 824; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 564

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Improper Rejection

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 100 (1) (d) (iv) - Conduct of Election Rules, 1961, rr. 22, 30 and 56 (2) (g) - Whether High Court was right in holding that appellant's election had become void u/s. 100(1)(d)(iv) due to misprinting of his name on ballot papers ? - Held, it is clear that symbol chosen by respondent No 1 has been correctly shown against misprinted name; and it would not be unreasonable to consider fact that a majority of voters concentrate on symbol rather than name - Misprinting of name does not render election of appellant void - To make election void respondent 1 has to prove material effect on election - Appeal allowed.
