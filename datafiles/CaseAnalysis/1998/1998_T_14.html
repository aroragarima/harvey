M.H. Devendrappa v Karnataka State Small Industries Development

Supreme Court of India

17 February 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1465; (1998) 3 SCC 732; (1998) SCC (L&S) 946; AIR 1998 SC 1064; 1998 (1) CLR 651; 1998 (78) FLR 685; JT 1998 (1) SC 719; 1998 (1) LLJ 120; 1998 (1) LLJ 1202; 1998 (2) LLN 419; 1998(1) SCALE 616; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 919; 1998 (2) SLJ 50; 1998 (3) SLT 53; 1998 (2) Supreme 118

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution of India, Arts.19(1)(a), 19(1)(c), 19(1)(g) and 19(4); Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, sch.2, item 6 - Public employment - Maintenance of discipline and prestige of organisation - Corporation employee writing letter to Governor and making allegations about mismanagement against head of his organisation - Issued press statement of political nature - Dismissed - As conduct amounting to misconduct detrimental to interests and prestige of Corporation - Plea, activities were done in exercise of fundamental rights - Held, Not tenable.
