Rangku Dutta @ Ranjan Kumar Dutta v State Of Assam

Supreme Court of India

20 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 367; (2011) 6 SCC 358; AIR 2011 SC 2321; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 2351; 2011 CRLJ 3113; 2011 (3) JCC 1650; JT 2011 (6) SC 126; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 524; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 502; 2011(6) SCALE 362; [2011] 8 S.C.R. 639

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, s. 19 - Non-compliance of s. 20(A)(1) - Effect thereof - Designated Court convicted appellant u/ss. 120B/302 of IPC r/w s. 3(2)(1) of the TADA Act - Instant appeal challenging conviction - Appellant contended that in the instant case, the FIR was recorded in violation of the provisions contained u/s. 20(A)(1) of the TADA Act, as a result whereof, the entire proceeding subsequent thereto was vitiated and this also vitiated the judgment and order of the Designated Court - Whether in instant case the mandatory requirement of s. 20(A)(1) was complied with? - Held, no information about the commission of an offence under the TADA Act could be recorded by the Police without the prior approval of the District Superintendent of Police - Therefore, the requirement of prior approval must be satisfied at the time of recording the information - If there was absence of approval at the stage of recording the information, the same could not be cured by subsequent carrying on of the investigation by the DSP - In the instant case, no approval was before recording the information - Therefore, the entire proceeding right from the registering of the FIR, filing of the charge-sheet and the subsequent trial was vitiated by a legal infirmity and there was a miscarriage of justice in holding the trial, ignoring the vital requirement of law - Impugned judgment of the Designated Court set aside - Appeal allowed.
