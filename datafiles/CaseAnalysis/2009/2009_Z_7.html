New India Assurance Company Limited v Zuari Industries Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1064; (2009) 9 SCC 70; 2009 (4) ACC 390; 2010 ACJ 448; 2009 (77) ALR 167; 2010 (2) AWC 1825; 2009 (4) CPJ(Sc) 19; 2009 (4) CTC 779; 2010 (1) MPLJ 536; 2010 (157) PLR 220; 2009 (4) RCR(Civil) 864; 2009(12) SCALE 98; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 1101; 2010 (1) UC 156

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Keywords: Fire Insurance Policy

Summary: Consumer Protection - Damages caused by fire - Claim - Claimant-respondent had taken Insurance Policies from Appellant-Insurance Company in respect of its factory - One policy was a fire policy and other was a consequential loss due to fire policy - Accident occurred - There was a short circuit which caused a flashover - Claimant-respondent approached Appellant-Insurance Company informing it about accident and making its claim - Appellant-Insurance Company rejected claim - Petition filed before National Commission - National Commission allowed claim of claimant-respondent - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether flashover and fire was proximate cause of damage in question? - Held, admitted by Appellant-Insurance Company before National Commission that it was flashover/fire which started chain of events which resulted in damage - It is evident from chain of events that fire was efficient and active cause of damage - (B) Whether damages caused were covered under fire policy? - Held, if proximate cause of loss or destruction to any other including other machines, apparatus, fixtures, fittings etc. or part of electrical installation is due to fire which is started in an electrical machine or apparatus all such losses because of fire in other machinery or apparatus is covered by Policy - As long as there is a fire which caused damage claim is maintainable, even if fire is for a fraction of a second - In fire policy word used therein is 'fire' and not 'sustained fire' - Repudiation of policy on ground that there was no sustained fire' not justified - No merit in appeal - Appeal dismissed.
