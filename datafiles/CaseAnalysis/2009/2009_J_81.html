Jayendra Vishnu Thakur v State Of Maharahstra And Another

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2003; (2009) 7 SCC 104; 2009 (3) MLJ(Crl) 1024; 2009(7) SCALE 757; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 591

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 299 - Invocation of provisions of s. 299 of CrPC - Record of evidence in absence of accused - Held, sub-Section (1) of s. 299 is in two parts - First part provides for proof of jurisdictional fact in respect of absconding of an accused person and second that there was no immediate prospect of arresting him - Both conditions contained in first part of s. 299, CrPC must be read conjunctively and not disjunctively - Satisfaction of one of requirements should be not sufficient - For invocation of provisions of s. 299, CrPC principle of natural justice is inbuilt in right of an accused - Appeal allowed.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 299 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, s. 14(5) - Order passed u/s. 299, CrPC - Legality of - Existence of jurisdictional facts - A proclamation u/s. 82 of CrPC was issued against appellant declaring him a proclaimed offender - Appellant and other accused were also booked under TADA - Thereafter, appellant was arrested in Delhi - State of Maharashtra filed writ application before HC of Delhi for securing presence of appellant in cases pending in State of Maharashtra including case in question, which was dismissed - On an application filed by State of Maharashtra to Designated Judge, TADA, Delhi for transfer of appellant to Maharashtra, Designated Judge refused to do so in view of order passed by HC - Appellant was formally arrested in present case and supplementary charge sheet was filed - Thereafter, applications were filed by Senior Public Prosecutor for exhibiting depositions of witnesses, who had since expired, which by reason of impugned order was allowed - Hence, present appeal - Held, for purpose of passing an order, be it u/s. 299 of CrPC or s. 14(5) of TADA, it was obligatory on the part of the Court to arrive at a finding on basis of materials brought on record by bringing a cogent evidence that jurisdictional facts existed so as to enable court concerned to pass an appropriate order on application filed by Special Public Prosecutor u/s. 299, CrPC - Once a person is arrested and/or is otherwise capable of being brought to court, proclamation ceases to have any effect - Appellant, after his arrest in Delhi was not an absconder and thus provisions of s. 299 of CrPC was not attracted - Further, art. 21 of Constitution, envisages a fair trial and by reason of such a right alone appellant was entitled not only to be informed about his fundamental right and statutory rights but it was obligatory on part of Special Public Prosecutor to place on record of requisite materials before Designated Judge to show that appellant, after his arrest in Delhi was not an absconder and thus provisions of s. 299, CrPC was not attracted - Impugned order set aside - Appeal allowed.

(C) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 299 - TADA, s. 14(5) - Application of sub-section (5) of s. 14 of Act - Held, s. 14(5) of TADA would be attracted only when accused is facing trial and/or otherwise represented through his advocate - If neither accused nor his pleader had an occasion to be before Court, sub- section (5) of s. 14 of TADA may not be held to have any application - In present case, sub-section (5) of Section 14 of TADA would not have been attracted since order of TADA Court specifically invoked s. 299, CrPC - Appeal allowed.

(D) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 299 - TADA, s. 14(5) - Distinction between - Held, a bare perusal of provisions of s. 299, CrPC and s. 14(5) of TADA it would be evident that they operate in different fields and ingredients of said provisions are different - Materials, which are, thus, required to be brought on record by prosecution for application of aforementioned provisions may be different, although they may be overlapping to some extent - Appeal allowed.
