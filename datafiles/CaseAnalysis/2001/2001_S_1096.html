State of Rajasthan and Others v Vatan Medical and General Store and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20193; (2001) 4 SCC 642; AIR 2001 SC 1937; JT 2001 (4) SC 187; 2001 (2) RCR(Civil) 553; 2001(3) SCALE 62; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 729; 2001 (5) SLT 146; 2001 (2) Supreme 608

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Medicinal and Toilet Preparations (Excise Duties) Act, 1955, Dangerous Drugs Act, 1930, Rajasthan Excise Rules, 1956, Spirituous Preparations (Inter-State Trade and Commerce) Control Act, 1955, Delhi Intoxicating Spirituous Preparations Import, Export, Transport, Possession and Sales Rules, 1952, Rajasthan Intoxicating Spirituous Preparations, Import, Export, Transport, Possession and Sales Rules, 1989

Summary: Health & Drug - Constitution - Rajasthan Excise Act, 1950, ss. 14, 15 of, 3 and 41 - Dangerous Drugs Act, 1930 - Drugs And Cosmetics Act, 1940, s. 2 - Industries (Development And Regulation) Act, 1951 - Punjab Excise Act, s. 3(12)(a) and 3(14) - Drugs (Control) Act, 1950 - Spirituous Preparations (Inter-State Trade And Commerce) Control Act, 1955 - Medicinal and Toilet Preparations (Excise Duty) Act, 1955 - Delhi Municipal Corporation Act, 1957 - Delhi Intoxicating Spirituous Preparations Import, Export, Transport, Possession and Sales Rules, 1952 - Rajasthan Excise Rules, 1956 - Rajasthan Intoxicating Spirituous Preparations, Import, Export, Transport, Possession and Sales Rules, 1989, r.3 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19(1)(g) - Whether Rajasthan ISP Rules constitutionally valid and is state legislature competent to enact same? - Direction issued that none of respondents (ie. writ petitioners before Rajasthan HC) and no person similarly situated shall be liable to be prosecuted before a criminal court for an offence under Rajasthan Excise Act, 1956 r/w Rajasthan ISP Rules and/or notification D/- 08-05-1990 for any act or omission done during period decision under appeal was holding field - Matter being academic is awaiting hearing by Constitution Bench - All appeals disposed of.
