V. Raja Kumari v P. Subbarama Naidu and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 900; (2004) 8 SCC 774; AIR 2005 SC 109; 2005 (1) BC 1; [2005] 124 Comp Cas 1; 2005 (2) CompLJ 20; 2005 CRLJ 127; JT 2004 (9) SC 431; 2004 (3) KLT 799; 2005 (43) MLJ 1087; 2004 (4) RCR(Criminal) 933; 2004(9) SCALE 199; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 882

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal

Keywords: Legal Notice

Summary: Contract and Commercial - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 r/w 142 - Whether non-service of notice is a ground for rejecting complaint even before it is numbered? - Whether cause of action has arisen at all as notice sent by complainant to accused was returned with endorsement 'house been locked'? - Held, where material is brought to show that there was false endorsement about non-availability of noticee, inference that is to be drawn has to be judged on background facts of each case - Complaint cannot be rejected on this ground - Appeal dismissed.
