Edward Keventer Private Limited v Bihar State Agricultural Marketing Board and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 510; (2000) 6 SCC 264; AIR 2000 SC 1796; JT 2000 (4) SC 393; 2000(3) SCALE 222; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 1194; 2000 (118) STC 431

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Challenged, Imposition of Market fee

Summary: Indirect Tax - Practice & Procedure - Bihar Agricultural Produce Market Act, 1960, s. 2(1)(a) - Imposition of Market fee - Challenged - Appellant company manufactures fruit drinks and markets it under the brand name of 'Frooti' and 'Appy' in the State - Respondents Agricultural Marketing Board treating 'Frooti' and 'Appy' as mango and apple products issued a notice to appellant to pay market fee or the products marketed under the brand name Frooty' and 'Appy', failing which action under the Act would be taken. Appellant challenged the demand and filed petition - Petition was dismissed - Appeal before Single Judge - Appeal was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether the products which are ready to serve beverages under the brand name 'Frooti' and 'Appy' fall under the description of mango and apple, specified in the Schedule.

Held, products like 'Frooti' and 'Appy' which are ready to serve beverages not being specified in the Schedule are not covered by the term agricultural produce, as defined in s. 2(1)(a) of the Act. The controversy in the case of Krishi Utpadan Samiti 1993 Indlaw SC 1288 , was whether gur-lauta and raskat and rabgolawat and salawat, which are products of molasses, are agricultural produce. SC while interpreting words 'means' and 'includes' used in the definition, was of the view that these words have to be given wider meaning and processed item of a good specified in Schedule would be agricultural produce. The definition of 'agricultural produce' provided that any processed item of specified good is an agricultural produce. Such is not the definition of 8 'agricultural produce' in the the Act with which are concerned in instant case. Thus, product 'Frooti" and 'Appy' not being specified in the Schedule, the respondent had no authority to demand any fee from the appellant on marketing the said products. Hence, order and judgment of the HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.
