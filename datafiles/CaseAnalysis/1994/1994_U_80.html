Shyam Babu Verma v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

08 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, N. Venkatachala, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1604; (1994) 2 SCC 521; (1994) SCC (L&S) 683; 1994 (27) ATC 121; 1994 (1) CLR 747; 1994 (68) FLR 812; JT 1994 (1) SC 574; 1994 (1) LLJ 815; 1994 (2) LLN 465; 1994(1) SCALE 469; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 700; 1994 (2) SLJ 99; 1994 (1) SLR 827; 1994 (1) UJ 797; 1994 (1) UPLBEC 521

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Pharmacy Act,1948

Summary: Service - Pharmacy Act, 1948, ss. 31(a), 31(b), 31(c), 31(d) and 32 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 32 - Reduction of pay - Proprietary - Whether order reducing scale of pay from Rs. 330-560 to Rs. 330-480 retrospectively, was arbitrary and discriminatory in nature? - Held, once it is established that petitioners do not belong to class of Pharmacists, who possessed qualifications mentioned in cls. (a) to (c) of s. 31 for whom separate scale of pay was recommended by Third Pay Commission, which was accepted by Govt. and implemented w.e.f. 01-01-1973, it is difficult to understand as to how petitioners can claim scale when they do not belong to same class or category - Petitioners were entitled only to pay scale of Rs. 330-480 in terms of recommendations of Third Pay Commission and only after 10 years, they became entitled to pay scale of Rs. 330-560 but as they have received scale of Rs. 330-560 since 1973 due to no fault of theirs and that scale is being reduced in year 1984 with effect from 01-01-1973, it shall only be just and proper not to recover any excess amount which has already been paid to them - Petition allowed.
