Krishna Pillai Rajasekharan Nair (D) By Lrs v Padmanabha Pillai (D) By Lrs. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1628; (2004) 12 SCC 754; AIR 2004 SC 1206; 2004 (2) ALD(SC) 21; 2004 (54) ALR 268; 2004 (1) AWC 570; 2004 (2) JhrCR(SC) 170; 2004 (2) KLT 61; 2004 (2) MLJ(SC) 103; 2004 (137) PLR 779; 2004 (3) RCR(Civil) 386; 2004 (1) RD 376; 2003(10) SCALE 956; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 832

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Foreclosure, Foreclosure Or Sale, Travancore Limitation Act,, Travancore Act

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Family & Personal - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 92 - Limitation Act, 1908, arts. 120, 148 - Mortgage - Right of subrogation - 1st defendant redeemed mortgaged suit property by making full payment of mortgage money - Plaintiff coparcener filed suit for declaration of title with recovery of possession, and in alternative, relief of partition - Trial Court decreed suit upholding plaintiff's entitlement to a share in suit property but subject to payment of an amount to reimburse 1st defendant by way of contribution towards amount spent by him in redeeming mortgage - Trial Court's decree was upheld by First Appellate Court dismissing appeal preferred by 1st defendant - However, HC in second appeal, formed an opinion that suit was not maintainable in the absence of relief of redemption of mortgage and further, suit was barred by art. 148 of Limitation Act - HC, therefore, remanded the matter to Trial Court with a direction to allow parties an opportunity of amending pleadings, so that plaintiff could seek relief of redemption and defendant could raise plea as to bar of limitation - Whether suit was not maintainable due to the absence of relief of redemption of mortgage - Held, instant case was a case of subrogation by operation of law and hence governed by first para of s. 92 of the 1882 Act - S. 92 of the 1882 Act did not have effect of a substitutee becoming a mortgagee - S. 92 conferred certain rights on redeeming co-mortgagor and also provided for remedy of redemption; foreclosure and sale being available to substitutee as they were available to person substituted - Rights subrogee exercised not as a mortgagee reincarnate but by way of rights akin to those vesting in mortgagee, hence a co-mortgagor could be a co- owner too - A property subject to mortgage was available, as between co-mortgagors, for partition subject to adjustment for burden on property - One of co-mortgagors, by redeeming mortgage in its entirety, could not claim a right higher than what he otherwise had, faced with a claim for partition by other co-owner - Co-mortgagor who redeemed mortgage in its entirety could not defeat legal claim for partition though he could insist on exercise of such legal right claimed by other co-owner-cum-co-mortgagor being made subject to exercise of equitable right to claim contribution vesting in him by subrogation - Suit filed in the instant case being a suit for partition primarily and predominantly and relief of redemption having been sought for only pursuant to direction made by HC in its order of remand, limitation for suit would be governed by art. 120 and not that of art. 148 of Limitation Act - For a suit for partition starting point of limitation was, when right to sue accrues, that is, when plaintiff had notice of his entitlement to partition being denied - In such a suit, right of redeeming co-mortgagor would be to resist claim of non-redeeming co-mortgagor by pleading his right of contribution and not to part with property unless non-redeeming co-mortgagor had discharged his duty to make contribution - Equitable defence taken by redeeming co-mortgagor in written statement that co-mortgagor did not make contribution, would not convert suit into a suit for redemption filed by non-redeeming co-mortgagor - Suit filed by plaintiff, therefore, was held as one within limitation and plaintiff was entitled to preliminary decree for partition - Impugned judgment and decree of HC were set aside and that of Trial Court were restored - Appeal allowed.

(B) Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 105 - Order of remand by HC - Appeal to SC - Maintainability - Held, s. 105(2) of CPC provided that where any party aggrieved by an order of remand from which an appeal lies did not appeal therefrom, he should thereafter be precluded from disputing its correctness - However, HC had in exercise of second appellate jurisdiction illegally reversed concurrent findings of fact and ordered remand - In an appeal to SC from final order of HC after remand, challenged even to first order of HC making remand, and, all proceedings taken thereafter as a result of illegal order of remand, was available to be laid - When matter reached a forum, superior to one which had made order of remand earlier, it could go into question of legality or validity of order of remand - Bar enacted by s. 105(2) applied up to level of that forum which had remanded matter earlier - S. 105(2) had no applicability to jurisdiction exercisable by SC by reference to art. 136 of Constitution and that was for reason that no appeal lied to SC against an order of remand; an appeal u/art. 136 of Constitution was only by special leave granted by SC - Further, s. 105(2) had no applicability to Privy Council and to Supreme Court - In the instant case, appeal preferred against judgment and decree passed by HC in an appeal arising from proceedings held pursuant to earlier order of remand, correctness of order of remand could be examined and gone into by SC - Appeal allowed.
