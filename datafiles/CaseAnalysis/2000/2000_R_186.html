Konkan Railway Corporation Limited and Another v Rani Construction Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 29; (2000) 8 SCC 159; AIR 2000 SCW 3908; 2000 (3) ARBLR 435; 2001 (1) AWC 59; 2001 (1) Bom.C.R. 784; 2000 (1) CLC 2147; 2001 (1) CompLJ 10; JT 2000 (Supp2) SC 150; 2001 (1) RCR(Civil) 263; [2000] Supp4 S.C.R. 278

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution; Corporate; Trade

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 11 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136 and 226 - English Arbitration Act (ENG) - Whether order of High Court, u/s. 11 of Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 on preliminary issues is a judicial order so, on facts, is liable to be set aside u/art. 136 of Constitution of India, or even if it is to be treated as administrative in nature, it is amenable to art. 136? - Delay proceedings - UNCITRAL Model - International Law - Parties may go first for Court to determine issues of jurisdiction - Held, if order was to be treated as administrative in nature, even order of Chief Justice of India (or his nominee) could be challenged first before a Single Judge of HC and then before a Division Bench and then u/art. 136 rather than being treated as a final order of this Court - That would only delay proceedings further - Similarly, if order of Chief Justice of HC or his nominee is treated as a judicial order, there would be only one appeal to this Court u/art. 136 of Constitution - It desirable that this Court re - examines matter - Direct papers to be placed before Hon'ble Chief Justice of India for passing appropriate orders - Order accordingly.
