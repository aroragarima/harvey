State of Punjab and others v Senior Vocational Staff Masters Association and others

Supreme Court of India

18 August 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

R.K. Agrawal, Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 618; (2017) 9 SCC 379

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Natural Justice, Equal pay for equal work, Civil Services, Experience, Violation Of, Relief, Selection, Hearing, Procedure, Qualification, Pay Scale, Rule, Grade, Merit, Posts, Qualifications, Appeal, EDUCATION, Higher Pay Scale, Cadre, Classification, Discrimination, Punjab State Education Class III (School Cadre) Service Rules, 1978, Punjab Civil Services (Revised Pay) (First Amendment) Rules, 1998, Notice, Employee, Constitution of India, 1950, Post, Right to, Appointment

Summary: Service - Notification - Pay scale increase - Applicability - Respondents/Association approached State Govt. claiming that they should be granted pay scales at par with Lecturers - State Govt. by Notification dt. 16-07-2003 held that only those Vocational Masters who were appointed prior to 08-07-1995 would be eligible for scale of pay of Rs. 6,400-10,640/- Respondents filed writ petition before HC challenging impugned notification - Single Judge directed State Govt. to give the benefit of Notification dt. 31-03-1995 to all Vocational Masters recruited prior to 08-07-1995 - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Notification dt. 16-07-2003 was valid in the eyes of law or not.

Held, HC was fully justified in declaring that the vocational masters are entitled to pay scale of Rs. 6,400-10,640/- on the ground that the nature of duties being discharged by vocational masters are the same as vocational lecturers and that there was no rationale behind making a classification between the two especially when both the categories were treated as one and the same in all the previous pay revisions since 1978 onwards. Vide notification dt. 31-03-1995, only the nomenclature of vocational masters was changed without changing their nature of duties and pay scales. Further, impugned order dt. 16-07-2003 came to be made behind the back of vocational masters without following any procedure known to law. Thus, there was flagrant violation of the principles of natural justice and respondents were made to suffer huge financial loss without being heard. No such order which has the effect of an employee suffering civil consequences should be passed without putting the concerned to notice and giving him a hearing in the matter. HC has rightly quashed the order dt. 16-07-2003 and directed State govt. to give benefits of Notification dt. 31-03-1995 to all Vocational Masters. Appeal dismissed.
