Elektron Lighting Systems Appellants Private Limited and another v Shah Investments Financial Developments and Consultants Private Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

20 November 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 771; 2016 (1) AWC 671; 2015 (4) CLT(SC) 286; JT 2015 (11) SC 145; 2015 (8) MLJ 629; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 397; 2015(12) SCALE 538

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Project work allotted - Alleged Illegality - Respondent no.3 Municipal Corporation invited tenders for replacement of existing street lights by Light Emitting Diodes (LED) fittings with refurbishment of street light infrastructure on Build, Operate and Transfer (BOT) basis - Further, response to E-tender notice was required to be made in two separate parts, namely, technical bid and price bid - Further, appellants and respondent no.1 submitted their bids but technical bids of latter were rejected as they did not fulfill terms as per tender notice - Further, work order was issued in favor of appellant - Further, HC vide impugned order held that though disqualification of petitioners was correct but extraordinary favour was shown to appellants who were awarded work order and same was quashed - Whether HC even after finding that technical bids of petitioners were rightly rejected, was justified in quashing work order given to appellants whose technical bid was accepted by municipal corporation.

Held, there appears to be no hurry on part of respondent no.3 municipal corporation, in awarding contract as tender had been issued on 1-8-2014 and same was finalized only on 3-9-2014, i.e. after period of more than one month. Pre-bid meetings were held and last date for submission of tender was extended twice from 20-8-2014 to 25-8-2014 and thereafter to 28-8-2014, which by itself shows that process was not carried out in haste. Exhaustive pre-bid meeting was held on 12-8-2014, which was stated to have been attended by eight prospective bidders and minutes of pre-bid meetings running into several pages changed many terms in favour of respondent Corporation to ensure even stricter contract execution responsibilities and thus became part of tender through issuance of two corrigenda. SC held that pre-bid meeting was held to understand requirements of contract viz. opinion of prospective bidders, to give sufficient time for bid preparation, evaluation of bids and award of contract and one month was consumed in carrying out said activities and in no way can it be termed as hurried process, as held by HC. Hence, SC held that HC has erred in law in holding that work order was illegally given to appellants in respect of replacement of street lights by LED fittings and refurbishment of street light infrastructure on BOT basis. Further, with respect to advertising rights, SC held that matter regarding advertising rights was separate, and Corporation which is statutory body and instrumentality of State should have acted fairly by making it open for all eligible to submit their offers. Hence, respondent no.3 was not justified in giving advertisement rights to appellants without inviting tender for it. Hence, to that extent, respondent No.3 has not acted fairly and is quashed. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Work awarded by authority in favor of party is not liable to be quashed when procedure adopted for allotting work is in accordance with law.
