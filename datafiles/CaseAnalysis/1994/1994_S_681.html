Shri Lakshmi Venkateshwara Enterprises Private Limited v Syeda Vajhiunnisa

Supreme Court of India

03 March 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1388; (1994) 2 SCC 671; JT 1994 (2) SC 175; 1994 (1) RCJ 437; 1994 (1) RentLR 350; 1994(1) SCALE 789

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Practice & Procedure - Karnataka Rent Control Act, 1961, ss. 21(1)(a), 21(1)(h), 29(1) and 29(4) - Respondents executed a lease agreement for a period of 32 years - Purpose of erection of cinema theater by tenant, appellant - Applied for a licence for construction - Construction not completed within two years - Application for renewal of licence - Objection by respondents, renewal was not granted - Construction not completed - Defaulted in payment of arrears of rent - Respondents filed an application u/s. 21(1)(a) of Act and for bona fide requirement u/s. 21(1)(h) of Act - Pending eviction proceedings, respondents filed an application u/s. 29(1) and (4) of Act because appellant had not paid rent in spite of notice - Trial Court passed an order of eviction - Revision to High Court, dismissed - Whether during subsistence of a contractual tenancy, it is open to landlord to resort to proceedings under Rent Control Act? - Held, careful reading of s. 21 of Act shows that if anything contrary is contained in any contract that cannot prevail, therefore provisions of Rent Control Act would apply notwithstanding contract, relied on Rai Brij Raj Krishna v. S.K. Shaw and Brothers, [1951 Indlaw SC 73] - Tenant in spite of the specific direction to deposit amount did not do so - Appeal dismissed.
