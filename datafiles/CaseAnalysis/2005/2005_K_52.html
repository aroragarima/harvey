Kailash Dwivedi v State of Madhya Pradesh and another

Supreme Court of India

03 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1669; (2005) 11 SCC 182

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 319 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 149, 302, 34 - Participation in crime - Implication - Complainant/respondent No. 2 filed application u/s. 319 of CrPC, to include appellant as accused on ground that during course of trial appellant's participation in crime was established - Trial Court dismissed application holding that nothing implicating appellant was noticed - On revision, HC reversed order of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in reversing order of Trial Court rejecting application of Respondent No. 2 filed u/s. 319 of CrPC - Held, HC did not properly take into consideration absence of overt act attributed to appellant in FIR - It also did not take into consideration fact that prosecution did not send him for trial after investigation and it also did not take into consideration fact that only one witness of all five witnesses examined said that appellant was carrying gun and at same time, he did not attribute any overt act to appellant - SC, in Krishnappa v. State of Karnataka 2004 Indlaw SC 688, held that for exercise of discretion u/s. 319 of CrPC, all relevant factors, have to be kept in view and order was not required to be made mechanically merely on ground that some evidence had come on record implicating person sought to be added as accused - Hence, HC was not justified in reversing order of Trial Court rejecting application of Respondent No. 2 filed u/s. 319 of CrPC - Appeal allowed.
