State Bank of India and others v Neelam Nag

Supreme Court of India

16 September 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 656; (2016) 9 SCC 491; AIR 2016 SC 4351; 2017 (1) AWC 385; 2016 (151) FLR 216; 2017 (1) MahLJ 740; 2017 (1) MPLJ 253; 2016(8) SCALE 826; [2016] 5 S.C.R. 278; 2016 (4) UPLBEC 2897

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Evidence, Transfer, Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, Criminal Trial, Charge-Sheet, Date, Examination, Appeal, Breach Of, Delay, Interest, Charges, Police, Misconduct, Banks, Writ Petition, Employee, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: (A) Service - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 18(1), s. 2(p) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 409 - Misconduct - Disciplinary proceedings - Justifiability - Respondent was appointed in clerical cadre of appellant/Bank - On 29-5-2006, respondent caused loss to Bank in the sum of Rs. 44,40,819/- by granting credit to co-accused - Respondent was placed under suspension for indulging in gross irregularities and misconduct including of misplacing the clearing instruments relating to various customers - FIR was lodged against respondent for offences punishable u/ss. 409, 34 IPC - Competent Authority initiated departmental enquiry against respondent - Respondent filed writ petition before HC challenging disciplinary proceedings - Single Judge allowed petition and directed appellants to forbear from proceeding with disciplinary proceedings until completion of trial - On appeal, DB dismissed appeal holding that respondent may suffer disadvantage and prejudice if she was compelled to disclose her defence in departmental proceedings which is to be used in criminal case pending against her - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in directing stay of disciplinary proceedings initiated by appellant against respondent until the closure of recording of prosecution evidence in criminal case instituted against respondent.

Held, though direction given by DB to the concerned Criminal Court to proceed with the trial on day-to-day basis. The progress of criminal case since then, by no means, can be said to be satisfactory. The fact that prosecution has named 18 witnesses does not mean that all the witnesses are material witness for substantiating the factum of involvement of respondent in introducing co-accused for opening a new bank account, to misplace the clearing instruments relating to various customers or for the payment released to undeserving customer causing huge financial loss to the bank. The charge in criminal case is for offences u/ss. 409, 34 of IPC, one of criminal breach of trust by a public servant. Therefore, SC accede to the contention of appellants that pendency of criminal case against respondent cannot be the sole basis to suspend disciplinary proceedings initiated against respondent for an indefinite period and in larger public interest, the order as passed in Stanzen Toyotetsu India Private Limited v. Girish V. and others, 2014 Indlaw SC 41, be followed even in the fact situation of instant case to balance the equities. Appeal partly allowed.

(B) Service - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 409 - Completion of trial - Settlement clauses - Comply with - Whether Cl. 4 of the Settlement would denude appellants from continuing with disciplinary proceedings pending against respondent.

Held, Cl.4 of the Settlement is not a stipulation to prohibit the institution and continuation of disciplinary proceedings, much less indefinitely merely because of pendency of criminal case against delinquent employee. On the other hand, it is an enabling provision permitting institution or continuation of disciplinary proceedings, if employee is not put on trial by prosecution within one year from the commission of offence or prosecution fails to proceed against him for want of any material. The term "completion of the trial" thereat, must be construed as completion of trial within a reasonable time frame. This clause cannot come to the aid of delinquent employee who has been named as an accused in a criminal case and more so is party to prolongation of the trial. SC is fortified in taking such view on the principle underlying the former part of same cl. which envisages that if Authority which has to start the prosecution refuses (read fails) to do so within one year from the commission of offence, departmental action can proceed under the provisions as set out in Cls. 11 and 12 of the Settlement. The arrangement predicated in case of Stanzen Toyotetsu India Private Limited, 2014 Indlaw SC 41, would meet the ends of justice. For, the disciplinary proceedings instituted against respondent cannot brook any further delay which is already pending for more than 10 years. SC directed Trial Court for criminal case pending against the respondent to be decided expeditiously but not later than 1 year. Trial Court shall take effective steps to ensure that witnesses are served, appear and are examined on day-to-day basis. In case any adjournment becomes inevitable, it should not be for more than a fortnight when necessary. Respondent was directed to extend full cooperation to Trial Court for an early disposal of trial, which includes cooperation by Advocate appointed by her. Appeal partly allowed.
