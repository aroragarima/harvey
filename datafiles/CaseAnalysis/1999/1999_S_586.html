Duncans Industries Limited v State of Uttar Pradesh and Other

Supreme Court of India

03 December 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1343; (2000) 1 SCC 633; AIR 2000 SC 355; 2000 ALJ 116; 2000 (1) AWC 464; 2000 (1) CLC 310; 2000 (1) CLT 11; 2000 (88) ECR 19; JT 1999 (9) SC 421; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 118; 2000 (4) RCR(Civil) 147; 2000 (91) RD 55; 1999(7) SCALE 344; 2000 (1) SLT 82; 1999 (10) Supreme 91

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Customs; Indirect Tax; Trade

Keywords: Immovable, Conveyance

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1956 - Indian Stamp Act, 1899, ss. 47A(2), 47 A(11), 27 and 56 - Valuation of property - Correctness - Registrar referred deed to Collector for proper valuation due to non disclosure of details - Whether by conveyance deed, plant and machinery were also transferred and if so, whether High Court was right in accepting valuation made by authorities for purpose of stamp duty? - Held, considering whether plant and machinery can be construed as immovable property or not, machineries which formed fertilizer plant, were permanently embedded in earth with intention not to remove for purpose of any sale - Law does not lay down that a court cannot review previous agreements to determine property that is actually conveyed - If convinced that method adopted for valuation is based on relevant materials then court will not interfere with finding of fact - Appeal dismissed.
