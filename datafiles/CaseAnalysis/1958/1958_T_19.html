Anant Gopal Sheorey v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

22 May 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 56; (1971) 1 SCC 593; AIR 1958 SC 915; 1958 CRLJ 1429; 1958 (2) MLJ(SC) 207; 1958 SCJ 1231; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 919

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Code of Criminal Procedure (Amendment) Act

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 61, 166, 166(c), 251, 251 and 342A - Criminal Procedure Code (Amendment) Act, 1955 - Indian Companies Act, 1956, s. 282 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 118 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 465 and 477 - Complaint against appellant and others u/s. 282 of Indian Companies Act and ss. 465 and 477A, IPC, 1860 - Proceedings commenced in 1954 - CrPC (Amendment) Act, 1955 (Amending Act) came into force on 02-01-1956 - Appellant made an application claiming right to appear as a witness on his own behalf u/s. 342 A of amended code - Dismissed - Whether to a pending prosecution provisions of amended code have become applicable? - No person has a vested right in any course of procedure - He has only right of prosecution or defence in manner prescribed for time being by or for court in which case is pending and if by an Act of Parliament mode of procedure is altered he has no other right than to proceed according to altered mode - Held, by s. 34 of amending Act, s. 251 of code was substituted by two ss i.e., 251 and 251 A - s. 251(a) deals with cases instituted on a police report, sub-cls.(b) with other cases - To former s. 251 A is applicable and to other cases procedure specified in other provisions in Chap. 21 is made applicable - S. 342A is in Chap. 24 and there is nothing in amending Act or amended code which makes provision of s. 342 A inapplicable to criminal proceeding, which are pending before a Magistrate and in which recording of evidence has commenced - Further held, plain construction of words used in s. 116 of amending Act, makes s. 342 A available to appellant - High Court misled into construing words in cl. (c) of s. 116 as if this Act had not been passed - Appellant is entitled as a competent witness for defence to testify in disproof of charges made against him or any other person charged together with him at same trial - Appeal allowed.
