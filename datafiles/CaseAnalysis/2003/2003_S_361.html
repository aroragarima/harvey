Bikau Pandey and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1063; (2003) 12 SCC 616; AIR 2004 SC 997; 2003 (4) CCR 472; 2004 (1) Crimes 36; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 267; JT 2004 (6) SC 47; 2003(10) SCALE 173; 2003 (1) SCW 6624; 2003 (7) SLT 356; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 201; 2003 (8) Supreme 429

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860 - ss. 148, 149, 302 - Arms Act, 1959 - s. 27 - Culpable homicide amounting to murder - Punishment therefor - Conviction and punishment awarded whether justified? - Common object u/s 149 whether made out? - Convicted by both the Trial Court and the HC - Held, merely because the witnesses happened to be relations of the deceased is not a ground to reject their testimony - The witnesses appear to be natural witnesses who were supposed to be at the place of occurrence - Further held, no interference would be made with the concurrent findings of fact based on pure appreciation of evidence, even if the Court was to take a different view on the evidence - The Court will normally not enter into reappraisal or the review of evidence unless the trial Court or the HC is shown to have committed an error of law or procedure and the conclusions arrived at are perverse - This Court cannot enter into the credibility of the evidence with a view to substitute its opinion for that of the trial Court or the HC It is only in rare or exceptional case where there is some manifest illegality or grave or serious irregularity resulting in miscarriage of justice that the Court would interfere with such findings of fact - Appeal dismissed.
