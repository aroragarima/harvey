Entertainment Tax Officer v Ambae Picture Palace

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

Yogeshwar Dayal, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1367; (1994) 1 SCC 209; JT 1993 (6) SC 221; 1993(4) SCALE 295; 1995 (96) STC 338

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Sale Price, Collected, Andhra Pradesh Entertainments Tax Act, 1939, Andhra Pradesh Entertainments Tax (Amendment) Act, 1985

Summary: Indirect Tax - Andhra Pradesh Entertainments Tax Act, 1939 - Andhra Pradesh Entertainments Tax (Amendment) Act, 1985 - Whether s. 1 of Act, 1985 to the extent of its applicability retrospectively between 07-09-1984 to 24-10-1984 is arbitrary, unreasonable and ultra vires the Constitution for having failed to provide any explanation for retrospective operation? - The Parliament or the state legislatures have competence to legislate prospectively as well as retrospectively and taxation laws are no exception to this power - Held, it is not for the state to justify or explain the necessity for the amendment in relation to retrospectively of the Act - No judgment of any court was sought to be circumvented by such amendment - Hence, no explanation required - Appeal allowed.
