Ram Nath International Construction Private Limited v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

21 October 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1614; (1997) 11 SCC 645; AIR 1998 SC 367; 1997 (2) ARBLR 589; 1998 (1) CLT 11; JT 1997 (8) SC 522; 1997(6) SCALE 462; 1998 (5) SLT 575; 1997 (9) Supreme 233; 1997 (2) UJ 776

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 13 and 23 - Ambit of issues that can be referred to arbitration - As to payment of extra items of work that are not specified in agreement - (A) Whether work in question was extra work or not - Held, matter was specifically referred to arbitrator by parties to agreement - Question was argued before arbitrator - (A) Speaking award was given with details as to why work was extra work and payment was required to be made - Therefore it cannot be said to be beyond scope of reference to arbitrator - (B) Whether arbitrator is debarred from granting interest in terms of agreement - Further held, arbitrator is not debarred from granting interest in terms of agreement as power can be exercised analogous to s. 34, CPC - Discretion is vested in arbitrator and can be granted to do complete justice between parties - Contractor is entitled to interest from date of reference to date of award - However, not entitled to any interest for pre-reference period - Appeal allowed - Appeal of respondent dismissed.
