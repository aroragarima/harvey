Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal I v Indian Molasses Company Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

12 August 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 254; (1970) 2 SCC 834; AIR 1970 SC 2067; [1970] 78 ITR 474; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 773

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Vocation

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 10(2)(Xv), 10(4A), 66(1) - Income-Tax Act, 1961 - Finance Act, 1956 Amount deposited for pension - Exemption claimed - Negatived - Challenged - A/y 1956-57 - Respondent appointed 'X' as its managing director and in order to provide pension to 'X' on his retirement executed deed of trust appointing three trustees to carry out that object and set apart Rs. 1, 09, 643 and Rs.4,364 per year for next six years - Respondent in order to provide 'enhanced pension' to 'X' executed supplementary deed of trust on 29-10-1954 and set apart an additional sum of Rs. 47, 607 to enable trustees to take out annuity policy with same terms of original trust deed - 'X' died in May 1955 and respondent had in its return for A/y1956-57 claimed total amount of Rs. 1, 83, 434 paid by respondent to trustees by original and supplementary trust deeds be allowed as permissible expenditure in computation of business profits of previous year ending 31-12-1955 - Income Tax Officer (ITO) disallowed claim without assigning any reasons - Respondent filed appeal and was dismissed by Appellate Assistant Commissioner confirming order observing that the amounts paid long before the commencement of the previous year were not admissible under s. 10(2)(xv) of the Act 1922 - Aggrieved respondent filed appeal and was allowed by Income-tax Appellate Tribunal holding that amount of Rs. 1, 83, 434 was 'effectively disbursed during the accounting year' and would be admissible allowance - At appellants instance Tribunal submitted statement of case to HC and HC had upheld Tribunals Order - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in allowing Tribunals findings - Held, HC was right in holding that expenditure of Rs.1,83,434 would be admissible allowance - But question whether that expenditure would represent revenue expenditure Tribunal had not found any facts on which admissibility of allowance may be determined and HC had declined to allow argument to be raised by appellant and so SC could not answer it without any material on record - HC had rightly held that it was not capital expenditure - For exemption u/s.10(2)(xv) of 1922 Act respondent should have established that amount set apart was laid out or expended wholly and exclusively for purpose of business of company and there was no discussion either by taxing authorities or Tribunal on this aspect - ITO did not record any reasons for disallowing expenditure and appellate assistant Commissioner disallowed it on ground that it was not debited in profit and loss account of respondent in previous year - Tribunal had erroneously assumed expenditure u/s.10(2)(xv) of the Act - It could not be assumed that on death of 'X' amounts previously set apart were deemed expended and admissible as expenditure u/ss. 10(2)(xv) and 10(4A) of the 1922 Act - Tribunal considered only two questions and decided them in favour of respondent - It should be established that expenditure laid out or expended wholly and exclusively for purpose of business authorised u/s. 10(4A) of the 1922 Act - HC had not allowed appellant to raise this as this was not raised before Tribunal wrongly relying on Commissioner of Income-tax v. Scindia Steam Navigation Co. Ltd, 1961 Indlaw SC 283 - Second question whether that expenditure would represent revenue expenditure needs enquiry and hence remitted back to Tribunal to decide whether expenditure laid out or expended wholly and exclusively for business of company - Order accordingly.
