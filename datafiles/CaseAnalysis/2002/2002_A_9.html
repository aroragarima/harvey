State of Karnataka v A.B. Nagaraj and another

Supreme Court of India

10 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1475; (2002) 10 SCC 1; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1381; AIR 2003 SC 666; 2003 (1) CCR 1; 2003 (2) Crimes 205; 2003 (3) CRJ 435; 2003 CRLJ 848; 2002(9) SCALE 249; 2002 (7) SLT 256; 2003 (2) Supreme 691; 2003 (1) UC 415

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: IPC - S 302 r/w 34 - convictions under - On appeal, the High Court of Karnataka acquitted both the accused - reliability of circumstantial evidence - Held, had the accused committed the murder they would have normally tried to flee from the place. On the other hand they answered the query of PW5. Further, the accused accompanied PW3, a manual labourer, who does not hold any position of authority, to the Forest Office. Further, the stand of the accused that they had no motive to do away with the deceased and they were actually in search of her cannot be brushed aside and is consistent with the material on record rather than the version of the prosecution. - Therefore, acquittal of the respondents by the High Court does not call for any interference.
