Bikash Bhushan Ghosh And Others v M/S. Novartis India Limited And Another

Supreme Court of India

27 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 458; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 242; (2007) 5 SCC 591; 2007(3) ALL MR 858; 2007 (6) AWC 6274; 2007 (3) CHN 37; 2007 (113) FLR 1183; JT 2007 (6) SC 432; 2007 (2) LLJ 837; 2007 (2) LLN 988; 2007(6) SCALE 537; 2007 (8) SCJ 53; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 985; 2007 (6) SLR 69

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Individual Dispute, Matter Remitted to high court

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1957 - Order of termination of the appellants passed for not obeying orders of transfer - Tribunal opined that the orders of termination passed against the appellants were illegal and they were directed to be re-instated in service with back wages - Appeal against the order of Division Bench of High Court wherein it was held that the State of West Bengal, being not the appropriate Government raised by the appellants, had no jurisdiction to make the reference - Held, transfer of the appellants had some nexus with the order of their termination from services, it is, therefore, not correct to contend that the State of West Bengal was not the appropriate government reference - Matter remitted to high court.
