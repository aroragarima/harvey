Union Of In v Dr. Kushala Shetty And Others

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 512; (2011) 12 SCC 69; 2011 (6) AWC 5798; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 365; JT 2011 (8) SC 160; 2011 (6) KarLJ 177; 2011 (8) MLJ(SC) 53; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 353; 2011(7) SCALE 654

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - National Highways Act, 1956, ss. 3A, 3A(1), 3B, 3C, 3C(1), 3C(2), 3D, 3D(1), 3E, 3F, 3G, 3H, 3I - Highway - Notification - Compensation - Act of 1956 was enacted by Parliament to provide for declaration of certain highways as national highways - Central Government issued notification dated 10.8.2005 for the acquisition of land in 18 villages - Special Land Acquisition Officer, Karnataka Industrial Area Development Board was appointed Competent Authority - Any person interested in lands may file objection before Competent Authority within 21 days from the date of publication of notification - Land owners filed objections on 26.9.2005 - After hearing the objectors Competent Authority rejected objections of land owners - Writ Petition was filed with prayer that objections and representations made by them against acquisition of their land had not been decided by Competent Authority - Single Judge dismissed all the writ petitions - Appeal was filed before DB - DB declined to quash the acquisition in its entirety - Hence instant appeal - Whether DB committed serious error by partially quashing acquisition proceedings - Petitioners contended maladministration by the political Government headed by the respondent and allegations of mala fides made against holder of an office which has a high responsibility in administration - Held, NHAI prepares and implements projects relating to development and maintenance of National Highways after thorough study by experts in different fields detailed project reports were prepared keeping in view relative factors including intensity of heavy vehicular traffic and larger public interest - Therefore, Courts are not at all equipped to decide upon viability and feasibility of particular project and whether particular alignment would sub-serve larger public interest - Thus, scope of judicial review is very limited and Court can nullify acquisition of land in rarest of rare cases - Particular project was found to be ex-facie contrary to mandate of law or tainted due to mala fides - Thus, Court said that neither any violation of mandate of the 1956 Act has been established nor charge of malice in fact had been proved - Impugned judgment was set aside and order passed by Single Judge dismissing objections filed by respondents was restored - Appeal allowed.
