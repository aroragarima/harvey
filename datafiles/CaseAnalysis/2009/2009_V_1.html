Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Kerala Abkari Shops (Disposal in Auction) Rules, 1974

Summary: Excise - Kerala Abkari Act; Kerala Abkari Shops (Disposal in Auction) Rules, 1974 - Interpretation of the provisions - Held, order of cancellation of licence need not be passed only because it is lawful to do so - Licensing Authority in such matters exercises a discretionary jurisdiction - In a given case, it is possible for a licensee to show that although in fact he had not paid the kist, he could not do so for sufficient or cogent reasons; he also could raise a plea which could have been accepted by the Licensing Authority that interest of justice would be subserved if he is allowed to deposit the amount with interest or penalty, as the case may be - Commissioner of Excise being a higher authority had already expressed his opinion that application of r. 6(28) of the Rules is automatic consequent upon the cancellation of licence in terms of r. 6(30) - Assistant Commissioner of Excise could not have taken a different view - Forfeiture of earnest money under a contract for sale of property whether movable or immovable, if the amount is reasonable, would not fall within s. 74 - Appeals allowed.
