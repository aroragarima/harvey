Lata Construction and Others v Dr. Rameshchandra Ramniklal Shah and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 874; (2000) 1 SCC 586; AIR 2000 SC 380; 2000 (40) ALR 669; 1999 ARBLR 469; 2000 (1) AWC 451; 2000 (2) Bom.C.R. 145; 1999 CPJ 46; JT 1999 (9) SC 359; 2000 (124) PLR 460; 2000 (3) RAJ 136; 1999(7) SCALE 245; 2000 (1) SCJ 137; 1999 (10) SLT 77; [1999] Supp1 S.C.R. 416; 1999 (9) Supreme 534

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Consumer Protection - Practice & Procedure -Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 24-A - Deficiency in service - Agreement for allotment of flat - Failed to do so - Respondents entered into a fresh agreement with appellants without prejudice to their rights under earlier agreement - Appellants did not honour commitments under both agreements - Complaint - National Commission decreed claim of respondents - Challenged - (A) Whether claim instituted by respondents before Commission was beyond time in as much as it was filed beyond period of two years prescribed u/s. 24-A of Consumer Protection Act, 1986? - Held, riots under earlier agreement of 1987 were kept alive even after second agreement, rights under first agreement had not been given up and there was no substitution of earlier agreement in its entirety by new agreement - Commission rightly treated cause of action to be a continuing cause of action and came to right conclusion that claim was not beyond time - Since whole of amount had not been paid to respondents who could recover whole of amount together with interest from appellant on giving seven days' notice, rights under old agreement did not come to an end and they could legally claim specific performance of that agreement for a flat being provided to them - Their claim was, therefore, not barred by time - (B) Maintainability of claim petition before Commission for compensation on ground of deficiency in service - Held, plea rejected by National Commission by placing reliance upon decision of Supreme Court in Lucknow Development Authority v. M. K. Gupta 1993 Indlaw SC 200 - Under original contract, appellants were under an obligation to provide a flat to respondents - This right would come to an end only when appellants had, in pursuance of subsequent contract, paid entire amount of Rs. 9, 51, 000 to respondents - Since they had not done so, respondents could legally invoke provisions of earlier contract and claim before Commission that there was deficiency in service on part of appellants - (C) Essential requirements of novation - Held, there should be complete substitution of a new contract in place of old - It is in that situation that original contract need not be performed - Substitution of a new contract in place of old contract which would have effect of rescinding or completely altering terms of original contract, has to be by agreement between parties - A substituted contract should rescind or alter or extinguish previous contract - But if terms of two contracts are inconsistent and they cannot stand together, subsequent contract cannot be said to be in substitution of earlier contract - Appeal dismissed.
