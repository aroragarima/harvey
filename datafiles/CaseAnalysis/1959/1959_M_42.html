State of Madhya Pradesh v Mubarak Ali

Supreme Court of India

03 February 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 137; AIR 1959 SC 707; 1959 CRLJ 920; 1960 (1) LLJ 36; 1959 (2) MLJ(SC) 105; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 201

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Illegal Gratification

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Criminal - Socio-Economic - Labour and Industrial Law - Crminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 4(1) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B,161, 164 and 165 A - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5(2) - Whether it is mandatory for magistrate to provide reasons when permitting investigation by an officer other than designated one u/s. 5 A of Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 - Held, where an officer, other than designated officer, seeks to make an investigation he should get order of a Magistrate empowering him to do so - Such order should be sought prior to conducting investigation - Ordinarily, reasons for giving permission should be disclosed on face of order - In case no reasons have been provided, it is duty of prosecution to establish that Magistrate in fact has taken into consideration relevant circumstances before granting permission to a subordinate police officer to investigate case - An order issued mechanically, without disclosing any reasons does not comply with s. 5 A of Act - Appeal dismissed.
