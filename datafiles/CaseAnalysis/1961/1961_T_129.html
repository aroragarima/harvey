Â Tirumala Chetti Rajaram v Tirumalachetti Radhakrishnayya Chetty

Supreme Court of India

27 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, K.C. Das Gupta, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 366; AIR 1961 SC 1795; 1962 (1) AnWR 248; 1962 (1) MLJ 248; 1962 (1) MLJ(SC) 248; 1962 (1) SCJ 568; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 452

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Court Fees Act, 1870 - High Courts Act - Constitution Of India, 1950 - If appellate decree passed by HC makes a variation in decision of Trial Court under appeal in favour of a party who intends to prefer an appeal against said appellate decree, can said decree be said to affirm decision of Trial Court or not u/art. 133 (1) of Constitution? - Held, reading clause as a whole and giving material words their plain grammatical meaning it seems prima facie to show that test of affirmance prescribed by clause can best be satisfied if we take appellate decree in its entirety and enquire whether said decree affirms decision of Trial Court considered in its entirety - It is a matter of comparing appellate decree with decision of Trial Court under appeal - If appellate decree affirms decree of Trial Court it is a decree of affirmance and if there is a variation made by appellate decree in decision of Trial Court appellate decree is not a decree of affirmance and this position would not be affected whether variation is made in favour of intending appellant or against him and whether variation made is minor or major - If two courts which have judged dispute between parties and applied their independent minds to it agree in their conclusions that appellate decision is one of affirmance and unless there is a substantial question of law no further right to appeal should lie - In determining question as to whether appellate decree passed by HC affirmed decision of trial court, appellate decree must be considered as a whole in relation to decision of Trial Court similarly considered as a whole - That is proper approach in applying test of affirmance - Principle of affirmance on which provision rests postulates either affirmance or variation by appeal court as an act of adjudication and that necessarily means decision of appeal court on merits - Appeal allowed.
