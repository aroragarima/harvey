Joint Commissioner of Income Tax, Surat v Saheli Leasing and Industries Limited

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Deepak Verma, K.G. Balakrishnan, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 368; (2010) 6 SCC 384; 2010 (253) E.L.T. 705; [2010] 324 ITR 170; JT 2010 (6) SC 139; 2010 (4) KarLJ 1; 2010(5) SCALE 350; [2010] 6 S.C.R. 747; 2011 (22) S.T.R. 97; [2010] 191 TAXMAN 165

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 260A and 271(1)(c) - Penalty u/s. 271(1)(c) - Assessee filed a nil return after claiming depreciation - During assessment proceedings, it was noticed that assessee had claimed depreciation, which was viewed to be incorrect - Thus, an amount of Rs. 24,22,531/- was disallowed out of depreciation - Penalty proceedings u/s. 271 (1) (c) of IT Act were initiated - Assessee contended that no penalty can be imposed on it, when returned income was NIL - Deputy Commissioner held that assessee was liable to pay penalty, with reference to such additions to income to be treated as its total income, with reference to explanation 4 (a) to s/ 271 (1) (c) of IT Act - Commissioner dismissed appeal filed against said order - Tribunal set aside the orders of Deputy Commissioner as well as Commissioner and deleted the penalty imposed on the assessee - DB dismissed the appeal filed against said order - Revenue contended that the point projected in this appeal stands answered in favour of the Revenue by SC in CIT vs. Gold Coin Health (P) Ltd 2008 INDLAW SC 1309 - Hence, present appeal - Whether tribunal and DB are right in coming to the conclusion that where assessed income is loss, penalty cannot be levied u/s. 271 (1) (c) of IT Act? - Held, no - HC has dealt with the appeal in a most causal manner - Order is not only cryptic but does not even remotely deal with the arguments which were sought to be projected by the Revenue before it - SC time and again, reminded the courts performing judicial functions, the manner in which judgments/orders are to be written but, it is, indeed, unfortunate that those guidelines issued from time to time are not being adhered to - This is reflected in the impugned order - SC in CIT vs. Gold Coin Health (P) Ltd 2008 INDLAW SC 1309 held that penalty u/s. 271(1)(c) is leviable even if the assessment is at a loss - In a first glance, it appeared that matter required to be referred to a larger Bench in view of CIT vs. Elphinstone Spinning and Weaving Mills Co 1960 INDLAW SC 350 but it is not so as ratio decidendi of Gold Coin fully covers the issue and the case of Elphinstone has no application to the facts of the said case - Question of law projected stands squarely answered in favour of the Revenue and against the Assessee in Gold Coin case - Impugned order passed by Income Tax Appellate Tribunal and confirmed by Division Bench are hereby set aside and quashed - Appeals allowed.
