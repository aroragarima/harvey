Case Analysis

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Fundamental Duties, Financial Matters, Minority Institution

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Education - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 142, 19(1)(g), 19(6), 26(a), 29(2), 30(1), 38, 41, 46, 51-A(j), 51A(h), 51A(j) - Issues (A) To what extent State can regulate admissions made by unaided (minority or non- Minority) educational institutions? - Can State enforce its policy of reservation and/or appropriate to itself any quota in admissions to such institutions? - (B) Whether unaided (minority and non-minority) educational institutions are free to devise their own admission procedure or whether direction made in Islamic Academy for compulsorily holding entrance test by State or association of institutions and to choose therefrom students entitled to admission in such institutions, can be sustained in light of Pai Foundation? (C) Whether Islamic Academy could have issued guidelines in matter of regulating fee payable by students to educational institutions? - (D) Can admission procedure and fee structure be regulated or taken over by Committees ordered to be constituted by Islamic Academy? - Neither the policy of reservation can be enforced by the State nor any quota of admissions can be carved out to be appropriated by the State in a minority or non-minority unaided educational institution - Minority institutions are free to admit students of their own choice including students of non-minority community as also members of their own community from other States, both to a limited extent only and not in a manner and to such an extent that their minority educational institution status is lost - Having regard to the larger interest and welfare of the student community to promote merit, achieve excellence and curb mal-practices, it would be permissible to regulate admissions by providing a centralized and single window procedure - Every institution is free to devise its own fee structure but the same can be regulated in the interest of preventing profiteering - No capitation fee can be charged - Judgment in Islamic Academy (2003 Indlaw SC 611), in so far as it evolves the scheme of two Committees, one each for admission and fee structure, does not go beyond the law laid down in Pai Foundation (2002 Indlaw SC 1375) and earlier decisions of this Court, which have been approved in that case - All the petitions, Civil Appeals and IAs be listed before appropriate Benches.
