Mrityunjoy Das and Another v Sayed Hasibur Rahaman and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20234; (2001) 3 SCC 739; AIR 2001 SC 1293; 2001 (2) AWC 1239; 2001 CRLJ 1702; JT 2001 (3) SC 592; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 260; 2001(2) SCALE 499; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 471; 2001 (2) SLT 819; 2001 (2) Supreme 395; 2001 (1) UC 526

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 - West Bengal Land Reforms Amendment Acts, 1981 - Whether, contempt alleged to have been committed by alleged contemners, can be said to have been established firmly without there being any element of doubt involved in matter? - Held, doubt persists and as such in any event respondents being alleged contemners are entitled to have benefit or advantage of such a doubt having regard to nature of proceeding as noticed herein before more fully - Contempt petition dismissed.
