Union Of India And Ors. v Manoharlal Narang

Supreme Court of India

02 March 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Khalid, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28158; (1987) 2 SCC 241; (1987) SCC (Cr) 311; AIR 1987 SC 1472; 1987 (3) BankCLR 18; 1987 (1) Crimes 774; 1987 CRLR 439; 1987 CrLR(SC) 439; 1987 (13) ECC 18; 1987 (30) E.L.T. 37; JT 1987 (1) SC 583; 1987(1) SCALE 468; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 454

Case Digest

Subject: Exchange Control & FDI

Summary: Criminal - COFEPOSA Act, 1974, s.3(1) - Smugglers And Foreign Exchange Manipulators (Forfeiture Of Property) Act, 1976, s.2(2) (c) expln. 2(ii) - (A) What are the grounds available to person against whom action is taken by invoking s. 2(2) Expln.of SAFEMA - (B) Whether notice and proceedings under the SAFEMA are sustainable when detention order is found to be invalid on ground of non-application of mind - Held, he is at liberty to raise all grounds available to him though such grounds were raised and found against in a proceedings initiated by the relative - Held could not be sustained - Appeal dismissed.
