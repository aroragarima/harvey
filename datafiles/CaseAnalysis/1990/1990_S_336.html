Jumman Khan v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

30 November 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 599; (1991) SCC (Cr) 283; (1991) 1 SCC 752; AIR 1991 SC 345; 1991 (1) Crimes 151; 1991 CRLJ 439; 1991 CRLR 98; 1991 CrLR(SC) 98; JT 1991 (1) SC 31; 1990(2) SCALE 3367; [1990] Supp3 S.C.R. 398; 1991 (1) UJ 328

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 134 and 136 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Appellant guilty of rape of a six year old child and was awarded death penalty by all courts - Accused contended that he was not heard on question of sentence and challenged constitutional validity of death penalty - Held, plea was not raised before courts - Plea did not contain merit as accused was heard by Trial Court and death sentence is constitutionally valid - Petition dismissed.
