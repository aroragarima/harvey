(1) M. C. Mehta; (2) T. N. Godavarman Thirumulpad v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, K.G. Balakrishnan, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 616; (2009) 6 SCC 142; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 353; 2009 (6) CompLJ 473; 2009 (3) CTC 592; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 856; 2009(7) SCALE 650; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 998

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Applications Disposed Of, Pollution Control Boards, Concession Rules, Mineral Concession, Sustainable Development, Degradation, Natural Resources, Mineral Conservation and Development Rules, 1988, Mineral Conservation, Punjab Land Preservation Act, 1900

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Mines & Minerals - Environment (Protection) Act, 1986, s. 3 - Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980, s. 2(ii) - Wildlife (Protection) Act, 1972, s. 18, 35 - Punjab Land Preservation Act, 1900 - Water (Prevention and Control) of Pollution Act, 1974 - Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981 - Mines and Minerals (Development & Regulation) Act, 1957, s. 13(2)(qq) - Mineral Concession Rules, 1960, rr. 22(5) and 27(1)(s)(i) - Mineral Conservation and Development Rules, 1988, rr. 34 and 37 - Environment Impact Assessment Notification, 1994 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21, 48A and 51A(g) - 'M.C. Mehta, (2004) 12 SCC 118'(Judgement), paras 18, 96, 63, 57, 84, 89 - Ecology of Aravallis Range - Land and Ecological degradation - Mining operation - Sustainable Development Principle(SD) - Mining within principle of SD, is balancing, and mining beyond SD is banning - 'Precautionary Principle' - Remedial Measures - Concept of 'Inter Generational Equities' - Has the situation as predicted in para 89 of judgement come about so as to warrant total stoppage of mining activity as stated in para 96(6) of the said judgment; and if so, what should be the duration of such ban/stoppage? - Held, Yes the situation has arrived - Held further, all mining operation to be suspended in the Aravalli Hill Range falling in the State of Haryana within the area of approx. 448 sq. kms in the Districts of Faridabad and Gurgaon including Mewat till Reclamation Plan duly certified by State of Haryana, MoEF and CEC is prepared in accordance with the statutory provisions contained in various relevant enactments as well as in terms of the Rules framed thereunder and the Guidelines - Several orders and directions were passed and issued in the judgement; one of the order D/- 29/30-10-2002, prohibited and banned all mining activity in the entire Aravalli Hills and the ban was extended to the entire hill range of Aravalli from Dholpur to Rajasthan; notification D/- 07-05-1992 by Central Govt.; Court tracked the fall out of such order; I.As. were moved stating that applications have been filed for Environment Impact Assessment(EIA) and for approval of plans and it is at such stage the Court ordered that no mining activity could be carried out without remedial measures being taken and for the purpose, it was necessary that EIA had to be done before any mining activity could be permitted; subsequently mining operations were carried out unscientifically with profiteering as the sole aim; wasteful manner of mining with complete disregard to mineral conservation aspect, rendering reclamation of mined area impossible; several drastic aspects noted by Court; mining operation allowed by State in areas where plantations were undertaken; operations destructive of groundwater table causing irreparable damage to the critical groundwater reserves; no effective mechanism to ensure compliance of various conditions stipulated while giving approvals; no deterrent action was taken against serious violations - Consequences of continuance violation of rules, noted - Para 57 of judgement stated that so long as mining operations are undertaken on SD principle, Court should not impose complete ban as it generates revenue for the State; para 89 left open the window for the Court to impose complete ban if emergent situation arises, wherein a warning in that regard was given - Such prediction in para 89 of the judgment, Court stressed, has come true - Position worsened, severe degradation of land and ecology - Court decided not to focus only on individual sites but to take a macro view of the matter - Satellite images indicate the devastation caused to the area by the extensive mining operations; Extraordinary situation demands extraordinary remedies; whole matter looked holistically - Applications opposing imposition of ban of mining major minerals disposed of - Applications for mining minor minerals adjourned - Applications disposed of.
