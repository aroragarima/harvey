State of Bombay v S. L. Apte and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 447; AIR 1961 SC 578; [1961] 31 Comp Cas 39; 1961 CRLJ 725; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 210; 1961 (1) SCJ 685; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 107

Case Digest

Subject: Accounts & Audits; Constitution; Corporate

Summary: Accounts & Audits - Corporate - Constitution - Insurance Act, 1938 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Audit - Illegitimate expenses - Double jeopardy - Respondents were Managing Directors - First respondent was given with power, control and possession inter alia of money belonging to company and second respondent as MD also was assisting first respondent in maintaining accounts of company - Audit conducted in between showed that money from time to time had been withdrawn from company by first respondent with assistance and sanction of second respondent, professedly for expenses of company and respondents could furnish no proper account of legitimate expenses for which amount was purported to be taken - Respondents were thereupon prosecuted for an offence u/s. 409 of IPC and also for an offence u/s. 105 of the 1938 Act - Magistrate convicted and sentenced respondents for offences with which they were charged - Respondents appealed to Court of Sessions Judge and Session Judge confirmed conviction and sentence on respondents u/s. 409 of IPC and set aside conviction u/s. 105 of the 1938 Act - Insurance Company obtained sanction of Advocate-General of Bombay u/s. 107 of the 1938 Act and filed complaint in Court of Judicial Magistrate, Poona, charged offence u/s. 105 of the 1938 Act - Magistrate took case on file and directed issue of process - Respondents made application before Magistrate praying that complaint against them should be dismissed - Magistrate overruled plea - State of Bombay filed an appeal to HC u/s. 417 of CrPC - Appeal was dismissed by Judges who however granted certificate on strength of which appeal had been preferred - Whether HC was correct in dismissing appeal considering art. 20(2) of Constitution - Held, there was no case of double jeopardy as embodied in art. 20(2) of the Constitution - Respondents were not being sought to be punished for same offence twice but for two distinct offences constituted or made up of different ingredients - Order of HC set aside and case would go back to Judicial Magistrate - Appeal allowed.
