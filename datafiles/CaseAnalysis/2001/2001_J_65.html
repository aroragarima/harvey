Dadi Jagannadham v Jammulu Ramulu and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, K.T. Thomas, N. Santosh Hegde, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20007; (2001) 7 SCC 71; AIR 2001 SC 2699; AIR 2001 SCW 3051; 2002 (1) ALD(SC) 37; 2002 (1) AllCJ 35; 2001 (45) ALR 58; 2001 (4) AWC 2847; 2001 (4) CTC 314; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 215; JT 2001 (6) SC 643; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 267; 2001 (92) RD 803; 2001(5) SCALE 402; 2001 (4) SCJ 582; 2001 (6) SLT 30; [2001] Supp2 S.C.R. 60; 2001 (6) Supreme 294; 2001 (2) UC 402

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21, rr. 89 and 92(2) - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 127 - Whether period of limitation for making deposit in an application to set aside sale of immovable property under O. 21, r. 89, CPC, 1908 is 30 days or 60 days from date of sale(as prescribed in art. 127 of Limitation Act)? - It is very clear from statement of objects and reasons, that period u/art. 127 of 1963 Act was extended from 30 days to 60 days in order to give more time to persons to make deposits - View expressed in P K Unni case 1990 Indlaw SC 718, that O. 21, r. 92(2) CPC, 1908 prescribes a period of limitation for making a deposit is not correct - Held, an application can be made within 60 days and, as no period for making a deposit is prescribed under O. 21, r. 92(2), CPC,1908 deposit can also be made within 60 days - Appeal dismissed.
