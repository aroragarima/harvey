Pares Nath Thakur v Smt. Mohani Dasi and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 May 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 122; AIR 1959 SC 1204; 1960 (26) CutLT 1; 1961 (1) KerLR 252; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 31; 1961 (1) SCJ 181; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 271

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 100 and 101 and O. 21, r.63 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 101, 102, 103 and 104 - (A) Whether High Court is competent to go beyond findings of fact recorded by two courts of fact (i.e.in second appeal) or not? - Held, however erroneous findings of fact recorded by courts of fact may be, HC cannot go into questions of fact in second appeal - U/ss. 100 and 101 of CPC, 1908 - Decision of reversing concurrent findings of fact of two courts below is based upon inferences drawn from evidence, oral and documentary - Even when it is an inference from other facts found on evidence is not a question of law, HC was not entitled to do this - Findings must be held to be unsound - (B) Whether onus of proof has lost its importance when evidence is adduced by both parties or not? - Further held, emphasis on onus of proof is laid with a view to examine whether that onus had been discharged by contesting party, deity - Both parties adducing their evidence result in losing importance of onus of proof - (C) Whether burden of proof is heavy on plaintiff who sues for declaration that registered documents are fictitious? - Further held, yes - Burden of proof is heavy on a plaintiff who sues for a declaration of a document solemnly executed and registered, as a fictitious transaction - Burden becomes doubly heavy when plaintiff seeks to set aside order of civil court, passed in execution proceedings, upholding claim of a third party to a property sought to be proceeded against in execution - (D) whether trust deed was result of a fictitious transaction is a question of fact or law? - Further held, such a question is essentially one of fact - Finding of fact, even when it is an inference from other facts found on evidence, is not a question of law, except in certain specified cases - Appeal allowed.
