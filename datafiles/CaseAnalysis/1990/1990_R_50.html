Raj Duggal v Ramesh Kumar Bansal

Supreme Court of India

06 August 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 254; (1991) Supp1 SCC 191; AIR 1990 SC 2218; 1991 (1) GLH 138; 2008(16) SCALE 166

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Denied, Challenged, Leave to defend

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 37 r. 2 - Leave to defend - Denied - Challenged - Appellant filed instant appeal against the order of HC,, who affirmed the order of Trial Court - Trial Court dismissed the leave to defend suit under O. 37 r. 2 CPC.

Held, if there is triable issue in sense that there is a fair dispute to be tried as to the meaning of document on which the claim is based or uncertainty as to amount actually due or where the alleged facts are of such a nature as to entitle defendant to interrogate the plaintiff or to cross-examine his witnesses leave should not be denied. Where also, defendant shows that even on a fair probability he was a bona fide defence, he ought to have leave. Summary judgments under O. 37 should not be granted where serious conflict as to matter of fact or where any difficulty on issues as to law arises. The court should not reject the defence of the defendant merely because of its inherent implausibility or its inconsistency. A triable issue arises. On the analogous provisions of O. 14 of the Rules of SC in England it was held that where the defence can be described as more than 'shadowy' but less than 'probable' leave to defend should be given.

Thus, appellant is granted leave to defend the suit on condition that amount of Rs. 20,000 deposited in instant case pursuant to the earlier orders made in proceeding shall, during pendency of the suit, be paid to and retained by respondent subject to the condition that if ultimately respondent fails in his suit he shall be liable to restitute the said sum of Rs. 20,000 to the appellant defendant with interest thereon at 9% per annum. Hence, order of HC and of Trial court are both set aside and leave to defend granted on aforesaid terms. Appeal allowed.
