Jacsons Thevara v Collector of Customs and Central Excise

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 11; (1991) 2 SCC 62; AIR 1991 SC 647; 1991 (32) ECC 89; 1991 (33) ECR 225; 1992 (61) E.L.T. 343; JT 1991 (1) SC 309; 1991(1) SCALE 137; 1991 (2) ShimLC 85; 1991 (1) UJ 544

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Indirect Taxes - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 28, 111(o) and 112 - Customs Tariff Act, 1975, sch. 1, chap. 84, Heading No. 84 66 - Custom Duty - (A) Reassessment - Levy of concessional duty on machinery imported for substantial expansion of industrial unit - Firm's business transferred to company and importer agreeing to transfer of machinery to company - But this fact suppressed at time of clearance -(B) Expression 'exempted' u/s. 111(O) - Covers case of partial exemption - Failure of importer to observe condition for benefit of concessional rate - Machainery can be reassessed to collect short levy of duty - Goods liable to confiscation u/s. 111(o) - Appeal dismissed.
