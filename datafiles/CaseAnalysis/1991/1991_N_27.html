Chander Mohan Khanna v National Council Of Educational Research Andtraining And Ors

Supreme Court of India

17 September 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 820; (1991) 4 SCC 578; AIR 1992 SC 76; AIR 1991 SCW 2749; 1992 (19) ATC 71; 1993 (66) FLR 967; JT 1991 (4) SC 233; 1992 (1) LLJ 331; 1992 (1) LLN 16; 1991(2) SCALE 773; 1992 (1) SLJ 17; 1991 (6) SLR 68; [1991] Supp1 S.C.R. 165; 1991 (2) UJ 747; 1992 (1) UPLBEC 18

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Education Norms

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 12 - 'State' - National Council of Educational Research and Training - Activities comprising undertaking of programmes and activities connected with co-ordination of research services and training - (A) Whether related to Govt. functions? - (B) Does Govt. control? - (C) Whether Organisation State? - Held, (A) Not wholly related to Govt. functions - (B) Govt. control confined only to proper utilisation of Govt. grant one of sources of income - (C) Organisation is not State - Petition dismissed.
