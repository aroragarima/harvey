Scientific Adviser To The Ministry Of Defenceand Ors. Etc. v S. Daniel And Ors. Etc. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, K.N. Saikia

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 864; (1990) Supp SCC 374; (1991) SCC (L&S) 355; 1991 (15) ATC 799; 1990 (2) CLR 80; JT 1990 (2) SC 544; 1990 (2) LLJ 295; 1990 (2) LLN 303; 1990(1) SCALE 731; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 440; 1991 (3) SLJ 29; 1990 (2) SLR 724; 1990 (1) UPLBEC 617

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Railway Establishment Code Rules, Railway Servants (Discipline and Appeal) Rules 1968, Central Civil Service (CCA) Rules 1965

Summary: Service - Administrative - Practice & Procedure - General Clauses Act,1897 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(1) - Central Civil Service (CCA) Rules, 1965, rr. 9(1),12, 12(2) (b),13 - Railway Establishment Code Rules 1985 - Railway Servants (Discipline and Appeal) Rules 19682(1) (a)(c),r.9 - Service - Disciplinary action - Challenged-Respondents were from defence and from railway - Disciplinary proceedings were initiated against respondents by their respective appointing authority which was which was questioned and challenged by respondents as not competent authority - Whether appointing authority could initiate disciplinary proceedings - Held, rr.12 and 13 of the 1965 Rules deals with disciplinary proceedings and disciplinary proceedings were to be instituted by President or by an authority directed by him in terms of r. 12(2) (b) of the 1965 Rules - Appellant was authority and by notification u/r.9(1) authorized director to be appointing authority for class III and Class IV posts in his ministry-r.9(1) empowers both appellant and director as appointing authorities - Coming to railway employees s. 2(1) (a) deals with appointment and s. 2(1) (c) deals with disciplinary authority - As per s.16 of the 1897 Act appointing authority had power to suspend or dismiss but was not authorized to conduct disciplinary proceedings - R.. 12(2) (b) envisages that highest authority would be disciplinary authority and hence disciplinary proceedings by director could not be treated as disciplinary proceedings by appellant - Statute throughout its provisions dealt that disciplinary power was different and separate from power of appointment and appellant delegated only appointment power but not disciplinary power - - As per r.8 only President was appointment authority to class I services u/r.2(a) and if interpretated President should make each and every appointment which was practically impossible -- It would be virtually difficult if disciplinary action had to be taken by superior authority against class III and IV employees and hence rendered necessary delegation of the power of appointment and could not be ignored in matters of disciplinary powers - President had issued notification u/r.12 of the 1965 Rules empowering certain authorities to exercise disciplinary proceedings from date of such notification would be perfectly valid - Stay on any disciplinary proceedings was set aside and would continue with disciplinary proceedings - Orders of Tribunal and HC were set aside and matters remitted back for fresh disposal - Appeals disposed of.
