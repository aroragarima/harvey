Ramvilas (Dead) Thr. Lrs. and another v Karim Khan and another

Supreme Court of India

01 December 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 876; (2017) 1 SCC 789; 2017 (120) ALR 213; 2017 (1) CGLJ 167; 2017 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 224; 2017 (2) MahLJ 686; 2017 (2) MPLJ 87; 2017 (134) RD 530; 2016(12) SCALE 548; [2016] 9 S.C.R. 742

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Transfer Of Property Act, 1882

Summary: Banking & Finance - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 58(e) - Loan repayment - Sale deed security - Validity - Appellants/defendants filed instant appeal challenging impugned order of HC wherein HC confirmed the findings of Courts below that Sale Deed dt. 14-06-1972 executed in favour of appellants was only a mortgage as a security for repayment of loan taken by respondent no. 1/plaintiff.

Held, Sale Deed executed by respondent in instant case does not embody any condition as stipulated in s. 58(e) of the Act. Courts below rightly held that Sale Deed would not constitute a mortgage by conditional sale. On the basis of oral and documentary evidence, Courts below recorded that Sale Deed and Agreement dt. 14-06-1972 having been executed on the same day and both ought to be read together. Courts below have also recorded a concurrent finding of fact that Sale Deed was executed only as a security for the loan and it was never the intention of respondent no. 1 to convey suit property. Since, respondent no. 1 has paid back the loan amount i.e. Rs.6,700/- as was evident from subsequent Agreement dt. 05-06-1974, Courts below rightly recorded the concurrent findings of fact that Sale Deed was not binding on respondent no. 1. Once repayment was made, respondent no. 1 was entitled to the declaration as prayed for. Further, the inadequacy of sale consideration stated in Sale Deed was yet another circumstance to indicate that respondent no. 1 could not have intended to sell the property to appellants. SC do not find any reason to interfere with impugned judgments of Courts below. Appeal dismissed.
