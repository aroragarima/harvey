Chairman, Bhartia Education Society & Anr. v State Of Himachal Pradesh & Ors

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 168; (2011) 4 SCC 527; 2011 (5) Bom.C.R. 241; 2011 (7) MLJ(SC) 324; 2011(3) SCALE 48; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 461

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - National Council for Teacher Education Act, 1993 - Admissions - Validity - A Society runs an Teachers Training Institute (Institute) which was recognized by National Council for Teacher Education (NCTE) by order dated 17.7.2000 for conducting Two-year Junior Basic Training (JBT) course, from the academic session 2000- 2001 - After getting recognition, the Institute applied for affiliation to the Examining Body - The Board granted affiliation to the Institute for the two-year JBT course (2001-2003) for the first year of the two-year course (2001-2002) and for the second year of the two-year course (2002-2003) - The Board however did not grant affiliation for the subsequent JBT courses and refused affiliation - State Government, however granted one time relaxation in regard to students admitted by the Institute for the academic sessions 1999-2001 and 2000-2002 and directed the Board to conduct the examination for those students - The students admitted by the Institute to the two-year JBT Course in the year 1999 filed writ petition praying for a direction to the Board to declare the first year JBT course results of 1999-2001 batch and a further direction to the Board to hold the second year examinations for the students belonging to the 1999-2001 batch - A student admitted by the Institute to the JBT course in the year 2002 filed writ petition seeking a direction to the Board to conduct the examinations for the students admitted for the academic session 2002-2004 - HC, by its common judgment, rejected the prayers in the said petitions - Instant appeals - (A) The students contended that there was some confusion in regard to the eligibility criteria/norms adopted by the state government and the Board, and benefit of the doubt/confusion should be extended to the students - Held, unless the students fulfilled the eligibility requirements stipulated by the Board which was the affiliating and examining authority, their admissions would be invalid and they could not be permitted to take the examination - As the Board found that 92 students did not fulfil the eligibility requirements, it rightly rejected their admission to the course - (B) Whether the admissions made in 1999, before recognition by NCTE, were valid? - Held, NCTE Act came into force on 1.7.1995 and the appointed day under the said Act was stated to be 17.8.1995 - After the appointed day, no institution could commence or offer a course or training in teacher education without recognition by the NCTE and consequently, no student could be admitted to such course or training nor could be permitted to appear in any examination relating to such course or training - In the instant case, Society established and started the Institute after the appointed day - Having regard to the clear provisions of the NCTE Act, before NCTE granted recognition on 17.7.2000, the Institute could not offer the JBT course nor admit any students to such course - Therefore, the admissions made by the Institute in the year 1999 for the academic session 1999-2001 were illegal and irregular and could not be approved, recognised or regularised - Decision of the HC rejecting the prayer of the students admitted in 1999 to regularize their admissions by directing the Board to permit them to appear for the JBT examination conducted by it was correct - (C) Whether HC rightly refused relief to the students admitted to 2002-2004 and 2003-2005 sessions on the ground that the admission of students by the Institute without affiliation to the Examining Body, was illegal and invalid? - It was submitted that having regard to the provisions of s. 14(6) of the NCTE Act, the examining body was bound to grant affiliation to an institution in regard to which recognition was granted by NCTE - Held, purpose of 'recognition' and 'affiliation' were different - The examining body could impose its own requirements in regard to eligibility of students for admission to a course in addition to those prescribed by NCTE - The state government and the examining body may also regulate the manner of admissions - In the instant case, Board had granted affiliation to the Institute for an earlier period and had also granted affiliations for the subsequent period - In view of facts that the students admitted in 2002 and 2003 already completed the course and were permitted by the Board which was the examining and affiliating authority to appear for the examinations, admissions of students to the Institute in the years 2002 and 2003 should be regularized subject to fulfilling the eligibility criteria prescribed by the Board and their results should be declared - Appeals disposed of.
