Roop Chand v Gopi Chand Thelia

Supreme Court of India

29 March 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 546; (1989) 2 SCC 383; AIR 1989 SC 1416; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 73; 1989 (2) RCR(Rent) 391; 1989(1) SCALE 733; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 184; 1989 (2) UJ 201

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Rajasthan Premises (Control) of Rent and Eviction Act ,1950, s. 13(1)(a) - Constitution, art.136 - CPC, 1908, O. 41, r. 27 - Tenancy - Tenant allowed a club to function in leased premises - Name board carrying name of club exhibited - Members gathering everyday and playing indoor games - Club a registered company and having its registered office in leased premises - (A) Whether the tenant parted with the possession and would be liable to be evicted - (B) Whether the the admission of Additional evidence in appeal by special leave in the absence of any satisfactory explanation be permitted - Tenant held to be parted with the possesion of the land ,and given three months time to vacate the premises - Permission to file documents refused - Appeal dismissed.
