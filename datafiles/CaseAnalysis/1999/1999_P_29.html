B. K. Narayana Pillai v Parameswaran Pillai and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 887; (2000) 1 SCC 712; AIR 2000 SC 614; 2000 (38) ALR 338; 2000 (1) CLT 92; JT 1999 (10) SC 61; 2000 (1) KLT 274; 2000 (2) MLJ(SC) 20; 2000 (125) PLR 547; 2000 (1) RCR(Civil) 511; 2000 (1) RCR(Rent) 10; 1999(7) SCALE 463; 1999 (10) SLT 451; [1999] Supp5 S.C.R. 271; 1999 (10) Supreme 229

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Easements

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - S. 60(b), Indian Easements Act, 1882 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - O. 6, r. 17, Code of Civil Procedure - Whether the appellant-defendant can amend the written statement by taking a plea that in case he is not held a lessee, which entitled the benefit of s. 60(b) of the Indian Easements Act, 1882? - Held, yes - Court in A. K. Gupta and Sons Ltd. v. Damodar Valley Corpn. (AIR 1967 SC 961967 AIR(SC) - The general rule, a party is not allowed by amendment to set up a new case or a new cause of action particularly when a suit on new case or cause of action is barred - In Weldon v. Neal ((1887) the amendment allowed even after the expiry of the statutory period of limitation - In Cooke v. Gill ((1873) no material fact could ever be amended - Appeals allowed.
