State of Gujarat v Mohammed Atik and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 April 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1533; (1998) 4 SCC 351; (1998) SCC (Cr) 936; AIR 1998 SC 1686; 1998 (2) CCR 108; 1998 (2) Crimes 92; 1998 CRLJ 2251; 1998 (2) JCC 204; JT 1998 (3) SC 60; 1998 (2) RCR(Criminal) 574; 1998(2) SCALE 633; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 664; 1998 (4) SLT 254; 1998 (3) Supreme 488

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act 1987, s. 15 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 10 and 24 - (A) Whether an usable confessional statement u/s. 15 become unusable merely because case is different or crime is different? - (B) Scope of s. 10 of Evidence Act - Held, does not become unusable - Any statement made by accused after his arrest, cannot fall within ambit of s. 10 and confessional statement of accused who is not alive would not be of any evidentiary use - A post-arrest statement made to a police officer, touching his involvement in conspiracy, would not fall within its ambit - Appeal allowed.
