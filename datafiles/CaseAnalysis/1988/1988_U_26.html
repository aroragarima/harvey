Supreme Court of India

04 August 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 439; (1988) 4 SCC 54; (1988) SCC (Cr) 900; AIR 1988 SC 1768; 1988 (2) CCC 625; 1988 (3) Crimes 513; 1988 CRLJ 1809; 1988 CRLR 736; 1988 CrLR(SC) 736; JT 1988 (3) SC 275; 1988(2) SCALE 301; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 223

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution of India, arts. 32 and 21 - Mandamus - Scope - Enforcement of law - Leaving on discretion of Central Govt. - Rules or guidelines as to - Handcuffing of arrestee in criminal case - Advocates Act brought into force or not - Held, mandamus to enforce statute cannot be issued to Govt. - However, can be issued to consider whether statute provision should be enforced.
