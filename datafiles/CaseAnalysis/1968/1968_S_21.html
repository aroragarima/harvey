Sanghi Jeevaraj Ghewar Chand & Ors. v Secretary, Madras Chillies, Grains Kirana Merchants

Supreme Court of India

16 July 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 107; AIR 1969 SC 530; 1968 (17) FLR 218; 1969 LabIC 854; 1969 (1) LLJ 719; 1969 (2) MLJ(SC) 38; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 366

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Service; Water

Keywords: Public Sector, Available Surplus, Full Bench Formula, Aligarh Muslim University Act, 1920, Bombay Lotteries and Prize Competitions Control and Tax Act, 1948, Prize Competitions Act, 1955, Banking Companies (Legal Practitioners Accounts) Act, 1949

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Bonus - Claim of - Whether in view of non-applicability of Act to establishments, not being factories and which employ less than 20 persons therein, and exemption of employees in an establishment in public sector though employing more than 20 persons, employees in both these establishments could claim bonus? - Held, yes - Though preamble states that Act is to provide for payment of bonus to persons employed in certain establishments and s. 1(3) provides that Act is to apply to factories and every other establishments in which 20 or more persons are employed, but these provisions do not, necessarily mean that Act was not intended to be a comprehensive and exhaustive law dealing with entire subject of bonus and persons to whom it should apply - Though s. 1(3) excludes an establishment other than a factory having less than 20 employees from application of Act, all establishments which are factories irrespective of number of persons employed therein and all establishments which are not factories but are having 20 or more employees are covered by Act - Therefore, only small establishments having less than 20 employees and which are not factories are excluded - Appeals allowed.
