P.R. Metrani v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

15 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 989; (2007) 1 SCC 789; AIR 2007 SC 386; [2006] 287 ITR 209; JT 2006 (10) SC 537; 2006(12) SCALE 429; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 1; [2006] 157 TAXMAN 325

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Assessment, Unexplained Expenditure, Taxation Laws (Amendment) Act, 1975

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 132, 132(4A) - Partnership firm - Assessment - Unexplained expenditure - Instant appeals were filed against judgment and order passed by HC vide which HC has allowed reference answering questions in favor of Revenue and against assessee.

Held, HC have taken correct view in holding that presumption u/s. 132(4A) of the Act is available only in regard to proceedings for search and seizure u/s. 132 of the Act. Such presumption shall not be available for framing regular assessment. HC of Karnataka in impugned judgment has clearly erred in holding to contrary. It may be clarified that though presumption u/s. 132(4A) of the Act is not available to authorities while framing regular assessment but material seized can be used as a piece of evidence in any other proceedings under the Act, all contentions are left open. Order passed by HC is set aside. Orders passed by assessing authorities as well as CIT (Appeals) are vitiated as they have proceeded to frame assessment raising presumption u/s. 132 (4A) of the Act same are set aside and case is remitted back to assessing authority for framing assessment afresh in accordance with law. Assessing authority shall now frame assessment in accordance with law, without being influenced by any of observations made in previous orders or this order. Appeals Allowed.
