B.S. Mathur and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, K.G. Balakrishnan, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1637; (2008) 10 SCC 271; AIR 2009 SC 137; JT 2008 (11) SC 173; 2008 (7) MLJ(SC) 1165; 2008(13) SCALE 300; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 436; 2009 (2) SLR 242; 2008 (3) UPLBEC 2798

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Solicitor General, Year Of Allotment, IAS (Regulation of Seniority) Rules, 1987

Summary: Delhi Higher Judicial Service Rules, 1970 - Petitioners directly recruited Additional District and Sessions Judges - Disputes about inter se seniority and preparation of the final seniority list - Writ petition against order approving the majority report of the Administrative Committee determining the seniority of the petitioners in terms of O.M. dated 03.07.1986 issued by the Department of Personnel and Training applicable to the Central Civil Services - Held, principle of inter se seniority contained in the O.M. of DOPT dated 3.7.1986 cannot supplant statutory Rules for determining the inter se seniority contained in the statutory Rules - Pre-conditions for applicability of the principle contained in the above referred O.M. never existed in the service in question - As specific provisions for determining the inter se seniority of the officers of DHJS have been made in the DHJS Rules, there is no question of taking any aid from any outside Rule as contained in the O.M - In case the seniority of the officers of DHJS is fixed by reference to their date of appointment, then nobody would suffer any injustice - Each and every officer would get due weight age of the service rendered by him or her in the cadre of DHJS - Petitions allowed.
