Arun Kumar Agrawal v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

01 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 723; (2014) 2 SCC 609; 2014 (4) AWC 3405; 2013 (6) Bom.C.R. 530; 2014 (1) CompLJ 39; 2013 (8) MLJ 332; 2013(13) SCALE 442; [2014] 3 S.C.R. 861; 2014 (4) SLR 313

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Securities Exchange Board of India

Keywords: Institutional, Recommend, Asset Management Company, All India Services (Death-Cum-Retirement Benefits) Rules, 1958, Income Tax Appellate Tribunal Members (Recruitment and Conditions of Service) Rules, 1963, IAS (Cadre) Rules, 1954, Unit Trust of India (Transfer of Undertaking and Repeal) Act, 2002, Securities & Exchange Board of India (Terms and Conditions of Service of Chairman and Members) Rules, 1992

Summary: (A) Securities Exchange Board of India - Practice & Procedure - Appointment - Challenged - Respondent no.4 was appointed as Chairman of SEBI - Petitioner challenged respondents appointment - Hence instant petition - Whether appointment of respondent as Chairman of SEBI was legal - Held, respondent No.4 had no role to play in grant of approval of deputation, once he fully disclosed that he had been working as Joint Secretary Banking - Respondent no.4 had no further role to play - It was farfetched submission that whilst respondent No.4 worked as Joint Secretary Banking that he should be said to have over-seen organization of UTI AMC - Petitioner had unnecessarily and without any basis tried to confuse that respondent No.4 would be disqualified for deputation in UTI AMC as he would have been privy to receiving some sensitive information with regard to its functioning - It was not justifiable reason to doubt legality of manner in which respondent No.4 continued to work in UTI AMC since he initially came on deputation in October 2005 - Statement made by respondent No.4 that such higher posts were generally not advertised, should not be said to be misleading or false statement - It was statement setting out general practice of appointments in commercial world on such posts - In affidavit filed by UOI, entire service history of respondent No.4 had been set out from time he joined erstwhile banking division of Department of Economic Affairs (DEA) as Joint Secretary - There was nothing surprising in respondent No.4 accepting post of Chairman, SEBI which carried much lesser emoluments than he enjoyed as Chairman, UTI AMC - Acceptance of lesser salary as Chairman of SEBI should not ipso facto lead to conclusion that he accepted position for purpose of abusing authority of Chairman - Allegations were malicious and without any basis and therefore should not be taken into consideration - Hence, there was no merit in submission of petitioner that post was kept vacant for 17 months to accommodate brother of respondent No.6 - Petition dismissed.

(B) Practice & Procedure - Appointment - PIL - Good faith - Maintainability - Whether instant petition was maintainable - Held, instant petition was not bona fide petition - According to respondents, petitioner had been set up by interested parties - Petitioner had unjustifiably attacked integrity of entire selection process - It was virtually impossible to accept submission that respondent No.6 was able to influence decision making process which involved active participation of ACC, a high powered Search-cum-Section Committee with final approval of Finance Minister and Prime Minister - Proposition was so absurd that allegations with regard to mala fide could have been thrown out at threshold - Respondents have successfully demonstrated how petitioner had cleverly distorted and misinterpreted official documents on virtually each and every issue - Petition did not satisfy test of utmost good faith which was required to maintain public interest litigation - Respondent No.2-SEBI in its affidavit has stated that petitioner was habitual litigant - He files writ petitions against individuals to promote vested interest without any relief to the public at large - Therefore, anxiety for removal of present Chairman of SEBI was not wholly unimaginable - Hence, instant petition was not maintainable for a variety of reasons - Petition dismissed.
