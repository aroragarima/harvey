India Agencies (Regd.), Bangalore v Additional Commissioner of Commercial Taxes

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, S.N. Variava, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1058; (2005) 2 SCC 129; AIR 2005 SC 1594; JT 2005 (1) SC 16; 2005 (1) KLT 327; 2004(10) SCALE 469; 2005 (139) STC 329; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 987

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941, Concessional Rate Of Tax, Sales Tax (Registration and Turnover) Rules, 1957, Central Sales Tax (Karnataka) Rules 1958, Central Sales Tax (Registration and Turnover) (Amendment) Rules 1976, Central Sales Tax (Registration and Turnover) (Amendment) Rules 1974

Summary: [A.] Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 62 - Sales Tax (Registration and Turnover) Rules, 1957- Central Sales Tax (Karnataka) Rules 1958, r. 6(b)(ii) - Central Sales Tax (Registration and Turnover) (Amendment) Rules 1976 - Central Sales Tax (Registration and Turnover) (Amendment) Rules 1974 - Concessional rate of tax on inter-state sales - Whether appellant is entitled for concessional rate of tax u/s. 8(4) without producing original of C-Form? - Held, in order to claim concessional rate of tax, original C-Form has to be attached to return - It is not a mere formality or technicality but is intended to achieve object of preventing forms being mis-used for commission of fraud and collision with a view to evade payment of taxes - Without producing original of C-Form appellant is not entitled for concessional rate of tax.
