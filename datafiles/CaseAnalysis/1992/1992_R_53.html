Sundra Naicka Vadiyar (Dead) By Lrs. and Another v Ramaswami Ayyar (Dead) By His Lrs

Supreme Court of India

07 January 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1027; (1995) Supp4 SCC 534; AIR 1994 SC 532; AIR 1993 SCW 3978

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Two suits for permanent injunction - On second appeal, result of High Court's decision was that R Ayyar's suit O. S. No. 315 of 1973 has been decreed while O. S. No. 239 of 1969 filed by S N Vadiyar against R Ayyar stands dismissed - Whether finding on this question of fact recorded by first appellate court in favour of S N Vadiyar could not have been disturbed by HC in second appeal? - Held, a perusal of impugned judgment of HC shows that there were good reasons for treating finding on question of possession recorded by first two courts to be vitiated - It appears to Court that ignoring some of documents which were vital for deciding question of possession also vitiated finding on question of possession recorded by trial court as well as first appellate court - Court has been taken through discussion of evidence made by HC and Court satisfied that there is no infirmity therein to permit any interference in these appeals - Appeals dismissed.
