Union of India and Others v Mangatu Ram Etc

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Saghir Ahmad, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1168; (1997) 6 SCC 59; AIR 1997 SC 2704; 1997 (2) CLT 481; JT 1997 (5) SC 627; 1997(4) SCALE 356; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 1121; 1997 (6) Supreme 47; 1997 (2) UJ 165

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950 - (A) Whether, the view of the High Court in not making any belting and granting uniform rates of compensation for all the lands is correct principle of law? - Held, the HC has adopt absolutely incorrect principle of law - It is seen that several fake deeds have been filed, in particular as per Ex. BA spoken through PW-3, 16.7 kanals of land were sold for Rs.1, 40, 000/-, it is settled legal position that it is the duty of the court to sit in the arm chair of a willing and prudent purchaser and seek answer to the question - (B)Whether, he would purchase the lands offered for sale with the existing features, at the same market value proposed by the court? - Held ,it is also settled law that though determination involves some guess work, it must have reasonable basis and feats of imagination should be eschewed - It is salutary duty of the court to award reasonable and adequate compensation - (C) Whether, the belting is necessary in the circumstances of these cases? - When a large extent of land under acquisition comprises of lands of several persons and some lands are abutting the main road and some lands are in the interior, the same would nor have the uniforms rate of market value - The HC had not kept this perspective in view in determining the compensation and thereby it had committed manifest error of law warranting interference - Appeals allowed.
