London Rubber Company Limited v Durex Products

Supreme Court of India

04 March 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 386; AIR 1963 SC 1882; 22 PTC 246; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 211

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Intellectual Property - Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Trade Marks Act, 1940, ss. 8(a) and 10(1) - Trade and Merchandise Marks Act, 1958, ss. 12(1) and 11(1) - Trade Marks Act, 1940 - (A) Difference between ss. 8 (a) and 10 (1) of Trade Marks Act 1940? - Held, object of both is to prohibit from registration marks which are likely to deceive or cause confusion - From marks being identical or similar or for some other reason - S. 8(a) is wide enough to cover any circumstance - S. 10(1) is limited to similarity in or resemblance between two marks - Where there is identity or similarity in two marks in respect of same goods or goods of same description registration at instance of another proprietor would be prohibited where a mark is already on register as being property of another proprietor - (B) On whom lies burden to prove that mark applied for registration is mark of proprietor who applies u/s. 11(1)(a)? - Further held, burden is on proprietor who applies - (C) What are duties of Registrar u/s. 8(a) of Act? - Further held, it has to be decided whether reputation is acquired by use of a mark or a name, mark at date of application for registration is used in a normal and fair manner, will not be reasonably likely to cause deception and confusion amongst a substantial number of persons - If used for goods applied in application - Have to decide judicially - With proper reasoning - (D)Whether court has power either to give that language a wider or narrower meaning than literal one? - Further held, yes, but only when other provisions of Act compel it to given such other meaning - It is duty of Court to given full effect to language used by Legislature - (E) Whether question of volume of use is a relevant question in considering 'honest concurrent use of trade mark u/s. 10 (2) of Act? - Further held, yes - Though it is factual - No hard and fast rule - Ordinarily showing commercial use of mark is sufficient - Trade mark is a kind of property and is entitled to protection under law, irrespective of its value in money so long as it has some business or commercial value - (F) What does 'Other special circumstances' u/s. 10(2) means? - Further held, a special circumstance must be connected with use of mark - It includes any circumstances peculiar to application in relation to subject matter of application - Use by an applicant of his mark before conflicting mark was registered or used - Socio economic consideration of user of articles is not a relevant circumstance - Appeal dismissed.
