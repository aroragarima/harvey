Buxa Dooars Tea Company Limited Etc. v State Of West Bengal And Others

Supreme Court of India

12 May 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 717; (1989) 3 SCC 211; (1989) SCC (Tax) 394; AIR 1989 SC 2015; 1989 (2) CalLT(SC) 58; [1989] 179 ITR 91; JT 1989 (2) SC 571; 1989(1) SCALE 1321; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 293; 1989 (74) STC 447; [1990] 48 TAXMAN 314; 1989 (2) UJ 514

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Tea Act, 1953, West Bengal Rural Employment and Production Act 1976, West Bengal Taxation Laws (Amendment) Act, 1981, West Bengal Taxation Laws (Amendment) Act, 1982

Summary: Agriculture & Agri Products - Constitution - West Bengal Rural Employment and Production Act, 1976, ss. 4(1) and 4(2)(aa) - Tea Act,1953,s. 25 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 246, 301 and 304(b) - Rural Employment Cess - Levy of - It is levy in respect of despatches of tea and not in respect of tea estates - Amendment imposing levy not introduced or moved in State legislature with previous consent of President - Constitutionality - Held, it does not satisfy provisions of art. 304(b) - Violative of art. 301 and also void for legislative competence as it pertains to covered field - Petition allowed.
