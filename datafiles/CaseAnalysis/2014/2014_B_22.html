Dinesh Singh v Bajaj Allianz General Insurance Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

23 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

N.V. Ramana, Ranjan Gogoi, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 291; (2014) 9 SCC 241; 2014 (2) ACC 427; 2014 ACJ 1412; 2014 (4) AWC 3881; 2014 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2389; 2015 (2) MahLJ 32; 2015 (2) MPLJ 138; 2014 (175) PLR 536; 2014 (3) RCR(Civil) 123; 2014(5) SCALE 537

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Entitlement of

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicle Act, 1988 - Motor accident claim - Enhancement of compensation - Entitlement of - Appellant/claimant met with an accident and was admitted in hospital - Appellant filed claim petition before Tribunal - Tribunal accepted evidence on record and awarded compensation - Dissatisfied with order passed by Tribunal, appellant as well as respondent filed appeals before HC, appellant seeking enhancement, while respondent for reduction - HC dismissed appeal filed by appellant and partly allowed appeal filed by respondent and reduced compensation awarded by Tribunal - Whether impugned judgment passed by HC was justified -

Held, in view of evidence produced by appellant that he had spent about Rs. 3,10,000/- towards medical expenditure, including conveyance and attendance fee, for period he was under treatment, same needs to be granted, and accordingly was granted same as awarded by Tribunal, and find fault with HC in reducing the same - Appellant was entitled to compensation of Rs. 33,10,160/- - Rs. 1,20,000/- towards pain and agony, Rs. 3,10,000/- towards medical expenditure, including conveyance, attendant fee etc., during the period of treatment, Rs. 3,08,160/- towards loss of income during hospitalization/treatment, Rs. 15,72,000/- towards loss of future income, Rs. 3,50,000/- towards loss of happiness and loss of amenities, Rs. 1,00,000/- towards loss of marriage prospects, Rs. 5,50,000/- towards future medical expenses, total Rs. 33,10,160/- - Above compensation amount should carry interest from date of filing of petition before Tribunal till date of payment - Impugned judgment passed by HC was set aside - Appeals allowed.
