Amar Singh and another v State of Rajasthan and another

Supreme Court of India

03 August 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 580; (2010) 9 SCC 64; AIR 2010 SC 3391; JT 2010 (8) SC 99; 2010 (160) PLR 163; 2011 (1) RLW 29; 2010(7) SCALE 734; [2010] 9 S.C.R. 526; 2010 (3) WLN(SC) 68

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 498A, 304B - Dowry death - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant/husband allegedly caused the death of deceased/wife - Trial Court convicted the appellant u/ss. 498A, 304B IPC - Appellant filed appeal before the HC - HC confirmed the conviction of appellant - Whether conviction of appellant is sustainable - Held, deceased made a statement to the prosecution witnesses within a couple of months before her death that she had been verbally harassed for not bringing adequate dowry - Evidence of prosecution witnesses with regard to the said statement of deceased is an admissible evidence u/s. 32 of Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Prosecution in the present case led sufficient evidence before the Court to raise a presumption that appellant had caused the death of the deceased and it was, therefore, for the appellant to rebut the said presumption - Therefore, Trial Court and HC were right in holding that the appellant was guilty of the offence u/s. 304B IPC, however, appellant was not charged for the offence of murder u/s. 302 IPC presumably because during investigation no materials were available to establish the offence u/s. 302 IPC against the appellant - Since there is no evidence as to the actual role played by the appellant in the death of the deceased, a punishment of ten years' imprisonment would suffice in the ends of justice - Hence, sentence of life imprisonment imposed on the appellant u/s. 304B IPC is reduced to ten years and the impugned judgment of the HC is modified accordingly - Appeal partly allowed.
