State Of West Bengal v Ashok Dey And Others

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, S.M. Sikri, J.M. Shelat, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 287; (1972) SCC (Cr) 128; (1972) 1 SCC 199; AIR 1972 SC 1660; 1972 CRLJ 1010; [1972] 2 S.C.R. 434

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Parliament & Legislature - Constitution - Arms Act, 1959 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19, 22, 132, 246 and 254 - West Bengal (Prevention of Violent Activities) Act, 1970, ss. 2, 3 and 13 - Whether art. 22(7) of Constitution, 1950 does not confer exclusive jurisdiction on Parliament to make a law for valid detention of persons for a period longer than three months and that State Legislature is fully competent, to make laws for detention? - Held, art. 22(7) merely invests Parliament with an overriding power and does not prohibit State Legislature from making a law either providing for preventive detention for a longer period than three month, when there is a provision for securing opinion of an Advisory Board - Appeals allowed.
