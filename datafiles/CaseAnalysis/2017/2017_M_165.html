Mukund Dewangan v Oriental Insurance Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, Amitava Roy, Sanjay Kishan Kaul

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 846; 2017 ACJ 2011; 2017 (4) AWC 4188; 2017 (3) KLT 1000; 2017 (6) MLJ 326

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Jurisdiction, Companies Act, 1956, Period, Motor Vehicle, Insurance Policy, Carrier, Insurance Company, Insurer, Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, Licensing, Central Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989, Secretary, Renewal, Passenger, Valid Licence, Goods Carriage, Licensed Driver, Light Motor Vehicle, Motor Vehicles Act, 1957, Karnataka Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1957, Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, Motor Vehicles Act, 2007

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988-Sections 2, 3 and 10-Central Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989-Rule 8-Driving licence-Reference of question-Whether a driver who is having a licence to drive 'light motor vehicle' and is driving 'transport vehicle' of that class is required additionally to obtain an endorsement to drive a 'transport vehicle'-Answered-'Light motor vehicle' as defined under Section 2 (21) of Act includes transport vehicle as per weight prescribed-Such transport vehicles not excluded from definition of 'light motor vehicle' by virtue of Amendment Act No. 54 of 1994-Licence issued under Section 10 (2) (d) continues to be valid after Amendment Act 54 of 1994-Effect of amendment of Form 4 by insertion of "transport vehicle" is related only to categories substituted in year 1994 and procedure to obtain driving licence for transport vehicle of class of 'light motor vehicle' continues to be same as it was not changed-There was no requirement to obtain separate endorsement to drive transport vehicle-If driver is holding licence to drive light motor vehicle, he can drive transport vehicle of such class without any endorsement to that effect-Matters were to be placed for hearing on merits before appropriate Bench.
