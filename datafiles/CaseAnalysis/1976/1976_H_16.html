State of Haryana v Haryana Co-Operative Transport Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 December 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 410; (1977) SCC (L&S) 113; (1977) 1 SCC 271; AIR 1977 SC 237; 1976 (33) FLR 413; 1977 LabIC 32; 1977 (1) LLN 31; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 306; 1977 (1) SLR 247; 1977 UJ 25

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 7(3)(d) and 9(1) - Scope and extent or jurisdiction u/arts. 226 and 227 - Maintainability of petition to set aside award of Labour Court - On the ground of non-qualification of presiding Officer to hold post of Judge u/s.7 (3) (d) 1947 Act - Also considering whether arts. 226 and 227 are whittled down by s.9(1) of Industrial Dispute Act - The challenge to appointement was made directly in a substantive proceeding and not collaterally - Held, the rights conferred by arts 226 and 227 can be abridged or taken away only by an appropriate amendment of the Constitution and their operation cannot be whittled down by a provision like the one contained in s. 9 (1) of the Act - It is open to the High Court in the exercise of their writ jurisdiction to consider the validity of appointment of any person as a chairman or a member of a Board or court or as a presiding officer of a Labour Court, Tribunal, or National Tribunal - Appeal dismissed.
