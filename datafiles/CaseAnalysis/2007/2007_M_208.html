Markfed Vanaspati And Allied Industries v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

14 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 897; (2007) 7 SCC 679; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 882; 2007(6) ALL MR 469; 2007 (4) AWC 4046; JT 2007 (11) SC 141; 2007 (6) MLJ(SC) 1490; 2007 (4) RCR(Civil) 358; 2007(11) SCALE 138; 2007 (7) SCJ 349; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 957

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Union of India, issued tender dated 29th June, 1989 for purchase of oil - Appellant offered to supply 1600 metric tons of different categories of oil - Appellant failed to supply the oil as per the delivery schedule - Appellant sought the benefit of the force majeure clause because the government had banned the use and process of rapeseed oil by manufacturers - Held, ban was imposed for the use of rapeseed oil for manufacturing Vanaspati but manufacturing of rapeseed oil was not debarred or restricted - Plea of force majeure clause taken by the appellant was found to be totally devoid of any merit - Award has been made rule of the court by Single Judge of the High Court and the findings of learned Single Judge have been affirmed by the Division Bench - Scope of interference in a non-speaking award is extremely limited - Court cannot probe into the mental process of the arbitrator - Appeal dismissed.
