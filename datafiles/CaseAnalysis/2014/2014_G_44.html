Hyder Consulting (UK) Limited v Governor, State of Orissa Through Chief Engineer

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, Sharad Arvind Bobde, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 799; (2015) 2 SCC 189; AIR 2015 SC 856; 2014 (4) CLT(SC) 373; JT 2014 (11) SC 575; 2015 (4) MahLJ 7; 2015 (3) MPLJ 232; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 165

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Awarding of interest on interest from the date of award, Power of Arbitral Tribunal, Grant of interest on interest

Summary: H. L. Dattu, J :

Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 31(7), 31(7)(a), 31(7)(b), 36 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 142 - Awarding of interest on interest from the date of award - Whether in light of the U.P. Cooperative Federation Limited v. Three Circles 2009 Indlaw SC 2050, and Mcdermott International Inc v. Burn Standard Company Limited and Others, 2006 Indlaw SC 1268, there exists any infirmity in the decision rendered by SC in the State of Haryana and others v. S. L. Arora and Company 2010 Indlaw SC 416 and whether s. 32(7)(b) of 1996 Act could be interpreted to include interest pendente lite within the sum payable as per the arbitral award, for the purposes of awarding post-award interest.

Held, decision in the McDermott case would not be applicable, since it does not pertain to the issue of granting compound interest on the post-award claim. SC, in McDermott case, did not consider the issue pertaining to award of 'interest upon interest' or compound interest. It merely held that the interest must be awarded on the principal amount upto the date of award. Thus, McDermott case would be wholly inapplicable to the issue for consideration by this Bench. The decision of Three Circles case did not place reliance on the ratio decidendi of the McDermott case but merely re-stated the contention raised by respondent therein to decide upon the issue of 'interest on interest' or compound interest. Therefore, the Three Cirlces case would be deemed as per incuriam in regard to the concept of awarding 'interest on interest' or compound interest, due to such an inadvertent erroneous reliance upon the McDermott case. The interest u/cl. 31(7)(b) of 1996 Act would be imposed on money awarded by the Arbitral Tribunal on the basis of the claims of the parties, and the said money cannot merge within it any interest as imposed in the period from the date of cause of action to the date of the award. Hence, there is no infirmity with the S.L. Arora case, whereby it was held that if the arbitral award is silent about interest from the date of award till the date of payment, the person in whose favour the award is made will be entitled to interest at 18% p.a on the principal amount awarded, from the date of award till the date of payment. Matter is remanded back to an appropriate Two-Judge Bench of SC for adjudication. State of Haryana and others v. S. L. Arora and Company, 2010 Indlaw SC 416, affirmed. Order accordingly.

S. A. Bobde, J.

Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 31(7), 31(7)(a), 31(7)(b), 36 - Power of Arbitral Tribunal - Awarding of interest on interest from the date of award.

Held, the significant words occurring in cl. (a) of s. 31(7) of 1996 Act are 'the sum for which the award is made'. Parliament has not added a qualification like 'principal' to the word 'sum,' and therefore, the word 'sum' here simply means 'a particular amount of money'. In s. 31(7) of 1996 Act, this particular amount of money may include interest from the date of cause of action to the date of the award. It is clear that the interest, the sum directed to be paid by the Arbitral Award u/cl. (b) of s. 31(7) of 1996 Act is inclusive of interest pendent lite. Thus, it is apparent that vide cl. (a) of s. 31(7) of 1996 Act, Parliament intended that an award for payment of money may be inclusive of interest, and the 'sum' of the principal amount plus interest may be directed to be paid by the Arbitral Tribunal for the pre-award period. Thereupon, the Arbitral Tribunal may direct interest to be paid on such 'sum' for the post-award period vide cl. (b) of s. 31(7) of 1996 Act, at which stage the amount would be the sum arrived at after the merging of interest with the principal; the two components having lost their separate identities. S.L. Arora case is wrongly decided in that it holds that a sum directed to be paid by an Arbitral Tribunal and the reference to the Award on the substantive claim does not refer to interest pendente lite awarded on the 'sum directed to be paid upon Award' and that in the absence of any provision of interest upon interest in the contract, the Arbitral Tribunal does not have the power to award interest upon interest, or compound interest either for the pre-award period or for the post-award period. Parliament has the undoubted power to legislate on the subject and provide that the Arbitral Tribunal may award interest on the sum directed to be paid by the Award, meaning a sum inclusive of principal sum adjudged and the interest, and this has been done by Parliament in plain language. State of Haryana and others v. S. L. Arora and Company, 2010 Indlaw SC 416, dissented. Order accordingly.

Abhay Manohar Sapre, J.

Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 31(7), 31(7)(a) - Arbitration award - Grant of interest on interest - Legality - Whether grant of interest by the Arbitral Tribunal u/s. 31(7) of the Act amounts to granting 'interest on interest'.

Held, for the purposes of an award, there is no distinction between a 'sum' with interest, and a 'sum' without interest. Once the interest is 'included in the sum' for which the award is made, the original sum and the interest component cannot be segregated and be seen independent of each other. Thus, in a pre-award stage, the legislation seeks to make no distinction between the sum award and the interest component in it. Therefore, the amount award u/s. 31(7)(a) of 1996 Act, whether with interest or without interest, constitutes a 'sum' for which the award is made. The expression 'grant of interest on interest' while exercising the power u/s. 31(7) of 1996 Act does not arise and, therefore, the Arbitral Tribunal is well empowered to grant interest even in the absence of clause in the contract for grant of interest. State of Haryana and others v. S. L. Arora and Company, 2010 Indlaw SC 416, dissented. Order accordingly.
