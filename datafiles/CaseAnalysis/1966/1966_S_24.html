Gordon woodroffe and Company v Sheikh M. A. Majid and Company

Supreme Court of India

22 March 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Ramaswami, K. Subba Rao, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 283; AIR 1967 SC 181; [1966] Supp S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Sale of Goods Act, 1930, ss. 4, 9, 11 and 12 - Contract Act, 1872, ss. 182 and 213 - Respondent a trader at Madras in hides and skins - 'Settled account' - Appellant a firm, doing business among other things as exporters of hides and skins - Case of defendants was that there was an outright purchase of the goods from the plaintiff for the purpose of resale in the United Kingdom (A) Distinction between Sale and Agency - Held, essence of sale is transfer of title to goods for price paid or to be paid - Transferee in such case becomes liable to transferor of goods as a debtor for price to be paid and not as agent for proceeds of sale - While essence of agency to sell is delivery of goods to a person who is to sell them, not as his own property but as the property of principal who continues to be owner of goods and who is therefore liable to account for proceeds - (B)Whether defendants were acting as del credere agents of the plaintiff or whether the defendants were outright purchasers of the goods supplied to them by the plaintiff - Held, defendants were purchasers of the plaintiffs goods under several contracts and not his agents for sale and the view taken by High Court on this aspect of the case is not correct and must be overruled - (C) Whether there was a settled account between the parties and whether it is open to the plaintiff to reopen it? - Held,legal position is that accounts are settled or stated if they are submitted and accepted as correct by other side to whom accounts have been rendered - Such a statement of accounts need not be in writing, nor is it necessary that before the accounts are settled, they should be gone into by parties and scrutinised and supported by vouchers - It is sufficient if the accounts are accepted and such acceptance may be inferred by conduct of parties - In the present case, the "settled account" "between the parties" falls within the second kind of "account stated" and it is to an account of this description that the equitable doctrine of "settled account" has to be considered - Further held, Trial Court has rejected the claim of plaintiff for reopening the accounts on any other ground and in view of this finding that the legal relationship between the parties was not one of agency, there is no reason for interfering with decision of the Trial Court by the Apex Court on this aspect of the case - Appeal allowed.
