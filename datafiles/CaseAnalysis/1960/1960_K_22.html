State of Bihar v Kripa Shankar Jaiswal

Supreme Court of India

14 October 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 247; AIR 1961 SC 304; 1961 CRLJ 447; 1961 (2) FLR 321; 1961 (1) LLJ 334; 1961 (1) SCJ 291; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Trade Union Act, 1926, Trade Unions Act

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 4, 18(3)(a), (d) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Terms of settlement - Noncompliance - Conviction - Maintainability - There were certain disputes between workmen and Management of Distillery, of which proprietor was respondent/accused - Settlement of dispute was arrived at and was signed by proprietor, manager of Distillery and by general secretary of workers Union u/s. 4 of the Act - Complaint was filed before Magistrate upon which respondent and manager of Distillery were convicted and sentenced for not carrying out terms of settlement - Respondent filed appeal before appellate court, which was dismissed - HC set aside order of conviction and acquitted respondent - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned order of HC was justified - Held, it would be erroneous view if it were said that for dispute to constitute industrial dispute it was requisite condition that it should be sponsored by recognised union or that all workmen of industrial establishment should be parties to it - Dispute became industrial dispute even where it was sponsored by union which was not registered as in instant case or where dispute raised was by some only of workmen because in either case matter fell within s. 18(3)(a)(d) of the Act - Further, after case was decided by Judicial Magistrate parties arrived at fresh settlement on 6-10-1956, as result of this out of discharged workmen 25, whose names were given in appendix attached to compromise, were reinstated w.e.f from 8-10-1956 and claim with regard to other discharged workmen was withdrawn - Further, this settlement was accepted by Industrial Tribunal by order dt. 10-10-1956, this showed that all disputes between parties have been settled and workmen were reinstated - State of Bihar v. Hiralal Kejrilal, 1959 Indlaw SC 106, relied on, 'public interest did not require that stale matter should be resuscitated' therefore, SC did not interfere u/art. 136 of the Constitution with order of HC - Appeal dismissed.
