Commissioner, Bangalore Development Authority v K.S. Narayan

Supreme Court of India

11 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1005; (2006) 8 SCC 336; AIR 2006 SC 3379; 2007 (7) AWC 6788; JT 2006 (9) SC 27; 2006(10) SCALE 163; 2006 (8) SCJ 1; [2006] Supp7 S.C.R. 186

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6 and 9 (the Act) - Bangalore Development Authority Act, 1976, ss. 17 and 19 (the Bangalore Act) - Notice - Appeal from decree passed by High Court granting relief for declaration of title and for possession of property as sought by Plaintiff - Whether sufficient notice in terms of s. 17 was served on plaintiff if so required by Bangalore Act? - Held, plaintiff purchased property more than one year after notification u/s. 17 was published - There was no occasion for serving any notice upon plaintiffs as required by s. 17(5) as their names could not have appeared in assessment list of Local Authority or in land revenue register at relevant time - Whole basis on which HC held acquisition proceedings to be invalid is erroneous and cannot be sustained - Question whether a civil suit is maintainable to challenge acquisition proceedings - No longer res integra - Civil Court cannot go into question of validity or otherwise of notifications issued u/ss. 4(1) and 6 of Act following the decision of State of Bihar v. Dhirendra Kumar (1995 Indlaw SC 1070) and Laxmi Chand v. Gram Panchayat, Kararia (1995 Indlaw SC 767) - Ground for assailing notification, that notice u/s. 17(5) was not served upon plaintiffs and its effect, could only be examined in a WP filed u/art. 226 before HC and not by Civil Court - Judgments and decrees passed by HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
