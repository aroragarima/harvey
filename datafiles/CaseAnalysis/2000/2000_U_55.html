Union Public Service Commission v Jagannath Mishra

Supreme Court of India

09 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3886; (2003) 9 SCC 237; (2003) SCC (L&S) 1178

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - Mal practice in exam - Punishment of debarment - Examiner while examining answer papers reported to appellant that respondent was suspected to have copied from another person - Appellant punished respondent debarring to appear in any competitive examination for 10 years - Respondent moved Tribunal in which order of punishment was set aside - Appellant filed petition before HC in which HC dismissed petition - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether it was principal that if invigilator not reported for adoption of malpractice in examination there was no mal practice - Held, it was true that there was no report from invigilator indicating any malpractice by respondent - Mere absence of such report would not be sufficient to exonerate delinquency - Tribunal and HC committed serious error by giving extra weightage for absence of any report from invigilator - It was not held as principle that wherever there was no report from invigilator indicating adoption of malpractice in any examination appropriate authority could not come to conclusion about adoption of malpractice - Decision of Tribunal and HC was set aside but directed that debarment in question would be for period of 5 years - Appeal allowed.
