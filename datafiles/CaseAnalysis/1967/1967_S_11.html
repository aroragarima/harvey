S. Sanyal v Gian Chand

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 421; AIR 1968 SC 438; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 536

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Ejection - Delhi and Ajmer Rent Control Act, 1952, ss. 2(g) and 13(1)(e) - Appellant, a tenant of a house, a part of which is used for a Girls' School and rest for residential purposes - Respondent purchased the house from owner by a sale deed and filed a suit for decree in ejectment - Trial Court dismissed suit and thereafter appeal was dismissed from that order holding that house being let for purposes non-residential as well as residential, a decree in ejectment could not be granted u/s. 13(1)(e) of 1952 Act - High Court in revision petition filed by respondent held a decree in ejectment limited to that portion of house which was used for residential' purposes by tenant could be granted, and remanded case to Rent Controller - Appeal filed by tenant against order of HC - Held, letting not being solely for residential purposes, further, Court had no jurisdiction to pass the order appealed from - Therefore, order passed by HC remanding case for determination, of the residential portion of the house occupied by appellant and for passing a decree in ejectment in respect of that part is without jurisdiction and must be set aside - Appeal allowed.
