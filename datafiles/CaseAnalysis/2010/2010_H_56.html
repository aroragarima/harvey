Rafeeqan (Dead) by Lr. v Hussan Bano

Supreme Court of India

06 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 708; (2010) 9 SCC 23; AIR 2010 SC 3318; 2010 (82) ALR 779; JT 2010 (9) SC 257; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 741; 2010 (2) RCR(Rent) 429; 2010(9) SCALE 33

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Practice & Procedure - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, s. 14D - Eviction u/s. 14D - Legality - Respondent/landlord filed an eviction petition u/s. 14D against appellant/tenant to recover the possession of demised premises for her own residential purposes - Respondent contended that since she was a widow, she was entitled to the recovery of possession of demised premises u/s. 14D - Although appellant failed to appear before the Rent Control Court (RCC), RCC dismissed the respondent's petition on the ground that the tenanted premises was let out by the previous owner from whom the respondent purchased the tenanted premises - RCC, further, held that demised premises was not let out either by the respondent or by her husband or by any of her blood relations, therefore, s. 14D would not be applicable to the present case - Respondent challenged the RCC order before the HC - HC, by impugned order, set aside the RCC order and directed the RCC to decide the eviction petition on merits - Hence the present petition challenging the HC order - Appellant contended that impugned HC order was erroneous in view of the Constitution Bench decision of SC in Nathi Devi vs. Radha Devi Gupta, 2004 INDLAW SC 1066 - Whether impugned HC order is valid in law - Held, SC, in Nathi Devi, held that a widow or her late husband who acquired a tenanted premises by sale or transfer cannot invoke the provisions of s. 14D to evict a pre- existing tenant - In view of the Constitution Bench decision of SC, impugned HC order was clearly erroneous - Constitution Bench decision in Nathi Devi, in view of the mandate of art. 141 of the Constitution of India, is binding on all subordinate Courts and HCs and the rights of the parties in a pending proceeding u/s. 14D of the Act must be governed by the law laid down in Nathi Devi case - Since respondent was admittedly a pre-existing tenant on the premises prior to the purchase of the property by the husband of the respondent/landlord, s. 14-D was not applicable for eviction of the appellant in the present case - Impugned HC order is set aside - Appeal allowed.
