Shri Gopal & Anr. v Subhash & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 65; (2004) 13 SCC 174; (2005) SCC (Cr) 98; AIR 2004 SC 4900; 2004 CRLJ 3349; JT 2004 (2) SC 158; 2004(2) SCALE 104; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 1085

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Ballistics

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 302 and 148 r/w 149 - Arms Act, s. 27 - Common object - Unlawful assembly - Whether there was common object on part of respondents in committing the alleged offence? - Object should be common to the persons who compose the assembly, that is, that they should all be aware of it and concur in it - Certain discrepancies in the prosecution case pointing that the view taken by the High Court as regard doubtful participation of the respondents herein is a possible view and once it is held that the prosecution case as regard material aspects relating to implication of certain accused persons in doubtful, it would not be proper for this Court to interfere with the judgment of the High Court even if a different view is possible to be taken - Impugned judgement upheld - Appeal dismissed.
