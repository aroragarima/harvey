Shailendra Bhardwaj & Others v Chandra Pal & Another

Supreme Court of India

21 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 470; (2013) 1 SCC 579; 2013 (97) ALR 673; 2013 (1) AWC 690; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 479; JT 2012 (11) SC 448; 2012 (8) MLJ(SC) 676; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 325; 2013 (118) RD 310; 2012(11) SCALE 162; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 1124

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Court Fee Act, 1870, ss. 7(iii), Schedule II - Uttar Pradesh Amendment Act, 1938, 7 (iv-A) Court Fee - Suit valuation - Plaintiffs/appellants suit property was valued and cost of the property was fixed and Court fee was paid - Trial Court decreed in favour of plaintiffs - On appeal HC affirmed order of Trial Court and dismissed appeal - Plaintiffs contended that Courts below committed error in holding that suit be valued and ad valorem court fee be paid u/s. 7 (iv-A) of the 1938 Act - Plaintiff had correctly valued suit and proper court fee was paid in accordance with art. 17(iii) of Schedule II of the 1870 Act - Defendants/respondent contended that HC rightly concluded that even though no consequential reliefs was prayed for as per 1938, Act, plaintiff should pay court fee u/s. 7 (iv-A) of the 1938 Act and that plaintiff had valued suit without noticing fact that State of U.P. had amended the 1870 Act by Act 19 of 1938 and in terms of s. 7 (iv-A) of the 1938 Act, the court fee had to be commuted according to value of the subject matter and an ad valorem court fee had to be paid - Hence instant appeal - Held, suit property covered by will had to be valued - Since s. 7 (iv-A) of the 1938 Act provided that payment of court fee in case where suit was for or involved in cancellation or adjudging/declaring null and void decree for money or instrument, art. 17 (iii) of Schedule II of the 1870 Act would not apply - Therefore 1938 Act was applicable in instant case despite the fact that no consequential relief was claimed - Court fees had to be commuted according to value of the subject matter and Trial Court and HC had correctly held - Therefore plaintiff had not noticed fact that article stood amended by State - Since s. 7 (iv-A) of the 1938 Act specifically provides for payment of court fee in case where the suit was for or involving cancellation or adjudging/declaring void or voidable an instrument securing property having money value art. 17 (iii) of Schedule II of the 1870 Act should not be applicable - There was no merit in instant appeal - Appeal dismissed.
