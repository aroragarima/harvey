Ramesh Baburao Devaskar and Others v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1025; (2007) 13 SCC 501; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1606; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 293; 2008 CRLJ 372; 2007 (4) JCC 3266; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 671; 2007(12) SCALE 272; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 197

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ante-Dated, Chance Witness, Self Inflicted

Summary: Murder - Appeal against conviction - Whether second First Information Report can be treated to be the First Information Report in relation to the incident and in any event any reliance can be placed thereupon? - Held, only information in regard to commission of an offence may not for all intent and purport satisfy the requirement of the First Information Report - When, however, the First Information Report is lodged by a person who claims himself to be aware of not only the commission of the offence, the name of the deceased and at least one of the accused who had committed the same, the could have been recorded on the basis thereof - A First Information Report cannot be lodged in a murder case after the inquest has been held - Proof of motive by itself may not be a ground to hold the accused guilty - Appeals allowed.
