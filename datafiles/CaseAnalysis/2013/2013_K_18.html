Kaini Rajan v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 619; (2013) 9 SCC 113; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 3721; 2013 CRLJ 4888; 2013 (4) JCC 2358; JT 2013 (12) SC 538; 2013 (4) MLJ(Crl) 62; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 365; 2013(11) SCALE 606; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 196

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Acquaintance

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 375, 376, 417 and 90 - Rape - Conviction - Challenged - Prosecution alleged that accused committed rape on PW2, without her consent and when she tried to make a hue and cry, she was silenced by the accused by stating that he would marry her - Further submitted that even after this incident, accused had sexual relationship with her on more than one occasions - PW2, later, became pregnant and gave birth to a boy - Accused not only not kept his promise to marry her, but even disputed the paternity of the child - Trial Court convicted the accused u/s. 376 IPC and sentenced to him undergo rigorous imprisonment for 7 years, together with a fine of Rs.25,000/- with default clause - On appeal, HC upheld the order of conviction and sentence awarded by the Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by the Trial Court as affirmed by the HC could be upheld - Held, impugned incident alleged to have occurred in day light and at a place near the compound, not within the four walls of a house or a building - Accused was not a stranger - PW2 had previous acquaintance with the accused or else in all probability she would have resisted forcefully, attracting passersby or people from the neighbourhood - Moreover, FIR was lodged after a period of 10 months from the date of incident - All these factors cast some shadow of doubt on the version of PW2 - Version of victim, in rape commands great respect and acceptability, but, if there were some circumstances which cast some doubt in the mind of the court of the veracity of the victim's evidence, then, it was not safe to rely on the uncorroborated version of the victim of rape - Trial Court as well as the HC had committed an error in holding that the accused was guilty of the offence punishable u/s. 376 IPC - Appeal allowed.
