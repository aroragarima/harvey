Workmen of Motipur Sugar Factory Private LIMITED v Motipur Sugar Factory

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 127; AIR 1965 SC 1803; 1965 (11) FLR 112; 1965 (2) LLJ 162; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 588

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938 - Factories Act, 1948, ss. 55, 64 (2)(d), 55(1), 55(2) - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10 and 33 - (A) Whether scope of reference mandated Tribunal to investigate into reason for discharge? - Held, question referred was wide and general in terms and asked Tribunal to decide whether discharge of workmen was justified or not - It was for Tribunal to investigate grounds on which discharge was based and decide whether those grounds justified discharge or not - If Tribunal found that discharge was due to use of go-slow tactics by workmen it would be entitled to investigate question whether use of go-slow tactics by workmen had been proved or not - (B) Whether a tribunal has power to investigate into matters to ascertain whether domestic enquiry has been conducted before dismissal of employee? - Further held, it will have jurisdiction not only to go into limited questions open to a Tribunal where domestic enquiry has been properly - But also to satisfy itself on facts adduced before it by employer whether dismissal or discharge was justified - In principle there is no difference whether matter comes before Tribunal for approval u/s. 33 or on a reference u/s. 10 - Appeal dismissed.
