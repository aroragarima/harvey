Pokar Ram v State of Rajasthan and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, A.P. Sen, V.D. Tulzapurkar

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 46; (1985) SCC (Cr) 297; (1985) 2 SCC 597; AIR 1985 SC 969; 1985 (1) Crimes 990; 1985 CRLJ 1175; 1985 CrLR(SC) 265; 1985(1) SCALE 918; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 780

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Grant of anticipatory bail - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 149, 302, 307, 327, 379 and 447 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 46(1), 438 and 439 - Accusation against respondent is that he has committed an offence of murder punishable u/s. 302 IPC - Respondent was granted bail after filing application u/s. 438 of CrPC,1973 which was allowed - Whether order of grant of bail to appellant was sustainable? - Gurbaksh Singh Sibbia v. State of Punjab, 1980 Indlaw SC 316, discussed - Held, No - Substantial distinction exists between an ordinary order of bail and an order of anticipatory bail - Former is granted after arrest and provides for release from custody of police - Anticipatory bail is granted in anticipation of arrest and is effective at time arrest is made - Considerations governing granting of anticipatory bail are materially different from considerations for grant of bail - Very compelling circumstances must be made out for granting bail to a person accused of committing murder and that to when investigation is in progress - Anticipatory bail is quashed - Appeal allowed.
