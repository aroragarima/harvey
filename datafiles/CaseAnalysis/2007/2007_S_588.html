State of Maharashtra and Another v Public Concern For Governance Trust and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 7; (2007) 3 SCC 587; AIR 2007 SC 777; 2007(2) ALL MR 410; 2007 (6) AWC 5563; 2007 (2) Bom.C.R. 686; JT 2007 (1) SC 315; 2007(1) SCALE 72; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 87; 2007 (3) UPLBEC 2149

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning Act, 1966 - Allotment of lands - High Court made observations against the then Chief Minister - Appeal for the limited purpose of expunging certain remarks made by the High Court - Held, High Court has, erred in holding that by making a notation please put up the applicants had blessings of the then Chief Minister - Allegations have been made against the then Chief Minister, however, he was not made party before the Court - Allegations made against him are one-sided and do not merit any consideration - Expunged the remarks/observations/strictures made against the appellant - Petitions allowed.
