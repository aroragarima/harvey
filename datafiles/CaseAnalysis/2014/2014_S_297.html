Basappa v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 130; (2014) 5 SCC 154; 2014 (2) ACC 1; 2014 ACJ 1016; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 1497; 2014 (2) KarLJ 545; 2014 (2) RCR(Criminal) 144; 2014(3) SCALE 87; [2014] 3 S.C.R. 391

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 279, 304A - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 187, 197 - Causing death by negligence - Acquittal - Reversal by HC - Sustainability - Appellant allegedly caused death of decease by driving tractor in negligent manner - Trial Court acquitted appellant - On appeal by State, HC reversed acquittal and convicted appellant u/ss. 279, 304A IPC - However, HC acquitted appellant u/ss. 187 and 197 of the Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in reversing order of acquittal passed by Trial Court - Held, HC did not take a view that Trial Court judgment acquitting accused was based on no material or perverse or wholly unreasonable or not a plausible view - HC did not take a view that there was non-consideration of any evidence or there was palpable misreading of evidence - It was not the stand of HC that there had been some miscarriage of justice in the way Trial Court had appreciated evidence - However, only stand of HC was that on the available evidence, another view was also reasonably possible in the sense that appellant accused could have been convicted - In such circumstances, HC was not justified in reversing acquittal - HC itself having acquitted appellant u/s. 187 of the Act on the ground of no evidence, whether it was possible, to hold him guilty u/ss. 279 and 304A of IPC, was itself a seriously doubtful question - Hence, impugned judgment was set aside - Appeal allowed.
