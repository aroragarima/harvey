Williams v Lourdusamy and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2752; (2008) 5 SCC 647; AIR 2008 SC 2212; 2008(4) ALL MR 427; 2008 (71) ALR 787; 2008 (3) AWC 2438; JT 2008 (5) SC 571; 2008 (5) MLJ(SC) 760; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 877; 2008(6) SCALE 697; [2008] 6 S.C.R. 929; 2008 (2) UC 1249

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Kudiyiruppu Act, Act 40 of 1971

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908-Section 11, Explanation VIII-Transfer of Property Act, 1882-Section 53A - Res judicata-Applicability - Suit by first respondent against appellant for permanent injunction-Question whether respondent put in possession in terms of oral agreement-Not in issue - Second respondent not impleaded-Decree for specific performance of contract not prayed for in suit-Trial Judge held that first respondent was in possession-And granted decree of permanent injunction-Thereafter appellant filed suit for declaration of title and recovery of possession - Both respondents impleaded - First respondent also filed suit for specific performance against appellant and second respondent - Both suits consolidated - Suit for specific performance dismissed-And suit of appellant for declaration of title and confirmation of possession decreed-First appeals dismissed-High Court relying on some stray observations, of trial court-Held, that as possession delivered on basis of purported oral agreement - Principles of res judicata attracted - Whether justified? - Held, "no" - Court dismissed suit for specific performance on ground that respondent failed to prove oral agreement-When suit not decreed-No question of his continuing in possession in part performance of contract in terms of Section 53A of T.P. Act-Would arise-Trial court and court of first appeal rightly held that principle of res judicata not attracted - Impugned judgment unsustainable-And set aside..
