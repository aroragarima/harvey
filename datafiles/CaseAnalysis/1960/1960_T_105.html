Â Sri Sudhansu Shekhar Singh Deo v State of Orissa and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 September 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 241; AIR 1961 SC 196; [1961] 41 ITR 743; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 779

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Constitution - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution of India, 1950 arts. 1, 3, 4, 5 - Orissa Agricultural Income Tax Act, 1947, ss. 2(1), 3 - Adaptation of Laws Order, 1950 - Former Indian State Ruler - Exemption from tax - Maintainability -Appellant/assessee was Ruler of former State disputed his liability to pay agricultural income tax and declined to furnish return - Agricultural Income tax Officer assessed appellant to pay tax for years 1949-50 to 1953-54 and also imposed penalty for failure to submit his returns for years 1949-50 and 1950-51 - Appellant preferred appeals before Appellate authorities, which were dismissed - Appellant filed petitions in HC challenging assessments made by taxing authorities and orders imposing penalty, HC dismissed petitions - Hence, instant appeals - Whether impugned order of the HC was justified - Held, privileges guaranteed by arts. 4, 5 of the Constitution were personal privileges of appellant as ex-Ruler and those privileges did not extend to his personal property - The 1947 Act imposed on agricultural income of 'every person' liability to pay agricultural income tax, by proviso to s. 3 of the 1947 Act, agricultural income of Central Govt, State Govt and of local authorities was exempt from tax, but this exemption was not extended to any other body or person - It was true that in definition of expression 'person' as originally enacted in s. 2(1) of the 1947 Act, Ruler of Indian State was expressly included and by Adaptation of Laws Order, 1950, reference to Rulers of Indian States was deleted as from 26-1-1950, but by that amendment, intention to exclude Rulers of Indian States from liability to pay agricultural income tax was not evinced between dates on which the 1947 Act was enacted and Adaptation of Laws Order, 1950 - Several political events of far reaching effect had taken place, in consequence of which appellant had ceased to be Ruler of Indian State - Amendment in definition of 'person' in s. 2(1) of the 1947 Act was made not with object of excluding Rulers of former Indian States from liability to pay tax, it was only made to delete clause which, in view of political changes, had no practical significance - Liability to pay tax was imposed by the 1947 Act and there was in the Act no express exemption in favour of appellant - Therefore, claim of appellant to exemption on ground that he was not 'person' could not be sustained - Appeals dismissed.
