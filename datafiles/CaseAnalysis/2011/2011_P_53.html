Prithipal Singh Etc v State Of Punjab & Anr. Etc

Supreme Court of India

04 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 825; (2012) 1 SCC 10; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 3931; 2012 (1) JCC 254; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 791; 2011 (4) RLW 3666; [2011] 14 S.C.R. 862

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Human Rights; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Police Torture

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34, 120-B - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 161 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused allegedly committed murder of deceased - Appellants were charged u/ss. 302 r/w s. 34 and 120-B IPC - Trial Court convicted accused for offence punishable u/ss. 302 r/w s. 34 and 120-B IPC and sentenced them to undergo imprisonment for life - On appeal, HC dismissed appeal and confirmed conviction and sentence of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether conviction of accused by Courts below was sustainable - Held, witnesses were so scared/terrified of action of police atrocities/criminal intimidation that they could not muster courage to reveal truth -Depositions made by witnesses in Court had been consistent with their statements recorded u/s. 161 CrPC - However, none of them could successfully prove same and plea of alibi taken by them was found to be false which points towards their guilt - Sufficient evidence was available on record in respect of abduction which was identified by prosecution witnesses - Though dead body of deceased could not be recovered from canal as investigation commenced after a long time, recovery of dead body was not a condition precedent for conviction of accused for murder - Hence, no reason to interfere with well-reasoned judgment and order of Courts below and same was upheld - Appeal dismissed.

Constitution - Human Rights - Police atrocities - Acceptability - Held, State should ensure prohibition of torture, cruel, inhuman and degrading treatment to any person, particularly at the hands of any State agency/police force - In absence of any research/data/ material, a general/sweeping remark that a 'substantial majority of population in country considered police force as an institution which violates human rights' could not be accepted - However, in instant case if there was some material on record to reveal police atrocities, Court should take stern action against erring police officials in accordance with law - Appeal dismissed.

Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 386(e) - Enhancement of sentence - Suo moto - Held, law could be summarised that HC in exercise of its power u/s. 386(e) CrPC was competent to enhance sentence suo moto - However, such a course was permissible only after giving opportunity of hearing to accused - Appeal dismissed.

Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Evidence Act 1872 - Evidence of accomplice - Reliability - Held, law on issue could be summarised to effect that deposition of an accomplice in a crime who had not been made an accused/put to trial, should be relied upon, however, evidence was required to be considered with care and caution - An accomplice who had not been put on trial was a competent witness as he deposes in Court after taking oath and there was no prohibition in any law not to act upon his deposition without corroboration - Appeal dismissed.

Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Evidence Act 1872, s. 134 - Evidence - Corroboration - Justifiability - Held, general rule Court could and might act on testimony of a single witness provided he was wholly reliable - There was no legal impediment in convicting a person on sole testimony of a single witness u/s. 134 of the Act - But if there were doubts about testimony, Court would insist on corroboration - However, in instant case if there was some material on record to reveal police atrocities, Court should take stern action against erring police officials in accordance with - Appeal dismissed.

Practice & Procedure - Extraordinary remedies - Validity - Held, while dealing with an unprecedented case, Court had to innovate law and might also pass unconventional order keeping in mind that extraordinary fact situation requires extraordinary measures - Appeal dismissed.

Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Recovery of - Held, in a murder case, it was not necessary that dead body of victim should be found and identified, i.e. conviction for offence of murder did not necessarily depend on corpus delicti being found - Corpus delicti in a murder case had two components - death as result, and criminal agency of another as means - Thus, where there was a direct proof of one, other might be established by circumstantial evidence - Appeal dismissed.
