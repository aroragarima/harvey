Â State of Rajasthan and Another v Sripal Jain

Supreme Court of India

24 January 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 291; AIR 1963 SC 1323; 1963 CRLJ 347; 1963 (1) LLJ 285; 1964 (1) SCJ 208; [1964] 1 S.C.R. 742

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Age Of Superannuation, Rajasthan Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1958, Rajasthan General Clauses Act, 1955

Summary: Service - Rajasthan General Clauses Act, 1955, s. 32 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 166 and 311 - Rajasthan Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1958, rr. 14 and 56 - Rajasthan Service Rules, r. 244(2) - Rules of Business, r. 31(vii)(a) - Whether a case of compulsory retirement u/r. 244(2) of Service Rules has to be submitted to Governor u/r. 31(vii)(a) of Rules of Business? - Held, taking all three clauses of item (vii) as a whole, it appears that item (vii) provides for a complete scheme with reference to three kinds of penalties namely, dismissal, removal and compulsory retirement and makes it incumbent that cases of this kind must be referred to Governor - Therefore, r. 31(vii)(a) speaks of compulsory retiring of an officer as a penalty and not compulsory retirement on reaching age of superannuation or u/r. 244(2) - It is therefore not necessary to submit papers with respect to compulsory retirement of respondent u/r. 244(2) to Governor - Appeal allowed.
