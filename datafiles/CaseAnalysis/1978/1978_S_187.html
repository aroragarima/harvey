Motilal Padampat Sugar Mills Company Limited v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, V.D. Tulzapurkar

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 56; (1979) SCC (Tax) 144; (1979) 2 SCC 409; AIR 1979 SC 621; 1979 ALJ 368; [1979] 118 ITR 326; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 641; 1979 (44) STC 42

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Sales Tax - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948, s. 4-A - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss. 25 and 4-A - Doctrine of promissory estoppel - Extent of enforceability against state - Plea of waiver - Raising at Writ petition - Maintainability of - Appellant is a limited Co. which is primarily engaged in business of manufacture and sale of sugar and it has also a cold storage plant and a steel foundry - A news item appeared in National Herald in which it was stated that State of Uttar Pradesh had decided to give exemption from sales tax for a period of three years u/s. 4-A of U P Sales Tax Act to all new industrial units in State with a view to enabling them to come on firm footing in developing stage - Appellant on basis of this announcement, addressed a letter to Director of Industries stating that in view of sales tax holiday announced by Govt., appellant intended to set up a hydrogenation plant for manufacture of vanaspati and sought for confirmation that this industrial unit, which they proposed to set up, would be entitled to sales tax holiday for a period of three years from date it commences production - The Director of Industries replied by his letter dated 14-10-1968, confirming that there will be no sales tax for three years on finished products of your proposed vanaspati factory from date it gets power connection for commencing production - (A) Whether appellant had waived his right under assurance given by 4th respondent on behalf of State Govt? - Held, no plea of waiver can be allowed to be raised unless it is pleaded and factual foundation for it is laid in pleadings - Plea of waiver was not taken by State Govt. - There can be no waiver unless person who is said to have waived is fully informed as to his right and with full knowledge of such right, he intentionally abandons it - No right for HC to have allowed plea of waiver to be raised against appellant - There is nothing to show that at date when appellant addressed letter D/- 25-06-1970, it had full knowledge of its right to exemption under assurance given by 4th respondent and that it intentionally abandoned such right - Appellant had not waived his rights under exemption under assurance given by 4th respondent - (B) Whether assurance given by 4th respondent on behalf of State Govt. that appellant would be exempt from sales tax for a period of three years from date of commencement of production could be enforced against State Govt. by invoking doctrine of promissory estoppel? - Held, whatever be nature of function which Govt. is discharging, Govt. is subject to rule of promissory estoppel and if essential ingredients of this rule are satisfied, Govt. can be compelled to carry out promise made by it - Further held, Govt. was bound to exempt appellant from payment of sales tax in respect of sales of vanaspati effected by it in State of Uttar Pradesh for a period of three years from date of commencement of production and was not entitled to recover such sales tax from appellant - Appeal allowed.
