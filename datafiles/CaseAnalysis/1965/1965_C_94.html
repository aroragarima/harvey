Life Insurance Corporation of India v Crown Life Insurance Company

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, S.M. Sikri, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 123; AIR 1965 SC 1985; [1965] 35 Comp Cas 741; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 474

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance

Keywords: Revenue Account, Liabilities, Life Insurance Fund, Controlled Business, Foreign Insurer, Prima facie surplus

Summary: Corporate - Life Insurance Corporation Act, 1956 ss. 10(2), 11, 35, cl. (d) - Insurance company - Defecit - Acquisition - Compensation - Appellant company took over business of respondent company, which was incorporated in Canada and disputes arose with regard to liability, debited under head 'life insurance fund' - Respondent claimed Rs. 27,86,658 towards compensation , but appellant agreed to pay Rs.1,11,466 - Appellant had referred said dispute to Tribunal which awarded compensation of Rs.27,86,658 - Hence, instant appeal - Whether interpretation of words 'life insurance fund' used in Part B of First Schedule of the Act by Tribunal was sustainable.

Held, "life insurance fund" in cl. (d) have special significance assigned to these words in the insurance Act and cannot be equated to the difference between total assets and liabilities apart from liabilities towards policies yet to mature. Further, if the words "life insurance fund" in cl. (d) are to be given the meaning there will be a clear inconsistency between cl. (d) and s. 35 of the Act. S. 35 permits a foreign insurer to take away what may be called excess assets but a foreign insurer is not bound to make an application u/s. 35. It is not in dispute that respondent has taken away excess assets with the permission of the Central Government u/s. 35 of the Act. Therefore, legislature intended the words "life insurance fund" to mean what they meant u/s. 10(2) of the Act and already said cl. (d) provides for past surplus in form 1, the responsibility for which passes on to the Life Insurance Corporation when it takes over the life business of an insurer. It is obvious that the share capital of an insurance company cannot be a part of the life insurance fund - Hence, Tribunal was right in its conclusion that the words "life insurance fund" as used in cl. (d) have the same meaning as that given to them u/s.10(2) of the Act r/w s. 11 and form D of the Third Schedule to the Act. Appeal dismissed.
