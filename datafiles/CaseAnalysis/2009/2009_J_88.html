Delhi Development Authority v Jitender Pal Bhardwaj

Supreme Court of India

09 October 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1445; (2010) 1 SCC 146; AIR 2010 SC 497; 2010 (3) AWC 2541; 2009 (163) DLT 618; JT 2009 (14) SC 220; 2010 (3) RCR(Civil) 572; 2009(13) SCALE 391; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 135

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Co-Owner

Summary: Land & Property - Allotment of flat - Cancellation - Respondent applied for allotment of flat as per Petitioner authority terms and conditions that neither the respondent nor any of his family members owned any land or plot or flat in Delhi and gave declaration to that effect - Petitioner authority allotted flat to respondent after 28 years - Respondent declared that he had in the meantime brought another flat - Petitioner cancelled the allotment - Respondent filed writ petition before HC - HC allowed the writ petition, by quashing petitioner's order of cancellation - Contention by respondent that he owned a residential house which was less than 65 sq.m. - Held, when a person acquires a flat in a multi-storeyed building and he gets an individual share of less than 65 sq.m. in the land on which the building stands, he is not barred from securing allotment from petitioner - Therefore, respondent entitled to get the allotment - Petition dismissed.
