Shiv Nandan Dixit v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

19 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1482; (2003) 12 SCC 636; JT 2003 (10) SC 262; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 600; 2003(10) SCALE 1105

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 5(1)(d) and 5(2) - Code of Criminal Procedure, ss. 196(2) - Indian Penal Code , 1860, ss. 120B and 161 - Two appellants convicted u/ss. 120B and 161, IPC, r/w ss. 5(1)(d) and 5(2) - Appeal - (A) Whether in view of provisions of s. 196(2) of CrPC, Trial Court could not have taken cognizance of offence punishable u/s. 120B, IPC without consent in writing of State Govt. or district Magistrate? - Held, s. 196(2) had no application because in respect of both offences, court had jurisdiction to pass a sentence of over two years' rigorous imprisonment - (B) Whether u/s. 161, IPC, as it stood at relevant point of time made it offence only if bribe is received/obtained? - Document in question was produced before income tax authorities for some official purpose and was in its custody - ITO directed return of document, he was doing an official duty - Further held, order that he passed for return of document was an official order and any act which has to be done to fulfill or comply with said order will also be an official act in that process he demands bribe, it would be an offence u/s. 161, IPC - Further held, incident in question having taken place nearly 23 years ago, appellants have already suffered sufficiently and should take a lenient view - Appeal partly allowed.
