Union Bank of India v Vishwa Mohan

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Kurdukar, Sujata V. Manohar, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 393; (1998) 4 SCC 310; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1129; AIR 1998 SC 2311; 1998 (79) FLR 327; JT 1998 (3) SC 118; 1998 (1) LLJ 1217; 1998 (3) LLN 90

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Union Bank of India Officers Employees (Discipline and Appeal) Regulations (1976) - Misconduct - Dismissal on ground of bribery, embezzlement, misappropriation - Non-supply of inquiry report - Prejudice to the case of delinquent - Validity of inquiry - Held, order of Disciplinary Authority as well as order of Appellate Authority since High Court felt that charges leveled against respondent after he was promoted as an officer were not of serious nature - HC also committed an error when it assumed that when respondent was promoted as a bank officer, he must be having a good report otherwise he would not have been promoted - Relating to misconduct of respondent was noticed by bank on which his promotion could have been withheld - HC has failed to apply its judicial mind to facts and circumstances of present case and erroneously concluded that non supply of inquiry report/finding has caused prejudice to respondent - Misconduct leveled against respondent could not be said to be so serious which would warrant punishment of dismissal - Appeal allowed.
