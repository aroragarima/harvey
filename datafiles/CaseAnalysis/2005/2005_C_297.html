Competent Authority v Barangore Jute Factory and others

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Kumar, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 825; (2005) 13 SCC 477; JT 2005 (10) SC 50; 2006 (1) RCR(Civil) 79; 2005(9) SCALE 493; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 421; 2006 (1) UPLBEC 40

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - National Highways Act, 1956, ss. 3A, 3C(1) - National Highways Laws (Amendment) Act, 1997, s. 3A(2) - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 5A - Compulsory acquisition of certain lands belonging to Writ Petitioners by Central Govt. - HC by its impugned judgment held that Notification regarding compulsory acquisition of land to be bad in law but in view of the fact that possession of acquired land had already been taken by authorities, ordered that an additional amount of compensation be awarded to land owners - Held, least that is required in such cases is that acquisition notification should let person whose land is sought to be acquired know what he is going to lose - Notification shows that there is no mention of any Plan - Description of land under acquisition in impugned Notification fails to meet legal requirement of a brief description of land, which renders Notification invalid - Taking of possession of lands is in total violation of statutory provisions - Construction of national highway on acquired land has already been completed - Directed to determine compensation as per market price of land on date of issuance of notification - Appeals disposed of.
