Somdev Kapoor v State of West Bengal and others

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, K.S. Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 665; (2014) 14 SCC 486; 2013 (4) CGLJ 612; 2013(12) SCALE 434; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 452

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Excise

Keywords: West Bengal Excise Rules, 1993

Summary: Excise - Administrative - West Bengal Excise (Selection of New Sites and Grant of License for Retail Sale of Liquor and Certain Other Intoxicants) Rules, 2003, r. 8 - Licence of liquor bar - Appellant was a proprietor of a Hotel and Restaurant which was being run since 1954 - Appellant made an application before the Collector of Excise, Calcutta for issuance of license to operate foreign liquor bar and restaurant - Appellant was given temporary license to run the liquor bar - Respondents filed a Writ Petition with the prayers to cancel, rescind and revoke the aforesaid temporary license issued to the appellant on the ground that it was not open for the appellant to run a liquor bar in the said restaurant which was in the vicinity of religious places and school - Further submitted that the religious places and school were situated within the distance of 550 feet of the premises where the license to operate the bar by the Excise Department was granted to the appellant and this was in violation of r. 8 of the Rules - HC directed to the Excise Department not to renew the license of the appellant which was expiring in the month of January 2013 - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC rightly directed the Excise Department not to renew the license of the appellant which was expiring in the month of January 2013 - Held, appellant could not have been granted for foreign liquor bar and restaurant license as there were many religious and educational institutions within the 1000 ft. of place from where the appellant was operating - HC had rightly issued mandamus not to renew the license of the appellant - Since the license was renewed on the basis of interim orders passed by SC, which was valid till December 2013, it would not be renewed thereafter - Appeal dismissed.
