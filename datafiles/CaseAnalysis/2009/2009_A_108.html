State of Tamil Nadu and Others v Amala Annai Higher Secondary School

Supreme Court of India

28 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1111; (2009) 9 SCC 386; 2009 (4) ESC 692; 2009 (123) FLR 522; JT 2009 (13) SC 78; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 1667; 2009(12) SCALE 57; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 913

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - Up-gradation of school - Appointment of non-teaching staff - Denial of sanction - Respondent school was upgraded from Middle School to High School - Respondent appointed a non-teaching staff without approval - Representation for according approval was made to appellant Competent Authority - No response from appellant - Respondent filed Writ Petition before HC - HC directed appellant to dispose representation - Appellant rejected respondent's representation on ground that school did not have sufficient strength to sanctioning further non-teaching post - Respondent filed another writ petition before HC after 7 years on the same cause - Single Judge directed appellant to accord sanction to post - Intra Court appeal of appellant was dismissed by DB - Legality - Held, rejection of earlier representation was not challenged by respondent, hence it attained finality - No new cause of action has arisen for filing another writ petition - Second round of litigation on same cause of action was an abuse of process of court - G.O.Ms. No.340 dt.1-4-1992 specified norms for sanctioning of posts applicable to new schools, not to upgraded schools - Appointment made by respondent on anticipation of approval, lacked merit for acceptance - Student strength of respondent during the relevant period was less than 300 which was against norms of relevant G.O. - Further, said G.O. was applicable only to clerks, not to any other non-teaching staffs - Creation and sanction of posts was prerogative of executive, hence HC could not have directed appellant to accord sanction for a post in respondent school - Appeal allowed.
