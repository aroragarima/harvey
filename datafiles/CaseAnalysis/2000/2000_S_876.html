Collector Of Central Excise, Meerut v Surya Roshni Ltd

Supreme Court of India

13 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3512; (2001) 9 SCC 407; 2000 (122) E.L.T. 3; JT 2000 (Supp1) SC 501; 2000 (41) RLT 249; 2000(7) SCALE 611; 2000 (7) SLT 279

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Earlier Order, Rubber, Goods, In Transit, Credit Note, Insurance Charge, Cost Of Transport, Cost Of Transportation, Transportation Charge, Tariff Value, Actual Expense, Duly Certified, Account, Cost, Appellate Tribunal, Equalised Freight, Transit Insurance, Transit Loss

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944 - Respondent manufacturer of electric bulb and tubes - Price charged includes two per cent towards transit risk insurance - Claim of deductions of 'transit losses/ breakages replenished to customers' from assessable value being transit insurance - Department contending such payment being compensation not allowable as deduction - Central Excise and Gold (Control) Appellate Tribunal held transit insurance was eligible for deduction while determining assessable value according to its earlier orders - Present appeal - Whether amount paid by respondent to its customers can be said to be on account of transit insurance and allowed as deductions from assessable value? - Held, in case of transportation what is included is cost of taking out insurance to cover goods transported - Payment made by respondent to its customers for breakages and losses cannot tantamount to insurance - Nor can, by any means, such compensation be treated as a part of cost of transportation - It is a clear case of making up to customer by means of a credit note monies that it has lost on account of breakages or losses in transit - Appeal allowed.
