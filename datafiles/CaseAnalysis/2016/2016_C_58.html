Baby @ Sebastian and another v Circle Inspector of Police, Adimaly

Supreme Court of India

26 July 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 550; AIR 2016 SC 3671; 2016(7) SCALE 444

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Presumption, FIR, Gold, Appreciation Of Evidence, Appreciation, Place Of Occurrence, Version, Reliance, Chain, Scene Of Occurrence, Two Views, Innocence, Credibility, Infirmity, Identification, Illegality, Locality, Chance Witness, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Suspicion

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 161, 164 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Deceased planned to elope with PW2 daughter of appellant no.1/accused - Appellant no.1 caught hold deceased and pushed him down in muddy water thereby strangulated and killed him - FIR was registered against appellant nos.1, 2 for offence punishable u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 IPC - Trial Court acquitted appellants holding that prosecution case against appellants is not free from reasonable doubt - On appeal, HC convicted appellants u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 IPC and sentenced them to undergo imprisonment for life - Hence, instant appeal - Whether prosecution evidence establishes beyond reasonable doubt commission of offence by accused u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 IPC.

Held, age of injuries present on PW2 and appellant no.2 matches with approximate time of incident in no way carves out an active role on part of both appellants in commission of murder of deceased. The material evidence on record does not reveal anything which incriminates appellants. Further, depositions of parents of deceased i.e., PW13 and PW14 in no way implicate both appellants as offenders. Rest of the prosecution witnesses have turned hostile and have not supported prosecution story on material facts to show that appellants were involved in the crime as alleged against them. Therefore, prosecution failed to prove guilt of appellants beyond reasonable doubt. Though prosecution have turned hostile, their alleged statements made to police u/s. 161 CrPC were not confronted to them and marked as exhibits. Further, IO has not spoken in his evidence anything about alleged statements of hostile witnesses recorded u/s. 161 CrPC as held by SC in case of V.K. Mishra v. State of Uttarakhand, 2015 Indlaw SC 512. Thus, placing reliance on their statements u/s. 161 CrPC by HC to record the finding of conviction is erroneous in law. HC failed to appreciate the same in arriving at different conclusion other than Trial Court in exercise of its appellate jurisdiction. Therefore, impugned judgment and order passed by HC was set aside. Appeal allowed.
