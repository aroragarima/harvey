Martin Burn Limited v Corporation of Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Raghubar Dayal, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 445; AIR 1966 SC 529; 1967 (1) ITJ 579; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 543

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Calcutta Municipal Act, 1923 - Calcutta Municipal Act, 1951, ss. 147 and 164 - Two appeals arise out of proceeding for ascertainment of annual value of premises occupied by appellant - Annual value was ascertained with a view to assess municipal rates payable in respect of premises - As valuation had originally been made by respondent corporation under repealed act, it is that 1923 Act by which question that arises will have to be determined - Whether order of remand by High Court was unjustifiable as it converted appellants' appeal to Court of Small Causes into a proceeding wholly alien to what it originally was meant for? - Held, order was made so that a legal liability for rates assessed on valuation made under it might fasten on appellant - Liability for rates is however a statutory liability under Act; it is not a liability to be imposed by order of court - Now Act nowhere states that rates may be fixed on basis of a valuation made by a court; it does not at all contemplate a valuation made by a court on its own - Such a valuation would be futile and would create no statutory liability - Though Act does not empower a court to make a valuation itself, it does seem to contemplate in ss. 147 and 164 a valuation made by Corporation being revised and a previous valuation altered, by a court in an appeal - However, irrespective of how HC described valuation to be made under its order, that that valuation cannot by any stretch of imagination be called a revised valuation or a previous valuation altered - What has happened here is that previous valuation has been cancelled - It was not within inherent power of HC to remand case for doing of a thing which Act did not countenance - Appeals allowed.
