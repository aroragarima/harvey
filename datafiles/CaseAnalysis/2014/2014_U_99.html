Fahim Ahmad and others v United India Insurance Company Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

25 March 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

N.V. Ramana, Ranjan Gogoi, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 183; (2014) 14 SCC 148; 2014 (2) ACC 238; 2014 ACJ 1254; AIR 2014 SC 2187; 2014(3) ALL MR 963; 2014 (3) Bom.C.R. 837; 2014 (2) CLT(SC) 144; JT 2014 (4) SC 156; 2014 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1437; 2014 (174) PLR 722; 2014 (2) RCR(Civil) 470; 2014 (2) RLW 1281; 2014(4) SCALE 177; 2014 (3) WLN(SC) 133

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 149(2)(a)(i)(a) - Accidental death - Claim - Compensation awarded - Liability of payment - Deceased husband of appellant No. 1 was dashed from behind by the offending vehicle and he died on the spot - Claim was filed of Rs. 5,00,000/- and Tribunal awarded compensation of Rs. 3,12,000/- - Tribunal held respondent No.1/Insurance Co. liable to pay the said compensation - On appeal filed by respondent No. 1, HC partly allowed the appeal holding that respondent No. 1 should have a right to recover the same from the owner of the offending tractor as there was breach of condition of the insurance policy - Hence instant appeal - Who was liable to pay the amount of compensation awarded by the Tribunal -

Held, at the time of the accident, a trolley was attached with the tractor, which was carrying sand for the purpose of construction - Merely because it was carrying sand would not mean that the tractor was being used for commercial purpose and consequently, there was a breach of the condition of policy on the part of the insured - There was nothing on record to show that the tractor was being used for commercial purpose(s) or purpose(s) other than agricultural purpose(s), i.e., for hire or reward, as contemplated u/s. 149(2)(a)(i)(a) of the Act - Although the plea of breach of the conditions of policy was raised before the Tribunal, yet neither any issue was framed nor any evidence led to prove the same - It was mandatory for respondent No. 1 not only to plead the said breach, but also substantiate the same by adducing positive evidence in respect of the same - In the absence of any such evidence, it could not be presumed that there was breach of the conditions of policy - Thus, there was no reason to fasten the said liability of payment of the amount of compensation awarded by the Tribunal on appellants - SC was of the view that, HC was not justified in transferring the burden of paying the amount of compensation from respondent No. 1 to appellants - Impugned judgment was set aside, in so far as the right to recover the amount awarded from the owner of the tractor - Appeal allowed.
