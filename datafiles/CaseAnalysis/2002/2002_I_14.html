K. M. Sharma v Income Tax Officer

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 818; (2002) 4 SCC 339; AIR 2002 SC 1715; 2002 (174) CTR 210; [2002] 254 ITR 772; JT 2002 (4) SC 10; 2002(3) SCALE 383; 2002 (3) SCJ 77; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 1047; 2002 (3) SLT 123; 2002 (3) Supreme 235; [2002] 122 TAXMAN 426

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 147, 148, 149, 150 - Land Acquisition - Capital Gains - Maintainability - Appellant's lands were acquired u/s. 6 of the Act and an award was passed by Commissioner granting compensation in favour of appellant -Trial Court held appellant entitled to 1/32 share of compensation and appellant was granted total compensation - On reference u/s. 18 of the Act, Trial Court awarded sum of Rs.1,10,20,624/- - Amount was paid to appellant amounts paid represented principal sum of compensation and interest - Before making payments, tax was deducted - Since lands acquired were agricultural lands and were acquired prior to 1-4-1970, capital gains tax was not leviable but tax was leviable on interest earned on amount awarded on year to year basis - Appellant requested that necessary action be taken u/s.147 of the Act to enable appellant as assessee to file his Income Tax Return - Appellant was served with impugned notices u/s. 148 of the Act for 16 assessments years - Appellant, in HC assailed notices issued for reassessment for assessment years 1968-69 to 1971-72 and for year 1982-83 on ground that proposed reassessment for those assessment years had already become barred by time u/s. 149 of the Act, for which in relevant periods maximum period of four years or seven years limitation was prescribed depending upon quantum of liability towards tax - HC by impugned Judgment accepted contention of Department upholding validity of assessment proceedings initiated by Department u/s. 148 of the Act - HC rejected contention of assessee that sub-s. (2) of s. 150 of the Act was explanation to sub-section (1) and proceedings for reassessment, which had already become barred by time u/s. 149 of the Act before 1-4-1989, could not have been commenced on amended provisions of sub- section (1) of s. 150 - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, on proper construction of provisions of s. 150 (1) and effect of its operation from 1- 4-1989, Court opined that provisions could not be given retrospective effect prior to 1-4-1989 for assessments which had already become final due to bar of limitation prior to 1-4- 1989 - Taxing provision imposing liability was governed by normal presumption that it was not retrospective and settled principle of law was that law to be applied was that which was in force in assessment year unless otherwise provided expressly or by necessary implication - Even procedural provision could not in absence of clear contrary intendment expressed therein be given greater retrospectively than was expressly mentioned so as to enable Authorities to affect finality of tax assessments or to open up liabilities, which had become barred by lapse of time -Therefore, sub-section (1) of s. 150, was amended with effect from 1-4-1989, did not enable Authorities to reopen assessments, which had become final due to bar of limitation prior to 1-4-1989 and this position was applicable equally to reassessments proposed on basis of Orders passed under the Act or under any other law - Hence, Judgment of HC was set aside and impugned notices issued by respondent of Income Tax Department against appellant for assessment years 1968-69 to 1971-72 and 1981-82 were quashed - Appeal allowed.
