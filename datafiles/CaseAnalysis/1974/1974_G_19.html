Golam Hussain Alias Gama v The Commissioner Of Police, Calcutta, And Others

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 411; (1974) 4 SCC 530; (1974) SCC (Cr) 566; AIR 1974 SC 1336; 1974 CRLJ 938; [1974] 3 S.C.R. 613

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Preventive Detention Act, 1950, Defence Of India Act, 1971

Summary: Maintenance of Internal Security Act, 1971,ss. 3, 3(1)(a)(ii) , 3(2), 12 and 13 - Validity of Detention order challenged - In the light of following ancillary issues - (A) Detenu discharged in criminal cases for want of evidence - Detention order passes on the same grounds - (B) Long interval (9 months) between incidents and order of detention -(C) Effect of non-specification of any definite period of detentions in the Order - (D) Detention on ground of prevention of public disorder - Acts imputed to detenu ex facia aimed at a particular person - Such acts when can be treated as invasion of public order - Held, merely because grounds of detention have been the subject-matter of criminal cases which have ended in discharge, it cannot be said that the order of detention is mala fide - Live link between grounds and the purpose of detention required but no mechanical test by counting the months of the interval is sound - Does not render the order illegal - Test indicated - Petition Dismissed.
