Mangal Singh and Others v Smt. Rattno and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 447; AIR 1967 SC 1786; 1968 MahLJ 54; 1968 MPLJ 55; 1967 (69) PLR 998; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 454

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Limited Heir, Succession & Inheritance, Marriage & Divorce

Summary: Land & Property - Hindu Women's Rights to Property Act, 1937 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 14(1) - Labhu died in year 1917 and on his death his widow, Smt. Harnam Kaur, who filed suit as plaintiff, came into possession of land - On an application made by collaterals of Labhu, Naib Tehsildar, by his order D/-26-06-1954, effected mutation in favour of these collaterals - Whether it can be held that this property was possessed by Smt. Harnam Kaur as envisaged by s. 14 of Act, so that she became full owner of this land? - Held, plaintiff had been dispossessed in year 1954 and was not able to recover possession from defendants appellants until her death in year 1958 - Expression used in s. 14(1) of Act was intended to cover cases of possession in law also, where lands may have descended to a female Hindu and she has not actually entered into them - It would of course, cover other cases of actual or constructive possession - On language of s. 14(1), therefore, court holds that this provision will become applicable to any property which is owned by a female Hindu, even though she is not in actual, physical or constructive possession of that property - Appeal dismissed.
