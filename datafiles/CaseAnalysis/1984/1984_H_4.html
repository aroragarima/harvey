Union of India and others v Haji Mastan Mirza

Supreme Court of India

23 February 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Varadarajan, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 455; (1984) 2 SCC 427; AIR 1984 SC 681; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1973, s. 3 - Smugglers and Foreign Exchange Manipulators (Forfeiture of Property) Act, 1976 - Detention - Non-supply of documents - Effect thereof - Respondent was detained u/s. 3(1) of the COFEPOSA - Thereafter, show-cause notice u/s. 6(1) of SAFEMA was issued to the respondent and his relatives and alleged associates founded on the respondent's detention under the detention order made u/s. 3(1) of COFEPOSA - Pursuant to that notice an order u/s. 7 of SAFEMA was passed forfeiting the properties of the respondent and his relatives - Respondent and all the other aggrieved persons filed appeals against that order - Respondent filed the application u/art. 226 of the Constitution and s. 482 of the CrPC challenging the validity of the impugned order of detention and the declaration passed and made u/s. 3(1) and 5(1) of COFEPOSA - Order of detention and the declaration were quashed by HC on ground that the respondent was not served with copies of documents referred to and relied upon in the grounds of detention served on the respondent - Whether the impugned order of detention under COFEPOSA was void and had to be quashed? - Held, failure to supply copies of documents referred to and relied upon in the grounds of detention for the purpose of detention under COFEPOSA vitiates the detention itself - In the instant case, copies of documents which were indisputably material documents and were referred to in the grounds of detention and relied upon for the purpose of detention were not supplied to the respondent - Therefore, the detention of the respondent was bad in law and the order of detention could not be sustained and was quashed - Also, action taken u/ss. 6 and 7 of SAFEMA was baseless and unsustainable in law - Conclusion reached by the HC was correct - Appeal dismissed.
