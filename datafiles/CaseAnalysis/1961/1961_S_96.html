Â Jiyajeerao Cotton Mills Limited v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 212; AIR 1963 SC 414; 1962 (2) SCJ 145; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 282; [1962] Supp1 S.C.R. 282

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Electricity Act, 1935, Taxation Laws Amendment Act

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Constitution - Central Provinces and Berar Electricity Duty Act, 1949, ss. 2(a), 2(d-l), 3 - Constitution Of India, 1950, sch. 7, List I, Entry 84 and List 2, Entry 53 - Government of India Act,1935, Sch VII, List 2, Item 48 B - (A) Whether a person who produces electricity for its own use is liable to pay duty u/s. 3 of Central Provinces and Berar Electricity Duty Act 1949? - Held, as producer is a person, who consumes electrical energy supplied for consumption - Such person is liable to pay duty u/s. 3 - Reading definitions of 'consumer' and 'producer' given in s. 2 (a) and (d-l) and omitting non-essentials, 'consumer' would include 'any person who consumes electrical energy supplied by person who generates electrical energy for his own consumption - U/s. 3 a person who generates electrical energy over hundred volts for his own consumption is liable to pay duty on units of electrical energy consumed by himself - (B) Whether levy of duty upon consumption of electrical energy be regarded as duty of excise falling within Entry 84 of List I in sch. VII of Constitution? - Further held, it cannot be - Electricity Duty Act was enacted under Item 48-B of List II in Sch VII of Govt. of India Act, 1935 - Entry 53 of List II of Constitution, to same effect - Word consumption has a wider meaning - It means also use up, spend etc. - Mere fact that a series of law were concerned only with a certain kind of use of electricity, that is consumption of electricity by persons other than producer cannot justify conclusion that British Parliament in using word 'consumption' in Item 48-B and Constituent Assembly in Entry 53 of List II wanted to limit meaning of 'consumption' in same way - Language used in legislative entries in Constitution must be interpreted in a broad way so as to give widest amplitude of power to legislature to legislate and not in a narrow and pedantic sense - Appeal dismissed.
