State of Himachal Pradesh v Tara Dutt and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, M.B. Shah, M. Srinivasan

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 840; (2000) SCC (Cr) 125; (2000) 1 SCC 230; AIR 2000 SC 297; 1999 (4) CCR 280; 2000 (1) Crimes 15; 2000 CRLJ 485; 2000 (1) JCC 121; JT 1999 (9) SC 215; 2000 (1) RCR(Criminal) 41; 1999(7) SCALE 183; 1999 (9) SLT 612; [1999] Supp4 S.C.R. 514; 1999 (9) Supreme 421

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure (Amendment) Act, 1978

Summary: Criminal - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120 B, 468, 420 and 109 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 417, 465 and 473 - Whether conviction in respect of a minor offence, where charges had been framed for a major offence, becomes barred u/s. 468 of CrPC? - S. 473 of CrPC being an enabling provision, whenever a Magistrate invokes said provision and condones delay, order of Magistrate must indicate that he was satisfied that delay has been properly explained and that it is necessary in interest of justice to condone delay - In view of fact that an offence u/s. 498 - A is an offence against society and, therefore, in matter of taking cognizance of said offence, Magistrate must liberally construe question of limitation but all same Magistrate has to be satisfied, in case of period of limitation for taking cognizance u/s. 468(2)(c) having expired that circumstances of case require delay to be condoned and further same must be manifest in order of Magistrate itself - Held, there is no bar in exercising discretion in view of s. 473 CrPC - Appeal allowed.
