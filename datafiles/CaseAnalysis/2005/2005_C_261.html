Sedco Forex International Drill. Inc. and Others v Commissioner of Income Tax and Another

Supreme Court of India

17 November 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 853; (2005) 12 SCC 717; AIR 2006 SC 428; 2005 (199) CTR 320; [2005] 279 ITR 310; JT 2005 (9) SC 639; 2005(9) SCALE 388; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 302; [2005] 149 TAXMAN 352

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 9(1)(ii) - Finance Act, 1983 - Agreement was executed between appellant-company and its employees - 'Field break' was defined in agreements to include training period being undergoing by attending classes in any part of world - Agreements further provided that such assignments would be obligatory and compulsory - Whether the assessing authority's assessment that the employees of the appellant including the salary for the field breaks as part of the total income u/s. 9 (1)(ii) of the Act, is correct in law? - The opinion of the High Court is contrary to the legislative history, context and construction of s. 9(1)(ii) - U/s. 4(1) of the Act the total income of the previous year of every person is subject to income tax - S. 5(2) defines the scope of total income as far as non residents are concerned - It is the receipt or accrual in India, whether deemed or actual, which determines the taxability under the Act - S. 9 of the Act defines income 'deemed to accrue or arise in India' - By Clause (ii) of sub-s. (1) of s. 9 'income which falls under the head 'Salaries' if it is earned in India' is included in such income - Referred on Commissioner of Income Tax vs. S.G. Pgnatale(1980 Indlaw GUJ 103) - Held, HC proceeded on incorrect hypothesis that field breaks were limited to training of employees' to render them more fit for service in India - Cardinal principle of tax law is that law to be applied is that which is in force in relevant assessment year unless otherwise provided expressly or by necessary implication - Employees' in this case had not in fact 'served' in India during field break period but they earned income in UK as UK residents - Appeal allowed.
