Ibrahim v Raju And Others

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 838; 2012 (3) ALD(SC) 60; 2012 (92) ALR 469; 2012 (1) AWC 213; 2011 (6) CTC 904; 2012 (2) MLJ(SC) 250; 2012 (167) PLR 102; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 863

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Administration of Justice (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act, 1933

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 166 - Motor accident claim - Enhancement of Compensation - Justifiability - Appellant met with an accident and sustained serious injuries - Appellant filed petition u/s. 166 of the Act claiming compensation - Tribunal held, accident was caused due to rash and negligent driving of vehicle and awarded compensation of Rs. 1,49,440 - On appeal, HC partly allowed appeal and and enhanced amount of compensation by a sum of Rs.40,000 - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Courts below were justified in awarding compensation to appellant - Held, neither Tribunal nor HC omitted to award compensation for expenses likely to be incurred by appellant for future treatment - On account of injuries suffered by him, prospects of appellant's marriage had considerably reduced and would not be able to enjoy matrimonial life - Therefore, award of Rs.50,000/- was enhanced to Rs.2 lacs for loss of marriage prospects and enjoyment of life - Compensation awarded for loss of future earning on account of permanent partial disablement was ex facie unreasonable and sum of Rs.2 lacs was awarded - Nagappa v. Gurudayal Singh 2002 Indlaw SC 1470, relied on - Thus, compensation awarded by Tribunal and HC was enhanced from Rs. 1,89,440/- to Rs.6 lacs - Appeal allowed.
