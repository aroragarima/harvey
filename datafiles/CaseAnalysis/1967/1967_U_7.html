Â B. Shama Rao v Union Territory of Pondicherry

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Vishishtha Bhargava, G.K. Mitter, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 103; AIR 1967 SC 1480; 1967 (2) ITJ 684; 1967 (2) MLJ 98; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 650; 1967 (20) STC 215

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution - Pondicherry General Sales Tax Act, 1965 - Pondicherry Sales Tax (Amednment) Act, 1966 - Pondicherry (Administration) Act, 1962 - Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1959 - Madras General Sales Tax Rules, 1959 - Whether Pondicherry General Sales Tax Act (Principal Act) was void and was a still-born legislation by reason of Pondicherry Legislature having abdicated its legislative function in favour of Madras State Legislature? - Held, it is difficult to see how such a case is not one of abdication or effacement in favour of another Legislature at least in regard to that particular matter - It is clear that Pondicherry Legislature not only adopted Madras Act as it stood at date when it passed Principal Act but also enacted that if Madras Legislature were to amend its Act prior to date when Pondicherry Govt. would issue its notification it would be amended Act which would apply - There was in these circumstances a total surrender in matter of sales tax legislation by Pondicherry Assembly in favour of Madras Legislature and for that reason we must hold that Act was void or as is often said 'still-born' - Petition allowed.
