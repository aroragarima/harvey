Commnr. of Customs (Imports), Mumbai v Messrs Tullow India Operations Limited

Supreme Court of India

28 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 707; (2005) 13 SCC 789; AIR 2006 SC 536; 2005 (189) E.L.T. 401; JT 2005 (10) SC 618; 2005(9) SCALE 58; [2005] Supp4 S.C.R. 973

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure; Trade

Summary: [A.] Customs - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 111(m), 111(o), 112(a), 14, 25 - Customs Tariff Act, 1975, s. 3 - Interpretation of notification issued in terms of s.25(1) of Act, being General Exemption No. 121 is in question - Essentiality certificate - Tribunal dismissed appeal of ONGC on ground that certificate had not been produced even before it and remitted matter back to Commissioner for consideration as regards acceptability thereof and consequently upon availability of exemption and related matter - Appealed herein - Held, Tribunal committed no illegality in remitting mater back to Commissioner - Appeal dismissed.
