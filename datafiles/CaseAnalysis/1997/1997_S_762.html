Sudhir Samanta v State of W. B. and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 October 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2992; (1998) SCC (Cr) 461; (1998) 1 SCC 581; AIR 1998 SC 289; 1998 CRLJ 495; JT 1997 (8) SC 677; 1997(6) SCALE 489; 1998 (5) Supreme 173

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304, Part II and 149 - Culpable homicide - Conviction with aid of s. 149 of Code - Land dispute - Assault by several armed trespassers resulting into death of complainant's son - No evidence of common object - (A) Vicarious liability of co-accused for overt act of accused - (B) Accused gave single blow with lathi on head of deceased and later another accused hit deceased with lathi - Survive for 32 hours thereafter - Whether acted with intention or only with knowledge - Held, not liable and their conviction, set aside - No intention but knowledge that their act was likely to cause death can be inferred so case falls u/s. 304, Part II and not u/s. 304, Part I of Code - Appeal allowed.
