Meesala Ramakrishan v State Of A.P.

Supreme Court of India

13 April 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1476; (1994) 4 SCC 182; (1994) SCC (Cr) 838; AIR 1994 SCW 1978; 1994 (2) CCR 385; 1994 (2) Crimes 114; 1994 (54) DLT 336; 1994 JCC 445; JT 1994 (3) SC 232; 1994(2) SCALE 569; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 497; 1996 (Supp) AWC 194

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Evidence Act, ss. 3 and 119 - Evidentiary value of dying declaration made by gestures - Appellant convicted on basis of dying declaration made by gestures - Held, dying declaration recorded on basis of nods and gestures is not only admissible but possesses evidentiary value, extent of which shall depend upon who recorded statement, what is his educational attainment, what gestures and nods were made, what were questions asked, whether they were simple or complicated and how effective or understandable nods and gestures were - In present case, questions being simple and short, recorder being a Magistrate, certifier of mental conscious state of deceased being a doctor, nods being effective and meaningful, full reliance could have been placed on statement of deceased as recorded to find appellant guilty u/s. 302 - Appeal dismissed.
