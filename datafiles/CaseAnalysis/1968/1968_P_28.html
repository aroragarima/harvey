Puttarangamma and 2 Others v M. S. Ranganna and 3 Others

Supreme Court of India

08 February 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 340; AIR 1968 SC 1018; 1968 (2) SCJ 668; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 119

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Family & Personal - Dispute as to fact that plaintiff died as a divided member of joint family as alleged in plaint or not - Whether it was not open to plaintiff to revoke or withdraw unambiguous intention to separate contained in plaint? - Held, when once a communication of intention is made which has resulted in severance of joint family status it was not thereafter open to plaintiff to nullify its effect so as to restore family to its original joint status - If intention of plaintiff had stood alone without giving rise to any legal effect, it could, of course, be withdrawn by plaintiff, but having communicated intention, divided status of Hindu joint family had already come into existence and legal consequences had taken effect - It was not, therefore, possible for plaintiff to get back to old position by mere revocation of intention - It is, of course, possible for members of family by a subsequent agreement to reunite, but mere withdrawal of unilateral declaration of intention to separate which already had resulted in division in status cannot amount to an agreement to reunite - It should also be stated that question whether there was a subsequent agreement between members to reunite is a question of fact to be proved as such - Appeal allowed.
