Balbir Singh v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

10 November 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, Faizan Uddin

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1112; (1995) 1 SCC 90; (1995) SCC (Cr) 202; AIR 1994 SCW 4981; 1995 (1) CCR 21; 1994 (3) Crimes 900; JT 1994 (7) SC 445; 1994(4) SCALE 904; [1994] Supp5 S.C.R. 422; 1995 (1) UJ 275

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Armed Forces, Air Force Act, 1950, Navy Act, 1957, Criminal Courts and Court Martial (Adjustment of Jurisdiction) Rules, 1952

Summary: Criminal - Air Force Act, 1950, ss. 124, 125 -IPC, 1860, 147, 325, 34, 302, 149 - CrPC, 1786, ss. 313 and 549 - (A) Whether offence for which Balbir Singh could be held liable for causing only one injury to deceased would not be one u/ss. 302/34 IPC, 1860? - Held, according to Dr. Sahni PW 8, death was caused by shock and haemorrhage due to injury to brain which was sufficient to cause death in ordinary course of nature - Keeping in view testimony of PW 7 Hazara Singh, PW 10 Karamjit Singh and Dr Sahni PW 8, it is obvious that by inflicting blows on head of deceased intention of appellants was to cause death of Pritam Singh and offence therefore was correctly found to be one u/ss. 302/34 IPC - (B) Whether trial of Nachhattar Singh was illegal and vitiated for lack of inherent jurisdiction of criminal court? - Held, it was argued that since no notice as envisaged by s. 475 CrPC and Rules framed thereunder r/w. ss. 124 and 125 of Air Force Act, 1950 was given by criminal court - A person subject to Act is charged with offences listed therein white on 'active service' he shall be deemed to be guilty of an offence against Act and could be tried by a court-martial irrespective of fact that victim is not subject to military, naval or air force law - It therefore follows that a person subject to Act would be deemed to be 'on active service' even when he is on casual leave - (C) Whether what ordinary criminal court is required to do when a person on 'active service' of Air Force is brought, before it for trial for any of offences listed in s. 72 of Act? - Held, it is here that s. 475 CrPC, 1952 Rules and ss. 124 and 125 of Air Force Act come into operation - Jurisdiction under which criminal courts have to take cognizance of civil offences is not taken away by any of provisions of Act or s. 475 CrPC and rules framed thereunder - Appeals dismissed.
