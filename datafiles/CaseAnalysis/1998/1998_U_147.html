S. K. Mathur and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1362; (1998) 4 SCC 134; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1034; AIR 1999 SC 129; 1998 (79) FLR 356; JT 1998 (2) SC 403; 1998 LabIC 3596; 1998 (2) LLJ 17; 1998 (3) LLN 74; 1998(2) SCALE 333; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 115; 1998 (3) SLJ 233; 1998 (2) SLR 250; 1998 (3) SLT 665; 1998 (3) Supreme 90

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Eligibility - Seniority - Cadre

Summary: Service - Seniority - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 and 309 - Bank Note Press Recruitment Rules, 1974 - Bank Note Press (Class III) Recruitment Rules, 1974 - For the posts of Inspector (Control), under art. 309 of the Constitution - Appellants shown senior to the direct recruits, i.e., respondents - Benefit of seniority was denied to them - (d) Writ filed with the High Court transferred to the Central Administrative Tribunal - Tribunal by its order denied benefit of past service to the petitioners - Tribunal held that a person on deputation is not entitled to the benefit of service rendered by him in the parent department - Whether the findings of the Tribunal were sustainable - Held, no - Respondents cannot be treated senior to appellants - Tribunal wrong in treating appellant to have joined department only from date of their substantive absorption - When recruitment is from different sources and integrated seniority list is prepared, benefit of service rendered on a similar post in a similar organization under the same employer will have to be given - Benefit of service rendered by the appellants cannot be denied - Tribunal acted contrary to the basic tenets of service law - Appeal allowed.
