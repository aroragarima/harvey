Vivek Batra v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

18 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 730; (2017) 1 SCC 69; AIR 2016 SC 4770; 2017 CRLJ 157; JT 2016 (10) SC 144; 2016 (4) RCR(Criminal) 679; 2017 (1) RLW 521; 2016(10) SCALE 64; [2016] 8 S.C.R. 177

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Omission, Irregularity, Government of India (Allocation of Business) Rules, 1961, Central Vigilance Commission Act, 2003, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, Corruption

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Central Vigilance Commission Act, 2003, s. 8(g) - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13, 19, 19(3) - Sanction for prosecution - Approval - Challenged - Appellant was an officer of Indian Revenue Service (IRS) - CBI/respondent no. 4 registered an FIR in respect of disproportionate assets to the known sources of appellant - Appellant was placed under suspension by authority concerned - CBI sought sanction for prosecution of appellant from competent authority - Central Vigilance Commission (CVC) recommended sanction for prosecution be granted - Finance Dept. referred the matter to Dept. of Personnel and Training (DOPT) which conveyed that sanction for prosecution can be granted - Competent authority by an order granted sanction for prosecution of appellant who challenged the same before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, sanction cannot be held invalid only for the reason that in the administrative notings different authorities have opined differently before competent authority took the decision in the matter. It is not a case where Finance Minister was not the competent authority to grant sanction. What was required u/s. 19 of the 1988 Act was that for taking the cognizance of an offence, punishable u/ss. 7, 10, 11, 13 and 15 of the 1988 Act committed by public servant is necessary by Central Govt. or State Govt. as the case may be and in the case of a public servant, who is neither employed in connection with affairs of Union or State, from the authority competent to remove him. Further, there was proper application of mind on the part of competent authority before the sanction was accorded. SC perusal of the said record does not indicate that any decision was taken by competent authority at any point of time, not to grant sanction so as to give the decision to grant sanction the colour of a review of any such earlier order, as has been contended before SC. The opinion of CVC, which was reaffirmed and ultimately prevailed in according the sanction cannot be said to be irrelevant for the reason that cl.(g) of s. 8 of the 2003 Act provides that it is one of the functions of CVC to tender advice to Central Govt. on such matters as may be referred to it by Govt. Thus, no reason to interfere with the impugned order passed by HC dismissing the writ petition. Trial Court was directed to conclude the trial expeditiously. Appeal dismissed.
