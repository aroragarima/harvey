Smt. Rani Kusum v Smt. Kanchan Devi and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 469; (2005) 6 SCC 705; AIR 2005 SC 3304; 2005 (61) ALR 132; 2005 (4) AWC 3861G; 2005 (4) JhrCR(SC) 118; JT 2005 (7) SC 409; 2005 (141) PLR 627; 2005 (3) RCR(Civil) 727; 2005 (99) RD 616; 2005 (4) RLW 2244; 2005(6) SCALE 545; [2005] Supp2 S.C.R. 752

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Consumer Protection; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 8, rr. 1, 10, s. 122 - Code of Civil Procedure (Amendment) Act, 2002 - Code Of Civil Procedure (Amendment) Act, 1999 - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 13(2) - Alleged non-compliance with requirements of o. 8, r.1 - Delay in filing written statement - Whether Court has discretion to extend period for filing written statement beyond 90 days? - Held, court in its discretion would have power to allow defendant to file written statement even after expiry of period of 90 days provided in o. 8, r.1 - No restriction that after expiry of ninety days, further time cannot be granted - Appeal dismissed.
