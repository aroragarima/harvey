Surya Kant Kadam v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 340; (2002) 9 SCC 445; (2002) SCC (L&S) 1115; AIR 2001 SC 2415; AIR 2001 SCW 2386; 2001 (2) AWC 1128; 2001 (2) CLR 307; 2001 (90) FLR 340; JT 2001 (3) SC 485; 2001 (34) LabIC 2366; 2002 (2) SLR 217; 2001 (4) Supreme 310

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Appellant appointed on compassionate appointment - Promotion - Whether promotion of similar appointed persons discriminatory? - Appellant has a justifiable grievance - True that appointment on compassionate ground in State of Karnataka is not governed by any statutory rules but by set of administrative instruction and as such is not enforceable in a Court of law - Held, appellant treated with hostile discrimination violative of art. 14 - Justifiable reason must exist for discrimination - Appeal allowed.
