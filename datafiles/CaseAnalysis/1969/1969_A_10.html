State Of Kerala v A.B. Abdul Khadir & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

30 July 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Ramaswami, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 152; (1969) 2 SCC 363; AIR 1970 SC 1912; 1969 KLT 649; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 700

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Trade

Keywords: Finance Act, 1960, Luxury Tax On Tobacco (Validation) Act, 1964

Summary: Administrative - Central Excise and Salt Act 1 of 1944 - Finance Act, 1960 - Luxury Tax on Tobacco (Validation) Act, 1964 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 301 and 304 - Licence fee - Levied - Legality - State was collecting licence fee from respondents on the strength of the rules framed by the State - In 1956 respondents filed O.P - HC for the refund of licence fee collected after 1-4-1950 on the ground that the Tobacco Act stood repealed by the 1960 Act - Because of the extension of the 1944 Act 1 to the Part B State and in consequence the notifications issued in August 1950 and January 1961 framing new rules for the issue of licences and prescribing fees therefor under the powers conferred by the Cochin and Travancore Acts were ab initio void because the Acts under which the notifications - HC dismissed the petition holding that the tax levied by virtue of the rules framed under the Tobacco Acts was not a duty of excise coming within the Union List but it was a tax on luxuries coming within entry 62 of the State List - On appeal SC allowed the appeal soon after the decision of SC respondent complained to appellant that a sum of Rs. 1,11,750 was illegally collected as licence fee, appellant refunded a sum of Rs. 73,500 but did not return the balance - Held, HC has not gone into the question whether the provisions of Act 9 of 1964 and the notification dt. 25-1-1951 issued under the Cochin Tobacco Act constitute such restrictions or impediments as directly and immediately hamper free flow of trade, commerce and intercourse and, therefore, fall within the prohibition imposed u/art. 301 of the Constitution - Unless the HC first comes to the finding on the available material whether or not there was infringement of the guarantee u/art. 301 of the Constitution the further question as to whether the statute was saved u/art. 304 of the Constitution did not arise and the principle laid down by instant Court in 1965 Indlaw SC 262 could not be invoked - Appeal allowed.
