State of Jharkhand and others Etc. v Shivam Coke Industries, Dhanbad, Etc.

Supreme Court of India

10 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 573; (2011) 8 SCC 656; [2011] 9 S.C.R. 1110; [2011] 43 VST 279

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Keywords: Bihar Finance Act, 1981, Bihar Finance (Amendment) Ordinance, 1989, Bihar Finance Amendment Act, 1984

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Practice & Procedure - Bihar Finance (Amendment) Ordinance, 1989, s. 7 - Bihar Finance Act, 1981, ss.46, 46(2), 46(4)-Bihar Finance Amendment Act, 1984, s. 10 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 3(a) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 137, s.137- Sales Tax - Assessment - Validity - Appeal against assessment of sales tax orders was preferred by respondent before Joint Commissioner of Commercial Taxes('JCCT') (Appeals), who remanded assessment orders to re-examine books of account and to re-determine nature of sales - Subsequently, Deputy Commissioner of Commercial Taxes('DCCT') passed revised assessment orders reversing previous inter-State sales into intra-State sales - Consequently, respondent filed application for refund of excess amount of tax paid by him - Inconsistency of revised assessment orders with appellate direction was found by new DCCT - JCCT(Administration) initiated proceeding suo motu u/s. 46(4) of the 1981Act and issued notice/Memo No. 883 directing respondent to furnish complete sets of books of account to ascertain legality and propriety of said revised assessment order, consequently, said revised assessment order was set aside by him - Respondent filed petition in HC and got notice/Memo No. 883 quashed - Appellant contended that findings arrived at by HC were erroneous and based on wrong readings of materials available on record - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Whether suo motu power of revision u/s. 46(4) of the 1981Act vested with Joint Commissioner was legally and properly exercised in instant case - Held, power of revision was vested with Tribunal and Joint Commissioner, which power was to be exercised on application by any person aggrieved, but subject to time limit prescribed - Commissioner, who on basis of application filed by aggrieved party, revised order passed by authority subordinate to him - JCCT had exercised power vested on him u/s. 46(4) of the 1981Act, which power in most cases concerning present appeals was exercised by him within period of 3 years but in some other cases beyond expiry of three years period - Aforesaid notice was issued by Joint Commissioner by exercising his individual suo motu power as provided u/s. 46(4) of the 1981Act - It was not case where such notice was issued on basis of application filed by Deputy Commissioner since there was absolutely no reference made of application sent by DCCT - Hence, it should not be said that while coming to aforesaid conclusion in impugned notice, JCCT was influenced only by opinion of DCCT - (B) Whether aforesaid exercise of power of drawing up revisional proceeding by exercising suo motu power was not exercised within period of limitation or within reasonable period of time - Held, period of limitation was prescribed for filing revision application by aggrieved party for initiation of revisional jurisdiction of JCCT which period was 90 days, as was stood at that relevant time - If legislature intended to provide for any period of limitation or intended to apply said provision of art. 137 into s. 46(4) of the 1981Act, legislature would have specifically said so in the Act itself - If no period of limitation had been prescribed, statutory authority should exercise its jurisdiction within reasonable period - Powers had been exercised within about 3 years of time in some cases and in some cases soon after the expiry of 3 years period - Hence, 3 years period should not be said to be very long period and therefore, power was exercised within reasonable period of time - (C) Whether order dt. 26-11-2007 passed by JCCT setting aside revised assessment order dt. 26-12-2003 was proper and could be maintained - Held, appellant could not dispute fact that respondent was somewhat handicapped in contesting aforesaid matter very effectively before JCCT - Hence, matter had been remitted to JCCT to pass order in accordance with law giving reasons for its decisions as expeditiously as possible - Order accordingly.
