Kumari Regina v St. Aloysius Higher Elementary School and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 688; (1972) 4 SCC 188; AIR 1971 SC 1920; 1972 (1) MLJ(SC) 37; [1971] S.C.R. 6; [1971] Supp S.C.R. 6

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Education - Madras Elementary Education Act, 1920 - Appellant Head Mistress in respondent school - Respondent's management served certain charges on appellant and called upon to reply same - Reply unsatisfactory - Appellant reduced to position of Asst. Teacher - Whether rules framed under Act, conferring authority upon educational authorities of State, were statutory and enforceable at instance of a person prejudicially affected by breach thereof? - Relationship between parties was master and servant - No mandatory injunction could be issued directing restoration of appellant as Head Mistress - Enforcement of rules is a matter between Govt. and management and a third party, such as a teacher, cannot derive any enforceable rights - Appeal dismissed.
