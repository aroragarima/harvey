T. Subramanian v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 3; (2006) 1 SCC 401; AIR 2006 SC 836; 2006 CRLJ 804; 2006 (1) JCC 197; JT 2006 (1) SC 101; 2006 (2) RLW 1152; 2006(1) SCALE 116; 2006 (1) SCJ 805; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 180

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 313, 4(1), 5(1)(a), 5(1)(d), 5(2) - IPC, 1860, s. 161 - Special Judge acquitted accused - HC reversed acquittal and convicted - Appeal against conviction - Held, sum of Rs. 200/- was paid by PW-1 to appellant - Question is whether appellant had demanded said amount as illegal gratification to show any official favour to PW-1 and whether said amount was paid by PW-1 and received by appellant as consideration for showing such official favour - Evidence shows that accused was falsely implicated at instance of PWs 1, 2 and 6 - If two views were possible from very same evidence, it cannot be said that prosecution had proved beyond reasonable doubt that appellant had received sum of Rs. 200/- As illegal gratification - Trial court was right in holding that charge against appellant was not proved - Appeal allowed.
