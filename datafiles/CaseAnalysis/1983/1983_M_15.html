S. K. Verma v Mahesh Chandra And Another

Supreme Court of India

02 September 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, D.A. Desai, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 70; (1983) 4 SCC 214; (1983) SCC (L&S) 510; AIR 1984 SC 1462; 1983 (47) FLR 313; 1983 LabIC 1483; 1983 (2) LLJ 429; 1983 (2) LLN 637; 1983(2) SCALE 199; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 799; 1983 (2) SLJ 570; 1983 (3) SLR 479; 1983 UJ 868

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Public Sector, Air Force Act, 1950, Life Insurance Corporation of India (Staff) Regulation 1960

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 ss. 2(a), 2(s) - Dismissal - Challenged - Appellant was working as Development Officer in LIC and was dismissed - Central Govt./appropriate Govt. u/s. 2(a) of the Act referred said dispute to Industrial Tribunal, which upheld preliminary objection and ruled that Appellant as Development Officer in LIC were not workmen u/s. 2(s) of the Act and said reference was held incompetent - Aggrieved appellant filed writ before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Development officers in LIC are workmen and they are considered as labour or management.

Held, terms and conditions of appointment shows that development officer is to be whole time employee of the LIC of India and that his operations are to be restricted to a defined area and he is liable to be transferred. He has no authority whatsoever to bind the Corporation in anyway and his principal duty appears to be to organise and develop the business of the Corporation in the area allotted to him and for that purpose to recruit active and reliable agents, to train them to canvass new business and to render post-sale services to policy-holders. He is expected to assist and inspire the agents. Even so he has not the authority to appoint agents or to take disciplinary action against them. He does not even supervise the work of the agents though he is required to train them and assist them. He is to be the 'friend, philosopher and guide' of the agents working within his jurisdiction and no more and he is expected to stimulate and excite the agents to work, while exercising no administrative control over them. In fact, it is admitted that he has no subordinate staff working under him and it is clear that the development officer cannot by any stretch of imagination be said to be engaged in any administrative or managerial work. He is a workman within the meaning u/s. 2 (s) of the Act. Hence, order of the Tribunal and the judgment of the HC are set aside and the matter is remitted to the Tribunal for disposal according to law. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Disputes between the forces of labour and management are not to be excluded from the operation of the Act by giving narrow and restricted meanings to expressions in the Act.
