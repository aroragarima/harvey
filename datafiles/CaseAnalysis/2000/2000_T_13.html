Sheo Nand and Others v Deputy Director of Consolidation, Allahabad and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 190; (2000) 3 SCC 103; AIR 2000 SC 1141; 2000 ALJ 703; 2000 (39) ALR 352; 2000 (2) AWC 1276; JT 2000 (2) SC 332; 2000 (3) MLJ(SC) 26; 2000 (91) RD 213; 2000(1) SCALE 648; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 619; 2000 (3) SLT 48; 2000 (3) Supreme 174

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Land & Property - Uttar Pradesh Consolidation of Holdings Act, 1954, ss. 11C and 48 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 296, sch. 7, list I, entry 32, list 2, entry 35, entry 44 - Succession Act, 1925, s. 34 - Escheat, lapse, bona vacantia - Vesting of property in Gaon Sabha on civil death - Owner, not heard of for 7 years - Persons claiming his property found not entitled to it - (A) Consolidation authorities recording property in Gaon Sabha's name - Validity - (B) Revision - Entries in revenue record found forged and fictitious - Powers of deputy director - Held, justified - He could re-open entire case in respect of all villages and adjudicate upon them in exercise of powers u/s. 48 of Act - Appeal dismissed.
