Lalita Jalan And Anr v Bombay Gas Co. Ltd. And Ors

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, S. Rajendra Babu, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 328; (2003) 6 SCC 107; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1281; AIR 2003 SC 3157; 2003 (2) CCR 75; 4 CLC 531; [2003] 114 Comp Cas 515; 2003 (3) CompLJ 75; 2003 (2) Crimes 444; 2003 (5) CRJ 508; 2003 CRLJ 2514; 2003 (97) FLR 828; 2003 (2) JCC 989; JT 2003 (4) SC 64; 2003 (2) LLJ 829; 2003 (41) MLJ 637; 2003 (4) MPLJ 8; 2003 (3) RCR(Criminal) 76; 2003(4) SCALE 52; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 589; 2003 (3) SLT 204; 2003 (3) Supreme 402

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Criminal; Family & Personal

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1956 - s. 630 - Constitution - Art 21 - Whether the appellants, having not vacated the flat after the death of the employee to whom it was allotted in his capacity as Director of the Company, comes within the ambit of s.630 of the Act? - Held, all those who have come in possession of the premises with the express or implied consent of the employee and have not vacated the premises would be withholding the delivery of the property to the company and, therefore, they are liable to be prosecuted u/s.630 of the Act - Further, the possession of the property by an employee or anyone claiming through him of such property is unlawful and recovery of the same on the pain of being committed to a prison or payment of fine cannot be stated to be unreasonable or irrational or unfair so as to attract the rigour of art.21 of the Constitution - Conflict in the two decisions of the SC - Held, the view expressed in J K (Bombay) Ltd is not correct and the view expressed in Abhilash Vinodkumar Jain is justified and should be accepted in interpreting the provision of s.630 of the Act - Appeal dismissed.
