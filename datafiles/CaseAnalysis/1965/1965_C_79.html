Mathura Prasad v Commissioner of Income Tax, Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 319; [1966] 60 ITR 428

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s.66 - Hindu undivided family - Assessment - Agreement of partnership - Salary - Allowance - Joint family funds - A Hindu undivided family/ HUF consisting of five brothers - J, H, M, S, B and their nephew/D, son of X, deceased owned considerable property and carried on different businesses in names of P, Q, R and S - A partition of properties and businesses of family was made among six branches and as a result partition of a sixth share was allotted in assets partitioned to smaller HUF of which M was manager - After partition of joint family estate, managers of six branches entered into an agreement of partnership to carry on in name of X businesses which were formerly conducted on behalf of larger HUF - For assessment year 1950-51 HUF of M filed a return for previous year Samvat 2006 - M filed another return in status of an individual for same previous year and in that year he disclosed an income of Rs.21,000 - It received as remuneration from firm of XJ - Whether sum of Rs. 21,000 received by M as salary from P was income of assessee family in his personal capacity - Held, Tribunal had found that M had become a partner in firm of XJ with aid of funds of HUF and as a partner of firm he was entrusted with management of P and he earned the allowance which was claimed to be salary - Right to draw allowance was in view of Tribunal made possible by use of family funds - Family funds enabled him to become a partner and to claim allowance for services rendered - Right to draw allowance aroused directly from joint family funds - M earned allowance sought to be brought to tax because of special aptitude he possessed for managing P and allowance claimed by him was not earned by use of joint family funds - Remuneration earned by a person from a partnership in which he was inducted because he brings into partnership his joint family funds, would be regarded as separate income of such person and not taxable in hands of Hindu undivided family - Appeal dismissed.
