Golla Jalla Reddy and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2855; (1996) 8 SCC 565; (1996) SCC (Cr) 713; 1996 (3) AD(SC) 709; AIR 1996 SC 3244; 1996 (2) CCR 168; 1996 (2) Crimes 242; 1996 CRLJ 2470; JT 1996 (4) SC 587; 1996(3) SCALE 791; [1996] Supp1 S.C.R. 772; 1996 (4) Supreme 499

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 20, 154 and 161 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 300 - Supreme Court (Enlargement Of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970 - Omissions in FIR as to name of certain witness by informant who rushed to police station for fear of life after seen three murders - (A) Material omissions in copy of statement of witness furnished to accused as compared to statement made u/s. 161 - If defence can rely upon same - (B) What is second report of incident after oral complaint? - Oral complaint disclosing cognizable offence and police investigation starting on its basis - (C) Inference of common intention to murder - Statement that accused while chasing deceased was joined by two other accused - Certain catching hold of deceased while other assaulted - Held, defence is entitled to - Written report has to be considered as statement u/s. 161 and not FIR and cannot be used for corroboration of evidence of informant - Further held, evidence coupled with fact of victim meeting with death with many injuries proves it - Appeal dismissed.
