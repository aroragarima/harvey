Chamoli District Co-Operative Bank Limited, Through Its Secretary/Mahaprandhak and another v Raghunath Singh Rana and others

Supreme Court of India

17 May 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhushan, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 386; AIR 2016 SC 2510; 2016(4) ALL MR 494; 2016 (117) ALR 538; 2016 (4) AWC 3457; JT 2016 (5) SC 333; 2016 (5) MLJ 32; 2016 (5) MLJ 318; 2016(5) SCALE 731; [2016] 7 S.C.R. 86

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Natural Justice, Suspension, Evidence, Award, Reasonable Opportunity, Violation Of, Enquiry Officer, Procedure, Representation, Appeal, Dismissal, Charges, Employer, Misconduct, Uttar Pradesh Co-operative Societies Act, 1965, U. P. Co-operative Societies Employees Service Regulations, 1975, Writ Petition, Notice, Employee, Right to

Summary: Service - Uttar Pradesh Co-operative Societies Employees Service Regulations, 1975, regn. 85 - Dismissal from service - Holding of Inquiry - Initially charge sheet dt.3-7-1992 was issued to employee leveling charges against him and inquiry was conducted and inquiry report was submitted but no further steps were taken on report though employee was placed under suspension - Later, fresh charge sheet containing charges already levelled in earlier charges sheet as well as six additional charges, was issued on 16-1-1993 and Disciplinary Authority passed order on 1-2-2002, dismissing employee from services - Aggrieved, employee filed writ petition before HC and DB of HC quashed dismissal order holding that impugned orders were passed without holding inquiry - Hence, instant appeal by appellant-Bank - Whether dismissal of employee is sustainable.

Held, no enquiry was conducted by appellant-Bank after issuance of charge sheet dt.16-1-1993. Hence, as employee was not given opportunity to produce his witnesses in his defence and not been given an opportunity of being heard in person, the statutory provisions as enshrined in regn.85(i)(b), were violated. Further, Disciplinary Authority issued show cause notice dt. 4-5-1993 to employee without holding inquiry and so, subsequent resolution by Disciplinary Authority taken in year 2000 without their being any further steps is unsustainable. Hence, HC rightly quashed dismissal order by giving liberty to bank to hold de-novo inquiry within a period of six months, if it so desires. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - As per regn.85 of Regulations, employee is to be given opportunity to produce at his own cost or to cross-examine witnesses in his defence and should also be given an opportunity of being heard in person, if so desires.
