Assistant Commercial Taxes Officer v M/S. Bajaj Electricals Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

06 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1921; (2009) 1 SCC 308; JT 2008 (12) SC 222; 2008(14) SCALE 380; [2008] 15 S.C.R. 513; [2008] 18 VST 436

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Sales Tax

Keywords: Waiver Of Penalty, Value Of Goods, Personal Effects, Concessional Rate Of Tax, Inter-State Trade, Exemption From Payment, Statement Of Objects And Reasons, Estimated Value, Sale In The Course Of Export, Transporter, Check Post, Penalty Order, Processing, Intention To Evade Tax, Sale Outside The State, Appeal By Special Leave, Interstate Sales, Interstate Sale, Rajasthan Sales Tax Rules, 1995

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(a), 301, 304 - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1994, ss. 78(2), 78(2)(a), 78(5) - Show cause notice - Penalty - Maintainability - Consignor vehicle was stopped for checking show cause notice was issued - A.O. declared that goods were imported without Declaration Form ST 18A and penalty was imposed - Aggrieved by decision appeal was made to Commissioner (A) which was dismissed - Respondent preferred appeal before Tribunal which was allowed - Aggrieved by decision revision petition was filed before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of lower Court was maintainable - Held, Commissioner (A) was right in holding that all excuses were trotted out as an afterthought and production of Form before Commissioner (A) was an afterthought - Further, Under the 1995 Rules, consignor was required to give said Form duly filled in when consignment was ordered and consignee had to see that Form was given to transporter with complete details duly filled by consignor - Furthermore, it was clear that it would be duty of consignee or his agent (transporter) to see that consignor fills Form - Therefore, said Form in ST 18A though signed, remained incomplete and details required were never supplied - Thus, penalty was correctly levied u/s. 78 (5) of the 1994 Act - Appeal allowed.
