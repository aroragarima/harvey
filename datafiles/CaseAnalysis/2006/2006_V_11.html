State of Gujarat and Others v Vali Mohmed Dosabhai Sindhi

Supreme Court of India

19 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 342; (2006) 6 SCC 537; AIR 2006 SC 2735; 2006 (6) AWC 6237; 2007 (2) G.L.R. 1064; JT 2006 (6) SC 468; 2006(7) SCALE 206; 2006 (5) SLR 120; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 685

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - Births, Deaths and Marriages Registration Act, I886 - Bombay Civil Services Rules, 1959, r. 171 - Change of date of birth not allowed - Held, rule clearly provides that request made for alteration of date of birth should not be entertained after preparation of service book of Govt. servant and in any event not after completion of probation period or after 5 years of continuous service whichever was earlier - Once an entry of age or date of birth has been made in service book, no alteration of entry afterwards should be allowed unless it is shown that entry was due to want of care on part of some person other than individual in question or is an obvious clerical error - Appeal allowed.
