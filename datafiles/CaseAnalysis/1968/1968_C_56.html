Laxmipat Singhania v Commissioner of Income Tax, Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 299; AIR 1969 SC 501; [1969] 72 ITR 291; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 904

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss.16(2), 23a (1), 23a (4) - W Ltd Company failed to declare dividend to extent of 60% of assessable income of company of its previous year as reduced by amount of income-tax and super-tax payable - Income-tax Officer ordered u/s.23A of Act determined the shares of different shareholders to whom the income was deemed to be distributed, but gave no effect to order by including proportionate shares in amount of deemed income in individual assessments of shareholders for appropriate assessment year - Income-tax Appellate Tribunal agreed with order of Income-tax Officer - On appeal, HC answered the question in affirmative - Hence instant appeal - Whether dividend of Rs.23, 328 which was credited in accounts of assessee during accounting period of a/y 1943-44 could be subjected to tax u/s.16(2) of Act - Held, cl. was enacted with the object of preventing double taxation of same income it was enacted thereby that if tax was paid by an assessee in respect of his proportionate share in dividend deemed to be distributed in consequence of an order u/s.23A of Act, any actual distribution of that dividend will not be liable to be taxed - It does not confer any option on Income-tax Officer to tax either deemed income in hands of shareholder on footing that it has accrued at the date of distribution u/s.23A(1) of Act or at the date of actual receipt of share u/s.23A(4) of Act- Provision which prevents double taxation in respect of same income, once at stage of deemed receipt, and another at stage of actual receipt, cannot be converted into an enactment enabling taxation at stage of receipt - Unable to agree with the observations of HC - Answer recorded by HC discharged - Appeal allowed.,
