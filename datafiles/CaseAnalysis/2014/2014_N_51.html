Nesar Ahmed and another v State of Jharkhand and others

Supreme Court of India

25 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 173; (2014) 11 SCC 394; AIR 2014 SC 1836; 2015(2) ALL MR 445; JT 2014 (5) SC 172; [2014] 3 S.C.R. 144; 2014 (3) SLR 607

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Jharkhand Primary School Appointment Rules, 2002

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 309 - Jharkhand Primary School Appointment Rules, 2002, rr. 4(d), 8(d) - Appointment - Trained teachers - Age relaxation - Legality - Petitioners after getting the requisite training they acquired the nomenclature of 'trained teachers' - They sought appointments in the schools run by respondent/State as assistant teachers - In essence, petitioners challenged amended rr. 4(d) and 8(d) of the Rules, claiming that these provisions were not only unconstitutional but in violation of the directions given in the earlier judgment - It was specifically pleaded that there could not have been upper age limit for appointment of trained teacher - Though applications were invited from only trained teachers but age relaxation upto 5 years only was given - That was challenged as arbitrary, malafide and against public interest - In the said judgment various other issues regarding composition of State Public Service Commission were touched and considered as well - Petitions which were filed certain trained teachers and their associations they were dismissed by the HC - HC thus refused to extend the benefit of total age relaxation but limited it upto 5 years, as envisaged in the Rule - No further challenge was laid to that judgment allowing it to attain finality - Appointments were made in accordance with the 2002 Rules - Thereafter another advertisement was issued in the year 2008 further and further appointments were made on the basis thereof - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned orders -

Held, it would be difficult to give any relief to petitioners - In fact, what petitioners were demanding now was sought to be given by the State in the form of un-amended r. 4 by providing one time relaxation in upper age limit - However, that Rule was struck down as un-Constitutional - Giving the relief claimed in writ petitions would amount to negating the judgment of the HC though it has become final - Moreover, recruitments were made in the year 2003 wherein many such teachers participated - For last 10 years, respondent was making appointments of trained teachers and it was not the case of petitioners that untrained teachers were appointed - Appointments were made by following the Recruitment Rules scrupulously - Petitioners in these writ petition did not even disclose the facts pertaining to the two rounds of litigation in HC - Only after the second recruitment process which was held in year 2008, instant writ petitions were filed in the year 2010 or thereafter - It would not be permissible to petitioners to compare their case with their counterparts in Bihar - As far as the counterparts in the State of Bihar were concerned they had filed writ petitions well in time i.e. way back in the year 2003 wherein those persons succeeded - In such circumstances SC would not be inclined to grant any relief to petitioners in these petitions filed u/art. 32 of the Constitution, more so when it was found that respondent had taken steps in conformity with the statutory recruitment rules framed under proviso to art. 309 of the Constitution - Petitions dismissed.
