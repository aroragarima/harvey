Commissioner Of Income Tax, Lucknow v Onkar Saran And Sons

Supreme Court of India

13 March 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, A.S. Anand, Veeraswami Ramaswami

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1133; (1992) 2 SCC 514; AIR 1992 SC 1139; 1992 (103) CTR 293; [1992] 195 ITR 1; JT 1992 (2) SC 567; 1992 (1) KLT 769; 1992(1) SCALE 651; [1992] 2 S.C.R. 187; [1992] 62 TAXMAN 440; 1992 (2) TLR 463

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 271(1)(c) - Concealment of income Penalty - Amendment of s. 271(1)(c) making penalty dependent on amount of income concealed - Return of income filed before amendment - Detection of concealed income - Notice u/s. 148 for reassessment - Return for reassessment filed after date of amendment - Reassessment completed - Imposition - Held, law applicable would be that prevailing on date of original returns - Penalty correctly scaled down on basis of tax sought to be avoided as provided before amendment - Appeals dismissed.
