A. P. Power Coordination Committee and others v Lanco Kondapalli Power Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

16 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 765; (2016) 3 SCC 468; AIR 2016 SC 1925; 2016 (2) CLT(SC) 246; JT 2015 (11) SC 52; 2015(11) SCALE 714; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 447

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Carriage by Air Act, 1972, Tariff, Tamil Nadu Shops and Establishments Act, 1947, Public Servants (Inquiries) Act, 1850, Indian Electricity Supply Act, 1948, Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Act, 1958

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 11 - Electricity Act, 2003, ss. 174, 86(1)(f) - Limitation Act, 1963, ss. 14, 5- Exclusion of period pursuing Arbitration proceeding - Reimbursement of Tax - Determination - State Electricity Board had invited bids for projects which was awarded to respondent - Further, respondent set up plant, however, completion of plant took more than scheduled period of 16 months - Further, respondent raised bill, however, bill for capacity charges was disallowed ground that it was not in accordance with agreement - Respondent company issued arbitration notice and filed application before HC for appointment of arbitrator - HC held that State Commission had authority to appoint arbitrator, hence, dismissed application - Appellant resisted claim on ground on limitation - Commission rejected claim of respondent company vide order on ground of limitation by holding that time spent in arbitration proceedings did not merit exclusion u/s. 14 of 1963 Act because it had not been pursued in good faith - Further, Tribunal allowed appeal of respondent Company vide impugned order and reversed findings of Commission on issue of limitation and directed Commission to pass appropriate follow up order on actual claims and interest - Further, with respect to reimbursement of Minimum Alternate Tax (MAT), same was allowed in favor of respondent Company - Whether impugned order passed by Tribunal in favor of respondent Company is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, with respect to limitation period, SC held that by itself 1963 Act will not be applicable to Commission under 2003 Act as Commission is not Court stricto sensu. Further, no separate limitation has been prescribed for exercise of power u/s. 86(1)f) of 2003 Act nor adjudicatory power of Commission has been enlarged to entertain even time barred claims, there is no conflict between provisions of 2003 Act and 1963 Act to attract provisions of s. 174 of 2003 Act. Further, SC held that claim coming before Commission cannot be entertained or allowed if it is barred by limitation prescribed for ordinary suit before civil court. However, in appropriate case, specified period may be excluded on account of principle underlying salutary provisions like ss. 5 or 14 of 1963 Act. Hence, SC held that respondent rightly chose to seek exclusion of period it was pursuing arbitration proceeding before HC on basis of principles underlying s. 14 of 1963 Act. Further, SC held that Tribunal has adopted just and lawful approach in examining relevant facts and in excluding entire period claimed by respondent which starts from notice for arbitration till application u/s. 11 of under 1996 Act before HC. Further, with respect to reimbursement of MAT, SC held that on account of upholding order of Tribunal on issue of limitation, claim of MAT for 2001-2005 cannot be treated as barred by limitation. Further, it is clear from agreement that parties were aware that tax regime keeps changing; hence, any advance income tax payable for income from project only had to be reimbursed by Board. Hence, Board cannot avoid liability to reimburse advance income tax paid by respondent on ground that MAT was new variety of tax concept introduced. Hence, impugned order passed by Tribunal is justified and in accordance with law. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - Limitation period will not be applicable in appropriate case when same is specifically provided under statute.
