Shri Sunil Siddharthbhai Etc. v Commissioner Of Income Tax, Ahmedabad Etc.

Supreme Court of India

27 September 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, Amarendra Nath Sen, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 335; (1985) 4 SCC 519; (1986) SCC (Tax) 50; AIR 1986 SC 368; 1985 CTR 172; [1985] 156 ITR 509; 1985(2) SCALE 755; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 102; [1985] supp3 S.C.R. 102; [1985] 23 TAXMAN 1400

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 45 and 48 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 5 - Capital gains - Transfer of capital assets - Appellant, was a partner in a partnership firm constituted under a deed of partnership - As his contribution to the capital of the partnership firm the appellant made over certain shares of limited companies which were held by him as his capital assets - The book value of those shares in appellant's account books was shown as Rs. 1,60,279, but on the date when he contributed those shares to the partnership firm he revalued the shares at the market value of Rs. 1,49,819 and debited the resulting difference of Rs. 10,460 to his capital account - Income Tax Officer (ITO), when drawing up the assessment order for the a/y 1974-75 in respect of the assessee, did not include the difference in the assessable income - However, Commissioner being of opinion that the difference between the market value of the shares and the cost of acquisition of the shares to the assessee should have been brought to tax as capital gains in view of s. 45 of the 1961 Act, exercised his revisional jurisdiction, and reopening the assessment and remanded the case to the ITO directing him to revise the assessment after computing the capital gains arising out of the transfer - On appeal, Tribunal held that while the transaction did amount to a transfer within the meaning of sub-section (47) of s. 2 of the 1961 Act it did not result in capital gains liable to tax - Hence, the present appeal - (A) Whether tribunal rightly held that the contribution in the form of shares by the appellant in the partnership firm did not result in capital gains liable to tax? - Held, whatever may be the character of the property which is brought in by the partners when the partnership is formed or which may be acquired in the course of the business of the partnership it becomes the property of the firm and what a partner is entitled to is his share of profits, if any, accruing to the partnership from the realisation of this property, and upon dissolution of the partnership to a share in the money representing the value of the property - No doubt, since a firm has no legal existence., the partnership property will vest in all the partner, and in that sense every partner has an interest in the property of the partnership - During the subsistence of the partnership, however, no partner can deal with any portion of the property as his own - Nor can he assign his interest in a specific item of the partnership property to anyone - His right is to obtain such profits, if any, as fall to his share from time to time and upon the dissolution of the firm to L share in the assets of the firm - Hence, there was a transfer of the shares when the assessee made them over to the partnership firm as his capital contribution - (B) Whether the appellant can be said to have received any consideration as that expression is understood in the scheme of capital gains under the Income-Tax Act? - Held, when the appellant transferred his shares to the partnership firm he received no consideration within the meaning of s. 48 of the 1961 Act nor did any profit or gain accrue to him for the purpose of s. 45 of 1961 Act - Appeals partly allowed.
