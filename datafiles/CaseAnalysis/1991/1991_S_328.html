Triveni Shankar Saxena v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 December 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 434; (1992) SCC (L&S) 440; (1992) Supp1 SCC 524; AIR 1992 SC 496; 1992 (19) ATC 931; 1992 (64) FLR 330; JT 1992 (1) SC 37; 1992 LabIC 299; 1992 (2) LLJ 23; 1992 (1) LLN 405; 1991(2) SCALE 1421; 1992 (1) SLR 359; [1991] Supp3 S.C.R. 534; 1992 (1) UPLBEC 41

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Character Roll, Uttar Pradesh Fundamental Rules, Railway Fundamental Rules

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 309 and 311 - Uttar Pradesh Fundamental Rules, rr. 14A and 14B - Termination of temporary service of Lekhapa l- (A) When lien is acquired? - (B) Legality of termination order on basis of adverse entries without casting stigma or disclosing penal consequences - (C) Ex gratia payment - Termination of temporary employee who was allowed to work 18 years despite adverse entries - Held, employee acquires lien on post only when he has been confirmed and made permanent on that post and not earlier - Not illegal - Ex gratia payment directed to be paid - Appeal dismissed.
