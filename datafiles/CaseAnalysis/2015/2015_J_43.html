Charu Kishor Mehta v Joint Charity Commissioner, Greater Bombay Region and others

Supreme Court of India

12 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 204; (2015) 8 SCC 207; 2015(3) ALL MR 897; 2015 (3) Bom.C.R. 42; JT 2015 (4) SC 357; 2016 (2) MahLJ 18; 2015 (3) RCR(Civil) 1; 2015(3) SCALE 486; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 846

Case Digest

Subject: Trusts & Associations

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Bombay Public Trusts Act, 1950 - Delegation of powers - Appellant filed application u/s. 41D of the Act before Commissioner making various allegations against respondent nos. 2 to 9 including deceased-MD alleging that they were liable for dismissal from their trusteeship of Trust for their acts of nonfeasance and malfeasance - However, Commissioner dismissed application against respondent nos. 2 to 9 holding that they were only negligent in putting blind faith in delegating their powers and duties to be performed as trustees of the Trust in favour of deceased-MD - Aggrieved, appellant filed writ petition before Single Judge of HC but the same was dismissed and further appeal before DB of HC was also rejected - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned judgments are sustainable.

Held, delegation of all the powers and functions by respondent nos. 2 to 9 and also the appellant in favour of the Managing Trustee of the Trust deceased-MD is permissible in law as the instrument of the Trust provided for that. Further, since appellant had not entered into witness box and also as similar charges as levelled against respondent nos. 2 to 9 were also pending against her before Commissioner for being signatory to Resolution dt.30-8-2001 in delegating her powers and functions to deceased-MD, Commissioner should not have accepted evidence of original respondent no. 10 before Commissioner and recorded the findings on charges in his order. Furthermore, Commissioner had rightly held that the guilt of respondent nos. 2 to 9 are not proved and has also held that they are negligent for only delegating their powers and functions to the Managing Trustee, deceased-MD but the charges leveled against them are not proved as they are not responsible for the alleged transactions therefore, there is no complicity on the allegations made against them. The said conclusion of Commissioner is based on facts and evidence on record, hence, has rightly exonerated respondent nos. 2 to 9 from the liability and has not imposed punishment u/s. 41D of the Act, in exercise of his power in a reasonable and fair manner and hence, same could not be said to be arbitrary and unreasonable in nature. Hence, HC has rightly concurred with the findings of the Commissioner in exonerating respondent nos. 2 to 9 from the charges levelled against them by passing well reasoned judgment and order. Hence, impugned judgment and order(s) of HC and order of Commissioner are legal and valid and the same could not be interfered with. Appeal dismissed.
