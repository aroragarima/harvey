State of Bombay v Automobile and Agricultural Industries Corporation, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

06 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 105; 1961 (12) STC 122

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1953, ss. 21(2)and 21(4) - Interpretation of s. 21(2) - Whether respondents prohibited from collecting general tax? - Under Act dealers in special goods were liable to pay two taxes, general tax and special tax - A person selling goods is enjoined by sub-section (2) of s. 21 not to collect from purchaser any amount by way of tax unless he is registered dealer or licensed dealer and is liable to pay tax in respect of such sale - This prohibition does not operate if a person is a registered dealer or a licensed dealer - Held, legislature appears expressly to enable a dealer to collect special tax and general tax if he is either a licensed dealer or a registered dealer and there is no ground for holding that prohibition against collection was to be read distributably - Sub-section (2) of s. 21 is a penal provision contained in a taxing statute and court cannot speculate contrary to plain intendment of words used about object of legislature - If legislature has failed to clarify its meaning by use of appropriate language, benefit thereof must go to taxpayer - It is settled law that in case of doubt, that interpretation of a taxing statute which is beneficial to taxpayer must be adopted - Further held, respondents being licensed dealers though not registered dealers are not prohibited from collecting general tax - Tribunal was right in holding that Sales Tax Officer has no jurisdiction to forfeit amount of general tax collected by respondents - Appeal dismissed.
