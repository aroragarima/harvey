Land Acquisition Officer and Mandal Revenue Officer v V. Narasaiah

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20254; (2001) 3 SCC 530; AIR 2001 SC 1117; 2001 (3) ALD(SC) 1; 2001 (43) ALR 164; 2001 (2) AWC 1014; JT 2001 (3) SC 157; 2001 (1) KLT 885; 2001 (3) RCR(Civil) 255B; 2001(2) SCALE 257; 2001 (2) SCJ 268; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 141; 2001 (2) SLT 532; 2001 (2) Supreme 187; 2001 (1) UC 597; 2001 (2) UPLBEC 1530

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1) and 51-A - High Court enhanced land value in land acquisition case taking into consideration two sale deeds, copy of which were marked without examining anybody connected with transaction recorded in instruments - Challenged - Whether court should not have taken into account sale price shown in sale deeds as claimant did not examine vendee or vendor or anybody else connected with sale? - Held, no - S. 51-A says that certified copy of a registered document "may be accepted as evidence of transaction recorded in such document" it enables court to treat what is recorded in document, in respect of transactions referred to therein, as evidence - However, this will not prevent any party who supports or opposes said document or transaction recorded therein to adduce other evidence to substantiate their stand regarding such transactions - Merely accepting them as evidence does not mean that court is bound to treat them as reliable evidence - Court could consider certified copies of sale deed without examining persons connected therein - State has burden to prove market value of lands acquired by it for which it may have to depend upon prices of lands similarly situated - HC cannot, therefore, be faulted for relying on transactions recorded though no one was examined for proving such transactions - No evidence had been adduced by state for creating any doubt regarding bona fides or genuineness of transactions mentioned therein - Appeal dismissed.
