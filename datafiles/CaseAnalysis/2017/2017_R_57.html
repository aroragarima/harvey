Union of India and others v Rakesh Kumar and others

Supreme Court of India

24 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhushan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 211; AIR 2017 SC 1691; 2017 (153) FLR 397; JT 2017 (3) SC 380; 2017 (2) LLJ 686; 2017(4) SCALE 16

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Computation Of, Regularisation, Leave, Pension, Permanent, Seniority, Employment, Rule, Date, Pensionary Benefits, Qualifying Service, Appeal, Regular, Absorption, Policy, Temporary Service, Scheme, Temporary, Relaxation, Railway Employees, Railway Services (Pension) Rules, 1993, Railways, Punjab Civil Services (General and Common Conditions of service) Rules, 1994, Lien, Writ Petition, Notice, Employee, Constitution of India, 1950, Post, Appointment

Summary: Service - Railway Services (Pension) Rules, 1993, rr. 107, 20, 31 - Regularisation of Contractual Labour - Grant of Pension Benefits Challenged - Respondent nos.1-24 /labours were initially appointed on casual labours, granted temporary status and subsequently regularised against regular post by appellant/union govt. - Tribunal allowed application of labour on ground that casual labour after grant of temporary status was entitled to reckon 100 percent period of service with temporary status for pensionary benefit - HC dismissed writ petition of appellant and upheld findings of Tribunal - Hence, instant appeals - Whether entire services of casual worker after obtaining temporary status till his regular absorption on a post was entitled to be reckoned for pensionary benefit or only 50 per cent period of such service could be reckoned for pensionary benefit.

Held, r. 20 of the Rules provides that qualifying service shall commence from the date the employee takes charge of the post to which he is first appointed either substantively or in an officiating or temporary capacity. The casual worker after obtaining temporary status is entitled to reckon 50% of his services till he is regularised on a regular/temporary post for the purposes of calculation of pension. Further, the casual worker before obtaining the temporary status is also entitled to reckon 50% of casual service for purposes of pension. Hence, casual workers who are appointed to any post either substantively or in officiating or in temporary capacity are entitled to reckon the entire period from date of taking charge to such post as per r. 20 of the Rules.Thus, the grant of temporary status of casual labour is not akin to appointment against a post and such contingency is not covered by r. 20 and the same is expressly covered by r. 31 which provides for 'half the service paid from contingencies shall be taken into account for calculating pensionary benefits on absorption in regular employment subject to certain conditions enumerated there in. Hence, impugned judgment of HC is set aside, thereby writ petitions of appellants are allowed. The judgments of Tribunal are set aside and applications of respondents are disposed of. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Grant of temporary status to casual labour is not equivalent to grant of an appointment against a post.
