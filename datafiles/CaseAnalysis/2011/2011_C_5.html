Sushil Suri v C.B.I. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 317; (2011) 5 SCC 708; AIR 2011 SC 1713; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 1994; 2011 (4) CompLJ 9; 2011 CRLJ 2939; JT 2011 (5) SC 480; 2011 (4) MLJ(Crl) 695; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 356; 2011 (3) RLW 2028; 2011(5) SCALE 412; [2011] 8 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 420, 409, 468 and 471 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Quashing of criminal prosecution - FIR was registered against the Company and its directors punishable u/s. 120B, r/w ss. 420, 409, 468 and 471 of IPC - Allegations against the appellant as also the co-accused was that the Company, acting through its directors in concert with the Chartered Accountants and some other persons: (i) conceived a criminal conspiracy and executed it by forging and fabricating a number of documents showing purchase of machinery in order to support their claim to avail hire purchase loan from PSB; (ii) on the strength of false documents, PSB parted with the money by issuing pay orders and demand drafts in favour of the Company and (iii) the accused opened six fictitious accounts in the banks to encash the pay orders/bank drafts issued by PSB in favour of the suppliers of machines, thereby directly rotating back the loan amount to the borrower from these fictitious accounts, and in the process committed a systematic fraud on the Bank (PSB) and obtained pecuniary advantage for themselves - Chief Metropolitan Magistrate took cognizance of the offences and summoned the appellants - Appellants filed petition u/s. 482 of the CrPC, praying for quashing of the Chargesheet mainly on the ground that once the Company had repaid the loan to PSB along with interest, no loss was caused to PSB and, therefore, they had not committed any offence for which Chargesheet had been filed - HC held that merely because the Company and its directors had repaid the loan to PSB they could not be exonerated of the offences committed by forging/fabricating the documents with the intention of defrauding the bank as well as the exchequer - HC held that on the peculiar facts of the case, the appellant was not entitled to any relief - Hence, instant appeal - Whether on facts in instant case, the HC was correct in law in declining to exercise its jurisdiction u/s. 482 of CrPC? - Held, s. 482 of the CrPC itself envisages three circumstances under which the inherent jurisdiction may be exercised by the HC, namely: (i) to give effect to an order under the CrPC; (ii) to prevent an abuse of the process of Court; and (iii) to otherwise secure the ends of justice - In the instant case, in the Chargesheet, the accused were alleged to have committed offences punishable u/s. 120B, r/w ss. 420, 409, 468 and 471 of IPC - Having regard to the modus operandi adopted by the appellant, it was not a fit case for exercise of jurisdiction by the HC u/s. 482 of the CrPC as also by SC u/art. 142 of the Constitution - Merely because the dues of the bank have been paid up, the appellant cannot be exonerated from the criminal liability - Chargesheet against him could not be quashed - Appeal dismissed.
