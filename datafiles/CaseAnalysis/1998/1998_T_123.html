Collector of Customs, Cochin v Trivandrum Rubber Works Limited, Chacki

Supreme Court of India

11 November 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1293; (1999) 2 SCC 553; 1999 (81) ECR 171; 1999 (106) E.L.T. 9; JT 1998 (9) SC 485; 1999 (8) SLT 360

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Customs - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 229 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, ss. 28 and 147(3) - Customs Act, 1962 - Respondent imported neoprene foam sheets - Cleared under a bill of entry for home consumption through their clearing agent - Goods were cleared on 09-04-1986 - On 09-10-1986, a notice to respondent issued by appellant u/s. 28 of Customs Act, 1962 - Notice of short levy of duty - Respondent was served with same notice on 14-10-1986 - Whether a notice to importer which is served on clearing agent of importer long after goods have been cleared by clearing agent, will amount to a valid notice to respondent? - U/s. 28, notice has to be served on 'the person chargeable with duty' - Person chargeable with duty in case of imports is importer - If clearing agent of importer is sought to be made liable as person chargeable with duty in circumstances set out in s. 147(3) r/w proviso, notice must be addressed to clearing agent and must set out that he is being made liable under proviso to s. 147(3) - Held, notice has been given u/s. 28 to owner/importer as a person chargeable to duty - Notice must, therefore, be served on owner/importer - Service on clearing agent of owner/importer long after clearing agent has ceased to deal with goods in question under Customs Act, cannot be treated as valid service of notice on owner/importer - Notice, therefore, cannot be construed as a valid notice against agent for recovery of any duty from agent under proviso to s. 147(3) - Further held, once clearing agents discharged all his duties and functions as such agent and goods in question have been cleared and delivered to importer/owner, his work as a clearing agent in respect of goods ordinarily comes to an end - Any notice served on him thereafter in respect of goods already cleared cannot be construed as a notice given in course of business of clearing goods concerned, transacted by him for principal - Appeal dismissed.
