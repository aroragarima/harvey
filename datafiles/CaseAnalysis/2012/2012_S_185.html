Suresh & Ors. v State Of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

22 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 477; (2013) 1 SCC 550; 2013 CRLJ 474; 2013 (1) JabLJ 217; 2013 (1) JCC (Narcotics) 9; JT 2012 (11) SC 428; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 229; 2012(11) SCALE 281; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 1157; 2012 (4) WLN(SC) 180

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs & Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 18 and 50 (1) - Noncompliance of s. 50 - Conviction - Challenged - Appellants/accused allegedly having possession of 'opium', total of 7kg and was seized from appellants and were arrested - Trial Court convicted appellants/accused u/s. 8 r/w s. 18 of the Act - On appeals, HC confirmed order of Trial Court and dismissed appeals - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that considering the mandate provided u/s. 50 of the Act as interpreted by 2 Constitution Benches of SC, viz., State of Punjab v. Baldev Singh, 1999 Indlaw SC 164 and Vijaysinh Chandubha Jadeja v. State of Gujarat 2010 Indlaw SC 939, the prosecuting authorities failed to apprise the right of suspect provided u/s. 50 of the Act, hence on this ground the conviction had to be set aside - Held, it was clear from Panchnama that appellants were not apprised about their right to be searched before a gazetted officer or a Magistrate but consent was sought for their personal search - Merely asking them as to whether they would offer their personal search to him, i.e., the police officer or to gazetted officer might not satisfy the protection afforded u/s. 50 of the Act as interpreted in 1999 Indlaw SC 164 - Further a reading of the judgments of Trial Court and HC also shown that in the presence of Panchas, the SHO merely asked all 3 appellants for their search by him and they simply agreed - Though in 1999 Indlaw SC 164, SC had not expressed any opinion as to whether the provisions of s. 50 were mandatory or directory but 'failure to inform' the person concerned of his right as emanating from s. 50 (1) might render the recovery of contraband suspect and conviction and sentence of an accused bad and unsustainable in law - In 2010 Indlaw SC 939, recently the Constitution Bench explained the mandate provided u/s. 50 (1) of the Act and concluded that it was mandatory and required strict compliance - Bench also held that failure to comply with the provision would render the recovery of illicit article suspect and vitiate the conviction if the same was recorded only on the basis of recovery of illicit article from person of the accused during such search - In instant case, search and seizure of suspect from the person of appellants/accused was bad and conviction was unsustainable in law - Hence, order of conviction passed by Courts below was set aside - Appeal allowed.
