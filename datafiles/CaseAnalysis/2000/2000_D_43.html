Gautam Paul v Debi Rani Paul and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 October 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2814; (2000) 8 SCC 330; AIR 2001 SC 61; AIR 2000 SCW 3870; 2001 (1) ALD(SC) 101; 2001 (43) ALR 106; 2001 (42) ALR 218; JT 2000 (1) SC 614; JT 2000 (Supp1) SC 614; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 46; 2001 (1) RCR(Civil) 60; 2001 (92) RD 208; 2000(7) SCALE 145; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 733; 2000 (7) SLT 496; [2000] Supp3 S.C.R. 733; 2000 (7) Supreme 249

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Partition Act, 1893, s. 4 - (A) Whether appellant could be said to be a member of family within meaning of s. 4? - (B) Whether in absence of transferee suing for partition a shareholder can invoke s. 4 and buy over such share? - Liberal interpretation - Held, (A) merely because a person is related by blood through a common ancestor it does not make him a member of family within meaning of term as used in s. 4 - (B) At no stage has appellant asked for partition or demanded possession of his share - Condition of a transferee suing for partition had not been fulfilled - Appeal disposed of.
