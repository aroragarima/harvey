Chaman Lal v The State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

06 March 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 95; (1970) SCC (Cr) 253; (1970) 1 SCC 590; AIR 1970 SC 1372; 1970 CRLJ 1266; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 913

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: IPC, 1860, s. 499 , First Exception, Ninth Exception - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 124 - (A) Defamation - (1) Good faith and bona fide - Proof of - (2) First Exception to provision - Imputation of truth of public good - (3) Exception - Imputation for protection of interest - (4) Punishment for defamation - Conviction of President of Municipal Committee - Facts found by Courts dispelling any semblance of good faith and on the contrary indicating lack of prudence and dignity with which a person occupying the office of President should act - (B) Evidence - Official communications - Extent of privilege - (A) Held, points for consideration stated - Truth of imputation and publication of imputation for public good must be proved by accused - Interest of the person has to be real and legitimate when communication is made - Reduction of simple imprisonment from three months to two months so that it would save him from disqualification for continuing as President of the Municipality held not warranted - (B) A privilege extends only to a communication upon the subject with respect to which privilege extends and privilege can be claimed in exercise of right or safeguard of interest which creates the privilege - Appeal Dismissed.
