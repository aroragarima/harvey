S. Nihaal Ahamed v Dean, Velammal Medical College Hospital and Research Institute and others

Supreme Court of India

30 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

C. Nagappan, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 699; (2016) 1 SCC 662; 2016(4) ALL MR 509; 2015 (6) AWC 5919; JT 2015 (9) SC 124; 2015(10) SCALE 328; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 242

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - Admission to medical college - Compensation - Appellants filed writ petitions before HC seeking directions to respondent-Medical College to admit them in first year of MBBS Course for academic year 2013-14 in their college - Single Judge by common order held that though appellants were not entitled for admission yet, are entitled to sum of Rs.3 Lakh as compensation payable by respondent-Medical College - Assailing denial of relief of admission, both appellants filed appeals and respondent-Medical College also filed appeals challenging grant of compensation - DB of HC dismissed writ appeals filed by appellants affirming view of Single Judge and allowed writ appeals of respondent-Medical College by holding that appellants are not entitled for compensation - Hence, instant appeals by appellants - Whether order of Single Judge denying relief of admission to Medical College and granting compensation is justifiable.

Held, respondent-Medical College drafted letters dt.24-9-2-143 directing appellants to appear for counseling on 26-9-2013 and marked copy of the same to the Monitoring Committee. However, said letters were posted only on 29-9-2013 as evident from post office seal affixed on the envelope produced by appellants. Hence, finding of Single Judge that the respondent-Medical College is at fault in not sending call letters in time is based on proper appreciation of factual matrix. Further, Single Judge had after elaborately considering facts and circumstances held that the appellants are entitled to sum of Rs.3 Lakh each as compensation payable by respondent-Medical College but the said direction was erroneously reversed by DB. Hence, appeals are partly allowed and impugned judgment in so far as setting aside order of Single Judge awarding compensation to the appellants, is set aside and order of Single Judge awarding compensation of Rs.3 Lakh to each of appellants is restored. Appeals disposed of.
