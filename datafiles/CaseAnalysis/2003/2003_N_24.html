Bibi Zubaida Khatoon with Amichand Aggarwal v Nabi Hassan Saheb and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 944; (2004) 1 SCC 191; AIR 2004 SC 173; 2003 (53) ALR 837; 2003 (6) AWC 5436; 2004 (1) CHN 139; 2004 (1) JhrCR(SC) 164; JT 2003 (8) SC 478; 2004 (1) RCR(Civil) 216; 2004 (96) RD 463; 2003(9) SCALE 393; 2003 (1) SCW 5858; 2003 (7) SLT 65; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 290; 2003 (7) Supreme 679

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - s. 115 - O. 1 r. 10, O. 22 r. 10, O. 6 r. 17 - Impleadment - Amendment of pleadings - Old suit - Trial Court rejected the prayer on the ground that the property having been purchased during pendency of the suit, the decree passed in the suit shall bind the transferee pendente-lite - Held, there is no absolute rule that the transferee pendente-lite without leave of the court should in all cases be allowed to join and contest the pending suits - Further held, both the Trial Court and the High Court has rightly exercised their discretion in rejecting the three applications for impleadment of the transferee pendente-lite as party to the suits and for amendment of the pleadings.
