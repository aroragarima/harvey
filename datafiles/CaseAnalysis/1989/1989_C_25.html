Durand Didier v Chief Secretary, Union Territory Of Goa

Supreme Court of India

29 August 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 212; (1990) SCC (Cr) 65; (1990) 1 SCC 95; AIR 1989 SC 1966; 1990 (2) Bom.C.R. 31; 1989 (3) Crimes 163; 1989 CRLR 718; 1989 CrLR(SC) 718; 1990 (40) DLT 139; 1990 (25) ECC 289; 1989 (25) ECR 321; JT 1989 (3) SC 507; 1989(2) SCALE 449; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 1025

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Personal Consumption

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 18, 20 and 21 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3 and 45 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 100 - Notification No. S.0. 827 (E) D/-14-11-85 issued by Ministry of Finance Department of Revenue - (A) Offence under 1985 Act - (1) Prosecution case that accused was apprehended after he fell down from motorcycle on which he was riding - Plea by accused that absence of any injury on his person belied prosecution case - Evidence showing that accused fell down immediately after he tried to accelerate his vehicle and not after vehicle had taken speed and escaped unhurt - (2) Accused apprehended at mid night - Seizure of contraband drugs from accused - Witnesses to search and seizure inhabitants of locality in which police out-post was situated - Nothing brought out in cross examination of panch witnesses so as to discredit their testimony - Fact that witnesses were not residing in vicinity of place of seizure - Plea that there was violation of statutory safeguards relating to search and seizure - (C) Expert evidence - Appreciation of - Chemical Analyst - Samples sent for analysis opined to be of prohibited drugs and substances - Analyst admitting that he did not know difference between narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances - Credibility of his evidence - (D) Conviction - U/ss. 20, 21 and 18 for possessing contraband drugs - Plea that very small quantity of drugs were recovered, so offence would fall u/s. 27 - Sentence of 10 years rigorous imprisonment and fine of Rs. 1 lac imposed on accused - Held, (A) Plea raised by accused is untenable - (B) Fact is immaterial and plea held, untenable - (C) Not by itself sufficient to disregard his opinion as analyst is not expected to know difference in legal parlance - (D) Quantity of drugs recovered from accused far in excess of permissible quantity stipulated under Notification so offence would not fall u/s. 27(a) and punishment held, was proper - Appeal dismissed.
