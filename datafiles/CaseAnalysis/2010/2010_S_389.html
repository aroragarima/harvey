Md. Ashif and others v State of Bihar and others

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 366; (2010) 5 SCC 475; 2010(3) ALL MR 925; 2010 (3) AWC 2754; 2010 (125) FLR 1010; 2010 (3) JhrCR(SC) 4; JT 2010 (5) SC 269; 2010 (4) LLJ 1; 2010(5) SCALE 217; [2010] 6 S.C.R. 191; 2011 (5) SLR 241

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Absorption - Legality of - Appellants/Voluntary Health Workers (VHWs) were appointed and promoted to the post of Primary Health Workers by the Chief Medical Officer on a monthly honorarium - Appellants continued in their service for 15 years - Appellants' services were terminated by the respondents on the ground that the appellants' promotion and absorption was not in accordance with the rules or procedure to be followed with - Appellants challenged their termination before HC - Single Judge set aside the termination, however, DB, in the letters patent appeal of the respondent, set aside the Single Judge order - Held, Chief Medical Officer who had made the appointments was not vested with the power to do so nor were the claims of other candidates eligible for appointments against the posts to which the appellants were appointed, considered - Appointments had come by way of absorption of the appellants who were working as VHWs on a monthly honorarium - DB correctly held that there was no cadre of VHWs who were working on an honorarium in State run dispensaries - Very nature of the appointment given to the appellants as VHWs was honorary in nature which entitled them to the payment of not more than the amount fixed for honorarium per month - Chief Medical Officer cannot regularize/absorb such VHWs doing honorary service against the post of Primary Health Workers which carried a regular pay-scale and which could be filled only in accordance with the procedure prescribed for that purpose - Appointment of the appellants against the posts of Primary Health Workers was manifestly illegal and wholly undeserved - Inasmuch as the said appointments came to be cancelled pursuant to the directions of the respondents no matter nearly a decade and a half later the termination could not be said to be illegal so as to warrant interference of a writ court for reinstatement of those illegally appointed - DB was justified in declining interference with the order of cancellation and dismissing the writ petitions - No interference - Appeal dismissed.
