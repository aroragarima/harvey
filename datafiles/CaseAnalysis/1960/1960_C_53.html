Bengal Textiles Association v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 345; AIR 1960 SC 1320; [1960] 39 ITR 723

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 60(3) - Business Profits Tax Act, 1947, s. 60(3) - Business Profits Tax Act, 1947, s. 4, proviso(c) - (A) Whether profits of assessee were exempt from taxation under Business Profits Tax Act of 1947 or not? - Held, no - Both on terms of letter D/-13-11- 1945, and under provisions of Income Tax Act exemption is inoperative in regard to claim made by association - Letter gave exemption on certain conditions in regard to income-tax, super-tax or excess profits tax - But u/s 60(3) of Income Tax Act power of exemption was not exercisable by Govt. after commencement of Indian Income Tax (Amendment) Act, 1939 - (B) Whether sum of Rs. 6, 00, 000 paid by Govt. of Bengal during chargeable accounting year ending on 31-12-1946, is in nature of subsidy and as such exempt from Business Profits Tax Act or not? - Held, sum of Rs. 6,00,000 was not paid by Govt. as a subsidy but towards expenses of association - Payment was a consideration for services to be rendered by association under terms of agreement under which payment was to be made - It was not gift but payment u/s. 4, proviso (c) - Appeal dismissed.
