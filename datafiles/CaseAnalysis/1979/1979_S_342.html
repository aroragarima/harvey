State Of U.P., Etc v Synthetics & Chemicals Ltd. & Ors. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

19 December 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

P.S. Kailasam, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 105; (1980) 2 SCC 441; AIR 1980 SC 614; 1980 ALJ 258; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 531; 1980 TAXLR 2294

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Beverage, Uttar Pradesh Excise (Amendment) Act 1976, Ethyl Alcohol (Price Control) Amendment Order, 1975

Summary: Excise - U.P. Excise (Amendment) Act, 1972 - U.P. Excise (Amendment) Act, 1976 - Industries (Development and Regulation) Act, 1951 - Denatured spirit - Validity of levy of vend fee - Legality - Appellants had licenses for wholesale vend of denatured spirit - Appellant filed writ petition against order of excise commissioner charging vend fee on wholesale license dealer of denatured spirit - Appellant also sought for direction to Excise Commissioner to refund the vend fee actually paid by appellants for period of 3 years prior to institution of writ petitions - Appellants contended that State was providing no service to trade of denatured spirit and, therefore, levy of fee was not justified - HC dismissed writ petitions by following the view of SC in Nashirwar v. State of Madhya Pradesh, 1974 Indlaw SC 199 and Har Shankar v. The Deputy Excise and Taxation Commissioner, 1975 Indlaw SC 424 - Hence instant appeals - Appellants challenged constitutional validity of 1976 Act as unconstitutional ad beyond legislative competence of State - Further contended that provisions of 1951 Act had taken control of fermentation industry and as such right to legislate by State with regard to denatured spirit and industrial alcohol was beyond competence of State Legislature - Whether imposition of vend fee on denatured spirit for grant of license for wholesale vend of denatured spirit was within competence of State Govt. - Held, State had u/r. 12 issued notification dt.30-12-1960 defining foreign liquor as meaning all rectified, perfumed, medicated and denatured spirit, wherever made - R. 17(2), no doubt purports to have been issued under rule making powers conferred on Govt. u/s. 40 (2) (d) which enabled Govt. to make rules for regulating import, export, transport for possession of any intoxicants - It might be noted that when amended r. 17 (2) was introduced on 3-11-1972, s. 24A was amended by 1972 Act and power of Excise Commissioner to accept payment for grant of licence for exclusive privilege could not be denied - After introduction of s. 24A, Excise Commissioner had right to grant privilege of selling of foreign liquor - Fact that s. 30 did not specifically mention s. 24A might not have made any difference - However, in order to remove all doubts, new s. 30 was introduced - S. 41 (3) was reenacted to enable fixation of fee payable for grant of exclusive or other privilege u/ss. 24 and 24A - S. 40 was also amended so as to give retrospective effect - S. 4 of 1976 Act also provides that 1972 Act should be deemed to be and always to have been as valid as if provisions of this Act were in force at all material times - Levy on persons who were purchasers was for possession of denatured spirit in excess of prescribed limit - Permission granted in their favour and allotment orders of specially denatured spirit prescribes terms and conditions under which allotment was made - Licences were granted to them under form F.L. 39 and they had to abide by those conditions - Appellants/petitioners having applied for and obtained licences in form F.L. 39 were bound to comply with conditions - Appeals dismissed.
