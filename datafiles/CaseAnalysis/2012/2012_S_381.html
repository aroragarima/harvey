Yanab Sheikh @ Gagu v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 569; (2013) 6 SCC 428; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 747; JT 2012 (12) SC 533; 2013 (1) MLJ(Crl) 224; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 898; 2012(12) SCALE 730; [2012] 13 S.C.R. 1150

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 324, 326 - Indian Explosives Act, 1884, s. 9(b)(ii) - Throwing of bomb - Instantaneous death - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) and another allegedly threw a bomb, which exploded on the chest of deceased causing his instantaneous death and thereby committed offences u/s. 9(b)(ii) of the Act and u/ss. 302, 324, 326 of IPC - Trial Court acquitted appellant and co-accused for offences u/s. 9(b)(ii) of the Act and u/s. 324 of IPC - However Trial Court convicted appellant for offence u/s. 302 of IPC and sentenced him to undergo imprisonment for life - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before HC - HC by impugned judgment and order dismissed appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that prosecution has not examined all witnesses without specifying any reason - Thus, adverse inference should be drawn against prosecution and that there were material discrepancies and variations in statements of witnesses - Whether impugned conviction of appellant was sustainable - Held, in instant case, PW8, 9 and PW10 were produced as witnesses before Court - After recording their introductory part in examination-in-chief, prosecution gave up those witnesses as having won over and tendered them for cross-examination - Trial Court in its order dt.3.7.1992 recorded and mentioned that witnesses were cross-examined by defence - It could not be said that defence of appellant had suffered any prejudice as a result of non-examination of said 3 witnesses - Evidence of PW8, supported the case of prosecution - It proved that the incident occurred on that day, pump was taken on hire and people of village during the day were drawing water from the 'L' pond - Thus, non-examination of these witnesses has neither prejudiced the case of prosecution nor would it be of any serious advantage to appellant - PW1 (complainant), PW5 and PW6 (eye witnesses) clearly supported the case of prosecution - Their statements, examined in conjunction with statement of PW11 (doctor) and PW14 (IO), clearly established the case of prosecution beyond any reasonable doubt - Trial Court in its judgment clearly stated that there was direct and circumstantial evidence against appellant implicating him with the commission of crime - Where the prosecution was able to establish the guilt of accused by cogent, reliable and trustworthy evidence, mere acquittal of one accused would not automatically lead to acquittal of another accused - It was only where the entire case of prosecution suffered from infirmities, discrepancies and where the prosecution was not able to establish its case, the acquittal of co-accused would be of some relevancy for deciding the case of other - Acquittal of a co-accused per se was not sufficient to result in acquittal of other accused - Appellant was not able to point out contradictions or discrepancies of any material nature in statements of witnesses - PW6, cousin of deceased has supported the prosecution version and his statement was duly corroborated by other witnesses - Absence of questions on behalf of appellant to concerned witnesses would show that appellant could not claim any advantage and thus, could not default the case of prosecution, particularly in facts of instant case - Impugned order of conviction was upheld - Appeal dismissed.
