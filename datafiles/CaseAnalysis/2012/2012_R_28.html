Raju @ Balachandran & Ors. v State Of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

27 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 486; (2012) 12 SCC 701; AIR 2013 SC 983; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 339; 2013 CRLJ 491; JT 2012 (11) SC 453; 2013 (1) MLJ(Crl) 318; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 567; 2012(11) SCALE 357; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 109

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 341, 326 - Murder - Interested Witness - Discrepancies - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant No. 1- accused is father of appellant No. 2 and No. 3 and were convicted for offence u/ss. 302,341,326 of IPC - Prosecution case was that appellant and deceased had previous enmity over a village ritual due to which appellants killed deceased - Trial Court believing evidence of PW5 convicted appellants - Appellant preferred appeal before HC, contending that witnesses had turned hostile except PW5 whose evidence also had discrepancies and further it could not be relied as he was interest witness - HC, dismissed appeal and held that it could not be said that PW5 was an unreliable witness there may have been some minor discrepancies in his description of events, and there was no reason for High Court to disbelieve him as he was believed by Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether Trial Court and HC, were justified in convicting appellants relying on evidence of interest witness - Held, SC, even after applying discerning scrutiny standard for evidence of PW5 found it difficult to overturn view expressed by both Courts in their acceptance of his evidence, his description of events was simple and straightforward and cross-examination does not demolish his version of events, in fact, cross examination was directed more at proving that someone else may have been assailant since deceased had an illicit relationship , but it was ruled out by PW-5 who did not want to shield real assailant and put blame for occurrence on someone else - Appeal dismissed.
