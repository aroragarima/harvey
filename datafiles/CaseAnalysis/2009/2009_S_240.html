Shiba Shankar Mohapatra & Ors. v State Of Orissa & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

12 November 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1527; (2010) 12 SCC 471; AIR 2010 SC 706; 2010 (2) ESC 191; 2010 (124) FLR 3; JT 2009 (14) SC 298; 2009(13) SCALE 689; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 866; 2009 (6) SLR 483

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Criteria For Promotion, Executive Instructions, Determination Of Seniority

Summary: Service - Inter se seniority - Determination of - Post of Sub-Inspector-General (SIs-g) was placed above the post of Sub-Inspector-Steno (SIs-s) in their inter se seniority by Tribunal vide its order in OA No. 316/2000 - Letter issued by the Home Department dated 3.2.1987 wherein it was instructed that the seniority of the SIs-s would be determined after their entry into general wing after passing the Inspector Training Course in pursuance thereof - Aggrieved SI-s filed writ petition before HC - HC dismissed the writ petition by holding that if the SIs-g found to be place below the SIs-s, they should be granted promotion from the date of the promotion of their counterparts in other wings subject to their eligibility criteria - Whether in absence of any statutory rule for determining inter se seniority in between SIs-s and SIs-g, the Tribunal could disturb the uninterrupted practice in the State to place SIs-s above SIs-g while preparing their inter se seniority - Held, in absence of any statutory rules, the executive instructions for fixing the inter se seniority of two wings of the Sub-Inspectors can be issued by the State Government - However, no such executive instruction has ever been issued - Letters issued by the Home Departments, being merely an opinion could not confer status of executive instructions - Issue before the HC was regarding the principle of seniority for preparation of a combined gradation list of SIs-s and SIs-g - However, HC failed to decide the said issue and instead directed preparation of a combined gradation list in conformity with eligibility criterion - Therefore, impugned HC judgement set aside to the extent of the direction for preparation of combined gradation list on the basis of eligibility - However, first part of the HC order has not been challenged, hence attained finality, and does not warrant any interference - Appeal disposed of.
