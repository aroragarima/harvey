Jose and Others v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 977; (1994) Supp3 SCC 1; (1994) SCC (Cr) 1659; 1994 (4) CCR 772; 1994 (3) Crimes 245; 1994 JCC 623; JT 1994 (5) SC 294; 1994(3) SCALE 813; 1994 (2) UJ 657

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970, s. 2 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 379 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 449, 302 and 34 - Murder - Appellants acquitted by Trial Court but convicted by High Court - Present appeal - Dying declaration - Whether HC correct in reversing acquittal order? - It is well settled that conviction can be based on dying declaration itself provided it is satisfactory and reliable and if there are any infirmities of such nature warranting further assurance, then courts have to look for corroboration - Held, dying declaration amply corroborated by eye-witness - Presence of A1 and A3 proved at spot of incident - If guilt of accused otherwise proved, motive loses its significance - Appeal of A2 allowed and that of A1 and A3 dismissed - Order accordingly.
