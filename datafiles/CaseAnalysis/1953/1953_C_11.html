Â Kshetra Mohan-Sannyasicharan Sadhukhan v Commissioner of Excess Profits Tax, West Bangal

Supreme Court of India

20 October 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, M. Patanjali Sastri, Vivian Bose, Ghulam Hasan, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 43; AIR 1953 SC 516; [1953] 24 ITR 488; 1953 SCJ 673; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 268

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes -Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 2, 26-a, 66((1) - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940, ss. 21, 7, 8, 8(1) - Partnership - Excess Profits Tax - Validity - During chargeable accounting periods status of assessee was that of firm registered u/s. 26-A of the 1961 Act - In chargeable accounting period ending 13-4-1944, there was no profit in excess of standard profit but there was deficiency - Assessee claimed that total deficiencies carried forward from previous years up to chargeable accounting period ending 13-4-1943 be added to sum and aggregate amount be carried forward u/s. 7 of the 1940 Act - Tax Officer rejected contention - There were three separate appeals by assessee to Appellate Assistant Commissioner against orders of Tax Officer - Appellate Assistant Commissioner confirmed assessments and dismissed appeals - Further appeals were taken to Tribunal - Tribunal dismissed all appeals - Further three applications were made before Tribunal u/s. 66(1) of the 1961 Act r/w s. 21 of the 1940 Act - Appellate Tribunal drew up statement of case and submitted for opinion of HC - HC in agreement with Appellate Tribunal agreed with order of Appellate Tribunal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether there was change in persons carrying on business within meaning of s. 8(1) of the 1940 Act, w.e.f 14-4-1943, when business, which had previously been carried on in partnership between two Dayabhaga Hindu undivided families, was carried on by partnership between separated male members of two families - Held, prior to 13-4-1943 business was carried on by partnership of two Hindu undivided families which prima facie meant partnership between two Kartas representing two Hindu undivided families and that from 14-4-1943, it became business of eight individual members of two disrupted families that question of law could arise - If assessee was not entitled to go behind facts so found by Appellate Tribunal in statement of case and as was implicit in question itself, then there could be no doubt that there had been change in persons carrying on business within meaning of s. 8 of the 1940 Act and it had not been argued otherwise - Hence, answer given by HC was correct - Appeal dismissed.
