Commissioner of Income Tax Bombay v Chugandas and Company, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

29 July 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 82; AIR 1965 SC 568; [1965] 55 ITR 17; [1964] 8 S.C.R. 332

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Finance Act, 1955 - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Double levy of tax - Profits and Gains - Limited Liability - Securities - Exemption Claim - 'C' & Co dealing in securities received certain sum as interest on securities - Firm discontinued its business and u/s. 25(3) of 1922 act claimed exemption from tax on plea that firm was carrying on business before the 1922 act was enacted - Income- tax Officer held that interest earned by the firm on securities being "liable to be assessed to tax" u/s. 8 and not u/s.10 and firm not entitled to benefit of exemption - On appeal, order confirmed - In further appeal, Income-tax Appellate Tribunal, reversed the order and held firm entitled to the benefit of the exemption in the year in which business was discontinued - Whether liability to assessment be limited to profits of business computable u/s.10 of Act - Held, yes - Legislature imposed by s.26(2) liability upon successor to be assessed for profits earned in a business carried on by his predecessor, and unless there was a clear intention expressed in the statute to include in that expression what in reality is not income, but is deemed income, liability to assessment would justifiably be limited to profits of the business which is computable u/s.10 - First grants an exemption because there was a double levy of tax, and an intention to exempt all income, profits and gains of business from taxation may be attributed to Legislature - S.26(2) fastens liability of predecessor, if he cannot be found, upon successor and must be strictly construed - Appeal dismissed.
