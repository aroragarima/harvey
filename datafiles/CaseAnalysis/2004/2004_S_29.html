State Of Tamil Nadu And Anr v S.V. Bratheep (Minor) And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

16 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, AR. Lakshmanan, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 185; (2004) 4 SCC 513; AIR 2004 SC 1861; 2004 (3) AWC 2473; 2004 (3) ESC 359; 2004 (3) JhrCR(SC) 217; JT 2004 (3) SC 416; 2004(3) SCALE 330; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 1218; 2004 (2) SLT 575; 2004 (2) UPLBEC 1306

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Summary: [A.] Education - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 19, 254 - Admissions to engineering colleges made without reference to minimum eligible marks prescribed by appellants - Order of HC stands modified to extent of stating that it is permissible for State Govt. to prescribe higher qualifications for purposes of admission to engineering colleges than what had been prescribed by AICTE -standard prescribed by State and considered by court is not contrary to same but is only complementary or supplementary to AICTE norms - If any admission has been made in any of colleges of students who fulfill directions issued by HC, their admissions shall be taken to have been made validly - Mere fact that Anna University allowed all engineering colleges to conduct entrance test by concerned colleges cannot militate against prescription of standards of excellence in education both by AICTE and Department of Higher Education - Petitions disposed of.
