Union of India and Others v Mahindra and Mahindra Limited, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

08 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, S.P. Bharucha, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1720; (1995) Supp2 SCC 372; 1995 (57) ECR 427; 1995 (76) E.L.T. 481; JT 1995 (9) SC 635; 1995 (7) RLT 341; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 595

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, s. 14(1) - Customs Valuation Rules, 1963, r. 8 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Whether Custom Authorities were right in holding that invoice value of CKD parts set out in invoices was not sole consideration for sale of goods and calling in aid provisions of s. 14(1)(b) r/w r.8 of Customs Valuation Rules? - The parties were dealing at arm's length, that seller and buyer have no interest in business of each other - There existed no nexus between lump sum payment under agreement for technical know-how and determination of price for supply of CKD packs or spare - Held, collaboration agreement entered into between parties is clear and it is not open to Revenue to construe it differently by reading into it something which is not - Appeal dismissed.
