Deputy Commissioner, KVS and others v J. Hussain

Supreme Court of India

04 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 659; (2013) 10 SCC 106; AIR 2014 SC 766; 2014 (1) AWC 222; 2014 (140) FLR 463; 2014 (2) RLW 1783; 2013(12) SCALE 416; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 898; 2013 (6) SLR 351; 2014 (1) UPLBEC 36

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Central Civil Services (C.C.A.) Rules, 1965-Rule 14-Central Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1964-Rule 20-Employment-Removal from service-Case where respondent-employee gone to place of work in fully drunken State-Going to place of work under influence of alcohol during working hours would itself be serious misconduct-Entering school premises in working hours in inebriated condition and thereafter forcibly entering into Principal's room would constitute serious misconduct-Penalty of removal of such misconduct cannot be treated as disproportionate-High Court totally down placed seriousness of misconduct-Reasoning of High Court -Unacceptable-Impugned judgment of High Court-Set aside-Decision of Tribunal, upholding punishment of removal of respondents from service-Restored.
