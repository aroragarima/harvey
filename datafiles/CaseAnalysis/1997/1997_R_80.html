Ram Harakh (Dead) By LRs v Hamid Ahmed Khan (Dead) By LRs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1488; (1998) 7 SCC 484; 1998 (4) AWC 778; JT 1998 (9) SC 227; 1999 (90) RD 284; 1999 (1) SLT 116

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950, s. 20(b) - Uttar Pradesh Consolidation of Holdingss Act, 1953, ss. 4, 5(2) and 48 - Uttar Pradesh Tenancy Act, 1939, ss. 63 and 189 - Uttar Pradesh Land Revenue Act, 1901, ss. 28 and 33 - Transfer of Property Act, 1808, s. 52 - (A) Whether, for applicability of s. 20(b) of U.P.Z. A. & L.R. Act, 1950, only existence of entry in Khasra or Khatauni or both would be sufficient and whether or not there is actual possession or that possession was legal or not, would be totally irrelevant? - Entry in appellant's favour saw light of day during time when there were already litigations pending between parties before competent civil courts at respondents' instance who were already having an earlier entry of personal cultivation in their favour being Entry 1354 Fasli; despite pendency of these litigations, 1356 Fasli Entry is said to have been effected by Naib Tahsildar in appellant's favour, this entry, therefore, on face of it, would be clearly incompetent and is hit by principles of lis pendens u/s. 52 of Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Held, once revisional authority had rightly come to conclusion that Entry 1356 Fasli was not a genuine entry at all and had no legal efficacy, plea that physical existence of such entry by itself would be sufficient to clothe appellants with a statutory right flowing in u/s. 20(b) would not survive at all - (B) Whether decree passed in first suit between parties operates as res judicata? - Whether or not earlier decree was by a competent court or whether it was ever taken in appeal or not raise highly disputed factual controversies - Further held, on facts of present case, contention of res judicata was waived by appellants before all authorities below and it was also not a pure question of law, hence too late to raise this contention for first time at argument stage in these appeals - Appeals dismissed.
