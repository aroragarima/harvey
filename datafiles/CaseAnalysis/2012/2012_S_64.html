Manharibhai Muljibhai Kakadia & Anr. v Shaileshbhai Mohanbhai Patel & Ors

Supreme Court of India

01 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 333; (2012) 10 SCC 517; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 4105; 2013 CRLJ 144; 2013 (2) G.L.R. 1440; 2012 (4) JCC 3105; JT 2012 (10) SC 61; 2013 (1) OLR 258; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 689; 2013 (1) RLW 94; 2012(9) SCALE 671; [2012] 8 S.C.R. 1015

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420, 467, 468, 471, 120-B - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 203, 401(2) - Complaint - Dismissal - Revision - Right to be heard - Entitled to - Respondent No. 1, filed a criminal complaint before Magistrate, against appellants (accused) for alleged offences u/s. 420, 467, 468, 471 and 120-B of IPC - Magistrate, u/s. 202 of CrPC directed enquiry and concerned IO had submitted summary report that dispute between parties were of civil nature and no offence was made out - Thereafter, Magistrate accepted summary report filed by IO - Aggrieved respondent No.1 filed revision application u/s. 397 r/w s. 401 of CrPC before HC - In said revision appellants filed an application, for joining them as respondents so that they could be heard - Single Judge dismissed application of appellants - Hence, instant appeal - Appellants contended that u/s. 401(2) of CrPC, they had right to be heard in a revision preferred by a complainant and that if revision application was accepted, that would have effect of revival of complaint and setting criminal process back in motion which would be prejudicial to appellants and appellants ought to be heard - Whether a suspect was entitled to hearing in a revision filed by complainant challenging an order of Magistrate dismissing complaint u/s. 203 of CrPC - Held, Single Judge observed that Magistrate had not taken cognizance of offences and there was no question of applicants being heard at the stage of revision application - Order passed by Magistrate remained no doubt that Magistrate took cognizance although he postponed issue of process by directing an investigation to be made by Police Officer - On a plain reading of s. 401(2) of CrPC, it could not be said that person against whom allegations of having committed offence were made in complaint and complaint was dismissed by Magistrate u/s. 203 of CrPC, had no right to be heard because no process was issued - Once a challenge was laid to dismissal of complaint by Magistrate u/s. 203 of CrPC at the instance of complainant in a revision petition before HC or Sessions Judge, by virtue of s. 401(2) of CrPC, the suspects get right of hearing before Revisional Court although such order was passed without their participation - Right given to 'accused' or 'the other person' u/s. 401(2) of CrPC being heard before Revisional Court to defend an order which operates in his favour should not be confused with proceedings before a Magistrate u/ss. 200, 202, 203 and 204 of CrPC - Accused or other person could not be deprived of hearing on face of express provision contained in s. 401(2) of CrPC - Stage was not important whether it was pre-process stage or post process stage - Thus, in a revision petition preferred by complainant before HC or Sessions Judge challenging an order of Magistrate dismissing complaint u/s. 203 of CrPC at the stage u/s. 200 of CrPC or after following the process contemplated u/s. 202 of CrPC, the accused or a person who was suspected to have committed crime was entitled to hearing by Revisional Court - However, if Revisional Court overturns order of Magistrate dismissing the complaint and complaint was restored to the file of Magistrate and it was sent back for fresh consideration, the persons who were alleged in complaint to have committed crime would have no right to participate in proceedings nor they were entitled to any hearing of any sort whatsoever by Magistrate until consideration of matter by Magistrate for issuance of process - Judgments of HCs to the contrary were overruled - P. Sundarrajan and others v. R. Vidhya Sekar, 2004 Indlaw SC 2007, referred to - Impugned order of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
