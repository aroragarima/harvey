State of Tamil Nadu v Messrs Dharangadhara Trading Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.H. Kania, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 397; (1988) 3 SCC 462; JT 1988 (2) SC 270; 1988(1) SCALE 852; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 805; 1988 (70) STC 92; 1988 (2) UJ 156

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Indirect Tax - Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, ss. 3 and 6(2)(b) - Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Act, s. 38 - Chemical Co. entered into an agreement to sell all its products with Trading Co. - Two sets of sales, one by Chemical Co. to Trading Co. and second by Trading Co. to outside buyers - In original assessment order for assessment year 1961-62 made by Sales Tax Officer, both sales treated as interstate sales - Consequently, central sales tax levied on first sale, but not on second sale - On revised assessment assessing authority treated first sales as intra-state sales and second sales as inter-state sales falling u/s. 3 of Central Tax Act - Both assesses file appeals before Appellate Asst. Commr. contending that both sales inter-state - Asst. Commr. rejects their contentions, dismisses appeals - Appeal filed before Tribunal, allowed - Tribunal came concludes that delivery effected by Chemical Co. to Trading Co. by delivery of documents of title, namely, respective invoices and railway receipts - Nature of sales by Chemical Co. to Trading Co. and question whether they were inter-State sales to be decided after taking into account further instructions given by buyers - Against decision of Tribunal, revision petitions u/s. 38 of Tax Act preferred by state High Court - HC upheld views of Tribunal and dismissed both revision petitions - An appeal preferred by state in case of Trading Co., namely, case pertaining to assessment of sales from Chemical Co. to Trading Co. - Whether conclusion drawn by Tribunal as well as HC justified? - Held, as Tribunal has rightly pointed out, agreement merely a general agreement and actual terms of contracts of sales as well as instructions of out of state buyers to be taken into account in determining nature of sales in question - Conclusions arrived at by Tribunal as well as HC that sales by Chemical Co. to Trading Co. were inter-state sales cannot be faulted - Conclusion finds some support from observations of this court in UOI v. K G Khosla and Co. (P) Ltd [(1979) 2 SCC 2421979 (2) SCC 2421979 Indlaw SC 49, 248 : (1979) 3 SCR 4531979 (3) SCR 4531979 Indlaw SC 259, 460 : 1979 SCC (Tax) 1011979 SCC(Tax) 1011979 Indlaw SC 49 : (1979) 43 STC 4571979 (43) STC 4571979 Indlaw SC - Appeal dismissed.
