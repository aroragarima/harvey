T. N. Godavarman Thirumulkpad v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2014; (1997) 2 SCC 267; AIR 1997 SC 1228; AIR 1997 SCW 1263; JT 1997 (10) SC 377; 1996(9) SCALE 269; [1996] Supp9 S.C.R. 982

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980, ss. 1 and 2 - Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972 - Main issue is conservation of forests - With focus on non - forest activities and felling of trees - Points under consideration - (A) Applicability of Act - (B) Meaning of 'Forest land' - (C) Unapproved non - forest activities to dealt with - (D) Felling of trees - In forest of Tirap and Changlang in Arunachal Pradesh - Except as per working plan of State Govt. - (E) Ensurance of implementation of directions - (F) Retrenchment of employees of saw mills and wood based industries which were closed by interim order of Supreme Court - Held, applies to all forests irrespective of nature of ownership or classification thereof taking dictionary meaning of word 'forest ' - In addition to dictionary sense, also include any area recorded as forest in Govt. record irrespective of ownership - All States directed to ensure total cessation of any such unapproved activity forthwith - Felling of trees of any kind banned, saw, veneer and ply-wood mills in Tirap, Changlang and in Assam directed to be closed, and movement of cut trees from North-Eastern States totally banned with certain exceptions - States directed to constitute expert committees do specified task and to oversee compliance of order of Supreme Court, with specific directions for certain states - Continue to receive full emoluments and not to be retrenched or removed from service - Application disposed of.
