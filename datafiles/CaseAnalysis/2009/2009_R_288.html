G.V. Sreerama Reddy & Anr. v Returning Officer & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 962; (2009) 8 SCC 736; AIR 2010 SC 133; 2010 (78) ALR 250; JT 2009 (10) SC 316; 2009 (6) KarLJ 422; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 937; 2009(11) SCALE 432; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 245

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Election Petition, General Elections, Election Agents, Dismissed, Returning Officer, Qualification For Membership, Representation Of People Act, 1951, Corrupt Practice, Trial Of Election Petitions, Decision Of The High Court, Presentation Of Petitions

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951, s. 81(1) - Election petition - Filing thereof - Whether election petition as presented was in accordance with s. 81(1) of the Act, and whether the High Court was right in dismissing the same as it was not presented by the candidate or elector? - Held, petition was not filed in accordance with s. 81(1) of Act; HC was right in dismissing the same - Provisions of s. 81(1) perused, object and scheme of Act, preamble of Act, noted - Election petition in question was presented by the advocate on behalf of candidate, and candidate was not present at the time of filing - Requirements u/s. 81(1) are extremely specific and clear - Act is a special statute and a self contained regime; thus intention of the Legislature must be considered - Court observed that in spite of existence of adequate provisions in the Civil Procedure Code relating to institution of a suit, Act contains elaborate provisions as to disputes regarding elections - On reason behind the requirement of filing of petition 'by' the petitioner, Court said that election petition is a very serious matter with a variety of consequences; since such a petition may lead to the vitiation of a democratic process, any procedure provided by an election statute must be read strictly; purpose behind the requirement is that HC may make preliminary verification which ensure that the petition is neither frivolous nor vexatious - Election petition was thus improperly presented - Impugned dismissal of petition by HC, upheld - Appeal dismissed.
