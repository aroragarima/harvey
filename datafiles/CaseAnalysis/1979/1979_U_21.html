Babu Singh and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 461; (1981) 3 SCC 628; AIR 1979 SC 1713

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Possession Of Land

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Possession of Land - Constitution of India, 1950 Art. 133(1)(a) - Land Acquisition Act, ss. 17, 5-A, 4 and 6 - State of Punjab published Notification D/-15-03-1963 notifying that different pieces of land were required for a public purpose - Simultaneously the notification recited that in view of the urgency the requirement of the provision of s. 5-A shall be dispensed with - The appellants made a representation to the Chandigarh Administration for allotment of alternative land and their request for allotment of alternative sites was rejected - Appellants filed Petition - (A) Whether issuing of notifications under ss. 4 and 6 simultaneously and therefore acquisition is bad in law? - After a notification is issued s. 5-A enables any person interested in the land which has been notified under s. 4, to submit his objections to the proposed acquisition within thirty days from the date of the issue of the notification - S. 6 confers power on the Govt. to make a declaration after taking into consideration the report submitted by the Collector under s. 5-A, that the land is required for the stated public purpose - If the urgency clause is applied, s. 17(4) enables the Govt. to dispense with the enquiry contemplated by s. 5-A - If application of the urgency clause permits the Govt. to dispense with enquiry under s. 5-A, obviously the very urgency would necessitate making of the declaration under s. 6 immediately after issuing the notification under s. 4 - Notification under s. 4(1) was issued on 15-03-1963 and in view of the urgency the Govt. by the same notification dispensed with the provision of s. 5-A and thereafter made a requisite declaration under s. 6(1) and issued the notification on the same day - Held, no illegality in the issuance of the notification under s. 4 and under s. 6 on the same day - Whether a declaration under s. 6 could only be made in respect of a public purpose set out and recited in the notification under s. 4 and as there is a material variation in the recitation of public purpose in the notification under s. 4 and 6, the declaration made under s. 6 is illegal and invalid? - Without examining the merit of contention in law it must fail in the facts of this case because no difference in the statement of public purpose in the notification under s. 4(1) and in the notification under s. 6(1) - The statement of public purpose under s. 4(1) notification is more comprehensive setting out details of improvement works while the one set out under s. 6(1) notification is more precise and restricted in terms - (C) Whether the State Govt. did not take possession for a period of six years after making a declaration and even after the award, the declaration under s. 6 got exhausted and no further action can be taken pursuant to the declaration under s. 6 and as the appellants are still in possession, the respondents should be restrained from disturbing their possession pursuant to the acquisition proceedings? - If the impugned notifications are valid and the declaration made under s. 6 has become final and an award under s. 12 is made, even if possession is not taken, the notification under s. 6 would be invalid - Held, the length of the delay is an important circumstance because of the nature of the acts done within the interval on the basis of the notification and declaration and, therefore, a challenge to a notification under s. 4 and a declaration under s. 6 of the Act should be made within a reasonable time thereafter - Challenge was after a period of six years and after the whole process of acquisition was over and the State Govt. had spent a considerable amount in carrying out the public purpose - Appeal fails.
