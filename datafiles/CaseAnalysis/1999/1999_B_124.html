Bhagirathi Jena v Board of Directors, O.S.F.C. and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 347; (1999) 3 SCC 666; (1999) SCC (L&S) 804; AIR 1999 SC 1841; 1999 (1) CLR 1074; 1999 (2) ESC 1180; 1999 (82) FLR 143; JT 1999 (3) SC 53; 1999 LabIC 2080; 1999 (1) LLJ 1236; 1999 (2) LLN 993; 1999(2) SCALE 545; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 354; 1999 (2) SLR 353; 1999 (2) SLR 355; 1999 (3) SLT 494; 1999 (4) Supreme 9

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Orissa State Financial Corporations Staff Regulations, 1959, regn. 44 (3) (c) - Retiral benefit - Determination of - Whether payment or arrears of salary and other allowances payable to appellant during period he was kept under suspension and upto date of superannuation? - Held, when employee who has been dismissed, removed or suspended is reinstated, Board shall consider and make a specific order - Firstly, regarding pay and allowances to be paid to employees for period of his absence from duty and secondly, whether or not said period shall be treated as a period on duty - It is directed to respondent to pay arrears of salary and allowances payable to appellant during period of suspension up to date of superannuation after deducting suspension allowance paid to him for said period and also to pay appellant, all retiral benefits otherwise payable to him in accordance with rules and regulations applicable as if there had been no disciplinary enquiry or order passed therein - Appeal allowed.
