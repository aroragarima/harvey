State of Bihar and Others v M. Neethi Chandra and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2532; (1996) 6 SCC 36; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 512; 1996 (4) CLT 196; JT 1996 (9) SC 86; 1996(7) SCALE 5; 1997 (1) SLR 583; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 696; 1996 (7) Supreme 483

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Education - Reservation - Underprivileged sections - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - (A) Whether while laying down procedure, High Court did not take note of eventuality in which number of reserved category candidates who qualify on basis of minimum marks may far exceed number of seats reserved for them? - Held, HC has not clarified its intentions with illustrations - Further Court held, in a particular year, number of such candidates may be much larger and thus method evolved by HC may create much hardship - Method will also not be in tune with principles of equality - Hence method evolved by HC will have to be struck down - If however, word 'adjusted' is read to mean considered along with other general merit list candidates, it will lose much of its value - (B) Apprehensions with regard to mode of allotment suggested by HC whether violative of arts. 14 and 16? - Further held, it is alleged that according to procedure evolved by HC, students at bottom of respective reserved category will necessarily be placed in same college, i.e.,college of last choice of candidates and that therefore this will be violative of arts. 14 and 16 - Apprehension of appellant is totally baseless because choice of subject as well as college will always be different for different students and this difference will exist even for those at end of list and in respect of their last choice - Further, even if such a situation does arise, same cannot be said to be violative of arts. 14 and 16 - Appeal disposed of.
