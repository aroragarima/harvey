Yash Pal Mittal v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

03 November 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, N.L. Untwalia, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 57; (1977) 4 SCC 540; (1978) SCC (Cr) 5; AIR 1977 SC 2433; 1978 CRLJ 189; 1977 CrLR(SC) 503; [1978] 1 S.C.R. 781

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Cheating - IPC, 1860,ss.120-B - CrPC, 1898,s.196A(2) - Whether trial u/s.120-B IPC is vitiated in absence of sanction u/s.196A(2) of CrPC,1898 - Held, No - S. 196A(2) is no bar to present trial in absence of sanction - Since the object of the criminal conspiracy is cheating by personation u/s.419 IPC punishable with imprisonment which may extend to three years, s. 196A(2) is no bar to the present trial in the absence of a sanction - Fact that the accused are charged with other non-cognizable offences in same trial cannot affect validity of trial - Appeal dismissed.
