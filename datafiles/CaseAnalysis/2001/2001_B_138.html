Rajinder Prasad v Bashir and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21060; (2001) 8 SCC 522; (2002) SCC (Cr) 28; AIR 2001 SC 3524; 2001 (4) CCR 60; 2001 (4) Crimes 1; 2002 (1) CRJ 228; 2002 CRLJ 90; 2001 (2) JCC 191; JT 2001 (7) SC 652; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 312; 2001(6) SCALE 414; 2001 (4) SCJ 249; 2001 (6) SLT 653; [2001] Supp3 S.C.R. 156; 2001 (7) Supreme 88; 2001 (2) UC 627

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Non-bailable Warrant

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 482 and 203 - Inherent powers of High Court - Held, object of criminal trial is to render public justice and to assure punishment to criminals keeping in view that trial is concluded expeditiously - Delaying tactics or protracting commencement or conclusion of criminal trial are required to be curbed effectively, lest interest of public justice may suffer - No special circumstances were spelt out in subsequent application for invoking jurisdiction of HC u/s. 482 of Code and impugned order liable to be set aside on this ground alone - Even on merits, HC committed a mistake of law by referring to provisions of s. 203 of Code and after setting aside impugned order directing Judicial Magistrate to hold inquiry as per s. 203(2) of Code before deciding inclusion of offence u/s. 395, IPC, 1860 - Present case is squarely covered by judgments which renders order impugned not sustainable under law - No question of referring to provisions of s. 319, CrPC, 1973 - In the given case, neither Magistrate is holding inquiry as contemplated u/s. 2(g), CrPC, 1973 nor trial had started - Magistrate was exercising his jurisdiction u/s. 190 of taking cognizance of an offence and issuing process - No bar u/s. 190, CrPC, 1973 that once process is issued against some accused on next date, Magistrate cannot issue process to some other person against whom there is some material on record, but his name is not included as accused in chargesheet - Appeal allowed.
