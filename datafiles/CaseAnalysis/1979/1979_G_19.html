State of Gujarat v Gujarat Revenue Tribunal and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 August 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 204; (1979) 4 SCC 40; AIR 1980 SC 91; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 233; 1979 UJ 683

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Bombay Personal Inams Abolition Act, 1952, ss. 5(2)(b) and 7 - Bombay Taluqdari Tenure Abolition Act, 1949, ss. 5(1)(b) and 6 - Bombay Land Revenue Code, 1879, s. 37(2) - Whether taluqdars or inamdars are entitled to settlement of grass-lands on hilly tracts as 'occupants' thereof u/s. 5(1)(b) of Taluqdari Abolition Act and s. 5(2)(b) of Inams Abolition Act? - These provisions speak of extinguishment of any right or interest in land which is 'waste or uncultivated but is culturable' - Lands in question not being fit for cultivation, were not 'culturable' and therefore do not fall within the ambit of these provision - Thus the grass-lands on hilly tracts which are incapable of any cultivation could not, in law, be treated to be uncultivated lands' within the meaning of s. 6, r/w explanation thereto - Held, disputed lands did not vest in the govt. u/s. 6 of Taluqdari Abolition Act and s. 7 of Personal Inams Abolition Act - Acts make no provision whatever for payment of any compensation for acquisition of rights of former taluqdars and inamdars in such lands, hence they are not entitled to any compensation either u/s. 7(1)(b)(i) of Taluqdari Abolition Act and s. 10(1)(b)(i) of Personal Inams Abolition Act - Appeals dismissed.
