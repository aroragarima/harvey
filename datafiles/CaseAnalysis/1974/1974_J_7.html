Jagir Singh v State (Delhi)

Supreme Court of India

04 December 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 316; (1975) SCC (Cr) 129; (1975) 3 SCC 562; AIR 1975 SC 1400; 1975 CRLJ 1009; 1975 CRLR 109

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Appeal against conviction for murder - Whether conviction of appellant can be found on prosecution evidence there being infirmities in evidence? - Name of assailant was very important part of information in regard to incident; if witnesses knew who assailant was, they would have definitely mentioned name of assailant to constable at Hospital who in his evidence, nowhere states that name of appellant was disclosed to him - Evidence of two eye-witnesses cannot be accepted as it is a definite improvement; it was only on next day that for first time name of appellant was given out; further, expert evidence shows that incident did not happen in manner deposed and their evidence being in conflict with unimpeachable expert evidence cannot be accepted - Held, these infirmities coupled with various circumstances, clearly show that evidence of two eye-witnesses is not at all worthy of credit and cannot be relied upon for purpose of holding that it was appellant who fired a short at deceased and killed him - Appeal allowed.
