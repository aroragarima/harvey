Rosiline George v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1535; (1994) 2 SCC 80; (1994) SCC (Cr) 304; 1993 (2) ALT 672; 1993 (4) CCR 443; 1993 (3) Crimes 583; JT 1993 (6) SC 51; 1994 (1) KLT 36; 1993(4) SCALE 230; 1994 (1) UJ 628

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Indian Independence Act, 1947, Fugitive Offenders Act, 1881

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Extradition Act, 1962 - Fugitive Offenders Act, 1881 - Government Of India Act, 1935 - Indian Independence Act, 1947 - Appellant was wanted in the United States of America, to stand trial for violation of Federal Fraud Statutes - Appellants appealed against the extradition proceedings, of the HC - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the extradition treaty could not be exercised - Held, that the 1931 treaty between India and United States of America as notified u/s. 3 of the Act was subsisting and operating between the two countries - It was not disputed that the offences alleged to have been committed by appellant in the letter of request by the State of America would, if committed in the local limits of the Magistrate, have given the Magistrate jurisdiction to inquire into the same - 1962 Act, being a special provision dealing with the extradition of fugitive criminals, should exclude from application the general provisions of the CrPC - Appellant, was wholly and solely responsible for the gross delay in the conclusion of the extradition proceedings against him - Magistrate was directed to record the evidence on behalf of the Central Govt., if necessary, on 25-10-1993, where after, he should give an opportunity to appellant to produce his evidence in rebuttal, and should send his report to the Govt. - In case appellant failed to present himself in the court, Magistrate should hold the proceedings in the jail premises and complete the inquiry in accordance with the above schedule - Appeals dismissed.
