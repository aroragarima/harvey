Mst. L.M.S. Ummu Saleema v B.B. Gujaral and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, A.P. Sen, Baharul Islam

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 407; (1981) 3 SCC 317; (1981) SCC (Cr) 720; AIR 1981 SC 1191; [1983] 53 Comp Cas 312; 1981 CRLJ 889; 1981 CRLR 587; 1981(1) SCALE 843; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 647; 1981 UJ 487

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Constitution, art. 22(5) - COFEPOSA Act, 1974, s. 3(1) - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 16 and 114 - Detention - (A) Non supply of casually referenced documents which were not relied upon by detaining authority while making detention order - (1) If prevented him from making effective representation - (2) Constitutionality - (B) Allege retraction from his original statement before detention order under certificate of posting - Defence sending its photostat copy to detaining authority along with representation - But refrained from sending copy of letter or retraction itself (which is not not traceable ) - Inference - (C) Validity of delay in disposal of representation which was despatched on 5, received on 13, put up and disposed of, on 19 as Authority not available from 13 to 16 - (A) Detenu not prevented from making effective representation - Art. 22 (5) is not violated - (B) It could be said that no such letter was posted as claimed by detenu - (C) Held, that there was no unaccountable or unreasonable delay in the disposal of the representation by the detaining authority - Petition Dismissed.
