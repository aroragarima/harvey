Kumari Madhuri Patil v Addl. Commissioner

Supreme Court of India

02 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 760; (1994) 6 SCC 241; (1994) SCC (L&S) 1349; AIR 1995 SC 94; AIR 1994 SCW 4116; 1994 (28) ATC 259; 1995 (2) Bom.C.R. 690; 1994 (68) FLR 1040; JT 1994 (5) SC 488; 1994(3) SCALE 935; 1994 (5) SLR 206; [1994] Supp3 S.C.R. 50

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Educational Institution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 15(1), 15(4), 16(1), 16(4), 46, 51-A(h), 226, 342 and 366(25) - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - (A) Whether approach adopted by High Court in not elaborately considering case is vitiated by an error of law? - HC is not a court of appeal to appreciate evidence - Committee which is empowered to evaluate evidence placed before it when records a finding of fact, it ought to prevail unless found vitiated by judicial review of any HC subject to limitations of interference with findings of fact - Held, findings of committee cannot be reversed only on ground that another view is possible - (B) Whether appellants are entitled to their further continuance in studies even after knowledge of their false social status certificate? - When it is found to be a case of fraud plea of promissory estoppel or equity have no application - Delay in process is inevitable but that factor should neither be considered to be relevant nor be an aid to compete course of study - Further Further held, no useful purpose would be served to debar Suchita from appearing for examination of final year MBBS, therefore, she is allowed to appear in final examination - She will not, however be entitled in future for any benefits on basis of fraudulent social status - Madhuri is in midway of her study in BDS in end of second year, she cannot continue her studies with her social status as Mahadeo Koli - Appeal dismissed.
