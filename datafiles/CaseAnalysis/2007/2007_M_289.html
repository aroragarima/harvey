Messrs O.N.G.C. Limited v Commissioner of Customs, Mumbai

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 792; (2007) 10 SCC 484; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 677; 2007 (215) E.L.T. 166; JT 2007 (10) SC 76; 2007(9) SCALE 529; 2007 (6) SCJ 10; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 743

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Indirect Tax - Customs - Interest - Entitlement - Appellant is a public sector undertaking wholly owned and controlled by the Central Government - Obtained services for obtaining requisite information which would assist it in the matter of exploration of oil and gas - On the amount paid, custom duty was sought to be levied - Exemption was claimed by the appellant in terms of an exemption notification issued by the Central Government - Despite such claim, assessment proceedings were initiated and completed - Application filed for a direction to the respondent to pay interest on the sum with effect from the date of recovery till the date of the appellant - Held, in principle as also in equity the appellant is entitled to interest on the amount deposited on application of principle of restitution - Amount deposited by the appellant shall carry interest at the rate of 6% per annum.
