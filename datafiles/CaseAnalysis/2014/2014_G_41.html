K. Madhava Reddy and others v Government of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 323; (2014) 6 SCC 537; AIR 2014 SC 2314; 2014 (4) ALD(SC) 177; 2014 (4) ALT(SC) 50; 2014 (142) FLR 1014; JT 2014 (5) SC 595; 2014(6) SCALE 64; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 348; 2014 (6) SLR 491

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: State Administrative Tribunal, Madras Village Panchayat Amendment Act, 1964, Andhra Pradesh Public Employment (Organisation of Local Cards and Regulation of Direct Recruitment) Order, 1975, Andhra Pradesh Labour Subordinate Service Rules

Summary: Service - Doctrine of prospective overruling - GoMs 14 and 22 - Ultra vires - Instant appeals were directed against an order passed by HC whereby the HC set aside the order passed by the Tribunal to the extent the same holds the judgment of SC in V. Jagannadha Rao and Ors. v. State of Andhra Pradesh and Ors. 2001 Indlaw SC 21018, to be prospective in its application - An order passed by HC dismissing a review petition filed by appellants against the said order has also been assailed - Hence instant petition - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified -

Held, respondents were not correct in suggesting as though petitioners had taken any deliberate or calculated risk by opting for promotion outside their cadres - Respondents while making that assertion ignored the fact that promotions were ordered by the State and not snatched by petitioners - That apart on the date the promotions were made there was no element of risk nor were the promotions made subject to the determination of any legal controversy as to the entitlement of the incumbents to such promotion - Not only that, the incumbents who were sent out on promotion as Assistant Labour Officers had subsequently been promoted as Assistant Labour Commissioners or Deputy Labour Commissioners - Such being the position reverting these officers at this distant point of time, to the posts of Senior Stenographers in their parent cadre does not appear to be either just, fair or equitable especially when upon reversion the State does not propose to promote them to the higher positions within their zone/cadre because such higher posts were occupied by other officers, most if not all of whom are junior to the petitioners and who may have to be reverted to make room for the petitioners to hold those higher posts - Reversion of petitioners to their parent cadre was therefore bound to have a cascading effect, prejudicing even those who were not parties before SC - Fact that petitioners were not arrayed as parties before the Tribunal or before HC also brought the fact situation of the instant case closer to that in Kailash Chand's case 2002 Indlaw SC 1748 - Law in instant case was, as in Kailash Chand's case 2002 Indlaw Sc 1748, in a state of flux - Such being the position, SC see no reason why the doctrine of prospective overruling could not be invoked in instant case - Just because, SC had not addressed that question in Jagannadha Rao's case was also no reason for us to refuse to do so in instant case - Apart, Jagannadha Rao's case was dealing with a different set of norms comprising GoMs No.14 and 22 referred to earlier - While the basic question whether such GoMs permitting promotion by transfer from one department to the cadre or zone to another may have been the same, it could not be denied that the rules with which SC was concerned in Jagannadha Rao's case were different from those with which we were dealing in instant case - SC felt that on the question of application of doctrine of prospective overruling, the judgment in Jagannadha Rao's case would not stand as an impediment for SC - Impugned orders passed by HC were set aside and hold that while GoMs No.14 and 22 were rightly declared to be ultra vires of the Presidential Order by the Tribunal, the said declaration should not affect the promotions and appointments made on the basis of the said GoMs prior to 7-11-2001, the date when Jagannadha Rao's was decided by SC - Appeals allowed.
