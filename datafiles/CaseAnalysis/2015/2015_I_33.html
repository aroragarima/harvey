Delhi International Airport Limited v International Lease Finance Corporation and others

Supreme Court of India

17 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 195; (2015) 8 SCC 446; AIR 2015 SC 1903; 2015 (111) ALR 216; 2015 (3) MLJ 212; 2015(3) SCALE 820; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 1040

Case Digest

Subject: Air Law; Constitution

Keywords: Government of India (Allocation of Business) Rules, 1961, Airports Authority of India (Management of Airports) Regulations, 2003

Summary: Air Law - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art.77 - Airports Authority of India (Management of Airports) Regulations, 2003, reg. 10 - Government of India (Allocation of Business) Rules, 1961, rr.3, 4 - Detention of Aircrafts - Orders of Central Govt. - Airline licence of respondent no. 8, Kingfisher Airlines (KAL) was suspended due to non-payment of parking, landing and housing charges of aircraft which was leased to KAL by respondent no. 1, and was detained - Later, other aircrafts of KAL lying at various airports also got detained at different airports due to non-payment of charges and subsequently deregistered - Hence, assailing order of detention of aircrafts and challenging vires of regn.10 of the Regn., respondent no. 1 filed writ petition before HC - However, during pendency of writ petition, meeting was held on 26-3-2013 by representatives of Civil Aviation Ministry, Central Board of Excise & Customs, etc. regarding release of aircrafts by airport operators - While so, HC vide impugned order directed all airports to release aircrafts in terms of decision taken in meeting dt.26-3-2013 on payment of parking charges up to 13-5-2013 - Aggrieved, instant appeal by appellant-DIAL - Whether minutes of meeting could override statutory regns.

Held, from combined reading of rr.3, 4, 4(2) and in light of decisions of SC, the minutes of meeting which is to be converted as a general or special order in writing by Central Govt. involving the abandonment of revenue or which has a financial implication on the Airports Authority of India which is under the control of Civil Aviation Ministry, is required to proceed only after the concurrence of Finance Department. Hence, sanctification by the concerned ministry and the concurrence of Finance Department is a mandatory condition in order to hold the minutes of the meeting dt.26-3-2013 as 'a general or special order in writing by the Central Govt.' In the absence of any such sanctification by the competent authority, mere minutes of the meeting would not give any indefeasible right to the appellant. Further, there is nothing on record to show that the minutes of the meeting had the concurrence of the Finance department and was either confirmed or approved by the concerned minister and such direction were not shown to have been issued pursuant to any decision taken by a competent authority in terms of Rules of Business framed u/art. 77 of the Constitution. Hence, without going into the merits of the matter, HC was not right in disposing of the matter in terms of the minutes of the meeting dt.26-3-2013 and so, impugned order is liable to be set aside. Further, appellant is at liberty to invoke the bank guarantee furnished by respondents. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - The minutes of the meeting do not become a general or special order in writing by the Central Govt. unless the same was sanctified and acted upon by issuing an order in the name of the President in the manner provided u/art.77(2) of the Constitution.
