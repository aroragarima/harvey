Western States Trading Co Ltd v Commissioner Of Income Tax, Central Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

18 January 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, K.S. Hegde, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 112; (1971) 1 SCC 329; AIR 1971 SC 2274; [1971] 80 ITR 21; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 383; 1971 UJ 190

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 10(2)(vii), 24(2) and 66 - Practice and Procedure - (A) Reference - Whether High Court can disturb finding of fact recorded by Tribunal - If they are not challenged before it by raising appropriate question - (B) Business loss - If can be set off - Sale of business after carrying it for a part of the accounting year - (C) Dividends received on shares forming assessee's trading assets is income from business - Set off against loss carried forward - Held, HC cannot disturb finding of fact recorded by Tribunal - Loss arising out of sale can be set-off - Can be set off against loss carried forward from earlier years - Appeal Allowed.
