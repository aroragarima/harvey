Dinaji And Ors. v Daddi And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 November 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

G.L. Oza, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 261; (1990) 1 SCC 1; AIR 1990 SC 1153; 1990 ALR 205; 1990 (1) CCC 63; 1990 (2) DMC 296; JT 1989 (4) SC 434; 1990 MahLJ 527; 1990 MPLJ 1; 1989(2) SCALE 1178; [1989] Supp2 S.C.R. 144

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Indian Regulation Act 1908, Hindu Adoptions And Maintenance Act, 1956.

Summary: Family & Procedure - Civil Procedure - Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956, ss. 12 and 13 - Hindu Succession Act - Registration Act, 1908, ss. 17(1)(b) and 49 - Son was adopted by a widow - Dispute regarding rights of adopted son and mother on property - Held, widow after death of her husband and after Hindu Succession Act came into force, become absolute owner of property and therefore property of her husband vested in her and merely by adopting child she could not be deprived of any of her rights in property - Adopted son get rights for which he is entitled after her death as per s. 12 proviso (C) - As per s. 17(1)(b) Act, 1908 that a document where any right in immovable property is either assigned or extinguished will require registration - Part of deed which refers to creation of immediate right in adopted son and divesting of right of adoptive mother in property will squarely fall within ambit of s. 17(1)(b) and therefore u/s. 49, this could not be admitted since it is not registered document - Widow entitled to right over property - Appeal allowed.
