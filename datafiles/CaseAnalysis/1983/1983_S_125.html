Ram Narayan Agarwal Etc Etc. v State Of Uttar Pradesh And Others

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, A.P. Sen, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 55; (1983) 4 SCC 276; (1983) SCC (Tax) 322; AIR 1984 SC 1213; 1983(2) SCALE 156; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 684; 1983 (54) STC 273; 1983 TAXLR 3008; 1983 UJ 682

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Withholding, Attachment And Sale, Recovery Of Arrears, Madras Revenue Recovery Act, 1864, Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Rules, 1952

Summary: Practice and Procedure - U.P.Z.A.& L.R. Act, 1950, ss. 279(1)(b),281 - Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Rules, 1952,rr. 247 to 253 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19, 19(1)(d) and 21 - U.P. Sales Tax Act, 1948 - Process - (A) Validity of the action taken by the authorities concerned against the petitioners for recovering the arrears of tax due and payable by them under the U.P. Sales Tax Act, 1948, by the arrest and detention in civil prison is accordance with s. 279(1)(b) r/w s.281 of the U.P.Z.A.& L.R. Act, 1950 and the Rules - Held, not violative of arts. 14, 19 (1) (d) and 21; and defaluter cannot be detained pursuant to warrant of arrest issued - (B) Applicability of provisions of s. 51, CPC,1908 to the process of arrest and detention under the Act - Provision would not in terms be applicable - Petition partly allowed.
