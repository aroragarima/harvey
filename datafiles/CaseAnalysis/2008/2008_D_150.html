M. Balakrishna Reddy v Director, Cbi, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

14 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 531; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 391; (2008) 4 SCC 409; AIR 2008 SC 1754; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 1727; 2008 (2) JCC 1202; JT 2008 (4) SC 162; 2008 (2) RCR(Criminal) 637; 2008(4) SCALE 617; [2008] 4 S.C.R. 1154

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Preventive Detention Act, 1950

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420, 471, 474, 467, 468 and 417 r/w s. 511 - Preliminary objection that alleged offences had been committed at Bhopal in the State of Madhya Pradesh and CBI had no power, authority or jurisdiction to institute criminal proceedings and before initiating proceedings under the Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, 1946 consent of the State Government is required dismissed - Appeal against - Held, decision produced by the respondent along with the counter-affidavit filed by the Superintendent of Police, CBI, Bhopal clearly sets out all the particulars required by s. 6 - It refers to the file/reference number, name of the department, the authority from whom it was issued and communicated to the concerned department of the Central Government - It, therefore, cannot be said that the State Government had not granted consent under s. 6 - Prosecution instituted by CBI against the appellant cannot be said to be without jurisdiction - Appeal dismissed.
