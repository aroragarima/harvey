Commissioner Central Excise, Mumbai-III v EMCO Limited

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 978; (2015) 10 SCC 321; 2015 (322) E.L.T. 394; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 925

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Show Cause Notice, Duty Of Excise, Central Excise Act, 1944, Excisable Goods, Sale of Goods Act, 1930, Central Excise (Valuation) Rules, 1975, Place Of Removal, Finance Act, 2000, Central Excise (Determination of Retail Sale Price of Excisable Goods) Rules, 2008

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 4, 4(3)(d) - Transaction value - Place of removal - Revenue issued notice to assessee for recovery of short excise duty paid contending that assessee was not including transportation and transit insurance cost while computing excise duty and that transaction value would include amount charged on account of transportation and transit insurance as it was covered by definition of 'transaction value' contained in s. 4(3)(d) of the Act - Later, Commissioner confirmed demand in show cause notice vide order dt.18-8-2002 - Aggrieved, assessee filed appeal before Appellate Tribunal and the same was allowed in view of decision of SC - Hence, instant appeals by Revenue contending that Appellate Tribunal passed non-reasoned cryptic order - Whether instant case is covered by judgment of SC in Escorts JCB Ltd. v. Collector of Central Excise, 2002 Indlaw SC 1171.

Held, it is to be determined whether the 'place of removal' was the factory gate of the respondent or it was the premises of the purchaser at the time of delivery of these goods. If the goods are cleared at the factory gate, then the excise duty has to be charged on the valuation of the goods to be arrived at the factory gate as that would be the place of removal of goods meaning that expenses which are incurred after removal of goods from factory gate namely freight, insurance and unloading charges etc. are not to be included in valuation of goods for the purposes of excise duty. However, Tribunal allowed appeal of assessee in a mechanical manner and overruled decision of Commissioner and even though there is detailed discussion in the order of Commissioner on the facts of the case yet Appellate Tribunal had not adverted to deal with such facts. The decision of Commissioner is overruled with single observation that the case is covered by judgment in Escorts JCB Ltd case without discussing as to how it was so covered, notwithstanding the fact that the decision as to which is the 'place of removal' depends upon the facts of each case. Hence, order of Tribunal is set aside and matter is remitted to Tribunal for fresh consideration. Further, after considering terms and conditions of the sale with the buyer Tribunal is to determine on that basis as to which was place of removal, whether factory gate of assessee or place of delivery. Order accordingly.

Ratio - 'Place of removal' is the place or premises from where the excisable goods are to be sold after their clearance from the factory and from where such goods are removed and as such, 'place of removal' is crucial determinative factor for purpose of valuation.
