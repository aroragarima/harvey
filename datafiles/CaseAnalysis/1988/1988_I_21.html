Ammal Chandra Dutt v Second Additional District Judge and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 November 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 202; (1989) 1 SCC 1; AIR 1989 SC 255; 1989 ALJ 27; 1988 ALR 794; 1988 (2) AWC 1513; JT 1988 (4) SC 291; 1988 (2) RCJ 691; 1989 (1) RCR(Rent) 334; 1988(2) SCALE 1450; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 722; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 722; 1989 (1) UJ 244

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, Uttar Pradesh (Temporary) Control of Rent and Eviction Act, 1947

Summary: Rent Control - Civil Procedure - Eviction - U.P. Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Rules, 1972, r. 18(1) - U P (Temporary) Control of Rent and Eviction Act, 1947,s. 3 - U P Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972 - Second respondent was gifted property in dispute by his mother - Property had been let out to appellant and he was residing as well as running a drug store in premises - Respondent sought permission for eviction on ground of urgent and reasonable requirement for own occupation which was rejected - Application was rejected by prescribed authority but was granted by Appellate Authority only partly - Whether second application for eviction was barred by r. 18? - R. 18(1) does not prohibit or bar filing of application for release of any building on any ground mentioned in s. 21(1) within a period of six months from date on which a final order was passed in previous application made u/s. 3 of Act, 1947 or within a period of six months from commencement of Act, 1972 - Rule of presumption enunciated in r. 18(1) is only to serve as a guideline to be followed by prescribed authority if he finds circumstances to remain unchanged and finding rendered in earlier application to have relevance even with reference to facts set out in second application - Held, no - Second application filed is changed circumstance - Second application for eviction was filed near about 5 years thereafter but within 6 months of commencement of Act, 1972 - In changed circumstances bar u/r. 18(1) would not operate and second application for eviction on ground of bona fide requirement was maintainable -Appeal dismissed.
