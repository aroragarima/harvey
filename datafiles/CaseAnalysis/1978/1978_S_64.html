Prakash Chand v State (Delhi Administration)

Supreme Court of India

20 November 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 271; (1979) 3 SCC 90; (1979) SCC (Cr) 656; AIR 1979 SC 400; 1979 CRLJ 329; 1979 CrLR(SC) 35; 1979 (15) DLT 68; 1979 MLJ 419; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 330; 1979 UJ 17

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, S. 167 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 145, 155 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 161 ,162(1) - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, S. 5(1) (d) r/w. s. 5(2) - Demand of Bribe - Substantial Evidence - Rejection of Evidence - Conviction - For sanction of revised submission of plan of P.W. 6/Architect, appellant/accused demanded bribe - P.W. 9 found sum from file of appellant and he was duly tried, convicted and sentenced by Trial Court and HC for an offence u/s. 5(1) (d) r/w. s. 5(2) of Act, 1988 and s. 161 of Code, 1860 and sentenced to suffer RI for a period of one year and to pay fine - Conviction and sentence were confirmed by HC - Hence instant appeal - Whether conviction of appellant was maintainable - Held, P.Ws. 1 and 2 were not truthful witnesses and they have given evidence to accommodate appellant - Hostile witnesses, confronted with their earlier statements and rejection of their evidence was permissible u/s. 155 of act, 1872 and Code of Criminal Procedure, s. 162(1) - Court did not saw any reason to rule out evidence relating to conduct of appellant, which led to circumstantial testimony of P.W. 6 - On consideration of entire evidence, Court was satisfied that appellant was rightly convicted and did not warrant any interference u/art. 136 of Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
