Sushil Kumar v Rakesh Kumar

Supreme Court of India

16 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 873; (2003) 8 SCC 673; AIR 2004 SC 230; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 575; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 753; 2003(8) SCALE 659; 2003 (6) SLT 586; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 802; 2003 (8) Supreme 149

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Representation of People Act, 1951 - ss. 36(2), 116-A - Constitution of India, 1950 - art. 173(b) - Election petition - Appellant filed petition on the ground that the respondent was under 25 years at the time of filing his nomination paper - Held, the materials on records taken in their entirety together with the circumstantial evidence goes to show that the respondent was not above the age of 25 years on the date of filing of the nomination - Further held, a person cannot be permitted to occupy an office for which he is disqualified under the Constitution. Election of the respondent declared as void.
