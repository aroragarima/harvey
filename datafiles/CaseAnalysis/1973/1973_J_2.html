Rosy Jacob v Jacob A. Chakramakkal

Supreme Court of India

05 April 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, A. Alagiriswami, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 120; (1973) 1 SCC 840; AIR 1973 SC 2090; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 918

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Minority, Adoption & Guardianship

Summary: Family & Personal - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 136 - Divorce Act, 1869, s. 41 - Guardians and Wards Act, 1890, s. 25 - (A) Revocation of special leave already granted u/art. 136, when open - (B) Guardians and Wards Act, 1890 - Judicial separation between husband and wife - Husband's right to apply for custody of children - Welfare of children - Duty of Court - Held, if misrepresentation of facts is deliberate and on a point having such relevance to question of special leave that if true facts were known it would have in all opportunity declined special leave - Under facts and circumstances of case, custody of daughter, even though she was more than 13 years of age, and that of youngest minor son was considered to be more beneficial with wife rather than with husband and duty of Court pointed out - Appeal allowed.
