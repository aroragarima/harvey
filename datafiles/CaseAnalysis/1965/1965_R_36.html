All India Reserve Bank Employees Association v Reserve Bank Of India

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 142; AIR 1966 SC 305; [1966] 36 Comp Cas 165; 1965 (11) FLR 137; 1965 (2) LLJ 175; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 25

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Fair Wage, Supervisor, National Tribunal, Cost Of Living, Labour Appellate Tribunal, Living Wage, Air Force Act, 1950, Indian Labour Conference, Reserve Bank Transfer of public Ownership Act 1948, Reserve Bank Of India (Staff) Regulations, 1948, Reserve Bank (Transfer to Public Ownership) Act, I948

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(s), 2(k) and 10(1A) - Constitution, art. 43 - Reserve Bank of India (Staff) Regulations, 1948, regns. 28 and 29 - Reserve Bank of India (Payment of Gratuity of Employees) Rules, 1947, r. 5(1)(b) - Industrial Dispute among RBI and its employees - Workman - (A) Supervisory staff of RBI - Some drawing wages less than Rs. 500 and while some drawing above - Dispute as to revision of salaries - Reference u/s. 10 (1A) - Validity - Who can raise dispute - (B) Meaning of 'Fair wages and living wages' - (C) Determination of wage structure - Acceptance of need based minimum wage formula determined at fifteenth Labour Conference in 1957 for of basic wage - Difference in the cost of living between members of clerical staff and subordinate staff - (D) Promotion is a matter of discretion as intended by regns. 28 and 29 of 1948 Regulations - Consideration of seniority and merit - (E) Extent of forfeiture of gratuity in case of dismissal for misconduct u/r. 5(1)(b) of 1947 Rules - (F) Some rule or guideline sought on ' Period of probation and confirmation' for temporary employees - (G) Whether Union 's demand to represent workers in a 'Dispute between individual workman and employer ' be allowed - (H) Whether Time for commencement of award u/s. 17A depends on the discretion of tribunal - Held, valid reference at the time it was made - Directive principle of living wages inthe light of art. 43 - Found to be proper co-efficient - Seniority and merit should ordinarily both have a part - Forfeiture except to recoup a loss occasioned to the Bank by reason of the misconduct of the employee is not justified - No hard and fast rule can be laid down - Such disputes cannot be allowed as internal administration would become impossible by such intervention - Discretion exercised on judicial principle will not be interfered with - Appeal Dismissed.
