Sou. Sandhya Manoj Wankhade v Manoj Bhimrao Wankhade and others

Supreme Court of India

31 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 94; (2011) 3 SCC 650; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 975; 2011 CRLJ 1687; 2011 (2) CTC 455; 2011 (2) G.L.R. 1111; 2011 (2) JCC 717; JT 2011 (3) SC 265; 2011 (1) KLT 609; 2011 (2) MLJ(Crl) 429; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 1; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 884B; 2011(2) SCALE 94; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 261; 2011 (1) WLN(SC) 53

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005, s. 2(q) - Interpretation of the expression 'respondent' in s. 2(q) of the Domestic Violence Act - Whether female relatives of the husband or male partner were excluded from the ambit of a complaint that can be made under the provisions of the Domestic Violence Act? - Held, although s. 2(q) defines a respondent to mean any adult male person, who was or had been in a domestic relationship with the aggrieved person, the proviso widens the scope of the said definition by including a relative of the husband or male partner within the scope of a complaint, which may be filed by an aggrieved wife or a female living in a relationship in the nature of a marriage - However, if the Legislature intended to exclude females from the ambit of the complaint, which could be filed by an aggrieved wife, females would have been specifically excluded, instead of it being provided in the proviso that a complaint could also be filed against a relative of the husband or the male partner - Hence, no restrictive meaning was given to the expression 'relative', nor has the said expression been specifically defined in the Domestic Violence Act to make it specific to males only - In such circumstances, it was clear that the legislature never intended to exclude female relatives of the husband or male partner from the ambit of a complaint that can be made under the provisions of the Domestic Violence Act - Appeal allowed.
