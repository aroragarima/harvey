Eerati Laxman v State Of A.P.

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1008; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 15; (2009) 3 SCC 337; AIR 2009 SC 1816; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 1217; 2009 CRLJ 1727; 2009 (3) JCC 1669; JT 2009 (2) SC 258; 2009 (3) MLJ(Crl) 947; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 942; 2009(2) SCALE 41; [2009] 1 S.C.R. 509

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Human Rights; Women & Children

Keywords: Family Law Reform Act, 1969

Summary: Criminal - Women & Children - Human Rights - Family Law Reform Act, 1969 - General Clauses Act, 1897, ss. 3(35), 3(66) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000, s. 20 - Juvenile Justice Act, 1986, ss. 2(h), 20 - Majority Act, 1875, s. 3 - Juvenile Accused - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant-accused was alleged to have committed murder of 'P' by pouring kerosene and setting her on fire with a matchstick - Contention of accused was rejected by Trial judge opining that accused was not a juvenile as no reliance could be placed on date of his birth recorded in registers of primary school - Revision application was filed before HC but HC reason of judgment and order matter was remanded back to Trial Court - Appeal preferred by appellant there against had been dismissed by reason of impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of HC arriving at conclusion that accused was not juvenile within meaning of provisions of the said Act was sustainable - Held, HC was not entirely correct in arriving at said conclusion - Said Act was a beneficent legislation - It, however, would not mean that principle of literal interpretation thereof should not be resorted to - Appellant, therefore, having been born on 10-5-1978, said day was to be counted as a whole day and, thus, he had not attained age of 16 years before 12 o'clock in midnight of previous day, i.e. 9-5-1978 - This aspect of matter was recently been considered in Achhaibar Maurya vs. State of Uttar Pradesh & ors. 2007 Indlaw SC 1343 - Hence, impugned judgment could not be sustained - Matter was remitted to Juvenile Justice Board in terms of s. 20 of the Act - Appeal allowed.
