Krushan Chandra Sahu Dr. and Others v State of Orissa and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 September 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 205; (1995) 6 SCC 1; (1995) SCC (L&S) 1321; AIR 1996 SC 352; AIR 1995 SCW 4177; 1995 (31) ATC 438; 1995 (2) CLR 100; 1995 (2) CLR 1006; JT 1995 (7) SC 137; 1996 LabIC 53; 1996 (1) LLJ 919; 1995(5) SCALE 329; 1996 (1) SLJ 117; 1995 (5) SLR 337; [1995] Supp3 S.C.R. 419

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Orissa Homoeopathy Medical Teaching Service Rule, 1980, rr. 5(1), 6, 8 and 10 - Central Council Act, 1973 - Whether the Tribunal was correct in finding that the selection of the appellants was vitiated by the procedure adopted by the Selection Board or by non-assignment of various discipline to the selected candidates? - In order to assess the suitability or real worth of a candidate of the post of junior teacher in the college, the basis, namely, the character rolls, adopted by the Selection Board was wholly arbitrary besides being without authority or jurisdiction - Selection criteria should be inclusive of the merits so that the best and most suitable could have been selected - Held, thus Tribunal was right in holding that the selection should have been held on a criteria which was inclusive of the merits so that the best and most suitable could have been selected - The appellants shall continue on ad hoc basis till a fresh selection is held - The respondent should amend the rule or issue necessary administrative/executive instructions laying down the basis on which the suitability of the candidates shall be determined - Appeal disposed of.
