Pancharan Dhara and Others v Monmatha Nath Maity (Dead) Th. LRs. and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 644; (2006) 5 SCC 340; AIR 2006 SC 2281; 2006 (4) ALD(SC) 18; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 226; JT 2006 (11) SC 89; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 81; 2006 (3) RCR(Civil) 212; 2006 (2) RCR(Rent) 1; 2006(6) SCALE 34; 2006 (6) SCJ 234; 2007 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 627; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 520

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Title Suit

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Title suit - Dismissed - Legality - Plaintiff appellant filed Title Suit before Trial Court and the same was dismissed - On appeal before Appellate Court the same was dismissed - Second appeal was filed before Single Judge - Single Judge affirmed order passed by Appellate Court dismissed the suit - Hence instant appeal.

Held, plea raised by counsel for appellant that suit was barred by limitation cannot be accepted as all Courts have arrived at finding of fact that period for execution of deed of sale had been extended. An oral agreement for sale is permissible in law. There is furthermore no dispute that agreement for sale was entered into by three directors of company. Subsequent letters written on behalf of Company clearly demonstrate that all directors were aware of said agreement. Company before Trial Court never chose to file any written statement or dispute contentions raised in plaint. Company, thus, never denied or disputed correctness or otherwise of contents of said agreement. Company never denied or disputed terms of agreement nor raised any plea that agreement was not binding on company or same was illegal. Before Courts below, execution of agreement was not denied. Thus, even in absence of resolution contract could not have been held to be invalid or illegal. Appeal dismissed.
