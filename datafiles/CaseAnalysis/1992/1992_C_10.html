Calcutta Electric Supply Company Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

15 January 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1126

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 32(1)(iii), 34(2), 41(2), 43(1) and 43(6) - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10(2)(vi) and 10(5)(a) - Assessee was entitled to an allowance of depreciation at percentage of actual cost or written down value of relevant asset owned by him and used for purposes of business - Common question arose regarding computation of depreciation on service lines installed by assesses and were computed as a percentage of written down value of asset - Hence instant appeal - Whether assessee entitled to depreciation on actual cost for A/Y's 1961-62 to 1965-66 - Held, the assessee was not entitled to depreciation - Assessee would have to pay tax on Rs. 5,000 by way of balancing charge though he had been allowed depreciation only to the extent of Rs. 2,710 - If the asset was sold for Rs. 2,500 in the previous year relevant to the A/Y 1963-64, according to the Department, he would have to pay tax on Rs. 2,500 whereas, under the old provisions, he would have got an allowance u/s. 32(1)(iii), of the 1961 Act - This was only a seeming anomaly for, the sums of Rs. 5,000 and Rs. 2,500 would be taxed not as a balancing charge but as capital gains which was quite consistent with the department's position that the assessee having paid nothing for the asset, its actual cost should be taken at nil, stand in which there was no absurdity - Appeals dismissed.
