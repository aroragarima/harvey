Denel (Proprietary Limited) v Govt. Of India, Ministry Of Defence

Supreme Court of India

09 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 25; (2012) 2 SCC 759; AIR 2012 SC 817; 2012 (91) ALR 206; 2012 (1) AWC 605; 2012 (1) CLT(SC) 22; 2012 (3) CTC 90; JT 2012 (1) SC 53; 2012 (3) MLJ(SC) 534; 2012 (3) RCR(Civil) 611; 2012(1) SCALE 73; [2012] 2 S.C.R. 897; 2012 (3) WLN(SC) 67

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Contracts & Commercial Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 11(4) and (6) - Appointment of arbitrator - Mandate - Independent arbitrator - Validity - Petition to appoint arbitrator to resolve disputes - Petitioner and respondent entered into contract for supply of Base Bleed Units with Arbitration clause - Disputes arose which were not settled mutually - DGOF appointed AGMOF as arbitrator to resolve disputes - Petitioner objected and apprehending that arbitrator would favorably incline towards employer issued notification u/s. 14 of Act stating that mandate of arbitrator had been terminated - In-spite of notification arbitrator continued with proceedings - Petitioner filed application before District Court - DC terminated mandate of sole arbitrator with observation that arbitrator had been biased and directed DGOF to be arbitrator or appoint Government Servant as arbitrator - Aggrieved petitioner filed instant petition seeking appointment of independent arbitrator - Petitioner contended that directions issued by DC were without authority or jurisdiction as such void ab initio and incorrect interpretation of s. 15 of Act - Respondent contended that petitioner had forfeited its right to appoint arbitrator as an arbitrator had already been appointed and duly notified to respondent, but respondent failed to forward its claim within 10 days as such petition is not maintainable - Whether arbitrator appoint by respondent would not be independent and appointment of arbitrator is necessary - Held, that even while exercising jurisdiction u/s. 11(6), Court is required to have due regard to provisions contained in s. 11(8) of the Act, which provides that apart from ensuring that arbitrator possesses necessary qualifications required of arbitrator by agreement of parties, Court shall have due regard to other considerations as are likely to ensure appointment of an independent and impartial arbitrator - Managing Director may not be in a position to independently decide dispute between parties - Retired Justice of HC is appointed as sole arbitrator - Petition allowed.
