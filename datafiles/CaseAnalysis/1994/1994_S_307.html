Maniram v State Of U. P.

Supreme Court of India

13 May 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, B.L. Hansaria, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 2190; (1994) Supp2 SCC 289; (1994) SCC (Cr) 1242; 1994 (2) CCR 546; 1994 (2) Crimes 957; 1994 CRLJ 3848; 1994 JCC 509; JT 1994 (4) SC 62; 1994(2) SCALE 970; [1994] Supp1 S.C.R. 63

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 149, 147 and 148 - Murder - Prior enmity between accused persons and deceased and some criminal and civil cases were already pending between them - Evidence of close relatives - Appellants convicted by Trial Court and also by High Court - Hence, present appeal - If evidence of prosecution is totally inconsistent with medical evidence, it is most fundamental defect in prosecution and unless this inconsistency is reasonably explained it is sufficient not only to discredit evidence but entire case - Held, main witness is highly interested witness and his evidence not consistent with medical evidence - Difficult to accept him as an eyewitness to occurrence and, therefore, it would not be safe to base conviction on solitary evidence of such a witness - Convictions set-aside - Appeal allowed.
