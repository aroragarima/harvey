Srish Kumar Choudhury v State Of Tripura And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

23 February 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, M.M. Punchhi, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 724; (1990) Supp SCC 220; AIR 1990 SC 991; JT 1990 (2) SC 27; 1990 LabIC 707; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 576; 1990 (5) SLR 222; 1990 (2) UJ 16

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Human Rights; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 342 and 366(25) - Constitution (Scheduled Tribes) (Union Territories) Order (1950), Item 15, Item 16, Item 17, Item 18 - Laskar community of Tripura - Until 1976, it was a schedule tribe - Later state Govt. declared it general - Whether community Schedule Tribe? - Held, no - It's not a schedule tribe - Presidents Order - Not open to Court to make any addition or subtraction from Presidential Order - Alteration only through legislation - Appeal dismissed.
