G.M. (Operations) S.B.I. and another v R. Periyasamy

Supreme Court of India

10 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sharad Arvind Bobde, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 839; (2015) 3 SCC 101; 2015 (144) FLR 1003; JT 2014 (13) SC 556; 2014(13) SCALE 718; 2015 (2) SLR 615

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Standard Of Proof, State Bank of India (Supervising Staff) Service Rules

Summary: Service - State Bank of India (Supervising Staff) Rules - Misappropriation of money - Dismissal from service - Legality - Respondent cashier in appellant bank was charge-sheeted for misappropriation of money - On the basis of Inquiry Report, disciplinary authority dismissed respondent from service - Respondent's department appeal was also dismissed - Respondent filed writ petition - Single Judge set aside dismissal order and directed appellant employer to reinstate respondent - Appellant's writ appeal before DB was dismissed thereafter - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, Single Judge committed an error in approaching the issue by asking whether the findings have been arrived on acceptable evidence or not and coming to the conclusion that there was no acceptable evidence, and that in any case the evidence was not sufficient. Whether the evidence was acceptable or not, was a wrong question, unless it raised a question of admissibility. The Single Judge was not entitled to go into the question of the adequacy of evidence and come to the conclusion that the evidence was not sufficient to hold the respondent guilty. On the question of shortage of money, DB merely upheld the findings of the Single Judge that there was no clinching evidence in support of the charges. The DB approved the findings of the Single Judge that the inquiry report that the shortage of cash occurred, when the respondent was a joint custodian, was based on surmise and conjecture. The DB did not care to advert to the evidence. That evidence rightly relied on by the enquiry officer which established that the shortage did occur. In fact the inquiring officer has given cogent reasons for rendering the findings that the shortage could not have occurred, when two acting cashiers had functioned. Hence, impugned order was set aside. Appellant was directed to pay an ad hoc sum to respondent. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - An administrative authority cannot be put to proof of the facts or conditions on which the validity of its order must depend, unless the opposite party can produce evidence which will shift the burden of proof on the shoulders of the administrative authority.
