Supreme Court of India

02 December 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Ruma Pal, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1034; (2005) 2 SCC 22; AIR 2005 SC 534; 2005 (2) ALD(SC) 79; 2005 (58) ALR 303; 2005 (1) AWC 132; 2005 (4) Bom.C.R. 872; 2005 (2) JhrCR(SC) 166; JT 2004 (10) SC 235; 2005 (1) KLT 26; 2005 (139) PLR 710; 2005 (1) RCR(Civil) 309; 2004(10) SCALE 153; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 599

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, ss. 10, 13 - Petition for divorce by husband - Mental cruelty - Whether there is mental cruelty to grant divorce? - Held, there was not even any discussion as to how evidence led was insufficient to establish mental cruelty - Trial Court analysed evidence in great detail and found that accepted stand of respondent-wife regarding her behaviour and conduct caused mental agony and amounted to mental cruelty - Appellant is entitled to a decree of divorce.
