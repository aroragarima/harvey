Income Tax Officer, Cuttack, and Others v Biju Patnaik

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 907; (1991) 1 SCC 161; AIR 1991 SC 464; 1991 (91) CTR 95; [1991] 188 ITR 247; JT 1990 (4) SC 731; 1990(2) SCALE 1248; [1990] Supp3 S.C.R. 488; [1991] 56 TAXMAN 165

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 147(a), 148 and 166 - Re-opening of assessment - Prima facie satisfaction of ITO as to two conditions in s. 147(a) i e income exigible to tax has escaped assessment and escapement was due to failure to disclose all material facts essential - Held, in an administrative action, though order does not ex facie disclose satisfaction of officer of necessary facts, but if record discloses same, notice or order does not per se become illegal - Appeal allowed.
