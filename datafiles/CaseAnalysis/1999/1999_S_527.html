Satwant Singh Sodhi v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1035; (1999) 3 SCC 487; AIR 1999 SC 2040; 1999 (2) ARBLR 1; 1999 (2) CLT 160; JT 1999 (2) SC 486; 1999 (2) RAJ 1; 1999(2) SCALE 301; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 230; 1999 (3) SLT 264; 1999 (3) Supreme 272

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 14 - Interim award - Finality - Determination of - Agreement was entered into between the appellant and the respondents - Disputes arose in respect of certain claims made by the appellant and the matter was referred to arbitration (respondent No.3) - Appellant sought for an interim award in respect of Item No.1 - Arbitrator in award dt. 26-11-1992 awarded a sum of Rs.7.45 lacs in respect of Item No.1 - Thereafter, arbitrator made another award on 28-1-1994 inclusive of Item No.1 and awarded a sum of Rs.3,75 lacs and interest - Appellant made an application u/s. 14 of the Act for making the awards as the rule of the court - Trial Court made the award as the rule of the court holding that the interim award in regard to Item No.1 should be made the rule of the court and that award having covered Item No.1 should not be taken note of in the award - On appeal, HC held that Trial Court erred in making the interim award the rule of the court which was superseded by the final award - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the question whether interim award is final to the extent it goes or has effect till the final award is delivered will depend upon the form of the award. If the interim award is intended to have effect only so long as the final award is not delivered it will have the force of the interim award and it will cease to have effect after the final award is made. If, on the other hand, the interim award is intended to finally determine the rights of the parties it will have the force of a complete award and will have effect even after the final award is delivered. The terms of the award dt. 26-11-1992 do not indicate that the same is of interim nature. Thus in the instant case, it was not open to the Arbitrator to re-determine the claim and make an award. Therefore, the view taken by the Trial Court that the earlier award made and written though signed was not pronounced but nevertheless had become complete and final, therefore, should be made the rule of the court appears to us to be correct with regard to Item No.1 inasmuch as the claim in relation to Item No.1 could not have been adjudicated by the Arbitrator again and it has been rightly excluded from the second award made by the Arbitrator on 28-1-1994. Therefore, the Trial Court has rightly ordered the award dt. 28-1-1994 to be the rule of the court except for Item No.1 and in respect of which the award dt. 26-11-1992 was ordered to be the rule of the court. Appeal allowed.
