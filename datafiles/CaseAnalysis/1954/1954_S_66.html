Â State of Rajasthan v Rao Manohar Singhji

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Ghulam Hasan, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 155; AIR 1954 SC 297; 1954 SCJ 439; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 996

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Constitution - Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - United State of Rajasthan Jagirdars (Abolition of Powers) Ordinance, 1948, s. 8 A - (A) Whether s. 8 A of Ordinance, 1948 is ultra vires Constitution?- Held, a proper classification must always bear a reasonable and just relation to things in respect of which it is proposed - In absence of any allegation supported by evidence it could not be held that Jagirdars of particular area to which category respondent belonged were differently situated to other Jagirdars - No rational basis for any classification or differentiation had been made out - S. 8A of impugned Ordinance as amended was a clear contravention of respondent's right u/art. 14 of Constitution and must be declared void - Appeal dismissed.
