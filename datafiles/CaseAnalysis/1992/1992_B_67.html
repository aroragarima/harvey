Bhushan Uttam Khare v Dean, B. J. Medical College and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 January 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Fathima Beevi, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 868; (1992) 2 SCC 220; (1992) SCC (L&S) 554; AIR 1992 SC 917; 1992 (20) ATC 223; JT 1992 (1) SC 583; 1992(1) SCALE 191; [1992] 1 S.C.R. 386; 1992 (1) SLR 649; 1992 (1) UJ 734

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Poona University Act, 1974, Poona University Ordinances

Summary: Constitution - Education - Poona University Act, 1974, s. 39 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 136 - Poona University Ordinances, s. 134 - Examination - Application for revaluation - University canceled earlier result and ordered again for revaluation - Writ Petition against order dismissed by High Court - Whether decision of the Executive Council canceling the earlier revaluation and directing a second revaluation was legally correct? - Court held, there had been sufficient material before the Executive Council to proceed in the manner in which it has done and it is not correct to say that the University had acted on non-existent rule for ordering revaluation - Ordinance 146 is comprehensive enough to include revaluation also further action,therefore, HC had not erred in not granting the relief sought for - Petition dismissed.
