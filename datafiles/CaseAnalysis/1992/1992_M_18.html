Collector Of Customs. Calcutta v M. Shashikant And Co.

Supreme Court of India

08 January 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, S. Ranganathan, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1195; (1992) Supp2 SCC 306; AIR 1992 SC 696; 1992 (38) ECC 98; 1992 (38) ECR 481; 1992 (57) E.L.T. 684; JT 1992 (1) SC 129; 1992(1) SCALE 1; [1992] 1 S.C.R. 7; 1992 (1) UJ 700

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947, s. 3 - Import Policy, 1978-79 - Export House Certificate - Additional licence - Earlier Supreme Court order directing that additional licence holders would be entitled to import only those items which were permitted under import policy of 1978-79 and also policy at time or import - Item imported banned under policy at time of import i.e. 1985-88 - Whether import authorised ? - Whether confiscation of goods justified ? - Held, import unauthorised - Confiscation justified - Appeal allowed.
