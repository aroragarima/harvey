Bank of New York Mellon London Branch v Zenith Infotech Limited

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 131; (2017) 5 SCC 1; AIR 2017 SC 1735; [2017] 201 Comp Cas 280; 2017 (2) CompLJ 6; 2017 (1) DRTC 457; JT 2017 (2) SC 524; 2017 (3) MLJ 614; 2017(3) SCALE 98

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Bankruptcy, Winding Up, Jurisdiction, Liquidation, Appellate Authority, Registration, Companies Act, 1956, Appellate, Industrial Company, Secretary, Professional, Reconstruction, Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985, Election, Moratorium, Delegation, Insolvency, Companies (Second Amendment) Act, 2002, Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Repeal Act, 2003, Board for Industrial and Financial Reconstruction Regulations, 1987, Companies Act, 2013, Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code, 2016

Summary: (A) Corporate - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985, ss. 13, 15, 3(e), 3(f), 3(n) - Board for Industrial and Financial Reconstruction Regulations, 1987, regns. 19, 19(4), 8(1), 8(2) - Reference application - Dismissal - Validity - Respondent no. 1/company filed a reference before Board u/s. 15 of the Act - Registrar refused application on ground that respondent no.1 was not an industrial company within the meaning of ss. 3(e) and 3(f) of the Act - On appeal, Secretary of Board dismissed the same - Further, second appeal before Chairman of Board was also dismissed - Respondent no. 1 filed a petition for winding up of company - HC passed orders for winding up of company which was upheld by DB - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the dismissal of application for Reference by Registrar, Secretary and Chairman of Board was within the jurisdiction of said authorities.

Held, regn. 19(5) of the Regulations requires Registrar or Secretary to make an endorsement of the date of receipt of Reference and thereafter on scrutiny thereof to register the same and place before Chairman for being referred to Bench. When the Regulations framed under the statute vests in Registrar or Secretary of Board power to "scrutinize" an application prior to registration thereof and thereafter to register and place the same before Bench, SC do not see how such power of scrutiny could be understood to be vesting in any of the said authorities the power to adjudicate the question as to whether a company is an industrial company within the meaning of s. 3(e) r/w ss. 3(f) and 3(n) of the Act. The specific stand of respondent no. 1 company on that regard need not detain Court save and except to state that by a detailed description of manufacturing process respondent no. 1 company had sought to contend that it is an industrial company. However, rejection of above stand could have been made only by a process of adjudication which power and jurisdiction clearly and undoubtedly was vested by the Act and the Regulations framed in a Bench of Board and not in authorities like Registrar and Secretary. HC was correct in coming to the conclusion that refusal of registration of reference sought by respondent Company by Registrar, Secretary/Chairman of Board was non-est in law. Appeal disposed of.

(B) Corporate - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985, ss. 13, 15, 3(e), 3(f), 3(n) - Board for Industrial and Financial Reconstruction Regulations, 1987, regns. 19, 19(4), 8(1), 8(2) - Companies Act, 1956, s. 481 - Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code of 2016, s. 252 - Winding up proceedings - Remedies sought - Whether the reference before Board stood foreclosed by the order of winding up of respondent Company and appointment of liquidator.

Held, relying on Rishabh Agro Industries Limited v. P. N. B. Capital Services Limited 2000 Indlaw SC 444 and Real Value Appliances Limited Etc., Engineering Kamgar Sangh v. Canara Bank and Others Etc. Vardhman Spinning and Gen. Mills Limited, 1998 Indlaw SC 726, core principles laid down in the said decisions of Court, namely, that immediately on registration of a reference u/s. 15 of the 1985 Act, enquiry u/s. 16 of the 1985 Act was deemed to have commenced and that winding up proceedings against a company stood terminated only after orders u/s. 481 of the 1956 Act were passed, would have to be noticed to adjudge the correctness of said view of HC. Thus, aforesaid question becomes redundant in view of conclusion that reference sought by respondent Company should be deemed to have been pending on the date of commencement of the Code, particularly, s. 252 thereof. It would be open to respondent Company to seek its remedies under the provisions of s. 252 of the Code r/w ss. 13, 14, 20 and 25 of the 1985 Act. Appeal disposed of.
