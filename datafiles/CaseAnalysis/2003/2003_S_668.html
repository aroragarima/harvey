Gurdev Singh and Another v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 577; (2003) 7 SCC 258; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1616; AIR 2003 SC 4187; 2003 (3) CCR 109; 2003 (3) Crimes 242; 2003 (6) CRJ 388; 2003 CRLJ 3764; JT 2003 (6) SC 386; 2003(6) SCALE 25; 2003 (4) SLT 802; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 80; 2003 (5) Supreme 320; 2003 (2) UC 1422

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 149, 302 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - ss. 235(2), 309(2), 354(3) - Murder of 15 Persons - Proved beyond reasonable doubt - Held, the entire incident is extremely revolting and shock the collective conscience of the community. The acts committed by the appellants are so gruesome, merciless and brutal that the aggravating circumstances far outweigh the mitigating circumstances - Death sentence confirmed.
