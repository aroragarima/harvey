Manujendra Dutt v Purendu Prosad Roy Chowdhury & Others

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 180; AIR 1967 SC 1419; 1967 (3) DLT 665; 1967 (1) MLJ(SC) 61; 1967 (1) SCJ 503; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 475

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bengal General Clauses Act, 1899

Summary: Land & Property - Rent Control - Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, art.227 - Bengal General Clauses Act, 1899, ss.3, 3(v), 3(vi), 5, 8, 9, 28 and 29 - Calcutta Thika Tenancy Act, 1949, s.3 - (A) Whether Controller had jurisdiction under Calcutta Thika Tenancy Act, 1949 after deletion of s.29 by Amendment Act VI of 1953 from it in respect of proceedings pending before him on that date? - Held, Controller continues to have jurisdiction to try proceeding pending before him at date when Act 6 of 1953 came into force in spite of deletion of s.29 by s.8 of Act 6 of 1953 - (B) Whether Thika Tenant had right as defined by Act to a notice provided under Deed of Lease? - Further held, non obstante provision in s.3 does not deprive tenant of his right to have notice to quit as provided in contract of lease - Effect of non obstante clause is that even where landlord has duly terminated contractual tenancy, he would be entitled to evict tenant provided his claim falls under any one or more of grounds in s.3 - Appeal allowed.
