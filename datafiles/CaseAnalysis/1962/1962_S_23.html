Â Madan Gopal Rungta v Secretary To The Government Oforissa

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 152; AIR 1962 SC 1513; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 906; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 906

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Mines Concession Rules 1949

Summary: Constitution - Mines & Minerals - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - Mines Concession Rules 1949, rr. 32, 57, 59 and 60 - Whether High Court could issue writs beyond its territorial jurisdiction with respect to art. 226 of Constitution? - Held, there is two-fold limitation on power of HC to grant a writ u/art. 226 - These limitation are firstly that power is to be exercised throughout territories in relation to which HC exercise jurisdiction, that is to say, writs issued by HC cannot run beyond territories subject to its jurisdiction - Secondly, that person or authority to whom HC is empowered to issue such writs must be within those territories, which clearly implies that they must be amenable to its jurisdiction either by residence or location within those territories - Further held, HC was right in holding that it had no jurisdiction to issue a writ u/art. 226 in present case as final order in this case was that of Central Govt. which was not situated within territories over which HC has jurisdiction - Appeal dismissed.
