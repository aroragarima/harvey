State of Maharashtra v P. K. Pathak

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 3; (1980) 2 SCC 259; (1980) SCC (Cr) 428; AIR 1980 SC 1224; 1980 CRLJ 923; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1628; 1980 UJ 320

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Excise - Practice & Procedure - Customs Act, 1962 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947 - Smuggled goods - Conviction - Sustainability - Two respondents and others were tried by Trial Court under various sections of the Act of 1962, as also S. 5 of the Act of 1947 r/w s. 120B of IPC - Trial Court found that search witnesses, PWs. 1, 2, 4 and 9, fully corroborated statements made by respondents in their confessions - Apart from recovery of smuggled articles at instance of respondents, circumstances under which respondent No. 1/accused no. 1was arrested, at instance of accused no. 5, from bus stand which were proved by PW. 9, was also relied upon by Magistrate in holding that case against respondents stood proved and convicted respondents u/s. 135(a) and (b) of the Act of 1962 r/w s. 120B IPC and also u/s. 5 of the Act, 1947 - Aggrieved by this appellant filed appeal before HC - HC disagreed with view of Magistrate and found that as no independent witness or witnesses from locality were taken by Customs authorities to witness search, no reliance could be placed on searches or recovery of smuggled articles and that thus since confessions were not corroborated, conviction of respondents was bad - HC also rejected evidence of PW. 9 independent search witness, on ground - (1) that he was not witness of locality, and (2) that he gave his assent to accompany the police and Customs Officers to witness the various recoveries wherever he was taken by police - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of HC was sustainable - Held, while respondent No. 2/accused no. 5got articles recovered from seashore, respondent No. 1/accused no. 1 got articles recovered from island which had to be reached through mechanized vessel, journey taking hour and half - Articles were found concealed in bushes growing on island - In these circumstances, it would neither be practical nor reasonable to expect any person of locality to witness search - Hence, fact that PW. 9 was approached by police and Customs authorities and accompanied them to witness search, made instance of respondents, by itself would not show that he was in any way interested or unreliable - Therefore, SC satisfied that judgment of HC acquitting respondents was vitiated by clear error of law and that of Magistrate convicting and sentencing respondents, as indicated above was restored - Appeal allowed.
