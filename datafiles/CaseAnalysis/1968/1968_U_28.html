Suresh Koshy George v University of Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 July 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 434; AIR 1969 SC 198; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 317

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: (A)Education - Disciplinary action - Guilty of malpractice - Breach of statutory rule -

- Appellant was student in first year Degree Course of five year integrated course of engineering - Vice Chancellor of University concluded that appellant was guilty of malpractice during examination and debarred appellant from appearing in any examination - Order of Vice Chancellor was impugned before HC in petition u/art. 226 of Constitution, where, Single Judge of HC allowed petition and set aside that order, however, his decision was reversed in appeal by DB of HC - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether DB of HC rightly negatived contention that by appointing respondent no.2 IO, Vice Chancellor had either breached statutory rule or contravened any principle of natural justice - Held, principal of college in which appellant appeared for his examination was not requested to appoint as IO, who was directly appointed by Vice Chancellor himself - Principal was father of appellants, Vice Chancellor, therefore, thought it proper that appoint some independent person as IO - However, rule under which Vice Chancellor was required to request Principal of concerned college to appoint IO was not statutory rule - Hence, Vice Chancellor could not be said to have contravened any law in appointing IO - Therefore, it could not be said that steps taken by Vice Chancellor were in contravention of principles of natural justice - Hence, DB of HC rightly negatived contention that by appointing respondent no.2 as IO, Vice Chancellor had breached statutory rule or contravened principle of natural justice - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Education - Copy of report - Availability of - Inquiry - Whether copy of report on which show cause notice was issued could be made available to person proceeded against or that another inquiry should be held thereafter - Held, appellant had been informed of charge against him long before inquiry began - Therefore, inquiry was held after due notice and in presence of appellant and he was allowed to cross-examine witnesses and appellant was permitted to adduce evidence in rebuttal of charge - Further, no rule either statutory or otherwise was brought to notice which required Vice Chancellor to make available to appellant copy of report submitted by IO - It was not case of appellant that he asked for copy of that report and that was denied to him - Thus, show cause notice was provided by law from that it did not follow that copy of report on which show cause notice was issued should be made available to person proceeded against or that another inquiry should be held thereafter - Appeal dismissed.
