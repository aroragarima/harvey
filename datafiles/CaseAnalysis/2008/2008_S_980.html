Subha B. Nair and Others v State of Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 912; (2008) 7 SCC 210; AIR 2008 SC 2760; 2008 (3) ESC 464; 2008 (118) FLR 318; JT 2008 (8) SC 585; 2008 (2) KLT 929; 2008 (3) LLN 629; 2008(9) SCALE 16; [2008] 9 S.C.R. 1058; 2009 (2) SLR 457; 2008 (3) UPLBEC 2668

Case Digest

Subject: Service; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Kerala Cooperative Societies Rules, 1969, Central Banks, Kerala Cooperative Societies (Amendment) Rules, 2004

Summary: Kerala Cooperative Societies Rules, 1969, r. 182 - Whether bank was disabled from appointing any more persons than what had been sanctioned by the order of Registrar? - Held, a decision on the part of an employer whether to fill up the existing vacancies or not is within its domain - Recruitment to a post having regard to the provisions contained in art. 320 of the Constitution must be made by the Committee in terms of the Statutory Rules - It may not be entirely correct that the Registrar will have no say in the matter - Financial heath of a bank is a relevant factor - Only because the Bank has sent a requisition or had been making recruitments in other categories of staff by itself may not be a ground for issuance of a writ - If the employer takes a policy decision not to fill up any existing vacancy, only because a person's name is found in the select list, the same by itself would be a ground to compel the bank to fill them up - Appeal dismissed.
