Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay City Ii v Shakuntala and Two Others

Supreme Court of India

18 July 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 455; AIR 1966 SC 719; [1961] 43 ITR 352; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 871

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 23A, 34 and 18 (5) - Finance Act, 1955 - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 23A (as it stood prior to it, amendment in 1955) - Hindu undivided family - Shareholder of Company - Assessment of income - Notional and total income - (A) Whether a Hindu undivided family can be registered as a share holder of a company? - Where shares stand in names of different members of family, notional dividend income deemed to be distributed under provisions of s. 23A of Act of 1922 cannot be treated as income of Hindu undivided family, though admittedly it was beneficiary of shares, but should be assessed in hands of share-holders, that is, persons in whose names shares stood registered in books of company - Held, a Hindu undivided family cannot be a registered share-holder of company - (B) Whether notional income has to be included in total income? - Further held, under express terms of section, artificial or notional income has to be included in total income of share-holder for purpose of assessing his total income - (C) Whether an order made u/s. 23A is an assessment? - Further held, It is clear from s. 23A of Income Tax Act, that an order made under it is not in itself an order of assessment; it has to be followed by an assessment on shareholder either u/ss. 23 or 34 - Appeal dismissed.
