Â Mangilal v Suganchand Rathi

Supreme Court of India

24 October 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 311; AIR 1965 SC 101; 1964 JabLJ 75; 1964 MPLJ 124; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 239

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 106 and 114 - Madhya Pradesh Accommodation Control Act, 1955, s. 4 - M P Accommodation Control Act, 1961 - (A) Whether notice dated 11-04-1959 was invalid for purpose of s. 106 of Transfer of Property Act? - Held, (A) Provisions of s. 4 of Accommodation Act are in addition to those of Transfer of Property Act and before a tenant can be evicted by a landlord he must comply both with provisions of s. 106 of Transfer of Property Act and those of s. 4 of Accommodation Act - HC rightly treated this as a composite notice u/s. 4(a) of Accommodation Act and s. 106 of Transfer of Property Act - However, said notice was ineffective as it does not fulfill requirements of s. 106 - (B) Whether tenant was not liable to be evicted because of provisions of s.4(a) of Accommodation Act as there were no arrears on date of suit? - Further held, ground set out in cls. (a) of s. 4 need not be shown by landlord to exist at date of institution of suit, all that is necessary for him to establish is that tenant was in fact in arrears, that he was given one month's notice to pay up arrears and that in spite of this he failed to pay those arrears within one month of service of notice on him - (C) Whether benefit of s. 114 of Transfer of Property Act providing relief against forfeiture for non-payment of rent is taken away by such interpretation of s. 4 of Accommodation Act? - Further held, effect of cls. a of s. 4 is merely to remove bar created by opening words of s. 4 on right which a landlord has u/s. 106 of Transfer of Property Act; it does not convert a periodic tenancy into one of fixed or indefinite duration nor insert therein a clause of re-entry on ground of nonpayment of rent - (D) Whether default made by defendant in paying arrears within 1 month of receipt of notice can be said to have been waived by plaintiffs on acceptance of rent? - Further held, Firstly, this is not a case to which illustration (a) to s. 113 of Transfer of Property Act which provides that acceptance of rent falling due after expiry of a notice to quit amounts to waiver of notice applies; secondly, no right u/s. 106 of Transfer of Property Act had accrued to plaintiffs because of ineffectiveness of notice in so far as termination of tenancy was concerned and, therefore, no question of waiver with respect to that part of notice arises; so far as right accruing u/s. 4 (a) of Accommodation Act is concerned, defendant having been under liability to pay rent even after giving of notice acceptance of rent by plaintiffs would not by itself operate as waiver - Appeal dismissed.
