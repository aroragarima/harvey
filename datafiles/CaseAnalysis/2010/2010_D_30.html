Shyamrao Maroti Korwate v Deepak Kisanrao Tekam

Supreme Court of India

14 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 734; (2010) 10 SCC 314; 2010 (82) ALR 902; 2010 (6) AWC 5464; 2011 (1) Bom.C.R. 164; 2011 (1) JhrCR(SC) 264; JT 2010 (9) SC 482; 2011 (2) MLJ(SC) 875; 2011 (2) MPLJ 141; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 647; 2010 (111) RD 645; 2010(9) SCALE 374; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 473

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Maternal Grandfather

Summary: Family & Personal - Women & Children - Guardians and Wards Act, 1890, ss. 7, 8, 25 - Guardianship of minor - Determination - Respondent married appellant's daughter and a son was born out of the said wedlock - Appellant's daughter died and consequently, appellant filed an application for appointing him as guardian of the minor - Respondent/father, subsequently, filed an application for appointment as guardian - Trial Court allowed appellant's application and appointed appellant/maternal grandfather as a guardian of minor till the minor attains 12 years of age - Trial Court dismissed respondent's application, however, visitation right granted to respondent - Respondent challenged the Trial Court order before the HC - HC reversed the Trial Court order and appointed respondent as guardian of minor - Hence the present appeal - Whether appellant/maternal grandfather entitled to get the guardianship of his deceased daughter's son - Held, father is the guardian of the minor child until he is found unfit to be a guardian of the minor, however, in deciding such question, welfare of the minor child is the paramount consideration and such a question cannot be decided merely on the basis of the rights of the parties under the law - Respondent got married within a year after the death of the mother of minor and also having a son through the second marriage - Further, respondent was working at a distance of 90 kms away from his residence - Minor was all along with the appellant and appellant's family since his birth where the minor was getting good education - Therefore, Trial Court was justified in appointing the appellant as guardian of the minor child till the age of 12 years in view of welfare of the minor as provided in s. 13 of the Act - However, since on completion of 12 years, a fresh decision is to be taken about entrusting the custody of the minor child - Impugned HC order is modified and visitation right granted to respondent - Appeal partly allowed.
