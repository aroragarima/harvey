Krishna Kumar Variar v Share Shoppe

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 482; (2010) 12 SCC 485; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 2031; 2010 CRLJ 3848; 2010 (3) RCR(Criminal) 121; 2010(4) SCALE 650; [2010] 5 S.C.R. 1153

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 415 and 420 - Appellant was an accused u/s. 415/420 of IPC in which summons were issued to him by Court at Delhi - Appellant challenged the summoning order on the ground that it was only the Court at Bombay which has jurisdiction to try and entertain the complaint - Application was rejected - Hence, present appeal - Held, in a case where the accused or any other person raises an objection that the Trial Court has no jurisdiction in the matter, the said person should file an application before the Trial Court making this averment and giving the relevant facts and Trial Court should after hearing both the sides and recording evidence, if necessary, decide the question of jurisdiction before proceeding further with the case - Impugned judgment and order set aside - Appeal allowed.
