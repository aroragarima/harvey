Association of Management of Private colleges and another v All India Council for Technical Education and others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 263; (2013) 8 SCC 271; AIR 2013 SC 2310; 2013 (4) AWC 3934; JT 2013 (6) SC 277; 2013 (5) MLJ 76; 2013 (2) RLW 1677; 2013(6) SCALE 349; [2013] 6 S.C.R. 1054; 2013 (4) SLR 246

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: MCA course, Permission from AICTE (All India Council for Technical Education)

Summary: (A) Education - Administrative - Constitution - University Grants Commission Act, 1956, ss. 25, 26 and 2(f) r/w ss. 12, 12A, 12B, 12(2)(c) - All India Council for Technical Education Act, 1987, ss. 2(g), 2(h) 2(i), r/w s. 10(k) - Constitution of India, 1950, Entry 66 List I, Entry 25 of List III of VII Schedule - MCA course - Permission from AICTE (All India Council for Technical Education) - Requirement - Appellant colleges preferred writ petitions before HC challenging the amended Regulation dt.16-8-2000 mainly on ground that it was ultra vires to 1987 Act as MCA course which were being run by appellant colleges did not fall under definition of technical education as contained in s. 2(g) of the 1987 Act and it was also challenged on ground that since amended Regulation had not placed before Houses of Parliament for approval they could not be enforced - HC dismissed the writ petitions and held that even though the University was not required to take permission from AICTE, its affiliated colleges were required to do so - Further, HC held that that appellant colleges should get its course of MCA ratified by AICTE as per prescribed format which according to appellants was in contravention of settled principles of interpretation of Statutes and also runs contrary to law laid down by Court - Aggrieved, appellants preferred instant appeals - Whether colleges affiliated to a university would come within purview of exclusion of definition of 'Technical Institution' as defined u/s. 2(h) of the 1987 Act - Held, it should be very much necessary to extract definition of 'technical institution', 'university' and 'technical education' in ss. 2(h), 2(i) and 2(g) respectively r/w s. 10(k) of the 1987 Act and also definition of s. 2(f) of the 1956 Act r/w ss. 12, 12A, 12B, 12(2) (c) of the 1956 Act - SC in Bharathidasan University v. All India Council for Technical Education, 2001 Indlaw SC 24, made it clear by making observation that it had examined the scope of enactment as to whether the 1987 Act prevails over the 1956 Act or fact of competent entries fall in Entry 66 List I vis-Ã -vis Entry 25 of List III of the VII Schedule of Constitution - However, it was made clear that the provisions of the 1987 Act and the 1956 Act that role of AICTE vis-Ã -vis universities was only advisory, recommendatory and one of providing guidance and had no authority empowering it to issue or enforce any sanctions by itself - It was rightly pointed out from UGC as directed by SC in these cases on question of affiliated colleges to university, that was very mechanical and it had simply and gratuitously without foundation, added as technical institutions including affiliated colleges without any legal foundation - However, provisions of the 1956 Act had examined by SC with reference to the provisions of 1987 Act in Bharathidasan University's case - Therefore, it had clearly laid down the principle that role of the 1987 Act was only advisory in nature and was confined to submitting report or giving suggestions to UGC for purpose of implementing its suggestions to maintain good standards in technical education in terms of definition u/s. 2(h) of the 1987 Act and to see that there should be uniform education standard throughout country to be maintained which was the laudable object of the 1956 Act for which it was enacted by Parliament - Provisions of the 1987 Act should be implemented through the UGC as the universities and its affiliated colleges were all governed by provisions of the 1987 Act, u/s. 12A of the 1956 Act r/w Rules Regulations that would be framed by UGC in exercise of its power u/ss. 25 and 26 of the 1956 Act - Therefore, Bharathidasan University case on all force be applicable to fact situation of instant appeals and applied the said principle in instant cases - Bharathidasan University v. All India Council for Technical Education, 2001 Indlaw SC 24, relied on - Appeal allowed.

(B) Education - Administrative - All India Council for Technical Education Act, 1987, s. 2(g) - MCA course - Technical education - Scope - Whether MCA course be construed as technical education in terms of definition u/s. 2(g) of the 1987 Act - Held, as per definition of 'technical education' u/s. 2(g) of the 1987 Act and non-production of any material by AICTE to show that MBA course was a technical education, it was held that MBA course was not a technical course within definition of the 1987 Act and in so far as reasons assigned for MCA course being 'technical education', same did not hold for MBA course - Therefore, for reasons assigned while answering points which were framed in so far as MCA course was concerned, approval from AICTE was not required for obtaining permission and running MBA course by appellant colleges - Appeals allowed.

(C) Education - Administrative - All India Council for Technical Education (Amendment) Regulation, 2000, regns. 8(c) and 8(iv) - All India Council for Technical Education Act, 1987, ss. 10(k) and 24 - Amendment of Regulations - Affiliated colleges for MCA courses - Applicability - Whether regns. 8(c) and 8(iv) by way of amendment in year 2000 inserting words 'MBA and MCA' before Architecture and Hotel Management courses was applicable to concerned colleges of appellants - Held, amended regn. Nos. 8(c) and 8(iv) of 2000 were introduced by the AICTE in exercise of its power u/s. 10(k) of the 1987 Act by adding MBA and MCA courses within purview of provisions of AICTE as it was included in Regulation as a technical education - It was case made out by appellant that amended Regulation had not placed before Parliament which was mandatory as per provisions of s. 24 of the 1987 Act, said contention had not disputed by AICTE in instant cases - However, not placing the amended Regulations on floor of Houses of Parliament as required u/s. 24 of the 1987 Act vitiates the amended Regulations in law - Appeals allowed.

(D) Education - Administrative - Bharathidasan University case - Applicability - Whether the law laid down by SC in Bharathidasan University's case, Adhiyaman Education and Research Institute case and Jaya Gokul Educational Trust case was applicable to fact situation of concerned colleges of appellants - Held, law laid down in Bharathidasan University case, for reasons recorded in the instant case in favour of appellants, said decision on all fours be applicable - It was distinguished Adhiyaman Education and Research Institute and Jaya Gokul Educational Trust cases from Bharathidasan University case in reasoning portion - Thus, Adhiyaman Education and Research Institute and Jaya Gokul Educational Trust Cases were not applicable to instant case - Bharathidasan University v All India Council for Technical Education, 2001 Indlaw SC 24, relied on - Jaya Gokul Educational Trust and another v Commissioner and Secretary To Government Higher Education Department, Thiruvanathapuram, Kerala State and Another, 2000 Indlaw SC 513, distinguished - State of Tamil Nadu and Another v Adhiyaman Educational and Research Institute and Others, 1995 Indlaw SC 1631, distinguished - Appeals allowed.
