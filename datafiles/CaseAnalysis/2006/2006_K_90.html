Kuldeep Singh v Government of NCT of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 811; (2006) 5 SCC 702; AIR 2006 SC 2652; JT 2006 (6) SC 199; 2006(6) SCALE 588; 2006 (7) SCJ 342; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 335

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 47 and 136 - Delhi Liquor License Rules, 1976 - Granting L-52 licenses - Liquor - Legitimate exception - (A) Is fundamental right violated by not granting licence under policy? - Held, policy decision valid and not to grant licence is justified - Relied on Director of Public Works and another v. HO PO Sang and Others [1961 Indlaw PC 8], Howrah Municipal Corpn. and Others v. Ganges Rope Co. Ltd. and Others [2003 Indlaw SC 1274], UOI and Others v. Indian Charge Chrome and Another [1999 Indlaw SC 670] and S.B. International Ltd. and Others v. Asst. Director General of Foreign Trade and Others, [1996 Indlaw SC 3416] - Held, whether High Court single Judge and letters patent appeal allowing writ petition granting license to petitioner is valid? - Held, perfectly valid not under policy decision - Appeal dismissed without any costs.
