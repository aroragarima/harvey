Krishna Chander Dutta (Spice) Private Limited v C.T.O.

Supreme Court of India

23 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1960; (1994) Supp2 SCC 265; 1994 (70) E.L.T. 501; JT 1994 (2) SC 97; 1994(1) SCALE 711; [1994] 2 S.C.R. 123; 1994 (93) STC 180

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Bengal Sales Tax Act, 1954, ss. 4 and 25 - Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941 - Notification No. 885-FT, D/- 1-5-1955 - Notification No. 1915-FT D/- 10-5-1963 - Whether sales of turmeric powder and pepper powder obtained from whole turmeric and whole pepper are exigible to sales tax under Act, 1954? - Entry in Notification No. 885-FT speaks of 'black and white pepper, whole, broken, ground or powdered or of any other form or description whatsoever' - It is equally significant that the Notification No. 1915-FT refers to these commodities 'as specified in Notification No. 885-FT' - Hence, it means black and white pepper, whether whole, powdered, broken or in any other form - Turmeric is also prescribed in Notification No. 885 in the same manner as black and white pepper and also because Notification No. 1915 refers to it with reference to the said earlier notification, hence, turmeric and turmeric powder must also be treated as same goods - Appeals allowed.
