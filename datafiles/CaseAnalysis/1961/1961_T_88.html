Â Messrs J. K. Jute Mills Company Limited v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 376; (1973) SCC (Tax) 163; AIR 1961 SC 1534; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 1; 1961 (12) STC 429

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Keywords: Tax Levied, Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax (Validation) Act, 1958

Summary: Sales Tax - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948, ss. 3-A, 8-A, 14 and 23 - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax (Validation) Act, 1958 - Companies Act, 1956 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 213 - (A) Whether impugned notification succeeded in amending s. 3-A of Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act? - Words, 'in form in which they were in force immediately before commencement of this Act', no doubt occur after word, 'notifications' - But then words, 'in form' can have no reference to impugned notification, because it had never changed form, whereas they were quite appropriate to s. 3-A, because it had been amended - (B) Whether Validating Act is ultra vires powers of State legislature under Constitution? - Where transaction is one of sale of goods as known to law, power of State to impose a tax thereon is plenary and unrestricted subject only to any limitation which Constitution might impose and in exercise of that power, it will be competent to legislature to impose a tax on sales which had taken place prior to enactment of legislation - Held, Validation Act is intra vires and impugned notification dated March 31, 1956, stands validated by it - Petition dismissed.
