Mehrawal Khewaji Trust (Regd.),Faridkot & Ors. v State Of Punjab & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

27 April 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 200; (2012) 5 SCC 432; AIR 2012 SC 2721; 2012(4) ALL MR 470; 2012 (4) AWC 3431; 2012 (2) CLT(SC) 163; 2012 (3) CTC 396; JT 2012 (4) SC 405; 2012 (2) KLT 542; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 893; 2012 (117) RD 289; 2012(4) SCALE 628; [2012] 4 S.C.R. 24

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Land acquisition - Enhancement of compensation - Entitlement - Appellants' land was acquired by respondent authority - Award was passed - Appellants sought enhancement of compensation - Reference Court enhanced quantum of compensation - Appellants filed appeal before HC - HC refused to interfere with order of Reference Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellants were entitled to enhancement of compensation - Held, land was of great potential value inasmuch as the same being strategically located at a commercial hub abutting main roads and surrounded by commercial building - Reference Court discarded all sale instances related to area less than one kanal and proceeded to consider other sale instances - Where sale deeds pertaining to different transactions were relied on behalf of Govt., transaction representing highest value should be preferred to rest unless there were strong circumstances justifying a different course - It was not desirable to take an average of various sale deeds placed before authority/court for fixing fair compensation - Hence, appellants had made out a case for enhancement of compensation - Appeal partly allowed.

(B) Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Land acquisition - Interest on solatium - Additional market value - Entitlement - Held, person entitled to compensation awarded was also entitled to get interest on aggregate amount including solatium - Claimants would be entitled for interest on solatium and additional market value if award of Reference Court or that of appellate Court did not specifically refer to question of interest on solatium and additional market value or where claim had not been rejected either expressly or impliedly - Hence, appellants were entitled to interest on solatium and additional market value - Appeal partly allowed.
