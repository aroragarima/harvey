Ugar Ahir and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

06 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, K.C. Das Gupta, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 441; AIR 1965 SC 277; 1965 CRLJ 256; 1965 MLJ 105

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 304 - Appeal against judgment confirming conviction of appellants passed against them by Addl. Sessions Judge u/s. 304 - Having made out a new case, Addl. Sessions Judge acquitted three of accused and convicted appellants u/s. 304 (Part I) - Whether Addl. Sessions Judge erred in reconstructing case for prosecution different from that with which it had come to court and also different from that which prosecution witnesses deposed in court - Maxim falsus in uno, falsus in omnibus (false in one thing, false in every thing) is neither a sound rule of law nor a rule of practice - It is duty of court to scrutinise evidence carefully and, in terms of felicitous metaphor, separate grain from chaff, but, it cannot obviously disbelieve substratum of prosecution case or material parts of evidence and reconstruct a story of its own out of rest - That is what courts have done in this case - In effect, courts disbelieved practically whole version given by witnesses - To reverse metaphor, courts removed grain and accepted chaff and convicted appellants - Conviction of appellants set aside - Appeal allowed.
