Kaushnuma Begum Smt. and Others v New India Assurance Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Act Of God

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 140 - Prohibition Act - Whether a claim can be maintained before Motor Accidents Claims Tribunal on basis of strict liability propounded in Rylands v Fletcher? - Held, like any other common law principle, which is acceptable to our jurisprudence, rule in Rylands v Fletcher can be followed at least until any other new principle which excels former can be evolved, or until legislation provides differently - Hence, court disposed to adopt rule in claims for compensation made in respect of motor accidents - 'No fault liability' envisaged in s. 140 of Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 is distinguishable from rule of strict liability - Compensation on account of accident arising from use of motor vehicles can be claimed under common law even without aid of a statute - Even apart from s. 140 of Motor Vehicles, 1939 a victim in an accident which occurred while using a motor vehicle, is entitled to get compensation from a tribunal unless any one of exceptions would apply - Appeal disposed of.
