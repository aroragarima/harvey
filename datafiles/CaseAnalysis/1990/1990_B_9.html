Union of India and Another v Babubhai Nylchand Mehta

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

N.M. Kasliwal, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 628; (1991) Supp2 SCC 348; AIR 1991 SC 407; 1991 CRLR 330; 1991 CrLR(SC) 330; 1991 (33) ECR 7; 1991 (51) E.L.T. 182; JT 1990 (4) SC 790; 1990(2) SCALE 1269; [1990] Supp3 S.C.R. 559; 1991 (1) UJ 488

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect tax - Central Excises And Salt Act, 1944, ss. 2(f), ss. ch 1, Item 17(2) - Purchase of kraft paper and other required material from open market and combining such material in its factory - Product is Bituminized waterproof packing paper which is different and distinct than original kraft paper having definite characteristic, use and value and discovered that none of its products were liable to payment of duty as same did not fall within expression 'manufactured' under s. 2(f) of Act - Held, coating and lamination and other process applied by company in its factory amounts to manufacture, new products come into being and therefore company is liable to payment of duty under Item 17(2) - Appeal allowed.
