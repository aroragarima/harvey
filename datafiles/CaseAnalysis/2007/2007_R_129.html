Union of India and Another v Ranchod and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1454; (2007) 14 SCC 326; AIR 2008 SC 938; 2008 (71) ALR 750; 2008 (1) AWC 148; 2008 (1) CTC 778; 2008 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 55; 2008 (2) MLJ(SC) 1132; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 23; 2007(13) SCALE 641; 2007 (8) SCJ 951; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 873

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 41, r. 31 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 9 and 18 - Land Acquisition - Notifications issued - Compensation fixed by award of collector found inadequate - Reference to Court u/s. 18 - Challenged the award of Reference Court before High Court by landholders and UOI - HC dismissed the appeal by a common order - Whether such dismissal is valid? - Held, common order of HC having been passed without application of mind - HC did not refer to even an iota of evidence adduced by parties - Large number of landholders filed separate objections u/s. 9 and had separately sought references u/s. 18 - Separately lead evidence in support of their cases - It is not a case where a single case may have large number of parties and evidence adduced is common for all of them - In determination of compensation large number of factors have to be seen - There being total non-compliance of mandatory provisions of O. 41, r.31 - Judgment of HC set aside and remanded - Appeal allowed.
