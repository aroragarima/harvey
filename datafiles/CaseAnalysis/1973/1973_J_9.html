Kailash Rai v Jai Jai Ram and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 77; (1973) 1 SCC 527; AIR 1973 SC 893; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 411

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Possession Of Land, United Provinces Tenancy Act 1939 (ENG), Suit for declaration, Bhumidhari rights

Summary: Land & Property - United Provinces Tenancy Act 1939 - Uttar Pradesh Zamindar Abolition and Land reforms Act 1950 - Possession of land - Suit for declaration - Bhumidhari rights - Entitled to - Appellant's mother R filed suit for declaration that she was entitled to property inherited from her father - As R died during pendency of suit, appellant K, got himself substituted as heir son of R - Trial Court decreed the appellants claim - Respondents defendants filed appeal before HC - HC called for a finding from District Court - District Court submitted its finding to effect that respondents were in exclusive possession of khudkasht and sir plots in dispute since 1947 - HC accepted the finding and allowed respondents appeal and dismissed appellants suit on ground that he was not in cultivatory possession of plots in dispute - Hence, appellants preferred instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, finding was that respondents had not raised plea of ouster - There was no indication in the 1950 Act or the 1939 Act that bhumidari rights were not intended to be conferred on all co-sharers or co-proprietors, who were entitled to properties, though only some of them might be in actual cultivation - One could very well visualise a family consisting of father and 2 sons, both of whom were minors - Normally, cultivation would be done only by father - Did it mean that when father was found to be cultivating land on 30-6-1952, he alone was entitled to bhumidhari rights in land and that his 2 minor sons were not entitled to any such rights - However, normal principal that possession by one co-sharer was possession for all had to be, applied - Further, even when one co-sharer was in possession of land, other co-sharers should be considered to be in constructive possession of land - HC was not justified in passing the impugned order - Impugned order passed by HC was set aside - Order accordingly.
