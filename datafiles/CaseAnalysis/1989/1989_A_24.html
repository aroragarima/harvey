Alembic Chemical Works Co. Limited v Commissioner Of Income Tax, Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 559; (1989) 3 SCC 329; (1989) SCC (Tax) 409; AIR 1989 SC 1913; 1989 (2) CompLJ 195; 1989 (77) CTR 1; [1989] 177 ITR 377; JT 1989 (2) SC 122; 1989(1) SCALE 885; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 302; [1989] 43 TAXMAN 312

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Mines and Minerals - Coking Coal Mines (Nationalisation) Act, 1972, s. 1 - Coal Mines Act , 1973, ss. 18(5), 20, 24A - Nationalisation of coal mines - The claims raised by creditors of owner of coal mines - Before the Commissioner in accordance with the provisions of the Coking Coal Mines Act, s.1 or Coal Mines Act, ss. 18(5), 20, 24A - Owners try to gain unfair interest by delaying payment - Whether the amount of interest which accrues under the Act is to be paid in its entirely to the owner or can be disbursed to the creditors of the owner -Held, interest can be utilised by Commissioner for satisfying claims - Appeal allowed.
