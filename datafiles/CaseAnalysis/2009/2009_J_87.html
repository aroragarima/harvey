Punjab State Electricity Board And Others v Jit Singh

Supreme Court of India

23 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1403; (2009) 13 SCC 118; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1076; 2009 (3) AWC 2846; 2009 (1) CLR 1037; 2009 (121) FLR 589; JT 2009 (7) SC 135; 2009(5) SCALE 25; 2009 (5) SLR 508

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Qualification And Experience

Summary: Service - Administrative - Ad hoc service - Eligibility for Pension - Retirement benefits - Entitlement - Respondent was appointed as security guard on ad hoc basis and his services was regularized - Respondent was retired on attaining age of superannuation on 31-7-2000 and received all pensionary benefits - Appellant/ Punjab Electricity Board had issued circular dt.23-1-2001 gave certain benefit of 'ad hoc service' to Board employees stating that period of ad hoc service would be counted towards retirement benefit on regularization, provided employees fulfill certain conditions and clarification was also issued by Board, by Memo No. 41614/659 dt. 14-2-2001, clarifying circular dt. 23-1-2001 - Respondent to avail said circular benefits made request to appellant/Board to count his ad hoc service in Board before regularization of his service for purpose of calculating pensionary benefits -Thus, said claim was rejected by appellant/Board vide its order dt. 5-10-2004 - Aggrieved, respondent filed writ petition before HC which granted relief to respondent - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in relying on decision of Kesar Chand, Naroli vs State of Punjab and Others 1988 Indlaw PNH 198 to annul order passed by appellant/Board dt. 5-10-2004 was justifiable - Held, respondent was employee of appellant/Punjab State Electricity Board and not employee of State Govt. and was not claiming any benefit under Punjab Civil Services Rules, but claiming benefit in view of circulars issued by appellant/Board - Respondent had not even questioned virus of circular issued by appellant/Board, wherein cutoff date was prescribed by limiting to those ad hoc employees who were still in service and whose services had been regularized etc., - Thus, it was suffice to state, that, order was non-speaking order in sense, it did not contain any reasons much less cogent reason so fair play requires recording precise and cogent reasons when order affects right of citizen - In impugned order, Court did not see any reason in order passed by authorities of appellant/Board - Hence, Order of HC was set aside and remitted matter to authorities of appellant/ Board to reconsider claim of respondent in accordance with law - Appeal disposed of.
