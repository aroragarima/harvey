Bonkya Alias Bharatshivaji Mane And Ors v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

27 September 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1282; (1995) 6 SCC 447; (1995) SCC (Cr) 1113; AIR 1996 SC 257; AIR 1995 SCW 4029; 1996 (1) CCR 27; 1995 (4) Crimes 129; 1996 CRLJ 414; JT 1995 (7) SC 194; 1995(5) SCALE 556; 1995 (3) SCJ 478; [1995] Supp4 S.C.R. 89

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987 - TADA, 1987, s. 3 - Accused charged for intention to strike terror u/s. 3 as well as for murder etc. - Evidence of eye-witnesses connecting accused with incident - Fact that deceased and injured witnesses belong to particular community is not sufficient to draw inference that attack by accused on them was intended to strike terror for particular section of society - Held, accused was not liable to be convicted for offence u/s. 3 but would be convicted for murder and offences u/ss. 307, 324 and 149 of Indian Penal Code, 1860.
