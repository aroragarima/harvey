Kishore Chandra Panigrahi v State of Orissa and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3710; (1996) 11 SCC 234; 1996 (9) AD(SC) 121; JT 1996 (9) SC 669; 1996(7) SCALE 958; 1997 (1) SLR 181; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 116

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Orissa Ministerial Services (Method of Recruitment and Conditions of Service of Clerks, Assts. in District Office and Office of Heads of Department) Rules, 1963 - Orissa Ministerial Services (Regularisation of Recruitment and Conditions of Service of Irregular Recruits in District Offices and Offices Subordinate thereto) Rules, 1986 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - Reversion - Legality - (A) Whether promotion of appellant to post of junior clerk can be held to be valid promotion conferring any right on appellant to continue post? - Temporary promotion of an employee to a higher post contrary to provisions of Recruitment Rules does not confer any right on employee against said promoted post and, therefore, reversion to substantive rank cannot be held to be penal in nature - Held, promotion of appellant to post of junior clerk in Class III was per se illegal being contrary to Statutory Recruitment Rules and did not confer any right on appellant who holds said post - Therefore, reversion is legal - (B) Whether order of reversion to substantive post of peon in Class IV can be held to be penal? - Further held, appointment being contrary to Statutory Rules - Governor in exercise of power u/art. 309 of Constitution came forward with Regularisation Rules - Regularisation Rules do not bring within their sweep case of promotion of an employee in Class IV to that of a clerk in Class III - Reversion valid - Appeal dismissed.
