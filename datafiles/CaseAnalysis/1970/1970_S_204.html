State of Punjab v Hiralal and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 284; (1970) 3 SCC 567; AIR 1971 SC 1777; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 267; 1971 (2) SLR 98

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16 - Promotion - Respondents No. 1 and 3 working in Forest Department of Govt. as Head Asst's. - Respondent No. 1 was senior to Respondent No. 3, whereas Respondent No. 3 belonged to a Scheduled Caste - Temporary promotion of Respondent No. 3 ignoring claim of Respondent No. 1 as per Govt. order - Respondent No. 1 moved High Court and promotion of Respondent No. 3 was quashed - HC held, reservation made for SC, STs and Backward Classes is not impermissible under Constitution in view of art. 16(4) as interpreted by Apex Court - Reservation for members of SC, STs and Backward Classes in promotion cases? - He who assails reservation under that article must satisfactorily establish that there has been a violation of art. 16(1) - Held, no material before Apex Court to conclude that impugned order is violative of art. 16(1) - Reservation of appointments u/art. 16(4) cannot be struck down on hypothetical grounds or on imaginary possibilities - Respondent No. 1 who was petitioner before, HC is not represented before Apex Court - Order of HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
