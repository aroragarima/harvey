Ramdev Food Products Private Limited v Arvindbhai Rambhai Patel and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 786; (2006) 8 SCC 726; AIR 2006 SC 3304; 2006 (6) ALD(SC) 36; 2007(1) ALL MR 402; 2007 (1) G.L.R. 594; JT 2006 (8) SC 393; 2006 (33) PTC 281; 2006(8) SCALE 631; [2006] Supp5 S.C.R. 521

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Keywords: Standards Of Weights And Measures Act, 1976, Infringement Of Trade Mark

Summary: Trade and Merchandise Marks Act, 1958 - Appeal to restrain respondents from using trade mark including trade name 'Ramdev Masala' on their products - Appellant - manufacturing company owning registered trade mark 'Ramdev' contended that respondents had business in trade name 'Swad' but had printed name of manufacturer 'Ramdev Masala' in such prominent manner that it created impression that the same was product of appellant Company - Held, use by any person other than person in whose name trade mark is registered, he will have statutory remedy in terms of s. 21 of the 1958 Act, ordinarily, therefore, two people are not entitled to same trade mark, unless there exists express licence in that behalf - If in absence of any express licence or agreement to use its label respondents use self-same trade mark, the same would not only lead to confusion but may also cause deception - What is registered is logo wherein words 'Ramdev' and 'Masala' are prominent; person may be held to be permitted to carry on business in spices as contradistinguished from permission to carry on manufacturing goods which are similar to that of appellant, but in terms of statutory provisions, respondents were not legally permitted to sell its products in packages or labels which would be deceptively similar to that of registered owner of trade mark - It is difficult to accept that each part of logo was required to be separately registered - When prima facie case is made out and balance of convenience is in favour of appellant, it may not be necessary to show more than loss of goodwill and reputation to fulfil condition of irreparable injury - Appeal allowed.
