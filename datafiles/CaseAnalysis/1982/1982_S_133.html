Maqsoodan and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

Baharul Islam, D.A. Desai, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 152; (1983) SCC (Cr) 176; (1983) 1 SCC 218; AIR 1983 SC 126; 1982 ALJ 1524; 1983 CRLJ 218; 1982(2) SCALE 1351; [1983] 2 S.C.R. 45; 1983 UJ 175

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302/34 and 307/34 - Murder - Conviction of - Dying declaration - Reliability of - Held, conviction of four appellants unsustainable in law - The statement written or verbal, of relevant facts made by a person who is dead, is called a dying declaration - It is relevant u/s. 32 of Evidence Act, when statement is made by person as to cause of his death, or as to any of circumstances of transaction which resulted in his death, in case, in which that person's death comes into question - When a person who has made a Statement, may be in expectation of death, is not dead, it is not a dying declaration and is not admissible u/s. 32 of Evidence Act - Common intention is a question of fact, it is subjective, but it can be inferred from facts and circumstances - HC therefore rightly held that appellants caused injuries with common intention, and was justified in convicting appellants u/ss. 302/34 of IPC, 1860 - Appeal dismissed.
