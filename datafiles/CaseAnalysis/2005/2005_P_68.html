(1) State of Himachal Pradesh; (2) State of v Pawan Kumar, Bhanwar Lal

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, R.C. Lahoti, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 285; (2005) 4 SCC 350; (2005) SCC (Cr) 943; AIR 2005 SC 2265; 2006 (1) CalLT(SC) 1; 2005 CRLJ 2208; JT 2005 (4) SC 373; 2005 (3) KLT 195; 2005 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 617; 2005 (2) RCR(Criminal) 621; 2005(4) SCALE 1; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 417; 2005 (2) ShimLC 107

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug

Keywords: Finance Act, 1972

Summary: Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985 - Whether safeguards provided by s. 50 regarding search of any 'person' would also supply to any bag, briefcase or any article or container etc. which is being carried by him? - Held, s. 50 can have no application on facts and circumstances of present cases as opium was allegedly recovered from bag, which was being carried by accused - High Court did not examine testimony of witnesses and other evidence on merits - Matter remitted back to High Court for a fresh hearing of appeal.
