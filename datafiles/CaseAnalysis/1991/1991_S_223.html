Scheduled Caste and Weaker Section Welfare Association (Regd.) and Another v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 April 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Fathima Beevi, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 493; (1991) 2 SCC 604; AIR 1991 SC 1117; AIR 1991 SCW 1010; JT 1993 (2) SC 184; 1991 (1) RCJ 520; 1991 (1) RCR(Rent) 690; 1991(1) SCALE 581; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 974; 1991 (1) UJ 628

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Socio-Economic - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 136 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 21 - Karnataka General Clauses Act 1897, s. 10 - Karnataka Slum Areas (Improvement and Clearance) Act, 1973, ss. 3 and 11 - Slum clearance - Implementation of - Alteration of the notification - Denial of opportunity to the affected party - Whether Govt. can rescind a notification under Karnataka Slum Areas (Improvement and Clearance) Act, 1973 without hearing appellants? - Held, it has been brought to the notice that about 100 persons had been living in areas under conditions which require implementation of scheme under Act for their redressal and once steps have been taken in that direction any variation that could affect occupants in areas was required to be made only after giving them an opportunity of being heard - When any alteration is sought to be made in original scheme, it becomes incumbent upon authorities to give an opportunity to persons who had been affected by earlier order and required to adopt a certain course of action - In this view of matter it is to be held that when a notification is made rescinding earlier notifications without hearing affected parties, it is clear violation of principle of natural justice - Appeal allowed.
