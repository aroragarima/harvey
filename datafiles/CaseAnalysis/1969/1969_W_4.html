Workmen In Buckingham and Carnatic Mills, Madras v Buckingham and Carnatic Mills, Madras

Supreme Court of India

31 July 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 11; 1969 (19) FLR 253; 1970 (1) LLJ 26

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946 - Mill-manager furnished chargesheet to workman for misconduct under standing order 13(d) - Senior labour officer authorized by mill-manager to record evidence and collect necessary materials - Whether mill-manager has acted legally in delegating to senior labour officer, in case, task of collecting necessary evidence? - Standing orders which have been certified under Act become part of statutory terms and conditions of service between industrial employer and his employees and that they will govern relations between parties - Held, no standing order prohibiting mill-manager from so authorizing senior labour officer enquiry proceedings which is conducted in a fair and impartial manner cannot said to be illegal unless some gross mistake has been done - Enquiry proceedings cannot be held to have contravened provisions of standing order 14 - No violation of principles of natural justice, nor is this a case of an enquiry being conducted by one officer and finding being recorded by another - Appeal dismissed.
