Ishwar Singh Bindra and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, Mohammad Hidayatullah, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 90; AIR 1968 SC 1450; 1969 CRLJ 19; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 219

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Health & Drug - Drugs & Medicine - Drugs Act, 1940, s. 3 - Drugs (Amendment) Act, 1962 - Drugs and Cosmetics (Amendment) Act, 1964, s. 16 - Complainant alleged that medicine was manufactured without license - Appellants were manager and partners of concern engaged in manufacture a unani medicinal preparation - Whether definition of drug u/s. 3 of Drugs Act, 1940 include unani preparation? - Held, definition of drug is widest in its ambit - Only exception is medicines and substances used or prepared for use in Ayurvedic or Unani systems - Matter remitted back to Trial Court for decision as to decide whether medicines were exclusively or prepared for use in Ayurvedic or Unani systems - Individual ingredient or component of preparation in question will not be determining factor - Appeal dismissed.
