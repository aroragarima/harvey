Ramilaben Hasmukhbhai Khristi and others v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

14 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1614; (2002) 7 SCC 56; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1575; AIR 2002 SC 2996; 2002 (3) CCR 159; 2002 (3) Crimes 206; 2002 (6) CRJ 248; 2002 CRLJ 3917; 2003 (1) G.L.R. 31; 2002 (3) JCC 1698; 2002 (3) JhrCR(SC) 148; JT 2002 (6) SC 54; 2002 (3) RCR(Criminal) 786; 2002(5) SCALE 631; 2002 (4) SLT 736; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 530; 2002 (5) Supreme 466; 2002 (2) UC 610

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302/120 B - Dying declaration - Appreciation of evidence - Whether dying declaration given by deceased can serve as a valid basis for conviction of accused? - Held, Court cannot be too technical and in substance if feels convinced about trustworthiness of statement which may inspire confidence, such a dying declaration can be acted upon, without any corroboration - No certificate of any doctor about mental fitness of Pragnesh Kumar to make such a statement appended in dying declaration - Repetition on part of injured indicating that he was conscious and declaring truth - Highly doubtful that he could give such lengthy statements with language used - None of dying declarations contains any certificate by doctor about mental fitness of deceased to make a statement although doctor was always available - In two dying declarations only indicated that he was conscious - No amount of subsequent statement of doctor can supplement endorsement of Certificate while recording dying declaration - First two statements recorded in quick succession without waiting for Magistrate to arrive even though steps had been taken for recording of dying declaration by Magistrate who actually got message at about 10:30 a.m. and had contacted doctor at hospital at 11:05 a.m. - Deviations from statement to statement consistency is conspicuously missing - First dying declaration does not say about Pushpaben sprinkling kerosene oil - Eight or nine persons living in lane are said to have surrounded him but who sprinkled oil and who lighted match stick is not stated - May not be possible to base conviction on sole basis of dying declarations - No eye-witness to incident - In two dying declarations he had stated about being beaten by accused persons but no such injuries are found in any report - Not a case in which conviction can be recorded only on basis of dying declarations which fail to inspire confidence - No other corroboration coming forth from any corner - In result prosecution story as put forward does not inspire confidence on basis of material placed on record - Convictions set aside - Appeals allowed.
