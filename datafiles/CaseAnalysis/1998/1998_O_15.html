Mallawwa etc. v Oriental Insurance Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 November 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, B.N. Kirpal, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1321; (1999) SCC (Cr) 58; (1999) 1 SCC 403; 1999 (1) ACC 112; 1999 ACJ 1; AIR 1999 SC 589; 1999 (1) AWC 478; 1999 (2) Bom.C.R. 515; [1999] 95 Comp Cas 629; JT 1998 (8) SC 217; 1999 (1) MLJ(SC) 87; [1998] Supp3 S.C.R. 152

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Contractual Liability

Summary: Insurance - Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 95 and 147 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 140 - Interpretation of s. 95 of Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Question arises in context of death of owner of goods being carried in a goods vehicle, and question is whether insurer of goods vehicle is liable to pay compensation awarded to his legal heirs - Accidents had taken place between 1971 and 1985 - S. 95 was amended by Act 56 of 1969 cl. (b) was substituted by a new clause - For purposes of s. 95, ordinarily a vehicle could have been regarded as a vehicle in which passengers were carried if vehicle was of that class - For purpose of construing a provision like proviso (ii) to s. 95(1)(b), correct test to determine whether a passenger was carried for hire or reward, would be whether there has been a systematic carrying of passengers - Only if vehicle is so used then that vehicle can be said to be a vehicle in which passengers are carried for hire or reward - To find out whether an insurer would be liable to indemnify an owner of a goods vehicle in a case of present nature, mere fact that passenger was carried for hire or reward would not be enough; it shall have to be found out as to whether he was owner of goods, or an employee of such an owner, and then whether there were more than six persons in all in goods vehicle and whether goods vehicle was being habitually used to carry passengers - As u/s. 95 risk to a passenger in a vehicle who is not carded for hire or reward is not required to be insured plea of counsel for insurance company will have to be accepted and insurance Co. held not liable under requirements of Motor Vehicles Act - Held, 1939 Act is now replaced by 1988 Act - S. 147 which corresponds to old S. 95 has been substantially altered by legislature - Therefore, above interpretation of s. 95 of 1939 Act will govern cases which have arisen under 1939 Act - Order accordingly.
