Commissioner of Income Tax, Assam v Himangshu Sekhar Chakravarty

Supreme Court of India

26 September 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 276; (1972) 4 SCC 576; AIR 1975 SC 15; [1974] 94 ITR 318

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 34(3), 66(2) - Procurement of paddy and rice - Malpractices in procurement - Illegal profits - Assessment - Agreement was entered into by firm with Govt. under which they were appointed as agent for procuring paddy and rice and supplying it to Govt. - Commission was appointed to enquire into malpractices in procurement and supply of paddy and rice, where the Commissioner calculated the total illegal profits earned by the procurers, but the HC rejected the Commissioner's contention - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether the assessee had made profits other than those according to its accounts in the procurement and supply of paddy and rice during the relevant period - Held, that the profiteering in the procurement and supply of paddy and rice could not be attributed to the assessee - Ample evidence on record showed that it was not assessee which made profits, but it was partners individually - Although illegal profits had been earned in procurement and supply of paddy and rice to Govt., assessee's share was nil - Appeals dismissed.
