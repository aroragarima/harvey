Shyam Narain Pandey v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

22 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 470; (2014) 8 SCC 909; 2015 CRLJ 250; JT 2014 (8) SC 541; 2014 (3) RCR(Criminal) 848; 2014(8) SCALE 740; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 923

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 389 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 144, 147, 148 and 302 - Stay of conviction - Appellant was convicted u/ss. 147, 148, 302/144 of IPC r/w s. 120B of IPC with life imprisonment and fine - Appellant was granted bail by HC - Thereafter, the appellant filed an application for staying the judgment of conviction which was dismissed by the impugned order - Hence, instant appeal - Whether judgment of conviction to be stayed -

Held, 'convict' means declared to be guilty of criminal offence by the verdict of court of law; that declaration was made after the court found him guilty of the charges which have been proved against him - Thus, in effect, if one pray for stay of conviction, he was asking for stay of operation of the effects of the declaration of being guilty - In the instant case, contention that the appellant would be deprived of his source of livelihood if the conviction was not stayed could not be appreciated - HC discussed in detail the background of the appellant, the nature of the crime, manner in which it was committed, etc. and rightly held that it was not a very rare and exceptional case for staying the conviction - Appeal dismissed.
