P. Suresh Kumar v R. Shankar

Supreme Court of India

08 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 252; (2007) 2 SCC (Cr) 696; (2007) 4 SCC 752; AIR 2007 SC 1774; 2007 (2) BC 389; 2007 (2) CalLT(SC) 67; 2007 (4) CompLJ 19; 2007 CRLJ 2951; 2007 (2) CTC 670; 2007 (1) JCC (NI) 106; JT 2007 (4) SC 343; 2007 (146) PLR 559; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 358; 2007 (2) RCR(Criminal) 166; 2007(4) SCALE 143; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 649; 2007 (4) UC 215

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Power Of The Court, Joint Account, Post Dated Cheque, Stop Payment, Joint Accounts

Summary: Negotiable Instruments (Amendment & Misc. Provisions) Act, 2002; Code of Criminal Procedure, s. 377 - Imposition of fine and/ or compensation - Whether in the peculiar facts and circumstances of this case, can delve deep into the matter so as to find out the culpability of the respondent herein and pass a judgment of acquittal in his favour - Held, there cannot be any doubt whatsoever that had the respondent been able to show that the cheque had been issued not in discharge of a debt but by way of a security pending determination of his liability by an auditor, the matter would have been different - Respondent has not been charged with any fraudulent action - Appellant furthermore had not preferred any appeal against the judgment of the learned Trial Judge for enhancement of the sentence - Respondent is directed to pay a compensation of Rs.7, 00, 000/- in stead and place of a fine of Rs.5, 000/- - Appeal disposed of.
