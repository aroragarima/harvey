Abrar v The State Of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, P. Sathasivam, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1082; (2011) 2 SCC 750; 2011 (1) ACR 1086; 2011 (1) ADJ 664; 2011 (1) AllCriC 245; 2011 (1) JhrCR(SC) 217; JT 2011 (3) SC 567; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 104; 2010(13) SCALE 380

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 r/w. s. 34 and s. 307- Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 161 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - The trial court held that as there were several discrepancies in the dying declarations of the deceased in the form of the FIR, they could not be relied upon and accordingly taking the murder as a blind one, acquitted the appellant - On appeal, HC reversed the judgment of the trial court and held that the so called discrepancies were insignificant that they could occur in any statement recorded in Court and the discrepancy with regard to the presence or otherwise of a light which figured in one statement and did not figure in the other was of little or no consequence in the facts - Hence, present appeal - Whether trial court rightly convicted appellant u/s. 302 r/w. s. 34 of IPC? - Held, it is true that there are some discrepancies in the dying declarations with regard to the presence or otherwise of a light or a torch - However, these are so insignificant that they call for no discussion - Moreover, it is also clear from the evidence that the injured had been in great pain while making the statements and if there were minor discrepancies inter-se the three dying declarations, they were to be accepted as something normal - The trial court was thus clearly wrong in rendering a judgment of acquittal solely on this specious ground - The dying declaration had recorded by the Tahsildar after the Doctor had certified the victim as fit to make a statement - The doctor also appeared in the witness box to support the statement of the Tahsildar - Therefore, no fault whatsoever could be found in the dying declarations - Impugned order of the HC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
