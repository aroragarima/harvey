State of Maharashtra through CBI Anti Corruption Branch, Mumbai v Balakrishna Dattatrya Kumbhar

Supreme Court of India

15 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 361; (2012) 12 SCC 384; 2012 (6) CTC 95; JT 2012 (10) SC 446; 2012 (4) MLJ(Crl) 499; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 740; 2012(10) SCALE 265; [2012] 9 S.C.R. 601

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 13(2) r/w s. 13(1)(e) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 389(1) - Corruption - Conviction - Suspension of conviction - Challenged - Respondent (accused) who was Superintended of Central Excise, allegedly possessed assets disproportionate to his disclosed source of income and committed offences punishable u/ss. 13(2) r/w 13(1)(e) of the Act - Trial Court convicted respondent and awarded him a sentence of 2 years - Aggrieved respondent filed an application u/s. 389(1) of CrPC for suspension of conviction before HC, which was allowed - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant (State) contended that instant case did not fall under exceptional circumstances under which, impugned order was warranted - Whether impugned order of suspension of conviction of respondent was justified- Held, Appellate Court in an exceptional case, might put conviction in abeyance along with sentence, but such power should be exercised with great circumspection and caution, for the purpose of which, applicant should satisfy Court as regards the evil that was likely to befall him, if said conviction was not suspended - Court should record in writing, its reasons for granting relief of suspension of conviction - Relief of staying order of conviction could not be granted only on the ground that an employee might lose his job, if same was not done - Instant case was not the case where damage if done, could not be undone as respondent (employee) if ultimately succeeds, could claim all consequential benefits - Impugned order was set aside - Appeal allowed.
