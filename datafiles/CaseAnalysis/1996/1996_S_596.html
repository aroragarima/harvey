Sreedam Chandra Ghosh v State of Assam and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2398; (1996) 10 SCC 567; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 13; AIR 1997 SC 251; JT 1996 (9) SC 283; 1996(7) SCALE 286; 1996 (6) SLR 721; 1996 (7) Supreme 337; 1997 (1) UJ 38

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Service - Constitution - Assam Secondary Education (Provincialised) Service Rules, 1982, r. 10 - (A) Appointment to post of Headmaster - Petitioner was asked to officiate as Headmaster till regular incumbent assumes office as Headmaster by way of stopgap arrangement - Whether petitioner has any right to post to hang on after regular incumbent has been transferred to post? - (B) Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Powers of court - Incumbent appointed to post of Headmaster by way of transfer - Petitioner holding said post on officiating basis stood in way of enforcement of transfer order passed by competent authority - Whether High Court is competent to enforce said transfer order and give directions to erring officers - Held, (A) Petitioner does not have any right to post to hang on - (B) HC is competent - Petition dismissed.
