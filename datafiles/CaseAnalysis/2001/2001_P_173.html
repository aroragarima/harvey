Roshan Deen v Preeti Lal

Supreme Court of India

02 November 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Accident - Compensation - Entitled to - Instant appeal was filed by appellant against impugned order passed by HC.

Held, it was explicitly clear that agreement reported before Commissioner which led to order had burgeoned in fraud. It got crystallized and a chicanery was played on Commissioner who was misled to believe that appellant and respondent had entered into an agreement. It is surprising how single Judge missed factual position that there was no dispute between parties, when application for recall of order was considered, that an artifice was disported in court at least by somebody in name of respondent. This is clear when respondent himself disowned having stated before Commissioner that an agreement was reached. Respondent stated that both parties have arrived at an agreement; therefore, nobody would come on behalf of applicant. When appellant submitted before Commissioner when he requested for recalling said order that no such agreement had been arrived at, Commissioner without difficulty noticed that respondent also submitted to Commissioner that he did not make any such statement before Commissioner. The whole deliberations before Commissioner smack of a fraud of a superlative degree played on Commissioner. Single Judge seems to have entertained a notion that once a Commissioner happened to pass an order, however illegal, unjust or inequitable it be, or even if Commissioner was convinced that order was wangled from him by playing a fraud on him he would be helpless and parties thereto would also be helpless except to succumb to such fraud. It was in this context that decision cited before Single Judge of HC required consideration by him. Impugned order is set aside and order passed by Commissioner is restored. Appeal disposed of.
