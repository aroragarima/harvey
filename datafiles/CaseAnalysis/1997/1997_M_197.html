Miss Kanta Udharam Jagasia v Shri C.K.S.Rao

Supreme Court of India

02 December 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1014; (1998) 1 SCC 403; AIR 1998 SC 569; 1998 (4) Bom.C.R. 297; JT 1997 (9) SC 556; 1998 (1) RCJ 144; 1997(7) SCALE 446; 1998 (1) SLT 287; [1997] Supp6 S.C.R. 91; 1997 (10) Supreme 251

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947 - Scientific officer - Tenant - Landlord - Eviction - Appellant claimed to be the owner of a flat in a building bearing plot no. 42 and preferred an application u/s. 13A1(i)A(ii) of the 1947 Act seeking eviction of the respondent tenant from the said premises - Plot bearing no. 42 was initially allotted in favor of the appellant's mother - Later, deed of Declaration was executed among the legal heirs of the deceased where the appellant and one of her brothers, were allotted the ground floor - Ground floor itself consisted of four flats and Flat nos. 3 and 4 fell to the share of the appellant - Flat nos. 2 to 4 were in possession of tenants - Respondent was tenant in flat no. 3 - Appellant was residing along with the family of her brother in flat no. 1 - Appellant had issues and thus preferred an application for possession of flat no. 3 alleging that she was a holder of scientific post in the Department of Bhabha Atomic Research Centre (BARC) and was entitled to invoke s. 13A1(i)A(ii) of the 1947 Act - Appellant had further enclosed necessary Certificate to support her claim that she was holding a scientific post in the Department of BARC and that she had no other suitable premises for her residence in the local area where the suit premises was situated - Thus the appellant alleged that her claim was bona fide and she was entitled for the possession of the said flat - Application for eviction was resisted by the respondent tenant - Matter was placed before the Competent Authority - Competent Authority held that the appellant had proved that she was the landlady qua respondent-tenant and that she was holding a scientific post in BARC to enable her to invoke s. 3A1(i)A(ii) of the 1947 Act and that the Certificate produced by her was proved - As the appellant had no other suitable accommodation and that her claim for possession was bona fide, an order of eviction of the respondent-tenant from the suit flat was passed by the Competent Authority - Respondent tenant appealed - HC held the Certificate originally enclosed along with the application for eviction was not in order and the Competent Authority ought not to have allowed the applicant-landlady to substitute the Certificate issued by the Competent Authority during the pendency of the eviction petition - Thus the HC reversed the order of Competent Authority and the eviction order was cancelled by the HC - Also it was held that certificate was not valid and appellant was thus not entitled to benefit u/s. 13A1(i)A(ii) of the 1947 Act - Appellant aggrieved - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the order of the HC was on valid grounds and whether the appellant was entitled to benefit u/s. 13A1(i)A(ii) of the 1947 Act - Held, the Certificate by the Competent Authority was on merit - HC had exceeded its revisional jurisdiction by interfering with the order of the Competent Authority without appreciating the limitation imposed by the 1947 Act - Appellant was entitled to benefit u/s. 13A1(i)A(ii) of the 1947 Act - Order of the HC was set aside and that of the competent Authority was restored - Appeal allowed.
