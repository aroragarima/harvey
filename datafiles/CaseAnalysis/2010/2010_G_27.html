Mathai @ Joby v George & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

19 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 193; (2010) 4 SCC 358; 2010 (79) ALR 759; 2010 (6) AWC 6243; JT 2010 (3) SC 160; 2010 (4) MLJ(SC) 362; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 495; 2010(3) SCALE 172; [2010] 3 S.C.R. 533

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136 and 145 - What kind of cases should be entertained u/art. 136 of the Constitution - Held, art. 136, is a discretionary remedy, and SC is not bound to interfere even if there is an error of law or fact in the impugned order - Use of the words "in its discretion" in art. 136 clearly indicates that art. 136 does not confer a right of appeal upon any party but merely vests a discretion in the SC to interfere in exceptional cases - However, it is not mentioned in art. 136 of the Constitution as to in what kind of cases said discretion should be exercised - Hence, some broad guidelines need to be laid down now by a Constitution bench of SC - Papers of this case be laid before Chief Justice of India for constitution of an appropriate Bench, to decide which kinds of cases should be entertained u/art. 136, and/or for laying down some broad guidelines in this connection - Matter to placed before Constitution Bench.
