Supreme Court of India

25 March 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah, A.K. Sarkar, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 471; AIR 1960 SC 866; 1960 CRLJ 1239; 1961 (1) SCJ 59; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 388

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Quashing of proceeding - Inherent power - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 561 A -Indian Penal Code,1860, ss. 120B, 420 and 114 - First Information Report against appellant for offences u/ss. 420, 114 and 120B - Appellant moved Punjab High Court u/s. 561-A for quashing proceedings initiated by First Information Report in question - Whether Punjab HC was in error in refusing to exercise inherent jurisdiction u/s. 561-A in favour of appellant? - Held, inherent jurisdiction of HC to quash proceedings can be exercised either to prevent abuse of process of court or otherwise to secure ends of justice - Criminal proceedings must be tried under provisions of Code, and HC would be reluctant to interfere in proceedings at an interlocutory stage - Inherent power cannot be exercised in regard to matters specifically covered by other provisions of Code - Delay in filing police report u/s. 173 cannot be ground to quash proceedings - Further held, in present case magistrate before whom police report has been filed has not yet applied his mind to merits of said report and request of appellant for quashing of proceedings is not covered by any specific provision of Code - Proceedings not at stage where merits can be considered since no cognizance has been taken as of yet by Magistrate - Appeal dismissed.
