Jiwani Kumari Parekh v Satyabrata Chakravorty, Managing Directorand Chief Executive

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

M.H. Kania, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 491; (1990) 4 SCC 737; AIR 1991 SC 326; 1990 (3) CCC 498; 1991 CRLJ 471; JT 1990 (4) SC 174; 1991 (1) RCJ 136; 1990(2) SCALE 833; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 246; 1990 (2) ShimLC 329; [1990] Supp2 S.C.R. 246; 1990 (2) UJ 717

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: West Bengal Premises Requisition and Control (Temporary Provisions) Act, 1947

Summary: Civil Procedure - Constitution - Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - West Bengal Premises Requisition and Control (Temporary Provisions) Act, 1947 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Contempt - Validity - Before a party can be committed for contempt, there must be a wilful or deliberate disobedience of orders of court - Held, facts of present case do not establish any such wilful or deliberate or reckless disobedience of order - Contempt petition is dismissed.
