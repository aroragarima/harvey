Raghubar Mandal Harihar Mandal v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

22 May 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 79; AIR 1957 SC 810; 1958 SCJ 22; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 37; 1957 (8) STC 779

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Sales Tax

Keywords: Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1947, Bihar Sales Tax Act

Summary: Sales Tax - Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1947, ss. 10 and 21 - Whether Sales Tax Officer is entitled u/s. 10(2)(b) of Act to make an assessment on any figures of gross turnover without giving any basis to justify adoption of that figure? - Held, in making assessment u/s. 10(2)(b) of Act, ITO is not entitled to make a pure guess and make an assessment without reference to any evidence or any material at all - There must be something more than bare suspicion to support assessment u/s. 10(2)(b) - Tax authority should have a basis for assessment u/s. 10(2)(b) - Appeal allowed.
