Solanki Chimanbhai Ukabhai v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

R.B. Misra, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 392; (1983) 2 SCC 174; (1983) SCC (Cr) 379; AIR 1983 SC 484; 1983 (1) Crimes 625; 1983 CRLJ 822; 1983 CRLR 212; 1983 (2) GLR 870; 1983(1) SCALE 198; 1983 UJ 401

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - CrPC, 1973, s. 386 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3 and 45 - (A) What are the powers of appellant Court in an appeal against acquittal as to appreciation of evidence - (B) Evidence - Testimony of eye-witnesses discarded due to inconsistency with medical evidence - Validity of - Main ground on which the evidence of the eye-witnesses have been discarded by the Sessions Judge, was that the medical evidence belied the prosecution case - Held, has full power to review at large the evidence on which the order of acquittal is founded and to reach a conclusion that upon such evidence , however matters to be considered by Appellate Court before reversing order of acquittal - Held, no ground to discard testimony of eye-witnesses. Appeal dismissed.
