Pankaja & Anr. v Yellappa (D) By Lrs. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

05 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 568; (2004) 6 SCC 415; AIR 2004 SC 4102; 2004 (57) ALR 277; 2004 (56) ALR 724; 2005 (1) ARC 638; 2004 (3) AWC 2733; 2005 (2) Bom.C.R. 273; 2004 (2) CGLJ 213; JT 2004 (6) SC 259; 2005 (1) LCD 133; 2004 (3) RCR(Civil) 723; 2004 (2) RD 45; 2004 (2) RD 451; 2004(6) SCALE 459

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Limitation Act - Amendment of plaint - Application rejected on ground that it is filed at a belated stage and amendment introduced a different relief than what was originally asked for - Whether in cases where delay has extinguished right of party by virtue of expiry of period of limitation prescribed in law, can court in exercise of its discretion take away right accrued to another party by allowing such belated amendments - Held, there is an arguable question whether limitation applicable for seeking relief of declaration on facts of this case falls under Entry 58 of Limitation Act or under Entries 64 or 65 of Limitation Act which question has to be decided in trial - Amendment allowed and direction issued to Trial Court to frame necessary issue - Appeals allowed.
