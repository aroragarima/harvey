State of Uttar Pradesh v Ramesh Prasad Misra and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2270; (1996) 10 SCC 360; (1996) SCC (Cr) 1278; 1996 (6) AD(SC) 209; AIR 1996 SC 2766; AIR 1996 SCW 3468; 1996 ALJ 1619; 1996 (3) CCR 115; 1996 (3) Crimes 181; 1996 CRLJ 4002; 1996 (2) DMC 368; 1997 (1) HLR 9; JT 1996 (9) SC 566; 1996(6) SCALE 90; [1996] Supp4 S.C.R. 631; 1996 (6) Supreme 339

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Indian Penal Code 1860, ss. 300 and 498A - Dowry death - Horrendous bed room murder of young married girl - Plea of alibi by accused was not established - Defence theory of stranger committing murder was also rejected - Strong evidence regarding demand for dowry from sister and father of deceased - Held, evidence of sister and father of deceased cannot be excluded by applying ss. 32 and 8 of Evidence Act - Presumption under s. 113-B of Evidence Act arises in such case - S. 498A of IPC is also attracted - Acquittal of accused was set aside - Death sentence converted into life imprisonment due to long lapse of time from date of commission of crime.
