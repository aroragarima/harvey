Bhimaji Shanker Kulkarni v Dundappa Vithappa Udapudi and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 457; AIR 1966 SC 166; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 145

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Bombay Tenancy And Agricultural Lands Act, 1948 - Mysore Tenancy Act, 1952, s. 46 - Whether transaction was an advance lease and not a mortgage? - Whether defendants are 'protected' tenants within meaning of Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948? - Held, if defendant to suit pleads that he is a tenant or a protected tenant or a permanent tenant and an issue arises whether he is such a tenant, Court must refer issue to Mamlatdar for determination, and must stay suit pending such determination - If any suit instituted in any civil court involves any issues which are required to be settled, decided or dealt with by any authority competent to settle, decide or deal with such issues under this Act, civil court shall stay suit and refer such issues to such competent authority for determination - Where civil court cannot entertain plea and accepts objection that it has no jurisdiction to try it, it should not proceed to dismiss suit straightaway - Proper procedure to adopt in such cases would be to direct party who raises such a plea to obtain a decision from Mamlatdar within a reasonable time - Civil court had no jurisdiction to determine whether defendant No 1 was a mortgagee in possession or a tenant, lower appellate court should have stayed suit pending decision of that question by Mamlatdar, and until such a decision was given, court could not proceed on footing that transaction evidenced by Ex.43 was a mortgage and defendant No 1 was a mortgagee and hold that nothing was due by plaintiff to defendants under transaction - Appeal dismissed.
