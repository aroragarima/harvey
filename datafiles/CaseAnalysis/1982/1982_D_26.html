Mahabir Prasad Verma v Dr. Surinder Kaur

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

Amarendra Nath Sen, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 180; (1982) 2 SCC 258; AIR 1982 SC 1043; 1982 (2) RCJ 205; 1982 (1) RCR(Rent) 615; 1982 (1) RentLR 591; 1982(1) SCALE 299; [1982] 3 S.C.R. 607; 1982 UJ 394

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: East Punjab Urban Rent Restrictions Act, 1949

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, s. 63 - East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1949, s. 13(2)(ii)(a) -(A) Credibility of tape-recorded conversation as an evidence - (B) Sub-letting - Sub -tenant continuing in possession after expiry of contract of tenancy entered into by tenant with written permission of landlord - Held, can be relied on only as corroborative evidence - Tenant not liable to eviction u/s. 13(2)(ii)(a) on that ground - Appeal Allowed.
