Commissioner of Central Excise, Nagpur v Indorama Synthetics (India) Limited

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 640; (2015) 10 SCC 793; 2015 (323) E.L.T. 20; JT 2015 (12) SC 124; 2015(9) SCALE 154

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Excisable Goods, Central Excise Valuation (Determination of Price of Excisable Goods) Rules, 2000

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 4 - Central Excise Valuation (Determination of Price of Excisable Goods) Rules, 2000, r. 6 - Consideration in form of drawback - Additional Consideration - Determination - Respondent assessee is engaged in manufacture of goods and same is cleared by payment of central excise duty - Further, goodscleared as 'deemed exports' to advance licence holders were at price lower than what was being charged to other buyers who did not hold advance licence - Commissioner issued show cause notice to assessee asking assessee to pay differential duty as additional consideration was to be included while arriving at 'transaction value' of said goods in terms of s. 4 of Act read with r. 6 of Rules - Commissioner took view that price was not sole consideration flowing from buyer to assessee. Not only such buyers, who were sold goods at lower price, were 'related persons', even goods were sold at depressed price. Hence, Commissioner confirmed demand of differential duty as mentioned in show cause notices and also levied penalties and interest - However, Tribunal held that drawback was received from Govt. and not from buyers, hence, such drawback could not be treated as additional consideration for purpose of arriving at 'transaction value' as per definition thereof u/s. 4 of Act - Whether consideration in form of drawback, which accrued in favour of assessee, would constitute 'additional consideration' received by assessee as per definition of 'transaction value' contained in s. 4 of Act read with r. 6 of Rules.

Held, assessee had supplied goods to particular type of buyers at much lower price than price charged from general buyers in normal course of trade as it had obtained facility of invalidating of advance licences from such buyers and procured imported raw material (duty free) against such licences for manufacturing of finished goods. Further, EXIM Policy on Advance Release Order speaks of mutuality of interest as assessee had procured duty free imported raw materials against invalidation of advance licence of consignees and in turn it sold finished goods to said consignees at lower prices as compared to other normal buyers. Hence, price was not only consideration. Further, assessee appeared to have derived double benefits in these transactions, i.e. enhanced sale and paid less duty on lower value; and imported duty free raw materials. Further, right to procure duty free imported raw material is being transferred to supplier by buyer. Hence, it indicates that flow back of additional considerations from buyer of said goods to seller, which is assessee. Further, IFGL Refractories Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, Bhubaneswar-II, 2000 Indlaw CEGAT 2530, relied on SC held that it was held in this case that in pursuance of contract of sale, there is directly flow of additional consideration from buyer to seller, hence, value thereof has to be added to price. Hence, SC failed to accept that where parties take advantage of policies of Govt. and benefits flowing therefrom, then such benefit cannot be said to be 'additional consideration'. Hence, SC held that consideration in form of drawback would come within ambit of additional consideration indirectly flowing from buyers to assessee. Hence, SC held that Commissioner has rightly come to conclusion with regard to fact that additional monetary consideration, in addition to price being paid for goods, i.e. transfer of advance import licence in favour of seller by buyer enabling seller of goods to effect duty free import of raw materials and bringing down cost of production/procurement, is consideration, monetary value of which has to be considered u/r. 6 of Rules. Hence, impugned order passed by Tribunal is set aside and order of Commissioner is restored. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Consideration in form of drawback, which accrued in favour of assessee, would constitute 'additional consideration' received by assessee as per definition of 'transaction value' contained in s. 4 of Act read with r. 6 of Rules.
