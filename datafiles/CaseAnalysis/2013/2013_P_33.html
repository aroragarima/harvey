State of Madhya Pradesh v Pradeep Sharma

Supreme Court of India

06 December 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 805; (2014) 2 SCC 171; AIR 2014 SC 626; 2014 (1) CGLJ 166; 2014 (1) JabLJ 365; 2014 (1) MLJ(Crl) 89; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 269; 2013(14) SCALE 626; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 772

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Proclaimed offender, Grant of anticipatory bail

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 82, 438 - Murder - Proclaimed offender - Grant of anticipatory bail - Justifiability - Respondents (accused) allegedly poisoned the deceased (son of complainant) - FIR was registered against respondent and other accused for offence u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC and a proclamation u/s. 82 of CrPC was issued - Aggrieved respondents filed an applications for anticipatory bail before HC, which were dismissed - Thereafter, respondents filed afresh application for anticipatory bail which was allowed by impugned order - Aggrieved Appellant (State) filed instant appeals - Whether HC was justified in granting anticipatory bail to respondents when investigation was pending - Held, if anyone was declared as an absconder/proclaimed offender in terms of s. 82 of CrPC, he was not entitled to the relief of anticipatory bail - In instant case, confessional statements of other accused revealed that respondents administered poisonous substance to the deceased - Statements of witnesses that were recorded and report of Forensic have confirmed the existence of poison in milk rabri - HC failed to appreciate that it was a settled position of law that where the accused was declared as an absconder and had not cooperated with investigation, he should not be granted anticipatory bail - Impugned orders were set aside - Appeals allowed.
