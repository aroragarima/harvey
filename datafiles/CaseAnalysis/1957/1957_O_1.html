T. S. Swaminathaudaya v Official Receiver of West Tanjore

Supreme Court of India

27 March 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Syed Jaffer Imam, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 154; AIR 1957 SC 577; 1957 (2) AnWR 53; 1957 (2) MLJ 53; 1957 SCJ 501; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 775

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Family & Personal - Land & Property - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Partition Suit - (A) What was nature of rights acquired by appellant in regard to payment and interest by respondent as representing 3rd Defendant's branch under terms of decree? - Held, when an owelty is awarded to a member on partition for equalization of shares on an excessive allotment of immovable properties to another member of joint family, it ordinarily creates a lien or a charge on land taken under partition - A lien or a charge may be created in express terms by provisions of partition decree itself - There would thus be creation of a legal charge in favour of member to whom such owelty is warded - If, however, no such charge is created in express terms, even so lien may exists because it is implied by very terms of partition in absence of an express provision in that behalf - High Court should have considered whether by reason of provisions for owelty contained in terms of that decree, there was, under circumstances, by necessary implication a lien or a charge created in favour of appellant for payment of sum of and interest out of properties falling to share of third Defendant's branch and there HC fell into an error (B)Whether decree of HC directing Official Receiver to pay money is binding on Official Receiver? - Further held, Official Receiver has to carry it out and he cannot claim refund saying no charge had been created over insolvent's estate in respect of them or because appellant was not entitled for any other reason to a priority in payment - Whether a charge had been created or not, or a right to priority exists or not, is irrelevant - It is enough that there is a decree against Official Receiver to pay money - In making payment, Official Receiver has carried out his obligations under decree - Decree stands and he has, therefore, no right to recover payments made - Appeals allowed.
