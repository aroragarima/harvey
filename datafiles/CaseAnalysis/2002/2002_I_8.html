Sushila v IInd Addl, District Judge, Banda & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1508; (2003) 2 SCC 28; AIR 2003 SC 780; JT 2002 (10) SC 410; 2003 (1) RCJ 44; 2003 (1) RCR(Rent) 89; 2002(9) SCALE 636; 2003 (1) SCW 202; 2003 (1) SLT 174; [2002] Supp5 S.C.R. 286; 2003 (1) Supreme 357; 2003 (1) UC 305

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: U.P. Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting Rent and Eviction) Rules, 1972 - Rule 16 - U.P. Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972 - eviction petition on the ground of bonafide need - held, the rule only prescribes certain factors, which have also to be taken into account while considering the application for eviction of a tenant on the ground of bonafide need. According to the guidelines provided in Rule 16 balance tilts in favour of the unemployed son of the landlady whose need is certainly bonafide and has also been so accepted by the respondent before us - the whole approach of the appellate court, in setting aside the order passed by the trial court and allowing the application of the petitioner landlady, seems to be quite unjustified and legally unsustainable.
