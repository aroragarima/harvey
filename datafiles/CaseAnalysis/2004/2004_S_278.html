Lella Srinivasa Rao v State Of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 166; (2004) 9 SCC 713; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1479; AIR 2004 SC 1720; 2004 (1) CCR 428; 2004 (1) JCC 645; JT 2004 (3) SC 12; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 63; 2004(2) SCALE 740; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 659; 2004 (2) SLT 535; 2004 (4) Supreme 98

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conviction

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 306, 498A - Cruelty - Conviction - Sustainability - Accused appellant committed offence punishable u/ss. 306, 498A of IPC - Trial Court passed conviction order - On appeal before HC, HC partly allowed the appeal - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified.

Held, father of deceased, PW1, in his deposition stated that misunderstandings arose between his daughter and her husband on account of fact that three children of deceased sister of appellant were being brought up in house of appellant which was objected to by deceased. If in those unfortunate circumstances three children of deceased sister of appellant were being brought up in his family, one cannot blame appellant or his parents for having shown compassion towards children of his deceased sister. If that is what caused annoyance to deceased, one cannot equate such conduct with cruelty or harassment. SC also find no reason why on this aspect of matter father of deceased should not speak truth. In any event, he and his family members were only persons who could have deposed about treatment meted out to deceased. Fact that these witnesses have been declared hostile by the prosecution, does not result in automatic rejection of their evidence. Even evidence of a hostile witness if it finds corroboration from facts of case may be taken into account while judging guilt of an accused. If their evidence is kept out of consideration, there is no other evidence to prove prosecution allegation of cruelty and harassment meted out to deceased. Regard to inconsistency in two dying declarations SC do not find it safe to act solely on them to convict appellant, and for that reason even accused no. 2, mother of appellant who has since served out her sentence. SC did not find that prosecution has failed to prove commission of offence. Appeal Allowed.
