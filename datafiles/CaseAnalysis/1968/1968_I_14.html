J. P. Jani, Income Tax Officer, Circle Iv, Ward-G, Ahmedabad v Induprasad Devshankar Bhatt

Supreme Court of India

20 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 421; AIR 1969 SC 778; [1969] 72 ITR 595; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 714

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Land & Property - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 142(1), 147, 148 and 297(2)(d)(ii) - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 34(1)(a) - Whether notice u/s. 148 of new Act could be issued to reopen assessment where right to reopen assessment was barred under old Act at date when new Act came into force? - Right of ITO to reopen assessment for year 1947-48 was barred under old Act before new Act came into force and it not permissible to construe s. 297(2)(d)(ii) of new Act as reviving right of ITO to reopen assessment which was already barred under old Act, such construction of s. 297(2)(d)(ii) would tantamount to giving of retrospective operation to that section which is not warranted either by express language of section or by necessary implication - Held, ITO cannot issue a notice u/s. 148 in order to reopen assessment of an assessee in a case where right to reopen assessment was barred under old Act at date when new Act came into force, therefore, notices D/- 13-11-1963 and 13-11-1963 were illegal and ultra vires - Appeal dismissed.
