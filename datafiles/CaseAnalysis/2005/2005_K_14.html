State of Karnataka v K. Gopalakrishna

Supreme Court of India

18 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 78; (2005) 9 SCC 291; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1237; AIR 2005 SC 1014; 2005 CRLJ 1436; 2005 (2) RCR(Criminal) 20; 2005(1) SCALE 643

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 201, 304, 498A - Sessions Judge found respondent guilty of offence and sentenced - HC set aside conviction - Appeal against acquittal - Held, it is unreasonable to reject evidence of a witnesses merely on ground that there is no consistency as to exact amount demanded by respondent - HC misread evidence on record and has completely ignored relevant evidence on record which was accepted by Trial Court - Restored judgment and order of Trial Court - Appeal allowed.
