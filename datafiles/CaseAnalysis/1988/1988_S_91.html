State (Delhi Admn.) v Jagjit Singh

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

B.C. Ray, N.D. Ojha

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 46; (1989) Supp2 SCC 770; (1990) SCC (Cr) 133; AIR 1989 SC 598; 1989 (1) AWC 191; 1989 CRLJ 986; JT 1988 (4) SC 715; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 1093

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 164, 306, 337, 339 and 406 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 132 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 307, 153, 153 A, 121 and 121 A - First information report's for bomb blasts in Delhi and Uttar Pradesh - Respondent turned approver and was granted pardon in bomb blast case in Delhi in 1985 but, resiled from their statements during trial - Respondent contended that he cannot be examined as approver in one case although he is shown as accused in other five cases on same facts and circumstances - High Court agreed with contention of respondent and hence, this Special Leave Petition by State - Held, s. 306(4) casts an obligation on prosecution to examine approver both in committing Court as well as in Trial Court - So, appellant who had been granted pardon in case F I R No. 238/85 has to be examined by prosecution in Trial Court, no matter that he had resiled from his earlier statement and tried to conceal what was within his knowledge with regard to offence in question - Further held, proviso to s. 132, Evidence Act, clearly protects a witness from being prosecuted on basis of answers given by him in a criminal proceeding which tend to discriminate him directly

or indirectly - Evidence of this case cannot be used against respondent in another First Information Report - Appeal allowed.
