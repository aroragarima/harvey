Tulsi Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

07 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2185; (1996) 6 SCC 63; (1996) SCC (Cr) 1118; 1996 (6) AD(SC) 14; AIR 1996 SC 3477; 1996 (3) CCR 106; 1996 (3) Crimes 319; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 534; 1996(5) SCALE 681; [1996] Supp4 S.C.R. 340; 1996 (7) Supreme 10

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal Procedure Code, 1974, s. 164 - Whether confession was voluntary or not? - Confession was recorded by Judicial Magistrate without indicating to accused that it could be used against him in a court of law - There being also no proof of Judicial Magistrate giving accused requisite caution that he was not bound to make it - Held, confession cannot be said to be voluntary - Mere endorsement in accordance with sub-section (4) after recording confession will not fulfill requirements of former sub-sections.
