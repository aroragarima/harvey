Shahejadkhan Mahebubkhan Pathan v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

05 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 359; (2013) 1 SCC 570; 2013 (2) G.L.R. 921; JT 2012 (10) SC 8B; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 684; 2012(10) SCALE 21; [2012] 8 S.C.R. 1177

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985 - Possession of Brown Sugar - Conviction - Harsh Sentence - Reduction of Sentence - Sustainability - Appellants-accused were convicted for offence u/ss. 8(c),21,29 of Act - Prosecution case was that appellant was carry brown sugar when search and seizure was made as per law - Trial Court convinced by evidence and material on record convicted and sentenced appellants to suffer rigorous imprisonment for 15 years and imposed fine of Rs 1.5 lakhs and in default to undergo RI for 3 years - Aggrieved appellants filed appeal before HC, which was dismissed by impugned orders - Hence instant appeal - Appellants contended that appellants were first time offenders and considering their age and poorness prayed for reduction of sentence - Whether conviction and sentence imposed by Trial Court was very harsh - Held, it was clear and reiterated that term of imprisonment in default of payment of fine was not a sentence, it was a penalty which a person incurs on account of non-payment of fine, if sentence was imposed, undoubtedly, an offender must undergo unless it was modified or varied in part or whole in judicial proceedings, however, imprisonment ordered in default of payment of fine stands on a different footing - When such default sentence was imposed, a person was required to undergo imprisonment either because he was unable to pay amount of fine or refused to pay such amount - Appellant could always avoid to undergo imprisonment in default of payment of fine by paying such an amount , SC, was of view that it was duty of Court to keep in view nature of offence, circumstances in which it was committed, position of offender and other relevant considerations such as pecuniary circumstances of accused person as to character and magnitude of offence before ordering offender to suffer imprisonment in default of payment of fine - Provisions of ss. 63 to 70 of IPC make it clear that an amount of fine should not be harsh or excessive, where a substantial term of imprisonment was inflicted, an excessive fine should not be imposed except in exceptional cases - Conviction recorded was confirmed and sentence imposed upon appellants to undergo RI for 15 years was modified to 10 years, and order of payment of fine of Rs.1.5 lakhs each was also upheld but order that in default of payment of fine, appellants shall undergo RI for 3 years was reduced to RI for 6 months - Appeals partly allowed.
