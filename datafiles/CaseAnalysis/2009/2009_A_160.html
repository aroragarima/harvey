Narendra Kante v Anuradha Kante And Others

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2040; (2010) 2 SCC 77; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 278; 2010 (1) ALD(SC) 153; 2010 (78) ALR 703; 2010 (1) AWC 921; 2010 (1) CLT(SC) 204; JT 2009 (15) SC 477; 2010 (3) MLJ(SC) 722; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 465; 2010 (109) RD 376; 2009(14) SCALE 665; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 491

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 39 rr. 1 and 2 - Temporary injunction - Balance of convenience - Irreparable loss and injury - Appellant filed suit for declaration and permanent injunction and also mandatory injunction in respect of the suit property - Application for ad-interim injunction filed in the suit was rejected by the Trial Court on the ground that a partition was effected between the legal heirs of deceased - Trial Court held that a Family Settlement was effected between the heirs of deceased - In appeal, HC observed that in case injunction was granted, it would be the defendants who would suffer irreparable loss and injury - HC permitted respondent No.10 to carry out construction activities over the disputed land, but it was restrained from alienating or transferring the property or from creating any third party right therein during the pendency of the suit - Whether the Courts below, including the HC was justified in refusing the appellant's prayer for grant of interim orders pending the hearing of the suit? - Held, Deed of Family Settlement document could not be relied upon since all the co-sharers were not signatories thereto - Further, respondent No.10 had already acquired rights in respect of the share of the respondent Nos. 8 and 9 to the suit property and in the event an interim order is passed preventing development of the portion of the property acquired by it, it would suffer irreparable loss and injury - Moreover, appellant was sufficiently protected by the order of the HC impugned in this appeal - No interference required in impugned order - Appeal disposed of.
