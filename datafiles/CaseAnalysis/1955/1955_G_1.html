Girja Datt Singh v Gangotri Datt Singh

Supreme Court of India

25 January 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 124; AIR 1955 SC 346

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Legal Advisor

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 68 - Issue (A) Whether will dated 17-03-1928 is proved to have been duly executed and attested - Issue (B) Whether award published by arbitrators on 17-01-1934 is binding on parties - Held,(A) No satisfactory evidence to come to conclusion that will was duly attested by witnessess - Neither of attesting witnessess were called as a witness to depose to fact of such attestation as required u/s. 68 - Will was not proved to have been duly executed and attested and thus, cannot furnish a basis of title to Respondent - (B) As decision on first question is determinative of these appeals, second question not argued - Will dated 17-03-1928 is not proved to have been duly executed and attested and appellants are therefore, entitled to succeed - Appeal allowed.
