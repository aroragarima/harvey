Sanatan Naskar & Anr. v State Of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

08 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 507; (2010) 8 SCC 249; AIR 2010 SC 3570; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 2961; 2010 CRLJ 3871; JT 2010 (8) SC 279; 2011 (1) MLJ(Crl) 687; 2010 (3) RCR(Criminal) 629; 2010(6) SCALE 699; [2010] 7 S.C.R. 1023; 2010 (2) UC 1289

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Ransacked

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302/34 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 27 - Conviction - Circumstantial evidence - Recovery of articles - Trial court convicted appellants on the ground that the prosecution has been able to establish by leading cogent and reliable evidence and the chain of circumstances leading to the commission of the offence by the accused persons - HC confirmed the order of trial court - Appellants challenged said order contending that prosecution has not been able to establish the guilt beyond reasonable doubt and confessions, alleged to have been recorded by the police officer on the basis of which recoveries were effected, are contrary to law and, therefore, could not be the basis of the conviction of the appellants - Whether the prosecution has been able to establish the chain of event and circumstances which certainly points out towards the involvement and guilt of the accused? - Held, yes - The articles have been duly identified by the owners during investigation as well as at the trial stage which were recovered from the possession of the accused at their instance - Moreover, PW 8 and PW 9 have specifically stated that on the date of occurrence they had seen the accused near the place of occurrence - They have also stated that the accused were known to the family of the deceased - Most important statement pointing towards the normal practice of the house and likely involvement of the accused is pointed out in the statement of PW6, the daughter-in-law of the deceased - Statement of PW 6 along with other prosecution witnesses is of definite significance - Accused was known to the family along with other accused and by giving just a bare denial or lack of knowledge he cannot tilt the case in his favour - Hence, trial court and HC rightly convicted appellant on the basis of circumstantial evidence - Appeal dismissed.
