Brijendralal Gupta and Another v Jwalaprasad and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 April 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 412; AIR 1960 SC 1049; 1962 (1) SCJ 307; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 650

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election

Keywords: Representation of the People Act, Representation Of People (Second Amendment) Act, 1951, Central Provinces and Berar Municipalities Act

Summary: Election - Representation of People (Second Amendment) Act, 1951 - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 36 and 116 A - Does failure of a candidate to specify his age as required by prescribed form of nomination paper amount to a defect of a substantial character? - Prima facie eligibility of person to stand as a candidate which depends under art. 173 of Constitution, inter alia, on his having completed age of 25 years is an important matter, and it is in respect of such an important matter that prescribed form requires candidate to make declaration - Requirement about completion of 25 years of age is outside presumption u/s. 36(7), and that must be reason why prescribed nomination form requires that candidate in signing said form must make a declaration about his age - Held, declaration about age is a matter of importance and failure to comply with said requirement cannot be treated as a defect of an unsubstantial character - Appeal allowed.
