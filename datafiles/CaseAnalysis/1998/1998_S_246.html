State of Andhra Pradesh and Others v K. Mohanlal and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 May 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 991; (1998) 5 SCC 468; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1383; JT 1998 (4) SC 110; 1998 (3) MLJ(SC) 86; 1998(3) SCALE 617; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 463; 1998 (5) SLT 171; 1998 (5) Supreme 50

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges

Keywords: Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1986, National Environment Tribunal Act, 1995

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Andhra Pradesh Land-Grabbing (Prohibition) Ordinance, 1982 - Andhra Pradesh Land-Grabbing (Prohibition) Act, 1982 - Consititution - Whether any incumbent Judicial Member/Revenue Member shall continue after receiving the opinion in this behalf of the Chief Justice of the High Court and such incumbent shall cease to function in case opinion adverse to his continuance is conveyed to him? Held - The HC, therefore, was not right in giving directions for mandatory consultation with the Chief Justice of the HC in the case of appointment of all members including Revenue Members of the Special Court - The HC was also not right in directing that existing appointments made to the Special Court should be placed before it for the opinion of the Chief Justice of the HC, and in giving the directions it did - Appeal allowed
