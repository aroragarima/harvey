State of A.P. v K. Purushotham Reddy and Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 259; (2003) 9 SCC 564; AIR 2003 SC 1956; 2003 (2) ALD(SC) 109; JT 2003 (3) SC 15; 2003(3) SCALE 88; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 832; 2003 (2) SLR 698; 2003 (4) SLT 33; 2003 (2) Supreme 630; 2003 (2) UPLBEC 1001

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Education; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Pith And Substance

Summary: Constitution of India - Andhra Pradesh State Council of Higher Education Act, 1988 - whether the State of Andhra Pradesh had the legislative competence to enact - Held, the provisions of the impugned Act clearly show that the State Act is in aid of the Parliamentary Act and it does not in any manner whatsoever entrench thereupon. It is, therefore, a valid piece of legislation. - Thus, only to the extent of conflict, the State law has to be struck down and not otherwise. The High Court committed a manifest error in striking down the entire Act without bestowing its consideration to the fact that the State Act deals with not only higher education but also intermediate education which in no manner deals with the subject matter of Entry 66 of List I of VII Schedule of the Constitution of India.
