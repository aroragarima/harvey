Indian Tube Company Private Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

14 January 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1124; 1992 (101) CTR 446; [1992] 194 ITR 102; JT 1992 (1) SC 112; 1992(1) SCALE 26; [1992] 1 S.C.R. 22; [1992] 60 TAXMAN 399

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Dividend Reserve, Surtax, General Reserve

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Corporate - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Companies Act, 1956, s. 271 - Companies (Profits) Surtax Act, 1964, s. 2(8), sch. II r. 1, sch. I, Table-A, regn. 87 - Whether, on facts and in circumstances of case, Tribunal was right in holding that a sum of Rs.76,00,000 which was paid as dividend for year 1962 following general meeting D/- 31-05-1963, out of dividend reserve of Rs.90,00,000 as on 01-01-1963, was not to be taken into account for computation of capital as on 01-01-1963, in pursuance of rules of second schedule to Companies (Profits) Surtax Act, 1964? - Held, a conjoint reading of scheme of Surtax Act, 1964 and Companies Act, 1956 suggests that appropriation made by board of directors by recommending payment of dividend, in nature of things, does not constitute a reserve - Resolution by general body of shareholders to declare dividend out of profits at a particular percentage crystallised into a liability, and subsequent payment relates back to relevant date, namely, closing of accounting year during which liability had arisen - Therefore, resolution of general body of shareholders D/- 31-05-1963, had retrospective effect in as much as it refers to profits of previous year ending 31-12-1962 - Therefore, a sum of Rs.76,00,000 worked out for payment of dividend and appropriated by subsequent resolution was only provision and residue of Rs.14,00,000 was reserve - Though general body of shareholders resolved and appropriated on 31-05-1963, dividend of Rs.76,00,000 from reserve of Rs.90,00,000, it related back to relevant assessment year and, therefore, as on 01-01-1963, Rs.76,00,000 was a provision and cannot be computed as capital - Only Rs.14,00,000 would be treated to be a reserve - Tribunal and High Court, therefore, have correctly laid down law and it does not warrant interference - Appeal dismissed.
