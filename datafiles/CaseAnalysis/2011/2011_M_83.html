State of Orissa and another v Mamata Mohanty

Supreme Court of India

09 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 96; (2011) 3 SCC 436; JT 2011 (2) SC 164; 2011 (4) MLJ(SC) 692; 2011(2) SCALE 377; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 704; 2011 (2) SLR 3

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Orissa Education (Recruitment and Conditions of Service of Teachers and Members of the Staff of Aided Educational Institutions) Rules, 1974, U.P. Secondary Education Services Commission Rules, 1983, Orissa Aided Educational Institutions Employee's Common Cadre and Inter transferability Rules, 1979

Summary: Service - Constitution - Orissa Education (Recruitment and Conditions of Service of Teachers and Members of the Staff of Aided Educational Institutions) Rules, 1974 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 - University Grants Commission (UGC) pay scale - Entitlement - Respondent was appointed as Lecturer and her appointment was approved by the 2nd appellant Director of Higher Education and she was granted the benefit of grant-in-aid - State Government, subsequently, revised the pay scale of Lecturers by vide notification dated 6-10-1989 as per the recommendation of UGC - However, notification dated 6-10-1989 would be applicable only to those persons who were manning the post and had 54% or equivalent grade in Masters Course - Respondent filed writ petition seeking direction to the appellant State to revise her pay scale at par with UGC scale - HC allowed the writ petition by relying on its earlier judgments - Hence the instant appeal by the appellant State - Appellant contended that respondent did not have the required qualification at the time of appointment, therefore, her appointment was invalid - Respondent contended that University had condoned the deficiency in eligibility/qualification by passing general orders from time to time - Whether the respondent entitled to the revised pay scale as per UGC recommendation - Held, procedure prescribed under the 1974 Rules had not been followed while making the appointment of the respondent at initial stage - At the time of appointment, there had been a requirement of 54% marks in Masters Course, however, respondent did not have such percentage of marks - Respondent's appointment had been approved by the 2nd appellant after a long time of her initial appointment - Respondent was, therefore, not eligible for the post of Lecturer - Fixation of eligibility fell within the exclusive domain of the executive and once it had been fixed by the State authorities under the 1974 Rules, the question of according relaxation by the concerned University could not arise and, therefore, the order of condonation was nullity - Further, such condonation was in violation of fundamental rights enshrined u/arts. 14 and 16 of the Constitution of the similarly situated persons, who did not apply considering themselves to be ineligible for want of required marks - Power to grant relaxation in eligibility had not been conferred upon any authority, either the University or the State and in absence thereof, such power could not have been exercised - In case a person could not get the benefit of grant-in-aid scheme unless he completed the deficiency of educational qualification, question of grant of UGC pay scale did not arise - Respondent's case had been entertained and relief had been granted by the HC without considering the issue of delay and laches merely placing reliance upon earlier judgments obtained by diligent persons approaching the courts within a reasonable time - Grievance of the respondent that not upholding the order passed by the HC in her favour would amount to a hostile discrimination was not worth acceptance for the reason that art. 14 of the Constitution envisages only positive equality - Concept of adverse possession of lien on post or holding over were inapplicable in service jurisprudence, however, in view of the elapse of long time, termination of the respondent was not desirable - Respondent, therefore, was not entitled to the UGC scale prior to the date of acquisition of higher qualification - Appeals disposed of.
