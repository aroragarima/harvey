Sri Jagannath Temple Managing Committee v Siddha Math and others

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 977; AIR 2016 SC 564; JT 2015 (12) SC 389; 2016 (1) MLJ 425

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Jurisdiction, Electricity, Record Of Rights, Operation, Special Act, Orissa Estates Abolition Act, 1951, Shri Jagannath Temple Act, 1955, Orissa Hindu Religious Endowments Act, 1951, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Constitution - Trusts & Associations - Land & Property - Orissa Estates Abolition Act, 1951, ss.2(oo), 8-A, 3-A - Shri Jagannath Temple Act, 1955, ss. 5, 30 - Temple lands - Special enactment - Initially State Govt. issued notification dt. 18-3-1974 u/s. 3-A of 1951 Act vesting estate of Lord Jagannath Mahaprabhu Bije, Puri in State Govt. but the same was challenged by Temple before HC - However, HC rejected claim of Temple and the same was upheld by SC - Later, State Govt. issued notification dt.18-4-1989 extending time for filing claims u/s. 8-A of 1951 Act and Temple filed claim case for recording lands in favour of Shree Jagannath Mahaprabhu Bije, Puri, Marfat Shree Jagannath Temple Managing Committee and vide order dt.30-11-1992, Collector observed that suit lands were recorded in name of Shri Jagannath Mahaprabhu Bije Srikhetra and settled suit lands in favour of Temple - However, during year 2000, respondent-Math filed writ petition before HC challenging order 30-11-1992 on ground that lands in question accorded status of 'amrutamanohi' and that they were recorded as Trust Estate as defined u/s. 2(oo) of 1951 Act and that lands had wrongly been settled in favour of Temple - HC by impugned judgment dt.7-7-2009 set aside order dt.30-11-1992 and hence, instant appeals by appellant-Temple and State Govt. and others - Whether suit lands could vest in respondent-Math in view of provisions of 1955 Act.

Held, in view of provisions of 1955 Act and the decision of SC in Raja Bira Kishore Deb v. Raja Birakishore, 1964 INDLAW SC 149, all endowments and properties belonging to the Temple vest in the Shri Jagannath Temple Managing Committee. Further, it could not be said that the provisions of 1951 Act will apply to the land of the appellant Temple Committee over the provisions of the Temple Act, 1955, which is special legislation. State Legislature enacted 1955 Act and vested land belonging to Temple in the Temple Management Committee by virtue of ss.5 and 30 of the 1955 Act and the object of the said Act was to provide for better administration and governance of the affairs of the Temple and its properties. Hence, proviso to s. 2(oo) of 1951 Act, by which the estates belonging to Temple of Lord Jagannath at Puri within the meaning of 1955 Act are deemed to be Trust Estates is in direct contravention and subversion of the provisions of 1955 Act. There is conflict between proviso of s. 2(oo) of 1951 Act and ss.5 and 30 of 1955 Act and both statutory provisions of the Acts could not survive together. However, s. 2(oo) proviso is in its entirety is not violative of the provisions of 1955 Act. It is only the first part of the proviso which is in contravention of 1955 Act and so, said proviso to that extent is struck down and also notification dt.18-3-1974 is quashed in so far as it relates to the property of Lord Jagannath Temple at Puri. Consequently, order 30-9-1981 passed by Tahsildar, settling the land in favour of Mahantas of various Maths as Marfatdars of Shri Jagannath Mohaprabhu Bije, Puri is in violation of provisions of 1955 Act and is so set aside. Further, once land already vested in Temple Committee u/ss. 5 and 30 of 1955 Act which is a special enactment to deal with the properties endowed to appellant-Temple Committee, the same could not have been divested by applying provisions of 1951 Act by way of an amendment to the Act by insertion of ss. 2(oo) and 3A in 1951 Act, as the operation of the said Act and 1955 Act are in different fields and the objects and intendment of two Acts are entirely different. Hence, in view of the provisions of the Temple Act, 1955, the settlement of the suit lands in favour of respondent-Math could not be sustained, as it is bad in law. Hence, impugned judgment of HC is set aside and first part of proviso of s. 2(oo) of 1951 Act, so far it pertains to properties of Lord Jagannath Temple at Puri is struck down. Further, in notification dt.18-3-1974, point no.(ii) is also quashed to extent it applies to lands and estate of Lord Jagannath Temple at Puri. Appeals by Temple Managing Committee are allowed and that of Math are dismissed. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Once a property is vested by an Act of Legislature, to achieve the laudable object, the same could not be divested by the enactment of any subsequent general law and vest such property under such law.
