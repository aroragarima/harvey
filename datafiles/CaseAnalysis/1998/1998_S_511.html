Bhagat Singh etc. v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 December 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1817; (1999) 2 SCC 384; AIR 1999 SC 436; 1999 ALJ 408; 1998 (4) AWC 130; 1999 (1) CLT 80; JT 1998 (8) SC 472; 1998(6) SCALE 475; 1998 (10) SLT 181; [1998] Supp3 S.C.R. 404; 1998 (9) Supreme 361; 1999 (1) UPLBEC 254

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution - Delhi Development Act, 1957 - Uttar Pradesh Urban Planning and Development Act, 1973 - Notification issued u/s. 4(1) stating that provisions of s. 5-A were being dispensed with, in view of urgency of matter in exercise of powers u/s. 17(4) of Act - (A) Whether there was such urgency which required dispensing with inquiry u/s. 5-A of Act? - (B) Whether acquisition would be invalid as land proposed to be acquired is for a purpose other than one permitted by master plan or zonal plan? - Held, (A) The subject satisfaction for dispensing with inquiry u/s. 5-A is based on sufficient material and cannot be faulted - Urgency clause was rightly invoked by Govt. - (B) There is no need that land proposed to be acquired by Govt. for a particular public purpose should be for same purpose or use mentioned in master plan or zonal plan for said area - Nor will acquisition be invalid merely because land proposed to be acquired is for a purpose other than one permitted by master plan or zonal plan applicable to that locality - Appeals dismissed.
