Â Bharat Surfactants (Pvt) Ltd And Anr. v Union Of India And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

17 May 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, S. Ranganathan, S. Mukharji, S. Natarajan, M.N. Venkatachaliah

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 24; (1989) 4 SCC 21; AIR 1989 SC 2054; 1989 (23) ECC 393; 1992 (39) ECR 28; 1989 (43) E.L.T. 189; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 239

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise

Keywords: Territorial Waters Of India, Entry Inwards, Tariff Valuation

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, ss. 12 and 15 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - (A) Whether rate of duty leviable on import should be that ruling on 11-07-1981, when vessel actually arrived and registered in Port of Bombay, or it should be ruling on towards end of July, 1981 when it was actually allowed to berth? - Held, u/s. 15 date of entry inwards of vessel is date recorded as such in customs register - (B) Whether levy of 150% is unconstitutional and void as a violation of art. 14 of Constitution because customs duty at 5 per cent only was levied on State Trading Corporation on similar imports of edible oil? - Further held, this contention already rejected in view of M Jhangir Bhatusha etc. v. UOI and Ors etc. (Civil Appeals Nos. 1924-27 of 1980 etc. ) - (C) Whether s. 15 is unconstitutional for reasons of being arbitrary and vague? - Further held, having regard to procedure for berthing of ship, there is no scope for submission that provision is invalid - Petition dismissed.
