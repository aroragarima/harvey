The Dharwad Distt. P.W.D. Literate Daily Wages Employees v State Of Karnataka And Ors. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

23 February 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, M.M. Punchhi, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 723; (1990) SCC (L&S) 274; (1990) 2 SCC 396; AIR 1990 SC 883; 1990 (12) ATC 902; 1990 (60) FLR 576; JT 1990 (1) SC 343; 1990 LabIC 625; 1991 (2) LLJ 318; 1990 (1) LLN 1011; 1990(1) SCALE 288; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 544; 1990 (2) SLR 43; 1990 (3) UPLBEC 2151

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Right To Work

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art.14, 32 and 39(d) - Equal pay for equal work - Directions by Karnataka Govt. to confirm daily rated and monthly rated employees as regular Govt. servants and for payment of normal salary at rates prescribed for appropriate categories of Govt. servants and other service benefits - Appellant employed on daily wage basis working for several years - Demand that they should be paid same wages as permanent employees employed to do identical work - Held, directed to pay salary to such workmen at rates equivalent to minimum pay in pay-scales of regular employees -State Govt. permitted to frame a more rational scheme - Appeal allowed.
