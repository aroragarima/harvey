Bur Singh and Another v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

13 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1624; (2008) 16 SCC 65; AIR 2009 SC 157; 2008 (4) JCC 2804; JT 2008 (11) SC 384; 2008 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3246; 2008 (6) Law Herald (P&H) 4113; 2009 (1) MLJ(Crl) 113; 2008 (4) RCR(Criminal) 834; 2008(13) SCALE 334; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 334

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Semi-Digested Food, Duty Of Court, Bloodstains

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 r/w s. 34 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, merely because the eye-witnesses are family members their evidence cannot per se be discarded - When there is allegation of interest, the same has to be established - Mere statement that being relatives of the deceased they are likely to falsely implicate the accused cannot be a ground to discard the evidence which is otherwise cogent and credible - Relationship is not a factor to affect credibility of a witness - Appeal dismissed.
