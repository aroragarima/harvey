Commissioner of Income Tax, Madurai v Mangayarkarasi Mills Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

21 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 886; (2011) 11 SCC 656; [2009] 315 ITR 114; JT 2009 (9) SC 658; 2009(9) SCALE 716; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 1187

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Cinema Theatre, Revenue's Appeal Allowed

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 31, 37 - Assessment Year 1995-96 - Computation - Deduction - Replace expenditure - Assessee claimed an amount of Rs. 61, 28, 150/-, being expenditure incurred on replacement of machinery, as revenue expenditure - Assessing Officer disallowed said claim and held said expenditure to be of a capital nature - Commissioner allowed appeal against said order and held that replacement of machinery by assessee constituted revenue expenditure - On appeal, Tribunal upheld order of Commissioner and dismissed appeal of revenue - Revenue filed appeal u/s. 260 A of Act before HC - HC relied on its own decision in CIT v. Janakiram Mills Ltd. 2005 INDLAW MAD 406 and CIT v. Loyal Textile Mills Ltd and dismissed appeal - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether expenditure incurred on replacement of machinery, amounts to 'revenue expenditure' deductible u/s. 37 of Act or 'current repairs' deductible u/s. 31 of Act? - Replacement expenditure is neither current repairs nor revenue - Held, each machine in a textile mill is part of integrated process of manufacture of yarn and is integrally connected to other machines in mill for production of final product - However, this interconnection does not take away independent identity and distinct function of each machine - Hence, expenditure of assessee is not of a revenue nature and thus, cannot be claimed as a deduction u/s. 37 of Act - In order to determine whether a particular expenditure amounts to 'current repairs' test is whether expenditure is incurred to 'preserve and maintain' an already existing asset and not to bring a new asset into existence or to obtain a new advantage - Replacement of such an old machine with a new one would constitute bringing into existence of a new asset in place of old one and not repair of old and existing machine - Thus, replacement of assets cannot amount to 'current repairs' - Expenditure of assessee is capital in nature - HC decision in Janakiram Mills case is not good law, hence set aside - Revenue's appeal allowed.
