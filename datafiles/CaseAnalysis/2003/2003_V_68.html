Central Bank of India v Vrajlal Kapurchand Gandhi and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 515; (2003) 6 SCC 573; AIR 2003 SC 3028; AIR 2003 SCW 3485; 2003 (52) ALR 548; 2004 (1) Bom.C.R. 141; 2003 (3) JhrCR(SC) 165; JT 2003 (5) SC 549; 2003 (2) RCJ 14; 2003 (2) RCR(Rent) 186; 2003(5) SCALE 271; 2003 (4) SLT 482; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 560; 2003 (4) Supreme 565

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947 - ss. 13(1)(g), 58 - Maharashtra Rent Act - s. 3(1)(b) - CPC, 1908 - s. 113 - Eviction on the ground of bona fide requirement - Whether after having allowed the amendment relating to constitutional validity of s. 3(1)(b), Maharashtra Rent Act, the High Court was justified in dismissing the writ application without examining the question - Held, the High Court should have considered the challenge to the constitutional validity of s. 3(1)(b), Maharashtra Rent Act, as raised by the appellant. It can certainly consider the effect of any earlier decision. The order of the High Court is set aside and the case is remitted back to the High Court for fresh adjudication on merits in accordance with law.
