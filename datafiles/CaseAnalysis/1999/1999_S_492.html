Ramprasad and Others v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

12 May 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 860; (1999) 5 SCC 30; (1999) SCC (Cr) 651; AIR 1999 SC 1969; 1999 (39) AllCriC 303; 1999 (2) AllCriR 1514; 2000 (5) Bom.C.R. 215; 1999 CRLJ 2889; [1999] 3 S.C.R. 519

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 32 and 133 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1974, s. 306 - Dying declaration - Person making it survives - Conviction - Held, statement cannot be treated as Dying declaration - Can be treated as statement recorded during investigation - Conviction solely on that basis - Permissible - But his testimony should pass test of reliability and corroboration in material particulars.
