Harpal Singh and Another v State of Himachal Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

14 November 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 40; (1981) SCC (Cr) 208; (1981) 1 SCC 560; AIR 1981 SC 361; 1981 CRLJ 1; 1981 CRLR 46; 1981 UJ 63

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conviction, Rape, Sustainability

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376 - Rape - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) and another was charged for raping the prosecutrix - Trial Court convicted appellant u/s. 376 of IPC and sentenced him to undergo RI for 4 years - On appeal, HC by impugned judgment dismissed the appeal - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, prosecution has proved the age of the girl by overwhelming evidence. There is the evidence of Doctor (PW14) who found that prosecutirx was about 15 years of age. This is corroborated by Ex.PF, which is an entry in the admission register maintained at Govt. Girls High School (wherein the prosecturix was a student) and which is proved by the Head Master. From the evidence, the conclusion is inescapable that prosecutrix was below 16 years of age at the time of the occurrence. Appeal dismissed.
