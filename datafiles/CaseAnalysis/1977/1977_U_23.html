Dharmendra Kumar v Usha Kumar

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

A.C. Gupta, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 96; (1977) 4 SCC 12; AIR 1977 SC 2218; 1977 (3) ALR 490; 1977 HLR 605; [1978] 1 S.C.R. 315; 1977 UJ 568

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, ss. 23(1)(a) and 13(1 A)(ii) - Wife's demand to relief - Wife's disinclination on offer of reunion - Application for dissolution of marriage by wife - Held, allegations by husband even if proved, do not disentitle wife for claims demanded - Conduct must be something more than just disinclination to reunion - Misconduct must be serious enough to justify denial of relief - Appeal dismissed.
