Rumana Begum v State of Andhra Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 August 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, N.P. Singh, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 463; (1993) Supp2 SCC 341; (1993) SCC (Cr) 551; 1992(3) SCALE 27

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950 - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, s. 3(1) - Preventive detention - Validity of - Whether representation made to Governor amounts to being made as one to State Govt? - Held, yes the position that a representation made to the Governor must be treated as one made to the Govt. was rightly accepted by the High Court on the analogy of the pronouncement of Supreme Court in Raghavendra Singh v. Superintendent, Distt. Jail, Kanpur, (1985 Indlaw SC 22) - Delay in representation amounts to violation of Constitutional Rights - In facts of case it is held that first representation made by detenu for revocation was one made to Governor - Not disposed of expeditiously - Unexplained and unreasonable delay vitiates detention - Appeal allowed.
