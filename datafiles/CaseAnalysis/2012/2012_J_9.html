Jai Prakash Singh v State of Bihar and another

Supreme Court of India

14 March 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 604; (2012) 4 SCC 379; 2012 CRLJ 2101; 2012 (3) JCC 1630; JT 2012 (3) SC 501; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 251; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302,34 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 438 - Murder - Previous Enmity - Anticipatory Bail - Sustainability - Appellant had lodge FIR u/ss. 302,34 of IPC alleging that when his brother-deceased after closing shop proceeded towards his house, respondents chased deceased, opened fired indiscriminate and killed deceased due to previous enmity - Respondent filed application for anticipatory before Sessions Judge, which was dismissed observing that strong motive had been found against respondent and presence of eye-witnesses - Appellant filed petitions u/s. 438 before HC, which was allowed on grounds that FIR itself made it evident that there was some previous dispute which led to quarrel and accused had fair antecedents - Hence these appeals - Appellant contended that HC committed grave error while granting anticipatory bail to respondents without considering gravity of offence and manner in which offence had been committed and without realising that FIR had been lodged promptly within a period of two hours of incident and both accused persons had been named therein - Respondents had opposed appeals contending that HC, had imposed very serious conditions while granting anticipatory bail, hence any interference at this stage is not required - Whether HC, was justified in granting anticipatory bail to respondents-accused - Held, Parameters for grant of anticipatory bail in a serious offence were required to be satisfied and further while granting such relief, court must record reasons therefore, Anticipatory bail could be granted only in exceptional circumstances where court was prima facie of view that applicant had falsely been enroped in crime and would not misuse his liberty - HC did not apply any of parameters, rather dealt with a very serious matter in a most casual and cavalier manner and showed undeserving and unwarranted sympathy towards accused - HC erred in not considering case in correct perspective and allowed applications on grounds that in FIR some old disputes had been referred to and accused had fair antecedents - Appeal disposed of.
