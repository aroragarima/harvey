State Of Karnataka v Sureshbabu Pukrajporral

Supreme Court of India

08 October 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 185; (1994) SCC (Cr) 435; (1994) 1 SCC 468; AIR 1994 SC 966; AIR 1994 SCW 1026; 1994 CRLJ 1216; JT 1993 (6) SC 48; 1993(4) SCALE 27

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376 and 366 - Respondent was found guilty of abduction and of rape of victim alleged to be below 16 years by lower court and later acquitted by High Court - Regarding rape statement of victim before Police as well as during cross-examination that accused did something to her which he ought not to have done could not be elicited and there were no other evidence to corroborate her testimony - Charges of kidnapping couldn't be proved as victim herself deposed that she went with accused voluntarily - Whether accused is guilty of abduction ? - PW 7's evidence shows that deceased went with accused voluntarily - When age is in doubt, then question of taking her away from lawful guardianship does not arise - Second requirement that taking or enticing away a minor out of keeping of lawful guardian is an essential ingredient of offence of kidnapping - Held, accused had not taken deceased away from keeping of her lawful guardian and something more has to be shown in a case of this nature like inducement - Appeal dismissed.
