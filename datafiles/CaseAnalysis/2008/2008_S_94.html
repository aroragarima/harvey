Vimlesh Kumari Kulshrestha v Sambhajirao And Anr

Supreme Court of India

05 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 143; (2008) 5 SCC 58; AIR 2009 SC 806; 2008 (70) ALR 768; 2008 (2) AWC 1927; 2008 (4) JhrCR(SC) 42; JT 2008 (2) SC 179; 2008 (4) MahLJ 692; 2008 (3) MLJ(SC) 295; 2008 (3) MPLJ 323; 2008 (1) RCR(Rent) 288; 2008 (104) RD 699; 2008(2) SCALE 195; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 399

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Suit for specific performance of contract - Suit decreed - High Court held that in view of O. 23 r. 1 of the Code of Civil Procedure, the permission for filing another suit on the same cause of action having not been obtained, second suit was not maintainable - Appeal against - Held first suit was withdrawn as an objection had been taken by the appellant in regard to payment of proper court fee - High Court was not correct in applying the provisions contained in O. 23 r. 1 - Oral evidence adduced by appellant herself suggests that the entire property was not to be sold as she was not a tenant in respect of the entire premises - Appeal dismissed.
