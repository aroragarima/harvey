Bejgam Veeranna Venkata Narasimloo and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 November 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1702; (1998) 1 SCC 563; AIR 1998 SC 542; JT 1997 (9) SC 274; 1997(7) SCALE 146; 1998 (1) SLT 199; [1997] Supp5 S.C.R. 389

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Procurement of rice, Controlled price, Recovery of excess payment

Summary: Administrative - Agriculture & Agri. Products - Contract & Commercial - Essential Commodities Act, 1955, ss. 3, 3(3A), 3(2)(f) - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 70 - Andhra Pradesh Rice (Procurement Ex-Mill Prices) Order, 1975 - Andhra Pradesh Paddy Procurement (Levy) Order, 1972 - Procurement of rice - Controlled price - Recovery of excess payment - Legality - Appellants (rice millers) supplied rice to Food Corporation rice milled from Khariff and Rabi crops for crop year 1975-76 and 1976-77 under the Order and Corporation paid them price fixed under the Order - Thereafter, respondent (State) issued notification that procurement price for various varieties of rice fixed in 1975 Order was brought down purportedly with retrospective effect from 7-9-1976 - That created an anomalous situation because in period between 7-9-1976 and 24-2-1977 rice was actually procured according to the slab laid down in 1972 Levy Order as amended by order dt.24-9-75 for which price was paid according to rates laid down in that order on the basis of Memorandum issued on 2-11-1976 - Appellants contended that it was not open to State Govt. to contend that Memorandum was not notified and thus, no right or obligation flowed from that Memorandum - If Memorandum was required to be notified, Govt. could not take advantage of its failure to notify it - Having acted on the basis of Un-notified Memorandum and having collected rice compulsorily from appellants on the strength of that Memorandum and paid appellants at the rate fixed by Memorandum, Govt. could not be heard to say that Memorandum was of no legal effect and payment was made under mistake of law - Held, it would be inequitable to permit Govt. to take plea of irregularity of its own Order after procuring rice on the basis of that order - S. 3(3A) of 1955 Act provided that where any person sells any essential commodity in compliance with an order made with reference to cl. (f) of s. 3(2) of 1955 Act, land of that section was mandatory - Price for the rice procured would have to be paid - In absence of any agreed price or controlled price, the price calculated with reference to average market rate prevailing in locality during the last 3 months would have to be paid to seller - Even if contention of State Govt. was upheld and Memorandum was held to be void and of no legal effect, State Govt. had a statutory duty to pay for the rice procured by it at the market rate calculated in the manner laid down by 1955 Act - In instant case, HC held that State Govt. could not fix procurement price of rice purchased by it retrospectively - HC held that a subordinate legislation could be said to be retrospective only when it took away or impaired any vested right acquired under existing laws or created new obligations or imposed a new duty or attached a new disability in respect to transactions or considerations already past - HC held that by notification dt.24-2-1977, Govt. did not reduce in any way the price legally payable to appellants because there was no such price in existence nor did it alter in any way the legal rights of appellants with regard to their sales for second year - Thus, if a portion of price paid by Corporation was taken away, appellants would be prejudicially affected - Appellants not only had acquired a vested right to be paid but actually received payment for rice sold - If rice was delivered without any valid procurement order, sellers were entitled to be paid at the market rate in terms of s. 70 of 1872 Act - Retrospective subordinate legislation has tried to take away a portion of the money the appellants had lawfully obtained - Judgment under appeal was set aside - Appeals allowed.
