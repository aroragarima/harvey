Securities and Exchange Board of India v Opee Stock-link Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

11 July 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Anil R. Dave, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 492; AIR 2016 SC 3825; 2016(4) ALL MR 979; 2016 (3) BC 694; [2016] 197 Comp Cas 435; JT 2016 (6) SC 402; 2016 (4) RCR(Civil) 43; 2016(6) SCALE 716; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 171

Case Digest

Subject: Securities Exchange Board of India

Keywords: Beneficial Owner, Companies, Stock, Contract, Indian Contract Act, 1872, Sale of Goods Act, 1930, Stock Exchange, Securities, Securities And Exchange Board Of India Act, 1992, Listing, Securities Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1956, Investors, Securities And Exchange Board Of India (Prohibition Of Fraudulent And Unfair Trade Practices Relating To Securities Markets) Regulations, 2003

Summary: Securities Exchange Board of India - Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Sale of Goods Act, 1930 - Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992, ss. 12A(a), 12A(b), 12A(c), 15Z - Securities Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1956, ss. 13, 2(i), 2(i)(b) - Securities and Exchange Board of India (Prohibition of Fraudulent and Unfair Trade Practices Relating to Securities Markets) Regulations, 2003, regs. 3, 4(1) - Shares sold - Irregularities - It was brought to notice of appellants/Security and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) that several serious irregularities/illegalities were committed by respondents so as to corner shares of companies by adopting certain unscrupulous, immoral and improper methods - Whole Time Member of SEBI came to conclusion that dealings of respondents were not fair and were in violation of the 1992 Act and the 2003 Regulations - On appeal, Securities Appellate Tribunal (SAT) allowed appeals and set aside orders passed by Whole Time Member and Adjudicating Officer, SEBI - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, because of undue advantage which respondents got some small investors or Retail Individual Investors (RII) should have not got the shares, which they ought to have been allotted. The fact that number of persons, having common address of their demat accounts, selling their shares at same price to a particular person before listing of shares of a company with a stock exchange is not a normal thing. Further, transfer of shares did not comply with requirements of provision of either ss. 13 or 2(i) of the 1956 Act. Therefore, off market trading indulged into by respondents was rightly held to be per se illegal by Whole Time Member. SAT, ought to have recorded specific reasons for arriving at a different conclusion but SC do not find any sound reason for coming to a different conclusion in impugned order. Thus, there was detailed discussion for coming to a particular conclusion in order which was passed by Whole Time Member of SEBI and no reason for SAT to disturb said finding without mentioning any strong and justifiable reason for coming to a different conclusion. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - The purpose of SEBI was to see that share market functions properly and effectively so that ultimately it may not adversely affect the economy of our country.
