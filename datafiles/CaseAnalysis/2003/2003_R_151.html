Supreme Court of India

18 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Defence of India Act, 1962, Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control, Bombay Land Requisition and Bombay Government Premises (Eviction) (Amendment) Act, 1996, Bombay Land Requisition Act, 1948, Bombay Rent Restriction Act, 1939, Requisitioned Land (Continuance of Powers) Act, 1947, Maharashtra Restoration of Lands to Scheduled Tribes Act, 1975, Bombay Rent, Hotel and lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, Requisition of Land (Continuance of Powers) Act, 1947, Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging Houses Rates Control, Bombay Land Requisition and Bombay Government Premises (Eviction) (Amendment) Ordinance, 1996, Bombay Rents, Hotels, Lodging House Rates Control, Bombay Land Requisition and Bombay Government Premises (Eviction) (Amendment) Act, 1997, Bombay Rents, Hotel Rates and Lodging Houses Rates (Control) Act, 1944, Bombay Land Acquisition (Amendment) Ordinance, 1994, Derequisition

Summary: Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control, Bombay Land Requisition and Bombay Government Premises (Eviction) (Amendment) Act, 1996 - i) whether the State Government has requisite legislative competence to enact the impugned amendments? ii) whether the impugned legislation is a colourable one and is an interference with the judicial mandate of Supreme Court contained in H.D. Vora's case and Grahak Sanstha Mancha and others, case or has the effect of overruling the decisions of this Court and hence violative of doctrine of separation of powers? And iii) whether the impugned enactment is violative of Article 14 of the Constitution as being arbitrary and unreasonable? - Held, the impugned Amending Act is intra vires and within the legislative competence of the State Legislature. It does not either directly or indirectly overrule the judgments of the Supreme Court and does not suffer from either arbitrariness or invidious discrimination.
