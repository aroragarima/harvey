Smt. Phulwari Jagdambaprasad Pathak v Shri R.H. Mendonca & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

26 July 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 379; (2000) 6 SCC 751; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1263; AIR 2000 SC 2527; 2000 (41) AllCriC 506; 2001 (1) AllCriR 414; 2000 (5) Bom.C.R. 802; 2000 (3) CCR 90; 2000 (3) Crimes 112; 2000 CRLJ 3944; JT 2000 (8) SC 209; 2000 (3) RCR(Criminal) 530; 2000(5) SCALE 302; 2000 (5) SLT 677; [2000] Supp1 S.C.R. 686; 2000 (5) Supreme 274

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Tort

Summary: Criminal - Maharashtra Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Slumlords, Bootleggers and Drug Offenders Act, 1981, ss. 2(b1), 3(1) and 3 - Arms Act, 1959 - Preventive detention - Subjective satisfaction of authority - In camera statements showing separate incidents of criminal activities of detenu - The assertions are not assailed as untrue nor can they be said to be irrelevant for the purpose of the order - Contention that the conclusion arrived at by the detaining authority that the detenu was a "dangerous person" within the meaning of Section 2(b-1) was vitiated - Question arise as to - (A) Validity of detention order - (B) Types of material which may or may not be taken into consideration by detaining authority in absence of any specification under the Act - Not specified under Act - Held, not vitiated, detention order not illegal, contention cannot be accepted and hence detention order under challenge does not suffer from any infirmity - Cannot be catalogued, it is left to discretion of detaining authority - Appeal dismissed.
