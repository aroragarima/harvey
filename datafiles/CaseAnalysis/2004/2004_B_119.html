Bijayini Dash and others v Loknath Mishra and others

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1837; (2005) 9 SCC 194

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, s.15 - Criminal contempt - Conviction - Prior consent - Complainants filed complaint petition before HC and sought punishment for contempt made by appellants/convicts - HC on said complaint petition convicted appellants for criminal contempt - On appeals, appellants challenged said order on various grounds including that they had not made derogatory statement attributed to them - Apart said grounds, appellants challenged said order of conviction on technical ground - Appellants alleged that under Act punishment for contempt could be invoked by party only with prior written consent of Advocate General of State u/s.15 of Act - Hence instant appeals.

Held, under Act, petition seeking punishment for contempt can be invoked by a party only with prior written consent of Advocate General of State u/s.15 of Act. In precedent, Court held that prior written sanction of Advocate General u/s.15 of Act is a mandatory requirement for invoking contempt jurisdiction of Court by a private party. In case of appellants, it is not disputed that complainants before HC did not obtain such prior permission from Advocate General. It is also not disputed that contempt proceedings have not been initiated by HC suo motu either under provisions of Act or u/art.215 of Constitution. It is clear from facts that contempt petitions nowhere state that such a sanction has been obtained by complainants. Show cause notice issued by HC though contained an alternate clause as to invoking of suo motu contempt jurisdiction by HC, same has been struck off indicating that cognizance of contempt complaint was taken at instance of private party. Nowhere in body of judgment of HC has indicated that it was exercising suo motu jurisdiction in regard to alleged contempt by appellants. In said factual background, and there being no dispute raised from any side that initiation of contempt was at instance of private party and there being no prior consent of Advocate General, these appeals are liable to be allowed on that sole ground. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Prior written sanction of Advocate General u/s.15 of 1971 Act is a mandatory requirement for invoking contempt jurisdiction of Court by a private party.
