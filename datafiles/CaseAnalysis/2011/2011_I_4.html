Union Of India & Ors v Ind- Swift Laboratories Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 116; (2011) 4 SCC 635; 2011 (265) E.L.T. 3; JT 2011 (2) SC 352; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 680; 2011(2) SCALE 589; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 1087; 2012 (25) S.T.R. 184; [2011] 40 VST 1

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 11A, 11AB, 32E, 32E(1), 32F, 32F(1), 32F(7) and 32M - CENVAT Credit Rules, 2004, rr. 3, 4 and 14 - Interest on CENVAT Credit - The respondent-manufacturer of bulk drugs, received inputs and capital goods from various manufacturers/dealers and availed CENVAT credit on the duty paid on such materials - On the basis of intelligence report, the factory premises of the respondent as also its group companies were searched which indicated that the respondent had taken CENVAT credit on fake invoices - Consequently, a show cause notice dt. 08.12.2006 was issued and the respondent approached the Settlement Commission - Before the Settlement Commission, respondent admitted all the allegations and duty liability - The respondent also deposited the entire duty of Rs. 5,71,47,148/- - Settlement Commission held that while the wrongful CENVAT credit was taken from the year 2001 to 31.03.2006, the payments refunds have been made on 22.02.2006, therefore, the respondent had the benefit of availing the large amount of CENVAT credit to which they were not entitled - Settlement Commission further held that the appropriate interest liability has to be borne by the respondent on such wrongful availment of CENVAT credit - The respondent filed miscellaneous application contending that the Revenue has calculated the said interest up to the date of the appropriation of the deposited amount and not up to the date of payment - Said application was dismissed by Settle Commission and held that the respondent shall pay simple interest @ 10 per cent per annum on CENVAT credit wrongfully availed from the date the duty became payable as per s. 11AB of the Act, till the date of payment - On appeal, HC held that on a conjoint reading of s. 11AB of the Act and that of rr. 3 and 4 of the Credit Rules, interest cannot be claimed from the date of wrong availment of CENVAT credit and that the interest would be payable from the date CENVAT credit was wrongfully utilized - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, the HC misread and misinterpreted the r. 14 of the Rules and wrongly read it down without properly appreciating the scope and limitation thereof - Rule 14 of the Rules specifically provides that where CENVAT credit has been taken or utilized wrongly or has been erroneously refunded, the same along with interest would be recovered from the manufacturer or the provider of the output service - The attempt of the HC to read down the provision by way of substituting the word "OR" by an "AND" so as to give relief to the assessee is found to be erroneous - Moreover, an order passed by the Settlement Commission could be interfered with only if the said order is found to be contrary to any provisions of the Act - In the instant case the order of the Settlement Commission clearly indicates that the said order, particularly, with regard to the imposition of simple interest @ 10 per cent per annum was passed in accordance with the provisions of r. 14 of the Rules but the HC wrongly interpreted the said Rule and thereby arrived at an erroneous finding - Impugned order of the HC set aside and order of the Settlement Commission restored - Appeal allowed.
