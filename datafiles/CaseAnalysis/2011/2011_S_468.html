Supreme Court Bar Association And Others v B. D. Kaushik and another

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 694; (2011) 13 SCC 774; 2012 (1) ALD(SC) 14; 2012 (2) AWC 1556; 2011 (8) MLJ(SC) 719; [2011] 15 S.C.R. 736

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Practicing, Bar Council of India Rules, 1975, Memorandum of Association and Rules, Regulations of Supreme Court Bar Association, Allotment of Lawyers' Chambers Rules, Regulations of SCBA

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Civil Procedure - Trusts & Associations - Practice & Procedure - Societies Registration Act, 1860,s. 12 - Supreme Court Rules, 1966, rr. 2(1)(b) - Code of Civil Procedure,1908, s. 151,O. 39 rr. 1 and 2 - Constitution of India,1950, arts. 136, 142, 145 (1)(a) - Advocates Act, 1961, ss. 17,34,49 - Bar Council of India Rules, 1975,rr.18,18(1)(iv) - Amendment - Challenged - Validity - Appellant /Supreme Court Bar Association received requisition to amend r. 18 of 1975 Act and accordingly General Body meeting was called for and proposed amendment was passed by majority of 85% of members present and voting - Appellant decided to hold elections and election Committee and implementation Committee was formed to implement 'One Bar One Vote' - As elections were declared resolution dt.18-2-2003 was recalled till completion of elections - Respondents challenged resolution dt.18-2-2003 by separate suits and Trial Court by common order dt.5-4-2003 granted temporary injunction orders in favour of respondents and against appellants - Hence, instant appeals - Whether Trial Court was justified in issuing temporary injunction orders - Held, primary duty of appellant was to promote welfare of advocates practicing in SC - R.18 of 1975 Act was amended to control bogus voting in elections and could not be regarded illegal or unconstitutional -No person should be enrolled as advocate in more than one State Bar Council -- Restrictions were existed in rr.18 and 18A even before r.18 of 1975 Act was amended and amendment would not change aims and objectives of appellant - Appellant duty was to promote interests of practicing advocates than non practicing advocates - Amendment restricts non practicing advocates -R.18(1)(iv) of 1975 Act declared non-active members would not have right to vote - In smt. Damyanti Naranga vs. The Union of India and others,1971 Indlaw SC 865 relied on confers right to form association necessarily implied that persons forming association should continue to be associated - Amendment had not taken away right to vote completely but had put only restrictions to promote and protect the privileges, interest and prestige of members of appellant - R.39 of the 1975 Act conferred powers on appellant to amend rules and bye-laws of the Association from time to time by resolution passed by at least 2/3rd of members present and voting at General Body Meeting - Authority to frame amend vary and rescind rules vests in Society General Body of members - Appellant was justified in amending as seen that there were only around 2,000 practicing advocates whereas enrolled members were 10,000 - SC gave directions to implement amended rule which projected principle of 'One Bar One Vote' - Regular practitioners should be identified by appellant from time to time by following procedure laid down in Vinay Balchandra Joshi Vs. Registrar General of SC ,1998 Indlaw SC 1990 - Appellant should constitute small committee of three senior advocates which would collect information about those members who had contested elections in other Bar Associations - Committee was directed to prepare lists of regular practicing members and non practicing members and third list of temporary members and directed those lists to be put up on appellant website and notice board - Aggrieved members should make representation to committee within 15 days and decision of committee would be final - Final list of regular practitioners of SC should be displayed by appellant and it was made mandatory that members should give declaration about whether he was a member of any another Bar and should give permanent declaration that he would follow 'One Bar One Vote' - Amendment made to r.18 of 1975 Act was legal and valid - Rights of two advocates who filed suits were not infringed or violated - Directions were issued to Trial Court to dispose suits - Appeals disposed of.
