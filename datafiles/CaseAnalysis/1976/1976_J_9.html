Jaswant Kaur v Amrit Kaur & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

25 October 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, P.K. Goswami, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 262; (1977) 1 SCC 369; AIR 1977 SC 74; 1977 HLR 731; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 925

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Succession Act, 1925, ss. 63 and 70 - Will - (A) Execution of, shrouded in suspicion - Evidence led by propounder of will - Appreciation - (B) Proof - Nature and standard of evidence required - (C) Revocation - Held, on fact and circumstances that the propounder had totally failed to discharge the heavy onus of explaining the suspicious circumstances surrounding the execution of the will and of establishing that the document which he propounded was the last will and testament of his grandfather - The propositions in respect of the nature and standard of evidence required to prove a will summarised - Revocation of an unprivileged will is an act only a little less solemn than the making of the will itself and has to comply with statutory requirements contained in s. 70 of the Act - Appeal Allowed.
