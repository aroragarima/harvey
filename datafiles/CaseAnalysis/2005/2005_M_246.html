Commissioner of Trade Tax U. P. and Another v Messrs Kajaria Ceramics Limited

Supreme Court of India

12 July 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 801; (2005) 11 SCC 149; AIR 2005 SC 2968; JT 2005 (6) SC 28; 2005(5) SCALE 355; 2005 (141) STC 406; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 437

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Indirect Tax - Uttar Pradesh Trade Tax Act, 1948, s. 4A - Uttar Pradesh Trade Tax Rules, 1948 - (A) Whether unit undergoing expansion is entitled under notification D/- 27-07-1991 benefit of exemption on additional fixed capital investment as a result of such expansion, or total fixed capital investment (being aggregate of original as well as additional fixed capital investment)? - Held, benefit of 1991 Notification as far as units undertaking expansion etc like respondent is limited to a percentage of additional fixed capital investment and not original and additional fixed capital only and not to a percentage of aggregate of original and additional fixed capital - (B) Whether respondents claim of one integrated expansion from 12000 TPA to 60000 TPA during period 12-8-1988 to 28-3-1994 is sustainable in fact or in law? - Further held, that there were in fact and in law three expansions and decide issue in favour of appellants - (C) Whether or not certain preoperative expenses form part of - Fixed Capital Investment for purpose of s. 4A of Act and notification D/- 27-07-1991? - Further held, underlying object of scheme of exemption u/s. 4A of Act, is to grant benefit by way of a quid pro quo for actual value of assets brought into State - Determination of such value would necessarily have to be an objective exercise - Notification and cannot by any principle of statutory interpretation be brought within definition of phrase in Explanation 4 to s. 4A - Issue is thus decided against respondent and in favour of appellants - Hence, preoperative expenses not part of fixed capital expenses - (D) Whether respondents, (allegedly) not having collected or realized any tax on strength of eligibility certificate, granted pursuant to High Court's judgment, and which was not stayed by Supreme Court, are entitled to any relief? - Further held, in absence of any order of stay by Supreme Court, appellants were bound to comply with impugned decision - Such compliance by itself cannot destroy appellants rights to press their appeals before Supreme Court - Appeal allowed.
