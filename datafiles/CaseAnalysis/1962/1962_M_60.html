Sheikh Abdul Kayum v Mulla Alibhai

Supreme Court of India

17 August 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 252; AIR 1963 SC 309; 1963 JabLJ 511; 1963 MPLJ 57; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 623

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Tort

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Muslim Law - Trusts Act, 1882, ss. 46 and 47 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 92 - (A) Whether trustees can transfer their duties, functions and powers to some other body of men and create trustees in their own place and renounce trust u/ss. 46 and 47 of Trust Act? - Held, no, unless this is clearly permitted by trust deed, or agreed to by entire body of beneficiaries - A trustee appointed is not bound to accept trust but having once entered upon trust he cannot renounce except with permission of Court or with consent of beneficiaries or by authority of trust deed itself - As a fiduciary relationship having been created, it is against interests of society in general that such relationship should be allowed to be terminated unilaterally - But it would be inapplicable to public or private religious or charitable endowments as per s. 1 of Act - On construction of cl. 5 of trust deed created for management of a school that provision for appointment of new trustees could not by any stretch of imagination be held to mean substitution of old body of trustees by a new body - Old trustees to add to their number under trust deed - (B) Whether in a suit filed u/s. 92, CPC relief can be granted even though not ask for it? - Further held, no, Court cannot pass any decree - Appeals allowed.
