Ram Karan (Dead) Through Lrs. and others v State of Rajasthan and others

Supreme Court of India

30 June 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 407; (2014) 8 SCC 282; AIR 2014 SC 3070; 2014 (106) ALR 240; JT 2014 (8) SC 40; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2876; 2014(8) SCALE 233; [2014] 6 S.C.R. 977; 2014 (3) WLN(SC) 49

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Rajasthan Tenancy Act, Rajasthan Tenancy (Second) Amendment Act, 1956, Rajasthan Tenancy Act, 1967, Land Record Rules

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Rajasthan Tenancy Act, 1955 - Sale deed - Execution - Void - Cancellation - Limitation - The Rajasthan Tenancy (Second) Amendment Act, 1956 (Act 28 of 1956), s. 42 - Instant appeal was directed against the judgment and order passed by DB, by the impugned judgment the HC dismissed the appeal preferred by appellant and upheld the order passed by Single Judge - Hence instant appeal - Whether in view of proviso to s. 42 inserted by Second Amendment Act No.28 of 1956, the sale deed executed on 12-1-1962 was void or not and whether the petition for cancellation of mutation was filed on time or not -

Held, 1956 Act (Act 28 of 1956) came into force on 22-9-1956 - Vendor executed the sale deed in favour of the vendee, predecessor in interest of appellant on 12-1-1962 i.e. after the second amendment - Appellants could not claim that their right was created much prior to the second amendment i.e. before proviso to s. 42 was inserted - Respondents rightly contended that the alleged sale deed dated 12-1-1962 was effected much after the date of coming into force (22-9-1956) of proviso to s. 42 - There was clear prohibition in making any sale by a member of Scheduled Castes or Scheduled Tribes in favour of person who was not member of Scheduled Castes or Scheduled Tribes since after 22-9-1956 - Transfer made on 12-1-1962 was against the said prohibition - Sale deed in question was alleged to be made when it was forbidden by law (proviso to s. 42) - No action was taken either by the Vendor or by the State for more than 31 years - Sale deed was executed on 12-1-1962 and the land was mutated in the name of appellants' predecessor in interest on 10-9-1963 - Neither any objection was raised nor was any application filed by vendors for restoration of land in their favour - Suit was filed by the Tehsildar, after more than 31 years - No ground was shown to file such petition after long delay nor it was mentioned as to whether the vendors i.e. original landholders made any application for restoration of land in their favour - Suit being filed beyond the reasonable period was fit to be dismissed - Additional Collector rightly dismissed the suit being barred by limitation - Proceeding for restoration of land initiated by the Tehsildar, was barred by limitation and was not maintainable - Impugned judgment passed by DB as well as judgement and order passed by Single Judge was set aside - Appeal allowed.
