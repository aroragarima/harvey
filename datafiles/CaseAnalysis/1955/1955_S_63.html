Â Hans Muller of Nurenburg v Superintendent, Presidency Jail, Calcutta and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 February 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 8; AIR 1955 SC 367; 1955 CRLJ 876; 1955 (1) MLJ(SC) 188; 1955 SCJ 324; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 1284

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Preventive Detention Act, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Extradition Act, 1870

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Constitution Of India, 1953 arts.14, 21, 22 and 32 - Foreigners Act, 1946, ss. 3(2)(c), 4(1) and 11(1) - Preventive Detention Act, 1950, s. 3(1)(b) - Whether there is any law in India vesting executive Govt. with power to expel a foreigner from this land as opposed to extraditing him? - Entries 9, 10, 17, 18 and 19 in Union List confer wide powers on Center to make laws about admission into and expulsion from India, about extradition and aliens and about preventive detention connected with foreign affairs - Held, Foreigners Act is not governed by provisions of Extradition Act; two are distinct and neither impinges on other - Even if there is a requisition and a good case for extradition, Govt. is not bound to accede to request - It is given an unfettered right to refuse; s. 3(1) of Extradition Act says 'the Central Govt. may, if it thinks fit' - Petition dismissed.
