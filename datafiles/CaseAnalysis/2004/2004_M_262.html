Delhi Development Authority and Another v Messrs Uee Electricals Engineering Private Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, G.P. Mathur, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1454; (2004) 11 SCC 213; AIR 2004 SC 2100; 2004 (3) AWC 1952; 2004 (110) DLT 447; JT 2004 (4) SC 562; 2004 (2) RCR(Civil) 463B; 2004(3) SCALE 565

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Tenders were invited by appellant for supply and installation of Clear Water Boosting Pumping Station - Respondent no.1 had also submitted tender - Regarding earlier dues director of respondent no.1 picked altercation with appellant official and physically assaulted him r - Counter cases were filed against each other in police station - Enquiry officer submitted report that appellant report was genuine and respondent only to defend himself filed counter case - - Tenders were opened and respondent no.1 was declared successful bidder - But in back drop of this issues Works Advisory Board decided not to consider tender of respondent No. 1 since show cause notice was being issued to it - Hence called for fresh tenders and respondent no.2 was given contract being highest bidder - Show cause notice was answered by respondent no.1 - Respondent no.1 filed Writ Petition questioning issuance of contract to respondent no. 2 and was allowed by HC holding that Company and its Director were two separate legal entities and misconduct of its director should not effect contract awarded to company - CRB debarred respondent No. 1 and its director for five years and was challenged by respondent no.1 in HC - Single judge allowed writ petition based on its earlier order and quashed orders of CRB and had given liberty to Appellant/authority to issue detailed and reasoned order - Based on HC directives appellant issued fresh order debarring respondent no. 1 and its Director for five years - Respondent filed appeal in HC and was allowed by DB holding that not awarding contract to the respondent No. 1 was not in accordance with law - Hence instant appeal by appellants - Whether Order of HC was justified - Held, administrative action was subject to control by judicial review only on three grounds of illegality irrationality and procedural impropriety - Courts should not interfere in administrative functions unless decision was tainted by any vulnerability - Person who seeks to invalidate or nullify any act or order of executive should establish charges of bad faith abuse or misuse of powers by administrative authority - General principle was director and company were separate legal entities but in many companies they act as alter ego - Company was vicariously responsible for acts of its directors as acts of company were done by directors and employees - To set aside executive orders mala fide manner with oblique motives of appellant should be proved - Respondent failed to prove any such malafides and so orders of appellant were not liable for judicial review - Impugned judgment of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
