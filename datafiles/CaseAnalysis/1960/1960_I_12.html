Corporation of City of Nagpur v Its Employees and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 February 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 90; AIR 1960 SC 675; 1960 (1) LLJ 523; 1960 MPLJ 495; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 942

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Central Provinces and Berar Industrial Disputes Settlement Act, 1947, ss. 2(14) and 39 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2(j) - City of Nagpur Corporation Act, 1948, ss. 6, 7, 57, 58, 79 and 333 - Appellant is Corporation of City of Nagpur constituted under City of Nagpur Corporation Act, 1948 - Disputes arose between Corporation and employees in various departments of Corporation in respect of wage-scales, gratuity, provident fund, house-rent, confirmation, allowances, etc. - Industrial court made an award holding that Corporation was an industry and further that all departments of Corporation were covered by said definition - Whether and to what extent activities of Corporation of City of Nagpur come under definition of 'industry'? - Held, definition of "industry" in Act is very comprehensive - It is in two parts; one part defines it from standpoint of employer and other from standpoint of employee - If an activity falls under either part of definition, it will be an industry within meaning of Act - History of industrial disputes and legislation recognizes basic concept that activity shall be an organized one and that which pertains to private or personal employment - Regal functions described as primary and inalienable functions of State though statutorily delegated to a corporation are necessarily excluded from purview of definition - Such regal functions shall be confined to legislative power, administration of law and judicial power - If a service rendered by an individual or a private person would be an industry, it would equally be an industry in hands of a corporation - If a service rendered by a corporation is an industry, employees in departments connected with that service, whether financial, administrative or executive, would be entitled to benefits of Act - If a department of a municipality discharges many functions, some pertaining to industry as defined in Act and other non-industrial activities, predominant functions of department shall be criterion for purposes of Act - Further held, Corporation is analogous to a big public company carrying out most of duties which such a company can undertake to do with difference that certain statutory powers have been conferred on Corporation for carrying out its functions more satisfactorily - Appeals dismissed.
