State of Kerala and Others v K. Prasad and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 July 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 764; (2007) 7 SCC 140; AIR 2007 SC 2701; 2007 (4) ESC 523; JT 2007 (9) SC 140; 2007 (3) KLT 531; 2007(9) SCALE 154; 2007 (5) SCJ 613; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 115; 2007 (5) SLR 60

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Government Policy

Summary: Kerala Education Rules, 1959 - Division Bench reversing the view of the Single Judge in regard to the upgradation of two aided schools in the State - Directed the State to treat both the schools at par with the two other schools which had been upgraded in the past - Appeal against - Held, it is difficult to hold that the decision of the appellant not to sanction upgradation of respondent schools because of paucity of funds was either arbitrary or unreasonable or manifestly erroneous to warrant interference by the Court - There is no denying the fact that opening of new schools or upgradation of aided schools does involve considerable financial commitment for the State - In the absence of gazette notification, calling for applications for raising of the grade of an existing school, the question of consideration of respondents applications/representations did not arise - Appeal allowed.
