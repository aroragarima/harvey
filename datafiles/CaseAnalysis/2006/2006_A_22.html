Aban Loyd Chiles Offshore Limited and Others v Commissioner of Customs, Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

07 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1017; (2006) 6 SCC 482; JT 2006 (7) SC 370; 2006(7) SCALE 544; 2006 (8) SCJ 308; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 290

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Misstatement, Territorial Waters Of India, Stores, Notice For Payment Of Duties Interest

Summary: Excise - Customs - Practice & Procedure - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 11 (A) & 11(A)(1) - Customs Act, 1962, s. 28 & 28(1) - Recovery of Customs Duty - Confiscation of Goods - Justifiability - Respondent issue notices to appellants by which ship stores were sought to be confiscated and customs duty and penalty to be levied - Commissioner of Customs confirmed demand and ordered confiscation of goods and levied duty - Appellants filed 5 appeals before Tribunal - Tribunal allowed 2 appeals and dismissed remaining 3 appeals while confirming order of Commissioner - Hence, instant appeals - Appellant contended that Tribunal erred in holding that extended period of limitation of 5 years as provided u/s. 28 of 1962, Act could not be invoked - Whether order passed by Tribunal was justifiable - Held, proviso to s. 28 could be invoked where payment of duty had escaped by reason of collusion or any willful mis-statement or suppression of facts - So far as 'mis-statement or suppression of facts' were concerned, they were qualified by word 'willful' - The word 'willful' preceding words 'misstatement or suppression of facts' clearly spells out that there had to be intention on part of assessee to evade duty - In instant case, in show cause notice it was not alleged that duty of custom could not been levied or paid by reason of collusion or willful mis-statement or willful suppression of facts - Appellants were not put to notice which of various omissions or commissions stated in proviso were committed by them to extend period of limitation from six months to five years - Appellants having not been put to notice did not had opportunity to meet case of Department - Appellants who were working on behalf of ONGC and as per its directions could not be accused of willful suppression of facts - All these facts were already to knowledge of respondent , who was not justified in invoking extended period of limitation - Thus, appellants were liable to pay duty for period of 6 months prior to date of issuance of show cause notice - Appeals partly allowed.
