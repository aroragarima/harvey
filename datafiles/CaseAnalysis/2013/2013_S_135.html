Stephanie Joan Becker v State And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

08 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, P. Sathasivam, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 76; (2013) 12 SCC 786; AIR 2013 SC 3495; 2013 (6) ALD(SC) 3; 2013(2) ALL MR 489; 2013 (97) ALR 467; 2013 (2) AWC 1119; 2013 (2) Bom.C.R. 539; 2013 (1) CLT(SC) 352; JT 2013 (3) SC 76; 2013 (3) MLJ 327; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 67; 2013 (119) RD 240; 2013(2) SCALE 312; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 951; 2013 (1) WLN(SC) 97

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Summary: Women & Children - Foreigners - Guardians and Wards Act, 1890, ss. 7, 26 - Guidelines for Adoption from India 2006 - Guidelines for Adoption from India, 2011 - Adoption of child by foreigner - Denial of approval - Legality - Appellant filed applications u/ss. 7 and 26 of 1890 Act in order to appoint her as guardian to orphan child and also for permission to take the child out of the Country for the purpose of adoption - Trial Court rejected said applications by order dt.17-9-2010 on the ground that appellant, being a single prospective adoptive parent, was aged about 53 years at relevant point of time whereas for a single adoptive parent the maximum permissible age as prescribed by Govt. of India Guidelines in force was 45 - On appeal, HC affirmed order of Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether order relating to adoption of the child should be passed by instant Court as the same was not dealt with in the erstwhile jurisdictions - Held, 2011 Guidelines squarely indicate that the procedural norms spelt out by 2006 Guidelines had been more elaborately reiterated and requirements of pre-adoption process u/r. 33 (3) and (4) were incorporated in 2011 Guidelines - By virtue of provisions of r.33 (2) it was 2011 Guidelines notified u/s. 41(3) of Juvenile Justice Act(Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000, which would now govern all matters pertaining to inter-country adoptions virtually conferring on said Guidelines a statutory flavour and sanction - In instant case, appellant's case for adoption was sponsored by an agency rendering service in USA which was recognized by Central Adoption Resource Authority (CARA) - Home Study Report of family of appellant indicated that appellant apart from being gainfully employed and financially solvent was a person of amicable disposition who had developed affinity for Indian culture and Indian children - Appellant, though unmarried, had support of her brother and other family members who had promised to look after the child in the event such a situation becomes necessary for any reason whatsoever - Child Study Report along with medical examination Report prepared by recognized agency in India was read and considered by appellant and it was only thereafter that she had indicated her willingness to adopt child in question - Before permitting instant process of inter country adoption to commence, all possibilities of adoption of child by Indian parent were explored which however did not prove successful - Matter was considered by No Objection Committee of CARA and as stated in the affidavit of said agency filed before SC, the No Objection Certificate dt.3-2-2010 was issued keeping in mind various circumstances peculiar to instant case - Further, apprehension raised by intervener, though may have been founded on good reasons, proved themselves wholly unsubstantiated in instant case - If foreign adoptive parent was otherwise suitable and willing, and consent of the child had also been taken and expert bodies engaged in the field were of the view that in instant case the adoption process would end in a successful blending of child in the family of appellant in USA, SC did not see as to how appellant could be understood to be disqualified or disentitled to the relief(s) sought by her in the proceedings in question -Hence, proposed adoption would be beneficial to the child apart from being consistent with legal entitlement of foreign adoptive parent - Therefore, order of Trial Court and HC were set aside - Appeal allowed.
