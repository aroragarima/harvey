Smt. H.B. Usha v D.S. Ramachandra

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1835; (1995) 2 SCC 49; JT 1995 (2) SC 89; 1994(5) SCALE 342; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 456

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Partnership firm - In the instant case parties to the appeal had decided to settle the disputes amicably - The terms of such settlement placed before the instant court - Whether, instant court had accepted the approach of the parties for amicable settlement - Held, instant court was in high spirit accepted the approach of the parties for amicable settlement - Accordingly order was passed declaring the amount which should be paid by the appellant to respondent who was, giving up half share in the partnership firm and the terms and conditions were cited for that payment - Appeal disposed of.
