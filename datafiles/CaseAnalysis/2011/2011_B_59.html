Lalit Kumar Modi v Board of Control for Cricket in India and others

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 604; (2011) 10 SCC 106; 2013 (1) AWC 141; JT 2011 (11) SC 376; 2012 (2) MLJ(SC) 285; 2011(11) SCALE 31; [2011] 15 S.C.R. 1004; 2012 (2) SLR 361

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Indian Bar Councils Act, 1926, Andhra Pradesh Civil Services (Disciplinary Proceedings Tribunal) Rules, 1961

Summary: (A) Trusts & Associations - Practice & Procedure - Membership in Society - Suspension - Disciplinary proceedings - Personal bias - 1st respondent Board organized a cricket competition under the banner 'Indian Premier League' (IPL) - Petitioner, In charge Chairman of IPL, was suspended from the 1st respondent on the ground that he had received kickbacks for assigning the telecast rights of IPL and had sought to create a parallel cricket body at the international level and thereby subvert the present International Cricket structure - Petitioner was served with three show cause notices - Petitioner replied to the show cause notices - 1st respondent constituted a Disciplinary Committee (Committee) u/r. 1(q) of its Rules - Petitioner requested the President of the 1st respondent to recuse himself from the Committee - President recused himself from the Committee and as a result, another person was appointed in his place - Petitioner filed writ petition challenging his suspension and the decision of reference of the matter to Committee - HC dismissed writ petition - Petitioner filed another application to the Committee seeking all the members to recuse themselves from functioning as members of the Committee - Committee rejected applications - Petitioner filed another writ petition challenging Committee's order of rejection - HC dismissed that writ petition also - In the meantime, 1st respondent constituted a regular Disciplinary Committee, however, it extended the Committee constituted for inquiring the charges against the petitioner - Petitioner filed a suit challenging 1st respondent's decision granting extension to the Committee - Single Judge rejected petitioner's notice of motion for injunction and DB dismissed consequent appeal thereafter - Whether the reclusion of the President himself from the Committee and the nomination of another person in his place was justifiable in the facts and circumstances of the case - Held, petitioner himself had objected to the President being the member of the Committee and that being the position, the President recused himself from the Committee - When a situation thus arouse, in view of the objection of the petitioner, the Society should not be left without a remedy - Rules laid down the terms of the contract amongst the members of the Society, and the terms could be altered only with the consent of the concerned members - Further, Rule did not say that if the President should not be a member of the Committee no substitution should take place, nor did it say that the substituting member should be one not objected by the delinquent against whom the enquiry was proposed - Said rule was being canvassed as a term of the contract of membership - A member of the Society having accepted the rules, agreed to the disciplinary authority of the three member Committee which was to be constituted under these rules - A delinquent member could not claim a right to dictate as to who should be the members of the Committee - Any such interpretation would lead to a situation that the delinquent would decide as to who should be the members of the Disciplinary Committee - Rule was elastic enough, and in an appropriate situation the word 'shall' could be read as 'may' - It was very clear that, normally the President should be a member of three Member Committee, but if for any reason his presence on the Committee was objected to on grounds of unfairness, and he recused himself there from, the 1st respondent certainly had the power to substitute him by some other person - Action of the respondents was sought to be defended on the basis of necessity - Petitions dismissed.

(B) Trusts & Associations - Practice & Procedure - Allegation of personal bias - Sustainability - Whether the allegation of bias against the members of the Disciplinary Committee was sustainable - Held, petitioner had, in clear terms stated that he was not making any personal allegations against two members of the Committee - Even the grievance against the third member cannot be said to be well founded - Petitioner was alleging institutional bias against the members of the Committee, which was only on the basis of their participation in the meetings of the 1st respondent Society - In that way, institutional bias could be alleged against every member of the Governing Council of IPL and the General Body of the 1st respondent which could not be accepted - Petitioner may have an apprehension, but it was not possible to say from the material on record that he was facing a real danger of bias - It cannot presume that the three member committee would not afford the petitioner a fair hearing, or that it would not render unbiased findings - Hence, Committee was validly constituted u/r.1(q) in view of the necessity arising due to the recusal of the President of the 1st respondent from the Committee - Petitions dismissed.
