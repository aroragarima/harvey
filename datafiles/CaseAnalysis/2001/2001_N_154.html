Supreme Court of India

16 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Phukan, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 129; (2001) 8 SCC 431; AIR 2001 SC 3806; 2001 (45) ALR 644; 2002 (1) CGLJ 408; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 75; JT 2001 (8) SC 612; 2001 (2) RCR(Rent) 511; 2001(7) SCALE 265; 2001 (7) SLT 340; [2001] Supp4 S.C.R. 192; 2001 (7) Supreme 622

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: It is for the landlord to decide how and in what manner he should live and that he is the best judge of his residential requirement. Further, the High Courts should always remember, while exercising control over the courts subordinate thereto, that the strength of power is not displayed solely in cracking a whip on errors, mistakes or failures, the power should be so wielded as to have propensity to prevent mistakes and to ensure exclusion of repetition, if committed once, innocently or unwittingly.
