West Bengal State Electricity Board v Patel Engineering Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19767; (2001) 2 SCC 451; AIR 2001 SC 682; AIR 2001 SCW 322; 2001 (1) ARBLR 540; 2001 (1) CompLJ 360; JT 2001 (2) SC 524; 2001 (3) RAJ 327; 2001(1) SCALE 255; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 352; 2001 (1) SLT 534; 2001 (1) Supreme 154

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Tenders - Rejected correction of mistakes - Challenged -- Appellant invited tenders for construction of project and bids were submitted - Tenders were opened and out of three bidders respondent no. 1 to 4 quoted lowest bid - While bids were under scrutiny respondent no. 1 to 4 by letters dt. 25-10-1999 and 17-12-1999 informed appellant that there were repeated typographical mistakes and sought permission to be corrected - Appellant by letter dt.18-12-1999 informed respondent no. 1 to 4 that good number of arithmetic errors were found and corrected in their bid during checking and corrected documents were communicated and sought their response in writing before 27-12- 1999 and caveat was also entered that said letter neither confirmed their bid not contract was awarded by appellant - Respondent.1 to 4 challenged appellants letter dt.18-12-1999 by filing writ petition in HC - Single Judge disposed of writ petition directing appellant to consider respondents 1 to 4 representation and pass necessary orders - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal and cross objections filed by respondent no. 1to4 in DB - DB by common judgment dismissed appeal and cross objections upholding order of Single Judge directed appellant to permit respondent Nos. 1 to 4 to correct errors in bid documents and then consider their bid along with other bids and take decision objectively and rationally - Hence, instant appeal - Whether DB was justified in upholding Order of Single Judge - Held, respondent.1 to 4 submitted letter seeking correction of mistakes after 6 months of submission of tender and after one and half month after opending of bids would be violative of cls. 24.1 and 24.3 and 29.1 and appellant should have sought clarification u/cl. 27.1 from respondent Nos. 1 to 4 - Purpose of issuing rules/instructions was to ensure their enforcement and relaxation or waiver of rule or condition by appellant in favour of one bidder would create justifiable doubts in minds of other bidders and such approach should always be avoided and to be done strictly in compliance with Rules - This being highly competitive global tender appellant was justified in not permitting respondent Nos. 1 to 4 to correct errors - HC erred in directing appellant to permit respondent Nos. 1 to 4 to correct errors in bid documents - Mistakes remained in bid documents due to gross negligence of not checking them before submission of bid - Cls. 24 and 27 of ITB permit modification or withdrawal of bids after bid submission but before deadline for submissions and not thereafter and hence were not entitled to rectify mistakes in bid - Principle of awarding contract to lowest tendered applies when all things were equal and rules and conditions could not be ignored simply because bid was low - Appellant had always power to negotiate with the next lowest bidder for awarding the contract - Judicial review could be exercised carefully - DB order pertaining to quashing of communication and letter dated 18-12-1999 comes under judicial review and was upheld -DB order directing appellant to permit respondent Nos. 1 to 4 to correct bid documents and to consider their bid was not judicial review and hence set aside - Appeal partly allowed.
