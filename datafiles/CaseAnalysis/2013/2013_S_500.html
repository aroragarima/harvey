Kamal Jora v State of Uttarakhand and anothers

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 378; (2013) 9 SCC 396; AIR 2013 SC 2242; 2013 (5) AWC 4815; JT 2013 (9) SC 229; 2013(7) SCALE 607; [2013] 8 S.C.R. 40; 2013 (3) UPLBEC 2097

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: U.P. Municipalities Act, 1916, Uttar Pradesh Municipality Act, 1959

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution - Uttar Pradesh Municipal Corporations Act, 1959, ss. 3(2), 8-AA(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 243Q(2) - Appointment of chairman - Notifications for dissolve of municipal council - Legality - Appellant was elected as Chairman of Municipal Council - Govt. issued 2 notifications, stated that Governor was pleased to notify for overall development of city conversion of existing smaller urban area into a larger urban area in exercise of powers u/s. 3(2) of the Act r/w art. 243Q(2) of Constitution and to further notify that area included in larger urban area would be total of area of Municipal Corporation - However, in other notification, it was stated that Governor had directed u/s. 8-AA(1) of the Act that existing Municipal Council would stand dissolved from date of issuance of notification and District Magistrate be appointed Administrator for administration of larger urban area of Municipal Corporation - Aggrieved, appellant again filed writ petition - Single Judge allowed the petition and quashed the 2 notifications - State filed appeal before DB, which was allowed the appeal and set aside the order of Single Judge - Hence instant appeal - Whether Single Judge was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, it was clear from public notice dt.29-6-2011 extracted that on receipt of written objections and suggestions, a hearing was conducted by Principal Secretary and during hearing persons were to be given an opportunity of personal hearing on objections - Hence, appellant was Chairman of Municipal Council could also participated in hearing in support of his objections - It could not find any infirmity in impugned judgment of DB of HC that an opportunity of hearing was actually given to all persons likely to be affected by 2 notifications - It was found the pursuant to 2 notifications, elections to Municipal Corporation was notified to be held and completed by 30-4-2013 - Hence, even if appellant succeeds on that point, it could not direct restoration of Municipality after Constitution of Municipal Corporation - DB was justified in passing the impugned order - Appeal dismissed.
