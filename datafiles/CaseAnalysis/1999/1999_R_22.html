D. Ramachandran v R. V. Janakiraman and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Srinivasan, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 432; (1999) 3 SCC 267; AIR 1999 SC 1128; 1999 (2) CLT 109; 1999 (1) CTC 715; JT 1999 (2) SC 94; 1999(1) SCALE 693; 1999 (1) SCJ 655; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 983; 1999 (2) SLT 484; 1999 (2) Supreme 454

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 81, 83 and 85 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6, r. 16 and O. 7, r. 11 - Preliminary objection as to tenability of petition - Court cannot dissect pleadings into several part and strike out portion not disclosing cause of action - Petition disclosed cause of action which if unrebutted could void election - Validity of rejection of petition at threshold u/O. 7, r. 11(a), that to without trial - Held, not justified - Appeal allowed.
