Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal Ii v Rajasthan Mines Limited, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 133; (1970) 2 SCC 158; AIR 1970 SC 1560; [1970] 78 ITR 45; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 517

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Agreement - Proprietary interest - Arrears of rent and royalty - Proprietary interest - Assessee was public limited Company incorporated dt. 23-1-1947 - 'A' was landlord covering about 312 villages of lands were rich in coal and fireclay - 'B' Development Co. Ltd., in custody of coal mining licence in about 14 of those villages - Company had prospecting licence for coal in fields with option to take further coat mining leases - By agreements assessee acquired from 'A' proprietary interest in all those leased out lands more fully specified in schedule appended to indenture - 'A' also transferred and assigned to assessee his right to receive arrears of rent and royalty from lessees - For assessment year 1948-1949, Income Tax Officer assessed entire amount of arrears of rent and royalty receivable from lessees and net amount included in assessment under that head was Rs. 2,55,733/- - Income Tax Officer treated entire arrears of rent and royalty amounting to Rs. 3,00, 332/- as revenue receipts of assessee taxable during assessment year 1950-1951 - Sale of lands by assessee as business transaction and taxed sum of Rs. 2,20,000/- as net profit of assessee arising from sale which profit was recomputed by Appellate Assistant Commissioner at Rs.2,80,000/- Income Tax Appellate Tribunal agreed with Appellate Assistant Commissioner - HC concluded that sums of Rs. 2,55,733/- and Rs. 3,00,332/- receivable by assessee as arrears of royalty and rent were not assessable as profits of assessee for assessment year 1948-1949 and 1950-1951 respectively - Hence, instant appeal - Whether assessee entitled to arrears of rent were assessable for assessment years 1948-1949 and 1950-51 respectively - Held, purchase of right to collect arrears of rent and royalty cannot be considered as income - Assessee purchased lessor's right from 'A' in pursuance of agreements entered in to by 'A' with third parties whose rights had been acquired by assessee - As per terms of conveyance, assessee became entitled to arrears of rent and royalty as purchaser of those rights and had no right to collect those arrears of rent and royalty as owner of property - Determining the price payable under conveyance arrears of rent and royalty were not taken into but does not change nature of right acquired by assessee - HC was justified in examining correctness of inference drawn by Tribunal on basis of primary facts found by that Tribunal and answered in favour of assessee - Appeal dismissed.
