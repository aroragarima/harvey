Surjit Kaur Gill and another v Adarsh Kaur Gill and another

Supreme Court of India

30 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 85; AIR 2014 SC 1476; 2014 (103) ALR 225; 2014 (3) AWC 2438; 2014 (2) CTC 199; 2014 (2) RCR(Civil) 36; 2014 (123) RD 15; 2014 (2) RLW 1477; 2014(2) SCALE 81; [2014] 2 S.C.R. 167; 2014 (2) WLN(SC) 74

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Rejection Of Plaint

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908-Order VII, Rule 11-Plaint-Rejection of plaint-Suit for partition of properties filed on original side of High Court-Application made under Order VII, Rule 11 moved by respondent No. 1-Contention of respondent/defendant that statements in plaint shows that suit for partition barred by law and, therefore, plaint ought to be rejected under Order VII, Rule 11 (d)-Single Judge of High Court dismissed application made under Order VII, Rule 11-Division Bench of High Court allowed application made by respondent No. 1 under Order VII, Rule 11-Plaint must show that it is barred by any law to attract application of Order VII, Rule 11-Language of various paragraphs in plaint and pleadings have to be seen in their entirety to ascertain its terms-For deciding application under Order VII, Rule 11, one has to look at plaint and decide whether it deserved to be rejected for ground raised-In instant case, it could not be held that no case made out for proceeding for a trial-Application made under Order VII, Rule 11 moved by respondent No. 1 stands rejected-View taken by Division Bench of High Court-Erroneous-Impugned judgment and order of Division Bench of High Court-Set aside-Singe Judge requested to hear and decide suit expeditiously.
