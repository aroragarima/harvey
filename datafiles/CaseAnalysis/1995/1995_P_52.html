Kashibai W/O Lachiram And Anr v Parwatibai W/O Lachiram And Ors

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1270; (1995) 6 SCC 213; AIR 1995 SCW 4631; 1995 (3) CCC 565; 1996 (2) CLT 197; 1996 (2) DMC 266; 1995 (2) HLR 712; JT 1995 (7) SC 48; 1996 (1) MLJ(SC) 45; 1995(5) SCALE 615; [1995] Supp4 S.C.R. 63

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Family & Personal - Civil Procedure - Women & Children - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Indian Succession Act, 1925, s. 63 - Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956, s. 7 - Suit for separate possession by partition - Defendants failed to establish that defendant 3 was taken in adoption - Plaintiff 1 was not a party to deed of adoption as she in her evidence has specifically stated that she did not sign deed of adoption nor she consented for such adoption - Alleged adoption being against consent of plaintiff 1, it was not valid by virtue of provisions of s. 7 of Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956 - Witness did not depose that deceased had signed alleged will in his presence - In absence of such evidence it is difficult to accept that execution of alleged Will was proved in accordance with law - Held, High Court committed a serious error in making observations that broad parameters of Nabi's evidence would show that deceased executed Will in his presence - Findings of HC are clearly based on assumptions and totally against weight of evidence on record - There was no justification for HC to interfere with well-reasoned findings of two courts below - Judgment and decree passed by HC set aside and Trial Court decree restored for separate possession by partition - Appeal allowed.
