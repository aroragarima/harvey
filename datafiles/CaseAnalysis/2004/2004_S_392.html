Harjinder Singh @ Bhola v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

27 July 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 503; (2004) 11 SCC 253; AIR 2004 SC 3962; 2004 CRLJ 3854; JT 2004 (6) SC 23; 2004 (3) RCR(Criminal) 836; 2004 (2) UC 1124

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 109, 302, 34 - HC upheld conviction ordered by Trial Court - Veracity of evidence - One witness, was given up by public prosecutor as being unnecessary - Not examined persons who gathered immediately after occurrence - Held, trial Court and HC not consider certain material aspects apparent from evidence and a mechanical acceptance of evidence of two chance witnesses whose evidence should have been evaluated with greater care and caution - Conviction and sentence set aside.
