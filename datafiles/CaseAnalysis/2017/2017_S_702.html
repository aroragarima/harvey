Swaraj Abhiyan v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

21 July 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 502; 2017 (6) MLJ 240

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Consumer Protection Act, 1986, Union Territory, Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act, 2005, Constitution of India, 1950, National Food Security Act, 2013, National Food Security Ordinance, 2013, National Food Security Bill, 2013, Andhra Pradesh Food Security Rules, 2017, Telangana Food Security Rules, 2015

Summary: Constitution - National Food Security Act, 2013, ss. 14, 15, 28, 29, 38, 40 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 256 - Distribution of Food Grains - Non compliance of Provisions - National Food Security Act was enacted in relation to distribution of entitled food grains or meals - Further the Act imposed obligation on State govts. to set up an integral grievance redressal mechanism - However, most of the State govts. failed to comply with said provision - Hence, instant writ petition.

Held, Central govt. might from time to time, give such directions, as it might consider necessary, to State govts. for the effective implementation of the provisions of the Act and State govts. should comply with such directions. Constitutional scheme invariably leads to the conclusion that at times those institutions meet and interact at various levels to achieve the cherished constitutional goal of co-operative federalism. It was not proper on part of States to ignore the plight of the common man in enforcing such important legislations, more so when such legislation is a welfare legislation. Mere schemes without any implementation are of no use. Meaningful dialogue between Center and State should resolve the issues which have emerged in the spirit of co-operative federalism. In instant case, various State govts. seem to be not so prompt in implementing the Act in its true letter and spirit. More breaches than compliance could be seen and which compelled to call the Chief secretaries of all states to appear before SC. Thus, a combined effort, both by Center and States, needs to be taken for effective implementation of the Act especially in the draught affected areas so as to save people from abject poverty and poor quality of life. States should take up this matter with much more seriousness and implement the Act in its true letter and spirit. Order accordingly.
