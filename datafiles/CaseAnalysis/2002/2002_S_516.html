Dilawar Balu Kurane v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

08 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Phukan, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 4; (2002) 2 SCC 135; (2002) SCC (Cr) 310; AIR 2002 SC 564; 2002 (1) CalLT(SC) 22; 2002 (1) CCR 61; 2002 (1) Crimes 243; 2002 (2) CRJ 284; 2002 CRLJ 980; 2002 (1) JCC 172B; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 490; JT 2002 (1) SC 6; 2002 (1) LLN 671; 2002 (1) RCR(Criminal) 451; 2002 (1) RCR(Criminal) 451B; 2002(1) SCALE 47; 2002 (1) SCJ 203; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 75; 2002 (1) SLT 99; 2002 (1) Supreme 55; 2002 (1) UC 289C

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: In exercising jurisdiction under Section 227 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, the Judge must consider the broad probabilities of the case, the total effect of the evidence and the documents produced before the court for the purpose of finding out if a grave suspicion against the accused has been made out or not.
