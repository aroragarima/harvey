Darshan Gupta v Radhika Gupta

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

J. S. KHEHAR, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 383; (2013) 9 SCC 1; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 85; 2013 (5) ALD(SC) 1; 2013(5) ALL MR 446; 2013 (100) ALR 701; 2013 (4) ALT(SC) 43; 2013 (4) AWC 4214; 2013 (4) Bom.C.R. 706; 2013 (3) CLT(SC) 16; 2013 (6) CTC 560; JT 2013 (10) SC 98; 2014 (1) MahLJ 99; 2013 (5) MLJ 319; 2014 (1) MPLJ 8; 2013 (172) PLR 769; 2013 (3) RCR(Civil) 663; 2013(7) SCALE 583; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 937

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Entitlement of, Decree of divorce

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 13(1)(iii)(ia), 9 - Dissolution of marriage - Decree of divorce - Entitlement of - Appellant husband filed petition before Family Court seeking dissolution of marriage u/cl. (ia) and (iii) of s. 13(1) of the Act and respondent wife filed another petition seeking restitution of conjugal rights u/s. 9 of the Act - Family Court dismissed petition filed by appellant and allowed petition of respondent and held that wife did not suffer from any mental disorder or unsoundness of mind - Respondent suffered from cognitive deficiency, which deficiency was acquired during her second pregnancy and that she could improve even further, had there been moral and emotional support by appellant - Appellant filed appeals before HC, which were dismissed - Hence instant appeals - Appellant contended that he was subjected to cruelty on account of intemperate behaviour of respondent and respondent was of incurable unsound mind, and suffered from mental disorder, that appellant could not be reasonably expected to live with her - Whether marriage of appellant and respondent was liable to be dissolved by decree of divorce -

Held, respondent suffered from mild to moderate cognitive deficiencies - Respondent was categorized by medical experts as individual of moderate intelligence - Material on record of revealed, that respondent would further benefit from neuro-psychological rehabilitation measures - Experts expressed that respondent exhibited normal and adequate emotional responses - Respondent right from beginning, fervently expressed desire to restore her relationship with her husband, and to live normal life in matrimonial relationship with appellant - Courts below were fully justified in recording, that behaviour of respondent could have easily been established through testimony of attendants who looked after respondent, as also, other staff, yet said witnesses were not produced by appellant, despite their availability - Appellant produced Dr. PW4 deposed that he did not observe any signs of aggressiveness in respondent - Since respondent was under care and treatment of PW4, he would have known of her erratic behaviour, if allegations of appellant were correct -In facts and circumstances of case, no decree of divorce should be granted on ground of irretrievable breakdown of marriage, for simple reason that breakdown was only from side of appellant - Respondent had consistently maintained, that she was intensely concerned with her future relationship with her husband and that her greatest and paramount desire was to rejoin her husband, and to live with him normally in matrimonial relationship once again - Appeal dismissed.
