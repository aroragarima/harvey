Oil & Natural Gas Corporation Ltd. v State Bank Of India, Overseas Branch, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

21 July 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 372; (2000) 6 SCC 385; AIR 2000 SC 2548; AIR 2000 SCW 2762; 2000 (4) Bom.C.R. 20; [2000] 102 Comp Cas 13; 2000 (4) CompLJ 290; JT 2000 (8) SC 141; 2001 (1) MLJ(SC) 18; 2000 (3) RCR(Civil) 606

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Exchange Control & FDI

Keywords: Exchange Control Manual

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908, O. 37, r. 3 - Recovery of money ( liquidated damages) for delay in completion of Contracted work - Appeal arises out of a suit filed to enforce a bank guarantee against the respondent under O. 37 CPC - Application seeking leave to defend the suit unconditionally being allowed - Order allowing application for leave filed by gaurantor bank challenged - Held, liable to be dismissed - In absence of plea relating to fraud the bank's defence would be untenable - Application dismissed. .
