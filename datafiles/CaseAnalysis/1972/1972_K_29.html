Khandu Sonu Dhobi and Another v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

15 February 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 107; (1972) 3 SCC 786; (1972) SCC (Cr) 854; AIR 1972 SC 958; 1972 CRLJ 593; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 510

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Misappropriation of Govt. money/property - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 218, 409 and 477 A - Whether a later act or willingness to do absolves accused of application of 'misappropriation' u/s. 403, IPC, 1860? - Held, it is no answer to a charge of criminal misappropriation that money was subsequently, after matter had been reported to high authorities, disbursed for purpose for which it had been entrusted - According to explanation 1 to s. 403 IPC, 1860 a dishonest misappropriation for a time only is 'misappropriation' within meaning of that section - It would not exonerate accused because willingness after matter had been reported to higher authorities could not efface or undo offence earlier committed by accused - Appeal dismissed.
