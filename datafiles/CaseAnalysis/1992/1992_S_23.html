Union Of India v Sampat Raj Dugar And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S. Ranganathan, Veeraswami Ramaswami

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 323; (1992) 2 SCC 66; AIR 1992 SC 1417; 1992 (38) ECC 83; 1992 (39) ECR 189; 1992 (58) E.L.T. 163; JT 1992 (3) SC 554; 1992(1) SCALE 167; [1992] 1 S.C.R. 269; 1993 (88) STC 176

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Collector Of Customs, Importation, Import Licence, Any Prohibition, Licensing Authority, Other Goods, Import and Export (Control) Act. 1947, Sea Customs Act 1962, Imports (Control) Order 1955

Summary: Customs - Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947, s. 3 - Imports Control Order, 1955, cl. 5(3)(ii) - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 111(d) and 111(o) - Import and Exports - Custom - Import of goods under valid licence - (A) Creation of fiction - By cl. 5(3)(ii) that licensee would be owner of goods from time of import - Import not contrary to law - Licensee abandoning goods - Exporter's title as to goods - (B) Confiscation of goods u/ss. 111(d) and 111(o), challenged - Held, exporter is not deprived of his title to imported goods - Goods not liable to be confiscated on date of their import as neither cl. (d) nor cl. (o) is attracted - Appeal dismissed.
