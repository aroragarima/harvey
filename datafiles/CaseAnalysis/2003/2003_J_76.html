Engineering Laghu Udyog Employees' Union v Judge, Labour Court and Industrial Tribunal and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, S.B. Sinha, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1252; (2003) 12 SCC 1; (2004) SCC (L&S) 974; AIR 2003 SC 4951; 2004 (5) ALD(SC) 18; 2004 (5) AWC 3894; 2004 (20) CLR 942; 2004 (2) ESC 213; 2004 (100) FLR 843; 2004 (2) JhrCR(SC) 117; 2004 (1) LLJ 1105; 2004 (1) LLN 1177; 2004 (3) MahLJ 331; 2004 (2) MLJ(SC) 169; 2004 (3) MPLJ 3; 2004(1) SCALE 666; 2003 (8) SLR 569; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 253; 2004 (2) Supreme 167

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Corporate - Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10, 11 and 33(2)(b) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Whether High Court rightly upheld the dismissal of the respondent and held that the charges levelled against Smt. Rukma are proved? - Where a domestic inquiry is found defective and the employer leads evidence before the Labour Court and subsequently the Labour Court gives its approval that the charges are proved, the order of termination would relate back to the date of original order of termination - If it is found ultimately by the Labour Court/ Industrial Tribunal that the employer had taken recourse to unfair labour practice or the order of termination has been passed malafide, it will be open to the Tribunal to pay compensation even in a case where ultimately charges are proved, despite holding that the order of termination is valid for the reason that principles of natural justice have not been complied with - No merit - Appeal dismissed.
