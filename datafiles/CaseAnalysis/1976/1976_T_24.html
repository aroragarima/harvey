Sone Valley Portland Cement Co. Ltd v The General, Mining Syndicate Pvt. Ltd

Supreme Court of India

24 August 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, Hans Raj Khanna, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 376; (1976) 3 SCC 852; AIR 1976 SC 2520; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 359

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Mines Or Minerals

Summary: Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1950, ss. 2(r), 4(a) and 10 - Mining lease - (A) Existing lease of mining rights on date of vesting - Sub-lessee from head lessee - Determination of his status and rights as a head lessee - (B) Interpretation of Statutes - Ascertainment of meaning of statute - Permissibility to resort to 'parliamentary exposition' revealed in later Act amending the earlier - Head lessee is not a tenure-holder and his rights as a head lessee of mines and minerals also did not cease - This principle has to be followed where a particular construction of the earlier Act will render the later incorporated Act ineffectual or otiose or inept - Appeal dismissed.
