Raman Tech. and Process Engg. Co. and Another v Solanki Traders

Supreme Court of India

20 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1579; (2008) 2 SCC 302; 2007 (7) AWC 6973; 2008 (2) MLJ(SC) 1058; 2008 (2) MPLJ 472; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 195; 2007(13) SCALE 419; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 409

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 38 r. 5 - Arrest or attachment before judgment - Plaintiff in a suit for recovery filed an application under O. 38 r. 5 of CPC for a direction to defendants to furnish security for the suit claim and in case of failure to do so, for attachment before judgment - Trial Court rejected the application on the ground that plaintiff had failed to make out a prima facie case - HC allowed the revision petition in view of fact that defendants were trying to remove the machinery from their business premises to another premises and directed defendants to furnish security for the suit amount - Hence, present appeal - Held, object of supplemental proceedings (applications for arrest or attachment before judgment, grant of temporary injunctions and appointment of receivers) is to prevent the ends of justice being defeated - Scheme of O. 38 of CPC and the use of the words 'to obstruct or delay the execution of any decree that may be passed against him' in r. 5 of CPC make it clear that before exercising the power under the said Rule, the court should be satisfied that there is a reasonable chance of a decree being passed in the suit against the defendant - A plaintiff should show, prima facie, that his claim is bona fide and valid and also satisfy the court that the defendant is about to remove or dispose of the whole or part of his property, with the intention of obstructing or delaying the execution of any decree that may be passed against him, before power is exercised under O. 38 r. 5 of CPC - Further, a defendant is not debarred from dealing with his property merely because a suit is filed or about to be filed against him - Shifting of business from one premises to another premises or removal of machinery to another premises by itself is not a ground for granting attachment before judgment - In the present case, particulars of the claim in the plaint were not specific - HC ignored the absence of prima facie case - HC ought not to have interfered with the order rejecting the application - Order of HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
