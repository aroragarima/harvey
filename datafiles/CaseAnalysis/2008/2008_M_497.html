Corporation Bank v Saraswati Abharansala and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1871; (2009) 1 SCC 540; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1097; 2009 (233) E.L.T. 3; JT 2008 (13) SC 113; 2008(15) SCALE 186; [2008] 16 S.C.R. 340; 2010 (18) S.T.R. 513; [2009] 19 VST 84

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Unjust Enrichment, Appeal allowed, Registered Dealer, Sales Tax Authorities, Rate Of Tax, Rate, Collected, Order In Appeal Or Revision, Bullion And Specie

Summary: Sales Tax - Plea for refund of the excess amount of sales tax collected from the respondent No.1 - Held, Sales tax is leviable on sale of goods - It must be collected by the dealer as an agent of the State at such rate as may be specified - Neither the State nor the agent is entitled to collect tax at a rate higher than specified - If the substantive provision of a statute provides for refund, the State ordinarily by a subordinate legislation could not have laid down that the tax paid even by mistake would not be refunded - If a tax has been paid in excess of the tax specified, save and except the cases involving the principle of 'unjust enrichment', excess tax realized must be refunded - Appeal allowed.
