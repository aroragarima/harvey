Ashoka Kumar Thakur v State Of Bihar And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

04 September 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1006; (1995) 5 SCC 403; (1995) SCC (L&S) 1248; AIR 1996 SC 75; AIR 1995 SCW 1731; 1995 (31) ATC 159; JT 1995 (6) SC 390; 1995 LabIC 2475; 1995(5) SCALE 115; 1995 (3) SCJ 705; 1996 (1) SLJ 155; 1995 (5) SLR 186; [1995] Supp3 S.C.R. 269; 1995 (2) UJ 727; 1995 (3) UPLBEC 1563

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Constitution Of India,1950, art. 16(4) - Reservation for backward class - Exclusion of 'creamy layer' - Criteria of - Held, Criteria laid down by Central Govt. in office Memorandum D/- 8-9-1993 - Approved - Criteria for identification of 'creamy layer' - Laid down by Bihar Reservation of Vacancies in Posts and Services (for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and other Backward Classes) (Amendment) Ordinance, 1995 and s. 3(b) of Uttar Pradesh Public Services Reservation of Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes and other Backward Classes Act, 1994 - Is violative of arts. 16(4) and 14 and against law laid down by Supreme Court in ' Mandal case'.
