Â State of Punjab v Messrs Jullunder Vegetables Syndicate

Supreme Court of India

01 November 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 230; AIR 1966 SC 1295; 1966 (68) PLR 435; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 457; 1966 (17) STC 326

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Sales Tax

Keywords: Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947, Dissolved Firm, C.P. And Berar Sales Tax Act, 1947, Orissa Sales Tax Rules, 1947, East Punjab General Sales Tax Act 1948, East Punjab General Sales Tax Rules 1949

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Income Tax & Direct Tax - Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947 - East Punjab General Sales Tax Act, 1948 - Income Tax Act, 1922 - Partnership Act, 1932 - C.P. and Berar Sales Tax Act, 1947 - Punjab General Sale, Tax Rules, 1949 - Orissa Sales Tax Rules, 1947 - Whether a firm could be assessed to sales-tax after it was dissolved? - Though under partnership law a firm is not a legal entity but only consists of individual partners, for tax law, income-tax as well as sales-tax, it is a legal entity - A firm was a separate assessable entity under Act and that there was no machinery provided under Act for assessing a firm after its dissolution in respect of its turnover of business before said dissolution - In interpreting a fiscal statute, Court cannot proceed to make good deficiencies, if there be any, in statute, it shall interpret statute as it stands and in case of doubt, it shall interpret it in a manner favorable to tax payer - Held, a firm once dissolved cannot be brought back into existence by any legal fiction for purposes of Sales Tax - Appeal dismissed.
