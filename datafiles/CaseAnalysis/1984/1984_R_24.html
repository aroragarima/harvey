Anandilal and Another v Ram Narain and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 May 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 324; (1984) 3 SCC 561; AIR 1984 SC 1383; 1984 (10) ALR 514; 1984(1) SCALE 943; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 806; 1984 (8) ShimLC 357; 1984 UJ 784

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice and Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963., s. 15, art. 136 - CPC, 1908, s. 48 - Limitation - (A) Whether a partial stay of execution of the decree staying sale of the attached property is within s. 15(1) of the Limitation Act, 1908 so as to entitle the decree-holder to claim exclusion of the period during which there was stay of sale but the property was to continue under attachment, for the purpose of computation of the period of limitation provided by s. 48 of the CPC, 1908 - Held, s.15 (1) was attracted - (B) Interpretation of Statutes - Beneficent construction - Provision of s.15(1) of the Limitation Act - Court liberally construing the beneficent provision held that word 'execution' in s. 15(1) embraces all the appropriate means by which a decree is enforced - (C) Repeal of s. 48 of CPC, 1908 - Effect of introduction of art. 136 in Limitation Act - Held, substance of s. 48 continues to be law - Appeal dismissed.
