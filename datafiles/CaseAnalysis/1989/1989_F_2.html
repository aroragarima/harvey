Owners And Parties Interested In M.V. "Valipero" Etc.Etc. v Fernandeo Lopez And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

19 September 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, M.N. Venkatachaliah, N.D. Ojha

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 232; (1989) 4 SCC 671; AIR 1989 SC 2206; 1990 (2) CalLT(SC) 1; 1989 (3) CompLJ 374; JT 1989 (4) SC 10; 1989(2) SCALE 642; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 187; [1989] supp1 S.C.R. 187

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Calcutta High Court Rules, 1914, ch. 224 - Is omission of witness signature on his deposition recorded on commission, a defect fatal to reception of deposition in evidence even when correctness and authenticity of deposition is undisputed? - Controversy on merits depends on correct construction of r. 4 of Ch. 22 applicable to original side - Requirement in r. 4 of witness signature on deposition has relevance to admission of witness of its correctness - Signature of witness is not a part of deposition and apart from acknowledging correctness of his deposition on deposition itself, it is not essential for any other purpose in this context - There is substantial compliance of this directory requirement where correctness and authenticity of deposition is undisputed - Compliance can be had of this requirement even by subsequent admission of correctness of deposition by witness, in case of dispute - Appeal allowed.
