Commissioner Of Income-Tax, Bangalore v M/S. R. Hanumanthappa And Son

Supreme Court of India

10 August 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 259; (1971) 3 SCC 592; AIR 1972 SC 175; 1971 (37) CutLT 91; [1971] 82 ITR 880; [1972] 1 S.C.R. 94; 1971 UJ 750

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Mysore Income Tax Act, 1923, ss.25(3),34,66 (2) - Partnership - Assessment - Partition - Discontinuance - Exemption - Succession - 'H' was assessed in status of Hindu undivided family and all family assets including cotton business were divided after disruption of joint family - After disruption of family partnership firm was formed on 22-11-1948, partners being three coparceners of Hindu Undivided Family - Business assets and liabilities falling to each coparcener's share were entered in their personal accounts and then retransferred to partnership firm as contribution of capital with exception of few trade debts - Assessment was sought to be reopened under provisions of s.34 of 1923 Act and additional demand of Rs. 2,25,942/- was raised by order of IO dt. 23-9-1959 - Before IO an exemption claimed on behalf of disrupted Hindu Undivided Family u/s.25 (3) of 1923 Act - HC answered in favour of assessee and against Revenue - Assessee was being assessed under 1922 Act and it could certainly claim benefit of s.25 (3) of 1923 Act - Whether assessee was entitled to exemption u/s. 25(3) of 1923 Act - Held, assessee had to pay tax in respect of income of same year both under earlier Statute and under later enactment - Removing this hardship and saving assessee from double taxation that provision was made in u/s.25 (3) of new Act to give relief to assessee to extent possible - Business was never discontinued so as to attract provision of S. 25 (3) of 1923 Act - Respondent assessee on the integrity of business carried on by Hindu Undivided Family broken by disruption of family and claimed that business of family deemed to totally ceased or discontinued on such disruption - Appeal allowed.
