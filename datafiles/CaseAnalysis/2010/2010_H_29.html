Gurbux Singh v Harminder Kaur

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 839; AIR 2011 SC 114; 2011 (1) AWC 735; 2011 (1) Bom.C.R. 235; JT 2010 (11) SC 58; 2011 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1212; 2010 (8) MLJ(SC) 1011; 2011 (161) PLR 130; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 822; 2011 (1) RLW 829; 2010(10) SCALE 659; [2010] 12 S.C.R. 275

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 13 - Decree of divorce - Cruelty - Appellant-husband filed a petition u/s. 13 of the Act for a decree of divorce on ground of cruelty - Trial Court held that the appellant-husband failed to substantiate the allegations of 'cruelty' and divorce petition was dismissed - Appeal filed thereagainst was also dismissed by HC - Hence, present appeal - Whether appellant-husband made out a case for divorce on the ground of 'cruelty' by the respondent-wife? - Held, it is essential for the appellant, who claims relief, to prove that a particular/part of conduct or behaviour resulted in cruelty to him - No prior assumptions can be made in such matters - Hence, it cannot be assumed that a particular conduct will, under all circumstances, amount to cruelty, vis-'-vis the other party - Aggrieved party has to make a specific case that the conduct of which exception is taken amounts to cruelty - Further, married life should be assessed as a whole and a few isolated instances over certain period will not amount to cruelty - Ill-conduct must be precedent for a lengthy period where the relationship has deteriorated to an extent that because of the acts and behaviour of a spouse, one party finds it extremely difficult to live with the other party no longer, may amount to mental cruelty - Sustained unjustifiable and reprehensible conduct affecting physical and mental health of the other spouse may lead to mental cruelty - However, making certain statements on the spur of the moment and expressing certain displeasure about the behaviour of elders may not be characterized as cruelty - Mere trivial irritations, quarrels, normal wear and tear of married life which happens in day to day life in all families would not be adequate for grant of divorce on the ground of cruelty - In the present, there was no such complaint by the appellant - Appellant projected few instances in which, according to him, the respondent abused his parents - On the basis of such instances, marriage cannot be dissolved - Appeal dismissed.
