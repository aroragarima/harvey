Jeyar Consultant and Investment Private Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

01 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 251; (2015) 7 SCC 705; [2015] 373 ITR 87; JT 2015 (4) SC 109; 2015 (3) MLJ 623; 2015(4) SCALE 410; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 979; [2015] 231 TAXMAN 273

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Finance (No.2) Bill, 1991

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 80HHC, 80HHC(3) - Export Business - Losses - Deductions - Appellant company was engaged in the business of export of marine products and also financial consultancy and trading in equity shares - Total business of appellant did not consist purely of exports but includes business within the country as well - Tribunal while dealing with the assessments of the appellant took the view that the deduction admissible to the appellant u/s. 80HHC is nil - HC affirmed the decision of Tribunal - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, from the scheme of s. 80HHC, it is clear that deduction is to be provided under sub-s. (1) thereof which is 'in respect of profits retained for export business'. Therefore, in the first instance, it has to be satisfied that there are profits from the export business. In the present case, since there are losses in the export business of appellant, question of providing deduction u/s. 80HHC does not arise and as a consequence, there is no question of computation of any such deduction in the manner provided under sub-s. (3). Appeal dismissed.
