Maharashtra Electricity Regulatory Commission v Reliance Energy Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

14 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1589; (2007) 8 SCC 381; AIR 2008 SC 976; 2007 (4) AWC 3662; 2008 (1) Bom.C.R. 738; JT 2007 (10) SC 365; 2007(10) SCALE 279; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 9

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Electricity Act, 2003, ss. 42(5) and 128(6) - Power of the Commission and to what extent the Commission can issue directions - Maharashtra Electricity Regulatory Commission (appellant-commission) issued notice to all its licensees/distribution companies in Maharashtra and made an inquiry from them with regard to raising of the bills by the said licensees/distribution companies on the basis other than the actual meter reading for the relevant period - Reply was filed by all the licensees/distribution companies - Thereafter, Commission issued direction that supplementary/amendment bills issued to be withdrawn and any amounts collected should be refunded to the concerned consumers - On appeal, Appellate Authority set aside the orders passed by the Commission and directed that each consumer should approach the forum created u/s. 42(5) of the Act for the individual grievances - Hence, present appeal u/s. 125 of the Act - Respondent-licensees/distribution companies submitted that the Commission has no power to issue such a direction - Held, Commission is empowered to grant licence and lay down the conditions of licence and to frame regulations and to see that the same are properly enforced and also power to enforce the conditions of licence under sub-section (6) of s. 128 - Further, Commission has power to issue a general direction to licencees that they should abide by conditions of the licence issued by them and charge only as per the tariff fixed under the Act - However, a blanket direction to refund the amounts collected by the licensees/distribution companies given by the Commission was not warranted - It would be proper to direct that all the licensees/distribution companies in the State of Maharashtra issue a general public notice in daily newspapers requiring their respective consumers to make their representations for redressal of their grievances in respect of the supplementary/amended bills - Licensees/distribution companies shall decide the individual cases received by them after giving a fair opportunity of hearing to the consumers - Appeal disposed of.
