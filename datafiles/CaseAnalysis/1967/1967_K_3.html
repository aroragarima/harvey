Syndicate Bank Limited v K. R. V. Bhat

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 200; AIR 1968 SC 231; 1968 CRLJ 1030; 1968 (16) FLR 39; 1968 LabIC 207; 1967 (2) LLJ 745; 1968 (2) MLJ(SC) 23; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 327

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 33 A and 37 - Whether in facts of case respondent was not a 'Workman' concerned with dispute pending before Industrial Tribunal ? - An order of discharge or dismissal can be passed only once in this case, order of dismissal is one passed, by Managing Director and not one passed by Board of Directors - No doubt, either by virtue of Standing Orders, or by virtue of a contract of service, a right of appeal may be given to a workman concerned, to challenge an order of dismissal - But appellate authority only considers whether order of dismissal has to be sustained, or whether it requires modification - Therefore, there is no question of appellate authority passing, again, an order of dismissal - Held, an employee once dismissed ceases to be a workman even if such order of dismissal is pending before appellate authority - Appeal allowed.
