Khemraj v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 277; (1976) SCC (Cr) 3; (1976) 1 SCC 385; AIR 1976 SC 173; 1976 CRLJ 192; 1976 CrLR(SC) 37; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 753; 1976 UJ 74

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, 1946 - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 417(2) - Investigation by Delhi Special Police - Prosecution launched - Acquitted - Appeal against acquittal by State Govt. - Whether Act of 1946 bars jurisdiction of state Govt. to direct presentation of appeals against acquittal? - Held, that Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, 1946, does not bar jurisdiction of State Govt. to direct presentation of appeals against acquittal - No objection can be taken about competency of appeal against acquittal in a case investigated by Establishment, being filed by Public Prosecutor under direction of State Govt. without impleading it as a party - Appeal dismissed.
