Supreme Court of India

10 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam, Ranjana Prakash Desai, Sharad Arvind Bobde, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 17; (2014) 3 SCC 92; 2014 (1) ADJ 727; AIR 2014 SC 1400; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 801; 2014 CRLJ 1118; JT 2014 (1) SC 412; 2014 (1) KLT 336; 2014 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 225; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 623; 2014 (1) RLW 727; 2014(1) SCALE 241; [2014] 2 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.319 - Summoning of a person as accused - During course of criminal inquiry or trial - Legality - Reference before SC arose out of a variety of views expressed by itself and several HCs of the country on the scope and extent of the powers of the courts under the criminal justice system to arraign any person as an accused during the course of inquiry or trial as contemplated u/s.319 of CrPC - (A) What would be the stage at which power u/s.319 CrPC could be exercised - Held, Constitution Bench of SC in one case had held that after committal, cognizance of an offence could be taken against a person not named as an accused but against whom materials were available from the papers filed by the police after completion of investigation - Such cognizance could be taken u/s.193 CrPC and the Sessions Judge should not wait till 'evidence' u/s.319 CrPC becomes available for summoning an additional accused - Dharam Pal & Ors. v. State of Haryana & Anr., 2004 Indlaw SC 1938, relied on - (B) Whether the word 'evidence' used in s.319(1) CrPC could only mean evidence tested by cross-examination or the court could exercise the power under the said provision even on the basis of the statement made in the examination-in-chief of the witness concerned - Held, considering the fact that u/s.319 CrPC a person against whom material was disclosed was only summoned to face the trial and in such an event u/s.319(4) CrPC the proceeding against such person was to commence from the stage of taking of cognizance, the Court need not wait for the evidence against the accused proposed to be summoned to be tested by cross-examination - (C) Whether the word 'evidence' used in s.319(1) CrPC was used in a comprehensive sense and included the evidence collected during investigation or the word 'evidence' was limited to the evidence recorded during trial - Held, s.319 CrPC, significantly, used two expressions that have to be taken note of i.e. (1) Inquiry (2) Trial - As a trial commences after framing of charge, an inquiry could only be understood to be a pre-trial inquiry - Inquiries u/ss.200, 201 and 202 CrPC; and u/s.398 CrPC were species of the inquiry contemplated by s.319 CrPC - Materials coming before the Court in course of such enquiries could be used for corroboration of the evidence recorded in the court after the trial commences, for the exercise of power u/s.319 CrPC, and also to add an accused whose name was shown in chargesheet - Thus, word 'evidence' in s.319 CrPC had to be broadly understood and not literally i.e. as evidence brought during a trial - (D) What was the nature of the satisfaction required to invoke the power u/s.319 CrPC to arraign an accused - Whether the power u/s.319 (1) CrPC could be exercised only if the court was satisfied that the accused summoned would in all likelihood be convicted - Held, though u/s.319(4)(b) CrPC the accused subsequently impleaded was to be treated as if he had been an accused when the Court initially took cognizance of the offence, the degree of satisfaction that would be required for summoning a person u/s.319 CrPC would be the same as for framing a charge - Difference in the degree of satisfaction for summoning the original accused and a subsequent accused was on account of the fact that the trial might have already commenced against the original accused and it was in the course of such trial that materials were disclosed against the newly summoned accused - Fresh summoning of an accused would result in delay of the trial - Therefore the degree of satisfaction for summoning the accused (original and subsequent) had to be different - (E) Did the power u/s.319 CrPC could extend to persons not named in the FIR or named in the FIR but not charge sheeted or who was discharged - Held, person not named in the FIR or a person though named in the FIR but not charge sheeted or a person who was discharged could be summoned u/s.319 CrPC provided from the evidence it appeared that such person could be tried along with the accused already facing trial - However, in so far as an accused who was discharged was concerned the requirement of ss.300 and 398 CrPC had to be complied with before he could be summoned afresh - Order accordingly.
