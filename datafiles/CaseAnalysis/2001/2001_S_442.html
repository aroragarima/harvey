Rajeev Chaudhary v State (Nct) of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

04 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20136; (2001) 5 SCC 34; (2001) SCC (Cr) 819; AIR 2001 SC 2369; 2001 (2) CCR 196; 2001 CRLJ 2941; 2001 (91) DLT 340; 2001 (3) G.L.R. 2090; 2001 (2) JCC 61; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 248; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 754; 2001(3) SCALE 671; 2001 (3) SCJ 264; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 508; 2001 (4) SLT 65; 2001 (4) Supreme 8; 2001 (2) UC 155

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1963

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 167(2)(a) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 386 and 502 - Whether there is difference in interpretation and construction of the expression 'offence punishable with imprisonment for a term of not less than ten years' and 'imprisonment which may extend to ten years'? - Held, the expression 'not less than' would mean that imprisonment should be 10 years or more and would cover only those offences for which punishment could be imprisonment for a clear period of 10 years or more - The expression that 'term which may extend to 10 years' means that imprisonment can be for a clear period of 10 years or less - It could not be said that minimum sentence would be 10 years or more - Appeal dismissed.
