Â Edwingson Bareh v State of Assam and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, P. Satyanarayana Raju

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 202; AIR 1966 SC 1220; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 770

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure, Legislative Power, Scheduled Tribes, Schedules, Constitution (Seventh Amendment) Act, 1956, Assam (Alteration Of Boundaries) Act, 1951, Naga Hills-Tuensang Area Act, 1957, State of Nagaland Act, 1962, Lushai Hills District (Change of Name) Act, 1954

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 244(2) and 368, Sch.7 - Appellant challenged constitutional validity of this notification issued by Governor of Assam to create a new autonomous District - Whether change in provisions of sch. 6 be validly introduced by Governor in exercise of powers conferred on him by para. 1 (3) ? - Held, para. 21 shows that Parliament has undoubtedly power to make any change in any of provisions contained in sch. 6, and part of this wide power has, however, been conferred on Governor, because Constitution makers apparently thought that Parliament need not be called upon to exercise its own power for bringing about comparatively smaller and minor changes in Part A of Table, and it accordingly decided to confer appropriate power on Governor to take action in that behalf - Appeal dismissed.
