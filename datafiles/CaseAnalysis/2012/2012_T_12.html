Bangalore Development Authority v The Air Craft Employees Cooperative Society Ltd. And Others

Supreme Court of India

24 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 512; (2012) 3 SCC 442; JT 2012 (1) SC 587; 2012 (3) MLJ(SC) 84; [2012] 4 S.C.R. 881

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Municipal Corporation Act, Constitutionality, Schedules, Andhra Pradesh (Agricultural Produce and Livestock) Markets Act, 1966, City of Bangalore Municipal Corporation Act, 1949, City of Bangalore Improvement Act, 1945, Haryana Rural Development Act, 1986, Kerala Rectified Spirit Rules, 1972, Bangalore Water Supply And Sewerage Act, 1964, Mysore Town and Country Planning Act, 1961, the Town Planning Act, Cauvery Water Supply Scheme, Cauvery Scheme

Summary: Administration- Constitution - Land & Property - Bangalore Development Authority Act, 1976, ss. 14, 15,16, 2(bb), 2(c), 2(j), 28A, 28B, 28C, 32, 32(1), 32(2),32(3), 32(4), 32(5), 32(5A), 5A, 65, 65B, 67,67(1)(a),67(1)(b) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 265, 300A - Mysore Town and Country Planning Act, 1961,ss. 1(c), 2(7), 4, 4(c), 4A(1),4A(3), 81B - Town Planning - Conditional Layout - Development Tax - Notification - Validity - Sustainability - With formation of new State, it was considered necessary to have a uniform law for planned growth of land use and development and for making and execution of town planning schemes and necessary consequential changes were made in nomenclature of various enactments including Town Planning Act - Provisions enabled State Govt. to issue notification and declare any area in State to be a local planning area for purposes of Act and constitute 'Planning Authority' having jurisdiction over that area, therefore, it was considered appropriate that a single authority like DDA should be set up for city and areas adjacent thereto which, in due course, would become part of city - State Govt. issued notification u/s. 2(c) of 1976, Act, specifying villages indicated within boundaries indicated in second Schedule to Notification to be areas for purposes of that clause - There was increase in population of city between 1970 and 1980, available civic amenities like roads, water supply system and supply of electricity were stretched to their limit - State Govt. further issued order and directed BDA to make a grant of Rs. 30 crores to BWSSB to be paid in installments from 1987-88 to 1989-90 by loading an extra amount as water supply component at rate of Rs. 10,000/- on an average per site for all layouts to be formed thereafter - BDA started collecting Rs.10,000/- per site, later levy under Cauvery Scheme was increased to Rs.1 lac per acre, it was realised that BDA had not been able to develop and distribute sites as expected, therefore, a proposal was submitted by Commissioner, BDA to State Govt. that contribution towards Cauvery Scheme may be distributed among those applying for change of land use and private layouts to be developed by house building societies and on major housing projects - State Govt. accepted suggestion of BDA and passed order for levy of charges under Cauvery Scheme at rate of Rs.2 lacs per acre - BDA passed Resolution for levy of charges at different rates on change of land use in different areas and Rs.1 lac per acre on layouts of housing societies and private lands as also sites formed by itself - Respondent submitted application for approval of layout in respect of 324 acres 30 guntas land which was considered in BDA's meeting and was approved subject to various conditions including payment of Rs.2 lacs per acre towards Cauvery Scheme and Rs.1 lac as Ring Road surcharge - Respondent challenged conditional sanction of its layout in Writ Petition and prayed for quashing demand of Rs.2 lacs per acre towards Cauvery Scheme and Rs.1 lac as Ring Road surcharge - During pendency of Writ Petition State legislature amended 1976 Act by Act. No.17/1994 and inserted sub-section (5A) in s. 32 authorizing BDA to demand sums in addition to those referred - Respondent amended writ petition and challenged Constitutional validity of newly inserted sub-section by asserting that provision was discriminatory and violative of art. 14 of Constitution and also that it gave unbridled and uncanalized power to BDA to demand additional sums for different schemes - Respondent further challenged conditional approval of civil work in another Writ Petition on ground that 1976 Act does not authorize such levies and that legislature had not laid down any guideline for creating such demand from private House Building Societies - DB considered question whether Notification dt. 1-3-1988 issued u/s. 2(c) of 1976, Act was invalid because names of villages or specified area had not been notified or published in the Official Gazette and whether in absence of such notification, villages in which societies had formed layouts could not be treated as part of BM Area - DB, concluded that demand made by BDA with support of s. 32(5A) was illegal and without jurisdiction and accordingly allowed writ petitions - Aggrieved instant appeal - Appellant contended that s. 32(5A) was not violative of art. 14 of Constitution inasmuch as it did not operate unequally qua allottees of sites of layouts prepared by house building societies on one hand and BDA layouts on other hand - Whether DB, was justified in holding that Act, enacted by State was unconstitutional - Held, under 1976 Act, BDA was obliged to provide different types of amenities to population of BMA including allottees of sites in layouts prepared by house building societies, it was quite possible that they may not be direct beneficiaries of one or other amenities made available by BDA, but this could not detract from fact that they would certainly be benefited by construction of Outer Ring Road and Intermediate Ring Road, Mass Rapid Transport System, etc., they would also be ultimate beneficiaries of Cauvery Scheme because availability of additional 270 MLD water to State would enable BWSSB to spare water for private layouts, it was neither pleaded case of respondents nor it had been argued that allottees of sites in layouts to be developed by private societies would not get benefit of amenities provided by BDA - Thus, charges demanded by BDA u/s. 32(5A) could not be termed as tax and declared unconstitutional on ground that same were not sanctioned by law enacted by competent legislature - Appeals allowed.
