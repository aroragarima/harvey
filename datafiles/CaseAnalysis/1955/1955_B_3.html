Baladin and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

18 October 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 106; AIR 1956 SC 181; 1956 CRLJ 345

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 134(1)(b) and 134(1)(c) - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 87 and 88 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 21, 120-B, 142, 148, 149, 201, 302, 325 and 452 - Murder - Unlawful assembly - Conspiracy by appellants to kill deceased, six male refugees - (A) Special leave to appeal to Supreme Court - Grant of - Art. 134(1)(a) and (b) lay down two conditions which confer a right of appeal to Supreme Court - Normally there is no right of appeal in any other type of case - Only exception is (c) where a right is conferred (an appeal 'shall, lie) if High Court 'certifies' that case is a fit one for appeal - It indicates that HC must bring its mind to bear on question and as in all cases of judicial orders and certificates, reasons for order must be apparent on face of order itself - It is not enough to say 'leave to appeal is given' and no more because an appeal is not allowed in ordinary way when conditions (a) and (b) are not satisfied - Held, in present case, where HC has merely said 'Leave to appeal to Supreme Court is granted' - It is impossible to gather what induced HC to grant this leave or what points of outstanding importance that require to be settled are, in opinion of HC, involved - Learned Judges have not even certified that this is a fit case for appeal - This omission as indicative of fact that HC did not realize responsibility that is cast upon it by art. 134(1)(C) and did not realize that its discretion has to be 'judicially' and not mechanically exercised - Appeal does not lie under sub-art. (c) - (B) Unlawful assembly - Conviction being member of that assembly - When justified? - Held, it is well settled that mere presence in an assembly does not make such a person a member of an unlawful assembly unless it is shown that he had done something or omitted to do something which would make him a member of an unlawful assembly, or unless case falls u/s. 142, IPC, 1860 - Appeal partly allowed.
