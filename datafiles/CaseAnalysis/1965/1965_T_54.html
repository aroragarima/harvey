P. Mohammed Meera Lebbai v Thirumalaya Gounder Ramaswamy Gounder and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, R.S. Bachawat, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 444; AIR 1966 SC 430; 1966 KerLJ 456; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 574

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure -Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 96 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 133 cl.(iii) and 136 - Travancore - Cochin High Court Act, 1949, s. 20 - (A) Appellant's suit for recovery of possession of certain property and for mesne profits - Whether appeal could be heard and disposed of by a single judge of HC? - Held, yes it can be - Right of appeal to HC - Does not mean right to be heard by specific number of judges - Appeal filed when Travancore Cochin High Court Act 5 of 1125. M. E. was in force - Repealed - Appellant had no vested right to be heard by Division Bench or to a further appeal to Supreme Court - (1) Whether Appellant had a vested right to be heard by Division Bench or to a further appeal to Supreme Court? - Further held, no - Travancore Cochin Act of 1125 ME did not purport to confer a right of appeal on parties, but merely dealt with procedural matters, matters which are dealt with by several HC's under Letters Patent - Therefore, claim for a vested right of appeal could not be entertained - No right u/art. 133 can be said to vest in him - Appeal dismissed.
