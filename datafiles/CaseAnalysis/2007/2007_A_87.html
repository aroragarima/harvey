State Of Karnataka v Ameer Jan

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 922; (2007) 1 SCC (Cr) 130; (2007) 11 SCC 273; AIR 2008 SC 108; 2008 CRLJ 347; JT 2007 (11) SC 183; 2008 (1) KarLJ 33; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 375; 2007(11) SCALE 207; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 1105

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Service; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Non-application Of Mind

Summary: Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - Interpretation and/ or application of the provisions of s. 19 - Order of sanction was issued by the Commissioner of Stamps solely relying on or on the basis of a purported report issued by the Inspector General of Police - High Court order of sanction being illegal, the judgment of conviction could not be sustained - Appeal against - Held, order granting sanction must be demonstrative of the fact that there had been proper application of mind on the part of the sanctioning authority - It was found that except the report, no other record was made available before the sanctioning authority - Order of sanction also stated so - PW-8 also did not have the occasion to consider the records except the purported report - Impugned judgment does not suffer from any legal infirmity - Appeal dismissed.
