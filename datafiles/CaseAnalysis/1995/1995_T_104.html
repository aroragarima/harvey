Uppari Venkataswamy and Others v The Public Prosecutor,High Court Of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

08 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 791; (1996) 7 SCC 232; (1996) SCC (Cr) 284; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 217; 1996 (2) CCR 104; 1995 (4) Crimes 799; JT 1995 (9) SC 33; 1995(7) SCALE 147; 1996 (1) SCJ 588

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970, s. 2 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 379 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 149, 302 and 307 - Appellants convicted for offence u/s. 302 r/w 149 and s. 307 and also u/ss. 3 and 5 of Explosive Substances Act - Whether conviction and sentence as imposed by High Court on accused stand justified in light of evidence on record? - Appellant court not entitled to interfere unless view of acquitting court is found to be perverse or infirm or palpably erroneous - Conduct of witnesses do not in any way affect core of their evidence which remains well sustained on record - Presence of witnesses on spot was not in way doubtful - Evidence of both witnesses are quite reliable and they had actually witnessed murderous attack on deceased 1 and 2 by accused party - Accused formed unlawful assembly and gathered together on spot to make murderous attack on occupants - Held, appellants rightly convicted for offence u/ss. 302 and 307 - Appeal dismissed.
