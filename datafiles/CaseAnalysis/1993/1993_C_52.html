Chinta Pulla Reddy and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1030; (1993) Supp3 SCC 134; (1993) SCC (Cr) 875; AIR 1993 SC 1899; 1993 (1) Crimes 1136; 1993 CRLJ 2246; 1993 CRLR 664; JT 1993 (3) SC 633; 1993(2) SCALE 354

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution -Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 300 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - (A) Special leave appeal against concurrent findings - Scope for reappreciation of evidence - (B) Murder trial - Evidence - Appreciation of - Out of six, two accused given benefit of doubt on certain ground - Same grounds also available so far as two more accused - No distinguishing feature - Participation of these accused in crime not established beyond reasonable doubt - If they are entitled to benefit of doubt - (C) Murder trial - Common intention - No specific injury caused by one out of six accused persons - Whether his conviction u/ss. 300/34 still justified - Held, (A) Since High Court has acquitted two out of six accused Supreme Court could appreciate material evidence in case - (B) These accused also held entitled to benefit of doubt - (C) Still justified - Appeal partly allowed.
