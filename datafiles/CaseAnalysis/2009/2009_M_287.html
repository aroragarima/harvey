Mohabbat and Others v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

03 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 823; (2009) 13 SCC 630; AIR 2009 SC 1893; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 1817; 2009 CRLJ 1752; 2009 (1) JCC 790; JT 2009 (2) SC 169; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 952; 2009(2) SCALE 133; [2009] 1 S.C.R. 883

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder reference - Appellant allegedly murdered the deceased by giving sword blows - Trial Court convicted appellant for murder - Appellants challenged the conviction before HC - HC dismissed appellants' appeals - Whether evidences of eye-witnesses can be discarded for the reason that eye-witnesses were the family members of the deceased - Held, merely because the eye-witnesses are family members their evidence cannot per se be discarded - Mere statement that being relatives of the deceased they are likely to falsely implicate the accused cannot be a ground to discard the evidence which is otherwise cogent and credible - Evidence of PW6 eye-witness was unshaked - Prosecution succeeded in establishing their case against 1st appellant - Appeal partly allowed.
