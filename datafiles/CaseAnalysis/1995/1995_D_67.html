State of Maharashtra v Digambar

Supreme Court of India

12 May 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Venkatachala, A.M. Ahmadi, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 301; (1995) 4 SCC 683; AIR 1995 SC 1991; AIR 1995 SCW 3116; JT 1995 (9) SC 310; 1995 (2) RentLR 190; 1995(4) SCALE 98; [1995] Supp1 S.C.R. 492

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice and Procedure - Constitution, arts. 136 and 226 - Limitation Act, 1908 - (A) Non-consideration of disentitlement to direction, order or writ for granting relief to citizen or any other person - Due to undue delay or laches - Effect on such direction, order or writ - (B) Whethre non-filing of appeals by State in some similar matters or rejection of some SLP in limine by SC put bar on SLP - (C) Relief granted by HC in judgement in appeal found to be unsustainable in appeal against it - Effect of - Held, such direction, order or writ, would be unsustainable - By itself, not a bar against State in filing SLP in other similar matters - All those judgments rendered by HC following judgment under appeal, would be liable to be interfered with and set aside - Appeal allowed.
