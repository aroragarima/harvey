Supreme Court of India

11 December 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, A.K. Sarkar, Raghubar Dayal, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 453; AIR 1964 SC 758; [1963] 48 ITR 222; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 949; [1963] Supp2 S.C.R. 949

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 4 (3) (vii) - Receipt of Renumeration - Recurring Income - Casual Income - Profits or Gains - Assessees/appellant were not employees of the Company and were not in receipt of remuneration - Payment was spaced over three years to appellant and Income Tax charged treating as recurring income and HC allowed appeal in favor of revenue/respondent - Hence, instant appeal - Whether receipt can be described u/s. 4 (3) (vii) of the Act as of a casual and nonrecurring nature and not by way of addition to remuneration of an employee - Held, Assessees were not employees of the Company and were not in receipt of remuneration - After termination of agreement they were to work as regular leap dealers if they cared - Receipt may only be described as a receipt of a casual and non-recurring nature if it were income profits or gains - Payment was spaced over three years did not make this a recurring not being income profits or gains u/s. 4 (3) (vii) has no application - Appeal allowed.
