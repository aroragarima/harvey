Sudhakar Vithal Kumbhare v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, S.B. Sinha, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1392; (2004) 9 SCC 481; AIR 2004 SC 1036; 2004 (54) ALR 602; 2003(10) SCALE 344; 2003 (1) SCW 6681; 2004 (1) SLR 407; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 746

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 342 - Reversion order - Appellant filed a petition u/art. 226 of Constitution challenging order of reversion - High Court dismissed petition - Whether appellant being a Scheduled Tribe known as Halba/Halbi which stands recognized both in State of Madhya Pradesh as well as in State of Maharashtra having their origin in Chhindwara region, a part of which, on States' reorganization, has come to State of Maharashtra, was entitled to benefits of reservation despite bifurcation thereof in terms of States' Reorganization Act? - Held, in view of fact that appellant's case was not referred to appropriate Committee, it will be open to Maharashtra State to refer matter to Scrutiny Committee for verifying eligibility of appellant - It is directed that appellant shall be reinstated forthwith as Asst. Engineer and shall continue to hold said post till matter is decided by Committee - Thus, judgment and order under challenge deserves to be set aside - Appeal is allowed.
