Pancham Chand and Others v State of Himachal Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 339; (2008) 7 SCC 117; 2008 (2) ACC 391; AIR 2008 SC 1888; 2008 (2) AWC 1109; JT 2008 (3) SC 498; 2008 (5) MahLJ 525; 2008 (4) MLJ(SC) 265; 2008 (4) MPLJ 99; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 680; 2008(3) SCALE 378; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 888; 2008 (2) UC 1082

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Permit Holder, Public Service Vehicle, Rta.

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Whether Chief Minister of a State has any role to play in a matter of grant of permit of a Stage Carriage Permit in terms of the provisions of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988? - Held, State, although, have a general control but such control must be exercised strictly in terms of art. 162 of the Constitution of India - Statutory authorities are bound to act in terms thereof, but per se the same does not authorize any Minister including the Chief Minister to Act in derogation of the statutory provisions - Constitution of India does not envisage functioning of the Government through the Chief Minister alone - Regional Transport Authority being a statutory body is bound to act strictly in terms of the provisions thereof - It cannot act in derogation of the powers conferred upon it - Appeal allowed.
