Major General Kapil Mehra and others v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

17 October 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 725; (2015) 2 SCC 262; 2015 (1) ALD(SC) 85; 2015 (2) AWC 2132; 2014 (4) CLT(SC) 225; JT 2014 (12) SC 119; 2015 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 964; 2015 (4) MahLJ 40; 2015 (1) MLJ 341; 2015 (3) MPLJ 3; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 4; 2014(12) SCALE 248

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Valuation Of Land

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 28, 34 - Land acquisition - Quantum of compensation - Challenge - Appellants' land was acquired - Award of compensation was passed - Appellant filed reference seeking enhanced compensation - Reference court rejected appellants' reference - On appeal, HC enhanced compensation - Appellants filed application seeking award with respect to court fee paid and interest on enhanced compensation - HC partly allowed application granting proportionate costs of Rs. 20,000 to appellant - Being dissatisfied with quantum, appellants filed instant appeal.

Held, appellants relied on lease deeds of small plots executed by respondent authority, are in the same vicinity of the acquired land and HC had taken the same as comparable sales. The size of the plots covered in the exemplars are smaller. No error found in the approach of the HC taking lease deed as comparable sales for fixation of market value. The HC was justified in taking the average of the said four exemplars and approach adopted by HC in averaging the sale prices of lease deeds cannot be said to be perverse. Having regard to the period of lease deeds and the date of issuance of s. 4 notification, addition of 20% is to be added for arriving at the value of 'freehold' property. Having regard to the extent of the land acquired and the development in and around acquired area, it is appropriate to make 35% deduction towards utilization of the land area in the layout for roads, drains, parks, playgrounds and civic amenities. So far as the expenditure for development of the large extent of land into a developed area by construction of proper roads, underground drainage, sewerage and erection of electricity lines, it is appropriate to make further deduction of 25%. On the enhanced compensation, HC ordered payment of interest at the rate of 9% from notification date to award date and thereafter at the rate of 15% per annum till the date of payment. Since the statutory interest u/s. 34 and also the interest in terms of s. 28 of Act had been awarded to the appellants, there is no merit in the grievance of the appellants as to the payment of interest. Further, HC rightly awarded proportionate costs and accordingly directed payment of such proportionate costs of over and above Rs.20,000/- as originally ordered. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - The market value is determined with reference to the open market sale of comparable land in the neighbourhood, by a willing seller to a willing buyer, on or before the date of preliminary notification, as that would give a fair indication of the market value in land acquisition proceedings.
