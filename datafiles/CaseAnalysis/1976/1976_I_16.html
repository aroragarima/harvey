State of Punjab and Another v Iqbal Singh

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 47; (1976) 2 SCC 1; (1976) SCC (L&S) 172; AIR 1976 SC 667; 1976 (33) FLR 39; 1976 LabIC 474; 1976 (2) LLJ 377; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 360; 1976 (1) SLR 525; 1976 UJ 260

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution, arts. 19(1)(f), 31(1), 226 and 311(2) - Punjab Civil Services (Pension) Rules, r. 6.4 - (A) Whether right of Govt. servant to pension is property - (B) Service Rules - Cut in pension - Requirement of opportunity to show cause - Held, is property u/arts. 31(1) and 19(1)(f) - Though an order imposing cut in pension and gratuity, u/r.6.4 is not one of reduction in rank falling within the purview of art. 311(2) of the Constitution, yet there can be no doubt that it adversely affected the civil servant and such an order could not have been passed without giving him a reasonable opportunity of making his defence - Appeal Dismissed.
