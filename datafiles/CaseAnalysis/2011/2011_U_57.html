D.P. Das v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

09 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 482; (2011) 8 SCC 115; 2011 (6) AWC 5667; 2011 (130) FLR 1071; 2011 (3) JabLJ 126; JT 2011 (9) SC 120; 2011 (8) MLJ(SC) 1074; 2011 (2) OLR 728; 2011(8) SCALE 469; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 739; 2011 (6) SLR 744

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Determination Of Seniority, Kerala State and Subordinate Services Rules, 1958

Summary: Service - Seniority list - Challenged - In the year 1983, Specialist Medical Officer (SMO) was recruited wherein appellant was one of the five recruited persons and he belonged to the category of Surgery - Subsequently, in the year 1991, post of the Indian Ordnance Factories Health Services were sanctioned for filling up amongst the SMOs cadre and specialist (Respondents) was recruited - Respondent no. 1 (Department) referred the matter to the UPSC for preparation of the common seniority list - Seniority list was prepared on the basis of performance in the interview and respondents were placed above the appellant - Appellant challenged said seniority list before tribunal on the ground that the Department never requested the UPSC to prepare a combined seniority list as per merit on the basis of performance in the interview - Tribunal dismissed said petition and held that the date of recommendation of the appellant who belongs to surgery discipline and the respondents belonging to medicine discipline was same i.e.14.4.1983 and the rules provide for fixing the seniority based on the date of recommendations of the UPSC maintaining inter se merit as per the recommendation - Appellant filed writ petition before HC who dismissed said writ petition, affirming the methodology adopted by the UPSC for fixing the seniority of two different disciplines whose recommendations were made on the same date - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether impugned order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, the law was clear that seniority was an incidence of service and where the service rules prescribe the method of its computation, it was squarely governed by such rules - However, in the absence of a provision ordinarily the length of service was taken into account - In the instant case, no record has been brought before the Court to ascertain merit wise position of the persons who were directly recruited, except the office memorandum of 1946, which refers to the acceptance of the age of the candidate as the determining factor for seniority - In the instant case, as there was no rule prescribed for the determination of seniority, Court was left with only the guideline flowing from the executive instruction of 1946, in order to evolve a just policy, for determination of seniority - Hence, determination of seniority of the officers who were recommended on the same date, age was the only valid and fair basis as such their seniority should be decided on the basis of age of the candidates who have been recommended - Hence, impugned order of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
