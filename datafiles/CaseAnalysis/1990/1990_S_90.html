Additional Secretary To The Government of India and Others v Smt Alka Subhash Gadia and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 December 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, A.M. Ahmadi, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 636; (1992) SCC (Cr) 301; (1992) Supp1 SCC 496; AIR 2001 SCW 463; [1991] 72 Comp Cas 336; 2001 CRLJ 463; 1991 CRLR 128; 1991 CrLR(SC) 128; 1991 (53) E.L.T. 481; JT 1991 (1) SC 549; 1990(2) SCALE 1352; [1990] Supp3 S.C.R. 583

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, s. 3(1) - Smugglers and Foreign Exchange Manipulators (Forfeiture of Property) Act, 1976, ss. 2(b), 6(1) - Detention order - Notice - Forfeit of properties - Legality - Detention order was passed on 13-12-1985 against respondent No.1's husband u/s. 3(1) of the 1974 Act - Respondent No.1's husband could not be served with said order as he was absconding - Hence a declaration was made against respondent No.1's husband u/s. 2(b) of the 1976 Act - Thereafter, a notice dt.31-3-1987 was issued to him u/s. 6(1) of the 1976 Act to show cause as to why properties mentioned in schedule to said notice should not be forfeited to Central Govt. for reasons recorded in accompaniment - Aggrieved, respondent No.1 filed writ petition before HC challenging detention order and show cause notice - HC held that writ petition was maintainable for challenging detention order even though detenu was not served with order and he had thus not surrendered to authorities - HC further directed that detention order, grounds of detention, and documents relied upon for passing detention order be furnished to detenu and that they should also be produced before Court - HC further held that officers were guilty of contempt of Court - Hence, instant appeals - Whether detenu or anyone on his behalf was entitled to challenge detention order without detenu submitting or surrendering to it - Held, when order and grounds were served and detenu was in a position to make out prima facie limited grounds on which they could be successfully challenged, Courts have power even to grant bail to detenu pending final hearing of his petition - Court could and did hear such petition expeditiously to give necessary relief to detenu - Where detenu, before being served with them, learns of detention order and grounds on which it was made, and satisfies Court of their existence by proper affirmation, Court did not decline to entertain writ petition even at pre-execution stage, on very limited grounds - Court no doubt even in such cases was not obliged to interfere with impugned order at that stage and might insist that detenu should first submit to it - Courts had exercised their powers at pre-execution stage, though such cases had been rare - Respondent No. 1 who was his wife had sought to challenge said order because show-cause notice issued to him, a copy of which was also sent to her - Thus assistance of HC was sought by respondent No.1 on behalf of detenu to secure order of detention with a view to defend proceedings under 1976 Act - No prima facie case was made out either before HC for challenging order of detention which would impel Court to interfere with it at pre-execution stage - HC issued contempt notice by its order - Both orders of HC directing appellants to furnish to detenu or to respondent No.1 or her counsel the order of detention, grounds of detention and documents supporting them as well as contempt notice were clearly illegal and unjustified and they were quashed - Appeals allowed.
