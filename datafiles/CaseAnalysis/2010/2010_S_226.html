Rubabbuddin Sheikh v State Of Gujarat And Ors

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 7; (2010) 2 SCC 200; AIR 2010 SC 3175; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 966; JT 2010 (1) SC 99; 2010 (2) MLJ(Crl) 735; 2010 (1) OLR 810; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 738; 2010(1) SCALE 306

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Transfer of investigation to CBI - Gujarat Police Authorities allegedly killed petitioner's brother in a fake encounter and caused the disappearance of his sister-in-law - Petitioner sought the issuance of direction for investigation of the case by Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) and the issuance of the writ of habeas corpus to produce his sister-in-law - Whether investigation can be transferred to CBI Authorities or any other independent agency when the charge sheet has already been submitted - In an appropriate case when the court feels that the investigation by the police authorities is not in the proper direction and in order to do complete justice in the case and as the high police officials are involved in the said crime, it was always open to the court to hand over the investigation to the independent agency like CBI - It cannot be said that after the charge sheet is submitted, SC is not empowered, in an appropriate case, to hand over the investigation to an independent agency like CBI - Held, considering the grave allegations made against the police officials of the State in respect of which some of them have already been in custody, it is proper and appropriate and in the interest of justice even at the stage of charge sheet, the investigation should be transferred to the CBI Authorities for proper and thorough investigation of the case - Therefore, direction issued to CBI to take up the investigation and to file the report within the specified time.

(B) Criminal - Transfer of investigation to CBI - Petitioner wrote a letter to the Chief Justice of India about the killing of his brother in a fake encounter and disappearance of his sister-in-law at the hands of Anti Terrorist Squad (ATS) of Gujarat Police and Rajasthan Special Task Force (RSTF) - Registry of the SC forwarded the letter to Director General of Police - State has submitted eight Action Taken Reports (ATR) to the SC - Whether investigation should be transferred to CBI although some of their high police officials were taken in custody and eight ATRs were submitted to SC - Held, accusations are directed against the local police personnel in which high police officials of the State have been made the accused - Therefore, it would be proper for the writ petitioner or even the public to come forward to say that if the investigation carried out by the police personnel of the State, the writ petitioner and their family members would be highly prejudiced and the investigation would also not come to an end with proper finding - If investigation is allowed to be carried out by the local police authorities, all concerned including the relatives of the deceased may feel that investigation was not proper and in that circumstances it would be fit and proper that the writ petitioner and the relatives of the deceased should be assured that an independent agency should look into the matter and that would lend the final outcome of the investigation credibility.

(C) Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Habeas corpus - Whether issuance of writ of habeas corpus warranted in the present matter - Held, charge sheet showed that petitioner's sister-in-law was taken by the police personnel, however, those police personnel were not listed as accused - Further, charge sheet showed that detenu was 'sent somewhere' - Third ATR exposed the fact that the said detenu was disposed of by burning in village Illol, however, it is not clear from the eight ATRs filed by the police authorities of the State as to what happened to detenu - Investigating agency of the State has made a false excuse for not conducting the NARCO Analysis of the accused in the present matter - Therefore, SC is not issued any formal order for the issuance of the habeas corpus but direction issued to the CBI to take up the investigation to find out the mode and manner in which the petitioner's sister-in-law was killed - Petition disposed of.
