Dilip Premnarayan Tiwari and others v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

10 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1894; (2010) 1 SCC 775; AIR 2010 SC 361; 2010 CRLJ 905; JT 2009 (15) SC 1; 2010 (2) MLJ(Crl) 321; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 230; 2009(14) SCALE 489; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 322; 2009 (3) UC 1788

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Mitigating Circumstance, Air Force, Blood Stained Clothes, Blood Stains, New Accused

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 120B, 302, 307 and 452 - Murder - Assault - Conviction - Death sentence - Challenged - Appellants convicted for offence punishable u/ss. 302, 307 and 452/34 and were awarded death sentence - In appeal, HC confirmed conviction and death sentence - Hence, present appeals - (A) Evidence of eyes witnesses PW-4 (sister of deceased) and PW-8 (mother of deceased) - Creditability - These witness were injured during assault - Held, PW-4 had seen all three accused persons and also identified them - Whole evidence of witness is extremely natural - Her whole evidence being evidence of injured eye-witness was wholly credible - Even her evidence, insofar as assault on herself and her father is also supported by medical evidence - Evidence of these two witnesses was rightly believed by Trial Court as also by HC and is enough to convict appellants - Conviction of appellants as ordered by Sessions Judge and confirmed by HC is correct - (B) Death sentence - Held, no effort on part of HC to consider mitigating circumstances - Murders were outcome of social issue like a marriage with a person of another caste - Psyche of the offender in the background of a social issue like an inter-caste-community marriage, though wholly unjustified would have to be considered in the peculiar circumstances of this case - In present case, mindset of accused No.1 and vicious caste issues leading to crime committed by him have to be considered - Death sentence awarded to appellants set aside - Followed Bachan Singh v. State of Punjab 1980 INDLAW SC 586 - However, in peculiar circumstances of this case, mere life imprisonment may not be adequate punishment for these accused persons - Considering overall circumstances, accused No.1 and 3 awarded complete 25 years of actual imprisonment - As accused No.2 had not had not participated in the actual assault he deserves life imprisonment in ordinary sense - Appeals disposed of.
