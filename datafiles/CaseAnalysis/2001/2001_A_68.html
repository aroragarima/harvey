Union of India v Adani Exports Limited

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 413; (2002) 1 SCC 567; AIR 2002 SC 126; 2002 (1) ALD(SC) 104; 2002 (79) ECC 457; 2001 (134) E.L.T. 596; 2002 (4) G.L.R. 2830; JT 2001 (9) SC 318; 2001 (47) RLT 884; 2001(7) SCALE 575; 2001 (7) SLT 612; 2001 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 37; [2001] Supp4 S.C.R. 631; 2001 (8) Supreme 48

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 14, r. 2 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Whether High Court has territorial jurisdiction to entertain special civil applications and grant relief, when none of facts pleaded in said applications are in any way connected with relief sought? - Oil and Natural Gas Commission v. Utpal Kumar Basu and Ors., 1994 Indlaw SC 1574 followed - In order to confer jurisdiction on a HC to entertain a writ petition or a special civil application, HC must be satisfied from entire facts pleaded in support of cause of action that those facts do constitute a cause so as to empower Court to decide a dispute which has, at least in part, arisen within its jurisdiction - Held, facts which have no bearing with lis or dispute involved in case, do not give rise to a cause of action so as to confer territorial jurisdiction on Court concerned - Appeals allowed.
