Union of India and another v U.P. State Warehousing Corporation

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 921; (1991) Supp2 SCC 730; AIR 1991 SC 1374; 1990 (3) CompLJ 301; 1990 (90) CTR 19; [1991] 187 ITR 54; JT 1990 (4) SC 451; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 523; [1990] Supp2 S.C.R. 523; [1990] 53 TAXMAN 581; 1991 (1) UJ 119

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 10(29) - Warehousing Corporation Act, 1962, s. 24(d) - Total income - Exemption - State Warehousing Corporation - Held, Corporation was an authority constituted under Act - Its activities as an agent would be undoubtedly activities facilitating marketing of commodities - Thus, assessee having fulfilled all tests was entitled to exemption u/s. 10(29) as claimed - Answered in favour of assessee.
