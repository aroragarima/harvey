T. Kaliamurthi And Another v Five Gori Thaikal Wakf And Others

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2171; (2008) 9 SCC 306; AIR 2009 SC 840; 2008(5) ALL MR 462; JT 2008 (9) SC 115; 2008 (7) MLJ(SC) 534; 2009 (1) MPLJ 37; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 879B; 2008(11) SCALE 52; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 758

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Keywords: Limitation Period, Suit For Possession, Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950, Muslim Wakfs Act, 1954, Contrary Intention, Alienations, Wakf (Amendment) Act, 1984, Wakf (Tamil Nadu Amendment) Act, 1982, Public Properly (Extension of the Limitation) Act, 1959

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Wakf Act, 1954, - Limitation Act, 1963, ss.6,28,31,52 A and 107 - Wakf Property - Possession - Eviction - Challenged - Respondents filed suit for recovery of suit schedule property and mesne profits before Trial Court which was dismissed and opined that appellants perfected title by adverse possession which was barred under the 1963 Act though suit schedule property was respondent's /Wakf Property - Aggrieved respondents filed appeal before Appellate Court which affirmed Trial Court's order - Aggrieved appellants filed 3 appeals before HC which affirmed concurrent findings of Courts below that suit properties were Wakf properties - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justifiable - Held, relied on Yeshwantrao Laxmanrao Ghatge and Another v Baburao Bala Yadav 1978 Indlaw SC 446 By L. Rs, in instant case Court opined that applicability of s.6 of the 1963 Act was inconsequential because admittedly, there was extinguishment of rights u/ss.28 and 107 of the 1963 Act could not revive those extinguished rights or barred claim - Hence, set aside order passed by HC and dismissed suits filed by respondents - Appeal disposed of .
