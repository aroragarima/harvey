Â State of Nagaland v G. Vasantha

Supreme Court of India

16 October 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Ray, J.C. Shah, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 584; AIR 1970 SC 537

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - Central Services (Temporary Services) Rules, 1949, r. 3(2) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 - Termination - Appointment as Asst. Teacher in Govt. Public School - Category of post, Class III (Non-Gazetted) - Appointment made by Chief Secretary of Nagaland - Order of termination passed by Deputy Director of Education - Five years of service at time of termination - Respondent approached High Court - HC quashed order of termination inter alia on ground of violation of art. 311 - Whether quashing of order of termination on ground of art. 311 proper? - Held, no - Inconsistent finding of HC in its judgment - HC stated that 'it is true that termination appears to be innocuous and not on any disciplinary or punitive grounds' and after recording this finding and in view of fact that respondent was only a temporary Govt. servant, whose services had been terminated under contract of service, no further question regarding validity of order arose - But HC has expressed opinion that art. 311 has been violated because it must be inferred that order has been passed by way of punishment, especially when respondent has been in service for over five years - Such reasoning found to be uncalled for and erroneous - Further held, art. 311 not applicable to case - Impugned judgment set aside - Appeal allowed.
