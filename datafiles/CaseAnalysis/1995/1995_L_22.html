Director Of Tribal Welfare, Government Of Andhra Pradesh v Laveti Giri And Another

Supreme Court of India

18 April 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 421; (1995) 4 SCC 32; (1995) SCC (L&S) 914; AIR 1995 SC 1506; AIR 1995 SCW 2289; 1995 (30) ATC 166; JT 1995 (3) SC 684; 1995 (2) MLJ(SC) 30; 1995(2) SCALE 815; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 430; 1995 (2) SLR 595; 1995 (2) UJ 535

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Educational Institution

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 15(4) - Whether High Court was justified in its finding that respondent is entitled to enter status as Scheduled Tribe ? - Burden of proof of social status is always on person who profound it to seek constitutional socioeconomic advantages - Respondents failed to produce social status certificate from native Tehsildar or Revenue Divisional Officer - Respondent lived in Malakpet within jurisdiction of Musheerabad Tahsildar but he produced certificate from Tehsildar, Vallabhnagar in Ranga Reddy District who had no jurisdiction to issue it - In case, certificate obtained or social status claimed is found to be false, parent/guardian/the candidate should be prosecuted for making claim - Held, respondent had already completed his engineering course, though he played fraud on Constitution depriving real tribal of benefit of education as an engineer - Respondent not entitled to any employment or any other advantage on basis of his false status as Scheduled Tribe - Appeal allowed.
