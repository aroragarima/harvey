Moti Lal v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

15 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1068; (2008) 11 SCC 20; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 950; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 882; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 2583; 2008 CRLJ 3543; 2008 (3) JCC 2079; JT 2008 (8) SC 271; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 796; 2008(10) SCALE 81; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 983

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Keywords: Mitigating Circumstance, Married Women, Self-Inflicted, Offence Punishable, Woman, Sex Crime

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 450, 376(1) - Appeal against conviction and sentence of five years and seven years rigorous imprisonment respectively and fine of Rs.2, 000/- and 1, 000/- respectively - Held, victim of sexual assault is not treated as accomplice and as such, her evidence does not require corroboration from any other evidence including the evidence of a doctor - Even if the doctor who examined the victim does not find sign of rape, it is no ground to disbelieve the sole testimony of the prosecutrix - There was no apparent reason for a married woman to falsely implicate the accused after scatting her own prestige and honour - Measure of punishment in a case of rape cannot depend upon the social status of the victim or the accused - It must depend upon the conduct of the accused, the state and age of the sexually assaulted female and the gravity of the criminal act - Accused has been rightly convicted and sentenced - Appeal dismissed.
