Boothalinga Agencies v V. T. C. Poriaswami Nadar

Supreme Court of India

22 April 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 126; AIR 1969 SC 110; 1969 (1) ALT(SC) 62; 1969 (2) MLJ 15; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 65

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Administrative - Contract & Commercial - Practice & Procedure - Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947, s. 5 - Imports (Control) Order, 1955, Cl. 5(4) - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 56 - Default of contract - damages - Entitlement of - Appellant/defendant no. 2 agreed to sell consignment to respondent/plaintiff, after taking advance contract was entered into in name of defendant no. 1 and owing to rise in prices appellant committed default - Trial Court concluded that both defendant nos.1 to 2 undertook with plaintiff to fulfill terms of contract and therefore granted decree in favour of plaintiff against both defendants - Defendant nos. 1 to 2 filed 2 appeals before HC against judgment of Trial Court - HC by its judgment dt. 16-3-1962 allowed appeal of defendant no. 1 and dismissed suit as against him and as regards appeal filed by defendant no. 2, HC reduced amount of damages - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned order of HC was justified - Held, argument of appellant was well-founded and should be accepted as correct, it was manifest that disposal of imported chicory which arrived at port on 13-12-1955 was governed by provisions of the Order which came into force on 7-12-1955 - Cl. 5(4) of the Order expressly provided that licensee should comply with all conditions imposed or deemed to be imposed under that clause - Therefore sale of imported goods would be direct contravention of cl. 5(4) and u/s. 5 of the 1947 Act any contravention of the 1947 Act or any order made or deemed to have been made under the Act was punishable with imprisonment up to 1 year or fine or both - Even though contract was enforceable on 26-11-1955 when it was entered into, performance of contract became impossible or unlawful after 7-12-1955 and so contract became void u/s. 56 of the 1872 Act after coming into force of the Order - Therefore, decree of HC was set aside and suit brought by respondent was dismissed in its entirety - Appeal allowed.
