Â Jabar Singh v Genda Lal

Supreme Court of India

20 December 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 231; AIR 1964 SC 1200; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 54

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Any Other Candidate, Decision Of The High Court

Summary: Election - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 97, 100 and 101 - What is nature and scope of enquiry contemplated by ss. 97, 100 and 101? - Held, scope of enquiry in a case falling u/s. 100(1)(d)(iii) is to determine whether any votes have been improperly cast in favour of returned candidate, or any votes have been improperly refused? - These are only two matters which would be relevant in deciding whether election of returned candidate has been materially affected or not - Result of s. 97(1),is that in dealing with a composite election petition, tribunal enquires into not only case made out by petitioner, but also counter-claim made by returned candidate - First part of enquiry in regard to validity of election of returned candidate must be tried within narrow limits prescribed by s. 100(1)(d)(iii) and latter part of enquiry which is governed by s. 101(a) will have to be tried on a broader basis permitting returned candidate to lead evidence in support of pleas which he may have taken by way of recrimination u/s. 97 (1) - Further held, in holding an enquiry either u/s. 100(1)(d)(iii) or u/s. 101, where s. 97 has not been complied with, it is not competent for Tribunal to order a general recount of votes preceded by a scrutiny about their validity - Appeal dismissed.
