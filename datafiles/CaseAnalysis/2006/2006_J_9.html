Ramji Rai & Another v Jagdish Mallah (Dead) Through L.Rs.& Another

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 935; (2007) 14 SCC 200; 2007 (1) ADJ 283; AIR 2007 SC 900; 2007 (66) ALR 307; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 236; 2007 (3) RCR(Civil) 680; 2006(13) SCALE 328; 2006 (Supp) RD 275; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 28

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: U.P. Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950 - Appellant sought permanent injunction restraining the defendants from interfering in the possession of the land in dispute or from raising boundary wall - Held, no reason to reverse the concurring findings - Lower appellate court should have dismissed the suit filed by the appellants only on the ground that the appellants had failed to prove that they were in possession of the disputed lands - Declaration that the appellants are not the owners, was not necessary - Appeal dismissed.
