Â Raghav Prapanna Tripathi v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 505; AIR 1963 SC 74; 1963 CRLJ 70; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 239

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Circumstantial Evidence - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 176, 201 and 302 - Appellant convicted for murder of his wife and child - (A) Whether conviction of appellant and other co-accused justified? - Held, yes - Circumstances considered by court were (1) Strained relations between appellant and his wife (2) Appellant and his accomplices left their village in a hurry and later absconded after his wife and child went missing (3) No attempt made by appellant to search his wife and child (4) Blood stained clothes of appellant and blood stains were found from house of appellant (5) Other Circumstances - Circumstances, are such which can explain hypothesis of guilt of appellant - Courts below having applied correct principles and on circumstances, rightly found appellant guilty - (B) Question regarding conviction of appellant Ramanuj Das and Jai Devi u/s. 201 IPC, 1860 - Further held, both had knowledge of removal of dead-bodies but what s.201 requires is causing any evidence of commission of offence to disappear or for giving any information respecting offence which a person knows or believes to be false - In this case there is no evidence of either - It is not shown that these two appellants caused any evidence to disappear - They were absconding but merely absconding will not fill gap or supply evidence which is necessary to prove ingredients of s. 201 - However, Ramanuj Das did have knowledge of commission of murder and he took no steps to inform police about it and has been rightly convicted u/s. 176 IPC, 1860 - Order accordingly.
