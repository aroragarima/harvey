Rajasthan State Road Transport Corporation and others v Gurudas Singh

Supreme Court of India

17 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1903; (2004) 13 SCC 418

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(1) - Reinstatement - Challenged - Respondent was appointed in the Corporation by an order issued by the Divisional Manager - Subsequently, services of the respondent were terminated by order passed by the Depot Manager - Respondent filed suit against said termination contending that his dismissal was invalid, illegal and contrary to the principles of natural justice - Trial Court decreed said suit and held that the dismissal of the respondent was invalid, illegal and contrary to the principles of natural justice - Trial court also directed that respondent be taken back into service and financial benefits were granted from the date of institution of the suit - On appeal, First Appellate Court held that the direction for reinstatement of respondent cannot be faulted, however, respondent was not entitled to get any financial benefits - HC held that the respondent was protected by art. 311(1) of the Constitution - Hence, the instant appeals - Whether order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, art. 311(1) of the Constitution was applicable only to a member of civil service or the Union of All India Service or Civil Service of a State or a person holding civil post under the Union or a State - Hence, direction of the trial court as affirmed by the First Appellate Court and the HC to reinstate the respondent cannot be maintained - However, as respondent brought to notice the order passed in batch of cases where employees similarly placed as the respondent, were given the benefit of reinstatement - Following the said order, the order of reinstatement of the shall be operative but the respondent shall not be entitled to back wages - Appeals disposed of.
