Â Commissioner of Sales Tax v Rewa Coal Fields Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, M.B. Shah, S.P. Bharucha, S. Rajendra Babu, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1107; (1999) 5 SCC 715; JT 1999 (10) SC 257; 2000(6) SCALE 354; 2002 (125) STC 212; 1999 (9) Supreme 335

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Sales Tax - Practice & Procedure - Madhya Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1958, s. 8 - Assessee operating coal mine - Registration sought for timber, kerosene oil, drilling bits, hewing implements of all kinds, dry cells, torches, cement and lime and electrical bulbs being 'raw materials consumed in course of manufacture' - Whether goods in respect of which registration was sought by assessee can be called as 'raw material' as per definition and hence eligible for registration u/s. 8 of Act? - Held, it is clear that, drilling bits apart, none of articles aforementioned can qualify to be articles 'consumed in process of manufacture' or, to put it in a different way, consumed in mining of coal - They may be used for purposes incidental to mining, but are not integral thereto - So far as drilling bits are concerned, they are used to bore holes in walls of mine - It can, therefore, be said that they are consumed in mining of coal - To that extent alone can assessee succeed - Appeal allowed.
