Mis. Swadesamltran Limited, Madras v Their Workmen

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 159; AIR 1960 SC 762; 1960 (1) FLR 337; 1960 (1) LLJ 504; 1960 SCJ 658; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 144

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Unjustified, Labour Appellate Tribunal, misa

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Retrenchment -Industrial dispute - Relief sought - Respondents/workmen threatened appellant on several ocassions to go on strike if their demands were not fulfilled - Appellant had promised to meet their demands if its financial position was improved and also communicated its proposals of retrenchment - Before respondents went on strike, appellant had terminated 39 workmen which led to industrial dispute - Tribunal held that appellant was justified in retrenchment of 39 workmen and strike declared by respondents was not justified - On appeal, Appellate Tribunal held that 15 employees should be reinstated and they should be given half the amount of back wages and awarded compensation at half month's wages including dearness allowance for each year of service to remaining 24 workmen - Hence, instant appeal - Whether retrenchment of 39 workmen was justified and if not, what relief retrenched workmen were entitled to - Held, once it was found that retrenchment was unjustified and improper it was for tribunals below to consider to what relief the retrenched workmen were entitled and ordinarily if a workman had been improperly and illegally retrenched he was entitled to claim reinstatement - Meanwhile if employer had engaged other workmen would not necessarily defeat claim for reinstatement of retrenched workmen; nor could the fact that protracted litigation in regard to dispute had inevitably meant delay, defeat such a claim for reinstatement - Therefore, held that in case of wrongful dismissal, discharge or retrenchment, a claim for reinstatement could not be defeated merely because time had lapsed or that employer had engaged fresh hands - Hence, compensation awarded to 15 and 24 workmen respectively, was a matter of discretion and as such was not open to challenge in instant appeal - Appeal dismissed.
