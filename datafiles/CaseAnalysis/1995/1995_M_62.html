State of Tamil Nadu and Another v Mahalakshmi Ammal and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 831; (1996) 7 SCC 269; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 375; AIR 1996 SC 866; 1995 (4) CCC 382; JT 1995 (9) SC 271; 1995(7) SCALE 118; 1996 (1) SCJ 304; [1995] Supp5 S.C.R. 451

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 9, 10, 11 and 11A - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - (A) Acquisition for housing scheme - Some land initially notified u/s. 4 excluded from acquisition by Govt. - Non-exclusion of petitioner's land - Whether can be assailed as discriminatory when petitioners land was practically gate way to housing scheme and its exclusion would frustrate entire scheme? - (B) Acquisition for housing scheme - Some survey was left out to be included in s. 4 notification - Errata notification published to include them - Whether Errata can be considered as fresh notification? - (C) Land Acquisition Act, ss. 11 and 11A Award - Delay in making - S. 4 notification issued in 1978 - Award made in 1986 and further award in 1990 - Initial award having been made within 2 years u/s. 11, whether delay in making of further award would vitiate it? - There being stay of dispossession, whether limitation would act as bar? - (D) Ss. 11, 9 and 10 of 1894 Act - Irregularity in service of notice u/ss. 9 and 10 - Whether vitiates award? - Held, (A) Not discriminatory - (B) Cannot be considered as fresh notification - It relates back to date of initial s. 4 notification - (C) Would not vitiate it - Limitation does not stand as an impediment as provided in s. 11A proviso - (D)Does not vitiate award - Such irregularity is curable - Appeal allowed.
