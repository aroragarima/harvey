Trivedi Himanshu Ghanshyambhai v Ahmedabad Municipal Corporation and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1019; (2007) 8 SCC 644; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 980; AIR 2007 SC 148; 2007 (4) AWC 4149; 2007 (4) ESC 554; JT 2007 (11) SC 561; 2007(12) SCALE 183; 2007 (7) SCJ 780; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 1041; 2007 (6) SLR 486; 2008 (1) UPLBEC 449

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Mode Of Selection

Summary: Service - Appeal against the order that appointment of the appellant as an Assistant Manager in Ahmedabad Municipal Corporation was bad, illegal and invalid and accordingly, liable to be quashed - Whether the appellant fulfilled the requirement of ten years experience on the administrative side for appointment to the post of Assistant Manager? - Held, High Court had failed to appreciate that the corporation, being the employer, is the best judge to decide whether the appellant had discharged the responsibilities on the administrative side and once the corporation came to a finding that the appellant had discharged not only the duties of an X-ray technician but also performed clerical/administrative work High Court was not justified in concluding that the appellant did not possess the administrative experience of more than ten years - It open to the High Court to entertain the writ petition challenging appointment of the appellant and other selected candidates at the instance of the unsuccessful candidates - Appeal allowed.
