Ishwar Dass Nassa and another v State Of Haryana And Others

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 719; (2012) 1 SCC 753; AIR 2012 SC 852; 2013 (96) ALR 319; 2012 (1) AWC 588; 2011 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3714; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 337; 2012 (117) RD 677; 2012 (1) RLW 664; 2011(13) SCALE 452; [2011] 16 S.C.R. 301

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Haryana Housing Board Act, 1971, Housing Board Haryana (Allotment, Management and Sale of Tenements) Regulations, 1972

Summary: Land & Property - Housing Board Haryana (Allotment, Management and Sale of Tenements) Regulations, 1972, regn. 11(4) - Charging additional money - Challenged - In response to an advertisement issued by the Board, the appellants applied for the houses proposed to be constructed at Sonepat - The competent authority allotted tenements of different categories to the appellants and allotment letters for the same were issued in favour of the appellants - Appellants deposited the amount in accordance with the stipulations contained in the allotment letters and executed Hire Purchase Tenancy Agreements - After about 10 years, the Estate Manager issued notices to the appellants and directed them to pay additional price in lieu of the enhanced compensation allegedly paid by Improvement Trust for the land which was sold to the Board - Appellants challenged said notices vide writ petition submitting that in view of clause 2(w) of the Hire Purchase Tenancy Agreement, the Board cannot demand additional price after 7 years of the allotment of tenements - Board contended that the appellants were under a moral obligation to share the burden of additional cost paid to the Improvement Trust - Single Judge rejected the appellant's challenged to the demand of additional price on the ground that once the cost of land gets increased on account of payment of higher compensation to the landowners the Board was entitled to demand additional price from the allottees - DB also dismissed appeals filed against said orders of the Single Judge - Hence, the instant appeals - Whether the Board could ignore the time limit of 7 years specified in clause 2(w) of the Hire Purchase Tenancy Agreement executed by the appellants as per the requirement of regn. 11(4) of the Housing Board Haryana (Allotment, Management and Sale of Tenements) Regulations, 1972 and demand additional price from them after 10 years of the allotment of tenements? - Held, in view of the bar contained in clause 2(w) of the Hire Purchase Tenancy Agreement, the Board could not revise the price after 7 years of the allotment of tenement - Once the Board has, after due deliberations, incorporated a prohibition against change in the price after a period of 7 years from the allotment of tenements, there was no reason why it should not be asked to honour the commitment made to the allottees that they will not live under the fear of being asked to pay additional price after an indefinite period - Hence, Single Judge and DB were not right in deciding the writ petitions - Impugned orders of Single Judge and DB set aside - Appeals allowed.
