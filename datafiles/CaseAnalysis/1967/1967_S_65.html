Â Andhra Sugars Limited and Another v State of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 418; AIR 1968 SC 599; 1968 (1) MLJ(SC) 117; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 705; 1968 (21) STC 212

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Keywords: Tax Levied, Defence of India Rules, 1939, Railway Act 1890, Andhra Pradesh Sugarcane (Regulation Of Supply And Purchase) Act, 1961, Iron and Steel (Control of Production and Distribution) Order, 1941, Uttar Pradesh Sugarcane Cess Act, 1956, Sugarcane Control Order, 1966, Sugar and Sugar Products Control Order, 1946

Summary: Sales Tax - Andhra Pradesh Sugarcane (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Act, 1961, ss. 3, 15, 16(1), (2), 28(2)(1), 23, 21 and 2(m) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Indian Contract Act, ss. 2, 13, 14 and 10 - Indian Sale of Goods Act, s. 4 - Indian Railways Act, 1890, s. 66 - Indian Electricity Act, 1910, s. 22 - Iron and Steel (Control of Production and Distribution) Order, 1941, cls. 4 and 5 - Whether s. 21 of Andhra Pradesh Sugarcane (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Act, 1961 is unconstitutional and ultra vires? - Held, u/s. 21, same rate of tax is levied on purchases of all cane required for use, consumption or sale in a factory - There is no discrimination between cane grown in State and cane imported from outside - As a matter of fact, under Act factory can normally buy only cane grown in factory zone - A non-discriminatory tax on goods does not offend art. 301 unless it directly impedes free movement or transport of goods - Establishment of new factories and expansion of existing factories need encouragement and incentives - The exemption in favour of new and expanding factories is based on legitimate legislative policy - S. 21 does not violate art. 14 - Petition dismissed.
