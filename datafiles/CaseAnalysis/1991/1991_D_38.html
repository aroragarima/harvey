Mangalore Chemicals and Fertilisers Limited v Deputy Commissioner of Commercial Taxes and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 August 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 501; (1992) Supp1 SCC 21; AIR 1992 SC 152; AIR 1991 SCW 2851; 1993 (49) ECR 23; 1991 (55) E.L.T. 437; JT 1992 (3) SC 482; 1991(2) SCALE 662; [1991] 3 S.C.R. 336; 1991 (83) STC 234

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957, s. 8A - Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941, s. 5(2) - Exemption from sales tax - State Government issued a notification dt. 30.06.1969 providing certain incentives to entrepreneurs starting new industries in the State - Said notification contains a package of reliefs and incentives including one concerning relief from payment of sales tax - Petitioner established new industry and claimed exemption from payment of sales tax under said notification - Authorities did not grant permission to the petitioner in the relevant a/y - Subsequently authorities refused to grant said permission on the ground that requisite permission cannot be granted after expiry of the respective a/y - Subsequently, Commercial Tax Officer issued 3 demand notices demanding payment of the sales tax - Petitioner moved HC against said demand notices - HC dismissed said writ petition - Hence, present appeal - Whether the grant of permission for adjustment could at all be made after the period to which such adjustment related had itself expired? - Held, petitioner sought permission for exemption much before commencement of respective a/y but it was held by the authorities without any justification - Moreover, permission of this nature was a technical requirement and could be issued making it operative from the time it was applied for - Impugned order of the HC set aside - Deputy Commissioner directed to grant said permission - Appeal allowed.
