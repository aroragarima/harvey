Vijaykumar v State By Inspector Of Police, Madras And Another

Supreme Court of India

21 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 477; (2009) 12 SCC 629; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1599; 2009 CRLJ 3056; JT 2009 (7) SC 36; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 161; 2009(6) SCALE 364; [2009] 6 S.C.R. 919

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Demeanour Of Witness, Hearing Of Appeal, Relative Witness

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 341, 302, 307, 302 r/w 34 - Acquittal by trial court - High Court reversed the acquittal - Appeal against - Held, relative witness would not allow the real culprit to escape and implicate the person who is innocent -Evidence has to be carefully analysed - Even assuming that the evidence relating to the arrest and recovery is not to be acted upon, the prosecution succeeds on the basis of the evidence of ocular testimony adduced by PWs. 1 to 3 - Appeals dismissed.
