Narayanswamy v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 801; (1971) 2 SCC 182; (1971) SCC (Cr) 507; AIR 1971 SC 1789; 1971 CRLJ 1301; [1971] S.C.R. 588; [1971] Supp S.C.R. 588

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 479 A - Perjury - Accused contended that he was not given opportunity to show cause against complaint - Whether opportunity to show cause is mandatory? - Held, show cause is not mandatory and it's up to discretion of trial court - What is important is that court must form an opinion that witness has either given intentionally false evidence or has intentionally fabricated false evidence and it is expedient in interests of justice that witness should be prosecuted for offence committed by him - Appeal dismissed.
