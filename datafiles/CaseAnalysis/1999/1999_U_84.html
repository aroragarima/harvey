Â Hyderabad Industries Limited and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 May 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, M.B. Shah, S.P. Bharucha, S. Rajendra Babu, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 809; (1999) 5 SCC 15; AIR 1999 SC 1847; 1999 (4) Bom.C.R. 308; 1999 (82) ECR 449; 1999 (108) E.L.T. 321; JT 1999 (4) SC 95; 1999 (32) RLT 541; [1999] 3 S.C.R. 471

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Additional Duty Of Customs, Tariff Act, 1934

Summary: Customs - Customs Tariff Act, 1975, s. 3(1) - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 3, Sch. 1, Tariff Item 22(F) - Appellant challenged levy of additional duty of customs u/s. 3(1) of 1975 Act - Appellant contended that no duty was payable as asbestos fiber which was imported had not been manufactured or produced but was a natural mineral and thus no duty was leviable - Govt. of India informed appellant that fiber was obtained was a process of manufacture and said item correctly fell within Tariff Item 22-F of 1st Schedule to Excise Act - Whether levying additional duty for import of fiber is valid in law? - Held for purpose of attracting additional duty u/s. 3(1) on import of manufactured or produced article actually manufacture or production of like article not necessary - Held, demand from appellant of additional duty is unsustainable and quashed - Respondents shall not be liable to refund any additional duty realised so far from appellants - Appeal allowed.
