Comed Chemicals Limited v C. N. Ramchand

Supreme Court of India

06 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2411; (2009) 1 SCC 91; AIR 2009 SC 494; 2008 (4) AWC 4009; 2008 (4) CompLJ 465; JT 2008 (11) SC 547; 2009 (1) MLJ(SC) 953; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 100; 2008(13) SCALE 717; [2008] 15 S.C.R. 567

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 2(1)(f) and 11 - Appointment of arbitrator - Applicant-company appointed respondent as Director (Technical) - It was alleged that respondent wanted to quit the applicant-company before completing the work assigned to him in violation of the agreement - Applicant-company sent a notice to the respondent for appointment of an arbitrator in accordance with Clause 12 of MoU - In view of non-agreement between the applicant-company and respondent, the applicant-company filed arbitration application u/s. 11 of the Act - (A) International Commercial arbitration - It was submitted that there was no 'commercial' element in the agreement and what was agreed between the parties was to provide 'technical know-how' and 'expertise' to the applicant-Company - Relied on SC in R.M. Investment & Trading Co. Pvt. Ltd. v. Boeing Co. & Anr., 1994 INDLAW SC 378 wherein it was held that the agreement to render consultancy service by the appellant to the respondent was 'commercial' in nature and there was commercial relationship between the parties - It was further observed that the expression 'commercial' should, therefore, be construed broadly having regard to the manifold activities which are integral part of international trade - Held, case is covered by clause (f) of sub-section (1) of s. 2 of the Act - It is a case of International Commercial Arbitration - (B) Applicability of Act - It was contended that respondent was appointed as an employee by the applicant-Company and the matter cannot be referred to Arbitral Tribunal - Held, it is well settled that a Director is not a mere employee or servant of the Company - Respondent was appointed as Director (Technical) and was allotted 40% equity shares in the subsidiary Company - Respondent was working in dual or double capacity, i.e. (i) as an employee, and (ii) as a Director - Functions to be performed by him were supervisory and related to policy making decisions in the affairs of the Company - Any dispute between the applicant-company and the respondent would, therefore, be covered by Clause 12 of the MoU which provides for arbitration - Application allowed.
