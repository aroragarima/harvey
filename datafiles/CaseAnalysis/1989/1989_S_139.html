Dibyasingh Malana and Others v State Of Orissa And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

N.D. Ojha, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 582; (1989) Supp2 SCC 312; AIR 1989 SC 1737; 1990 (69) CutLT 1; JT 1989 (2) SC 210; 1989(1) SCALE 1069; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 604

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Orissa Land Reforms Act, 1960, s. 37(b) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 31 C and 39(b) - Family - Definition of, u/s. 37(b) Orissa Land Reforms Act - (A) Constitutionality of s. 37(b) - (B) Definition neither meaningless nor of doubtful - Whether can be interpreted as excluding land fallen to share of major but unmarried son by adding word 'or' between words 'major' and 'married'? - (C) Supreme Court - Practice and procedure - Hypothetical questions - Plea that by definition land of a married daughter is liable to be clubbed twice i.e. firstly, with father and secondly, with husband - Held, (A) cannot be challenged as protection u/art. 31-C is available to s. 37(b) - (B) Cannot be interpreted so - (C) Plea not considered as none of parties was a married daughter - Appeal dismissed.
