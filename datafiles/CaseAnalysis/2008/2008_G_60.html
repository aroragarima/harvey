National Insurance Company Limited v General Insurance Dev. Officers Asson. And Others

Supreme Court of India

03 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2282; (2008) 5 SCC 472; AIR 2008 SC 2657; 2008(4) SCALE 768; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 1087; 2008 (6) SLR 43

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance; Service

Keywords: Terms And Conditions Of Service, General Insurance Business, Excessive Delegation, Employees Of The Corporation, Life Insurance (Business Nationalization) Act, 1956, General Insurance (Nationalisation and Revision of Pay Scales and Other Conditions of Service of Supervisory, Clerical and Subordinate Staff) Second Amendment Scheme, 1980, General Insurance Business (Nationalisation) Amendment Ordinance, 1984, CBDT Rules

Summary: Service - Insurance - General Insurance (Business Nationalization) Act, 1972, ss. 17A, 16(1)(g) - General Insurance (Rationalisation of Pay Scales and Other Conditions of Service of Development Staff) Amendment Scheme, 2003 (2003 Scheme) - Appellant/Company introduced the 2003 Scheme by amending the corresponding principal scheme of 1976 - Respondent/Association of Development Officers challenged the legality of 2003 Scheme on the ground that there was a unilateral change of service conditions of the Development Officers in Class II category - Respondents contended that Development Officers suffered not only financial loss due to the introduction of 2003 Scheme but there would be a great deal of inconvenience caused because of the transferring mode of Development Officers from one division to another on the basis of cost ratio - Whether judicial interference is called for in the present matter - Held, since modifications of principal Scheme 1976 appear to have been done for the purpose of rationalization, there is no scope for interference because essentially a policy decision is immune from judicial review unless it is founded on no rational basis or material to justify the change in policy - By virtue of s. 17A of the Act, Central Government was empowered to amend the scheme u/s. 16(1)(g) and the authority of the Government thereof was upheld by SC in Kishan Prakash Sharma and Ors. v. Union of India and Ors., 2001 INDLAW SC 19879 - Further, there was no need for any consultation with the employees before the introduction of the new scheme - Therefore, there is no scope for a judicial interference in the present matter, however, it would be in the interests of the officers and the insurance companies if the Development Officers who work within the cost ratio are not transferred unless the transfer is required to be done in public interest - Appeals allowed.
