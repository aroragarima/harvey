State of Uttar Pradesh v Gambhir Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 316; (2005) 11 SCC 271; AIR 2005 SC 2439; 2005 CRLJ 2579; JT 2005 (5) SC 553; 2005(4) SCALE 342

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302, s. 34 IPC, s.302/34 - HC acquitted respondents - Appeal against acquittal - Held, presence of three witnesses becomes doubtful if their evidence is critically scrutinized - If on same evidence two views are reasonably possible, one in favour of accused must be preferred - No reason to interfere with order of HC in an appeal against acquittal - Appeal allowed.
