Hindustan Steels Ltd., Rourkela v A. K. Roy & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 374; (1969) 3 SCC 513; AIR 1970 SC 1401; 1970 (20) FLR 234; 1970 LabIC 1166; 1970 (1) LLJ 228; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 343

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution, art. 226 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, sch. 3, item 11 - (A) Industrial Dispute - (1) Illegal discharge or dismissal or workman - Compensation awarded instead of reinstatement - Discretionary - Mechanical exercise of, by Tribunal in awarding reinstatement - HC refused to interfere in writ jurisdiction - (2) Assessment of compensation - Compensation for a period of two years at the rate of salary last drawn by the concerned workmen - (B) Constitution - Writ of Certiorari u/art.226 - When issued - Refusal held unjustifiable - Held, would meet the ends of justice - If a statutory tribunal exercise its discretion on the basis of irrelevant considerations or without regard to relevant considerations, certiorari may properly issue to quash its order - Appeal Allowed.
