Tek Chand v Dile Ram

Supreme Court of India

24 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20328; (2001) 3 SCC 290; (2001) SCC (L&S) 555; AIR 2001 SC 905; 2001 (2) ALD(SC) 1; JT 2001 (2) SC 114; 2001 (2) MLJ(SC) 45; 2001 (1) RCR(Civil) 727; 2001(1) SCALE 471; 2001 (2) SCJ 118; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 527; 2001 (1) SLT 709; 2001 (1) Supreme 444

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 116A - Constitution of India, 1950 - Civil Services Rules - Bombay Civil Service Rules Central Civil Service (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1965, r. 48A - Respondent filed election petition challenging election of appellant - Respondent sought for setting aside election of appellant on ground that it was void as, one of contesting candidates was holding office of profit under Govt. of Himachal Pradesh on date of filing of his nomination paper as well as on date of scrutiny thereof - His nomination paper was improperly and wrongly accepted by Returning Officer which in turn had materially affected result of election insofar as it concerned returning candidate-appellant - (A) Whether result of election, insofar as it concerned appellant, had been materially affected to declare it void? - It is for election petitioner to prove by positive and reliable evidence that either improper acceptance of nomination of candidate or on account of non-compliance with provisions of Constitution or Act, Rules or orders etc. that wasted votes would have been distributed in such a manner among remaining candidates that any candidate other than returned candidate would have polled highest number of valuable votes - Courts cannot set aside election of returned candidate on surmises and conjectures unless established by positive evidence that election of returned candidate has been materially affected - Verdict given by majority of voters in a constituency in favour of an elected candidate to represent a constituency in a State Legislative Assembly or Parliament cannot be lightly annulled or negatived in absence of specific, acceptable and convincing evidence in support of grounds raised in an election petition - Held, HC committed a manifest error in concluding that result of election of appellant had been materially affected on account of improper acceptance of nomination paper of - Election of appellant insofar it concerned him had not been materially affected - (B) Whether at relevant time was holding an office of profit being in service of State of Himachal Pradesh and as such his nomination paper was improperly accepted? - Held, on date of filing and scrutiny of nomination paper of, he should be deemed to have been voluntarily retired by operation of proviso to sub-rule (2) of r. 48-A - Application for impleadment of and State of Himachal Pradesh in election petition rejected as neither 'N' nor State of Himachal Pradesh were necessary or proper parties to be impleaded in election petition - Appeal allowed.
