Mahadeolal Kanodia v Administrator-General Ofwest Bengal

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 175; AIR 1960 SC 936; 1961 (1) SCJ 15; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 578

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Calcutta Thika Tenancy Act, 1949, Calcutta Thika Tenancy Amendment Act, 1953, Thika Tenancy Act, 1949, Thika Tenancy Amendment Act (WB 6 of 1953)

Summary: Land & Property - Calcutta Thika Tenancy (Amendment) Act, 1953, s. 1(2) - Bengal General Clauses Act, 1899, s. 8 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 8 - Thika Tenancy Act, 1949, s. 28 - Thika Tenancy Amendment Act, 1953, s. 1(2) - Whether provisions of s. 28 could be applied by a court in a case, where an application had been made by a tenant for relief under that section and such application was pending for disposal on date omission became effective, by reason of Amendment Act coming into force? - Held, language used here has one meaning only - Act in its new shape with added benevolent provisions and minus former benevolent provisions in s. 28 has to be applied to all pending proceedings, including execution proceedings and proceedings pending u/s. 28 of original Act on 21-10-1952 - Held, view taken by High Court in this case, that effect of s. 1(2) of Calcutta Thika Tenancy (Amendment) Act, 1953, is that all pending applications u/s. 28 of original Act must be dismissed is correct - Appeal dismissed.
