Jal Mahal Resorts Private Limited v K. P. Sharma and others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Gyan Sudha Misra, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 342; (2014) 8 SCC 804; JT 2014 (7) SC 134; 2014(7) SCALE 336; [2014] 11 S.C.R. 765

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Keywords: Legality, Environmental degradation, Cancellation of lake restoration project

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Environmental degradation - Cancellation of lake restoration project - Legality - HC cancelled Environment and Monument Improvement/Preservation and Tourism Development Project by declaring it as illegal which was awarded to petitioner appellant via global tender floated in 2003 and finally granted in 2005 after all requisite approvals as per appellant under Environmental Law including Environment Impact Assessment under the Act and notifications issued there under of Pollution Control Board - Hence instant appeal - Whether order of HC was legal -

Held, in view of cancellation of project, HC directed immediate dismantling and removal of entire project and diversion of two drains which was done to purify waters of man made artificial water body and detritus - Figures and conclusions in impugned order itself indicated enormous difficulty and repetitive failures of State Government to either implement restoration itself or to get any private entity to do so over period of approx. 20 years from 1984 to year 2003 - Attempts immediately preceding present tender from year 2000 to 2002 had also failed - Despite attendance of as many as 20 major participants who attended pre-bid meeting, no one except petitioner appellant and three other ultimately came forward - Proposal was ex-facie not attractive one for potential investors, and all attempts to restore Lake and develop area as tourism hub failed when appellant was nowhere in picture - Appellant's contention that PIL before HC lacked bonafide and good faith should not be brushed aside totally although same had neither been reason with HC nor with SC to reject petition as SC ignored delay and lacked of bonafide on part of PIL - In instant case, where policy decision was taken way back from 1976 followed by Master Plans to develop particular chunk of land by adopting mode of private/public partnership method and global tender was floated, obviously private players were bound to participate specially in age when private partnership was not anathema - Hence, unless Detailed Project Report, Master Plan, Revenue Record indicated nature of land that project was fraught with risk of environmental degradation which could establish with facts and figures that decision was not in public interest, interference by Court adopting overall view smelling foul play at every level of administration was bound to make governance impossibility - SC was prone to infer that disputed area of lease deed borne out from revenue record was clearly confined to14.15 acres plus 8.65 acres and balance area of lease deed could not have been interfered with so as to set aside entire project - Period of lease which was granted in favour of appellant for period of 99 years and same should be reduced to period of 30 years only which could be the maximum period of the lease for the land under the rules which should start ordinarily from the date of its execution so as to expire on or before the period of 30 years - In interest of justice, that after expiry of 30 years of lease period and in case lease deed was not renewed in favour of appellant, State Government should compensate appellants at market value of project including compensation for loss of business and profit - It was clarified that in event of any dispute arose with respect to quantum of compensation, it might be resolved by availing remedy of arbitration mechanism provided in lease deed - It was further clear by that project comprising lease hold land was not in conflict with development of lake area or Jal Mahal monument so as to raise issues or concern regarding lake area or environment degradation as restoration and maintenance of Jal Mahal should not possibly disturb monument or lead to environmental degradation - In any view, dispute being confined to lease hold area for development of project which SC have now resolved, SC directed that appellant should be entitled to re-start project forthwith subject to what was recorded - Hence, judgment and order of HC was quashed and set aside to extent by which lease deed had been cancelled except area of 13 bighas 17 biswas equivalent to 8.65 acres and balance disputed area claimed to be lake bed comprising 14.15 acres should be notionally treated as part of lease deed but same should remain construction free zone where neither State Government nor appellant should have right to raise any construction on this area as same should remain exclusively for use of public promenade walkway free of charge - Order accordingly.
