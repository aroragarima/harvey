Ganpat v State Of Haryana & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 790; (2010) 12 SCC 59; 2011 CRLJ 701; JT 2010 (10) SC 450; 2010 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3377; 2010 (4) MLJ(Crl) 215; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 539; 2010(10) SCALE 237; [2010] 12 S.C.R. 400

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: (A) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, ss. 34, 148, 302, 323, 324, 325 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 319 - Acquittal - Challenged - Case u/ss. 148, 302, 323, 324, 325 r/w s. 34 of IPC was registered against accused persons - Accused persons pleaded that they were innocent and there was danger to their life and that the complainant party was the aggressor - Trial Court convicted 6 accused persons for the offences u/ss. 148, 302, 323, 324, 325 r/w s. 149 and one accused person was convicted u/s. 324 of IPC - Appeals were filed before HC - Appellant also filed criminal revision against judgment of Trial Court for not holding guilty four of the eleven accused, u/s. 302/149 of IPC - HC allowed the appeals and acquitted all the eleven accused persons and dismissed the criminal revision petition filed by the appellant - Hence, present appeal - Whether interference required in the impugned order of the HC acquitting all the eleven accused? - Held, from the evidence of prosecution witnesses as well as the defence that it was clear that accused persons also sustained injuries - There was no proper explanation by the prosecution about the injuries sustained by the accused - Further, there was no definite evidence as to the place of occurrence - Hence, from evidence of PWs 12, 13 as well as medical evidence about the injuries sustained by the persons belonging to the complainant's and accused party and also conduct of Investigating Officer in not recording statement of the injured accused, it was clear that two groups of people clashed inter se with weapons causing injuries to each other - Complainant party was the aggressor and in the absence of definite material and explanation from the prosecution side, the HC was right in acquitting all of them - Appeals dismissed.

Obiter dictum

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Principles to be kept in mind by Appellate court while dealing with appeals, particularly, against an order of acquittal - Held, (i) appellate court can review the evidence upon which the order of acquittal is founded and to come to its own conclusion; (ii) appellate court can also review the trial court's conclusion with respect to both facts and law (iii) While dealing with the appeal preferred by the State, it is the duty of the appellate court to marshal the entire evidence on record and by giving cogent and adequate reasons may set aside the judgment of acquittal (iv) order of acquittal is to be interfered with only when there are "compelling and substantial reasons" for doing so - (v) when the Trial Court has ignored the evidence or misread the material evidence or has ignored material documents like dying declaration/report of ballistic experts, etc. the appellate court is competent to reverse the decision of the trial court depending on the materials placed - Appeals dismissed.
