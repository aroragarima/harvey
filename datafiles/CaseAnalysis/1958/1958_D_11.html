Kanhaiyalal v Dr. D. R. Banaji and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 40; AIR 1958 SC 725; 1958 JabLJ 501; 1958 MPLJ 477; 1958 SCJ 891; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 333

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Attachment Of Property

Summary: Land & Property - Municipalities and Local Governments - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure,1908, ss. 9, 155, 157, 192, O. 21 r. 52, O. 40, r. 1 - Berar Land Revenue Code, 1928, ss. 157, 192, 155, 141 and 149 - One Bhagchand Jairamdas was occupant of a plot, situated in District town of Yeotmal -Bhagchand aforesaid had executed a mortgage-bond in favour of one Abubakar-The mortgagee aforesaid instituted a suit to enforce said mortgage - A receiver was appointed on during pendency of suit in respect of mortgaged properties including plot described above -(A)Whether a sale of property in custody of court or public officer is valid without notice being issued to such court or public officer?- Although notice to authorities is necessary, but absence of such notice would not render sale void ab initio as jurisdiction of court ordering sale does not however depend on issue of notice - Proceedings against a property in possession and management of a receiver without leave of court is illegal and would be rendered as contempt of court - Held, it was assumed for purposes of case that such a sale was only voidable and not void ab initio and could be declared illegal in a proper proceedings or a suit - (B) Whether other courts or tribunals can initiate proceedings against a property for which a receiver has already been appointed? - If other Courts or Tribunals of co-ordinate or exclusive jurisdiction were to permit proceedings to go on independently of Court which has placed custody of property in hands of Receiver, there was a likelihood of confusion in administration of justice and a possible conflict of jurisdiction - Further held, Property in custody of law through its duly appointed receiver is exempted from judicial process initiated by other courts or tribunals - (C) Whether a mortgage suit for sale of mortgaged land by receiver appointed barred? - Further held, sale of mortgage land without leave of court and without notice to receiver is illegal and therefore for recovery of its possession such a suit is no barred - Appeal dismissed.
