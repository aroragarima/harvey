The Kerala State Co-Operative Marketing Federation v State Bank Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

30 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 30; (2004) 2 SCC 425; 2004 (3) ALD(SC) 39; 2004 (1) AllCriC 693; 2004 (54) ALR 719; 2004 (2) AWC 1136; 2004 (2) BC 1; 2004 (4) Bom.C.R. 114; 2004 (2) CHN 81; 2004 (2) CLR 136; [2004] 118 Comp Cas 546; 2004 DRTC 70; JT 2004 (1) SC 621; 2004 (1) KLT 1117; 2004 (137) PLR 840; 2004 (3) RCR(Civil) 167; 2004 (3) RCR(Criminal) 21; 2004(2) SCALE 169; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 1; 2004 (1) SLT 826; 2004 (1) Supreme 969; 2004 (2) UPLBEC 1554

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal

Keywords: Banking Law, Banker And Customer

Summary: [A.] Banking & Finance - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 131, 131A - Bills of Exchange Act, 1882, s. 82 - Held, banker made no enquiry even after it was found out that that a cheque had been forged and stop payment notice had been issued Respondent bank has not discharged burden which lay upon it to show that it had acted in good faith and without negligence Banker, who encashes a cheque, in respect of which his client had no title, would become liable in conversion or for money received.
