K.R. Mohan Reddy v Messrs Net Work Inc. Represented Tr. M.D

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1427; (2007) 14 SCC 257; AIR 2008 SC 579; 2008 (1) AWC 322; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 1253; 2007 (4) RCR(Civil) 770; 2007 (2) RCR(Rent) 582; 2007(12) SCALE 587; 2007 (8) SCJ 233; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 872

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 41, r. 27 - Application for adducing additional evidence of the respondent - Allowed by the High Court - Legality of - Held, O. 41, r. 27(1)(a), (aa) and (b) refer to three different situations - Power of Appellate Court to pass any order there under is limited - Appellate Court must arrive at a finding that one or other conditions enumerated there under is satisfied - Good reason must also be shown for not producing evidence in Trial Court - Conditions precedent for application of O. 41, r. 27(1)(aa) and (b) are different - If cl. (aa) is to apply applicant has to show that ingredients precedent mentioned therein are satisfied - If cl. (b) is to apply Appellate Court is bound to consider entire evidences on record and come to an independent finding for arriving at a just decision - Respondent in its application categorically stated that books of accounts had been misplaced and same were discovered a few days prior to filing of said application while office was being shifted - HC did not enter into that question at all - HC held that application for adduction of additional evidence should be allowed, same being a requirement of court and/or was otherwise for substantial cause - HC failed to apply O. 41, r. 27 in correct perspective - Appellate Court should not pass an order so as to patch up weakness of evidence of unsuccessful party before Trial Court, but it will be different if court itself require evidence to do justice between parties - Ability to pronounce judgment is to be understood as ability to pronounce judgment satisfactorily to mind of court - Mere difficulty is not sufficient to issue such direction - Judgment set aside - Respondent may file additional affidavit in support of its application u/O. 41, r. 27 - Appeal disposed of.
