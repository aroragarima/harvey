Haryana Financial Corporation v Gurcharan Singh and another

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Radhakrishnan, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 833; 2014 (102) ALR 207; 2014 (1) AWC 212; 2014 (1) BC 242; 2014 (1) CLT(SC) 45; 2014 (1) CTC 98; JT 2014 (1) SC 289; 2014 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 269; 2014 (176) PLR 1; 2014 (1) RCR(Civil) 383; 2014 (122) RD 276; 2013(15) SCALE 296; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 832; 2014 (2) WLN(SC) 14

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Transfer of Property (Amendment) Supplementary Act, 1929

Summary: Transfer of Property Act, 1882-Sections 100 and 59-Charge-First respondent gave written undertaking that he would not dispose of his properties during currency of loan-Undertaking submitted by first respondent was on duly attested stamp paper, but was not registered under Registration Act-Question involved-Whether undertaking created any charge over properties mentioned in favour of appellant-Corporation?-No registered mortgage deed executed by first respondent and no title deed of property handed over by first respondent to appellant-Corporation-Mere undertaking that person would not dispose of properties mentioned, during currency of loan, would not confer any charge on immovable properties mentioned therein-Mere undertaking to create mortgage is not sufficient to create interest in any immovable property-Even if purpose of decree obtained in civil suit by second respondent as against first respondent was fraudulent and collusive one so to defeat undertaking given by first respondent in favour of appellant-Corporation, that would not confer any charge over properties, unless undertaking is registered-No error in judgment of lower appellate court which was affirmed by High Court.
