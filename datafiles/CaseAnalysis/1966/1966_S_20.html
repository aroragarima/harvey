Â Seth Gulabchand v Seth Kudilal and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 359; AIR 1966 SC 1734; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 623

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Evidence Act, s. 3 - Contract Act, s. 23 - Appreciation of evidence - 'Standard of proof' -Requirement of proving beyond reasonable doubt -Meaning of 'morality' within s. 23 - Held, scope of 'proved', 'disproved' in Civil and Criminal cases are alike - Evidence Act applies same standard of proof in all civil cases - It makes no difference between cases in which charges of fraudulent or criminal character are made and cases in which such charges are not made - In cases of Civil nature, requirement of proving 'beyond reasonable doubt' is not required - Immorality within s. 23 of Indian Contract Act is not confined to sexual immorality alone - Bribery is also clearly covered by s. 23 - Further held, fact that party is alleged to have accepted bribe in a civil case does not convert it into a criminal case, and ordinary rules applicable to civil cases apply - Appeal dismissed.
