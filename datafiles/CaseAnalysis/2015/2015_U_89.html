Surti Gupta v United India Insurance Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

17 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 319; 2015 (2) ACC 354; 2015 ACJ 1755; 2015 (3) ALD(SC) 189; 2015 (110) ALR 528; 2015 (2) AWC 1858; JT 2015 (3) SC 556; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 595; 2015(3) SCALE 795; 2015 (2) WLN(SC) 32

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor accident claim - Enhanced compensation - Entitlement - Appellant's mother met with an accident and succumbed to injuries - Appellant filed claim petition for compensation - Tribunal dismissed claim petition on the ground that appellant could not prove to be legal representation of deceased - However, appellant's appeal before HC was allowed and awarded compensation of Rs. 6,30,000 - Being dissatisfied with the amount of compensation, appellant filed instant appeal seeking enhancement.

Held, salary of the deceased at the time of her death taken by the HC is on the lower side considering that deceased was employed as a permanent teacher in a government school and she must have had at least 20-25 years of work experience at the time of her death. Hence, it would be just and proper to take the monthly income of the deceased at Rs.6,000/- per month. Further, on addition of 30% to the income of the deceased towards future prospects as per the principles laid down by SC in Sarla Verma v. Delhi Transport Corporation and Another, 2009 Indlaw SC 488, the monthly income for the calculation of future loss of dependency of the appellant would be Rs.7,800/-. Therefore, the annual income comes to Rs.93,600/-. On deduction of 1/3rd of the annual income towards personal expenses and applying the appropriate multiplier as per the principles laid down by in Sarla Verma case, the future loss of dependency suffered by the appellant is calculated at Rs.8,73,600/-. Further, the HC has certainly erred in awarding a meagre amount of only Rs.15,000/- for loss of estate, loss of love and affection and funeral expenses. Therefore, Rs.1,00,000/- is awarded towards loss of love and affection as per SC decision in Juju Kuruvila & Ors. v. Kunjujamma Mohan & Ors., 2013 Indlaw SC 355. An amount of Rs.1,00,000/- is awarded towards loss of estate as per the decision of SC in Kalpanaraj & Ors. v . Tamil Nadu State Transport Corporation, 2014 Indlaw SC 282. Further, a sum of Rs.25,000/- is awarded towards funeral expenses as per the principles laid down by SC in Rajesh & Ors. v. Rajbir Singh & Ors., 2013 Indlaw SCO 1392. The HC has further erred in awarding an interest at the rate of 6% per annum only, instead of 9% per annum on the compensation amount as per the principles laid by SC in Municipal Corporation of Delhi v. Association of Victims of Uphaar Tragedy, 2011 Indlaw SC 746. Hence, an interest at the rate of 9% per annum on the compensation amount is awarded. Thus, the total enhanced compensation payable to the appellant by the respondent-Insurance Company will be Rs.10,98,600/- with interest at the rate of 9% p.a. from the date of filing of the application till the date of payment. Appeal allowed.
