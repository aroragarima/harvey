State of Kerala v P. Sugathan and Another

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3155; (2000) 8 SCC 203; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1474; AIR 2000 SC 3323; 2000 CRLJ 4584; JT 2000 (Supp1) SC 161; 2000 (3) KLT 666; 2000 (4) RCR(Criminal) 369; 2000(6) SCALE 487; [2000] Supp3 S.C.R. 409

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 161 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 120B, 201, 302, 306 and 342 - (A) Charge of criminal conspiracy? - Held, there must be a meeting of minds resulting in ultimate decision taken by conspirators regarding commission of an offence and where factum of conspiracy is sought to be inferred from circumstances, prosecution has to show that circumstances giving rise to a conclusive or irresistible inference of an agreement between two or more persons to commit an offence - (B) Whether conviction and sentence of appellant was not legal and valid? - Further held, no reason to disbelieve statement of PW 1 - Besides his ocular testimony there is sufficient corroborative evidence which connects A1 with commission of crime of murder of deceased - Circumstantial evidence produced in case is sufficient to connect accused with commission of crime - It does not lead to any other inference than one of his involvement in crime - He has withstood cross examination and proved factum of death of deceased by A1 and destruction of his body - Appeal dismissed.
