Ghanshyam Sarda v Sashikant Jha, J. K. Jute Mills Company Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

18 November 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 824; (2017) 1 SCC 599; 2017 (2) ALD(SC) 19; 2017 (1) CLT(SC) 430; [2017] 200 Comp Cas 101; JT 2016 (11) SC 482; 2017 (2) MPLJ 520; 2017 (1) RCR(Civil) 1; 2016 (4) RCR(Criminal) 895; 2016(12) SCALE 128

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Jurisdiction, Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, Registration Act, 1908, Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Contempt Petition (Civil) No. 338 of 2014 in Civil Appeal No. 10221 of 2014, with Contempt Petition (C) No. 375/2014 in Civil Appeal No. 10223/2014

(A) Practice & Procedure - Registration Act, 1908, s. 47 - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 54 - Order of Court - Disobeyed - Challenged - Petitioners filed instant contempt petitions seeking that contemnors be punished for disobeying the Order of 08-05-2014 and at the same time the Conveyance Deed dt. 02-07-2014 alienating Katihar property be declared null and void - Whether any alienation or transfer was effected after the Order of 08-05-2014.

Held, conveyance deed was executed on 04-04-2013 on which date the entire consideration stood paid by transferee and was credited to the account of Company and as such title passed in favour of transferee well before the Order of 08-05-2014 and what was done on 02-07-2014 was a mere ministerial act. The documents presented for registration in April 2013 were not accepted for want of adequate stamp and registration fees. Such infirmity was removed and documents were then presented for registration. In such circumstances order of 08-05-2014 was not in any way violated by them. If the order was passed on 08-05-2014 restraining any alienation of capital assets of Company, Directors/servants of company ought to have taken steps to inform alleged Contemnor no. 7 to refrain from registering the document on 02-07-2014. Neither such steps were taken nor was Court informed on 08-05-2014 about the document executed on 04-04-2013, in which event SC could have passed appropriate Orders including restraint on registration. SC was of view that entire exercise was a clever device employed by Company and its Directors, in that, first an authority in favour of a Director was created who then resigned as Director but continued to register the document on the basis of erstwhile authorization and at the same time the person having resigned could claim lack of knowledge of Order of restraint passed by SC. Their actions were deliberate and designed to flout the Order of 08-05-2014. The involvement of alleged Contemnor nos.1,4,5,6,7 and 8 in the transfer assets of Company in teeth of Order of 08-05-2014 was apparent and clear. Petitions disposed of.

(B) Practice & Procedure - Registration Act, 1908, s. 47 - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 54 - Transfer of property - Violation - Whether effect of transfer or alienation of Katihar property in violation of Order of 08-05-2014.

Held, document dt. 04-04-2013 did not by itself create any interest nor did the title pass upon execution of such document on 04-04-2013 but it was only after registration on 02-07-2014 that title in Katihar property passed from Company in favour of transferee. It was clear that Katihar property was transferred in the teeth of Order of 08-05-2014 and ex facie there has been violation of the Order passed by SC. It was not the case of alleged contemnors that after adoption of Auditors' Report and Directors' Report a decision was taken by Company to sell or dispose of its Katihar property in pursuance of which due authorization was given to a competent person to execute the documents on behalf of Company. In instant case Company and its Directors/servants were certainly guilty of transgressing or violating Order of 08-05-2014 however, transferee and its Directors/servants have not violated the Order of 08-05-2014. The transferee and its Directors/servants were neither parties to proceedings nor were they served with the Order of 08-05-2014. No further orders were called for invalidating the registration dt. 02-07-2014. Further, according to the record transferee had parted with full consideration way back on 04-04-2013. SC do not think it appropriate to exercise power to invalidate the effect of registration of document on 02-07-2014. Thus, SC found Company and its Directors/servants namely alleged Contemnor nos.1, 4, 5, 6, 7 and 8 guilty of having violated Order of 08-05-2014 and imposed fine on Contemnors. Petitions disposed of.

Contempt Petition (C) Nos. 24-25/2015 in Civil Appeal Nos. 10224-10225/2014

(C) Practice & Procedure - Registration Act, 1908, s. 47 - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 54 - Judgment implementation - Objection - Legality - Petitioners filed instant contempt petitions alleging that contemnors have obstructed the implementation of judgment dt. 13-11-2014 passed by SC.

Held, the Acts alleged were in the nature of legal proceedings initiated by the contemnors and as such SC do not find any reason to invoke contempt jurisdiction. Petitions disposed of.
