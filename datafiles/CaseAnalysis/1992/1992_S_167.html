Chandra Mohan Tiwari And Another v State Of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1272; (1992) 2 SCC 105; (1992) SCC (Cr) 252; AIR 1992 SC 891; 1992 (3) CCR 688; 1992 (1) Crimes 553; 1992 CRLJ 1091; 1992 CrLR(SC) 200; JT 1992 (3) SC 258; 1992(1) SCALE 131; [1992] 1 S.C.R. 313; 1992 (1) UJ 533

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 379 - Evidence Act 1872, s. 3 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300, 363, 366 and 376 - Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970, s. 2 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 134(1), 134 A and 136 - (A) Scope of s. 379 of CrPC - High Court reversed acquittal and convicted accused - Maintainability of appeal against acquittal before SC - (B) Murder - Motive - Proof - Accused kidnapped victim and committed rape - Case u/ss. 363, 366 and 376 of IPC pending against them - Charge of murdering victim while they were enlarged on bail - (C) Credibility of interested witness - Held, (A) Lies as of right to SC - (B) There was overwhelming evidence both oral and documentary establishing strong motive for accused to commit murder - (B) They are not necessarily false witnesses though such witnesses must be subjected to close scrutiny - Appeal dismissed.
