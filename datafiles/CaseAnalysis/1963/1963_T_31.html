Â State of Punjab v S. Rattan Singh

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 359; AIR 1964 SC 1223; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 1098

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Summary: Banking & Finance - Debt Laws - Patiala Recovery of State Dues Act (4 of 2002 BK),ss. 4 and 11 - Provincial Insolvency Act, 1920, ss. 4, 24 and 33 - (A) Powers of head of department u/s. 4 - Scope - Held, he can determine not only amount of debt due but also whether alleged defaulter is defaulter or not - (B) Jurisdiction and power of Insolvency Court - Further held, insolvency court not precluded by s. 11 from going into question whether alleged debtor was really debtor and liable to pay sums said to be payable by him - It is well-settled that insolvency Court can, both at time of hearing petition for adjudication of a person as an insolvent and subsequently at stage of proof of debts, re-open transaction on basis of which creditor had secured judgment of a Court against debtor - Further held, insolvency Court can go behind a decree and probe into genuineness of debt on which it is founded - Appeal dismissed.
