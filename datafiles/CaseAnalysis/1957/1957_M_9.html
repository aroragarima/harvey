Martin Burn Limited v R.N Banerjee

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, S.K. Das, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 110; AIR 1958 SC 79; 1958 (1) LLJ 247; 1958 SCJ 255; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 514

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Labour Appellate Tribunal, Industrial Disputes 66 (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 151, O.41 r.21 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 33 - Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950, ss. 9, 22, 7 and 23 - (A) Whether Labour Appellate Tribunal has jurisdiction to set aside an ex parte order and restore appellant's application under s. 22 of Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act? - Held, s. 9 of Act refers to jurisdictional powers of Labour Court in both, original, appellate jurisdiction - Irrespective of jurisdiction Labour Appellate Tribunal is exercising, it is vested with powers as are vested in a civil Court under CPC,1908, when latter is hearing an appeal - Thus, Labour Appellate Tribunal has jurisdiction to set aside an ex parte order and restore appellant's application u/s. 22 of Act - (B) Whether Tribunal has authority to adjudicate upon any industrial dispute arising between employer and workman?- Further held, as per s. 22 of Act, Tribunal is only to consider whether ban levied on employer should be lifted - Tribunal may look into matter otherwise if it feels that employer did not provide to workman an opportunity to have his say in matter of charges leveled against him - Tribunal should substitute its own judgment for judgment in question - It has only got to consider whether view taken is a possible view on evidence on record - Appeal dismissed.
