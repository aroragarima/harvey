Jivarajbhai Ujamshi Sheth and Others v Chintamanrao Balaji and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 205; AIR 1965 SC 214; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 480

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Indian Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 14(2) - Partnership firm - Settlement of accounts - Arbitrator award - Disputed - Validity - Appellants and respondents were partners of firm and deed of partnership was executed between them - Appellants desired to retire from partnership and issued notice to remaining partners and matter was referred to sole arbitrator who passed award dt. 9-1-1959 - Award was filed in Trial Court and was objected by respondents - Trial Court upheld objections of respondents and set aside Award - On appeal, HC confirmed decision of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Orders of Trial Court and HC were justified - Held, arbitrator was bound to settle 'final account' of retiring partners strictly as per terms and conditions of partnership agreement - Arbitrator should first make valuation of firm in respect of movable and immovable properties and value of debts due etc before settling account of retiring partners - Arbitrator took only goodwill value in determining amounts to be allotted to retiring partners but failed to take valuation of outstandings, the stock-in-trade and movables and immoveable property as per partnership deed - Arbitrator should value assets of firm as per 'book value' but excluded his jurisdiction by including depreciation and appreciation of property, outstandings and dead stock - Award of arbitrator was not valid and deserved to be set aside - Judgments of Trial Court and HC were justified - Appeal dismissed.
