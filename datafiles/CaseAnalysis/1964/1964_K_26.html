State of Rajasthan and Another v Karamchand Thappar and Bros

Supreme Court of India

09 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 437; AIR 1965 SC 913; 1965 (16) STC 412

Case Digest

Subject: Trade; Contract & Commercial

Summary: (A) Trade - Contract & Commercial - Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1954 s. 2(f) - Colliery Control Order, 1945, cl.8 - Supply of coal - Applicability of provision - Petitioner carried business of supplying coal throughout India - Petitioner branch retail business and supervised supply of coal to consumers by collieries - Petitioner secured monopoly to sell coal of collieries belonging to equitable coal company for certain areas - Company entered into contract with Govt. of Rajasthan for supply of coal for one month to power house - Various similar agreements were entered into for regular supply of coal to said power house - Petitioner supplied coal under agreements - Petitioner claimed that it was getting brokerage from equitable coal company, whose coal was supplied to power house - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether petitioner fell within s. 2(f) of the Act - Held, coal could be supplied to State of Rajasthan - Petitioner was not dealer within definition of word 'dealer' in first para. of s. 2(f) of the Act - Petitioner did not sell or supply coal - Petitioner was to procure supply of coal - Under direction issued u/cl. (8) of the Order, it was equitable coal company that supplied coal directly to Rajasthan Govt. - No property in coal ever passed or could pass to petitioner - However, agreement contemplated sale by petitioner to Rajasthan Govt. agreement could not be carried out in view of provisions of Colliery Control Order, and supplies from collieries were concerned, because collieries could not but obey directions issued u/cl. (8) of the Order - Therefore, petitioner did not fall within s. 2(f) of the Act - Appeal allowed.

(B) Trade - Agent - Sale - Whether petitioner could be called agent - Held, cl. (1) of agreement mentioned that collieries from which coal was supplied and these collieries belonged to equitable coal company - Petitioner charged no remuneration from Rajasthan Govt.- It had to supply coal at controlled rate and, therefore, profit could make from brokerage which was received from equitable coal company - Therefore, petitioner was acting as agent of equitable coal company - Thus, petitioner was an agent within explanation to s. 2(f) of the Act, and, therefore, he was deemed to be 'dealer' for purposes of the Act - appeal was accepted and case remitted to HC - Appeal allowed.
