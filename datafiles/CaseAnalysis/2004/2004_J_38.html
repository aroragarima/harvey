State Of Haryana v Jarnail Singh And Others

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 347; (2004) 5 SCC 188; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1571; AIR 2004 SC 2491; 2004 (2) CGLJ 129; 2004 CRLJ 2541; JT 2004 (Supp2) SC 362; 2004 (2) KLT 1024; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 960; 2004(5) SCALE 245; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 864

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 15, 41, 42, 42(1), 43, 50 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, s. 428 - Non compliance with requirements of ss 42, 50 - If a public conveyance is searched in a public place, officer making search is not required to record his satisfaction - S 50 of NDPS Act was not applicable since contraband was recovered on search of a vehicle and there was no personal search involved - Requirement of proviso to s 42 was also not required to be complied with since recovery was made at a public place and was, therefore, governed by Section 43 of Act which did not lay down any such requirement - Superintendent of Police was a member of search party and was exercising his authority u/s. 41 of NDPS Act, proviso to Section 42 not attracted - Appeal allowed.
