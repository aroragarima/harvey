State Through SPE and CBI, Ap v M. Krishna Mohan and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1064; (2007) 14 SCC 667; AIR 2008 SC 368; 2007 (4) JCC 3294; JT 2007 (12) SC 413; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 882; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 570

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Fingerprints, Specimen Handwriting

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 409, 420 467 and 477-A r/w 120-B; Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 - Appeal against acquittal - High Court completely misdirected itself in passing a judgment of acquittal in favour of the respondents - Trial Judge had assigned cogent reasons in support of its findings - High Court did not meet the reasonings - Giving of finger impressions or of specimen writing or of signatures by an accused person, though it may amount to furnishing evidence in the larger sense, is not included within the expression "to be a witness" - Upheld the conviction and sentence as awarded Trial Judge as against respondent No.1, in view of special reasons recorded impose a sentence of rigorous imprisonment of three months on respondent No.2 - Appeals allowed.
