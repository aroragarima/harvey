Messrs Prakash Cotton Mills Private Limited v B. Sen and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 5; AIR 1979 SC 673; 1979 (4) E.L.T. 241

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Customs Act, 1962 - Import of textile goods - Duty liability - Rate of duty - Appellant/assessee was importer and trader of textile products - Customs Authority charged duty on goods that are cleared from warehouse - Appellant files revision on said order of Authority on ground that said goods were imported before amendment of ss. 14 & 15 of Act which were not liable to duty - Revisional Court had dismissed revision of assessee - Hence instant appeals.

Held, that Customs Authorities and Central Govt. were quite right in taking view that rate of duty applicable to imported goods had to be determined according to law which was prevalent on date they were actually removed from warehouse, namely, amended ss. 14 and 15 of 1962 Act. There is therefore no force in argument that requirement of amended s. 15 of Act should have been ignored simply because goods were imported before it came into force or that their bills of lading or bills of entry were lodged before that date. As Court finds no force in these appeals, they are dismissed with costs. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - Rate of duty applicable to imported goods had to be determined according to law which was prevalent on date they were actually removed from warehouse.
