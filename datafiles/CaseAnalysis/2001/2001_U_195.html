Gurdev Singh v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 November 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19925; (2002) SCC (Cr) 191; (2002) 1 SCC 545; AIR 2002 SC 10; 2001 (4) CCR 305; 2002 (1) Crimes 115; 2002 (2) CRJ 691; JT 2001 (9) SC 374; 2001(8) SCALE 27; 2001 (7) SLT 698; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 105; 2001 (8) Supreme 121; 2002 (1) UC 126

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: COFEPOSA, Foreign Trade (Development And Regulation) Act, 1992, Foreign Trade (Regulation) Rules, 1993

Summary: Criminal - Detention - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974 - Customs Act, 1962 - Foreign Trade (Development And Regulation) Act, 1992 - Foreign Trade (Regulation) Rules, 1993 - Petitioner is father of detenue who was detained u/s. 3 of COFEPOSA, 1974 - Whether detention order was legal? - Question whether detention order suffers from non - application of mind by detaining authority depends on facts and circumstances of case, nature of activities alleged against detenu, materials collected in support of such allegations, propensity and potentiality of detenu in indulging in such activities etc. - Act does not lay down any set parameters for arriving at subjective satisfaction by detaining authority - Held, appeal is devoid of any merit - Appeal dismissed.
