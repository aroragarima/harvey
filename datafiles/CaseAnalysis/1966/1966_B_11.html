Basant Singh v Janki Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 August 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 309; AIR 1967 SC 341; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal

Summary: Civil Procedure - Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Women & Children - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 22, r. 10 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 17 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 14 - Ramyad Singh was a member of a joint family and has eight annas interest in the joint family properties - He died issueless, leaving his widow, Bhagwano Kunwar - The date of his death is in dispute - After his death, his widow filed the suit for partition of the joint family properties claiming eight annas share therein - She contended that Ramyad Singh died in 1939 after the passing of the Hindu Women's Rights to Property Act, 1937, and she was entitled to maintain the suit for partition - The defendants contended that Ramyad Singh died ill 1936 before the passing of the Act and she was entitled to maintenance only - The main point in controversy is, did Ramyad Singh die in 1936 or in 1939? - If he died in 1936, Bhagwano Kunwar was not entitled to maintain the suit for partition and the suit was liable to be dismissed? - Held, in the plaint filed by the respondents contained a clear admission that Ramyad Singh died in 1939 - It is true that Bhagwano Kunwar relied only upon the statement that Ramyad Singh died in 1939 and was not prepared to accept the statement that she had surrendered her share to the other members and was entitled to maintenance only - But she tendered the entire plaint, and she did not object to the admissibility or proof of any of the statements made therein - All the statements in the plaint are admissible as evidence - The court is not bound to accept all the statements as correct - The court may accept some of the statements and reject the rest - The statement in the plaint as to the date of death of Ramyad Singh must be read as an admission in favour of Bhagwano Kunwar - An admission by a party in a plaint signed and verified in a prior suit is an admission within the meaning of s. 17 of Act and may be proved against him in other litigations - Appeal allowed.
