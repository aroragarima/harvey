Dalchand v Municipal Corporation, Bhopal and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 June 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 58; (1984) SCC (Cr) 311; (1984) 2 SCC 486; AIR 1983 SC 303; 1982 BLJR 420; 1983 CRLJ 448; 1982 CRLR 297; 1982(1) SCALE 573; 1982 UJ 559

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, r. 9(j) - Was r. 9(j) mandatory or directory? - Held, weighing of consequence of holding a provision to be mandatory or directory is vital and, more often than not, determinative of very question whether provision is mandatory or directory - Where design of statue is avoidance or prevention of public mischief, but enforcement of a particular provision literally to its letter will tend to defeat design, provision must be held to be directory, so that proof of prejudice in addition to non-compliance of provision in necessary to invalidate act complained of - Where effect of non-compliance with rule was such as to wholly deprive right of person to challenge Public Analyst's Report by obtaining report of Director of Central Food Laboratory, there might be just cause for complaint, as prejudice would then be writ large - Where no prejudice was caused there could be no cause for complaint - R. 9(j) was directory and not mandatory - Petition dismissed.
