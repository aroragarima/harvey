Sovintorg (India) Limited v State Bank of India, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

11 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 212; (1999) 6 SCC 406; AIR 1999 SC 2963; 2000 (1) ALD(SC) 14; 1999 (37) ALR 130; 1999 (4) AWC 3308; 2000 (1) Bom.C.R. 41; 1999 (2) CCC 34; [2000] 99 Comp Cas 126; 2000 (3) CompLJ 398; 1999 (2) CPJ(Sc) 4B; JT 1999 (6) SC 10; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 5; [1999] Supp1 S.C.R. 337

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Summary: Consumer Protection - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 s. 34 - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Retention of amount - Compensation and interest - Entitlement - Appellant/company deposited cheque for Rs. 1,00,000/- for collection with respondent/bank, though proceeds of cheque were collected on 17-6-1983 but they were not deposited in appellant's account - Appellant filed complaint before State Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission detailing entitlement to principal amount, interest @ 24%, compensation for business losses and nominal damages for loss of prestige - State Commission partly allowed complaint by directing respondent to pay Rs. 1,00,000/- with interest at rate of 12% - National Commission confirmed order of State Commission - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant was entitled to payment of compensation and interest on allegedly wrong retention of amount by respondent - Held, State Commission as well as National Commission had concurrently found that amount realised by collection of cheque in question could not be deposited apparently on basis of understanding between parties which authorized bank to keep same as margin money for guarantee furnished by Bank on behalf of complainant company to Chief Controller of Exports and Imports, it was found that bank was not wrong in having retained said amount in its custody - Appellant further found to have not proved as to from which date contract for guarantee stood terminated, however, said contract was found to be in force as late as in 1987 - Therefore, in absence of any negligence, SC did not find any substance in submission made by appellant to modify orders of State Commission and National Commission for directing payment of compensation on allegedly wrong retention of amount as was submitted in complaint - There was no contract between parties regarding payment of interest on delayed deposit or on account of delay on part of opposite party to render services, interest could not be claimed u/s. 34 CPC as its provisions have not been specifically made applicable to proceedings under the Act - However, general provision of s. 34 being based upon justice, equity and good conscious would authorise Redressal Forums and Commissions to also grant interest appropriately under circumstance of each case and interest might also be awarded in lieu of compensation or damages in appropriate cases - Thus, appeal was partly allowed by modifying orders of State Commission as well as National Commission with direction that appellant should be entitled to payment of Rs. 1,00,000/- with interest at rate of 15% per annum with quarterly rests from date when amount was received by it till date of payment - Appeals allowed.
