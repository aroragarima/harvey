State of Bihar v Murad Ali Khan and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 33; (1988) 4 SCC 655; (1989) SCC (Cr) 27; AIR 1989 SC 1; 1989 BLJ 168; 1988 BLJR 795; 1988 (3) Crimes 822; 1989 CRLJ 1005; 1988 CRLR 712; JT 1988 (4) SC 124; 1988(2) SCALE 933; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 455; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 455

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Wild Life Protection Act, 1972, ss. 2(16), 9(1), 51 and 56 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 429 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 20(2) - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 210, 300 and 482 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 26 - (A) Wild Life Protection Act - (1) Offence under - Cognizance - When can be taken of - (2) Double jeopardy - Same offence - Offence u/s. 9(1) r/w s. 51 of Act - (B) CrPC, 1973 - Exercise of inherent powers - Allegations in complaint, taken on their face value amounting to offence against Wild Life Protection Act - Quashing of proceeding by High Court, as no prima facie offence made out - Permissibility - Held, (A) Cognizance can be taken only on complaint of particular statutory functionary, even if police register a case for alleged offence against Act, provisions of s. 210, CrPC would not be attracted - (B) Is not same or substantially same as offence u/s. 429 IPC - Impermissible - Appeals allowed.
