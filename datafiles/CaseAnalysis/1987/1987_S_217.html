Aarti Gupta And Ors. v State Of Punjab And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, G.L. Oza, M.N. Venkatachaliah

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28399; (1988) 1 SCC 258; (1988) SCC (L&S) 322; AIR 1988 SC 481; 1988 (1) CCC 977; JT 1987 (4) SC 613; 1987(2) SCALE 1273; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 244; 1988 (1) UJ 351

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 226 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Quota for SC / ST candidates in admission to MBBS/BDS - Competency of Govt. as to lowering of qualifying percentage of marks due to non-availability of sufficient candidates for reserved quota - Arbitrariness and bar as imposed by estoppel, if - Held, Govt. is competent to do - Action is neither arbitrary nor hit by rules of estoppel - Appeal dismissed.
