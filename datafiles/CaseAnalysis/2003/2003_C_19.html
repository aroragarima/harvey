Lalit Popli v Canara Bank and others

Supreme Court of India

18 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 164; (2003) SCC (L&S) 353; (2003) 3 SCC 583; AIR 2003 SC 1795; 2003 (51) ALR 2; 2003 (5) AWC 3574; 2003 (4) ESC 421; 2003 (97) FLR 1153; 2003 (2) JhrCR(SC) 126; JT 2003 (5) SC 494; 2003 LabIC 1170; 2003 (2) LLJ 324; 2003 (2) LLN 313; 2003(2) SCALE 358; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 100; 2003 (2) SLJ 409; 2003 (4) SLR 737; 2003 (2) SLT 418; 2003 (2) Supreme 253; 2003 (2) UPLBEC 1673

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Service - order of dismissal - on plea the learned Single Judge held that the Enquiry Officer should not have rested his decision on the opinion of the Handwriting Expert - held, evidence of Handwriting Expert need not be invariably corroborated. It is for the Court to decide whether to accept such an uncorroborated evidence or not. Further, a bank employee deals with public money. The nature of his work demands vigilance with the inbuilt requirement to act carefully. Any carelessness invites action. - Whether the learned Single Judge was justified in interfering with the order of dismissal passed by the disciplinary authority - held, the jurisdiction of the High Court is circumscribed by limits of judicial review to correct errors of law or procedural errors reading to manifest injustice or violation of principles of natural justice. Judicial review is not akin to adjudication of the case on merits as an Appellate Authority.
