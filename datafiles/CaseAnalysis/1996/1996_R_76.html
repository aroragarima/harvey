Commercial Taxes Officer, Circle D, Jaipur v Rajasthan Electricity Board, Jaipur

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3746; (1997) 10 SCC 330; 1997 (104) STC 89

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, ss. 8(1) and 8(3)(b) - Central Sales Tax (Registration and Turnover) Rules, 1957, r. 13 - Which all items can be held to be related to business of generation and distribution of electricity for purpose of falling within scope of s. 8 of Act, 1956? - What is used in distribution of electricity or intended for such use falls within scope of s. 8(3)(b) r/w r. 13 of Central Sales Tax Rules - Held, if a process or activity was so integrally related to ultimate manufacture of goods so that without that process or activity manufacture may, even if theoretically possible, be commercially inexpedient, goods intended for use in process or activity as specified in r. 13 would qualify for special treatment, motor vehicles and soap, paints, raincoats and battery cells, to extent mentioned, are integrally related to distribution of electricity and their non - use would make distribution commercially inexpedient - Appeals dismissed.
