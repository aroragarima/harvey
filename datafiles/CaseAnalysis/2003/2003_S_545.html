Paramjeet Gambhir and another v State of Madhya Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

13 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 231; (2003) 4 SCC 276; AIR 2003 SC 1338; 2003 (51) ALR 207; 2003 (2) ESC 189; JT 2003 (5) SC 99; 2003(3) SCALE 240; 2003 (5) SLR 137; 2003 (3) SLT 217; 2003 (2) Supreme 883; 2003 (2) UPLBEC 1700

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Medical and Dental Post-Graduate Examination Rules, 2001

Summary: Admission - mid-term - MP Medical and Dental Post-Graduate Examination Rules, 2002 - challenge to the vires of Rule 15.8 of 2002 Rules - Held, the State Government made a provision in the 2002 Rules whereby the system of opt for waiting was abolished but subsequently in the counter-affidavit filed in the writ petition it was pleaded that Rule 15.8 had been deleted and the old system of opt for waiting had been revived. In view of this stand of the State Government the appellants were entitled to participate in the second counselling wherein all the vacant seats/colleges should have been made available. - Directions issued to the respondents to consider the candidature of the appellants for giving them admission in a Post-Graduate course in the disciplines in which seats are still lying vacant taking into consideration their rank and choice.
