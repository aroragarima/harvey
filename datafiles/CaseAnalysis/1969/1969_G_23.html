Goswami Shri Mahalaxmi Vahuji v Ranchhoddas Kalidas and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 September 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, Vishishtha Bhargava, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 493; (1969) 2 SCC 853; AIR 1970 SC 2025; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 275

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Suit for declaration - Whether properties detailed in plaint Sch. A and B are properties of a public religious trust? - Held, specific plea taken in written statement that Vallabh Cult does not permit any dedication in favour of an idol and in fact there was no dedication in favour of that idol - 1st defendant took plea which was wholly inconsistent with that had been pleaded earlier - Burden of proof - Public temples are generally built or raised by public and deity installed to enable members of public or a section thereof to offer worship - If a temple is proved to have originated as a private temple or its origin is unknown or lost in antiquity then there must be proof to show that it is being used as a public temple - Appearance of a temple is a relevant circumstance, it is by no means a decisive one - In examining evidence adduced by plaintiffs in proof of fact that temple in question is a public temple, tests laid down by courts for determining whether a given temple is a public temple or not - Temple has all along been primarily maintained from contributions made by devotees belonging to Vallabha School - Income from temple properties has to be primarily used for expenses of Sevas and Utsavas in temple, upkeep, renovation and improvements of temple premises but subject to these demands, Maharaj has a right to utilise temple income in maintaining himself and his family in a reasonably comfortable manner - Appeal dismissed.
