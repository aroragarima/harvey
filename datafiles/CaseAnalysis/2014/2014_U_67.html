Safai Karamchari Andolan and others v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

27 March 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, N.V. Ramana, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 188; (2014) 11 SCC 224; 2014 (3) AWC 2835; 2014 (3) CTC 177; JT 2014 (4) SC 409B; 2014 (3) KarLJ 529; 2014 (3) LLJ 334; 2014(4) SCALE 165; [2014] 4 S.C.R. 197; 2014 (2) UPLBEC 1157

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Human Rights; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Protection of Civil Rights Act, 1955, Untouchability, Untouchability (Offences) Act, 1955, Employment Of Manual Scavengers And Construction Of Dry Latrines (Prohibition) Act, 1993, Directions, Prohibition of Employment as Manual Scavengers and their Rehabilitation Act, 2013, National Commission for Safai Karamcharis Act, 1993, Practice of untouchability, Traditional occupation, Removal of human excrements from dry toilets, Prohibition of employment of manual scavengers, Enforcement of 1993 Act

Summary: Prohibition of Employment as Manual Scavengers and their Rehabilitation Act, 2013 (2013 Act)-Employment of Manual Scavengers and Construction of Dry Latrines (Prohibition) Act, 1993 (1993 Act)-Enactment of 2013 Act in no way, neither dilutes constitutional mandate of Article 17 of the Constitution nor does it condone inaction on part of Union and State Governments under 1993 Act-What 2013 Act does in addition is to expressly acknowledge Articles 17 and 21 of the Constitution rights of persons engaged in sewage cleaning and cleaning tanks as well persons cleaning human excretion on railway tracks-State Governments and Union Territories to fully implement various provisions of 2013 Act and take appropriate action for non-implementation as well as violation of provisions contained in 2013 Act-Inasmuch as Act 2013 occupies entire field, no further monitoring by Supreme Court-Persons aggrieved permitted to approach authorities concerned at first and thereafter High Court having jurisdiction-Certain directions issued.
