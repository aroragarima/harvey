Â Basant Kumar Sarkar and Others v Eagle Rolling Mills Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 February 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 112; AIR 1964 SC 1260; 1964 BLJR 477; 1964 (8) FLR 334; 1965 (44) ILR(Pat) 1000; 1964 (2) LLJ 105; 1964 (2) SCJ 566; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 913

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Employees State Insurance Act, 1948, s.1(3) - Minimum Wages Act, 1948 - Excessive delegation - Notices - Struck down - Invalid - Appellants/workmen of three respondent concerns alleged that impugned section had contravened art. 14 of Constitution and suffers from vice of excessive delegation and as such was invalid - HC rejected plea and dismissed - Main grievance made by appellants was that as a result of s.1(3) of 1948 Act - Act does not prescribe any considerations in light of which Central Govt. proceed to act u/s.1(3) and conferred Central Govt. treated as invalid - Hence, instant appeals - Whether notices and circulars issued by respondent No.1 u/s.1(3) of 1948 Act was valid - Held, cannot amount to excessive delegation - Notices were invalid and should be struck down - Proper remedy was available to appellants to ventilate their grievances in respect of notices and circulars was to take recourse to s.10 of 1948 Act or seek relief u/ss. 74, 75 of Act - Appeals were not entitled to curtail benefits provided to appellants by them and that benefits were not similar quantitatively to benefits under scheme which had been brought into force under Act - Impugned s.1(3) of 1948 Act was not shown to be constitutionally invalid - S.1(3) of 1948 Act suffers from excessive, delegation and was therefore invalid - Appeal disposed of.
