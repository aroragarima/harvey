Union of India and Another v N. Chandrasekharan and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1942; (1998) 3 SCC 694; (1998) SCC (L&S) 916; AIR 1998 SC 795; JT 1998 (1) SC 295; 1998 LabIC 613; 1998 (2) LLN 16; 1998(1) SCALE 264; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 419; 1998 (3) SLJ 21; 1998 (1) SLR 612; 1998 (2) SLT 651; 1998 (1) Supreme 457; 1998 (2) UPLBEC 1164

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Research, Personality

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Promotion of purchase officer in Space Research Organisation - Selection 30% marks allotted to interview and 20% to ACR's - Constitutionality of Weightage - Plea, marks allotted are unduly disproportionate or that authorities cannot fix minimum marks - Held, cannot be termed as arbitrary or violative of arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution - Cannot subsequently raise plea - Appeal allowed.
