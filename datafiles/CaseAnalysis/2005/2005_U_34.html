People’S Union For Civil Liberties v Union Of India & Anr

Supreme Court of India

18 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1833; (2005) 2 SCC 436; JT 2005 (1) SC 283; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 494

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Human Rights; Service

Keywords: Equal Remuneration Act, 1976, The United Nations

Summary: (A) Human Rights - Advocates & Judges - Service - Protection of Human Rights Act, 1993, s. 3(2)(d) - National Human Rights Commission (NHRC) - Appointment of police officer as member - Permissibility - Respondent no. 2, a police officer, retired as Director of Central Bureau of Investigation was appointed as member of NHRC - Whether a police officer would fall in category stipulated u/s. 3(2)(d) and was appointment of such a person consistent with language of section and true intendment of the Act - Held, number of cases reported to NHRC related to acts of omission and commission by the members of police forces - NHRC Report for the year 2001-02 showed that a large number of cases relating to custodial deaths and police encounter deaths came up for inquiry and consideration before Commission - Officers of police forces while being members of service necessarily came across such cases and an individual officer might be very good but something inbuilt in service as a class was relevant consideration - Commission had also to deal with types of cases, which officers had sometimes to defend, on account of nature of their service - Further, knowledge or practical experience in relation to commission of crime, investigation and solving a crime which may show violation of human rights was one thing and knowledge or experience relating to protection of life, liberty, equality and dignity of individual guaranteed by Constitution or embodied in International Covenants and enforceable by Courts in India was altogether different - Requirement of s. 3(2)(d) was of latter and not former - Eligibility of a person to be considered on basis of who had to become a part of decision-making process of NHRC and not process of investigation which Commission might direct to be conducted - Scheme of the Act was to protect and implement human rights including those envisaged in art. 21 of Constitution and International Covenants - Members referred to in s. 3(2)(d) were required to have knowledge and practical experience in matters relating to human rights of type expected from those covered u/s. 3(2)(a), (b) and (c) - Further, person to be appointed u/s. 3(2)(d) should also be one who should act as a Chairperson under the contingency contemplated by s. 7 of the Act - In construing any provision in domestic legislation which was ambiguous, in sense that it was capable of more than one meaning, meaning which conformed most closely to provisions of any international instrument was to be preferred, in absence of any domestic law to contrary - Thus, s. 3(2)(d) was to be read keeping in view of the Paris principles 1991 - Further, proposal to appoint police officers on two earlier occasions was dropped when Chairperson of NHRC expressed his opinion against appointments of such persons - Hence, construing s. 3(2)(d) of the Act in consonance with Paris principles 1991, a police officer would be ineligible to be appointed as a member of NHRC - Rule made absolute - Appeal allowed.

(B) Human Rights - Advocates & Judges - Service - Protection of Human Rights Act, 1993, s. 4 - Appointment of member of NHRC - Consultation of appointment Committee - Requirement - Whether effective consultation with Committee members was absent and, therefore, recommendation was not in accordance with s. 4 of the Act - Held, u/s. 4 every appointment was required to be made after obtaining the recommendations of a Committee - Requirement of the section was not of 'consultation' but of recommendation of the Committee that were required to be made after taking into consideration all relevant factors eschewing irrelevant factors - Since notice of meeting had been given to Leader of Opposition in the Council of States, it should not be said that recommendations of Committee would stand vitiated as a result of his non-participation - However, for meaningful and purposeful recommendation, there ought to be complete disclosure of relevant factors considering that appointment was being recommended for a highly expert body in relation to protection of human rights - Members of Committee were not informed that on earlier two occasions, views of Chairperson of NHRC were sought and since Chairperson was opposed to the appointment of a member of the force, proposal was dropped - Appeal allowed.

D. M. Dharmadhikari

(C) Human Rights - Advocates & Judges - Service - Protection of Human Rights Act, 1993, s. 3(2)(d) - National Human Rights Commission (NHRC) - Appointment of police officer as member - Permissibility - Whether a police officer would fall in category stipulated u/s. 3(2)(d) and was appointment of such a person consistent with language of section and true intendment of the Act - Held, merely because there were instances of human rights violations by some members of the police was no ground to exclude all police officers as a class from the membership of the Commission - Paris principles 1991 could at best be taken aid of to understand and interpret the provisions of the Act but not to substitute or supplement it - Selection of a high-ranking police officer known for high integrity and character with record of commendable service should not be held to be beyond purview of s. 3(2)(d) of the Act - S. 3(2)(d) empowered Selection Committee to induct two members other than Judges, having knowledge and practical experience in matters relating to human rights - As membership of Commission was required to be of a pluralistic complexion, induction by selection of members, either from police force or public would in no manner adversely affect independence, efficacy or functioning of Commission - A police officer, on his appointment to Commission, would enjoy same rights and independence of action as other members do - It should not be said that under the provisions of s. 3(2), police officers were not intended to be included in membership of NHRC - A police officer qua purely a police officer, with no knowledge or practical experience in handling matters of human rights, might be found unfit to be chosen as member but a police officer with necessary knowledge and experience in matters of human rights should not be held to be disqualified merely because he was a police officer - Perception of Selection Committee regarding fitness of a particular person chosen for membership, by considering him to be possessing required knowledge and practical experience in matters relating to human rights, should not be faulted by Court by substituting its own opinion for opinion of Selection Committee - Such a course of action was not legally permissible to Court - Court should not sit over selection of Selection Committee unless there was found to be any infraction of law under which selection was made - Service record of respondent no. 2, in any manner, made him disqualified for appointment as member of Commission and it showed his knowledge and practical experience in matters relating to human rights - Merely because a more suitable person ought to have been chosen was no ground to invalidate appointment of respondent no. 2 who should not be held to be disqualified u/s. 3(2)(d) of the Act - Appeal dismissed.
