Mousam Singha Roy & Ors v State Of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 641; (2003) 12 SCC 377; JT 2003 (7) SC 176; 2003(6) SCALE 533

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Evidence Of Handwriting Expert

Summary: Indian Penal code, 1860 - ss. 34, 120-B, 201, 302 - Circumstantial evidence - Held, the circumstances relied on by the prosecution in this case are neither fully established nor are consistent with the hypothesis of the guilt of the accused. These circumstances do not exclude the hypothesis of innocence of the accused, therefore, the appellants are entitled to the benefit of reasonable doubt - Further held, the burden of proof in a criminal trial never shifts, and it is always the burden of the prosecution to prove its case beyond reasonable doubt on the basis of acceptable evidence.
