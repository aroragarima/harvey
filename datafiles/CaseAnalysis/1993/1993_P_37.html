Parvej Aktar and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, L.M. Sharma, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1127; (1993) 2 SCC 221; JT 1993 (1) SC 453; 1993(1) SCALE 456; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 803; 1993 (1) UJ 489

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Controlled, Sugarcane Control Order, Constitution (Third Amendment) Act, 1954, Handlooms (Reservation Of Articles For Production) Act, 1985, Cotton Textiles (Control) Order 1948, Handlooms (Reservation of Articles for Production) Rules, 1986

Summary: Constitution - Constitution (Third Amendment) Act, 1954 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Essential Commodities Act, 1955 - Handlooms (Reservation of Articles for Production) Act, 1985 - Industries (Development and Regulation) Act, 1951 - Reasonable restriction - Validity - Ultra vires - Living wage - Reservation - The Handlooms (Reservation of Articles for Production) Act, 1985 was passed to provide for reservation of certain articles for exclusive production by handlooms so as to protect the handloom sector - The 1985 Act u/s. 4 provided for constitution of an Advisory Committee to make recommendations to the Central Govt. to determine the of any article or class of articles that may be reserved for exclusive production by handlooms - Central Govt. constituted an Advisory Committee and on its recommendations an order was issued directing, certain articles/class of articles to be exclusively reserved for production by handlooms - Order was challenged by the instant W.P. on the grounds that the 1985 Act was ultra vires the Constitution and violative of arts. 14 and 19(1)(g) of the Constitution - Whether the 1985 Act was violative of art. 14 and whether the reservation of certain articles was violative of art. 19(1)(g) of the Constitution, and thus, an unreasonable restriction - Held, a monopoly right could not be conferred on a citizen under the Constitution nor could it be justified under the Constitution - There was no question of monopoly created in favor of handloom industry - Certain kinds of textiles were reserved to the handloom industry and rest were available for power loom owners, which they manufactured - Only those items which had traditionally been manufactured on handlooms had been reserved for the handloom sector - Protection had been given by the Govt. to handloom weavers because the livelihood of handloom weavers was threatened due to the production of all types of items and varieties by the power loom industry - Handloom weavers were economically very poor and had no alternative employment in the rural areas unless protected through reservation of varieties for them - Restrictions were not only reasonable but also fully justified u/art. 43 of the Constitution - Also, the 1985 Act was not violative of art. 14 of the Constitution, a reasonable classification was permissible under the equality clause - Reservation was only to protect the handloom sector which was otherwise highly disadvantaged the main articles manufactured by power loom sector were not included in reservation and thus there was no unreasonable restriction u/art. 19(1)(g) of the Constitution - Petition dismissed.
