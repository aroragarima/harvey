Dharampal and Others v Uttar Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 842; (2008) 12 SCC 208; 2008 (3) ACC 1; 2008 (6) ADJ 332; AIR 2008 SC 2312; 2008 (72) ALR 484; 2008 (3) AWC 2480; 2008 (1) CCC 369; 2008 (4) JhrCR(SC) 79; 2008 (2) KLT 691; 2008 (5) MLJ(SC) 752; 2008 (151) PLR 759; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 808; 2008(7) SCALE 360; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 194

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Award Of Interest

Summary: Motor Vehicle Act, 1988 - Quantum of interest to be awarded on the compensation awarded to the legal representatives of the deceased who died in an accident - Held, interest is compensation for forbearance or detention of money, which ought to have been paid to the claimant. No rate of interest is fixed under s. 171 of the Act and the duty has been bestowed upon the court to determine such rate of interest - Rate at which the interest is to be awarded would normally depend upon the bank rate prevailing at the relevant time - Appellants would be entitled to be paid interest at the rate of 7.5% from the date of application till the date of payment - Appeal disposed of.
