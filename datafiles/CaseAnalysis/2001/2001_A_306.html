O. N. G. C. and Another v Association of Natural Gas Consuming Industries

Supreme Court of India

26 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 295; (2001) 6 SCC 627; AIR 2001 SC 2796; 2001 (45) ALR 293; 2001 (2) ARBLR 696; 2002 (1) G.L.R. 618; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 127; JT 2001 (6) SC 156; 2002 (3) RCR(Civil) 314; 2001(5) SCALE 25; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 50; 2001 (2) UC 270

Case Digest

Subject: Petroleum & Gas; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Petroleum & Gas - Contract & Commercial - Renewal of contract - Enhancement of price - Delayed payment - Entitlement of interest - Restitution - Respondents entered into agreements with applicant/ ONGC(Oil and Natural Gas Commission) whereby they had agreed to take natural gas from applicant for purpose of industries run by them - Further, contracts came to end and applicant proposed that contract should be renewed but at enhanced price of gas - Respondents filed writ petition before HC challenging increase in price of gas - HC allowed writ petition and set aside price which had been determined by applicant - Pursuant to certificate of fitness having been granted, appeals were filed - During pendency of appeals, interim order was passed which was similar in terms to one which had been passed by HC - After appeals were allowed, applicants became entitled to receive difference in price of gas - Hence, instant Application - Whether ONGC/applicant was entitled to claim interest from respondents for delayed payment of principal amount - Held, it was true that contracts between parties came to end and gas would be supplied only if fresh written contract had been entered into which was not done - Payment was not made but subsequently after case was disposed of money was required to be paid then recipient would be entitled to charge interest by way of restitution - Even if in main judgment no direction was given with regard to payment of interest, ONGC could not be deprived of its right to claim restitution - Implication of disposal of appeals would be that interim orders which had prohibited ONGC from realising any sum in addition to one fixed by interim order came to end and ONGC would thereafter claim money due to it - ONGC was under obligation by virtue of interim orders to comply with terms of earlier contracts and to supply gas in manner provided - That part of contract was performed by ONGC who thus became entitled to recover from industries price which had originally been charged by them - For late payment of amount, cl. 5 of Contract had contemplated payment of interest at rate and manner specified - ONGC was entitled to claim interest from respondents at rate specified in cl. 5 of agreements - Application allowed.
