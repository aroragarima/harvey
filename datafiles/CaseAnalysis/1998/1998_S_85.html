All India Radio v Santosh Kumar and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1670; (1998) 3 SCC 237; (1998) SCC (L&S) 833; AIR 1998 SC 941; 1998 (1) CLR 684; 1998 (1) CLT 190; 1998 (78) FLR 814; JT 1998 (1) SC 662; 1998 LabIC 969; 1998 (1) LLJ 817; 1998 (1) LLN 959; 1998 (2) MLJ(SC) 18; 1998(1) SCALE 538; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 702; 1998 (2) SLJ 63; 1998 (1) SLR 755; 1998 (3) SLT 128; 1998 (1) Supreme 503

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Telephone

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2(j) - Industry - All India Radio and Doordarshan - Held, both are in commercial activity for profit as getting commercial advertisements telecast or broadcast - Cannot be said to be of purely sovereign nature - Both therefore are All India Radio and Doordarshan industries.
