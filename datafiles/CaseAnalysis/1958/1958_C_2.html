State of Uttar Pradesh v C. Tobit and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, A.K. Sarkar, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 154; AIR 1958 SC 414; 1958 CRLJ 809; 1958 SCJ 735; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 1275

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 419 - (A) Whether interpretation of statute can be ascertained with subject of enactment and object which Legislature has in view?- Held, it is well settled that words of a statute, when there is doubt about their meaning, are to be understood in sense in which they best harmonise with subject of enactment and object which Legislature has in view - Their meaning is found not so much in a strict grammatical or etymological propriety of language, nor even in its popular use, as in subject or in occasion on which they are used, and object to be attained - Therefore, to come to a decision as to true meaning of a word used in a statute one has to enquire as to subject-matter of enactment and object which Legislature had in view - (B) Whether 'Copy' used in s. 419 of CrPC means certified copy? - Further held, having regard to context and purpose of s. 419, CrPC, copy to be filed along with petition of appeal must be a certified copy - In many sections 'copy' is intended to serve only as a notice to person concerned or public and is not intended to be acted upon by a Court for purpose of making a judicial order thereon - Object and purpose of such sections are distinguishable from those of s. 419 where copy is intended to be acted upon by Appellate Court for purpose of founding its judicial decision on it - Appeal dismissed.
