A. Devendran and another v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

21 October 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1606; (1997) 11 SCC 720; (1998) SCC (Cr) 220; AIR 1998 SC 2821; 1997 (4) Crimes 181; 1998 CRLJ 814; JT 1997 (8) SC 619; 1997(6) SCALE 516; [1997] Supp4 S.C.R. 591; 1997 (9) Supreme 237

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Keywords: Curable, Ballistics

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 306, 307 and 465 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3 and 114, Illustration (a) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300, 302 and 411 - Murder and robbery - Accused entered house, killed two old ladies and car driver and looted jewellery etc - Grant of pardon u/s. 307 after commitment of case to Court of Session - (A) In whom power lies? - (B) Powers of Chief Judicial Magistrate to cure this irregularity - (C) Compliance with provisions of s. 306(4) if necessary - (D) Applicability of s. 465 to patent defect of jurisdiction - (E) When presumption u/s. 114, Illustration (a) can be drawn - Evidence establishing commission of offence - Corroborated by medical evidence - Weapon identified - Loot recovered on statement - Presumption can be drawn - Beyond reasonable doubt - Main accused if liable - (H) Recovery of some of jewelleries etc belonging to informant after two months of occurrence from two other accused - If sufficient to convict them for murder by taking recourse to presumption u/s. 114, Illustration (a) of Act - (I) Strangulation and pistol shot - But not a deprived mind - Number of death not determinative factor - No evidence of either diabolical, ghastly or gruesome death - If case fall within 'rarest of rare cases' for death penalty - Held, power to grant pardon thereafter lies only with Court to which commitment is made - Not a curable irregularity - Not necessary - Cannot be applied - Depends on evidence and circumstances of case - Liable to be convicted u/ss. 302 and 326, IPC - Not sufficient, but they can be convicted u/s. 411 of IPC and extreme penalty of death cannot be awarded - Appeal disposed of.
