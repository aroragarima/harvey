Jarnail Singh and Others v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

26 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1079; (2009) 9 SCC 719; AIR 2010 SC 3699; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 3053; 2009 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2917; 2009 (4) RCR(Civil) 92; 2009(12) SCALE 47; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 774

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Chance Witness, Fire Arms

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.302, 148, 149, 307, 364, 342, 506, 201, 120-B - Arms Act, 1959, s.30 - Conviction u/ss. 302, 148, 149, 307, 364, 342, 506, 201, 120-B and s.30 of Arms Act - Land dispute - Deceased was murdered with intention of taking possession of land - Appeal against conviction - (A) Acquittal of co-accused 'B' and 'G' - Effect thereof - Statement of PW-18 in respect of fact of hatching a conspiracy for taking possession of said disputed land forcibly by 'B' and 'G', at bus stand was not worthy of credence - PW-18, a chance witness could not explain under what circumstances he was present at bus stand at said time - Evidence of a chance witness requires a very cautious and close scrutiny and a chance witness must adequately explain his presence at place of occurrence - Held, HC justified in discarding part of prosecution case relating to conspiracy - However, in fact situation of present case, acquittal of said two co-accused has no bearing on present appeal - (B) Names of appellants not been mentioned in FIR - Effect thereof - Held, FIR is not encyclopedia of all facts relating to crime - Only requirement is that at time of lodging FIR, informant should state all those facts which normally strike to mind and help in assessing gravity of crime or identity of culprit briefly - Motive for committing offence has fully been explained therein that they wanted forcible possession of land from complainant party - (C) Delay in lodging FIR - Held, delay in lodging FIR has fully been explained by prosecution and both courts below have accepted same - (D) Dead body of deceased was recovered after about two weeks of incident - Body was completely in a decomposed state - Appellant contended that body was not in a condition where one could identify body and in such a fact situation, appellants ought to have, also been acquitted by HC - Dead body identified by two fellow labourers and medical evidence is same as that of ocular evidence and dead body was found with clothes which deceased was wearing at time of incident - (E) Identification parade not held - Effect thereof - Names of appellants not mentioned in FIR - Held, in view of fact that accused had been identified by witnesses at time of arrest itself, holding identification parade would have been inconsequential - No merit in appeal - Appeal dismissed.
