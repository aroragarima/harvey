U. P. State Road Tpt. Corporation and Others v Musai Ram and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 January 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 435; (1999) 3 SCC 372; (1999) SCC (L&S) 686; 1999 (4) AWC 3292; 1999 (2) CLR 312; 1999 (3) ESC 2442; 1999 (83) FLR 226; JT 1999 (6) SC 6; 1999 (2) LLJ 175; 1999 (3) LLN 433; 1999 (5) SLR 11; 1999 (5) SLT 297; 1999 (9) Supreme 168; 2000 (1) UPLBEC 165

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Service - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 80 - Enquiry proceedings - Validity of - Whether the High Court was right in set aside the Enquiry Report? - Held, respondent himself chose not to cross-examine Asst. Traffic Inspector - Respondent was given ample opportunity to establish his case - When respondent did not challenge reports filed by Asst. Traffic Inspector, there is no question of violation of principles of natural justice - Question whether authority can act upon reports of Asst. Traffic Inspector or not and whether these reports should be accepted or not is a matter which has to be examined by enquiry officer - Court does not sit in appeal over findings of enquiry officer - If findings are based on uncontroverted material placed before enquiry officer, it cannot be said that these findings are perverse - Judgment of HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
