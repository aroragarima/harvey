Jindas Oil Mill & Ors. v Godhra Electricity Co. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

26 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 247; (1969) 1 SCC 781; AIR 1969 SC 1225; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 836

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Labour & Industrial Law; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Supply Act, 1956, s. 57(A) - Indian Electricity Act, 1910 - Increase in electricity charges - Justifiability - Plaintiffs/appellants who were consumers of electricity in one area sued the respondent/company on behalf of all the consumers in that area seeking to restrain the respondent from enforcing the enhanced charges sought to be collected from the consumers of power used for lights and fans as well as of motive power - Appellants contended that the respondent was not competent to enhance the charges, in question without the matter having been considered by rating committee - Appellants suits to restrain the respondent from levying the proposed increased charges were decreed by Trial Court - Those decrees were affirmed by the first appellate court as well as by Single Judge in second appeals but the appellate bench of HC reversed those decrees and dismissed the suits holding that under 1956 Act as amended in 1956 the respondent had unilateral right to enhance the charges subject to the conditions prescribed in the VI Schedule to 1956 Act - According to the appellants they had vested right to be governed by the charges fixed in 1952 until the same was revised by the Govt. on the basis of the recommendation of rating committee - It was also contended on behalf of appellant that the amendments made in 1956' did not affect the charges fixed in 1952 and they continued to rule till altered by the Govt. in accordance with law - Respondent repudiated those contentions - Respondent denied that the appellants had any vested right in the charges fixed - It was contended on its behalf that the amendments made to 1956 Act have substantially altered the scheme as regards levying charges, it was open to licensee to alter the charges fixed by the Govt. unilaterally subject to the conditions prescribed in s. 57(A) and in Sch. VI of 1956 Act - Held, SC in one case was called upon to consider the scope of s. 57.(A) of 1956 Act and the Sch. VI - Therein the controversy was whether the appellant therein was entitled' to levy charges more than the maximum charges prescribed in its licence issued in 1932 - SC held that the maximum charges stipulated in the licence no longer governed the right of the licensee to enhance the charges, his rights were exclusively governed by the provisions contained in paragraph 1 of Sch. VI of 1956 Act - It was true that in that case SC was considering the right of the licensee vis-a-vis his right under the licence granted under the 1910 Act but that difference was not material - What SC in fact considered was the right of the licensee under the existing law to enhance the charges - Amalgamated Electricity 'Co., Ltd. v. N. S. Bhathena and Anr., 1964 Indlaw SC 138, relied on - Appeals dismissed.
