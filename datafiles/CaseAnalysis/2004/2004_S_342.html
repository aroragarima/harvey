Simon & Ors. v State Of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 55; (2004) SCC (Cr) 646; (2004) 2 SCC 694; AIR 2004 SC 2775; 2004 (1) CCR 233; 2004 (1) Crimes 361; JT 2004 (2) SC 124; 2004(2) SCALE 112; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 1164; 2004 (2) SLT 39; 2004 (1) Supreme 577

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Human Rights

Keywords: Indian Evidence Act, 1972

Summary: Sentencing - enhancement of life imprisonment to capital punishment -Bachan Singh's case followed - the manner in which the crime was committed clearly shows that any person can contemplate the disastrous effect of blasting of landmines - crime was diabolically planned. Appellants are threat and grave danger to society at large - must have anticipated that their activity would result in elimination of large number of lives - 22 persons losing their lives and many receiving grievous injuries - punishment enhanced. Expiry of limitation - for state to file appeal - held no bar for Supreme Court to enhance sentence to death
