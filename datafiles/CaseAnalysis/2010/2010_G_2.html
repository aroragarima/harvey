Meghmala and others v G. Narasimha Reddy and others

Supreme Court of India

16 August 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 663; (2010) 8 SCC 383; 2010 (6) ALD(SC) 1; JT 2010 (8) SC 658; 2010 (7) MLJ(SC) 693; 2010 (2) OLR 778; 2010(8) SCALE 237; [2010] 10 S.C.R. 47

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Land Grabbing (Prohibition) Rules, 1988

Summary: Land & Property - Andhra Pradesh Land Grabbing (Prohibition) Act, 1982 - Land grabbing - Vendors of the impugned land executed a sale deed in favour of the appellants and put them in possession of the impugned land - Appellant filed complaint against respondents alleging that they forcible grabbed appellant's land and raised construction thereon - Appellants sought their dispossession and action against them under the provisions of the Act, 1982 - Respondents submitted that in respect of the impugned land, there was an agreement to sell, in favour of the society by the vendors and once such an agreement to sell had been executed, vendors had no right to transfer the land in favour of the appellant - Respondents also submitted that the said society had allotted the suit land in their favour, therefore, the application was liable to be rejected - Special Court held that appellant was the owner of the impugned land and that the respondents had no right, title or claim over the suit land - On appeal, HC affirmed the order of the Special Court - Respondents filed review petition before HC and while said review petition was pending before HC, appellants filed execution proceedings before Special Court which allowed said execution application and put appellants in possession of the impugned land - Respondents filed application raising the issue of fraud and misrepresentation - HC directed the Special Court to decide the applications fresh as the applications required certain inquiry on factual matters - Hence, present appeals - Held, an agreement to sell did not confer any right on the Society - Respondents have never been able to show as under what circumstances they are interested in the suit land because before the Special Court in the first round they failed to show any document that land had ever been transferred by the tenure holders/owners in favour of the Society or the Society had made any allotment in their favour or they were member of the said Society or they obtained any sanction from statutory authority to raise the construction - Moreover, Special Court in execution proceedings was fully satisfied regarding the identity of land on the basis of revenue record and came to the conclusion that there was no misrepresentation or fraud on the part of the appellant - Impugned order of the HC set aside - Appeals allowed.
