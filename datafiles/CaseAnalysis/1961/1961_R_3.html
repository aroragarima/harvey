Gobald Motor Service Limited and Another v R. M. K. Veluswami and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 61; AIR 1962 SC 1; 1962 (1) MLJ 105; 1962 (1) MLJ(SC) 105; 1962 (1) SCJ 206; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 929; 1961 (112) STC 816

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Labour & Industrial Law; Sales Tax

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Labour & Industrial Law - Fatal Accidents Act, 1855, ss. 1, 2 - Fatal accidents - Negligent Act - Pecuniary loss - Damages - Liability of owner - Ascertainment - Bus run by appellant met with an accident as a result of which X died - X's dependents and heirs brought a suit for compensation u/s. 1 of the 1855 Act for loss of pecuniary benefit sustained by them personally and u/s. 2 thereof for loss sustained by estate on account of death of X - Both the Tribunal & HC found that bus was driven at an excessive speed and there was negligence on part of driver and that appellants were liable for the same - On question of damages HC confirmed amount of compensation of Rs. 25,000/- u/s. 1 of the 1855 Act for loss of pecuniary advantage and of Rs. 6,000/- u/ s. 2 of the Act for loss of expectation of life - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Whether accident was due to any negligence on part of the driver - Held, that on basis of evidence and on broad probabilities it was found that speed at which the bus was driven was excessive having regard to nature of ground on which accident happened - There was a presumption that accident was caused by negligence of the driver - As driver was acting in course of his employment the master would be liable for such accident unless presumption was rebutted by master - In instant case on account of negligence of driver in course of his employment the accident happened and therefore appellant was held liable for same - (B) Whether HC & Tribunal were right in awarding compensation u/s.1 of the 1855 Act for pecuniary loss sustained by widow and sons of deceased? Held - That actual extent of pecuniary loss to aggrieved party might depend upon data which could not be ascertained accurately but ought to be necessarily be an estimate, or even partly a conjecture - Shortly stated, general principle was that pecuniary loss could be ascertained only by balancing on one hand loss to claimants of future pecuniary benefit and on other any pecuniary advantage which from whatever sources came to them by reason of death - Balance of loss and gain to a dependant by death might be ascertained - Where HC & Tribunal have on relevant material placed before them ascertained amount of damages under head of pecuniary loss by dependants of deceased could not be disturbed in second appeal except for compelling reason - (C) Whether sum awarded as damages u/s. 2 of the 1855 Act for loss of expectation of life would go towards reduction of compensation awarded for pecuniary loss sustained u/s. 1 of the Act, as otherwise it would be duplication of damages in respect of the same wrong - Held, in instant case u/s. 1 of the Act sum of compensation was arrived at by taking into consideration, inter alia, the reasonable provision the deceased if alive would have made for his widow and sons - u/s.2 figure for damages awarded for loss to estate represents damages for mental agony, suffering and loss of expectation of life thus there was no duplication in awarding damages under both heads - Rights of action under ss.1, 2 of Fatal Accidents Act were quite distinct and independent - If a person taking benefit under both sections was same, he could not be permitted to recover twice over for the same loss - In awarding damages under both heads, there should not be duplication of same claim - if any part of compensation representing loss to estate goes into calculation of personal loss u/ s. 1 of the Act that portion should be excluded in giving compensation u/s. 2 and vice versa - Appeal dismissed.
