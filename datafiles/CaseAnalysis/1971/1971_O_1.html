C. K. Daphtary and Others v O. P. Gupta and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, C.A. Vaidyialingam, A.N. Grover, J.M. Shelat, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 517; (1971) SCC (Cr) 286; (1971) 1 SCC 626; AIR 1971 SC 1132; 1971 CRLJ 844; [1971] S.C.R. 76; [1971] Supp S.C.R. 76

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression, Freedom Of Press, Attorney-General

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Judges (Inquiry) Act, 1968 - Parliamentary Proceedings (Protection of Publication) Act, 1956 - Scandalous remarks made against judges - Contempt of Court - Guilty - Imprisonment - Scandalous remarks passed by Respondent No.1 against Judge - - Petition filed by 'D' and three other advocates alleged that contempt of Court committed by Respondent Nos.1,2 through its proprietor, and Respondent no.3 - Hence instant petition - Whether respondent No. 1 was guilty of contempt of Court - Held, neither art. 105(2) nor 1956 Act protect first respondent in respect of contempt of court committed as there was malice intention with Respondent - Respondent committed gross contempt of Court - Respondent took advantage of retirement of Justice 'S' and hurled fresh abuses on him in Court which constituted gross contempt of two Judges - Order accordingly.
