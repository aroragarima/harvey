Natasha Singh v Cbi (State)

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 300; (2013) 5 SCC 741; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 845; 2013 CRLJ 3346; 2013 (3) JCC 1808; JT 2013 (9) SC 57; 2013(4) M.P.H.T. 423; 2013 (2) OLR 577; 2013(6) SCALE 676; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 539; 2013 (2) WLN(SC) 90

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 311 - Additional evidence - Application for examination of witnesses - Rejection - Sustainability - Instant appeal was preferred against impugned judgment and order passed by the HC, by way of which it had affirmed the order passed by the Trial Court, dismissing application filed by appellant u/s. 311 CrPC, observing that examination of witnesses sought to be examined by appellant-accused was in fact unnecessary, and would in no way assist in the process of arriving at a just decision with respect to the case - Whether the evidence proposed to be adduced was relevant or not - Held, Trial Court prejudged the evidence of witness sought to be examined by appellant and thereby cause grave and material prejudice to appellant as regards her defence, which tantamounts to a flagrant violation of principles of law governing the production of such evidence in keeping with the provisions of s. 311 CrPC - Trial Court reached the conclusion that production of such evidence by defence was not essential to facilitate a just decision of case - Such an assumption was wholly misconceived and was not tenable in law as the accused had every right to adduce evidence in rebuttal of the evidence brought on record by prosecution - Examination of the hand-writing expert might therefore be necessary to rebut the evidence of PW40, and a request made for his examination ought not to have been rejected on the sole ground that opinion of hand-writing expert would not be conclusive - HC had simply quoted relevant paragraphs from the judgment of the Trial Court and had approved the same without giving proper reasons, merely observing that the additional evidence sought to be brought on record was not essential for the purpose of arriving at a just decision - Furthermore, the same was not a case where if the application filed by appellant was allowed, the process would have taken much time - In fact, disallowing the said application, had caused delay - No prejudice would have been caused to the prosecution, if the defence was permitted to examine witnesses - Impugned judgment and order of Trial Court, as well as of HC were set aside - Application u/s. 311 CrPC filed by appellant was allowed - Appeal allowed.
