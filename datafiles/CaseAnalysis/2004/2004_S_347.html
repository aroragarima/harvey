Chacko Alias Aniyan Kunju & Ors v State Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

21 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 20; (2004) 12 SCC 269; AIR 2004 SC 2688; JT 2004 (1) SC 358; 2004 (1) KLT 884; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 900; 2004 (1) UC 609

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 134 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 299, 300, 302, 304, 34 - Sessions Judge convicted and sentenced all accused - Petition file by appellants dismissed by HC - Evidence of solitary witness - Held, if evidence of a solitary witness is unblemished and beyond all possible criticism and Court is satisfied that witness was speaking truth, then on his evidence alone conviction can be maintained - Exercise of right of private defence - Further held, it has to be established that accused persons were under such grave apprehension about safety of their life and property that retaliation to extent done was absolutely necessary - Further held, accused convicted u/s. 304 Part I r/w s 34, IPC, 1860, instead of s 302.
