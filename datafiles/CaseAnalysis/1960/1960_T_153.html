Associated Cement Companies Limited v Their Workmen

Supreme Court of India

03 March 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 139; AIR 1960 SC 777; 1960 (1) FLR 349; 1960 (1) LLJ 491; 1960 SCJ 690; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 157

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: majority act

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act (14 of 1947), ss. 19(6), 18, 10 and 2(K) - Whether a registered trade union which is representing a minority of workmen governed by an award can give notice to other party intimating its intention to terminate award u/s. 19(6) of Industrial Disputes Act 1947? - Held, yes, it can - S.19(6) refers to all workmen bound by award - Notice to terminate said award can be given not by an individual workman but by a group of workmen acting collectively either through their union or otherwise, and it is not necessary that such a group or union through which it acts should represent majority of workmen - It is open to a minority of workmen or a minority union to terminate award by which they, along with other employees, are bound just as much as it is open to them to raise an industrial dispute under Act - Appeal dismissed.
