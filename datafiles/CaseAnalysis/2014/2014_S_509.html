Chandana Das and another v State of West Bengal and others

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 923; JT 2014 (14) SC 118; 2014(14) SCALE 1

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Service

Keywords: Educational Institutions, Gujarat University Act, 1949, West Bengal Board of Secondary Education Act, 1963, Management of Recognised Non-Government Institutions (Aided and Unaided) Rules, 1969

Summary: T. S. THAKUR, J.

(A) Service - Education - Rules for Management of Recognised Non-Government Institutions (Aided and Unaided), 1969, r. 28 - Appointment - Non-approval - Effect - Appellants were appointed as teachers on temporary basis in respondent No. 4 Sikh Community school - Appointments quashed by respondent District Inspector of Schools on the ground of not meeting prior approval - Appellants challenged respondent order before HC - Single Judge allowed appellants writ petition holding that institution being a linguistic minority institution was entitled to select and appoint its teachers - However, DB, on respondent's appeal, set aside Single Judge order holding that appointment was not in compliance with r. 28 of the Rules - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, appellants were both duly qualified for appointment as teachers in the subject concerned. They have been serving for a considerable length of time on a meagre salary which the institution has been paying to them in the absence of the State Govt. recognising the appointments and releasing grant in aid against their posts. Further, vacancies had subsequently arisen against which the appointments of the appellants could be approved and the salary payable to them from the date of such vacancies becoming available released. If that be so, why the appointments of the appellants should not be approved with effect from the date of such vacancies becoming available against which such appointments could be regularised. Hence, impugned orders of DB was set aside and appeals were allowed. Order accordingly.

R. BANUMATHI, J (Dissenting)

(B) Service - Education - Rules for Management of Recognised Non-Government Institutions (Aided and Unaided), 1969, rr . 8(3), 28, 33 - Appointment - Non-approval - Effect - Whether appointment of appellants in respondent No. 4 School is legally valid.

Held, merely because an educational institution is established by a religious or linguistic minority, it does not automatically become a minority institution for the purposes of claiming right of administration and for getting grant-in-aid. The Sikh Community being a minority in the State of West Bengal does not necessarily imply that the respondent No. 4 School would be minority institution as per law. According to the official respondents, minority status was never granted to respondent No. 4 School and only Special Constitution of Management was granted to the school. The school was only granted Special Constitution in terms of the Rules. There is nothing on record to show that the school was granted minority status by the competent authority. The respondent No. 4 School has not produced any document to show that it has been accorded the minority status. In the absence of any order by the competent authority, the respondent No. 4 School is bound by the mandate contained in the Rules. When the respondent No. 4 School has not been granted minority status, it cannot appoint its own teachers dehors r. 28 of the Rules and hence, cannot seek direction for approval of appellants' appointment and arrears of salary. Having accepted the Special Constitution in terms of r. 8(3), the respondent No. 4 School cannot turn round and contend that it is a minority institution as per special rules framed in terms of r. 33. As rightly held by the HC, when the respondent No. 4 School has accepted the special constitution and has not claimed to be a minority institution, the appellants who are merely employees of such an institution, cannot contend that the institution was a minority institution entitled to appoint its own teachers. Since impugned order of the HC does not suffer from any infirmity warranting interference, the appeals are dismissed. Order accordingly.
