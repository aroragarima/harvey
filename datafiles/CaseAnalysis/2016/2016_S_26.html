Sadhu Saran Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

N.V. Ramana, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 179; (2016) 4 SCC 357; AIR 2016 SC 1160; 2016 CRLJ 1908; 2016 (2) JCC 1295; JT 2016 (2) SC 475; 2016 (2) MLJ(Crl) 88; 2016 (2) RCR(Criminal) 319; 2016 (4) RLW 3070; 2016(2) SCALE 629; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 913

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Presumption, Kidnapping, FIR, Oral Evidence, Blood, Blunt, Death Sentence, Medical Evidence, Ocular Evidence, Dealing, Injured Witness, Interfere, Inconsistency, Appeal Against Acquittal, Enmity, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Police Regulation Act

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 302, 307, 504 - Murder - Acquittal Challenged - Respondent no.2/accused nos. 1-5 were held guilty of murder of deceased 'X' by Trial Court - Trial Court convicted all accused u/s.302/149 of IPC and sentenced to suffer imprisonment for life with fine of Rs. 10,000 in default to suffer for two years RI- Further, convicted u/s. 307/149 of IPC and sentenced to suffer RI for 7 years with fine of Rs. 5,000 in default to undergo one year RI - convicted u/s. 148 of IPC and sentenced to suffer RI for two years with fine of Rs. 1000 in default to undergo 6 months RI - However, death sentence was awarded to two accused - HC in appeal by accused set aside judgment of Trial Court and acquitted accused of all the charges - Hence, instant appeals by brother of deceased.

Held, the appellate Court, while dealing with an appeal against acquittal, has no absolute restriction in law to review and relook the entire evidence on which the order of acquittal is founded. If the appellate Court, on scrutiny, finds that the decision of the Court below is based on erroneous views and against settled position of law, then the interference of the appellate Court with such an order is imperative. In instant case, in view of circumstances, it can be safely held that the accused respondents had strong motive to commit the offence against the complainant party. Further, concur with the finding of the Trial Court that the accused have failed to establish their plea of alibi. With regard to death sentence awarded to two accused, Trial Court does not convince us that this is the rarest of the rare cases which warrants the penalty of death sentence. Hence, impugned judgment and order passed by the High Court is set aside and modify the sentence awarded by Trial Court in respect of default of fine, attached to conviction under above sections of IPC. Appeals allowed.
