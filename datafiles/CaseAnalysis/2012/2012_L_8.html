Lee Kun Hee & Ors. v State Of U.P. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

01 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 111; (2012) 3 SCC 132; AIR 2012 SC 1007; 2012 CRLJ 1551; JT 2012 (2) SC 237; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 884; 2012(2) SCALE 152; [2012] 4 S.C.R. 287

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 403, 405, 415, 418, 420 and 423 / 120B and 34 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 200 - Negotiable Instrument Act, 1881 - Breach of Contract - Negotiable Instrument - Summoned - Validity - Seller-complainant had entered into contract/agreement with appellants-accused for supply of goods - Goods were supplied as per agreement and bills of exchange were endorsed in favour of seller - Bills of exchange was not honored by appellant, seller issue legal notice and thereafter filed Criminal complaint before Magistrate u/ss. 403, 405, 415, 418, 420 and 423 / 120B and 34 of IPC - Magistrate examined seller and supporting documents passed order summoning appellants - Appellants preferred appeal against orders of Magistrate before HC, HC dismissed - Aggrieved instant appeal - Respondents contended raising several issues such as (A) Maintainability of complaint on basis of Jurisdiction - Held, considering previous decisions of this court which passed orders stating criminal offences are considered as altogether local, and are justiciable only by the courts of that country where offence was committed - Hence appellant being of foreign nationality, their residence outside India, and fact that they were not present in India when offence(s) was/were allegedly committed, are of no consequence, in view of aforesaid decision rendered by this Court, therefore, no merit in first contention - (B) Bills of exchange was drawn at other Country, as such that Country had jurisdiction - Held, this contention is rejected for exactly same reasons which had weighed while dealing with primary contention raised for jurisdiction - (C) Appellant not privy to contract/agreement - Held, it was expressly averred that all appellants were involved in "each and every act done by company", appellants were individually and jointly liable for commission of offences emerging from complaint - How they were liable, is a question of evidence, which would emerge only after evidence is recorded by trial court as one of accused had supported accusations levelled by complainant - even pleadings, prima facie demonstrate connectivity of appellants, with foundational basis expressed in complaint, therefore, at present juncture, it was undesirable to exculpate appellants from proceedings initiated by complainant - (D) Civil suit for recovery pending in foreign court, liability emerging out of bill of exchange, case was civil in nature - Held, that in offences of nature contemplated under summoning order, there can be civil liability coupled with criminal culpability - What a party had been deprived of by an act of cheating, can be claimed through a civil action - Same deprivation based on denial by way of deception, emerging from an act of cheating, would also attract criminal liability - In instant case, monetary consideration under bill of exchange, cannot be claimed in criminal proceedings, for that relief remedy would be only through a civil suit, it was therefore not possible to accept, that since a civil claim had been raised by complainant, based on alleged breach of agreement, it can be prevented from initiating proceedings for penal consequences for alleged offences committed by accused under IPC - Appeal dismissed.
