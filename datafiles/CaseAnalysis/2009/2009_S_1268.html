New India Assurance Company Limited v Suresh Chandra Aggarwal

Supreme Court of India

10 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, B. Sudarshan Reddy

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 845; (2009) 15 SCC 761; AIR 2009 SC 2987; 2009(6) ALL MR 964; 2009 (76) ALR 473; 2009 (4) AWC 3291; 2009 (4) CalLT(SC) 8; 2009 (4) CPJ(Sc) 14; JT 2009 (9) SC 77; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 500; 2009 (4) RLW 3522; 2009(9) SCALE 449; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 885

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Insurance

Keywords: Cover Note, Renewal Of Licence, Person In Charge, Licence-Holder

Summary: Consumer Protection - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 3(1), 5, 15 - Insurance claim - Repudiation of - No valid driving licence at relevant time - Claimant insured his car - Insured vehicle met with an accident - Claimed lodged - Claimed repudiated on the ground that the driver of the vehicle was not holding an effective driving licence at the time of accident - Driving licence of the deceased driver had expired on 25-10-1991 i.e. four months prior to the date of accident and it was renewed w.e.f. 23-03-1992 - State Commission observed that as licence had been renewed subsequently and since driver had not been disqualified to hold such a licence, the claim of the claimant could not be rejected on the ground that the licence had not been renewed on time - Whether State Commission right in holding that driver of the insured vehicle had an effective driving licence at the time of the accident? - In case application for renewal is filed after more than 30 days after the date of its expiry, proviso to sub-section (1) of s. 15 of the Act, gets attracted and the licence is renewed only w.e.f the date of its renewal, meaning thereby that in the interregnum between the date of expiry of the licence and the date of its renewal, there is no effective licence in existence - Held, in present case application for renewal of licence not filed within 30 days from the date of its expiry in terms of s. 15 of the Act - Therefore, deceased driver did not hold an effective driving licence, as required in the terms and conditions governing the policy on the date of accident - Further held, in view of the clear mandate of s. 3 of the Act, the deceased driver was not even permitted to drive the insured vehicle in a public place - Claimant violated the provisions of s. 5 of the Act by entrusting the vehicle to a person who did not hold a valid licence on the date of the accident - Appellant was not liable to indemnify the claimant for the loss suffered by him in the accident of the insured vehicle - Impugned order set aside - Appeal allowed.
