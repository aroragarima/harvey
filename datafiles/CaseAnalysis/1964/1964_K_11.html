Â State of Mysore and Another v K. N. Chandrasekhara and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 July 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Raghubar Dayal, S.M. Sikri, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 442; AIR 1965 SC 532; 1965 (1) ALT(SC) 333

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - Recruitment - Mysore State (Recruitment) Rules, 1958, r. 9(2), Schedule - Recruitment to cadre of Munsifs in Judicial Service of State - Two methods of recruitment to cadre are prescribed; by competitive examination and by promotion of persons in service in High Court and Courts subordinate to HC - Eligibility stood amended - Written test and viva voce test for examination candidates, and interview for promotion candidates - New policy adopted by Public Service Commission fixing 45% of marks for viva voce test as qualifying marks for candidates belonging to scheduled castes and 55% for other candidates - List of candidates for both categories, issued - Issuance of list challenged - HC gave finding that assessment of marks at vive voce test must be discarded - HC issued certain suggestions for Governor, which was adopted by amending Rules - Fresh viva voce test held, only three candidates who had applied for direct recruitment, and those candidates who had applied both for appearing at competitive examination and for promotion, were invited to appear, but candidates who had applied only for promotion were not so invited - Process completed - List issued - Again challenged by six of petitioners, challenging appointment of ten candidates selected for promotion - HC issued writ of mandamus directing Commission to include names of six petitioners - Findings of HC and issuance of writ, whether proper? - Issuance of writ of mandamus uncalled for, as HC could quash list but could not direct for such inclusion of six petitioners - Findings of HC, rejected procedure adopted by commission, upheld - Impugned judgment of HC, set aside - Order accordingly.
