K.K. Saravana Babu v State of Tamil Nadu and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1329; (2008) 9 SCC 89; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 679; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 578; JT 2008 (9) SC 243; 2008 (4) RCR(Criminal) 107; 2008(11) SCALE 572; [2008] 12 S.C.R. 468

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Land & Property

Keywords: Drug offenders, Under-Trial, Women, Single Act, Communal Tension, Tamil Nadu Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Bootleggers, Drug Offenders, Forest Offenders, Goondas, Immoral Traffic Offenders, Sand Offenders, Slum Grabbers and Video Pirates Act, 1982, Madras Maintenance of Public O. Act, 1949

Summary: Constitution, arts. 19, 21 - Tamil Nadu Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Bootleggers, Drug Offenders, Forest Offenders, Goondas, Immoral Traffic Offenders, Sand Offenders, Slum Grabbers and Video Pirates Act, 1982 - Detention order u/s. 3(1) - Legality - Appeal against dismissal of habeas corpus petition - Land grabbing case pending in Trial court u/s, s. 420, 465, 466, 467, 468 r/w 471 and 120(B) IPC - Subsequent suit u/ss. 420, 465, 466, 467, 468 r/w 471 and 120(B) IPC - And his activities adverse to the interest of land owners and prospective buyers - Detaining authority concluded that in case detenu is let out on bail he would again indulge in similar type of offences - Activities prejudicial to the public order, if - Held, even assuming the allegations as true, offences can be called prejudicial to public order and detenu can be dealt with under the ordinary criminal law if it becomes imperative - Order is illegal, unsustainable , quashed on the threshold submission of the detenu without examining other submissions - Appeal allowed.
