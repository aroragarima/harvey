Â Check Post Officer and Others v K. P. Abdulla Bros

Supreme Court of India

23 November 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 189; (1970) 3 SCC 355; AIR 1971 SC 792; 1971 (2) MLJ(SC) 89; [1971] 2 S.C.R. 817; 1971 (27) STC 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Keywords: Person Affected, Check Post, Check Post Officer, Madras General Sales Tax Act 1959 (No 1 of 1959)

Summary: Sales Tax - Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1959, ss. 42, 43 and 44 - Confiscation of goods on check post u/s. 42(3)(a) - Validity of - Held, power to confiscate the goods and to levy penalty in lieu of confiscation, when in respect of the goods found in a vehicle the driver of the vehicle is not carrying with him the documents specified therein, is not a provision which is ancillary or incidental to the power to tax sale of goods - Hence, such confiscation is invalid - Appeal dismissed.
