Ganga Dutt Murarka v Kartik Chandra Das and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 February 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 312; AIR 1961 SC 1067; 1962 (2) MLJ 161; 1962 (2) MLJ(SC) 161; 1962 (2) SCJ 584; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 813

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Indian Contract Act, Presidency Small Cause Courts Act, West Bengal Premises Rent Control (Temporary Provisions) Act, 1950, Bengal Premises Rent Control (Temporary Provisions) Act

Summary: Rent Control - West Bengal Premises Rent Control 1948 - West Bengal Premises Rent Control Act, 1950, s. 12 - Transfer of Property Act, ss. 106 and 116 - Holding over - Meaning of - Whether acceptance of amounts paid by appellant confer upon him right of a tenant 'holding over' within meaning of s. 116 of Transfer of Property Act? - Held, acceptance of rent by landlord from a statutory tenant whose lease has already expired could not be regarded as evidence of a new agreement of tenancy, and it would not be open to such a tenant to urge, by way of defence, in a suit for ejectment brought against him - Tenancy of appellant was determined by efflux of time and subsequent occupation by him was not in pursuance of any contract express or implied, but was by virtue of protection given by successive statutes - This occupation did not confer any rights upon appellant and was not required to be determined by a notice prescribed by s. 106 of Transfer of Property Act - Appeal dismissed.
