M/s. Sree Narayana Chandrika Trust v Commissioner of Gift Tax, Kerala

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 481; (2003) 11 SCC 350; 2003 (181) CTR 395; [2003] 261 ITR 279; 2003 (2) KHCACJ 429; 2003 (2) KLT 683; 2003(4) SCALE 333; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 958; 2003 (3) SLT 356; 2003 (4) Supreme 48; 175 TAXATION 639; [2003] 129 TAXMAN 477

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Gift Tax Act, 1958 - s. 4(1)(a) - Although it may be possible to say in the appellant's case that relinquishment of the share of a profit/loss by a partner in favour of the inducted partner may amount to a transfer, the contention that it was for inadequate consideration so as to amount to a taxable gift within the meaning of section 4(1)(a) of the Gift Tax Act cannot be accepted. The contribution of Rs. 25,000 towards the capital together with the obligations undertaken of sincerely and faithfully carrying on the business for common advantage of the firm was adequate consideration for reallocating the share of the profits and giving 12% of the share in favour of the incoming partner. Even assuming that there was a transfer of 12% of the share profit/loss in favour of the incoming partner by the appellant assessee, it was not a situation of transfer for inadequate consideration so as to amount to a taxable gift within the meaning of section 4(1)(a) of the Gift Tax Act.
