S. Anil Kumar @ Anil Kumar Ganna v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 424; (2013) 7 SCC 219; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 2959; 2013 (5) KarLJ 245; 2013(9) SCALE 125; [2013] 7 S.C.R. 408

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304-B, 498-A - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, ss. 3, 4 and 6 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 113-A, 113-B - Dowry death - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) allegedly ill-treated his wife (deceased) with cruelty since she failed to bring the amount demanded by appellant - Appellant was charge sheeted for offence u/ss. 498-A and 304-B of IPC and u/s. 3, 4 and 6 of the Act - Trial Court on appreciation of evidence on record acquitted appellant - However, on appeal Single Judge by impugned judgment reversed the judgment of acquittal and convicted appellant and sentenced him to under RI for 7 years - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeal - Whether impugned conviction of appellant was justified - Held, there were contradictory statements which could not be stated to be a minor contradiction as was suggested by prosecution before Single Judge - Improvement in statements of PW1 and 12 was clear - Allegation about the demand of dowry amount, gold ornaments and harassment and torture made by appellant on deceased was not disclosed and mentioned in FIR or before the Tahsildar (PW21) who recorded the initial evidence - In Ex.P2 and complaint (Ex.P3) absolutely there was no evidence to show that Rs.25,000/- was demanded and Rs.10,000/- was given to appellant either at place 'B' or at 'M' - Once prosecution failed to prove the basic ingredients of harassment or demand of dowry and evidence brought on record were doubted by Trial Court, it was not open to HC to convict appellate on presumption referring to s. 113-A or 113-B of 1872 Act - Presumption of innocence of accused being primary factor, in absence of exceptional compelling circumstances and perversity of judgment, it was not open to HC to interfere with judgment of Trial Court in a routine manner - Judgment of Trial Court was restored - Impugned judgment was set aside - Appeal allowed.
