Pondicherry State Cooperative Consumer Federation v Union Territory of Pondicherry

Supreme Court of India

13 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1094; (2008) 1 SCC 206; JT 2007 (13) SC 198; 2007(13) SCALE 61; 2007 (8) SCJ 463; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 1118; [2007] 10 VST 630

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Keywords: Small Scale Industrial Unit, Small Industrial Unit, Pondicherry General Sales Tax Act, 1967

Summary: Pondicherry General Sales Tax Act, 1967 - Judgment of High Court denying the exemption from payment of Sales Tax is under challenge in this appeal - Held, since in the present case the exemption was granted to all small scale industrial units registered with the Director of Industries and since the Assessee was recognized and certified as a small industrial unit, engaged in the activity of re-packing of edible oil and further since the exemption was granted with the open eyes to this particular industry, the State cannot be allowed to run around and take a stance that the appellant - assessee was not entitled to the exemption on the ground that it did not manufacture any goods - Appeal allowed.
