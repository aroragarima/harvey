Lok Ram v Nihal Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 140; (2006) 10 SCC 192; (2006) 3 SCC (Cr) 532; AIR 2006 SC 1892; 2006 CRLJ 2366; 2006 (2) JCC 743; JT 2006 (4) SC 464; 2006 (2) RCR(Criminal) 707; 2006 (3) RLW 2126; 2006(4) SCALE 240; 2006 (5) SCJ 631; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 1018

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - CrPC, 1973, ss. 169, 319, 319(1) - IPC, 1860, s. 304(B), 34, 406, 498(A) - Scope and ambit of s.319 - HC directed trial court to proceed against appellant by summoning him - Appeal against - Held, if it is satisfied that any person other than accused has committed an offence he is to be tried together with accused - Trial court rejected application only on ground that complainant was an interested witness and therefore, sufficient ground did not exist to take action against accused persons - Trial court does not appear to be relevant and, therefore, HC has rightly interfered in matter - Appeal dismissed.
