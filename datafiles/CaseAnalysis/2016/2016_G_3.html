Director General of Income Tax (Admn.) and another v GTC Industries Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 420; AIR 2016 SC 2825; [2016] 198 Comp Cas 71; 2017 (1) CompLJ 327; 2017 (1) DRTC 314; JT 2016 (7) SC 565; 2016(5) SCALE 639; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 1010; [2016] 240 TAXMAN 209

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Interest And Penalty, Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985, Income Tax Dues, Attachment, Income-tax Act, 1961

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Corporate - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985 - Sick company - Sanctioned Scheme - Company became sick company in 1997 as its net worth eroded and filed reference before Board and Board appointed Operating Agency (OA) - OA submitted Draft Rehabilitation Scheme (DRS) to Board in which income tax reliefs were proposed and Board approved sanctioned scheme submitted by OA vide order dt.16-2-2002 - However, after DRS was circulated and before it is sanctioned, during intervening period, income tax demand of Rs.366 Crore was intimated by revenue to OA - While so, when scheme was still in operation, revenue filed petition u/s. 22(1) of the Act seeking permission to recover the outstanding due of Rs.426.37 Crore which were raised after date of sanctioned scheme and Board passed order directing revenue to release sum of Rs.4.28 Crore withheld by revenue and further directed ITD to expedite settlement of disputed demands - Later, Company submitted before Board that its net-worth became positive on 31-3-2007 and sought de-registration from the Act and Board passed order dt.29-6-2007 holding that since net worth of company had turned positive, it seized to be sick industrial undertaking and discharged company from purview of the Act - Later, revenue demanded tax and penalty and aggrieved by the same, Company filed application before Board seeking stay of action proposed to be taken by Revenue - Board passed order directing Revenue not to take any coercive action and Revenue challenged said order of Board before Appellate Authority wherein Appellate Authority held that ITD could not have initiated any coercive action for recovery of its due against Company since the unimplemented provisions of sanctioned rehabilitation scheme for the unexpired period of the scheme are still under implementation - Aggrieved, revenue filed writ petition before HC wherein HC passed impugned orders dt.16-8-2011 dismissing writ petition with observations that appropriate remedy for petitioner is to move Board for lifting of bar u/s.22 of the Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned orders of HC could be upheld.

Held, Scheme had already expired on 31-3-2011 and application for extension of the Scheme was filed before Board which was dismissed and appellate authority upheld said order and so, no scheme was in operation. Further, in year 2007 itself, the net worth of Company had turned positive and it was no more a sick company. Thus, the Revenue had right to recover arrears of income tax after 2007 and in any case after 31-3-2011 when the Scheme expired. Further, in its remarks made while dealing with particular argument of the Company, Appellate Authority only pointed out the breach committed by Company in not taking prior permission but nowhere permitted the Company to resort to same as that opportunity was already lost. However, such observations have not given the Company any liberty to approach the Board. Hence, filing of such application by Company before Board seeking modifications is, therefore, totally untenable move on the part of the Company and is not maintainable in law. Under such circumstances, impugned orders of HC are unsustainable and Revenue had right to recover arrears of income tax after 2007 and in any case after 31-3-2011 when the Scheme expired. However, with regard to quantum of dues that were to be recovered from Company by Revenue, parties are directed to approach Board. Further, Department is entitled to take steps for attachment of properties of the Company as per the provisions of the Act. Appeal allowed.
