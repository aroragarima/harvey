Export Credit Guarantee Corpn. …Appellant Of India Ltd. v M/S Garg Sons International

Supreme Court of India

17 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 26; (2014) 1 SCC 686; 2013 (97) ALR 195; 2013 (2) AWC 1541; [2013] 178 Comp Cas 341; 2013 (5) CompLJ 214; 2013 (2) CPJ(Sc) 1; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 428; 2013(1) SCALE 410; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 336

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Insurance

Summary: Consumer Protection - Insurance - Consumer Protection of Act, 1986, ss. 19 - Policy agreement - Default information - Non-compliance - Claim - Denied - Challenged - Appellant (Govt. company) was insurer - Respondent (company) purchased a policy for the purpose of insuring a shipment to a foreign company - Respondent allegedly committed default in payments towards such policy with respect to said consignment - Thereafter, respondent presented 17 claims, but were rejected on the ground that respondent did not comply cl. 8(b) of insurance agreement, which stipulated period within the insurer was to be informed about any default committed by a foreign importer - Aggrieved respondent filed complaints before State Commission, which were allowed - Aggrieved appellant filed appeals u/s. 19 of the Act - National Commission by impugned judgment allowed rejected 5 claims - Hence, instant appeals - Appellant contended that respondent failed to ensure compliance with mandatory requirement u/cl. 8 (b) of policy agreement, owing to which, claims with respect to which said information was not furnished within stipulated time period, were wrongly allowed - Whether respondent was entitled to claim without compliance of mandatory clause of policy agreement - Held, if conditions referred to under policy agreement, it become evident that insured should make a declaration in prescribed form on 15th of every month as regards whether or not, there was any default committed by foreign importer, either in part, or in full, for a period exceeding 30 days from the date on which payment fell due, with respect to shipments made within policy period - Noncompliance with said term(s) of contract, would exonerate the insurer of all liability in that regard - Thus, endeavour of Court should always be to interpret the words used in contract in the manner that would best express the intention of parties - Insured could not claim anything more than what was covered by insurance policy - It was not permissible for Court to substitute terms of contract itself, under the garb of construing terms incorporated in agreement of insurance - No exceptions could be made on the ground of equity - Liberal attitude adopted by Court, by way of which it interferes in terms of an insurance agreement, was not permitted - Same should certainly not be extended to the extent of substituting words that were never intended to form a part of agreement - Record established that insured failed to comply with requirement of cl. 8(b) of agreement informing the insurer about non-payment of outstanding dues by foreign importer within stipulated time except in 2 cases - Appeals disposed of.
