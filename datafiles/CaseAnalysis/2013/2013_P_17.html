Hardevinder Singh v Paramjit Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 9; (2013) 9 SCC 261; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 873; 2013(1) ALL MR 946; 2013 (96) ALR 833; 2013 (1) AWC 772; 2013 (1) CGLJ 267; 2013 (1) CLT(SC) 31; 2013 (1) CTC 409; JT 2013 (1) SC 249; 2014 (2) MahLJ 126; 2013 (2) MLJ 226; 2014 (1) MPLJ 487; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 968; 2013 (119) RD 735; 2013(1) SCALE 120; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 903; 2013 (2) WLN(SC) 1

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Maintainability, Suit For Possession, Right of appeal of defendant, Legal injury

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Family & Personal - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 96, 100 - Suit for possession - Right of appeal of defendant - Legal injury - Second appeal - Maintainability - Plaintiff claiming to be a co-sharer filed the suit for possession of suit land to the extent of his share treating the will alleged to have executed in favour of defendant nos. 1 to 4 as null and void with the consequential prayer for restraining them from alienating suit property in any manner - Plaintiff claimed suit land was ancestral coparcenary and Joint HUF - Trial Court held that suit land was a Joint HUF property; that defendant nos. 1 to 4 had failed to dispel the suspicious circumstances in the execution of Will in favour of defendant nos. 1 to 4 and, thus will was null and void; that mutation did not create any impediment on the rights of the plaintiff and other natural heirs of the testator; and that they were entitled to get joint possession of the suit land as per their shares in accordance with the law of natural succession - Aggrieved defendants filed an appeal which was allowed - Aggrieved defendant no. 5 (appellant/brother of plaintiff) filed second appeal before HC - Single Judge by impugned judgment held that appeal was not maintainable at the instance of defendant no. 5 u/s. 100 of CrPC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appeal filed by defendant no. 5 was maintainable -

Held, ss. 96 and 100 of CPC make provisions for preferring an appeal from any original appeal or from a decree in an appeal respectively - Said provisions did not enumerate the categories of persons who could file an appeal - If a judgment and decree prejudicially affects a person, needless to emphasize, he could prefer an appeal - Though the HC had referred to Smt. Ganga Bai v. Vijay Kumar and others, 1974 Indlaw SC 138 and Banarsi and others v. Ram Phal, 2003 Indlaw SC 159, yet it had not applied the ratio correctly to the facts - SC had clearly stated that if a person was prejudicially or adversely affected by the decree, he could maintain an appeal - In instant case, plaintiff claiming to be a co-sharer filed the suit and challenged the will - Defendant no. 5, the brother of plaintiff, supported his case - In an appeal at the instance of defendant nos. 1 to 4, the judgment and decree was overturned - Plaintiff entered into a settlement with the contesting defendants who had preferred the appeal - Such a decree, prejudicially affects the defendant no. 5 and, thus, he could have preferred an appeal - Instant appellant was a person aggrieved and was prejudicially affected by the decree and, hence, appeal could not have been thrown overboard treating as not maintainable - Impugned judgment of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
