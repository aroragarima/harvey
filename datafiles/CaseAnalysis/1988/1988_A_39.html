Andhra Kesari Educational Society v Director of School Education and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 November 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 279; (1989) 1 SCC 392; AIR 1989 SC 183; JT 1988 (4) SC 431; 1988(2) SCALE 1334; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 893; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 893; 1989 (1) UJ 42

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Education - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Affliation and permission - Starting new college - Grant of - Whether judgment and order D/- 24-04-1987 of Division Bench of High Court of Andhra Pradesh dismissing Writ Petition No. 1645 of 1987 is correct? - Held, while considering validity of earlier cancellation of permission, HC had laid down certain guidelines and issued some directions for obedience - HC made that order in interest of institution and students, though strictly speaking it was beyond power of HC - The HC did make it clear that if those conditions were not complied with within prescribed period - Institution shall cease to function - Record reveals that there were many deficiencies in institution - Reports of District Educational Officer and Inspection Commission of Nagarjuna University indicated that appellant did not satisfy all requirements for granting permission or affiliation - There is no fault with order of HC - Writ Petition dismissed.
