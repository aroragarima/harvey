Valliamma Champaka Pillai v Sivathanu Pillai and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 August 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, P.N. Shingal, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 194; (1979) 4 SCC 429; AIR 1979 SC 1937; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 354; 1980 UJ 312

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, arts. 144 and 148 - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 18 - States Reorganisation Act, 1956 - Acknowledgement of debt - Recital in the document - Accrual of right - (A) Whether a mere recital in a document as to existence of a past liability constitute an 'acknowledgement' within s. 18 of Limitation Act, 1963? - U/s. 18, Limitation Act, 1963 one of essential requirements for a valid 'acknowledgement' is that writing concerned must contain an admission of a subsisting liability - A mere admission of a past liability is not sufficient to constitute such an 'acknowledgement' - Held, a mere recital in a document as to existence of a past liability, coupled with a statement of its discharge, does not constitute an 'acknowledgement' within s. 18, Limitation Act, 1963 - (B) Whether High Court was justified in holding that suit items 40, 42 and 44 in instant case were time barred? - Further held, since limitation started running in 1913 or 1918, suit was time-barred from every point of view - The finding of HC that plaintiff's claim in regard to suit items 31 to 40, 42 and 44 was time barred - Appeal dismissed.
