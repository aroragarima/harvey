Messrs Bishwanath Prasad Radhey Shyam v Hindustan Metal Industries

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 345; (1979) 2 SCC 511; AIR 1982 SC 1444; 1979 ALJ 290; 22 PTC 731; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 757

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Summary: Designs Act, 1911, ss. 2(8), 4, 10, 26(1) - Patents Act, 1970, ss. 2(j), 10, 13(4), 43, 64 - (A) Essentials for grant of Patent - (B) Whether 'Invention' involves novelty and inventive step - Mixed question of law and fact - (C) If validity is gauranteed by Grant of Patent by Controller - (D) Interpretation - Construction of 'Specifications' - Held, it must have novelty and utility - Test to determine, indicated - Its validity is not guaranteed thereby - proper way to construe a specification is first to read the description of the invention the invention is to be claimed - Appeal Allowed.
