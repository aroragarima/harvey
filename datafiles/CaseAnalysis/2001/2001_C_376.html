hilakuri Gangulappa v Revenue Divisional Officer, Madanpalle and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20235; (2001) 4 SCC 197; AIR 2001 SC 1321; 2001 (2) ALD(SC) 114; JT 2001 (3) SC 430; 2001 (2) MLJ(SC) 33; 2001 (2) RCR(Civil) 659; 2001 (92) RD 585; 2001(2) SCALE 466; 2001 (2) SCJ 381; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 419; 2001 (2) SLT 698; 2001 (2) Supreme 365; 2001 (1) UC 589

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Indian Stamp Act, 1899, ss. 40 and 47-A - Suit filed before Munsif Court - Main relief claimed in suit is enforcement of an agreement executed on 26-06-1986 for sale of an immovable property - Agreement was produced in court was found to be insufficiently stamped - Munsif impounded it and forwarded instrument to Revenue Divisional Officer - Revenue Divisional Officer imposed a penalty equivalent to ten times of deficiency - Whether correct? - Court has power to admit document in evidence if party producing same would pay stamp duty together with a penalty amounting to ten times deficiency of stamp duty - When court chooses to admit document on compliance with such condition court need forward only a copy of document to Collector, together with amount collected from party for taking adjudicatory steps - But if party refuses to pay amount aforesaid Court has no other option except to impound document and forward same to Collector - On receipt of document through either of said avenues Collector has to adjudicate on question of deficiency of stamp duty - If Collector is of opinion that such instrument is chargeable with duty and is not duly stamped 'he shall require payment of proper duty or amount required to make up same together with a penalty of an amount not exceeding ten times amount of proper duty or of deficient portion thereof' - Held, Trial Court did not asked appellant, if it finds that instrument is insufficiently stamped, as to whether he would remit deficient portion of stamp duty together with a penalty amounting to ten times deficiency - Directed Munsif to consider first whether document is insufficiently stamped and if he finds that question in affirmative he has to adopt next step - Appeal allowed.
