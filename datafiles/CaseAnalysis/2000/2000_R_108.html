Collector Of Central Excise v Rallis India Ltd

Supreme Court of India

19 July 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3493; (2001) 10 SCC 437; 2003 (90) ECC 434; 2002 (142) E.L.T. 19; JT 2000 (10) SC 254; 2001 (42) RLT 581; 2000 (8) Supreme 172

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Paid, Within Six Months, Payment Of Duty, Tariff Item, Claim For Refund, Prescribed Period, Short Levy, Duty Paid, Refund Of Duty, Refund Claim, Home Consumption, Total Production, Barred By Time, Contrary View, Refund Of Excise Duty, Paid Under Protest, Excess Amount, By Or On Behalf Of, Adjusted, Sodium Hydrosulphite, Ambiguous Word, Recovery Of Short Levy, Unambiguous Word

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944 - Central Excises And Salt Act, 1944 - Constitution - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 8(1), 9, 11 and 173J - Notification No. 270/77, D/-20-08-1977 - Whether application for refund which was filed by respondent before Excise Authority was within prescribed period of limitation? - Period of limitation has to be determined with a reference to language of r. 11 r/w r. 173 J and is not to be influenced by provisions of notification D/-20-08-1977 - Held, period of limitation would commence u/r. 11 r/w r. 173 J from date of payment and/or from date of adjustment in case where there is a current account maintained u/r. 9 - Appeal allowed.
