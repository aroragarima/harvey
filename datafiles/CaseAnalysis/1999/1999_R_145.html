Handloom House, Ernakulam v Regional Director, Esi

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1129; (1999) 4 SCC 7; (1999) SCC (L&S) 834; AIR 1999 SC 1697; JT 1999 (3) SC 291; 1999 (1) LLJ 1319; 1999 (2) LLN 682; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 943; 1999 (2) SLR 767; 1999 (3) UPLBEC 1957

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Employees State Insurance (Amendment) Act, 1951 - Employees State Insurance Act, 1948, s. 2(22) - Whether incentive bonus and sales commission would fall within aforesaid third category of 'wages' as defined in s. 2(22) of Employees State Insurance Act? - Held, yes the main body of the definition encompasses within its fold three kinds of payments made to the employees - First is, all remuneration paid or payable in cash on fulfillment of the terms of employment - The second is any payment made to an employee in respect of any period of authorized leave etc. - The third is, other additional remuneration paid at intervals not exceeding two months - Appeal allowed.
