Gyasuddin Khan @ Md. Gyasuddin Khan v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 969; (2003) 12 SCC 516; AIR 2004 SC 210; 2003 (4) CCR 369; 2004 (1) Crimes 90; 2004 (2) CRJ 177; 2004 CRLJ 395; 2003 (3) JCC 1952; JT 2003 (9) SC 1; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 402; 2003(9) SCALE 401; 2003 (1) SCW 5924; 2003 (7) SLT 147; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 367; 2003 (8) Supreme 499

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ballistics

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 302 - Police personnel killed 3 of his colleagues - Both the Trial Court and the High Court convicted the accused - Held, it has been established beyond doubt that the appellant killed the three policemen. The conviction of the appellant under s. 302 is upheld - Further held, the indiscriminate killing of fellow-policemen resorted to by a member of disciplined force is no doubt an aggravating factor but it is offset by other mitigating circumstances. Death sentence is not the appropriate sentence in the instant case and is set aside.
