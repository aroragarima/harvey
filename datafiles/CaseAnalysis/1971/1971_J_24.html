Â Jayantilal Amrathlal v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, S.M. Sikri, G.K. Mitter, A.N. Grover, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 703; (1972) 4 SCC 174; AIR 1971 SC 1193; 1971 UJ 384

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 352 - Gold Control Rules, 1963, r. 126(1) - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 6 - Gold seizure - Show cause notice for confiscation u/r. 126-M - Validity of - Whether High Court is justified in validating proceedings commenced by Collector of Central Excise by means of notice D/- 5-06-1965? - Held, there are no provisions in Gold (Control) Act, 1968 which are inconsistent with r. 126(I)(10) of the Gold Control Rules - Action taken under that rule must be deemed to be continuing in view of s. 6 of General Clauses Act, 1897 - Absence of saving clause in a new enactment preserving rights and liabilities under repealed law is neither material nor decisive of question whether it has taken away those rights and liabilities - HC that proceedings commenced by Collector of Central Excise by means of notice D/- 5-06-1965 must be deemed to be continuing - Appeal dismissed.
