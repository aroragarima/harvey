Suman Kapur v Sudhir Kapur

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2082; (2009) 1 SCC 422; AIR 2009 SC 589; 2009 (1) ALD(SC) 33; 2008(6) ALL MR 937; 2009 (74) ALR 327; 2009 (1) AWC 169; 2009 (1) Bom.C.R. 88; JT 2008 (12) SC 70; 2009 (1) MLJ(SC) 1345; 2009 (2) MPLJ 393; 2008 (4) RCR(Civil) 837; 2008(14) SCALE 404; [2008] 15 S.C.R. 972

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Family & Personal

Keywords: Grand-Mother

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, ss. 13, 13(1)(ia), 13(1)(ib) - Termination of Pregnancy - No Consent - Mental cruelty - Divorce - Sustainability - Respondent-husband, filed petition for divorce u/s. 13(1)(ia) and (ib) of 1955, Act, on grounds of cruelty and desertion - Trial Court held that husband was not entitled to decree of divorce on ground that wife had deserted husband for continuous period of not less than two years immediately preceding presentation of petition - Trial court however, held that it was fully established by husband that there was cruelty on part of wife - Appeal preferred before HC, which appreciated evidence on record and confirmed decree of divorce passed by Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC had committed any error in confirming decree passed by Trial court - Held, it could not be said that by recording finding as to mental cruelty by wife against husband, Courts below had committed any illegality - Further, cruelty would be established if conduct itself was proved or admitted - Absence of intention should not make any difference in case, if by ordinary sense in human affairs, act complained of could otherwise be regarded as cruelty - Mens rea was not a necessary element in cruelty - Relief to party could not be denied on ground that there had been no deliberate or willful ill-treatment - Court found no infirmity in approach of HC - Finding relating to mental cruelty recorded by Trial Court and confirmed by HC suffers from no infirmity and Court see no reason to interfere - Since, decree of divorce was confirmed on ground of mental cruelty as held by both courts, i.e. Trial Court as well as by HC, no relief could be granted so far as reversal of decree of courts below was concerned - Appeal disposed of.
