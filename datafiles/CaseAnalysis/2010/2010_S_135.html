Dalco Engineering Private Limited and another v Shree Satish Prabhakar Padhye and another

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, R.M. Lodha, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 236; (2010) 4 SCC 378; AIR 2010 SC 1576; 2010 (3) AWC 2854; 2010 (4) Bom.C.R. 776; [2011] 164 Comp Cas 275; 2010 (2) ESC 294; 2010 (125) FLR 311; JT 2010 (3) SC 450; 2010 (2) KLT 447; 2010 (3) LLJ 64; 2010 (4) MLJ(SC) 383; 2010(3) M.P.H.T. 128; 2010(3) SCALE 427; 2010 (6) SLR 127

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Service

Summary: (A) Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Persons with Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection of Rights and Full Participation) Act, 1995, ss. 2k and 47 - Companies Act, 1956 - Termination - Respondent was employed as Telephone Operator by the appellant, private limited company incorporated under the provisions of the Companies Act, 1956 for more than two decades - Respondent's services was terminated by appellant on the ground that he had become deaf - Respondent complained to the Disability Commissioner alleging that he was fit, able and normal when he joined service of the appellant and he acquired the hearing impairment during the period of service, he should have been continued in employment in some suitable post - Disability Commissioner made an order suggesting to the employer to undertake a social responsibility, by re- employing the respondent to discharge any other work - Said suggestion was not accepted by the employer - Respondent filed writ petition submitting that instead of making a mere suggestion ought to have issued a direction to the employer - HC allowed said petition and directed the employer to reinstate the respondent and shift him to a suitable post - HC further held that respondent, though a private limited company, was an 'establishment' as defined u/s. 2(k) of the Act and consequently s. 47 of the Act enjoined it not to dispense with the services of its employee who acquired a disability - Hence, present appeal - Whether respondent in the first case and the appellant in the second case are entitled to claim any relief with reference to s. 47 of the Act? - Held, respondent in the first case and appellant in the second case are not establishments, within the meaning of that expression in s. 2(k) of the Act, s. 47 of the Act will not apply - Appeal disposed of.

(B) Corporate - Companies Act, 1956 - Establishment - Whether a company incorporated under the Companies Act (other than a Government company as defined in s. 617 of the Companies Act, 1956) is an 'establishment' as defined in s. 2(k) of the Act? - Held, no - There is indication in the definition of 'establishment' itself, which clearly establishes that all companies incorporated under the Companies Act are not establishments - Enumeration of establishments in the definition of 'establishment' specifically includes 'a Government Company as defined in s. 617 of the Companies Act, 1956' - Legislature, took pains to include in the definition of 'establishment' only one category of companies incorporated under the Companies Act, that is the 'Government Companies' as defined in s. 617 of the Companies Act - Inclusion of only a specific category of companies incorporated under the Companies Act, 1956 within the definition of 'establishment' necessarily and impliedly excludes all other types of companies registered under the Companies Act, 1956, from the definition of 'establishment' - Legislative intent was to apply s. 47 of the Act only to such establishments as were specifically defined as 'establishment' u/s. 2(k) of the Act and not to other establishments - Private employers, whether individuals, partnerships, proprietary concerns or companies (other than Government companies) are clearly excluded from the 'establishments' to which s. 47 of the Act will apply - Appeal disposed of.
