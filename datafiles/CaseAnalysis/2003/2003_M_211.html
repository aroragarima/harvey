The Assistant Commissioner, Assessment-II, Bangalore and Others v Messrs Velliappa Textiles Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 777; (2003) 11 SCC 405; AIR 2004 SC 86; 2003 (4) CCR 50; 2003 (4) Crimes 267; 2004 CRLJ 1221; 2003 (184) CTR 193; 2003 (157) E.L.T. 369; [2003] 263 ITR 550; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 99; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 695; 2003(7) SCALE 530; 2003 (1) SCW 5647; 2003 (5) SLT 509; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 763; 2003 (7) Supreme 227

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Criminal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Taxation Laws (Amendment) Act, 1975, Crimes Act, 1914, Defence (General) Regulations, Canadian Criminal Code (ENG), Indian Penal Code (Amendment) Bill, 1972, Commonwealth Criminal Code Act, 1995, Indian Merchandise Act, Prevention of Food Adulteration (Amendment) Act, 1976

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Corporate - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 51 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, o.21, rr. 32, 33 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 19, s. 5, s. 6 - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 2(31), 266B, 276-B, 276C, 276E, 277, 278, 278A, 278B, 279, 279(1), 279(2) - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, ss. 190(1)(b), 190(1)(c), 197, 235, 360, 482 - Companies Act, 1956 - Whether a company can be attributed with mens rea on basis that those who work or are working for it have committed a crime and can be convicted in a criminal case - Whether a company is liable for punishment of fine if provision of law contemplates punishment by way of imprisonment only or a minimum period of punishment by imprisonment plus fine whether fine alone can be imposed - Held, first respondent company cannot be prosecuted for offences u/ss. 276 C, 277 and 278 r/w s.278 since each one of these sections requires imposition of a mandatory term of imprisonment coupled with a fine and leave no choice to Court to impose only a fine - Further held, courts would be shirking their responsibility of imparting justice by holding that prosecution of a company is unsustainable merely on ground that being juristic person it cannot be sent to jail to undergo sentence - Companies are growing in size and have huge resources and finances at their command - In course of their business activity they may sometimes commit breach of law of land or endanger other's lives - More than four thousand people lost life and thousands others suffered permanent impairment in Bhopal on account of gross criminal act of a multinational corporation - It will be wholly wrong to allow a company to go away scot free without even being prosecuted in event of commission of a crime only on ground that it cannot be made to suffer part of mandatory punishment HC is wholly erroneous in law - Further, appeal is dismissed as regards first respondent and it is allowed as regards prosecution against second respondent.
