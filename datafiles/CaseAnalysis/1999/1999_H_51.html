Colgate Palmolive (India) Limited v Hindustan Lever Limited

Supreme Court of India

18 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 206; (1999) 7 SCC 1; AIR 1999 SC 3105; AIR 1999 SCW 3050; 1999 (4) CompLJ 1; 1999 (2) CPJ 17; JT 1999 (6) SC 89; [1999] Supp1 S.C.R. 560

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Corporate

Summary: Monopolies and Restrictive Trade Practices Act, 1969, s. 12 A - Monopolies and Restrictive Trade Practices Regulations, 1991, Regn. 76 - Temporary injunction - Grant of - Power of commission, as to - 'Suraksha Chakra' as displayed in advertisement of tooth paste producing company - No allegations in complaint against term 'Suraksha Chakra' nor allegations that it was misleading or unfair trade practice - 'Suraksha Chakra' was only to complete advertisement portion as incidental issue - Order of Commission for restraining company against displaying 'Suraksha Chakra' in advertisements - Passed after taking into consideration U. S. Trade Commission's Report in respect of advertisement of tooth paste in U. S. by parent company - Held, no factual similarity between two cases - Complaint in instant case filed only as counter against earlier application against complainant - 'Suraksha Chakra' displayed in advertisement for about 13 years - Order of temporary injunction bad.
