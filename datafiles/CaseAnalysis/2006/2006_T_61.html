The Secretary,Malankara Syrian Catholic College v T. Jose and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 860; (2007) 1 SCC 386; AIR 2007 SC 570; 2007 (3) Bom.C.R. 199; 2006(13) SCALE 1; 2007 (3) SCJ 700; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 644

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Eligibility Conditions

Summary: [A.] Education - Kerala Literacy, Scientific and Charitable Socieities Registration Act, 1955 - Kerala University Act, 1974, s. 57(3) - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(f), 28, 30(1), 30(2) - Kerala University Act, 1969, ss. 53(1), 53(2), 53(3) - Kerala Education Rules, r. 44A - To what extent, State can regulate right of minorities to administer their educational institutions, when such institutions receive aid from State?; Whether right to choose a Principal is part of right of minorities under art.30(1) to establish and administer educational institutions of their choice?; If so, s 57(3) of Act would violate art.30(1) of Constitution of India? - Held, all laws made by State to regulate administration of educational institutions, and grant of aid, will apply to minority educational institutions also - If any such regulations interfere with overall administrative control by Management over staff, or abridges/dilutes, in any other manner, right to establish and administer educational institutions, such regulations, to that extent, will be inapplicable to minority institutions - Right to choose Principal is an important part of right of administration and even if institution is aided, there can be no interference with said right - s.57(3) clearly interferes with right of minority management to have a person of their choice as head of institution and thus violates art.30(1) - s.57(3) of Act cannot therefore apply to minority run educational institutions even if they are aided - Appeals allowed.
