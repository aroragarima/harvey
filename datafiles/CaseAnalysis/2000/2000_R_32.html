Union of India and Another v R. S. Sharma

Supreme Court of India

18 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, D.P. Mohapatra, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 690; (2000) 4 SCC 394; (2000) SCC (L&S) 653; AIR 2000 SC 2337; 2000 (3) AWC 1811; 2000 (85) FLR 915; JT 2000 (4) SC 649; 2000 LabIC 2392; 2000 (2) LLJ 1271; 2000 (3) LLN 4; 2000(3) SCALE 409; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 151; 2000 (3) SLJ 349; 2000 (2) SLR 428; 2000 (5) SLT 107; 2000 (3) Supreme 409; 2000 (2) UPLBEC 1635

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16 - Govt. Employee - Promotion - 'Sealed cover procedure' adopted while pendency of investigations - Recommendations of Departmental Promotion Committee had to remain in sealed cover in view of cl. (iv) of para 2 of office memorandum providing procedure - Effect of subsequent deletion of cl. (iv) - Held, not material as candidate's case was covered also u/cl. (iii) in view of order granting sanction to prosecute was passed by President - Appeal allowed.
