M. T. Enrica Lexie and anothers v Doramma

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 166; (2012) 6 SCC 760; AIR 2012 SC 2134; 2012 CRLJ 2845; JT 2012 (4) SC 608; 2012 (3) RCR(Criminal) 710B; 2012(5) SCALE 134; [2012] 4 S.C.R. 174

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; International

Keywords: Voyage

Summary: International - Carriers & Transportation - Clearance for vessel to sail - FIR was lodged by owner of the Indian registered fishing boat alleging that while the fishing boat was sailing through the Arabian Sea, incriminate firing was opened by an Italian Ship (appellant No. 1) - As a result of firing from the appellant No. 1 vessel, two innocent fishermen who were on board the fishing boat died and the other occupants of the boat saved their lives as they were lying in reclining position on the deck of the boat - Appellant No. 1 vessel was intercepted and brought to the Port of Cochin - Two Marines who allegedly committed the offence were arrested - Appellant No. 1 vessel was directed that the vessel shall not continue her voyage without prior sanction of competent authority - Appellants submitted that although all the agencies had completed their respective investigations, none of them were giving official clearance for the vessel to sail and that necessitated them to file a writ Petition before the HC for appropriate directions and permission to the appellant No. 1 vessel for sailing and proceeding with her voyage - Writ Petition was allowed directing the respondent Nos. 1 and 2 to allow the appellant No. 1 vessel to commence her voyage on certain conditions - On appeal, DB of the HC noted that investigation in the matter was not yet complete and no charge-sheet had been filed and matter needed to be considered by the concerned Judicial Magistrate - DB set aside the order of the Single Judge - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the order passed by the Single Judge could be allowed to stand as it was or deserved to be modified - Republic of Italy stated that the it was agreeable to give assurance to SC that if the presence of the four Marines was required by any Court or in response to any summons issued by any Court or lawful authority, the Republic of Italy shall ensure their presence before the appropriate Court or such authority - Held, assurance given by the Republic of Italy to secure the presence of these four Marines, if required by any court or lawful authority, fully meets the ends of justice and protects wholly the interest of the State Govt. - In no way it affected the State Govt. right to proceed with the investigation and prosecute the offenders - Hence, subject to the compliances by the appellants, the State Govt. and its authorities to allow the appellant No. 1 vessel to commence her voyage - Appeal disposed of.
