Union of India v Nanak Singh

Supreme Court of India

31 January 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 33; AIR 1968 SC 1370; 1969 (5) DLT 48; 1970 (3) LLJ 10; 1968 (2) SCJ 881; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 887; 1968 SLR 826

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Termination of employment - Res judicata - Respondent hold post of Field Inspector in Office of Custodian, as temporary employee - Additional Settlement Commissioner who was holding office of Additional Custodian terminated employment of respondent after giving him one month's salary in lieu of notice - Respondent filed petition before HC - HC by order dismissed petition - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether judgment of HC in petition operated as res judicata in Civil Suit filed by respondent - Held, it was unable to agree with respondent that HC reserved to respondent right to agitate question about authority of Additional Settlement Commissioner in separate suit - There was no such express reservation, and it could not be implied, for such implication was inconsistent with final order passed by HC - However, HC was in error in holding that appeal could be decided only on first point - Order of dismissing petition could still operate as res judicata in respect of both points on which petition was founded - Additional Settlement Commissioner had no authority to terminate employment of respondent - - However, once appeal was allowed and petition was dismissed, dismissal of petition operated as rejection of both grounds on which it was founded - Thus, judgment of Judicial Commissioner was held not to operate as res judicata in subsequent suit between parties to previous suit, because dispute was not decided on its merits in previous suit expressly or even by implication - Appeal allowed.
