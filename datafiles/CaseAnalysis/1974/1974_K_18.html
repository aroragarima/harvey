Karbhari Bhimaji Rohamare v Shanker Rao Genuji Kolhe and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 November 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Alagiriswami, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 194; (1975) 1 SCC 252; AIR 1975 SC 575; [1975] 2 S.C.R. 753

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Office Of Profit Under

Summary: Election - Bombay Industrial Relations Act, 1946, ss. 86 B, 86 C, 86 G, 86 H, 86 I and 86 J - Bombay Civil Rules, r. 1(1)(b) - Bombay Legislature Members (Removal of Disqualifications) Act, 1956, s. 2 - Disqualification - On the ground of holding office of profit - Whether respondent no. 1 held an office of profit? - Merely because part of payment made to 1st respondent is called honorarium and part of payment daily allowance, it cannot come to conclusion that daily allowance is sufficient to meet his daily expenses and honorarium is a source of profit - Payments made to 1st respondent cannot be a source of profit unless he stays with some friends or relatives or stays in a dharamshala - Law regarding question whether a person holds an office of profit should be interpreted reasonably having regard to circumstances of case and times with which one is concerned, as also class of person whose case which is dealing with and not divorced from reality - Appeal dismissed
