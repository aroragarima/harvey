G. Nagamma and Another v Siromanamma and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1969; (1996) 2 SCC 25; 1996 (1) CLT 161; JT 1998 (4) SC 484; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 684; 1995(7) SCALE 353; 1996 (1) SCJ 188; [1995] Supp5 S.C.R. 701

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Conditional Sale

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 O. 16 r. 17 - Amendment of the plaint - Application for Amendment rejected as the original plaint the suit was for specific performance and the reconveyance was not incorporated in the sale deed and that therefore the amendment was not warranted - High Court held that the amendment would change the nature of the suit as well as cause of action - Held, it is settled law that the plaintiff is entitled to plead even inconsistent pleas - In this case, they are seeking alternative reliefs - The application was for amendment of the plaint whereby neither cause of action could change nor the relief could be materially affected - Appeal allowed.
