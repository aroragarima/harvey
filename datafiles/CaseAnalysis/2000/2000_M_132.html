U. P. State Road Transport Corporation and Others v Mahesh Kumar Mishra and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 688; (2000) SCC (L&S) 356; (2000) 3 SCC 450; AIR 2000 SC 1151; 2000 ALJ 865; 2000 (2) AWC 1475; 2001 (1) CGLJ 197; 2000 (2) CLR 10; JT 2000 (3) SC 173; 2000 LabIC 1031; 2000 (1) LLJ 1113; 2000 (2) LLN 399; 2000(2) SCALE 367; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 439; 2000 (2) SLR 435; 2000 (2) UPLBEC 1195

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: The bus conductor was found to have issued short distance tickets to all passengers, there was a dispute about point at which passengers boarded bus - Decision based only upon report of transport inspector which was also signed by delinquent and no passenger was examined at that time - Held, that not only Supreme Court but also High Court can interfere with punishment inflicted upon delinquent employee if, that penalty, shocks conscience of court after disciplinary proceedings.
