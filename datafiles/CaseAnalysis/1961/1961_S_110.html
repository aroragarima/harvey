Â Burmah Construction Company v State of Orissa and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.R. Madholkar, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 45; AIR 1962 SC 1320; 1962 (28) CutLT 150; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 242; 1961 (12) STC 816; [1962] Supp1 S.C.R. 242

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947, s. 14 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Refund - (A) Whether s.14 ultra vires State Legislature? - S. 14 imposes an obligation upon Collector to refund to a dealer any amount paid by such dealer in excess of amount due from him under Act - Granting refund of tax improperly or illegally collected and restriction on exercise of that right are both ancillary or subsidiary matters relating to primary head of tax on sale of goods - Held, provisions of s. 14 of Act are not ultra vires State Legislature - (B) Whether petition for refund of amount collected by State Sales Tax Authorities maintainable? - Further held, petition in present case is for enforcement of liability of Collector imposed by statute to refund a tax illegally collected and it was maintainable; but it can only be allowed subject to restrictions which have been imposed by Legislature - It is not open to claimant to rely upon statutory right and to ignore restrictions subject to which right is made enforceable - High Court was right in restricting order of refund in petition u/art. 226 of Constitution - Order of refund passed by HC, requires to be slightly modified - Appeal dismissed.
