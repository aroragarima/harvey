State Of U.P v Kamal Klshore Saini

Supreme Court of India

06 November 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

B.C. Ray, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28289; (1988) 2 SCC (Cr) 107; (1988) 1 SCC 287; AIR 1988 SC 208; 1988 CRLJ 405; 1988 CRLR 40; 1988 CrLR(SC) 40; JT 1987 (4) SC 285; 1987(2) SCALE 989; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 859

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Arbitration and ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 20 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Acquiescence of a party to arbitration proceeding - Participating in it for long time - Susequent contention that due to some disability matter is legally incapable of being referred to arbitrator - Maintainability of contention - Held, cannot be allowed thereafter to contend so - As there was no demur but something which can be called acquiescence on the part of the respondents which precludes them from challenging the participation (sic proceedings)Appeal allowed.
