Glanrock Estate (Private) Limited v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

09 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 746; (2010) 10 SCC 96; JT 2010 (9) SC 568; 2011 (5) RCR(Civil) 129; 2010(9) SCALE 270; [2010] 12 S.C.R. 597

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Constitution (Seventeenth Amendment) Act, 1964, Gudalur Janmam Estates (Abolition and Conversion into Ryotwari) Act, 1969, Constitution (Thirty-Fourth Amendment) Act, 1974, Constitution (Twenty-Fourth Amendment) Act, 1971, Constitution (Twenty-Sixth Amendment) Act, 1971, Constitution (Twenty-Ninth Amendment) Act, 1972, Central Educational Institutions (Reservation in Admission) Act, 2006, Kerala Private Forests (Vesting and Assignment) Act, 1971, Tamil Nadu Land Reforms (Fixation of Ceiling on Land) Act, 1961, Constitution (Ninety Third Amendment) Act, 2005, Constitutional Amendment Act, Tamil Nadu Land Reforms (Fixation of Ceiling on Land) Second Amendment Act, 1972

Summary: Constitution - Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 31B - Constitution (Thirty- fourth Amendment) Act, 1974 - Gudalur Janmam Estates (Abolition and Conversion into Ryotwari) Act, 1969 - Tamil Nadu Land Reforms (Fixation of Ceiling on Land) Second Amendment Act, 1972 - Constitutional validity of the Constitution (Thirty-fourth Amendment) Act, 1974 - By the Amendment Act, the Gudalur Janmam Estates (Abolition and Conversion into Ryotwari) Act, 1969 [Janmam Act] stood inserted in the Ninth Schedule to the Constitution as Item No. 80 - (A) Petitioner contended that by reason of the forests vesting in the State under the Janmam Act 'the rule of equality in law' stood violated which violation amounted to abrogation of art. 14 - Held, doctrine of classification u/art. 14 has several facets and none of those facets have been abrogated by the Constitution (Thirty-fourth Amendment) Act, 1974 - Further, 'similarly situated test' is not attracted in present case for the reason that the two Acts, namely, the Janmam Act, which seeks to abolish a tenure, is distinct and separate from the Ceiling Act, 1972 - Therefore, in the present case, not even an ordinary principle of equality u/art. 14, is violated - (B) Whether Tamil Nadu State Legislature lacked legislative competence to enact Janmam Act? - It was submitted that the Tamil Nadu Legislature did not have legislative competence to enact s. 3 of the Janmam Act insofar as the said Act related to transfer of forests in Janmam estates to the State without any public purpose - Held, requirement of public purpose and compensation are not legislative requirements of the competence of Legislature to make laws under Entry 18, List II or Entry 42, List III, but are conditions or restrictions u/art. 31(2) of the Constitution as the said article stood in 1969 - Therefore, when the Janmam Act was put in the Ninth Schedule in 1974, the Act received immunity from art. 31(2) with retrospective effect - (C) Whether the Janmam Act could not be applied because of the Ceiling Act,1972? - Held, scope and ambit of the two Acts are completely different and they operate in different spheres - No merit in the argument of the petitioners that both the Acts operated in the same field and, consequently, it was not open to the State Government to act according to the provisions of the Janmam Act - Petitions dismissed.
