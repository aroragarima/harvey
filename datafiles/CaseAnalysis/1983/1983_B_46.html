Union of India and Others v Bombay Tyre International Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, R.S. Pathak, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 359; (1983) 4 SCC 210; (1983) SCC (Tax) 315; AIR 1984 SC 420; 1984 (2) ECC 102; 1983 ECR 653; 1983 (12) E.L.T. 869; 1983 MPLJ 441; 1983(1) SCALE 521; 1984 TAXLR 2436; 1983 UJ 611

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Wholesale Cash Price, Of The Like Kind And Quality, Publicity

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 4 - (A) Determination of value of excisable article for determining excise duty leviable? - Held, considering position under 1944 prior to amendment of 1973 and after that Position explained - (B) Determination of admissibility or otherwise of deductions - Held, position as regards admissibility or otherwise of deductions is same under amended s. 4 as it was under that section as it stood unamended - (C) What are expense deductible from wholesale price determinable u/s. 4(1) and (2)? - Held, no deductions can be made save and except in respect of (a) trade discount (b) amount of excise duty, sales tax and other taxes; and (c) averaged freight - (D) Applicability of principles where tariff value has been fixed up - The principles laid down in amended Act XXII of 1973 of Central Excises and Salt Act cannot apply where tariff value has been fixed in respect of any excisable goods u/s. 3(2) - (E) Validity of definition of 'related person' in s. 4 (4)(c) of Act - Held, on proper interpretation of definition of 'related, words a relative and a distributor of assessee do not refer to any distributor but they are limited only to a distributor who is a relative of assessee within meaning of Companies Act, 1956 and hence definition is not unduly wide and does not suffer from any constitutional infirmity; it is within legislative competence of Parliament - Order accordingly.
