Kamalabai Jethamal v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

18 January 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 124; AIR 1962 SC 1189; 1962 (64) BomLR 517; 1962 CRLJ 273; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 632

Case Digest

Subject: Socio-Economic; Women & Children

Keywords: Woman, Brothel, Prostitution

Summary: Criminal - Women & Children - Socio-Economic - Suppression of Immoral Traffic in Women and Girls Act, 1956, ss. 3, 3(2), 4(1), 7 and 18 - Criminal Procedure Code, ss. 52 and 103 - Conviction u/ss. 3(2) and 4(1) of Act - (A) Conviction challenged - Held, it may be that search was contrary to spirit or of CrPC but fact remains that High Court has accepted that there was a search and a hundred rupees currency note was recovered - There is evidence to show that amount was used for purpose of procuring a girl for prostitution - Payment must therefore be held to be proved - Appellant is guilty of offences of which she was accused - (B) Whether HC can order eviction of appellant - Provision in s. 18 of Act provides that if magistrate is satisfied that portion is being used as a brothel or for carrying on prostitution then magistrate may pass orders directing eviction of occupier - There is a specific provision in s. 18 that court convicting a person of offences u/s. 3 or s. 7 of Act can pass such an order - Further held, HC ordered conviction of appellant u/s. 3 of Act and therefore it had power to order her eviction - Appeal dismissed.
