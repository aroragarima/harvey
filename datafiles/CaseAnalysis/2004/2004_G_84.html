M.T. Khan and Others v Government of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 11; (2004) 2 SCC 267; AIR 2004 SC 2934; 2004 (55) ALR 91; 2004 (2) AWC 1458; 2004 (1) CCR 45; 2004 (1) Crimes 254; 2004 CRLJ 815; JT 2004 (1) SC 146; 2004 (2) LLN 103; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 191; 2004(1) SCALE 336; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 117; 2004 (1) SLR 722; 2004 (1) SLT 105; 2004 (1) Supreme 237

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Advocates & Judges - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 124, 148, 162, 165, 168, 177, 216, 234, 280, 367, 53, 63, 74, 76 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 13 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, ss. 24, 25, 302 - Advocates Act, 1961, s. 23 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 92 - Appointment - Additional Advocate General - Authority of state - Whether expression used in art 165 contemplates appointment of more than one Advocate General - Held, State by amending provisions of ss.24, 25, CrPC, 1973, may make a law regulating appointment of Public Prosecutor or Additional Public Prosecutor Such a law can also be made for regulating appointment of other State counsel - Further held, once it is held that such persons who are although designated as Additional Advocate Generals are not authorised to perform any constitutional or statutory functions, indisputably such an appointment must be held to have been made by State in exercise of its executive power and not in exercise of its constitutional power - Consequently, Additional Advocate General so appointed is not in constitutional scheme and does not hold constitutional office.
