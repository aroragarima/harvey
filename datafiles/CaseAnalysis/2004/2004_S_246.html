Smt. Dayamathi Bai v Sri K.M. Shaffi

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 562; (2004) 7 SCC 107; AIR 2004 SC 4082; 2004 (57) ALR 290; 2004 (56) ALR 728; 2004 (3) AWC 2588; 2004 (4) JhrCR(SC) 5; 2005 (1) LCD 272; 2005 (140) PLR 798; 2004 (3) RCR(Civil) 721; 2004 (2) RD 719; 2004(6) SCALE 440; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 336

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC - Evidence - Suit for declaration as to title of property being one plot - Mode of proof - Whether HC erred in holding that registered certified copy of sale deed was admissible in evidence as document produced was more than 30 years old? - When plaintiff submitted a certified copy of sale deed in evidence and when sale deed taken on record and marked as an exhibit at trial stage, appellant did not raise any objection - Held, it was not open to appellant to object to mode of proof before lower appellate Court - Appeal dismissed.
