Pancho and another v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

20 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 696; (2011) 10 SCC 165; AIR 2012 SC 523; 2012 CRLJ 832; 2011 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3372; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 665; 2011(12) SCALE 187; [2011] 12 S.C.R. 1173

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 392 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 -Robbery - Murder- Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants allegedly robbed and murdered the deceased by giving him shots - Trial Court convicted and sentenced the appellants for robbery and murder - HC commuted the sentence but confirmed the conviction - Whether the conviction of appellants, based on 2nd appellant's extra-judicial but retracted confession, was sustainable - Held, in dealing with a case against an accused, the court should not start with the confession of a co-accused; it should begin with other evidence adduced by the prosecution and after it had formed its opinion with regard to the quality and effect of the said evidence, then it was permissible to turn to the confession in order to receive assurance to the conclusion of guilt which the judicial mind was about to reach on the said other evidence - Extra-judicial confession made by the 2nd appellant (A1) was the main plank of the prosecution in the instant case - Evidence of PW4 indicated that before whom A1was stated to have confessed that 1st appellant (A2) had shot dead deceased with country made pistol - Prosecution evidence did not indicate that A1 and PW4 knew each other intimately - It was, therefore, difficult to accept the prosecution case that A1 made any extra-judicial confession to PW4 - Further, A1's statement recorded u/s. 313 of the Code, he had denied that he made any such statement and such retraction further made a dent in the alleged extra-judicial confession - Prosecution alleged that recovery had been made in pursuance of A1's extra-judicial confession - Recovery of country made pistol was made more than about six months after the date of incident - Prosecution had not led any evidence to show as to in whose custody such pistol was during the period of six months after the incident - In his statement recorded u/s. 313 of the Code, A2 had denied that any such recovery was made from him - Even assuming that the recovery was proved, it was unable to hold in the absence of any other cogent evidence that it was sufficient to establish that A2 caused the fatal firearm injury to deceased with the said pistol - Apart from the pistol which was stated to have been recovered from A2, the prosecution had relied on certain other discoveries made at the instance of the accused - Articles which were stated to have been discovered were easily available in the market - Evidence relating to discovery of these articles should, therefore, be rejected - Hence, so far as A2 is concerned, except the evidence of alleged belated discovery of certain articles at his instance, there was no other evidence on record to connect him to the offence in question - When there was no other evidence of sterling quality on record establishing his involvement, he should not be convicted on the basis of the alleged extra-judicial confession of the co-accused A1was also not credible - Once A1's extra- judicial confession was obliterated and kept out of consideration, his conviction also could not be sustained because it was come to the conclusion that the alleged discovery of articles at his instance could not be relied upon - Hence, there was no credible evidence to persuade to uphold the conviction of A1 - Impugned judgment of conviction and sentence of the appellants were set aside - Appeal disposed of.
