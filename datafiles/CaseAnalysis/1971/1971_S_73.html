Himachal Pradesh Administration v Shri Om Prakash

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, D.G. Palekar

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 550; (1972) SCC (Cr) 88; (1972) 1 SCC 249; AIR 1972 SC 975; 1972 CRLJ 606; 1974 (2) ILR(Del) 73; 1972 (2) MLJ(SC) 16; 1972 (1) SCJ 691; [1972] 2 S.C.R. 765

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 157 and 510 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 8 and 27 - Murder - Conviction by Trial Court - Acquittal by High Court - Legality of - Held, calling of the same persons to witness several searches or recoveries u/s. 27 of the Evidence Act, it objectionable, and would render the search or the recovery doubtful and suspect, if not invalid - There is no justification for HC in jettisoning cogent evidence of a conclusive nature on mere conjectures and on omnibus ground that witnesses were not independent or impartial, is without jurisdiction - Evidence in this case is sufficient to justify conviction of accused of an offence of murder - Set aside judgment of acquittal of HC, convict accused u/s. 302 and sentence him to life imprisonment - Appeal disposed of.
