Dhupa Chamar and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

02 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1636; (2002) 6 SCC 506; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1389; AIR 2002 SC 2834; AIR 2002 SCW 3217; 2002 (3) CCR 113; 2002 (4) Crimes 65; 2002 (6) CRJ 124; 2002 CRLJ 3764; 2002 (2) JCC 1332; JT 2002 (5) SC 530; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 327; 2002(5) SCALE 447; 2002 (4) SLT 611; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 412; 2002 (6) Supreme 12

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 148, 149 and 323 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused persons Nos. 1 to 4 armed with bhalas allegedly murdered deceased - Respondent /State approached Trial Court which convicted appellant Nos.1 and 2 u/ss. 302 and 148, appellant Nos. 3 and 4 u/s. 302 r/w s.149 and u/ss.148 and 323 IPC to undergo life and rigorous imprisonment respectively - Aggrieved appellants/accused persons filed appeal before HC which confirmed Trial Court's order and modified appellant No.2's conviction converted from 302 IPC into u/s.302/149 IPC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether lower Court's order was sustainable - Held, relied on Balmiki alias Munna v. State of M.P., 1999 Indlaw SC 673, question with regard to nature of offence had to be determined on facts and in circumstances of each case - Nature of injury, whether it was on vital or non-vital part of body, weapon used, circumstances in which injury was caused and manner in which injury was inflicted were all relevant factors which might go to determine required intention or knowledge of offender and offence committed by him -Therefore, in instant case nature of injury whereby important blood vessels were ruptured inasmuch aorta and artery were cut and when doctor opined that death was caused as result of severe haemorrhage and shock due to rupture of great veins, undoubtedly, it could be reasonably inferred there from that such solitary injury inflicted upon deceased was sufficient to cause death in ordinary course of nature - Further, circumstance would show that accused intentionally inflicted injury and same would indicate such state of mind of appellant - Thus, however, submitted maximum sentence that had been awarded was one year and as they have served out more than that, they should be directed to be released forthwith - Court found force in said submission as it had been pointed out that appellant No.2 had remained in jail for period of seven years and each of appellant No.3 and appellant No. 4 two years three months - Hence, appeal of appellant Nos. 2, 3 and 4 was allowed in part and their conviction and sentence u/s.302/149 IPC were set aside and acquitted from said charge and conviction and sentence under other sections were confirmed but as appellants had already served out sentences awarded there under and directed to be released forthwith if not required in any other case - Appeal disposed of.
