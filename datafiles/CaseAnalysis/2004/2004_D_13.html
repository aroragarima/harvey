Dalip Singh v Mehar Singh Rathee and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 July 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 536; (2004) 7 SCC 650; 2004(10) SCALE 112

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC - Sale deed - Specific performance - Whether suit was barred under O 2 r. 2, or o 23 r. 1? - Held, plea that suit barred under o 23 r. 1, CPC was not taken before HC therefore appellant cannot be permitted to take this plea now - When an objection regarding bar to filing of suit under O 2 r. 2, is taken, it is essential for court to know what exactly was cause of action which was alleged in previous suit in order that it might be in a position to appreciate whether cause of action alleged in second suit is identical with one that was subject matter of previous suit - As plea had not been raised in written statement and an issue framed on this point, no opportunity was provided to respondent No 1 to lead evidence to rebut - In absence of pleadings and proof of identity of cause of action appellant could not be permitted to raise plea of bar - HC order confirmed - Appeal dismissed.
