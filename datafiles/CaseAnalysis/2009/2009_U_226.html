Uttar Pradesh Public Service Commision v Satya Narayan Sheohare and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1912; (2009) 5 SCC 473; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1881; 2009 (3) LLN 45; 2009(4) SCALE 425; [2009] 4 S.C.R. 491; 2009 (4) SLR 497; 2009 (2) UPLBEC 1709

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Public Services (Reservation for Schedule Castes, Schedule Tribes and Other Backward Classes) Act, 1994

Summary: Service - Uttar Pradesh Public Services (Reservation for Schedule Castes, Schedule Tribes and Other Backward Classes) Act, 1994, s. 15 - Reservation - Respondents applied for the examination of Civil Judge (Jr. Division) in general category, conducted by appellant Commission - Government added the castes to which the respondents belonged as backward classes by amending the First Schedule to the Act - Respondents made representations to appellant to accept their caste certificate and extend the benefit of reservation to them - Appellant rejected the representation on the reason that respondents had applied in general category - Respondents filed writ petition before HC - HC allowed the writ - Whether respondents are entitled to claim the benefit of reservation, in view of the special provision contained in s. 15 of the Act - Held, Act was amended before the commencement of the written test, therefore, respondents became entitled to claim the benefit of reservation - Further, respondents secured the necessary certificates and gave their representations to appellant without any delay - HC allowed respondents writ petition on the basis of principles underlying s. 15 of the Act, therefore, no interference called for - Appeals disposed of.
