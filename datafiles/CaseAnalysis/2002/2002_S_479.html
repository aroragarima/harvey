Bhagwan Singh and Others v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

22 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1611; (2002) 4 SCC 85; (2002) SCC (Cr) 736; AIR 2002 SC 1621; 2002 (2) CCR 28; 2002 (2) Crimes 42; 2002 (3) CRJ 553B; 2002 CRLJ 2024; 2002 (2) JCC 727; JT 2002 (3) SC 387; 2002 (2) RCR(Criminal) 593; 2002(3) SCALE 169; 2002 (2) SLT 653; 2002 (2) Supreme 567; 2002 (1) UC 562

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure , 1973, ss. 145, 147, 378 and 379 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 ss. 96, 100, 148, 149, 302, 336, 337 and 427 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - (A) Whether u/s. 378 of CrPC, 1973 High Court cannot disturb finding of facts of Trial Court even if it found that view taken by Trial Court was not proper? - Held, HC has full powers to review evidence upon which an order of acquittal is based and generally it will not interfere with order of acquittal because by passing an order of acquittal presumption of innocence in favour of accused is reinforced - (B) Whether HC was justified in holding that accused persons, involved in occurrence, had shared common object? - A mere innocent person, in an assembly of persons or being a by-stander does not make such person a member of an unlawful assembly but where persons forming assembly are shown to be having identical interest in pursuance of which some of them come armed, others though not armed would, under normal circumstances, be deemed to be members of unlawful assembly - Further held, facts and circumstances of case unequivocally prove existence of common object of such persons forming unlawful assembly who had come on spot and attacked complainant party in consequence of which three precious lives were lost - HC was, therefore, justified in holding that accused persons, involved in occurrence, had shared common object - Y - (C) Whether statements of witness losses its weight because his name is not mentioned in FIR? - Further held, it cannot be said that names of all accused persons were not mentioned in FIR which entitled some of them to be acquitted - No requirement of law for mentioning names of all witnesses in FIR, object of which is only to set criminal law in motion - Appeal dismissed.
