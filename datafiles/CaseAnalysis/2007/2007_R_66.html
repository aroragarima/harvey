Madan Lal Kapoor v Rajiv Thapar And Others

Supreme Court of India

31 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 893; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 437; (2007) 7 SCC 623; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 826; 2007 CRLJ 4684; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 157; 2007(11) SCALE 3; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 686

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Revision Petition dismissed in default for non-prosecution - Appeal against - Held, matter relates to administration of criminal justice - A criminal matter cannot be dismissed for default and it must be decided on merits - In the impugned order, there are no reasons and the merits have not been considered at all - Matter remitted back to the High Court - Appeal allowed.
