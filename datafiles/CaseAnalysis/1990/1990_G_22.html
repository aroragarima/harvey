T. Devaki v Government of Tamil Nadu and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 March 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, Kuldip Singh, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 845; (1990) SCC (Cr) 348; (1990) 2 SCC 456; AIR 1990 SC 1086; 1990 (2) Crimes 1; 1990 CRLJ 1140; 1990 CrLR(SC) 260; JT 1990 (1) SC 444; 1990(1) SCALE 389; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 836

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Preventive Detention Act, 1950, Tamil Nadu Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Boot leggers, Drug Offenders, Goondas, Immoral Traffic Offenders and Slum Grabbers Act, 1982

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Tamil Nadu Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Bootleggers, Drug offenders, Forest-offenders, Immoral Traffic Offenders and Slum Grabbers Act, 1982, s. 3(1) - Detention - Report of sponsoring authority - Justifiability - Petitioner, wife of detenu filed instant petition challenging impugned order of detention passed by respondents u/s. 3(1) of the Act -Whether impugned order of detention was sustainable - Held, incident which was basis for detention of detenu took place in presence of detaining authority - However, Magistrate had stated that though he was present on dais but did not witness incident as he was concentrating on proceedings of Seminar and preparing replies to queries raised by Speakers at Seminar - Thus, if detaining authority was himself present and was an eye-witness to occurrence on basis of which detention order was made, it was imperative for detaining authority to have honestly and bona fide formed requisite opinion in making order of detention on basis of his own knowledge and perception instead of relying more on version of incident as placed before him by sponsoring authority - However, where detaining authority was himself present at scene of occurrence he should have relied more on his own observation and knowledge than on report of sponsoring authority - In instant case detaining authority though present at scene of occurrence did not support incident as presented to him by sponsoring authority - Hence, there was non-application of mind by detaining authority in making impugned order of detention which was quashed - Petition allowed.
