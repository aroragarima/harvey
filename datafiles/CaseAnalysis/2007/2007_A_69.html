Kunhamma @ Lakshmi Ammas Children and Another v Akkali Purushothaman and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1410; (2007) 11 SCC 181; 2007 (3) CHN 134; JT 2007 (6) SC 147; 2007 (3) KLT 599; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 818; 2007(5) SCALE 719; 2007 (4) SCJ 310

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Kerala Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act 1965

Summary: Rent Control - Kerala Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act 1965, s. 11(3) - Whether the petitioners are entitled for an eviction as prayed u/s. 11(3) of the Act? - Held, it is virtually the accepted position since long that the personal necessity envisaged under the Act would include re-possession of the demised premises by the landlord for the purposes of its demolition so as to widen the entrance to another building belonging to the landlord in the immediate vicinity - Ramniklai Pitambardas Mehta's case(AIR 1964 SC 16761964 AIR(SC) 16761964 Indlaw SC 462 ) referred - Interpretation given to s. 11(3) has not only stood the test of time but is even otherwise a correct enunciation of the law - Onus lies on the tenant to prove that he was dependent on the income derived from the business being carried on from the demised premises and that there was no other suitable building to which he could shift his business - Appeal dismissed.
