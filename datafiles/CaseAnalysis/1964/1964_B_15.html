Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 96; AIR 1964 SC 1006; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 261; 1964 (15) STC 450

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Collected

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Constitution - Madhya Bharat Sales Tax Act, ss. 3 and 5 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 226, 301 and 304 - Whether Sales Tax notifications issued u/s. 3 and 5 of Act are violative of art. 301 of Constitution, if so whether High Court can order Refund of same? - It is not unreasonable to think that Act itself contemplated sales by an importer of goods as meaning only sales by him of goods imported, also that notification did not make dealers who dealt only in home grown or home produced tobacco liable to pay tax - That by itself is sufficient to bring in vice of discrimination which is purpose of art. 304(a) to prevent - HCs have power for purpose of enforcement of fundamental rights and statutory rights to give consequential relief by ordering repayment of money realised by Govt. without authority of law - Power to give relief u/art. 226 is a discretionary power and unreasonable delays cannot be condoned - Held, this tax is, therefore, not within saving provisions of art. 304(a) and thus it contravenes of art. 301 but those guilty of delay cannot claim refund - Appeal Nos. 861-867 partly allowed.
