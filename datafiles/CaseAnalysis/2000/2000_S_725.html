Deep Chandra v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2914; (2001) 10 SCC 606; 2001 (88) FLR 508; 2001 (1) LLJ 742

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 25-F - Industrial dispute - Appellant put in more than 240 days from year 1982 to 1988 of service - Retrenched without following procedure - Labour Court granted reinstatement with back-wages - Writ petition - Whether High Court correct in interfering with award of Labour Court? - Held, HC approached matter rather strangely as it went at a tangent to consider not only whether casual worker's services can be put to an end to but if award made by Labour Court would make him permanent employee, so on and so forth - It lost sight of point in issue that is, when an employee had put in service for more than 240 days in each year for several years whether his services can be put to an end to without following procedure prescribed u/s. 25-F of Industrial Disputes Act - If there has been violation thereof such an employee will have to be reinstated in his original service on same terms and conditions in which he was working earlier - Order made by HC set aside and award of Labour Court restored - Appeal allowed.
