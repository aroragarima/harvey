Ram Lal Narang Etc. Etc v State Of Delhi (Admn.)

Supreme Court of India

10 January 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 298; (1979) 2 SCC 322; (1979) SCC (Cr) 479; AIR 1979 SC 1791; 1979 CRLJ 1346; 1979 CrLR(SC) 154; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 923

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 406, 411 and 420 - Antiquities and Art Treasures Act, 1972, s. 25 - Antique pillars stolen and illegally exported to london by appellants - Cognizance of offence already being investigated under different court - Whether right of police to further investigate after cognizance of case had been taken by Magistrate is barred? - Held, there was no provision in CrPC, 1898 which, expressly or by necessary implication, barred right of police to further investigate after cognizance of case had been taken by Magistrate - Practice, convenience and preponderance of authority, permitted repeated investigations on discovery of fresh facts - Notwithstanding that a Magistrate had taken cognizance of offence upon a police report submitted u/s. 173 of CrPC,1898, right of police to further investigate was not exhausted and police could exercise such right as often as necessary when fresh information came to light - No bar against repeated investigations - No illegality - Appeal dismissed.
