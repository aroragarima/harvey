Gram Panchayat of Village Naulakha v Ujagar Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 475; (2000) 7 SCC 543; AIR 2000 SC 3272; 2001 (43) ALR 92; JT 2000 (Supp2) SC 1; 2001 (127) PLR 287B; 2000 (4) RCR(Civil) 749; 2001 (92) RD 53; 2000(6) SCALE 681; 2000 (7) SLT 320; [2000] Supp3 S.C.R. 457; 2000 (7) Supreme 102

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Punjab Village Common Land (Regulation) Act, 1961, s. 7 - Indian Evidence Act, s. 44 - Appellant filed an application u/s. 7 of Act and an order was passed in its favour by Collector on D/- 12-07-1988 - Collector found that an earlier decree obtained by respondents against appellant for injunction on D/- 10-06- 1975 was a decree obtained by respondents in collusion with then Sarpanch and was not binding on Panchayat in present proceedings - Learned Judges of HC did not go into question of collusion or merits of case but felt bound by a decision of a Full Bench of Punjab and Haryana HC in Gram Panchayat v Jagar Ram - Full Bench judgment laid down that statutory authorities under Act could not ignore an earlier decree against Panchayat on ground of its being collusive and fraudulent, unless Panchayat had first filed an independent suit to set aside said decree or sought declaration that decree was collusive or fraudulent - Whether principle laid down by Full Bench in Jagar Ram case is correct and that earlier judgment in present case is binding on basis of principle of res judicata? - Held, view taken by Full Bench in Jagar Ram case is not correct and in fact, it runs contrary to provisions of s. 44 of Indian Evidence Act - U/s. 44, a party can, in a collateral proceeding in which fraud may be set up as a defence, show that a decree or order obtained by opposite party against him was passed by a court without jurisdiction or was obtained by fraud or collusion and it is not necessary to bring an independent suit for setting it aside - Further, earlier suit by respondent against Panchayat was only a suit for injunction and not one on title - No question of title was gone into or decided - Said decision cannot, therefore, be binding on question of title - Appeal allowed.
