M/S Larsen And Tubro Limited v The Commissioner Of Central Excise, Pune-Ii

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 451; (2007) 9 SCC 617; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 974; 2007 (5) Bom.C.R. 163; 2007 (211) E.L.T. 513; JT 2007 (6) SC 440; 2007(6) SCALE 524; 2007 (4) SCJ 207; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 1141

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Central Excise and Salt Act, 1944 - Appellant undertook a contract for construction of bridges for Konkan Railway and thus manufactured Pre Stressed Concrete Girders (PSC Girders) which was then transported to the site of construction of bridges of the Railways but was not registered with the authorities of the Central Excise - Show cause notice issued on 27.1.94 to the appellant as to why Central Excise duty should not be demanded and recovered from and as to why penalty should not be imposed and plant & machinery and manufactured goods not be confiscated - Notice withdrawn for want of approval of proper authority - Another show cause notice dated on 1.5.1996 issued on the same premise alleging suppression of fact on the part of the appellant - Whether there was suppression of facts on the part of the noticees so as to invoke extended period?- Held, notice should ordinarily be issued within the prescribed period of limitation but only in exceptional cases, the said period could be extended to 5 years; when in the original notice, such an allegation had not been made, the same could not have been made subsequently as the facts alleged to have been suppressed by the appellant were known to them; while considering imposition of penalty under Section 11A of the Act, the Commissioner has refused to impose any penalty upon the appellant on the premise that it was not guilty of any act of mala fide, thus the Revenue was not justified in invoking the extended period of limitation in the instant case - Appeal allowed.
