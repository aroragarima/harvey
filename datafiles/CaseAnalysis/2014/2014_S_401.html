Joginder Pal and others Etc. v State of Punjab and others

Supreme Court of India

23 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 371; (2014) 6 SCC 644; 2014(5) ALL MR 957; 2014 (5) AWC 5386; JT 2014 (7) SC 417; 2014(7) SCALE 438; [2014] 6 S.C.R. 383

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cancellation, Punjab Civil Services (Punishment and Appeal) Rules, 1970, Legality, Termination order, Judicial appointments

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Judicial appointments - Termination order - Legality - Services of all petitioners/officers in category of Nominated Executive Officers were terminated - Vide another order services of direct recruits were terminated - Aggrieved by dismissal order writ petitions were filed before HC - HC after examining matter, accepted aforesaid argument of respondent/State that it was not possible to segregate tainted candidates from untainted ones, leaving no option for State but to cancel entire selection process - Hence instant appeals - Whether entire process could be labelled as vitiated because of purported manipulations, forgery and fraud or, to put it otherwise, once non-tainted persons were segregated from tainted ones, would it still be justified to quash entire selection, even when non-tainted made into service because of their merit -

Held, issue of entire selection process having been vitiated would have arisen only if findings of Committee were that it was not possible to distinguish cases of tainted from non-tainted ones and there was possibility that all of them would have got benefit of wrong doings of 'S', and his accomplices - Fortunately for these appellants, it was not so as they have been found innocent - Appellants get ensconced, earning a safe place, once they were removed from category of nefarious persons - Though tainted candidates had rightly received their comeuppance, but innocent persons could not be punished with them - It was difficult to accept fallibilistic conclusion of HC - Once untainted officers, who were appointed under same environment, was allowed to continue, there was no reason to deprive this benefit of such recourse to PCS (Executive Branch) and Allied Services - HC had recorded in impugned judgment that 66% cases were found to be of persons given appointment who were tainted, which influenced entire selection process - Aforesaid percentage was worked out by taking cases of direct recruits and nominated candidates together - If figures were separately taken, out of 93 direct recruits, 76 had joined and only 10 were found to be tainted - Percentage of such tainted candidates in nominated category was much higher, i.e. 80% - It was argued that cases of direct recruits could not be taken along with those in nominated category, who influenced decision in their matter as well - This was also supportive and important fact which goes in favor of these appellants viz. non-tainted direct recruits - Discursive exercise prompt us to set aside judgment of HC in respect of these persons with direction that appellants be allowed to join duties forthwith - It was however made clear that intervening period during which they remained out of service should not count for seniority or any other benefit - However, these persons should be given benefit of service rendered by them earlier viz. from September 1999 till 22-5-2002, when they actually worked, for purpose of seniority and future promotion etc - Appeals partly allowed.
