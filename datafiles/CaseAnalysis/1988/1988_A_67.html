State of Karnataka v Ayyanahalli Bakappa and Sons

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 471; (1988) SCC (Tax) 437; (1988) 3 SCC 587; JT 1988 (2) SC 471; 1988(1) SCALE 1182; 1988 (71) STC 202; 1988 (2) UJ 250

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Karnataka Sales Tax Act 1957

Summary: Sales Tax - Karnataka Sales Tax Act 1957, s. 12 A - Assessment completed accepting return - Reassessment resulting addition of turnover of safety matches to turnover - Whether in peculiar facts of case when same turnover had once been assessed under Mysore Act by treating three depots as dealers, a second set of assessment could be made? - Sale of safety matches is liable to first point tax at prescribed rate - Held, since one set of tax has already been collected from depot holders at point of sale of assessee within State, there is no justification to allow a second set of tax to be raised - Appeal dismissed.
