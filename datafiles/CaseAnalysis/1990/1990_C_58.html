Plasmac Machine Manufacturing Company Private Limited v Collector of Central Excise, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

27 November 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Saikia, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 198; (1991) Supp1 SCC 57; AIR 1991 SC 999; 1991 CRLR 231; 1991 CrLR(SC) 231; 1991 (31) ECC 221; 1991 (32) ECR 1; 1991 (51) E.L.T. 161; JT 1990 (4) SC 549; 1990(2) SCALE 1149; 1992 (84) STC 107; [1990] Supp3 S.C.R. 384; 1991 (1) UJ 282

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 35L - Classification - Validity - Appellants were manufacturers of injection moldings machines - Tie bar nuts were stated to be components of injection molding machines (tailor-made) - Appellants submitted their classification list for the year 1981-82 under tariff item 68 along with enclosure of 19 parts - Tie bar nuts were shown against serial No. 15. - Assistant Collector of Central Excise classified and approved tie bar nuts under tariff item 52 and not under 68 -Superintendent forwarded classification list and directed appellants to file separate classification list for tie bar nuts under tariff item 52 and take out licence for same and also to furnish value and clearance of tie bar nuts for years 1980-81 and 1981-82 - Aggrieved, appellants appealed to Collector of Central Excise (Appeals) who, while allowing appeal, ordered that tie bar nuts be classified under tariff item 68 - Dept. appealed to Tribunal - Tribunal held that from evidence on record it was found that according to appellants tie bar nuts had function of fastening for machine and contention that main function of tie bar nuts was not to fasten any part but to adjust distance between two platens did not change complex of instant case, and that there was no doubt that tie bar nuts would merit classification under tariff item 52 - Department's appeal was allowed by Tribunal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by Tribunal was justified - Held, there was no dispute that tie bar nuts conform to nuts - In Indo International Industries v. Commissioner of Sales Tax 1981 Indlaw SC 186, SC observed that in interpreting items in statutes like Excise Act or Sales Tax Act, whose primary object was to raise revenue and for which purpose they classified diverse products, articles and substances, resort should be have not to scientific and technical meaning of terms or expressions used, but to their popular meaning - Meaning attached to them by those dealing in them - Fact that in instant case Tribunal held in dissent that tie bar nuts were not fastening nuts would be of no avail to appellant - Hence, tie bar nuts function of fixing platens and that of fastening were not basically different, and appellants had called goods as 'nuts' - Tribunal was correct in classifying tie bar nuts under tariff item 52 - Appeal dismissed.
