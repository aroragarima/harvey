Amit Singh v State Of Maharashtra & Anr

Supreme Court of India

08 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 525; (2011) 13 SCC 744; JT 2011 (8) SC 634; 2011 (2) OLR 740; 2011 (164) PLR 312; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 859; 2011(8) SCALE 439; [2011] 9 S.C.R. 890

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000 - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Rules, 2007 - Complaint was registered u/ss. 307, 392, 341, 34, 506 r/w s. 34 of the IPC and ss. 3, 5, 25 and 27 of the Arms Act, 1959 against accused persons including petitioner - Trial Court held all the four accused persons to be guilty of offences punishable u/ss. 396, 506, 341, 379 r/w s. 120-B of IPC and also u/s. 3 r/w s. 25(1-B) and s. 5 r/w s. 27 of the Arms Act, 1959 - On appeals, HC allowed the appeals filed by A-2 and A-3 and dismissed the appeals filed by A-1 and A-4 (petitioner) - Special Leave Petition filed thereagainst was also dismissed by SC - Instant writ petition was filed by the petitioner praying that he was a Juvenile at the time of the alleged offence and therefore, he could be tried only by the Juvenile Justice Board (Board) - Petitioner submitted that he had not completed 18 years of age as on the date of commission of the offence though he had completed 18 years as on 01.04.2001 i.e. the date of implementation of the Act - Whether benefit of juvenility should be extended to the petitioner, even after final conviction? - Petitioner had substantiated that he was a juvenile as per the Act and he could be tried only by the Board and hence the matter should be referred before the Board for trial - Held, as per the s. 7A, claim of juvenility to be raised before any court at any stage, even after final disposal of the case - In the instant case, date of birth of the petitioner was 10.05.1982 and on the date of the alleged incident which took place on 01.05.1999, his age was below 18 years, hence on the date of the incident, the petitioner was a juvenile in terms of the Act because he had not completed 18 years of age and was entitled to get the benefit of provisions u/ss. 2(l), 7A, 20 and 64 of the Act - Petitioner had already undergone 12 years in jail since then which was more than the maximum period for which a juvenile may be confined to a special home - Petitioner directed to be released from the custody - Writ petition allowed.
