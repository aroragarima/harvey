Pampakavi Rayappa Belagali v B. D. Jatti and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 October 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 423; (1970) 3 SCC 147; AIR 1971 SC 1348; [1971] 2 S.C.R. 611

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Ordinarily Resident

Summary: Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 5(c), 100(d)(iv), 116A , 123, 129, 2(1)(e) and chap.III of Part II - Representation of the People Act, 1950, ss.16 and 19(b), 16, 19(b) and 24 - Constitution, art. 173S - Election - (A) Competency for contesting assembly election - Relevaency of condition u/s. 19(b) of 1950 Act as to being ordinarily resident of Constituency (B) Scope of enquiry - under an election petition - Question whether returned candidate was ordinarily resident u/s.19(b) - (C) Charge of corrupt practice - Finding of fact reached by trial Judge - Interference ,if - (D) Elections must be freely and fairly conducted - Duty of police and Govt. officers - Held, not relevant - Question cannot be enquired except in accordance with provisions of 1950 Act - Interference with by Supreme Court (Rule of prudence) - Duty of police and Government officers pointed out

Appeal Dismissed.
