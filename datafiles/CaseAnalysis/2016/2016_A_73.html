Mangalore Electricity Supply Company Limited v Amr Power Private Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

15 September 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

L. Nageswara Rao, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 985; AIR 2016 SC 4397; 2016 (119) ALR 910; JT 2017 (2) SC 147; 2016(8) SCALE 798

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Tariff, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 23 r. 1 - PPA declaration - Termination - Validity - Appellant/distribution licensee and respondent no. 1 entered into a Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) on 02-08-2006 for supply of 24 MW of electricity - Respondent no. 1 approached Commission/respondent no. 2 for a declaration that PPA executed on 02-08-2006 was null and void - Respondent no. 2 passed an interim order directing appellant to synchronize the plant and pay for the power at the rate of Rs. 2.80 per unit - Respondent no. 1 issued a Notice of Termination of PPA on 22-07-2011 - Appellant filed petition before respondent no. 2 seeking quashing of termination notice dt. 22-07-2011 - Respondent no. 2 dismissed petition upholding Termination Notice and declared that appellant was not entitled for a direction to respondent no. 1 to act in accordance with PPA dt. 02-08-2006 - On appeal, tribunal dismissed appeal - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, art. 9.2.2 contemplates that the failure of appellant in performing its financial and other material obligations under PPA would constitute an event of default on its part. The said Article also provides for respondent no. 1 being permitted to sell electricity to third parties in the event of payment default by appellant for a continuous period of 3 months. Further, interest on delayed payments as provided for in art. 6 of PPA was not paid and a Letter of Credit was not opened. In view of the default mentioned by respondent no. 1 in the notice not being remedied within a period of 30 days, SC was of the opinion that respondent no. 2 and Tribunal were correct in upholding the termination notice dt. 22-07-2011. Both the parties to PPA were bound by the terms and were free to resort to action in accordance with the provisions contained therein. It was an admitted fact that respondent no. 1 continued to supply power to appellant on payment of Rs.2.80 per unit even after the notice of termination dt. 22-07-2011. There was a refusal of open access to respondent no. 1 in pending proceedings. There was also an interim order for maintenance of status quo by respondent no. 2. In view of the fact that the power was being generated by respondent no. 1 and had to be supplied, the continuation of the supply made by respondent no. 1 after the Termination Notice dt. 22-07-2011 cannot be taken to be a condonation of the events of default by appellant. SC do not find any error in the judgment of Tribunal confirming the Order of respondent no. 2. Appeal dismissed.
