Manik Taneja and another v State of Karnatka and another

Supreme Court of India

20 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 43; (2015) 7 SCC 423; 2015 CRLJ 1483; 2015 (2) JCC 926; JT 2015 (1) SC 237; 2015 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1029; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 765; 2015(1) SCALE 484; [2015] 1 S.C.R. 156

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Comments

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 353 and 506 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Quashing of criminal proceedings - Appellant No.1 and his wife met with an accident with an auto rickshaw - Matter was amicably settled between the injured and the appellants and no FIR was lodged - However, appellants were directed to meet Police Inspector - Being aggrieved with the manner with which they were treated, the appellants posted comments on the Facebook page, accusing Police inspector of his misbehaviour and also forwarded an email complaining about the harassment meted out to them at the hands of the Police Inspector - Police Inspector filed a complaint regarding the same and subsequently FIR was registered against the appellants for offences punishable u/ss. 353 and 506 of IPC - Petition was filed u/s. 482 of CrPC before the HC seeking to quash the FIR and the criminal proceedings initiated against them on the ground that the complaint was an afterthought - HC dismissed the petition stating that the petition was filed at a premature stage - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, there is nothing on record to show that the appellants either assaulted the respondents or used criminal force to prevent the respondent No. 2 from discharging his official duty. Taking the uncontroverted allegations, ingredients of the offence u/s. 353 of IPC are not made out. Further, from the facts and circumstances of the case, it appears that there was no intention on the part of the appellants to cause alarm in the minds of the respondent No. 2 causing obstruction in discharge of his duty. Furthermore, Facebook is a public forum meant for helping the public and the act of appellants posting a comment on the Facebook may not attract ingredients of criminal intimidation in s. 503 of IPC. Hence, it would be unjust to allow the process of the court to be continued against the appellants and consequently the order of the HC is liable to be set aside. Appeal allowed.
