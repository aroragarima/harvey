Union of India and another v Lt. Col. P. K. Choudhary and others

Supreme Court of India

15 February 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 156; (2016) 4 SCC 236; AIR 2016 SC 966; 2016 (3) AWC 3105; JT 2016 (2) SC 274; 2016 (2) MLJ 184; 2016(2) SCALE 393; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 426

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Armed Forces, Corps, Artillery, Defence Service Regulations, Armed Forces Tribunal Act, 2007, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: (A) Defence & Security Forces - Creation of additional vacancies - Application of recommendation to all streams of Army - Determination - Respondents were commissioned into various Army Service Corps (ASC) i.e. streams of Indian Army after they successfully passed out from Indian Military Academy/Officers Training Academy - Further, Govt. constituted Committee with view to explore ways and means for enhancing operational preparedness of Indian Army in its fighting capabilities especially in Combat Arms - Committee submitted its report recommending lowering age of Officers in Indian Army in order to enhance optimal combat effectiveness of Army - Hence, Committee recommended creation of additional vacancies in ranks of Colonel - Hence, Govt. released vacancies in phased manner, however, in second phase when vacancies were released problem arose as additional vacancies were sanctioned by effecting upgradation in phased manner spread over period of five years, however there was denial of pro-rata share, hence, respondents who were serving in Arms Support and Service Corps of Army challenged Govt.'s policy on 29-1-2009 on ground that same was discriminatory, arbitrary and violative of fundamental rights guaranteed to them - Tribunal vide impugned order allowed application filed by respondent and circular dt. 20-1-2009 issued by Govt. was quashed with direction that Govt. shall create supernumerary posts so that additional vacancies so created were allocated to all three streams on pro rata basis - Whether Committee recommend lowering of age profile and consequent creation of additional vacancies for all three streams viz. Arms, Arms Support and Services.

Held, report of Committee shows that it emphasized need for bringing down age profile of Unit Commanders in Operational Units only. Committee recognized Armoured Corps, Infantry, Mechanised Infantry, Artillery, AD, Engineers and signals as operational formations leaving out Army Service Corps (ASC), Army Ordnance Corps (AOC), Electronical and Mechanical Engineers (EME) and other Minor Corps. SC held that there was neither any recommendation regarding reduction in age profile of Unit Commanders in ASC, AOC and EME nor was there any recommendation for creation of additional vacancies to benefit officers serving in those formations. Hence, argument that Committee had recommended creation of 1484 vacancies for benefit of Officers serving in all formations is, therefore, without any basis and is accordingly rejected. Hence, ASC, AOC, EME Officers did not benefit from creation of additional vacancies, there was neither any violation of recommendations made by Committee nor was distribution of additional vacancies discriminatory as alleged. Appeals partly allowed.

(B) Defence & Security Forces - Recommendation for additional vacancies - Alleged Illegality - Determination - Whether recommendations made by Committee regarding need for creation of additional vacancies in second phase and their allocation on 'Command Exit Model' is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, because allocation of vacancies in first phase was made by Army Headquarters ignoring recommendations of Committee and Govt. decision cannot possibly result in reversal of Govt. decision nor can it negate Command Exit Model. Further, because earlier allocation was not reversed as officers had picked up their ranks does not affect binding nature of Govt. decision that allocation should be on 'Command Exit Model'. Hence, adjustment/set off of vacancies so allocated against entitlement of arms and arms support in second tranche does not appear to be justified. SC held that if Army Headquarters committed mistake in allocating vacancies on pro rata basis contrary to recommendations and decision of Govt., any such error cannot adversely affect officers serving in arms and arms support who may have been entitled to higher number of vacancies in second tranche but who were deprived of such allocation on account of error in previous allocation made on pro rata basis. Hence, SC held that recommendations of Committee regarding allocation of additional vacancies on 'Command Exit Model' basis had been accepted by Govt. and that allocation of first tranche of 750 vacancies by Army Headquarters on pro rata basis contrary to Govt. decision and recommendations of Committee did not affect validity of decision nor did it amount to reversal of said decision or its dilution in any manner. Appeals partly allowed.

(C) Defence & Security Forces - Adjustment to excess allocation - Alleged illegality in allocation - Determination - Respondent contended that allocation of 734 vacancies comprising second tranche should have been made without any adjustment based on earlier pro rata allotment, meaning thereby that deficit proportionate to number that has been set off/adjusted should be made up by fresh creation - Whether there was any illegality, irregularity or unfairness in matter of allocation of vacancies to Arms Support on 'Command Exit Model' principle.

Held, allocation of 734 vacancies, comprising second tranche, when made on standalone basis without any adjustment of excess allocated in first tranche is only right method for allocation. Further, excess allocated in first tranche, against which officers who may not have otherwise picked up higher rank were promoted, cannot possibly deny rightful due to those who would be entitled to claim promotion against vacancies in second tranche. Hence, second tranche should be allocated on standalone basis. Hence, SC held that to extent of deficit of 141 vacancies in cadres of Colonel to Arms Support (Artillery, AAD, Engineers and signals), unfair distribution of vacancies from out of second tranche were released by Govt. Further, SC held that additional 141 vacancies which ought to have been allocated to Arms Support in year 2009 were unfairly denied to them. Hence, additional creation be deemed to have been available for being filled-up as in year 2009 but to be actually filled-up in 5 years between 2009-2014. Further, those who pick-up next rank against said vacancies shall have benefit of retrospective seniority as is practice in Army but such seniority on appointment shall not entitle them to benefit of higher pay-scale or arrears against post to which they are promoted. Appeals partly allowed.

(D) Defence & Security Forces - Officers serving in different stream forming singlr cadre - Benefit claimed - Justification - Whether Officers serving in Arms, Arms Support and Services constitute single cadre?

Held, transferability which is one of essential attributes of posts comprising single cadre is absent in case of service officers on one hand and those serving in Arms and Arms Support on other. Hence, SC held that officers serving in Service stream of Army do not constitute single cadre with officers serving in Arms and Arms Support, even though they are drawing same salary, holding same rank and serving same employer with similar service benefits. Appeals partly allowed.

(E) Defence & Security Forces - Alleged Conduct of Govt. to deliver benefit - Determination - Whether legitimate expectation arise in factual situation of present case.

Held, there is no real basis for respondents to argue that Govt. had either by representation or by any sustained course of conduct created impression in minds of respondents that any additional vacancies created to lower age profile of commanding officers serving in Combat Arms or Combat Arms Support shall also benefit those serving in Service Streams of Army. There is no factual basis laid by respondents in pleadings before Tribunal to suggest that any such impression was gathered by officers serving in Service Streams. Further, Regulations clearly provide for grant of retrospective effect to promotions only with view to restore seniority. Further, plea of legitimate expectation doe not prevail over policy introduced by Govt. which does not suffer from any perversity, unfairness or unreasonableness or which does not violate any fundamental or other enforceable rights vested in respondents. Hence, impugned order passed by Tribunal is set aside and appellants are directed to create 141 additional posts of Colonel to be allocated to 'Combat Support' stream for being utilized by appointing officers who are eligible for promotions against same as in year 2009 over period of 5 years till 2014. Appeals partly allowed.

Ratio - In case of service matters, policy issued by authority cannot be quashed when same is not arbitrary, discriminatory and violative of fundamental right of citizen.
