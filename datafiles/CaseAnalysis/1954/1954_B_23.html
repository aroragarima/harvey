Â State of Bombay v Bombay Education Society and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 May 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Ghulam Hasan, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 129; AIR 1954 SC 561; 1954 (56) BomLR 1211; 1954 (2) MLJ(SC) 359; 1954 SCJ 678; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 568

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Indian Companies Act, 1913, Prohibition Act

Summary: Education - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 29(2), 30(1) - Indian Companies Act, 1913 - Prohibition Act, arts. 29(2), 337, O. 2054(a) and 29(2) - Circular Order - Admission to English Medium - Denial of Admission - Infringement of Constitutional Right - Educational Inspector of Anglo-Indian Schools sent a circular to Headmaster intimating that Headmaster should not to make any admission for the academic year, for students, who did not belong to Anglo-Indian Community or non-Asiatic descent - Respondents members and Directors of Society/Anglo-Indian Community presented Civil Application before HC u/art. 226 of Constitution praying for issue of mandamus restraining appellant/State, and others from enforcing said order and to allow respondent to admit to non-Anglo-Indian citizens or citizens of Asiatic descent of any standard in the said school, which was accepted - Hence instant appeal - Whether right of students who were not Anglo-Indians/Asiatic decent were infringed u/arts. 29(1) and 30(1) of Constitution and Whether in consequence of order, any change was necessary in the existing procedure for equitable distribution of total grant among individual Anglo-Indian Schools - Held, Powers of appellant/State could not be lightly questioned certainly, so far as their exercise was not inconsistent with or contrary to fundamental rights guaranteed to citizens - Prohibition against admission of pupils, who were not Anglo-Indians or citizens of non-Asiatic descent into Anglo-Indian Schools would compel authorities of such Schools to commit a breach of their Constitutional obligation u/art. 337 of Constitution and thereby forfeit their constitutional right to special grants - Order could not be regarded as unconstitutional - Appeals disposed of.
