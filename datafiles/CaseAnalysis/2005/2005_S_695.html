K.S. Nagamuthu v State of Tamil Nadu and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 November 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 748; (2006) 4 SCC 792; AIR 2006 SC 374; JT 2005 (10) SC 216; 2006 (1) RCR(Criminal) 380; 2006 (72) RLT 207; 2005(9) SCALE 534; 2006 (2) SCJ 88

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: [A.] Exchange Control & FDI - Criminal - Conservation Of Foreign Exchange And Prevention Of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, s. 3(1)(i) - Order of detention - Challenge to detention order on ground that letter of appellant retracting alleged voluntary statement made before Customs officials was not placed before detaining authority who passed impugned order - Held, there is no reason why it should not have been placed before detaining authority for its consideration - It has not been disputed that letter of retraction contained relevant material, which ought to have been considered by detaining authority before passing an order of detention - Since relevant material was withheld from detaining authority, order of detention must be struck down as being illegal - Petition allowed.
