Kubic Dariusz v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

18 January 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Saikia, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 992; (1990) SCC (Cr) 227; (1990) 1 SCC 568; AIR 1990 SC 605; 1990 (1) Crimes 397; 1990 CRLJ 796; 1990 CRLR 178; 1990 CrLR(SC) 178; 1990 (48) E.L.T. 171; JT 1990 (1) SC 38; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 98

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Foreign National

Summary: Criminal - Detention - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, s. 3(1) - Petitioner was apprehended with gold in his possession - Detention order passed to prevent the detenu from smuggling goods - Whether the detention of the petitioner was justified - Held, no - Detention order and other necessary documents were in English which was not understood by detenu - Service of the grounds of detention on the detenu was a very precious constitutional right - If the detention order and grounds were not understood by detune, same had to be explained to the detenue - Continued detention rendered illegal by non-consideration of his representation by the appropriate Govt. - Detention vitiated - Petition allowed.
