Murari Mohan Deb v Secretary To The Government of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, V. Balakrishna Eradi, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 276; (1985) 3 SCC 120; (1985) SCC (L&S) 588; AIR 1985 SC 931; 1985 LabIC 957; 1985 (2) LLJ 176; 1985 (2) LLN 257; 1985(1) SCALE 662; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 639; 1985 (2) SLJ 33; 1985 (2) SLR 170; 1985 UJ 1007

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Constitution - Administrative - Service - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 - Whether penalty of compulsory retirement was imposed upon appellant in violation of principles of natural justice? - Held, an oral request to correct description of first respondent would have satisfied procedural requirement and appellant should not have been made to suffer for a minor and mere technical error in drafting - Order of compulsory retirement according to relevant service rules cannot be styled as imposing a penalty and art. 311(2) will not be attracted - Enquiry appears to have been held in violation of principles of natural justice and is vitiated - If enquiry was illegal, any punishment imposed as a result of enquiry must fail - Order of compulsory retirement is bad for more than one reason and is set aside - Payment of back wages and pension ordered - Appeal allowed.
