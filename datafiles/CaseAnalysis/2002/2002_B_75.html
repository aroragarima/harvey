United India Insurance Company Limited v Bhushan Sachdeva and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2146; (2002) 2 SCC 265; (2002) SCC (Cr) 320; 2002 (1) ACC 258; AIR 2002 SC 662; 2002 (47) ALR 185; 2002 (1) AWC 600; [2002] 110 Comp Cas 15; 2002 (1) CRC 387; 2002 (95) DLT 451; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 393; JT 2002 (1) SC 208; 2002 (1) MLJ(SC) 157; 2002 (130) PLR 436; 2002(1) SCALE 223; 2002 (1) SCJ 389; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 352; 2002 (1) SLT 257; 2002 (1) Supreme 177; 2002 (1) UC 481

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Constitution of India, 1950 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 173 - What is remedy of insurance Co. if it is aggrieved of award passed by a Motor Accident Claims Tribunal? - Held, so long as insured has not challenged award passed against him and so long as liability would only fall on insurance Co., it is inequitable to deny a remedy of appeal to insurance Co. - Stand of appellant that it cannot file an appeal at all before High Court u/s. 173 of Act is based on an erroneous assumption - Hub of s. 173 is that right of appeal is conferred on 'any person aggrieved by an award of a Claims tribunal' - It is open to insurance Co. to invoke right u/s. 173 of Act as insured had failed to appeal against award passed against him - Appeal disposed of.
