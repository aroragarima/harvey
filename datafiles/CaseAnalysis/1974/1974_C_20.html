Chawla and Another v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 449; (1974) 4 SCC 579; (1974) SCC (Cr) 615; AIR 1974 SC 1039; 1974 CRLJ 791; 1974 CRLR 280; [1974] 3 S.C.R. 340

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Death penalty - Delayed proceedings - Accused were convicted and sentenced to death for causing murder of deceased and on appeal, HC commuted death sentence of one accused but confirmed death sentences of appellants - Hence, instant appeal against death sentence inflicted on appellants - Whether death sentence awarded to appellants could be upheld.

Held, even though version of accused was not sufficient to make a case of private defence, yet, coupled with the Kanungo's report, and the surrounding circumstances, it could be that tragedy was probably precipitated by the deceased's insistence on cultivation and possession of the disputed land including those fields of which actual possession had been duly delivered by the consolidation authorities to the accused. Further, death penalty has been brooding over the heads of appellants for long period and the same is mitigating factor. Hence, even though conviction of appellants was upheld, yet, death sentences awarded to appellants was commuted to that of imprisonment for life. State of Bihar v. Pashupati Singh and another, 1973 INDLAW SC 192, followed. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - If there has been a long interval between the date of the offence and the consideration of appeal by SC, then capital sentence for the commission of offence u/s. 302 of IPC for which accused has undergone a long period of mental agony would not be correct.
