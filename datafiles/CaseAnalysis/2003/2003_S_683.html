J.P. Bansal v State of Rajasthan and another

Supreme Court of India

12 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 264; (2003) 5 SCC 134; (2003) SCC (L&S) 605; AIR 2003 SC 1405; 2007 (4) CompLJ 28; JT 2003 (3) SC 169; 2003 (2) LLN 405; 2003(3) SCALE 154; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 933; 2003 (2) SLJ 252; 2003 (3) SLR 50; 2003 (3) SLT 94; 2003 (2) Supreme 856

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Service

Keywords: Foreign Allowance

Summary: Service - payment of compensation - Rajasthan Taxes and Tribunal Act, 1995 - S. 3(5) - prayer for issuing a writ of mandamus to the State of Rajasthan to pay compensation on cessation of functioning as Chairman of the abolished Rajasthan Taxation and Tribunal - turned down by learned Single Judge and Division Bench of the Rajasthan High Court - appeal - Held, no specific provision for payment of compensation in the present case - plea of legitimate expectation - claims on legitimate expectation required reliance on representation and resultant detriment in the same way as claims based on promissory estoppel. - The principles of legitimate expectation have no application to the facts of the present case.
