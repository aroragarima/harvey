Â K. M. S. Lakshmanier and Sons v Commissioner of Income Tax and Excess Profits Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

23 January 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Patanjali Sastri, M. Patanjali Sastri, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, N. Chandrasekhara Aiyar, Vivian Bose, Ghulam Hasan

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 133; AIR 1953 SC 145; [1953] 23 ITR 202; 1953 (1) MLJ(SC) 609; [1953] S.C.R. 1057

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Fixed Deposit, Forward Contracts, Capital Receipt, Excess Profits Tax

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940, r. 2A - Borrowed money - Character of sums received - Appellants were merchants and were sole selling agents for yarn manufactured by Madura Mills Co.- During chargeable accounting period appellants received from their customers sums amounting to Rs. 7,69,569 and they claimed before Excess Profits Tax Officer that said sum was to be treated as 'borrowed money' u/r. 2A of the Rules in the Act and excess profits tax was payable by them for chargeable accounting period - Excess Profits Tax Officer rejected claim and assessed appellants to excess profits tax of Rs. 25,404 - Appeals to Appellate Assistant Commissioner and Tribunal failed - Hence, appellants applied to Tribunal for reference of question of law - Division Bench of HC decided question against appellants - Hence, instant appeal - Whether moneys deposited by customers with appellants as security deposits were 'borrowed money' within meaning of r. 2A of the Act throughout chargeable accounting, or during any part of that chargeable accounting period - Held, sums in question were received by appellants under three different arrangements with their customers evidenced by circulars issued to them - Amounts received under one or more agreements were borrowed moneys and computation of average capital was to be considered - Lower courts had not differentiated at any stage so far about character of sums received, as it held that sums received under arrangements was not borrowed money within the meaning of r. 2-A of the Act - Different character of sums received under agreements which were in force during chargeable accounting period was to be considered - Form of question referred to court clearly recognised and admitted distinction between parts of chargeable accounting period - Order of lower court was set aside and question referred was answered in affirmative with reference to last part of chargeable accounting period, and answered in negative with reference to rest of that period - Hence, order was set aside - Order accordingly.
