K. Bhagirathi G. Shenoy and Others v K. P. Ballakuraya and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1564; (1999) 4 SCC 135; AIR 1999 SC 2143; JT 1999 (2) SC 563; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 438

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Kerala Land Reforms Act, 1964, ss. 3(1)(ii) and 72 B - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 106 - Provisions relating to tenancy - Exemption from application of - Lease of building along with land surrounding it - Lessee claiming right, title thereof under s. 72-B - Lessor was having building with appurtenant land and lessee needed a house to live in at time of execution of lease - Lessee was prohibited to use land surrounding building to generate profit under lease deed - Held, said facts showing that building was prime consideration for lease - Large area of surrounding land under such circumstances alone cannot be determinative factor - Consequentially lessee cannot claim any right.
