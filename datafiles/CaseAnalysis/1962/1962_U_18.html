Bootamal v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

27 March 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 428; AIR 1962 SC 1716; 1962 (64) PLR 1043; [1963] 1 S.C.R. 70

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 art. 31 - Suit for compensation was filed for compensation on account of non-delivery - Time of notice correspondence relevant - Whether the suit of the appellant was barred by limitation? - High Court held that the goods ought to have been delivered at the most within five or six months of the booking and therefore the suit was 'barred by limitation - Held, that the goods ought to have been delivered even taking into account the extraordinary circumstances prevailing on account of partition within five or six months of the date on which they were sent - The stating point of the limitation remained unaffected by the conduct of the parties or by correspondence between them.
