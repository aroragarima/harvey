Â Rt. Rev. Magr. Mark Netto v Govt. Of Kerala & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, Y.V. Chandrachud, R.S. Sarkaria, A.P. Sen, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 496; (1979) 1 SCC 23; AIR 1979 SC 83; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 609

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Education - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 30 (1) and 136 - Kerala Education Rules (1959), Chap VI r. 12 (iii) - Constitutional validity of r. 12(iii) chap VI challenged u/art. 30(1) of Constitution - Dominant object of Rule does not seem to be for sake of discipline or morality - Any apprehension of deterioration in moral standards of students if co-education is permitted in secondary schools does not seem to be main basis of this Rule, although it may be a secondary one - Self imposed restriction by management in vogue for a number of years restricting admission for boys only, per se, is wholly insufficient to cast a legal ban on them not to admit girls ban provided in Rule 12 (iii) as already adverted to is of a very limited character and for a limited purpose - Held, Christian community in locality, for various reasons which are not necessary to be alluded to here, wanted girls also to receive their education in this school and specially of their community - They did not think it in their interest to send them to Mulsim girls school which is an educational institution run by other minority community - In that view of matter Rule in question in its wide amplitude sanctioning withholding of permission for admission of girls students in boys minority school is violative of art. 30 - If so widely interpreted it crosses barrier of regulatory measures and comes in region of interference with administration of institution, a right which is guaranteed to minority u/art. 30 - Rule, therefore, must be interpreted narrowly and is held to be inapplicable to a minority educational institution in a situation of kind in present case - It is not necessary or advisable to strike down Rule as a whole but to restrict its operation and make it inapplicable to a minority educational institution in a situation like one which arose in this case - Appeal allowed.
