Ruston and Hornsby (I) Limited v T. B. Kadam

Supreme Court of India

24 July 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Alagiriswami, P.K. Goswami, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 34; (1976) 3 SCC 71; (1976) SCC (L&S) 381; AIR 1975 SC 2025; 1975 (31) FLR 173; 1975 LabIC 1455; 1975 (2) LLJ 352; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 119

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, sch. 2, item 3, ss. 2A, 10 and 15 - Service - Domestic inquiry - Dismissal - (A) Charge of duspected dishonesty in connection with company's property - (B) Reference of dispute u/s.10 r/ w s. 2A of the Act - Facts giving rise to dispute falling u/s. 2A arising before section came into force - Validity of reference - (C) Reference, of dispute to labour Court - Powers of such Court - Scope - Held, on basis of facts held that charge was one of attempt to steal company's property and workman being watchman, charge was serious one and, if held proved, he deserved dismissal - Would not make the reference invalid - Only power which the Labour Court has is to consider whether the enquiry was proper and if it was so no further question arises - Appeal Allowed.
