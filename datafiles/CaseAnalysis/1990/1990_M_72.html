Messrs Jain Exports Private Limited And Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 January 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 668; (1993) Supp3 SCC 87; 1991 (32) ECR 338; 1990 (47) E.L.T. 213; 1991(1) SCALE 51

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, ss. 125 and 131(3) - Question relating to determination of quantum of redemption fine - Held, Tribunal committed apparent error in refusing to take into account extenuating circumstances leading to import of disputed goods for purposes of determining quantum of redemption fine - While determining question of quantum of redemption fine it is essential to consider facts and circumstances relevant to bona fide conduct of importer in importing goods - Questions of bona fide import is relevant for determining quantum of redemption fine - Remand matter to Appellate Tribunal to determine question of quantum of redemption fine - Petition allowed.
