State of Madhya Pradesh and Another v Kumari Nivedita Jain and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

Amarendra Nath Sen, Y.V. Chandrachud, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 392; (1981) 4 SCC 296; AIR 1981 SC 2045; 1981(3) SCALE 1512; [1982] 1 S.C.R. 759

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Medical Council Act, 1956 - Admission - Eligibility - Violation - Relaxation - State of M made rules for admission to Medical Colleges in exercise of its executive power and not by statutory - According to rules, State reserved seats for different categories - State passed order relaxing conditions relating to minimum qualifying marks for two categories - Respondent obtained necessary qualifying marks, could not secure her admission, in general category - Respondent alleged that she would secure admission if seats in reserved categories had thrown open to candidates in general category on account of failure on part of candidates belonging to reserved category - Respondent contended that order of Govt. contravenes regn. II of Medical Council and would hit s. 19 of 1956 Act, and it destroy equality and violate arts. 14 and 15 of Constitution - HC allowed petition of respondent and struck down order of State - Hence instant appeal - Whether order passed by State was in violation of regn. II or of art. 15 of Constitution - Held, all candidates qualified for admission to medical courses on basis of regn. I of Council could not be admitted into medical college for dearth of seats - It was clear that regn. II recommending process of selection was outside authority of Council u/s. 33 of Act was directory and did not had mandatory force - Order could be clearly supported u/art. 15(4) of Constitution as relaxation did not offend art. 14 of Constitution - No relaxation of condition regarding eligibility for admission into medical colleges - Hence, order was not liable to be struck down as being violation of regn. II or of art. 15 of Constitution - Authorities directed to treat respondent as regular student of college as she had admitted and to allow her to continue her studies - Appeals disposed of.
