Vijay Lakshmi v Punjab University and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 791; (2003) 8 SCC 440; AIR 2003 SC 3331; 2003 (53) ALR 454; 2004 (1) ESC 8; 2003 (4) G.L.R. 3759; JT 2003 (8) SC 259; 2003 LabIC 3485; 2004 (137) PLR 124; 2003(8) SCALE 65; 2003 (1) SCW 4731; 2004 (1) SLJ 387; 2004 (1) SLR 620; 2003 (6) SLT 318; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 1034; 2003 (7) Supreme 256

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Education; Women & Children

Keywords: Employment Under The State

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950 - arts. 14, 15, 16 - Punjab University Calendar Volume-III - rr. 5, 8, 10 - Whether preference given to a woman for being appointed as a principal of the government college for girls is held to be violative of arts. 14, 15 and 16, Constitution of India, 1950 - Held, interpreting arts 14 to 16, rr. 5 to 8, Punjab University Calendar Volume-III, providing for appointment of lady principal in women's college or a lady teacher therein cannot be held to be violative either of art. 14 or art. 16 of the Constitution, because classification is reasonable and it has a nexus with the object sought to be achieved - Further held, the State Government is empowered to make such special provisions under art. 15(3) of the Constitution. This power is not restricted in any manner by art. 16.
