(1) Chikkarangaiah and Others; (2) State of v (1) State of Karnataka; (2) Chikkarangaiah and

Supreme Court of India

02 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1085; (2009) 17 SCC 497; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 3060; 2009 CRLJ 4667; 2009 (4) JCC 2644; 2009 (6) KarLJ 369; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 410; 2009(12) SCALE 146; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 1182

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Police Record, Serologist Report

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.149, 302 and 326 - Grievous hurt - Conviction - Challenged - Land dispute between deceased and his brother (PW-1) on one side and A-5 and A-1, A-2 and A-11 on other side - Prosecution case, that on day of incident, accused persons came armed with clubs and chopper and brutally assaulted the deceased indiscriminately - On same day, A-1, A-21, A-9, A-3, A-12, A-11, A-13 and A-10 armed with clubs chased PW-6 and assaulted him with a view to prevent him from informing about the incident with respect to the assault on deceased - Deceased and PW-6 were shifted to Hospital - Case registered against accused persons - Trial court acquitted all the accused - On appeal, HC partly allowed appeal filed by State by convicting accused persons for offence u/s.326 r/w s.149 of IPC while maintaining order of acquittal u/s.302 IPC passed by Trial Court - Hence, present appeals - (A) Whether findings with regard to order of acquittal by Courts below suffer from any infirmity? - Held, no reason to interfere with concurring order of acquittal recorded by Trial Court as affirmed by HC for offence u/s. 302 IPC - (B) Conviction of accused persons u/ss.326 r/w s.149 IPC - Whether proper? - Trial Court gave benefit of doubt to accused, since PW-6 did not give names of each of accused individually before Doctors (PW-13 and PW-14) - Held, Trial Court failed to consider fact that it was not possible for PW-6 to give name of each of accused individually since he had clearly mentioned A-3, A-13 and 'others' - Reasoning of Trial Court that implication of accused was an afterthought does not stand to reason, since PW-6 implicated accused persons, who assaulted him, at earliest opportunity when he made a statement to Police on very next day and testified in Court to that effect - No contradictions or omissions elicited from PW-6 in his evidence by the defence with respect to the identification of the accused persons - No reason to disbelieve and discard the evidence of PW-6 who though did not speak about the main incident with respect to the assault on the deceased but clearly stated that while he was going to the bus stop he was assaulted by accused persons - Further held, manner in which all accused chased and attacked PW-6 would clearly make out a case for an offence u/s. 326 r/w s.149 IPC - Common object of all accused was to cause grievous injury to PW-6 - Conviction u/s.326 r/w s.149 upheld - Appeals disposed of.
