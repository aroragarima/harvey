Â Modern Dental College and Research Centre and others v State of Madhya Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Anil R. Dave, R.K. Agrawal, Adarsh Kumar Goel, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 389; (2016) 7 SCC 353; AIR 2016 SC 2601; JT 2016 (4) SC 476; 2016 (4) MLJ 52; 2016(4) SCALE 478; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 579; 2016 (3) UPLBEC 2021

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Constitution of India, Backward Classes, Limitation, Special Provision, Fixation, Medical College, State Legislature, Expenditure, Delhi School Education Act, 1973, Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, Fairness, Reasonable Restriction, Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Government of India Act, 1935, Medical Council Of India, All India Council For Technical Education Act, 1987, Reasonableness, Right To Information Act, 2005, Exploitation, Minorities, Constitution (Forty-Second Amendment) Act, 1976, Central Universities Act, 2009, Constitution (Ninety Third Amendment) Act, 2005, Madhya Pradesh Niji Vyavsayik Shikshan Sanstha (Pravesh Ka Viniyaman Avam Shulk Ka Nirdharan) Adhiniyam, 2007, Jawaharlal Nehru Krishi Vishwavidyalaya Adhiniyam, Constitution of India, 1950, Maulana Azad National Urdu University Act, 1996, Baba Saheb Bhimrao Ambedkar University Act, 1994, Indira Gandhi National Tribal University Act, 2007, Rajiv Gandhi Prodyogiki Vishwavidyalaya Adhiniyam, Rashtriya Vidhi Sansthan Vishwavidyalaya Act, 1997, Madhya Pradesh Private Medical and Dental Post Graduate Courses Entrance Examination Rules, 2009, Admissions Rules, 2008

Summary: Education - Constitution - Niji Vyavasayik Shikshan Sanstha (Pravesh Ka Viniyamam Avam Shulk Ka Nirdharan) Adhiniyam, 2007 - Admission Rules, 2008 - Madhya Pradesh Private Medical and Dental Post Graduate Courses Entrance Examination Rules, 2009 - Fundamental Rights - Unaided colleges - Petitioners, which are unaided private medical and dental colleges, filed writ petitions before HC challenging validity of the Act, and 2008 Rules and 2009 Rules, in respect provisions relating to admission, fixation of fee, reservation and eligibility for admission - However, vide impugned judgment, HC upheld validity of impugned legislation and hence, instant appeals - Whether impugned legislation is violative of fundamental right of petitioners.

Held, in exercise of their 'right to occupation', private institutions could not transgress rights of students and the Act does not give unbridled power to authority to determine the fee. Determination of fee has to be based on factors stipulated in s.9 of the Act and though fundamental rights of colleges to run their administration includes fixation of fee, yet, such right has to be balanced with the rights of students, so that they are not subjected to exploitation in form of profiteering. Hence, State had legislative competence to enact impugned legislation to hold common entrance test for admission to professional educational institutions and to determine fee and HC rightly upheld validity of impugned legislation. Further, regulations sought to be imposed by impugned legislation on admission by common entrance test conducted by State and determination of fee are in compliance of directions and observations made by SC in T.M.A. Pai, Islamic Academy of Education and P.A.Inamdar cases. Further, regulations on admission process are necessary in larger public interest and welfare of student community to ensure fairness and transparency in admission and to promote merit and excellence. Further, regulation on fixation of fee is to protect the rights of the students in having access to higher education without being subjected to exploitation in form of profiteering. Hence, impugned judgment of HC upholding validity of impugned legislation is upheld. Petitions disposed of.
