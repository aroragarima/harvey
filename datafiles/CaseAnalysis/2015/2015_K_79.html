Pepsico India Holding Private Limited v Krishna Kant Pandey

Supreme Court of India

06 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 5; (2015) 4 SCC 270; 2015(5) ALL MR 434; 2015 (2) AWC 2061; 2015 (144) FLR 607; JT 2015 (1) SC 36; 2015 (1) LLJ 559; 2015(1) SCALE 77; [2015] 1 S.C.R. 288; 2015 (2) SLR 86; 2015 (1) UPLBEC 1

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Rules, 1957

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2 - Jurisdiction of HC - Interference in order of Tribunal - Respondent, while working on the post of Fleet Executive was terminated from service by giving one month's salary, in lieu of the notice - Reference was made - Industrial Tribunal dismissed the reference stating that at the relevant time the respondent was working as a Fleet Executive which was supervisory in nature and did not fall within the definition of 'labour' as defined u/s. 2(z) of the Act - On appeal, HC quashed order of the Tribunal and directed it to proceed with the adjudication of the respondent's case on merit - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the HC committed grave error in holding that although he is not covered under the definition of workman as defined u/s. 2(z) of the Act he shall be classified as a workman. The HC further exceeded its jurisdiction in advising the Govt. to make an amendment in S. 2(z) of the Act and to exclude some clauses. The order passed by the HC cannot be sustained in law and is set aside. The order passed by the Tribunal restored. Appeal allowed.
