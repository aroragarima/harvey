Gram Panchayat Kakran v Additional Director of Consolidation and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 October 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1315; (1997) 8 SCC 484; JT 1997 (8) SC 430; 1997(6) SCALE 382; [1997] Supp4 S.C.R. 442; 1997 (8) Supreme 535

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - East Punjab Holdings (Consolidation and Prevention of Fragmentation) Act, 1948, ss. 19, 20, 21 and 21(2) - East Punjab Holdings (Consolidation and Prevention of Fragmentation) Rules, 1949, rr. 16(ii) and 18 - Whether an application u/s. 42 for reopening repartition after lapse of limitation period maintainable? - Party aggrieved by scheme or by repartition cannot make an application u/s. 42 after an unreasonably long lapse of time - Even where no period of limitation is prescribed, party aggrieved is required to move appropriate authority for relief within a reasonable time - Application made after period of limitation must satisfy authorities that there was sufficient cause for not making application within such period - Held, there is no satisfactory explanation for inordinate delay of 40 years in making application u/s. 42 - Application cannot be accepted - Appeal allowed.
