U.P. State Brassware Corporation Limited and v Udai Narain Pandey

Supreme Court of India

08 December 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 806; AIR 2006 SC 586; 2006 (1) AWC 667; 2006 (1) CLR 39; JT 2005 (10) SC 344; 2005(10) SCALE 61; 2006 (1) SCJ 459; 2006 (1) SLR 11; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 609; 2006 (1) UPLBEC 356

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Industrial Disputes (Amendment And Miscellaneous Provisions) Act, 1956, Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1984

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2 (oo)(bb), 2(z), 25F, 6-N, 6O - Industrial Disputes (Amendment and Miscellaneous Provisions) Act, 1956, s. 31(1) - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 141 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 7, r. 7 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 106 - Validity of direction to pay back wages on a declaration that order of termination was invalid - Termination of service of Respondent by appellant - Appointment of Respondent on contractual basis for fixed tenure - Labour court holding that Respondent worked for more than 240 days, directed payment of entire back wages - Writ petition - Dismissed - Contention that appointment of respondent being a contractual one for fixed period, s. 6-N would have no application - Establishment of Appellant wherein Respondent could be directed to be reinstated had been sold on 26-03-1993 - Decision to close down establishment by State of UP like other public sector organizations had been taken as far back on 17-11-1990 wherefor a GO had been issued- Thus s. 6o would have applied in terms whereof compensation would be payable in same manner as if he was retrenched u/s. 6 - No plea raised by Respondent in his written statement that he was not gainfully employed in said period - Back wages paid to Respondent confined to 25% of total back wages - Impugned judgment of Labour court and HC set aside.
