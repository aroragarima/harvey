Scooters India and Others v Vijai E. V. Eldred

Supreme Court of India

03 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2114; (1998) 6 SCC 549; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1611; 1999 (2) CLR 231; 1999 (81) FLR 87; JT 1998 (8) SC 204; 1999 (3) LLJ 138

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Respondent remained absent from duty without any leave or intimation - Absence unauthorised - Appellant treated this absence for more than 10 days as termination of contract of service as he was deemed to have left service of appellant on his own accord and his name was accordingly struck off - High Court granted relief of reinstatement with three years' back wages in addition to holding that cl. 9.3.12 of standing orders as invalid - Held, there was no occasion for HC to entertain writ petition directly for adjudication of an industrial dispute involving termination of disputed questions of fact for which remedy under industrial laws was available to workman - Writ petition should have been dismissed on ground of laches - Appeal allowed.
