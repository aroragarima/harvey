Harbeer Singh and another v Sheeshpal and others

Supreme Court of India

20 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 784; AIR 2016 SC 4958; 2017 CRLJ 169; JT 2016 (10) SC 426; 2016(10) SCALE 211; [2016] 8 S.C.R. 380

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Conspiracy, Vehicle, Omission, Reasonable Doubt, Interfere, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 161, 162 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 149, 302 - Murder - Acquittal - Sustainability - Respondent/accused along with co-accused with an intention to kill deceased dragged him upto the Dhaba from a jeep causing death of deceased - Charge sheet was filed against accused u/ss. 149, 302, 120B IPC - Trial Court convicted accused and sentenced them to life imprisonment u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 IPC - On appeal, HC allowed appeals and set aside the judgment and order passed by Trial Court and acquitted all the accused persons - Hence instant appeals.

Held, HC noted that there were chance witnesses whose statements should not have been relied upon. The evidence of eye witnesses was not truthful, reliable and trustworthy and cannot form the basis of conviction. HC concluded that those witnesses were interested witnesses and their testimony were not corroborated by independent witnesses. SC was fully in agreement with the reasons recorded by HC in coming to that conclusion. Their presence at the scene of occurrence at the time of the incident was highly unnatural as also their ability to individually and correctly identify each of the accused from a considerable distance, especially when it was dark at the alleged place of occurrence was itself suspect. Besides that prosecution was unable to convincingly connect the jeep of accused with the incident beyond reasonable doubt. Further, owing to other lapses in investigation, as recorded by HC, SC was convinced that the prosecution was unable to prove its case beyond all reasonable doubt. The view taken by HC in the facts and circumstances of the case appears to be a reasonably plausible one. Thus, no ground to interfere with the judgment passed by HC. Appeals dismissed.
