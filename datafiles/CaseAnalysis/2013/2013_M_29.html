MAA Binda Express Carrier and another v Northeast Frontier Railway and others

Supreme Court of India

29 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 776; (2014) 3 SCC 760; AIR 2014 SC 390; 2014 (1) AWC 679; 2014 (1) BC 76; 2013 (8) MLJ 540; 2013(14) SCALE 226; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 529; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 540; 2014 (1) WLN(SC) 28

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Tender process

Summary: Constitution of India-Articles 14 and 226-Tender matters-Judicial review-Scope of-Limited-Cancellation of tender process-Bidders participating in tender process-Cannot insist that their tenders should be accepted simply because given tender is highest or lowest depending upon whether contract is for sale of public property or for execution of works on behalf of Government-All that participating bidders entitled to is fair, equal and non-discriminatory treatment in matter of evaluation of their tenders-Tenderer has enforceable right in court who is competent to examine whether aggrieved party treated unfairly or discriminated against to detriment of public interest-Tenders invited not open to judicial scrutiny unless it is found that same have been tailor made to benefit any particular tenderer or class of tenderers-Decision to cancel tender process by railway administration - Held-Not discriminatory or mala fide-Competent authority decided to cancel tender process, not violated any fundamental right of appellant nor could action of respondent be termed unreasonable so as to warrant any interference-Division Bench of High Court perfectly justified in setting aside order of single Judge.
