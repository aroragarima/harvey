Gherulal Parakh v Mahadeodas Maiya and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Syed Jaffer Imam, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 246; AIR 1959 SC 781; 1959 (2) MLJ(SC) 81; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 406

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Municipalities & Local Govts - Partnership Act, 1932, s. 42 - Contract Act, 1872, ss. 23 and 30 - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 133 and 136 - (A) Whether to constitute a wagering contract there must be proof to pay only differences in price u/s. 30 of Contract Act? - Held, to constitute a wagering contract there must be proof that contract was entered into upon terms that performance of contract should not be demanded but only difference in prices should be paid - There should be common intention between parties to wager that they should not demand delivery of goods but should take only difference in prices on happening of an event u/s. 30 of Contract Act - (1) Whether wagering contracts are forbidden by law u/ss. 23 and 30 of Contract Act? - Further held, wagering contracts though void are not forbidden by law - Though a wager is void and unenforceable, it is not forbidden by law and, therefore, object of a collateral agreement is not unlawful u/s. 23 of Contract Act - Partnership being an agreement within meaning of s. 23 of Contract Act, it is not unlawful, though its object is to carry on wagering transactions - (B) Whether Supreme Court accepts concurrent finding of facts u/arts. 133 and 136 of Constitution? - Further held, usual practice of Supreme Court to accept concurrent finding of fact under Constitution of India, arts. 133 and 136 - (C) What is duty of court regarding public policy? - Further held, public policy or policy of law is an illusive concept, it has been described as untrustworthy guide - Primary duty of a court of law is to enforce a promise which parties have made and to uphold sanctity of contracts which form basis of society, but in certain cases, court may relieve them of their duty on a rule founded on public policy, doctrine of public policy is extended not only to harmful cases but also to harmful tendencies - It should only be invoked in clear and incontestable cases of harm to public - (D) Whether wagering contracts can be struck down on grounds of public policy? - Further held, common law of England and that of India have never struck down contracts of wager on ground of public policy, indeed they have always been held to be not illegal notwithstanding fact that statute declared them void - There is no definite head or principle of public policy evolved by courts or laid down by precedents which would directly apply to wagering contracts - (E) Whether wagering contracts can be said to be immoral? - Further held, wagering contracts cannot be said to be immoral u/s. 30 of Contract Act - Immorality word is restricted to sexual immorality - Appeal dismissed.
