Pepsico India Holdings Private Limited v State of Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1030; (2009) 13 SCC 55; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1986; 2009(8) SCALE 125; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 1073; [2009] 24 VST 67

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax; VAT

Keywords: Exemption From Payment Of Tax, Eligibility Certificate, Appeal allowed, Collector Of Customs, Opportunity Of Being Heard, Tax Payable, Exemption Notification, Registration Certificate, Purchase Tax, Notice Of Demand, Entitlement, Exemption Provision, Last Purchase, New Industrial Unit, Manufacturing Unit, Small Scale Industrial Unit, Kerala Surcharge On Taxes Act, 1957, Industrial Policy, Overriding Public Interest

Summary: Sales Tax - Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1963, s. 10 - Exemption - Notification dt. 3-11-1992 amended by notifications dt. 31-12-1999 and 31-3-2000 - Appellant intended to set up a medium scale industrial unit in State of Kerala by relying on notification dt. 3-11-1992 - KSIDC by its letter confirmed that new industrial unit would be exempted from payment of sales tax for first seven years subject to a ceiling of 100% of capital investment - Pursuant to said assurance given to appellant, it entered into an agreement for lease in respect of 50 acres of land for setting up new industrial unit and a sum of Rs.2, 77, 64, 000/- towards amount of consideration for acquisition of said land - Aforementioned Notification dt. 3-11-1992, however, was amended by a notification dt. 31-12-1999 and 31-12-2000 which withdrew exemption except for those units which have been registered as sick prior to 1.1.2000, will be allowed benefit of exemption or deferment - Appellant commenced commercial production on and from 6-3-2001 - Demand notice for sum of Rs.47, 83, 769/- was issued to appellant - Appellant contended that it was exempt from payment of any sales tax having fulfilled all requirements in terms of aforementioned exemption notification - Revenue contended that appellant had not complied with essential conditions for grant of exemption from payment of sales tax as advance payment in specified manner had not been made by it before 1-1-2000 - Whether appellant is entitled to benefits of notification dt. 3-11-1992? - Held, appellant has discharged burden of proof that conditions mentioned in said notification was fulfilled - Appellant admittedly has not realized any tax from its purchasers - In order to keep faith and maintain good governance it is necessary that whatever representation is made by Government or its instrumentality which induces other party to act, Government should not be permitted to withdraw from that - Hence, respondents must, thus, be held to be bound by doctrine of promissory estoppel - Appellant entitled to benefits of said notification - Appeal allowed.
