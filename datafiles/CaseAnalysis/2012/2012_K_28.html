Ramdas Bansal (D) … v Kharag Singh Baid & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

19 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 496; (2012) 2 SCC 548; JT 2012 (2) SC 270; [2012] 4 S.C.R. 583

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Calcutta Thika Tenancy Act, 1949, Calcutta Thika Tenancy (Acquisition and Regulation) Act, 1981, West Bengal Thika Tenancy (Acquisition and Regulation) Act, 2001

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Calcutta Thika Tenancy Act, 1949 - Calcutta Thika Tenancy (Acquisition and Regulation) Act, 1981 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 41 r. 27 - West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act, 1956, s. 13(6) - West Bengal (Acquisition and Regulation) Act, 2001 - Deed of lease - Mismatch of property - Challenged - It was the specific case of appellant that the property mentioned in the First Schedule to the plaint contained in Part I and Part II was not identical to the area shown in the map annexed to the Deed of Lease - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that no notice of eviction, as envisaged u/s. 13(6) of 1956 Act was given before filing of the eviction suit - Particulars given in Parts I and II of the First Schedule and the map as Annexure B to the plaint were incorrect - Lease had never been acted upon by the parties and the same was, by necessary implication cancelled and movables indicated in Annexure C to the plaint belonged to the Appellant and the question of payment of damages did not, therefore, arise - Held, point relating to a portion of the demised premises being Thika Tenancy and thus covered by the provisions of the 1981 Act, was raised before HC, which, however, negated such contention upon holding that the Respondents were not Thika Tenants since the building was constructed on the land in question before 1949 Act, came into operation - Placing reliance on the doctrine of separation of possession from ownership, DB further held that appellant had failed to establish that the Respondents or their predecessors-in-interest were Thika Tenants of the suit property - DB of HC also held that even after execution of the lease deed in favour of the Respondents, lessor remained the owner of the property, whereas respondents' father merely got the right to enjoyment of the property and could not, therefore, be said to be the Thika Tenant within the meaning of the definition given in the subsequent legislations - On such reasoning, DB rejected the application filed on behalf of appellant under O. 41 r. 27 CPC to bring on record subsequent facts to prove his status as tenant of portion of the structure in relation to which the appellant had acquired the status of Bharatia after the acquisition of Thika Tenancies under the 1981 Act - Law relating to Thika Tenancies in relation to Calcutta and Howrah, as it existed prior to the Acquisition Act of 1981, was 1949 Act, which excluded leases of land exceeding 12 years' duration - Instant lease being one for 20 years, same stood excluded from the operation of the 1949 Act, when it was executed - In any event, having been granted lease for period of twenty one years in respect of the building standing on the suit premises, comprising premises in which X Cinema was located, appellant could never claim to be Thika Tenant in respect of the suit premises as defined either under the 1949 Act, 1981 Act, as well as 2001 Act - Appeal dismissed.
