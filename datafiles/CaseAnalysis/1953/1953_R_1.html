R. Mathalone v Bombay Life Assurance Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

19 May 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Vivian Bose, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 54; AIR 1953 SC 385; 1954 (56) BomLR 30; [1954] 24 Comp Cas 1; 1953 (2) MLJ(SC) 259; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 117

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Capital Issue

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1913, s. 34, Table A, regn. 18 - Trusts Act, 1882, ss. 15, 32 and 94 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 40, r. 1 - (A) Whether on facts and circumstances of this case R was under a legal obligation as a trustee to apply for and obtain on behalf of P 384 new shares which appertained to shares sold by R to S - (B) Whether requisition made on R by M/s Craigie, Blunt and Caroe by their letter was sufficient in law to call upon him to apply for shares of new issue and whether R committed default as a trustee in not complying with this requisition - Held, receiver not entitled to acquire new shares - Trustee, not entitled to indemnity till he suffers an injury - Trustee cannot be asked to invest his own money for benefit of 'cestui que trust' - Requisition by some disclosed and other undisclosed beneficiaries not proper direction, and for disobeying an inconclusive or ineffective direction he cannot be mulcted in damages, irrespective of his attitude - Appeal No. 52 dismissed and Appeal No. 53 allowed.
