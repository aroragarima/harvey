R.S. Nayak v A.R. Antulay And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 824; (1986) SCC (Cr) 256; (1986) 2 SCC 716; AIR 1986 SC 2045; [1986] 2 S.C.R. 621

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: RANGANATH MISRA, J.

(A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 5(2), 5(1)(d) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 161, 165, 384, 420 - Corruption - Refusal to frame charge - Appellant lodged complaint alleging respondent Chief Minister and others had committed several offences under the Act - Trial Court framed 21 charges and refused to frame remaining 22 charges - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Trial Court was justified in refusing to frame 22 charges - Held, if evidence was accepted for half the number of charges relating to similar offences, there could hardly be any scope to reject 22 draft charges - Facts were interconnected and there could be no justification to reject the charge of criminal conspiracy - Reasoning given by Trial Court for draft charges relating to ss. 161 and 165 IPC and s. 5(2) r/w s. 5(1) of the Act could not be sustained - Trial Court failed to take note of presumption u/s. 4 of the Act while dealing with charges u/ss. 161 and 165 IPC and s. 5(1)(a) and (b) of the Act - Provisions of ss. 161 and 165 IPC and s. 5 of the Act were intended to keep public servant free from corruption and thus ultimately ensure purity in public life - Evidence in the instant case, therefore, should have been judged keeping these aspects in view - Trial Court failed to analyze evidence which he had at great length extracted keeping proper angle of approach in view - Further, charge u/s. 420 IPC should be framed by Trial Court against respondent - Hence, prima facie case was established by prosecution in respect of allegations for charges u/ss.120B, 161, and 165 and 420 IPC, as also u/s. 5(1) r/w s. 5(2) of the Act - Trial Court's conclusion was not made on a proper assessment and was not sustainable - Appeal party allowed.

P.N. BHAGWATI, J. (Concurring)

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 4, 5(1)(a) (b), 161, 165 - Presumption of bribery - Applicability - Whether presumption u/s. 4 would apply only after framing the charge - Held, court should examine evidence as it stands without rebuttal and come to a conclusion whether on the basis of such evidence the court would convict accused - Where the offence charged against accused was u/s. 161 or s. 165 or s. 5 (1) (a) or (b) the Court should necessarily apply presumption u/s. 4 while considering whether on the basis of unrebutted evidence which is before it the court would convict the accused - Appeal partly allowed.

(C) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 5(2), 5(1)(d) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 161, 165, 384, 420 - Corruption - Prima facie case - Proving of - Whether prosecution was able to establish prima facie case against respondent - Held, a prima facie case was made out by prosecution in respect of transaction of no objection certificate and 35th, 36th and 37th charges should have been framed against respondent - On the evidence led by prosecution a prima facie case should be held to have been made out against respondent in respect of transaction of donations and 29th, 30th and 31st charges ought in circumstances to have been framed against respondent - A prima facie case was made out by prosecution for framing 23rd, 24th, 25th, 41st, 42nd and 43rd draft charges against respondent - Trial Court fell into an error in discharging respondent in respect of these charges - Further, evidence on record merely for the purpose of deciding whether the evidence was of such a nature that, if unrebutted, it would warrant conviction of respondent - It would be open to respondent to rebut this evidence and to make out his defence when trial proceeds against him on charges already framed by Trial Court and additional charges directed to be framed against him - Appeal partly allowed.
