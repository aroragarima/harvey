Shri Ram Narain v Simla Banking and Industrial Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Jagannadhadas, Vivian Bose, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 29; AIR 1956 SC 614; [1956] 26 Comp Cas 280; 1956 SCJ 579; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 603

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Preventive Detention Act, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, misa, Banking Companies (Legal Practitioners Accounts) Act, 1949, Banking Companies (Amendment) Act, Companies (Amendment) Ordinance

Summary: Banking & Finance - Indian Companies Act, 1956 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Banking Companies (Legal Practitioners Accounts) Act, 1949 - Banking Companies (Amendment) Act,s. 2 and 45-B - Companies (Amendment) Act - Companies (Amendment) Ordinance - Preventive Detention Act, 1950 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 39 - Companies Act, 1913, ss. 171 and 232 - Displaced Persons (Debts Adjustment) Act, 1951, ss. 4 and 13 - Appellant took up residence in India as displaced person - Amount of Rs. 1,00,000 due from Bank - Bank did not pay amount due to Appellant but adjusted it towards part payment of a sum of Rs. 4,00,000 which is alleged to have been due from appellant to Bank which appellant disputed and denied - Displaced Persons (Debts Adjustment) Act, 1951 was passed providing certain facilities and reliefs to displaced debtors and displaced creditors - Taking adavantage of ss. 4 and 13 of Act Appellant filed an application before a tribunal claiming Rs. 1,00,000 - Meanwhile an application under Indian Companies Act, 1913 was made in High Court by some creditor for winding up of Bank - High Court stayed all proceedings against Bank - However, before order was communicated to tribunal, application of Appellant was disposed of and a decree was passed against Bank - Such decree was stayed by High Court - Tribunal passed an order staying execution - Winding up of Bank was finally ordered - Appellant filed Application that execution case should now be proceeded - Case transferred to another High Court - Order passed in favour of Appellant - (A) Whether there is anything in Displaced Persons (Debts Adjustment) Act, 1951 which overrides jurisdiction conferred by s. 45-B, Banking Companies (Amendment) Act, 1953? - Prima facie by virtue of s. 28 of Displaced Persons (Debts Adjustment) Act jurisdiction to execute Tribunal's decree is in Tribunal - But it is equally clear that jurisdiction to decide any of claims which must necessarily arise in execution of decree is vested in High Court by virtue of s. 45-B of Banking Companies Act - Each being a special Act, ordinary principle that a special law overrides a general law does not afford any clear solution in this case - Therefore, it is desirable to determine overriding effect of relevant provisions in these two Acts, in a given case, on much broader considerations of purpose and policy underlying two Acts - Purpose of Banking Companies Act is for speedy disposal of winding up proceedings - It is meant to impart speedy stability to financial credit structure in country - Displaced Persons (Debts Adjustment) Act is meant for relief and rehabilitation of displaced persons - It is by and large a measure for rehabilitation of displaced debtors - In a given case, between provisions of Banking Companies Act and provisions of Displaced Persons (Debts Adjustment) Act, in so far as such provisions relate to displaced debtors, it is unable to find that jurisdiction so clearly and definitely vested in High Court by s. 45-B of Banking Companies Act that they can be said to be overridden or displaced by anything in Displaced Persons (Debts Adjustment) Act, 1951 in so far as, they relate to displaced creditors - Overriding provision of Banking Companies Act, so far as a displaced creditor is concerned, is substantially only as regards jurisdiction - Held, that in present case overriding effect of s. 45-B of Banking Companies Act deprives him only of facility of pursuing his execution in jurisdiction of Tribunal but no reason why he should not get benefit of other provisions, which may give him an advantage and are not inconsistent with any of other specific provisions of Banking Companies Act - (B) Whether in terms of s. 45-C there has been a valid transfer of execution proceeding to Punjab High Court - Execution proceeding filed by appellant before Tribunal,continued to remain pending by date when Banking Companies (Amendment) Act, 1953, came into operation - This application was, therefore, a pending application for purposes of s. 45-C of Banking Companies Act - S. 45-C(1) definitely imposes a bar on any pending matter in any other court being proceeded with except in manner provided therein - Jurisdiction of that other Court to proceed with a pending proceeding is made to depend on fact that its pendency is brought to notice of appropriate High Court - Pendency not brought to notice of Court by Liquidator within three months, thus nothing to prevent Court exercising its power of transfer at such time - Therefore, an argument as to invalidity of order of transfer cannot be entertained - Held, that view taken by High Court that it had exclusive jurisdiction in respect of present matter and that there was a valid transfer to itself is correct - Appeal dismissed.
