Hussnain International v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 118; (2011) 11 SCC 566; 2011 (265) E.L.T. 24; JT 2011 (2) SC 303; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 605; 2011(2) SCALE 658; 2012 (25) S.T.R. 191

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Customs - Practice & Procedure - Foreign Trade (Development and Regulation) Act, 1992, s. 11(2) - Penalty - The appellant carried on the business of export of brass art-wares and other Indian handicrafts - Two Advance Licences were issued to the appellant for import of 240 MT's of brass dross/ash against each of the licences for the C.I.F. value of Rs.24,64,800/- and Rs.24,64,000/- respectively with the condition to export of 80 MT's of brass art-ware against each of the licences for FOB value of US dollars 2,37,082.95 and US dollars 2,38,545 within a period of 12 months - As the appellant failed to discharge its export obligation under the licences, two show-cause notices were issued to the appellant to show cause why penalty u/s. 11(2) of the Act will not be imposed on it - Subsequently, Adjudicating Authority imposed a penalty of Rs. 1,30,00,000/- in respect of each of the two licences on the appellant u/s. 11(2) of the Act - Appellant filed appeals against said orders of the penalty alongwith applications for stay of recovery of the penalty - The appellant was intimated to make pre-deposit of Rs. 5,00,000/- towards the penalties - The appellant failed to make the pre-deposit of Rs.5,00,000/- and consequently the appeals were dismissed - Single Judge dismissed appeal filed against said order - DB allowed the appellant to deposit a sum of Rs. 20,00,000/- in each of the two appeals within 8 weeks and further directing that on such deposits being made the Appellate Authority may dispose of the appeals on merits - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether the order of the DB could be upheld? - Held, the second proviso to sub-section (1) of s. 15 of the Act states that in the case of an appeal against a decision or order imposing a penalty or redemption charges, no such appeal shall be entertained unless the amount of the penalty or redemption charges has been deposited by the appellant - The third proviso to sub-section (1) of s. 15 of the Act, however, states "where the Appellate Authority is of opinion that the deposit to be made will cause undue hardship to the appellant, it may, at its discretion, dispense with such deposit either unconditionally or subject to such conditions as it may impose" - Hence, under the Act discretion is vested in the Appellate Authority to dispense with a pre-deposit of penalty either unconditionally or subject to such condition as the Appellate Authority may impose - If in exercise of such discretion, the Appellate Authority in the instant case dispensed with the pre-deposit penalty of Rs.1,30,00,000/- in each of the two appeals subject to the appellant depositing a sum of Rs.5,00,000/-, the DB ought not to have enhanced the amount of pre-deposit to Rs.20,00,000/- for each of the two appeals - Impugned order of the DB set aside - Appellant is directed to deposit the sum of Rs. 5,00,000/- as directed by the Appellate Authority within two months from today - Appeals allowed.
