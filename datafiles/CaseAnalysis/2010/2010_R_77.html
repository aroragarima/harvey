Van Vibhag Karamchari Griha Nirman Sahkari Sanstha Maryadit (Regd.) v Ramesh Chander and others

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 900; AIR 2011 SC 41; 2011 (2) ALD(SC) 101; 2011 (84) ALR 681; 2011 (1) AWC 559; 2011 (1) JabLJ 278; JT 2010 (11) SC 360; 2010 (8) MLJ(SC) 1023; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 620; 2011 (112) RD 389; 2011 (1) RLW 777; 2010(11) SCALE 99; [2010] 12 S.C.R. 1045

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Cooperative Society Act, 1960

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 54 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6, r. 17 and O. 2 r. 2 - Limitation - Application for amendment of pleadings - Respondent no. 1 entered into an oral agreement with the appellant to sell his land to the him at Rs.2 lacs per hectare - The agreement to sell was executed and signed by respondent - Subsequently, Indore Development Authority declared a Scheme which included the disputed land - Respondent no. 1 filed an application to the District Collector stating that he had sold the disputed land to the appellant and possession of the same had been handed over to the appellant - Thereafter, respondent no. 1 issued a public notice in a local daily that he is the owner of the impugned land and in possession of the same and any action taken by the appellant over such land would not affect the rights of ownership and possession of the respondent no. 1 over the land - Respondent no. 1 also stated that the agreement to sell and Power of Attorney in favour of the appellant stood cancelled - Appellant filed suit for permanent injunction which was dismissed by the trial court on the ground that the impugned suit was not filed for relief of specific performance - Appellant moved an application for amendment of pleadings for inclusion of the relief of specific performance of contract - Said application was dismissed - Hence, present appeal - Whether the appellant has a cause of action to file a suit for specific performance? - Held, the refusal by the respondent no. 1 to acknowledge the right of the appellant over the land in its public notice definitely furnishes the appellant with a cause of action to file a suit for specific performance but it has not filed such a suit within the period of limitation prescribed for filing such a suit - Therefore, the suit filed by appellant is hit by the provisions of O. 2 r. 2 of CPC - Hence, the plea of specific performance, cannot be granted to the appellant - Impugned order of the HC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
