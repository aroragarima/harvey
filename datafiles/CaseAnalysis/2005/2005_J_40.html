Jamshed N. Guzdar, Bombay Civil And Sessions Court Bar Association, The State Of Maharashtra, Jaimini B. Chinai And Others Etc, The State Of Madhya Pradesh v Jamshed N. Guzdar & Others, State Of Maharashtra And Others, The Perfect Pottery Co. Ltd. & Others

Supreme Court of India

11 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, K.G. Balakrishnan, B.N. Srikrishna, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 13; (2005) 2 SCC 591; AIR 2005 SC 862; 2005 (3) Bom.C.R. 139; JT 2005 (1) SC 370; 2005 (2) MahLJ 392; 2005 (2) MPLJ 181; 2005(1) SCALE 192; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 223

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Doctrine Of Pith And Substance, Indian High Courts Act, 1861, Bombay City Civil Court Act, 1948, Govt. of India Act, 1935, Andhra State Act, 1953, North America Act, Bombay City Civil Court and Bombay Court of Small Causes (Enhancement of Pecuniary Jurisdiction & Amendment) Act, 1986, Maharashtra High Court (Hearing of Writ Petitions by Division Bench and Abolition of Letters Patent Appeals) Act, 1986, Patent (Amendment) Act, 1948, Mysore Civil Court Act, 1964

Summary: Constitutional validity of Bombay City Civil Court and Bombay Court of Small Causes (Enhancement of Pecuniary Jurisdiction & Amendment) Act, 1986 (Maharashtra Act No. XV of 1987) and Maharashtra High Court (Hearing of Writ Petitions by Division Bench and Abolition of Letters Patent Appeals) Act, 1986 - Legislative competence of the State legislatures of Maharashtra and Madhya Pradesh in passing the enactments - Whether decision of High Court of Madhya Pradesh striking down provisions of Madhya Pradesh Uchcha Nyayalaya (Letters Patent Appeals Samapti) Adhiniyam, 1981 abolishing Letters Patent appeals as invalid is sustainable? - Held, it was competent for the State Legislatures to pass law relating to general jurisdiction of the High Courts dealing with the topic 'administration of justice' under Entry 11-A of List III. Assuming that incidentally 1986 Act and the Adhiniyam touch upon the Letters Patent, the 1986 Act and Adhiniyam cannot be declared either as unconstitutional or invalid applying doctrine of pith and substance - Uphold the constitutional validity of 1987 Act, 1986 Act and the Adhiniyam.
