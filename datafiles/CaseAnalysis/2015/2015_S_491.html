Abdul Waheed v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

01 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 747; (2016) 1 SCC 583; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 4931; JT 2015 (9) SC 444; 2016 (1) RCR(Criminal) 91; 2015(9) SCALE 471; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 741

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Pistol, Blows, Cartridges, Lathi

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 148, 304 Part II and 323 r/w 149 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) and other co-accused allegedly opened fire on complainant party and complainant's brother (deceased) sustained fracture injuries and died - Trial Court convicted appellant u/s. 302 of IPC for murder of deceased and sentenced him to undergo life imprisonment - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before HC, which was dismissed by impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that there was discrepancy in the place of occurrence and that complainant party were aggressors and that conviction could only be u/s. 304 Part II of IPC.

Held, appellant and accused party were having enmity against the deceased on account of civil suit and filing of contempt petition. Admittedly, accused persons were close relations of appellant hence they also rendered a helping hand in the commission of the crime and all of them formed an unlawful assembly with common intention of inflicting injuries to the complainant party. There is plausible and cogent evidence establishing motive of the accused person for committing the alleged crime. Proof of motive of the accused towards the deceased heightens the possibility of the crime. Proof of motive adds weight and value to the evidence of the eye-witnesses. Evidence of PW1 (injured witness) cannot be doubted and his evidence stands on a higher footing. At the time of occurrence PW3 and 4 were present in the house of PW3 and on hearing the hues and cries of the occurrence, both of them rushed to the spot and their evidence is fully corroborated by the evidence of PW1. Courts below recorded concurrent findings of fact that the evidence of PW1, 3 and 4 remains unshaken and in spite of lengthy cross-examination of these witnesses, the accused was not able to bring any point shaking credibility of PW 1, 3 and 4. When gun is used and the person who fires the gun must be presumed to have knowledge and intention that he is inflicting an injury which in the ordinary course of nature is sufficient to cause death and the offence is clearly murder. Having regard to the enmity and the weapon used, the Courts below rightly held that appellant was guilty of committing the murder of deceased. Appeal dismissed.
