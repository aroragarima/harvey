State of Goa v Babu Thomas

Supreme Court of India

29 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1236; (2005) 8 SCC 130; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1995; 2005 CRLJ 4379; JT 2005 (12) SC 204; 2005(7) SCALE 659; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 712

Case Digest

Subject: Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7, 13, 19(1)(c) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 161, 165 - Goa Shipyard Officer's Conduct, Disciplines and Appeal Rules 1979 - Illegal gratification- Dismissal - Maintainability - Respondent was employed in Shipyard Limited and was arrested by CID, Anti Corruption Bureau on charge that he demanded and accepted illegal gratification from complainant - Charge sheet was filed u/ss. 7, 13 of the 1988 Act and u/ss. 161, 165 IPC before Special Judge - Prosecution also filed sanction order dt 2-1-1995 issue by Company Secretary and another sanction order dt. 7-9-1997 was issued which states that it would have retrospective effect from dt. 14-9-1994 - Respondent was dismissed from service on 21-1-1997 - Aggrieved by which petition was filed before HC which was allowed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether sanction order passed was maintainable - Held, sanction order was not a mere irregularity, error or omission - Further, first sanction order dt 2-1-1995 was issued by an authority that was not a competent authority to have issued such order under Rules and second sanction order dt. 7-9-97 was also issued by an authority, which was not competent to issue same under relevant rules, apart from fact that same was issued retrospectively w.e.f. 14-9-94, which was bad - Furthermore, cognizance was taken by the Special Judge on 29-5-95 - Therefore, when Special Judge took cognizance on 29-5-1995, there was no sanction order under law authorizing him to take cognizance - Thus, was a fundamental error which invalidates cognizance as without jurisdiction - Appeal disposed of.
