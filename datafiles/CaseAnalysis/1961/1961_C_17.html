Â Collector of Customs, Madras v Nathella Sampathu Chetty and another

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Mohammad Hidayatullah, A.K. Sarkar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 1; AIR 1962 SC 316; 1962 CRLJ 364; 1983 ECR 2198; 1999 (110) E.L.T. 157; 1962 (1) MLJ(SC) 43; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 786

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Sea Customs Act, 1878, s. 178 A - Whether s. 178 A Act was constitutionally valid and not in violation of art. 19 (1)(f) of Constitution? - Rule as to burden of proof enacted by section applies to contravention of a notification u/s. 8(1) of Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1947, by virtue of its being deemed to be a contravention of a notification u/s. 19 of Sea Customs Act - Preliminary requirement of s. 178 A that officer seizing should entertain 'a reasonable belief that goods seized were smuggled' was satisfied - Placing burden of proof on person in whose possession gold found was not violative of art. 19 (1) (f) - It is in interest of general public - Held, s. 178 A of Act constitutionally valid - Petition dismissed.
