Mohmed Inayatullah v The State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

09 September 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 237; (1976) SCC (Cr) 199; (1976) 1 SCC 828; AIR 1976 SC 483; 1976 CRLJ 481; 1975 CRLR 567; 1975 CrLR(SC) 567; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 715

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 27, 114 , Illus. a (A) Information - Limit and scope of provable information - Presumption u/s.114(a) - Availability - (B) Scope of expression 'fact discovered' u/s. 27 of the Act - (C) Interpretation - Construction of statement of accused for determining admissible portions - Held, presumption not available - Includes not only the physical object produced but also the place from which it is produced and the knowledge of the accused as to this - Statement had to be split up into its components and to separate the admissible from the inadmissible portion or portions and only those components or portions which were the immediate cause of the discovery would be legal evidence and not the rest which must be excised and reject - Appeal Allowed.
