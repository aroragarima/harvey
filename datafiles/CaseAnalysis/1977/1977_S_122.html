Shaikh Abdul Azees v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

28 March 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 333; (1977) SCC (Cr) 378; (1977) 2 SCC 485; AIR 1977 SC 1485; 1977 CRLJ 1121; 1977 CRLR 252; 1978 MLJ 1; [1977] 3 S.C.R. 393; 1977 UJ 436

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Penal Code, 1860, s. 303 - CrPC, 1898, s. 401 - Constitution, art. 161 - 'Interpretation of expression 'Being under sentence of imprisonment for life' - Murder - Life imprisonment -Conditional remission u/s. 401 of CrPC, 1898 - Breach of condition ( second murder) - Appellant ceases to be under sentence of imprisonment for life - Held, s. 303 is not attracted and Court is not obliged to impose sentence of death - Appeal Partly Allowed.
