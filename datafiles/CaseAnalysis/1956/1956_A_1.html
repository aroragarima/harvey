A. M. Allison v B. L. Sen

Supreme Court of India

21 December 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 43; AIR 1957 SC 227; 1957 (1) LLJ 472; 1957 SCJ 268; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 359

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Minimum Wages Act,1948, s. 3, and 5(2) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 133(1)(c) and 226 - Appeal against a judgment of High Court of Assam dismissing appellants application u/art. 226 of Constitution challenging orders of first respondent, Deputy Commissioner, Sibsagar, whereby he allowed applications filed on behalf of labourers employed in Teok Tea Estate and Dalim Tea Estate u/s. 20 of Minimum Wages Act - (A)Whether basic wages fixed by notification was 'minimum time rate'? - Held, what was fixed by notification was not merely 'a minimum time rate' irrespective of existing work-load or task which used to be performed by labourers - But was a minimum wage which, though fixed for time work, was necessarily correlated to work-load or task then being performed by these labourers so that whatever extra work was done by labourers in excess of existing work-load or task of plucking 16 seers of tea leaves in case of male labourers and 12 seers of tea leaves in case of female labourers had to be paid for in accordance with practice then prevailing -(B) Whether High Court and Supreme Court has power to refuse writ u/art. 226 of Constitution? - Held, High Court has power to refuse writ if it is satisfied that there was no failure of justice - And in appeals which, are directed against orders of High Court in applications u/art. 226 - Supreme Court can refuse to interfere unless it is satisfied that justice of case requires it - But where it is not so satisfied it will not interfere - Appeal dismissed.
