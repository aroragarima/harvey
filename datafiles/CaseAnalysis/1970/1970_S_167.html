P. Sirajuddin Etc v State of Madras Etc

Supreme Court of India

09 March 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 339; (1970) SCC (Cr) 240; (1970) 1 SCC 595; AIR 1971 SC 520; 1971 CRLJ 523; 1971 (2) MLJ(SC) 97; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 931

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 5, 5A and 5(1)(b) - CrPC, 1898, ss. 160, 161, 162, 163 and 164, 156 and 337 - Constitution, art. 14 - (A) Criminal Procedure - (1) Public servant charged for serious misdemeanour - Preliminary enquiry necessary before lodging FIR - Duty of enquiring officer - (2) Preliminary enquiry - Granting Of amnesty by enquiring Officer to persons who were to be examined as prosecution witnesses, is highly irregular - Neither Code nor Act recognises immunity from prosecution - Grant of pardon - If discretionary - (3) 'Any person having any connection with the official functions of himself ' - Includes any subordinate of person who accepts valuable things -

(B) Constitution - Discrimination - Public servant, head of department found actively responsible for directing commission of offences by his subordinates in particular manner - Held, grant of pardon is not in discretion of police authorities - He cannot be allowed to take plea that subordinates must also be joined as co-accused with him - Appeal Dismissed.
