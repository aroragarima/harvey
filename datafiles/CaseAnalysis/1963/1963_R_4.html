Â R.P. Kapur v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Raghubar Dayal, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 204; AIR 1964 SC 787; 1966 (2) LLJ 164; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 431

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Ultra Vires, Contract Of Service, Master And Servant Relationship

Summary: Service - All India Services Act, 1951 - Govt. of India Act, 1935, s. 241(1) - Indian Independence Act, 1947, s. 10 - All India Services (Overseas Pay, Passage and Leave Salary) Rules, 1957, r. 3 - All India Service (Discipline and Appeals) rules, 1955, rr. 3, 7, 10 and 49 - Indian Administrative Service Recruitment Rules, 1954, r. 3 - Validity of order of suspension - Held, right guaranteed to a member of former Secretary of State's Services with respect to interim suspension (as distinct from suspension as a punishment) is that such a member cannot be so suspended except by appointing authority which in changed circumstances is Govt. of India - That right has been violated by r. 7 of Discipline Rules insofar as it permits any authority other than Govt. of India to suspend pending a departmental enquiry or pending a criminal charge a public servant who was a member of the former Secretary of State's Services - Appeal allowed.
