Vutukuru Lakshmaiah v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

24 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 293; JT 2015 (4) SC 340; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 1028; 2015(5) SCALE 478; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 750

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: (A) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 149, 302, 304 - Alleged Assault and Murder - Conviction - Justification - Prosecution stated that deceased was witness in murder case wherein accused-appellant, A-1, was accused, hence, deceased along with PW1 was assaulted by accused persons with weapons as a result of which deceased suffered injuries and was admitted in hospital where Magistrate, PW 18 recorded dying declaration of deceased at hospital - Trial court convicted accused appellant u/ss. 302 and 148 of IPC - Order passed by Trial court was confirmed by HC - Appellant contended that they could not have been convicted in aid of s. 149 of IPC as charge framed against them was simplicitor s. 302 IPC.

Held, on perusal of evidence on record, facts and circumstances clearly bring out that there was unlawful assembly. Each of accused person was very well aware that they are tried for being part of assembly which was armed with weapons and hence, it was unlawful. On close scrutiny of evidence on record, it is difficult to hold that any prejudice has been caused to accused appellants. Appeals dismissed.

(B) Criminal - Alleged Assault & Murder - Conviction - Reliability of witness - Whether prosecution witnesses are chance witnesses, for there is no occasion on their part to be at scene of crime.

Held, prosecution has been able to establish presence of witnesses at place of occurrence. Further, plea that there was no electricity and, therefore, it would not have been possible on part of witnesses to see accused-appellants by lighting earthen mud lamp does not deserve commendation, for witnesses have categorically deposed that they were able to see accused persons and participation of accused-appellants. Hence, despite keen scrutiny of their evidence, SC is unable to put them in category of so-called 'chance witnesses'. Appeals dismissed.

(C) Criminal - Alleged Murder - Recording of dying declaration of deceased - Justification - Whether dying declaration of deceased recorded by Magistrate is acceptable and reliable.

Held, at time of recording of dying declaration, as material would show, declarant was absolutely in conscious state and there is endorsement in that regard by treating doctor. Further, submission that dying declaration is eminently vague is neither correct nor is it based on any material on record. On scanning of dying declaration, declarant had named accused nos. 1,2,3,5,8,and 9. Hence, in absence of any kind of infirmity or inherent contradiction or inconsistency or any facet that would create serious doubt on dying declaration, SC failed to discard it. Appeals dismissed.

(D) Criminal - Conviction - Pleas of alibi - Rejection - Whether non-acceptance of plea of alibi is justified.

Held, prosecution has been able to clearly establish presence of accused-appellant A-1, appellant in criminal appeal, at scene of occurrence. Further, initial onus put on prosecution having been discharged, burden shifts to accused to establish plea of alibi with certainty. HC held that plea of alibi of accused-appellant, A -1, could not be accepted as his presence has been proven and SC held that said opinion of HC is based on material brought on record and hence, there is no reason to differ with same. Appeals dismissed

(E) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 304 - Alleged murder - Motive - Determination - Whether appellant accused had intention to commit murder of deceased.

Held, injury report showed that there are five stab injuries at different parts of body. Further, evidence on record shows that deceased was assaulted as he was witness in murder case wherein accused-appellant, A-1, was accused. SC held that considering nature of injuries and previous animosity, it is not fit case where offence u/s. 302 of IPC should be converted to s. 304 Part I of IPC. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - Conviction of accused cannot be challenged when prosecution has established their guilty beyond reasonable doubt.
