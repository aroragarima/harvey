H.D. Singh v Reserve Bank of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 September 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Khalid, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 280; (1985) 4 SCC 201; (1985) SCC (L&S) 975; AIR 1986 SC 132; 1986 (29) DLT 77; 1985 (51) FLR 494; 1986 (1) LLJ 127; 1985 (2) LLN 1037; 1985(2) SCALE 607; 1985 (2) SLJ 457; [1985] Supp2 S.C.R. 842; 1985 UJ 1733; 1986 (1) UJ 324

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(oo), 25F, 2A, 25F and 2(ra), sch.5, item 10 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - Industrial Dispute - (A) Retrenchment - Striking off name of workmen from rolls - Amounts to retrenchment - Non-observance of procedure in s. 25-F of 1947 Act - (B) Unfair labour practice - Confidential circular of Reserve Bank of India - Directing officers by confidential circular that Tikka Mazdoors(persons helping examiners of notes/coins) should not be engaged continuously - But offered work on rotation basis and characterising mazdoors as Badli workers - Validity - (C) Continuous service for not less than one year - Workman in Reserve Bank of India claiming to have worked for more them 240 days in a period of 12 months - Bank failing to produce record - Inference - Held, retrenchment is invalid and dispute is covered by s. 2A of the 1947 Act - Direction amounts to unfair labour practice - Inference has to be drawn that workman's case is true - Appeal allowed.
