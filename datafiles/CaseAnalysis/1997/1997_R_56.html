State of Maharashtra v Rajendra Jawanmal Gandhi

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 682; (1997) 8 SCC 386; (1998) SCC (Cr) 76; AIR 1997 SC 3986; 1997 (3) CCR 197; 1997 (3) Crimes 285; 1997 CRLJ 4657; JT 1997 (8) SC 43; 1997(6) SCALE 80; 1997 (3) SCJ 175; [1997] Supp4 S.C.R. 68; 1997 (8) Supreme 129

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic; Women & Children

Summary: Terrorist Affected Areas (Special Courts) Act, 1984 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1974, s. 222(3) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376 and 511 - Powers of Court was in question for charging u/s. 376 for rape - Evidence showing that though actually rape was not committed accused attempted to commit rape on victim who was 12 years old - Accused could be convicted u/s. 376 r/w s. 511 though attempt to commit rape was not separately charged - Held, sentence of five years rigorous imprisonment and fine of Rs. 40,000 would be proper.
