Niranjan and Company P. Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal-I and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 282; (1986) Supp SCC 272; AIR 1986 SC 1853; 1986 (52) CTR 270; [1986] 159 ITR 153; 1986(1) SCALE 465; [1986] 1 S.C.R. 916; 1986 TAXLR 867; 1986 (2) UJ 142

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income tax & Direct Taxes - Income tax Act, 1961, s. 147 - Assessment - Balance-Sheet - Profit and loss - Revised return - Jurisdiction - Appellant/assessee filed its return along with copy of Balance-Sheet and profit and loss account in November, 1962 showing an income of Rs.2.092 as its profit - According to appellant, a mistake had occurred in preparation of return, inasmuch as profit of Rs. 10,718.46 arising from construction words had been left out from return - Income-tax Officer made an assessment after taking into account the profit from construction work - Appellant/assessee again filed a revised return showing general profit of Rs. 2.092 as also profit from construction work aggregating Rs.12,797.65 - But no copy of Balance-Sheet or Profit and loss account was annexed the revised return - Income-tax Officer issued notice to appellant u/s. 147 of 1961 Act on ground that revised return was not before Income-Tax Officer when assessment Order was originally made but came to her possession later on - Appellant challenge before HC jurisdiction of Income-tax-Officer to issue notice - Single Judge dismissed application and Division Bench confirmed the order of the Single Judge in appeal preferred by appellant - Whether assessee filling revised return voluntarily after making of first assessment relevant for jurisdiction to issue notice u/s. 147 of 1961 Act - Held, u/s. 147(a) of Act completed assessment can only be reopened - If there was omission or failure on part of assessee to make return or to disclose fully and truly all material and relevant facts - Second right under cl.(b) of s. 147 of Act, under which Income-tax Officer had to reopen completed assessment was that notwithstanding that there was no omission or failure on part of assessee either to make return or to disclose fully and truly all material facts, Income-tax Officer in consequence of information in his possession subsequent to first assessment had reason to believe that income chargeable to tax escaped assessment - After expiry of time to make return, if an assessee files return before assessment was made, then Income-tax Officer was bound to take cognizance of that return and cannot ignore that return - But after completion of an assessment, assessee was not entitled to take benefit of another return filed by him, nor Income-tax Officer obliged or entitled to take that return into consideration except by process of re-opening assessment - Appeal dismissed.
