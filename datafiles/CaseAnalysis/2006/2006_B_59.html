Vidyawati Gupta and Others v Bhakti Hari Nayak and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 33; (2006) 2 SCC 777; AIR 2006 SC 1194; 2006 (2) AWC 1580; 2006 (2) CTC 146; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 200; JT 2006 (2) SC 278; 2006 (2) MLJ(SC) 312; 2006 (1) RCR(Civil) 727; 2006(2) SCALE 203; 2006 (2) SCJ 630; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 9

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Indian High Courts Act

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - Indian High Courts Act, 1861 - CPC, 1908, o. 4, rr. 1, 3, o. 6, r. 15, o. 7, rr. 1, 11, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, ss. 129, 2, 26 - General Clauses Act, 1897 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 8 - Original Side Rules of Calcutta HC - Institution of suits - Amended requirements of o. 6, r.15(4) - Non- Filing of an affidavit in support of pleadings in plaint at time of presentation - Alleged non compliance of amended requirements - DB held that after defect was removed suit must be deemed to have been duly instituted wef 28-07-2004 and not before that date and consequently interlocutory order that had been passed by learned single Judge at a point of time when suit had not been duly instituted could not survive - Appeal against - Held, even though amended provisions of o. 6 are attracted in matter of filing of plaints in Original Side of Calcutta HC on account of reference made to o. 6, r. 1 of Chapter 7 of Original Side Rules, non-compliance thereof at initial stage did not render suit non-est - Views expressed by Calcutta HC, being contrary to established legal position - Appeal allowed.
