GMG Engineering Industries and another v ISSA Green Power Solution and another

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 373; AIR 2015 SC 2675; 2015 (5) ALD(SC) 32; 2015(4) ALL MR 401; 2015 (112) ALR 1; 2015 (4) AWC 4229; 2016 (2) CalLT(SC) 17; 2015 (3) CTC 859; JT 2015 (5) SC 244; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2330; 2015 (4) MLJ 321; 2015 (3) RCR(Civil) 366; 2015 (129) RD 269; 2015(6) SCALE 551; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 1108; 2015 (2) WLN(SC) 109

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Condition to deposit money - Condonation of delay - As sale transaction entered into between appellants and respondents could not be completed, respondents issued legal notice to appellants - Appellants in reply offered to return said amount but without interest - Respondents filed suit for recovery of money with interest and recovery of advance which were decreed ex-parte by Trial Court - On application, ex-parte decree was set aside and delay was condoned by imposing condition to deposit sum - On revision, HC upheld order imposing condition to deposit money as a condition precedent and dismissed revisions - Hence, instant Appeals - Whether appellants have satisfactorily explained reasons for delay and order imposing condition to deposit money is sustainable.

Held, while Trial Court has exercised discretion to condone delay in filing applications to set aside ex-parte decrees, Trial Court should not have imposed such unreasonable and onerous condition of depositing entire suit claim respectively in suits when issues are yet to be decided on merits. While considering revision, HC should have kept in view that parties are yet to go for trial and appellants ought to have been afforded opportunity to contest suits on merits. When S.L.Ps came up for admission SC passed conditional order that subject to deposit sum of before trial Court, notice shall be issued to respondents. Appellants have deposited amount before trial Court. Since appellants have satisfactorily explained reasons for delay and with view to provide opportunity to appellants to contest suit, impugned order is liable to be set aside. Thus, order imposing condition to deposit money as condition precedent to set aside ex-parte order and condoning delay is unsustainable because appellants have satisfactorily explained reasons for delay. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - When there is no negligence, inaction or want of bonafide is imputable to appellants, delay has to be condoned.
