Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v K. H. Chambers, Madras

Supreme Court of India

09 November 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 331; AIR 1965 SC 970; [1965] 55 ITR 674; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 43

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Banking & Finance - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 25(4) - Whether assessee could be allowed claim of relief u/s. 25 (4)? - Substantial assets of business were transferred and remaining assets including name of transferred business were retained to ensure carrying of transferred business without being unduly burdened - Succession involves change of ownership and it implies that substantially identity and continuity of business are preserved - Decisions referred did not lay down that finding of succession is one of fact upon which High Court has no jurisdiction to decide - Held, transfer of business herein was regarded as succession and therefore relief u/s. 25(4) is available to assessee - Appeal dismissed.
