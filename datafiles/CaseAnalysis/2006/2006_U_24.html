Udayani Ship Breakers Limited v Commissioner of Customs and Central Excise, Rajkot

Supreme Court of India

08 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 590; (2006) 3 SCC 345; 2006 (195) E.L.T. 3; JT 2006 (2) SC 336; 2006(2) SCALE 277; 2006 (3) SCJ 124; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 138

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Trade

Keywords: Contiguous Zone Of India

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, ss. 2, 14 and 22 - Customs Tariff Act, 1975 - Customs Valuation (Determination of Price of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988, rr. 3 and 4(1) - Whether Tribunal had erred in holding that appellant could not be granted abatement of duty u/s. 22 of Customs Act? - To claim benefit of abatement u/s. 22, party claiming abatement has to satisfy Assessing Authority that a case had been made out u/s. 22 for abatement of duty on damaged or deteriorated goods - Reduction in price to Rs. 12,01,00,000/- Was not during course of international trade but domestic trade - Adoption of two different values for same goods for purpose of charging duty of customs u/s. 12 of Customs Act and s. 3 of Customs Tariff Act is not only unprecedented but also patently illegal - Held, Tribunal is right in holding that Commr. (Appeals) had erred in giving benefit to appellant for abatement of duty u/s. 22 of Act - Appeal dismissed.
