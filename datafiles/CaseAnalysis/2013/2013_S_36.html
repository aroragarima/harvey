State of West Bengal and others v Sankar Ghosh

Supreme Court of India

28 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Radhakrishnan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 796; (2014) 3 SCC 610; AIR 2014 SC 405; 2014(3) ALL MR 435; 2014 (1) AWC 604; 2013(14) SCALE 245; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 516; 2014 (3) SLR 682

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Calcutta Police Regulations, 1968

Summary: Employment - Dismissal-Reinstatement-Respondent, member of disciplined force-At relevant point of time he was working as Sepoy on deputation with traffic department of Police-Respondent dismissed from service due to his involvement in criminal case, wherein he was charged with offences under Sections 392, 395 and 412 of I.P.C. read with Sections 25 and 27 of Arms Act, 1959 for his complicity in commission of dacoity-Question involved-Whether respondent, who was dismissed from service following disciplinary proceedings, is liable to be reinstated on acquittal by criminal court on ground of identity of charges in departmental as well as criminal proceedings?-Held, "no"-Even if there is identity of charges levelled against respondent before criminal court as well as before Enquiry Officer-Order of discharge or acquittal of police officer by criminal court shall not be bar to award of departmental punishment-Tribunal and High Court committed mistake in holding that since respondent acquitted by criminal court of some charges, reinstatement was automatic-Finding recorded by Tribunal and confirmed by High Court-Erroneous-No rule of automatic reinstatement on acquittal by criminal court even though charges levelled against delinquent before Enquiry Officer as well as criminal court are same-Impugned order of High Court affirming order of Tribunal-Set aside.
