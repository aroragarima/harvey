Collector of Bombay v Nusserwanji Rattanji Mistri and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 February 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Vivian Bose, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 93; AIR 1955 SC 298; 1955 (57) BomLR 723; 1955 SCJ 339; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 1311

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay City Land Revenue Act, 1876, s. 8 - Foras Act, 1851, s. 2 - Land Acquisition Act, 1857 - Crown Grants Act, 1895, s. 3 - (A) What effect acquisition proceedings taken by Govt. under Land Acquisition Act during years 1864 to 1867 have on rights of parties? - Held, effect of land acquisition proceedings was only to extinguish rights of occupants in lands and to vest them absolutely in Govt. and right of latter to levy assessment was not subject-matter of those proceedings - If afterwards lands were not assessed to revenue, it was because there could be no question of Govt. levying assessment on its own lands - (B) Whether sale deed imposes any limitation on right of Crown to assess lands? - Held, there is no recital in deed that purchasers are entitled to hold lands free of assessment, infact a clause in deed clearly negatives such a right - (C) Whether assessment which Govt. was entitled to levy u/s. 8 of the Act was limited to what was payable under Foras Act and had no right to levy assessment at a rate exceeding same? - Held, when lands were acquired, entire 'estate, right, title and interest' subsisting thereon became extinguished, and lands vested in Govt. absolutely free from Foras tenure and when they were sold by Govt., purchasers obtained them as freehold and not as Foras lands - On repeal of Foras Act long prior to sale deed, rights under Foras Act were no longer available in respect of lands - Further held, right of Govt. to levy assessment u/s. 8 of Act is not limited by any right in respondents - Appeal allowed.
