State Of Madhya Pradesh v Man Singh And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

13 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 626; (2003) 10 SCC 414; 2003 (3) CCR 158; 2003 (3) Crimes 381; 2003 (6) CRJ 642; 2003 (3) JCC 1339; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 252; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 232; 2003(6) SCALE 429; 2003 (4) SLT 927; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 460; 2003 (6) Supreme 202

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 34, 304, 323, 324 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - ss. 157, 164 - Acquittal by High Court alleging deficiencies in the prosecution version - Held, minor discrepancies do not corrode credibility of otherwise acceptable evidence. The circumstances highlighted by the High Court to attach vulnerability to evidence of the injured witnesses are clearly inconsequential. It is a settled position in law that omission to mention the name of the assailants in the requisition memo perforce does not render prosecution version brittle - Further held, looking at the nature of injuries and the manner of assaults established by evidence, s. 302 IPC has clear application, and not s. 304, Part II as contended.
