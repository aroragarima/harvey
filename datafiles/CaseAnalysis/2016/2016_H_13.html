Union of India and others v Hamdard (Waqf) Laboratories

Supreme Court of India

25 February 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 292; (2016) 6 SCC 621; AIR 2016 SC 1124; 2016 (333) E.L.T. 193; JT 2016 (3) SC 93; 2016(3) SCALE 15; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 744

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Excise Duty, Duty Of Excise, Central Excise Act, 1944, Refund Of Duty, Central Excises And Customs Laws (Amendment) Act, 1991, Beverage, Central Excise Tariff Act, 1986

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Interest on refund duty amount - Assessee filed appeal before SC assailing demand of differential duty on Rooh Afza, and after adverting to issue on classification of Rooh Afza, SC allowed appeal and observed that Sharbat Rooh Afza is a non-alcoholic beverage and that it would fall under tariff heading 2201.90 - Later, assessee filed application for grant of refund and amount of Rs.3,74,00,000 was refunded but as no interest was paid, assessee filed petition before HC wherein HC opined that liability for payment of interest is statutory and accordingly allowed petition with costs - Hence, instant appeal by Revenue - Whether there was delay in grant of refund and consequently, assessee is entitled to payment of interest on refund amount.

Held, it is obligatory on the part of the Revenue to intimate the assessee to remove the deficiencies in the application within two days and, in any event, if there are still deficiencies, it could proceed with adjudication and reject the application for refund. However, the adjudicatory process could not be carried on beyond three months and the same is required to be concluded within three months. Ranbaxy Laboratories Limited, 2011 INDLAW SC 752 followed. Hence, there is no infirmity in orders of HC and so, the same is upheld. Appeal dismissed.
