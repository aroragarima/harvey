Dr. T. V. Jose v P. M. Chacko Alias Thankachan and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19947; (2001) 8 SCC 748; (2002) SCC (Cr) 94; 2001 (2) ACC 626; AIR 2001 SC 3939; 2002 (1) AllCJ 770; 2001 (2) AllCriC 626; [2001] 107 Comp Cas 406; 2002 (1) CompLJ 55; 2002 (1) CRC 81; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 345; JT 2001 (8) SC 179; 2001 (3) KLT 633; 2002 (131) PLR 159; 2002 (1) RCR(Civil) 120; 2001(6) SCALE 506; 2001 (4) SCJ 20; 2001 (6) SLT 825; [2001] Supp3 S.C.R. 366; 2001 (7) Supreme 257

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Road Traffic Act, 1972, Gratuitous, Motor Vehicle (Passenger Insurance) Act, 1971

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 - Motor Vehicle (Passenger Insurance) Act, 1971, s. 95 - Road Traffic Act, 1972 - One Anthony Alexander succumbed to his injuries as a result of a car accident - Respondents nos. 1 to 6 are LRs. of said Anthony Alexander - Appellant claimed that on date of accident car belonged to Roy Thomas - High Court held that appellant was owner of car - (A) Whether HC was wrong in ignoring oral evidence on record, which clearly showed that appellant was not owner of car on date of accident? - Held, HC was not right in holding that appellant continued to be owner as name had not been changed in records of RTO - There can be transfer of title by payment of consideration and delivery of car - Evidence on record shows that ownership of car had been transferred - However appellant still continued to remain liable to third parties as his name continued in records of RTO as owner - Appellant could not escape that liability by merely joining Mr Roy Thomas in these appeals - (B) Whether even in a third party policy insurance company would be liable for a claim by a passenger in car? - Further held, where policy is not merely a statutory policy, terms of policy have to be considered to determine liability of insurer - It is not required that a policy of insurance should cover risk to passengers who are not carried for hire or reward - As u/s. 95 risk to a passenger in a vehicle who is not carried for hire or reward is not required to be insured plea of counsel for insurance company will have to be accepted and insurance Co. held not liable under requirements of Motor Vehicles Act - Appeals dismissed.
