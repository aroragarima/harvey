Ramanathan v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

18 April 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Shingal, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 170; (1978) 3 SCC 86; (1978) SCC (Cr) 341; AIR 1978 SC 1204; 1978 CRLJ 1137; 1978 CrLR(SC) 318; 1979 (1) MLJ(SC) 1; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 694

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Keywords: Turban, Microscope

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.302, 307 and 460 - Arms Act, s. 27 - Appellant murdered deceased and his son and also injured wife of deceased - Assault was caused at residence of deceased - Convicted u/s. 302 (on two counts), s. 307 and s. 460, IPC and s. 27 of the Arms Act - Conviction challenged - (A) Identification parades - Identification parades have been in common use for a very long time, for the object of placing a suspect in a line up with other persons for identification is to find out whether he is the perpetrator of the crime - Held, there was satisfactory evidence to prove that at least two of the witnesses emphatically claimed from very beginning of the incident that they had noticed culprit and had in fact described him and had claimed that they could identify him, the holding of a test identification parade was absolutely necessary - Fact that such a parade was held within two days of the arrest of the appellant and was held by a Judicial Magistrate with all the necessary precautions and arrangements, leaves no room for doubt trial the evidence of the test identification was of considerable importance - (B) Conviction - Held, wife of deceased witnesses whole occurrence and appellant and she was also assaulted by appellant and was injured - Prosecution case is proved beyond reasonable doubt - Prosecution has led satisfactory evidence to prove that the appellant was an expert who had taken regular training in the use of firearms and had passed it with credit - Order of conviction was well reasoned and justified - Appeal dismissed.
