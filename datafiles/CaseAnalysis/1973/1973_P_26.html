Hira Nath Misra and Others v Principal, Rajendra Medical College, Ranchi and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

D.G. Palekar

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 25; (1973) 1 SCC 805; AIR 1973 SC 1260; 1973 (2) LLJ 111; 1974 (1) SCJ 223

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Women & Children - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Applicability of Rules of natural justice - Enquiry into complaint of inmates of Girls hostel against certain male students of that college about night raid without clothes - Statement of girl students, if required to be recorded in presence of male students - When committee whose integrity could not be impeached, collected and sifted evidence given by girls - Principles of natural justice held, are not inflexible and may differ in different circumstances - When a proper enquiry is held by an enquiry committee consisting of three respectable and independent members of staff as appointed by Principal of a Medical College to enquire into complaint of inmates of girls hostel against certain male students of that college about their indecent behaviour with them in hostel compound itself during odd hours of night, in such a case rules of natural justice does not require that statement of girl students should be recorded in presence of male students concerned or that latter should be furnished with report of enquiry committee - Appeal dismissed.
