Bihar Public Service Commission and Others v Manoj Kumar Pandey and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1828; (1996) 11 SCC 664; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 653; AIR 1997 SC 293; AIR 1997 SCW 41; 1997 (75) FLR 116; JT 1996 (10) SC 246; 1996(8) SCALE 200; 1997 (1) SLR 50; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 280

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Education Norms

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 320 - (A) Validity of decision of Public Service Commission - Decision to have centralised valuation of answer books adopted by majority of members present - Subsequently on circulation of note prepared by Secy., majority of members felt that matter of centralised valuation may be reconsidered - Whether that means that decision taken earlier stood nullified? - (B) Functioning of Public Service Commission - Whether appointment of examiners made on basis of panel approved by committee constituted in accordance with rule suffers from any illegality? - Held, (A) That does not mean that decision stood nullified - (B) Appointment cannot be treated suffered from any illegality - Appeal allowed.
