Â Minerva Mills Limited and others v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

31 July 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, A.C. Gupta, N.L. Untwalia, P.S. Kailasam, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 622; (1980) 3 SCC 625; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 206

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Sick Textile Undertakings (Nationalisation) Act, 1974, Constitutionality, Constitution (Seventeenth Amendment) Act, 1964, Constitution (Twenty-Ninth Amendment) Act, 1972, Constitution (Thirty-Ninth Amendment) Act, 1975, Constitution (Fourth Amendment) Act, 1955, Resettlement of Displaced Persons (Land Acquisition) Act, 1948, United Provinces Land Acquisition (Rehabilitation of Refugees) Act, 1948, Constitution (Thirty-Eighth Amendment) Act, 1975, House of the People (Extension of Duration) Act, 1976, Constitution (First Amendment) Bill, Bihar Displaced Persons Rehabilitation (Acquisition of Land) Act, 1950, Constitution of India Bill, 1895, Irish Constitution, 1937, Maharashtra Agricultural Lands (Ceiling on Holdings) Amendment Act, 1975, Maharashtra Agricultural Lands (Lowering of Ceiling on Holdings) (Amendment) Act, 1975, Maharashtra Agricultural Lands (Lowering of Veiling on Holdings) and (Amendment) Act, 1972

Summary: (A) Constitution - Constitution (42nd Amendment) Act, 1976, ss. 4, 55 - Industries (Development and Regulation) Act, 1951, s. 15, 18A - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 31B, 31C - Sick Textile Undertakings (Nationalisation) Act, 1974, ss. 3(2), 39 - Amendment of Constitution - Power of Parliament - Validity - Petitioner no. 1 was company owned textile undertaking this undertaking was nationalized and taken over by Central Govt. under provisions of the 1974 Act - Central Govt. appointed Committee u/s. 15 of the 1951 Act to make full and complete investigation of affairs of petitioner no. 1 - Committee submitted report based on which Central Govt. passed order u/s. 18A of the 1951 Act, authorizing respondent no. 2 to take over management of petitioner no. 1 on ground that its affairs were being managed in manner highly detrimental to public interest - Hence, instant petitions - Whether ss. 4, 55 of the 1976 Act was valid - Held, S. 4 & 55 of the Constitution 42nd Amendment Act was beyond amending power of Parliament and was void since it damages basic or essential features of Constitution and destroys its basic structure by a total exclusion of challenge to any law on the ground that it is inconsistent with, or takes away or abridges any of the rights conferred by Art. 14 or Art. 19 of the Constitution, if the law was for giving effect to policy of State towards securing all or any of the principles laid down in Part IV of the Constitution - Since it removes all limitations on power of Parliament to amend Constitution and confers power upon it so as to damage or destroy its basic or essential features or its basic structure - Petitions disposed of.

(B) Constitution - Constitution (42nd Amendment) Act, 1976, ss. 4, 55 - Constitution of India, 1950, Part III & IV - Hormony - Fundamental Rights - Directive Principles - Whether directive principles of State policy contained in Art IV can have primacy over fundamental rights conferred by Part III of the Constitution - Held, significance of perception that Parts III and IV together constitute core of commitment to social revolution and they, together, were conscience of Constitution was to be traced to deep understanding of scheme of the Indian Constitution - Indian Constitution was founded on the bed-rock of balance between Parts III and IV - To give absolute primacy to one over other was to disturb harmony of the Constitution - This harmony and balance between fundamental rights and directive principles was an essential feature of basic structure of the Constitution - Petitions disposed of.
