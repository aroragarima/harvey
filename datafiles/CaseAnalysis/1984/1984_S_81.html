H. D. Vora v State Of Maharashtra & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 112; (1984) 2 SCC 337; AIR 1984 SC 866; 1984 (2) Bom.C.R. 239; 1984 (2) RCJ 311; 1984 (1) RCR(Rent) 457; 1984(1) SCALE 353; [1984] 2 S.C.R. 693; 1984 UJ 381

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land and Property - Bombay Land Requisition Act, 1948, s. 6(4)(a) - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Appeal against the order of HC directing authorities to deregulation the flat and to take steps to evict the appellant(allottee tenant) and to handover possession to respondent (purchaser of building) - Purchaser challenging the validity of the order of requisition - Grounds, it did not set out the public purpose for requisitioned - (A) Question arise whether an order of requisition of premises can be continued for an indefinite period of time or it must necessarily be of temporary duration - Held, order of requisition even if it was valid when made, ceased to be valid and effective after the expiration of a reasonable period of time (30 years ) - (B) Allottee giving amount directly to landlord - Neither a govt. servant nor a (but was) homeless person - Whether he became direct tenant - Held, appellant came into occupation as an allottee under the order of requisition u/s. 6(4)(a) of 1948 Act -It did not have the effect of putting an end to the order of requisition - Appeal dismissed.
