Sujoy Sen @ Sujoy Kr. Sen v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1619; (2007) 6 SCC 32; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 47; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 704; 2007 (104) CLT 806; 2007 CRLJ 3727; 2007 (2) JCC 1547; JT 2007 (6) SC 465; 2007(6) SCALE 560; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 112

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant/accused allegedly murdered the deceased - Trial Court convicted the appellant for murder - HC confirmed the conviction in appeal - Hence the instant appeal - Whether the conviction in the instant case was sustainable - Held, first informant in the FIR stated that the appellant had entered into his house during his absence, however, he did not mention appellant's name in the FIR - Had the first informant seen the accused entering into the house at the time of the incident he would have definitely mentioned the fact in the FIR - Statement of PW4 that he saw the accused entering into the house of the first informant could not be believed as he had not stated that fact in his statement to the Police recorded u/s. 161 CrPC - A vital link, therefore, in the chain of circumstances was missing in the instant case - Prosecution had not been able to prove the chain of circumstances linking the appellant to the crime beyond reasonable doubt, hence the appellant was entitled to the benefit of doubt - Impugned orders of the Courts below were set aside - Appeal allowed.
