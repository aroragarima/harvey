Bhupinderpal Singh and Others v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 567; (2000) 5 SCC 262; (2000) SCC (L&S) 639; AIR 2000 SC 2011; 2000 (85) FLR 902; JT 2000 (5) SC 557; 2000 LabIC 1980; 2000 (2) LLN 870; 2000(4) SCALE 1; 2000 (2) SLR 608; 2000 (4) SLT 793; 2000 (4) Supreme 644; 2000 (3) UPLBEC 1909

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Music

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16 and 142 - Appointment - Eligibility - Cut off date by reference, how to be reckoned? - Held, cut off date by reference is date set down by relevant service rules and if there be no cut off date appointed by rules then such date as may be appointed for purpose in advertisement calling for applications - If there is no such date appointed then eligibility criteria shall be applied by reference to last date appointed by which applications have to be received by competent authority - Case law discussed - Not entitled to be allowed to continue - Appeal allowed.
