B. Leelavathi v Honnamma and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 397; (2005) 11 SCC 115; 2005 (60) ALR 148; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 142; JT 2005 (11) SC 589; 2005 (99) RD 113; 2005(4) SCALE 729; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 146

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Bangalore Development Authority Act, s. 64 - Bangalore Development Authority (3rd Amendment) Act, 1993, s. 9 - Suit for declaration of ownership of suit property - HC decreed suit and set aside sale deed holding that sale deed in favour of appellant as void and not binding on respondent no 1 - Whether order of HC sustainable? - Held, HC has erroneously set aside judgment and decree passed by trial Court on ground of non-issuance of notice by BDA to plaintiff-respondent before executing sale deed in favour of appellant and that plaintiff-respondent had perfected her title by way of adverse possession - Finding recorded by HC that plaintiff-respondent had issued a valid notice u/s. 64 to BDA before filing of suit is contrary to evidence present on record - Judgment of HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
