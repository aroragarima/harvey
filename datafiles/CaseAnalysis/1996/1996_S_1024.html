Rameshwar Dass Gupta v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2356; (1996) 5 SCC 728; 1996 (6) AD(SC) 630; AIR 1997 SC 410; 1997 (1) AWC 126; 1996 (3) CCC 373; JT 1996 (7) SC 657; 1996 (2) LLN 925; 1996 (87) RD 490; 1996(6) SCALE 374; 1997 (1) SLJ 50; 1996 (5) SLR 667; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 224; 1996 (6) Supreme 640

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 47 - Powers of executing court in execution of decree - Amount due to dismissed employee calculated by taking arrears of salary, gratuity, pension etc., and certain sum decreed in favour of employee - Executing court granting decree for interest which was not part of decree for execution on ground of delay and unreasonable stand taken in execution - Whether said decree is without jurisdiction? - Held, without jurisdiction - Executing court cannot travel beyond decree under execution - Petition dismissed.
