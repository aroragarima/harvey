Sankalchan Jaychandbhai Patel and Others v Vithalbhai Jaychandbhai Patel and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2411; (1996) 6 SCC 433; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 721; 1996 (4) CLT 201; JT 1996 (9) SC 110; 1996(7) SCALE 481; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 333; 1996 (7) Supreme 658

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Bombay Revenue Jurisdiction Act, 1876 - Whether s. 11 is a bar for maintainability of suit? - There is a prohibition on Civil Court to entertain any suit against Govt. on account of any act or omission of any Revenue Officer, unless plaintiff first proves that he previously brought it by way of an appeal before competent authority and within time prescribed - Without availing of that remedy, he cannot present suit against State s. 9 of CPC does not expressly or by necessary implication, prohibits jurisdiction of civil court to entertain suit based on title - Mutation entries are only to enable State to collect revenues from persons in possession and enjoyment of property and that right, title and interest as to property should be established dehors entries - Entries are only one of modes of proof of enjoyment of property - Mutation entries do not create any title or interest therein - Held, view taken by learned Single Judge, with due respect, is not correct in law - Civil suit is clearly maintainable - High Court rightly granted injunction restraining appellants from alienating land - Appeal dismissed.
