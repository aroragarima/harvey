Hiralal Pandey And Ors. v State Of U.P. …..

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 128; (2012) 5 SCC 216; AIR 2012 SC 2541; 2012 CRLJ 2421; 2012 (3) JCC 1671; JT 2012 (4) SC 117; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 127; 2012 (3) RCR(Criminal) 120; 2012(4) SCALE 369; [2012] 3 S.C.R. 1066

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 323, 325, 147, 302, 34 - Murder - Eye-Witnesses - Reliability - Conviction - Sustainability - FIR against appellants was lodged for offence u/ss. 323, 325, 147 of IPC - Charge sheet was filed u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Trial Court, relying on evidence of PW1 and PW2, convicted appellants - Appellants filed appeal before HC, which affirmed conviction and sentence of appellants - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether Trial Court was justified in convicting appellants on evidence of PW1 and PW2 - Held, no definite evidence was there to come to conclusion that time of incident in FIR was first written in pencil and was thereafter erased and again written and that time of incident as recorded in FIR was doubtful - Medical evidence supported eyewitness accounts of PW1 and PW2 - SC, in State of Uttar Pradesh v. Bhagwant Kishore Joshi, 1963 INDLAW SC 268, held that it was necessary for accused to throw a reasonable doubt that prosecution evidence was such that it should have been manipulated or shaped by reason of irregularity in matter of investigation, or that he was prevented by reason of such irregularity from putting forward his defence or adducing evidence in support thereof, but where prosecution evidence had been held to be true and where accused had full say in matter, conviction should not obviously be set aside on ground of every irregularity or illegality in matter of investigation - Unless lapses on part of investigation were such as to cast reasonable doubt about prosecution story or seriously prejudice defence of accused, Court should not set aside conviction - Appeal dismissed.
