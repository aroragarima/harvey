State Of Uttar Pradesh And Ors. v Dr. Anupam Gupta Etc.

Supreme Court of India

13 February 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 334; (1993) Supp1 SCC 594; AIR 1992 SC 932; AIR 1992 SCW 746; 1992 (3) AWC 1804; JT 1992 (4) SC 422; 1992(1) SCALE 332; [1992] 1 S.C.R. 643; 1992 (2) SLR 67; 1992 (2) UJ 378; 1992 (2) UPLBEC 1288

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Equality of opportunity, Medical Council, Equality, Meritorious

Summary: Education - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 133 and 226 - Admission to Post Graduate Medical Courses - (A) Entrance - Prescription of 50% marks to general candidates and 40% to SC and ST together with 1.65% weightage of total marks - Constitutionality - (B) Appeal - New plea - Pure question of law arising from record - (C) Scheme for admission - Permissibility as to outweigh - (D) Vacancies of seats - If admission in midstream can be given on its basis - Held - (A) Is constitutional and valid - (B) Though not raised before High Court can be considered for first time in appeal by Supreme Court - (C) Technicality would not be permitted to outweigh salutary scheme in larger public interest - (D) Not a ground to give admission in midstream - Appeal allowed.
