Dcm Financial Services Limited v J.N. Sareen and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 938; (2008) 8 SCC 1; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 401; AIR 2008 SC 2255; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 2272; 2008 (6) Bom.C.R. 504; [2008] 144 Comp Cas 55; 2008 (4) CompLJ 265; 2008 CRLJ 3178; 2008 (2) JCC (NI) 233; JT 2008 (6) SC 609; 2008 (2) KLT 762; 2009 (1) MPLJ 593; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 270; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 152; 2008(8) SCALE 54; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 603

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal

Keywords: Legal Notice, Post Dated Cheque, Post Dated Cheques

Summary: Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 141 - Effect of a post dated cheque vis-a-vis prosecution in terms of s. 141 of NI Act, 1881 in issue - Whether a signatory of the cheque would come within the purview of s. 141 of the Act? - Held, specific averments in the complaint petition so as to satisfy the requirements of s. 141 of the Act are imperative - Mere fact that at one point of time some role has been played by the accused may not by itself be sufficient to attract the constructive liability u/s. 141 of the Act - A person who had resigned with the knowledge of the complainant in 1996 could not be a person incharge of the Company in 1998 when the cheque was dishonoured - Appeal dismissed.
