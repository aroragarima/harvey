Arun S/o Shankar Dokhe and another v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 445; 2017(4) ALL MR 958; 2017(6) SCALE 54

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Dismissed, Maharashtra Zilla Parishads and Panchayat Samitis Act, 1961, Maharashtra Zilla Parishads and Panchayat Samitis (Manner and Rotation of Reservation of Seats) Rules, 1996

Summary: Election - Maharashtra Zilla Parishads and Panchayat Samitis (Manner and Rotation of Reservation of Seats) Rules, 1996, rr. 6, 9 and 10 - Maharashtra Zilla Parishads and Panchayat Samitis Act, 1961, s. 12 (2) (d) - Local government election - Reservation to electoral division - Legality - Appellant filed writ petition seeking to de-reserve block in question, which was reserved for Other Backward Class category (Women) - HC dismissed writ petition in view of declaration of election schedule and programme by respondents and held that there was constitutional bar to entertain writ petition - Petitioners contended that State Authorities, including Election Commission, failed to abide by the mandate of rr. 6, 9 and 10 of the Rules - Further, s. 12 of the Act provides for division of District into Electoral Divisions - Further, s. 12 (2) (d) of the Act predicated that one half (including the number of seats reserved for women SC, ST and the category of Backward Class citizens) of total number of seats to be filled in by direct election in a Zilla Parishad should be reserved for women and such seats should be allotted 'by rotation' to different Electoral Divisions in Zilla Parishad.

Held, scheme regarding rotation of seats reserved for women or backward class of citizens etc., is a very complex process. Further, election process of phase one has concluded with declaration of results in respect of other Electoral Divisions, the same cannot be undermined. Any attempt to make adjustment even in respect of one constituency i.e. electoral division in question, would create an imbalance in the ratio of seats to be reserved for the respective categories as mandated by the provisions of the Act and the Rules. Further, Petitioners pointed out dichotomy in the provisions of the Act, in particular s. 12(2) (d), which postulates allotment of seats by rotation to different Electoral Divisions in a Zilla Parishad whereas the Rules provides for allocation of seats by drawing of lots. There appears to be some confusion if not conflict in the case of reconciling the mandate of reservation of seats for women in terms of s. 12(2) (d) and reservation of seats for women in reserved categories in terms of the Rules. The fact remains that the validity of provisions contained in the Rules has not been challenged in the writ petition. Further, the election schedule notified on 11-1-2017 by the State Election Commission has proceeded to its logical end in respect of all other Electoral Divisions of the concerned Zilla Parishad/Panchayat Samitis. There is no challenge to those elections nor those elections have been made subject to the outcome of the present appeals. As a result, no effective relief can be granted to the Petitioners. Thus, any attempt to do so would inevitably upset the ratio of reservation of seats for women in the other Electoral Divisions where the election process is complete. Further, accepting the Petitioners' prayer in the writ petition at this stage would result in creating an imbalance in that ratio, which will be in violation of the letter and spirit of the law. Petition dismissed.
