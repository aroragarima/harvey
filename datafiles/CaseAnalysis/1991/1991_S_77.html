F.A. Sapa v Singora and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 May 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, Veeraswami Ramaswami, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 246; (1991) 3 SCC 375; AIR 1991 SC 1557; AIR 1991 SCW 1492; JT 1991 (2) SC 503; 1991(1) SCALE 939; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 752; 1991 (2) UJ 692

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure 1908, Allegations Of Corrupt Practice, Source Of Information, High Court Rules, Purity

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 81(1), 81(3), 83, 86, 86(1) and 86(5) - Conduct of Election Rules, 1961, r. 1 - Election petition - (A) Amendment - Sought within purview of s. 86(5) - (B) Mere defect in verification - Whether fatal to maintainability? - (C) Whether Schedule or Annexure constitutes integral part of petition? - Held, (A) High Court should be liberal in allowing same - (B) Is not fatal to maintainability of Petition - (C) Constitutes integral part of election petition - Provision of s. 81(3) can be said to be complied within letter and spirit - Requirements of s. 81(3) must be satisfied - HC should direct petitioner to cure defects within time stipulated by it - Appeal partly allowed.
