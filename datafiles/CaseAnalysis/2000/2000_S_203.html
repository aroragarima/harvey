Shyamdeo Pd. Singh v Nawal Kishore Yadav

Supreme Court of India

28 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2835; (2000) 8 SCC 46; AIR 2000 SC 3000; JT 2000 (9) SC 486; 2000(6) SCALE 135; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 668

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Delimitation Of Constituencies, Ordinarily Resident, Bihar Intermediate Education Council Act, 1992, Electors Registration Rules, 1960

Summary: Representation of People Act 1951, ss. 21, 27(5), 62 and 100 - Representation of People Act, 1950, s. 16 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 329(b) - Electors Registration Rules, 1960, r. 31(3) - Whether illegality or irregularity in preparation of electoral roll, can be a ground to declare election void? - Whether delay in disposal of objections related with electoral roll should be deprecated? - Whether person enrolled can be prohibited from voting? - Held, this cannot be a ground to declare election void - Delay in disposal of objections has to be deprecated - Person enrolled cannot be prohibited from voting, unless he is covered either by term of disqualification or by not being qualified.
