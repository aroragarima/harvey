Hardeo Singh v State of Bihar and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 518; (2000) 5 SCC 623; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1008; AIR 2000 SC 2245; 2000 (2) CCR 218; 2000 (3) Crimes 1; 2000 CRLJ 2978; 2000 (2) JCC 505; JT 2000 (7) SC 30; 2000 (3) LLN 541; 2000 (2) RCR(Criminal) 870; 2000(4) SCALE 595; 2000 (4) SLT 733; [2000] Supp1 S.C.R. 222; 2000 (4) Supreme 261

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Illegal Gratification

Summary: Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120 - B - Quashing of charge - Illegal gratification - Against action beyond power - Accused, Branch Manager of Bank - No direct evidence to support prosecution - Held, natural inference may be that accused took advantage of his position - Providing overdraft facility upon some other consideration - Some connecting link or connecting factor somewhere is good enough for framing of charge - Refusal to quash proceedings.
