S. K. Rathi v Prem Hari Sharma and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3023; (2001) 9 SCC 377; (2002) SCC (L&S) 91; 2000 (41) ALR 109; 2000 (4) AWC 2713; 2000 (3) CLR 31; JT 2000 (8) SC 267; 2000 (33) LabIC 3677; 2000 (5) SLR 212; 2000 (3) UPLBEC 2433

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Whether respondent no. 1 is entitled to continue as an acting Principal of College till 30-06-2000 as has been ordered by High Court? - Held, as per a decision of Govt. contained in document D/-16-02-1999, in which it was, inter alia, stated that for teachers like respondent no. 1 age of superannuation was 60 years - Said decision further states that no extension in service shall be granted but 'if date of superannuation of a teacher does not fall on June 30, teacher shall continue in service till end of academic session i.e., June 30, following - This is clause on which reliance is placed by learned Counsel in support of decision of HC - This decision would enable respondent no 1 to continue as a teacher, which is his substantive appointment, up to 30 th June, following day when he attained age of 60 years, but this clause cannot allow him to continue as an acting Principal which is a different post altogether - There is no decision of Govt. giving extension beyond age of 60 years to a Principal - This being so, appeal is allowed.
