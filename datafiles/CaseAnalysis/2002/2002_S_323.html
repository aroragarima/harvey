Allarakha K. Mansuri v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 96; (2002) 3 SCC 57; (2002) SCC (Cr) 519; AIR 2002 SC 1051; 2002 (1) CCR 163; 2002 (1) Crimes 322; 2002 (3) CRJ 249; 2002 CRLJ 1489; JT 2002 (2) SC 63; 2002 (1) RCR(Criminal) 748; 2002(2) SCALE 131; 2002 (2) SCJ 38; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 1011; 2002 (2) SLT 21; 2002 (1) Supreme 622; 2002 (1) UC 372

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: If, the judgment of the trial court shows that the view taken by it was uncalled for, not based upon the facts of the case or the legal evidence tendered in the case and was the result of conjectures, imagination and hypothesis, then, the High Court was justified in re-examining the whole evidence produced in the case and to hold that the conclusions arrived at by the trial court were factually and legally incorrect.
