Hotel and Restaurant Association v Star India Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

24 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1054; (2006) 13 SCC 753; AIR 2007 SC 1168; 2007 (5) AWC 4932; 2007 (1) CCC 290; 2007 (1) CompLJ 46; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 511; 2006(12) SCALE 543; 2007 (5) S.T.R. 161; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 602

Case Digest

Subject: Telecom

Keywords: Register of Interconnect Agreements (Broadcasting and Cable Services) Regulation, 2004, Indian Tariff Act, Imports and Exports Control Act, Cotton Textile Cess Act, 1948, Cotton Textiles Companies (Management of Undertakings and Liquidation or Reconstruction) Act, 1967

Summary: Telecom - Cable Television Networks (Regulation) Act, 1995 - Telecom Regulatory Authority of India Act, 1997, ss. 11 and 14 - Register of Interconnect Agreements (Broadcasting and Cable Services) Regulation, 2004 - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Indian Telegraph Act, 1885 - Indian Wireless Telegraphy Act, 1933 - Indian Tariff Act - (A) Whether members of appellants' associations are consumers and, thus, were entitled to invoke jurisdiction of TDSAT in terms of s. 14 of Telecom Regulatory Authority of India Act? - Held, guests in Hotels do not have any privity of contract with broadcasters or cable operators - Their identity is not known to broadcasters or cable operators - A guest may not watch TV or in fact room may remain unoccupied but amount under contract by owners of hotels whether with broadcasters or cable operators remains unchanged - Thus it can be concluded that members of appellants' associations are consumers - Further, what is excluded is a complaint of an individual consumer and not a group of consumers - Thus, indisputably, TDSAT would be entitled to entertain a complaint by a group of consumers against a service provider - TRAI directed to carry out process of framing tariff - Appeal allowed.
