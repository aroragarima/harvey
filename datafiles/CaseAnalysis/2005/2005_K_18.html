K. Kalimuthu v State By DSP

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 773; (2005) 4 SCC 512; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1291; AIR 2005 SC 2257; 2005 CRLJ 2190; 2005 (2) JhrCR(SC) 254; JT 2005 (11) SC 48; 2005 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 474; 2005 (2) RCR(Criminal) 463; 2005(3) SCALE 566; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 197 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 197 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 5(1)(d), 5(2) - Public Servants - Corruption Charges - Sustainability - Appellants were charged with various offences under IPC and s. 5(2) r/w s. 5(1)(d) of the 1947 Act - Appellants contended that in absence of requisite sanction u/s. 197 CrPC, it was beyond jurisdiction of Court to take cognizance of alleged offences - Order passed by Special Judge, in favour of appellant in appeal relating was set aside rejected - Consequently, HC rejected plea raised by appellants about protections available u/s. 197 CrPC - Appellants contended that HC failed to notice true scope and ambit of s. 197 CrPC - Hence, instant appeals - Whether protection u/s. 197 CrPC, criminal proceedings for offences alleged to have been committed by appellants while appellants were acting or purporting to act as public servants, was available to appellants - Held, protection u/s. 197 CrPC had certain limits and was available only when alleged act done by public servant was reasonably connected with discharge of his official duty and was not merely cloak for doing objectionable act - Before s. 197 CrPC could be invoked, it should be shown that official concerned was accused of offence alleged to have been committed by him while acting or purporting to act in discharge of his official duties - Concept of s. 197 CrPC did not get immediately attracted on institution of complaint case - So far public servants were concerned, cognizance of any offence, by any Court, was barred by s. 197 CrPC unless sanction was obtained from appropriate authority, if offence, alleged to have been committed, was in discharge of official duty - It was prima facie found that act or omission for which appellants were charged had reasonable connection with discharge of their duty then it should be held to official to which applicability of s. 197 CrPC should not be disputed - Question relating to need of sanction u/s. 197 CrPC was not necessarily be considered as soon as complaint was lodged, however, question might arise at any stage of proceeding - Hence, there was no infirmity in judgment of HC declining to consider applicability of S. 197 CrPC at present juncture - Appeals disposed of.
