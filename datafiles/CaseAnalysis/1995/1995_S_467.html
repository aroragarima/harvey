Khoday Distilleries Limited and Others v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, K. Ramaswamy, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 894; (1996) 10 SCC 304; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 278; AIR 1996 SC 911; JT 1995 (9) SC 449; 1995(7) SCALE 262; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 759

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Karnataka Excise Act, 1966, s. 71 - Karnataka Excise (Sale of Indian and Foreign Liquors) Rules, 1968, r. 3(11) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Andhra Pradesh Distillery Rules, 1970, r. 2(11) - Distributor licence - Provision for granting it to state owned Co. - Compulsion on manufacturers of liquors to sale liquor to state owned Co. - Loss of rebate granted earlier on export outside country outside state - Held, Act itself provides that number of licences can be regulated by state - Neither rule is outside purview of Act - Nor provision outside purview of rule - Manufacturers of liquor could not complain that rules are arbitrary when all were similarly affected - Not violative of art. 14.
