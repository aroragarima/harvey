Messrs Ballabhdas Agarwal v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 376; AIR 1966 SC 814; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 967; 1962 (13) STC 278; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 967

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1947, s. 6 - Whether in cases where a reference is made to the High Court and the appeal is brought only against the order of ITAT, interference by Supreme Court would, in fact, be setting aside of the judgment of the HC without there being an appeal to the court, is valid? - Held, the appellant is not entitled to agitate the correctness or otherwise of the decision given by the tribunal in regard to the questions which were agitated before the HC and decided against the appellant, against which no appeal has been brought - It has not been shown that there was any such breach of the rules of natural justice or violation of any principal of law which would be a good ground for interference direct with the orders of the Board of Revenue in an appeal u/art. 136 of the Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
