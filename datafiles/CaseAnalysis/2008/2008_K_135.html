Kashi Ram and Others v State Of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 114; (2008) 3 SCC 55; (2008) 1 SCC (Cr) 608; AIR 2008 SC 1172; JT 2008 (2) SC 127; 2008(2) SCALE 67; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 101

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Self Defence, Revenue Record, Mitigating Circumstance, Blood-Stained Earth, Right Of Self Defence, Criminal Law Act, 1967

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 304 Part-II, 304 Part-II r/w/ 149- Appeal against conviction and sentence - Whether accused appellants had right of private defence and this is the case of exceeding the right of private defence meaning thereby, inflicting more harm than it was necessary for the purpose of defence? - Held, according to the findings of courts below, it was clearly a case of exceeding the right of private defence - Appellants indeed inflicted more harm than it was necessary for the purpose of defence - Right of private defence cannot be claimed when the accused are aggressors particularly when the members of the complainant party were totally unarmed - Appeal dismissed.
