British Airways Plc v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

06 November 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 409; (2002) 2 SCC 95; AIR 2002 SC 391; 2003 (89) ECC 770; 2002 (139) E.L.T. 6; JT 2001 (9) SC 544; 2001 (47) RLT 264; 2001(8) SCALE 73; 2001 (8) SLT 130; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 152; 2001 (8) Supreme 138

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Written Order, Gazette Of India, Attorney-General

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, ss. 2(31), 42, 45, 116 and 148 - Whether appellant was 'person incharge' for conveyance liable u/s. 116 of Customs Act? - Accepting submission of appellants that after issuance of an order u/s. 42 of Act no proceedings could be initiated against officer in charge of carrier would amount to negating working of Act and defeating object sought to be achieved by it - Held, even after compliance of provisions of s. 42 if it is found that goods unloaded are short of quantity, designated officer u/s. 116 of Act can proceed with matter and impose penalty after following procedure prescribed under Act - Appeal dismissed.
