State Bank of India and Others v S.N. Goyal

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 798; (2008) 8 SCC 92; AIR 2008 SC 2594; 2008 (3) AWC 2755; 2008 (117) FLR 967; JT 2008 (6) SC 398; 2008 (3) LLJ 567; 2008 (3) LLN 452; 2008 (5) MLJ(SC) 695; 2008(7) SCALE 415; [2008] 7 S.C.R. 631; 2008 (5) SLR 76

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Contract Of Personal Service, Reduction Of Pay

Summary: State Bank of India Officers Service Rules - Misconduct - Removal from service - Trial court directed the appellant Bank to reinstate the respondent - Appeal against - Held, failure on the part of the High Court to take note of the substantial questions of law involved, has led to unwarranted dismissal of the second appeal and calls for interference - Held, minimum pleading that was necessary was that the Appointing Authority acted on extraneous material in arriving the decision or acted on the advice or recommendation of an Authority who was not concerned with the Enquiry - In the absence of appropriate pleading on a particular issue, there can be no adjudication of such issue - Adjudication of a dispute by a civil court is significantly different from the exercise of power of judicial review in a writ proceedings by the High Court - Appeals allowed.
