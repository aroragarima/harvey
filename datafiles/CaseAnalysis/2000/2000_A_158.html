Raipur Development Authority v Anupam Sahkari Griha Nirman Samiti and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Misra, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 239; (2000) 4 SCC 357; JT 2000 (4) SC 60; 2000(3) M.P.H.T. 139; 2000(2) SCALE 703; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 781; 2000 (3) SLT 631; 2000 (3) Supreme 37

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Town and Country Development Act, 1973, Madhya Pradesh Cooperative Housing Act, 1960.

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Madhya Pradesh Cooperative Housing Act, 1960, ss. 14, 24, 25, 26, 27, 28, 29, 30, 31, 32, 53 and 54 - Madhya Pradesh Town and Country Development Act, 1973, ss. 18, 30(5) and 50 - Madhya Pradesh Town and Country Development Rules, 1975, r. 18 - Respondent 1 applied for development of land u/s. 29 on 2-6-1986 - 60 days expire on 2-8-1986 - (A) Whether in view of s. 30(5) of 1973 Act, it could be said it to be a case of deemed permission? - Held, five communications sent to respondent 1 - Letter reveals that period of sixty days had not come to an end - Thus there is no deemed permission - (B) Whether draft scheme was not published within two years from date of publication of declaration u/s. 50(2)? - (1) What would be starting point for computing period of two years? - Held, even if after publication of first intention, either it is given a go-by or otherwise on rethinking, if another such intention is published it would be a valid notice when it is published u/s. 50(2) - (2) If that be so, period of limitation would start from latter such publication? - In present case it would be 6-9-1985 - Publication within a period of two years - Held, when publication in Form XIV in Madhya Pradesh Gazette is made, compliance with form and procedure of this Rule is complete - Publication in one or more local Hindi newspaper as stated in sub-r. (2) of r. 18 is to give due publicity to public at large so that they may file their objections to draft scheme - Though publication in gazette is also a notice to public at large it is always open to legislature, as in present case, to give extra publicity to public through publication in any local daily - Further held, submission that for computing period of two years compliance with publication would only be completed if it is also published simultaneously in local newspaper has no merit - So for counting period of thirty days, it is date when draft scheme is published in newspaper which is to be taken as date of starting point for counting period of thirty days for filing objection and suggestion - Appeal allowed.
