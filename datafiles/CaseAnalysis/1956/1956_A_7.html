Karnani Properties Limited v Augustin

Supreme Court of India

09 November 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 99; AIR 1957 SC 309; 1957 SCJ 177; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 20

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: West Bengal Premises Rent Control (Temporary Provisions) Act, 1950

Summary: Rent control - West Bengal Premises Rent Control (Temporary Provisions) Act, 1950, ss. 2(8) and 9 - Landlord supplied without any additional charge electrical energy for consumption by tenant and was responsible for repairs of electric installations and sanitary fittings -(A)Whether holdings are premises? - Held, definition of 'premises' is in very wide terms and includes not only gardens, grounds and outhouses, if any, appertaining to a building or part of a building, but also furniture supplied by landlord for tenants use and any fittings affixed to building, thus indicating that legislature was providing for all kinds of letting - (B) Whether, if by a stipulation between landlord and tenant, landlord agrees to provide for additional amenities like electric power for consumption and such other facilities, case is taken out of operation of Act? - Held, supply of amenities aforesaid would not make any difference to application of Act to premises in question - 'Rent' included not only what is ordinarily described as rent in an agreement between a landlord and a tenant but also payment in respect of special amenities provided by landlord under agreement between him and his tenant - (C) Which clause of s. 9 applies to premises? - Held, cl.(g) of s. 9 of Act empower Rent Controller and other authorities under Act to determine standard rent after taking into consideration all constituents which make up total sum shown in agreement as monthly rent - Residuary cl.(g) applies to terms of tenancy in these cases - Appeal partly allowed.
