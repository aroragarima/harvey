The Municipal Corporation of Greater Bombay v Shri Laxman Iyer and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 875; (2003) 8 SCC 731; (2004) SCC (Cr) 252; 2003 (3) ACC 551; AIR 2003 SC 4182; 2004 (54) ALR 427; 2004 (1) AWC 176; JT 2003 (8) SC 108; 2004 (1) MLJ(SC) 82; 2004 (136) PLR 446; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 764; 2003(9) SCALE 2; 2003 (1) SCW 5505; 2003 (6) SLT 706; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 984; 2003 (7) Supreme 492

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Consumer Protection; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - s. 110-A - Claim petition - Contributory negligence - Quantum of compensation - Held, considering the age of the claimants, it can never exceed 10 even by the most liberal standards. Worked out on that basis amount comes to Rs. 3.6 lakhs at the monthly expected income fixed by the Tribunal and confirmed by the High Court Further held, looking into the nature of the contributory negligence of the deceased after making an appropriate deduction which can reasonably be fixed at 25%, the compensation amount payable by the Corporation can be fixed at Rs. 3 lakhs including the amount awarded by the Tribunal and confirmed by the High Court for loss of expectation of life. Interest at the rate as awarded by the High Court is maintained from the date of application for compensation.
