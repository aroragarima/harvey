Ali M.K. and Ors. v State of Kerala & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

22 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 384; (2003) 11 SCC 632; (2004) SCC (L&S) 136; AIR 2003 SC 4006; 2003 (97) FLR 1003; 2003 (2) KHCACJ 738; 2003 LabIC 2131; 2003(4) SCALE 197; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 826; 2003 (1) SCW 2931; 2003 (2) SLJ 380; 2003 (4) SLT 118; 2003 (3) Supreme 575

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Kerala State and Subordinate Service Rules, 1958, r. 18, r. 24, r. 28, r. 8 - Scope, content and ambit of - Full Bench judgment of Kerala HC under challenge - Held, normal function of a proviso is to except something out of enactment or to qualify something enacted therein which but for proviso would be within purview of enactment - Vital question is whether appointment is made in exigencies of public service - Further held, Note I is a deeming provision and provides that appointment made in pursuance of an application invited, sponsored or recommended by Govt. or other competent authority shall be deemed to be an appointment made in exigencies of public service for purpose of r. 8 - Full Bench found that Note I applies because appointments of non-official respondents in Co-operative Department were made in pursuance of applications invited, sponsored and recommended by Govt. - Member appointed to another service would not loose his lien on post in original service when not confirmed in subsequent department - Appointment is deemed to be made in public exigency - Candidate thus, not ineligible for promotion in view of Proviso 4 to r. 8 - In view of this factual finding, conclusions are in order - Appeals dismissed.
