Rodemadan India Limited v International Trade Expo Centre Limited

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 131; (2006) 1 SCC 651; AIR 2006 SC 3456; 2006 (3) AWC 2262; 2007 (1) CalLT(SC) 18; 2006 (3) CTC 557; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 3; JT 2006 (5) SC 203; 2006 (4) MLJ(SC) 318; 2006(4) SCALE 385; 2006 (5) SCJ 105

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: [A.] Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 11(6), 2(1)(e), 2(1)(f), 42, 9 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 11, 145, 6 - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 299, 300 - Supreme Court Rules, 1966, o. 7, r. 1 - Application for appointment of a Presiding Arbitrator/ Chairperson of Arbitral Tribunal under arbitration agreement - Whether preliminary conditions necessary for exercise of designate's power u/s.11(6) are satisfied, especially whether there exists a valid arbitral agreement? - What kind of evidence is designate u/s.11(6) required to place reliance on to arrive at a finding on preliminary jurisdictional issues? - Held, arbitral agreement was not vitiated on any of grounds - There exists a valid arbitration agreement which contemplates that that all disputes between parties under that agreement be referred to arbitration - Dispute between Petitioner, which is a foreign company and Respondent and is therefore, an 'International Commercial Arbitration' within meaning of s.2(1)(f) - There is a dispute between parties where both parties are subject to an arbitration agreement - Appointed arbitrators have failed to reach an agreement upon a Chairperson/ Presiding Arbitrator of Arbitral Tribunal - All preliminary conditions specified in s.11(6) have been met - Petition allowed.
