Indian and Eastern Newspaper Society v Commissioner of Income Tax, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

31 August 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, P.N. Bhagwati, V.D. Tulzapurkar

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 138; (1979) 4 SCC 248; AIR 1979 SC 1960; 1979 (12) CTR 190; [1979] 119 ITR 996; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 442; [1979] 2 TAXMAN 197; 1980 UJ 305

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 147(b) - A/ys 1960-61 to 1963-64 - Re-assessment - Information - Opinion of Internal Audit Party of Income Tax Department - Assessee-society, was a professional association of newspapers established with the principal object of promoting the welfare and interest of all newspapers - Assessee owned a building in which a conference hall and rooms were let out on rent to its members as well as to outsiders - Income from that source was assessed to tax all along as income from business - In the course of auditing the income-tax records pertaining to the assessee for the a/ys 1960-61 to 1963-64, the internal audit party of the Income Tax Department expressed the view that the money realised by the assessee on account of the occupation of its conference hall and rooms should not have been assessed as income from business - It said that an assessment should have been made under the head 'Income from property' - Income Tax Officer treated the contents of the report as 'information' in his possession for the purpose of s. 147(b) of IT Act, 1961, and reassessed the income on that basis - On appeal, Asst. Commissioner held that in law opinion of Internal Audit Party could not be said that the ITO had any 'information' in his possession enabling him to take action u/s. s. 147(b) of the IT Act - On appeal, Tribunal held that the ITO had jurisdiction to proceed u/s. 147(b) of IT Act - Hence, present appeal - Whether view expressed by an internal audit party of the Income-Tax department on a point of law be regarded as 'information' for the purpose of initiating proceedings u/s. 147(b) of the IT Act, 1961? - Held, an internal audit party of the income-tax department expressed the view that the receipts from the occupation of the conference hall and rooms did not attract s. 10 of the Act and that the assessment should have been made u/s. 9 of IT Act - While ss. 9 and 10 of IT Act can be described as law, the opinion of the audit party in regard to their application is not law - It is not a declaration by a body authorised to declare the law - Hence, opinion of Internal Audit Party of Income Tax Department is not an 'information' within meaning of s. 147(b) of IT Act - Re-opening of assessment on that basis illegal - Order accordingly.
