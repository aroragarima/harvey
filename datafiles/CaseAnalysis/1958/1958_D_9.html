Inamati Mallappa Basappa v Desai Basavaraj Ayyappa and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 April 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, A.K. Sarkar, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 47; AIR 1958 SC 698; 1958 (2) MLJ(SC) 155; 1958 SCJ 953; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 611

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Any Other Candidate, Trial Of Election Petitions, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Notice Of Recrimination, Representation Of People (Second Amendment) Act, 1951

Summary: Election - Criminal - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 23, r.1 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898 - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 97, 80, 83, 123(1)(a)(b), 123(6), 90(1), 100(1), 108, 109, 110, 117, 112, 113, 90, 119, 88, 89, 91, 93, 95 and 96 - Appellant and Respondents contesting candidates for election - Appellant was declared elected by a majority - Respondent 1 presented to Election Commission a Petition, claiming a declaration that election of Appellant was void and that he had been duly elected as he had secured next highest number of valid votes - Appellant gave notice of his recrimination - (A) Whether 1st respondent is entitled to withdraw his claim ? - Whole constituency alive to fact that result of election duly declared is questioned on various grounds - If it were permissible for him to withdraw or abandon a part of his claim on analogy of O. 23, r.1, of CPC, he would make a virtue of necessity and withdraw or abandon that part of his claim so as to avoid any investigation in Election Petition itself in regard to himself or any other candidate sponsored by him - S. 108 deals with withdrawal of petitions before appointment of Tribunals and provides that an election petition may be withdrawn only by leave of Election Commission - Held, that on basis of provisions of Representation of People Act, 1951 election petition once presented continues for benefit of whole constituency and cannot come to an end merely by withdrawal thereof by petitioner or even by his death or by death or withdrawal of opposition by respondent but is liable to be continued by any person who might have been a petitioner - (B)Whether 1st respondent is entitled to abandon a part of his claim in manner he has done ? - If constituency as a whole is interested in petition presented before Election Tribunal no such withdrawal or abandonment of a part of claim could ever be permitted without giving an opportunity to any person who might have been a petitioner to continue proceedings and pursue petition to its logical conclusion - Held, that on a due consideration of provisions of Representation of People Act, 1951,the provisions of O. 23, r.1, do not apply to election petitions and it would not be open to a petitioner to withdraw or abandon a part of his claim once an election petition was presented to Election Commission, more so when such a withdrawal or abandonment of a part of claim would have effect of depriving returned candidate or any other party to petition of right of recrimination which had accrued to him under s. 97 of R P Act - (C) Whether a right to recrimination accrues? - Right of recrimination which has once accrued to returned candidate or any other party to petition u/s. 97 of R P Act cannot be taken away, and returned candidate or any other party to petition would in such circumstances be entitled to give evidence to prove that election of petitioner or any other candidate sponsored by him would have been void if he had been returned candidate and a petition had been presented calling in question his election - Held, that order passed by Election Tribunal allowing abandonment of a part of claim by first respondent and precluding appellant from giving evidence to prove that election of first respondent would have been void if he had been returned candidate was clearly erroneous and liable to be set aside - Appeal allowed.
