Â Municipal Corporation Of Thecity Of Ahmedabad And Ors. v Jan Mohammed Usmanbhai And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

R.B. Misra, E.S. Venkataramiah, V. Balakrishna Eradi, V. Khalid, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 298; (1986) 3 SCC 20; AIR 1986 SC 1205; 1986 (2) CCC 680; 1986 CRLR 385; 1986 CrLR(SC) 385; 1986(1) SCALE 1180; [1986] 2 S.C.R. 700

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Central Provinces and Berar Animal Preservation Act, 1949

Summary: Constitution - Constitution, arts.19(1)(g), 19(6), 226, 14 - Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporation Act, 1949, s. 466(1)(D)(b) - Standing Orders by Municipal Commissioner for preservation, protection and improvement of livestock - Constitutionality of Orders - (A) Slaughter houses - Constitutionality of Standing Orders declaring certain days as holidays - When days not ill chosen, prohibition not unreasonable and is in public interest - (B) Classification - Butchers who slaughter cattle formed well defined class - Distinguishes them from those who kill goats and sheep - Close connection with the object - Held, orders are not unconstitutional - Standing Orders, therefore, adopt a classification based on sound and intelligible basis and hence, they are constitutional - Appeal allowed.
