Commissioner of Expenditure-Tax, Gujarat, Ahmedabad v Darshan Surendra Parekh

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 168; AIR 1968 SC 1125; [1968] 69 ITR 683; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 589

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Indirect Tax - Expenditure- tax Act, 1957, s. 4 (i) (ii) - Hindu Undivided Family - Tax - Leviable - Liability - In a proceeding for assessment of tax under the 1957 Act, of the Hindu Undivided Family (HUF) for the A/y 1958-59 the Expenditure Tax Officer brought to tax Rs. 20,508/- - Order of the Tax Officer was confirmed by the Appellate Assistant Commissioner and the Appellate Tribunal - Tribunal referred to the HC u/s. 25(1) of the Act, three questions, out of which only two survive for consideration - HC answered the two questions in favour of assesse - Hence instant appeals - Held, questions raised were only about the liability to tax, the figures were not in dispute - SC was of the view, for the reasons already set out in dealing with the assessment year 1958-59, that the expenditure incurred by 'S' out of his personal estate was not liable to be included in the taxable expenditure for the year 1959-60 - If the amount expended from out of the trust estate be held, for reasons already set out to be expended by the trustees, the case falls within the terms of cl. (i), if it be held that the expenditure was incurred by or on behalf of the children after the income was received from the trustees it would fall within cl. (ii) - Legislature by the amended clause (ii) expressly provided that where the assessee was a HUF, any expenditure incurred by any dependant of assessee from or out of any income or property transferred directly or indirectly to the dependant by assesse, was liable to be included - Words were not susceptible of the interpretation that dependant who incurred the expenditure should be other than the dependant to whom property was transferred by the assesse - Expenditure incurred for his own purposes by dependant to whom the property was transferred by the HUF clearly falls within s. 4 (ii) as amended - SC therefore modify the order of the HC - Answer to the first question for each year would be in the negative - Answer to the second question would be in the affirmative - It should, however, be understood that answer did not imply that the amount of Rs. 10,321/- in respect of the A/y 1958-59 was the amount spent by the trustees - In disposing of the appeal u/s. 25(6) of the Act - Tribunal should make appropriate adjustments in declaring the liability of the assessee to pay tax in respect of the expenditure incurred from the trust estate by the trustees after making the permissible deductions u/ss. 5 and 6 of the Act - In view of the partial success, there would be no order as to costs in SC and in the HC - Order modified.
