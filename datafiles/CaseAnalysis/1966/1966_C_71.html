Standard Mills Company Limited v Commissioner of Wealth Tax, Bombay City

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 272; AIR 1967 SC 595; [1967] 63 ITR 470; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 768

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Wealth Tax Act, 1957, ss. 7(2)(a), 7, 2(m), 27(1) - Assessment - Deduction - Claim - Entitlement - Appellant company had placed 4 claims to be deducted from its net wealth for A/Y 1957-58 which was rejected by Wealth tax officer - Aggrieved appellant appealed before Assistant commissioner which had accepted claim of last installment of advance tax and rejected other claims - Appellant appealed before Tribunal which upheld claim nos. 1, 2 and 4 and rejected claim no. 3 - Commissioner had referred matter u/s. 27(1) of the Act, to HC which had answered claim nos. 1 and 2 in favour of appellant and claim no. 3 in favour of respondent and claim no. 4 was not pressed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC order in respect of claim no. 2 and 3 was sustainable - Held, u/s. 2(m) of the Act it was clear that tax officer should first determine aggregate value of all assets belonging to assessee on valuation date, and then determine aggregate value of all debts owed by assessee on valuation date - Excess of aggregate value of assets over debts was net wealth - Aggregate value of assets must be computed in accordance with provisions u/s. 7 of the Act - Therefore, aggregation of value of all debts owed by assessee on valuation date, u/s. 7 of the Act had no operation and s. 7 of the Act does not deal with computation of net wealth - It deals with computation of aggregate value of assets - Wealth tax officer was competent u/s. 7 where assessee was carrying on business of which accounts are maintained regularly, to determine net value of assets of business as a whole - But in doing so he determines value of assets of business as a whole, and not net wealth of business - Hence, claim no.2 was answered in favour of appellant and claim no. 3 was answered in favour of revenue - Appeal partly allowed.
