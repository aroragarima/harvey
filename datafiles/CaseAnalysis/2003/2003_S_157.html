Canara Bank and others v Shri Debasis Das and others

Supreme Court of India

12 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 269; (2003) SCC (L&S) 507; (2003) 4 SCC 557; AIR 2003 SC 2041; 2003 (3) AWC 1853; 2003 (3) CalLT(SC) 52; 2003 (2) CLR 1; 2003 (2) ESC 204; 2004 (2) JhrCR(SC) 16; JT 2003 (3) SC 183; 2003 (2) LLJ 531; 2003 (2) LLN 444; 2003(3) SCALE 220; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 968; 2003 (2) SLJ 345; 2003 (3) SLR 64; 2003 (3) SLT 729; 2003 (2) Supreme 793; 2003 (4) UPLBEC 3043

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Written Statement Of Defence

Summary: Canara Bank Officer Employees' (Conduct) Regulations, 1976 - Regulations 6(18) and 6(21) - Scope and ambit of - whether principles of natural justice have been violated? - Held, at no stage the employee pleaded prejudice. Regulation 6(18) has not been correctly interpreted, and therefore, the conclusions regarding prejudice are indefensible. The judgment of the Division Bench confirming that of the Learned Single Judge be quashed so far as it relates to the question of violation of principles of natural justice. - Further, since, there was no consideration of the merits of the case, it would be in the fitness of things of direct examination of the documents by the expert in terms of Learned Single Judge's order.
