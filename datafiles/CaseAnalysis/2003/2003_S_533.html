Baldev Singh and Another v State of M.P.

Supreme Court of India

06 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 2; (2003) 9 SCC 45; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1740; AIR 2003 SC 2098; 2003 (1) CCR 126; 2003 (1) Crimes 384; 2003 (4) CRJ 448; 2003 CRLJ 880; 2003 (1) JCC 171; JT 2003 (1) SC 10; 2003 (1) RCR(Criminal) 860; 2003(1) SCALE 20; 2003 (1) SLT 254; 2003 (1) Supreme 306

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: IPC - S. 302 - conviction and sentence under - appeal - The case rests on the testimony of the two eye witnesses - Whether they can be believed as witnesses of truth? - Both the eye-witnesses, being chance witnesses, do not assign any convincing reason for being at the place of incident at that abnormal hour of the day in full summer. - Held, since, there is grave doubt on the presence of the two eye witnesses at the place of the incident so as to have witnessed the incident, the conviction of the two accused appellants, which rests on the testimony of the two witnesses, cannot be sustained.
