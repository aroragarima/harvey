Bharat Sanchar Nigam Limited v Vodafone Essar Gujarat Limited

Supreme Court of India

23 September 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 812; AIR 2016 SC 4455; 2016 (4) CompLJ 289; JT 2016 (12) SC 497; 2016 (4) RCR(Civil) 950; 2016(9) SCALE 166; [2016] 7 S.C.R. 746

Case Digest

Subject: Telecom

Keywords: Dot, Indian Telegraph Act, 1885, Telecom Regulatory Authority of India Act, 1997, Telecom Regulatory Authority Of India, Telecommunication Interconnection Usage Charges (IUC) Regulations, 2003, Telecommunication Interconnection Usage Charges Regulations, 2003

Summary: CIVIL APPEAL No. 8107 of 2010

(A) Telecom - Indian Telegraph Act, 1885, s. 4(1) - Telecom Regulatory Authority of India Act, 1997, s. 36 - Telecommunication Interconnection Usage Charges (IUC) Regulation 2003 - Highest slab rate - Challenged - Respondent entered into an Interconnect Agreement with appellant - Circular dt. 23-06-2003 was issued by Department of Telecommunication (DoT) gave directions to service providers on how to prevent tampering of CLI - Appellant by circular dt. 28-01-2004 implemented circular of DOT with the Regulations - Appellant raised a bill of Rs.3,54,94,916/- on respondent at the rate of Rs.5.65 per minute for the period between July to September 2004 - Respondent approached Tribunal challenging demand raised by appellant - Tribunal held that demand raised by appellant was illegal and unjustified inasmuch as Interconnect Agreement between the parties did not carry any stipulation that in the event any invalid or tempered CLI was transferred to appellant network, appellant would be entitled to raise demand at highest slab rate - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, one of the recitals to Interconnect Agreement was to the effect that appellant reserves right to modify the terms and conditions of agreement, if it receives a direction from licensor or any other competent authority to that effect. The circular dt. 28-01-2004, clearly, was not pursuant to any direction from licensor but was unilaterally issued stipulating that charges at the highest applicable rate would be levied for calls coming with invalid CLI. The circular dt. 28-01-2004 being unilateral, does not become a part of Interconnect Agreement inasmuch as respondent had consented to be bound by any additional/fresh terms and conditions only if the same were issued by competent authority or pursuant to the directions of competent authority. However, in the 2003 Regulations there was no stipulation for levying charges in the manner it was done. It was noticed that before circular dt. 28-01-2004 came to be issued by appellant, TRAI had issued an directive dt. 24-11-2003 and a circular dt. 20-01-2004 to all operators advising them not to tamper with CLI of any call and not to offer or accept any call without CLI. BSNL's action in receiving calls originating from respondent's network without CLI and further decision to charge such calls at the highest rate would be clearly against the directions of TRAI. Appellant could not also discharge its burden to show as to how respondent even by tampering with its network could wrongly receive and route international calls when it did not have an International Long Distance Operator License. SC do not find fault with the conclusions recorded by Tribunal in impugned order. Appeals disposed of.

CIVIL APPEAL NO.8108 OF 2010

(B) Telecom - Indian Telegraph Act, 1885, s. 4(1) - Telecom Regulatory Authority of India Act, 1997, s. 36 - Telecommunication Interconnection Usage Charges (IUC) Regulation 2003 - Access charges - Amendment - Prospective effect - Appellant and respondent entered into an Interconnect Agreement dt. 15-02-2002 that governed two licenses under the 1885 Act - For the period May to June, 2005 a demand of Rs.59,40,94,834/- was raised by appellant for invalid and incomplete CLI calls handed over by respondent to appellant network - Respondent approached Tribunal challenging impugned demand and Tribunal set aside the demand raised by appellant - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, Cl. 6.4.6 of Interconnect Agreement between the parties dealt with computation of access charges. The July, 2004 amendment, prospective in nature, dealt with the liability in case of unauthorized calls i.e. calls other than specified for a particular trunk group. The subsequent Addenda dt. 19-07-2005 dealt with calls without CLI and charges applicable. The recital to Addenda clearly states that it was prospective in operation. If that was so, SC do not see how on the strength of Cl. 6.4.6 which came into effect from 19-07-2005 the demand for the period upto June 2005 could have been raised by appellant. Further, finding of Tribunal that demand raised by appellant would not be justified in view of the certificate issued by manufacturer of switchgear instituted in respondent's POI, a pure finding of fact, would provide an additional plank. Appeals disposed of.

CIVIL APPEAL NO.1105 OF 2013

(C) Telecom - Indian Telegraph Act, 1885, s. 4(1) - Telecom Regulatory Authority of India Act, 1997, s. 36 - Telecommunication Interconnection Usage Charges (IUC) Regulation 2003 - Circular issued - Double charges - Validity - Appellant raised two bills against respondent in respect of cellular services - Respondent approached Tribunal challenging impugned order wherein Tribunal partly allowed demand for the period 21-7-2004 to November, 2004 by holding that for the said period appellant would be entitled to charge respondent for double number of actual calls which did not have any CLI on the basis of the circular of appellant dt. 13-06-2005 - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, Tribunal failed to notice bill dt. 23-07-2008 for the period May, 2003 to January, 2004 was solely with respect to calls with invalid CLI. The period of demand was before the date of addendum to Cl. 6.4.6 i.e. 19-07-2005. The second bill dt. 30-06-2009 for period February to November, 2004 was a consolidated bill for non-CLI calls as well as trunk group violation. For the latter violation demand as mentioned in the said bill was Rs.76.26 lakhs. This later demand, in part, appears to be in order in light of the Addenda to Cl. 6.4.6 dt. 21-07-2004. SC allow to the extent by holding that liability for trunk group violation for the period 21-07-2004 to November, 2004 can be legitimately levied on respondent in terms of Cl. 6.4.6 added in Interconnect Agreement by Addenda dt. 21-07-2004. Appellant might work out the precise quantum of penalty on the aforesaid basis which would be paid by respondent. Appeals disposed of.

CIVIL APPEAL NO. 8269 of 2010

(D) Telecom - Indian Telegraph Act, 1885, s. 4(1) - Telecom Regulatory Authority of India Act, 1997, s. 36 - Telecommunication Interconnection Usage Charges (IUC) Regulation 2003 - Demand notices issued - Applicability - Respondent challenged the demand notices dt. 03-09-2006, 23-03-2007 and 09-04-2007 issued by appellant whereby it called upon respondent to pay an amount of Rs.10,63,88,772/- in terms of Cl. 6.4.6 of the Interconnect agreement - Tribunal set aside demands on ground that as Cl. 6.4.6 was added to Interconnect agreement between parties to instant case by addendum dt. 01-12-2005 w.e.f. 14-11-2003, the same can have no application to period prior thereto - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, for the remaining period i.e. November, 2003 to May 2004 during which period Cl. 6.4.6 was in force, finding of Tribunal that respondent should be given an opportunity and quantum of loss suffered by appellant should be computed accordingly would require a close look. The demand raised for the period from May to November 2003, as held earlier, should be set aside while for the period from 14-11-2003 to May, 2004 should be determined in accordance with Cl. 6.4.6 of the Agreement as brought into effect with retrospective effect from 14-11-2003. The premise on which Tribunal had held necessity of affording an opportunity to respondent for determination of quantum of loss suffered by appellant for the period from November 2003 to May 2004 proceeded on the basis that Cl. 6.4.6 was a penal clause. Appeals disposed of.
