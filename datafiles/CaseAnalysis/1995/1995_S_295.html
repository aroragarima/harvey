E.K. Chandrasenan v State Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 2223; (1995) 2 SCC 99; (1995) SCC (Cr) 329; AIR 1995 SC 1066; 1995 (1) CCR 71; 1995 (1) Crimes 421; 1995 CRLJ 2060; JT 1995 (1) SC 496; 1995(1) SCALE 159; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 277; 1995 (1) UJ 381

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Methyl

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 120B and 320 - Constitution, arts.136 and 142 - Sale of adulterated arrack - Many deaths resulting and cases of loss of eyesight occurring due to consumption - (A) Conspiracy - Proof - Significance of - (1) Absence of direct evidence as regards particular accused relating to conspiracy - Significance - (2) Involvement of particular accused , when he is not present in particular meeting with certain accused - (B) Grievous hurt - (1) Accused supplying arrack after mixing dangerous substance like methyl alcohol , resulting in many deaths - If causing of hurt voluntarily - (2) Proof - 24 persons losing eyesight permanently - Hurt in question, if grievous - (3) Sentence - (4) Mitigating circumstances - Accused 72 years of age - Prime mover - (C) Constitution - Extent of Supreme Court's powers as to enhancement of sentence - Held, (A) It is without significance as conspiracies are secretly planned - Not material, conspiracy can be proved by circumstantial evidence - (B) Considering nature of substance that person responsible for mixing had knowledge that consumption of liquor distributed was likely to cause very serious adverse effects - Has to be regarded as 'grievous' because of what is stated in clause secondly - Accused held, not entitled to be treated differently - Mitigating circumstance of age gets neutralised - (C) SC can suo motu issue rule u/art. 142 - Petition dismissed.
