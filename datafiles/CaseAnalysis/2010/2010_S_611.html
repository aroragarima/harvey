Poonam v Sumit Tanwar

Supreme Court of India

22 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 198; (2010) 4 SCC 460; AIR 2010 SC 1384; 2010 (6) ALD(SC) 63; 2010 (79) ALR 720; 2010 (3) AWC 2213; 2010 (4) Bom.C.R. 244; 2010 (3) CalLT 56; 2010 (2) JhrCR(SC) 189; JT 2010 (3) SC 259; 2010 (3) MLJ(SC) 1418; 2010 (1) OLR 749; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 551; 2010(3) SCALE 266; [2010] 3 S.C.R. 557

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, ss. 13-B(1) and 13-B(2) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Divorce - Waiving off statutory period of 6 months - Petitioner and respondent got married on 30-11-2008 according to Hindu rites in Delhi - They separated just after two days of their marriage i.e. on 02-12-2008 - Petition for dissolution of marriage by mutual consent was filed u/s. 13-B(1) of 1955 Act - Family court directed them to wait for statutory 6 months for second motion - Petitioner filed writ petition u/art. 32 of the Constitution claiming violation of her fundamental rights and urged apex court to waive off the period of 6 months and annul the marriage by a decree of divorce - Whether writ petition filed by petitioner is maintainable? - Held, no - Family Court passed an order strictly in accordance with law asking the parties to wait for statutory period of six months to file the second motion in a divorce case - In such a fact-situation, it is not permissible to suggest that the aforesaid order has violated or infringed any of the fundamental rights or any legal right of the parties - Moreover, petitioner's advocate is not able to explain under what circumstances, the petition has been filed and as to whether such a petition is maintainable or whether relief of dissolution of marriage could be sought by the parties directly from SC in a case, wherein the marriage had taken place only a year and three months ago - Petitioner's advocate was not able even to explain that even if the Court considers to issue the writ, to whom it would be issued as the only parties in the case are wife and husband, who are seeking the divorce by consent - Petition has been filed without any sense of responsibility either by the parties or their counsel - Petition dismissed.
