Yogesh Narain Saxena v State of Uttaranchal

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Lokeshwar Singh Panta, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 197; (2009) 16 SCC 547; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 191; 2007 (2) ALT 225; 2007 (2) CalLT(SC) 100; 2007 CRLJ 2282; JT 2007 (4) SC 604; 2007(4) SCALE 247; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 856

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Post- Mortem Report

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - s. 302 - Appeal against the judgment High Court confirming the Session Court's judgment of conviction and sentence for life in respect of the offence punishable under Sec. 302 - Appellant alleged to have strangulated and killed his neighbour's 10 years old son - Circumstantial evidence against the accused of his situation of debtness, behavioural changes, recovery of dead body from inner room of his quarter and recovery of chappals of the deceased from water drain outside the appellant's home, receipt of trunk call booked to make ransom call by the accused proved the allegation - Held, there are number of impelling circumstances attending this case leading to an irresistible and inescapable conclusion that it was the appellant and the appellant alone who caused the death - Appeal dismissed.
