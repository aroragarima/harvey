Management of Tocklai Experimental Station v Workmen and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 224; AIR 1962 SC 1340; 1962 (4) FLR 132; 1961 (2) LLJ 694; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 557; [1962] Supp1 S.C.R. 557

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Research, Chamber Of Commerce, Housing

Summary: Labour & Industrial law - Cross appeals Nos. 459 & 460 under instant case arise out of award passed by Tribunal under industrial dispute between management and workmen of Experimental Station total six demands were raised before Tribunal and under instant case SC concerned with only three of them i.e. Demand No. 1 (a) Junior staff employees' of Station should be given pension in lieu of existing practice of paying gratuity which was rejected by Tribunal Demand No. 3(b) Reference to claim for free housing accommodation or adequate allowance in lieu thereof which was partially allowed by tribunal, unsatisfied with these two demands workmen filed instant appeal no. 460 and third demand was bonus for junior staff which was partially allowed by Tribunal and ordered Station to pay Pujja bonus at same rate paid to employees of Tea Association aggrieved Station filed instant appeal No. 459 - That was how two cross-appeals arises before SC - Whether award passed by Tribunal was justified - Held, appeal No. 460 preferred by workmen could not be seriously pressed as Tribunal was fully justified in refusing demand of workmen's for pension in lieu of existing practice of paying gratuity by considering relevant facts conclusion of Tribunal was well founded and with regards to demand no. 3(b) which was subject-matter of appeal tribunal has increased house allowance at a flat rate of Rs. 20/instead of Rs. 10/and this increased rate has been paid by Station as from date when award became enforceable and workmen's contends that the Tribunal should have made an award granting demand for accommodation or in the alternative should have awarded larger amount by way of house allowance - SC was not impressed by this argument, demand for provision of housing accommodation can be reasonably entertained where it appears that financial position of employer can bear burden involved in said demand and according to present economic conditions prevailing in industry responsibility for providing housing accommodation cannot reasonably be placed solely on shoulders of employer and In due course problem may have to be tackled by industry in co-operation with State - Hence Tribunal has considered financial position of Station, urgency of damned made by workmen, and has come to conclusion that demand for housing accommodation was not justified and that ends of social justices would be met under instant case if a flat rate of enhancement of Rs. 20/- was awarded - Thus appeal No. 460 preferred by workmen was not satisfied - With regard to appeal No. 459 approach adopted by Tribunal was not justified as in dealing with alternative claim for puja bonus which was not made in demand and which had not been expressly referred to tribunal was entirely erroneous as claim for pujja bonus proceeds on entirely different considerations it was customary puja bonus undoubtedly prevails in many industries in B but there are certain tests which have to be applied in determining validity of claim amount by of way of puja bonus, it must be shown, has been consistently paid by employer to his employees from year to year at same rate, that it has been paid even in years of loss and that it had no relation to profit made by employer during relevant year - Thus decision of Tribunal awarding puja bonus to workmen cannot be sustained and award of Tribunal was absolutely vague and must be set aside - Thus appeal no. 459 was allowed and appeal no. 460 was dismissed - Appeals disposed of.
