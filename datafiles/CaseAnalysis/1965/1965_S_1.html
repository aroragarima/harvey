Erode Electricity Distribution Company Limited v Erode Electricity Distribution Company Limited Employees

Supreme Court of India

03 November 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 222; AIR 1966 SC 808; 1966 (12) FLR 205; 1966 (1) LLJ 443; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 498

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Retirement, Age Of Retirement, Festival, Certification, Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946

Summary: Corporate - Labour & Industrial Law - Factories Act, 1948 - Indian Electricity Act, 1910 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946 - Whether it is permisible for an industrial establishment to have two sets of standing orders to govern relevant terms and conditions of its employees? - If court examines scheme of relevant provisions of Act in light of matters specified in Schedule in respect of which standing orders are required to be made, it appears that two sets of standing orders cannot be made under Act - One of objects of Act was to introduce uniformity of terms and conditions of employment in respect of workmen belonging to same category and discharging same or similar work under an industrial establishment - Held, conclusion appears to be irresistible that object of Act is to certify standing orders in respect of matters covered by Schedule, and having regard to these matters, standing orders so certified would be uniform and would apply to all workmen alike who are employed in any industrial establishment - Appeal dismissed.
