M. P. Singh, Deputy Superintendent of Police v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

16 January 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28522; (1987) 1 SCC 592; AIR 1987 SC 485; 1987 (3) ATC 44; 1987 (1) CCC 434; 1987 (54) FLR 185; JT 1987 (1) SC 146; 1987 LabIC 468; 1987 (1) LLN 447; 1987(1) SCALE 47; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 1014; 1988 (2) SLJ 95; 1987 (1) SLR 345; 1987 (1) UJ 412

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 and 311 - Special pay - In view of arduous nature of their duties - Discrimination between 'deputationists' and 'non-deputationists' getting Rs. 150/- and Rs. 100/- respectively - Violation of arts. 14 and 16 - Held, that appellants be paid similar to deputationist as paying different rates of special pay does not pass test of classification permissible u/arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution of India since it does not bear any rational relation to object of classification - Appeal allowed.
