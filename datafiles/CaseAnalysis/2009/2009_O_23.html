Raj Rani & Ors. v Oriental Insurance Co. Ltd. & Ors

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 762; (2009) 13 SCC 654; 2009 (4) ACC 910; 2009 (76) ALR 798; 2009 (4) AWC 3342; 2009 (156) PLR 789; 2009(7) SCALE 701; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 1168; 2009 (3) UC 1862

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Lump Sum Payment, Use Of Motor Vehicle

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 166 and 163A - Claim of compensation u/s. 166 - Vehicular accident - Collision of car with a stationary truck - Deceased was driving the car accompanying one of his colleague - Deceased was an assistant engineer in a statutory organisation - Being aggrieved by the judgement of Trial Court and High Court present appeal preferred - (A) Whether allowances apart from DA, are to be counted for the purpose of determining annual income? - Yes; 'National Insurance Company Ltd. Indira Srivastava, (2008) 2 SCC 763' - Factum of salary undisputed, which was Rs. 17, 431/mn - (B) Whether immediate future prospect, a relevant factor? - Yes - Claimants were contending that deceased was about to be promoted to the post of executive engineer - After promotion income would have been around 25, 000/mn, Court noted - Court on contention being raised in that behalf observed that for the purpose of computation of the total amount of compensation u/s. 163A the future prospect may not be of much relevance, but in a case where claim petition has been filed in terms of s. 166 it would be a relevant factor - (C) Multiplier specified in sch. II of Act are stricto sensu not applicable in s. 166 cases, but whether they have any relevance? - Yes - Factor of high income of the deceased is a factor that has to be keep in mind at the time of applying appropriate multiplier - Further family background and income of family also are relevant factor - (D) Multiplier of 15 adopted by Tribunal whether justified? - Held, Yes - (E)Tribunal and HC had deducted 1/3 from the total compensation on ground of contributory negligence, whether such deduction justified? - Held, Yes - Precedents quoted, wherein principle of '50:50' in cases of contributory negligence was discussed and applied - Truck in instant case was stationary thus some amount of negligence on the part of the deceased cannot be ruled out, Court observed - Thus claimant entitled to only 50% of total amount of loss of dependency - (E) Tribunal had deducted 1/3rd from the amount of compensation on the rationale that the amount is being paid in lump sum, whether justified? - Held, No - Practice disapproved by decisions of Court - On basis of above findings Court calculated amount of compensation to Rs. 12, 72, 930, plus Rs. 7, 000/- for funeral expenses and consortium, with interest @7% pa - Appeal disposed of.
