Tameeshwar Vaishnav v Ramvishal Gupta

Supreme Court of India

08 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 23; (2010) 2 SCC 329; AIR 2010 SC 1209; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 660; 2010 (1) BC 357; 2010 CRLJ 1237; 2010 (1) CTC 849; 2010 (1) JCC (NI) 58; JT 2010 (1) SC 119; 2010 (2) MLJ(Crl) 501; 2010(2) M.P.H.T. 481; 2010 (3) MPLJ 7; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 684; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 730; 2010 (1) RLW 873; 2010(1) SCALE 134; [2010] 1 S.C.R. 204; 2010 (1) UC 344

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138(a), 138(b) and 142 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Filing of complaint - Limitation period - Cheques issued by appellant in favour of respondent were dishonoured - Respondent issued notices as contemplated u/cl. (b) of the proviso to s. 138 of the Act - However, respondent did not take any steps to file the complaint within the period prescribed in s. 142 of the Act - Respondent sent a second notice to the appellant - Thereafter, respondent filed a complaint u/s. 138 of the Act, for dishonour of a cheque, on which process was issued by the Magistrate - Criminal revision thereagainst was dismissed - Petition filed u/s. 482. CrPC was also dismissed - Hence, present appeals - Whether when the respondent did not take any action on the basis of the first notice issued, a second notice in regard to the self-same cheque was barred u/s. proviso to s. 138 of the Act and Magistrate erred in taking cognizance on the complaints filed by the respondent? - Held, provisions of s. 138 and clauses (a), (b) and (c) to the proviso indicate that a cheque has to be presented to the bank within a period of six months from the date on which it is drawn or within the period of its validity, whichever is earlier - In the instant case, complaints were filed beyond the period of limitation and the Magistrate erred in taking cognizance on the complaints filed on the basis of the second notices issued - Orders of the Magistrate taking cognizance on the criminal complaint set aside - Appeals allowed.
