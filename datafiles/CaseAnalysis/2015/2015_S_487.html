State of Uttar Pradesh v Om Pal Singh

Supreme Court of India

15 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 979; 2015(10) SCALE 786

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, Retrenchment, Labour Court, Junior, Labour, Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Dispute Act, 1947,s.4-K - Dismissal from service - Reinstatement - Claim of back wages - Respondent was workman under appellant Department, was terminated from service - Labour Court found that practice of intermittent breaks followed by appellant amounted to unfair labour practice and termination of respondent no.1 was violation of s.4-K of Act - On writ petition, HC affirmed order of Labour Court and dismissed petition of appellant - Hence instant appeals.

Held, in civil appeal No.577/2008, Labour Court found that âWW-1 had stated clearly that employees junior to respondent are still working with Dept. and same had been admitted by management in cross examination. Attendance register calling on from management had not been submitted and it was informed that register could not be traced. In this position whole statement on oath of EW-1 will have to be admitted that he had worked continuously. In addition to that workman had shown in para I same gap in the service period which is in total 42 days. Even if said period is excluded from service period total work days is still continuing and junior employees are still working then work termination of workman will be involved retrenchment and in same rules regarding retrenchment were not followed. Therefore it is invalid and illegal.â In view of matter, contention that engagement of respondent no.1 was for only a particular project cannot be appreciated at all. Court do not find any ground to interfere with well-reasoned award passed by Industrial Tribunal and as endorsed by HC. However, Court does not find it proper in facts of case to sustain award of back wages in facts of appellant case. Therefore, award is modified only to extent that respondent no.1 shall not be entitled for back wages for period he was not actually in service and for all other purposes his services shall be treated as continuous. In civil appeal No. 5773/2008, in facts and circumstances of case, Court is not inclined to take a different view than what was taken by HC in modifying order on back wages and sustaining award in all other respects. As rightly held by HC, it is a case of unjust denial of work. Appeals disposed of.
