Â Sachindra Mohan Nandy & Ors v State Of West Bengal & Ors

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 706; (1971) 1 SCC 657; AIR 1971 SC 961; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 795; 1971 UJ 279

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - West Bengal Land (Requisition and Acquisition) Act, 1948, s. 3(1) - Chandernagore (Merger) Act, 1954, s. 17 - Chandernagore (Assimilation of Laws) Act, 1955, ss. 3, 4 - Acquisition of property - Notification - Sustainability - In year 1960, Collector of Hooghly made two orders u/s. 3(1) of the Act, 1948, and such orders had been conferred upon him by notification No. 3775-L. A., dt. 11-5-1948, and that notification had authorised Collector to exercised powers u/s. 3(1) of the Act, 1948 - When such notification was passed Chandernagore, where requisitioned land was situated, was not part of West Bengal and it was on that fact one argument, mentioned by appellants were Acquisition Act had never been extended and made applicable to Chandernagore - Appellants appealed before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was sustainable - Held, two points were raised by appellants counsel before this Court and regarding point no. 1, it seems to this Court that there was no force in contentions - S. 3 of Chandernagore (Meger) Act, 1954 made Chandernagore part of State of West Bengal and s. 17 extended Acquisition Act to it - Acquisition Act was law within meaning of law contained in s. 2(c) of the Act, 1955 because it related to matter enumerated in List II in Seventh Schedule to the Constitution - Insofar as Acquisition Act related to Entry 42 of List III it was applied by s. 3 of the Act, 1955 and s. 17 of the Act, 1954, read with definition of word law in s. 2(d) of latter Act - Coming to point no. 2, this Court urge with HC that Collector of Hooghly had authority to issue orders of requisition in question - If order of requisition was by Collector then notification of 1948 applies and Collector of Hooghly would be authorised to issue orders requisitioning in respect of land existing in Chandernagore because Chandernagore had come within limits of his jurisdiction - Notification might be construed to refer to limits of District as it exist on date of exercised of powers conferred by notification - If orders of requisition were issued by Additional District Magistrate, then he had authority by virtue of notification, dt. 15-9-1959, mentioned above - Therefore, there was no force in that point - Notification of 15-9-1959, amounts to special appointment within definition of 'Collector' - Thus, SC referred following question to Constitution Bench which had answered it in negative - Appeal dismissed.
