Mohd. Arif @ Ashfaq v State Of Nct Of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

10 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 484; (2011) 13 SCC 621; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 992; 2012 (2) CGLJ 229; JT 2011 (9) SC 563; 2011 (4) MLJ(Crl) 455; 2011(8) SCALE 328; [2011] 10 S.C.R. 56

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Foreigners - Indian Penal Code, 1860,ss. 121, 121A, 120-B, 302, 468, 471 and 474 - Foreigners Act, 1946, s. 14 - Red Fort attack case - Wage war against Government of India - Conviction - Death sentence - As the prosecution case, appellant was a Pakistani national and who joined a terrorist organization called Lashker-e-Toiba - Prosecution alleged that appellant was responsible for the incident of shooting inside the Red Fort in which three Army soldiers were killed - This was a well planned conspiracy and the responsibility of incident was taken up by Lashkar-e-Toiba - For the conspiracy, the appellant illegally entered India and he was receiving huge amounts of money to make it possible for himself to execute his design - Appellant falsely created and forged number of documents - Idea was to legitimize his stay in India for which he got prepared a false ration card, a false license and also opened bank accounts with the false addresses - Accused persons were charged for the offence punishable u/ss. 121, 121A and 120-B r/w s. 302 of IPC - Appellant was individually charged for the offence punishable u/s. 120-B of IPC, u/s. 3 of the Arms Act r/w ss. 25 and 27 of the Arms Act as also ss. 4 and 5 of the Explosive Substances Act and offence punishable u/s. 14 of the Foreigners Act for illegally entering into India without valid documents - Appellant was convicted for the offence punishable u/ss. 120-B, 121 and 121-A of IPC, ss. 186/353/120-B of IPC, s. 120-B of IPC r/w s. 302 of IPC, ss. 468/471/474 of IPC and also u/s. 420 r/w s. 120-B of IPC - Appellant was also held guilty for the offence punishable u/s. 25 of the Arms Act, s. 4 of the Explosive Substances Act and s. 14 of the Foreigners Act - Appellant was awarded death sentence for his convictions u/s. 121 of IPC as also u/s. 302 r/w S. 120-B of IPC - On appeal, HC also confirmed the death sentence awarded by the Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal filed by appellant challenging his conviction by the Trial Court and the HC as also the death sentence awarded to him - (A) It was contended that no such incident of outsiders going into the Red Fort and shooting ever happened and that the said shooting was as a result of the brawl between the Army men themselves - Held, senior officers of the police had reached the spot and their evidence only confirmed the dastardly incident of shoot out - There was enormous documentary evidence about seizure of blood sample, seizure from the spots, seizure of blood stained clothes, seizure of magazine, live cartridges and empties, seizure of rope and cap, seizure of various articles from Red Fort to suggest that the incident as, suggested by prosecution, did take place - Incident of shoot out did take place in which three persons lost their lives - (B) Unauthorizedly entering India - Held, significant circumstances were relied on by the prosecution to show that the appellant had unauthorizedly entered India and that he wanted to establish his identity in India and for that purpose, he got prepared a fake and forged ration card and on that basis, applied for a driving license and also opened bank accounts; only purpose in doing this was to establish that he was living in Delhi legitimately as an Indian national - Appellant used a forged ration card and got a driving license giving a false address - (C) Investigation - Held, investigation in instant case was both scientific and fair investigation - Timely recording of the D.D. Entries, scientific investigation using the computer, the depth of investigation and the ability of the investigating agency to reach the very basis of each aspect lend complete credibility to the fairness of the investigation - Also, trial held before the trial Judge was the epitome of fairness, where every opportunity was given to the accused persons and more particularly, to appellant - (D) Conspiracy - Conviction - It was contended that the appellant alone could not have been convicted for the conspiracy, since all the other accused were acquitted - Held, once the prosecution proved that there was a meeting of minds between two persons to commit a crime, there would be an emergence of conspiracy - Fact that barely within minutes of the attack, the BBC correspondents in Srinagar and Delhi were informed, proved that the attack was not a brainchild of a single person - Information reached to BBC correspondent at Srinagar and Delhi sufficiently proved that there was a definite plan and a conspiracy - Also, role of other militants was clear from the wireless message intercepted at the instance of BSF - For the execution of the nefarious plans, the militants (more than one in number) entered under the guise of watching Son et Lumiere show and while doing so, they smuggled arms inside the Red Fort - All this had to be a pre-planned attack for which the militants must have made a proper reconnaissance, must have also found out the placements of Army barracks and the escape route from the backside of the Red Fort - Offence u/s. 121 of conspiring to wage a war was proved against the appellant, for which he had been rightly held guilty for the offence punishable u/ss. 121 and 121-A of IPC - Appellant was also rightly held guilty for the offence punishable u/s. 120-B r/w S. 302 of IPC - (E) Death sentence - Trial Court awarded the death sentence to the appellant for the offence u/s. 121 of IPC for waging war against the Government of India and for offence u/s. 120B r/w S. 302 of IPC - Held, instant case was a rarest of rare case - This was nothing but an undeclared war by some foreign mercenaries like the appellant and his other partner in conspiracy - In conspiring to bring about such kind of attack and then carrying out their nefarious activities in systematic manner to make an attack possible was nothing but an attempt to question the sovereignty of India - Conspiracy to attack the Indian Army unit stationed in Red Fort and the consequent un-provoked attack could not be described excepting as waging war against India - This was a case where the conscience of the community would get shocked and it would definitely expect the death penalty for the appellant - This was a unique case where there was one most aggravating circumstance that it was a direct attack on the unity, integrity and sovereignty of India by foreigners - Appellant was a foreign national and had entered India without any authorization or even justification - Death sentence was the only sentence in the peculiar circumstance of instant case - Death sentence confirmed - Appeals dismissed.
