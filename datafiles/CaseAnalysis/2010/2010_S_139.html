Shyam Telelink Ltd. Now Sistema Shyam Teleservices Ltd. v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

05 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 828; (2010) 10 SCC 165; 2010(6) ALL MR 919; 2011 (1) AWC 835; 2010 (4) CompLJ 274; JT 2010 (11) SC 50; 2010(10) SCALE 545; [2010] 12 S.C.R. 927

Case Digest

Subject: Telecom

Keywords: Indian Telecom Act, 1885

Summary: Telecom - Telecom Regulatory Authority of India Act, 1997, s. 14(a)(i) r/w. s. 14A(1) - Appellant granted a licence for providing basic telecom services in Rajasthan Circle - Subsequently, a licence agreement was executed between appellant and Government of India which required the appellant to start commercial operations within 12 months from the date on which the agreement was executed - In March 2001 a demand was raised by the respondent in respect of liquidated damages for the delay in the commissioning of the service - Aggrieved by the said demand appellant approached the Tribunal submitting that it was ready to commence commercial operations in the last week of February 1999 and had sought permission to do so from the respondent which permission was arbitrarily refused by the respondent, hence, recovery of the liquidated damages was bad - Tribunal dismissed the petition filed by the appellant - Hence, present appeal - Whether tribunal rightly upheld demand raised by the respondent in respect of liquidated damages from appellant? - Held, the fact that the appellant was not ready to commence commercial operations in February 1999 is evident from its own letter dt. 19.07.1999 in which the appellant had clearly admitted that the system was not yet ready for such operations - Hence, tribunal perfectly justified in holding that the commercial operations were started only on 05.06.2000 and that for the intervening period such operations could not be commenced on account of deficiencies that were attributed entirely to the defects in the system which the appellant had installed - Denial of permission to the appellant was neither arbitrary nor mala fide especially when the conditions in the licence agreement were not complied with - Hence, computation of the liquidated damages for non- commencing of the services as well as limiting the same to a total amount of Rs.8 crores was in conformity with the licence conditions executed between the parties - Demand upheld - Appeal dismissed.
