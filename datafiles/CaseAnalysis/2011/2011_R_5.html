Bharat Sanchar Nigam Limited v R. Santhakumari Velusamy and others

Supreme Court of India

06 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 641; (2011) 9 SCC 510; 2012 (5) AWC 4711; JT 2011 (10) SC 558; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 301; 2011(10) SCALE 136; [2011] 14 S.C.R. 502; 2011 (6) SLR 568

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Service

Summary: Administrative - Service - One Time-Bound Promotion scheme (OTBP) - Biennial Cadre Review (BCR) Scheme - Promotion - Applicability of Reservation - Entitlement - In appellant department there were four grades of employees - Appellant /Telecom department introduced OTBP scheme in year 1983-84 under which regular employees who had completed 16 years of service in grade, were placed in next higher grade - After some years, employees unions demanded second time-bound promotion on completion of 26 years of service - Govt. decided that second time bound promotion was not feasible - However, to provide relief from stagnation in grade, Govt. decided to had BCR under which specified percentage of posts could be upgraded on basis of functional justification - BCR scheme was accordingly introduced vide Circular dt. 16- 10- 1990 - It was made applicable to those cadres in Group C and Group D employees who were in regular service as on 1-.1-.1990 and had completed 26 years of satisfactory service in basic cadres were to be screened by duly constituted Committee to assess their performance and determine their suitability for advancement and if they were found suitable, to be upgraded in higher scale and upgradation was restricted to 10 percent of posts in Grade III - By Circular dt. 1-3 -1996 - Govt. issued clarification that promotion to Grade IV would be given from among officials in Grade III on basis of their seniority in basic grade, subject to fulfillment of other conditions and that normal rules of reservation would apply to promotions in Grade IV - Said circular was challenged by respondents on ground that principles of reservation would not apply for upgradation of existing posts which did not carry any change in duties and responsibilities before Tribunal - Held that department could not apply reservation rules while upgrading posts under BCR scheme and directed department to take appropriate action for effecting promotions to upgraded posts without applying reservation roster - Appellant filed writ petition before HC which was dismissed - Hence , instant appeal - Whether rules of reservation would apply to up-gradation of posts - Held, in Union of India vs. Pushpa Rani Indlaw SC 2607 relied on - Upgradation merely confers financial benefit by raising scale of pay of post without there being movement from lower position to higher position - In upgradation, candidate continues to hold same post without any change in duties and responsibilities but merely gets higher pay scale - Where process of upgradation was simplicitor, there was no need to apply rules of reservation - Which was evident from terms of BCR scheme and Circular dt. 13-12-1995 which provided that promotions to Grade IV were to be based on seniority in basic grade from among officers in Grade III, subject to fitness determined as per OTBP manner, that was screening to ascertain whether there were any adverse comments or punishment against employee concerned - Therefore, BCR scheme was upgradation scheme which gave relief against stagnation - It neither involve any new post, or involve advancement to higher post nor involve any process of selection for conferment of benefit of higher pay-scale - Upgradation was given to senior most 10 percent of BCR scale employees in Grade III strictly as per seniority - BCR scheme as per circular dt. 16-10- 1990 was thus scheme for upgradation simplicitor without involving any creation of additional posts or any process of selection for extending benefit - Such scheme of upgradation did not invite rules of reservation - Hence, set aside orders of HC and Tribunal - Appeals allowed.
