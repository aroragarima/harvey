Esher Singh v State Of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

15 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 187; (2004) 11 SCC 585; AIR 2004 SC 3030; JT 2004 (3) SC 391; 2004(3) SCALE 267; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 1180

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Private Party

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 30 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120A, 120B, 224, 302, 43 - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 254, 27 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, s. 2(b) - Terrorist And Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 15, 3(3), 4, 5, s. 6 - Power u/art. 136 is not to be confused with ordinary appellate power exercised by appellate courts and appellate tribunals under specific statutes - Plenary power, 'exercisable outside purview of ordinary law' to meet pressing demands of justice - Power is vested in this Court but right to invoke Court's jurisdiction is vested in no one - Exercise of power of this Court is not circumscribed by any limitation as to who may invoke it Where a judgment of acquittal by HC has led to a serious miscarriage of justice this Court cannot refrain from doing its duty and abstain from interfering on ground that a private party and not State has invoked Court's jurisdiction - Court can entertain appeals against judgments of acquittal by HC at instance of interested private parties also - Circumstances that Code does not provide for an appeal to HC against an order of acquittal by a subordinate Court, at instance of a private party, has no relevance to question of power under art.136 - TADA - Trial Court was justified in holding that accusations u/s. 120 B were not made out so far as offences under IPC are concerned - Motive for killing is concerned, evidence is clear to extent that A-1 wanted removal of deceased from bodies of various trusts and educational institutions and not his removal from this world In absence of adequate material to establish commission of offences punishable u/s. 302 or 302 r/w Section 120 B and Section 3(3), 5 and 6 of TADA and Section 27 of Arms Act, as rightly held to have been not established by trial Court - Essential ingredient of offence of criminal conspiracy is agreement to commit an offence - In cases where agreement is for accomplishment of an act which by itself constitutes an offence, then in that event no overt act is necessary to be proved by prosecution because in such a situation, criminal conspiracy is established by proving such an agreement - Mere proof of an agreement between accused for commission of such a crime alone is enough to bring about a conviction u/s. 120 B and proof of any overt act by accused or by any one of them would not be necessary.
