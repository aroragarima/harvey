S.K. Singh v Central Bank of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2447; (1996) 6 SCC 415; (1997) SCC (L&S) 39; 1996 (9) AD(SC) 283; 1997 (1) BC 1; 1996 (74) FLR 2632; JT 1996 (9) SC 542; 1997 (1) LLJ 537; 1996(7) SCALE 587; 1997 (1) SLJ 235; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 542; 1996 (5) Supreme 527; 1997 (5) Supreme 527; 1996 (2) UJ 831

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Administrative - Misconduct - Disciplinary proceedings - Removal from service - Petitioner was working as Branch Manager in respondent/Bank - Sum of Rs.20,000 was found to be short in cash of Branch - Disciplinary proceedings were initiated against petitioner - Enquiry was conducted and petitioner was removed from service - Petitioner challenged order of his removal in appeal which was dismissed - Writ petition filed by petitioner was also dismissed by Writ Court and on appeal, it was confirmed - Hence, instant petition.

Held, Writ Court as well as Division Bench of HC asked petitioner as to what prejudice petitioner has suffered for non-supply thereof. There was no adequate explanation offered by petitioner. Copy of report was not supplied, on facts, as no prejudice was proved it was not case warranting interference. Instant case, though copy of report was not supplied, petitioner was asked by Lower Courts what prejudice he suffered on account of non-supply of report, but he was not able to give satisfy reply as to prejudice caused to him on account of non-supply of enquiry report. There is no illegality in decision taken by HC. Petition dismissed.

Ratio - Copy of report needs to be supplied to delinquent employee to enable him to make representation against proposed action.
