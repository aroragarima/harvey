Life Insurance Corporation of India and Another v S. Sindhu

Supreme Court of India

04 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 408; (2006) 5 SCC 258; AIR 2006 SC 2366; 2006 (3) AWC 2759; 2006 (2) CCC 303; 2006 (2) CPJ(Sc) 18; 2006 (3) CTC 583; JT 2006 (5) SC 484; 2006 (3) RLW 2549; 2006(5) SCALE 375; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 854

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Insurance

Keywords: Contractual Liability, Death Of The Assured

Summary: [A.] Insurance - Interest Act, 1978, ss. 3(1), 3(3), 4(1) - Whether in case of a lapsed life insurance policy, Life Insurance Corporation of India while paying reduced sum payable by treating it as a paid-up policy, is liable to pay interest in regard to premiums paid from respective dates of payment of premiums to date of settlement? - Held, reduced sum calculated as per Table in Condition No (4) of Policy, became due only on death of assured - No interest is payable either under contract of insurance, or under any statute, or under Interest Act from respective dates of payment of premium to date of settlement of claim - LIC is not liable to pay any interest - Appeal allowed.
