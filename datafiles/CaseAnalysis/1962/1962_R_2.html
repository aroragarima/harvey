R. G. Jacob v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Raghubar Dayal, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 258; AIR 1963 SC 550; 1963 CRLJ 486; 1963 (2) MLJ(SC) 79; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 800

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Trade

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 165 - (A) What is meaning of word 'subordinate' u/s. 165? - Taking of bribe by subordinate, whether should be connected to functions in order to attract provisions of s. 165 of IPC ? - Held, s. 165 has been so worded as to cover cases of corruption which do not come within s. 161 or s. 162 or s. 163 - Under s. 163 without any limitation there is no justification for reading into word limitation suggested by words 'in respect of those very functions' - Legislature has expressed its legislative intention of making punishable such administrative subordinates also ,who have no connection with functions with which business or transaction is concerned - Appeal dismissed.
