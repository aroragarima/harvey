Uttar Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation v Mohd. Ghilman Sharif and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 880; (2009) 16 SCC 86; 2009 (3) ACC 662; 2009 (6) ADJ 719; 2009 (4) AWC 3212; JT 2009 (9) SC 665; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 840; 2009(9) SCALE 685; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 1149

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Rt a, Regional Transport Authority, Appeal disposed of, Opportunity Of Hearing, Permit Holder, Private Operator, Transport Department, Approved Scheme, Notified Route, State Transport Appellate Tribunal, Existing Operator

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicle Act, 1939 - Motor Vehicle Act, 1988 - Scheme of nationalization route - Exclusive right of operation to appellant Corporation - Total exclusion of private operators - Renewal of permit - Objections filed - Hearing Authority allowed existing operators, such as Respondent Nos. 1 and 2, to ply on routes in question along with Corporation - WP filed - HC held that Scheme had lapsed - Challenged to said order was upheld and matters were remanded to HC - Meanwhile, State Transport Appellate Tribunal directed permits to be renewed subject to decisions in WP - HC allowed WP and set aside orders passed by Hearing Authority holding that permit holders who were granted permits prior to 1986 were entitled to get compensation according to provisions of Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - On 28.3.2008 State Govt. issued Notification allowing private operators to operate on notified route in question along with appellant-Corporation - Notification challenged - Allahabad HC restrained authority from issuing permits on notified routes - Private respondents filed WP - HC directed State Transport Authority to consider applications filed by respondents - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC correct in directing that State Transport Authority to decide as to whether permit of Respondent No.1 was subsisting and if same was found to be subsisting respondents would not be prevented from plying their vehicles on route in question? - Held, renewal of permits upon orders of State Transport Appellate Tribunal was subject to decision in WP - Said WP was disposed of by Allahabad HC in favour of appellant Corporation - While disposing of said WP HC categorically held that permit holders who had been granted permit before 13.2.1986 i.e., before date of publication of Scheme, whose permits were going to be affected by approved Scheme on 29.5.1993, were only entitled to compensation in terms of provisions of Act - Directions given by HC set aside - Appeal disposed of.
