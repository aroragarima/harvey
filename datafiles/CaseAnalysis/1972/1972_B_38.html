Baradakanta Mishra, Ex-Commissioner of Endowments v Bhimsen Dixit

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Dwivedi, Y.V. Chandrachud, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 202; (1973) SCC (Cr) 360; (1973) 1 SCC 446; AIR 1972 SC 2466; 1973 CRLJ 19; 1973 CrLR(SC) 425; 1973 CutLT 461; [1973] 2 S.C.R. 495

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Orissa Hindu religious Endowments Act, ss. 9, 27 and 41 - Contempt proceedings - Appellant, member of Superior Judicial Service, at relevant time functioning as Commr. of Hindu religious endowments - Appellant deliberately avoided to follow High Court decision by giving wrong and illegitimate reasons - Whether appellant guilty of contempt? - Where superior court's order staying proceedings is disobeyed by inferior court to whom it is addressed, latter court commits contempt of court for it acts in disobedience to authority of former court - Held, deliberate and malafide conduct of not following binding precedent of HC is contumacious, does not unduly enlarge domain of contempt - It would not stifle a bona fide act of distinguishing binding precedent, even though it may be taken out to be mistaken - Appellant guilty of contempt - Appeal dismissed.
