Shailendra Kumar v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 November 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, Arijit Pasayat, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19919; (2002) SCC (Cr) 230; (2002) 1 SCC 655; AIR 2002 SC 270; AIR 2001 SCW 4984; 2001 (4) CCR 340; 2002 (1) CHN 104; 2002 (1) Crimes 197; 2002 (2) CRJ 600; 2002 CRLJ 568; JT 2001 (10) SC 111; 2001(8) SCALE 320; 2001 (8) SLT 224; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 371; 2001 (8) Supreme 513; 2002 (1) UC 89

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 149, 323 and 449 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 242 and 244 - Criminal Trial - Presence of Prosecution Witness - (A) Absence of Investigating Officer - During the trial - Held, it was duty of Sessions Judge to issue summons to Investigating Officer if he failed to remain present at time of trial of case - The presence of Investigating Officer at time of trial is must - It is his duty to keep witnesses present - If there is failure on part of any witness to remain present, it is duty of court to take appropriate action including issuance of bailable/non-bailable warrants, as case may be-It should be well understood that prosecution cannot be frustrated by such methods and victims of crime cannot be left in a lurch - (B) Scope of s.311 of CrPC, 1973 - Held, s. 311 empowers court to summon material witnesses though not summoned as witnesses and to examine or recall and re-examine if their evidence appears to it to be essential to just decision of case - Bare reading of section reveals that it is of a very wide amplitude and if there is any negligence, laches or mistakes by not examining material witnesses, courts function to render just decision by examining such witnesses at any stage is not, in any way, impaired-purpose is to arrive at a just decision of case - Impugned order set aside - States application u/s. 311 allowed - Appeal allowed.
