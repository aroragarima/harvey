N. Subramania Iyer v Official Receiver, Quilon

Supreme Court of India

24 May 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, P. Govinda Menon, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 144; AIR 1958 SC 1; 1958 KerLJ 17; 1958 KLT 1195; 1958 SCJ 172; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 257

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure - Power of Attorney - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 11 - Travancore Regulation VIII of 1090 (=1915), ss. 35(iii) and 53 - General Clauses Act, 1897, ss. 3 and 3(22) - Travancore General Clauses Act (II of 1072=1897), s. 6(2) - Indian General Clauses Act (X of 1897), s. 2(22) - Provincial Insolvency Act, 1920, s. 53 - Presidency Towns Insolvency Act, 1909, s. 55 - United State of Travancore and Cochin Interpretation and General Clauses Act, 1125, ss. 2 and 2(6) - Insolvency - Receiver's application for annulment of transfer - (A)Onus of proving a transaction upon - Held, it is settled law in insolvency proceedings that burden of proving a particular transaction is not supported by good faith and valuable consideration lies on Official Receiver who challenges transaction not on transferee - (B)Finding in insolvency Proceeding, if res judicata - Held, res judicata is not applied in instant case as matter that directly arose for determination in it was whether impugned transaction was not bona fide or for valuable consideration so far as transferee was concerned and that was not in issue in insolvency proceedings, nor had he been found in such proceedings to be privy to any act of insolvency intended to defeat or delay creditors - (C) Test of Good faith - Crucial question for decision in such a case would be whether transferee was wanting in bona fides in respect of transfer sought to be annulled - Correct test would be one of honesty as laid down by s. 2(22) Of Indian General Clauses Act and not that of due care and attention as contemplated by s. 2(6) of Travancore and Cochin General Clauses Act - Appeal allowed.
