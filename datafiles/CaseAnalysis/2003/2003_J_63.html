State of Orissa and Others v Joginder Patjoshi and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 992; (2004) 9 SCC 278; (2004) SCC (L&S) 730; AIR 2004 SC 1039; 2004 (100) FLR 85; 2003(9) SCALE 659; 2003 (1) SCW 6686; 2004 (1) SLR 371; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 492

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Land & Property; Service

Summary: Orissa Revised Scales of Pay Rules, 1985 - rr. 8(1)(a), 8(1)(b) - Pay scale - Revision - Interpretation - Held, the principle of liberal interpretation, which is applied in case of a beneficent legislation, has no application in the instant case inasmuch as by reason of r. 8 of the said Rules, the state had merely specified the mode and manner of application thereof. The same was necessary having regard to the difficulty which may cause to the employees who might have been receiving higher emoluments than the minimum prescribed under the revised pay scale at a point of time when the revised pay scale came into force - Further held, there being no ambiguity in r. 8(1)(a), the writ petitioners were only entitled to the minimum of the revised scale.
