M.R.F. Limited v Inspector Kerala Government

Supreme Court of India

11 November 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1691; (1998) 8 SCC 227; AIR 1999 SC 188; 1999 (82) FLR 951; JT 1998 (7) SC 532; 1999 (1) KLT 83; 1998 LabIC 3613; 1999 (1) LLJ 289; 1999 (1) LLN 1; 1998(6) SCALE 107; 1998 (9) SLT 187; [1998] Supp2 S.C.R. 632; 1998 (8) Supreme 417

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression

Summary: Kerala Industrial Establishments (National and Festival Holidays) Act, 1958, s. 3 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 43, 19(6), 19(1)(g) and 14 - Amendment Act increased number of national and festival holidays - To give effect to directive principles contained in art. 43 - Whether compulsory closure on national and festival holidays can be challenged as unreasonable - Whether opportunity of hearing needed to be given to representatives of industry - Held, principles of natural justice cannot be imported in matter of legislative action - Those affected by that law cannot raise a grievance that there should have been an opportunity of hearing before such law was passed - Amendment is not violative of art. 19(1)(g).
