Â Lennart Schussler And Anr v Director Of Enforcement & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

14 October 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, S.M. Sikri, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 383; (1970) SCC (Cr) 73; (1970) 1 SCC 152; AIR 1970 SC 549; 1970 CRLJ 707; 1970 (1) MLJ 33; 1971 (1) MLJ(SC) 33; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 760

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Fema

Keywords: Defence of India Rules 1962

Summary: Criminal - Conspiracy - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1947 - ss 4, 5, 9, 21 and 23 - Defence of India Rules - R.132A - Appellants acquired foreign exchange in an illegal manner - Complaints filed against them and their application for quashing complaints was dismissed - Whether first appellant can be charged for offences of conspiracy and rule 132 A of the Defence of India Rules - Held, Yes - When the agreement between appellants took place, it was not an offence under the Defence of India Rules nor under the FERA to acquire foreign exchange in a foreign country - Agreement continued even after amendments were made to the legislations, making acquisition of foreign exchange an offence - Acquisition of foreign exchange amounts to conspiracy under section 120 B of IPC - Appeal dismissed.
