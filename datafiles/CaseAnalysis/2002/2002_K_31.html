B.L. Sreedhar and others v K.M. Munireddy (Dead) and others

Supreme Court of India

05 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1462; (2003) 2 SCC 355; AIR 2003 SC 578; 2003 (1) CGLJ 252; 2003 (1) JhrCR(SC) 137; JT 2002 (10) SC 363; 2003 (1) RCR(Civil) 251; 2002(9) SCALE 183; 2002 (7) SLT 135; [2002] Supp4 S.C.R. 601; 2002 (8) Supreme 561

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution

Keywords: Karnataka Village Offices Abolition Act, 1961, Mysore Village Offices Act, 1908, Mysore Village Offices Abolition Act, 1961

Summary: CPC - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - S. 115 - rule of estoppel - an estoppel seems to be when, in consequences of some previous act or statement to which he is either party or privy, a person is precluded from showing the existence of a particular state of facts. - Held, if a man either by words or by conduct has intimated that he consents to an act which has been done and that he will not offer any opposition to it, although it could not have been lawfully done without his consent, and he thereby induces others to do that which they otherwise might have abstained from, he cannot question legality of the act he had sanctioned to the prejudice of those who have so given faith to his words or to the fair inference to be drawn from his conduct. - The rule of estoppel has clear application, and in view of this finding it is not necessary to go into the question whether Explanation 6 of Section 11 C.P.C. has any application or not.
