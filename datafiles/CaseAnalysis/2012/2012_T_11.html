V. Chandrasekaran & Anr. v The Administrative Officer & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 300; (2012) 12 SCC 133; 2012 (95) ALR 884; 2012 (6) AWC 5462; 2013 (1) CalLT 17; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 121; JT 2012 (9) SC 260; 2013 (1) OLR 324; 2012 (4) RCR(Civil) 588; 2012(9) SCALE 142; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 603

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (A)Land & Property - Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 18994, s. 4, 5-A, 6, 16, 17(2), 48 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 21 - Acquisition - Subsequent transfer of land - Validity - Predecessors of appellants (land owners) filed objections u/s. 5-A of the Act against land acquisition notification dt.15-5-1978, which was issued by Respondent (authorities) to acquire the land belonged to predecessors of appellants - Thereafter, predecessors of appellants filed a writ petition before HC and challenged declaration issued u/s. 6 of the Act and notification - Single Judge quashed the declaration, however upheld the notification - Aggrieved appellants filed appeal before DB and challenged the said notification, which was allowed, against which State filed an appeal before SC, which was dismissed - Meanwhile, having received compensation under the acquisition proceedings, tenure-holders/persons-interest in land i.e., predecessors of appellants transferred said land to appellants - Appellants claimed to be in possession of land and filed a writ petition before HC for quashing of notification dt.15-5-1978 pertaining to land comprised 9 survey numbers after 3 decades - Single Judge by order dt.1-11-2010 allowed petition holding that declaration u/s. 6 of the Act was quashed in toto and no fresh declaration was issued thereafter and land acquisition proceedings had lapsed - Aggrieved Housing Board (contesting respondent) filed appeal, which was allowed reversing Single Judge order - Hence, instant appeals - Whether subsequent purchaser could challenge the acquisition proceedings - Held, a person who purchases land subsequent to issuance of a notification u/s. 4 of the Act with respect to it, was not competent to challenge the validity of acquisition proceedings on any ground whatsoever, for the reason that sale deed executed in his favour did not confer upon him, any title and at the most he could claim compensation on the basis of his vendor's title - Appeals dismissed.

(B)Land & Property - Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 18994, s. 4, 5-A, 6, 16, 17(2), 48 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 21 - Acquisition - Challenged - Benefit of - Whether acquisition challenged by one-whether others could take the benefit of same - Held, in the event that person interested did not filed objections in response to a notice issued u/s. 5-A of the Act, and did not challenged acquisition proceedings, the quashing of declaration issued u/s. 6 of the Act in some other case, would not ensure any benefit to such person - More so, where possession of land was already taken, and such land stands vested in State, as provided u/ss. 16 and 17(2) of the Act, prior to the date of decision of Court quashing the declaration in toto, no benefit could be taken by him - Appeals dismissed.

(C)Land & Property - Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 18994, s. 4, 5-A, 6, 16, 17(2), 48 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 21 - Acquisition - Purpose of - Divesting of land - Whether land once vested in Govt.- whether could be divested - Held, once the land was vested in the State, free from all encumbrances, it could not be divested and proceedings under the Act would not lapse, even if an award was not made within the statutorily stipulated period - Land, once acquired, could not be restored to tenure holders/persons-interested, even if it was not used for the purpose for which it was so acquired, or for any other purpose either - Proceedings could not be withdrawn/abandoned under the provisions of s. 48 of the Act, or u/s. 21 of 1897 Act, once the possession of land was taken and the land vests in State, free from all encumbrances - once land was acquired and it vests in State, it was not the concern of land owner, whether land was being used for purpose for which it was acquired or for any other purpose - He becomes persona non-grata once the land vests in State - State neither had requisite power to re-convey the land to person-interested, nor could such person claim any right of restitution on any ground, whatsoever, unless there was some statutory amendment to that effect - Appeals dismissed.

(D)Land & Property - Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 18994, s. 4, 5-A, 6, 16, 17(2), 48 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 21 - Acquisition - Payment of compensation - Transfer of land - Claim of title - Entitled to - Whether impugned order of DB reversing the order of Single Judge was justified - Held, in instant case, tenure holders/person-interested neither filed objections u/s. 5-A of the Act, nor have they challenged land acquisition proceedings, so far as land in question was concerned, instead they chose to withdraw compensation awarded in year 1983 and 1986; after the expiry of about 3 decades and hence, they could not be permitted to challenge the acquisition proceedings on any ground whatsoever - Appellants could not claim title/relief better than what original vendors were entitled to - Appellants have claimed reliefs in writ petitions with respect to not just land in question but in relation to land which was subject matter of an earlier litigation by their predecessors-in-interest - HC did not declare the acquisition proceedings to be void, or purpose for which the land was acquired not to be a 'public purpose' and there was no direction whatsoever, to restore the possession of said land to tenure holders, upon refund of compensation amount by them - Appellants approached the Court with clean hands, and were not entitled for any relief - Sale deeds in favour of appellants are void and unenforceable - Appeals dismissed.
