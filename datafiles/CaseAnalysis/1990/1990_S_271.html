State of Maharashtra and Another v Madhukar Narayan Mardikar

Supreme Court of India

23 October 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 964; (1991) SCC (Cr) 1; (1991) 1 SCC 57; AIR 1991 SC 207; 1991 (1) Bom.C.R. 238; JT 1990 (4) SC 169; 1991 (1) LLJ 269; 1991 (1) SLJ 164; 1991 (1) SLR 140

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Perverse conduct

Summary: Service - Perverse conduct - Removal from service - Justifiability - Respondent (employee) allegedly visited a hutment of one 'B', in uniform and demanded to have sexual inter-course with her - Respondent was charged for his perverse conduct - Inquiry Officer submitted a detailed report holding charges as proved and recommended the dismissal of respondent - Inspector General of Police (IGP) on an examination of report prima facie concurred with findings recorded by Inquiry Officer and directed notice to issue to respondent to show cause why he should not be dismissed from service - Thereafter, respondent filed a detailed reply to second show cause notice - However, respondent was dismissed - Aggrieved respondent filed an appeal against order of dismissal which was partly allowed by removal from service - Aggrieved respondent approached HC by impugned judgment quashed impugned order of removal on the ground that respondent was denied a reasonable opportunity to meet the charges levelled against him as the dept. had failed to supply him with copies of documents - Aggrieved appellant (State) filed instant appeal - Whether impugned judgment passed by HC was justified - Held, even a woman of easy virtue was entitled to privacy and no one could invade her privacy as and when he likes - It was not open to any and every person to violate her person as and when he wishes - She was entitled to protect her person if there was an attempt to violate it against her wish - She was equally entitled to protection of law - Thus, merely because she was a woman of easy virtue, her evidence could not be thrown overboard - At the most the officer called upon to evaluate her evidence would be required to administer caution unto himself before accepting her evidence - But in instant case evidence of 'B' was not only corroborated in material particulars by evidence of her husband but also by evidence of Sub-Inspector 'G' and other members of police party who had accompanied him on receipt of a phone call from respondent - HC was completely wrong in concluding that evidence of 'B' was not corroborated in material particulars by independent evidence - HC embarked upon a re-appreciation of evidence as if it were sitting in appeal against decision of departmental authorities - Its re-appreciation of evidence was unsustainable -Order of removal from service passed by appellant authority was restored - Impugned judgment passed by HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
