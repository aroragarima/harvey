Syed Ahmed Aga Etc. v State Of Mysore & Another

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, Hans Raj Khanna, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 140; (1975) 2 SCC 131; AIR 1975 SC 1443; [1975] Supp S.C.R. 473

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Constitution, arts. 19(1)(g), 19(6), 32 and 304(b), Proviso - Karnataka Silkworm Seed and Cocoon (Regulation of Production, Supply and Distribution) (Amendment) Act, 1969, ss. 3, 4, 5 and 7 - (A) Maintainabuility of Writ Petition u/art. 32 - Petition challenging legality of an Amending Act on ground of imposing additional restrictions without complying with the provisions of art. 304(b) Proviso - (B) Amendment Act, 1969 not assented to by President like Principal Act - No additional restrictions imposed - Amendments introduced by Amending Act - Validity - (C) Alleging infringement of fundamental right guaranteed u/art. 19(1)(g) - Petition against - Maintainability - Held, individuals affected by the violation of the guarantees u/art. 304 can also complain, at the same time, of infringement of their right guaranteed u/art. 19(1)(g) provided a breach of the former involves violation of the latter also as it would ordinarily do - Held not invalid for want of previous sanction of President u/art. 304(b) Proviso - Although the petition would lie, yet it cannot be allowed until such an infringement, falling outside art. 19(6), has been established - Petition Dismissed.
