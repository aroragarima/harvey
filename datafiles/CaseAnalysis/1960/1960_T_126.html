Â State of West Bengal and Others v Naba Kumar Seal

Supreme Court of India

29 August 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 392; AIR 1961 SC 16; 1961 (1) SCJ 235; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 368

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - West Bengal Development and Planning Act, 1948 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Whether Govt. of West Bengal was bound to frame a development scheme under provisions of West Bengal Land Development and Planning Act when it exercised its power of emergency u/s. 7 of Act? - Held, s. 7 is in nature of a proviso to s. 6 - S. 7 provides that in cases of urgency, if state Govt. is satisfied that preparation of a development scheme is likely to be delayed, it may make a declaration u/s. 6 that land was needed for a public purpose, even though no development scheme has either been prepared or sanctioned u/s. 5 - Section, therefore, in clear terms, authorises state Govt. to issue necessary declaration u/s. 6, which puts machinery of land acquisition proceedings into motion, if it is satisfied that public purpose necessitating acquisition of land in question would be subserved without preparation of a development scheme - Appeal allowed.
