Commissioner of Income Taxbombay v E.D.Sheppard

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Raghubar Dayal, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 439; AIR 1963 SC 1343; [1963] 48 ITR 237; [1964] 1 S.C.R. 163

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Termination - Compensation - Profit in lieu of salary - Taxable income - Respondent was given shares by partnership as compensation for loss of employment resulting from premature termination of services - Income Tax Officer bought the value of shares of assesse as consideration for past services - Judicial members disagreed with each other and appellant filed appeal in HC - Tax assessed in head of profit in lieu of salary by appellant and HC ordered against appellant that it was not in profit in lieu of salary and not taxable - Hence, instant appeal - Whether sum of Rs. 2,21,000/- received by respondent was taxable to income tax as 'profit in lieu of salary' u/s. 7 of the Act - Held, sum of Rs. 2,21,000/- received by respondent as employee from his employers on occasion of termination of his services after appropriate notice of one month was not a payment made as compensation for loss of employment and therefore amounted to 'profit in lieu of salary' in view of explanation 2 to s. 7(1) of the Act and was as such taxable to income-tax - Hence, sum of Rs. 2,21,000/- received by respondent was taxable to income-tax as 'profit in lieu of salary' u/s. 7 of the Act - Appeal dismissed.
