Suman Sethi v Ajay k. Churiwal and another

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Phukan, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 816; (2000) 2 SCC 380; (2000) SCC (Cr) 414; AIR 2000 SC 828; 2000 (38) ALR 712; 2001 (2) BC 144; 2000 (5) Bom.C.R. 271; 2001 (1) CalLT(SC) 1; [2000] 100 Comp Cas 444; 2000 (2) CompLJ 40; 2000 CRLJ 1391; 2000 (232) G.L.R. 1071; JT 2000 (1) SC 493; 2000(2) M.P.H.T. 411; 2000 (1) RCR(Criminal) 780; 2000(1) SCALE 385; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 601

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal

Summary: Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138, proviso(b), 139 - Cheque dishonour - Notice of demand u/ cl. (b) to proviso - Notice, however, cannot be faulted if in addition to cheque amount any other sum by way of interest, cost etc. is separately indicated - Cheque amount clearly stated by holder of cheque - Incidental charges and notice charges were also claimed - Held, cheque amount and incidental charges being severable, presumption as contemplated in s. 139 in favour of holder will arise only in respect of cheque amount - Drawer absolved from criminal liability if he pays cheque amount.
