Rajkumar v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

25 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 117; (2014) 5 SCC 353; 2014 CRLJ 1943; 2014 (2) JabLJ 194; 2014 (2) JCC 1217; JT 2014 (3) SC 587; 2014 (1) MLJ(Crl) 699; 2014 (2) RCR(Criminal) 45; 2014(3) SCALE 42; [2014] 3 S.C.R. 212

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 376, 450 and 511 - Rape of minor - Death sentence - Converted to life imprisonment - Challenged - Prosecution alleged that appellant raped and murdered prosecutrix (minor girl) - Trial Court treated it to be a case of extreme culpability and a rarest of rare case awarding death sentence u/s. 302 IPC with a fine of Rs. 3,000/- and u/s. 376 IPC, the appellant was awarded rigorous life imprisonment and a fine of Rs.3,000/- - HC confirmed the death sentence and dismissed the appeal filed against said order - Hence, instant appeals - Whether order of the HC could be upheld -

Held, in view of the concurrent findings of fact recorded by the courts below, particularly in respect of the DNA report to the extent that the semen of the appellant was found in the vagina swab of the prosecutrix and that she died of asphyxia caused by strangulation, Court affirmed the findings of fact recorded by the courts below - For awarding the death sentence, there must be existence of aggravating circumstances and the consequential absence of mitigating circumstances - In the instant case, defenceless minor girl who was in appellant's custody, he was liable to be punished severely but it was not a case which falls within a category of rarest of rare cases - Hence, Court set aside the death sentence and award life imprisonment - Appellant must serve a minimum of 35 years in jail without remission, before consideration of his case for pre-mature release - Appeals disposed of.
