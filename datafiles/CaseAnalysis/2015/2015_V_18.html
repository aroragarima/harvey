Venkatesh and another v Commissioner, Bangalore Development Authority

Supreme Court of India

24 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 839; 2016 (1) ALD(SC) 115; 2015 (113) ALR 700; 2016 (2) CLT(SC) 42; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 725; 2016 (130) RD 435; 2015(10) SCALE 27; [2015] 11 S.C.R. 454

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Acquisition of Suit Land - Order Challenged - Appellant along with others apprehending dispossession from property by development authority approached HC seeking restrained order against BDA - Writ petitions were disposed by HC reserving liberty to approach Civil Court - Aggrieved plaintiffs filed suit before Trial Court, which answered issues, such as claim of adverse possession, lawful enjoyment in affirmative, and issue of acquisition of land by defendant in negative - BDA filed appeals before HC and HC affirmed decree passed in one of suit thereby dismissed appeal and other 4 connected appeals were allowed - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, a party claiming adverse possession must prove that his possession is peaceful, open and continuous. The possession must be adequate in continuity, in publicity and in extent to show that their possession is adverse to the true owner. It must start with a wrongful disposition of the rightful owner and be actual, visible, exclusive, hostile and continued over the statutory period. In instant case, the question is not just whether the plaintiffs were in possession, but whether they had by being in adverse possession for the statutory period of 12 years perfected their title. That question has neither been adverted to nor answered in the judgment impugned in this appeal. Such being the case the High Court, erred in dismissing the appeal filed by the authority. The fact that the plaintiffs had not and could not possibly establish their adverse possession over the suit property should have resulted in dismissal of the suit for an unauthorized occupant had no right to claim relief that would perpetuate his illegal and unauthorized occupation of property that stood vested in the authority. Hence, impugned judgment passed by HC in appeal no. 911/2002 filed by authority is set aside, thereby dismiss plaintiff suit. Further, other appeals are dismissed. Appeals disposed of.
