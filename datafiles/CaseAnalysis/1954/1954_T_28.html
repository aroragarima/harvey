Â Wazir Chand v State of Himachal Pradesh.

Supreme Court of India

22 April 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Vivian Bose, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 34; AIR 1954 SC 415; 1954 CRLJ 1029; 1954 SCJ 600; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 408

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 19 and 31 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 523 - (A) Whether an appeal is preferred when goods in possession of petitioner are seized by police under no authority of law and without order of Magistrate and not under any of ss. 51, 95, 98 and 165 of CrPC, 1898 ? - Held, seizure of goods from possession of petitioner amounts to an infringement of fundamental right both u/arts. 19 and 31 of Constitution and relief can therefore be granted under art. 226 of Constitution - Seizure of goods under no authority of law and without order of Magistrate and not under any of ss. 51, 95, 98 and 165 of CrPC, 1898 infringes fundamental right - (B) Whether an application u/s. 523 of CrPC, 1898 bar remedy under art. 226? - Further held, an application u/s. 523 of CrPC, 1898 will not bar remedy under art. 226 since illegal seizure of goods is an infringement of fundamental rights - (C) Whether Magistrate has any jurisdiction to order return of goods on an application u/s. 523 of CrPC, 1898 made by petitioner? - Further held, application u/s. 523 of CrPC, 1898 in this particular case cannot be sustained as s. 523 had no application to facts and circumstances of case, therefore, Magistrate had no jurisdiction to return these goods to petitioner - Appeal preferred under art. 226 - Appeal allowed.
