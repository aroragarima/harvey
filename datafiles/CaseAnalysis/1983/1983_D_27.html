Alijan Mian And Another v District Magistrate, Dhanbad

Supreme Court of India

13 September 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

R.B. Misra, A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 136; (1983) 4 SCC 301; (1983) SCC (Cr) 840; AIR 1983 SC 1130; 1983 (2) Crimes 760; 1983 CRLJ 1649; 1983 CrLR(SC) 481; 1983(2) SCALE 280; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 939

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Constitution, art. 22 - National Security Act, 1980 - (A) Preventive detention - (1) Detaining authority aware of the fact tht detenu is already in jail custody as criminal case pending on date of order - Also satisfied that if they are enlarged on bail ( likelihood), there was apprehension of breach of public order - Validity of detention - (2) Pendency of criminal proceedingsas to same subject matter - If barred - (B) Apprehension of breach of public order - Subjective satisfaction - (1) Detenu throwing bomb on large gathering for cultural programme - (2) Whether one incident is sufficient - (3) Certain incidents leading to filing of FIRs - Effect of absence of allegations in FIRs about disturbance of public order - (A) Held, valid - No bar - (B) A case of public order and it disturbs tranquility and even tempo of life of public - Depends on nature of incident - Detention order not vitiated when detaining authority had additional material before it for being satisfied as to apprehension of breach of public order - Petition Dismissed.
