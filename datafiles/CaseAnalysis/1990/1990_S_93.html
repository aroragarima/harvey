J.L. Varandani v Smt. Ashalata Mukherjee (Dead) By Lrs

Supreme Court of India

03 August 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, M.M. Punchhi, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 214; (1990) 4 SCC 40; JT 1990 (3) SC 365; 1990 (2) RCR(Rent) 374; 1990 (2) RentLR 163; 1990(2) SCALE 164; [1990] 3 S.C.R. 610; 1990 (2) UJ 410

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958 - West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act, 1956, ss. 17(2), 17(2-A), 17(3) and 17(4) - Whether appellant entitled to relief u/s. 17(4)? - As per s. 17(2-B) which is a mandatory provision provides that no application for extension of time for deposit or payment of any amount u/s. 17(2-A)(a) shall be entertained unless it is made before expiry of time specified in sub-section (1) - Held, no application was made for extension of time - Therefore, in absence of such application it cannot be contended that court is deemed to have condoned delay - Mere fact that court has not passed an order striking of defence as contemplated u/s. 17(3) because of tenant's failure to deposit within time specified in order passed u/ss. 17(2) and 17(2-A) does not necessarily lead to a presumption that time was extended - That being position it must be held that appellant tenant failed to make deposit of rent as per order u/ss. 17(2) and 17(2-A) - Since such a deposit is condition precedent for seeking relief u/s. 17(4) appellant who has not fulfilled same cannot claim said relief - Appeal dismissed.
