Â Chandra Bhushan & Anr. v Deputy Director Of Consolidation (Regional), U.P. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 142; AIR 1967 SC 1272; 1967 ALJ 264; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 286

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - Uttar Pradesh Consolidation of Holdings Act, s. 48 - Whether High Court was justified in rejecting petition if there appears to be a delay? - Ordinarily in absence of a specific statutory rule, HC may be justified in rejecting a petition for a writ of certiorari against judgment of a Subordinate Court or tribunal, if on a consideration of all circumstances, it appears that there is undue delay - In absence of a statutory rule period prescribed for preferring an appeal to HC is a rough measure - In each case primary question is whether applicant has been guilty of laches or undue delay - Keeping in view fact that HC and its offices were, without previous intimation, closed some time about mid-day for Diwali holidays, and Court and its offices re-opened on 13-11-1961 there is no reason to think that appellants would not have presented petition within time - Held, in present case, there are special circumstances which justify departure from general rule - Appeal allowed.
