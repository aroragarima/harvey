C.B.I. v Ashok Kumar Aggarwal

Supreme Court of India

22 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 859; (2014) 14 SCC 295; AIR 2014 SC 827; 2014 CRLJ 930; 2014 (2) RCR(Criminal) 213; 2013(14) SCALE 280

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: disproportionate assets, Validity of sanction

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 91, 92 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 19, 19(3), 19(3)(b), 19(4) - Disproportionate assets - Validity of sanction - Appellant/CBI registered a case against accused/respondent u/ss. 19, 19(3), 19(3)(b), 19(4) of the Act - Trial Court took cognizance and issued summon to respondent - Respondent filed application before Trial Court challenging validity of sanction and the same was dismissed - Respondent filed revision before HC for setting aside said order of Trial Court - HC vide impugned order set aside order of Trial court - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified -

Held, undoubtedly stage of examining validity of sanction was during trial and SC did not propose to say that validity should be examined during stage of inquiry or at pretrial stage - Fact-situation warrant different course altogether as impugned order had already been partly complied with before filing petition before Court - Appellant admittedly did not disclose material facts in this petition - Had said facts been disclosed perhaps Court would not have entertained this petition and matter could have been concluded by Trial Court much earlier - Affidavit filed by sanctioning authority might tilt balance in favor of respondent if duly supported by deponent and not disclosing material fact i.e., filing of such an affidavit by sanctioning authority before Trial Court, indicates serious and substantial prejudice to respondent - Material on record revealed that it could be case of serious prejudice to respondent so far as decision making process by sanctioning authority was concerned - Benefit of interim protection granted in favor of appellant where appellant has not disclosed material facts, should be neutralized - Court opined that peculiar facts and circumstances of case do not warrant any interference - Appeal dismissed.
