Girish Raghunath Mehta v Inspector of Customs and another

Supreme Court of India

07 September 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 641; AIR 2016 SC 4317; 2016 ALL MR (Cri) 4472; 2016 CRLJ 5075; 2016 (344) E.L.T. 803; 2016 (4) JCC (Narcotics) 277; JT 2016 (9) SC 88; 2016 (4) RCR(Criminal) 307; 2016(8) SCALE 692; [2016] 5 S.C.R. 204

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Narcotic Drugs And Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, Constitution of India, 1950, Investigating Officer, Maharashtra NDPS Rules, 1985

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 15, 42, 43, 67, 8(c) - Maharashtra NDPS Rules, 1985 - Possession of Narcotics Drugs - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant/accused allegedly found selling crushed opium poppy straw without any bills on cash basis to co-accused - Trial Court convicted accused u/s. 15 r/w s. 8(c) of the Act and sentenced him to undergo RI for 4 years with fine, to further undergo RI for 6 months for illegal sale of 30 Kgs. of poppy straw to co-accused - On appeal, HC partly allowed appeal, quashed and set aside conviction and sentence for illegal possession poppy straw powder and upheld conviction and sentence for illegal sale transaction of 30 kgs. of poppy straw powder - Hence instant appeals - Whether there was any perversity or illegality in impugned judgments of Courts below.

Held, in instant case there is adequate evidence to prove sale of contraband by appellant for which co-accused has been convicted and sentenced. Courts below have concurrently held that appellant was found to have sold the contraband to co-accused without any licence. The said finding, inter alia, is based on the evidence of PW1/Inspector of Customs who seized the contraband from co-accused. Further, evidence in the form of statement of appellant himself (Ex.20) u/s. 67 of the Act before his arrest clearly shows that appellant had sold the contraband to co-accused- who did not have any licence to purchase thereof. However, connection of contraband with appellant was clearly established after which the burden was on appellant to show that he had effected sale to an authorized person. Recovery from co-accused was from an open place to which s. 42 of the Act is not attracted. At the time of production of gunny bag no objection was raised on behalf of appellant that the bag did not carry any label or sign of identity. Thus, absence of label and sign of identity could not be presumed. The samples were duly tested by chemical analyzer and were found to be intact. Thus, no serious infirmity in findings recorded by Courts below in convicting and sentencing appellant. Appeals dismissed.
