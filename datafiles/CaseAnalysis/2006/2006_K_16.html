Rajesh Kumar Aggarwal and Others v K.K. Modi and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 92; (2006) 4 SCC 385; AIR 2006 SC 1647; 2006 (3) ALD(SC) 61; 2006 (63) ALR 335; 2006 (2) AWC 1886; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 58; JT 2006 (3) SC 607; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 70; 2006 (2) RCR(Civil) 577; 2006 (3) RLW 1882; 2006(3) SCALE 312; 2006 (3) SCJ 268; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 175

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: [A.] Corporate - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 6, r. 17, s. 151 - Indian Trusts Act, 1882, ss. 60, 61 - Suit for declaration, permanent injunction and mandatory injunction against respondents, Trustees of Trust and Secretary of Trust - Appellants are beneficiaries of trust - Application for amendment of plaint allowed by single judge but dismissed by Appellate Court on ground that proposed amendment introduces a totally different, new and inconsistent case and that application does not appear to have been made in good faith and at instance of some one behind curtain - Appeal against - Held, entire case of plaintiff revolves around equity shares of GPI and that dividend declared thereon are not accounted for - Prayer by way of amendment was sought to amend plaint and to incorporate relief of mandatory injunction directing defendants to sell shares of GPI held by Trust and use sale proceeds thereof for benefit of beneficiaries - Amendment sought for does not introduce a new cause of action inconsistent with case made out in original plaint - Appeals allowed.
