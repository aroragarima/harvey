Tarsem Singh v Ramesh Chand Aggarwal

Supreme Court of India

09 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2355; (2009) 16 SCC 348

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Eviction

Summary: Rent Control - Himachal Pradesh Urban Rent Control Act, 1987, s. 15(2) - Eviction - Retirement - Appellant filed eviction petition on the ground that he was retiring and so required the premises for his own residence - Competent authority dismissed the petition on the ground that the appellant was owning other properties - Appellant filed revision which was also dismissed on the ground that appellant had not filed a certificate from the authority competent about his removal from service - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, it must be mentioned that when the appellant retired, a notification was also issued setting out that the appellant had so retired and that notification was also placed on record in the eviction petition. In the absence of any particular form or requirement of retirement certificate, the HC erred in holding that the letter was not sufficient. The letter clearly indicated that the appellant was retiring. Further, in this case, there is also a notification. The notification is in the name of the Governor and the Governor is the authority competent to remove him from service. This notification confirms the fact of retirement. The appellant has also filed an affidavit, as required under the section. Therefore, all the requirements of s. 15(2) of the 1987 Act have been complied with. Appeal allowed.
