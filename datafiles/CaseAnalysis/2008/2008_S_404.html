Monotosh Saha v Special Director, Enforcement Directorate and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1336; (2008) 12 SCC 359; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 881; 2008 (6) AWC 6414; 2009 (1) CalLT(SC) 1; 2008 (229) E.L.T. 492; JT 2008 (11) SC 146; 2008 (8) MLJ(SC) 304; 2008 (4) RCR(Criminal) 348; 2008(11) SCALE 603; [2008] 12 S.C.R. 444

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Fema

Keywords: Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, Adjudication Proceedings and Appeal Rules, 1974

Summary: Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999; Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973 - M/s Godsons (India) and its proprietor, the present appellant had acquired foreign exchange contravening the provisions of s. 8(1) - Penalty of Rs.25 lakhs - Order directing deposit of 60% of the penalty amount for the purpose of entertaining the appeal - Appeal against - Held, on merely establishing a prima facie case, interim order of protection should not be passed - But if on a cursory glance it appears that the demand raised has no leg to stand, it would be undesirable to require the assessee to pay full or substantive part of the demand - While dealing with the application twin requirements of considerations i.e. consideration of undue hardship aspect and imposition of conditions to safeguard the realization of penalty have to be kept in view - Appeal disposed of.
