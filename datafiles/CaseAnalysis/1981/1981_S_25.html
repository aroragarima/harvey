Superintendent and Remembrancer of Legal Affairs, West Bengal v Satyen Bhowmick and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 January 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 120; (1981) 2 SCC 109; (1981) SCC (Cr) 342; AIR 1981 SC 917; 1981 CRLJ 341; 1981 CrLR(SC) 136; 1981(1) SCALE 179; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 661

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 126 - CrPC, 1973,s. 548 - Official Secrets Act, 1923,s. 14 - Interpretation of s. 14 of Official Secrets Act, 1923 - Held, s. 14 apart from providing that proceedings of Court may be held in camera under circumstances mentioned in section, does not in any way affect or override provisions of CrPC relating to enquiries or trials held thereunder - S. 14 does not in any way deprive valuable rights of accused to get copies of statements recorded by Magistrate or statements of witnesses recorded by police or documents obtained by Police during investigation nor does s. 14 in any way affect right of accused to get copies u/s. 548 of CrPC - Opening words of s. 14 do not amount to a non obstante cl. but are merely in nature of an enabling provision reserving inherent powers of Court to exclude public from proceedings if Court is of opinion that it is just and expedient to do so - Further held, there was absolutely no impropriety on part of Magistrate in not taking action against defence lawyer for his refusal to show his register because lawyer had rightly claimed privilege u/s. 126 of Evidence Act as register contained instructions given by client which being privileged could not be disclosed to Court - Hence strictures passed by HC on Magistrate as also on lawyer of defence were totally unwarranted - Appeal disposed of.
