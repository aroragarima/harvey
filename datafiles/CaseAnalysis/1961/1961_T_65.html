Supreme Court of India

27 January 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, J.L. Kapur, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 354; AIR 1962 SC 29; 1962 CRLJ 99; 1961 (2) FLR 586; 1961 (2) LLJ 77; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 592

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Mines & Minerals; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Factories Act, 1948, ss. 2 (m), 2 (k)and 92 - Whether salt work can be brought under ambit of premises under Factories Act? - Held, premises is a generic term meaning open land or land with buildings or buildings alone - Expression 'premises including precincts' does not necessarily mean that premises must always have precincts - Even buildings need not have any precincts - Though, some of the provisions of Act cannot be applicable to salt work where process of converting sea water into salt is carried on in open - An occupier is to comply with such provisions of Act which apply to factory he is working - Hence, such salt work would come within meaning of word 'premises' used in definition in s. 2 (m) - Appeal dismissed.
