Tripura Goods Transport Association & Anr v Commissioner Of Taxes & Ors

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Misra, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 423; (1999) 2 SCC 253; AIR 1999 SC 719; JT 1998 (9) SC 62; 1998(6) SCALE 620; 1999 (1) SLT 42; 1999 (112) STC 609; [1998] Supp3 S.C.R. 622; 1998 (9) Supreme 517

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Constitution - Sales Tax- Tripura Sales Tax Act, 1976, ss. 29, 30, 32, 36A and 38B - Tripura Sales Tax Rules, 1994, rr.46A, 63A and 64A - Constitution, sch. 7, list 2, entry 54, art. 301 - Challenge to constitutional validity of the Tripura Sales Tax (11th Amendment) Rules, 1994 and ss. 29, 32 and 36A of Tripura Sales Tax Act, 1976 including notifications D/- 23-09-1994 and 15-10-1994 - (A) The provisions of ss.29, 30, 32, 36A, 38B of Act and rr.46A, 63A, 64A of the 1994 Rules alleged to be lacking legislative competence - (B) Whether requirement of s. 38-B for transporter of taxable goods in state to obtain certificate of registration from Commissioner of Taxes is violative of art. 301 and restraint on trade - Held, neither ss. 29, 30, 32, 36A nor rr. 46A, 63A, 64-A lack any legislative competence - They fall under list 2, entry 54 of 7th sch. of Constitution - Does not impede free flow of trade, it is brought to check evasion of tax and is not violative of art. 301 of Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
