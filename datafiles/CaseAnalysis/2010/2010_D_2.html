Contship Container Lines Limited v D. K. Lall and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 214; (2010) 4 SCC 256; 2011 ACJ 540; AIR 2010 SC 1704; 2010 (4) ALD(SC) 53; 2010 (82) ALR 28; 2010 (3) AWC 2481; 2010 (3) Bom.C.R. 24; 2008 (4) CPJ(Sc) 12; 2013 (297) E.L.T. 321; JT 2010 (3) SC 201; 2010(3) SCALE 148; [2010] 3 S.C.R. 460

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Consumer Protection; Insurance

Keywords: Carriage Of Goods By Sea, Underwriter, Marine Insurance, Utmost Good Faith, Insurable Property, Marine Adventure, Circumstance, Marine Insurance Act, 1906

Summary: Consumer Protection - Insurance - Carriers & Transportation - Marine Insurance Act, 1963 - Indian Carriage of Goods by Sea Act, 1925 - Deficiency of service - Liability of carrier - Exporter hired services of a Transport System for export of iron furniture and iron handicraft items and miniature paintings to purchasers in Spain - All items were packed in 122 cartons - Container was put on board in a vessel belonging to appellant for shipment to Spain - Exporter had also obtained a Marine Cargo/Inland transit insurance policy to cover risks enumerated in policy - When consignment reached Spain, out of 122 cartons one carton comprising miniature paintings was not delivered to the consignee - Claim for payment of compensation on account of alleged deficiency of service was denied by Shipping Company and Insurance Company - Exporter approached National Commission - National Commission held that Insurance Policy was obtained on representation that transactions between exporter and purchasers were on CIF (Cost Insurance and Freight) basis whereas consignment was in fact been sent on FOB (Free on Board) basis which absolved Insurance Company of any liability for failure of insured to maintain utmost good faith essential for a marine insurance policy - National Commission further held that service provided by carrier was deficient but held that liability of carrier for payment of compensation to consignee was limited by provisions of Sea Act - Review petition filed against said order was dismissed - Hence, present appeal - (A) Liability of insurance company - Held, in B.K. Wadeyar v. Daulatram Rameshwarlal 1961 INDLAW SC 404 SC held that in FOB contracts for sale of goods, property is intended to pass and does pass on shipment of goods - National Commission was, therefore, right in holding that seller had no insurable interest in goods thereby absolving insurance company of liability to reimburse loss, if any, arising from mis-delivery of such goods - In the present case, duty to make a complete disclosure not having been observed by exporter, claim made by shipper against insurance company was rightly rejected by National Commission on ground that shipper had not observed utmost good faith while obtaining insurance cover - (B) Liability of carrier - Held, carton containing miniature paintings had been mis-delivered by carrier who ought to have taken care to deliver same to consignee concerned - Carrier liable - (C) Determination of compensation - Held, Bill of Lading was only document on basis of which compensation could be determined against carrier in terms of provisions of Sea Act and Schedule thereto - In present case, National Commission has instead of going by number of packages entered in Bill of Lading, has gone by packages mentioned in packing list - National Commission could not go beyond Bill of Lading and award compensation on basis of packing list - Further, National Commission have gone by unamended provisions of r. 5 in which amount of compensation was stipulated to be US$ 100 per package - After amendment to Schedule in year 1992 amount of compensation was to be paid in terms of Special Drawing Rights - (D) Whether shipper would be entitled to value of goods mis-delivered? - Held, compensation by reference to value of goods lost or damaged can be claimed only if nature or value of such goods was declared by shipper before shipment and inserted in Bill of Lading - Requirement of 'insertion of same in Bill of Lading' was not satisfied in the present case - Bill of Lading does not mention either nature or value of goods - That being so, compensation of rupee equivalent of 666.67 Special Drawing Rights was only amount that could be awarded by Commission to shipper - Appeals disposed of.
