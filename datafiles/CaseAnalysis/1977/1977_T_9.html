Ram Lakhan Singh and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, A.C. Gupta, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 84; (1977) 3 SCC 268; (1977) SCC (Cr) 474; AIR 1977 SC 1936; 1977 CRLJ 1566; 1977 CrLR(SC) 294; [1978] 1 S.C.R. 125

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 286, 367 -Indian Evidence Act, 1872, 3, 54 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Dacoity with murder - Benefit of doubt - Families at daggers drawn - Only deceased family examined - Non-examination of neighboring independent witnesses - Whether Supreme Court had jurisdiction for re examination of evidence in an appeal? - Held, When appreciation of evidence against accused by Sessions Judge as well as High Court is replete with infirmities affecting very quality of appreciation in appeal u/art. 136 of Constitution by accused persons sentenced to death by both above Courts for offence of murder, SC will have to undertake for itself, in interest of justice, a thorough examination of evidence and entire circumstances to satisfy itself about guilt of accused who have been awarded extreme penalty under law - Whether conviction of accused was justified? - After having examined entire evidence and circumstances in this case SC unable to affirm conviction on oral testimony of aforesaid five witnesses - The prosecution has not established charges against accused beyond reasonable doubt - Further held, four accused granted benefit of reason - Able doubt and acquit them of all charges - The judgment and order of HC sentencing accused to death and other sentences are set aside and accused shall be released from detention forthwith - Appeal allowed.
