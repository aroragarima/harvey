State Of Tamil Nadu Through Superintendent Of Police,Cbi/Sit v Nalini And 25 Others

Supreme Court of India

11 May 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 810; (1999) 5 SCC 253; (1999) SCC (Cr) 691; AIR 1999 SC 2640; AIR 1999 SCW 1889; 1999 CRLJ 3124; JT 1999 (4) SC 106; 1999(3) SCALE 241; [1999] 3 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Sovereignty And Integrity Of India, Batteries, Dress, Joint Trial, Second Trial, Approver, Developments, TADA Act, Autrefois Convict, Acquaintance, Cyanide, Inflammable Substances Act, 1952

Summary: The Judgment was delivered by Thomas J.- Rajiv Gandhi, a former Prime Minister of India was assassinated on 21-5-1991 at a place called Sriperumbudur in Tamil Nadu. The assassin was an adolescent girl named Dhanu who was made into a human bomb and she got herself exploded at 10.19 p.m. at a very close proximity to the visiting former Prime Minister. These appeals by right are under Section 19 of the Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987 (TADA for short). - Appeal Dismissed.
