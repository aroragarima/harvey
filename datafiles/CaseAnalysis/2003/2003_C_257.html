Indra Bhanu Gaur v Committee Management of M.M. Degree College and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 957; (2004) SCC (L&S) 176; (2004) 1 SCC 281; AIR 2004 SC 248; 2004 (1) ALD(SC) 14; 2004 (2) AWC 1381; 2004 (1) CLR 286; 2004 (4) ESC 532; 2003 (99) FLR 1040; JT 2003 (8) SC 471; 2004 (1) LLN 16; 2003(9) SCALE 454; 2003 (1) SCW 5959; 2004 (1) SLJ 370; 2004 (1) SLR 232; 2003 (7) SLT 196; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 327; 2003 (8) Supreme 1; 2004 (1) UPLBEC 457

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Service

Summary: Service - Termination - Appellant found guilty of irregularities and illegalities in the conduct of examination by the Committee appointed by the Vice-Chancellor - Denial of opportunity - Held, unless prejudice is shown and established, mere non-payment of subsistence allowance cannot ipso facto be a ground to vitiate the proceedings in every case. It has to be specifically pleaded and established as to in what way the affected employee is handicapped because of non-receipt of subsistence allowance. Unless that is done, it cannot be held as absolute proposal in law that non-payment of subsistence allowance amounts to denial of opportunity and vitiates departmental proceedings - Further held, matter remitted for fresh adjudication.
