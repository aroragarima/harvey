Animal Welfare Board of India v A. Nagaraja and others

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 412; (2014) 7 SCC 547; 2014 (4) Bom.C.R. 26; JT 2014 (7) SC 1; 2014 (2) KLT 717; 2014 (4) MLJ 92; 2014(6) SCALE 468; [2014] 6 S.C.R. 646

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Keywords: Spirit, Environment, Venue, Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act, 1890, Prevention of Cruelty to Draught and Pack Animals Rules, 1965, Performing Animals (Registration) Rules, 2001, Tamil Nadu Regulation of Jallikattu Act, 2009, Constitutional 47th Amendment Act, 1956, Animal Welfare Act, 2010, Animals Welfare Act, 2006, Austrian Federal Animal Protection Act, Prevention of Cruelty to Animals (Transportation of Animals on Foot) Rules, 2001, Performing Animal Rules, 1973

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21, 51A, 246 and 254 - Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act, 1960, ss. 3, 11, 21, 22 and 28 - Tamil Nadu Regulation of Jallikattu Act, 2009, ss. 2 an 5 - Rights of animals - Jallikattu, Bullock-cart races - Torture and cruelty meted out to bullocks - Animal Welfare Board of India (ABWI), a statutory Board, constituted for the promotion of animal welfare and for the purpose of protecting the animals from being subjected to unnecessary pain or suffering has taken up a specific stand that Jallikattu, Bull/Bullock-cart races etc., conducted in the States of Tamil Nadu and Maharashtra violate the provisions of the 1960, Act, particularly, ss. 11(1)(a), 11(1)(m) and 22 of the 1960, Act - ABWI high-lighted the manner in which Jallikattu was being conducted, especially in the Southern Part of the State of Tamil Nadu, and how the bulls involved were physically and mentally tortured for human pleasure and enjoyment - Further, ABWI submitted that the Bulls which were forced to participate in the race were subjected to considerable pain and suffering, which violated s. 3 and ss. 11(1)(a) and (m) of the 1960, Act r/w arts. 51A(g) and 21 of the Constitution and hence exhibition or training them as performing animals be completely banned - (A) Whether Bulls could be used as a performing animals for Jallikattu and Bullock-cart Race -

Held, Bulls could not be used as a Performing Animals for Jallikattu and Bullock-cart Race, since they were draught and pack animals, not anatomically designed for such performances.

(B) Whether 2009, Act was repugnant to the provisions of the 1960, Act and the rules made thereunder and State could not give effect to it in the absence of the assent of the President u/art. 254 of the Constitution -

Held, repugnancy between the Parliamentary Legislation and State Legislation arise in two ways: (i) where the legislations, though enacted with respect to the matters in their allotted sphere, overlap conflict and (ii) where two legislations were with respect to the same matters in the concurrent list and there was a conflict - In both the situations, the Parliamentary legislation would predominate in the first by virtue of the non-obstante clause in art. 246(1) of the Constitution, and in the second by reason of art. 254(1) of the Constitution - In the instant case, 2009, Act was an anthropocentric legislation enacted not for the welfare of the animals, unlike 1960, Act, which was an eco-centric legislation, enacted to ensure the well-being and welfare of the animals and to prevent unnecessary pain or suffering of the animals - State Act safeguards the interest of the organizers and spectators while conducting the event of Jallikattu - Further, s. 2(c) of 2009, Act speaks of 'taming of bulls' which was inconsistent and contrary to the provisions of Chapter V of 1960, Act - Bull tamers, therefore, tame the bulls at the arena, thereby causing strain, stress, inflict pain and suffering, which 1960, Act wanted to prevent u/s. 11 of the 1960, Act - Further, 1960, Act, made an offence, if any person solely with a view to provide entertainment, incite any animal to fight - Fight could be with an animal or a human being - S. 5 of 2009, Act envisaged a fight between a Bull and Bull tamers; such fight was prohibited u/s. 11(1)(m)(ii) of 1960, Act r/w s. 3 of the 1960, Act - 1960, Act, therefore, cast not only duties on human beings, but also confer corresponding rights on animals, which was being taken away by the State Act (2009, Act) by conferring rights on the organizers and Bull tamers, to conduct Jallikattu, which was inconsistent and in direct collision with ss. 3, 11(1)(a), 11(1)(m)(ii) and 22 of the 1960, Act r/w arts. 51A(g) & (h) of the Constitution and hence repugnant to the 1960, Act, which was a welfare legislation and hence declared unconstitutional and void, being violative of art. 254(1) of the Constitution.

(C) Whether the events that were being conducted in the States of Tamil Nadu and Maharashtra were in violation of ss. 3, 11(1)(a) & (m), 21 and 22 of the 1960, Act r/w art. 51A(g) and (h) of the Constitution and the notification dtd. 11-7-2011 -

Held, AWBI was right in its stand that Jallikattu, Bullock-cart Race and such events per se violate ss. 3, 11(1)(a) and 11(1)(m)(ii) of 1960, Act - Uphold the Notification dtd. 11-7-2011 issued by the Central Government, consequently, Bulls could not be used as performing animals, either for the Jallikattu events or Bullock-cart Races in the State of Tamil Nadu, Maharashtra or elsewhere in the country - Rights guaranteed to the Bulls u/ss. 3 and 11 of 1960, Act r/w arts. 51A(g) & (h) of the Constitution could not be taken away or curtailed, except u/ss. 11(3) and 28 of 1960, Act - AWBI and Govts. were directed to take steps to prevent the infliction of unnecessary pain or suffering on the animals, since their rights have been statutorily protected u/ss. 3 and 11 of 1960, Act - Petitions disposed of.
