Chandrika Misir and Another v Bhaiya Lal

Supreme Court of India

31 July 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

D.G. Palekar, A. Alagiriswami

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 172; (1973) 2 SCC 474; AIR 1973 SC 2391; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 290

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950 art.136 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 47, 100 and 101 - Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1951, s. 209 - (A) New plea - Where court is inherently lacking in jurisdiction - Plea as to jurisdiction may be raised at any stage, even if it was not raised in Trial Court - (B) Suit for ejectment of person retaining possession of land of bhumidhar contrary to law - Jurisdiction of civil court - Held, such a plea can be raised even in execution proceedings on ground that decree is a nullity - Must be filed in court mentioned in Column 4 of Sch.II as jurisdiction of ordinary civil court is barred by s. 331 - Appeal dismissed.
