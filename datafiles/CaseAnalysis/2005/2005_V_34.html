Commissioner of Central Excise, Aurangabad v Videocon International Limited

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, AR. Lakshmanan, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1027; (2005) 13 SCC 261; 123 ECR 7; 2005 (180) E.L.T. 312; 2005 (66) RLT 892; 2006 (147) STC 87

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: P />Sales Tax - Assessment of tax - Determination of - Assessee was selling its products through their Depots in different states and paid sales tax whereas in some States, assessee had benefit of exemption from sales tax - Assessment was made by Assessing Officer holding that price was higher in that State, that price should be applicable for sales throughout India - Hence, instant Appeals.

Held, in all matters concerned assessee was only selling its products through their Depots. In some States where assessee had to pay sales tax they deducted sales tax paid from price of goods. In States where assessee had benefit of exemption from sales tax, they paid sales tax on price without deducing the sales tax element. Therefore, plea of department that as price was higher in that State, that price should be applicable for sales throughout India. Such stand is clearly untenable. Therefore, Collector (Appeals) and Tribunal has rightly passed order in favor of assessees. Hence, there is no ground for interfere. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - When assessee paid all taxes arises in different states then assessment of tax for same in Indian cannot be sustained.
