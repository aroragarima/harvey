Bharat Forge Company Limited v Uttam Manohar Nakate

Supreme Court of India

18 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 36; (2005) SCC (L&S) 298; (2005) 2 SCC 489; AIR 2005 SC 947; 2005 (3) Bom.C.R. 762; 2005 (21) CLR 533; 2005 (116) DLT 513; 2005 (104) FLR 812; JT 2005 (1) SC 303; 2005 (1) LLJ 738; 2005 (1) LLN 625; 2005(1) SCALE 345; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 545; 2005 (2) SLR 43; 2005 (1) UPLBEC 868

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Disciplinary Proceeding, Disciplinary Authority

Summary: Service - Labour & Industrial Law - Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions and Prevention of Unfair Labour Practices Act, 1971 , ss. 26, 27, 28 - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946 - Commission of misconduct - Dismissal from service - Appeal herein against order of reinstatement - Whether punishment imposed was disproportionate? - Held, in absence of any plea of factual victimization and furthermore in absence of any foundational fact having been laid down for arriving at a conclusion of legal victimization, Division Bench committed a manifest error in invoking cl (a) - In facts and circumstances of case and having regard to past conduct of Respondent as also his conduct during domestic enquiry proceeding, it cannot be said that quantum of punishment imposed upon Respondent was wholly disproportionate to his act of misconduct or otherwise arbitrary - Appeal allowed.
