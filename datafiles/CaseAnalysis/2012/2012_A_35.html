State of Uttar Pradesh and others v Ambrish Tandon and another

Supreme Court of India

20 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 603; (2012) 5 SCC 566; 2012 (2) ADJ 545; 2012 (2) AWC 1836; 2012 (1) CTC 556; JT 2012 (4) SC 48; 2012 (3) MLJ(SC) 714; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 865; 2012 (115) RD 399; 2012 (2) RLW 1443; [2012] 1 S.C.R. 422

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Demand Notice

Summary: Administrative - Land & Property - Indian Stamp Act, 1899, ss. 47A,33 - Stamp Duty deficiency - Demand Notice - Quashed - Sustainability - During inspection of building of respondent it was found that stamp duty deficiency of Rs. 23,50,000/- was paid less during time of purchase by respondents - Case u/ss. 47A,33 of Act was registered and Additional Collector (Finance & Revenue) directed respondents to pay deficiency stamp duty and also imposed penalty for Tax evasion - Demand notice claiming an amount of Rs. 38,30,500/- plus 10% recovery charges were issued - Aggrieved respondents filed writ petition in nature of certiorari for quashing - HC, allowed petition of petitioner and quashed demand notice - Hence instant appeal - Respondents contended that stamp duty was paid on total sale consideration and when notice was issued they had submitted objections which was not considered and further they had not been given sufficient opportunity and orders were passed in most arbitrary manner - Appellant contended that as per Act and rules made therein, there was provision for appeal and not writ petition since writ petition u/art. 226 was not permissible - Whether HC, was justified in interfering with order passed by Additional Collector - Held, merely because property was being used for commercial purpose at later point of time may not be a relevant criterion for assessing value for purpose of stamp duty, nature of user was relatable to date of purchase and it was relevant for purpose of calculation of stamp duty - Though matter could have been considered by Appellate Authority in view of our reasoning that there was no serious objection and in fact said alternative remedy was not agitated seriously and in view of factual details based on which HC had quashed order passed by Additional District Collector, hence SC, was not inclined to interfere at this juncture as there were no valid ground for interference - Appeal dismissed.
