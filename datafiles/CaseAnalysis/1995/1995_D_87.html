Dr Vimal (Mrs) v Bhaguji and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 May 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, Faizan Uddin

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1379; (1996) 9 SCC 351; AIR 1995 SC 1836; JT 1995 (6) SC 198; 1995(3) SCALE 423; [1995] Supp1 S.C.R. 392

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 123 and 123 A - Corrupt practices in election - Speeches in election meeting alleged to be made with consent of candidate on score of religion and to promote communal hatred - Speeches published in newspaper - Court inference against authenticity of speeches - Whether elected candidate guilty of corrupt practice? - Held, court can draw adverse inference against authenticity of gist of speeches - Elected candidate cannot be termed as guilty of corrupt practice in absence of clinching and un-impeachable evidence - Final decision regarding issue cannot be made without giving collaborators, an opportunity of being heard - Appeal allowed.
