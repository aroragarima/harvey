Messrs Agrawal Trading Corporation and Others v Assistant Collector of Customs, Calcutta and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, K.S. Hegde, D.G. Palekar

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 743; (1972) SCC (Cr) 317; (1972) 1 SCC 553; AIR 1972 SC 648; [1972] 42 Comp Cas 532; 1973 CRLJ 474; 1973 (2) CTR 378; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1467; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 85

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Customs - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950 - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1947, ss. 23A, 23C, 8 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 3(42) - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Sale of Goods Act, 1930 - Sea Customs Act 1878, ss. 167, 167(3), 167(37), 167(8), 19 - Currency - Burden of Proof - Maintainability - Customer authorities served notice on appellant firm by pointing out that exportation of Indian currency out of India was in contravention of s. 8 (2) of Foreign Exchange Regulations Act 1947 r/w Reserve Bank of India Notification dated 27-2-1951 - It was asked to show cause and to produce within four days of receipts of notice, permit, if any, of Reserve Bank of India, for export of Indian currency and failing to do sowould be liable for prosecution u/s. 23(1) r/w s. 8(2) of the 1947 Act - On 13-4-1959, appellant firm replied to notice denying that firm had anything to do with despatch of box containing currency notes; that it was not aware of any person RS or IL, or that BT had ever dispatched consignment in question or visited any Air office in connection therewith - HC in reply affidavit affirmed on 11-11-1960 to affidavit in opposition, GLG one of partners of firm went even to extent of denying that BT was Cashier of firm, notwithstanding fact that in earlier reply to show cause notice as also in Writ Petition, it was tacitly assumed that he was Cashier - Apart from criminal prosecutions that were launched against partners, in penalty proceedings firm was held to be knowingly concerned in offence and accordingly, a fine of Rs. 1,000/- was imposed on it u/s. 167(3) of the 1878 Act with further personal liability of Rs. 1,000/u/s. 167(37) of the Act - It was further fined Rs. 51,0001- u/s. 167(8) of Act r/w s. 23 (1) of the 1947 Act - Order was challenged before HC and HC issued rule but later discharged it - Against that order appeal was filed and was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, facts of instant case clearly disclosed well laid plain - Currency notes were secreted in cavity and were sought to be dispatched out of country in package which ostensibly looked innocuous - Manner in which attempt was made was to hood-wink Customs officials and escape their detection - Further, consignor and consignee were not shown as real persons but were fictitious so that even if attempt to smuggle out of country currency notes was detected, real persons could not be traced - Charges and expenses incurred in connection with dispatch found in entries in books of account of firm were same those relating to offending package which was being dispatched to Hong Kong - Freight mentioned in account slip exact amount which appeared on consignment note in respect of that offending package - Amount sought to be sent was half lakh of rupees which could hardly be within means of Cashier leading to inescapable inference that firm through its partners was concerned in attempt to transgress restrictions u/s. 8 of the 1947 Act and liable to penal action by virtue of s.23A under provisions of the 1878 Act - Hence, HC was justified in concluding that firm was interested in sending currency notes out of India in clandestine manner - Appeal dismissed.
