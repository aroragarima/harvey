Electronics Trade And Technology Development v Indian Technologists And Engineers[Electronics]

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1120; (1996) SCC (Cr) 454; (1996) 2 SCC 739; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 900; AIR 1996 SC 2339; AIR 1996 SCW 840; 1996 (27) ALR 364; 1996 (1) BC 217; 1996 (2) Bom.C.R. 150; 1996 (1) CCR 136; [1996] 86 Comp Cas 30; 1996 (3) Crimes 82; 1996 CRLJ 1692; 1996 JCC 155; JT 1996 (1) SC 643; 1996 (4) KarLJ 351; 1996 (1) KarLJ 726; 1996(1) SCALE 636; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 843; 1996 (1) Supreme 744

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal

Summary: Negotiable Instruments Act 1881, s.138 - Drawer Instruct his banker for non payment - Bank returned the Cheque to payee with such endorsement - Payee issued notice - Payment not made within 15 days from date of receipt of notice - Whether dishonest intention can be inferred - Held, primafacie case made out - Dishonest intention.
