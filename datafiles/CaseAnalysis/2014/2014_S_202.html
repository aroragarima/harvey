Mukesh v State of Chhattisgarh

Supreme Court of India

25 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 650; (2014) 10 SCC 327; 2014 CRLJ 4900; JT 2014 (10) SC 532; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 447; 2014(11) SCALE 418

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conviction

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376 - Rape - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant/accused committed offence punishable u/s. 376 of IPC - Trial Court passed conviction order - Appellant filed appeal before HC and the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC should have given benefit of doubt to appellant based on contradictions regarding date of incident, FIR, charge sheet and statements of prosecutrix and prosecution witnesses.

Held, most important fact, that prosecutrix had narrated incident of rape immediately after its commission, gives us strong reason to believe version of prosecution. Conduct of other witnesses including that of her husband is very natural. Evidence of PW12, who is neighbour of accused and as such has neither any enmity with accused nor was he friend with PW10 and others has also supported case of prosecution. PW-5, who had medically examined accused, has stated in his report, that he was capable of having sex. Further, from place of occurrence, broken bangles of prosecutrix were recovered and seizure memo, was prepared in this respect. Delay in lodging FIR has been well explained by prosecution and thus, it cannot be considered a ground for acquittal of accused. It is clear from facts and circumstances of case that prosecutrix, being married lady, could not have lodged FIR on her own, especially in case of Indian circumstances. As stated in facts on record, her husband was not in village and returned on following evening of incident. Incidence had occurred late in night and there was no elder person of family present to go to Police Station and lodge complaint regarding incident. Hence, it is natural for her to wait for her husband to return. This fact is verified by the statements of PW11 and PW2. With regard to alleged discrepancy regarding date of occurrence of incident is also disregarded by SC in light of facts and circumstance of case. Evidence on record is sufficient to affirm guilt of accused on charge framed against him. Hence, accused is not entitled to benefit of doubt as pleaded by him before SC. SC do not see any cogent reason to interfere with findings of fact recorded by Courts below. Appeal dismissed.
