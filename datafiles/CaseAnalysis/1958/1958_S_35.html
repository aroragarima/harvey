Â Messrs Kasturi and Sons Private Limited v Shri N. Salivateeswaran and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 30; AIR 1958 SC 507; 1958 (1) LLJ 527; 1958 (2) MLJ(SC) 130; 1958 SCJ 844; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950, Working Journalists (Conditions of Service) and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1955

Summary: Constitution - Payment of Wages Act, 1936 - Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 11 - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act,1938 - Working Journalists (Conditions of Service) and Miscellaneous Provisions Act,1955, s. 17 - Agreement - Termination - Recovery of money - Ultra vires - Petitioner was registered a daily newspaper - Supply of news by respondent no.1 / journalist to Hindu was under an agreement under which paid fixed monthly honorarium - Respondent no.1 left India - Petitioner relieved of duties of respondent no.1 and terminated - Respondent no.1 made an application u/s.17 of 1955 Act - Petitioner provides only for mode of recovery of any money due to working journalist - S.17 of 1955 act was ultra vires and void - Hence, instant petition - Petitioner challenged u/art. 32 of Constitution - Whether Competent petition was ultra vires u/s.17 of 1955 Act - Held, it was clear u/s.17 of 1955 Act that State Govt. would be entitled to specify for purpose of holding an enquiry - Challenged Competent petition was vires u/s.17 of 1955 Act - No question about fundamental rights of petitioner was involved and grievance against order passed by respondent No.2 cannot be ventilated by petition u/art.32 of constitution - Respondent No.2 had no jurisdiction to entertain respondent No.1 application but petition itself was not competent u/art.32 of constitution - Petition dismissed.
