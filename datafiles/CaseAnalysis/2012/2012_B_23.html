Food Corporation Of India & Ors. v Bhartiya Khadya Nigam Karmchari Sangh & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 64; (2012) 2 SCC 307; AIR 2012 SC 703; 2012 (5) AWC 4635; 2012 (132) FLR 698; JT 2012 (1) SC 139; 2012 (2) MLJ(SC) 803; 2012(1) SCALE 276; [2012] 1 S.C.R. 230; 2012 (2) SLR 1; 2012 (1) UPLBEC 324

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Food Corporations Act, 1964, Food Corporation of India (Staff) Regulations, 1971

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 -Additional higher qualification - Incentive to in-service employees - Allegation of discrimination - Effect - -Appellant Corporation introduced a Scheme providing for increments to employees on acquiring prescribed additional qualification during their service - Benefit was extended to employees who were in-service and not to recruits already possessing prescribed additional qualification - One employee challenged Circular on the ground that it resulted in discrimination between in-service employees and persons recruited already possessing - HC declared Circular in question as discriminatory and directed to withdraw benefits - Hence, instant appeal - Whether grant of incentives only to in-service employees, who acquired professional qualifications after entering in service, and denial of same to those who had acquired same professional qualifications before entering service was invalid in law, being violative of arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution - Held, there was substantial merit in stand of appellant - Classification adopted by appellant was between an employee obtaining a higher qualification after joining service and an employee who already possessed such qualification before joining service - Main purpose of this classification was to grant an incentive to employees already in service to motivate them to acquire higher qualifications for their own benefit as well as of their employer, i.e. appellant - , Classification sought to be made by appellant between two sets of employees bear a just and rational nexus to object sought to be achieved by introducing said incentive Scheme - Grant of incentive in relation to in-service employees, in no way amounted to discrimination between in-service employees and employees recruited with higher qualification, offending either arts. 14 or 16 of Constitution, particularly when incentive was in form of a special increment as 'personal pay' to be merged in pay at time of promotion to next higher grade and thus, having no bearing on inter-se seniority and/or to future promotion to next higher grade - Hence, impugned HC order holding Circular in question to be discriminatory and in violation of arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution could not be sustained - Appeal allowed.
