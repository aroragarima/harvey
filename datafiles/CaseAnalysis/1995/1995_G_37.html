Gian Chand v Gopala And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

18 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1456; (1995) 2 SCC 528; JT 1995 (2) SC 513; 1995(1) SCALE 824; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 412

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Contract & Commercial - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 4(1) - Contract Act, 1872, s. 33 - (A) Whether sale deed executed pursuant to publication of notification u/s. 4(1) valid? - (B) Whether appellant entitled to obtain refund of earnest money paid under agreement? - Held, (A) any sale transaction or encumbrances created by owner after publication of notification u/s. 4(1) would be void and does not bind State - (B) contract in question being a contingent contract based on uncertain future events, that event having occurred by notification issued u/s. 6, contract became impossible of performance - Thus, contracting party is entitled to enforce terms of contract for refund of earnest money - Appeal allowed.
