BSES Limited v Messrs Tata Power Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 871; (2004) 1 SCC 195; AIR 2004 SC 760; 2004 (3) JhrCR(SC) 187; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 376; 2004 (2) MLJ(SC) 1; 2003(8) SCALE 758; 2003 (1) SCW 6931; 2003 (6) SLT 486; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 932; 2003 (8) Supreme 108

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Electricity & Energy

Summary: Electricity Regulatory Commissions Act, 1998 - s. 27 - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948 - ss. 2(4A), 2(3) - Dispute on commercial terms between BSES and TPC - Standby charges - Order given by the Commission challenged - High Court remitted the matter to the commission for de novo consideration - Held, the procedure adopted by the Commission was not fair and proper inasmuch as the Chairman did not participate in the meetings which other two members had with the consultants, whereunder a formula was devised. Under reg. 21, the quorum for proceedings before the Commission shall be three - Further held, the High Court was perfectly justified in remitting the matter to the Commission for de novo consideration and no exception can be taken to such a course of action.
