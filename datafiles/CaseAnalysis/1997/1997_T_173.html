Tube Investments of India Limited v Trade Industries, Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 10; (1997) 6 SCC 35; 1997 ARBLR 171; 1997 (17) PTC 655

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Keywords: Visual similar Trademark

Summary: Intellectual Property - Visual similar Trademark - Passing off - Validity - Appellants were manufacturers of bicycles and bicycle parts - The appellants have a registered trade mark bearing their initials TI in two concentric circles. It is registered in the year 1972 and the registration is still in force - The respondent is a manufacturer of saddle covers on which he uses mark of appellant - Appellant filed instant appeal for grant of interim injunction restraining the respondent from using his mark.

Held, the visual similarity between the two marks coupled with the fact that the respondent's mark is used on bicycle saddle covers, prima facie posed a real danger that the product of the respondent can be passed off as a product of the appellants. Appellants are registered holders of their mark which is specifically registered for bicycle parts. Interim injunction is granted restraining the respondent from using his mark for any items manufactured by him for use in connection with bicycles or falling in Class 12. Appeals allowed.
