Raju Pandurang Mahale v State of Maharashtra and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 120; (2004) 4 SCC 371; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1259; AIR 2004 SC 1677; 2004 (1) CCR 349; 2004 (2) Crimes 204; 2004 CRLJ 1441; 2004 (1) JCC 488; JT 2004 (2) SC 425; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 936; 2004(2) SCALE 408; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 287; 2004 (2) SLT 295; 2004 (2) Supreme 234; 2004 (1) UC 723

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Women & Children

Keywords: Wrongful Confinement

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 292, 302, 323, 34, 340, 342, 354, 376 (2)(g) - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - Conviction by trial court affirmed in appeal by impugned judgment of Bombay HC - Looked at from any angle conclusions of Trial Court and HC in convicting appellant do not suffer from any infirmity to warrant interference in exercise of powers under art.136 of Constitution of India, 1950 sentences imposed by no stretch of imagination can be said to be on higher side - Dismissed.
