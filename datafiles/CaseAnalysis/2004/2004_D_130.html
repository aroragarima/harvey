Dr. Mahachandra Prasad Singh v Chairman, Bihar Legislative Council and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, R.C. Lahoti, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 915; (2004) 8 SCC 747; 2005 (1) JhrCR(SC) 114; JT 2004 (9) SC 218; 2004(9) SCALE 81; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 692; 2005 (1) UPLBEC 125

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Constitution, sch. 10, para 2(1) r/w art. 191(2) - Disqualification - Petitioner was elected as a member of the Bihar Legislative Council (MLC) from Tirhut Graduate Constituency as a candidate of Indian National Congress - The notification for holding elections to Fourteenth Lok Sabha was issued in March, 2001 - The petitioner contested the said election from Maharajganj Parliamentary Constituency as an independent candidate - Shri Salman Rageev, a member of Bihar Legislative Council, sent a petition to the Chairman of the Legislative Council on 10th June, 2004 stating, inter alia, that the petitioner, who was a member of the Congress Party, had contested the parliamentary election from Maharajganj Constituency as an independent candidate and consequently in view of the provisions of the Tenth Schedule to the Constitution he had become disqualified for being a member of the House - Whether petitioner suffered from disqualification - Anti defection law, its genesis deliberated upon - In instant case, the Chairman of the Legislative Council on fact of his fighting as independent candidate, concluded that petitioner has given up his membership of Indian National Congress - Court said that 'This being a matter of record, the petitioner could not possibly dispute them, and that is why he has admitted these facts in the writ petition as well' - Held, petitioner has incurred the disqualification u/para. 2(1)(a) of Schedule and decision of Chairman, upheld - Petition dismissed.
