M/s:MODI Food Products Company Limited v Shri Faqir Chand Sharma and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 27; AIR 1956 SC 628; 1956 ALJ 816; 1956 (1) LLJ 749; 1956 (2) MLJ(SC) 39; 1956 SCJ 574; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 560

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950 - Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950, s. 23 - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946 - Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1953, s. 25C - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(k), 10 - Whether workmen are entitled to compensation under proviso (b) to s. 25-C? - Held, proviso would apply only if workmen had been paid compensation for 45 days, and were again laid off for further periods of more than one week at a time - It is clear that lay off which falls within proviso (b) to s. 25-C must be distinct from that for which compensation had been paid in accordance with proviso (a) to s. 25C and subsequent thereto in point of time and as, in present case, there was one continuous lay off for entire period, proviso (b) could have no application - Respondents are entitled to compensation only for 45 days as provided in proviso (a), and that as appellant had offered to pay same by its notice D/- 12-7-1954, there was no alteration of conditions of service within s. 22 of Act - Appeal allowed.
