Gopalakrishnan v Cochin Devaswom Board

Supreme Court of India

19 July 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, R.V. Raveendran, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1444; (2007) 7 SCC 482; AIR 2007 SC 3162; 2007 (69) ALR 163; 2007 (4) AWC 4024; 2007 (5) CTC 165; JT 2007 (10) SC 567; 2007 (4) KLT 965; 2007 (6) MLJ(SC) 751; 2008 (1) MPLJ 235; 2007 (103) RD 593; 2007(10) SCALE 572; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Exparte Order

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 23, rr. 3 and 3A - Compromise of suit - Bar to suit - Examining allegations of fraud against statutory authority still open by the High Court - Though decrees of Civil Courts which have attained finality should not be interfered - Bar contained in r. 3A will not come in the way of HC examining the validity of a compromise decree, when allegations of fraud/collusion are made against a statutory authority which entered into such compromise - Order passed by HC directing Temple Board to take possession of Temple property from respondent - Board entered into compromise with respondent to give up their rights in property in question in lieu of respondents giving possession of another property which was also a Temple property and got a compromise decree passed - Challenge to such compromise decree cannot be rejected, where fraud or collusion by officers of a Statutory Board is made out - When HC had directed Board to take possession of suit land immediately from respondents in a complaint it was improper for Board to enter into a settlement with respondents, giving up right, title and interest in suit property without permission of Court - Properties of deities, temples and Devaswom Boards, require to be protected from wrongful claims and misappropriation - Compromise decree set aside - Appeal allowed.
