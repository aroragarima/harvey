Lal Singh and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

04 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 927; (2003) 12 SCC 554; AIR 2004 SC 299; 2003 (4) CCR 292; 2003 (4) Crimes 482; 2004 CRLJ 378; JT 2003 (8) SC 488; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 209; 2003(9) SCALE 256; 2003 (1) SCW 6133; 2003 (6) SLT 692; 2003 (8) Supreme 93

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.394, 411- Code of Criminal Procedure 1973 - Train robbery - HC affirmed conviction u/s. 394, IPC, but set aside conviction and sentence u/s. 411, IPC - Held, prosecution has successfully established recovery of currency notes from possession of appellants which were subject matter of robbery - Further held, delay in holding test identification parade was not inordinate, and nothing has been elicited from investigating officer as well as Special Executive Magistrate who held test identification parade that appellants had been shown to witnesses before holding of test identification parade or that there was any irregularity in holding test identification parade - Further, conviction of appellants is based not solely on evidence of identification by prosecution witnesses, but also on basis of corroborative evidence in form of recoveries of looted currency notes from possession of appellants substantive evidence of identification in Court is, therefore, supported by corroborative evidence which is unimpeachable in nature and, therefore, conviction of appellants is fully justified.
