Union Of India & Anr. v Association Of Unified Telecom Service Providers Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

11 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 733; (2011) 10 SCC 543; AIR 2012 SC 1693; [2011] 14 S.C.R. 657

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Telecom

Keywords: Service Provider, Indian Tolls Act, 1851

Summary: Telecom - Practice & Procedure - Tele Com regulatory Authority of India Act, 1997, ss. 11(1), 14(a)(i) and 18 - Indian Telegraph Act, 1885, s. 4 (1) - Adjusted Gross Revenue - License - Sustainability - Appellants granted licenses to respondent/licensees and they had to pay stipulated fixed license fee every year - During period 1994 to 1999, respondents defaulted in payment of license fee and made representation to Govt ., Ministry of Telecommunications for relief against high license fee for survival of telecom industry - Govt. considered representations and after number of deliberations with respondents offered new package, known as 'National Telecom Policy 1999, Regime' giving option to respondents to migrate from fixed license fee to revenue sharing fee - Accordingly respondents were intimated them by offering to change over to NTP-99 regime - Respondents took new licenses which provided them to pay certain percentage of Gross Revenue as license fee annually - In 2003 respondents challenged validity of definition of Adjusted Gross Revenue before Tribunal by its order dt. 7-7-2006 directed TRAI to send its recommendations -- Appellant filed appeal u/s.18 of the 1997 Act which was dismissed on ground that TRAI had submitted its recommendations to Tribunal and dismissed appeal- Hence, instant appeal - Whether common judgment and order dt.30-8-2007 of Tribunal was sustainable - Held, Tribunal could adjudicate any dispute between licensor and respondents/licensee u/s.14(a)(i) of the 1997 Act, - One such dispute could be that computation of Adjusted Gross Revenue made by licensor and demand raised on basis of such computation was not in accordance with license agreement- When such dispute was raised against particular demand, Tribunal had to go into facts and materials on basis of which demand was raised and decided - Whether demand was in accordance with license agreement and in particular definition of Adjusted Gross Revenue in license agreement and could also interpret terms and conditions of license agreement - However, from order dt.7-7-2006 instead of challenging any demands made on them, respondents/licensees had questioned validity of definition of Adjusted Gross Revenue in licenses given to them and Tribunal had finally decided in its order dt. 3008-.2007 as to what items of revenue would be part of Adjusted Gross Revenue and what items of revenue would not be part of Adjusted Gross Revenue without going into facts and materials relating to demand on a particular respondents/licensee - In supplementary order held that if demands had been raised, Court should grant two months' time to respondents/licensees to raise dispute before Tribunal against demands and during period of two months, demands would not be enforced - Appeals disposed of.
