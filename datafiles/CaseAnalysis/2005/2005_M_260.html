Commissioner of Central Excise-I, New Delhi v Messrs S.R. Tissues Private Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 450; (2005) 6 SCC 310; 2005 (197) CTR 437; 2005 (186) E.L.T. 385; JT 2005 (7) SC 475; 2005 (3) RCR(Civil) 759; 2005(6) SCALE 156; [2005] Supp2 S.C.R. 355

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax; Trade

Summary: [A.] Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 2(f) and 35-L(b) - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Whether process of unwinding, cutting and slitting to sizes of jumbo rolls of tissue paper would amount to 'manufacture' on first principles or u/s. 2(f) of Act? - Held, no new product had emerged on winding, cutting/slitting and packing - Character and end-use did not undergo any change on account of abovementioned activities and, therefore there was no manufacture on first principles - S. 2(f) not applicable - Appeals dismissed.
