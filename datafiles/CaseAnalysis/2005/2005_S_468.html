Tata Iron and Steel Company Limited v State of Jharkhand and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 482; (2005) 4 SCC 272; AIR 2005 SC 2871; 2005 (2) JhrCR(SC) 235; JT 2005 (3) SC 582; 2005(3) SCALE 431; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 1207; 2005 (140) STC 284

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: New Industrial Unit, Finance Act, 1981

Summary: Sales Tax - Companies Act, 1956 - Finance Act, 1981 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Bihar Reorganisation Act, 2000 - Industries (Development And Regulation) Act, 1951 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - (A) Whether as Industrial Policy covers both new unit as well as existing units and CRM having been treated to be a new unit, High Court committed an error in not directing grant of benefits to its HRM as an existing unit? - Held, notification D/- 12-01-2002, although it extends tax benefits both to new units as also existing units, but thereby it does not contemplate that grant of benefit should be extended to separate existing units although they may be producing same but technically different products - Manufactured item is saleable steel - Quality of manufactured steel from CRM and HRM may have difference but as on date of coming into force of Jharkhand Industrial Policy as also notifications issued under 1981 Act both CRM and HRM were existing units, each one of them cannot get benefit thereof - (B) Whether such Industrial Policy as also notifications having not referred to 'company', 'assessee' or 'dealer', each unit of appellant was entitled to benefit of notifications irrespective of fact as to whether they are new or existing units? - Further held, Govt. of Jharkhand has declared its Industrial Policy on or about 25-08-2001, effective date therefore being 15-11-2000 - Said policy was put in force from 15- Appellant's cold rolled mill as also hot rolled mill, thus, are existing units within meaning of Jharkhand State Policy, in terms whereof if a benefit is granted under one policy, no other benefit would be available - Appeals dismissed.
