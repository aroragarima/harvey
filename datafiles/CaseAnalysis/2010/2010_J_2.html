N. B. C. C. Limited v J. G. Engineering Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 3; (2010) 2 SCC 385; AIR 2010 SC 640; 2010(2) ALL MR 417; 2010 (1) AWC 589; JT 2010 (1) SC 132; 2010 (2) MLJ(SC) 805; 2010 (2) MPLJ 310; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 725; 2010(1) SCALE 138; [2010] 1 S.C.R. 109

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Land & Property

Summary: (A) Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 14 - Extension of time for arbitration - Held, where arbitration agreement itself provides procedure for enlargement of time and parties have taken recourse to it, and consented to enlargement of time by arbitrator, Court cannot exercise its inherent power in extending time fixed by parties in absence of consent of either of them - Order accordingly.

(B) Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 14 - Termination of mandate of arbitration - Appellant entered into contract with respondent - Appellant terminated contract - Arbitration clause invoked - Arbitrator was appointed - However, arbitration process had come to a standstill due to inaction of respondent - Thereafter, respondent filed application before HC seeking removal of the arbitrator - Pursuant to order of HC, a new arbitrator was appointed - Arbitrator concluded proceedings after the expiry of prescribed time period - However, both parties extended time to conclude arbitration proceeding - Arbitrator failed to publish award even within extended period - Respondent filed an application before HC for a declaration that mandate of arbitrator had already stood terminated - HC by its impugned order, terminated mandate of arbitrator and appointed a new Arbitrator for deciding dispute between parties - Hence, present special leave petition - (A) Whether HC was justified in terminating mandate of arbitration? - Held, arbitrator was bound to make and publish his award, within time mutually agreed to by parties, unless parties consented to further enlargement of time - In present case, arbitrator failed to publish the award within the time limit fixed by the parties, and hence, HC was justified in terminating mandate of the arbitrator - No fault with impugned order of HC in this regard - (B) Whether HC erred in not allowing appellant to decide upon appointment of an arbitrator pursuant to sub-section (2) of s. 15 of Act? - Held, before appointing an arbitrator u/s. 11(6) of Act, HC failed to take into consideration effect of s. 11(8) of Act - Matter remanded to HC for fresh decision of application u/s. 11(6) of Act - Appeals allowed to above extent - Order accordingly.
