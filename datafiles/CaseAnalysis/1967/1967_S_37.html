Workmen of Shri Rangavilas Motors Private Limited and Another v Shri Rangavilas Motors Private Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 February 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, Mohammad Hidayatullah, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 129; AIR 1967 SC 1040; 1967 (14) FLR 332; 1967 (2) LLJ 12; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 528

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Bombay Industrial Relation Act, 1946

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Bombay Industrial Relation Act, 1946 - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 10 (1) - (A) Whether dispute referred by State Govt. to Labour Court is an industrial dispute? - High Court rightly observed that once findings of fact recorded by Labour Court are accepted, there is no doubt in law that in circumstances of this case, what was originally an individual grievance of appellant did assume, at time reference was made by Govt., the character of an industrial dispute - (B) Whether State Govt. of Mysore was not appropriate Govt. to make reference and whether reference should have been made by Central Govt. to a National Tribunal? - Applying well known tests of jurisdiction, a Court, or Tribunal would have jurisdiction if parties reside within jurisdiction or if subject matter of dispute substantially arises within jurisdiction - Applying above principles to facts of this case it is quite clear that subject matter of dispute in this case substantially arose within jurisdiction of Mysore Govt. - (C) Whether any dispute relating to termination of service of fourth respondent is included in order of reference? - On its true interpretation order of reference was quite clear and that question of termination of services of fourth respondent was included in order of reference - (D) Whether points of dispute actually referred fall within scope of items enumerated in second Schedule of Industrial Disputes Act and are therefore within competence of Labour Court? - Regarding fourth point, with respect, HC misinterpreted first proviso to cl. (d) to s. 10(1) - It is not necessary that order of reference should expressly state that it is because of proviso that a reference is, being made to Labour Court, and if reference can be justified on facts, there is nothing in Act which makes such a reference invalid - Held, HC erred in holding that first proviso to s.10 (1)(d) did not apply to facts of this case - (E) Whether on question of transfer there was any dispute at all for adjudication? - This point was not taken before Labour Court and thus was not allowed - Further held, Judgement of HC is set aside and order of Labour Court restored - Appeal allowed.
