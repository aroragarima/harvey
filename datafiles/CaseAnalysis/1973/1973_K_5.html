K. K. Chari v R. M. Seshadri

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, A. Alagiriswami, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 88; (1973) 1 SCC 761; AIR 1973 SC 1311; 1974 (1) MLJ(SC) 14; 1973 RCJ 589; 1973 RCR(Rent) 342; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 691

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte

Summary: Rent Control - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 s. 106 - Madras Buildings (Lease & Rent Control) Act, 1960 s. 10(3)(a)(i) - Eviction - Challenged - Appellant was tenant and his land lady filed suit for eviction of appellant - Meanwhile suit property was advertised for sale and appellant had purchased said property and respondent was tenant of suit premises under vendor - eviction order was passed and appellant had to vacate premises of which he was in occupation as tenant - Pursuant to said order appellant issued two notices to respondent terminating his tenancy u/s. 106 of 1882 Act - As respondent had not surrendered possession of premises, appellant had filed suit for eviction u/s. 10(3)(a)(ii) of 1960 Act before Trial court - Both appellant and respondent had settled matter and compromise petition was filed in court - Appellant filed eviction petition after stipulated time was lapsed and respondent failed to abide by compromise decree to execute order of eviction which gave time to respondent to vacate and deliver possession of property - Respondent filed appeal against said order before HC which reversed order and accepted contentions of respondent - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by Trial court directing eviction of respondent was sustainable.

Held, that the decree for eviction has been passed solely on the basis of the compromise entered into between the parties. Therefore, it is clear from the various matters referred to, that the court was satisfied about the bona fide requirement of the landlord. Therefore, the decree for eviction is neither void nor in executable. Hence, appeal is allowed and order and judgment of HC are set aside and the order of Trial court will stand restored. Appeal allowed.
