Kerala Solvent Extractions Limited v A. Unnikrishnan and another

Supreme Court of India

30 November 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, S. Mohan, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1791; (2006) 13 SCC 619

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - False representation - Termination of services - Reinstatement - Justifiability - Appellant terminated respondent's services due to false representation of qualifications - Labour Court held that conduct of respondent did not amount to false representation - On aggrieved petition by appellant, Single Judge disapproved view of Labour Court and held that there would be no hardship or prejudice caused to appellant if respondent was reinstated - Hence, instant petition - Whether impugned order if Single Judge was justifiable - Held, reliefs granted by courts should be seen to be logical and tenable within framework of law and should not incur and justify criticism that jurisdiction of courts tend to degenerate into misplaced sympathy, generosity and private benevolence - It was essential to maintain integrity of legal reasoning and legitimacy of conclusions - Expansive judicial mood of mistaken and misplaced compassion at expense of legitimacy of process would eventually lead to mutually irreconcilable situations and denude judicial process of its dignity, authority, predictability and respectability - Both Labour Court and HC erred - Orders of Labour Court and of HC set aside - Dispute raised by respondent before Labour Court was dismissed - Appeal allowed.
