Shivram Poddar v Income Tax Officer, Central Circle Ii, Calcutta, and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 253; AIR 1964 SC 1095; 1964 (1) ITJ 476; [1964] 51 ITR 823

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 44 - Whether income earned by firm in year could be assessed to tax u/s. 44 after firm was dissolved? - Business of firm was discontinued on dissolution of firm - Firm was unregistered and by discontinuance of business it neither ceased to be liable to pay tax on income earned by it nor could a procedure different from one prescribed under Chap. IV apply for assessment of income of that firm - Appeal dismisssed.
