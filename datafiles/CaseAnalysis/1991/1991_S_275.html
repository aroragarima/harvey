Jaswant Singh Nerwal and another v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 991; (1991) Supp1 SCC 313; (1991) SCC (L&S) 1019; AIR 1993 SCW 863; 1991 (16) ATC 932; 1991 (62) FLR 564; JT 1991 (1) SC 474; 1991 (1) LLJ 580; 1991(1) SCALE 395; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 411; 1991 (2) SLR 124; 1991 (1) UJ 740

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Punjab Civil Service Rules 1930

Summary: Service - Punjab Civil Service Rules, 1930 - Whether selections of individuals for Punjab Civil Services once completed can be cancelled and conducted again? - Held, nothing to prove that appellant's father being a member of Public Service Commission, had other members keep track of comparatives in order to single out Bansal as a successful candidate - Appellant is an officer in Punjab Civil Service and his displacement would serve nobody's purpose due to time lag - Appellant's first preference was Excise and Taxation officer but had intimated Commission about his change of preference to Punjab Civil Service - Change of preference could be made and it

being intimated and approved timely, appellant's first preference to a post in Punjab Civil Service ranked superior to preference of appellant in Civil Appeal No. 334 of 1978, because of their ranking in examination - Civil Appeal No. 833 of 1977 allowed - Civil Appeal No. 334 of 1978 dismissed.
