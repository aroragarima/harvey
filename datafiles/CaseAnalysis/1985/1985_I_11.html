Workmen Employed By Messrs Indian Oxygen Limited v Messrs Indian Oxygen Limited

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, V. Balakrishna Eradi, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 511; (1985) 3 SCC 177; (1985) SCC (L&S) 611; AIR 1986 SC 125; [1985] Supp1 S.C.R. 111

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 4 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - There are two rival unions of workmen employed by Indian Oxygen Ltd. in its industrial undertaking at Kanpur - They are Indian Oxygen Karamchari Union and Indian Oxygen Shramik Sangh - It was stated that while wage structure is uniform in respect of workmen employed by Company all over country dearness allowance formula varies from centre to centre - Respondent in its attempt to thwart demand being pursued entered into a settlement with Shramik Sangh in respect of dearness allowance and then approached Labour Commr., Kanpur for registering settlement - Failing to obtain registration, Company unilaterally enforced new scheme of dearness allowance linked to all-India average consumer price index prepared by Labour Bureau, Simla - Karamchari Union did not accept revisel formula, and pressed its demand that dearness allowance should be linked to All-India consumer price index number prepared by Labour Bureau, Simla for Kanpur centre - Purpose of dearness allowance being to neutralise a portion of increase in cost of living, it should ordinarily be on a sliding scale and provide for an increase on rise in cost of living and a decrease on a fall in cost of living - Held, basis of fixation of wages and dearness allowance is industry-cum-region - Employees getting same wages should get same dearness allowance, irrespective of whether they are working as clerks or members of subordinate staff or factory workmen - Additional financial burden which a revision of wage structure or dearness allowance would impose upon an employer, and his ability to bear such burden, are very material and relevant factors to be taken into account - Appeal partly allowed.
