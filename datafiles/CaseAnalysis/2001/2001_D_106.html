Dtc Retired Employees' Association and Others v Delhi Transport Corporation and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19856; (2001) 6 SCC 61; (2001) SCC (L&S) 919; AIR 2001 SC 1997; 2001 (4) ALD(SC) 16; 2001 (91) DLT 484; 2001 (90) FLR 770; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 144; 2001 LabIC 1793; 2001 (2) LLJ 258; 2001(4) SCALE 30; 2001 (3) SCJ 277; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 559; 2001 (4) SLT 155; 2001 (4) Supreme 218

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Prohibition Act, Madhya Pradesh Panchayats Act, 1962

Summary: Service - Employees' Provident Funds Act, 1952 - Payment Of Gratuity Act, 1972 - (A) Whether the appellants were liable to return gratuity in lieu of the pension scheme initiated by the respondents? - It is a condition precedent that in order to get the benefit of the Pension Scheme, they have to refund the gratuity received by them - It is neither illegal nor unjust - (B) Whether sufficient time was given to the appellants to exercise their option for the pension scheme? - As far as the time is concerned, the letter D/- 23-11-1992 conveyed that DTC shall obtain option from its employees within 30 days from the date of issue of circular - However, DTC, in fact, extended the time twice - Therefore, the retired employees had, in fact, more than one month's time to exercise their option - Those employees who had received the benefit failed to exercise their option and thus disentitled themselves - Held, Appeal dismissed.
