Sheralli Wali Mohammed v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

09 August 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, S.M. Sikri, A.N. Ray, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 517; (1973) 4 SCC 79; (1973) SCC (Cr) 726; AIR 1972 SC 2443; 1972 CRLJ 1523; 1972 MPLJ 1015; 1973 UJ 204

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 84 - Murder - Unsound mind - Husband killing wife and children - Unclear motive - No attempt of running away from crime spot - Whether accused committed offence under insanity? - To establish that acts done are not offences u/s. 84, it must be proved clearly that, at time of commission of acts, appellant, by reason of unsoundness of mind, was incapable of either knowing nature of act or that acts were either morally wrong or contrary to law - Question to be asked is, is there evidence to show that, at time of commission of offence, he was laboring under any such incapacity - On this question, state of his mind before and after commission of offence is relevant - General burden of proof that an accused person is in a sound state of mind is upon prosecution - Held, there is nothing to indicate that he had not necessary means rea when he committed offence no evidence to show that accused was not in a sound state of mind while committing offence - Mere fact that no motive has been proved why accused murdered his wife and child or, fact that he made no attempt to run away when door was broke open, would not indicate that he was insane or, that he did not have necessary means rea for commission of offence - Normalcy in work and conduct - No evidence to show that accused was insane at time of commission of offence - Appeal dismissed.
