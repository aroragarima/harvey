Rakesh Baban Borhade v State of Maharashtra and another

Supreme Court of India

19 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 779; (2015) 2 SCC 313; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 395; JT 2014 (13) SC 131; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 96; 2014(13) SCALE 93

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure (Amendment) Act, 2005

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420, 406, 423, 424 r/w s. 34 - Anticipatory bail - Entitled to - Appellant was charged for offences u/ss. 420, 406, 423, 424 r/w s. 34 IPC on a private complaint - Appellant filed application for anticipatory appeal - HC, by impugned order, dismissed application - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, dispute is purely a business transaction based on two MOUs. According to the appellant- accused, he sold the land belonging to his father as legal representative of his father and there is no illegality involved in it. Whether the appellant-accused has sold the property in his capacity as the legal heir of his father or as a representative of the company and whether there was any dishonest intention to cheat the complainant remains to be seen only when the parties adduce oral and documentary evidence. Since the transaction is in the nature of commercial transaction and since the appellant has also shown his bonafide by depositing rupees one crore, pending further investigation, anticipatory bail could be granted to the appellant. Appeals allowed.
