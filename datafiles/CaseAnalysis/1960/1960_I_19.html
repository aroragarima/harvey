Assam Oil Company v Its Workmen

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 163; AIR 1960 SC 1264; 1960 (1) FLR 190; 1960 (1) LLJ 587; 1961 (1) SCJ 137; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 457

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Trade Union Act, 1926, misa

Summary: Labour & Industrial law - Service - Trade Union Act, 1926 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2(s) - (A) Whether Industrial Tribunal has jurisdiction to entertain disputes questioning contractual power of employer to discharge his employee under terms of contract ? - Held, yes, it has jurisdiction - Employer may, in accordance with terms of contract, dismiss an employee - Dismissal must be ordered in bonafide exercise of power and not as a punitive measure - While employer's right to exercise his option in terms of contract has to be recognised, employee's right to expect security of tenure also needs to be taken into account - Industrial Tribunal has jurisdiction to question validity, propriety or legality of such an order of dismissal - (B) Whether joining a trade union is a valid ground for dismissing an employee in private employment? - Held, joining a trade union is fundamental right of every citizen - Joining of trade union as main ground for dismissal is not justified - (C) Whether all wrongful dismissals can be made good by an order of reinstatement ? - Held, generally, a wrongfully dismissed employee is entitled to reinstatement - In cases such as those involving trust and secrecy, it would not be expedient to order reinstatement - Compensation might be proper relief - Appeal partly allowed.
