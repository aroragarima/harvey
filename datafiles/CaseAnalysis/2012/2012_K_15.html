Benarsi Krishna Committee & Ors. v Karmyogi Shelters Pvt. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

21 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 305; (2012) 9 SCC 496; 2012(6) ALL MR 415; 2012 (6) AWC 5700; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 51; 2012 (5) CTC 519; JT 2012 (9) SC 111; 2013 (1) MahLJ 560; 2012 (7) MLJ(SC) 711; 2013 (1) MPLJ 250; 2012 (4) RCR(Civil) 584; 2012(9) SCALE 251; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 1175

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss.31(5) and 34(3) - Dispute - Arbitral award - Sustainability - Whether the service of an Arbitral Award on the agent of a party amounts to service on the party itself, having regard to the provisions of ss.31(5) and 34(3) of Act -

Held, having taken note of the submissions advanced on behalf of the respective parties and having particular regard to the expression 'party' as defined in s.2(h) of the Act read with the provisions of ss. 31(5) and 34(3) of Act, HC was not inclined to interfere with the decision of DB of HC impugned in these proceedings - Expression 'party' was amply dealt with in two cases of SC - Union of India v Tecco Trichy Engineers and Contractors (Referred) 2005 Indlaw SC 195 and also The State Of Maharashtra & Ors v M/S. Ark Builders Pvt. Ltd. (Referred) 2011 Indlaw SC 133, relied on - It was one thing for an Advocate to act and plead on behalf of a party in a proceeding and it was another for an Advocate to act as the party himself - Expression 'party', as defined in S. 2(h) of the 1996 Act, clearly indicates a person who is a party to an arbitration agreement - Said definition was not qualified in any way so as to include the agent of the party to such agreement - Any reference, therefore, made in ss.31(5) and 34(2) of Act could only mean the party himself and not his or her agent, or Advocate empowered to act on the basis of a Vakalatnama - In such circumstances, proper compliance with s.31(5) of Act would mean delivery of a signed copy of the Arbitral Award on the party himself and not on his Advocate, which gave the party concerned the right to proceed u/s.34(3) of Act - View taken in one case was in relation to the authority given to an Advocate to act on behalf of a party to a proceeding in the proceedings itself, which could not stand satisfied where a provision such as s.31(5) of Act was concerned - Pushpa Devi Bhagat (D) Th. Lr. Sadhna Rai v Rajinder Singh and Others (Referred) 2006 Indlaw SC 315, relied on - Said provision clearly indicated that a signed copy of the Award has to be delivered to the party - Accordingly, when a copy of the signed Award was not delivered to the party himself, it would not amount to compliance with the provisions of s.31(5) of Act - In the instant case, since a signed copy of the Award had not been delivered to the party itself and the party obtained the same on 15-12-2004, and the petition u/s. 34 of the Act was filed on 3-2-2005, it had to be held that the said petition was filed within the stipulated period of three months as contemplated u/s.34(3) of Act - Consequently, the objection taken on behalf of the petitioner could not be sustained and was rightly rejected by DB of HC - Petition dismissed.
