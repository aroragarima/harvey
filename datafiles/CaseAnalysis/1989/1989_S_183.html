Dineshchandra Jamnadas Gandhi v State of Gujarat and Another

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 400; (1989) SCC (Cr) 194; (1989) 1 SCC 420; AIR 1989 SC 1011; 1989 (1) AWC 323; 1989 CRLJ 889; 1989 CrLR(SC) 212; 1989 (40) E.L.T. 230; JT 1989 (1) SC 83; 1989(1) SCALE 92; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 138

Case Digest

Subject: Health & Drug; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Supari, Sells, Prevention of Food Adulteration Rules 1955

Summary: Health & Drug - Socio-Economic - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 432 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954 - Prevention of Food Adulteration Rules, 1955, rr. 28 and 29(f) - (A) Whether 'supari' concerned in this case was a 'fruit product or not? - (B)Whether within meaning of rr. 29(f) or (m) respectively and, accordingly, use in it of permitted coal tar dyes or food colors was prohibited or permitted? - Held, 'fruit products' in r. 29(f) has since been amended enumerating precisely specific products in which food colors permitted by r. 28 could be used - This amendment which came into force with effect from November 15, 1984 deleted expression 'fruit products' and in its place specifically enumerated items u/r. 29(f) in which use of permitted coal-tar food colors was allowed - This is a fit case in which appropriate Govt. should exercise its executive powers of remission of substantive sentence of imprisonment - U/s. 432 CrPC, 1973 or under other law appropriate to case, direct that imposition of substantive sentence of imprisonment shall be postponed till appellant's prayer for remission, which appellant shall make within a month from now before appropriate Govt. or authority, is considered and disposed of taking into account observation made in the judgment - Appeal dismissed.
