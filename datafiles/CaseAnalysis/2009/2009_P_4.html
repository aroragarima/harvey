(1) State of Kerala and Another; (2) Malayora v (1) Peoples Union For Civil Liberties, Kerala

Supreme Court of India

21 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 921; (2009) 8 SCC 46; JT 2009 (9) SC 579; 2009(10) SCALE 25; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 142

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Competence Of The Legislature, Constitutionality, Constitution (Scheduled Tribes) Order, 1950, Life Insurance Corporation (Modification of Settlements) Act, 1976, Maharashtra Restoration of Lands to Scheduled Tribes Act, 1974, Agency Tracts Interest and Land Transfer Act, 1917, Kerala Government Land Assignment Act, 1960, Kerala Scheduled Tribes (Restriction on Transfer of Lands and Restoration of Alienated Lands) Act, 1975, Kerala Scheduled Tribe (Restriction on Transfer of Lands and Restoration of Alienated Lands)Amendment Act, 1996, Kerala Restriction on Transfer by and Restoration of Lands to the Scheduled Tribes Act, 1999, Kerala Scheduled Tribes (Restriction on Transfer of lands and Restoration of Alienated Land) Rules, 1986, A.P. Scheduled Areas Land Transfer Regulation, 1959, Civil Appeals disposed of

Summary: (A) Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Kerala Scheduled Tribes (Restriction on Transfer of Lands and Restoration of Alienated Lands) Act, 1975 - Kerala Restriction on Transfer by and Restoration of Lands to the Scheduled Tribes Act, 1999, ss. 5 (1), 5 (2) - Constitutionality - HC held that the provisions of 1999 Act were unconstitutional and void - Appeal against - Whether proviso to ss. 5 (1) and 5 (2) of 1999 Act is a colorable legislation? - Held, 1975 Act dealt by transferred agricultural and non-agricultural land by members of ST - However, 1999 Act confined only to agricultural land - State Legislature only has the requisite legislative competence for enacting Act 1999in relation with parent Act, 1975 - Act of mala fide on the part of legislature is also beyond the province of judicial review - No motive can be attributed to Legislature for enacting a statute - Constitutionality of statute must be considered in view of provisions of Constitution - HC held itself that Legislature had competence to enact 1999 Act by virtue of Entry 51, List II of 7th Schedule - No material was before HC to establish 1999 Act was confiscatory nature - State is expected to enact or amend the law as to keep with the changing social condition - HC committed a fundamental error - Therefore, proviso to ss. 5 (1) and 5 (2) cannot be held to be colorable.P /&gt;(B) Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 254 - Kerala Scheduled Tribes (Restriction on Transfer of Lands and Restoration of Alienated Lands) Act, 1975 - Kerala Restriction on Transfer by and Restoration of Lands to the Scheduled Tribes Act, 1999 - Presidential Assent - Enactment of law - Whether Presidential Assent was necessary for enacting a statute which came within the purview of List II of 7th Schedule of the Constitution? - Held, transfer of land under 1975 Act comes within the purview of Entry 6, List III of 7th Schedule - A Parliamentary Act in that behalf already existed i.e., Transfer of Property Act - Presidential Assent was necessary u/art. 254 (2) only when 1975 Act could be conflict with the Transfer of Property Act - However, conflicted Act was repealed and new Act brought in its place under Entry 49, List II, the Presidential Assent was not required - 1999 Act was a new Act within the purview of a different entry and different List - Further, art. 254 would only attract where one Act enacted by State Legislature and another Act by Parliament under Concurrent List.P /&gt;(C) Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Kerala Scheduled Tribes (Restriction on Transfer of Lands and Restoration of Alienated Lands) Act, 1975 - Kerala Restriction on Transfer by and Restoration of Lands to the Scheduled Tribes Act, 1999 - Mandamus - Effect - What is the effect of issuance of Mandamus by HC with regard to the implementation of statutory provision of 1975 Act? - Held, 1975 Act was a conditional legislation, came into force only on 1986 - No benefits conferred upon a person or group of person when writ of Mandamus issued by the HC for implementation of 1975 Act - Writ issued for implementation of provisions does not mean direction for restoration of lands in question to ST members - State obliged the writ by enacted a new Act i.e., 1999 Act - Further, issuance of Mandamus not conferring rights upon the parties.P /&gt;(D) Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 300-A - Kerala Scheduled Tribes (Restriction on Transfer of Lands and Restoration of Alienated Lands) Act, 1975 - Kerala Restriction on Transfer by and Restoration of Lands to the Scheduled Tribes Act, 1999 - Vested right - Right u/art. 14 - Whether vested right of vendor-ST members was in conflict with the right of vendee u/art. 14 of Constitution? - Held, payment of compensation to the vendee was a pre-condition for actual restoration of dispossessed land to ST members - Therefore, vested right of vendors accrued only after the payment of compensation - 1975 Act deprives the right of land owners u/art. 300-A of Constitution - If the compensation has not been paid, vendees land could not be deprived - Question of applicability of art. 14 would not arise in the case of vested right has not been taken away.P /&gt;(E) Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Kerala Scheduled Tribes (Restriction on Transfer of Lands and Restoration of Alienated Lands) Act, 1975 - Kerala Restriction on Transfer by and Restoration of Lands to the Scheduled Tribes Act, 1999, s.22 - Revival of repealed Act - Whether striking down of an unconstitutional enactment would result revival of an earlier repealed Act? - Held, State Legislature repealed only those provisions of 1975 Act which were in derogation of provisions of 1999 Act - Enactment of 1999 Act was not the repeal of 1975 Act as whole - Intention of the State was to modify the 1975 Act, which would not amount to a repeal - Repealing clause in the 1999 Act is clear and unambiguous.P /&gt;(F) Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Kerala Scheduled Tribes (Restriction on Transfer of Lands and Restoration of Alienated Lands) Act, 1975 - Kerala Restriction on Transfer by and Restoration of Lands to the Scheduled Tribes Act, 1999 - Agricultural land and non-agricultural land - Classification provided in 1999 Act - Validity - Held, State has no legislative competence in relation to non-agricultural land - State could not have deprived the right of non-agricultural land holders - However, requirement of payment of compensation applicable in the case of non-agricultural land - State legislature has no competence to repeal that portion in 1975 Act.
