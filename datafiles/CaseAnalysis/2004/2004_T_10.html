T. N. Alloy Foundry Company Limited v T. N. Electricity Board and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, S.H. Kapadia, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1143; (2004) 3 SCC 392; 2004 (55) ALR 148; 2004 (2) AWC 1514; JT 2004 (2) SC 613; 2004(2) SCALE 533; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 352

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Corporate - Indian Companies Act - Sick company - Could not run due to non-supply of electrical energy - Suit for damages in 1993 - Interlocutory application for amendment of plaint in 1998 - To enhance claim for damages - Whether view taken by High Court in rejecting amendment of appellant was erroneous? - Held, it is not disputed that Appellate Court has a coextensive power of Trial Court - Discretion exercised by HC in rejecting plaint was in conformity with law - No merit - Appeal dismissed.
