Commissioner Of Income Tax, Kolkata v M/S. Hoogly Mills Co. Ltd

Supreme Court of India

22 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 835; (2007) 1 SCC 571; 2006 (206) CTR 301; [2006] 287 ITR 333; JT 2006 (10) SC 393; 2006(12) SCALE 266; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 265; [2006] 157 TAXMAN 347

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: [A.] Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 32, 32(1)(ii) - Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972, s. 4(1) - Assessee claimed that since amount of Rs. 3. 5 crores towards gratuity is capital expenditure hence it is entitled to depreciation on sum u/s.32?- Held, expenditure on taking over gratuity liability is a capital expenditure, yet no depreciation is allowable on same because s.32 states that depreciation is allowable only in respect of buildings, machinery, plant or furniture, being tangible assets, and know- How patents, copyrights, trade marks, licenses, franchises or other business or commercial rights of similar nature being intangible assets - No depreciation can be claimed in respect of gratuity liability even if it is regarded as capital expenditure - Appeal allowed.
