Dana Yadav Alias Dahu and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

13 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1803; (2002) 7 SCC 295; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1698; AIR 2002 SC 3325; 2002 (4) CCR 11; 2002 (4) Crimes 307; 2003 (1) CRJ 205; JT 2002 (7) SC 68; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 314; 2002(6) SCALE 447; 2002 (4) SCJ 296; 2002 (5) SLT 296; [2002] Supp2 S.C.R. 363; 2002 (6) Supreme 508

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.302, 149, 436 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Identification - Appellants along with accused were convicted by Trial Court u/ss. 302/149 , 307/149 and 436 IPC - On appeals convictions and sentences of appellants had been upheld by HC whereas accused had been acquitted - Hence, instant appeals - Whether HC committed any error in upholding conviction - Held, accused was known to prosecution witnesses from before, identification by witnesses in Court was not affected by fact that prayer for holding test identification parade made by accused on ground that he was not known to prosecution witnesses was not granted and accordingly conviction of accused was upheld - Hence, HC had not committed any error in upholding convictions of appellant nos. 1, 2, 4, 6 and 7 and sentences awarded against them - Appellant no. 3 was not found FIR neither known to informant nor to any of prosecution witnesses and although no test identification parade was held, he was identified in Court for first time - Hence, convictions and sentences were set aside and he was acquitted of all charges - Appeals disposed of.
