M. Ramappa v Sangappa and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 August 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 67; AIR 1958 SC 937; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 1167

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Mysore Village Offices Act

Summary: Election - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 191 and 310 - Mysore Village Offices Act, 1908, ss. 6, 7 and 8 - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 100(1)(c) - Whether certain persons were holders of offices of profit under Govt. and were therefore disqualified u/art. 191 of for being chosen as members of a legislative assembly ? - Held, yes - Duties of village officers are fixed by Govt. and these officers work under direction, control and supervision of Govt. - They are officers, who are appointed to their offices by Govt. though it may be that Govt. has no option in certain cases but to appoint an heir of last holder - Though there may be a hereditary right to hold an office, it is not held till an appointment to it is made by Govt. and that there is no one except Govt. under whom office can be held - Petition dismissed.
