Commissioner of Income Tax, and Others v Tarsem Kumar and Another

Supreme Court of India

17 July 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 18; (1986) 3 SCC 489; AIR 1986 SC 1477; 1986 (58) CTR 129; 1986 (8) ECR 504; 1986 (26) E.L.T. 10; [1986] 161 ITR 505; JT 1986 SC 220; 1986(2) SCALE 47; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 294; 1986 TAXLR 1037; [1986] 27 TAXMAN 305; 1986 (2) TLR 1037

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Income-tax Rules, 1962 - Undisclosed Income - Petitioner/respondent was travelling by car alleged to be belonging to his brother from Ambala to Batala - Petitioner in that application was searched along with his driver and Customs authorities took into possession Rs.93,500 in Indian currency, 10 gold sovereigns and car - Petitioner was produced before a Duty Magistrate and was granted bail - Customs department took proceedings u/s.110(2) of 1962 Act and extended the period of issuing of show cause notice u/s.124 of 1962 Act - Proceedings were challenged in HC and order of Customs authority u/s.110(2) was quashed by an order of HC - Appeal against that decision was dismissed by the division bench along with this petition by HC - Hence instant appeal - Order of Income Tax Department passed u/s.132 of Act and r.112(II) of 1962 Rules was challenged - Whether Income-tax authorities entitled to realize amount of money or to recover books of account or documents in accordance with law - Held, expression 'seizure' and context expression 'search' where the location of the property to Govt. - It could not be said that one Govt. department could search any other Govt. department, and seize those documents - As per referred case word 'seizure' implied forcibly taking from the owner or who has possession and who was unwilling to part with possession - Custody was with police and inappropriate to accept position that income tax department which was another department of UOI had to be armed with authority to seize from unwilling persons - Unable to sustain the challenge to order, impugned in appeal - With observations that it would be open to Income-tax authorities to approach appropriate authorities to realize amount of money or to recover books of account or documents in accordance with law - Appeal dismissed.
