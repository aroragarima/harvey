K. M. S. Reddy, Commissioner of Income Tax, Kerala v West Coast Chemicals and Industries Limited

Supreme Court of India

20 March 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 370; [1962] 46 ITR 135; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 960; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 960

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Travancore Income-Tax , s. 25 - Whether transaction of sale of raw materials along with business, including machinery, plant and premises is a revenue sale or a realisation sale consequent to winding up of business? - Where a slump price is paid and no portion is attributable to stock-in-trade, it may not be possible to hold that there is a profit other than what results from appreciation of capital - Held, this was a winding up sale with a view to realising capital assets of assessee company and not a sale in course of business operations, which alone would had attracted tax, if profit resulted - (B) Whether sale of match, machinery and premises was distinct from sale of chemicals? - Further held, agreement clearly indicated that assessee company was keeping factory going entirely on behalf of buyer; there was no identifiable price for chemicals and raw materials except by comparing two prices offered to be paid by buyer, from which alone it is impossible to infer that chemicals and raw materials were sold in ordinary way of business - Appeal dismissed.
