P. R. Deshpande v Maruti Balaram Haibatti

Supreme Court of India

11 August 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1285; (1998) 6 SCC 507; AIR 1998 SC 2979; AIR 1998 SCW 2830; JT 1998 (5) SC 389; 1999 (1) KLT 51; 1998 (2) RCJ 354; 1998 (2) RCR(Rent) 216; 1998(4) SCALE 426; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 1079; 1998 (6) SLT 601; 1998 (6) Supreme 303

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Karnataka Rent Control Act, 1961, s. 21 - Appeal by special leave by tenant - Maintainability - Order of eviction against tenant - Held, appeal cannot be dismissed - Merely because tenant had given undertaking to High Court - Statutory remedy of appeal cannot be scuttled or foreclosed by such undertaking - Non-deposit of rent by tenant during pendency of proceedings - Revision by him against order of eviction not maintainable.
