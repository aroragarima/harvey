Â British India Steam Navigation Company Limited v Jasjit Singh, Additional Commissioner of Customs

Supreme Court of India

03 February 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 3; AIR 1964 SC 1451; [1964] 34 Comp Cas 689; 1999 (110) E.L.T. 266

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Custom - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136 and 226 - Sea Customs Act, 1878, ss. 52 A and 167 - Foreign shipping vessel contravene s. 52 A of customs Act - Penalties - Appeal against order of High Court u/art. 226 - Issues are - (A) Whether foreign company can claim fundamental right u/art. 19(1)(f)? - (B) What question can be argued in appeal? - (C) Supreme Court's interference sought in light of arts. 26, 32 and 136 - Whether within Jurisdiction of Supreme Court to interfere construction alone? - Held, not being a citizen of India, foreign company is not entitled to claim fundamental right - Only question of jurisdiction can be argued - Supreme Court interfere only when alternative remedies have been already pursued - Appeals and petitions dismissed.
