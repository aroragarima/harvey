Municipal Corporation of Delhi and others v North Delhi Power Limited (Now Tata Power Delhi Distribution Limited) and another

Supreme Court of India

10 August 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

L. Nageswara Rao, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 611; (2016) 8 SCC 456; AIR 2016 SC 3744; 2016 (5) AWC 4828; 2016 (232) DLT 588; JT 2016 (8) SC 58; [2016] 9 S.C.R. 633

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Municipal Corporation, Delhi Municipal Corporation Act, 1957, Delhi Electricity Reform Act, 2000, International Airports Authority Act, 1971, Delhi Electricity Reforms (Transfer Scheme) Rules, 2001, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Delhi Electricity Reform Act, 2000, s. 20 - Delhi Municipal Corporation Act, 1957, ss. 114, 119, 119(1), 120, 120(1), 120(1)(c), 169 - International Airports Authority Act, 1971, s. 12(3) - Delhi Electricity Reforms (Transfer Scheme) Rules, 2001, r. 12(1) - Vacant plot - Exigibility of tax - Challenged - Assessment and Collection Dept. of appellant no. 1/Corporation determined rateable value of a vacant plot of 8,080 sq. meters allotted to respondents - Respondents filed an appeal u/s. 169 of the 1957 Act before District Judge, challenging impugned order - District Judge allowed appeal holding that land in dispute stood transferred to State Govt. and it was entitled for exemption from payment of property tax in view of s. 119(1) of the 1957 Act - Aggrieved, appellant no. 1 filed writ petition before HC - Single Judge allowed petition holding that provisions of s. 120(1)(c) of the 1957 Act were applicable as respondents were entitled to let out the properties on which basis it became liable to pay taxes and there is no material to establish that the Rules ruled out liability of respondents from municipal taxation - On appeal, DB held that respondents were owners of land and land rights and distribution licence issued by Commission u/s. 20 of the 2000 Act is distinct from licence for land granted in its favour - DB remanded matter back to Dpty. Assessor and Collector for determination as to whether respondents are liable to pay property tax - Hence, instant appeals - Whether exigibility of tax over the land in question was valid.

Held, s. 119 of the 1957 Act exempts the properties of Union from taxation. The administration of Union Territories is by Central Govt. but that does not mean that Union Territories become merged with Central Govt. They are centrally administered but retain their independent entity. It is clear that transfer of all the assets including land to the Govt. is a transitory step as the Holding Company is to be in total control. Further, distribution undertaking with assets mentioned in Items I, II & III of Part I of Schedule F were transferred to Distribution Company. The proviso to Items I, II & III of Part I of Schedule F to the Rules contemplates that land which was exclusively and primarily being used for business purpose by the transferee before transfer does not form part of the assets transferred and transferee would be a licnesee of Govt. for the said land on payment of a nominal amount. Thus, in view of the fact that Govt. was not to hold any asset and the vesting of land in Govt. was only transitory in nature SC uphold the findings of HC that Holding Company is the owner of land. Appeals disposed of.

(B) Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Delhi Electricity Reform Act, 2000, s. 20 - Delhi Municipal Corporation Act, 1957, ss. 114, 119, 119(1), 120, 120(1), 120(1)(c), 169 - International Airports Authority Act, 1971, s. 12(3) - Delhi Electricity Reforms (Transfer Scheme) Rules, 2001, r. 12(1) - Un-let land - Tax liability - Validity - Whether incidence of tax u/s. 120(1) of the 1957 Act was valid.

Held, s. 120(1)(c) of the 1957 Act, the person who has a right to let would be liable to pay tax for un-let land and admittedly instant land is un-let. Incidence of tax has to be decided by Authority after taking into consideration the provisions of the Act, rules and the licences, including the distribution licence. SC do not agree with the findings of HC. S. 120(1)(c) of the 1957 Act contemplates that a person who has the right to let out un-let land is liable to pay tax. His status as a lessor or licensee is irrelevant. If the distribution licence empowers Distribution Company to let out land, notwithstanding the fact that Distribution Company is a licensee as per Schedule 'F' of the Rules, it would still have to pay the tax. SC confirms the order of remand passed by HC in impugned judgment with a direction to Deputy Assessor and Collector to consider the provisions of the 1957 Act, the 2000 Act, the Rules and Distribution licence issued u/s. 20 of the 2000 Act for deciding the matter pertaining to the incidence of tax. Appeals disposed of.
