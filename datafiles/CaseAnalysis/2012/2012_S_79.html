Shaleen Kabra v Shiwani Kabra

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

A.R. Dave, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 169; (2012) 5 SCC 355; AIR 2012 SC 2467; 2012 (5) ALD(SC) 77; 2012 (95) ALR 119; 2012 (3) ALT 57; 2012 (4) AWC 3445; 2012 (5) Bom.C.R. 60; 2012 (2) CLT(SC) 145; JT 2012 (4) SC 618; 2012 (2) OLR 300; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 974; 2012(5) SCALE 193; 2012 (2) WLN(SC) 72

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 - Custody of child - Appellant and the respondent were living separately and were involved in various litigations since then, including a petition for divorce u/ss. 13(1)(i) & 1A of the Act filed by the appellant- father - Appellant, who was an IAS officer, had sought certain modifications in the arrangement of custody of the children and in this respect, filed applications before the Trial Court, which were allowed - On appeal, HC permitted respondent to have custody of the younger son, whereas the appellant was to have custody of the elder son - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was correct in directing separation of two children - Held, children were very much attached to each other - Welfare of both the children would be best served if they remain together - Further, children should be with the appellant-father as respondent-mother was not in a position to look after the educational need of the elder son - Appellant-father, who was a member of Indian Administrative Service and was a well groomed person, with the help of his father, who was also a professor, would be able to take very good care of the children - Impugned order passed by HC set aside and order of the Trial Court restored, subject to modification of conditions-arrangements - Appeals allowed.
