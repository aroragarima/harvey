State of Madras v Messrs Radio and Electricals Limited

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 88; AIR 1967 SC 234; 1966 (18) STC 222; [1966] Supp S.C.R. 198

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Concessional Rate Of Tax, Declared Goods, Resale, Central Sales Tax Act 1957, Central Sales Tax (Registration and Turnover) Rules 1957

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act 1957, (s 7) - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Purchase - Judicial examination - Certificate of registration - Respondents in this appeal had business in electrical equipment and were registered dealers under the 1957 Act - Certain goods were purchased by The Bombay State Electricity Board which was engaged in the production of electric energy for a certain value and they were liable to pay tax at the rate of 1 percent on the turnover u/s. 8 (1) of the 1957 Act - Assistant Commissioner of Commercial Taxes confirmed the order on the ground that transformers and other goods purchased by the Electricity Board for use in the production of electrical energy were not intended, to be used in the manufacture of goods for sale within the meaning of s. 8(3)(b) of the 1957 Act, because electricity was not at the material time 'goods' within the meaning of the Act - Whether it was the function of the selling dealer to enquire whether the requirement of the purchasing dealer was bona fide or even was or is not within the certificate of registration of that dealer - Held, whether or not the goods were in fact used for the stated purposes or even usable for such a purpose, so long as the purchasing dealer had furnished the required declaration to the selling dealer, the selling dealer becomes under law entitled to the benefit of s. 8(1) of the Act - It is no function of the selling dealer to enter into a judicial examination of whether the goods are in fact used or usable for the manufacture or processing of goods for sale by the purchasing dealer - Purchasing dealer declared that they were required for such a purpose and were further so specified in his form of registration granted by the sales tax authorities - Thus, it was not the function of the selling dealer to enquire whether the requirement of the purchasing dealer is bona fide or even is or is not within the certificate of registration of that dealer - Appeals dismissed.
