State of Orissa v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, R.V. Raveendran, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 339; (2010) 5 SCC 674; 2010 (4) ALD(SC) 138; JT 2010 (6) SC 16; 2011 (5) MLJ(SC) 151; 2010(5) SCALE 14; [2010] 5 S.C.R. 1160

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Zamindar, Conveyancing Act, 1881, Government of India (Constitution of Orissa) Order, 1936, Government of India (Excluded and partially Excluded areas) Order, 1936, State of Andhra Under Andhra State Act, 1953, Madras Impartible Estate Act, 1904

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Constitution - Parliament & Legislature - Government of India (Constitution of Orissa) Order, 1936, s. 3(2)(3) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 131 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 80 - Territorial dispute between States - Suit before SC - Maintainability - Plaintiff/State claimed that the disputed area was a part of its territory and it had remained with plaintiff within its administrative jurisdiction when plaintiff/State was created and later on when the Constitution was enforced - Since the creation of defendant/State, defendant/State enforced its own administration over the disputed area - Plaintiff referred the matter to Central Government, however, Central Government expressed its inability to intervene in the matter - Hence plaintiff filed the present suit before SC u/art. 131 of the Constitution against defendant/State for declaration and for recovery of area - Defendant contended that since the plaintiff/State had not claimed administrative jurisdiction over the disputed area and neither did it assert its claim before the then Governor General at the time of the creation of plaintiff/State, no relatable question can be raised after the enforcement of art. 1(2) of the Constitution - (a) Whether present suit is maintainable u/art. 131 of the Constitution - Held, plaintiff prayed in the suit for a declaration that the defendant is liable to vacate the disputed area - Plaintiff has not sought any increase, alteration or diminishing of any area but only a declaration that the disputed area comes under the administrative jurisdiction of the plaintiff/State - Art. 131 itself does not put fetters on SC to decide present original suit and there would be no encroachment on the constitutionally sanctioned power of the Parliament to alter state boundaries - Therefore, original jurisdiction of SC is not barred with reference to the proviso of art. 131 of the Constitution and hence, the present suit is maintainable - (b) Whether the suit is maintainable in view of s. 3 (2) and (3) of Order, 1936 - Held, dispute between both the states germinated in 1957, which was well after independence and at that time the position of the Governor General had become obsolete and the Union Parliament was the supreme law making body in the country, however, exclusion of judicial scrutiny in the 1936 Orissa Order which was notified in the pre-independence period cannot be mechanically carried forward to the post-independence period - Therefore, it is futile to invoke the authority of the Governor General as contemplated under the 1936 Orissa Order and hence, present suit is maintainable u/art. 131 of the Constitution which came into effect after independence - (c) Whether the suit is maintainable in the absence of notice u/s. 80 CPC - Held, procedural provisions which regulate the admissibility of civil suits before ordinary civil courts do not apply in the strict sense when SC exercises its original jurisdiction to decide suits between States - Therefore, s. 80 of CPC would not be applicable in a suit u/art. 131 of the Constitution - Suit dismissed.

(B) Land & Property - Constitution - Parliament & Legislature - Government of India Act, 1935, s. 3(2) - Whether the Disputed Area was within the territories of the Plaintiff/State as constituted under the Constitution of India - Held, a map was prepared by the Government of India as also by the erstwhile Presidency of Madras as per s. 3(2) of the Act and part II of the first schedule and that map showed that the villages in dispute were not territorially contiguous with the bounds of the plaintiff/State - Said villages were situated at some distance from the inter-state boundary and therefore, it would not be coming within the plaintiff/State's territory - Further, plaintiff admitted in a letter sent by its then the Chief Secretary to the Secretary of Central Government that the disputed area was outside the external land boundary of the plaintiff/State - Therefore, disputed area is not within the territories of plaintiff/State - Suit dismissed.

(C) Land & Property - Constitution - Parliament & Legislature - Government of India (Constitution of Orissa) Order, 1936 - Did the disputed area form a part of the Jeypore (Impartible) Estate originally and subsequently does it form part of the province of Plaintiff/State and whether plaintiff had exercised administrative jurisdiction over the disputed area - Held, disputed area was part of the Jeypore (Impartible) Estate before the notification of the Orissa Order, however, the plaintiff failed to establish that it had governed the disputed area prior to the constitution of the defendant/State in 1953, especially in light of the fact that the disputed area was located at a considerable distance from the inter-state boundary - Documents relied upon by the plaintiff did not show that the plaintiff had exercised administrative jurisdiction over the disputed area, since the same was surrounded by villages that had undeniably been under the administrative control of the defendant/State - Plaintiff admitted that till the abolition of the Jeypore Estate, it was not the plaintiff/State but the Zamindari which had collected land revenue from the disputed area - Therefore, the Order-in-Council under Orissa Order 1936 did not intend to include the disputed area within the administrative control of plaintiff/State - Suit dismissed.

(D) Land & Property - Constitution - Parliament & Legislature - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 131 - Territorial dispute - Relief prayed for - Entitlement - Whether the plaintiff is entitled to any relief in the present suit - Held, plaintiff has failed to establish that it had exercised administrative control over the disputed area after the creation of plaintiff/State in 1936, however, defendant produced documents which entailed that it was the defendant/State and its predecessor states which had been exercising the administrative jurisdiction over the disputed area - Further, defendant demonstrated that all the disputed areas are part of the present territory of defendant/State - Plaintiff could not establish that the inhabitants of the disputed area recognize plaintiff's regional language as their first language - Suit dismissed.
