Idhyadhar v Manikrao and Another

Supreme Court of India

17 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1006; (1999) 3 SCC 573; AIR 1999 SC 1441; 1999 (35) ALR 738; 1999 (3) Bom.C.R. 564; JT 1999 (2) SC 183; 1999(2) SCALE 93; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 1168; 2000 (Supp) RD 399

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Land & Property

Keywords: Conditional Sale

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 8, r. 2, O. 16, r. 1 A, s. 100 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 54 - Completion of Sale - Evidence - Non entrance in witness box by party to suit for cross examination - Inference of - Production of witness - Without summons and not enlisted as witness - Effect of - Held, payment of whole price at time of execution of deed - Is not sine qua non to completion of sale - Non appearance for cross examination has adverse inference to party - Testimony of witness without summons cannot be rejected.
