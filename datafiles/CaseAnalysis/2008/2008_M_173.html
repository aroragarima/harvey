State of Maharashtra v Madhukar Wamanrao Smarth

Supreme Court of India

24 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2344; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 52; (2008) 5 SCC 721; AIR 2008 SC 1793; 2008 CRLJ 2591; JT 2008 (4) SC 461; 2009 (1) MLJ(Crl) 543; 2008 (2) RCR(Criminal) 556; 2008(4) SCALE 412; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 303

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 389 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Grant of bail - Suspension of sentence - Respondents were guilty of having committed cheating, preparing forged and false documents for the purpose of cheating, committing criminal breach of trust by forming criminal conspiracy in furtherance of their common intention and were convicted by the Trial Court - Appeal against conviction was filed before HC and also for grant of bail by suspension of sentence in terms of s. 389, CrPC - HC granted bail to respondents on the ground that bail was granted during trial and the liberty was not misused and that there was likelihood of delay in disposal of the appeals - Whether order passed by HC justified? - Held, parameters to be observed by the HC while dealing with an application for suspension of sentence and grant of bail have been highlighted by SC - Gravity of the offence, the sentence imposed and several other similar factors need to be considered by the Court - Reasons indicated by the HC for granting bail do not satisfy the parameters - Impugned order is indefensible and set aside - Matter remitted back to the HC - Appeals allowed.
