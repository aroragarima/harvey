Â Thakur Raghubir Singh v Court of Wards, Ajmer, and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 May 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Ghulam Hasan, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 15; AIR 1953 SC 373; 1953 SCJ 505; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 1049

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Ajmer Revenue and Land Records Act, 1950, Ajmer Tenancy And Land Records Act, 1950, Ajmer Government Wards Regulation, 1888

Summary: Constitution - Land & Property- Ajmer Tenancy and Land Records Act,1950, s. 112 - Ajmer Govt. Wards Regulation, 1888, ss. 6 and 27 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(f), 19(5) and 31 A - Constituttional validity of Ajmer Tenancy and Land Records Act - (A) Whether law enacted in s. 112 is saved either by art. 19(5) or 31A of Constitution? - Held,the law enacted in s. 112 is not saved either by art. 19 (5) or 31 A of Constitution - It manifestly infringes fundamental right of a landlord guaranteed by art. 19(1)(f) and is, therefore, void to that extent - (A1)Whether exercise of discretion u/s. 112 Ajmer Tenancy and Land Records Act, 1950 can be questioned in suit filed? - Held,the result of combined operation of s. 112 of Act of 1950 and of provisions of Regn.1 of 1888 is that Court of Wards can in its own discretion and on its subjective determination, assume superintendence of property of a landlord who habitually infringes rights of his tenants - Exercise of this discretion cannot be questioned in any manner in a civil court- (A2)Whether deprivation of property for an indefinite period of time merely on subjective determination of an executive officer is application of 'reasonable restriction' in art. 19(5) of Constitution ? - Held, When a law deprives a person of possession of his property for an indefinite period of time merely on subjective determination of an executive officer, such a law cannot be construction of word 'reasonable' be described as coming within that expression, because it completely negatives fundamental right by making its enjoyment depend on mere pleasure and discretion of executive, citizen affected having no right to have recourse for establishing contrary in a civil court - (A3) Whether interpretation of word 'modification in context of art. 31A includes a mere suspension of right of management of estate ?- Word 'modification' in context of article only means a modification of proprietary right of a citizen like an extinguishment of that right and cannot include within its ambit a mere suspension of right of management of estate for a time - Petition allowed.
