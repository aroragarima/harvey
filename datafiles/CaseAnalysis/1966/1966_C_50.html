Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v Prithvi Insurance Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 163; AIR 1967 SC 853; [1967] 63 ITR 632; 1967 (1) MLJ(SC) 57; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 943

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Insurance

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 24(2) - Two businesses constituted one business - Co. was entitled to carry on both businesses under its Memorandum of Association - Whether business of life insurance and general insurance can be regarded as same business and if loss suffered in life insurance business can be carried forward and set off against profit of Co. in general insurance business of subsequent year? - Presence of one common administrative organization and common expenses incurred in connection with salary of staff, postage, staff welfare fund and general charges - Inter-connection and inter-dependence and unity are furnished in this case by existence of common management, common business organisation, common administration, common fund and a common place of business - Held, High Court was right in holding that life insurance business and general insurance business constitute same business - Appeal dismissed.
