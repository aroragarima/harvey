Nadia District Primary School Council and Another v Sristidhar Biswas and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 413; (2007) 12 SCC 779; (2008) 2 SCC (L&S) 946; AIR 2007 SC 2640; 2007 (3) ESC 382; JT 2007 (6) SC 188; 2007(6) SCALE 159; 2007 (5) SCJ 484; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 586; 2007 (3) SLR 536

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: West Bengal Primary Education Act, 1970

Summary: Panel for appointment of primary teachers was prepared-Notification dated 5th September, 1973 provided that while giving appointment out of the panel already prepared, preference should be given to the trained candidates for appointment to all the additional posts - Preparation of panel for appointment to the post of primary teachers omitting to include trained candidates was challenged in Sirazul Haque Mallick case- Division Bench passed an order on concession directing to give appointment to the petitioners in the existing vacancies and in vacancies arising in immediate future - Similary placed petitioner given benefit of appointment in Dibakar Pal's case - Held, in the present case, the panel was prepared in 1980 and the petitioners approached the court in 1989 after the decision in Dibakar Pal's case, such persons should not be given any benefit by the Court when they allowed more than nine years to elapse, delay is very significant in matters of granting relief and Courts cannot come to the rescue of the persons who are not vigilant of their rights, the judgment given in Mallick's case is not binding as it does not decide the law - Appeal allowed.
