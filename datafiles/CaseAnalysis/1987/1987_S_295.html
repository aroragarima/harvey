Rajan Kumar Machananda v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

23 November 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28487; (1990) Supp SCC 132; (1990) SCC (Cr) 537; 1990 CRLR 602B; 1988 (14) ECR 3; JT 1987 (4) SC 637; JT 1987 (4) SC 637A

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 397(3) and 482 - Second revision not maintainable - Whether bar u/s. 397(3) CrPC should have been taken note of to reject revision at instance of State Govt. or action taken by High Court in exercise of its inherent power has to be sustained ? - Held, yes - Merely by saying that jurisdiction of HC for exercise of its inherent power was being invoked statutory bar could not have been overcome - HC had no jurisdiction to entertain revision - Appeal allowed.
