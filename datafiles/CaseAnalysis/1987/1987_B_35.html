Baldev Singh and Others v State of Himachal Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28423; (1987) 2 SCC 510; (1987) SCC (Cr) 519; AIR 1987 SC 1239; 1987 (1) CCC 959; JT 1987 (2) SC 103; 1987(1) SCALE 724; 1987 (1) UJ 537

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Himachal Pradesh Municipal Act 1968

Summary: Municipalities and Local Governments - Himachal Pradesh Municipal Act 1968, s.256 - Constitution of notified area - An attempt was made to constitute a notified area as provided u/s.256 of the Act by including portions of four villages - Residents thereof appeal by special leave against summary dismissal of writ petition challenging the notification - (A) Whether inclusion of agricultural area through administrative act is valid - (B) Whether prior opportunity of hearing to residents of locality to be given as it involves civil consequences - Held, purely agricultural villages cannot be included in Notified Area - Must be afforded, though not clearly provided in s. 256 of the Act - Appeal allowed.
