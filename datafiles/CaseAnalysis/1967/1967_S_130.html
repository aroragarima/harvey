Jai Charan Lal v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 233; AIR 1968 SC 5; 1967 ALJ 936; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 981

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1916

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Uttar Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1916, s. 87 A(3) - Appellant elected as a member of Municipal Board - Notice of intention to move a motion of non-confidence in appellant was presented by certain members of Board - Date for meeting of Board fixed - Appellant filed a petition u/art. 226 of Constitution, 1950 in High Court of Allahabad asking that meeting be stopped - By that date petition was listed adjourned meeting was held and non-confidence motion was passed unanimously - Appellant asked HC to quash resolution of Board - HC declined to do so - Whether meeting itself was contrary to provisions of s. 87-A of U.P. Municipalities Act and resolution therefore being ultra vires and illegal was void? - (A) Whether notice which was sent out by District Magistrate by registered post did not allow seven clear days before date of meeting as required by latter part of sub-s. (3) of s. 87A? - Held, Sub-section says that District Magistrate shall send notice not less than seven clear days before date of meeting and word 'send' shows that critical date is date of dispatch of notice - As notice was sent on 17 th and meeting was to be called on 25th, it is obvious that seven clear days did intervene and there was no breach of this part of section - (B) Whether District Magistrate had to convene meeting for consideration of motion on a date which was not earlier than thirty days from date on which notice u/sub-s. (2) of 87A was delivered to him? - 'Not earlier than thirty days' means that it should not be 29th day, but there is nothing to show that language excludes 30th day from computation - Date on which meeting was to be called need not be excluded provided by doing so one did not go in breach of expression 'not earlier than thirty days ' - 25th of November was 30th day counting from October 26 leaving out initial day and therefore it cannot be described as earlier than thirty days - Held that it was not earlier than thirty days from date on which notice u/s. (2) was delivered to the District-Magistrate - (C) Whether action of Addl. Civil Judge, who was nominated to preside over meeting and was unable to do same violated provisions of s. 5? - Sub-s. (4) provides that if presiding judicial officer does not attend meeting, meeting stands automatically adjourned after half an hour - U/sub-s. (5) if judicial officer is unable to preside at meeting he may, after recording his reasons, adjourn meeting to such other date and time as he may appoint - It is not necessary that judicial officer should be present at meeting and then adjourn it for purposes of sub-s. (5) - Judicial officer can take action in advance - This will be convenient all round because it will save members from attendance on that day - Held, that this was done in this case and action was correct - Appeal dismissed.
