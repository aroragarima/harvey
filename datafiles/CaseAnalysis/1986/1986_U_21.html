Minerva Mills Limited And Ors. Etc. Etc. v Union Of India And Ors

Supreme Court of India

09 September 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 192; (1986) 4 SCC 222; AIR 1986 SC 2030; 1988 (3) BankCLR 151; 1986 (2) CCC 880; [1987] 61 Comp Cas 406; JT 1986 SC 375; 1986(2) SCALE 381; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 718; 1987 (1) UJ 308

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Textiles

Keywords: Sick Textile Undertakings (Nationalisation) Act, 1974

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Corporate - Limitation Act, 1963 - Industries (Development and Regulation) Act, 1951, ss.15, 16 and 18A - Sick Textile Undertakings (Nationalisation) Act, 1974, ss. 2(j) sch.1, 4(1)5(1), 19(3), 21, 25 and 27 - Constitution, arts14,19, 31C and 39(b) - Declaration of undertaking as Sick - Legislative judgments must be looked upon with respect - Take over of management on the ground that it was managed in manner highly detrimental to public interest - Enquiry u/s.15 and 18 - Copy of report not supplied - Company owning unit given reasonable opportunity of being heard - Questions arise - (A) Whether the takeover of the management can be challenged - (B) Can the declaration of undertaking as sick textile undertaking by its inclusion in sch.1, s. 2(j) be cancelled - (C) Giving of directions to undertaking u/s.16 is a condition precedent to takeover - (D) Whether non supply of copy of report amounts to denial of principles of natural justice u/art. 226 of the Constitution - (E) Being in furtherance of purpose of art. 39(b), the Act enjoys protective umbrella of art. 31C - Whether 1974 Act violates arts. 14, 19 and thereby damage the basic structure of the Constitution - (F) Whether the vacant land within compound of undertaking vests in the Govt. as an asset in the light of s. 4(1) of the 1974, Act -Held, takeover not open to challenge on ground that financial assistance was given by State Govt. - Very strong grounds would be necessary to set declaration at naught - Direction is not a condition precedent - No denial of natural rights - Act not open to challenge on ground that it violates arts. 14 and 19 and it do not damage the basic structure of the Constitution - Vacant land held to be vest in the govt. - Petition Dismissed.
