Lilawati Agarwal (Dead) By Lrs. and Others v State of Jharkhand

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 603; (2008) 15 SCC 464; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1775; 2008 (72) ALR 182; 2008 (2) AWC 1849; JT 2008 (4) SC 624; 2008 (2) KLT 224; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 78; 2008(6) SCALE 272; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 1160

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - ss. 23(1-A), 23(2) and s. 28 - Appeals relate to entitlement of claimants/appellants for benefits u/ss. 23(1-A), 23(2) and s. 28 of the Act - Notification u/s. 4(1) of the Act was issued on 21.4.1965, s. 6 notification was issued on 10.11.1966 and Collector's Award was made on 6.4.1972, s.30 of Land Acquisition (Amendment) Act, 1984 was introduced and made operative with effect from 24.9.1984, reference court decided reference on 30.9.1985, High Court held that in view of decision of Apex Court in K.S. Paripoornan v. State of Kerala appellant was not entitled to the benefit u/ss. 23(1-A), 23(2) and s. 28 of the Act - Appellant submitted that benefit u/s. 23(1-A) may not be available in view of what has been stated in K.S. Paripoornan's case ('Paripuranan I') yet in view of decision of Apex Court in Union of India v. Raghubir Singh and in K.S. Paripoornan v. State of Kerala 'Paripoornan II', benefit u/s. 23(2) and s. 28 of the Act are available - Held, in Raghubir Singh's case two terminus points were fixed i.e. Award by the Collector or decision of the reference Court must have been taken between 30.4.1982 and 24.9.1984 and it has been clearly stated that every case "must" have been decided between the aforesaid terminus; in Paripuranan II's case it was observed that restrictive interpretation should not be given - It is appropriate to refer the matter to larger bench to consider correctness of the view expressed in Paripurnan II's case holding that a restricted interpretation should not be given - Matter Referred to a Larger Bench.
