Â Central Potteries Limited v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 159; AIR 1966 SC 932; [1963] 1 S.C.R. 166; 1962 (13) STC 472

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Central Provinces & Berar Sales Tax Act, 1947, ss. 3, 8, 10 and 11(i) - Whether appellant is not liable to pay tax under provisions of Central Provinces and Berar Sales Tax Act, 1947 on ground alleged that it had not been validly registered as a dealer u/s. 8 of Act? - Held, this liability to pay tax is not conditional on registration of dealer u/s. 8 - Position of dealer who has obtained a certificate of registration which turns out to be invalid cannot on principal, be distinguished from that of one who has failed to obtain a certificate - Appellant is liable to pay tax - Appeal dismissed.
