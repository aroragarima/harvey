Ramesh Kumari v State (N.C.T. of Delhi) and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 139; (2006) 2 SCC 677; AIR 2006 SC 1322; 2006 CRLJ 1622; 2006 (1) CTC 666; 2006 (127) DLT 636; 2006 (1) JCC 468; JT 2006 (2) SC 548; 2006 (2) RCR(Criminal) 197; 2006(2) SCALE 457; 2006 (3) SCJ 530; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 403; 2006 (1) UC 454

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Cognizable offence - Complaint - Registration - CBI - Information of a cognizable offence had been filed by appellant before Station House Officer (SHO) but was not registered - Matter was brought to notice of Police Commissioner without any result - Appellant approached HC by filing Criminal W.P. - HC dismissed petition on ground that contempt petition was pending and appellant had an alternative remedy - Hence, instant appeal - Whether non registration of complaint for cognizable offence was justified - Held, allegation was against police personnel, interest of justice would be better served if case was registered and investigated by an independent agency like CBI - Direction was given that CBI should register case and investigate of complaint filed by appellant - CBI could collect complaint from SHO, Police Station - Complainant would provide photocopies of complaint in case of original complaint was not traceable in Police Station - Since, mater was pending from 1997 CBI was directed to register case and complete investigation within period of three months from date of judgment - HC was requested to expedite disposal of Contempt Petition in any event not later than three months from date of judgment for which parties should give co-operation - Registry should despatch copies of order to CBI and HC - Appeals disposed of.
