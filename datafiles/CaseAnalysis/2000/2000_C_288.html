Amar Nath Singh v Continental Constructions Limited, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

14 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2961; (2001) 10 SCC 760; 2001 (1) LLJ 184; 2000 (8) SLT 729

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Loss of eyesight - Compensation - Held, medically it was assessed that loss of vision was only 80% - Though there has been a loss of eyesight but evidence presented by respondents reflects that appellant is fit for work and same has been claimed by appellant - Out of Rs. 1,97,000 deposited in High Court towards compensation and penalty, which has been withdrawn by appellant, a sum of Rs. 1,00,000 shall be retained by appellant while a balance of Rs. 97,000 shall be refunded to respondent in six months - Appeal disposed of.
