Warner Hindustan Limited v Collector of Central Excise, Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

03 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 95; (1999) 6 SCC 762; AIR 1999 SCW 4916; 2001 (95) ECR 36; 1999 (113) E.L.T. 24; 1999 (34) RLT 595

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Tariff, Heading 30, 03.30, 17.04 and 3003.19 - Whether 'Halls Ice Mint tablets' rightly classified as 'Ayurvedic medicines'? - Held, Tribunal wrong in allowing appeal of Excise authorities and classifying mint tablets as items of confectionery under Heading 17.04 - Further held, correct course for Tribunal to have followed was to have dismissed appeal of Excise authorities, making it clear that it was open to Excise authorities to issue a fresh show cause notice to appellant on basis that tablets were classifiable under Heading 17 04 as items of confectionery - Impermissible for Tribunal to consider a case that is laid for first time in appeal because stage for setting out factual matrix is before authorities below - Appeal allowed.
