Nagendra Nath Mondal v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 401; (1972) SCC (Cr) 227; (1972) 1 SCC 498; AIR 1972 SC 665; 1972 CRLJ 482; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 75; 1972 UJ 553

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Preventive Detention Act, 1950

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 22(2) - West Bengal (Prevention of Violent Activities) Act, 1970, s. 3(3) - Preventive detention - Validity of Writ of Habeas corpus - (A) Plea of additional grounds - Raised at the stage of hearing - Sustainability - Held, additional grounds could be raised at the stage of hearing in Habeas corpus petition from jail - (B) Delay in considering the representation - Violation of art. 22(5) - Held, representation made by detenu was received by Govt. on 27-05-1971 - It was not considered and disposed of till 01-07-1971 - The, delay in deciding representation was of 34 days, but part of it was due to fact that representation and record remained with Board - There is a just and proper analogy between this case - There can be no hard and fast rules with regard to time which Govt. can or should take - That each case must be decided on its own facts - In circumstances of present case, it was unable to say that delay was so inordinate as to affect validity of petitioner's detention - Petition dismissed.
