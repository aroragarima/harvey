Union of India and others v Balwant Singh

Supreme Court of India

22 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 509; JT 2015 (7) SC 368; 2015(8) SCALE 201; 2015 (5) SLR 633

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Assam Rifles Act, 1941

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Alleged Misconduct - Discharge from Service - Justification - Respondent was inducted into service of Assam Rifles as Rifleman on 25-11-1991 and claimed to have discharged his duties to absolute satisfaction of his superiors, and earned promotions to higher ranks till 2007, when he came to hold rank of Havaldar - However, show cause was issued dt. 27-8-2008 informing him, that he had earned four 'Red Ink entries' - Further, order of discharge was passed on 7-2-2009 was passed - Further, representation of respondent before higher authority was rejected - Lower Court dismissed petition filed by respondent, however, order was reversed by DB - Hence, present appeal.

Held, after three 'Red Ink entries' were issued to respondent, wherein he was severely reprimanded, he was issued notice dt. 2-3-2008 informing him that one further 'Red Ink entry' would entail discharge from service. Respondent acknowledged receipt of aforesaid notice, and undertook to ensure that he would not earn any further 'Red Ink entry'. However, yet another 'Red Ink entry' was issued to respondent on 11-8-2008. Further, punishment was inflicted on respondent, after last of four 'Red Ink entries', on 29-12-2008, when he was again severely reprimanded and issued further 'Red Ink entry' on 6-1-2009, for having absented himself without leave and for having been found in intoxicated state, while on duty, on 29-12-2008. SC held that dismissal order passed by authority against respondent was in accordance with law. Hence, impugned order passed by DB is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Dismissal order passed by authority based on evidence on record and proved misconduct cannot be quashed.
