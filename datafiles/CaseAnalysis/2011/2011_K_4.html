Thermax Limited And Others v K. M. Johny And Others

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 695; (2011) 13 SCC 412; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 3603; 2012 (2) CGLJ 541; 2012 CRLJ 438; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 393; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 409; [2011] 14 S.C.R. 154

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Criminal - Contract & Commercial - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure,1973,s. 156(3) - Indian Penal Code,1860, ss. 34, 120B, 405, 406, 420 - Cancellation of contract - Complaint - Maintainability - Appellant gave contract to respondent for manufacture of stationary L.P.G. storage tanks - Later appellant had cancelled contract as respondent no.1 failed to execute contract - Respondent No. 1 filed complaint before respondent no.2 / Crime Branch alleged appellants had to pay Rs.68,79,750/ and was not paid till date - As respondent no.2 had not took any cognizance, complaint was filed in Trial Court and on Magistrate directions respondent no.2 to initiated proceedings against appellant company - Aggrieved appellant filed writ in HC and HC set aside Trial Courts order and matter remitted back for hearing both parties - On HC directions to produce documents respondent no.2 submitted in Trial Court that records were destroyed - Hence ,Trial Court by order dt.20-8-2007 called for report u/s. 156(3) CrPC from respondent no. 2 - Aggrieved, appellant filed writ before HC which was dismissed- Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in confirming Trial Court calling for report u/s. 156(3) CrPC from respondent no.2/Crime Branch - Held , in proceedings u/s. 156(3) CrPC complaint should disclose material ingredients of ss.405, 406, 420 and 34 IPC and dishonest intention to deceive another person had to be proved - Dishonest intention could not be seen from complaint and entire dispute pertains to contractual obligations which was of civil nature - Complaint lacks necessary ingredients u/ss. 405, 406, 420 and 34 IPC - Complainant had earlier filed 3 complaints with similar allegations and were closed as alleged claim was of civil nature and complainant suppressed same and filed instant complaint - No specific allegations against appellant Nos. 2 to 8 and were added only to gain wrongfully - Respondent no.1 had dishonestly filed criminal complaint in 2002 as his claim was barred by limitation as alleged disputes pertained to 1993 to 1995 - Magistrate had committed grave error in calling for report u/s. 156(3) CrPC - HC ought to have quashed proceedings as there were several infirmities - Dispute pertains to civil nature and criminal prosecution could not be initiated - Trial Courts and HC Orders were set aside - Appeal allowed.
