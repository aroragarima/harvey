Â Thomas Dana v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

04 November 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 1; AIR 1959 SC 375; 1959 CRLJ 392; 1983 ECR 1678; 1999 (110) E.L.T. 63; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 274; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 274

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Constitution - Excise - Customs - Constitution of India, 1950 arts. 20(2) and 32 - Sea Customs Act, 1878, s. 167 - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1947, ss. 23 and 23B - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 40 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 3(37) - Whether a proceeding before Sea Customs Authorities under s. 167(8) of Sea Customs Act, 1878 amounts to 'prosecution' within meaning of art. 20(2) of Constitution ? - Held, no - 'prosecution' means a proceeding by way of indictment or information in Criminal Courts - Chief Customs Officer or any other officer lower in rank than him in Customs department is not a Court - Appeal dismissed.
