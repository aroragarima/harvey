Indian Oil Corporation Limited and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 September 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

A.C. Gupta, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 203; (1980) Supp SCC 426; AIR 1981 SC 446; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 673; 1981 (47) STC 1; 1980 TAXLR 1795; 1980 UJ 928

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax -Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, ss. 3 and 9 - Sale - Inter-state or intra state -Goods transferred from Bihar to Uttar Pradesh - Assessed both under Central sale tax and state Sales Tax Act - Whether State of UP had jurisdiction to collect sales tax on sale of naphtha under State Act ? - As movement of naphtha commences from Barauni in Bihar, sales tax payable on sales of naphtha under agreement can be assessed and collected only by authorities in State of Bihar on behalf of Govt. of India - Held, that sale was inter-state and u/s. 9 of SC ST Act, authorities under State of Bihar were right to collect sales tax on behalf of Govt. of India and therefore, State of UP could not collect sales tax on sale of naphtha under State Act - Appeal allowed.
