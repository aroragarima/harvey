Pushpa Devi Bhagat (D) Th. Lr. Sadhna Rai v Rajinder Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 315; (2006) 5 SCC 566; AIR 2006 SC 2628; 2006 (64) ALR 614; 2006 (3) AWC 3134; 2006 (4) JhrCR(SC) 30; JT 2006 (6) SC 235; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 258; 2006 (3) RCR(Civil) 479; 2006 (2) RCR(Rent) 150; 2006(7) SCALE 8; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 370

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 43, rr. (1)(u), 1(m), 3, ss. 151, 152, 96, 96(3) - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Whether appeal filed by petitioner u/s. 96 against consent decree was maintainable? - Whether compromise arrived at on 23/5/2001 resulting in a consent decree D/-18/7/2001 was not a valid compromise u/o.23, r. 3? - Held, no appeal is maintainable against a consent decree having regard to specific bar contained in s.96(3) - Only remedy available to a party to a consent decree to avoid such consent decree, is to approach court which recorded compromise and made a decree in terms of it, and establish that there was no compromise - There is a valid compromise in writing and signed by parties (represented by counsel) - Appeal dismissed.
