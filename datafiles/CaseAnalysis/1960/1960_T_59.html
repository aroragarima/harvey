Â Bhopal Sugar Industries Limited v Income Tax Officer, Bhopal

Supreme Court of India

02 September 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 387; AIR 1961 SC 182; [1960] 40 ITR 618; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 35; 1961 (1) SCJ 191; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 474; 1960 (40) STC 618

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s.66(1) - Assessment - Market value - Deduction - Agricultural expenses - Appellant was manufacturer of sugar - During the year of account ending, appellant/company purchased sugar-cane from cultivators at various purchasing centers - Average cost of transporting sugar-cane from various centers to factory was stated - During same period appellant company grew its own sugar cane to the extent and brought same along with cultivators sugar-cane to its factory for manufacturing sugar - For sugar-cane grown on its own farms appellant/company claimed as its market value - Appellant/Company deducted from market value as agricultural expenses of harvesting loading and claimed balance agricultural income to be deducted from computation of its total income for assessment year - Respondent accepted agricultural expenses but computed market value of sugar-cane grown on appellant/company's own farms - Appellate Assistant Commissioner allowed to be deducted from total income of appellant - Income Tax Appellate Tribunal found that there was no dispute before Tribunal as to agricultural expenses and Tribunal had to decide related to the market value of 6,78,490 maunds of sugar-cane grown on appellant company's own farms - Hence instant appeal - Whether failure of legal duty by respondent was malicious of principle of justice - Held, that refusal to carry out directions which a superior Tribunal had given in exercise of its appellate powers was in effect a denial of justice - Respondent failed to carry out legal duty imposed on him and such failure was destructive of a basic principle of justice, writ of mandamus should issue ex debito justitiae to compel respondent to carry out directions given to him by Income Tax Appellate Tribunal - It was unnecessary to consider decisions referred to above except merely to state that in none of them arose any question of condoning a refusal by an inferior tribunal to carry out the directions given to that tribunal by superior tribunal in undoubted exercise of its appellate powers, on the ground that order of superior tribunal was wrong - Appeal allowed.
