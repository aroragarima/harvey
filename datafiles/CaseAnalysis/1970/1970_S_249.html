State of West Bengal and Others v Bata Krishna

Supreme Court of India

17 November 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 16; (1970) 3 SCC 612; AIR 1971 SC 156; 1971 LabIC 23; 1970 (2) LLJ 698; 1971 (2) SLR 600; 1971 UJ 78

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution, arts. 19(1)(a), 19(1)(b) and 19(2) and 311 - Government Servants Conduct Rules, 1926 - Civil Services - (A) Sub-Inspector - Charges of breach of rr. 20 and 23 of 1926 Rules - Disciplinary proceedings - Suspension pending enquiry with 1/4th basic pay as subsistence allowance - Charges not proved - Only conclusion that 'he was politically connected' - Order asking him to resume duty from date of order with a warning administered as a disciplinary penalty - Validity - (B) Whether r.20 (1) or r.23 (1) violates fundamental right of freedom of speech and expression guaranteed by art.19(1)(a) and (b) of Constitution and are not saved by art. 19 (2) - Order held was without any basis and could not be sustained - Expression of opinion by Letters Patent Bench of Calcutta High Court was merely academic and not necessary for decision of case as rr. 20 and 23 were not attracted - Appeal Dismissed.
