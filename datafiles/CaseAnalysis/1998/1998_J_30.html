U. P. Avas Evam Vikas Parishad v Jainul Islam and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, B.N. Kirpal, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1399; (1998) 2 SCC 467; AIR 1998 SC 1028; JT 1998 (1) SC 231; 1998 (2) MLJ(SC) 104; 1998(1) SCALE 185; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 254; 1998 (2) SLT 326; 1998 (1) Supreme 340; 1998 (1) UPLBEC 650

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 23 and 28 - Uttar Pradesh Awas Evam Vikas Parishad Adhiniyam, 1966, s. 55 - (A) Acquisition of land - Compensation - Determination - Amendments introduced in Land Acquisition Act, 1984 relating to determination of payment of compensation viz. ss. 23(1-A), 23(2) and 28 as amended by 1984 Act - Whether would be applicable to acquisition for purpose of Adhiniyam? - (B) Determination of compensation - Agreements for sale relied upon by party - No material to show that agreements had matured into sale transactions - Not much assistance could be derived from such agreements for determining market value of acquired land - Whether application for adducing additional evidence for filing agreement rightly rejected? - (C) Determination of market value - Whether direction about deduction of one third of price towards cost of development for housing scheme involving construction of roads and other amenities is illegal? - Held, (A) Amendments would be applicable to acquisition for purpose of Adhiniyam - (B) rightly rejected - (C) Such direction regarding determination of market value is illegal - Appeal dismissed.
