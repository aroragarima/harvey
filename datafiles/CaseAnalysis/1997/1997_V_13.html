K. S. Vidyanadam v Vairavan

Supreme Court of India

06 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2551; (1997) 3 SCC 1; AIR 1997 SC 1751; 1997 (2) CLT 70; 1997 (1) CTC 628; JT 1997 (2) SC 375; 1997 (88) RD 613; 1997(1) SCALE 739; 1997 (1) SCJ 415; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 993; 1997 (2) Supreme 597

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 20 - Specific performance of agreement to sale - Time limit prescribed in contract for taking steps by one or other party - (A) Whether amounts to making time essence of contract and whether same can be ignored by court? - (B) Whether purchaser entitled to specific performance? - Total inaction on part of purchaser for around 21 years was therefore in violation of terms of agreement - Held, (A) may not amount to making time essence of contract but it cannot be ignored by court altogether on that ground - (B) Delay was coupled with substantial rise in prices, three times between date of agreement and date of suit notice - Delay has brought about a situation where it would be inequitable to give relief of specific performance to purchaser - Appeal allowed.
