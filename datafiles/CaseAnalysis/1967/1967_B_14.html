Â Bejoy Lakshmi Cotton Mills Limited v State of West Bengal and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 January 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 149; AIR 1967 SC 1145; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 406

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 166 - West Bengal Land Development and Planning Act, 1948, s. 4 - The Society Of Farmers and Rural Industrialists, of which third respondent is Secretary, requested State of West Bengal, to acquire, compulsorily, certain lands for establishment of an Agricultural Colony for creating better living conditions therein - First respondent issued' a notification, u/s. 4 of Act, stating that an extent of lands is likely be needed for a public purpose - A major portion of land comprised in this notification belonged to appellant's Mills -(A) Whether for authentication of Governor, order u/art. 166 of Constitution confirms that his action needs are accordance with law? - Held, authentication makes conclusive that order has been made by Governor and not whether, in making order, Governor has acted in accordance with law - (B) Whether issue of a notification, under s. 4 of Act, is a matter which should hive been dealt with by Minister-in-Charge himself or could be delegated ? - Further held, issue of a notification under s. 4 of Act and satisfaction to be arrived at that land in area in question is required or is likely to be required for a public purpose are matters which do not require to be dealt with by Minister himself - Appeal dismissed.
