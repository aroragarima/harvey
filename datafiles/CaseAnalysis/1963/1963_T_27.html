Â Firm and Illuri Subbayya Chetty and Sons v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

25 January 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 265; AIR 1964 SC 322; 1963 (2) ALT 206; 1963 (2) ALT(SC) 206; [1963] 50 ITR 93; 1964 (1) MLJ(SC) 5; [1964] 1 S.C.R. 752; 1963 (14) STC 680

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Municipalities & Local Governments; Sales Tax

Keywords: Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939

Summary: Indirect Tax - Sales Tax - Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Act, 1939, s. 18 A - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 9 - (A) Whether a suit by an assessee for recovery of a sum of money against state on ground that said amount had been illegally recovered from him as Sales Tax under Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939 is maintainable? - Held, no - It is not maintainable in view of bar enacted in s. 18 A of that Act - There is no express provision made in Act under which suit can be said to have been filed, it falls under prohibition contained in s. 18 A - Expression 'any assessment made under this Act' in s. 18 A is wide enough to cover all assessments made by appropriate authorities irrespective of whether said assessments are correct or not - It is activity of assessing officer acting as such officer which is intended to be protected and as soon as it is shown that exercising his jurisdiction and authority under this Act, an assessing officer has made an order of assessment, that clearly falls within scope of s. 18 A - Appeal dismissed.
