State Of Karnataka & Ors. v Munikadirappa & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 862; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 92; JT 2010 (11) SC 263; 2011 (3) KarLJ 257; 2010(11) SCALE 2; [2010] 12 S.C.R. 250

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Karnataka Land Reforms Act

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Apportionment of compensation - Acquisition of land - Respondents filed applications before the Land Tribunal for grant of occupancy rights in their favour in respect of certain Inam lands, which was granted - Said order was challenged by owner of the land - During the pendency of the said proceedings, lands in question were acquired by the Bangalore Development Authority - Single Judge of the HC held that Land Tribunal was right in holding that the respondents were cultivating the land in question as tenants and in granting occupancy rights to them - However, in view of the fact that the lands in question stood acquired, Single Judge directed that compensation payable for the lands in question shall be apportioned between the owner of the land and respondents in the ratio of 60:40 - On appeal, DB held that since the respondents were found to be in cultivating occupation of the land on the appointed date they shall be deemed to be so even on the date of acquisition and that they shall be entitled to claim full compensation payable for the land acquired from them - Hence, present appeals - Whether order passed by HC was justified? - Held, question as to who was entitled to claim how much compensation for parcels of land acquired by the Government was a matter which had to be agitated by the persons interested only in terms of the provisions of the Land Acquisition Act - In the present, in the appeals filed by the respondents, DB was concerned only with the limited question whether the directions regarding apportionment of the compensation was justified - DB erred in determining the extent of compensation payable to the respondents - Hence, while grant of occupancy-tenancy rights in favour of the respondents qua the parcels of land in their respective possession as on the appointed date shall stand affirmed, the question as to who is entitled to what compensation for the acquisition of said lands in question left open to be determined in appropriate proceedings under the Land Acquisition Act - Appeals disposed of.
