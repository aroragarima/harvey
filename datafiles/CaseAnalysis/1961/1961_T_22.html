Â Shyamapada Chakrabertty and Others v Controller of Insurance, Government of India and

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 229; AIR 1962 SC 1355; [1962] 32 Comp Cas 258; 1962 (1) KerLR 493; 1962 (2) SCJ 162; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 130

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Insurance

Summary: Insurance Act, 1938, ss. 35(3) and 36 - Companies Act, 1913, ss. 10, 12, 55, 86 H, 100 and 153 - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 39, 100 and 298 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14 - Insurance Act, 1938 - Insurance Company - Transfer of business to another insurance company - Power to sell under memorandum of association - In general meeting agreeing to transfer - Sanction to transfer accorded u/s. 36 Insurance Act - Modification clause in scheme of transfer - Notice given to shareholders - Subsequent modification in pursuance of clause - Questions arise - Whether (A) Transfer amount to alteration of memorandum or is virtual winding up which has to be effected only under Companies Act? - (B) Agreement is violative of s. 86 H of Companies Act? - (C) Disappearance of assets of company, for any reason, can cause reduction of share capital? - (D) Rights of insurance agent to receive commission in respect of renewal premiums, against company affected u/ss. 36 and 44 of Insurance Act? - (E) Sanction is violative of art. 14 of Constitution? - (F) Effect of sanction to modified scheme without notice of modified scheme? - Held, transfer does not amount to alteration and transfer cannot be said to be virtual transfer, in light of s. 298 of companies act, as s. 35 r/w s. 36 makes provisions which override provisions of Companies Act, moreover question of corporate power in sense it arose in Bisgood's case, does not arise in case - Does not violate s. 86 H, it being merely tentative, subject to final approval by company at general meeting - No capital reduction - Rights of agent are not affected but remain - Sanction being given in best interest of policy-holders and on initiative of companies concerned is not violative of art. 14 - Held that scheme in substance was one sanctioned after notice u/s 35(3) - Appeal dismissed.
