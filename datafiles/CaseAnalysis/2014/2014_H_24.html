Sanjay Verma v Haryana Roadways

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, Shiva Kirti Singh, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 58; (2014) 3 SCC 210; 2014 (1) ACC 473; 2014 ACJ 692; AIR 2014 SC 995; 2014 (103) ALR 204; 2014 (2) AWC 1907; 2014 (1) CTC 745; JT 2014 (2) SC 384; 2014 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1098; 2014 (1) OLR 851; 2014 (174) PLR 641; 2014 (1) RCR(Civil) 914; 2015 (1) RLW 325; 2014(1) SCALE 682; [2014] 1 S.C.R. 924

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: (1) Motor Vehicles Act, 1988-Sections 166 and 168-Compensation-Enhancement-Injury case-Appellant self-employed person was of 25 years age and was married at time of accident-Appellant had one son of 1-1/2 years of age-Tribunal awarded sum of R 3,00,000 with interest @ 9% p.a. as compensation-High Court awarded sum of R 8,08,052 with interest @ 6% p.a. as compensation-No fault in order of High Court which proceeds on basis that annual income of appellant at time of accident was R 41,300-Taking into account age of appellant and fact that he had steady income, as evidenced by income-tax returns, addition of 50% to income that appellant was earning at time of accident would be justified-Multiplier of 17 appropriate instead of 15-R 10,53,153 to be awarded under head "loss of income"-R 1,38,552 awarded for medical expenses-Maintained-"Future treatment" and "pain and suffering" are two distinct and different heads and cannot be clubbed together-High Court wrongly clubbed together aforesaid head and awarded compensation of R 50,000 under head "future treatment" and "pain and suffering"-Grant of full compensation, as claimed in claim petition, i.e., R 3,00,000 under head "future treatment", would meet ends of justice-Considering injuries sustained by appellant which had left him paralyzed for life, sum of R 3,00,000 to be awarded on account of "pain and suffering"-Claim of R 2,00,000 made by appellants towards attendant charge awarded in full-Total compensation of R 19,91,702 instead of R 8,08,052 awarded to appellant-Compensation amount enhanced by sum of R 11,83,650-Rate of interest awarded by High Court, i.e., 6%-Maintained-Enhanced amount to carry interest @ 6% p.a.-Impugned order of High Court-Modified.(2) Motor Accident Compensation-Compensation for pain and suffering-Monetary compensation for pain and suffering-Is at best palliative-Correct dose will have to be determined on case to case basis.
