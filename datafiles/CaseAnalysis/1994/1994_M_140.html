State Of W.B v Mohammed Khalid

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 573; (1995) SCC (Cr) 266; (1995) 1 SCC 684; AIR 1995 SC 785; AIR 1995 SCW 559; 1994 (4) CCR 821; 1995 (1) Crimes 397; JT 1994 (7) SC 660; 1994(4) SCALE 1048; 1995 (1) SCJ 617; [1994] Supp1 S.C.R. 16; 1995 (1) UJ 469

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Precedent, TADA Act, Bombs, Explosion

Summary: Criminal - Constitution, art. 226 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 - TADA Act, 1987, ss. 3, 4 and 19 - Explosive Substances Act, 1908, ss. 3 and 5 - (A) Constitution - Scope of jurisdiction to interferein writ jurisdiction, with Order passed after perusing police papers - Sanction to prosecute for preparation and possession of bombs which tantamount to terrorising people under TADA Act - (B) TADA - Existence of 26 live bombs is a clear indication of conspiracy - Confessional statements - Cognizance - Interference by analysing material on record in the light of the fact that order taking cognizance was not reasoned order - Held, no interference in writ jurisdiction - Order need not be reasoned and interference by analysing material on record is not permissible in writ jurisdiction - Appeal allowed.
