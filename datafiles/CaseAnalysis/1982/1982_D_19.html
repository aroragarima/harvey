Board Of Trustees Of The Port Of Bombay v Dilipkumar Raghavendranath Nadkarni Band Others

Supreme Court of India

17 November 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 143; (1983) SCC (L&S) 61; (1983) 1 SCC 124; AIR 1983 SC 109; 1983 (2) Bom.C.R. 334; 1983 (46) FLR 30B; 1983 LabIC 419; 1983 (1) LLJ 1; 1983 (1) LLN 314; 1983 MahLJ 1; 1983 MLJ 1; 1982(2) SCALE 1097; [1983] 1 S.C.R. 828; 1983 (1) SLJ 256; 1983 (1) SLR 464; 1982 UJ 897

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service; Water

Summary: Constitution, art. 226 - Trade Union Act, 1926 - Principles of natural justice - Employer represented by legally trained officers in domestic enquiry while request of employee to be represented by lawyer refused - Held, amounts to denial of reasonable opportunity - Appeal dismissed.
