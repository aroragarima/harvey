Bengal and Assam Investors Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

02 November 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 227; AIR 1966 SC 1514; [1966] 59 ITR 547; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 471

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Excess Profits Tax, Debenture Holder, Ejusdem Generis, Business Income or Loss, Finance Bill, Finance Act

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940 - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10 - Whether in case of assessee, an investment company, its dividend income is part of its profits and gains chargeable to tax u/s. 10 of 1922 Act? - That on principle before dividends on shares can be assessed u/s. 10, assessee, be it an individual or a company or any other entity must carry on business in respect of shares that is to say, assessee must deal in those shares - It is evident that if an individual person invests in shares for purpose of earning dividend he is not carrying on a business - Held, no authority has been cited before court that in case of an individual to acquire and hold shares with object of receiving dividends is to carry on business - Thus, courts are unable to hold that if a company does same, it carried on business within s. 10 of Act - Appeal dismissed.
