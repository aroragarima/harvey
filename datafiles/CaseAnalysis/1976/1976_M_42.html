Mechalec Engineers and Manufacturers v Messrs Basic Equipment Corporation

Supreme Court of India

01 November 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, A.N. Ray, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 352; (1976) 4 SCC 687; AIR 1977 SC 577; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 1060; 1976 UJ 953

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: CPC,1908, O. 37, r. 3(2) and s. 115 - Practice and Procedure - Order granting unconditional leave to defend - Interference u/s. 115 with the question that whether defences were honest and bona fide - When justified - Held, it is only in cases where the defence is patently dishonest or so unreasonable that it could not reasonably be expected to succeed that the exercise of discretion by the trial Court to grant leave unconditionally may be questioned - Appeal allowed.
