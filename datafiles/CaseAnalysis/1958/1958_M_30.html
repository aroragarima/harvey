Earnest John White v Mrs. Kathleen Olive White and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 158; AIR 1958 SC 441; 1958 ALJ 671; 1958 (2) MLJ 125; 1958 (2) MLJ(SC) 125; 1958 SCJ 839; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 1410

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Divorce Act, 1869, ss. 14 and 7 - (A) Whether Supreme Court can interfere with finding of facts by appellate court? - Held, Supreme Court will not ordinarily interfere with findings of fact given by trial Judge and Appeal Court but if in giving findings court ignore certain important pieces of evidence and other pieces of evidence which are equally important are shown to have been misread and misconstrued and SC comes to conclusion that on evidence taken as a whole no tribunal could properly as a matter of legitimate inference arrive at conclusion that it has then in that case interference by SC will be called for - (B) Whether Judge, if satisfied beyond reasonable doubt as to commission of matrimonial offence he would be satisfied within meaning of s. 14 of Divorce Act? - Further held, in divorce cases be such that if Judge is satisfied beyond reasonable doubt as to commission of matrimonial offence he would be satisfied within meaning of s. 14 of Act - Terms of s. 14 make it plain that when Court is to be satisfied on evidence in respect of matrimonial offences guilt must be proved beyond reasonable doubt and it is on that principle that Courts in India would act and reason for adopting this standard of proof is grave consequence which follows a finding of guilt in matrimonial causes - In a suit based on a matrimonial offence it is not necessary and it is indeed rarely possible to prove issue by any direct evidence for in very few cases can such proof be obtainable -(1) Whether words 'satisfied on evidence' implies some duty of court?- Further held, words 'satisfied on evidence' imply that duty of Court is to pronounce a decree if satisfied that case for petitioner has been proved but dismiss petition if not so satisfied - Appeal allowed.
