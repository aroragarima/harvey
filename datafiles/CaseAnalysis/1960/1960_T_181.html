Vanguard Fire and General Insurance Company Limited, Madras v Messrs Fraser and Ross and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 167; AIR 1960 SC 971; [1960] 30 Comp Cas 133; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 857

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Insurance

Summary: Insurance - Companies Act, 1956 - Insurance Act, 1938, ss. 2D, 3, 5D, 7, 8, 9, 10, and 33 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Companies Act, 1913, s. 2 - General Clauses Act, 1897 - Whether Company having closed all its insurance business order can be passed against it under s. 33? - Yes -Held - When s. 2D speaks of satisfaction or 'provision otherwise' for the liabilities of insurance business which is closed, it contemplates such satisfaction or 'provision otherwise' over and above the deposit made under s. 7 - Hence, there are some liabilities still pending and they are not satisfied and no provision has been made otherwise for them irrespective of the security deposit - Appeal dismissed.
