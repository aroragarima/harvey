Â Indrajit Barua and Others v Election Commission of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 September 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, P.N. Bhagwati, Amarendra Nath Sen, V. Balakrishna Eradi, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 381; (1985) 4 SCC 722; AIR 1986 SC 103; 1985(2) SCALE 678; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 225; [1985] supp3 S.C.R. 225

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Election - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 329(b) - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 81 and 21(2) Proviso - Election - (A) Can be challenged only in manner prescribed by Representation of the People Act - Writ Petition u/art. 226, challenging elections to State Legislature - Maintainability - (B) Preparation of electoral rolls - If a process of election - (C) Challenge - Ground, of defective electoral roll - Held, not maintainable - It is not a process of election - In a suitable case challenge to the electoral roll for not complying with the requirements of the law may be entertained but the election of a candidate is not open to challenge - Order accordingly
