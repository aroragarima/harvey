K. R. Chinna Krishna Chettiar v Sri Ambal and Company, Madras and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 April 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, S.M. Sikri, V. Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 170; (1969) 2 SCC 131; AIR 1970 SC 146; 1970 (1) MLJ(SC) 21; 22 PTC 258; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 290

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Summary: Intellectual Property - Trade & Merchandise Act, 1958, ss. 2 (j) and 12(1) - Resemblance in Trade Mark - Registration - Allocation of - Whether, if appellant's mark is used in a normal and fair manner in connection with snuff and if similarly fair and normal user is assumed of existing registered marks, will there be such a likelihood of deception that mark ought not to be allowed to be registered? - Held, there is no evidence of actual confusion, but that might be due to fact that appellant's trade is not of long standing - There is no visual resemblance between two marks, but ocular comparison is not always decisive test - Resemblance between two marks must be considered with reference to ear as well as eye - There is a close affinity of sound between Ambal and Andal - Distinguishing feature of respondent's mark is Ambal while that of appellant's mark is Andal two words are deceptively similar in sound - Appeal dismissed.
