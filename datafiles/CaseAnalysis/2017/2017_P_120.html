Harjas Rai Makhija (Dead) Through Lrs. v Pushparani Jain and another

Supreme Court of India

02 January 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 2; (2017) 2 SCC 797; 2017 (120) ALR 698; 2017 (1) CGLJ 380; 2017 (1) CLT(SC) 246; 2017 (1) CTC 323; 2017 (1) JCC 330; JT 2017 (1) SC 19; 2017 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 209; 2017 (3) MahLJ 90; 2017 (1) MLJ 437; 2017 (2) MPLJ 257; 2017 (1) RCR(Civil) 998; 2017 (134) RD 811; 2017(1) SCALE 68; 2017 (1) WLN(SC) 113

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Agreement For Sale, Specific Performance, CONTRACT & COMMERCIAL, Performance, Specific Performance Of, Attorney, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Summary: Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 41 r. 27 - Sale by GPA Holder - Order Challenged - Respondent/plaintiff obtained possession of suit plot allotted by development authority with help of 'X' GPA holder - 'X' entered into agreement of sale of suit property to appellant/defendant/purchaser - Trial Court decreed suit for recovery of possession and directed handover of possession of suit property and dismissed suit filed by purchaser - First Appellate Court dismissed appeals and application u/O. 41 r. 27 of CPC for want of failure to prove execution of GPA by respondent - Trial Court dismissed appellant another suit for declaration of decree obtained by fraud by respondent as void and DB of HC in dismissed appeal of appellant on ground that certified copy GPA could not be accepted as valid evidence - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, when there is an allegation of fraud by non-disclosure of necessary and relevant facts or concealment of material facts, it must be inquired into. It is only after evidence is led coupled with intent to deceive that a conclusion of fraud could be arrived at. A mere concealment or non-disclosure without intent to deceive or a bald allegation of fraud without proof and intent to deceive would not render a decree obtained by a party as fraudulent. To conclude in a blanket manner that in every case where relevant facts are not disclosed, the decree obtained would be fraudulent, is stretching the principle to a vanishing point. In instant case, fraud has not been proved but merely alleged. Hence, impugned judgment and order passed by HC and Trial Court does not warrant any reason to interfere with it. Appeal dismissed.
