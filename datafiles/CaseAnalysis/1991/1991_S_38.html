Amirtham Kudumbah v Sarnam Kumdumban

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

T.K. Thommen, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 612; (1991) 3 SCC 20; AIR 1991 SC 1256; 1991 (17) ALR 536; 1991 (2) AWC 1192; 1991 (2) CCC 373; 1991 (44) DLT 357; 1991 (2) DMC 20; JT 1991 (2) SC 425; 1991 (2) MLJ 3; 1991 (2) MLJ(SC) 3; 1991(1) SCALE 757; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 389; 1991 (2) UJ 410

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Mere Right To Sue

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 6(e) - Hindu Minority and Guardianship Act, 1956, ss. 8(3) and 5(b) - Transfer of property by natural guardian of minor - Transfer made without permission of Court and without legal necessity - Purchaser of property from minor within three years after he attained majority - Whether transferee from minor after he attained majority, can file a suit to set aside alienation made by minor's guardian or said right is one to be exercised only by minor - Transfer of property made by guardian was a voidable transaction and it was, therefore, open to minor to challenge it and seek recovery of possession - Such a right of minor is a right or interest in property which he himself or any person claiming under him may enforce by instituting a suit as per s. 8(3) - Any person claiming under him must necessarily include a purchaser - Appeal dismissed.
