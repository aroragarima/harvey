Dr. Bool Chand v Chancellor, Kurukshetra University

Supreme Court of India

04 September 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 194; AIR 1968 SC 292; 1968 LabIC 232; 1968 (2) LLJ 135; 1967 (69) PLR 1031; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 434; 1968 SLR 119

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Kurukshetra University Act, 1956

Summary: Service - Government of India Act, 1935 - Punjab General Clauses Act, 1898, ss. 14 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Kurukshetra University Act, 1956, ss. 4, 7, 8 and 14 - Tenure - Termination of - Whether Chancellor had power to terminate tenure of office of a Vice-Chancellor? - Held, there is no express provision in Kurukshetra University Act or Statutes there under which deals with termination of tenure of office of Vice-Chancellor - A power to appoint ordinarily implies a power to determine employment - Court is unable to hold that a person appointed a Vice-Chancellor is entitled to continue in office for full period of his appointment even if it turns out that he is physically decrepit, mentally infirm, or grossly immoral - Absence of a provision setting up procedure for determining employment of Vice-Chancellor in Act or Statutes or Ordinances does not, lead to inference that tenure of office of Vice-Chancellor is not liable to be determined - Appellant had fullest opportunity of making his representation and that enquiry held by Chancellor was not vitiated because of violation of rules of natural justice - Appeal dismissed.
