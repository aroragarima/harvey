Ranchhodji Chaturji Thakore v The Superintendent Engineer, Gujaratelectricity Board, Himma

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1409; (1996) 11 SCC 603; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 547; AIR 1996 SC 1802; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 77; 1996(8) SCALE 443; 1997 (1) SLR 14; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 14; 1997 (1) Supreme 152

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Dismissal - Criminal - Petitioner convicted of crime under IPC, s. 302 r/w s. 34 - Petitioner challenged the validity of the dismissal order - Pending disposal, petitioner acquitted - Single judge directed the respondent to reinstate him into the service - Whether petitioner is entitled to back wages? - Held, backwages payable only if petitioner was unlawfully prevented from discharging the duties - Since the petitioner had involved himself in a crime, though he was later acquitted, he had disabled himself from rendering the service on account of conviction and incarceration in jail - Under these circumstances, petitioner is not entitled to payment of back wages - Petition dismissed.
