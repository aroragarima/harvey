Raghuvansh Dewanchand Bhasin v State of Maharashtra and another

Supreme Court of India

09 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 562; (2012) 9 SCC 791; 2011 (6) CTC 228; JT 2011 (10) SC 253; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 689; 2013(1) M.P.H.T. 129; 2012 (1) OLR 5; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 212; 2012 (1) RLW 713; 2011(10) SCALE 233; [2011] 11 S.C.R. 300

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 324 - Bailable offence - Issuance of non-bailabla warrant - Justifiability - Criminal complaint filed against appellant advocate u/s. 324 IPC - Magistrate issued non-bailable warrant against appellant due to his non-appearance - Magistrate cancelled the non-bailable warrant on the appearance of appellant in the subsequent posting - However, complainant with the assistance of 2nd respondent Inspector of Police executed the non-bailable warrant and arrested the appellant in a public gathering on Independence day despite the cancellation of non-bailable warrant - Appellant petitioned to HC for the issuance of writ praying disciplinary action against 2nd respondent and adequate compensation and damages thereof - HC allowed the writ petition directing the 2nd respondent to pay by way of costs to the appellant from his own account - Being dissatisfied with the relief granted by the HC, appellant preferred the instant appeal - Whether the issuance of non-bailable warrant against appellant was manifestly justified in the facts and circumstances of the case - Held, having regard to nature of the complaint against the appellant and his stature in the community and the fact that admittedly the appellant was regularly attending the court proceedings, it was not a fit case where non-bailable warrant should have been issued by the Magistrate - Attendance of the appellant could have been secured by issuing summons or at best by a bailable warrant - Issuance of non-bailable warrant against appellant was manifestly unjustified - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 324 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - Cancellation of non-bailable warrant - Execution of warrant - Legality - Whether there was any violation of personal liberty of appellant while executing the non-bailable warrant against him - Held, any form of degrading treatment would fall within the inhibition of art. 21 of the Constitution - In the instant case, 2nd respondent was aware that the non-bailable warrant issued on account of failure on the part of the appellant to attend the court proceedings on 7-8-2002, was returnable only on 31-10-2002 - 2nd respondent was duty bound to execute the warrant as expeditiously as possible but there was no justifiable reason for the urgency in executing the warrant on a National holiday, more so when it had been issued more than a week ago and even the complaint against the appellant was in relation to the offence punishable u/s. 324 of the IPC - Complaint related to the year 2000 and at the relevant time, the offence punishable u/s. 324 of the IPC was a bailable offence - It was apparent from the record that the warrant was executed at the behest of the complainant in order to denigrate and humiliate the appellant at a public place, in public view, during the course of Independence day celebrations - 2nd respondent, in collusion with the complainant, played with the personal liberty of the appellant in a high handed manner - Unfortunate sequel of an unmindful action on the part of 2nd respondent was that the appellant, a practicing Advocate, with no criminal history, remained in police custody for quite some time without any justification whatsoever and suffered unwarranted humiliation and degradation - Further, 2nd respondent did not perform his duty in the manner expected of a responsible police officer - As a matter of fact, being the guardian of the liberty of a person, a heavy responsibility devolved on 2nd respondent to ensure that his office was not misused by the complainant to settle personal scores - So-called urgency or promptness in execution led to undesirable interference with the liberty of the appellant, hence such a conduct cannot receive a judicial imprimatur - Appeal dismissed.

(C) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 324 - Cancellation of non-bailable warrant - Execution of warrant - Violation of personal liberty - Compensation - Entitlement - Whether on facts and circumstances of the case, the appellant was entitled to monetary compensation in addition to what had already been awarded to him by the HC - Held, being a practicing Advocate himself, the appellant was fully conversant with the court procedure and, therefore, should have procured a copy of memo/order dated 12-8-2002, whereby the non-bailable warrant was cancelled by the court - Appellant applied and obtained a copy of such order only on 16-8-2002 - Though the conduct of 2nd respondent in arresting the appellant, ignoring his plea that the non-bailable warrant issued by the court in a bailable offence had been cancelled, deserves to be deplored, yet, strictly speaking the action of 2nd respondent in detaining the appellant on the strength of the warrant in his possession, perhaps motivated, cannot be said to be per se without the authority of law - In that view of the matter, no other action against 2nd respondent was warranted as he had been sufficiently reprimanded - No ground was made out warranting interference with the impugned judgment of the HC - Appeal dismissed.
