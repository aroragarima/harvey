Buta Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K. Ramaswamy, Veeraswami Ramaswami

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 47; (1991) SCC (Cr) 494; (1991) 2 SCC 612; AIR 1991 SC 1316; AIR 1991 SCW 1022; 1991 (2) Crimes 46; 1991 CRLJ 1464; 1991 CRLR 384; JT 1991 (5) SC 366; 1991(1) SCALE 597

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 95 and 300 - Murder - (A) Delay in lodging of first information report - Held, yes, it vitiates prosecution - Further delay in forwarding special report to Magistrate as well as case papers to hospital - Delay in lodging of first information report and suspicion that it was delayed with view to concocting prosecution case and further delay in forwarding special report to Magistrate as well as case papers to hospital showed that investigation was not above board - (B) Whether in facts and circumstances, appellant has right of private defence u/s. 95 IPC, 1860 and entitled to benefit of doubt - Version supported by objective evidence cannot be brushed aside lightly unless properly explained - Appellant and his wife fought to repel attack and in course of incident both sides sustained injuries - Appellant and his wife clearly defending themselves - Hence right of private defence - Appellant entitled to benefit of doubt - Appeal allowed.
