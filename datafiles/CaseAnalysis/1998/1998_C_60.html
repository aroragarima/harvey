Indian Oil Corporation Limited v Chief Inspector of Factories and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 July 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1030; (1998) 5 SCC 738; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1433; AIR 1998 SC 2456; [1998] 94 Comp Cas 64; 1998 (77) ECR 656; 1999 (113) E.L.T. 761; 1998 (80) FLR 45; JT 1998 (4) SC 631; 1998 LabIC 2735; 1998 (2) LLJ 604; 1998 (4) LLN 37; 1998(4) SCALE 116; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 598; 1998 (6) SLT 239; 1998 (5) Supreme 505

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Factories Act, 1948 - S. 2(n) proviso (2)(ii) of Act - Question was who is deemed occupier of Factory of Indian Oil Corporation (Govt. owned), whether some Director or another person appointed by Govt.? - Ultimate control over all affairs of corporation was vested with Central Govt. - Held, any other person appointed by Central Govt. to manage affairs of factories shall be deemed to be occupier.
