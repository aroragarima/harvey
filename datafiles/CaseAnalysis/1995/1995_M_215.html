Real Food Products Limited and Others v A.P. State Electricity Board And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

01 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, K.S. Paripoornan, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1413; (1995) 3 SCC 295; AIR 1995 SC 2234; 1996 (2) CPJ 21; JT 1995 (3) SC 88; 1995(2) SCALE 91; 1995 (3) SCJ 138; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 396

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, s. 784 - Nature and effect of the direction given by the State Govt. u/s. 78A of the Act.

Held, the question has to be examined in the context of the facts of the present case which is confined to the charging of the flat rate per H.P. for agricultural pump sets. The nature of the function of the Board in fixing the tariffs and the manner of its exercise has been considered at length in the earlier decisions of this Court and it does not require any further elaboration in the present case. S. 78A of Act uses the expression 'the Board shall be guided by such directions on questions of policy as may be given to it by the State Government.' It does appear that the view expressed by the State Govt. on a question of policy is in the nature of a direction to be followed by the Board in the area of the policy to which it relates. Appeals dismissed.
