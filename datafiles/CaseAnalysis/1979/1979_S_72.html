Commissioner of Gift Tax, Bombay Etc v Smt. Kusumben D. Mahadevia Etc

Supreme Court of India

05 December 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 172; (1980) 2 SCC 238; (1980) SCC (Tax) 239; AIR 1980 SC 769; 1980 (14) CTR 366; [1980] 122 ITR 38; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 357; [1980] 3 TAXMAN 16; 1980 UJ 163

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Investment Company, Break Up Method

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Gift Tax Act, 1958, s. 7(1)(3)(t) - Gift Tax Rules, r. 10(2) - Valuation of Shares - Profit Earning Method - Higher Tax Liability - Break UP Method - Wealth Tax officers did not accept figures of valuation claimed by appellants on basis of profit earning method and valued shares at much higher figures by applying break-up method and higher tax liability was made - On appeals to AAC, same was reduced - Respondent/Revenue preferred appeals to Tribunal, also rejected appeals - Hence instant appeal - Whether Tribunal was right in holding that shares of an investment company had to be valued only on basis of adoption of valuation of shares by applying profit earning method and appeal maintainable - Held, dividend method of valuation might be rejected - Fluctuations of profits and uncertainty of conditions at date of valuation, prevent any reasonable estimation of profit earning capacity of company, and hence, valuation by break-up method would be justified - Profit earning method was, therefore, only method which could properly be applied for arriving at valuation of shares of company - Tribunal was right in accepting figures of valuation based on application of profit earning method - Tribunal was wrong in determining value of shares by applying profit earning method, so far as valuation under Gift Act was concerned - HC was therefore, justified in refusing to call for a reference - Appeal disposed of.
