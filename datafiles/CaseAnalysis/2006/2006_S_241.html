Jagjit Singh v State Of Haryana & Ors

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, C.K. Thakker, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 933; (2006) 11 SCC 1; AIR 2007 SC 590; 2007 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 233; 2007 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 295; 2006(13) SCALE 335; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 521

Case Digest

Subject: Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Constitution (Ninety-First Amendment) Act, 2003, Haryana Legislative Assembly (Disqualification of Members on ground of Defection) Rules, 1986

Summary: Petitions challenge legality of orders passed by the Speaker of Haryana Legislative Assembly disqualifying petitioners from being members of the Assembly - Ground common to all the petitions is the violation of principles of natural justice - Held, Speaker was justified in coming to the conclusion that despite various opportunities, no valid proof or evidence was placed on record by the petitioner to show that indeed a split had taken place in the original political party - Finding that the claim of split was made as an afterthought to escape disqualification under paragraph 2(1)(a) of the Tenth Schedule cannot be held to be unreasonable or perverse - Petitions dismissed.
