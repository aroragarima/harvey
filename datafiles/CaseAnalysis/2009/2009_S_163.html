Savitri Agarwal and Others v State of Maharashtra and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 864; (2009) 8 SCC 325; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 683; AIR 2009 SC 3173; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 3084; 2009 CRLJ 4290; 2009 (3) JCC 2348; JT 2009 (9) SC 460; 2009 (4) MLJ(Crl) 201; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 792; 2010 (1) RLW 266; 2009(9) SCALE 514; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 978

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1970

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 438 - Grant of bail to person apprehending arrest - Direction therefor issued by Sessions Judge for appellants/accused who were charged u/ss. 498A r/w 34 and 304-B of IPC, and s. 3 and 4 of Dowry Prohibition Act - Grant of anticipatory bail reversed by High Court - Whether proper? - Held, No - Deliberation entered into on the discretionary power available to Court of Session and High Court u/s. 438 CrPC and scope thereof - Guidelines issued in 'Shri Gurbaksh Singh Sibbia & Ors vs. State of Punjab (1980) 2 SCC 565' by the Constitution Bench of the Court, quoted - HC found to have committed serious error in reversing order of Sessions Judge - Facts and circumstances of the case was duly considered by the Sessions Judge, viz. two dying declarations of the deceased wherein death was stated to be accidental; and the statements of the members of Women Cell who had dealt with the case when on a certain date the deceased had left the house with the intention to commit suicide - Judicial exercise found to be exercised with no perversity or error so as to justify interference by the HC - Merely because the HC had different view on same set of material which had been taken into consideration by the Sessions Judge, it was not a valid ground to label the order passed by Sessions Judge as perverse - Court further found HC to have overlooked the distinction of factors relevant for rejecting bail in a non-bailable case in the first instance and the cancellation of bail already granted - 'rejection of bail in a non-bailable case at the initial stage and the cancellation of bail had to be considered or dealt with on different basis', 'Dolat Ram vs. State of Haryana, (1995) 1 SCC 349' - Nothing was brought to the notice of the Court from which it could be inferred that the appellants had not co-operated in the investigations or had, in any manner, abused the concession of bail granted to them - Impugned order set aside - Appeals disposed of.
