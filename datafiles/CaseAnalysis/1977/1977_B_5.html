Kanta Goel v B.P. Pathak and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 April 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Sarkaria, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 46; (1977) 2 SCC 814; AIR 1977 SC 1599; 2005 (118) DLT 176; 1979 (1) RCR(Rent) 485; 1977 (2) RentLR 1; [1977] 3 S.C.R. 412; 1977 UJ 283

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 2(e),2(i),14 A,14 A(1) Provison 25 B - Landlord & tenant - (A) Interpretation of expressions 'In his name' and 'let out by him' - (B) If only one co-heir of deceased can sue for eviction, when others have no objection - (C) Right of Government servant landlord to get back premises, it can be used against all tenants - Held, implications of, explained - Entitled to institute proceedings for eviction against tenant qua landlord - Can be used only against one and not all tenants - Appeal allowed.
