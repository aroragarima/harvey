Union of India v Prafulla Kumar Samal and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 November 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 252; (1979) 3 SCC 4; (1979) SCC (Cr) 609; AIR 1979 SC 366; 1979 CRLJ 154; 1978 CrLR(SC) 666; 1979 MLJ 361; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 229

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1974, s. 227 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - (A) Test and duty of Court in determination of question regarding discharge - (B) Concurrent findings of two courts below - Interference - (A) Held, no universal rule however if two views are equally possible and if Judge is satisfied that evidence giving rise to some suspicion is not grave suspicion against accused, he will be fully within his right to discharge accused - Judge has to consider broad probabilities, total effect, basic infirmities and so on - (B) Supreme Court held, would be most reluctant to interfere in absence of any special circumstances - Appeal dismissed.
