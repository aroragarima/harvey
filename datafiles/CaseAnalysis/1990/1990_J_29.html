State of Madhya Pradesh v Jhaddu and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 September 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 611; (1991) Supp1 SCC 545; (1991) SCC (Cr) 716; 1990 CRLR 689; JT 1990 (4) SC 57; 1990(2) SCALE 515

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.148, 149 and 302 - Conflict between two factions - Respondents accused of murder - Convicted by Trial Court u/s. 148 and s. 302 r/w s. 149 IPC - On appeal, High Court held accused liable u/s. 304 Part II r/w s. 149 IPC as assailants did not have intention to kill but by use of lathis on a vital part they could be imputed with knowledge that death was likely result - Appeal by State - Held, having regard to nature of injuries death was not caused intentionally, respondents are rightly convicted u/s. 304 Part II r/w s. 149 IPC - Appeals dismissed.
