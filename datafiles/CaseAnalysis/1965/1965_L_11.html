Damji Valji Shah And Another v Life Insurance Corporation Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

08 April 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 170; AIR 1966 SC 135; [1965] 35 Comp Cas 755; 1965 (2) CompLJ 90; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 665

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Insurance

Keywords: Actuary, Insurance Business, Life Insurance Business, Revenue Account, Liabilities, Life Insurance Fund, Controlled Business

Summary: Companies Act, 1956, 1956, s. 446 - Life Insurance Corporation Act ,1956, ss. 15 and 41 - Company - Insurance and other business - Winding up order - Scope of s.41 of LIC Act -Being special provisions prevail over s. 446 of Companies Act - And s. 44 (a) of LIC Act cannot be taken advantage of - Affect on winding up proceedings and jurisdiction of Tribunal - (B) Amount transferred from General Deptt. to Life Insurance Deptt. - Whether LIC can recover the amount from the company u/s. 15 of the LIC act - Held, in no way affect its jurisdiction to continue the proceedings - Held, amount was not transferred as a loan so retransfer not according to law and LIC could recover the amount - Appeal Dismissed.
