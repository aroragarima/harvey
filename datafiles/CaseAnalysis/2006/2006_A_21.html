Indian Bank v ABS Marine Products Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

18 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 150; (2006) 5 SCC 72; AIR 2006 SC 1899; 2006 (63) ALR 822; 2006 (3) AWC 2276; 2007 (1) BC 112; 2007 (1) CalLT(SC) 1; 2006 (2) DRTC 1; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 295; JT 2006 (5) SC 281; 2006 (4) MLJ(SC) 870; 2006 (2) RCR(Civil) 624; 2006(4) SCALE 423; 2006 (Supp) RD 145; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 52

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Legally Recoverable

Summary: [A.] Banking & Finance - Recovery Of Debts Due To Banks And Financial Institutions Act, 1993, ss. 18, 19, 19(6), 31 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 142, 226, 227 - CPC, 1908, s. 9 - Non-disbursal of loans - Alleged non-payment of amounts advanced to borrower - Whether subject-matter of borrower's suit before HC and Bank's application before Tribunal were inextricably connected and whether provisions of Debts Recovery Act mandate transfer of an independent suit filed by a borrower against a Bank before a civil court to Tribunal, in event of Bank filing a recovery application against borrower before Tribunal, to be tried as a counter-claim in Bank's application? - Held, claim of Bank was for an ascertained sum due from borrower, claim of borrower was for damages - It cannot be said that borrower's suit and Bank's application were inextricably connected - Suit filed by first respondent against Bank in HC for recovery of damages, being an independent suit, and not a counter-claim made in application filed by bank, Bank's application for transfer of said suit to Tribunal was misconceived and not maintainable - Observation in Abhijit Tea Co Pvt Ltd(2000 Indlaw SC 334) that suit filed by borrower against Bank has to be transferred to Tribunal for being tried as a counter- Claim in applications of Bank, is to be construed as an observation in exercise of power u/art.142 in order to do complete justice between parties - Decision in Abhijit is distinguishable both on facts and law - Appeals dismissed.
