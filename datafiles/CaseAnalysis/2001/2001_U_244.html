Subrata Sen and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20563; (2001) 8 SCC 71; (2001) SCC (L&S) 1237; AIR 2001 SC 3634; 2002 (1) CLR 370; 2001 (91) FLR 761; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 199; JT 2001 (8) SC 100; 2001 (34) LabIC 3715; 2001(6) SCALE 382; 2001 (4) SCJ 51; 2002 (1) SLJ 110; 2001 (6) SLT 615; [2001] Supp3 S.C.R. 140; 2001 (7) Supreme 140

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Burmah Oil Company (Acquisition of Shares of Oil India) Act, s. 11 - Income-tax Rules, 1962 r. 89(2) - Petitioners retired from Indian Oil Corporation - Govt. of India issued notification by which pension formula was revised - Petitioner contended that they are entitled to pension on basis of revised formula - Respondent No. 2 submitted that petitioners were governed by non-contributory pension scheme - Whether retired employees are entitled to benefit of revised pension scheme? - Held, petitioners were not required to contribute any amount for getting pension - It is liability of company to pay as and when it becomes due - There is no new scheme of payment of pension and pension is to be paid according to 40 percent average of last 10 months salary including average dearness allowance - Petition partly allowed and respondents are directed to give pensionary benefits according to new notification - Petition disposed of.
