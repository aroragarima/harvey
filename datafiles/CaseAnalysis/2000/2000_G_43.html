Commissioner of Income Tax v Gem India Manufacturing Company

Supreme Court of India

05 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.P. Bharucha, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 755; (2001) 10 SCC 733; 2002 (172) CTR 615; [2001] 249 ITR 307; [2001] 117 TAXMAN 368

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Finance Act, 1975

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Finance Act, 1975, s. 2(8) - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 80-I - Whether tribunal was right to hold that assessee, engaged in cutting and polishing of diamonds, amounts to manufacturing or production of goods and is entitled to deduction u/s. 80-I of 1961 Act? - Held, no material placed on record before tribunal to hold that raw diamonds were not same thing as polished and cut diamonds and they are different entities in commercial world - Raw and uncut diamond is subjected to process of cutting and polishing which yields polished diamond - Polished diamond is not new article or thing which is result of manufacture or production - Appeal allowed.
