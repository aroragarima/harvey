Kamla Kanayalal Khushalani v State of Maharashtra and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 January 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 429; (1981) SCC (Cr) 287; (1981) 1 SCC 748; AIR 1981 SC 814; [1983] 53 Comp Cas 23; 1981 CRLJ 353; 1981 CrLR(SC) 570; 1981(1) SCALE 253; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 459; 1981 UJ 79

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution ,art.21,22(5) - Petition by the sister of the detenu praying for release of detenu on the premise that the safeguards provided by the constitution not complied with - The documents and materials were not supplied to the detenue along with detention order - There was a delay of 25 days in disposing of representation of detenu without any explanation - (A) Whether the documents and materials relied upon in the order of detention have to be supplied to the detenu without delay? - The documents and materials relied upon in the order of detention form an integral part of the grounds and must be supplied to the detenu pari passu the grounds of detention - Held, If the documents and materials are supplied later, then the detenu is deprived of an opportunity of making an effective representation against the order of detention - Before an order of detention can be supported, the constitutional safeguards must be strictly observed - Petition allowed.
