Â Amarsarjit Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 228; AIR 1962 SC 1305; 1962 (64) PLR 842; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 346; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 346

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 133(1)(a) and 136 - Punjab Resumption of Jagir Act, 1957, ss. 2 (1) and 7 - Whether certain jagirs in the State of Punjab known as Cis- Sutlej jagir are liable to be resumed under the provisions of the Punjab Resumption of Jagirs Act, 1957? - Held, jagir fall within s. 2(1)(a) Punjab Resumption of Jagir Act - Relied on Bhomicharas in Thakur Amar Singji's case 1955 Indlaw SC 58 - Petition dismissed.
