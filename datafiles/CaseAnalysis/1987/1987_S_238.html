Biswanath Ghosh v State Of West Bengal And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

16 February 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28573; (1987) 2 SCC 55; (1987) SCC (Cr) 259; AIR 1987 SC 1155; 1987 (1) CalLT(SC) 13; 1987 CRLJ 1052; 1987 CRLR 408; 1987 CrLR(SC) 408; JT 1987 (1) SC 555; 1987(1) SCALE 435; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 305

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973 - Acquittal without waiting for arrival of records and without perusing evidence of prosecution - Whether High Court was justified in allowing appeal of accused persons against their conviction under ss. 148 and 302 r/w 149 IPC, without having records of Court of Session before it and without perusal of evidence adduced by prosecution - Held, u/s. 385 CrPC, it was obligatory for HC to fix a date for hearing of appeal and then send for records of Court of Session and hear parties on merits - Order of acquittal set aside - Direction to HC to admit appeal to its file and dispose it off afresh.
