Sat Pal Mohindra v Surindra Timber Stores

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, M. Jagannadha Rao, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1282; (1999) 5 SCC 696; 1999 (37) ALR 782; 2000 (1) AWC 397; 1999 (3) CalLT(SC) 74; JT 1999 (9) SC 79; 1999 (9) SLT 449; 1999 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 24; 1999 (9) Supreme 196

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Consumer Protection - Use of sub-standard material - Fabrication of doors and windows - Cases filed with District Commission which came to be dismissed - Petitioner filed case in Civil Court claiming the price of the material supplied by it - State Commission dismissed the consumer complaint and relegated the parties to the Civil Court - Whether State Forum was justified in dismissing the appeal filed by the appellant without recording any finding on the merits of the appeal? - Held, no - Filing of the suit by the respondent during the pendency of the proceedings before the Consumer Forum for a different type of relief could not oust the jurisdiction of the Consumer Forum - Order of the State Forum is completely erroneous - Matter remanded to State Forum for its disposal on merits - Appeal allowed.
