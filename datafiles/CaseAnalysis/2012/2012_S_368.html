Maa Vaishno Devi Mahila Mahavidyalaya v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 556; (2013) 2 SCC 617; 2013 (3) MahLJ 97; 2013 (1) MLJ 864; 2013 (2) MPLJ 472; 2012(12) SCALE 440; [2012] 13 S.C.R. 810

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: National Council For Teacher Education Rule, 1997, Tamil Nadu Private College Regulation Act, 1976, National Council for Teachers Education (Recognition, Norms and Procedure) Regulations, 2005

Summary: Education - Constitution - National Council for Teacher Education Act, 1993 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Non-grant of affiliation - Cut-off date - Instant petitions filed u/art. 32 of the Constitution challenging the order of the university/authorities declining to grant affiliation again in view of the cut-off date fixed by SC - whether the university and the State Government were justified in rejecting the application or not granting application for affiliation on the ground that there was a cut-off date and/or the conditions of recommendation/affiliation had not been satisfied -

Held, grant of recognition or affiliation to an institute was a condition precedent to running of the courses by the Institute - It was the requirement of law that affiliation should be granted by the affiliating body in accordance with the prescribed procedure and upon proper application of mind - Process for grant of recognition, affiliation and thereby sanctioning of commencement of the courses in terms of the Regulations and the orders of SC gave an outer period of approximately 270 days, i.e. 9 months, from 1st September to 10th May of the year immediately preceding the concerned academic year - Thus, for the entire process to be within this framework, it must be completed within the aforestated period - In the instant case, serious disputes were raised with regard to the fulfillment of the conditions of recognition and/or affiliation - There was no fault with the view taken by the authorities concerned - 10th of May had been provided as the cut-off date, after which no affiliation for the current academic year would be granted - This, being the law stated by SC, was binding on all concerned, including any authority - Authorities have rightly acted in declining to entertain and/or refusing affiliation to the institutions being beyond the cut-off date - Adherence to the schedule was the obligation of the authorities and the institutions cannot raise any grievance in that regard - Further, schedule was prescribed in relation to recognition and affiliation - Recognition and affiliation granted as per the schedule to be applicable for the current academic year - No Authority/person/ Council/Committee shall be entitled to vary the schedule for any reason whatsoever - Appeals disposed of.
