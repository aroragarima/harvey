Arjun Singh v Mohindra Kumar and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 362; AIR 1964 SC 993; 1964 (2) ALT(SC) 341; 1964 (2) ILR(All) 590; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 946

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 9, r.7 - Appellant filed three applications in three suits for setting aside ex parte orders passed against him - Plaintiff filed a counter-affidavit in which he disputed truth of statement regarding appellant's illness - These three applications were disposed of by a common judgment of Civil Judge - HC in revision held that it is conceded that no ex parte decree has yet been passed and only order passed is that case shall proceed ex parte against appellant - In view of fact that no decree has yet been passed, setting aside of ex parte order was not absolutely necessary' - Whether findings of the HC is legal? - Held, ground on which the objection is based is that 29-05-58 was date adjourned at instance of defendant-applicant - It appears from record that on 28-05-58 cases were adjourned to 29-05-58 on a joint application of parties to effect that a compromise would be filed and it was not an adjournment sought by defendant alone, moreover, that application was made by him in his own suit No 20 of 1953 and other two suits had also naturally to be adjourned as all three of them were consolidated - Adjournment of those two suits, therefore, cannot be said to be at instance of defendant - Suit 134 of 1956 was decreed not on merits but really ex parte as had been expressly stated by Civil Judge when he passed that decree - Application filed by appellant under O. 9, r.13 for setting aside ex parte decree passed in suit 134 of 1956 is remanded to trial Judge for disposal on merits in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
