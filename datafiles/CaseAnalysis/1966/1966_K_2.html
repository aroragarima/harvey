Â K. P. Chowdhary v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.M. Sikri, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 117; AIR 1967 SC 203; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 919

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Forest Contract Rules

Summary: (A) Contract & Commercial - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 299(1) - Madhya Pradesh Land Revenue Code, 1959, s. 155(b) - Auction - Two contracts - Recover of arrears - Legality - A notice was issued by Divisional Forest Officer, for auction of various contracts in that division - Appellant bid for two contracts and his were highest bids - Appellant raised a dispute as to marking of trees according to material notified at time of auction - Thereafter contracts were re-auctioned however, at the time of re-auction there was a deficiency in two contracts together of Rs. 51,5001- - Appellant was asked to send that amount to Divisional Forest Officer - When he failed to do so, a letter was addressed to Tehsildar by Divisional Forest Officer for recovering amount as arrears of land revenue under conditions of auction - Appellant filed a writ petition challenging impugned letter claiming that amount could not be recovered as arrears of land revenue as contract was not signed or completed by him - HC dismissed writ petition holding that amount could be recovered as arrears of land revenue under the Code - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC's view that art. 299 of Constitution did not hit an implied contract and therefore amount could be recovered u/s. 155(b) of the Code was correct - Held, view taken by HC that s. 155 (b) of the Code which provides for recovery of money as arrears of land revenue would therefore enure in favour of Govt. and enable it to recover deficiency could not be sustained - That clause provides for recovery of all moneys falling due to State Govt. under any grant, lease or contract and says that they should be recoverable in same manner as arrears of land revenue - HC was of view that word contract in that clause includes an implied contract - But if there could be no implied contract between Govt. and another person in view of mandatory provision of art. 299(1) of Constitution there should be no question of recovery of any money under an implied contract u/s. 155(b) of the Code - Hence, view taken by HC that that amount could be recovered u/s. 155(b) of the Code was not correct - Appeal allowed.

(B) Contract & Commercial - Constitution of India, 1950 - Madhya Pradesh Land Revenue Code, 1959 - Arrears - Recovery - Investigation - Validity - Whether amount could be recovered as arrears of land revenue under any other provision of law - Held, that question require investigation before petition could be finally disposed of - That question had still to be investigated and it would be for State to show whether amount could be recovered under any provision of law or rules relating to forest contracts - So matter would have to be remanded for further investigation on those lines - Matter aws remanded back to HC for further investigation - Appeal allowed.
