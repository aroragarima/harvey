Hari Singh Nagra and others v Kapil Sibal and others

Supreme Court of India

15 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 600; (2010) 7 SCC 502; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 55; 2011 CRLJ 102; JT 2010 (8) SC 56; 2010 (3) MLJ(Crl) 728; 2010 (3) RCR(Criminal) 948; 2010(7) SCALE 375; [2010] 8 S.C.R. 879

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Legal Education, Practicing

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, s. 2(c) - Contempt of SC - Sustainability - Petitioner/advocates filed a contempt petition before the HC alleging 1st respondent/advocate wrote a message in SC advocates' souvenir denigrating the institution of judiciary - HC directed to implead 4th and 5th respondent/advocates to the contempt petition as they were editors of the souvenir - 4th and 5th respondents were impleaded, however, they filed a Transfer Petition before the SC to transfer the case to SC - Transfer Petition allowed and SC Registry placed the matter before the SC - Hence the present petition - Whether sufficient case is made out by the petitioners to initiate contempt proceedings against the respondents - Held, sending and/or publication of the message in the SC advocates' souvenir did not scandalize or tend to scandalize, or lower or tend to lower the authority of any court nor prejudiced, or interfered or tended to interfere with the due course of any judicial proceedings; or interfered or tended to interfere with or obstructed or tended to obstruct, the administration of justice in any other manner, within the meaning of 'criminal contempt' as defined in s. 2(c) of the Act - Message sent by 1st respondent to be published in the SC advocates' souvenir would have to be regarded as fair criticism - Impugned article nowhere targets a particular judge or a case of an attack on a Judge which is scurrilous, offensive, intimidatory or malicious beyond condonable limits, in respect of a judgment or his conduct - Impugned article was an expression of opinion about an institutional pattern and it does not affect the administration of justice - Further, nothing on the record to show that the SC advocates' souvenir in which the message was printed was sold to the public - Therefore, no case was made out against 4th and 5th respondents who were subsequently impleaded in the contempt petition - Contempt proceedings were dropped - Notice discharged - Petition dismissed.
