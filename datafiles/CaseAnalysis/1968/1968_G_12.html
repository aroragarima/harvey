Godhra Borough Municipality v Godhra Electricity Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

20 March 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 142; AIR 1968 SC 1504; [1969] 39 Comp Cas 395; 1968 (9) G.L.R. 914; [1969] 73 ITR 44; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 481

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Land & Property - Bombay Municipal Boroughs Act, 1925, s. 73 - A/ys1956-58- Capital value - Assessment - Legality - Assessments were made by appellant/municipality on respondent/assessee u/s. 73 of the Act - For A/ys appellant fixed valuation of properties belonging to respondent at Rs. 3,25,000 - On appeal by respondent, Judicial Magistrate fixed valuation of properties at Rs. 90,000 - Appellant filed revision before Sessions Judge, who fixed valuation at Rs. 1,25,000 - On appeal, HC reduced valuation to Rs. 90,000 - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned judgment and order of HC was justified - Held, SC was unable to accept views expressed or reasoning given in judgment of HC, s. 73 of the Act empowered municipality to impose rate on buildings or lands - Word 'rate' had not been defined in the Act but it had well known meaning - 'Rate was used with respect to tax which was levied on net annual value or ratable value of lands and buildings and not on their capital value, it would therefore not be wrong to say that in legislative history and practice in England up to 1925, rate for purpose of local taxation meant tax on annual value of lands and buildings liable to such taxation', Patel Gordhandas Hargovindas v. Municipal Commissioner, Ahmedabad 1963 Indlaw SC 123, relied on - It would therefore be noticed that figures given in balance sheet were merely statements in terms of form given in Schedule VI, they had no relevance in determining capital value of property for purpose of assessment to rate - Therefore, true method of determination of capital value was not adopted in Courts below, SC set aside judgment and order of HC and remanded matter back to District Judge for him to determine capital value in light of observations made after giving opportunity to parties to adduce evidence on subject - Appeal allowed.
