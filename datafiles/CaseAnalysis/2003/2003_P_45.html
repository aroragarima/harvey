State of West Bengal and Others v Pantha Chatterjee and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 505; (2003) 6 SCC 469; (2003) SCC (L&S) 894; AIR 2003 SC 3569; 2003 (4) ESC 441; 2003 (98) FLR 625; JT 2003 (5) SC 448; 2003 LabIC 2613; 2003 (3) LLN 842; 2003(5) SCALE 173; 2004 (1) SLJ 135; 2003 (4) SLR 722; 2003 (4) SLT 326; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 427; 2003 (4) Supreme 441; 2003 (2) UPLBEC 1835

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Master And Servant Relationship

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 311 - West Bengal Home Guards Act, 1962 - Part time Border Wing Home Guards - Pitiable conditions of service and nominal emoluments - Discrimination vis-Ã -vis other regular Border Wing Home Guards of West Bengal and Border Security Force Personnel, although performing similar duties and discharging same responsibilities - Whether burden of expenditure is on Central Govt. or State Govt. - Held, after working for such a long period, it is too much to say that their deployment was of a casual and voluntary nature and Central Govt. will not be concerned with them and that it would be responsibility of State Govt. alone - Further held, it is no doubt true that State of West Bengal being in position of an employer of respondent-petitioners, owes primary responsibility of making all payments on account of salary, allowances and other perquisites to them as admissible to permanent staff of Border Wing Home Guards, but this burden of expenditure must be ultimately borne by Central Govt.
