N.K. Wahi v Shekhar Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 677; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 203; (2007) 9 SCC 481; AIR 2007 SC 1454; 2007 (2) BC 438; 2007 (3) Bom.C.R. 88; [2007] 137 Comp Cas 939; 2007 (2) CompLJ 10; 2007 (138) DLT 783; 2007 (1) JCC (NI) 112; 2007 (3) JhrCR(SC) 55; 2007 (2) KLT 1021; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 379; 2007 (2) RCR(Criminal) 266; 2007(4) SCALE 188; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 883

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: New Offence

Summary: Banking & Finance - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138, 141(1) and 141(2) - Companies Act, 1956 - Criminal complaint u/s. 138 r/w s. 141 of Act - Respondents filed application for dropping proceedings stating that they were not Directors of company and further there was no allegation against them in terms of s. 141 of Act - Application dismissed - High Court quashed proceedings so far as respondents are concerned - Hence, present appeal - (A) Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 141(1) and 141(2) - Held, it is necessary to specifically aver in a complaint u/s. 141 that at time offence was committed, person accused was incharge of, and responsible for conduct of business of company - This averment is an essential requirement of s. 141 and has to be made in a complaint - Without this averment being made in a complaint, requirements of s. 141 cannot be said to be satisfied - (B) Liability of directors of company - Held, merely being a director of a company is not sufficient to make person liable u/s. 141 of Act - Director in a company cannot be deemed to be in charge of and responsible to company for conduct of its business - Requirement of s. 141 is that person sought to be made liable should be in charge of and responsible for conduct of business of company at relevant time - This has to be averted as a fact as there is no deemed liability of a director in such cases - Appeal dismissed.
