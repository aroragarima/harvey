Deny Bora v State of Assam

Supreme Court of India

27 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 673; (2014) 14 SCC 42; 2015 CRLJ 68; 2014 (4) JCC 2678; JT 2014 (11) SC 295; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3456; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 172; 2014(10) SCALE 208; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 1111

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Murder, Convcition

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 19, 3, 4 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant accused along with his associate allegedly committed murder of deceased(doctor) - Case was registered u/s. 302, 34 IPC and u/ss. 3, 4 of the Act - Designated Court acquitted accused under TADA, however convicted and sentenced accused u/s. 302 IPC - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order recorded by Court was sustainable.

Held, Trial Judge placed reliance on the evidence of PW-14 who has come forward for recording his statement almost after 2 years and 8 months. The only explanation he has given is that he was threatened by the co-accused. It is interesting to note after his statement was recorded, the accused was arrested after 6 years and nothing happened to him during the said period. Thus the plea of threat to keep him silent for almost 2 years and 8 months does not inspire confidence. Apart from that, as his testimony would show accused had enquired about the deceased and he had accompanied them to the house of the deceased on one day, when the deceased Doctor was absent. His acquaintance with the accused-appellant was hardly fortnight old, but he along with accused and another had gone to the clinic of the deceased where the other person pretending as patient went inside. It is in his evidence that accused had fired at the deceased as a result of which he fell down and died. That the said witness could keep such an incident without disclosing to anyone, defies prudence and baffles commonsense. His plea of being threatened for such long period to have the sustained silence, is unacceptable and there is no hesitation in holding that his testimony is thoroughly and wholly unreliable. Therefore, considered view by Court was that the conviction recorded by Designated Court on his testimony alone without any corroboration is totally unsustainable. Hence, judgment of conviction is set aside - Appeal allowed.
