Nectar Beverages Pvt. Ltd. v Deputy Commissioner Of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 841; (2009) 15 SCC 374; [2009] 314 ITR 314; JT 2009 (13) SC 471; 2009(9) SCALE 200; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 1174

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Profits Chargeable To Tax, Balancing Charge, Short Term Capital Gains

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 32(1), 41(1), 41(2) - Finance Act, 1995 - Assessment years 1990-91 to 1998-99 - Depreciation - Balancing charges - Assessee earmarked sale proceeds from bottles and crates as 'miscellaneous income' whereas, in case of other assessees, said sale proceeds have been earmarked specifically under Heading 'Profits from sale of assets' - W.e.f. assessment year 1988-89, S. 41(2), which inter alia dealt with profit on sale of depreciable asset (balancing charge), stood deleted - Department sought to tax Rs. 50, 850/- holding that sale proceeds of 100% depreciated and written off assets can still be treated as business income of assessee u/s. 41(1) of 1961 Act - Assessee contended that bottles and crates bought before 1.4.1995 were sold in previous year relevant to assessment year in question, however, on account of deletion of s. 41(2) profits on sale of such bottles and crates were not taxable under that sub-section - Whether concept of 'balancing charge' in s. 41(2) could be read into s. 41(1) of Act, 1961? - Held, bottles and crates purchased prior to 31.3.1995 did not form part of block of assets, hence, profits on sale of such assets were not taxable as a balancing charge, neither u/s. 41(1) nor u/s. 50 - Bottles and crates purchased after 1.4.1995, on account of deletion of proviso to s. 31(1)(ii) (vide Finance Act, 1995) such bottles and crates formed part of block of assets and consequently such assets purchased after 1.4.1995, in this case, became exigible to capital gains tax u/s. 50 - Matter remanded to A.O. to go through computation submitted by assessee and find out whether earmarking 'profits from sale of assets' as 'miscellaneous income' has resulted in understatement of net profits at pre-Section 28 stage - Appeal allowed
