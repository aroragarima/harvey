State of Rajasthan and another v Bhagwan Das Agrawal and others

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 844; (2013) 16 SCC 574; 2014 CRLJ 1006; 2014 (1) JCC 372; JT 2014 (1) SC 145; 2014 (1) MLJ(Crl) 236; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 513; 2014 (1) RLW 182; 2013(15) SCALE 343

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Explosives Rules, 1983

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420, 467, 468, 471, 120-B, 201 and 34 - Explosives Act, 1884, ss. 5, 9B, 9C - Explosive Substances Act, 1908 - Unauthorised and illegal supply of explosives - FIR - Quashing of - Sustainability - FIR was registered in respect of alleged unauthorized and illegal supply of explosives by respondent No. 1 - Charge sheet against respondent No. 1 was filed u/ss. 420, 467, 468, 471, 120-B, 201 and 34 IPC and ss. 9B, 9C of the 1884 Act and ss. 4 and 6 of the 1908 Act - Respondent No. 1 filed petition u/s. 482 CrPC and sought relief to hold that the proceedings based on the subsequent FIR registered u/ss. 5, 9B, 9C of the 1884 Act - HC allowed the petition - Hence instant appeals - Whether proceedings should continue at Dholpur or at the two places Sagar and Chanderi - Held, nature and manner of offences committed by accused persons were not identical but were different, for example, in respect of FIR Crime No.130 of 2010 the accused persons in connivance with respondent No.1 delivered 103 trucks of explosives to the Magazines of M/s. A Explosives and 60 trucks of explosives to M/s. B.M. Traders - It was alleged that the Magazines of M/s. G Explosives and M/s. S Explosives were not operational since many years and with the forged documentation in the name of the said firms the explosives were purchased by M/s. A Explosives and M/s. B.M. Traders and subsequently those explosives were sold to some unknown persons - In respect of those FIRs, one accused was arrested and from whose custody 498 non electronic detonators were recovered - In respect of another FIR, during investigation, it has come on the record that those explosives were sold for terrorist activities - Offence means any act or omission made punishable by law - Fountain head of all the three cases might be at Dholpur from where truck loaded with explosives moved to different destinations but from that it could not be said that the acts and omissions which constitute the offence were the same - Same offence, in SC's opinion, would mean that acts and omissions which constitute the offence were one and the same - Except the allegation that the explosives were loaded at Dholpur, the mode and manner in which the offence was committed at different places were not the same - Impugned order passed by the HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
