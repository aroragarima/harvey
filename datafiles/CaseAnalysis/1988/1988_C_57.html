Administrator General of West Bengal v Collector, Varanasi

Supreme Court of India

16 February 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 928; (1988) 2 SCC 150; AIR 1988 SC 943; JT 1988 (1) SC 529; 1988(1) SCALE 484; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 1025; 1988 (1) UJ 642

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 23(b) - Valuation of the property - Market value - Determination of - (A) Whether estimate of market-value of acquired land at Rs. 200 per decimal is unreasonably low and is arrived at ignoring evidence on record and settled principles of valuation? - Held, valuation cases must be dealt with just as much from point of view of hypothetical purchase as of claimant - Valuation itself must often be more or less a matter of guesswork - But it is obviously wrong to fix upon a valuation which, judged by everyday principles, no purchaser would be likely to give - In present case, Rs. 200 per decimal for large extent of acquired land works out to 40% of 'retail' price even if we take, Rs. 500 as 'retail' price - That apart, in case of land with potentialities for a more profitable use, it is necessary to acknowledge, and make due allowance for, possibility that land might not be applied for prospective use at all or not so applied within a reasonable time - There is, therefore, no justification to interfere with determination of market-value of land approved by High Court - (B) Compensation for building and structures - Enhancement of - Whether valuation of buildings and structures at a mere Rs. 57,660 calls for an upward revision? - Held, an estimate of 50% of cost of building may, again on a rough and ready basis, be deducted towards depreciation - Market-value of building as on date of preliminary notification could accordingly be fixed at Rs. 2,00,000 - Further held, compensation for buildings and structures is enhanced from Rs. 57,660 to Rs. 2,00,000, - (C) Compensation for tree-growth - Enhancement of - Whether award made for tree-growth is inadequate and is required to be valued higher? - Held, evidence shows that there were 471 fruit-bearing trees and plants: 13 timber trees and 12 Bamboo clusters - Though there is some evidence as to value of yield, this may not be a relevant factor having regard to principles of valuation appropriate to case - There is no evidence about timber value and fuel value of trees - Further held, having regard to large number of trees and to fact that some of them were timber trees, award lump sum of Rs. 7,500 should be under this head - Accordingly, compensation for tree-growth is enhanced from Rs. 355.85 to Rs. 7,500 - (D) Higher rate of solatium and interest - Entitlement of - Whether appellant is entitled to benefit of s. 23(2) of Act as introduced by Uttar Pradesh Law Acquisition (Amendment) Act 1972 on the ground that proceedings were pending in appeal before the court on date the amendments came into force? - Held, reserved liberty to appellant to contest the case after the final pronouncement in the case of Bhag Singh &Ors. v. Union Territory of Chandigarh, (1985 Indlaw SC 246) - Appeal allowed.
