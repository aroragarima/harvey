Prithvi (Minor) v Mam Raj and others

Supreme Court of India

19 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 156; (2004) 13 SCC 279; (2005) SCC (Cr) 198; AIR 2004 SC 2729; JT 2004 (2) SC 582; 2004(2) SCALE 580; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 530; 2004 (1) UC 662

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Human Rights; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 307, 460 - Appeal against acquittal under art.136 - Normal rule is that this court does not interfere under art.136 of Constitution in an appeal against acquittal unless judgment of HC is perverse - Judgment appealed falls into this category and requires interference at hands of this Court - Had there been a reasonable approach to appreciation of evidence, there would have been no reason to interfere with findings recorded by learned Addl Sessions Judge - Judgment of HC set aside and convictions rendered by District and Sessons Judge affirmed -nonetheless circumstances of case do not suggest that it is one of 'rarest of rare cases' where death sentence has to be awarded - Appeal allowed.
