Safi Mohd. v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 247; (2013) 8 SCC 601; AIR 2013 SC 2519; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 2648; JT 2013 (6) SC 550; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 902; 2013 (2) RLW 1623; 2013(6) SCALE 191; [2013] 6 S.C.R. 339

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Air Force, Supply of classified information to foreign country

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Official Secrets Act, 1923, ss. 3(1)(c), 3 and 9 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120-B - Supply of classified information to foreign country - Conviction - Sustainability - Superintendent of Police CID, lodged an FIR for the offences punishable u/ss. 3 and 9 of the Act r/w s. 120-B IPC with the Special Police Station against accused who was found roaming in suspicious circumstances in the Air Force Area and was arrested on 7-3-1990 - Appellant used to supply secret information to the Pakistani Intelligence and handed over Rs.6500/- to him for working for Pak Intelligence Agency - Later on, on further disclosure other accused were arrested - Charge sheet was filed against appellant u/ss. 3 r/w s. 9 and 5 of the Act - Trial Court convicted appellant u/s. 3(1)(c) of the Act - Aggrieved on appeal HC dismissed the appeal - Hence instant appeal - Whether SC was required to interfere with the impugned judgment of HC - Held, after referring to the evidence of PW22 and 24, search of the house of appellant and seizure of certain documents along with diary particularly - Handwritten map prepared with certain markings, it proved the prosecution case - No doubt independent witnesses turned hostile, but Trial Court had rightly accepted the testimony of police witnesses after proper appreciation of their evidence and he had rightly placed reliance upon police witnesses to prove the seizure of documents from house of appellant and therefore the same could not be held to be bad in law as contended by appellant - Finding recorded by both the Courts below regarding search and seizure of documents which affect the integrity and security of the country was concurrent finding of fact rightly recorded by the HC after proper appreciation and appraisal of evidence on record - Same could not be interfered with by SC in exercise of its jurisdiction - Even if the search was made by the IO in illegal manner, the same did not affect the legality of search and investigation made by IO with regard to seizure of documents from the house of appellant in view of the law laid down by SC - From the evidence produced by prosecution in instant case, it was clear that documents of strategic importance to the Nation were recovered from the possession of appellant and other accused and they have failed to give satisfactory explanation about the documents being in their possession - SC was of the view that instant case was not a fit case for interference with the impugned judgment - Having regard to the nature of charges made against appellant u/ss. 3, 9 and 5 of the Act as he was found to be guilty along with other accused persons and rightly convicted and sentenced them - Appeal dismissed.
