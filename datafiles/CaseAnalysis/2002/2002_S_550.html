Orissa Textile and Steel Limited and another v State of Orissa and others

Supreme Court of India

17 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, S.S.M. Quadri, N. Santosh Hegde, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Challenged, Constitutionality of provision

Summary: Constitution - Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s.25- O - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 6-W - Constitutionality of provision - Challenged - Section 25- O of Industrial Disputes Act was incorporated by the Amendment Act - Some HC held that amended s.25-O of Industrial Disputes Act arid or s. 6-W of U.P. Industrial Disputes Act still suffered from substantial vice and was unconstitutional - Some other HC upheld validity of amended s.25-O of Industrial Disputes Act and/or s.6-W U.P. Industrial Disputes Act - Instant appeals were filed challenging Constitutional validity of s. 25- O of 1947 Act.

Held, a proper reading of sub-s. (5) of amended s. 25-O of Industrial Disputes Act shows that, in the context in which it is used, the word 'may' necessarily means 'shall'. Thus the appropriate Govt. 'shall' review the Order if an application in that behalf is made by the employer or the workmen. An opportunity of being heard would have to be given to the employer, workmen and all interested persons. This review and/or reference under amended S.25-O would be in addition to a judicial review u/art. 226 or art. 32 of the Constitution. Amended s.25-O of Industrial Disputes Act is very different from s.25-O (as it then stood). Amended s.25-O of Industrial Disputes Act recognizes that if there are exceptional circumstances then there could be no compulsion to continue to run the business. The employer must show that it has become impossible to continue to run the establishment. Looked at from this point of view, the restrictions imposed are reasonable and in the interest of general public. Amended s.25-O of Industrial Disputes Act is the law which lays down the restriction. There is nothing vague or ambiguous in its provision. It is s.25-O of Industrial Disputes Act which gives the power to grant or refuse permission. Each case would have to be decided on its own facts and on the basis of circumstances prevailing at the relevant time. Amended s.25-O of Industrial Disputes Act is not ultra-vires the Constitution. It is saved by art. 19(6) of the Constitution - Appeals and Writ Petitions sent back to DB for decision in accordance with law - Order accordingly.

Ratio - In interest of general public it is possible to restrict, for limited period of time, right to close down business.
