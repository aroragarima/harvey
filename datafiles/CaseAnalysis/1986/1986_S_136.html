Ishwar Singh Bagga And Ors. Etc. v State Of Rajasthan Etc.

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 225; (1987) 1 SCC 101; AIR 1987 SC 628; 1987 CRLR 38; 1987 CrLR(SC) 38; JT 1986 SC 892; 1986(2) SCALE 881; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 300; 1987 (1) UJ 66

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers and Transportation - Constitution of India, 1950 - Transport Corporation Act, 1950 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - (A) Determination of legality of notification by State Govt. authorising officers of SRTC to discharge powers u/s.129 A - When the expression 'other persons' in s. 129 or s.129A does not include officers of SRTC - Contention that appointment of the officers of the Corporation who are their rivals in motor transport business as officers entitled to exercise powers conferred under s.129A of the Act was violative of art.19(1)(g) of the Constitution - (B) CrPC, 1973, ss. 43, 94 and 357 - Fine levied on persons accused of running motor vehicles unauthorisedly on the notified routes - Suit for recovery of such amount by SRTC as compensation for damages - Ordered to be paid over to the Corporation - Legality of - Held, not valid and expression 'other person' has to be read ejusdem generis with the words 'any police officer' - Magistrate is not competent - Appeal allowed.
