Vijayan v Sadanandan K. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 595; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 296; (2009) 6 SCC 652; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1435; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 3143; 2009 (3) BC 282; 2009 (3) CGLJ 412; 2009 CRLJ 2957; 2009 (3) CTC 786; 2009 (3) JCC 1774; JT 2009 (7) SC 152; 2009 (2) KLT 618; 2009 (3) MLJ(Crl) 1001; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 969; 2009(7) SCALE 19; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 463; 2009 (2) UC 1153

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal

Keywords: Power Of The Court, Recovery Of Fine

Summary: Criminal - Banking & Finance - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Whether a default sentence can be imposed when compensation is awarded under s. 357(3) of Cr.P.C? - Held, any money other than a fine payable on account of an order passed under the Code shall be recoverable as if it were a fine - While imposing a sentence of fine, the Court would be competent to include a default sentence to ensure payment of the same - While awarding compensation under s. 357(3) Cr.P.C., the Court is within its jurisdiction to add a default sentence of imprisonment - Petition dismissed.
