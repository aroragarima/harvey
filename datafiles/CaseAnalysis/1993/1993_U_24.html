Ajay Agarwal v Union Of India And Ors

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1142; (1993) 3 SCC 609; (1993) SCC (Cr) 961; AIR 1993 SC 1637; AIR 1993 SCW 1866; 1993 (2) ALT 396; 1993 (3) CCR 254; [1994] 79 Comp Cas 1; 1993 (2) Crimes 342; 1993 CRLJ 2516; 1993 CRLR 445; 1993 CrLR(SC) 445; JT 1993 (3) SC 203; 1993(2) SCALE 757; [1993] 3 S.C.R. 543; 1993 (2) UJ 461

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Criminal

Keywords: Continuous

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120 B, 420, 468 and 471 - Criminal Procedure Code, s. 188 - Whether offence u/s. 120-B r/w s. 420 and s. 471 of IPC were committed outside country? - Any isolated act or omission committed at Dubai was insufficient to constitute an offence - Illegal act of dishonestly inducing bank at Chandigarh was committed not by preparation of bill at Dubai but its presentation in pursuance of agreement to cheat - Submission thus founded was on residence or on preparation of bills of lading or encashment at Dubai are of no consequence - Nor is there any merit in submission that even part of offence would attract s. 188 as section operates when offence is committed outside India - Held, therefore provisions of s. 188 Criminal Procedure Code were not attracted - Appeal disposed of.
