Sujata Uday Patil v Uday Madhukar Patil

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1242; (2006) 13 SCC 272; 2007 (2) ALD(SC) 45; 2007 (66) ALR 787; 2007 (3) ALT 43; 2007 (2) AWC 1421; 2007 (1) Bom.C.R. 858; 2007 (1) CTC 266; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 229; 2007 (147) PLR 521; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 404; 2006(13) SCALE 412; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 955

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Keywords: Grounds For Divorce, In Lieu Of Maintenance, Continuous, Matrimonial Offence, Marriage Laws (Amendment) Act, 2003

Summary: Criminal - Family & Personal - Divorce - Cruelty - Maintenance - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 125 - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 13 - Respondent and appellant were husband and wife and they had a child out of wedlock - Respondent filed a divorce petition against Appellant wife on ground of cruelty and desertion for two years - Decree for divorce was granted - Appeals filed against decree of divorce by wife were dismissed by Trial Court and High Court - District Court directed respondent to pay alimony for appellant-wife as well as son - Whether relationship of marriage between appellant and respondent is liable to be terminated? - Held, yes - Further held, cruelty may be inferred from facts and matrimonial relations of parties and interaction in their daily life disclosed by evidence - From material on record, cruelty on part of appellant established - Respondent has to maintain appellant and son - Decree for divorce upheld - Directions issued - Appeal dismissed.
