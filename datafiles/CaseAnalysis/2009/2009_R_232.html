Ranvir Yadav v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 579; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 92; (2009) 6 SCC 595; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1439; 2009 CRLJ 2962; 2009 (3) JCC 1853; JT 2009 (15) SC 414; 2009(7) SCALE 60; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 653

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Fire-Arm Injuries

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, s. 302 - Cr.P.C, 1973, s. 313 - Appeal against judgment of High Court directing reversal of judgment of acquittal recorded by Trial Court - Whether all the relevant questions were put during examination under s. 313? - Held, a conviction based on the accused's failure to explain what he was never asked to explain is bad in law - No incriminating materials were put to the accused under s. 313 - No accusation specifically put in question during examination - Appeal partly allowed.
