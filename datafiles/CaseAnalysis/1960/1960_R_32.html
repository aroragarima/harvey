Ramnarain Sons Private Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

05 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 358; AIR 1961 SC 1141; [1961] 41 ITR 534; 1961 (2) MLJ(SC) 29; 1961 (2) SCJ 155; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 904

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Tax - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 , s.66(1) - Purchase price - Capital asset - Market price - Acquisition of shares - Appellants/private limited company registered under Indian Companies Act, 1913 and carried on business as brokers, managing agents and dealers in shares and securities - X were managing agents of A public limited company and 2,507 out of a total issue of 3,200 shares - Appellants purchased shares from X 1,507 of A Mills at rate of Rs.2,321-8-0 per share and having obtained a voting right and acquired managing agency rights of Mills - Remaining one thousand shares were acquired from X by Directors of appellants at rate of Rs. 1,500 - Appellants sold 400 out of shares purchased by them and suffered a loss of Rs. 1,78,438 and loss suffered by appellants in accounting year by sale of shares including 400 shares of A Mills was Rs. 1,92,834 and crediting Rs. 1,05,907 earned as profit in certain other share transactions net loss suffered in share transactions in year of account amounted to Rs. 86,927 - Appellants claimed loss on sales in trade in shares and loss on valuation of stock-in- trade and Income Tax Officer disallowed loss suffered by appellants in sale of A Mills shares - Tribunal allowed loss on sale of 400 shares of A Mills and not allowed loss arising out of valuation of A Mills shares at end of year of account - HC agreed with opinion of Tribunal that shares of A Mills were not stock-in-trade of appellants and shares were purchased by the appellants with object of acquiring managing agency and dismissed notice of motion taken out by appellants - Whether acquisition of managing agency was an acquisition of a capital asset and loss incurred by sale of shares was of a capital nature - Held, A Mills were not stock-in-trade of appellants and shares were purchased by appellants with object of acquiring managing agency - Shares acquired by appellants formed capital asset and loss suffered by sale of 400 shares being a capital loss was not in computation of income a permissible deduction - HC was right in holding acquisition of managing agency was an acquisition of capital asset and loss incurred by sale of 400 shares was of a capital nature - If acquisition of shares was not acquisition of a stock in trade but of a capital asset appellants by valuing shares at cost or market price whichever was lower could not bring difference between purchase price and valuation made by them into trading account - Appeal dismissed.
