Ramdev Food Products Private Limited v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

16 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, T.S. Thakur, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 193; (2015) 6 SCC 439; AIR 2015 SC 1742; 2015 CRLJ 2382; 2015 (3) G.L.R. 2231; 2015 (2) JCC 994; JT 2015 (3) SC 323; 2015 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1412; 2015 (2) MLJ(Crl) 112; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 372; 2015 (2) RLW 1722; 2015(3) SCALE 622; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 283

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Power Of Police

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 156, 202 - Investigation - Police enquiry - Appellant filed complaint alleging that accused no. 1 committed offences by executing forged partnership documents and sought direction for investigation u/s. 156(3) of CrPC - However, Magistrate directed police to conduct inquiry and submit report u/s. 202 of CrPC - Hence, appellant approached HC but HC refused to interfere with said order of Magistrate directing police to inquire instead of investigation u/s. 156 of CrPC as sought by appellant - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Magistrate erred in seeking report u/s. 202 of CrPC instead of directing investigation u/s. 156 (3) of CrPC.

Held, Magistrate has given reasons, which have been upheld by HC and the case has been held to be primarily of civil nature. The accused is alleged to have forged partnership. Whether such forgery actually took place and whether it caused any loss to the complainant and whether there is the requisite mens rea are the questions which are yet to be determined. The Magistrate has not found clear material to proceed against the accused. Even a case for summoning has not yet been found. While a transaction giving rise to cause of action for a civil action could also involve a crime in which case resort to criminal proceedings could be justified, yet as there is judicially acknowledged tendency in the commercial world to give colour of criminal case to a purely commercial transaction, Magistrate and HC rightly held that in present case report u/s. 202 was right course instead of direction u/s. 156(3) and police is not justified in exercising power of arrest in the course of submitting report u/s. 202. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Where on account of credibility of information available, or weighing the interest of justice it is considered appropriate to straightaway direct investigation such a direction is issued u/s. 156 of CrPC. However, cases where Magistrate takes cognizance and postpones issuance of process are cases where Magistrate has yet to determine 'existence of sufficient ground to proceed' and they fall under category of cases u/s. 202 of CrPC.
