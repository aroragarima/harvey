Raju Devade v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

29 June 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhushan, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 452; AIR 2016 SC 3209; 2016 ALL MR (Cri) 3134; 2016 CRLJ 3568; 2016 (3) JCC 2182; JT 2016 (6) SC 430; 2016 (4) MLJ(Crl) 519; 2016 (3) RCR(Criminal) 700; 2016(5) SCALE 818; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 818

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Dying Declaration, Value, Kerosene, Reliance, Doctor, Hanging, Identification, Burning, Accidental, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Husband, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 302, 313 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 307 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Deceased/victim had love affair with appellant/accused - Accused allegedly poured kerosene on deceased and set her on fire - Accused was charged for offence u/s. 307 IPC - Trial Court convicted accused u/s. 302 IPC and sentenced him to undergo life imprisonment - On appeal, HC dismissed appeal and upheld impugned judgment of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned judgment and conviction of Courts below was sustainable.

Held, oral statement of deceased was recorded by police which followed by recording of dying declaration by Trial Court in which same statement was made by victim implicating accused of crime. The theory of burn being caused by chimney was rightly rejected by Courts below by giving cogent reasons. Therefore, conviction was rightly recorded relying on dying declaration of deceased. The death has been caused by burn injuries, which is proved on record. The theory put up by appellant that it was accidental death was rightly rejected and prosecution by cogent evidences having proved the prosecution case. Thus, Courts below had rightly convicted accused of offence u/s. 302 IPC. Appeal dismissed.
