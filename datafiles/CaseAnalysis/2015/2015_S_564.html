Swastika Enterprises and another v Commissioner of Customs and others

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 545; (2015) 10 SCC 573; 2015 (322) E.L.T. 423; JT 2015 (12) SC 418; 2015(8) SCALE 476; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 1151

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 28, 46 - Finance (No.2) Act, 1998, ss. 87(m), 95(ii)(b), 153 - Exemption from duty - Failure to grant benefit of duty - Justification - Appellants carry on business of importing old ships for purpose of ship breaking and disposing of scrap - Appellant imported vessel and for clearance of goods, filed bill of entry u/s. 46 of 1962 Act, however, contended that import of goods in question was exempted from levy of 'additional customs duty' under exemption notification dt. 28-2-1993 - However, respondent authority failed to agree same and duty was demanded from appellant - Further, HC directed appellant to submit bank guarantee for 50% of disputed amount and personal bond for balance 50% - Further, scheme was introduced by Govt. under 1998 Act for settlement of dispute relating to tax arrears both for direct taxes and indirect taxes - Appellant opted to avail scheme, however, respondent authority vide order rejected declaration of appellant on ground that in appellants' case, no show-cause notice/demand notice had been issued and, hence, by virtue of s. 95(ii)(b) of 1998 Act, Scheme did not apply - Lower Court passed order in favor of appellant and held that endorsement on Bill of Entry constituted sufficient notice of demand to attract Scheme, however, DB reversed order passed by lower court - Whether appellant is eligible to take benefit of Scheme in question.

Held, contingency of issuing show-cause notice u/s. 28 of 1962 Act would arise where duty has not been paid either on ground that it was not levied at all or was short levied or where duty has been erroneously refunded, however, such situation did not arise in instant case. Further, when Bill of Entry was filed by appellants, after examining matter, endorsement was made thereupon that additional duty. SC held that 1998 Act does not contain any specific provision prescribing manner in which customs duty would be assessed or demanded in respect of goods imported under Bill of Entry for home consumption. Endorsement on Bill of Entry and return thereof to importer asking importer to pay amount therein would amount to issuing demand. Further, SC held that after endorsement on Bill of Entry, it was admittedly served upon appellants in manner specified u/s. 153. Further, SC held that it was demand raised by way of endorsement on Bill of Entry that prompted appellants to challenge same by filing petition before HC. Revenue never took plea that case was premature in sense that no demand had been crystallized in absence of show-cause notice or adjudication order and hence, petition was not competent. Hence, parties understood that endorsement on Bill of Entry and service thereof upon appellants was notice of demand. SC held that with reference to provisions of Scheme, endorsement shall have to be treated as notice of demand u/s. 87(m) of 1998 Act which defines 'tax arrears'. Further, SC held that when it is found in broader sense that there were tax arrears and appellants were called upon to pay said tax, mischief contained in s. 95(ii)(b) of Act would not be attracted. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is set aside and appellant is liable to take benefit of scheme. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Benefit provided by authority under scheme cannot be denied when procedure for availing benefit is duly followed.
