Samaj Parivartan Samudaya & Ors v State Of Karnataka & Ors

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Aftab Alam, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 246; (2012) 7 SCC 407; AIR 2012 SC 2326; JT 2012 (5) SC 291; 2012 (6) KarLJ 20; 2012 (3) RCR(Criminal) 788; 2012 (4) RLW 3689; 2012(5) SCALE 525; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 1074

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Degradation

Summary: Criminal - Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - Environment(Protection) Act, 1986, s. 33 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21,32,136 - Illegal Mining - Failure to take action by State - Investigation by CBI - Sustainability - Petitioner filed petitioner under art. 32 of Constitution aggrieved by illegal mining and transportation of illegally mined minerals and lack of action on part of Govt. and prayed for cancelling of 'raising contracts' or sub-lease executed by Govt. - Concerned with rampant pilferage and illegal extraction of natural wealth and resources, particularly iron ore, and environmental degradation and disaster that may result from unchecked intrusion into forest areas, SC, felt compelled to intervene and had constituted Central Empowered Committee -CEC to examine and monitor various activities infringing laws protecting environment and also preventive or punitive steps that may be required to be taken to protect environment - CEC filed its report and suggested recommendation before SC, Court noted that illegal extracts were prime facie and felt that CBI should examine alleged illegalities and present a status report - Aggrieved parties to lis filed objections pleading that CEC filed report without providing opportunity of being heard which would cause prejudice to them - Hence this petition - Whether order of investigation to CBI passed by court would cause prejudice to parties of lis - Held, facts of case reveal an unfortunate state of affairs which had prevailed for a considerable time in mentioned districts of both States, CEC had recommended, and complainant and petitioners had also highlighted, a complete failure of State machinery in relation to controlling and protecting environment, forests and minerals from being illegally mined and exploited - Wherever and whenever State fails to perform its duties, Court shall step in to ensure that Rule of Law prevails over abuse of process of law, such abuse may result from inaction or even arbitrary action of protecting true offenders or failure by different authorities in discharging statutory or legal obligations in consonance with procedural and penal statutes - Court expressed its concern about rampant pilferage and illegal extraction of natural wealth and resources, particularly, iron ore, as also environmental degradation and disaster that may result from unchecked intrusion into forest areas - only option was to examine in depth huge monetary transactions which were effected at cost of national wealth, natural resources, and to punish offenders for their illegal, irregular activities, protection of these resources was, and was constitutional duty of State and its instrumentalities and thus, Court should adopt a holistic approach and direct comprehensive and specialized investigation into such events of past - Petition disposed of.
