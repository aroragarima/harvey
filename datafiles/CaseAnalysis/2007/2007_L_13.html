Commissioner of Income Tax v Lakshmi Machine Works

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 632; (2007) 11 SCC 126; AIR 2007 SC 2385; [2007] 290 ITR 667; JT 2007 (6) SC 236; 2007(6) SCALE 168; 2007 (5) SCJ 710; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 622

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947, Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940, Business Profits Tax Act

Summary: Income Tax - Section 80HHC, Income Tax Act, 1961 - Total turnover - Deduction - Non-inclusion of sales tax and excise duty - Assessee had computed the allowable deduction under s. 80HHC without taking into account in the total turnover the sales tax and excise duty - Department issued notice under s. 143(2) - Assessing Officer held that under s. 80HHC, Explanation (ba), deduction from 'total turnover' was restricted only to three items, namely, profit on sale of import licence, duty drawback and CCS - Assessee's argument for non-inclusion of sales tax and excise duty was not accepted by the Assessing Officer - On appeal, the Commissioner held that sales tax and excise duty were liabilities of the assessee to the Government and as they were shown separately from the value of the goods, they were not included in the 'total turnover' for working out the deduction under s. 80HHC - Whether excise duty and sales tax were includible in the 'total turnover', which was the denominator in the formula contained in s. 80HHC(3) as it stood in the material time - Further appeal by the Department was dismissed by the Tribunal - Held, during the relevant year, there existed a dual system for computation of export profits - Even if the assessee was an exclusive dealer in exports, commission was not includible as it did not spring from the 'turnover' - Just as interest, commission etc. did not emanate from the 'turnover', so also excise duty and sales tax did not emanate from such turnover - Since excise duty and sales tax did not involve any such turnover, such taxes had to be excluded - Excise duty and sales tax are indirect taxes and are recovered by the assessee on behalf of the Government - If they are made relatable to exports, the formula under s. 80HHC would become unworkable.
