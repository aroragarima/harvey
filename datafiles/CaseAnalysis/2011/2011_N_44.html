Marabasappa (D) by LRs. and others v Ningappa (D) by LRs. and others

Supreme Court of India

08 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 627; (2011) 9 SCC 451; 2011(6) ALL MR 428; 2012 (92) ALR 199; 2012 (1) AWC 116; 2012 (3) Bom.C.R. 627; 2012 (1) CLT(SC) 154; JT 2011 (11) SC 68; 2012 (1) MahLJ 1; 2012 (1) MPLJ 1; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 370; 2012 (116) RD 252; [2011] 12 S.C.R. 435

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Hindu Succession Act, 1955, Stridhana

Summary: Land & Property - Family & Personal - Hindu Succession Act, 1955, s. 14 - Stridhana - Joint family property - Validity - At time of marriage, deceased mother of appellant-defendant was gifted land (Stridhana land) under registered Gift Deed by her father - Deceased mother purchased lands from income of gifted land - With income from above two lands, she purchased another land - Deceased mother had one daughter and four sons, of whom 1 son was deceased without heirs and another son was deceased and was represented by his legal heirs - During her lifetime, deceased mother relinquished her share in one of the purchased land in favour of present appellant - Through oral partition other land was given to respondent-plaintiff and heirs of deceased son - Will was executed bequeathing stridhana land to daughter - Respondents-plaintiffs filed suit before Trial Court alleging that entire property mentioned above was joint family property and was not personal property of mother of appellant-defendant and hence, a prayer for partition and separate possession of 1/3rd share was made - Trial Court held that said properties were self-acquired properties of mother of appellant-defendant - On appeal, HC set aside judgment of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether properties in dispute were personal acquisitions of appellant's deceased mother or part of joint family property - s. 14 of the Act, clearly provided that any property of female Hindu was her absolute property on which she had full ownership - Explanation to s. 14 (1) further clarified that Hindu woman had full ownership over any property that she had acquired on her own or as stridhana - Such property was not to be treated as part of joint Hindu family property and she could thus dispose of the same as per her wish - Therefore, lands acquired with income acquired from sthridhana land was not joint family property but absolute property of deceased mother - Thus, properties in dispute were required to be partitioned among family members in accordance with law - Therefore, judgment and decree passed by Trial Court was restored - Appeal allowed.
