State of Rajasthan and Others v D.R. Laxmi and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, Faizan Uddin, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1470; (1996) 6 SCC 445; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 562; AIR 1996 SCW 3970; 1996 (4) CCC 118; 1996 (4) CLT 191; JT 1996 (9) SC 327; 1997 (1) MLJ(SC) 116; 1996(7) SCALE 316; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 221; 1996 (7) Supreme 753; 1997 (1) UJ 445

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Constitution - Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 6 and 17(4) - (A) Whether the notification u/s. 4(1) should contain the declaration that the lands are waste or arable lands? - S. 4(1) of the Act does not required to specify the nature of the land, i.e. whether it is arable or waste land - Object of the publication of the notification u/s. 4(1) was that the land is needed for a public purpose - Held, the view, therefore, of the High Court that the notification u/s. 4(1) should contain a declaration of the nature of the land is clearly erroneous - (B) Whether the land is waster or arable? - Whether the land is waster or arable, is a mixed question of facts and law - It depends upon the facts in each case - There is no definition of the word 'arable' in the original Land Acquisition Act since they were not arable lands, the exercise of the s. 17(4) was not justified in law - Though the lands in this case situated in urban area, the Urban Land Ceiling Act itself recognises existence of the agricultural lands within the urban agglomeration and they are dealt with accordingly - When the lands were capable of the raising crops, they remained to be arable u/s. 17(4) by the Govt. was not bad in law - Further held, on facts, it is an arable land capable to cultivation - View of the HC, therefore, was clearly erroneous - (C) Whether the absence of the publication of the substance of the notification in locality renders the entire proceedings void? - Since compliance of the requirement of the publication of the notification u/s. 4(1) in the Gazette as well as publication of the substance of the notification in the locality now under the Amended Act in the newspaper, is mandatory requirement - Further Held, the HC under those circumstances should not have interfered with the acquisition and quashed the notification and declaration u/ss. 4 and 6 respectively - (D) Whether the HC was justified in exercising the power u/art. 226? - When there is inordinate delay in filing the writ petition and when all steps taken in the acquisition proceedings have become final, the court should be loathe to quash the notifications - Further held, HC has, no doubt, discretionary powers u/art. 226 of the Constitution to quash the notification u/s. 4(1) and declaration u/s. 6 - But it should be exercised taking all relevant factors into pragmatic consideration - Appeal allowed.
