Charan Dass v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 490; (1980) 4 SCC 468; AIR 1980 SC 1306; 1980 UJ 569

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Act, 1954 - Property - Retrospective - Appellant, and respondent nos. 3 to 5 were displaced persons who came to the State of East Punjab and applied for suitable accommodation - The property in dispute was situate at Ambala which was allotted to appellant and respondent nos. 3 to 5 - At first the entire property was allotted to appellant, in pursuance of an order by way of some sort of adjustment of his verified claim - Subsequently, portions of the property allotted to appellant were transferred to respondents - Appellant filed an appeal against this allotment to the Deputy Chief Settlement Commissioner who by his order allowed the appeal and remanded the matter to the Assistant Settlement Commissioner to dispose of the property after hearing the parties and considering the valuation reports - After the order of remand, the Assistant Settlement Commissioner by his order divided the house into two portions - Ms. K who had been deprived of her portion filed an appeal before the Chief Settlement Commissioner who again remanded the case for further division of the property according to Rules - Subsequently, as no division of the property was possible, the property was auctioned and was purchased by respondent no. 6 who deposited the amount of purchase before the concerned authorities - HC made observation and extracted r. 30 - Hence, the present appeal - Whether appeal should be allowed - Held, as a result of the decision of HC, r. 30 stood restored to its original position and in respect of the property in dispute the logical consequence, therefore, of the decision of HC which, had taken a correct view, the position was that the auction proceedings stood annulled and the parties stood restored to the status quo ante - Therefore appeal had to be allowed because HC by its order under appeal had decided the case on the footing that r. 30 would apply with retrospective effect - Appeal allowed.
