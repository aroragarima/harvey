S.Rm.M.Ct.M. Tiruppani Trust v The Commissioner Of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 518; (1998) 2 SCC 584; AIR 1998 SC 1107; 1998 (145) CTR 176; [1998] 230 ITR 636; JT 1998 (1) SC 599; 1998(1) SCALE 501; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 653; 1998 (2) SLT 693; 1998 (3) Supreme 233; [1998] 96 TAXMAN 635; 1998 (2) TLR 289

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, s. 11(1) - Charitable Income - Accumulating Income - Exemption - Assessee/appellant was a Charitable Trust and income of Trust accumulated for a period of ten years and applied Rs. 8 lakhs for charitable purposes in India - Exemption from taxation claimed by appellant but refused and action of respondent affirmed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether assessee entitled to accumulate income and claim exemption from income-tax u/s. 11 of the Act - Held, yes - Assessee applied Rs. 8 lakhs for charitable purposes in India by purchasing a building which was to be utilized as a hospital - Income of assessee entitled to an exemption u/s. 11(1) of the Act - Further, u/s. 11(1)(a) of the Act assessee can accumulate 25% of its total income pertaining to relevant assessment year and claim exemption in respect thereof and s. 11(1)(a) of the Act did not require investment of this limited accumulation in Government securities - Assessee entitled to accumulate income and claim exemption from income-tax u/s. 11 of the Act - Appeal allowed.
