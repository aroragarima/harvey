Manik Lal Majumdar and Others v Gouranga Chandra Dey and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, R.C. Lahoti, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 18; (2005) 2 SCC 400; AIR 2005 SC 1090; JT 2005 (1) SC 275; 2005 (1) RCR(Rent) 146; 2005(1) SCALE 308; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 437

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Tripura Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1975

Summary: Land and Property - Rent Control - Tripura Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1975 - Arrears of Rent - Maintainability - Appeal - Whether an appeal preferred under s.20 of Act without payment to landlord or deposit with appellate court all arrears of rent admitted by tenant to be due is maintainable or is liable to be rejected on that ground alone? - Held, tenant may be precluded from challenging order of Rent Control Court as memorandum of appeal filed by him without making payment or deposit of arrears of rent would be liable to be rejected straightaway - An appeal under s.20 may be filed or presented without payment to landlord or deposit with appellate authority all arrears of rent admitted by tenant to be due and it cannot be held to be incompetent - Appeal disposed of.
