Annasaheb Bapusaheb Patil and Others Etc v Balwant and Balasaheb Babusaheb Patil(Dead) By Lrs. and Heirs

Supreme Court of India

06 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Mohan, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 442; (1995) 2 SCC 543; AIR 1995 SC 895; AIR 1995 SCW 709; 1995 (3) Bom.C.R. 379; JT 1995 (1) SC 370; 1995(1) SCALE 100; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 88; 1995 (1) UJ 488

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Family Custom, Coparcener, Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Land & Property - Family & Personal - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Maharashtra Revenue Patels (Abolition of Office) Act, 1962, s. 5 - Watandari system - Partition - Parties to the suit belong to HUF - After the 1962 Act, all properties attached to the Patel watan were abolished - Defendant obtained a regrant u/s. 5 of the 1962 Act - Appellants filed suit for partition - HC passed order that after re-grant, the defendant was entitled for the disputed property as his personal property and it was not partible - Appellants aggrieved - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, on regrant, the attached watan lands assumed the character of self-acquired property - Held, that watans had been abolished and all the incidents attached to the watandari including the preexisting custom, operation of law or any decree or order of the court were nullified by statutory operation - All the members of the family became entitled to claim right to partition by survivorship - The 1962 Act had come into force after the 1956 Act became operational - Regrant was made in 1965 and in the same year the right to claim partition accrued to all the members of the family - Plaintiff appellants were entitled to partition and separate possession to the extent of their 1/2 share in the properties of the defendant - Appeal allowed.
