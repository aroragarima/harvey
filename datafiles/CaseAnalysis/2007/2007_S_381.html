Paramjit Singh @ Mithu Singh v State Of Punjab Through Secretary (Home)

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

B. SUDARSHAN REDDY, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1651; (2007) 13 SCC 530; AIR 2008 SC 441; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 1042; 2007(12) SCALE 728; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 776

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 302 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Murder - Common intention - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant was chargesheeted for the offence punishable u/s. 302 of the IPC and other accused were charged u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of the IPC - Trial Court held that there was delay in lodging the FIR and accordingly acquitted all the accused giving them the benefit of doubt - On appeal, HC held that there was no delay in lodging the FIR - HC also reversed the finding as regards the presence of the eye-witnesses and held that there was no reason to doubt the presence of PW-3 and PW-4 at the scene of occurrence - HC held that the presence of eye-witnesses was natural - HC also held that the injuries found on the body of the deceased were inflicted by sharp cutting weapons which was supported by the medical evidence - HC convicted the appellant for the offence punishable u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Delay in lodging the First Information Report - Held, in the facts and circumstances of the instant case, it could not be said that the FIR was ante-timed and brought into existence after some deliberations - (B) Whether under the Punjab Police Rules information must be reduced to writing and be entered in the police station daily diary and only thereafter the FIR was to be issued? - Held, procedure as regards the registration of information relating to the commission of a cognizable offence and the procedure for investigation was structured and regulated by Chapter XII of the CrPC - The procedure prescribed was required to be followed scrupulously by the Officer-in-charge of the Police Station - Punjab Police Rules did not in any manner override the provisions of the Code of Criminal Procedure - The said rules were meant for the guidance of the Police Officers in the State and supplement the provisions of the CrPC but not supplant them - Further, the truth and veracity of contents of the FIR could not in all cases be tested with a reference to the entries made in the police station daily diary which was maintained under the Punjab Police Rules - (C) Evidence of eye-witnesses - Held, evidence of PW-3 and 4 was consistent - (D) Whether injuries inflicted by the appellant were not sufficient to cause the death of the victim and, therefore, the common intention to kill was not evident and therefore, appellant could not be convicted for the offence punishable u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of the IPC? - Held, to convict the person vicariously u/s. 34 of IPC, it was not necessary to prove that each and every one of them had indulged in such overt act of inflicting deadly injuries - It would be enough if the material available on record disclosed that the overt act of one or more of the accused was or were done in furtherance of common intention - In the instant case, common intention shared by the appellant was evident from the fact that he was armed with deadly weapon and inflicted two injuries on the victim - Further, all the accused attacked the deceased and caused injuries in furtherance of the common intention to murder the deceased - In such a situation the nature of injuries inflicted by the appellant on the victim and whether those injuries were sufficient in the ordinary course to cause death did not have insignificance - Each one of them was liable for that act of murder as if the act of murder was done by each one of them - Appeal dismissed.
