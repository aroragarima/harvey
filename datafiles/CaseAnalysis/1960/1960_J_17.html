Jestamani Gulabrai Dholkia and Others v Scindia Steam Navigation Company, Bombay and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 November 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 286; AIR 1961 SC 627; 1961 (2) FLR 196; 1961 (1) LLJ 318; 1961 (2) SCJ 260; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 811

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: AIR LAW, Air Corporation Act 1953

Summary: Service - Air Corporations Act, 1953, s. 20(1) - Services transferred - Challenged - Appellants who were in service of respondent/Scindias were transferred by way of loan to Air Services of India Limited (ASI) but Scindias retained right to recall appellants - Later Govt. of India contemplated nationalisation of existing air lines operating in India with effect from June 1953 or thereabouts appellants addressed to Scindias requesting to be taken back by Scindias - Scindias send reply that all persons working in ASI would be governed by cl. 20 of Air Corporation Bill of 1953, when Bill was enacted into law and also pointed out that said clause would apply to all those actually working with ASI on appointed day irrespective of whether they were recruited directly or transferred to ASI from Scindias or other associated concerns - In meantime demand was made by appellants in which Scindias were asked to give an assurance that in event of retrenchment of any loaned staff by proposed Corporation within first five years without any fault, said staff would be taken back by Scindias and other demands were also made which was rejected - Aggrieved appellants replied to Scindias that appellants could not accept contention contained in circular dt. 6-5-1953 and moved to Tribunal through reference which was rejected - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of Tribunal was sustainable - Held, it was not disputed that appellants were working for ASI and were being paid by it; their hours of work as well as control over their work was all by ASI and from that it would naturally follow that they were employees of ASI, even though they might not had directly recruited by it - It was true that there were certain special features of their employment with ASI and that they were on same terms and conditions of service as were enjoyed by employees of Scindias in matter of remuneration, leave, bonus, etc - It might also be that they could not be, dismissed by ASI and Scindias may have had to take action in case it was desired to dismiss them - Further it might be that they could be recalled by Scindias and it may even be that they might have option to go back to Scindias but those were only three special terms of their employment with ASI - Subject to those special terms, they would for all purposes be employees of ASI and thus would in law be in employment of ASI both on 1-7-1952 and on 1-8-1953 - Therefore, existence of those special terms in case of appellants would not in law make them any less employees of ASI, for whom they were working and who were paying them, who had power of control and direction over them; who would grant them leave, fix their hours of work and so on - There could in SC opinion be no doubt that subject to those special terms appellants were in employ of ASI in law prior to 1-7-1952 and would still be in its employ immediately before 1-8-1953 - Consequently, they would clearly be governed by s. 20(1) of the 1953, Act, as they did not exercise option given to them by proviso to s. 20(1), of the 1953, Act, they became employees of Corporation from 1-8-1953, by terms of statute - However, last point that had urged was that even if s. 20(1) of the 1953, Act, applies, Scindias were bound to take back appellants but there was no force in that contention either - As soon as appellants became by force of law employees of Corporation, as they did so become on 1-8-1953, in circumstances of instant case, they had no further right against Scindias and could not; claim to be taken back in their employment on ground that they were still their employees, in spite of operation of s. 20(1) of the Act nor could they claim any of alternative benefits specified in order of reference, as from 1-8-1953, they were by operation of law only employees of Corporation and could have no rights whatsoever against Scindias and tribunal's decision was correct - Appeal dismissed.
