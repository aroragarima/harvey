Bank of India and Another v Degala Suryanarayana

Supreme Court of India

12 July 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 866; (1999) 5 SCC 762; (1999) SCC (L&S) 1036; AIR 1999 SC 2407; 1999 (5) ALD(SC) 1; 1999 (3) ESC 1927; JT 1999 (4) SC 489; 1999 (2) LLJ 682; [1999] 3 S.C.R. 824; 1999 (6) Supreme 39; 1999 (3) UPLBEC 1743

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Bank of India Officer Employees (Discipline and Appeal) Regulations, 1976 - Judicial review - Disciplinary proceedings - Respondent was working in Middle Management Grade-II and was due for promotion - Disciplinary proceedings were initiated against him alleging certain misappropriations - While the complaint was under investigation the respondent was interviewed for promotion in the year 1981-82 - Result of the interview was withheld on the ground of pendency of criminal proceedings against him - Enquiry Officer submitted a report recording a finding that none of the charges levelled against the respondent was proved - Disciplinary Authority reversed the finding of the Enquiry Officer on charge 1(b) and held the respondent guilty of misconduct and imposed upon him the punishment of reduction of pay one stage which was a major punishment - On appeal, Single Judge held that though the Disciplinary Authority had jurisdiction to record a finding at variance with the one recorded by Enquiry Officer, but in the facts and circumstances of the case, the finding arrived at by the Disciplinary Authority was perverse - Single Judge allowed the writ petition and set aside the order of penalty passed against the respondent - Appeals were filed - Order of die Single Judge was and appeals were dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Single Judge of the HC was justified in interfering with the findings recorded by die Disciplinary Authority in reversal of die findings recorded by the Enquiry Officer.

Held, the Court exercising the jurisdiction of judicial review would not interfere with the findings of fact arrived at in the departmental enquiry proceedings excepting in a case of mala fides or perversity i.e., where there is no evidence to support a finding or where a finding is such that no man acting reasonably and with objectivity could have arrived at that findings. The Court cannot embark upon reappreciating the evidence or weighing the same like an appellate authority. In the instant case, disciplinary Authority has taken into consideration the evidence, the finding and the reasons recorded by the Enquiry Officer and then assigned reasons for taking a view in departure from the one taken by the Enquiry Officer. The Disciplinary Authority has then recorded its own findings setting out the evidence already available on record in support of the finding arrived at by the Disciplinary Authority. The finding so recorded by the Disciplinary Authority was immune from interference within the limited scope of power of judicial review available to the Court. Single Judge as well as the DB of HC were not right in setting aside the finding of the Disciplinary Authority and restoring that of the Enquiry Officer. The High Court has clearly exceeded the bounds of power of judicial review available to it while exercising writ jurisdiction over a departmental disciplinary enquiry proceeding and therefore the judgments of the Single Judge and the DB cannot be sustained to that extent. The appeal filed by the bank of India deserves to be allowed to mat extent. Appeal partly allowed.
