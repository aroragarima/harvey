Union of India and Others v V. B. Hajela

Supreme Court of India

11 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3027; (1997) 10 SCC 531; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1627

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Service

Keywords: Master And Servant Relationship

Summary: Service - Central Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1965, r. 10(4) - Central Civil Services (Pension) Rules, r. 9(2)(a) - Whether r. 10(4) of Rules, 1965 could not be invoked against respondent because he had not been suspended from service at any stage during pendency of earlier disciplinary proceedings? - Held, in view of decision in Nelson Motis case 1992 Indlaw SC 1256, it must be held that tribunal was in error in holding that r. 10(4) could not be invoked because respondent was not placed under suspension earlier - Further held, respondent retired on 28-02-1991 prior to judgment of tribunal D/- 08-08-1991 in earlier proceedings whereby order of compulsory retirement was set aside - But disciplinary proceedings that had been initiated against respondent under charge-sheet D/- 27-02-1987 were not quashed and remained still pending - By its judgment D/- 08-08-1991, tribunal had indicated that said decision would not preclude disciplinary authority from reviving disciplinary proceedings and continuing with it in accordance with law from stage of supplying inquiry report - In these circumstances, it was open to disciplinary authority to continue said proceedings from stage of supplying inquiry report - Disciplinary authority decided to do so and necessary sanction u/r. 9(2)(a) of Pension Rules was accorded by President - Thus both requirements for applicability of r. 10(4) were satisfied in present case and respondent has to be treated as deemed to have been placed under suspension w.e.f. 27-05-1988, date of passing of original order of compulsory retirement, in view of r. 10(4) of Rules, 1965 - Appeal allowed.
