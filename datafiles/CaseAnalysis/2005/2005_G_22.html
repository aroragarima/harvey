Ganga Kumar Srivastava v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

20 July 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 419; (2005) 6 SCC 211; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1424; AIR 2005 SC 3123; 2005 CRLJ 3454; JT 2005 (6) SC 356; 2005 (3) RCR(Criminal) 707; 2005(5) SCALE 535

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 5(2), 6(1)(c) - IPC, 1860, s. 161 - Offence of taking bribe - Appeal against conviction - Whether on evidence and materials on record, conviction and sentence recorded against appellant are justified or they require to be set aside? - Held, judgments and orders of court below are liable to be set aside on ground that such findings of fact and appreciation of evidence are vitiated as evidence adduced by prosecution fell short of test of reliability and acceptability, and, as such, it was highly unsafe on part of courts below to act upon it - Exonerate appellant from charges found against him - Appeal allowed.
