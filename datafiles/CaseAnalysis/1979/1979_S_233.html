Pohalya Motya Valvi v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 397; (1980) SCC (Cr) 261; (1980) 1 SCC 530; AIR 1979 SC 1949; 1979 CRLJ 1310; 1980 CRLR 185

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Both appellant and deceased were brothers consumed liquor and both brothers were gone to house of PW 8 and from that place to accused no. 2 was alleged to have joined them - After appellant alone returned to house and on query by widow of deceased, as to where her husband had gone appellant was found to be evasive in his reply - Appellant and accused no. 2 were charge-sheeted for having committed offences u/ss. 302 and 201 r/w s. 34 IPC - Trial Court held that prosecution had failed to bring home charge against both accused and acquitted them - On appeal, HC disagreed with some of findings of Trial Court and was further of opinion that established circumstances unequivocally point to guilt of appellant as being murderer of his brother/deceased and accordingly convicted him and sentenced him as above - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of HC was sustainable - Held, prosecution had attempted to offer evidence of motive which would be valuable in case dependent on circumstantial evidence but both Courts have discarded evidence of motive as well as evidence bearing on proof of extra-judicial confession alleged to have been made by appellant and SC must accept this concurrent finding of both courts - Hence, when appellant was detained by investigating officer he had put on dhoti which had some scattered stains of human blood, discovery of blood-stained spear alleged to have been used in causing injuries to deceased, on information given by appellant being found to be unconvincing, only circumstance proved was one of recovery of blood-stained dhoti of appellant - Applying test of circumstantial evidence this was wholly insufficient to bring home charge - Therefore, conviction of the appellant for offence u/s. 302 IPC and sentence of imprisonment for life imposed upon him by HC were set aside and he was acquitted of charge - Appeal allowed.
