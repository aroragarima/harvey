Cawnpore Tannery Limited, Kanpur v S. Guha and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 November 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 496; AIR 1967 SC 667; 1961 (3) FLR 237; 1961 (2) LLJ 110

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 25-H - Termination - Whether management of appellant have wrongfully and/or unjustifiably kept Mr. Guha out of employment from time when there was scope for his re-employment? - Held, definition of term 'workman' even prior to its amendment in 1956 would have included a person like Mr. Guha whose services were terminated - Definition of 'workmen' includes a person who had been dismissed, discharged or retrenched - Even before 25-H was added to Act industrial adjudication generally recognized principle that if an employer retrenched services of an employee on ground that employee in question had become surplus, it was necessary that whenever employer had occasion to employ another hand retrenched workman should be given an opportunity to join service - Further held, offer would be conditioned by consideration of category to which retrenched employee belonged - Appeal dismissed.
