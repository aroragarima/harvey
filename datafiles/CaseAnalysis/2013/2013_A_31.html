The Official Liquidator, U.P. And Uttarakhand v Allahabad Bank And Others

Supreme Court of India

12 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 146; (2013) 4 SCC 381; AIR 2013 SC 1823; 2013(3) ALL MR 906; 2013 (98) ALR 702; 2013 (2) BC 191; [2013] 177 Comp Cas 426; 2013 (2) CompLJ 225; 2013 (2) CTC 564; 2013 (1) DRTC 609; JT 2013 (4) SC 175; 2013 (4) MahLJ 535; 2013 (3) MLJ 359; 2013 (3) MPLJ 310; 2013 (120) RD 115; 2013(3) SCALE 401; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 207

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Recovery Officer, Setting aside of auction at instance of official liquidator

Summary: Banking & Finance - Corporate - Companies Act, 1956 - Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993, s. 9 - Auction - Recovery certificate - Legality - Respondent/Bank filed application for recovery of amount before Tribunal and the same was decreed - Debt Recovery Certificate was issued for recovery of amount which was transferred to DRT - Company petition was filed - Company Judge had passed an order for winding up of company - Recovery Officer attached immoveable properties of wound-up company - Respondent filed an application before Company Court and the same was allowed - Auction-purchaser filed an application before Company Judge to hand over possession of properties accordingly order was passed - Aggrieved respondent filed appeal before DB and the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether Company Judge under 1956 Act had jurisdiction at instance of Official Liquidator to set aside auction or sale held by Recovery Officer under 1993 Act -

Held, Official Liquidator could not even take recourse to doctrine of election - Official Liquidator had only one remedy, to challenge order passed by Recovery Officer before DRT - An order passed by DRT was appealable - Thus, Official Liquidator could only take recourse to mode of appeal and further appeal under 1993 Act and not approach Company Court to set aside auction or confirmation of sale when sale was confirmed by Recovery Officer under 1993 Act - Company Court had imposed a condition that permission of Company Court should be obtained before sale of properties, immoveable or moveable, was confirmed or finalized - When bank was permitted to go ahead with proposed sale of assets of company under liquidation by way of auction but such sale was subject to confirmation by Company Court and all parties were aware about condition as to confirmation of sale by Company Court, it was not open to Recovery Officer to confirm sale and, therefore, sale was set aside by Company Court, being in violation of order - Hence, it was not an authority for proposition that Official Liquidator could approach Company Court to set aside auction or sale conducted by Recovery Officer of DRT - View expressed by DB was concurred with holding that Official Liquidator could prefer an appeal before DRT - DRT was directed to decide appeal within time framed after offering an opportunity of hearing to all concerned - Appeal disposed of.
