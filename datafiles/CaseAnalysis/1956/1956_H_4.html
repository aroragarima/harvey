Â Hari Khemu Gawali v Deputy Commissioner of Police, Bombay and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Syed Jaffer Imam, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 24; AIR 1956 SC 559; 1956 (58) BomLR 995; 1956 CRLJ 1104; 1956 SCJ 599; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 506

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Specially Empowered, Preventive Detention Act, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, City of Bombay Police Act, 1902, Criminal Law (Amendment) Act, 1908, misa, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Criminal Law (Amendment) Act

Summary: Criminal - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - City of Bombay Police Act, 1902 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 107 and 110 - Criminal Law (Amendment) Act - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 143, 147, 148, 149, 144, 146, 324 and 353 - Police Act - Preventive Detention Act, 1950 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 22(4)-Bombay Police Act, 1951, ss. 57, 59, 55, 56, 60 and 61 - Bombay Prevention of Gambling Act, 1887 - Bombay Prohibition Act, 1949 - Criminal Law (Amendment) Act, 1908 - Petitioner, who claims to be a citizen of India, was born in Bombay and had been, before order of externment, residing in Bombay - An 'externment order' was passed against him - That order was set aside by Govt. on appeal - Order of detention passed against him under Preventive Detention Act, 1950 - Released from detention - Thereafter, petitioner was charged with possession of liquor - Ultimately discharged - Thereafter, petitioner was arrested by a special wing of Crime Branch, CID, on allegation made by police that they were seen running away on sight of a police van and were found in possession of knives and other weapons - Notice served - Respondent 1 ultimately passed order of externment - Govt. of Bombay dismissed appeal - (A) Whether s. 57 contravened art. 19(1)(d) and (e) of Constitution and that provisions of that section imposed unreasonable restrictions on petitioner's fundamental rights of free movement and residence? - To attract provisions of this section, two essential conditions must be fulfilled - (1) that there should have been a previous conviction under Chapter XII, XVI or XVII, IPC, or two previous convictions under Acts mentioned in cl. (b), or three previous convictions within a period of three years under Acts mentioned in cl. (c) - (2) that authority named should have reason to believe that a person coming within purview of any of cls. (a),(b) and (c) is likely again to engage himself in commission of an offence similar to that for which he had been previously convicted - Previous convictions under three clauses aforesaid have been placed in three different categories - For preventing a breach of public peace or invasion of private rights State has sometimes to impose certain restrictions on individual rights - Duty of State is not only to punish offenders against penal laws of State but also to take preventive action - S. 57 is an instance of State taking preventive measures in interest of public and for safeguarding individual's rights - In doing so State may have to curb an individual's activities and put fetters on his complete freedom of movement and residence in order that greatest good of greatest number may be conserved -(B) Whether unlike Preventive Detention laws there was no provision in impugned law for an Advisory Board which could scrutinise material on which officers or authorities contemplated by s. 57? - Held, it cannot be said that existence of an Advisory Board is a sine qua non of constitutionality of a legislation - (C) Whether action can be taken by police? - In order to take preventive action under s. 57 legislature has entrusted police officers or magistrates of higher ranks to examine facts and circumstances of each case brought before them by Criminal Investigation Department - But legislature has provided certain safeguards against tyrannical or wholly unfounded orders being passed by higher ranks of police or magistracy - (D) Whether provisions relating to hearing any evidence are illusory? - Held, grounds available to an externee very limited in their scope because if evidence were available which could be adduced in public, such a person could be dealt with under preventive sections of CrPC - Present case cannot be dealt with under preventive sections of CrPC - (E) Whether orders are legal? - Held, it cannot be laid down as a general proposition of law that a previous order of discharge or acquittal cannot be taken into account by authorities when dealing with persons under any one of these provisions - Petitioner not been a victim of an arbitrary order - No grounds for issuing any writ or direction to authorities concerned or for quashing orders impugned - Appeals dismissed.
