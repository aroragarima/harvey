Kailash Verma v Punjab State Civil Supplies Corporation and

Supreme Court of India

18 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 33; (2005) SCC (Cr) 538; (2005) 2 SCC 571; JT 2005 (2) SC 420; 2005 (1) RCR(Criminal) 727; 2005(1) SCALE 399; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 527

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 397(3), 482 - IPC,1860, ss.406, 428 - Quashing - Partnership - Crime registered against appellant alleging he committed offences punishable u/s. 406, 428 - Order of discharge by by Chief Judicial Magistrate and revisional court confirmed that order - Whether HC justified in exercising its inherent power u/s.482 of CrPC? - Held, appellant was discharged by Chief Judicial Magistrate and revisional court confirmed that order after elaborately considering facts and circumstances of case - It cannot be said that there was miscarriage of justice warranting interference by HC - Order of discharge passed by Magistrate in favour of appellant affirmed - Appeals allowed.
