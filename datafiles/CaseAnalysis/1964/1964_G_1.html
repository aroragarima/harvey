State of Madras v G. J. Coelho

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 249; AIR 1965 SC 321; 1964 (2) ITJ 215; [1964] 53 ITR 186; 1964 (2) SCJ 400; [1964] 8 S.C.R. 60

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Madras Plantations Agricultural Income Tax Act, 1955, 5(e) - Assessment - Claim for deductions - Disallowance - Challenged - Granted - Validity - Respondent/assessee, purchased estate consisting of certain plantations - Out of total sale price of, he borrowed certain amount, at interest - For relevant assessment year, assessee claimed to deduct interest on that sum in that Agricultural Income Tax Officer(ITO) disallowed to some extent and allowed to certain extent - Assessee made appeal to Assistant Commissioner and that concluded in failure - Further, assessee made appeal to Agricultural Income Tax Appellate Tribunal and that also resulted into failure to which assessee filed revision application to HC u/s. 54(l) of the Act - HC held that deduction claimed by assessee fell within scope of s. 5 (e) of the Act, and that whole amount should had been deducted from his assessable income and ordered that assessment be revised accordingly - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether HC was right in holding that deduction claimed by assessee fell within scope of s. 5(e) of the Act, and that whole amount should had been deducted from his assessable income - Held, it was impossible to dissociate character of assessee as owner of plantation and as son working plantation - payment of interest on amount borrowed for purchase of plantation when whole transaction of purchase and working of plantation was viewed as integrated whole, was so closely related to plantation that expenditure could be said to be laid out or expended wholly and exclusively for purpose of plantation - Thus, Act purported to tax was agricultural income and not agricultural receipts as from agricultural receipts should be deducted all expenses which in ordinary commercial accounting should be debited against receipts - Further, there was nothing in the Act which prohibited such expenses from being deducted - Hence, HC was right in holding that deduction claimed by assessee fell within scope of s. 5(e) of the Act, and that whole amount should had been deducted from his assessable income - Appeal dismissed.
