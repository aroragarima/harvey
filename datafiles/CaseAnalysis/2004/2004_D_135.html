State (Anti Corruption Branch) Govt. of N.C.T. of v Dr. R.C. Anand & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 327; (2004) 4 SCC 615; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1380; AIR 2004 SC 3693; 2004 (2) Crimes 267; 2004 (111) DLT 61; 2004 (2) JCC 806; JT 2004 (Supp1) SC 205; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 504; 2004(4) SCALE 523; 2004 (3) SLT 153; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 161; 2004 (3) Supreme 317

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: All India Institute of Medical Sciences Regulations, 1999, All India Institute of Medical Sciences Act, 1956, Central Civil Services Classification Control and Appeal Rules

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - All India Institute Of Medical Sciences Act, 1956, s. 29(1) - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 19 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, s. 482 - All India Institute of medical Sciences Regulations, 1999 - Central Civil Services Classification Control and Appeal Rules - Whether Governing Body's decision, granting sanction to proceed against respondent, suffered from any infirmity - When Authority competent to accord sanction is Governing Body under statutory Regulations and that body, as in this case takes a decision there was no necessity for recording reasons to differ from view expressed by President who had legally no role to pay - There was no question of any ratification involved - HC's judgment is indefensible and is quashed - Matter pending before Special Judge shall now proceed in accordance with law.
