Jagdish Chand Sharma v Narain Singh Saini (Dead) Through his Lrs. and others

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Amitava Roy, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 303; (2015) 8 SCC 615; AIR 2015 SC 2149; 2015 (4) ALD(SC) 69; 2015 (110) ALR 883; 2015 (3) AWC 2753; 2015 (3) Bom.C.R. 481; 2015 (2) CLT(SC) 107; JT 2015 (4) SC 468; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2126; 2016 (2) MahLJ 366; 2015 (3) MLJ 879; 2016 (1) MPLJ 597; 2015 (3) RCR(Civil) 139; 2015 (128) RD 555; 2015(5) SCALE 749; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 397; 2015 (4) WLN(SC) 49

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Oath, Bequest

Summary: Family & Personal - Land & Property - Indian Succession Act, 1925, ss. 63, 276 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 71 - Denial of attestation of Will - Production of additional evidence - Appellant filed application u/s. 276 of 1925 Act seeking grant of Letter of Administration on ground that testator executed Will in his favour bequeathing properties to him and also that testator was not favorably disposed towards his own children for their disagreeable conduct and activities - However, respondents objected and upon assessment of evidence adduced, Trial Court concluded that appellant proved Will as executed by testator and granted Letter of Administration - Aggrieved, respondents filed appeal before HC and by impugned judgment and order, HC reversed verdict of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeals - Whether execution of Will and attestation thereof is proved within the meaning of s. 63(c) of 1925 Act.

Held, account of relevant facts bearing on execution and attestation of Will as provided by AWs 1 and 5, though is thoroughly inadequate qua the prescriptions of s. 63(c) of 1925 Act, yet, it does not amount to denial of execution or failure to recollect the said event as contemplated in said provision. Further, testimony of AW3 does not conform to the imperatives of s. 63(c) of 1925 Act and his narration on affirmation at the trial does not either by itself meet the essentialities of s. 63(c) of 1925 Act nor could be construed to be a supplement of the evidence of AWs 1 and 5 to furnish the proof of execution and attestation of the Will as enjoined by law. The evidence of AWs 1,3,5 analyzed collectively or in isolation, does not evince animo attestandi, an essential imperative of valid attestation of a Will. Hence, testimony of said witnesses fall short of the probative content to construe Will to be a validly executed and attested Will as envisaged in law. Further, the evidence of AWs 1,3,5 does not exhibit either denial of the execution of the Will or their failure to recollect the said phenomenon and thus, does not attract the applicability of s. 71 of the 1872 Act. Further, it could not accepted that testator would have preferred appellant to be the legatee of his property as set out in Will, by denying his wife, children and grand children who were alive and with whom he had very warm affectionate and cordial relationship. Hence, in that view also bequest is ex-facie unnatural, unfair, improbable thus reflecting on the testator's cognizant, free, objective and discerning state of mind at the time of alleged dispensation. Hence, HC had appropriately appreciated the law and facts in the right perspective and so, impugned decision does not call for any interference. Hence, appeals are dismissed. Appeals dismissed.
