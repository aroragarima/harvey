Digambar Adhar Patil v Devram Girdhar Patil (Died) and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1554; (1995) Supp2 SCC 428; AIR 1995 SC 1728; 1995(2) SCALE 802; 1995 (1) SCJ 557; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 133; 1995 (2) UJ 358

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948, s. 32 B - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 35 - (A) Question relating to determination of ceiling area limit - (B) Whether necessary that partition should be effected by registered partition deed - Held, (A) land held by claimant as owner and also with land cultivated by him on behalf of his minor son within ceiling limit - Land was also allotted to his brother after partition - Such land should be excluded - Claimant entitled to purchase land in question which was cultivated by him as tenant - (B) Under Hindu Law it is not necessary that partition should be effected by registered partition deed, even a family arrangement is enough to effectuate partition between coparceners and to confer right to a separate share and enjoyment thereof - Appeal dismissed.
