Bhajan Lal v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 September 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 166; (1971) 1 SCC 34; [1971] 2 S.C.R. 497

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Punjab Security of Land Tenures Act, 1953, s. 18 - Petitioner applied u/s. 14-A of Act for an order in ejectment against his tenant, Shadi - Application was dismissed by Asst. Collector and that order was confirmed in appeal by Collector - Financial Commr. set aside order and remanded case for a fresh decision by order D/- 08-01-1962 - On 20-02-1961 Shadi applied to Asst. Collector to purchase lands u/s. 18 of Act - By order D/- 05-10-1962, Financial Commr. remanded case for determining whether shadi was in occupation of lands for six years before date of petition - In proceeding started by petitioner, Asst. Collector held that shadi was liable to be evicted from lands because he had without sufficient cause committed default in paying rent, in proceeding filed by shadi Asst. Collector declared that shadi was entitled to purchase lands from petitioner - In a revision application, Financial Commr. upheld order of Asst. Collector - Against order whereby shadi was declared entitled to purchase lands, Bhajan Lal applied to High Court of Punjab for an order setting aside order of Financial Commr. - Whether shadi will be entitled to purchase land? - Held, tenancy does not stand terminated merely because a proceeding in ejectment is instituted - The tenancy is determined only in conditions, prescribed by s. 9 and in manner provided by s. 14-A - Shadi was a tenant prior to date of institution by petitioner of proceeding in ejectment and he continued to remain a tenant till an order was passed by Asst. Collector on 30-04-1964 - But before that date shadi had exercised his right to purchase land and that right to purchase land would not be defeated merely because on a date subsequent thereto an order in ejectment was passed against him - Shadi, had therefore, at date when he initiated proceeding u/s. 18 right to purchase land - Appeal dismissed.
