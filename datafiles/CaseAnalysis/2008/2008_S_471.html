State Of Andhra Pradesh v M. Madhusudhan Rao

Supreme Court of India

24 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1682; (2008) 15 SCC 582; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 547; JT 2008 (11) SC 454; 2008 (4) RCR(Criminal) 931; 2008(14) SCALE 118; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 1170

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Past Conduct

Summary: IPC, 1860, s. 498A - Appeal against acquittal - Held, for the purpose of s. 498-A harassment simpliciter is not "cruelty" and it is only when harassment is committed for the purpose of coercing a woman or any other person related to her to meet an unlawful demand for property etc., that it amounts to "cruelty" punishable under s. 498-A - It is essential that the delay in lodging the report should be satisfactorily explained - Judgment of the High Court, acquitting the respondent, does not suffer from any infirmity, warranting interference - Appeal dismissed.
