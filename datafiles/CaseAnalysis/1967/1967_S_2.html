Associated Hotels of India Limited, Delhi v S. B. Sardar Ranjit Singh

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 403; AIR 1968 SC 933; 1968 (70) PLR 88; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 548

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi and Ajmer Rent Control Act, 1952, s. 13(1) - (A) Whether appellant is liable to evict premises on ground of sub letting and whether appellant was entitled to protection from eviction u/s. 13? - Held, a room in a hotel is a part of hotel premises - A sub-letting of a room in a hotel in contravention of cls. (b) and (c) of proviso to s. 13(1) is a ground for eviction under Act - S. 2(g) provides that 'premises' does not include 'a room in a hotel or lodging house' - Sub-lessee of a room in a hotel is, therefore, not a tenant and cannot claim protection u/s. 13 from eviction, nor can he ask for fixation of standard rent - The sub lettings of rooms are sufficient grounds of eviction under cls. (b) and (c) of proviso to s. 13(1) - Respondent is entitled to evict appellant on grounds mentioned in cls. (b) (i), (c) (i) and (k) of proviso to s. 13(1) - Appeal dismissed.
