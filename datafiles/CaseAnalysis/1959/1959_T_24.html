Â Indian Hume Pipe Company, Limited v Their Workmen

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Sudhi Ranjan Das, S.K. Das, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 81; AIR 1959 SC 1081; 1959 (2) LLJ 357; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 948

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Rehabilitation, Labour Appellate Tribunal, Available Surplus, Full Bench Formula

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938, s. 10(2) - Constitution of India, 1950,art. 136 -Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Claim of bonus - Disputed - Challenged - Respondents/workers of appellant/ company demanded for payment of six-months wages as bonus for year 1954-55 and matter was referred to Tribunal - Tribunal allowed claim of respondents and ordered for payment of bonus equal to 4 1/2 months basic wages subject to some conditions - Hence, instant appeal by appellant - Whether award of Tribunal was justified - Held, for calculating bonus formula, notional normal depreciation should be deducted from gross profits - Gross profits of appellant was correctly arrived by Tribunal but erred in taking depreciation value taken by IT authorities - Tribunal was right in calculating bonus on an all india basis - Appellant was directed to pay 4 months basic wages as aggregate bonus for year 1954-55 and appellant would get refund of income-tax on bonus paid by it - Order of Tribunal granting bonus for 4 Â½ months was modified to 4 months - Appeal allowed.
