Ministry of Labour and Rehabilitation and Another v Tiffin's Barytes Asbestos and Paints Limited and

Supreme Court of India

16 July 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, V. Balakrishna Eradi, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 400; (1985) 3 SCC 594; (1985) SCC (L&S) 902; AIR 1985 SC 1391; 1985 (2) LLJ 412; 1985(2) SCALE 56; [1985] Supp2 S.C.R. 302

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Minimum Wages Act, 1948, ss. 5(1)(a) and 9 - (A) Labour laws - (1) Interpretation of expression 'independent persons' u/s. 9 - Govt. employees, if included - (2) Representative of employer - If required to be engaged for profits - Held, govt. employees not included - And representative need not be a person engaged for profit in particular employment - (B) Constitution - Notification fixing wages - Scope of interference in writ jurisdiction - Held, notifications fixing minimum wages are not to be lightly interfered with u/art. 226 of the Constitution on the ground of some irregularities in the Constitution of the Committee or in the procedure adopted by the Committee except on the most substantial grounds - Appeals allowed.
