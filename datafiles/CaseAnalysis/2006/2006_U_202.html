State of Kerala v Unni

Supreme Court of India

01 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1021; (2007) 2 SCC 365; AIR 2007 SC 819; 2007(1) ALL MR 476; 2007 (1) KLT 151; 2006(13) SCALE 208; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 931

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Regulation Of Trade, Foreign Material

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, s. 136 - Abkari Act, ss. 5, 56 and 57 - Kerala Rectified Spirit Rules, 1972, s. 2(b) - Abkari Shops (Disposal in Auction) Rules, 2002, r. 9(2) - Manufacture or sale of liquor - Whether High Court in declaring r. 9(2) of Akbari shop rule valid and s. 56(b) of Act as legal are valid and denied of granting license are valid? - Held, licensees are not guilty of any adulteration and renewal of license denied is rightly done - Appeals arising out of Special Leave Petition (Civil) Nos. 3612 of 2005, 6769-75 of 2005 and 7003 of 2006 filed by the State of Kerala are dismissed and Appeals arising out of Special Leave Petition (Civil) Nos. 5130 of 2005, 14189 of 2006 and 18142 of 2006 are allowed without any costs.
