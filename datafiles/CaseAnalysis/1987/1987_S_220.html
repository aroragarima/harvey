Tota Singh And Anr. v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

01 April 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Balakrishna Eradi, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28143; (1987) SCC (Cr) 381; (1987) 2 SCC 529; AIR 1987 SC 1083; 1987 (2) Crimes 54; 1987 CRLJ 974; 1987 CRLR 351; 1987 CrLR(SC) 351; JT 1987 (2) SC 20; 1987(1) SCALE 657; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 747

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Practice and Procedure - CrPC, 1973, ss.378 and 386 - Determination of jurisdiction of the appellate court in dealing with an appeal against an order of acquittal - Held, if case manifest illegality or the conclusion could not be arrived at by any Court acting reasonably and judiciously and is, therefore, liable to be characterised as perverse - But not when two peiws possible - Appeal allowed.
