Wasim Beg v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 March 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1955; (1998) 3 SCC 321; (1998) SCC (L&S) 840; AIR 1998 SC 1291; 1998 (2) AWC 1342; 1998 (2) CLR 160; 1998 (78) FLR 910; JT 1998 (2) SC 354; 1998 LabIC 1233; 1998 (1) LLJ 1209; 1998 (3) LLN 578; 1998(2) SCALE 224; 1998 (2) SLJ 80; 1998 (2) SLR 174; 1998 (3) SLT 702; 1998 (3) Supreme 21; 1998 (2) UPLBEC 1117

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, Sch. 2, Item 6 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16, 14 and 133 - Discharge of confirmed employee - Natural justice - Termination of service - Opportunity of being heard, prescribed by Service Rules - Appellant, in senior managerial position in Corporation - Not working even after interim stay by High Court - Trying to spoil customers of Corporation - Appellant, alleged to have created a situation wherein Corporation should be compelled to accept him or suffer huge losses - Financial condition of Corporation very poor - Held, stay order vacated by HC relying upon affidavit filed by Corporation - Word 'may' used, for opportunity to hear employee, has to be construed as 'shall' - Principles of natural justice are to be complied with.
