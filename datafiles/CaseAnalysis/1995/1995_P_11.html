Secretary To Government of Madras and another v P. R. Sriramulu and another

Supreme Court of India

22 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, S.B. Majmudar, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 855; (1996) 1 SCC 345; AIR 1996 SC 676; 1996 (1) BC 286; JT 1995 (8) SC 305; 1995(6) SCALE 589; [1995] Supp5 S.C.R. 551; 1996 (1) UJ 612

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Banking & Finance; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution of India,1950, sch. 7, list 2, entry 3 - Tamil Nadu Court fees and Suits Valuation Act, 1955, sch. 1, art. 1 - Fee or tax - Determination of impost as fee - Criteria some special service as quid pro quo should be intended to class of citizens which is intended to be benefited by service - General correlation between amount collected and expenses incurred in providing service should exist - Held, aforesaid conditions satisfied - Impost is fee - Collection of amount incidentally and indirectly benefiting general public also - Is irrelevant.
