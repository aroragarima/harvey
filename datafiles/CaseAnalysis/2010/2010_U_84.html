Union of India and others v A. Durairaj (Dead) By Lrs.

Supreme Court of India

01 December 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, P. Sathasivam, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1108; AIR 2011 SC 1084; 2011 (4) AWC 3489; 2011 (128) FLR 1053; JT 2011 (3) SC 254; 2011 (4) MLJ(SC) 345; 2011(1) SCALE 494; 2011 (3) SLR 96

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: (A) Service - Denial of promotion - Delay in challenge - Respondent/Senior Section Engineer in Railway was recommended for ad hoc promotion for the post of Signal and Telecommunication Engineer (ASTE) in 1976, however, medical examination report of respondent showed that he was colour blind and therefore, he was not given ad hoc promotion as ASTE - Respondent appeared for the written test for regular promotion for the post of ASTE in the year 1980 but failed to qualify and was not promoted - Though there were several subsequent examinations for promotion to the post of ASTE, the respondent did not participate -Respondent was promoted to the post of Assistant Works Manager on ad hoc basis in the year 1998 wherein his medical report was favourable to him - Since Assistant Works Manager post was equivalent to the post of ASTE, respondent sought his promotion as ASTE in the year 1999-2000 with retrospective effect from 1976 - Appellant rejected respondent's request - Respondent filed application before the Tribunal - Tribunal dismissed respondent's application holding that if the respondent was aggrieved by his non-promotion as ASTE on ad hoc basis in the year 1976, he should not have kept quiet for more than 23 years and it was not open for him to seek reopening of the issue in the year 1999-2000 - Respondent challenged the Tribunal order before the HC, however, in the meantime, respondent was given promotion to the post of ASTE - HC, by impugned order, directed the appellant to pay a compensation of Rs. 2 lakhs on account of respondent's loss of opportunity and mental agony - Hence the present appeal by appellant/Railway - Whether the claim of the respondent ought to have been dismissed on the ground of delay and laches - Held, on account of the non-challenge, the issue relating to respondent's non- selection in 1976 attained finality and the same issue could not have been reopened in the year 1999-2000, on the ground that medical tests conducted in 1998 and 2000 showed him to be not colour blind - Further, where a claim is raised beyond a decade or two from the date of cause of action, the employer will be at a great disadvantage to effectively contest or counter the claim, as the officers who dealt with the matter and/or the relevant records relating to the matter may no longer be available - Therefore, even if no period of limitation is prescribed, any belated challenge would be liable to be dismissed on the ground of delay and laches - Therefore, HC ought to have affirmed the order of the Tribunal dismissing the application of the respondent for retrospective promotion from 1976, on the ground of delay and laches - Appeal allowed.

(B) Service - Denial of promotion - Negligence of Medical Board - Proof of - Whether there was any negligence or arbitrariness from the part of Medical Board in examining the respondent - Held, respondent contended that the 1976 diagnosis of colour blindness was erroneous and he could not be made to suffer due to the negligence of the then Medical Board - HC found that qualified Medical Board had examined respondent in the year 1976 and rendered a bona fide opinion based on the results of the medical examination - HC also found that the tests conducted in the year 1998 and 2000, disclosed a different condition because of the reason that the equipment used in 1976 was unable to diagnose the extent of colour blindness accurately; and the more sophisticated equipments available in 1998-2000 for testing, made it possible to measure and ascertain the exact extent of colour blindness - However, 2000 test reiterated the 1976 diagnosis that respondent had impaired colour discrimination - Therefore, even if the test report of 2000 had demonstrated that the test report of 1976 was erroneous, it would not be possible to attribute any negligence or carelessness in regard to the earlier medical opinion or report, as subsequent test results were recorded by using equipment based on scientific and technological advances, which were not available at the time of earlier tests - In view of the clear findings by the HC that the medical opinion of 1976 was bona fide and that even if the respondent had been found medically fit in 1976, without passing the written examination in 1980 or thereafter, he could not have been promoted as ASTE on regular basis - Hence, there was no negligence or arbitrariness either in regard to the Medical Examination or in regard to his non-promotion to an ad hoc position in 1976 - Appeal allowed.

(C) Service - Medical unfitness - Denial of promotion - Award of compensation - Legality - On the facts and circumstances, whether the HC was justified in awarding a compensation of Rs. 2 lakhs to the respondent - Held, HC found that respondent was not entitled to promotion - HC also held that what was denied was only an ad hoc promotion and found that the respondent failed in the written examination in the year 1980 and therefore would not have been entitled to regular promotion, even if he was not colour blind - Failure to promote the respondent on ad hoc basis in 1976 had no bearing on his chances of regular promotion - At all events the rejection of respondent's candidature for ad hoc promotion was for justifiable reasons - Therefore, HC was not justified in granting compensation on a vague assumption that respondent had suffered loss of opportunity and mental agony on account of what transpired in 1976 - Sympathy cannot erase the clear principles of law and findings of fact, or the effect of delay and laches - Therefore, impugned HC order is set aside and restored the Tribunal order - Appeal allowed.
