State of West Bengal and Others v Swapan Kumar Guha and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, A. Varadarajan, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 203; (1982) SCC (Cr) 283; (1982) 1 SCC 561; AIR 1982 SC 949; [1983] 53 Comp Cas 114; 1982 (1) CompLJ 217; 1982 CRLJ 819; 1982(1) SCALE 38; [1982] 3 S.C.R. 121

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Criminal

Keywords: Reserve Bank, Speculative

Summary: Constitution, 1950, arts. 136 and 226 - Prize Chits and Money Circulation Schemes (Banning) Act, 1978, Pre., ss. 2(c), 3 and 4 - CrPC. 1974, ss. 154 and 157 - Appeal against decision of HC quashing investigation against a money circulating firm and its partners - FIR not disclosing commission of cognizable offence - (A) Nature and scope of proceeding u/art.226 - HC quashing investigation - Validity - (B) Object of 1978 Act - Money circulation scheme - What is - Determination - Test - Firm indulging in high risk investments - Not illegal - Payment of interest in excess of stipulated rate - (C) Quashing of investigation upheld - When firm likely to secret large funds and destroy incriminating documents if returned to it - Held, HC justified in quashing investigation - Not a scheme for making easy and quick money - In the interest of countless small depositors, documents were directed to be retained by police for two months and Government should be given opportunity to institute inquiry into affairs of firm - Appeals dismissed.
