Yash Ahuja And Others v Medical Council Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

17 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, K.G. Balakrishnan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1546; (2009) 10 SCC 313; 2010 (1) ALD(SC) 125; 2010 (2) AWC 2054; JT 2009 (12) SC 587; 2009(12) SCALE 687; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 667; 2009 (6) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Schedules, Indian Medical Council (Amendment) Act, 2001, Screening Test Regulations, 2002, Eligibility Certificate Regulations, 2002

Summary: Constitution - Education - Indian Medical Council Act, 1956 - Screening Test Regulations, 2002 - Eligibility Certificate Regulations, 2002 - Registration of medical professionals by the Medical Council of India - Foreign medical qualifications - Scheme of reciprocity - Recognition of medical qualifications granted by medical institutions in countries outside India with which scheme of reciprocity exists in India - Qualification of MBBS granted by Kathmandu University was recognized as medical qualification in respect of students of Manipal College of Medical Sciences, Pokhara - Due to lack of uniformity in the standards of medical education in various foreign countries, it was decided to make a provision in the Act to enable the MCI to conduct a screening test - Act was amended and s. 13(4A) of the Act was inserted prescribing screening test in India - Appellants obtained MBBS qualification from Kathmandu University - However, on completion of internship, they were denied permanent registration on the ground that they had not cleared the prescribed screening test - (A) Whether MCI justified in asking the appellants and others who have obtained MBBS qualification from Kathmandu University to qualify the screening test prescribed by the Regulations? - Held, serious aberrations were noticed in the standard of medical education in some of the foreign countries, which were not on par with the standards of medical education available in India - It was, therefore, felt necessary by Parliament to make a provision to enable the Council to conduct a screening test - This is the remedy that s. 13(4A) of the Act has provided - Scope of s. 13(4A) of the Act is quite clear and covers all foreign medical institutions falling within the ambit of ss. 12 and 13 of the Act - MCI is obliged to stipulate the screening test in the case of all those candidates, who obtained medical qualification from medical institutions outside India filling within the purview of ss. 12 and 13 of the Act in view of the statutory provisions of s. 13(4A) of the Act - (B) Retrospective application - Held, provisions of s. 13(4A) of the Act are not being applied retrospectively but from the date specified by the Central Government - Therefore, plea based on retrospective application of s. 13(4A) of the Act cannot be accepted - Appeals dismissed.
