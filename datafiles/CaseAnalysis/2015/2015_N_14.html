Mahila Ramkali Devi and others v Nandram (Dead) Through Lrs. and others

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 362; AIR 2015 SC 2270; 2015 (4) ALD(SC) 108; 2015(4) ALL MR 426; 2015 (111) ALR 471; 2015 (4) AWC 3544; 2015 (4) CLT(SC) 92; 2015 (2) JabLJ 326; JT 2015 (5) SC 248; 2015 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2839; 2015 (5) MLJ 109; 2015 (128) RD 784; 2015(6) SCALE 426; 2016(6) SCALE 426; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 974

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Land Revenue Code, 1959, M.P. Land Revenue Code, 1954, Madhya Bharat Land Revenue and Tenancy Act, M.P. Land Revenue Code (Amendment) Act, 1961

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Madhya Pradesh Land Revenue Code, 1959, ss. 164, 165 - Title to property - Amendment to plaint - Justification - Appellant filed suit for declaration of title and possession of suit property in question on ground that before last holder of property in question died issueless and she had executed Will in favour of appellant no.1 and probate of Will was also stated to have been obtained - Appellants challenged validity of sale deed purported to have been executed by deceased holder in favour of defendant no.3 and sale deed executed in favour of deceased defendant no.1 and deceased defendant no.2 and alleged that defendant nos.1 and 2 were thus in illegal possession of suit property - Trial Court held that Will in favor of appellant no.1 is proved and she had become successor of deceased holder through probate and sale deeds in favor of defendant was not proved, hence, defendants were directed to handover property to appellant - Further, order passed by Trial Court was affirmed by appellant Court - HC held that suit was within period of limitation and there is concurrent finding of both lower Courts that documents were forged, based on evidence of handwriting expert and depositions of witnesses - Further, HC held that deceased holder was not entitled to transfer property by way of Will in view of ss. 164 and 165 of Code - Application for amendment of plaint filed by appellant no.1 to make appellant nos. 2 to 5 fall under Class XVII of Code was rejected HC on ground that same would change nature of suit which was filed 40 years ago, as claim was made solely on basis of Will and not on basis of inheritance - HC allowed appeal vide impugned judgment as appellants had no locus standi to file suit as deceased holder could not have transferred her interest through Will. Hence, present appeal by special leave.

Held, while disposing of appeal, HC has not gone into amended plaint. By amendment, appellant not only sought to add names of appellant nos. 2 to 5 but also sought to make necessary amendment in plaint. SC held that it is well settled that rules of procedure are intended to be handmaid to administration of justice. Party cannot be refused just relief merely because of some mistake, negligence, inadvertence or even infraction of rules of procedure. Court always gives relief to amend pleading of party, unless it is satisfied that party applying was acting malafide or that by his blunder he had caused injury to his opponent which cannot be compensated for by order of cost. Hence, since appellant sought amendment in original plaint, HC ought not to have rejected application. Further, SC held that in view of amendment of plaint, substantial issue no.2, as formulated by HC, needs to be decided by HC afresh. However, substantial question no.1 and 3 decided by HC is affirmed. Matter remanded to lower court. Order accordingly.

Ratio - Amendment to plaint cannot be denied to party to suit when same did not cause injury to opposite party and is not with malafide intention.
