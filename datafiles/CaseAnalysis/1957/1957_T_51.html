W. O. Holdsworth and v State of Uttar

Supreme Court of India

04 September 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, S.K. Das, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 26; AIR 1957 SC 887; [1958] 33 ITR 472; 1958 SCJ 82; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 296

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Agricultural income Tax act, 1948, Trusts Act, 1882

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Trusts & Associations - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Uttar Pradesh Agricultural Income Tax Act, 1948, s. 11(1) - Indian Trusts Act, 1882, s. 3 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Deemed assessee in respect of agricultural income-tax - Manager, receiver, administrator or like liable to pay tax - Legal owners of trust - Trustees- Joint interest - (A)Whether common manager, receiver, administrator or like is deemed to be assessee in respect of agricultural income-tax so payable by each such person and is liable to pay same or not? - Held, that he is deemed to be assessee in respect of agricultural income-tax so payable by each such person and is liable to pay same as per s. 11(1) - Such common manager, receiver, administrator or like would certainly be covered by definition of person contained in s. 2(11) - In so far as he holds land from which agricultural income is derived - (A1)Whether agricultural income-tax is levied on computation of whole agricultural income which has come to hands of common manager, receiver, administrator or like on behalf of agricultural income derived therefrom? - Held, that agricultural income-tax is levied not on computation of whole agricultural income which has come to his hands but is limited to aggregate of sums payable as agricultural income tax by each of persons jointly interested in such land or in agricultural income - Tax payable by each of such persons is computed on actual amount of agricultural income derived from such land and received by him and aggregate of sums payable as agricultural income tax by each of such persons is assessed on such common manager, receiver, administrator or like with result that he pays substantially lower than what he would have otherwise - (B)Whether such common manager receiver, administrator or like holds property on their behalf as agent or representatives and not as owner? - Held, that no doubt he holds trust property for benefit of beneficiaries but he does not hold it on their behalf - Trustees is legal owner of trust property and property vest in him as such - (B1) Whether s. 11(1) come into operation on such common manager, receiver, administrator or like? - Held, that s. 11(1) does not come in to operation - It can only come into operation where land from which agricultural income is derived is held by such common manager, receiver, administrator or like as agent or representative of persons jointly interested in such land or in agricultural income derived therefrom -(C) Whether beneficiaries are also not necessarily persons who are jointly interested in such land or in agricultural income derived therefrom? - Held, that term 'jointly interested' is well known in law and predicates an undivided interest in land or in agricultural income derived - Distinguished from a separate or an individual interest therein - They have a common interest therein, that cannot make that interest a joint interest in land or in agricultural income derived - Appeal dismissed.
