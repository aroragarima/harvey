M/S Speedline Agencies v M/S T. Stanes & Co. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 498; (2010) 6 SCC 257; 2010 (81) ALR 281; [2010] 160 Comp Cas 33; 2010 (6) CompLJ 1; 2010 (4) CTC 573; JT 2010 (6) SC 1; 2010 (2) RCR(Rent) 229; 2010(5) SCALE 670

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Rent Control

Keywords: Companies Act, 1913, Tamil Nadu Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1978

Summary: Rent Control - Corporate - Tamil Nadu Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1960 - Tamil Nadu Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1978 - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 391 and 394 - Landlord-company filed petition u/ss. 10(3)(a)(i) and (iii) of the Tamil Nadu Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1960 against appellant-tenant on the ground that it required the building and premises for their own use and occupation - Rent Controller allowed the petition and directed eviction of the appellant-tenant - Appeal filed thereagainst was dismissed - Appellant-tenant filed revision before HC - During the pendency of said proceeding, by a Scheme of Amalgamation, landlord-company was transferred to respondent u/ss. 391 to 394 of the Companies Act, 1956 and this was duly approved by the HC - HC dismissed the revision filed by the appellant-tenant against order of eviction - Present appeal - Appellant-tenant submitted that upon the amalgamation of the landlord-company with the respondent, the new entity was not entitled to continue the eviction proceedings u/s. 10(3)(a)(i) and (iii) of the Act since the need of the new entity will be different - Held, as per the Scheme of Amalgamation all proceedings in which Transferor Company (landlord-company) was a party be continued, prosecuted and enforced by or against the Transferee Company in the same manner and to the same extent as it would be or might have been continued, prosecuted and enforced by or against the Transferor Company as if the Scheme had not been made - Transferor Company in its entirety merged with the Transferee Company - Further, the amalgamation of the landlord-company with the respondent involved not merely the transfer of the particular leasehold property but the entire business of the landlord-company including the requirement of the leasehold premises for the acquired business - Hence, requirement of landlord-company that it required it for its own business and for residential purposes of its employees continues to exist also for the transferee company since the entire business of the transferor company stood transferred to the transferee company - Requirement of the company has neither been satisfied nor extinguished - Order of eviction eminently just, fair and equitable as ordered by two authorities and confirmed by the HC - No interference required - Appeal dismissed.
