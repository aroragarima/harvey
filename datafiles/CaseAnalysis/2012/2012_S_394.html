FOR EDUCATIONAL USE ONLY

Subramanian Swamy v A. Raja

Supreme Court of India

24 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 251; AIR 2012 SC 3336; 2012 CRLJ 4443; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 175; 2012(7) SCALE 520

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Telecom

Keywords: Access Providers, Bombay Separation of Judicial and Executive Functions (Extension) and the Code of Criminal Procedure (Provision for Uniformity) Act, 1958

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 5 (3), 13 (1) (d) (iii) - Telecom Regulatory Authority of India Act, 1997 - Allocation of spectrum - Criminal conspiracy - Petitioner filed criminal complaint before Trial Court against 'R'/former minister of telecommunications - Special Court took cognizance of case against 'R' - After investigation, CBI disclosed that monetary involvement was much more and charge was laid - Petitioner deposed before Trial Court that 'R'/accused no. 1 could not committed offences alleged against him, but for active connivance of 'C'/former Finance Minister - It was alleged that both 'R' and 'C' were jointly and severely responsible - Hence 'C' was also guilty of commission of offence under 1988 Act for which 'R' was already facing trial - Further, it was also pointed out that 'C' was also guilty of breach of trust on the question of national security for not disclosing that 'E' and 'T' were black-listed by the Home Ministry - Further, there was enough incriminating materials on record for carrying out the investigation against 'C' and for making him an accused in the case - It was also alleged that 'C' had played a vital role in the subversion of the process of issuance of Letter of Intent (LOI), Unified Access Service (UAS) Licences and allocation of spectrum in the year 2007-08 - It was alleged that 'C' was also complicit in fixing the price of spectrum licence at 2001 level and permitting 2 companies, which received licence that was 'S' company and 'U' company and to dilute their shares even before roll-out of their services - Trial Court found no substance in the allegations raised against 'C' and found that he had no role in the subversion of the process of issuance of the LOI, UAS Licences and allocation of spectrum in the year 2007-08 - Trial Court held that there was no evidence on record that he was acting in pursuant to criminal conspiracy, while being party to the 2 decisions regarding non-revision of spectrum pricing and dilution of equity by 2 companies - Consequently, the prayer made for carrying out investigation against 'C' and to make him an accused was rejected by order dt. 4-2-2012 - Hence instant petition - Petitioner contended that Trial Court had failed to notice vital aspects u/s. 13 (1) (d) (iii) of 1988 Act and had wrongly rejected the prayer for conducting investigation against 'C' and to array him as an accused - Further contended that the necessity of conducting a thorough investigation by the CBI into the role of the then Finance Minister 'C' in the matter of fixing the spectrum pricing and allowing the sale of equity by 'S' and 'U' - In that process, 'C' had over-ruled the officers of his own Ministry who favoured auction / market-based pricing of spectrum and instead allowed various companies to make windfall profits - Further, it was also stated that he had allowed the above-mentioned companies to sell off their shares without charging any Govt's. share of its premium on account of spectrum valuation and without enforcing his own agreement with the then Telecom Minister - Further contended that based on facts, 'C' was also equally responsible - Non-revision of spectrum price though specifically recommended by the GoMs in the year 2003 would indicate, that 'C' colluded up with 'R' in non-auctioning of the spectrum and went on for allotment of first come first served basis at 2001 rates - Further, 'C' had not revised his position from giving away 4.4 MHz of spectrum at 2001 prices and giving away 6.2 MHz of spectrum at 2001, thus causing huge loss to the exchequer - 'C' was also instrumental along with 'R' for allowing companies like 'S' and 'U' to sell off their shares without charging any Government's share of its premium - Held, it was clear from the facts that neither 'C' nor the officials of Minister of Finance had any role in the various decisions taken by TRAI on 28-8-2007, decision taken by the Internal Committee of DoT and the decision of the Telecom Commission taken on 10-10-2007 - Further, 'C' had no role in the exchange of those communications or the expression of opinions of the decisions taken between 'R' and the Prime Minister's Office, a situation created by 'R' and the officials of DoT - Neither 'C' nor the officials of the Ministry of Finance did figure in those communications and hence the allegation of involvement of 'C' in the 2G Scam had to be examined in that background - Further, it was also stated in the press release that DoT was implementing a policy of first-come- first-served for grant of UAS licences under which initially an application which was received first would be processed first and thereafter if found eligible would be granted LoI and then whosoever complied with the conditions of LoI first would be granted UAS licence - Materials made available would not indicate any role played by 'C' on the steps taken by 'R' and DoT - On 'C' instructions, Ministry of Finance on 9-1-2008 sent a note to the Prime Minister on 15-1-2008 on spectrum charges - Noticeably, this letter was sent at a time when Finance Secretary's view was rejected by 'R' and the officers of the DoT and that 'R' views were not overturned even by the Prime Minister's Office - Therefore, allegation that the attempt of 'C' was to hide the illegalities in the award of licences was unfounded - Prime Minister's Office had not taken any contrary view to that of 'C' and no materials were also made available when SC was dealing with the case relating to cancellation of licences, wherein UoI was a party - Hence, it was difficult to conclude, on the materials available that 'C' had conspired with 'R' in subverting the process of issuance of LoI, UAS Licences and allocation of spectrum - 'C' and 'R' met on 29-5-2008 and 12-6-2008 for resolving the then outstanding issues relating to the allocation and pricing 2G and 3G Spectrums - Meeting of 2 Ministers would not by itself be sufficient to infer the existence of a conspiracy - Even before those meetings, as instructed by Finance Minister, Finance Secretary and Telecom Secretary had already met on 24-4-2008, had agreed that it might not be possible to charge operators already having allocation upto 6.2 MHz and the principle of equity and level playing field would require that the operators who get fresh allotment of Spectrum upto 6.2MHz for GSM too should not be charged for Spectrum upto 6.2 MHz for GSM - Therefore, the allegation that 'C' had over-ruled his officers' views and had conspired with 'R' was without any basis - There was no materials on record to show that 'C' had abused his position as a Minister of Finance or conspired or colluded with 'R' so as to fix low entry fee by non-visiting spectrum charges fixed in the year 2001 - No materials were also made available even for a prima facie conclusion that 'C' had deliberately allowed dilution of equity of the 2 companies, i.e. 'S' and 'U' - No materials was also available that 'C' had used any corrupt or illegal means for obtaining any pecuniary advantage for himself or any other persons including 'R' - No case was made out to interfere with the order of Trial Court dt. 4-2-2012 - Order accordingly.
