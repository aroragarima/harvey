Industrial Finance Corporation of India Limited v Cannanore Spinning and Weaving Mills Limited and

Supreme Court of India

12 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 250; (2002) 5 SCC 54; AIR 2002 SC 1841; 2002 (3) ALD(SC) 113; 2002 (2) ARBLR 676; 2002 (2) BC 430; [2002] 110 Comp Cas 685; 2002 (4) CompLJ 337; JT 2002 (4) SC 318; 2002 (3) MLJ(SC) 144; 2002(3) SCALE 574; 2002 (3) SCJ 102; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 1093; 2002 (3) SLT 185; 2002 (3) Supreme 427

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Textiles; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Principal Debtor, Contract Of Guarantee, Impossibility Of Performance

Summary: Indian Contract Act- Section 141- Whether surety will be entitled to every remedy which the creditor has against the principal debtor- Held, surety is entitled to the securities given to the creditor, both before and after the contract of surety. In case contract stands dissipated then there is cessation of liability to the extent of such dissipation or extinction.
