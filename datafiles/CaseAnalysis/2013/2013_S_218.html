Shyam Narain v State Of Nct Of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 333; (2013) 7 SCC 77; AIR 2013 SC 2209; 2013 CRLJ 3009; JT 2013 (9) SC 42; 2013 (2) OLR 735; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 102; 2013(7) SCALE 392; [2013] 8 S.C.R. 951

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376(2)(f) - Rape - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant accused allegedly committed offence punishable u/s. 376(2)(f) IPC - Trial Court convicted appellant u/s. 376(2)(f) IPC and sentenced him to undergo rigorous imprisonment for life - Aggrieved, appellant preferred appeal before HC, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Trial Court was justified in convicting the appellant - Held, it was an assault on individuality and inherent dignity of a woman with mindset that she should be elegantly servile to men - Rape was a monstrous burial of her dignity in darkness - It was a crime against holy body of a woman and soul of society and such a crime was aggravated by manner in which it had - In instant case, victim was 8 year old girl who possibly would be deprived of dreams of 'Spring of Life' and might be psychologically compelled to remain in 'Torment of Winter' when she suffers, collective at large also suffers - Such a singular crime created an atmosphere of fear which was historically abhorred by society - It demands just punishment from Court and to such a demand, Courts of law were bound to respond within legal parameters - It was a demand for justice and award of punishment had to be in consonance with legislative command and discretion vested in Court - Trial Court and HC were justified in convicting the appellant - Appeal dismissed.
