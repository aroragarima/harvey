Shaji K. Joseph v V. Viswanath and others

Supreme Court of India

22 February 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Anil R. Dave, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 176; (2016) 4 SCC 429; AIR 2016 SC 1094; 2016 (117) ALR 5; 2016 (3) AWC 2718; 2016 (3) Bom.C.R. 322; JT 2016 (2) SC 404; 2016(2) SCALE 614; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 65

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Dentists Act, 1948, Dental Council (Election) Regulations, 1952

Summary: Election - Dentists Act, 1948, ss. 3, 3(a), 5 - Dental Council (Election) Regulations, 1952, reg.20 - Rejection of Nomination - Direction for Fresh Election Challenged - Respondent no.1 nomination paper to contest in election as member to Council was rejected by respondent no.3/Returning officer - Single Judge in writ petition set aside order of returning officer and directed to conduct fresh election after including name of respondent no.1 - DB of HC dismissed appeal of appellant and upheld order of Single Judge - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, once the election process starts, it would not be proper for the courts to interfere with the election process. In instant case, the HC was not right in interfering with the process of election especially when the process of election had started upon publication of the election program and more particularly when an alternative statutory remedy was available to respondent no.1 by way of referring the dispute to the Central Government as per the provisions of s. 5 of the Act r/w. reg. 20 of the Regulations. Hence, the impugned judgment is set aside and direct that the result of the election should be published. Appeal allowed.
