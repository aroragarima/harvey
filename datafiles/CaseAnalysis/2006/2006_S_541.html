Arvinder Singh Bains v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 828; (2006) 6 SCC 673; (2006) SCC (L&S) 1523; AIR 2006 SC 2265; JT 2006 (11) SC 553; 2006 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1697; 2006(6) SCALE 339; 2006(6) SCALE 358; 2006 (7) SCJ 88; 2006 (4) SLR 798; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 886; 2006 (3) UPLBEC 2946

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - Administrative - Constitution - Constitution of India,1950 - Punjab Civil Services (Executive Branch) (Class I) Rules,1976, rr. 7,8,12,14,18,21 - Recruitment - Seniority list - Challenged - Validity - State Govt. advertised in 1976 and in 1980 - Appellant and respondent no.3 were selected by Punjab Public Service Commission from 1980 advertisement - Promotees from other Registers (other than Register-B) were appointed as PCS in 1984 to 85 - In 1988 tentative seniority list of selected candidates and direct recruitments (Register-B) was prepared and circulated which was objected by appellant - On 1-9-1994 final seniority list of Register-B candidates was prepared and circulated - Aggrieved appellant and respondent No.3 filed writ petition in HC challenging seniority list and was dismissed by Single Judge holding that there was nothing in Rules from which it could be inferred that candidates from various services were required to be selected simultaneously and that seniority could not be linked to year of vacancy - Aggrieved appellant preferred appeal and was dismissed by DB - Hence instant appeal - Whether Orders of HC were justified - Held, disputes related to seniority arose out of posts of 1978 to 1982 - Candidates of Registers A-1, A-II, A-III and C were appointed in 1984 and candidates of Register-B were appointed in 1986 - Appointments were to be made when names have been entered in all registers -and actual appointment was by virtue of r.18 - Direct recruits have precedence over others including seniority also - Treating joining date as basis of determining seniority would lead to discretion - SC in Mervyn Coutindo & Ors Vs. Collector of Customs Bombay & Ors, 1966 Indlaw SC 6 held that it was mandatory to apply Rota and Quota in determining seniority when provided under rules - Action of authorities was based on mis interpretation of provisions of r. 21 of the 1976 Rules and was therefore liable to be set aside - Rota and quota must necessarily be reflected in seniority list - Action of State Govt. was contrary to the 1976 Rules and seniority must be based on collective interpretation of rr.18 and 21 of the 1976 Rules - Directions issued to respondent/State Govt.to prepare fresh seniority list of appellants who belong to PCS (EB) in accordance with rr.18 and 21 of the 1976 Rules by fixing seniority according to roaster and to grant all consequential benefits - Appeal allowed.
