Anoop Jaiswal v Government Of India And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

24 January 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 99; (1984) SCC (L&S) 256; (1984) 2 SCC 369; AIR 1984 SC 636; 1984 (48) FLR 258; 1984 LabIC 343; 1984 (1) LLJ 337; 1984 (1) LLN 479; 1984(1) SCALE 105; [1984] 2 S.C.R. 453; 1984 (1) SLJ 428; 1984 (1) SLR 426; 1984 UJ 925

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Punjab Civil Services (Judicial Branch) Rules, Indian Penal Code (Probation) Rules 1954

Summary: Service - Indian Police Service (Probation) Rules, 1954 r.12(b) - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 14, 311(2) - Service - Termination - Challenged - Appellant was selected by UPSC for appointment in Indian police service and was undergoing training as probationer - All probationers got delayed for training due to heavy rain and appellant was held responsible as he was one of ring leaders - Appellant gave his explanation to Director of academy and without holding enquiry, recommended Govt. that appellant should be discharged from service and order of discharge was passed u/r. 12(b) of Rules - Aggrieved appellant made representation to Govt. which was rejected - Appellant filed writ before HC contending order of discharge was violating provisions u/arts.14, 311(2) of Constitution, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, Director of academy wished to make the case of the appellant an example for others including those other probationers who were similarly situated so that they may learn a lesson there from. Further, art. 311(2) of the Constitution was attracted and as the impugned order amounts to a termination of service by way of punishment and an enquiry should have been held in accordance with the said constitutional provision had admittedly having not been done, the impugned order is liable to be struck down. Accordingly the judgment of the HC and the impugned order discharging the appellant from service is set aside. Thus, appellant should now be reinstated in service with the same rank and seniority he was entitled to before the impugned order was passed as if it had not been passed at all and he is also entitled to all consequential benefits including the appropriate year of allotment and the arrears of salary and allowances upto the date of his reinstatement. Appeal allowed.
