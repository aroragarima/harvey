Â Chief Commissioner, Ajmer v Radhey Shyam Dani

Supreme Court of India

15 November 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 59; AIR 1957 SC 304; 1957 SCJ 191; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 68

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Representation of the People Act, Constitution (Fifth Amendment) Act, 1955, Ajmer State Municipalities Election rules 1955, Ajmer Merwara Municipalities regulation 1925 (6 of 1925)

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Election - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133(1)(c), 136 - Ajmer Merwara Municipalities regulation 1925 , r. 6 - Ajmer State Municipalities Election rules 1955 - Electoral roll - Rectification - Sustainability - In view of impending elections after period of suspension was over appellant framed Ajmer State Municipalities Election Rules, 1955 and published them in Govt. Gazette - Appellant notified election programme and also authenticated and published electoral roll - electoral roll had been corrected and altered by orders of Sub-Divisional Officer on certain days but respondent's name was alleged to have been incorrectly described - Respondent applied for correction and nomination was rejected - Respondents application for rectification of mistake in Parliamentary Electoral Roll was also rejected - Respondent filed petition in Court of Judicial Commissioner against appellant and District Magistrate for mandamus against appellant - Judicial Commissioner upheld contention of respondent - Application made by appellant for certificate u/art. 133(1)(c) of Constitution - Judicial Commissioner rejected application - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Judicial Commissioner restraining District Magistrate from holding elections and poll to Ajmer Municipal Committee on 9-9-1955 was justified - Held, electoral roll of Ajmer Municipality which was authenticated and published by Chief Commissioner on 8-8-1955, was not in conformity with provisions of s. 30(2)?? and relevant provisions of Regulation and could not form basis of any valid elections to be held to Ajmer Municipal Committee - Appeal dismissed.
