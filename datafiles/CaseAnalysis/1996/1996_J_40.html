Union of India and Others v Jain Shudh Vanaspati Limited and Anr

Supreme Court of India

08 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 188; (1996) 10 SCC 520; AIR 1996 SC 2696; 1996 (66) ECR 666; 1996 (86) E.L.T. 460; 1996(5) SCALE 873; [1996] Supp4 S.C.R. 418

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, ss. 28, 47 and 124 - (A) Issue of show cause notice u/s. 28 for duty not levied - Same will be issued only after clearance u/s. 47 - Is it necessary that order u/s. 47 has to be first revised before show cause notice u/s. 28 is issued? - (B) Show cause notice to importer for duties not levied and for confiscation of goods - Alleged in show cause notice for confiscation that oil was imported in stainless steel containers which are borrowed - Further allegation that containers were so painted as to resemble mild steel - High Court quashing notices - Whether proper? - Held, (A) Not necessary - (B) Not proper - HC should not have stultified investigation still at show cause stage, by going into facts - Same could not be but with intention to slip them through customs - HC should have taken note - Appeal allowed.
