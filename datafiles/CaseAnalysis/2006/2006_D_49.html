Messrs Indian Drugs and Pharmaceuticals Limited v Devki Devi and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 293; (2006) 5 SCC 523; AIR 2006 SC 2691; 2006 (7) AWC 7188; 2007 (4) CompLJ 73; JT 2006 (6) SC 136; 2006(6) SCALE 559; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 279; 2006 (3) UPLBEC 2365

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(p), 6N - Industrial Disputes (Central) Rules, 1957, r. 58 - If inference as to settlement can be drawn in the circumstances of the case? - Workers' Union of appellant company took up cause of widows and dependents of employees who died in harness and demanded that they should be given employment in company - Management agreed to consider Union's suggestion sympathetically and certain job works were given on contract basis - On bare reading of minutes of meeting it was clear that there was in fact no settlement - Cost of contract, nature of work and time allowed had been clearly indicated in each of contracts - There was no material to conclude that contract was not a job contract and in fact employment had been given - Consequently termination of contract on account of poor financial condition of company, would not amount to retrenchment - Appeals allowed.
