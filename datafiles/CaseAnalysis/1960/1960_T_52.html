Â Tobacco Manufacturers (India) Limited v Commissioner of Sales Tax, Bihar, Patna

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 394; AIR 1961 SC 402; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 106; 1961 (12) STC 87

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Sales Tax

Keywords: Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1959

Summary: Administrative - Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Constitution -Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 286 - (A) When is subordinate Tribunal not under duty to carry out directions given by superior tribunal? - Held, as a result of order passed by superior tribunal, subordinate tribunal is directed to give effect to a particular judgment of and interpretation is left to subordinate tribunal - In such a situation subordinate court not under duty to carry out directions given by superior tribunal - (B) What is meaning of term 'Explanation Sales' in art. 286 of Constitution in relation to imposition of tax? - Held, sales of goods delivered for consumption outside State of Bihar which involved a tax liability were made up of two types of transactions (a) those in which goods thus delivered were for consumption in State of first delivery or first destination and (b) those in which goods thus delivered were for consumption, not in State of first delivery but in other States - Claim was that on proper construction of art. 286(1) and (2) they were entitled to have both these types of sales excluded from their taxable turnover under Bihar Sales Tax Act - Further held, (i) that sales of type (a) would be exempt from levy of tax under Bihar Sales-tax Act by reason of their being 'inside' sales within State of delivery-cum-consumption and therefore being 'outside' sales - State of Bihar (ii) that sales of type (b) would be liable to levy of tax in State of Bihar and tax levied on those sales would continue to be valid - Appeal dismissed.
