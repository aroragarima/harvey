N. V. Srinivasa Murthy v N. V. Gururaja Rao

Supreme Court of India

10 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 2095; (2005) 10 SCC 566

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Second appeal - Framing substantial question of law - HC interfered with the concurrent findings recorded by the courts below without framing a substantial question of law as enjoined by s. 100 of CPC - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, HC can exercise its jurisdiction u/s. 100 of CPC in second appeal only on the basis of substantial question of law framed at the time of admitting the appeal and the second appeal has to be heard and decided only on the basis of such duly framed substantial question of law, if any. Judgment rendered by the HC in second appeal without following the aforesaid procedure is not sustainable in law. In the instant case, the HC has assumed jurisdiction u/s. 100 of CPC without framing a substantial question of law, and interfered with the concurrent findings recorded by the courts below. The impugned order of the High Court is set aside and the case is remitted back to the HC for disposal afresh in accordance with law. Appeal allowed.
