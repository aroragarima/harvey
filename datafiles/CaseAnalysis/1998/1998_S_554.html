Union of India and Others v Subedar Ram Narain and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 September 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, G.T. Nanavati, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1195; (1998) 8 SCC 52; AIR 1998 SC 3225; JT 1998 (6) SC 383; 1999 (2) SLJ 278; 1998 (5) SLR 1; [1998] Supp1 S.C.R. 616

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Court Martial

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Constitution - Army Act, 1950, ss. 40(a), 48, 71, 73 - Enrollment - Indian Army - General Court Martial - Dismissal - Pensionary benefits - Eligibility - Respondent enrolled in Indian Army on in 1962 and was promoted to rank of Subedar Major in March 1984 - While serving, he was kept in close arrest with effect from 17-11-1988 and was court-martialed under provisions of 1950 Act - Respondent was charged u/s. 40(a) of the 1950 Act, using criminal force against his superior officer, and s. 48 of 1950 Act for being in state of intoxication while on duty - General Court Martial found respondent guilty and subsequently respondent was dismissed from service on 1-8-1989 - Respondent preferred appeal against said Order to chief of army staff and same was rejected - Respondent filed petition in HC for grant of pensionary benefits - HC concluded that as Court martial had not passed order depriving respondent of pensionary benefits, he would be entitled to same despite dismissal from service - Hence instant appeal by Union Govt - Whether respondent, who was junior commissioned officer, would be ineligible for pensionary benefits in respect of his previous service on his being dismissed under Army Act - Held, first sentence of regn. 113(a) army pension regulations would clearly provide that individual who was dismissed under provisions of Army Act would be ineligible for pension or gratuity in respect of all previous service - When punishment of dismissal was inflicted u/s. 71(e) of the 1950 Act, provisions of regn. 113(a) army pension regulations would be attracted - Result of punishment was that benefit of pension or gratuity which was given under the regn would be taken away - Order of dismissal under provisions of the Act in case of employee like respondent would make him ineligible for pension or gratuity - For person to be eligible to grant of pension or gratuity it would be imperative that he should not have been dismissed from service - Dismissal under provisions of the Act was, therefore, disqualification for getting pension or gratuity - Appeals allowed.
