Subhash Kumar v State Of Bihar And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

09 January 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, N.D. Ojha

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 913; (1991) 1 SCC 598; AIR 1991 SC 420; AIR 1991 SCW 121; 1991 (1) CompLJ 209; JT 1991 (1) SC 77; 1991(1) SCALE 8; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 5; 1991 (1) UJ 533

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 and 32 - Prevention of pollution - (A) Maintainability of public interest litigation - (B) Locus standi - Held, (A) Can be filed for ensuring enjoyment of pollution free water and air which is included in right to live u/art. 21 - (B) Direction issued for recourse to proceeding, however, can be by person genuinely interested in protecting society - Same cannot be invoked by person or group of persons for vindication of his grudge or enmity for or enforcing personal interest - Petition dismissed.
