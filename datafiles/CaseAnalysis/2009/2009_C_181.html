Mcorp Global Private Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Ghaziabad

Supreme Court of India

12 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 175; (2009) 3 SCC 420; [2009] 309 ITR 434; JT 2009 (3) SC 490; 2009(2) SCALE 538; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 213

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Leasing Business, Leasehold Rights

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 - High Court held that the appellant (assessee) is not entitled to claim depreciation under s. 32(1)(ii) in respect of two separate transactions dated 15.2.1991 and 15.3.1991 - Held, AO had granted depreciation in respect of 42, 000 bottles out of the total number of bottles (5, 46, 000), by reason of the impugned judgment - That benefit is sought to be taken away by the Department, which is not permissible in law - AO was right in coming to the conclusion that transaction dated 15.3.1991 was not proved and that the assessee was not entitled to claim depreciation of Rs. 30, 17, 122/- in respect of the second transaction - Appeal partly allowed.
