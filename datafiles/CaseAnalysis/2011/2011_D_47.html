Diljit Singh Bedi v Shiromani Gurudwara Prabhandhak Committee, Sri Amritsar

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 303; (2011) 5 SCC 417; AIR 2011 SC 1633; 2011 (129) FLR 725; JT 2011 (5) SC 442; 2011 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2003; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 860; 2011(5) SCALE 273; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 1048; 2011 (3) SLR 703

Case Digest

Subject: Service; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Sikh Gurdwaras (Supplementary) Act, 1925

Summary: Service - Trusts & Associations - Sikh Gurdwaras Act, 1925, s. 69 - Termination - Challenged - A news item with incriminating photographs showing appellant, Asst. Secretary of Shiromani Gurudwara Prabhandhak Committee (SGPC), with a woman published in local newspaper - The SGPC (Respondent) constituted a Sub-Committee to hold an inquiry against the appellant - In said inquiry appellant submitted that the said photographs were of himself and his wife - Sub-Committee accepted the explanation of the appellant and recommended that the appellant be reinstated in his post - On the basis of the inquiry report of the Sub-Committee, the Executive Committee of the SGPC resolved to reinstate the appellant in service - However, appellant submitted his resignation and the resignation was accepted by the President of the SGPC - Subsequently, appellant made a representation complaining that his resignation was obtained by coercion - President of the SGPC cancelled the accepting of resignation of the appellant and relieved appellant from service on the ground that the Executive Committee had not confirmed the order to reinstate the appellant in service - Appellant filed writ petition challenging the order of the President of the SGPC terminating his services - HC dismissed said petition - Hence, the instant appeal - (A) Whether the appellant had resigned from the post of Assistant Secretary of the SGPC or whether his services were terminated by the Executive Committee of the SGPC? - Held, as per s. 69 of the Act, only the Executive Committee of the SGPC had the statutory power to remove any employee of the SGPC - Therefore, the acceptance of the resignation of the appellant by the President of the SGPC was of no legal consequence - Hence, instant case was not a case of resignation from service by the appellant but of termination of service of the appellant by the Executive Committee of the SCPC - (B) Whether the termination of service of the appellant by the Executive Committee of the SGPC by resolution was legally valid? - Held, an employee of the SGPC can be dismissed from service for bad character only after the charges of misconduct were established in an inquiry conducted by an inquiry committee - In the instant case, the Sub- Committee constituted by the Executive Committee of the SGPC had accepted the explanation of the appellant that the photographs which were published in the local newspapers were of his wife - Thus, without a finding in an inquiry that the appellant was guilty of conduct which had defamed the SGPC, the HC could not have taken a view in the impugned order that the appellant had brought a bad name to the SGPC and he had been rightly relieved from service - The order of the SGPC terminating the services of the appellant was, therefore, not legally valid and was accordingly quashed - Impugned order of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
