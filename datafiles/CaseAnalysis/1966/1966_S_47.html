Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v Sri Rajendra Mills Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

25 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 214; AIR 1967 SC 819; 1967 (1) AnWR 76; 1967 (1) ITJ 316; [1967] 63 ITR 609; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 934

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 42(1) - Transfer - Sale - Profits - Taxable income - Respondents were public limited companies - Each of assessee companies had branch at Pudukottai engaged in production and sale of cotton yarn - Sale proceeds of branches were periodically deposited in branch of Madurai Bank Ltd - Three assessee companies borrowed moneys from Madurai branch of bank and on security of fixed deposits made by their branches with Pudukottai branch of Bank - Assessment proceedings of assessee companies for various years under dispute - Income Tax Officer included entire profits and interest receipts from Pudukottai branches and overdrafts availed by assessee companies in British India far exceeded available profits - Hence instant appeal - Whether interest earned on fixed deposits was taxable income - Held, Income Tax authorities were right in holding that entire interest earned on fixed deposits was taxable - Pudukottai branch of Bank had transmitted funds deposited by assessee companies for enabling Madurai branch to advance loans at interest to assessee companies and transmission of funds was made with knowledge of assessee companies who were major shareholders of the Bank - Entire transactions formed part of basic arrangement or scheme between creditor and debtor that money should be brought into British India after it was taken by borrower outside taxable territory - Appeal allowed.
