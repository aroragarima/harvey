M. M. T. C. Limited and Another v Medchl Chemicals and Pharma Private Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 November 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20378; (2002) SCC (Cr) 121; (2002) 1 SCC 234; AIR 2002 SC 182; AIR 2001 SCW 4793; 2002 (46) ALR 190; 2002 (1) AWC 80; 2002 (1) BC 280; 2005 (4) BC 59; 2002 (1) Bom.C.R. 218; 2001 (4) CCR 316; [2002] 108 Comp Cas 48; 2002 (1) CompLJ 58; 2002 (1) Crimes 156; 2002 (2) CRJ 573; 2002 CRLJ 266; 2002 (1) JCC 15; JT 2001 (9) SC 563; 2002 (1) RCR(Criminal) 318; 2001(8) SCALE 191; 2001 (8) SLT 83; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 265; 2001 (8) Supreme 227; 2002 (1) UC 104

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal

Keywords: Dishonour of cheques

Summary: Banking & Finance - Criminal - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138 and 142 - (A) Who can lodge complaint u/s. 138? - As per s. 142 complaint can be made by payee or holder in due course of cheque - Anyone can set criminal law in motion by filing complaint - Court cannot decline to take cognizance - If special provision for taking cognizance prescribed under a statute, complainant must satisfy such eligibility criterion - Also, different persons can represent a company in course of proceedings - In present case criteria u/s. 142 satisfied as complaint is in name and on behalf of complainant Company - Held, complaint could not have been quashed by High Court - (B) Whether HC could have decided about reliability or genuineness of allegations made in complaint at stage of hearing and quash petition? - At initial stage of proceedings HC not justified in entertaining and accepting plea on merits of case - Burden of proving that there was no existing debt or liability is on accused, which is to be discharged in trial - Further held, at this stage, HC could not have concluded that there was no existing debt or liability - (C) Whether a complaint u/s. 138 could only be maintained only if cheque dishonored for reason of insufficient funds or if cheque amount exceeds amount in account? - Offence u/s. 138 made out even when cheque dishonored by reason of 'stop - payment' instruction - Presumption u/s. 139 attracted in such case also - Further held, complaint cannot be quashed on this ground - Appeals disposed of.
