Arvind Mills Limited v Messrs Associated Roadways

Supreme Court of India

12 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 934; (2004) 11 SCC 545; AIR 2004 SC 5147; 2004 CCC 17; 2007 (2) CPJ(Sc) 1; 2004 (4) MLJ(SC) 109; 2005(5) SCALE 319

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Consumer Protection

Summary: Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Carriers Act, 1865, ss. 9 and 10 - Whether complaint in absence of notice u/s. 10 of Carriers Act, 1865 can be entertained under Consumer Protection Act, 1986? - Fact that remedies under Consumer Protection Act are in addition to and not in derogation of any other law does not mean that title rights under Carriers Act can be exercised, except in accordance with manner provided under Act - Ss. 9 and 10 form an integral scheme by which a common carrier is fastened with liability irrespective of proof of negligence - Merely because procedure under Consumer Protection Act is summary in nature, does not in any way warrant abrogation of requirement to serve notice u/s. 10 of Carriers Act before fastening any liability under that Act on carriers - Appeal dismissed.
