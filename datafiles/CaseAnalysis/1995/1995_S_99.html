State of Rajasthan v Sevanivatra Karamchari Hitkari Samiti

Supreme Court of India

03 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 599; (1995) 2 SCC 117; (1995) SCC (L&S) 415; AIR 1995 SCW 1469; 1995 (29) ATC 199; 1995 (1) CLR 422; 1995 (70) FLR 434; JT 1995 (1) SC 315; 1995 LabIC 1628; 1995(1) SCALE 40; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 8; 1995 (1) SLJ 199; 1995 (2) SLR 121; 1995 (1) UJ 329

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Computation Of Pension, Eligibility - Cut Off Date

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Central Civil Services(Pension) Rules, 1972 - Rajasthan Service Rules, 1951, r. 268-H - Whether r. 268-H of the Rajasthan Service Rules was discriminatory between two sets of Govt. employees entitled to get family pension only on the basis of such Govt. servant's remaining in service on 29-02-1964 and thereafter? - Held, the Govt. had taken into consideration the need for a liberalised pension scheme for those Govt. servants who were in service on 29-02-1964 and who would be retiring thereafter - It is not necessary to go into the question as to whether the liberalised benefit for pension should have also been accorded to the Govt. servants retiring prior to 29-02-1964 because such exercise being a matter of policy decision for the executive, must be left to the consideration of the State Govt. - It is permissible to introduce different retiral benefit schemes for Govt. servants - R. 268-H cannot, therefore, be held violative of art. 14 of the Constitution - Appeal allowed.
