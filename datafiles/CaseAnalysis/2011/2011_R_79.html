State Of Kerala v Raneef

Supreme Court of India

03 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 1; (2011) 1 SCC 784; 2011 CRLJ 982; 2011 (1) JCC 293; JT 2011 (1) SC 10; 2011 (1) KLT 242; 2011 (3) MLJ(Crl) 761; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 381; 2011(1) SCALE 8; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 590

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: McCarran Act, 1950

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 202 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 173 - Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act, 1967, s. 43D(5) - Grant of bail - Legality - Prosecution case was that respondent, a medical practitioner, in pursuance of a previous plan medically treated one of the wounded accused who was injured while assaulting the victim - It was also alleged that respondent, along with the other accused was a member of the Popular Front of India (PFI) - Bail was granted to respondent - Hence, present appeal - Prosecution relied on the proviso to s. 43D(5) of the Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act which states that the accused shall not be released on bail if the Court is of the opinion that there are reasonable grounds for believing that the accusation against such person is prima facie true - Whether respondent entitled to be released on bail? - Held, case against the respondent was very different from that against the alleged assailants - There was no allegation that the respondent was one of the assailants - At this stage there was no prima facie proof that the respondent was involved in the crime - Hence the proviso to s. 43D(5) not been violated - Further, respondent, being a doctor, was under the oath to attempt to heal a patient - However, only offence that can be leveled against the respondent was that u/s. 202 of IPC, that is, of omitting to give information of the crime to the police, and this offence also to be proved beyond reasonable doubt - Also, there was no evidence that the PFI was a terrorist organization, and hence the respondent cannot be penalized merely for belonging to the PFI - There was no reason why he should be denied bail - Appeal dismissed.
