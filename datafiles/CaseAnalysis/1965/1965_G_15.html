Gurucharan Das Chadha v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 206; AIR 1966 SC 1418; 1976 (2) ALT(SC) 222; 1966 CRLJ 1071; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 678

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Indirect Tax; Service; Socio-Economic

Summary: Advocated & Judges -Contempt of Courts Act, 1952, s. 1 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898 - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1947 - Criminal Law Amendment Act, 1952 - All India Services (Conduct) Rules, 1954 - Contempt of Court - Transfer petition u/s. 527, CrPC, 1898 pending in Supreme Court - Whether Supreme Court can transfer a case from a Special Judge subordinate to one High Court to another Special Judge subordinate to another HC ? - Held, Supreme Court in exercise of its jurisdiction and power u/s. 527, CrPC, 1898 can transfer a case from a Special Judge subordinate to one HC to another Special Judge subordinate to another HC - Whether transfer of instant case is to be granted? - Nothing had been said by petitioner which would show that there was in any manner an interference direct or indirect with investigation of offences alleged against him or trial of case before Special Judge - A general feeling that some persons were hostile to petitioner was not sufficient - There must be material from which it could be inferred that persons who were so hostile were interfering or were likely to interfere either directly or indirectly with course of justice -Of this there was no tract - Nor did petitioner allege anything against Special Judge who was trying case - Further held, in this view of matter court would decline to order transfer of case from Special Judge - Petition dismissed.
