Noorahammad and others v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 85; (2016) 3 SCC 325; AIR 2016 SC 679; 2016 ALL MR (Cri) 859; 2016 (4) CalLT 40; 2016 CRLJ 1232; 2016 (2) JCC 974; JT 2016 (1) SC 582; 2016 (2) KarLJ 177; 2016 (1) RCR(Criminal) 961; 2016(2) SCALE 104; 2016 (2) WLN(SC) 30

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Prosecution Witness, Identification Parade, Prosecution Evidence, Test Identification Parade, Identity, Distance, Identification In Court, Karnataka Forest Act, 1963, Conjectures

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 324, 353, 379, 411 - Karnataka Forest Act, 1963, s. 24(e) - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Test identification parade - Trial Court conducted trial against appellants for offences punishable u/ss. 302, 324, 353, 379 and 411 of IPC read with s. 24(e) of the Act and upon analyzing evidence, acquitted all appellants from charges leveled against them - Aggrieved, State preferred appeal before HC and vide impugned judgment, HC convicted all appellants for offences punishable u/ss. 304 part II, 324, 353, 379 and 411 read with s. 34 of IPC - Hence, instant appeal by appellants praying for acquittal - Whether prosecution evidence established beyond reasonable doubt commission of offences by appellants u/ss. 304 part II, 324, 353, 379 and 411 of IPC read with s. 34 of IPC.

Held, evidence on record clearly showed the material contradictions and discrepancies in the prosecution evidence especially the testimonies of PWs 6,8,1. Evidence adduced by prosecution to support its version does not prove beyond reasonable doubt the offences leveled against appellants. Further, PWs identified appellants in court for the first time, during trial, in 1997-98 and incident occurred in year 1995. Hence, considering undisputed facts like occurrence of forest officers, except one present at the place of incident, test identification parade (TIP) should have been carried out at the instance of IO. Hence, identification of appellants by PWs for first time after gap of more than 2 years from date of incident is not beyond reasonable doubt. Material contradictions in prosecution evidence cast a shadow of doubt upon the prosecution story and render the same unreliable and not trustworthy in the eyes of law, which HC has failed to appreciate. Hence, judgment and order of HC is set aside and all appellants are acquitted. Appeal allowed.
