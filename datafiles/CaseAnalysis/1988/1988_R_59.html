Union of India and Others v Ranjit Thakur

Supreme Court of India

20 January 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, M.N. Venkatachaliah

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 331; (1988) Supp SCC 525; (1988) SCC (Cr) 915; 1988 CRLR 793

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Challenged, Dismissal from service

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Indian Army Act, 1950, s. 130 - Dismissal from service - Challenged - Held, prima facie with some justification was that Court-Martial was a 'Summary Court-Martial' and not 'Summary General Court-Martial' and that to former, the provisions of s. 130 of the Act were not attracted. However, even then if the point taken in the review petition as to the inapplicability of s. 130 of the Act is held to be well founded, the decision, however, appears supportable on findings on contentions. But the error, if any, as so the inapplicability of s. 130 of the Act should not be left uncorrected. Order accordingly.
