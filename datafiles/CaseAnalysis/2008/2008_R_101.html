Mahesh Yadav & Anr. v Rajeshwar Singh & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2029; (2009) 2 SCC 205; AIR 2009 SC 1064; 2009(2) ALL MR 422; 2009 (75) ALR 187; 2009 (2) AWC 1361; 2009 (1) CTC 638; 2009 (3) MPLJ 297; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 422; 2009 (107) RD 55; 2009 (1) RLW 688; 2008(16) SCALE 192; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 726

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Review Application, Decree In Original Suit

Summary: Suit for declaration of title and possession - Held, High Court may not be correct in holding that only because a joint written statement was filed, an application for ex parte decree was not maintainable - Evidence adduced on behalf of the appellants were not analysed for arriving at a finding as to whether a case for setting aside an ex parte decree has been made out by the appellants or not - An order setting aside the ex parte decree being a judicial order should have been supported by reasons - Judge could not have allowed the application without following the legal principles on the basis whereof such an order could be passed - Appeal allowed.
