Jamiruddin Ansari v Central Bureau Of Investigation & Anr

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 759; (2009) 6 SCC 316; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 1033; AIR 2009 SC 2781; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 2790; 2009 CRLJ 3961; 2011 (5) RCR(Criminal) 743; 2009(7) SCALE 263; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 759

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Private Party

Summary: Criminal - Maharashtra Control of Organised Crime Act, 1999, ss. 9(1)(4), 23(2) and 25 - Whether an investigation could be ordered by the Special Court constituted under MCOCA, save and except in accordance with s. 23(1) of MCOCA, and interplay, if any, between s. 9(1) and s. 23 of MCOCA? - Held, s. 9 of MCOCA cannot be read or invoked independent of s. 23 - There was a raid conducted by respective authorities under Prevention of Corruption Act against one HN who was caught red handed accepting certain amount of bribe on behalf of one NS - NS filed a private complaint against certain authorities who were involved in such raid, alongwith other persons u/s. 9 of MCOCA - Special Judge by its order ordered formation of Special Investigation Team - Chief of SIT preferred writ petition against such order and other proposed accused persons also had preferred respective writ petitions - Two divergent views came out from two different Division Benches of High Court so the matter referred to the Full Bench, wherein majority expressed the view which is the issue in the instant case - DB had held that while entertaining a private complaint u/s. 9 of MCOCA, the Special Court could take recourse to the provisions of s. 156(3) of CrPC, and thus had order investigation of such private complaint - Impugned majority verdict had upheld the DB view - Submissions heard and provision of s. 9(1)(4), 23 and 25 perused - S. 9(1) indicates that the Special Court may take cognizance of any offence without the accused being committed to it for trial, either on receiving a complaint of facts or upon a police report of such facts, which clearly indicates that the Sp. Court is also empowered to take cognizance of an offence under MCOCA even on a private complaint; said power is however controlled by the provisions of s. 23(2) of Act which provides that no Sp. Court shall take cognizance of any offence under Act without the previous sanction of a police Officer not below the rank of Additional Director General of Police - Provisions of s. 9(1) have to be read in harmony with the provisions of s. 23(2) as far as private complaints are concerned - As per s. 25 provisions of MCOCA would have an overriding effect over the provisions of CrPC; thus invocation of s. 156(3), CrPC for ordering a special inquiry on a private complaint and taking cognizance thereupon, without traversing the route indicated in s. 23 of MCOCA held to be improper - Court noted the scheme and object of MCOCA; provisions of s. 23 are the safeguards provided against the invocation of the provisions of the Act which are extremely stringent and far removed from the provisions of the general criminal law, thus if submissions of respondents are accepted than the very purpose of introducing such safeguards lose their very raison d'etre - Court gave harmonious construction to the provisions of s. 9(1) and s. 23 as to that upon receipt of such private complaint the Sp. Judge has to forward the same to the officer indicated in cl.(a) of sub/s. (1) of s. 23 to have an inquiry conducted into the complaint by a police officer indicated in cl. (b) of sub/s. (1) and only thereafter take cognizance of the offence complained of, if sanction is accorded to the Sp. Court to take cognizance of such offence u/sub/s.(2) of s. 23 - Minority view of Full Bench of HC upheld - Directions issued - Appeals disposed of.
