R. Dalmia v Commissioner of Income Tax, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

19 January 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 222; (1977) 2 SCC 467; AIR 1977 SC 988; [1977] 106 ITR 895; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 654

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Substantial Interest

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 2(6c)(iii) - Income Tax Act - Income - Assesseereceiving benefits from the company - Not a director - Only beneficial owner of shares - Whether such benefits amounts to income of assessee - Considering the meaning of word 'Concerned' - Held, amount was his income and liable to tax u/s.2 (6c)(iii) of the Act - word 'concern' is not a term of art having a precise, fixed meaning - Appeal Dismissed.
