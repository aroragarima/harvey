H. V. Jayaram v Industrial Credit and Investment Corporation of Others

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1274; (2000) 2 SCC 202; AIR 2000 SC 579; 1999 (4) CCR 338; 2000 (1) CLC 251; 2000 (1) CompLJ 321; 2000 CRLJ 736; 2000 (84) FLR 436; 2000 (1) JCC 67; JT 1999 (10) SC 55; 2000(2) M.P.H.T. 387; 2000 (1) RCR(Criminal) 315; 1999(7) SCALE 481; 1999 (10) SLT 474; [1999] Supp5 S.C.R. 336; 1999 (10) Supreme 233; 2000 (157) TAXATION 530; [2000] 108 TAXMAN 82

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law; Trade

Summary: Corporate - Criminal - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 53 and 113(2) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 201 - Whether complaint for offences punishable u/s. 113(2) of Companies Act, be filed only where registered office of company is situated or where complainant is residing? - S. 113 should be considered with s. 53, which prescribes mode of delivery by sending document by post at registered address of company and sub-s. (2) is deeming provision for delivery of such letter - Held, cause of action would arise at place where registered office of company is situated - Appeal dismissed.
