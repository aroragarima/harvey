Lakshmi and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

29 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1780; (2002) 7 SCC 198; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1647; AIR 2002 SC 3119; 2002 (3) CCR 270; 2002 (4) Crimes 113; 2002 (6) CRJ 678; JT 2002 (6) SC 392; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 82; 2002(6) SCALE 152; 2002 (5) SLT 83; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 733; 2002 (6) Supreme 69

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302., 149, 201 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Trial Court acquitted S and B remaining six were convicted of offences - It was held that prosecution had established that said six accused had formed unlawful assembly with common object of committing murders of R and R; while R and D had no arms, remaining four were armed with deadly weapons gun and pistols; murder was committed in funeral ground with view to take revenge by burning their dead bodies along with dead body of I as accused thought that R and R were responsible for committing murder of I - For offence u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 IPC, imprisonment for life was imposed on all six besides other sentences for offences on which they were found guilty including rigorous imprisonment for three years for offence u/s. 201 r/w s. 149 IPC - Three criminal appeals and one criminal revision were preferred before HC challenging judgment and order of Trial court - One appeal was filed by six accused challenging their conviction and sentence - Two appeals were filed by State in one appeal acquittal of S and B was questioned and in other State prayed for enhancement of imprisonment for life imposed on B on ground that he had committed murder while undergoing life imprisonment and, therefore, death penalty should have been imposed on him - Criminal revision was filed by complainant praying for enhancement of sentence of six accused persons - Appeals and revision petitions were disposed of by common judgment and order of HC - Both appeals of State and criminal revision were dismissed - Criminal appeal filed by accused persons was partly allowed - Conviction and sentence of D was set aside conviction and sentence of R for offence other than that u/s. 201/149 was set aside - Conviction of R for offence u/s. 201/149 was converted to one u/s. 201 IPC and sentence of three years imposed on him by Trial court was confirmed - Conviction of other accused persons was in substance confirmed with only modification that their conviction u/ss. 302/149 and 201/149 was converted to one u/ss. 302 and 201 IPC and sentences imposed by Trial court were maintained - Hence instant appeals - Whether order passes by HC was justified - Held, Court had minutely examined evidence and by no stretch of reasoning, any fault could be found with finding of Trial court - By adopting erroneous approach HC held that role of D was only to extent of catching hold of R or for coming to conclusion that there was no such need or that there was any change of story - Clearly, there was consistency right from stage of recording of FIR as to manner of commission of crime and up to stage of deposition by PW1 corroborated by other eye-witnesses PW2 and PW5 - From evidence, it was fully established that deceased had to be immobilized as he was carrying in his neck his revolver; he was immobilized by catching hold of by R and D which facilitated his shooting by B and L and R and D with others threw him on pyre of I - R and D had been charged for offence u/s. 302/149 IPC - Appeal filed by R and appeal of other accused was dismissed and appeals of State were allowed and impugned judgment and order was set aside - Court further ordered that of Trial court holding R and D guilty for offences u/ss. 302/149 IPC and 201/149 IPC and consequently sentencing them as well be restored - Appeals disposed of.
