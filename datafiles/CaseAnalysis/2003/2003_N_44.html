Bachhu Narain Singh and another v Naresh Yadav And Others

Supreme Court of India

19 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1798; (2003) 12 SCC 647; AIR 2004 SC 3055; JT 2003 (10) SC 407; 2003(10) SCALE 932

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 149, 379, 148, 147 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 27 - Respondent accused allegedly murdered the deceased - Trial Court convicted the respondents u/ss. 302/148 IPC and s. 27 of the Arms Act - However, HC, in appeal, acquitted the respondents from the alleged charges - Whether the order of acquittal recorded by the HC was sustainable - Held, no reason to take a different view because the findings recorded by the HC were fully supported by the evidence on record and the circumstances of the case - In the first instance there appeared to be no reason why no one stated before the investigating officer who came to the place of occurrence that he had witnessed the occurrence as an eyewitness - Since they claimed to be eyewitnesses and large number of persons had gathered at the place of occurrence when the investigating officer reached that place with the police force, the normal course of human conduct would have been, for any of the eyewitnesses to immediately inform the investigating officer that he had witnessed the occurrence - PW9, the informant was a brother of the deceased and If he had seen the occurrence, nothing prevented him from lodging the report immediately - PW9 appeared to have come on the scene more than an hour and a half after the investigating officer had come to the place of occurrence - PW9's presence, therefore, at the time of occurrence appeared to be highly doubtful - Fact that the report was lodged within 2 1/2 hours of the occurrence and was, therefore, not unduly delayed, did not explain why it was not lodged earlier in the peculiar facts and circumstances of the case - If the investigating officer was present at the place of occurrence and the eyewitnesses were also present, they would not have kept quiet till about 9.00 a.m. when, for the first time, PW9, appeared before him and lodged the report - Hence, there was serious doubt about the presence of the eyewitnesses when the investigating officer came to the place of occurrence, and that also casted a serious doubt as to their presence at the time when the occurrence took place - It was not a case of the prosecution that after the occurrence the eyewitnesses had gone elsewhere - In fact, the evidence of the investigating officer was to the effect that a large crowd had gathered at the place of occurrence - PW9 in the report had stated that the revolver of deceased had been taken away by one of the accused -However, in the course of his deposition he had to admit that the revolver of deceased was found under his pillow in the course of investigation - HC had adversely commented on the credibility of PW9 - According to PW9, respondent decapitated the deceased, however, none of the other witnesses has mentioned about respondent beheading the deceased and that also showed the extent to which the informant could go in making out a false case because if what is stated was the fact, nine other eyewitnesses noticing the same occurrence from different places could not have missed noticing that fact - Impugned HC order was set aside - Appeals dismissed.
