Laxman Lal (Dead) Through Lrs. And Anr. v State Of Rajasthan And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

01 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 122; (2013) 3 SCC 764; AIR 2013 SC 1578; JT 2013 (3) SC 457; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 331; 2013 (2) RLW 1220; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 218

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Rajasthan Land Acquisition Act, Rajasthan Land Acquisition (Amendment and Validation) Act, 1981, Land Acquisition (Rajasthan Amendment) Act, 1987

Summary: Land & Property - Rajasthan Land Acquisition Act, 1953, ss. 4, 4(5), 5-A, 17(1), 17(4) - Rajasthan Land Acquisition (Amendment and Validation) Act, 1981, s. 5(2) - Preliminary notification - Commencement of proceedings - Amendment - Validity - State Govt. issued notification dt. 1-5-1980 u/s. 4 of the Act acquiring land of petitioners for public purpose - Aggrieved, appellants filed writ petitions challenging said notification - Single Judge dismissed writ petition - On intra Court appeal, DB dismissed appeal - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Whether invocation of power of urgency and dispensation of inquiry u/s. 5-A after 7 years of issuance of preliminary notification u/s. 4 of the Act were sustainable - Held, a very valuable right conferred on land owner/person interested u/s. 5-A of the Act had been taken away without any justification - It was so because bus stand construction would have taken some time -Exercise of power by State Govt. u/s. 17(1) r/w s. 17(4) of the 1953 Act and dispensation of inquiry u/s. 5-A of the Act could not be legally sustained and had to be declared as such - Hence, invocation of power of urgency by State for such acquisition might not be legally sustainable - (B) Whether preliminary notification u/s. 4 of the 1953 Act, issued on dt. 1-5-1980 had lapsed since declaration u/s. 6 of the 1953 Act was made on 19-3-1987 after expiry of 2 years from commencement of the 1981 Act - Held, s. 5(2) of the 1981 Act mandates that no declaration u/s. 6 of the 1953 in respect of notice issued u/s. 4(5) of the 1953 Act before commencement of the 1981 Act should be made after expiry of 2 years from commencement of the 1981 Act was mandatory and permits no departure - Thus, in instant case preliminary notification dt. 1-5-1980, which was followed by notice u/s. 4(5) of the 1953 Act before commencement of the 1981 Act, had lapsed and did not survive since declaration u/s. 6 of the 1953 Act was made much beyond time limit prescribed in law - Hence, impugned order of HC were set aside - Appeal allowed.
