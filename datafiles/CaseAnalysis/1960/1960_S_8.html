Â Tata Iron and Steel Company, Limited, Bombay v S. R. Sarkar and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 August 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam, A.K. Sarkar, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 107; AIR 1961 SC 65; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 379; 1960 (11) STC 655

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Tax On The Sale Or Purchase Of Goods, Collected, Practice, West Bengal Sales Tax Act, 1954, Transfer Of Documents Of Title, Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1959, Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1952

Summary: Constitution - Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 19(1)(g) - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, (before amendment by Act 31 of 1958), ss. 2(a), 3(a)(b)and 4(2) - (A) Whether art. 19(1)(g) of Constitution is infringed by goods being taxed twice by levying officials of two states ? - Held, a threat by state to realize tax, without authority of law, from a citizen by using coercive machinery of an Act is an infringement of fundamental right guaranteed to him u/art. 19(1)(g) and gives to aggrieved citizen a right to seek relief by a petition under Constitution - Commercial Tax Officer of two different states are levying central tax on goods transferred from one state to other in respect of an inter-state sale tax is leviable only once - Art. 19 is infringed - Petition allowed.
