Narinder Singh Rao v Avm Mahinder Singh Rao And Others

Supreme Court of India

22 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

R. M. Lodha

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 184; (2013) 9 SCC 425; AIR 2013 SC 1470; 2013 (4) ALD(SC) 85; 2013 (99) ALR 41; 2013 (3) AWC 2778; 2013 (2) CLT(SC) 118; JT 2013 (8) SC 364; 2013 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1872; 2013 (170) PLR 766; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 679; 2014 (124) RD 546; 2013 (3) RLW 1987; 2013(5) SCALE 6; 2013 (4) WLN(SC) 38

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Family & Personal - Civil Procedure - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Non full-fledged right over property - Execution of Will - Entitlement of - 'S'/testatrix executed 'Will' on 1-6-1989 and she bequeathed suit property to one of her sons i.e. appellant/defendant no. 1 - After death of testatrix, respondent No.1/plaintiff filed suit for partition before Trial Court - Respondent no. 1 contended that Will was not genuine and therefore, said Will could not have been acted upon and as 'S' was survived by 8children, suit property would be inherited by all children - Thus, each child had 1/8th share in the suit property - Trial Court dismissed suit - On appeal, Lower Appellate Court dismissed said appeal though allowed appeal on question of court fee - Aggrieved by said order, respondent no. 1 filed appeal before HC - On the issue with regard to court fee, cross objection was filed by appellant - Appeal was partly allowed whereas cross objection was dismissed - Hence instant appeals - Held, so far as inheritance of suit property by appellant in pursuance of Will executed by 'S' dt.1-6-1989, the finding of Lower Appellate Court had to the effect that Will was validly executed by 'S' - HC rightly accepted the findings arrived at by Lower Appellate Court - Further, no evidence was adduced to the effect that property originally belonged to testatrix - HC found that 'G'/husband of testatrix died intestate and was the owner of property at the time of his death, suit property should have been inherited by his widow, namely testatrix and his eight children in equal share, as per the provisions of 1956 Act - In that view of matter, HC arrived at the conclusion that suit property would be inherited by all 9 heirs i.e. testatrix and her eight children and therefore, testatrix had inherited only 1/9th of the right and interest in suit property whereas 1/9th of the right and interest in suit property belonged to each child of 'G' - Though the Will executed by testatrix was treated as a validly executed Will, testatrix , who had only 1/9th of the right and interest in suit property, could not have bequeathed more than her interest in suit property - If testatrix was not a full-fledged owner of suit property, she could not have bequeathed the entire suit property to appellant, who had claimed entire property by virtue of Will executed by testatrix - Hence, HC rightly came to the conclusion that 1/9th share in suit property belonged to testatrix would be inherited by appellant by virtue of Will executed by her - In addition to his own right and interest in suit property to the extent of 1/9th share, which appellant had inherited from his father, appellant would get 1/9th share in suit property as he also inherited share of his mother testatrix whereas all other children of 'G' would get 1/9th share each in suit property - Thus, appellant would be having 2/9th share in suit property - Aforesaid finding arrived at by Court below were absolutely just and proper - Further, Court below had rightly come to the conclusion that the issue with regard to court fee was a matter between litigant filing the suit and the court and defendants need not have bothered about the same - Appeals dismissed.
