Kwality Manufacturing Corporation v Central Warehousing Corporation

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1911; (2009) 5 SCC 142; 2009 (3) CalLT(SC) 1; JT 2009 (4) SC 570; 2009 (4) MPLJ 303; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 415; 2009(4) SCALE 205

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Reasoned Award

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 30, 33 - Validity effect of award - Appellant-contractor entered into a contract of supply with respondent Corporation - Appellant failed to deliver the qualitative item as per specifications - Respondent rejected the entire supply - Appellant invoked arbitration clause - Arbitrator made award - Respondent filed application for setting aside the award before HC - Single judge dismissed application - Respondent filed intra-court appeal - DB allowed the appeal and set aside the entire award - Whether HC can interfere with the findings of fact by arbitrator - Held, findings of fact recorded by the arbitrator cannot be interfered with except to the extent of inconsistency in the findings - Approach of the DB was contrary to the principles of s. 30 and s. 33 of Act - DB has proceeded as if it was sitting in appeal over the award of the arbitrator and has reassessed the evidence - Inconsistency referred to by the DB relates to only a clearly separable issue, therefore, DB ought to have modified the award only to that extent of inconsistency instead of setting aside the entire award - Hence, amount payable by the respondent-Corporation to the appellant is reduced - Appeal partly allowed.
