Union Of India v M/S Banwari Lal & Sons (P) Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

12 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 288; (2004) 5 SCC 304; AIR 2004 SC 1983; 2004 (55) ALR 22; 2004 (2) ARBLR 81; 2004 (3) AWC 2499; 2004 (111) DLT 52; JT 2004 (4) SC 519; 2004 (2) RAJ 108; 2004 (2) RCR(Civil) 580; 2004(4) SCALE 443; 2004 (3) SLT 145

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Keywords: Delhi Rent Control (Amendment) Act, 1988

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Requisitioning and Acquisition of Immovable Property Act, 1952 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 17, 4, 6 - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958 - Delhi Rent Control (Amendment) Act, 1988 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - Whether use and occupation of property by appellant after 10/3/1987 was wrongful and illegal and in nature of trespass; and whether arbitrator failed to take into account relevant factors in assessing damages awarded in favour of respondent - In view of permission granted by Court enabling appellant to use and occupy property up to 31/3/1993, it cannot be said that possession of appellant was illegal and wrongful and in nature of trespass - Damages were claimable not on basis of mesne profits but on basis of fair rent - Large number of relevant factors have not been taken into account by arbitrator while awarding damages - Appeal allowed.
