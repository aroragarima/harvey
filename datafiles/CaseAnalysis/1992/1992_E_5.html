Bhide Girls Education Society v Education Officer, Zila Parishad, Nagpur and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 November 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

N.M. Kasliwal, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 768; (1993) Supp3 SCC 527; (1994) SCC (L&S) 78

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Appointment - Legality - Respondent no. 3 sent an application to Director of Education to be appointed as Headmistress - Education Officer passed order - Appellant society filed petition before HC for quashing communication and the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal.

Held, in view of the law laid down by Court, the Govt. hereby directs that when there is isolated post which arose in beginning or in future year at time of promotion, principle of reservation would not apply and that post would be treated as unreserved post. In our view, decision given by Court in Dr. Chakradhar Paswan case concludes the controversy raised in the case before us. It is an admitted position that there is only one post of Headmistress in the High School run by appellant society and as such there cannot be any reservation on such post. Order passed by HC is set aside and communication addressed by respondent no. 1 Education Officer to the appellant society is quashed. Appeal allowed.
