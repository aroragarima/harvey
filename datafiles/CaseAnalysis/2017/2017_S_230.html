Sudha Renukaiah and others v State of A. P.

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhushan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 312; AIR 2017 SC 2124; JT 2017 (4) SC 364; 2017 (2) RCR(Criminal) 693; 2017(4) SCALE 624

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Presumption, Weapon, Bone, Motor, Appreciation, Spot, Reliance, Doctor, Dead Body, Medical Evidence, Two Views, Injured Witness, Interfere, Knife, Axe, Fracture, Consciousness, Registered, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Complainant

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 302, 307 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused allegedly attacked deceased and PW5 with axes, knives, rods and sticks causing instant death of deceased - FIR was registered u/ss. 147, 148 and 302 r/w s. 149 IPC - Trial Court held that accused were entitled to benefit of doubt and acquitted accused - On appeal, HC convicted accused u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 IPC and sentenced them to undergo life imprisonment - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, PW17/doctor appeared before Court who had conducted postmortem of deceased in his statement has stated that injuries could be caused with battle axes and knives. Doctor himself has admitted that he has not described the shapes of injuries, depth and middle of injuries. The above medical evidence does not lead to the conclusion that injuries as noticed by Doctors could not have been caused by axe, knives and battle axe. The eye-witnesses, PWs.1,2,3 and 5 have clearly mentioned about the weapons used by accused which eye-witnesses accounts are in accordance with medical evidence. Thus, mere non-showing of weapons to Doctors at the time of their depositions in Court was inconsequential and in no manner weakens the prosecution case. The eye-witnesses after lapse of time cannot give picture perfect report of the injuries caused by each accused and minor inconsistencies were inconsequential. In exercise of Appellate power u/s. 386 CrPC HC has full power to reverse an order of acquittal and if accused were found guilty they could be sentenced according to law. In instant case, reasoning of Trial Court in discarding the evidence of injured witness and other eye-witnesses was found perverse. HC did not commit any error in reversing the order of acquittal and convicted accused. From the eye-witnesses account, as noticed above and for the reasons given by the HC, SC was of view that HC was correct in setting aside the order of acquittal and convicting accused. Appeals dismissed.
