West Bengal State Warehousing Corporation v Indrapuri Studio Private Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 871; AIR 2011 SC 47; JT 2010 (12) SC 72; 2010(11) SCALE 129

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Land & Property

Keywords: West Bengal Premises Requisition and Control Act, 1947

Summary: Land & Property - Arbitration & ADR - West Bengal Premises Requisition and Control Act, 1947, ss. 2(d), 3, 11(1)(e), 11(1)(f), 12(a), 12(b) and 12(c) - Premises belonging to respondent No.1 was requisitioned by the State Government u/s. 3 of the Act - After taking possession of the requisitioned premises, the State Government transferred the same to the appellant - Thereafter, State Government appointed an Arbitrator for determination of compensation payable to respondent No.1 in lieu of the requisition of its property - Compensation was fixed by Arbitrator - Appellant filed an appeal u/s. 11(1)(f) challenging the said award - HC held that a person acquiring interest in the property does not have the right to participate in the arbitration proceedings or file an appeal against the award - Therefore, appellant cannot be treated as a person interested in the compensation payable on account of requisition of the premise - Hence, present appeal - Whether appellant falls within the definition of the expression 'person interested' contained in s. 2(d) of the Act? - Held, a person for whose benefit the premises are requisitioned or to whom the requisitioned premises are transferred does not have any locus to participate in the process of determination of compensation by agreement, or in the matter of appointment of an Arbitrator or reference of case to the Arbitrator or nomination of an assessor - A person like the appellant can neither submit opinion u/s. 11(1)(d) of the Act as to the fair amount of compensation nor the Arbitrator is obliged to give notice and opportunity of hearing to such person u/s. 11(1)(e) r/w s. 12(a), (b) or (c) of the Act - Therefore, such person is neither entitled to copy of the award as of right nor he can challenge the award by filing an appeal u/s. 11(1)(f) of the Act - HC did not commit any error by declaring that the appeal filed by the appellant was not maintainable - Appeal dismissed.
