Workmen of Messrs Dharampal Premchand (Saughandhi) v Messrs Dharampal Premchand (Saughandhi)

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 115; AIR 1966 SC 182; 1965 (11) FLR 53; 1965 (1) LLJ 668; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 394

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Individual Dispute

Summary: Corporate - Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Service - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(k), 10 and 12 - Whether an Association in which no other employee of Company except aggrieved ones are member can Raise a dispute under Act? - It is required that a dispute raised by a dismissed employee cannot become an industrial dispute, unless it is supported either by his Union or, in absence of a Union, by a number of workmen - Persons who supported cause, were employees of same employer, if they were not, it was thought that they could not be regarded as interested in dispute and as such, their support may not convert an individual dispute into an industrial dispute - Considerations which would be relevant in dealing with a individual employee's dismissal, would not be material in dealing with a case where a large number of employees have been dismissed on same day - Held, such an association can raise an Industrial Dispute - Appeal allowed.
