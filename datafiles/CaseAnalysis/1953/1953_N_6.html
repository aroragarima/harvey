Natvarlal Punjabhai and Another v Dadubhai Manubhai and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 November 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Vivian Bose, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 83; AIR 1954 SC 61; 1954 (1) MLJ(SC) 69; 1954 SCJ 34; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 339

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 28 - Adverse possession - Whether widow by executing deed of surrender completely extinguished defendant's adverse possession? - Held, rights acquired by adverse possession available against widow and not against heirs of deceased husband - Alienation of widow's right's cannot be done by executing deed of surrender in favour of defendants even though surrender title of transferee was not given to defendant - Person who by adverse possession having interest in widow estate cannot be ousted from possession when widow is alive, to take property subject to transfers made to widow so as to allow transfers to remain valid for her lifetime - Appeal dismissed.
