Jasbir Singh v Lt. Governor, Delhi and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1091; (1999) 4 SCC 228; (1999) SCC (Cr) 517; 1999 (2) CCR 89; 1999 (3) Crimes 9; 1999 (79) DLT 198; JT 1999 (2) SC 638; 1999 (2) RCR(Criminal) 454; 1999(2) SCALE 628; 1999 (2) SCJ 379; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 706; 1999 (4) SLT 1; 1999 (4) Supreme 237

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Foreign Exchange Management Act, 2000 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - West Bengal Prevention of Violent Activities Act, 1970 - Maintenance of Internal Security (Amendment) Ordinance - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, ss. 3(1), 11 and 3(3) - Smugglers and Foreign Exchange Manipulators Act - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - Order of detention passed against appellant - (A) Whether since grounds of detention having been served on appellant on 30-3-1994 though order of detention was served on 25-3-1994, there has been an infraction of s. 3(3), COFEPOSA Act, 1974 and, therefore, detention got vitiated? - Effect of defining period from such a day until such a day within which an act is to be done is to exclude first day and to include last day - U/s. 3(3) of the COFEPOSA Act, 1974 the legislators themselves have fixed the time-limit within which the grounds of detention could be served on the detenu - Expression 'as soon as may be' cannot be read in isolation from the phrase 'but ordinarily not later than 5 days' - It cannot be said that non-service of the grounds of detention on the very same day when the order of detention was served on the detenu even though the grounds might have been prepared constitutes infraction of sub-s. (3) of s. 3 - Held, for computing period of 5 days D/- 25-3-1994 has to be excluded and so being done there is no infraction of s. 3(3) when grounds were served on 30-3-1994 - (B) Non-consideration of representation by Central Govt. - U/s. 11, COFEPOSA Act, 1974 a power of revocation lies with Central Govt. - Power is supervisory power and is intended to be an additional check or safeguard against improper exercise of power of detention by detaining authority or State Govt. - Where representation had not been addressed to Central Govt. even though indicated in grounds of detention then it cannot be said that any representation made by detenu to Advisory Board ought to have been considered by Central Govt. - Held, question of infraction of constitutional right of detenu because of representation addressed to Advisory Board had not been considered by Central Govt. does not arise - (C) Delay in disposing of representation - No inflexible rule that delay in considering representation in all cases ipso facto would be sufficient to render detention void - Right to make a representation is undoubtedly a constitutional right of detenu and such a representation should be considered as expeditiously as possible - But what is reasonable expedition will depend upon facts of each case - Held, unable to hold that there has been an unusual delay in disposing of representation - Appeal fails.
