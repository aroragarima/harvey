Haroon Haji Abdulla v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 1; AIR 1968 SC 832; 1968 CRLJ 1017; 1999 (110) E.L.T. 309; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 641

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Customs - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B r/w s. 161(81) - Sea Customs Act, 1878 - Foreign Exchange Regulations Act, 1947 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 30, 133 - Corroborative Evidence - Confession - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant and other accused persons conspired to smuggle gold into India from Middle East and were allegedly charged for offences u/s. 120B IPC r/w s. 161(81) of the 1878 Act and 1947 Act - Respondents approached Chief Presidency Magistrate, accused were jointly tried among them 17 were discharged - Accused Nos. 11,12,13 and 16 were acquitted and others were convicted along with appellant - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC 's judgment was sustainable - Held, offence in instant case was detected on night of 13-8-1961 and investigation went on till morning of 14th - Thereafter respondent/customs authorities served notices upon various suspects and recorded their statements of PW1, other witness and confession of Bengali were recorded and there was no time gap between PW1 and Bengali - HC had very searchingly examined evidence of PW1 and applied checks which must always be applied to accomplice evidence before it was accepted and there was corroboration to evidence of PW1 in respect of appellant from confession of Bengali given independently and in circumstances which exclude any collusion or malpractice regarded being had to provisions of s. 133 of the 1872 Act - Hence, Court did not want to interfere in instant appeal and hold same opinion of HC about veracity of PW1 - Appeal dismissed.
