M. M. Parikh, Income Tax Officer, Specialinvestigation Circl v avanagar Transport and Industriesltd. and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 November 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 114; AIR 1967 SC 823; [1967] 63 ITR 663; 1967 (1) SCJ 285; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 38

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Distributable Income

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Finance Act, 1957 - Finance Act, 1959 - Finance Act, 1955 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 23 A and 34 - Limitation Act, 1963 - Whether an order u/s 23 A as amended by Finance Act, 1955, and as further modified by Finance Act, 1957 by ITO directing payment of additional super tax was an order of assessment which could only be made before expiry of period of limitation prescribed by s.34(3) of Income-tax Act, 1922? - Held, Income-tax Act contemplates making of diverse orders by ITOs directing payments of sums of money by tax-payers which are of nature of orders for payment of tax, but which are still not orders of assessment nor does prescribing of procedure for determining and imposing tax liability make it an order of assessment - Before imposing liability for additional super-tax, ITO has to determine whether Co.is one to which provisions of s. 23 A apply ITO has also to determine whether Co. has distributed within twelve months immediately following expiry of previous year statutory percentage of total income of Co. as reduced by taxes and levies prescribed therein - Tax Officer has also to determine whether, having regard to loss incurred by Co. in earlier years or to smallness of profits made in previous year, payment of a dividend or a larger dividend than that declared would be unreasonable - Process to be followed is not process of assessment, but of determining whether liability should be charged and imposed - Provisions of s. 23 A have to be construed as they stood before Act of 1961 was enacted, and mere fact that Legislature has chosen to specify a period of limitation for making an order imposing liability u/s. 104 of Act of 1961 upon a Co. which has failed to distribute statutory percentage of its distributable income will not justify an inference that such a period of limitation was implicit in previous Act - Provisions of s. 34 are not attracted and order is not one of assesment - Appeal allowed.
