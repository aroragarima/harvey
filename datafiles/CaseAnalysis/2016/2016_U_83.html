Verhoeven, Marie-Emmanuelle v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 321; (2016) 6 SCC 456; AIR 2016 SC 2165; 2016 CRLJ 2597; JT 2016 (5) SC 591; 2016 (3) MLJ(Crl) 577; 2016(4) SCALE 426; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 48

Case Digest

Subject: Foreigners; International

Keywords: Sovereign, Extradition, Jurisdiction, Opportunity, Communication, Expert, CONSTITUTION, Settlement, President, Prayer, Contents, Preventive Detention, Prosecutor, Dominion, Foreign State, History, Alternative, Gazette, Attack, Urgent, International Law, Extradition Act, 1962, Treaty, Extradition Act, 1870, Indian Extradition Act, 1903, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Practice & Procedure, Inquiry, Criminal, Indian Independence (International Arrangements) Order, 1947

Summary: Foreigners - International - Extradition Act, 1962, s. 2(d) - Extradition of accused - Existence of Extradition Treaty - Determination - Republic of Chile sought extradition of petitioner who is believed to be French national and is accused of being conspirator in assassination of Chilean Senator on 1-4-1991 - Further, petitioner was sought to be extradited from India but HC held that extradition proceedings initiated against her were not in accordance with law - Requisition has been made by Republic of Chile for petitioners extradition based on extradition treaty between India and Chile - Whether there is binding extradition treaty in terms of s. 2(d) of Act between India and Chile.

Held, SC of Justice of Chile vide order dt. 11-11-2015 held that Extradition Treaty of 2-1-1897 between Republic of Chile and United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland is existing Extradition Treaty between Chile and India. Further, Treaty was ratified by parties and enacted in Chile on 14-4-1898 and is published in Official Gazette in Chile on 22-4-1898. Further, there is sufficient material to conclude that from 1897-1898 onwards, Govt. of British India and Govt. of India considered itself bound by Extradition Treaty entered into with Republic of Chile on 26-1-1897 and Govt. of India has always been of view that Extradition Treaty is in force in India. Hence, both from point of view of Chile and India, Extradition Treaty is in existence and binding upon each State. Further, SC held that Extradition Treaty was not unilaterally terminated as Prime Minister in Parliament recognized Extradition Treaty with Chile and statutory Act which specifically gave recognition through s. 2(d) of Act thereof to all extradition treaties entered into prior to 15-8-1947. Hence, SC held that there is binding extradition treaty between India and Chile. Further, SC directed that Magistrate should decide on extradition of petitioner on merits of case and evidence before him. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Request for extradition of accused is justified and in accordance with law when there exist a binding extradition treaty between two countries.
