Rashtriya Ispat Nigam Limited v M/S Dewan Chand Ram Saran

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 451; (2012) 5 SCC 306; AIR 2012 SC 2829; 2012 (4) ALD(SC) 151; 2012(3) ALL MR 972; 2012 (93) ALR 257; 2012 (4) AWC 3462; 2012 (3) Bom.C.R. 608; JT 2012 (4) SC 436; 2012 (3) RCR(Civil) 720; [2012] 4 S.C.R. 1; 2012 (26) S.T.R. 289; [2012] 50 VST 443

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Service Tax; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Voluntary Retirement, Finance Act, 1997, Finance Act,2000

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Service Tax - Contract & Commercial - Finance Act, 1994 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 34 - Deduction of Service Tax - Violation of Terms - Arbitration Award - Sustainability - Appellant was engaged in manufacture of steel products and pig-iron for sale in domestic and export markets - Respondent was carrying on business of transportation of goods - Formal contract was entered on 'Terms and conditions for handling of iron and steel materials - Service Tax was introduced under chap. V of 1994 Act - Appellant deducted 5% tax on bills of respondent for period 30-11-1997 to 6-8-1999 - However, deductions were refused by respondent and raised dispute for arbitration u/cl. 15 of terms and conditions - Arbitrator dismissed respondent's claim petition holding that respondent was forwarding and clearing contractor and liable to pay service tax- Respondent filed petition u/s. 34 of 1996 Act before HC - Single Judge allowed petition, and set aside award concluding that insofar as service tax under 1994 Act was concerned, appellant as assessee was liable to pay tax - Appellant preferred appeal before DB, which upheld decision of Single Judge - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether HC, was justified in allowing petition and concluding appellant as assessee - Held, assuming that cl. 9.3 was capable of two interpretations, view taken by arbitrator was clearly a possible if not a plausible one - It was not possible to say that arbitrator had travelled outside his jurisdiction, or that view taken by him was against terms of contract - That being position, HC had no reason to interfere with award and substitute its view in place of interpretation accepted by arbitrator - Respondent as contractor had to bear service tax u/cl. 9.3, liability in connection with discharge of his obligations under contract - Appellant could not be faulted for deducting service tax from bills of respondent u/cl. 9.3, and there was no reason for HC to interfere in view taken by arbitrator which was based, in any case on a possible interpretation of cl. 9.3 - HC, as well as DB clearly erred in interfering with award rendered by arbitrator and hence, set-aside - Appeal allowed.
