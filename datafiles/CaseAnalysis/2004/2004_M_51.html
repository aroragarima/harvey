Indian Banks’ Association, Bombay & Ors. v M/S Devkala Consultancy Service & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 353; (2004) 11 SCC 1; AIR 2004 SC 2615; 2004 (5) ALD(SC) 68; 2004 (4) AWC 3544; 2004 (3) BC 1; [2004] 120 Comp Cas 612; 2004 (189) CTR 157; 2004 DRTC 515; [2004] 267 ITR 179; JT 2004 (4) SC 587; 2004(4) SCALE 628; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 225; 181 TAXATION 43; [2004] 137 TAXMAN 69

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Interest Tax Act, 1974, Loans And Advances

Summary: Banking & Finance - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Interest Tax Act, 1974, ss. 26C, 4, 5, 6 - Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1970 - Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1980 - Finance Act, 1991 - Reserve Bank Of India Act, 1934, s. 42(1B) - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 43D - Banking Regulation Act, 1949, ss. 21, 35A, 36(1) - Persons With Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection Of Rights And Full Participation) Act, 1995, ss. 28, 29 - Authority of bankers to round up existing interest rates to 0.25% - Action of taking recourse to Section 26 C would arise only when chargeable interest is calculated whereupon only incidence of tax under said Act is required to be passed on to borrowers by way of additional interest - Said Act is a taxing statute - Union of India under said Act cannot direct or permit bankers or financial institutions to raise interest - Reserve Bank of India was not an authority for construction of a statute - Approval granted by Reserve Bank of India was wholly without jurisdiction and ultra vires provisions of said Act - A fund may be created for benefit of disadvantaged people - Comptroller and Auditor General of India to effect recoveries of all excess amount realised by Union of India by way of interest tax and interest by banks and other financial institutions and create corpus of such fund therefrom - Appellant and other concerned banks are also hereby directed to contribute to extent of Rs. 50 lakhs each in said fund.
