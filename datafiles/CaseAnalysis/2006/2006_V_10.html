Vadla Chandraiah v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1279; 2007 CRLJ 770; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 258; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 523; 2006(14) SCALE 108; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 343

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Subsequent Conduct, Sufficient In Ordinary Course Of Nature, Injuries Cumulatively

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300, 302 and 304 Part-II - Whether offence committed by appellant punishable u/s. 302 IPC, 1860 or u/s. 304 Part-II? - If quarrel continues for a long time, it would be presumed that there was no premeditation - Exception 4 to s. 300 IPC, 1860 can be invoked if death is caused (a) without premeditation (b) in a sudden fight (c) without offender having taken undue advantage or acted in a cruel or unusual manner and (d) fight must have been with person killed - For application of exception 4, it is not sufficient to show that there was a sudden quarrel and there was no premeditation, it must further be shown that that offender has not taken undue advantage or acted in cruel or unusual manner - Held, appellant shall be imputed with knowledge that injury inflicted by him was likely to cause death - He is therefore liable to be convicted u/s. 304 Part II - Appeal allowed.
