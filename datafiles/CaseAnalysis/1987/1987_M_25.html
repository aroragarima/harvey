M.S.Jagadambal v Southern Indian Education Trust And Ors

Supreme Court of India

02 November 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28157; (1988) Supp SCC 144; AIR 1988 SC 103; JT 1987 (4) SC 484; 1987(2) SCALE 925; 1988 (1) SCJ 342; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 722; 1988 (1) UJ 86

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Special Relief Act, 1963

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 6 - (A) Plea raised in appeal by defendants for remand to consider question of adverse possession - Trial court not framed issue as to adverse possession - Evidence for plea not produced although opportunity not denied - Whether case can be remanded? - (B) Specific Relief Act - Validity of setting aside finding (that plaintiff was in possession of property ) by appellant Court without due regard to probability of case and considerations that weighed with trial Judge - Held, case cannot be remanded at appellate stage to enable defendants to make good their lapse - Not proper - Appeal allowed.
