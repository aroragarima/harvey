Â Union Of India Etc. v Valluri Basavaiah Chaudhary Etc. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Bhagwati, V.R. Krishna Iyer, V.D. Tulzapurkar

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 236; (1979) 3 SCC 324; AIR 1979 SC 1415; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 802

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Parliament & Legislature - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 249, 250 and 252 - Constitution (Fifteenth Amendment) Act,1963 - Government of India Act,1935 - Andhra Pradesh (Telengana Area) District Municipalities Act, 1956, 244(1)(c) - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976 - Legislative Powers - Condition Precedent - Compulsory Acquisition - Resolutions vested in Parliament with power to regulate in eleven States by imposition of a ceiling on urban immovable property and acquisition of such property in excess of this ceiling - Expression 'immovable property' took in lands of every description, agricultural lands, urban lands or of any other kind - Effect of passing of a resolution u/art. 252(1) of Constitution was that Parliament, which had no power to legislate which was the subject of resolution, becomes entitled to legislate with respect to it - State legislature ceased to have a power to make a law relating to that matter - Whether imposition of ceiling on urban immovable property Schedule to 1976 Act, could be struck down and Whether impugned 1976 Act was beyond legislative competence of Parliament - Held, Court hold that 1976 Act, was not beyond legislative competence of Parliament insofar as State was concerned - In a law relating to imposition of ceiling on vacant land in urban agglomerations throughout territory of India, Act would automatically apply from the date of its application to those States which had passed resolution u/art. 252(1) of Constitution and would extend to adopting States from the date of resolutions passed by legislatures of such States - Legislation of imposition of ceiling on urban immovable property, and Schedule to the Act could not therefore, be struck down - Appeals succeed and were allowed - Judgment of HC was set aside, and was declared that Act, 1976, was and had always been in force in the State - HC had erred in holding that 1976 Act could not apply to urban agglomeration - U/s. 244(1)(c)(iii) of 1976, Act master plan should designate land subject to compulsory acquisition under powers conferred by Act or any other law - Otherwise, master plan prepared for town could not be treated to be a master plan as prepared in accordance with law - View taken by HC was wholly unwarranted - Appeals disposed of.
