Parle Products Private Limited v J. P. and Company Mysore

Supreme Court of India

28 January 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, C.A. Vaidyialingam, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 94; (1972) 1 SCC 618; AIR 1972 SC 1359; 22 PTC 346; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 289

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Intellectual Property

Summary: Intellectual Property - Trade and Merchandise Marks Act, 1958, ss. 2(d) and 29 - Constitution of India, 1953 art. 136 - (A) Infringement of Trademark - Test to determine deceptive similarity - (B) Constitution - Appeal by special leave to Supreme Court - Interference with concurrent finding of fact, if - Held, it would be enough if impugned mark bears such an overall similarity to registered mark as would be likely to mislead a persons usually dealing with one to accept other - Where a finding is arrived at not on proper consideration of law on subject, same is liable to be set aside in appeal by special leave - Appeal allowed.
