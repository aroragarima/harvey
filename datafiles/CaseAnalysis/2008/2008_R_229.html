Commissioner of Central Excise, Bolpur v Ratan Melting and Wire Industries

Supreme Court of India

14 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, H.S. Bedi, P. Sathasivam, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1670; (2008) 13 SCC 1; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1169; 2008 (231) E.L.T. 22; 2008 (4) KLT 607; 2009 (1) MLJ(SC) 192; 2008(13) SCALE 353; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 653; 2008 (12) S.T.R. 416

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of

Summary: Excise - Whether circulars can be given primacy over the decisions? - Held, circulars and instructions issued by the Board are no doubt binding in law on the authorities under the respective statutes, but when the Supreme Court or the High Court declares the law on the question arising for consideration, it would not be appropriate for the Court to direct that the circular should be given effect to and not the view expressed in a decision of this Court or the High Court - Circular which is contrary to the statutory provisions has really no existence in law - Appeals disposed of.
