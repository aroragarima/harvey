Union of India and Others Etc v Chowgule and Company Private Limited Etc

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1082; (1992) Supp3 SCC 141; 1992 (2) ACC 84; AIR 1992 SC 1376; AIR 1992 SCW 1373; JT 1992 (3) SC 169; 1992(1) SCALE 989; [1992] 2 S.C.R. 992; 1992 (2) UJ 370

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Goa Daman and Diu Motor Vehicles Taxation Act 1965

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Taxation Act 1965, s. 4 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 2(18) - Whether dumpers and shovels were adapted and suitable for use on roads and hence liable to be taxed under Taxation Act? - Held, yes - Very nature of these vehicles make it clear that they are not manufactured or adapted for use only in factories or enclosed premises - Mere fact that Dumpers or Rockers as suggested are heavy and cannot move on roads without damaging them is not to say that they are not suitable for use on roads - High Court was right in concluding that Dumpers and Rockers are vehicles adapted or suitable for use on roads and being motor vehicles per se, were liable to taxation on footing of their use or kept for use on public roads, network of which, State spreads, maintains it and keeps available for use of motor vehicles and hence entitled to a regulatory and compensatory tax - Appeals allowed.
