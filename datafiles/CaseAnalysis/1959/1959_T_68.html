Â Central Bank of India v Their Workmen(and connected appeals)

Supreme Court of India

12 May 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Syed Jaffer Imam, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 121; AIR 1960 SC 12; [1959] 29 Comp Cas 367; 1959 (2) LLJ 205; 1960 SCJ 842; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 200

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Labour Appellate Tribunal, Available Surplus, Banking Companies (Legal Practitioners Accounts) Act, 1949, Banking Companies (Amendment) Act, Companies (Amendment) Act, Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, Industrial Disputes (Amendment And Temporary Provisions) Act, 1951, Industrial Disputes (Banking Companies) Decision Act

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(b) and 10 - Banking Companies (Amendment) Act, 1956 - Banking Companies Act, 1949, ss. 10(1)(b)(ii) and 10(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Industrial dispute - Banks and employees - Bonus - Referred to ad hoc tribunal - Claim not covered and barred by law - Appellate tribunal has a different view - (A) Competency of appeal u/art. 136 and appellate power in this regard - (B) Interpretation of statutes - Items in order of reference, statement of objects and reasons, meaning of 'shall employ' and 'any person' u/s. 10 of Banking Companies Act and Statutory rules - (C) Retrospective effect of 1956 Act - Appellate power of Supreme Court u/art. 136 - Held, decision amounts to award, competent appeal and Supreme Court in exercise of appellate powers does not give opinions on hypothetical questions - Not correct to interpret them as items in legislative lists, not admissible for construing section ,and it cannot control actual words used, rule cannot enlarge meaning of s. 10(I) - Meaning cannot be restricted to those on managerial or administrative staff only, Act is not retrospective - Appeal partly allowed.
