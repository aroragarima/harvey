R.L. Marwaha v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

12 August 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28805; (1987) 4 SCC 31; (1987) SCC (L&S) 350; 1987 (4) ATC 584; 1987 (2) CCC 593; 1987 (55) FLR 861; JT 1987 (3) SC 292; 1987 (2) LLJ 536; 1987(2) SCALE 245; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 928; 1987 (4) SLR 728; 1987 (2) UJ 422

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Autonomous

Summary: Service - Pensionary liability - Entitled to - Petitioner entered the service of Indian Council of Agricultural Research(ICAR) was a pensionable post and post or posts held by him in ICAR were also pensionable posts - Petitioner retired from service of ICAR after attaining age of superannuation - Petitioner applied to ICAR even before his retirement requesting it to count his service in Central Govt. as part of his qualifying service for pension - Petitioner received a reply from ICAR stating that according to the then existing policy Govt. had not accepted any pensionary liability in cases like that of petitioner and that there were no rules authorizing ICAR to accept charge of pensionary liability in respect of period of his service rendered in Central Govt. - Aggrieved, petitioner filed instant petition - Whether an employee of an autonomous body established under auspices of Central Govt. was entitled to claim benefit of period of service rendered by him in a pensionable post under Central Govt. prior to his service being absorbed in autonomous body for computing qualifying service for purposes of pension- Held, it was prospective in operation in sense that extra benefit could be claimed only after 29-8-1984 that was the date of issue of Govt. order - But it certainly looks backward and takes into consideration past event that was period of service under Central Govt. for purposes of computing qualifying service because such additional service could only be service rendered prior to date of issue of Government order - Whoever had rendered service during any past period should be entitled to claim additional financial benefit of that service if he was alive on 29-8-1984 under Govt. order but w.e.f.29-8-1984 - Respondents were directed to revise pension payable to petitioner in accordance with Govt. order by giving him benefit of service rendered by him in Central Govt. while computing his qualifying service for pension - Petition allowed.
