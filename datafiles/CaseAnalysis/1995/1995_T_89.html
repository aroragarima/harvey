Metal Box India Limited v The Collector Of Central Excise, Madras

Supreme Court of India

10 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1751; (1995) 2 SCC 90; AIR 1995 SC 750; 1995 (49) ECC 67; 1995 (56) ECR 625; 1995 (75) E.L.T. 449; JT 1995 (1) SC 479; 1995 (6) RLT 519; 1995(1) SCALE 109; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 136

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Corporate Body

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, ss. 11 A, Proviso, 4(1)(a) - Excise Duty - Assessee manufacturing and marketing metal containers - (A) Limitation for demand of duty which was short levied on account of willful suppression of relevant facts by assessee - (B) Computation of value of exciseable goods - Treatment of - (1) Huge interest-free advances given by wholesale buyer to assessee - (2) Trading discount - In light of fact discount given to wholesale buyer and not given uniformly to all its customers - Held, longer period of limitation u/s. 11 A Proviso to would be available to department - Notional rate of interest on said advances could be reloaded in price so as to reflect correct price of goods sold by assessee - Not to be included in value of excisable goods and fact is not relevant - Appeal partly allowed.
