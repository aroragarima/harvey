Hemlata Kantilal Shah v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 October 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

Baharul Islam, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 322; (1981) 4 SCC 647; (1982) SCC (Cr) 16; AIR 1982 SC 8; 1982 (2) Bom.C.R. 218; [1983] 54 Comp Cas 559; 1982 CRLJ 150; 1981(3) SCALE 1657; [1982] 1 S.C.R. 1028

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14, 32, 22(5), 136 and 226 - Conservation Of Foreign Exchange And Prevention Of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, ss. 8(e) and 3 - Delay in detaining person after incident of smuggling palladium - Legal representation before Advisory Board rejected - Legality of detention challenged - On grounds of delay in passing order of detention, non supply of particulars by Govt. u/art. 22(5), posibility of prosecution - Moreover issue under consideration are detention under Customs Act and preventive detention - Duty of Court u/arts. 32, 226 and 136 in case of preventive detention - Held, delay ipso facto is not fatal to detention - Govt. need not inform that import of such metal is prohibited - Possibility of prosecution is no bar to detention - Both ways of detention are not discriminatory - Duties considered - Petition dismissed.
