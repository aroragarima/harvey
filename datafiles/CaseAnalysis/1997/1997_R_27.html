R.S. Ajara and Others v State of Gujarat and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1062; (1997) 3 SCC 641; (1997) SCC (L&S) 851; JT 1997 (3) SC 573; 1997 (2) LLJ 120; 1997(2) SCALE 516; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 597; 1997 (2) SLR 327; 1997 (3) Supreme 94

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Right To Promotion

Summary: Service - Assistant Conservator of Forests (Gujarat Forest Service Class II) Recruitment Rules, 1981 - Deputy Conservator of Forests (Gujarat Forests Service) Recruitment Rules, 1987 - Direct recruits and promotes - Inter se seniority - Promotion - Legality - Appellants were appointed on post of Asst. Conservator of Forests by direct selection by Gujarat Public Service Commission - Appellants sent for 2-year training and after completing said course, they were appointed as Assistant Conservator of Forests - State issued provisional seniority list wherein names of appellants were not included and thereafter final seniority list was issued on 27-10-1988 - State Govt. passed impugned resolution dt.31-1-1992 and issued provisional seniority list on 29-1-1993 of Assistant Conservators of Forests as on 1-1-1992 - Promotee officers filed application challenging said resolution dt.31-1-1992 and seniority list dt.29-1-1993 - Single Judge held that 1981 Rules did not make any provision regarding fixation of seniority and that it was permissible for State Govt. to lay down principle for fixation of seniority of direct recruits by resolution dt.31-1-1992 and said resolution did not violate provisions of 1981 Rules - On appeals, DB disposed of appeals and held that resolution dt.31-1-1992 was violative of statutory 1981 rules of recruitment to the post of Asst. Conservator of Forests, more particularly r. 8 and deserved to be struck down since it was inconsistent with said rules - DB upheld final seniority list published on 27-10-1988 as valid and legal and upheld select list which was prepared on basis of said final seniority list - Hence instant appeals were filed by State - Appellant contended that DB were in error in holding that said resolution was inconsistent with 1981 Rules and that it was bad on account of having given retrospective effect - Held, 1981 Rules did not lay down any principle in the matter of fixation of seniority of Assistant Conservator of Forests who were recruited under provisions of said Rules - Since the Rues were silent it was open to State Govt. to lay down the principle for fixation of seniority by an administrative order and resolution dt.31-1-1992 could not be held to be bad on the ground that it was inconsistent with 1981 Rules - 1981 Rules did not contain any principle governing seniority of Assistant Conservators of Forests appointed under said Rules - Provisional seniority list dt.27-10-1988 did not include names of directly recruited Assistant Conservators of Forests even though they were appointed in 1982 much before publication of those seniority lists - Single Judge had rightly held that these seniority lists were not valid - Right to promotion it might be stated that select list which was prepared on 24-10-1989 for the promotion on the post of Deputy Conservator of Forests and which was approved on 19-7-1990 was rightly found to be defective by Single Judge since selection was to be made for 15 posts and as per handbook published by General Administration Department, Government of Gujarat under heading 'Classification of Posts for the purpose of Promotion' 45 candidates were required to be considered from the cadre of Assistant Conservators of Forests and Departmental Promotion Committee which prepared select list on 24-10-1989 and considered only 23 candidates from the cadre of Assistant Conservators of Forests - Said select list was, therefore, rightly found to be invalid by Single Judge - Therefore, promotee officers could not claim that any right to promotion had accrued to them on said of the said select list and same was adversely affected as a result of resolution dated 31-1-1992 - Appeals allowed.
