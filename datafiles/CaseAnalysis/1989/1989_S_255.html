Â India Cement Limited and Others v State of Tamil Nadu and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 October 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, B.C. Ray, E.S. Venkataramiah, G.L. Oza, K.N. Singh, Ranganath Misra, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 284; (1990) 1 SCC 12; AIR 1990 SC 85; [1991] 188 ITR 690; JT 1989 (4) SC 190; 1989(2) SCALE 953; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 692; [1989] supp1 S.C.R. 692

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Keywords: Companies Act, 1913, Tamil Nadu Panchayats Act, 1958, Mineral Concession Rules 1960

Summary: Indirect Tax - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 245, 246 and 265 - Entry 49, List 2 - Madras Panchayat Act, 1958, ss. 115 and 116 - Madras Panchayat (Amendment) Act, 1964 - Whether levy of cess on royalty is within competence of State Legislature? - Held, no - Charge of royalty should be on land, labour and capital - Cess proposed by Madras Village Panchayat Act proposes is an additional tax - Royalty which is a levy or tax on extracted mineral is not a tax or a levy on land - Cess charged on royalty cannot be said to be a levy or tax on land - Cess cannot be upheld under Entry 49 List II by State Legislature - Order accordingly.
