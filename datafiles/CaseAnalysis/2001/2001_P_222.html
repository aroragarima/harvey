Jaswant Singh v Punjab Poultry Field Staff Association and others

Supreme Court of India

26 November 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 162; (2002) SCC (L&S) 116; (2002) 1 SCC 261; AIR 2002 SC 231; 2002 (1) CLR 9; 2002 (92) FLR 337; JT 2001 (9) SC 646; 2002 (35) LabIC 135; 2001(8) SCALE 278; 2002 (1) SLR 208; 2001 (8) SLT 190; 2001 (8) Supreme 454

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Promotion - Cancellation - Legality - Appeal was preferred from the decision of the HC setting aside the appointment of appellant as a chick sexer in the Department of Animal Husbandary of the State Govt. - Appellants promotion to the post of chick sexer was accordingly quashed - Hence instant appeal.

Held, basis of the appellants claim for promotion is the order of the District Judge. It is the case of the respondent authorities also that the order of promotion was passed pursuant to the District Judges directive. Directive to grant the appellant benefits etc. was conditional upon the respondent authorities determining whether the appellant had been working as a chick sexer. Respondent authorities passed the order of promotion only after being satisfied that the appellant had in fact worked as a chick sexer. There is also ample un- controverted evidence on record in support of this. However, such a conclusion would not necessarily result in the benefit of promotion being granted to the appellant unless promotion was permissible according to law. The promotional rules whether prior or subsequent to 1992, both of which have been quoted earlier, do not permit the promotion of the appellant. The 1980 Rules were amended in 1983 so that the three month Poultry Training Course required in order to qualify for the post of chick sexer was changed to a twelve month Poultry Training Course. Admittedly, the appellant had not undergone training for one year. As far as the 1992 rules are concerned, the respondent No.1 correctly submitted that the appellant could not have been promoted as a chick sexer in terms thereof as he was not holding any of the feeder posts. Therefore, while the appellant's promotion to the post of chick sexer cannot be upheld, given the fact that the appellant had discharged the duties of a chick sexer, he was at least entitled to the pay and other allowances attributable to that post during the period he carried out such duties. While upholding the order of the HC, setting aside the order of the appellants promotion, SC direct the respondent authorities to pay the appellant for the period he rendered service as a chick sexer at the scales of pay together with all allowances to which chick sexers were entitled at the relevant time. Appeal disposed of.
