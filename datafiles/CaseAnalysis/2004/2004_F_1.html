L.S. Synthetics Ltd. v Fairgrowth Financial Services Ltd. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

06 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 715; (2004) 11 SCC 456; 2004 (4) BC 477; [2004] 122 Comp Cas 185; 2004 (4) CompLJ 158; JT 2004 (7) SC 254; 2004(7) SCALE 427; 2005 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 656; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 109

Case Digest

Subject: Capital Market; Corporate

Keywords: Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1986, Custodian

Summary: Capital Market - Special Courts (Trial of Offences Relating to Transactions in Securities) Act 1992, s. 9A - Notification by custodian as to respondent being a notified company under Act for purposes of Act - Appellant a borrower from the notified respondent/company - Proceeding initiated before the Special Court praying for a direction upon the appellant herein to pay to the Custodian sum due which was admitted as due by the appellant also - Plea of limitation raised as to that it having furnished full details of the amount in question to the Custodian in 1993, the claim was barred by limitation and the said Act did not enable the respondent herein to recover any time barred debts from it and further plea raised as to that transaction in question having not arisen out of transactions in securities, the Special Court had no jurisdiction to deal with the matter and furthermore was also contended that in that view of the matter Section 9A of the said Act must be read down - Pleas raised whether tenable? - Scheme of Act, powers of Special Court perused - On reading down of s. 9A Court observed that purpose of the said Act is to discharge the liabilities of the Government Banks, financial institutions, mutual funds, etc. and for the said purpose, the statute itself provides that all properties belonging to the notified person shall stand attached - Once a statutory attachment comes into force, although the properties in question unlike the provisions of some other Acts do not vest in the Custodian but the same evidently remain under the control of the Special Court, thus contention on s. 9A, rejected - On limitation Court laid down that provisions of the Limitation Act would inter alia apply only when a suit is filed or a proceeding is initiated for recovery of an amount and not where a property is required to be applied towards the claims pending before the tribunal for the purpose of discharge of the liabilities of the notified person in terms of Section 11 of the said Act, thus limitation would not apply - Pleas raised found to be without merit - Appeal dismissed.
