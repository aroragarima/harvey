State of Bombay v Sardar Venkat Rao Krishna Rao Gujar

Supreme Court of India

06 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, A.K. Sarkar, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 533; AIR 1966 SC 991; 1962 MPLJ 753; [1963] 1 S.C.R. 428

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Bombay Reorganisation Act, 1960

Summary: Land & Property - Madhya Pradesh Abolition of Proprietary Rights (Estates, Mahals, Alienated Lands) Act, 1950, ss. 4(1)(a) and 5(a) - Respondent was a proprietor whose proprietary interest in village was abolished by Act by virtue of s. 4(1)(a) - By virtue of Bombay Re-Organisation Act, 1960, s. 4(1)(a) right now vested in State of Maharashtra - Respondent contended that not only sheds and land on which those sheds were erected but also open uncovered ottas and chabutras should also have been settled with him by virtue of provisions of s. 5(a) of Act along with land appurtenant to those structures - Whether ottas and chabutras in bazar area could not be held to be buildings contemplated u/s. 5(1)(a) r/w s. 4(1)(a) of Act 1 of 1941 and could not be settled with ex-proprietor under law? - Held, reading s. 4(1)(a) along with s. 5(a) it is clear that where any buildings belonging to proprietor exist on any portion of abadi and that land, together with land appurtenant to those buildings, had to be settled with exproprietor - Land on which bazar is held is part of village abadi land and, therefore, all buildings standing on such land would fall within s. 5(a) of Act and would have to be settled with ex-word 'buildings' should, therefore, be given its literal meaning as something which is built - Hence, even uncovered ottas and chabutras fall within term 'building' as used in s. 5(a) of Act and, therefore, along with land appurtenant to them they must be settled with respondent - Appeal dismissed.
