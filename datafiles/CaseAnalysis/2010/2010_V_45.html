Chanmuniya v Virendra Kumar Singh Kushwaha & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 845; (2011) 1 SCC 141; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 29; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 346; 2011 (2) Bom.C.R. 787; 2011 CRLJ 96; 2011 (1) CTC 101; JT 2010 (11) SC 132; 2010 (4) MLJ(Crl) 504; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 801; 2010 (4) RCR(Criminal) 704; 2010(10) SCALE 602; [2010] 12 S.C.R. 223

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Cr.P.C., 1898, Criminal Procedure Code, 1872

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 7(1) - Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 125 - Presumption of marriage - Whether or not presumption of a marriage arises when parties live together for a long time, even without a valid marriage, thus giving rise to a claim of maintenance u/s. 125 of CrPC? - Held, there is a divergence of judicial opinion on the interpretation of the word 'wife' in s. 125 of CrPC - Hence, matter referred to Larger bench to decide the following questions (1) Whether a man and woman living together as husband and wife for a considerable period of time would raise the presumption of a valid marriage between them and whether such a presumption would entitle the woman to maintenance u/s. 125 of CrPC?; (2) Whether strict proof of marriage is essential for a claim of maintenance u/s. 125 of CrPC having regard to the provisions of Domestic Violence Act, 2005?; (3) Whether a marriage performed according to customary rites and ceremonies, without strictly fulfilling the requisites of s. 7(1) of the Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, or any other personal law would entitle the woman to maintenance u/s. 125 of CrPC? - Matter referred to Larger bench.
