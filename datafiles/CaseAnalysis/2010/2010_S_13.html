S. R. Srinivasa and Others v S. Padmavathamma

Supreme Court of India

22 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 306; (2010) 5 SCC 274; 2010 (4) ALD(SC) 160; 2010 (3) AWC 3137; 2010 (3) CGLJ 433; 2010 (2) CLT(SC) 231; 2010 (5) CTC 364; JT 2010 (4) SC 296; 2010 (6) KarLJ 1; 2010 (2) OLR 286; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 210; 2010 (111) RD 675; 2010(4) SCALE 245; [2010] 4 S.C.R. 981; 2010 (3) UC 1565

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Will - Proof of - Plaintiff, 4th defendant and 'X' inherited the suit property from predecessor-in-interest by way of a Will - 1st defendant, the legal heir of predeceased husband of X, took the possession of suit property after the demise of X on the basis of a Will allegedly executed by X in favour of him - Plaintiff filed a suit for declaration against 1st defendant - Trial Court dismissed the suit - First Appellate Court allowed the appeal of plaintiff holding that 1st defendant failed to prove the genuineness of the alleged Will in favour of him - Defendants filed second appeal before HC - HC allowed the second appeal - Whether HC order is sustainable - Held, Will in question was not duly proved by defendants - None of the attesting witnesses were examined - Scribe of the Will testified that he had signed the Will only with the intention to attest the same - Genuineness or legality of the Will was never admitted by the plaintiffs - HC committed a serious error in setting aside the well considered findings which the First Appellate Court had recorded upon correct analysis of the pleadings and the evidence - First Appellate Court on analysis of the entire evidence had clearly recorded cogent reasons to conclude that the execution of the Will is surrounded by suspicious circumstances, however, HC committed a serious error by totally disregarding the suspicious circumstances surrounding the execution of the Will - HC failed to exercise proper care and caution by not thoroughly examining the evidence led by the party, especially when it was not in agreement with the reasons recorded by the First Appellate Court - Therefore, HC order is set aside and First Appellate Court order is restored - Appeal allowed.
