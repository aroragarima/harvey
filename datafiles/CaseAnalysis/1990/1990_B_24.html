Bank of India and another v T.S. Kelawala and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 1004; (1990) 4 SCC 744; (1991) SCC (L&S) 170; 1991 (15) ATC 747; 1990 (60) FLR 898; JT 1990 (2) SC 339; 1990 (2) LLJ 39; 1991 (2) LLN 251; 1990 SCALE 140B; [1990] 3 S.C.R. 214; 1990 (3) SLJ 81; 1990 (4) SLR 248; 1990 (2) UJ 424; 1991 (1) UPLBEC 283

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Maharashtra Recognition Of Trade Unions And Prevention Of Unfair Labour Practices Act

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions and Prevention of Unfair Labour Practices Act, items 9 and 6, schs. 4 and 2 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 9(A) - Payment of Wages Act, 1936, ss. 2(rr), 7(2)(b) and 9 - (A) Whether employer is entitled to deduct wages for relevant period when admittedly employees refuse to work by going on strike? - Held, employees go on strike during crucial working hours which generate work for rest of day - Bank had made it clear, in advance, that if they went on strike for four-hours they would not be entitled to wages for whole day and hence they need not report for work thereafter but if they choose to enter Bank's premises on their own choice - Bank was therefore not liable to pay either full day's salary or even pro rata salary - It is not a mere presence but work that they do which constitute terms of contract and for which they are paid - (B) Whether there exists an implied right in employer Bank to take action as it has done? - Held, wages are payable only if contract of employment is fulfilled and not otherwise - Although employees may strike only for some hours but there is no work for rest of day as in present case, employer may be justified in deducting salary for the whole day - (C)Whether Company was justified in denying to workers full monthly wages for month of July 1984?- Held, employer have an allegation that workers were indulged in go-slow and as a result there was a negligible production in month of July 1984 - Go-slow is a serious misconduct and a more damaging breach of contract of employment - Hence, once it is proved, those guilty of it have to face consequences which may include deduction of wages and even dismissal from service - Slow may be indulged in by an individual workman or only some workmen - Industrial Court has recorded a finding that pro rata deduction of wages made by Company for month of July 1984 did not amount to an act of unfair labour practice within meaning of said Act - Appeals allowed.
