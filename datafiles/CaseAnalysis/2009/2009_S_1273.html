Suresh Kumar Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 696; (2009) 17 SCC 243; JT 2009 (13) SC 53; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 78; 2009(7) SCALE 629; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 1068

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Public Document, Indian Evidence Act, 1972

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B and 498A - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 113A and 113B - Dowry death - Cruelty to wife - How the term 'soon before her death' occurring in s. 304B, IPC apply in the facts and circumstances of the case? - 'Proximity test' - Deceased died due to extensive burn injuries on 08-12-1993 - Date of marriage was in dispute, whether it is 1983 or 1987? - Statement of first informant/PW1/brother of deceased in the FIR and his deposition perused - Ingredients of s. 304B IPC and 113A/113B Evidence Act perused; 'death within seven years of marriage' and ''soon before her death', she was subjected to cruelty or harassment by her husband or any relative.. for, or in connection with, any demand for dowry, ..' - PW1/brother inter alia deposed that marriage had taken place sometime in April-May, 1987 and at that time dowry demand could not have been met, and within one month of her marriage deceased came back and told female members of her maternal home that in-laws had demanded 'chain' and a 'ring' and also assaulted her; six months thereafter allegedly while 'gauna' ceremony was being performed, deceased informed him that they should meet the demands otherwise appellant would kill her; he was informed by daughter of one Maiku who was married in the village of appellant as to that one year before the death of the deceased, accused persons had burnt her and she had brought the deceased and got her treated by doctor and after healing she sent the deceased back with cousin of appellant and thereafter the deceased was in the house of her husband for six months - In cross examination PW1 however accepted that appellant was married only to Asha Devi/deceased and he denied suggestion that the marriage took place 14 years prior to the death; further admitted position noted that in the voter list of 1988 one Shiv Kumari was entered as wife of appellant for which fact he showed ignorance when asked questions in that behalf; Court further noted the fact of filing of application by PW1 for correction of his statement, correction was from 'in our place the name of girl is changed soon after coming to her in-laws house, after marriage' to 'at our place the name of girl is not changed soon after coming to ...' - Court noted that in his first disposition he had accepted the custom of changing of name of bride by her in-laws - Court deduced from all these factual positions - On being asked, PW1 could not point out as to in which month the marriage had taken place - When defence disputes date of marriage, it is obligatory on prosecution to prove the same in order to take benefit of s. 304B IPC and 113A/113B of Evidence Act - PW1's only piece of evidence which can be said to be admissible was the one when deceased had informed him when she came back to her parental home after six months of 'guana', that she might be killed for the demands; thereafter PW1 talked to appellant and matter was set at rest; there was nothing to show that any cruelty or harassment was meted out on that ground thereafter; Court noted that it is in this context effect of expression 'soon before death' has to be considered - Court observed that the harassment which is said to have been caused in connection with the demand of dowry other than the incident in question, was one year prior to the incident; thus the question arose as to 'would a gap of one year would answer the description of term 'soon before her death'? - Precedents referred and relied - Term is flexible and its applicability would depend on factual matrix of the case, as no fix period can be indicated; it must undergo the test known as 'proximity test' - Prosecution has to bring on record that the dowry demand was not too late and not too stale before the death of the victim - Some harassment which had taken place one year prior to the death without something more, could not have been considered to be a cruelty which had been inflicted soon before the death of the deceased - Held further, it does not satisfy the proximity test - Conviction u/s. 304B set aside and u/s. 498A upheld - Appeal partly allowed.
