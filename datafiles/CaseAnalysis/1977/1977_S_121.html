Â State of Karnataka v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 November 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Bhagwati, N.L. Untwalia, P.N. Shingal, Jaswant Singh, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 258; (1977) 4 SCC 608; AIR 1978 SC 68; [1978] 2 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution

Keywords: Emergency Provisions, Constitution of Jammu and Kashmir, Solicitor General, Existing Laws, Judges (Inquiry) Act, 1968, Indian Independence Act, 1947, Administrative Relations Between The Union And The States, Legislative Lists, Constitution (Thirty-Ninth Amendment) Act, 1975, Contempt Of Courts Act, 1952, Public Servants (Inquiries) Act, 1850, Public Servants Inquiries Act, 1850

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 131 - Commission of Inquiry Act, 1952, s. 3 - Commission of inquiry constituted to inquire about the charges of corruption, nepotism and misuse of power against Chief Minister and other ministers of Karnataka - Suit filed by State of Karnataka for declaration that notification constituting a commission of enquiry was invalid - Whether suit filed by the state is maintainable? - Held, action affecting State Govt. in their capacity as ministers would be matter concerning State - Claims of State Govt. is matter of State - U/art. 131 it is not necessary that plaintiff should have legal right of its own - State having sufficient interest to maintain suit art. 131 - Suit u/art. 131 is maintainable - Notification constituting commission of inquiry against Chief Minister and other ministers is valid - Federal structure of constitution is not affected by such notification - Setting of commission cannot be construed as to thwart democracy - Commission of Inquiry should be strictly in accordance with the Constitution and should not affect Centre-State relationship - Suit dismissed.
