Vedica Procon Private Limited v Balleshwar Greens Private Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

13 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 559; (2015) 10 SCC 94; AIR 2015 SC 3103; 2015 (3) CLT(SC) 243; [2015] 192 Comp Cas 285; 2016 (1) CompLJ 253; JT 2015 (7) SC 297; 2015 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3241; 2015 (6) MLJ 607; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 140; 2016 (131) RD 224; 2015(8) SCALE 713; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 1099

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Liquidation, Adequacy Of

Summary: Corporate - Auction sale - Confirmation of sale - Appellant was highest bidder in auction conducted in respect of land of company in liquidation and appellant had offered Rs.148 Crore for said land - While so, respondent no. 1 preferred appeal against order of HC granting extension of time to deposit balance amount by appellant and also expressed its willingness to raise its offer to an amount of Rs.160 Crore for land in question and also offered to deposit said amount within 72 hrs - DB of HC passed various directions in said appeal and in pursuance of the same, Company Judge on application made by respondent no. 1, passed order recalling order dt.17-12-2013 and set aside sale made in favor of appellant - Aggrieved, appellant challenged the same before DB of HC, but the same was dismissed by impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeals - Whether the impugned order is justified.

Held, the highest bid of the appellant was accepted by Company Court and all the stake-holders of the company in liquidation were heard before such an acceptance. Nobody ever objected including respondent no. 1 at that stage on any ground whatsoever. Further, even though the property in question became more valuable in view of the subsequent development, yet, it is not a relevant consideration in determining the legality of the order dt.17-12-2013. Furthermore, there is no specific format in which sale conducted by official liquidator is to be confirmed by the Company Court. The totality of circumstances clearly indicate that the sale in favour of appellant was confirmed by the order dt.17-12-2013. Hence, HC was not justified in recalling order 17-12-2013 and so, appeals of appellant are allowed. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - A subsequent higher offer is no valid ground for refusing confirmation of a sale or offer already made.
