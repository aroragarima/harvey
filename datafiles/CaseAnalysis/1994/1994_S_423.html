Â Khoday Distilleries Limited and Others v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 October 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, K. Ramaswamy, M.N. Venkatachaliah, B.P. Jeevan Reddy, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1123; (1995) 1 SCC 574; AIR 1995 SCW 313; JT 1994 (6) SC 588; 1994(4) SCALE 528; [1994] Supp4 S.C.R. 477

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Trade

Keywords: Morality, Eastern Bengal And Assam Excise Act, 1910, Assam Revenue Tribunal (Transfer Of Powers) Act, 1948, Madras Motor Vehicles Rules, 1940, Industrial Courts Act, 1919, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Bombay Lotteries and Prize Competition Control and Tax (Amendment) Act, 1952, Bombay Lotteries and Prize Competition Control and Tax Act, 1948, Cochin Tobacco Act, 1084, Jammu and Kashmir Excise Act 1958, Karnataka Excise (Distillery and Warehouse) (Amendment) Rules, 1989, Karnataka Excise (Sale of Indian and Foreign Liquors) (Amendment) Rules, 1989, Kerala Foreign Liquor Rules, 1974

Summary: Constitution - Trade - Constitution of India, 1950 - A.P. (Regulation of Wholesale Trade, Distribution and Retail Trade in Indian Liquor and Foreign Liquor, Wine and Beer) Act, 1993 - Special leave petitions were filed by various petitioners arguing that they had a fundamental right to carry on trade in liquor and for a declaration that the 1993 Act dealt with only with the taking over of trade but not business in liquor and, therefore, the State had no right to prevent petitioners from carrying on with the business of liquor during the validity of their licences - Whether State could prevent petitioners from carrying on with the business of liquor as apart from trade, during the unexpired period of the licences - Held, in these petitions, the contention raised was that the 1993 Act dealt with taking over only of 'trade' and not 'business' in liquor and, therefore, petitioners could not be prevented from carrying on the 'business' of wholesale dealing in Indian Made Foreign Liquor during the period of the validity of their FL-15 licences issued to them - It was apparent that the word 'trade' might include all the connotations of the word 'business' - Hence, after the taking-over of the trade, .i.e. the activity of buying and selling liquor, there was no activity left with petitioners to carry on under the licence held by them - The petitions were frivolous and dismissed - Petitions dismissed.
