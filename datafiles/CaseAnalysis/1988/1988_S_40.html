Shiv Charan Singh S/O Shri Angad Singh v Chandra Bhan Singh S/O Shri Mahavir Singh And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

19 January 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 325; (1988) 2 SCC 12; AIR 1988 SC 637; 1988 (1) CCC 897; JT 1988 (1) SC 145; 1988(1) SCALE 140; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 713; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 716; 1988 (1) UJ 533

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951, s.116-A - Whether finding recorded by HC that improper acceptance of nomination paper of Kanhaiya Lal had materially affected result of appellant's election was based on conjectures and surmises and not on any legal evidence? - Held, if number of votes secured by candidate whose nomination was improperly accepted was not disproportionately large as compared with difference between number of votes secured by successful candidate and candidate securing next highest number of votes, and if votes secured by candidate whose nomination was improperly accepted bears a fairly high proportion to votes secured by successful candidate, there was a reasonable probability that result of election had been materially affected and one may venture to hold that fact as proved - It is not possible to draw any inference or act on probability and to record a finding that majority of wasted votes would have gone to Roshan Lal in such a way as to affect result of appellant's election - Findings recorded by HC that result of election of appellant was materially affected is not sustainable in law - Election petitioners have failed to prove that result of election of appellant was materially affected on ground of improper acceptance of nomination paper of Kanhaiya Lal - Appeal allowed.
