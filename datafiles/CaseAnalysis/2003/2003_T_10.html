Uma Devi Nambiar and Others v T.C. Sidhan (Dead)

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1185; (2004) 2 SCC 321; AIR 2004 SC 1772; 2004 (54) ALR 131; 2004 (2) CHN 56; 2004 (3) JhrCR(SC) 208; JT 2003 (10) SC 470; 2004 (2) KLT 75; 2004 (3) MLJ(SC) 7; 2004 (1) RCR(Civil) 320; 2003(10) SCALE 624; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 637; 2003 (8) Supreme 658

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Indian Succession Act, 1925 - ss. 192-195 - Will - Validity and genuineness - Possession of ancestral home and the two garages - Possession - Held, the order of the High Court refusing to interfere with the order of the District Judge needs to be vacated - Further held, possession to be delivered forthwith to the appellants Further, it shall be for the respondents to establish the genuineness of the Will in the manner recognised by law in the appropriate proceeding, and thereafter seek for possession including the claim for any mesne profits in such proceedings.
