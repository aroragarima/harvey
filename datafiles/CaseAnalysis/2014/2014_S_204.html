Satvir Singh v State of Delhi Through CBI

Supreme Court of India

20 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 561; (2014) 13 SCC 143; AIR 2014 SC 3798; 2014 CRLJ 4399; 2014 (4) JCC 2586; JT 2014 (10) SC 67; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3286; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 40; 2015 (1) RLW 453; 2014(9) SCALE 528; [2014] 9 S.C.R. 247

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conviction, Illegal Gratification

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1), 13(1)(d), 20, 7 - Illegal gratification - Conviction - Sustainability - Accused/appellant committed offence punishable u/ss. 13(1), 13(1)(d), 20, 7 of the Act - Trial Court passed acquittal order - Respondent/prosecution filed appeal before HC - HC reversed order of acquittal and convicted the appellant - Hence instant appeal - Whether findings and reasons recorded on charges by HC in reversing findings of acquittal recorded by Trial Court were based on proper re-appreciation of legal evidence on record and within legal parameters laid down by SC in its decisions -

Held, HC in exercise of its appellate jurisdiction has exceeded its parameters laid down by SC in reversing the acquittal order of Trial Court. Therefore, findings are not only erroneous in law but also vitiated in law. HC has not noticed the very important lacuna in prosecution case that as per evidence of PW2 and PW3, bribe money which was sought to be given to appellant was in black rexine bag and not in brown rexine bag as shown to witnesses before Trial Court by the prosecution. Neither two witnesses nor C.B.I. officials put any signature or identification mark on bottles containing solution which is most crucial evidence in case to prove acceptance of gratification by appellant from complainant. As per statements of PW2 and C.B.I. officials, GC notes were not counted. It is a matter of serious doubt of acceptance notes containing in black rexine bag were touched by appellant. Findings and reasons recorded by HC are supported with statements of law laid down SC upon which counsel of appellant has rightly placed reliance. Exercise of appellate jurisdiction by HC to reverse judgment and order of acquittal was not only erroneous but also suffers from error in law and liable to be set aside. SC answered in favor of appellant after adverting to legal evidence and rival legal contentions urged on behalf of parties. The SC arrived at conclusions after accepting well founded submissions made by counsel of appellant. SC have to restore judgment and order of acquittal of Trial Court by setting aside impugned judgment and order on sentence of HC. Appeal allowed.
