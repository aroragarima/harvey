Suresh Nanda v Central Bureau of Investigation

Supreme Court of India

24 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

P.P. Naolekar, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 360; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 121; (2008) 3 SCC 674; AIR 2008 SC 1414; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 1189; 2008 CRLJ 1599; 2008 (5) CTC 277; 2008 (147) DLT 397; 2008 (3) G.L.R. 2333; 2008 (1) JCC 414; JT 2008 (2) SC 174; 2008 (1) RCR(Criminal) 897; 2008(2) SCALE 46; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 1212

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Foreigners; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Power Of Police

Summary: Foreigners - Passports Act, 1967, ss. 10(3)(e) and 10A - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 104, 165(5) and 102(1)(3) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - Whether retention of passport by CBI has not been in conformity with provisions of s. 10(3)(e) as it is not a proper authority as per s. 10A of Act? - Held, Police may have power to seize a passport u/s. 102(1), CrPC, 1973 but, it does not have power to impound same - Impounding of a passport can only be done by Passport Authority u/s. 10(3) of Passports Act, 1967 - Appeal allowed.
