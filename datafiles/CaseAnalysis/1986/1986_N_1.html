N. M. Nageshwaramma and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 746; (1986) Supp SCC 166; AIR 1986 SC 1188; 1986(1) SCALE 1198

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - Andhra Pradesh Education Act, 1982, ss. 2(33), 20 and 21 - Establishment of unauthorised privately managed Teacher Training Institutes - Contention of administrative vacuum in between the date of commencement of Act and making of rules - Whether such institutes established during the interregnum eligible to get permission and recognition - Held, position before enactment of Andhra Pradesh Education Act, 1982 was that executive instructions contained in GOMS No. 169 D/- 19-02-1975 held field and governed situation - After commencement of Act, no pvt. institution could be established except in accordance with provisions of Act and rules made thereunder - Permission had to be granted and recognition accorded in manner prescribed by rules - When rules were not made, there certainly was a void - In instant case issue is regarding case of those persons who established Teacher Training Institutes without obtaining permission of Govt. and then sought permission of Govt. - Their application proceeded as if GOMS No. 169 was applicable - They invited Govt. to consider applications submitted by them and also obtained a rule of High Court, that their applications should be considered by Govt. - Having obtained a rule from HC that their applications for grant of permission should be considered by Govt., they cannot be allowed to turn round and say that it was not open to Govt. to reject their applications for permission after consideration - In case of those who never submitted any application, Govt. was under no obligation to consider question whether they should be granted permission to establish institutions, and if in obedience to directions of HC, Govt. considered question whether they should be granted permission - Govt. cannot be found fault with for refusing to grant permission after such consideration - In either case it is unable to hold that Govt. acted illegally in refusing to grant permission - It is not appropriate that jurisdiction of court either u/art. 32 of Constitution or art. 226 should be frittered away for such a purpose - Teachers Training Institutes are meant to teach children of impressionable age - Cannot let loose on innocent and unwary children, teachers who have not received proper and adequate training - Training for a certain minimum period in a properly organised and equipped Training Institute is probably essential before a teacher may be duly launched - Petitions dismissed.
