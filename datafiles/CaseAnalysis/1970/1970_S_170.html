State of Madras v C. V. Parekh and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, S.M. Sikri, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 407; (1970) 3 SCC 491; (1971) SCC (Cr) 97; AIR 1971 SC 447; 1971 CRLJ 418

Case Digest

Subject: Trade

Summary: Essential Commodities Act, 1955, s. 10 - Iron and Steel Control Order, 1956, cl.(5) - Sale of pig iron in contravention of cl. (5) of 1956 Order - By godown clerk of company in conspiracy with representative of another Firm - Manager and MD also prosecuted - Absence of evidence to inferr that they had any knowledge of sale, or that they took part in the negotiations for sale or in the sale itself - Conviction - Validity - Held, mere circumstance that they were in de facto management of affairs of company and were aware of arrival of goods which were subsequently sold by clerk, could only create suspicion, but not proof against them - Manager and director held could not be convicted - Conviction cannot be fastened on them by applying s.0 as liability of persons in charge can arise under that section only when contravention is by company itself - Appeal Dismissed.
