Commissioner of Income Tax v Sirpur Paper Mills

Supreme Court of India

18 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1192; (1999) 3 SCC 596; AIR 1999 SC 1264; 1999 (153) CTR 89; [1999] 237 ITR 41; JT 1999 (2) SC 328; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 10; [1999] 103 TAXMAN 352; 1999 (2) TLR 418

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act 1961, s. 36(1)(iv) - Income Tax Rules, 1962, rr. 87 and 88 - Extent of deductions allowable - Interpretation there of - Whether board can impose conditions? - If so can conditions take on nature of curtailments? - Held, yes, s. 36(1)(iv) r/w s. 28, Cl. (iv) but it cannot take form of curtailments - First condition under Board's notification D/-21-10-1965 is that total amount of contribution shall not exceed 25% of employees salary, this is a condition which Board was empowered to impose, having regard to provisions in this behalf in r. 88 - Second condition is that only 80% of amount actually paid by employer can be allowed as a deduction - One is requirement that amount must be actually paid other is that deduction shall only be of 80% - Section states that deduction shall be wholly allowed. It permits Board to specify conditions but conditions cannot have effect of curtailing scope of deduction - Deduction permitted by section cannot be cut down under guise of imposing a 'condition' - Last condition is that deduction shall be spread out equally over a period of five years commencing with assessment year relating to previous year in which amount was paid - This is no 'condition' - Deduction is available in assessment year relating to previous year in which payment was made and it must be so granted - Appeal allowed.
