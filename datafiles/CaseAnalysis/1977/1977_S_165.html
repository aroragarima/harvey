Magan Bihari Lal v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

15 February 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 167; (1977) 2 SCC 210; (1977) SCC (Cr) 313; AIR 1977 SC 1091; 1977 CRLJ 711; 1977 CrLR(SC) 130; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 1007; 1977 UJ 235

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860 ss. 468, 411, 420, 109 - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant was convicted by Trial court u/ss. 468, 411, 420, r/w s.109 of IPC - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before appellate court which was dismissed and revision before HC was also dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the material on record was sufficient to justify the conviction of the appellant.

Held, after going through the evidence, it is difficult to sustain the conviction of the appellant on the basis of two pieces of evidence against the appellant - The evidence of the handwriting expert, who identified the handwriting on the forged Railway Receipt Ex. PW 10/A as that of the same person who wrote the specimen signatures Exs. PW 27/37 to PW 27/57 and the evidence showing the presence of the appellant with 'X' when the talk took place in connection with the sale of the iron sheets. Thus, two pieces of evidence are at not at all satisfactory and in any event on conviction can be rounded on them and only other evidence which was sought to be relied upon on behalf of the prosecution was that showing the presence of the appellant in Ludhiana when there was talk in regard to the sale of the iron sheets. Therefore, it does appear to us prima facie 'X' was a fictitious person and the iron sheets were taken off by 'Y' company for themselves on the strength of the forged Railway Receipt PW 10/A. Hence, due to police inaction, they appear to have escaped and a small man like the appellant seems to have been made a scapegoat and that the prosecution has totally failed to bring the charge against the appellant and order of conviction was set aside. Appeal allowed.
