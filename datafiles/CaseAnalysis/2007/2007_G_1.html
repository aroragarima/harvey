(1) Chief Commercial Manager, South Central v (1) G. Ratnam and Others; (2) M. Subramanyam

Supreme Court of India

22 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Lokeshwar Singh Panta, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 829; (2007) 8 SCC 212; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 851; AIR 2007 SC 2976; 2007 (6) ALD(SC) 11; 2007 (7) AWC 7405; JT 2007 (10) SC 378; 2007(10) SCALE 321; 2007 (6) SCJ 468; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 259; 2007 (5) SLR 677

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Executive Instructions, Vigilance Manual, Loss Of Seniority

Summary: Railway Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1968; Railway Services (Conduct) Rules, 1966 - Respondent was found having demanded more money against the EFT amount - Penalty of removal from service - Courts below held that investigating agency had conducted departmental traps against the respondents in violation of the mandatory provisions contained in paragraphs 704 and 705 of the Vigilance Manual, 1996 and ordered reinstatement - Appeal against - Held, administrative rules, regulations and instructions, which have no statutory force, do not give rise to any legal right in favour of the aggrieved party and cannot be enforced in a court of law against the administration - Instructions contained in paragraphs 704 and 705 of the Vigilance Manual, 1996 are procedural in character and not of a substantive nature - Violation thereof, if any, by the investigating officer in conducting departmental trap cases would not ipso facto vitiate the departmental proceedings initiated against the respondents on the basis of the complaints submitted by the investigating officers to the railway authorities - Appeals allowed.
