Rajan Purohit & Ors. v Rajasthan University Of Health Science & Ors

Supreme Court of India

30 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 331; (2012) 10 SCC 770; 2012 (5) AWC 5331; JT 2012 (8) SC 265; 2012(8) SCALE 71; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 299; 2012 (5) SLR 661

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Education

Keywords: Osmania University Act, A.P. Education Act

Summary: Education - Administrative - Constitution - Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, s. 10A, regn. 5 (2) - Admission - Legality - Appellant college affiliated from university of Medical Sciences decided that students would be made available for 85% of seats in medical colleges in State through Pre-Medical Test 2008 remaining 15% seats of colleges would constitute NRI quota which would be filled by colleges - Govt. of India issued permission to College with an annual intake capacity of 150 students with effect from academic year 2008-2009 u/s. 10A of the Act - Candidates would be selected on basis of merit in first year MBBS course for 2008-2009 - Some candidates were selected through Medical Test - 2008 and placed in waiting list - Candidates filed petitions before HC which was allowed and declared that admissions made by College in MBBS course for academic year 2008-2009 against 85% of the seats were illegal and directed State to hold counselling from waiting list of students of Medical Test - 2008 - HC held that admission of 6 students was in violation of regn. 5 (2) of the Act which required that students could be admitted on basis of their merit as determined in Competitive Entrance Examination - Hence instant appeal - Whether admission of 6 students was in breach of regn. 5 (2) of the Act were not in issue in writ petitions - Held, violation of regn. 5 (2) of the Act were by College - Case of Medical Council of India (MCI) was not that 6 students were not eligible for admission to MBBS Course in accordance with eligibility criteria laid down in regn. 4 of the Act, but were not selected in Medical Test-2008, which was competitive entrance examination conducted in accordance with regn. 5 (2) of the Act, 6 students had got admission to MBBS course not on basis of their merit determined in Medical Test -2008 in accordance with regn. 5 (2) of the Act but on basis of their marks in 10+2 and they were beneficiaries of violation of regn. 5 (2) of the Act - Hence, for reasons stated u/art. 142 of the Constitution was invoked and directed that admission of 6 students in MBBS Course would not be disturbed subject to condition that each of 6 students pay to State Govt. Rs.3 lacs for development of infrastructure of govt. medical colleges within period of 3 months failing their admission would be cancelled - However, college had violated provisions of regn. 5(2) of the Act and to prevent breach of the Act in future, College was directed to surrender 6 seats in MBBS course for academic year 2012-2013 to State Govt. to be filled on the basis of Medical Test or any other common entrance test conducted by State Govt. - Impugned orders of HC were modified - Order accordingly.
