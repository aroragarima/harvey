Union of India and Others v J. Ahmed

Supreme Court of India

07 March 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 20; (1979) SCC (L&S) 157; (1979) 2 SCC 286; AIR 1979 SC 1022; 1979 (38) FLR 344; 1979 LabIC 792; 1979 (2) LLJ 14; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 504; 1979 SLJ 308; 1979 (1) SLR 840

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Death-cum-Retirement Benefits Rules, 1958, r. 16 - All India Service Rule, 1955, r. 20 - Suspension from service - Retirement - Extension - Removal from service - Pursuant to disturbances in Nowgong city and District area, respondent who was Deputy Commissioner and District Magistrate of place was suspended - Various charges were framed against him - Respondent submitted his explanation, Enquiry Officer was appointed - Respondent u/r. 16 of the 1958 Rules was to retire on 1-2-1962, but a day prior Governor of Assam directed that respondent then under suspension was to be retained in service for period of three months beyond date of his retirement - Ultimately, by order dated 11-10-1963 President, after consultation with Union Public Service Commission, imposed penalty of removal from service on respondent - Respondent submitted memorial to President u/r. 20 of the 1955 Rules against imposition of penalty, but it was rejected - Respondent filed petition in HC in which two contentions were raised - HC held that respondent's retention in service beyond period of retirement was not legal and valid, therefore, respondent was not removed from service from which he had retired and punitive or disciplinary action taken against him after retirement date was completely without jurisdiction and wholly unjustified, and it was quashed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC order was correct - Held, there was no case for disciplinary proceeding against respondent - Inquiry was held to be established that respondent was not fit to hold responsible post - Respondent was actually retiring from service and there was no question of his anymore holding responsible position - However, inquiry was initiated and he was retained in service beyond date of his moral retirement till final order was made which removed him from India Administrative Service - Appeared that there was large scale disturbances in State - There followed usual search for scapegoat and respondent came handy - Some charges were framed, but none constituted misconduct in law - Some charges were mere surmises - Substance of allegations were that he was not very efficient officer and lacked quality of leadership and was deficient in faculty of decision-making - These deficiencies in capacity had not constituted misconduct - Respondent was not a young person who was to continue in post for long period - He was retained in service beyond period of his normal retirement with view to punishing him was wholly unjustified - HC, therefore rightly concluded that respondent was no longer in service on date on which order removing him from service was made, thus order was illegal and void - Appeal dismissed.
