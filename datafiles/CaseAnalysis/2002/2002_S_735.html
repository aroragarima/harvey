Fritiz T.M. Clement and another v Sudhakaran Nadar and another

Supreme Court of India

01 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 123; (2002) 3 SCC 605; AIR 2002 SC 1148; AIR 2002 SCW 910; 2002 (2) ALD(SC) 125; 2002 (1) AllCJ 391; 2002 (48) ALR 256; 2002 (1) ARC 476; 2002 (2) AWC 1348; 2002 (2) CalLT(SC) 52; JT 2002 (2) SC 389; 2002(2) SCALE 377; 2002 (2) SLT 271; 2002 (2) Supreme 149; 2002 (1) UC 679

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908-Order I, Rule 10-Money suit for recovery of 'path-way fee' and 'quarrying fee' with future interest-Permission for amendment of plaint at belated stage rejected by lower courts-But amendments sought not making out new case-Plaint ill-drafted with cryptic and inaccurate averments-No prejudice could possibly be caused to respondents-Rather they would be in better position to know exact case of plaintiffs and proceed accordingly-Hence, amendments in plaint barring para 4 (d) allowed with costs of Rs. 20,000 payable to respondents for seeking amendment at belated stage.
