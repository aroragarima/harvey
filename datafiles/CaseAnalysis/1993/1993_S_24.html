State of Haryana v S.M. Sharma and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 799; (1993) Supp3 SCC 252; (1993) SCC (L&S) 1072; AIR 1993 SC 2273; 1993 (25) ATC 594; 1993 (2) CLR 203; 1993 (67) FLR 858; JT 1993 (3) SC 740; 1993 LabIC 1533; 1993(2) SCALE 583; [1993] 3 S.C.R. 280; 1993 (2) SLR 557

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908, O. 7, r. 10, ss. 20 and 20 - Bombay Relief Undertakings (Special Provisions) Act,1958, ss. 3 r/ w 4(1)(a)(iv) - (A) Whether dismissal of suit on the ground that the court had no jurisdiction to try suit is proper - (B) Appellate court permitted the objection as to Jurisdiction - Third condition u/s. relating to consequent failure of justice was not satisfied - Whether the Court was justified in allowing objection to jurisdiction of Court in appeal - (C) Suit for recovery of loan amount - Cheques (for the amount and those issued on default) issued on bank at place with in the jurisdiction of the Court - Endorsement on deposit receipt (without ouster clause) issued by debtor making it subject to jurisdiction of Court at other place - Whether s. 20 of CPC,1908 would exclude jurisdiction of Court within jurisdiction of which earlier transactions took place to entertain suit- (D) Whether proceeding pending outside the state of Gujarat gets suspended on declaration of industry as relief undertaking u/s. 3 of 1958 Act by virtue of s. 4(1)(a)(iv) for the period the notification applies - Held, proper order to pass is not dismissal of suit but return of suit for presentation to proper Court. - The Court was not justified in allowing objection to jurisdiction of Court in appeal - It would not exclude jurisdiction of Court within jurisdiction of which earlier transactions took place to entertain suit - Gujarat Legislature is not competent to regulate, modify or extinguish the obligations and liabilities incurred by a 'relief undertaking' (declared as such under Section 3 of the Bombay Act) outside the State of Gujarat nor can it suspend or stay the suit or other proceedings relating to such obligations and liabilities - Appeal allowed.
