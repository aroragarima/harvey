Commissioner of Income Tax v Express Newspapers Limited

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1406; (1998) 3 SCC 106; 1998 (146) CTR 571; [1998] 230 ITR 477; JT 1998 (1) SC 347; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 288; [1998] 96 TAXMAN 548

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax - Finance Act, 1964 - Companies Act, 1956, s. 205 - Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Whether declared as dividend on 06-12-1962, by the Board of Directors, but payable only on 16-01-1963, could be taken into account in withdrawing the rebate admissible under the first proviso to the Finance Act of 1964 by reference to sub-cl. (c) of cl. (i) of the second proviso to the same Act? - Held - The decision of the Board of Directors on 06-12-1962 to pay interim dividend cannot be construed as meaning declaration of dividend by the company - This being so what would be relevant is the distribution of the dividend in January 1963, thereby attracting the provisions of cl. (c) of proviso 2(i) and the Income Tax authorities were therefore right in reducing the rebate in the manner in which they did for the Assessment Year 1964-65 - Appeal allowed.
