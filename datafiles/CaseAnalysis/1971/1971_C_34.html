Col. Dr. B. Ramachandra Rao v State of Orissa and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 August 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, Subimal Chandra Roy, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 615; (1972) 3 SCC 256; (1972) SCC (Cr) 481; AIR 1971 SC 2197; 1971 (37) CutLT 1064; 1971 UJ 767

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Reckless

Summary: Constitution, art. 32 - Writ of Habeas corpus - (A) Illegal detention - In habeas corpus proceedings - Things to be considereedd by Court - (B) Habeas corpus- To whon it cannot be granted - Held, in habeas corpus proceedings the Court is to have regard to the legality or otherwise of the detention at the time of the return and not with reference to the institution of the proceedings - Writ of habeas corpus cannot be granted to a person undergoing the sentence of imprisonment imposed on him by a competent court and to a person committed to jail custody by a competent Court by an order which prima facie does not appear to be without jurisdiction or wholly illegal - Appeal Dismissed
