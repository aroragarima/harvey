Ram Kisto Mandal and Another v Dhankisto Mandal

Supreme Court of India

15 July 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 103; AIR 1969 SC 204; 1969 BLJR 270; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 342

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Sonthal Parganas Settlement Regulation, 1872, ss. 27(1) and 27(2) - Santal Tenancy (Supplementary Provisions) Act, 14 of 1949 - Whether an exchange of land situate in Sonthal Parganas for land situate elsewhere is invalid by reason of provisions of s. 27(1) of Sonthal Parganas Settlement Regulation, 1872? - Lands in question, set out in Schedule B to plaint were raiyati lands and were governed by said Regulation - Held, s. 27 of Regulation laid down an absolute bar to sales of rights of a raiyat - Language of s. 27 is clear and unambiguous - It prohibits any transfer of a holding by a raiyat either by sale, gift, mortgage or lease or by any other contract or agreement; section is comprehensive enough to include a transfer of holding by way of an exchange - Sub-s. (2) of s. 27 in clear terms enjoins upon courts not to recognise any transfer of such lands by sale, mortgage, lease etc. or by or under any other agreement or contract whatsoever - Language of s. 27(1) is comprehensive enough to include any agreement or contract of exchange and, consequently it must be held, given other conditions of that section, would apply to a transaction of exchange - It is true, that ss. 27 and 28 of Regulation were repealed by Santal Tenancy (Supplementary Provisions) Act, 14 of 1949 - But s. 27 was in force when said transaction of exchange was made and governed transaction made - That transaction being invalid and void, fact that s. 27 was subsequently repealed made no difference as repeal could not have, effect of rendering an invalid and void transaction a valid and binding transaction - Appeal allowed.
