High Court of Judicature At Bombaythrough Its v Shri Udaysingh S/O Ganpatrao Naiknimbalkar and

Supreme Court of India

09 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 947; (1997) 5 SCC 129; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1132; AIR 1997 SC 2286; 1998 (2) Bom.C.R. 138; 1997 (1) CLR 1122; 1997 (2) CLT 348; 1997 (76) FLR 532; JT 1997 (5) SC 298; 1997 (4) LLN 77; 1997 (2) MahLJ 578; 1997(4) SCALE 26; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 803; 1997 (4) SLR 690; 1997 (5) Supreme 123; 1997 (2) UJ 66

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Government servant

Summary: Constitution of India, art.311 - Govt. Servant - Dismissal - Validity - Allegation, Judge having demanded illegal gratification - Party complained to her advocate, - In turn complaining to District Judge through Asstt. Govt. Pleader - District Judge adverse remarked in character roll of delinquent - Delinquent admitted District pleader had no hostility - Held, there might some foundation for District Judge to form opinion that delinquent was actuated with proclivity to commit corruption - Disciplinary Committee recommended dismissal and State Govt. accepted it - Dismissal justified.
