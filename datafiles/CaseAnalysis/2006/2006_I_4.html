Ram Saran v I.G. of Police, Crpf and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 575; (2006) SCC (L&S) 351; (2006) 2 SCC 541; AIR 2006 SC 3530; 2006 (5) AWC 4873; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 90; JT 2006 (2) SC 267; 2006(2) SCALE 131; 2006 (2) SCJ 273; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 1102; 2006 (2) SLR 460

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: CCS

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Central Reserve Police Force Act, 1949, s. 11(1) - CCS (CCA) rule, 1965, r. 27 - Whether Govt. servant who was not qualified or eligible in terms of recruitment rules etc for initial recruitment in service, should be retained in service and should be given pension or not? - In a disciplined force there was no scope for taking lenient views for a person who obtained employment on basis of forged document - It was pointed out that on basis of binding instructions contained in Govt. of India, Department of personnel and Training, OM No. 11012/7/91 Estt (A) D/-19-05-93 (G O No. 29/93) dismissal from service was only punishment imposable and also according to r. 24 of CCS (Pension) Rules not entitle to pension - Held, after inquiry as provided in r. 14 of CCS(CCA) Rules, 1965 if charges are proved, Govt. servant should be removed or dismissed from service - Appeal dismissed.
