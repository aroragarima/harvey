K. M. Ibrahim v K. P. Mohammed and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1786; (2010) 1 SCC 798; AIR 2010 SC 276; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 2613; 2010 (1) BC 36; 2010 (1) Bom.C.R. 52; 2010 (1) CompLJ 336; 2010 CRLJ 525; 2010 (1) DRTC 469; 2010 (1) G.L.R. 1049; 2010 (1) JhrCR(SC) 75; JT 2009 (14) SC 651; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 1033; 2010 (2) MPLJ 630; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 595; 2010 (1) RLW 984; 2009(14) SCALE 262; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 1300; 2010 (1) UC 123

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Banking & Finance - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 320, 357(3) - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Dishonour of cheque - Appellant issued a cheque to first respondent which was dishonoured on account of insufficiency of funds - Respondent issued statutory notice to appellant calling upon him to pay amount due - Appellant failed to pay amount in time and respondent filed a complaint - Trial Court found accused guilty of offence with which he had been charged - Appellant filed Appeal - While affirming conviction, Appellate Court reduced sentence and fine - High Court dismissed revision -Present Appeal - Whether an offence under s. 138 of Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, could be compounded u/s. 147 of said Act read with s. 320 Cr.P.C. - Appellant contended that since a specific power had been given to parties to a proceeding under Negotiable Instruments Act under Section 147 to compound offence, there could be no reason as to why same cannot be permitted even after conviction - In O.P. Dholakia vs. State of Haryana & Anr. 1999 INDLAW SC 825, it was held that since petitioner had already entered into a compromise with complainant Court in peculiar facts and circumstances of case, granted permission to compound offence and while doing so, Court also indicated that necessarily conviction and sentence u/s. 138 of Act stood annulled -Held, once a person is allowed to compound a case as provided for u/s. 147 of Negotiable Instruments Act, conviction u/s. 138 of said Act should also be set aside - application u/s. 147 of Negotiable Instruments Act was made by parties after proceedings had been concluded before Appellate Forum, however, s. 147 of aforesaid Act does not bar parties from compounding an offence u/s. 138 even at appellate stage of proceedings - Since parties have settled their disputes, parties are allowed to compound offence -Appeal allowed
