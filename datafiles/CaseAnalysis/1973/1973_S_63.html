Jamuna Chaudhari & Ors. v State Of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 168; (1974) SCC (Cr) 250; (1974) 3 SCC 774; AIR 1974 SC 1822; 1974 CRLJ 890; 1974 CrLR(SC) 73; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 609

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: CrPC, 1898, ss. 156 and 367 - IPC, 1860, s. 300 - (A) Criminal Procedure - (1) Investigation by police - Duty of investigating officers - (2) Judgment in appeal - Reasons for decision/conclusions reached by High Court should be clear - (B) IPC, 1860 - Murder - Appreciation of evidence - (1) Prosecution nor defence coming out with the whole and unvarnished truth - (2) Benefit of doubt - Several persons including accused persons injured in incident - None except three out of sixteen deposing that fatal injury was caused by accused J - (3) Several persons injured and one died - Statements of persons injured showing that they remembered those who inflicted injuries upon them - Evidentiary value of - (A) Held, duty is not merely to bolster up a prosecution case with such evidence as may enable the Court to record a conviction but to bring out the real unvarnished truth - HC held did not comply with requirements of s.367 - (B) Court can only try to guess or conjecture to decipher truth if possible - Held J was entitled to benefit of doubt - An injured witness, in any case, would not easily substitute a wrong person for his actual assailant - Appeal Partly Allowed.
