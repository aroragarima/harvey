Rotash v State Of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

06 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 909; (2006) 12 SCC 64; (2007) 2 SCC (Cr) 382; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1765; 2007 CRLJ 758; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 870; 2006(13) SCALE 186; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 264

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, s. 302 r/w 34 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, intention of a person having a common intention to commit the crime must be judged from the totality of the circumstances - Prior-concert on the part of accused may be determined having regard to subsequent conduct of accused - Prior-concert in the instant case has also been proved, inter alia, by subsequent conduct - Common intention on the part of the appellant in committing the crime stands established - Trial Judge and High Court have not committed any error in passing impugned judgment of conviction and sentence - Appeal dismissed.
