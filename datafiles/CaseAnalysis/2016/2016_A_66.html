Union of India v Ambica Construction

Supreme Court of India

16 March 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, Ranjan Gogoi, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 226; (2016) 6 SCC 36; AIR 2016 SC 1441; 2016 (3) ALD(SC) 183; 2017(3) ALL MR 404; 2016 (117) ALR 478; 2016 (3) AWC 2653; 2016 (3) Bom.C.R. 568; 2016 (2) CLT(SC) 59; 2016 (3) CompLJ 91; JT 2016 (3) SC 233; 2016 (2) KLT 741; 2016 (5) MahLJ 210; 2016 (2) MLJ 683; 2016 (3) MPLJ 544; 2016 (2) OLR 46; 2016 (2) RCR(Civil) 638; 2016(3) SCALE 328; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 810

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Arbitration Agreement, Arbitral Tribunal, Jurisdiction, Arbitration Act, 1940, Arbitration And Conciliation Act, 1996, Award Interest, Conciliation, Absence, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 31(7)(a) - Award of pendent lite interest - Terms of contract - Instant reference as to whether Arbitrator has power to award pendente lite interest in case contract bars the same in cases covered by 1940 Act.

Held, if contract expressly bars award of interest pendente lite, the same could not be awarded by Arbitrator. Further, bar to award interest on delayed payment by itself will not be readily inferred as express bar to award interest pendente lite by Tribunal, as ouster of power of Arbitrator has to be considered on various relevant aspects referred to in decisions of SC, and it would be for DB to consider case on merits. Reference answered accordingly. Order accordingly.

Ratio - S.31(7) of 1996 Act confers power on Arbitrator to award interest pendente lite, 'unless otherwise agreed by parties'.
