Shramik Uttarsh Sabha v Raymond Woollen Mills Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 197; (1995) 3 SCC 78; (1995) SCC (L&S) 631; AIR 1995 SC 1137; 1995 (3) Bom.C.R. 450; 1995 (1) CLR 607; 1995 (70) FLR 884; JT 1995 (2) SC 284; 1995 LabIC 1591; 1995 (2) LLJ 301; 1995 (1) LLN 804; 1995(1) SCALE 533; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 967; 1995 (2) SLR 65; 1995 (1) UJ 522

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Bombay Industrial Relation Act, 1946, (BIR Act) - Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions and Preventive of Unfair Labour Practices Act, 1971, (MRTU and PULP Act), s. 21 - Whether the representative union under Bombay Industrial Relation Act, 1946, have exclusive right to represent employees of concerned industry in complaints relating to unfair labour practices under Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions and Preventive of Unfair Labour Practices Act, 1971, other than those specified in items 2 and 6 of Sch. IV thereof? - Held, Bombay Industrial Relation Act, 1946, which is earlier statute, was enacted because 'it was expedient to provide for regulation of relations of employers and employees in certain matters to consolidate and amend law relating to settlement of Industrial disputes - Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions and Preventive of Unfair Labour Practices Act, 1971, was enacted 'to provide for recognition of trade unions for facilitating collective bargaining for certain undertakings, to state their rights, and obligations; to consider powers on unrecognised unions; to provide for declaring certain strikes and lock-outs as illegal strikes and lock-outs; to define and provide for prevention of certain unfair labour practices - There is commonality in their objects and their provisions - Obvious intent of legislature which enacted them was that they should operate in tandem and complement each other in respect of industries to which Bombay Industrial Relation Act, 1946, had been made applicable - Two statutes must be read together - S. 21 of Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions and Preventive of Unfair Labour Practices Act, 1971 - No employee in an undertaking to which provisions of Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 applies shall be allowed to appear or act or be allowed to be represented in any proceeding relating to unfair labour practices specified in items 2 and 6 of Sch. IV except through recognised union - Reference is to employees in an undertaking to which Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, applies and not to employees in an undertaking to which Bombay Industrial Relation Act, 1946, applies - Apart there from, section permits an employee, not an union other than recognised union, to so appear - Provisions of s. 21 do not, therefore, lead to conclusion that an union other than a representative union can appear in proceedings relating to all unfair labour practices other than those specified in items 2 and 6 of Sch. IV - It is true that an order of Industrial Court in concerned proceedings would bind all employees of concerned industry even though they may be some among them owe allegiance not to representative union but to appellant - Objective of provisions of Bombay Industrial Relation Act, 1946, and Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions and Preventive of Unfair Labour Practices Act, 1971, read together, and embargo placed upon representation by any one other than representative of employees, who, for most part, is representative union, except in matters pertaining to an individual dispute between an employee and employer, is to facilitate collective bargaining rationale - It is in interest of industrial peace and in public and national interest that employer should have to deal, in matters which concern all or most of its employees, only with a union which is representative of them - It may be that a union which was representative of employees may have in course of time lost that representative character - It is then open, under provisions of Bombay Industrial Relation Act, 1946, for a rival union to seek to replace it - Appeal dismissed.
