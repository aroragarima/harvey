Praful Sudhakar Parab v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

29 June 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhushan, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 522; AIR 2016 SC 3107; 2016 ALL MR (Cri) 3104; JT 2016 (6) SC 160; 2016 (4) MLJ(Crl) 436; 2016 (3) RCR(Criminal) 707; 2016(5) SCALE 808; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 95

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Circumstantial Evidence, Child Witness, Chain, Dead Body, Last Seen, Inference, Clothes, Husband, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Conviction based on circumstantial evidence - Last seen theory - Accused was put on trial on allegation of committing murder of victim and upon perusal of evidence, Trial Court held that though there is no eye witness of the scene, yet, basing on evidence of PWs 8,11, it was accused who was last seen with victim and that victim went out on 7-12-1996 after 9 p.m., so, chain of events clearly pointed out that it was accused who committed murder and so, convicted accused basing on circumstantial evidence - On appeal, HC relied on statement of PW 8 in holding that it was accused who was last seen with the victim but had not considered evidence of child witness PW 11 and affirmed conviction of accused and dismissed appeal - Hence, instant appeal by accused - Whether conviction of accused based on circumstantial evidence could be upheld.

Held, in instant case, there is no long time gap. The victim went along with the accused on 7-12-1996 after 9 p.m. and next day morning the wife carried rigorous search, met the accused and took him to police station. There is no time gap between accused being last seen together and discovery of dead body. Hence, evidence of last seen together becomes very relevant and important and has rightly been relied by lower courts. Further, instant case is not a case of solitary evidence of last seen together but sufficient evidence was led to complete the chain of events and link the accused to the crime and HC after elaborately considering all the evidence on record has rightly dismissed appeal of accused. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - As per decision of SC, conviction could be based solely on circumstantial evidence but it should be tested on the touch stone of law relating to circumstantial evidence.
