Â Endupuri Narasimham and Son v State of Orissa and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 March 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 29; AIR 1961 SC 1344; 1962 (1) MLJ(SC) 32; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 314; 1961 (12) STC 282

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Constitution - Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 286(2) - Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947, s. 5(2) (a) (ii) - Constitution (Sixth Amendment) Act, 1956 - (A) Whether tax is invalid on ground that purchases in question were made in course of inter-state trade? - Held, two conditions have to be satisfied - A sale of goods and a transport of those goods from one State to another under contract of sale - Unless both these conditions are satisfied - There can be no sale in course of interstate trade - On same principles, a purchase made inside a State, for sale outside State cannot itself be held to be in course of interstate- (B) Whether tax imposed is in contravention of art. 286(2)?- Further held, it is essential that there must be transport of goods from one State to another under contract of sale or purchase, a purchase made inside a State, for sale outside State cannot itself be held to be in course of inter- Imposition of a tax thereon is not repugnant to art. 286(2) of Constitution - Petition dismissed.
