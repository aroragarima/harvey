Manoharan v Sivarajan and others

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 754; (2014) 4 SCC 163; 2014 (102) ALR 228; 2014 (2) ALT(SC) 6; 2014 (1) AWC 902; 2013 (4) BC 679; 2014 (1) Bom.C.R. 121; 2013 (4) CLT(SC) 287; 2013 (6) CTC 765; 2013 (4) KLT 828; 2014 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1988; 2014 (5) MahLJ 3; 2014 (3) MPLJ 495; 2014 (173) PLR 810; 2014 (1) RCR(Civil) 247; 2014 (122) RD 285; 2013(14) SCALE 347; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 471; 2014 (2) WLN(SC) 131

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Dismissal Of Suit, Kerala State Legal Services Authorities Rules, 1998, Deficiency of Court fee, Delay in appeal

Summary: Constitution of India-Article 39A-Code of Civil Procedure, 1908-Section 149-Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987-Section 12 (h)-Court fee-Deficiency-Rejection of suit for non-payment of court fee-Appellant could not pay court fee due to financial difficulty because of which his suit got rejected-Held-Appellant did not deserve dismissal of original suit by court for non-payment of court fee-He rather deserved more compassionate attention from trial court in light of Article 39A, which is equally applicable to district judiciary-Court fee of appellant deserved to be waived or provided by District Legal Services Authority, instead of rejection of suit-Trial court erred in rejecting case of appellant due to non-payment of court fee-Delay of appellant in non-payment of court fee - Condoned-Impugned judgments and decree of trial court and High Court-Set aside-If for any reason, not possible for appellant to pay court fee, in such event, he is at liberty to approach jurisdictional district legal service authority and Taluk Legal Services Committee seeking for grant of legal aid for sanction of court fee amount payable on suit before trial court-If such application filed, same to be considered by such committee-Case remanded back to trial court to adjudicate on rights of parties on merit.
