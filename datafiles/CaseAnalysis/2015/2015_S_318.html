Cochin Port Trust v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

22 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, R.K. Agrawal, Arun Mishra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 287; JT 2015 (5) SC 596; 2015 (4) MLJ 102; 2015(5) SCALE 536; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 343; [2015] 80 VST 341

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Carrying On Business, Major Port Trusts Act, 1963, Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1959

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1963, ss. 2(g), 2(viii) - Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Act, 1959 - Assessee claimed to be dealer - Liable to pay sales tax - Appellant Trust is statutory authority was constituted for rendering port services and assessee's specific activity was in dealing in scrap items - AO held that for A/Y's in question, assesee is liable to pay sales tax for sale of scrap items - Further, appellant authority held that assessee is dealer under Act which was affirmed by Tribunal and HC - Assessee relied on earlier decision of HC and contended that it is not dealer under 1959 Act - Hence, present appeal.

Held, under 1959 Act, from very definition of dealer, it follows that person would not be dealer in respect of goods sold or purchased by him unless he carries on business of buying and selling such goods. However, definition of dealer u/s. 2(viii) under 1963 Act is wide and specifically includes persons who have effected sale or transfer of goods irrespective of said sale or transfer being in course of business or not. Hence, contradistinction between definition of 'dealer' under 1959 Act and 1963 Act makes it clear that observations of HC in earlier case which refer to definition of 1959 Act and interprets it to reach conclusion of Trust not being eligible to tax, cannot be accepted in instant case. Further, by amendment act legislature has specifically brought in Port Trust also within definition of 'dealer' u/s. 2(g) of 1963 Act, hence, substratum of earlier judgment has been lost. Hence, SC held that activities of assessee in respect of buying, selling, supplying or distributing goods, executing works contract, transferring right to use any goods or supplying by way of or as part of any service, any goods directly or otherwise, whether for cash or for deferred payment or for commission, remuneration or other valuable consideration, whether in course of business or not, would fall within purview of s. 2(viii) of 1963 Act. Hence, assessee-Port Trust would fall within meaning of 'dealer' u/s. 2(viii) of 1963 Act and is consequently assessable to tax under Act. Order passed by lower Court is justified and in accordance with law. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Assessee which fall under meaning dealer under Act due to activities conducted by it would be liable to pay sales tax.
