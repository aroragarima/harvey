Canara Bank, represented by its Deputy General Manager v C. S. Shyam and another

Supreme Court of India

31 August 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Abhay Manohar Sapre, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 657

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: SERVICE, Appeal, Interest, Right To Information Act, 2005, Writ Petition, Employee

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Right to Information Act, 2005, ss. 6, 8(1)(j), 8(j) - Information sought -Public interest - Validity - Appellant/bank filed instant appeal challenging impugned order of HC whereby HC upheld judgment passed by Single Judge dismissing writ petition of appellant challenging the order of Central Information Commission holding that appellant should provide the information sought by respondent no.1 under the Act.

Held, information sought by respondent no.1 of individual employees working in the bank was personal in nature, secondly, it was exempted from being disclosed u/s. 8(j) of the Act and neither respondent no.1 disclosed any public interest much less larger public interest involved in seeking such information of the individual employee and nor any finding was recorded by Central Information Commission and HC as to the involvement of any larger public interest in supplying such information to respondent no. 1. Therefore, application made by respondent no.1 u/s. 6 of the Act was wholly misconceived and was rightly rejected by Public Information Officer and Chief Public Information Officer whereas wrongly allowed by Central Information Commission and HC. Impugned order of HC and Central Information Commission was set aside and restore orders passed by Public Information Officer and Chief Public Information Officer. Appeal allowed.
