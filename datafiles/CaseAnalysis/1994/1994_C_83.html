M/S. Astra Pharmaceuticals (P) Limited v Collector Of Central Excise, Chandigarh.

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 749; (1995) 2 SCC 84; 1995 (49) ECC 24; 1995 (56) ECR 638; 1995 (75) E.L.T. 214; JT 1995 (1) SC 276; 1995 (6) RLT 445; 1995(1) SCALE 17; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 692

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Tariff Item, Medicinal and Toilet Preparations (Excise Duties) Act, 1955

Summary: Excise - Medicinal & Toilet Preparations (Excise Duties) Act, 1955 Trade And Merchandise Marks Act, 1958 - Central Excises Act, 1944, s. 11-A and 35-L - Trade and Merchandise Marks Act, 1958 - Whether Dextrose injections manufactured by appellant were dutiable under Tariff Item 14-E? - Marks, symbols, monogram, label, signature or other words which are used in medicinal preparation or its container should be such as to indicate that medicine is a special preparation made by manufacturer - 'AP' or 'ASTRA' on container or packing was used to project image of manufacturer generally - It did not establish any relationship between mark and medicine - Held, Dextrose injections manufactured by appellant in relevant years were not patent and proprietary medicines dutiable under Tariff Item 14-E - Appeal allowed.
