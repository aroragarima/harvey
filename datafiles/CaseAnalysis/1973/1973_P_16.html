Phul Rani & Ors. v Naubat Rai Ahluwalia

Supreme Court of India

14 March 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 350; (1973) 1 SCC 688; AIR 1973 SC 2110; 1973 RCJ 521; 1973 RCR(Rent) 364; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 679

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Rent control - Eviction petition - Death of plaintiff - Plaintiff filed ejectment application against his tenant and sought possession of premises on ground of personal requirement - Application was dismissed by Rent Controller on preliminary ground and so, plaintiff filed appeal and during pendency of the same, plaintiff died - Tribunal set aside decision recorded by Rent Controller on preliminary issue and remanded ejectment application for decision on merits - Tenant filed second appeal to HC against order of remand, but the same was dismissed by HC - While so, Additional Rent Controller passed order of eviction against tenant as heirs of plaintiff required premises bona-fide for their occupation and on appeal, tenant contended that right to sue had not survived to heirs of plaintiff - Tribunal rejected contention of tenant and confirmed order of eviction on merits - However, on further appeal, HC held that right to sue did not survive to hires of plaintiff and dismissed ejectment application - Hence, instant appeal by heirs of plaintiff - Whether cause of action would survive to legal representatives of plaintiff or application abates.

Held, requirement pleaded in ejectment application and on which the plaintiff has founded his right to relief is his personal requirement. The appellants' emergence in the proceedings will require the determination of wholly different and distinct issues. Their requirements and not that of the plaintiff and the availability of them-not to the plaintiff-of other reasonably suitable residential accommodation will now form the centre of conflict. Amongst appellants are 2 married daughters of deceased plaintiff and 2 children of a deceased daughter of plaintiff. Their requirement would be basically different from that of the plaintiff and plaintiff's right to sue will thereafter not survive to the appellants and they could not glean the benefit of the original right to sue. Hence, in view of nature of claim made and bundle of facts constitute the plaintiff's cause of action; his right to sue will not survive to his legal representatives. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Pleadings of plaintiff alone could reveal true nature of right asserted to plaintiff in the ejectment proceedings.
