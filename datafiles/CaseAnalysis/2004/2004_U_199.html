Kalyani Packaging Industry v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 465; (2004) 6 SCC 719; 2004 (168) E.L.T. 145; 2004 (62) RLT 821; 2005 (141) STC 116

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Excise - Sales Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944 - Whether petitioners can rely on circular of 1995 when concerned period is August 1990 to January 1991? - Collector of Central Excise, Vadodara v Dhiren Chemical Industries 2002 Indlaw SC 1351 clarified that when an exemption notification uses words 'has already been paid', benefit of that notification would only be available if duty has, as a matter of fact, been paid and has been paid at appropriate or correct rate - Thus para 9 of Dhiren Chemical's case was incorporated to ensure that cases where benefits of exemption Notification had already been granted, revenue would remain bound - Purpose was to see that such cases were not reopened - Where as a result of dispute matter is sub-judice a court/tribunal is, after Dhiren Chemical's case, bound to interpret as set out in that judgment - Held, circular, being of 1995, can be of no assistance to petitioners - Interpretation given in Dhiren Chemical's case would apply - Petition dismissed.
