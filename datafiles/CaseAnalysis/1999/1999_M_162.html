Mudigonda Chandra Mouli Sastry v Bhimanepalli Bikshalu and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 515; (1999) 7 SCC 66; AIR 1999 SC 3095; 1999 (5) ALD(SC) 101; JT 1999 (6) SC 86; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 19; [1999] Supp1 S.C.R. 69

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Andhra Pradesh Buildings (Lease, Rent and Eviction) Control Act, 1960, ss. 10(4)(i) and 22 - Tenancy - (A) Immunity from eviction is available to tenant engaged in notified employment - Availability of protection on transfer in service whereby no longer required to cater essential services - (B) Revisional jurisdiction of High Court - Concurrent finding of fact of bona fide requirement is not legally infirm - Contrary finding in reassessment of evidence, if proper - Held, protection from eviction is not available to him - Not proper, particularly when tenant's wife had already acquired another vacant accommodation and tenant had also transferred to another city - Appeal allowed.
