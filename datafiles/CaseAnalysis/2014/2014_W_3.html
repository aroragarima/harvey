Oil and Natural Gas Corporation Limited v Western Geco international Limited

Supreme Court of India

04 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Adarsh Kumar Goel, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 587; (2014) 9 SCC 263; AIR 2015 SC 363; 2014 (107) ALR 333; 2014 (6) AWC 5898; 2016 (2) CompLJ 21; JT 2014 (10) SC 288; 2015 (2) MLJ 108; 2014(10) SCALE 328

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Pendente lite - Award - Legality - Instant appeal was filed by Appellant Corporation against order passed by DB whereby application filed by appellant Corporation has been partly allowed and order passed by Single Judge affirmed with modification that award of pendente lite and future interest by Tribunal should stand deleted - Whether impugned order passed by DB was justified -

Held, No less important is principle now recognized as salutary juristic fundamental in administrative law that a decision which is perverse or so irrational that no reasonable person would have arrived at the same will not be sustained in a Court of law. Decisions that fall short of standards of reasonableness are open to challenge in Court of law often in writ jurisdiction of Superior Courts but no less in statutory processes where ever same are available. Arbitrators fail to draw an inference which ought to have been drawn or if they have drawn an inference which is on face of it, untenable resulting in miscarriage of justice, adjudication even when made by an arbitral tribunal that enjoys considerable latitude and play at joints in making awards will be open to challenge and may be cast away or modified depending upon whether the offending part is or is not severable from the rest. Arbitrators clubbed entire period between 16/10/2001 and 21/3/2002 for purposes of holding appellant Corporation responsible for delay, they committed an error resulting in miscarriage of justice apart from fact that they failed to appreciate and draw inferences that logically flow from such proved facts. SC have no hesitation in rejecting contention urged on behalf of respondent that the arbitral award should not despite infirmities pointed out by us be disturbed. That brings SC to last submission that deduction on account of taxes not paid should have been allowed by respondent/Tribunal. Tribunal correctly held that no part of work was undertaken outside Singapore which was to be executed on a turnkey basis for a price that was pre-determined. Arbitrators rightly held that no taxes were payable under the Act so as to entitle Corporation to deduct any amount on that account by reason of non-payment of such taxes. Award made by arbitrators modified to extent with a proportionate reduction in amount payable to respondent. Appeal allowed.
