T.A. Ahammed Kabeer v A.A. Azeez and others

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 353; (2003) 5 SCC 650; AIR 2003 SC 2271; JT 2003 (4) SC 110; 2003 (2) KHCACJ 344; 2003 (2) KLT 472; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 250; 2003(3) SCALE 757; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 511; 2003 (3) SLT 390; 2003 (3) Supreme 584

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Election Dispute, Chief Electoral Officers

Summary: Representation of the People Act, 1951 - Ss. 97, 100(1)(d)(iii) & (iv) - election petition - held, the pleadings and proof in the matter of recount have relevance for the purpose of determining the question of jurisdiction to permit or not to permit recount. Once the jurisdiction to order recount is found to have been rightly exercised, thereafter it is the truth as revealed by the result of recounting that has to be given effect to - a case of error in counting would fall within the purview of Section 100(1)(d)(iv) and not Section 100(1)(d)(iii). - On the available evidence an inference as to the result of the election having been materially affected insofar as the returned candidate is concerned, cannot be drawn.
