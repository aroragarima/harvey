Â Ramesh and Another v Seth Gendalal Motilal Patni and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 January 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, P. Satyanarayana Raju

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 395; AIR 1966 SC 1445; 1966 (68) BomLR 776; 1966 (2) SCJ 762; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 198

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Board of Revenue Act, 1913 - Prohibition Act - Madhya Pradesh Abolition of Proprietary Rights (Estates, Mahals, Alienated Lands) Act, 1950, s. 19 (1) - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 133 (a)(b), 226 and 227 - Abolition Act, ss. 17 (a), 12 and 22 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 109 and 110 - On passing of Abolition Act, appellants applied u/s 19(1) of Act for determination of their debts - First respondent, a creditor, had a mortgage debt originally but it had resulted in a decree - Respondent objected u/s 21 that this had ceased to be secured debt or secured claim for application of s. 17(a) of Abolition Act - Claims Officer accepted objection and discharged claim by order D/- 24-12-1962 - Respondent appealed to Commr. - Order of Claims Officer was later held to have revived but claim could not be discharged as action u/s 22(1) had not been taken - Appellants thereupon filed a petition u/arts. 226 and 227 of constitution in High Court of Bombay on ground that Commr. had no jurisdiction to entertain and decide appeal and that Claims Officer had ordered continuation of proceedings and so order of Commr. was wrong - HC summarily dismissed petition by its first order D/- 21-09-1964 against which SLP (Civil) No. 395 of 1965 has been filed - Appellants next applied for a certificate which was refused by order D/- 01-02-1965, impugned in present appeal - (A) Whether appellants were entitled to a certificate as of right u/art. 133 (1)(a) or (b)? - Present appeal involves a consideration of sub-cls. (a) and (b) only because it is submitted, certificate was claimable as of right - Art.133 must cover all civil proceedings because no exception is indicated - In present case Claims Officer purported to exercise a jurisdiction under which he could order discharge of a debt which means that order affected civil rights of parties - Here civil rights of parties were directly involved and proceeding before HC was thus a civil proceeding - First requisite for application of art. 133(1) is thus satisfied - (B) What are different kinds of decisions from which appeals lie u/art. 133? - Held, art. 133 not only discards distinction between appellate and original jurisdictions but deliberately used words which are as wide as language can make them - Intention is not only to include all judgments, decrees and orders passed in exercise of appellate and ordinary original civil jurisdiction but also to make language wide enough to cover other jurisdictions under which civil rights would come before HC for decision - As other requirements of article were satisfied HC was in error in refusing certificate in this case - Order dated 01-02-1965 is set aside - Appeal allowed.
