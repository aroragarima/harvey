Suresh v Mahadevappa Shivappa Danannava and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 114; (2005) 3 SCC 670; (2005) SCC (Cr) 783; AIR 2005 SC 1047; JT 2005 (2) SC 462; 2005 (2) RCR(Criminal) 29; 2005(2) SCALE 169; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 131

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Land & Property

Keywords: Counter-Affidavit, Charge - Sheet, Code of Criminal Procedure, Cognizance of an offence, Cognizable offence, Criminal Procedure, Criminal Procedure Code

Summary: MIscellaneous - Agreement to sell - Failure to perform - Private complaint - Whether criminal offence under s. 420 IPC proved?; whether private complaint maintainable? - Held, allegations contained in the complaint are of civil nature and do not prima facie disclose commission of alleged criminal offence under s. 420 - Complaint was filed after a lapse of 11 years therefore private complaint filed by respondent No 1 is not at all maintainable - Appeal allowed.
