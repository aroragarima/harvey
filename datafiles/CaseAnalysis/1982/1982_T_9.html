Devji Vallabhbhai Tandel Etc v The Administrator Of Goa, Daman & Diu & Anr

Supreme Court of India

29 March 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

Baharul Islam, D.A. Desai, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 118; (1982) 2 SCC 222; (1982) SCC (Cr) 403; AIR 1982 SC 1029; [1983] 54 Comp Cas 571; 1982 CRLJ 799; 1982 CrLR(SC) 157; 1982(1) SCALE 246; [1982] 3 S.C.R. 553

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Fema

Summary: Constitution, arts. 21, 22(5), 74, 162 and 239 - Government of Union Territories Act, 1963, s. 44 - COFEPOSA Act, 1974, ss. 2(f), 3, 3(1), 5 and 8 - Detention - (A) Scope of s. 4 of 1963 Act - Competency of Administrator of UTs to pass order of detention - He is not a purely constitutional functionary like President or Governor - And not bound to act on advice of Council of Ministers in all matters - (B) Right of reprsentation - Copy of detention order served in English - But copy of grounds served in language understood by detenu - Specific mention of preventing him from smuggling goods - No handicap in submitting representation - (C) Scope of right of representation - (A) Held, he is competent to pass order of detention - (B) No violation of art. 22 (1) on ground that order of detention was in English - (C) Detenu not entitled to be heard personally nor through lawyer - Need not be produced before Magistrate within 24 hours of detention - Nor entitled to consult or be defended by lawyer of his choice - Petition Dismissed.
