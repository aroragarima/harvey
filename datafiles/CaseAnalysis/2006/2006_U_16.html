U.P.S.R.T.C. Limited v Sarada Prasad Misra and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 628; (2006) 4 SCC 733; (2006) SCC (L&S) 857; AIR 2006 SC 2466; 2006 (3) AWC 2257; JT 2006 (5) SC 114; 2006(4) SCALE 507; 2006 (5) SCJ 218; 2007 (1) SLR 382; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 1; 2006 (2) UPLBEC 1653

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2 A - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Whether action of employers in terminating the services of the respondent/ workman is legal and/or valid? - If not, then to what relief the concerned workman is entitled? - The action of termination of services of the respondent was taken in 1975, but an application was made for the first time only in July, 1982 i.e., after seven years - Neither the Conciliation Officer should have submitted 'failure report', nor the State Government should have referred the matter for adjudication to Labour Court - The Labour Court ought not to have passed an award of reinstatement with continuity in service and back wages - Appointment of the workman was ad hoc and purely temporary - He was paid one month's salary on 06-09-1975 when his services were terminated - There was delay on the part of the workman/respondent and the action was not maintainable and no relief ought to have been granted by the Labour Court or by the High Court - Since the respondent had invoked jurisdiction of Labour forum after seven years, it would not be appropriate to direct the appellant Corporation to pay full wages for the intervening period - Held, The reinstatement is restored - Respondent is not entitled to his wages in accordance with wages from 1975 to 1984 - Held, The appeal is accordingly disposed of - Appeal is partly allowed.
