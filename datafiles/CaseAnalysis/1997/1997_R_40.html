High Court of Judicature at Allahabad through its Registrar v Raj Kishore Yadav and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, A.M. Ahmadi, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2435; (1997) 3 SCC 11; AIR 1997 SC 1186; 1997 ALJ 795; JT 1997 (3) SC 47; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 429; 1997 (3) UPLBEC 1817

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Court Of Record, Indian Independence Act, 1947

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 - Writ petition challenging vireof High Court Rules - (A) Whether decision rendered without notice to HC on administrative side violative of natural justice and liable to be set aside - Held, it is violative of natural justice and therefore liable to be set aside - (B) Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 215 and 225 - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss. 2 and 23 - Whether contempt jurisdiction is independent jurisdiction of original nature or review jurisdiction? - Further held, it is independent jurisdiction of original nature - Procedure of its exercise can be framed by HC u/s. 23 of Act or art. 225 of Constitution - Appeal allowed.
