Mahant Dharam Das Etc. Etc. v The State Of Punjab And Ors

Supreme Court of India

14 January 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, A.N. Ray, Hans Raj Khanna, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 35; (1975) 1 SCC 343; AIR 1975 SC 1069; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 160

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Anganbari Sevika, Titles, Madras Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Act, 1951, Sikh Gurdwaras Act, 1925, Citizenship Rules, 1956 (As amended upto 20-4-2000)

Summary: Constitution, arts. 14, 19(1)(f), 26, 133, 226 and 372 - Sikh Gurdwaras Act, 1925, ss. 3(4), 7(3), 7(5) and 8 - Gurudwara - Declared as a Sikh Gurdwara (long prior to the Constitution) - Managed by Interim Gurdwara Board constituted in 1946 by Firman of Erstwhile Ruler of State - Locus standi to challenge the factum that it is a Sikh Gurdwara - (B) Validity of ss. 3(4) and 7(5) of the 1925 Act - (C) Limitation - Period of 90 days prescribed u/s.8 for filing the petition - (D) Presumption as to constitutional validity of the provision - Held, petitioner could not be allowed to challenge the factum that the Gurdwara is a Sikh Gurdwara - Provisions do not suffer from any constitutional or other legal impediment - Period is not unreasonable - Interference by Supreme Court -Appeal Dismissed.
