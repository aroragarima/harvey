E. D. Sassoon and Company Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

29 August 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 261; (1973) 3 SCC 67; 1973 (1) ITJ 387; [1972] 86 ITR 757; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 1084; 1972 TAXLR 1180

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Business Income or Loss

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 25(3) - Incorporation of companies - Articles of association - Purchase of shares and securities - Assessment of tax - Firm carried on business of dealers in shares and securities - In accounting year 1919 firm incurred loss but in 1920 large profits made in London Exchange Account - Firm claimed depreciation in shares and securities - Further, two companies were incorporated, former company and latter was assessee company - According to cl. 4 of articles of association of company the assessee company had entered into agreement that company took over business of firm and also purchased shares and securities - Assessee company was assessed under 1922 Act - In A/Y 1949-50 assessee company discontinued its business and claimed exemption u/s. 25(3) of Act - Claim was rejected by Income Tax Officer(IO) - Appellate Assistant Commissioner found that business discontinued in year of assessment was not charged to tax under 1918 Act - Tribunal observed that firm was assessed to tax under 1918 Act and disallowed relief on ground that assessee company had not intended to do business of dealing in securities acquired from old firm - HC answered referred questions against revenue and decided that assessee company entitled to relief u/s. 25(3) of Act - Hence instant appeal - Whether, assessee company was entitled to relief u/s. 25(3) of 1922 Act - Held, there was no material on record to justify Income Tax Authorities or Tribunal that shares and stocks transferred to assessee's company were only intended as investments - It was not that assessee company had not dealt in shares and stocks in that year nor was there any basis for IO and Appellate Assistant Commissioner in holding that former took over share holding from firm and not assessee company - Shares and securities were only transferred to former company in 1922 - Assessee company was entitled to relief u/s.25(3) of Act - Judgment of HC was confirmed - Appeal dismissed.
