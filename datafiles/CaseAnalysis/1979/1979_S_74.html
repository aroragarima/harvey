Ratan Lal Shinghal v Smt. Murti Devi

Supreme Court of India

21 August 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 374; (1980) 4 SCC 258; AIR 1980 SC 635; 1980 ALJ 1136; 1979 (5) ALR 595

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Civil Procedure - Uttar Pradesh Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972 - Eviction of tenant - Prospective Statutory operation - Claim for exemption - Petitioner was tenant and respondent landlord sought eviction of petitioner - Respondent sought eviction of petitioner relying on 1972 Act that new buildings constructed during the period of 10 years would be given exemption - Petitioner sought 1972 Act would not apply - Whether 1972 Act by which new buildings constructed during the period of 10 years would be given exemption from operation of Act apply to buildings constructed prior to the amendment.

Held, ordinarily a statute like this is prospective in operation unless there is clear legislative intent to the contrary. Point of law is of frequent occurrence and may affect judicially a number of tenants in these days of accommodation scarcity. 1972 Act is prospective and applies only to buildings brought into being de novo after the Act came into force. Petitioner has forfeited his right to persuade Court to permit him to raise a new question. Petitioner by consent will be allowed one year to give vacant possession from today and he will put an undertaking in writing to that effect within two weeks. Petition partly allowed.

Ratio - 1972 Act by which new buildings constructed during the period of 10 years would be given exemption from the operation of the Act, does not apply to buildings constructed prior to the amendment.
