Collector and Deputy Commissioner, Sultanpur, and Another v Raja Jagdish Pratap Sahi

Supreme Court of India

05 November 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 253; AIR 1965 SC 909; 1965 ALJ 705; 1965 CRLJ 18; 1965 (1) ITJ 437; [1965] 57 ITR 784; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 29

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Uttar Pradesh Agricultural Income Tax Act, 1948, s. 3 - Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950, ss. 6, 26, 68 and 2(13) - Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Rules, r. 8-A - Whether an order adjusting liability for amount due as tax payable under U.P. Agricultural Income Tax Act against compensation bonds cannot be made by High Court? - Held, r. 8-A states that amount due from an intermediary for any period prior to date of vesting shall be realized in manner indicated in cls. (a) and (b) - Rule is in terms mandatory and obliges Collector to realize tax in manner provided - Expression 'Without prejudice to right of State Govt. to recover dues' with which rule opens does not transform that duty into an option - If, for any reason, adjustment cannot be made effective and amount due to state as tax or as land revenue cannot be recovered by adjustment, right of State Govt. to recover dues in any other manner permitted by law is not to be prejudiced - Collector had no option in matter of adjustment of liability to pay agricultural income Tax against compensation amount due to respondent which was 'still due' - Further held, s. 3 of U.P. Agricultural Income Tax Act emphatically charges total agricultural income of previous year to tax - It is true that assessment under Act is made after close of previous year, but income which is liable to tax is income of previous year - Case clearly fell within terms of s. 6(d) and benefit of r. 8-A was admissible to respondent - In making order HC has proceeded on assumption that compensation bonds remain to be delivered to respondent - There are no materials on which truth of assumption may be ascertained - Order passed by HC set aside - Case remanded back to HC for deciding whether there are any compensation bonds remaining to be delivered, and if not, whether by any appropriate order or direction, adjustment of tax liability against compensation due to respondent, which has been directed by HC u/r. 8-A can be made effective - Order accordingly.
