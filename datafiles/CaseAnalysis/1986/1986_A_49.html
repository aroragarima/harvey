Chandavarkar Sita Ratna Rao v Ashalata S. Guram

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 516; (1986) 4 SCC 447; AIR 1987 SC 117; 1986 (3) Bom.C.R. 304; JT 1986 SC 619; 1987 (1) RCJ 321; 1987 (1) RCR(Rent) 154; 1987 (1) RentLR 684; 1986(2) SCALE 500; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 866

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 15, 52, 106 and 108 - Easements Act, 1882, ss. 52 and 53 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 227 - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, ss. 5 and 15 - (A)Whether High Court in exercise of powers u/art. 227 of Constitution interfere with findings of facts by appropriate authorities? - Powers of judicial interference u/art. 227 of Constitution on orders of judicial or quasi-judicial nature, are not greater than power u/art. 226 of Constitution - Art. 226 confers power of interference to quashing an impugned order on ground of a mistake apparent on face of record - Art. 227 of Constitution confers limited power of interference to extent that tribunal functions within limits of its authority - HC should not interfere with a finding of inferior tribunal except where findings are perverse and not based on any material evidence leading to miscarriage of injustice - (B) Whether and how far a statutory tenant governed by Bombay Rent Act, 1947 could have created a valid licence before 1973 - Licensees created whether by landlords or tenants before 1-2-1973 and in occupation of at least single room are entitled to be tenants - Specific term for creating licence whether exists or not in original agreement is immaterial - Until a decree of eviction is passed against tenant, tenant can create a licence and where licence was created before 01-02-1973, licensee must, by express terms of s. 15A of Act, continue to be a tenant of landlord in respect of premises in question - Tenant protected by a statute is entitled to create a licence - No distinction could be made between statutory tenant and a contractual tenant - Non obstante clause cannot be so interpreted as to exclude from benefit of s. 15A licensees of tenants who were not specifically entitled to sub-lease - Appeal allowed.
