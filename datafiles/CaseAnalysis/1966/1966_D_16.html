Mst. Ramrati Kuer v Dwarika Prasad Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 August 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 306; AIR 1967 SC 1134; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 153

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s.32 (3),114 and 158 - A Deed of gift executed on 32-07-1953 by Mst. Phuljhari Kuer in favour of appellant Ramrati Kuer claimed to be not binding on plaintiffs respondents - Mst. Phuljhari Kuer was originally a defendant but died during pendency of suit - (A) Whether statement of Mst. Phuljhari Kuer is admissible u/s.32(3) of Evidence Act, 1872? - U/s.32(3) of Act of 1872, a statement of a person who is dead is admissible when statement is against pecuniary or proprietary interest of person making it, or when if true, it would expose him or would have exposed him to a criminal prosecution or to a suit for damages - Statement of Mst. Phuljhari Kuer is against her proprietary interest - There is no doubt that this statement of Mst. Phuljhari Kuer is against her proprietary interest - Held, statement of Mst. Phuljhari Kuer would be admissible in evidence u/s. 32(3) as she is dead - Of course, it would not be an admission so far as appellant is concerned, but it would certainly be a piece of evidence to be taken into consideration -(B)Whether statement made in gift deed of 1953 contradicts earlier statement and whether value to be attached to statement in gift deed? Further held, on facts that statement made in gift deed did not directly contradict her earlier statement and that it carried little value as compared to her earlier statement - (C) Whether statements made by Mst. Phuljhari Kuer in gift deed would be admissible in view of s.158 of Act of 1872? - Statement in 1925 was made when there was no trouble whatsoever in family and therefore that statement is entitled to great weight - Statement made in gift deed was apparently made at time when troubles had begun and in any case a person making a gift of property would say how she had title to property and such a statement would in circumstances have little value - Held, statement is admissible u/s.158 of Act of 1872 and it completely overweighs circumstances on which appellant relies - Appeal dismissed.
