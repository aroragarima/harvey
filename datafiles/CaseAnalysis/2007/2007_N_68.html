Yallwwa and Others v National Insurance Company Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 536; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 247; (2007) 6 SCC 657; 2007 (3) ACC 269; AIR 2007 SC 2582; 2008 (1) ALD(SC) 117; 2007(4) ALL MR 822; 2007 (68) ALR 473; 2007 (4) ALT 45; 2007 (3) AWC 2480; JT 2007 (8) SC 1; 2007 (3) KLT 91; 2007 (4) MahLJ 543; 2007 (5) MLJ(SC) 322; 2007 (3) MPLJ 260; 2007 (148) PLR 82; 2007 (3) RCR(Civil) 265; 2007(8) SCALE 77; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 1113; 2007 (3) UC 1818

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Death Or Permanent Disablement, Maharashtra Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Tribunal directed both the owner as also Respondent No. 1 (Insurance Company) to deposit a sum of Rs. 50, 000/- each for every deceased within a period of one month - Appeal against - Whether an order passed under s. 140 is an appealable one? - Held, an order of the Tribunal awarding compensation under s. 140 is appealable under s. 173 as it amounts to an award under s. 173 - Appeal dismissed.
