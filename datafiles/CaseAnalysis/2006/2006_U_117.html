Pallava Granite Industries (India) Private Limited v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1371; (2007) 15 SCC 30; JT 2006 (10) SC 318; 2006(11) SCALE 511; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 633

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Granite, Andhra Pradesh Minor Mineral Concession Rules, 1966

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Land & Property - Administrative - Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957, ss. 13, 17A, 17A(2), 5(1) - Grant of mining lease - Revoking G.O. - Depriving mining rights - Succeed - An area admeasuring Acs. 86.50 in Survey no. 55/5 being agricultural lands vested as surplus lands u/s. 11 of the Act surrendered by land holders to State Govt. - Later on, it was realized that these lands had galaxy granites and therefore proposals were made by Collector and Director of Mines and Geology to lease out said area for mining purposed - By G.O. No. 1290 dt. 27-8-1991, State Govt. accepted proposals and decided to appropriate by leasing out lands for mining u/s. 14(6) of the Act, out of total area surrendered lands, an area admeasuring Acs. 60 be leased out to appellants - G.O. No. 1290 was challenged in PIL, when notice of PIL was received by State Govt. , G.O. No. 1361 dt. 11-9-1991 came to be issued by which earlier G.O. No. 1290, stood cancelled and this cancellation was challenged by appellant's petition in HC - Single Judge of HC held that cancellation of G.O. No. 1290 was ab initio void and Dist. Collector was directed to execute surface lease in favour of appellants in terms of G.O. No. 1290 - Aggrieved by judgment , State Govt. preferred Writ appeal before DB and pending writ appeal, Collector wrote to Director of Mines and Geology stating that in view of judgment dt. 18-10-1996 he had no objection to grant of mining lease in terms of G.O. No. 1290 - DB stating that there was no infirmity in judgment of Single Judge and there was violation of rules of natural justice in as much as decision to cancel G.O. No.1290 was taken without hearing and without giving reasons and was therefore void and accordingly writ appeal was dismissed - However, it was clear by DB that its judgment and order dt. 24-3-2004, it was totally unnecessary to examine validity of G.O.Nos. 267 and 268 respectively since subsequent decision dt. 29-10-1999 Central Govt. had granted approval for reserving an area admeasuring Acs. 61.50 as granite bearing area and further held, decision to cancel G.O.No. 1290 was valid, that reservation of an area admeasuring Acs. 61.50 were in public interest and that there were no mala fides in cancelling G.O.No. 1290 as alleged by appellants - Hence, instant appeal - Whether decision to cancel G.O.No.1290 was valid in law and whether that decision stood vitiated by mala fides - Held, order of DB was upheld and property belongs to State Govt. and mineral vests in State Govt., so State had decided to earn more revenue by inviting global tenders and State had obtained prior approval of Central Govt. - Hence, Central Govt. had restricted its approval to an area admeasuring Acs.61.50. and in circumstances, did not find any illegality in State Govt.'s order of reserving area admeasuring Acs.61.50 for mining operations through private/public sector enterprises - Therefore, under G.O.No.1290 were inchoate rights, these rights never stood crystallized and no mining lease was ever granted by State Govt. to appellants and there was no bar in reserving an area admeasuring Acs.61.50 for exploitation of galaxy granite through State public sector undertaking - Appeal dismissed.
