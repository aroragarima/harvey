P. N. Krishna Lal and Others v Government of Kerala and Another

Supreme Court of India

17 November 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1178; (1995) Supp2 SCC 187; (1995) SCC (Cr) 466; AIR 1995 SCW 1325; JT 1994 (7) SC 608; 1994(5) SCALE 1; 1995 (2) SCJ 1; [1994] Supp5 S.C.R. 526

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Universal Declaration of Human Rights, Dangerous Drugs Act, 1930, Madras Prohibition Act, 1937, Statutory Presumption, Drugs[and Cosmetics] Act, 1940, Prevention of Food Adulteration (Extension to Kohima and Mokokchung Districts) Repeal Act, 2002

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Dangerous Drugs Act, 1930 -Government Of India Act, 1935 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Madras Prohibition Act, 1937 - Prevention of Food Adulteration Repeal Act, 2002 - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954 - Universal Declaration Of Human Rights - Noxious substance - Adulteration - Conviction - Violation - Appellants were licencees of arrack liquor retail shops charged for offences punishable u/s. 57A (1) (3) for having mixed noxious substance with liquor as it consumption, would endanger human life - Appellants were charged for offences before competent criminal courts - Appellants assailed constitutionality of Amendment Act, contended that provisions 57-A and 57-B was in violation of arts.14, 20(3) ,21 of Constitution - Hence instant appeal - Whether provisions 57-A and 57-B was in violation of arts.14, 20(3) ,21 of Constitution - Held, there was no inherent right in citizen to trade in adulterated intoxicated liquor by retail or wholesale - It was obvious that dealing in liquor inherently pernicious was within legislative competence under Act - Trial Court had wide discretion to impose sentence based on fact situation and circumstances to minimum sentence prescribed under Act - Object of Amendment Act is to put down menace of adulteration of arrack - Possession of liquor by appellants was offence u/s.57-A (3) and offence created could not be declared to be ultra vires on its basis - Appellants charged for offence stand as class and there was no invidious discrimination on proof of charge for punishment envisaged under relevant provisions of ss.57-A or 57-B and provisions were not in violation of arts.14, 20(3),21 of the Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
