C. K. Sasankan v Dhanalakshmi Bank Limited

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 247; (2009) 11 SCC 60; AIR 2009 SC 3171; 2009 (76) ALR 894; 2009 (3) AWC 2355; 2011 (1) BC 122; 2009 (2) CTC 381; JT 2009 (4) SC 477; 2009 (4) MLJ(SC) 156; 2009 (155) PLR 591; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 436; 2009 (107) RD 707; 2009(3) SCALE 724; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 494; 2009 (1) UC 654

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure

Keywords: Commercial Transaction, Bank Rate, Overdraft Facility, Quarterly Rest, Principal Sum Adjudged, New India Assurance Co.

Summary: Banking & Finance - CPC, 1908, s. 34 - Appeal against declaration that the debts due to the bank is Rs. 28, 50, 707.03 together with interest @ 25% per annum compounding with quarterly rests from 17.7.1991 i.e. from the date of filing of the suit till the date of the Judgment with a simple interest at the rate of 19.4% per annum from 11.08.2001 till realization - Held, quantum and rate of interest which the appellant in the present case is entitled to would be in accordance with the provisions of s. 34 - According to the provisions of s. 34 of the Code interest is to be awarded at a reasonable rate and on the principal amount - In absence of any agreement or statutory provision or a mercantile usage, interest payable can be only at the market rate and such interest is payable upon establishment of totality of circumstances justifying exercise of such equitable jurisdiction - Rate of interest as awarded for pendente lite and future interest is exorbitant - Directed that pendente lite and future interest at the rate of 9% shall be paid - Appeal partly allowed.
