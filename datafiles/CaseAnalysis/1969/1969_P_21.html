Nathulal v Phoolchand

Supreme Court of India

16 October 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 208; (1969) 3 SCC 120; AIR 1970 SC 546; 1970 ALJ 442; 1970 ALJ 742; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 854; 1970 UJ 119

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract Act, 1872, s. 31 and 55 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 53A - Madhya Bharat Land Revenue and Tenancy Act, 1950, s. 70(8) - Contract - (A) Conditional agreement - To transfer land - Statute requirement of prior permission of authority - (B) Readiness and willingness - Purchaser need not produce money or vouch a concluded scheme for financing the transaction - (C) Provision of s. 53A of 1882 Act - Defence of part performance - Conditions essential - Sale of land - Necessity of permission - Held, agreement must be deemed subject to implied condition - Purchaser need not produce money or vouch a concluded scheme for financing the transaction - Held, s.70(8) of Madhya Bharat Land Revenue and Tenancy Act creates no bar - Appeal Dismissed.
