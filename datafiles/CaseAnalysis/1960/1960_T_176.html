Â Shivji Nathubhai v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 January 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 430; AIR 1960 SC 606; 1960 MPLJ 516; 1960 (62) PLR 641; 1960 SCJ 579; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 775

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Mineral Concession Rules, 1949, rr. 29, 32, 52, 54 and 55 - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1941, s. 6 - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) (Amendment) Act, 1957 - Whether decisions taken by Central Govt. under Acts of 1949 and 1957 are quasi-judicial or administrative in nature in so far as cancelling lease contract without hearing appellant? - Held, when statutory rule grants a right to any party aggrieved to make a review application to Central Govt.,it certainly follows that person in whose favour order is made has also a right to represent his case before authority to whom review application is made - It is in circumstances apparent that as soon as r. 52 gives a right to an aggrieved party to apply for review a lis is created between him and party in whose favour grant has been made - Unless, therefore, there is anything in statute to contrary it will be the duty of authority to act judicially and its decision would be a quasi-judicial act - Rules and Act, as they stood at relevant time, Central Govt. was acting in a quasi-judicial capacity while deciding application u/r. 54 - Appeal allowed.
