T.V. Venugopal v Ushodaya Enterprises Ltd. & Another

Supreme Court of India

03 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 184; (2011) 4 SCC 85; 2011 (1) CLT(SC) 243; JT 2011 (3) SC 225; 2011 (2) MLJ(SC) 849; 2011 (45) PTC 433; 2011(3) SCALE 182; [2011] 4 S.C.R. 1000

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Intellectual Property

Keywords: Trade Marks Rules, 1959, Federal Anti Dilution Act, 1996

Summary: Intellectual Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Infringement - Passing off the trade mark - Appellant was the sole proprietor of a firm carrying on business inter alia as manufacturers of and dealers in incense sticks (agarbathis) in the name and style of 'Ashika's Eenadu' - Respondent, engaged in the business of publishing a newspaper in Telugu entitled as 'Eenadu', filed a suit for infringement of copyrights and passing-off- trade mark - Appellant contended that the word 'Eenadu' was a well known appearing in all the South Indian languages - It means 'today' in Telugu and in Tamil, Malayalam and Kannada it means 'this land' - Therefore, no absolute monopoly could either be claimed or vest in any single proprietor in respect of the entire spectrum of goods and/or services - HC decreed said suit in favour of the respondent - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether HC rightly decreed said suit in favour of the respondent? - Held, respondent's mark 'Eenadu' had acquired extraordinary reputation and goodwill in the State of Andhra Pradesh - Respondent's products and services were correlated, identified and associated with the word 'Eenadu' in the entire State of Andhra Pradesh - 'Eenadu' means literally the products or services provided by the respondent company in the State of Andhra Pradesh - Hence, appellant could not be referred or termed as an honest concurrent user of the mark 'Eenadu' - By adopting the mark 'Eenadu' in the State of Andhra Pradesh, the appellant clearly wanted to ride on the reputation and goodwill of the respondent - Permitting the appellant to sell his product with the mark 'Eenadu' would be encroaching on the reputation and goodwill of the respondent and this would constitute invasion of proprietary rights vested with the respondent - Hence, impugned judgment of the HC upheld - Appeals disposed of.
