Kamlesh Verma v Mayawati and others

Supreme Court of India

08 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 491; (2013) 8 SCC 320; AIR 2013 SC 3301; 2013 (4) CTC 882; JT 2013 (12) SC 155; 2013(10) SCALE 113; [2013] 11 S.C.R. 25

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Reviewing judgment

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B r/w s. 420, 467, 468 and 471 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(2) r/w s. 13(1)(d), 13(1)(e) - Cheating - Reviewing judgment - Legality - SC by order dated 16-07-2003 in M.C. Mehta vs. Union of India & Ors., 2003 Indlaw SC 1785, directed respondent no.2-CBI to conduct inquiry on basis of IA filed in petition alleging various irregularities committed by officers/persons concerned in Taj Heritage Corridor Project and to submit preliminary report - By order dated 21-08-2003 in M.C. Mehta vs. Union of India 2003 Indlaw SC 1800, SC issued certain directions to CBI to interrogate and verify assets of persons concerned with regard to outflow of Rs. 17 crores which was alleged to have been released without proper sanction for project - CBI was directed to conduct inquiry to execution of Taj Heritage Corridor Project under Taj Trapezium Zone (TTZ) area at which culminated into registration of FIR dated 05-10-2003 u/s. 120-B r/w s. 420, 467, 468 and 471 IPC and u/s. 13(2) r/w s. 13(1)(d) of the Act on several persons including petitioner - On same date CBI/ACP, lodged another FIR was filed u/s. 13(2) r/w s. 13(1)(e) of the Act only against petitioner alleging to acquisition of disproportionate movable and immovable assets of petitioner and her close relatives - SC order dated 06-07-2012 quashed FIR dated 05-10-2003 holding that order dated 18-09-2003 did not contain any specific direction regarding lodging of FIR in matter of disproportionate assets case against petitioner and CBI exceeded its jurisdiction in lodging same and allowed application for intervention - Hence instant petition - Whether petitioner had made out case for reviewing judgment and order dated 06-07-2012 and satisfied criteria for entertaining in review jurisdiction - Held, it was clear that FIR was lodged under orders and directions of SC - Earlier orders which were passed at time of original hearing and same orders and arguments were advanced by then Additional Solicitor General for CBI as well as on behalf of intervener - In order dtd. 06-07-2012, it was dealt with and duly considered at length and it was clarified that anything beyond Taj Corridor matter was not subject matter of reference before Taj Corridor Bench and CBI was not justifying in proceeding with FIR dated 05-10-2003 since order dated 18-09-2003 did not contain any specific direction regarding lodging of FIR in matter of disproportionate assets case against petitioner - It was made clear that matter relating to claim of CBI, intervener or stand of petitioner except directions relating to Taj Heritage Corridor Project which was only lis before petition - Hence, there was no material within parameters of review jurisdiction to go into earlier order dated 06-07-2012 - Petition disposed of.
