Joseph Vilangandan v Executive Engineer, Buildings and Roads (P.W.D.)

Supreme Court of India

20 March 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 320; (1978) 3 SCC 36; AIR 1978 SC 930; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 514; 1978 UJ 324

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Blacklisting - Validity - Appellant, a Govt. Contractor of 16 years standing, was executing major building contracts - Executive Engineer (Respondent No. 1) invited tenders for executing certain repairs to the English and Mathematics Blocks of the Maharaja College - Appellant submitted a tender - Formal agreement was executed, by the appellant and the Executive Engineer - Appellant alleged that there was delay in starting the work was due to the delay in handing over the building to him - However, the Executive Engineer cancelled contract and debarred the appellant from any further contract under the Buildings & Roads Division - Writ petition was filed, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, blacklisting has the effect of preventing a person from the privilege and advantage of entering into lawful relationship with the Govt. for purposes of gains. The fact that a disability is created by the order of blacklisting indicates that the relevant authority is to have an objective satisfaction. Fundamentals of fair play require that the person concerned should be given an opportunity to represent his case before he is put on the black list. Relied on notice dated 17-4-1968, given by the Executive Engineer to the appellant. The judgment of the HC set aside. Appeal allowed.
