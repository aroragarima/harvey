State of Bihar, Etc v Kapil Singh, Etc

Supreme Court of India

18 April 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, S.M. Sikri, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 123; AIR 1969 SC 53; 1969 CRLJ 279; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 810

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 164 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34 and 411 - Murder - Blood stained articles recovered from appellants - One eye-witness - Appellants convicted by Sessions Judge - One of appellants acquitted by High Court - Special Leave Petition preferred by state against acquittal and also by other appellants against convictions - Whether evidence of single eye-witness of 12 yrs can be relied upon for purpose of conviction? - Held, girl was kept confined in police station for many hours before recording her statement - Such statement can not be relied on - Prosecution failed to prove case beyond reasonable doubt - Appeal against convictions allowed, appeal against acquittal dismissed.
