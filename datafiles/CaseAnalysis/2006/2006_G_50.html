Government of India and Another v George Philip

Supreme Court of India

16 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1295; (2006) 13 SCC 1; AIR 2007 SC 705; 2006 (7) AWC 7649; 2007 (112) FLR 1067; JT 2006 (10) SC 487; 2006(12) SCALE 122; 2007 (2) SLR 769; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 108; 2007 (1) UPLBEC 468

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - Administrative - Central Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1964, r. 3(1)(iii) - Central Civil Services (Classification and Control of Appeal ) Rules, 1965, rr. 14 & 15(4) - Grant of Leaves for Specific Purpose - Compulsory Retirement - Reinstatement - Justifiability - Appellants ordered for compulsory retirement of respondent, in violation undertaking given by him, stating that he would not register for a Ph.D. degree and would not request for extension of leave - Respondent challenged order before Tribunal, which quashed punishment - Appellants filed review which was dismissed - Respondent filed application before Tribunal, which set aside penalty and directed reinstatement with full back wages - Appellants filed writ petition before HC, which set aside order of appellant with direction that if respondent reports for duty within period of 6 months, should be reinstate and not entitled to any back wages - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that respondent had never indicated at any point of time that he wanted to enroll himself for a Ph.D. degree and Courts below were erroneous in passing order of reinstatement - Held, jurisdiction exercised by Tribunal and HC was limited one and while exercising power of judicial review, they could not set aside punishment altogether or impose some other penalty unless they find that there had been a substantial noncompliance of rules of procedure or a gross violation of rules of natural justice which had caused prejudice to employee, which had resulted in miscarriage of justice or punishment was shockingly disproportionate to gravamen of charge - In instant case, leave was granted for 2 years, which would count for increment - There could be no doubt that respondent violated undertaking given by him that he would not register for Ph.D. degree and not request for extension of leave - Thus, punishment of compulsory retirement imposed upon respondent could not be held to be disproportionate, much less shockingly disproportionate - Further there was absolutely no ground on which Tribunal or HC could interfere with the order passed by appellants - Hence, judgment of Tribunal and order of HC were set aside and affirmed order of appellants - Appeal allowed.
