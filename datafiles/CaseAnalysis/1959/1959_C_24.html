Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay City v M. K. Kirtikar

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 233; AIR 1960 SC 186; [1959] 36 ITR 360

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Double taxation - Payment of super-tax - Permissible deduction - Commission - Profit and Loss account - 'D' firm used to pay to each of its three employees, including respondent, a commission - Income-tax Officer found that profit and loss account of Colour Department of firm was debited by way of commission pad to three employees including respondent - Respondent claimed that, two-thirds of commission paid and certain amount was disallowed and taxed in hands of the firm, he was entitled to an exemption - Income-tax Officer rejected this claim - On appeal, appellate Commissioner upheld - Further appeal to Tribunal rejected - HC answered in favor of assessee/respondent - Hence instant appeal - Whether deduction claimed was permissible - Held, department urges, that the amount was disallowed not because it had been paid out of profits or determined with reference to profits of business, but because he held it to be excessive and unnecessary and not a permissible deduction u/s. 10(2)(xv) of the said Act - It is clear that primary object and purpose of notification is to prevent double taxation on the same amount - HC has placed reliance on s. 55 of the said Act where the words charged, levied and paid used in connection with payment of super-tax - Appeal allowed.
