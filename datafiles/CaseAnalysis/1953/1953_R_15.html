Â Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay City v Royal Western India Turf Club Limited

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, M. Patanjali Sastri, Vivian Bose, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Ghulam Hasan

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 87; AIR 1954 SC 85; [1953] 24 ITR 551; 1953 (2) MLJ(SC) 828; 1953 SCJ 739; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 289

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Horse Racing, Income-tax Act (XI of 1922).

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 10(1) and 10(6) - Respondent was incorporated with the object to take over the assets, effects and liabilities of the then unincorporated club, to carry on the business of a Race Course Company in all branches, to establish any Clubs, Hotels and other conveniences, to carry on the business of Hotel Keepers, Tavern Keepers, licensed victuallers and refreshment purveyors and to sell, improve, manage, develop, lease, mortgage, dispose of or otherwise deal with all or any part of the property of the company, whether movable or immovable - Respondent received large sums of money on admission tickets from members as well as from non-members, besides other moneys on other accounts - Respondent claimed that four items of receipts i.e. season admission ticket from members Rs. 23, 635, daily admission gate tickets from members Rs. 51, 777, use of private boxes by members Rs. 21, 490 and income from entries and forfeits received from the members whose horses did not run in the races during the season Rs. 82, 490 should be excluded from assessment - Income Tax Officer held that all the four items were receipts from business falling u/s. 10(1) of the IT Act or, in the alternative, were receipts by an association performing specific services for its members for remuneration definitely related to those services within the meaning of s. 10(6) of the Act and assessed accordingly - Asst. Commissioner dismissed appeal filed against said order - Tribunal held that none of the sums in question could be said to be profits or gains of a business coming under section 10(1) of IT Act - HC held that the first three items were not taxable either u/s. 10(1) or s. 10(6) and that the fourth item was taxable under both the said sub-sections of that section - Hence, present appeal - Whether these items are receipts from business carried on by the company and whether these items are receipts by a trade or professional or similar association performing specific services for its members for remuneration definitely related to those services? - Held, section 10(6) has no application, for the company is not a trade or professional or similar association within the meaning of that sub-section - All the items of receipts from members referred to in the questions were received by the company from business with its members within the meaning of s. 10(1) of IT Act and that none of them was received by the company as a trade, professional or similar association within the meaning of s. 10(6) of IT Act - Hence, said items comes with the meaning of s. 10(1) of IT Act and are not related to those services - Appeal allowed.
