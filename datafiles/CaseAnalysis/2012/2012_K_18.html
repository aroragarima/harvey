State of Haryana and another v Kartar Singh (Dead) through Lrs.

Supreme Court of India

29 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 581; 2013 (96) ALR 701; 2013 (1) AWC 996; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 848; 2013 (118) RD 828; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 162

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Administrative - Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4,6,23(1A), 23(2) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1908, ss. 151, 152 - Land Acquisition - Compensation - Enhancement - Amendment - Validity - Sustainability - Govt. issued notification u/s. 4 of Act proposing to acquire land for residential and commercial area - Declaration was made u/s. 6 of and award came to be passed fixing the market value of the acquired land at the rate of Rs. 270/- per Biswa, respondents were not satisfied with market value determined by Land Acquisition Collector, sought reference u/s. 18 of Act - Matter was referred to civil court for determination of compensation for compulsory acquisition of respondents' land - Reference court enhanced compensation rate of Rs. 22/- per square yard and also awarded solatium at 15% on enhanced amount of compensation and interest at 6% from date of dispossession till payment was made as awarded - State dissatisfied with determination of compensation by reference court- preferred first appeal before HC, which was dismissed - State special leave petition against award and decree of HC but was unsuccessful - Meanwhile Act was amended and respondents made application u/ss. 151, 152 of CPC before HC, praying for benefits of amended provisions in ss. 23(1-A), 23(2) of Act - HC allowed application and granted benefits of amended Act - Respondents then filed another execution petition for execution of award and decree - Appellants, raised objection that award and decree passed by HC, was without jurisdiction and, therefore, not executable and enforceable - Executing court, overruled objection and held that it was not open to executing court to go behind decree, appellants challenged order of executing court by filing a revision petition before HC, which had been dismissed by impugned order - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC, was justified in modifying award and decree as per amendment of Act - Held, Legal position was no more res integra that an award and decree having become final under Act could not be amended or altered seeking enhancement of statutory benefits under amended provisions brought in by Amendment Act in Act by filing petitions u/ss. 151,152 of CPC - Hence, award and decree passed by HC, on 28-4- 1989 had to be held to be without jurisdiction and nullity, a plea of nullity of a decree could always be set up before executing court and any judgment and order which was a nullity never acquires finality and was thus open to challenge in executing proceedings - Appeals disposed of.
