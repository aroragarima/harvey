State of Orissa and Others v Brundaban Sharma and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 January 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1304; (1995) Supp3 SCC 249

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, s. 4 (1) - Orissa Estate Abolition Act, 1951, s. 38-B - (A) Whether Govt. (appellants) was bound to acquire land in question? - Held, Govt. as owner, need not acquire its own land merely because earlier proceedings were mistakenly resorted to acquire land and latter on withdrawn and fresh notification published in which acquisition was omitted and road was laid on it - (B) Whether lease of respondent was valid? - Held, respondent's claim not corroborated by any records - Patta granted to respondent by Gokulanand was on a white plain paper without approval of competent authority - Collection of land revenue or rent does not operate as recognition of pre-existing rights, title or interest in land - (C) Whether Board of Revenue was justified in exercising jurisdiction u/s. 38-B? - Held, object of conferment of power on Board of Revenue is to prevent collusive or fraudulent acts on part of intermediaries and lower level officers to defeat object of Act - Board was empowered to initiate action only after s. 38-B came into force thus exercising suo motu revisional power over orders, decisions made or proceedings taken earlier - Action taken by Board of Revenue was legal and valid and brooked no delay, after it came to Board's knowledge - Order passed by Tehsildar without Board's confirmation is non est - Board, either suo moto or on a Collector's report, can call for and examine record of any proceeding in which, any authority subordinate to Board, has made any decision or passed an order under Act, to satisfy itself as to regularity of proceeding or to correctness, legality or propriety thereof - If it appears to Board that a decision is not correct, it is empowered to, after providing a hearing to affected party modify, annul, reverse or remit case to authority for decision according to law - High Court not right in opining that exercise of power u/s. 38-B unwarranted - It committed illegality in quashing Board's order - Order of HC is set aside - Appeal allowed.
