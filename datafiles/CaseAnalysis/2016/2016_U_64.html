Karma Dorjee and others v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

D.Y. Chandrachud, T.S. Thakur, L. Nageswara Rao

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 887; (2017) 1 SCC 799; AIR 2017 SC 113; JT 2016 (12) SC 124; 2016 (8) MLJ 707; 2016(12) SCALE 770; [2016] 9 S.C.R. 968

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Human Rights

Keywords: Protection Of Human Rights Act, 1993, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Scheduled Castes and the Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Amendment Act, 2015

Summary: Human Rights - Constitution - Protection of Human Rights Act, 1993 - North-eastern states - Discrimination - Awareness of history - Petitioners filed instant writ petitions in public interest for guidelines to be set down to curb acts of discrimination prevalent in society against citizens of nation drawn from north-eastern states - Petitioners contended that paradox of secular India where on one hand, students from north-eastern states who move to other parts of country in search of employment and education learn in the process culture and traditions of rest of the country on the other hand, there was an absence of reciprocating sensitivity towards and awareness of their concerns.

Held, involvement of law enforcement machinery was alone not sufficient to resolve the problem. Mind-sets have to be changed including in universities, colleges and educational institutions, places of work and in society. Sensitivity and inclusion have to be fostered. In order to achieve that greater awareness of history and rich cultural traditions of north-east was required to be inculcated. The problems faced by persons from north-east traverse a whole range of issues, from the mundane issues of daily life to matters of education, employment, social security and fundamental right to live in dignity. Govts. both at centre and states have a non-negotiable obligation to take positive steps to give effect to Country's commitment to racial equality. Such commitment was embodied in constitutional rights, fundamental duties, statutory provisions and in international obligations which were assumed by Country. Respondents/UOI should take proactive steps to monitor the redressal of issues pertaining to racial discrimination faced by citizens of nation drawn from north-east. For that purpose, a regular exercise of monitoring and redressal should be carried out by a Committee consisting of Joint Secretary (North-east), Ministry of Home Affairs and two other members to be nominated by UOI. The work of Committee should be widely publicised in electronic and print media, including in north eastern states. Committee should be accessible to grievances, suggestions and complaints. Committee should meet periodically and preferably at monthly intervals to monitor the redressal of all such grievances including the implementation of recommendations to the extent to which they were accepted by UOI. A decision might also be taken by UOI on whether any of the other recommendations should be accepted. Petitions disposed of.
