Rajni Sanghi v Western Indian State Motors Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

01 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 762; 2016 (1) ALD(SC) 56; 2016 (1) AWC 196; 2015 (4) CLT(SC) 264; [2016] 194 Comp Cas 486; 2016 (2) CompLJ 401; JT 2016 (1) SC 115; 2015 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4380; 2016 (1) MLJ 308; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 941; 2015(13) SCALE 20; 2016 (2) WLN(SC) 115

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Family Business, Family Settlement

Summary: Family & Personal - Family Partition - Arbitral Award pending - Family settlement pre-eminence over award - It is stated that there was partition of family business between family members - Further, due to dispute, parties entered into arbitration agreement and appointed arbitrator for affecting partition of family business - Award passed by arbitrator was filed with HC - Further, objection was raised by respondent party against award - Further, application was filed before HC to make award a rule of Court - HC, Rajasthan vide order set aside award passed by arbitrator - Order of HC is upheld by DB - Further, scheme of reconstruction and family settlement arrived between parties representing three groups was approved by HC, Rajasthan - Further, HC, Delhi set aside award of arbitrator on ground of award passed by HC's, Bombay and Rajasthan approving schemes of reconstruction and also undertaking of party that he will withdraw his petition in HC, Delhi through which he had prayed for making award of arbitrator as rule of Court - Whether any good ground was available to HC, Delhi for invalidating award and for refusing to make it rule of court.

Held, when Courts having jurisdiction were allowed to proceed and decide properties available at Bombay and record family arrangement in respect of other matters in company proceeding before Rajasthan HC, judgments and orders in these proceedings cannot be ignored or obliterated on account of pendency of award still waiting to be made rule of court. Hence, award has to be set aside on ground that it is otherwise invalid on date it is being considered for being made rule of court. SC held that since this course of action is available and has been rightly adopted by Delhi HC, SC found it unnecessary to examine that even in absence of any ground for setting aside such award, could court concerned refuse to make award rule of court. Further, SC held that family arrangements made before Rajasthan HC in 1994 before Company Judge and in 1995 before DB need to be protected and given pre-eminence over award which is yet not made rule of court. Further, SC held that if parties settle their disputes amicably by agreement, even post-award, such settlement/agreement will prevail in view of requirement of Act that award will acquire status of decree only when it is made rule of court after rejection of all objections. Hence, SC held that there is no objection in upholding family arrangements made before HC at Rajasthan and judgment of Bombay HC which has attained finality and they deserve to have pre-eminence over award in question. Further, if any party fails to act as per arrangements within three months, aggrieved party will be free to initiate appropriate proceedings including those of contempt before HC or seek execution of agreements through other appropriate proceedings. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - In case of partition between family members, family arrangements is protected and given pre-eminence over award which is yet not made rule of court.
