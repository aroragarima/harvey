Rajendra Kumar v Kalyan (Dead) By Lrs

Supreme Court of India

02 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2844; (2000) 8 SCC 99; AIR 2000 SC 3335; 2000 (40) ALR 714; 2001 (1) Bom.C.R. 533; JT 2000 (8) SC 359; 2000 (3) MLJ(SC) 170; 2000 (3) RCR(Civil) 745; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 114

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Court Of Limited Jurisdiction

Summary: Civil Procedure - Res judicata - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 11 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 226 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 14 - Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, ss. 8 and 12 - Applicability of doctrine of res judicata or constructive res judicata u/s. 11 CPC, r/w Explanation VIII thereto in terms of provisions of Amendment Act - Doctrine of res judicata or constructive res judicata predominantly is a principle of equity, good conscience and justice - It would neither be equitable nor fair nor in accordance with principles of justice that issue concluded earlier ought to be permitted to be raised later in a different proceeding - Judicial verdict has its special sanctity and cannot be subject-matter of discussion at any future time involving identical or similar issues - Held, 'R' a party to previous suit, and issue regarding 'A' and 'M' adoption having been decided against 'R' specifically, therefore plaintiff was litigating under same title and regard to provisions of s. 11 r/w Explanation VIII, earlier decision would operate as a res judicata - Appeal dismissed.
