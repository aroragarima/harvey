Bishnu Biswas and others v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

02 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 211; (2014) 5 SCC 774; AIR 2014 SC 1570; 2014 (142) FLR 818; JT 2014 (4) SC 462; 2014(4) SCALE 468; [2014] 4 S.C.R. 625

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Appointments, Awarding of marks, Change of criteria of selection in midst of selection process

Summary: Service - Change of criteria of selection in midst of selection process - Awarding of marks - Appointments - Legality - Respondent (authorities) issued advertisement calling for applications from eligible candidates as well as from those who were registered with the Employment Exchange for appointment to the 8 posts of Group 'D' staff - In Pursuance thereto, appointment letters were issued to appellants (appointees) - Unsuccessful candidates challenged the appointments and filed applications before tribunal, which was allowed, quashing such appointments as equal marks were earmarked for both written examination and interview which was impermissible in law and that interview was never part of the recruitment process and thereby ordering initiation of fresh recruitment process - Aggrieved appellants challenged the said order before HC - HC upheld the reasoning of Tribunal but modified the order to the extent of continuing the recruitment process from the point it stood vitiated - In pursuance of the judgment and order of HC Court, termination letters were issued to appellants - Aggrieved appellants filed instant appeals - Appellants contended that once selection was over and candidates appeared without any protest, they could not be permitted to make a summer salt and challenge the selection as a whole and thus, judgments impugned were liable to be set aside -

Held, it was evident that Courts had always frowned upon prescribing higher percentage of marks for interview even when the selection was on the basis of written test as well as on interview - Appropriate allocation of marks for interview, where selection was to be made by written test as well as by interview, would depend upon the nature of post and no straightjacket formula could be laid down - Further there was a distinction while considering the case of employment and of admission for an academic course - Courts have repeatedly emphasized that for the purpose of admission in an education institution, the allocation of interview marks would not be very high but for the purpose of employment, allocation of marks for interview would depend upon the nature of post - In instant case, rules of the game were changed after conducting the written test and admittedly not at the stage of initiation of the selection process - Marks allocated for oral interview was the same as for written test i.e. 50% for each - Manner in which marks were awarded in the interview to the candidates indicated lack of transparency - Candidate who secured 47 marks out of 50 in the written test was given only 20 marks in the interview while large number of candidates got equal marks in the interview as in written examination - Candidate who secured 34 marks in written examination was given 45 marks in interview - Similarly, another candidate who secured 36 marks in written examination was awarded 45 marks in interview - Fact that so called selected candidates were not in employment, was also a relevant factor to decide case finally - If whole selection was scrapped most of the candidates would be ineligible at least in respect of age as the advertisement was issued more than 6 years ago - Thus, in facts of instant case direction of HC Court to continue with the selection process from the point it stood vitiated did not require interference - Appeals dismissed.
