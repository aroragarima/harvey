State of Bihar v Subhash Singh

Supreme Court of India

03 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2486; (1997) 4 SCC 430; AIR 1997 SC 1390; 1997 (2) AWC 698; 1997 (2) CLT 82; JT 1997 (2) SC 463; 1997 (2) MLJ(SC) 57; 1997 (1) SCJ 391; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 850

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 53, 73, 74, 154, 163 and 166 - Condonation of delay - In this case delay in compliance is of one year and five months and officer has not explained cause of delay - High Court imposed personal costs against officer - Petition against order imposing costs personally against respondent passed by HC - Held, imposition of costs on officers for filing appeals causes public injustice and gives manipulators an opportunity to compound camouflage - Secondly, it will be counter-productive and officers would desist to pursue genuine cases of public benefit or importance or of far-reaching effect on public administration or exchequer deflecting course of justice - Thus, court before imposing costs personally against officers should be circumspect and keep at back of its mind facts and circumstances in each case - Otherwise, public justice will suffer irremediably - Since delay has not been explained by officer, thus, HC was constrained to impose personal costs against officer - No interference required - Petition dismissed.
