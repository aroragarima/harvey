Abati Bezbaruah v Deputy Director General Geological Survey of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 194; (2003) 3 SCC 148; (2003) SCC (Cr) 746; 2003 (1) ACC 352; AIR 2003 SC 1817; 2003 (51) ALR 111; JT 2003 (5) SC 205; 2003(2) SCALE 120; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 1229; 2003 (2) SLT 65; 2003 (2) Supreme 178; 2003 (1) UC 649

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act - Ss. 166 and 168 - accident - claim for compensation - held, question as to what should be rate of interest would depend upon the facts and circumstances of each case. Award of interest would normally depend upon the bank rate prevailing at the relevant time -amount of interest should, having regard to the facts and circumstances of the case, be paid at the rate of 9% per annum - how the multiplicand should be arrived at - held, the victim at the relevant time was 40 years of age. The Tribunal and the High Court, therefore, cannot be said to have committed an error in applying the multiplier of 15.
