India Thermal Power Limited etc. v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 149; (2000) 3 SCC 379; AIR 2000 SC 1005; 2000 (3) ALD(SC) 109; 2000 (2) CLT 41; JT 2000 (2) SC 171; 2000(1) SCALE 612; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 925; 2000 (3) SLT 141; 2000 (3) Supreme 147

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Electricity Supply Act, 1984

Summary: Contract Act, 1872, s. 10 - Statutory Contract - What amounts to - Power purchase agreement - Held, is statutory Contract only to extent, that it contains provisions regarding determination of tariff - If prescribed terms and conditions is a must under statute then that contract becomes a statutory contract - Court would not substitute decision of Govt. on mere ground that it would have been better if a different criteria had been adopted, criteria fixed by State Govt. bona fide.
