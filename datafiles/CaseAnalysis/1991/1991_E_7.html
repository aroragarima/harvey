B. Sundara Rami Reddy v Election Commission of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 February 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 516; (1991) Supp2 SCC 624; AIR 1991 SCW 772

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 1, r. 10 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 82 and 86(4) - Whether Election Commission of India is a necessary or proper party to election petition? - Held, only those may be joined as respondents to an election petition who are mentioned in ss. 82 and 86(4) and no others - U/s. 82 of Representation of People Act, 1951, returned candidate is a necessary party as a respondent and where relief for a declaration is claimed that election petitioner, or any other candidate be duly elected, all contesting candidates are necessary to be impleaded as respondents to petition - No other person or authority - Election Commission of India is therefore not a necessary party to an election petition - Petition dismissed.
