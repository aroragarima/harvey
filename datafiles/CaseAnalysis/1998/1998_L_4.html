State of Uttar Pradesh v Lakhmi

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.M. Punchhi, M. Srinivasan

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1757; (1998) 4 SCC 336; (1998) SCC (Cr) 929; AIR 1998 SC 1007; 1998 ALJ 680; 1998 (1) CCR 303; 1998 (1) Crimes 179; 1998 CRLJ 1411; JT 1998 (1) SC 679; 1998 (1) RCR(Criminal) 754; 1998(1) SCALE 590; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 850; 1998 (3) SLT 97; 1998 (2) Supreme 5

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1974, s. 313 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 300, exception I and s. 304, Part I - Murder - Grave and sudden provocation - Accused held guilty of committing murder of his wife - Evidence showing accused had seen something lascivious between his wife and other person while entering his house - Held, benefit of exception I to s. 300 - Conviction altered from s. 300 to one u/s. 304, Part I.
