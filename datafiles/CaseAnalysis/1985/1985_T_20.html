The Deputy Commissioner Of Agricultural Income Tax And Sales T v Indian Explosives Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, S. Mukharji, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 248; (1985) 4 SCC 119; (1985) SCC (Tax) 527; AIR 1985 SC 1689; 1985 (3) CompLJ 247; 1985 ECR 2593; 1985 (22) E.L.T. 331; 1985(2) SCALE 483; 1985 (60) STC 310; [1985] Supp2 S.C.R. 623; 1986 (1) UJ 391

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Customs; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Sale In The Course Of Export

Summary: Sales Tax - Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1950, ss. 2(j), 5 and 6 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 286(1)(b) - Sales in the course of import - Whether the sales effected by the respondent assessee in the three concerned years of goods imported and supplied to customers on the strength of Actual Users' Import Licences of the customers were exempt from sales tax under the 1950 Act on the ground that these were sales in the course of import or the goods into the territory of India - Movement of the goods from the foreign country in pursuance of pre-existing contract of sale - Held, exempted as in the course of import - Appeal dismissed.
