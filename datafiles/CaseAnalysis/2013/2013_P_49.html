Priyadarshini College Of Computer Science And Another v Manish Kumar And Others

Supreme Court of India

24 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, J. S. KHEHAR

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 63; (2013) 11 SCC 802; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 888; 2013(2) ALL MR 434; 2013 (2) AWC 1973; 2014 (1) CLT(SC) 246; JT 2013 (2) SC 196; 2013 (3) MLJ 197; 2013(2) SCALE 56; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 622; 2013 (1) SLR 732

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - Practice & Procedure - Appellant no. 1 (College) was imparting technical education for various branches and invited applications against vacant sets for various branches including admission for 2nd year (3rd semester) of Engineering for Diploma Holders/B.Sc with Mathematics eligibility with minimum 60% marks - Respondent no. 1 (student) applied for 3rd semester admission for course of B.Tech (Computer Science) in appellant college - However appellant admitted respondent no. 1, though actually he secured 56% marks - University refused to issue admit card to appeal in examination - Thereafter, appellant cancelled admission of respondent no. 1 - Aggrieved respondent no. 1 filed a petition before HC, which was allowed and appellant was directed to compensate respondent no. 1 for loss suffered - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before DB, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that there was a specific clause in admission form about disclosure of percentage of marks of qualifying subject but in that respondent no. 1 did not disclosed - Whether appellant was liable to compensate respondent no. 1 - Held, every candidate applying for a particular course in College was expected to go through advertisement thoroughly including eligibility criteria prescribed for each course and after fulfillment of required conditions, state the correct particulars in application form failing which he/she could not claim any benefit for his/her own wrong - in instant case, eligibility condition was 60%, however, respondent no. 1 has secured only 56% marks applied for said course with an intention to secure admission by playing fraud with appellant-College - Single Judge failed to take note of that relevant aspect which was merely affirmed by DB - Advertisement calling for applications in which appellant specifically mentioned that minimum 60% marks in B.Sc. Mathematics was eligibility criteria and based on same, respondent No.1-candidate applied for same - In enrolment form in cl. 17(ii) respondent no. 1 specifically stated that he secured 56% marks and in view of assertion made by respondent No.1 in Cl. 7 of declaration that he had secured 60% marks, appellant accepted his form and admitted him in course - It was relevant to point out that when deficiency was pointed out by University, appellant College refunded the entire fees received by them from respondent no. 1 - Finding fault with appellant was erroneous and appellant could not be held liable for the act of respondent no. 1 - Impugned orders passed by HC were set aside - Appeal allowed.
