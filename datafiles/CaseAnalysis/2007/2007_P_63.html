Peerless General Finance and Investment Company v Poddar Projects Ltd. & Anr

Supreme Court of India

13 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 166; (2007) 2 SCC 431; AIR 2007 SC 1875; 2007 (2) BC 18; 2007 (2) CHN 141; [2007] 136 Comp Cas 197; 2007 (1) CompLJ 416; 2007(3) SCALE 175; 2007 (7) SCJ 722; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 573

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Instrument Of Transfer

Summary: Companies Act, 1956, s. 111A - Appellant-company were being held by Poddar Udyog Limited - Part of Business division of Poddar Udyog Limited was transferred to the respondent No. 1 - Respondent No. 1 sold the shares to the respondent No. 2 - Respondent lodged the shares with the appellant-company herein for registration - Refused - Company Law Board directed appellant-company to register the original shares in favour of the respondent No. 1 - Appeal against - Held, during the pendency of the appeal, the appellant-company had complied with the direction of the Calcutta High Court - Appeal disposed of as having become infructuous.
