Provash Chandra Dalui And Anr. v Biswanath Banerjee And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

03 April 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Saikia, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 500; (1989) Supp1 SCC 487; AIR 1989 SC 1834; 1989 (2) CalLT(SC) 43; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 92; 1990 (1) RCR(Rent) 191; 1989(1) SCALE 844; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 401; 1989 (2) UJ 412

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Calcutta Thika Tenancy Act, 1949

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 8 and 105 - Calcutta Thika Tenancy Act, 1949, s. 2(5) - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - (A) Lease - (1) Deed - Construction - (2) Whether alteration in intrinsic character thereof can be brought about by pleadings? - (B) Condition precedent for becoming Thika tenant - Stated period of lease should be less than 12 years - Period of lease made 10 years in first instance to be extended for another five years and to be further extended for further period of five years - (C) Evidence Act - Waiver and estoppel - Distinction - Held, (A) Court must construe it with reference to its object and all of its terms, look at words and if they are clear, they must be accepted - Cannot be brought about - (B) Lease was for not less than 12 years - (C) Essential element of waiver is intentional relinquishment of known right or such conduct as warrants aforesaid inference whereas in estoppel, intent is immaterial - Appeal dismissed.
