Kapildeo Mandal & Ors v State Of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

29 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

P.P. Naolekar, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1191; (2008) 16 SCC 99; AIR 2008 SC 533; 2008 CRLJ 730; 2008 (1) JCC 143; JT 2007 (13) SC 202; 2008 (1) RCR(Criminal) 234; 2007(13) SCALE 560; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 668

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Close Range, Firearm Injuries, Match-Box, Relative Witness

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 302 r/w 149, 452, 148, 323 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, witnesses are related and their relations were strained with the appellants on account of the litigation - While appreciating variance between medical evidence and ocular evidence, oral evidence of eye-witness has to get primacy as medical evidence is basically opinionative - Medical evidence when specifically rules out the injury claimed to have been inflicted as per the eye- witnesses' version, then the court can draw adverse inference to the effect that the prosecution version as being put forth before the court, is not trustworthy - Appeals allowed.
