State of Uttar Pradesh v Bhagwant Kishore Joshi

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 268; AIR 1964 SC 221; 1964 CRLJ 140; 1964 (1) LLJ 60; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 71

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 156, 157 and 537 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5 A - (A) Whether accused has been prejudiced by irregularity in matter of investigation? - Held, accused can throw a reasonable doubt that prosecution evidence is such that it must have been manipulated or shaped by reason of irregularity in matter of investigation - But where prosecution evidence has been held to be true and where accused had full say in matter, conviction cannot obviously be set aside on ground of some irregularity or illegality in matter of investigation - There must be a sufficient nexus, either established or probabilized, between conviction and irregularity in investigation - (2) Having regards to fact that not only trial was fair and evidence convincing, but even earlier defect was rectified by having practically a de novo investigation in strict compliance with provision of CrPC, it could not be held that accused had been prejudiced by illegality committed by police in first stage of investigation - (B)What is object in enactment of s. 5 A of Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947? - Further held, it is enacted for preventing harassment to a Govt. servant - Investigation, except with previous permission of a Magistrate, is not permitted to be made by an officer below rank of Deputy Superintendent of Police - Said statutory safeguards must be strictly complied with - Conceived in public interest and were provided as a guarantee against frivolous and vexatious prosecution - Appeal dismissed.
