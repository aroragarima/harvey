State of Rajasthan v Ram Prasad and Another

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 105; (2001) 9 SCC 395; 2001 (1) ACC 90; 2001 (1) LLJ 177; 2001 (7) SLR 800; 2000 (8) SLT 636

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 - Death of workman by lightning - Whether death by lightning is a act of God and, therefore employer is not liable to pay compensation? - If workman is injured as a result of natural force such as lightning, though this in itself has no connection with employment she can recover compensation by showing that such employment exposed her to such injury - Held, workman was working on site and would not have been exposed to such hazard of lightning striking her had she not been working - Appeal dismissed.
