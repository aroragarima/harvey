State of Rajasthan and Another v Karam Chand Thappar and Brothers (Coal Sales) Limited, Jaipur

Supreme Court of India

27 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 67; AIR 1969 SC 343; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 861; 1969 (23) STC 210

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Demand Notice, Tax On The Sale Or Purchase Of Goods, Collected, Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1954, Sales Tax Laws Validation Act, 1956, Andhra Pradesh Sugar-cane (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Act, 1961

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Sales Tax Laws Validation Act, 1956 - Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1954 - Inter-State sales - Levy of tax - Quashing order - Under contract with Equitable Coal Company assessee acquired monopoly rights to supply on behalf of collieries, coal in certain areas - Under agreement with State assessee supplied coal to State Power Houses - Sales Tax Officer assessed to tax turnover of assessee from supply of coal to State - As against in petition filed by assessee, accepted plea that assessee was not dealer within meaning of 1954 Act and quashed assessment - In appeal by State HC issued writ quashing assessment - Hence, instant Petition - Whether there was sale of coal within meaning of 1954 Act and order of assessment made by Sales Tax Officer was liable to be quashed from under 1956 Act - Held, there was agreement to sell coal between State and assessee acting as agent of Equitable Coal Company - Price chargeable was fixed under Colliery Control Order - Effect of Control Order was only to superimpose upon agreement between parties rate fixed under Order - On that account it could not be said that relation between supplier and person to whom coal was supplied was not contractual - Agreement of sale between parties competent to contract and in pursuance of agreement of sale, property in goods supplied passed to purchaser for price agreed to be paid - Transaction was one of sale of goods within meaning of 1954 Act - Hence, State was entitled to levy tax by virtue of provisions of 1956 Act and order of assessment levying tax on turnover for that period could be upheld - Petition allowed.
