Shreenath Corporation and others v Consumer Education and Research Society and others

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 444; (2014) 8 SCC 657; 2014(5) ALL MR 412; 2014 (106) ALR 703; 2014 (4) AWC 4031; 2014 (4) Bom.C.R. 705; 2014 (4) CompLJ 11; 2014 (3) CPJ(Sc) 1; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3094; 2014 (6) MahLJ 526; 2015(1) M.P.H.T. 370; 2014 (4) MPLJ 444; 2014 (175) PLR 785; 2014 (3) RCR(Civil) 887; 2014(8) SCALE 394; [2014] 9 S.C.R. 356; 2014 (3) WLN(SC) 74

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Summary: Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, ss. 17 and 19 - Interim order - Legality - Complaints u/s. 17(1) of the Act were filed by different persons before the Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (State Commission) against the appellants (opposite parties) - State Commission allowed the applications in part and directed the appellants to pay certain amount with interest in favour of the complainants - On appeal, National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (National Commission) had passed conditional interim order - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned interim order passed by the National Commission was contrary to the provisions of s. 19 of the Act -

Held, second proviso to s. 19 of the Act mandate pre-deposit for consideration of an appeal before the National Commission - Pre-deposit condition to deposit 50 percent of the amount in terms of the order of the State Commission or Rs.35,000/ being condition precedent for entertaining appeal, it had no nexus with the order of stay - Further, entertainment of an appeal and stay of proceeding pursuant to order impugned in the appeal stands at different footings, at two different stages - One (pre-deposit) had no nexus with merit of the appeal and the other (grant of stay) depend on prima facie case; balance of convenience and irreparable loss of party seeking such stay - No interference with the impugned order passed by the National Commission was called for - Appeals dismissed.
