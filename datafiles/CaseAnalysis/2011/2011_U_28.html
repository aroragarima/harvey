Supreme Court of India

17 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Mukundakam Sharma, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Swatanter Kumar, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 103; (2011) 3 SCC 404; 2011 (265) E.L.T. 11; [2011] 332 ITR 58; JT 2011 (6) SC 372; 2011 (7) MLJ(SC) 609; 2011(2) SCALE 494; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 971; 2011 (21) S.T.R. 593; [2011] 39 VST 1

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Central Excise Valuation (Determination of Price of Excisable Goods) Rules, 2000

Summary: Excise - Practice & Procedure - Central Excise Act, 1944 - Approval of Committee on Disputes (CoD) - Central Government issued show cause notice to the assessee (PSU) alleging that the assessee was not entitled to avail MODVAT/CENVAT credit in respect of inputs whose values stood written off - Assessee contended that it was a financial requirement that an inventory more than 3 years old had to be written off/derated in value though that such derating in value did not mean that the inputs were unfunctionable as said inputs were lying in the factory and they were useful for production and therefore they were entitled to MODVAT/CENVAT credit - Said contention was rejected by the Commissioner and the demand against the assessee stood confirmed - Assessee decided to challenge the same by filing an appeal before Tribunal, accordingly, applied before the CoD - The CoD refused to grant clearance - Assessee filed writ petition contending that in an identical case the CoD granted clearance to another assessee - HC dismissed said writ petition - Hence, the instant petition - Whether contention of the assessee could be accepted and instant petition could be allowed on the ground of the discrimination? - Orders requiring PSUs to obtain CoD approval for filing an appeal reacalled - Held, the idea behind setting up of CoD was to ensure that resources of the State are not frittered away in inter se litigations between entities of the State, which could be best resolved, by an empowered CoD - The machinery contemplated was only to ensure that no litigation comes to Court without the parties having had an opportunity of conciliation before an in-house committee - However, experience has shown that despite best efforts of the CoD, the mechanism has not achieved the results for which it was constituted and has in fact led to delays in litigation as on same set of facts, clearance was given in one case and refused in the other - This had led a PSU to institute a SLP in SC on the ground of discrimination - Hence, said mechanism has outlived its utility - In the changed scenario, time has come to recall the directions of SC in its various Orders reported as Oil and Natural Gas Commission vs. Collector of Central Excise 1991 INDLAW SC 17, Oil and Natural Gas Commission vs. Collector of Central Excise 1994 INDLAW SC 74 and Oil and Natural Gas Corporation Limited vs. City and Industrial Development Corporation, Maharashtra and Others 2007 INDLAW SC 733 - Order accordingly.
