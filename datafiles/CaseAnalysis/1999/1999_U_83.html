Hyderabad Asbestos Cement Products and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, N. Santosh Hegde, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 576; (2000) 1 SCC 426; AIR 2000 SC 314; 2000 (88) ECR 15; 2000 (115) E.L.T. 20; JT 1999 (9) SC 505; 2000 (36) RLT 3; 1999(7) SCALE 358; 1999 (10) SLT 268; [1999] Supp5 S.C.R. 155; 1999 (10) Supreme 70

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Tariff Act, 1934

Summary: Excise - Central Excise and Salt Act, 1944 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 56A - Whether benefit of pro forma credit procedure specified in r. 56 - A(1) is available to appellants though raw materials consumed by appellants in their manufacture of final products are excisable under tariff items different from one under which their final products are excisable? - To avail benefit of pro forma credit u/r. 56 - A inputs which go to manufacture specified finished excisable goods must be exigible to payment of duty under same tariff item or sub-item, or else, if such inputs are exigible to tax under different tariff items or sub-items then they must be covered by specific sanction of Central Govt. granting remission or adjustment of duty on those inputs as provided by proviso (ii)(b) - Admittedly there is no such specific sanction - Held, raw materials consumed being excisable under tariff items different from one under which finished products are excisable appellants have been rightly denied benefit of pro forma credit - Appeal dismissed.
