Chairman-cum-Managing Director, National Textiles Corporation Limited and Others v N.T.C. (WBAB & O) Ltd. Employees Union and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Kumar, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 868; (2003) 11 SCC 31; (2004) SCC (L&S) 295; AIR 2004 SC 179; 2004 (4) ESC 524; 2003 (99) FLR 808; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 534; 2003 LabIC 3722; 2003 (3) LLJ 1102; 2003(8) SCALE 613; 2003 (1) SCW 5867; 2003 (6) SLR 441; 2003 (6) SLT 499; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 722; 2003 (7) Supreme 380; 2004 (1) UPLBEC 330

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Textiles; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Emoluments

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - Sick Textile Undertakings (Nationalization) Act, 1974, ss.3(1), 5 - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provision) Act, 1986 - Claim of staff/sub staff engaged by various textile mills under NTC for 'equal pay for equal work' in parity with pay scales prevailing for staff working in corporate offices of mills - NTC and most of its subsidiaries incurring losses and majority of them are sick companies facing proceeding under SICA - Held, workers deserve some relief though not parity of pay scales with staff/sub-staff working in corporate offices, but certainly on account of revision of pay scales/increase of D A or emoluments from time to time as and when fell due during period of nearly three decades since when, no revision of their pay scale has been made - Therefore, Central Govt. directed to take appropriate steps which if so required may include appointment of an expert to work out extent of relief which ought to be granted to workers in mills - Further held, pendency of rehabilitation schemes before BIFR is not a sufficient ground for us to deny relief to staff/sub staff working in mills - As per provisions of s 5(2)(c), Sick Textile Undertakings (Nationalisation) Act, wages, salaries and other dues of employees of sick textile undertakings after takeover of their managements by Central Govt. are responsibility of Central Govt. - Central Govt. has failed to discharge its responsibility for all these years by raising such specious pleas Central Govt. has to discharge its responsibility de hors BIFR schemes.
