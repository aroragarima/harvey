V. K. Sharma v State (Delhi Administration)

Supreme Court of India

13 March 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, A. Alagiriswami

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 155; (1975) SCC (Cr) 277; (1975) 1 SCC 784; AIR 1975 SC 899; 1975 (1) ALR 262; 1975 CRLJ 776; 1975 CRLR 283; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 922; 1975 UJ 443

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Illegal Gratification

Summary: Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 4,5A and 6 - Penal Code, 1860, s. 161 - (A) Clerk in Central Secretariat - Temporarily transferred to Rationing Department - Competent authority for sanction for his prosecution - (B) Scope of s. 5A of thre Act - (C) Availability of presumption u/s. 4 - Held, Deputy Secretary, Department of Community Development and Co-operation, Govt. of India was the competent authority to accord sanction for the prosecution of the accused - Provision is not meant to clothe a person with authority or competency to lay trap - Not available for proving charge u/s.5(2) r/w s. 5(1)(d) - Appeal Dismissed.
