John K. Abraham v Simon C. Abraham and another

Supreme Court of India

05 December 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 800; (2014) 2 SCC 236; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 379; 2014 (102) ALR 732; 2014 (1) BC 3; 2014 (1) Bom.C.R. 141; 2014 CRLJ 2304; 2014 (206) DLT 234; 2014 (1) JCC (NI) 31; 2014 (1) KLT 90; 2014 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 266; 2014 (3) MahLJ 280; 2014 (2) MPLJ 328; 2014 (1) OLR 385; 2014 (173) PLR 787; 2014 (1) RCR(Civil) 314; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 267; 2013(14) SCALE 589; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 753

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 118, 138, 139 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 255(1), 357(1) - Dishonour of cheque - Conviction - Sustainability - Respondent (complainant) filed a complaint u/s. 138 of the Act against appellant (accused) - Trial Court found the appellant not guilty and acquitted him u/s. 255(1) of CrPC - Aggrieved respondent filed an appeal before HC, which was allowed by impugned judgment convicting appellant and imposed fine as compensation u/s. 357(1) of CrPC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in reversing the judgment passed by Trial Court - Held, in order to draw presumption u/s. 118 read along with s. 139 of the Act, burden was heavily upon the respondent to have shown that he had required funds for having advanced the money to appellant; that the issuance of cheque in support of payment advanced was true and that the appellant was bound to make the payment as had been agreed while issuing the cheque in favour of respondent - Respondent was not even aware of the date when substantial amount was advanced by him to appellant, that he was not sure as to who wrote the cheque, that he was not even aware when and where exactly the transaction took place for which the cheque came to be issued by appellant - Serious lacuna in evidence of respondent, noted by Trial Court, which factor was failed to be examined by HC while reversing judgment of Trial Court - Impugned judgment was set aside - Appeal allowed.
