James Anderson, Administrator of The Estate of Thelate Henry v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

04 March 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 160; AIR 1960 SC 751; 1960 (62) BomLR 646; [1960] 39 ITR 123; 1960 SCJ 665; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 167

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Family & Personal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 12 B(1), proviso. 3 and 24- B - (A) Whether s. 24-B of Income Tax Act, 1922, limits liability of Administrator or Executor? - Held, he is as much an assessee under Act, as any other individual and if he makes capital gains, he is as much liable to pay tax as any other individual - S. 24-B does not limit liability of Administrator or Executor to cases referred to under that section - (B) Whether s. 12 B(1) of Income Tax Act 1922 and proviso. 3 include transaction amounting to a distribution of capital assets under a will? - Held, administration of estate of deceased, sells certain shares and securities belonging to deceased for purpose of distributing assets amongst legatees and thereby realises more than their cost price - Transaction cannot amount to a distribution of capital assets under a will, so as to bring administrator within ambit of 3rd proviso to s. 12 B(1) - So he is liable to pay tax on capital gain u/s. 12 B(1) - Appeal dismissed.
