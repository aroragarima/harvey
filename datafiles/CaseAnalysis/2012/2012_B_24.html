Bhartiya Seva Samaj Trust Tr. Pres. & Anr. v Yogeshbhai Ambalal Patel & Anr

Supreme Court of India

14 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 295; (2012) 9 SCC 310; AIR 2012 SC 3285; 2012 (135) FLR 554; 2012 (7) MLJ(SC) 127; 2012(8) SCALE 698; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 1054; 2013 (1) SLR 533

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Bombay Primary Education (Gujarat Amendment) Act, 1986

Summary: Service - Bombay Primary Education (Gujarat Amendment) Act, 1986, s. 40B - Termination - Challenged - Appellant (Trust) terminate the services of respondent no. 1 on the ground that his appointment was in contravention of the statutory provisions of Act and particularly, in violation of the Schedule attached thereto - Respondent No.1 challenged said order for reinstatement with all back wages - Tribunal allowed said application of the respondent No.1 and directed the appellant to reinstate respondent No. 1 and also to pay him the back wages - The Single Judge dismissed application filed against said order and held that the termination was in utter disregard of the statutory provisions of s. 40B of the Act which requires to serve a show cause notice to the employee and seeking approval of the statutory authorities before giving effect to the order of termination - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that the respondent No.1 possesses the qualification of B.Sc.; B.Ed., but the required qualification for a Primary School Teacher was Primary Teachers Certificate (PTC) - Thus, the respondent No. 1 did not possess the qualification making him eligible for the post - Once the order was bad in its inception, it cannot be sanctified by lapse of time - Whether impugned order of the Single Judge could be upheld - Held, if a party has committed a wrong, he cannot be permitted to take the benefit of his own wrong - In the instant case, Tribunal as well as HC, after appreciating the evidence on record, recorded the findings to the effect that there had been two fold violation of s. 40B of the Act, firstly, no notice was issued to the respondent No.1 and secondly, no approval from the competent authority was sought for by the School management - The case do not warrant review of the orders passed by the HC as well as by the Tribunal - Appeal dismissed.
