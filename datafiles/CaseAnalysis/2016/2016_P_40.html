Philomina K. A. v District Collector Alappuzha and another

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 335; AIR 2016 SC 2225; 2016(4) ALL MR 468; 2016 (4) AWC 3698; JT 2016 (5) SC 51; 2016 (3) WLN(SC) 149

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative

Keywords: Kerala Freedom Fighters' Pension Rules, 1971

Summary: Administrative - Kerala Freedom Fighters' Pension Rules, 1971, r. 10 - Entitlement of pension by unmarried daughter - Rejection - Determination - Appellant is unmarried daughter of veteran freedom fighter who was given pension - Further, after death of appellant's father, pension was granted to her mother - Further, after death of appellant's mother, appellant applied for said pension on ground that she was unmarried daughter of veteran freedom fighter, hence, is entitled to get pension under Rules - However, application of appellant was rejected on ground that though appellant was unmarried daughter, however, she had son from illegitimate relation and she was staying with her nephew - Hence, present appeal.

Held, as per report of Tehsildar, she had no income and she started living with her nephew after demise of her parents. Hence, she never became independent of her father. SC held that HC is in error in relying upon fact that she has son who was born of extra-marital relationship of hers with somebody ignoring fact that he did not live with appellant and is living with his wife at his wife's house. Hence, all ingredients which are required to be satisfied to earn pension as per r. 10 of Rules stand satisfied in present case. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is quashed aside and appellant is entitled to pension w.e.f. 14-9-2012, as her mother passed away on 13-9-2012. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Applicant is entitled to pension after death of pension holder when all requirements to earn pension is satisfied.

Kerala Freedom Fighters' Pension Rules, 1971
