Satyaprata Sahoo & Ors v State Of Orissa & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

03 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 241; (2012) 8 SCC 203; AIR 2012 SC 2977; 2012 (5) AWC 4380; 2012 (2) OLR 570; 2012(7) SCALE 153; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 204; 2012 (6) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Education

Keywords: Postgraduate Medical Education Regulations, 2000, Post Graduate Medical Education (Amendment) Regulations, 2000, Postgraduate Medical Education (Amendment) Regulations, 2009, M.C.I. Regulation

Summary: Education - Administrative - Constitution - Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, ss. 10A, 33, r/w. s. 20 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - MCI regulations, 2000, cl. 9(2)(d) - Post graduation medical course admissions - Prospectus - Entrance test - Direct category - In-service category - Weightage - Sustainability - Prospectus was issued for post-graduate (medical) selection, 2012 - Appellants appeared in entrance examination as direct candidates (open category) and had qualified purely on merit for admission to post graduate (medical) courses 2012, in govt. medical colleges - Appellants, aggrieved by cl. 11.2 of prospectus contended that the clause wholly arbitrary, discriminatory and went contrary to ratio laid down by SC in various decisions - Appellants also prayed for quashing medical council of india (MCI) notification No. 51210 of 17-11-2009 provided weightage marks to in-service candidates applied through direct category, which according to appellants, was a clear encroachment and appropriation of seats earmarked for direct category candidates which had to be filled up purely on merit, subject to rule of reservation - Appellants challenged was repelled by Single Judge as well as by DB - Hence, instant appeals - Whether validity of cl. 11.2 of prospectus for selection of candidate challenged by appellant be sustainable - Held, by virtue of third proviso to cl. 9(2)(d) and cl. 11.2 of Prospectus candidates who fell under in-service category were given a weightage through which they should make an in-road into direct candidates category while retaining their rights to get admission for P.G. Course through in-service category - Seats earmarked for open category by way of merit were few in number and encroachment by in-service candidates into that open category would violate cl. 9(1)(a) of MCI regulations - Direct category or open category was a homogeneous class which consisted of all categories of candidates who were fresh from college, who had rendered service after MBBS in Government or private hospitals in remote and difficult areas like hilly areas, tribal and rural areas and so on - However, there should be no encroachment from one category to another - Candidates of in-service category should not encroach upon open category, so also vice-versa - Hence, proviso to cl. 9(2)(d) of MCI regulations was quashed to extend indicated above as well as cl. 11.2 of prospectus issued for admission to post graduate medical examination 2012, in State - Appeals allowed.
