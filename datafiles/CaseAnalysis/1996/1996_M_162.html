Smt. Rashmi Kumar v Mahesh Kumar Bhada

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.T. Nanavati, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2058; (1997) 2 SCC 397; (1997) SCC (Cr) 415; 1997 (1) AD(SC) 45; 1997 (4) Bom.C.R. 145; 1997 (1) CCR 168; 1997 (1) HLR 535; JT 1996 (11) SC 175; 1999 (2) RCR(Criminal) 43; 1996(9) SCALE 388; [1996] Supp10 S.C.R. 347

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Family & Personal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 125, 173, 200, 406 and 482 - Breach of trust by husband on his refusing to return stridhana property, viz., jewellery, wearing apparel etc. - Properties gifted to her before marriage, at time of marriage or at time of giving farewell or thereafter are her stridhana properties - (A) Whether fact of a wife's having been driven out from matrimonial home without taking along with her stridhana properties, amounts to entrustment with husband within meaning of s. 405, IPC? - It is her absolute property with all rights to dispose at her won pleasure - Stridhana property does not become a joint property of wife and husband and husband has no title or independent dominion over property a owner thereof - Held, essential ingredients for establishing an offence of criminal breach of trust laid down - In view of finding that stridhana property is exclusive property of wife on proof that entrusted property or dominion over stridhana property to her husband or any other member of family, there is no need to establish any further special agreement to establish that property was given to husband or other member of family - Majority view in Pratibha Rani 1985 Indlaw SC 318 requires no reconsideration - (B) High Court in impugned order quashed proceedings on two grounds, (i) appellant did not make out any case in complaint and (ii) it is barred by limitation - Whether HC was right in quashing complaint/proceedings u/s. 482? - It is not province of court to appreciate evidence at stage with reference to material and come to conclusion that no prima facie case is made out - Held, HC clearly in error in attempting to sift evidence with reference to averments made by respondent in counter-affidavit to find out whether or not offence had been made out - Further held, period of limitation to be three years if offence is punishable with imprisonment for a term exceeding one year but not exceeding three years - Since offence alleged to have been committed by respondent is punishable u/s. 406, complaint is required to be filed within three years from date of commission of offence - Complaint was filed within three years from date of demand and refusal by respondent - Complaint was filed within limitation - Appeal allowed.
