Mohd. Saud and another v Dr. (Maj.) Shaikh Mahfooz and others

Supreme Court of India

25 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 897; (2010) 13 SCC 517; 2011 (84) ALR 775; 2011 (1) ALT 53; 2010 (4) JhrCR(SC) 179; 2011 (1) MLJ(SC) 364; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 617; 2011 (112) RD 501; 2010(11) SCALE 283; 2010 (4) WLN(SC) 28

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100A and O. 43 r. 1 - Maintainability of Letters Patent Appeal (LPA) - Additional District Judge passed an interim order in a Civil Suit - Against the aforesaid interim order, a first appeal u/O. 43 r. 1 was filed before HC - Single Judge decided said issue on 6.8.2008 - Against said judgment of the HC, the LPA was filed - Full Bench of HC held that the said LPA was not maintainable in view of s. 100-A CPC - Hence, present appeal - Whether a LPA is maintainable before the DB against the judgment of the Single Judge? - Held, LPA in question was not maintainable because if it is held to be maintainable then the result will be that against an interlocutory order of the District Judge there may be two appeals, first to the Single Judge and then to the DB of the HC, but against a final judgment of the District Judge there can be only one appeal - This would be strange, and against the very purpose of object of s. 100-A of CPC, that is to curtail the number of appeals - Hence, Full Bench of the HC has taken a correct view - Appeals dismissed.
