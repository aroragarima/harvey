CEAT Limited v Anand Abasaheb Hawaldar and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 49; (2006) 3 SCC 56; AIR 2006 SC 1172; 2006 (2) Bom.C.R. 533; 2006 (1) CLR 804; JT 2006 (2) SC 426; 2006(2) SCALE 352; 2006 (3) SCJ 183; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 254; 2006 (2) SLR 679

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions and Prevention of Unfair Labour Practices Act, 1971, s. 3(5) - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2(s) - Unfair labour practice - Contention that one of benefits which was given to employees who had accepted VRS-II, namely payment of a sum of Rs. 90, 000/- Ex-gratia, had not been extended to complainants who had retired pursuant to VRS-I in 1992 - Industrial Court directed appellant to pay Rs. 90, 000/- To each of employees who had retired under VRS-I - Held, appellant-company was not guilty of favouritism or partiality - There is also no plea or proof that employer indulged in any violence or force to coerce 337 employees to accept VRS- Complaint of unfair labour practice is not established under Items 5, or 9 or 10 of Schedule 4 to Act - Appeal allowed.
