Neel Kumar @ Anil Kumar v The State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 444; (2012) 5 SCC 766; 2012 (3) CGLJ 312; JT 2012 (5) SC 23; 2012 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1672; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 941; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 696

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, ss. 302,376(2)(f), 201 - Rape - Guardian of Child - Blood Stained cloths - Death Sentence - Sustainability - Appellant-accused was convicted for offence u/ss. 302,376(2)(f), 201 of IPC - Prosecution case was that appellant father of deceased minor child had raped her and left her injured to die - Trial Court convinced by evidence on record convicted appellant holding that appellant was guardian of child and was duty bound to safeguard victim and accused had kept mum and had not given any information to any law enforcing agency or even to mother of victim, if somebody else would have committed offence it was but natural that appellant would have taken steps to initiate legal action to find out culprit, silence on his part in spite of such grave harm to his daughter was again a very strong incriminating circumstance against him - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before HC, which was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that he had been falsely enroped in offence by complainant-wife as relationship between them had been strained - State, had vehemently opposed appeal contending that appellant had committed most heinous crime, if he could commit rape of his own 4 years old daughter, society could not be safeguarded from such a person - Whether conviction of accused by Trail Court was sustainable and whether death sentence ordered was justified - Held, shirt and pant belonging to appellant recovered on basis of his disclosure statement and taken into possession and report of FSL showed that shirt and pant of appellant were stained with blood, however, no explanation had been given by appellant as to how blood was present on his clothes, presence of blood on his clothes was found to be incriminating circumstance against him - Further it was duty of accused to explain incriminating circumstance proved against him while making a statement u/s. 313 CrPC, keeping silent and not furnishing any explanation for such circumstance was an additional link in chain of circumstances to sustain charges against him, recovery of incriminating material at his disclosure statement duly proved is a very positive circumstance against him - There was no reason to disbelieve above evidence and circumstances nor there was any reason to doubt commission of offence by appellant and recovery of incriminating material on his disclosure statement - Incriminating circumstances taken into consideration by courts below could reasonably be inferred, so far as sentence part was concerned, in view of law referred, SC, were of considered opinion that case does not fall within rarest of rare cases, however, considering nature of offence, age and relationship of victim with appellant and gravity of injuries caused to her, appellant could not be awarded a lenient punishment - SC, set aside death sentence and awarded life imprisonment - Appeal disposed of.
