Vineet Kumar and others v State of U. P. and another

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhushan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 472; 2017 (5) ADJ 438; AIR 2017 SC 1884; 2017 (3) CTC 751; 2017 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 949; 2017 (2) RCR(Criminal) 497B

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Rape, FIR, First Information Report, Jurisdiction, Final Report, Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, Proof, Inherent Power, Protest, Inherent Jurisdiction, Criminal Proceedings, Abuse, Quash, Medical Examination, Cheque, Process Of Court, Cognizable, Dowry, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, Medical Report, Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, Registered, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Husband, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Complainant

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376(d),323,452 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Quashing of criminal proceedings - Complainant filed application against all three accused alleging commission of offence u/ss.376(d),323 and 452 of IPC - However, after completion of investigation and taking into consideration the materials collected during the investigation, IO concluded that no such incident took place on 22-10-2015 as alleged by complainant and later submitted Final Report before Trial Court for initiating proceeding u/s. 182 of CrPC against complainant - However, respondent no.2 moved Protest Petition and the same was allowed - Later, Magistrate passed order dt.3-8-2016 summoning accused and revision against the same was dismissed Sessions Judge by order dt.22-10-2016 - Aggrieved, accused filed application u/s.482 of CrPC before HC to quash orders dt.3-8-2016 and 22-10-2016 but the same was rejected - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned orders are sustainable .

Held, all family members of the complainant were living in the same house and brother of husband of complainant and his wife, in their statements before IO have admitted monetary transactions of his brother with the accused. Further, they also categorically denied that alleged incident happened in their house. Further, statements of both of them were part of Case Diary and was material which ought to have been looked into which was submitted by IO in the Final Report. Further, no medical examination was got done on the date of incident or even on next day or on 7-11-2015, when IO asked the complainant and her husband to get done the medical examination and later on it was done on 20-11-2015, which was wholly irrelevant. Apart from bald assertions by complainant that all accused have raped, there was nothing which could have led the Courts to form an opinion that instant case is fit a case of prosecution which ought to be launched. Further, it is a fit case where HC to ought to have exercised its jurisdiction u/s.482 of CrPC and quashed criminal proceedings. Hence, appeal is allowed and judgment of HC and orders dt.3-8-2016 and 22-10-2016 of lower courts including entire criminal proceedings are quashed. Appeal allowed.
