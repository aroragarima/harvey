C.I.T., Mumbai v M/S. Walfort Share & Stock Brokers P. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 521; (2010) 8 SCC 137; [2010] 326 ITR 1; JT 2010 (7) SC 484; 2010(6) SCALE 471; [2010] 7 S.C.R. 748; [2010] 192 TAXMAN 211

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Finance Bill, 2001

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 10(33), 14A - Loss on dividend stripped transaction - Admissibility - Appellant/Broker of the Stock Exchange purchased the tax free mutual fund units, however, incurred loss on its resale - Appellant/assessee, in its return, claimed tax exemption for the dividend received from the units and also claimed a set-off of loss incurred on the sale of the units - Assessment Officer (AO) accepted the claim of exemption with regard to the dividend, however, rejected the claim of set-off with regard to the loss of sale on the ground that a dividend stripping transaction was not a business transaction and since such a transaction was primarily for the purpose of tax avoidance, the loss so-called was an artificial loss created by a pre-designed set of transaction - Being aggrieved by the disallowance, appellant filed an appeal before the Commissioner - Commissioner confirmed AO' s order - Appellant filed appeal before the Tribunal - Tribunal set aside Commissioner's order holding that appellant was entitled to set-off the said loss from the impugned transactions against its other income chargeable to tax - Tribunal's order, subsequently, affirmed by the HC - Hence the present appeal - Whether in dividend stripping transaction the loss on sale of units could be considered as expenditure in relation to earning of dividend income exempt u/s. 10(33), disallowable u/s. 14A of the Act - Held, s. 14A disallows certain expenditures incurred to earn exempt income from being deducted from other income which is includible in the 'total income' for the purpose of chargeability to tax - A deduction for expenditure or loss which is not within the prohibition of s. 14A must be allowed if it is on the facts of the case a proper Debit Item to be charged against the incomings of the business in ascertaining the true profits - For attracting s. 14A, there has to be a proximate cause for disallowance, which is its relationship with the tax exempt income - Pay-back or return of investment is not such proximate cause, hence, s. 14A is not applicable in the present case - Thus, in the absence of such proximate cause for disallowance, s. 14A cannot be invoked - Return of investment cannot be construed to mean 'expenditure', hence there is no expenditure incurred in terms of s. 14A in the present case - Since the impugned transaction was not coming u/s. 14A, the same can be exempted u/s. 10(33) of the Act - No infirmity in the impugned HC order - Appeals dismissed.
