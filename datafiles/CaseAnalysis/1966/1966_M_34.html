Pandit Sri Chand and Others v Messrs Jagdish Parshad Kishan Chand and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 375; AIR 1966 SC 1427; 1966 (68) PLR 291D; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 451

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 16, r. 12 and O. 22, r. 12 - Abatement of appeal - Held, relied on State of Punjab v Nathu Ram - Representative of deceased appellant has to bring on record within prescribed time under O. 16, r. 12 - Mere fact that obligation arising under a covenant may be enforced severally against all covenantors does not make liability of each covenantor distinct - It is true that in enforcement of claim of decree-holder properties belonging to sureties individually may be sold separately - But that is because properties are separately owned and not because liability arises under distinct transactions - It must therefore be held that appeal has abated, because representatives of second appellant have not been brought on record within time permitted by r. 14 of O. 16 of Supreme Court Rules, 1950, and delay in filing petition to bring representatives on record has not been condoned - Appeal dismissed.
