M/S. Jaipur Udyog Ltd. & Anr. v Commissioner of Income Tax, Delhi, Rajasthanand

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 188; AIR 1969 SC 470; [1969] 71 ITR 799; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 193

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Companies Act, 1913

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1961, ss.72, 80 and 141 - Income Tax - Scope of assessment u/s.141 - Assessee claiming to deduct loss in respect of previous year in excess of loss certified - (A) Whether I.T.O. can disallow the same in the light of ss.72 and 80 - (B) Whether he is obliged to demand advance-tax only for the amount provisionally assessed by way of regular assessment in the light of s. 210(3) - Held, ITO cannot adjudicate upon claim and disallow the same -There must be valid provisional assessment on basis of which advance tax is demanded - Appeal Allowed.
