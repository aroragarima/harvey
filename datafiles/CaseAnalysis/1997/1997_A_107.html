State Of Rajasthan v Ani @ Hanif And Others

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 752; (1997) 6 SCC 162; (1997) SCC (Cr) 851; AIR 1997 SC 1023; AIR 1997 SCW 973; 1997 (1) CCR 151; 1997 (1) Crimes 146; 1997 CRLJ 1529; JT 1997 (1) SC 460; 1997(1) SCALE 287; 1997 (1) SCJ 97; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 199; 1997 (2) Supreme 228

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Summary: Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss.165 and 137, Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.300, 149 and 307, Criminal Procedore Code, 1973, s.154 - Whether the court has the power to put a question to the witness in a trial - Whether the conviction of the accused for the murder is proper in the light of the fact that there is confusion regarding time of recording FIR - Held, the court is expected to actively participate in the trial and therefore, can question the witness during cross-examination or chief examination to elicit truth - Conviction of the accused is correct and mere discrepancy regarding time of recording FIR not to castigate the testimony of the eye-witness - However, evidence of injured witness not sufficient to establish case against two accused persons, hence, they are entitled to benefit of doubt.
