Sunil Kumar v Escorts Yamaha Motors Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 797; (1999) 8 SCC 468; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1466; AIR 2000 SC 27; 2000 CRLJ 174; JT 1999 (8) SC 413

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal

Keywords: Dishonour of cheques

Summary: Banking & Finance - Criminal - Constitution - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 406, 420 and 468 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Requirements - Held, necessary ingredients of alleged offence not made out - Circumstances indicate that FIR lodged to pre-empt filing of criminal complaint u/s. 138 - Abuse of process of court - High Court therefore, well within its power in quashing FIR as otherwise it would tantamount to an abuse of process of Court - No justification to interfere u/art. 136 - Appeal dismissed.
