Union of India and others v Prem Narain Mishra

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 4137; (2001) 10 SCC 481

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Seniority list

Summary: Service - Seniority list - Challenged - Sustainability - Respondent was appointed as Commercial Clerk in appellant Eastern Railway - Respondent was asked to work as Enquiry-cum-Reservation Clerk in scale of Rs 150-560 on ad hoc basis as stopgap arrangement - Gradation list was circulated other Commercial Clerks aforementioned were shown senior to respondent - Feeling aggrieved by seniority list, respondent filed petition before HC which was subsequently transferred to Tribunal - Petition was allowed by Tribunal - While petition was pending before HC and Tribunal, steps were taken for promotion to selection scale of Rs 455-700 which involved written test followed by oral test - Respondent could not appear in written test - Later respondent appeared in written test held but failed to obtain minimum qualifying marks - Selection panel containing names of other persons who were junior to respondent was prepared - By order pay scales were revised and respondent was placed senior in grades Rs 330-560 and Rs 425-640 and he would not get any benefit of seniority as he could not qualify in written examination -Feeling aggrieved by order respondent filed petition before Tribunal wherein he sought direction that his seniority might be fixed above other person and he be placed in grade of Rs 700-900 on basis that other person was in that grade - Tribunal held that since respondent had been found to be senior to others, railway administration was duty-bound to consider him for each promotion on higher grades whenever those persons who were found junior to respondent were considered for any higher grade or higher promotion and that he was entitled to grade of Rs 700-900 (revised scale Rs 2000-3200) which was granted to other person - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order of Tribunal was sustainable - Held, prior to revision of pay scales selection was made for purpose of promotion to higher grade of Rs 455-700 and in that selection while other person was selected, respondent was not selected since he failed to obtain minimum qualifying marks in written test - However it did not appear from record that any order was passed promoting other person to scale of Rs 455-700 on the basis of the said selection - Date when pay scales were revised respondent and other person who were in same grade of Rs 425-640 and by virtue of judgment of Tribunal respondent was senior to other person in the said grade - Any promotion to higher grade after revision of pay scales had to be made on the basis of said seniority - Direction given by Tribunal that respondent should be given scale of Rs 700-900 (revised scale Rs 2000-3200) appellant had pointed out that other person had not been given said scale of Rs 700-900 as claimed by respondent and that he was promoted to the grade of Rs 550-750 (revised scale Rs 1600- 2660) - Hence, direction given by Tribunal should not, therefore be upheld and had to be set aside - All that respondent should claim was that he be considered for higher grade/grades on same basis on which persons junior to him have been considered - Appeal dismissed.
