Suthendraraja Alias Suthenthira Raja Alias Santhan and Others v State Through DSP/CBI, SIT Chennai and another

Supreme Court of India

08 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, K.T. Thomas, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 620; (1999) 9 SCC 323; (2000) SCC (Cr) 463; AIR 1999 SC 3700; AIR 1999 SCW 3734; 1999 (4) CCR 102; 1999 (4) Crimes 116; 1999 CRLJ 4587; 1999 (2) JCC 617; JT 1999 (8) SC 132; 1999 (4) RCR(Criminal) 548; 1999(6) SCALE 381; 1999 (3) SCJ 615; 1999 (8) SLT 343; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 540; 1999 (8) Supreme 438

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Foreigners; International

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 137 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities(Prevention) Act, 1987 - Foreigners Act, 1946 - Death reference case and Criminal Appeal - Review is only on question of sentence - Assassination of former PM Rajiv Gandhi - Conviction - After examination of role of each accused - Death penalty to four and life imprisonment for three, as per role played - Crime without intent to strike terror not offending s. 3 of TADA - Death sentences filed review petition - Pointing errors not affecting ultimate result - (A) Plea, they should also be awarded sentence of life imprisonment - (B) Maintainability of petition - (C) One member of Bench of three - Judges, preferring imprisonment for life for particular accused - Effect on treating case as 'rarest of rare cases' - Held, plea not tenable - Petition not maintainable - Said fact should be sufficient to treat case as such and bench should review order of sentence of death in respect of that accused - Petition dismissed.
