State of Uttar Pradesh v Satish

Supreme Court of India

08 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 83; (2005) 3 SCC 114; (2005) SCC (Cr) 642; AIR 2005 SC 1000; 2005 CRLJ 1428; JT 2005 (2) SC 153; 2005(2) SCALE 33; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 1132

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bride Burning

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 27 - IPC, 1860, ss. 201, 302, 363, 366, 376(2) - CrPC 1973, ss. 366 - Accused found guilty by Trial Court held to be one falling under rarest of rare category - HC acquitted accused - Case rests upon circumstantial evidence - Appeal against acquittal - Held, considering view expressed by this Court in Bachan Singh's case (1980 Indlaw SC 586) and Machhi Singh's case (1983 Indlaw SC 116), the case at hand falls in rarest of rare category and death sentence awarded by trial Court was appropriate - Acquittal of respondent-accused is clearly unsustainable and is set aside - Appeal allowed.
