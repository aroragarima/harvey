Central Bank of India v Ravindra and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, A.S. Anand, K.T. Thomas, N. Santosh Hegde, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19930; (2002) 1 SCC 367; AIR 2001 SC 3095; AIR 2001 SCW 4468; 2002 (2) ALD(SC) 97; 2001 (45) ALR 695; 2002 (1) BC 150; 2002 (2) Bom.C.R. 616; [2001] 107 Comp Cas 416; 2002 (2) CTC 354; JT 2001 (9) SC 102; 2002 (1) KLT 743; 2002 (1) MLJ(SC) 109; 2002 (1) RCR(Civil) 49; 2001(7) SCALE 351; 2001 (5) SCJ 133; 2001 (7) SLT 400; [2001] Supp4 S.C.R. 323; 2001 (7) Supreme 765

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Banker And Customer, Agreement Of Hypothecation, Attorney-General

Summary: Civil Procedure - Banking & Finance - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 34(1) - Banking Regulation Act, 1949, ss. 21A and 35A - Whether creditor can charge interest from his debtor on periodical rests and also capitalise same so as to make it a part of principal? - Expression 'the principal sum adjudged' may include amount of interest, charged on periodical rests, and capitalised with principal sum actually advanced, so as to become an amalgam of principal in such cases where it is permissible or obligatory for court to hold so - 1956 amendment cannot be read as intending, expressly or by necessary implication, to deprive court of its power to award future interest on amount of principal sum adjudged - Corpn Bank v. D. S. Gowda, 1994 Indlaw SC 345 and Bank of Baroda v. Jagannath Pigment and Chemicals, 1996 Indlaw SC 1060 affirmed - Held, subject to a binding stipulation contained in a voluntary contract between parties and/or an established practice or usage interest on loans and advances may be charged on periodical rests and also capitalised on remaining unpaid, principal sum actually advanced coupled with interest on periodical rests so capitalised is capable of being adjudged as principal sum on date of suit - Further Court held, principal sum so adjudged is 'such principal sum' within meaning of s. 34, CPC, 1908 on which interest pendente lite and future interest i.e., post-decree interest, at such rate and for such period which court may deem fit, may be awarded by court - (3) Corpn Bank v. D. S. Gowda, 1994 Indlaw SC 345 and Bank of Baroda v Jagannath Pigment and Chem, 1996 Indlaw SC 1060 have been correctly decided - Reference answered.
