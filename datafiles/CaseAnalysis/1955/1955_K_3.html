Â State of Madras and Another v K.M. Rajagopalan

Supreme Court of India

27 September 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Jagannadhadas, Vivian Bose, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 23; AIR 1955 SC 817; 1956 (1) MLJ(SC) 12; 1955 (57) PLR 532; 1956 SCJ 19; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 541

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Automatic Termination, Indian Independence Act, 1947, Government of India Act 1935, misa

Summary: Service - Government of India Act, 1935, s. 240 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 133 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 80 - Indian Independence Act, 1947, s. 10(2) - Whether order for termination of services of respondent, member of Indian Civil Service, as from afternoon of 14-08-1947 was null, void and inoperative? - Apart from the fact that Secy. of State and his services disappeared as from 15-08-1947, s. 10(2) of Indian Independence Act, art. 7(1) of India (Provisional Constitution) - Order proceeded on a clear, unequivocal recognition of validity of various special orders, individual arrangements made, amount to an implicit statutory recognition of principle of automatic termination of services brought about by political change - Therefore, services of respondent came to an automatic termination on emergence of Indian Dominion - Special order and arrangement affecting his case that, as made in pursuance of Viceroy's announcement resulted in his service not being continued from and after 15-08-1947, and respondent is not entitled to declaration prayed for - Appeal allowed.
