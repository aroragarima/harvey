Kamlesh Kumar v State of Bihar and another

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 819; (2014) 2 SCC 424; AIR 2014 SC 660; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 348; 2014 (4) CompLJ 16; 2014 CRLJ 1071; 2014 (1) CTC 329; 2014 (1) JCC (NI) 42; JT 2014 (1) SC 209; 2014 (4) MahLJ 30; 2014 (1) MLJ(Crl) 230; 2014(3) M.P.H.T. 512; 2014 (2) MPLJ 672; 2014 (1) OLR 456; 2014 (174) PLR 701; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 332; 2014 (3) RLW 1880; 2013(15) SCALE 202; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 793

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Legal Notice

Summary: Banking & Finance - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s.138 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.482 - Filing of Cheque bounce case - Beyond period of limitation - Maintainability - Appellant was facing trial in the complaint filed by respondent No.2 u/s. 138 of Act - According to the appellant, criminal complaint was not maintainable and no such proceedings could be launched against him - Appellant, therefore, approached HC in the form of a petition u/s. 482 of the CrPC for quashing of the order whereby the Court of Magistrate had taken cognizance of the complaint filed by the respondent No.2 issued summons to the appellant - Petition, however, was dismissed by the HC vide impugned judgment - Solitary reason given by the HC while dismissing the petition was that trial had already commenced and two witnesses were already examined and discharged - Hence instant appeal - Appellant submitted that even on admitted facts the complaint was untenable as it was clearly time barred and not filed within the stipulated period prescribed in law and therefore the HC could not have scuttled the issue raised by the appellant by merely relegating the appellant to the Trial Court when the issue could be decided on the admitted facts on records - , further, submitted that the appellant had approached the High Court without loss of any time and if during the pendency of the petition filed by the appellant under Section 482, CrPC two witnesses were examined in the meantime, that factor could not have weighed against the appellant - Held, period of limitation was not to be counted from the date when the cheque in question was presented in the first instance on 25-10-2008 or the legal notice was issued on 27-10-2008, inasmuch as the cheque was presented again on 10-11-2008 - For the purposes of limitation, in so far as legal notice was concerned, it was to be served within 30 days of the receipt of information by the drawyee from the bank regarding the return of the cheque as unpaid - Therefore, after the cheque was returned unpaid, notice was to be issued within 30 days of the receipt of information in this behalf - That was the period of limitation provided for issuance of legal notice calling upon the drawer of the cheque to make the payment - After the sending of the notice 15 days time was to be given to the noticee, from the date of receipt of the said notice to make the payment, if that was already not done - If noticee failed to make the payment, the offence could be said to have been committed and in that event cause of action for filing the complaint would accrue to the complainant and he was given one month time from the date of cause of action to file the complaint - Applying the principles, SC found that cheque was presented, second time, on 10-11-2008 - Complainant, however, sent the legal notice on 17-12-2008 i.e. much after the expiry of the 30 days - It was clear from the complaint filed by the complainant himself that he had gone to the bank for encashment the cheque on 10-11-2008 but the cheque was not honoured due to the unavailability of the balance in the account - It was, thus, clear from averment made by the complainant himself that he had gone to the bank for encashing the cheque on 10-11-2008 and found that because of unavailability of sufficient balance in the account, the cheque was bounced - Therefore, it became obvious that complainant come to know about the same on 10-11-2008 itself - In view of this admission in the complaint about the information having been received by the complainant about the bouncing of the cheque on 10-11-2008 itself, no further enquiry was needed on that aspect - It was, thus, apparent that complainant received the information about the dishonor of the cheque on 10-11-2008 itself - However, complainant did not send the legal notice within 30 days therefrom - SC, thus, found that the complaint filed by complainant was not maintainable as it was filed without satisfying all the three conditions laid down in s.138 of Act - MSR Leathers vs. S.Palaniappan & Anr. 2012 Indlaw SC 624, relied on - Petition allowed.
