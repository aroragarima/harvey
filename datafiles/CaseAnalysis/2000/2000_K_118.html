State of Gujarat and Others v Kaushikbhai K. Patel and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 445; (2000) 5 SCC 615; 2000 (2) ACC 46; AIR 2000 SC 2175; 2001 (1) G.L.R. 388; JT 2000 (6) SC 484; 2000(4) SCALE 680; 2000 (4) SLT 460; [2000] Supp1 S.C.R. 61; 2000 (4) Supreme 231

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950 - Bombay Motor Vehicles Tax Act 1958 - Bombay Motor Vehicles Tax Act 1958, s. 3 A(5) - Levy of tax on omnibus - Claim for non user exceeding three months - Section requiring claimant to satisfy authorities for non user for reasons beyond his control - Owner or a person in possession or control of vehicle should satisfy reasons beyond control for non - use of vehicle is attached to omnibuses - Held, taxes are levied on vehicles using roads or in any way forming part of flow of traffic not on vehicle which do not use roads at all - What is material and relevant is use of road - Reasons for non - use of roads is immaterial and irrelevant - When tax itself is compensatory for use of roads owner or a person in possession or control of vehicle should satisfy reasons beyond control for non - use of the vehicle is attached to omnibuses and not to other vehicles.
