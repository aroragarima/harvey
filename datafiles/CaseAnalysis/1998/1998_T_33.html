Mohd. Iqbal M. Shaikh and Others v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1637; (1998) 4 SCC 494; (1998) SCC (Cr) 1064; AIR 1998 SC 2864; 1998 (5) Bom.C.R. 475; 1998 (2) CCR 114; 1998 (2) Crimes 106; 1998 CRLJ 2537; 1998 (2) JCC 180; JT 1998 (3) SC 135; 1998 (2) RCR(Criminal) 335; 1998(2) SCALE 686; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 734; 1998 (4) SLT 284; 1998 (3) Supreme 596

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Keywords: communal harmony, communal violence, communalism

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, Ss. 120(B), 147, 148, 149, 302, 326, 436, 506 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, Ss .3(2)(1),19 - Demolition - Communal riots - Conviction - Sanction - Appellants and six other stood charged u/ss.120(B), 147, 148, 149, 302, 326, 436, 506 of IPC and s.3(2)(1) of 1987 Act for ghastly occurrence wherein six persons died out of burn injuries being locked in a room and room having been put to fire by putting petrol on it - After demolition of mosque at Ayodya communal riots erupted all over country - Appeals by 11 accused persons u/s.19 of 1987 Act were directed against judgment passed by Additional Court - Whether conviction of any of accused appellants could be sustained and Whether sanction accorded u/s.20A(2) was valid- Held, could not be a valid sanction in eye of law - Evidence was wholly unsustainable and as such appellants were entitled to be acquitted - Sanctioning authority accorded sanction, same would be sufficient to hold that there was a valid sanction - No valid sanction as required u/s. 20A(2) of Act - No illegality in taking cognizance and trying accused persons under TADA - Appeals disposed of.
