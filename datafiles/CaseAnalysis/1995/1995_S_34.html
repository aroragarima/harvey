State Of Bihar And Others v Sachchidanand Vishore Prasad Sinha And Others

Supreme Court of India

16 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1425; (1995) 3 SCC 86; AIR 1995 SC 885; JT 1995 (1) SC 458; 1995 (1) RentLR 512; 1995(1) SCALE 240; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 256

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Tort

Summary: Income & Direct Taxes - Patna Municipal Corporation Act, 1952, ss. 130 and 227 - Assessment of Annual Rental Value of Holding Rules, 1993, rr. 3(1)(a), 3(1)(c) and 5 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Levy of municipal taxes - Valuation of holdings - (A) Classification of buildings by statutory authority - Validity - Whether mere possibility of better classification can be a ground to strike it down - (B) Striking down of r. 3(1) on ground of division of Municipal Corporation area with reference to roads and not on basis of zones - (C) Determination of annual rental value as per notifications u/rr. 3 and 5 - More uniform method adopted to eliminate arbitrariness and corruption - But rate of tax substantially reduced - Maintainability of notification - Held, not a ground, more particularly in taxing enactment - No - Not liable to be struck down - Appeal allowed.
