Banamali Das v Rajendra Chandra Mardaraj Harichandan and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 August 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Bhagwati, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 136; (1976) 1 SCC 54; AIR 1975 SC 1863; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 212; 1975 UJ 626

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Parliament & Legislature

Summary: Election - Parliament & Legislature - Representation of the People Act, 1951, s. 81 - Conduct of Election Rules 1961, r. 56 (7) - Assembly election - Double counting of ballot papers - Legality - Respondent no. 1 filed election petition u/s. 81 of the Act on 13-4-1974 challenging election of appellant and prayed that, instead, he himself should be declared as successful candidate - Respondent 1 challenged election of appellant mainly on the ground that through an error Returning officer did not enter results of second round of counting on Table No. 13 in Form No. 20, as prescribed by r. 56(7) of the Rules - -It was alleged that results of second round of counting on Table No. 14 were erroneously entered twice in Form No. 20, once as against second round of Table No. 14 and once as against second round of Table No. 13 - HC set aside appellant's election and declared respondent no. 1 as successful in election - Hence instant appeal - Whether respondent no. 1 discharged the onus of proving that result of second round of counting on 13th Table was not at all recorded in Form No, 20 and whether result of second round of counting on Table No. 14 was erroneously entered as against second round of Table No. 13 - Held, a mere look at the entries in Form No. 20 relating to second round of counting on Table Nos. 13 and 14 would disclose the error committed in making entries therein - 'Entries made in respect of Table No. 14 were accurate, but precisely those very figures were through some error carried to second round of Table No. 13 - It could seldom happen that 5contesting, candidates would secure precisely the same number of votes in the same round of counting on 2different tables, when a thousand ballot papers were distributed to the two tables by picking them up at random from a common drum or receptacle - However, it was unnecessary to speculate about any such possibility because it was incontrovertible that entries in Check Memo relating to second round of counting on Table No. 13 were not transferred to appropriate column of Form No. 20 - Therefore, SC up hold finding of HC that result of second round of 14th Table came to be recorded twice and that true result of second round of counting on Table No. 13 was entirely omitted while making entries in Form No. 20 - It should follow that respondent no.1 had secured maximum number of valid votes and was therefore entitled to be declared as successful candidate - Further, HC was in error in directing for recount of all ballot papers, merely because his suggestion was accepted by parties appearing before him that court should take a recount of all the ballot papers - Respondent No.1 who filed election petition had not asked for such a recount and defence of appellant to petition was that entries in Form No. 20 reflected a true picture and contained no error - Consent to recount was given only by appellant and respondents No. 1 and other respondents who had contested election did not appear at trial of election petition but they certainly had no notice that, a recount would be suggested or accepted when there was no plea about it in the pleadings of parties - Appeal dismissed.
