Commissioner of Income Tax v Karam Chand Thapar and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2280; (1996) 10 SCC 575; AIR 1996 SC 2667; 1996 (135) CTR 443; [1996] 222 ITR 112; JT 1996 (7) SC 280; 1996(5) SCALE 843; [1996] Supp4 S.C.R. 651; [1996] 88 TAXMAN 40; 1996 (2) TLR 784

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 28 - Trading receipt - Assessment years 1953-54, 1956-57, 1957-58, 1958-59, 1959-60, 1960-61, 1961-62 and 1962-63 - (A) Whether, Tribunal was right in holding that amounts received by assessee by way of under-charges, do not constitute its trading receipts and that accordingly neither surplus of receipts remaining unpaid nor amounts transferred by assessee to profit and loss account could by assessed as income of assessee in years in relevant years? - There is nothing in facts of case to suggest that money received by assessee from colliery companies actually belonged to consignees and was not assessee's own money - Answered in negative and in favour of Revenue - Appeals allowed.
