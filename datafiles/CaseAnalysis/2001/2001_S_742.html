Daya Singh v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

20 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20277; (2001) 3 SCC 468; (2001) SCC (Cr) 553; AIR 2001 SC 1188; AIR 2001 SCW 936; 2001 (2) Crimes 93; 2001 CRLJ 1268; JT 2001 (3) SC 16; 2001 (1) RCR(Criminal) 842; 2001(2) SCALE 122; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 1115; 2001 (2) SLT 247; 2001 (2) Supreme 583

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 9 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 302, 307 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 3, 5 - Murder - Delay in identification parade - Conviction - Reliability - 14 Accused were tried for various offences including ss. 3 and 5 of the 1987 Act - Trial Court convicted appellant for committing offence of murder of 'G' and attempting to commit murder of PWs - Appellant was also convicted u/ s. 302 r/w s. 34 IPC for committing murder and s. 5 of the 1987 Act for possessing one AK 47 rifle with cartridges- Trial Court acquitted rest of accused - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Trial court was justified in convicting accused - Held identification parades were not primarily meant for Court - They were meant for investigation purposes - There was no lapse on part of Investigating Officer in holding test identification parade - Further identification in Court was out of 14 persons, that itself would lend credence to identification by witnesses - For this purpose, Trial Court had rightly observed to effect that physical features of accused must have been embedded in memory - From evidence and cross-examination of these two witnesses, it was apparent that they gained enduring impression of identity of accused during incident - Therefore, delay in trial for one reason or other and thereafter identification of accused in Court after seven or eight years would not affect evidence of these two witnesses - Therefore, Trial Court was justified in convicting accused and acquitting rest of accused - Appeal dismissed.
