Surajsinh Alias Sonu Surajsinh Collectorsinh Alias Sevaram Rajput v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

18 April 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhushan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 294; AIR 2017 SC 2091; JT 2017 (5) SC 460; 2017 (2) RCR(Criminal) 721; 2017(5) SCALE 27

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Kidnapping, Child Witness, Marriage, Ransom, Arms Act, 1959, Right To Information Act, 2005, Registered, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Complainant

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 114, 307, 332, 34, 353, 364(A), 379 - Kidnapping - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant/accused allegedly kidnapped complainant's son and demanded a ransom - FIR was registered against accused u/ss. 364(A), 114, 307, 332, 353 and 34 IPC - Trial Court found accused guilty for offence u/ss. 364(A), 114 IPC and sentenced them life imprisonment - On appeal, HC dismissed appeal affirming judgment of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, Court did not commit any error in believing PW11 whose identity card was used for obtaining SIM Card who appeared and has stated that he had lost his ID Card and Driving Licence six to seven months ago, which was utilised for obtaining SIM Card. Court has rightly believed PW11 whose no connection was found with the entire episode of kidnapping and ransom call. Trial Court found that complainant and accused were not known to each other and there was no reason for complainant and his relatives to give false evidence against accused. In cross-examination of PW6, not even a suggestion was given that he was giving evidence on tutoring by someone. Even if, SC ignore the evidence of PW7, guilt of accused was proved by other relevant and material evidence as noticed by Court below. Fact that appellant was acquitted from charges u/ss. 307, 332 and 353 IPC by giving benefit of doubt does not in any manner help appellant insofar as conviction recorded against him u/ss. 364(A) and 114 IPC was concerned. Thus, prosecution case of kidnapping victim and taking victim from one place to another was fully proved and evidence of child witness was corroborated by evidence of PW3, PW5, PW13, PW14 and PW17. Trial Court has thoroughly marshaled oral and documentary evidence on record. HC on re-appreciation of such evidence has affirmed the order of conviction of appellant. SC do not find any error in judgments of Courts below warranting interference. Appeal dismissed.
