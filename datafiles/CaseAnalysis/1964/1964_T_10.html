D. C. Dewan Mohideen Sahib and Sons v Industrial Tribunal, Madras

Supreme Court of India

06 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 157; AIR 1966 SC 370; 1964 (9) FLR 86; 1964 (2) LLJ 633; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 646

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial - Industrial Disputes Act, 194, ss. 2(k), 2(s) and 10 - Appellant proprietor of two bidi concern - Wages of workers referred to Industrial Tribunal -Appellant filed two writ petitions in High Court holding tribunal decision wrong - (A) Whether contractor under whom workers worked is an employee of bidi concern? - Held, that contractors were not independent contractors and were mere employees or were functioning as branch managers of various factories, their remuneration being dependent upon work turned out - (B) Whether applleant is real employer of workers? - Held that appellants were real employers of workmen and so-called intermediaries or so-called independent contractors who were in some cases ex-employees, were no more than agents of appellants - (C) Whether reduction of annas two in wages of workers employed under agents of appellants was justified? - Further held, that reduction in wages by two annas per thousand bidis was not justified and workmen were entitled to old rates It therefore ordered reduction in wages to be restored - Appeal dismissed.
