Gopinder Singh v Forest Department Of Himachal Pradesh And Ors

Supreme Court of India

17 August 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 375; (1990) Supp SCC 272; AIR 1991 SC 433; JT 1990 (3) SC 561; 1990(2) SCALE 334; [1990] 3 S.C.R. 797; 1990 (2) UJ 564

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Land & Property - Socio - Economic - Himachal Pradesh Nautor Land Rules, 1968, r. 7(a) - (A) Eligibility for allotment of nautor land - Person having two bighas of land but otherwise earning more than Rs. 2000/- per annum - Held, not eligible for grant of nautor land - Object of granting nautor land under rules is to help poor and unprovided for residents of Himachal Pradesh -(B) Land Seized - Grounds - Appellant was earning more than Rs. 2000/- per annum and cut down trees without approval of Divisional Forest Officer - Further held, that allotment was rightly rejected - Appeal dismissed.
