Subh Ram And Others v Haryana State And Another

Supreme Court of India

20 October 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2064; (2010) 1 SCC 444; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 241; 2010 (1) ALD(SC) 89; 2010 (78) ALR 226; 2010 (1) AWC 244; JT 2009 (14) SC 115; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 979; 2010 (109) RD 305; 2009(13) SCALE 528; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 287

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 23 and 24 - Determination of compensation - Deduction towards development cost - Land Acquisition Collector offered compensation for acquiring land for establishment of a jail @ Rs. 60,000/- per acre, Rs. 50,000/- per acre and Rs. 40,000/- per acre - Reference Court enhanced compensation uniformly to Rs. 36.20 per sq. yd. i.e. Rs. 1,75,200/- per acre - Said determination by Reference Court was affirmed by HC - Claimants filed appeals against said orders, contending that the compensation awarded is inadequate on the ground that sale of one acre of land for a price of Rs.30,000/- per acre relied on by the Collector ought to have been excluded from consideration, while determining the market value and that the deduction of one-third of the market value of small plots, towards development cost, is erroneous and no deduction ought to have been made - Whether compensation of acquired land could be enhanced? - Held, to determine the value of large tract of acquired land, it is necessary to make an appropriate deduction therefrom towards development cost - Having regard to the fact that all the acquired lands adjoin a State Highway and the proximate availability of facilities which can be easily accessed for development, it would be appropriate to limit the deduction to 40% towards development cost, instead of the usual higher percentage of deduction ranging from 50% to 67% - Thus, market value would work out to be Rs.59/34 per sq.yd or Rs.2,87,200/- per acre - Compensation increased for the acquired lands to Rs. 2,87,500/- per acre plus solatium at 30% with interest on the total compensation - Appeal allowed.
