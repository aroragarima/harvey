Tarinikamal Pandit And Ors. v Perfulla Kumar Chatterjee (Dead) By L.R.S

Supreme Court of India

21 February 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

P.S. Kailasam, V.R. Krishna Iyer, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 267; (1979) 3 SCC 280; AIR 1979 SC 1165; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 340

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Mortgage Property - Sale - Public Auction - Conveyance - Suit property belonged to appellant and executed a mortgage in favor of Bank, which instituted suit against appellant for recovery of amount and for enforcement of mortgage - HC passed a decree against appellant and appointed Official Liquidator of Bank as Receiver of mortgaged property including suit properties - Final decree was passed, directing sale of mortgaged property including suit properties by public auction subject to confirmation by HC - Property was auctioned and sale in favor of respondent was confirmed by HC - Agreement was entered into between appellants and respondent for payment of balance consideration - Respondent filed an application in the HC which directed execution of conveyance in favor of respondent alone - Trial Court decreed suit and respondent/defendant preferred an appeal to HC, which accepted appeal and dismissed suit - Hence instant appeal - Whether First appellants would entitled to any relief - Held, Court viewed that if amount was not advanced as a loan, but paid towards acquiring of a share in property, relief could not be denied - Hence, appellants/plaintiffs 2 and 3 were entitled to 1/4th share of property - Respondent/defendant had been in possession of property since purchase and appellants were entitled to their share of rents collected by them - There would be a decree for partition and separate possession of 1/4th share each of appellants 2 and 3 and had to pay to respondent Rs. 3,500 each and interest per annum from date of conveyance - First appellants would not be entitled to any relief and only his suit was dismissed - Appeal allowed.
