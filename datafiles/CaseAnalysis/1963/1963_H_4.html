Hari Narain v Badri Das

Supreme Court of India

04 March 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 124; AIR 1963 SC 1558; 1964 (1) MLJ(SC) 25; 1964 (1) SCJ 51; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 203

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Rajasthan Premises (Control of Rent and Eviction) Act, 1950, s. 13 - Whether appellant making inaccurate and misleading material statements is entitled to grant of Special Leave Petiton? - Held, there is no doubt that unambiguous and categorical statement made in last ground of appellant's petition for special leave is wholly untrue - Appellant has made an equally inaccurate statement - Appeal may come on for hearing long after special leave is granted, that counsel appearing at stage of admission may not be same as at stage of final hearing, and Bench that granted special leave may not necessarily deal with appeal at final stage - It is of utmost importance that in making material statements and setting forth grounds in applications for special leave, care must be taken not to make any statements which are inaccurate, untrue or misleading - Appeal dismissed.
