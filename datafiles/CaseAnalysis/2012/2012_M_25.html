The Church Of Christ Charitable Trust & Educational Charitable Society, Represented By Its Chairman v M/S Ponniamman Educational Trust Represented By Its Chairperson/Managing Trustee

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 189; (2012) 8 SCC 706; 2012 (6) ALD(SC) 36; 2012 (93) ALR 915; 2012 (4) AWC 3883; 2012 (3) CGLJ 499; 2012 (3) CLT(SC) 169; 2012 (6) MahLJ 738; 2012 (5) MLJ(SC) 601; 2012 (4) MPLJ 578; 2012 (2) OLR 501; 2012 (168) PLR 627; 2012 (3) RCR(Civil) 811; 2012 (117) RD 262; 2012(6) SCALE 121; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 404; 2012 (3) WLN(SC) 33

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Civil Procedure - Trusts & Associations - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7 r. 11 - Rejection of plaint - Cause of action - Defendant No.1 (Society), the owner of the suit property, entered into an agreement for sale of suit property in favour of defendant No. 2 on the condition that the transaction should be completed within 6 months after obtaining clearance from Income Tax and other departments - Defendant No. 1 executed a registered power of attorney in favour of the defendant No. 2 limited for the purpose of empowering him to represent the defendant No. 1 before the statutory authorities - Defendant No. 1 revoked the said registered power of attorney executed in favour of the defendant No. 2 by a registered document alleging various reasons - Defendant No. 2 failed to comply with the commitments made, the defendant No. 1 cancelled the agreement for sale - Later, a MoU was entered into between the respondent and defendant No. 2 in which defendant No. 2 agreed to sell the suit property as the agreement holder and power of attorney agent of the defendant No. 1 - Respondent filed suit for specific performance of the agreement and also for interim injunction restraining the defendants from in any way dealing with or alienating the suit property - Defendant No. 1 filed application under O. 7 r. 11 of CPC praying for rejection of the plaint - The Single Judge rejected the said plaint of respondent and directed that the suit can be proceeded against the defendant No. 2 - The Single Judge also rejected the application for interim injunction - DB while dismissing the appeals against the order rejecting the applications for interim injunction, allowed the appeal against the rejection of the plaint - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether DB was right in reversing the decision of the Single Judge - Held, O. 7 r. 11 of CPC states that where the plaint does not disclose a cause of action, the relief claimed was undervalued and not corrected within the time allowed by the Court, insufficiently stamped and not rectified within the time fixed by the Court, barred by any law, failed to enclose the required copies and the respondent fail to comply with the provisions of O.7 r. 9 of CPC, the Court has no other option except to reject the same - Power under O. 7 r. 11 of the CPC can be exercised at any stage of the suit either before registering the plaint or after the issuance of summons to the defendants or at any time before the conclusion of the trial - Hence, in view of the shortfall in the plaint averments, statutory provisions, namely, O. 7 rr. 11, 14(1) and 14(2), Form Nos. 47 and 48 in Appendix A of the CPC which were statutory in nature, the Single Judge has correctly concluded that in the absence of any cause of action shown as against the defendant No. 1, the suit cannot be proceeded either for specific performance or for the recovery of money advanced which according to the respondent was given to the defendant No. 2 in the suit and rightly rejected the plaint as against the defendant No. 1 - Unfortunately, the DB failed to consider all those relevant aspects and erroneously reversed the decision of the Single Judge - Hence, impugned order of the DB set aside and order of the Single Judge to be restored - Appeal allowed.
