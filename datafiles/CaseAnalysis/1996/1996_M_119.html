United India Insurance Company Limited v MKJ Corporation (And Vice Versa)

Supreme Court of India

21 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3885; (1996) 6 SCC 428; 1996 (6) AD(SC) 631; AIR 1997 SC 408; 1996 (3) CCC 365; [1998] 92 Comp Cas 331; 1996 (3) CPJ 8; 1997 (5) CTJ 649; JT 1996 (7) SC 503; 1996(6) SCALE 258; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 20; 1996 (6) Supreme 620

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Insurance - Insurance Act 1938, Pre, s. 64(c) - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 126 - Insurance coverage for riots and strikes and malicious damages and spoilage - Exclusionary clause of liability in instructions of Tariff Advisory Committee not incorporated in Policy of Insurance Co. - Whether insured is bound by this clause? - Damage to leather in process was caused due to strike organised by workmen of insured - Whether Insurance Co. liable to pay insured amount for spoilage of leather caused due to strike organised by workmen ? - It had undertaken liability for riot or strike, damage due to riot or strike - Held, insured is not bound by this clause - Insurance Co. liable to pay - Insurance Co. also directed to pay interest @ 12% per annum - Appeals disposed of.
