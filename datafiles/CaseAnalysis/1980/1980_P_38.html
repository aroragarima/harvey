Public Prosecutor, Government of Andhra Pradesh v Boya Jaggapuram Venkateswarlu and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 389; (1980) Supp SCC 480; (1981) SCC (Cr) 444; AIR 1980 SC 1876; 1980 CRLJ 1301; 1980 CRLR 702

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Murder, Sustainability, Acquittal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 324 and 302 r/w s. 149 - Murder - Acquittal - Sustainability - Respondents (A1 to 8) allegedly murdered the deceased persons - Trial Court convicted A1 to 8 u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 of IPC on three counts of murder and sentenced each of them to death and also convicted u/s. 148 of IPC, and A4 was convicted u/s. 324 of IPC for causing hurt to PW1 - On appeal, HC by impugned judgment acquitted all accused person - Aggrieved appellant (State) filed instant appeal - Pending appeal A1 and A4 were murdered and appeal against them was abated.

Held, injuries on PW1, lent assurance to her presence at the time and place of the murders in question. PW1 was no doubt the wife of deceased and the mother of the other two deceased, and as such, was an interested witness. But there is no evidence to show that she had any previous ill will or hostility against the accused persons or a motive to falsely implicate the accused persons. Medical evidence supports her testimony in regard to the nature of the weapons with which the fatal injuries were inflicted on the deceased persons. The very circumstance that three persons had been surrounded and hacked to death in broad daylight, further indicates that the number of the assailants could be more than eight. The large number of injuries inflicted on the victims also point to the same conclusion. PW3 corroborates PW1 not only with regard to what the latter told her about the murders in the field and her sustaining the injury, but also with regard to the post-occurrence visit of the accused persons, with blood-stained clothes and with blood-stained weapons and enquiring of her in abusive language regarding the whereabouts of her husband. HC has not discussed the evidence of PW3 at length. It has simply discarded it as "artificial". Court is unable to agree with that sweeping remark. The Trial Court, which had advantage of observing the demeanour of PW3, has assessed the evidence of PW3. The guarded view of the evidence of PW3, taken by the Trial Court cannot be said to be unreasonable. Thus, the evidence of PW3 cannot be wholly brushed aside as 'artificial'. It had been rightly accepted and used by the Trial Court to seek further corroboration or assurance of the testimony of PW1. Reasons given by the HC for rejecting the evidence of PW1 and PW3, in particular, and the prosecution case in general, were not sound, and did not justify the reversal of the view of the evidence taken by the trial Court and consequent acquittal of the accused persons. Impugned acquittal of A3, 5,7 and 8 is set aside and are convicted u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 of IPC on three counts for the murders of deceased persons and sentence each of them on each of the counts of imprisonment for life. Appeal allowed.
