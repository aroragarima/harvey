Registrar General High Court of Judicature at Madras v R. Perachi and others

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 664; (2011) 12 SCC 137; AIR 2012 SC 232; 2012(1) ALL MR 453; JT 2011 (10) SC 505; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 289; 2011(10) SCALE 606; [2011] 12 S.C.R. 661; 2011 (5) SLR 760

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - Administrative - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 216 and 235 - Fundamental Rules of Tamil Nadu Govt., r.14(A)(d) - Judicial Service - Promotion - Transfer - Sustainability - Respondent no.1 was working as Sheristadar, and was expecting regular promotion to post of P.A. to District Judge - Respondent no.1 and others were transferred outside district by order issued by appellant on behalf of HC on administrative grounds - Respondent no. 1 filed petition before DB for his non-consideration for post and challenged transfer order - DB passed order setting aside transfer and direction to immediately restore respondent no.1 and 2 - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that decision of DB was erroneous on both grounds, viz that transfer was punitive and not passed by competent authority - Whether order passed by DB was sustainable - Held, in instant case respondent no.1 had been transferred to another district retaining him in same cadre with same pay as well as his seniority - Therefore, said transfer could not be said to be punitive merely because his promotional chances get affected due to transfer - Further action taken by appellant was fully justified and within authority of HC - No observations were made against respondent no.1 nor any stigma was attached - DB was not expected to take a different view from point of view of judicial discipline formulated in R.M. Gurjar Vs. High Court of Gujarat ,AIR 1992 SC 2000 1992 Indlaw SC 710 and Gauhati High Court Vs. Kuladhar Phukan , 2002 (4) SCC 524 2002 Indlaw SC 204 - Integrity of officers functioning in administration was of utmost importance to retain confidence of litigants in fairness of judicial system - Therefore, impugned judgment and order of DB were wholly unsustainable, which was incomplete disregard of law laid down by this Court - Hence, impugned judgment and order was set aside - Appeal allowed.
