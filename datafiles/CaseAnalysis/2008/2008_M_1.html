M/S. Karnataka State Forest Industries Corporation v (1) Indian Rocks; (2) State of Karnataka and

Supreme Court of India

24 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2114; (2009) 1 SCC 150; AIR 2009 SC 684; JT 2008 (12) SC 388; 2009 (2) KarLJ 193; 2008(14) SCALE 98; [2008] 15 S.C.R. 96

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Security Deposit, Karnataka Forest Rules, 1969, Telegraph Rules, 1951

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 12, 136, 14, 162, 226, 299 - Indian Telegraph Act, 1885 - Karnataka Forest Act, 1963, ss. 50, 62 - Karnataka Forest Rules, 1969 - Karnataka Minor Mineral Concession Rules, 1994 - Telegraph Rules, 1951 - Tender-cum-Allotment sale - Arbitrary - Challenged - Appellant/Govt. Undertaking engaged in sale of granite seized and confiscated granite blocks to persons who intend to purchase in Tender-cum-Allotment sale - Respondent participated in tender process and was successful in purchase of Granite - In terms of said tender, respondent was required to pay not only 10% of value of goods but also taxes and other statutory dues paid by appellant - Appellant had paid an amount of Rs.6,85,642/- being royalty amount, Forest Development Tax, Karnataka Sales Tax and Surcharge on Karnataka Sales Tax etc. - Respondent allegedly refused to pay amount to which it was said to be otherwise liable in terms of Cl. 8 of terms and conditions of tender - There appears to be a dispute as regards amount payable by respondent on aforementioned account insofar as whereas according to appellant amount of granite including tax and other statutory liabilities in respect of 10 blocks of granite which had been received by the respondent and transported by it was Rs. 3,25,193.90; according to respondent, total amount of goods received was Rs. 2,39,969.35 - Appellant contended that contract was not concluded within stipulated period of time as entire payment was not made by June, 1995 or before lifting of the stones whichever was earlier, as envisaged u/cl. 9(b) of terms and conditions of tender - Ultimately by its letter dated appellant informed respondent to immediately remit amount and lift granite blocks by failing which, it was threatened, the amount deposited shall be forfeited, stating: Respondent filed writ petition - It was not in dispute that those permits were not granted in favour of respondents - Respondents in their writ petition while contending that agency of appellant was terminated - Single Judge allowed writ petition directing appellant to refund a sum of Rs. 3,75,905.35 with interest from date of issuance of letter - Writ appeal preferred by appellant had been dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether State in exercise of its power u/art. 162 of Constitution could issue a binding direction so as to confer a legal right on third party having regard to cancellation of contract of agency by State in favour of appellant - Held, appellant was guilty of suppressio veri and suggestio falsi and its action in entire matter appears to be wholly unfair - It was in a dominant position in terms of provisions of the contract but then in a case of this nature when its authority to continue to deal with the granite blocks came to be questioned, it was obligatory on its part to clear its position in this behalf - Although ordinarily superior Court in exercise of its writ jurisdiction would not enforce terms of a contract qua contract, it was trite that when an action of State was arbitrary or discriminatory and, thus, violative of art. 14 of Constitution a writ petition would be maintainable - There could not be any doubt whatsoever that writ of mandamus could be issued only when there exists legal right in writ Petition and corresponding legal duty on part of State, but then if any action on part of State was wholly unfair or arbitrary, superior courts were not powerless - In view facts and circumstances of case in its entirety and to legal propositions as noticed, Court opined that these were not cases in which SC should exercise its discretionary jurisdiction u/art. 136 of Constitution - Appeals dismissed.
