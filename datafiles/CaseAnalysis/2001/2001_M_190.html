Makhan Lal Bangal v Manas Bhunia and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, A.S. Anand, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20362; (2001) 2 SCC 652; AIR 2001 SC 490; 2001 (1) ALD(SC) 134; JT 2001 (1) SC 252; 2001(1) SCALE 11; 2001 (1) SCJ 186; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 17; 2001 (1) SLT 353; 2001 (1) Supreme 28

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Framing Of Issues

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 98, 99, 100(i)(d)(ii), 116-A, 123(2), 123(4) and 123(7) - Appeal u/s. 116-A of Representation of People Act, 1951 (Representation of People Act) filed by a candidate who won at election but has lost in election petition - Elections for Legislative Assembly - Appellant was declared elected - Respondent 1 filed an election petition - High Court allowed election petition and set aside election of appellant declaring same to be void - Corrupt practices committed election agent within meaning of s. 100(i)(d)(ii) of Representation of People Act - Election petition alleged commission of corrupt practices as defined in sub-ss. (2),(4) and (7) of s. 123 of Representation of People Act, 1951 - Challenged - S. 98 of Representation of People Act, 1951 provides for an order at conclusion of trial of an election petition being made by HC whereby (a) election petition may be dismissed, (b) election of all or any of returned candidates may be declared to be void, (c) in addition to preceding relief, election petitioner or any other candidate may be declared to have been duly elected - Election of appellant in present case could not be declared void by making an order u/s. 98 on ground contained in s. 100(1)(b) of Representation of People Act, 1951 without prior compliance of s. 99 - Held, absence of notice u/s. 99 of Representation of People Act vitiates final order made u/s. 98 by HC declaring election to be void - Fatal defect in present case vitiates judgment under appeal and an appropriate course, in facts and circumstances of case, would be to set aside judgment under appeal and remand case to HC for deciding election petition afresh after compliance with provisions of s. 99 of Representation of People Act, 1951 - Appeal allowed.
