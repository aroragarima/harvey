Hec Voluntary Retired Employees Welfare Society and Another v Heavy Engineering Corporation Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 597; (2006) SCC (L&S) 602; (2006) 3 SCC 708; AIR 2006 SC 1420; 2006 (5) AWC 5146; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 211; JT 2006 (3) SC 102; 2006(2) SCALE 660; 2006 (3) SCJ 622; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 678; 2006 (2) SLR 723; 2006 (1) UPLBEC 976

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Companies Act, 1956 - Whether appellants could claim benefit of revised scale of pay after taking voluntary retirement? - Held, in V Kasturi's case a new rule was introduced - Question was that if a person was eligible for pension at time of his retirement and if he survives till time of subsequent amendment of relevant pension scheme, whether he would become entitled to enhanced pension? - It was held that employees could be divided in two categories, i.e, those who were eligible for pension at time of his retirement and those who were not - Whereas in case of first category benefit of amended provisions would be applicable, but in second it would not - Appeal dismissed.
