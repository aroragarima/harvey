Ajit Kumar Nag v G.M.(P.J.)Indian Oil Corporation Limited Haldia

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, S.N. Variava, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 585; (2005) 7 SCC 764; (2005) SCC (L&S) 1020; 2005 (107) FLR 407; JT 2005 (8) SC 425; 2005 (4) RLW 2326; 2005(7) SCALE 409; 2006 (1) SLR 808; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 314

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution - Administrative - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 12, 136, 14, 226, 227, 309, 311(2), 32 - IPC, 1860, ss. 147, 149, 323, 341, 506 - Natural justice - Petition challenging validity of Standing Order 20(vi) of Certified Standing Orders of Indian Oil Corporation Ltd being arbitrary and against principles of natural justice and appeal against dismissal of appellant - It empowers and authorizes General Manager of Corporation to dismiss an employee without following rule of audi alteram partem and without observing principles of natural justice - Held, acquittal of appellant by a Judicial Magistrate does not ipso facto absolve him from liability under disciplinary jurisdiction of Corporation - Sufficient safeguards have been provided in Standing Orders and action taken by General Manager under Standing o.20(vi) could be challenged in appeal under Standing Order 21 and in High Court u/arts. 226, 227 and in this Court u/arts. 32, 136 of Constitution, same cannot be held arbitrary, unreasonable or ultra vires art.14 of Constitution - Adequate material had not been placed on record which would go to show that order was malicious or mala fide - Action taken by General Manager of respondent Corporation dismissing appellant petitioner from service as legal and lawful - Petition and appeal dismissed.
