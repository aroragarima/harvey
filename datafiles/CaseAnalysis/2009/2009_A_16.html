High Court of Delhi and Others v A. K. Mahajan and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 659; (2009) 12 SCC 62; AIR 2009 SC 2497; 2009 (160) DLT 159; 2009 (3) ESC 480; JT 2009 (9) SC 306; 2009(8) SCALE 419; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 437; 2011 (7) SLR 207

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Feeder Post, Sources Of Recruitment, Delhi High Court Staff (Seniority) Rules, 1971, Retrospectivity

Summary: Service - Delhi High Court Establishment (Appointment and Conditions of Service) Rules, 1972, Sch. II(As stood amended vide Notification D/- 07-08-1995), rr. 3 and 7 - Post of Assistant Registrar(AR), a promotional post - Feeder posts therefor, posts of Superintendents, Court Masters and Private Secretaries - Criteria for promotion being seniority, from the joint seniority lists for such three feeder posts - 5 vacancies - Due to anomaly and imbalance felt by Superintendents and Court Masters in such joint seniority list, as to that if promotion were based on such criteria as per existing Rules, all the 5 posts were likely to be filled only by Private Secretaries, since they were much more in number and they were promoted in large numbers, representations were made - Amendment in question made with retrospective effect introducing system of creating three separate seniority lists and providing a principle of rotational promotion - Retrospective operation had the effect of covering 5 vacant posts of Assistant Registrar - Writ petitions filed by two of Private Secretary category candidates, one of whom was called for interview and was not called again for another interview, challenging retrospectivity of operation - (A) Whether because of the retrospective nature of the amendment, the amendment itself could be invalidated to the extent of retrospectivity? - 'Where the amendment, having retrospective operation, which has the effect of taking away a benefit already available to the employee, then such a provision is arbitrary, discriminatory and violative of the rights guaranteed u/arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution of India' laid down in 'Chairman, Railway Board Vs. C.R. Rangadhamaiah 1997(6) SCC 623' - (i) Whether amended Rules in question affected any benefit already given or what was the benefit which was available to the employee/petitioner? - Held, No - HC in its judgment observed that the benefit of consideration, which was available in terms of interview, prior to the retrospective amendment was not available to him after the amendment of the Rules; such observation found to be incorrect - There can be no benefit of consideration; to be considered is a right of employee but merely being considered, in itself, is not a benefit as it may or may not result in the selection or promotion of an employee and hence it is in the nature of a chance; a mere chance of promotion being affected by amendment is inconsequential; promotion is not a right of the employee, a mere chance of it if affected cannot and does not invalidate the action on the part of employer - Held further, that only benefit of consideration was available to the employee/petitioner, thus there was no benefit which was already available to him which was affected - In instant case petitioner who was to be interviewed was considered later on and was promoted also; on this Court reasoned that, right of consideration may accrue at a particular point of time or subsequently thereto; merely because at a particular point of time the employee is not considered, does not mean the total denial of the consideration of the employee - (B) Whether the amendments could be introduced w.e.f. 01-07-1993? - Held, Yes - Last appointment to the post of Assistant Registrar was made just one month before this date and if 5 posts were to be filled-in in the next interview as per existing rules i.e. unamended Rules, than the same imbalance and anomaly would have been perpetuated further; correcting such imbalance was the need of the day and indeed the imbalance would have affected the morale of the other two categories viz. Superintendents and Court Masters - Impugned judgement set aside - Appeals disposed of.
