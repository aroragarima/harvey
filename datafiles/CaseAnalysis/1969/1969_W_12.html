Works Manager, Central Railway Workshop,Jhansi v Vishwanath And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

09 October 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, J.M. Shelat, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 201; (1969) 3 SCC 95; AIR 1970 SC 488; 1970 (20) FLR 316; 1970 (1) LLJ 351; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 726; 1970 UJ 19

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: CPC, 1908, O. 22, rr. 2, 4 and 115 - Constitution, art. 136 - Factories Act, 1948, ss. 2(1), Pre. - Payment ofwages Act - (A) Civil Procedue - Joint petition under Payment of Wages Act by respondents - Order in their favour by one judgment - All respondents made parties in appeal to Supreme Court - One of them died but his LRs not brought on record - If abate appeal - (B) Constitution - Appeal by special leave - Finding of fact - Nature of work done by persons in railway workshop - Finding on - Nature and reassessment of , before Supreme Court on special leave to appeal - (C) Factories Act, 1948 - (1) Persons doing clerical duties and otherwise, if falls within definition of 'worker' u/s.2(1) - (2) Interpretation of Statutes - Preamble of the Act - Construction of - (A) Appeal held did not abate - (B) Finding on is finding of fact and binding on High Court in revision against the decision of the Court of Appeal u/s.17, Payment of Wages Act and also not open to reassessment before SC on special leave to appeal - (C) They are workers - Factories Act is a social enactment to achieve social reform and must receive liable construction to achieve legislative purpose without doing violence to language - Appeal Dismissed.
