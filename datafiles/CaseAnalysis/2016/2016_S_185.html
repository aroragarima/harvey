State Trading Corporation India Limited v New Delhi Municipal Council

Supreme Court of India

03 February 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 106; AIR 2016 SC 1269; 2016 (2) AWC 1495; JT 2016 (2) SC 600; 2016(2) SCALE 267; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 492; 2016 (1) WLN(SC) 134

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Annual Value, Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, Punjab Municipal Act, 1911, New Delhi Municipal Council Act, 1994, Assessment of property tax, Fixation

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - New Delhi Municipal Council Act, 1994, s. 63 - Assessment of property tax - Fixation - Appellant filed instant appeals against the order of HC, where in the HC taken the view that since there is already a payment of rent by the sub-lessee, there need not be any other exercise for assessment of the reasonable rent - The High Court has based its decision under bye-law 12 of the New Delhi Municipal Committee Byelaws Relating to the Assessment and Collection of House Tax.

Held, in regard to the agreement with the Government of India for payment of 25% of the gross rent fetched from the sub-lessee, SC is inclined to hold that the 25% that is being paid to the Government of India by the lessee out of the rent collected from the sub-lessee is inflated to include the extra 25% since the rent actually available to the lessee is only 75% of the amount actually paid by the sub-lessee to the lessee. Therefore, going by the principle settled by SC in the case of Dewan Daulat Rai Kapoor v New Delhi Municipal Committee and Another 1979 Indlaw SC 154, the rateable value u/s. 63 of the Act, in the case of the appellants coming under the second category has to be fixed on the basis of 75% of the amount received from the sub-lessee by the appellants. On that basis, the rateable value of the premises both tenanted and self-occupied will be fixed by the assessing officer. This is however, subject to the production of proof of payment/adjustment/ appropriation of the 25% by the lessee with the Government of India. As for the first category, where the building is self-occupied and where there is no sub-lease, the annual rent will have to be fixed as held by this Court in the case of Dewan Daulat Rai Kapoor v New Delhi Municipal Committee and Another 1979 Indlaw SC 154, and in the case of India Automobiles (1960) Limited v Calcutta Municipal Corporation 2002 Indlaw SC 1945 on the basis what the landlord might reasonably expect to get from a hypothetical tenant. Such fixation has to be made only as per the Act. It is for the assessing officer to make the fixation in accordance with law. Hence, impugned judgments are set aside. Appeals allowed.
