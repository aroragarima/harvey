Dharma Nand and another v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1883; (2004) 10 SCC 609; 2006 (1) SLR 109

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Termination

Summary: Defence& Security Forces - Termination - Legality - Petitioner no.1 and petitioner no.2 was appointed as salesman at station Canteen Rifles Regimental Centre - Petitioners were promoted as storekeepers In-charge and their remuneration was enhanced - Petitioners were terminated from service and was informed that they completed 5 years tenure, and their service was no longer required - Petitioners claimed that they should have been treated as Central Govt. employees and their termination was illegal - Whether employees working in Canteen Stores Department under Defence Ministry could be treated as Govt. servants or not - Held, petitioners were working as canteen employees which was under Defence Ministry and they were entitled to be treated Govt. servants - Respondents submitted that petitioners with others were appointed as canteen employees on temporary basis and appointment itself was given for fixed term and on completion of term, their services were terminated - Rules framed for canteen employees was that canteen employees should have been treated as Gov.t servants that by itself was sufficient to hold that Rules framed for such temporary appointment were not to be applicable to these employees - If these petitioners should have been treated as Govt. servants, services could not have been terminated on ground that their services were no longer required - Only ground stated for terminating service was that it was only for 5 years tenure and their services were no longer required - SC held that termination was illegal and petitioners was entitled to be reinstated in service - Petitioners was also entitled to get consequential benefits and to get consolidated amount from date of termination - Petition allowed.
