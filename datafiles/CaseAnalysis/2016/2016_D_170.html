Golla Rajanna and others v Divisional Manager and others

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 843; (2017) 1 SCC 45; 2016 (4) ACC 795; 2017 ACJ 1; AIR 2016 SC 5382; 2017 (2) ALD(SC) 14; 2017 (1) Bom.C.R. 317; 2017 (152) FLR 141; JT 2016 (11) SC 227; 2017 (1) LLJ 92; 2017 (1) OLR 320; 2017 (1) RCR(Civil) 311; 2016(12) SCALE 266; [2016] 9 S.C.R. 469; 2016 (4) WLN(SC) 221

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Disablement, Employee's [Workmen's] Compensation Act, 1923

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Employees (Workmen's) Compensation Act, 1923, ss. 30, 30(1), 4(1)9c )(ii) - Disability - Reduction in Compensation Challenged - Appellants/ workmen suffered disability during the course of employment with respondent/ employer - Workmen Compensation Commissioner awarded compensation to employers based on wages - HC reduced compensation awarded in appeal by insurance company - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, under the scheme of the Act, the Workmen's Compensation Commissioner is the last authority on facts. The Parliament has thought it fit to restrict the scope of the appeal only to substantial questions of law, being a welfare legislation. Further, as per s. 4(1)(c)(ii) of the Act, the percentage of permanent disability needs to be assessed only by a qualified medical practitioner. The Workmen's Compensation Commissioner has passed the order based on the certificate of disability issued by the doctor and which has been duly proved before the Workmen's Compensation Commissioner. HC has missed this crucial question of limited jurisdiction and has ventured to re-appreciate the evidence and recorded its own findings on percentage of disability for which also there is no basis. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is set aside and the order that of Commissioner is restored. Appeals allowed.
