Ape Belliss India Limited v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 333; (2001) 9 SCC 96; AIR 2001 SC 2491; 2001 (132) E.L.T. 8; JT 2001 (6) SC 117; 2001(4) SCALE 642; 2001 (5) SLT 519; 2001 (5) Supreme 463

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Entry referable to sections

Summary: Customs - Customs Tariff Act, 1975 - Classification of goods - Entry referable to sections - Legality - Appellant imported stainless steel rolled special blades sections and filed a Bill of Entry claiming classification of the said imported goods under Tariff Heading 73.15(1) - Assistant Collector classified said goods and passed order - On appeal before Collector, Collector passed impugned order - Appellant filed appeal before Tribunal - Tribunal agreed with Collector and held that he was justified in classifying goods under Heading 73.15(2) since the said goods specifically fell within Entry referable to sections of stainless steel under Tariff Heading 73.15(2) - Hence instant appeals.

Held, this is clear from use of word and if the intention of the trade notice was to treat the two types of alloy steels as stainless steel then it would have been made clear by using the word or instead of the word and in the said view of the matter, Court opined that if an alloy steel is known as stainless steel in the trade and also has more than 11% chromium in it then alone the same could be considered as stainless steel and not otherwise. In instant case, assuming that the alloy steel imported by appellant is known in trade as stainless steel still since on an analysis it is found that the chromium content is less than 11%, the same could not have been classified as stainless steel for purpose of the Act because the second condition as to the content of chromium is not satisfied. In our considered opinion, goods imported by appellant will have to be classified under Tariff Heading 73.15(1) since it is not specified anywhere else. Impugned orders are set aside. Appeal allowed.
