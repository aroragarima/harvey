Commissioner of Income Tax. Bombay v Indian Engineering and Commercial Corporation Pvt

Supreme Court of India

13 April 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 695; (1993) 3 SCC 246; AIR 1993 SC 1540; 1993 (112) CTR 56; [1993] 201 ITR 723; JT 1993 (2) SC 683; 1993(2) SCALE 496; [1993] 3 S.C.R. 86; [1993] 68 TAXMAN 39; 1993 (2) TLR 503

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 40A(5) and 40(a)(v) - Whether in addition to salary, amount paid by assessee as commission on sales in cash to its Directors would be deductible - Held, such a commission being paid does not fall under ' Where the assessee incurs any expenditure which results directly or indirectly ' - It cannot also fall under two sub clauses of cls. 3 since they speak of an expenditure or allowance in respect of any assets of the assessee used by the employee - Appeal dismissed.
