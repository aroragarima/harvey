Union of India and others v Shah Goverdhan L. Kabra Teachers College

Supreme Court of India

23 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1165; (2002) 8 SCC 228; AIR 2002 SC 3675; 2003 (1) JhrCR(SC) 34; JT 2002 (8) SC 269; 2002(7) SCALE 435; 2002 (3) SCJ 674; 2002 (6) SLT 152; [2002] Supp3 S.C.R. 220; 2002 (7) Supreme 318; 2003 (1) UPLBEC 421

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Education - National Council for Teachers Education Act, 1993, s.17(4) - Constitutional validity of - Whether the impugned legislation can be held to be a law dealing with coordinated development of education system within Entry 66 of the List I of the Seventh Schedule or it is a law dealing with the service conditions of an employee under the StateGovt.- Held, entries in the different lists should be read together without giving a narrow meaning to any of them - Interpretation of statute - Conclusion of the HC that s.17(4) is ultra-vires being beyond the competence of the Union legislature cannot be sustained - Doctrine of 'pith and substance'- Further held, in case of conflict between entries in List I and List II, the same has to be decided by application of the principle of 'pith and substance'- The doctrine of 'pith and substance' has to be applied not only in cases of conflict between the powers of two legislatures but in any case where the question arises whether a legislation is covered by particular legislative power in exercise of which it is purported to be made - Appeals allowed.
