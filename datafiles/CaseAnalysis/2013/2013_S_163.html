Babubhai Bhimabhai Bokhiria And Anr. v State Of Gujarat And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

30 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 324; (2013) 9 SCC 500; AIR 2013 SC 3648; 2013 CRLJ 1547; 2013 (2) JCC 926; JT 2013 (8) SC 79; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 134; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 542; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 665

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Indian penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 201, 120-B, 465, 468 and 471 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 319 - Arms Act, 1959, s.25 - Constitution of India, 1950, art.21 - Murder - Prosecution - Sustainability - Petitioner allegedly committed offence of murder, disappearance of evidence of offence, criminal conspiracy, forgery, illegal possession of arms and acting in contravention of rule or directions punishable u/ss. 302, 201 r/w. ss.34, 120-B, 465, 468 and 471 of IPC, s. 25 of 1959 Act and s. 135 of 1951 Act - Petitioner was summoned by Additional District Judge in exercise of his power u/s. 319 of the CrPC and was ordered to be made co- accused in the case - Petition was filed before HC against order of Additional District Judge which was also dismissed - Criminal Miscellaneous Petition was at that stage filed by petitioner in which he prayed for his addition as a party to proceedings and for vacation of order dt. 17-12-2008 by which further steps in the case were stayed - In the alternative petitioner prayed for grant of bail to him - DB of SC Three judge bench of SC allowed the prayer for impleadment but directed that the prayer for grant of bail should be considered by the regular Bench - That was precisely how Criminal Miscellaneous seeking vacation of the stay order and/or grant of bail and Criminal Miscellaneous petition filed by the petitioner in the special leave petition came up for hearing - Hence instant petition - Petitioner contended that he had been in custody for over six years - Even so there were no prospects of the Constitution Bench taking up the reference in the near future which implied that unless this Court either vacates the said order passed on 17-12-2008 or grants bail to the applicant, there was no chance of the applicant or other persons who were similarly languishing in jail for years seeing the end of their trial and resultant agony - It was also contended that although the special leave petition was filed on behalf of the petitioner in the main petition only and although the prayer for stay made in Criminal Miscellaneous petition, he had simply asked for stay of the judgment and final order passed by the HC - Order passed by SC on 17-12-2008 was, however, understood as though the trial itself was stayed in toto - It was not only depriving the applicant of his fundamental right of a speedy trial but also depriving him of his personal liberty with hardly any chances of an early conclusion of the trial in the near future - Petitioner contended that even if the order passed by Trial Court and affirmed by the HC was eventually upheld and the addition of the petitioner in the special leave petition was declared to be justified, the said petitioner could be tried separately as there was no legal bar to such a trial - Held, in the light of the settled legal position no longer possible to question the legitimacy of the right to speedy trial as a part of the right to life u/art. 21 of the Constitution - Essence of Art. 21 of the Constitution lied not only in ensuring that no citizen was deprived of his life or personal liberty except according to procedure established by law, but also that such procedure ensures both fairness and an expeditious conclusion of the trial - It was in that backdrop not possible to countenance a situation where addition of petitioner as an accused to the case at hand would lead to an indefinite suspension of trial and eventual recall of 134 witnesses already examined against the applicant who has been was in jail for over six years - There was, therefore, no reason for a blanket stay against the progress of the trial before Trial Court and HC qua other accused persons - In the totality of the above circumstances, therefore, SC was inclined to modify order dt. 17-12-2008 by which further proceedings before Trial Court were brought to a halt - SC made it clear that while the stay of the trial against petitioner should continue qua the said petitioner, Trial Court should be free to proceed with the trial qua the other accused persons - Javed Ahmed Abdul Hamid Pawala v. State of Maharashtra, 1984 Indlaw SC 348 and Triveni Ben v. State of Gujarat, 1989 Indlaw SC 43, relied on - Petition partly allowed.
