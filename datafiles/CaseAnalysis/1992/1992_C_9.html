Post And Telegraph Board And Ors. v C.S.N. Murthy

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, Veeraswami Ramaswami, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 362; (1992) 2 SCC 317; (1993) SCC (L&S) 710; AIR 1992 SC 1368; 1992 (21) ATC 664; 1992 (2) AWC 1081; 1992 (1) CLR 815; 1992 (64) FLR 956; JT 1992 (2) SC 357; 1992 LabIC 1410; 1993 (2) LLJ 866; 1992 (1) LLN 948; 1992(1) SCALE 727; [1992] 2 S.C.R. 338; 1992 (3) SLJ 16; 1992 (2) SLR 352; 1992 (1) UJ 549; 1992 (2) UPLBEC 829

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Fundamental Rule

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14 - Fundamental Rules, r. 56(j) - Exercise of power as to compulsory retirement - Nature of delinquency and whether it is of such degree as to require compulsory retirement? - Exercise of power bona fide and on material on record - Interference by Courts - Held, matters assigned primarily for Govt. to decide upon - Courts should not interfere - Appeal allowed.
