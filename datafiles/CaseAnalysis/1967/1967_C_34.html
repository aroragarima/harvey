Commissioner of Income Tax West Bengal, Calcutta v Smt. Anusuya Devi

Supreme Court of India

28 November 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 166; AIR 1968 SC 779; [1968] 68 ITR 750; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 466

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Gross negligence - Collusion - Assessment proceedings - High denomination notes - Declaration - Reassessment - A died his estate was assessed to tax on a total income from salary and other sources - Widow of A encashed high denomination notes and made a declaration as required by Ordinance - Proceeding taken for reassessment of income of A and explanation given that, A during the last 30. years of his life, giving gifts to his wife and also setting apart money exclusively for his wife and children and that fund so accumulated - Income-tax Officer disbelieved the explanation furnished - Appealed to Commissioner , who agreed - On further appeal, Tribunal rejected the application - HC answered in affirmative - Hence instant appeal -Whether persons looking after safety of important record of assessment proceedings can escape charge of negligence, due to collusion - Held, no - It was a startling revelation that entire record of an assessee's case before Income-tax Officer and Appellate Commissioner was found missing, and was not been traced - Even if collusion be ruled out, persons concerned in looking after safety of important record of proceedings of assessment cannot escape a charge of gross negligence - Without an appeal by assessee, to set aside that order of HC and to direct the Tribunal , order of HC set aside - If Tribunal refuses to refer a case u/s.66(1) which arises out of its order, proper course is for aggrieved party to move HC to require the Tribunal u/s.66(2) to refer the same - Appeal allowed.
