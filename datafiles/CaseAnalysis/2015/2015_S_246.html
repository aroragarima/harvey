Indra Dalal v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

29 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 420; 2015 CRLJ 3174; 2015 (3) JCC 1893; JT 2015 (5) SC 405; 2015 (2) MLJ(Crl) 699; 2015(7) SCALE 17; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 1083

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Clerk, Inadmissible

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120B - Murder - Alleged Conspiracy - Conviction - Deceased was shot dead by accused boy who had come to his house while he was residing with his wife and brother - Complainant wife alleged that conspiracy was planned against her husband and death of deceased was as result of result of revenge - Trial Court acquitted one accused and convicted other accused u/ss. 120 B read with s. 302 of IPC - Order passed by Trial Court is affirmed by HC - Whether prosecution has been able to prove involvement of appellants with aid of s. 120-B of IPC.

Held, prosecution had produced one witness, PW7 who was allegedly witness of conspiracy, however, during trial, he did not support prosecution version and was declared hostile. Hence, there is no witness to this conspiracy. SC held that conviction is recorded by Trial court and upheld by HC against these appellants primarily on basis of their confessional statements and recovery of scooter from house of conspirer. Further, confessions were made in police custody after arrest of appellant accused. SC held that approach of HC relying upon confessional statements, otherwise inadmissible, with aid of 'other connected evidence' is contrary to law. Further, information provided by all accused appellants in form of confessional statements, has not led to any discovery. Further, prosecution failed to file necessary evidence to prove how ownership of vehicle changed hands and came to be recorded in name of accused appellant. Taking into consideration circumstantial evidence, SC held that prosecution has failed to prove, beyond reasonable doubt, charge of conspiracy against appellants with aid of s. 120-B of IPC. Impugned order passed by lower Court against appellants is set aside. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Charges with respect to conspiracy has to be established through circumstantial evidence or other evidence produced.
