Union of India v Gurbachan Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 472; (1997) 5 SCC 59; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1118; AIR 1997 SC 2641; 1997 (76) FLR 489; JT 1997 (5) SC 176; 1997 (2) LLJ 827; 1997 (2) LLN 457; 1997(4) SCALE 1; 1997(5) SCALE 1; 1997 (2) SCJ 109; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 890; 1998 (1) SLJ 15; 1997 (4) SLR 310; 1997 (5) Supreme 484

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Industrial Disputes Act, s. 33 C(ii) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133 - Direction issued to Railway Board to retained employee in service beyond retirement age by giving re-employment - Objection aggrieved employer u/s. 33 C - Labour Court can interpret award and work out wages payable thereunder but cannot adjudicate on fresh claims - Therefore, decision of Labour Court granting him relief was inconsistent with law but cannot be interfered keeping in view facts and circumstances of case - Appeal dismissed.
