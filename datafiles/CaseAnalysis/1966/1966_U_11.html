Bungo Steel Furniture Private Limited v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

30 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 154; AIR 1967 SC 378; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 633

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 30 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 73 - Sale of Goods Act, 1930, ss. 55 and 56 - Setting aside of award by division bench of High Court on two contracts - Whether setting aside award of arbitrator on ground that he had failed to apply his mind and there was a mistake of law on face of award in estimation of damages for wrongful termination of contract is valid? - When setting aside an award court cannot consider whether view of arbitrator on evidence is justified or not - Considering principles which apply to exercise of power of a court to set aside an award of an arbitrator order of HC was not justified - An arbitrator in deciding a dispute before him does not record his reasons and does not indicate principles of law on which he has proceeded then award is not vitiated - This award is not to be interpreted as proceeding on any basis that value of steel used up in making component parts was held by him on some principle to be compensation payable by Govt. - Arbitrator's conclusion that a certain amount should be paid by Govt. as compensation for wrongful termination of contract and in his discretion he laid down that that amount is equal to value of steel as it existed after it had been converted into component parts - Held, an award where compensation granted to the extent of arbitrator's discretion without indicating his reasons, such a decision cannot be held to be erroneous on face of record - Appeals allowed.
