Jagrup Singh v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 73; (1981) 3 SCC 616; (1981) SCC (Cr) 768; AIR 1981 SC 1552; 1981 BBCJ 213; 1981 CRLJ 1136; 1981 CrLR(SC) 462; 1981(3) SCALE 1807; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 839; 1981 TAXLR 1136; 1981 UJ 820

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 304 Part II and 300 clause thirdly - Scope of clause thirdly of s. 300 - Under clause thirdly of s. 300 culpable homicide is murder if both following conditions are satisfied : (a) that act which causes death is done with intention of causing a bodily injury and (b) that injury intended to be inflicted is sufficient in ordinary course of nature to cause death - It must be proved that there was an intention to inflict that particular bodily injury which, in ordinary course of nature, was sufficient to cause death viz. that injury found to be present was injury that was intended to be inflicted - Virsa Singh case followed - Held, if a man is hit with blunt side on head with sufficient force, it is bound to cause, as here, death - There can be no doubt that it was used with certain amount of force because there was cerebral compression - But that by itself is not sufficient to raise an inference that appellant intended to cause such bodily injury as was sufficient to cause death - He could only be attributed with knowledge that it was likely to cause an injury which was likely to cause death - Matter, therefore, does not fall within clause thirdly of s. 300 - Conviction of appellant u/s. 302 is altered to one u/s 304 Part II - Order accordingly.
