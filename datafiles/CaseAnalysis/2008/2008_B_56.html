Bachhaj Nahar v Nilima Mandal and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 September 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2111; (2008) 17 SCC 491; AIR 2009 SC 1103; 2009 (1) AWC 706; JT 2008 (13) SC 255; 2009 (4) MLJ(SC) 900; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 855; 2008(15) SCALE 158; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 621

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Form Of Pleadings, Review Application, Subject- Matter

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 Absence of Pleading - Easement Right - Evidence- Respondents nos. 1 and 2-Plaintiffs filed suit for declaration, possession and injunction against appellant-Defendant no.1 in respect to suit property - Plaintiffs claimed that suit property was part of 'A' schedule property purchased by them under sale deed - Defendant no.1 resisted suit contending that he had purchased property from second defendant under sale deed and suit property actually formed part of his property -Trial Court decreed suit in part and held that suit property was part of plaintiffs' property and that defendant no. 1 had encroached over part of it to an extent of 15 sq. ft - Trial Court held that as defendant no. 1 had already put up his construction over encroached portion and was using it instead of directing him to deliver back possession thereof, he should pay Rs.100/- as price of encroached portion to the plaintiffs - Feeling aggrieved, defendant no.1 filed appeal - Appellate Court allowed appeal filed by defendant no.1 - As consequence suit of plaintiffs was dismissed - Aggrieved plaintiffs filed second appeal before HC - HC by judgment allowed second appeal - HC held that plaintiffs had failed to make out title to suit property - HC was of view that case based on easementary right could be considered even in absence of any pleading or issue relating to an easementary right, as evidence available was sufficient to make out easementary right over suit property - Defendant no.1 sought review of said judgment - Review petition was dismissed by HC - Hence, instant appeals - Whether HC was justified considering easementary right even in absence of any pleading or issue relating to easementary right, as evidence available was sufficient to make out easementary right over suit property - Held, - SC had repeatedly held that pleadings were meant to give to each side intimation of case of other so that it might be met, to enable Courts to determine what was really at issue between parties, and to prevent any deviation from course which litigation on particular causes must take - When there was no prayer for particular relief and no pleadings to support such relief, and when defendant had no opportunity to resist or oppose such a relief, if Court considers and grants such relief, it will lead to miscarriage of justice - HC ignored principles relating to object and necessity of pleadings - Even though right of easement was not pleaded or claimed by plaintiffs, and even though parties were at issue only in regard to title and possession, it made out for first time in second appeal, a case of easement and granted relief based on an easementary right - In absence of claim by plaintiffs based on easementary right, defendant no. 1 did not have an opportunity to demonstrate that plaintiffs had no easementary right - In absence of pleadings and an opportunity to defendant no.1 to deny such claim, HC could not have converted suit for title into suit for enforcement of easementary right - Appellate Court had recorded finding of fact that plaintiffs had not made out title - HC in second appeal did not disturb said finding - As no question of law arose for consideration, HC ought to have dismissed second appeal - Even if HC felt that case for easement was made out at, best liberty could have been reserved to plaintiffs to file separate suit for easement - But HC could not, in second appeal, while rejecting plea of the plaintiffs that they were owners of suit property, grant relief of injunction in regard to an easementary right by assuming that they had an easementary right to use schedule property as a passage - Appeals allowed.
