Supreme Court of India

25 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 294; (2001) 6 SCC 347; AIR 2001 SC 2516; 2001 (2) AllCJ 1573; 2001 (5) ALT(SC) 9; 2001 (2) ARBLR 650; 2001 (3) AWC 2385; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 39; JT 2001 (6) SC 1; 2001 (3) MLJ(SC) 145; 2001 (2) RAJ 540; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 288; 2001(4) SCALE 534; 2001 (4) SCJ 313; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 1190; 2001 (5) SLT 437; 2001 (5) Supreme 326

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 30 and 33 - Arbitration award - Interference of Court u/ss. 30 and 33 - Whether High Court justified in setting aside award passed by Arbitrator? - Reappraisal of evidence by Court is not permissible, unless there exists a total perversity in award or judgment is based on a wrong proposition of law - Award of Arbitrator is ordinarily final and conclusive unless a contra intention is disclosed in agreement itself - No evidence on record that umpire has overstepped his jurisdiction or has travelled beyond agreement - Held, Court ought not to have entertained objection and set aside award as passed by chosen forum of parties - HC thus was in manifest error in entertaining appeal and setting aside award - Appeal allowed.
