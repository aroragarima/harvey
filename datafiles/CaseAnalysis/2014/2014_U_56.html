Manzoor Ali Khan v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

06 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 504; (2015) 2 SCC 33; AIR 2014 SC 3194; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 3784; 2014 (5) Bom.C.R. 543; 2014 CRLJ 4257; JT 2014 (9) SC 9; 2015 (4) MahLJ 86; 2014 (6) MLJ 241; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 89; 2014(9) SCALE 202; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 569

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s.19 - Enforcement of fundamental rights - Petition, by way of public interest litigation, sought direction to declare s.19 of the Act unconstitutional and to direct prosecution of all cases registered and investigated under the provisions of Act against the politicians, M.L.As, M.Ps and Govt. officials, without sanction as required u/s.19 of Act - Whether s.19 of the Act was unconstitutional and whether any further direction was called for in public interest and for enforcement or fundamental rights -

Held, issue raised in the petition was no longer res integra - Requirement of sanction had salutary object of protecting an innocent public servant against unwarranted and mala fide prosecution - Undoubtedly, there could be no tolerance to corruption which undermined core constitutional values of justice, equality, liberty and fraternity - At the same time, need to prosecute and punish the corrupt was no ground to deny protection to the honest - Mere possibility of abuse could not be a ground to declare a provision, otherwise valid, to be unconstitutional - Exercise of power had to be regulated to effectuate the purpose of law - Matter had already been dealt with in various decisions of SC - Vineet Narain & Ors. vs. Union of India & Anr. 1996 Indlaw SC 3437, relied on - In a recent judgment of SC, the question for consideration was whether a private citizen had locus to prosecute a public servant and to obtain sanction and how an application for sanction was to be dealt with - Dr. Subramanian Swamy v Dr. Manmohan Singh And Another 2012 Indlaw SC 509, relied on - It was held that any application for sanction sought even by a private citizen should be looked into expeditiously and decided as per the observations of SC in one case - Vineet Narain & Ors. vs. Union of India & Anr. 1996 Indlaw SC 3437, relied on and guidelines framed by the CVC which were circulated vide Office Order - Thus while it was not possible to hold that the requirement of sanction was unconstitutional, the competent authority had to take a decision on the issue of sanction expeditiously as already observed - Fine balance had to be maintained between need to protect a public servant against mala fide prosecution on the one hand and the object of upholding the probity in public life in prosecuting the public servant against whom prima facie material in support of allegation of corruption existed, on the other hand - Petition disposed of.
