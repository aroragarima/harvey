Rajasthan State Road Transport Corporation v Indag Rubber Limited

Supreme Court of India

05 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1367; (2006) 7 SCC 700; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1856; 2006 (4) AWC 3590; JT 2006 (8) SC 461; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 47; 2006(9) SCALE 18; [2006] Supp5 S.C.R. 743

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Arbitral award - Challenged - Agreement was executed between a Company and Corporation for purchase of certain materials - As per arbitration clause any dispute between parties would be referred to the Chairman of the Corporation - Dispute arose - Arbitration clause invoked - Arbitrator decided all issues in favour of Corporation - District Judge confirmed award passed by Arbitrator and issued a decree in terms of award in favour of Corporation - Appeal filed before HC - Single Judge set aside judgment and decree passed by District Judge and remanded the matter to the Corporation for fresh adjudication of dispute - Hence, present Special Leave Petition - Held, all materials have been considered by arbitrator and a finding of fact was recorded by the arbitrator and same was made rule of court by District Judge - Single Judge erroneously took upon himself to sit as a court of appeal and disturbed this finding of fact - View taken by Single Judge of High Court cannot be sustained - Decree passed by District Judge making award rule of Court affirmed - Appeal allowed.
