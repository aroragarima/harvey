Ramji Gupta and another v Gopi Krishan Agrawal (Dead) and Lrs.

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 233; (2013) 9 SCC 438; AIR 2013 SC 3099; 2013 (4) ALD(SC) 178; 2013 (99) ALR 66; 2013 (5) ALT(SC) 12; 2013 (3) AWC 2782; 2013 (3) CLT(SC) 88; 2013 (3) CTC 98; JT 2013 (10) SC 443; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 898; 2013(5) SCALE 665; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 398

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Rent recovery proceedings - Issue of title - Adjudication by Small Causes Court - Scope - Respondent No.1 filed suit against appellants alleging that latter had defaulted in making payment of rent - Appellants claimed that they were the owners of suit premises - Trial Court (Small Causes Court) decreed suit holding that appellants had been in default of payment of rent - Appellants' appeal before First Appellate Court was dismissed - HC affirmed First Appellate Court's order - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that Trial Court had no jurisdiction to adjudicate title of suit as the issue of title had already been adjudicated in an earlier suit - Whether instant suit was barred by res judicata - Held, a matter which was collaterally or incidentally in issue for the purpose of deciding a matter which was directly in issue in the case, should not be made the basis for a plea of res judicata - A question regarding title in a small cause suit, should be regarded as incidental only to substantial issue in suit, and therefore, when a finding as regards title to immovable property was rendered by a Small Causes Court, res judicata should not be pleaded as a bar in subsequent regular suit, for the determination or enforcement of any right or interest in the immovable property - In the instant case therefore, Trial Court had rightly refused to go into such issue, and neither could any fault be found with the findings recorded by the courts below in this regard - Further, appellants were tenants of the original respondents, the question of title could not be adjudicated at the behest of the appellants under any circumstance - No interference called for - Appeal dismissed.
