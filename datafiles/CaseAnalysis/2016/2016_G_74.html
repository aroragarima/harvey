Gulzari Lal v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 71; (2016) 4 SCC 583; AIR 2016 SC 795; 2016 ALL MR (Cri) 880; 2016 CRLJ 1349; 2016 (1) JCC 731; JT 2016 (2) SC 1; 2016 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 438; 2016 (2) RLW 1364; 2016(2) SCALE 118

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Voluntary, Head Constable, Concurrent

Summary: Criminal - India Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 323, 34 - Dying declaration without certificate of fitness - Conviction - Challenged - FIR was registered against appellant and co-accused for committing murder of deceased and voluntarily causing injuries to another - Upon analyzing evidence, Trial Court convicted appellant and co-accused-O u/ss. 302, 323 read with s. 34 of IPC and passed sentence and acquitted co-accused-K - Aggrieved, appeal before HC was preferred but during pendency of appeal, co-accused-O died - HC, after re-appreciation of evidence on record, inter-alia observed that case was not only based on dying declaration of deceased but also on un-shattered testimony of injured eye witness which was deemed to be base pillar to prove charges against accused/appellant and dismissed appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether conviction of appellant could be upheld.

Held, in view of law laid down by SC in Paras Yadav & Others v. State of Bihar, 1999 Indlaw SC 437, dying declaration of deceased could be relied as at the time of recording statement by PW7-Head Constable, deceased was found to be mentally fit to give statement regarding occurrence. Further, conviction by HC was based not only on statements made by deceased but also on un-shattered testimony of PW1-eye-witness and statement of PW11- independent witness. Furthermore, evidence of PW7-Head Constable was shown to be trustworthy and has been accepted by Trial Court and HC and view taken by HC does not suffer from any infirmity and the same is in order. Hence, impugned judgment and order of HC could not be interfered with. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - A valid dying declaration could be made without obtaining a certificate of fitness of declarant by medical officer.
