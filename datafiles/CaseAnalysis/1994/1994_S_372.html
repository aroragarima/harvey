Tata Engineer and Locomotive Company Limited v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

07 October 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 655; (1994) 6 SCC 479; 1994 (74) E.L.T. 193; JT 1994 (6) SC 491; 1994(4) SCALE 508; 1995 (96) STC 211; [1994] Supp4 S.C.R. 410

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Concessional Rate Of Tax, Processing

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Sales Tax - Bihar Finance Act, 1981, s. 13(1)(b) - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Whether any raw material, including those which were otherwise finished products, used by appellant in manufacture or processing of commercial vehicles, spare parts and other engineering products were 'Industrial raw material (inputs)' within the meaning of the expression used in the notification issued by the State Govt. on 12-4-1982 in exercise of its power u/s. 13(1 )(b)? - Any goods which could be included in the expression 'raw material' would be entitled to concessional rate of tax provided it was useable in manufacture or processing of goods - Held, appellant shall be entitled to concessional rate of tax at 1% for such items as tyres, tubes and batteries which were used in the manufacture of the vehicle etc. - Appeal allowed.
