Tiwari Kanhaiyalal v Commissioner of Income Tax, Delhi

Supreme Court of India

13 March 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, A. Alagiriswami

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 317; (1975) 4 SCC 101; (1975) SCC (Cr) 312; AIR 1975 SC 902; 1975 CRLJ 781; 1975 CrLR(SC) 275; 1975 CTR 78; [1975] 100 ITR 5; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 927; 1975 TAXLR 499

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 297(2), cls.(f), (g) and (h) - Constitution, art. 20(1) - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 28(4) - Assessments relating to years earlier than year ending 31-03-1962 - But completed after coming into force of 1961 Act - Proceeding for imposition of penalty initiated under 1961 Act - Institution of prosecution against assessee for alleged commission of offences under 1961 or 1922 Act - Whether violative of s. 28(4) of the 1922 Act or art. 20(l) of Constitution - Held, s. 28(4) was not a bar to the launching of the prosecution, for the alleged commission of offences u/s. 52 of the 1922 Act as no provision like s. 28 (4) is to be found either in s. 271 or in any other section of the 1961 Act - The said provision is not available to bar against either of the two Acts - Even art. 20 (1) of the Constitution does not help the assessee as it is not a post facto legislation which is pressed into service against him - Appeals Dismissed.
