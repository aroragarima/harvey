Â Atyam Veerraju and Others v Pechetti Venkanna and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 420; AIR 1966 SC 629; 1976 (2) ALT(SC) 291; 1967 (1) MLJ(SC) 4; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 831

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Madras Hindu Religious Endowments Act, 1927, ss. 18 and 57 - Appeal for recovery of possession of agricultural Inam lands - (A)Whether deity is owner of suit lands? - Held, documents filed by plaintiff disclose that in all public records of village of Cherukuvada, deity is shown as inamdar of suit lands - In schedule to scheme, suit lands are shown to be property of deity in possession and enjoyment of Nuli Subba Rao - (B)Transfer u/art. 134-B - Legality of - Held, the transfer contemplated by art. 134-B is an illegal or unauthorised transfer by a previous manager - Art. 134-B does not apply to a suit for recovery of a property, where the property has been lawfully transferred by a previous manager, and the transfer remains effective after his death, resignation or removal - The lease of 1851 by the previous manager was lawful and binding on the temple, and continued of its own force until 1949 - Consequently, art. 134-B has no application to the present suit - The suit is one by a landlord to recover possession from a tenant and is governed by art. 139 - The tenancy was determined in 1949, and the suit being instituted on 1-11-1954 is well within time - Appeal allowed.
