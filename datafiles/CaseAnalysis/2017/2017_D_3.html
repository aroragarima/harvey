D. N. Joshi (Dead), Through Lrs. and others v D. C. Harris and another

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 931; AIR 2017 SC 3105

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Jurisdiction, Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, Second Appeal, Landlord And Tenant, Delivery Of Possession, Code Of Civil Procedure (Amendment) Act, 1976, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Summary: Land & Property â Civil Procedure â Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s.100, O.42 â Transfer of Property Act, 1882 â Framing of substantial question of law â Predecessor in title of plaintiffs purchased suit property from Zamir Ahmed who got the property vide gift deed dt.31-5-1949 executed in his behalf by his sister â After purchasing suit property from Zamir Ahmad, plaintiffâs predecessor in title demanded rent for suit property from predecessor in title of defendants â However, defendant denied title of plaintiff, and so, plaintiff filed suit for eviction and also for arrears of rent and damages â Trial Court dismissed suit and on appeal, District Judge affirmed judgment of Trial Court and held that gift deed dt.31-5-1949 was invalid as delivery of possession by donor to donee had not been proved â Aggrieved, plaintiffs filed second appeal before HC in 1974 under un-amended s.100 of CPC but it was not listed for hearing for about 6 years and in the year 1980, appeal was listed but dismissed for default â However, on application moved by plaintiffs, appeal was restored and admitted â HC vide impugned judgment and decree, held that gift deed dt.31-5-1949 was valid and was accompanied by possession and allowed appeal of plaintiffs and decreed suit in favour of plaintiffs and ordered eviction of defendants from suit property â Hence, instant appeal by defendants contending that HC admitted second appeal without formulating substantial question of law and that HC re-appreciated evidence to overturn concurrent findings of facts on merit recorded by lower courts â Whether impugned judgment of HC is sustainable.

Held, considering facts of the case, there has been compliance of s.100 of CPC as also O.42 of CPC. So, defendants could not raise issue of non-formulation of substantial question of law while admitting appeal, having failed to challenge the order admitting second appeal and also order allowing plaintiffs to amend the memorandum of second appeal. Further, HC formulated substantial question of law and heard parties on it. Hence, contention of defendants could not be accepted. Further, HC analyzed entire issue in correct perspective and justly relied upon another decision of HC between plaintiffs and another tenant in relation to selfsame gift deed. The gift deed was held to be valid in said case and it was also held that title of property was passed on to plaintiffs. Under such circumstances, it is just and appropriate to follow the same view. Hence, decree of eviction passed by HC against defendants and impugned judgment of HC could not be interfered with and so, same is upheld. Appeal dismissed.
