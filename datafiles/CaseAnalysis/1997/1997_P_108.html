Prem Singh and Others v Joginder Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2504; (1997) 10 SCC 195

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Punjab Pre-emption Act, 1913 - Parents of respondent 4 had transferred disputed property owned by them to two sets of respondents, namely, respondents 1 to 3 and Shri Hardayal Singh and others - Respondent 4 Jaspal made an application for exercising right of pre-emption under Act in respect of both said transfers - While second appeal was pending in High Court, Act was declared ultra vires by this court - Pursuant to such decision of this court, HC set aside impugned decree by which right of pre-emption was allowed in favour of respondent 4 - Review petition was filed by appellant inter alia on allegation that during pendency of said appeal, they had purchased suit properties on valuable consideration from respondent 4 and accordingly they had acquired a valid title to suit properties - Whether appellants got a valid title to suit properties? - Held, Act on basis of which vendor of appellant, namely, respondent 4 had claimed right of pre-emption and obtained decree was declared ultra vires by this court - Accordingly, respondent 4 was not entitled to a decree for pre-emption - Consequently, respondent 4 did not acquire any title to suit property - Hence, on date when respondent 4 purported to transfer disputed property in favour of appellants, he had no title whatsoever to said property - Accordingly, no title had been conveyed to appellants on basis of which appellants may claim said property - Appeals dismissed.
