Subhash Chander v State (Chandigarh Admn.) and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 November 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 569; (1980) 2 SCC 155; (1980) SCC (Cr) 376; AIR 1980 SC 423; 1980 CRLJ 324; 1980 MLJ 423; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 44

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Withdrawal of Prosecution - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 380 and 411 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 321 and 494 - Charges were framed by Trial Court against respondents Nos. 2 and 3 u/s. 411 IPC, 1860 and one other person u/s. 380 IPC, 1860 - During pendency of criminal case, Asst. Public Prosecutor applied for withdrawal from prosecution u/s. 321 CrPC, 1973 - Court accepted request and directed acquittal of respondents No. 2, while continuing case against remaining two accused - Challenged provision s. 494, CrPC, 1973 - Public Prosecutor is entrusted by CrPC, 1973 with a limited power to withdraw from a prosecution, with court's consent whereupon case comes to a close - Two matters which are significant are (a) Whether considerations are germane, and (b) Whether actual decision was made or only obeyed by Public Prosecutor - Held, it is abundantly clear that Asst. Public Prosecutor made an independent study of informing himself of materials placed before court and then sought permission to withdraw from prosecution and did not yield to District Magistrate - Petition dismissed.
