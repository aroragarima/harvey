Sunil Kumar Kori and others v Gopal Das Kabra and another

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

L. Nageswara Rao, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 687; (2016) 10 SCC 467; AIR 2016 SC 4664; 2017 (1) AWC 124; 2016 (3) JabLJ 241; JT 2016 (12) SC 186; 2017 (2) MahLJ 491; 2016 (7) MLJ 363; 2017 (2) MPLJ 541; 2016 (4) RCR(Civil) 662; 2016(9) SCALE 392; [2016] 5 S.C.R. 374

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Election

Keywords: Constituency, Right To Vote, Dismissed, Vote, Ordinarily Resident, Registration of Electors Rules, 1960, representation of the People Act, 1950, Cantonments Act, 2006, Cantonment Electoral Rules 2007, the Act

Summary: Election - Cantonments Act, 2006, s. 28 - Cantonment Electoral Rules, 2007, r. 10(3) - Rights of residents of illegal constructions in Cantonment area - Respondent no.1 filed writ petition challenging voters list contending that same was prepared in willful disobedience of directions issued by HC in earlier writ petition filed by him and in violation of r. 10(3) of the Rules - Single judge of HC allowed writ petition and issued directions to prepare voters list as per r. 10(3) of the Rules by removing names of encroachers and inhabitants of illegally constructed houses - Aggrieved, UOI and others filed appeals but the same were dismissed upholding judgment of Single Judge - Later, in pursuance of orders of SC, review petition was filed before HC but the same was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal challenging orders of HC in appeal and review petitions regarding right to vote of encroachers and other persons who are living in illegally constructed houses within a Cantonment area.

Held, as per r.10(3) of the Rules, the names of electors needs to be arranged according to house numbers. The persons who are living in illegally constructed houses which are not assigned any number will not be entitled for inclusion in the electoral roll to be prepared in accordance with r.10(3). R.10(3) is not in conflict with s. 28 of the Act but is strictly in conformity with s. 28 making only persons living in houses with numbers eligible to vote. Further, DB of HC recorded finding that encroachers are not entitled for allotment of house numbers to illegal structures and such structures will be marked as unauthorized. The Cantonment Board has not been authorized to include the encroachers in the voters list. Hence, clear directions in petition disentitle the persons living in illegally constructed house from being included in voters list. Hence, appeals are dismissed. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - As per s. 28 of the Act, a person who is not less than 18 years of age and who has resided in a Cantonment area for a period of not less than six months immediately preceding the qualifying date is entitled to be enrolled as an elector.
