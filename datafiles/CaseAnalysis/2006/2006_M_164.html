Commissioner of Central Excise, Pune v Messrs Cadbury India Limited

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 364; (2006) 7 SCC 228; AIR 2006 SC 2838; 2006 (200) E.L.T. 353; JT 2006 (7) SC 147; 2006(7) SCALE 484; 2006 (7) SCJ 360; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 207

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Excisability of goods

Summary: [A.] Excise - Central Excise (Valuation) Rules, r. 6(b)(ii) - Valuation of milk crumbs, refined milk chocolate and four other products manufactured by respondent in its factory at Induri, Pune and captively consumed in that factory and other factories of respondent in manufacture of chocolate - Held, intermediate products are not sold nor are marketable - There can be no question of including expenses of factory which produces final product namely chocolate eg advertising, insurance and another expenses in their valuation as was sought to be added by Commissioner (Appeals) and Assistant Commissioner - Appeals dismissed.
