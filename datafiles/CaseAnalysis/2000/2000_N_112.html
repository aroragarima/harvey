Syed Mohammed v New India Assurance Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

08 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Misra, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 4144; (2003) 11 SCC 744

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance

Keywords: Warranty Clause

Summary: Insurance - Marine Insurance Act, 1963 - Denial of insurance money - Validity - Appellant was the owner of the fishing vessel which, while anchored at the coast of Purakkad, drifted from that place, was lost and destroyed - Appellant informed the respondent (New India Assurance Company Ltd.) - As the claim could not be settled on account of denial by the respondent, the suit was filed - Trial Court decreed the suit holding against the Insurance Company by rejecting its contention that the appellant had violated the warranty clause of Insurance policy - On appeal, HC held that the appellant had violated the warranty clause, as, at the relevant time, watch and ward was not present in the vessel in question - Hence, Insurance Company could not be fastened with the liability as in view of this violation the Insurance Company was discharged from the liability to pay the compensation - Hence, instant appeal - Whether it could be said that there was violation of the warranty clause - Held, watch and ward was in the vessel till 1.00 a.m. in the night - It was only on account of rough weather at sea he retraced back to the shore for a few hours - PW3 also stated, even other persons in the vessel which according to PW5 were seven, returned back to the shore - This temporary absence of watch and ward for his own security when threatened by the fury of the rough sea could not be construed to be a violation of the warranty clause - There had not been any breach of the warranty clause by the appellant and the HC erred in declining to grant the claim of the appellant based on this violation - Appellant was entitled to the compensation - Judgment of the Trial Court restored - Appeal allowed.
