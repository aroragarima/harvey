Ram Shankar Misra v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

P.S. Kailasam, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 358; (1980) SCC (Cr) 217; (1980) 1 SCC 255; AIR 1979 SC 727; 1979 ALJ 462; 1980 CRLJ 820; 1979 CRLR 396

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Health & Drugs - Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940, ss. 23(4), 27, 25(1) and 25(4) - Inspector of Drugs went to shop of appellant and purchased four packets of Prednisolone tablets and sent same to Director, Central Drugs Laboratory analysis - Report of Director indicated that tablets were of sub-standard quality - Complaint was lodged by Drugs Inspector - (A) Whether since sample taken by Inspector was not sent to Director through Court his report is inadmissible in evidence? - S. 25(1) provides that Govt. Analyst to whom a sample of any drug or cosmetic has been submitted for test or analysis, shall deliver to Inspector submitting it a signed report in triplicate in prescribed form - There is no restriction as to how a sample of drug or cosmetic has to be submitted by Drugs Inspector - Mode prescribed u/s. 25(4) is one method of sending it to Director of Central Drugs Laboratory - It is significant that s. 25(4) starts with words 'unless sample has been tested or analysed in Central Drugs Laboratory', these words clearly indicate that apart from mode prescribed in s. 25(4), sample can be sent for analysis to Central Drugs Laboratory - Sample is sent for analysis under provision of s. 23(4) - Held, there is no prohibition under Act or Rules barring Inspector from sending sample direct to Director, Central Drugs Laboratory - Courts below have found that sample was for sale and not necessary to consider same - Appeal dismissed.
