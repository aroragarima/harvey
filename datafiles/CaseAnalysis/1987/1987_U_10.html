Ravindra Kumar Misra v U.P. State Handloom Corporation Ltd. And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

15 October 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28783; (1987) Supp SCC 739; (1988) SCC (L&S) 361; AIR 1987 SC 2408; 1988 (6) ATC 617; 1987 (55) FLR 884; JT 1987 (4) SC 105; 1988 LabIC 56; 1988 (1) LLJ 73; 1988 (1) LLN 321; 1987(2) SCALE 766; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 501; 1988 (2) SLJ 48; 1988 (1) SLR 526; 1988 (1) UJ 11

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Laws - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, sch. 2, item 3 - Industrial Dispute - (A) Public Sector Undertaking employee on temporary basis - Termination (As per Service Rules) - Order innocuous without any stigma or evil consequences visiting him - Challenged - (B) Compensation - Not entitled, ordinarily, as per law - Held, order is not open to challenge - Because appellant was put out of employment at an advanced stage and he could not get alternative employment, Supreme Court called upon corporation to pay a consolidated amount of Rs. 25,000 which is to be paid to appellant - Appeal dismissed.
