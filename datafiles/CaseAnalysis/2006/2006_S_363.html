Shailesh Jasvantbhai and Another v State of Gujarat and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 571; (2006) 2 SCC 359; (2006) 1 SCC (Cr) 499; 2006 CRLJ 1132; 2006 (2) G.L.R. 1428; 2006 (1) JCC 263; JT 2006 (1) SC 639; 2006 (1) RCR(Criminal) 704; 2006(1) SCALE 561; 2006 (1) SCJ 756; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 477

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 114, 307, 324 and 504 - Bombay Police Act, s. 135 - By impugned judgment while upholding conviction recorded by Trial Court, High Court reduced sentence to period already undergone, but awarded compensation to victims - Whether HC was right in taking a lenient view as both accused were having no criminal antecedent? - Undue sympathy to impose inadequate sentence would do more harm to justice system to undermine public confidence in efficacy of law and society could not long endure under such serious threats - Accused was involved in large number of cases and details of nine cases - Bail granted to accused was canceled for breach of conditions imposed by court for grant of bail - Dhananjoy Chatterjee v. State of W B, 1994 Indlaw SC 174 'The Court must not only keep in view rights of criminal but also rights of victim of crime and society at large while considering imposition of appropriate punishment' - Matter remitted back to HC for a fresh hearing on question of sentence - Appeal allowed.
