Â Synthetics And Chemicals Limited Etc. v State Of U.P. And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

25 October 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, B.C. Ray, E.S. Venkataramiah, G.L. Oza, K.N. Singh, Ranganath Misra, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 286; (1990) 1 SCC 109; AIR 1990 SC 1927; JT 1989 (4) SC 267; 1989(2) SCALE 1045; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 623; 1991 (80) STC 270; [1989] supp1 S.C.R. 623

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Indian Independence Act, 1947, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Indian Power Alcohol Act, 1948

Summary: Constitution - Indirect tax - Parliament & Legislature - Constitution of India, 1950, Sch 7, List 1, Item 84, List 2, Item 51, art. 245, 246, Sch 7, List 1, Entry 84, List 2, Entry 51 - Uttar Pradesh Excise Act, 1910, ss. 24 A and 24 B - Industries Development and Regulation Act, 1951, s. 18 G - Bombay Prohibition Act, 1949, s. 49 - Andhra Pradesh Excise Act, 1968, s. 21 - Tamil Nadu Prohibition Act, 1937, ss. 4 and 54 - (A) Consumption of alcohol meaning of - Direct physical intake by human beings and not utilisation in manufacturing process - Must be understood in its common and normal sense - Industrial alcohol - Is incapable of being consumed by a normal human being - Expression 'Consumption' must also be understood in sense of direct physical intake by human beings in this context - It is true that utilisation in some form or other is consumption for benefit of human beings if industrial alcohol is utilised for production of rubber, tyres used utilisation of those tyres in vehicle of man cannot in context in which expression has been used in Constitution, be understood to mean that alcohol has been for human consumption - 'alcoholic liquor for human consumption' they meant at that time and still expression means that liquor which as it is consumable in sense capable of being taken by human being as such as beverage of drinks - Entry 84, List I must be understood in that light - It is also not possible to accept that vend fee in Uttar Pradesh is a pre-constitution imposition and would not be subject to art. 245 of Constitution - Present extent of imposition of vend fee is not a pre-Constitution imposition, as can be seen from change of rate from time to time - (B) Whether levy of tax is an exercise of sovereign or police power? - Held, levy of tax on intoxicants and alcohol is an exercise of sovereign power - Constitution does not recognise police power as such - Indian Constitution as a sovereign State has power to legislate on all branches except to limitation as to division of powers between Center and States and also subject to fundamental rights guaranteed under Constitution - This power of sovereignty is, however, subject to Constitutional limitations - Right to tax or levy imposts must be in accordance with provisions of Constitution - States have power to regulate use of alcohol and that power must include power to make provisions to prevent and/or check industrial alcohol often being used as intoxicating or drinkable alcohol - Judged by pith and substance of impugned legislation, these levies cannot be treated as part of regulatory measures - State has power to regulate though perhaps not as emanation of police power, but as an expression of sovereign power of State - That power has its limitations - (C) Whether states entitled to levy impost under framework of constitution? - Held, no - In respect of industrial alcohol States are not authorised to impose impost they have purported to do - This will not affect any impost so far as potable alcohol - It will not affect fee - State legislature had no authority to levy duty or tax on alcohol which is not for human consumption as that could only be levied by Center - Appeal allowed.
