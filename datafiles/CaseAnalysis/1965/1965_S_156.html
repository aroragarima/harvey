Sarju Prasad Saha v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 March 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, R.S. Bachawat, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 510; AIR 1965 SC 1763; 1966 ALJ 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4 (1), 5-A, 6, 9, 11 and 17(4) - Notification issued on 10-11-1960 u/s. 4(1) of Land Acquisition Act by Govt. of U.P. intimating that an area was likely to be needed for a public purpose - Simultaneously therewith Govt. of U.P. in purported exercise of powers u/s 17 (4) of Act directed that provisions of s. 5-A of Act shall not apply to land notified - Later Govt. of U.P. on 29-11-1960 notified u/s. 6 of Act that Governor was satisfied that land mentioned in Schedule was needed for a public purpose and directed Collector, Basti to take order for acquisition of land - Whether in respect of land under acquisition, a notification under sub-s. (4) of s. 17 of Act excluding application of s. 5-A could not be issued? - Held, by s. 17, two distinct powers exercisable by appropriate Govt. in case of urgency are conferred; first is power to take possession before award of compensation is made by Collector, and second is power to dispense with an enquiry which Act contemplates has to be made u/s 5-A (2) - It is to be noticed that power exercisable in case of urgency to take possession of land needed for a public purpose before an award is made has been extended by U.P. Legislature to cases where land is acquired for or in connection with sanitary improvements of any kind of planned development - Legislature has, however, not authorised Govt. to direct that provisions of s. 5-A shall not apply to acquisitions of land other than waste or aerable land - Since area under dispute was not waste or arable land, notification D/- 10-11-1960 issued was invalid - As notification u/s. 6 was issued without following procedure prescribed by s. 5-A, which was obligatory, that notification also must be declared invalid - Both notifications are set aside - Appeal partly allowed.
