S.M. Nilajkar and others v Telecom District Manager, Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 302; (2003) 4 SCC 27; (2003) SCC (L&S) 380; AIR 2003 SC 3553; 2003 (2) AWC 1648; 2003 (2) CLR 233; 2003 (5) ESC 224; 2003 (97) FLR 608; JT 2003 (3) SC 436; 2003 (2) KHCACJ 311; 2003 (2) LLJ 359; 2003 (3) MahLJ 9; 2003 (2) MPLJ 529; 2003(3) SCALE 533; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 156; 2003 (3) SLR 20; 2003 (3) SLT 22; 2003 (3) Supreme 53

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Contract Of Employment, Voluntary Retirement, Disciplinary Proceeding, Disciplinary Authority

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss.25F, 25FFF - Whether the workmen recruited for discharging temporary job under a project can insist on compliance of s.25F of the Act if their services are dispensed with on the project coming to an end? - Held, compliance of s.25F shall be subject to such relaxations as are provided by s.25FFF - The undertaking having been closed on account of unavoidable circumstances beyond the control of the employer, i.e. by its own force as it was designed and destined to have a limited life only, the compensation payable to the workman under cl. (b) of s.25F shall not exceed his average pay for three months - This is so because of failure on the part of respondent employer to allege and prove that the termination of employment fell within sub-cl (bb) of cl (oo) of s.2 of the Act - Further held, the delay in the case at hand has not been so culpable as to disentitle the appellants for any relief - The learned Single Judge has rightly denied the relief of back-wages while directing the appellants to be reinstated - Appeals allowed.
