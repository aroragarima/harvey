Mantoo Sarkar v Oriental Insurance Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2001; (2009) 2 SCC 244; (2009) 1 SCC (Cr) 738; 2009 (1) ACC 40; AIR 2009 SC 1022; 2009(2) ALL MR 475; 2009 (1) AWC 496; 2009 (4) MLJ(SC) 674; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 417; 2008(16) SCALE 197; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 753; 2009 (3) UC 1476

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Place Of Suing, Overvaluation

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Interpretation and/or application of s. 166 (2) in regard to territorial jurisdiction of a Tribunal - Held, High Court unfortunately in its judgment did not assign sufficient or cogent reason as to why the Tribunal committed any illegality in holding that he had the jurisdiction to entertain the claim petition - If the Tribunal had the jurisdiction to entertain a claim petition under the Motor Vehicles Act, Court should not have, in absence of any finding of sufferance of any prejudice on the part of the first respondent, entertained the appeal - Whether the extraordinary jurisdiction under art. 142 of the Constitution shall be exercised or not would depend upon the fact of the each matter - Law in this case does not come in the way of exercise of such jurisdiction - Appeal allowed.
