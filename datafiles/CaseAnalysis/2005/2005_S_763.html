State of Uttar Pradesh v Neeraj Awasthi and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1017; (2006) SCC (L&S) 190; (2006) 1 SCC 667; 2006 (1) AWC 875; JT 2006 (1) SC 19; 2005(10) SCALE 286; 2006 (1) SCJ 275; 2006 (1) SLR 774; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 906

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: (1) U. P. Krishi Utpadan Mandi Adhiniyam, 1964 (Act)-Sections 23, 26F, 26L and 26M-U. P. Agricultural Produce Market Committees (Centralised) Service Regulations, 1984 (Service Regulations)-Regulations 2 (e), 11 and 12-U. P. Agricultural Produce Markets Board (Officers and Staff Establishment) Regulations, 1984 (Establishment Regulations)-Regulations 2 (e), 11 and 12-Employment - Termination-Regularisation-State Government issuing objections to canal appointments of employees of Market Committees irregularily appointed during period from 1.4.1996 to 30.10.1997-Accordingly, service of 1,021 employees terminated-Whether termination of services of employees valid?-Held, âyesâ-Definition of 'employee' in Regulation 2 (e) does not include persons employed on daily wages, work-charged and on part-time basis-Meaning thereby that persons so employed are not employees-When appointment de hors Act and Rules-No legal relationship of employer and employee-Such employees have no legal right to continue in service-Though directions of State Government not strictly in accordance with law and not binding on Board-Same cannot be said to be wholly irrational-If directions confined to period between 1.4.1996 to 30.10.1997 following basic principle of 'last come first go'-Directions cannot be termed as arbitrary or discriminatory - If employees are workmen within meaning of U. P. Industrial Disputes Act-They are protected thereunder-Then, no question of compliance with natural justice-High Court erred in holding that principles of natural justice were required to be complied with-Board/Market Committees cannot make appointment in violation of Act and Regulations-No permanent temporary status can be granted to an employee by way of regularisation-High Court not justified in issuing directions for framing scheme of regularisation of employees-Impugned judgment set aside-Direction to fill all existing vacancy strictly in accordance with law expeditiously and perferably within six months-Candidature of terminated employees also to be taken into consideration-Age-bar to be relaxed if it could be done.

(2) Constitution of India-Articles 14 and 16 - Employment-Appointments made de hors Rules and without following procedure known in law-And in flagrant violation of constitutional scheme laid down in Articles 14 and 16-Though might have been made in exignecies of services-Still are wholly illegal and without jurisdiction.
