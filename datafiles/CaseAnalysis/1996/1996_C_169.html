Steel Authority of India Limited v Collector of Central Excise

Supreme Court of India

30 July 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 185; (1996) 5 SCC 484; 1996 (6) AD(SC) 49; AIR 1996 SC 2544; 1997 (68) ECR 43; 1996 (88) E.L.T. 314; JT 1996 (9) SC 113; 1996 (15) RLT 493; 1996(5) SCALE 479; [1996] Supp4 S.C.R. 163; 1996 (5) Supreme 669

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 196 - Alleged that a substantial quantity of raw naphtha was not used by the appellant in the manufacture of fertilizer - Whether raw naphtha was intended for the manufacture of fertilizer? - Need not be proved that raw naphtha was actually used in the manufacture of fertilizer - All that was required to be proved that raw naphtha was used for the purpose and intention of manufacturing fertilizer - Held, benefit of exemption notification available to Appellant in regard to the raw naphtha that is utilised in its plant for the manufacture of fertiliser but which, for reasons over which it had no control, did not, in fact, result in the manufacture of fertilizer but had, at the interim stage of reformed gas, to be vented out - Appeal allowed.
