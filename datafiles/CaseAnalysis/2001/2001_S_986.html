Uday Mohanlal Acharya v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

29 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20681; (2001) 5 SCC 453; (2001) SCC (Cr) 760; AIR 2001 SC 1910; AIR 2001 SCW 1500; 2001 (2) CGLJ 302; 2001 (2) Crimes 150; 2002 (1) CRJ 597; 2001 CRLJ 1832; 2001 (2) G.L.R. 1148; JT 2001 (4) SC 262; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 452; 2001(3) SCALE 29; 2001 (3) SCJ 1; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 878; 2001 (3) SLT 289; 2001 (3) Supreme 142; 2001 (2) UC 58; 2001 (2) UC 65

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Bail - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 56, 57, 154, 167, 173, 209, 437 and 439 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 406 and 420 - Maharashtra Protection of Interest of Depositors (Financial Establishment) Act, 1999, ss. 13 and 14 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21 and 22(2) - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, s. 20(4)(b) - (A) Whether an accused can avail his indefeasible right for being released on bail if a challan is not filed within period stipulated thereunder? - Held, indefeasible right accruing to accused is enforceable only prior to filing of challan and does not survive or remain enforceable on challan being filed, if already not availed of - Once challan has been filed, question of grant of bail has to be considered and decided only with reference to merits of case - If he exercises right within time allowed by law and is released on bail under such circumstances, he cannot be rearrested on mere filing of charge - sheet - (B) When right accrues to accused for being released on bail and when it gets extinguished? - Further held, magistrate could prescribe maximum period for detention - Accused shall be released on bail if he is prepared to and does furnish bail, 'compulsive bail' - Right of an accused to be released on bail after expiry of maximum period of detention can be denied only when an accused does not furnish bail - Bail has to be in accordance with order passed by magistrate - (C) Whether accused who was entitled to be released on bail not having made an application when such right had accrued, can exercise that right at a later stage of proceeding? - Further held, no, because if an accused person fails to exercise his right he cannot contend that he had an indefeasible right to exercise it at any time - (D) Whether expression 'if already not availed of' mean that an accused files an application for bail and offers his willingness for being released on bail or does it mean that a bail order must be passed, accused must furnish bail and get him released on bail? - Further held, an accused is said to be availed of his indefeasible right, moment he files an application for being released on bail and offers to abide by terms and conditions of bail - Rights whether accrued or not to an accused, will have to be considered on date, he filed application for bail and not with reference to any later point of time - Appeal allowed.
