R. Kempraj v Messrs. Barton Son and Company

Supreme Court of India

29 August 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 499; (1969) 2 SCC 594; AIR 1970 SC 1872; 1974 RCJ 18; 1971 RCR(Rent) 813; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 140

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Rule Against Perpetuity

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 14 - Lease deed with an option to lease to renew same - Lessee informing lessor for renewal - Non compliance by lessor - Lessee filed suit for specific performance of covenant - Challenged by lessor as renewal condition hit by rule against perpetuity - Whether a renewal option given to a lessee, initially for a period of 10 years and to be renewed after every 10 years is hit by rule of perpetuity? - Rule against perpetuity is founded on principle that liberty of alienation to mean an act by which a living person conveys property in present or in future to one or more other living persons etc. words 'living persons' include a Company or association or body of individuals - Lease is not a mere contract but it is a transfer of an interest in land and creates a right in rem - Lease in perpetuity can be created but even then an interest still remains in lessor which is called a reversion - S. 14 is applicable only where there is transfer of property - Even if creation of a lease hold interest is a transfer of a right in property and would fall within expression 'transfer of property' transfer was for a period of ten years only by means of indenture - Stipulation relating to renewal could not be regarded as transferring property or any rights therein - No interest in property created by provision of renewal - Appeal dismissed.
