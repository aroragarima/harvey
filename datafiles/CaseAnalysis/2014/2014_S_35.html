Union of India and another v Sanjeev V. Deshpande

Supreme Court of India

12 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, A.K. Sikri, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 516; (2014) 13 SCC 1; AIR 2014 SC 3625; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 3713; 2014 CRLJ 4329; JT 2014 (9) SC 230; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3234; 2014 (2) OLR 707; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 75; 2014(9) SCALE 335; [2014] 11 S.C.R. 981

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug

Keywords: Possess, Poppy, Opium Act, 1857, Dangerous Drugs Act, 1930, Opium Act, 1878

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substance Act, 1985, ss. 8, 37 - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substance Rules, 1985, rr. 53, 64 - Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940 - Possession of contraband substance - Bail - Rejection - Accused were alleged to be in possession of some psychotropic substance mentioned in the Schedule to the 1985 Act - In some of these cases, bail was granted by the concerned High Court and in some cases, bail was rejected - Aggrieved by such orders, either the State or the accused preferred instant appeals - Whether persons accused of committing an offence under the 1985 Act could be enlarged on bail in view of the stipulations contained u/s. 37 of the Act -

Held, analysis of the provisions of chapters VI and VII of the 1985 Rules, SC was of the opinion, both these Chapters contain Rules permitting and regulating the import and export of narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances other than those specified in the Schedule-I to the 1985 rules subject to various conditions and procedure stipulated in Chapter VI - Whereas Chapter VII deals exclusively with various other aspects of DEALING IN psychotropic substances and the conditions subject to which such DEALING IN was permitted - SC was of the opinion that rr. 53 and 64 of the 1985 Rules were really in the nature of exception to the general scheme of Chapters VI and VII respectively containing a list of narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances which could not be dealt in any manner notwithstanding the other provisions of these two chapters - SC was of the clear opinion that neither r. 53 nor r. 64 of the 1985 Rules was a source of authority for prohibiting the DEALING IN narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances, the source was s. 8 of the Act - Complete analysis of the implications of s. 80 of the 1985 Act was not really called for in the instant case - It was only required to be stated that essentially the 1940 Act deals with various operations of manufacture, sale, purchase etc. of drugs generally whereas 1985 Act deals with a more specific class of drugs and, therefore, a special law on the subject - Further the provisions of the Act operate in addition to the provisions of the 1940 Act - Correctness of the orders impugned in all the Criminal Appeals was normally required to be considered by the Bench of appropriate strength - SC deemed it appropriate to remit all matters to the concerned HC for passing of appropriate orders - Appeals disposed of.
