P. Kiran Kumar v A.S. Khadar and others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 294; (2002) 5 SCC 161; AIR 2002 SC 2286; 2002 (3) ALD(SC) 109; 2002 (2) AllCJ 1341; 2002 (47) ALR 797; 2002 (2) ARC 134; 2002 (4) AWC 2709; 2002 (2) CGLJ 51; JT 2002 (Supp1) SC 29; 2002 (3) MLJ(SC) 73; 2002 (131) PLR 830; 2002(4) SCALE 384; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 775; 2002 (3) SLT 558; 2002 (4) Supreme 30; 2002 (2) UC 305

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte, Ex-Parte Order, Set Aside

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure (Amendment) Act, 1976 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss.151, 96, O.9 r.13 - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s.110A - Ex parte order - Set aside - Justifiability - Appellant filed claim petition before Tribunal claiming compensation against respondent nos.1 and 2 - Tribunal set respondents ex-parte and awarded compensation to appellant - Aggrieved application filed by respondents was allowed by Tribunal and respondents were permitted to file their written statement - Revision filed by appellant was dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Appellant contended that application for setting aside ex-parte decree must be held to be incompetent in view of dismissal of appeal filed by respondent No.2 before HC.

Held, in the present case, admittedly an appeal had been preferred by respondent No. 2 and the same was dismissed as barred by limitation. In view of the dismissal of the earlier appeal, the application under O. 9 r. 13 of CPC for setting aside an ex parte decree/award was not maintainable and the Tribunal erred in setting aside the ex-parte decree/award made against the respondents. The HC failed to notice this point in spite of the fact that the same had been specifically raised. The impugned order of the HC and that of the Tribunal setting aside the ex parte award are set aside. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - If appeal against ex parte decree has been filed and appeal has been dismissed on any ground other than dismissed as withdrawn, then application under O. 9 r. 13 of CPC would not be maintainable and cannot be entertained.
