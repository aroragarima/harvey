Beckodan Abdul Rahiman v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 242; (2002) 4 SCC 229; (2002) SCC (Cr) 791; AIR 2002 SC 1810; 2002 (44) AllCriC 1077; 2002 (2) CCR 71; 2002 (2) Crimes 375; 2002 (4) CRJ 41; 2002 CRLJ 2529; 2002 (81) ECC 8; 2002 (2) JCC 894; JT 2002 (4) SC 68; 2003 (1) KHCACJ 70; 2002 (2) RCR(Criminal) 385; 2002 (3) RCR(Criminal) 404; 2002(3) SCALE 564; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 53; 2002 (3) SLT 155; 2002 (3) Supreme 597; 2002 (2) UC 157

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conviction, Possession of contraband, Non-compliance of mandate

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 18, 31(a), 36, 37, 41, 42, 42(1), 42(2), 43, 50, 9(c) - Possession of contraband - Conviction - Non-compliance of mandate - Appellant/accused was allegedly found to be in possession of contraband - Appellant was convicted u/s. 9(c) r/w s. 18 of the Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of Trial Court is sustainable as there is violation of provisions of ss. 42 and 50 of the Act.

Held, the provisions of s. 42(2) of the Act and the mandate of s. 50 of the Act were not complied with by the prosecution which rendered the case as not established. In view of the violation of the mandatory provisions of the Act, the appellant was entitled to be acquitted. Both the Trial Court as well as the HC have failed to consider this aspect of the matter which warrants the setting aside of the impugned judgment. Impugned judgment set aside. The appellant is directed to set at liberty forthwith. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Provisions of ss. 42 and 50 of the Act are mandatory and their non-compliance would render investigation illegal.
