Bharti Cellular Limited v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

05 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 827; (2010) 10 SCC 174; 2010 (6) AWC 6017; 2010 (4) CompLJ 285; JT 2010 (11) SC 153; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 850; 2010(10) SCALE 552; [2010] 12 S.C.R. 725

Case Digest

Subject: Telecom; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Telecom - Contract & Commercial - Telecom Regulatory Authority of India Act, 1997, ss. 14 (a)(I) and 18 - Licence Agreement executed between the appellant and the Government of India, provided for payment of fixed amount towards licence fee for the first 3 years of the licence period and from the 4th year onwards the licence fee payable was to be on the basis of number of subscribers of the service provider - Appellant filed petition before tribunal submitting that although it had a provisional operational clearance from the respondent effective from 29.08.1995 and an interface/service approval from 26.09.1995, it could commence commercial services only from 15.11.1995 meaning thereby the Licence Agreement should be deemed to have become operative only from 15.11.1995 - Appellant further submitted that respondents, however, treated 26.09.1995 i.e. the date when the interface/service clearance was given as the date of commencement of the Licence Agreement and computed the licence fee dues, interest, penal interest, liquidated damages etc. with reference to the said date - Tribunal held that respondents had clarified to the appellant and other cellular operators that the basis for calculating the number of subscribers for determining the licence fee shall be the total figure of IMSI in the Home Location Register - Tribunal also held that as appellant gave its unconditional acceptance to the entire Migration package on 27.07.1999, hence, appellant was not entitled to raise any issue that related to the pre-migration period - Tribunal also held that the appellant had not led any evidence before it and that the question regarding Unit Call Rate was raised by it at any stage either before or after the licence was issued for the year 1994 and that the issue relating to the Licence Agreement could not be agitated being a pre- migration package - Tribunal further held that the respondents were entitled to recover not only the outstanding licence dues but also interest due on the same for the period of default - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether the methodology adopted by the Respondent for arriving at the number of subscribers from the 4th year of the Licence Agreement was in order? - Held, no legal infirmity in the view taken by the Tribunal as once the petitioner had specifically and unconditionally agreed to accept the Migration Package and given up all disputes relating to Licence Agreement for the period upto 31.07.1999, it was not open to it to turn around and agitate any such dispute after availing of the Migration Package - (B) Whether the Petitioner is entitled to the benefit of reduction in the unit call rate w.e.f. 1.05.1999 for calculating the per subscriber licence fee? - Held, the Tribunal has rightly held that the petitioner had not led any evidence before it and that the question regarding Unit Call Rate was raised by it at any stage either before or after the licence was issued for the year 1994 and that the issue relating to the Licence Agreement could not be agitated being a pre- migration package - (C) Whether the respondent can levy penal interest on the licence fee from 1.02.2000 till the actual date of payment? - Held, tribunal has rightly held that to the extent condition stipulated a deadline i.e. 31st January, 2000 it was open to the respondent to charge simple interest on the overdue amount for keeping the licence valid instead of terminating the same on the ground of default - Impugned order of Tribunal upheld - Appeal dismissed.
