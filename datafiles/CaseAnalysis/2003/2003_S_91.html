Ram Chandra Singh v Savitri Devi And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

09 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 842; (2003) 8 SCC 319; 2004 (54) ALR 400; 2004 (1) BC 187; 2004 (1) JhrCR(SC) 4; JT 2005 (11) SC 439; 2003(8) SCALE 505B; 2003 (6) SLT 202; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 543; 2003 (7) Supreme 267

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - O. 20 r. 18 - O. 26 rr. 13, 14(2) - Effect of fraud on court - Respondent became a defaulter in the matter of repayment of loan - Bank filed suit as regards mortgaged property - Sisters of the mortgagor claimed their share in the mortgaged property on the basis of a preliminary decree passed in a partition suit - Held, the High Court failed to notice the principle 'actus curiae neminem gravabit' - Further held, once it is held that a judgment and decree has been obtained by practising fraud on the court, it is trite that the principles of res judicata shall not apply. The High Court, therefore, committed a serious error in referring to the earlier orders passed by it so as to shut the doors of justice on the face of appellant for all time to come.
