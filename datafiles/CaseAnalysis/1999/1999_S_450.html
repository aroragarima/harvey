Ramesh S/o Laxman Gawli v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 713; (2000) SCC (Cr) 206; (2000) 1 SCC 243; AIR 1999 SC 3759; 1999 (37) ALR 419; 1999 (4) CCR 71; 1999 CRLJ 4603; 1999 (2) JCC 500; JT 1999 (6) SC 627; 1999 (4) RCR(Criminal) 227; 1999(5) SCALE 641; 2000 (1) SCJ 354; 1999 (8) SLT 160; [1999] Supp2 S.C.R. 603; 1999 (8) Supreme 116

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Code of Criminal procedure, 1973, s. 154 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 300 - Whether FIR lodged is fabricated and conviction based on evidence of eye - witness is correct? - Held, FIR lodged is not said to be fabricated in view of explanation of investigating officer - Further, fully corroborated evidence of eye - witness by independent witnesses are sufficient to convict accused and hence there is no interference in his conviction.
