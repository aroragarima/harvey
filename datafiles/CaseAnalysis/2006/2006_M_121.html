Baldev Singh and Others Etc v Manohar Singh and Another Etc

Supreme Court of India

03 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 648; (2006) 6 SCC 498; AIR 2006 SC 2832; 2006 (6) ALD(SC) 29; 2006 (64) ALR 895; 2006 (3) AWC 2939; 2006 (6) Bom.C.R. 710; 2006 (4) JhrCR(SC) 186; JT 2006 (7) SC 139; 2006 (3) RCR(Civil) 844; 2006 (2) RCR(Rent) 265; 2006 (101) RD 594; 2006(7) SCALE 517; 2006 (6) SCJ 305; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 259; 2006 (3) UC 1709

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6, r. 17 - Whether High Court, as well as, Trial Court erred in rejecting application for amendment of written statement filed by appellants/ defendants? - Ragu Thilak D John v. S Rayappan and Ors, 2001 Indlaw SC 2033, followed - Commencement of trial as used in proviso to O. 6, r. 17 CPC, 1908 must be understood in limited sense as meaning final hearing of suit, examination of witnesses, filing of documents and addressing of arguments, and parties are yet to file their documents - Held, HC as well as trial court erred in rejecting application - Orders of HC and Trial Court are set aside, application for amendment of written statement is allowed - Appeals allowed.
