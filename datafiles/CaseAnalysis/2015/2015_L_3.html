Fatehji and Company and another v L. M. Nagpal and others

Supreme Court of India

24 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

C. Nagappan, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 294; (2015) 8 SCC 390; AIR 2015 SC 2301; 2015 (111) ALR 515; 2015 (4) ALT(SC) 57; 2015 (4) AWC 3351; 2015 (3) CLT(SC) 77; 2015 (3) CTC 218; 2016 (2) MahLJ 389; 2015 (4) MLJ 495; 2016 (1) MPLJ 557; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 999; 2015 (129) RD 163; 2015 (4) RLW 3089; 2015(5) SCALE 488; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 389; 2015 (4) WLN(SC) 47; 2015 (4) WLN(SC) 193

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7 r. 11 - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 54 - Arising of cause of action - Filing of Suit - Period of Limitation - Lease was granted to lessee by Govt. whereby he sold his right to defendant no.1 - Further, defendant no.1 entered into sale agreement on 2-7-1973 with plaintiff in respect to property in question and date for execution of sale deed was fixed for 2-12-1973 - Extension of time for execution of sale deed on plea of obtaining permission to sell property from lessor was sought by defendants by way of letters and last of such extension was sought for six months w.e.f. August 1976 - However, defendant's son claimed that suit property was joint family property - Plaintiff filed suit contending that final cause of action accrued and arose after August 1991 when defendants began hiding themselves and avoiding plaintiffs and they filed suit on 29-4-1994 seeking for decree for specific performance of agreement of sale dt. 2-7-1973 against defendants - Further, defendant nos. 1 to 3 filed application under O.7 r. 11 of CPC on 10-10-1996 seeking for rejection of plaint as barred by law of limitation which was allowed by Trial court, however, HC reversed order passed by Trial Court and passed order in favor of plaintiff - Whether suit filed by plaintiff was within limitation period.

Held, last extension for period of six months w.e.f. 1-8-1976 sought for by defendants expired on 1-2-1977 and present suit seeking for specific performance was filed by plaintiffs on 29-4-1994, much beyond period of three years. SC held that original cause of action became available to plaintiffs on 2-12-1973, date fixed for performance of contract and thereafter same stood extended till 1-2-1977 as requested by defendants. Though plaintiffs claimed that oral extension of time was given, no particulars as to when and how long, were not mentioned in plaint. Further, even after knowing dishonest intention of sons of defendant no.2 with regard to suit property in year1985, plaintiffs did not file suit immediately, hence, suit having been filed in year 1994 is barred by limitation u/art. 54 of Act. SC held that HC committed manifest error in reversing well considered order of Trial Court rejecting plaint as barred by law of limitation and impugned judgment of HC is set aside and order of Trial court is restored. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Suit against order is not maintainable when same is filed beyond limitation period specified under statute.
