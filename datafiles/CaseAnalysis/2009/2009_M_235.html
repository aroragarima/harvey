Maruti Suzuki Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, Delhi-III

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 976; (2009) 9 SCC 193; 2009 (240) E.L.T. 641; 2009(11) SCALE 446; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 301

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Additional Duties of Excise (Textiles and Textile Articles) Act, 1978, Provisional Collection Taxes Act, 1931, Central Excise Rules, 2000

Summary: Excise - CENVAT Credit Rules, 2002, rr. 2(g), 6(1), 6(2) - Penalty - CENVAT Credit - Input (Naptha) - Assessee claimed CENVAT credit on 'input' used for generation of electricity which was denied by dept. on ground that part of electricity stood cleared outside factory to its joint ventures, vendors etc. - Assessee contended that since electricity was neither exempted nor chargeable to 'nil' rate of duty, r. 6(1) was not applicable in case of naphtha used in generation of electricity or steam and, therefore, assessee entitled to avail full credit on naphtha - Therefore, wheeling out a part of electricity generated in factory of assessee to its joint ventures or vendors could not have deprived assessee of credit of duty paid on naphtha used as fuel in their factory - P /&gt;(A) Whether an assessee would be entitled to claim CENVAT credit in cases where it sells electricity outside factory to joint ventures, vendors or gives it to grid for distribution? - Unless and until impugned input used in or in relation to manufacture of final product within factory of production, said item would not become an eligible input - Held, when electricity generation is a captive arrangement and requirement is for carrying out manufacturing activity, electricity generation also forms part of manufacturing activity and 'input' used in that electricity generation is an 'input' used in manufacture of final product - However, to extent excess electricity is cleared to grid for distribution or to joint ventures, vendors, and that too for a price (sale) process and use test fails - Therefore, to extent of clearance of excess electricity outside factory to joint ventures, vendors, grid etc. would not be admissible for CENVAT credit - Assessee is entitled to credit on eligible inputs utilized in generation of electricity to extent to which they are using produced electricity within their factory - P /&gt;(B) Whether penalty could be imposed on assessee? - Held, penalty not leviable on assessee as on said issue conflict of views expressed by various Tribunals/High Court - Hence, although assessee appeals failed dept. will not impose penalty on assessee - Appeals dismissed.
