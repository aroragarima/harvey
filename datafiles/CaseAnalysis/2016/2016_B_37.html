Vishal N. Kalsaria v Bank of India and others

Supreme Court of India

20 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 63; (2016) 3 SCC 762; AIR 2016 SC 530; 2016(2) ALL MR 920; 2016 ALL MR (Cri) 1322; 2016 (115) ALR 442; 2016 (1) BC 471; 2016 (2) Bom.C.R. 67; 2016 (2) CompLJ 369; 2016 (2) CTC 319; 2016 (226) DLT 474; 2016 (1) DRTC 305; JT 2016 (1) SC 335; 2016 (1) KLT 623; 2016 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1137; 2016 (5) MahLJ 555; 2016 (1) MLJ 583; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 911; 2016 (1) RCR(Rent) 81; 2016 (131) RD 564; 2016(1) SCALE 472; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 419

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: Registration Act, 1908, Borrower, Maharashtra Rent Control Act, 1999, Bombay Rent Act, 1947

Summary: Rent Control - Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Maharashtra Rent Control Act, 1999, ss.15, 33 - Securitization and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act, 2002 - Tenancy - Secured Creditors - As respondents failed to repay loan amount, respondent-Bank filed application u/s. 14 of 2002 Act before Chief Metropolitan Magistrate seeking possession of mortgaged properties which are in actual possession of appellant-tenant and said application was allowed and directions were passed to take possession of secured assets vide order dt.8-4-2011 - Appellant-tenant was served with notice to vacate premises but fearing eviction, appellant-tenant filed Rent Suit and Small Causes Court allowed application and passed interim order of injunction in favour of appellant-tenant, restraining respondent no.4 from obstructing possession of appellant-tenant over suit premises during pendency of suit vide order dt.8-6-2011 - Later, appellant-tenant filed application as intervenor to stay execution of order dt.8-4-2011 but the same was dismissed and it was also held that when secured creditor takes action u/ss. 13 or 14 of 2002 Act to recover possession of the secured interest and recover loan amount by selling same in public auction, then it is not open for Court to grant an injunction u/s.33 of 1999 Act and also that order dt.8-6-2011 passed by Small Causes Court is not binding on respondent-Bank as it was not party to proceedings - Hence, instant appeal by appellant and other appeals by appellants concerning similar issue - Whether provisions of 2002 Act override provisions of 1999 Act.

Held, a tenant could not be arbitrarily evicted by using provisions of 2000 Act as that would amount to stultifying statutory rights of protection given to tenant. A non obstante cl. i.e., s.35 of 2000 Act, could not be used to bulldoze the statutory rights vested on the tenants under 1999 Act. Further, the expression 'any other law for the time being in force' as appearing in s. 35 of 2000 Act could not mean to extend to each and every law enacted by Central and State legislatures. It could only extend to laws operating in the same field. Further, if provisions of 2000 Act were to override provisions of 1999 Act, denuded which would amount to subverting the law enacted by State Legislature and such a situation was not contemplated by Parliament while enacting 2000 Act and hence, same is untenable in law. Hence, impugned judgments and orders of HC are set aside and appeals are allowed. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - When once tenancy is created, a tenant could be evicted only after following due process of law, as prescribed under the provisions of 1999 Act and provisions of 2002 Act could not be used to override provisions of 1999 Act.
