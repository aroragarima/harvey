Rai Sahib Ram Jawaya Kapurand Others v The State Of Punjab(And Connected Petitions)

Supreme Court of India

22 April 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Bijan Kumar Mukherjea

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 16; AIR 1955 SC 549; JT 1991 (4) SC 548; 1955 (2) MLJ(SC) 59; 1955 (57) PLR 444; 1955 SCJ 504; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 225

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Education - Appropriation Act - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(g), 32, 19(6), 31, 298, 266(3), 204, 203, 202, 53(1), 154, 73, 162 and 75 - Government of India Act, 1935, ss. 8 and 49 (2) - Education Department of Punjab Govt. - Monopoly in trade - Nationalisation of text books - Writ of Mandamus - (A) Whether executive of State is competent without any legislative sanction to engage in any trade creating monopoly ? - Held, executive power of a State as per Constitution shall extend to matters with respect to which Legislature of State has power to make laws, executive function comprises both determination of policy as well as carrying it into execution, however executive power of State shall be limited by Constitution or by any law made by Parliament upon Union or authorities thereof - (B) Whether Appropriation Acts are a substitute for specific legislation and that they validate only expenses out of consolidated funds for particular years for which they are passed? - Held, Appropriation Acts cannot be said to give a direct legislative sanction to trade activities themselves - But so long as trade activities are carried on in pursuance of policy which executive Govt. has formulated with tacit support of majority in, legislature, no objection on score of their not being sanctioned by specific legislative provision can possibly be raised - Objections could be raised only in regard to expenditure of public funds for carrying on of trade or business and to these Appropriation Acts would afford a complete answer - (C) Whether fundamental rights of petitioners have been violated by notifications and Acts of executive of Govt. of Punjab undertaken by them in furtherance of their policy of nationalisation of text books for school students? - Held, there is no fundamental right in publishers that any of books printed and published by them should be prescribed as text books by school authorities or if they are once accepted as text books they cannot be stopped or discontinued in future - (D) Whether prospect of having particular customers can be be a right to property and compensation due on its deprivation? - Held, a mere chance or prospect of having particular customers cannot be said to be a right to property or to any interest in an undertaking within the meaning of art. 31(2) of Constitution and no question of payment of compensation can arise because petitioners have been deprived of the same - Petition dismissed.
