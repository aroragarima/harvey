Sunil Sharma & Ors. v Bachitar Singh & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

07 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 280; (2011) 11 SCC 425; 2011 (2) ACC 552; 2011 ACJ 1441; 2011 (4) ALD(SC) 109; 2011 (3) AWC 2904; JT 2011 (4) SC 232; 2011 (2) KLT 451; 2011 (5) MLJ(SC) 205; 2011 (164) PLR 638; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 708; 2012 (1) RLW 419; 2011(4) SCALE 383; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 576

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Enhancement of compensation - Deceased was returned on her scooter, when the offending vehicle driven by the second respondent hit her and ran over her - Deceased was declared dead when taken to the hospital - Appellants (Legal heirs of the deceased) filed a claim petition claiming Rs. 40,00,000/- as compensation along with interest @ 24% p.a. - Tribunal applied multiplier of 11 and awarded compensation of Rs.7,92,000/- along with interest at the rate of 6% p.a. to the appellants - On appeal, HC applied the multiplier of 14 and awarded Rs.2,16,000/- over and above what was awarded by tribunal - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, deductions made by the Tribunal on account of HRA, CCA and medical allowance were done on an incorrect basis - Deduction towards EPF and GIS should also not have been made in calculating the income of the deceased - Respondents were directed to jointly and severally pay total compensation of Rs.22,60,000/- to the appellants with interest @ 6% p.a. - Appeal allowed.
