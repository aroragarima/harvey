Bhusawal Borough Municipality v Amalgamated Electricity Company Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 December 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 221; AIR 1966 SC 1652; 1964 (2) ALT(SC) 262; 1964 (1) KerLR 237; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 905

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Error Of Law

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Bombay Electricity Supply (Licensed Undertakings War Costs) Order, 1944, cl. 5 proviso II - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. XLI r. 27 - Electricity charges - Claim of refund - Decision of Provincial Govt. under cl. 5 Proviso II when both parties stated their respective cases - Appellant contended that dispute between them was never referred to Govt. and document Ex. 68 was mere communication of opinion of Govt. - Undisputed position as to that Provincial Govt. was final authority for deciding question under Proviso II of cl. 5 - High Court in second appeal relied upon Ex. 68 and set aside judgment of Trial Court and First Appellate Court - Contention raised that relying upon Ex. 68 for first time amounted to admission of additional evidence in second appeal to which O. XLI r. 27 was not applicable - Held, contention is untenable - Court observed that Trial Court and District Court had wholly misconstrued Ex. 68 which is not merely of evidentiary value but is one upon which claim of respondent No. 1 for surcharge is based - Misconstruction of such a document is an error of law and HC in second appeal would be entitled to correct it - Impugned judgment upheld - Appeal dismissed.
