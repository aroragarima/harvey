Â Bhikusa Yamasa Kshatriya and Another v Sangamner Akola Taluka Bidi Kamgar Union and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 275; AIR 1963 SC 806; 1963 (6) FLR 74; 1962 (2) LLJ 736; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 524

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Minimum Wages Act, 1948, ss. 3(3)(iv) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(f) - Notification - Payment of minimum wages - Revision - Legality - State Govt. fixed minimum rates of wages payable to workers exercising authority under provisions of the 1948 Act - By notification dt. 19-4-1955, State Govt. extended notification revising minimum wages to appellants/employers - Appellants filed petition before HC praying for writ declaring s. 3(3)(iv) of the 1948 Act and notification dt. 19-4-1955 ultra vires, void and illegal - HC dismissed petition on ground that s. 3(3)(iv) of the 1948 Act and notification dt. 19-4-1955 didn't violate fundamental rights guaranteed by constitution - Hence, instant appeal - Appellants contended that notification dt. 19-4-1955 was discriminatory and violated fundamental right of equality before law guaranteed by Constitution - Held, fixation of rates of wages and revision thereof were manifestly preceded by detailed survey and enquiry and rates were brought into force after full consideration of representations which were made by a section of employers concerned - It would be difficult in circumstances to hold that notification dt. 19-4-1955, which fixed different rates of minimum wages for different localities, was not based on intelligent differential having rational nexus with object of the Act, and thereby violated art. 14 of Constitution - It was obvious that no uncontrolled or arbitrary power was exercised by Govt. - It exercised power to fix rates of minimum wages after considering reports of committees and sub-committees appointed in that behalf and of Advisory Committees and Advisory Board formed for coordinating work of the committees, and revised them after giving full consideration to representations made by employers likely to be affected thereby - It was held that notification dt. 19-4-1955 did not violate art. 14 of Constitution - It was further held that constitution of committees and Advisory Board did not contravene statutory provisions in that behalf prescribed by Legislature - Appeal dismissed.
