S. Shanmugam Pillai and Others v K. Shanmugam Pillai and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 549; (1973) 2 SCC 312; AIR 1972 SC 2069; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 570

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Dedication, Charity

Summary: Hindu Law - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Land & Property - (A) Estoppel - Alienation by widow - (B) Determining factors as to validity of family arrangement - (C) Religious & charitable endowments - Dedication of property whether complete or partial - Determination - Held, presumptive reversioner coming under any one of specified categories is precluded from questioning transaction, when succession opens and when he becomes actual reversioner except when reversioner is a minor - When agreement is entered into bona fide and terms thereto are fair in circumstances of a particular case - Is a question of fact to be determined in each case on terms of relevant document - Appeal dismissed.
