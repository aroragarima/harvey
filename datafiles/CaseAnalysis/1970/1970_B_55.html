Budhsen v State Of U.P.

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 136; (1970) 2 SCC 128; (1970) SCC (Cr) 343; AIR 1970 SC 1321; 1970 CRLJ 1149; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 564; 1970 UJ 689

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, s. 9 - Constitution, art. 136 - Practice and Procedure - (A) Identification evidence - Nature of - Identification parades - Essentials - Value of identification evidence - (B) Apeal by special leave - Supreme Court will not ordinarily interfere with conclusions of fact properly arrived at by High Court on appreciation of evidence except in certain circumstances - Entire case depending on identification of accused found on test identification parades - HC not appreciating evidentiary value of parades - And proceeding on erroneous legal assumption that it is substantive evidence and on basis of such evidence alone conviction can be based - Court also ignoring important evidence in regard to manner of ID parades and other connected circumstances - Held, on facts that the test ID parades in the case could not be considered to provide safe and trustworthy evidence on which the conviction of accused could be sustained - SC will discard such legally infirm evidence of identification - Appeal Allowed.
