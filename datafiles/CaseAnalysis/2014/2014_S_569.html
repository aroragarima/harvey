State of Karnataka v Suvarnnamma and another

Supreme Court of India

14 October 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 806; (2015) 1 SCC 323; JT 2014 (12) SC 412; 2015 (3) KarLJ 539; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 772; 2014(12) SCALE 292; [2014] 10 S.C.R. 778

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: False Plea, Infirmities, Former Statement

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B and 498A - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, ss. 3, 4 and 6 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Dowry death - Acquittal - Challenged - Prosecution alleged that the deceased (wife) was married to X and was living with X and accused (Mother-in-law) - Deceased was not treated well and was harassed for dowry - Accused brought kerosene can, poured kerosene on the deceased and ignited the fire due to which deceased died - Trial Court convicted accused u/s. 498A and 304-B of IPC and ss. 3, 4 and 6 of Act - On appeal, HC Court reversed the decision of the Trial Court and acquitted the accused - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the overwhelming evidence to prove the demand of dowry has been rejected on account of minor discrepancies about the place at which the negotiations took place or the persons in whose presence demand was made. Such minor contradictions are not enough to discredit the version of demand of dowry. HC has not at all discussed the truthfulness or otherwise of the plea of the accused that though she was at home, she had no knowledge of burn injuries. This stand in her statement u/s. 313 CrPC is clearly false. She was expected to know the incident and make disclosure thereof, absence of which was a circumstance against them. There is objective medical evidence which by itself shifts the burden on the accused to explain circumstances in which burn injuries were caused in her house. Any infirmity in the statement u/s. 313 CrPC could not be treated to be fatal. Impugned judgment of HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.
