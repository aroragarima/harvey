Rustom Kerawalla Foundation v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

03 August 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 587; JT 2017 (8) SC 45

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Fixation, Medical College, Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987, MHADA (Disposal of Land) Regulations, 1982

Summary: Education - Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1987, ss. 2, 3, 3(1), 3(3), 4, 4(2)(a), 4(2)(b), 4(3), 6, 7 - Rent expenditure - Disallowed - Validity - Respondent no. 2/Deputy Director of Education had disallowed the expenses incurred by appellants/School towards rent in respect of school building in the sum of Rs.2.50 crores p.a. - Aggrieved, appellants filed writ petitions before HC challenging impugned orders - HC disposed of writ petitions holding that appellants cannot recover any amount in excess of the amount reworked by respondent no. 2 unless approved by State Govt. - Hence, instant appeals - Whether appellants were entitled to take the entirety of lease rent into account while having the fee structure approved in terms of provisions of the Act.

Held, foundation was always in the picture right since the beginning and was named as affiliated trust in the deed in question. It was only later that sub-lessee came to be inducted to which the Foundation was presently paying rupees 2.50 crores every year towards rent. All the evaluation reports relied upon and placed on record have computed the rent payable on the basis of what was payable in market to similar premises in the locality in question. Evaluation reports have gone on the basis of locational advantages of land, which are nothing but elements depending upon the market value of the locality in question. Since, the lease deed and subsequent sub-lease in instant case was on non-competitive basis and purely under the discretionary quota of State Govt. the locational advantages of the land ought to be completely severed and segregated and what should be the basis for computation should be purely that what was expended "on land and buildings". The expenditure on lands being to the tune of Rs.35.02 lakhs, what the Foundation and the School were entitled to was only that which represents reasonable return on such investment and the facilities therein, or the rent as claimed, whichever is lower. SC remand the matter to State Govt. for fresh consideration and pass appropriate orders within 3 months. However, in case the fees as proposed by appellants were not approved, consequential orders for refund in terms of s. 3(3) of the Act should be made. Appeals disposed of.
