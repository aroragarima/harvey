K. K. Gohil v State of Gujarat and others

Supreme Court of India

12 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Arun Mishra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 556; (2015) 9 SCC 652; 2015 (146) FLR 987; JT 2015 (9) SC 536; 2015 (6) MLJ 499; 2015(8) SCALE 673; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 788; 2015 (6) SLR 776

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Higher Grade, Revision of Pay Rules, 1998

Summary: Service - Entitlement to high grade scale - Failure to grant benefit - Determination - Appellant joined service on 16-11 1989 as peon in respondent Department and was later promoted - Appellant had completed nine years of service and was granted first higher grade scale - However, appellant failed to meet one of requirement of scheme i.e. passing of departmental examination, which appellant had not been able to clear because such examination was not conducted at all by department - Further, authorities held that even if department has not conducted examination, it is disqualification of employee concerned to be eligible to get higher grade scale and same was withdrawn - Further, appeal filed by appellant was dismissed - Whether appellant is entitled for higher grade scale under Scheme.

Held, as per Govt. resolution dt. 16-8-1994, it is manifest that grant of higher grade scale to eligible employees who have completed nine years of service is permissible, provided that employee is eligible to get promotion on basis of his overall performance, qualifications and passing examination if prescribed. It is also material that if employee gets higher grade scale without passing any competitive examination, he will have to clear departmental examination otherwise grant of higher grade scale is to be withdrawn - However, by circular dt. 24-11-2004, State Govt. modified earlier Resolution taking note of HC's order and directed that in cases where for getting higher pay scales departmental examination is necessary then in such cases it is equally necessary that departmental examination should be organised in time. Further by Govt. Order dt. 22-6-2006, it was specifically brought to notice of Department that if higher departmental examination is not organised during eligibility period for getting higher pay scales then in such case higher pay scale benefit cannot be stalled on such ground. SC held that in present case, higher pay scale was ordered to be granted to appellant after completion of nine years but same was withdrawn on basis of earlier circular of 1994. HC failed to consider subsequent circular of 2004 and based on circular of 1994, order withdrawing benefit was upheld. Hence, impugned order passed by lower court is set aside. Hence, appellant is entitled to higher pay scale on completion of nine years of service. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Failure to take in to consideration subsequent circulars and decisions of Govt. granting benefit to employee is not justified.
