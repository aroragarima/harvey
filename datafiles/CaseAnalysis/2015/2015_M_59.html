Mangalore Ganesh Beedi Works v Commissioner of Income Tax and another

Supreme Court of India

15 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 734; (2016) 2 SCC 556; [2015] 378 ITR 640; JT 2015 (9) SC 523; 2015(10) SCALE 701

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Partnership, Association Of Persons, Goodwill, Partnership Firm, Legal Expenses

Summary: (A) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, s. 37 - Legal expenses incurred - Deduction claimed - Justification - Assessee started business of manufacturing beedis which was later converted into partnership firm - However, due to differences, partnership firm was wound up vide order of HC - Further, HC directed sale of partnership firm as going concern to highest bidder amongst partners - Auction was conducted in which three of erstwhile partners forming association of persons emerged as highest bidders which was accepted by OL - Further, assessee firm filed return and claimed deduction as revenue expenditure permissible u/s. 37 of Act towards legal expenses incurred - Further, assessee also claimed depreciation u/ss. 35A and 35AB of Act towards acquisition of Intellectual Property Rights such as rights over trademark, copyright and technical know-how - Further, assessee claimed depreciation on capitalizing value of Intellectual Property Rights by treating them as plant - AO passed order rejecting claim of assessee under all three heads - Further, appeal was allowed in part and it was held that assessee was entitled to deduction towards legal expenses - However, claim of assessee regarding deduction or depreciation on Intellectual Property Rights was rejected by appellant authority - Further, Tribunal allowed appeal of assessee and rejected appeal of Revenue - However, HC vide impugned order reversed order passed by Tribunal - Whether amount claimed as revenue expenditure by Association of persons which was constituted by three partners of erstwhile firm, can be allowed as permissible deduction in hands of said Association of persons u/s. 37 of Act.

Held, Tribunal allowed case of assessee and held that concern was going concern, hence, legal expenses incurred were for defending business of going concern and for protecting its interests. It could not be said that expenses were personal in nature, nor could it be said that expenses were unreasonable or not bona fide. However, HC reversed order passed by Tribunal. SC held that there is clear finding of fact by Tribunal that legal expenses incurred by assessee were for protecting its business and that expenses were incurred after 18-11-1994. There is no reason to reverse finding of fact particularly since nothing has been shown to conclude that finding of fact was perverse in any manner whatsoever. Further, if finding of fact arrived at by Tribunal were to be set aside, specific question regarding perverse finding of fact ought to have been framed by HC. However, Revenue did not seek framing of any such question. Hence, SC held that HC was not justified in upsetting finding of fact arrived at by Tribunal, particularly in absence of substantial question of law being framed. Hence, impugned judgment passed by HC is set aside and order passed by Tribunal in favor of assessee is restored. Appeals disposed of.

(B) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 32, 35 A, 35 AB, 43(3) - Acquistion of Intellectual Property - Depreciation claimed - Justification - Whether assessee was entitled to claim any deduction on alleged expenditure for acquisition of patent (trademarks) rights, copyrights and know-how, in terms of s. 35A and 35AB of Act.

Held, Tribunal accepted after detailed discussion contention of assessee that trademarks, copyrights and technical know-how alone were comprised in assets of business and not goodwill. Further, when revenue alleges that it is goodwill and not trademarks etc. that is transferred, onus will be on revenue to prove it, which it was unable to do. Further, Tribunal held that assets are self-generated and would not attract capital gains. Further, decision of Tribunal has been accepted by Revenue and there is no reason why different conclusion should be arrived at in so far as assessee is concerned. However, HC denied any benefit to assessee u/s. 35A and s. 35AB of Act since it was held that what was auctioned off was only goodwill and no amount was spent by association towards acquisition of trademarks, copyrights and know-how. SC opined to leave open question of applicability of s. 35A and s. 35AB of Act for appropriate case because revenue submitted that if assessee is given benefit of s. 32 read with s. 43(3) of Act as has been done by Tribunal, assessee would be quite satisfied. Appeals disposed of.

(C) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 32, 43(3) - Expenditure incurred for acquisition of Intellectual Property - Claim of Benefit - Determination - Whether assessee is entitled to any benefit u/s. 32 read with s. 43(3) of Act thereof for expenditure incurred on acquisition of trademarks, copyrights and know-how.

Held, so far as assessee is concerned, trademarks, copyrights and know-how acquired by it would come within definition of 'plant' being commercially necessary and essential. Further, s. 32 of Act as it stood at relevant time did not make any distinction between tangible and intangible assets for purposes of depreciation. Further, distinction came in by way of amendment after A/Y. is concerned in present case. Hence, assessee is entitled to benefit of depreciation on plant i.e. on trademarks, copyrights and know-how in terms of s. 32 of Act as it was at relevant time. Hence, SC is in agreement with view taken by Tribunal in this regard that assessee would be entitled to benefit of s. 32 read with s. 43(3) of Act thereof. Further, by denying that trademarks were auctioned to highest bidder, Revenue is seeking to re-write clause of agreement between erstwhile partners of firm. Hence, it is difficult to appreciate that trademarks were not auctioned off and only goodwill in erstwhile firm was auctioned off. Hence, assessee is entitle to benefit u/s. 32 read with s. 43(3) of Act thereof for expenditure incurred on acquisition of trademarks, copyrights and know-how. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Assessee is entitled to claim deductions and other benefits under Act when same such deduction are allowed under statute.
