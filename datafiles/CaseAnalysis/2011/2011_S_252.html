Sudam @ Rahul Kaniram Jadhav v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 442; (2011) 7 SCC 125; 2011 CRLJ 4240; JT 2011 (7) SC 335; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 411; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 526; 2011(7) SCALE 415; [2011] 6 S.C.R. 1104

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 302 - Murder - Circumstantial evidence - Conviction - Death sentence - Sustainability - Appellant allegedly murdered four children and a woman - Trial Court convicted the appellant for murder and sentenced him to death - HC, in appeal, confirmed conviction and death sentence - Whether the order of conviction passed against the appellant was sustainable - Held, evidence of PW5, mother of the deceased woman and PW6, first wife of appellant clearly evidenced that the deceased woman along with the four children were living with the appellant - Evidence of PW8 revealed that deceased woman and children were last seen alive with the appellant - Further, appellant had also made extra-judicial confession before PW6, and PW9 that he had committed the murder on account of the harassment meted out to him by deceased woman - Evidence of PWs. 5, 6, 8 indicated that the appellant had motive to commit the crime - Further, appellant absconded and he was unable to explain how the woman with whom he was living as husband and wife and the children met the homicidal death - Guilt on the basis of the circumstantial evidence the prosecution was required to establish that the circumstances proved lead to one and the only conclusion towards the guilt of the accused - Circumstances proved in the instant case led to one and the only conclusion that the appellant had committed the murder of all the five persons - Hence, impugned conviction recorded by the Trial Court was upheld - (B) Whether the instant case would fall in the category of rarest of rare case and thereby justifiable in awarding death sentence to appellant - Held, manner in which the crime had been committed clearly showed it to be premeditated and well planned - It seemed that all the four children and the woman were brought near a pond in planned manner, strangulated to death and dead bodies of the children thrown in the pond to conceal the crime - Appellant not only killed the woman but crushed her head to avoid identification - Killing four children, tying the dead bodies in bundles of two each and throwing them in the pond would not have been possible, had the appellant not meticulously planned the murders - It showed that the crime had been committed in a beastly, extremely brutal, barbaric and grotesque manner - It had resulted into intense and extreme indignation of the community and shocked the collective conscience of the society - Appellant was a menace to the society who could not be reformed - Lesser punishment should be fraught with danger as it may expose the society to peril once again at the hands of the appellant - Case in hand fell in the category of the rarest of the rare cases and the Trial Court did not err in awarding the death sentence and the High Court confirming the same - Death sentence of the appellant was upheld - Appeal dismissed.
