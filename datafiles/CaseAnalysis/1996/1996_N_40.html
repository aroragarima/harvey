Anthony C. Leo v Nandlal Bal Krishnan and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1778; (1996) 11 SCC 376; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 494; AIR 1997 SC 173; AIR 1996 SCW 4338; 1996 (4) CCC 237; JT 1996 (9) SC 672; 1997 (1) RCR(Rent) 301; 1997 (1) RentLR 504; 1996(7) SCALE 829; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 669; 1996 (8) Supreme 493

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Distribution Of Assets

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Bombay Rent Control Act - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Respondents, partners obtained a lease of plot from Municipal Corporation of Greater Bombay - Constructed a building on said plot - On dispute receiver appointed by High Court - Appellant claims tenancy in respect of a room in said premises - Tenanted premises had already in it lofts and two stand-like boxes attached on outer wall - Contended that to evict Appellants report submitted by receiver of illegality of such constructions and serving of liquor in premises - What is role of receiver and what is extent of his jurisdiction? - Held, receiver is appointed by court when court entertains a view that for preservation of properties in suit, till rights of parties to suit are finally adjudicated, such properties should be preserved by exercising control and supervision of same through officer of court, receiver - Where a receiver appointed by court is in actual physical possession of a property, no one, whoever he may be, can disturb possession of receiver - Rule that receiver's possession will not be disturbed without leave of court is, however, not applicable if receiver is not in actual physical possession of property - Since properties in a suit is being managed, maintained and administered by court through receiver, receiver is under an obligation to take all reasonable steps for preservation and maintenance of such properties - If for such preservation, action in civil or criminal court is necessary, receiver is to draw attention of court of relevant facts necessitating such legal action and take leave of court to institute appropriate legal proceedings for preservation of property - O. 40, r. 1(2), CPC, 1908 maintains that court and its officer, receiver, does not possess any right higher than right a party to suit possesses - Where a Rent Act is applicable, inter se rights and obligations of landlord and tenant are regulated and controlled by such Rent Act - In dealing with rights and obligations which a third party may have in respect of a property in which a receiver has been appointed, receiver, like a party to suit, will have same limitation - Therefore, there is no manner of doubt that no order for eviction of tenant can be passed by court at instance of its officer, receiver, without taking recourse to appropriate proceedings for eviction of tenant under appropriate statute regulating and governing inter se rights of landlord and tenant - If a tenant resorts to unauthorised and illegal activity in respect of tenanted premises when such premises is in custodia legis, for prevention of such illegal and unauthorised activities not consistent with any right flowing from incidence of his tenancy, it may not be necessary to institute a suit for preventing tenant from such illegal activities; but court, being apprised by receiver of such illegal activities of a tenant will be within its right to pass suitable order or direction against tenant for prevention of illegal and unauthorised activities after giving tenant reasonable opportunity to place his defences - Since court must be presumed to be fully unbiased in deciding allegation of unauthorised and illegal activities of a tenant necessity of adjudication of such dispute by another court by bringing a legal action before it is neither necessary nor expedient - Question of summary adjudication by court appointing receiver or relegating receiver to a regular suit for adjudication of dispute concerning third party will depend on nature of dispute and defence claimed by third party - Appellant stated that structures in question were there before his induction as a tenant - Appellant has also claimed right to operate in a portion of tenanted premises a permit room for serving liquor - Such contentions should not be decided in a summary proceeding to dispose of reports of receiver or a complaint by a party to suit about alleged illegal activities by a tenant in a property in suit - Appeal allowed.
