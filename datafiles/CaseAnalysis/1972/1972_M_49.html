Manu Bhusan Roy Prodhan v State of West Bengal and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, Y.V. Chandrachud, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 681; (1973) SCC (Cr) 469; (1973) 3 SCC 663; AIR 1973 SC 295; 1974 CRLJ 401; 1973 CrLR(SC) 41; [1973] 2 S.C.R. 842

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: communal harmony, communal violence, communalism

Summary: Criminal - Maintenance of Internal Security Act, 1971, s. 3(1)(a)(ii) - Detention - Vague Ground - Solitary assault on an individual - Prosecution failed to show nature of weapon used, injuries inflicted, motive of assault or how long after assault injured person died - Charged of disturbing public peace - (A)Whether murderous assault on an individual would disturb public order? - Held,solitary assault on one individual which may well be equated with an ordinary murder can hardly be said to disturb public peace or place public order in jeopardy, so as to bring case within purview of Act of 1971 - It can only raise a law and order problem and no more, its impact on society as a whole cannot be considered to be so extensive, widespread and forceful as to disturb normal life of community - (B) Whether an order of detention can be sustained where one of two grounds of detention is no relation to public order? - Held,where one of two grounds on which detention order is based has no relevance or relation to disturbance of public order but that ground is not of unessential nature and its exclusion from consideration might reasonably have affected subjective satisfaction of detaining authority -Held, detention cannot be sustained though other ground is quite germane to problem of maintenance of public order - Petition allowed.
