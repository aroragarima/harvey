Commissioner of Income Tax, Kerala v Thayaballi Mulla Jeevaji Kapasi (Decd.) (By His

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 286; [1967] 66 ITR 147

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Assessment - Notice - Reassessment - Illegal - Respondent submitted return disclosing net business loss - Income-tax Officer computed total income of respondent and brought it to tax - Income-tax Officer by reassessment u/s. 23(3) and s. 34 of Indian Income-tax Act, 1922 , brought to tax additional amount of Rs. 61,036 - Respondent contended that proceedings for reassessment was illegal and invalid because notice u/s. 34(1)(a) was not served on him - Respondent appealed before Appellate Assistant Commissioner was rejected and order of the Income-tax Officer was confirmed - Income-tax Appellate Tribunal set aside order of reassessment holding that there was no proper service of notice - Tribunal referred to HC - HC favoured respondent - Hence instant appeal by appellant - Whether there was any irregularity in service of notice - Held, there was clear evidence on record that process-server affixed on conspicuous part of business premises of respondent - Second notice was affixed and process-server made requisite endorsement - Hence, it cannot affect validity of service - Income-tax Officer held necessary inquiry and declared notice duly served and there was no irregularity in service of notice - Appeal allowed.
