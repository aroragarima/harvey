L. R. Shivaramagowda etc. v T. M. Chandrashekar (Dead) By LRs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 December 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Srinivasan, A.S. Anand, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 870; (1999) 1 SCC 666; AIR 1999 SC 252; 1999 (1) CLT 43; JT 1998 (8) SC 278; 1998(6) SCALE 361; 1998 (10) SLT 33; [1998] Supp3 S.C.R. 241; 1998 (9) Supreme 193

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 100(1)(d)(iv), 123(6), 77(1), 77(2) and 100(1)(b) - Election petition - Allegation, expenditure in excess of prescribed limit by returned candidate - Failure to aver specifically - That due to said corrupt practice or by non-compliance with provisions of Act or of rules result of election has been materially affected - Affidavit - Not disclosing source of information - Held, affidavit not in conformity with prescribed Form No 25 - Hence failure of compliance of r. 94-A of Conduct of Election Rules - Very serious defect - Setting aside of election - Not proper.
