Karan Singh v State Of Haryana & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

28 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 342; (2013) 12 SCC 529; AIR 2013 SC 2348; 2013 CRLJ 3227; JT 2013 (9) SC 23; 2013 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1894; 2013 (3) MLJ(Crl) 103; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 205; 2013(7) SCALE 435; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 1166

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, u/s. 302 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant/accused allegedly attacked deceased with a rope around her neck and dragged her deeper into fields causing her death - Trial Court convicted accused u/s. 302 IPC and sentenced to undergo life imprisonment - On appeal, HC affirmed judgment and sentence of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned judgment and sentence of Trial Court as affirmed by HC was sustainable - Held, no reason for false implication of appellant who being Sarpanch of village was an influential person - PW8 was an independent witness and there was no ground to disregard his testimony - No attempt was made by defence to falsify allegation of non-payment of sum of Rs.47,000/- as consideration for sale of a buffalo by deceased to appellant - Further, there was adequate evidence on record to show that SI (PW9) who conducted investigation at its initial stage had not acted in accordance with law and had favoured appellant - Thus, unless lapses made on part of Investigating authorities were such so as to cast a reasonable doubt on case of prosecution or seriously prejudice defence of accused, Court would not set aside conviction of accused merely on ground of tainted investigation - Appeal dismissed.
