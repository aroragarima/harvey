Girishbhai Dahyabhai Shah v C.C.Jani & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1164; (2009) 15 SCC 64; 2009 (2) FAC 194; 2009 (3) JCC 2419; JT 2009 (12) SC 80; 2009 (3) KLT 596; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 1134; 2009(11) SCALE 5; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 229

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, S. 482 - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954, ss. 13 (1) & (2), 14A, 16(1)(a)(i) - Curd sample collected - Appellant applied for examination of the second sample - Report of Public Analyst served 15 months after the samples had been collected - Whether the appellant was prevented from applying for analysis of the second sample or not? - Held, in Municipal Corporation of Delhi v. Ghisa Ram 1967 (2) SCR 116, only on receipt of the report of the Public Analyst u/ss.(1) to the effect that the article of food is adulterated, can a prosecution be launched and a copy of the report could be supplied to the accused - Appellant was prevented from applying for analysis of the second sample and by that time the second sample of curd had deteriorated and was not capable of being analysed - In absence of any valid and reliable report with regard to the second sample no reason to continue with the proceedings which have lasted for 28 years - Appeal allowed.
