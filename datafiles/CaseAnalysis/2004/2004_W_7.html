Union of India v West Coast Paper Mills Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 311; (2004) 3 SCC 458; AIR 2004 SC 3079; 2004 (3) AWC 2027; 2004 (114) ECR 5; 2004 (166) E.L.T. 290; 2004 (2) JhrCR(SC) 204; 2004 (3) JhrCR(SC) 222; JT 2004 (Supp1) SC 20; 2004 (137) PLR 298; 2004 (2) RCR(Civil) 256; 2004(3) SCALE 23; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 642; 2004 (2) SLT 477; 2004 (3) Supreme 630

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 14(2) - Code Procedure Code, 1908 - (A) Whether suits filed by respondent for refund of amount of freight collected by Railway Administration to extent, it was in violation of declaration given by tribunal, were barred by limitation? - Period lost during pendency of writ proceedings is liable to be excluded from computing period of limitation u/s. 14(2) of Limitation Act - Cause of action arose to plaintiff on 14-10-1970 when dispute came to be finally adjudicated upon by this court and in view of 3-judges bench decision D/- 05-02-2004, there is no manner of doubt that decision of tribunal has merged with decision of this court D/- 14-10-1970 - Held, suit filed by respondent West Coast Paper Mills Limited was within period of limitation - (B) Whether claim preferred by respondents can be said to be a claim for refund of an 'overcharge' - Further held, freight paid by respondents was as per rates notified - Thus present one is not a case of overcharge at all - It is a case of illegal recovery of freight on account of being unreasonable and in violation of s. 28 of Act - Appeals dismissed.
