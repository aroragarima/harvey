Devinder Singh and others v State of Haryana and another

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1647; 2006(6) SCALE 507; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 218

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Haryana Ceiling on Land Holding Act, 1972 - Possession of land - Principal to natural justice - Jurisdiction - Appellants filed suit for declaration to effect that they were owners in possession of equal share of land and order of allotment and declaration of surplus area so far as said land was concerned were ineffective, inoperative and against principles of natural justice, null and void and as such not binding on rights of plaintiffs, however, suit was decreed by Senior Sub Judge - As against appeal filed by respondents was decreed by Additional District Judge - Thereafter, second appeal was carried before HC in which by impugned judgment dismissed appeal - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether suit was clearly entertainable and Civil Court had jurisdiction in appellants case - Held, procedures prescribed in the Act in matter of filing of return, determination of ceiling and selection of permissible area had not been followed - It hade been pointed out that in return filed by father of plaintiffs, age of plaintiffs were clearly mentioned - By time of adjudication plaintiff had attained majority and, therefore, had interest in property - Though plaintiff did not claim any land beyond permissible they had right so far as choice of land was concerned and, therefore, Trial Court had rightly decreed suit - Unfortunately, first Appellate Court and HC lost sight of relevant provisions and held that Civil Court had no jurisdiction - Appeal dismissed.
