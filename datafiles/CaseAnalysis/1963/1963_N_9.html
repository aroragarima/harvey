Nandeshwar Prasad and Another v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 141; AIR 1964 SC 1217; 1965 (1) SCJ 90; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 425

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4 and 6 - Kanpur Urban Area Development Act, 1945 - (A) Whether as acquisition was for scheme No XX of Board, action should have been taken under Kanpur Act and as this was not done entire proceedings are bad including issue of notifications u/ss. 4 and 6? - Held, where acquisition is, as in present case, by Govt. under Land Acquisition Act, for public purpose though that purpose may be purpose of Board, Kanpur Act has no application at all, and Govt. proceeds to acquire under provisions of Land Acquisition Act alone - Contention therefore on behalf of appellants that Kanpur Act has not been complied with and therefore proceedings for acquisition of land are bad has no force and must be rejected - (B) Whether s. 17(4) could not apply in present case and no notification u/s. 6 could be issued unless s. 5-A had been complied with and notification u/s. 6 is bad, even if notification u/s. 4 is good? - Further held, to usual procedure to be followed before notification u/s. 6 is issued, there is an exception u/s. 17, and that is why in s. 6, words 'if any' in clause after considering report, if any, made u/s. 5 A - When action is taken u/s. 17(4), it is not necessary to follow procedure in s. 5-A and a notification u/s 6 can be issued without a report from Collector u/s. 5-A - It is not necessary even where Govt. makes a direction u/s. 17 (1) that it should also make a direction u/s. 17(4) - There has been no change by U P Act in sub-s. (1) and therefore when sub-s. (4) speaks of any land to which sub-s. (1) applies it still refers only to waste or arable land and no other - It was not open to State Govt. to say in notification u/s. 4 that proceedings u/s. 5-A shall not take place - This part of notification u/s. 4 is therefore beyond powers of State Govt. - In consequence notification u/s. 6 also as it was issued without taking action u/s. 5-A must fall - Appeal allowed.
