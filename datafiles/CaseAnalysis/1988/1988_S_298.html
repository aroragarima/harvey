Smt. Kuldip Kaur v Surinder Singh And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

03 November 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.P. Thakkar, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 212; (1989) SCC (Cr) 171; (1989) 1 SCC 405; AIR 1989 SC 232; 1988 (14) ALR 846; 1989 (1) Crimes 1; 1989 CRLJ 794; 1989 CRLR 25; 1989 CrLR(SC) 25; 1989 (1) DMC 1; 1989 (1) HLR 19; JT 1988 (4) SC 412; 1988(2) SCALE 1329; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 762

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 125, 128 and 401 - Whether detaining husband in jail for failing to pay arrears of maintenance passed in wife's favour would suffice in a case where arrears of maintenance allowance still remained unrecovered? - Held, sentencing a person to jail is a mode of enforcement and not a mode of satisfaction of liability - Purpose of sending to jail is to oblige a person liable to pay who refuses to comply with order without sufficient cause - No useful purpose would be served by merely sending husband to jail.
