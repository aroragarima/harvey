Lakshman Exports Limited v Collector Of Central Excise

Supreme Court of India

18 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, N. Santosh Hegde, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1316; (2005) 10 SCC 634; 2002 (143) E.L.T. 21

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 33 and 11 A - Principles of natural justice - Request to cross-examine raw material supplier - Request in reply to show cause notice so as to establish that the goods in question had been accounted for and appropriate amount of excise duty had been paid - Difference of opinion between Vice President and President, CEGAT whether a request for cross-examination was made by the assessee? - Assessee has specifically asked for cross-examination of the representative of two concerns to establish that the goods in question has been accounted for and appropriate amount of excise duty has been paid - Impugned order set aside and case remanded with the direction that the assessing authority shall hear the matter de novo after ensuring that the lacunae pointed out in the order of the Vice President are met - Appeal allowed.
