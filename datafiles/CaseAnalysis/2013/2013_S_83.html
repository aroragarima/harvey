Lakshmibai National Institute Of Physical Education And Another v Shant Kumar Agrawal

Supreme Court of India

11 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 100; (2013) 11 SCC 595; AIR 2013 SC 1544; 2013 (2) AWC 1308; 2013 (2) JabLJ 242; JT 2013 (7) SC 142; 2013(2) SCALE 388; 2013 (3) SLR 690; 2013 (3) UPLBEC 2091

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Central Civil Service (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1965, r. 14 - Uttar Pradesh Fundamental Rule, r. 56 - Registration of Births and Deaths Act, 1969 - Date of birth - Retirement - Challenged - Respondent (employee) was Lower Division Clerk (LDC) worked in respondent (Dept.) - Respondent filed a suit against Board of secondary for direction that his date of birth 15-1-1948 was correct and be recorded in his secondary school certificate (SSC), which was decreed based on certificate issued by local municipality - Aggrieved Board filed an appeal, which was allowed by Lower Appellate Court, as time barred suit - Aggrieved respondent filed second and review appeal before HC, which were dismissed - Thereafter, respondent was appointed in service of appellant no. 1 and claimed that his date of birth as 15-1-1948, based on Trial Court decree - Management of appellant no. 1 was rejected the same and retired respondent from service - Aggrieved respondent filed a petition before HC, which was allowed quashing retirement of respondent based Note 5 of r. 56 of Fundamental Rules and on decree passed by Trial Court - However, respondent was charge sheeted for holding an inquiry u/r. 14 of 1965 Rules - Aggrieved respondent filed a petition before HC, which was dismissed - Aggrieved respondent filed an appeal before DB, which was allowed by impugned judgment holding that management of appellant could not have retired respondent by altering his date of birth basing on SSC and by ignoring certificate issued under 1969 Act - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that after securing employment on the basis of date of birth as 20-2-1942, respondent was estopped from claiming that his correct date of birth was 15-1-1948 - Whether respondent's date of birth as declared by Trial Court, which was reversed by Lower Appellate Court could be relied upon for the purpose of continuing in service beyond age of superannuation - Held, if 15-1-1948 was to be treated as correct date of birth of respondent, then he could not have appointed as LDC on 27-5-1965 - However, fact was that respondent was appointed as LDC and served in that capacity for about 1 year - Respondent could not explain as to how could he be appointed in Govt. service at the age of 17 years and 4 months, as there was provision in service rules which postulate appointment of a minor in Govt. service - Thus, entry made in the birth certificate issued on 25-2-1970 could not be made foundation of a declaration that respondent's correct date of birth was 15-1-1948 - Respondent at the time of joining as LDC did not produce any evidence showing his date of birth as 15-1-1948 - At the time of his appointment in employment of appellant no. 1, respondent did not produce birth certificate dt.25-2-1970 issued under 1969 Act by local municipality - Rather, respondent got date of birth entered in service book by producing copy of judgment of Trial Court, which was set aside by Lower Appellate Court - Respondent suppressed fact that Lower Appellate Court had reversed judgment of Trial Court - Thus, DB committed serious error by setting aside orders passed by Single Judge - Impugned judgment was set aside - Appeal allowed.
