Bhuwalka Steel Industries Limited and another v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

24 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, Abhay Manohar Sapre

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 213; (2017) 5 SCC 598; 2017 (348) E.L.T. 393; 2017(3) SCALE 707

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Excise Duty, Duty Of Excise, Central Excise Act, 1944, Rate Of Duty, Central Excises And Salt Act, 1944, Excisable Goods, Indian Evidence Act, 1872, Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985, Central Excise Rules, 1944, Duty Liability, Enactment, Finance Act, 1997, Hot Re-Rolling Steel Mills Annual Capacity Determination Rules, 1997, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Indirect Tax - Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, ss. 2(d), 3, 3A, 4 - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Hot Re-Rolling Steel Mills Annual Capacity Determination Rules, 1997, rr. 3, 3(3), 5 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 96ZO, 96ZO(3), 96ZP - Determination of Annual Capacity - Order Challenged - Appellants/ manufacturers owns three plants and manufactured goods which were subject to levy of excise duty u/s. 3 of 1944 Act, at rates set forth in I schedule of 1985 Act - Further, in exercise of powers u/s. 3A(2) of 1944 Act, rules were framed by govt. of India vide notification and determined annual capacity of production of factory - Appellants aggrieved by determination approached Tribunal as well as HC - Single Judge of HC dismissed writ petitions of manufacturers and DB of HC dismissed intra court appeals - Hence, instant appeals - Whether s.3A(2) of 1944 Act and/or r. 5 of 1977 Rules, really create fictions.

Held, s. 3 of 1944 Act authorises the levy and collection of a duty of excise on all excisable

goods which are produced or manufactured in India. Both s. 3A(2) of the Act and r. 5 of 1997 Rules deal with the procedure for the determination of the quantum of production of a factory producing 'NOTIFIED GOODS'. Hence, to determine the exact quantum of goods produced by any manufacturer, there are various possible ways:1. constant manual observation or account keeping is the most basic process by which the quantum of goods manufactured could be determined; 2. adoption of a statistical measure for establishing the quantum of goods ;3. by drawing an appropriate presumption having regard to the technical data relating to the machinery employed by the manufacturer etc. Fiction always conflicts with the reality whereas presumption may be proved to be true. It therefore follows that there is no possibility of a fiction being rebutted by evidence. The belief flowing from s. 3A (2) of the Act regarding the annual production of a manufacturer could be rebutted by adducing evidence. S.3A (4) provides for such rebuttal. Therefore, s. 3A (2) of 1944 Act, embodies only a rule of evidence (presumption) but does not create a legal fiction. However, r. 5 of 1997 Rules provides that the determination of the ACP made in accordance with the procedure contained in r.3 of 1997 Rules, is liable for correction in some cases, in the circumstances indicated therein. Further, r. 96ZP(3) of 1944 Rules, deals with the assessment of duty for those assessees who choose to opt for the payment of the excise duty on a monthly basis and further stipulates that an assessee seeking to avail the scheme (concessional rate of duty) u/r. 96ZP(3) is required to make application in the prescribed format. The only similarity between rr. 96ZO(3) and 96ZP(3) of 1944 Rules, is that both the rules seek to eliminate the benefit of the procedure u/s. 3A(4) of the Act, in cases of those assessees who choose to opt for levy and collection of excise duty in accordance with the r.3(3) of the Rules, which are exceptions to the general rules of levy and collection of duties provided u/r. 96ZO and 96ZP. Hence, the judgment in Venus Castings and Supreme Steels require a further examination and the question of law be settled by a Bench of an appropriate strength. Order accordingly.
