Jaspal Kaur Cheema and another v Industrial Trade Links and others

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Abdul Nazeer, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 812; (2017) 8 SCC 592; JT 2017 (7) SC 13; 2017 (5) MLJ 884; 2017(7) SCALE 230

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Estoppel, Indian Evidence Act, 1872, East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1949, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Summary: Rent Control - Eviction - Title of landlord - Validity - Appellants filed eviction petition against respondents from suit premises for personal necessity - Respondents filed an application seeking amendment of written statement for adding the plea that appellants were not the owners of suit premises, which was dismissed by Rent Controller - Respondents filed revision petition before HC challenging the same - HC allowed revision and set aside impugned order of Rent Controller - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, for a contract of tenancy admitting the right of landlord should not be allowed to put his landlord in some inequitable situation taking undue advantage of the position that he got and any probable defect in the title of his landlord. In instant case, it was not disputed by respondents that they were put in possession of the premises as tenants thereof by appellants. In the circumstances, respondents cannot dispute the title of landlord in respect of said premises. The said plea was not raised by respondents in written statement and cannot be permitted to introduce the said plea by way of amendment at such belated stage. Rent Controller was right in rejecting their application for amendment. HC was not justified in allowing the application of respondent to amend their written statement. Impugned order of HC was set aside and that of Rent Controller was restored. Appeals allowed.
