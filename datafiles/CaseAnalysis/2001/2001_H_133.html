Salkia Businessmen's Association and Others v Howrah Municipal Corporation and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20045; (2001) 6 SCC 688; AIR 2001 SC 2790; 2001 (44) ALR 731; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 155; JT 2001 (6) SC 173; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 106; 2001(5) SCALE 114; 2001 (3) SCJ 370; 2001 (5) SLT 686; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 374; 2001 (5) Supreme 803; 2001 (2) UC 426

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative

Summary: Administrative - Issue - What is the efficacy of an order passed by the Court in terms of the memorandum of compromise or an agreement, filed in proceedings on the basis of which the court proceedings came to be finally disposed of? - An order D/- 13-02-1991 came to be passed by a learned Single Judge of the Calcutta High Court in Civil Order No. 14861 (W) of 1990 - In the proceedings, an application for settling the disputes was filed by the parties and the learned counsel appearing for the parties also submitted that the writ petition be disposed of in terms of the said application for settlement - The matter stood disposed - The authorities, instead of abiding by the terms of the orders noticed, indicated that spaces would be allotted to the members of the Association by way of alternative accommodation and that the same be accepted by them on ownership basis - Aggrieved, the appellants moved the HC by means of another writ petition seeking for directions to allot alternative accommodation in terms of the earlier orders of the Court, binding between the parties and not to flout the same - The learned Single Judge, by his order dismissed the writ petition, in limine, as of no merits - Held, HC failed to do justice to its own orders - If Courts are not to honour and implement their own orders, and encourage party litigants - Be they public authorities, to invent methods of their own to short-circuit and give a go-by to the obligations and liabilities incurred by them under orders of the Court - The rule of law will certainly become a casualty in the process - A costly consequence to be zealously averted by all and at any rate by the highest Courts in the States in the Country - It does not require any extraordinary exercise to hold that the memorandum and terms of the compromise in this case became part of the orders of the HC itself when the earlier writ petition was finally disposed of in the terms noticed supra, notwithstanding that there was no verbatim reproduction of the same in the order - The orders passed in this regard were not to be viewed as a matter of mere contract between the parties - They have to be complied with in letter and spirit.
