Jai Kaur and Others v Sher Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 506; AIR 1960 SC 1118; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 975

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 35 - (A) Whether under customary law governing Jats of Grewal Jats in Ludhina to which parties belong, daughter or collaterals are preferential heirs as regards non-ancestral property - (B) Whether this gift is such acceleration of succession in favour of daughters as is permissible under law? - Held, customary law as regards succession to non-ancestral property as recorded generally for Punjab in Paragraph 23 of Rattigan's Digest i.e, daughter is preferred to collaterals, and consequently, second and third appellants were next reversioners to that portion of father's property which has been found to be non-ancestral - (B) In a gift to a stranger, there is no effacement of limited owner nor is there any effacement in respect of property which is retained therefore, no such effacement of limited owner in this case, as would accelerate daughter's rights by converting future contingent right into a present vested right - Hindu Law doctrine of surrender does not therefore, make gift of non-ancestral property to daughters valid beyond widow's life time - Conclusion that this deed of gift in favour of daughter is not valid even as regards non-ancestral property, beyond donor's life time is correct and must be maintained - High Court rightly decreed suit in favour of plaintiffs in respect of non-ancestral property - Appeal dismissed.
