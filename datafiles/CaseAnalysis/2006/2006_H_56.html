Hindustan Zinc Limited v Friends Coal Carbonisation

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 629; (2006) 4 SCC 445; 2006 (2) AWC 2071; JT 2006 (4) SC 392; 2006 (2) RCR(Civil) 619; 2007(1) SCALE 1; 2006 (5) SCJ 294

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Public policy of India - Whether for calculating price escalation, difference should been with reference to base price of washery coal used and not with reference to base price of a lower quality of washery coal? - Neither respondent nor Arbitral Tribunal could assume that contract price of Rs. 2231/- was based on base price of washery grade II as on 08-11-1991 - Having regard to escalation clause, price increase should be with reference to coal that is used - It cannot be worked out by taking difference between higher cost of superior quality coal and lower base price of inferior quality of coal - Set aside judgment of High Court and restore judgment of trial court - Appeal allowed.
