Krishnan And Ors. v State Of Haryana And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 298; (2013) 14 SCC 24; AIR 2013 SC 2139; 2013 CRLJ 2639; 2013 (3) JCC (Narcotics) 109; JT 2013 (9) SC 117; 2013 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1905; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 91; 2013(6) SCALE 663; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 254

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, s. 32A - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 21, 72, 161 - S. 32-A of the Act - Overriding effect - Extent - Appellants filed writ petition before HC questioning the validity of letter issued by Deputy Inspector General (DIG) giving effect to s. 32 of the Act - HC held that provisions of s. 32-A of the Act would have overriding effect, notwithstanding anything contained in CrPC or any other law thus, appellants were not entitled for relief sought - Hence, instant appeal - Whether s. 32A of the Act was violative of arts. 72 and 161 of Constitution - Held, SC while examining similar issue had considered the three Judge Bench judgment in Dadu @Tulsidas v. State of Maharashtra, 2000 Indlaw SC 797 - However, a two judge bench had held a contrary view to the three judge bench decision - While dealing with a similar situation, Constitution bench of SC held that judgment of a co-ordinate Bench or larger Bench was binding - However, if a Bench of two Judges concluded that an earlier judgment of three Judges was so very incorrect that in no circumstances it could be followed, the proper course for it to adopt was to refer the matter to a Bench of three Judges setting out, the reasons why it could not agree with the earlier judgment - If, then, the Bench of three Judges also came to the conclusion that the earlier judgment of a Bench of three Judges was incorrect, reference to a Bench of five Judges was justified - Hence, matter required to be considered by a larger bench, either by a three Judges Bench first or by a five Judges Bench directly - Order accordingly.
