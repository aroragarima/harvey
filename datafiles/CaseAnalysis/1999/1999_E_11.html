Andhra Pradesh State Electricity Board v Easun Engineering Company

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 2039; (2002) 10 SCC 458

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Electricity & Energy - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 133, 134 - Sale of Goods Act, 1930, s. 63 - Cancellation of contract - Claim for damages - Allowed - Challenge - Board floated tender for supply of transformers to that respondent Company submitted its tender which was accepted - Schedule to terms of delivery of transformers was fixed but it appeared that schedule was not adhered to and time for supply of transformers was extended from time to time - Due to certain disputes regarding delivery of material contract was cancelled and bank guarantee which had been given by respondent Company after taking advance from Board was revoked - Board then filed suit in Court for damages with future interest and that suit was decreed in favour of Board - Respondent Company then filed appeal before HC which was allowed and suit of appellant Board was dismissed - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether there was any question of law which was required to be answered by SC - Held, as per available evidence on record, it was not found that if any question of law much less any substantial question of law, if any, arose in instant case, it was for HC to grant certificate to appeal to SC under Arts. 133, 134-A of the Constitution - Further, as per s. 63 of the Act which prescribed that when there was reference to reasonable time, question what was reasonable time was question of fact - But fact remained that whole of evidence was before Court and findings arrived at were on basis of appreciation of evidence - Thus, no question of law did arose - Therefore, it was purely case of appreciation of evidence and HC was not right in granting certificate on mere plea that substantial question of law arose which needed decision by SC - Hence, certificate granted by HC was revoked - Appeal dismissed.
