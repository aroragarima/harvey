Gazi Khan @ Chotia v State Of Rajasthan And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 693; (1990) 3 SCC 459; (1991) SCC (Cr) 24; AIR 1990 SC 1361; 1990 (2) Crimes 482; 1990 CRLJ 1420; 1990 CrLR(SC) 414; 1990 (29) ECC 5; JT 1990 (3) SC 28; 1990(1) SCALE 869; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 831; 1990 (2) ShimLC 107

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Health & Drug

Summary: Criminal - Detention - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - Prevention of Illicit Traffic in Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1988, s. 3 - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985 - Constitution - Petitioner detained as he was involved in illegal business of smuggled charas and heroin and other psychotropic substances - Delay in considering representation of detenu - Counter affidavit filed by police officer who had no idea of facts of case - Held, when allegation of mala fide or abuse of powers or personal bias is attributed to detaining authority said authority should himself swear to counter-affidavit - In absence of any such allegation in petition a counter-affidavit may be sworn by a responsible officer who personally dealt with or proceed case or by an officer duly authorised under rules of business of Govt. concerned - Further held, there is a breach of constitutional obligation as enshrined u/art. 22(5) of Constitution - Detention quashed - Appeal allowed.
