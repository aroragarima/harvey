Rambaran Prosad v Ram Mohit Hazra and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 59; AIR 1967 SC 744; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 293

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Assignees, Special Relief Act, 1963

Summary: Land & Property - Contract & commercial - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 14, 40 and 54 - Special Relief Act, 1963 - Whether covenant for preemption was merely a personal covenant between contracting parties and was not binding against successors interest or assignees of original parties to contract? - It is true that preemption clause does not expressly state that it is binding upon assignees or successors-interest, but, having regard to context and circumstances in which award was made, it is manifest that preemption clause must be construed as binding upon assignees or successors-interest of original contracting parties - A contract in absence of a contrary intention express or implied will be enforceable by and against parties and their legal heirs and LRs. including assignees and transferees - Held, in present case, there is nothing in language of preemption clause or other clauses of award to suggest that parties had any contrary intention - Appeal dismissed.
