D. K. Sharma v Ram Sharan Yadav and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 September 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 589; (1993) Supp2 SCC 117; AIR 1993 SC 95; JT 1992 (5) SC 466; 1992(2) SCALE 482; 1992 (2) UJ 455

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 100 - Elected candidate found to be under statutory disqualification - Declaration of candidate securing second largest Votes as elected - Proof that Voters had notice and were aware of disqualification before they voted, necessary - No Voter stating that he voted in favour of elected candidate despite knowledge of his disqualification - Neither any leaflet, poster, or pamphlet adduced in evidence to show that - Whether Petitioner was entitled to be declared as elected - Held, petitioner was not entitled to be declared as elected - Petition dismissed.
