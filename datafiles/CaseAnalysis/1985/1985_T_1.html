Moped Indian Limited v Assistant Collector of Central Excise, Nellore and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 February 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 451; (1986) 1 SCC 125; 1986 (1) CompLJ 187; 1986 CrLR(SC) 73; 1986 (8) ECC 63; 1986 (7) ECR 333; 1986 (23) E.L.T. 8; 1985(2) SCALE 1379; [1985] Supp1 S.C.R. 954

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Trade Discount

Summary: Excise - Levy of excise duty - Deduction of amount - Entitled to - Appellants manufacture Mopeds and entered into agreements with dealers in connection with sale of Mopeds manufactured by them and agreements were substantially in same terms - Asst. Collector issued a notice to appellants demanding a sum of Rs. 6,96, 177.09 representing amount of differential duty in respect of mopeds manufactured and cleared by appellants from their factory during period 1-10-1975 to 30-4-1979 - Aggrieved, appellants filed writ petition before HC - Appellants contended that they were entitled to deduction of amount of commission in arriving at excisable value of mopeds and demands made by Asst. Collector was not justified - HC uphold the notice issued by Asst. Collector demanding a sum of Rs. 6,96,177.09 representing amount of differential duty in respect of Mopeds manufactured by appellants and cleared from their Factory during period from 1-10-1975 upto 30-4-1979 - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, amount allowed to dealers had referred to in agreement as commission but level given by parties could not be determinative because it was for Court to decide whether amount was trade discount or not, whatever be name given to it - It was clear that agreement was between appellants and dealers on principal to principal basis - However, cls. of agreement which clearly show beyond doubt that under agreement, mopeds were sold by appellants to dealers and dealers did not act as agents of appellants for purpose of effecting sales on behalf of appellants - It was clear from cl.5 (a) of the agreement that bills in respect of mopeds delivered to dealers were to be sent by appellants through their bankers and it was responsibility of dealers to retire bills for purpose of taking delivery of mopeds - Relationship between appellants and dealers was clearly on principal to principal basis and in circumstances it was difficult to see how amount of Rs.11, 145 and 165 allowed to dealers in respect of different varieties of mopeds could be regarded as anything other than trade discount - Appellants charged to dealer price of mopeds sold to them less amount of Rs.110, Rs.145 and Rs.165 in respect of different varieties of mopeds - That amounts allowed to dealers were clearly trade discount liable to be deducted from price charged to dealers for mopeds purpose of arriving at exciseable value of moped - Impugned judgment passed by HC was set aside and notice issued by Asst. Collector was quashed - Appeal allowed.
