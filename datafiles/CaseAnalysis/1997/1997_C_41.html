Hanamanthappa and Another v Chandrashekharappa and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2473; (1997) 9 SCC 688; AIR 1997 SC 1307; 1997 (2) CLT 46; JT 1997 (2) SC 528; 1997 (2) MLJ(SC) 107; 1997(2) SCALE 59; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 846; 1997 (2) Supreme 410; 1997 (1) UJ 808

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6, r. 17, O. 7, r. 10 A - Limitation Act, 1908 - Amendment of plaint by respondents which was returned for presentation to proper court - Can be treated as fresh plaint - Whether it can be dismissed on ground that it contained averments not made in original plaint? - Held, it cannot be dismissed, it is not always necessary to seek amendment of plaint - Petition dismissed.
