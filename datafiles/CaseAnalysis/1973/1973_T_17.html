Lalji and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

14 August 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, A. Alagiriswami

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 177; (1973) SCC (Cr) 921; (1973) SCC (Cr) 923; (1974) 3 SCC 295; AIR 1973 SC 2505; 1973 CRLJ 1769; 1973 CrLR(SC) 589; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 367; 1973 UJ 790

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal -Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 149 and 141 - Formation of unlawful assembly - Defence contended that it was a free fight between two groups - Both factions were already at bad terms - Appellant was constructively liable for act - Whether at a place where unpremeditated free fight happens accused liable to form an unlawful assembly? - Held, that where quarrel arose as a result of remonstrance and counter remonstrance and an unpremeditated free fight takes place it cannot be said that accused who were present there formed an unlawful assembly - Each of accused persons should be held liable for his own act and not vicariously liable for acts of others- Order accordingly.
