Commissioner Of Income Tax, West Bengal-Iii, Calcutta v M/S. Indian Sugar Mills Association

Supreme Court of India

05 November 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

A.C. Gupta, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 102; (1975) 3 SCC 479; AIR 1975 SC 506; 1974 (3) CTR 278; [1974] 97 ITR 486; [1975] 2 S.C.R. 605

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 4(3), cl. (i) - Trade Unions Act, 1926, s.2(h) - Rules of Association, r. 3 - Assessment - Membership of Association - Exemption - Interest - Public utility - Trade Union Association registered under 1926 Act any individual, firm or company owning or managing a sugar mill or factory was eligible for membership of Association - R. 3 of states various objects for which association was established and claimed on behalf of Association that business it carried on was in nature of property under trust or legal obligation to apply income for charitable purposes within meaning of s. 4(3), cl. (i) of 1922 Act - Claim for exemption appears to based on objects mentioned in rr. 3(a)(b) and cl. (a) of r. 4. Cls. (a) (b) of r. 4 regulate application of funds of Association - IO and Tribunal all rejected claim though not exactly for same reasons - Whether income derived by respondent was exempt from tax u/s.4 (3)(i) of 1922 Act - Held, exemption u/s. 4(3) (i) can be claimed if income was wholly for religious car charitable purposes this requirement was satisfied - Association as primary to declare others in apparent conflict with them and rr. 3, 4 and 64, all framed by Association as Trade Union, co-exist - Difficult to accept that only cls. (a) and (b) of r. 3 represent primary objects of Association and other Rules are all ancillary and incidental - It was not possible to agree that Association apprehended income derived from its business wholly for charitable purposes - Other objects cannot also be called charitable purposes within meaning of s. 4 (3) (i), even assuming that in some remote and indirect manner they might be some public utility - Appeal disposed of.
