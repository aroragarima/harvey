Salabuddin Mohamed Yunus v State Of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

28 September 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Madon, Y.V. Chandrachud, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 214; (1984) Supp SCC 399; (1985) SCC (L&S) 53; AIR 1984 SC 1905B; 1985 (50) FLR 83; 1984 LabIC 1712; 1984 (2) LLN 887; 1984(2) SCALE 543; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 930; 1984 (2) SLJ 471; 1985 (3) SLR 55; 1985 UJ 235

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Hyderabad Currency Demonentisation (Consequential and Miscellaneous Provisions) Act 1953, Hyderabad Civil Service Refutations 1954

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution, arts. 136, 311 - Seniority - Appellant joined service in state of Andhra Pradesh prior to coming into force of the Constitution - Retired as a Secretary to the Government of Hyderabad, Legal Department - Whether he was entitled to be paid the salary of a High Court Judge from October 1, 1947, being the date from which the recommendations of a Pay and Service Commission which had been set up had been implemented up to the date of his retirement ? - Held, the State of Andhra Pradesh is to pay to the Appellant the amounts due to him as per the directions given by the Supreme Court in earlier Judgment - Appeal allowed.
