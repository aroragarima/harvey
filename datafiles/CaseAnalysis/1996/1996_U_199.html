Dharambir Singh v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, Faizan Uddin, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1294; (1996) 6 SCC 702; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 301; 1996 (4) CLT 316; JT 1996 (9) SC 698; 1996(7) SCALE 956; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 566; 1997 (5) Supreme 303

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957, ss. 11 and 11(2) - Whether petitioner had any preferential rights in grant of mining lease? - The petitioner had taken the plea that since he alone had discovered the mines, he has got a preferential right over any other person - Held, while granting a prospecting licence or mining lease, the area of discretion has been circumscribed by several factors enumerated in s. 11 - In grant of mining lease of a property of the State, the State Govt. has a discretion to grant or refuse to grant any prospective licence or licence to any applicant and State Govt. is required to exercise its discretion, subject to the requirements of the laws - Further held, no applicant has a right, much less vested right, to the grant of mining lease for mining operations in any place within the State - Therefore, the tribunal of the Central Govt. has rightly held that it being in the area of discretion of the State Govt., merely because the applicant had applied for, the State Govt. was not enjoined to grant the mining lease - Petition dismissed.
