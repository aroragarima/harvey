Prithi v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

27 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 568; (2010) 8 SCC 536; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 2954; JT 2010 (7) SC 567; 2010 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3190; 2010 (4) MLJ(Crl) 776; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 262; 2010(7) SCALE 344; [2010] 9 S.C.R. 33; 2010 (2) UC 1309

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 r/w s. 149 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant allegedly shot the deceased to death - Trial Court convicted the appellant for murder - Appellant challenged the conviction before the HC - HC dismissed appellant's appeal - Whether the prosecution evidence established that the deceased was murdered and the commission of crime was made out against the appellant - Held, testimony of PW9/eye-witness cannot be rejected on the ground that he was a related witness - Presence of PW9 at the time and place of incident was also established from the evidence of PW6 - Evidence of PW9 got corroborated by the recovery of a gun along with empty as well as unused cartridges from the site - Fact that an incident occurred in which PW9 sustained injuries and deceased died was amply established by PW9's evidence - Evidence of PW9 was trustworthy and direct evidence of PW9 leaves no manner of doubt that deceased was dead and the members of the unlawful assembly (including the appellant) armed with deadly weapons were responsible for his death - Evidence of PW9 and the other evidence available on record showed that no error had been committed by the HC in affirming the conviction of the appellant for the offences punishable u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 - Appeal dismissed.
