State of Maharashtra v Raj Kumar

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 244; (1982) 3 SCC 313; (1983) SCC (L&S) 11; AIR 1982 SC 1301; 1982 LabIC 1597; 1982 (2) SLJ 549

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Validity of Recruitment Rules - Weightage given to rural candidate regarding rural job - Whether recruitment rules in question are violative of arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution? - Held, that rules are violative of arts. 14 and 16 as rule was framed with object of taking officers who had full knowledge of rural life, its problems, aptitudes, etc. in order to make a constructive contribution to upliftment of rural life, however, rule when applied did not seem to fulfill object sought to be achieved because any person who has not lived in a village can appear for SSC from a village and become entitled to be selected - Case of rule providing for weightage to a rural candidate - The rules provide that during viva-voce Board would put relevant questions to judge suitability of candidate for working in rural areas and to test his knowledge of rural problems -This being a sufficient safeguard to test ability of candidate express provision for giving weightage would virtually convert merit into demerit and demerit into merit and would be per se violative of art. 14 of Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
