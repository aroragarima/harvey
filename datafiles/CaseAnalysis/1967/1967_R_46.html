Nagpur Electric Light and Power Company, Limited v Regional Director Employees State

Supreme Court of India

02 March 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 137; AIR 1967 SC 1364; 1967 (14) FLR 370; 1967 (2) LLJ 40; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 92

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Factories Act, 1948, s. 2 - Employees State Insurance Act, 1948, ss. 2 (9) and 75 - Mines Act, 1923 - Whether certain employees of Nagpur Electric Light and Power Co. Ltd. are employees within the meaning of s. 2(9) of Employees State Insurance Act? - High Court held, this manufacturing process is carried on by Company not only in building called workshop or receiving station but over whole area over which process of transmission is carried on including sub-stations where electricity is stored and supplied to consumers by further transmission lines - Court cannot accept this line of reasoning - It is a startling proposition that every inch of wide area over which transmission lines are spread is a factory within meaning of s. 2 of Factories Act - Definition of an employee in Employees State Insurance Act is wider than that of a worker in Factories Act - Object of Employees' State Insurance Act is to secure sickness maternity, disablement and medical benefits to employees of factories and their dependents whether working inside factory or establishment or elsewhere - Held, all workers in question including clerks and administrative staff are engaged in connection with this work - None of them is employed in any separate establishment unconnected with work of factory - Some of employees work outside factory, but their duties are connected with work of factory they are therefore employees within meaning of s. 2(9) (i) - Appeals dismissed.
