Kedar Nath and Others v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

08 November 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 366; (1993) Supp1 SCC 7; (1993) SCC (Cr) 276; AIR 1991 SC 1224; 1991 CRLJ 989

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, ss. 201, 300, 411 and 201 - (A) Murder - Proof - Empty cartridge near dead body, only conjecture - Prosecution proceeding due to recovery, on presumption of murder - No other evidence - (B) Receiver of stolen property - Conviction of - No charge u/s. 411 - (C) Causing of disappearance of evidence of offence - Mitigating circumstances for sentence for offence which occured 17 years ago - Appeal pending before Supreme Court for 11 years - Held, evidence relating to charge of murder insufficient and unacceptable - Supreme Court refused to convict in absence of charge for occurrence taking place some 17 years before - Ends of justice would be met, if sentence is reduced to period already undergone - Order accordingly.
