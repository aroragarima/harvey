Vikas Vashishth v Allahabad High Court and others

Supreme Court of India

26 July 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1992; (2004) 13 SCC 485; 2004(8) SCALE 233

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Advocates & Judges - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Judges - En Masse Leave - Disciplinary Action - Maintainability - On 19-4-2004, 25 judges of Punjab and Haryana HC had proceeded on casual leave - Petitioner Claimed that en masse leave of judges amounted to strike - Petitioner prayed in PIL for declaring act of omission and commission on part of judges to go on en masse leave even for an hour was unconstitutional, illegal and misconduct and would amount to strike, hence, respondents-State should make adequate rules which provide mechanism for taking appropriate action against judges at every level who violate law - Hence, instant petition - Whether en masse leave of judges amounted to strike and disciplinary action against judges was maintainable - Held, petition was based entirely on newspaper reports - There was no factual foundation for impleading all HCs in petition - It was writ large that it had been filed without any effort at verifying facts by petitioner personally - Hence, writ petition was entirely misconceived - Petition dismissed.
