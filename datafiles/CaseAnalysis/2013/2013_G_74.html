Gurnaib Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

10 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 727; (2013) 7 SCC 108; 2013 CRLJ 3212; 2013 (3) JCC 1775; JT 2013 (8) SC 1; 2013 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2457; 2013(7) SCALE 89; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 563

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Adjourn

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B, 306 and 498A(a) - Dowry death - Conviction - Challenged - Prosecution alleged that deceased (Wife) had committed suicide because of the cruelty meted out to her by the accused (husband) and his relatives - Trial Court had accepted the evidence of the brother of deceased, PW1, PW4, and PW5 that there was demand of dowry and convicted all the accused persons u/s. 304B of IPC and sentenced each of them to undergo rigorous imprisonment for 7 years and to pay a fine of Rs.10,000/- each - HC partly allowed the appeal filed against said order - HC acquitted X (Father-in-law) but as far as accused was concerned, it modified the sentence by setting aside the fine component - Revision filed against said order was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the deceased was driven to commit suicide because of the harassment meted out to her in connection with demand of dowry - Held, to test the acceptation of the submission, Court have thought it apt to scrutinize the evidence of PWs1, 4 and 5 - PW1, had only made a bald statement that the accused persons were not satisfied with the dowry and were asking deceased to bring a sum of Rs.50,000/- - Similar was the testimony of PWs4 and 5 - That apart, nothing had been stated by the witnesses - It had been deposed by the father that the deceased had written 2 to 3 letters stating about the demand of dowry but the said letters have not brought in evidence - That apart, the brother, in cross-examination, had refuted the same - Thus, on the base of such sketchy evidence, it was difficult to concur with the finding that there was demand of dowry by the husband and the harassment pertained to such a demand - However, basic ingredients of the offence u/s. 306 IPC have been established by the prosecution inasmuch as the death had occurred within 7 years in an abnormal circumstance and the deceased was meted out with mental cruelty - Thus, Court convert the conviction from one u/s. 304B IPC to that u/s. 306 IPC - However, as the accused had spent almost 5 years in custody, Court limit the period of sentence to the period already undergone - Appeal partly allowed.
