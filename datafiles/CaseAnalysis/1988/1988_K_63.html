Collector Of Central Excise, Madras v Kutty Flush Doors and Furniture Company Private

Supreme Court of India

28 March 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 624; (1988) Supp SCC 239; AIR 1988 SC 1164; 1988 CRLR 329; 1988 CrLR(SC) 329; 1988 (36) DLT 12; 1988 (17) ECC 37; 1988 (17) ECR 68; 1988 (35) E.L.T. 6; JT 1988 (2) SC 93; 1988(1) SCALE 705; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 363; 1988 (70) STC 314

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944 - Excise Duty - Central Excise Tariff, item 68 - Appeal us. 35L(b) of the Act - 'Manufacture' - Conversion of timber logs into sawn timber - Tribunal concluding that no new product emerged by sawing of timber - Validity - Tribunal held, had applied correct principles and its conclusion was unassailable - Petition dismissed.
