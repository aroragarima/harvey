Commissioner of Income Tax, Kolkata v Mukundray K. Shah

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 351; (2007) 4 SCC 327; AIR 2007 SC 1963; [2007] 290 ITR 433; JT 2007 (5) SC 363; 2007(5) SCALE 553; 2007 (6) SCJ 920; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 1104; [2007] 160 TAXMAN 276

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Substantially Interested

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 132 - Whether payments made by M. K. Shah Exports Pvt. Ltd. during the Accounting Year ending 31.3.2000 (Assessment Year 2000-01) amounting to Rs.5.99 crores was made to the two firms M/s. M.K. Foundation and M/s. M.K. Industries for the benefit of respondent-assessee? - Held, Tribunal has concluded that the payment routed through MKF and MKI was for the benefit of the assessee - This was a finding of fact - It was not perverse - High Court should not have interfered with the said finding - Appeals allowed.
