Nagarjuna Constn. Company Limited v Government of India and another

Supreme Court of India

09 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Anil R. Dave, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 421; (2013) 1 SCC 721; JT 2012 (11) SC 303; 2012(11) SCALE 101; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 1064; 2012 (28) S.T.R. 561; [2013] 59 VST 119

Case Digest

Subject: Service Tax; VAT

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Value Added Tax Act, 2005, Works Contract (Composition Scheme For Payment of Service Tax) Rules, 2007

Summary: Service Tax - VAT - Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1957 - Andhra Pradesh Value Added Tax Act, 2005 - Works Contract (Composition Scheme for Payment of Service Tax) Rules, 2007, r. 3, 3(3) - Assessment - Composition Scheme - Notification - Eligibility - Sustainability - Appellant-construction company had executed various contracts which were in nature of composite construction contracts and had paid Sales Tax/ VAT on those contract under 1957, Act and 2005, Act - Appellant paid service tax under categories of taxable services - Respondent issued notification whereby service tax was required to be paid only on 33% of total value, subject to condition of non availment of CENVAT credit on inputs or option of composition was offered, appellant wanted to opt for scheme but department issue circular clarifying that Classification of a taxable service was determined based on nature of service provided whereas liability to pay service tax was related to receipt of consideration - Appellant had paid service tax prior to issuance of notification as such he was not entitled to avail Composition Scheme - Appellant filed writ petition before HC, challenging vires of Impugned Circular which was dismissed holding that Circular was wholly in conformity with provisions of r. 3(3) of 2007 Rules and that Impugned Circular merely reiterated eligibility criterion specified in r. 3(3) of 2007 Rules - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether Circular issued was vires and whether appellant was eligible to be taxed under Composition scheme - Held, it was an admitted fact that appellant-assessee had already paid service tax on basis of classification of works contract which was in force prior to 1-7-2007, it could not be said that appellant had exercised a particular option with regard to mode of payment of tax after 1-7-2007 with regard to reclassified works contract, hence not availing of CENVAT credit was absolutely irrelevant - Those who had paid tax as per provisions and classification existing prior to 1-7-2007 and those who opted for payment of tax under provisions of r. 3 of 2007 Rules and paid tax before exercising option belong to different classes and, therefore, it could not be said that Impugned Circular or provisions of r. 3(3) of 2007 Rules were discriminatory - Appeal dismissed.
