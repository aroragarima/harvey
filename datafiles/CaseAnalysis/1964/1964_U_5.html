Â B. N. Tiwari v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 December 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.M. Sikri, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 201; AIR 1965 SC 1430; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 421

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution

Keywords: Central Services Rule of 1952 (as modified in 1955)

Summary: Administrative - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16 - Central Services Rule of 1952 (as modified in 1955) - Schedule castes and scheduled tribes - Carry forward rule - Petitioner was Assistant in Grade IV of Central Secretariat Service - Next post which petitioner expected to get was of Section Officer (Assistant Superintendent) - Petitioner appeared for limited competitive examination and secured 37th position in order of merit - Press communication stated that out of 48 vacancies expected to be filled, 32 were reserved for schedule castes and scheduled tribes and 16 were unreserved - Eventually, only 43 appointments were made - Heavy reservation for scheduled castes and scheduled tribes was based on 'carry forward' rule which was put into force from 1955 - In 1952 supplementary instructions were issued, and thereafter further changes were made in1955 - Hence, rule had not provided for any lapse but said that unutilised quota was not to be carried forward for more than two years - Result of substitution of the 1955 rule was that paras. 5(3) and 5(4) of the 1952 rule ceased to exist - Devadasan's case, relied on - Petitioner contended that there was no carry forward rule in existence as the 1955 carry forward rule was struck down by Court and the 1952 rule had ceased to exist by substitution made by Govt. of India in 1955 - Petitioner submitted that if carry forward rule of 1952 was still deemed to exist that rule was bad being violative of art. 16 of Constitution - Petitioner finally contended that carry only reservation that was possible in examination of 1960 was 12 1/2% for scheduled castes and 5 % for scheduled tribes, thus entitled him to be appointed on that basis - Petitioner sought direction that appointments of certain candidates belonging to scheduled castes and scheduled tribes over and above the reserved quota of 171 % be set aside and Union Public Service Commission was to be directed to announce result of said examination afresh after receiving 12 1/2 % of vacancies for scheduled castes and 5% for scheduled tribes - Hence, instant petition - Whether the carry forward rule of 1952 can still be said to exist although it was bad for reasons stated in Devadasan's case and petitioner entitlement to appointment even if carry forward rule of 1952 had not existed - Held, by promulgating new carry forward rule in 1955, Govt. cancelled carry forward rule of 1952 - However, when Court struck down carry forward rule as modified in 1955, it had not meant that carry forward rule of 1952 was revived - After judgment of this Court in Devadasan's case, no carry forward rule at all - However, judgment was only concerned with that part of instructions which dealt with carry forward rule; in no manner had it addressed reservation for scheduled castes and scheduled tribes - Adjustment in reservation between scheduled castes and tribes had nothing to do with carry forward rule from year to year - As rule no longer existed its constitutional validity was not to be decided - According to calculation 43 vacancies were actually filled, out of which 9 were to go to scheduled castes and scheduled tribes together, and 34 were to go to unreserved quota - Petitioner was 36th on unreserved quota and therefore even on basis of there being no carry forward rule only 34 candidates were to be appointed from unreserved quota and petitioner being 36th on his own showing could not claim appointment - No order as to costs - Petition dismissed.
