State of West Bengal v Kailash Chandra Pandey

Supreme Court of India

13 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 867; (2004) 12 SCC 29; 2005 CRLJ 135; 2004 (4) RCR(Criminal) 743; 2004(8) SCALE 749; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 437

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Cross-Examination, Eye-Witness, Charge - Sheet

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 7 - Conviction awarded by Trial Court - Reversal by High Court - Whether proper? - Held, there is no reason to disbelieve entire prosecution story when a man accepts anything in right hand in normal course of human conduct and if he has kept money in left hand pocket prosecution cannot be held responsible - Accused received currency notes and hand wash of water turned into pink and left hand pocket of trouser was also washed and colour of the water also turned into pink, putting these two evidence together, there remains no doubt about prosecution case - Sufficient, cogent and reliable evidence is available on record which fully established guilt of accused - Appeal allowed.
