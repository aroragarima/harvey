Mahatma Education Society's Pillai's Institute of Information Technology, Engineering, Media Studies and Research v All India Council for Technical Education and others

Supreme Court of India

16 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Anil R. Dave, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 97; JT 2015 (2) SC 477; 2015(2) SCALE 418; 2015 (3) SLR 471

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: the Regulations, All India Council for Technical Education (Grant of Approvals for the Technical Institutions) Regulations, 2012

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Education - All India Council for Technical Education (Grant of Approvals for the Technical Institutions) Regulations, 2012 - Grant of approval - Educational institutions - Petitioner society was imparting education to students through its colleges for the last 15 years - However, petitioner was not having land as per the provisions of the Regulations, therefore, approval was denied for the academic year 2014-15 - Petition was filed before HC, which was disposed of with observation that it was open to respondent no.1 Council to take appropriate action in the matter - Council did not grant approval to the institutions run by the petitioner as Regulation had not been complied with - Hence, instant petition.

Held, petitioner society has been imparting education to students through its colleges for the last 15 years. If approval is not granted, the students, who have already been admitted by an interim order of the HC for the academic year 2014-15, would be put to great inconvenience and difficulties for no fault on their part. In the circumstances, as an exceptional case, respondent no.1 directed to grant a letter of approval to the concerned colleges managed by the petitioner for the academic year 2014-15. Petition allowed.
