Commissioner of Customs and Central Excise, Meerut v Pawan Kumar Gupta

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2243; (2010) 15 SCC 327; 2011 (271) E.L.T. 10

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Indirect Tax - Customs - Customs Act, 1962, s. 112(b) - Evasion of customs duty - Levy of penalty - On allegations of mis-declaration of value and quantity in imports to evade customs duty, a show cause notice was issued respondents - Adjudicating authority imposed penalties u/s.112(b) of Act on ground that both respondents were dealing with goods which were liable for confiscation - Tribunal held that respondents were not liable to pay any penalty and penalty imposed on them by adjudicating authority was set aside - Hence instant appeals.

Held, show cause notice was issued to respondents and their statements were recorded and immediately thereafter they retracted from their statements. Even according to appellant-Commissioner of Customs the respondents were to get Rs. 1.50 paise for each bearing sold in local market. It was contended on behalf of respondents that they were not aware about nature of goods. As far as respondent âTâ is concerned, goods were never received by him nor did he ever dealt with them to make him liable for a penal action u/s. 112(b) of Act. There is no allegation that âTâ ever acquired possession of these goods. In view of Court, as far as âTâ is concerned, no interference is called for and appeal filed by Commissioner of Customs against him being devoid of merit is dismissed. In respect of another respondent âPâ, allegation was that he was present at premises of M/s. âDâ and was sorting out ball bearings and he was to issue challan in name of bogus firm. Tribunal found that case of âPâ is different from that of âTâ and found him guilty u/s. 112(b) of Act but on consideration of totality of facts and circumstances, penalty imposed was reduced from 1.5 crores to 5 lakhs. In facts and circumstances of case, no interference is called for. Appeal filed against âPâ is also dismissed. Appeals disposed of.
