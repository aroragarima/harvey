Narinder S. Chadha and others v Municipal Corporation of Greater Mumbai and others

Supreme Court of India

08 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 830; AIR 2015 SC 756; 2015 (1) Bom.C.R. 188; 2014 (4) CLT(SC) 321; JT 2014 (13) SC 471; 2015 (1) OLR 278; 2014(13) SCALE 575

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Cigarettes And Other Tobacco Products (Prohibition Of Advertisement And Regulation Of Trade And Commerce, Production, Supply And Distribution) Act, 2003, Prohibition of Smoking in Public Places Rules, 2008

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution - Cigarettes and other Tobacco Products (Prohibition of Advertisement and Regulation of Trade and Commerce, Production, Supply & Distribution) Act, 2003, s. 6 - Prohibition of Smoking in Public Places Rules, 2008, rr. 2 and 4 - Legality of Circular - Instant appeals filed against the order passed by HC holding that the impugned circular dt. 4-7-2011 only implemented the Act and the Rules and dismissed the challenge to the said circular - Whether the impugned circular dt. 4-7-2011 travels outside the Act and the Rules or merely seeks to implement the said Act and the Rules as they stand.

Held, the Act was really in implementation of World Health Assembly Resolutions and was enacted to put a total ban on advertising of tobacco products and to prevent the sale of tobacco products to minors. S. 6 of the Act permits the sale of cigarettes and any other tobacco products, except to persons under 18 years of age and in an area within a radius of 100 yards of any educational institution. It is clear that any condition which prohibits the sale of cigarettes or any other tobacco products in premises licenced by the Municipal Corporation would amount to adding another exception which would be impermissible in law. Further, where smoking is allowed in a smoking area or space, r. 4(3) of Rules makes it clear that such place can be used for the purpose of 'smoking'. U/r. 2(f) of the Rules words and expressions not defined in the Rules but defined in the Act shall have the meanings, respectively, assigned to them in the Act. 'Smoking' with a Hookah would be permissible u/r. 4(3) of the Rules and the expression 'no other service shall be allowed' refers to services other than the providing of a Hookah. It is, thus, evident that the added words in cl. (C) of Condition No.35 are clearly ultra vires the Act and the Rules. Hence, HC judgment set aside and first paragraph of Condition No.35 and the added words in (C) of Condition No.35 of the impugned circular deleted. Appeals allowed.
