Murari Lal Gupta v Gopi Singh

Supreme Court of India

25 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, G.P. Mathur, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1962; (2005) 13 SCC 699

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 406 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 ss. 406, 420 - Agreement - Breach - Respondent filed complaint against petitioner u/ss. 406, 420 IPC for specific performance of agreement - Trial court directed respondent to file civil suit - On appeal, complaint was dismissed- Aggrieved respondent filed revision which was allowed - Hence, instant transfer petition.

Held, that criminal proceedings initiated by the respondent against the petitioner are wholly unwarranted and complaint is an abuse of the process of the court and the proceedings are, therefore, liable to be quashed. - Merely because an agreement to sell was entered into which the petitioner failed to honour, it cannot be said that the petitioner has cheated the respondent and prima facie no case for prosecution u/s. 420 of CrPC or u/s. 406 of IPC is made out. - Thus, complaint filed by the respondent and that too at Madhepura against the petitioner, who is a resident of Delhi, seems to be an attempt to pressurise the petitioner for coming to terms with the respondent is directed to be quashed. - Petition allowed.
