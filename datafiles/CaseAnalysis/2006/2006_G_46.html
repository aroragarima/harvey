Government of Andhra Pradesh and Others v A. Venkata Rayudu

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 873; (2007) 1 SCC 338; 2006 (6) AWC 5440; 2006(11) SCALE 264; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 73

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Service - Dismissal - Validity of Andhra Pradesh C.S. (CCA) Rules, r. 24(1) - Respondent worked as General Manager - Charges were framed against him - Enquiry - Show cause notice issued - Dismissed from service - Tribunal found that in case of misconduct committed by employees jointly, enquiry ought to have been conducted jointly as required u/r. 24(1) of A.P.C.S. (CCA) Rules - Tribunal found that order of dismissal was not sustainable and accordingly was set aside - Challenged - It is a settled principle of natural justice that if any material is sought to be used in an enquiry, then copies of that material should be supplied to party against whom such enquiry is held - Charge-sheet should not be vague but should be specific - In Charge No. 1, what is mentioned is that respondent violated Orders issued by Govt. - However, no details of these orders have been mentioned in Charge No. 1 - Copies of said G.Os or directions of Govt. were not even placed before Enquiry Officer - Hence, Charge No. 1 was not specific and hence no finding of guilt can be fixed on basis of that Charge - Respondent cannot be found guilty for offence charged - Appeal dismissed.
