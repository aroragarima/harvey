Chairman School of Buddhist Philosophy,Leh,Ladakh(J and K) v Makhan Lal Matto And Anr

Supreme Court of India

25 July 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 351; (1990) 4 SCC 6; (1990) SCC (L&S) 558; 1990 (14) ATC 836; 1990 (61) FLR 614; JT 1990 (3) SC 339; 1991 (1) LLJ 610; 1990 (2) LLN 1010; 1990(2) SCALE 139; [1990] 3 S.C.R. 515; 1990 (4) SLR 741; 1990 (2) UJ 459

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution Of India 1950, arts. 12 and 16 - Whether petitioner is entitled to advertise post of principal on basis of revised qualification? - Held, Board of Management is fully competent to alter or amend rules in any manner and at any time - Affidavit by Chairman of Board of management who is Additional Secretary to Govt. of India to effect that rules were amended in 1978 with approval of Govt. of India, should have put an end to controversy - Qualifications for post of Principal of School stood validly revised - Advertisement issued on January 5, 1982 was in accordance with Rules and High Court was not justified in quashing same - Appeal allowed.
