State Of Maharashtra v Abu Salem Abdul Kayyum Ansari & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

05 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 812; (2010) 10 SCC 179; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 280; 2011 CRLJ 1; JT 2010 (11) SC 65; 2010 (4) MLJ(Crl) 336; 2010 (4) RCR(Criminal) 633; 2010(10) SCALE 514; [2010] 12 S.C.R. 204; 2010 (3) UC 1877

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Approver

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 307 and 308 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, s. 19 - Right to cross examine an accomplice - A builder was allegedly murder by hired gangsters and an attempted to murder was made on his brother - After completion of investigation, certain persons were arrested - Respondents could not be tried as they were absconding - Designated Court acquitted the accused who were tried - On appeal, SC partly set aside the judgment of the Designated Court and convicted some of the accused who were tried - Thereafter, respondent No. 1 was arrested by Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) in another case and subsequently he was taken into custody by Anti Terrorist Squad in connection with the murder case - Police submitted charge sheet against the respondent nos. 1 to 5 - Respondent no. 3 expressed his desire to disclose truly and fully the facts pertaining to conspiracy which had taken place at Dubai in connection with the murder - Thereafter, Investigating Officer made an application u/s. 307 of CrPC before the Designated Court that respondent no. 3 may be pardoned due to his readiness and willingness to disclose true and correct facts about the conspiracy hatched by them for the murder - Designated Court tendered pardon to the respondent no. 3 and permitted the prosecution to examine him as witness in the trial - Respondent no. 3 (approver) was called as witness for the prosecution; Special Public Prosecutor put a specific question to the approver as to whether he wanted to tell the Court about the conspiracy of murder to which his answer was in the negative - Immediately, Special Public Prosecutor issued a certificate u/s. 308 of CrPC that the approver has not complied with the condition on which pardon was tendered to him and, therefore, he may be tried separately - However, Designated Court granted permission to the defence to cross examine respondent no. 3 - Hence, present appeal - Whether, the accused has a right to cross examine an accomplice who was tendered in evidence by the prosecution as approver but later on pardon tendered to him was withdrawn on a certificate of the Public Prosecutor u/s. 308 of CrPC and he (approver) was not been further examined by the prosecution as its witness? - Held, once an accomplice is granted pardon, he stands discharged as an accused and becomes witness for the prosecution - Further, once the pardon is withdrawn or forfeited on the certificate given by the Public Prosecutor that such person has failed to comply with the condition on which the tender was made, he is reverted to the position of an accused and liable to be tried separately and the evidence given by him, if any, has to be ignored in toto and does not remain legal evidence for consideration in the trial against the co-accused, albeit such evidence may be used against him in the separate trial where he gets an opportunity to show that he complied with the condition of pardon - He cannot be compelled to be a witness - There is no question of such person being further examined for the prosecution and, therefore, no occasion arises for the defence to cross examine him - In the present case, Designated Court erred in treating the respondent no. 3 hostile witness; it failed to consider that once the pardon granted to him stood forfeited, on the certificate issued by the Special Public Prosecutor, he was relegated to the position of an accused and did not remain a witness - No justification to permit the defence to cross examine the respondent no. 3 - Appeal allowed.
