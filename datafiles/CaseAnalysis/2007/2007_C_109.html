Commissioner Of Central Excise v Gas Authority Of India Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

13 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1532; (2007) 15 SCC 91; 2008 (232) E.L.T. 7

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Adjustment Of Duty

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 57D - Nature of product - Benefit of Modvat credit - Show cause notice was issued to assessee by Superintendent, Central Excise in which it was alleged by Department that notice/assessee had cleared Lean Gas in bulk to various companies as natural gas under claiming nil rate of duty - In said show cause notice allegation was based on footing that Lean Gas was by-product after due manufacturing process stood carried out on natural gas to extract LPG - Assessee claimed Modvat credit u/r. 57D of the Rules which stated that Modvat credit could not be denied or varied except in certain circumstances - Hence, instant Appeals - Whether Lean Gas produced by Gas Authority of India Limited (GAIL) was manufactured marketable final product or by-product - Held, from show cause notice which was foundation of demand made by Department, Lean Gas was by-product which emerged when LPG was extracted from natural gas - As provided that case of Department itself in show cause notice was that Lean Gas was by-product then there was no basis for denying benefit of Modvat credit to assessee during relevant period u/r. 57D of the Rules - Nowhere in show cause notice it was alleged by Department that Lean Gas was final product - Ultimately, assessee was required to reply to show cause notice and if allegation proceeded on basis that Lean Gas was by-product, then there was no question of assessee disputing that statement made in show cause notice - Tribunal had recorded that clearance was given to GAIL to prefer appeal to Tribunal against order passed by adjudicating authority and Commissioner Appeals - Hence, no such clearance was given in favour of Central Govt. which had preferred appeals - Appeals dismissed.
