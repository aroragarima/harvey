Chandigarh Administration And Anr v Jagiit Singh And Anr.Etc

Supreme Court of India

10 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1752; (1995) 1 SCC 745; AIR 1995 SC 705; 1995 (25) ALR 522; 1995 (2) BC 191; JT 1995 (1) SC 445; 1995 (1) RentLR 346; 1995(1) SCALE 131; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 126

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Summary: Administrative - Theory of discrimination - Principle for issuing writs - Held, order in case of similarly situated person no ground for issuing a favourable writ - If order in favour of other person is found to be contrary to law or not warranted in facts and circumstances of his case, it is obvious that such illegal or unwarranted order cannot be made basis of issuing a writ compelling respondent-authority to repeat illegality or to pass another unwarranted order - No equality in illegality - High Court to follow norms of writ jurisdiction - Orders of administrative authorities, no precedents - Each case to be decided on its own merits, factual and legal, as per relevant legal principles - Appeal allowed.
