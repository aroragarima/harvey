Â State of Jammu and Kashmir v Bakshi Ghulam Mohammad

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, J.R. Madholkar, R.S. Bachawat, J.M. Shelat, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 101; AIR 1967 SC 122; [1966] Supp S.C.R. 401

Case Digest

Subject: Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Constitution of Jammu and Kashmir

Summary: Parliament & Legislature - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 -Constitution of India, 1950 - Natural justice - Cross Examination - Habeas corpus - Constitution of Jammu and Kashmir - After the accession of the State of Jammu and Kashmir to India 1947, a responsible Govt. was set up by the Prime Minister and members of the Cabinet - Prime Minister was dismissed from office and a new Govt. was formed - New Constitution was framed for Jammu & Kashmir - In the first elections held under the Constitution, a party called the National Conference got the majority of votes - In the Govt. that was formed, Mr. A became the Prime Minister - Mr. A resigned and he became the Deputy Prime Minister of the State - He held these offices one after the other, for a total period of about sixteen years - Political rivalry took place - Some of the legislators wanted to bring in vote of no-confidence against Mr. B's Ministry - Mr. A and some of his supporters were arrested under the Defence of India Rules - Whether these were the matters of public importance - Held, first it was said that these matters were not of public importance because they had to be so at the date of the Notification and they, were not so on that date as Mr. A did not then hold any office in the Govt. - It was next said that there was no evidence of public agitation in respect of the conduct complained of and this showed that they were not matters of public importance - Public were not likely to agitate over this matter for they would not know about the mineral wealth at all - The matter won't be of public importance unless there was public agitation over it - There was no evidence whether there was public agitation or not - But Court stated that the matters were of public importance even if there was no public agitation over them - Appeal allowed.
