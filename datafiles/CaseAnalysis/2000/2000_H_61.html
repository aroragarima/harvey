State of Rajasthan v Harphool Singh (Dead) Through His Lrs

Supreme Court of India

04 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 257; (2000) 5 SCC 652; 2000 (4) ALD(SC) 18; 2000 (40) ALR 8; 2001 (1) CGLJ 220; JT 2000 (5) SC 546; 2000 (3) RCR(Civil) 191; 2000(4) SCALE 336; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 958; 2000 (5) SLT 151; 2000 (4) Supreme 215

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Rajasthan Colonisation Act, 1954, ss. 22, 24 and 25 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 80 and 100 - Plaintiff - respondent claimed that he was holding possession of property since time immemorial by fencing it and in year 1955 he constructed a house on disputed plot and started living therein - ADM/Secy., issued a notice calling upon him to vacate encroachment - (A) Whether order passed by ADM in exercise of his powers u/s. 22 of Act has become final and jurisdiction of civil court stands ousted in respect of such matters by virtue of s. 25? - Held, powers and procedure u/s. 22 of Act, are no substitute for civil court's jurisdiction and powers to try and adjudicate disputes of title relating to immovable property - (B) Whether respondent has a claim on property by adverse possession? - In order to substantiate such a claim of adverse possession ingredients of open, hostile and continuous possession with required animus, as laid down by courts should be proved for a continuous period of 30 years - Plaintiff claims to have put up construction in 1955 and there is absolutely no concrete and independent material to prove same, except an oral assertion - Further held, even if plaintiff's allegations and claims, as projected in plaint, are accepted in toto, period of so-called adverse possession would fall short by 5 years of required period - On materials on record plaintiff could not be held to have substantiated his claim of perfection of title by adverse possession to public property - Appeal allowed.
