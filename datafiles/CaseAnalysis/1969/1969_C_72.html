V. Verghese and Another v Commissioner of Income Tax, Kerala

Supreme Court of India

13 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, V. Ramaswami, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 277; [1970] 76 ITR 460; 1969 KLT 701

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income tax act, 1922, s.4 (3) - Agricultural Income - Capital Asset - Order of court was delivered in computing income of the appellant's father to tax for assessment year 1959-60 - Income-tax Officer included Rs.75, 000 received under an agreement for cutting and removing trees from 500 acres - Appellate Assistant Commissioner after calling for a report on certain facts confirmed the order - Tribunal detained that receipt was of a capital nature and deleted it from the taxable income - Commissioner of Income-tax, Tribunal referred question to the HC - Land was retained by assessee but a part of the asset was disposed of in its entirety by selling the trees with roots once and for all - Amount realized by sale as firewood of old and useless gravelia trees grown and maintained in tea gardens for the purpose of affording shade to tea plants was capital receipt and not revenue receipt - Whether Income-tax Appellate Tribunal was correct in holding that Rs.75,000 being income from felling of trees from forests is not subject to income-tax - Held, it was not necessary for the purpose of case to enter upon a detailed analysis of the principle underlying the decisions and to resolve the conflict - It was clear that the trees were not removed with routs - Stumps of the trees were allowed to remain in the land so that the trees may regenerate - If a person sells merely leaves or fruit of the trees or even branches of the trees it would be difficult to hold realization that was not of the nature of income - Where the trunks are cut so that the stumps remain intact and capable of regeneration, receipts from sale of the trunks would be in the nature of income - Tree was a part of the land - But by selling a part of trunk, assessee does not necessarily realize a part of his capital - Appeal dismissed.
