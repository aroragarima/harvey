Jaypee Rewa Cement v Commissioner of Central Excise, M. P.

Supreme Court of India

22 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, Shivaraj V. Patil, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20009; (2001) 8 SCC 586; AIR 2001 SC 3935; 2001 (98) ECR 193; 2001 (133) E.L.T. 3; JT 2001 (7) SC 261; 2001 (46) RLT 491; 2001(6) SCALE 96; 2001 (6) SLT 423; [2001] Supp2 S.C.R. 39; 2001 (6) Supreme 753

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax; Trade

Keywords: Central Excise Rules

Summary: Excise - Customs Tariff Act, 1975 - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 57-A, 57-F and 57-J - Whether it is necessary for explosives to be used within factory premises where manufacture of cement takes place? - According to r. 57-J, when Central Govt. by notification specified inputs used in manufacture of intermediate products received by manufacturer for use in or in relation to manufacture of final product, then all such products on which duty has been paid credit will be allowed - Held, even in respect of inputs used in manufacture of intermediate product which product is then used for manufacture of a final product, manufacturer would be allowed credit on duty paid in respect of input - Appeals allowed - CAs Nos. 279-82, 2368, 1628, 1629-30 and 1792 of 2001 dismissed.
