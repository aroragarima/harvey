Â State Of Assam & Anr. v Kuseswar Saikia And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

17 October 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.M. Shelat, C.A. Vaidyialingam, A.N. Grover, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 211; (1969) 3 SCC 505; AIR 1970 SC 1616; 1970 LabIC 1336; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 928; 1969 SLR 883; 1970 UJ 140

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution, arts. 233(1) and 235 - Judicial Services - (A) Promotion of person to be Additional District Judge, in whom vests - When ose of judicial service but holding post inferior to a District Judge vests in the High Court - (B) Reconstitution of subordinate judicial service in State by amending rules - Leaving no scope for exercise of power of High Court to make promotions - For persons below the rank of DJs ( including ADJ) - New hierarchy of Courts cannot be ignored by High Court - Held, promotion of person to be ADJ, in whom vests in Governor - Remedy is not to go against the Civil Courts Act as amended but to have the amendment rescinded - Appeal Allowed.
