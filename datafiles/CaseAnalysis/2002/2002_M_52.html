Collector of Central Excise, Chandigarh v M/s. Smithkline Beecham Consumer Health Care Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

20 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1559; (2003) 2 SCC 169; AIR 2003 SC 829; 2003 (2) ALD(SC) 30; 2003 (85) ECC 241; 2003 (108) ECR 42; 2003 (151) E.L.T. 5; 2003 (1) MLJ(SC) 158; 2002(9) SCALE 764; 2003 (1) SCW 267; 2003 (1) SLT 169; [2002] Supp5 S.C.R. 633; 2003 (1) Supreme 86

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, s.11A - Central Excise Rules, 1944 - r. 8(1) - Notification No 201/79 dated 4/6/1979 - Scheme thereunder - Scope and ambit of - Held, s. 11A provides for recovery of duties not levied or not paid or short levied or short-paid or erroneously refunded, such scheme under the notification is essentially linked with quantification and collection - The method of collection does not affect the essence of duty, but only relates to the machinery of collection for administrative convenience - Emphasis was on the duty of excise leviable on the manufactured item and duty of excise paid on the inputs available for adjustment - Therefore, s.11A had no application to such a situation - To that extent the Tribunal was not justified in its conclusions - However, the assessee had specifically questioned applicability of the notification after same was rescinded - Tribunal has not recorded any finding in this regard - The effect of s.38A, which was introduced with retrospective effect, is also to be considered - Matter remanded back to the Tribunal for consideration of these aspects - Appeals Disposed Of.Appeals against common judgment of the Customs Excise & Gold (Control) Appellate Tribunal -
