Laxmi Devi and Others v Mohammad Tabbar and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 926; (2008) 12 SCC 165; 2008 (2) ACC 364; AIR 2008 SC 1858; 2008 (3) ALD(SC) 129; 2008(3) ALL MR 872; 2008 (3) AWC 2346; 2008 (2) CTC 621; JT 2008 (4) SC 637; 2008 (5) MLJ(SC) 469; 2008 (151) PLR 452; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 39; 2008(4) SCALE 683; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 436; 2008 (2) UC 953

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Higher Multiplier

Summary: Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Compensation to deceased wife as settled by High Court at notional income of 15,000 and reduction in multiplier from 16 to 12 challenged - Relied on UPSRTC v. Trilok Chandra, 1996 Indlaw SC 2879 - Held, since, age of deceased was only 35 years and left with four minor daughters and young wife, as per sch 2 multiplier in case of person between 35-40 years is 16, so proper multiplier would be 14 as value of notional income has been increased and claimants entitled to a sum of Rs. 3,45,000/- with 6% interest - Appeal allowed.
